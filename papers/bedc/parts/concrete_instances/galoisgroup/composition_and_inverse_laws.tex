\begin{theorem}[GaloisGroupUp composition associativity row]
\label{thm:galoisgroup-composition-associativity-row}
Let $x$, $y$, and $z$ be accepted fixed-base automorphism-action rows of an
automorphism-action packet
$(E,G,b,a,m,i,c,\rho,\lambda)$ of
\autoref{def:galoisgroup-bhist-automorphism-action-packet}. Suppose the target
endpoint of $x$ is $\hsame$-classified with the source endpoint of $y$, the
target endpoint of $y$ is $\hsame$-classified with the source endpoint of $z$,
and the two finite $\mathsf{Cont}$ parenthesizations
$(z\circ y)\circ x$ and $z\circ(y\circ x)$ are both displayed by the composition
row $m$. Then the two parenthesized rows are classified by the inherited
$\GroupUp$ associativity row, transported along the endpoint $\hsame$ rows from
the public $\NameCert_{\GaloisExtUp}$ boundary of $E$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project $x$, $y$, and $z$ through
\autoref{def:galoisgroup-bhist-automorphism-action-packet}. The GaloisExtUp
public boundary of \autoref{thm:galoisext-public-obligation-boundary} supplies
the accepted source, middle, and target endpoints together with the classifier
rows that identify the two displayed middle endpoints.

Apply \autoref{thm:galoisgroup-composition-closure} first to $x$ and $y$, then
to the resulting row and $z$. This constructs the finite $\mathsf{Cont}$ row for
$(z\circ y)\circ x$ with the required fixed-base witness and provenance.

Apply \autoref{thm:galoisgroup-composition-closure} in the other order, first
to $y$ and $z$ and then to $x$ with the result. This constructs the finite
$\mathsf{Cont}$ row for $z\circ(y\circ x)$ over the same outer endpoints.

The $\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$ dependency $G$ supplies the operation, unit,
associativity, congruence, and two-sided inverse-law surface named in
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-group-namecert}. Its associativity row
classifies the two operation parenthesizations in $G$, and the endpoint
$\hsame$ rows from $E$ transport that classification to the displayed
automorphism-action rows.

Repacking the transported classifier, the two composition ledgers, the
fixed-base witnesses, and the $\Pkg$ provenance gives the claimed
GaloisGroupUp classifier row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GaloisGroupUp inverse cancellation rows]
\label{thm:galoisgroup-inverse-cancellation-rows}
For every accepted fixed-base automorphism-action row $x$ of an
automorphism-action packet
$(E,G,b,a,m,i,c,\rho,\lambda)$ of
\autoref{def:galoisgroup-bhist-automorphism-action-packet}, let $x^{-1}$ be the
inverse row supplied by \autoref{thm:galoisgroup-inverse-closure}. The composed
rows $x^{-1}\circ x$ and $x\circ x^{-1}$ are accepted by
\autoref{thm:galoisgroup-composition-closure} and are classified with the unit
automorphism row of \autoref{thm:galoisgroup-unit-automorphism-row} by the
inherited left and right inverse laws of the $\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$ dependency,
with endpoint transport through the public $\NameCert_{\GaloisExtUp}$ boundary.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.GaloisGroupUp.GaloisGroupAutomorphismActionCompositionPacket\_inverse\_cancellation\_rows}
\begin{proof}
Start with the accepted row $x$ and unpack it using
\autoref{def:galoisgroup-bhist-automorphism-action-packet}. The endpoint rows,
fixed-base row, classifier row, inverse row, provenance row, and finite ledger
all live in the same packet, while
\autoref{thm:galoisext-public-obligation-boundary} supplies the public
GaloisExtUp classifier rows needed for endpoint transport.

Use \autoref{thm:galoisgroup-inverse-closure} to obtain the accepted inverse
row $x^{-1}$. Its source and target are the reversed endpoints of $x$, and its
fixed-base witness is transported by the same $\hsame$ comparisons on the base
endpoint.

Apply \autoref{thm:galoisgroup-composition-closure} to $x$ followed by
$x^{-1}$, and again to $x^{-1}$ followed by $x$. These applications produce the
two finite composition ledgers and preserve the fixed-base witness on both
sides.

The $\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$ packet $G$ supplies the operation, unit, congruence,
associativity, and two-sided inverse-law rows from the GroupUp surface named in
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-group-namecert}. The right inverse law classifies
$x\circ x^{-1}$ with the unit row, and the left inverse law classifies
$x^{-1}\circ x$ with the unit row.

Finally, \autoref{thm:galoisgroup-unit-automorphism-row} identifies that unit
row with the fixed-base identity automorphism row over $E$. Repacking the two
inverse-law classifications with the endpoint $\hsame$ transports and the
ledger $\lambda$ gives the two claimed GaloisGroupUp cancellation rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GaloisGroupUp identity row closure]
\label{thm:galoisgroup-identity-row-closure}
For an automorphism-action packet $(E,G,b,a,m,i,c,\rho,\lambda)$ of
\autoref{def:galoisgroup-bhist-automorphism-action-packet}, the $\GroupUp$
unit row of $G$, read through the GaloisExtUp identity classifier of $E$ and
the fixed-base row $b$, is an accepted $\GaloisGroupUp$ automorphism-action row.
It fixes every displayed base endpoint by $\hsame$, carries only rows listed in
$\lambda$, and uses no equality of host fields or ambient automorphism sets.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.GaloisGroupUp.GaloisGroupAutomorphismActionPacket\_identity\_row\_closure}
\begin{proof}
Project the packet to its consumed rows $E$ and $G$. The Galois-extension
projection supplies the displayed extension endpoints, the identity classifier,
and the exactness rows on the public extension surface. The group projection
supplies the unit endpoint, the unit classifier rows, and the provenance needed
to read the unit as a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ row.

Now combine those projections with the fixed-base row $b$. For each displayed
base endpoint, the identity classifier leaves the endpoint in its original
$\hsame$ class, and $b$ records that this comparison is part of the finite
automorphism ledger. Thus the unit action fixes every displayed base endpoint.

Finally, repack the base row, identity action row, classifier row $c$,
provenance row $\rho$, and the same finite ledger $\lambda$. The resulting row
has the packet shape required by
\autoref{def:galoisgroup-bhist-automorphism-action-packet}, so it is accepted
by the public $\NameCert_{\GaloisGroupUp}$ carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GaloisGroupUp composition classifier congruence]
\label{thm:galoisgroup-composition-classifier-congruence}
Let $x,x',y,y'$ be accepted fixed-base automorphism-action rows in an
automorphism-action packet $(E,G,b,a,m,i,c,\rho,\lambda)$ of
\autoref{def:galoisgroup-bhist-automorphism-action-packet}. Suppose $x$ is
classified with $x'$, $y$ is classified with $y'$, the target endpoint of $x$
is classified with the source endpoint of $y$, and the target endpoint of
$x'$ is classified with the source endpoint of $y'$. Then the composite of
$x$ with $y$ is classified with the composite of $x'$ with $y'$. The
classification uses only inherited GaloisExtUp classifier transport,
$\GroupUp$ operation congruence, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$
provenance from the packet.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.GaloisGroupUp.GaloisGroupAutomorphismActionPacket\_composition\_classifier\_congruence}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.GaloisGroupUp.GaloisGroupAutomorphismActionCompositionPacket\_classifier\_sameSig\_congruence}
\begin{proof}
First transport the extension faces. The GaloisExtUp classifier of $E$
transports source, middle, and target endpoints across the displayed
$\hsame$ rows, and the $\Cont$ rows in $\lambda$ record the two compatible
composition ledgers.

Next transport the fixed-base faces. Since $x$ is classified with $x'$ and
$y$ is classified with $y'$, their fixed-base witnesses compare by the
classifier row $c$. The middle endpoint comparisons align the two ways of
reading the shared extension endpoint, so the composed fixed-base witness is
again compared by $\hsame$.

Apply the $\GroupUp$ operation congruence from $G$ to the operation endpoints
underlying the two composites. The $\mathsf{Pkg}$ row $\rho$ identifies these
operation endpoints as dependency rows from $G$, while the acted-on extension
endpoints remain dependency rows from $E$.

Repack the transported source, middle, target, fixed-base, operation, and
ledger rows as the composed classifier row. This gives classifier-equivalent
composites without introducing any host automorphism equality.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.GaloisGroupUp.GaloisGroupAutomorphismActionCompositionPacket\_classifier\_congruence}

\begin{theorem}[GaloisGroupUp automorphism ledger exactness]
\label{thm:galoisgroup-automorphism-ledger-exactness}
For an automorphism-action packet $(E,G,b,a,m,i,c,\rho,\lambda)$ of
\autoref{def:galoisgroup-bhist-automorphism-action-packet}, the public ledger
$\lambda$ hides exactly the dependency rows, fixed-base rows, identity rows,
action rows, composition rows, inverse rows, classifier rows, provenance rows,
and exactness rows exposed by the $\GaloisGroupUp$ automorphism surface. Every
public consumer of the packet factors through those rows and does not require
host automorphism equality, equality of ambient function spaces, or equality of
uncarried quotient representatives.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the packet coordinates. The rows $E$ and $G$ give the dependency
surfaces, $b$ gives the fixed-base surface, the unit endpoint of $G$ with the
identity classifier of $E$ gives the identity surface, $m$ gives composition,
$i$ gives inverse, $c$ gives classifier comparison, and $\rho$ gives
provenance. The exactness rows are precisely the exactness rows already named
by the dependency packets and the automorphism-action surface.

These projected rows account for every public operation used by a
$\NameCert_{\GaloisGroupUp}$ consumer. Carrier acceptance uses the dependency
and fixed-base rows; identity uses the unit and identity-classifier rows;
composition and inverse use $m$ and $i$; classifier transport uses $c$ together
with $\hsame$ and $\Cont$; provenance uses $\rho$.

Conversely, start from exactly those finite rows and their $\mathsf{Pkg}$
assignments. Repacking them as $E$, $G$, $b$, $a$, $m$, $i$, $c$, $\rho$, and
$\lambda$ reconstructs the public automorphism-action packet. Since no
coordinate contains host automorphism equality, no consumer can depend on such
an equality through the public ledger.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.GaloisGroupUp.GaloisGroupAutomorphismActionPacket\_automorphism\_ledger\_exactness}

\begin{theorem}[GaloisGroupUp inverse involution row]
\label{thm:galoisgroup-inverse-involution-row}
For every accepted fixed-base automorphism-action row $x$ of an
automorphism-action packet
$(E,G,b,a,m,i,c,\rho,\lambda)$ of
\autoref{def:galoisgroup-bhist-automorphism-action-packet}, let $x^{-1}$ be the
inverse row supplied by \autoref{thm:galoisgroup-inverse-closure}, and let
$(x^{-1})^{-1}$ be the inverse row obtained by applying the same closure row to
$x^{-1}$. Then $(x^{-1})^{-1}$ is accepted by the same packet and is classified
with $x$ by the inherited inverse-involutivity row of the
$\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$ dependency, transported through the public
$\NameCert_{\GaloisExtUp}$ endpoint classifier. The classification uses only
the fixed-base row $b$, the inverse row $i$, the classifier row $c$, the
provenance row $\rho$, and the finite ledger $\lambda$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the accepted row $x$ through
\autoref{def:galoisgroup-bhist-automorphism-action-packet}. Its source and
target endpoints are public GaloisExtUp endpoints from $E$, its operation
endpoint is a public GroupUp endpoint from $G$, and its fixed-base face is an
$\hsame$ comparison over the displayed base endpoint.

Apply \autoref{thm:galoisgroup-inverse-closure} to $x$. The resulting row
$x^{-1}$ is accepted by the same packet, has the source and target endpoints
reversed, and carries the inverse operation endpoint supplied by the
$\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$ surface of $G$. Its fixed-base face is transported along
the same base-endpoint $\hsame$ comparisons listed in $\lambda$.

Apply \autoref{thm:galoisgroup-inverse-closure} again to $x^{-1}$. This gives
an accepted row $(x^{-1})^{-1}$ whose displayed endpoints have been reversed
back to the orientation of $x$. The public boundary
\autoref{thm:galoisext-public-obligation-boundary} supplies the GaloisExtUp
classifier rows identifying these twice-transported endpoints with the original
source and target endpoints of $x$.

On the group side, the inverse operation endpoint of $(x^{-1})^{-1}$ is
classified with the operation endpoint of $x$ by the inverse-involutivity law
of the GroupUp dependency, for instance
\autoref{thm:group-left-inverse-involutive}. The same law may be read through
the right-inverse presentation of \autoref{thm:group-right-inverse-involutive};
both readings land in the classifier surface already named by $G$.

Transport that GroupUp classifier comparison across the endpoint classifier of
$E$. The fixed-base witnesses match because both $x$ and $(x^{-1})^{-1}$ fix
the same displayed base endpoint after the two endpoint transports, and the
classifier row $c$ compares exactly the endpoint, fixed-base, action, and
inverse faces recorded in the packet. Repacking these comparisons with
$\rho$ and $\lambda$ gives the claimed GaloisGroupUp classifier row between
$(x^{-1})^{-1}$ and $x$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GaloisGroupUp inverse of composition row]
\label{thm:galoisgroup-inverse-of-composition-row}
Let $x$ and $y$ be accepted fixed-base automorphism-action rows of an
automorphism-action packet
$(E,G,b,a,m,i,c,\rho,\lambda)$ of
\autoref{def:galoisgroup-bhist-automorphism-action-packet}. Suppose the target
endpoint of $y$ is $\hsame$-classified with the source endpoint of $x$, so that
$ x\circ y $ is an accepted composite by
\autoref{thm:galoisgroup-composition-closure}. Let $(x\circ y)^{-1}$ be the
inverse row supplied by \autoref{thm:galoisgroup-inverse-closure}, and let
$x^{-1}$ and $y^{-1}$ be the inverse rows of $x$ and $y$. Then the row
$y^{-1}\circ x^{-1}$ is accepted by the same packet and is classified with
$(x\circ y)^{-1}$ by the public GaloisGroupUp classifier. The comparison is the
inverse-of-product row of the inherited $\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$ surface,
transported through the endpoint classifier of the public
$\NameCert_{\GaloisExtUp}$ boundary.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack $x$ and $y$ through
\autoref{def:galoisgroup-bhist-automorphism-action-packet}. Their source and
target endpoints are public GaloisExtUp endpoints from $E$, their operation
endpoints are public GroupUp endpoints from $G$, and the displayed middle
endpoint comparison is an $\hsame$ row in the finite ledger $\lambda$.

Apply \autoref{thm:galoisgroup-composition-closure} to the composable pair
$y$ followed by $x$. This gives the accepted composite $x\circ y$, with source
endpoint the source of $y$, target endpoint the target of $x$, fixed-base
witness obtained by composing the two fixed-base witnesses, and operation
endpoint read through the multiplication row $m$ of $G$.

Apply \autoref{thm:galoisgroup-inverse-closure} to this composite. The row
$(x\circ y)^{-1}$ is accepted, has the reversed outer endpoints, and carries
the inverse operation endpoint of the GroupUp product underlying $x\circ y$.
Apply the same inverse-closure row separately to $x$ and to $y$. The target
endpoint of $x^{-1}$ and the source endpoint of $y^{-1}$ are the same middle
endpoint comparison, read in the reversed direction through the public boundary
of \autoref{thm:galoisext-public-obligation-boundary}. Hence another use of
\autoref{thm:galoisgroup-composition-closure} accepts the composite
$y^{-1}\circ x^{-1}$ over the same reversed outer endpoints as
$(x\circ y)^{-1}$.

On the GroupUp side, let the operation endpoints of $x$ and $y$ be read as
$g_x$ and $g_y$ inside the packet $G$. The composition row $m$ reads the
operation endpoint of $x\circ y$ as the product $g_x g_y$, while the inverse
row $i$ reads the operation endpoints of $x^{-1}$ and $y^{-1}$ as $g_x^{-1}$
and $g_y^{-1}$. The inherited inverse-of-product law
\autoref{thm:group-inverse-mul-reverse} gives the internal classifier row
$$
\hsame((g_x g_y)^{-1},g_y^{-1}g_x^{-1}).
$$
Thus the GroupUp operation endpoint carried by $(x\circ y)^{-1}$ is classified
with the GroupUp operation endpoint carried by $y^{-1}\circ x^{-1}$.

Transport this GroupUp classifier comparison through the GaloisExtUp endpoint
classifier. The source endpoint of $(x\circ y)^{-1}$ and the source endpoint of
$y^{-1}\circ x^{-1}$ are both the target endpoint of $x$, and their target
endpoints are both the source endpoint of $y$, up to the displayed $\hsame$
rows from $E$. The fixed-base witnesses also match: inverse closure preserves
the fixed-base comparison for each row, and composition closure composes those
fixed-base comparisons along the same middle endpoint transport.

Finally use the classifier surface described in
\autoref{thm:galoisgroup-fixed-base-classifier}. Its clauses compare exactly
the dependency classifiers, extension endpoints, fixed-base rows, action rows,
composition rows, inverse rows, and $\Pkg$ provenance recorded by
$(E,G,b,a,m,i,c,\rho,\lambda)$. Repacking the endpoint transports, the
GroupUp inverse-of-product classifier, the two composition ledgers, the
fixed-base witnesses, and the shared provenance row $\rho$ gives the claimed
GaloisGroupUp classifier row between $(x\circ y)^{-1}$ and
$y^{-1}\circ x^{-1}$.
\end{proof}
