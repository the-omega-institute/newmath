Following Bourbaki and Kelley on bornologies and Bishop--Bridges on bounded constructive metric data, the $\CauchyBornologyUp$ packet records the finite bounded-family surface needed by the Real and Completion route before any consumer asks for ambient completeness or a selected bound. It sits between the dyadic arithmetic of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, finite observations from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, regular rational readback from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, ball witnesses from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metricball-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rational-ball-namecert}, effective Cauchy source data from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-effective-cauchy-sequence-namecert}, rate budgets from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-rate-budget-namecert}, complete-metric consumers from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completemetric-namecert}, and the terminal $\RealUp$ seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet is a bounded-family NameCert interface, not a host supremum theorem, quotient-stream equality, countable-choice schedule, selected bound, or ambient Real completeness principle.

\begin{definition}[CauchyBornology carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-bornology-carrier}
A $\CauchyBornologyUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
B_{\mathrm{cau}}=(F,R,M,U,W,Q,A,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $F$ is the bounded-family source row. It lists the finite family requests whose members may be inspected by Real-facing or Completion-facing consumers.
\item $R$ is the dyadic radius-budget row read through $\DyadicRatCoreUp$. It stores the displayed centers, radii, slack conversions, and comparison budgets assigned to the accepted family requests.
\item $M$ is the $\MetricBallUp$ and $\RationalBallUp$ witness ledger, recording that each accepted source request is routed through the corresponding displayed ball witness and radius comparison.
\item $U$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window row. It records the finite observation windows that may be opened for the accepted family before regular rational readback is exposed.
\item $W$ is the $\CauchyRateBudgetUp$ row. It records the monotone tolerance-depth schedule used to decide which windows of $U$ are late enough for the displayed radius budget $R$.
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row obtained only after the bounded-family source, radius budget, ball witness ledger, stream windows, and rate schedule have been displayed.
\item $A$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row reached downstream of $F,R,M,U,W,Q$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the source, radius budget, ball witnesses, stream windows, rate schedule, regular readback, Real seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from any bounded-family request through $F,R,M,U,W,Q,A,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyBornologyUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\MetricBallUp$, $\RationalBallUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\CauchyRateBudgetUp$, $\EffectiveCauchySequenceUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\CompleteMetricUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyBornologyUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $F,R,M,U,W,Q,A,H,C,P,N$. The carrier has no coordinate for a host supremum, a selected bound, quotient-stream equality, countable choice, open-cover compactness, trichotomy, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyBornology NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-bornology-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CauchyBornologyUp$ carrier
$$
B_{\mathrm{cau}}=(F,R,M,U,W,Q,A,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyBornologyUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier exposure for the bounded-family source, dyadic radius budgets, $\MetricBallUp$ and $\RationalBallUp$ witnesses, $\StreamNameUp$ windows, $\CauchyRateBudgetUp$ schedule, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, $\RealUp$ seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming;
\item classifier stability, requiring accepted family reads in $F$ to be preserved componentwise by the displayed transport row $H$;
\item bounded-family ledger exactness, requiring every accepted family request to pass through the dyadic radius budget $R$ and the ball witness ledger $M$ before any regular readback is exposed;
\item radius-budget monotonicity, requiring each deeper tolerance request in $W$ to use a radius budget in $R$ at least as late and at least as tight as the displayed predecessor row;
\item completion-facing handoff, requiring any $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\CompleteMetricUp$, or $\CauchyRateBudgetUp$ consumer to read $Q$ and $A$ only after $F,R,M,U,W$ have been displayed;
\item non-escape, requiring $P$ and $N$ to export no host supremum, selected bound, quotient-stream equality, countable choice, open-cover compactness, trichotomy, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{enumerate}
No CauchyBornology NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier exposure is row projection from \autoref{def:cauchy-bornology-carrier}. The projected rows are exactly the bounded-family source $F$, dyadic radius budget $R$, ball witness ledger $M$, stream windows $U$, rate schedule $W$, regular readback $Q$, Real seal $A$, and structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

Classifier stability is componentwise. The row $H$ transports accepted family requests, radius budgets, ball witnesses, windows, schedules, readbacks, seals, replay, provenance, and local naming without adding a host-family predicate.

Ledger exactness is forced by the order of the displayed route. A family request first opens $F$, then reads its dyadic budget from $R$, then verifies the corresponding $\MetricBallUp$ or $\RationalBallUp$ witness in $M$ before any $\RegSeqRatUp$ row can be consumed.

Radius-budget monotonicity is the local content of $W$ read against $R$. Each deeper tolerance-depth request must replay the predecessor budget and then read the displayed later budget, so the schedule cannot jump to an unrecorded radius or to an ambient bound.

Finally, the completion-facing handoff is exhausted by $Q$ and $A$. Since $P$ and $N$ package only the listed rows, the excluded host supremum, selected bound, quotient-stream equality, countable choice, open-cover compactness, trichotomy, and ambient Real completeness data have no coordinate from which to escape.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyBornology completion handoff]
\label{thm:cauchy-bornology-completion-handoff}
Every $\RealUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\CompleteMetricUp$, or $\CauchyRateBudgetUp$ consumer of an accepted $\CauchyBornologyUp$ carrier reads bounded-family data only through the displayed route
$$
F \longmapsto R \longmapsto M \longmapsto U \longmapsto W \longmapsto Q \longmapsto A \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
Consequently the consumer obtains finite bounded-family source rows, dyadic radius budgets, ball witnesses, stream windows, rate schedules, regular readback, the terminal Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming, and obtains no host supremum, selected bound, quotient-stream equality, countable choice, open-cover compactness, trichotomy, or ambient Real completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from an accepted consumer request. By \autoref{def:cauchy-bornology-carrier}, the only bounded-family source coordinate is $F$, and every analytic row downstream of it is displayed inside the same finite packet.

The radius and ball data are next. The dyadic budget $R$ supplies the radius, slack, and comparison rows, while $M$ records the corresponding $\MetricBallUp$ and $\RationalBallUp$ witnesses. Thus no consumer can read boundedness before the displayed radius and ball ledgers are opened.

The sequence-facing data are downstream of those ledgers. The $\StreamNameUp$ row $U$ gives the finite windows, the $\CauchyRateBudgetUp$ row $W$ gives the late-window schedule, and the $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $Q$ is available only after $F,R,M,U,W$ have been displayed.

The terminal handoff then reaches the $\RealUp$ seal $A$ and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$. These rows preserve the same route for $\EffectiveCauchySequenceUp$ and $\CompleteMetricUp$ consumers, so the packet exports only the listed bounded-family data and none of the excluded host principles.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyBornology bounded-family non-escape]
\label{thm:cauchy-bornology-bounded-family-nonescape}
Let $B_{\mathrm{cau}}=(F,R,M,U,W,Q,A,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyBornologyUp$ carrier. Any bounded-family fact exported from $B_{\mathrm{cau}}$ is a rowwise consequence of the displayed source, dyadic radius budget, ball witness ledger, stream window, rate schedule, regular readback, Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows. It is not a host supremum, selected bound, quotient-stream equality, countable choice, open-cover compactness, trichotomy, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness datum.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier rows. The analytic coordinates are exactly $F,R,M,U,W,Q,A$, and the structural coordinates are exactly $H,C,P,N$.

A bounded-family read has no other entry point. If the read concerns a member of the family, it begins at $F$; if it concerns a radius, it passes through $R$; if it concerns containment, it passes through $M$; and if it concerns Cauchy or completion data, it proceeds through $U,W,Q,A$.

The structural rows preserve only those coordinates. Componentwise $\hsame$ transport and displayed $\Cont$ replay cannot manufacture a host supremum or a selected bound, while provenance and local naming record only the finite packet.

Therefore every exported bounded-family fact is confined to the displayed rows, and each excluded host principle has no coordinate, replay edge, provenance entry, or local NameCert row.
\end{proof}
