\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\LocatedLimitUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-locatedlimit-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, the $\LocatedLimitUp$ packet records located limits as explicit modulus-and-window data for displayed real-name sequences. It is a finite route from the Cauchy-equivalence surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchysequenceequivalence-namecert}, the regular-rational readback surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the finite stream-window surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, and the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert} to the real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet does not introduce host real equality, quotient limits, countable choice, trichotomy, or ambient completeness.

\begin{definition}[LocatedLimit carrier]
\label{def:located-limit-carrier}
A $\LocatedLimitUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(S,M,T,Q,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the displayed $\RealUp$ sequence surface whose entries are read only through named finite windows;
\item $M$ is the Cauchy/modulus ledger recording the explicit tolerance budget for the displayed sequence;
\item $T$ is the located finite-window schedule that says which sequence windows and dyadic tolerances are inspected for each requested precision;
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row obtained from the scheduled windows and the modulus ledger;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ limit seal reached through $Q$ and through no independent host-limit coordinate;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the sequence surface, modulus ledger, finite-window schedule, regular readback, and real seal;
\item $C$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ replay routes by which consumers reread the same windows and tolerances;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LocatedLimitUp$, $\RealUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedLimitUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a selected host limit, quotient equality of streams, countable-choice schedule, real trichotomy, or ambient completeness principle.
\end{definition}
\leandef{BEDC.Derived.LocatedLimitUp.LocatedLimitUp}

\begin{definition}[LocatedLimit classifier]
\label{def:located-limit-classifier}
Two accepted $\LocatedLimitUp$ packets
$$
L=(S,M,T,Q,E,H,C,P,N)
\quad\hbox{and}\quad
L'=(S',M',T',Q',E',H',C',P',N')
$$
are classified together exactly when their displayed finite-window schedules expose the same dyadic tolerance comparisons after componentwise transport. Thus the classifier compares $L$ and $L'$ only by shared windows in $T,T'$, the dyadic budgets read by $M,M'$, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback rows $Q,Q'$, and the $\RealUp$ seal rows $E,E'$ reached from those data.

No classifier step may use host real equality, quotient equality of streams, a selected limiting subsequence, countable choice, trichotomy, or an ambient completeness row.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[LocatedLimit NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:located-limit-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\LocatedLimitUp}$ surface for the carrier of \autoref{def:located-limit-carrier} consists of five finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission: the accepted object is exactly the displayed packet $L=(S,M,T,Q,E,H,C,P,N)$;
\item classifier: comparison is the finite-window and dyadic-tolerance classifier of \autoref{def:located-limit-classifier};
\item transport: $H$ and $C$ preserve the same sequence surface, modulus ledger, schedule, readback, and seal rows under accepted consumer motion;
\item modulus exactness: every limit-facing tolerance request is answered by the stored Cauchy/modulus ledger $M$ together with the schedule $T$;
\item Real-seal route: every $\RealUp$ limit read factors through $Q,E,H,C,P,N$ after the displayed rows $S,M,T$ have been read.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.LocatedLimitUp.LocatedLimitNameCertObligations}
\leanvariant{BEDC.Derived.LocatedLimitUp.LocatedLimitCarrier\_namecert\_obligations}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from the packet $L=(S,M,T,Q,E,H,C,P,N)$. The sequence surface, modulus ledger, located schedule, regular readback, real seal, transport rows, replay routes, provenance, and local naming row are exactly the coordinates named by the carrier.

The classifier row is finite because it reads only the windows named by $T,T'$ and the dyadic tolerances supplied by $M,M'$. The regular readbacks $Q,Q'$ and real seals $E,E'$ are compared only after those finite observations have been transported.

For transport, use $H$ to move the displayed rows componentwise and $C$ to replay the same consumer route. The replay never creates a new modulus, a host limit, or a quotient of streams; it returns to the stored rows.

Modulus exactness follows because the only tolerance ledger in the carrier is $M$, and the only located schedule is $T$. Therefore every downstream tolerance request has to pass through $M$ and $T$ before it can reach $Q$ or $E$.

The Real-seal route is then the visible composition from $S,M,T$ to $Q$ and finally to $E$, with $H,C,P,N$ carrying transport, replay, provenance, and naming. The excluded completeness, choice, trichotomy, quotient, and host-equality principles have no carrier coordinate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LocatedLimit Real seal route]
\label{thm:located-limit-real-seal-route}
Every $\RealUp$ limit read exported by an accepted $\LocatedLimitUp$ carrier factors through the displayed route
$$
S,M,T,Q,E,H,C,P,N .
$$
The sequence surface $S$ supplies the real-name entries, the ledger $M$ supplies the explicit Cauchy/modulus budget, the schedule $T$ chooses only finite windows for each dyadic tolerance, the row $Q$ repacks those observations as a $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, and the row $E$ is the sole $\RealUp$ limit seal available to consumers. The rows $H,C,P,N$ provide transport, replay, provenance, and local naming for the same route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. Its real-facing coordinates are not free-standing; $E$ is stored together with the sequence surface $S$, the modulus ledger $M$, the located schedule $T$, and the regular readback $Q$.

Read the limit request in finite order. A consumer first asks $T$ for the displayed windows attached to the requested dyadic tolerance, then reads the matching budget in $M$, and then uses $Q$ to view the resulting data as a regular rational name.

Only after that route is visible can the consumer read $E$. The transport and replay rows $H$ and $C$ preserve the same route, while $P$ and $N$ record provenance and local naming.

Thus every $\RealUp$ limit read factors through $S,M,T,Q,E,H,C,P,N$. Since no other coordinate exists, the packet exports no real completeness theorem, selected host limit, quotient equality of streams, countable-choice schedule, trichotomy, or bridge-checked standard interpretation.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.LocatedLimitUp.LocatedLimit\_real\_seal\_route}

\begin{theorem}[LocatedLimit window schedule exactness]
\label{thm:located-limit-window-schedule-exactness}
For every accepted $\LocatedLimitUp$ carrier $L=(S,M,T,Q,E,H,C,P,N)$, each limit-facing tolerance request reads the displayed schedule row $T$ and the modulus ledger $M$ before it reaches the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row $Q$ or the $\RealUp$ seal row $E$. The exactness claim is the finite routing statement of \autoref{def:located-limit-carrier} and \autoref{def:located-limit-classifier}, with the obligation order supplied by \autoref{thm:located-limit-namecert-obligations} and the terminal route supplied by \autoref{thm:located-limit-real-seal-route}.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.LocatedLimitUp.LocatedLimitWindowScheduleExactness}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:located-limit-carrier}. The only rows that can answer a tolerance request before readback are the schedule row $T$ and the modulus ledger $M$.

Use the classifier of \autoref{def:located-limit-classifier} to see that comparison is by shared finite windows and dyadic budgets, not by a host limit. Then apply \autoref{thm:located-limit-namecert-obligations}: modulus exactness is one of the named local obligations and it places $M$ and $T$ before every read of $Q$ or $E$.

Finally apply the route of \autoref{thm:located-limit-real-seal-route}. Since $E$ is reached only after $S,M,T,Q$, every tolerance request has already passed through $T$ and $M$ before any regular readback or real seal can be exported.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LocatedLimit schedule monotonicity]
\label{thm:located-limit-schedule-monotonicity}
For every accepted $\LocatedLimitUp$ carrier $L=(S,M,T,Q,E,H,C,P,N)$, enlarging a displayed precision request refines the stored finite-window schedule through the same modulus ledger $M$ and schedule row $T$. The refined request may expose later sequence windows, stronger dyadic tolerances, and a downstream $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row, but it may not replace $M$, allocate a new schedule row, or bypass the Real-seal route of \autoref{thm:located-limit-real-seal-route}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:located-limit-carrier}. The only rows that answer precision requests before readback are the modulus ledger $M$ and the finite-window schedule $T$.

Use \autoref{def:located-limit-classifier} to compare the original request and the enlarged request through transported finite windows and dyadic budgets. The classifier has no host limit or quotient-stream coordinate, so the comparison remains inside $M$ and $T$.

Apply \autoref{thm:located-limit-namecert-obligations} and \autoref{thm:located-limit-window-schedule-exactness}. Their modulus exactness clause places $M,T$ before every read of $Q$ or $E$, and the exactness theorem identifies this placement as the stored route for every displayed tolerance request.

Finally use \autoref{thm:located-limit-real-seal-route}. Since the Real seal is reached only after $S,M,T,Q$, a stronger request can only refine the visible windows and tolerances in the same schedule; it cannot introduce a separate modulus or an independent seal.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LocatedLimit Real seal comparison]
\label{thm:located-limit-real-seal-comparison}
Let $L=(S,M,T,Q,E,H,C,P,N)$ and $L'=(S',M',T',Q',E',H',C',P',N')$ be accepted $\LocatedLimitUp$ carriers. If their transported schedule rows, modulus ledgers, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback rows, and terminal $\RealUp$ seal rows match under the displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ routes, then any comparison between the two accepted Real seals is exhausted by those rows. No comparison may appeal to host real equality, quotient equality of streams, a selected limit, countable choice, trichotomy, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read both carriers through \autoref{def:located-limit-carrier}. The only comparison-facing rows are the sequence surfaces, modulus ledgers, schedule rows, regular readbacks, Real seals, transport rows, replay rows, provenance rows, and local naming rows.

Apply the classifier of \autoref{def:located-limit-classifier}. It compares accepted carriers through shared finite windows, dyadic budgets, readback rows, and Real seal rows after componentwise transport; it does not expose host real equality or quotient stream equality.

Use \autoref{thm:located-limit-namecert-obligations} to force the comparison through the transported schedule and modulus rows before any readback or seal is consumed. Then \autoref{thm:located-limit-window-schedule-exactness} identifies the finite-window route as the exact route for each displayed precision request.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:located-limit-real-seal-route}. The terminal Real seal is reached only after $S,M,T,Q$ on one side and $S',M',T',Q'$ on the other. Therefore matching transported schedules, modulus ledgers, readbacks, and seal rows exhaust the accepted comparison, and the excluded external principles have no carrier coordinate from which to act.
\end{proof}
