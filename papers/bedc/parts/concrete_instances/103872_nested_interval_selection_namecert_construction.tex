\begin{definition}[Nested interval selection carrier]
\label{def:nested-interval-selection-carrier}
A $\NestedIntervalSelectionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(I,L,B,W,R,D,Q,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $I$ is the accepted $\NestedIntervalUp$ finite chain whose visible intervals are being read.
\item $L$ is the $\LocatedIntervalUp$ row that records the locatedness tests available for each displayed endpoint window.
\item $B$ is the $\ClosedBoundedIntervalUp$ endpoint row anchoring the lower and upper interval bounds.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window row selecting the finite interval prefix inspected by the consumer.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row produced from the displayed endpoint windows.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger recording endpoint widths, midpoint tolerances, and the Cauchy budget requested of the selected row.
\item $Q$ is the finite selector row saying that the selected regular-rational approximant is read from the displayed window $W$ and ledger $D$.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row available only after $I,L,B,W,R,D,Q$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for all carrier rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the route
$$
I,L,B \longmapsto W,D \longmapsto R,Q \longmapsto E .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\NestedIntervalSelectionUp$, $\NestedIntervalUp$, $\LocatedIntervalUp$, $\ClosedBoundedIntervalUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\NestedIntervalSelectionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate asserts that these rows form one finite selection packet. The classifier compares accepted packets only by componentwise agreement of $I,L,B,W,R,D,Q,E,H,C,P,N$ under the displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ routes; it has no coordinate for a chosen intersection point, quotient stream equality, countable choice, trichotomy, or ambient real completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Nested interval selection NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:nested-interval-selection-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\NestedIntervalSelectionUp$ carrier
$$
S=(I,L,B,W,R,D,Q,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\NestedIntervalSelectionUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission, nested-chain exposure through $I$, located endpoint exposure through $L$, closed bounded endpoint exposure through $B$, finite window exposure through $W$, regular rational readback through $R$, dyadic tolerance accounting through $D$, selector-row exactness through $Q$, real-seal handoff through $E$, componentwise transport through $H$, displayed continuation replay through $C$, package provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The projection exposes the nested interval source, located endpoint tests, closed bounded endpoint anchor, finite window, regular readback, dyadic tolerance ledger, selector row, real seal, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.

The analytic obligations are exactly the visible rows. The nested-chain row gives the finite interval source, the located and closed bounded rows give endpoint admissibility, the window and dyadic rows give the inspected prefix and tolerance budget, and the regular readback plus selector row give the selected approximant.

The structural obligations are $H,C,P,N$. They transport, replay, source, and name the same finite route. Since the carrier contains no chosen point, quotient real equality, countable-choice function, trichotomy row, or real-completeness theorem, no further local $\NameCert$ obligation can be read.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}

Next read the Cauchy budget. The window row $W$ chooses the inspected finite prefix, and the dyadic ledger $D$ records the endpoint widths and tolerances used by the selector row $Q$. This is a finite $\BHist$ route, so the consumer receives no external sequence of choices.

Finally pass through the readback and seal rows. The $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $R$ supplies the regular-rational approximant, and $E$ may be read as a $\RealUp$ seal only after $I,L,B,W,D,R,Q$ are displayed. \label{thm:nested-interval-selection-nonescape}

\begin{proof}
