Following Bishop and Bridges on constructive absolute value for real names, the $\RealAbsoluteValueUp$ packet records absolute value as a finite NameCert read over the current L10 $\RealUp$ construction. It seals the dyadic endpoint of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicabsolutevalue-namecert} into regular real-name data without importing host real equality, trichotomy, or an ambient ordered-field object.

\begin{definition}[Real absolute-value carrier]
\label{def:real-absolute-value-carrier}
A $\RealAbsoluteValueUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
A=(S,R,\delta,D,M,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule row naming the finite read windows used to inspect the real name.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular-name row for the input real, including the rational approximants and regularity ledger.
\item $\delta$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row used to choose dyadic approximants at each displayed window.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicAbsoluteValueUp$ endpoint readback row, applying dyadic absolute value to the inspected dyadic cores.
\item $M$ is the $\CauchyModulusUp$ stability budget that bounds the replay of the endpoint readback across later windows.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row that packages the resulting regular absolute-value name.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the schedule, regular-name data, dyadic tolerance, dyadic endpoint readback, modulus budget, and real seal.
\item $C$ records the $\Cont$ replay routes from schedule inspection through dyadic readback to the sealed regular real name.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\DyadicAbsoluteValueUp$, $\CauchyModulusUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RealAbsoluteValueUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The accepted carrier contains no host real equality test, no trichotomy branch, no order-completion shortcut, no RealAlgOrder completion claim, and no absolute-value operation detached from the displayed regular-name and dyadic endpoint rows.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Real absolute-value NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:real-absolute-value-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted packet $A=(S,R,\delta,D,M,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\RealAbsoluteValueUp}$ obligation surface consists exactly of carrier admission, dyadic absolute-value stability, regular-sequence seal compatibility, triangle nonescape, and ledger exactness for the displayed rows $S,R,\delta,D,M,E,H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission reads the schedule, regular-name data, dyadic tolerance, dyadic endpoint readback, stability budget, and real seal from the finite packet. The support rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, package, and name exactly those displayed rows.

Dyadic absolute-value stability is local to $D$ and $\delta$. The endpoint readback uses the dyadic absolute-value packet, while the tolerance row fixes the finite accuracy at which the dyadic core is inspected.

Regular-sequence seal compatibility is supplied by $M$ and $E$. The modulus budget records enough stability to replay the endpoint readback across later windows, and the $\RealUp$ seal packages the resulting regular name without adding a host real comparison.

Ledger exactness follows by projection from the carrier. Every exported read is one of $S,R,\delta,D,M,E,H,C,P,N$, so no trichotomy branch, order-completion shortcut, or RealAlgOrder completion field can be recovered from the packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real absolute-value triangle nonescape]
\label{thm:real-absolute-value-triangle-nonescape}
Every triangle-facing consumer of an accepted $\RealAbsoluteValueUp$ packet reads only the regular-name seal $E$ together with its schedule, dyadic tolerance, dyadic endpoint readback, modulus budget, componentwise transport, continuation replay, package provenance, and local naming rows. The packet therefore exposes the triangle surface as a stability obligation over regular real names and dyadic absolute-value readback, not as host real order, trichotomy, or a completed $\RealAlgOrderUp$ structure.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier rows of \autoref{def:real-absolute-value-carrier}. The only endpoint operation is the dyadic readback $D$, and the only real-facing result is the $\RealUp$ seal $E$.

The triangle-facing route must use the schedule and tolerance rows to compare finite read windows, then use $M$ to replay the resulting regular-name stability. Transport by $H$ and replay by $C$ keep the route tied to those same displayed rows.

Package provenance $P$ and local naming $N$ identify the output as the $\RealAbsoluteValueUp$ surface. Since the carrier has no host real equality test, no trichotomy coordinate, and no RealAlgOrder completion field, a triangle consumer cannot escape the regular-name and dyadic-readback ledger.
\end{proof}
