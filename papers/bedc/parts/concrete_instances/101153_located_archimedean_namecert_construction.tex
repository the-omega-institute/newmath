\begin{definition}[LocatedArchimedean carrier]
\label{def:located-archimedean-carrier}
A $\LocatedArchimedeanUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(L,I,A,D,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)

with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $L$ is the $\LocatednessModulusUp$ row receiving the positive tolerance request and exposing the finite locatedness budget.
\item $I$ is the $\RealIntegerPartUp$ row that supplies the integer-part and bracket route attached to the same real-name source.
\item $A$ is the $\ArchimedeanApproximationUp$ row that records the finite bound, rational source, dyadic tolerance, and approximation window used by the search.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicDensityRealUp$ witness row available at the displayed radius after the locatedness and approximation rows have been opened.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window family opened for the tolerance request, integer-part bracket, approximation row, and dyadic-density witness.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row attached to the windows in $S$ before any Real-facing consumer receives the search boundary.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after $L,I,A,D,S,R$ have been displayed in the same history.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the locatedness request, integer-part bracket route, Archimedean approximation row, dyadic-density witness, stream windows, readbacks, real seal, continuation rows, provenance, and local naming.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from every precision, interval, or completion-facing request through $L,I,A,D,S,R,E$ without inserting a second Archimedean search.

\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedArchimedeanUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only through these displayed rows. It has no coordinate for trichotomy, quotient real equality, selected integer search, countable choice, hidden completion data, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[LocatedArchimedean NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:located-archimedean-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\LocatedArchimedeanUp$ carrier
$$
K=(L,I,A,D,S,R,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedArchimedeanUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier exposure for the locatedness request $L$, integer-part bracket route $I$, Archimedean approximation row $A$, dyadic-density witness $D$, stream windows $S$, regular readback $R$, real seal $E$, and structural rows $H,C,P,N$;
\item located search compatibility, requiring $L$ to provide the tolerance budget before any integer-part or approximation row is consumed;
\item bracket and approximation exactness, requiring $I$ and $A$ to name the same finite search boundary rather than independent host searches;
\item dyadic witness handoff, requiring $D$ to be read only after the locatedness, bracket, approximation, window, and readback rows have been displayed;
\item transport and replay stability, requiring $H$ and $C$ to preserve the accepted status of $L,I,A,D,S,R,E$ under componentwise movement and downstream requests;
\item non-escape, excluding trichotomy, quotient real equality, selected integer search, countable choice, hidden completion data, ambient Real completeness, Lean verification, and bridge checking from the local certificate surface.
\end{enumerate}
No LocatedArchimedean obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet $K=(L,I,A,D,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)$. The projection exposes exactly the locatedness request, integer-part bracket route, approximation row, dyadic witness, finite windows, regular readback, real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

The located search compatibility clause is the first gate. The tolerance and locatedness budget is already fixed by $L$, so the later integer-part and approximation rows cannot choose a separate search request.

The bracket and approximation clauses use the sibling surfaces of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-integer-part-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-archimedeanapproximation-namecert}. The rows $I$ and $A$ are displayed in the same $\BHist$ packet and are transported together by $H$, so their search data are read as one finite boundary.

The dyadic witness clause then applies \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadic-density-real-namecert}. The witness row $D$ is downstream of the locatedness, bracket, and approximation rows, and the windows $S$ with readback $R$ expose the same finite observation surface before the seal $E$ is reached.

Finally, $C$ replays all downstream requests through the listed rows, while $P$ and $N$ provide only provenance and local naming. Since the carrier has no field for the excluded principles, the displayed rows exhaust the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedArchimedeanUp}$ surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LocatedArchimedean dyadic-density handoff]
\label{thm:located-archimedean-dyadic-density-handoff}

$$
\LocatednessModulusUp \longmapsto \RealIntegerPartUp \longmapsto \ArchimedeanApproximationUp \longmapsto \DyadicDensityRealUp \longmapsto \StreamNameUp \longmapsto \RegSeqRatUp \longmapsto \RealUp .

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a dyadic-density read from an accepted carrier. The only dyadic-density coordinate is $D$, and it appears after the locatedness, integer-part, and approximation rows in the displayed packet.

The locatedness row $L$ fixes the tolerance budget. The integer-part route $I$ and approximation row $A$ then bind the finite Archimedean search boundary used by that budget, so the witness cannot arise from a detached host search.

The witness is exposed only through the window and readback rows $S$ and $R$. These are the same StreamName and RegSeqRat surfaces used by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and they precede the terminal $\RealUp$ seal $E$.

The rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve, replay, source, and name the same route. Hence every dyadic-density witness factors through the stated finite handoff and exports none of the excluded host or quotient data.
\end{proof}
