\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RationalCanonicalFormUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-rational-canonical-form-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Frobenius and standard linear algebra treatments of finite-dimensional operators, the $\RationalCanonicalFormUp$ packet records the rational canonical form of a displayed finite linear operator as BEDC NameCert data. It consumes the finite matrix rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-matrix-namecert}, the linear-map surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-linearmap-namecert}, the polynomial source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-polynomial-namecert}, the module and vector-space carriers of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-module-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-vecspace-namecert}, and the invariant-factor route supplied by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-smith-normal-form-namecert}. The packet names only a finite certificate surface for companion-block assembly; it does not assert a host Jordan form theorem, algebraic closure, eigenvalue selection, basis choice outside the displayed rows, or a global classification oracle.

\begin{definition}[Rational canonical form carrier]
\label{def:rational-canonical-form-carrier}
A $\RationalCanonicalFormUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
F=(V,L,M,S,I,B,A,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $V$ is the $\VecSpaceUp$ or $\ModuleUp$ source row naming the finite underlying carrier and its scalar action.
\item $L$ is the $\LinearMapUp$ row naming the endomorphism whose canonical form is being read.
\item $M$ is the $\MatrixUp$ presentation row for the same endomorphism over the displayed finite basis data already carried by $V$ and $L$.
\item $S$ is the consumed $\SmithNormalFormUp$ dependency row applied to the polynomial matrix route attached to $M$.
\item $I$ is the invariant-factor $\PolynomialUp$ row, listing monic polynomial factors with the divisibility ledger exposed by $S$.
\item $B$ is the companion-block row, assembling one finite block for each invariant factor in $I$ and recording the row and column positions used by the assembly.
\item $A$ is the block-assembly exactness row, stating that the displayed $\MatrixUp$ presentation is transported to the companion-block matrix through the same $\LinearMapUp$ and $\PolynomialUp$ rows.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the vector or module source, linear map, matrix presentation, Smith-normal dependency, invariant factors, companion blocks, assembly row, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ stores displayed $\Cont$ replay from a canonical-form request through $V,L,M,S,I,B,A$ and back to the same finite operator surface.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RationalCanonicalFormUp$, $\MatrixUp$, $\LinearMapUp$, $\PolynomialUp$, $\ModuleUp$, $\VecSpaceUp$, $\SmithNormalFormUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RationalCanonicalFormUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for selected eigenvalues, algebraic closure, host similarity classification, non-displayed basis choice, quotient matrix equality, or a detached canonical-form oracle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Rational canonical form NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:rational-canonical-form-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RationalCanonicalFormUp$ carrier
$$
F=(V,L,M,S,I,B,A,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\RationalCanonicalFormUp}$ surface consists exactly of finite source admission for $V,L,M$, Smith-normal dependency routing through $S$, invariant-factor exposure through $I$, companion-block assembly through $B$, block-assembly exactness through $A$, componentwise $\hsame$ stability through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier of \autoref{def:rational-canonical-form-carrier}. The algebraic source rows are exactly the vector or module carrier $V$, the endomorphism row $L$, the finite matrix presentation $M$, the Smith-normal dependency row $S$, the invariant-factor row $I$, and the companion-block assembly rows $B,A$.

The invariant factors are not read from a host classification theorem. They are exposed as $\PolynomialUp$ rows whose divisibility and matrix-reduction dependency is routed through $\SmithNormalFormUp$ before any companion block is assembled.

The companion-block row $B$ is finite because it is indexed by the displayed invariant-factor list $I$. The exactness row $A$ then compares the assembled block matrix with the original $\MatrixUp$ presentation through the same $\LinearMapUp$ endpoint and componentwise $\hsame$ transport.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name only this finite route. Since the carrier contains no coordinate for eigenvalue selection, algebraic closure, host similarity classification, non-displayed basis choice, quotient matrix equality, or a detached canonical-form oracle, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}
