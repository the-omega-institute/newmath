\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\MetricProjectionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-metricprojection-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Hilbert projection theory and constructive convex analysis, the $\MetricProjectionUp$ packet records the nearest-point surface for a displayed closed convex subset of a Hilbert carrier as finite BEDC data. It sits after the Hilbert source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-hilbert-namecert}, the closed convex source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-convexset-namecert}, the metric distance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the complete metric limit surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completemetric-namecert}, and the real-name readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet names the projection route without importing a host nearest-point selector, quotient equality of points, countable choice, trichotomy, or ambient real completeness.

\begin{definition}[MetricProjection carrier]\label{def:metric-projection-carrier}
A $\MetricProjectionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(H,C,D,I,W,E,T,R,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $H$ is the $\HilbertUp$ source row, including the inner-product carrier, norm ledger, and induced metric route.
\item $C$ is the closed convex source row, recording the admitted subset, convex-combination stability, and closedness boundary over the Hilbert source.
\item $D$ is the metric distance ledger, exposing only displayed $\MetricUp$ distance comparisons between the external point, subset witnesses, and candidate endpoints.
\item $I$ is the located infimum budget for the squared-distance values over the displayed closed convex source.
\item $W$ is the approximating minimizing window, consisting of finite candidates, tolerances, and the monotone comparison schedule used to approach the located infimum.
\item $E$ is the projection endpoint row, recording the carried subset point admitted as the limit of the minimizing window.
\item $T$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $H,C,D,I,W,E,R,P,N$.
\item $R$ records the displayed $\Cont$ replay from distance comparisons through located infimum, minimizing windows, endpoint admission, and closest-point reads.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\HilbertUp$, $\ConvexSetUp$, $\MetricUp$, $\CompleteMetricUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MetricProjectionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The local classifier compares accepted carriers only by these displayed rows. It has no coordinate for a host projection map, selected representative, quotient equality of Hilbert points, ambient choice principle, or global completeness theorem outside the named sources.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[MetricProjection NameCert obligations]\label{thm:metric-projection-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\MetricProjectionUp$ carrier $Q=(H,C,D,I,W,E,T,R,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\MetricProjectionUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier exposure for the Hilbert source, closed convex source, metric distance ledger, located infimum budget, approximating minimizing window, projection endpoint, componentwise transport, replay route, package provenance, and local naming row. Every projection-facing consumer must read the endpoint through $H,C,D,I,W,E,T,R,P,N$ in that order of dependency, and no consumer receives a host nearest-point selector, quotient representative, trichotomy decision, countable-choice schedule, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness theorem from the packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier exposure is projection from \autoref{def:metric-projection-carrier}. The tuple lists the Hilbert source, closed convex source, metric distance ledger, located infimum budget, minimizing window, endpoint, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row as its only coordinates.

The projection route is finite. Distance reads pass through $D$, lower-bound and approximation reads pass through $I$ and $W$, and endpoint admission passes through $E$ after the closed convex source row $C$ and complete metric source are displayed.

The remaining rows preserve the same route. Transport $T$ moves only the displayed coordinates, replay $R$ consumes them in the stated dependency order, and $P,N$ record provenance and local naming. Since the carrier lacks rows for host selectors, quotient representatives, trichotomy, countable choice, and ambient real completeness, those objects cannot be exported by the local NameCert.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetricProjection closest-point nonescape]\label{thm:metric-projection-closest-point-nonescape}
Every accepted closest-point read exported by a $\MetricProjectionUp$ carrier factors through the displayed located infimum budget $I$, approximating minimizing window $W$, and projection endpoint row $E$ over the Hilbert, closed convex, metric, complete metric, and real readback sources. Thus a consumer can use the carried endpoint only as a finite BEDC projection endpoint, not as a host nearest-point selector or an unconstrained choice of representative.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with an accepted closest-point read. By \autoref{thm:metric-projection-namecert-obligations}, the read reaches the local naming row only through $H,C,D,I,W,E,T,R,P,N$.

The rows $I$ and $W$ provide the located lower bound and finite approximation route for squared distances. The endpoint row $E$ is admitted only after the minimizing window is replayed over the closed convex source and the complete metric limit surface.

Consequently the exported point is tied to the displayed finite route. Changing the endpoint by appealing to a host projection map, a selected representative, or a choice principle is not a move inside the carrier, so such data cannot escape through the $\MetricProjectionUp$ packet.
\end{proof}
