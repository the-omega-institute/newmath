\begin{definition}[UniformCompletionReflector carrier]
\label{def:uniform-completion-reflector-carrier}

$$

$$

\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the uniform source row, naming the uniform-space carrier, entourage basis, and separatedness boundary inspected by the reflector.
\item $W$ is the Cauchy filter or finite-window row, recording the certified countable names, regular Cauchy windows, and tolerance comparisons admitted from $X$.
\item $Y$ is the completion target row, displaying the completed uniform carrier only through the completion data certified by $W$.
\item $J$ is the dense embedding row from $X$ into $Y$, including the finite image windows and density witnesses visible to downstream consumers.
\item $Q$ is the universal arrow row: every displayed uniformly continuous map from $X$ into a complete separated target factors through the same $Y,J$ surface by a unique displayed route.

\begin{theorem}[UniformCompletionReflector NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:uniform-completion-reflector-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\UniformCompletionReflectorUp$ carrier
$$
U=(X,W,Y,J,Q,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\UniformCompletionReflectorUp}$ surface consists exactly of uniform source admission through $X$, certified Cauchy-window readback through $W$, completion-target display through $Y$, dense embedding through $J$, universal arrow exactness through $Q$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, package provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$. The certificate therefore exposes the same finite route to completion consumers, Real-facing consumers, and countable-name consumers without exporting quotient completion equality or selected limits.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:uniform-completion-reflector-carrier}. The source row $X$ fixes the uniform object that can be inspected, while $W$ records the only Cauchy filter or regular-window evidence available to the reflector.

The completion target and dense embedding rows are downstream of those inputs. Thus $Y$ and $J$ can be read only after the source and Cauchy-window rows have been displayed, and neither row carries a host quotient or selected representative.

The universal arrow row $Q$ is part of the same packet. Any factorization read must use the displayed $Y,J$ route and cannot replace it with an ambient completion object or a choice-selected limit.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same rows componentwise. Since the carrier has no additional equality, selector, or host-limit coordinate, the listed rows exhaust the NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\label{thm:uniform-completion-reflector-universal-arrow}

\begin{proof}

The factorization route is consequently displayed. A consumer can inspect the uniformly continuous source map only through $X$ and $W$, then read its extension through the public $Y,J,Q$ rows.
