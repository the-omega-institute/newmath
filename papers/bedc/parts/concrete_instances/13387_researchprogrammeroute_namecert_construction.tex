\begin{definition}[Research programme route carrier]
\label{def:research-programme-route-carrier}
A $\ResearchProgrammeRouteUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$

with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $P$ is the phase-route row, recording the current research phase, admissible next phase, and the displayed route through the certificate programme;
\item $S$ is the $\ProgrammeStrengthLedgerUp$ row, recording claim strength, dependency status, theory and verification status, bridge status, refusal, and local naming;
\item $K$ is the $\KernelPhaseRefusalUp$ row, recording the phase-boundary refusal that blocks targets outside the displayed kernel phase;
\item $V$ is the $\VerificationFailureRoadmapUp$ row, recording checked-report failure, downgrade route, roadmap boundary, cannot-claim data, and upgrade-registry data;

\item $X$ is the exit-criterion row, recording which phase, strength, refusal, failure-roadmap, export, and review rows must be present before the route can leave the current research phase;

\label{thm:research-programme-route-namecert-obligations}

\begin{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Research programme route phase refusal]
\label{thm:research-programme-route-phase-refusal}
Every accepted $\ResearchProgrammeRouteUp$ packet reads the phase-route row $P$ together with the kernel-refusal row $K$ before any programme phase can be treated as admissible. A target blocked by $K$ cannot be exported by strengthening $S$, by citing the roadmap $V$, by packaging through $E$, or by appealing to operator review $O$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the carrier rows. The route row $P$ and refusal row $K$ are separate displayed coordinates, so phase admissibility is not read from strength, roadmap, export, or review data alone.

Apply the kernel-phase refusal sibling. Its accepted packet records the current phase, attempted target, dependency blocker, refusal verdict, transport, continuation, provenance, and local name. This row remains visible inside $\ResearchProgrammeRouteUp$ as $K$.

The rows $S,V,E,O$ can report strength, failure, export, and review only for the same displayed packet. They do not contain a second phase-admission coordinate. Hence a target refused by $K$ remains outside the accepted route.
\end{proof}

It remains to compare $O$ with $X$. The exit-criterion row is a separate displayed coordinate, not a conclusion of review preference. Therefore operator review cannot erase, promote, bypass, or replace any listed programme boundary.
