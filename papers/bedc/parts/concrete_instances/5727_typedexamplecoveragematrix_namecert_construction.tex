The $\TypedExampleCoverageMatrixUp$ packet realizes the TypedExamples audit-map vision of \autoref{ch:metacic-typedexamples-audit-map} as a finite coverage matrix. It sits beside the MetaCIC gallery of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacictypedexamplesgallery-namecert}, the typed-example gallery of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-typedexamplegallery-namecert}, the worked-example seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacic-typedexamples-seal-namecert}, the regression-spend budget of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-typedexampleauditbudget-namecert}, and the beta-step boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-betastepboundary-namecert}. Those siblings expose gallery rows, checker rows, seal rows, budget rows, and beta-boundary rows; this chapter binds them as one finite row-indexed coverage object for checker-audit consumers.

\begin{definition}[Typed example coverage matrix carrier]
\label{def:typed-example-coverage-matrix-carrier}
A $\TypedExampleCoverageMatrixUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
M=(F,G,C,B,R,E,H,K,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $F$ is the finite family index for accepted typed-example families;
\item $G$ records, for each row of $F$, the paired gallery cells inherited from the MetaCIC and typed-example gallery packets;
\item $C$ records the checker cells attached to the same rows;
\item $B$ records the beta-boundary cells attached to the same rows;
\item $R$ records the regression-spend cells and their accepted coverage budget;
\item $E$ is the exactness row asserting that every accepted family in $F$ has the displayed $G,C,B,R$ cells and no extra coverage cell;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the family, gallery, checker, beta, regression-spend, exactness, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item $K$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which checker-audit consumers read exactly the cells $F,G,C,B,R,E$;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over the sibling gallery, seal, budget, beta-boundary, transport, continuation, and exactness rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\TypedExampleCoverageMatrixUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for checker completeness, subject reduction, normalization, arbitrary beta closure, or unindexed regression spending.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Typed example coverage matrix NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:typed-example-coverage-matrix-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:typed-example-coverage-matrix-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\TypedExampleCoverageMatrixUp}$ surface consists exactly of five finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for $M=(F,G,C,B,R,E,H,K,P,N)$;
\item family-index exactness through $F$ against the accepted typed-example family rows;
\item paired gallery, checker, beta, and regression-spend cell exposure through $G,C,B,R$;
\item matrix exactness through $E$, requiring each accepted row to carry all four cell kinds and no unindexed cell;
\item transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming through $H,K,P,N$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection of the displayed packet. The row $F$ supplies the finite family index before any coverage cell is read.

The rows $G,C,B,R$ are paired by position. A consumer can inspect a gallery cell, checker cell, beta cell, or regression-spend cell only as the cell attached to the same family row.

The exactness row $E$ records that the four cell kinds are jointly present for every accepted family and that no extra coverage coordinate exists outside the matrix. The rows $H,K,P,N$ then transport, route, name, and package only the displayed rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Typed example coverage matrix regression exactness]
\label{thm:typed-example-coverage-matrix-regression-exactness}
Every regression-spend cell in an accepted $\TypedExampleCoverageMatrixUp$ packet is paired with the unique displayed family row and with the corresponding gallery, checker, and beta cells. Conversely, every accepted family row has a displayed regression-spend cell in $R$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet to $F,G,C,B,R,E$. The exactness row $E$ is read against the common family index $F$, so each regression-spend entry is indexed before it is exposed to a checker-audit consumer.

For a displayed spend cell, $E$ supplies the matching gallery, checker, and beta cells in the same row. For a displayed family row, $E$ supplies the corresponding regression-spend cell in $R$.

Since the carrier has no unindexed spend coordinate, these two readings exhaust regression coverage for the packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Typed example coverage matrix non-escape]
\label{thm:typed-example-coverage-matrix-nonescape}
Every checker-audit consumer of an accepted $\TypedExampleCoverageMatrixUp$ carrier reads coverage only through the displayed matrix rows $F,G,C,B,R,E,H,K,P,N$. The consumer cannot replace a missing gallery, checker, beta, or regression-spend cell by a sibling packet alone, an ambient checker theorem, or an unindexed budget row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Let a checker-audit consumer route be given. The route must pass through the displayed continuation row $K$ and therefore reaches only the family, cell, exactness, transport, provenance, and local naming rows.

The sibling packets provide provenance for the cell contents, but the matrix pairing is carried by $F$ and $E$. A sibling gallery, seal, budget, or beta-boundary packet alone does not store the combined cell row.

No coordinate in the carrier names an ambient checker theorem or an unindexed budget row. Hence the accepted consumer surface is exactly the finite coverage matrix.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Typed example coverage matrix spend boundary]
\label{thm:typed-example-coverage-matrix-spend-boundary}
If a checker-audit consumer spends a regression budget from an accepted $\TypedExampleCoverageMatrixUp$ packet, the spend is licensed only for the family row whose gallery, checker, beta, and regression-spend cells are all displayed in the same matrix row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
A regression spend is read from $R$ through the continuation route $K$. The exactness row $E$ identifies the family row in $F$ and the paired gallery, checker, and beta cells in $G,C,B$.

The transport row $H$ preserves the same row identity under componentwise $\hsame$ movement, while $P$ and $N$ record provenance and local naming only. They do not create a second spend endpoint.

Therefore a spend is confined to the displayed matrix row that carries all four cell kinds.
\end{proof}
