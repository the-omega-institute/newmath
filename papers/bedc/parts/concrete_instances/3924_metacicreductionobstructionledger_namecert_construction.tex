The $\MetaCICReductionObstructionLedgerUp$ packet gives the obstruction vision of \autoref{ch:metacic-subject-reduction-obstruction} a finite BEDC naming surface. It reads the subject-reduction carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-subjectreduction-namecert}, the closed generator audit packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closedgeneratoraudit-namecert}, and the generator audit closure packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-generatorauditclosure-namecert} as the surrounding ledger rows for the four MetaCIC discharge obligations. The packet does not prove those obligations; it names their position, their subject-reduction handoff, and the non-escape boundary that prevents an unrecorded closed theorem from being exported.

\begin{definition}[MetaCIC reduction obstruction ledger carrier]
\label{def:metacic-reduction-obstruction-ledger-carrier}
A $\MetaCICReductionObstructionLedgerUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
R=(S,B,A,L,P,G,J,O,H,C,Q,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ records the $\SubjectReductionUp$ row consumed by downstream type-preservation packets;
\item $B$ records the beta-substitution discharge row;
\item $A$ records the application-argument stability discharge row;
\item $L$ records the lambda-domain preservation discharge row;
\item $P$ records the Pi-domain preservation discharge row;
\item $G$ records the $\ClosedGeneratorAuditUp$ source for closed generator and substitution audit evidence;
\item $J$ records the $\GeneratorAuditClosureUp$ source for generator-closure replay;
\item $O$ records the obstruction row for the closed subject-reduction request;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the subject-reduction row, the four discharge rows, the two generator-audit rows, the obstruction row, replay routes, provenance, and local naming;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ routes through which consumers read the parameterized subject-reduction row, the four discharge rows, the generator-audit rows, and the obstruction row;

The carrier contains no coordinate for a closed subject-reduction theorem, hidden proof of a discharge row, host-calculus preservation theorem, quotient transport, classical selection, proposition extensionality, or unrecorded proof oracle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC reduction obstruction ledger NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:metacic-reduction-obstruction-ledger-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\MetaCICReductionObstructionLedgerUp$ carrier
$$
R=(S,B,A,L,P,G,J,O,H,C,Q,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\MetaCICReductionObstructionLedgerUp}$ surface has four finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item subject-reduction exposure: $S$ is the only displayed subject-reduction row available to a consumer;
\item discharge inventory: $B,A,L,P$ are the only rows that may feed the beta-substitution, application-argument, lambda-domain, and Pi-domain sockets;
\item generator-audit anchoring: $G$ and $J$ are the only rows from which closed-generator and generator-closure audit evidence may be read;
\item obstruction exactness: $O,H,C,Q,N$ route every closed subject-reduction request back to the displayed obstruction ledger unless all four discharge rows are supplied as proof rows for $S$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:metacic-reduction-obstruction-ledger-carrier}. The rows $S,B,A,L,P,G,J,O,H,C,Q,N$ are the complete packet inventory, so the local name has no source outside that tuple.

The subject-reduction read is confined to $S$. The four discharge rows $B,A,L,P$ are listed separately, and the generator-audit rows $G,J$ remain attached to their own displayed coordinates.

The obstruction row $O$ handles the closed request. Transport through $H$, replay through $C$, provenance through $Q$, and naming through $N$ keep that request inside the same finite packet rather than replacing it by an unrecorded theorem.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC reduction obstruction ledger non-escape]
\label{thm:metacic-reduction-obstruction-ledger-nonescape}
Every consumer of an accepted $\MetaCICReductionObstructionLedgerUp$ carrier can read only the rows
$$
S,B,A,L,P,G,J,O,H,C,Q,N.
$$
In particular, the packet exports no closed subject-reduction theorem, hidden discharge proof, host-calculus preservation principle, quotient transport, classical selection, proposition extensionality, or unrecorded proof oracle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the finite carrier. Each exported coordinate is one of the displayed rows, and each public replay route is recorded in $C$.

Apply \autoref{thm:metacic-reduction-obstruction-ledger-namecert-obligations}. The subject-reduction row, four discharge rows, generator-audit rows, and obstruction row exhaust the local $\NameCert$ surface.

Since the excluded objects do not occur as coordinates of $R$, componentwise transport and package provenance cannot export them. A consumer may only replay the rows named by the ledger.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC reduction obstruction ledger subject-reduction handoff]
\label{thm:metacic-reduction-obstruction-ledger-subjectreduction-handoff}
An accepted $\MetaCICReductionObstructionLedgerUp$ packet hands subject-reduction consumers to the displayed $\SubjectReductionUp$ row $S$ together with the four obligation rows $B,A,L,P$. A consumer obtains a subject-reduction read only by supplying those four rows as the discharge inputs required by $S$; otherwise the closed request is answered by the obstruction row $O$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the handoff coordinate $S$ from the carrier. It is the only subject-reduction row recorded in the packet.

The rows $B,A,L,P$ are the four displayed discharge inputs. The carrier does not contain a second source that could supply those inputs invisibly.

If the four rows are not supplied as proof rows for $S$, the route $C$ sends the closed subject-reduction request to $O$. Thus the handoff is finite and explicit: either the consumer supplies the displayed obligations, or the ledger returns the obstruction row.
\end{proof}

Its rows link the four discharge obligations to $\SubjectReductionUp$, $\ClosedGeneratorAuditUp$, and $\GeneratorAuditClosureUp$ while keeping the closed subject-reduction request behind the explicit obstruction row.

Use \autoref{thm:metacic-reduction-obstruction-ledger-subjectreduction-handoff} for the subject-reduction route and \autoref{thm:metacic-reduction-obstruction-ledger-nonescape} for the exclusion boundary.
