\begin{definition}[Registry no-smuggling predicate carrier]

A $\RegistryNoSmugglingPredicateUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$

\begin{enumerate}
\item $T$ is the formalization-target row, recording the $\MachineInterfaceFormalizationTargetUp$ name, namespace, statement skeleton, dependency surface, expected status, and not-claimed surface being checked;
\item $E$ is the registry-export row, recording the $\RegistryExportSurfaceUp$ registration, blocking, status, machine-target, audit, and non-smuggling fields for the same candidate export;
\item $G$ is the consistency-gate row, recording the $\RegistryExportConsistencyGateUp$ status, audit-route, formal-target readback, and no-smuggling decision;

\begin{theorem}[Registry no-smuggling predicate NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:registry-no-smuggling-predicate-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:registry-no-smuggling-predicate-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\RegistryNoSmugglingPredicateUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following five obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for $R=(T,E,G,U,H,C,P,N)$;
\item target and export exposure through $T$ and $E$;
\item consistency and no-smuggling routing through $G$ and $U$;
\item machine-boundary replay, componentwise transport, and package provenance through $C,H,P$;
\item local naming through $N$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the displayed packet $R=(T,E,G,U,H,C,P,N)$. The target row $T$ names the machine-facing object being checked, while $E$ records the registry export surface that would otherwise expose it to a theorem-facing consumer.

The consistency-gate row $G$ and classifier row $U$ decide whether the same candidate passes no-smuggling. The classifier row keeps the five early refusal modes separate: philosophical smuggling, mathematical smuggling, host smuggling, roadmap-as-theorem, and cannot-claim conflict.

The rows $H,C,P,N$ then preserve and name the same decision across transport, replay, package provenance, and local certification. Since no additional coordinate exists, the five displayed obligation rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[No-smuggling refusal blocks theorem-evidence export]
\label{thm:registry-no-smuggling-predicate-refusal-blocks-export}
If an accepted $\RegistryNoSmugglingPredicateUp$ packet marks a registry export by a no-smuggling refusal row, then that export cannot be read as theorem evidence by the same machine-facing registry route. Any later theorem evidence must pass through a distinct accepted packet whose classifier row no longer carries the refusal.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the candidate export through $E$ and the same target through $T$. The gate row $G$ compares that export with the target status, dependency surface, and audit route before a machine-facing consumer may use it.

When $U$ records a no-smuggling refusal, the displayed route is refusal rather than admission. The sibling classifier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-smugglingpatternclassifier-namecert} supplies the classification row, while the machine-interface boundary keeps the route from importing a hidden theorem object.

The transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows preserve the refused route. Therefore the same packet cannot export theorem evidence; a theorem-evidence export requires a separately displayed packet with its own accepted classifier row.
\end{proof}
