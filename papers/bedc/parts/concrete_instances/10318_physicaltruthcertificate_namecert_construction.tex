\begin{definition}[Physical truth certificate carrier]
\label{def:physical-truth-certificate-carrier}
A $\PhysicalTruthCertificateUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
T=(S,F,O,D,I,L,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the finite source-trace and observation-bundle row from which the claim is read;
\item $F$ is the $\RealityConstrainedOpenFitUp$ row exposing finite fit, open continuation, and finite refutation;
\item $O$ is the $\RealityConstrainedObjectivityUp$ row exposing observer-anchor change and invariant content;
\item $D$ is the tower-descent row recording which operations survive the approximation route;
\item $I$ is the stability row naming the continuation domain over which the claim may be reused;
\item $L$ is the ledger row recording hidden tower levels, compression, idealization, provenance, and residue;
\item $R$ is the failure-surface row naming finite defeating observations, signature mismatches, or domain exits;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the source, fit, objectivity, descent, stability, ledger, failure, route, provenance, and local-name rows;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay through all rows of $T$;
\item $P$ records $\Pkg$ provenance over $\PhysicalTruthCertificateUp$, $\RealityConstrainedOpenFitUp$, $\RealityConstrainedObjectivityUp$, $\PhysicalModelAuditUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\PhysicalTruthCertificateUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for model--reality identity, final confirmation, unledgered idealization, detached observer certainty, hidden truth oracle, host physical equality, or truth export outside the displayed packet.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Physical truth certificate NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:physical-truth-certificate-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:physical-truth-certificate-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\PhysicalTruthCertificateUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $T=(S,F,O,D,I,L,R,H,C,P,N)$, open-fit exposure through $F$, observer-invariance exposure through $O$, tower-descent exposure through $D$, stability exposure through $I$, ledger exactness through $L$, failure-surface exposure through $R$, componentwise transport through $H$, displayed route replay through $C$, provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.PhysicalTruthCertificateUp.PhysicalTruthCertificateNameCertObligations}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission projects the finite packet of \autoref{def:physical-truth-certificate-carrier}. No row outside $S,F,O,D,I,L,R,H,C,P,N$ can be read by the local certificate.

Open-fit exposure is the role of $F$. It imports finite fit, open continuation, and finite refutation only as a displayed sibling row, so the certificate cannot identify finite support with final confirmation.

Observer-invariance exposure is the role of $O$. It requires the invariant and anchor-change route before the physical truth claim can be public, so one observer history cannot stand in for the full packet.

Descent, stability, ledger, and failure exposure are the remaining content rows. The descent row $D$ licenses operations through the tower, $I$ names the reuse domain, $L$ records hidden residue and provenance, and $R$ names defeat conditions.

The transport, continuation, provenance, and naming rows $H,C,P,N$ then preserve exactly the displayed packet. Thus the local $\NameCert$ surface is exhausted by the listed obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Physical truth certificate open-fit non-escape]
\label{thm:physical-truth-certificate-open-fit-nonescape}
Every consumer of an accepted $\PhysicalTruthCertificateUp$ packet must read physical truth through the displayed source row, open-fit row, observer-invariance row, descent row, stability row, ledger row, failure-surface row, transport row, route row, provenance row, and local name. No consumer may replace that route by model--reality identity, a final-fit oracle, unledgered continuation, host equality, or a bare mathematical model row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:physical-truth-certificate-namecert-obligations}. They force every accepted route to expose $S,F,O,D,I,L,R,H,C,P,N$ before a physical truth claim is read.

The open-fit row $F$ is finite and defeasible. It supplies observed fit, continuation, and refutation rows, but it has no coordinate for final fit, model identity, or host equality.

The ledger and failure rows $L$ and $R$ close the remaining escape routes. A consumer that omits them loses the record of compression, idealization, hidden residue, and possible defeat, so it no longer reads the accepted packet.

Therefore a physical truth read can only be the displayed route through the full certificate carrier. The excluded identity, finality, unledgered, host-equality, and bare-model routes have no row from which to be exported.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Physical truth certificate observer invariance]
\label{thm:physical-truth-certificate-observer-invariance}
Let $T=(S,F,O,D,I,L,R,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\PhysicalTruthCertificateUp$ carrier. If a consumer exports a physical truth claim from $T$, then the claim is read through the observer-invariance row $O$ and the componentwise transport row $H$ before the route $C$ and local name $N$ are exposed. A claim that depends on one observer history without the displayed $O,H,C,N$ route is not a $\PhysicalTruthCertificateUp$ export.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.PhysicalTruthCertificateUp.physicalTruthCertificate\_observer\_route\_fields}
\begin{proof}

Now read the route and naming rows. The continuation route $C$ and local name $N$ can replay and name only the rows already displayed by the carrier; they cannot create observer invariance after the fact.

If a consumer depends on one observer history alone, then it omits the invariant packet and anchor-change discipline carried by $O$. The route may still be local evidence, open fit, or model audit material, but it is not an export of the physical truth certificate.
\end{proof}
