\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\SubjectFreeObservationUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-subject-free-observation-namecert}
\origin{ai}

The \SubjectFreeObservationUp packet realizes the subject-free observation discipline of \autoref{sec:visions-philosophy-observation-subject-time} as a finite NameCert surface. It consumes the observer-history identity packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observerhistoryidentity-namecert}, the observer-filter split of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observer-filter-namecert}, the kernel observation sieve of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-kernelobservationsieve-namecert}, and the local clock budget of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-localclockbudget-namecert}. The packet records an observation as displayed $\Cont$ replay, treats observer identity as $\hsame$ over history rows, and exposes the absence of any global subject parameter as a ledgered exclusion.

\begin{definition}[Subject-free observation carrier]
\label{def:subject-free-observation-carrier}
A \SubjectFreeObservationUp carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(O,H,K,D,L,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $O$ is the displayed $\Cont$ observation row, recording the observation event as a continuation over the current history surface;
\item $H$ is the $\hsame$ observer-history identity row certified by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observerhistoryidentity-namecert};
\item $K$ is the subject-free signature row, recording that the observation is typed by its $\BHist$, $\Cont$, and $\hsame$ rows rather than by an auxiliary subject carrier;
\item $D$ is the no-subject exclusion row, refusing a subject-substance coordinate, owner map, global observer parameter, or private viewpoint object outside the displayed history rows;
\item $L$ is the public ledger-event row, exposing the accepted observation event to downstream consumers as a named finite record;
\item $C$ records $\Cont$ replay for observation readback, signature inspection, no-subject checking, and ledger-event exposure;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\mathsf{ObserverHistoryIdentity}^{\uparrow}$, $\ObserverFilterUp$, $\mathsf{KernelObservationSieve}^{\uparrow}$, $\LocalClockBudgetUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\SubjectFreeObservationUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares carriers only through $O,H,K,D,L,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for a subject-substance, host observer identity, owner function, global viewpoint, or observation source outside the displayed $\BHist$, $\Cont$, and $\hsame$ rows.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Subject-free observation NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:subject-free-observation-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:subject-free-observation-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\SubjectFreeObservationUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $S=(O,H,K,D,L,C,P,N)$, displayed $\Cont$ observation through $O$, observer-history identity through $H$, subject-free signature exposure through $K$, no-subject exclusion through $D$, public ledger-event exposure through $L$, replay through $C$, provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier. The tuple lists the observation row, observer-history identity row, subject-free signature row, no-subject exclusion row, public ledger-event row, replay row, provenance row, and local naming row.

Read the observation and identity rows first. The observation is the displayed $\Cont$ row $O$, while observer identity is supplied by the $\hsame$ row $H$ from the observer-history identity surface.

The rows $K$ and $D$ then separate admissible signature data from excluded subject data. The signature is built from the displayed history, continuation, and sameness rows, and the exclusion row records that no subject-substance coordinate is available.

Finally $L,C,P,N$ expose, replay, source, and name the same packet. Since the carrier contains no hidden subject coordinate, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Subject-free observation no-subject parameter]
\label{thm:subject-free-observation-no-subject-parameter}
If a purported observation packet requires a subject-substance coordinate outside the displayed $\BHist$, $\Cont$, and $\hsame$ rows, then it is not an accepted \SubjectFreeObservationUp carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect \autoref{def:subject-free-observation-carrier}. The only observation source is $O$, the only observer-identity row is $H$, and the only signature row is $K$.

The no-subject row $D$ records the forbidden coordinates before any public ledger event is exposed. A subject-substance coordinate outside the displayed rows would have to be read either from $K$ or from $L$.

Both routes are blocked by the carrier inventory. The signature row records only the subject-free observation type, and the ledger row exposes only the accepted finite event, so a packet requiring an auxiliary subject parameter falls outside the classifier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Subject-free observation ledger event]
\label{thm:subject-free-observation-ledger-event}
Every public event exported by an accepted \SubjectFreeObservationUp packet factors through
$$
O,H,K,D,L,C,P,N.
$$
The event is a displayed $\Cont$ observation with $\hsame$ observer-history identity and a no-subject ledger, not a host-side subject report.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the accepted packet $S=(O,H,K,D,L,C,P,N)$. The public event row is $L$, and it is read only after the observation row $O$, observer-history row $H$, signature row $K$, and exclusion row $D$ have been displayed.

Replay through $C$ preserves this order for consumers. Provenance through $P$ names the observer-history, observer-filter, kernel-sieve, and local-clock-budget source rows, while $N$ names the local certificate.

No additional report channel appears in the packet. Thus the exported event is exactly the finite ledgered observation route and cannot be interpreted as a host-side subject report.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Subject-free observation sibling independence]
\label{thm:subject-free-observation-sibling-independence}
The \SubjectFreeObservationUp carrier is not bijective, under NameCert-row preservation, to $\mathsf{ObserverHistoryIdentity}^{\uparrow}$, $\ObserverFilterUp$, $\mathsf{KernelObservationSieve}^{\uparrow}$, $\LocalClockBudgetUp$, or $\mathsf{ConsciousObserverState}^{\uparrow}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Compare the sibling surfaces. $\mathsf{ObserverHistoryIdentity}^{\uparrow}$ supplies $\hsame$ observer-history identity, $\ObserverFilterUp$ separates selected and omitted rows, $\mathsf{KernelObservationSieve}^{\uparrow}$ filters kernel-facing observations, $\LocalClockBudgetUp$ budgets local observation windows, and $\mathsf{ConsciousObserverState}^{\uparrow}$ records current observer-state rows.

None of those siblings carries the full tuple $O,H,K,D,L,C,P,N$ with both the displayed $\Cont$ observation row and the no-subject exclusion ledger. A NameCert-row-preserving bijection would have to preserve those two rows together with the public ledger-event row.

Because the siblings each supply only a proper dependency surface, $\SubjectFreeObservationUp$ remains a distinct finite carrier for subject-free observation packets.
\end{proof}
