\begin{definition}[Finite sequence peak carrier]
\label{def:finite-sequence-peak-carrier}
A $\FiniteSequencePeakUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
P=(L,O,D,W,R,E,H,C,G,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $L$ is the $\ListUp$ row naming the finite sequence entries and their displayed length.
\item $O$ is the $\TotalOrderUp$ comparison row used to compare only entries already present in $L$.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ budget row carrying the finite comparison and error tolerances.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ bounded-window row that records the inspected segment supplied to L10 consumers.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row that turns the bounded window and dyadic budgets into a regular rational observation of the selected peak.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row, exposed only after $L,O,D,W,R$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $L,O,D,W,R,E$ and for the structural rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a bounded-window request through
$$
L,O,D,W \longmapsto R \longmapsto E .
$$
\item $G$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\FiniteSequencePeakUp$, $\ListUp$, $\TotalOrderUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteSequencePeakUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $L,O,D,W,R,E,H,C,G,N$. The carrier has no coordinate for an infinite subsequence selector, sequential compactness theorem, host maximum operator, quotient stream representative, selected real limit, or ambient completeness witness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Finite sequence peak NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:finite-sequence-peak-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\FiniteSequencePeakUp$ carrier $P=(L,O,D,W,R,E,H,C,G,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteSequencePeakUp}$ surface consists exactly of finite list admission through $L$, total-order comparison through $O$, dyadic budget accounting through $D$, bounded-window exposure through $W$, regular rational readback through $R$, terminal $\RealUp$ seal handoff through $E$, componentwise transport through $H$, replay through $C$, provenance through $G$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:finite-sequence-peak-carrier}. The analytic rows are exactly the finite list row, comparison row, dyadic budget row, bounded-window row, regular rational readback, and Real seal.

The selector route is finite. The consumer first reads the entries named by $L$, compares only those entries through $O$, and spends only the dyadic tolerances recorded in $D$ before a bounded window is available.

The bounded-window row $W$ is the only source for the regular rational readback $R$. Thus the Real-facing endpoint $E$ is downstream of $\ListUp$, $\TotalOrderUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, and $\RegSeqRatUp$ data.

Finally $H,C,G,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite route. Since the carrier contains no coordinate for sequential compactness, countable choice, host extrema, quotient streams, selected limits, ambient completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\label{thm:finite-sequence-peak-regseqrat-selector-handoff}

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

Read the dyadic and window rows next. The budget row $D$ records the finite comparison tolerances, while $W$ records the bounded segment consumed by the L10 route.
