\begin{theorem}[Prod product carrier sibling dependency]
\label{thm:prod-orphan-product-carrier-dependency}
Every public $\ProdUp$ product-carrier read depends on the finite-data sibling route that places Bool endpoint discrimination from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bool-namecert}, Sum branch exposure from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-sum-namecert}, and List spine readback from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-list-namecert} around the componentwise product envelope. The product packet then exports only ordered component carriers, component ledgers, $\hsame$ transport, $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ rows; it does not become an isolated host pair or a detached finite-data object.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the existing $\ProdUp$ componentwise carrier. Its public read exposes a left component, a right component, the component ledgers, and the structural rows that transport and replay those components.

The sibling finite-data route supplies the surrounding classifiers. Bool fixes endpoint discrimination, Sum supplies tagged branching, and List supplies spine readback for repeated finite data. These rows are dependencies of downstream product consumers, but they do not replace the product envelope.

Thus every product-carrier exit remains componentwise. The exported object is the same $\ProdUp$ carrier with sibling finite-data dependencies recorded in the ledger, not an isolated host pair.
\end{proof}
