\begin{theorem}[Prod public finite-data exit package]
\label{thm:prod-public-finite-data-exit-package}
Let $L,R:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be BEDC source-history carriers, let
$\sim_L,\sim_R:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be their
source classifiers, and suppose
$\mathsf{cert}_L:\NameCert(L,\sim_L)$ and
$\mathsf{cert}_R:\NameCert(R,\sim_R)$ are the source certificate rows. Assume
also a witness of
$\mathsf{ProdPairRepCoherent}_{L,R,\sim_L,\sim_R}$. Then the public product
finite-data exit package consists of the following rows.
\begin{enumerate}
  \item The componentwise classifier carries the semantic certificate
  $$
  \mathsf{SemanticNameCert}(\mathsf{ProdHistoryCarrier}(L,R),
  \mathsf{ProdHistoryCarrier}(L,R),
  \mathsf{ProdHistoryCarrier}(L,R),
  \mathsf{ProdComponentHistoryClassifier}_{L,R,\sim_L,\sim_R}).
  $$
  \item For any displayed componentwise classifier witness between histories
  $h,k:\mathsf{BHist}$, the left and right displayed-pair ledger endpoints are
  readable as
  $$
  \mathsf{ProdDisplayedPairLedger}_{L,R}(h)
  \land
  \mathsf{ProdDisplayedPairLedger}_{L,R}(k).
  $$
  \item For the same histories $h,k:\mathsf{BHist}$, every componentwise
  classifier witness descends to the checked product history envelope
  classifier
  $$
  \mathsf{ProdHistoryClassifier}(L,R,h,k).
  $$
  \item The obligation surface exposes exactly the source $\NameCert$ rows,
  the componentwise classifier row, the displayed-pair ledger row, the descent
  row, the $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ transport rows, and the $\mathsf{Pkg}$
  provenance rows named by \autoref{thm:produp-obligation-surface-package}.
\end{enumerate}
Consequently the public product object reads only $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$,
$\Cont$, the displayed-pair ledger, the source certificates, and
$\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance rows.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ProdUp.ProdPublicFiniteDataExitPackage}
\begin{proof}
First apply
\autoref{thm:prod-component-history-classifier-semantic-name-certificate} to
the two source certificates and the
$\mathsf{ProdPairRepCoherent}_{L,R,\sim_L,\sim_R}$ witness. This supplies the
componentwise semantic certificate row.

Next fix histories $h,k:\mathsf{BHist}$ and a componentwise classifier
witness between them. Apply
\autoref{thm:prod-component-history-classifier-displayed-ledger-endpoints} to
read both displayed-pair ledger endpoints.

Then apply
\autoref{thm:prod-component-history-classifier-descends-to-envelope} with the
same componentwise witness and the source soundness rows. This is the descent
row from the componentwise classifier into the checked product history
envelope.

Finally read the obligation rows from
\autoref{thm:produp-obligation-surface-package}. The four rows above mention
no host tuple equality and no data beyond the listed $\mathsf{BHist}$ carrier,
$\hsame$, $\Cont$, displayed-pair ledger, source certificate rows, and
$\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance, so they repackage exactly the advertised public
finite-data product surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Product finite-data public exit consumer universality]
\label{thm:prod-finite-data-public-exit-consumer-universality}
Let $L,R:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be source-history carriers, let
$\sim_L,\sim_R:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be their
source classifiers, and let
$\mathsf{cert}_L:\NameCert(L,\sim_L)$ and
$\mathsf{cert}_R:\NameCert(R,\sim_R)$ be the source certificate rows. Assume a
displayed witness of
$\mathsf{ProdPairRepCoherent}_{L,R,\sim_L,\sim_R}$.

Every public finite-data consumer of a product history whose reads are
invariant under the componentwise semantic certificate, the displayed-pair
ledger endpoints, the descent-to-envelope row, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and
$\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance factors through the package displayed in
\autoref{thm:prod-public-finite-data-exit-package}. The factorization reads
only the package rows: source $\NameCert$ rows, the componentwise classifier
certificate, displayed-pair ledger endpoints, descent to the product history
envelope, $\hsame$ transport, $\Cont$ continuation, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$
provenance.

No step of the factorization exposes host tuple equality, outside-ledger
component projections, or a quotient of pair representatives.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:prod-public-finite-data-exit-package}. It displays the
candidate rows available to any public finite-data read of the product
history: the semantic componentwise certificate, displayed-pair ledger
endpoints, descent to the envelope classifier, source certificate rows,
$\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance.

Next apply \autoref{thm:prod-finite-data-consumer-row-exhaustion}. The
consumer invariance assumptions force every public read to be one of those
displayed rows; there is no separate host tuple row and no projection outside
the displayed product ledger.

It remains to identify endpoint reads. Use
\autoref{thm:prod-component-ledger-endpoint-determinacy}. Any endpoint datum
read by the consumer is already the displayed-pair ledger endpoint supplied by
the public package, so endpoint access cannot introduce a second product
representation or a quotient of pair representatives.

Thus all public consumer reads are exhausted by the rows of
\autoref{thm:prod-public-finite-data-exit-package}, and the consumer factors
through that package.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Finite-data product, sum, and list envelope separation]
\label{thm:finite-data-product-sum-list-envelope-separation}
Any public finite-data consumer spanning $\ProdUp$, $\SumUp$, and $\ListUp$
must preserve three distinct envelope rows. The product route reads through the
displayed-pair ledger and the componentwise classifier of
\autoref{thm:prod-public-finite-data-exit-package}. The sum route reads through
the visible tag and branch classifier of
\autoref{thm:sum-history-classifier-tag-separation}. The list route reads
through the spine bridge and prefix classifier of
\autoref{thm:list-represented-spine-classifier-displayed-exactness}.

Consequently no public consumer may replace these rows by host tuple equality,
a generic finite-data quotient, or a common untagged carrier. Such a
replacement would erase the ledger/classifier boundary that distinguishes pair
display, tag display, and spine display.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the product public exit package and its consumer universality. By
\autoref{thm:prod-public-finite-data-exit-package} and
\autoref{thm:prod-finite-data-public-exit-consumer-universality}, every public
product read factors through source certificate rows, the displayed-pair
ledger, the componentwise classifier, descent to the product history envelope,
$\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance.

For sums, use the checked tag-separation surface. The theorem
\autoref{thm:sum-history-classifier-tag-separation} forces mixed visible tags
to be impossible, while the same-tag stability rows keep each accepted read
inside the branch classifier attached to its tag.

For lists, use the spine bridge exactness theorem. The displayed spine row and
prefix classifier identify the accepted list route through the spine bridge,
not through a detached host sequence or an untagged finite-data carrier.

Inspecting the three rows gives different ledger/classifier boundaries:
products expose pair display plus componentwise classification, sums expose tag
display plus branch classification, and lists expose spine display plus prefix
classification. A single host tuple equality, generic quotient, or untagged
carrier would identify these boundaries, so it is not a public finite-data
consumer for the three constructors at once.
\end{proof}
