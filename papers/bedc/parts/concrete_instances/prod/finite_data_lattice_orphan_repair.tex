\begin{theorem}[Prod finite-data dependency lattice]
\label{thm:prod-finite-data-lattice-orphan-repair}
Every public $\ProdUp$ finite-data read is positioned between the endpoint discrimination of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bool-namecert}, the partiality surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-option-namecert}, the tagged branching surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-sum-namecert}, and the spine-iteration surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-list-namecert}. The product packet exports only the ordered-pair envelope after those sibling routes have supplied the finite-data lattice, and it keeps payload carriers, branch tags, list spines, component ledgers, $\hsame$ transport, $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ rows inside the existing $\ProdUp$ envelope.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the componentwise product surface. The public product read exposes left and right component projections through the displayed product history carrier, the componentwise classifier, the displayed-pair ledger, and source $\NameCert$ rows.

The sibling lattice supplies the finite-data routes around that envelope. Bool supplies endpoint discrimination, Option supplies possible absence, Sum supplies tagged branching, and List supplies spine iteration; none of these rows replaces the product component ledgers or turns them into host-level tuple equality.

Finally apply the finite-data readiness rows already recorded in the product chapter. They route every public finite-data exit through componentwise projection, $\hsame$ transport, $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local product naming row, so the read cannot escape the existing $\ProdUp$ envelope.
\end{proof}
