\label{thm:prod-component-history-classifier-inversion}

$\sim_L,\sim_R:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be their source

displayed source transitivity laws
$$
\forall a\,b\,c.\;a\sim_L b\to b\sim_L c\to a\sim_L c
\qquad\hbox{and}\qquad
\forall a\,b\,c.\;a\sim_R b\to b\sim_R c\to a\sim_R c.
$$
If
$\mathsf{ProdPairRep}_{L,R}(h,l,r)$,
$\mathsf{ProdPairRep}_{L,R}(k,l',r')$, and
$\mathsf{ProdComponentHistoryClassifier}_{L,R,\sim_L,\sim_R}(h,k)$ hold, then
$l\sim_L l'$ and $r\sim_R r'$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the componentwise classifier witness to obtain component histories
$l_h,r_h,l_k,r_k$ with pair representations for $h$ and $k$, together with
$l_h\sim_L l_k$ and $r_h\sim_R r_k$. Apply pair-representation coherence at
$h$ to the displayed representation $(l,r)$ and the witness representation
$(l_h,r_h)$, yielding $l\sim_L l_h$ and $r\sim_R r_h$. Apply coherence at
$k$ to the witness representation $(l_k,r_k)$ and the displayed
representation $(l',r')$, yielding $l_k\sim_L l'$ and $r_k\sim_R r'$. The
displayed source transitivity hypotheses compose the left chain
$l\sim_L l_h\sim_L l_k\sim_L l'$ and the right chain

$\mathsf{ProdPairRep}_{L,R}(k,l_k,r_k)$, and component links
$l_h\sim_L l_k$ and $r_h\sim_R r_k$. The two $\hsame$-sound assumptions turn
the component links into $\hsame(l_h,l_k)$ and $\hsame(r_h,r_k)$. The pair
representations expose $\Cont(l_h,r_h,h)$ and $\Cont(l_k,r_k,k)$, so
