\begin{definition}[Product pair-representation coherence]
\label{def:prod-pair-rep-coherent}
For source classifiers $\sim_L$ and $\sim_R$, define
$\mathsf{ProdPairRepCoherent}_{L,R,\sim_L,\sim_R}$ to mean that any two pair
representations of the same product history classify their corresponding
components:
$$
  \forall h\,l\,r\,l'\,r'.\;
  \mathsf{ProdPairRep}_{L,R}(h,l,r)\wedge
  \mathsf{ProdPairRep}_{L,R}(h,l',r')
  \to (l\sim_L l')\wedge(r\sim_R r').
$$
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Product e0 unit coherence obstruction]
\label{thm:prod-pair-rep-e0-unit-coherence-absurd}
Let $L$ and $R$ be source-history predicates on $\mathsf{BHist}$. If $L(\emp)$,
$L(\Ezero(\emp))$, $R(\emp)$, and $R(\Ezero(\emp))$ all hold, then
$\mathsf{ProdPairRepCoherent}_{L,R,\hsame,\hsame}$ is impossible.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The endpoint $\Ezero(\emp)$ has two displayed pair representations. The
left-unit presentation uses components $(\emp,\Ezero(\emp))$ and the
continuation law $\Cont(\emp,\Ezero(\emp),\Ezero(\emp))$. The right-unit
presentation uses components $(\Ezero(\emp),\emp)$ and the continuation law
$\Cont(\Ezero(\emp),\emp,\Ezero(\emp))$. Coherence for the two $\hsame$
classifiers would force $\hsame(\emp,\Ezero(\emp))$ on the left components,
contradicting BEDC history no-confusion.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Product raw pair representation coherence is not automatic]
\label{thm:prod-pair-rep-coherence-not-automatic}
Let $\mathsf{TopCarrier}(h):=\mathsf{True}$ for every $h:\mathsf{BHist}$, and take both source classifiers to be $\hsame$. Then $\neg\mathsf{ProdPairRepCoherent}_{\mathsf{TopCarrier},\mathsf{TopCarrier},\hsame,\hsame}$ holds.

The per-object fit is: traditional target ordered-pair readback; BEDC source $\mathsf{BHist}$ with $\Cont$ and $\hsame$; carrier $\mathsf{ProdPairRep}$; classifier $\hsame$; stability obligation coherence; ledger displayed pair boundary; core theorem non-automaticity; exactness obstruction; standard bridge raw append ambiguity; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The endpoint $\Ezero(\emp)$ has two raw pair presentations under $\mathsf{TopCarrier}$. The left-unit presentation uses components $(\emp,\Ezero(\emp))$, and the right-unit presentation uses components $(\Ezero(\emp),\emp)$. If product pair-representation coherence were automatic for the two $\hsame$ classifiers, applying it to these two presentations of the same endpoint would force $\hsame(\emp,\Ezero(\emp))$ on the left components, and also $\hsame(\Ezero(\emp),\emp)$ on the right components. BEDC history no-confusion separates the empty history from a zero-step endpoint, so the forced $\hsame(\emp,\Ezero(\emp))$ is impossible. Thus the componentwise ordered-pair certificate needs displayed coherence data and cannot be recovered from raw continuation alone.
\end{proof}
