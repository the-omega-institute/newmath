\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\SequentialCompletenessCriterionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-sequential-completeness-criterion-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop--Bridges and Kelley, the $\SequentialCompletenessCriterionUp$ packet records the finite criterion by which sequence completion evidence is read as real sequential completeness data. It sits after the sequence-completion surfaces of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-sequential-completion-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-sequence-completion-namecert}, reads the real-facing seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-sequential-completeness-namecert}, and keeps the metric criterion aligned with \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-sequentiallycompletemetric-namecert}.

\begin{definition}[SequentialCompletenessCriterion carrier]
\label{def:sequential-completeness-criterion-carrier}
A $\SequentialCompletenessCriterionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(S,C,R,M,W,G,E,H,T,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the $\SequentialCompletionUp$ row naming the displayed sequence-completion interface consumed by the criterion.
\item $C$ is the $\CauchySequenceCompletionUp$ row naming the regular Cauchy sequence completion route available to the same finite packet.
\item $R$ is the $\RealSequentialCompletenessUp$ row, exposed only after the sequence-completion and Cauchy-sequence completion rows have been displayed.
\item $M$ is the $\SequentiallyCompleteMetricUp$ row, recording the metric criterion surface through which later metric consumers read sequential completeness.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row for the sequence observations inspected by the criterion.
\item $G$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row attached to the displayed stream windows and Cauchy schedules.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached from the regular readback and real sequential completeness rows.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $S,C,R,M,W,G,E$.
\item $T$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from sequence windows through Cauchy completion, metric criterion data, regular readback, and the real seal.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\SequentialCompletenessCriterionUp$, $\SequentialCompletionUp$, $\CauchySequenceCompletionUp$, $\RealSequentialCompletenessUp$, $\SequentiallyCompleteMetricUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\SequentialCompletenessCriterionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate asserts exactly that these rows form one finite criterion packet. It has no coordinate for quotient completion, countable choice, host equality of sequences or completion points, open-cover compactness, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[SequentialCompletenessCriterion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:sequential-completeness-criterion-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\SequentialCompletenessCriterionUp$ carrier $Q=(S,C,R,M,W,G,E,H,T,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\SequentialCompletenessCriterionUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the sequence-completion row, Cauchy-sequence completion row, real sequential-completeness row, sequentially complete metric row, stream-window row, regular readback row, real seal row, componentwise transport, displayed replay, provenance, and local naming. Any real sequential-completeness consumer must read
$$
S,C,W,G,R,M,E,H,T,P,N
$$
before it can export a criterion result, and the export contains no quotient completion, countable choice, host equality, open-cover compactness, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:sequential-completeness-criterion-carrier}. The rows $S,C,R,M,W,G,E,H,T,P,N$ are the only rows available to a consumer of the local name.

The sequence-facing part of the criterion is supplied by $S$ and $C$. The finite observation schedule is supplied by $W$, and the regular rational readback is supplied by $G$. Thus the criterion never begins from a host sequence or from a quotient completion object.

The real-facing part is then forced through $R,M,E$. The real sequential-completeness row and the sequentially complete metric row name the criterion surface, while the $\RealUp$ seal can be read only after the displayed regular readback route has been consumed.

Finally $H,T,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite route. Since the packet contains no row for countable choice, host equality, open-cover compactness, quotient completion, or ambient real completeness, the listed rows exhaust the local $\NameCert$ obligations.
\end{proof}
