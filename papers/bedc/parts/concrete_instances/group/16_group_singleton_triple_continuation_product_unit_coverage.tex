\begin{theorem}[Group singleton triple continuation product-unit coverage and visibility]
\label{thm:group-singleton-triple-continuation-product-unit-coverage-and-visibility}
For histories $P,Q,S,T,U,V,W,X,r:\mathsf{BHist}$ with $\ContR(P,Q,S)$,
$\ContR(S,T,U)$, and $\ContR(U,V,W)$, the concrete singleton $\GroupUp$
instance satisfies
$$
R_{\emp}(\mathsf{append}(W,X),\emp)\Longleftrightarrow
A_{\emp}(P)\land A_{\emp}(Q)\land A_{\emp}(T)\land A_{\emp}(V)\land
A_{\emp}(X).
$$
Under the left-hand classifier, all three continuation witnesses exclude
visible results:
$$
\bigl(\ContR(P,Q,\Ezero(r))\Rightarrow\mathsf{False}\bigr)\land
\bigl(\ContR(P,Q,\Eone(r))\Rightarrow\mathsf{False}\bigr)\land
\bigl(\ContR(S,T,\Ezero(r))\Rightarrow\mathsf{False}\bigr)\land
\bigl(\ContR(S,T,\Eone(r))\Rightarrow\mathsf{False}\bigr)\land
\bigl(\ContR(U,V,\Ezero(r))\Rightarrow\mathsf{False}\bigr)\land
\bigl(\ContR(U,V,\Eone(r))\Rightarrow\mathsf{False}\bigr).
$$
The ten-field fit names the traditional target terminal-group three-step
continuation product-unit coverage with visibility guard; BEDC source concrete
singleton $\GroupUp$ over $\mathsf{BHist}$ plus three $\ContR$ witnesses and
append; carrier $A_{\emp}$; classifier $R_{\emp}$; stability from product-unit
continuation coverage, terminal collapse, and terminal classifier packaging;
ledger the three continuation witnesses, suffix endpoint, and visible result
endpoint; exactness the displayed iff and six contradictions; downstream
consumer $\QuotientGroupUp$ path compression; and status
$\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:group-singleton-continuation-product-unit-triple-coverage}
to the third continuation witness $\ContR(U,V,W)$. This rewrites the left-hand
classifier as
$$
A_{\emp}(U)\land A_{\emp}(V)\land A_{\emp}(X).
$$

Use \autoref{thm:group-singleton-continuation-terminal-collapse} under
$\ContR(S,T,U)$ to identify $A_{\emp}(U)$ with $R_{\emp}(S,T)$. Then apply the
first row of \autoref{thm:group-singleton-terminal-classifier-package} to
rewrite that classifier as $A_{\emp}(S)\land A_{\emp}(T)$. The condition is
now
$$
A_{\emp}(S)\land A_{\emp}(T)\land A_{\emp}(V)\land A_{\emp}(X).
$$

Apply \autoref{thm:group-singleton-continuation-terminal-collapse} again, this
time under $\ContR(P,Q,S)$, to identify $A_{\emp}(S)$ with $R_{\emp}(P,Q)$.
The first row of \autoref{thm:group-singleton-terminal-classifier-package}
replaces $R_{\emp}(P,Q)$ by $A_{\emp}(P)\land A_{\emp}(Q)$, yielding the
displayed five-factor classifier.

For visibility, assume the left-hand classifier. The forward direction just
constructed supplies the carrier factors for $P,Q,T,V,X$, and the two terminal
collapse steps also supply the hidden endpoint carriers $A_{\emp}(S)$ and
$A_{\emp}(U)$.

Repack $A_{\emp}(P)\land A_{\emp}(Q)$, $A_{\emp}(S)\land A_{\emp}(T)$, and
$A_{\emp}(U)\land A_{\emp}(V)$ into the adjacent endpoint classifiers
$R_{\emp}(P,Q)$, $R_{\emp}(S,T)$, and $R_{\emp}(U,V)$ by the reverse direction
of the terminal classifier package.

Apply the visible-result clause of
\autoref{thm:group-singleton-continuation-terminal-collapse} at each boundary.
The three repacked classifiers respectively rule out visible results for
$\ContR(P,Q,-)$, $\ContR(S,T,-)$, and $\ContR(U,V,-)$, which gives the six
displayed implications.
\end{proof}
