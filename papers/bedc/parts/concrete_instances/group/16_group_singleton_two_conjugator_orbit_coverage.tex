\section{Singleton two-conjugator orbit coverage}
\label{sec:group-singleton-two-conjugator-orbit-coverage}

\begin{theorem}[Group singleton two-conjugator orbit coverage]
\label{thm:group-singleton-two-conjugator-orbit-coverage}
For histories $s,t,x,y:\mathsf{BHist}$ in the concrete singleton history group,
define
$$
\mathsf{Conj}_u(z):=\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(u,z),\mathsf{inv}(u)).
$$
If $A_{\emp}(s)$ and $A_{\emp}(t)$, then
$$
\mathsf{Conj}_s(x)\sim_{N_{\emp}}\mathsf{Conj}_t(y)
\Longleftrightarrow A_{\emp}(x)\land A_{\emp}(y).
$$
The ten-field fit is: traditional target terminal two-conjugator orbit
coverage; BEDC source concrete singleton $\GroupUp$ over $\mathsf{BHist}$;
carrier $A_{\emp}$; classifier the terminal normalizer orbit relation;
stability from action carrier coverage plus total orbit coverage; ledger the
two conjugating endpoints and two acted endpoints; core theorem list
\autoref{thm:group-singleton-normalizer-action-certificate} and
\autoref{thm:group-singleton-normalizer-orbit-total-coverage}; exactness the
displayed iff; downstream consumer $\QuotientGroupUp$; status
$\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use \autoref{thm:group-singleton-normalizer-action-certificate} with $s$ and
$x$, and again with $t$ and $y$. In the singleton carrier, the action carrier
coverage identifies $A_{\emp}(\mathsf{Conj}_s(x))$ with $A_{\emp}(x)$ and
$A_{\emp}(\mathsf{Conj}_t(y))$ with $A_{\emp}(y)$.

Apply \autoref{thm:group-singleton-normalizer-orbit-total-coverage} to the pair
$(\mathsf{Conj}_s(x),\mathsf{Conj}_t(y))$. It converts the orbit statement into
the endpoint carrier conjunction
$$
A_{\emp}(\mathsf{Conj}_s(x))\land
A_{\emp}(\mathsf{Conj}_t(y)).
$$

Substitute the two carrier identifications from the action coverage step. After
reassociating the conjunction, the right-hand side is exactly
$A_{\emp}(x)\land A_{\emp}(y)$.
\end{proof}
