\begin{theorem}[Group singleton inverse classifier fiber]
\label{thm:group-singleton-inverse-classifier-fiber}
For histories $x,y:\mathsf{BHist}$ in the concrete singleton history group of
Definition~\ref{def:concrete-singleton-history-group-instance}, the constant
singleton inverse has exactly the terminal classifier fiber:
$$
R_{\emp}(\mathsf{inv}(x),y)\Longleftrightarrow A_{\emp}(y).
$$
The ten-field fit is: traditional target terminal-group inverse image; BEDC
source concrete singleton $\GroupUp$ over $\mathsf{BHist}$; carrier $A_{\emp}$;
classifier $R_{\emp}$; stability from singleton classifier reflexivity,
symmetry, transitivity, and inverse closure; ledger the ignored inverse input
$x$ and the terminal endpoint $\emp$; core theorem this block plus
Theorem~\ref{thm:group-singleton-history-semantic-namecert}; exactness the
displayed iff; standard bridge the one-element group inverse fiber; status
$\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold the concrete singleton inverse from
Definition~\ref{def:concrete-singleton-history-group-instance}. For every
history $x$, $\mathsf{inv}(x)$ is definitionally $\emp$, so the displayed left
side is the classifier row $R_{\emp}(\emp,y)$.

For the forward direction, unpack $R_{\emp}(\emp,y)$. By definition of
$R_{\emp}$ this exposes three fields: $A_{\emp}(\emp)$, $A_{\emp}(y)$, and
$\hsame(\emp,y)$. Project the right carrier field to obtain $A_{\emp}(y)$.

For the reverse direction, assume $A_{\emp}(y)$. The singleton carrier contains
$\emp$ by the reflexive endpoint witness $\hsame(\emp,\emp)$. The assumed carrier
fact identifies $y$ with $\emp$; applying singleton symmetry gives the endpoint
witness $\hsame(\emp,y)$. Repack these three fields as $R_{\emp}(\emp,y)$, hence
as $R_{\emp}(\mathsf{inv}(x),y)$ after the inverse unfolds.
\end{proof}
