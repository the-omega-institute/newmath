\section{Singleton conjugation collapse}
\label{sec:group-singleton-conjugation-collapse}

\begin{theorem}[Group singleton conjugation fixed carrier]
\label{thm:group-singleton-conjugation-fixed-carrier}
For histories $a,x:\mathsf{BHist}$ in the concrete singleton history group of
Definition~\ref{def:concrete-singleton-history-group-instance}, if
$A_{\emp}(a)$, then
$$
R_{\emp}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a,x),\mathsf{inv}(a)),x)
\Longleftrightarrow A_{\emp}(x).
$$
The ten-field fit is: traditional target terminal-group inner action; BEDC
source concrete singleton $\GroupUp$ over $\mathsf{BHist}$; carrier
$A_{\emp}$; classifier $R_{\emp}$; stability from singleton multiplication
closure, inverse closure, and terminal classifier exactness; ledger the hidden
conjugating endpoint and constant inverse; core theorem list
\autoref{thm:group-singleton-history-semantic-namecert},
\autoref{thm:concrete-singleton-history-group-laws}, and
\autoref{thm:group-singleton-terminal-classifier-package}; exactness predicate
the displayed iff; standard bridge inner action of the terminal group; status
$\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the forward direction, apply the first row of
\autoref{thm:group-singleton-terminal-classifier-package} to the displayed
classifier. It rewrites the classifier as the pair consisting of the conjugation
word carrier fact and $A_{\emp}(x)$, so projecting the second component gives
the required carrier condition on $x$.

For the reverse direction, start from $A_{\emp}(a)$ and $A_{\emp}(x)$. The
multiplication-closure row of
\autoref{thm:group-singleton-terminal-classifier-package} places
$\mathsf{mul}(a,x)$ in $A_{\emp}$, and the inverse-closure row places
$\mathsf{inv}(a)$ in $A_{\emp}$.

Apply multiplication closure once more to put
$\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a,x),\mathsf{inv}(a))$ in the singleton carrier.
With this carrier fact and $A_{\emp}(x)$, the terminal classifier exactness row
of \autoref{thm:group-singleton-terminal-classifier-package} packages the
classifier
$$
R_{\emp}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a,x),\mathsf{inv}(a)),x).
$$
\end{proof}
