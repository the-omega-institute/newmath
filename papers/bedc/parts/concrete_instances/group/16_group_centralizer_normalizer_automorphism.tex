\section{Normalizer action on the centralizer}
\label{sec:group-normalizer-action-on-centralizer}

\begin{theorem}[Group centralizer normalizer conjugation automorphism]
\label{thm:group-centralizer-normalizer-conjugation-automorphism}
For a BEDC history-level group operation over $\mathsf{BHist}$ with the same
associativity, left and right identity laws for $e$, multiplication
congruence, inverse congruence, and two-sided inverse laws as in
\autoref{thm:group-centralizer-normalizer-subgroup}, let $Z_a$ be the
centralizer carrier of \autoref{def:group-centralizer-carrier}, let $N_a$ be
the normalizer carrier of \autoref{def:group-centralizer-normalizer-carrier},
and write
$$
\mathsf{Conj}_s(x):=
\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(s,x),\mathsf{inv}(s)).
$$
If $N_a(s)$ holds, then conjugation by $s$ restricts to $Z_a$ and is
classifier-bijective on that carrier:
\begin{enumerate}
\item If $Z_a(x)$, then $Z_a(\mathsf{Conj}_s(x))$.
\item For every $y:\mathsf{BHist}$ with $Z_a(y)$, the witness
$x:=\mathsf{Conj}_{\mathsf{inv}(s)}(y)$ satisfies $Z_a(x)$ and
$\hsame(\mathsf{Conj}_s(x),y)$.
\item For all $x,y:\mathsf{BHist}$ with $Z_a(x)$ and $Z_a(y)$,
$$
\hsame(\mathsf{Conj}_s(x),\mathsf{Conj}_s(y))
\Longleftrightarrow
\hsame(x,y).
$$
\end{enumerate}
The ten-field fit is: traditional target centralizer normalizer action; BEDC
source $\GroupUp$ over $\mathsf{BHist}$; carrier $Z_a$; classifier $\hsame$;
stability obligations normalizer preservation, inverse-conjugation witness,
and conjugation classifier exactness; ledger conjugating element, inverse
word, and centralizer membership witnesses; core theorem list
\autoref{thm:group-centralizer-normalizer-subgroup},
\autoref{thm:group-conjugation-classifier-exactness}, and
\autoref{thm:group-inverse-mul-reverse}; exactness predicate conjugation is a
classifier automorphism of $Z_a$; standard bridge ordinary normalizer action
on a subgroup; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The restriction row is the first clause of $N_a(s)$. Starting from
$Z_a(x)$, that clause returns $Z_a(\mathsf{Conj}_s(x))$ directly.

For the classifier-surjectivity row, let $y$ satisfy $Z_a(y)$ and set
$x:=\mathsf{Conj}_{\mathsf{inv}(s)}(y)$. The second clause of $N_a(s)$ gives
$Z_a(x)$ from $Z_a(y)$.

It remains to compare the returned element with $y$. Expanding the two
conjugation words gives a product whose outer factors are $s$ and
$\mathsf{inv}(s)$, with the inverse word
$\mathsf{inv}(\mathsf{inv}(s))$ in the middle. Associativity moves the
parentheses, the two-sided inverse laws reduce the adjacent inverse products
to $e$, and the unit laws reduce the remaining word to $y$. The inverse-word
calculation is the same group calculation recorded by
\autoref{thm:group-inverse-mul-reverse}.

For classifier exactness, apply
\autoref{thm:group-conjugation-classifier-exactness} to the conjugating word
$s$. Its preservation direction gives
$\hsame(x,y)\Rightarrow
\hsame(\mathsf{Conj}_s(x),\mathsf{Conj}_s(y))$, and its reflection direction
gives the converse implication. Restricting the inputs to $Z_a(x)$ and
$Z_a(y)$ therefore makes conjugation by $s$ a classifier automorphism of the
centralizer carrier.
\end{proof}
