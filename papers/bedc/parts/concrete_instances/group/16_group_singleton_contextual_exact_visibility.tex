\section{Singleton contextual exact visibility}
\label{sec:group-singleton-contextual-exact-visibility}

\begin{theorem}[Group singleton two-sided contextual continuation product-unit exact visibility]
\label{thm:group-singleton-two-sided-contextual-continuation-product-unit-exact-visibility}
For histories $L_0,R_0,L_1,R_1,P,Q,S,T,r:\mathsf{BHist}$, if
$A_{\emp}(L_0)$, $A_{\emp}(R_0)$, $A_{\emp}(L_1)$, $A_{\emp}(R_1)$, and
$\ContR(P,Q,S)$, then
$$
\begin{array}{c}
R_{\emp}(\mathsf{append}(\mathsf{append}(L_0,\mathsf{append}(S,T)),L_1),
\mathsf{append}(\mathsf{append}(R_0,\emp),R_1))\\
\Longleftrightarrow\\
\bigl(A_{\emp}(P)\land A_{\emp}(Q)\land A_{\emp}(T)\bigr)\land
\bigl(\ContR(P,Q,\Ezero(r))\Rightarrow\mathsf{False}\bigr)\land
\bigl(\ContR(P,Q,\Eone(r))\Rightarrow\mathsf{False}\bigr).
\end{array}
$$
The ten-field fit is: traditional target terminal-group contextual
product-unit exact visibility; BEDC source concrete singleton $\GroupUp$ over
$\mathsf{BHist}$ plus $\ContR$ and visible continuation endpoints; carrier
$A_{\emp}$; classifier $R_{\emp}$; stability from contextual product-unit
coverage and visible continuation exclusion; ledger the two context pairs,
continuation witness, suffix factor, and endpoint seed $r$; core theorem list
\autoref{thm:group-singleton-two-sided-contextual-continuation-product-unit-coverage}
and
\autoref{thm:group-singleton-two-sided-contextual-continuation-product-unit-visible-exclusion};
exactness the displayed iff package; downstream consumer concrete
$\GroupUp$ visibility classification; and status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the forward direction, assume the displayed contextual classifier. Apply
\autoref{thm:group-singleton-two-sided-contextual-continuation-product-unit-coverage}
to the four context carrier witnesses and $\ContR(P,Q,S)$. Its forward
direction gives the carrier triple
$A_{\emp}(P)\land A_{\emp}(Q)\land A_{\emp}(T)$.

Apply
\autoref{thm:group-singleton-two-sided-contextual-continuation-product-unit-visible-exclusion}
to the same context data, the same continuation witness, and the displayed
classifier. This supplies the two visible endpoint exclusions for
$\Ezero(r)$ and $\Eone(r)$. Conjoining the carrier triple with these two
exclusions gives the right-hand package.

For the reverse direction, assume the right-hand package. Its first component
is the carrier triple $A_{\emp}(P)\land A_{\emp}(Q)\land A_{\emp}(T)$. Apply
the reverse direction of
\autoref{thm:group-singleton-two-sided-contextual-continuation-product-unit-coverage}
with the four context carrier witnesses and $\ContR(P,Q,S)$ to reconstruct
the displayed contextual classifier.

The two exclusion clauses remain part of the assumed package. They are not
needed for the classifier reconstruction, but their presence is exactly what
makes the right-hand side an exact visible-classification package rather than
only the carrier coverage statement.
\end{proof}
