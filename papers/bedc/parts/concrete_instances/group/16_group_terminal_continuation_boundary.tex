\section{Terminal continuation boundary}
\label{sec:group-terminal-continuation-boundary}

\begin{definition}[Group terminal continuation boundary]
\label{def:group-terminal-continuation-boundary}
The terminal continuation boundary for the concrete singleton $\GroupUp$
instance is the $\mathsf{BHist}$ surface with carrier $A_{\emp}$, classifier
$R_{\emp}$, multiplication $\mathsf{mul}(h,k)=\mathsf{append}(h,k)$, unit
$e=\emp$, and inverse $\mathsf{inv}(h)=\emp$. Its continuation row is read
only through $\ContR(P,Q,S)$ together with the terminal collapse
$R_{\emp}(P,Q)\Longleftrightarrow A_{\emp}(S)$ supplied by
\autoref{thm:group-singleton-continuation-terminal-collapse}.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Group terminal continuation boundary laws]
\label{thm:group-terminal-continuation-boundary-laws}
On the boundary of Definition~\ref{def:group-terminal-continuation-boundary},
the following laws are available to $\GroupUp$ consumers:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A_{\emp}$ is inhabited and closed under $\mathsf{append}$.
\item $e=\emp$ is a left and right unit for $\mathsf{append}$ on $A_{\emp}$.
\item $\mathsf{inv}(h)=\emp$ is closed on $A_{\emp}$ and supplies both inverse
classifier rows against $e$.
\item $R_{\emp}$ is stable under $\hsame$ transport and under continuation
readback through $\ContR$.
\end{enumerate}
The ledger rows are exactly the singleton carrier, terminal classifier,
continuation witness, append multiplication, empty unit, and constant inverse
rows already present in the concrete singleton $\GroupUp$ instance.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier, unit, inverse, associativity, multiplication-congruence, and
classifier-stability rows are the concrete rows of
\autoref{thm:concrete-singleton-history-group-laws}.

Continuation readback is supplied by
\autoref{thm:group-singleton-continuation-terminal-collapse}: under a witness
$\ContR(P,Q,S)$, the input classifier $R_{\emp}(P,Q)$, the result classifiers,
and the result carrier $A_{\emp}(S)$ are the same terminal condition.

For append products against the unit, apply the product-unit rows such as
\autoref{thm:group-singleton-continuation-product-unit-triple-coverage} and
\autoref{thm:group-singleton-double-continuation-product-unit-chain-coverage}.
They reduce each displayed continuation product-unit classifier to the carrier
facts on the visible endpoints, so no operation outside the boundary is needed.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Group terminal continuation consumer surface]
\label{thm:group-terminal-continuation-consumer-surface}
Every downstream use of this concrete $\GroupUp$ boundary may read only the
following public surface: $\mathsf{BHist}$, the carrier $A_{\emp}$, the
classifier $R_{\emp}$, $\ContR$ and $\mathsf{append}$ as the multiplication
boundary, $e=\emp$, the constant inverse $\mathsf{inv}(h)=\emp$, $\hsame$
transport, and the ledger rows cited in
\autoref{thm:group-terminal-continuation-boundary-laws}. It does not expose
non-singleton group carriers, subgroup lattices, quotient groups, or host
inverse objects.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The definition of the boundary lists exactly the exported objects. The algebraic
rows of \autoref{thm:group-terminal-continuation-boundary-laws} are all derived
from the singleton carrier, terminal classifier, append, continuation, empty
unit, constant inverse, and $\hsame$ transport.

Any consumer statement that asks for a product, unit, inverse, or continuation
row can therefore be discharged by one of those ledger rows. A non-singleton
carrier, subgroup lattice, quotient group, or host inverse object is not among
the exported objects and is not used by the cited rows, so it has no readable
role on this boundary.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Group terminal continuation inverse obstruction]
\label{thm:group-terminal-continuation-inverse-obstruction}
On the boundary of \autoref{def:group-terminal-continuation-boundary}, every
accepted inverse read of a carried history $h$ is classified by $R_{\emp}$
against $\emp$. Thus a consumer row requiring an inverse endpoint outside the
singleton carrier $A_{\emp}$ is outside the exported $\GroupUp$ boundary.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The boundary definition fixes the inverse operation to the constant row
$\mathsf{inv}(h)=\emp$. By
\autoref{thm:group-terminal-continuation-boundary-laws}, this row is closed on
$A_{\emp}$ and supplies both inverse classifier rows against the unit
$e=\emp$.

Continuation readback does not add another inverse endpoint. The terminal
collapse theorem
\autoref{thm:group-singleton-continuation-terminal-collapse} identifies the
readback classifier with the singleton carrier condition, so every accepted
continuation read remains a read against $\emp$.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:group-terminal-continuation-consumer-surface}. The
exported surface lists the constant inverse and explicitly excludes host
inverse objects. Therefore any consumer row whose inverse endpoint is not the
singleton row $\emp$ asks for data that the boundary does not export.
\end{proof}
