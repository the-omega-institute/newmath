\begin{theorem}[Group singleton normalizer action single-orbit fiber]
\label{thm:group-singleton-normalizer-action-single-orbit-fiber}
For histories $s,x,y:\mathsf{BHist}$ in the concrete singleton history group,
define
$$
\mathsf{Conj}_s(x):=\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(s,x),\mathsf{inv}(s)).
$$
If $A_{\emp}(s)$ and $A_{\emp}(x)$, then the terminal normalizer action fiber is
single-orbit exact:
$$
\mathsf{Conj}_s(x)\sim_{N_{\emp}} y \Longleftrightarrow A_{\emp}(y).
$$
The ten-field fit is: traditional target terminal normalizer action fiber; BEDC
source concrete singleton $\GroupUp$ over $\mathsf{BHist}$; carrier
$A_{\emp}$; classifier $\sim_{N_{\emp}}$; stability from
Theorem~\ref{thm:group-singleton-normalizer-action-certificate},
Theorem~\ref{thm:group-singleton-normalizer-orbit-exactness}, and
Theorem~\ref{thm:group-singleton-terminal-classifier-package}; ledger the
conjugator, source endpoint, and target endpoint; core theorem this block;
exactness the displayed iff; downstream consumer $\QuotientGroupUp$; and status
$\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:group-singleton-normalizer-action-certificate} to $s$ and
$x$. This gives $A_{\emp}(\mathsf{Conj}_s(x))$, so the acted source endpoint is
inside the singleton carrier.

For the forward direction, unpack the orbit relation at
$\mathsf{Conj}_s(x)$ and $y$. Its witness gives a terminal classifier row whose
target endpoint is $y$.

The classifier exactness row of
Theorem~\ref{thm:group-singleton-terminal-classifier-package} reads that row as
the pair of carrier facts for the acted witness endpoint and $y$. Projecting the
second component gives $A_{\emp}(y)$.

For the reverse direction, assume $A_{\emp}(y)$. The terminal classifier
exactness row gives
$$
R_{\emp}(\mathsf{Conj}_s(x),y).
$$
With the source carrier already built and the target carrier now assumed,
Theorem~\ref{thm:group-singleton-normalizer-orbit-exactness} repacks this
classifier as
$\mathsf{Conj}_s(x)\sim_{N_{\emp}} y$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Group singleton normalizer action orbit identity]
\label{thm:group-singleton-normalizer-action-orbit-identity-iff}
For histories $s,x,y:\mathsf{BHist}$ in the concrete singleton history group
instance, write
$$
\mathsf{Conj}_s(z):=\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(s,z),\mathsf{inv}(s)).
$$
If $A_{\emp}(s)$, $A_{\emp}(x)$, and $A_{\emp}(y)$, then
$$
\mathsf{Conj}_s(x)\sim_{N_{\emp}}y \Longleftrightarrow
x\sim_{N_{\emp}}y.
$$
The ten-field fit names the traditional target as terminal normalizer quotient
identity action; BEDC source concrete singleton $\GroupUp$ over
$\mathsf{BHist}$; carrier $A_{\emp}$; classifier the normalizer orbit relation
exact against $R_{\emp}$; stability from the singleton normalizer action
certificate, orbit exactness, and the terminal classifier symmetry and
transitivity rows; ledger the conjugating singleton endpoint and the two orbit
endpoints; core theorem this block; exactness the displayed orbit identity iff;
downstream consumer the singleton $\QuotientGroupUp$ action classifier; and
status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:group-singleton-normalizer-action-certificate} to $s$ and $x$.
It gives both $A_{\emp}(\mathsf{Conj}_s(x))$ and the classifier row
$R_{\emp}(\mathsf{Conj}_s(x),x)$.

For the forward direction, assume
$\mathsf{Conj}_s(x)\sim_{N_{\emp}}y$. Orbit exactness, applied at
$(\mathsf{Conj}_s(x),y)$ with the carrier rows just named and $A_{\emp}(y)$,
converts this orbit edge into
$$
R_{\emp}(\mathsf{Conj}_s(x),y).
$$

Use the symmetry row for the terminal classifier to turn
$R_{\emp}(\mathsf{Conj}_s(x),x)$ into
$R_{\emp}(x,\mathsf{Conj}_s(x))$. Transitivity with the displayed classifier
gives $R_{\emp}(x,y)$, and the reverse direction of orbit exactness repacks this
as $x\sim_{N_{\emp}}y$.

For the reverse direction, assume $x\sim_{N_{\emp}}y$. Orbit exactness at
$(x,y)$ turns this orbit edge into $R_{\emp}(x,y)$.

Compose the certificate classifier $R_{\emp}(\mathsf{Conj}_s(x),x)$ with
$R_{\emp}(x,y)$ by terminal classifier transitivity. The result is
$$
R_{\emp}(\mathsf{Conj}_s(x),y).
$$

Finally use orbit exactness in the classifier-to-orbit direction at
$(\mathsf{Conj}_s(x),y)$. The carrier input is
$A_{\emp}(\mathsf{Conj}_s(x))$ from the action certificate together with
$A_{\emp}(y)$, so the displayed classifier becomes
$\mathsf{Conj}_s(x)\sim_{N_{\emp}}y$.
\end{proof}
