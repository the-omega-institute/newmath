\section{Singleton inverse image exactness}
\label{sec:group-singleton-inverse-image-exactness}

\begin{theorem}[Group singleton inverse image exactness]
\label{thm:group-singleton-inverse-image-exactness}
For histories $h,t:\mathsf{BHist}$, if $A_{\emp}(h)$, then the concrete
constant inverse classifies precisely the singleton carrier:
$$
R_{\emp}(\mathsf{GroupSingletonInv}(h),t)\Longleftrightarrow A_{\emp}(t).
$$
The ten-field fit is: traditional target terminal-group inverse image; BEDC
source concrete singleton $\GroupUp$ over $\mathsf{BHist}$; carrier
$A_{\emp}$; classifier $R_{\emp}$; stability from constant inverse closure and
terminal classifier packaging; ledger the hidden inverse endpoint; core theorem
this block plus Theorem~\ref{thm:group-singleton-terminal-classifier-package};
exactness the displayed iff; downstream consumers $\QuotientGroupUp$ and
singleton normalizer-action proofs; and status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Assume $A_{\emp}(h)$. The concrete singleton inverse unfolds to the constant
endpoint $\emp$, so $A_{\emp}(\mathsf{GroupSingletonInv}(h))$ is the reflexive
singleton witness at $\emp$.

For the forward direction, unpack
$R_{\emp}(\mathsf{GroupSingletonInv}(h),t)$. The right carrier field is exactly
$A_{\emp}(t)$.

For the reverse direction, assume $A_{\emp}(t)$. Combine the reflexive carrier
witness for $\mathsf{GroupSingletonInv}(h)$ with the supplied carrier witness
for $t$. Symmetry turns the endpoint witness for $t$ into a path from $\emp$ to
$t$, and transitivity composes it with the reflexive identification of
$\mathsf{GroupSingletonInv}(h)$ with $\emp$. These three fields package
$R_{\emp}(\mathsf{GroupSingletonInv}(h),t)$.
\end{proof}
