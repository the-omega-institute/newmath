\section{Concrete singleton equation solver determinacy}
\label{sec:group-singleton-equation-solver-determinacy}

\begin{theorem}[Group singleton equation solver determinacy]
\label{thm:group-singleton-equation-solver-determinacy}
In the concrete singleton history group of
Definition~\ref{def:concrete-singleton-history-group-instance}, with carrier
$A_{\emp}$, classifier $R_{\emp}$, multiplication
$\mathsf{mul}:=\mathsf{append}$, unit $\emp$, and inverse
$\mathsf{inv}(h):=\emp$, let $a,b,c,d,x,y:\mathsf{BHist}$. If
$A_{\emp}(a)$, $A_{\emp}(b)$, $A_{\emp}(c)$, $A_{\emp}(d)$, $A_{\emp}(x)$,
and $A_{\emp}(y)$, then the concrete solver is deterministic in the following
same-target forms:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item If $R_{\emp}(\mathsf{mul}(a,x),b)$ and
  $R_{\emp}(\mathsf{mul}(a,y),b)$, then $R_{\emp}(x,y)$.

  \item If $R_{\emp}(\mathsf{mul}(x,a),b)$ and
  $R_{\emp}(\mathsf{mul}(y,a),b)$, then $R_{\emp}(x,y)$.

  \item If $R_{\emp}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a,x),c),d)$ and
  $R_{\emp}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a,y),c),d)$, then $R_{\emp}(x,y)$.
\end{enumerate}
The ten-field fit is: traditional target terminal-group solution determinacy;
BEDC source concrete singleton $\GroupUp$ over $\mathsf{BHist}$; carrier
$A_{\emp}$; classifier $R_{\emp}$; stability from concrete solver exactness plus
classifier symmetry and transitivity; ledger hidden equation target and
contexts; core theorem list
Theorem~\ref{thm:group-singleton-equation-cancellation-concrete-solver} plus
the classifier equivalence fields; exactness same-target solution determinacy;
downstream consumers $\QuotientGroupUp$ and $\FieldUp$ algebra-reduct packages;
status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Theorem~\ref{thm:group-singleton-equation-cancellation-concrete-solver} gives
the three concrete rows for the same carrier, classifier, multiplication, unit,
and inverse. The concrete classifier fields supply symmetry and transitivity on
$R_{\emp}$ whenever the displayed endpoints are in $A_{\emp}$.

\begin{enumerate}
  \item For the left-multiplication row, apply the forward direction of the
  concrete solver to $R_{\emp}(\mathsf{mul}(a,x),b)$. This transports $x$ to
  the endpoint $\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{inv}(a),b)$.

  Apply the same forward direction to $R_{\emp}(\mathsf{mul}(a,y),b)$. Now
  $y$ is transported to the same solved endpoint. Use classifier symmetry on the
  second solved comparison, then classifier transitivity through the common
  endpoint to obtain $R_{\emp}(x,y)$.

  \item For the right-multiplication row, apply the forward direction of the
  concrete solver to both assumptions. The two transported comparisons classify
  $x$ and $y$ by the common endpoint
  $\mathsf{mul}(b,\mathsf{inv}(a))$.

  Symmetry on the solved comparison for $y$, followed by transitivity through
  that common endpoint, gives $R_{\emp}(x,y)$.

  \item For the two-sided row, first use symmetry on
  $R_{\emp}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a,y),c),d)$. Transitivity with
  $R_{\emp}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a,x),c),d)$ through the common target
  $d$ gives
  $$
  R_{\emp}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a,x),c),
  \mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a,y),c)).
  $$

  Apply the two-sided cancellation row of
  Theorem~\ref{thm:group-singleton-equation-cancellation-concrete-solver} to
  this classifier. The result is $R_{\emp}(x,y)$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{proof}
