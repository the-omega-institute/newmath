\section{Normalizer orbit classifier stability}
\label{sec:group-normalizer-orbit-classifier-stability}

\begin{theorem}[Group centralizer normalizer orbit classifier certificate]
\label{thm:group-centralizer-normalizer-orbit-classifier-certificate}
For a BEDC history-level group operation over $\mathsf{BHist}$ with the same
associativity, left and right identity laws for $e$, multiplication
congruence, inverse congruence, and two-sided inverse laws as in
\autoref{thm:group-centralizer-normalizer-orbit-equivalence}, let $Z_a$,
$N_a$, $\mathsf{Conj}_s$, and $\sim_{N_a}$ be as in
\autoref{def:group-centralizer-normalizer-orbit-relation}. For
$x,y,x',y':\mathsf{BHist}$, assume $Z_a(x)$, $Z_a(y)$, $Z_a(x')$,
$Z_a(y')$, $\hsame(x,x')$, and $\hsame(y,y')$. Then endpoint transport
preserves and reflects orbit reachability:
$$
x\sim_{N_a}y
\Longleftrightarrow
x'\sim_{N_a}y'.
$$
Together with
\autoref{thm:group-centralizer-normalizer-orbit-equivalence}, this
endpoint-transport row supplies the quotient-free classifier fields on the
centralizer carrier: carrier reflexivity, symmetry, transitivity, and
stability under replacement of both exposed endpoints by $\hsame$-equal
histories.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the forward direction, unpack an orbit witness $s$ for
$x\sim_{N_a}y$. Keep the same normalizer witness $N_a(s)$ and replace only the
source and target endpoints.

Multiplication congruence transports the conjugation word
$\mathsf{Conj}_s(x)$ across $\hsame(x,x')$, giving a comparison between
$\mathsf{Conj}_s(x')$ and $\mathsf{Conj}_s(x)$. Compose this comparison with
the original endpoint witness $\hsame(\mathsf{Conj}_s(x),y)$ and then with
$\hsame(y,y')$. The carried assumptions $Z_a(x')$ and $Z_a(y')$ repack the
same $s$ as a witness for $x'\sim_{N_a}y'$.

For the reverse direction, apply the same argument after exchanging
$(x,y)$ with $(x',y')$. Symmetry of $\hsame$ turns the displayed endpoint
comparisons into $\hsame(x',x)$ and $\hsame(y',y)$, so the witness for
$x'\sim_{N_a}y'$ transports back to a witness for $x\sim_{N_a}y$.

The classifier certificate now has its structural rows from
\autoref{thm:group-centralizer-normalizer-orbit-equivalence}: reflexivity,
symmetry, and transitivity on the carrier $Z_a$. The two endpoint-transport
directions above add stability under $\hsame$-replacement of the visible
source and target endpoints, without passing to a quotient object.
\end{proof}
