\begin{theorem}[Group algebra hierarchy public exit package]
\label{thm:group-algebra-hierarchy-public-exit-package}
The public $\GroupUp$ package exported by this chapter is exactly the union of
two concrete $\mathsf{BHist}$ surfaces: the singleton empty-history carrier of
\autoref{def:group-concrete-history-instance} and the finite-word carrier of
\autoref{def:group-finite-word-history-instance}. Its multiplication, unit,
inverse, product closure, inverse boundary, and $\NameCert$ packaging are the
rows supplied by \autoref{thm:group-concrete-instance-schema-unlock},
\autoref{thm:group-finite-word-operation-closure},
\autoref{thm:group-finite-word-inverse-boundary}, and
\autoref{thm:group-finite-word-namecert-package}, with the monoid dependency
read through \autoref{thm:group-concrete-monoid-dependency}. No host group
carrier, quotient group object, external operation table, or ambient equality
is exported by the public package.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.GroupUp.GroupUp\_StdBridge}
\begin{proof}
Project the singleton rows first. The carrier is $A_{\emp}$, the classifier is
$R_{\emp}$, multiplication is append, the unit is $\emp$, and inverse is the
constant empty endpoint of \autoref{def:group-concrete-history-instance}.
\autoref{thm:group-concrete-instance-schema-unlock} supplies the corresponding
operation, unit, associativity, congruence, inverse, and classifier rows, while
\autoref{thm:group-concrete-monoid-dependency} records that the append ledger is
the consumed $\MonoidUp$ dependency.

Next project the finite-word rows. The carrier and classifier are the finite
word spine and componentwise singleton classifier of
\autoref{def:group-finite-word-history-instance}. Product closure is
\autoref{thm:group-finite-word-operation-closure}, the reversal inverse
boundary is \autoref{thm:group-finite-word-inverse-boundary}, and the resulting
finite $\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$ packet is
\autoref{thm:group-finite-word-namecert-package}.

These two projections exhaust the public exit: singleton endpoints and finite
singleton words, both with displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ ledgers. Since every
operation row is read from those carriers and their classifiers, the exported
package contains no additional host group carrier, quotient object, operation
table, or ambient equality relation.
\end{proof}
