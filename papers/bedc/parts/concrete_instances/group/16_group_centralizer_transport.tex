\section{Centralizer transport}
\label{sec:group-centralizer-transport}

\begin{theorem}[Group centralizer fixed-element transport]
\label{thm:group-centralizer-fixed-element-transport}
For a BEDC history-level group operation over $\mathsf{BHist}$ with
multiplication congruence, let $Z_a$ and $Z_b$ be the centralizer carriers of
\autoref{def:group-centralizer-carrier} for fixed elements
$a,b:\mathsf{BHist}$. If $\hsame(a,b)$, then for every
$x:\mathsf{BHist}$,
$$
Z_a(x) \Longleftrightarrow Z_b(x).
$$
The ten-field fit is: traditional target transport of centralizers along
internal equality of the fixed element; BEDC source $\GroupUp$ over
$\mathsf{BHist}$; carrier the family $Z_a$; classifier $\hsame$; stability
obligation multiplication congruence in the fixed-element slot; ledger the
fixed-element comparison; core theorem list
\autoref{def:group-centralizer-carrier}, multiplication congruence, and
$\hsame$ symmetry and transitivity; exactness predicate the displayed iff;
standard bridge ordinary equality-invariant centralizers; status
$\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold the centralizer carrier. The assertion $Z_a(x)$ is the comparison
$\hsame(\mathsf{mul}(x,a),\mathsf{mul}(a,x))$, and $Z_b(x)$ is the
comparison $\hsame(\mathsf{mul}(x,b),\mathsf{mul}(b,x))$.

For the forward direction, assume $Z_a(x)$. Symmetry turns
$\hsame(a,b)$ into $\hsame(b,a)$, and multiplication congruence in the
fixed-element slot transports it to
$\hsame(\mathsf{mul}(x,b),\mathsf{mul}(x,a))$. Transitivity with the
$Z_a(x)$ witness gives
$\hsame(\mathsf{mul}(x,b),\mathsf{mul}(a,x))$.

Multiplication congruence also transports $\hsame(a,b)$ through the left
fixed-element slot, giving
$\hsame(\mathsf{mul}(a,x),\mathsf{mul}(b,x))$. A final transitivity step
therefore yields
$\hsame(\mathsf{mul}(x,b),\mathsf{mul}(b,x))$, which is $Z_b(x)$.

For the reverse direction, exchange $a$ and $b$. The same calculation starts
from $Z_b(x)$, uses symmetry of $\hsame(a,b)$ where the fixed element is
transported from $b$ back to $a$, and concludes with
$\hsame(\mathsf{mul}(x,a),\mathsf{mul}(a,x))$, namely $Z_a(x)$.
\end{proof}
