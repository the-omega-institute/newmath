\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for \(\GroupUp\)}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-group-namecert}

The group interface \(\GroupUp\) extends \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-monoid-namecert} with a unary inverse operation \({}^{-1} : C \to C\) satisfying left and right inverse laws under the carrier's classifier. It is not a primitive structure; it is licensed only by composing the monoid certificate with the inverse field.

\begin{remark}[Roadmap chapter]
\label{rem:concrete-instances-group-namecert-roadmap}
This chapter records the licensing target. Lean markers identify the
algebraic fields and derived laws that are formalized for the certificate.
\end{remark}

\section{Carrier definition}

\begin{definition}[Group carrier]
\label{def:group-carrier}
Use a monoid setup extended with inverse operation \(a^{-1}\).
\end{definition}

\section{Concrete singleton history group instance}
\label{sec:group-concrete-singleton-history-instance}

\begin{definition}[Concrete singleton history group instance]
\label{def:concrete-singleton-history-group-instance}
Let $A_{\emp}$ be the singleton history source of
Definition~\ref{def:list-singleton-history-source} at $a_0=\emp$. Define
the concrete history operations and classifier by
$$
\mathsf{mul}(h,k):=\mathsf{append}(h,k),\qquad
\mathsf{inv}(h):=\emp,\qquad
e:=\emp,
$$
and
$$
R_{\emp}(h,k)\Longleftrightarrow
A_{\emp}(h)\land A_{\emp}(k)\land \hsame(h,k).
$$
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Concrete singleton history group laws]
\label{thm:concrete-singleton-history-group-laws}
The tuple $(A_{\emp},R_{\emp},\mathsf{mul},e,\mathsf{inv})$ first supplies
the singleton-history $\mathsf{SemanticNameCert}$ for $\GroupUp$ over
$\mathsf{BHist}$, and also satisfies carrier inhabitance, multiplication
closure, inverse closure, left and right unit laws, associativity up to
$\hsame$, multiplication congruence, inverse congruence, and left and right
inverse laws.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The $\mathsf{SemanticNameCert}$ component is packaged by the carrier-aware
classifier $R_{\emp}$. Its inhabited carrier is witnessed by
$\hsame(\emp,\emp)$. Reflexivity, symmetry, and transitivity are inherited
from $\hsame$ after unpacking and repacking the carrier endpoints required by
$R_{\emp}$.

Carrier transport follows by composing singleton endpoint witnesses through
$\emp$: if a source endpoint and its classifier partner are both related by
$R_{\emp}$, then the transported endpoint remains in $A_{\emp}$ by
transitivity through their displayed witnesses to $\emp$.

Carrier inhabitance is the reflexive endpoint witness
$\hsame(\emp,\emp)$, which is exactly the singleton carrier condition from
Definition~\ref{def:list-singleton-history-source} at $a_0=\emp$.

For multiplication closure, suppose $A_{\emp}(h)$ and $A_{\emp}(k)$. The two
carrier facts identify $h$ and $k$ with $\emp$. The BEDC continuation unit
laws used in Theorem~\ref{thm:concrete-history-monoid-laws} reduce
$\mathsf{append}(h,k)$ to the same endpoint class as $\emp$, using symmetry
and transitivity of $\hsame$ to compose the displayed witnesses.

Inverse closure is immediate because $\mathsf{inv}(h)$ is definitionally
$\emp$. The singleton carrier witness is again $\hsame(\emp,\emp)$.

The left unit law reduces $\mathsf{append}(\emp,h)$ to $h$ by the continuation
left-unit law, and the right unit law reduces $\mathsf{append}(h,\emp)$ to
$h$ by the continuation right-unit law. The endpoint witnesses required by
$R_{\emp}$ are supplied by the carrier facts for $\emp$ and $h$.

Associativity is the continuation associativity theorem cited in
Theorem~\ref{thm:concrete-history-monoid-laws}: the histories
$\mathsf{append}(\mathsf{append}(h,k),l)$ and
$\mathsf{append}(h,\mathsf{append}(k,l))$ are $\hsame$-equivalent. Closure of
the two products follows from the multiplication-closure clause.

Multiplication congruence follows by transporting the two input $\hsame$
witnesses across $\mathsf{append}$ and then composing with the same
continuation associativity and unit laws. Since all carrier elements are in
the singleton class of $\emp$, the source and target products both lie in
$A_{\emp}$ and are related by $\hsame$.

Inverse congruence is constant: both inverses are $\emp$, so the classifier
witness is the pair of singleton carrier witnesses together with
$\hsame(\emp,\emp)$.

For the inverse laws, $\mathsf{append}(\mathsf{inv}(h),h)$ is
$\mathsf{append}(\emp,h)$ and $\mathsf{append}(h,\mathsf{inv}(h))$ is
$\mathsf{append}(h,\emp)$. The continuation unit laws reduce these products to
$h$, and the carrier fact $A_{\emp}(h)$ identifies $h$ with $e=\emp$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Group singleton classifier append context cancellation]
\label{thm:group-singleton-classifier-append-context-cancel}
For histories $L,R,Q,S:\mathsf{BHist}$, if the displayed left contexts are in
the singleton group carrier $A_{\emp}$, then appending those contexts to the
two classifier endpoints preserves and reflects the singleton classifier:
$$
A_{\emp}(L)\Rightarrow A_{\emp}(R)\Rightarrow
\bigl(
R_{\emp}(\mathsf{append}(L,Q),\mathsf{append}(R,S))
\Longleftrightarrow
R_{\emp}(Q,S)
\bigr).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
In the forward direction, the two appended endpoints are classified as
singleton endpoints. Empty-result inversion for append splits each endpoint
witness, giving $Q\hsame\emp$ and $S\hsame\emp$. These two carrier witnesses,
together with transitivity through $\emp$, assemble $R_{\emp}(Q,S)$.

Conversely, combine the supplied singleton witnesses for $L$ and $R$ with the
endpoint carrier fields in $R_{\emp}(Q,S)$. Empty-result construction for
append gives singleton witnesses for $\mathsf{append}(L,Q)$ and
$\mathsf{append}(R,S)$, and transitivity through $\emp$ gives the classifier
comparison between the two appended endpoints.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Group singleton classifier two-sided context cancellation]

For histories $L,R,L',R',Q,S:\mathsf{BHist}$, if $A_{\emp}(L)$,

The ten-field fit names the traditional target as terminal-group two-sided

terminal-group contexts; and status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the forward direction, apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:group-singleton-classifier-right-context-cancel} to cancel

Then apply Theorem~\ref{thm:group-singleton-classifier-append-context-cancel}

\begin{theorem}[Group singleton classifier product-unit split]
\label{thm:group-singleton-classifier-product-unit-split}
For histories $p,q:\mathsf{BHist}$, the concrete singleton group classifier
satisfies
$$
R_{\emp}(\mathsf{append}(p,q),\emp)
\Longleftrightarrow
A_{\emp}(p)\land A_{\emp}(q).
$$
The ten-field fit names the traditional target as a product equal to the unit
in the one-element group; the BEDC source as the concrete singleton $\GroupUp$
instance over $\mathsf{BHist}$; carrier $A_{\emp}$; classifier $R_{\emp}$;
stability as append empty-result construction and inversion; ledger factors
$p,q$; core theorem list
Theorem~\ref{thm:concrete-singleton-history-group-laws} together with the BEDC
append empty-result inversion; exactness predicate the displayed iff; standard
bridge the terminal group unit-product split; and status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold $R_{\emp}$ and $A_{\emp}$. In the forward direction, the classifier
supplies $A_{\emp}(\mathsf{append}(p,q))$, so
$\hsame(\mathsf{append}(p,q),\emp)$ holds. Append empty-result inversion splits
this witness into $A_{\emp}(p)$ and $A_{\emp}(q)$, giving the displayed
conjunction.

Conversely, assume $A_{\emp}(p)$ and $A_{\emp}(q)$. Append empty-result
construction gives $A_{\emp}(\mathsf{append}(p,q))$, while reflexivity gives
$A_{\emp}(\emp)$. Symmetry and transitivity of $\hsame$ then package the
endpoint comparison required by
$R_{\emp}(\mathsf{append}(p,q),\emp)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Group singleton continuation result carrier exactness]
\label{thm:group-singleton-continuation-result-carrier-iff}
For histories $P,Q,R:\mathsf{BHist}$ with $\ContR(P,Q,R)$, the singleton group
classifier relates the continuation inputs exactly when the continuation result
lies in the singleton carrier:
$$
R_{\emp}(P,Q)\Longleftrightarrow A_{\emp}(R).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Assume $R_{\emp}(P,Q)$. The classifier exposes endpoint witnesses
$A_{\emp}(P)$ and $A_{\emp}(Q)$. Transporting the displayed continuation
against the empty right-unit continuation carries the result endpoint to
$\emp$, and therefore classifies $R$ as a singleton-carried history.

Conversely, assume $A_{\emp}(R)$. Transport the continuation result to $\emp$
along this carrier witness. Empty-result inversion for continuation and append
then recovers endpoint witnesses $A_{\emp}(P)$ and $A_{\emp}(Q)$. Composing
these endpoint witnesses through $\emp$ packages the input pair classifier
$R_{\emp}(P,Q)$.
\end{proof}
