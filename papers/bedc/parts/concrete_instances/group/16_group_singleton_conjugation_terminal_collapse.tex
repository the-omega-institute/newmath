\section{Singleton conjugation terminal collapse}
\label{sec:group-singleton-conjugation-terminal-collapse}

\begin{theorem}[Group singleton conjugation terminal collapse]
\label{thm:group-singleton-conjugation-terminal-collapse}
For histories $s,x:\mathsf{BHist}$, if $A_{\emp}(s)$, then singleton
conjugation by $s$ is exactly terminal carrier membership of the middle
endpoint:
$$
R_{\emp}(\mathsf{append}(\mathsf{append}(s,x),\mathsf{GroupSingletonInv}(s)),\emp)
\Longleftrightarrow A_{\emp}(x).
$$
The ten-field fit is: traditional target terminal-group conjugation identity;
BEDC source concrete singleton $\GroupUp$ over $\mathsf{BHist}$; carrier
$A_{\emp}$; classifier $R_{\emp}$; stability from inverse closure, two-sided
contextual cancellation, product-unit split, and unit transport; ledger the
conjugating endpoint $s$ and the hidden inverse endpoint
$\mathsf{GroupSingletonInv}(s)$; exactness the displayed iff; standard bridge
conjugation in the one-element group; downstream consumer terminal normalizer
and quotient-visibility collapse; and status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Assume $A_{\emp}(s)$. The inverse row of
\autoref{thm:group-singleton-terminal-classifier-package}, applied to the
concrete singleton inverse of $s$, supplies
$A_{\emp}(\mathsf{GroupSingletonInv}(s))$. The empty endpoint has
$A_{\emp}(\emp)$ by reflexivity, so the four context carriers needed for the
two-sided contextual product-unit split are available.

Transport the displayed right endpoint along the empty unit laws, and transport
the middle factor $x$ along the right unit law, so the classifier has the
two-sided product-unit shape with contexts $s,\emp$ and
$\mathsf{GroupSingletonInv}(s),\emp$:
$$
R_{\emp}(\mathsf{append}(\mathsf{append}(s,\mathsf{append}(x,\emp)),
\mathsf{GroupSingletonInv}(s)),\mathsf{append}(\mathsf{append}(\emp,\emp),\emp)).
$$

Apply \autoref{thm:group-singleton-two-sided-contextual-product-unit-split} with
$p=x$ and $q=\emp$. This cancels the singleton contexts and identifies the
transported classifier with $A_{\emp}(x)\land A_{\emp}(\emp)$.

For the forward direction, project the first component of this conjunction. For
the reverse direction, combine the assumed $A_{\emp}(x)$ with the reflexive
carrier witness $A_{\emp}(\emp)$, apply the product-unit split in the
constructive direction, and transport the unit laws back to the displayed
conjugation endpoint against $\emp$.
\end{proof}
