\begin{theorem}[Group singleton conjugated commutator invariance]
\label{thm:group-singleton-conjugated-commutator-invariance}
For histories $s,x,y:\mathsf{BHist}$ in the concrete singleton history group of
Definition~\ref{def:concrete-singleton-history-group-instance}, define
$$
\mathsf{Conj}_s(z):=\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(s,z),\mathsf{inv}(s))
$$
and use $\mathsf{Comm}_{\emp}$ as in
\autoref{thm:group-singleton-commutator-terminal-collapse}. If
$A_{\emp}(s)$, $A_{\emp}(x)$, and $A_{\emp}(y)$, then
$$
R_{\emp}(\mathsf{Comm}_{\emp}(\mathsf{Conj}_s(x),\mathsf{Conj}_s(y)),
\mathsf{Comm}_{\emp}(x,y)),
$$
and both commutator endpoints classify against $\emp$:
$$
R_{\emp}(\mathsf{Comm}_{\emp}(\mathsf{Conj}_s(x),\mathsf{Conj}_s(y)),\emp)
\land R_{\emp}(\mathsf{Comm}_{\emp}(x,y),\emp).
$$
The ten-field fit is: traditional target terminal commutator conjugation
invariance; BEDC source concrete singleton $\GroupUp$ over $\mathsf{BHist}$;
carrier $A_{\emp}$; classifier $R_{\emp}$; stability from action closure,
commutator closure, and terminal classifier exactness; ledger the conjugator
plus two commutator inputs; core theorem this composite invariance; exactness
the displayed concrete commutator classifier; downstream consumers $\AbGroupUp$
and $\QuotientGroupUp$; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply Theorem~\ref{thm:group-singleton-normalizer-action-certificate} to
$(s,x)$ and again to $(s,y)$. These two instances put
$\mathsf{Conj}_s(x)$ and $\mathsf{Conj}_s(y)$ in the singleton carrier
$A_{\emp}$.

Next apply Theorem~\ref{thm:group-singleton-commutator-terminal-collapse} to
the original pair $(x,y)$. It gives the carrier fact for
$\mathsf{Comm}_{\emp}(x,y)$ and the classifier
$R_{\emp}(\mathsf{Comm}_{\emp}(x,y),\emp)$.

Apply the same commutator-collapse theorem to the conjugated pair
$(\mathsf{Conj}_s(x),\mathsf{Conj}_s(y))$. The action-carrier facts just built
supply its hypotheses, so the theorem gives both
$A_{\emp}(\mathsf{Comm}_{\emp}(\mathsf{Conj}_s(x),\mathsf{Conj}_s(y)))$ and
$R_{\emp}(\mathsf{Comm}_{\emp}(\mathsf{Conj}_s(x),\mathsf{Conj}_s(y)),\emp)$.

Finally use the terminal classifier row of
Theorem~\ref{thm:group-singleton-terminal-classifier-package} on the two
commutator carrier facts. It classifies the conjugated commutator endpoint
against the original commutator endpoint by $R_{\emp}$, while the two
commutator-collapse applications provide the two classifiers against $\emp$.
\end{proof}
