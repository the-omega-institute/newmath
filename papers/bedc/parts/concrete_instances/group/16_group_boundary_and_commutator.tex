\begin{theorem}[Group singleton compiled concrete instance boundary]
\label{thm:group-singleton-compiled-concrete-instance-boundary}
The singleton carrier $A_{\emp}$, classifier $R_{\emp}$, multiplication
$\mathsf{append}$, unit $\emp$, and constant inverse $\emp$ of
Definition~\ref{def:concrete-singleton-history-group-instance} simultaneously
supply the singleton-history $\mathsf{SemanticNameCert}$ for $\GroupUp$ and
the full concrete group algebraic-law package over $\mathsf{BHist}$:
carrier inhabitance, multiplication closure, inverse closure, the left and
right unit laws, associativity up to $\hsame$, multiplication congruence,
inverse congruence, and the left and right inverse laws.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The certificate component is exactly
Theorem~\ref{thm:group-singleton-history-semantic-namecert}. It packages the
carrier-aware classifier $R_{\emp}$ with the append multiplication, empty unit,
and constant empty inverse as the singleton-history
$\mathsf{SemanticNameCert}$ for $\GroupUp$.

The algebraic-law component is
Theorem~\ref{thm:concrete-singleton-history-group-laws}. Its rows give carrier
inhabitance, closure under multiplication and inverse, the two unit laws,
associativity, multiplication congruence, inverse congruence, and the two
inverse laws for the same carrier, classifier, multiplication, unit, and
inverse. The two cited blocks therefore have identical concrete data, so they
assemble the stated concrete instance boundary.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Group singleton left multiplication classifier coverage]
\label{thm:group-singleton-left-mul-classifier-coverage}
For histories $a,x,b:\mathsf{BHist}$ in the concrete singleton history group of
Definition~\ref{def:concrete-singleton-history-group-instance}, define
$$
\mathsf{L}_a(x):=\mathsf{mul}(a,x).
$$
If $A_{\emp}(a)$, then
$$
R_{\emp}(\mathsf{L}_a(x),b)
\Longleftrightarrow A_{\emp}(x)\land A_{\emp}(b).
$$
The ten-field fit is: traditional target terminal-group left-translation
coverage; BEDC source concrete singleton $\GroupUp$ over $\mathsf{BHist}$;
carrier $A_{\emp}$; classifier $R_{\emp}$; stability from terminal classifier
exactness and multiplication-carrier exactness; ledger the fixed left factor
and translated endpoint; core theorem this block; exactness the displayed iff;
downstream consumer $\QuotientGroupUp$; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold $\mathsf{L}_a$. For the forward direction, apply the terminal
classifier row of Theorem~\ref{thm:group-singleton-terminal-classifier-package}
to $R_{\emp}(\mathsf{mul}(a,x),b)$. This gives
$A_{\emp}(\mathsf{mul}(a,x))$ and $A_{\emp}(b)$.

Apply the multiplication-carrier exactness row of the same theorem to
$A_{\emp}(\mathsf{mul}(a,x))$. It identifies this carrier fact with
$A_{\emp}(a)\land A_{\emp}(x)$, and the fixed witness $A_{\emp}(a)$ lets us
project $A_{\emp}(x)$. Together with $A_{\emp}(b)$, this gives the right side.

For the reverse direction, assume $A_{\emp}(x)\land A_{\emp}(b)$.
The multiplication-carrier exactness row combines the fixed witness
$A_{\emp}(a)$ with $A_{\emp}(x)$ to build
$A_{\emp}(\mathsf{mul}(a,x))$.

The terminal classifier row then packages
$A_{\emp}(\mathsf{mul}(a,x))$ and $A_{\emp}(b)$ as
$R_{\emp}(\mathsf{mul}(a,x),b)$, which is the displayed classifier after
unfolding $\mathsf{L}_a$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Group singleton commutator terminal collapse]
\label{thm:group-singleton-commutator-terminal-collapse}
For histories $x,y:\mathsf{BHist}$ in the concrete singleton history group of
Definition~\ref{def:concrete-singleton-history-group-instance}, define
$$
\mathsf{Comm}_{\emp}(x,y):=
\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(x,y),\mathsf{inv}(x)),\mathsf{inv}(y)).
$$
If $A_{\emp}(x)$ and $A_{\emp}(y)$, then
$A_{\emp}(\mathsf{Comm}_{\emp}(x,y))$ and
$R_{\emp}(\mathsf{Comm}_{\emp}(x,y),\emp)$.
The ten-field fit is: traditional target terminal-group commutator fiber; BEDC
source concrete singleton $\GroupUp$ over $\mathsf{BHist}$; carrier
$A_{\emp}$; classifier $R_{\emp}$; stability from multiplication closure,
inverse closure, and terminal classifier exactness; ledger the two factors and
inverse endpoints; core theorem this block plus
Theorem~\ref{thm:group-singleton-terminal-classifier-package}; exactness
terminal commutator classified with the unit; downstream consumers
$\AbGroupUp$ and $\QuotientGroupUp$; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from $A_{\emp}(x)$ and $A_{\emp}(y)$. The inverse row of
Theorem~\ref{thm:group-singleton-terminal-classifier-package} places
$\mathsf{inv}(x)$ and $\mathsf{inv}(y)$ in $A_{\emp}$.

Apply the multiplication-closure row three times. It first places
$\mathsf{mul}(x,y)$ in $A_{\emp}$, then places
$\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(x,y),\mathsf{inv}(x))$ in $A_{\emp}$, and finally
places $\mathsf{Comm}_{\emp}(x,y)$ in $A_{\emp}$.

The carrier inhabitance row gives $A_{\emp}(\emp)$. With the carrier fact for
$\mathsf{Comm}_{\emp}(x,y)$ and the carrier fact for $\emp$, the terminal
classifier row of Theorem~\ref{thm:group-singleton-terminal-classifier-package}
packages $R_{\emp}(\mathsf{Comm}_{\emp}(x,y),\emp)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Group singleton commutator unit exactness]
\label{thm:group-singleton-commutator-unit-exactness}
For histories $x,y:\mathsf{BHist}$ in the concrete singleton history group of
Definition~\ref{def:concrete-singleton-history-group-instance}, define
$$
\mathsf{Comm}_{\emp}(x,y):=
\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(x,y),\mathsf{inv}(x)),\mathsf{inv}(y)).
$$
Then the commutator is classified with the unit exactly when both inputs lie in
the singleton carrier:
$$
R_{\emp}(\mathsf{Comm}_{\emp}(x,y),\emp)
\Longleftrightarrow A_{\emp}(x)\land A_{\emp}(y).
$$
The ten-field fit is: traditional target terminal-group commutator fiber
exactness; BEDC source concrete singleton $\GroupUp$ over $\mathsf{BHist}$;
carrier $A_{\emp}$; classifier $R_{\emp}$; stability from product-unit
splitting, context cancellation, inverse closure, and terminal classifier
exactness; ledger the two factors and two constant inverse endpoints; core
theorem this exactness plus
Theorem~\ref{thm:group-singleton-classifier-product-unit-split} and
Theorem~\ref{thm:group-singleton-contextual-product-unit-split}; exactness the
displayed iff; downstream consumers $\AbGroupUp$ and $\QuotientGroupUp$; status
$\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold the concrete operations. Here $\mathsf{inv}$ is the constant endpoint
$\emp$ and $\mathsf{mul}$ is append, so the left side is the terminal classifier
of the nested append word
$\mathsf{append}(\mathsf{append}(\mathsf{append}(x,y),\emp),\emp)$ against
$\emp$.

For the forward direction, project the classifier to the carried endpoint
$\emp$. The unit endpoint is carried, so terminal classifier exactness reduces
the hypothesis to singleton carrier membership of the nested append word.

Apply Theorem~\ref{thm:group-singleton-classifier-product-unit-split} to split
off the outer constant inverse endpoint, then apply the same product-unit split
to remove the inner constant inverse endpoint. The remaining classifier is the
product-unit classifier for $\mathsf{append}(x,y)$, and a final application gives
exactly $A_{\emp}(x)\land A_{\emp}(y)$.

For the reverse direction, assume $A_{\emp}(x)\land A_{\emp}(y)$.
Theorem~\ref{thm:group-singleton-commutator-terminal-collapse} applies to these
two carrier witnesses and gives
$R_{\emp}(\mathsf{Comm}_{\emp}(x,y),\emp)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Group singleton commutator carrier exactness]
\label{thm:group-singleton-commutator-carrier-exactness}
For histories $x,y:\mathsf{BHist}$ in the concrete singleton history group of
Definition~\ref{def:concrete-singleton-history-group-instance}, with
$\mathsf{Comm}_{\emp}$ defined as in
\autoref{thm:group-singleton-commutator-terminal-collapse}, the commutator
carrier and terminal classifier are exactly the two input carrier conditions:
$$
A_{\emp}(\mathsf{Comm}_{\emp}(x,y))
\Longleftrightarrow A_{\emp}(x)\land A_{\emp}(y).
$$
Moreover,
$$
R_{\emp}(\mathsf{Comm}_{\emp}(x,y),\emp)
\Longleftrightarrow A_{\emp}(x)\land A_{\emp}(y).
$$
The ten-field fit is: traditional target terminal-group commutator fiber
exactness; BEDC source concrete singleton $\GroupUp$ over $\mathsf{BHist}$;
carrier $A_{\emp}$; classifier $R_{\emp}$; stability append-empty split plus
terminal classifier exactness; ledger the two factors and constant inverse
endpoints; core theorem this exactness plus
\autoref{thm:group-singleton-commutator-terminal-collapse}; exactness the
displayed iff; downstream consumers $\AbGroupUp$ and $\QuotientGroupUp$; status
$\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold the concrete singleton group operations. The multiplication is
$\mathsf{append}$ and the inverse endpoint is constant, so
$\mathsf{inv}(x)=\emp$ and $\mathsf{inv}(y)=\emp$. Thus the commutator carrier
condition is the singleton-empty condition on the append product whose two right
factors are empty endpoints.

For the forward carrier direction, append-empty inversion first removes the
outer empty inverse endpoint, then removes the inner empty inverse endpoint, and
finally applies the singleton append-empty split to the remaining product
$\mathsf{append}(x,y)$. This yields $A_{\emp}(x)$ and $A_{\emp}(y)$.

For the reverse carrier direction, the supplied witnesses for $x$ and $y$
rebuild $A_{\emp}(\mathsf{append}(x,y))$ by the singleton append-empty split.
The reflexive empty witnesses for the two constant inverse endpoints then
rebuild the full commutator carrier by append-empty construction.

It remains to read the classifier against the terminal endpoint. The first row
of \autoref{thm:group-singleton-terminal-classifier-package} identifies
$R_{\emp}(\mathsf{Comm}_{\emp}(x,y),\emp)$ with
$A_{\emp}(\mathsf{Comm}_{\emp}(x,y))\land A_{\emp}(\emp)$, and
$A_{\emp}(\emp)$ is reflexive. Composing this terminal classifier exactness with
the carrier equivalence gives the displayed classifier iff.
\end{proof}
