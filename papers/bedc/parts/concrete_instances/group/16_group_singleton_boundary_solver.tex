\section{Concrete singleton equation solver}
\label{sec:group-singleton-equation-solver}

\begin{theorem}[Group singleton equation-cancellation concrete solver]
\label{thm:group-singleton-equation-cancellation-concrete-solver}
In the concrete singleton history group of
Definition~\ref{def:concrete-singleton-history-group-instance}, with carrier
$A_{\emp}$, classifier $R_{\emp}$, multiplication
$\mathsf{mul}:=\mathsf{append}$, unit $\emp$, and inverse
$\mathsf{inv}(h):=\emp$, the concrete data supply the following
carrier-aware solver rows for histories $a,b,c,d,x:\mathsf{BHist}$:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item If $A_{\emp}(a)$, $A_{\emp}(x)$, and $A_{\emp}(b)$, then
  $$
  R_{\emp}(\mathsf{mul}(a,x),b)
  \Longleftrightarrow
  R_{\emp}(x,\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{inv}(a),b)).
  $$

  \item If $A_{\emp}(a)$, $A_{\emp}(x)$, and $A_{\emp}(b)$, then
  $$
  R_{\emp}(\mathsf{mul}(x,a),b)
  \Longleftrightarrow
  R_{\emp}(x,\mathsf{mul}(b,\mathsf{inv}(a))).
  $$

  \item If $A_{\emp}(a)$, $A_{\emp}(b)$, $A_{\emp}(c)$, and
  $A_{\emp}(d)$, then
  $$
  R_{\emp}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a,b),c),
  \mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a,d),c))
  \Longrightarrow R_{\emp}(b,d).
  $$
\end{enumerate}
The ten-field fit is: traditional target terminal-group equation solving;
BEDC source concrete singleton $\GroupUp$ over $\mathsf{BHist}$; carrier
$A_{\emp}$; classifier $R_{\emp}$; stability from concrete group laws plus
generic left and right equation exactness and two-sided cancellation; ledger
equation factors and inverse endpoints; core theorem list
Theorem~\ref{thm:concrete-singleton-history-group-laws},
Theorem~\ref{thm:group-left-mul-equation-exactness},
Theorem~\ref{thm:group-right-mul-equation-exactness}, and
Theorem~\ref{thm:group-two-sided-cancellation}; exactness the three displayed
rows; downstream consumers $\QuotientGroupUp$ and $\FieldUp$ algebra-reduct
packages; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Theorem~\ref{thm:concrete-singleton-history-group-laws} supplies the concrete
group rows for the same carrier, classifier, multiplication, unit, and inverse.
In particular, it gives multiplication closure, inverse closure, associativity,
multiplication congruence, and the two inverse laws needed by the generic
equation and cancellation theorems.

\begin{enumerate}
  \item For the left-multiplication row, closure places
  $\mathsf{mul}(a,x)$ and $\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{inv}(a),b)$ in $A_{\emp}$.
  Unpacking $R_{\emp}(\mathsf{mul}(a,x),b)$ exposes the $\hsame$ component
  $\mathsf{mul}(a,x)\hsame b$.

  Apply Theorem~\ref{thm:group-left-mul-equation-exactness} to this component.
  The forward direction gives
  $\hsame(x,\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{inv}(a),b))$, which repacks with the carrier
  rows as $R_{\emp}(x,\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{inv}(a),b))$.

  For the reverse direction, start from the unpacked component
  $\hsame(x,\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{inv}(a),b))$. The backward direction of
  Theorem~\ref{thm:group-left-mul-equation-exactness} gives
  $\mathsf{mul}(a,x)\hsame b$, and the same endpoint carrier rows repack it as
  $R_{\emp}(\mathsf{mul}(a,x),b)$.

  \item For the right-multiplication row, closure places
  $\mathsf{mul}(x,a)$ and $\mathsf{mul}(b,\mathsf{inv}(a))$ in $A_{\emp}$.
  Unpacking the classifier exposes the corresponding $\hsame$ equation.

  Apply Theorem~\ref{thm:group-right-mul-equation-exactness}. Its forward
  direction transports $\mathsf{mul}(x,a)\hsame b$ to
  $\hsame(x,\mathsf{mul}(b,\mathsf{inv}(a)))$, and carrier closure repacks the
  right-hand classifier.

  In the reverse direction, the same theorem transports the unpacked
  $\hsame(x,\mathsf{mul}(b,\mathsf{inv}(a)))$ component back to
  $\mathsf{mul}(x,a)\hsame b$. Endpoint carrier closure repacks the left-hand
  classifier.

  \item For the two-sided cancellation row, repeated multiplication closure
  places both displayed products in $A_{\emp}$. Unpacking the assumed
  classifier gives the $\hsame$ component between those two products.

  Apply Theorem~\ref{thm:group-two-sided-cancellation} to the $\hsame$
  component. The result is $\hsame(b,d)$, and the original carrier hypotheses
  $A_{\emp}(b)$ and $A_{\emp}(d)$ repack it as $R_{\emp}(b,d)$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{proof}
