\begin{theorem}[Group monoid semigroup sibling route]
\label{thm:group-monoid-semigroup-sibling-route}
Every public $\GroupUp$ operation read consumes the $\SemigroupUp$ associativity surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-semigroup-namecert} and the $\MonoidUp$ unit surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-monoid-namecert} before inverse rows are meaningful. The $\GroupUp$ packet adds inverse rows to those sibling obligations; it does not replace associativity, left unit, right unit, carrier transport, continuation, provenance, or local naming rows supplied by the semigroup and monoid certificates.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First read the binary operation as a semigroup operation. The associativity surface supplied by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-semigroup-namecert} is the row that makes iterated products stable under the public carrier classifier.

Next read the same operation through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-monoid-namecert}. The left and right unit rows make the empty-history unit available before inverse equations can be interpreted as group equations.

Finally attach the $\GroupUp$ inverse rows. They certify left and right inverse behavior inside the already associated and unital operation surface, while the structural transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows remain the sibling rows already consumed by the route.
\end{proof}
