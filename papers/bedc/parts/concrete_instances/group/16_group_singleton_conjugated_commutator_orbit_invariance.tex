\begin{theorem}[Group singleton conjugated commutator orbit invariance]
\label{thm:group-singleton-conjugated-commutator-orbit-invariance}
For histories $s,x,y:\mathsf{BHist}$ in the concrete singleton history group of
Definition~\ref{def:concrete-singleton-history-group-instance}, define
$$
\mathsf{Conj}_s(z):=\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(s,z),\mathsf{inv}(s))
$$
and use $\mathsf{Comm}_{\emp}$ as in
\autoref{thm:group-singleton-commutator-terminal-collapse}. If
$A_{\emp}(s)$, $A_{\emp}(x)$, and $A_{\emp}(y)$, then
$$
\mathsf{Comm}_{\emp}(\mathsf{Conj}_s(x),\mathsf{Conj}_s(y))
\sim_{N_{\emp}}
\mathsf{Comm}_{\emp}(x,y)
\land
\mathsf{Comm}_{\emp}(x,y)
\sim_{N_{\emp}}
\mathsf{Comm}_{\emp}(\mathsf{Conj}_s(x),\mathsf{Conj}_s(y)).
$$
The ten-field fit is: traditional target terminal commutator conjugation on
normalizer orbits; BEDC source concrete singleton $\GroupUp$ over
$\mathsf{BHist}$; carrier $A_{\emp}$; classifier the normalizer orbit relation
exact against $R_{\emp}$; stability from conjugated commutator classifier
invariance and terminal classifier exactness; ledger the conjugator and two
commutator inputs; core theorem this block; exactness the displayed bidirectional
orbit relation; downstream consumer $\QuotientGroupUp$; status
$\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply Theorem~\ref{thm:group-singleton-conjugated-commutator-invariance} to
$s$, $x$, and $y$. It gives the classifier
$$
R_{\emp}(\mathsf{Comm}_{\emp}(\mathsf{Conj}_s(x),\mathsf{Conj}_s(y)),
\mathsf{Comm}_{\emp}(x,y))
$$
and the two classifiers against $\emp$.

The terminal classifier package turns those endpoint classifiers into carrier
facts. Concretely, Theorem~\ref{thm:group-singleton-terminal-classifier-package}
applied to each commutator endpoint and $\emp$ yields
$A_{\emp}(\mathsf{Comm}_{\emp}(\mathsf{Conj}_s(x),\mathsf{Conj}_s(y)))$ and
$A_{\emp}(\mathsf{Comm}_{\emp}(x,y))$.

Use the classifier-to-orbit direction of
Theorem~\ref{thm:group-singleton-normalizer-orbit-exactness} with these two
carrier facts. The displayed $R_{\emp}$ row becomes
$\mathsf{Comm}_{\emp}(\mathsf{Conj}_s(x),\mathsf{Conj}_s(y))\sim_{N_{\emp}}
\mathsf{Comm}_{\emp}(x,y)$.

For the reverse orientation, apply classifier symmetry from
Theorem~\ref{thm:group-singleton-terminal-classifier-package} to the same
$R_{\emp}$ row. Orbit exactness then converts the symmetric classifier into
$\mathsf{Comm}_{\emp}(x,y)\sim_{N_{\emp}}
\mathsf{Comm}_{\emp}(\mathsf{Conj}_s(x),\mathsf{Conj}_s(y))$.
\end{proof}
