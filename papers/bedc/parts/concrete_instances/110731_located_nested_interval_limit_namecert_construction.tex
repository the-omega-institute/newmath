\begin{definition}[Located nested-interval limit carrier]
\label{def:located-nested-interval-limit-carrier}
A $\LocatedNestedIntervalLimitUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(I,D,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $I$ is the finite nested located-interval window. It records the inspected interval chain, endpoint rows, located containment witnesses, and the finite separation reads available at each displayed stage.
\item $D$ is the dyadic shrink ledger. It records the dyadic endpoint comparisons, width bounds, and tolerance requests read through $\DyadicRatCoreUp$.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule row. It names exactly the finite endpoint, midpoint, and shrink-ledger observations that may be queried before regular rational readback.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row obtained from the scheduled located-interval observations and the dyadic shrink ledger.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row, visible only after $I,D,S,R$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for interval windows, shrink ledgers, stream schedules, regular rational handoffs, real seals, replay rows, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a located nested-interval request through $I,D,S,R,E$ and then through the structural rows.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LocatedNestedIntervalLimitUp$, $\NestedDyadicCauchyUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedNestedIntervalLimitUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $I,D,S,R,E,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for completed interval intersections, quotient stream equality, host real equality, selected limits, countable choice, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Located nested-interval limit NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:located-nested-interval-limit-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\LocatedNestedIntervalLimitUp$ carrier
$$
L=(I,D,S,R,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedNestedIntervalLimitUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the finite located interval window, dyadic shrink ledger, StreamName schedule, RegSeqRat handoff, Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item located nesting exposure, requiring every interval read to be one of the displayed rows of $I$ together with its displayed containment and located separation witnesses;
\item dyadic shrink exactness, requiring every tolerance request to be discharged by a visible width or endpoint comparison stored in $D$ and read through $\DyadicRatCoreUp$;
\item schedule compatibility, requiring $S$ to expose only the finite interval, endpoint, midpoint, and ledger observations used by $D$ and $R$;
\item RegSeqRat handoff exactness, requiring $R$ to be formed only after the interval window, shrink ledger, and schedule rows have been projected;
\item Real-seal nonescape, requiring $E$ to be consumed only after $I,D,S,R$ and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$ have been displayed.
\end{enumerate}
No local obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:located-nested-interval-limit-carrier}. The projection exposes one finite located nested-interval window, one dyadic shrink ledger, one StreamName schedule, one RegSeqRat handoff, one Real seal, and the transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows.

Located nesting exposure is forced by $I$. A consumer may inspect only interval rows already present in the finite window, and each such row carries its displayed containment and located separation witnesses.

Dyadic shrink exactness is forced by $D$. Each tolerance request is answered by a displayed dyadic width or endpoint comparison, so no host intersection or selected limit is needed to choose a tail.

The schedule and handoff rows are downstream. The row $S$ records the finite observations that may be opened, and $R$ repacks exactly those observations under the shrink ledger as a regular rational Cauchy surface.

Finally $E,H,C,P,N$ close the route. The Real seal is reachable only after the regular handoff has been displayed, while the structural rows only transport, replay, source, and name the same finite data. Therefore no completed intersection, quotient stream equality, host real equality, countable choice, ambient completeness, Lean verification, or bridge-checking coordinate is present.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Located nested-interval RegSeqRat handoff]
\label{thm:located-nested-interval-limit-regseqrat-handoff}
Let $L=(I,D,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\LocatedNestedIntervalLimitUp$ carrier. Every $\RegSeqRatUp$ consumer read exported by $L$ factors through the displayed route
$$
I\longmapsto D\longmapsto S\longmapsto R\longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
The route reads a finite located nested-interval window, a dyadic shrink ledger, and the scheduled endpoint observations before it exports the regular rational Cauchy row. The terminal $\RealUp$ seal $E$ is available only after this handoff has been displayed.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the displayed located interval window $I$. Its rows give the only endpoint, midpoint, containment, and located separation data available to a regular rational consumer.

Use the dyadic shrink ledger $D$ to answer the displayed tolerance request. The chosen comparison is part of the same finite packet and is read through $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, so there is no external modulus, choice-selected tail, or host interval intersection.

The schedule row $S$ then fixes which interval observations are opened. The handoff row $R$ is obtained by reading those scheduled observations under the shrink discipline and repacking them as the $\RegSeqRatUp$ surface.

The rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name that handoff, and the seal row $E$ remains downstream of it. Since the carrier has no quotient-stream, host-real, selected-limit, choice, or completeness coordinate, every regular rational read factors through the displayed route.
\end{proof}
