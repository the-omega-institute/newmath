\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ApproachSpaceUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-approach-space-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Lowen, the $\ApproachSpaceUp$ packet records convergence by finite distance-to-set and tolerance observations rather than by a quotient completion or a selected limit object. It sits between the metric carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the open-neighbourhood carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-topology-namecert}, and the Cauchy-filter completion boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyfiltercompletion-namecert}. The packet names an L10 convergence object that can be consumed by metric, topology, and filter-completion routes while keeping quotient points, host real equality, countable choice, and ambient completeness outside the carrier.

\begin{definition}[ApproachSpace carrier]
\label{def:approach-space-carrier}
An $\ApproachSpaceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
A=(X,D,T,F,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the point row, naming the finite point observations and the local classifier surface used by later convergence consumers.
\item $D$ is the distance-to-set and tolerance row, recording displayed requests of the form "the observed point is within the listed tolerance of the listed observed set" without importing a host-valued distance function on all subsets.
\item $T$ is the topology-neighbourhood row, recording the open-neighbourhood observations generated by the displayed tolerances and the topology-facing classifier inherited from $\TopologyUp$.
\item $F$ is the Cauchy-filter handoff row, naming which finite Cauchy-filter requests may consume $D$ and $T$ before a completion-facing read reaches $\CauchyFilterCompletionUp$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for point observations, distance-to-set tolerances, topology-neighbourhood rows, Cauchy-filter handoffs, replay, provenance, and local naming.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a convergence query through $X,D,T,F,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ApproachSpaceUp$, $\MetricSpaceUp$, $\TopologyUp$, $\CauchyFilterCompletionUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ApproachSpaceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier of an accepted packet compares convergence observations only through $X,D,T,F,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for quotient completion points, equality in a host real line, countable-choice selectors, ambient completeness, or a hidden global distance-to-all-subsets oracle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[ApproachSpace NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:approach-space-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\ApproachSpaceUp$ carrier
$$
A=(X,D,T,F,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\ApproachSpaceUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the point row, distance-to-set tolerance row, topology-neighbourhood row, Cauchy-filter handoff row, componentwise transport, displayed replay, provenance, and local naming;
\item classifier transport, requiring every accepted convergence comparison to preserve $X,D,T,F$ through the displayed $\hsame$ row $H$;
\item metric and topology compatibility, requiring metric-origin tolerance reads to enter through $D$ and topology-origin neighbourhood reads to enter through $T$ before they are compared as one approach-space observation;
\item Cauchy-filter handoff, requiring completion-facing reads to pass through $F$ before any $\CauchyFilterCompletionUp$ consumer uses the packet;
\item non-escape, excluding quotient points, host $\RealUp$ equality, countable choice, ambient completeness, selected completion representatives, and any hidden bridge claim.
\end{enumerate}
No local obligation is stored outside these five rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:approach-space-carrier}. The finite tuple displays point observations, distance-to-set tolerances, topology-neighbourhood observations, the Cauchy-filter handoff, componentwise transport, replay, provenance, and local naming.

The metric and topology routes meet only at the displayed classifier surface. A metric-origin read must expose $D$, while a topology-origin read must expose $T$; transport through $H$ preserves both rows componentwise before the replay row $C$ compares them as one convergence observation.

The completion-facing route is similarly visible. The row $F$ is the only Cauchy-filter handoff coordinate, so a completion consumer reaches $\CauchyFilterCompletionUp$ only after reading the finite rows named by $F$ together with the same transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

Finally inspect the carrier inventory. No coordinate stores quotient completion points, host real equality, countable-choice selectors, selected representatives, ambient completeness, or bridge data. Hence the listed obligations exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ApproachSpace completion handoff]
\label{thm:approach-space-completion-handoff}
For every accepted $\ApproachSpaceUp$ carrier
$$
A=(X,D,T,F,H,C,P,N),
$$
every Cauchy-filter completion consumer must read distance-to-set tolerances through $D$, topology-neighbourhood observations through $T$, and the Cauchy-filter handoff through $F$ before reaching any completion-facing row. The shared finite convergence packet is therefore
$$
D,T \longmapsto F \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
It exports no quotient completion point, selected representative, host $\RealUp$ equality, countable-choice selector, or ambient completeness row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:approach-space-carrier}. The rows $D,T,F$ are the only distance-to-set, topology-neighbourhood, and Cauchy-filter handoff coordinates available to a completion-facing consumer.

Apply the metric and topology compatibility clause of \autoref{thm:approach-space-namecert-obligations}. A metric-origin tolerance read must enter through $D$, and a topology-origin neighbourhood read must enter through $T$, before the packet treats them as one approach-space observation.

The Cauchy-filter handoff clause then forces the completion-facing read through $F$. The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ only transport, replay, source, and name that same finite packet.

Finally use the non-escape clause from the obligation theorem. Since the carrier contains no quotient completion point, selected representative, host real equality, countable-choice selector, or ambient completeness coordinate, none of those objects can be reached after the handoff.
\end{proof}
