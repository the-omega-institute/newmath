\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\DiniDerivativeWindowUp$}

\origin{human}

Following Saks and Bishop--Bridges constructive analysis, the $\DiniDerivativeWindowUp$ packet records the finite window surface by which a real-valued interval observation exposes upper and lower Dini rows before any differentiability-boundary consumer may read them. It sits below the broader Denjoy--Young--Saks boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-denjoy-young-saks-namecert}, and it uses the $\RealUp$ seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the $\StreamNameUp$ observation schedule of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, and the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}. The chapter names only a finite Dini-window carrier; it does not claim measure-theoretic alternatives, almost-everywhere differentiability, selected derivatives, or ambient real completeness.

\begin{definition}[DiniDerivativeWindow carrier]
\label{def:dini-derivative-window-carrier}
A $\DiniDerivativeWindowUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
W=(I,F,U,L,O,S,R,D,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $I$ is the interval-cell row naming the inspected source cell and its endpoint readback surface.
\item $F$ is the function-observation window row, storing the finite source-target observations visible at the inspected cell.
\item $U$ is the upper Dini row, containing the displayed upper right, upper left, and two-sided upper difference-quotient bounds for the window.
\item $L$ is the lower Dini row, containing the corresponding lower right, lower left, and two-sided lower bounds.
\item $O$ is the oscillation cell, recording the finite comparison between the upper and lower rows at the displayed tolerance scale.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule that orders the finite observations.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row for the scheduled rational observations.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row used to compare the windowed difference quotients.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal visible only after $I,F,U,L,O,S,R,D$ have been replayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the interval, observation, Dini-row, oscillation, schedule, readback, tolerance, seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a Dini-window request through finite observations, regular readback, dyadic tolerance, and the Real seal.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\DiniDerivativeWindowUp$, $\RealUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DiniDerivativeWindowUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a measure structure, an almost-everywhere classifier, a selected derivative, quotient real equality, trichotomy, countable choice, or ambient completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[DiniDerivativeWindow NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:dini-derivative-window-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\DiniDerivativeWindowUp$ carrier $W=(I,F,U,L,O,S,R,D,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\DiniDerivativeWindowUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the interval cell, function-observation window, upper Dini row, lower Dini row, oscillation cell, StreamName schedule, RegSeqRat readback, DyadicRatCore tolerance, Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item Dini-row separation, requiring the upper row $U$ and lower row $L$ to remain distinct visible rows before an oscillation cell can compare them;
\item schedule-readback exactness, requiring every inspected observation to pass through $S$ and $R$ before the tolerance ledger $D$ is consulted;
\item Real-seal handoff, requiring $E$ to remain downstream of the finite Dini window and never upstream of it;
\item ledger refusal, excluding measure-theoretic alternatives, almost-everywhere claims, selected derivatives, quotient real equality, trichotomy, countable choice, and ambient completeness.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:dini-derivative-window-carrier}. The packet displays exactly the interval, observation, upper Dini, lower Dini, oscillation, schedule, readback, tolerance, Real-seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

The Dini-row separation is forced by the row list. The oscillation row $O$ reads both $U$ and $L$, but neither row is identified with the other, and neither can be replaced by the schedule, readback, tolerance, or seal rows.

Replay by $C$ gives the only consumer route. A request passes through $I,F,U,L,O,S,R,D$ before it can reach $E$, while $H,P,N$ transport, source, and name the same route. Hence no off-packet measure principle, selected derivative, quotient equality, or completeness coordinate can supply an additional obligation.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DiniDerivativeWindow Real-seal nonescape]
\label{thm:dini-derivative-window-real-seal-nonescape}
Every $\RealUp$-, $\RegSeqRatUp$-, or $\StreamNameUp$-facing read of an accepted $\DiniDerivativeWindowUp$ packet factors through the finite route
$$
I,F,U,L,O,S,R,D \longmapsto E \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
Thus a consumer can inspect interval cells, function-observation windows, upper and lower Dini rows, oscillation cells, StreamName schedules, RegSeqRat readbacks, DyadicRatCore tolerance rows, a terminal Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming. It cannot read a measure structure, an almost-everywhere theorem, a selected derivative, quotient real equality, trichotomy, countable choice, or ambient completeness from the packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with any read aimed at the Real seal, regular readback, or stream schedule. Carrier admission exposes those rows only as components of the packet in \autoref{def:dini-derivative-window-carrier}.

Use the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:dini-derivative-window-namecert-obligations}. The request must pass through the displayed interval cell, function-observation window, separated upper and lower Dini rows, oscillation cell, schedule, readback, and dyadic tolerance ledger before the Real seal can be read.

The structural rows preserve the same dependency. Transport by $H$ and replay by $C$ cannot add a hidden measure space, selected derivative, quotient equality, trichotomy, choice principle, or completeness theorem, while $P$ and $N$ source and name only the displayed finite route.
\end{proof}
