\begin{corollary}[ApophaticName root citation safety strict obstruction]
\label{cor:apophatic-name-root-citation-safety-strict-obstruction}
For every accepted $\ApophaticNameUp$ packet
$$
A=(S,R,G,L,H,C,P,N),
$$

\end{corollary}
\begin{proof}

It says that every root citation exported by the accepted packet names only the socket, request, refusal gate, refusal ledger, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows $S,R,G,L,H,C,P,N$. Thus a consumer-facing citation has no exported coordinate for a generated witness, host object, quotient representative, hidden theorem, supplied dependency, assumed proposition, closure principle, or hidden external validation.

Therefore any displayed failure, refusal, separation, or countermodel-style row that a root consumer can cite is read as part of that same refusal route, not as a second source of theorem content or host-side evidence.

The strict obstruction is the separation between a permitted citation and a barred rereading. In the permitted direction, the consumer may cite the exported source through $S,R,G,L,H,C,P,N$. In the barred converse or reuse direction, the consumer tries to turn that cited route back into supplied content, a host theorem, a quotient representative, a hidden theorem, an assumed proposition, a closure principle, or hidden external validation. The export theorem removes every off-packet citation coordinate, while route exhaustion removes every post-gate escape from the refusal rows.

Finally, root citation safety and the readback package, \autoref{thm:apophatic-name-root-citation-safety} and \autoref{thm:apophatic-name-root-citation-readback-package}, keep this conclusion at the root boundary. They identify the public readback surface with the finite packet and do not add any claim about all ambient hosts or all possible external theories. Hence the obstruction blocks exactly the named converse or reuse route through the accepted packet and carries no global impossibility theorem, host theorem, or hidden external validation beyond $S,R,G,L,H,C,P,N$.
\end{proof}
