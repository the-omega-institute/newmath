The $\WeierstrassMTestUp$ interface names the finite majorant packet behind the Weierstrass M-test as used in Bishop--Bridges constructive analysis: uniform convergence is read from displayed majorant rows and finite tail ledgers. The chapter packages finite majorant rows for uniformly bounded series. \begin{definition}[Weierstrass M-test carrier]
\label{def:weierstrass-mtest-carrier}
A $\WeierstrassMTestUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
W=(F,M,B,T,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $F$ is a displayed family of $\FastConvergentSeriesUp$ summand windows, indexed only by the finite inspection rows named by the packet;
\item $M$ is the majorant $\FastConvergentSeriesUp$ packet whose tail rows supply the common comparison budget;
\item $B$ is the finite domination ledger, recording each inspected summand-window bound against the corresponding majorant row;
\item $T$ is the uniform tail ledger obtained by combining the domination ledger $B$ with the fast tail schedule of $M$;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row that reads the uniform tail ledger as regular rational convergence data;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached through $R$ and through no separate real-facing coordinate;
\item $H$ records the componentwise $\hsame$ transports for source windows, majorant rows, domination ledgers, tail ledgers, handoff rows, and seal rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which consumers read the source family, the majorant packet, the domination ledger, the uniform tail ledger, the regular handoff, and the real seal;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\FastConvergentSeriesUp$, $\DyadicTailBoundUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, and the finite rational comparison rows used by the ledger;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\WeierstrassMTestUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only through these displayed rows and their recorded $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ provenance.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Weierstrass M-test NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:weierstrass-mtest-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\WeierstrassMTestUp}$ surface consists of carrier admission, finite majorant domination, uniform tail exactness, $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff, and $\RealUp$ seal factorization for the carrier of \autoref{def:weierstrass-mtest-carrier}. \end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is the projection of the finite packet $W$. The displayed family $F$, majorant packet $M$, domination ledger $B$, tail ledger $T$, handoff row $R$, and seal row $E$ are all stored coordinates.

The majorant obligation projects $B$. Each inspected source summand window is paired with the corresponding comparison row of $M$, so the certificate exposes a finite domination record instead of an ambient boundedness theorem.

Uniform tail exactness projects $T$ and composes it with the $\FastConvergentSeriesUp$ tail surface of $M$ and the $\DyadicTailBoundUp$ budget rows. Repacking $T$ gives the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff $R$, and the $\RealUp$ seal $E$ then reads only through that handoff.

The rows $H$, $C$, $P$, and $N$ certify componentwise transport, consumer reads, dependency provenance, and local naming for the same packet. Since no coordinate names compact open covers, host completeness, quotient series, or a selected limiting sum, none can be exported by the certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Weierstrass M-test majorant tail exactness]
\label{thm:weierstrass-mtest-majorant-tail-exactness}
For every accepted carrier
$$
W=(F,M,B,T,R,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the uniform tail ledger $T$ is obtained by composing the finite domination ledger $B$ with the tail schedule of the majorant $\FastConvergentSeriesUp$ packet $M$. Every displayed source-family tail comparison consumed from $T$ therefore factors through the same majorant packet, the same finite domination rows, and the same $\DyadicTailBoundUp$ budget surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project $M$ and $B$ from the carrier. The packet $M$ supplies the fast-convergent majorant tail schedule, while $B$ records the inspected domination comparisons between the summand windows in $F$ and the majorant rows.

For a requested uniform precision, read the threshold supplied by $M$ and apply the domination rows in $B$ only over the finite inspected windows. The resulting comparison is recorded as the corresponding row of $T$.

The $\DyadicTailBoundUp$ budget rows certify that the tolerance used by the comparison is the displayed one. Thus a consumer of $T$ cannot replace the majorant packet, domination ledger, or tail budget by unrecorded boundedness data.
\end{proof}

A $\RegSeqRatUp$ consumer can read the resulting uniformly convergent name only through those rows and through the stored $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ provenance.

Repack that ledger as regular rational sequence evidence. The handoff retains the majorant schedule and domination rows because they are stored in the same $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet and reached by the displayed continuation routes.
