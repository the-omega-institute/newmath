The $\WeierstrassMTestUp$ interface names the finite majorant packet behind the Weierstrass M-test as used in Bishop--Bridges constructive analysis: uniform convergence is read from displayed majorant rows and finite tail ledgers. The chapter packages finite majorant rows for uniformly bounded series. It does not assert open-cover compactness, host completeness, quotient equality of function series, or an unrecorded infinite summation object.

The uniform-tail budget is consumed through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformcauchycriterion-namecert}, the regular-sequence handoff through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the real-facing seal through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. Thus the carrier below records the L10 handoff lattice already used by its majorant, tail, regularity, and real-seal rows rather than treating the M-test packet as a kernel-only island.

\begin{definition}[Weierstrass M-test carrier]
\label{def:weierstrass-mtest-carrier}
A $\WeierstrassMTestUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
W=(F,M,B,T,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $F$ is a displayed family of $\FastConvergentSeriesUp$ summand windows, indexed only by the finite inspection rows named by the packet;
\item $M$ is the majorant $\FastConvergentSeriesUp$ packet whose tail rows supply the common comparison budget;
\item $B$ is the finite domination ledger, recording each inspected summand-window bound against the corresponding majorant row;
\item $T$ is the uniform tail ledger obtained by combining the domination ledger $B$ with the fast tail schedule of $M$;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row that reads the uniform tail ledger as regular rational convergence data;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached through $R$ and through no separate real-facing coordinate;
\item $H$ records the componentwise $\hsame$ transports for source windows, majorant rows, domination ledgers, tail ledgers, handoff rows, and seal rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which consumers read the source family, the majorant packet, the domination ledger, the uniform tail ledger, the regular handoff, and the real seal;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\FastConvergentSeriesUp$, $\DyadicTailBoundUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, and the finite rational comparison rows used by the ledger;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\WeierstrassMTestUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only through these displayed rows and their recorded $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ provenance.
\end{definition}
