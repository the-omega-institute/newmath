\begin{definition}[Edelstein fixed-point carrier]\label{def:edelstein-fixed-point-carrier}
An $\EdelsteinFixedPointUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
E=(X,K,F,O,C,S,H,R,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the underlying $\MetricUp$ source row, carrying the point classifier and the distance observations inspected by the fixed-point consumer.
\item $K$ is the $\CompactMetricUp$ row over $X$, exposing only the finite compactness windows and cluster-service rows accepted by the compact-metric certificate.
\item $F$ is the self-map row, presented through the same metric carrier and never as an untracked host function.
\item $O$ is the orbit row generated from $F$, recording the displayed Picard-style finite prefix consumed by the fixed-point search.
\item $C$ is the strict contraction-comparison row: whenever the inspected points are classified as distinct by the metric row, the displayed distance from $F(x)$ to $F(y)$ is strictly below the displayed distance from $x$ to $y$.
\item $S$ is the $\SequentialContinuityUp$ row for $F$, binding cluster reads of the orbit to the image row without adding a semantic continuity theorem outside the displayed packet.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for metric, compactness, self-map, orbit, contraction-comparison, sequential-continuity, continuation, provenance, and naming rows.
\item $R$ stores displayed $\Cont$ replay from the orbit row to the compact cluster row, from the cluster row through sequential continuity, and from the strict comparison row to the fixed-point equality boundary.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\EdelsteinFixedPointUp$, $\MetricUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\SequentialContinuityUp$, $\BanachContractionUp$, $\PicardIterationUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\EdelsteinFixedPointUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $X,K,F,O,C,S,H,R,P,N$. It has no coordinate for quotient completion, countable choice, a Banach contraction constant, an ambient complete real line, or a host-selected fixed point.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Edelstein fixed-point NameCert obligations]\label{thm:edelstein-fixed-point-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\EdelsteinFixedPointUp$ carrier $E=(X,K,F,O,C,S,H,R,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\EdelsteinFixedPointUp}$ surface consists exactly of metric carrier admission through $X$, compact-metric finite inspection through $K$, self-map admission through $F$, Picard-style orbit exposure through $O$, strict contraction comparison through $C$, sequential-continuity cluster transport through $S$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $R$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:edelstein-fixed-point-carrier}. The metric row $X$ and compact-metric row $K$ are the only source rows available to the certificate, so every inspected point, distance comparison, and compactness read is routed through those two displayed surfaces.

The self-map row $F$ and orbit row $O$ provide the visible finite Picard-style prefix. The strict comparison row $C$ is separate from the Banach contraction surface: it records pointwise distance decrease on distinct displayed pairs and carries no global contraction ratio.

The sequential-continuity row $S$ is the only route from a compact cluster read of the orbit to an image read under $F$. Transport through $H$, replay through $R$, provenance through $P$, and naming through $N$ preserve this same finite route, so the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Edelstein compact-route nonescape]\label{thm:edelstein-fixed-point-compact-route-nonescape}
Every fixed-point read exported by an accepted $\EdelsteinFixedPointUp$ packet factors through the internal route
$$
X\longmapsto K\longmapsto O\longmapsto S\longmapsto C
$$
together with $F,H,R,P,N$. The route may compare with the $\BanachContractionUp$ and $\PicardIterationUp$ neighbours as named dependency rows, but it does not consume a Banach contraction constant, quotient completion equality, selected subsequence principle, choice operator, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a fixed-point read. The carrier first exposes the metric source $X$ and the compactness service $K$, so the consumer can only inspect displayed finite compact-metric windows and the point classifier attached to $X$.

The orbit row $O$ supplies the visible Picard-style finite prefix for the self-map $F$. Compactness is consumed only through $K$, and the image of a cluster read is transported only through the sequential-continuity row $S$.

The strict comparison row $C$ is then the only row that can rule out a non-fixed distinct-pair boundary. Since $C$ contains no global ratio and the remaining rows $H,R,P,N$ only transport, replay, source, and name the same finite data, the exported route cannot leave the displayed Edelstein packet for choice, quotient completion, or ambient completeness.
\end{proof}
