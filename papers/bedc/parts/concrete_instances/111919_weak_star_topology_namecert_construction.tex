It sits over the normed carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-normedspace-namecert}, the finite weak-topology window of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-weak-topology-namecert}, the bounded-linear-operator surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-boundedlinearoperator-namecert}, and the scalar seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. \begin{definition}[Weak-star topology carrier]
\label{def:weak-star-topology-carrier}
A $\WeakStarTopologyUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(V,W,L,T,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $V$ is the source $\NormedSpaceUp$ row whose points are read only through the displayed normed-space carrier and its scalar rows.
\item $W$ is the sibling $\WeakTopologyUp$ observation-window row, reused as the finite neighbourhood interface rather than as a completed dual topology.
\item $L$ is the finite bounded-linear-functional row. Each entry is a $\BoundedLinearOperatorUp$ read from $V$ to the displayed scalar line, not a selected element of an unrestricted dual space.
\item $T$ is the test-window row, listing the finitely many functional reads, source points, scalar tolerances, and comparison requests inspected by the consumer.
\item $R$ is the $\RealUp$ scalar readback row for observed functional values and their finite tolerance comparisons.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the normed source, weak-window interface, bounded-linear-functional rows, test windows, scalar readbacks, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a requested weak-star read through $T$, then through $L,V,W,R$, and finally through the structural rows.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\WeakStarTopologyUp$, $\NormedSpaceUp$, $\WeakTopologyUp$, $\BoundedLinearOperatorUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\WeakStarTopologyUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $V,W,L,T,R,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for Banach--Alaoglu compactness, selected dual elements, quotient function spaces, choice, or ambient real completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Weak-star topology NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:weak-star-topology-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\WeakStarTopologyUp$ carrier $S=(V,W,L,T,R,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\WeakStarTopologyUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the displayed $\NormedSpaceUp$ row $V$, sibling $\WeakTopologyUp$ window row $W$, finite bounded-linear-functional row $L$, test-window row $T$, scalar readback row $R$, componentwise transport $H$, displayed replay $C$, provenance $P$, and local naming $N$. Every public weak-star neighbourhood read must cite these rows before it reaches a scalar comparison.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:weak-star-topology-carrier}. The only source carrier is $V$, the only weak-neighbourhood interface is $W$, and the only functional data are the finitely listed bounded-linear reads in $L$.

The test-window row $T$ fixes which functional reads and source points are inspected. Scalar comparison then passes through $R$, so the public read receives finite $\RealUp$ observations rather than a selected dual element or a completed function space.

Transport, replay, provenance, and naming are supplied by $H,C,P,N$. Since the carrier contains no further coordinates, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Weak-star finite-window nonescape]
\label{thm:weak-star-topology-finite-window-nonescape}
Every scalar observation exported by an accepted $\WeakStarTopologyUp$ carrier factors through
$$
V,W,L,T,R,H,C,P,N .
$$
Thus a weak-star neighbourhood, convergence, or bounded-operator consumer may inspect only finite bounded-linear-functional windows and their $\RealUp$ scalar comparisons. It receives no Banach--Alaoglu compactness theorem, no selected dual element, no quotient function space, no choice principle, and no ambient $\RealUp$ completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with an exported scalar observation. By \autoref{thm:weak-star-topology-namecert-obligations}, the observation must expose the source row $V$, the weak-window row $W$, the finite functional row $L$, and the test-window row $T$.

The scalar row $R$ is the only route to real-valued readback. It records finite observed values and tolerance comparisons, not a completed dual-space object or a quotient of functions.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve and name that same route. Since the excluded compactness, selection, quotient, choice, and completeness principles have no carrier coordinate, they cannot be exported by the accepted packet.
\end{proof}

\label{thm:weak-star-topology-banach-alaoglu-boundary}

\begin{proof}
