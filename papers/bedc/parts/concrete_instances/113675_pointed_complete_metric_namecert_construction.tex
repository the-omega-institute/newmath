\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\PointedCompleteMetricUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-pointed-complete-metric-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges on complete metric spaces with a chosen origin, the
$\PointedCompleteMetricUp$ packet records a metric carrier, a completion
service, and a base-pointed Cauchy request route as one finite BEDC
NameCert surface. It sits next to the $\MetricUp$ carrier of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the
$\CompleteMetricUp$ completion row of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completemetric-namecert}, the $\RealUp$
distance seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the
$\RegSeqRatUp$ rational schedule of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the $\StreamNameUp$
window ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}. The
packet names the rooted complete-metric route without importing a selected host
point, quotient equality on completed points, open-cover compactness, countable
choice, or an ambient real-completeness theorem.

\begin{definition}[PointedCompleteMetric carrier]
\label{def:pointed-complete-metric-carrier}
A $\PointedCompleteMetricUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
P=(M,C,B,R,H,K,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the $\MetricSpaceUp$ carrier row, including the metric distance,
classifier, and local comparison data available to rooted Cauchy consumers.
\item $C$ is the $\CompleteMetricUp$ Cauchy-limit row, exposing the
completion service only for regular Cauchy requests already displayed over
$M$.
\item $B$ is the base-point row, stored as named metric data over $M$ rather
than as a host point or as a point in a quotient completion.
\item $R$ is the rooted Cauchy request row. It records the finite
$\StreamNameUp$ windows and $\RegSeqRatUp$ rational tolerance reads that start
at $B$ and are sealed by the $\RealUp$ distance rows before the completion
service $C$ is inspected.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the metric carrier,
completion row, base point, rooted Cauchy requests, provenance, replay, and
local naming rows.
\item $K$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a rooted Cauchy request
through $M,B,R,C,H$ and into the local certificate row.
\item $N$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance and local
$\NameCert_{\PointedCompleteMetricUp}$ row for exactly this displayed packet.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only by transporting these seven rows
together. It has no coordinate for a selected host point, a quotient-completion
representative, a choice-selected limit, open-cover compactness, or ambient
equality on real-valued distances.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[PointedCompleteMetric NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:pointed-complete-metric-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\PointedCompleteMetricUp$ carrier
$P=(M,C,B,R,H,K,N)$, the local
$\NameCert_{\PointedCompleteMetricUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier
admission for the $\MetricSpaceUp$ row $M$, completion-service admission for
$C$, base-point admission for $B$, rooted Cauchy request admission for $R$,
componentwise transport through $H$, replay through $K$, and provenance with
local naming through $N$. Each rooted request must display its
$\StreamNameUp$ windows, $\RegSeqRatUp$ tolerance reads, and $\RealUp$
distance seals before the $\CompleteMetricUp$ row is available.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:pointed-complete-metric-carrier}. The
rows $M,C,B,R,H,K,N$ are the only coordinates available to a downstream
NameCert consumer.

The rooted request is not a separate completion object. It begins at the
base-point row $B$, reads the stream windows and rational tolerance schedule
inside $R$, and reaches the completion service only through the row $C$
displayed over the same metric carrier $M$.

Transport and replay preserve the route componentwise. The row $H$ moves the
metric, completion, base-point, and rooted-request data together, while $K$
replays the same displayed path for consumers. Since $N$ records only
provenance and local naming for that packet, no unlisted host point,
representative selector, open-cover compactness principle, quotient equality,
or ambient real-completeness theorem can enter the certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PointedCompleteMetric completion anchor]
\label{thm:pointed-complete-metric-completion-anchor}
Let $P=(M,C,B,R,H,K,N)$ be an accepted
$\PointedCompleteMetricUp$ carrier. Every $\CompleteMetricUp$ completion read
exported by $P$ is anchored at the same $\MetricSpaceUp$ carrier $M$ and the
same base-point row $B$ used by the rooted Cauchy request row $R$. Thus a
completion consumer may inspect $C$ only after the route
$$
M,B,R \longmapsto C \longmapsto H,K,N
$$
has been displayed.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read $M,B,R$ from the carrier first. The base point is a row over the metric
carrier, and the rooted Cauchy request row is displayed over the same source.

The completion row is downstream of that route. By the carrier definition,
$C$ is the complete-metric service for requests already displayed over $M$,
not a detached complete object. Therefore a consumer cannot inspect $C$ before
the metric carrier and rooted base-point request are visible.

Finally apply transport and replay. The rows $H$ and $K$ preserve the order
$M,B,R,C$, and the naming row $N$ records only that finite packet. The
completion anchor is therefore the displayed rooted route, not a selected host
endpoint or quotient representative.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PointedCompleteMetric rooted Cauchy route]
\label{thm:pointed-complete-metric-rooted-cauchy-route}
Every rooted Cauchy consumer of an accepted $\PointedCompleteMetricUp$ packet
factors through the finite route
$$
\MetricSpaceUp,\ \StreamNameUp,\ \RegSeqRatUp,\ \RealUp,\ \CompleteMetricUp
\longmapsto M,B,R,C,H,K,N .
$$
The route supplies the base-pointed completion handoff needed by downstream
Cauchy and completion chapters, while refusing selected host points, selected
subsequences, quotient real equality, choice, open-cover compactness, and an
ambient completeness theorem for $\RealUp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the rooted request row. It records the stream windows and regular
rational tolerance reads over the displayed metric source and base point, so a
consumer receives only finite observations before completion is considered.

Next use the real seal and completion row. The distance observations are
sealed as $\RealUp$ data inside the same route, and the
$\CompleteMetricUp$ row is available only after those observations have been
displayed.

Transport, replay, provenance, and local naming do not add analytic content.
They preserve and name the finite route through $H,K,N$. Hence every rooted
Cauchy read factors through the displayed packet and cannot escape to selected
host points, selected subsequences, quotient equality, choice, open-cover
compactness, or ambient real completeness.
\end{proof}
