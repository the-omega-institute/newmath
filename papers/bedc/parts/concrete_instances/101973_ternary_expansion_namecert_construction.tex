Following Bishop--Bridges constructive analysis and Cantor's middle-third construction, the $\TernaryExpansionUp$ packet records finite base-three digit-prefix rows as displayed BEDC readback data. It does not store a host infinite ternary stream, quotient real, endpoint convention selector, or chosen representative. Its role is to route inspected base-three prefixes through the finite stream surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the base-three rational place-value ledger parallel to \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-decimalexpansion-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-binaryexpansion-namecert}, the dyadic comparison discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the regular rational handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the terminal $\RealUp$ seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, and the retained-third consumer surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cantorset-namecert}.

\begin{definition}[TernaryExpansion carrier]\label{def:ternary-expansion-carrier}
A $\TernaryExpansionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
T=(D,W,V,Q,R,A,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the finite ternary digit ledger, whose rows record inspected base-three digits together with any local sign or integral-prefix convention carried by the packet.
\item $W$ is the place-window ledger, listing the finite prefix windows and request precisions exposed to $\StreamNameUp$ consumers.
\item $V$ is the base-three rational place-value row that evaluates each inspected prefix as a rational sum with denominator a displayed power of three.
\item $Q$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance-comparison row that compares the base-three rational place-value row with the dyadic precision language consumed by downstream real readers.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row reached from the same digit, window, place-value, and dyadic-comparison rows.
\item $A$ is the optional $\CantorSetUp$ retained-prefix row, present exactly when the inspected digits in $D$ are the retained-third digits used by the Cantor set surface.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row exposed only after $D,W,V,Q,R$ and, when present, $A$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ is the componentwise $\hsame$ transport row for ternary digits, prefix windows, base-three place values, dyadic comparisons, regular handoff, retained-prefix handoff, and real-seal coordinates.
\item $C$ is the displayed $\Cont$ replay row that reads finite ternary windows, place-value rows, dyadic comparisons, handoff rows, retained-prefix rows, and real seals without adding a host stream.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\TernaryExpansionUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\CantorSetUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and the local naming row.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\TernaryExpansionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate accepts exactly such finite base-three readback packets. It has no coordinate for a completed ternary stream, quotient equality of expansions, endpoint uniqueness convention, selected representative, trichotomy principle, measure-theoretic normality row, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness witness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[TernaryExpansion RegSeqRat readback]\label{thm:ternary-expansion-regseqrat-readback}
Every $\RealUp$-facing read of an accepted $\TernaryExpansionUp$ carrier factors through the finite route
$$
\StreamNameUp,\ \TernaryExpansionUp,\ \DyadicRatCoreUp,\ \RegSeqRatUp,\ \RealUp .
$$
Concretely, the consumer first reads a finite ternary window, then the base-three place-value row for that window, then the dyadic comparison row, then the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff, and only then the displayed $\RealUp$ seal. The read uses no host infinite stream, quotient equality, selected representative, trichotomy decision, or $\RealUp$ completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted carrier $T=(D,W,V,Q,R,A,E,H,C,P,N)$. The $\StreamNameUp$ entrance is the finite window row $W$ together with the digit rows of $D$ selected by that window, so a real-facing consumer cannot read a ternary prefix before a finite observation window has been displayed.

The place-value row $V$ evaluates that same finite prefix as a rational sum with base-three denominators. The comparison row $Q$ is the only route from this base-three ledger into the dyadic tolerance discipline used by $\RegSeqRatUp$ and later real-name consumers.

The handoff row $R$ is therefore downstream of $D,W,V,Q$, and the seal row $E$ is downstream of $R$. The rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, package, and locally name the same finite route without adding any external stream or real representative.

Thus every real-facing read factors through the displayed finite window, place-value, dyadic-comparison, regular-handoff, and real-seal rows. The excluded host stream, quotient equality, representative choice, trichotomy, and completeness principles are not coordinates of the carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[TernaryExpansion CantorSet handoff]\label{thm:ternary-expansion-cantorset-handoff}
When all inspected digits in a $\TernaryExpansionUp$ prefix are retained-third digits, the finite ternary prefix factors through the $\CantorSetUp$ retained-prefix surface before any $\CantorSetUp$ or Cantor-function consumer reads it. The handoff uses the same digit ledger $D$, window ledger $W$, retained-prefix row $A$, transport row $H$, replay row $C$, provenance row $P$, and local naming row $N$, and it exports no host subset, selected infinite branch, quotient real equality, endpoint uniqueness convention, or $\RealUp$ completeness row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Assume the inspected prefix window of $W$ selects only retained-third digit rows from $D$. By the carrier definition, precisely in this case the optional row $A$ is present as the $\CantorSetUp$ retained-prefix handoff for that same finite window.

The continuation row $C$ reads the digit window before it reads $A$, and the transport row $H$ keeps the digit rows, the window, and the retained-prefix row componentwise attached. Hence a $\CantorSetUp$ consumer receives the same finite prefix, not a separate branch object.

The provenance row $P$ and local certificate row $N$ package the handoff over $\TernaryExpansionUp$ and $\CantorSetUp$. Any later Cantor-set or Cantor-function consumer therefore enters through the retained-prefix row already displayed by the ternary packet.

No host subset, selected infinite branch, quotient equality, endpoint convention, or completeness row is listed in the carrier, so none can be exported by this handoff.
\end{proof}
