\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CauchyFilterBasisUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-filter-basis-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop--Bridges constructive analysis and the Bourbaki--Kelley terminology of filter bases, the $\CauchyFilterBasisUp$ packet records the finite basis generator that sits between regular Cauchy windows and saturated Cauchy-filter evidence. It reads the finite windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readbacks of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the dyadic tolerance rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}; it hands the resulting directed basis to the saturation surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyfilter-namecert} before any $\RealUp$ seal from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert} can be inspected. The packet is a finite generator interface, not a completed filter, not a quotient of Cauchy names, and not a host real equality principle.

\begin{definition}[Cauchy filter basis carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-filter-basis-carrier}
A $\CauchyFilterBasisUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(B,S,R,D,F,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $B$ is the finite list of basis elements. Each basis element names a nonempty finite family of late-window neighbourhood requests over the same observation surface.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window row. It records the finite stream windows inspected by the basis elements and the tail indices at which those windows become available.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row. It turns the displayed stream windows in $S$ into regular rational observations before any filter-basis comparison is made.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row. It stores the dyadic radius and inclusion ledger used to compare the rational observations carried by $R$.
\item $F$ is the $\CauchyFilterUp$ saturation handoff row. It consumes the directed basis generated by $B,S,R,D$ and exposes it only as the finite-filterbase surface already named by $\CauchyFilterUp$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $B,S,R,D,F$ and for the continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a basis request through $S$, $R$, $D$, and then the saturation handoff $F$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\CauchyFilterUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyFilterBasisUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a completed filter object, quotient stream equality, selected representatives, countable choice, trichotomy, host real equality, or completeness of $\RealUp$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Cauchy filter basis classifier]
\label{def:cauchy-filter-basis-classifier}
The classifier of an accepted $\CauchyFilterBasisUp$ packet relates two basis reads precisely when their finite basis lists $B$ admit a common displayed refinement whose $\StreamNameUp$ windows, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readbacks, and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance rows agree after the componentwise $\hsame$ transport recorded in $H$, and when the resulting saturation handoff is the same $\CauchyFilterUp$ row $F$ after replay by $C$.

Equivalently, comparison is the finite common-refinement route
$$
B,S,R,D \longmapsto F \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
It is not equality of completed filters, not equality of quotient Cauchy names, and not equality of host real limits.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy filter basis NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-filter-basis-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CauchyFilterBasisUp$ carrier $Q=(B,S,R,D,F,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyFilterBasisUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the finite basis elements, $\StreamNameUp$ windows, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readbacks, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance rows, $\CauchyFilterUp$ saturation handoff, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming row;
\item common-refinement classification for two basis reads, using only the displayed basis elements and their transported window, readback, and tolerance rows;
\item directedness of the generated basis, requiring every pair of displayed basis elements to name a third displayed basis element refining both after the $S,R,D$ rows are replayed;
\item saturation exactness, requiring the handoff to $\CauchyFilterUp$ to consume the generated finite basis before any filter-facing or real-facing consumer can proceed;
\item ledger non-escape, excluding completed filters, quotient streams, selected representatives, countable choice, trichotomy, host real equality, and $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored outside those rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-filter-basis-carrier}. The projection exposes exactly the finite basis list, the window row, the regular readback row, the dyadic tolerance row, the saturation handoff, and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

Classifier transport is componentwise. The rows in $H$ move the basis elements, windows, rational readbacks, dyadic tolerances, and saturation handoff to a common finite comparison surface, while $C$ replays the same dependency order.

Directedness is read from the finite list $B$ together with the tolerance ledger $D$. For two displayed basis elements, the accepted packet names a common refinement whose windows and rational observations are routed through $S$ and $R$ before dyadic inclusion is checked in $D$.

Saturation exactness is the role of $F$. Since every consumer reaches $F$ only after the basis, window, readback, and tolerance rows have been replayed, the package and local naming rows cannot export any completed-filter, quotient, choice, host-equality, or real-completeness principle.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy filter basis RegSeqRat handoff]
\label{thm:cauchy-filter-basis-regseqrat-handoff}
Let $Q=(B,S,R,D,F,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyFilterBasisUp$ packet. Every regular-rational consumer of a displayed basis element factors through the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window row $S$, then through the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row $R$, then through the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row $D$, before the generated basis can be handed to $\CauchyFilterUp$ by $F$.

The handoff route is
$$
B \longmapsto S \longmapsto R \longmapsto D \longmapsto F \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
It does not bypass the regular readback row and does not select a Cauchy representative or host real limit.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a consumer of a displayed basis element. Carrier admission lists such a consumer under $B$, but the basis element only stores a finite neighbourhood request, not a rational observation by itself.

Replay the continuation row $C$. The request first reaches the stream-window row $S$, where the relevant finite observations and tail indices are displayed.

The next row is $R$. It turns the finite stream observations into $\RegSeqRatUp$ readbacks, after which the dyadic tolerance row $D$ checks the requested radius and inclusion ledger.

Only then can the same generated basis be consumed by $F$ as a $\CauchyFilterUp$ saturation handoff. The remaining rows transport, package, and name that same route, so no bypass through a selected representative, quotient stream, host real limit, or choice principle is available.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy filter basis Real seal non-escape]
\label{thm:cauchy-filter-basis-real-seal-nonescape}
For every accepted $\CauchyFilterBasisUp$ carrier $Q=(B,S,R,D,F,H,C,P,N)$, any $\RealUp$-facing read exposed by the provenance row $P$ factors through the $\CauchyFilterUp$ saturation handoff $F$ after the finite basis route $B,S,R,D$ has already been replayed. Therefore the packet cannot export a $\RealUp$ seal directly from basis elements, stream windows, rational readbacks, or dyadic tolerance rows.

The only real-facing route visible in the packet is
$$
B,S,R,D \longmapsto F \longmapsto P,N .
$$
It is a non-escape condition for the basis generator: the chapter names finite basis data and its saturation handoff, not a completed real, not a chosen limit, and not a proof that $\RealUp$ is complete.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the provenance row $P$. Its $\RealUp$ occurrence is recorded only as a downstream boundary of the $\CauchyFilterUp$ handoff, not as a coordinate of the basis rows $B,S,R,D$.

The continuation row $C$ fixes the route order. A real-facing read must first replay the basis element, stream window, regular readback, and dyadic tolerance rows, and then pass through the saturation handoff $F$.

Transport by $H$ preserves the same order under classifier comparison. The local naming row $N$ names the packet only after this replay, so no separate real seal can be read from an earlier coordinate.

Thus every $\RealUp$-facing route remains downstream of $\CauchyFilterUp$. The packet carries no completed real, selected limit, host equality, countable-choice schedule, trichotomy, or real-completeness certificate.
\end{proof}
