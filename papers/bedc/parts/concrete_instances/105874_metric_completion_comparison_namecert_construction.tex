Following Bishop and Bridges and Kelley on constructive metric completions, the $\MetricCompletionComparisonUp$ packet records the finite comparison route by which two dense completions of the same metric source are read as the same completion surface. It sits after the universal-property surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-completion-universal-property-namecert}, the dense-source row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-completion-density-source-namecert}, the metric dense-image packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-completion-dense-embedding-namecert}, the regular Cauchy sequence-space packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-sequence-space-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the finite stream-window surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet names constructive uniqueness of dense metric completions through displayed comparison rows; it is not a quotient representative theorem, not a selected-limit operator, not countable choice, and not an ambient completeness assertion for $\RealUp$.

\begin{definition}[MetricCompletionComparison carrier]
\label{def:metric-completion-comparison-carrier}
A $\MetricCompletionComparisonUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(U,D,E_0,E_1,S,R,W,Q,A,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $U$ is the $\CauchyCompletionUniversalPropertyUp$ row used twice, once to extend the first dense embedding into the second completion and once in the opposite direction.
\item $D$ is the $\CauchyCompletionDensitySourceUp$ row that fixes the common dense source before either comparison map is read.
\item $E_0$ and $E_1$ are $\MetricCompletionDenseEmbeddingUp$ rows for the two displayed dense completions of the same metric source.
\item $S$ is the $\RegularCauchySequenceSpaceUp$ row recording the common regular Cauchy sequence-space interface inspected by both completions.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row attached to the finite regular rational windows of $S$.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window schedule shared by the source, the two dense embeddings, and the comparison maps.
\item $Q$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger carrying the precision budgets used to compare the two completion reads.
\item $A$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after $D,E_0,E_1,S,R,W,Q$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the dense-source, dense-embedding, sequence-space, readback, window, tolerance, seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the two comparison directions and of the round-trip reads through the same finite rows.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\MetricCompletionComparisonUp$, the eight sibling chapters named above, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MetricCompletionComparisonUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $U,D,E_0,E_1,S,R,W,Q,A,H,C,P,N$ and their displayed replay paths. It has no coordinate for quotient representatives, selected Cauchy limits, host equality of completion classes, countable choice, trichotomy, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[MetricCompletionComparison NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:metric-completion-comparison-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\MetricCompletionComparisonUp$ carrier $K=(U,D,E_0,E_1,S,R,W,Q,A,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\MetricCompletionComparisonUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the universal-property row, common dense-source row, paired dense embeddings, regular Cauchy sequence-space row, regular rational readback, stream-window schedule, dyadic tolerance ledger, terminal real seal, componentwise transport, displayed replay, provenance, and local naming. The two comparison directions must read the same $D,S,R,W,Q,A$ rows before either round-trip read is accepted.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:metric-completion-comparison-carrier}. The rows $D,E_0,E_1,S,R,W,Q,A$ display the common source, the paired dense embeddings, the regular Cauchy sequence-space interface, the finite readback windows, the dyadic tolerances, and the terminal real-name seal.

Apply the universal-property row $U$ in the two displayed directions. The first use extends $E_0$ across the dense source into the second completion surface, and the second use extends $E_1$ back across the same dense source. Both uses are forced to read the same dense-source, sequence-space, regular-readback, stream-window, tolerance, and seal rows.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ then preserve, replay, source, and name that finite route. Since the carrier contains no row for quotient representatives, selected limits, host equality, countable choice, trichotomy, or ambient real completeness, no additional NameCert obligation can be projected from the packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetricCompletionComparison universal route]
\label{thm:metric-completion-comparison-universal-route}
Every constructive comparison between the two dense completions named by an accepted $\MetricCompletionComparisonUp$ packet factors through the finite route
$$
\MetricCompletionDenseEmbeddingUp,\ \CauchyCompletionDensitySourceUp,\ \RegularCauchySequenceSpaceUp,\ \RegSeqRatUp,\ \StreamNameUp,\ \DyadicRatCoreUp,\ \RealUp \longmapsto \CauchyCompletionUniversalPropertyUp .
$$
The exported result is the displayed pair of comparison maps with finite round-trip evidence on the common dense source. It is not a quotient-completion equality proof, a selected representative, a selected limiting operator, a countable-choice principle, or an ambient $\RealUp$ completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the obligation surface of \autoref{thm:metric-completion-comparison-namecert-obligations}. A completion-comparison consumer must first read the paired dense-embedding rows $E_0,E_1$ and the common dense-source row $D$.

The finite observation route is then fixed by $S,R,W,Q,A$. The regular Cauchy sequence-space row supplies the shared source discipline, the regular rational and stream-window rows supply the inspected finite windows, the dyadic row supplies the precision ledger, and the real seal is reached only after those rows are displayed.

Finally use the two universal-property reads stored in $U$. They produce comparison maps in both directions and check the round-trip evidence only on the displayed dense source. Transport and replay through $H$ and $C$, with provenance and local naming through $P,N$, preserve the same route and export no excluded host-side principle.
\end{proof}
