\begin{definition}[RealSequenceCauchyCriterion carrier]
\label{def:real-sequence-cauchy-criterion-carrier}
A $\RealSequenceCauchyCriterionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(L,S,R,D,T,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $L$ is the accepted $\RealSequenceLimitUp$ row naming the displayed sequence, candidate limit, and finite convergence classifier;
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule that lists the inspected sequence windows and limit windows for each requested tolerance;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row for the scheduled sequence and limit observations;
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger containing the tolerance rows and rational comparison cells used by the tail-window test;
\item $T$ is the finite Cauchy tail-window classifier: each accepted tolerance request is assigned one displayed tail index and the paired windows used to compare two later sequence reads through $R$ and $D$;
\item $E$ is the $\RegularCauchyEquivalenceUp$ comparison boundary that records the two sequence reads as a nonquotient regular-Cauchy comparison before any $\RealUp$ handoff;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $L,S,R,D,T,E$ and the local naming rows;

\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[RealSequenceCauchyCriterion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:real-sequence-cauchy-criterion-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RealSequenceCauchyCriterionUp$ carrier $K=(L,S,R,D,T,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\RealSequenceCauchyCriterionUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission, limit-row exposure through $L$, finite window exposure through $S$, regular rational readback through $R$, dyadic tolerance comparison through $D$, Cauchy tail-window classification through $T$, nonquotient regular-Cauchy comparison through $E$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, local naming, and ledger refusal for quotient streams, selected representatives, countable choice, trichotomy, and ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:real-sequence-cauchy-criterion-carrier}. The accepted packet exposes the limit row, stream schedule, regular rational readback, dyadic ledger, Cauchy classifier, comparison boundary, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

The limit-row exposure is finite because $L$ is consumed only through the scheduled windows $S$. For each requested tolerance, the convergence classifier already present in $\RealSequenceLimitUp$ supplies a displayed tail window comparing later sequence reads to the candidate limit.

The Cauchy classifier $T$ reads two later sequence windows through the same $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $R$ and the same $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger $D$. The comparison first factors each sequence read through the displayed limit row and then composes the two finite dyadic comparisons inside the recorded tolerance budget.

The comparison boundary $E$ prevents the route from becoming quotient equality of real streams. Rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same packet, so the local certificate has no coordinate from which a selected representative, a choice function for all tails, a trichotomy oracle, or ambient completeness of $\RealUp$ can be exported.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RealSequenceCauchyCriterion limit-Cauchy handoff]
\label{thm:real-sequence-cauchy-criterion-limit-cauchy-handoff}
Every $\RealSequenceLimitUp$ display accepted by a $\RealSequenceCauchyCriterionUp$ carrier yields a Cauchy tail-window route through finite $\RegSeqRatUp$ and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ rows. Concretely, for each requested tolerance in the classifier row $T$, the carrier exposes a tail index and two later sequence windows whose regular rational readbacks are compared by the dyadic ledger before the comparison is handed to $\RegularCauchyEquivalenceUp$ and any downstream $\RealUp$ consumer.

Thus a displayed sequence-limit packet is enough to serve Cauchy behaviour at the finite BEDC window level, without quotient streams, selected representatives, countable choice, real trichotomy, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the accepted carrier $K=(L,S,R,D,T,E,H,C,P,N)$. The row $L$ supplies the displayed sequence-limit packet, while $S$ lists the finite sequence and limit windows that may be inspected.

For a tolerance request, read the tail entry of $T$. It selects a displayed tail index and two later sequence windows inside the same schedule $S$. Their rational observations are read through $R$, and the required tolerance cells are read through $D$.

Apply the sequence-limit obligations of \autoref{thm:real-sequence-limit-namecert-obligation-surface} to both later windows. Each window is compared to the same candidate limit through the recorded dyadic tolerance ledger. Combining those two finite comparisons gives the Cauchy tail-window comparison recorded by $T$.

Finally pass the comparison through the nonquotient boundary $E$ before any $\RealUp$ handoff. Since all reads are coordinates of $K$ and all replay goes through $C$, the route introduces no quotient stream, representative selector, countable choice, trichotomy decision, or ambient completeness row.
\end{proof}
