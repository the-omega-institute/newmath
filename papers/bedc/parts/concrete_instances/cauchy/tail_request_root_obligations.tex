\begin{theorem}[Cauchy tail request carrier]
\label{thm:cauchy-tail-request-carrier}
The $\CauchyUp$ root tail-request surface is the finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
T=(W,Q,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
whose rows are finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows, a directed later-window request, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerances, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, a terminal $\RealUp$ seal, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert_{\CauchyUp}$ naming. It is the same finite request surface isolated by \autoref{def:cauchy-tail-request-carrier} and consumed by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finite-cauchy-filter-namecert}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the Cauchy seed carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-seed-carrier}. The rows $W,D,R,E,H,C,P,N$ already expose finite windows, dyadic tolerance, regular readback, Real sealing, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming.

Insert the directed request row $Q$ between the window row and the tolerance row. The resulting tuple is exactly the $\CauchyTailRequestUp$ carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-tail-request-carrier}, with the same $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ sibling anchors.

Because \autoref{def:finite-cauchy-filter-carrier} reads its tail coordinate as a finite directed request row, the root Cauchy packet supplies the carrier surface required by the finite filter route without adding quotient filters, selected limits, host equality, choice, or Real completeness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy directed-tail NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-directed-tail-namecert-obligations}
For the root tail-request packet $T=(W,Q,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)$ of \autoref{thm:cauchy-tail-request-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyUp}$ directed-tail obligation surface consists exactly of carrier admission, request classifier visibility, stability under later-window refinement, tolerance exactness, readback exactness, ledger routing, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming. These obligations are read through the sibling rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finite-cauchy-filter-namecert}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is the tuple display in \autoref{thm:cauchy-tail-request-carrier}. The request classifier is the row $Q$, and it is visible only after the finite window row $W$ has named the inspected StreamName windows.

Stability and tolerance exactness are read from $Q$ and $D$ together. A later-window refinement changes only the displayed request route and must keep the dyadic budget attached to that route before any regular rational readback is accepted.

Readback exactness and ledger routing place $R$ downstream of $W,Q,D$ and place $E$ downstream of $R$. Thus the Real-facing seal cannot serve as a selected limit, quotient-stream comparison, host equality witness, or ambient completeness principle.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ supply transport, replay, provenance, and local naming. Projecting \autoref{thm:cauchy-tail-request-namecert-obligations} gives the same row list for the named request surface, while \autoref{thm:finite-cauchy-filter-namecert-obligations} identifies the finite-filter consumer that reads that request.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy filter Real seal route]
\label{thm:cauchy-filter-real-seal-route}
Every $\RealUp$-facing Cauchy consumer that reads a filter, completion, or regular-name seal factors through the finite tail-request packet of \autoref{thm:cauchy-tail-request-carrier} before it can read $E$. The route is
$$
\StreamNameUp \longmapsto \CauchyTailRequestUp \longmapsto \DyadicRatCoreUp \longmapsto \RegSeqRatUp \longmapsto \RealUp,
$$
with $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows preserved throughout.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a Real-facing Cauchy read. The seed boundary of \autoref{thm:cauchy-seed-real-seal-boundary} already forces the read through finite windows, dyadic tolerance, regular readback, and the terminal Real seal.

Refine the seed route by \autoref{thm:cauchy-tail-request-carrier}. The finite request row $Q$ is inserted before the tolerance ledger, so no downstream consumer reaches $D$, $R$, or $E$ without first reading the directed-tail request surface.

Apply \autoref{thm:cauchy-directed-tail-namecert-obligations}. Its ledger and exactness clauses keep $W,Q,D,R,E$ in order, and its structural clauses preserve the same route through $H,C,P,N$.

Finally compare with \autoref{thm:finite-cauchy-filter-real-route}. The filter consumer reads the same finite route before consuming the Real seal, so every Real-facing Cauchy answer factors through the displayed tail request rather than through quotient filters, selected limits, countable choice, host equality, or Real completeness.
\end{proof}
