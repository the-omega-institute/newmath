¥begin{theorem}[Cauchy seed NameCert obligations]¥label{thm:cauchy-seed-namecert-obligations}

¥begin{enumerate}
¥item carrier admission for finite $¥StreamNameUp$ windows, $¥DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerances, $¥RegSeqRatUp$ readback, terminal $¥RealUp$ sealing, componentwise transport, displayed replay, provenance, and local naming;
¥item tolerance exactness, requiring every Cauchy comparison to read its dyadic budget from $D$ before any regular rational readback is accepted;
¥item window readback exactness, requiring $R$ to be generated from the displayed windows $W$ and tolerances $D$;
¥item Real seal discipline, requiring $E$ to remain downstream of $W,D,R$ and never to serve as a selected limit or host equality object;
¥item structural stability under $¥hsame$ transport $H$ and displayed $¥Cont$ replay $C$;
¥item provenance and local naming through $P$ and $N$.
¥end{enumerate}
No seed obligation certifies quotient stream equality, selected limits, host real equality, trichotomy, countable choice, or ambient completeness.
¥end{theorem}
¥begin{proof}
Project the carrier of ¥autoref{def:cauchy-seed-carrier}. The accepted packet exposes only the finite window row, dyadic tolerance row, regular rational readback, Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

Tolerance exactness is read from $D$ after the window row $W$ has been displayed. The regular readback row $R$ is downstream of both rows, so it cannot introduce an independent rational sequence or a choice schedule.

The Real seal row $E$ is terminal for the seed route. It is consumed only after $W,D,R$ are visible, and therefore cannot act as a selected limit, quotient equality, or host real equality witness.

Finally $H$ and $C$ preserve the same component rows under transport and replay, while $P$ and $N$ source and name the packet. Since no remaining coordinate exists, the listed rows exhaust the seed-level NameCert surface.
¥end{proof}
