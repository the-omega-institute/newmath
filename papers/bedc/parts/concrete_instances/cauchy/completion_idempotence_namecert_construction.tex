\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CauchyCompletionIdempotenceUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-completion-idempotence-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges constructive completion and Kelley/Bourbaki completion terminology, the $\CauchyCompletionIdempotenceUp$ packet records the finite BEDC certificate for flattening a completion of a completion back to the same displayed completion route. It sits over the monadic transformer of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchycompletionmonad-namecert}, the unit route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchycompletionunit-namecert}, the counit route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-completion-counit-namecert}, the reflective boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completionreflection-namecert}, and the separated completion seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-hausdorffcompletion-namecert}. The packet also reads the terminal $\RealUp$ seal, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the finite-window naming surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, and the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}; it does not form quotient completions, choose representatives, compare host equality, assume countable choice, or import an ambient completeness theorem.

\begin{definition}[Cauchy completion idempotence carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-completion-idempotence-carrier}
A $\CauchyCompletionIdempotenceUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
I=(X,Y,M,U,V,E,R,S,D,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the first completion row, carrying the displayed completion-facing source obtained from the original regular or metric data.
\item $Y$ is the second completion row, carrying the repeated-completion read before flattening.
\item $M$ is the $\CauchyCompletionMonadUp$ row that records the finite completion transformer used by both $X$ and $Y$.
\item $U$ is the $\CauchyCompletionUnitUp$ row admitting source-side data into the first completion surface.
\item $V$ is the $\CauchyCompletionCounitUp$ row returning completion-facing data through the displayed real-seal route.
\item $E$ is the combined $\CompletionReflectionUp$ and $\HausdorffCompletionUp$ seal row through which the flattened read is consumed.
\item $R$ is the $\RealUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row exposed after the finite route has been flattened.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row through which the regular readback is named.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger for every precision request used by $S$ and $R$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $X,Y,M,U,V,E,R,S,D$ and the structural rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from repeated completion reads through unit, monad, counit, reflection, separated seal, finite window, dyadic ledger, regular readback, and real seal.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyCompletionIdempotenceUp$, $\CauchyCompletionMonadUp$, $\CauchyCompletionUnitUp$, $\CauchyCompletionCounitUp$, $\CompletionReflectionUp$, $\HausdorffCompletionUp$, $\RealUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyCompletionIdempotenceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for quotient completion equality, a selected representative of a completion class, host equality of completed points, countable-choice schedules, open-cover compactness, ambient completeness, or completeness of $\RealUp$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyCompletionIdempotence NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-idempotence-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\CauchyCompletionIdempotenceUp$ carrier $I=(X,Y,M,U,V,E,R,S,D,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyCompletionIdempotenceUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following five rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the first completion row, second completion row, monad row, unit row, counit row, reflection and separated-seal row, real and regular readback row, stream window, dyadic ledger, componentwise transport, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item first-completion exposure, requiring every source-side read to enter the completion-facing surface through $U$ and the monad row $M$ before the first completion row $X$ is consumed;
\item repeated-completion flattening, requiring every read of $Y$ to return through $V$ and then through $E$ before the same $R,S,D$ route is exposed to a consumer;
\item classifier stability, requiring transported packets with the same $X,Y,M,U,V,E,R,S,D$ route to be identified by the carried $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ data;
\item ledger non-escape, stating that no quotient completion equality, selected representative, host equality, countable-choice schedule, open-cover compactness, ambient-completeness theorem, or $\RealUp$ completeness theorem is exported by the packet.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:cauchy-completion-idempotence-carrier}. The tuple lists exactly the two completion rows, monad, unit, counit, reflection and separated seal, readback, finite window, dyadic ledger, transport, replay, provenance, and local name.

First-completion exposure uses the unit and monad rows. A source-side read cannot reach the first completion surface by an external quotient operation; it enters through $U$, is routed by $M$, and is then recorded in $X$.

Repeated-completion flattening reads the second completion row $Y$ only through the counit row $V$. The reflection and separated-seal row $E$ then returns the repeated read to the same real-facing route $S,D,R$ used by the first completion packet.

Classifier stability is componentwise transport along $H$ followed by replay through $C$. Since the carrier fields are exhausted by $X,Y,M,U,V,E,R,S,D,H,C,P,N$, the excluded quotient, representative, host-equality, choice, compactness, ambient-completeness, and real-completeness principles have no row from which a consumer could read them.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyCompletionIdempotence flattening surface]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-idempotence-flattening-surface}
Let $I=(X,Y,M,U,V,E,R,S,D,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyCompletionIdempotenceUp$ carrier. Every repeated-completion consumer read exported by $I$ factors through the finite route
$$
Y\longmapsto V\longmapsto E\longmapsto S\longmapsto D\longmapsto R\longmapsto X\longmapsto(M,U,H,C,P,N).
$$
Thus the completion-of-completion read is flattened to the same finite completion route, and the packet exports no quotient completion, selected representative, host equality, countable-choice row, or ambient completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the repeated-completion row $Y$. The carrier exposes $Y$ only together with the counit row $V$, so a consumer read starts by returning the repeated completion data through the displayed counit route.

The reflection and separated-seal row $E$ is then forced by the packet. It records that the returned read is consumed through the same completion boundary rather than through a second quotient-completion object.

The finite observation part is the route $S,D,R$. The stream window $S$ names the inspected request, the dyadic ledger $D$ accounts for its tolerance, and the regular real-facing readback $R$ supplies the observation consumed by the first completion row $X$.

Finally $M$ and $U$ identify the original completion admission route, while $H$, $C$, $P$, and $N$ transport, replay, source, and name the packet. No coordinate remains for a selected representative, host equality, choice schedule, quotient completion, or ambient completeness theorem, so the exported surface is exactly the displayed finite flattening route.
\end{proof}
