It refines the modulus route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-realcauchymodulus-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchymodulus-namecert}, reads finite observations through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, uses the regular rational handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, records dyadic tolerances through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and reaches the terminal seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. \begin{definition}[Cauchy real locator carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-real-locator-carrier}
A $\CauchyRealLocatorUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(M,S,D,Q,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the modulus schedule row. It records the displayed RealCauchyModulus and CauchyModulus threshold data used for every locator request.
\item $S$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window family inspected by the locator at the requested precision and side test.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, carrying the positive dyadic scale, comparison cells, and slack budget for the displayed windows.
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row that turns the inspected windows into regular rational data before any $\RealUp$ seal is consumed.
\item $R$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ locator seal. It records only the Real-facing locator boundary delivered after $M,S,D,Q$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the modulus schedule, stream windows, dyadic ledger, regular readback, terminal locator seal, replay row, provenance row, and local naming row.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a locator request through $M,S,D,Q,R,H,P,N$.

\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyRealLocatorUp}$ row.

The carrier compares accepted packets only through the displayed rows $M,S,D,Q,R,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for host Real equality, quotient streams, selected limits, countable choice, trichotomy, open-cover compactness, or ambient Real completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy real locator NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-real-locator-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CauchyRealLocatorUp$ carrier
$$
L=(M,S,D,Q,R,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyRealLocatorUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the modulus schedule, finite stream windows, dyadic tolerance ledger, regular rational readback, terminal Real locator seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming;
\item modulus exactness, requiring every locator request to use the displayed row $M$ rather than an independent threshold, host convergence theorem, or selected limit;
\item window and tolerance compatibility, requiring the inspected windows in $S$ to be compared only through the dyadic ledger $D$;
\item RegSeqRat readback, requiring the rational data consumed by the locator seal to pass through the displayed row $Q$;
\item route ordering, requiring $R$ to be read only after $M,S,D,Q$ have been displayed;
\item nonescape, excluding host Real equality, quotient streams, selected limits, countable choice, trichotomy, open-cover compactness, ambient Real completeness, Lean verification, and bridge checking.
\end{enumerate}
No local locator obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:cauchy-real-locator-carrier}. The accepted packet displays exactly the modulus schedule, finite windows, dyadic ledger, regular readback, terminal locator seal, and structural rows.

Modulus exactness is forced by the single coordinate $M$. Every locator request reads its threshold from this row, so the packet has no second convergence witness, hidden host completeness theorem, or selected limit coordinate.

Window and tolerance compatibility are supplied by $S$ and $D$. The stream row exposes only finite windows, while the dyadic row records the positive scale, comparison cells, and slack budget used to interpret those windows.

The readback and ordering clauses follow from $Q$ and $R$. The regular rational row is the only rational readback coordinate, and the terminal Real locator seal occurs downstream of $M,S,D,Q$.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite route. Since the carrier inventory contains no row for host Real equality, quotient streams, selected limits, countable choice, trichotomy, open-cover compactness, ambient completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy real locator modulus route]
\label{thm:cauchy-real-locator-modulus-route}

$$
M \longmapsto S,D \longmapsto Q \longmapsto R \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
Thus the request reaches the $\RealUp$ locator seal only through the visible modulus schedule, finite stream windows, dyadic tolerance ledger, and $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with an accepted carrier $L=(M,S,D,Q,R,H,C,P,N)$ and a displayed locator request. The only threshold coordinate available to the request is $M$, so the modulus schedule is read first.

The finite observation step is next. The request opens the window row $S$ and interprets its tolerance through $D$, whose dyadic ledger records the positive scale and comparison cells.

The rational handoff is then forced through $Q$. No independent stream or host rational approximation is present in the carrier, so the locator request reaches the Real-facing boundary only after the regular rational readback has been displayed.

Finally the terminal row $R$ is consumed and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve the same route. This proves the stated factorization and excludes a bypass through selected limits, quotient streams, countable choice, trichotomy, open-cover compactness, or ambient Real completeness.
\end{proof}
