\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CauchyModulusSpaceUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-modulus-space-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, the
$\CauchyModulusSpaceUp$ packet records a carrier-space interface for explicit
Cauchy modulus schedules. It is anchored in the single-modulus certificate of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchymodulus-namecert}, the finite stream
windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular
rational readback surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert},
the dyadic tolerance ledger of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the terminal real seal
of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, and the fast Cauchy sequence
route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-fast-cauchy-sequence-namecert}. The
chapter treats a family of modulus schedules as finite naming data, not as a
quotient of real sequences or as an ambient completeness theorem.

\begin{definition}[Cauchy modulus space carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-modulus-space-carrier}
A $\CauchyModulusSpaceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
M=(I,S,W,D,R,Q,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $I$ is the finite index row for the family of explicit modulus schedules.
\item $S$ assigns to each displayed index a $\CauchyModulusUp$ schedule row,
including the monotone precision requests used by the family.
\item $W$ records the finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows inspected by the schedule
family.
\item $D$ is the dyadic tolerance ledger, read through $\DyadicRatCoreUp$.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row attached to the scheduled windows.
\item $Q$ records the $\RealUp$ seal boundaries at which a scheduled read may
leave rational and dyadic data for a real-facing consumer.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $I,S,W,D,R,Q,C,P,N$.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from an index and precision request
through the schedule row, stream window, dyadic tolerance ledger, regular
rational readback, and real seal boundary.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyModulusSpaceUp$,
$\CauchyModulusUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$,
$\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and the local naming row.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyModulusSpaceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only after transporting the displayed
rows componentwise by $\hsame$. It has no coordinate for an unscheduled Cauchy
oracle, quotient equality of sequences, a selected real limit, countable choice,
or an ambient completion theorem.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy modulus space NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-modulus-space-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CauchyModulusSpaceUp$ carrier
$$
M=(I,S,W,D,R,Q,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyModulusSpaceUp}$ surface consists exactly of
carrier admission for the finite index family, schedule admission for the
displayed $\CauchyModulusUp$ rows, finite $\StreamNameUp$ window admission,
dyadic tolerance compatibility, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback compatibility,
$\RealUp$ seal-boundary compatibility, componentwise $\hsame$ stability,
displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming for the packet.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CauchyUp.CauchyModulusSpaceNameCertObligations}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier of
\autoref{def:cauchy-modulus-space-carrier}. The analytic data are precisely
$I,S,W,D,R,Q$, and the structural rows are precisely $H,C,P,N$.

Schedule admission is rowwise over $I$. Each index exposes its explicit modulus
schedule before any stream window is inspected, so the packet cannot replace the
family by an unscheduled oracle.

The tolerance and readback rows are then forced through $D$ and $R$. The real
boundary row $Q$ is available only after those rational and dyadic ledgers have
been displayed.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same route. Since no
excluded host object is a carrier coordinate, the listed rows exhaust the local
NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy modulus space finite route]
\label{thm:cauchy-modulus-space-finite-route}
Every real-facing comparison of two accepted $\CauchyModulusSpaceUp$ carriers
factors through finite schedule rows, finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows, dyadic
tolerance ledgers, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readbacks, $\RealUp$ seal boundaries,
componentwise $\hsame$, displayed $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and the local $\NameCert$
rows. The route exports a shared modulus-comparison surface for later
diagonal $\RealUp$ completion arguments without introducing quotient sequence
equality, selected limits, countable choice, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CauchyUp.CauchyModulusSpaceFiniteRoute}
\begin{proof}
Open both carriers using
\autoref{def:cauchy-modulus-space-carrier}. A comparison can inspect only the
finite index rows, schedule rows, stream windows, tolerance ledgers, readback
rows, and seal boundaries.

Componentwise $\hsame$ in $H$ transports the two packets to a common finite
surface. Displayed $\Cont$ replay in $C$ keeps each precision request attached
to its schedule, stream window, dyadic tolerance, regular readback, and real
seal boundary.

The provenance and naming rows $P,N$ package that finite surface as the local
certificate. Thus later diagonal completion routes can share modulus comparison
data without receiving any quotient or choice-based object.
\end{proof}

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

family is displayed by $I,S$, and every inspected request is routed through the
finite observation rows $W,D,R,Q$ before the structural rows $H,C,P,N$ can name

comparison has already factored through finite schedule rows, stream windows,
dyadic tolerances, regular rational readbacks, and the terminal Real seal

The $\CauchyUp$ root route receives exactly that finite schedule surface. Since
the carrier has no coordinate for quotient equality, selected limits, countable
choice, or ambient completeness, the seed interface cannot export any of those
host objects to L10 Real and Completion consumers.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyModulusSpace tail composition]
\label{thm:cauchy-modulus-space-tail-composition}
For every accepted $\CauchyModulusSpaceUp$ carrier
$$
M=(I,S,W,D,R,Q,H,C,P,N),
$$
two displayed modulus schedules in the family compose only through finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance rows, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, and terminal $\RealUp$ seal rows. The composed tail read is the finite route
$$
I,S,W,D,R,Q \longmapsto H,C,P,N
$$
and it exports no quotient sequence equality, selected limit, countable choice, or ambient completeness object.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-modulus-space-carrier}. The two schedules are displayed rows under $S$ indexed by $I$, and their inspected tails must pass through the same finite window row $W$.

Apply the finite route and seed interface, \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-space-finite-route} and \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-space-seed-interface}. These results force the tolerance ledger $D$, regular readback $R$, and Real seal boundary $Q$ to remain visible before the composed tail can be consumed.

Finally replay the structural rows. Componentwise transport $H$, displayed continuation $C$, provenance $P$, and local naming $N$ preserve the same finite schedule surface, so tail composition does not add quotient equality, selected limits, countable choice, or ambient completeness.
\end{proof}

\label{thm:cauchy-modulus-space-filter-handoff}

$$

\begin{proof}
Begin with a filter-facing read. The seed interface of \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-space-seed-interface} already routes every scheduled request through finite stream windows, dyadic tolerances, regular rational readback, the Cauchy root route, and the terminal Real seal.

Use tail composition from \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-space-tail-composition}. It shows that composing displayed schedules preserves the same finite route through $I,S,W,D,R,Q$ before structural replay.

The filter consumer therefore receives only the transported and replayed packet named by $H,C,P,N$. Since the carrier contains no quotient equality coordinate, no selected limit, no countable-choice schedule, and no ambient completeness row, CauchyFilter and CauchyFilterCompletion may consume only the displayed finite handoff.
\end{proof}
