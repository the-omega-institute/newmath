\begin{definition}[Sequence Cauchy root observation carrier]
\label{def:sequence-cauchy-root-observation-carrier}
For the root $\CauchyUp$ sequence route, a finite observation carrier is a $\BHist$ packet
$$
O=(S,R,D,H,C,P,N)
$$
where $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite observation row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and $H,C,P,N$ are respectively componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ rows. The carrier has no quotient sequence coordinate, host-sequence equality coordinate, selected limit, or ambient completion proof.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Sequence Cauchy root stream obligation]
\label{thm:sequence-cauchy-root-stream-obligation}
Every accepted root $\CauchyUp$ sequence route factors through the finite observation carrier of \autoref{def:sequence-cauchy-root-observation-carrier}. Its local obligation surface consists of StreamName window exposure, RegSeqRat readback exposure, DyadicRatCore tolerance accounting, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the root stream carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-root-stream-carrier}. Its stream row supplies the finite observation windows and its witness ledger supplies only displayed comparisons.

Route those windows through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, then through the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and finally through the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}. These three rows are exactly $S,R,D$ in the observation carrier.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite route. Since the carrier has no quotient or host-sequence equality coordinate, every accepted Cauchy read remains inside the displayed NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Sequence Cauchy hsame-stable tail classifier]
\label{thm:sequence-cauchy-hsame-stable-tail-classifier}
Let $O=(S,R,D,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted sequence Cauchy root observation carrier. Any tail classifier read transported by $H$ preserves the same StreamName window row $S$, the same RegSeqRat readback row $R$, and the same DyadicRatCore tolerance ledger $D$. Consequently the transported tail classifier remains a root $\CauchyUp$ read and cannot introduce quotient sequence equality, host-sequence equality, selected limits, or ambient completion.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier $O$. The tail classifier has access only to $S,R,D,H,C,P,N$, and $H$ is declared componentwise over exactly those rows.

Transporting a tail read by $H$ therefore replaces no observation row by an external sequence and adds no equality oracle. The StreamName, RegSeqRat, and DyadicRatCore rows stay visible before the classifier verdict is read.

The transported classifier is then repacked with the same provenance and local naming rows. This is the root Cauchy tail surface, not a quotient or host-sequence equality claim.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Sequence Cauchy Cont-stable replay ledger]
\label{thm:sequence-cauchy-cont-stable-replay-ledger}
For every accepted sequence Cauchy root observation carrier $O=(S,R,D,H,C,P,N)$, each displayed $\Cont$ replay of a Cauchy request must read $S$, then $R$, then $D$, and then the structural rows $H,P,N$. The replay ledger is therefore stable under finite-window refinement and remains a finite NameCert ledger for StreamName, RegSeqRat, and DyadicRatCore data.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a displayed replay row $C$. By the carrier surface, $C$ is a replay through the finite observation carrier and not through an ambient sequence object.

The replay order exposes the StreamName window before the regular rational readback and the dyadic tolerance ledger. A finite-window refinement may restrict $S$, but the refined route still factors through the corresponding $R$ and $D$ rows.

Finally the replay is named by $P,N$ and transported by $H$. The ledger remains finite because every replayed request is read through displayed windows and tolerance rows rather than through quotient or host-sequence equality.
\end{proof}
