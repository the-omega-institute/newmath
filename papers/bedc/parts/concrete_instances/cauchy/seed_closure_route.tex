\begin{theorem}[Cauchy seed closure route]
\label{thm:cauchy-seed-closure-route}
Every public route through the $\CauchyUp$ hub is read through the sibling surfaces of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-remainder-estimate-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert} before any Real-facing completion consumer can use it. \end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the Cauchy carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-carrier}. Its public readback is already ordered through $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and the terminal $\RealUp$ seal-facing row.

Apply \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-readback-route} and \autoref{thm:cauchy-core-streamname-regseqrat-window}. These results force every regular rational read to pass through finite stream windows, dyadic tolerances, the displayed modulus row, and the compatibility row before $R$ is inspected.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:cauchy-core-regseqrat-real-seal}. The terminal Real-facing seal remains downstream of the displayed regular rational and remainder-estimate routes. Since none of the cited sibling surfaces carries selected limits, quotient stream equality, choice, trichotomy, host equality, or ambient completeness, the hub route exports none of those objects.
\end{proof}
