Following Bishop and Bridges on constructive completion and the finite-limit language of regular category theory, the $\CauchyCompletionLeftExactnessUp$ packet records the boundary where a displayed Cauchy-completion route preserves the finite-limit rows that its source already exposes. It sits beside the Cauchy source route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-namecert}, the metric-completion universal-property route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-completion-universal-property-namecert}, the functorial completion route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchycompletionfunctor-namecert}, the regular-rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. \begin{definition}[Cauchy completion left-exactness carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-completion-left-exactness-carrier}
A $\CauchyCompletionLeftExactnessUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(S,U,D,K,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the source finite-limit row, recording the displayed terminal, product, equalizer, or kernel-pair data available before completion is read.
\item $U$ is the completion unit row, recording the source-to-completion unit reads for every object named by $S$.
\item $D$ is the dense image row, keeping each completed finite-limit comparison tied to the same source rows and unit rows.
\item $K$ is the kernel-pair preservation row, recording that equalizer and pullback comparisons are read through the displayed source kernel-pair data rather than through host quotient equality.
\item $E$ is the extension ledger, recording the universal-property and functorial completion rows used to transport finite-limit witnesses into completion-facing reads.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $S,U,D,K,E,C,P,N$.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a finite-limit request through $S,U,D,K,E,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyCompletionLeftExactnessUp$, $\CauchyUp$, $\MetricCompletionUp$, $\CauchyCompletionFunctorUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyCompletionLeftExactnessUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through these rows. It has no coordinate for quotient completion equality, selected representatives, selected finite limits, countable choice, host equality of completed objects, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy completion left-exactness NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-left-exactness-namecert-obligations}

$$

\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}

The source row supplies the terminal, product, equalizer, or kernel-pair data; the unit row embeds the displayed source names into completion-facing reads; the dense image row keeps those reads tied to source data; and the kernel-pair row confines exactness comparisons to displayed finite rows.

The remaining rows $E,H,C,P,N$ transport and name the same route. Since quotient completion equality, selected representatives, selected finite limits, countable choice, host equality, and ambient completeness have no carrier coordinate, projection of the displayed rows exhausts the NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}
