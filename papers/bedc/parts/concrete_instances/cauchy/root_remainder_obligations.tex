\begin{theorem}[Cauchy carrier root obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-carrier-root-obligations}
Let
$$
R=(S,D,Q,T,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted root $\CauchyUp$ remainder carrier. Here $S$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window row, $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row, $T$ is the $\CauchyTailDiameterUp$ tail-diameter row, $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row, and $H,C,P,N$ are the componentwise transport, displayed replay, provenance, and local naming rows. The local root obligation surface is exactly carrier admission for these rows, tolerance exactness through $D$, readback exposure through $Q$, tail-diameter exposure through $T$, terminal sealing through $E$, and structural stability through $H,C,P,N$.

No accepted root carrier contains a selected limit, quotient stream, countable-choice schedule, host real equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the displayed $\BHist$ carrier $R$. The stream row $S$ is the only source of visible observations, and the dyadic ledger $D$ is the only source of precision requests and comparison budgets.

The regular readback row $Q$ is downstream of $S$ and $D$, while the tail-diameter row $T$ is downstream of the same finite windows and tolerances. Thus the root carrier records a finite remainder surface rather than a selected completed object.

The terminal seal row $E$ may be read only after $S,D,Q,T$ have been displayed. The rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name that same finite route. Since there are no further coordinates, the listed rows exhaust the root obligation surface and the excluded principles cannot be exported.
\end{proof}
