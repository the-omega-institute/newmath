\begin{theorem}[FormalBall radius-order NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:formal-ball-radius-order-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:formal-ball-radius-order}, the local $\NameCert_{\FormalBallRadiusOrderUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $O=(B,R,D,L,H,C,P,N)$, dyadic radius comparison through $D$, rounded refinement through $L$, basis handoff through the $\FormalBallBasisUp$ row named by $P$, completion non-escape through the $\FormalBallCompletionUp$ boundary, componentwise transport through $H$, displayed replay through $C$, provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.

It cannot read a quotient completion equality, choose a representative, select a limit, use countable choice, invoke compactness, or assume completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:formal-ball-radius-order}. The tuple displays the formal-ball packet, radius row, dyadic comparison ledger, lower-neighborhood row, transport, replay, provenance, and local name.

Dyadic radius comparison is confined to $R$ and $D$. The formal-ball row $B$ supplies the source center-radius packet, while $D$ records the finite comparison data used to recognize a smaller radius.

Rounded refinement and basis handoff are then read through $L$ and $C$. The lower-neighborhood row records the smaller formal-ball window, and replay sends that window to the basis and completion-facing rows carried in $P$.

Completion non-escape follows from row exhaustion. Since the carrier has no quotient-completion equality, selected representative, selected limit, countable-choice schedule, compactness principle, or ambient complete-space coordinate, none of those objects can be exported by the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}
