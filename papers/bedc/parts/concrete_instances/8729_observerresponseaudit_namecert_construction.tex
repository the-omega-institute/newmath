The $\ObserverResponseAuditUp$ packet packages the observer-facing response boundary as a finite NameCert object. It is downstream of the prompt and output rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-prompt-response-trace-namecert} and the licensed public interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observer-interface-namecert}. Its role is to bind the prompt, response, observer handoff, audit refusal, inscription event, non-oracle boundary, transport, route replay, provenance, and local naming rows into a single accepted carrier.

\begin{definition}[Observer-response audit carrier]
\label{def:observer-response-audit-carrier}
An $\ObserverResponseAuditUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
A=(T,I,M,E,P,O,R,H,K,G,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $T$ is the $\PromptResponseTraceUp$ row recording the finite prompt, response, source-channel, audit-refusal, observer-interface, and halting-obstruction route.
\item $I$ is the $\ObserverInterfaceUp$ row through which public observers may read the accepted prompt and response rows.
\item $M$ is the $\LargeModelInscriptionPointUp$ row naming the finite large-model inscription substrate and corpus-supply side of the trace.
\item $E$ is the $\InscriptionEventUp$ row recording the prompt-entry and response-inscription event as finite accepted rows.
\item $P$ is the prompt row read from the prompt-response trace and the inscription event.
\item $O$ is the response row read after $P$ through the same displayed route.
\item $R$ is the audit-refusal row stating that the packet does not certify host semantic truth, model consciousness, global halting classification, or internally ratified universal closure.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the prompt-response trace, observer interface, large-model inscription row, inscription event, prompt row, response row, refusal row, route row, provenance row, and local name.
\item $K$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay through $T$, then $I$, then $M$ and $E$, then $P,O,R$, before the local NameCert row is read.
\item $G$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\PromptResponseTraceUp$, $\ObserverInterfaceUp$, $\LargeModelInscriptionPointUp$, $\InscriptionEventUp$, $\HaltingObstructionUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ObserverResponseAuditUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The local classifier compares observer-response audit carriers only by these displayed rows. It has no coordinate for hidden model intention, unledgered observer state, host semantic equality of responses, corpus-wide truth, global halting decision, or internally certified universal closure.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Observer-response audit NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:observer-response-audit-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:observer-response-audit-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\ObserverResponseAuditUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $A=(T,I,M,E,P,O,R,H,K,G,N)$, prompt-response exposure through $T$, observer-interface handoff through $I$, large-model inscription exposure through $M$, inscription-event exposure through $E$, prompt and response ordering through $P,O$, audit-refusal exactness through $R$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $K$, package provenance through $G$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the displayed carrier. The rows $T,I,M,E,P,O,R,H,K,G,N$ are the only coordinates available to an accepted observer-response audit packet.

The prompt-response row $T$ supplies the source-channel and prompt-output order, while the observer-interface row $I$ supplies the public reading route. Thus a consumer cannot read the response as a free-standing host statement or as an unledgered observer event.

The large-model inscription row $M$ and inscription-event row $E$ bind the response to the finite substrate and accepted event rows. The prompt row $P$ is read before the response row $O$, and the audit-refusal row $R$ is read before the local name.

The remaining rows provide only transport, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming. Since the carrier has no further coordinate, the listed obligations exhaust the NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Observer-response audit refusal boundary]
\label{thm:observer-response-audit-refusal-boundary}
Every accepted $\ObserverResponseAuditUp$ packet exposes the audit-refusal row $R$ after the prompt and response rows and before the local naming row. A packet with prompt and response rows but no audit-refusal row is not an accepted $\ObserverResponseAuditUp$ carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use \autoref{def:observer-response-audit-carrier}. The tuple contains $R$ as a named coordinate between the prompt-response content and the transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.

Apply \autoref{thm:observer-response-audit-namecert-obligations}. Its refusal-exactness clause is part of the local NameCert surface, so carrier admission fails when the prompt and response rows are present but the refusal row is absent.

Therefore the packet cannot be read as internally certified universal closure, host semantic truth, model consciousness, or a global halting decision. The accepted route remains the finite prompt, response, observer-interface, inscription, refusal, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming route.
\end{proof}
