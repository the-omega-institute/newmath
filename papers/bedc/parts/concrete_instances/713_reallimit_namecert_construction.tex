\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RealLimitUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-reallimit-namecert}

The $\RealLimitUp$ interface names the finite saturated frontier where a
scheduled regular rational stream can be read as a real-facing seal source.
Following Bishop--Bridges constructive analysis, convergence is carried by
explicit Cauchy and modulus data, not by a choice-selected limiting point. The
packet below therefore records only certified stream windows, dyadic tolerance
rows, regular-sequence reads, and the visible $\RealUp$ endpoint row available
to downstream consumers.

\begin{definition}[RealLimit BHist carrier]
\label{def:reallimit-bhist-carrier}
A $\RealLimitUp$ packet is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ source row
$$
R=(S,Q,E,D,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is a $\StreamNameUp$ schedule selecting the finite windows through
which the approximation stream is inspected;
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ source stream read on those scheduled windows;
\item $E$ is the proposed $\RealUp$ seal endpoint consumed by real-facing
interfaces;
\item $D$ is the displayed family of $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance windows used
for finite error reads;
\item $H$ records the componentwise $\hsame$ rows for stream schedule,
regular-sequence source, seal endpoint, and dyadic tolerance rows;
\item $C$ records the visible $\Cont$ routes through which a consumer reaches
the selected windows and their common refinements;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance row naming the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$,
$\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, and L0--L5 kernel dependencies;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RealLimitUp}$ ledger row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate is
$\mathsf{RealLimitPacket}(S,Q,E,D,H,C,P,N)$. Its classifier compares two
accepted packets by passing to common finite refinements of the stream schedules
and reading the same $\RegSeqRatUp$ tolerance row there. All agreement is then
transported componentwise through the displayed $\hsame$ rows and the same
$\Cont$ routes; no completed real equality or quotient of streams is used.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[RealLimit NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:reallimit-namecert-obligation-surface}
The local $\NameCert_{\RealLimitUp}$ surface consists exactly of five rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier: the finite packet of
\autoref{def:reallimit-bhist-carrier} with its schedule, source stream,
seal endpoint, dyadic tolerance windows, transport, continuation, provenance,
and local ledger;
\item classifier: common finite schedule refinements followed by the same
$\RegSeqRatUp$ tolerance read;
\item stability: componentwise $\hsame$ transport and visible $\Cont$
refinement preserve the selected stream window and tolerance comparison;
\item exactness/readback: every $\RealUp$ read factors through the displayed
$\RegSeqRatUp$ source stream and the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance window used by
the schedule;
\item ledger: the $\Pkg$ and $\NameCert$ rows expose only the
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, and L0--L5
kernel dependencies.
\end{enumerate}
This is an obligation surface for finite constructive limit names; it is not a
claim of real completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:reallimit-bhist-carrier}. The displayed
coordinates already list the schedule, source stream, proposed seal endpoint,
dyadic tolerance rows, transport witnesses, continuation routes, provenance,
and local ledger.

For the classifier row, compare two packets only after selecting a common finite
refinement of their visible schedules. The $\RegSeqRatUp$ source stream is read
at that refined window, and the dyadic tolerance row is the one named by the
same packet coordinate.

Stability follows by transporting each visible coordinate through its stored
$\hsame$ row and reading refinements only through the displayed $\Cont$ routes.
The readback row is exact because the sole $\RealUp$ coordinate is the seal
endpoint $E$, and $E$ is reached from the regular-sequence and dyadic tolerance
rows already present in the packet.

The ledger row is exhausted by $P$ and $N$. Since the carrier has no coordinate
for a completed real, no quotient stream, and no choice-selected limit, those
objects cannot be exported by the local certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RealLimit RegSeqRat stream-name handoff]
\label{thm:reallimit-regseqrat-streamname-handoff}
Every accepted $\RealLimitUp$ packet hands a $\StreamNameUp$ schedule to a
$\RegSeqRatUp$ source stream before any $\RealUp$ seal read is available. More
precisely, a real-facing consumer may read the seal endpoint only through the
scheduled regular-sequence tolerance window, its $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ error row,
and the displayed $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ provenance.

The handoff exports no $\RealUp$ completeness theorem, no equality of quotient
streams, and no choice-selected limiting object.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the accepted packet
$R=(S,Q,E,D,H,C,P,N)$ through
\autoref{def:reallimit-bhist-carrier}. The coordinate $S$ selects the finite
observation windows, while $Q$ supplies the regular rational stream read on
those windows.

The dyadic tolerance coordinate $D$ records the finite error window used for the
same read. A consumer reaches it only through the continuation routes in $C$,
and endpoint replacement is constrained by the componentwise transport rows in
$H$.

The proposed real-facing endpoint is the single coordinate $E$. Because $E$ is
packaged together with $S$, $Q$, $D$, $H$, $C$, $P$, and $N$, any read of $E$
has the scheduled regular-sequence handoff as its visible source. There is no
remaining coordinate from which a completeness principle, stream quotient, or
selected limiting value could be obtained.
\end{proof}
