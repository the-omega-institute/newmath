\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RealLimitUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-reallimit-namecert}

The $\RealLimitUp$ interface names the finite saturated frontier where a
scheduled regular rational stream can be read as a real-facing seal source.
Following Bishop--Bridges constructive analysis, convergence is carried by
explicit Cauchy and modulus data, not by a choice-selected limiting point. The
packet below therefore records only certified stream windows, dyadic tolerance
rows, regular-sequence reads, and the visible $\RealUp$ endpoint row available
to downstream consumers.

\begin{definition}[RealLimit BHist carrier]
\label{def:reallimit-bhist-carrier}
A $\RealLimitUp$ packet is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ source row
$$
R=(S,Q,E,D,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is a $\StreamNameUp$ schedule selecting the finite windows through
which the approximation stream is inspected;
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ source stream read on those scheduled windows;
\item $E$ is the proposed $\RealUp$ seal endpoint consumed by real-facing
interfaces;
\item $D$ is the displayed family of $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance windows used
for finite error reads;
\item $H$ records the componentwise $\hsame$ rows for stream schedule,
regular-sequence source, seal endpoint, and dyadic tolerance rows;
\item $C$ records the visible $\Cont$ routes through which a consumer reaches
the selected windows and their common refinements;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance row naming the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$,
$\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, and L0--L5 kernel dependencies;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RealLimitUp}$ ledger row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate is
$\mathsf{RealLimitPacket}(S,Q,E,D,H,C,P,N)$. Its classifier compares two
accepted packets by passing to common finite refinements of the stream schedules
and reading the same $\RegSeqRatUp$ tolerance row there. All agreement is then
transported componentwise through the displayed $\hsame$ rows and the same
$\Cont$ routes; no completed real equality or quotient of streams is used.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[RealLimit NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:reallimit-namecert-obligation-surface}
The local $\NameCert_{\RealLimitUp}$ surface consists exactly of five rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier: the finite packet of
\autoref{def:reallimit-bhist-carrier} with its schedule, source stream,
seal endpoint, dyadic tolerance windows, transport, continuation, provenance,
and local ledger;
\item classifier: common finite schedule refinements followed by the same
$\RegSeqRatUp$ tolerance read;
\item stability: componentwise $\hsame$ transport and visible $\Cont$
refinement preserve the selected stream window and tolerance comparison;
\item exactness/readback: every $\RealUp$ read factors through the displayed
$\RegSeqRatUp$ source stream and the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance window used by
the schedule;
\item ledger: the $\Pkg$ and $\NameCert$ rows expose only the
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, and L0--L5
kernel dependencies.
\end{enumerate}
This is an obligation surface for finite constructive limit names; it is not a
claim of real completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:reallimit-bhist-carrier}. The displayed
coordinates already list the schedule, source stream, proposed seal endpoint,
dyadic tolerance rows, transport witnesses, continuation routes, provenance,
and local ledger.

For the classifier row, compare two packets only after selecting a common finite
refinement of their visible schedules. The $\RegSeqRatUp$ source stream is read
at that refined window, and the dyadic tolerance row is the one named by the
same packet coordinate.

Stability follows by transporting each visible coordinate through its stored
$\hsame$ row and reading refinements only through the displayed $\Cont$ routes.
The readback row is exact because the sole $\RealUp$ coordinate is the seal
endpoint $E$, and $E$ is reached from the regular-sequence and dyadic tolerance
rows already present in the packet.

The ledger row is exhausted by $P$ and $N$. Since the carrier has no coordinate
for a completed real, no quotient stream, and no choice-selected limit, those
objects cannot be exported by the local certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RealLimit RegSeqRat stream-name handoff]
\label{thm:reallimit-regseqrat-streamname-handoff}
Every accepted $\RealLimitUp$ packet hands a $\StreamNameUp$ schedule to a
$\RegSeqRatUp$ source stream before any $\RealUp$ seal read is available. More
precisely, a real-facing consumer may read the seal endpoint only through the
scheduled regular-sequence tolerance window, its $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ error row,
and the displayed $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ provenance.

The handoff exports no $\RealUp$ completeness theorem, no equality of quotient
streams, and no choice-selected limiting object.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.RealLimitUp.RealLimitPacket\_scheduled\_handoff\_transport}
\begin{proof}
Read the accepted packet
$R=(S,Q,E,D,H,C,P,N)$ through
\autoref{def:reallimit-bhist-carrier}. The coordinate $S$ selects the finite
observation windows, while $Q$ supplies the regular rational stream read on
those windows.

The dyadic tolerance coordinate $D$ records the finite error window used for the
same read. A consumer reaches it only through the continuation routes in $C$,
and endpoint replacement is constrained by the componentwise transport rows in
$H$.

The proposed real-facing endpoint is the single coordinate $E$. Because $E$ is
packaged together with $S$, $Q$, $D$, $H$, $C$, $P$, and $N$, any read of $E$
has the scheduled regular-sequence handoff as its visible source. There is no
remaining coordinate from which a completeness principle, stream quotient, or
selected limiting value could be obtained.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RealLimit common-refinement classifier]
\label{thm:reallimit-common-refinement-classifier}
For accepted $\RealLimitUp$ packets
$$
R=(S,Q,E,D,H,C,P,N)
\quad\text{and}\quad
R'=(S',Q',E',D',H',C',P',N'),
$$
the local classifier can place $R$ and $R'$ in the same real-limit name only
through a displayed common finite $\StreamNameUp$ schedule refinement. On that
common refinement it reads the same $\RegSeqRatUp$ tolerance row, the displayed
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$ error rows, and the componentwise $\hsame$, $\Cont$,
$\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows of the two packets. The classifier has no row for
completed real equality, quotient-stream equality, or a selected limit.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project both packets through \autoref{def:reallimit-bhist-carrier}. The
schedule coordinates $S,S'$ are the only sources of observation windows, and
the source-stream coordinates $Q,Q'$ are the only regular rational rows
available at those windows.

Refine the displayed schedules to a common finite window. On that window the
classifier reads the $\RegSeqRatUp$ tolerance row named by the source stream
coordinates and the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ error rows named by $D,D'$.

The remaining comparison data is componentwise. Transport is supplied by
$H,H'$, continuation routes by $C,C'$, package provenance by $P,P'$, and local
ledger evidence by $N,N'$. Repacking these displayed rows gives the classifier,
and the carrier contains no coordinate from which completed real equality,
quotient-stream equality, or a selected limit could be read.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RealLimit transport stability boundary]
\label{thm:reallimit-transport-stability-boundary}
For an accepted $\RealLimitUp$ packet, $\hsame$ transport and $\Cont$
refinement preserve the selected $\StreamNameUp$ schedule, the
$\RegSeqRatUp$ source stream, the $\RealUp$ seal endpoint, the
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance windows, the $\Pkg$ provenance row, and the local
$\NameCert_{\RealLimitUp}$ ledger. Every transported row remains a displayed
row of the finite packet surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the packet $R=(S,Q,E,D,H,C,P,N)$ from
\autoref{def:reallimit-bhist-carrier}. The coordinates $H$ and $C$ are exactly
the stored componentwise transport and refinement routes for the schedule,
source stream, seal endpoint, dyadic tolerances, package provenance, and local
ledger.

Apply \autoref{thm:reallimit-common-refinement-classifier} to identify the
common finite schedule surface on which a transported comparison is allowed to
take place. The classifier exposes only the displayed schedule, source,
tolerance, transport, continuation, provenance, and certificate rows.

Transporting along $H$ and refining along $C$ therefore keeps every consumer
row inside the same finite packet surface. Since no other carrier coordinate is
available, the seal endpoint, dyadic tolerance windows, provenance, and ledger
remain displayed rows rather than hidden real-completion data.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RealLimit readback ledger exhaustion]
\label{thm:reallimit-readback-ledger-exhaustion}
Every $\RealUp$-facing read from an accepted $\RealLimitUp$ packet factors
through the scheduled $\RegSeqRatUp$ source, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance
row, the displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ routes, the $\Pkg$ provenance row, and
the local $\NameCert_{\RealLimitUp}$ ledger. The readback surface excludes
$\RealUp$ completeness, quotient-stream equality, and selected limiting
objects.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with \autoref{thm:reallimit-regseqrat-streamname-handoff}. It routes a
real-facing read through the finite $\StreamNameUp$ schedule, the
$\RegSeqRatUp$ source stream, the dyadic tolerance row, transport,
continuation, package provenance, and local certificate.

Use \autoref{thm:reallimit-common-refinement-classifier} for paired readbacks.
It shows that compatible packet reads share only a displayed common finite
schedule refinement and the same scheduled tolerance surface.

Use \autoref{thm:reallimit-transport-stability-boundary} for replacement of
rows along $\hsame$ and $\Cont$. The transported read remains on the visible
packet coordinates, so the ledger is exhausted by $S,Q,D,H,C,P,N$ together
with the seal endpoint $E$.

No coordinate of the carrier stores real completeness, quotient-stream
equality, or a selected limiting object. Those objects therefore cannot appear
in the $\RealUp$-facing readback ledger.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RealLimit classifier obligation]
\label{thm:reallimit-classifier-obligation}
For accepted $\RealLimitUp$ packets
$R=(S,Q,E,D,H,C,P,N)$ and $R'=(S',Q',E',D',H',C',P',N')$, the local classifier
may identify their real-limit names only through common finite
$\StreamNameUp$ schedule refinements, the same $\RegSeqRatUp$ tolerance read on
those refinements, transported $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ windows, and the visible
$\hsame$ and $\Cont$ routes carried by the two packets. It has no classifier
row for completed $\RealUp$ equality, quotient-stream equality, or a selected
limit.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the two carriers using \autoref{def:reallimit-bhist-carrier}. The
schedule coordinates $S$ and $S'$ are the only rows that choose finite
observation windows, and the regular-sequence coordinates $Q$ and $Q'$ are the
only rows read at those windows.

Apply \autoref{thm:reallimit-regseqrat-streamname-handoff}. The handoff forces
each real-facing read to pass through the scheduled $\RegSeqRatUp$ source
stream and its $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row.

Thus a classifier comparison can only pass to common finite schedule
refinements, compare the same tolerance read there, and transport the dyadic
windows through $H,H'$ and $C,C'$. Since the carrier has no additional row for
completed real equality, quotient-stream equality, or a selected limit, no such
row can enter the classifier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RealLimit stability obligation]
\label{thm:reallimit-stability-obligation}
Componentwise $\hsame$ transport and visible $\Cont$ refinement preserve the
selected $\StreamNameUp$ schedule, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ source stream, the
$\RealUp$ seal endpoint, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance rows, the $\Pkg$
provenance row, and the local $\NameCert_{\RealLimitUp}$ row of an accepted
$\RealLimitUp$ packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project $R=(S,Q,E,D,H,C,P,N)$ from
\autoref{def:reallimit-bhist-carrier}. The coordinates $H$ and $C$ are exactly
the stored transport and refinement witnesses for the visible rows of the
packet.

Use \autoref{thm:reallimit-regseqrat-streamname-handoff} to identify the
consumer path through $S$, $Q$, $D$, $H$, $C$, $P$, and $N$ before the seal
endpoint $E$ is read. Any refinement of the consumer path is therefore a
refinement of these displayed rows.

The componentwise $\hsame$ rows preserve each named coordinate, while the
$\Cont$ routes restrict refinements to the visible observation windows. Hence
the selected schedule, source stream, seal endpoint, dyadic tolerance rows,
package row, and local certificate row remain the same visible packet data.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RealLimit readback obligation]
\label{thm:reallimit-readback-obligation}
Every $\RealUp$ read from an accepted $\RealLimitUp$ packet factors through the
displayed $\RegSeqRatUp$ source stream and the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance
window selected by its $\StreamNameUp$ schedule. The readback cannot inspect a
real-completeness principle, quotient-stream equality, or a selected limiting
object.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the packet $R=(S,Q,E,D,H,C,P,N)$. The endpoint coordinate $E$ is the
only real-facing seal row, and the schedule, source stream, and tolerance
coordinates $S$, $Q$, and $D$ are the finite data attached to that row.

Apply \autoref{thm:reallimit-regseqrat-streamname-handoff}. It routes every
read of $E$ through the scheduled regular rational stream, the displayed
dyadic tolerance window, and the visible transport, continuation, package, and
certificate rows.

No coordinate of \autoref{def:reallimit-bhist-carrier} stores completeness,
quotient-stream equality, or a selected limit. Therefore the readback surface
has no source from which any of those objects can be read.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RealLimit ledger obligation]
\label{thm:reallimit-ledger-obligation}
The $\RealLimitUp$ ledger exposes only $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$,
$\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, L0--L5 kernel rows, $\Pkg$, and
$\NameCert_{\RealLimitUp}$. It exposes no completed real, quotient stream, host
equality, analytic convergence theorem, or selected limit.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the provenance and local ledger coordinates $P$ and $N$ from
\autoref{def:reallimit-bhist-carrier}. By definition, $P$ names the
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, and L0--L5
kernel dependencies, while $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RealLimitUp}$ row.

Apply \autoref{thm:reallimit-regseqrat-streamname-handoff}. The handoff shows
that the real-facing endpoint is reached only through the same finite schedule,
regular-sequence, dyadic tolerance, transport, continuation, package, and
certificate rows.

The ledger is therefore exhausted by the displayed package and certificate
coordinates. Since no carrier coordinate names a completed real, quotient
stream, host equality, analytic convergence theorem, or selected limit, the
ledger cannot expose any of them.
\end{proof}
