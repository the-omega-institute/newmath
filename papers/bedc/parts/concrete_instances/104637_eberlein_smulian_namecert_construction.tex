It sits beside the sequential compactness carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-sequentialcompact-namecert}, the compact metric surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, the metric row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, and the Cauchy sequence space boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchysequencespace-namecert}. \begin{definition}[Eberlein--Smulian finite weak-window carrier]
\label{def:eberlein-smulian-finite-weak-window-carrier}
An $\EberleinSmulianUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(F,H,K,M,Q,W,U,L,E,T,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $F$ is the functional-analysis source row naming the bounded linear-test context and the finite weak-test family inspected by the packet.
\item $H$ is the Hilbert or Banach-facing source row that supplies the normed carrier, bounded sequence cells, and finite comparison windows.
\item $K$ is the compactness handoff row, read through $\CompactMetricUp$ and $\SequentialCompactUp$ sibling surfaces when a finite compact window is displayed.
\item $M$ is the metric row used to compare finite windows and to route Cauchy-style estimates through the sibling metric certificate.
\item $Q$ is the Cauchy sequence equivalence boundary: two displayed subsequence windows are compared only through finite regular-window reads and not through quotient sequence equality.
\item $W$ is the weak-test ledger listing the finitely many linear tests, tolerances, and observation windows requested by the consumer.
\item $U$ is the subsequence-selection row recording the displayed indices retained at each finite weak-test window.
\item $L$ is the compactness handoff ledger that repacks the retained subsequence windows as a compact or sequentially compact local packet.
\item $E$ is the terminal weak-cluster or convergence seal offered to downstream consumers after the ledgers have been read.
\item $T$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $F,H,K,M,Q,W,U,L,E$.
\item $C$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ replay routes from weak tests to retained subsequence windows and from compactness handoff to the terminal seal.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over the source rows, weak-test ledger, subsequence rows, compactness handoff, transport, replay, and local naming.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\EberleinSmulianUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares two accepted packets only through these rows. It has no coordinate for a global weak topology, an unrestricted functional separator, a choice function selecting subsequences from arbitrary sequences, a quotient of sequence tails, or an ambient compactness theorem.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Eberlein--Smulian seed obligations]
\label{thm:eberlein-smulian-seed-obligations}
For every accepted $\EberleinSmulianUp$ carrier $S=(F,H,K,M,Q,W,U,L,E,T,C,P,N)$, the local NameCert surface consists of:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the finite weak-window packet of \autoref{def:eberlein-smulian-finite-weak-window-carrier};
\item weak-test exposure through the finite ledger $W$ over the displayed source rows $F$ and $H$;
\item subsequence-window exposure through $U$, with retained indices visible before any cluster or convergence seal is exported;
\item compactness handoff through $K$ and $L$, routed through the sibling compact and sequential compactness certificates rather than through open-cover compactness;
\item metric and Cauchy-window comparison through $M$ and $Q$ before any equality or convergence read is consumed;
\item componentwise transport, replay, provenance, and local naming through $T,C,P,N$.
\end{enumerate}
These obligations do not include Banach-space weak compactness in full generality, unrestricted linear-functional choice, countable choice, quotient sequence equality, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier rows of \autoref{def:eberlein-smulian-finite-weak-window-carrier}. The rows $F,H,K,M,Q,W,U,L,E,T,C,P,N$ are the only data visible to the local classifier.

The weak-test side is finite. The ledger $W$ lists the requested tests and tolerances, and the source rows $F,H$ bind those tests to the displayed normed or Hilbert-facing carrier before a subsequence row is read.

The subsequence side is also finite. The row $U$ records the retained indices for the inspected windows, while $M$ and $Q$ keep metric comparison and Cauchy-window comparison inside displayed rows.

The compactness handoff is downstream from those ledgers. The rows $K$ and $L$ repack the retained windows through the compact metric and sequential compactness sibling surfaces, and $E$ is exposed only after $T,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same packet. Since the excluded principles are absent from the carrier, they cannot discharge a local NameCert obligation.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Eberlein--Smulian nonescape boundary]
\label{thm:eberlein-smulian-nonescape-boundary}
Every consumer of an accepted $\EberleinSmulianUp$ packet factors through the finite route
$$
W \longmapsto U \longmapsto (K,L) \longmapsto (M,Q) \longmapsto E
$$
with componentwise $\hsame$ transport, $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert_{\EberleinSmulianUp}$ evidence. Thus a weak-cluster or sequential-compactness read cannot bypass the weak-test ledger, the retained subsequence windows, the compactness handoff, or the metric and Cauchy-window comparison rows.

The boundary exports no unrestricted weak topology, no selected subsequence for arbitrary input streams, no quotient equality of sequence tails, no open-cover compactness theorem, and no ambient completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted packet $S$. A consumer reaches the terminal seal $E$ only by following the continuation row $C$, so its first readable data are the weak-test ledger $W$ and the displayed source rows $F,H$.

The retained windows are then read through $U$. The indices are visible finite rows, and the comparison rows $M,Q$ keep the resulting tail and equality checks inside displayed metric and Cauchy-window data.

Next read the compactness handoff. The rows $K,L$ connect the retained windows to the compact metric and sequential compactness sibling certificates before any weak-cluster or convergence seal can be consumed.

Finally, $T,C,P,N$ bind the route to one NameCert packet. Since the carrier contains none of the excluded global objects, the consumer has no escape route around the displayed finite ledgers.
\end{proof}
