Following Hardy and Knopp, the $\ArithmeticoGeometricSequenceUp$ packet records the finite BEDC surface for a sequence whose next observation is generated from an initial value, a fixed multiplier, and an inhomogeneous geometric row. It reads the finite stream windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The chapter names the recurrence evidence used by downstream $\RealUp$ sequence calculus; it does not assert a closed-form host equality, selected infinite stream, quotient sequence identity, countable choice, or ambient real completeness.

\begin{definition}[Arithmetico-geometric sequence carrier]
\label{def:arithmetico-geometric-sequence-carrier}
An $\ArithmeticoGeometricSequenceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
A=(I,M,G,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $I$ is the initial-value row, recording the first named rational or real-facing approximation from which the recurrence is read.
\item $M$ is the multiplier row, displaying the fixed scalar used by each recurrence step.
\item $G$ is the inhomogeneous geometric row, carrying the finite geometric contribution read at the displayed step.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ observation row, listing the finite sequence windows and indices exposed to a consumer.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row, converting the displayed windows into regular rational evidence before any real seal is consumed.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row, reached only after the initial, multiplier, geometric, stream-window, and regular-readback rows have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the initial value, multiplier, inhomogeneous geometric row, stream observations, regular readback, real seal, replay, provenance, and local name.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a finite index and tolerance request through the same recurrence rows.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ArithmeticoGeometricSequenceUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ArithmeticoGeometricSequenceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only through $I,M,G,W,R,E,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for a completed infinite sequence object, host closed-form equality, quotient stream equality, selected limit, countable choice, or ambient completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Arithmetico-geometric sequence NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:arithmetico-geometric-sequence-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\ArithmeticoGeometricSequenceUp$ carrier
$$
A=(I,M,G,W,R,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\ArithmeticoGeometricSequenceUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the initial value, fixed multiplier, inhomogeneous geometric row, StreamName observations, RegSeqRat readback, Real seal, componentwise transport, displayed replay, provenance, and local naming; recurrence visibility requiring every displayed step to read $I,M,G,W$ before $R$; seal discipline requiring every $\RealUp$ consumer to pass through $R$ before $E$; and nonescape excluding host closed forms, quotient stream equality, selected limits, countable choice, and ambient real completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:arithmetico-geometric-sequence-carrier}. The accepted packet displays exactly the rows $I,M,G,W,R,E,H,C,P,N$.

The recurrence read starts with $I,M,G,W$. The initial-value row fixes the displayed starting observation, the multiplier row records the scalar reused by the finite recurrence route, the inhomogeneous geometric row supplies the finite forcing term, and the StreamName row lists the inspected sequence windows.

The regular readback and seal rows are downstream. A consumer must pass through $R$ before it can read the $\RealUp$ seal $E$, and componentwise $\hsame$ transport in $H$ together with displayed $\Cont$ replay in $C$ preserves the same recurrence route.

Finally, $P$ and $N$ only source and name the displayed packet. Since the carrier has no coordinate for a completed infinite stream, a host closed-form equality, quotient sequence identity, countable choice, selected limits, or ambient completeness, the listed obligations exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}
