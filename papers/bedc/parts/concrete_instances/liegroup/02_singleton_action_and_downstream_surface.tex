\begin{definition}[LieGroup singleton translation and conjugation rows]
\label{def:liegroup-singleton-translation-and-conjugation-rows}
For a carried singleton parameter $s$ and carried singleton input $x$, the
action rows attached to
\autoref{def:liegroup-singleton-smooth-group-carrier} are
$$
\mathsf{Left}_s(x):=\mathsf{mul}(s,x),\qquad
\mathsf{Right}_s(x):=\mathsf{mul}(x,s),
$$
$$
\mathsf{Conj}_s(x):=\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(s,x),\mathsf{inv}(s)).
$$
The adjoint readback row is the differential of $\mathsf{Conj}_s$ at $\emp$,
read on the singleton tangent carrier as the constant zero endomap of the
singleton $\LieAlgebraUp$ carrier. Thus translation, conjugation, and adjoint
readback all use the same carried predicate $L_{\emp}$ and the same singleton
chart endpoint.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[LieGroup singleton left-translation smoothness]
\label{thm:liegroup-singleton-left-translation-smooth}
If $L_{\emp}(s)$ and $L_{\emp}(x)$ hold, then
$\mathsf{Left}_s(x)$ from
\autoref{def:liegroup-singleton-translation-and-conjugation-rows} is carried by
$L_{\emp}$, is classified by the singleton classifier with endpoint $\emp$, and
is read in the singleton atlas as the constant smooth transition on the sole
overlap.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The group row of
$\mathsf{BEDC.Derived.GroupUp.GroupSingletonHistory\_laws}$ gives closure of
$\mathsf{mul}(s,x)$ under the singleton carrier whenever both inputs are carried
by $L_{\emp}$. This is exactly the left-translation carrier row after unfolding
\autoref{def:liegroup-singleton-translation-and-conjugation-rows}.

By \autoref{thm:liegroup-singleton-carrier-obligation}, the carried group result
is the same object as the singleton manifold point. Therefore the sole chart
domain accepts the result and reads it at the constant endpoint $0_{\RealUp}$.

The overlap transition is the singleton constant transition supplied by
\autoref{thm:manifold-singleton-transition-smoothness}. Hence the left
translation is smooth as a singleton-atlas self-map.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LieGroup singleton right-translation smoothness]
\label{thm:liegroup-singleton-right-translation-smooth}
If $L_{\emp}(s)$ and $L_{\emp}(x)$ hold, then
$\mathsf{Right}_s(x)$ from
\autoref{def:liegroup-singleton-translation-and-conjugation-rows} is carried by
$L_{\emp}$, is classified by the singleton classifier with endpoint $\emp$, and
is read in the singleton atlas as the constant smooth transition on the sole
overlap.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The multiplication-closure row of
$\mathsf{BEDC.Derived.GroupUp.GroupSingletonHistory\_laws}$ applies symmetrically
to $\mathsf{mul}(x,s)$. Since $L_{\emp}(x)$ and $L_{\emp}(s)$ hold, the

The carrier identification in
\autoref{thm:liegroup-singleton-carrier-obligation} then moves this accepted
group value into the singleton chart domain without changing the predicate being
used. The classifier endpoint is still $\emp$.

Finally, \autoref{thm:manifold-singleton-transition-smoothness} reads the only
self-overlap as a constant smooth transition. Thus the right translation is a
smooth singleton-atlas self-map.
\end{proof}

\autoref{thm:liegroup-singleton-left-translation-smooth} to $s$ and $x$. The

Next use the inverse-closure row from

Apply the right-translation row

$\mathsf{mul}(s,x)$ and the carried inverse $\mathsf{inv}(s)$. The resulting

The $\LieAlgebraUp$ singleton rows identify the Lie-algebra carrier with the same
singleton tangent carrier at $\emp$. Therefore the conjugation differential is
the constant zero endomap on that carrier, which is exactly the adjoint readback
row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LieGroup singleton conjugation action law]
\label{thm:liegroup-singleton-conjugation-action-law}
For carried histories $L_{\emp}(s)$, $L_{\emp}(t)$, and $L_{\emp}(x)$, the two
iterated endpoints
$$
\mathsf{Ad}_{\emp}(s,\mathsf{Ad}_{\emp}(t,x))
$$
and
$$
\mathsf{Ad}_{\emp}(\mathsf{append}(s,t),x)
$$
are both classified by the singleton classifier $R_{\emp}$, hence represent the
same carried singleton endpoint.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First unfold the action on the left. The inner endpoint is
$\mathsf{append}(\mathsf{append}(t,x),\emp)$, and
\autoref{thm:liegroup-singleton-conjugation-carrier-and-smoothness} carries it
back into $L_{\emp}$. Applying the same theorem to $s$ and this inner endpoint
therefore carries the whole left-hand endpoint.

Next unfold the action on the right. Group singleton closure carries
$\mathsf{append}(s,t)$, and another application of
\autoref{thm:liegroup-singleton-conjugation-carrier-and-smoothness} carries
$\mathsf{Ad}_{\emp}(\mathsf{append}(s,t),x)$.

It remains to compare the classifier rows. By the singleton group classifier,
every carried endpoint is $\hsame$ to $\emp$. The $\Cont$ associativity and
readback rows allow the nested append words on both sides to be read as
singleton terminal words, and the group singleton closure removes the terminal
factor. Thus both displayed endpoints satisfy $R_{\emp}$ against $\emp$, and
therefore against each other by the restricted singleton classifier.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.LieGroupUp.LieGroupSingleton\_conjugation\_action\_law}
