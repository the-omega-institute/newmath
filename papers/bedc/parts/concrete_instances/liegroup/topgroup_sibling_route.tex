\begin{theorem}[LieGroup singleton TopGroup sibling route]
\label{thm:liegroup-singleton-topgroup-sibling-route}
The singleton smooth-group package of $\LieGroupUp$ is readable by the
$\TopGroupUp$ sibling surface through the same singleton $\GroupUp$ operation
rows. The product and inverse rows of
\autoref{thm:liegroup-singleton-operation-smoothness-obligation} land on the
continuous product and inverse obligation families of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-topgroup-namecert}, as organized by
\autoref{thm:topgroup-product-inverse-obligation-triad} and
\autoref{thm:topgroup-cont-ledger-operation-exhaustion}. This route exports no
non-singleton Lie theory, ambient topology beyond the displayed continuous
operation rows, or $\bridgeCheckedV$ claim.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
By \autoref{thm:liegroup-singleton-source-lock}, the $\LieGroupUp$ carrier and
classifier are the singleton history predicate $L_{\emp}$ and the restricted
$\hsame$ row already used by the singleton $\GroupUp$ package. Thus the
operation side of the package exposes multiplication, inverse, and unit through
the same finite group rows rather than through a separate Lie-theoretic carrier.

The smoothness obligation
\autoref{thm:liegroup-singleton-operation-smoothness-obligation} reads those
two operation graphs through the singleton chart as constant smooth maps. The
$\TopGroupUp$ chapter reads exactly the analogous public operation boundary as
product-neighbourhood and inverse-neighbourhood continuity rows; its
\autoref{thm:topgroup-product-inverse-obligation-triad} repacks carrier
compatibility, product transport, and inverse transport, and
\autoref{thm:topgroup-cont-ledger-operation-exhaustion} exhausts the finite
$\Cont$ ledger by those two operation families.

Therefore the sibling route is the common singleton $\GroupUp$ operation
surface together with the displayed continuity rows. The route does not add a
non-singleton Lie group, external topology source, exponential or
representation theory, or a verified standard bridge.
\end{proof}
