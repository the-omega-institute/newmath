\begin{theorem}[List spine bridge source refinement]
\label{thm:list-spine-bridge-source-refinement}
Let $A,B:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be BEDC history source carriers, and
let $\sim_A$ and $\sim_B$ be source classifiers. The ten-field fit is:
traditional target finite-list source refinement; BEDC source $\mathsf{BHist}$
with $\mathsf{ListSpineRep}$; carrier $\mathsf{ListHistoryCarrier}$; classifier
$\mathsf{ListSpineBridgeClassifier}$; stability obligation carrier and
classifier preservation under source refinement; ledger finite spine
representation; core theorem the three displayed implications below; exactness
inherited from $\mathsf{ListSpineRep}$; standard bridge finite-spine readback;
status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$. Assume the source carrier and source classifier
of $A$ are included in those of $B$:
$$
\forall h:\mathsf{BHist}.\; A(h)\to B(h)
$$
and
$$
\forall x\,y:\mathsf{BHist}.\; x\sim_A y\to x\sim_B y.
$$
Then the displayed list-spine data transport from $A$ to $B$:
$$
\mathsf{ListSpineRep}_A(h,xs)
\to
\mathsf{ListSpineRep}_B(h,xs),
$$
$$
\mathsf{ListSpineBridgeClassifier}_{A,\sim_A}(h,k)
\to
\mathsf{ListSpineBridgeClassifier}_{B,\sim_B}(h,k),
$$
and
$$
\mathsf{ListSpineHistoryClassifier}_{A,\sim_A}(h,k)
\to
\mathsf{ListSpineHistoryClassifier}_{B,\sim_B}(h,k).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Proceed by induction on the displayed $\mathsf{ListSpineRep}_A(h,xs)$ witness.
The nil representation keeps the same endpoint equality. In the cons case, the
carrier inclusion turns the recorded source datum $A(a)$ into $B(a)$, while the
induction hypothesis transports the tail representation and the continuation
and endpoint witnesses are reused unchanged. For the finite-spine classifier
component, induct on the displayed $\mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(\sim_A,xs,ys)$
witness and apply the classifier inclusion at each cons head. A bridge witness is
then repacked with the transported left and right spines and the transported
finite-spine classifier. The certificate classifier clause follows by unfolding
that it is the bridge classifier and rebuilding the same endpoints.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[List spine representation endpoint transport]
\label{lem:list-spine-representation-endpoint-transport}
Let $A:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be a BEDC history source carrier. For all
histories $h$ and $h'$ and every finite list spine $xs$,
$\mathsf{ListSpineRep}_A(h,xs)$ and $\hsame(h,h')$ imply
$\mathsf{ListSpineRep}_A(h',xs)$.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Proceed by induction on the displayed finite spine. The empty spine is transported
by composing the endpoint empty witness with $\hsame(h,h')$. For a cons spine,
keep the recorded head payload and tail representation, and compose the outer
history endpoint witness with $\hsame(h,h')$; the payload and tail witnesses are
unchanged.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[List spine bridge nil-cons separation under coherence]
\label{thm:list-spine-bridge-nil-cons-separation-under-coherence}
Let $A:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be a BEDC history source carrier, let
$\sim_A$ be a source classifier, and assume
$\mathsf{ListSpineRepCoherent}_{A,\sim_A}$. For all histories $h$ and $k$, all
source histories $a$, and all finite spines $xs$, the bridge classifier separates
the nil and cons displayed endpoints:
$$
\mathsf{ListSpineRep}_A(h,\mathsf{nil})
\to
\mathsf{ListSpineRep}_A(k,\mathsf{cons}(a,xs))
\to
\mathsf{ListSpineBridgeClassifier}_{A,\sim_A}(h,k)
\to
\bot.
$$
It also separates the symmetric cons-vs-nil orientation:
$$
\mathsf{ListSpineRep}_A(h,\mathsf{cons}(a,xs))
\to
\mathsf{ListSpineRep}_A(k,\mathsf{nil})
\to
\mathsf{ListSpineBridgeClassifier}_{A,\sim_A}(h,k)
\to
\bot.
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the bridge witness into finite spines $u$ and $v$, endpoint presentations
$\mathsf{ListSpineRep}_A(h,u)$ and $\mathsf{ListSpineRep}_A(k,v)$, and a
finite-spine classifier $\mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(\sim_A,u,v)$. In the
nil-vs-cons orientation, apply the coherence datum at endpoint $h$ to relate the
displayed $\mathsf{nil}$ spine to $u$, and at endpoint $k$ to relate $v$ to the
displayed $\mathsf{cons}(a,xs)$ spine. By
Theorem~\ref{thm:list-classifier-case-exactness}, the comparison from
$\mathsf{nil}$ to $u$ forces $u$ to have nil outer shape, and the bridge
classifier from $u$ to $v$ then forces $v$ to have nil outer shape. The
comparison from $v$ to the displayed $\mathsf{cons}(a,xs)$ spine is therefore a
mixed nil-cons classifier witness, contrary to
Lemma~\ref{lem:list-classifier-nil-cons-no-confusion}. In the cons-vs-nil
orientation, the first coherence comparison forces $u$ to have cons outer shape,
the bridge classifier forces $v$ to have cons outer shape, and the endpoint
coherence comparison against the displayed nil spine is the symmetric impossible
mixed classifier witness.
\end{proof}

$\ContR(b,u,q)$, and $\hsame(k,\Eone(q))$. Apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:list-public-classifier-cons-endpoint-inversion} to the two
displayed cons endpoints and the original public classifier witness. It yields
$\mathsf{Rel}(a,b)$ and
$\mathsf{ListSpineHistoryClassifier}_{A,\mathsf{Rel}}(t,u)$, which together
with the displayed endpoint data give the required existential witness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[List public carrier constructor readback under spine coherence]
\label{thm:list-public-carrier-constructor-readback-under-spine-coherence}
Let $A:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be a BEDC history source carrier, let
$\mathsf{Rel}:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be a source
classifier, and assume $\mathsf{ListSpineRepCoherent}_{A,\mathsf{Rel}}$. The
ten-field fit is: traditional target finite-list carrier endpoint readback;
BEDC source $\mathsf{BHist}$ with $\ContR$; carrier
$\mathsf{ListHistoryCarrier}_A$; classifier
$\mathsf{ListSpineHistoryClassifier}_{A,\mathsf{Rel}}$; stability from spine
coherence; ledger one generated history endpoint; core theorem public carrier
nil-or-cons readback; exactness by generated carrier cases, history
no-confusion, spine coverage, and classifier constructor inversion; standard
bridge finite spines; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$. For every history
$h:\mathsf{BHist}$, if $\mathsf{ListHistoryCarrier}_A(h)$ holds, then
$$
\begin{array}{l}
\left(
\begin{array}{l}
\hsame(h,\emp)\land\\
\neg\exists a\,t\,p:\mathsf{BHist}.\;
\left(
\begin{array}{l}
A(a)\land \mathsf{ListHistoryCarrier}_A(t)\land
\ContR(a,t,p)\land\\
\hsame(h,\Eone(p))
\end{array}
\right)
\end{array}
\right)\\
\lor\\
\exists a\,t\,p:\mathsf{BHist}.\;
\left(
\begin{array}{l}
A(a)\land \mathsf{ListHistoryCarrier}_A(t)\land
\ContR(a,t,p)\land \hsame(h,\Eone(p))\land\\
\neg\hsame(h,\emp)\land\\
\forall a'\,t'\,p':\mathsf{BHist}.\;
\left(
\begin{array}{l}
A(a')\land \mathsf{ListHistoryCarrier}_A(t')\land
\ContR(a',t',p')\land\\
\hsame(h,\Eone(p'))
\end{array}
\right)
\to
\left(
\mathsf{Rel}(a,a')\land
\mathsf{ListSpineHistoryClassifier}_{A,\mathsf{Rel}}(t,t')
\right)
\end{array}
\right).
\end{array}
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply Theorem~\ref{thm:list-history-carrier-generated-cases} to the displayed
carrier witness for $h$. The nil case gives $\hsame(h,\emp)$. If a cons
presentation of the same endpoint also existed, composing the empty endpoint
with the displayed $\Eone$ endpoint would give $\hsame(\emp,\Eone(p))$ for some
$p$, contradicting Theorem~\ref{thm:history-no-confusion} and yielding the nil
alternative.

In the cons case, keep the displayed histories $a,t,p$ with $A(a)$,
$\mathsf{ListHistoryCarrier}_A(t)$, $\ContR(a,t,p)$, and
$\hsame(h,\Eone(p))$. Nonemptiness follows by the same no-confusion argument:
an additional $\hsame(h,\emp)$ witness would compose with $\hsame(h,\Eone(p))$
to contradict Theorem~\ref{thm:history-no-confusion}.

It remains to compare any alternate cons presentation
$a',t',p':\mathsf{BHist}$ of the same $h$. Apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:list-spine-representation-coverage} to the two tail carrier
witnesses for $t$ and $t'$, obtaining finite tail spines $xs$ and $xs'$ with
$\mathsf{ListSpineRep}_A(t,xs)$ and $\mathsf{ListSpineRep}_A(t',xs')$.

Build two cons spine representations of $h$, one over $\mathsf{cons}(a,xs)$
and one over $\mathsf{cons}(a',xs')$. The assumed
$\mathsf{ListSpineRepCoherent}_{A,\mathsf{Rel}}$ classifies these two
presentations by $\mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(\mathsf{Rel},\mathsf{cons}(a,xs),
\mathsf{cons}(a',xs'))$.

Invert this finite-spine classifier through the cons branch of
Theorem~\ref{thm:list-classifier-case-exactness}. The inversion yields
$\mathsf{Rel}(a,a')$ and
$\mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(\mathsf{Rel},xs,xs')$.

Repack the two tail representations and the finite-spine tail classifier through
Definition~\ref{def:list-spine-representation} to obtain
$\mathsf{ListSpineHistoryClassifier}_{A,\mathsf{Rel}}(t,t')$.
\end{proof}
