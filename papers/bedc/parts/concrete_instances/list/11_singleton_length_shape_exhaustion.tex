\section{Singleton source length shape exhaustion}
\label{sec:list-singleton-source-length-shape-exhaustion}

\begin{theorem}[List singleton source public length shape exhaustion]
\label{thm:list-singleton-source-public-length-shape-exhaustion}
Fix a BEDC history $a_0:\mathsf{BHist}$ and let $A_{a_0}$ be the singleton
history source of Definition~\ref{def:list-singleton-history-source}. For every
history $h$, if $\mathsf{ListHistoryCarrier}_{A_{a_0}}(h)$ holds, then one of
the following two disjoint public length shapes is available:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item $\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_{A_{a_0}}(h,0)\land \hsame(h,\emp)$.
  \item There exist a finite length value $n$ and histories
  $a,t,p:\mathsf{BHist}$ such that
  $$
  \left(
  \begin{array}{l}
  \mathsf{ListPublicLength}_{A_{a_0}}(h,n+1)\land
  A_{a_0}(a)\land\\
  \mathsf{ListHistoryCarrier}_{A_{a_0}}(t)\land
  \mathsf{ListPublicLength}_{A_{a_0}}(t,n)\land\\
  \ContR(a,t,p)\land \hsame(h,\Eone(p))
  \end{array}
  \right).
  $$
\end{enumerate}
The nil alternative and every displayed cons alternative are disjoint by BEDC
history no-confusion between $\emp$ and $\Eone(p)$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply Theorem~\ref{thm:list-singleton-source-finite-data-certificate-package}
at the fixed singleton source. Its totality clause gives a finite length value
$m$ with $\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_{A_{a_0}}(h,m)$.

Case-analyze the finite length value $m$.

In the zero case, the zero readback clause of
Theorem~\ref{thm:list-singleton-source-finite-data-certificate-package}
identifies $\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_{A_{a_0}}(h,0)$ with
$\hsame(h,\emp)$. Pair the given zero public length witness with this endpoint
witness to obtain the nil alternative.

In the successor case, write $m=n+1$. The successor exactness clause of
Theorem~\ref{thm:list-singleton-source-finite-data-certificate-package}
turns $\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_{A_{a_0}}(h,n+1)$ into histories
$a,t,p:\mathsf{BHist}$ with $A_{a_0}(a)$,
$\mathsf{ListHistoryCarrier}_{A_{a_0}}(t)$,
$\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_{A_{a_0}}(t,n)$, $\ContR(a,t,p)$, and
$\hsame(h,\Eone(p))$. These data are exactly the displayed cons alternative.

For disjointness, suppose the nil alternative and one displayed cons
alternative both hold. Compose symmetry of the nil endpoint witness
$\hsame(h,\emp)$ with the cons endpoint witness $\hsame(h,\Eone(p))$ to obtain
$\hsame(\emp,\Eone(p))$. The BEDC history no-confusion theorem,
Theorem~\ref{thm:history-no-confusion}, excludes this empty-versus-one
identification.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ListUp.FramedListHistoryCarrier\_public\_length\_shape\_exhaustion}

\begin{theorem}[List singleton source classified shape synchrony]
\label{thm:list-singleton-source-classified-shape-synchrony}
Fix a BEDC history $a_0:\mathsf{BHist}$ and let $A_{a_0}$ be the singleton
history source of Definition~\ref{def:list-singleton-history-source}. For all
histories $h,k:\mathsf{BHist}$, if
$\mathsf{ListSpineHistoryClassifier}_{A_{a_0},\hsame}(h,k)$ holds, then
exactly one synchronized public endpoint shape is available:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item
  $$
  \mathsf{ListPublicLength}_{A_{a_0}}(h,0)\land
  \mathsf{ListPublicLength}_{A_{a_0}}(k,0)\land
  \hsame(h,\emp)\land \hsame(k,\emp).
  $$
  \item There exist a finite length value $n$ and histories
  $a,b,t,u,p,q:\mathsf{BHist}$ such that
  $$
  \left(
  \begin{array}{l}
  \mathsf{ListPublicLength}_{A_{a_0}}(h,n+1)\land
  \mathsf{ListPublicLength}_{A_{a_0}}(k,n+1)\land\\
  A_{a_0}(a)\land A_{a_0}(b)\land
  \mathsf{ListHistoryCarrier}_{A_{a_0}}(t)\land
  \mathsf{ListHistoryCarrier}_{A_{a_0}}(u)\land\\
  \mathsf{ListPublicLength}_{A_{a_0}}(t,n)\land
  \mathsf{ListPublicLength}_{A_{a_0}}(u,n)\land\\
  \ContR(a,t,p)\land \ContR(b,u,q)\land
  \hsame(h,\Eone(p))\land \hsame(k,\Eone(q))\land\\
  \hsame(a,b)\land
  \mathsf{ListSpineHistoryClassifier}_{A_{a_0},\hsame}(t,u)
  \end{array}
  \right).
  $$
\end{enumerate}
The nil endpoint shape and every displayed cons endpoint shape are mutually
exclusive by BEDC history no-confusion between $\emp$ and $\Eone(p)$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:list-singleton-source-finite-data-exit-constructor-audit} to
the fixed singleton source. For the given classifier witness, its public data
give a common endpoint length, public-length uniqueness, successor exactness,
finite endpoint spine ledgers, and the nil-nil or cons-cons constructor
disjunction.

In the nil-nil constructor case, the zero-length characterization in the same
certificate turns $\hsame(h,\emp)$ and $\hsame(k,\emp)$ into
$\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_{A_{a_0}}(h,0)$ and
$\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_{A_{a_0}}(k,0)$. Pair these two public lengths with
the two endpoint witnesses to obtain the first alternative.

In the cons-cons constructor case, the constructor disjunction supplies
histories $a,b,t,u,p,q:\mathsf{BHist}$ with both heads in $A_{a_0}$, both tails
in $\mathsf{ListHistoryCarrier}_{A_{a_0}}$, both $\ContR$ ledgers, both endpoint
readbacks, $\hsame(a,b)$, and the tail classifier
$\mathsf{ListSpineHistoryClassifier}_{A_{a_0},\hsame}(t,u)$.

Apply the common endpoint length clause of
Theorem~\ref{thm:list-singleton-source-finite-data-exit-constructor-audit} to
the tail classifier. This gives a finite value $n$ with
$\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_{A_{a_0}}(t,n)$ and
$\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_{A_{a_0}}(u,n)$. The reverse direction of successor
exactness transports the two $\ContR$ ledgers and these tail lengths to
$\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_{A_{a_0}}(h,n+1)$ and
$\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_{A_{a_0}}(k,n+1)$.

The endpoint spine ledgers supplied by the certificate give the carrier
presentations needed to apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:list-singleton-source-public-length-shape-exhaustion} to the
classified endpoints $h$ and $k$. Apply the same theorem to the exposed tails
$t$ and $u$ using their carrier witnesses. These four one-endpoint applications
attach the public readback shape witnesses to the data supplied by the
constructor audit.

In the present cons-cons branch, endpoint nil alternatives would identify
$\emp$ with $\Eone(p)$ or with $\Eone(q)$ after symmetry and transitivity of
$\hsame$, so Theorem~\ref{thm:history-no-confusion} excludes them.
Public-length uniqueness then identifies the resulting endpoint successor
lengths with the $n+1$ lengths already obtained from the tail classifier, while
the displayed tail public lengths remain the common length data for $t$ and
$u$.

Collect the common length $n$ and the six constructor histories. The displayed
data are exactly the second alternative. The same no-confusion argument
separates the nil endpoint shape from any cons endpoint shape.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[List singleton source classified zero length iff empty]
\label{thm:list-singleton-source-classified-zero-length-iff-empty}
Fix a BEDC history $a_0:\mathsf{BHist}$ and let $A_{a_0}$ be the singleton
history source of Definition~\ref{def:list-singleton-history-source}. For all
histories $h,k:\mathsf{BHist}$, the following conditions are equivalent:
$$
\left(
\begin{array}{l}
\mathsf{ListSpineHistoryClassifier}_{A_{a_0},\hsame}(h,k)\land\\
\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_{A_{a_0}}(h,0)\land
\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_{A_{a_0}}(k,0)
\end{array}
\right)
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
\left(\hsame(h,\Ezero(\emp))\land \hsame(k,\Ezero(\emp))\right).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the forward direction, start from the classifier witness and the two
zero public-length witnesses. The zero-length characterization in
Theorem~\ref{thm:list-singleton-source-finite-data-certificate-package} turns the
$h$-side witness into $\hsame(h,\Ezero(\emp))$ and the $k$-side witness into
$\hsame(k,\Ezero(\emp))$.

The two endpoint comparisons are exactly the displayed empty endpoint
conjunction. The classifier witness is used only to keep the statement tied to
classified singleton-source endpoints.

For the reverse direction, assume
$\hsame(h,\Ezero(\emp))\land\hsame(k,\Ezero(\emp))$. Apply the
reverse zero-length characterization from
Theorem~\ref{thm:list-singleton-source-finite-data-certificate-package} to obtain
$\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_{A_{a_0}}(h,0)$ and
$\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_{A_{a_0}}(k,0)$.

It remains to rebuild the endpoint classifier. Use the nil-nil branch of the
constructor-disjunction field in
Theorem~\ref{thm:list-singleton-source-finite-data-exit-constructor-audit}; the
two empty endpoint comparisons are precisely the nil-nil data. Together with the
two reconstructed zero public-length witnesses, this gives the left-hand
condition.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[List singleton source classified successor length inversion]
\label{thm:list-singleton-source-classified-successor-length-inversion}
Fix a BEDC history $a_0:\mathsf{BHist}$ and let $A_{a_0}$ be the singleton
history source of Definition~\ref{def:list-singleton-history-source}. For all
histories $h,k:\mathsf{BHist}$ and every finite length value $n$, if
$$
\mathsf{ListSpineHistoryClassifier}_{A_{a_0},\hsame}(h,k)\land
\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_{A_{a_0}}(h,n+1)\land
\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_{A_{a_0}}(k,n+1),
$$
then there exist histories $a,b,t,u,p,q:\mathsf{BHist}$ such that
$$
\left(
\begin{array}{l}
A_{a_0}(a)\land A_{a_0}(b)\land
\mathsf{ListHistoryCarrier}_{A_{a_0}}(t)\land
\mathsf{ListHistoryCarrier}_{A_{a_0}}(u)\land\\
\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_{A_{a_0}}(t,n)\land
\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_{A_{a_0}}(u,n)\land\\
\ContR(a,t,p)\land \ContR(b,u,q)\land
\hsame(h,\Eone(p))\land \hsame(k,\Eone(q))\land\\
\hsame(a,b)\land
\mathsf{ListSpineHistoryClassifier}_{A_{a_0},\hsame}(t,u)
\end{array}
\right).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply Theorem~\ref{thm:list-singleton-source-classified-shape-synchrony} to the
classifier witness. It gives the synchronized nil endpoint shape or a
synchronized cons endpoint shape.

In the nil endpoint shape, the theorem supplies
$\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_{A_{a_0}}(h,0)$ and
$\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_{A_{a_0}}(k,0)$. Public-length uniqueness from
Theorem~\ref{thm:list-singleton-source-finite-data-certificate-package}
identifies these zero witnesses with the displayed successor witnesses
$\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_{A_{a_0}}(h,n+1)$ and
$\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_{A_{a_0}}(k,n+1)$. The zero-length characterization
from the same certificate is incompatible with a successor public endpoint, so
the nil branch is empty.

In the cons endpoint shape, the synchrony theorem supplies a finite value $m$
and histories $a,b,t,u,p,q:\mathsf{BHist}$ with the two endpoint public lengths
at $m+1$, both singleton-source heads, both carrier tails, both tail public
lengths at $m$, the two $\ContR$ ledgers, the two endpoint readbacks,
$\hsame(a,b)$, and the tail classifier
$\mathsf{ListSpineHistoryClassifier}_{A_{a_0},\hsame}(t,u)$.

Apply public-length uniqueness from
Theorem~\ref{thm:list-singleton-source-finite-data-certificate-package} to the
public length witnesses for $h$ at $m+1$ and at $n+1$. The same argument for
$k$ gives the identical successor index comparison, and successor injectivity
for finite length values identifies $m$ with $n$.

Transport the two tail public-length witnesses along this length equality.
Keeping the constructor histories and all ledger, endpoint, head, and classifier
witnesses supplied by the cons shape gives exactly the displayed six-history
package.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[List singleton source classified successor component exactness]
\label{thm:list-singleton-source-classified-successor-component-exactness}
Fix a BEDC history $a_0:\mathsf{BHist}$ and let $A_{a_0}$ be the singleton
history source of Definition~\ref{def:list-singleton-history-source}. For all
histories $h,k:\mathsf{BHist}$ and every finite length value $n$, the following
conditions are equivalent:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item
  $\mathsf{ListSpineHistoryClassifier}_{A_{a_0},\hsame}(h,k)$,
  $\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_{A_{a_0}}(h,n+1)$, and
  $\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_{A_{a_0}}(k,n+1)$ all hold.
  \item
  There exist histories $a,b,t,u,p,q:\mathsf{BHist}$ such that
  $$
  \left(
  \begin{array}{l}
  A_{a_0}(a)\land A_{a_0}(b)\land
  \mathsf{ListHistoryCarrier}_{A_{a_0}}(t)\land
  \mathsf{ListHistoryCarrier}_{A_{a_0}}(u)\land\\
  \mathsf{ListPublicLength}_{A_{a_0}}(t,n)\land
  \mathsf{ListPublicLength}_{A_{a_0}}(u,n)\land\\
  \ContR(a,t,p)\land \ContR(b,u,q)\land
  \hsame(h,\Eone(p))\land \hsame(k,\Eone(q))\land\\
  \hsame(a,b)\land
  \mathsf{ListSpineHistoryClassifier}_{A_{a_0},\hsame}(t,u)
  \end{array}
  \right).
  $$
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the forward direction, apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:list-singleton-source-classified-successor-length-inversion} to
the classifier witness and the two displayed successor public lengths. The
result is exactly the six-history component package in the second condition.

For the reverse direction, start from histories $a,b,t,u,p,q:\mathsf{BHist}$
and the displayed component data. The successor-length equivalence field of
Theorem~\ref{thm:list-singleton-source-finite-data-exit-constructor-audit}
applied to $h$ rebuilds
$\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_{A_{a_0}}(h,n+1)$ from $a,t,p$, the tail public length
at $n$, the $\ContR(a,t,p)$ ledger, and the endpoint readback
$\hsame(h,\Eone(p))$.

Apply the same successor-length field to $k$ with $b,u,q$. This rebuilds
$\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_{A_{a_0}}(k,n+1)$ from the second tail public length,
the second $\ContR$ ledger, and the endpoint readback through $\Eone(q)$.

It remains to reconstruct the endpoint classifier. Use the cons branch in the
constructor-disjunction field of
Theorem~\ref{thm:list-singleton-source-finite-data-exit-constructor-audit} with
the same six histories. The supplied head membership, tail carriers, ledgers,
endpoint readbacks, head comparison, and tail classifier satisfy its displayed
premises, so it yields
$\mathsf{ListSpineHistoryClassifier}_{A_{a_0},\hsame}(h,k)$.

Together these three reconstructed witnesses give the first condition.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[List singleton source classified successor endpoint nonempty]
\label{thm:list-singleton-source-classified-successor-endpoint-nonempty}
Fix a BEDC history $a_0:\mathsf{BHist}$ and let $A_{a_0}$ be the singleton
history source of Definition~\ref{def:list-singleton-history-source}. For all
histories $h,k:\mathsf{BHist}$ and every finite length value $n$, if
$$
\mathsf{ListSpineHistoryClassifier}_{A_{a_0},\hsame}(h,k)\land
\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_{A_{a_0}}(h,n+1)\land
\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_{A_{a_0}}(k,n+1),
$$
then
$$
\neg\hsame(h,\emp)\land \neg\hsame(k,\emp).
$$
The ten-field fit is: traditional target finite-list successor endpoint
exclusion; BEDC source $\mathsf{BHist}$ with singleton source $A_{a_0}$;
carrier $\mathsf{ListHistoryCarrier}_{A_{a_0}}$; classifier
$\mathsf{ListSpineHistoryClassifier}_{A_{a_0},\hsame}$; stability from public
successor length inversion; ledger cons endpoint readbacks through $\Eone(p)$
and $\Eone(q)$; core theorem this endpoint exclusion; exactness by BEDC history
no-confusion; standard bridge finite spines; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ListUp.FramedListBridgeClassifier\_public\_successor\_endpoint\_nonempty}
\begin{proof}
Apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:list-singleton-source-classified-successor-length-inversion} to
the classifier witness and the two successor public-length witnesses. It gives
histories $a,b,t,u,p,q:\mathsf{BHist}$, in particular the endpoint readbacks
$\hsame(h,\Eone(p))$ and $\hsame(k,\Eone(q))$.

For the first exclusion, suppose $\hsame(h,\emp)$. Symmetry gives
$\hsame(\emp,h)$, and transitivity with the readback $\hsame(h,\Eone(p))$ gives
$\hsame(\emp,\Eone(p))$. The BEDC history no-confusion theorem,
Theorem~\ref{thm:history-no-confusion}, rules out this empty-versus-one
identification.

The second exclusion is identical with $k$ and $q$. From $\hsame(k,\emp)$,
symmetry and transitivity with $\hsame(k,\Eone(q))$ give
$\hsame(\emp,\Eone(q))$, again contradicting
Theorem~\ref{thm:history-no-confusion}. These two exclusions form the displayed
conjunction.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[List singleton source classified double-successor tail nonempty]
\label{thm:list-singleton-source-classified-double-successor-tail-nonempty}
Fix a BEDC history $a_0:\mathsf{BHist}$ and let $A_{a_0}$ be the singleton
history source of Definition~\ref{def:list-singleton-history-source}. For all
histories $h,k:\mathsf{BHist}$ and every finite length value $n$, if
$$
\mathsf{ListSpineHistoryClassifier}_{A_{a_0},\hsame}(h,k)\land
\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_{A_{a_0}}(h,(n+1)+1)\land
\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_{A_{a_0}}(k,(n+1)+1),
$$
then there exist histories $a,b,t,u,p,q:\mathsf{BHist}$ such that
$$
\left(
\begin{array}{l}
A_{a_0}(a)\land A_{a_0}(b)\land
\mathsf{ListHistoryCarrier}_{A_{a_0}}(t)\land
\mathsf{ListHistoryCarrier}_{A_{a_0}}(u)\land\\
\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_{A_{a_0}}(t,n+1)\land
\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_{A_{a_0}}(u,n+1)\land\\
\ContR(a,t,p)\land \ContR(b,u,q)\land
\hsame(h,\Eone(p))\land \hsame(k,\Eone(q))\land\\
\hsame(a,b)\land
\mathsf{ListSpineHistoryClassifier}_{A_{a_0},\hsame}(t,u)\land\\
\neg\hsame(t,\emp)\land \neg\hsame(u,\emp)
\end{array}
\right).
$$
The ten-field fit is: traditional target finite-list two-step constructor
readback; BEDC source singleton $\mathsf{BHist}$; carrier
$\mathsf{ListHistoryCarrier}$; classifier
$\mathsf{ListSpineHistoryClassifier}$; stability from successor component
exactness; ledger exposed cons endpoints and tail public lengths; core theorem
this block; exactness by endpoint no-confusion through the cited nonempty
theorem; standard bridge finite spines; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:list-singleton-source-classified-successor-component-exactness}
at length $n+1$ to the classifier witness and the two displayed
double-successor public-length witnesses. The forward direction supplies
histories $a,b,t,u,p,q:\mathsf{BHist}$ with the singleton heads, tail carriers,
tail public lengths at $n+1$, the two $\ContR$ ledgers, the endpoint readbacks,
the head comparison, and the tail classifier
$\mathsf{ListSpineHistoryClassifier}_{A_{a_0},\hsame}(t,u)$.

Apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:list-singleton-source-classified-successor-endpoint-nonempty}
to this tail classifier and the two tail public-length witnesses at $n+1$. It
gives $\neg\hsame(t,\emp)\land \neg\hsame(u,\emp)$.

Keeping the same six histories and appending these two tail exclusions to the
successor component package gives the displayed existential conclusion.
\end{proof}
