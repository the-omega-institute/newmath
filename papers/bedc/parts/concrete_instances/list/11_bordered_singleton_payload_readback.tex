\section{Bordered singleton payload readback}
\label{sec:list-bordered-singleton-payload-readback}

\begin{theorem}[List classifier bordered singleton payload readback]
\label{thm:list-classifier-bordered-singleton-payload-readback}
For finite BEDC-history lists $p$, $p'$, $s$, and $s'$ and histories $x$ and
$y$, if
$$
\mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(\hsame,p,p'),
\quad
\mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(\hsame,s,s'),
\quad
\mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(\hsame,p{++}(x::\mathsf{nil}){++}s,
p'{++}(y::\mathsf{nil}){++}s')
$$
hold, then $\hsame(x,y)$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply Theorem~\ref{thm:list-classifier-append-two-sided-cancel-classified} to
the displayed prefix classifiers, suffix classifiers, and bordered singleton
classification. This cancels the classified boundaries and gives
$\mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(\hsame,x::\mathsf{nil},y::\mathsf{nil})$.

Apply Theorem~\ref{thm:list-classifier-hsame-singleton-iff} in the forward
direction. The singleton classifier witness reads back exactly the payload
relation $\hsame(x,y)$.
\end{proof}

The ten-field fit is: traditional target finite-list singleton payload
readback; BEDC source $\mathsf{BHist}$; carrier finite BEDC-history spines;
classifier $\mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(\hsame,-,-)$; stability from classified
prefix and suffix cancellation; ledger hidden prefix and suffix context; core
theorem singleton payload readback; exactness by cancellation and singleton
inversion; standard bridge finite-spine payload extraction; status
$\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.

\begin{theorem}[List represented-spine bordered singleton payload readback]
\label{thm:list-represented-spine-bordered-singleton-payload-readback}
Let $A:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be a BEDC history source carrier, let
$\mathsf{cert}:\NameCert(A,\hsame)$, and assume
$\mathsf{ListSpineRepCoherent}_{A,\hsame}$. For finite BEDC-history spines
$p,p',s,s':\mathsf{HistSpine}$ and histories $x,y,h,k:\mathsf{BHist}$, suppose
$$
\mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(\hsame,p,p')
$$
and
$$
\mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(\hsame,s,s')
$$
hold. If
$$
\mathsf{ListSpineRep}_A(h,p{++}(x::\mathsf{nil}){++}s),
\quad
\mathsf{ListSpineRep}_A(k,p'{++}(y::\mathsf{nil}){++}s')
$$
hold, and if $\mathsf{ListSpineHistoryClassifier}_{A,\hsame}(h,k)$ holds, then
$\hsame(x,y)$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply Theorem~\ref{thm:list-represented-spine-classifier-displayed-exactness}
to the two displayed endpoint representations and the public represented-spine
classifier. This reads the endpoint classifier as
$$
\mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(\hsame,
p{++}(x::\mathsf{nil}){++}s,
p'{++}(y::\mathsf{nil}){++}s').
$$

The classified prefix and suffix hypotheses now place the displayed finite
spines in the scope of
Theorem~\ref{thm:list-classifier-bordered-singleton-payload-readback}. Applying
that theorem cancels the classified borders and reads back the singleton
payload relation $\hsame(x,y)$.
\end{proof}

The ten-field fit is: traditional target finite-list represented endpoint
payload readback; BEDC source $A$ with $\NameCert(A,\hsame)$; carrier
$\mathsf{ListHistoryCarrier}_A$; classifier
$\mathsf{ListSpineHistoryClassifier}_{A,\hsame}$; stability from source
certificate plus spine-representation coherence; ledger represented endpoints
with classified prefix and suffix borders; core theorem bordered singleton
payload readback; exactness by displayed-spine public classifier conversion and
finite-spine border cancellation; standard bridge represented finite spines;
status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
