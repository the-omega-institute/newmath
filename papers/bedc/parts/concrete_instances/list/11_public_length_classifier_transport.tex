\begin{theorem}[List public length classifier transport]
\label{thm:list-public-length-classifier-transport}
Let $A:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be a BEDC history source carrier, let
$\sim_A$ be a source classifier, and let
$\mathsf{cert}:\NameCert(A,\sim_A)$. Assume
$\mathsf{ListConsBoundaryCoherent}_{A,\sim_A}$. Then for all histories $h,k$
and all public length values $n$, a classifier witness
$\mathsf{ListSpineHistoryClassifier}_{A,\sim_A}(h,k)$ transports the chosen
public length witness across the classified endpoints:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item if $\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_A(h,n)$ holds, then
  $\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_A(k,n)$ holds;
  \item if $\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_A(k,n)$ holds, then
  $\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_A(h,n)$ holds.
\end{enumerate}
The roadmap ten-field fit is: traditional target classifier-stable finite-list
length readback; BEDC source $\mathsf{BHist}$ with $\NameCert$; carrier
$\mathsf{ListHistoryCarrier}_A$; classifier
$\mathsf{ListSpineHistoryClassifier}_{A,\sim_A}$; ledger chosen public length
witnesses; exactness through $\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_A$ and classified
endpoint readback; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Fix $h,k,n$ and a witness
$C:\mathsf{ListSpineHistoryClassifier}_{A,\sim_A}(h,k)$.

For transport from $h$ to $k$, assume
$L_h:\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_A(h,n)$. Apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:list-public-length-readback-total} to $C$ to obtain a public
length value $m$ with witnesses $\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_A(h,m)$ and
$\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_A(k,m)$.

Apply Theorem~\ref{thm:list-public-length-readback-well-defined} to the same
classifier witness $C$, the given witness $L_h$, and the endpoint witness
$\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_A(k,m)$. This identifies $n=m$. Rewrite the
endpoint witness at $k$ along this equality to obtain
$\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_A(k,n)$.

For transport from $k$ to $h$, assume
$L_k:\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_A(k,n)$. Apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:list-public-length-readback-total} to $C$ again to obtain a
public length value $m$ with witnesses
$\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_A(h,m)$ and $\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_A(k,m)$.

Apply Theorem~\ref{thm:list-public-length-readback-well-defined} to the same
classifier witness $C$, the endpoint witness $\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_A(h,m)$,
and the given witness $L_k$. This identifies $m=n$. Rewrite the endpoint
witness at $h$ along this equality to obtain
$\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_A(h,n)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[List public length classifier unary token transport]
\label{thm:list-public-length-classifier-unary-token-transport}
Let $A:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be a BEDC history source carrier, let
$\sim_A$ be a source classifier, and let
$\mathsf{cert}:\NameCert(A,\sim_A)$. Assume
$\mathsf{ListConsBoundaryCoherent}_{A,\sim_A}$. Then for all histories $h,k$
and all public length values $n$, a classifier witness
$\mathsf{ListSpineHistoryClassifier}_{A,\sim_A}(h,k)$ transports each finite
spine presentation together with its derived unary length token:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item if $\mathsf{ListSpineRep}_A(h,xs)$ and $\ell(xs)=n$, then there exists
  $ys:\mathsf{HistSpine}$ such that
  $$
  \mathsf{ListSpineRep}_A(k,ys)\land
  \ell(ys)=n\land
  \hsame(\mathsf{ListUnaryLength}(xs),\mathsf{ListUnaryLength}(ys)).
  $$
  \item if $\mathsf{ListSpineRep}_A(k,ys)$ and $\ell(ys)=n$, then there exists
  $xs:\mathsf{HistSpine}$ such that
  $$
  \mathsf{ListSpineRep}_A(h,xs)\land
  \ell(xs)=n\land
  \hsame(\mathsf{ListUnaryLength}(ys),\mathsf{ListUnaryLength}(xs)).
  $$
\end{enumerate}
The roadmap ten-field fit is: traditional target classifier-stable finite-list
length readback with internal unary evidence; BEDC source $\mathsf{BHist}$ with
$\NameCert$; carrier $\mathsf{ListHistoryCarrier}_A$; classifier
$\mathsf{ListSpineHistoryClassifier}_{A,\sim_A}$; ledger the public finite
length value and its BEDC unary token; exactness through
$\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_A$, $\mathsf{ListSpineRep}_A$, and
$\mathsf{ListUnaryLength}$; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Fix a classifier witness
$C:\mathsf{ListSpineHistoryClassifier}_{A,\sim_A}(h,k)$.
Theorem~\ref{thm:list-public-length-readback-total} applied to $C$ supplies a
common public length value $m$ for the classified endpoints.

Unpack Definition~\ref{def:list-public-length-readback} for those two public
witnesses, giving classifier-selected finite spines $x_0$ and $y_0$ with
representations of $h$ and $k$ and with finite values $\ell(x_0)=m$ and
$\ell(y_0)=m$. Unfolding $C$ through the list-spine representation definition
selects the same endpoints with a finite-spine classifier witness
$\mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(\sim_A,x_0,y_0)$.

For transport from $h$ to $k$, assume the displayed public presentation of $h$
is witnessed by $xs$ and $\ell(xs)=n$. Repack it through
Definition~\ref{def:list-public-length-readback} as
$\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_A(h,n)$. The $k$-side selected spine gives
$\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_A(k,m)$, and
Theorem~\ref{thm:list-public-length-readback-well-defined} aligns the finite
values $n=m$. Rewriting $\ell(y_0)=m$ along this equality gives
$\ell(y_0)=n$.

It remains to compare unary tokens. First turn the cons-boundary coherence
hypothesis into $\mathsf{ListSpineRepCoherent}_{A,\sim_A}$ by
Theorem~\ref{thm:list-spine-coherence-from-cons-boundary-coherence}. Applying
that coherence datum to the two $h$-presentations, $xs$ and $x_0$, gives
$\mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(\sim_A,xs,x_0)$.

Apply Theorem~\ref{thm:list-classifier-unary-length-token-hsame} to this
comparison to obtain
$\hsame(\mathsf{ListUnaryLength}(xs),\mathsf{ListUnaryLength}(x_0))$. Apply the
same theorem to the selected endpoint classifier to obtain
$\hsame(\mathsf{ListUnaryLength}(x_0),\mathsf{ListUnaryLength}(y_0))$.

By transitivity of $\hsame$, these combine to
$\hsame(\mathsf{ListUnaryLength}(xs),\mathsf{ListUnaryLength}(y_0))$. Taking
$ys=y_0$ gives the first existential.

For transport from $k$ to $h$, use the symmetric argument. The displayed
presentation of $k$ repacks as $\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_A(k,n)$; the
$h$-side selected spine gives $\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_A(h,m)$; and
Theorem~\ref{thm:list-public-length-readback-well-defined} aligns $n=m$, hence
$\ell(x_0)=n$.

Coherence compares the displayed $k$-spine with the selected $y_0$ spine, so
Theorem~\ref{thm:list-classifier-unary-length-token-hsame} gives their unary
token comparison.

The selected endpoint classifier gives the reverse comparison from
$\mathsf{ListUnaryLength}(y_0)$ to $\mathsf{ListUnaryLength}(x_0)$ by symmetry
of $\hsame$ after applying
Theorem~\ref{thm:list-classifier-unary-length-token-hsame}. Transitivity of
$\hsame$ combines the two comparisons, and choosing $xs=x_0$ gives the second
existential.
\end{proof}
