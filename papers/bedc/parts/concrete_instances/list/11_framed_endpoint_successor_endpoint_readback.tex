\begin{theorem}[Framed list public length successor endpoint readback]
\label{thm:list-public-length-successor-endpoint-readback}
Let $A:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be a BEDC history source carrier, let
$\sim_A$ be a source classifier, and assume
$\mathsf{ListSourceHsameCompatible}(A,\sim_A)$. If a history $h$ has framed
public length \(n+1\), then \(h\) exposes a head, tail spine, exact predecessor
length, and framed cons endpoint:
$$
\begin{array}{l}
\mathsf{FramedListPublicLength}_A(h,n+1)\to
\exists a:\mathsf{BHist},\,xs:\mathsf{HistSpine}.\;
A(a)\land
\mathsf{FramedListSpineRep}_A(h,\mathsf{cons}(a,xs))\land\\
\ell(xs)=n\land
\hsame(h,\Eone(\mathsf{PairFrame}(a,\mathsf{FramedListEndpoint}(xs)))).
\end{array}
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply framed public-length constructor readback to the successor witness. This
exposes \(a\), \(xs\), the source row \(A(a)\), a displayed framed spine
representation of \(h\) by \(\mathsf{cons}(a,xs)\), and a tail public-length
witness at \(n\).

The displayed spine representation restricts its entry predicate to the tail
entries of \(xs\), while reflexivity gives the endpoint representation of
\(\mathsf{FramedListEndpoint}(xs)\) by \(xs\). Thus the tail also has the
canonical public length \(\ell(xs)\). Well-definedness of framed public length
identifies \(\ell(xs)=n\). The endpoint row is the cons-endpoint component of
the displayed framed spine representation.
\end{proof}
