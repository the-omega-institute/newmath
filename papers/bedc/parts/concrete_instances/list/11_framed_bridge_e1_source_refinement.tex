\begin{theorem}[Framed list bridge one-left source refinement]
\label{thm:framed-list-bridge-e1-left-source-refinement}
Let $A,B:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be BEDC history source carriers and
let $\sim_A,\sim_B$ be source classifiers. Assume source refinement preserves
carrier membership and head classification:
$$
\forall x:\mathsf{BHist}.\; A(x)\to B(x),
\qquad
\forall x\,y:\mathsf{BHist}.\; x\sim_A y\to x\sim_B y.
$$
Then every framed bridge from the displayed one-headed endpoint $\Eone(t)$ to
$k$ over $(A,\sim_A)$ refines to a framed bridge over $(B,\sim_B)$:
$$
\mathsf{FramedListBridgeClassifier}_{A,\sim_A}(\Eone(t),k)
\to
\mathsf{FramedListBridgeClassifier}_{B,\sim_B}(\Eone(t),k).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the framed bridge into finite spines $xs,ys$, two
$\mathsf{FramedListSpineRep}$ witnesses, and a finite-spine classifier. The
carrier inclusion transports every entry field in both spine representations
without changing the displayed endpoints. Induction on the finite-spine
classifier transports each head relation through the classifier inclusion and
rebuilds the tail classifier. Repacking the same two spines gives the refined
bridge at the visible one-left endpoint.
\end{proof}
