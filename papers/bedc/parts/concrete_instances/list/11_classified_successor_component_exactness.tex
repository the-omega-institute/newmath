\begin{theorem}[List classified successor component exactness]
\label{thm:list-classified-successor-component-exactness}
Let $A:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be a BEDC history source carrier, let
$\sim_A$ be a source classifier, and let
$\mathsf{cert}:\NameCert(A,\sim_A)$. Assume
$\mathsf{ListConsBoundaryCoherent}_{A,\sim_A}$. For histories $h,k$ and every
finite length value $n$,
$$
\left(
\begin{array}{l}
\mathsf{ListSpineHistoryClassifier}_{A,\sim_A}(h,k)\land\\
\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_A(h,n+1)\land
\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_A(k,n+1)
\end{array}
\right)
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
\exists a\,b\,t\,u\,p\,q:\mathsf{BHist}.\;
\left(
\begin{array}{l}
A(a)\land A(b)\land
\mathsf{ListHistoryCarrier}_A(t)\land
\mathsf{ListHistoryCarrier}_A(u)\land\\
\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_A(t,n)\land
\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_A(u,n)\land\\
\ContR(a,t,p)\land \ContR(b,u,q)\land\\
\hsame(h,\Eone(p))\land \hsame(k,\Eone(q))\land\\
a\sim_A b\land
\mathsf{ListSpineHistoryClassifier}_{A,\sim_A}(t,u)
\end{array}
\right).
$$
The ten-field fit is: traditional target finite-list classified successor
component coverage; BEDC source $\mathsf{BHist}$ with $\ContR$; carrier
$\mathsf{ListHistoryCarrier}_A$; classifier
$\mathsf{ListSpineHistoryClassifier}_{A,\sim_A}$; stability from
cons-boundary coherence and source $\NameCert$; ledger common successor public
lengths on both endpoints; core theorem successor component exactness;
exactness by public successor length construction and public-object
constructor audit; standard bridge finite spines; status
$\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the forward direction, apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:list-classified-successor-component-readback} to the
displayed classifier witness and the public successor length witness for $h$.
It returns histories $a,b,t,u,p,q$ with the two source heads, the two tail
carriers, the predecessor public lengths, the two continuation witnesses, the
two successor endpoint presentations, the head classifier witness, and the
tail classifier witness. These are exactly the six-component package on the
right-hand side.

For the reverse direction, assume the displayed six-component package. Apply
the reverse direction of
Theorem~\ref{thm:list-public-successor-length-constructor-exactness} to the
$h$-side data $A(a)$, $\mathsf{ListHistoryCarrier}_A(t)$,
$\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_A(t,n)$, $\ContR(a,t,p)$, and
$\hsame(h,\Eone(p))$. This gives
$\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_A(h,n+1)$.

Apply the same public successor constructor exactness theorem to the $k$-side
data $A(b)$, $\mathsf{ListHistoryCarrier}_A(u)$,
$\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_A(u,n)$, $\ContR(b,u,q)$, and
$\hsame(k,\Eone(q))$. This gives
$\mathsf{ListPublicLength}_A(k,n+1)$.

It remains to recover the classified endpoint relation. Use the cons branch of
Theorem~\ref{thm:list-public-object-constructor-audit}, instantiated with the
same $A$, $\sim_A$, source certificate, and cons-boundary coherence witness.
The displayed heads, tails, continuations, endpoint presentations,
$a\sim_A b$, and
$\mathsf{ListSpineHistoryClassifier}_{A,\sim_A}(t,u)$ form exactly its
cons-constructor package, so the reverse direction of the audit yields
$\mathsf{ListSpineHistoryClassifier}_{A,\sim_A}(h,k)$. Combining this
classifier witness with the two public successor length witnesses gives the
left-hand side.
\end{proof}
