\begin{definition}[Framed list public length readback]
\label{def:framed-list-public-length-readback}
For a BEDC history source carrier $A:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$, define
$\mathsf{FramedListPublicLength}_A(h,n)$ by
$$
\mathsf{FramedListPublicLength}_A(h,n)
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
\exists xs:\mathsf{HistSpine}.\;
\mathsf{FramedListSpineRep}_A(h,xs)\land \ell(xs)=n.
$$
Here $\ell$ is the finite-spine presentation length read from the framed spine
representation. It is not a separate $\NatUp$ operation and is not an additional
field of the framed semantic certificate.
\end{definition}
\leandef{BEDC.Derived.ListUp.FramedListPublicLength}

\begin{theorem}[Framed list public length readback is well-defined]
\label{thm:framed-list-public-length-readback-well-defined}
Let $A:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be a BEDC history source carrier and let
$\sim_A$ be a source classifier. Assume
$\mathsf{ListSourceHsameCompatible}(A,\sim_A)$. Then the framed public length
readback has the following two properties.
\begin{enumerate}
  \item If $\mathsf{FramedListPublicLength}_A(h,n)$ and
  $\mathsf{FramedListPublicLength}_A(h,m)$, then $n=m$.
  \item If $\mathsf{FramedListBridgeClassifier}_{A,\sim_A}(h,k)$ and
  $\mathsf{FramedListPublicLength}_A(h,n)$, then
  $\mathsf{FramedListPublicLength}_A(k,n)$.
\end{enumerate}
The roadmap ten-field fit is: traditional target finite-list framed length
readback; BEDC source $\mathsf{BHist}$; carrier
$\mathsf{FramedListHistoryCarrier}_A$; classifier
$\mathsf{FramedListBridgeClassifier}_{A,\sim_A}$; stability obligation framed
endpoint coherence; ledger finite-spine length witness; core theorem
well-definedness and classifier preservation; exactness via
$\mathsf{FramedListSpineRep}$; standard bridge finite spines; status
$\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For uniqueness at one endpoint, unpack the two
$\mathsf{FramedListPublicLength}_A$ witnesses. They give finite spines $xs$ and
$ys$, representations $\mathsf{FramedListSpineRep}_A(h,xs)$ and
$\mathsf{FramedListSpineRep}_A(h,ys)$, and displayed equalities
$\ell(xs)=n$ and $\ell(ys)=m$. Apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:framed-list-spine-length-determinism} to the two framed
representations of $h$, obtaining $\ell(xs)=\ell(ys)$. Chain the inverse of
$\ell(xs)=n$, this equality, and $\ell(ys)=m$ to get $n=m$.

For preservation along the framed bridge classifier, unpack the public length
witness for $h$ as a finite spine $xs_0$ with
$\mathsf{FramedListSpineRep}_A(h,xs_0)$ and $\ell(xs_0)=n$. Unpack the bridge
classifier into finite spines $xs,ys$ with
$\mathsf{FramedListSpineRep}_A(h,xs)$,
$\mathsf{FramedListSpineRep}_A(k,ys)$, and
$\mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(\sim_A,xs,ys)$.

Use Theorem~\ref{thm:framed-list-bridge-classifier-represented-length} with the
displayed representative $xs_0$ at $h$, the bridge representative $ys$ at $k$,
and the bridge classifier witness to obtain $\ell(xs_0)=\ell(ys)$. Chain this
length equality with $\ell(xs_0)=n$, then choose $ys$ as the framed public length
witness for $k$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ListUp.FramedListPublicLength\_well\_defined}

\begin{theorem}[Framed list public length constructor readback]
\label{thm:framed-list-public-length-constructor-readback}
Let $A:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be a BEDC history source carrier. For
every history $h$ and every finite length value $n$, the framed public length
readback exposes the finite-spine constructors:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item $\mathsf{FramedListPublicLength}_A(h,0)$ holds if and only if
  $\mathsf{FramedListSpineRep}_A(h,\mathsf{nil})$ holds.
  \item If $\mathsf{FramedListPublicLength}_A(h,n+1)$ holds, then
  $$
  \exists a:\mathsf{BHist},\,xs:\mathsf{HistSpine}.\;
  A(a)\land
  \mathsf{FramedListSpineRep}_A(h,\mathsf{cons}(a,xs))\land
  \mathsf{FramedListPublicLength}_A(\mathsf{FramedListEndpoint}(xs),n).
  $$
\end{enumerate}
The roadmap ten-field fit is: traditional target framed finite-list constructor
readback; BEDC source $\mathsf{BHist}$; carrier
$\mathsf{FramedListHistoryCarrier}_A$; classifier-independent spine exposure;
ledger $\mathsf{FramedListPublicLength}_A$; core theorem nil and successor
public-length inversion; exactness by finite framed-spine constructor analysis;
standard bridge finite spines; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the zero forward direction, unfold
Definition~\ref{def:framed-list-public-length-readback}. A public length witness
with value $0$ supplies a finite spine $xs$ with
$\mathsf{FramedListSpineRep}_A(h,xs)$ and $\ell(xs)=0$. Case-analyze $xs$. The
cons case has successor length and cannot have value $0$, while the nil case is
the required framed nil representation.

For the zero reverse direction, choose the nil finite spine in
Definition~\ref{def:framed-list-public-length-readback}. The displayed
$\mathsf{FramedListSpineRep}_A(h,\mathsf{nil})$ witness and
$\ell(\mathsf{nil})=0$ repack as
$\mathsf{FramedListPublicLength}_A(h,0)$.

For the successor clause, unfold the same definition. A witness for
$\mathsf{FramedListPublicLength}_A(h,n+1)$ gives a finite spine $xs_0$ with
$\mathsf{FramedListSpineRep}_A(h,xs_0)$ and $\ell(xs_0)=n+1$. The nil case would
force $\ell(\mathsf{nil})=n+1$, impossible for finite lengths, so the exposed
spine has the form $\mathsf{cons}(a,xs)$.

The cons representation already supplies $A(a)$ and
$\mathsf{FramedListSpineRep}_A(h,\mathsf{cons}(a,xs))$. Its entry predicate
restricts to the tail entries of $xs$, and reflexivity gives
$\hsame(\mathsf{FramedListEndpoint}(xs),\mathsf{FramedListEndpoint}(xs))$.
Together these build
$\mathsf{FramedListSpineRep}_A(\mathsf{FramedListEndpoint}(xs),xs)$.

The displayed successor length equality identifies $\ell(xs)$ with $n$.
Repacking the tail representation and this predecessor length through
Definition~\ref{def:framed-list-public-length-readback} gives
$\mathsf{FramedListPublicLength}_A(\mathsf{FramedListEndpoint}(xs),n)$, so $a$
and $xs$ are the required constructor witnesses.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ListUp.FramedListPublicLength\_constructor\_endpoint\_readback}

\mathsf{FramedListBridgeClassifier}_{A,\sim_A}(h,k)

\begin{theorem}[Framed list bridge classifier constructor exactness]
\label{thm:framed-list-bridge-classifier-constructor-inversion}
Let $A:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be a BEDC history source carrier, let
$\sim_A$ be a source classifier, let $\mathsf{cert}:\NameCert(A,\sim_A)$, and
assume $\mathsf{ListSourceHsameCompatible}(A,\sim_A)$. For histories
$h,k:\mathsf{BHist}$, finite spines $xs,ys$, and source histories
$a,b:\mathsf{BHist}$, the framed bridge classifier has the following constructor
exactness behavior:
The roadmap certificate reads: traditional target finite-list framed bridge
constructor exactness; BEDC source $\mathsf{BHist}$ with $\mathsf{PairFrame}$
and $\mathsf{FramedListEndpoint}$; carrier
$\mathsf{FramedListHistoryCarrier}_A$; classifier
$\mathsf{FramedListBridgeClassifier}$; stability from the source $\NameCert$
plus $\mathsf{ListSourceHsameCompatible}$; ledger displayed framed spines; core
theorem nil-nil introduction, mixed nil/cons separation, and cons-cons iff;
exactness finite-spine constructor readback; standard bridge finite spines;
status $\mathsf{CheckedCert}$.
\begin{enumerate}
  \item if $\mathsf{FramedListSpineRep}_A(h,\mathsf{nil})$ and
  $\mathsf{FramedListSpineRep}_A(k,\mathsf{nil})$ hold, then
  $\mathsf{FramedListBridgeClassifier}_{A,\sim_A}(h,k)$ holds;
  \item if $\mathsf{FramedListSpineRep}_A(h,\mathsf{nil})$,
  $\mathsf{FramedListSpineRep}_A(k,\mathsf{cons}(b,ys))$, and
  $\mathsf{FramedListBridgeClassifier}_{A,\sim_A}(h,k)$ hold, then $\bot$;
  \item if $\mathsf{FramedListSpineRep}_A(h,\mathsf{cons}(a,xs))$,
  $\mathsf{FramedListSpineRep}_A(k,\mathsf{nil})$, and
  $\mathsf{FramedListBridgeClassifier}_{A,\sim_A}(h,k)$ hold, then $\bot$;
  \item if $\mathsf{FramedListSpineRep}_A(h,\mathsf{cons}(a,xs))$ and
  $\mathsf{FramedListSpineRep}_A(k,\mathsf{cons}(b,ys))$ hold, then

  $$
  \mathsf{FramedListBridgeClassifier}_{A,\sim_A}(h,k)
  \Longleftrightarrow
  (a\sim_A b)\land \mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(\sim_A,xs,ys).
  $$

\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
In the nil-nil case, the nil branch of
Theorem~\ref{thm:list-classifier-case-exactness} gives

$$
\mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(\sim_A,\mathsf{nil},\mathsf{nil}).
$$

The reverse direction of
Theorem~\ref{thm:framed-list-bridge-classifier-displayed-spine-exactness}
repackages this finite-spine classifier with the two displayed nil endpoint
representatives.

In the nil-cons case, apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:framed-list-bridge-classifier-displayed-spine-exactness} to the
two displayed endpoint representatives and the bridge witness. This yields

$$
\mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(\sim_A,\mathsf{nil},\mathsf{cons}(b,ys)).
$$

The mixed nil-cons clause of Theorem~\ref{thm:list-classifier-case-exactness}
eliminates that finite-spine classifier.

The cons-nil case is symmetric. Displayed-spine exactness gives

$$
\mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(\sim_A,\mathsf{cons}(a,xs),\mathsf{nil}),
$$

and the mixed cons-nil clause of
Theorem~\ref{thm:list-classifier-case-exactness} eliminates this shape.

In the cons-cons case, displayed-spine exactness turns the bridge witness into

$$
\mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(\sim_A,\mathsf{cons}(a,xs),\mathsf{cons}(b,ys)).
$$

The cons-cons clause of Theorem~\ref{thm:list-classifier-case-exactness}
projects the source-head classifier $a\sim_A b$ and the tail classifier
$\mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(\sim_A,xs,ys)$.

Conversely, suppose the source-head classifier $a\sim_A b$ and the tail
classifier $\mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(\sim_A,xs,ys)$ are given. The cons-cons
clause of Theorem~\ref{thm:list-classifier-case-exactness} rebuilds

$$
\mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(\sim_A,\mathsf{cons}(a,xs),\mathsf{cons}(b,ys)).
$$

The reverse direction of
Theorem~\ref{thm:framed-list-bridge-classifier-displayed-spine-exactness}
repackages this finite-spine classifier as
$\mathsf{FramedListBridgeClassifier}_{A,\sim_A}(h,k)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Framed list bridge classifier equivalence fields]
\label{thm:framed-list-bridge-classifier-equivalence-fields}

$\sim_A$ be a source classifier, let $\mathsf{cert}:\NameCert(A,\sim_A)$, and
assume $\mathsf{ListSourceHsameCompatible}(A,\sim_A)$. Define the framed carrier
by
$$

\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
\exists xs:\mathsf{HistSpine}.\;\mathsf{FramedListSpineRep}_A(h,xs).
$$

Then $\mathsf{FramedListBridgeClassifier}_{A,\sim_A}$ satisfies the five
classifier fields below. Carrier-relative reflexivity:
$$
\mathsf{FramedListHistoryCarrier}_A(h)\to
\mathsf{FramedListBridgeClassifier}_{A,\sim_A}(h,h).
$$
Endpoint exposure for any bridge witness:
$$
\mathsf{FramedListBridgeClassifier}_{A,\sim_A}(h,k)\to
\mathsf{FramedListHistoryCarrier}_A(h)\land
\mathsf{FramedListHistoryCarrier}_A(k).
$$
Symmetry:
$$
\mathsf{FramedListBridgeClassifier}_{A,\sim_A}(h,k)\to
\mathsf{FramedListBridgeClassifier}_{A,\sim_A}(k,h).
$$
Transitivity:
$$
\mathsf{FramedListBridgeClassifier}_{A,\sim_A}(h,k)\to
\mathsf{FramedListBridgeClassifier}_{A,\sim_A}(k,u)\to
\mathsf{FramedListBridgeClassifier}_{A,\sim_A}(h,u).
$$
Carrier transport:
$$
\mathsf{FramedListHistoryCarrier}_A(h)\to
\mathsf{FramedListBridgeClassifier}_{A,\sim_A}(h,k)\to
\mathsf{FramedListHistoryCarrier}_A(k).
$$
The ten-field fit is: traditional target finite-list framed bridge equivalence
fields; BEDC source $\mathsf{BHist}$; carrier framed spine representation;
classifier $\mathsf{FramedListBridgeClassifier}_{A,\sim_A}$; stability
obligations the five displayed clauses; ledger finite displayed spines; core
theorem this fields theorem; exactness by displayed-spine exactness and framed
coherence; standard bridge finite spines; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Endpoint exposure unpacks the bridge witness into finite spines $xs,ys$ with
$\mathsf{FramedListSpineRep}_A(h,xs)$,
$\mathsf{FramedListSpineRep}_A(k,ys)$, and
$\mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(\sim_A,xs,ys)$; the two representation witnesses
are exactly the two framed-carrier witnesses.

For carrier-relative reflexivity, choose the displayed spine supplied by the
framed-carrier witness at $h$ on both endpoints. Induct over that finite spine.
The nil case is the nil constructor of $\mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}$. In the
cons case, the entry predicate stored in $\mathsf{FramedListSpineRep}$ supplies
$A$ for the displayed head, so $\mathsf{cert.equiv\_refl}$ gives the head
classifier witness and the induction hypothesis supplies the tail witness.

For symmetry, unpack the bridge witness, apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:list-classifier-symm-from-namecert} to the finite-spine
classifier witness, and rebuild the bridge with the two displayed endpoints
swapped. For transitivity, unpack bridge witnesses for $h,k$ and $k,u$. The two
middle spines both represent $k$, so Theorem~\ref{thm:framed-list-spine-coherence}
compares them. Compose the first bridge classifier, the middle comparison, and
the second bridge classifier by two applications of
Theorem~\ref{thm:list-classifier-trans-from-namecert}, then rebuild the bridge
from the exposed first and last spines. Carrier transport is the right endpoint
projection of endpoint exposure, with the displayed left-carrier premise matching
the $\NameCert$ field shape.
\end{proof}
