\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\LocatedIntervalUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-locatedinterval-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\LocatedIntervalUp$ packet names a finite interval surface whose endpoints are carried as located real names while its inspection ledgers remain rational and dyadic. In the Bishop--Bridges constructive-analysis setting, an interval is read through explicit endpoint names, rational cells, dyadic refinements, and finite real-name windows; it is not a host interval primitive and does not choose completed endpoint values.

\begin{definition}[LocatedInterval carrier]
\label{def:located-interval-carrier}
A $\LocatedIntervalUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
J=(L,U,I,D,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $L$ is the lower endpoint row, a $\LocatedRealUp$ packet displayed in the same history;
\item $U$ is the upper endpoint row, a $\LocatedRealUp$ packet displayed in the same history;
\item $I$ is a $\RationalIntervalUp$ cell ledger whose endpoint and containment rows give the rational windows currently enclosing the endpoint reads;
\item $D$ is the optional finite family of $\DyadicIntervalUp$ refinement rows attached to selected rational cells;
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window ledger naming the finite endpoint observations inspected from $L$ and $U$;
\item $R$ records the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readbacks used by those endpoint windows;
\item $E$ records the $\RealUp$ seal-facing rows for the lower and upper endpoint names;
\item $H$ records the componentwise $\hsame$ transports for located endpoints, rational cells, dyadic refinements, stream windows, readbacks, and seal rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ read routes by which a finite consumer reaches exactly those endpoint, interval, refinement, window, readback, and seal rows;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LocatedRealUp$, $\RationalIntervalUp$, $\DyadicIntervalUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, and the local rational source rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedIntervalUp}$ provenance row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate is the assertion that these rows form one finite located interval packet. It has no coordinate for a host real interval, an endpoint choice functional, a quotient of real names, or an ambient order-completeness theorem.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[LocatedInterval NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:located-interval-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\LocatedIntervalUp}$ obligation surface consists of five finite rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier habitation, given by any displayed pair of $\LocatedRealUp$ endpoint rows together with a rational cell ledger enclosing the inspected endpoint windows;
\item endpoint transport, which moves the lower and upper $\LocatedRealUp$ rows componentwise through the stored $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ witnesses;
\item interval containment exactness, which says that every containment read is one of the rational cells in $I$ or one of the dyadic refinements in $D$ routed by the packet;
\item refinement handoff, which permits a $\DyadicIntervalUp$ refinement row only as a finite refinement of a displayed $\RationalIntervalUp$ cell;
\item non-escape of host real intervals, which forces every endpoint, containment, and seal read through $L$, $U$, $I$, $D$, $S$, $R$, $E$, $H$, $C$, $P$, and $N$.
\end{enumerate}
These rows are exactly the naming-certificate surface for located real endpoint data bound to rational and dyadic interval ledgers.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier habitation is obtained by packaging the two displayed endpoint names with one rational cell ledger and the finite observation windows used to read them. The packet stores those rows directly, so no external interval carrier is needed.

Endpoint transport is componentwise. The lower endpoint row and the upper endpoint row each move along their own $\hsame$ comparison and the displayed $\Cont$ route that reads the corresponding finite window.

Containment exactness is a projection from the carrier. A rational containment read must come from $I$, and a dyadic containment read must come from one of the refinement rows in $D$; both are routed by the same history and package provenance.

For the refinement handoff, read a dyadic row only together with the rational cell it refines. Repacking that pair preserves the interval surface because the consumer still sees the same endpoints, window routes, and readback rows.

The non-escape row follows by inspecting the coordinates of \autoref{def:located-interval-carrier}. There is no field from which a host interval, chosen endpoint value, quotient real, or ambient completeness principle could be exported.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LocatedInterval endpoint transport]
\label{thm:located-interval-endpoint-transport}
Let two accepted $\LocatedIntervalUp$ packets be related by the displayed componentwise transport rows. Then the lower $\LocatedRealUp$ endpoint, the upper $\LocatedRealUp$ endpoint, the $\RationalIntervalUp$ cell ledger, every present $\DyadicIntervalUp$ refinement row, the $\StreamNameUp$ windows, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readbacks, and the $\RealUp$ seal rows transport together as one located interval packet.

Consequently a consumer of the transported packet can read only the transported endpoint rows and the transported interval ledgers carried by the same $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ provenance.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.LocatedIntervalUp.LocatedIntervalPacket\_endpoint\_transport}
\begin{proof}
Project both packets through \autoref{def:located-interval-carrier}. The hypotheses give separate transport rows for the lower endpoint, upper endpoint, rational cell ledger, dyadic refinements, stream windows, readbacks, and seal rows.

Transport the two endpoint names first. Their $\LocatedRealUp$ windows and $\RealUp$ seal-facing rows move by the stated componentwise $\hsame$ witnesses and keep the same finite read routes.

Next transport the interval ledger. The rational cells in $I$ and the dyadic refinement rows in $D$ are carried by their own displayed comparisons, so each transported containment row remains attached to the transported endpoint windows.

Finally repack the transported rows with the transported package and local naming provenance. Since the carrier admits only these projected rows, the transported packet has no additional consumer route beyond the displayed endpoint and interval data.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LocatedInterval carrier habitation]
\label{thm:locatedinterval-carrier-habitation}
For a displayed finite history row
$$
J=(L,U,I,D,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
as in \autoref{def:located-interval-carrier}, the carrier-habitation obligation for $\NameCert_{\LocatedIntervalUp}$ is witnessed exactly by the displayed $\LocatedRealUp$ endpoint rows $L$ and $U$, the $\RationalIntervalUp$ cell ledger $I$, the optional $\DyadicIntervalUp$ family $D$, the $\StreamNameUp$ windows $S$, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readbacks $R$, the $\RealUp$ seal rows $E$, the componentwise $\hsame$ transports $H$, the displayed $\Cont$ routes $C$, the $\Pkg$ provenance $P$, and the local certificate row $N$.

No further carrier coordinate is required or available.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the packet through \autoref{def:located-interval-carrier}. Each row named in $J$ is part of the same finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ display, and the package provenance records that those rows are read under $\LocatedRealUp$, $\RationalIntervalUp$, $\DyadicIntervalUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$.

The habitation witness is therefore the finite tuple $J$ itself, read with its displayed routes. Since the carrier definition contains no host interval coordinate and no chosen endpoint value, the certificate cannot draw on any ambient interval object.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LocatedInterval containment exactness]
\label{thm:locatedinterval-containment-exactness}
For a displayed finite history row
$$
J=(L,U,I,D,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
as in \autoref{def:located-interval-carrier}, every containment read accepted by the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedIntervalUp}$ is routed by $C$ to either the $\RationalIntervalUp$ cell ledger $I$ or a present $\DyadicIntervalUp$ refinement row in $D$. Endpoint movement used by such a read is the movement supplied by \autoref{thm:located-interval-endpoint-transport}.

Thus containment exactness is a finite projection from $J$, not a claim about all host intervals between two completed real values.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier coordinates. The only interval-containment rows available to a consumer are the rational cells in $I$ and the dyadic refinement rows in $D$, and every admissible read must pass through the displayed $\Cont$ route $C$ and the provenance $P$.

If the containment read is compared after endpoint transport, apply \autoref{thm:located-interval-endpoint-transport} first. The lower and upper $\LocatedRealUp$ rows, their finite windows, the readbacks, and the seal-facing rows move together, so the transported containment route still lands in the transported copy of $I$ or $D$.

There is no separate containment source in \autoref{def:located-interval-carrier}. Exactness therefore follows by ruling out any read not projected from the listed finite rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LocatedInterval dyadic refinement handoff]
\label{thm:locatedinterval-dyadic-refinement-handoff}
For a displayed finite history row
$$
J=(L,U,I,D,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
as in \autoref{def:located-interval-carrier}, a $\DyadicIntervalUp$ row may be handed to a finite consumer only together with the $\RationalIntervalUp$ cell in $I$ that its displayed route refines. Under the endpoint movement of \autoref{thm:located-interval-endpoint-transport}, the handoff remains attached to the transported rational cell ledger and the transported endpoint windows.

The handoff obligation is therefore internal to $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ over the rows of $J$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a present dyadic refinement row in $D$. By \autoref{def:located-interval-carrier}, that row is stored only as part of the same finite packet as the rational ledger $I$, with the route $C$ displaying how the refinement is reached from the inspected interval data.

Transported endpoint reads are handled before the handoff. Apply \autoref{thm:located-interval-endpoint-transport} to move $L$, $U$, $I$, $D$, $S$, $R$, and $E$ through the componentwise $\hsame$ rows in $H$ while preserving the consumer routes.

The consumer can then receive the dyadic row only with its rational parent cell and the same package provenance. No rule in the carrier permits a dyadic refinement detached from $I$ or from the displayed finite history.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LocatedInterval real-seal non-escape]
\label{thm:locatedinterval-real-seal-non-escape}
For a displayed finite history row
$$
J=(L,U,I,D,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
as in \autoref{def:located-interval-carrier}, every seal-facing read accepted by the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedIntervalUp}$ factors through the $\LocatedRealUp$ endpoint rows $L$ and $U$, the $\StreamNameUp$ windows $S$, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readbacks $R$, the $\RealUp$ rows $E$, the componentwise $\hsame$ transports $H$, the displayed $\Cont$ routes $C$, the $\Pkg$ provenance $P$, and the local row $N$. Endpoint movement is exactly the movement supplied by \autoref{thm:located-interval-endpoint-transport}.

Consequently the packet cannot export a host real interval, a selected endpoint value, quotient real equality, or ambient order completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the seal-facing part of the carrier. The only real rows present are the lower and upper $\LocatedRealUp$ endpoint displays, their finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows, the associated $\RegSeqRatUp$ readbacks, and the $\RealUp$ rows explicitly recorded in $E$.

When a seal-facing read is transported, apply \autoref{thm:located-interval-endpoint-transport}. The same componentwise $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ data move the endpoint windows, readbacks, rational cells, dyadic refinements, and seal rows as one packet.

It remains to exclude non-displayed real data. The carrier in \autoref{def:located-interval-carrier} has no coordinate for an ambient interval, no quotient of real names, and no completeness principle; every accepted read must therefore remain inside $J$ and its local $\NameCert$ provenance.
\end{proof}
