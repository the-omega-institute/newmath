\begin{theorem}[Weighted deformation spine NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:weighted-deformation-spine-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\WeightedDeformationSpineUp$ carrier
$$
W=(S,L,D,B,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\WeightedDeformationSpineUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for trigger support, weight ledger, deformation route, boundary-tile readback, componentwise transport, displayed replay, provenance, and local naming. The weighted deformation route must preserve the nested connected supports and the spine path
$$
\mathrm{UAG}-\mathrm{UAA}-\mathrm{UGA}-\mathrm{AGA}-\mathrm{AGG}
$$
with branch
$$
\mathrm{AGA}-\mathrm{AAA}-\mathrm{AUA}.
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:weighted-deformation-operator-carrier}. The support, weight, route, boundary, transport, replay, provenance, and local name are exactly the displayed coordinates.

The route is weighted, not merely supported. The row $L$ must be read before $D$, because the nested connected supports are selected by maximum-mass connected candidates from the source theorem.

The boundary readback is also explicit. The row $B$ attaches the Stop, AGR, AAA, and AUA modules to the DNA-window six boundary-tile source, so a consumer cannot reinterpret the spine as an ungrounded graph path.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ preserve and name the same rows. Since biological mechanism, stochastic dynamics, quotient codon equality, hidden enumeration, and unweighted trigger boundaries have no carrier coordinate, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Weighted deformation spine trigger stability]
\label{thm:weighted-deformation-spine-trigger-stability}
For accepted $\WeightedDeformationSpineUp$ carriers, componentwise $\hsame$ transport preserves the trigger support $S$, weight ledger $L$, deformation route $D$, and boundary-tile readback $B$ as one packet. A deformation consumer may vary only through displayed transport and replay rows; it cannot keep the same route while dropping the weight ledger, or keep the same support while replacing the boundary readback by an unrecorded trigger classifier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the accepted carrier $W=(S,L,D,B,H,C,P,N)$. The transport row $H$ is componentwise across support, weights, route, boundary readback, replay, provenance, and local naming.

The weight ledger is tied to the route. If $D$ is preserved, the maximum-mass connected-candidate interpretation still requires the weights stored in $L$; otherwise the deformation boundary is no longer the weighted spine named by the packet.

The boundary readback is tied to the trigger support. If $S$ is preserved, the Stop, AGR, AAA, and AUA module readback must remain the displayed row $B$, not a hidden classifier outside the carrier.

Thus every stable trigger read preserves $S,L,D,B,H,C,P,N$ together. The packet cannot be reduced to support alone, route alone, or weights alone.
\end{proof}
