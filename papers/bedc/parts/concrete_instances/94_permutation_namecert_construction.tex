\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\PermutationUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-permutation-namecert}

The permutation interface $\PermutationUp$ packages the carrier of bijections of a $\NameCert_{\FinSetUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finset-namecert}) onto itself. It supplies the underlying carrier for $\NameCert_{\SymGroupUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-symgroup-namecert}) and the action data for combinatorial group-theoretic certificates.

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:permutation-carrier}

\begin{definition}[Permutation finite-set bijection carrier]
\label{def:permutation-finset-bijection-carrier}
Fix a finite-set source certificate $\NameCert_{\FinSetUp}$ with carrier row $F$, classifier $\sim_F$, finite-spine endpoint row $xs$, and enumeration ledger as in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finset-namecert}. A BEDC-facing $\PermutationUp$ carrier row over $F$ is the $\mathsf{BHist}$-visible package
$$
(\mathsf{src},\mathsf{tgt},G,G^{-1},W,C,A,L)
$$
with the following rows.
\begin{enumerate}
\item $\mathsf{src}$ and $\mathsf{tgt}$ are the source and codomain rows for the same carried $\FinSetUp$ object, so the displayed domain and codomain are the same certificate row rather than two unrelated carriers.
\item $G$ and $G^{-1}$ are the forward and backward graph rows, represented by $\Pkg$ provenance and $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ reads over $\mathsf{BHist}$.
\item $W$ is the pair of inverse-witness rows saying that every forward read is met by a backward read and every backward read is met by a forward read at the corresponding endpoint.
\item $C$ is the classifier row: two graph presentations classify together exactly when their source and target endpoint reads agree by the $\FinSetUp$ classifier and the graph rows are transported by $\hsame$-compatible package evidence.
\item $A$ is the action row sending a carried source endpoint through $G$ to its carried target endpoint.
\item $L$ is the ledger containing the hidden enumeration witness for the chosen finite spine, the hidden order witness used to name positions, the $\Pkg$ provenance for the two endpoint copies, and the probe-read entries that justify graph coverage.
\end{enumerate}
The only visible carrier fields are the two $\FinSetUp$ endpoint rows, the two graph rows, the inverse-witness rows, the classifier row, the action row, and the ledger boundary. Enumeration and order witnesses are not exposed as host data; they are consumed through $L$.
\end{definition}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:permutation-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[Permutation finite-set carrier source obligation]
\label{thm:permutation-finset-carrier-source-obligation}
The carrier source for $\PermutationUp$ over \autoref{def:permutation-finset-bijection-carrier} is exactly the displayed $\FinSetUp$ row together with the two graph rows $G$ and $G^{-1}$ and the inverse-witness rows $W$. The carrier has no additional host function row, host order row, or external enumeration row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the roadmap ten-field template at the carrier-source slot. The $\NameCert_{\FinSetUp}$ input supplies the BEDC carrier, its $\mathsf{BHist}$ endpoint reads, and the $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ spine used by the finite-set row.

The forward and backward graph rows are then ordinary $\Pkg$-tracked rows over the same endpoint data. The inverse-witness rows are the BEDC-visible part that says those two graph rows face one another on the carried endpoints.

Conversely, any row with precisely this data repacks into the carrier slot of the template, while hidden enumeration and order choices remain in the ledger. The existing $\FinSetUp$ permutation relation $\mathsf{FinsetEnumerationPermutation}$ is the later Lean source for the probe-spine comparison used by this repacking.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Permutation bijection classifier obligation]
\label{thm:permutation-bijection-classifier-obligation}
Classifier agreement for two $\PermutationUp$ carrier rows is graph agreement transported by $\hsame$ and by the $\FinSetUp$ classifier on endpoint reads. It is not equality of host functions, equality of hidden enumerations, or equality of host order choices.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The classifier slot in the roadmap ten-field template receives the $\NameCert$ classifier from the finite-set endpoint row. Source reads and target reads are therefore compared through the $\FinSetUp$ classifier, with no appeal to host-level equality.

The graph comparison is carried by $\Pkg$ provenance and $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ reads over $\mathsf{BHist}$. Applying $\hsame$ transport to those package rows preserves the visible graph agreement while leaving enumeration and order witnesses inside the ledger.

The later Lean source is the $\FinSetUp$ permutation relation: $\mathsf{FinsetEnumerationPermutation}$ supplies the constructive spine relation whose reads can be transported through the finite-set classifier. This is exactly graph agreement in BEDC form.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Permutation composition and inverse obligation]
\label{thm:permutation-composition-inverse-obligation}
Composition and inverse for $\PermutationUp$ are obligations over the same graph classifier as \autoref{thm:permutation-bijection-classifier-obligation}. If two carrier rows satisfy the displayed graph and inverse-witness data, then the composition rows are required to close under the same carrier by the two identity classifications
$$
G^{-1}\circ G \sim_{\mathsf{Graph}} \mathsf{id}_{\mathsf{src}}
$$
and
$$
G\circ G^{-1} \sim_{\mathsf{Graph}} \mathsf{id}_{\mathsf{tgt}},
$$
again read through $\hsame$, $\Pkg$, and $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ data over $\mathsf{BHist}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The roadmap ten-field template treats composition and inverse as rows attached to the same public carrier, not as external host operations. Thus the source and target endpoints remain the two copies of the $\NameCert_{\FinSetUp}$ row fixed in \autoref{def:permutation-finset-bijection-carrier}.

Use the graph classifier from \autoref{thm:permutation-bijection-classifier-obligation}. The forward graph, backward graph, and inverse-witness rows give the two displayed identity classifications after transport by $\hsame$ and by the $\Pkg$ provenance recorded for the graph reads.

Closure under the displayed carrier follows because every composed endpoint is still a $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ read of a $\mathsf{BHist}$ endpoint classified by the fixed finite-set row. The later Lean source remains $\mathsf{FinsetEnumerationPermutation}$, whose reflexive, symmetric, and transitive behavior supplies the spine discipline needed for these identity rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Permutation action and ledger obligation]
\label{thm:permutation-action-ledger-obligation}
The action row consumed by $\SymGroupUp$ is read from applying the graph row $G$ to carried $\FinSetUp$ elements. Coverage, soundness, and completeness of that action are obligations over the graph reads, while enumeration choices, order choices, and position names remain ledger data in $L$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply the action slot of the roadmap ten-field template. A $\SymGroupUp$ action reads a source endpoint from the $\NameCert_{\FinSetUp}$ carrier and then follows the graph row $G$ through its $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ read.

Coverage says every carried source endpoint has such a graph read. Soundness says the returned target endpoint is carried by the same finite-set certificate. Completeness says any second graph read with the same source endpoint is classified with the action output by the graph classifier and $\hsame$ transport.

The ledger $L$ stores the hidden enumeration, order witness, position naming, and $\Pkg$ provenance that justify those reads. The later Lean source is again $\mathsf{FinsetEnumerationPermutation}$, because it records the finite-spine permutation relation without exposing the hidden enumeration as a public carrier field.
\end{proof}
