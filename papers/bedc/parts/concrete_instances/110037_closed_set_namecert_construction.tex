\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ClosedSetUp$ Complement-Open Route}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-closed-set-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bourbaki and Bishop-style topology, the $\ClosedSetUp$ packet records a closed-set surface as finite complement-open and boundary readback data rather than as a host powerset predicate. It sits after the topology source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-topology-namecert}, the optional metric boundary route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, and the real-name boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}; it supplies the closed-row consumed by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compact-image-closed-namecert}.

\begin{definition}[Closed-set carrier]
\label{def:closed-set-carrier}
A $\ClosedSetUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
F=(T,M,O,L,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $T$ is the topology source row whose open-neighborhood observations provide the ambient interface for the set;
\item $M$ is the optional metric boundary row, used only when a metric source is displayed and otherwise absent from the classifier;
\item $O$ is the complement-open witness row, recording the finite open observations by which an exterior request is accepted;
\item $L$ is the limit and closure readback row, exposing only displayed finite convergence or adherence tests attached to $T,M,O$;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the topology source, optional metric boundary, complement-open witness, limit readback, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of closed-set requests through the same topology, metric, complement-open, and readback rows;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ClosedSetUp$, $\TopologyUp$, $\MetricUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ClosedSetUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only through $T,M,O,L,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for a host complement operator, quotient point equality, a selected limit, or ambient compactness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Closed-set NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:closed-set-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\ClosedSetUp$ carrier $F=(T,M,O,L,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\ClosedSetUp}$ surface consists exactly of finite obligations for carrier admission; topology-source exposure through $T$; optional metric-boundary compatibility through $M$; complement-open witnessing through $O$; limit and closure readback through $L$; componentwise $\hsame$ stability through $H$; displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$; $\Pkg$ provenance through $P$; and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:closed-set-carrier}. The accepted packet displays the topology source, optional metric boundary, complement-open witness, limit readback, transport, replay, provenance, and local name as one finite $\BHist$ surface.

The topology and metric obligations are read first. A closed-set request begins with $T$, and any metric boundary information is available only through the displayed optional row $M$.

The complement-open and limit-readback obligations are then $O$ and $L$. Exterior evidence is an open witness over the displayed topology source, while closure or limit inspection is a finite readback row attached to that witness and boundary route.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ preserve, replay, source, and name exactly those rows. Since the carrier has no coordinate for host complements, quotient point equality, selected limits, or ambient compactness, the listed obligations exhaust the local certificate surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Closed-set complement-open route]
\label{thm:closed-set-complement-open-route}
Every topology, metric, Real, compact-image, or boundary-facing consumer of an accepted $\ClosedSetUp$ carrier reaches the closed boundary only through the route
$$
T,M,O,L,H,C,P,N .
$$
Thus a closed-set read factors through the topology source, optional metric boundary, complement-open witness, transport, replay, provenance, and local NameCert rows, and not through a host complement, quotient point equality, selected limit, or ambient compactness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier $F=(T,M,O,L,H,C,P,N)$. The topology source $T$ is the first row available to any consumer, and the optional metric boundary $M$ can only refine that same displayed source.

The complement-open witness $O$ is the only row that exposes exterior information. The limit and closure readback row $L$ is downstream of $T,M,O$, so a consumer cannot inspect a boundary point before reading the displayed open-complement route.

The structural rows do not add a second closed-set surface. Componentwise transport $H$, replay $C$, provenance $P$, and local naming $N$ preserve the displayed route for topology, metric, Real, compact-image, and boundary-facing consumers.

Because no carrier coordinate stores a host complement operator, quotient point equality, selected limit, or ambient compactness theorem, no such data can be exported through the $\ClosedSetUp$ certificate.
\end{proof}
