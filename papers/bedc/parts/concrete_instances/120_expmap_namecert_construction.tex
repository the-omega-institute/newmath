\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ExpMapUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-expmap-namecert}

The exponential-map interface $\ExpMapUp$ packages the canonical map $\mathfrak{g} \to G$ from the Lie algebra to the Lie group of a connected Lie-group carrier. It depends on $\NameCert_{\LieGroupUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-liegroup-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\LieAlgebraUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-liealgebra-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:expmap-carrier}

\begin{theorem}[ExpMap carrier obligation surface]
\label{thm:expmap-carrier-obligation-surface}
The $\ExpMapUp$ carrier is the $\mathsf{BHist}$ graph package whose source projection is licensed by the $\LieAlgebraUp$ tangent carrier and whose target projection is licensed by the $\LieGroupUp$ carrier. A carried row consists of a source tangent row $x$, a target group row $g$, and a displayed finite BEDC flow ledger connecting them through $\Cont$ and $\mathsf{hsame}$ rows. The graph row is generated only by that finite ledger; it does not assert equality of host functions, analytic exponentials, or ambient manifold maps.

Its ten-field fit is: traditional target the exponential graph $\mathfrak{g}\to G$; BEDC source the paired $\LieAlgebraUp/\LieGroupUp$ certificates; carrier the $\mathsf{BHist}$ graph package; classifier the componentwise source-target classifier with ledger transport; stability the transport fields supplied by the two dependencies together with $\mathsf{hsame}$ on the ledger; ledger the finite $\Cont$ flow display; core theorem list this carrier surface, classifier stability, and zero-flow composition; exactness the assertion that every public row is one such graph package; standard bridge none; status proof target for the $\NameCert_{\ExpMapUp}$ obligation surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the $\LieAlgebraUp$ NameCert surface to obtain the admissible tangent-source rows, and project the $\LieGroupUp$ NameCert surface to obtain the admissible target group rows. These two projections give the endpoints of the graph package without mentioning a host-level function.

Unfold the graph package as a source row, a target row, and a finite ledger of $\Cont$ steps with $\mathsf{hsame}$ endpoint transport. The displayed ledger is then recorded as the hidden data in the per-object certificate template, so every carried $\ExpMapUp$ row is generated by BEDC rows alone.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ExpMap classifier stability obligation surface]
\label{thm:expmap-classifier-stability-obligation-surface}
Two $\ExpMapUp$ graph rows are classified exactly by agreement of their $\LieAlgebraUp$ source classifiers, agreement of their $\LieGroupUp$ target classifiers, and $\mathsf{hsame}$ transport of the displayed finite flow ledger. The classifier is reflexive, symmetric, transitive, and carrier-transporting by the corresponding source certificate field, target certificate field, and $\mathsf{hsame}$ transport field.

Its ten-field fit is: traditional target graph equality for the exponential map; BEDC source the source classifier, target classifier, and finite ledger classifier; carrier the graph package of \autoref{thm:expmap-carrier-obligation-surface}; classifier the three-part relation just displayed; stability componentwise transport for source, target, and ledger rows; ledger the transported finite flow display; core theorem list the carrier surface and this stability surface; exactness public graph classification by the three visible components; standard bridge none; status proof target for classifier stability in $\NameCert_{\ExpMapUp}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply the classifier fields from the $\LieAlgebraUp$ and $\LieGroupUp$ dependency certificates componentwise. Reflexivity, symmetry, transitivity, and carrier transport for the source and target projections are inherited from those two fields.

For the ledger component, use $\mathsf{hsame}$ reflexivity for the unchanged ledger, $\mathsf{hsame}$ symmetry to reverse a displayed transport, and $\mathsf{hsame}$ transitivity to concatenate transports. Repacking the three transported components gives exactly the graph-row classifier and its carrier-transport clause.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ExpMap zero and flow-composition obligation surface]
\label{thm:expmap-zero-flow-obligation-surface}
The zero-tangent row of the $\LieAlgebraUp$ source maps to the identity row of the $\LieGroupUp$ target, and composable finite $\ExpMapUp$ flow rows compose by $\Cont$ associativity up to $\mathsf{hsame}$. The ledger provenance for the composed row is restricted to the zero row, the two displayed flow rows, and the $\Cont$ associativity witness; no host flow equation or analytic uniqueness principle is part of the obligation.

Its ten-field fit is: traditional target zero-to-identity and exponential-flow composition; BEDC source the $\LieAlgebraUp$ zero row, the $\LieGroupUp$ identity row, and finite $\Cont$ ledgers; carrier the graph package of \autoref{thm:expmap-carrier-obligation-surface}; classifier the stability surface of \autoref{thm:expmap-classifier-stability-obligation-surface}; stability $\mathsf{hsame}$ transport for zero, identity, and composed ledgers; ledger the empty ledger and the two composable finite ledgers; core theorem list zero, unit, and associativity rows; exactness every composed public row has precisely that provenance; standard bridge none; status proof target for zero and local-flow compatibility in $\NameCert_{\ExpMapUp}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Represent the zero tangent by the empty finite flow ledger. Reading this ledger through $\mathsf{cont\_left\_unit}$ and $\mathsf{cont\_right\_unit}$ identifies its target endpoint with the $\LieGroupUp$ identity row up to $\mathsf{hsame}$.

For two composable flow rows, concatenate their finite $\Cont$ ledgers. The associativity witness $\mathsf{cont\_assoc\_hsame}$ transports the concatenated display to the carried graph row, and the classifier stability surface transports the source and target endpoints along with it. Thus the composed row has exactly the displayed ledger provenance.
\end{proof}
