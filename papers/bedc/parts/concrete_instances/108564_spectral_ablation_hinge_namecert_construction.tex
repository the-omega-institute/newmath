\begin{theorem}[SpectralAblationHinge NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:spectral-ablation-hinge-namecert-obligations}

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SpectralAblationHinge loss route]
\label{thm:spectral-ablation-hinge-loss-route}

$$

$$
Deleting the spectral-loss ledger $L$ prevents the carrier from exporting an accepted hinge ranking, even when the deletion row, killed-walk row, orbit-module row, and tail-coupling row remain displayed.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:spectral-ablation-hinge-namecert-obligations}. They require finite deletion admission, killed-walk admission, and spectral-loss ledger exactness before orbit-module or hinge-ranking data is read.

The ranking row is downstream of the loss row. The orbit-module row $O$ and tail-coupling row $T$ classify the deletion, but neither row stores the spectral loss itself.

If $L$ is deleted, the displayed route cannot reach $R$ as an accepted hinge-ranking row. The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ only transport, replay, source, and name accepted rows, so they cannot reconstruct the missing loss ledger.
\end{proof}
