\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\FiniteObservationTraceFilterUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-finite-observation-trace-filter-namecert}
\origin{ai}

The $\FiniteObservationTraceFilterUp$ packet realizes the finite verification
trace of \autoref{def:transcendental-input-finite-trace} as a concrete
NameCert surface. It sits beside the finite-trace GAP socket of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finite-trace-gap-socket-namecert} and the
closed observation ledger of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closedobservationsystem-namecert}. Its role is
to filter observed trace rows through finite kernel checks while keeping the
universal-induction boundary visible as a GAP-facing refusal.

\begin{definition}[Finite observation trace filter carrier]
\label{def:finite-observation-trace-filter-carrier}
A $\FiniteObservationTraceFilterUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
F=(O,T,K,A,G,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $O$ is the closed-observation source row containing the finite observed
states and their monotone ledger.
\item $T$ is the finite verification trace row, listing inspected indices,
their predicates, and the closed-generation checks visible at those indices.
\item $K$ is the kernel-filter row that records which trace entries are
accepted by finite verification and which are rejected as outside the carried
window.
\item $A$ is the audit row for non-escape: it records that no universal
quantifier, induction schema, future-observation oracle, or hidden host rule is
imported by the filter.
\item $G$ is the GAP-socket row exposed when a consumer asks the finite trace
to act as a universal statement.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $O,T,K,A,G$ and the
local certificate data.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from an observation request to the
trace row, from the trace row to the kernel filter, and from a universal
request to the GAP row.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, closed observation,
finite-trace rows, GAP sockets, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteObservationTraceFilterUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only by these rows. It has no
coordinate for a future observation, a substrate-internal induction principle,
a universal closure over all indices, or a host-level truth predicate.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Finite trace filter acceptance boundary]
\label{thm:finite-observation-trace-filter-acceptance-boundary}
For every accepted $\FiniteObservationTraceFilterUp$ carrier
$F=(O,T,K,A,G,H,C,P,N)$, a trace entry is available to a downstream consumer
only after it appears in the finite trace row $T$, is checked by the
kernel-filter row $K$, and is replayed through $C$ with provenance $P$ and
local name $N$. Entries outside the displayed trace are not refused as false;
they are routed to the GAP row $G$ when a consumer asks for universal closure.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier. The observed source is $O$, the trace is $T$, and the
filter row is $K$; no other row stores accepted trace entries.

The replay row $C$ makes the accepted route explicit. It starts from the
observation request, reads the finite trace entry, and then checks that entry
against $K$.

When the requested entry is not in the displayed trace, the packet exposes the
GAP row $G$ rather than converting absence into a universal claim. The audit
row $A$ records this non-escape boundary, while $P,N$ source and name the
finite route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Finite trace filter NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:finite-observation-trace-filter-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\FiniteObservationTraceFilterUp}$ surface consists
exactly of closed-observation source admission, finite trace exposure,
kernel-filter acceptance, non-escape audit, GAP-socket routing, componentwise
$\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local
naming. These obligations certify finite trace filtering only; they do not
certify induction, field completeness, universal closure, or future-observation
coverage.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission exposes the tuple
$$
F=(O,T,K,A,G,H,C,P,N).
$$
The source, trace, filter, audit, and GAP rows are the only content rows.

Apply the acceptance boundary of
\autoref{thm:finite-observation-trace-filter-acceptance-boundary}. It
identifies the accepted route through $T,K,C,P,N$ and routes universal requests
to $G$.

Transport through $H$ preserves this row structure componentwise. Since the
carrier has no row for induction, field completeness, universal closure, or
future observations, the listed rows exhaust the NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Finite trace filter TasteGate worklist]
\label{thm:finite-observation-trace-filter-tastegate-worklist}
Any Lean TasteGate implementation for $\FiniteObservationTraceFilterUp$ must
separate finite trace acceptance from algebraic adequacy by carrying explicit
\texttt{Nontrivial} and \texttt{FieldFaithful} instance obligations for the
numeric side of the filter. The paper packet records the finite trace boundary
only; those instances are downstream formal work and cannot be inferred from
closed observation rows alone.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The filter carrier records finite observation, trace, kernel acceptance,
audit, GAP, transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows. None of these rows
asserts that a numeric carrier has more than one element or that field
operations are faithful to the intended readback.

The acceptance boundary therefore cannot discharge \texttt{Nontrivial} or
\texttt{FieldFaithful} by projection. Those obligations must be visible in
the Lean TasteGate work for any numeric consumer of the filter.

This keeps the concrete packet honest: it certifies finite observation
filtering, while algebraic adequacy remains a separate formal instance
surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Finite trace filter GAP handoff]
\label{thm:finite-observation-trace-filter-gap-handoff}
If a downstream consumer treats an accepted $\FiniteObservationTraceFilterUp$
packet as evidence for a universal statement, the packet exports only the
finite accepted rows and the GAP socket $G$. It supplies no substrate-internal
induction rule, no future-observation oracle, no total trace closure, and no
host truth predicate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the NameCert obligations. They expose finite source, trace, filter,
audit, GAP, transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows.

A universal request is not one of the accepted trace entries. By the acceptance
boundary, such a request is routed to $G$ rather than accepted by $K$.

The non-escape audit row $A$ records that this is a refusal boundary. Since
the carrier has no row for induction, future observations, total closure, or a
host truth predicate, the GAP handoff is the only universal-facing export.
\end{proof}
