\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\SeparatedQuotientMetricUp$}

Following Bishop and Bridges constructive analysis and the Bourbaki--Kelley separated-reflection language, the $\SeparatedQuotientMetricUp$ packet records the standard passage from a pseudometric surface to a separated metric surface as finite BEDC data. It starts with the pre-separated distance rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-pseudometric-namecert}, exposes the separated classifier route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separatedmetric-namecert}, and hands only the routed classifier to the metric consumer of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}. Its numerical readback is confined to the regular rational windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert} and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet therefore names the constructive zero-distance separation route without adding quotient primitives, host equality, trichotomy, countable choice, selected limits, or ambient completeness of $\RealUp$.

\begin{definition}[SeparatedQuotientMetric carrier]
\label{def:separated-quotient-metric-carrier}
A $\SeparatedQuotientMetricUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(X,P,Z,S,R,E,H,C,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the source point-history admission row. It records the carried point names and the classifier inherited from the source packet, but it does not identify point rows by host equality.
\item $P$ is the source $\PseudometricUp$ packet. It supplies the displayed distance witness, dyadic tolerance ledger, zero-distance boundary, stream windows, regular rational readback, and real-distance seal available before separation.
\item $Z$ is the displayed zero-distance evidence row. It records finite requests showing that two admitted point rows have pseudometric distance zero through the rows of $P$.
\item $S$ is the separated classifier output. It consumes $Z$ and records the finite classifier evidence that the two source point histories have the same separated point reading.
\item $R$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$ finite-window handoff. It names the stream windows and regular rational approximants that a metric or real consumer may inspect before the separated classifier is read.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal reached from the pseudometric distance readback after the windows in $R$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for source point admission, pseudometric rows, zero-distance evidence, separated classifier output, finite windows, regular readback, real seal, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ replay from point admission through pseudometric distance inspection, zero-distance evidence, separated classifier output, finite-window readback, and real sealing.
\item $N$ is both the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\PseudometricUp$, $\SeparatedMetricUp$, $\MetricUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows and the local $\NameCert_{\SeparatedQuotientMetricUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier is a finite classifier certificate. It has no quotient-space coordinate, no host equality coordinate, no selected representative, no trichotomy row, no countable-choice selector, no selected limit, and no ambient completeness theorem for $\RealUp$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[SeparatedQuotientMetric NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:separated-quotient-metric-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\SeparatedQuotientMetricUp$ carrier $Q=(X,P,Z,S,R,E,H,C,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\SeparatedQuotientMetricUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the source point row, source $\PseudometricUp$ packet, zero-distance evidence, separated classifier output, finite-window and regular-rational handoff, $\RealUp$ seal, componentwise transport, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item zero-distance classifier exposure, requiring every displayed zero-distance witness in $Z$ to be consumed by the separated classifier row $S$ before any point-identity surface is exported;
\item separated readback, requiring the metric-facing consumer to read only the classifier output in $S$ and not an equivalence class or host equality proof;
\item $\RegSeqRatUp$ and $\RealUp$ handoff, requiring finite windows and regular rational approximants in $R$ to precede the real seal $E$ for every distance read;
\item transport and replay stability, requiring $H$ and $C$ to move the source, zero-distance, classifier, readback, real-seal, provenance, and naming rows componentwise and in the displayed order;
\item provenance visibility, requiring $N$ to name exactly the listed BEDC dependencies and the local NameCert row;
\item quotient non-escape, excluding quotient metric spaces, host equality, trichotomy, countable choice, selected representatives, selected limits, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, and bridge checking.
\end{enumerate}
No separated-quotient metric NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:separated-quotient-metric-carrier}. The packet displays precisely the source point row, source pseudometric packet, zero-distance evidence, separated classifier output, finite-window and regular-rational handoff, real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

Zero-distance classifier exposure follows from the order $P,Z,S$. The pseudometric packet supplies the distance surface, the row $Z$ records the zero-distance evidence, and the row $S$ is the first coordinate that can export separated point-classifier evidence.

Separated readback is therefore classifier readback. A metric-facing consumer receives the output of $S$, not a quotient class, selected representative, or host equality proof.

The numerical handoff is read through $R$ and $E$. Finite stream windows and regular rational approximants appear before the real seal, so a consumer cannot inspect a detached real distance without the displayed finite readback.

Transport and replay stability are the roles of $H$ and $C$. They move the listed coordinates componentwise and replay them in the carrier order, preserving the route from pseudometric evidence to separated classifier output.

The provenance and non-escape clauses are inventory checks on $N$ and on the fields of $Q$. Since no quotient metric space, host equality, trichotomy, choice selector, selected representative, selected limit, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness theorem, Lean marker, or bridge row appears in the carrier, none can be part of the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SeparatedQuotientMetric zero-distance route]
\label{thm:separated-quotient-metric-zero-distance-route}
Let $Q=(X,P,Z,S,R,E,H,C,N)$ be an accepted $\SeparatedQuotientMetricUp$ packet. Every consumer that reads point identity from pseudometric zero-distance evidence must factor through the displayed route
$$
\PseudometricUp\to Z\to S\to\SeparatedMetricUp\to\MetricUp .
$$
In particular, every $\MetricUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, or $\RealUp$ consumer sees the point-identity surface only after the zero-distance evidence has passed through the separated classifier $S$. The packet exports no alternative route through quotient metric spaces, host equality, trichotomy, countable choice, selected limits, selected representatives, or ambient completeness of $\RealUp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted packet $Q$. The source pseudometric row $P$ exposes distance witnesses and zero-distance boundary data, while $Z$ records the displayed zero-distance evidence for the pair of source point histories.

Before a point-identity surface can be read, the replay row $C$ sends the evidence in $Z$ through the separated classifier output $S$. This is the only coordinate in $Q$ that turns zero-distance evidence into separated classifier evidence.

Metric consumers read the resulting separated classifier before treating the point surface as metric-separated. Regular rational and real consumers read finite windows through $R$ and the real seal $E$ only along the same replay route, so they cannot bypass the classifier by inspecting the pseudometric row directly.

The exclusions follow from the carrier inventory. Quotient metric spaces, host equality, trichotomy, countable-choice selectors, selected limits, selected representatives, and ambient $\RealUp$ completeness are not fields of $Q$, and the provenance row names no such route.
\end{proof}
