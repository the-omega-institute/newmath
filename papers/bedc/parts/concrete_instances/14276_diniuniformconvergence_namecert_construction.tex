Following Bishop and Bridges, the $\DiniUniformConvergenceUp$ packet records the constructive finite-net surface of the Dini phenomenon over compact metric data. It reads compact-metric domain rows from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, finite total-bounded nets from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-totallybounded-namecert}, monotone continuous-map family rows from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuousmap-namecert}, uniform-limit comparison rows from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformlimit-namecert}, terminal real-name seals from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, finite stream windows from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, and regular rational readbacks from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}. The packet is a finite certificate for extracting a displayed uniform modulus from monotone pointwise windows; it is not open-cover compactness, countable choice, quotient real equality, host real equality, or a selected limit principle.

\begin{definition}[DiniUniformConvergence carrier]
\label{def:diniuniformconvergence-carrier}
A $\DiniUniformConvergenceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
D=(K,T,F,M,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $K$ is the $\CompactMetricUp$ domain row, including the visible compact-metric carrier, distance witness, and metric transport surface used by the family;
\item $T$ is the $\TotallyBoundedUp$ finite-net row, listing the inspected net points and dyadic radius budgets over the same domain row;
\item $F$ is the monotone family of $\ContinuousMapUp$ rows, indexed only by the finite observation schedule admitted by the packet;
\item $M$ is the dyadic modulus schedule assigning each tolerance row to a finite net radius, a family index, and a comparison budget;
\item $W$ is the family of $\StreamNameUp$ windows through which all inspected function values and limiting values are read;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row attached to those windows, carrying the regular rational comparisons used by the monotone test;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row for the limiting value at each inspected window, reached only after $W$ and $R$ have been displayed;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $K,T,F,M,W,R,E$;
\item $C$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ replay routes for the finite-net, monotone-family, window, readback, and seal reads;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CompactMetricUp$, $\TotallyBoundedUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\UniformLimitUp$, $\RealUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DiniUniformConvergenceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for an open cover, no countable-choice selector, no quotient equality of real names, no host real equality proof, no selected limit, and no ambient compactness theorem beyond the displayed finite-net packet.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Monotone window obligation]
\label{thm:diniuniformconvergence-obligation-monotone-window}
Let $D=(K,T,F,M,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\DiniUniformConvergenceUp$ carrier. Every monotonicity read used by the packet is confined to the displayed $\ContinuousMapUp$ family rows in $F$, the finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows in $W$, and the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row $R$. In particular, a consumer cannot replace the monotone window obligation by an open-cover argument, a countable-choice schedule, quotient real equality, host real equality, or a selected limiting value.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The only family data available to the monotonicity classifier are the rows $F$ together with the windows $W$ and regular rational readback $R$.

The schedule $M$ names finite dyadic tolerances and finite family indices. It does not quantify over all indices by a hidden selector; it only points to the rows already present in the packet.

Transport and replay are componentwise. The rows $H$ and $C$ can reread the same finite windows and continuous-map family rows, but they cannot introduce an open cover, quotient equality of real names, host equality, or a selected limit. Hence the monotone obligation is exactly the displayed finite-window obligation.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Uniform modulus extraction]
\label{thm:diniuniformconvergence-uniform-modulus-extraction}
For every accepted $\DiniUniformConvergenceUp$ carrier $D=(K,T,F,M,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)$ and every dyadic tolerance row named by $M$, the packet exports a finite uniform modulus row whose source is the total-bounded net $T$, whose value comparisons are read through $W$ and $R$, and whose limiting seals are the displayed $\RealUp$ rows in $E$. The extracted row supplies a $\UniformLimitUp$-facing modulus surface without open-cover compactness, countable choice, quotient real equality, host real equality, or selected limits.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a tolerance row in $M$. Its coordinates name a finite net radius, a finite family index, and the comparison budget to be checked on the net points carried by $T$.

For each listed net point, read the relevant $\ContinuousMapUp$ value through $F$, the finite window through $W$, the regular rational comparison through $R$, and the limiting seal through $E$. Monotonicity lets the same family index control all later inspected rows on that finite window surface.

The compact-metric information used here is exactly the total-bounded finite-net row, not an open-cover subcover. Since the packet contains no selector for countable families, no quotient equality of real names, no host real equality proof, and no detached limit coordinate, the resulting modulus is a finite row over $T,M,W,R,E$ and can be handed to the $\UniformLimitUp$ surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DiniUniformConvergence NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:diniuniformconvergence-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\DiniUniformConvergenceUp}$ surface for the carrier of \autoref{def:diniuniformconvergence-carrier} consists of five finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission: the accepted object is exactly the packet $D=(K,T,F,M,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)$ with its compact-metric domain, total-bounded finite nets, monotone continuous-map family, dyadic schedule, stream windows, regular readbacks, real seals, transports, continuations, provenance, and local naming row;
\item classifier: comparison reads only the displayed domain row $K$, finite-net row $T$, family rows $F$, modulus schedule $M$, windows $W$, readbacks $R$, and seals $E$;
\item transport stability: $H$ and $C$ preserve the same compact-metric, finite-net, family, window, readback, and seal rows under accepted consumer motion;
\item exactness: every uniform-modulus export is supplied by the displayed rows $T,M,W,R,E$ after the componentwise transport and replay routes are fixed;
\item ledger: every $\UniformLimitUp$-facing consumer first reads $K,T,F,M,W,R,E,H,C,P,N$ and receives no open cover, countable-choice selector, quotient real equality, host real equality, or selected limit.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from the finite packet. Each coordinate named in \autoref{def:diniuniformconvergence-carrier} is a displayed row, so the local certificate admits exactly that inventory.

The classifier is finite because every comparison request starts with a row of $M$ and then reads only the finite net points in $T$, the family rows in $F$, the windows in $W$, the regular rational readback in $R$, and the real seals in $E$.

Transport stability uses $H$ componentwise and then replays the same finite route through $C$. No transport step adds an open cover, quotient equality, host equality, selected limit, or choice schedule.

Exactness and the ledger clause follow from the same inventory. A uniform-limit-facing consumer receives the finite modulus route over $T,M,W,R,E$ together with provenance and local naming; the carrier exposes no other route by which an unrestricted classical Dini theorem could enter.
\end{proof}
