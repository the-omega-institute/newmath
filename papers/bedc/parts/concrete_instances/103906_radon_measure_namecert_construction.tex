\section{Radon Measure Carrier}
\label{sec:radon-measure-carrier}

\begin{definition}[RadonMeasure carrier]
\label{def:radon-measure-carrier}
A $\RadonMeasureUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
R=(X,M,O,K,V,D,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the compact metric source row, read through the $\CompactMetricUp$ and $\MetricUp$ surfaces.
\item $M$ is the base finite measure row, read through the $\MeasureUp$ event-value boundary.
\item $O$ is the open-ball locator row naming the open-ball cover requests visible to the packet.
\item $K$ is the compact inner approximant ledger for finite regular lower reads.
\item $V$ is the finite cover outer approximant ledger, routed through finite Vitali cover rows when cover refinement is requested.
\item $D$ is the distribution-facing value row exposed to downstream $\DistributionUp$ consumers.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $X,M,O,K,V,D,C,P,N$.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from compact metric source and base measure row through open-ball localization, compact inner approximation, finite cover outer approximation, distribution value, provenance, and local naming.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RadonMeasureUp$, $\MeasureUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\MetricUp$, $\OpenBallUp$, $\FiniteVitaliCoverUp$, $\DistributionUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RadonMeasureUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $X,M,O,K,V,D,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for host sigma-additivity, choice-selected compact approximants, quotient equality of measures, an ambient measurable-space completion, or a standard-model measure object outside the displayed finite rows.
\end{definition}

\section{Regularity Obligations}
\label{sec:radon-measure-obligations}

\begin{theorem}[RadonMeasure NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:radon-measure-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RadonMeasureUp$ carrier $R=(X,M,O,K,V,D,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\RadonMeasureUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission through $X$ and $M$, regularity classifier stability through $O,K,V$, inner approximation exactness through $K$, outer approximation exactness through $V$, finite-cover ledger visibility through the $\FiniteVitaliCoverUp$ handoff, distribution-facing value exposure through $D$, componentwise transport through $H$, displayed replay through $C$, provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$. It refuses host sigma-additivity, choice-selected approximants, quotient measure equality, and ambient standard-model measure theory as public coordinates.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:radon-measure-carrier}. The source row $X$ and measure row $M$ are the only admitted compact metric and finite measure inputs.

The locator row $O$, compact inner ledger $K$, and finite cover outer ledger $V$ are the only regularity classifier rows. Inner and outer exactness are therefore read from the displayed approximation ledgers rather than from a host regularity theorem.

The rows $D,H,C,P,N$ expose distribution values, componentwise transport, replay, provenance, and local naming for the same finite packet. Since no remaining coordinate stores host sigma-additivity, choice-selected approximants, quotient measure equality, or an ambient standard-model measure object, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RadonMeasure compact regularity route]
\label{thm:radon-measure-compact-regularity-route}
Every accepted $\RadonMeasureUp$ packet routes a compact metric finite-cover request through the open-ball locator row $O$, the compact inner approximant ledger $K$, the finite Vitali cover outer ledger $V$, and the base measure value row $M$ before any distribution consumer can read the value row $D$. A request that bypasses one of $O,K,V,M$ is not an accepted RadonMeasure compact-regularity route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the replay row $C$ and the provenance row $P$. They bind compact metric source data to open-ball localization, compact inner approximation, finite cover outer approximation, and base measure valuation before the distribution-facing row is exposed.

The transport row $H$ preserves the same sequence componentwise. Thus a consumer can read $D$ only after the route has displayed $O,K,V,M$ inside the same packet.

Dropping any one of $O,K,V,M$ leaves no finite row witnessing the corresponding localization, inner read, outer read, or measure value. Such a route is outside the accepted carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RadonMeasure weak consumer boundary]
\label{thm:radon-measure-weak-consumer-boundary}
Downstream $\DistributionUp$ and $\RieszRepresentationUp$ consumers of an accepted $\RadonMeasureUp$ packet read only the displayed regular measure packet $R=(X,M,O,K,V,D,H,C,P,N)$. They cannot infer host measure equality, unlisted integration theorems, choice-selected compact approximants, ambient sigma-additivity, or any representation theorem not displayed by the packet rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier exposes downstream access through $D$ together with the replay, transport, provenance, and local naming rows. These rows record only finite regular-measure data over the displayed compact metric, open-ball, compact approximant, finite cover, and base measure inputs.

No carrier coordinate names host measure equality, an integration theorem, a selector for compact approximants, ambient sigma-additivity, or a representation theorem. Therefore a distribution or Riesz-facing consumer receives the weak displayed packet and no stronger host-theoretic consequence.
\end{proof}
