The $\LorentzFrameRateUp$ interface records the finite packet at the boundary where $\MaxCausalRateUp$ becomes an observer-symmetric frame-rate ledger. It does not introduce Lorentz geometry, a metric tensor, synchronized clocks, global frames, or host real-valued speed. It names only the BEDC rows by which the maximum-rate surface can be read uniformly across the displayed histories of a multi-Hist configuration.

\begin{definition}[LorentzFrameRate carrier]
\label{def:lorentz-frame-rate-carrier}
A $\LorentzFrameRateUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(M,X,R,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is an accepted $\MultiHistConfigUp$ row displaying the finite observer-history configuration and its no-global-sync boundary;
\item $X$ is the finite family of accepted $\CrossHistCausalUp$ witness rows over selected history pairs from $M$;
\item $R$ is an accepted $\MaxCausalRateUp$ row whose witness family is the displayed family $X$ over the same configuration row $M$;
\item $S$ is the observer-symmetry classifier row, recording that each selected observer read of the maximum-rate surface is the same finite unary-bound row of $R$ after componentwise transport;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $M$, $X$, $R$, $S$, the continuation routes, the package row, and the local naming row;
\item $C$ records the visible $\Cont$ routes from the displayed multi-Hist, causal, maximum-rate, and symmetry rows into the local certificate;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LorentzFrameRateUp$, $\MultiHistConfigUp$, $\CrossHistCausalUp$, $\MaxCausalRateUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LorentzFrameRateUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exports a finite observer-symmetric maximum-rate packet. Its classifier preserves the displayed maximum-rate row under accepted observer transport while excluding global frames, synchronized clocks, metric tensors, host real-valued speed, and Lorentz geometry as primitive input.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[LorentzFrameRate boundary]
\label{thm:lorentz-frame-rate-boundary}
Every accepted $\LorentzFrameRateUp$ carrier presents the next inter-Hist locality boundary as the finite packet
$$
L=(M,X,R,S,H,C,P,N).
$$
A consumer may read the multi-Hist configuration row, the selected cross-Hist causal witnesses, the maximum-rate row, the observer-symmetry classifier, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, visible $\Cont$ routes, package provenance, and the local $\NameCert_{\LorentzFrameRateUp}$ row. No other frame-rate surface is public through this certificate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The rows $M,X,R,S,H,C,P,N$ are exactly the admitted coordinates of \autoref{def:lorentz-frame-rate-carrier}.

Read $M$ and $X$ first. These rows keep the boundary tied to the displayed multi-Hist configuration and the selected cross-Hist causal witnesses.

Next read $R$ and $S$. The maximum-rate row supplies the finite unary-bound surface, while the observer-symmetry classifier records that transported observer reads preserve that same displayed row.

Finally route all consumer access through $H,C,P,N$. The resulting boundary is exhausted by the listed rows, so no additional frame-rate surface is exported.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LorentzFrameRate non-escape]
\label{thm:lorentz-frame-rate-non-escape}
An accepted $\LorentzFrameRateUp$ carrier cannot export a global frame, synchronized clock, metric tensor, host real-valued speed, or Lorentz geometry as a primitive input. Any consumer request for those objects leaves the finite rows
$$
M,X,R,S,H,C,P,N
$$
rather than projecting from the local $\NameCert_{\LorentzFrameRateUp}$ surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier inventory contains only the displayed multi-Hist row, cross-Hist witness family, maximum-rate row, symmetry classifier, transport rows, continuation routes, provenance, and local naming row.

Apply the non-escape clauses of the upstream $\MultiHistConfigUp$, $\CrossHistCausalUp$, and $\MaxCausalRateUp$ certificates. Those rows already exclude global synchronization, unlisted causal witnesses, metric geometry, and ambient real-valued rates from their local surfaces.

The symmetry classifier $S$ adds no coordinate of the excluded kind. It records preservation of the finite maximum-rate row under accepted observer transport, so it cannot become a frame, a clock, a tensor, a host speed, or Lorentz geometry.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LorentzFrameRate NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:lorentz-frame-rate-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:lorentz-frame-rate-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\LorentzFrameRateUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission, configuration inheritance from $\MultiHistConfigUp$, causal-witness inheritance from $\CrossHistCausalUp$, maximum-rate inheritance from $\MaxCausalRateUp$, observer-symmetry preservation of the shared unary-bound row, componentwise $\hsame$ stability, displayed $\Cont$ locality, $\Pkg$ provenance, and non-escape for global frames, synchronized clocks, metric tensors, host real-valued speed, and Lorentz geometry as primitive input.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:lorentz-frame-rate-carrier}. The finite packet supplies every row named by the local certificate.

The inherited rows are read in dependency order. The configuration row $M$ supplies the displayed histories, the family $X$ supplies the selected cross-Hist causal witnesses over those histories, and the maximum-rate row $R$ supplies the shared unary-bound surface over the same witnesses.

Observer-symmetry preservation is the role of $S$. It states that the selected observer reads of $R$ remain the same displayed maximum-rate row after componentwise transport.

The rows $H,C,P,N$ provide the remaining stability, locality, provenance, and local naming data. Since no coordinate of the carrier is a global frame, synchronized clock, metric tensor, host real-valued speed, or Lorentz geometry, the non-escape obligation is the displayed inventory boundary.
\end{proof}
