\begin{definition}[LorentzFrameRate carrier]
\label{def:lorentz-frame-rate-carrier}
A $\LorentzFrameRateUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(M,X,R,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is an accepted $\MultiHistConfigUp$ row displaying the finite observer-history configuration and its no-global-sync boundary;
\item $X$ is the finite family of accepted $\CrossHistCausalUp$ witness rows over selected history pairs from $M$;
\item $R$ is an accepted $\MaxCausalRateUp$ row whose witness family is the displayed family $X$ over the same configuration row $M$;
\item $S$ is the observer-symmetry classifier row, recording that each selected observer read of the maximum-rate surface is the same finite unary-bound row of $R$ after componentwise transport;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $M$, $X$, $R$, $S$, the continuation routes, the package row, and the local naming row;
\item $C$ records the visible $\Cont$ routes from the displayed multi-Hist, causal, maximum-rate, and symmetry rows into the local certificate;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LorentzFrameRateUp$, $\MultiHistConfigUp$, $\CrossHistCausalUp$, $\MaxCausalRateUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LorentzFrameRateUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exports a finite observer-symmetric maximum-rate packet. Its classifier preserves the displayed maximum-rate row under accepted observer transport while excluding global frames, synchronized clocks, metric tensors, host real-valued speed, and Lorentz geometry as primitive input.
\end{definition}

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

Read $M$ and $X$ first. These rows keep the boundary tied to the displayed multi-Hist configuration and the selected cross-Hist causal witnesses.

\begin{theorem}[Frame-rate observer-symmetry transport]
\label{thm:frame-rate-observer-symmetry-transport}
Let
$$
L=(M,X,R,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\LorentzFrameRateUp$ carrier. The observer-symmetry row $S$ transports the displayed maximum-rate row $R$ componentwise under the $\hsame$ data in $H$: every selected observer read named by $S$ has the same $\MaxCausalRateUp$ unary-bound surface as $R$ after applying the corresponding component of $H$, and the transported read remains routed through the same finite continuation packet $C$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier and read the symmetry coordinate $S$. By \autoref{def:lorentz-frame-rate-carrier}, $S$ records exactly the observer reads whose maximum-rate surface is compared with the displayed row $R$.

Next read the transport coordinate $H$. Its $R$- and $S$-components move the maximum-rate row and each observer-symmetry read together, so the comparison is componentwise rather than a global frame comparison.

Finally use the continuation coordinate $C$. It keeps every transported observer read on the displayed route from $M$ and $X$ to $R$ and then to the local certificate, so the shared unary-bound surface is preserved as a finite $\MaxCausalRateUp$ row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Frame-rate rate-row inheritance]
\label{thm:frame-rate-rate-row-inheritance}
For an accepted $\LorentzFrameRateUp$ carrier
$$
L=(M,X,R,S,H,C,P,N),
$$
the maximum-rate row $R$ is inherited only from the same upstream $\MultiHistConfigUp$ row $M$ and $\CrossHistCausalUp$ witness family $X$ displayed by the carrier. No consumer of $L$ may read $R$ as a rate row over a different configuration, an unlisted causal-witness family, or an ambient host geometry.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier definition binds $R$ to $M$ and $X$: $R$ is the accepted $\MaxCausalRateUp$ row whose witness family is the displayed family $X$ over the same configuration row $M$.

The continuation routes preserve this dependency. A consumer reaches $R$ only through the visible route from the multi-Hist configuration row to the selected cross-Hist causal witnesses and then to the maximum-rate row.

The provenance row $P$ records the same dependency surface. Since neither the carrier nor the provenance row contains a different configuration, an unlisted witness family, or an ambient geometry coordinate, the inheritance of $R$ is limited to the displayed $M$ and $X$ rows.
\end{proof}
