\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\EstimatorUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-estimator-namecert}

The estimator interface $\EstimatorUp$ packages a sequence of measurable functions of i.i.d. samples taking values in a parameter space, with consistency, bias, and variance certificates. It depends on $\NameCert_{\RandomVarUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-randomvar-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\IndependenceUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-independence-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:estimator-carrier}

\begin{definition}[EstimatorUp BHist source surface]
\label{def:estimator-bhist-source-surface}
The $\EstimatorUp$ $\mathsf{BHist}$ source surface consists of displayed
$\RandomVarUp$ sample rows, an $\IndependenceUp$ sample-family row, estimator
endpoint rows, bias and variance witness rows, componentwise $\hsame$
transport, finite $\Cont$ readback ledgers, and $\Pkg$ provenance naming the
source certificates. A carried estimator packet is accepted only through these
rows: the sample rows expose the random-variable inputs, the independence row
records the finite sample-family boundary, the endpoint rows name the
estimator outputs, and the bias and variance witnesses are read by finite
continuation ledgers from the same packet.
\end{definition}
\leandef{BEDC.Derived.EstimatorUp.EstimatorBHistSourceSurface}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:estimator-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[EstimatorUp source obligation]
\label{thm:estimator-source-obligation}
Every $\EstimatorUp$ $\mathsf{BHist}$ source packet is sourced exactly by the
$\RandomVarUp$ sample rows, the $\IndependenceUp$ sample-family row, the
estimator endpoint rows, the bias and variance witness rows, finite $\Cont$
readback, and $\Pkg$ provenance of
\autoref{def:estimator-bhist-source-surface}. It includes no host
probability-space equality, no asymptotic normality row, and no completed
statistical convergence claim.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the source surface definition. Its sample coordinates are displayed
$\RandomVarUp$ rows, and its family coordinate is the single
$\IndependenceUp$ row that relates those samples inside $\mathsf{BHist}$.

The estimator endpoint, bias, and variance rows are then read by the listed
finite $\Cont$ ledgers. The $\Pkg$ provenance names exactly the certificates
used for those reads, so there is no separate source from an ambient
probability space.

Repacking those rows gives the whole source packet. Since the definition
contains no row for host probability-space equality, asymptotic normality, or
completed statistical convergence, none is exported by the source obligation.
\end{proof}

\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.EstimatorUp.EstimatorBHistSourceSurface\_source\_obligation}

\begin{theorem}[EstimatorUp transport obligation]
\label{thm:estimator-transport-obligation}
The classifier transport obligation for $\EstimatorUp$ is componentwise
$\hsame$ transport of the displayed $\RandomVarUp$ samples, the
$\IndependenceUp$ sample-family row, the estimator endpoint rows, the bias and
variance witness rows, and the finite $\Cont$ ledgers of
\autoref{def:estimator-bhist-source-surface}. Transport preserves the same
$\Pkg$ provenance and does not identify host probability spaces, derive
asymptotic normality, or complete any statistical convergence argument.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with two displayed estimator packets related componentwise by $\hsame$.
The sample rows transport through their $\RandomVarUp$ certificates, while the
sample-family row transports through the $\IndependenceUp$ certificate.

Next transport the endpoint, bias, and variance rows along the listed
component comparisons. The finite $\Cont$ ledgers are stable under the same
endpoint replacement, so the readback packet remains a $\mathsf{BHist}$
estimator packet with unchanged $\Pkg$ provenance.

The transported witness is therefore still built only from the source surface.
No step introduces host probability-space equality, asymptotic normality, or a
completed convergence theorem.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.EstimatorUp.EstimatorBHistSourceSurface\_transport\_obligation}

\begin{theorem}[EstimatorUp finite Cont-readback determinacy]
\label{thm:estimator-finite-cont-readback-determinacy}
Let $E_0$ and $E_1$ be accepted $\EstimatorUp$ $\mathsf{BHist}$ packets. Suppose
they display the same $\RandomVarUp$ sample rows, the same $\IndependenceUp$
sample-family row, matching estimator endpoint, bias, and variance witness
rows, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, the same finite $\Cont$ ledgers, and
the same $\Pkg$ provenance from
\autoref{def:estimator-bhist-source-surface}. Then every public estimator
readback row is identical after the displayed transports. No asymptotic
normality row and no completed convergence row can mediate this readback.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project both accepted packets through
\autoref{thm:estimator-source-obligation}. The only source rows exposed are the
displayed sample rows, sample-family row, endpoint rows, bias and variance
witness rows, finite continuation ledgers, and package provenance listed in
\autoref{def:estimator-bhist-source-surface}.

Next apply \autoref{thm:estimator-transport-obligation} componentwise. The
matching $\hsame$ transports identify the sample, independence, endpoint, bias,
and variance rows while preserving the same finite $\Cont$ ledgers and the same
$\Pkg$ provenance.

Compare the finite continuation ledgers row-by-row. Since the ledgers are the
same and their endpoints have already been transported into the same displayed
rows, every public readback entry of $E_0$ agrees with the corresponding entry
of $E_1$.

Repack the common rows using
\autoref{thm:estimator-namecert-obligation-surface}. The public
$\NameCert_{\EstimatorUp}$ surface has no field for asymptotic normality or
completed statistical convergence, so neither can act as an intermediate
readback witness.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.EstimatorUp.EstimatorBHistSourceSurface\_finite\_cont\_readback\_determinacy}

\begin{theorem}[EstimatorUp bias and variance ledger exclusion]
\label{thm:estimator-bias-variance-ledger-exclusion}
Let $E$ be an accepted $\EstimatorUp$ $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet. If $E$ exposes
a public bias witness row or a public variance witness row, that row factors
through the bias or variance coordinate of
\autoref{def:estimator-bhist-source-surface}, the finite $\Cont$ readback
ledgers displayed there, and the same $\Pkg$ provenance naming the source
certificates. No host probability-space equality, asymptotic normality row,
completed convergence theorem, or external statistical model supplies a
public bias or variance witness for $\NameCert_{\EstimatorUp}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet through
\autoref{thm:estimator-source-obligation}. The projection lists the displayed
sample rows, the sample-family row, estimator endpoint rows, bias and
variance witness rows, finite continuation ledgers, and package provenance as
the complete source surface for $E$.

Transport this projected packet by
\autoref{thm:estimator-transport-obligation}. Componentwise $\hsame$ movement
keeps the bias and variance coordinates inside the same displayed packet and
preserves the finite $\Cont$ ledgers together with their $\Pkg$ provenance.

Now compare public readback rows using
\autoref{thm:estimator-finite-cont-readback-determinacy}. Any exposed bias or
variance witness row is determined by the displayed finite ledger row after
the listed transports, so it cannot be replaced by a row outside the source
surface.

The source surface has no coordinate for host probability-space equality,
asymptotic normality, completed convergence, or an external statistical
model. Hence none of those data can enter the public
$\NameCert_{\EstimatorUp}$ witness boundary.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.EstimatorUp.EstimatorBHistSourceSurface\_bias\_variance\_ledger\_exclusion}

\begin{theorem}[EstimatorUp NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:estimator-namecert-obligation-surface}
The public $\NameCert_{\EstimatorUp}$ obligation surface is precisely the
$\mathsf{BHist}$ source surface of
\autoref{def:estimator-bhist-source-surface}, the source obligation of
\autoref{thm:estimator-source-obligation}, and the transport obligation of
\autoref{thm:estimator-transport-obligation}. Its public reads expose only
sample, independence, endpoint, bias, variance, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\Pkg$
rows, and exclude host probability-space equality, asymptotic normality, and
completed statistical convergence.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier row is the displayed source surface. By
\autoref{thm:estimator-source-obligation}, every accepted estimator packet
unpacks into the named sample, independence, endpoint, bias, variance,
continuation, and provenance rows.

The classifier and stability rows are supplied by
\autoref{thm:estimator-transport-obligation}. Componentwise $\hsame$ moves the
visible rows and preserves the finite ledgers, so the transported packet
reassembles under the same $\NameCert_{\EstimatorUp}$ interface.

Combining these two obligations gives the exact public surface. The surface is
anchored in $\mathsf{BHist}$ and contains no field for host probability-space
equality, asymptotic normality, or completed statistical convergence.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.EstimatorUp.EstimatorBHistSourceSurface\_namecert\_obligation\_surface}
