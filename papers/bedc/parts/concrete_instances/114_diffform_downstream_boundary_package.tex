\begin{definition}[Differential-form downstream boundary package]
\label{def:diffform-root-downstream-boundary-package}
The root downstream boundary package for $\DiffFormUp$ is the visible
certificate surface
$$
\mathsf{DiffFormDownstreamBoundary}_{M,T}.
$$
It consists exactly of the carried-form rows of
\autoref{def:diffform-bhist-form-carrier}, the classifier rows of
\autoref{def:diffform-bhist-form-classifier}, the exterior-input rows of
\autoref{def:diffform-exterior-derivative-visible-input-package}, the wedge
rows supplied by \autoref{def:diffform-wedge-degree-ledger},
\autoref{thm:diffform-wedge-ledger-coverage},
\autoref{thm:diffform-wedge-probe-concatenation-coverage},
\autoref{thm:diffform-wedge-probe-concatenation-classifier-stability}, and
\autoref{thm:diffform-wedge-degree-classifier-stability}, the finite
antisymmetry rows of \autoref{def:diffform-antisymmetry-chain-ledger}, and the
downstream scope rows of
\autoref{thm:diffform-exterior-derivative-downstream-scope},
\autoref{thm:diffform-derham-input-boundary}, and
\autoref{thm:diffform-wedge-derivative-input-boundary}. The package has no
field for $d^2=0$, closedness, nondegeneracy, contact nonintegrability, or a
standard bridge.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Differential-form exterior input root closure]
\label{thm:diffform-exterior-input-root-closure}
Every row of
$\mathsf{DiffFormExteriorInput}_{M,T}(\omega,D\omega)$ that a downstream
$\DeRhamUp$, $\SymplecticUp$, or $\ContactUp$ chapter may read is already a
row of $\mathsf{DiffFormDownstreamBoundary}_{M,T}$. In particular, the
source carrier, output carrier, successor-degree continuation, transported
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ rows, transported $\TensorProductUp$ tensor row,
retained scalar endpoint comparison, and restricted source ledger are all
root-visible rows, and no second exterior-derivative or closedness row is
introduced by passing through the package.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the exterior-input package of
\autoref{def:diffform-exterior-derivative-visible-input-package}. Its source
and output are carried form rows of
\autoref{def:diffform-bhist-form-carrier}; its comparison clauses are exactly
the $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, $\TensorProductUp$, scalar
endpoint, and source-ledger rows listed there and in
\autoref{def:diffform-bhist-form-classifier}.

Repack those rows into
\autoref{def:diffform-root-downstream-boundary-package}. The boundary package
keeps the exterior-input row as a visible component, and it retains the
classifier-stability interface through the same componentwise histories.

Finally apply the downstream scope theorem
\autoref{thm:diffform-exterior-derivative-downstream-scope}. That theorem
allows downstream exterior-derivative chapters to consume the carrier,
classifier, degree-raise candidate, endpoint transport, and exterior-algebra
substrate, but it does not add a second derivative, closedness, nondegeneracy,
or contact nonintegrability row. Thus exterior input closes at the root
boundary package without importing any downstream law.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DiffFormUp.DiffFormExteriorInput\_root\_transport\_closure}

\begin{theorem}[Differential-form wedge derivative boundary compatibility]
\label{thm:diffform-wedge-derivative-boundary-compatibility}
When a carried row participates both in an admitted wedge row and in an
accepted exterior-input row, their common downstream boundary is exactly the
intersection recorded by
\autoref{thm:diffform-wedge-derivative-input-boundary} inside
$\mathsf{DiffFormDownstreamBoundary}_{M,T}$. The common rows are the carried
form row, its unary degree row, the wedge degree continuation ledger, the
one-step exterior-derivative continuation ledger, the transported probe,
tensor, scalar endpoint, antisymmetry, and source rows, and their
$\sim_{\DiffFormUp}$ classifier comparisons.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DiffFormUp.DiffFormDownstreamBoundary\_exterior\_wedge\_common\_rows}
\begin{proof}
Read the wedge side first. Wedge admission is bounded by
\autoref{thm:diffform-wedge-ledger-coverage}; the probe output is bounded by
\autoref{thm:diffform-wedge-probe-concatenation-coverage}; the degree
bookkeeping is the visible ledger of
\autoref{def:diffform-wedge-degree-ledger}. Classifier transport for these
rows is supplied by
\autoref{thm:diffform-wedge-probe-concatenation-classifier-stability} and
\autoref{thm:diffform-wedge-degree-classifier-stability}.

Read the exterior-input side next. By
\autoref{def:diffform-exterior-derivative-visible-input-package}, an accepted
input exposes the source and output carried rows, the successor-degree
continuation, transported probe and tensor rows, scalar endpoint comparison,
and the operator-owned source ledger.

Now apply \autoref{thm:diffform-wedge-derivative-input-boundary}. It states
that the shared visible data are precisely the rows named by both readings,
with no Leibniz rule and no nilpotence row. The antisymmetry portion is
controlled by \autoref{def:diffform-antisymmetry-chain-ledger} and remains
classifier-stable through the displayed $\DiffFormUp$ comparison rows.

These rows are all components of
\autoref{def:diffform-root-downstream-boundary-package}. Therefore the wedge
and exterior-input interfaces are compatible at the downstream boundary
without importing any closedness, nondegeneracy, contact nonintegrability, or
operator-square assertion.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Differential-form downstream boundary exhaustion]
\label{thm:diffform-downstream-boundary-exhaustion}
The package $\mathsf{DiffFormDownstreamBoundary}_{M,T}$ exhausts the
$\DiffFormUp$ root rows visible to $\DeRhamUp$, $\SymplecticUp$, and
$\ContactUp$. Any downstream consumption of $\DiffFormUp$ must factor through
the carrier and classifier rows, exterior-input rows, wedge rows,
antisymmetry-chain rows, and the three downstream scope rows listed in
\autoref{def:diffform-root-downstream-boundary-package}; no downstream
consumer can project a closedness, nondegeneracy, contact nonintegrability,
nilpotence, or bridge row from $\DiffFormUp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an arbitrary downstream read of a $\DiffFormUp$ row. The carrier
and classifier projections are exhausted by
\autoref{def:diffform-bhist-form-carrier},
\autoref{def:diffform-bhist-form-classifier},
\autoref{thm:diffform-carrier-hsame-transport}, and
\autoref{thm:diffform-form-classifier-stability}. These rows mention only
$BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$,
$\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$, and $\NameCert_{\TensorProductUp}$.

For derivative input, apply
\autoref{thm:diffform-exterior-input-root-closure}. It repacks every accepted
exterior-input row into the boundary package and leaves all operator and
geometric laws outside the root surface.

For wedge interaction, apply
\autoref{thm:diffform-wedge-derivative-boundary-compatibility}. It identifies
the wedge-degree, probe-concatenation, antisymmetry, and exterior-input rows
that can be shared at the downstream boundary, and it excludes the Leibniz
and nilpotence rows.

Finally use the downstream scope rows
\autoref{thm:diffform-exterior-derivative-downstream-scope},
\autoref{thm:diffform-derham-input-boundary}, and
\autoref{thm:diffform-wedge-derivative-input-boundary}. They name
$\DeRhamUp$, $\SymplecticUp$, and $\ContactUp$ as the downstream consumers
and restrict their access to the same visible carrier, classifier, wedge,
antisymmetry, exterior-input, transport, and source ledgers. Since
\autoref{def:diffform-root-downstream-boundary-package} contains exactly
those rows, every downstream read factors through it and no hidden row remains
to be consumed.
\end{proof}
