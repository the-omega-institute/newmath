\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\BoundedVariationUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-boundedvariation-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\BoundedVariationUp$ interface names the finite partition-variation packet used by real-analysis consumers of $\RealUp$ data. It follows the Jordan bounded-variation surface treated in Rudin, \emph{Principles of Mathematical Analysis}, and Folland, \emph{Real Analysis}, but expresses every read as a finite partition ledger over regular rational names, dyadic observations, endpoint transports, and package provenance. The packet does not introduce a completed function space, a supremum over all partitions, a variation measure, or a host real-valued function quotient.

Its dependency list is $\RealUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, and interval-partition rows supplied by $\RationalIntervalUp$ or $\NestedIntervalUp$ when such endpoints are available.

\begin{definition}[BoundedVariation carrier]
\label{def:bounded-variation-carrier}
A $\BoundedVariationUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
B=(I,\Pi,X,D,V,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $I$ is the rational or nested interval row on which the variation packet is read;
\item $\Pi$ is a finite ordered interval-partition ledger with displayed endpoint rows and common-refinement rows;
\item $X$ records the $\RealUp$ endpoint values or their $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular-name representatives at the partition points;
\item $D$ records the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observation rows used to compare endpoint differences on each partition edge;
\item $V$ is the finite dyadic variation-sum ledger attached to the displayed partition;
\item $R$ records common-refinement comparison rows for transported partitions and endpoint reads;
\item $H$, $C$, $P$, and $N$ are respectively the visible $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert_{\BoundedVariationUp}$ provenance rows.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through common refinements of $\Pi$, transported endpoint values, dyadic variation-sum ledgers, endpoint-difference comparisons, and the displayed provenance rows.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[BoundedVariation NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:bounded-variation-namecert-obligations}
Every accepted $\BoundedVariationUp$ packet with carrier \autoref{def:bounded-variation-carrier} carries the following naming-certificate obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier habitation by a constant $\RealUp$ or $\RegSeqRatUp$ endpoint row, with zero dyadic variation sum on every displayed finite partition;
\item classifier comparison by common refinement of the finite partition ledgers and componentwise endpoint transport;
\item dyadic variation-sum exactness for each displayed partition edge, read through $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observations and $\RegSeqRatUp$ endpoint comparisons;
\item carrier transport under displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ routes for interval rows, partitions, endpoint names, dyadic observations, variation sums, and refinement rows;
\item boundary exclusion of completed function spaces, variation measures, host supremum principles, quotient functions, and hidden partitions.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For habitation, take a constant real-name or regular-rational endpoint row. Every edge of a displayed finite partition has equal endpoint reads, so the dyadic edge differences and their finite sum are the zero variation ledger.

Classifier comparison is by common refinement. Refine two displayed partition ledgers to a shared finite ledger, transport the endpoint rows componentwise, and compare the dyadic edge observations along that shared list.

Variation-sum exactness is read edge by edge from the carrier. The $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observations give the displayed endpoint-difference rows, and the finite sum row $V$ records exactly those edge rows and no unlisted partition.

Transport uses the stored $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ coordinates. Moving the interval, partition, endpoint, dyadic, sum, and refinement rows along those routes repacks the same finite packet with the same local $\NameCert$ provenance.

The boundary exclusion follows because the carrier has no coordinate for a completed function space, variation measure, host supremum over all partitions, quotient of functions, or hidden partition family.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BoundedVariation variation-ledger exactness]
\label{thm:bounded-variation-variation-ledger-exactness}
For every accepted $\BoundedVariationUp$ packet and every displayed finite partition row $\Pi$, the public variation read factors through the interval row, the ordered endpoint ledger, the $\RealUp$ or $\RegSeqRatUp$ endpoint names, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ edge-difference observations, and the finite dyadic variation-sum row $V$. A common-refinement read transports the same edge data and repacks the same finite sum ledger.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the displayed partition row $\Pi$. Its ordered endpoint ledger gives the only edges whose variation contribution may be read by the packet.

For each edge, read the two endpoint names through $X$ and the dyadic comparison row through $D$. The regular-name and real-seal data are therefore consumed before the finite variation sum is formed.

The row $V$ stores the finite sum of exactly those displayed edge observations. When a common refinement is supplied by $R$, the endpoint transports and dyadic edge rows are repacked along the refinement ledger, so the public read remains a finite partition-sum read.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BoundedVariation carrier transport]
\label{thm:bounded-variation-carrier-transport}
Let $B$ and $B'$ be accepted $\BoundedVariationUp$ packets. If their interval rows, finite partition ledgers, endpoint-name rows, dyadic observation rows, variation-sum ledgers, and common-refinement rows are transported componentwise by displayed $\hsame$ routes and read through the corresponding $\Cont$ routes, then the local classifier identifies the same bounded-variation packet on every common finite refinement.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold the classifier of \autoref{def:bounded-variation-carrier}. It compares only the interval row, partition ledger, endpoint names, dyadic observations, finite variation sums, refinement rows, and provenance.

Move each of those coordinates along the displayed $\hsame$ routes. The $\Cont$ routes preserve the read order from an interval request to a partition, then to endpoint names, edge observations, and the finite sum row.

Apply \autoref{thm:bounded-variation-variation-ledger-exactness} on the common refinement. The transported endpoint and dyadic rows repack the same finite variation ledger, leaving no extra classifier coordinate on which the packets could differ.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BoundedVariation no-escape boundary]
\label{thm:bounded-variation-no-escape-boundary}
The $\BoundedVariationUp$ public boundary exports only finite interval-partition rows, transported endpoint names, dyadic edge-difference observations, common-refinement ledgers, finite variation sums, and visible $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ provenance. It exports no completed function-space carrier, variation measure, supremum over all partitions, quotient of real-valued functions, or hidden partition family.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier coordinates of \autoref{def:bounded-variation-carrier}. The only analysis-facing data are the displayed interval row, the finite partition ledger, endpoint names, dyadic observations, the finite sum row, and common-refinement rows.

The exactness theorem \autoref{thm:bounded-variation-variation-ledger-exactness} shows that every public variation read factors through those rows. The transport theorem \autoref{thm:bounded-variation-carrier-transport} shows that moving the packet preserves the same finite ledger.

No coordinate names a completed function space, variation measure, host supremum over all partitions, quotient function, or hidden partition family. Hence none of those objects is exported by the public boundary.
\end{proof}
