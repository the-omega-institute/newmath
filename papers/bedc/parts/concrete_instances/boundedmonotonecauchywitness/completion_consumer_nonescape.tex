\begin{theorem}[Bounded monotone Cauchy witness tail envelope public export]
\label{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-tail-envelope-public-export}
Let $W=(S,F,\sigma,\mu,\lambda,I,Q,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\BoundedMonotoneCauchyWitnessUp$ carrier, and let
$$
E=(S,F,\sigma,\mu,\lambda,I,H,C,P,N)
$$
be its scheduled tail envelope. The envelope $E$ exports the single public pre-seal service for completion-facing consumers: source reads pass through $S$, regular rational reads pass through $F$, window requests pass through $\sigma$, tolerance answers pass through $\mu$, dyadic and upper-bound budget reads pass through $\lambda$, trap reads pass through $I$, and the transport, continuation, provenance, and naming routes pass through $H,C,P,N$.

Thus every public tail-envelope read is already a read through $S,F,\sigma,\mu,\lambda,I,H,C,P,N$ before the $\RealUp$ seal row $Q$ is consumed. The export contains no selected limit, quotient real equality, host real equality, trichotomy, ambient completeness, open-cover compactness, or choice-like tail selector.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.BoundedMonotoneCauchyWitnessUp.BoundedMonotoneCauchyWitnessCarrier\_tail\_envelope\_public\_export}
\begin{proof}
Start with the scheduled windows. By \autoref{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-window-coverage}, the source, regular-sequence, Cauchy witness, dyadic ledger, and trap-facing reads all pass through $S,F,\sigma,\mu,\lambda,I$ on the same displayed window schedule.

Next use the finite-read route. By \autoref{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-finite-observation-lock}, Real-facing finite observations are locked to the displayed rows of $W$, so the public pre-seal route cannot replace $S,F,\sigma,\mu,\lambda,I$ by an unrecorded completion object.

The exactness and handoff theorems identify the public boundary. Apply \autoref{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-trap-seal-exactness} to keep the located trap exact for the same envelope $E$, and apply \autoref{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-seal-handoff} to locate the later $\RealUp$ seal after the displayed pre-seal service.

Finally, \autoref{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-window-exhaustion} rules out any second tail service. Since the accepted packet exposes no coordinate beyond the listed rows, the envelope exports exactly the public service $S,F,\sigma,\mu,\lambda,I,H,C,P,N$ before $Q$ is read.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Bounded monotone Cauchy witness completion consumer non-escape]
\label{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-completion-consumer-nonescape}
Let $W=(S,F,\sigma,\mu,\lambda,I,Q,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\BoundedMonotoneCauchyWitnessUp$ carrier, and let $E=(S,F,\sigma,\mu,\lambda,I,H,C,P,N)$ be its scheduled tail envelope. Every completion-facing consumer of the bounded monotone Cauchy witness factors through the public envelope $E$, the $\RealObservationBudgetUp$ finite-read route, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff, the $\LocatedIntervalUp$ trap, and the $\RealUp$ seal.

Consequently the consumer has no escape route to a selected limit, quotient real equality, host real equality, trichotomy, ambient completeness, open-cover compactness, or choice-like tail selector. Its entire public dependency surface is the finite package $S,F,\sigma,\mu,\lambda,I,Q,H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.BoundedMonotoneCauchyWitnessUp.BoundedMonotoneCauchyWitnessCarrier\_completion\_consumer\_nonescape}
\begin{proof}
First build the envelope route. The public export theorem above identifies $E$ as the only pre-seal service, and \autoref{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-window-coverage} keeps every inspected source and tail window on that service.

Next attach finite observation. By \autoref{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-finite-observation-lock}, every Real-facing finite read is routed through the displayed finite rows of $W$. The regular rational handoff is the $F$ coordinate of that same packet, and the window ledger is the shared $\sigma,\mu,\lambda$ route.

The trap and seal then close the route. Apply \autoref{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-trap-seal-exactness} to keep $I$ exact for the scheduled envelope, and apply \autoref{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-seal-handoff} to pass from the trap side to the $\RealUp$ seal row $Q$ without introducing a second completion-facing input.

It remains to exclude escapes. \autoref{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-root-unblock} gives the root consumer exactly the finite package $S,F,\sigma,\mu,\lambda,I,Q,H,C,P,N$, while \autoref{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-window-exhaustion} rules out a hidden tail service. Therefore any completion-facing consumer factors through the displayed envelope, finite-read route, regular-sequence handoff, located trap, and seal, and no selected limit, equality principle, completeness principle, compactness row, or choice-like selector is exposed.
\end{proof}

Consequently the route cannot be read as a selected limit, quotient real equality, host real equality, trichotomy, ambient completeness, open-cover compactness, or choice-like selector. The only public pre-seal completion data are the scheduled envelope and the finite apartness-budget rows already displayed by $W$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the public export theorem. \autoref{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-tail-envelope-public-export} identifies $E$ as the exported pre-seal service and lists $\sigma,\mu,\lambda,I$ among the rows read before the seal.

Next use the finite-observation lock. By \autoref{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-finite-observation-lock}, every Real-facing finite read remains on the displayed rows of $W$, so no consumer can replace the apartness and budget schedule by an unrecorded completion object.

The trap and seal then fix the order of consumption. Apply \autoref{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-trap-seal-exactness} to keep $I$ on the same located trap surface, and apply \autoref{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-seal-handoff} to place the $\RealUp$ seal row $Q$ after that finite surface.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-window-exhaustion} and \autoref{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-completion-consumer-nonescape}. The first rules out a second tail service, and the second rules out escape through limit selection, equality principles, completeness, compactness, or choice-like selection. \begin{theorem}[Bounded monotone Cauchy witness envelope uniqueness]
\label{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-envelope-uniqueness}
Let $W=(S,F,\sigma,\mu,\lambda,I,Q,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\BoundedMonotoneCauchyWitnessUp$ packet. If two completion-facing consumer routes for $W$ are admitted before the $\RealUp$ seal row is read, then both routes consume the same scheduled tail envelope rows
$$
S,F,\sigma,\mu,\lambda,I,H,C,P,N
$$
and only then read the seal row $Q$.

Any claimed difference between the two pre-seal envelopes is not a second completion route for $W$. It is routed to the refusal boundary for selected limits, quotient real equality, host equality, trichotomy, ambient completeness, open-cover compactness, or choice-like selectors.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First identify the exported envelope. By \autoref{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-tail-envelope-public-export}, the only public pre-seal service is the displayed tuple $S,F,\sigma,\mu,\lambda,I,H,C,P,N$.

Next apply completion non-escape. \autoref{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-completion-consumer-nonescape} forces every completion-facing consumer of $W$ through that public envelope, the finite-read route, the regular handoff, the located trap, and the seal row.

The apartness and budget rows are fixed before the seal by \autoref{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-completion-consumer-apartness-budget}. Thus two admitted routes cannot differ on $\sigma,\mu,\lambda,I$ while remaining completion-facing routes for the same accepted packet.

It remains to rule out a second finite envelope with the same visible role. The finite-observation lock \autoref{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-finite-observation-lock} keeps Real-facing finite reads on the displayed rows of $W$, and \autoref{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-window-exhaustion} rules out a hidden tail service. Therefore any disagreement is outside the finite envelope and is handled by the existing refusal boundary for the listed non-envelope principles.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Bounded monotone Cauchy witness completion observation route]
\label{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-completion-observation-route}
Let $W=(S,F,\sigma,\mu,\lambda,I,Q,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\BoundedMonotoneCauchyWitnessUp$ packet. Every completion-facing observation route for $W$ first consumes the rows
$$
S,F,\sigma,\mu,\lambda,I,H,C,P,N,
$$
then passes through the finite $\RealObservationBudgetUp$ route, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff, the $\LocatedIntervalUp$ trap, and only then reads the $\RealUp$ seal row $Q$.

The route exports no selected limit, quotient real equality, host equality, trichotomy, ambient completeness, open-cover compactness, or choice-like selector. Thus completion consumers read the finite observation surface before any Real-facing seal is consumed.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First expose the scheduled envelope. By \autoref{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-tail-envelope-public-export}, the public pre-seal rows of $W$ are exactly $S,F,\sigma,\mu,\lambda,I,H,C,P,N$.

Next route the completion consumer through the finite observation surface. Apply \autoref{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-completion-consumer-nonescape} to force the read through the public envelope, the finite observation route, the regular rational handoff, the located trap, and the later seal row.

The apartness and budget rows fix the order of the finite segment. By \autoref{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-completion-consumer-apartness-budget}, $\sigma,\mu,\lambda,I$ are consumed before the $\RealUp$ seal row $Q$, so the seal cannot precede the finite observation route.

Finally use envelope uniqueness. \autoref{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-envelope-uniqueness} rules out a second pre-seal envelope, and the non-escape conclusion excludes selected limits, equality principles, trichotomy, completeness, compactness, and choice-like selectors from the exported route.
\end{proof}
