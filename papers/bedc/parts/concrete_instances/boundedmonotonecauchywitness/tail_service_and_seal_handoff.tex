\begin{definition}[Bounded monotone Cauchy witness scheduled tail envelope]
\label{def:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-scheduled-tail-envelope}
Let
$$
W=(S,F,\sigma,\mu,\lambda,I,Q,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\BoundedMonotoneCauchyWitnessUp$ carrier. The scheduled tail envelope of $W$ is the finite $\BHist$ subpacket
$$
E=(S,F,\sigma,\mu,\lambda,I,H,C,P,N)
$$
read before the $\RealUp$ seal row $Q$.

Its rows are ordered as follows: $S$ supplies the bounded monotone source generator and its range, monotonicity, and upper-bound rows; $F$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff read from that source; $\sigma$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ family of inspected source and regular-sequence windows; $\mu$ is the $\CauchyModulusUp$ answer to each dyadic tolerance request; $\lambda$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance and upper-bound budget; $I$ is the $\LocatedIntervalUp$ trap reached from the same budget; $H$ records the componentwise $\hsame$ transports; $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes; $P$ records $\Pkg$ provenance over the sibling concrete-instance rows; and $N$ records the local $\NameCert_{\BoundedMonotoneCauchyWitnessUp}$ naming surface.

Thus $E$ is the public tail-serving part of $W$: every row needed to answer a tolerance request is displayed before $I$ or $Q$ is consumed, and $Q$ is the seal row read after this envelope rather than a coordinate inside it.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Bounded monotone Cauchy witness window coverage]
\label{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-window-coverage}
Let $W=(S,F,\sigma,\mu,\lambda,I,Q,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\BoundedMonotoneCauchyWitnessUp$ carrier, and let $E=(S,F,\sigma,\mu,\lambda,I,H,C,P,N)$ be its scheduled tail envelope. Every tolerance request made to $W$ factors through $E$ and, more specifically, through the ordered service $S,F,\sigma,\mu,\lambda$ before the located trap $I$ or the $\RealUp$ seal $Q$ can be read.

Consequently the request is answered by the source generator, regular rational handoff, scheduled windows, Cauchy modulus answer, and dyadic budget rows of the envelope. It cannot be answered by quotient equality, host real equality, a selected limit, trichotomy, ambient completeness, open-cover compactness, or a choice-selected tail.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.BoundedMonotoneCauchyWitnessUp.BoundedMonotoneCauchyWitnessCarrier\_window\_coverage}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier using \autoref{def:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-carrier}. The rows $S,F,\sigma,\mu,\lambda,I,Q,H,C,P,N$ are the whole accepted packet, and the scheduled envelope removes only the seal row $Q$ from the pre-seal tail service.

Use \autoref{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-tail-window-service}. It sends each tolerance request through $S,F,\sigma,\mu,\lambda$ before any located-trap or completion-facing row is available.

Next apply \autoref{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-cauchy-modulus-extraction-factorization}. The modulus answer is read through the same $\sigma,\mu,\lambda$ schedule, so the request has no alternate tail threshold outside the envelope.

Finally invoke \autoref{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-ledger-exhaustion}. Since the carrier has no further public read coordinate, a consumer cannot reach $I$ or $Q$ without first passing through the displayed source, handoff, window, modulus, and budget rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Bounded monotone Cauchy witness trap-seal exactness]
\label{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-trap-seal-exactness}
Let $W=(S,F,\sigma,\mu,\lambda,I,Q,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\BoundedMonotoneCauchyWitnessUp$ carrier, and let $E=(S,F,\sigma,\mu,\lambda,I,H,C,P,N)$ be its scheduled tail envelope. Any read of the located trap $I$ or the $\RealUp$ seal $Q$ factors through $E$ before the read is exported.

The exported trap or seal is therefore exact for the displayed bounded-source, regular-handoff, scheduled-window, modulus-answer, dyadic-budget, transport, continuation, provenance, and naming rows. It cannot import quotient equality, host real equality, selected limits, trichotomy, ambient completeness, open-cover compactness, or choice-selected tails.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.BoundedMonotoneCauchyWitnessUp.BoundedMonotoneCauchyWitnessCarrier\_trap\_seal\_exactness}
\begin{proof}
Start with the located trap route of \autoref{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-located-trap-factorization}. It places every read of $I$ after $S,F,\sigma,\mu,\lambda$ and keeps the transport, continuation, provenance, and naming rows visible.

Then use \autoref{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-real-seal-schedule-exhaustion}. The seal row $Q$ is available only through the same schedule, dyadic ledger, Cauchy witness, and located trap route.

Combine this with \autoref{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-seal-factorization-triad}. The seal factors through the public monotone-tail, Cauchy-criterion, and finite-observation routes, all of which still read the displayed packet rows rather than an external completion object.

Ledger exhaustion supplies the final exclusion. After $E$ has been read, the only remaining row is the seal row $Q$, so there is no carrier coordinate for quotient equality, host equality, selected limits, trichotomy, completeness, compactness, or choice-selected tails to enter the trap or the seal.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Bounded monotone Cauchy witness scheduled tail downstream coverage]
\label{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-scheduled-tail-downstream-coverage}
Let $W=(S,F,\sigma,\mu,\lambda,I,Q,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\BoundedMonotoneCauchyWitnessUp$ carrier, and let $E=(S,F,\sigma,\mu,\lambda,I,H,C,P,N)$ be its scheduled tail envelope. Every accepted local consumer of the scheduled tail is covered by the displayed finite packet
$$
S,F,\sigma,\mu,\lambda,I,Q,H,C,P,N.
$$
More precisely, a tolerance consumer is exhausted by the source, regular-rational handoff, scheduled-window, modulus, and dyadic-ledger rows $S,F,\sigma,\mu,\lambda$; a finite-observation consumer is exhausted by those rows together with the located trap and seal rows $I,Q$; a trap or seal consumer is exhausted by $E$ followed by $Q$; and any transport, continuation, provenance, or naming consumer is exhausted by $H,C,P,N$.

Thus the scheduled tail has no accepted downstream coordinate for quotient equality, host real equality, selected limits, trichotomy, ambient completeness, open-cover compactness, hidden tail thresholds, or choice-selected tails. Those alternatives remain outside the accepted $\BoundedMonotoneCauchyWitnessUp$ packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the scheduled envelope from \autoref{def:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-scheduled-tail-envelope}. The envelope displays exactly the pre-seal rows $S,F,\sigma,\mu,\lambda,I,H,C,P,N$, and the only post-envelope row of the accepted carrier is the $\RealUp$ seal row $Q$.

First consider a tolerance consumer. By \autoref{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-window-coverage}, every tolerance request factors through $E$ and specifically through $S,F,\sigma,\mu,\lambda$ before either $I$ or $Q$ can be read. Hence the tolerance side is already exhausted by the finite service rows of the envelope, with no hidden tail selector or completion input.

Next consider a finite-observation, trap, or seal consumer. The finite-observation route of \autoref{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-finite-observation-lock} reads the same source, window, modulus, dyadic-budget, located-trap, and seal rows. For direct trap and seal reads, \autoref{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-trap-seal-exactness} says that every read of $I$ or $Q$ factors through $E$ before export and is exact for the displayed source, handoff, schedule, modulus, budget, transport, continuation, provenance, and naming rows.

The remaining local consumers in this file are the root, window-exhaustion, and seal-handoff consumers. By \autoref{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-root-unblock}, the root consumer receives the finite package $S,F,\sigma,\mu,\lambda,I,Q,H,C,P,N$. By \autoref{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-window-exhaustion}, the scheduled windows and dyadic ledger exhaust the public tail service. By \autoref{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-seal-handoff}, the public handoff is the same finite-row package, first serving the tail windows, then extracting the modulus, then reading the located trap, and finally exporting $Q$.

Combining these cases, every accepted local consumer reads one of the enumerated finite service, ledger, coverage, trap, seal, transport, continuation, provenance, or naming rows. Since the carrier has no coordinate beyond those rows, the excluded quotient-equality, host-equality, selected-limit, trichotomy, completeness, compactness, hidden-threshold, and choice-selected-tail alternatives cannot be accepted downstream reads of the packet.
\end{proof}
