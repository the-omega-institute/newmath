\begin{theorem}[Bounded monotone Cauchy witness convergence-seal input package]
\label{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-convergence-seal-input-package}
Let $W=(S,F,\sigma,\mu,\lambda,I,Q,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\BoundedMonotoneCauchyWitnessUp$ carrier. Every downstream convergence-seal input exported by $W$ factors through the displayed rows
$$
S,F,\sigma,\mu,\lambda,I,Q,H,C,P,N
$$
before it is read by the $\BoundedMonotoneConvergenceSealUp$ consumer of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bounded-monotone-convergence-seal-namecert}.

The factorization presents the bounded source through the public $\MonotoneCauchyUp$ row, the explicit witness through the public $\CauchyCriterionUp$ row, the window schedule through $\StreamNameUp$, the dyadic tolerance and upper-bound ledger through $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, the regular rational handoff through $\RegSeqRatUp$, the compatibility boundary through $\CauchyLimitSealUp$, and the sealed real-facing row through $\RealUp$. It exports no selected limit, quotient real equality, host real equality, trichotomy, ambient completeness, open-cover compactness, or choice-like tail selector.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the local dependency package. By \autoref{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-scoped-dependency-package}, every public read of $W$ is already confined to the displayed bounded-source, regular-rational, stream, Cauchy-witness, dyadic, located-trap, Real-seal, transport, continuation, provenance, and naming rows.

Next pass through the public tail envelope. \autoref{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-tail-envelope-public-export} identifies $S,F,\sigma,\mu,\lambda,I,H,C,P,N$ as the pre-seal service and places $Q$ after that service as the $\RealUp$ seal row. The Real-completion export of \autoref{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-real-completion-export} keeps the same rows visible to Real-facing consumers.

Finally compare with the downstream seal chapter. Its carrier reads a bounded witness, public monotone tail-window row, public Cauchy criterion row, regular-rational handoff, stream schedule, dyadic ledger, limit-seal compatibility boundary, and Real seal. The local package above supplies exactly those inputs from $W$, so the convergence-seal consumer has no route around the accepted witness packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Bounded monotone Cauchy witness seal-input transport]
\label{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-seal-input-transport}
Let $W=(S,F,\sigma,\mu,\lambda,I,Q,H,C,P,N)$ and $W'=(S',F',\sigma',\mu',\lambda',I',Q',H',C',P',N')$ be accepted $\BoundedMonotoneCauchyWitnessUp$ carriers. If the component rows are related by the displayed $\hsame$ transports in $H,H'$ and the continuation routes in $C,C'$ preserve the same $\Cont$ reads, then the convergence-seal input package of \autoref{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-convergence-seal-input-package} is preserved componentwise.

Thus transport may replace a row only by its corresponding transported row $S',F',\sigma',\mu',\lambda',I',Q',H',C',P',N'$. It cannot create a detached convergence-seal input, a second compatibility boundary, a selected limiting representative, quotient real equality, host real equality, trichotomy, ambient completeness, open-cover compactness, or a choice-like tail selector.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from classifier transport. The local transport theorem for $W$ moves only the displayed carrier coordinates and keeps the source, witness, trap, Real-seal, continuation, provenance, and naming sockets componentwise aligned.

Apply the convergence-seal input package to both carriers. For $W$, every exported seal input is read through $S,F,\sigma,\mu,\lambda,I,Q,H,C,P,N$; for $W'$, every exported seal input is read through $S',F',\sigma',\mu',\lambda',I',Q',H',C',P',N'$.

Use the assumed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ preservation to compare matching coordinates. Since the package has no coordinate outside the displayed rows, componentwise transport preserves the whole package and has no source from which an extra seal input or excluded completion principle could be produced.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Bounded monotone Cauchy witness completion-route exhaustion]
\label{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-completion-route-exhaustion}
For every accepted $\BoundedMonotoneCauchyWitnessUp$ carrier $W=(S,F,\sigma,\mu,\lambda,I,Q,H,C,P,N)$, any completion-facing route that can be consumed by $\BoundedMonotoneConvergenceSealUp$ is exhausted by the witness package of \autoref{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-convergence-seal-input-package}. In particular, the route cannot bypass $W$ to selected limits, quotient real equality, host equality, trichotomy, ambient completeness, open-cover compactness, or choice-like tail selectors.

The only available route to the public convergence handoff is the finite chain from the bounded source and witness rows through the public $\MonotoneCauchyUp$, $\CauchyCriterionUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\CauchyLimitSealUp$, and $\RealUp$ rows recorded by the downstream seal chapter.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First use completion-consumer non-escape. \autoref{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-completion-consumer-nonescape} says that every completion-facing consumer factors through the public envelope, finite-read route, regular-sequence handoff, located trap, and $\RealUp$ seal of the accepted witness packet.

Then apply the convergence-seal input package. Finally use the Real-completion export and public tail-envelope export. Together they leave no public row outside the accepted witness package. A route asking for a selected limit, quotient real equality, host equality, trichotomy, ambient completeness, open-cover compactness, or choice-like tail selector therefore asks for data absent from the completion-facing surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Bounded monotone Cauchy witness finite seal-route determinacy]
\label{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-finite-seal-route-determinacy}
Let $W=(S,F,\sigma,\mu,\lambda,I,Q,H,C,P,N)$ and $W'=(S',F',\sigma',\mu',\lambda',I',Q',H',C',P',N')$ be accepted $\BoundedMonotoneCauchyWitnessUp$ carriers. Suppose the displayed source, witness, monotonicity, modulus, localization, interval, seal, transport, continuation, provenance, and naming rows agree componentwise through the $\hsame$ data in $H,H'$, and suppose the displayed $\Cont$ routes in $C,C'$ preserve the same reads of those rows. Then every $\BoundedMonotoneConvergenceSealUp$ consumer obtains the same finite seal-read surface from $W$ and $W'$.

The determined surface is exactly the finite route through $\MonotoneCauchyUp$, $\CauchyCriterionUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\CauchyLimitSealUp$, and $\RealUp$ recorded by the displayed rows. It does not compare selected limits, quotient real equality, host equality, trichotomy, ambient completeness, open-cover compactness, or choice-like tail selectors.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply \autoref{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-convergence-seal-input-package} to both carriers. It confines every downstream convergence-seal read to the displayed rows of the accepted packet and identifies the public route through the monotone, Cauchy, stream, dyadic, regular-rational, limit-seal, and Real rows.

Next use \autoref{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-seal-input-transport}. The assumed componentwise $\hsame$ data and the matching displayed $\Cont$ routes transport each row of $W$ to the corresponding row of $W'$ without adding a detached convergence-seal input or a second compatibility boundary.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-completion-route-exhaustion}. Since every completion-facing route consumable by $\BoundedMonotoneConvergenceSealUp$ is exhausted by the same finite chain, there is no remaining coordinate on which the two accepted packets can differ at the seal-read surface.

The excluded objects are outside the route just used. A selected limit, quotient real equality, host equality, trichotomy, ambient completeness, open-cover compactness, or choice-like tail selector would require data absent from both displayed packets, so none can distinguish the two downstream reads.
\end{proof}
