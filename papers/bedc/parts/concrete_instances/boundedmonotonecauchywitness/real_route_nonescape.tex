\begin{theorem}[Bounded monotone Cauchy witness Real route non-escape]
\label{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-real-route-nonescape}
Let $W=(S,F,\sigma,\mu,\lambda,I,Q,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\BoundedMonotoneCauchyWitnessUp$ carrier. Any Real-and-completion consumer of $W$ factors through the finite package
$$
S,F,\sigma,\mu,\lambda,I,Q,H,C,P,N.
$$
The consumer must read the bounded monotone source $S$, the regular rational handoff $F$, the scheduled windows $\sigma$, the Cauchy modulus answer $\mu$, the dyadic and upper-bound budget $\lambda$, the located trap $I$, the $\RealUp$ seal $Q$, and the transport, continuation, provenance, and naming rows $H,C,P,N$ before it can use the packet.

Consequently the Real-and-completion route has no escape to a selected limit, quotient real equality, host real equality, trichotomy, ambient completeness, open-cover compactness, or choice-like tail selector.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier. By \autoref{def:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-carrier}, the accepted packet exposes exactly the displayed rows $S,F,\sigma,\mu,\lambda,I,Q,H,C,P,N$ to later consumers.

The tail service is exhausted before the Real seal is reached. Apply \autoref{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-window-coverage} and \autoref{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-window-exhaustion}; every tolerance and finite-window request passes through $S,F,\sigma,\mu,\lambda$ before the trap or seal can be read.

The trap and seal remain exact for the same displayed envelope. Use \autoref{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-trap-seal-exactness} to keep reads of $I$ and $Q$ attached to the scheduled tail envelope, and then use \autoref{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-seal-handoff} to identify the exported Real-and-completion handoff as the single package $S,F,\sigma,\mu,\lambda,I,Q,H,C,P,N$.

Finally invoke \autoref{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-ledger-exhaustion}. Since the ledger has no remaining public coordinate after those rows are read, a consumer cannot project a selected limit, quotient real equality, host real equality, trichotomy, ambient completeness, open-cover compactness, or choice-like tail selector from the accepted packet.
\end{proof}
