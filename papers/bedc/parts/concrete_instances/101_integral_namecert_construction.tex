\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\IntegralUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-integral-namecert}

The integral interface $\IntegralUp$ packages a measure-respecting integration operation on suitable function carriers over $\NameCert_{\MeasureUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-measure-namecert}). It generalises the contour integral $\NameCert_{\ContourUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-contour-integral}) to Riemann/Lebesgue settings on real and abstract carriers.

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:integral-carrier}

\begin{theorem}[Integral carrier obligation]\label{thm:integral-carrier-obligation}
An $\IntegralUp$ certificate fixes its carrier as $\mathsf{BHist}$ rows over four visible sources: the cited $\NameCert_{\MeasureUp}$ source, the $\NameCert_{\ContourUp}$ comparison source, the function-carrier row for admissible integrands, and the integration-operation endpoint. The carrier row exposes only those named histories and their packaged probes, so hidden partition, simple-function, and convergence choices are ledger data rather than carrier equality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the $\NameCert_{\MeasureUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\ContourUp}$ sources named by the chapter introduction. Their certified histories provide the measure row and the contour comparison row, while $\mathsf{BHist}$ supplies the common history spine on which the function-carrier and integration-operation endpoints are read.

The per-object template then places each observable endpoint in a $\Pkg$ row and compares histories only through $\hsame$. This fixes the carrier boundary before any analytic completion claim is made.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Integral classifier obligation]\label{thm:integral-classifier-obligation}
Two $\IntegralUp$ endpoints are classified together only when their source measure rows, function-carrier rows, and integral endpoints are related by the visible $\MeasureUp$ and $\RealUp$ classifiers. The classifier is therefore the displayed BEDC comparison of measure source, integrand source, and value endpoint, not a host quotient on functions or measures.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Separate the comparison into the three visible rows named in \autoref{thm:integral-carrier-obligation}. Source equality is read from the $\MeasureUp$ classifier, function equality is read from the admissible integrand carrier over that source, and value equality is read from the $\RealUp$ endpoint classifier.

The packaging step records these comparisons as internal $\Pkg$ evidence. Since every row is obtained from the displayed certificates, no external quotient relation can identify two integral endpoints.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Integral measure-respecting stability obligation]\label{thm:integral-operation-stability-obligation}
The $\IntegralUp$ integration operation is stable under classifier transport of both the measure source and the integrand endpoint. Under this transport it preserves the ledger rows for hidden partition data, simple-function approximants, and convergence witnesses attached to the certified operation.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the $\MeasureUp$ stability row and transport the source history through the visible classifier. The $\ContourUp$ comparison row supplies the endpoint-compatible comparison source for contour-shaped instances, while $\Cont$ records that the operation continues to read the transported rows.

It remains to carry the integrand endpoint and the hidden operation data. Use $\hsame$ for the integrand history comparison and NameCert carrier transport for the packaged endpoint; the attached partition, simple-function, and convergence witnesses remain in the ledger rows rather than becoming unrecorded equalities.
\end{proof}
