\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\IntegralUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-integral-namecert}

The integral interface $\IntegralUp$ packages a measure-respecting integration operation on suitable function carriers over $\NameCert_{\MeasureUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-measure-namecert}). It generalises the contour integral $\NameCert_{\ContourUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-contour-integral}) to Riemann/Lebesgue settings on real and abstract carriers.

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:integral-carrier}

\begin{theorem}[Integral function-carrier obligation]\label{thm:integral-function-carrier-obligation}
An $\IntegralUp$ carried endpoint is a $\mathsf{BHist}$ row whose visible source data are the cited $\NameCert_{\MeasureUp}$ row, the function-carrier row of admissible integrands over that measure source, and the $\NameCert_{\ContourUp}$ comparison boundary. The endpoint carrier records only those named histories, their $\Pkg$ provenance, and their probe bundles; hidden partition, approximation, and convergence choices are not carrier equality.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.IntegralUp.IntegralVisibleCarrierRow\_readback}
\leanvariant{BEDC.Derived.IntegralUp.IntegralFunctionCarrier\_obligation}
\begin{proof}
Use the carrier field of the roadmap ten-field template. The traditional target is integration, the BEDC source row is the displayed $\NameCert_{\MeasureUp}$ history, and the comparison source is the displayed $\NameCert_{\ContourUp}$ boundary.

The carrier field is then the function-carrier row over that measure source, together with the integral endpoint history. Repacking these rows through $\mathsf{BHist}$ and $\Pkg$ fixes the visible endpoint before classifier, operation, stability, ledger, exactness, and dependency rows are considered.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Integral measure-respecting classifier obligation]\label{thm:integral-measure-respecting-classifier-obligation}
The $\IntegralUp$ classifier relates carried integrands exactly when the $\MeasureUp$-visible domain row and the $\ContourUp$ comparison row agree under the same $\hsame$ and $\Pkg$ provenance. The comparison is therefore a BEDC classifier on displayed histories and packages, not a host quotient of integrands and not host function equality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply the classifier field of the roadmap ten-field template to \autoref{thm:integral-function-carrier-obligation}. The source classifier is the visible $\MeasureUp$ comparison on domain rows, while the boundary classifier is the visible $\ContourUp$ comparison on the contour-shaped row.

The integrand rows are compared only after those two source comparisons share the same $\hsame$ provenance and the same $\Pkg$ packaging discipline. This supplies the classifier, stability source, and exactness input fields without adding a host-level equivalence relation.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Integral operation stability and ledger obligation]\label{thm:integral-operation-stability-ledger-obligation}
The $\IntegralUp$ integration operation is admitted only with a measure-respecting stability row, ledger entries for hidden partition and approximation data, exactness rows for the displayed carrier and classifier, and dependency rows citing the $\MeasureUp$ source and the $\ContourUp$ comparison boundary. No operation row is admitted without these stability, ledger, exactness, and dependency entries.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the operation and stability fields of the roadmap ten-field template after the carrier and classifier fields have been fixed. Measure-respecting stability says that transporting the $\MeasureUp$ row and the admissible integrand row along the displayed classifier transports the operation endpoint as a BEDC history.

The ledger field records the hidden partition choices, approximation rows, and convergence witnesses used by the operation. The exactness field then states coverage, soundness, and completeness for the displayed carrier and classifier, while the dependency field cites exactly the $\MeasureUp$ and $\ContourUp$ source rows consumed by the operation.
\end{proof}
