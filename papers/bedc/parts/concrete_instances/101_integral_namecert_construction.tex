\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\IntegralUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-integral-namecert}

The integral interface $\IntegralUp$ packages a measure-respecting integration operation on suitable function carriers over $\NameCert_{\MeasureUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-measure-namecert}). It generalises the contour integral $\NameCert_{\ContourUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-contour-integral}) to Riemann/Lebesgue settings on real and abstract carriers.

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:integral-carrier}

\begin{theorem}[Integral function-carrier obligation]\label{thm:integral-function-carrier-obligation}
An $\IntegralUp$ carried endpoint is a $\mathsf{BHist}$ row whose visible source data are the cited $\NameCert_{\MeasureUp}$ row, the function-carrier row of admissible integrands over that measure source, and the $\NameCert_{\ContourUp}$ comparison boundary. The endpoint carrier records only those named histories, their $\Pkg$ provenance, and their probe bundles; hidden partition, approximation, and convergence choices are not carrier equality.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.IntegralUp.IntegralVisibleCarrierRow\_readback}
\leanvariant{BEDC.Derived.IntegralUp.IntegralFunctionCarrier\_obligation}
\begin{proof}
Use the carrier field of the roadmap ten-field template. The traditional target is integration, the BEDC source row is the displayed $\NameCert_{\MeasureUp}$ history, and the comparison source is the displayed $\NameCert_{\ContourUp}$ boundary.

The carrier field is then the function-carrier row over that measure source, together with the integral endpoint history. Repacking these rows through $\mathsf{BHist}$ and $\Pkg$ fixes the visible endpoint before classifier, operation, stability, ledger, exactness, and dependency rows are considered.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Integral measure-respecting classifier obligation]\label{thm:integral-measure-respecting-classifier-obligation}
The $\IntegralUp$ classifier relates carried integrands exactly when the $\MeasureUp$-visible domain row and the $\ContourUp$ comparison row agree under the same $\hsame$ and $\Pkg$ provenance. The comparison is therefore a BEDC classifier on displayed histories and packages, not a host quotient of integrands and not host function equality.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.IntegralUp.IntegralMeasureRespectingClassifier\_obligation}
\leanvariant{BEDC.Derived.IntegralUp.IntegralVisibleCarrierRow\_classifier\_boundary\_transport}
\begin{proof}
Apply the classifier field of the roadmap ten-field template to \autoref{thm:integral-function-carrier-obligation}. The source classifier is the visible $\MeasureUp$ comparison on domain rows, while the boundary classifier is the visible $\ContourUp$ comparison on the contour-shaped row.

The integrand rows are compared only after those two source comparisons share the same $\hsame$ provenance and the same $\Pkg$ packaging discipline. This supplies the classifier, stability source, and exactness input fields without adding a host-level equivalence relation.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.IntegralUp.IntegralVisibleCarrierRow\_measure\_respecting\_classifier}

\begin{theorem}[Integral operation stability and ledger obligation]\label{thm:integral-operation-stability-ledger-obligation}
The $\IntegralUp$ integration operation is admitted only with a measure-respecting stability row, ledger entries for hidden partition and approximation data, exactness rows for the displayed carrier and classifier, and dependency rows citing the $\MeasureUp$ source and the $\ContourUp$ comparison boundary. No operation row is admitted without these stability, ledger, exactness, and dependency entries.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.IntegralUp.IntegralOperationStabilityLedger\_obligation}
\begin{proof}
Use the operation and stability fields of the roadmap ten-field template after the carrier and classifier fields have been fixed. Measure-respecting stability says that transporting the $\MeasureUp$ row and the admissible integrand row along the displayed classifier transports the operation endpoint as a BEDC history.

The ledger field records the hidden partition choices, approximation rows, and convergence witnesses used by the operation. The exactness field then states coverage, soundness, and completeness for the displayed carrier and classifier, while the dependency field cites exactly the $\MeasureUp$ and $\ContourUp$ source rows consumed by the operation.
\end{proof}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:integral-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[IntegralUp public NameCert surface]\label{thm:integral-public-namecert-surface}
The carrier row of \autoref{thm:integral-function-carrier-obligation}, the classifier row of \autoref{thm:integral-measure-respecting-classifier-obligation}, and the operation, stability, and ledger row of \autoref{thm:integral-operation-stability-ledger-obligation} assemble the exported public $\NameCert_{\IntegralUp}$ surface over the displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\Pkg$, $\hsame$, $\NameCert_{\MeasureUp}$, and $\NameCert_{\ContourUp}$ dependencies. Its public rows are exactly the visible carrier, classifier, measure-respecting stability, operation ledger, exactness, and dependency rows; partitions, approximations, convergence witnesses, standard bridges, and host function equality remain ledger data or outside this surface.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.IntegralUp.IntegralPublicNameCert\_surface}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:integral-function-carrier-obligation}. It fixes the visible $\mathsf{BHist}$ carrier, the $\Pkg$ provenance, and the named $\NameCert_{\MeasureUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\ContourUp}$ dependencies read by the public $\IntegralUp$ endpoint.

Apply \autoref{thm:integral-measure-respecting-classifier-obligation}. It supplies the classifier row over the same $\hsame$ and $\Pkg$ provenance, so the public surface compares displayed histories rather than host-level functions.

Apply \autoref{thm:integral-operation-stability-ledger-obligation}. The roadmap ten-field template then reads the operation, measure-respecting stability, ledger, exactness, and dependency rows as visible public certificate fields. The same template keeps partitions, approximations, convergence witnesses, standard bridges, and host function equality as ledger or out-of-scope data, so the three obligation rows repack as the exported public $\NameCert_{\IntegralUp}$ surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[IntegralUp conservative standard bridge]\label{thm:integral-conservative-standard-bridge}
The public $\NameCert_{\IntegralUp}$ surface of \autoref{thm:integral-public-namecert-surface} admits a conservative standard interpretation with the following six fields.
\begin{enumerate}
  \item The BEDC side is exactly the public surface of \autoref{thm:integral-public-namecert-surface}.
  \item The standard side is an ordinary integration-interface readback over the same displayed $\MeasureUp$ and $\ContourUp$ rows.
  \item Forward readback exposes only the visible carrier, classifier, measure-respecting stability, operation ledger, exactness, and dependency rows.
  \item Reverse admission accepts only packages with those displayed rows.
  \item Classifier preservation is the classifier row of \autoref{thm:integral-measure-respecting-classifier-obligation}.
  \item No host data leak through the bridge: partitions, approximations, convergence witnesses, and host function equality remain non-public ledger data by \autoref{thm:integral-operation-stability-ledger-obligation}.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.IntegralUp.IntegralUp\_conservative\_standard\_bridge}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:integral-function-carrier-obligation}. It fixes the displayed $\MeasureUp$ row, the admissible function-carrier row, the $\ContourUp$ comparison boundary, and the $\mathsf{BHist}$ endpoint read by both sides of the bridge.

Apply \autoref{thm:integral-measure-respecting-classifier-obligation}. The classifier preserved by the standard reading is therefore the displayed $\hsame$ and $\Pkg$ comparison on measure and contour histories, not host function equality.

Apply \autoref{thm:integral-operation-stability-ledger-obligation}. Its ledger boundary keeps partitions, approximations, convergence witnesses, and operation presentations as non-public data, while its stability, exactness, and dependency rows are visible certificate fields.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:integral-public-namecert-surface}. Forward readback can expose only the public rows listed there, and reverse admission can accept only packages carrying those same rows over the displayed $\MeasureUp$ and $\ContourUp$ sources. Thus the standard integration-interface reading is conservative over the public $\IntegralUp$ surface.
\end{proof}
