\begin{theorem}[PinGroup scoped reflection-parity source]
\label{thm:pingroup-scoped-reflection-parity-source}
The scoped reflection-parity source of $\PinGroupUp$ is exactly the finite
carrier surface consisting of the reflection-parity carrier of
\autoref{def:pingroup-reflection-parity-carrier}, the inherited
$\SpinGroupUp$ inclusion row, the $\CliffordUp$ reflection product row, the
displayed $\hsame$ transports, the finite $\Cont$ routes, and the $\Pkg$
provenance rows supplied by $\NameCert_{\SpinGroupUp}$ and
$\NameCert_{\CliffordUp}$.

Every accepted consumer read of the parity source factors through those rows,
and any packet with those rows repacks as the same scoped $\PinGroupUp$
reflection-parity source. No host orthogonal group, quotient Clifford unit,
matrix representation, or third source row is part of the scoped source.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with \autoref{thm:pingroup-parity-source-scope-package}. It identifies
the source rows as the inherited Spin branch, the Clifford reflection branch,
the carried $\mathsf{BHist}$ histories, and the displayed transport and
provenance rows.

Then apply \autoref{thm:pingroup-parity-carrier-inversion}. The inversion
record attaches each accepted parity-carrier row to exactly one of the two
source projections and carries the same $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\Pkg$ data.

Finally use \autoref{thm:pingroup-parity-namecert-obligation-surface}. It
repackages the listed rows as the scoped $\NameCert_{\PinGroupUp}$ obligation
surface and excludes host orthogonal, matrix, quotient, and third-source data.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PinGroup scoped Spin-Clifford boundary]
\label{thm:pingroup-scoped-spin-clifford-boundary}
The scoped Spin-Clifford boundary of $\PinGroupUp$ is the common finite
boundary between the inherited $\SpinGroupUp$ inclusion row and the
$\CliffordUp$ reflection product row. It is determined by the Spin boundary
row, the Clifford reflection product row, the reflection-parity classifier row,
the $\hsame$ endpoint transports, the finite $\Cont$ ledger, and the shared
$\Pkg$ provenance.

Two scoped boundary packets determine the same public boundary exactly when
those rows agree. The boundary exports no independent Pin carrier datum beyond
the reflection-parity carrier and no host full-orthogonal action beyond the
displayed Clifford action row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First read the inherited Spin side through
\autoref{thm:pingroup-spin-subrow-classifier-readback}. It shows that a Spin
source row contributes only the inherited classifier and the identity
reflection ledger.

Next read the Clifford side through
\autoref{thm:pingroup-reflection-ledger-scope-package}. The Clifford product
and reflection ledger are exactly the carried reflection entries, the finite
transport ledger, and the source certificate rows.

Finally combine these rows using
\autoref{thm:pingroup-parity-transport-ledger-exhaustion}. The exhaustion
statement fixes the shared parity classifier, endpoint transports, ledger
routes, and provenance, so agreement on those rows is precisely agreement of
the scoped Spin-Clifford boundary.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PinGroup scoped consumer projection exhaustion]
\label{thm:pingroup-scoped-consumer-projection-exhaustion}
Every scoped consumer projection from $\PinGroupUp$ is exhausted by the
consumer readback packet of
\autoref{def:pingroup-consumer-readback-packet}, the reflection-parity carrier,
the Spin inclusion row, the Clifford reflection product row, the Clifford
action row, the $\hsame$ transports, the finite $\Cont$ routes, and the $\Pkg$
provenance rows. Conversely, any finite packet carrying exactly those rows is
the public scoped consumer surface for later $\PinGroupUp$ consumers.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the consumer through
\autoref{thm:pingroup-consumer-boundary-scope-package}. It records precisely the
reflection ledger, Clifford action row, Spin boundary row, source projections,
transport rows, witnesses, provenance, and source certificates.

Then apply \autoref{thm:pingroup-downstream-unblock-package}. The downstream
package exports the same finite consumer rows and does not import a host
orthogonal group, quotient Clifford unit, or matrix model.

Finally use \autoref{thm:pingroup-root-downstream-threshold-surface}. It
identifies the root-facing consumer threshold packet with the same scoped
rows, so every later consumer projection is exhausted by the displayed
readback packet and every such packet repacks as the scoped public surface.
\end{proof}
