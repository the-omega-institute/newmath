\begin{definition}[Positive interpretability classifier practice carrier]

\item $E_1$ is the after-practice explanation-state row, including the classifier label after the practice change.
\item $J$ is the $\ClassifierNoveltyWitnessUp$ row exposing a common-source comparison and the before/after classifier disagreement.

\begin{theorem}[Positive interpretability classifier practice NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:positive-interpretability-classifier-practice-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\PositiveInterpretabilityClassifierPracticeUp$ carrier $P=(E_0,E_1,J,T,L,Q,D,B,H,C,R,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\PositiveInterpretabilityClassifierPracticeUp}$ surface consists exactly of before-state admission, after-state admission, classifier-novelty witness, practice-taxonomy exposure, effective replacement ledger, public cost row, debt row, benefit row, componentwise transport, displayed replay, package provenance, and local naming. A practice-level classifier shift is accepted only when $J$ displays before/after classifier disagreement and $Q,L,D,B$ display the public cost and ledger rows used by the report.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:positive-interpretability-classifier-practice-carrier}. The rows $E_0,E_1,J$ expose the before state, after state, and common-source classifier disagreement.

Next read the practice rows. The taxonomy row $T$ says which practice event is being classified, while $L$ records downstream use and $Q,D,B$ record public cost, debt, and benefit.

Finally $H,C,R,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same packet. Since no other coordinate can supply a hidden practice shift, hidden cost convention, ledger-only completion, or positive-discovery verdict, the listed rows are exactly the local obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Practice classifier separation from positive discovery]
\label{thm:positive-interpretability-classifier-practice-separation}
An accepted $\PositiveInterpretabilityClassifierPracticeUp$ packet classifies the practice event that changes the interpretability audit surface; it does not by itself classify the result as $\PositiveInterpretabilityClassifierUp$. A downstream positive-discovery read must separately supply the positive classifier's net-information rows and cannot replace them by the practice-taxonomy row $T$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use \autoref{thm:positive-interpretability-classifier-practice-namecert-obligations}. The practice packet carries $T$ as an event-kind row and records before/after classifier disagreement through $J$.

The positive-discovery packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-positive-interpretability-classifier-namecert} requires its own cost-protocol and net-information rows. The practice row $T$ is not one of those rows, and the carrier here has no coordinate that asserts positive net information.

Thus the practice classifier can record a classifier-practice shift without accepting a positive-discovery verdict. Any downstream positive claim must pass through the separate positive classifier surface.
\end{proof}

\label{thm:positive-interpretability-classifier-practice-public-cost-ledger-nonescape}

\end{proof}
