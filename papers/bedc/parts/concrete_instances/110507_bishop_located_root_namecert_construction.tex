Following Bishop and Bridges constructive located-root arguments, the $\BishopLocatedRootUp$ packet records the finite bisection route by which a located sign-changing bracket is read as a terminal $\RealUp$ root seal. It sits after the bisection convergence surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bisection-convergence-namecert}, the located intermediate-value route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-intermediatevalue-namecert}, the finite stream windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet names a constructive root certificate over displayed finite rows; it does not assert full $\RealUp$ completeness, classical trichotomy, quotient real equality, selected host roots, or countable choice.

\begin{definition}[Bishop located root carrier]
\label{def:bishop-located-root-carrier}
A $\BishopLocatedRootUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
R=(B,O,S,D,Q,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $B$ is the located bracket row naming two displayed endpoint windows and the retained interval boundary.
\item $O$ is the opposite-sign witness row, read as positive apartness data for the two endpoint evaluations.
\item $S$ is the bisection schedule row selecting the retained subintervals and midpoint tests.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger recording widths, midpoint radii, sign-test budgets, and the requested terminal precision.
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row produced from the finite bisection windows.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal reached only after $B,O,S,D,Q$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the bracket, sign witness, bisection schedule, dyadic ledger, regular readback, Real seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a root request through $B,O,S,D,Q,E,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BishopLocatedRootUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\BishopLocatedRootUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through these rows and their replay path. It has no coordinate for a host root object, real trichotomy, quotient real equality, ambient completeness, countable choice, or a detached limit point.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Bishop located root NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:bishop-located-root-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\BishopLocatedRootUp$ carrier
$$
R=(B,O,S,D,Q,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\BishopLocatedRootUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the located bracket, opposite-sign witness, bisection schedule, dyadic tolerance ledger, regular rational readback, terminal Real seal, componentwise transport, displayed continuation replay, provenance, and local naming row; bisection-route exposure; Real-seal nonescape; and refusal of host roots, trichotomy, quotient real equality, ambient completeness, countable choice, and detached limit points.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:bishop-located-root-carrier}. The accepted packet exposes exactly the bracket, sign witness, schedule, dyadic ledger, regular readback, Real seal, and structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

The bisection route is forced by the displayed order. A root-facing consumer must inspect the located bracket and opposite-sign row before it can read midpoint choices, dyadic widths, or the regular readback.

The terminal Real seal is downstream of those rows. Since the carrier has no coordinate for a host root, trichotomy, quotient equality, ambient completeness, countable choice, or a detached limit, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Bishop located root bisection route]
\label{thm:bishop-located-root-bisection-route}
Every accepted $\BishopLocatedRootUp$ root read factors through the finite route
$$
B \longmapsto O \longmapsto S \longmapsto D \longmapsto Q \longmapsto E
$$
before any downstream Real or Completion consumer can inspect the root seal. In particular, the read uses the bisection convergence route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bisection-convergence-namecert}, the finite-window surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the terminal $\RealUp$ seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted root read. The located bracket row $B$ and opposite-sign witness $O$ are the only sources for the retained interval and sign-change data.

Apply the bisection schedule row $S$ and dyadic ledger $D$. These rows record the finite midpoint choices, interval widths, and tolerance budgets consumed by the bisection convergence route.

Read $Q$ and $E$ only after the finite schedule and ledger have been displayed. The $\RegSeqRatUp$ row supplies the regular rational source, and the $\RealUp$ seal records the terminal root-facing name. The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same packet, so no host root, trichotomy, quotient equality, countable choice, or ambient completeness coordinate can enter between the bracket and the seal.
\end{proof}
