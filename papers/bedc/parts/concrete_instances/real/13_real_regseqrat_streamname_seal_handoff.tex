\begin{theorem}[RealUp RegSeqRat StreamName seal handoff]
\label{thm:realup-regseqrat-streamname-seal-handoff}
The rows of \autoref{thm:regseqrat-streamname-scoped-interface}, together
with the finite-window seal and endpoint readback rows displayed in the
$\RealUp$ stream-prefix certificate boundary, determine exactly the $\RealUp$
seal handoff package. Its exported rows are the
$\mathsf{RatStreamNameClassifier}$ row, the
$\mathsf{RealUnaryStreamClassifier}$ transport row, the certified unary
endpoint carriers, the finite-window ledger, and the seal/readback provenance.

No quotient carrier, host equality, real completeness, arithmetic package,
order package, or metric-completeness row is exported by this handoff.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First project the scoped stream-name interface. Theorem~\ref{thm:regseqrat-streamname-scoped-interface}
supplies the $\mathsf{RatStreamNameClassifier}$ row, the transported
$\mathsf{RealUnaryStreamClassifier}$ row, and the certified unary endpoint
carrier rows used by the $\RealUp$ stream-prefix certificate.

Next read the finite-window seal boundary. Theorems
\ref{thm:regseqrat-finite-window-seal-coverage} and
\ref{thm:regseqrat-finite-window-real-seal-interface} identify the finite
window, the pointwise dyadic observation rows, and the $\RegSeqRat$ spine
ledger consumed by the public $\RealUp$ seal.

Finally use the endpoint readback and hidden-data ledger rows of the
stream-prefix certificate boundary. They repack the displayed classifier,
transport, endpoint, finite-window, and provenance rows as the stated seal
handoff package, while excluding every off-table claim: quotient carriers,
host equality, completeness, arithmetic, order, and metric-completeness data.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RealUp open phase seal exactness]
\label{thm:realup-open-phase-seal-exactness}
A finite $\RealUp$ seal handoff is accepted precisely when its displayed rows
factor through one $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet containing the four open-phase
faces:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observation rows for the finite unary window;
  \item the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window classifier and transported stream
  name rows;
  \item the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regularity spine ledger over the same scheduled
  observations;
  \item the $\RealUp$ seal, endpoint readback, and provenance ledger.
\end{enumerate}
The factorization uses the displayed $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and
$\NameCert$ rows, and it exports no quotient carrier, host equality, real
completeness, arithmetic package, order package, or metric-completeness row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the forward direction, begin with an accepted finite $\RealUp$ seal
handoff. Theorem~\ref{thm:realup-regseqrat-streamname-seal-handoff} projects
the seal handoff into its stream-name classifier row, transported RealUp unary
classifier row, certified unary endpoint carriers, finite-window ledger, and
seal/readback provenance.

Now separate the four open-phase faces. Theorem~\ref{thm:regseqrat-streamname-scoped-interface}
supplies the $\StreamNameUp$ classifier, transported stream-name rows, and the
regular-sequence interface rows. Theorems
\ref{thm:regseqrat-finite-window-seal-coverage} and
\ref{thm:regseqrat-finite-window-real-seal-interface} identify the same finite
window as the place where the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observations, the
$\RegSeqRatUp$ spine ledger, and the $\RealUp$ seal/readback ledger are read.
Thus every accepted handoff factors through one displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$
packet with the stated $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and $\NameCert$
rows.

For the reverse direction, start from such a shared $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet.
The scoped stream-name interface repacks the $\StreamNameUp$ classifier and
regularity spine rows, while the finite-window seal coverage theorem attaches
the dyadic observation rows to that same window.

Finally apply
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-finite-window-real-seal-interface} and then
\autoref{thm:realup-regseqrat-streamname-seal-handoff}. The first theorem
turns the shared finite-window packet into the $\RealUp$ seal-facing package,
and the second identifies that package as the finite seal handoff. Since every
row used in both directions is one of the displayed four faces, no quotient
carrier, host equality, completeness, arithmetic, order, or
metric-completeness datum is introduced.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RealUp finite seal source uniqueness]
\label{thm:realup-finite-seal-source-uniqueness}
Two finite $\RealUp$ seal handoffs over the same $\StreamNameUp$
finite-window classifier rows, the same accepted $\RegSeqRatUp$ regularity
spine ledger, and the same displayed $\RealUp$ seal endpoint and readback
provenance expose the same finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ seal source surface when
their $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and $\NameCert$ rows agree
componentwise.

The shared source surface is the finite packet carrying the public
$\StreamNameUp$ window classifier, the pointwise $\DyadicRatCoreUp$
observation rows, the accepted $\RegSeqRatUp$ spine rows, and the displayed
$\RealUp$ seal and readback ledger. No quotient carrier, host equality,
completeness row, arithmetic package, order package, or metric-completeness
datum can distinguish the two reads.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project both handoffs through
\autoref{thm:realup-regseqrat-streamname-seal-handoff}. The projected rows are
the same $\StreamNameUp$ classifier, transported unary classifier, endpoint
carrier rows, finite-window ledger, and seal/readback provenance because the
displayed $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and $\NameCert$ rows agree
componentwise.

Apply \autoref{thm:realup-open-phase-seal-exactness} to each projected
handoff. Each accepted read factors through one finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
with the four open-phase faces: dyadic observations, stream-name classifier
rows, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regularity spine ledger, and the $\RealUp$ seal
endpoint/readback provenance.

Use \autoref{thm:regseqrat-finite-window-seal-coverage} and
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-finite-window-real-seal-interface} on the shared
finite window. These theorems identify the dyadic observation rows and the
regular-rational spine rows as the same finite seal-facing packet for both
reads.

Thus the two handoffs expose one finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ seal source surface.
Every coordinate available to the seal has already been matched
componentwise, and the off-table quotient, host-equality, completeness,
arithmetic, order, and metric-completeness data provide no further
distinguishing row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RealUp seal source factorization boundary]
\label{thm:realup-seal-source-factorization-boundary}
Any finite $\RealUp$ seal source consumed by downstream $\RealUp$ or
$\RegSeqRatUp$ consumers factors through the unique four-face
$\mathsf{BHist}$ packet consisting of the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observation rows,
the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window classifier rows, the $\RegSeqRatUp$
regularity spine rows, and the $\RealUp$ seal and readback provenance rows.
Componentwise $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and $\NameCert$ agreement on
these rows is sufficient to identify the visible seal source for every such
consumer.

The boundary exports no quotient carrier, host equality, completeness datum,
arithmetic package, order package, or metric-completeness row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply \autoref{thm:realup-open-phase-seal-exactness}. It factors an
accepted finite seal handoff through the four open-phase faces: dyadic
observations, stream-name classifier rows, the regular-rational spine, and the
seal/readback provenance.

Next use \autoref{thm:realup-finite-seal-source-uniqueness}. Componentwise
agreement of the displayed $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and $\NameCert$
rows identifies the finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ seal source surface for any two
reads with the same four faces.

Finally cite \autoref{thm:realup-regseqrat-streamname-seal-handoff}. The
handoff theorem says the finite seal-facing rows are exactly the classifier,
transport, endpoint, finite-window, and provenance rows consumed by the
$\RealUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$ boundary, so quotient carriers, host equality,
completeness, arithmetic or order packages, and metric-completeness rows remain
outside the consumer-visible source.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RealUp open-phase four-face pullback]
\label{thm:realup-open-phase-four-face-pullback}
Compatible $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observation rows, $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window
classifier rows, $\RegSeqRatUp$ regularity spine rows, and $\RealUp$
seal/readback provenance determine a unique finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ pullback
packet. The packet is the shared source whose four projections are exactly
those rows, with the displayed $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and
$\NameCert$ data as its gluing coordinates.

Conversely, every accepted finite $\RealUp$ seal source projects to exactly
those four open-phase faces. No quotient carrier, host equality, real
completeness, arithmetic package, order package, or metric-completeness row is
part of the pullback packet or any of its four projections.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with compatible displayed rows for the four faces. Apply
\autoref{thm:realup-open-phase-seal-exactness} to read them as the accepted
finite seal-facing package over one $\mathsf{BHist}$ source shape.

Next apply \autoref{thm:realup-finite-seal-source-uniqueness}. Since the four
faces agree componentwise through the displayed $\hsame$, $\Cont$,
$\mathsf{Pkg}$, and $\NameCert$ rows, any two packets with those projections
expose the same finite seal source surface. This gives the unique pullback
packet determined by the compatible rows.

For the converse, begin with an accepted finite $\RealUp$ seal source. Apply
\autoref{thm:realup-seal-source-factorization-boundary}. Its consumer-visible
factorization is precisely the dyadic observation face, the stream-name
finite-window classifier face, the regularity-spine face, and the RealUp
seal/readback provenance face.

The same boundary theorem, together with
\autoref{thm:realup-open-phase-seal-exactness}, excludes every off-table
coordinate. Thus quotient carriers, host equality, real completeness,
arithmetic and order packages, and metric-completeness rows cannot appear in
the pullback source or in its projected faces.
\end{proof}
