\begin{theorem}[Real L10 finite-cover exit threshold gluing]
\label{thm:real-l10-finite-cover-exit-threshold-gluing}
Let $\mathcal{U}$ be a finite family of accepted Phase Real and Completion
exit packets. Suppose every member of $\mathcal{U}$ satisfies the objectwise
exit threshold of
\autoref{thm:real-l10-objectwise-exit-threshold-equivalence}, and suppose
that every pairwise overlap agrees on the displayed $\DyadicRatCoreUp$
endpoint and radius ledger, the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window schedule, the
$\RegSeqRatUp$ regularity and classifier rows, the $\RealUp$ seal rows, and
the shared $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support rows.
Then the family glues to one accepted four-face Phase Real and Completion
exit packet satisfying the same objectwise exit threshold.

The glued exit packet is only the finite four-face packet over those displayed
rows. It exports no aggregate route-level $\formalstatus$, no aggregate bridge
row, no quotient stream, no selected limit, no countable-choice row, no
metric-completeness datum, and no $\RealUp$ completeness claim.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:real-l10-objectwise-exit-threshold-equivalence} to each
member of $\mathcal{U}$. Thus every local packet contributes exactly the four
face records for $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and
$\RealUp$, with the threshold read from the local closurestatus rows of those
faces.

We first glue the regular-sequence and real-seal faces. Apply
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-real-seal-finite-cover-gluing} to the finite family of
$\RegSeqRatUp$ regularity rows and $\RealUp$ seal rows. The overlap
agreement hypothesis is precisely the shared regularity, classifier, seal,
$\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ surface required for that
gluing. Apply
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-finite-cover-real-seal-gluing-uniqueness} to identify
the resulting seal row on common overlaps.

Next glue the scheduling face. Use
\autoref{thm:streamname-common-refinement-public-exactness} and
\autoref{thm:streamname-finite-window-cover-refinement-classifier} to pass to
a common finite-window presentation of the $\StreamNameUp$ schedules. Apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-finite-request-cover-gluing} to export one schedule
row for the finite family, still over the displayed finite-window data and not
over a quotient stream or selected limit.

The dyadic face is fixed by the overlap ledger. Apply
\autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-finite-tail-scale-window-pullback-exactness} and
\autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-finite-tail-endpoint-invariance}. These read the
shared endpoint and radius data through the displayed $\DyadicRatCoreUp$
ledger, so the glued packet has the same dyadic endpoint and radius rows on
all refined windows.

Combining the glued RegSeqRat/Real, StreamName, and DyadicRatCore rows gives
one accepted four-face exit packet by
\autoref{thm:real-l10-four-face-exit-sufficiency}. Finally apply
\autoref{thm:real-l10-face-status-noncollapse} and
\autoref{thm:real-l10-face-leantarget-noncollapse}. The glued packet remains
objectwise: its closure, verification, Lean-target, and bridge rows stay on
their source faces and do not form an aggregate route-level status, bridge,
quotient, selected-limit, choice, metric-completeness, or $\RealUp$
completeness datum.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}

It remains to keep the descent facewise. Apply
\autoref{thm:real-l10-face-status-noncollapse} and

subcover gluing from creating an aggregate status, bridge, quotient-stream,

completeness reading. Hence the coarse packet $P$ carries exactly the same

\begin{theorem}[Real L10 terminal finite-cover refinement invariance]

\begin{proof}
Assume first that $\mathcal U$ witnesses the terminal finite-cover threshold.
Apply \autoref{thm:real-l10-terminal-finite-cover-threshold-equivalence} to
$\mathcal U$. The cover therefore presents the terminal four-face exit packet
over the displayed dyadic, StreamName, RegSeqRat, and local Real faces with
the shared support rows.

\autoref{thm:streamname-common-refinement-terminal-subcover-exactness} to keep

Now apply \autoref{thm:real-l10-finite-cover-exit-threshold-gluing}. Each cell

Then \autoref{thm:real-l10-terminal-finite-cover-threshold-equivalence} reads
the glued refined packet as a witness for $\mathcal V$.

For the reverse direction, assume that $\mathcal V$ witnesses the threshold.
Apply \autoref{thm:real-l10-finite-cover-exit-threshold-descent} along the
certified refinement map from $\mathcal V$ to $\mathcal U$. The preservation
hypotheses make the descended packet carry exactly the displayed dyadic,

Finally apply
\autoref{thm:real-l10-terminal-finite-cover-face-minimality} and

$\mathcal U$ exactly when it certifies $\mathcal V$.

The construction never promotes route-level data into an objectwise face.
Aggregate $\formalstatus$ rows, bridge rows, quotient streams, selected
limits, choice principles, metric-completeness data, and ambient $\RealUp$
completeness remain outside the refined terminal finite-cover threshold.
\end{proof}
