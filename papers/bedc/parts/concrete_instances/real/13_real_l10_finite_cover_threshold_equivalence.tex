\begin{theorem}[Real L10 finite-cover exit threshold gluing]
\label{thm:real-l10-finite-cover-exit-threshold-gluing}
Let $\mathcal{U}$ be a finite family of accepted Phase Real and Completion
exit packets. Suppose every member of $\mathcal{U}$ satisfies the objectwise
exit threshold of
\autoref{thm:real-l10-objectwise-exit-threshold-equivalence}, and suppose
that every pairwise overlap agrees on the displayed $\DyadicRatCoreUp$
endpoint and radius ledger, the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window schedule, the
$\RegSeqRatUp$ regularity and classifier rows, the $\RealUp$ seal rows, and
the shared $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support rows.
Then the family glues to one accepted four-face Phase Real and Completion
exit packet satisfying the same objectwise exit threshold.

The glued exit packet is only the finite four-face packet over those displayed
rows. It exports no aggregate route-level $\formalstatus$, no aggregate bridge
row, no quotient stream, no selected limit, no countable-choice row, no
metric-completeness datum, and no $\RealUp$ completeness claim.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:real-l10-objectwise-exit-threshold-equivalence} to each
member of $\mathcal{U}$. Thus every local packet contributes exactly the four
face records for $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and
$\RealUp$, with the threshold read from the local closurestatus rows of those
faces.

We first glue the regular-sequence and real-seal faces. Apply
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-real-seal-finite-cover-gluing} to the finite family of
$\RegSeqRatUp$ regularity rows and $\RealUp$ seal rows. The overlap
agreement hypothesis is precisely the shared regularity, classifier, seal,
$\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ surface required for that
gluing. Apply
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-finite-cover-real-seal-gluing-uniqueness} to identify
the resulting seal row on common overlaps.

Next glue the scheduling face. Use
\autoref{thm:streamname-common-refinement-public-exactness} and
\autoref{thm:streamname-finite-window-cover-refinement-classifier} to pass to
a common finite-window presentation of the $\StreamNameUp$ schedules. Apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-finite-request-cover-gluing} to export one schedule
row for the finite family, still over the displayed finite-window data and not
over a quotient stream or selected limit.

The dyadic face is fixed by the overlap ledger. Apply
\autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-finite-tail-scale-window-pullback-exactness} and
\autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-finite-tail-endpoint-invariance}. These read the
shared endpoint and radius data through the displayed $\DyadicRatCoreUp$
ledger, so the glued packet has the same dyadic endpoint and radius rows on
all refined windows.

Combining the glued RegSeqRat/Real, StreamName, and DyadicRatCore rows gives
one accepted four-face exit packet by
\autoref{thm:real-l10-four-face-exit-sufficiency}. Finally apply
\autoref{thm:real-l10-face-status-noncollapse} and
\autoref{thm:real-l10-face-leantarget-noncollapse}. The glued packet remains
objectwise: its closure, verification, Lean-target, and bridge rows stay on
their source faces and do not form an aggregate route-level status, bridge,
quotient, selected-limit, choice, metric-completeness, or $\RealUp$
completeness datum.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real L10 finite-cover exit threshold descent]
\label{thm:real-l10-finite-cover-exit-threshold-descent}
Let $P$ be an accepted Phase Real and Completion exit packet, and let
$\mathcal{V}$ be a finite certified subcover of $P$. Suppose every member of
$\mathcal{V}$ satisfies the objectwise exit threshold of
\autoref{thm:real-l10-objectwise-exit-threshold-equivalence}. Suppose moreover
that each subcover member agrees with $P$ on the displayed $\DyadicRatCoreUp$
endpoint and radius ledger, the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window schedule, the
$\RegSeqRatUp$ regularity and classifier rows, the $\RealUp$ seal rows, and the
shared $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support rows. Then
$P$ satisfies the same objectwise exit threshold.

The descended threshold is only the coarse four-face threshold of $P$. It does
not assert an aggregate route-level $\formalstatus$, an aggregate bridge row, a
quotient stream, a selected limit, a countable-choice row, a
metric-completeness datum, or a $\RealUp$ completeness claim.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:real-l10-finite-cover-exit-threshold-gluing} to the finite
certified subcover $\mathcal{V}$. The hypotheses on the local threshold and on
the shared $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$,
$\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows give one glued
accepted four-face packet satisfying the objectwise exit threshold.

We now identify this glued packet with the coarse packet $P$. The agreement
assumptions say that every displayed endpoint, radius, finite-window,
regularity, classifier, seal, history, sameness, continuity, package, and
NameCert coordinate of the glued packet is the corresponding coordinate of
$P$. Apply \autoref{thm:real-l10-objectwise-exit-threshold-equivalence} to read
the glued threshold as the four local face records of $P$.

It remains to keep the descent facewise. Apply
\autoref{thm:real-l10-face-status-noncollapse} and
\autoref{thm:real-l10-face-leantarget-noncollapse}. These prevent the
subcover gluing from creating an aggregate status, bridge, quotient-stream,
selected-limit, countable-choice, metric-completeness, or $\RealUp$
completeness reading. Hence the coarse packet $P$ carries exactly the same
objectwise exit threshold and no route-level strengthening.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real L10 terminal pullback coherence]
\label{thm:real-l10-terminal-pullback-coherence}
Let $T$ be an accepted terminal finite-window $\RealUp$ read presented through
the public $\StreamNameUp$--$\RegSeqRatUp$ route. Suppose its StreamName
window, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, accepted $\RegSeqRatUp$ pointwise
seal and readback, $\RealUp$ seal row, and shared $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$,
$\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support agree with a four-face Phase Real and
Completion packet $P$. Then $T$ satisfies the same objectwise exit threshold as
$P$ in the sense of
\autoref{thm:real-l10-objectwise-exit-threshold-equivalence}.

Conversely, any terminal finite-window $\RealUp$ read satisfying that
objectwise threshold and carrying the displayed StreamName, DyadicRatCore,
RegSeqRat, Real seal, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows
factors through the StreamName--$\RegSeqRat$--$\RealUp$ terminal pullback over
the four-face Phase Real and Completion packet.

The coherent terminal read is only this displayed finite-window pullback. It
does not assert an aggregate route-level $\formalstatus$, an aggregate bridge
row, a quotient stream, a selected limit, a countable-choice row, a
metric-completeness datum, or a $\RealUp$ completeness claim.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First read the terminal route. Apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-realup-terminal-pullback-factorization}. The
accepted terminal $\RealUp$ read factors through exactly the displayed
StreamName window rows, accepted $\RegSeqRatUp$ pointwise seal rows,
$\RealUp$ finite-window seal boundary, $\hsame$ transports, finite $\Cont$
ledger, bundle membership, observations, and $\Pkg$ provenance. Apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-real-finite-window-route-factorization} to
include the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger in the same finite route.

Now compare the resulting route with the four-face Phase Real and Completion
packet $P$. The agreement assumptions identify the StreamName window,
DyadicRatCore tolerance, RegSeqRat seal and readback, Real seal, $\BHist$,
$\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ coordinates of the terminal read
with the corresponding objectwise faces of $P$. Apply
\autoref{thm:real-l10-objectwise-exit-threshold-equivalence} to read the exit
threshold from those four local closurestatus records.

For the converse direction, start with a terminal finite-window $\RealUp$ read
that already carries the objectwise threshold and the displayed finite route
rows. Apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-real-finite-window-route-factorization} to
recover the finite StreamName--RegSeqRat route, and then apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-realup-terminal-pullback-factorization} to
repack the same rows as the terminal pullback boundary.

It remains to keep the terminal comparison at the same threshold. Apply
\autoref{thm:real-l10-finite-cover-exit-threshold-descent} to descend any
finite-window cover presentation back to the coarse packet $P$. Finally apply
\autoref{thm:real-l10-face-status-noncollapse} and
\autoref{thm:real-l10-face-leantarget-noncollapse}. These keep formalstatus,
bridge, Lean-target, quotient-stream, selected-limit, choice,
metric-completeness, and $\RealUp$ completeness data attached only to the
source faces, so the pullback coherence gives no route-level strengthening.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real L10 terminal finite-cover threshold equivalence]
\label{thm:real-l10-terminal-finite-cover-threshold-equivalence}
Let $T$ be an accepted terminal finite-window $\RealUp$ read over the public
$\StreamNameUp$--$\RegSeqRatUp$ route, with displayed $\DyadicRatCoreUp$
endpoint and radius ledger, $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window schedule,
$\RegSeqRatUp$ regularity and classifier rows, local $\RealUp$ seal rows, and
shared $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows. Then $T$
satisfies the current Phase Real and Completion objectwise exit threshold if
and only if every finite certified cover of the displayed terminal route glues
and descends to the same four-face packet over $\DyadicRatCoreUp$,
$\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and the local $\RealUp$ seal, with the shared
$\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.

The equivalence refuses aggregate route-level $\formalstatus$, aggregate
bridge rows, quotient stream equality, selected limits, countable choice,
metric-completeness data, and ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the forward direction, apply
\autoref{thm:real-l10-terminal-pullback-coherence}. The accepted terminal read
is compared with the four-face Phase Real and Completion packet through the
displayed StreamName window, DyadicRatCore tolerance ledger, RegSeqRat
readback and classifier rows, local Real seal, and shared support rows.

Now apply \autoref{thm:real-l10-finite-cover-exit-threshold-gluing}. Each
finite certified cover member carries the objectwise threshold read from the
same local faces, and the overlap hypotheses are exactly the shared
$\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows. Apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-completion-four-object-terminal-pullback} to keep
the glued cover inside the terminal four-object packet over
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$.

For the reverse direction, suppose every finite certified cover of the
displayed terminal route glues and descends to the same four-face packet. Apply
\autoref{thm:real-l10-finite-cover-exit-threshold-descent}. The descended
coarse packet carries the same objectwise threshold as its finite cover
members.

It remains to identify this descended packet with the accepted terminal route.
Apply \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-l10-exit-namecert-interface} to read the
dyadic endpoint, radius, distance, and local NameCert interface as the
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$ face of the same support packet. Apply
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-streamname-dyadic-real-audit-clean-exit-readiness} to
read the StreamName, RegSeqRat, DyadicRatCore, and local Real rows as one
audit-clean four-face exit surface.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:real-l10-terminal-pullback-coherence}. The terminal
read and the descended four-face packet have the same displayed faces and the
same $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support rows, so the
threshold is exactly the current objectwise Phase Real and Completion
threshold. The cited noncollapse clauses in the gluing, descent, interface,
readiness, and terminal-pullback results leave no aggregate status, bridge
row, quotient stream equality, selected limit, choice principle,
metric-completeness datum, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness coordinate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real L10 terminal finite-cover face minimality]
\label{thm:real-l10-terminal-finite-cover-face-minimality}
Let $T$ be an accepted terminal finite-window $\RealUp$ read satisfying the
current Phase Real and Completion objectwise exit threshold, with displayed
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint and radius ledger, $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window
schedule, $\RegSeqRatUp$ regularity and classifier rows, local $\RealUp$ seal
rows, and shared $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
Then every finite certified cover witnessing the terminal finite-cover
threshold equivalence for $T$ is face-minimal in the following sense.
\begin{enumerate}
\item The cover must retain the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$,
$\RegSeqRatUp$, and local $\RealUp$ faces on the displayed terminal support.
\item The cover must retain the shared $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$,
and $\NameCert$ rows through the same common support.
\item Deleting any one of these faces or support rows makes the resulting
subcover inadmissible as a witness for the terminal threshold.
\item No aggregate $\formalstatus$, bridge row, quotient stream, selected
limit, countable-choice row, metric-completeness datum, or ambient $\RealUp$
completeness row can replace the deleted face or support row.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply
\autoref{thm:real-l10-terminal-finite-cover-threshold-equivalence}. The
accepted threshold for $T$ is equivalent to gluing and descent of finite
certified covers to the same four-face packet over $\DyadicRatCoreUp$,
$\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and the local $\RealUp$ seal, with the shared
$\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.

Apply \autoref{thm:real-l10-terminal-pullback-coherence}. This identifies the
accepted terminal read with that four-face packet, so a cover witnessing the
threshold is not merely a route credential: it is a cover whose visible support
contains the dyadic ledger, the StreamName finite window, the RegSeqRat
classifier and readback rows, the local Real seal, and the shared support rows.

It remains to rule out removing a displayed face. Use
\autoref{thm:streamname-common-refinement-terminal-subcover-exactness} for the
StreamName finite-window subcover, use
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-common-tail-terminal-four-face-cocycle-uniqueness} for
the RegSeqRat common-tail and cocycle rows, and use
\autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-l10-exit-namecert-interface} for the dyadic endpoint,
radius, distance, and local $\NameCert$ interface. These results make each
face visible in the common support consumed by the terminal packet.

Finally apply the noncollapse clauses from
\autoref{thm:real-l10-terminal-finite-cover-threshold-equivalence},
\autoref{thm:real-l10-face-status-noncollapse}, and
\autoref{thm:real-l10-face-leantarget-noncollapse}. They keep route-level
status, bridge, quotient-stream, selected-limit, choice,
metric-completeness, and ambient completeness data outside the objectwise face
inventory, so none of those rows can compensate for a missing
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, local $\RealUp$, or shared
support row.
\end{proof}
