\begin{theorem}[RealSeparability RegSeqRat forward anchor]
\label{thm:real-separability-regseqrat-forward-anchor}
Every $\RealSeparabilityUp$ density read that is forwarded to a Real-facing consumer first consumes the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert} and the terminal $\RealUp$ seal route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The read is therefore anchored in displayed regular-rational windows and Real seal data before the separability row is exported.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted $\RealSeparabilityUp$ carrier to its density route. The consumer can inspect rational density only after the regular readback rows have exposed the finite $\RegSeqRatUp$ window data.

The same route then passes through the $\RealUp$ seal before any density observation is exported. Thus the forward read is a child route of the RegSeqRat and Real chapters, not a detached dense subset, quotient real equality, selected dense point, countable-choice schedule, or ambient completeness principle.
\end{proof}
