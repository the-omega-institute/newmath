\begin{theorem}[Real L10 formal-boundary exit certificate]
\label{thm:real-l10-formal-boundary-exit-certificate}
An accepted current Phase Real and Completion terminal $\RealUp$ read exports
exactly the finite four-face boundary
$$
\DyadicRatCoreUp \to \StreamNameUp \to \RegSeqRatUp \to \RealUp
$$
with the displayed support rows
$$
\begin{gathered}
\BHist_{\DyadicRatCoreUp},\ \BHist_{\StreamNameUp},\
\BHist_{\RegSeqRatUp},\ \BHist_{\RealUp},\\
\hsame_{\DyadicRatCoreUp},\ \hsame_{\StreamNameUp},\
\hsame_{\RegSeqRatUp},\ \hsame_{\RealUp},\\
\Cont_{\DyadicRatCoreUp},\ \Cont_{\StreamNameUp},\
\Cont_{\RegSeqRatUp},\ \Cont_{\RealUp},\\
\Pkg_{\DyadicRatCoreUp},\ \Pkg_{\StreamNameUp},\
\Pkg_{\RegSeqRatUp},\ \Pkg_{\RealUp},\\
\NameCert_{\DyadicRatCoreUp},\ \NameCert_{\StreamNameUp},\
\NameCert_{\RegSeqRatUp},\ \NameCert_{\RealUp}.
\end{gathered}
$$
Its formal boundary is the objectwise product of the four local face records:
the DyadicRatCore endpoint and radius ledger, the StreamName terminal-window
packet, the RegSeqRat finite-request section, and the local Real seal. This
product is not a fifth route-level formalstatus, bridge row, selected limit,
quotient stream, host equality, ambient completeness row, metric-completeness
datum, arithmetic or order package, or standard bridge.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First fix the finite terminal witness. Apply
\autoref{thm:real-l10-finite-window-witness-determinacy}. The accepted
terminal read determines exactly the fused StreamName terminal-window packet,
the DyadicRatCore endpoint and radius ledger, the RegSeqRat finite-request
section, the local Real seal, and the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$,
$\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.

Next fix the route. Apply \autoref{thm:real-l10-source-route-exactness}. It
places every such witness on the ordered path
$$
\DyadicRatCoreUp \to \StreamNameUp \to \RegSeqRatUp \to \RealUp.
$$
Apply \autoref{thm:real-l10-streamname-regseqrat-completion-route-triangle} to
identify the StreamName classifier pullback, the RegSeqRat finite-request
section, and the Real source route as three readings of the same public
boundary.

Now read the audit boundary. Apply
\autoref{thm:real-l10-current-phase-exit-audit-pullback}. It identifies the
accepted Phase Real and Completion audit as the product of the four local face
records, with closurestatus, formalstatus, leantarget, and bridgestatus rows
read from the corresponding faces.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:real-l10-face-axis-independence}. It keeps paper
closure, formalstatus, leantarget, and bridgestatus face-local and blocks any
reading in which their product becomes a route-level status, aggregate bridge,
quotient stream, selected limit, host equality, ambient completeness row,
metric-completeness datum, arithmetic or order package, or standard bridge.
Thus the certificate is exactly the four-face finite boundary and its
objectwise formal record product.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real L10 four-face order rigidity]
\label{thm:real-l10-face-order-rigidity}
Let an accepted current Phase Real and Completion terminal read have precisely
the visible faces $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and
$\RealUp$. Then the read is accepted only as the ordered route
$$
\DyadicRatCoreUp \to \StreamNameUp \to \RegSeqRatUp \to \RealUp.
$$
No permutation, skipped edge, or bypass through the same four local records is
an accepted exit route. In particular, quotient streams, host real equality,
selected limits, countable choice, ambient completeness, metric-completeness
data, aggregate formalstatus tokens, and standard bridge rows cannot replace
or reorder the four-face route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First fix the source route. Apply
\autoref{thm:real-l10-source-route-exactness}. It assigns every accepted
terminal finite-window witness to the displayed path from $\DyadicRatCoreUp$
through $\StreamNameUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$ to $\RealUp$.

Next compare the middle and terminal readings. Apply
\autoref{thm:real-l10-streamname-regseqrat-completion-route-triangle}. The
StreamName terminal-window classifier, the RegSeqRat finite-request section,
and the Real source route are three readings of the same public boundary over
the same support rows. A permutation of the faces would change one of these
source, middle, or terminal readings, so it is not the accepted triangle.

Now read the audit coordinate. Apply
\autoref{thm:real-l10-current-phase-exit-audit-pullback}. The current Phase
Real and Completion exit audit is the objectwise product of the four local
closurestatus, formalstatus, leantarget, and bridgestatus records, not an
aggregate route token that can be reassigned to another order.

Finally exclude bypasses. Apply
\autoref{thm:real-l10-incomplete-face-exit-obstruction}. Deleting,
substituting, or bypassing any one face leaves no accepted L10 exit route.
Thus quotient streams, host real equality, selected limits, countable choice,
ambient completeness, metric-completeness data, aggregate formalstatus tokens,
and standard bridge rows cannot supply a reordered or shortened route. The only
accepted exit route with those four visible faces is the ordered four-face
route above.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real L10 terminal order window witness envelope]
\label{thm:real-l10-terminal-order-window-witness-envelope}
An accepted current Phase Real and Completion terminal $\RealUp$ read satisfies
the Real L10 exit envelope if and only if both of the following hold:
\begin{enumerate}
\item the read has the ordered route
$$
\DyadicRatCoreUp \to \StreamNameUp \to \RegSeqRatUp \to \RealUp;
$$
\item the finite-window witness rows are exactly the rows fixed by
\autoref{thm:real-l10-finite-window-witness-determinacy}: the fused
$\StreamNameUp$ terminal-window packet, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint and
radius ledger, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ finite-request section, the local $\RealUp$
seal, and the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$
rows.
\end{enumerate}
The envelope contains no quotient streams, selected limits,
countable-choice rows, metric-completeness data, aggregate formalstatus rows,
aggregate bridge rows, or $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the forward direction, first read the route. Apply
\autoref{thm:real-l10-face-order-rigidity}. It forces any accepted terminal read
with the four visible faces to use the ordered path
$$
\DyadicRatCoreUp \to \StreamNameUp \to \RegSeqRatUp \to \RealUp.
$$

Next read the window data. Apply
\autoref{thm:real-l10-finite-window-witness-determinacy}. The public boundary
recovers exactly the fused StreamName terminal-window packet, the DyadicRatCore
endpoint and radius ledger, the RegSeqRat finite-request section, the local Real
seal, and the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$
rows.

For the reverse direction, assume the ordered route and the finite-window
witness rows. Apply
\autoref{thm:real-l10-formal-boundary-exit-certificate}. The route and witness
data repack as the four-face formal boundary exported by the accepted Phase Real
and Completion terminal read.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:real-l10-face-axis-independence}. It keeps the
certificate face-local, so quotient streams, selected limits,
countable-choice rows, metric-completeness data, aggregate formalstatus rows,
aggregate bridge rows, and $\RealUp$ completeness cannot be smuggled into the
exit envelope. Hence the envelope is exactly the conjunction of the ordered
route and the finite-window witness rows.
\end{proof}
