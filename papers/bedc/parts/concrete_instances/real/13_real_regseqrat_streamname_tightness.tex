\begin{theorem}[Real finite-window seal tightness]
\label{thm:real-regseqrat-streamname-tightness}
A $\RealUp$ finite-window seal is admissible exactly when its $\RegSeqRatUp$ tail budget, $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window section, and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ denominator ledger agree on a common $\hsame$-compatible finite request. The common request carries the displayed $\BHist$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows used by the public seal.

Equivalently, a proposed seal whose RegSeqRat tail selector, StreamName request section, and dyadic denominator ledger do not factor through one common finite request is not an accepted $\RealUp$ public seal. The statement uses only the displayed BEDC route and does not invoke host real equality, quotient streams, selected limits, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the forward direction, start with the Real dyadic RegSeq cross-reference lattice of \autoref{thm:real-dyadic-regseq-crossref-lattice}. It places every accepted Real current-phase name read on the DyadicRatCore, StreamName, RegSeqRat, and local Real faces.

Next apply the finite request fusion and finite-window seal exhaustion results, \autoref{thm:real-regseqrat-finite-request-fusion} and \autoref{thm:real-finite-window-seal-exhaustion}. These identify the public seal with a displayed finite request whose StreamName section, RegSeqRat readback, dyadic tolerance ledger, and support rows are componentwise compatible.

For the reverse direction, assemble the common request through the sibling route. The RegSeqRat terminal budget exhaustion supplies the tail-budget side, the StreamName finite request section supplies the public window, and the DyadicRatCore ledger supplies the denominator data for the same displayed request.

Finally repack the route as a RealUp public seal by the RegSeqRat-to-StreamName handoff and the dyadic Stream-RegSeq route. Since each cited route refuses quotient streams, host equality, selected limits, and ambient completeness, the common finite request is the whole admissibility witness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real finite-cover seal pasting criterion]
\label{thm:real-finite-cover-seal-pasting-criterion}
Let a finite cover of certified $\StreamNameUp$ windows be given, with pairwise and triple overlaps satisfying the $\StreamNameUp$--$\RegSeqRatUp$ finite-cover pasted-source hypotheses. Suppose the local handoff rows consume the same displayed $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ finite ledger, and suppose each local $\RegSeqRatUp$ tail satisfies the $\RealUp$ finite-window tightness criterion of \autoref{thm:real-regseqrat-streamname-tightness}.

Then the local finite-window $\RealUp$ seal rows paste to one admissible $\RealUp$ public seal over the finite cover. Conversely, a proposed public seal over such a finite cover is admissible exactly when there exists a common finite surface whose $\StreamNameUp$ pasted source, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger, and $\RegSeqRatUp$ common-tail boundary are the displayed local rows on every window and overlap.

The pasted surface is a finite BEDC datum: it records the relevant $\BHist$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows on the cover index, on every binary overlap, and on every triple overlap required by the StreamName cocycle. It exports no quotient stream, host real equality, selected limit, countable choice, metric-completeness datum, ambient completeness row, or second $\RealUp$ seal coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First build the finite cover surface. Apply \autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-finite-cover-pasted-source-exhaustion} to the certified StreamName windows and their pairwise and triple overlap data. This produces one pasted StreamName source surface, with the overlap rows still expressed as finite $\BHist$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ data.

Next transport the boundary rows through the RegSeqRat and dyadic faces. The overlap compatibility is exactly the common-tail Real boundary equivalence of \autoref{thm:regseqrat-common-tail-real-boundary-equivalence}; the denominator ledger is carried through the four-face route by \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-regseqrat-real-pullback-composition}. Hence every local handoff reads from the same finite dyadic ledger and the same RegSeqRat common-tail surface.

For the forward direction, apply \autoref{thm:real-regseqrat-streamname-tightness} on each window. The local admissibility witnesses all factor through the pasted StreamName source and the common dyadic ledger, so their Real seal rows have the same finite public support and therefore paste to one admissible public seal.

For the reverse direction, start from an admissible public seal over the cover and read its local finite-window restrictions. The tightness criterion supplies the local RegSeqRat tail budgets, StreamName sections, and dyadic denominator rows; the pasted-source and pullback routes identify these rows with one common finite surface. Thus admissibility is exactly the existence of that displayed pasted StreamName/DyadicRatCore/RegSeqRat surface, with no quotient, host-equality, choice, limit, or completeness datum added.
\end{proof}

The interface exports no quotient stream equality, selected limit, host real equality, countable-choice selector, ambient completeness principle, metric-completeness row, or standard bridge row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

The admissible seal is read exactly through a common finite request carrying the StreamName section, RegSeqRat tail budget, DyadicRatCore denominator ledger, and local Real rows.

Next apply \autoref{thm:real-finite-cover-seal-pasting-criterion}. It identifies the finite public support of the seal with the pasted finite surface on the displayed $\BHist$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows, so the local windows and their overlaps have one public finite support.

Thus the four displayed faces form one finite pullback interface, and uniqueness follows because each cited route reads the same finite rows rather than a second external coordinate.

For the reverse direction, begin with such a four-face finite pullback interface. The dyadic scale-window packet, StreamName finite-window section, RegSeqRat common-tail boundary, and local Real seal-source rows supply the common request required by \autoref{thm:real-regseqrat-streamname-tightness}.

Finally repack the common request as a public seal and use \autoref{thm:real-finite-cover-seal-pasting-criterion} to pass across the displayed finite cover. The same exclusion clauses in the four cited routes leave no quotient stream equality, selected limit, host real equality, countable choice, ambient completeness, metric completeness, or standard bridge row to add.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real L10 four-face refinement stability]
\label{thm:real-l10-four-face-refinement-stability}
Let a $\RealUp$ finite-window public seal factor through the four displayed faces $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and the local $\RealUp$ seal-source rows as in \autoref{thm:real-l10-four-face-finite-pullback-public-interface}. Any $\hsame$-compatible finite-window refinement that preserves the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ scale-window packet, the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window section, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ common-tail boundary, the local seal row, the displayed $\Cont$ routes, the $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local $\NameCert$ rows determines the same public finite pullback interface and repacks as the same admissible finite-window seal.

Conversely, any accepted refinement of that public interface exposes exactly those refined four faces. It exports no quotient stream equality, selected limit, host real equality, countable-choice selector, ambient completeness row, metric-completeness row, standard bridge row, or second public seal coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the displayed public seal. By \autoref{thm:real-regseqrat-streamname-tightness}, admissibility is exactly factorization through one common finite request carrying the StreamName section, RegSeqRat tail budget, DyadicRatCore denominator ledger, and the local Real rows.

Apply \autoref{thm:real-finite-cover-seal-pasting-criterion} to the refined finite windows. Since the refinement preserves the scale-window packet, finite-window section, common-tail boundary, local seal row, displayed $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ rows, the pasted finite surface has the same public support on every window and overlap.

Next use the StreamName and RegSeqRat pullback determinacy rows, together with \autoref{thm:real-l10-four-face-finite-pullback-public-interface}. They identify the refined StreamName window, RegSeqRat readback, DyadicRatCore ledger, and Real seal row with the same four-face finite pullback interface, rather than with a second external coordinate.

For the converse, read an accepted refinement through the public interface theorem. Its admissibility witness must factor through the four displayed faces, and the tightness and pasting criteria force the refined rows to be exactly the preserved scale-window, finite-window, common-tail, local-seal, route, provenance, and certificate rows.

The exclusion clauses are inherited from the same cited routes. None of them exports quotient stream equality, selected limits, host real equality, countable choice, ambient completeness, metric completeness, or standard bridge data, so the accepted refinement cannot expose any such row.
\end{proof}
