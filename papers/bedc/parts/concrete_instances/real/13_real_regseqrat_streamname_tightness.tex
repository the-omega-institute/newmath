For any accepted current Phase $\RealUp$ and Completion finite-window public classifier packet, the visible witness surface is minimal for the displayed four-face separatedness route. Removing the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ face removes the endpoint, radius, and distance ledger data; removing the $\StreamNameUp$ face removes the finite schedule; removing the $\RegSeqRatUp$ face removes the regular-tail classifier; and removing the local $\RealUp$ seal-source rows removes the seal boundary. In each case, \autoref{thm:real-l10-four-face-classifier-separatedness} is no longer applicable by the displayed route.

Likewise, removing any displayed support row among $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ destroys either the provenance or the transport needed to read the public finite-window witness. Conversely, the full four-face packet determines the same public classifier surface by \autoref{thm:real-l10-four-face-classifier-separatedness}. The minimality statement is internal to the displayed BEDC rows and excludes quotient streams, host real equality, selected limits, countable choice, ambient completeness, metric completeness, standard bridge rows, and any second classifier coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First read the packet through the tightness criterion. By \autoref{thm:real-regseqrat-streamname-tightness}, an accepted finite-window seal is exactly a common finite request carrying the RegSeqRat tail budget, StreamName finite-window section, DyadicRatCore denominator ledger, local Real rows, and the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support.

Now remove one face at a time. Without the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger, the route no longer contains the endpoint, radius, and distance data used to compare public classifier surfaces. Without the $\StreamNameUp$ section, the dyadic and regular-tail rows no longer supply the finite schedule on which the witness is read. Without the $\RegSeqRatUp$ common-tail boundary, the finite schedule and dyadic ledger no longer supply the regular-tail classifier. Without the local $\RealUp$ seal-source rows, the three imported faces no longer supply the seal boundary.

The support rows are load-bearing for the same reason. Dropping $\BHist$ or $\NameCert$ removes the finite provenance of the displayed witness, dropping $\hsame$ removes the componentwise identification needed across windows, dropping $\Cont$ removes the transport route, and dropping $\Pkg$ removes the package row that connects the public presentation to its certified carrier.

Finally restore the whole surface. The finite pullback interface of \autoref{thm:real-l10-four-face-finite-pullback-public-interface} repacks the four faces and support rows as one public interface, while \autoref{thm:real-common-tail-terminal-four-face-classifier-equivalence} and \autoref{thm:real-l10-four-face-classifier-sheaf-condition} identify the terminal and coverwise classifier reads. Then \autoref{thm:real-l10-four-face-classifier-separatedness} gives determinacy of the public classifier from exactly those visible rows.

These citations are non-reconstruction results: they specify the rows consumed by the tightness, finite-pullback, and separatedness routes, but they do not construct counter-packets after a row is deleted. The exclusion clause follows from the same route inventory. None of the cited results exports quotient stream equality, host real equality, selected limits, countable choice, ambient completeness, metric completeness, standard bridge rows, or a second classifier coordinate, so no such datum is available inside this proof as a replacement for a missing face or support row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real L10 four-face classifier functoriality]
\label{thm:real-l10-four-face-classifier-functoriality}
Let $r$ be a finite-request refinement map between two current Phase $\RealUp$ and Completion classifier presentations. Suppose $r$ preserves the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger rows, the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window section, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ common-tail classifier rows, the local $\RealUp$ seal-source row, and the displayed support
$$
\BHist,\quad \hsame,\quad \Cont,\quad \Pkg,\quad \NameCert.
$$

Then transport along $r$ carries a public $\RealUp$ classifier surface to the same public classifier surface. Conversely, any equality of transported public classifier surfaces is witnessed exactly on the four faces $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and local $\RealUp$ together with those displayed support rows. The functoriality surface exports no quotient stream, selected limit, host real equality, countable-choice row, ambient completeness, metric-completeness row, standard bridge row, or second classifier coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First read the refinement map through the sibling certificates. The $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger is supplied by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the finite request schedule by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, and the common-tail classifier rows by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}. These are the three imported faces consumed by the local $\RealUp$ seal-source row.

Apply \autoref{thm:real-l10-four-face-refinement-stability}. Since $r$ preserves the dyadic ledger, finite-window section, common-tail boundary, local seal row, displayed $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and $\NameCert$ rows, the refinement determines the same finite pullback interface and repacks as the same admissible public seal.

Next compose transport with the public interface. By \autoref{thm:real-l10-four-face-interface-composition}, a transported interface over the refined request is the one-step interface over the composite finite request, with the same $\BHist$, componentwise $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support rows. Thus the transported surface has no independent classifier coordinate beyond the four displayed faces.

For the converse, start from equality of transported public classifier surfaces. Separatedness in \autoref{thm:real-l10-four-face-classifier-separatedness} reads any possible disagreement back to one of the DyadicRatCore ledger rows, StreamName finite-window rows, RegSeqRat common-tail rows, local Real seal-source rows, or displayed support rows. Therefore equality is witnessed precisely by agreement on those rows.

Finally use \autoref{thm:real-l10-four-face-witness-minimality} to rule out a smaller witness inventory. Removing any one of the four faces or any one of $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ destroys the displayed route on which separatedness is read. The same row inventory excludes quotient streams, selected limits, host real equality, countable choice, ambient completeness, metric completeness, standard bridge rows, and a second classifier coordinate from both directions of the functoriality statement.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real L10 cofinal classifier refinement invariance]
\label{thm:real-l10-cofinal-classifier-refinement-invariance}
Let $\mathcal P$ be a current Phase $\RealUp$ and Completion finite-window public classifier packet. Let $\mathcal R$ be a cofinal family of finite-request refinements of $\mathcal P$: every finite public readback request for $\mathcal P$ is dominated by some refinement in $\mathcal R$. Suppose that each refinement in $\mathcal R$ preserves the four displayed faces $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and the local $\RealUp$ seal-source rows, and also preserves the displayed support rows
$$
\BHist,\quad \hsame,\quad \Cont,\quad \Pkg,\quad \NameCert.
$$

Then all public classifier surfaces read from refinements in $\mathcal R$ determine the same $\RealUp$ classifier surface as $\mathcal P$. Conversely, if two cofinal refined reads determine different public $\RealUp$ classifier surfaces, then the disagreement is witnessed at some finite refinement stage on one of the displayed DyadicRatCore ledger rows, StreamName finite-window rows, RegSeqRat common-tail rows, local Real seal-source rows, or $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support rows.

The invariance is only an assertion about the displayed finite BEDC classifier surface. It asserts no formal-status row, bridge row, quotient stream equality, selected limit, countable-choice row, ambient completeness row, metric-completeness row, standard bridge row, or second public classifier coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First fix one refinement in $\mathcal R$. The preservation hypotheses are exactly the hypotheses of \autoref{thm:real-l10-four-face-classifier-functoriality}, so transport along that refinement reads the refined public classifier surface as the same public classifier surface carried by $\mathcal P$ on the displayed four faces and support rows.

Now compare two refinements in the cofinal family. Cofinality gives a further finite request dominating the two finite reads. Applying functoriality along the two induced refinement maps transports both classifier surfaces to the same displayed four-face packet over that common finite request. Hence every refined read in $\mathcal R$ determines the same public $\RealUp$ classifier surface.

For the converse, suppose two refined public classifier surfaces disagree. By \autoref{thm:real-l10-four-face-classifier-separatedness}, any such disagreement is already visible on one of the DyadicRatCore ledger rows, StreamName finite-window rows, RegSeqRat common-tail rows, local Real seal-source rows, or displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support rows.

The witness is finite because both disagreeing surfaces come from finite-request refinements, and cofinality supplies a common finite refinement stage on which the two reads can be compared. The separatedness witness therefore appears at such a finite stage, not in an ambient quotient, limit, choice, completeness, bridge, or duplicate classifier coordinate.

Finally, \autoref{thm:real-l10-four-face-witness-minimality} rules out shrinking the displayed witness inventory, while \autoref{thm:real-l10-classifier-status-axis-separation} keeps the classifier claim separate from audit status. Thus the theorem states cofinal invariance of the public classifier surface only, with no assertion about formal status, bridge status, quotient streams, selected limits, countable choice, ambient completeness, or metric completeness.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real L10 classifier refinement category laws]
\label{thm:real-l10-classifier-refinement-category-laws}

$$
\DyadicRatCoreUp,\quad \StreamNameUp,\quad \RegSeqRatUp,\quad \RealUp
$$
where the $\RealUp$ face is the local seal-source row, together with the support rows
$$
\BHist,\quad \hsame,\quad \Cont,\quad \Pkg,\quad \NameCert.
$$

If $r : \mathcal P \to \mathcal Q$ and $s : \mathcal Q \to \mathcal R$ are finite-request refinements preserving those four faces and support rows, then their composite $s \circ r$ preserves the same public $\RealUp$ classifier surface. Conversely, if the classifier surfaces transported by a composite refinement disagree, then the disagreement is witnessed at a finite stage on one of the DyadicRatCore ledger rows, StreamName finite-window rows, RegSeqRat common-tail rows, local Real seal-source rows, or displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support rows.

These laws are only laws for the displayed finite BEDC refinement action. They assert no quotient stream equality, host real equality, selected limit, countable-choice row, ambient completeness row, metric-completeness row, bridge row, or second public classifier coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the identity refinement, the finite request, four classifier faces, and support rows are unchanged. Applying \autoref{thm:real-l10-four-face-classifier-functoriality} to this preservation data reads the transported public classifier surface as the original public $\RealUp$ classifier surface.

Now consider refinements $r : \mathcal P \to \mathcal Q$ and $s : \mathcal Q \to \mathcal R$. The preservation hypotheses for $r$ and $s$ identify the DyadicRatCore ledger, StreamName finite-window section, RegSeqRat common-tail rows, local Real seal-source row, and the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support across the two steps.

Apply \autoref{thm:real-l10-four-face-interface-composition}. The two-step transport is the same finite interface as transport along the composite finite request, with no additional classifier coordinate. Then \autoref{thm:real-l10-four-face-classifier-functoriality} gives preservation of the public classifier surface for $s \circ r$.

For the converse, suppose the composite transport gives two different public classifier surfaces. By \autoref{thm:real-l10-four-face-classifier-separatedness}, such a difference must already be visible on one of the four displayed faces or on one of the listed support rows.

Finally, \autoref{thm:real-l10-terminal-cofinal-pullback-determinacy} places that witness at a finite refinement stage whenever the comparison is made through a terminal cofinal pullback. The cited route inventory exports only the displayed finite BEDC rows, so quotient streams, host real equality, selected limits, countable choice, ambient completeness, metric completeness, bridge rows, and a second classifier coordinate cannot supply an additional obstruction or a repair.
\end{proof}
