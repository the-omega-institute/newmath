\begin{theorem}[Real separability Cauchy-equivalence lattice route]
\label{thm:real-separability-cauchy-equivalence-lattice-route}
Every $\RealSeparabilityUp$ finite density read that is consumed by a Cauchy-equivalence or completion classifier must pass through the displayed $\RealUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, and $\CauchyEquivalenceSetoidUp$ sibling rows. The route exposes finite density windows and dyadic readback before the Cauchy-equivalence classifier is read, and it exports no quotient real equality, selected dense enumeration, countable choice, or $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the density route of \autoref{thm:real-separability-rational-dense-handoff}. It reads the rational dense enumeration through finite dyadic and rational-density surfaces before the terminal $\RealUp$ seal is consumed.

Next use the completion-facing sibling route, \autoref{thm:real-separability-cauchy-completion-route}. It forces the same density witness through $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, and $\RegSeqRatUp$ finite windows before any completion-facing consumer receives the read.

Finally attach the Cauchy-equivalence classifier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-equivalence-setoid-namecert}. The classifier may consume only the displayed finite windows and sibling rows already supplied by the separability route. Since neither route includes quotient real equality, a selected dense enumeration, countable choice, or $\RealUp$ completeness, the reciprocal lattice edge cannot export any of those claims.
\end{proof}
