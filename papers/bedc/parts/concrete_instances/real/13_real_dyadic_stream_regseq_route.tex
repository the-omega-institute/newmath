\begin{theorem}[Real dyadic StreamName RegSeqRat route]
\label{thm:real-dyadic-stream-regseq-route}
Every accepted $\RealUp$ open-phase seal read whose source is presented to an
L10 consumer as a $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observation ledger, a $\StreamNameUp$
finite-window row, a $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular readback row, and the local
$\RealUp$ seal factors through one ordered finite route:
$$
\DyadicRatCoreUp \to \StreamNameUp \to \RegSeqRatUp \to \RealUp .
$$
The route is transported only by the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$,
$\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows of those four surfaces.

No accepted read on this route exports quotient real equality, host limits,
countable choice, ambient completeness, an independent host completion
surface, or a direct local seal edge bypassing the dyadic, stream, and
regular-rational rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the L10 interface totality theorem
\autoref{thm:real-l10-dependency-lattice-interface-totality}. It already
places every accepted Phase Real or Completion interface read on exactly the
four visible faces of the Real L10 dependency lattice: DyadicRatCore
observations, StreamName finite-window rows, RegSeqRat regular-rational rows,
and the local Real seal.

Next apply the source lattice theorem
\autoref{thm:real-streamname-regseqrat-source-lattice}. It identifies the
StreamName, RegSeqRat, and DyadicRatCore handoff as one source lattice before
the Real seal is public, with only the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$,
$\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ coordinates available for transport and packaging.

Finally use terminal budget stability,
\autoref{thm:real-streamname-regseqrat-terminal-budget-stability}. Changing
the finite observation budget changes only which displayed finite rows are
queried, not the carrier of the route. Thus the open-phase seal read remains
on the single ordered finite route and has no coordinate for quotient real
equality, host limits, countable choice, ambient completeness, host
completion, or a direct seal bypass.
\end{proof}
