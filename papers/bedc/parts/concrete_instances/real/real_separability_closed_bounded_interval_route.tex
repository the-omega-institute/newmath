\begin{theorem}[Real separability closed bounded interval route]
\label{thm:real-separability-closed-bounded-interval-route}
Every $\RealSeparabilityUp$ density route that feeds a closed bounded interval consumer factors through the displayed $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ rows before the consumer named by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closedboundedinterval-namecert} may inspect its interval certificate. The route exposes dyadic rational cells, regular rational readback, terminal Real seals, and the shared transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows; it does not expose a selected dense point, quotient real equality, hidden endpoint chooser, open-cover compactness, or ambient completeness row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier of \autoref{def:real-separability-carrier}. Its density data is finite and is already attached to dyadic rational cells, regular rational readback, terminal Real seals, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming.

Route the density data to \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closedboundedinterval-namecert}. A closed bounded interval consumer can use the separability packet only after the dyadic cell and the regular rational readback have been displayed.

The $\RealUp$ seal is then read as the terminal boundary of the same finite route. Since no coordinate of the separability packet contains a selected dense point, quotient equality, endpoint chooser, open-cover compactness, or ambient completeness row, the closed bounded interval handoff is exactly the displayed dependency route.
\end{proof}
