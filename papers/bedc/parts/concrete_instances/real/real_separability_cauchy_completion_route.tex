\begin{theorem}[Real separability Cauchy completion route]
\label{thm:real-separability-cauchy-completion-route}
Every dense dyadic witness exported by an accepted $\RealSeparabilityUp$ packet feeds $\CauchyCompletionFunctorUp$, $\BishopCompletionCriterionUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, and $\RealUp$ only through finite windows and dyadic readback. The route first displays the finite $\StreamNameUp$ observation window, then the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint and tolerance ledger, then the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row, and only then the completion or Real-facing seal. It has no coordinate for quotient stream equality, ambient completeness, selected host limits, or an unbounded dense-set search.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the density carrier and finite density lattice already present in the $\RealSeparabilityUp$ packet. They expose dense dyadic witnesses as finite certificate rows rather than as a global choice function.

The dyadic lattice link places each witness on a $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint and tolerance ledger. The sibling route through $\StreamNameUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$ then reads that ledger through a finite window before any completion-facing consumer can use it.

Finally the completion consumers receive the same finite readback. Since the packet has no row for quotient streams, ambient completeness, selected host limits, or unbounded dense search, $\CauchyCompletionFunctorUp$ and $\BishopCompletionCriterionUp$ can consume the witness only through the displayed finite-window and dyadic-readback route.
\end{proof}
