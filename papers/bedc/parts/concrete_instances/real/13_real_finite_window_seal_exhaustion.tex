\begin{theorem}[Real finite-window seal exhaustion]
\label{thm:real-finite-window-seal-exhaustion}
Every terminal $\RealUp$ seal read used by a Real-completion consumer factors through the displayed $\StreamNameUp$ finite window, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local $\RealUp$ seal row. Equivalently, the terminal read is exhausted by the finite route
$$
\DyadicRatCoreUp \to \StreamNameUp \to \RegSeqRatUp \to \RealUp
$$
together with the visible $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support rows.

The read cannot add quotient stream equality, host real equality, selected host limits, ambient completeness, countable-choice selectors, metric-completeness data, or a standard bridge row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the terminal seal through the existing $\RealUp$ finite-window rows. The name-seal carrier of \autoref{def:real-name-seal-carrier} displays the finite-window schedule, dyadic observation, RegSeqRat regularity row, finite ledger, local seal row, and the support rows $H,C,P,N$.

Apply \autoref{thm:real-name-seal-source-factorization}. It forces every public seal read to pass through the displayed source route from the $\StreamNameUp$ window and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observation to the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback and then to the local $\RealUp$ seal.

Use the sibling routes already present in the hub. The StreamName finite-window face is the public interface of \autoref{thm:streamname-public-finite-window-interface}, the RegSeqRat handoff is \autoref{thm:regseqrat-real-seal-handoff}, and the DyadicRatCore tolerance source is \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-real-phase-source-coverage}. Together with \autoref{thm:real-streamname-regseqrat-seal-route}, these rows repack the read as the four-face route in the statement.

Finally apply the local non-escape and readback surface supplied by \autoref{thm:real-name-seal-four-face-coverage} and \autoref{thm:real-regseqrat-terminal-budget-exhaustion}. Those theorems expose no quotient stream equality, host real equality, selected host limits, ambient completeness, choice selector, metric-completeness row, or standard bridge row, so a terminal Real-completion consumer cannot acquire any of those objects from the finite seal read.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real finite-window seal refinement monotonicity]
\label{thm:real-finite-window-seal-refinement-monotonicity}
Let $\mathcal R$ be an accepted public $\RealUp$ terminal seal read whose
finite boundary factors through the four faces
$$
\DyadicRatCoreUp \to \StreamNameUp \to \RegSeqRatUp \to \RealUp.
$$
Suppose $\mathcal R'$ is a second finite request whose $\StreamNameUp$ window
refines the displayed window of $\mathcal R$, while preserving the same
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint, radius, and distance ledger, the same
$\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier and readback rows, the same local $\RealUp$ seal
row, and the same support rows
$$
\BHist,\quad \hsame,\quad \Cont,\quad \Pkg,\quad \NameCert.
$$
Then $\mathcal R'$ is again an accepted finite-window $\RealUp$ seal read.
Moreover, $\mathcal R'$ exports no coordinate outside the same four-face
packet and the displayed support rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the accepted read $\mathcal R$. By
\autoref{thm:real-finite-window-seal-exhaustion}, its public terminal seal
surface is exhausted by the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$,
$\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ faces together with the displayed $\BHist$,
$\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support rows.

Now restrict the refined request to the same four-face cover. The cover normal
form of \autoref{thm:real-terminal-seal-four-face-cover-normal-form} says that
finite refinements of the displayed $\StreamNameUp$ windows remain in the
same terminal seal packet when the dyadic ledger, RegSeqRat classifier and
readback rows, Real seal row, and support rows are preserved.

Next apply terminality from
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-completion-four-object-terminal-pullback}. Since
the refined request exposes exactly the same four faces and the same support
rows after restriction, it factors through the terminal finite BEDC pullback
available to Real-seal-facing consumers.

Finally transport the accepted status along the unchanged support rows using
\autoref{thm:real-regseqrat-terminal-budget-four-face-naturality}. Naturality
keeps the RegSeqRat budget and local Real seal readback on the same accepted
four-face route, so $\mathcal R'$ is an accepted finite-window seal read. The
non-escape clauses in the cited exhaustion, normal-form, pullback, and
naturality results leave no coordinate outside the four faces and the
displayed support rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real terminal finite-section pullback exactness]
\label{thm:real-terminal-finite-section-pullback-exactness}
Let $\mathcal S$ be a finite section of a terminal $\RealUp$ seal read. Then
$\mathcal S$ is accepted exactly when its visible pullback is the ordered
four-face packet
$$
\DyadicRatCoreUp \to \StreamNameUp \to \RegSeqRatUp \to \RealUp
$$
with the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
The $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ face is the terminal scale-window packet, the
$\StreamNameUp$ face is the tail-compatible finite-window request, the
$\RegSeqRatUp$ face is the common-tail finite-request readback surface, and
the $\RealUp$ face is the local terminal seal row.

Thus a finite section is accepted by the terminal seal precisely through this
visible BEDC pullback. It has no accepted coordinate for quotient streams,
host real equality, selected limits, countable choice, ambient completeness,
metric-completeness rows, standard bridge rows, or hidden completion carriers.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the forward direction, start with an accepted finite section
$\mathcal S$. By \autoref{thm:real-finite-window-seal-exhaustion}, the
terminal seal read is exhausted by the four displayed faces and by the
$\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support rows.

Refine the section inside that cover. The refinement monotonicity theorem
\autoref{thm:real-finite-window-seal-refinement-monotonicity} keeps any
visible finite refinement on the same four-face route, so the accepted section
cannot leave the displayed terminal packet while preserving its support rows.

Now identify the three source faces. The StreamName tail-compatible request,
\autoref{thm:streamname-tail-compatible-window-request}, supplies the finite
window row. The RegSeqRat common-tail finite-request pullback,
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-common-tail-finite-request-real-seal-pullback},
identifies the common-tail readback with the same finite Real seal boundary.
The DyadicRatCore scale-window monotonicity and terminal fusion theorems,
\autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-scale-window-monotonicity} and
\autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-scale-window-terminal-fusion}, force the dyadic
entry face onto one terminal scale-window packet.

For the reverse direction, suppose the visible pullback is exactly this
ordered four-face packet with the displayed support rows. The StreamName
request, RegSeqRat pullback, and DyadicRatCore terminal fusion results assemble
the section as a finite route into the local $\RealUp$ seal row. Applying
\autoref{thm:real-finite-window-seal-refinement-monotonicity} to that route
gives an accepted finite-window terminal seal section.

The exclusion clauses are inherited from the same cited routes and from
\autoref{thm:real-finite-window-seal-exhaustion}. Quotient stream equality,
host real equality, selected limits, countable choice, ambient completeness,
metric-completeness rows, standard bridge rows, and hidden completion carriers
are not rows of the pullback, so they cannot be used to accept another finite
section of the terminal seal.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real terminal finite-section classifier determinacy]
\label{thm:real-terminal-finite-section-classifier-determinacy}
Let $\mathcal S$ and $\mathcal T$ be accepted finite sections of terminal
$\RealUp$ seal reads. Suppose their visible $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ faces carry the
same endpoint, radius, and distance ledger, their $\StreamNameUp$ faces display
the same finite window, their $\RegSeqRatUp$ faces display the same common-tail
classifier and readback rows, their local $\RealUp$ faces display the same
terminal seal row, and their support rows
$$
\BHist,\quad \hsame,\quad \Cont,\quad \Pkg,\quad \NameCert
$$
are the same displayed rows. Then $\mathcal S$ and $\mathcal T$ determine the
same public $\RealUp$ classifier route.

Conversely, every public $\RealUp$ classifier route accepted at this terminal
finite-section boundary is determined by exactly the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$
endpoint-radius ledger, the $\StreamNameUp$ finite window, the
$\RegSeqRatUp$ common-tail readback surface, the local $\RealUp$ seal row, and
the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support rows.
The route carries no quotient stream equality, host real equality, selected
limits, countable choice, ambient completeness, metric-completeness data,
standard bridge row, or hidden completion carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First compare two accepted sections. By
\autoref{thm:real-terminal-finite-section-pullback-exactness}, each accepted
finite section is represented by the same ordered four-face pullback whenever
its dyadic ledger, StreamName window, RegSeqRat common-tail readback, local Real
seal row, and displayed support rows agree.

Now refine both representatives to the common finite window. The preservation
clause in \autoref{thm:real-finite-window-seal-refinement-monotonicity} keeps
the accepted status and forbids any extra coordinate from entering during this
finite-window comparison.

Terminal uniqueness supplies the classifier route. Apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-completion-four-object-terminal-pullback} to the
common $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ faces
with the displayed support rows; the terminal pullback admits only one public
Real classifier route over those faces.

Finally transport that route along the RegSeqRat budget boundary using
\autoref{thm:real-regseqrat-terminal-budget-four-face-naturality}. This gives
the same public classifier route for $\mathcal S$ and $\mathcal T$.

For the converse, start with an accepted public classifier route at the
terminal finite-section boundary. Pullback exactness identifies its accepted
boundary with the four displayed faces and support rows, and terminality makes
that representation unique. Naturality then transports the route back to the
RegSeqRat common-tail readback without adding quotient stream equality, host
real equality, selected limits, countable choice, ambient completeness,
metric-completeness data, a standard bridge row, or a hidden completion carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real terminal finite-cover seal descent]
\label{thm:real-terminal-finite-cover-seal-descent}
Let $\{\mathcal S_i\}_{i\in I}$ be a finite family of accepted terminal
$\RealUp$ finite sections. Suppose the visible $\StreamNameUp$ windows of the
family satisfy the finite-cover pasted source hypotheses, the induced
$\RegSeqRat$ handoff packages consume one shared $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ finite
ledger on every displayed overlap, and the local $\RealUp$ seal classifier
rows agree on all pairwise overlaps and satisfy the displayed triple-overlap
cocycle. Suppose also that the support rows
$$
\BHist,\quad \hsame,\quad \Cont,\quad \Pkg,\quad \NameCert
$$
restrict compatibly on the same finite cover.

Then the family descends to one public $\RealUp$ classifier route over the
finite union of the windows. This descended route is exhausted by the pasted
$\StreamNameUp$ source surface, the shared $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ finite ledger,
the pasted $\RegSeqRat$ handoff surface, the local $\RealUp$ seal row, and the
displayed support rows. It exports no quotient stream equality, selected
limit, countable-choice selector, ambient completeness theorem,
metric-completeness row, standard bridge row, or hidden completion carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First paste the finite source surface. Apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-finite-cover-pasted-source-exhaustion} to
the certified $\StreamNameUp$ windows, their displayed overlaps, their
triple-overlap cocycles, and the shared DyadicRatCore finite ledger. This
produces one RegSeqRat and finite-window Real source surface over the finite
union.

Next paste the seal rows. The overlap agreement of the local $\RealUp$
classifier rows and the triple-overlap seal cocycle are exactly the hypotheses
of \autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-finite-cover-pasted-seal-exhaustion}, so
the local seal classifiers form one finite-window Real seal surface over the
same finite union.

Now identify each local section with the terminal four-face packet. Use
\autoref{thm:real-terminal-finite-section-pullback-exactness} on every
accepted $\mathcal S_i$. The only visible rows of each local section are the
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ faces
together with the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and
$\NameCert$ rows.

The shared dyadic ledger is the same finite-tail input consumed by
\autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-finite-tail-window-factorization}. That route
places the pasted source and pasted seal surfaces on the displayed
$$
\DyadicRatCoreUp \to \StreamNameUp \to \RegSeqRatUp \to \RealUp
$$
packet and leaves no auxiliary equality, selected-limit, completeness, or
bridge coordinate.

Finally apply
\autoref{thm:real-terminal-finite-section-classifier-determinacy}. Since the
pasted finite union has one dyadic ledger, one StreamName source surface, one
RegSeqRat handoff surface, one local Real seal row, and compatible support
rows, the public terminal classifier route is determined uniquely over that
finite union. The non-escape clauses of the cited pasted-source, pasted-seal,
pullback, determinacy, and finite-tail factorization routes give exactly the
exclusions in the statement.
\end{proof}
