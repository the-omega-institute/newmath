\begin{theorem}[Real finite-window seal exhaustion]
\label{thm:real-finite-window-seal-exhaustion}
Every terminal $\RealUp$ seal read used by a Real-completion consumer factors through the displayed $\StreamNameUp$ finite window, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local $\RealUp$ seal row. Equivalently, the terminal read is exhausted by the finite route
$$
\DyadicRatCoreUp \to \StreamNameUp \to \RegSeqRatUp \to \RealUp
$$
together with the visible $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support rows.

The read cannot add quotient stream equality, host real equality, selected host limits, ambient completeness, countable-choice selectors, metric-completeness data, or a standard bridge row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the terminal seal through the existing $\RealUp$ finite-window rows. The name-seal carrier of \autoref{def:real-name-seal-carrier} displays the finite-window schedule, dyadic observation, RegSeqRat regularity row, finite ledger, local seal row, and the support rows $H,C,P,N$.

Apply \autoref{thm:real-name-seal-source-factorization}. It forces every public seal read to pass through the displayed source route from the $\StreamNameUp$ window and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observation to the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback and then to the local $\RealUp$ seal.

Use the sibling routes already present in the hub. The StreamName finite-window face is the public interface of \autoref{thm:streamname-public-finite-window-interface}, the RegSeqRat handoff is \autoref{thm:regseqrat-real-seal-handoff}, and the DyadicRatCore tolerance source is \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-real-phase-source-coverage}. Together with \autoref{thm:real-streamname-regseqrat-seal-route}, these rows repack the read as the four-face route in the statement.

Finally apply the local non-escape and readback surface supplied by \autoref{thm:real-name-seal-four-face-coverage} and \autoref{thm:real-regseqrat-terminal-budget-exhaustion}. Those theorems expose no quotient stream equality, host real equality, selected host limits, ambient completeness, choice selector, metric-completeness row, or standard bridge row, so a terminal Real-completion consumer cannot acquire any of those objects from the finite seal read.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real finite-window seal refinement monotonicity]
\label{thm:real-finite-window-seal-refinement-monotonicity}
Let $\mathcal R$ be an accepted public $\RealUp$ terminal seal read whose
finite boundary factors through the four faces
$$
\DyadicRatCoreUp \to \StreamNameUp \to \RegSeqRatUp \to \RealUp.
$$
Suppose $\mathcal R'$ is a second finite request whose $\StreamNameUp$ window
refines the displayed window of $\mathcal R$, while preserving the same
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint, radius, and distance ledger, the same
$\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier and readback rows, the same local $\RealUp$ seal
row, and the same support rows
$$
\BHist,\quad \hsame,\quad \Cont,\quad \Pkg,\quad \NameCert.
$$
Then $\mathcal R'$ is again an accepted finite-window $\RealUp$ seal read.
Moreover, $\mathcal R'$ exports no coordinate outside the same four-face
packet and the displayed support rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the accepted read $\mathcal R$. By
\autoref{thm:real-finite-window-seal-exhaustion}, its public terminal seal
surface is exhausted by the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$,
$\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ faces together with the displayed $\BHist$,
$\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support rows.

Now restrict the refined request to the same four-face cover. The cover normal
form of \autoref{thm:real-terminal-seal-four-face-cover-normal-form} says that
finite refinements of the displayed $\StreamNameUp$ windows remain in the
same terminal seal packet when the dyadic ledger, RegSeqRat classifier and
readback rows, Real seal row, and support rows are preserved.

Next apply terminality from
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-completion-four-object-terminal-pullback}. Since
the refined request exposes exactly the same four faces and the same support
rows after restriction, it factors through the terminal finite BEDC pullback
available to Real-seal-facing consumers.

Finally transport the accepted status along the unchanged support rows using
\autoref{thm:real-regseqrat-terminal-budget-four-face-naturality}. Naturality
keeps the RegSeqRat budget and local Real seal readback on the same accepted
four-face route, so $\mathcal R'$ is an accepted finite-window seal read. The
non-escape clauses in the cited exhaustion, normal-form, pullback, and
naturality results leave no coordinate outside the four faces and the
displayed support rows.
\end{proof}
