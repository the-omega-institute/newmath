\begin{theorem}[RealSeparability forward metric-completion route]
\label{thm:real-separability-forward-metric-completion-route}
Every $\MetricCompletionUp$ or $\CauchyEquivalenceSetoidUp$ consumer of a $\RealSeparabilityUp$ packet first reads the $\RealUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, dense-embedding, and $\CountableDenseSubsetUp$ rows displayed by the separability carrier. The route exports finite density and readback data only through those rows; it exports no host dense subset, selected nearest point, quotient real equality, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the Cauchy completion route of \autoref{thm:real-separability-cauchy-completion-route}. It places every dense dyadic witness on finite $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ readback rows before a completion-facing consumer receives it.

Next use the completion-facing display route of \autoref{thm:real-separability-completion-facing-display-route}. That route adds the dense source and $\CountableDenseSubsetUp$ boundary required by metric-completion consumers.

Finally apply the RegSeqRat dependency of \autoref{thm:real-separability-regseqrat-dependency}. It keeps the witness on the displayed regular-rational surface, so a $\MetricCompletionUp$ or $\CauchyEquivalenceSetoidUp$ consumer cannot obtain a host dense subset, selected nearest point, quotient real equality, countable choice, or ambient completeness principle from the separability packet.
\end{proof}
