\begin{theorem}[Real separability to closed bounded interval forward route]
\label{thm:real-separability-closed-bounded-interval-forward-route}
Every $\ClosedBoundedIntervalUp$ consumer of an accepted $\RealSeparabilityUp$ density packet reads the finite dense windows only through the displayed $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RationalIntervalUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ route. In particular, the endpoint and containment consumers of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closedboundedinterval-namecert} receive rational cells, dyadic tolerance rows, finite stream windows, regular rational readback, and terminal Real seals before any local interval row is inspected.

The forward route exports no selected dense point, quotient real equality, ambient completeness row, hidden endpoint chooser, or open-cover compactness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier of \autoref{def:real-separability-carrier}. Its finite density rows expose rational candidates together with the dyadic tolerance ledger, stream-window schedule, regular rational readback, terminal Real seal, componentwise transport, replay, provenance, and local naming.

Next read the interval-facing route through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rationalinterval-namecert}. A closed bounded interval consumer can inspect endpoint and containment data only after the rational cell row has been displayed, and the dyadic refinement remains attached to that same finite rational ledger.

Now pass through the $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ rows. They supply the finite endpoint windows, the regular rational readbacks, and the terminal Real seals that \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closedboundedinterval-namecert} may consume when forming its local interval certificate.

Since the separability packet contains no selected dense point, quotient real equality, ambient completeness row, hidden endpoint chooser, or open-cover compactness principle, the handoff is exactly the displayed finite route.
\end{proof}
