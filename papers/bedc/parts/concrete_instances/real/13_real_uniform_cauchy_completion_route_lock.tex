\begin{theorem}[Real uniform Cauchy completion route lock]
\label{thm:real-uniform-cauchy-completion-route-lock}
Let
$$
U=(I,W,M,Q,T,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\UniformCauchyCriterionUp$ packet read by a Phase Real and
Completion consumer. If the consumer uses $U$ to reach a $\RealUp$ seal or a
completion-facing endpoint, then its route factors through
$$
I \longmapsto W \longmapsto M \longmapsto Q \longmapsto T \longmapsto E
$$
together with the $\StreamNameUp$ window row, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$
tolerance ledger, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular readback row, and the local
$\RealUp$ seal rows of \autoref{def:real-l10-dependency-lattice}.

The admitted route exposes only the finite packet rows $I,W,M,Q,T,E,H,C,P,N$
and the shared $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support
used by those sibling rows. It cannot export quotient streams, selected host
limits, host real equality, countable choice, open-cover compactness, ambient
completeness, or an additional completion carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the UniformCauchyCriterion finite-family seal obligation surface.
\autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-finite-family-seal-obligation-package}
and \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-finite-family-route-lock} display
the rows $I,W,M,Q,T,E,H,C,P,N$ and force the route from the finite index
ledger to the Real seal handoff through the single chain
$I \longmapsto W \longmapsto M \longmapsto Q \longmapsto T \longmapsto E$.

Now attach the completion-budget rows. The real completion budget lock
\autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-real-completion-budget-lock} places any
$\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ read after the same budget package, while the
completion-functor budget handoff
\autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-completion-functor-budget-handoff}
keeps functor-facing access inside the finite package and the regular
shared-tail sibling.

Next identify the Real-side dependency lattice. The L10 dependency lattice
\autoref{def:real-l10-dependency-lattice}, the source-route non-escape theorem
\autoref{thm:real-l10-source-route-nonescape}, and the terminal source lock
\autoref{thm:real-l10-terminal-source-lock} route every Phase Real or
Completion read through the StreamName, DyadicRatCore, RegSeqRat, and local
Real seal rows, with only $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and
$\NameCert$ support.

Finally compose the two boundaries with the UniformCauchyCriterion finite
budget envelope
\autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-real-completion-finite-budget-envelope}.
The UniformCauchyCriterion side supplies the ordered budget chain, and the
Real L10 side supplies the four sibling rows consumed by RealUp. Since the
cited non-escape and budget-lock theorems expose no quotient stream, selected
host limit, host equality, countable-choice row, open-cover compactness,
ambient completeness, or extra completion carrier, the Real-and-Completion
consumer has no route to export any of them.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real uniform Cauchy four-object terminal determinacy]
\label{thm:real-uniform-cauchy-four-object-terminal-determinacy}
Let
$$
U_0=(I_0,W_0,M_0,Q_0,T_0,E_0,H_0,C_0,P_0,N_0)
$$
and
$$
U_1=(I_1,W_1,M_1,Q_1,T_1,E_1,H_1,C_1,P_1,N_1)
$$
be accepted $\UniformCauchyCriterionUp$ packets read by Phase Real and
Completion consumers. Suppose the two reads expose the same finite family
ledger, shared $\StreamNameUp$ window family, modulus schedule,
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row, tail ledger, $\RealUp$ seal row, and the same
displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support rows.
Suppose also that their completion-facing boundaries expose the same four
faces
$$
\StreamNameUp,\quad \DyadicRatCoreUp,\quad \RegSeqRatUp,\quad \RealUp.
$$
Then the two completion-facing reads factor through the same terminal finite
BEDC pullback.

Conversely, every accepted uniform-Cauchy completion-facing read whose public
surface is the finite budget
$$
I,W,M,Q,T,E,H,C,P,N
$$
together with those four faces is determined by that finite budget and by the
four boundary faces. The resulting terminal read exports no quotient stream
family, no host real equality, no selected family limit, no ambient $\RealUp$
completeness theorem, no metric-completeness datum, no countable-choice row,
and no standard bridge row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First lock the finite uniform-Cauchy route. By
\autoref{thm:real-uniform-cauchy-completion-route-lock}, any Phase Real and
Completion consumer of an accepted $\UniformCauchyCriterionUp$ packet must pass
through the ordered rows $I,W,M,Q,T,E,H,C,P,N$ before it reaches either a
$\RealUp$ seal or a completion-facing endpoint.

Next apply the finite-family Real seal route determinacy theorem,
\autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-finite-family-real-seal-route-determinacy}.
Since the two reads expose the same family ledger, window family, modulus
schedule, dyadic tolerance row, and tail ledger, they reach the same finite
route through the seal rows $E,H,C,P,N$.

Now attach the completion boundary. The seal-completion boundary
\autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-real-seal-completion-boundary} keeps every
completion-facing consumer on the same finite public surface, and
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-completion-four-object-exit-route} identifies the
Real-and-Completion exit packet with the four displayed faces
$\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$.

With those faces fixed, use
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-completion-four-object-terminal-pullback}. The
terminal pullback theorem identifies any two accepted reads that expose the
same four faces and the same $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and
$\NameCert$ support rows. Hence the two completion-facing reads determine the
same terminal finite BEDC pullback.

For the converse direction, start with an accepted uniform-Cauchy
completion-facing read whose public surface is exactly the finite budget and
the four boundary faces. The route-lock theorem supplies the ordered
UniformCauchyCriterion budget; the seal-completion boundary places the read at
the Real seal boundary; the four-object exit route reads that boundary as the
StreamName, DyadicRatCore, RegSeqRat, and RealUp packet. Terminal pullback then
repackages the accepted read as the unique finite BEDC pullback determined by
those rows.

The exclusion clause is inherited from the same citations. None of the locked
budget rows or four boundary faces carries a coordinate for quotient stream
families, host real equality, selected family limits, ambient Real completeness,
metric-completeness data, countable choice, or standard bridge rows, so none of
that data can distinguish two reads after the finite budget and the four faces
agree.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real uniform Cauchy four-face status exactness]
\label{thm:real-uniform-cauchy-four-face-status-exactness}
Let $U=(I,W,M,Q,T,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted
$\UniformCauchyCriterionUp$ packet read by a Phase Real and Completion
consumer. Suppose the public surface of the read is the finite budget
$I,W,M,Q,T,E,H,C,P,N$ together with the four boundary faces
$$
\StreamNameUp,\quad \DyadicRatCoreUp,\quad \RegSeqRatUp,\quad \RealUp.
$$
Then the read is status-exact precisely when the $\StreamNameUp$ face is read
from its own closurestatus and formalstatus record, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$
face is read from its own closurestatus and formalstatus record, the
$\RegSeqRatUp$ face is read from its own closurestatus and formalstatus
record, and the $\RealUp$ face is read from its own closurestatus and
formalstatus record.

No aggregate formal-status token, fifth completion object, quotient stream,
selected limit, countable-choice row, metric-completeness datum, or standard
bridge row can replace any one of the four face records. In particular, a
completion-facing packet whose finite budget is accepted but whose evidence
collapses two of the four status records into a single aggregate token is not a
status-exact Real-and-Completion read.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First lock the ordered budget route. By
\autoref{thm:real-uniform-cauchy-completion-route-lock}, every accepted
completion-facing read using $U$ reaches the Real seal boundary only through
the displayed finite budget chain and the four Real L10 owners.

Next apply terminal determinacy over the four faces. The theorem
\autoref{thm:real-uniform-cauchy-four-object-terminal-determinacy} identifies
the completion-facing read with the finite terminal pullback determined by
$\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$, together
with the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$
support rows.

The status part is objectwise. The roadmap two-axis discipline records closure
and verification independently at each object, so the StreamName, DyadicRatCore,
RegSeqRat, and RealUp faces each contribute their own closurestatus and
formalstatus rows to the pullback. Reading one face's record gives no
coordinate for another face's record.

Therefore status exactness is equivalent to exposing all four objectwise status
records. The route lock and terminal pullback expose no aggregate formal-status
token, fifth completion object, quotient stream, selected limit,
countable-choice row, metric-completeness datum, or standard bridge row, so
none of those objects can fill a missing face.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real RegSeqRat--StreamName audit-clean handoff]
\label{thm:real-regseqrat-streamname-auditclean-handoff}
Every Phase Real and Completion consumer that reaches the $\RealUp$ seal
through an accepted $\UniformCauchyCriterionUp$ packet must pass through the
$\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, the
$\RegSeqRatUp$ regular readback row, and the local $\RealUp$ seal before it can
claim an audit-clean L10 handoff.

Equivalently, the handoff is readable only through the displayed
UniformCauchyCriterion rows, the four Real L10 row owners of
\autoref{def:real-l10-dependency-lattice}, and the shared $\BHist$, $\hsame$,
$\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support. Quotient streams, selected host
limits, host real equality, countable choice, and ambient completion have no
coordinate in the audit-clean handoff route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the route lock of
\autoref{thm:real-uniform-cauchy-completion-route-lock}. It already forces any
Phase Real and Completion read through the finite UniformCauchyCriterion chain
before a $\RealUp$ seal or completion-facing endpoint is exposed.

Then read the Real-side lattice. The dependency lattice of
\autoref{def:real-l10-dependency-lattice} names the four row owners used by
the route: $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$.

The exclusion boundary is inherited from the Real L10 source theorems.
\autoref{thm:real-l10-source-route-nonescape} removes quotient streams,
selected host limits, host real equality, countable choice, and ambient
completion from the source route, while
\autoref{thm:real-l10-terminal-source-lock} makes the Real seal terminal over
the displayed source rows.

Thus an audit-clean handoff cannot be claimed before the consumer has exposed
the StreamName finite window, DyadicRatCore tolerance ledger, RegSeqRat
readback, and RealUp local seal. Since none of the excluded host objects occurs
as a row in that route, none can supply an alternate handoff coordinate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real uniform Cauchy budget pullback exhaustion]
\label{thm:real-uniform-cauchy-budget-pullback-exhaustion}
Let $U=(I,W,M,Q,T,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\UniformCauchyCriterionUp$
packet read by a Phase Real and Completion consumer. The consumer reaches a
public $\RealUp$ exit if and only if its finite budget chain
$$
I \longmapsto W \longmapsto M \longmapsto Q \longmapsto T \longmapsto E
$$
pulls back to exactly the four L10 row owners: the $\StreamNameUp$ finite
window, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular
readback and classifier row, and the $\RealUp$ terminal seal.

This pullback exhausts the public exit surface. No quotient stream, selected
host limit, countable-choice selector, ambient-completeness row, or hidden
completion carrier supplies a fifth owner or an alternate Real exit.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First take the forward direction. By
\autoref{thm:real-uniform-cauchy-completion-route-lock}, any consumer that uses
$U$ to reach a $\RealUp$ seal or completion-facing endpoint has already passed
through the finite UniformCauchyCriterion budget chain
$I \longmapsto W \longmapsto M \longmapsto Q \longmapsto T \longmapsto E$.

Apply \autoref{thm:realup-l10-input-factorization} to the resulting public
$\RealUp$ read. The Real-side factorization admits the read only through the
L10 stack whose visible owners are $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$,
$\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$.

The row ownership is exact. The StreamName exit exactness theorem
\autoref{thm:streamname-four-object-exit-exactness} identifies the public
Real-completion exit with the finite StreamName window, the regular-source row,
the dyadic observation ledger, and the Real seal boundary. The RegSeqRat source
lattice exhaustion theorem
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-l10-source-lattice-exhaustion} assigns those rows to the
four displayed owners and leaves no unassigned source row.

For the reverse direction, assume the finite budget chain of $U$ pulls back to
exactly those four owners. The $\StreamNameUp$ window supplies the finite
source surface, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger supplies the dyadic
budget rows, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback and classifier row supplies the regular
source handoff, and the $\RealUp$ terminal seal supplies the public exit.

Recompose the same four rows using
\autoref{thm:streamname-four-object-exit-exactness}. The exactness theorem
packs the four-object route as a public Real-and-completion exit, while
\autoref{thm:realup-l10-input-factorization} verifies that this is the RealUp
input surface admitted by Phase Real and Completion.

Finally, the exclusions are inherited from the same route. The route lock
\autoref{thm:real-uniform-cauchy-completion-route-lock} and the three exactness
theorems just cited expose only the displayed finite rows, $\BHist$, $\hsame$,
$\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support. Since quotient streams, selected host
limits, countable choice, ambient completeness, and hidden completion carriers
are not rows of that packet, none can provide a fifth owner or a second public
$\RealUp$ exit.
\end{proof}
