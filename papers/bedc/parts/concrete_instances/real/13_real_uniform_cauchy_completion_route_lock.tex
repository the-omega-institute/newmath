\begin{theorem}[Real uniform Cauchy completion route lock]
\label{thm:real-uniform-cauchy-completion-route-lock}
Let
$$
U=(I,W,M,Q,T,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\UniformCauchyCriterionUp$ packet read by a Phase Real and
Completion consumer. If the consumer uses $U$ to reach a $\RealUp$ seal or a
completion-facing endpoint, then its route factors through
$$
I \longmapsto W \longmapsto M \longmapsto Q \longmapsto T \longmapsto E
$$
together with the $\StreamNameUp$ window row, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$
tolerance ledger, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular readback row, and the local
$\RealUp$ seal rows of \autoref{def:real-l10-dependency-lattice}.

The admitted route exposes only the finite packet rows $I,W,M,Q,T,E,H,C,P,N$
and the shared $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support
used by those sibling rows. It cannot export quotient streams, selected host
limits, host real equality, countable choice, open-cover compactness, ambient
completeness, or an additional completion carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the UniformCauchyCriterion finite-family seal obligation surface.
\autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-finite-family-seal-obligation-package}
and \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-finite-family-route-lock} display
the rows $I,W,M,Q,T,E,H,C,P,N$ and force the route from the finite index
ledger to the Real seal handoff through the single chain
$I \longmapsto W \longmapsto M \longmapsto Q \longmapsto T \longmapsto E$.

Now attach the completion-budget rows. The real completion budget lock
\autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-real-completion-budget-lock} places any
$\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ read after the same budget package, while the
completion-functor budget handoff
\autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-completion-functor-budget-handoff}
keeps functor-facing access inside the finite package and the regular
shared-tail sibling.

Next identify the Real-side dependency lattice. The L10 dependency lattice
\autoref{def:real-l10-dependency-lattice}, the source-route non-escape theorem
\autoref{thm:real-l10-source-route-nonescape}, and the terminal source lock
\autoref{thm:real-l10-terminal-source-lock} route every Phase Real or
Completion read through the StreamName, DyadicRatCore, RegSeqRat, and local
Real seal rows, with only $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and
$\NameCert$ support.

Finally compose the two boundaries with the UniformCauchyCriterion finite
budget envelope
\autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-real-completion-finite-budget-envelope}.
The UniformCauchyCriterion side supplies the ordered budget chain, and the
Real L10 side supplies the four sibling rows consumed by RealUp. Since the
cited non-escape and budget-lock theorems expose no quotient stream, selected
host limit, host equality, countable-choice row, open-cover compactness,
ambient completeness, or extra completion carrier, the Real-and-Completion
consumer has no route to export any of them.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real uniform Cauchy four-object terminal determinacy]
\label{thm:real-uniform-cauchy-four-object-terminal-determinacy}
Let
$$
U_0=(I_0,W_0,M_0,Q_0,T_0,E_0,H_0,C_0,P_0,N_0)
$$
and
$$
U_1=(I_1,W_1,M_1,Q_1,T_1,E_1,H_1,C_1,P_1,N_1)
$$
be accepted $\UniformCauchyCriterionUp$ packets read by Phase Real and
Completion consumers. Suppose the two reads expose the same finite family
ledger, shared $\StreamNameUp$ window family, modulus schedule,
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row, tail ledger, $\RealUp$ seal row, and the same
displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support rows.
Suppose also that their completion-facing boundaries expose the same four
faces
$$
\StreamNameUp,\quad \DyadicRatCoreUp,\quad \RegSeqRatUp,\quad \RealUp.
$$
Then the two completion-facing reads factor through the same terminal finite
BEDC pullback.

Conversely, every accepted uniform-Cauchy completion-facing read whose public
surface is the finite budget
$$
I,W,M,Q,T,E,H,C,P,N
$$
together with those four faces is determined by that finite budget and by the
four boundary faces. The resulting terminal read exports no quotient stream
family, no host real equality, no selected family limit, no ambient $\RealUp$
completeness theorem, no metric-completeness datum, no countable-choice row,
and no standard bridge row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First lock the finite uniform-Cauchy route. By
\autoref{thm:real-uniform-cauchy-completion-route-lock}, any Phase Real and
Completion consumer of an accepted $\UniformCauchyCriterionUp$ packet must pass
through the ordered rows $I,W,M,Q,T,E,H,C,P,N$ before it reaches either a
$\RealUp$ seal or a completion-facing endpoint.

Next apply the finite-family Real seal route determinacy theorem,
\autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-finite-family-real-seal-route-determinacy}.
Since the two reads expose the same family ledger, window family, modulus
schedule, dyadic tolerance row, and tail ledger, they reach the same finite
route through the seal rows $E,H,C,P,N$.

Now attach the completion boundary. The seal-completion boundary
\autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-real-seal-completion-boundary} keeps every
completion-facing consumer on the same finite public surface, and
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-completion-four-object-exit-route} identifies the
Real-and-Completion exit packet with the four displayed faces
$\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$.

With those faces fixed, use
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-completion-four-object-terminal-pullback}. The
terminal pullback theorem identifies any two accepted reads that expose the
same four faces and the same $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and
$\NameCert$ support rows. Hence the two completion-facing reads determine the
same terminal finite BEDC pullback.

For the converse direction, start with an accepted uniform-Cauchy
completion-facing read whose public surface is exactly the finite budget and
the four boundary faces. The route-lock theorem supplies the ordered
UniformCauchyCriterion budget; the seal-completion boundary places the read at
the Real seal boundary; the four-object exit route reads that boundary as the
StreamName, DyadicRatCore, RegSeqRat, and RealUp packet. Terminal pullback then
repackages the accepted read as the unique finite BEDC pullback determined by
those rows.

The exclusion clause is inherited from the same citations. None of the locked
budget rows or four boundary faces carries a coordinate for quotient stream
families, host real equality, selected family limits, ambient Real completeness,
metric-completeness data, countable choice, or standard bridge rows, so none of
that data can distinguish two reads after the finite budget and the four faces
agree.
\end{proof}
