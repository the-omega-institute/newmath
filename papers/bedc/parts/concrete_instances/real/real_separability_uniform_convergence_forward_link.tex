\begin{lemma}[Real separability to uniform convergence forward link]
\label{lem:real-separability-uniform-convergence-forward-link}
Every $\UniformConvergenceUp$ consumer of an accepted $\RealSeparabilityUp$ density packet first reads the $\CountableDenseSubsetUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ rows displayed by the separability carrier. In particular, the consumer reaches the uniform-convergence boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniform-convergence-namecert} only after the finite dense source, dyadic tolerance ledger, stream-window schedule, regular rational readback, terminal Real seal, componentwise transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows have all been exposed.

The forward link exports no selected dense point, quotient real equality, host dense subset, countable-choice search, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness coordinate.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier of \autoref{def:real-separability-carrier}. Its rows $Q,D,W,R,E,T,H,C,P,N$ expose the rational source, dyadic tolerance ledger, finite stream windows, regular rational readback, terminal Real seal, dense-source row, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming.

Use the completion-facing and density routes already displayed by the separability chapter. They place the $\CountableDenseSubsetUp$ boundary downstream of the dyadic and rational density rows, while the $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ rows remain the only route by which Real-facing consumers inspect the dense packet.

Now pass to \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniform-convergence-namecert}. A uniform-convergence consumer may reuse the density packet only through finite tolerance and window data, so it must read the same $\CountableDenseSubsetUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ rows before reaching its own uniform boundary.

Since the separability carrier contains no host dense subset, selected dense point, quotient real equality, countable-choice search, or ambient completeness row, the forward link supplies only the displayed finite lattice edge.
\end{proof}
