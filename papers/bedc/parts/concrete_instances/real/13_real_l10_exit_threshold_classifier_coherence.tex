\begin{theorem}[Real L10 exit-threshold classifier coherence]
\label{thm:real-l10-exit-threshold-classifier-coherence}
For any finite Phase $\RealUp$ and Completion packet over $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and the local $\RealUp$ seal rows, the following two data are equivalent to one displayed four-face record.

First, the packet has public classifier agreement in the sense of the Real L10 four-face classifier pullback and sheaf condition. Second, the same packet satisfies the objectwise exit threshold for the current Phase Real and Completion audit route. The displayed record carries exactly the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger rows, the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window rows, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regularity and common-tail rows, the local $\RealUp$ seal rows, and the support rows
$$
\BHist,\quad \hsame,\quad \Cont,\quad \Pkg,\quad \NameCert.
$$

The classifier coordinate and the exit-threshold coordinate are read from that same record, but neither coordinate supplies the other. The statement excludes quotient streams, host real equality, selected limits, countable choice, metric-completeness data, $\RealUp$ completeness, aggregate formalstatus rows, and aggregate bridge rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First read the classifier coordinate. Apply \autoref{thm:real-l10-four-face-classifier-pullback} and \autoref{thm:real-l10-four-face-classifier-sheaf-condition}. They identify public classifier agreement with the finite four-face surface over the DyadicRatCore ledger, StreamName finite-window rows, RegSeqRat regularity and common-tail rows, local Real seal rows, and the displayed support rows.

Next read the exit-threshold coordinate. Apply \autoref{thm:real-l10-objectwise-exit-threshold-equivalence}. It says that the current Phase Real and Completion exit threshold is exactly the objectwise threshold over the same four local face records, rather than a route-level aggregate status.

Now combine the two readings. The classifier theorems and the threshold theorem consume the same four concrete faces and the same finite support rows, so their conjunction is equivalent to the single displayed record carrying those rows. In the forward direction, the classifier agreement supplies the classifier face rows and the threshold equivalence supplies the objectwise audit rows on those faces. In the reverse direction, the displayed record feeds the classifier pullback and sheaf condition, and it also feeds the objectwise threshold equivalence.

It remains to show noninterference of the two coordinates. Apply \autoref{thm:real-l10-classifier-status-axis-separation}. Classifier agreement creates no formalstatus, leantarget, bridgestatus, or bridge row, while audit admissibility creates no classifier overlap, finite-window tightness row, pairwise terminal classifier equivalence, or triple common-tail cocycle.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:real-l10-face-status-noncollapse} and \autoref{thm:real-l10-face-leantarget-noncollapse}. These keep the status and Lean-target data attached to the four displayed faces and rule out an aggregate replacement. Since the cited routes export only the listed finite BEDC rows, no quotient stream, host equality, selected limit, choice principle, metric-completeness datum, $\RealUp$ completeness row, aggregate formalstatus row, or aggregate bridge row is available as an extra coordinate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real L10 classifier-threshold source conservativity]
\label{thm:real-l10-classifier-threshold-source-conservativity}
Let $\mathcal P$ be a finite Real L10 four-face packet satisfying both public classifier agreement and the objectwise exit threshold over $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and the local $\RealUp$ rows. Then the accepted source of $\mathcal P$ is exactly the displayed four-face record over $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, local $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$.

Adding the threshold coordinate does not create a fifth source, an aggregate $\formalstatus$ row, an aggregate bridge row, a quotient stream, a selected limit, a metric-completeness datum, a countable-choice row, or a $\RealUp$ completeness claim.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First collapse the hypotheses to one record. By \autoref{thm:real-l10-exit-threshold-classifier-coherence}, public classifier agreement and the objectwise exit threshold are read from the same four-face packet, whose only displayed sources are the DyadicRatCore ledger rows, StreamName finite-window rows, RegSeqRat regularity and common-tail rows, local Real seal rows, and the support rows $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$.

Next identify that record as the accepted source. Apply \autoref{thm:real-l10-four-face-classifier-pullback} to read classifier agreement as the public four-face surface, and apply \autoref{thm:real-l10-objectwise-exit-threshold-equivalence} to read the threshold objectwise on those same faces. The uniqueness comparison is then the source-uniqueness statement of \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-scale-window-real-exit-source-uniqueness}: the accepted Real exit source is the displayed DyadicRatCore-to-Real four-face route, not an additional carrier.

It remains to separate the source from verification and bridge coordinates. By \autoref{thm:real-l10-classifier-status-axis-separation}, classifier rows do not manufacture $\formalstatus$, leantarget, bridgestatus, or bridge rows, and the audit rows do not manufacture classifier overlap or finite-window tightness data. Thus adjoining the threshold coordinate preserves the four-face source and supplies no aggregate verification or bridge row.

Finally, every excluded object would require a coordinate absent from the four cited readings. Quotient streams and selected limits are not part of the finite $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support; metric completeness, countable choice, and $\RealUp$ completeness are not exported by the objectwise threshold; and an aggregate status or bridge row is ruled out by the axis-separation theorem. Hence the accepted source remains exactly the displayed four-face record.
\end{proof}

Thus every face record and every displayed support row is indispensable for the current Real L10 roadmap exit read. The cited routes provide no quotient stream, selected limit, countable-choice selector, metric-completeness datum, aggregate formalstatus row, aggregate bridge row, or $\RealUp$ completeness coordinate that could fill the missing position.
