\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RealSeparabilityUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-real-separability-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges' constructive treatment of the real line by rational approximants, and the Bourbaki description of separability by a countable dense subset, the $\RealSeparabilityUp$ packet records the finite BEDC surface at which rational density is available to $\RealUp$ consumers. It sits on the rational naming row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rat-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the finite observation windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, and the countable density boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-countable-dense-subset-namecert}. The chapter names rational dense enumeration as finite Real data; it does not introduce host real equality, choice over balls, quotient streams, or ambient completeness.

\begin{definition}[Real separability carrier]
\label{def:real-separability-carrier}
A $\RealSeparabilityUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(Q,D,W,R,E,T,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $Q$ is the $\RatUp$ enumeration row. It exposes rational candidates by finite index windows and records the order in which a density request may inspect them.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row attached to the same request window, supplying the dyadic precision against which the rational candidates are tested.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ observation-window row opened by $D$, so the consumer reads only the finite name segment required by the requested tolerance.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row, transporting the inspected rational candidate and window data into the regular rational route used by the real-name interface.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row. It records that the dense rational window is consumed as Real data only after $Q,D,W,R$ have already been displayed.
\item $T$ is the dense-window exactness ledger. It records that every density read first cites an index in $Q$, then a dyadic tolerance in $D$, then a finite window in $W$, then the regular readback in $R$, and only then the terminal seal $E$.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the rational enumeration, tolerance, window, readback, seal, exactness ledger, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ stores displayed $\Cont$ replay from a Real-facing density request through $Q,D,W,R,T$ and into the terminal seal row $E$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RealSeparabilityUp$, $\RatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\CountableDenseSubsetUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RealSeparabilityUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for host real equality, a choice-selected dense point, quotient stream equality, ambient completeness, trichotomy, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Real separability NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:real-separability-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RealSeparabilityUp$ carrier
$$
S=(Q,D,W,R,E,T,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\RealSeparabilityUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the rational enumeration, dyadic tolerance, StreamName window, RegSeqRat readback, Real seal, dense-window exactness ledger, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item rational enumeration exposure, requiring every density read to cite the displayed $\RatUp$ index row $Q$ before any tolerance, window, readback, or Real seal is consumed;
\item dense-window exactness, requiring $T$ to bind the same request through $Q,D,W$ so that the inspected rational candidate is tested only inside the opened finite window;
\item RegSeqRat handoff, requiring the finite rational data exposed by $Q,D,W$ to pass through $R$ before it can be read by the Real-facing boundary;
\item RealUp seal non-escape, requiring $E,P,N$ to export no host real equality, choice-selected dense point, quotient stream equality, ambient completeness, trichotomy, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{enumerate}
No separability obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:real-separability-carrier}. The tuple lists exactly the rational enumeration, tolerance, finite observation window, regular rational readback, Real seal, exactness ledger, and structural rows.

Rational enumeration exposure is forced by the placement of $Q$. A density request cannot inspect a candidate until the displayed $\RatUp$ index row has been read as finite packet data.

Dense-window exactness is the row $T$ governed by $Q,D,W$. It fixes the same rational index, dyadic tolerance, and finite StreamName window before any Real-facing read is admitted.

The RegSeqRat handoff is then the row $R$. It transports the inspected rational candidate and window data into the regular rational route, so the Real boundary receives a regular readback rather than a host real point.

Finally $E,H,C,P,N$ seal, transport, replay, source, and name only the displayed route. Since the carrier contains no coordinate for host equality, selected dense points, quotient streams, ambient completeness, trichotomy, Lean verification, or bridge checking, the listed obligations exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real separability rational dense handoff]
\label{thm:real-separability-rational-dense-handoff}
Every $\RealUp$ density consumer admitted by an accepted $\RealSeparabilityUp$ packet factors through the finite route
$$
\RatUp \longrightarrow \DyadicRatCoreUp \longrightarrow \StreamNameUp \longrightarrow \RegSeqRatUp \longrightarrow \RealUp
$$
before any separability read is exported. In particular, the consumer reads the same displayed rational enumeration row $Q$, dyadic tolerance row $D$, finite window row $W$, regular rational readback row $R$, and terminal seal row $E$ before it can consume the local $\NameCert_{\RealSeparabilityUp}$ surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a density consumer admitted by the carrier of \autoref{def:real-separability-carrier}. Projection exposes the finite rows $Q,D,W,R,E,T,H,C,P,N$.

The first analytic row is $Q$, the rational enumeration. The consumer therefore starts with a displayed $\RatUp$ index window, not with a choice-selected dense point or an ambient real value.

The tolerance and observation rows follow. The dyadic row $D$ supplies the requested precision, and the StreamName row $W$ opens exactly the finite window read at that precision.

The handoff to $R$ places the inspected rational candidate into the regular rational route. Only after that readback does the terminal seal $E$ present the datum as $\RealUp$.

The exactness ledger $T$, componentwise transport $H$, replay $C$, provenance $P$, and local naming row $N$ preserve this order. Hence every exported density read factors through $\RatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ before separability is consumed.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real separability dyadic-rational density handoff]
\label{thm:real-separability-dyadic-rational-density-handoff}
Every accepted $\RealSeparabilityUp$ density consumer whose tolerance row is served by an accepted $\DyadicDensityRealUp$ witness and whose interval witness is served by $\RationalDensityRealUp$ factors through the finite route
$$
\DyadicRatCoreUp \longrightarrow \DyadicDensityRealUp \longrightarrow \RationalDensityRealUp \longrightarrow \RatUp \longrightarrow \StreamNameUp \longrightarrow \RegSeqRatUp \longrightarrow \RealUp .
$$
The route uses only the displayed carrier rows $Q,D,W,R,E,T,H,C,P,N$ together with the dyadic-density surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadic-density-real-namecert} and the rational-density surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rational-density-real-namecert}. It exports no host real equality, choice-selected dense point, quotient stream, trichotomy, or ambient completeness witness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted $\RealSeparabilityUp$ carrier as
$$
S=(Q,D,W,R,E,T,H,C,P,N).
$$
The row $D$ is the only tolerance row exposed to the density consumer, while $Q,W,R,E$ are the rational enumeration, finite window, regular readback, and terminal Real seal rows consumed by the existing rational dense handoff.

Read the tolerance request through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadic-density-real-namecert}. An accepted $\DyadicDensityRealUp$ witness names the dyadic source inside $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ before the Real-facing window and readback rows are inspected, so the request supplies a displayed dyadic witness rather than a selected host real point.

Next read the interval witness through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rational-density-real-namecert}. The accepted $\RationalDensityRealUp$ packet turns the displayed interval and dyadic tolerance into a visible $\RatUp$ witness row, with the rational witness routed through its own finite window and ledger rows before any Real seal is available.

Compose those two witness routes with \autoref{thm:real-separability-rational-dense-handoff}. The consumer first sees the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ source, then the dyadic-density witness, then the rational-density witness, and only then the rational enumeration, StreamName window, RegSeqRat readback, and Real seal recorded by $Q,W,R,E$.

The remaining rows $T,H,C,P,N$ preserve the same order as exactness ledger, componentwise transport, displayed replay, provenance, and local naming. Since none of the three packets contains a coordinate for host real equality, a choice-selected dense point, quotient stream equality, trichotomy, or ambient completeness, the composed handoff cannot export any such data.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real separability finite-density exhaustion]
\label{thm:real-separability-finite-density-exhaustion}
Every admitted $\RealSeparabilityUp$ density read is exhausted by the displayed finite route
$$
\RatUp \longrightarrow \DyadicRatCoreUp \longrightarrow \StreamNameUp \longrightarrow \RegSeqRatUp \longrightarrow \RealUp \longrightarrow \CountableDenseSubsetUp .
$$
Equivalently, the consumer reads only the carrier rows $Q,D,W,R,E,T,H,C,P,N$ together with the finite rational-density and dyadic-density handoff surfaces before the local separability certificate is exported.

The route exports no choice-selected dense point, quotient stream, trichotomy row, ambient completeness witness, host real equality, selected nearest point, or detached dense subset.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:real-separability-carrier}. The rows $Q,D,W,R,E$ give the rational enumeration, dyadic tolerance, StreamName window, RegSeqRat readback, and terminal Real seal, while $T,H,C,P,N$ provide exactness, transport, replay, provenance, and the local naming row.

Apply \autoref{thm:real-separability-rational-dense-handoff}. It forces every density consumer through $\RatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ before any separability read is available.

Next apply \autoref{thm:real-separability-dyadic-rational-density-handoff}. The dyadic and rational density witnesses are inserted into the same finite route, so the density source is displayed by the $\CountableDenseSubsetUp$ boundary and not by an external dense point.

The structural rows $T,H,C,P,N$ preserve that order and add no analytic coordinate. Since the carrier and the two handoff routes contain no choice-selected dense point, quotient stream, trichotomy, ambient completeness, host real equality, selected nearest point, or detached dense subset, the displayed finite route exhausts the admitted density read.
\end{proof}
