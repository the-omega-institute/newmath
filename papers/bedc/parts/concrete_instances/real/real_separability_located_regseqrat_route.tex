\begin{theorem}[RealSeparability located RegSeqRat density readback]
\label{thm:real-separability-located-regseqrat-density-readback}
Every $\RealSeparabilityUp$ density-window consumer must pass through the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the $\LocatedRegSeqRatUp$ locatedness route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-located-regseqrat-namecert}, finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance cells, and the terminal $\RealUp$ seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert} before exporting a dense-read handoff. The exported handoff is finite regular-sequence and locatedness data; it is not quotient real equality, a selected dense point, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the RealSeparability carrier. Its density-window route names finite observations and regular-rational reads before any real-facing handoff is available.

Apply the RegSeqRat schedule route. The schedule supplies the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback and finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows, while the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance cells record the displayed comparison budgets consumed by the density request.

The located regular-sequence route then supplies the $\LocatedRegSeqRatUp$ locatedness data. Only after these rows are displayed can the handoff reach the terminal $\RealUp$ seal.

Thus the dense-read handoff is a finite readback route through regular sequences, locatedness, stream windows, dyadic tolerances, and the Real seal. Since the carrier exports none of the excluded coordinates, no quotient real equality, selected dense point, countable choice, or ambient completeness coordinate is exported.
\end{proof}
