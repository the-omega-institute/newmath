\begin{theorem}[Real L10 face status non-collapse]
\label{thm:real-l10-face-status-noncollapse}
Any current Phase Real and Completion audit read through the four faces
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ preserves
the four local closure records as separate face reports. No conjunction of
those records can be read as a new formalstatus token, a $\bridgeCheckedV$
aggregate, a $\RealUp$ completeness claim, or a standard bridge for the
composite exit route.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.RealUp.RealL10FaceStatusNoncollapse}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:real-l10-current-phase-exit-audit-pullback}. It identifies
the current exit audit as the product of the four local face records and
states that the exit packet contributes no fifth formal token or aggregate
bridge.

The two-axis convention of
\autoref{sec:roadmap-closure-and-verification-axes} reads closure and
verification status locally. A face report can therefore be cited as a face
report, but the conjunction of face reports is not itself a further
formalstatus entry.

Finally use the exactness and non-escape inputs named in the pullback proof:
\autoref{thm:real-l10-source-route-nonescape},
\autoref{thm:real-l10-dependency-lattice-interface-totality},
\autoref{thm:real-l10-four-face-exactness}, and
\autoref{thm:real-l10-streamname-exit-route-correspondence}. They keep the
composite exit route on the four displayed faces and provide no row for
RealUp completeness or for a standard bridge over the product.
\end{proof}

\label{thm:real-l10-face-axis-defect-localization}

$\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ does not

\autoref{thm:real-l10-face-axis-independence}. Then the defect is localized to

limit, countable-choice row, metric-completeness row, aggregate formalstatus,
aggregate bridge row, route-level leantarget, or $\RealUp$ completeness claim
can serve as the defect carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:real-l10-face-axis-independence}. The admissible coordinate
inventory is the displayed facewise list: paper closure, formalstatus,
leantarget, and bridgestatus over the four Real L10 faces. The same theorem
excludes route-level completion vocabulary and aggregate status vocabulary from
that inventory.

Apply \autoref{thm:real-l10-face-status-noncollapse}. It keeps paper closure
and formalstatus attached to the local closure records of
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$. Thus a
defect in either status coordinate is witnessed on at least one displayed face

Apply \autoref{thm:real-l10-face-leantarget-noncollapse}. It keeps leantarget
and bridgestatus attached to their source faces and supplies no route-level
target or aggregate bridge row. Thus a defect in either target coordinate is

Finally use the two-axis discipline of

formalstatus, leantarget, and bridgestatus are reported as separate axes, and

countable-choice row, metric-completeness row, aggregate formalstatus,
