\begin{definition}[Real name seal carrier]
\label{def:real-name-seal-carrier}
A $\RealUp$ name-seal carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
R=(D,S,Q,L,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observation row recording the endpoint, radius, and finite tolerance data available to the read;
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window schedule that exposes the requested observations;
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regularity row that records the pointwise regular-rational readback;
\item $L$ is the finite ledger tying the selected windows, dyadic observations, radius rows, and regularity checks to one request surface;
\item $E$ is the local $\RealUp$ seal row exposed only after $D,S,Q,L$ have been displayed;
\item $H$ records the componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $D,S,Q,L,E,C,P,N$;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes from the finite-window schedule through the dyadic and regularity rows to the seal row;
\item $P$ records $\Pkg$ provenance over $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RealUp}$ row for this name-seal packet.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a quotient stream, a selected host limit, host real equality, host sequence equality, or an ambient completeness theorem.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Real name seal source factorization]
\label{thm:real-name-seal-source-factorization}
Every $\RealUp$ seal read accepted through the carrier of \autoref{def:real-name-seal-carrier} factors through the displayed source route
$$
S,D,Q,L \longmapsto E
$$
together with the visible $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows. Equivalently, the seal may be read only after the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window schedule, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observation row, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regularity row, and the finite ledger have all been consumed through the same packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet through \autoref{def:real-name-seal-carrier}. The only seal coordinate is $E$, and the only source rows that lead to it are $S,D,Q,L$ with the transport, continuation, package, and naming rows $H,C,P,N$.

The route is the local RealUp form of \autoref{thm:real-regseqrat-streamname-lattice-route}. That theorem places the Phase Real and Completion seal path behind the StreamName, RegSeqRat, and DyadicRatCore sibling surfaces before the local seal is public.

The RegularCauchyName seal results provide the readback discipline used by the same boundary. Apply \autoref{thm:regularcauchyname-real-seal-consumer-scope}, \autoref{thm:regularcauchyname-real-seal-ledger-exhaustion}, and \autoref{thm:regularcauchyname-seal-readback-exhaustion}: a real-seal consumer is downstream of the displayed finite window, dyadic observation, regularity ledger, package provenance, and local NameCert rows. Repacking those rows as $D,S,Q,L,E,H,C,P,N$ gives the stated factorization.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real name seal NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:real-name-seal-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:real-name-seal-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\RealUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the finite packet $R=(D,S,Q,L,E,H,C,P,N)$;
\item classifier transport by the displayed $\hsame$ rows over the dyadic observation, finite-window schedule, regularity row, finite ledger, and seal row;
\item stability of the same route under the displayed $\Cont$ rows;
\item ledger exhaustion by $D,S,Q,L$ before the seal row $E$ is public;
\item source factorization through \autoref{thm:real-name-seal-source-factorization}.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from the displayed tuple. The packet lists every public row available to the local RealUp name-seal read, so the carrier and classifier have no hidden coordinate beyond $D,S,Q,L,E,H,C,P,N$.

Transport and stability are componentwise. The $H$ row moves the displayed source and seal rows, while the $C$ row replays the same finite route from the StreamName schedule through the dyadic observation, RegSeqRat readback, ledger, and seal.

Ledger exhaustion is supplied by \autoref{thm:real-name-seal-source-factorization} together with the RegularCauchyName exhaustion theorems cited there. Thus the local $\NameCert_{\RealUp}$ obligations are exactly carrier, classifier, stability, ledger, and source-factorization obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real name seal classifier stability]
\label{thm:real-name-seal-classifier-stability}
Let
$$
R=(D,S,Q,L,E,H,C,P,N)
\qquad\text{and}\qquad
R'=(D',S',Q',L',E',H',C',P',N')
$$
be accepted $\RealUp$ name-seal packets for the carrier of \autoref{def:real-name-seal-carrier}. Suppose the displayed source rows $D,S,Q,L$ and $D',S',Q',L'$ and the seal rows $E,E'$ are related componentwise by the $\hsame$ transports recorded in $H$ and $H'$. Suppose also that the displayed $\Cont$ routes in $C$ and $C'$ preserve the same source reads from the finite-window schedule through the dyadic observation row, the regularity row, and the finite ledger. Then the local $\RealUp$ seal classifier presents the same public seal row for $R$ and $R'$ after passing through the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observation, the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regularity row, the finite ledger, and the local $\RealUp$ seal.

This classifier equality has no coordinate for quotient streams, selected host limits, host equality, ambient completeness, metric-completeness data, or choice rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project both accepted packets through \autoref{def:real-name-seal-carrier}. The only analytic coordinates available to the classifier are the dyadic observation rows $D,D'$, the finite-window schedules $S,S'$, the regularity rows $Q,Q'$, the ledgers $L,L'$, and the seal rows $E,E'$; the rows $H,H',C,C',P,P',N,N'$ supply transport, replay, provenance, and naming data.

Apply \autoref{thm:real-name-seal-source-factorization} to both packets. Each public seal read factors through the displayed route from the StreamName window, DyadicRatCore observation, RegSeqRat regularity row, and finite ledger before reaching the local seal. Apply \autoref{thm:real-name-seal-namecert-obligations} to read the same route as the NameCert classifier-transport and route-stability surface.

Now compose the sibling faces of \autoref{thm:real-streamname-regseqrat-seal-route}. The StreamName face is fixed by \autoref{thm:streamname-public-finite-window-interface}; the DyadicRatCore face is fixed by \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-real-phase-source-coverage}; the RegSeqRat face is fixed by \autoref{thm:regseqrat-real-seal-handoff}; and the local Real face is fixed by \autoref{thm:real-stream-finite-prefix-exactness}. The componentwise $\hsame$ rows identify the corresponding displayed rows, and the $\Cont$ rows replay the same source reads through those faces.

The two accepted packets therefore present the same finite public route to the seal classifier, hence the same public seal row. Since the carrier and the cited faces expose only those finite BEDC rows, no quotient stream, selected host limit, host equality, ambient completeness theorem, metric-completeness datum, or choice row can enter the classifier comparison.
\end{proof}

\end{proof}
