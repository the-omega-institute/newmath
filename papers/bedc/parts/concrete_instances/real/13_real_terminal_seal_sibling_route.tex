\begin{theorem}[Real terminal seal sibling route]
\label{thm:real-terminal-seal-sibling-route}
Every public $\RealUp$ terminal seal read factors through the finite
$\StreamNameUp$ window, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, and the
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger before it reaches the local Real seal.
The route is the cross-chapter certificate route through
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert},
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}; its transport data
are only the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$
rows already exposed by those sibling chapters.

Consequently an accepted terminal seal read has no route that bypasses the
finite StreamName window, RegSeqRat readback, or DyadicRatCore tolerance
ledger. In particular it exports no host real equality, quotient-stream
equality, ambient completeness principle, countable-choice selector, selected
limit, or independent host-completion row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the handoff theorem
\autoref{thm:realup-regseqrat-streamname-seal-handoff}. It reads the public
$\RealUp$ seal as a package whose visible rows are the StreamName classifier
window, the RegSeqRat readback surface, the dyadic observation ledger, and
the local Real seal provenance. The sibling chapter references in the
statement are exactly the public sources of those rows.

Next apply the dyadic StreamName RegSeqRat route theorem
\autoref{thm:real-dyadic-stream-regseq-route}. It orders the exposed rows as
one finite BEDC path from $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observations through the
$\StreamNameUp$ finite window and $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback before the
$\RealUp$ seal is read. Since the route carries only $\BHist$, $\hsame$,
$\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ coordinates, it has no coordinate for a host
completion or quotient-stream equality.

Finally use the finite-window seal tightness theorem
\autoref{thm:real-regseqrat-streamname-tightness}. Tightness says that an
accepted terminal seal is admissible exactly when the three sibling rows
agree on one common finite request. Any proposed route omitting one of those
rows therefore fails to be an accepted public $\RealUp$ terminal seal, and
any route adding host real equality, ambient completeness, countable choice,
or a selected limit lies outside the displayed certificate surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real terminal seal four-face cover normal form]
\label{thm:real-terminal-seal-four-face-cover-normal-form}
Let $\{\mathcal R_i\}_{i\in I}$ be a finite family of public $\RealUp$
terminal seal reads. Suppose each local route exposes the same four faces
$$
\StreamNameUp,\quad \DyadicRatCoreUp,\quad \RegSeqRatUp,\quad \RealUp,
$$
where the $\StreamNameUp$ finite windows agree pairwise on their restrictions,
the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint, radius, and distance ledgers agree on every
overlap, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier and regularity rows agree on the same
finite request sections, the local $\RealUp$ seal-source rows agree on the
common boundary, and all routes use the shared support rows
$$
\BHist,\quad \hsame,\quad \Cont,\quad \Pkg,\quad \NameCert.
$$

Then the family descends to one finite-cover normal-form packet for the public
$\RealUp$ terminal seal. Conversely, any finite-cover normal-form packet with
exactly those four faces and support rows restricts to the local terminal seal
routes $\mathcal R_i$. The packet exports no quotient stream equality, host
real equality, selected limit, countable choice, ambient completeness,
metric-completeness datum, or standard bridge row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First read every local seal through the sibling route of
\autoref{thm:real-terminal-seal-sibling-route}. Each $\mathcal R_i$ is thereby
placed on the StreamName finite window, the RegSeqRat readback face, the
DyadicRatCore tolerance ledger, and the local Real seal-source row, with only
the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support.

Next build the finite-cover surface. Apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-terminal-fusion-finite-cover-cocycle-exactness} to the
pairwise StreamName window agreements. The common terminal fused packet is
then transported through the RegSeqRat finite-request section supplied by
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-finite-request-terminal-budget-factorization}.

Now transport the dyadic and Real boundary faces. Use
\autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-regseqrat-real-pullback-composition} to identify
the endpoint, radius, distance, classifier, regularity, readback, and
seal-source rows as one pullback surface over the same finite requests.

For the descent direction, apply
\autoref{thm:real-finite-cover-seal-pasting-criterion}. The pasted
StreamName surface, the shared dyadic ledger, the RegSeqRat classifier and
regularity rows, and the local Real seal-source rows therefore form one
finite-cover normal-form packet for the terminal seal.

For the restriction direction, start with such a normal-form packet and
restrict it to any window of the finite cover. The same pasting criterion and
the sibling route read the restricted packet back as the local terminal seal
route $\mathcal R_i$, because no additional coordinate is present beyond the
four displayed faces and the shared support rows.

The exclusion clause is inherited from the cited routes. None of them exports
quotient stream equality, host real equality, selected limits, countable
choice, ambient completeness, metric-completeness data, or a standard bridge
row, so those data cannot be recovered from either direction of the normal
form.
\end{proof}
