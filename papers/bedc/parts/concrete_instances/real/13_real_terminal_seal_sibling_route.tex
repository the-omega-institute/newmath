\begin{theorem}[Real terminal seal sibling route]
\label{thm:real-terminal-seal-sibling-route}
Every public $\RealUp$ terminal seal read factors through the finite
$\StreamNameUp$ window, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, and the
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger before it reaches the local Real seal.
The route is the cross-chapter certificate route through
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert},
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}; its transport data
are only the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$
rows already exposed by those sibling chapters.

Consequently an accepted terminal seal read has no route that bypasses the
finite StreamName window, RegSeqRat readback, or DyadicRatCore tolerance
ledger. In particular it exports no host real equality, quotient-stream
equality, ambient completeness principle, countable-choice selector, selected
limit, or independent host-completion row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the handoff theorem
\autoref{thm:realup-regseqrat-streamname-seal-handoff}. It reads the public
$\RealUp$ seal as a package whose visible rows are the StreamName classifier
window, the RegSeqRat readback surface, the dyadic observation ledger, and
the local Real seal provenance. The sibling chapter references in the
statement are exactly the public sources of those rows.

Next apply the dyadic StreamName RegSeqRat route theorem
\autoref{thm:real-dyadic-stream-regseq-route}. It orders the exposed rows as
one finite BEDC path from $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observations through the
$\StreamNameUp$ finite window and $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback before the
$\RealUp$ seal is read. Since the route carries only $\BHist$, $\hsame$,
$\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ coordinates, it has no coordinate for a host
completion or quotient-stream equality.

Finally use the finite-window seal tightness theorem
\autoref{thm:real-regseqrat-streamname-tightness}. Tightness says that an
accepted terminal seal is admissible exactly when the three sibling rows
agree on one common finite request. Any proposed route omitting one of those
rows therefore fails to be an accepted public $\RealUp$ terminal seal, and
any route adding host real equality, ambient completeness, countable choice,
or a selected limit lies outside the displayed certificate surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real terminal seal four-face status pullback]
\label{thm:real-terminal-seal-four-face-status-pullback}
Every public $\RealUp$ terminal seal read whose route factors through
$$
\StreamNameUp,\quad \RegSeqRatUp,\quad \DyadicRatCoreUp,\quad \RealUp
$$
reads its audit and status data exactly as the four objectwise closurestatus
records of those faces. The $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$,
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$, and local $\RealUp$ records keep their own closurestatus,
formalstatus, leantarget, and bridgestatus rows; the terminal seal route
creates no route-level $\formalstatus$ and no aggregate bridge row.

Conversely, any terminal seal read carrying those four face records together
with the displayed
$$
\BHist,\quad \hsame,\quad \Cont,\quad \Pkg,\quad \NameCert
$$
rows factors through the same terminal seal sibling route and through the same
four-face terminal pullback boundary. This equivalence does not assert
quotient stream equality, selected limits, countable choice,
metric-completeness data, or any $\RealUp$ completeness claim.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First locate the route. Apply
\autoref{thm:real-terminal-seal-sibling-route}. The public terminal seal read
is confined to the StreamName window, RegSeqRat readback, DyadicRatCore
tolerance ledger, and local Real seal row, with only the displayed $\BHist$,
$\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support.

Next read the status data objectwise. Apply
\autoref{thm:real-l10-four-face-status-lookup} and
\autoref{thm:real-l10-current-phase-exit-audit-pullback}. These identify the
audit boundary with the product of the four local closurestatus records, while
leaving each closurestatus, formalstatus, leantarget, and bridgestatus entry
attached to its own face.

For the converse, start with a terminal seal read carrying exactly those four
face records and the displayed support rows. Apply
\autoref{thm:real-l10-terminal-pullback-coherence}. The objectwise status
threshold and the shared StreamName, RegSeqRat, DyadicRatCore, Real,
$\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ coordinates repack the
read as the same terminal pullback boundary.

The exclusion clause follows from the same three cited results. None of their
routes contains a coordinate for a route-level $\formalstatus$, aggregate
bridge row, quotient stream equality, selected limit, countable-choice row,
metric-completeness datum, or $\RealUp$ completeness claim.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real terminal seal four-face cover normal form]
\label{thm:real-terminal-seal-four-face-cover-normal-form}
Let $\{\mathcal R_i\}_{i\in I}$ be a finite family of public $\RealUp$
terminal seal reads. Suppose each local route exposes the same four faces
$$
\StreamNameUp,\quad \DyadicRatCoreUp,\quad \RegSeqRatUp,\quad \RealUp,
$$
where the $\StreamNameUp$ finite windows agree pairwise on their restrictions,
the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint, radius, and distance ledgers agree on every
overlap, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier and regularity rows agree on the same
finite request sections, the local $\RealUp$ seal-source rows agree on the
common boundary, and all routes use the shared support rows
$$
\BHist,\quad \hsame,\quad \Cont,\quad \Pkg,\quad \NameCert.
$$

Then the family descends to one finite-cover normal-form packet for the public
$\RealUp$ terminal seal. Conversely, any finite-cover normal-form packet with
exactly those four faces and support rows restricts to the local terminal seal
routes $\mathcal R_i$. The packet exports no quotient stream equality, host
real equality, selected limit, countable choice, ambient completeness,
metric-completeness datum, or standard bridge row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First read every local seal through the sibling route of
\autoref{thm:real-terminal-seal-sibling-route}. Each $\mathcal R_i$ is thereby
placed on the StreamName finite window, the RegSeqRat readback face, the
DyadicRatCore tolerance ledger, and the local Real seal-source row, with only
the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support.

Next build the finite-cover surface. Apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-terminal-fusion-finite-cover-cocycle-exactness} to the
pairwise StreamName window agreements. The common terminal fused packet is
then transported through the RegSeqRat finite-request section supplied by
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-finite-request-terminal-budget-factorization}.

Now transport the dyadic and Real boundary faces. Use
\autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-regseqrat-real-pullback-composition} to identify
the endpoint, radius, distance, classifier, regularity, readback, and
seal-source rows as one pullback surface over the same finite requests.

For the descent direction, apply
\autoref{thm:real-finite-cover-seal-pasting-criterion}. The pasted
StreamName surface, the shared dyadic ledger, the RegSeqRat classifier and
regularity rows, and the local Real seal-source rows therefore form one
finite-cover normal-form packet for the terminal seal.

For the restriction direction, start with such a normal-form packet and
restrict it to any window of the finite cover. The same pasting criterion and
the sibling route read the restricted packet back as the local terminal seal
route $\mathcal R_i$, because no additional coordinate is present beyond the
four displayed faces and the shared support rows.

The exclusion clause is inherited from the cited routes. None of them exports
quotient stream equality, host real equality, selected limits, countable
choice, ambient completeness, metric-completeness data, or a standard bridge
row, so those data cannot be recovered from either direction of the normal
form.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real terminal seal four-face cover descent determinacy]
\label{thm:real-terminal-seal-four-face-cover-descent-determinacy}
Let $\mathcal P$ and $\mathcal Q$ be finite-cover normal-form packets for the
public $\RealUp$ terminal seal. Suppose both packets expose exactly the four
faces
$$
\StreamNameUp,\quad \DyadicRatCoreUp,\quad \RegSeqRatUp,\quad \RealUp,
$$
agree on all overlap restrictions for those faces, and use only the displayed
support rows
$$
\BHist,\quad \hsame,\quad \Cont,\quad \Pkg,\quad \NameCert.
$$

Then $\mathcal P$ and $\mathcal Q$ determine the same public $\RealUp$
terminal seal read. Conversely, equality of the public terminal seal read
recovers exactly the common $\StreamNameUp$ finite window, the
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint-radius-distance ledger, the $\RegSeqRatUp$
finite-request classifier and regularity rows, and the local $\RealUp$
seal-source row. It recovers no quotient-stream coordinate, host-real
coordinate, selected limit, countable-choice selector, ambient-completeness
principle, metric-completeness datum, or standard-bridge coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read both packets through the sibling route of
\autoref{thm:real-terminal-seal-sibling-route}. This places $\mathcal P$ and
$\mathcal Q$ on the same four public faces and confirms that their support
surface is exactly the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and
$\NameCert$ surface.

Apply the normal-form theorem
\autoref{thm:real-terminal-seal-four-face-cover-normal-form} to both packets.
The hypotheses on overlap restrictions put the two packets on one
finite-cover surface, because the StreamName windows, dyadic tolerance rows,
RegSeqRat readback rows, and Real seal-source rows are identified on every
common section.

The common section is determined by the finite cover itself. Use
\autoref{thm:streamname-terminal-fusion-finite-cover-cocycle-exactness} for
the fused StreamName window, and use
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-finite-request-terminal-budget-factorization} for the
RegSeqRat finite-request budget over that window.

It remains to transport the dyadic and Real rows across the common section.
Apply \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-regseqrat-real-pullback-composition} to the
endpoint, radius, distance, classifier, regularity, readback, and seal-source
rows. They are therefore the same pullback data for $\mathcal P$ and
$\mathcal Q$.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:real-finite-cover-seal-pasting-criterion}. The
pasting criterion says that a four-face packet with those overlap
restrictions has a unique public terminal seal read, so $\mathcal P$ and
$\mathcal Q$ determine the same read.

For the converse, start from equality of the public terminal seal read and
read it back through the same sibling route and normal-form theorem. The
restriction data are exactly the four recovered faces listed in the statement.
The exclusion clause follows because every cited route uses only the displayed
support rows and has no coordinate for quotient streams, host reals, selected
limits, countable choice, ambient completeness, metric completeness, or a
standard bridge.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real terminal seal finite-cover cocycle exactness]
\label{thm:real-terminal-seal-finite-cover-cocycle-exactness}
Let $\{\mathcal R_i\}_{i\in I}$ be a finite family of local terminal-seal
reads for the public $\RealUp$ surface. Suppose every $\mathcal R_i$ exposes
exactly the four faces
$$
\StreamNameUp,\quad \DyadicRatCoreUp,\quad \RegSeqRatUp,\quad \RealUp,
$$
with support rows
$$
\BHist,\quad \hsame,\quad \Cont,\quad \Pkg,\quad \NameCert,
$$
and suppose that for every overlap $i,j$ the restrictions of the
$\StreamNameUp$ finite window, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$
endpoint-radius-distance ledger, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ finite-request classifier
and regularity rows, and the local $\RealUp$ seal-source row agree. Assume
also that on every triple overlap $i,j,k$ the two restriction routes from
$\mathcal R_i$ to the common section coincide:
$$
(\mathcal R_i|_{ij})|_{ijk}=(\mathcal R_i|_{ik})|_{ijk},
\qquad
(\mathcal R_i|_{ij})|_{ijk}=(\mathcal R_j|_{ij})|_{ijk}.
$$

Then the family pastes to a unique public $\RealUp$ terminal-seal read whose
restriction to each finite cover member is $\mathcal R_i$. Conversely, any
public $\RealUp$ terminal-seal read restricts to such a finite cocycle on the
four displayed faces. This exactness exports no quotient stream equality, host
real equality, selected limit, countable choice, ambient completeness,
metric-completeness datum, or standard bridge row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First put every local read in four-face normal form. Apply
\autoref{thm:real-terminal-seal-four-face-cover-normal-form}; each
$\mathcal R_i$ is read as a finite StreamName window, a DyadicRatCore
endpoint-radius-distance ledger, a RegSeqRat finite-request classifier with
regularity rows, and a local Real seal-source row over the shared $\BHist$,
$\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support.

Next paste the StreamName face. The overlap and triple-overlap assumptions are
exactly the cocycle data used by
\autoref{thm:streamname-terminal-fusion-finite-cover-cocycle-exactness}, so
the finite windows fuse to one terminal StreamName packet and restrict back to
the given windows.

Transport the remaining faces over that fused window. Use
\autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-regseqrat-real-pullback-composition} to carry the
dyadic endpoint, radius, and distance rows together with the RegSeqRat
classifier, regularity, readback, and Real seal-source rows as one pullback
surface over the common finite requests.

Now apply \autoref{thm:real-finite-cover-seal-pasting-criterion}. The pasted
StreamName packet and transported pullback rows give a public terminal-seal
read, and its restriction to every cover member is the original
$\mathcal R_i$.

Uniqueness is the determinacy content of
\autoref{thm:real-terminal-seal-four-face-cover-descent-determinacy}. Any two
public reads with those restrictions have the same four faces on every overlap,
hence determine the same terminal-seal read.

For the converse, start with a public $\RealUp$ terminal-seal read and restrict
it along the finite cover. The normal-form theorem supplies the four local
faces, the StreamName fusion theorem gives the cocycle equations on triple
overlaps, and the pasting criterion identifies the recovered public read with
the original one.

The exclusion clause is stable under each cited step: none of the normal-form,
fusion, pullback-composition, pasting, or determinacy results introduces
quotient stream equality, host real equality, selected limits, countable
choice, ambient completeness, metric-completeness data, or a standard bridge
row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real terminal seal audit noncollapse]
\label{thm:real-terminal-seal-audit-noncollapse}
Let $\{\mathcal R_i\}_{i\in I}$ be a finite family of local terminal-seal
reads satisfying the hypotheses of
\autoref{thm:real-terminal-seal-finite-cover-cocycle-exactness}, and let
$\mathcal R$ be the resulting public $\RealUp$ terminal-seal read. Then the
audit rows visible from $\mathcal R$ over the four displayed faces
$$
\StreamNameUp,\quad \DyadicRatCoreUp,\quad \RegSeqRatUp,\quad \RealUp
$$
are exactly the objectwise closurestatus, formalstatus, leantarget, and
bridgestatus rows of those four faces. The finite-cover descent supplies no
aggregate route-level status row, no replacement leantarget, and no
standard-bridge row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:real-terminal-seal-finite-cover-cocycle-exactness}. The
public read $\mathcal R$ is obtained only by pasting the StreamName,
DyadicRatCore, RegSeqRat, and local Real terminal-seal faces over their common
$\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support.

Next read the audit coordinate through
\autoref{thm:real-l10-current-phase-exit-audit-pullback}. That pullback
identifies the current Phase Real and Completion audit report with the
objectwise product of the four face reports, so the pasted terminal-seal route
has no separate audit carrier beyond the displayed face rows.

Apply \autoref{thm:real-l10-face-status-noncollapse}. The closurestatus,
formalstatus, and bridgestatus rows therefore remain attached to their
respective faces and are not replaced by a route-level status row. Apply
\autoref{thm:real-l10-face-leantarget-noncollapse}; the same locality holds
for the leantarget rows.

Finally reuse the exclusion clause of
\autoref{thm:real-terminal-seal-finite-cover-cocycle-exactness}. Since the
descent has no coordinate for quotient streams, host reals, selected limits,
countable choice, ambient completeness, metric completeness, or a standard
bridge, the audit read cannot manufacture an aggregate bridge or verification
record.
\end{proof}
