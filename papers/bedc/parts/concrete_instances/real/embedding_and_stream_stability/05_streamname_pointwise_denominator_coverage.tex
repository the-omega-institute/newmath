\begin{definition}[Real unary selected denominator row]
\label{def:real-unary-selected-denominator-row}
For observation maps $s,t:\Hist\to\Hist$ and a certified unary observation
$n:\Hist$, the selected denominator row records the complete RatUp denominator
data at that observation:
$$
\begin{array}{l}
\mathsf{RealUnarySelectedDenominatorRow}(s,t,n)
\Longleftrightarrow\\
\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(s(n),t(n))\land
\mathsf{PositiveUnaryDenominator}(s(n))\land
\mathsf{PositiveUnaryDenominator}(t(n))\land\\
\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(s(n))\land
\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(t(n))\land
(\hsame(s(n),\emp)\to\mathsf{False})\land\\
(\hsame(t(n),\emp)\to\mathsf{False})\land
\forall z:\Hist,
((\hsame(s(n),\Ezero(z))\to\mathsf{False})\land\\
\hspace{3.3cm}(\hsame(t(n),\Ezero(z))\to\mathsf{False})).
\end{array}
$$
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Real unary StreamNameUp pointwise denominator coverage]
\label{thm:real-unary-streamname-pointwise-denominator-coverage}
For observation maps $s,t:\Hist\to\Hist$, assume
$$
\mathsf{RatStreamNameCarrier}(s)
\quad\text{and}\quad
\mathsf{RatStreamNameCarrier}(t).
$$
Then the following three clauses are equivalent, in the sense that any one of
them constructs the other two:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $\mathsf{RatStreamNameClassifier}(s,t)$.
\item $\mathsf{RealUnaryStreamClassifier}(s,t)$.
\item $\forall n,\ \mathsf{UnaryHistory}(n)\to
\mathsf{RealUnarySelectedDenominatorRow}(s,t,n)$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The StreamNameUp and Real unary classifier clauses are the same pointwise
RatUp classifier under the two carrier assumptions. Apply
\autoref{thm:real-unary-streamname-classifier-exactness} to identify them.

Assume the Real unary classifier and fix a certified unary observation $n$.
Evaluating the classifier at $n$ gives
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(s(n),t(n))$. Apply
\autoref{thm:rat-history-classifier-positive-denominators} to this row, then
apply \autoref{thm:positive-unary-denominator-unary-and-nonempty} separately
to the two positive denominator witnesses. This supplies the classifier, both
positive unary denominators, both unary endpoint rows, and both nonempty
exclusions.

It remains to fill the zero-headed exclusions. For an arbitrary history $z$,
transport the positive unary denominator witness for $s(n)$ along
$\hsame(s(n),\Ezero(z))$ by
\autoref{thm:rat-positive-unary-denominator-hsame-transport}, then contradict
\autoref{thm:rat-positive-denominator-e0-absurd}. The same argument for
$t(n)$ gives the right endpoint exclusion, so the selected denominator row is
constructed.

Conversely, assume the pointwise selected denominator rows. For each certified
unary observation $n$, project the first conjunct of
$\mathsf{RealUnarySelectedDenominatorRow}(s,t,n)$ to obtain
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(s(n),t(n))$. Repack this pointwise classifier
with the two carrier assumptions as $\mathsf{RatStreamNameClassifier}(s,t)$,
and use the exactness theorem again to recover the Real unary classifier
clause.
\end{proof}

Fit. Traditional target: certified rational observation streams for RealUp.
BEDC source: $\StreamNameUp+\RealUp+\RatUp$. Carrier:
$\mathsf{RatStreamNameCarrier}$. Classifier pair:
$\mathsf{RatStreamNameClassifier}$ and
$\mathsf{RealUnaryStreamClassifier}$. Stability: pointwise RatUp denominator
transport. Ledger: unary observation index and zero-headed probes. Core
theorem:
\autoref{thm:real-unary-streamname-pointwise-denominator-coverage}. Exactness:
the three-way equivalence. Standard bridge: finite rational observation windows
for RegSeqRat/RealUp. Status: $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat finite-window dyadic observation coverage]
\label{thm:regseqrat-finite-window-dyadic-observation-coverage}
For observation maps $s,t:\Hist\to\Hist$, histories $a,w,k:\Hist$, and
RegSeqRat stream certificate spines for $s$ and $t$, assume
$$
\mathsf{RatStreamNameCarrier}(s),\quad
\mathsf{RatStreamNameCarrier}(t),\quad
\mathsf{RatStreamNameClassifier}(s,t),\quad
\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(a),\quad
\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(w),\quad
\mathsf{UnaryOffsetLe}(k,w).
$$
Let $n=\mathsf{append}(a,k)$. Then the selected bounded observation supplies
the finite RealUp window and the dyadic endpoint rows:
$$
\begin{array}{l}
\mathsf{RealUnaryStreamWindowClassifier}(s,t,a,w)\land
\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(s(n),t(n))\land\\
\mathsf{DyadicRatCoreObservationRow}(s(n))\land
\mathsf{DyadicRatCoreObservationRow}(t(n)).
\end{array}
$$
Here $\mathsf{DyadicRatCoreObservationRow}$ is the package described in
\autoref{def:dyadic-rat-core-observation-row}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:real-unary-streamname-finite-window-coverage} to the two
carrier rows, the displayed StreamNameUp classifier, and the unary anchor and
bound. This constructs
$\mathsf{RealUnaryStreamWindowClassifier}(s,t,a,w)$.

Project the window at the selected offset $k$ by
\autoref{lem:real-unary-stream-window-endpoint-projection}. The witness
$\mathsf{UnaryOffsetLe}(k,w)$ supplies the bounded row, so the selected
observation $n=\mathsf{append}(a,k)$ carries
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(s(n),t(n))$.

Unfolding the offset witness gives $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(k)$. Unary append
closure with $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(a)$ gives
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(n)$, the certified observation index needed by the two
RegSeqRat spines.

Apply \autoref{thm:regseqrat-dyadic-observation-coverage} to the spine for
$s$ at $n$, and apply the same theorem to the spine for $t$ at $n$. These two
applications supply the DyadicRatCore observation rows for $s(n)$ and $t(n)$.
Together with the selected classifier and the finite window, these are exactly
the displayed package.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat finite-window dyadic source exhaustion]
\label{thm:regseqrat-finite-window-dyadic-source-exhaustion}
For observation maps $s,t:\Hist\to\Hist$, histories $a,w,k:\Hist$, and
RegSeqRat stream certificate spines for $s$ and $t$, assume
$$
\mathsf{RatStreamNameCarrier}(s),\quad
\mathsf{RatStreamNameCarrier}(t),\quad
\mathsf{RatStreamNameClassifier}(s,t),\quad
\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(a),\quad
\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(w),\quad
\mathsf{UnaryOffsetLe}(k,w).
$$
Let $n=\mathsf{append}(a,k)$. Then the selected bounded observation exhausts the
scoped DyadicRatCore source package at the two endpoints $s(n)$ and $t(n)$:
$$
\begin{array}{l}
\mathsf{RealUnaryStreamWindowClassifier}(s,t,a,w)\land
\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(s(n),t(n))\land\\
\mathsf{DyadicRatCoreObservationRow}(s(n))\land
\mathsf{DyadicRatCoreObservationRow}(t(n)).
\end{array}
$$
Moreover, the carrier, classifier, stability, and finite ledger faces read at
$s(n)$ and $t(n)$ are exactly the faces named by
\autoref{thm:dyadic-rat-core-scoped-source-package}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-finite-window-dyadic-observation-coverage}. It supplies
the finite $\RealUp$ window, the selected pointwise
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(s(n),t(n))$, and the two
$\mathsf{DyadicRatCoreObservationRow}$ witnesses at $s(n)$ and $t(n)$.

Next identify the source faces. Apply
\autoref{thm:dyadic-rat-core-scoped-source-package} to the two endpoint rows.
Its carrier face is the rational-history carrier together with positive unary
denominator and dyadic-radius data; its classifier face is the displayed
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$ row together with $\hsame$ transport; its
stability and finite ledger faces are the ones already recorded by the
RegSeqRat spine.

No further package data are read. The selected index $n=\mathsf{append}(a,k)$
comes from the finite RealUp window, and the endpoint source data are exactly
the two DyadicRatCore observation rows identified with the scoped package
faces. Thus the bounded RegSeqRat spine and the DyadicRatCore observation row
exhaust the scoped source package at both endpoints.
\end{proof}

Moreover, the carrier, classifier, stability, and finite ledger faces read at

\autoref{thm:dyadic-rat-core-scoped-source-package}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply the transitivity clause of
\autoref{thm:rat-stream-name-certificate-fields} to the two displayed

$\mathsf{RatStreamNameClassifier}(s,t)$ and

source package. Hence the composed finite-window observation carries exactly
the DyadicRatCore source package at the endpoints $s(n)$ and $u(n)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat finite-window dyadic transport package]
\label{thm:regseqrat-finite-window-dyadic-transport-package}
For observation maps $s,t,s',t':\Hist\to\Hist$ and histories $a,w,k:\Hist$,
assume RegSeqRat stream certificate spines for all four streams and
$$
\mathsf{RatStreamNameCarrier}(s),\quad
\mathsf{RatStreamNameCarrier}(t),\quad
\mathsf{RatStreamNameCarrier}(s'),\quad
\mathsf{RatStreamNameCarrier}(t'),
$$
$$
\mathsf{RatStreamNameClassifier}(s,t),\quad
\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(a),\quad
\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(w),\quad
\mathsf{UnaryOffsetLe}(k,w).
$$
Let $n=\mathsf{append}(a,k)$, and assume the selected pointwise transport rows
$$
\hsame(s(n),s'(n))
\quad\text{and}\quad
\hsame(t(n),t'(n)).
$$
Then the transported selected endpoints carry the finite-window dyadic package
$$
\begin{array}{l}
\mathsf{RealUnaryStreamWindowClassifier}(s,t,a,w)\land\\
\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(s'(n),t'(n))\land\\
\mathsf{DyadicRatCoreObservationRow}(s'(n))\land
\mathsf{DyadicRatCoreObservationRow}(t'(n)).
\end{array}
$$
Here $\mathsf{DyadicRatCoreObservationRow}$ is the package described in
\autoref{def:dyadic-rat-core-observation-row}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-finite-window-dyadic-observation-coverage} to the
untransported streams $s,t$, their RegSeqRat spines, the two
$\mathsf{RatStreamNameCarrier}$ rows, the displayed StreamNameUp classifier,
and the unary anchor, bound, and offset. This supplies the original
$\mathsf{RealUnaryStreamWindowClassifier}(s,t,a,w)$, the selected
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(s(n),t(n))$, and the DyadicRatCore observation
rows at $s(n)$ and $t(n)$.

Transport the selected classifier next. Apply
\autoref{thm:rat-history-classifier-hsame-transport} to the selected
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(s(n),t(n))$ and to the two pointwise
$\hsame$ rows. The result is the transported selected classifier
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(s'(n),t'(n))$.

It remains to expose the transported DyadicRatCore rows. The offset witness
gives the certified unary row for $k$, and unary append closure with
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(a)$ gives $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(n)$. Apply
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-dyadic-observation-coverage} to the RegSeqRat spine
for $s'$ at $n$, and apply the same theorem to the spine for $t'$ at $n$.
These two applications provide the dyadic-radius ledger tokens and endpoint
carrier faces for $s'(n)$ and $t'(n)$.

The denominator face is stable under the selected pointwise transports. From
the untransported DyadicRatCore rows, project the positive unary denominator
rows at $s(n)$ and $t(n)$, then transport them along
\autoref{thm:rat-positive-unary-denominator-hsame-transport} using
$\hsame(s(n),s'(n))$ and $\hsame(t(n),t'(n))$. These transported denominator
rows agree with the denominator components of the $s'$ and $t'$ DyadicRatCore
packages supplied by the previous paragraph.

Repack the original finite window, the transported selected classifier, and
the two transported DyadicRatCore observation rows. This is exactly the
displayed finite-window dyadic transport package.
\end{proof}

$$
Let $n=\mathsf{append}(a,k)$, and assume the selected pointwise transport rows

\hsame(s(n),s'(n)),\quad
\hsame(t(n),t'(n)),\quad
\hsame(s'(n),s''(n)),\quad

\mathsf{DyadicRatCoreObservationRow}(s''(n))\land
\mathsf{DyadicRatCoreObservationRow}(t''(n)).

$\hsame(s(n),s''(n))$ and $\hsame(t(n),t''(n))$ at the selected index.

Next apply
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-finite-window-dyadic-transport-package} with target

Finally identify the package faces. Apply
\autoref{thm:dyadic-rat-core-scoped-source-package} to the two endpoint rows

the finite ledger is the unary selected index together with the dyadic-radius
tokens stored by the RegSeqRat spines.
\end{proof}
