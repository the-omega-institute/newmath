\begin{definition}[RealUp obligation boundary]
\label{def:realup-obligation-boundary}
The RealUp obligation boundary is the package of six fields that must be
present before the interface name $\RealUp$ is used as a closed BEDC
obligation scope:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier data for $\mathsf{RealConstantHistoryCarrier}$,
$\mathsf{RatStreamNameCarrier}$, and the $\RegSeqRat$ spine;
\item classifier data for $\mathsf{RealConstantHistoryClassifier}$,
$\mathsf{RealUnaryStreamClassifier}$, and
$\mathsf{RatStreamNameClassifier}$;
\item pointwise $\hsame$ stability and classifier transport along selected
stream observations;
\item the $\mathsf{RealLedgerPolicy}$ finite observation ledger and the
dyadic endpoint ledger rows;
\item the $\RealUp$ seal attached to the rational stream certificate spine;
\item consumption of the $\RegSeqRat$ certificate spine by the $\RealUp$ seal
without reading any data outside the displayed carriers, classifiers,
stability rows, and ledger rows.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[RealUp carrier and classifier obligations]
\label{thm:realup-carrier-classifier-obligations}
At the RealUp obligation boundary, the carrier and classifier fields are
supplied by the already stated constant-history and stream-name packages:
$$
\begin{array}{l}
\mathsf{RealConstantHistoryCarrier}\quad
\mathsf{RatStreamNameCarrier}\quad
\mathsf{RegSeqRat\ spine\ carrier}\\
\mathsf{RealConstantHistoryClassifier}\quad
\mathsf{RealUnaryStreamClassifier}\quad
\mathsf{RatStreamNameClassifier}.
\end{array}
$$
These are exactly the carrier and classifier entries of
\autoref{def:realup-obligation-boundary}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First build the carrier face. Apply
\autoref{thm:real-constant-stream-carrier-reindexed-streamname-bridge} to
transport the constant real stream carrier into the stream-name carrier
presentation. This supplies the $\mathsf{RealConstantHistoryCarrier}$ and
$\mathsf{RatStreamNameCarrier}$ rows used by the boundary.

Next build the stream classifier face. Apply
\autoref{thm:real-unary-streamname-classifier-exactness}; under the two
stream-name carrier rows it identifies the pointwise
$\mathsf{RatStreamNameClassifier}$ and the Real unary stream classifier.

Finally build the constant-history classifier face. Apply
\autoref{thm:real-constant-history-classifier-e1-iff-rat} to read the
$\mathsf{RealConstantHistoryClassifier}$ row through the rational endpoint
classifier at the e-one observation. The three displayed applications are
precisely the carrier and classifier fields named in
\autoref{def:realup-obligation-boundary}.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RealUp stability and ledger obligations]
\label{thm:realup-stability-ledger-obligations}
At the RealUp obligation boundary, the stability and ledger fields are
supplied by the stream-name denominator packages, the pointwise Real unary
denominator coverage, and the finite Real ledger policy. In particular, every
certified unary observation carries the pointwise $\hsame$ transport,
classifier transport, positive denominator rows, finite-window denominator
rows, and dyadic ledger rows required by
\autoref{def:realup-obligation-boundary}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the pointwise stream-name stability. Apply
\autoref{thm:real-unary-streamname-pointwise-denominator-coverage}; it turns
the Real unary stream classifier and the stream-name carriers into the
selected denominator row at each certified unary observation. That row includes
the pointwise RatUp classifier and the denominator witnesses needed for
$\hsame$ transport.

Next restrict to a selected finite window. Apply
\autoref{thm:real-unary-streamname-finite-window-coverage} and then the
selected endpoint denominator packages in this sibling group. The selected
endpoint package supplies the bounded denominator rows, and the finite-window
dyadic observation coverage supplies the dyadic endpoint rows.

It remains to name the ledger face. The finite observations, dyadic comparison
witnesses, regularity certificates, and classifier comparison certificates are
the fields of \autoref{def:real-ledger-policy}. Combining that policy with the
pointwise and finite-window packages gives exactly the stability and ledger
entries named in \autoref{def:realup-obligation-boundary}.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RealUp seal and RegSeqRat consumption obligations]
\label{thm:realup-seal-and-regseqrat-consumption-obligations}
At the RealUp obligation boundary, the seal field and the RegSeqRat-consumption
field are supplied by the rational stream certificate spine. The resulting
package consumes only the scoped $\RegSeqRat$ spine, the dyadic ledger rows,
the finite-window dyadic source rows, and the displayed $\RealUp$ seal.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First attach the seal to the rational stream spine. Apply
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-realup-seal-consumes-stream-spine}; this is the seal
entry of the RealUp obligation boundary and records that the stream spine is
read through the $\RealUp$ interface.

Next identify the scope being consumed. Apply
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-stream-spine-scoped-package} to collect the carrier,
classifier, stability, and finite ledger faces of the $\RegSeqRat$ spine.
Apply \autoref{thm:regseqrat-dyadic-ledger-and-real-seal-obligation} to attach
the dyadic ledger and the RealUp seal to that same scoped package.

Finally exhaust the selected endpoint source data. Apply
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-finite-window-dyadic-source-exhaustion}; it states that
the selected finite-window observations read exactly the DyadicRatCore source
rows at the two endpoints. Therefore the seal consumes the displayed
$\RegSeqRat$ spine and no additional BEDC data, which is the last field of
\autoref{def:realup-obligation-boundary}.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RealUp obligation boundary package]
\label{thm:realup-obligation-boundary-package}
The six fields of \autoref{def:realup-obligation-boundary} are exhausted by
\autoref{thm:realup-carrier-classifier-obligations},
\autoref{thm:realup-stability-ledger-obligations}, and
\autoref{thm:realup-seal-and-regseqrat-consumption-obligations}. The package
reads only the displayed $\RealUp$, $\RegSeqRat$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RatUp$,
unary-observation, $\hsame$, and finite-ledger rows, with no quotient,
completion object, Classical datum, or external metric primitive.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read \autoref{def:realup-obligation-boundary} field by field.

Fields one and two are the carrier and classifier faces. They are exactly the
content of \autoref{thm:realup-carrier-classifier-obligations}: the
constant-history carrier, stream-name carrier, $\RegSeqRat$ spine carrier,
constant-history classifier, unary stream classifier, and stream-name
classifier are supplied by the displayed RealUp, StreamNameUp, RegSeqRat, and
RatUp rows.

Fields three and four are the stability and ledger faces. They are exactly the
content of \autoref{thm:realup-stability-ledger-obligations}: pointwise
$\hsame$ transport, classifier transport, positive-denominator rows,
finite-window rows, and dyadic ledger rows are read from the unary-observation
and finite-ledger packages.

Fields five and six are the seal and RegSeqRat-consumption faces. They are
exactly the content of
\autoref{thm:realup-seal-and-regseqrat-consumption-obligations}: the
$\RealUp$ seal is attached to the rational stream certificate spine and
consumes only the scoped $\RegSeqRat$ spine, dyadic ledger rows,
finite-window source rows, and displayed seal.

No remaining field is present in the definition. Hence the three named
theorems exhaust the boundary package, and the read surface is precisely the
listed BEDC data, not quotient representatives, completion objects,
Classical data, or an external metric primitive.
\end{proof}
