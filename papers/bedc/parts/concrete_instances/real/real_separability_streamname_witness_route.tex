\begin{theorem}[Real separability StreamName witness route]
\label{thm:real-separability-streamname-witness-route}
Every $\RealSeparabilityUp$ countable dense witness consumed by an L10 $\RealUp$ route must expose the $\StreamNameUp$ finite windows, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular readback, and the terminal $\RealUp$ seal before it is read as density data. The witness route is grounded in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}; it supplies no host dense subset, selected enumeration, quotient real equality, countable-choice selector, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the L10 certificate route of \autoref{thm:real-separability-l10-certificate-route}. It places the density read on displayed sibling rows rather than on a host dense subset or selected enumeration.

The $\StreamNameUp$ row supplies the finite windows at which the candidate dense witness is inspected. The $\RegSeqRatUp$ row supplies the regular rational readback attached to those windows, so the density datum is not available as a detached rational list.

Only after those two rows have been displayed can the route consume the terminal $\RealUp$ seal. Therefore every L10 separability witness read exposes StreamName windows, RegSeqRat readback, and the Real seal before it is read as density data, with all excluded choice and quotient coordinates remaining absent.
\end{proof}
