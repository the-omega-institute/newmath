\begin{theorem}[RealSeparability forward Cauchy-equivalence route]
\label{thm:real-separability-forward-cauchy-equivalence-route}
Every $\RealSeparabilityUp$ dense-read consumer that uses $\CauchyEquivalenceSetoidUp$ data factors through the finite classifier boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-equivalence-setoid-namecert}. The route first displays the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback rows, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance rows, the finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows, and the terminal $\RealUp$ seal before exporting a dense-read surface. Thus the Cauchy-equivalence classifier is consumed as finite BEDC data, not as quotient stream equality, selected dense enumeration, countable choice, host real equality, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the RealSeparability hub through its existing dense-read children. Those files expose finite stream windows, regular rational readback, dyadic tolerance rows, and the terminal real seal before any dense surface is exported.

Only after those rows are displayed may a consumer call the Cauchy-equivalence classifier. The classifier boundary is therefore the chapter named by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-equivalence-setoid-namecert}, with $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, and $\RealUp$ providing the finite route into it.

Since the route has no coordinate for quotient stream equality, selected dense enumeration, countable choice, host real equality, or ambient completeness, it exports only the displayed dense-read classifier data.
\end{proof}
