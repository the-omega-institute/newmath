\section{StreamName and RegSeqRat dependency route}
\label{sec:real-streamname-regseqrat-dependency-route}

\begin{theorem}[Real seal StreamName-RegSeqRat dependency route]
\label{thm:real-streamname-regseqrat-dependency-route}
Every $\RealUp$ seal read consumes the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert} before it reads the regularity and seal rows supplied by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}. Thus the real-name carrier is routed through finite scheduled observations and regular rational-source ledgers before any completion-facing consumer can use the $\RealUp$ seal.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The Real carrier of \autoref{def:real-carrier} is specified as a sealed regular rational stream name. Its observations are StreamName rows, and its regularity certificate is exposed through the RegSeqRat source surface.

Project the StreamName surface first. A finite observation request fixes the scheduled window through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, so the later seal read cannot introduce an unnamed observation stream.

Then project the RegSeqRat surface. The regularity and classifier rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert} supply the rational-source ledger consumed by the Real seal, so a completion-facing Real read is downstream of both sibling packets.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real seal DyadicRatCore-StreamName-RegSeqRat dependency route]
\label{thm:real-dyadic-streamname-regseqrat-dependency-route}
Every accepted current-phase $\RealUp$ seal read factors through the ordered finite route
$$
\DyadicRatCoreUp \to \StreamNameUp \to \RegSeqRatUp \to \RealUp .
$$
The DyadicRatCore endpoint and radius ledger is read before the StreamName finite-window schedule, the RegSeqRat regularity and classifier rows, and the local Real seal. The route exports no quotient streams, selected limits, host real equality, ambient completeness, metric-completeness rows, countable choice, or Real completeness coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the Real carrier of \autoref{def:real-carrier}. The preceding dependency route, \autoref{thm:real-streamname-regseqrat-dependency-route}, places every Real seal read downstream of the StreamName finite-window rows and the RegSeqRat regular-rational source rows.

Insert the DyadicRatCore face before those rows. The RegSeqRat bridge handoff exactness of \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-regseqrat-bridge-handoff-exactness} says that the RegSeqRat finite-window handoff consumes the displayed DyadicRatCore endpoint and radius ledger, while the StreamName four-object terminal pullback of \autoref{thm:streamname-real-completion-four-object-terminal-pullback} keeps the DyadicRatCore, StreamName, RegSeqRat, and local Real faces in one finite terminal packet.

Finally apply Real L10 finite-window witness determinacy, \autoref{thm:real-l10-finite-window-witness-determinacy}. It identifies the public seal boundary with exactly the fused StreamName terminal-window packet, the DyadicRatCore endpoint and radius ledger, the RegSeqRat finite-request section, the local Real seal, and the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows. Hence the accepted current-phase seal read has the stated ordered route and none of the excluded quotient, selected-limit, host-equality, completeness, metric-completeness, choice, or Real-completeness coordinates.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real L10 four-face route order uniqueness]
\label{thm:real-l10-four-face-route-order-uniqueness}
Every current-phase terminal $\RealUp$ seal section whose displayed rows are the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint and radius ledger, the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window schedule, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier, regularity, and transport rows, and the public $\RealUp$ seal row has
$$
\DyadicRatCoreUp \to \StreamNameUp \to \RegSeqRatUp \to \RealUp
$$
as its unique BEDC route. Any attempted route omitting or permuting one face either loses one of the displayed rows or introduces an off-table coordinate, namely quotient stream equality, a selected limit, host real equality, ambient completeness, metric-completeness, countable choice, or a second $\RealUp$ seal coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The forward order is the route of \autoref{thm:real-dyadic-streamname-regseqrat-dependency-route}. It already reads the DyadicRatCore endpoint and radius ledger before the StreamName finite-window schedule, then the RegSeqRat regularity and classifier rows, and finally the local Real seal.

The order of the first three faces cannot be permuted without dropping a displayed row. The DyadicRatCore handoff exactness of \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-regseqrat-bridge-handoff-exactness} makes the endpoint and radius ledger the finite handoff read by RegSeqRat, while the StreamName four-object terminal pullback of \autoref{thm:streamname-real-completion-four-object-terminal-pullback} keeps the DyadicRatCore, StreamName, RegSeqRat, and Real faces in one terminal packet.

The public seal boundary is fixed by Real L10 finite-window witness determinacy, \autoref{thm:real-l10-finite-window-witness-determinacy}. RegSeqRat terminal-section exhaustion, \autoref{thm:regseqrat-real-seal-terminal-section-exhaustion}, rules out a fifth-face witness or a shortcut witness between the regular rational section and the Real seal. Therefore every alternative route either omits one of the four displayed faces or leaves the table by adding one of the excluded quotient, selected-limit, host-equality, completeness, choice, or duplicate-seal coordinates.
\end{proof}
