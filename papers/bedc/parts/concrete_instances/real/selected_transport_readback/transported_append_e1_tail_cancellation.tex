\begin{theorem}[Real stream classifier transported selected appended e-one tail cancellation]
\label{thm:real-stream-classifier-transported-selected-append-e1-tail-hsame-cancel}
For rational-observation streams $x,x',y,y':\mathbb{N}\to\Hist$, index $n$,
and histories $d,e,tail_D,tail_E$, if the source stream endpoints are
classified and the transported selected endpoints display as appended e-one
tails, then tail equality cancels the transported displayed tails and recovers
the denominator head comparison:
$$
\begin{array}{l}
(\forall i,\ \hsame(x(i),x'(i)))\Longrightarrow
(\forall i,\ \hsame(y(i),y'(i)))\Longrightarrow\\
\qquad
\mathsf{RealStreamClassifier}(x,y)\Longrightarrow
\hsame(x'(n),\Eone(d\cdot\Eone(tail_D)))\Longrightarrow\\
\qquad
\hsame(y'(n),\Eone(e\cdot\Eone(tail_E)))\Longrightarrow
\hsame(tail_D,tail_E)\Longrightarrow
\hsame(d,e).
\end{array}
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Evaluate $\mathsf{RealStreamClassifier}(x,y)$ at the selected index $n$ to
obtain the RatUp point classifier for $x(n)$ and $y(n)$. Use the two pointwise
$\hsame$ rows to transport that classifier to the endpoints $x'(n)$ and
$y'(n)$.

Transport the RatUp point classifier again across the two displayed appended
e-one seals. This gives a RatUp classifier between
$\Eone(d\cdot\Eone(tail_D))$ and $\Eone(e\cdot\Eone(tail_E))$.

Apply RatUp e-one tail readback to compare
$d\cdot\Eone(tail_D)$ with $e\cdot\Eone(tail_E)$. The supplied tail comparison
identifies the two e-one suffixes, and
\autoref{thm:append-hsame-right-context-cancel} cancels the common suffix to
leave exactly $\hsame(d,e)$.
\end{proof}
