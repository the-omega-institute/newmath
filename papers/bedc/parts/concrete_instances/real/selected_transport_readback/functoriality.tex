\begin{theorem}[Real selected transport readback functoriality]
\label{thm:real-selected-transport-readback-functoriality}
Let $W_0$ be a finite $\RealUp$ observation window with finite probe bundle
$\mathcal{P}:\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ and selected bundled observation
$n:\Hist$ with $\mathsf{InBundle}(n,\mathcal{P})$. Suppose two displayed
unary-context transports
$$
\mathcal{T}_{01}:W_0\to W_1,\qquad
\mathcal{T}_{12}:W_1\to W_2
$$
are composable over the same selected $\StreamNameUp$ source, and suppose their
composite transport $\mathcal{T}_{02}$ is the displayed unary-context
composition of $\mathcal{T}_{01}$ and $\mathcal{T}_{12}$. Assume each of
$\mathcal{T}_{01}$, $\mathcal{T}_{12}$, and $\mathcal{T}_{02}$ preserves the
visible $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRat$, DyadicRatCore,
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, $\mathsf{InBundle}$,
$\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\mathsf{RealLedgerPolicy}$ rows at the selected
finite window.

Then the selected readback after $\mathcal{T}_{02}$ is the same finite
$\RegSeqRat$ tail-classifier row as the stagewise readback obtained by first
reading after $\mathcal{T}_{01}$ and then reading after $\mathcal{T}_{12}$.
Equivalently, the following two displayed packets expose one selected
tail-classifier row:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item the endpoint package, contextual package, appended e-one tail package,
  common e-one certified-shape envelope, cancellation envelope, and selected
  tail-class exactness read after $\mathcal{T}_{02}$;
  \item the same six readback packages read after $\mathcal{T}_{01}$ and then
  transported through $\mathcal{T}_{12}$.
\end{enumerate}
The common row is determined only by the displayed
$\StreamNameUp/\RegSeqRat$/DyadicRatCore finite-window surface, the
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ and $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ membership rows, the
$\hsame/\Cont/\Pkg$ ledger, and the finite $\mathsf{RealLedgerPolicy}$ row. It
exports no quotient, completion, Classical representative,
metric-completeness, arithmetic or order, standard-bridge, or hidden selector
row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First read the selected endpoints through the composite transport. Apply the
selected endpoint package
\autoref{thm:real-stream-classifier-transport-selected-endpoint-common-e1-tail-readback}
and the transported contextual package
\autoref{thm:real-stream-classifier-transported-contextual-full-endpoint-package}
to $\mathcal{T}_{02}$. These give the transported selected endpoint rows, the
contextual continuation rows, and the common e-one tail comparison at $W_2$.

Next enrich that composite readback with the displayed endpoint certificates.
Use
\autoref{thm:real-stream-classifier-transported-contextual-displayed-e1-appended-tail-exclusion-package}
and
\autoref{thm:real-stream-classifier-transport-selected-common-e1-certified-shape-envelope}
to attach the appended-tail exclusions, positive-denominator rows, unary
endpoint rows, and the common certified-shape envelope. Apply
\autoref{thm:real-stream-classifier-transported-selected-certified-e1-tail-cancellation-envelope}
to identify the displayed appended e-one denominator heads.

Now perform the same read in two stages. The first transport
$\mathcal{T}_{01}$ supplies the same endpoint and contextual rows at $W_1$.
The second transport $\mathcal{T}_{12}$ preserves exactly the same
$\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRat$, DyadicRatCore, $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$,
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, $\mathsf{InBundle}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and
$\mathsf{RealLedgerPolicy}$ rows, so the stagewise packet has the same six
certificate faces as the composite packet.

Finally apply
\autoref{thm:real-stream-classifier-transported-selected-tail-class-exactness}
to both packets. Tail-class exactness says that a selected readback is
classified only by the displayed e-one tail class and its finite
$\RegSeqRat$ classifier row. Since the endpoint, contextual, appended-tail,
common-shape, cancellation, and selected-tail exactness faces agree under the
preserved ledger, the composite readback and the stagewise readback expose the
same finite tail-classifier row.

The exclusion clause follows from the same readback surface. None of the cited
packages contains a quotient carrier, completion object, Classical
representative, metric-completeness certificate, arithmetic or order
operation, standard-bridge datum, or hidden selector row, and the composite
transport is formed inside the displayed unary-context ledger.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real selected transport readback identity]
\label{thm:real-selected-transport-readback-identity}
Let $W$ be a finite $\RealUp$ observation window with finite probe bundle
$\mathcal{P}:\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ and selected bundled observation
$n:\Hist$ with $\mathsf{InBundle}(n,\mathcal{P})$. Suppose the displayed
unary-context transport
$$
\mathcal{I}_W:W\to W
$$
is identity on the visible $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRat$, DyadicRatCore,
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, $\mathsf{InBundle}$,
$\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\mathsf{RealLedgerPolicy}$ rows at this
selected finite window.

Then the selected readback through $\mathcal{I}_W$ exposes exactly the
original finite $\RegSeqRat$ tail-classifier row attached to $n$ in $W$.
The readback exports no quotient, completion, metric-completeness, standard
bridge, arithmetic or order structure, hidden selector, or additional endpoint
row beyond the displayed finite-window ledger.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Specialize \autoref{thm:real-selected-transport-readback-functoriality} to the
identity composite $\mathcal{I}_W=\mathcal{I}_W\circ\mathcal{I}_W$. The
identity hypotheses make the composite packet and the stagewise packet use the
same visible $\StreamNameUp/\RegSeqRat$/DyadicRatCore surface and the same
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, $\mathsf{InBundle}$,
$\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\mathsf{RealLedgerPolicy}$ rows.

The endpoint package, contextual package, common-shape envelope, cancellation
envelope, and selected-tail exactness rows cited in the functoriality theorem
therefore read back the endpoint package already present at $W$. Since
selected-tail exactness identifies a selected readback only through the
displayed e-one tail class and its finite $\RegSeqRat$ classifier, the
identity transport cannot change the tail-classifier row.

The exclusion clause is inherited from the same finite ledger projections.
Each cited package is a projection of the finite observation window and none
contains a quotient carrier, completion object, metric-completeness
certificate, standard bridge, arithmetic or order operation, hidden selector,
or additional endpoint row.
\end{proof}
