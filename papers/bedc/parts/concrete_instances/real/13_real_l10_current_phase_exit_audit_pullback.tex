\section{Real L10 current phase exit audit pullback}
\label{sec:real-l10-current-phase-exit-audit-pullback}

\begin{theorem}[Real L10 four-face status lookup]
\label{thm:real-l10-four-face-status-lookup}
Any accepted current Phase Real and Completion exit read through
$$
\DyadicRatCoreUp \to \StreamNameUp \to \RegSeqRatUp \to \RealUp
$$
reads four separate local closurestatus records. The $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ face
contributes $\matureClosure$ with $\axiomCleanV$ and paperBridge status; the
$\StreamNameUp$ face contributes $\matureClosure$ with $\bridgeCheckedV$; the
$\RegSeqRatUp$ face contributes $\matureClosure$ with $\axiomCleanV$ and
paperBridge status; and the $\RealUp$ face contributes $\matureClosure$ with
$\bridgeCheckedV$.

These four entries are objectwise face records. They are not an aggregate
formalstatus token, an aggregate bridge row, or a route-level closurestatus
record for the composite exit.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The four faces are the Real L10 faces named by
\autoref{def:real-l10-dependency-lattice}. The local records are read from the
same concrete chapters that supply the face data:
\autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-standard-source-bridge} for
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$, \autoref{thm:streamname-public-interface-export-package},
\autoref{thm:streamname-standard-sequence-bridge}, and
\autoref{thm:streamname-scoped-interface-package} for $\StreamNameUp$,
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-real-seal-handoff} for $\RegSeqRatUp$, and
\autoref{thm:realup-phase-real-completion-boundary} for $\RealUp$.

The closurestatus block attached to the DyadicRatCore handoff records
$\matureClosure$, $\axiomCleanV$, and paperBridge status. The StreamName public
interface closurestatus records $\matureClosure$ and $\bridgeCheckedV$. The
RegSeqRat real-seal boundary closurestatus records $\matureClosure$,
$\axiomCleanV$, and paperBridge status. The RealUp certificate-boundary
closurestatus records $\matureClosure$ and $\bridgeCheckedV$.

By the two-axis convention of
\autoref{sec:roadmap-closure-and-verification-axes}, a closure entry and a
verification entry remain attached to the object whose closurestatus block
contains them. Reading the four faces therefore produces four objectwise face
records and does not create a fifth formalstatus token, route-level bridge row,
or composite closurestatus record.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real L10 current phase exit audit pullback]
\label{thm:real-l10-current-phase-exit-audit-pullback}
For any accepted current Phase Real and Completion exit read through the four
faces
$$
\DyadicRatCoreUp \to \StreamNameUp \to \RegSeqRatUp \to \RealUp
$$
of the $\RealUp$ L10 dependency lattice, the audit boundary is exactly the
product of the four local face records. The $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ and
$\RegSeqRatUp$ faces contribute their $\axiomCleanV$ BEDC-side finite-route
records; the $\StreamNameUp$ and $\RealUp$ faces contribute their
$\bridgeCheckedV$ records.

The exit packet contributes no fifth formal token, no aggregate bridge, no
$\RealUp$ completeness claim, no quotient stream, no selected limit, and no
metric-completeness datum. Its only admissible audit read is the displayed
four-face product, with each status entry imported by reference from the
corresponding local closure record.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The four faces are the faces named by
\autoref{def:real-l10-dependency-lattice}. Apply
\autoref{thm:real-l10-source-route-nonescape} and
\autoref{thm:real-l10-dependency-lattice-interface-totality} to confine every
accepted current Phase Real and Completion interface read to the displayed
DyadicRatCore, StreamName, RegSeqRat, and local Real rows.

Next apply \autoref{thm:real-l10-four-face-exactness}. It identifies
acceptance with the finite packet over exactly those four faces, carried only
by the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$
coordinates. The StreamName exit correspondence,
\autoref{thm:real-l10-streamname-exit-route-correspondence}, names the same
packet as the current Phase Real and Completion exit route.

It remains to read the audit status. Apply
\autoref{thm:real-l10-four-face-status-lookup}. It reads the DyadicRatCore,
StreamName, RegSeqRat, and RealUp closurestatus entries as four objectwise face
records and excludes any aggregate formalstatus token or aggregate bridge row.

Therefore the exit audit is the product of the four cited local records. Since
the exactness and correspondence theorems export no fifth face, no quotient
stream, no selected limit, no Real completeness row, no metric-completeness
row, and no aggregate bridge coordinate, the exit packet cannot contribute any
additional audit datum.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real L10 face status non-collapse]
\label{thm:real-l10-face-status-noncollapse}
Any current Phase Real and Completion audit read through the four faces
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ preserves
the four local closure records as separate face reports. No conjunction of
those records can be read as a new formalstatus token, a $\bridgeCheckedV$
aggregate, a $\RealUp$ completeness claim, or a standard bridge for the
composite exit route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:real-l10-current-phase-exit-audit-pullback}. It identifies
the current exit audit as the product of the four local face records and
states that the exit packet contributes no fifth formal token or aggregate
bridge.

The two-axis convention of
\autoref{sec:roadmap-closure-and-verification-axes} reads closure and
verification status locally. A face report can therefore be cited as a face
report, but the conjunction of face reports is not itself a further
formalstatus entry.

Finally use the exactness and non-escape inputs named in the pullback proof:
\autoref{thm:real-l10-source-route-nonescape},
\autoref{thm:real-l10-dependency-lattice-interface-totality},
\autoref{thm:real-l10-four-face-exactness}, and
\autoref{thm:real-l10-streamname-exit-route-correspondence}. They keep the
composite exit route on the four displayed faces and provide no row for
RealUp completeness or for a standard bridge over the product.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real L10 face Lean target non-collapse]
\label{thm:real-l10-face-leantarget-noncollapse}
Any accepted current Phase Real and Completion audit read through
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ preserves
each face's Lean-target row and bridge-status row as local records of that
face. No $\bridgeCheckedV$ face can supply the Lean target or bridge row for
another face, and the composite route creates neither a route-level Lean
target nor an aggregate bridge row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:real-l10-current-phase-exit-audit-pullback}. It reads the
accepted exit audit as the product of the four local face records over the
displayed Real L10 dependency lattice.

Apply \autoref{thm:real-l10-face-status-noncollapse}. It keeps the closure
and verification reports objectwise, so a $\bridgeCheckedV$ entry is a record
of its own face rather than a credential for a different face.

Now project the target and bridge rows along the same exactness data used by
the pullback. The source-route non-escape, interface-totality, four-face
exactness, and StreamName exit-correspondence inputs provide coordinates only
for $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$.

Since those inputs provide no fifth face, no route-level Lean-target
coordinate, and no aggregate bridge coordinate, the local Lean-target and
bridge-status rows remain attached to the faces from which they are read.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real L10 current phase exit certificate]
\label{thm:real-l10-current-phase-exit-certificate}
An accepted Phase Real and Completion exit certificate is exactly the
four-face product
$$
\DyadicRatCoreUp \times \StreamNameUp \times \RegSeqRatUp \times \RealUp
$$
read through the Real L10 source-route, interface-totality, four-face
exactness, StreamName exit-correspondence, and audit-pullback records. Its
certificate content is the cited local closure evidence: the
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$ faces contribute their $\axiomCleanV$
records, while the $\StreamNameUp$ and $\RealUp$ faces contribute their
$\bridgeCheckedV$ records.

The certificate exports no $\RealUp$ completeness claim, no quotient stream,
no selected limit, no metric-completeness row, no aggregate bridge, and no
formalstatus token beyond the four local face reports.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:real-l10-current-phase-exit-audit-pullback}. It identifies
the accepted Phase Real and Completion exit audit with the product of the
four local face records over $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$,
$\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$.

The source-route and interface-totality inputs used there confine the exit
read to those faces. The four-face exactness theorem gives the product
shape, and the StreamName exit correspondence identifies the same product as
the current Phase Real and Completion exit route.

Apply \autoref{thm:real-l10-face-status-noncollapse}. It says that the four
face reports remain separate audit entries and cannot be fused into a further
formalstatus token, aggregate bridge, Real completeness row, quotient stream,
selected limit, or metric-completeness row.

Thus the accepted exit certificate is precisely the four-face product read
through the named route and exactness records, with only the displayed local
closure evidence exported.
\end{proof}
