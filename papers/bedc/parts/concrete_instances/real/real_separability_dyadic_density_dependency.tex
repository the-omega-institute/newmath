\begin{theorem}[Real separability dyadic density dependency]
\label{thm:real-separability-dyadic-density-dependency}
Every $\RealSeparabilityUp$ density read factors through the regular rational windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the terminal seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert} before it exports a separability witness. The dense dyadic candidate is visible only as a finite $\RegSeqRatUp$ window with a $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger and a downstream $\RealUp$ seal, not as a detached host dense subset or selected nearest point.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted $\RealSeparabilityUp$ packet to its density route. The requested approximation is first read through the finite $\RegSeqRatUp$ window, so the consumer receives a displayed regular rational observation rather than an unlogged rational point.

The $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ row then supplies the density scale and the tolerance comparison used by that same window. This makes the dyadic witness part of the finite dependency lattice, not an external density theorem.

Finally the $\RealUp$ seal consumes the regular window and dyadic tolerance rows before the separability row is public. Hence separability reads dense dyadic witnesses only through RegSeqRat windows and RealUp sealing.
\end{proof}
