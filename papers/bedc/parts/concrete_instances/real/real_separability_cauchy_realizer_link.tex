\begin{theorem}[Real separability Cauchy realizer link]
\label{thm:real-separability-cauchy-realizer-link}
Every density read exported by an accepted $\RealSeparabilityUp$ packet to a $\CauchyRealizerUp$ consumer factors through the finite rational-window route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-realizer-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}. The read first displays a stream window, then a regular rational candidate, then the dyadic tolerance ledger used by the Cauchy realizer, and only then reaches the terminal Real-facing density seal. It exports no host dense subset, selected nearest point, quotient real equality, countable-choice schedule, or ambient Real completeness coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the RegSeqRat schedule handoff of \autoref{thm:real-separability-regseqrat-schedule-handoff}. It routes every density read through the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, and terminal $\RealUp$ seal.

The $\CauchyRealizerUp$ consumer reads exactly that kind of finite request-to-window route by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-realizer-namecert}. Feeding the density read to the realizer therefore changes only the downstream consumer, not the rows inspected by the separability packet.

Since neither the schedule nor the Cauchy realizer chapter supplies host dense subsets, selected nearest points, quotient equality, countable choice, or ambient completeness as carrier coordinates, the linked route remains a finite rational-window handoff.
\end{proof}
