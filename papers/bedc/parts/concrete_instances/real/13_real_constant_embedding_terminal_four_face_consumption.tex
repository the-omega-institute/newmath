\begin{theorem}[Real constant embedding terminal four-face consumption]
\label{thm:real-constant-embedding-terminal-four-face-consumption}
The concrete constant rational embedding
$$
d\longmapsto (\mathsf{RatConstStream}_d,\Eone(d))
$$
from \autoref{thm:real-constant-rational-embedding-certificate} is accepted
as a $\RealUp$ public seal read only through the four Phase Real and Completion
faces
$$
\DyadicRatCoreUp,\quad \StreamNameUp,\quad \RegSeqRatUp,\quad \RealUp.
$$
Its visible support rows are precisely the $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$,
$\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows carried by those faces. The consumption route
therefore exports no quotient stream equality, selected host limit, ambient
completeness row, metric-completeness datum, countable choice, or standard
bridge row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with \autoref{thm:real-constant-rational-embedding-certificate}. It
packages the hidden rational seed $d$, the constant stream
$\mathsf{RatConstStream}_d$, and the e-one seal $\Eone(d)$ as one concrete
$\BHist$-anchored rational-to-real name.

Apply \autoref{thm:regseqrat-constant-real-seal-handoff}. The same constant
source reaches the local $\RealUp$ seal only after the constant
$\StreamNameUp$ schedule, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint and radius rows, and
the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regularity and classifier ledger have been read through
their displayed support rows.

Next apply \autoref{thm:streamname-realup-constant-observation-handoff}. This
keeps the constant observation surface at the pointwise StreamName boundary
and leaves regularity, dyadic seal data, and the Real seal classifier as
downstream obligations rather than additional source coordinates.

Finally use \autoref{thm:real-open-phase-four-object-terminal-route}. That
terminality theorem reads every accepted public seal or completion-facing
route through exactly the four displayed faces with $\BHist$, $\hsame$,
$\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support. Hence the constant rational
embedding is consumed by the same terminal four-face boundary, and the excluded
quotient, selected-limit, completeness, choice, metric-completeness, and
standard-bridge rows cannot replace any face of the route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real constant embedding terminal four-face pullback]
\label{thm:real-constant-embedding-terminal-four-face-pullback}
Any two accepted presentations of the concrete constant rational embedding
which agree on the displayed
$$
\DyadicRatCoreUp,\quad \StreamNameUp,\quad \RegSeqRatUp,\quad \RealUp
$$
faces and on the shared $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$
rows determine the same public $\RealUp$ seal route. Conversely, any terminal
four-face packet with those support rows determines the constant embedding
seal read.

The pullback exports no quotient stream equality, selected host limit,
ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, metric completeness, countable choice, or
standard bridge row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply
\autoref{thm:real-constant-embedding-terminal-four-face-consumption}. Each
accepted presentation of the constant rational embedding is already consumed
only through the four displayed faces and the shared $\BHist$, $\hsame$,
$\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.

Next use \autoref{thm:regseqrat-constant-real-seal-handoff}. Agreement on the
constant $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint data, the constant $\StreamNameUp$
schedule, and the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regularity and classifier ledger gives one
visible handoff surface for the $\RealUp$ seal.

Apply \autoref{thm:streamname-realup-constant-observation-handoff}. This keeps
the constant observation at the pointwise StreamName boundary and leaves the
dyadic, regularity, and Real seal rows as the downstream four-face packet
rather than as extra source coordinates.

Finally invoke \autoref{thm:real-open-phase-four-object-terminal-route}. Two
accepted reads that agree on the four faces and the listed support rows have
the same terminal finite route to the public $\RealUp$ seal. In the reverse
direction, a terminal packet carrying exactly those faces and support rows is
therefore read as the constant embedding seal route supplied by the handoff
theorems. Since none of the cited packets carries quotient stream equality,
selected host limits, ambient completeness, metric completeness, countable
choice, or a standard bridge row, no such row can replace any face of the
pullback.
\end{proof}
