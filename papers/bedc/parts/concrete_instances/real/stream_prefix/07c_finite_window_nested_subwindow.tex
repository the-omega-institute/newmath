\begin{theorem}[Real stream finite-window nested subwindow extraction]
\label{thm:real-stream-window-nested-subwindow-extraction}
For rational-observation streams $x,y:\mathbb{N}\to\Hist$ and indices
$n,j,q,r,s,m$, if
$$
\mathsf{RealStreamWindowClassifier}(x,y,n,m),
$$
$j+r\le m$, and $q+s\le r$, then
$$
\mathsf{RealStreamWindowClassifier}(x,y,(n+j)+q,s).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:real-stream-window-subwindow-extraction} to the source
window with shift $j$ and radius $r$. The bound $j+r\le m$ produces the first
subwindow
$$
\mathsf{RealStreamWindowClassifier}(x,y,n+j,r).
$$

Apply the same subwindow theorem again to this derived window with shift $q$
and radius $s$. The bound $q+s\le r$ gives
$$
\mathsf{RealStreamWindowClassifier}(x,y,(n+j)+q,s).
$$

Every endpoint of the nested window has the form $(n+j)+(q+k)$ with $k\le s$.
The second bound places $q+k$ inside the first subwindow, and the first bound
places $j+(q+k)$ inside the original window. Thus the displayed classifier is
exactly the repeated finite-window extraction.
\end{proof}
