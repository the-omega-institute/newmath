\begin{theorem}[Real stream-prefix carrier obligation]
\label{thm:real-stream-prefix-carrier-obligation}
A RealUp stream-prefix candidate satisfies the carrier face only when every
selected rational-observation endpoint exposes the RatUp endpoint carrier rows
through the $\RegSeqRat$ spine. Concretely, for rational-observation streams
$x,y:\mathbb{N}\to\Hist$ and a certified finite prefix, the candidate carrier
must contain the $\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(x(i))$ and
$\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(y(i))$ rows for each certified endpoint $i$ named
by the prefix, and those rows must be the endpoint rows read from
Definition~\ref{def:regseqrat-stream-certificate-spine},
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-carrier-obligation}, and the prefix endpoint and
readback blocks of \autoref{thm:real-stream-prefix-classifier-endpoint}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold Definition~\ref{def:regseqrat-stream-certificate-spine}. Its carrier
ledger is indexed by certified unary observations and supplies the rational
stream-name carrier together with endpoint rational-history carrier rows.

Apply \autoref{thm:regseqrat-carrier-obligation} to identify those endpoint
rows as the RatUp carrier face demanded by the spine. For a finite prefix,
\autoref{thm:real-stream-prefix-classifier-endpoint} selects the endpoint
classifier at the displayed prefix index, and the prefix readback blocks keep
that selected endpoint attached to the same observation row.

Thus a RealUp stream-prefix carrier cannot use an external carrier datum: the
visible endpoint carriers are exactly the
$\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}$ rows projected from the certified unary
observation spine and read back at the selected prefix endpoints.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real stream-prefix classifier obligation]
\label{thm:real-stream-prefix-classifier-obligation}
The classifier face required by the RealUp certificate boundary is supplied by
the pair of predicates $\mathsf{RealStreamClassifier}$ and
$\mathsf{RealStreamPrefixClassifier}$. For rational-observation streams
$x,y:\mathbb{N}\to\Hist$, the global classifier gives every finite prefix by
\autoref{thm:real-stream-finite-prefix-exactness}, each finite prefix exposes
its selected endpoint by
\autoref{thm:real-stream-prefix-classifier-endpoint}, and the global
classifier rows are closed under the symmetry and transitivity operations of
\autoref{thm:real-stream-classifier-symmetry} and
\autoref{thm:real-stream-classifier-transitivity}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a global $\mathsf{RealStreamClassifier}(x,y)$ row. Apply
\autoref{thm:real-stream-finite-prefix-exactness} in the forward direction to
obtain $\mathsf{RealStreamPrefixClassifier}(x,y,n)$ for every selected finite
prefix $n$.

For a chosen prefix, apply
\autoref{thm:real-stream-prefix-classifier-endpoint}. This projects the
RatUp endpoint classifier
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(x(n),y(n))$, so the finite classifier carries
the same endpoint face required by the certificate boundary.

Conversely, the reverse direction of
\autoref{thm:real-stream-finite-prefix-exactness} repacks the family of finite
prefix classifiers into the global stream classifier. Symmetry and
transitivity are then the global operations supplied by
\autoref{thm:real-stream-classifier-symmetry} and
\autoref{thm:real-stream-classifier-transitivity}, with endpoint projection
returning to the finite face whenever the boundary asks for a selected row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real stream-prefix stability obligation]
\label{thm:real-stream-prefix-stability-obligation}
The RealUp stream-prefix stability face is closed under $\hsame$ transport,
unary-context wrapping, and selected continuation packages. More precisely,
\autoref{thm:real-stream-prefix-classifier-hsame-transport},
\autoref{thm:real-stream-prefix-unary-context-closed},
\autoref{thm:real-stream-classifier-hsame-transport}, and
\autoref{thm:real-stream-classifier-selected-continuation-full-shape-package}
preserve the endpoint classifier rows used by
$\mathsf{RealStreamPrefixClassifier}$ and repack them as the corresponding
global $\mathsf{RealStreamClassifier}$ rows when all finite prefixes are
available.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For finite prefixes, apply
\autoref{thm:real-stream-prefix-classifier-hsame-transport} to transport each
selected endpoint classifier along the two pointwise $\hsame$ comparisons.
Apply \autoref{thm:real-stream-prefix-unary-context-closed} when the endpoints
are wrapped by unary prefix and tail contexts; the induction in that theorem
keeps every endpoint RatUp classifier in the prefix ledger.

For global stream rows, apply
\autoref{thm:real-stream-classifier-hsame-transport}. It transports the
pointwise classifier family and therefore preserves the global classifier face
seen by finite-prefix exactness.

For selected continuations, apply
\autoref{thm:real-stream-classifier-selected-continuation-full-shape-package}.
It supplies the endpoint shape, denominator, unary, and exclusion rows attached
to the same selected continuation data. Repacking through
\autoref{thm:real-stream-finite-prefix-exactness} identifies the finite prefix
and global classifier faces, so all displayed stability operations preserve
the RealUp classifier rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real stream-prefix ledger and seal obligation]
\label{thm:real-stream-prefix-ledger-seal-obligation}
Finite observation windows, dyadic ledger rows, and the $\RegSeqRat$ seal are
sufficient for the visible RealUp seal obligation. The ledger is the finite
policy of Definition~\ref{def:real-ledger-policy}; its classifier stability is
the package of
\autoref{thm:real-unary-stream-classifier-stability-package}; its scoped
stream-name interface is
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-streamname-scoped-interface}; and the seal is consumed
by \autoref{thm:regseqrat-dyadic-ledger-and-real-seal-obligation} without any
quotient or completion datum.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Gather the finite observation rows named by
Definition~\ref{def:real-ledger-policy}. They record the selected window
indices, dyadic comparison witnesses, regularity certificates, and classifier
comparison certificates used by the RealUp boundary.

Apply \autoref{thm:real-unary-stream-classifier-stability-package} to keep the
unary stream classifier stable across the recorded observations. Then apply
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-streamname-scoped-interface} to read those observations
as the scoped $\RegSeqRat$ stream-name interface, including the RatUp endpoint
rows exposed by the spine.

Finally, apply
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-dyadic-ledger-and-real-seal-obligation}. The theorem
consumes exactly the dyadic ledger rows and the scoped stream-name interface,
so the visible RealUp seal follows without quotient representatives, metric
completion data, or any carrier outside the $\RegSeqRat$ spine.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RealUp finite-window tail determinacy]
\label{thm:realup-finite-window-tail-determinacy}
Let $W$ and $W'$ be selected finite $\RealUp$ observation windows over the
same rational-observation streams. Suppose that both windows carry the same
certified unary endpoint row and both expose the common e-one tail readback
used by \autoref{thm:realup-finite-window-exhaustion-obligation}. Then the
$\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}$ endpoint rows, unary denominator witnesses,
dyadic source rows, and $\mathsf{RealLedgerPolicy}$ tail rows selected by $W$
and $W'$ classify to the same finite-window surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:real-stream-prefix-classifier-obligation} to the shared
certified unary endpoint. The classifier obligation identifies the selected
finite endpoint classifier with the RatUp endpoint rows read from the
$\RegSeqRat$ spine, so $W$ and $W'$ begin with the same carried
$\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}$ face.

Next apply \autoref{thm:real-stream-prefix-stability-obligation}. The common
e-one tail readback is a selected continuation package over the same unary
endpoint row, and stability transports the endpoint classifier through the
unary denominator, tail, and $\hsame$ rows without creating a second endpoint
surface.

Apply \autoref{thm:real-stream-prefix-ledger-seal-obligation} to the finite
dyadic and ledger rows exposed by the two windows. Since the seal consumes
only the displayed dyadic source rows, the scoped $\RegSeqRat$ interface, and
the $\mathsf{RealLedgerPolicy}$ tail rows, both windows feed the same visible
seal data into the RealUp boundary.

Finally use \autoref{thm:realup-finite-window-exhaustion-obligation}. Its
finite-window correspondence repacks exactly those endpoint, denominator,
dyadic, and tail rows as the public finite-window surface. The two selected
windows therefore determine classifier-equivalent finite-window readbacks at
the shared e-one tail endpoint.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RealUp RegSeqRat window seal restriction]
\label{thm:realup-regseqrat-window-seal-restriction}
Let $W$ be a finite $\RealUp$ observation window carrying the scoped
$\RegSeqRat$ stream-name interface, the dyadic ledger rows, and the Real
ledger tail rows used by the visible seal obligation. If $W'$ is a certified
sub-window of $W$, then $W'$ carries the corresponding seal package by
restriction, with the same $\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}$ endpoint rows selected
from the $\RegSeqRat$ spine. The restricted seal exposes no quotient,
completion, arithmetic closure, order closure, or metric-completion claim.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the visible seal package on $W$. By
\autoref{thm:real-stream-prefix-ledger-seal-obligation}, this package is
assembled from the scoped $\RegSeqRat$ stream-name interface, the finite
dyadic ledger, and the Real ledger tail rows; it does not require quotient or
completion data.

Restrict the selected rows to the certified sub-window $W'$. Finite-window
membership for $W'$ reads each selected observation as a member of $W$, so the
same $\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}$ endpoint rows and dyadic ledger entries are
available at the smaller window.

Apply \autoref{thm:realup-finite-window-tail-determinacy} to the shared
tail readback. The endpoint, denominator, dyadic, and Real ledger tail rows
selected by $W'$ classify to the same finite-window surface as their
counterparts in $W$.

Finally repackage only those restricted rows into the seal boundary. Finite
window exhaustion supplies no additional arithmetic, order, quotient, or
metric-completion component, so the seal over $W'$ is exactly the restricted
$\RegSeqRat$-to-$\RealUp$ seal package.
\end{proof}
