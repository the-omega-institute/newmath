\begin{theorem}[Real stream finite-window adjacent partition]
\label{thm:real-stream-window-adjacent-partition}
For rational-observation streams $x,y:\mathbb{N}\to\Hist$ and indices
$n,m,r$,
$$
\mathsf{RealStreamWindowClassifier}(x,y,n,m+1+r)
\Longleftrightarrow
\mathsf{RealStreamWindowClassifier}(x,y,n,m)\land
\mathsf{RealStreamWindowClassifier}(x,y,n+(m+1),r).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the forward direction, assume the joined window
$\mathsf{RealStreamWindowClassifier}(x,y,n,m+1+r)$. Apply
\autoref{thm:real-stream-window-subwindow-extraction} with shift $0$ and
radius $m$; the bound $0+m\le m+1+r$ is immediate, so the left piece is
$\mathsf{RealStreamWindowClassifier}(x,y,n,m)$.

Apply \autoref{thm:real-stream-window-subwindow-extraction} again with shift
$m+1$ and radius $r$. The bound $(m+1)+r\le m+1+r$ identifies exactly the
right adjacent piece
$\mathsf{RealStreamWindowClassifier}(x,y,n+(m+1),r)$. These two extracted
windows form the conjunction.

For the reverse direction, assume the two adjacent pieces. Apply
\autoref{thm:real-stream-window-adjacent-concatenation} to the left piece and
the right piece at anchor $n+(m+1)$. The concatenation theorem repacks the
bounded endpoint witnesses into
$\mathsf{RealStreamWindowClassifier}(x,y,n,m+1+r)$.
\end{proof}
