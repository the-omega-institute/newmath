\begin{definition}[Real stability certificate]
\label{def:real-stability-certificate}
The stability certificate consists of:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item pointwise stream stability inherited from $\StreamNameUp$ through
  \autoref{thm:real-unary-stream-classifier-stability-package}
  \item transport of the $\RegSeqRat$ regularity witness along stream classification
  \item classifier reflexivity, symmetry, and transitivity by dyadic tolerance comparison
  \item compatibility of constant dyadic embedding with the closeness classifier
  \item closure of real operations only when supplied as certified $\RegSeqRat$ transformations
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[RegSeqRat stream certificate spine]
\label{def:regseqrat-stream-certificate-spine}
A $\RegSeqRat$ stream certificate spine for a rational-observation stream
$s:\Hist\to\Hist$ is a rational stream-name carrier
$\mathsf{RatStreamNameCarrier}(s)$ together with a regularity witness stable
under $\mathsf{RealUnaryStreamClassifier}$. Its ledger records the unary
observation indices, the endpoint $\RatUp$ carriers exposed at those indices,
the pointwise $\RatUp$ classifiers read from the stream classifier, and the
dyadic-tolerance requests used by the regularity witness.

The ten-field fit is: traditional target Bishop regular rational sequence;
BEDC source $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$, $\RatUp$, and
$\StreamNameUp$; carrier $\mathsf{RatStreamNameCarrier}$ plus the regularity
witness; classifier $\mathsf{RealUnaryStreamClassifier}$; stability pointwise
RatUp reflexivity, symmetry, transitivity, and $\hsame$ transport; ledger
finite observations and tolerance witnesses; core theorem list
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-stream-classifier-stability-spine} and
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-realup-seal-consumes-stream-spine}; exactness pointwise
stream classification; standard bridge RealUp seal; status
$\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat carrier and classifier obligations]
\label{thm:regseqrat-obligation-carrier-classifier}
Let $s,t:\Hist\to\Hist$ be rational-observation streams. A
$\RegSeqRat$ stream certificate spine for $s$ and $t$, together with a recorded
row $\mathsf{RealUnaryStreamClassifier}(s,t)$, supplies the carrier and
classifier obligations for the $\RegSeqRat$ interface: the
$\mathsf{RatStreamNameCarrier}$ rows for the two streams, the
$\mathsf{RealUnaryStreamClassifier}$ row as the stream classifier, endpoint
$\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}$ rows at every certified unary observation, and
the dyadic-tolerance ledger requests named by the regularity witness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold Definition~\ref{def:regseqrat-stream-certificate-spine}. The carrier
field of each spine is already the required
$\mathsf{RatStreamNameCarrier}$ row, and the displayed
$\mathsf{RealUnaryStreamClassifier}(s,t)$ row is the classifier field used by
the interface.

For endpoint carrier rows, apply
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-stream-classifier-stability-spine} to the carried
streams and the displayed classifier row. Its endpoint clause returns
$\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(s(n))$ and $\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(t(n))$ for
each certified unary observation $n$.

The remaining ledger entries are exactly those named in the spine definition:
the unary observation index, the two endpoint carriers, the pointwise RatUp
classifier read from the stream classifier, and the dyadic-tolerance request
handled by the regularity witness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat stability and transport obligations]
\label{thm:regseqrat-obligation-stability-transport}
Let $s,t,u,s',t':\Hist\to\Hist$ be carried rational-observation streams with
recorded rows $\mathsf{RealUnaryStreamClassifier}(s,t)$ and
$\mathsf{RealUnaryStreamClassifier}(t,u)$. If the endpoint transports are
pointwise over unary observations, then the $\RegSeqRat$ obligation set
contains reflexivity, symmetry, transitivity, and pointwise $\hsame$ transport
for $\mathsf{RealUnaryStreamClassifier}$ over those carried streams.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:real-unary-stream-classifier-stability-package} to the
three carried streams, the two displayed classifier rows, and the two
pointwise $\hsame$ transport rows. The package gives the reflexive classifier
on $s$, the reversed classifier from $t$ to $s$, and the composite classifier
from $s$ to $u$.

For transport, use
\autoref{thm:real-unary-streamname-transported-classifier}. It repacks the
pointwise RatUp transport data along certified unary observations as the
transported StreamNameUp classifier row, so the same BEDC point classifiers
serve as the $\RegSeqRat$ transport obligation.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat reindexed constant stream spine]
\label{thm:regseqrat-reindexed-constant-stream-spine}

$\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(d)$ supplies $\RegSeqRat$ stream certificate
spines for both $\mathsf{RatConstStream}_d\circ r$ and
$\mathsf{RatConstStream}_d$. If $\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(d,e)$ is

$\mathsf{RatConstStream}_d\circ r$ and
$\mathsf{RatConstStream}_e\circ q$ carry compatible $\RegSeqRat$ stream
certificate spines and the classifier row
$\mathsf{RealUnaryStreamClassifier}(\mathsf{RatConstStream}_d\circ r,
\mathsf{RatConstStream}_e\circ q)$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First build the carried stream rows. Apply
\autoref{thm:rat-stream-name-independent-reindexed-constant-classifier} to the
carrier row of $d$ to obtain the stream-name carrier for
$\mathsf{RatConstStream}_d\circ r$, and apply
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-constant-stream-spine} to the same carrier row to obtain
the unreindexed constant-stream spine.

The regularity ledger for $\mathsf{RatConstStream}_d\circ r$ is the same
constant ledger used in \autoref{thm:regseqrat-constant-stream-spine}. At any
certified unary observation index, the schedule $r$ is read before the constant
stream is evaluated, and the exposed endpoint is still $d$. Thus every dyadic
tolerance request again compares the RatUp point $d$ with itself and is
discharged by RatUp classifier reflexivity.

Now suppose $\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(d,e)$ is supplied. Its endpoint
carrier rows provide the corresponding constant regularity ledgers for the
$d$-stream and the $e$-stream. The StreamNameUp classifier row follows from
\autoref{thm:rat-stream-name-independent-reindexed-constant-classifier}, while
\autoref{thm:real-constant-independent-reindexed-stream-bridge} reads the same
point classifier as the RealUp unary-observation stream classifier for the two
independently reindexed constant streams.

Combining these rows gives the two compatible $\RegSeqRat$ spines together with
the displayed $\mathsf{RealUnaryStreamClassifier}$ row. No quotient,
completion, or host real structure is used.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat constant and transport obligation]
\label{thm:regseqrat-constant-and-transport-obligation}
The constant and transport obligations for $\RegSeqRat$ are the inhabitation of
the constant rational-observation stream and pointwise $\hsame$ transport of
stream classifiers. For every $d:\Hist$ with
$\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(d)$, $\mathsf{RatConstStream}_d$ carries a
$\RegSeqRat$ spine. If $s,t$ are classified streams and $s',t'$ are pointwise
$\hsame$ transports over certified unary observations, then the transported
stream-name classifier $\mathsf{RatStreamNameClassifier}(s',t')$ is part of
the same obligation set.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Definition~\ref{def:regseqrat-stream-certificate-spine} identifies the
constant case as a spine over a stream-name carrier plus a regularity ledger.
Apply \autoref{thm:regseqrat-constant-stream-spine} to the supplied
$\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(d)$; the resulting ledger is constant at every
certified unary observation.

For transport, apply
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-stream-classifier-stability-spine} to the two endpoint
$\hsame$ rows. It supplies the transported pointwise $\RatUp$ classifiers and
repackages them as $\mathsf{RatStreamNameClassifier}(s',t')$.

The scoped package
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-stream-spine-scoped-package} places the constant and
transport clauses in one certificate package, and
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-realup-seal-consumes-stream-spine} shows that these
clauses are sufficient for the downstream RealUp seal to consume the stream
classifier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat dyadic ledger and RealUp seal obligation]
\label{thm:regseqrat-dyadic-ledger-and-real-seal-obligation}
The dyadic-ledger and $\RealUp$ seal obligation for $\RegSeqRat$ consists of
finite unary observation indices, the endpoint $\RatUp$ rows read at those
indices, the dyadic-tolerance witnesses used by regularity, and the seal export
of the recorded $\mathsf{RealUnaryStreamClassifier}$ row. Whenever sealed
$\RealUp$ candidates expose streams satisfying the $\RegSeqRat$ spine and
record their stream comparison row, the seal exports the RealUp observation
classifier required by \autoref{def:real-classifier-specification}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold Definition~\ref{def:regseqrat-stream-certificate-spine}. Its ledger is
finite at the level of requested unary observations and dyadic-tolerance
checks, and each ledger entry is backed by endpoint $\RatUp$ carrier and
classifier rows.

The constant ledger is supplied by
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-constant-stream-spine}. The general endpoint and
transport rows are supplied by
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-stream-classifier-stability-spine}, while
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-stream-spine-scoped-package} gathers the ledger,
classifier, constant, and transport faces as one scoped certificate package.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:regseqrat-realup-seal-consumes-stream-spine}. The
seal reads the already recorded
$\mathsf{RealUnaryStreamClassifier}$ row and exports exactly the observation
classifier required by the RealUp boundary, without adding quotient or
completion data.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real unary stream classifier stability package]
\label{thm:real-unary-stream-classifier-stability-package}
For rational-observation maps $s,t,u,s',t':\Hist\to\Hist$, suppose
$$
\mathsf{RatStreamNameCarrier}(s),\quad
\mathsf{RatStreamNameCarrier}(t),\quad
\mathsf{RatStreamNameCarrier}(u),
$$
$$
\mathsf{RealUnaryStreamClassifier}(s,t),\quad
\mathsf{RealUnaryStreamClassifier}(t,u),
$$
and suppose the endpoint transports are pointwise over unary observations:
$$
\forall n,\ \mathsf{UnaryHistory}(n)\to\hsame(s(n),s'(n)),
\quad
\forall n,\ \mathsf{UnaryHistory}(n)\to\hsame(t(n),t'(n)).
$$
Then the same BEDC unary observation rows supply the following stability
package:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item $\mathsf{RatStreamNameClassifier}(s,t)$.
  \item $\mathsf{RealUnaryStreamClassifier}(s,s)$,
  $\mathsf{RealUnaryStreamClassifier}(t,s)$, and
  $\mathsf{RealUnaryStreamClassifier}(s,u)$.
  \item For every certified unary observation $n$, both endpoint RatUp carrier
  rows $\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(s(n))$ and
  $\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(t(n))$.
  \item $\mathsf{RealUnaryStreamClassifier}(s',t')$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold Definition~\ref{def:rat-stream-name-carrier},
Definition~\ref{def:rat-stream-name-classifier}, and
Definition~\ref{def:real-unary-observation-stream-classifier}. After this
unfolding, both StreamNameUp and RealUp classifier rows are the same pointwise
RatUp classifier over histories $n$ equipped with
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(n)$.

The first conclusion is therefore just the displayed
$\mathsf{RealUnaryStreamClassifier}(s,t)$ read under the StreamNameUp
classifier name. For $\mathsf{RealUnaryStreamClassifier}(s,s)$, fix a
certified observation $n$; the carrier row for $s$ gives
$\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(s(n))$, and RatUp classifier reflexivity gives the
required point classifier at that observation.

For the reversed classifier $\mathsf{RealUnaryStreamClassifier}(t,s)$, read the
point classifier from $\mathsf{RealUnaryStreamClassifier}(s,t)$ and apply RatUp
classifier symmetry. For $\mathsf{RealUnaryStreamClassifier}(s,u)$, read the
two point classifiers from the assumptions at the same certified observation
and compose them by RatUp classifier transitivity.

For endpoint carriers, fix a certified unary observation $n$ and unpack the
RatUp classifier obtained from $\mathsf{RealUnaryStreamClassifier}(s,t)$ at
that observation. Its endpoint data are precisely
$\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(s(n))$ and $\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(t(n))$.

For transport, fix the same certified observation $n$. Apply
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier\_hsame\_transport}$ to the point classifier from
$\mathsf{RealUnaryStreamClassifier}(s,t)$ and to the two pointwise $\hsame$
witnesses from $s,t$ to $s',t'$. Repacking the transported point classifier
over all certified unary observations gives
$\mathsf{RealUnaryStreamClassifier}(s',t')$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.RealUp.RealUnaryStreamClassifier\_streamName\_stability\_package}

\begin{theorem}[Real unary stream-name transported classifier]
\label{thm:real-unary-streamname-transported-classifier}
For rational-observation maps $s,t,s',t':\Hist\to\Hist$, suppose
$$
\mathsf{RatStreamNameCarrier}(s),\quad
\mathsf{RatStreamNameCarrier}(t),\quad
\mathsf{RealUnaryStreamClassifier}(s,t),
$$
and suppose the endpoint transports are pointwise over unary observations:
$$
\forall n,\ \mathsf{UnaryHistory}(n)\to\hsame(s(n),s'(n)),
\quad
\forall n,\ \mathsf{UnaryHistory}(n)\to\hsame(t(n),t'(n)).
$$
Then $\mathsf{RatStreamNameClassifier}(s',t')$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold Definition~\ref{def:rat-stream-name-carrier},
Definition~\ref{def:rat-stream-name-classifier}, and
Definition~\ref{def:real-unary-observation-stream-classifier}. The
$\mathsf{RealUnaryStreamClassifier}(s,t)$ row supplies the RatUp point
classifier at each certified unary observation, while the two displayed
carrier hypotheses supply the endpoint stream carriers before transport.

Fix a certified unary observation $n$ with witness
$\nu:\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(n)$. Apply
\autoref{thm:rat-history-classifier-hsame-transport} to the RatUp point
classifier read from $\mathsf{RealUnaryStreamClassifier}(s,t)$ at $(n,\nu)$
and to the two pointwise $\hsame$ witnesses from $s,t$ to $s',t'$. This gives
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(s'(n),t'(n))$.

Read the endpoint carrier fields of this transported RatUp classifier. The
left field gives $\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(s'(n))$ and the right field gives
$\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(t'(n))$. Since $(n,\nu)$ was arbitrary, these
fields build $\mathsf{RatStreamNameCarrier}(s')$ and
$\mathsf{RatStreamNameCarrier}(t')$.

The same transported RatUp point classifier, again for arbitrary $(n,\nu)$,
supplies the pointwise classifier row of
$\mathsf{RatStreamNameClassifier}(s',t')$. Repack that pointwise row with the
two transported stream carriers to obtain the claimed StreamNameUp
classifier.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.RealUp.RealUnaryStreamClassifier\_streamName\_transport}

The ten-field fit is: traditional target transport of a unary real stream-name
classifier; BEDC source StreamNameUp read through the RealUp unary classifier;
carrier the transported RatUp endpoint carrier rows for $s'$ and $t'$;
classifier the transported RatUp point classifier; stability pointwise
$\hsame$ transport along unary observations; ledger the observation witness
and the two endpoint transport witnesses; core theorem this block; exactness
$\mathsf{RatStreamNameClassifier}(s',t')$ rather than only
$\mathsf{RealUnaryStreamClassifier}(s',t')$; standard bridge the downstream
RealUp certificate boundary; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.

\begin{definition}[Real ledger policy]
\label{def:real-ledger-policy}
Record finite stream observations, dyadic comparison witnesses, regularity certificates, and classifier comparison certificates when sameness is used.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[RealUp certificate obligations]
\label{def:real-certificate-obligations}
The carrier, source, pattern, classifier, stability, and ledger data listed above are the required fields for using the interface name $\RealUp$.
\end{definition}

\paragraph{Primitive boundary.}
$\RealUp$ is not a BEDC primitive. It is admitted only when those obligations are supplied.

\end{remark}
