\begin{theorem}[Real stream prefix finite-tail transported endpoint denominator package]
\label{thm:real-stream-prefix-finite-tail-transported-endpoint-denominator-package}
For rational-observation streams $x,y:\mathbb{N}\to\Hist$, indices $n,m$, and
transported endpoint histories $u,v:\Hist$, assume
$$
\mathsf{RealStreamPrefixClassifier}(x,y,n),
$$
$$
\forall k<m,\ \mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(x(n+k+1),y(n+k+1)),
$$
$$
\forall i,\ \mathsf{UnaryHistory}(x(i)),
$$
and
$$
\hsame(x(n+m),u),\quad \hsame(y(n+m),v).
$$
Then the transported finite-tail boundary carries the endpoint denominator
package:
$$
\begin{array}{l}
\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(u,v)\land
\mathsf{PositiveUnaryDenominator}(u)\land
\mathsf{PositiveUnaryDenominator}(v)\land\\
\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(u)\land
\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(v)\land
(\hsame(u,\emp)\to\mathsf{False})\land
(\hsame(v,\emp)\to\mathsf{False}).
\end{array}
$$
Ten-field fit: traditional target finite-tail transported RealUp boundary
package; BEDC source RealUp stream prefixes with RatUp finite tail classifiers;
carrier rational-observation streams and transported endpoint histories;
classifier $\mathsf{RealStreamPrefixClassifier}$ followed by
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$; stability by tail extension with unary anchor,
endpoint projection, and endpoint $\hsame$ transport; ledger base prefix length,
tail length, selected boundary, and two endpoint transport rows; core theorem
this finite-tail boundary package; exactness classifier, positive denominator,
unary, and nonempty rows for $u$ and $v$; standard bridge RegSeqRat finite
boundary observations; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First extend the classified prefix through the displayed finite tail. Apply
\autoref{thm:real-stream-prefix-tail-extension-with-unary-anchor} to the prefix
through $n$, the tail classifier row, and the pointwise unary anchor on $x$.
This supplies $\mathsf{RealStreamPrefixClassifier}(x,y,n+m)$, together with the
left unary boundary row kept in the construction.

Next apply \autoref{thm:real-stream-prefix-classifier-endpoint} at the boundary
index $n+m$. The selected endpoint classifier is
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(x(n+m),y(n+m))$.

Transport this RatUp classifier along the two displayed $\hsame$ rows by
\autoref{thm:rat-history-classifier-hsame-transport}. The transported classifier
is $\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(u,v)$.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:rat-history-classifier-positive-denominators} to the
transported classifier, and then apply
\autoref{thm:positive-unary-denominator-unary-and-nonempty} separately to the
two positive-denominator witnesses. These readback rows provide the two
positive denominator witnesses, the two unary endpoint witnesses, and the two
nonempty exclusions, so the displayed package repacks directly.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real stream prefix finite-tail transported full endpoint package]
\label{thm:real-stream-prefix-finite-tail-transported-full-endpoint-package}
For rational-observation streams $x,y:\mathbb{N}\to\Hist$, indices $n,m$,
transported endpoint histories $u,v:\Hist$, and histories
$a,b,z_U,z_V:\Hist$, assume
$$
\mathsf{RealStreamPrefixClassifier}(x,y,n),
$$
$$
\forall k<m,\ \mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(x(n+k+1),y(n+k+1)),
$$
$$
\forall i,\ \mathsf{UnaryHistory}(x(i)),
$$
and
$$
\hsame(x(n+m),u),\quad \hsame(y(n+m),v),\quad
\hsame(u,\Eone(a)),\quad \hsame(v,\Eone(b)).
$$
Then the transported finite-tail boundary carries the full endpoint package:
$$
\begin{array}{l}
\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(u,v)\land
\mathsf{PositiveUnaryDenominator}(u)\land
\mathsf{PositiveUnaryDenominator}(v)\land\\
\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(u)\land
\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(v)\land
(\hsame(u,\emp)\to\mathsf{False})\land
(\hsame(v,\emp)\to\mathsf{False})\land\\
(\hsame(u,\Ezero(z_U))\to\mathsf{False})\land
(\hsame(v,\Ezero(z_V))\to\mathsf{False})\land\\
\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(a)\land
\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(b)\land
\hsame(a,b).
\end{array}
$$
Ten-field fit: traditional target finite transported rational observation
boundary; BEDC source RealUp prefix classifier plus RatUp endpoint classifier
and $\mathsf{BHist}$ transports; carrier transported endpoint histories;
classifier $\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$; stability endpoint $\hsame$
transport; ledger base prefix, finite tail, transported endpoints, and endpoint
display tests; core theorem this block; exactness the displayed full package;
standard bridge RegSeqRat finite boundary readback; status
$\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply
\autoref{thm:real-stream-prefix-finite-tail-transported-endpoint-denominator-package}
to the prefix classifier, finite-tail RatUp classifier row, pointwise unary
anchor, and two transported endpoint rows. This gives the transported
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(u,v)$, both positive denominator witnesses, both
unary endpoint rows, and both nonempty exclusions.

Next derive the zero-headed exclusions. If $u$ is displayed as
$\Ezero(z_U)$, transport the positive denominator witness for $u$ across that
display and apply RatUp positive-denominator zero-headed absurdity. The right
endpoint uses the same argument with $v$ and $z_V$.

Finally transport the transported classifier across the two displayed
$\Eone$ comparisons. RatUp e-one tail classifier exactness then reads back the
two unary tails and their internal sameness, namely
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(a)$, $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(b)$, and $\hsame(a,b)$.
Together with the denominator package and the two zero-headed exclusions, these
rows repack as the displayed full endpoint package.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RealUp StreamName tail denominator selector handoff]
\label{thm:realup-streamname-tail-denominator-selector-handoff}
Let $W_0$ and $W_1$ be selected finite $\RealUp$ observation-window packages
over the same StreamName endpoints $s,t:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{BHist}$, the
same finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ window $\mathcal P$, and the same accepted
$\RegSeqRat$ spine row. Suppose they are connected by a StreamName RegSeqRat
RealUp tail-selector transport packet. Let $x,y:\mathbb{N}\to\Hist$ be the
transported Real stream-prefix endpoint streams read from the selected finite
window, let $n,m$ be the displayed base and finite-tail lengths, and let
$u,v:\Hist$ be transported endpoints satisfying
$$
\mathsf{RealStreamPrefixClassifier}(x,y,n),
$$
$$
\forall k<m,\ \mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(x(n+k+1),y(n+k+1)),
$$
$$
\forall i,\ \mathsf{UnaryHistory}(x(i)),
$$
and
$$
\hsame(x(n+m),u),\qquad \hsame(y(n+m),v).
$$
Then the $\RealUp$ consumer boundary exposes exactly the shared StreamName
public denominator selector selected by
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-realup-tail-denominator-selector}, together
with the transported finite-tail endpoint denominator package
$$
\begin{array}{l}
\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(u,v)\land
\mathsf{PositiveUnaryDenominator}(u)\land
\mathsf{PositiveUnaryDenominator}(v)\land\\
\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(u)\land
\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(v)\land
(\hsame(u,\emp)\to\mathsf{False})\land
(\hsame(v,\emp)\to\mathsf{False}).
\end{array}
$$
Equivalently, the handoff is a single finite boundary over the shared
StreamName selector, the accepted $\RegSeqRat$ finite row, the transported
Real stream-prefix endpoints, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\Pkg$.
It adds no quotient row, completion object, Classical representative,
metric-completeness certificate, arithmetic or order row, standard bridge row,
or external selector.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-realup-tail-denominator-selector} to the
transport packet between $W_0$ and $W_1$. This identifies the selected finite
public denominator selector carried by the StreamName side with the selector
read by the finite $\RealUp$ ledger at $W_1$.

Next apply
\autoref{thm:real-stream-prefix-finite-tail-transported-endpoint-denominator-package}
to the transported Real prefix classifier, the finite RatUp tail row, the
pointwise unary anchor, and the two endpoint $\hsame$ rows. This supplies
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(u,v)$, the two positive unary denominator rows,
the two unary endpoint rows, and the two nonempty endpoint exclusions.

Finally use
\autoref{thm:real-stream-prefix-finite-tail-transported-full-endpoint-package}
as the readback boundary showing that the same finite-tail hypotheses admit no
additional quotient, completion, representative, metric, arithmetic, order, or
standard-bridge data. The consumer therefore sees only the shared StreamName
denominator selector and the transported endpoint denominator package displayed
above.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RealUp StreamName tail selector transport determinacy]
\label{thm:realup-streamname-tail-selector-transport-determinacy}
Let $H_0$ and $H_1$ be two $\RealUp$ StreamName tail-denominator selector
handoffs over the same StreamName endpoints $s,t:\mathsf{BHist}\to
\mathsf{BHist}$, the same finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ window $\mathcal P$,
and the same accepted $\RegSeqRat$ spine row. Suppose both handoffs read their
public denominator selector through
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-realup-tail-denominator-selector}. Suppose
the first handoff exposes transported Real stream-prefix endpoints $u_0,v_0$
and the second exposes transported Real stream-prefix endpoints $u_1,v_1$ from
the same finite row.

Then the two exposed endpoint denominator packages are classified by the same
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$ row and expose the same positive-unary
denominator rows, unary endpoint rows, and nonempty-endpoint exclusions. In
particular the consumer cannot distinguish $H_0$ from $H_1$ by changing the
endpoint package: both handoffs reduce to the shared StreamName selector, the
same $\RegSeqRat$ finite spine, and the same transported package over
$\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\Pkg$. No quotient, completion,
arithmetic or order row, standard bridge row, or external selector row can
separate the two handoffs at this boundary.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply
\autoref{thm:realup-streamname-tail-denominator-selector-handoff} to $H_0$.
The result identifies the public consumer boundary of the first handoff with
the StreamName denominator selector selected by
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-realup-tail-denominator-selector} and with
the transported finite-tail endpoint denominator package supplied by
\autoref{thm:real-stream-prefix-finite-tail-transported-endpoint-denominator-package}.

Apply the same handoff theorem to $H_1$. The hypotheses have not changed: the
StreamName endpoints, the finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ window, and the
accepted $\RegSeqRat$ spine row are the same, and the public selector is read
through the same StreamName theorem. Thus the second endpoint package is read
from the same finite-tail denominator package rows as the first.

The endpoint-package theorem records exactly the
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$ row, the positive-unary denominator witnesses,
the unary endpoint rows, and the two nonempty-endpoint exclusions. Since both
handoffs consume that same row data, replacing $H_0$ by $H_1$ leaves each of
those rows fixed at the consumer boundary.

Finally the handoff theorem states the negative boundary: no quotient,
completion object, arithmetic or order certificate, standard bridge, or
external selector is added. Hence no such row can distinguish the two handoffs,
and the exposed endpoint denominator package is determined by the shared
StreamName selector and the shared $\RegSeqRat$ finite row.
\end{proof}
