\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat dyadic ledger and RealUp seal obligation]
\label{thm:regseqrat-dyadic-ledger-and-real-seal-obligation}
The dyadic-ledger and $\RealUp$ seal obligation for $\RegSeqRat$ consists of
finite unary observation indices, the endpoint $\RatUp$ rows read at those
indices, the dyadic-tolerance witnesses used by regularity, and the seal export
of the recorded $\mathsf{RealUnaryStreamClassifier}$ row. Whenever sealed
$\RealUp$ candidates expose streams satisfying the $\RegSeqRat$ spine and
record their stream comparison row, the seal exports the RealUp observation
classifier required by \autoref{def:real-classifier-specification}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold Definition~\ref{def:regseqrat-stream-certificate-spine}. Its ledger is
finite at the level of requested unary observations and dyadic-tolerance
checks, and each ledger entry is backed by endpoint $\RatUp$ carrier and
classifier rows.

The constant ledger is supplied by
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-constant-stream-spine}. The general endpoint and
transport rows are supplied by
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-stream-classifier-stability-spine}, while
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-stream-spine-scoped-package} gathers the ledger,
classifier, constant, and transport faces as one scoped certificate package.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:regseqrat-realup-seal-consumes-stream-spine}. The
seal reads the already recorded
$\mathsf{RealUnaryStreamClassifier}$ row and exports exactly the observation
classifier required by the RealUp boundary, without adding quotient or
completion data.
\end{proof}

$$
\mathsf{RealStreamWindowClassifier}(x,y,n,m),
$$

$$
x_j(k)=x(n+j+k),\qquad y_j(k)=y(n+j+k).
$$
Then the finite $\RegSeqRat$ regularity surface for the shifted window
$(x_j,y_j,0,r)$ inherits the same endpoint
$\mathsf{DyadicRatCoreObservationRow}$ carrier, classifier, stability, and

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:real-stream-window-subwindow-extraction} to the certified
window with shift $j$ and radius $r$. The bound $j+r\le m$ gives the shifted
finite certificate at anchor $n+j$ and radius $r$, which is the zero-based
certificate for the tail-shifted streams $x_j$ and $y_j$ at window $(0,r)$.

Next apply
\autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-public-observation-subwindow-stability}. The
subwindow keeps the public DyadicRatCore observation-row carrier, classifier,
stability, and finite dyadic ledger rows fixed while changing only the finite
anchor read by the stream certificate.

Finally read the shifted certificate through
\autoref{thm:streamname-public-finite-window-interface} and close with
\autoref{thm:realup-finite-window-exhaustion-obligation}. The RealUp
finite-window consumer therefore receives the same DyadicRatCore endpoint rows
from the tail-shifted $\RegSeqRat$ surface that it receives from the original
finite window, with no additional primitive or bridge.
\end{proof}
