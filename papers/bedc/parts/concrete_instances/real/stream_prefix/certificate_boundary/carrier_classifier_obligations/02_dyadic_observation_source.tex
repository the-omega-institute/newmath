\begin{definition}[DyadicRatCore observation row]
\label{def:dyadic-rat-core-observation-row}
A DyadicRatCore observation row for a BEDC history $d$ is the package consisting
of a $\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(d)$ witness, a positive unary denominator row
$\mathsf{PositiveUnaryDenominator}(d)$, and the dyadic-radius token used by the
$\RegSeqRat$ regularity check. Its classifier face is inherited from
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$, together with $\hsame$ transport of the
positive-denominator row.
\end{definition}
\leandef{BEDC.Derived.RealUp.DyadicRatCoreObservationRow}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicRatCore observation-row classifier transport]
\label{thm:dyadic-rat-core-observation-row-classifier-transport}
Let $d,e,d',e':\Hist$. Suppose $d$ and $e$ carry
$\mathsf{DyadicRatCoreObservationRow}$ packages, suppose
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(d,e)$ holds, and suppose
$\hsame(d,d')$ and $\hsame(e,e')$ hold. If the dyadic-radius tokens for
$d'$ and $e'$ are the same certified unary-observation requests as the
tokens for $d$ and $e$, transported along those two $\hsame$ rows inside the
$\RegSeqRat$ ledger, then $d'$ and $e'$ carry
$\mathsf{DyadicRatCoreObservationRow}$ packages and
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(d',e')$ holds.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack Definition~\ref{def:dyadic-rat-core-observation-row}. The source rows
at $d$ and $e$ consist of rational-history carriers, positive unary
denominator rows, and dyadic-radius tokens attached to certified
$\RegSeqRat$ unary observations.

Apply the RatUp classifier transport to
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(d,e)$ along $\hsame(d,d')$ and
$\hsame(e,e')$. This gives the transported classifier
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(d',e')$, and the transported carrier rows are
read from the same rational-history endpoint spine.

The denominator component follows by positive-denominator transport. Applying
\autoref{thm:rat-positive-unary-denominator-hsame-transport} to the two
$\hsame$ rows carries the positive unary denominator rows from $d$ to $d'$
and from $e$ to $e'$.

Finally, the dyadic-radius component is ledger-attached to the same certified
unary-observation requests in the $\RegSeqRat$ spine. Transporting the
endpoint rows changes neither the request names nor the recorded
dyadic-tolerance tokens, so no completion or quotient datum is introduced.
Repacking the transported carrier, denominator, token, and classifier rows
gives the stated DyadicRatCore observation-row transport.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicRatCore scoped source package]
\label{thm:dyadic-rat-core-scoped-source-package}
The DyadicRatCore source used by $\RegSeqRat$ is the concrete BEDC scoped
source package over $\Hist$ whose carrier, classifier, stability, and ledger
faces are all read from the rational-history endpoint spine. For each endpoint
history $d$, the carrier face is
$\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(d)$ together with
$\mathsf{PositiveUnaryDenominator}(d)$ and the dyadic-radius token recorded by
the $\RegSeqRat$ regularity request. For endpoints $d,e$, the classifier face
is $\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(d,e)$ together with $\hsame$ transport of the
positive-denominator rows. The stability face is inherited from the $\RatUp$
carrier and classifier transports and from the pointwise classifier obligation
of $\RegSeqRat$. The ledger face is exactly the finite family of
dyadic-tolerance requests already recorded in the $\RegSeqRat$ spine.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier face. Definition~\ref{def:dyadic-rat-core-observation-row}
names the three endpoint rows: the rational-history carrier, the positive
unary denominator, and the dyadic-radius token. The endpoint carrier and the
finite dyadic request are supplied by
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-carrier-obligation}, while
\autoref{thm:rat-history-carrier-positive-denominator-equivalence} converts
the carrier row into the positive-denominator row.

Next read the classifier and stability faces. The pointwise stream classifier
obligation in \autoref{thm:regseqrat-classifier-obligation} exposes the
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(d,e)$ endpoint comparison used by the
DyadicRatCore source. RatUp carrier and classifier transport carries the
rational-history rows along the same $\hsame$ comparison, and
\autoref{thm:rat-positive-unary-denominator-hsame-transport} carries the
positive-denominator row.

It remains only to identify the ledger. No request outside the
$\RegSeqRat$ spine is used: the dyadic-radius token is attached to the same
certified unary observation entry that supplies the endpoint rational row.
Thus the source package has precisely the stated carrier, classifier,
stability, and finite ledger faces over $\Hist$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Phase Real dyadic stream RegSeq scope]
\label{thm:real-phase-dyadic-stream-regseq-scope}
Every DyadicRatCore request consumed by the Phase Real $\RegSeqRat$ spine is
exactly a $\mathsf{DyadicRatCoreObservationRow}$ over $\mathsf{BHist}$. Its
carrier, positive-denominator, dyadic-radius, classifier, and ledger faces are
read from the $\RatUp$ carrier and classifier transports together with the
$\RegSeqRat$ unary observation request. No $\RealUp$ completeness row, quotient
row, completion object, metric-completeness row, Classical datum, or standard
bridge row is part of this DyadicRatCore source scope.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack \autoref{def:dyadic-rat-core-observation-row}. A consumed endpoint row is
the rational-history carrier, positive unary denominator, and dyadic-radius
token attached to the certified unary observation entry of the $\RegSeqRat$
spine.

Apply \autoref{thm:dyadic-rat-core-scoped-source-package}. Its carrier face is
the endpoint $\RatUp$ row, its classifier face is the
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$ comparison, and its stability face is the same
$\hsame$ transport used by the $\RegSeqRat$ pointwise classifier obligation.

The remaining ledger component is the finite dyadic-tolerance request already
named by the $\RegSeqRat$ carrier and classifier obligations. Repacking these
faces gives exactly the Phase Real DyadicRatCore request scope, and there is no
slot in that package for quotient, completion, metric, Classical, or $\RealUp$
completeness data.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicRatCore observation row hsame transport]
\label{thm:dyadic-rat-core-observation-row-hsame-transport}
Let $d,e:\Hist$. If $d$ has a DyadicRatCore observation row in the sense of
\autoref{def:dyadic-rat-core-observation-row} and $\hsame(d,e)$ holds, then
$e$ has a DyadicRatCore observation row. Its $\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(e)$
component is the $\RatUp$ carrier transport of the carrier component at $d$,
its $\mathsf{PositiveUnaryDenominator}(e)$ component is transported by
\autoref{thm:rat-positive-unary-denominator-hsame-transport}, and its
dyadic-radius token is the same certified observation ledger entry of the
$\RegSeqRat$ spine.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the row named by
\autoref{def:dyadic-rat-core-observation-row}. It consists of a
$\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(d)$ row, a
$\mathsf{PositiveUnaryDenominator}(d)$ row, and a dyadic-radius token attached
to a certified unary observation entry in the $\RegSeqRat$ regularity ledger.

Transport the carrier face along $\hsame(d,e)$ using the $\RatUp$ carrier
transport already used in
\autoref{thm:dyadic-rat-core-scoped-source-package}. The compatibility between
the rational-history carrier and the positive-denominator row is the one
identified by
\autoref{thm:rat-history-carrier-positive-denominator-equivalence}.

Now apply
\autoref{thm:rat-positive-unary-denominator-hsame-transport} to carry the
positive-denominator component from $d$ to $e$. The endpoint rational row and
the certified dyadic request are the rows recorded by
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-carrier-obligation} inside the scoped spine of
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-stream-spine-scoped-package}.

The dyadic-radius token is not recomputed during transport: it remains attached
to the same certified observation ledger entry used by the source package.
Thus the transported carrier row, the transported positive-denominator row,
and the unchanged ledger token repack as a DyadicRatCore observation row for
$e$.
\end{proof}
