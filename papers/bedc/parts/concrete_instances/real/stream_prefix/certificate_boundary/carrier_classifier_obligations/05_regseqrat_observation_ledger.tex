\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat dyadic observation coverage]
\label{thm:regseqrat-dyadic-observation-coverage}
Let $s:\Hist\to\Hist$ carry a $\RegSeqRat$ stream certificate spine, and let
$n$ be a certified $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ observation index of that spine. Then
the exposed endpoint $s(n)$ has a DyadicRatCore observation row in the sense of
\autoref{def:dyadic-rat-core-observation-row}. Moreover, if a stream classifier
is transported pointwise along $\hsame$ to a target endpoint $s'(n)$, then the
same pointwise transport carries this DyadicRatCore observation row to
$s'(n)$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold Definition~\ref{def:regseqrat-stream-certificate-spine}. At the
certified observation index $n$, \autoref{thm:regseqrat-carrier-obligation}
supplies the endpoint $\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(s(n))$ row and the
dyadic-tolerance witness recorded in the regularity ledger.

The positive-denominator component is read from the RatUp carrier interface:
\autoref{thm:rat-history-carrier-positive-denominator-equivalence} converts the
endpoint carrier row into $\mathsf{PositiveUnaryDenominator}(s(n))$. These
three pieces are exactly the DyadicRatCore observation row of
\autoref{def:dyadic-rat-core-observation-row}.

For the transported endpoint, apply
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-classifier-obligation} to recover the pointwise
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$ row carried by the stream classifier. Its
$\hsame$ comparison transports the endpoint carrier/classifier face through the
existing $\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$ transport, and
\autoref{thm:rat-positive-unary-denominator-hsame-transport} transports the
positive-denominator row to $s'(n)$.

The dyadic-radius token is attached to the same certified observation request
in the spine ledger, so no additional completion or quotient datum is needed.
Thus the transported package is again a DyadicRatCore observation row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicRatCore observation row finite-ledger extraction]
\label{thm:dyadic-rat-core-observation-row-finite-ledger-extraction}
Let $s:\Hist\to\Hist$ carry a $\RegSeqRat$ stream certificate spine. For every
finite BEDC ledger bundle $L$ of certified $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ observation
requests in that spine, there is a finite family indexed by the entries
$n\in L$ whose $n$-th member is a
$\mathsf{DyadicRatCoreObservationRow}(s(n))$ in the sense of
\autoref{def:dyadic-rat-core-observation-row}. This family is read from the
DyadicRatCore scoped source package of
\autoref{thm:dyadic-rat-core-scoped-source-package}; its carrier and classifier
faces are precisely the $\RegSeqRat$ carrier and classifier obligations of
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-carrier-obligation} and
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-classifier-obligation}.

Moreover, if a stream classifier transports the spine pointwise to a target
stream $s':\Hist\to\Hist$, then the same finite family transports endpointwise:
for each certified request $n\in L$, the transported endpoint row is a
$\mathsf{DyadicRatCoreObservationRow}(s'(n))$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Induct on the finite ledger bundle $L$. The empty bundle has no certified
observation entry, so the indexed family and its transported endpoint family
are empty.

For a cons bundle, write the displayed head request as the certified
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ observation index $n$ and write $L'$ for the remaining
finite bundle. Apply
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-dyadic-observation-coverage} to the head request in the
$\RegSeqRat$ spine. It supplies the row
$\mathsf{DyadicRatCoreObservationRow}(s(n))$ by unpacking
\autoref{def:dyadic-rat-core-observation-row}, with the carrier datum supplied
by \autoref{thm:regseqrat-carrier-obligation} and the classifier transport
datum supplied by \autoref{thm:regseqrat-classifier-obligation}.

The source package identification in
\autoref{thm:dyadic-rat-core-scoped-source-package} says that no additional
ledger face is used beyond these certified unary observation entries. Therefore
the tail induction hypothesis extracts the finite family over $L'$ from the
same spine and transports it along the same pointwise classifier.

Adjoin the head row to the tail family. The transported head row is provided by
the transported clause of
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-dyadic-observation-coverage}, and the transported tail is
the induction hypothesis. Hence the cons bundle extracts and transports the
claimed finite family endpointwise.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat constant and transport obligation]
\label{thm:regseqrat-constant-and-transport-obligation}
The constant and transport obligations for $\RegSeqRat$ are the inhabitation of
the constant rational-observation stream and pointwise $\hsame$ transport of
stream classifiers. For every $d:\Hist$ with
$\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(d)$, $\mathsf{RatConstStream}_d$ carries a
$\RegSeqRat$ spine. If $s,t$ are classified streams and $s',t'$ are pointwise
$\hsame$ transports over certified unary observations, then the transported
stream-name classifier $\mathsf{RatStreamNameClassifier}(s',t')$ is part of
the same obligation set.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Definition~\ref{def:regseqrat-stream-certificate-spine} identifies the
constant case as a spine over a stream-name carrier plus a regularity ledger.
Apply \autoref{thm:regseqrat-constant-stream-spine} to the supplied
$\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(d)$; the resulting ledger is constant at every
certified unary observation.

For transport, apply
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-stream-classifier-stability-spine} to the two endpoint
$\hsame$ rows. It supplies the transported pointwise $\RatUp$ classifiers and
repackages them as $\mathsf{RatStreamNameClassifier}(s',t')$.

The scoped package
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-stream-spine-scoped-package} places the constant and
transport clauses in one certificate package, and
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-realup-seal-consumes-stream-spine} shows that these
clauses are sufficient for the downstream RealUp seal to consume the stream
classifier.
\end{proof}
