\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat carrier obligation]
\label{thm:regseqrat-carrier-obligation}
The carrier obligation for $\RegSeqRat$ is exactly the
$\mathsf{RatStreamNameCarrier}$ row together with the regularity witness over
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ observations. Concretely, for a rational-observation
map $s:\Hist\to\Hist$, a $\RegSeqRat$ carrier consists of
$\mathsf{RatStreamNameCarrier}(s)$ and a ledger which, at each certified unary
observation index $n$, exposes the endpoint $\RatUp$ carrier row
$\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(s(n))$ and the dyadic-tolerance witness demanded by
the stream regularity check.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold the spine named in
Definition~\ref{def:regseqrat-stream-certificate-spine}. Its first component
is the $\mathsf{RatStreamNameCarrier}$ row, and its ledger is indexed only by
certified $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ observations.

The endpoint carrier rows are supplied by
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-stream-classifier-stability-spine}; the constant case is
supplied by \autoref{thm:regseqrat-constant-stream-spine}. The public package
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-stream-spine-scoped-package} records these rows as the
carrier face of the scoped spine.

The seal consumer
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-realup-seal-consumes-stream-spine} uses no carrier data
outside this spine. Thus the carrier obligation is precisely the named
stream-name carrier plus the unary-observation regularity ledger.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat classifier obligation]
\label{thm:regseqrat-classifier-obligation}
The classifier obligation for $\RegSeqRat$ is exactly
$\mathsf{RealUnaryStreamClassifier}$ read with
$\mathsf{RatStreamNameClassifier}$ endpoint recovery. For carried streams
$s,t:\Hist\to\Hist$, a classifier row records the pointwise $\RatUp$
comparison over every certified $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ observation and
recovers the two endpoint stream-name carrier rows used by
$\mathsf{RatStreamNameClassifier}(s,t)$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from Definition~\ref{def:regseqrat-stream-certificate-spine}. The spine
does not introduce a separate classifier relation; it stores the RealUp unary
stream classifier over the same BEDC unary observations.

Apply \autoref{thm:regseqrat-stream-classifier-stability-spine}. It reads the
pointwise $\RatUp$ classifier rows from
$\mathsf{RealUnaryStreamClassifier}$, recovers the endpoint carrier rows, and
repackages the transported data as a
$\mathsf{RatStreamNameClassifier}$ row. The constant boundary is the reflexive
case supplied by \autoref{thm:regseqrat-constant-stream-spine}.

The scoped package
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-stream-spine-scoped-package} lists the reflexive,
symmetric, transitive, and transported classifier rows together. The seal
consumer \autoref{thm:regseqrat-realup-seal-consumes-stream-spine} confirms
that the same row is the classifier exported through the $\RealUp$ interface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat constant and transport obligation]
\label{thm:regseqrat-constant-and-transport-obligation}
The constant and transport obligations for $\RegSeqRat$ are the inhabitation of
the constant rational-observation stream and pointwise $\hsame$ transport of
stream classifiers. For every $d:\Hist$ with
$\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(d)$, $\mathsf{RatConstStream}_d$ carries a
$\RegSeqRat$ spine. If $s,t$ are classified streams and $s',t'$ are pointwise
$\hsame$ transports over certified unary observations, then the transported
stream-name classifier $\mathsf{RatStreamNameClassifier}(s',t')$ is part of
the same obligation set.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Definition~\ref{def:regseqrat-stream-certificate-spine} identifies the
constant case as a spine over a stream-name carrier plus a regularity ledger.
Apply \autoref{thm:regseqrat-constant-stream-spine} to the supplied
$\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(d)$; the resulting ledger is constant at every
certified unary observation.

For transport, apply
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-stream-classifier-stability-spine} to the two endpoint
$\hsame$ rows. It supplies the transported pointwise $\RatUp$ classifiers and
repackages them as $\mathsf{RatStreamNameClassifier}(s',t')$.

The scoped package
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-stream-spine-scoped-package} places the constant and
transport clauses in one certificate package, and
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-realup-seal-consumes-stream-spine} shows that these
clauses are sufficient for the downstream RealUp seal to consume the stream
classifier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat dyadic ledger and RealUp seal obligation]
\label{thm:regseqrat-dyadic-ledger-and-real-seal-obligation}
The dyadic-ledger and $\RealUp$ seal obligation for $\RegSeqRat$ consists of
finite unary observation indices, the endpoint $\RatUp$ rows read at those
indices, the dyadic-tolerance witnesses used by regularity, and the seal export
of the recorded $\mathsf{RealUnaryStreamClassifier}$ row. Whenever sealed
$\RealUp$ candidates expose streams satisfying the $\RegSeqRat$ spine and
record their stream comparison row, the seal exports the RealUp observation
classifier required by \autoref{def:real-classifier-specification}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold Definition~\ref{def:regseqrat-stream-certificate-spine}. Its ledger is
finite at the level of requested unary observations and dyadic-tolerance
checks, and each ledger entry is backed by endpoint $\RatUp$ carrier and
classifier rows.

The constant ledger is supplied by
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-constant-stream-spine}. The general endpoint and
transport rows are supplied by
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-stream-classifier-stability-spine}, while
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-stream-spine-scoped-package} gathers the ledger,
classifier, constant, and transport faces as one scoped certificate package.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:regseqrat-realup-seal-consumes-stream-spine}. The
seal reads the already recorded
$\mathsf{RealUnaryStreamClassifier}$ row and exports exactly the observation
classifier required by the RealUp boundary, without adding quotient or
completion data.
\end{proof}
