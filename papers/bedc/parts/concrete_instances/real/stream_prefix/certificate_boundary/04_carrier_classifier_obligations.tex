\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat carrier obligation]
\label{thm:regseqrat-carrier-obligation}
The carrier obligation for $\RegSeqRat$ is exactly the
$\mathsf{RatStreamNameCarrier}$ row together with the regularity witness over
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ observations. Concretely, for a rational-observation
map $s:\Hist\to\Hist$, a $\RegSeqRat$ carrier consists of
$\mathsf{RatStreamNameCarrier}(s)$ and a ledger which, at each certified unary
observation index $n$, exposes the endpoint $\RatUp$ carrier row
$\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(s(n))$ and the dyadic-tolerance witness demanded by
the stream regularity check.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold the spine named in
Definition~\ref{def:regseqrat-stream-certificate-spine}. Its first component
is the $\mathsf{RatStreamNameCarrier}$ row, and its ledger is indexed only by
certified $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ observations.

The endpoint carrier rows are supplied by
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-stream-classifier-stability-spine}; the constant case is
supplied by \autoref{thm:regseqrat-constant-stream-spine}. The public package
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-stream-spine-scoped-package} records these rows as the
carrier face of the scoped spine.

The seal consumer
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-realup-seal-consumes-stream-spine} uses no carrier data
outside this spine. Thus the carrier obligation is precisely the named
stream-name carrier plus the unary-observation regularity ledger.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat classifier obligation]
\label{thm:regseqrat-classifier-obligation}
The classifier obligation for $\RegSeqRat$ is exactly
$\mathsf{RealUnaryStreamClassifier}$ read with
$\mathsf{RatStreamNameClassifier}$ endpoint recovery. For carried streams
$s,t:\Hist\to\Hist$, a classifier row records the pointwise $\RatUp$
comparison over every certified $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ observation and
recovers the two endpoint stream-name carrier rows used by
$\mathsf{RatStreamNameClassifier}(s,t)$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from Definition~\ref{def:regseqrat-stream-certificate-spine}. The spine
does not introduce a separate classifier relation; it stores the RealUp unary
stream classifier over the same BEDC unary observations.

Apply \autoref{thm:regseqrat-stream-classifier-stability-spine}. It reads the
pointwise $\RatUp$ classifier rows from
$\mathsf{RealUnaryStreamClassifier}$, recovers the endpoint carrier rows, and
repackages the transported data as a
$\mathsf{RatStreamNameClassifier}$ row. The constant boundary is the reflexive
case supplied by \autoref{thm:regseqrat-constant-stream-spine}.

The scoped package
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-stream-spine-scoped-package} lists the reflexive,
symmetric, transitive, and transported classifier rows together. The seal
consumer \autoref{thm:regseqrat-realup-seal-consumes-stream-spine} confirms
that the same row is the classifier exported through the $\RealUp$ interface.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[DyadicRatCore observation row]
\label{def:dyadic-rat-core-observation-row}
A DyadicRatCore observation row for a BEDC history $d$ is the package consisting
of a $\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(d)$ witness, a positive unary denominator row
$\mathsf{PositiveUnaryDenominator}(d)$, and the dyadic-radius token used by the
$\RegSeqRat$ regularity check. Its classifier face is inherited from
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$, together with $\hsame$ transport of the
positive-denominator row.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicRatCore scoped source package]
\label{thm:dyadic-rat-core-scoped-source-package}
The DyadicRatCore source used by $\RegSeqRat$ is the concrete BEDC scoped
source package over $\Hist$ whose carrier, classifier, stability, and ledger
faces are all read from the rational-history endpoint spine. For each endpoint
history $d$, the carrier face is
$\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(d)$ together with
$\mathsf{PositiveUnaryDenominator}(d)$ and the dyadic-radius token recorded by
the $\RegSeqRat$ regularity request. For endpoints $d,e$, the classifier face
is $\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(d,e)$ together with $\hsame$ transport of the
positive-denominator rows. The stability face is inherited from the $\RatUp$
carrier and classifier transports and from the pointwise classifier obligation
of $\RegSeqRat$. The ledger face is exactly the finite family of
dyadic-tolerance requests already recorded in the $\RegSeqRat$ spine.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier face. Definition~\ref{def:dyadic-rat-core-observation-row}
names the three endpoint rows: the rational-history carrier, the positive
unary denominator, and the dyadic-radius token. The endpoint carrier and the
finite dyadic request are supplied by
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-carrier-obligation}, while
\autoref{thm:rat-history-carrier-positive-denominator-equivalence} converts
the carrier row into the positive-denominator row.

Next read the classifier and stability faces. The pointwise stream classifier
obligation in \autoref{thm:regseqrat-classifier-obligation} exposes the
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(d,e)$ endpoint comparison used by the
DyadicRatCore source. RatUp carrier and classifier transport carries the
rational-history rows along the same $\hsame$ comparison, and
\autoref{thm:rat-positive-unary-denominator-hsame-transport} carries the
positive-denominator row.

It remains only to identify the ledger. No request outside the
$\RegSeqRat$ spine is used: the dyadic-radius token is attached to the same
certified unary observation entry that supplies the endpoint rational row.
Thus the source package has precisely the stated carrier, classifier,
stability, and finite ledger faces over $\Hist$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicRatCore observation row hsame transport]
\label{thm:dyadic-rat-core-observation-row-hsame-transport}
Let $d,e:\Hist$. If $d$ has a DyadicRatCore observation row in the sense of
\autoref{def:dyadic-rat-core-observation-row} and $\hsame(d,e)$ holds, then
$e$ has a DyadicRatCore observation row. Its $\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(e)$
component is the $\RatUp$ carrier transport of the carrier component at $d$,
its $\mathsf{PositiveUnaryDenominator}(e)$ component is transported by
\autoref{thm:rat-positive-unary-denominator-hsame-transport}, and its
dyadic-radius token is the same certified observation ledger entry of the
$\RegSeqRat$ spine.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the row named by
\autoref{def:dyadic-rat-core-observation-row}. It consists of a
$\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(d)$ row, a
$\mathsf{PositiveUnaryDenominator}(d)$ row, and a dyadic-radius token attached
to a certified unary observation entry in the $\RegSeqRat$ regularity ledger.

Transport the carrier face along $\hsame(d,e)$ using the $\RatUp$ carrier
transport already used in
\autoref{thm:dyadic-rat-core-scoped-source-package}. The compatibility between
the rational-history carrier and the positive-denominator row is the one
identified by
\autoref{thm:rat-history-carrier-positive-denominator-equivalence}.

Now apply
\autoref{thm:rat-positive-unary-denominator-hsame-transport} to carry the
positive-denominator component from $d$ to $e$. The endpoint rational row and
the certified dyadic request are the rows recorded by
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-carrier-obligation} inside the scoped spine of
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-stream-spine-scoped-package}.

The dyadic-radius token is not recomputed during transport: it remains attached
to the same certified observation ledger entry used by the source package.
Thus the transported carrier row, the transported positive-denominator row,
and the unchanged ledger token repack as a DyadicRatCore observation row for
$e$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat dyadic observation coverage]
\label{thm:regseqrat-dyadic-observation-coverage}
Let $s:\Hist\to\Hist$ carry a $\RegSeqRat$ stream certificate spine, and let
$n$ be a certified $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ observation index of that spine. Then
the exposed endpoint $s(n)$ has a DyadicRatCore observation row in the sense of
\autoref{def:dyadic-rat-core-observation-row}. Moreover, if a stream classifier
is transported pointwise along $\hsame$ to a target endpoint $s'(n)$, then the
same pointwise transport carries this DyadicRatCore observation row to
$s'(n)$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold Definition~\ref{def:regseqrat-stream-certificate-spine}. At the
certified observation index $n$, \autoref{thm:regseqrat-carrier-obligation}
supplies the endpoint $\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(s(n))$ row and the
dyadic-tolerance witness recorded in the regularity ledger.

The positive-denominator component is read from the RatUp carrier interface:
\autoref{thm:rat-history-carrier-positive-denominator-equivalence} converts the
endpoint carrier row into $\mathsf{PositiveUnaryDenominator}(s(n))$. These
three pieces are exactly the DyadicRatCore observation row of
\autoref{def:dyadic-rat-core-observation-row}.

For the transported endpoint, apply
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-classifier-obligation} to recover the pointwise
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$ row carried by the stream classifier. Its
$\hsame$ comparison transports the endpoint carrier/classifier face through the
existing $\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$ transport, and
\autoref{thm:rat-positive-unary-denominator-hsame-transport} transports the
positive-denominator row to $s'(n)$.

The dyadic-radius token is attached to the same certified observation request
in the spine ledger, so no additional completion or quotient datum is needed.
Thus the transported package is again a DyadicRatCore observation row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat constant and transport obligation]
\label{thm:regseqrat-constant-and-transport-obligation}
The constant and transport obligations for $\RegSeqRat$ are the inhabitation of
the constant rational-observation stream and pointwise $\hsame$ transport of
stream classifiers. For every $d:\Hist$ with
$\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(d)$, $\mathsf{RatConstStream}_d$ carries a
$\RegSeqRat$ spine. If $s,t$ are classified streams and $s',t'$ are pointwise
$\hsame$ transports over certified unary observations, then the transported
stream-name classifier $\mathsf{RatStreamNameClassifier}(s',t')$ is part of
the same obligation set.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Definition~\ref{def:regseqrat-stream-certificate-spine} identifies the
constant case as a spine over a stream-name carrier plus a regularity ledger.
Apply \autoref{thm:regseqrat-constant-stream-spine} to the supplied
$\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(d)$; the resulting ledger is constant at every
certified unary observation.

For transport, apply
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-stream-classifier-stability-spine} to the two endpoint
$\hsame$ rows. It supplies the transported pointwise $\RatUp$ classifiers and
repackages them as $\mathsf{RatStreamNameClassifier}(s',t')$.

The scoped package
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-stream-spine-scoped-package} places the constant and
transport clauses in one certificate package, and
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-realup-seal-consumes-stream-spine} shows that these
clauses are sufficient for the downstream RealUp seal to consume the stream
classifier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat dyadic ledger and RealUp seal obligation]
\label{thm:regseqrat-dyadic-ledger-and-real-seal-obligation}
The dyadic-ledger and $\RealUp$ seal obligation for $\RegSeqRat$ consists of
finite unary observation indices, the endpoint $\RatUp$ rows read at those
indices, the dyadic-tolerance witnesses used by regularity, and the seal export
of the recorded $\mathsf{RealUnaryStreamClassifier}$ row. Whenever sealed
$\RealUp$ candidates expose streams satisfying the $\RegSeqRat$ spine and
record their stream comparison row, the seal exports the RealUp observation
classifier required by \autoref{def:real-classifier-specification}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold Definition~\ref{def:regseqrat-stream-certificate-spine}. Its ledger is
finite at the level of requested unary observations and dyadic-tolerance
checks, and each ledger entry is backed by endpoint $\RatUp$ carrier and
classifier rows.

The constant ledger is supplied by
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-constant-stream-spine}. The general endpoint and
transport rows are supplied by
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-stream-classifier-stability-spine}, while
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-stream-spine-scoped-package} gathers the ledger,
classifier, constant, and transport faces as one scoped certificate package.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:regseqrat-realup-seal-consumes-stream-spine}. The
seal reads the already recorded
$\mathsf{RealUnaryStreamClassifier}$ row and exports exactly the observation
classifier required by the RealUp boundary, without adding quotient or
completion data.
\end{proof}
