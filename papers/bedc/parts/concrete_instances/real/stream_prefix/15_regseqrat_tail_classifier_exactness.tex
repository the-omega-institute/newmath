tail-window normal form.

Now apply
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-tail-classifier-reindexed-cover-compatibility}. It
shows that the same projection remains valid after finite-window cover
gluing, so the reindexed boundary does not depend on a hidden choice of local
tail classifier.

Finally combine the StreamName side with
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-pointwise-handoff} and
\autoref{thm:streamname-transported-window-regseqrat-realup-feed}. These
handoff theorems expose exactly the finite StreamName rows that a
$\RegSeqRat$ or $\RealUp$ consumer may read. Repacking those rows with the
RegSeqRat tail normal form gives the stated $\RealUp$ boundary and leaves no
slot for seal, completion, quotient, host-index, order, arithmetic, bridge, or
hidden selector data.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat tail-classifier reindexed finite-cover compatibility]
\label{thm:regseqrat-tail-classifier-reindexed-cover-compatibility}
Let $\{W_i\}_{i\in I}$ be a finite family of selected finite $\RealUp$
observation-window packages with compatible local selected $\RegSeqRat$
tail-classifier rows on every certified overlap. Let $\rho_i$ be certified
unary reindexers for the member windows, and let $\rho$ be the certified unary
reindexer induced on the displayed finite union window. Suppose these
reindexers preserve the displayed $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRat$,
DyadicRatCore, $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$,
$\mathsf{InBundle}$, finite $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and
$\mathsf{RealLedgerPolicy}$ rows, and suppose the transported overlap rows are
the same rows as the overlaps of the reindexed member windows.

Then the selected $\RegSeqRat$ tail classifier obtained by first gluing the
finite cover and then reindexing by $\rho$ is the same finite-window readback
row as the classifier obtained by reindexing each member window by $\rho_i$
and then applying finite-cover gluing to the reindexed cover. The compatibility
surface contains no quotient, completion, Classical representative,
metric-completeness, arithmetic or order row, standard-bridge row, or hidden
regular-sequence selector row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First glue the original cover with
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-tail-classifier-finite-cover-sheaf}. The compatible
local selected rows determine a unique global selected tail row on the
displayed finite union, and each member row is its pullback.

Next transport this global row along $\rho$. The one-step case is
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-tail-classifier-reindexing-stability}, and the
finite-window schedule is handled by
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-tail-classifier-reindexing-composition}. Thus the
glue-then-reindex construction has a displayed selected row on the reindexed
finite union.

Now reindex each member window by $\rho_i$. The same stability theorem
identifies every transported local classifier with the selected classifier on
the corresponding reindexed member window, and the transported overlap
hypothesis gives the compatibility condition required by
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-tail-classifier-finite-cover-sheaf}. Therefore the
reindexed cover also glues to a selected global tail row.

It remains to compare the two global rows. On each reindexed member window,
both constructions pull back to the same transported local selected
$\RegSeqRat$ tail classifier. Apply
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-tail-classifier-pullback-uniqueness} on the reindexed
finite cover to identify the two global rows. The row inventory used in each
step is exactly the displayed $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRat$, DyadicRatCore,
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, $\mathsf{InBundle}$,
$\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\mathsf{RealLedgerPolicy}$ inventory, so the
listed quotient, completion, Classical, metric-completeness, arithmetic,
order, standard-bridge, and hidden selector rows are excluded.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat tail-classifier nested-cover associativity]
\label{thm:regseqrat-tail-classifier-nested-cover-associativity}
Let $\{W_i\}_{i\in I}$ be a finite family of selected finite $\RealUp$
observation-window packages. For each $i$, let $\{W_{ij}\}_{j\in J_i}$ be a
finite certified subcover of $W_i$ by selected finite windows, and suppose
all first-level and second-level overlaps preserve the displayed
$\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRat$, DyadicRatCore, $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$,
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, $\mathsf{InBundle}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and
$\mathsf{RealLedgerPolicy}$ rows.

Then the selected $\RegSeqRat$ tail-classifier row obtained by first gluing
each subcover $\{W_{ij}\}_{j\in J_i}$, then gluing the resulting member
windows $\{W_i\}_{i\in I}$, is the same finite-window row as the selected
classifier obtained by flattening the family $\{W_{ij}\}_{i,j}$ and gluing
that single certified finite cover once. The associativity surface exports no
quotient, completion, Classical representative, metric-completeness,
arithmetic or order row, standard-bridge row, or hidden regular-sequence
selector row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First glue each certified subcover $\{W_{ij}\}_{j\in J_i}$. By
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-tail-classifier-finite-cover-sheaf}, every compatible
subfamily determines a unique selected tail-classifier row on $W_i$, and the
pullback of that row to each $W_{ij}$ is the displayed local classifier.

Next glue the resulting family of member windows. The same finite-cover
theorem gives a selected global row over the finite union of the $W_i$, and
the overlap hypotheses ensure that its pullback to each $W_i$ is the row
obtained from the corresponding subcover.

Now flatten the two-level family to the certified finite cover
$\{W_{ij}\}_{i,j}$. Apply
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-tail-classifier-reindexed-cover-compatibility} to
identify the flattened cover with the same displayed finite-window inventory
used by the staged construction; the reindexing data here is only the
certified inclusion and ordering of the flattened subwindows.

It remains to compare the staged row with the flattened row. Both pull back
to the same selected $\RegSeqRat$ tail-classifier row on every certified
subwindow $W_{ij}$, so
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-tail-classifier-pullback-uniqueness} identifies the
two global rows. Each step used only the displayed $\StreamNameUp$,
$\RegSeqRat$, DyadicRatCore, $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$,
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, $\mathsf{InBundle}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and
$\mathsf{RealLedgerPolicy}$ rows, leaving no slot for the excluded host or
completion data.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat tail-classifier atlas descent]
\label{thm:regseqrat-tail-classifier-atlas-descent}
Let $\mathcal{A}=\{W_i\}_{i\in I}$ be a finite atlas of selected finite
$\RealUp$ observation-window packages. Suppose every pairwise overlap
$W_i\cap W_j$ and every triple overlap $W_i\cap W_j\cap W_k$ preserves the
displayed $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRat$, DyadicRatCore,
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, $\mathsf{InBundle}$,
$\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\mathsf{RealLedgerPolicy}$ rows.

Then the atlas exposes a single selected $\RegSeqRat$ tail-classifier row on
the finite union of its windows. For every chart $W_i$, the chartwise selected
tail-classifier row factors through this atlas row, and this factorisation is
unique among finite-window mediators preserving the displayed rows. The
descent surface exports no quotient, completion, Classical representative,
metric-completeness row, arithmetic or order row, standard-bridge row, or
hidden selector row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:regseqrat-tail-classifier-exactness} to each selected chart.
This identifies the chartwise tail-classifier row with the displayed
$\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRat$, DyadicRatCore, unary-history, probe-bundle,
membership, transport, continuation, provenance, and ledger inventory.

Next form the finite atlas union. By
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-tail-classifier-union-exhaustion}, the union consumes
exactly the rows contributed by the selected windows and their certified
overlaps. No additional host row is introduced while passing from the chart
family to the finite union.

Now decompose the atlas by its chart partition. The coherence result
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-tail-classifier-partition-coherence} says that the
selected row obtained from the union is the same row obtained by assembling the
partition pieces with their displayed overlap transports.

Triple refinements are handled by
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-tail-classifier-nested-cover-associativity}. It
identifies the staged gluing over pairwise overlaps with the flattened finite
cover over all chart and triple-overlap pieces, so the resulting atlas row is
independent of the order in which the certified finite overlaps are read.

It remains to show the universal factorisation claim. A chartwise selected
tail pulls back from the atlas row by construction. If another finite-window
mediator has the same pullback on every chart while preserving the displayed
rows, then
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-tail-classifier-pullback-uniqueness} identifies that
mediator with the atlas row. Thus each chartwise tail factors uniquely through
the displayed atlas descent row, and the excluded quotient, completion,
Classical, metric, arithmetic, order, bridge, and hidden-selector data have no
projection in the construction.
\end{proof}
