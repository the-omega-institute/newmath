\begin{theorem}[Real stream selected appended e-one tail cancellation]
\label{thm:real-stream-classifier-selected-append-e1-tail-hsame-cancel}
For rational-observation streams $x,y:\mathbb{N}\to\Hist$, index $n$, and
histories $d,e,tail_D,tail_E$, if the selected stream endpoints display as
appended e-one tails, then tail equality cancels the displayed tails and
recovers the denominator head comparison:
$$
\begin{array}{l}
\mathsf{RealStreamClassifier}(x,y)\Longrightarrow
\hsame(x(n),\Eone(d\cdot\Eone(tail_D)))\Longrightarrow\\
\qquad
\hsame(y(n),\Eone(e\cdot\Eone(tail_E)))\Longrightarrow
\hsame(tail_D,tail_E)\Longrightarrow
\hsame(d,e).
\end{array}
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Evaluate the stream classifier at the selected index to obtain the RatUp
history classifier for $x(n)$ and $y(n)$. Transport this classifier across the
two displayed e-one endpoint comparisons. RatUp e-one tail readback gives the
comparison between $d\cdot\Eone(tail_D)$ and $e\cdot\Eone(tail_E)$. The tail
comparison identifies the e-one suffixes, and BEDC append right cancellation
leaves exactly $\hsame(d,e)$.
\end{proof}
