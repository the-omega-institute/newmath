\begin{theorem}[Real separability Cauchy-dense forward link]
\label{thm:real-separability-cauchy-dense-forward-link}
Every $\RealSeparabilityUp$ packet routes finite dyadic density witnesses through the dense Cauchy route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-dense-embedding-namecert} and the Cauchy completion reflector of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchycompletionreflector-namecert} before a downstream metric-completion consumer reads a countable dense surface. \end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted $\RealSeparabilityUp$ carrier to its rational enumeration, dyadic tolerance, finite window, regular readback, terminal Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows. The dyadic witness is therefore a finite row of the separability packet rather than a detached dense subset.

After this route has displayed the dense Cauchy source, the Cauchy completion reflector named by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchycompletionreflector-namecert} supplies the completion-facing handoff.

Finally, componentwise $\hsame$ transport and $\Cont$ replay preserve the same displayed route into downstream metric-completion consumers. Since no row in the packet names quotient equality, a selected dense point, countable choice, or ambient completeness, none of those objects is exported by the forward link.
\end{proof}
