\begin{theorem}[Real dyadic tail-radius ledger dependency]
\label{thm:real-dyadic-tail-radius-ledger-dependency}
Every accepted $\RealUp$ seal-facing consumer that uses the dyadic tail-radius
route first factors through
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadic-tail-radius-ledger-namecert} before the
local $\RealUp$ seal row is consumed. When the same consumer is
completion-facing and reads a witness ledger, it also factors through
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-witness-ledger-namecert} before the
completion-facing $\RealUp$ seal row is consumed.

Equivalently, the admitted finite route is confined to the displayed chain
$$
\DyadicRatCoreUp \to \StreamNameUp \to \RegSeqRatUp
\to \DyadicTailRadiusLedgerUp \to \CauchyWitnessLedgerUp \to \RealUp
$$
with the $\CauchyWitnessLedgerUp$ row present only for completion-facing
witness reads. The route exports no quotient stream equality, host real
equality, selected external limit, countable choice, or ambient completeness
principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the Real dyadic StreamName RegSeqRat route of
\autoref{thm:real-dyadic-stream-regseq-route}. It places every accepted
open-phase seal read whose source is presented through dyadic observations,
finite StreamName windows, and regular-rational readback on the ordered route
$\DyadicRatCoreUp \to \StreamNameUp \to \RegSeqRatUp \to \RealUp$.

Next apply the DyadicTailRadiusLedger finite route package from
\autoref{thm:dyadic-tail-radius-ledger-scoped-route-package}. Its carrier
inserts the finite precision request, tail-window ledger, StreamName windows,
dyadic readback, RegSeqRat handoff, and Real seal row into one ledger route.
Thus a seal-facing consumer using the dyadic tail-radius route cannot reach
the Real seal before that ledger has been consumed.

For completion-facing witness reads, apply the CauchyWitnessLedger budget
factorization and Real completion handoff,
\autoref{thm:cauchy-witness-ledger-budget-factorization} and
\autoref{thm:cauchy-witness-ledger-real-completion-handoff}. These force the
consumer through the finite witness ledger before the completion-seal
classifier and the Real-facing completion read are available.

Finally combine the non-escape boundaries of the three routes:
\autoref{thm:real-dyadic-stream-regseq-route},
\autoref{thm:dyadic-tail-radius-ledger-nonescape}, and
\autoref{thm:cauchy-witness-ledger-forbidden-coordinate-exclusion}. Since none
of the composed carriers has a coordinate for quotient stream equality, host
real equality, selected external limits, countable choice, or ambient
completeness, the composed Real dependency route exports none of them.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real dyadic tail-radius seal determinacy]
\label{thm:real-dyadic-tail-radius-seal-determinacy}
Let two accepted $\RealUp$ seal-facing consumers use the dyadic tail-radius
route. If they expose the same $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint and radius rows,
the same $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window rows, the same $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff
rows, the same $\DyadicTailRadiusLedgerUp$ route, the same
$\CauchyWitnessLedgerUp$ witness row whenever the read is completion-facing,
and the same displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$
support rows, then they determine the same finite $\RealUp$ seal boundary.

Conversely, every admitted $\RealUp$ seal boundary that uses this dyadic
tail-radius route is read through exactly those rows: dyadic endpoint and
radius data, finite StreamName windows, regular-rational handoff, tail-radius
ledger route, the completion-facing witness row when present, and the displayed
$\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support. The statement
does not assert $\RealUp$ completeness, quotient-stream equality, host real
equality, selected external limits, countable choice, or metric-completion
equality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply \autoref{thm:real-dyadic-tail-radius-ledger-dependency}. It fixes
the ordered dependency route
$$
\DyadicRatCoreUp \to \StreamNameUp \to \RegSeqRatUp
\to \DyadicTailRadiusLedgerUp \to \CauchyWitnessLedgerUp \to \RealUp
$$
with the witness-ledger row present only for completion-facing reads. Thus two
accepted consumers with the same displayed rows have the same ordered route to
the Real seal.

Next use \autoref{thm:real-dyadic-stream-regseq-route}. The initial segment of
the route is already determined by the dyadic observations, finite StreamName
windows, RegSeqRat regular readback, and the structural $\BHist$, $\hsame$,
$\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows. Hence the two consumers cannot differ
before the tail-radius ledger is reached.

Now apply \autoref{thm:dyadic-tail-radius-ledger-scoped-route-package} together
with \autoref{thm:dyadic-tail-radius-ledger-nonescape}. The package fixes the
finite tail-radius packet on the route, and non-escape prevents an additional
quotient, host-equality, selected-limit, unbounded-search, independent-ledger,
or ambient-completeness coordinate from entering the seal boundary.

For completion-facing reads, apply
\autoref{thm:cauchy-witness-ledger-budget-factorization},
\autoref{thm:cauchy-witness-ledger-real-completion-handoff}, and
\autoref{thm:cauchy-witness-ledger-forbidden-coordinate-exclusion}. These
place the witness row behind the finite budget route, hand it to the Real
completion-facing consumer through the displayed support rows, and exclude
quotient streams, host Real equality, selected external limits, countable
choice, and ambient completeness from the read.

The forward determinacy follows because every row on the seal-facing route has
been fixed componentwise. The converse follows by reading any admitted boundary
through the same factorization and non-escape results: there is no public
coordinate outside the listed finite dyadic, StreamName, RegSeqRat,
tail-radius, witness-ledger, and structural support rows, so the listed rows
are exactly the finite $\RealUp$ seal boundary for this route.
\end{proof}
