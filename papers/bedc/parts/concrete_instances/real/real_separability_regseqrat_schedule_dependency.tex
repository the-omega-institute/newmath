\label{thm:real-separability-regseqrat-schedule-dependency}
Every countable dense route exported by an accepted $\RealSeparabilityUp$ packet consumes the regular-Cauchy schedule through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic-density witness surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadic-density-real-namecert}, and the terminal seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. \begin{proof}
Use \autoref{def:real-separability-regseqrat-schedule} to expose the finite schedule rows $S,R,D,E,H,C,P,N$. The $\RegSeqRatUp$ row supplies the regular rational readback consumed by the density request, while the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row fixes the scale at which that request is inspected.

Next read the dyadic-density surface through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadic-density-real-namecert}. Its witness is attached to the same finite window and tolerance rows before the route reaches the terminal $\RealUp$ seal.

The transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows are the same rows already carried by the accepted $\RealSeparabilityUp$ packet. Since no carrier coordinate names a selected nearest point, host dense subset, quotient equality, countable-choice selector, or ambient completeness theorem, the schedule dependency is exactly the displayed RegSeqRat and dyadic-density route.
\end{proof}
