\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\TotallyBoundedCompletionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-totallyboundedcompletion-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges constructive analysis, the $\TotallyBoundedCompletionUp$ packet records the finite route by which a totally bounded metric carrier passes through constructive completion while retaining explicit net data. It reads the finite-net surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-totallybounded-namecert}, the precompact bridge of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-precompactmetric-namecert}, the basis generator of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-filter-basis-namecert}, the dense embedding row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completionembedding-namecert}, the constructive completion route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-constructive-metric-completion-namecert}, and the uniformly continuous handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniform-continuous-extension-namecert}. The packet is a finite completion-facing certificate for displayed total-bounded net ledgers; it is not an open-cover compactness theorem, a quotient completion, a selected limit-point operation, a countable-choice principle, or a full completeness theorem for $\RealUp$.

\begin{definition}[Totally bounded completion carrier]
\label{def:totally-bounded-completion-carrier}
A $\TotallyBoundedCompletionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(X,T,N,F,E,C,S,U,H,P,L)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the source metric carrier row, including the accepted point histories, distance-witness rows, and classifier transports read by the total-bounded and completion-facing coordinates.
\item $T$ is the $\TotallyBoundedUp$ finite-net ledger. For each displayed positive tolerance it records the closed $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ of centers, $\mathsf{InBundle}$ membership rows, center-carrier rows, distance witnesses, and rational bound ledgers.
\item $N$ is the net-refinement row. It records how a finer tolerance request refines a previously displayed finite net without replacing the net by an open cover or by a host finite set.
\item $F$ is the $\CauchyFilterBasisUp$ row generated from the same metric and net data, including the finite basis packets and common-refinement reads consumed by the completion route.
\item $E$ is the $\CompletionEmbeddingUp$ row that sends source observations into the dense completion boundary while preserving the displayed metric and net ledgers.
\item $C$ is the $\ConstructiveMetricCompletionUp$ route through regular Cauchy names, dyadic tolerances, Cauchy-filter basis data, dense embedding, separated comparison, and uniformly continuous extension.
\item $S$ is the separated completion equality row. It compares completion reads only through the displayed Cauchy-filter and zero-distance route.
\item $U$ is the $\UniformContinuousExtensionUp$ handoff row for consumers that extend uniformly continuous maps from the dense source through the completed packet.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the source metric, finite nets, net refinements, Cauchy-filter basis, completion embedding, constructive completion route, separated equality, extension handoff, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\MetricSpaceUp$, $\TotallyBoundedUp$, $\PrecompactMetricUp$, $\CauchyFilterBasisUp$, $\CompletionEmbeddingUp$, $\ConstructiveMetricCompletionUp$, $\UniformContinuousExtensionUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $L$ is the local $\NameCert_{\TotallyBoundedCompletionUp}$ row naming the displayed packet and the permitted reads from it.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier on such packets is componentwise transport through $H$ together with replay through the displayed route rows. No coordinate exports open-cover compactness, quotient representatives, selected limit points, countable choice, an ambient complete metric space, or detached $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[TotallyBoundedCompletion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:totally-bounded-completion-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\TotallyBoundedCompletionUp$ carrier
$K=(X,T,N,F,E,C,S,U,H,P,L)$, the local $\NameCert_{\TotallyBoundedCompletionUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed source metric, total-bounded net ledger, net-refinement row, Cauchy-filter basis, dense completion embedding, constructive completion route, separated equality, uniformly continuous handoff, transport ledger, provenance, and local naming row;
\item finite-net persistence, requiring every completion-facing tolerance request to read the centers, membership constructors, distance witnesses, and rational bounds recorded in $T$ and refined through $N$;
\item basis and embedding replay, requiring each completion read to consume $F$ and $E$ before it reaches the constructive completion route $C$;
\item separated and extension handoff, requiring equality of completion reads to pass through $S$ and every uniformly continuous consumer to pass through $U$ after $F,E,C$ have been displayed;
\item ledger non-escape, excluding open-cover compactness, quotient completion, selected limit points, countable choice, detached $\RealUp$ completeness, and ambient completeness.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored outside these displayed rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:totally-bounded-completion-carrier}. The packet lists the metric source, finite-net ledger, net-refinement row, Cauchy-filter basis, dense embedding, constructive completion route, separated equality, extension handoff, transport ledger, provenance, and local naming row.

Finite-net persistence is read from $T$ and $N$. A tolerance request first inspects the closed $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, its $\mathsf{InBundle}$ constructors, the center-carrier rows, and the metric distance witnesses in $T$; refinement then follows the displayed row $N$ and keeps the same ledger style at the finer tolerance.

Basis and embedding replay is forced by the route order. The Cauchy-filter basis row $F$ consumes the finite net data before a completion-facing read is formed, and the embedding row $E$ places source observations inside the dense completion boundary before the constructive route $C$ can be read.

The separated and extension obligations are then local to $S$ and $U$. Equality of completion reads is compared through $S$, while uniformly continuous consumers can inspect the completed packet only through $U$ after the basis, embedding, and constructive completion rows are already present.

Finally, the provenance row $P$ and local naming row $L$ name only the displayed coordinates. Since none of those coordinates contains open-cover compactness, quotient representatives, selected limit points, countable choice, detached Real completeness, or ambient completeness, no such datum can be exported by the NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[TotallyBoundedCompletion net handoff]
\label{thm:totally-bounded-completion-net-handoff}
Let $K=(X,T,N,F,E,C,S,U,H,P,L)$ be an accepted $\TotallyBoundedCompletionUp$ packet. Every completion-facing consumer that requests finite total-bounded data factors through
$$
X,T,N \longmapsto F \longmapsto E \longmapsto C \longmapsto S,U \longmapsto H,P,L .
$$
Thus the consumer receives the original finite net ledger and its refinement row as part of the completion route, rather than an open cover, a selected convergent subnet, a quotient completion class, or an ambient compactness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the source metric row $X$ and the finite-net ledger $T$. These two rows provide the point classifier, distance witnesses, center names, membership constructors, and rational bounds that any total-bounded read may inspect.

The refinement row $N$ is the only refinement operation available to the consumer. It moves from one displayed tolerance request to a finer one while retaining the same finite ledger shape, so the consumer cannot replace the packet by an open cover or by a host-level finite set.

The completion route then consumes this refined net data through $F$, $E$, and $C$. The Cauchy-filter basis is generated from the displayed rows, the embedding row places source observations densely in the completion boundary, and the constructive completion route records the finite regular-Cauchy and dyadic reads.

The separated equality row $S$ and the uniformly continuous handoff $U$ are downstream of that route. Transport, provenance, and local naming through $H,P,L$ preserve the same dependency order, which proves the stated factorization and excludes the unavailable compactness, subnet, quotient, and ambient-completeness reads.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[TotallyBoundedCompletion ledger non-escape]
\label{thm:totally-bounded-completion-no-escape}
For every accepted $\TotallyBoundedCompletionUp$ packet $K=(X,T,N,F,E,C,S,U,H,P,L)$, every exported completion read remains inside the finite ledger
$$
X,T,N,F,E,C,S,U,H,P,L .
$$
It cannot produce open-cover compactness, a quotient completion, a selected limit point, countable choice, or full $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project an exported completion read from the carrier. By \autoref{def:totally-bounded-completion-carrier}, the read has access only to the eleven displayed coordinates and their componentwise transport rows.

Apply \autoref{thm:totally-bounded-completion-namecert-obligations}. Finite-net persistence keeps the read inside $T,N$; basis and embedding replay moves it through $F,E,C$; separated and extension handoff moves it through $S,U$; and the structural rows $H,P,L$ record only the same route.

Every excluded principle is absent from this route. Open-cover compactness, quotient representatives, selected limit points, countable-choice schedules, and full $\RealUp$ completeness have no carrier coordinate, no replay edge, and no provenance row in the packet, so the exported read cannot escape the displayed finite ledger.
\end{proof}
