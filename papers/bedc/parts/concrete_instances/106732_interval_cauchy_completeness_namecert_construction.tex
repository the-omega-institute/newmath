Following Bishop and Bridges constructive analysis, the $\IntervalCauchyCompletenessUp$ packet records the closed-interval Cauchy completeness route as finite BEDC NameCert data. It reads the endpoint and containment rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closedboundedinterval-namecert}, the regular rational sequence route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the finite stream windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the terminal real seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, and the located interval boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-located-complete-interval-namecert}. The packet names the interval-contained Cauchy readback surface; it does not assert open-cover compactness, selected subsequences, quotient equality, trichotomy, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\section{Interval Cauchy Completeness Carrier}
\label{sec:interval-cauchy-completeness-carrier}

\begin{definition}[Interval Cauchy completeness carrier]
\label{def:interval-cauchy-completeness-carrier}
An $\IntervalCauchyCompletenessUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
I=(B,Q,D,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $B$ is the accepted $\ClosedBoundedIntervalUp$ interval row, exposing lower and upper endpoint reads, endpoint order, rational containment, dyadic containment, and the local closed-interval NameCert surface.
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular-Cauchy row for the inspected rational approximants, including the finite modulus windows used by the interval read.
\item $D$ is the positive $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger used to compare successive approximants, endpoint slack, and interval containment at the displayed scale.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite window row selecting exactly the stream observations consumed by the regular-Cauchy and interval-containment checks.
\item $R$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after $B,Q,D,W$ have been replayed.
\item $E$ records that the sealed read remains inside the same closed interval source row $B$ through the located complete interval boundary.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for endpoint rows, regular-Cauchy windows, dyadic tolerances, StreamName windows, the Real seal, interval-membership evidence, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a finite Cauchy-read request through $W,Q,D,B$ and only then to $R$ and $E$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\IntervalCauchyCompletenessUp$, $\ClosedBoundedIntervalUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\LocatedCompleteIntervalUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\IntervalCauchyCompletenessUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The accepted carrier contains no open-cover compactness coordinate, no selected subsequence, no quotient equality of real names, no trichotomy row, no countable-choice schedule, and no ambient completeness field for $\RealUp$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Interval Cauchy completeness NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:interval-cauchy-completeness-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\IntervalCauchyCompletenessUp$ carrier $I=(B,Q,D,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\IntervalCauchyCompletenessUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission, endpoint-row exactness, regular-Cauchy window consumption, dyadic tolerance exactness, StreamName window confinement, Real seal handoff, interval-membership readback, componentwise transport, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming for the displayed rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:interval-cauchy-completeness-carrier}. The projection exposes the closed interval row $B$, the regular rational row $Q$, the dyadic ledger $D$, the finite stream window row $W$, the Real seal $R$, the interval-membership evidence $E$, and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

Endpoint-row exactness is supplied by $B$. Every endpoint, order, rational containment, and dyadic containment read is already a row of the closed bounded interval packet before the Cauchy consumer can inspect $Q,D,W,R$, or $E$.

Regular-Cauchy and tolerance exactness are supplied by $Q,D,W$. The finite stream window names the observations, the regular rational row names the Cauchy approximants and modulus windows, and the dyadic ledger names the positive tolerances used in the endpoint and successor comparisons.

Finally $R$ and $E$ close the seal route, while $H,C,P,N$ preserve and name only the displayed packet. Since no row stores open-cover compactness, a selected subsequence, quotient equality, trichotomy, countable choice, or ambient Real completeness, none of those objects can enter the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Interval Cauchy completeness regular seal]
\label{thm:interval-cauchy-completeness-regular-seal}
Let $I=(B,Q,D,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\IntervalCauchyCompletenessUp$ packet. Every $\RealUp$-facing read exported by $I$ first consumes the finite route
$$
B,W,Q,D \longmapsto R \longmapsto E,H,C,P,N .
$$
Thus the sealed real output is visible only after the closed-interval endpoint rows, StreamName observation windows, regular-Cauchy readback, and dyadic tolerance ledger have all been displayed.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a Real-facing consumer of $I$. Carrier admission exposes $R$, but only as the seal row downstream of $B,W,Q,D$.

Replay the continuation row $C$. The request first reads the closed interval row $B$, then the StreamName window $W$, the regular rational Cauchy row $Q$, and the dyadic tolerance ledger $D$.

Only after this finite replay can $R$ be read as a $\RealUp$ seal. The interval-membership row $E$ then reads the same sealed output back through the source interval, and the structural rows $H,P,N$ transport, package, and name that route without adding any separate completeness principle.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Interval Cauchy completeness no open-cover route]
\label{thm:interval-cauchy-completeness-no-open-cover}
Every consumer of an $\IntervalCauchyCompletenessUp$ packet receives a finite regular-Cauchy interval route through $B,Q,D,W,R,E,H,C,P,N$. The packet has no row from which an open-cover compactness proof, a selected subsequence, quotient equality for real names, trichotomy, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness can be recovered.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier of \autoref{def:interval-cauchy-completeness-carrier}. Its non-structural rows are exactly the closed interval source, regular rational Cauchy readback, dyadic tolerance ledger, StreamName window, Real seal, and interval-membership readback.

Open-cover compactness and selected subsequences would require separate rows for cover indices, finite subcover choice, or subsequence selection. Quotient equality, trichotomy, countable choice, and ambient Real completeness would likewise require decision, selection, or host-completeness coordinates not present in the packet.

The only exported route is therefore the displayed finite Cauchy interval route. Componentwise transport, continuation replay, provenance, and local NameCert evidence preserve that route; they do not create any of the excluded coordinates.
\end{proof}
