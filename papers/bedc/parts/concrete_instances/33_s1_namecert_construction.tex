\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for \(\SOneUp\)}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-s1-namecert}

The circle interface $\SOneUp$ is a certificate-obligation schema over the product carrier $\RealUp \times \RealUp$ from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. Its carrier is the unit-circle subspace: pairs $(x,y)$ equipped with a certified equation witness that $x^2 + y^2$ is classified as the real unit. The interface is not a primitive topological space; it is admitted only when the product real witnesses, equation witness, componentwise classifier, and equation transport data are supplied.

\begin{theorem}[SOne Real metric bridge boundary]
\label{thm:sone-real-metric-bridge-boundary}
The public $\SOneUp$ bridge boundary over the $\RealUp$ product carrier
consists only of the two $\RealUp$ component rows, the product carrier ledger,
the unit-equation witness, the componentwise classifier, and equation
transport. It does not expose angle parametrisation, winding data, covering
quotients, compactness, or connectedness.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.S1Up.SOneRealMetricBridgeBoundary\_public\_rows}
\begin{proof}
Read the carrier through the child file
\autoref{sec:s1-carrier}. The carrier readbacks expose the two real component
histories, the product-history row, the continuation ledger, and the displayed
unit-equation witness.

Read the certificate through the child file certificate surface. The component
classifier exposes only $\hsame$ on the two real coordinates, while the hidden
equation and point ledgers are determined and transported by the equation
transport and carrier transport rows.

Repack those rows as the public bridge boundary. Every exported field is one
of the RealUp component, product carrier, unit-equation, component classifier,
or equation-transport rows already displayed in this chapter, so no angle,
winding, covering, compactness, or connectedness datum is part of the boundary.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SOne RegSeqRat metric observation boundary]
\label{thm:sone-regseqrat-metric-observation-boundary}
An admitted $\SOneUp$ point over the $\RealUp$ product carrier exposes to any
metric-observation consumer only the two component $\RealUp$ finite-window read
surfaces, the $\RegSeqRat$ stream-name and real-seal rows attached to those
components, the product carrier ledger, the unit-equation witness, the
componentwise $\hsame$ classifier rows, and equation transport. Compactness,
connectedness, angle parametrisation, winding data, quotient-circle
presentation, and metric-completeness rows are not exported by this boundary.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the public $\SOneUp$ bridge boundary in
\autoref{thm:sone-real-metric-bridge-boundary}. It already restricts the circle
read to the two real component rows, the product carrier ledger, the
unit-equation witness, the componentwise classifier, and equation transport.

Now read each real component through
\autoref{thm:realup-scoped-finite-window-read-surface}. The component access
there is a finite-window read surface, so a metric-observation consumer sees
only the selected real rows rather than an ambient metric-complete real line.

For the regular-sequence presentation, use
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-streamname-scoped-interface} for the stream-name rows and
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-finite-window-real-seal-interface} for the finite-window
real-seal rows. These are attached componentwise to the two $\RealUp$ reads and
do not add a new circle-level carrier.

Combining the three component readings with the $\SOneUp$ bridge boundary gives
exactly the listed consumer surface. Since no cited surface exports compactness,
connectedness, angle data, winding data, quotient-circle presentation, or
metric-completeness rows, none of those rows is available through the admitted
$\SOneUp$ observation boundary.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.S1Up.SOneRegSeqRatMetricObservationBoundary\_public\_rows}

\begin{theorem}[SOne public certificate boundary]
\label{thm:sone-public-certificate-boundary}
The public $\NameCert_{\SOneUp}$ boundary is exactly the $\RealUp$ pair
carrier, product ledger, unit-equation witness, componentwise $\hsame$
classifier, equation transport, and finite-window $\RegSeqRat$ read surfaces
isolated by the $\SOneUp$ bridge and metric-observation boundaries. No
compactness, connectedness, angle, quotient, or completeness row is part of the
public certificate boundary.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.S1Up.SOne\_public\_namecert\_obligation\_assembly}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:sone-real-metric-bridge-boundary}. It identifies the public
circle boundary with the two $\RealUp$ component rows, product carrier ledger,
unit-equation witness, componentwise classifier, and equation transport.

Apply \autoref{thm:sone-regseqrat-metric-observation-boundary}. It refines the
component access to the finite-window $\RegSeqRat$ stream-name and real-seal
read surfaces while preserving the same product, equation, classifier, and
transport rows.

The public $\NameCert_{\SOneUp}$ can therefore export only the intersection of
those already isolated rows. Since the first boundary excludes angle data,
winding data, covering quotients, compactness, and connectedness, and the
second also excludes quotient-circle presentation and metric-completeness rows,
the certificate has exactly the displayed RealUp-pair, equation, classifier,
transport, and finite-window read surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SOne componentwise classifier equation transport]
\label{thm:sone-componentwise-classifier-equation-transport}
Let $P$ and $Q$ be accepted public $\SOneUp$ packets over $\mathsf{BHist}$.
Suppose the two $\RealUp$ component rows of $P$ and $Q$ are related by the
componentwise $\hsame$ classifier of
\autoref{def:sone-component-classifier-relation}. Then the unit-equation
witness row, product point ledger row, and both $\RegSeqRat$ finite-window read
surfaces of $P$ transport to the corresponding public rows of $Q$. This
consumer reads no angle, quotient-circle, winding, compactness, or connectedness
datum.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.S1Up.SOneComponentClassifier\_equation\_transport}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:sone-real-metric-bridge-boundary}. It restricts the public
circle packet to the two $\RealUp$ component rows, the product ledger, the
unit-equation witness, the componentwise classifier, and the equation-transport
row.

Use \autoref{def:sone-component-classifier-relation} to compare the two
accepted packets component by component. Hidden-ledger determinacy in that
definition identifies the product-point continuation and the unit-equation
witness once the two displayed $\RealUp$ component rows are related by
$\hsame$.

Apply \autoref{thm:sone-regseqrat-metric-observation-boundary}. The same two
component comparisons refine to the finite-window $\RegSeqRat$ stream-name and
real-seal read surfaces on both coordinates, without adding a circle-level
carrier.

Finally use \autoref{thm:sone-public-certificate-boundary}. The public
certificate contains exactly those RealUp, product, equation, classifier, and
finite-window rows, so the componentwise classifier comparison is sufficient to
transport the whole public finite-window circle boundary while leaving all
topological circle data unread.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SOne standard topological bridge boundary]
\label{thm:sone-standard-topological-bridge-boundary}
Any standard topological-circle bridge for $\SOneUp$ may read only the two
$\RealUp$ component finite-window surfaces, the product ledger, the
unit-equation witness, the componentwise classifier, and equation transport.
Compactness, connectedness, angle parametrisation, winding data, quotient-circle
presentation, and metric-completeness rows remain outside the bridge.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.S1Up.SOneStandardTopologicalBridgeBoundary\_component\_rows}
\begin{proof}
Begin with \autoref{thm:sone-real-metric-bridge-boundary}. It confines a public
circle bridge to the two real component rows, product carrier ledger,
unit-equation witness, componentwise classifier, and equation transport.

Refine the two component reads through
\autoref{thm:sone-regseqrat-metric-observation-boundary}. The real components
may be consumed through their finite-window $\RegSeqRat$ read surfaces, but
that refinement still does not expose compactness, connectedness, angle data,
winding data, quotient presentation, or metric-completeness rows.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:sone-public-certificate-boundary}. It identifies the
public certificate boundary with the same RealUp-pair, product, equation,
classifier, transport, and finite-window read rows. Therefore the standard
topological bridge boundary has exactly those readable rows and no topology
data outside the displayed certificate surface.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.S1Up.SOneStandardTopologicalBridgeBoundary\_public\_rows}

\begin{theorem}[SOne standard topological circle acceptance factorization]
\label{thm:sone-standard-topological-circle-acceptance-factorization}
Every accepted standard topological-circle bridge row for $\SOneUp$ factors
through exactly the $\RealUp$-pair carrier, finite-window $\RegSeqRat$
component reads, product ledger, unit-equation witness, componentwise
classifier, and equation transport isolated by
\autoref{thm:sone-real-metric-bridge-boundary},
\autoref{thm:sone-regseqrat-metric-observation-boundary}, and
\autoref{thm:sone-public-certificate-boundary}. No compactness, connectedness,
angle, winding, quotient-circle, or metric-completeness row can enter the
bridge unless supplied by a later certificate.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.S1Up.SOneStandardTopologicalCircleAcceptance\_factorization\_public\_rows}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted bridge row through
\autoref{thm:sone-standard-topological-bridge-boundary}. The projection leaves
only the two $\RealUp$ component rows, product ledger, unit-equation witness,
componentwise classifier, and equation transport.

Refine those component rows with
\autoref{thm:sone-regseqrat-metric-observation-boundary}. Each real component
is read by a finite-window $\RegSeqRat$ stream-name and real-seal surface, and
the refinement preserves the same product, equation, classifier, and transport
rows.

Finally use \autoref{thm:sone-public-certificate-boundary}. It shows that the
public certificate has no additional readable coordinate beyond the RealUp-pair
carrier, finite-window reads, product ledger, unit-equation witness,
componentwise classifier, and equation transport. Hence topology-specific
compactness, connectedness, angle, winding, quotient-circle, and
metric-completeness rows are absent unless a later certificate supplies them.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SOne standard bridge componentwise transport stability]
\label{thm:sone-standard-bridge-componentwise-transport-stability}
Let $P$ and $Q$ be accepted public $\SOneUp$ packets. Suppose their two
$\RealUp$ component rows are related by
\autoref{def:sone-component-classifier-relation}. If $P$ admits a standard
topological-circle bridge row, then $Q$ admits a standard topological-circle
bridge row with the same readable $\RealUp$ component finite-window surfaces,
product ledger, unit-equation witness, componentwise classifier, and equation
transport. Compactness, connectedness, angle, winding, quotient-circle, and
metric-completeness rows remain unavailable.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.S1Up.SOneStandardBridgeComponentwiseTransport\_stability}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:sone-componentwise-classifier-equation-transport}. The
componentwise classifier relation transports the product ledger,
unit-equation witness, equation-transport row, and the two finite-window
$\RegSeqRat$ read surfaces from $P$ to $Q$.

Next apply \autoref{thm:sone-standard-topological-bridge-boundary}. The
standard bridge row for $P$ has no readable coordinate outside those
component, product, equation, classifier, transport, and finite-window rows, so
the transported data already identify the public bridge boundary available to
$Q$.

Finally use
\autoref{thm:sone-standard-topological-circle-acceptance-factorization}. The
transported boundary repacks as the same public standard topological-circle
bridge surface for $Q$, while compactness, connectedness, angle, winding,
quotient-circle, and metric-completeness data remain absent from the bridge.
\end{proof}
