\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for \(\SOneUp\)}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-s1-namecert}

The circle interface \(\SOneUp\) is licensed by Bishop reals from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert} together with a setoid classifier identifying real values that differ by an integer multiple of \(2\pi\). The classifier is encoded as an inductive predicate \(\sim_{2\pi}(x, y)\) generated by reflexivity and shift-by-\(2\pi\) constructors, not as a quotient-type, so that \(\mathsf{Quot.sound}\) is never invoked. It is not a primitive topological space; it is licensed only as a setoid over the real source.

\section{Carrier definition}

\begin{definition}[SOne carrier]
\label{def:s1-carrier}
Use Bishop real witnesses with an inductive setoid predicate generated by equality and shifts by integer multiples of \(2\pi\).
\end{definition}

\section{The certificate}

\begin{definition}[SOne source specification]
\label{def:s1-source-specification}
The source is \(\RealUp\) plus the integer shift source from \(\IntUp\).
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[SOne pattern specification]
\label{def:s1-pattern-specification}
The pattern is real-angle formation with certified \(2\pi\)-shift steps.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[SOne classifier specification]
\label{def:s1-classifier-specification}
The classifier is the inductive relation \(x\sim_{2\pi}y\), not a quotient representative.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[SOne stability certificate]
\label{def:s1-stability-certificate}
The stability certificate consists of:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item reflexivity of the shift relation
  \item symmetry by inverse integer shift
  \item transitivity by adding shift witnesses
  \item compatibility with real sameness
  \item closure under adding \(2\pi k\)
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[SOne ledger policy]
\label{def:s1-ledger-policy}
Record the real source and the integer shift witness used in classifier derivations.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Concrete \(\SOneUp\) naming certificate]
\label{thm:concrete-s1-namecert}
The real-setoid tuple forms a valid NameCert for \(\SOneUp\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{corollary}[\(\SOneUp\) is licensed, not primitive]
\label{cor:s1-licensed-not-primitive}
\(\SOneUp\) is licensed as a setoid over \(\RealUp\), not as a quotient circle.
\end{corollary}

\begin{remark}[Zero-axiom encoding note]
\label{rem:s1-zero-axiom-note}
Replaces quotient construction and \(\mathsf{Quot.sound}\) with an inductive setoid classifier.
\end{remark}
