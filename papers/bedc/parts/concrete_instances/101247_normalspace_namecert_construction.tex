\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\NormalSpaceUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-normalspace-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Kelley and Munkres, with Urysohn and Bourbaki as the separation-theory background, normal spaces are read here as the finite BEDC surface that separates two displayed closed-set rows by disjoint open-neighborhood rows. The $\NormalSpaceUp$ packet sits after the open-set discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-topology-namecert}, beside the point-separation surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-hausdorffspace-namecert}, before the separation-function route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-urysohn-lemma-namecert}, and also beside the positive-open route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cozero-set-namecert}, the observable-open classifier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-sierpinski-space-namecert}, and the Cauchy-facing topology interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyspace-namecert}. It names only the local separation route; it does not import a selected complement, excluded middle, open-cover compactness, choice-selected functions, host powersets, or a function extension theorem.

\begin{definition}[Normal space carrier]
\label{def:normal-space-carrier}
A $\NormalSpaceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
N=(T,F_0,F_1,D,U_0,U_1,H,K,P,L)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $T$ is the $\TopologyUp$ source row, exposing the ambient open-row discipline and the finite observation surface used by the separation read.
\item $S$ is the $\HausdorffSpaceUp$ compatibility row when a point-separation consumer supplies separated names before closed-pair separation is requested; it is read only as displayed neighbourhood data and is not part of the closed-pair classifier.
\item $F_0$ and $F_1$ are the two closed-set witness rows, displayed as ledger entries over the same topological source rather than as host complements.
\item $D$ is the disjointness ledger, recording that the two closed-set witnesses have no accepted common membership cell inside the displayed packet.
\item $U_0$ and $U_1$ are the separating open-neighborhood rows, with $F_0$ contained in $U_0$, $F_1$ contained in $U_1$, and no accepted overlap between $U_0$ and $U_1$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the topology source, closed-set witnesses, disjointness ledger, open-neighborhood rows, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $K$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a separation request through $T,F_0,F_1,D,U_0,U_1,H,P,L$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\NormalSpaceUp$, $\TopologyUp$, $\HausdorffSpaceUp$, $\UrysohnLemmaUp$, $\CozeroSetUp$, $\SierpinskiSpaceUp$, $\CauchySpaceUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $L$ is the local $\NameCert_{\NormalSpaceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $T,F_0,F_1,D,U_0,U_1,H,K,P,L$, with $S$ available only as a compatibility handoff from the sibling Hausdorff surface. It has no coordinate for selected complements, host closed-set predicates, host powersets, excluded middle, Urysohn functions, choice-selected functions, Tietze extension data, paracompact refinements, classical separation principles not displayed by the rows, or open-cover compactness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Normal space NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:normal-space-namecert-obligations}
\label{thm:normal-space-namecert-obligation-surface}
For every accepted $\NormalSpaceUp$ carrier
$$
N=(T,F_0,F_1,D,U_0,U_1,H,K,P,L),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\NormalSpaceUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the topology source, the two closed-set witnesses, the disjointness ledger, the separating open-neighborhood pair, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming;
\item Hausdorff compatibility, requiring any point-separation input to arrive through the displayed $\HausdorffSpaceUp$ neighbourhood row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-hausdorffspace-namecert} before it can be compared with the closed-pair request;
\item closed-pair exposure, requiring every separation request to display $F_0,F_1$ over the same topology source before any open-neighborhood row can be consumed;
\item open-separation exactness, requiring $U_0,U_1$ to contain the displayed closed witnesses and to carry the packet's own disjointness read for the two open neighborhoods;
\item $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ transport, requiring componentwise transport and replay to preserve the topology source, the closed pair, the disjointness ledger, and the two separating open rows;
\item Urysohn handoff discipline, requiring any later $\UrysohnLemmaUp$ consumer of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-urysohn-lemma-namecert} to receive only the displayed closed pair, disjointness ledger, and separating open-neighborhood rows;
\item no excluded-middle or classical-complement escape, requiring $H,K,P,L$ to export no selected complement, host closed-set predicate, host powerset, excluded middle, choice-selected function, Urysohn function, Tietze extension datum, paracompact refinement, classical separation principle not displayed by the rows, or open-cover compactness principle.
\end{enumerate}
No local normal-space obligation is stored outside these displayed rows.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.NormalSpaceUp.NormalSpacePacket\_semantic\_name\_certificate}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:normal-space-carrier}. The accepted packet displays exactly the topology source, the closed-pair witnesses, the disjointness ledger, the open-neighborhood pair, and the structural transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows.

Closed-pair exposure follows from the order of the carrier. A separation request first reads $T$ and then the two closed-set witnesses $F_0,F_1$ over that same source; no open-neighborhood row is available before the closed pair has been displayed.

Open-separation exactness is the projection to $D,U_0,U_1$. The ledger $D$ records disjointness for the displayed closed pair, while $U_0$ and $U_1$ record the two open neighborhoods, the two containment reads, and the open-neighborhood disjointness read internal to the packet.

Transport and replay are exhausted by $H$ and $K$. These rows move the topology source, closed-pair witnesses, disjointness ledger, and open-neighborhood pair componentwise and replay the same finite route.

The Hausdorff compatibility row is only a sibling handoff. It can supply displayed separated-neighbourhood evidence from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-hausdorffspace-namecert}, but it cannot replace the closed-pair route or add host point equality.

The Urysohn handoff is also downstream only. A later consumer from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-urysohn-lemma-namecert} receives $T,F_0,F_1,D,U_0,U_1,H,K,P,L$ as displayed rows, not a selected separating function.

Finally, non-escape follows from the absence of any further classifier coordinate. The provenance and local NameCert rows name only this finite separation route, so no row stores a selected complement, excluded middle, host closed-set predicate, host powerset, choice-selected function, Urysohn function, Tietze extension datum, paracompact refinement, classical separation principle not displayed by the rows, or open-cover compactness principle.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Normal space open-separation route]
\label{thm:normal-space-open-separation-route}
Every separation read exported by an accepted $\NormalSpaceUp$ packet factors through the displayed closed-pair row and the two open-neighborhood rows before the local naming row can be inspected.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.NormalSpaceUp.NormalSpacePacket\_open\_separation\_route}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a separation request for the displayed normal-space surface. Carrier admission exposes the topology source $T$, but it exports no local normal-space name before the closed-set witnesses are read.

The request then reads the displayed closed pair $F_0,F_1$ and the disjointness ledger $D$. This fixes the pair to be separated and prevents substitution by an external complement or host predicate.

Only after $F_0,F_1,D$ have been displayed may the request inspect the open-neighborhood rows $U_0,U_1$. These rows supply the two containment reads and the disjointness read for the open neighborhoods.

The replay and transport rows then carry this same factorization to the local naming row $L$. Thus every exported separation read passes through the displayed closed pair and the two separating open-neighborhood rows before local naming.
\end{proof}

Apply the open-separation route \autoref{thm:normal-space-open-separation-route}. It fixes the order in which a separation read reaches local naming: first the topology source and closed pair, then the disjointness ledger, then the two open-neighborhood rows.

The transport row $H$ moves those displayed coordinates componentwise, while the replay row $K$ replays the same finite route after transport.
