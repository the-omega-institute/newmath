\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ProperMetricSpaceUp$}

Following Bourbaki and Kelley, the $\ProperMetricSpaceUp$ packet records the constructive metric-space interface in which displayed closed metric balls carry compactness-style readback for completion consumers. It sits after the metric carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the open-ball boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metricball-namecert}, the closed-ball containment surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closed-metric-ball-namecert}, the compact-metric certificate of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, the bounded-family completion route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-bornology-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet is a finite closed-ball compactness ledger, not an open-cover compactness theorem, a selected finite subcover, quotient point equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[Proper metric space carrier]
\label{def:proper-metric-space-carrier}
A $\ProperMetricSpaceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
P_{\mathrm{pms}}=(X,B,R,K,O,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the $\MetricUp$ row carrying the point classifier, distance witnesses, and metric transport surface.
\item $B$ is the displayed family of $\ClosedMetricBallUp$ rows indexed by the accepted center-radius windows of $X$.
\item $R$ is the radius ledger, recording nonnegative radius rows, strict-boundary handoffs to $\MetricBallUp$, and $\RealUp$ comparison readback.
\item $K$ assigns compactness-style $\CompactMetricUp$ readback to each displayed closed ball in $B$.
\item $O$ records compatibility between the closed-ball family and the $\CauchyBornologyUp$ bounded-family route consumed by completion-facing readers.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the metric row, closed-ball family, radius ledger, compactness readback, bornology compatibility, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a proper-metric request through $X,B,R,K,O,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ProperMetricSpaceUp$, $\MetricUp$, $\MetricBallUp$, $\ClosedMetricBallUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\CauchyBornologyUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ProperMetricSpaceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier compares accepted packets only through these rows and exposes no host subset of points, open-cover compactness object, selected compactness witness outside the displayed closed ball, quotient metric equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness principle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Proper metric space NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:proper-metric-space-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\ProperMetricSpaceUp$ carrier
$$
P_{\mathrm{pms}}=(X,B,R,K,O,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\ProperMetricSpaceUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the metric row $X$, closed-ball family row $B$, radius ledger $R$, compactness readback $K$, bornology compatibility row $O$, componentwise transport $H$, displayed replay $C$, package provenance $P$, and local naming $N$; center-radius compatibility between $X,B,R$; compact readback exactness for each displayed ball through $K$; completion-facing bounded-family compatibility through $O$; and non-escape for host subsets, open-cover compactness, selected subcovers, quotient point equality, and ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:proper-metric-space-carrier}. The metric row, closed-ball family, radius ledger, compact readback, bornology compatibility, and structural rows are all displayed before a consumer reads the local naming row.

Center-radius compatibility is forced by the order of the rows. The row $X$ supplies the metric source, $B$ supplies only closed balls over that source, and $R$ supplies the radius comparisons and strict-boundary handoffs used by $\MetricBallUp$ consumers.

Compactness readback is attached ball by ball. The row $K$ is indexed by the displayed closed-ball family $B$, so a consumer cannot replace it by an open-cover compactness proof or by a compactness witness for an undisplayed ball.

Finally $O,H,C,P,N$ preserve the same route for completion-facing and bounded-family consumers. Since the carrier has no coordinate for the excluded host principles, the listed rows exhaust the NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Proper metric space closed-ball compactness readback]
\label{thm:proper-metric-space-closed-ball-compactness-readback}
Every compactness-style read of a displayed closed ball in an accepted $\ProperMetricSpaceUp$ packet factors through
$$
\MetricUp,\quad \ClosedMetricBallUp,\quad \CompactMetricUp,\quad \RealUp,\quad \BHist,\quad \hsame,\quad \Cont,\quad \Pkg,\quad \NameCert .
$$
The $\ClosedMetricBallUp$ row supplies the center-radius containment, the radius ledger supplies the ordered-real comparison, and the $\CompactMetricUp$ row supplies the compactness readback attached to that same displayed ball. The readback exports no open-cover compactness theorem, selected finite subcover, quotient point equality, host closed subset, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the closed-ball family row $B$ of \autoref{def:proper-metric-space-carrier}. Each accepted ball read is indexed by a center-radius window over the metric row $X$ and by a radius comparison in $R$.

Read the compactness row $K$ only after that ball is fixed. Its index is the displayed closed-ball row, so compactness-style data are attached to the same metric source, center, radius, and containment comparison.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name this finite route. No row names an open cover, a chosen subcover, a quotient point equality, or an ambient completeness theorem.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Proper metric space ball-bornology boundary]
\label{thm:proper-metric-space-ball-bornology-boundary}
Every completion-facing consumer of an accepted $\ProperMetricSpaceUp$ packet reads bounded-family data through the displayed boundary
$$
\MetricBallUp,\quad \ClosedMetricBallUp,\quad \CauchyBornologyUp,\quad \CompactMetricUp,\quad \RealUp .
$$
Open-ball handoffs use the strict-radius side of $R$, closed-ball handoffs use $B$, bounded-family requests use $O$, and compactness-style requests use $K$. Thus the consumer receives a finite ball and bornology boundary rather than a host bounded set, selected bound, open-cover compactness proof, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the radius ledger $R$. Its strict-radius entries are the only route to $\MetricBallUp$ consumers, while its non-strict entries are the radius comparisons used by the $\ClosedMetricBallUp$ family $B$.

The bornology compatibility row $O$ is downstream of those ball rows. A completion-facing consumer therefore reads bounded-family data only after the metric row, radius ledger, and ball family have been displayed.

Compactness-style information remains attached through $K$. The same transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows preserve the boundary, so the route cannot turn into a host bounded set, selected bound, open-cover compactness proof, or ambient Real-completeness datum.
\end{proof}
