\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RelationalObjectivityUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-relational-objectivity-namecert}
\origin{ai}

The $\RelationalObjectivityUp$ packet realizes the objectivity-without-privileged-observer surface of \autoref{sec:objectivity-without-privileged-observer} as a finite NameCert carrier. It consumes the finite inter-Hist family of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-multihistconfig-namecert}, the observer-history identity surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observerhistidentity-namecert}, the public other-minds commitment of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-othermindscommitment-namecert}, the locality-cell evidence of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-localitycell-namecert}, and the public alignment packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-crossperspectivealignment-namecert}. The packet is not a view from nowhere; it is the displayed finite route through which observer-family rows, invariant evidence, anchor-stability rows, ledgers, transports, package provenance, and local naming are read together.

\begin{definition}[RelationalObjectivity invariant-packet carrier]
\label{def:relational-objectivity-invariant-packet-carrier}
A $\RelationalObjectivityUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
R=(F,I,A,L,T,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $F$ is the finite observer-family row, read through the $\MultiHistConfigUp$ packet and the relevant $\ObserverHistIdentityUp$ rows;
\item $I$ is the invariant-packet row, recording the inter-observer relation, the classifier under which the relation is compared, and the claim read from that invariant;
\item $A$ is the anchor-stability row, recording that changing the source observer anchor preserves the invariant claim by the displayed classifier;
\item $L$ is the ledger row, recording the observation, continuation, locality-cell, other-minds commitment, and cross-perspective alignment evidence used by the invariant read;
\item $T$ is the componentwise transport row, storing the $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ routes that replay the invariant across observer-family coordinates;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over the vision objectivity surface and the five concrete sibling packets cited above;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RelationalObjectivityUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a privileged observer, global synchronization frame, hidden observer substance, unledgered invariant, or anchor-free claim.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[RelationalObjectivity observer-family source]
\label{def:relational-objectivity-observer-family-source}
An observer-family source for $\RelationalObjectivityUp$ is the row $F$ of an accepted carrier $R=(F,I,A,L,T,P,N)$ together with its displayed routes to $\MultiHistConfigUp$ and $\ObserverHistIdentityUp$. A read of $F$ gives only the finite family of observer histories and their public identity rows; it does not supply a distinguished evaluator of the family.
\end{definition}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.RelationalObjectivityUp.RelationalObjectivityObserverFamilySource}

\begin{definition}[RelationalObjectivity NameCert classifier]
\label{def:relational-objectivity-namecert-classifier}
The local $\NameCert_{\RelationalObjectivityUp}$ classifier compares two accepted carriers only through the displayed coordinates $F,I,A,L,T,P,N$. Two reads agree when their observer-family rows are matched by the same public identity and multi-Hist routes, their invariant-packet rows determine the same claim under the displayed classifier, their anchor-stability and ledger rows are transported by $T$, and their provenance and local naming rows agree.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[RelationalObjectivity anchor-stability obligation]
\label{thm:relational-objectivity-anchor-stability-obligation}
Every accepted $\RelationalObjectivityUp$ read of a claim factors through the anchor-stability row $A$ of an accepted carrier $R=(F,I,A,L,T,P,N)$. A consumer may treat the claim as relationally objective only after the invariant-packet row $I$ and the anchor-stability row $A$ are displayed in the same finite packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier through \autoref{def:relational-objectivity-invariant-packet-carrier}. The invariant-packet coordinate $I$ names the relation, classifier, and claim, while $A$ records stability under change of observer anchor.

The classifier of \autoref{def:relational-objectivity-namecert-classifier} compares carriers only through the displayed rows. Hence a read that lacks $A$ has no coordinate from which anchor change can be checked.

The coordinate list also excludes an anchor-free claim and a privileged observer. Therefore the objective read is available only through the finite pair $I,A$ transported by $T$ and named by $N$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.RelationalObjectivityUp.RelationalObjectivityAnchorStabilityObligation}

\begin{theorem}[RelationalObjectivity invariant-ledger exactness]
\label{thm:relational-objectivity-invariant-ledger-exactness}
For every accepted $\RelationalObjectivityUp$ carrier $R=(F,I,A,L,T,P,N)$, the ledger row $L$ is exactly the displayed evidence surface for the invariant read: observer-history identity, multi-Hist configuration, locality-cell evidence, other-minds commitment, cross-perspective alignment, continuation replay, and componentwise transport. No invariant claim may be accepted through an unledgered observation or hidden synchronization coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read $L$ from the carrier definition. Its declared evidence is the observation, continuation, locality-cell, other-minds commitment, and cross-perspective alignment data used by the invariant read, with the observer family supplied by $F$ and the replay route supplied by $T$.

The sibling packets cited in the chapter opening supply the public rows consumed by this ledger. They provide finite multi-Hist, observer-identity, other-minds, locality-cell, and cross-perspective surfaces, not ambient observer access.

Since the carrier explicitly has no coordinate for hidden observer substance, unledgered invariant evidence, global synchronization, or an anchor-free claim, ledger exactness is exhausted by the displayed rows $F,I,A,L,T,P,N$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.RelationalObjectivityUp.RelationalObjectivityInvariantLedgerExactness}

\begin{theorem}[RelationalObjectivity no-privileged-anchor certificate]
\label{thm:relational-objectivity-no-privileged-anchor-certificate}
Every accepted $\RelationalObjectivityUp$ packet certifies relational objectivity without selecting a privileged observer anchor. Changing the source observer route may transport the invariant through $T$ and check stability through $A$, but it cannot create a global observer, a synchronized external frame, or a host-level equality between observer histories.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the observer-family source of \autoref{def:relational-objectivity-observer-family-source}. The row $F$ exposes only a finite family and its public identity routes, with no distinguished evaluator.

The anchor-stability obligation of \autoref{thm:relational-objectivity-anchor-stability-obligation} explains how a claim is read after anchor change: the invariant row $I$ and stability row $A$ must remain visible in the same finite packet and are transported by $T$.

Finally inspect the carrier exclusions in \autoref{def:relational-objectivity-invariant-packet-carrier}. The packet has no coordinate for a privileged observer, global synchronization frame, hidden observer substance, or anchor-free claim. Thus anchor transport remains an internal certificate route rather than a promotion to an external standpoint.
\end{proof}
