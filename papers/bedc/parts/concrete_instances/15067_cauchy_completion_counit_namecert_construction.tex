\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CauchyCompletionCounitUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-completion-counit-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges constructive completion and Kelley completion terminology, the $\CauchyCompletionCounitUp$ packet records the finite BEDC certificate for retracting a completion-facing name back through the terminal real seal whenever the name is already displayed by the Real/RegSeqRat route. It is the counit side of the boundary opened by the unit packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchycompletionunit-namecert} and the reflection packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completionreflection-namecert}. The packet consumes the terminal $\RealUp$ seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the finite-window naming surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, and the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}; it does not form quotient streams, compare host real equality, choose representatives, assume countable choice, or appeal to ambient completeness.

\begin{definition}[CauchyCompletionCounit carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-completion-counit-carrier}
A $\CauchyCompletionCounitUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(M,E,U,R,S,D,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the completion-facing source row whose accepted reads must be returned to the displayed real-seal boundary.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal through which the returned read is consumed.
\item $U$ is the $\CauchyCompletionUnitUp$ route that records how source-side data entered the completion-facing surface.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row supplying regular rational observations before the seal is inspected.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row through which the regular readback is named.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger that accounts for every precision request used by $S$ and $R$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $M,E,U,R,S,D$ and the structural rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from completion-facing reads through the unit route, window row, dyadic ledger, regular readback, and real seal.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyCompletionCounitUp$, $\CauchyCompletionUnitUp$, $\CompletionReflectionUp$, $\RealUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyCompletionCounitUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for quotient completion equality, host real equality, selected representatives, countable-choice schedules, open-cover compactness, ambient completeness, or completeness of $\RealUp$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyCompletionCounit NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-counit-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\CauchyCompletionCounitUp$ carrier $Q=(M,E,U,R,S,D,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyCompletionCounitUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the completion-facing source row, terminal Real seal, unit route, regular readback, stream window, dyadic ledger, componentwise transport, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item counit exposure, requiring every completion-facing read in $M$ to return through $U$ before the seal $E$ can be consumed;
\item readback exactness, requiring every returned read to pass through $S$, $D$, and $R$ before reaching $E$;
\item classifier stability, requiring transported packets with the same $M,E,U,R,S,D$ route to be identified by the carried $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ data;
\item ledger non-escape, stating that no quotient completion equality, host real equality, selected representative, countable-choice schedule, open-cover compactness, ambient-completeness theorem, or $\RealUp$ completeness theorem is exported by the packet.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:cauchy-completion-counit-carrier}. The tuple lists exactly the completion-facing source, terminal Real seal, unit route, regular readback, stream window, dyadic ledger, transport, replay, provenance, and local name.

Counit exposure reads the completion-facing source row $M$ together with the unit route $U$. A consumer cannot inspect $E$ directly from $M$; the displayed replay row $C$ first returns the read through the route that admitted the source-side data.

Readback exactness is then forced by the order of the packet. The finite-window row $S$ names the request, the dyadic ledger $D$ accounts for its tolerance, and the regular readback row $R$ supplies the rational observation before the terminal seal $E$ is consumed.

Classifier stability is componentwise transport along $H$ followed by replay through $C$. Since the carrier fields are exhausted by $M,E,U,R,S,D,H,C,P,N$, the excluded quotient, host-equality, representative-choice, compactness, ambient-completeness, and real-completeness principles have no row from which a consumer could read them.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyCompletionCounit real-seal retraction]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-counit-real-seal-retraction}
Let $Q=(M,E,U,R,S,D,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyCompletionCounitUp$ carrier. Every $\RealUp$ consumer read exported by $Q$ factors through the displayed retraction route
$$
M\longmapsto U\longmapsto S\longmapsto D\longmapsto R\longmapsto E\longmapsto(H,C,P,N).
$$
Thus the counit returns completion-facing source data to the terminal Real seal only as finite BEDC route data, not as a quotient completion point, not as host real equality, not as a selected representative, and not as countable choice or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier and begin with the completion-facing source row $M$. The packet exposes $M$ only with the unit route $U$, so any retraction read starts by returning through the displayed route that admitted the source row.

The finite observation part is then fixed. The stream window $S$ supplies the named finite request, the dyadic ledger $D$ records its precision, and the regular rational readback $R$ supplies the observation consumed by the real seal.

The terminal row $E$ is reached only after that finite route has been replayed. Transport by $H$ and continuation by $C$ preserve the same route for equivalent packets, while $P$ and $N$ record its provenance and local certificate.

No further row is available in the carrier. Therefore the exported real-seal read is exactly the displayed retraction route, with no quotient point, host equality, selected representative, choice schedule, or ambient completeness witness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyCompletionCounit unit-counit triangle]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-counit-unit-counit-triangle}
For every accepted $\CauchyCompletionCounitUp$ carrier $Q=(M,E,U,R,S,D,H,C,P,N)$ whose completion-facing source row was admitted by the $\CauchyCompletionUnitUp$ route $U$, the composite
$$
M\longmapsto U\longmapsto S,D,R\longmapsto E
$$
has the same displayed $\RealUp$ seal read as the direct regular route
$$
S,D,R\longmapsto E .
$$
The triangle is witnessed by the shared finite rows $S,D,R,E,H,C,P,N$ and does not assert equality of quotient completions, host real equality, a chosen representative, countable choice, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the unit side first. The row $U$ records the admission of source-side data into the completion-facing row $M$, and the counit packet requires any return from $M$ to pass back through the same displayed route before $E$ is consumed.

Read the direct regular side next. The rows $S$, $D$, and $R$ already name the finite window, tolerance ledger, and regular rational readback that feed the terminal Real seal $E$.

Both sides therefore present the same seal-facing route once the completion-facing row has been returned through $U$. Componentwise transport in $H$ and replay in $C$ identify the shared rows, while $P$ and $N$ record that the comparison is internal to the same local certificate.

The carrier contains no quotient-completion equality, host real equality, selected representative, choice schedule, or ambient completeness coordinate. The triangle is therefore a finite-route triangle over $S,D,R,E$, not an external equality principle.
\end{proof}
