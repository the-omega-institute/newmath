\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\AscoliModulusUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-ascolimodulus-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\AscoliModulusUp$ interface names the finite equicontinuity-modulus packet consumed by $\ArzelaAscoliUp$. It is the constructive Bishop--Bridges finite-ledger object that binds a family of $\ContinuousMapUp$ rows over a $\CompactMetricUp$ source to one shared source-radius row for a requested $\RatUp$ target tolerance, with finite-net stability and displayed handoff rows for compact-continuity consumers. The packet is a BEDC carrier over $\CompactMetricUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\UniformContinuityUp$, $\EquicontinuityFamilyUp$, $\ArzelaAscoliUp$, $\RatUp$, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ rows; it is not an open-cover compactness theorem, a subsequence selector, or a host function-space compactness principle.

\begin{definition}[AscoliModulus carrier]
\label{def:ascoli-modulus-carrier}
An $\AscoliModulusUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
A=(X,Y,F,T,R,\mathcal{P},S,E,W,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the source $\CompactMetricUp$ row, including its displayed metric row and the finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ net rows used by downstream compact-continuity reads;
\item $Y$ is the target metric row read by the family of maps, with target-distance comparisons routed through displayed metric witnesses;
\item $F$ is a finite family ledger of $\ContinuousMapUp$ rows from $X$ to $Y$;
\item $T$ is the requested positive $\RatUp$ target-tolerance row;
\item $R$ is the shared positive $\RatUp$ source-radius row selected for the whole finite family ledger;
\item $\mathcal{P}$ is the finite source-net $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ used to test the shared radius against compact source reads;
\item $S$ records finite-net stability data, saying that source histories lying in one bundled radius window remain inside the modulus window demanded by every family row in $F$;
\item $E$ records the $\EquicontinuityFamilyUp$ row whose finite tolerance ledger supplies the uniform family-side reading of $T$ and $R$;
\item $W$ records the $\UniformContinuityUp$ pointwise rows used at bundled source centers and the $\ArzelaAscoliUp$ consumer handoff row;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for source metric, target metric, family rows, tolerance rows, radius rows, probe bundles, finite-net stability rows, equicontinuity rows, uniform-continuity rows, and handoff rows;
\item $C$ records the visible $\Cont$ routes by which compact-metric, continuous-map, uniform-continuity, equicontinuity-family, Arzela--Ascoli, rational, and probe-bundle consumers read the packet;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CompactMetricUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\UniformContinuityUp$, $\EquicontinuityFamilyUp$, $\ArzelaAscoliUp$, $\RatUp$, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, $\hsame$, and $\Cont$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\AscoliModulusUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exports only finite family, tolerance, radius, probe-bundle, stability, equicontinuity, uniform-continuity, transport, continuation, package, and naming rows. It contains no open-cover compactness row, no subsequence-choice row, no ambient function-space topology, and no host compactness theorem.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[AscoliModulus equicontinuity ledger]
\label{thm:ascoli-modulus-equicontinuity-ledger}
For an accepted $\AscoliModulusUp$ carrier of \autoref{def:ascoli-modulus-carrier}, the rows $F,T,R,E,H,C,P,N$ determine one finite equicontinuity ledger: the requested tolerance row $T$ is read through $\RatUp$, the shared source-radius row $R$ is read through the same rational classifier, every displayed $\ContinuousMapUp$ row in $F$ consumes that same $R$, and the $\EquicontinuityFamilyUp$ coordinate $E$ records this family-wide reading without introducing a separate modulus function.

The ledger is stable under the stored $\hsame$ transports and visible $\Cont$ routes. Any consumer reading it receives a finite tolerance-to-radius row over the displayed family ledger, not an external function assigning radii to all host tolerances.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AscoliModulusUp.AscoliModulusPacket\_equicontinuity\_ledger}
\begin{proof}
Project the tolerance row $T$, source-radius row $R$, and finite family ledger $F$ from the carrier. The rational rows are visible $\RatUp$ histories, and every family entry is a displayed $\ContinuousMapUp$ row over the same source and target coordinates.

Read the $\EquicontinuityFamilyUp$ coordinate $E$. Its finite family-side ledger binds the tolerance history to one radius history uniformly over the listed family rows, so no family entry may request an unrecorded source radius.

Finally route the ledger through $H$, $C$, $P$, and $N$. Componentwise transport preserves the same tolerance row, radius row, family ledger, and local naming boundary, so a downstream consumer can only recover the finite ledger displayed in the carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[AscoliModulus finite-net stability]
\label{thm:ascoli-modulus-finite-net-stability}
Let $A$ be an accepted $\AscoliModulusUp$ carrier with finite source-net coordinate $\mathcal{P}$ and stability coordinate $S$. For every bundled source center in $\mathcal{P}$ and every family row in $F$, the stability row $S$ routes a source history inside the displayed radius window $R$ through the corresponding $\ContinuousMapUp$ and $\UniformContinuityUp$ rows, producing a target-distance read bounded by the requested tolerance row $T$.

The stability claim is finite in both indices: it ranges only over the bundled probe centers and the displayed family ledger. It does not assert a global open cover, a host-space uniform structure, or compactness of any function space.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the compact source coordinate $X$ and finite bundle $\mathcal{P}$. The bundle exposes only finitely many center rows, and each center read is routed through the source metric data already stored in $X$.

For a fixed bundled center, inspect the stability coordinate $S$. It points to the radius row $R$, the requested tolerance row $T$, and the listed family rows in $F$, so each family read is checked against the same source-radius window.

Use the pointwise rows collected in $W$ to pass from the source metric comparison to the target metric comparison. Since $S$ is indexed only by the finite bundle and the finite family ledger, the resulting stability certificate cannot export an infinite cover or a host function-space compactness assertion.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[AscoliModulus NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:ascoli-modulus-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\AscoliModulusUp}$ surface consists exactly of five finite rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier habitation by the finite packet of \autoref{def:ascoli-modulus-carrier};
\item rational tolerance and radius classifier transport for $T$ and $R$ through $\RatUp$;
\item family-wide equicontinuity ledger transport for $F$ and $E$ through $\ContinuousMapUp$ and $\EquicontinuityFamilyUp$;
\item finite-net stability over $\mathcal{P}$ and $S$ through $\CompactMetricUp$, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, and $\UniformContinuityUp$;
\item Arzela--Ascoli handoff through $W,H,C,P,N$, forcing every downstream read to remain inside displayed $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local naming rows.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier habitation is immediate from \autoref{def:ascoli-modulus-carrier}. The source, target, family, tolerance, radius, probe-bundle, stability, equicontinuity, uniform-continuity, handoff, transport, continuation, package, and naming rows are all visible coordinates of one finite packet.

The rational transport row handles the tolerance and radius histories. The family transport row handles the continuous-map ledger and the equicontinuity-family coordinate, preserving the fact that the same radius row is shared by every displayed family entry.

The finite-net stability row is \autoref{thm:ascoli-modulus-finite-net-stability}. Repacking it with the handoff coordinate $W$ and the provenance rows $H,C,P,N$ gives the whole local certificate surface, with no additional carrier coordinate through which open-cover compactness, subsequence choice, or host function-space compactness could enter.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[AscoliModulus Arzela-Ascoli handoff]
\label{thm:ascoli-modulus-arzela-ascoli-handoff}
Every $\ArzelaAscoliUp$ consumer reading an accepted $\AscoliModulusUp$ carrier obtains a finite equicontinuity-modulus row: the finite family ledger $F$, requested tolerance row $T$, shared source-radius row $R$, source-net bundle $\mathcal{P}$, stability row $S$, and equicontinuity row $E$ are repacked through $W,H,C,P,N$ as the equicontinuity modulus data named by the $\ArzelaAscoliUp$ carrier.

The handoff supplies only the finite modulus rows needed by a constructive Arzela--Ascoli packet. It does not supply open-cover compactness, subsequence choice, compactness of a host function space, or a topology on all maps from the source to the target.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the handoff coordinate $W$. It records exactly the $\UniformContinuityUp$ pointwise reads and the $\ArzelaAscoliUp$ consumer boundary that may read the $\AscoliModulusUp$ packet.

Attach the finite equicontinuity ledger of \autoref{thm:ascoli-modulus-equicontinuity-ledger} and the finite-net stability row of \autoref{thm:ascoli-modulus-finite-net-stability}. Together these rows provide the family ledger, requested tolerance, shared radius, bundled source centers, and target-distance stability data expected by the Arzela--Ascoli equicontinuity coordinate.

Finally route the repacked rows through $H$, $C$, $P$, and $N$. Since every exported item is already a visible carrier coordinate, the handoff gives $\ArzelaAscoliUp$ only a finite BEDC-kernel modulus packet and no ambient compactness or choice principle.
\end{proof}
