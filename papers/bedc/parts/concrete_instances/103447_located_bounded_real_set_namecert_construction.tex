\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\LocatedBoundedRealSetUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-located-bounded-real-set-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges constructive analysis, the $\LocatedBoundedRealSetUp$ packet records the finite located-membership, boundedness, and approximation rows that a supremum-facing consumer may inspect before any least-upper-bound principle is invoked. It sits over the closed interval boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedinterval-namecert}, the dyadic rational core of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the finite stream-window discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet supplies the finite source rows consumed by the located supremum route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedsupremum-namecert} and the dyadic supremum boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadic-supremum-namecert}; it does not assert that every bounded located subset has a supremum.

\begin{definition}[Located bounded real set carrier]
\label{def:located-bounded-real-set-carrier}
A $\LocatedBoundedRealSetUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
B=(M,I,D,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the located-membership window row. It records the finite membership and apartness requests that have been opened for the displayed real-name family.
\item $I$ is the closed interval bound row, carried through $\LocatedIntervalUp$. It supplies the lower and upper endpoint windows that bound every accepted member row.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ approximation ledger. It records the lower and upper dyadic approximants used by membership, interval containment, and supremum-facing reads.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row naming the inspected member windows, interval endpoint windows, and dyadic comparison windows.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row for the displayed member observations and interval endpoint observations.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after the located-membership, interval-bound, dyadic, stream-window, and regular-readback rows have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the membership window, interval bound, dyadic approximation ledger, stream windows, regular readbacks, real seals, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from each set-facing or supremum-facing request through $M,I,D,W,R,E,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LocatedBoundedRealSetUp$, $\LocatedIntervalUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedBoundedRealSetUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate admits exactly these ten rows. Its classifier compares accepted packets only through $M,I,D,W,R,E,H,C,P,N$ and has no coordinate for Heine--Borel compactness, arbitrary least-upper-bound completeness, trichotomy, countable choice, host set equality, or $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Located bounded real set NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:located-bounded-real-set-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\LocatedBoundedRealSetUp$ carrier
$$
B=(M,I,D,W,R,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedBoundedRealSetUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the located-membership window, the closed interval bound, the dyadic approximation ledger, the stream windows, the regular readbacks, the real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item located-membership exposure, requiring every membership or apartness request to enter through the displayed row $M$ and through one of the finite windows named by $W$;
\item interval-bound exposure, requiring every accepted member read to retain the $\LocatedIntervalUp$ row $I$ before a boundedness conclusion is exported;
\item dyadic approximation exactness, requiring every lower or upper approximation read to use the dyadic scales and comparison rows recorded in $D$;
\item regular readback handoff, requiring $R$ to be the only route from the displayed dyadic and stream-window data into regular rational observations;
\item real-seal non-escape, requiring $E$ to remain downstream of $M,I,D,W,R$ and to export no completed supremum or host set object;
\item transport, continuation, provenance, and naming stability, requiring $H,C,P,N$ to preserve and source exactly the displayed rows componentwise.
\end{enumerate}
No located-bounded-set obligation is stored outside those rows.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet $B=(M,I,D,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)$. The carrier exposes the located-membership row, the interval bound, the dyadic ledger, the finite windows, the regular readbacks, and the real seal as the whole analytic surface.

A membership read starts with $M$ and $W$. The row $M$ supplies the displayed membership or apartness question, while $W$ fixes the finite real-name and endpoint windows in which that question is read.

The boundedness read then passes through $I$ and $D$. The $\LocatedIntervalUp$ row supplies the closed interval boundary, and the dyadic ledger supplies the lower and upper approximation rows used to compare the inspected member data with that boundary.

The regular and real-facing rows are downstream. The row $R$ is the only regular rational handoff, and the row $E$ is exposed only after the membership, interval, dyadic, and window rows have been retained.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same packet. Since Heine--Borel compactness, arbitrary least-upper-bound completeness, trichotomy, countable choice, host set equality, and $\RealUp$ completeness have no carrier coordinate or replay edge, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Located bounded real set interval-bound handoff]
\label{thm:located-bounded-real-set-interval-bound-handoff}
Every $\LocatedSupremumUp$ or $\DyadicSupremumUp$ consumer of an accepted $\LocatedBoundedRealSetUp$ carrier factors through the finite route
$$
M,I \longmapsto D,W \longmapsto R \longmapsto E \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
Thus the consumer receives located membership windows, the $\LocatedIntervalUp$ closed bound, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ lower and upper approximation rows, finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readbacks, the terminal $\RealUp$ seal, componentwise transport, continuation replay, package provenance, and local naming. It receives no Heine--Borel compactness, arbitrary least-upper-bound completeness, trichotomy, countable-choice schedule, host set equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Start with a supremum-facing read of the accepted carrier. The packet contains no supremum coordinate, so the read must first enter through the located-membership row $M$ and the closed interval bound row $I$.

The approximation stage is finite. The dyadic row $D$ records the lower and upper comparison scales, and the stream row $W$ names exactly the inspected member and endpoint windows used by those comparisons.

The readback and seal rows then fix the public handoff. The row $R$ translates the displayed dyadic and stream-window data into regular rational observations, and $E$ seals only that observed finite boundary.

The structural rows preserve this route for both sibling consumers. A $\LocatedSupremumUp$ reader obtains its located family, upper-bound, and lower-approximation source rows from the displayed package, while a $\DyadicSupremumUp$ reader obtains the dyadic bound and approximation rows from $D,W,R$. No structural row can introduce compactness, completeness, trichotomy, countable choice, or host set equality because those data are absent from the carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Located bounded real set non-escape]
\label{thm:located-bounded-real-set-nonescape}
Let $B=(M,I,D,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\LocatedBoundedRealSetUp$ carrier. Every real-facing, interval-facing, or supremum-facing read exported by $B$ is exhausted by the rows $M,I,D,W,R,E,H,C,P,N$. In particular, the packet exports a finite bounded located-set source and not a Heine--Borel theorem, an arbitrary least-upper-bound theorem, a trichotomy decision, a countable-choice selector, a host set equality witness, or a $\RealUp$ completeness principle.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Inspect the accepted packet coordinate by coordinate. The only set-facing coordinate is $M$, the only bound coordinate is $I$, the only approximation coordinate is $D$, and the only finite observation coordinate is $W$.

The only handoff to regular rational and real-name consumers is $R$ followed by $E$. Any read that skips these rows has not followed the displayed carrier route, and any read that stays on the route can expose only the rows already named in the packet.

Transport, continuation replay, provenance, and naming do not enlarge the analytic surface. They preserve the same finite rows under $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$, so no escaped compactness, completeness, trichotomy, choice, or host set equality coordinate remains to be read.
\end{proof}
