\begin{definition}[Reality-constrained TruthCert carrier]
\label{def:reality-constrained-truth-cert-carrier}
A $\RealityConstrainedTruthCertUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(S,\Sigma,K,T,U,D,I,L,F,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the finite source row recording observation traces, source bundles, and the claim site.
\item $\Sigma$ is the reality-facing signature row extracted from the source and compared against the model-facing signature.
\item $K$ is the classifier row naming the sameness relation used for signature comparison.
\item $T$ is the tower row recording approximation levels and the endpoint at which the claim is read.
\item $U$ is the stability row naming the continuation class over which finite fit may be reused.
\item $D$ is the descent row listing the operations that survive the tower route.
\item $I$ is the invariant row recording observer-anchor transport and public content.
\item $L$ is the ledger row recording compression, idealization, hidden residue, provenance, and packaging.
\item $F$ is the failure-surface row naming finite defeats, domain exits, classifier mismatches, and later observations that can overturn the claim.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RealityConstrainedTruthCertUp}$ row binding the nine displayed TruthCert fields as one packet.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for model--reality identity, unbounded confirmation, unledgered compression, observer privilege, hidden classifier choice, or truth export outside the nine displayed TruthCert rows.
\end{definition}

\label{thm:reality-constrained-truth-cert-field-coverage}

Any accepted export of a reality-constrained truth claim must read these fields through $S,\Sigma,K,T,U,D,I,L,F$ before the local name $N$ is exposed.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

The classifier field is the row $K$. The tower field is the row $T$, while the stability and descent fields are $U$ and $D$.

The invariant field is the row $I$, the ledger field is the row $L$, and the failure-surface field is the row $F$. The local name $N$ binds these rows but adds no further truth coordinate.

Thus the NameCert surface covers the nine TruthCert fields and is exhausted by them. A consumer that omits any one row reads a projection of the packet, not an accepted $\RealityConstrainedTruthCertUp$ export.
\end{proof}

\label{thm:reality-constrained-truth-cert-sibling-dependency}

\begin{proof}

The stability row $U$ consumes the physical-induction seal, because finite fit can be reused only over the displayed continuation class. Consequently the packet is composite rather than atomic: its accepted route depends on the open-fit, objectivity, explanation, physical-induction, and tower-compression siblings, and no single sibling alone carries the full TruthCert surface.
