\begin{definition}[Reality-constrained TruthCert carrier]
\label{def:reality-constrained-truth-cert-carrier}
A $\RealityConstrainedTruthCertUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(S,\Sigma,K,T,U,D,I,L,F,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the finite source row recording observation traces, source bundles, and the claim site.
\item $\Sigma$ is the reality-facing signature row extracted from the source and compared against the model-facing signature.
\item $K$ is the classifier row naming the sameness relation used for signature comparison.
\item $T$ is the tower row recording approximation levels and the endpoint at which the claim is read.
\item $U$ is the stability row naming the continuation class over which finite fit may be reused.
\item $D$ is the descent row listing the operations that survive the tower route.
\item $I$ is the invariant row recording observer-anchor transport and public content.
\item $L$ is the ledger row recording compression, idealization, hidden residue, provenance, and packaging.
\item $F$ is the failure-surface row naming finite defeats, domain exits, classifier mismatches, and later observations that can overturn the claim.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RealityConstrainedTruthCertUp}$ row binding the nine displayed TruthCert fields as one packet.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for model--reality identity, unbounded confirmation, unledgered compression, observer privilege, hidden classifier choice, or truth export outside the nine displayed TruthCert rows.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Reality-constrained TruthCert field coverage]
\label{thm:reality-constrained-truth-cert-field-coverage}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:reality-constrained-truth-cert-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\RealityConstrainedTruthCertUp}$ surface covers exactly the source, signature, classifier, tower, stability, descent, invariant, ledger, and failure-surface fields of the certified physical truth packet. Any accepted export of a reality-constrained truth claim must read these fields through $S,\Sigma,K,T,U,D,I,L,F$ before the local name $N$ is exposed.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission exposes exactly $Q=(S,\Sigma,K,T,U,D,I,L,F,N)$. The source field is the row $S$, and the signature field is the row $\Sigma$.

The classifier field is the row $K$. The tower field is the row $T$, while the stability and descent fields are $U$ and $D$.

The invariant field is the row $I$, the ledger field is the row $L$, and the failure-surface field is the row $F$. The local name $N$ binds these rows but adds no further truth coordinate.

Thus the NameCert surface covers the nine TruthCert fields and is exhausted by them. A consumer that omits any one row reads a projection of the packet, not an accepted $\RealityConstrainedTruthCertUp$ export.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Reality-constrained TruthCert sibling dependency]
\label{thm:reality-constrained-truth-cert-sibling-dependency}
Every accepted $\RealityConstrainedTruthCertUp$ packet consumes the open-fit, objectivity, explanation, physical-induction, and tower-compression dependencies supplied by the sibling certificate family. The open-fit dependency controls finite fit and finite defeat; the objectivity dependency controls invariant observer reading; the explanation dependency controls source-to-claim handoff; the physical-induction dependency controls stability over continuations; and the tower-compression dependency controls descent, ledger, and hidden residue.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the carrier rows of \autoref{def:reality-constrained-truth-cert-carrier}. The rows $S,\Sigma,K,F$ require the finite source, signature comparison, classifier, and defeat surface supplied by the open-fit certificate family.

The invariant row $I$ requires the observer-anchor discipline supplied by the objectivity certificate family. Without that row, the packet can carry a private observation route but not a public reality-constrained truth claim.

The source-to-claim handoff is supplied by the explanation certificate family. It binds signatures, derivation routes, and admissible explanatory use before the local truth certificate can be read.

The stability row $U$ consumes the physical-induction seal, because finite fit can be reused only over the displayed continuation class. The tower, descent, and ledger rows $T,D,L$ consume the tower-compression certificate, because endpoint claims must retain their approximation route and hidden residue.

Consequently the packet is composite rather than atomic: its accepted route depends on the open-fit, objectivity, explanation, physical-induction, and tower-compression siblings, and no single sibling alone carries the full TruthCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Reality-constrained TruthCert carrier admission]
\label{thm:reality-constrained-truth-cert-carrier-admission}
An accepted $\RealityConstrainedTruthCertUp$ packet exposes exactly the carrier
$$
Q=(S,\Sigma,K,T,U,D,I,L,F,N).

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the carrier definition
\autoref{def:reality-constrained-truth-cert-carrier}. Its displayed packet is
exactly $Q=(S,\Sigma,K,T,U,D,I,L,F,N)$.

Apply the field-coverage theorem

source, signature, classifier, tower, stability, descent, invariant, ledger,
and failure-surface fields with the rows $S,\Sigma,K,T,U,D,I,L,F$, and it
reserves $N$ for local naming.

No remaining row is available for carrier admission. A consumer that asks for a
second truth coordinate is therefore asking outside the admitted packet rather
than reading an accepted $\RealityConstrainedTruthCertUp$ export.
\end{proof}

The field-coverage theorem
\autoref{thm:reality-constrained-truth-cert-field-coverage} then makes $L$ a
