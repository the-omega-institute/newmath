The $\MetaCICClosurePreservationAuditUp$ packet turns the closure-preservation audit map of \autoref{ch:metacic-closure-preservation-audit-map} into a finite NameCert audit object. It sits between the row-level seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closure-preservation-audit-seal-namecert}, the downstream subject-reduction socket of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-subject-reduction-discharge-socket-namecert}, and the closed-normal boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-normal-form-consistency-seal-namecert}. The packet records where closedness travels through shift, substitution, beta-step, and beta-star routes; it does not add a typing-preservation route.

\begin{definition}[MetaCIC closure-preservation audit packet]
\label{def:metacic-closure-preservation-audit-packet}
A $\MetaCICClosurePreservationAuditUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
M=(A,S,R,V,U,B,Z,X,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ is the audit-map anchor naming \autoref{ch:metacic-closure-preservation-audit-map};
\item $S$ is the sibling audit-seal row from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closure-preservation-audit-seal-namecert};
\item $R$ is the closed shift row for the shift-one closedness route;
\item $V$ is the closed variable-substitution row;
\item $U$ is the closed full-substitution row;
\item $B$ is the closed one-step beta row;
\item $Z$ is the closed finite beta-star row;
\item $X$ is the subject-reduction frontier row, pointing to \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-subject-reduction-discharge-socket-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-normal-form-consistency-seal-namecert};
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the anchor, seal, five closedness rows, frontier row, route rows, provenance, and local naming;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which consumers read the anchor, seal, closedness rows, beta-star export, frontier boundary, provenance, and local naming;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over the audit map, audit seal, subject-reduction socket, normal-form consistency seal, componentwise $\hsame$, displayed $\Cont$, and local $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MetaCICClosurePreservationAuditUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for closed subject reduction, full typed preservation, quotient equality of beta paths, host conversion, global normalization, or confluence.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC closure-preservation audit finite obligation surface]
\label{thm:metacic-closure-preservation-audit-finite-obligation-surface}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:metacic-closure-preservation-audit-packet}, the local $\NameCert_{\MetaCICClosurePreservationAuditUp}$ obligation surface consists exactly of carrier admission for
$$
M=(A,S,R,V,U,B,Z,X,H,C,P,N),
$$
audit-map anchoring through $A$, sibling audit-seal replay through $S$, the five closedness rows $R,V,U,B,Z$, the subject-reduction frontier row $X$, componentwise transport through $H$, displayed continuation reads through $C$, package provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the displayed packet $M$. The audit anchor $A$ fixes the source map, and $S$ imports the finite audit-seal interface without replacing the local rows $R,V,U,B,Z$.

The five closedness rows are then read directly. They account for shift, variable substitution, full substitution, one-step beta, and finite beta-star closedness. The row $X$ is separate: it points to the subject-reduction socket and the normal-form consistency seal as frontier consumers, not as extra closedness theorems.

Finally inspect $H,C,P,N$. Transport, route replay, provenance, and local naming preserve the rows already present in $M$. Since the carrier contains no other coordinate, the listed rows exhaust the finite obligation surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC closure-preservation audit beta-star closedness export]
\label{thm:metacic-closure-preservation-audit-beta-star-closedness-export}
Every accepted $\MetaCICClosurePreservationAuditUp$ packet exports beta-star closedness only through the displayed row $Z$, its componentwise $\hsame$ transport in $H$, its displayed $\Cont$ replay in $C$, the provenance row $P$, and the local naming row $N$. A consumer from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-normal-form-consistency-seal-namecert} may cite this export as a closedness row, but not as subject reduction, typed conversion transport, or closed consistency.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the packet projection and isolate the beta-star coordinate $Z$. The theorem-row inventory in \autoref{thm:metacic-closure-preservation-audit-finite-obligation-surface} names $Z$ as the only beta-star closedness row.

Route replay then passes through $H,C,P,N$. These rows preserve and expose the same beta-star row; they do not introduce a typing derivation, a conversion rule, or a closed-consistency theorem.

For a normal-form consumer, the allowed read is therefore a finite closedness export. Any stronger claim would require a coordinate outside $M$, and the carrier definition provides none.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC closure-preservation audit subject-reduction frontier separation]
\label{thm:metacic-closure-preservation-audit-sr-frontier-separation}
Every subject-reduction-facing read of an accepted $\MetaCICClosurePreservationAuditUp$ packet factors through the frontier row $X$ and then through the finite socket of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-subject-reduction-discharge-socket-namecert}. The read may use the closedness rows $R,V,U,B,Z$ as audit evidence, but it cannot manufacture a closed subject-reduction theorem, discharge the four subject-reduction rows, or bypass the normal-form boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-normal-form-consistency-seal-namecert}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the frontier coordinate $X$. Its role is to name the crossing from closure preservation to subject-reduction consumers, and its cited socket is the only displayed route for that crossing.

The closedness rows $R,V,U,B,Z$ remain available as evidence rows. They are transported by $H$ and replayed by $C$, but their conclusions are closedness statements, not typed preservation statements.

The normal-form consistency seal receives beta-star closedness only as a bounded input. Because the packet has no coordinate for closed subject reduction or full consistency, the frontier read stops at the socket and the normal-form boundary.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[MetaCIC closure-preservation audit strict local obstruction]
\label{cor:metacic-closure-preservation-audit-strict-local-obstruction}
Let an accepted $\MetaCICClosurePreservationAuditUp$ packet be read through the carrier
$$
M=(A,S,R,V,U,B,Z,X,H,C,P,N)
$$
of \autoref{def:metacic-closure-preservation-audit-packet}. Any consumer-facing negative use of its subject-reduction frontier is a strict local obstruction: it blocks only the converse route from closedness preservation to closed subject reduction, only the reuse of beta-star closedness as typed preservation, and only the bypass of the socket and normal-form boundary named in \autoref{thm:metacic-closure-preservation-audit-sr-frontier-separation}. It cannot be reread as a global impossibility theorem, a host theorem, a countermodel to closure preservation, an external validation principle, or a hidden discharge of an unlisted coordinate.
\end{corollary}
\begin{proof}
Project the packet using \autoref{def:metacic-closure-preservation-audit-packet}. Its positive closedness coordinates are exactly the rows $R,V,U,B,Z$, with $Z$ singled out by \autoref{thm:metacic-closure-preservation-audit-beta-star-closedness-export} as the beta-star closedness export. The carrier also displays the frontier row $X$, the componentwise transport row $H$, the continuation row $C$, the provenance row $P$, and the local naming row $N$.

The only subject-reduction-facing crossing supplied by the packet is the one identified in \autoref{thm:metacic-closure-preservation-audit-sr-frontier-separation}: a read factors through $X$ and then through the finite subject-reduction socket. The same theorem allows $R,V,U,B,Z$ to be used as audit evidence, but it denies manufacture of closed subject reduction, discharge of the subject-reduction rows, and bypass of the normal-form boundary. Thus the obstruction applies to the attempted converse or reuse route that treats closedness evidence as typed preservation evidence.

No stronger negative reading is available from the finite carrier. The definition explicitly omits coordinates for closed subject reduction, full typed preservation, quotient equality of beta paths, host conversion, global normalization, and confluence. The transport, continuation, provenance, and naming rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve access to displayed rows; they do not add a host theorem, a global countermodel, or an external validation channel. Therefore every negative consumer-facing use remains the named local obstruction and rejects any unlisted coordinate.
\end{proof}
