$$

\begin{enumerate}

\item $W$ is the GAP socket row attached to the claim that the finite trace does not close;
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule that replays the observation windows of $T$;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row obtained from the replayed finite windows when the observations are consumed by the real-and-completion interface;
\item $Q$ is the downstream $\RealUp$ seal row, present only as a consumer-facing boundary for the finite readback;

\begin{theorem}[FiniteTraceGapSocket NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:finite-trace-gap-socket-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:finite-trace-gap-socket-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteTraceGapSocketUp}$ surface consists of five finite rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item trace carrier: every accepted object exposes the tuple $G=(T,W,S,R,Q,H,C,P,N)$ and the bounded observation list $T$;
\item socket classifier: classifier comparison transports the trace row, GAP socket row, replay schedule, readback row, real seal row, provenance row, and local naming row by the displayed $\hsame$ data;
\item finite observation replay: each consumer read of an observed window factors through $T$, then through the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule $S$, and then through the displayed $\Cont$ route into $R$;
\item ledger non-escape: the package row $P$ exhausts the available dependencies and exposes no induction rule, hidden infinite stream, selected limit, host equality, trichotomy, or completeness datum;
\item $\StreamNameUp/\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff: the finite replay schedule $S$ is the only source for the $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $R$, and the real seal $Q$ is readable only after that handoff.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Trace carrier exposure is projection from \autoref{def:finite-trace-gap-socket-carrier}. The bounded list $T$, GAP socket $W$, replay schedule $S$, readback row $R$, seal row $Q$, transports, continuation routes, provenance, and local naming row are all coordinates of one finite $\BHist$ packet.

Socket classifier stability is componentwise. The stored $\hsame$ rows in $H$ compare exactly the displayed coordinates, and the $\Cont$ rows in $C$ replay the same trace, socket, schedule, readback, seal, package, and naming routes after transport.

For finite observation replay, a consumer first reads a window from $T$. The only stream-facing route then passes through the schedule $S$, and the only regular-readback route passes from $S$ to $R$ through the displayed continuation ledger.

Ledger non-escape follows from the package inventory. Since $P$ names only the displayed BEDC rows, any induction rule, infinite stream, selected limit, host equality test, trichotomy branch, or completeness principle would require an extra coordinate absent from $G$.

Finally, the $\StreamNameUp/\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff is the route $T$ to $S$ to $R$ to $Q$. Thus the real-and-completion consumer sees only finite replay data and the visible GAP socket that records what remains unclosed.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FiniteTraceGapSocket real-window boundary]
\label{thm:finite-trace-gap-socket-real-window-boundary}
Every $\RealUp$ or completion-facing consumer of an accepted $\FiniteTraceGapSocketUp$ packet factors through the finite route
$$
T \to W \to S \to R \to Q \to H \to C \to P \to N.
$$
The consumer may inspect the observed finite windows and the GAP socket that marks the unclosed universal claim, but it cannot read induction, host completeness, a selected real limit, or an infinite observation stream from the packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier $G=(T,W,S,R,Q,H,C,P,N)$. The only observation source is the finite trace $T$, and the only record of what the trace does not close is the GAP socket $W$.

Apply \autoref{thm:finite-trace-gap-socket-namecert-obligations}. Its finite observation replay row routes every observed window through $S$ and then into $R$, while its handoff row exposes $Q$ only after that regular readback.

The ledger non-escape row rules out the unavailable structure. Therefore every real-window or completion-facing read remains inside the displayed packet and treats $W$ as a socket rather than as an internally discharged universal claim.
\end{proof}
