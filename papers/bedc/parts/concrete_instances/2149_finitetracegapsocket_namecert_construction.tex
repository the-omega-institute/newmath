\begin{theorem}[FiniteTraceGapSocket NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:finite-trace-gap-socket-namecert-obligations}

\item finite observation replay: each consumer read of an observed window factors through $T$, then through the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule $S$, and then through the displayed $\Cont$ route into $R$;
\item ledger non-escape: the package row $P$ exhausts the available dependencies and exposes no induction rule, hidden infinite stream, selected limit, host equality, trichotomy, or completeness datum;
\item $\StreamNameUp/\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff: the finite replay schedule $S$ is the only source for the $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $R$, and the real seal $Q$ is readable only after that handoff.

For finite observation replay, a consumer first reads a window from $T$. The only stream-facing route then passes through the schedule $S$, and the only regular-readback route passes from $S$ to $R$ through the displayed continuation ledger.

Ledger non-escape follows from the package inventory. Since $P$ names only the displayed BEDC rows, any induction rule, infinite stream, selected limit, host equality test, trichotomy branch, or completeness principle would require an extra coordinate absent from $G$.

Finally, the $\StreamNameUp/\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff is the route $T$ to $S$ to $R$ to $Q$. Thus the real-and-completion consumer sees only finite replay data and the visible GAP socket that records what remains unclosed.
