\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CompactUniformModulusTransferUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-compact-uniform-modulus-transfer-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop-style compact metric uniform continuity, the $\CompactUniformModulusTransferUp$ packet records the finite handoff that transfers compact finite-net and pointwise continuity data into a uniform modulus row. It consumes the finite compact source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compact-namecert}, the compact metric net surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, the continuity interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformcontinuity-namecert}, the finite stream and regular readback rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}. The packet is a finite modulus-transfer NameCert surface, not an open-cover compactness proof or a host modulus selector.

\begin{definition}[Compact uniform modulus transfer carrier]
\label{def:compact-uniform-modulus-transfer-carrier}
A $\CompactUniformModulusTransferUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
M=(K,G,U,S,R,D,F,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $K$ is the $\CompactUp$ finite-net source row.
\item $G$ is the $\CompactMetricUp$ metric coverage row for the same finite centers and source distance witnesses.
\item $U$ is the $\UniformContinuityUp$ pointwise-to-uniform continuity row consumed by the transfer.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window row listing the inspected finite centers, tolerance requests, and pointwise-radius reads.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row attached to those windows.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger used for finite radius comparison and shrinkage.
\item $F$ is the modulus-transfer ledger that records the finite lower-bound handoff into the downstream uniform-modulus consumer row.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for compact, compact-metric, uniform-continuity, stream, readback, dyadic, transfer, replay, provenance, and local-name rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay through $K,G,U,S,R,D,F,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CompactUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\UniformContinuityUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CompactUniformModulusTransferUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for open-cover compactness, selected centers, selected Lebesgue numbers, host modulus functions, quotient point equality, countable choice, or ambient metric completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Compact uniform modulus transfer obligations]
\label{thm:compact-uniform-modulus-transfer-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\CompactUniformModulusTransferUp$ carrier $M=(K,G,U,S,R,D,F,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CompactUniformModulusTransferUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the compact finite-net row, compact-metric coverage row, uniform-continuity row, stream windows, regular rational readbacks, dyadic tolerance ledger, modulus-transfer ledger, componentwise transport, continuation replay, package provenance, and local naming; source lock between $K,G,U$; finite-window exactness through $S,R$; dyadic tolerance exactness through $D$; and non-escape from open-cover compactness, selected centers, selected Lebesgue numbers, host modulus functions, quotient equality, choice, and ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:compact-uniform-modulus-transfer-carrier}. The compact row, compact-metric row, continuity row, stream windows, readbacks, dyadic tolerance ledger, transfer ledger, transport rows, replay rows, provenance row, and local name are displayed as one finite packet.

The source lock is the shared read of $K,G,U$. A transfer consumer cannot use the compact finite-net row without the compact-metric distance surface and the continuity row that supplies pointwise radius data.

Finite-window and dyadic exactness are read through $S,R,D$. The ledger $F$ can therefore transfer only the displayed finite lower-bound data, and $H,C,P,N$ preserve that route without adding any excluded compactness or choice principle.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Compact uniform modulus transfer handoff]
\label{thm:compact-uniform-modulus-transfer-handoff}
Every accepted $\CompactUniformModulusTransferUp$ packet hands a downstream uniform-modulus consumer only the finite route
$$
\CompactUp,\ \CompactMetricUp,\ \UniformContinuityUp,\ \StreamNameUp,\ \RegSeqRatUp,\ \DyadicRatCoreUp,\ \BHist,\ \hsame,\ \Cont,\ \Pkg,\ \NameCert .
$$
The handoff records compact finite-net coverage, pointwise continuity radius reads, finite stream windows, regular rational readbacks, dyadic tolerance comparison, and the transfer ledger. It exports no open-cover compactness theorem, selected net center, selected Lebesgue number, host modulus function, quotient equality, countable choice, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the transfer obligations of \autoref{thm:compact-uniform-modulus-transfer-namecert-obligations}. They identify the compact, compact-metric, continuity, stream, readback, dyadic, and transfer rows as the only NameCert-readable data.

Route the downstream consumer through $F$. The ledger can be consumed only after $K,G,U,S,R,D$ have supplied the finite-net, pointwise-radius, window, readback, and tolerance rows.

Finally use $H,C,P,N$ to transport, replay, source, and name the same packet. Since the excluded compactness, selection, equality, choice, and completeness data have no coordinate in the carrier, the downstream handoff is exactly the displayed finite modulus-transfer route.
\end{proof}
