\chapter{Concrete Instance Manifest v1.4}
\label{ch:v14-concrete-manifest}

This part is the v1.4 increment. It does not rewrite the frozen finite kernel, the proof sprint, or the proof-standing layer. Its purpose is narrower: to turn two previously template-level components into concrete paper-level instances.

\begin{enumerate}
  \item the first concrete globalization instance, built from signatures, package tokens, and gap ledgers;
  \item the first concrete naming-certificate constructions, namely \(\NatUp\) and \(\AddUp\).
\end{enumerate}

\begin{principle}[Concrete-instance increment]
A proof-standing layer shows that a theorem spine can stand. A concrete-instance layer shows that the spine is inhabited. In this increment, we therefore construct minimal inhabitants of the \(\PkgPol\), \(\GapPol\), and \(\NameCert\) templates.
\end{principle}

\begin{definition}[Scope of v1.4]
The v1.4 concrete-instance layer is restricted to finite-kernel data. It does not expand real-like, circle-like, fold-like, or completed arithmetic interfaces. In particular, \(\Seal\)-based completion remains a thin future module.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Status]
The constructions in this part are paper-level instance constructions. They are intended to be translated into the external implementation contract, but this part itself is not an implementation artifact.
\end{remark}
