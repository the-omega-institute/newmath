\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\DyadicSubsequenceUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-dyadic-subsequence-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges, constructive subsequence extraction is read as a finite choice of inspected windows and monotone index evidence before any compactness conclusion is invoked. The $\DyadicSubsequenceUp$ packet records that L10 selection surface over the bounded real sequence rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-bounded-sequence-namecert}, the finite observation route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the compactness-facing route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bolzanoweierstrass-namecert}. It packages dyadic tolerance windows, monotone unary reindexing, and Real-seal readback as displayed NameCert data rather than as an ambient subsequence selector or a completed compactness theorem.

\begin{definition}[Dyadic subsequence carrier]
\label{def:dyadic-subsequence-carrier}
A $\DyadicSubsequenceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(A,W,D,I,O,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ is the bounded real sequence source row, exposing only the displayed sequence name, bound certificate, and finite request interface.
\item $W$ is the finite window ledger, recording the sequence windows inspected before each subsequence output is admitted.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row, storing dyadic comparison budgets for window selection, bound reuse, and later readback.
\item $I$ is the monotone unary reindexing row, recording a finite increasing index map from output positions to inspected source positions.
\item $O$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ output-window row, exposing the selected subsequence only by finite windows.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row reached from $O,W,D,I$.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row for each accepted readback request, exposed only after the source, window, tolerance, index, stream, and regular-readback rows are displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the bounded sequence source, windows, dyadic ledger, monotone index row, stream windows, regular readback, Real seals, replay, provenance, and local naming.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a subsequence or compactness-facing request through $A,W,D,I,O,R,E,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RealBoundedSequenceUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DyadicSubsequenceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $A,W,D,I,O,R,E,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for a global choice function, quotient stream, selected limit, ambient compactness theorem, or host completeness of $\RealUp$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Dyadic subsequence classifier]
\label{def:dyadic-subsequence-classifier}
Two accepted $\DyadicSubsequenceUp$ carriers
$$
S=(A,W,D,I,O,R,E,H,C,P,N)
\quad\text{and}\quad
S'=(A',W',D',I',O',R',E',H',C',P',N')
$$
are classified as the same local subsequence certificate exactly when the displayed bounded-source rows agree, the finite window ledgers present the same inspected source windows, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance rows agree on every recorded comparison budget, the monotone unary reindexing rows choose the same finite output-to-source positions, the $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ readback rows agree after the recorded $\hsame$ transports, and the $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows replay and name the same packet.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Dyadic subsequence NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:dyadic-subsequence-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\DyadicSubsequenceUp$ carrier $S=(A,W,D,I,O,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\DyadicSubsequenceUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the bounded sequence source, finite windows, dyadic tolerance rows, monotone index row, finite stream output, regular rational readback, terminal Real seals, componentwise transport, continuation replay, package provenance, and local naming. Every compactness-facing read of the packet is classified only by these displayed rows and exports no global selector, quotient stream, selected limit, ambient compactness theorem, or host Real-completeness coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:dyadic-subsequence-carrier}. The tuple displays the bounded source, inspected windows, dyadic budgets, monotone reindexing row, output stream windows, regular readback, Real seals, and structural rows.

The classifier of \autoref{def:dyadic-subsequence-classifier} fixes equality of accepted packets by those same rows. Thus a NameCert consumer cannot compare subsequence data by an off-packet selector or by an ambient limit object.

The structural obligations project $H,C,P,N$. They transport, replay, source, and name only the displayed packet, so the excluded global choice, quotient-stream, selected-limit, compactness, and Real-completeness coordinates remain absent.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Dyadic subsequence window extraction]
\label{thm:dyadic-subsequence-window-extraction}
Every finite output window exported by an accepted $\DyadicSubsequenceUp$ packet factors through the route
$$
A\longmapsto W\longmapsto D\longmapsto I\longmapsto O\longmapsto R\longmapsto E\longmapsto H\longmapsto C\longmapsto P\longmapsto N.
$$
The route first opens a bounded real sequence source, then records the inspected source windows, dyadic tolerances, and monotone unary reindexing before exposing the $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ readback rows. No output window may be read directly from an ambient subsequence choice function or from a Bolzano-Weierstrass compactness conclusion.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from an accepted carrier $S=(A,W,D,I,O,R,E,H,C,P,N)$. A finite output-window request is replayed through $C$, so it must expose the bounded source $A$, the source windows $W$, the dyadic tolerance ledger $D$, and the monotone index row $I$ before it reads the output stream row $O$.

Regular rational readback and Real sealing occur after those rows. The packet reaches $R$ and $E$ only through the displayed stream window and tolerance route, with $H$ preserving componentwise transport.

Finally $P$ and $N$ source and name the same finite route. Since no carrier coordinate stores an ambient selector or compactness theorem, every exported output window remains a finite window-extraction certificate.
\end{proof}
