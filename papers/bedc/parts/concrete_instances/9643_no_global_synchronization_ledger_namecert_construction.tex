\begin{definition}[NoGlobalSynchronizationLedger carrier]
\label{def:no-global-synchronization-ledger-carrier}
A $\NoGlobalSynchronizationLedgerUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(H_0,H_1,R,B,O,I,K,S,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $H_0$ and $H_1$ are the two source history rows whose local reads are compared;
\item $R$ is the positive no-global-synchronization refusal row, recording that no shared synchronization $\BHist$, global clock, privileged frame, total observer order, or selected all-observer map is admitted;
\item $B$ is the $\NoGlobalSyncBoundaryUp$ boundary row whose refusal surface is being consumed;
\item $O$ is the $\LocalityCellUp$ row that binds the two local read sites before a causal or rate consumer can read them;
\item $I$ is the $\InterHistInvariantUp$ row that receives the displayed local comparison without adding a global frame;
\item $K$ is the downstream consumer row for $\CrossHistCausalUp$, $\ObserverLocalityTransportUp$, or $\MaxCausalRateUp$;
\item $S$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the histories, refusal row, boundary row, locality cell, invariant row, consumer row, routes, provenance, and local name;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay for reading exactly those rows;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\NoGlobalSynchronizationLedgerUp$, $\NoGlobalSyncBoundaryUp$, $\LocalityCellUp$, $\ObserverLocalityTransportUp$, $\InterHistInvariantUp$, $\CrossHistCausalUp$, $\MaxCausalRateUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\NoGlobalSynchronizationLedgerUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a shared synchronization history, global clock, privileged host frame, total observer order, quotient of histories, metric spacetime, host equality oracle, or selected limit.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[NoGlobalSynchronizationLedger NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:no-global-synchronization-ledger-namecert-obligations}

$$
L=(H_0,H_1,R,B,O,I,K,S,C,P,N),
$$
refusal exactness through $R$, boundary consumption through $B$, observer-locality exposure through $O$, invariant readback through $I$, downstream handoff through $K$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $S$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, package provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:no-global-synchronization-ledger-carrier}. The rows $H_0,H_1,R,B,O,I,K,S,C,P,N$ are exactly the histories, refusal row, boundary row, locality row, invariant row, consumer row, transport, replay, provenance, and naming surface.

The refusal and boundary obligations are separate. The row $R$ is the positive ledger coordinate saying which global synchronization data are absent from the accepted packet, while $B$ records the boundary packet from which that refusal is inherited.

The locality, invariant, and consumer obligations then read $O,I,K$ after the two source histories and the refusal row have been displayed. The structural rows $S,C,P,N$ transport, replay, package, and name the same finite packet, so no further NameCert obligation remains outside the listed coordinates.
\end{proof}
