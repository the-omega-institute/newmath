It sits after the broader Cauchy-name surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchysequencespace-namecert}, the finite schedule rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance core of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. \begin{definition}[RegularCauchySequenceSpace carrier]
\label{def:regular-cauchy-sequence-space-carrier}

$$

$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $F$ is a finite family of $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular rational sequence rows.
\item $\rho$ is the regular-rate ledger assigning dyadic Cauchy budgets to the rows of $F$.
\item $\sigma$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule that chooses the inspected sequence and finite window.
\item $W$ records the finite common-window data used by the scheduled family inspection.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row attached to the requested regularity budget.
\item $Q$ is the surrounding $\CauchySequenceSpaceUp$ row through which the same family can be read as a Cauchy-name-space object.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after $F,\rho,\sigma,W,D,Q$ have been displayed.

\begin{theorem}[RegularCauchySequenceSpace NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-sequence-space-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RegularCauchySequenceSpaceUp$ carrier $R=(F,\rho,\sigma,W,D,Q,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchySequenceSpaceUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission, regular-rate ledger exposure, scheduled finite-window stability, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance exactness, $\CauchySequenceSpaceUp$ handoff, $\RealUp$ seal routing, componentwise transport, displayed continuation replay, package provenance, local naming, and refusal of quotient streams, selected limits, countable choice, host real equality, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, and bridge checking.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-sequence-space-carrier}. The accepted packet exposes the regular rational family $F$, the regular-rate ledger $\rho$, the schedule $\sigma$, the finite window row $W$, and the dyadic tolerance row $D$ before any Cauchy-space or Real-facing consumer can read the packet.

The Cauchy-name-space handoff is then forced through $Q$. The surrounding $\CauchySequenceSpaceUp$ row can consume the same family, schedule, and tolerance data, but it receives them only as displayed rows of this finite carrier.

Finally the terminal seal $E$ is downstream of $F,\rho,\sigma,W,D,Q$ and is transported only by $H$ and replayed only by $C$. The provenance row $P$ and local naming row $N$ close the certificate surface, while the absence of any additional carrier coordinate excludes quotient streams, selected limits, countable choice, host real equality, ambient completeness, Lean verification, and bridge checking.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegularCauchySequenceSpace real handoff]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-sequence-space-real-handoff}
Every $\RealUp$ consumer of an accepted $\RegularCauchySequenceSpaceUp$ carrier factors through the displayed route
$$
F,\rho,\sigma,W,D,Q,E,H,C,P,N.
$$
Thus the consumer first reads regular rational sequence data, then a regular-rate dyadic budget, then a scheduled finite window, then the $\CauchySequenceSpaceUp$ handoff, and only then the terminal real-name seal. The route exports no quotient of Cauchy names, no selected limiting representative, no host real equality, and no ambient $\RealUp$ completeness assertion.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with an accepted carrier $R=(F,\rho,\sigma,W,D,Q,E,H,C,P,N)$. The only regular sequence data available to a consumer are the rows $F$ and $\rho$, and the only finite observation channel is the schedule and window pair $\sigma,W$ together with the dyadic tolerance row $D$.

Use \autoref{thm:regular-cauchy-sequence-space-namecert-obligations}. It binds regular-rate exposure, scheduled window stability, readback, and tolerance exactness to the same finite packet.

The Cauchy-space read reaches $Q$ only after those rows have been displayed, and the Real-facing read reaches $E$ only after $Q$ has been exposed. Since the carrier definition lists no quotient, selected limit, host real equality, or ambient completeness coordinate, every $\RealUp$ consumer route is exhausted by the displayed rows.
\end{proof}
