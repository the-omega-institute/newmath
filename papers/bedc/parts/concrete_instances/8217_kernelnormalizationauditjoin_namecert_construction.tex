\begin{theorem}[Kernel-normalization audit join NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:kernel-normalization-audit-join-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:kernel-normalization-audit-join-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\KernelNormalizationAuditJoinUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $J=(K,A,Q,N,S,B,R,H,C,P,L)$, kernel-stamp exposure through $K$, axiom-query exposure through $A$, normalization-audit replay through $Q$, closed-normal handoff through $N$, subject-reduction boundary exposure through $S$, non-escape through $B$, route-join ledger exposure through $R$, componentwise transport through $H$, displayed replay through $C$, package provenance through $P$, and local naming through $L$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:kernel-normalization-audit-join-carrier}. The rows $K,A,Q,N,S$ are the only substantive audit rows: kernel acceptance, axiom-query ancestry, normalization replay, closed-normal endpoint, and subject-reduction boundary.

The join ledger $R$ and continuation row $C$ determine how a consumer reads those rows together. The transport, provenance, and local naming rows preserve the same finite inventory. Since $J$ contains no further coordinate, the displayed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}

Next read the normalization and consistency side. The normalization-audit row $Q$ supplies only the candidate-mediated replay surface, while $N$ supplies the closed-normal endpoint and $S$ keeps the subject-reduction obstruction visible.
