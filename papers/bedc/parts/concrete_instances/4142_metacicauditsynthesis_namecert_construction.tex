\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\MetaCICAuditSynthesisUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-metacicauditsynthesis-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\MetaCICAuditSynthesisUp$ packet realizes the audit synthesis of \autoref{ch:metacic-audit-synthesis} as a finite BEDC naming certificate. It sits beside the confluence packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacic-confluence-audit-packet-namecert}, the decidable checker packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacicdecidableauditpacket-namecert}, and the closed-normal consistency packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closednormalconsistencypacket-namecert}: those chapters expose single audit families, while the present chapter records how their status rows and the blocked subject-reduction handoff are read together without creating a hidden full-consistency theorem.

\begin{definition}[MetaCIC audit synthesis carrier]
\label{def:metacic-audit-synthesis-carrier}
A $\MetaCICAuditSynthesisUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(M,N,D,C,R,B,H,T,P,L)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the submap-status row for the subject-reduction, confluence, normalisation, substitution, decidability, and closed-consistency assembly maps named in \autoref{ch:metacic-audit-synthesis};
\item $N$ is the closed-normal consistency row, reading the available normal-form endpoint through the sibling packet \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closednormalconsistencypacket-namecert};
\item $D$ is the decidable-checker row, reading local typing and bounded normal comparison through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacicdecidableauditpacket-namecert};
\item $C$ is the confluence-status row, reading conditional bridges and closed-normal fragments through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacic-confluence-audit-packet-namecert};
\item $R$ is the subject-reduction handoff row, naming the required discharge bundle and recording that the closed arbitrary-proof lift is not available inside this packet;
\item $B$ is the blocked-edge ledger row, listing full closed consistency, arbitrary closed Church--Rosser, unrestricted beta-conversion decidability, and hidden normalization transport as non-exported endpoints;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the displayed status, handoff, blocked-ledger, route, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item $T$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ route by which a consumer reads submap status first, then closed-normal consistency, then confluence and decidability siblings, and finally the blocked handoff ledger;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance row tying the synthesis packet to the MetaCIC audit overview and its concrete sibling packets;
\item $L$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MetaCICAuditSynthesisUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no coordinate proving closed subject reduction, no coordinate proving full closed consistency, no quotient over MetaCIC terms, and no host-level global decision procedure.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC audit synthesis NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:metacic-audit-synthesis-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:metacic-audit-synthesis-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\MetaCICAuditSynthesisUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $S=(M,N,D,C,R,B,H,T,P,L)$, submap-status exposure, closed-normal consistency exposure, decidable-checker exposure, confluence-status exposure, blocked subject-reduction handoff exposure, blocked-edge ledger exposure, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ route replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local naming row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier of \autoref{def:metacic-audit-synthesis-carrier}. The rows $M,N,D,C,R,B,H,T,P,L$ are the whole public packet, so carrier admission and local naming are direct projections.

The positive audit surface is read through $M,N,D,C$. The row $M$ records the six submap statuses as finite entries, while $N,D,C$ route the positive closed-normal, decidable-checker, and confluence fragments through their concrete sibling packets.

The boundary surface is read through $R$ and $B$. The row $R$ keeps the subject-reduction discharge bundle explicit, and $B$ records the endpoints that the synthesis does not export.

Finally $H,T,P,L$ provide transport, route replay, provenance, and local naming for the same finite surface. Since no additional coordinate exists, the NameCert obligations are exhausted by the displayed packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC audit synthesis map boundary]
\label{thm:metacic-audit-synthesis-map-boundary}
Every accepted $\MetaCICAuditSynthesisUp$ carrier reads the six MetaCIC audit maps only through the submap-status row $M$ and the sibling rows $N,D,C$. Agreement by $\hsame$ on $M,N,D,C,H,T,P,L$ preserves the displayed synthesis route, but it does not identify subject-reduction, confluence, normalisation, substitution, decidability, and consistency assembly as one theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the row $M$. It is a finite status row: each audit family is named with its available result and its non-claim boundary.

The sibling rows $N,D,C$ give concrete reads of three positive surfaces. They point to the closed-normal consistency, decidable checker, and confluence packets without merging their carriers.

Componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$ and route replay through $T$ preserve this status layout. They compare and replay the displayed rows; they do not construct a new theorem that collapses the six audit families into one hidden endpoint.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC audit synthesis closed-normal handoff]
\label{thm:metacic-audit-synthesis-normal-consistency-handoff}
An accepted $\MetaCICAuditSynthesisUp$ carrier may hand a consumer to the closed-normal consistency packet through $N$ and to the confluence and decidable-checker packets through $C$ and $D$. The handoff remains a closed-normal, bounded-checker, and conditional-confluence read; it is not a transport from arbitrary closed proof terms to closed beta-strong normal inhabitants of the internal false row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project $N$ first. It records the already-normal consistency endpoint supplied by the closed-normal packet, so the consumer receives the normal-fragment contradiction rule with its displayed closedness, typing, beta-strong, and false-type rows.

Next project $C$ and $D$. These rows supply confluence-status and decidable-checker reads beside the normal endpoint, not a route that changes arbitrary proof terms into normal proof terms.

The missing transport would have to pass through $R$, because $R$ is the subject-reduction handoff row. Since $R$ is recorded as a required handoff rather than a discharged theorem, the accepted handoff stops at the closed-normal packet and the sibling audit reads.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC audit synthesis blocked-edge ledger]
\label{thm:metacic-audit-synthesis-blocked-edge-ledger}
The blocked-edge ledger row $B$ of an accepted $\MetaCICAuditSynthesisUp$ carrier is stable under the componentwise classifier. Agreement on the positive rows $M,N,D,C$ and the structural rows $H,T,P,L$ cannot remove $B$, cannot replace $R$ by a closed subject-reduction theorem, and cannot export full closed consistency, arbitrary closed Church--Rosser, unrestricted beta-conversion decidability, or hidden normalization transport.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The classifier for the packet compares the displayed rows componentwise. Therefore an agreement that preserves $M,N,D,C,H,T,P,L$ still leaves $R$ and $B$ as named coordinates of the same carrier.

The row $R$ says where a later subject-reduction discharge would have to enter. The row $B$ records the endpoints that are unavailable unless that handoff is separately supplied.

Because the carrier has no further coordinate, there is no projection by which a consumer can erase $B$ or reinterpret $R$ as a proved closed subject-reduction theorem. The listed full-consistency, Church--Rosser, beta-conversion, and normalization-transport claims remain outside the packet.
\end{proof}
