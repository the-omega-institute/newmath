Following Horn and Johnson, \emph{Matrix Analysis}, the $\FiniteDimensionalSpectralGapUp$ packet records the finite matrix-analysis route by which a displayed matrix or finite-dimensional operator exposes an isolated eigen-window and a positive separation radius before any completed host spectrum is imported. It sits after the matrix carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-matrix-namecert}, the linear-map interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-linearmap-namecert}, the vector-space carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-vecspace-namecert}, the spectral-radius boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-spectral-radius-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet names a finite separation certificate for downstream perturbation and spectral-theorem routes; it does not choose eigenvectors, complete a spectrum, or identify a host operator with an ambient spectral object.

\begin{definition}[Finite-dimensional spectral-gap carrier]
\label{def:finite-dimensional-spectral-gap-namecert-carrier}
A $\FiniteDimensionalSpectralGapUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
G=(M,T,V,E,D,R,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the square $\MatrixUp$ row, exposing the finite entry table and shape ledger.
\item $T$ is the $\LinearMapUp$ or finite operator row read from $M$ over the displayed $\VecSpaceUp$ source $V$.
\item $V$ is the finite-dimensional $\VecSpaceUp$ row, carrying the basis-window and scalar-action data used by the matrix/operator comparison.
\item $E$ is the finite eigen-window row. It lists the displayed candidate eigenvalue cells, multiplicity tags when present, and the matrix-action comparison rows used to test each cell.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ separation-radius row, recording a positive dyadic distance between the distinguished eigen-window and every other displayed spectral window.
\item $R$ is the $\SpectralRadiusUp$ bound row, exposing the radius or norm-growth boundary used to keep the displayed windows inside the same finite operator packet.
\item $S$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row for the gap radius and the accepted eigen-window endpoints.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $M,T,V,E,D,R,S$ and the structural rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the route
$$
\MatrixUp,\LinearMapUp,\VecSpaceUp,\SpectralRadiusUp,\DyadicRatCoreUp
\longmapsto E,D,R,S
\longmapsto \FiniteDimensionalSpectralGapUp .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\FiniteDimensionalSpectralGapUp$, $\MatrixUp$, $\LinearMapUp$, $\VecSpaceUp$, $\SpectralRadiusUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteDimensionalSpectralGapUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $M,T,V,E,D,R,S,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for a completed host spectrum, selected eigenvectors, spectral projections, Schur or Jordan form, quotient equality of matrices, ambient compactness, or choice.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Finite-dimensional spectral-gap NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:finite-dimensional-spectral-gap-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\FiniteDimensionalSpectralGapUp$ carrier
$$
G=(M,T,V,E,D,R,S,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteDimensionalSpectralGapUp}$ surface consists exactly of five finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item matrix/operator admission for the displayed $\MatrixUp$ row $M$, $\LinearMapUp$ row $T$, and $\VecSpaceUp$ row $V$;
\item finite eigen-window admission through $E$, including only the displayed candidate cells and matrix-action comparison rows;
\item dyadic separation and real sealing through $D$ and $S$, requiring the gap radius and eigen-window endpoints to be read from displayed dyadic and real-name rows;
\item spectral-radius compatibility through $R$, bounding the accepted windows inside the same finite operator packet without replacing them by a completed spectrum;
\item componentwise transport, displayed replay, provenance, and local naming through $H,C,P,N$.
\end{enumerate}
No spectral-gap obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:finite-dimensional-spectral-gap-namecert-carrier}. The matrix, operator, and vector-space rows $M,T,V$ are the only source rows from which a consumer can read the finite-dimensional action.

The eigen-window obligation is the row $E$. It displays candidate cells and the corresponding action comparisons, so the packet exposes a finite windowed test rather than a selected eigenbasis or a host eigenspace.

The gap radius is read through $D$ and $S$. The dyadic row records positive separation from the other displayed windows, and the real seal records the endpoint data that downstream consumers may inspect.

The spectral-radius row $R$ supplies only a finite bound-facing boundary over the same operator packet. Together with $H,C,P,N$, it transports, replays, sources, and names the displayed surface, so the five listed rows exhaust the local certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Finite-dimensional spectral-gap nonescape]
\label{thm:finite-dimensional-spectral-gap-nonescape}

$$

$$

A downstream consumer receives no completed host spectrum, no selected eigenvector, no spectral projection, no Schur or Jordan decomposition, no quotient matrix equality, and no choice principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted packet $G=(M,T,V,E,D,R,S,H,C,P,N)$. Carrier projection first exposes the matrix row, the induced linear-map row, and the finite vector-space source.

The eigen-window row $E$ is downstream of those rows. It can compare displayed candidate cells with the matrix action, but it cannot introduce an eigenvector selector or an ambient spectral set because neither appears as a carrier coordinate.

The separation data are then read through $D$ and $S$. The positive dyadic radius and its real seal are attached to the same displayed window, so a consumer cannot detach the gap from the finite packet that produced it.

Finally the spectral-radius bound $R$ and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$ keep the readback within the same transported and replayed surface. Since the excluded spectral objects have no field in the packet, the route cannot escape to a host spectrum or a choice-based eigenvalue selection.
