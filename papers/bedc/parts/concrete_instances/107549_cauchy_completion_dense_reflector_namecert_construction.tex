\begin{theorem}[CauchyCompletionDenseReflector NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-dense-reflector-namecert-obligations}

\item carrier admission for the source completion schedule, dense-image row, adjunction row, finite windows, dyadic tolerance ledger, regular readback, real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming;
\item dense-source exposure, requiring every completion-facing read to expose $S$ and then $I$ before the real-seal row $R$ can be consumed;
\item reflector exactness, requiring the dense image $I$ and source schedule $S$ to be replayed through the same $\BHist$, $\hsame$, and $\Cont$ rows before any completion consumer is served;
\item universal-row handoff, requiring every extension or uniqueness consumer to pass through the adjunction row $A$ after the dense image and before the terminal real seal;
\item classifier transport, requiring all accepted comparisons to use only the displayed componentwise transport $H$, replay $C$, provenance $P$, and local name $N$;
\item ledger refusal, excluding quotient completion representatives, selected limits, countable choice, host equality of completed objects or real names, and ambient completeness.
\end{enumerate}
No dense-reflector obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:cauchy-completion-dense-reflector-carrier}. The accepted packet exposes exactly the rows $S,I,A,W,T,Q,R,H,C,P,N$ and no detached completion object.

Dense-source exposure is read in order. The source completion schedule $S$ supplies the finite completion transformer route, and the dense image row $I$ places the displayed source inside the completion seal before any real-facing read reaches $R$.

Reflector exactness and universal-row handoff then use the same route. The rows $H$ and $C$ replay the schedule and dense-image coordinates componentwise, while $A$ is the only adjunction row available to extension and uniqueness consumers.

Finally inspect the provenance and local naming rows. The row $P$ names only the completion monad, dense embedding, adjunction, StreamName, RegSeqRat, DyadicRatCore, Real, and structural BEDC rows, and $N$ packages exactly that surface. Hence the listed refusals cannot be exported by an accepted carrier.
\end{proof}
