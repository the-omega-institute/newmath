\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\LimitSpaceUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-limit-space-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Kelley and Bishop--Bridges convergence-space and limit-space terminology, the $\LimitSpaceUp$ packet records the finite interface by which a displayed Cauchy-filter or filter-base row is read against an observation schedule, a dyadic tolerance ledger, a regular rational readback, and a terminal real limit seal. It consumes the Cauchy-filter surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyfilter-namecert}, the finite observation schedule of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance core of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the regular rational route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet names a convergence-space interface, not a quotient filter, selected limit, host equality of convergent objects, or ambient completeness theorem.

\begin{definition}[LimitSpace carrier]
\label{def:limit-space-carrier}
A $\LimitSpaceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(F,S,D,R,L_0,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $F$ is a $\CauchyFilterUp$ or filter-base row, carrying the finite filter requests and Cauchy-stability witnesses permitted at this limit-space site;
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ observation schedule that records which finite windows of $F$ are inspected;
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger attached to those windows;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row that turns the scheduled and tolerated observations into regular rational data;
\item $L_0$ is the $\RealUp$ limit-seal row reached only after $F,S,D,R$ have been displayed;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the filter, schedule, tolerance, readback, limit seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a convergence request through $F,S,D,R,L_0,H,P,N$;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LimitSpaceUp$, $\CauchyFilterUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LimitSpaceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for quotient filters, selected limits, countable choice, host equality of convergent filters, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, or bridge evidence.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[LimitSpace NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:limit-space-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\LimitSpaceUp$ carrier
$$
L=(F,S,D,R,L_0,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\LimitSpaceUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the Cauchy-filter or filter-base row, StreamName schedule, DyadicRatCore tolerance ledger, RegSeqRat readback, Real limit seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item filter-source exposure, requiring every limit-space consumer to read $F$ before any scheduled observation, tolerance comparison, readback, or limit seal is inspected;
\item schedule and tolerance discipline, requiring $S$ and $D$ to determine the finite windows and dyadic budgets used by the readback row;
\item regular readback discipline, requiring $R$ to be downstream of $F,S,D$ and upstream of the terminal seal;
\item limit-seal non-escape, requiring the $\RealUp$ row $L_0$ to be read only as a seal for the displayed readback route;
\item structural preservation through $H,C,P,N$;
\item refusal of quotient filters, selected limits, countable choice, host equality of convergent filters, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, and bridge evidence.
\end{enumerate}
No local obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:limit-space-carrier}. The tuple displays exactly the Cauchy-filter or filter-base source, finite schedule, dyadic tolerance ledger, regular rational readback, real limit seal, and structural rows.

The source obligation is first. A convergence consumer cannot reach a scheduled observation or terminal seal without reading $F$, because no other row carries filter-source data.

The schedule and tolerance obligations are then forced by $S$ and $D$. They determine the finite windows and dyadic budgets that the regular readback row may inspect, so $R$ is downstream of the displayed observation discipline rather than a separate selected representative.

Finally $L_0$ is exposed only as the real seal for that readback route, while $H,C,P,N$ preserve, replay, source, and name the same finite packet. Since the excluded principles have no carrier coordinate, they cannot become NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LimitSpace Cauchy-filter consumer route]
\label{thm:limit-space-cauchy-filter-consumer-route}
Every $\CauchyFilterUp$-facing consumer of an accepted $\LimitSpaceUp$ carrier factors through the displayed route
$$
F \longmapsto S,D \longmapsto R \longmapsto L_0 \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
The consumer first reads the Cauchy-filter or filter-base row, then the StreamName schedule and DyadicRatCore tolerance ledger, then the RegSeqRat readback, and only then the Real limit seal. The route exports no quotient filter, no selected limit, no host equality of convergent filters, no countable-choice schedule, and no ambient completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The first row $F$ is the only source for Cauchy-filter or filter-base data, so every filter-facing read starts there.

The next step is the finite observation discipline. The schedule row $S$ names the inspected windows and the tolerance row $D$ names the dyadic budgets attached to them; without those two rows the regular readback row has no displayed finite request to answer.

The readback and seal rows are downstream. The row $R$ converts the scheduled observations into regular rational data, and $L_0$ seals the resulting route as a $\RealUp$ limit row without selecting an external limit object.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve and name the same route. Because the carrier contains no quotient-filter coordinate, selected-limit coordinate, host equality row, choice schedule, or ambient completeness row, every accepted consumer factors through the displayed path.
\end{proof}
