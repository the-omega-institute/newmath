\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CrossHistCausalRouteUp$}

The $\CrossHistCausalRouteUp$ interface realizes the route layer of the Inter-Hist Locality chain of \autoref{ch:visions-inter-hist-locality}. It sits after the observer-Hist identity surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observerhistidentity-namecert}, the causal witness surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-crosshistcausal-namecert}, the maximum-rate boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-maxcausalrate-namecert}, the invariant observer-gate surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-interhistinvariant-namecert}, and the finite replay surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuationtrace-namecert}. Its role is to bind those five siblings into one finite route certificate before a later causal-rate packet reads the maximum-rate row.

\begin{definition}[Cross-Hist causal-route carrier]
\label{def:cross-hist-causal-route-carrier}
A $\CrossHistCausalRouteUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(A,B,D,M,S,G,T,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ and $B$ are the two observer-history slots, each admitted through $\ObserverHistIdentityUp$;
\item $D$ is the displayed finite family of $\CrossHistCausalUp$ causal rows over the ordered observer slots and selected unary slot ledgers;
\item $M$ is the $\MaxCausalRateUp$ row attached to exactly the causal family $D$;
\item $S$ is the observer-symmetry gate, recording that the route family remains readable after swapping the two observer slots through the displayed transports;
\item $G$ is the observer non-escape gate, refusing a global frame, synchronized clock, metric tensor, or host-valued speed for the route read;
\item $T$ is the $\ContinuationTraceUp$ row that replays the accepted route from the observer slots through $D$, $M$, $S$, and $G$;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the observer slots, causal rows, maximum-rate row, observer gates, continuation trace, package row, and local naming row;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which a consumer reads $A,B,D,M,S,G,T,H,P,N$;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CrossHistCausalRouteUp$, $\ObserverHistIdentityUp$, $\CrossHistCausalUp$, $\MaxCausalRateUp$, $\InterHistInvariantUp$, $\ContinuationTraceUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CrossHistCausalRouteUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier is a finite route packet. It contains no global observer frame, synchronized clock, metric tensor, host real-valued speed, detached maximum-rate read, or causal witness outside the displayed route family.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cross-Hist causal-route NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cross-hist-causal-route-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:cross-hist-causal-route-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\CrossHistCausalRouteUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for
$$
Q=(A,B,D,M,S,G,T,H,C,P,N),
$$
observer-slot admission, displayed causal-row replay, maximum-rate attachment to $D$, both observer-gate rows $S,G$, continuation-trace replay through $T$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ access, package provenance, and the local naming row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission projects the finite tuple in \autoref{def:cross-hist-causal-route-carrier}. The rows $A,B,D,M,S,G,T,H,C,P,N$ are the complete public inventory of the route packet.

The observer slots are read through $A$ and $B$, and the causal rows are exactly the displayed family $D$. The maximum-rate row $M$ is attached to that same family, so no consumer can read a detached maximum over unlisted causal data.

The two observer gates are $S$ and $G$. The row $S$ supplies the observer-swap route, while $G$ refuses global synchronization, privileged frames, metric tensors, and host-valued speeds.

The continuation trace $T$, transport row $H$, displayed routes $C$, provenance $P$, and local naming row $N$ replay and name the same finite route. Since the carrier has no coordinate outside those rows, the listed obligations exhaust the $\NameCert_{\CrossHistCausalRouteUp}$ surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cross-Hist causal-route maximum-rate gate]
\label{thm:cross-hist-causal-route-maximum-rate-gate}
An accepted $\CrossHistCausalRouteUp$ packet cannot expose a maximum-rate read unless the displayed causal-route family $D$ and both observer-gate rows $S,G$ are present in the same carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier through \autoref{def:cross-hist-causal-route-carrier}. The only maximum-rate coordinate is $M$, and its definition attaches it to the displayed causal family $D$.

Apply \autoref{thm:cross-hist-causal-route-namecert-obligations}. The local route obligations require the observer-symmetry gate $S$ and the observer non-escape gate $G$ before the maximum-rate row is exposed to a consumer.

Therefore a read that omits $D$, $S$, or $G$ is not a read of the accepted route packet. The packet supplies no second rate row, hidden causal family, global frame, or synchronized clock through which such a read could be recovered.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cross-Hist causal-route sibling independence]
\label{thm:cross-hist-causal-route-sibling-independence}
The $\CrossHistCausalRouteUp$ packet stores all five sibling surfaces: $\ObserverHistIdentityUp$ for observer slots, $\CrossHistCausalUp$ for causal rows, $\MaxCausalRateUp$ for the bound row, $\InterHistInvariantUp$ for observer-gate reading, and $\ContinuationTraceUp$ for finite replay. It is not bijective to any one of those siblings, because deleting any one displayed surface removes a row required by \autoref{def:cross-hist-causal-route-carrier}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier coordinates. The rows $A,B$ carry the observer slots, $D$ carries the causal family, $M$ carries the maximum-rate row, $S,G$ carry the observer-gate boundary, and $T$ carries route replay.

Each listed sibling supplies a different row kind consumed by the route packet. Removing the observer slots leaves no anchors for $D$; removing $D$ leaves no causal family for $M$; removing $M$ leaves no maximum-rate route; removing $S,G$ leaves no observer-gate boundary; removing $T$ leaves no finite replay.

Thus the route packet is a finite product of the five displayed surfaces with transport, access, provenance, and naming rows. No projection to a single sibling can be inverted without recreating the deleted rows.
\end{proof}
