\begin{definition}[Fast Cauchy completion carrier]
\label{def:fast-cauchy-completion-carrier}
A $\FastCauchyCompletionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
M=(M_0,S,D,Q,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M_0$ is the $\CauchyModulusUp$ threshold row. It records the finite precision request, the displayed tail index, and the modulus budget that every downstream completion or Real read must consume.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row opened at the threshold named by $M_0$.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger for the same window, including dyadic radii, endpoint comparisons, rounding rows, and tolerance arithmetic.
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row obtained from $S$ and $D$ under the budget exposed by $M_0$.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal handoff reached only after $M_0,S,D,Q$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the modulus row, stream windows, dyadic ledger, regular readback, Real seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a finite completion or Real precision request through $M_0,S,D,Q,E,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\FastCauchyCompletionUp$, $\RealCauchyModulusUp$, $\CauchyModulusUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\CauchyRateComparisonUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\FastCauchyCompletionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $M_0,S,D,Q,E,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for quotient streams, selected limits, trichotomy, countable choice, host equality, or an ambient completeness theorem for $\RealUp$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Fast Cauchy completion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:fast-cauchy-completion-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\FastCauchyCompletionUp$ carrier $M=(M_0,S,D,Q,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\FastCauchyCompletionUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed Cauchy-modulus threshold, stream-window row, dyadic tolerance ledger, regular rational readback, Real seal handoff, and structural rows $H,C,P,N$;
\item modulus discipline, requiring every finite precision request to read the threshold row $M_0$ before any stream window, dyadic tolerance, regular readback, or Real seal is exposed;
\item stream and dyadic readback, requiring $S$ and $D$ to be read under the same displayed modulus budget before the $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $Q$ may be consumed;
\item Real-seal handoff, requiring $E$ to be terminal with respect to $M_0,S,D,Q$ and to expose no detached Real object or ambient completeness theorem;
\item transport and replay stability, requiring $H$ and $C$ to preserve the same ordered route under componentwise sameness and displayed continuation replay;
\item ledger non-escape, requiring $P$ and $N$ to name only the displayed rows and to export no quotient stream, selected limit, trichotomy, countable-choice, host-equality, or ambient-completeness coordinate.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Fast Cauchy completion modulus Real seal]
\label{thm:fast-cauchy-completion-modulus-real-seal}
Let $M=(M_0,S,D,Q,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\FastCauchyCompletionUp$ carrier. Every Real-facing read exported by $M$ factors through the displayed modulus route
$$
M_0 \longmapsto S \longmapsto D \longmapsto Q \longmapsto E \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
Consequently a Real or Completion consumer receives a Cauchy-modulus threshold, finite stream windows, dyadic tolerance arithmetic, regular rational readback, the terminal Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming before it can read the Real-facing datum. The route cannot use quotient streams, selected limits, trichotomy, countable choice, host equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a Real-facing read of $M$. By \autoref{def:fast-cauchy-completion-carrier}, the only Real coordinate is $E$, and the definition places it downstream of the modulus threshold $M_0$, stream window $S$, dyadic ledger $D$, and regular readback row $Q$.

The first part of the route is fixed by the modulus and finite-window rows. The threshold $M_0$ supplies the finite tail budget, $S$ exposes only the corresponding stream observations, and $D$ supplies the dyadic tolerances and endpoint comparisons for those observations.

The second part of the route is fixed by regular readback and the Real seal. The row $Q$ is consumed only after $S$ and $D$, and $E$ seals the Real-facing datum only after the regular row has been displayed.

Finally, $H,C,P,N$ preserve and name the same dependency order. Because the excluded objects have no coordinate in the carrier and no replay edge in the displayed route, every Real-facing read factors through the modulus route shown in the statement.
\end{proof}
