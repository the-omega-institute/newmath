\begin{theorem}[Diagonal tail selector TailCofinalitySchedule factorization]
\label{thm:diagonal-tail-selector-tail-cofinality-factorization}
For every accepted $\DiagonalTailSelectorUp$ packet
$$
G=(R,n,\mu,k,W,D,T,S,H,C,P,N),
$$
each Real-seal consumer of $S$ factors first through the $\TailCofinalityScheduleUp$ route from unary precision to finite window, dyadic ledger, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, and $\RealUp$ seal before using the diagonal selector rows. Thus the consumer reads the precision request and finite-window schedule before it uses $k,W,D,T,S,H,C,P,N$ as the diagonal completion-facing packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with \autoref{thm:tail-cofinality-schedule-diagonal-handoff}. It orders the schedule-facing route from the unary precision row through the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row, and the $\RealUp$ seal.

Apply \autoref{thm:diagonal-tail-selector-limit-handoff}. It identifies the diagonal Real-completion consumer with the finite packet $R,n,\mu,k,W,D,T,S,H,C,P,N$, so the schedule route is consumed before the selector rows are used as a completion-facing package.

Finally use \autoref{thm:diagonal-tail-selector-tail-meet-consumption-scope}. The transported tail-meet rows remain inside the same displayed source, window, dyadic, regular-rational, seal, transport, route, provenance, and naming surface. Hence the factorization has no escape channel through quotient streams, selected limits, countable choice, ambient completeness, host real equality, or hidden tail oracles.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Diagonal tail selector Real seal boundary scope]
\label{thm:diagonal-tail-selector-real-seal-boundary-scope}

$$
G=(R,n,\mu,k,W,D,T,S,H,C,P,N),

\autoref{thm:diagonal-tail-selector-tail-meet-consumption-scope}. In

$\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, $\NameCert$, $\StreamNameUp$,
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RegularCauchyTailMeetUp$,
$\CauchyLimitSealUp$, and $\RealUp$, with $S$ reached only after
$R,n,\mu,k,W,D,T,H,C,P,N$ are in scope.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:diagonal-tail-selector-real-seal-factorization}. Every
$\RealUp$ seal read from $S$ factors through the finite selector packet and
its transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.

Use \autoref{thm:diagonal-tail-selector-window-source-scope} to identify the
source side of that packet. The rows $R,n,\mu,k,W,D$ supply exactly the
finite $\StreamNameUp$ and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ source window before $T$ and
$S$ are available.

Use \autoref{thm:diagonal-tail-selector-tail-meet-consumption-scope} for the
regular-tail side. When $T$ is supplied by the shared-threshold route, it is
consumed only as the transported $\RegularCauchyTailMeetUp$ certificate
inside the same structural rows.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:diagonal-tail-selector-seal-nonescape}. The
$\CauchyLimitSealUp$ and $\RealUp$ boundary therefore has no escape channel
outside the displayed BEDC route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Diagonal tail selector downstream non-escape scope]

\begin{proof}

cofinal-window exhaustion, and seal non-escape over one finite route.

The window source scope then supplies the finite source boundary. Apply

The Real seal boundary scope supplies the completion-facing boundary. Apply
\autoref{thm:diagonal-tail-selector-real-seal-boundary-scope} and then
\autoref{thm:diagonal-tail-selector-seal-nonescape}; downstream consumers
