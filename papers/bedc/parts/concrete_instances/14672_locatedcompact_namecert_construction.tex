\begin{definition}[LocatedCompact carrier]
\label{def:located-compact-carrier}
A $\LocatedCompactUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(X,L,F,A,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the compact carrier row, naming the inspected metric or real-facing source as a finite BEDC carrier rather than a host compact space;
\item $L$ is the locatedness test row, returning for each displayed point-and-radius request the finite comparison data that witnesses whether the request lies inside the located boundary or is apart from it by a recorded margin;
\item $F$ is the finite-net witness row, exposing for each positive tolerance a closed $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ of centers, $\mathsf{InBundle}$ membership rows, and coverage distances that factor through the $\TotallyBoundedUp$ surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-totallybounded-namecert};
\item $A$ is the apartness and separatedness row, tying the locatedness test to the $\ApartnessRealUp$ boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-apartnessreal-namecert} and to the separated completion discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-hausdorffcompletion-namecert};
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for carrier, locatedness, finite-net, apartness, separatedness, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ routes from finite-window requests through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the net row $F$, the locatedness row $L$, and the terminal $\RealUp$ seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert};

\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedCompactUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only through these eight displayed rows. It has no coordinate for an open cover, a selected finite subcover, a quotient compactum, host choice, a host completion object, or an ambient completeness theorem.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[LocatedCompact NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:located-compact-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\LocatedCompactUp$ carrier $K=(X,L,F,A,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedCompactUp}$ surface consists of five finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed compact carrier row $X$ together with its locatedness, finite-net, apartness, transport, continuation, provenance, and naming support;
\item locatedness stability, stating that the test row $L$ is preserved by the stored $\hsame$ transports and by the displayed comparison rows reaching the $\RealUp$ seal;
\item finite-net exposure, stating that each tolerance request reaches only the closed $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, $\mathsf{InBundle}$, center, radius, and coverage-distance rows displayed in $F$;
\item apartness and separatedness compatibility, stating that $A$ is the only route from located-distance evidence to the separated completion and apartness-real sibling surfaces;
\item ledger exactness for the $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ row, with no hidden open-cover, quotient, choice, or ambient-completeness coordinate.
\end{enumerate}
These obligations name located compactness as a finite BEDC packet over the displayed L10 sibling route, not as a host compact-space principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet $K=(X,L,F,A,H,C,P,N)$. The projection exposes exactly the compact carrier row, locatedness test, finite-net witness, apartness and separatedness row, componentwise transports, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming row.

The locatedness obligation is componentwise. A transported point-and-radius request is read through $H$, and the same displayed comparison route in $L$ supplies the inside-or-apart evidence before any $\RealUp$ seal is consumed.

The finite-net obligation is the $\TotallyBoundedUp$ handoff. A tolerance request reaches the closed $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ in $F$, then reads only its bundled centers, membership constructors, radii, and coverage-distance rows. No external list of centers or selected subcover is present.

The apartness and separatedness obligation uses $A$ as the unique boundary row. It ties the located-distance evidence to the apartness-real and Hausdorff-completion sibling surfaces while preserving the same $H,C,P,N$ support.

Finally, exactness follows from the carrier definition: $C$ replays only the displayed route, $P$ records only the named sibling certificates, and $N$ names the local packet. Since the carrier has no additional coordinate, the five obligations exhaust the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedCompactUp}$ surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LocatedCompact totally bounded handoff]
\label{thm:located-compact-totally-bounded-handoff}

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

Read the finite-net handoff through $F$. The handoff is the same finite-net surface consumed by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-totallybounded-namecert}: a positive tolerance request, a closed $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ of centers, membership constructors, and coverage rows.

Read the locatedness handoff through $L$. The comparison evidence is attached to the same carrier row $X$ and is transported only by $H$ before the continuation route $C$ reaches the real-facing seal.

The separated and apartness consumers can use $A$ only after those displayed handoffs are present. Since no open-cover, finite-subcover selector, quotient compactum, host-choice row, or ambient-completeness field occurs in the packet, compact and complete consumers receive exactly the finite-net and locatedness rows displayed above.
\end{proof}
