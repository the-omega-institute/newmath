It sits after the located-sign surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-intermediatevalue-namecert} and the bracket surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-root-bracket-namecert}, and it hands its retained windows to the finite stream, regular rational, dyadic tolerance, and real-name interfaces of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The certificate names the bisection-to-Cauchy seal route; it does not select a root, quotient real names, or import ambient completeness.

\begin{definition}[BisectionRootCauchySeal carrier]
\label{def:bisection-root-cauchy-seal-carrier}
A $\BisectionRootCauchySealUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
X=(I,S,M,V,B,W,R,D,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $I$ is the interval row. It records the initial lower and upper endpoint windows, their rational or dyadic cells, the endpoint order evidence, and the interval identity inherited by each retained branch.
\item $S$ is the sign row. It stores the displayed opposite endpoint signs and the branch orientation used by the bisection route.
\item $M$ is the modulus row. It records the requested regular Cauchy tolerance, the bisection depth sufficient for that tolerance, and the endpoint precision consumed by sign and midpoint reads.
\item $V$ is the $\IntermediateValueUp$ ledger row. It records the finite located-sign obligation surface that admits the sign-change interval and its displayed continuity data.
\item $B$ is the branch ledger. It stores the finite bisection choices, midpoint sign rows, retained subintervals, and regularity checks for the requested depth.
\item $W$ is the retained-window row. It records the lower and upper windows retained after the branch prefix and the diameter comparison demanded by the regular Cauchy read.
\item $R$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window row. It exposes the retained bisection windows as finite stream observations, one request depth at a time.
\item $D$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ readback row. It stores the regular rational readback, dyadic tolerance rows, midpoint-radius bounds, and rounding discipline used by completion-facing consumers.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row. It is available only after the retained windows and regular readback rows have been read.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the interval, signs, modulus, intermediate-value ledger, branch ledger, retained windows, stream windows, regular and dyadic readback, Real seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a root-location or completion request through endpoint inspection, sign inspection, modulus use, intermediate-value admission, finite bisection, retained-window exposure, regular readback, and Real-seal consumption.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BisectionRootCauchySealUp$, $\IntermediateValueUp$, $\RealRootBracketUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\BisectionRootCauchySealUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $I,S,M,V,B,W,R,D,E,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for selected roots, quotient reals, real trichotomy, open-cover compactness, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, host root equality, Lean verification, or bridge checking.

\begin{theorem}[BisectionRootCauchySeal NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:bisection-root-cauchy-seal-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\BisectionRootCauchySealUp$ carrier
$$
X=(I,S,M,V,B,W,R,D,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\BisectionRootCauchySealUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following five finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the interval, signs, modulus, intermediate-value ledger, branch ledger, retained windows, stream windows, regular and dyadic readback, Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item endpoint, sign, and modulus discipline, requiring $I,S,M$ to display the initial sign-change interval, the precision budget, and the bisection depth before any retained window is exposed;
\item finite bisection branch regularity, requiring $V$ and $B$ to keep every midpoint sign row, branch choice, retained interval, and diameter comparison attached to the same displayed sign-change packet;
\item StreamName, RegSeqRat, and Dyadic handoff, requiring $W,R,D$ to expose retained windows first as finite stream observations and then as regular rational readback with dyadic tolerance rows;
\item Real-seal nonescape, requiring $E,H,C,P,N$ to export only the displayed bisection-to-Cauchy route and no selected root, quotient real equality, real trichotomy, open-cover compactness, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, host root equality, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{enumerate}
No BisectionRootCauchySeal obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:bisection-root-cauchy-seal-carrier}. The accepted packet displays exactly the interval, signs, modulus, intermediate-value ledger, branch ledger, retained windows, regular readback, Real seal, and structural rows.

The endpoint, sign, and modulus discipline is the entry stage. The rows $I$, $S$, and $M$ must be read before a branch prefix can be inspected, so the packet cannot replace the finite sign-change data by a host interval or selected root.

Finite bisection branch regularity is the middle stage. The row $V$ admits the located-sign surface, while $B$ records only midpoint signs and retained intervals produced from that surface at the depth requested by $M$.

The StreamName, RegSeqRat, and Dyadic handoff is the readback stage. The retained windows $W$ are exposed through $R$ as finite stream observations and through $D$ as regular rational and dyadic tolerance rows before the Real seal is available.

Finally, $E,H,C,P,N$ seal, transport, replay, package, and name the same route. Since the omitted principles have no coordinates in the carrier, none of the five obligation rows can export them.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BisectionRootCauchySeal regular handoff]
\label{thm:bisection-root-cauchy-seal-regular-handoff}
Every $\RealUp$ or completion-facing root-location read of an accepted $\BisectionRootCauchySealUp$ carrier factors through the displayed finite bisection route
$$
I,S,M,V \longmapsto B \longmapsto W \longmapsto R,D \longmapsto E,H,C,P,N
$$
before consuming the $\RealUp$ seal. The read first opens the sign-change interval and modulus rows, then the finite branch ledger, then the retained windows, then the StreamName, RegSeqRat, and Dyadic readback rows, and only then the Real-facing seal. The handoff exports a regular Cauchy seal surface rather than a selected root or host equality theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted packet $X=(I,S,M,V,B,W,R,D,E,H,C,P,N)$. The only entry coordinates for a root-location read are the interval, sign, and modulus rows $I,S,M$, together with the intermediate-value ledger $V$.

The bisection stage is then forced through $B$. This row stores the finite branch prefix, midpoint signs, retained intervals, and diameter comparisons, so no downstream consumer can read a regular Cauchy window before the branch data have been fixed.

The regular readback stage is $W,R,D$. The retained windows in $W$ become finite stream observations through $R$, and the regular rational and dyadic tolerance rows in $D$ supply the completion-facing read.

The Real-facing stage is terminal. The seal $E$ is consumed only after the regular readback route is fixed, while $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, package, and name the same finite route. Thus every listed read factors through the displayed bisection route before it reaches the $\RealUp$ seal.
\end{proof}
