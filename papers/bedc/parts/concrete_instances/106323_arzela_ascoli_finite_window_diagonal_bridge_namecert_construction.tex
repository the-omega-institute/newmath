\begin{theorem}[Finite-window diagonal bridge route]
\label{thm:arzela-ascoli-finite-window-diagonal-route}
Every bridge read from an accepted
$\ArzelaAscoliFiniteWindowDiagonalBridgeUp$ carrier factors through the finite
route
$$
\AscoliDiagonalUp \longrightarrow
(\EquicontinuityFamilyUp,\CompactMetricUp) \longrightarrow
\CompleteMetricUp \longrightarrow
\StreamNameUp \longrightarrow
\RegSeqRatUp \longrightarrow
\RealUp .
$$
The route first reads the accepted AscoliDiagonal carrier, then the compact
source nets and equicontinuity ledger, then the complete target surface, then
the finite diagonal StreamName windows, then RegSeqRat readback and the Real
seal. The exported surface is a finite NameCert bridge and no choice-selected
subsequence or host function-space compactness object.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Let $B=(K,E,Y,D,W,R,S,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted carrier. Project $D$ first. Its
accepted $\AscoliDiagonalUp$ row fixes the diagonal extraction surface before
any Arzela-Ascoli consumer can inspect a target read.

Next project $K$ and $E$. The compact source row supplies finite net windows,
and the equicontinuity-family row supplies the shared tolerance-to-radius
ledger used by those windows.

Project $Y$ to read the complete-metric target boundary. The bridge can then
inspect only the finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows recorded in $W$, so each target
read is tied to displayed source-net points and displayed family indices.

The readback row $R$ records the corresponding $\RegSeqRatUp$ tolerance and
distance data, and the seal row $S$ repacks the finite readback as the visible
$\RealUp$ boundary. Componentwise transport through $H$, replay through $C$,
provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$ preserve exactly those
rows.

Since the carrier contains no unbounded subsequence selector, no ambient
compact-open topology, and no host function-space compactness coordinate,
every bridge read factors through the displayed finite route and exports
nothing beyond it.
\end{proof}
