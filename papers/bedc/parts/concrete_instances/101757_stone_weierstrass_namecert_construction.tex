\begin{definition}[Stone--Weierstrass carrier]
\label{def:stone-weierstrass-carrier}

$$
S=(K,A,U,V,R,M,E,H,C,P,N)
$$

\begin{enumerate}
\item $K$ is the compact source row, read through the $\CompactUp$ interface and its finite cover or finite-net witnesses.
\item $A$ is the algebra row, exposing only finitely named $\ContinuousMapUp$ generators over $K$ together with displayed addition, multiplication, scalar, and constant-one operations routed through $\RingUp$ and $\RealUp$.
\item $U$ is the unit and constants ledger, recording the visible constant real functions admitted into the algebra row.
\item $V$ is the separation ledger, assigning each displayed apartness request between named compact points a generator in $A$ and a finite real-window comparison that separates the two inspected points.
\item $R$ is the real-valued evaluation ledger, reading every generator, algebraic combination, and constant through the same $\RealUp$ windows used by the compact and continuous-map surfaces.
\item $M$ is the compact-continuity modulus row, recording the finite continuity and compactness data needed before approximation is requested.
\item $E$ is the approximation handoff row, sending an inspected continuous target, a rational error row, and the displayed algebra data to a $\WeierstrassApproximationUp$ consumer.

\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through these rows. It has no coordinate for nonconstructive compactness, choice-selected dense subsets, host function equality, an ambient function-space quotient, a host supremum norm, or ambient completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Stone--Weierstrass NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:stone-weierstrass-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\StoneWeierstrassUp$ carrier
$$
S=(K,A,U,V,R,M,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\StoneWeierstrassUp}$ surface consists of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the compact source, algebra, constants, separation, real-valued evaluation, compact-continuity modulus, approximation handoff, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item compact-source compatibility, requiring every point and finite witness consumed by the algebra and separation rows to pass through the same $\CompactUp$ row $K$;
\item algebra closure visibility, requiring $A$ and $U$ to display the continuous generators, constants, and finite algebraic operations used by the packet rather than a host algebra of all functions;
\item separation exactness, requiring each separation request in $V$ to name the generator, the two compact-point windows, and the real-window apartness comparison used to distinguish them;
\item evaluation and modulus routing, requiring $R$ and $M$ to mediate every real-valued read before any approximation handoff is exposed;
\item approximation handoff exactness, requiring $E$ to send the inspected continuous target and rational error through the displayed algebra data before it reaches $\WeierstrassApproximationUp$;
\item componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming through $H,C,P,N$;
\item ledger refusal for nonconstructive compactness, choice-selected dense subsets, host function equality, ambient function-space quotients, host supremum norms, ambient completeness, Lean verification, and bridge checking.
\end{enumerate}
No Stone--Weierstrass obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:stone-weierstrass-carrier}. The packet exposes exactly the compact source, algebra, constants, separation, evaluation, modulus, approximation, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

The compact-source and algebra clauses are then fixed by row membership. The row $K$ is the only compact input surface, while $A$ and $U$ are the only places where generators, constants, and algebraic operations are named.

For a separation request, the row $V$ supplies a displayed generator and the corresponding real-window comparison. The evaluation row $R$ routes that comparison through $\RealUp$, and $M$ supplies the compact-continuity information needed before a downstream approximation read is allowed.

The approximation handoff is the row $E$. It sends the inspected continuous target and rational error to the $\WeierstrassApproximationUp$ consumer only after the algebra, separation, evaluation, and modulus rows have been displayed.

Finally, $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite route. Since none of the excluded objects occurs as a carrier coordinate, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}
