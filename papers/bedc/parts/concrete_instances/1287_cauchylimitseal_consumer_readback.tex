\begin{theorem}[CauchyLimitSeal consumer readback]
\label{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-consumer-readback}
A downstream $\RealUp$ or completion consumer reads back exactly the sealed route assembled by the root-unblock package, together with the tail-extraction row of \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-tail-extraction} and the uniqueness boundary of \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-uniqueness-boundary}. The readback exports only the displayed $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$, $\RealUp$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply the root-unblock package of \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-root-unblock-package}. It gives the consumer one route from the accepted seal packet to the downstream $\RealUp$ or completion boundary.

Attach tail extraction. The tail row is already projected from the same displayed regular family, schedule, observation ledger, completion diagonal, and transport ledger, so it does not add a second consumer route.

Finally attach the uniqueness boundary. Equality-facing reads remain on the null-sequence and tail-comparison route admitted by the packet, and the readback has no coordinate for quotient completion, host uniqueness, selected limits, hidden family limits, or ambient completeness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyLimitSeal RegSeqRat StreamName route lock]
\label{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-regseqrat-streamname-route-lock}
Every accepted $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ packet consumed by a $\RealUp$ or completion-facing route factors through the displayed $\StreamNameUp$ finite window of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the $\RealUp$ seal row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, and the $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ consumer boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-cauchy-completion-namecert}. The factorization uses only the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows already exposed by the accepted seal route.

Consequently the accepted consumer route exports no quotient stream, host sequence equality, selected limit, ambient completeness, metric-completion row, or standard bridge row outside that finite L10 route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the consumer readback theorem, \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-consumer-readback}. It confines every downstream $\RealUp$ or completion consumer to the accepted seal packet, its root-unblock route, tail-extraction row, uniqueness boundary, and the displayed $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$, and $\RealUp$ rows.

Next attach the finite route theorems already exposed on the Real side. The four-face canonical route of \autoref{thm:real-l10-four-face-canonical-route} and the seal lattice of \autoref{thm:real-streamname-regseqrat-seal-lattice} force any completion-facing $\RealUp$ consumer carrying a $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ row through the ordered $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ faces before the seal row is read.

Finally use the terminal comparison exhaustion of \autoref{thm:real-l10-terminal-comparison-exhaustion} together with the $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ consumer boundary cited above. These rows keep the completion-facing endpoint on the same displayed histories, transports, continuations, provenance, and local names, so no quotient stream, host sequence equality, selected limit, ambient completeness, metric-completion coordinate, or standard bridge coordinate remains available to the consumer.
\end{proof}
