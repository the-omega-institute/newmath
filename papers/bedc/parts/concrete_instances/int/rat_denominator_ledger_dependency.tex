\label{thm:int-rat-denominator-ledger-dependency}
The $\IntUp$ signed-pair carrier supplies the numerator and denominator pair surface consumed by the positive-denominator $\RatUp$ ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rat-namecert}. \end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

Transport is componentwise. The $\hsame$ rows preserve the signed-pair components, while the displayed $\Cont$ replay keeps the numerator/denominator read inside the finite ledger rather than in a host quotient.

Finally, local $\NameCert$ provenance records that the $\RatUp$ denominator route reads an already displayed $\IntUp$ signed-pair surface. Hence the dependency is a visible ledger edge from $\IntUp$ to $\RatUp$, not a detached rational carrier.
\end{proof}
