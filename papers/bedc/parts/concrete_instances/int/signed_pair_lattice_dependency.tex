\begin{theorem}[IntUp signed-pair additive lattice dependency]
\label{thm:int-signed-pair-additive-lattice-dependency}
The $\IntUp$ signed-pair presentation is an additive child of the arithmetic lattice. Its balancing rows consume the unary magnitude rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-nat-namecert} and the continuation-compatible addition rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-add-namecert}; the later $\RatUp$ denominator presentation of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rat-namecert} consumes the resulting signed numerator row rather than rebuilding an independent integer quotient.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the signed-pair carrier from the integer interface. Its magnitude coordinate is unary, so it is supplied by the Nat row, and its balancing step is additive, so it is routed through the Add continuation ledger.

The rational side then reads the same signed-pair surface as numerator data before pairing it with the denominator row. Thus $\RatUp$ receives an already balanced $\IntUp$ row, with no separate quotient of integer pairs or field-style rational equality introduced at the integer boundary.
\end{proof}
