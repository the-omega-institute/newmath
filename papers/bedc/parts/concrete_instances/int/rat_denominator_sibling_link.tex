\section{Rat Denominator Sibling Link}
\label{sec:int-rat-denominator-sibling-link}

The $\IntUp$ signed-pair rows are the horizontal source for the $\RatUp$ denominator surface in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rat-namecert}. The signed numerator first passes through \autoref{lem:int-rat-signed-denominator-dependency} and the additive denominator route of \autoref{thm:int-rat-additive-denominator-route}; only then does the rational carrier read that balanced integer row beside its positive denominator witness. Thus the denominator and additive-denominator certificates consume an already displayed $\IntUp$ signed-pair route rather than a standalone rational quotient or field-equality row. The precise dependency is that the $\RatUp$ positive-denominator ledger consumes the $\IntUp$ signed-pair normalization route before it reads the rational denominator carrier in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rat-namecert}.
