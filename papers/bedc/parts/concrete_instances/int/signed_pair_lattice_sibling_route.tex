\begin{theorem}[Int signed-pair lattice sibling route]\label{thm:int-signed-pair-lattice-sibling-route}
Every signed-pair read exported by the $\IntUp$ hub to downstream $\RatUp$ or $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ consumers factors through the unary magnitude rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-nat-namecert} and the balancing row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-add-namecert} before any denominator or mantissa data are exposed. Consequently a rational numerator read from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rat-namecert} and a dyadic mantissa read from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert} receive the same displayed route: first the two $\NatUp$ magnitude rows, then the $\AddUp$ balance witness identifying their signed-pair difference, and only then the consumer-specific denominator, scale, or mantissa ledger.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the $\IntUp$ hub orientation as the boundary condition. It names $\NatUp$ as the source of unary magnitude data and $\AddUp$ as the balancing surface that makes a signed pair public as integer data.

A $\RatUp$ consumer cannot inspect a numerator before the signed-pair interface has displayed both magnitudes and the balancing row. Thus its denominator route is downstream of the $\NatUp,\AddUp$ lattice rather than an alternate source of integer evidence.

The same argument applies to a $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ mantissa consumer. The dyadic ledger can read signed mantissa data only after the integer route has opened the two unary magnitudes and the displayed balance witness.

Therefore both downstream consumers share the same finite sibling route through $\NatUp$ and $\AddUp$. Denominator and mantissa coordinates are exposed only after that route has been fixed.
\end{proof}
