\begin{theorem}[Integer history classifier same-tag readback]
\label{thm:int-history-classifier-same-tag-readback}
For all $m,n:\mathsf{BHist}$, the public integer history classifier reads back
exactly payload equality on each visible signed tag:
$$
\mathsf{IntHistoryClassifier}(\Ezero(m),\Ezero(n))
\Longleftrightarrow
\mathsf{Unary}(m)\land\mathsf{Unary}(n)\land\hsame(m,n).
$$
The one-tag branch has the same payload criterion:
$$
\mathsf{IntHistoryClassifier}(\Eone(m),\Eone(n))
\Longleftrightarrow
\mathsf{Unary}(m)\land\mathsf{Unary}(n)\land\hsame(m,n).
$$
The ten-field fit is the following. The traditional target is same-sign
equality of signed integers. The BEDC source consists of
$\mathsf{IntHistoryCarrier}$, $\mathsf{IntHistoryClassifier}$, $\hsame$, and
constructor no-confusion. The carrier is the same-tag unary-history carrier.
The classifier is the displayed two-branch definition. Stability is given by
transport along the payload $\hsame$. The ledger records the sign tag together
with the unary magnitude. The core theorem is this same-tag readback statement.
Exactness is the two displayed iff rows. The downstream consumer is the
$\RatUp$ integer numerator readback. The status is $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold $\mathsf{IntHistoryClassifier}$ at same-tag endpoints.

For the zero-tag forward direction, keep the matching $\Ezero$ branch. The
endpoint witnesses have the form $\hsame(\Ezero(m),\Ezero(m'))$ and
$\hsame(\Ezero(n),\Ezero(n'))$, so constructor inversion reads back
$\hsame(m,m')$ and $\hsame(n,n')$. Transitivity with the stored payload
$\hsame(m',n')$ yields $\hsame(m,n)$, and the same branch supplies
$\mathsf{Unary}(m)$ and $\mathsf{Unary}(n)$ after the endpoint readback. The
opposite $\Eone$ branch would force an $\Ezero$ endpoint to be $\hsame$ to an
$\Eone$ endpoint, contradicting constructor no-confusion.

For the zero-tag reverse direction, assume
$\mathsf{Unary}(m)\land\mathsf{Unary}(n)\land\hsame(m,n)$. Choose the
$\Ezero$ branch with payload witnesses $m$ and $n$, use reflexivity for the
two visible endpoint witnesses, and use the supplied payload $\hsame(m,n)$.

The one-tag forward direction is identical after replacing $\Ezero$ by
$\Eone$. The matching branch gives endpoint inversion and payload transport;
the mixed branch contradicts $\Eone$/$\Ezero$ no-confusion.

For the one-tag reverse direction, choose the $\Eone$ branch with payload
witnesses $m$ and $n$, reflexive visible endpoints, the two unary witnesses,
and the supplied payload $\hsame(m,n)$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.IntUp.IntHistoryClassifier\_same\_tag\_readback\_iff}

\begin{theorem}[Integer canonical difference-pair/history bridge]
\label{thm:int-canonical-difference-pair-history-bridge}
For all $m,n:\mathsf{BHist}$, if $\mathsf{Unary}(m)$ and
$\mathsf{Unary}(n)$, then the canonical same-sign difference pairs read back
exactly to the corresponding public signed-history classifier branches:
$$
\bigl(
\mathsf{IntPairClassifier}((m,\emp),(n,\emp))
\Longleftrightarrow
\mathsf{IntHistoryClassifier}(\Ezero(m),\Ezero(n))
\bigr)
\land
\bigl(
\mathsf{IntPairClassifier}((\emp,m),(\emp,n))
\Longleftrightarrow
\mathsf{IntHistoryClassifier}(\Eone(m),\Eone(n))
\bigr).
$$
The ten-field fit is the following. The traditional target is canonical signed
unary integers. The BEDC source consists of $\mathsf{IntPairClassifier}$,
$\mathsf{IntHistoryClassifier}$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$,
$\mathsf{append}$, $\Ezero$, and $\Eone$. The carrier is
$\mathsf{IntPairCarrier}$ together with $\mathsf{IntHistoryCarrier}$. The
classifier is the displayed same-sign bridge. Stability is inherited from the
append unit laws and same-tag readback. The ledger records the hidden empty
counter and the visible sign tag. The core theorem is this bridge. Exactness is
the two iff clauses. The downstream consumer is the $\RatUp$ numerator
readback and $\IntUp$ closure. The status is $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Assume $\mathsf{Unary}(m)$ and $\mathsf{Unary}(n)$. For the positive canonical
pair,
unfold $\mathsf{IntPairClassifier}$ at $((m,\emp),(n,\emp))$. The carrier
clauses reduce to the unary witnesses for $m$, $n$, and the two empty counters.
The classifier payload is
$\hsame(\mathsf{append}(m,\emp),\mathsf{append}(n,\emp))$, and the right
append unit laws identify this with $\hsame(m,n)$.

Apply Theorem~\ref{thm:int-history-classifier-same-tag-readback} to the
$\Ezero$ branch. Its readback criterion is exactly the same pair of unary
witnesses together with $\hsame(m,n)$, so the positive displayed
$\Longleftrightarrow$ follows.

For the negative canonical pair, unfold $\mathsf{IntPairClassifier}$ at
$((\emp,m),(\emp,n))$. The cross-addition payload is
$\hsame(\mathsf{append}(\emp,n),\mathsf{append}(\emp,m))$. The left append unit
laws reduce this to $\hsame(n,m)$, and symmetry of $\hsame$ changes the
orientation to $\hsame(m,n)$.

Apply Theorem~\ref{thm:int-history-classifier-same-tag-readback} to the
$\Eone$ branch. The same unary witnesses and the reoriented payload
$\hsame(m,n)$ are precisely its public-history criterion, giving the negative
displayed $\Longleftrightarrow$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Integer contextual canonical difference-pair/history bridge]
\label{thm:int-contextual-canonical-pair-history-bridge}
For $a,m,n:\mathsf{BHist}$ with $\mathsf{Unary}(a)$,
$\mathsf{Unary}(m)$, and $\mathsf{Unary}(n)$, the positive-context canonical
difference pair reads back exactly to the zero-tag public signed-history
classifier:
$$
\mathsf{IntPairClassifier}((\mathsf{append}(a,m),\emp),
(\mathsf{append}(a,n),\emp))
\Longleftrightarrow
\mathsf{IntHistoryClassifier}(\Ezero(m),\Ezero(n)).
$$
The negative-context canonical difference pair reads back exactly to the
one-tag public signed-history classifier:
$$
\mathsf{IntPairClassifier}((\emp,\mathsf{append}(m,a)),
(\emp,\mathsf{append}(n,a)))
\Longleftrightarrow
\mathsf{IntHistoryClassifier}(\Eone(m),\Eone(n)).
$$
The ten-field fit is the following. The traditional target is contextual
signed-unary integer readback. The BEDC source consists of
$\mathsf{IntPairClassifier}$, $\mathsf{IntHistoryClassifier}$,
$\mathsf{Unary}$, $\mathsf{append}$, $\emp$, $\Ezero$, and $\Eone$. The
carrier is canonical difference pairs and public signed histories. The
classifier is pair cross-addition plus the public same-tag classifier.
Stability is one-sided context exactness. The ledger records the hidden common
unary context. The core theorem is this bridge. Exactness is the two displayed
iff rows. The standard bridge is ordinary cancellation of equal signed unary
shifts. The status is $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the zero-tag row, apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:int-pair-classifier-one-sided-append-context-exactness} to
the common positive context $a$ with canonical endpoints
$((m,\emp),(n,\emp))$. The hypothesis $\mathsf{Unary}(a)$ supplies the context
witness for this exactness step. The positive-counter clause strips or
introduces the shared left append context, yielding an equivalence with
$\mathsf{IntPairClassifier}((m,\emp),(n,\emp))$.

Apply Theorem~\ref{thm:int-canonical-difference-pair-history-bridge} to the
positive canonical pair. The assumptions $\mathsf{Unary}(m)$ and
$\mathsf{Unary}(n)$ supply its endpoint hypotheses, so composition of the two
equivalences gives the displayed zero-tag readback.

For the one-tag row, apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:int-pair-classifier-one-sided-append-context-exactness} to
the common negative context $a$ with canonical endpoints
$((\emp,m),(\emp,n))$. The hypothesis $\mathsf{Unary}(a)$ supplies the context
witness for this exactness step. The negative-counter clause strips or
introduces the shared right append context, yielding an equivalence with
$\mathsf{IntPairClassifier}((\emp,m),(\emp,n))$.

Apply Theorem~\ref{thm:int-canonical-difference-pair-history-bridge} to the
negative canonical pair. The same unary endpoint hypotheses identify this
canonical classifier with
$\mathsf{IntHistoryClassifier}(\Eone(m),\Eone(n))$, completing the second
displayed equivalence.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Integer canonical same-sign pair readback]
\label{thm:int-canonical-same-sign-pair-readback}
For all $m,n:\mathsf{BHist}$, the canonical positive difference-pair
classifier reads back exactly to unary payload sameness:
$$
\mathsf{IntPairClassifier}((m,\emp),(n,\emp))
\Longleftrightarrow
\mathsf{Unary}(m)\land\mathsf{Unary}(n)\land\hsame(m,n).
$$
The canonical negative difference-pair classifier has the same public payload
criterion:
$$
\mathsf{IntPairClassifier}((\emp,m),(\emp,n))
\Longleftrightarrow
\mathsf{Unary}(m)\land\mathsf{Unary}(n)\land\hsame(m,n).
$$
The ten-field fit is the following. The traditional target is canonical
positive and negative signed unary integer equality. The BEDC source consists
of $\mathsf{IntPairClassifier}$, $\mathsf{IntPairCarrier}$, $\mathsf{append}$,
$\emp$, and $\hsame$. The carrier is the canonical difference-pair carrier.
The classifier is cross-addition sameness. Stability is supplied by the append
unit laws and symmetry of $\hsame$. The ledger records the hidden empty
counter. The core theorem is this direct readback. Exactness is the two
displayed iff rows. The downstream consumer is the $\RatUp$ numerator
readback. The status is $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.IntUp.IntPairClassifier\_canonical\_same\_sign\_readback\_iff}
\begin{proof}
Unfold $\mathsf{IntPairClassifier}$ at the positive canonical endpoints
$((m,\emp),(n,\emp))$.

For the positive forward direction, the carrier projections give
$\mathsf{Unary}(m)$ and $\mathsf{Unary}(n)$, with the two hidden counters
carried by $\emp$. The classifier payload is
$$
\hsame(\mathsf{append}(m,\emp),\mathsf{append}(n,\emp)).
$$
The right append unit laws reduce this payload to $\hsame(m,n)$.

For the positive reverse direction, assume
$\mathsf{Unary}(m)\land\mathsf{Unary}(n)\land\hsame(m,n)$. Repack the two
canonical carriers from the unary witnesses for $m$ and $n$ together with the
empty unary carrier for $\emp$. The supplied payload $\hsame(m,n)$ transports
through the right append unit laws to the cross-addition payload required by
$\mathsf{IntPairClassifier}$.

Unfold $\mathsf{IntPairClassifier}$ at the negative canonical endpoints
$((\emp,m),(\emp,n))$.

For the negative forward direction, the carrier projections again give
$\mathsf{Unary}(m)$ and $\mathsf{Unary}(n)$. The classifier payload is
$$
\hsame(\mathsf{append}(\emp,n),\mathsf{append}(\emp,m)).
$$
The left append unit laws reduce it to $\hsame(n,m)$, and symmetry of
$\hsame$ orients the conclusion as $\hsame(m,n)$.

For the negative reverse direction, assume
$\mathsf{Unary}(m)\land\mathsf{Unary}(n)\land\hsame(m,n)$. Symmetry first
turns the payload into $\hsame(n,m)$, and the left append unit laws transport
it to
$$
\hsame(\mathsf{append}(\emp,n),\mathsf{append}(\emp,m)).
$$
Together with the two canonical carriers, this is exactly the negative
instance of $\mathsf{IntPairClassifier}$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Integer canonical opposite-sign zero collapse]
\label{thm:int-canonical-opposite-sign-zero-collapse}
For all $m,n:\mathsf{BHist}$, if $\mathsf{Unary}(m)$ and
$\mathsf{Unary}(n)$, then the opposite-sign canonical difference pair is
classified only at zero:
$$
\mathsf{IntPairClassifier}((m,\emp),(\emp,n))
\Longleftrightarrow
\hsame(m,\emp)\land\hsame(n,\emp).
$$
The ten-field fit is the following. The traditional target is opposite-sign
canonical integer equality. The BEDC source consists of
$\mathsf{IntPairClassifier}$, $\mathsf{IntPairCarrier}$, $\mathsf{append}$,
$\emp$, and $\hsame$. The carrier is the unary difference-pair carrier. The
classifier is cross-addition sameness. Stability is supplied by append empty
inversion and history empty readback. The ledger records the hidden zero
counters. The core theorem is this zero-collapse block. Exactness is
canonical opposite-sign collapse. The standard bridge is the ordinary fact
that $m+n=0$ for unary naturals forces both summands to be zero. The status is
$\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Assume first that $m$ and $n$ are unary. Unfold
$\mathsf{IntPairClassifier}$ at $((m,\emp),(\emp,n))$. The carrier clauses
come from the unary hypotheses together with the empty-counter carrier facts.
The classifier payload is
$$
\hsame(\mathsf{append}(m,n),\mathsf{append}(\emp,\emp)).
$$
The append unit law reduces the right endpoint to $\emp$.

By empty readback for $\hsame$, the payload gives
$\mathsf{append}(m,n)=\emp$. Apply append empty inversion to obtain
$m=\emp$ and $n=\emp$. These equalities yield
$\hsame(m,\emp)\land\hsame(n,\emp)$ by reflexivity and transport.

Conversely, assume $\hsame(m,\emp)\land\hsame(n,\emp)$ together with the
unary hypotheses. Empty readback turns the two displayed readbacks into
$m=\emp$ and $n=\emp$. Since $\emp$ is unary, both endpoints
$(m,\emp)$ and $(\emp,n)$ satisfy $\mathsf{IntPairCarrier}$ after transport.

It remains to build the cross-addition payload. Transport along the two empty
equalities and the append unit laws reduces it to $\hsame(\emp,\emp)$, which
is reflexive. Repacking the two carriers and this payload gives
$\mathsf{IntPairClassifier}((m,\emp),(\emp,n))$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Integer zero sign-refined boundary]
\label{thm:int-zero-sign-refined-boundary}
The difference-pair zero classifier and the visible history classifier expose
different interfaces at zero. The pair-zero class is classified:
$$
\mathsf{IntPairClassifier}((\emp,\emp),(\emp,\emp)).
$$
At the same time, the signed-history classifier rejects the two mixed visible
zero displays:
$$
\bigl(\mathsf{IntHistoryClassifier}(\Ezero(\emp),\Eone(\emp))\to\mathsf{False}\bigr)
\land
\bigl(\mathsf{IntHistoryClassifier}(\Eone(\emp),\Ezero(\emp))\to\mathsf{False}\bigr).
$$
Consequently, a classifier-reflecting bridge from difference pairs to signed
histories must either remain inside one visible branch or choose a canonical
zero branch before reflecting the pair-zero class.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The empty history is unary, so the pair $(\emp,\emp)$ satisfies
$\mathsf{IntPairCarrier}(\emp,\emp)$. Apply the reflexivity component of
\autoref{thm:intup-difference-pair-classifier-equivalence-fields} to this
carrier witness. This gives
$\mathsf{IntPairClassifier}((\emp,\emp),(\emp,\emp))$.

For the signed-history clauses, instantiate
\autoref{thm:int-history-classifier-mixed-visible-tag-exclusion} with
$m=\emp$ and $n=\emp$. The two conjuncts are exactly the exclusions for
$\mathsf{IntHistoryClassifier}(\Ezero(\emp),\Eone(\emp))$ and
$\mathsf{IntHistoryClassifier}(\Eone(\emp),\Ezero(\emp))$.

These two facts describe distinct public interfaces. The pair quotient records
the integer zero class, while the history certificate keeps the visible sign
tag in the endpoint shape unless a bridge explicitly selects a same-branch
zero representative.
\end{proof}
