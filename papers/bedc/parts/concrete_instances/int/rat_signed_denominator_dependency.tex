\begin{lemma}[IntUp signed rows feed the RatUp denominator certificate]
\label{lem:int-rat-signed-denominator-dependency}
The $\RatUp$ carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rat-namecert} consumes
the $\IntUp$ signed-pair normalization before any field-style rational row is
licensed. Concretely, a rational representative presents signed numerator
data through the $\IntUp$ carrier and then pairs that row with the positive
unary denominator witness; the denominator certificate is read in the same
finite packet as the signed numerator, not as an independent field quotient.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
The signed-pair interface supplies the visible sign and unary-magnitude rows
for the numerator, together with the local normalization that identifies the
two written zeroes. The $\RatUp$ carrier then reads those signed rows beside a
positive unary denominator row.

Because the rational row is admitted only after both pieces are displayed, the
later field-style rational operations inherit a certified signed
representative and a certified positive denominator. No unlisted quotient of
integer pairs or ambient field equality is introduced by this dependency.
\end{proof}
