\section{Difference-pair context stability}
\label{sec:int-difference-pair-balanced-context-stability}

The difference-pair presentation of $\IntUp$ must remain stable when both
representatives receive the same positive unary context and the same negative
unary context. This is the signed-pair form of ordinary quotient stability for
the relation $(p,n)\sim(q,m)$ given by cross-addition sameness.

\begin{theorem}[Integer pair classifier balanced append context]
\label{thm:int-pair-classifier-balanced-append-context}
For $a,b,p,n,q,m:\mathsf{BHist}$, if $\mathsf{Unary}(a)$,
$\mathsf{Unary}(b)$, and
$\mathsf{IntPairClassifier}((p,n),(q,m))$, then
$$
\mathsf{IntPairClassifier}
((\mathsf{append}(a,p),\mathsf{append}(n,b)),
(\mathsf{append}(a,q),\mathsf{append}(m,b))).
$$
The ten-field fit is: traditional target signed-pair integer quotient
stability under adding the same positive and negative contexts; BEDC source
$\mathsf{IntPairCarrier}$, $\mathsf{IntPairClassifier}$, $\mathsf{Unary}$,
$\mathsf{append}$, and $\hsame$; carrier difference pairs; classifier
cross-addition sameness; stability balanced context extension; ledger the added
positive and negative unary contexts; core theorem this block; exactness
preservation of the quotient classifier; standard bridge ordinary $(p,n)\sim
(q,m)$ stability under $(p+a,n+b)$; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the classifier witness into the two difference-pair carrier witnesses
and the cross-addition comparison
$\mathsf{append}(p,m)\hsame\mathsf{append}(q,n)$. The carrier witnesses give
unary evidence for $p$, $n$, $q$, and $m$.

Unary append closure with the unary contexts $a$ and $b$ supplies all four
endpoint carrier components:
$\mathsf{append}(a,p)$, $\mathsf{append}(n,b)$,
$\mathsf{append}(a,q)$, and $\mathsf{append}(m,b)$.

For the cross-addition component, reassociate the left endpoint by
$\mathsf{append\_assoc}$:
$$
\mathsf{append}(\mathsf{append}(a,p),\mathsf{append}(m,b))
\hsame
\mathsf{append}(a,\mathsf{append}(\mathsf{append}(p,m),b)).
$$

The right endpoint reassociates in the same way:
$$
\mathsf{append}(\mathsf{append}(a,q),\mathsf{append}(n,b))
\hsame
\mathsf{append}(a,\mathsf{append}(\mathsf{append}(q,n),b)).
$$

Transport the original comparison through the common left prefix $a$ and the
common right suffix $b$. Transitivity of $\hsame$ with the two reassociation
steps gives the required cross-addition sameness, and together with the four
unary endpoint components this repacks exactly as the displayed
$\mathsf{IntPairClassifier}$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.IntUp.IntPairClassifier\_balanced\_append\_context}

\begin{theorem}[Integer pair classifier balanced append context reflection]
\label{thm:int-pair-classifier-balanced-append-context-reflection}
For $a,b,p,n,q,m:\mathsf{BHist}$, if $\mathsf{Unary}(a)$,
$\mathsf{Unary}(b)$, and
$$
\mathsf{IntPairClassifier}
((\mathsf{append}(a,p),\mathsf{append}(n,b)),
(\mathsf{append}(a,q),\mathsf{append}(m,b))),
$$
then $\mathsf{IntPairClassifier}((p,n),(q,m))$.
The ten-field fit is: traditional target signed-pair quotient reflection under
adding the same positive and negative contexts; BEDC source
$\mathsf{IntPairCarrier}$, $\mathsf{IntPairClassifier}$, $\mathsf{Unary}$,
$\mathsf{append}$, $\hsame$, append associativity, append cancellation, and
unary append factorization; carrier difference pairs; classifier cross-addition
sameness; stability reflected balanced context cancellation; ledger the
removable positive and negative unary contexts; core theorem this reflection
block; exactness reflection of the quotient classifier; standard bridge
ordinary cancellation of $(p+a,n+b)$ back to $(p,n)$; status
$\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the displayed contextual classifier into its two carried endpoints and
the cross-addition comparison between the contextual pairs. Unary append
factorization applied to those carried endpoints, together with the supplied
unary witnesses for $a$ and $b$, recovers unary evidence for $p$, $n$, $q$,
and $m$.

Reassociate the contextual cross-addition comparison on both sides. The left
endpoint has the same $\hsame$-shape as
$$
\mathsf{append}(a,\mathsf{append}(\mathsf{append}(p,m),b)),
$$
and the right endpoint has the same $\hsame$-shape as
$$
\mathsf{append}(a,\mathsf{append}(\mathsf{append}(q,n),b)).
$$

Transitivity of $\hsame$ with these reassociation witnesses converts the
displayed classifier comparison into a comparison with the common left prefix
$a$ and the common right suffix $b$ exposed. Append-left cancellation removes
the shared prefix and yields a comparison between
$\mathsf{append}(\mathsf{append}(p,m),b)$ and
$\mathsf{append}(\mathsf{append}(q,n),b)$.

Append-right cancellation then removes the shared suffix $b$, giving
$\mathsf{append}(p,m)\hsame\mathsf{append}(q,n)$. Repack this recovered
cross-addition sameness with the recovered carrier witnesses for $(p,n)$ and
$(q,m)$ to obtain $\mathsf{IntPairClassifier}((p,n),(q,m))$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.IntUp.IntPairClassifier\_balanced\_append\_context\_reflection}

\begin{theorem}[Integer pair classifier balanced append context exactness]
\label{thm:int-pair-classifier-balanced-append-context-exactness}
For $a,b,p,n,q,m:\mathsf{BHist}$, if $\mathsf{Unary}(a)$ and
$\mathsf{Unary}(b)$, then
$$
\mathsf{IntPairClassifier}
((\mathsf{append}(a,p),\mathsf{append}(n,b)),
(\mathsf{append}(a,q),\mathsf{append}(m,b)))
\Longleftrightarrow
\mathsf{IntPairClassifier}((p,n),(q,m)).
$$
The ten-field fit is: traditional target balanced signed-pair context
exactness; BEDC source $\mathsf{IntPairCarrier}$,
$\mathsf{IntPairClassifier}$, $\mathsf{Unary}$, $\mathsf{append}$, and
$\hsame$; carrier difference pairs; classifier cross-addition sameness;
stability common positive and negative unary context transport; ledger the
removable positive unary context $a$ and removable negative unary context $b$;
core theorem this exactness block; exactness the displayed iff; standard bridge
ordinary quotient cancellation under simultaneous numerator and denominator
context; downstream consumer one-sided context exactness and $\RatUp$
numerator-denominator context cancellation; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the forward direction, assume the contextual classifier. The hypotheses
$\mathsf{Unary}(a)$ and $\mathsf{Unary}(b)$ put the displayed classifier in the
scope of Theorem~\ref{thm:int-pair-classifier-balanced-append-context-reflection},
which removes the common positive and negative contexts and yields
$\mathsf{IntPairClassifier}((p,n),(q,m))$.

For the reverse direction, assume
$\mathsf{IntPairClassifier}((p,n),(q,m))$. Applying
Theorem~\ref{thm:int-pair-classifier-balanced-append-context} with the same
unary witnesses for $a$ and $b$ transports the classifier through the balanced
append context, giving exactly the contextual classifier in the displayed
statement.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Integer pair classifier one-sided append context exactness]
\label{thm:int-pair-classifier-one-sided-append-context-exactness}
For $a,p,n,q,m:\mathsf{BHist}$, if $\mathsf{Unary}(a)$, then both
one-sided context laws are exact:
\begin{enumerate}
\item positive counters:
$$
\mathsf{IntPairClassifier}((\mathsf{append}(a,p),n),
(\mathsf{append}(a,q),m))
\Longleftrightarrow
\mathsf{IntPairClassifier}((p,n),(q,m));
$$
\item negative counters:
$$
\mathsf{IntPairClassifier}((p,\mathsf{append}(n,a)),
(q,\mathsf{append}(m,a)))
\Longleftrightarrow
\mathsf{IntPairClassifier}((p,n),(q,m)).
$$
\end{enumerate}
The ten-field fit is: traditional target signed-pair integer quotient
one-sided context exactness; BEDC source $\mathsf{IntPairCarrier}$,
$\mathsf{IntPairClassifier}$, $\mathsf{Unary}$, $\mathsf{append}$, and
$\hsame$; carrier difference pairs; classifier cross-addition sameness;
stability positive and negative one-sided unary contexts; ledger the common
unary context $a$ and the empty opposite context; core theorem this pair of
equivalences; exactness preservation and reflection through balanced contexts
plus componentwise transport; standard bridge ordinary cancellation after
adding the same positive or negative integer counter; status
$\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the positive forward implication, assume the contextual classifier on
$((\mathsf{append}(a,p),n),(\mathsf{append}(a,q),m))$. The empty history is
unary, and the continuation unit laws identify $n$ and $m$ with
$\mathsf{append}(n,\emp)$ and $\mathsf{append}(m,\emp)$. The componentwise
transport field of
Theorem~\ref{thm:intup-difference-pair-classifier-equivalence-fields}
therefore puts the witness into the balanced-context shape with positive
context $a$ and negative context $\emp$.

Apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:int-pair-classifier-balanced-append-context-reflection} to
that balanced-context witness. It cancels the common positive context and the
empty negative context, giving
$\mathsf{IntPairClassifier}((p,n),(q,m))$.

For the positive reverse implication, assume
$\mathsf{IntPairClassifier}((p,n),(q,m))$. Apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:int-pair-classifier-append-positive-context} with the unary
context $a$ to obtain the displayed positive-context classifier.

For the negative forward implication, assume the contextual classifier on
$((p,\mathsf{append}(n,a)),(q,\mathsf{append}(m,a)))$. The continuation unit
laws identify $p$ and $q$ with $\mathsf{append}(\emp,p)$ and
$\mathsf{append}(\emp,q)$. Componentwise transport places the witness into
the balanced-context shape with positive context $\emp$ and negative context
$a$.

Apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:int-pair-classifier-balanced-append-context-reflection} to
cancel those balanced contexts. The result is exactly
$\mathsf{IntPairClassifier}((p,n),(q,m))$.

For the negative reverse implication, start from
$\mathsf{IntPairClassifier}((p,n),(q,m))$. Apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:int-pair-classifier-balanced-append-context} with the empty
positive context and the unary negative context $a$, then use the continuation
unit laws and componentwise transport to replace
$\mathsf{append}(\emp,p)$ and $\mathsf{append}(\emp,q)$ by $p$ and $q$.
\end{proof}
