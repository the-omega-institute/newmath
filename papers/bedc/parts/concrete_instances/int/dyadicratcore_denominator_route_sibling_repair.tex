\begin{theorem}[IntUp DyadicRatCore denominator route sibling repair]
\label{thm:int-dyadicratcore-denominator-route-sibling-repair}
The $\IntUp$ signed-pair and denominator rows form the sibling input consumed by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert} on the Real-and-Completion route. A $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ consumer first reads the $\IntUp$ signed-pair carrier through the $\RatUp$ denominator normalization surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rat-namecert}, then uses the dyadic mantissa support of \autoref{thm:int-dyadic-mantissa-real-route} before any $\RealUp$ seal boundary is available.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the signed-pair denominator route already displayed for $\RatUp$. The integer carrier supplies a sign row and unary magnitude row, and the rational denominator surface reads that balanced signed row beside its positive denominator witness.

Next use the dyadic mantissa route. It sends the same signed coordinate through the rational and dyadic core surfaces before the Real-and-Completion chain may read a seal boundary.

Thus the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ chapter consumes a sibling input from $\IntUp$ rather than a detached host integer or field equality. The available route is exactly the signed-pair row, the denominator normalization row, and the dyadic mantissa support.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[IntUp L10 DyadicRatCore denominator handoff]
\label{thm:int-dyadicratcore-l10-denominator-handoff}
Every L10 Real-and-Completion read of a $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ mantissa/denominator packet factors through the displayed $\IntUp$ signed-pair row, the $\RatUp$ denominator normalization surface, and the dyadic mantissa route before $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, or $\RealUp$ may consume the packet. The handoff is finite-row data only: it exposes the signed-pair row, denominator normalization row, and mantissa route named by \autoref{thm:int-dyadicratcore-denominator-route-sibling-repair}, not host integers, quotient rationals, selected limits, or $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the sibling route of \autoref{thm:int-dyadicratcore-denominator-route-sibling-repair}. Its source is the displayed $\IntUp$ signed-pair row, and its first consumer is the $\RatUp$ denominator normalization surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rat-namecert}.

Pass next through the dyadic face of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}. The mantissa coordinate is still read as finite signed-pair data, and the denominator coordinate is still the normalized rational denominator row. This is the only input the L10 Real-and-Completion route supplies to the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ packet.

Only after that packet is visible may the downstream consumers read it: $\StreamNameUp$ through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, $\RegSeqRatUp$ through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and $\RealUp$ through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. None of these reads adds a host integer, quotient rational, selected limit, or completeness witness; they consume the same finite handoff row.
\end{proof}
