\begin{theorem}[IntUp DyadicRatCore denominator route sibling repair]
\label{thm:int-dyadicratcore-denominator-route-sibling-repair}
The $\IntUp$ signed-pair and denominator rows form the sibling input consumed by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert} on the Real-and-Completion route. A $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ consumer first reads the $\IntUp$ signed-pair carrier through the $\RatUp$ denominator normalization surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rat-namecert}, then uses the dyadic mantissa support of \autoref{thm:int-dyadic-mantissa-real-route} before any $\RealUp$ seal boundary is available.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the signed-pair denominator route already displayed for $\RatUp$. The integer carrier supplies a sign row and unary magnitude row, and the rational denominator surface reads that balanced signed row beside its positive denominator witness.

Next use the dyadic mantissa route. It sends the same signed coordinate through the rational and dyadic core surfaces before the Real-and-Completion chain may read a seal boundary.

Thus the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ chapter consumes a sibling input from $\IntUp$ rather than a detached host integer or field equality. The available route is exactly the signed-pair row, the denominator normalization row, and the dyadic mantissa support.
\end{proof}
