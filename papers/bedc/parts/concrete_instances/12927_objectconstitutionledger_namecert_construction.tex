The $\ObjectConstitutionLedgerUp$ packet realizes the object-constitution claim of \autoref{thm:philosophy-object-constitution-licensed-pattern} as finite BEDC data. It sits after the focused history ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-focusedledger-namecert}, the observer-history identity surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observerhistoryidentity-namecert}, the public inscription event surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-inscriptionevent-namecert}, the concept registry surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-conceptregistrysurface-namecert}, and the scientific-object packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-scientificobject-namecert}. The chapter does not add a hidden object substrate to \autoref{ch:visions-philosophy}; it records the appearance of an object as focused history, continuation, classifier, ledger, gap, provenance, and local naming rows.

\begin{definition}[Object-constitution ledger carrier]
\label{def:object-constitution-ledger-carrier}
An $\ObjectConstitutionLedgerUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
O=(F,I,E,R,S,A,K,L,G,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $F$ is the $\FocusedLedgerUp$ row exposing the focused records-side history in which the object pattern is read.
\item $I$ is the $\ObserverHistoryIdentityUp$ row naming the observer-history identity surface for the same focused read.
\item $E$ is the $\InscriptionEventUp$ row recording the public event by which the constituted object is available as record data.
\item $R$ is the $\ConceptRegistrySurfaceUp$ row naming the concept entry or registry route under which the object is classified.
\item $S$ is the $\ScientificObjectUp$ row when the constituted object is used by a scientific-object consumer.
\item $A$ is the stable-appearance row recording the focused pattern exposed by $F,I,E,R$ before any object name is accepted.
\item $K$ is the classifier row that decides which focused appearances count as the same object-facing pattern.
\item $L$ is the constitution ledger row recording continuation, classifier stability, registry readback, and inscription provenance.
\item $G$ is the gap row listing unclosed appearance, classifier, registry, empirical, and scientific-object commitments.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $F,I,E,R,S,A,K,L,G$ and the local certificate row.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from focused history through observer identity, inscription, registry, scientific-object read, stable appearance, classifier, ledger, gap, provenance, and local name.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ObjectConstitutionLedgerUp$, $\FocusedLedgerUp$, $\ObserverHistoryIdentityUp$, $\InscriptionEventUp$, $\ConceptRegistrySurfaceUp$, $\ScientificObjectUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ObjectConstitutionLedgerUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a bare object behind appearances, a private subject-state object, a host-side identity predicate, an unregistered concept, or an unledgered scientific object.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Object-constitution ledger NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:object-constitution-ledger-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:object-constitution-ledger-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\ObjectConstitutionLedgerUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $O$, focused-ledger exposure through $F$, observer-history identity exposure through $I$, inscription-event exposure through $E$, concept-registry exposure through $R$, scientific-object exposure through $S$, stable-appearance exposure through $A$, classifier exposure through $K$, constitution-ledger exposure through $L$, gap exposure through $G$, componentwise transport through $H$, displayed replay through $C$, provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from the displayed packet. The rows expose the focused records-side history, observer-history identity, public inscription event, registry route, scientific-object route, stable appearance, classifier, ledger, gap, transport, replay, provenance, and local name.

The appearance rows are read first. The focused ledger $F$ supplies the records-side focus, $I$ binds the observer-history identity of that focus, $E$ makes the read public as an inscription event, and $R$ supplies the concept-registry classifier surface.

The object-facing rows are then forced. The stable-appearance row $A$ and classifier row $K$ must be present before the constitution ledger $L$ can name the pattern, and the gap row $G$ remains visible before $N$ is readable. If a scientific-object consumer is present, it reaches the object through $S$ and the same $H,C,P,N$ rows rather than through a separate object substrate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Object-constitution ledger non-escape]
\label{thm:object-constitution-ledger-non-escape}
Every accepted $\ObjectConstitutionLedgerUp$ object read factors through
$$
F,I,E,R,S,A,K,L,G,H,C,P,N.
$$
A consumer that omits the focused ledger, observer-history identity, inscription event, concept-registry surface, stable appearance, classifier, constitution ledger, gap row, provenance, or local NameCert row cannot export the constituted object as a BEDC object name.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier inventory of \autoref{def:object-constitution-ledger-carrier}. The packet contains exactly the displayed rows $F,I,E,R,S,A,K,L,G,H,C,P,N$ and no row for bare object existence, hidden subject state, host identity, or unregistered concept membership.

Apply \autoref{thm:object-constitution-ledger-namecert-obligations}. The local certificate makes each appearance, classifier, ledger, gap, transport, replay, provenance, and name row mandatory for an accepted object-constitution read.

The sibling packets supply only their respective surfaces. $\FocusedLedgerUp$ does not by itself name an object, $\ObserverHistoryIdentityUp$ does not by itself certify a concept, $\InscriptionEventUp$ does not by itself stabilize an appearance, $\ConceptRegistrySurfaceUp$ does not by itself provide empirical object use, and $\ScientificObjectUp$ does not erase the focused-appearance ledger. Therefore a consumer that drops any mandatory row reads a projection of the packet, not the constituted object name.
\end{proof}
