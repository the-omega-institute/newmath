The $\ObserverLocalityCellUp$ interface packages the finite two-observer cell described in \autoref{def:consciousness-universe-locality-cell}. It is a local naming surface: two observer-history rows, two event-description rows, paired $\Gap$ acknowledgements, componentwise transport, continuation routes, provenance, and a local certificate are visible, while global spacetime, a shared clock, a manifold coordinate chart, and an all-observer frame are not part of the carrier.

\begin{definition}[ObserverLocalityCell carrier]
\label{def:observer-locality-cell-carrier}
An $\ObserverLocalityCellUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(O_0,O_1,R_0,R_1,G_0,G_1,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $O_0$ and $O_1$ are the two observer-history rows being compared;
\item $R_0$ and $R_1$ are the two local event-description rows recorded inside those histories;
\item $G_0$ and $G_1$ are paired $\Gap$ acknowledgement rows, each committing its own observer side to the coherence of $R_0$ and $R_1$ as descriptions of one event;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the observer histories, event rows, acknowledgement rows, continuation routes, package row, and local name row;
\item $C$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which a consumer reads the two observer histories, the two event rows, and the two acknowledgement rows;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ObserverHistoryIdentityUp$, $\CrossHistCausalUp$, $\FiniteMultiHistPacketUp$, $\Hist$, $\BHist$, $\Gap$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ObserverLocalityCellUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only after transporting these displayed coordinates to a common two-history surface. The carrier has no coordinate for an external event object, global observer frame, synchronized clock, metric geometry, manifold point, or instantaneous remote record.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[ObserverLocalityCell NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:observer-locality-cell-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:observer-locality-cell-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\ObserverLocalityCellUp}$ surface consists of five finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed packet $L=(O_0,O_1,R_0,R_1,G_0,G_1,H,C,P,N)$;
\item classifier transport to a common observer-history and event-row surface;
\item paired $\Gap$ acknowledgement exactness, requiring both $G_0$ and $G_1$ to name the same transported pair of event rows;
\item stability under the componentwise $\hsame$ row $H$ and displayed $\Cont$ routes in $C$;
\item non-escape for global frames, synchronized clocks, manifold coordinates, ambient metric geometry, and instantaneous remote records.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:observer-locality-cell-carrier}. The rows $O_0,O_1,R_0,R_1,G_0,G_1,H,C,P,N$ display the two observer histories, the two event records, the paired acknowledgements, transport, routes, provenance, and local name.

Classifier transport uses $H$ to move every displayed coordinate to a common two-history surface. Once transported, the only consumer reads are the routes recorded in $C$.

The acknowledgement exactness row reads $G_0$ and $G_1$ together. Each side commits to the same transported pair $(R_0,R_1)$, so the named cell is the paired commitment surface rather than an external event object.

The non-escape clause follows from the carrier inventory. No row supplies a global frame, synchronized clock, manifold coordinate, ambient metric, or instantaneous remote record.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ObserverLocalityCell GAP coherence]
\label{thm:observer-locality-cell-gap-coherence}
Let $L=(O_0,O_1,R_0,R_1,G_0,G_1,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\ObserverLocalityCellUp$ carrier. Every coherence read of the cell factors through both acknowledgement rows $G_0$ and $G_1$ after transporting $O_0,O_1,R_0,R_1$ by $H$ and replaying the displayed routes in $C$. A one-sided acknowledgement is not a locality cell.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet. The only rows that assert cross-observer coherence are $G_0$ and $G_1$; the observer histories and event-description rows merely supply the local records being compared.

Transport the two histories and the two event rows through $H$. The routes in $C$ then read the transported event rows before reading the two acknowledgement rows.

Because the carrier requires both $G_0$ and $G_1$, a coherence read that omits one side cannot satisfy the displayed packet. Thus the cell is exactly the paired $\Gap$ commitment over the transported event rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}
Start with a consumer route into the accepted packet. The carrier projection exposes the two histories, two event rows, and paired acknowledgement rows as the only observational data.

For an $\ObserverHistoryIdentityUp$ consumer, the relevant read is the identity-preserving history surface $O_0,O_1$ together with the transported event rows. The remaining data are $H,C,P,N$. They transport, route, record provenance, and name the local cell; they do not supply a clock, global event, metric cone, chart, or synchronization map.
\end{proof}
