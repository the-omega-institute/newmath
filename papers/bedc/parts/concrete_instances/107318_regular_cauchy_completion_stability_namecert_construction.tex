\begin{definition}[Regular Cauchy completion stability carrier]
\label{def:regular-cauchy-completion-stability-carrier}
A $\RegularCauchyCompletionStabilityUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(Q,W,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ source row, carrying the regular rational Cauchy name and its finite tolerance schedule.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window row that selects the finite source observations inspected by the completion route.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, recording the dyadic radii and comparison rows used to certify regularity on the selected windows.
\item $R$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached from the regular sequence, stream windows, and dyadic tolerance ledger.
\item $E$ is the $\CauchyCompletenessWitnessUp$ row, exposing only the displayed completion-witness surface attached to $Q,W,D,R$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the regular sequence, stream windows, dyadic ledger, real seal, completion witness, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from $Q$ through $W,D,R,E$ and back to the local naming row.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RegularCauchyCompletionStabilityUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\CauchyCompletenessWitnessUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyCompletionStabilityUp}$ row.

The classifier compares accepted packets only through $Q,W,D,R,E,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for quotient stream equality, a chosen limit representative, ambient completeness of $\RealUp$, countable choice, trichotomy, or a second completion route.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy completion stability NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-completion-stability-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RegularCauchyCompletionStabilityUp$ carrier $S=(Q,W,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyCompletionStabilityUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the displayed rows; regular-source exposure through $Q$; finite window selection through $W$; dyadic tolerance accounting through $D$; real-seal confinement through $R$; completion-witness attachment through $E$; componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$; displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$; provenance through $P$; and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-completion-stability-carrier}. The accepted packet displays exactly the regular rational source, the finite window schedule, the dyadic tolerance ledger, the terminal real seal, and the completion-witness row.

The stability route is read in source order. The row $Q$ supplies the regular Cauchy name, $W$ selects the finite observations that a consumer may inspect, and $D$ records the dyadic tolerance comparisons visible on those observations.

The rows $R$ and $E$ then bind the completion-facing read. The real seal $R$ is reached only after $Q,W,D$ have been displayed, and the completion witness $E$ attaches to that same route rather than to an external completeness principle.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the route. Since no coordinate stores quotient stream equality, a chosen limit representative, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, countable choice, trichotomy, or a second completion route, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy completion idempotence boundary]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-completion-stability-idempotence-boundary}

$$
\RegSeqRatUp \longmapsto \StreamNameUp \longmapsto \DyadicRatCoreUp \longmapsto \RealUp \longmapsto \CauchyCompletenessWitnessUp .

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

The completion read reaches $R$ and $E$ only along this route. Thus the real seal and the completion witness are already attached to the finite regular-source path before any consumer receives the packet.
