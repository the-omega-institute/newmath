\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\WeakLawLargeNumbersUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-weak-law-large-numbers-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Kolmogorov and Bishop--Bridges on finite averages and probability estimates, the $\WeakLawLargeNumbersUp$ packet names the displayed finite-average route behind the weak law of large numbers. It sits after the normalized probability surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-probspace-namecert}, the measurable random-variable rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-randomvar-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet is a finite certificate for Chebyshev-controlled averages; it is not an almost-sure convergence theorem, a central limit theorem, a countable-choice subsequence selector, a host probability-limit principle, or an ambient $\RealUp$ completeness result.

\begin{definition}[Weak law finite-average carrier]\label{def:weak-law-large-numbers-carrier}
A $\WeakLawLargeNumbersUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
(P,R,A,V,T,E,H,C,Q,N).
$$
Here $P$ is the source $\ProbSpaceUp$ row carrying the total-event normalization and event classifier. The row $R$ is the finite family of $\RandomVarUp$ entries read over that same probability source. The row $A$ records the displayed finite average of the entries in $R$, while $V$ records the variance or uniform second-moment bound used by the estimate. The row $T$ is the Chebyshev tail row for the event where the finite average differs from its displayed center by more than the requested tolerance. The row $E$ is the $\RealUp$ epsilon seal naming the positive tolerance and the final probability bound. The row $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport, $C$ records $\Cont$ replay of the finite averaging route, $Q$ records $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ProbSpaceUp$, $\RandomVarUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$, and $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\WeakLawLargeNumbersUp}$ row. The classifier compares accepted carriers only through these ten rows.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Weak law NameCert obligations]\label{thm:weak-law-large-numbers-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\WeakLawLargeNumbersUp$ carrier $(P,R,A,V,T,E,H,C,Q,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\WeakLawLargeNumbersUp}$ surface consists exactly of the finite rows in \autoref{def:weak-law-large-numbers-carrier}. Carrier admission displays the probability source, the random-variable family, the finite-average row, the variance-bound row, the Chebyshev tail row, the epsilon seal, the transport and replay rows, the provenance row, and the local naming row. The packet imports no host law of large numbers, selected subsequence, external probability-limit operation, countable-choice principle, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness witness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the carrier rows in order. The probability source $P$ supplies the measurable-event classifier and total-mass normalization, and the random-variable row $R$ exposes only the finitely displayed variables over that source.

The average row $A$ and variance row $V$ are then internal finite data. They record the algebraic averaging expression and the bound used by the tail estimate before any limiting statement is visible.

Finally read $T,E,H,C,Q,N$. The Chebyshev tail row and epsilon seal name the probability estimate at the requested tolerance, while transport, replay, provenance, and local naming keep the same finite route under classifier replacement. Since no carrier coordinate stores an infinite limit or subsequence selector, the listed rows exhaust the local certificate surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Weak law Chebyshev handoff]\label{thm:weak-law-large-numbers-chebyshev-handoff}
Every accepted $\WeakLawLargeNumbersUp$ Chebyshev read factors through the route
$$
P\longmapsto R\longmapsto A\longmapsto V\longmapsto T\longmapsto E\longmapsto H,C,Q,N
$$
before any probability-bound consumer receives the finite-average estimate. Thus the Chebyshev step is handed to later probability and $\RealUp$ formalization as a displayed finite source, finite random-variable row, average row, variance-bound row, tail-event row, epsilon seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming data.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a probability-bound request. The only probability source exposed by the packet is $P$, and the only random-variable data exposed over it are the finite rows in $R$.

The request must next read the average and variance-bound rows. The average row $A$ fixes the finite expression being estimated, and $V$ fixes the bound that the Chebyshev tail row may consume.

Only after these rows are displayed can $T$ and $E$ be read as a bound on the exceptional event. The structural rows $H,C,Q,N$ then preserve that same route for downstream consumers, so the handoff is the finite Chebyshev estimate itself rather than a host-side convergence theorem.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Weak law finite-average nonescape]\label{thm:weak-law-large-numbers-finite-average-nonescape}
Every accepted $\WeakLawLargeNumbersUp$ probability-limit-facing read remains inside the finite-average route of \autoref{def:weak-law-large-numbers-carrier}. In particular, a consumer can read a tail estimate only after displaying the source probability row, finite random-variable rows, average row, variance-bound row, Chebyshev tail row, $\RealUp$ epsilon seal, componentwise transport, replay, provenance, and local $\NameCert$ row. No accepted read may replace this route by almost-sure convergence, central limit normalization, countable-choice subsequence selection, a host probability-limit operator, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Let a consumer request the finite-average estimate from an accepted carrier. Projection of \autoref{def:weak-law-large-numbers-carrier} shows that the request first meets $P,R,A,V,T,E$ and has no independent coordinate for a limit object.

The displayed Chebyshev row $T$ is downstream from the variance-bound row $V$ and upstream from the epsilon seal $E$. Hence any probability bound read is already tied to the visible finite average and its tolerance.

The remaining rows $H,C,Q,N$ do not add new analytic content. They transport equal displayed histories, replay the finite route, record the package provenance, and attach the local name. Therefore every accepted probability-limit-facing read stays inside the finite-average certificate surface and cannot escape to the excluded convergence or completeness principles.
\end{proof}
