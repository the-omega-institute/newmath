\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\GaugeIntegralUp$}

Following Henstock, Kurzweil, and Bishop--Bridges constructive analysis, the $\GaugeIntegralUp$ packet names gauge-partition integration by finite tagged subdivisions, visible sum rows, Darboux gap comparison, regulated-integral compatibility, and Real/Completion handoff data. It consumes the gauge-subdivision surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cousin-lemma-namecert}, the tagged-mesh sum boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-riemannintegral-namecert}, the upper-lower gap route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-darbouxintegral-namecert}, the finite step primitive route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regulatedintegral-namecert}, and the Real/Completion seal rows used throughout the L10 substrate. The packet is a finite naming surface for one displayed interval and integrand window; it is not general open-cover compactness, a selected family of partitions, a quotient of functions, measure completion, or ambient real completeness.

\begin{definition}[Gauge integral carrier]
\label{def:gauge-integral-carrier}
A $\GaugeIntegralUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
G=(I,\Gamma,T,S,D,Q,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows.
\begin{enumerate}
\item $I$ is the interval row, naming the endpoints, endpoint order, and the cell boundary data exposed to every downstream partition and sum read.
\item $\Gamma$ is the positive gauge row. Each displayed tag request carries a local positive radius and the finite observation window by which that radius is read.
\item $T$ is the Cousin tagged-subdivision row, routed through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cousin-lemma-namecert} from $I$ and $\Gamma$.
\item $S$ is the Riemann tagged-sum row, routed through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-riemannintegral-namecert} from the same tags, cells, integrand reads, and mesh data displayed by $T$.
\item $D$ is the Darboux gap row, routed through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-darbouxintegral-namecert} by comparing upper and lower step bounds against the same interval and integrand windows.
\item $Q$ is the regulated-integral compatibility row, routed through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regulatedintegral-namecert} whenever the integrand window is carried by a displayed regulated-function approximation ledger.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, and $\CompletionUp$ handoff row reached only after the gauge, Cousin, Riemann, Darboux, and regulated-compatibility rows have been recorded.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the interval, gauge, tagged subdivision, tagged sum, Darboux gap, regulated compatibility, Real/Completion handoff, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a gauge-integral request through
$$
I,\Gamma \longmapsto T \longmapsto S \longmapsto D \longmapsto Q \longmapsto R \longmapsto H,P,N .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\GaugeIntegralUp$, $\CousinLemmaUp$, $\RiemannIntegralUp$, $\DarbouxIntegralUp$, $\RegulatedIntegralUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\CompletionUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\GaugeIntegralUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $I,\Gamma,T,S,D,Q,R,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for open-cover compactness, choice-selected partitions, quotient functions, measure completion, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Gauge integral NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:gauge-integral-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\GaugeIntegralUp$ carrier
$$
G=(I,\Gamma,T,S,D,Q,R,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\GaugeIntegralUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following obligation rows.
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed interval, positive gauge, Cousin subdivision, Riemann tagged sum, Darboux gap, regulated compatibility, Real/Completion handoff, componentwise transport, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item gauge locality, requiring every tag row in $T$ to read its radius from $\Gamma$ and to certify that the tagged cell lies inside the corresponding local gauge window;
\item tagged-subdivision exactness through $\CousinLemmaUp$, requiring $T$ to be the finite output of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cousin-lemma-namecert} over the same $I$ and $\Gamma$ rows;
\item Darboux/Riemann compatibility, requiring the tagged-sum row $S$ and the gap row $D$ to inspect the same cells, endpoint comparisons, integrand reads, and step-bound data;
\item regulated-integral compatibility, requiring $Q$ to consume only the finite step approximation and error ledger exposed by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regulatedintegral-namecert};
\item Real/Completion non-escape, requiring $R$ to expose $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, and $\CompletionUp$ data only after $T,S,D,Q$ are present;
\item structural faithfulness, requiring $H,C,P,N$ to transport, replay, source, and name no open-cover compactness theorem, choice-selected partition family, quotient of functions, measure completion, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness principle.
\end{enumerate}
No local obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:gauge-integral-carrier}. The accepted packet displays the interval, gauge, subdivision, sum, gap, compatibility, handoff, transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows.

Gauge locality and subdivision exactness are read from $\Gamma$ and $T$. The $\CousinLemmaUp$ route supplies the finite tagged partition over the displayed interval and positive gauge, so no selected partition family or general compactness theorem is available to the classifier.

The Riemann and Darboux rows are then compared as finite ledger coordinates. The tagged-sum row $S$ and gap row $D$ share the interval cells, endpoint comparisons, integrand reads, and step-bound data, so their compatibility is a row equality inside the packet rather than a host theorem about all refinements.

The regulated row $Q$ can be consumed only after the regulated-integral approximation ledger is displayed. The Real/Completion row $R$ is downstream of $T,S,D,Q$, and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$ replay and name exactly that route. Since the excluded principles have no carrier coordinate, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Gauge integral Cousin--Riemann handoff]
\label{thm:gauge-integral-cousin-riemann-handoff}
Let $G=(I,\Gamma,T,S,D,Q,R,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\GaugeIntegralUp$ carrier. Every consumer read that reaches the $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, or $\CompletionUp$ handoff row $R$ first routes the gauge partition through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cousin-lemma-namecert}, then the tagged-sum row through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-riemannintegral-namecert}, then the gap comparison through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-darbouxintegral-namecert}, and then the step-compatible route through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regulatedintegral-namecert}. The handoff order is
$$
I,\Gamma \longmapsto T \longmapsto S \longmapsto D \longmapsto Q \longmapsto R .
$$
No accepted packet exposes a $\RealUp$ or $\CompletionUp$ read before this finite route is present.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.GaugeIntegralUp.GaugeIntegralCousinRiemannHandoff}
\begin{proof}
Start with a consumer read of $R$. By \autoref{def:gauge-integral-carrier}, the row $R$ is not an independent coordinate: it is specified as the handoff row reached after the gauge, Cousin, Riemann, Darboux, and regulated-compatibility rows have been recorded.

The first non-structural rows are $I$ and $\Gamma$. They display the interval and positive gauge. The row $T$ is then the Cousin tagged-subdivision output over those same rows, so every later sum sees a finite tagged partition already certified by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cousin-lemma-namecert}.

The row $S$ reads this tagged subdivision through the Riemann tagged-sum surface. The row $D$ compares the same cells and integrand windows through the Darboux gap boundary, and $Q$ reads the regulated step approximation route when such a regulated ledger is present.

Only after these rows are present can the continuation row replay a handoff to $R$. Componentwise $\hsame$ transport and $\Pkg$ provenance preserve the same order, and the local $\NameCert$ row names no independent Real or Completion source. Hence every accepted Real/Completion read factors through the displayed Cousin--Riemann--Darboux--regulated route.
\end{proof}
