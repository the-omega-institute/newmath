\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\InscriptionEventBufferSealUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-inscriptioneventbufferseal-namecert}
\origin{ai}

The $\InscriptionEventBufferSealUp$ packet realizes a replayable finite window
of inscription events. It sits beside the event carrier of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-inscriptionevent-namecert}, the gap ledger of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-inscription-gap-ledger-namecert}, the
inscription point of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-inscriptionpoint-namecert},
the audit trace of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-inscriptionaudittrace-namecert},
and the digest inscription packet of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-digestinscription-namecert}. Those siblings
provide single-event admission, gap accounting, pointwise commitment, audit
trace routing, and digest naming; the buffer seal binds a finite replay window
without exporting an unbounded inscription stream.

\begin{definition}[Inscription event buffer seal carrier]
\label{def:inscription-event-buffer-seal-carrier}
An $\InscriptionEventBufferSealUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
B=(E,W,R,D,A,H,Q,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $E$ is the finite inscription-event row, listing the accepted event
records admitted into the buffer;
\item $W$ is the finite buffer-window row, naming the bounded replay interval
and its visible endpoints;
\item $R$ is the replay row, recording the ordered finite readback route
through the events in $W$;
\item $D$ is the digest row, binding the replayed window to its digest
inscription and digest provenance;
\item $A$ is the audit-route row, recording accepted checks, gap residues, and
consumer-facing route obligations for the replay;
\item $H$ is the componentwise $\hsame$ transport row for event, window,
replay, digest, and audit-route presentations;
\item $Q$ is the displayed $\Cont$ row carrying replay consumers through the
same finite window;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over the event, window, replay, digest,
audit, transport, continuation, and local naming rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\InscriptionEventBufferSealUp}$ row naming
exactly this replay buffer seal.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for an unbounded inscription stream, a
host-level replay oracle, or replay data outside the finite event window.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Inscription event buffer seal NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:inscription-event-buffer-seal-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:inscription-event-buffer-seal-carrier}, the
local $\NameCert_{\InscriptionEventBufferSealUp}$ surface consists exactly of
carrier admission for $B=(E,W,R,D,A,H,Q,P,N)$, finite window exactness, replay
row determinacy, digest attachment, audit-route coverage, componentwise
$\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local
naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from
\autoref{def:inscription-event-buffer-seal-carrier}. The accepted packet
contains exactly the rows $E,W,R,D,A,H,Q,P,N$.

Window exactness comes from $W$ and replay determinacy from $R$. The event row
$E$ supplies only the finite event records admitted into that window, and the
replay row orders only those records.

Digest attachment and audit-route coverage are supplied by $D$ and $A$. The
digest row binds the finite replay to the digest inscription surface, while
the audit-route row records accepted checks, gap residues, and consumer-facing
obligations.

Finally $H,Q,P,N$ transport, continue, package, and name the same finite buffer
seal. Since these rows add no unbounded stream or host replay oracle, the
listed obligations exhaust the local certificate surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Inscription event buffer seal non-escape]
\label{thm:inscription-event-buffer-seal-nonescape}
No accepted $\InscriptionEventBufferSealUp$ packet exports an unbounded
inscription stream, a host-level replay oracle, or a replay route outside the
displayed rows $E,W,R,D,A,H,Q,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier of
\autoref{def:inscription-event-buffer-seal-carrier}. Its only event-facing row
is $E$, its only window row is $W$, and its only replay row is $R$.

Apply the NameCert obligations of
\autoref{thm:inscription-event-buffer-seal-namecert-obligations}. Every
accepted replay must attach the digest row $D$, audit-route row $A$, transport
row $H$, continuation row $Q$, provenance row $P$, and local name $N$ to that
same finite window.

Thus an unbounded inscription stream, host-level replay oracle, or hidden
replay route has no coordinate in the accepted packet. Such a demand can only
be rejected at the audit-route boundary, not exported by the buffer seal.
\end{proof}
