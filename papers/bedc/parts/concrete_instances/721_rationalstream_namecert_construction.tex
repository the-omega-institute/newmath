\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RationalStreamUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-rationalstream-namecert}

The $\RationalStreamUp$ interface names finite packets for pointwise rational
stream observations on the path from $\RatUp$ and $\StreamNameUp$ to
$\RegSeqRatUp$ and the $\RealUp$ seal. Following Bishop-style constructive
analysis of rational Cauchy streams, the packet keeps the observation
schedule, rational point rows, classifier rows, and provenance visible without
forming quotient Cauchy classes or selecting a completed real limit.

\begin{definition}[RationalStream carrier]
\label{def:rational-stream-carrier}
A $\RationalStreamUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
R=(u,s,q,\rho,h,c,p,n)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $u$ is the $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ index row naming the certified
finite observation positions available to the packet;
\item $s$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule row selecting which rational
observation is read at each displayed unary index;
\item $q$ is the family of pointwise $\RatUp$ carrier rows read along the
schedule $s$;
\item $\rho$ is the family of pointwise $\RatUp$ classifier rows comparing
scheduled rational observations at the same displayed indices;
\item $h$ records the componentwise $\hsame$ transport rows for indices,
schedules, rational carriers, and rational classifiers;
\item $c$ records the $\Cont$ routes through which finite index windows and
their scheduled rational reads are exposed to consumers;
\item $p$ is the visible $\Pkg$ provenance row naming the $\RatUp$,
$\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ dependency certificates used
by the packet;
\item $n$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RationalStreamUp}$ registration row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate is
$\mathsf{RationalStreamPacket}(u,s,q,\rho,h,c,p,n)$. Its classifier compares
two accepted packets only by transporting the displayed schedule and applying
the pointwise $\RatUp$ classifier rows on the certified unary indices.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[RationalStream pointwise RatUp obligations]
\label{thm:rational-stream-pointwise-rat-obligations}
Every accepted $\RationalStreamUp$ packet exposes its rational observations
only through the pointwise $\RatUp$ carrier family $q$ and classifier family
$\rho$ indexed by the displayed $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ row $u$. Reflexive,
symmetric, transitive, and transported comparisons of rational observations
are therefore obligations over the stored $\RatUp$ rows, not over a quotient
or host rational stream object.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:rational-stream-carrier}. The only
coordinates that contain rational point data are $q$ and $\rho$, and both are
indexed through the visible unary row $u$ and schedule row $s$.

The transport coordinate $h$ moves the displayed point rows by $\hsame$, while
$c$ records the continuation routes used to read finite windows. Repacking
these projections gives the four pointwise comparison obligations entirely on
the stored $\RatUp$ rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RationalStream StreamName schedule boundary]
\label{thm:rational-stream-streamname-schedule-boundary}
The $\StreamNameUp$ schedule boundary of an accepted $\RationalStreamUp$
packet is exactly the row $s$ together with the unary index row $u$, the
continuation routes $c$, and the transported schedule witnesses in $h$. A
consumer may ask which finite rational observation is read at a displayed
index, but the packet supplies no unbounded stream object outside those
scheduled finite reads.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the schedule coordinate $s$ from the carrier. Its domain of visible reads
is the unary index row $u$, and each consumer route is one of the continuation
rows stored in $c$.

If the same schedule is transported, the corresponding witness is one of the
$\hsame$ rows in $h$. No other coordinate stores a stream selector, so the
schedule boundary is exhausted by these rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RationalStream RegSeqRat handoff]
\label{thm:rational-stream-regseqrat-handoff}
A $\RegSeqRatUp$ consumer of an accepted $\RationalStreamUp$ packet may read
only the finite stream-name schedule $s$, unary index row $u$, pointwise
$\RatUp$ carrier and classifier rows $q$ and $\rho$, and the displayed
$\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ provenance. The handoff records a
regularity request surface for rational finite windows; it does not assert
Cauchy completeness, selected limits, or equality of completed real values.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the accepted packet and project the coordinates read by a regular
sequence consumer. The schedule $s$ chooses the finite observations, $u$
names their indices, and the rows $q$ and $\rho$ provide the rational carrier
and classifier data at those indices.

The provenance coordinates $h$, $c$, $p$, and $n$ record transport,
continuation, dependency, and registration information. Repacking exactly
these rows gives the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff surface, with no coordinate left
for completion or a selected limit.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RationalStream NameCert seed obligations]
\label{thm:rational-stream-namecert-seed-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\RationalStreamUp}$ seed surface consists of five rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier: the finite packet of
\autoref{def:rational-stream-carrier};
\item classifier: pointwise $\RatUp$ comparison over the scheduled unary
indices;
\item stability: $\hsame$ transport of the unary index row, schedule row,
point carriers, and point classifiers;
\item ledger: the finite windows, continuation routes, package provenance,
and local naming registration visible in $c$, $p$, and $n$;
\item $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff: the regular rational-stream request surface of
\autoref{thm:rational-stream-regseqrat-handoff}.
\end{enumerate}
These five rows form the seed obligation sketch for rational stream packets
on the current $\RealUp$ and completion path.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier and classifier are obtained by projecting the packet coordinates
named in \autoref{def:rational-stream-carrier}. The pointwise obligation
theorem identifies the rational comparison rows, and the schedule-boundary
theorem identifies the finite observation schedule.

Stability is the projection of the displayed $\hsame$ coordinate $h$. The
ledger is the projection of $c$, $p$, and $n$, and the regular-sequence row is
the handoff supplied by
\autoref{thm:rational-stream-regseqrat-handoff}. These projections account
for every carrier coordinate, so the five seed obligations are complete for
the displayed packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RationalStream common-window classifier stability]
\label{thm:rational-stream-common-window-classifier-stability}
If two accepted $\RationalStreamUp$ packets classify together, then their
comparison factors through a displayed common finite unary window. On that
window the two $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ rows are related by the stored
$\hsame$ witnesses, the $\StreamNameUp$ schedules select the same displayed
finite observation positions, and every rational comparison is one of the
pointwise $\RatUp$ classifier rows $\rho$ transported along the packet data.
The stability claim exposes only finite windows and stored rational rows; it
does not identify quotient Cauchy classes, choose a limit, or assert equality
inside completed $\RealUp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the two carrier tuples from \autoref{def:rational-stream-carrier}.
The only classifier coordinates are the pointwise rational rows $\rho$, and
the only index transport coordinates are the displayed $\hsame$ rows in $h$.

Use \autoref{thm:rational-stream-pointwise-rat-obligations} to reduce every
rational comparison to the stored $\RatUp$ carrier and classifier rows. Use
\autoref{thm:rational-stream-streamname-schedule-boundary} to identify the
finite schedule rows and the common unary window on which the comparison is
read.

The remaining coordinates $c$, $p$, and $n$ record consumer routes,
dependency provenance, and local registration. None supplies a quotient
Cauchy object, a selected limiting real, or completed $\RealUp$ equality, so
the common-window classifier stability is exhausted by the displayed finite
packet rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RationalStream common-denominator window exhaustion]
\label{thm:rationalstream-common-denominator-window-exhaustion}
Any finite consumer read comparing scheduled rational observations from two
accepted $\RationalStreamUp$ packets can be refined to one displayed unary
window and one displayed common-denominator $\RatUp$ comparison ledger over
the pointwise carrier and classifier rows. The refinement uses only the
stored $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ indices, the two $\StreamNameUp$ schedules,
the pointwise rational carrier rows $q$, the pointwise classifier rows
$\rho$, and the $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ transport routes already present in the
packets. It introduces no quotient Cauchy classes, selected limits, or
completed $\RealUp$ equality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the consumer read and project the two accepted carrier packets of
\autoref{def:rational-stream-carrier}. The StreamName schedule boundary
\autoref{thm:rational-stream-streamname-schedule-boundary} identifies the
finite unary indices on which the compared observations are read.

Apply the common-window classifier stability theorem
\autoref{thm:rational-stream-common-window-classifier-stability}. It refines
the two schedule reads to a single displayed finite unary window and transports
the pointwise rows along the stored $\hsame$ witnesses.

On that window, use the pointwise RatUp obligations of
\autoref{thm:rational-stream-pointwise-rat-obligations}. Each scheduled
comparison is a rational comparison between displayed $\RatUp$ rows, so the
finite list of denominators read by the consumer can be represented by one
common-denominator comparison ledger inside the same pointwise carrier and
classifier surface.

The ledger is routed through the displayed $\Cont$ rows and repacked with the
same package and local naming data. Since every row used in the construction
is already a finite packet coordinate, the refinement has no coordinate from
which a quotient Cauchy class, selected limit, or completed $\RealUp$ equality
could be obtained.
\end{proof}

row. The transported packet is again accepted with the same finite schedule

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

$(u,s,q,\rho,h,c,p,n)$ in \autoref{def:rational-stream-carrier}. The transport
coordinate $h$ is componentwise, so it names exactly the allowed movements of
the unary index row, schedule row, rational carriers, and rational

The schedule boundary theorem
\autoref{thm:rational-stream-streamname-schedule-boundary} identifies the
finite stream-name surface after transport. The pointwise obligation theorem

rows inside the transported $\RatUp$ data, and
\autoref{thm:rational-stream-regseqrat-handoff} records that $c$, $p$, and
$n$ are the only consumer, provenance, and registration rows exposed to the

Repacking these transported coordinates gives another accepted packet with

\end{proof}

The classifier comparison is read only along the schedule boundary from
\autoref{thm:rational-stream-streamname-schedule-boundary}. Transport through

\end{proof}
