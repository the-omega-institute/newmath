\begin{definition}[De Rham standard boundary bridge packet]
\label{def:derham-standard-boundary-bridge-packet}
The De Rham standard boundary bridge packet is the bridge-facing row obtained
by packaging the public boundary source packet of
\autoref{def:derham-boundary-source-packet}, the public classifier exactness
surface of \autoref{thm:derham-boundary-public-classifier-exactness}, and the
three bridge source-scope rows
\autoref{thm:derham-bridge-input-source-scope},
\autoref{thm:derham-bridge-classifier-source-scope}, and
\autoref{thm:derham-bridge-ledger-source-scope}.

Its exported fields are the boundary preimage, the zero endpoint, the cocycle
readback, the finite graph rows over $\DiffFormUp$, $\CohomologyUp$, and
$\mathsf{BHist}$, the public boundary classifier
$\mathsf{DeRhamBoundary}_{d_{\mathrm{dR}}}$, and the provenance tying those
rows to the De Rham packet. It exports no de Rham isomorphism, quotient
carrier, host cochain complex, or external comparison row.
\end{definition}
\leandef{BEDC.Derived.DeRhamUp.DeRhamStandardBoundaryBridgePacket}

\begin{theorem}[De Rham standard boundary classifier compatibility]
\label{thm:derham-standard-boundary-classifier-compatibility}
The standard boundary bridge packet of
\autoref{def:derham-standard-boundary-bridge-packet} reads the same boundary
classifier as the encoded classifier
$\mathsf{DeRhamBoundary}_{d_{\mathrm{dR}}}$ of
\autoref{def:derham-chain-complex-boundary-row}, represented in Lean by
$\mathsf{BEDC.Derived.DeRhamUp.DeRhamBoundary}$.
For every bridge-visible boundary endpoint, classifier acceptance in the bridge
packet is equivalent to acceptance by that displayed boundary predicate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the double-exterior packet boundary. By
\autoref{thm:derham-double-exterior-derivative-boundary}, the accepted packet
produces the zero endpoint comparison, the boundary predicate
$\mathsf{DeRhamBoundary}_{d_{\mathrm{dR}}}$, and the cocycle row.

Transport the endpoint only through
\autoref{thm:derham-boundary-zero-endpoint-transport}. This keeps the same
cochain preimage and therefore keeps the same boundary classifier rather than
introducing a quotient or external cochain-complex equality.

Read back the cocycle by \autoref{thm:derham-cocycle-readback}. The cocycle
row is a consequence of the displayed differential and zero endpoint, so it
does not change the boundary classifier.

Finally apply
\autoref{thm:derham-boundary-public-classifier-exactness}. It repacks the
double-exterior boundary, zero-endpoint transport, cocycle readback, and
finite ledger rows into the public classifier surface. Since the standard
bridge packet is defined from exactly that public surface and the three
bridge source-scope theorems, it reads the same
$\mathsf{DeRhamBoundary}_{d_{\mathrm{dR}}}$ classifier.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DeRhamUp.DeRhamStandardBoundaryBridgePacket\_classifier\_compatibility}

\begin{theorem}[De Rham bridge consumer ledger exhaustion]
\label{thm:derham-bridge-consumer-ledger-exhaustion}
Every bridge consumer row of the standard boundary bridge packet of
\autoref{def:derham-standard-boundary-bridge-packet} is one of the boundary
preimage row, the zero endpoint row, the cocycle readback row, the finite graph
rows over $\DiffFormUp$, $\CohomologyUp$, and $\mathsf{BHist}$, or the package
provenance row linking those fields to the public De Rham boundary source.

Conversely, those rows repack into the standard boundary bridge packet. No
consumer row exports a de Rham isomorphism, quotient carrier, external cochain
complex, host manifold comparison, or cohomology representative.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DeRhamUp.DeRhamStandardBoundaryBridgePacket\_consumer\_ledger\_exhaustion}
\begin{proof}
Project the packet fields from
\autoref{def:derham-standard-boundary-bridge-packet}. Its source component is
\autoref{def:derham-boundary-source-packet}, so the available ledger rows are
the boundary preimage, double-exterior packet data, cocycle row, finite source
rows, and package provenance.

Apply the bridge input and classifier scope theorems. The input scope
\autoref{thm:derham-bridge-input-source-scope} admits only the displayed
$\DiffFormUp$, $\CohomologyUp$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, and square-zero rows. The
classifier scope \autoref{thm:derham-bridge-classifier-source-scope} admits
only $\hsame$ endpoint transport and cochain differential stability.

Apply \autoref{thm:derham-bridge-ledger-source-scope} for the final ledger
projection. It identifies the bridge ledger with the boundary preimage, zero
endpoint, cocycle readback, finite graph rows, and package provenance, with no
remaining branch for quotient, isomorphism, or external cochain-complex data.

For the converse direction, repack those projected rows with
\autoref{thm:derham-boundary-public-classifier-exactness}. The classifier
compatibility theorem above supplies the same boundary predicate, and the
three source-scope theorems close the bridge-facing packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[De Rham standard-boundary source exhaustion]
\label{thm:derham-standard-boundary-source-exhaustion}
Every standard-boundary bridge source row for the public $\DeRhamUp$ boundary
surface projects to one of the displayed $\DiffFormUp$ form rows,
$\CohomologyUp$ cochain rows, $\mathsf{BHist}$ endpoint rows,
exterior-square-zero rows, $\hsame$ endpoint-transport rows, or $\Pkg$
provenance rows of \autoref{def:derham-standard-boundary-bridge-packet}.
Conversely, those rows repack the standard-boundary bridge source surface.

No quotient cohomology carrier, de Rham isomorphism row, host cochain complex,
host manifold comparison, or selected cohomology representative is part of the
standard-boundary source.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DeRhamUp.DeRhamStandardBoundaryGraphLedger\_source\_exhaustion}
\begin{proof}
Project the source packet through
\autoref{def:derham-standard-boundary-bridge-packet}. Its visible source is
\autoref{def:derham-boundary-source-packet}, so the only available raw rows are
the boundary preimage, the double-exterior packet, the cocycle readback, the
finite graph rows over $\DiffFormUp$, $\CohomologyUp$, and $\mathsf{BHist}$,
and the package provenance.

Apply \autoref{thm:derham-bridge-input-source-scope}. It identifies the input
side with the displayed carried forms, cochain-differential rows,
$\mathsf{BHist}$ endpoints, and the square-zero row
$d_{\mathrm{dR}}(d_{\mathrm{dR}}\omega)=0$.

Apply \autoref{thm:derham-bridge-classifier-source-scope} and
\autoref{thm:derham-bridge-ledger-source-scope}. The classifier scope admits
only $\hsame$ endpoint transport and cochain-differential stability, while the
ledger scope admits only boundary preimage, zero endpoint, cocycle readback,
finite graph rows, and package provenance.

For the converse direction, use
\autoref{thm:derham-boundary-public-classifier-exactness} together with
\autoref{thm:derham-standard-boundary-classifier-compatibility}. The displayed
rows reconstruct the same boundary predicate and the same bridge-facing source
packet, with no branch for quotient, isomorphism, host-comparison, or
representative data.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[De Rham standard-boundary classifier transport]
\label{thm:derham-standard-boundary-classifier-transport}
Classifier transport across the standard-boundary bridge for the public
$\DeRhamUp$ boundary surface uses only $\hsame$ endpoint transport and
cochain-differential stability. In particular, the transported classifier is
the same displayed boundary predicate
$\mathsf{DeRhamBoundary}_{d_{\mathrm{dR}}}$ on the transported endpoint, and
the transport does not read a quotient class, a de Rham isomorphism, a host
manifold comparison, or a chosen cohomology representative.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the classifier identified by
\autoref{thm:derham-standard-boundary-classifier-compatibility}. It is the
same predicate $\mathsf{DeRhamBoundary}_{d_{\mathrm{dR}}}$ as the public
boundary classifier of
\autoref{thm:derham-boundary-public-classifier-exactness}.

Transport the endpoint by
\autoref{thm:derham-boundary-zero-endpoint-transport}. That theorem keeps the
cochain preimage fixed, moves only the displayed endpoint by $\hsame$, and
preserves the cochain-differential row that witnesses the boundary predicate.

Now apply \autoref{thm:derham-bridge-classifier-source-scope}. It says that
all bridge-visible classifier movement is exhausted by precisely that
$\hsame$ endpoint transport plus cochain-differential stability.

The input and ledger scope theorems,
\autoref{thm:derham-bridge-input-source-scope} and
\autoref{thm:derham-bridge-ledger-source-scope}, add only the finite displayed
rows and package provenance. Therefore the classifier transport has no
remaining source from which quotient, isomorphism, host-manifold, or
representative data could enter.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DeRhamUp.DeRhamStandardBoundaryBridgePacket\_classifier\_transport}

\begin{theorem}[De Rham standard-boundary consumer readback]
\label{thm:derham-standard-boundary-consumer-readback}
Every downstream consumer of the standard-boundary bridge packet reads only
the boundary preimage row, the cocycle row, the finite graph rows over
$\DiffFormUp$, $\CohomologyUp$, and $\mathsf{BHist}$, and the package
provenance rows displayed by \autoref{def:derham-standard-boundary-bridge-packet}.
No consumer row reads a quotient carrier, a de Rham isomorphism, a host
manifold comparison, or a selected cohomology representative.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the consumer surface by
\autoref{thm:derham-bridge-consumer-ledger-exhaustion}. It lists exactly the
boundary preimage, zero endpoint, cocycle readback, finite graph rows, and
package provenance rows available to a bridge consumer.

Use \autoref{thm:derham-standard-boundary-source-exhaustion} to identify the
source side of those consumer rows. The source contributes only the displayed
$\DiffFormUp$, $\CohomologyUp$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, square-zero, $\hsame$, and
$\Pkg$ rows.

Use \autoref{thm:derham-standard-boundary-classifier-transport} for the
classifier side. It keeps consumer-visible classifier movement inside
$\hsame$ endpoint transport and cochain-differential stability.

Finally repack through
\autoref{def:derham-standard-boundary-bridge-packet}. Since both the source
and classifier projections have already been exhausted by displayed rows, the
consumer readback cannot contain quotient, isomorphism, host-manifold, or
representative data.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[De Rham standard-boundary zero cocycle example]
\label{thm:derham-standard-boundary-zero-cocycle-example}
For the zero cochain row carried by the public $\DeRhamUp$ boundary packet, the
standard-boundary bridge exports a zero cocycle consumer row. The row consists
of the zero endpoint in $\mathsf{BHist}$, the square-zero
$d_{\mathrm{dR}}(d_{\mathrm{dR}}0)=0$ witness, the $\CohomologyUp$ cocycle
readback, and the $\Pkg$ provenance already displayed by
\autoref{def:derham-standard-boundary-bridge-packet}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the zero cochain through
\autoref{thm:derham-standard-boundary-consumer-readback}. The consumer surface
allows only the boundary preimage, cocycle, finite graph, and provenance rows of
the standard-boundary packet.

Apply \autoref{thm:derham-standard-boundary-source-exhaustion}. The zero
cochain row is one of the displayed $\CohomologyUp$ cochain rows, and the zero
endpoint is one of the displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ endpoint rows.

Finally use \autoref{thm:derham-standard-boundary-classifier-transport}. It
keeps the boundary classifier inside $\hsame$ endpoint transport and
cochain-differential stability, so the zero endpoint yields the cocycle row
without adding quotient or representative data.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[De Rham standard-boundary coboundary consumer example]
\label{thm:derham-standard-boundary-coboundary-consumer-example}
For any displayed boundary preimage $\omega$ in the public $\DeRhamUp$
boundary packet, the standard-boundary bridge exports the coboundary consumer
row
$$
\begin{gathered}
\omega
\xrightarrow{d_{\mathrm{dR}}}
d_{\mathrm{dR}}\omega
\xrightarrow{d_{\mathrm{dR}}}
0 .
\end{gathered}
$$
The consumer receives the boundary preimage row, the zero endpoint row, the
cocycle readback row, and the finite $\DiffFormUp$, $\CohomologyUp$,
$\mathsf{BHist}$, and $\Pkg$ rows of the standard-boundary packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the preimage row supplied by
\autoref{thm:derham-standard-boundary-source-exhaustion}. It is one of the
displayed cochain rows in the public boundary source packet, and its endpoint is
one of the displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ rows.

Use \autoref{thm:derham-standard-boundary-classifier-transport} to keep the
boundary classifier on the same displayed endpoint after $\hsame$ transport.
The cochain-differential row therefore remains a boundary row for
$d_{\mathrm{dR}}\omega$ with zero endpoint.

Project the exported consumer surface through
\autoref{thm:derham-standard-boundary-consumer-readback}. It gives exactly the
preimage, zero endpoint, cocycle readback, finite graph, and provenance rows,
which are the displayed coboundary consumer row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[De Rham standard-boundary finite graph completeness]
\label{thm:derham-standard-boundary-finite-graph-completeness}
Every consumer row of the standard-boundary bridge packet factors through the
finite rows already listed by the bridge packet: $\DiffFormUp$ form rows,
$\CohomologyUp$ cochain and cocycle rows, $\mathsf{BHist}$ endpoint rows,
$\hsame$ endpoint transports, and $\Pkg$ provenance. Thus the standard-boundary
packet is complete for finite graph consumers of the displayed boundary
surface.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DeRhamUp.DeRhamStandardBoundaryGraphLedger\_finite\_graph\_completeness}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:derham-standard-boundary-consumer-readback}. It restricts
every downstream consumer to the boundary preimage, cocycle, finite graph, and
provenance rows of the standard-boundary packet.

Apply \autoref{thm:derham-standard-boundary-source-exhaustion} for the source
factorization. The source side is exhausted by the displayed $\DiffFormUp$,
$\CohomologyUp$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, square-zero, $\hsame$, and $\Pkg$ rows.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:derham-standard-boundary-classifier-transport}. The
classifier side has no additional branch beyond $\hsame$ endpoint transport and
cochain-differential stability. Combining the two projections gives finite
graph completeness for the bridge packet.
\end{proof}
