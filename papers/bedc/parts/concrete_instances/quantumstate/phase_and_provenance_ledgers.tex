\begin{theorem}[QuantumStateUp provenance row exactness]
\label{thm:quantumstate-provenance-row-exactness}
For every carrier packet
$$
\mathcal{Q}=(\mathcal{H},\mathcal{P},v,n,\phi,p,\lambda_H,\lambda_P,\rho)
$$
of \autoref{def:quantumstate-bhist-carrier}, the provenance row $\rho$
partitions the public rows of $\mathcal{Q}$ exactly into the consumed
$\NameCert_{\HilbertUp}$ packet $\mathcal{H}$, the consumed
$\NameCert_{\ProjectiveSpaceUp}$ packet $\mathcal{P}$, the displayed
norm row $n$, phase row $\phi$, projective endpoint row $p$, Hilbert
endpoint row $v$, and inherited classifier rows $\lambda_H,\lambda_P$.
No observable row, density-matrix row, channel row, spectral row,
host-vector-equality row, or quotient row belongs to the public
$\mathsf{BHist}$ packet witnessed by $\rho$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.QuantumStateUp.QuantumStateBHistCarrier\_provenance\_row\_exactness}
\begin{proof}
Use the carrier display in \autoref{def:quantumstate-bhist-carrier} as the
row inventory. The packet has the two consumed $\NameCert$ rows
$\mathcal{H}$ and $\mathcal{P}$, the endpoint and ledger rows
$v,n,\phi,p$, the inherited classifiers $\lambda_H,\lambda_P$, and the
provenance row $\rho$. The row $\rho$ is therefore the $\mathsf{Pkg}$
record tying exactly those rows into one finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet.

The Hilbert portion is supplied by
\autoref{thm:quantumstate-namecert-obligation-surface}: it exposes the
carrier, classifier, stability, ledger, exactness, and hidden phase rows
but no downstream observable object. The projective portion is supplied by
\autoref{thm:quantumstate-projective-phase-classifier}: it reads phase
classification through $\phi$ and $\lambda_P$, not through a host quotient.

Finally apply the consumer boundary in
\autoref{thm:quantumstate-projective-consumer-boundary}. A downstream row
may inspect only the named Hilbert, projective, phase, norm, and provenance
fields. Together with the global-phase stability theorem
\autoref{thm:quantumstate-global-phase-stability}, this proves that
$\rho$ is exact for the displayed $\NameCert$ package and excludes every
observable, density-matrix, channel, spectral, host-vector-equality, and
quotient row from the public surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[QuantumStateUp phase ledger exhaustion]
\label{thm:quantumstate-phase-ledger-exhaustion}
Every hidden row used to classify two $\QuantumStateUp$ carrier packets is
one of the displayed phase row $\phi$, inherited Hilbert classifier
$\lambda_H$, inherited projective classifier $\lambda_P$, norm-one ledger
$n$, endpoint $\hsame$ transport row, or provenance row $\rho$ from the
carrier of \autoref{def:quantumstate-bhist-carrier}. Conversely, those
rows reconstruct exactly the projective phase classifier of
\autoref{thm:quantumstate-projective-phase-classifier} inside the consumed
$\NameCert_{\HilbertUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\ProjectiveSpaceUp}$ packets.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.QuantumStateUp.QuantumStatePhaseLedgerSpine\_normalized\_cont}
\begin{proof}
Start from two displayed packets
$$
\mathcal{Q}=(\mathcal{H},\mathcal{P},v,n,\phi,p,\lambda_H,\lambda_P,\rho)
$$
and
$$
\mathcal{Q}'=(\mathcal{H}',\mathcal{P}',v',n',\phi',p',\lambda'_H,\lambda'_P,\rho').
$$
The carrier definition lists the only rows available to the classifier.
Thus a hidden classifier row must be read from the phase row, one of the
inherited classifiers, the norm ledger, the endpoint $\hsame$ transport
data, or the $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance record.

For exhaustion, use \autoref{thm:quantumstate-namecert-obligation-surface}
to identify the public $\NameCert_{\QuantumStateUp}$ obligations and
\autoref{thm:quantumstate-projective-consumer-boundary} to rule out
consumer access to a stronger equality. The stability row in
\autoref{thm:quantumstate-global-phase-stability} accounts for every
endpoint transport through $\hsame$, while the provenance exactness of
\autoref{thm:quantumstate-provenance-row-exactness} keeps those transports
inside the same finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ package.

For reconstruction, read the Hilbert endpoint through $\lambda_H$, read the
norm ledger through $n$, and read the projective endpoint comparison through
$\phi$ and $\lambda_P$. These are precisely the inputs named by
\autoref{thm:quantumstate-projective-phase-classifier}; repacking them with
$\rho$ recovers the same projective phase classifier and adds no hidden
row beyond the displayed ledger.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[QuantumStateUp consumer readback completeness]
\label{thm:quantumstate-consumer-readback-completeness}
Every downstream $\ObservableUp$, $\DensityMatrixUp$, and
$\QuantumChannelUp$ consumer row reading $\NameCert_{\QuantumStateUp}$
factors through the five public fields named in
\autoref{thm:quantumstate-projective-consumer-boundary}: the Hilbert source
row $\mathcal{H}$, projective endpoint row $p$, global-phase classifier
$\phi$, norm ledger $n$, and package provenance $\rho$. Conversely, any
consumer depending only on those five fields is stable under
\autoref{thm:quantumstate-global-phase-stability}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The forward direction is a readback claim. By
\autoref{def:quantumstate-bhist-carrier}, a consumer begins with a finite
$\mathsf{BHist}$ packet carried by $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance. By
\autoref{thm:quantumstate-namecert-obligation-surface}, the public
$\NameCert_{\QuantumStateUp}$ surface supplies the carrier, classifier,
stability, ledger, exactness, and hidden phase obligations, but it supplies
no observable, density-matrix, channel, or spectral row.

Apply \autoref{thm:quantumstate-projective-consumer-boundary}. The rows
that remain readable by $\ObservableUp$, $\DensityMatrixUp$, and
$\QuantumChannelUp$ consumers are exactly $\mathcal{H}$, $p$, $\phi$, $n$,
and $\rho$. The projective classifier theorem
\autoref{thm:quantumstate-projective-phase-classifier} explains how the
endpoint and phase comparison behind those five fields is classified
without exposing host vector equality or a quotient primitive.

For the converse direction, suppose a consumer depends only on
$\mathcal{H}$, $p$, $\phi$, $n$, and $\rho$. Transport the Hilbert and
projective source data by the $\hsame$ rows governed by $\lambda_H$ and
$\lambda_P$. The global-phase stability theorem
\autoref{thm:quantumstate-global-phase-stability} preserves the phase row
and norm ledger, and \autoref{thm:quantumstate-provenance-row-exactness}
preserves the finite package boundary. Therefore the consumer readback is
unchanged under certified global-phase transport.
\end{proof}
