\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\QuantumStateUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-quantumstate-namecert}

The quantum-state interface $\QuantumStateUp$ packages a unit vector in a Hilbert space carrier together with the equivalence under global phase. It depends on $\NameCert_{\HilbertUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-hilbert-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\ProjectiveSpaceUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-projectivespace-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:quantumstate-carrier}

\begin{definition}[QuantumStateUp BHist carrier]
\label{def:quantumstate-bhist-carrier}
A $\QuantumStateUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
\mathcal{Q}=(\mathcal{H},\mathcal{P},v,n,\phi,p,\lambda_H,\lambda_P,\rho).
$$
The row $\mathcal{H}$ is a consumed $\NameCert_{\HilbertUp}$ row. The row
$\mathcal{P}$ is a consumed $\NameCert_{\ProjectiveSpaceUp}$ row over the
displayed projective line determined by $\mathcal{H}$. The endpoint $v$ is the
unit-vector row projected from $\mathcal{H}$, $n$ is the norm-one ledger row,
$\phi$ is the global-phase row, and $p$ is the projective-line endpoint row
projected from $\mathcal{P}$.

The rows $\lambda_H$ and $\lambda_P$ are the inherited Hilbert and projective
classifier rows, respectively. The row $\rho$ is the $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance
record witnessing that the two consumed certificates and all displayed endpoint
rows belong to the same finite packet. No carrier datum outside
$\mathcal{H}$, $\mathcal{P}$, $v$, $n$, $\phi$, $p$, $\lambda_H$, $\lambda_P$,
and $\rho$ is part of the public $\QuantumStateUp$ row.
\end{definition}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:quantumstate-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[QuantumStateUp Hilbert source boundary]
\label{thm:quantumstate-hilbert-source-boundary}
For every carrier packet $\mathcal{Q}$ of
\autoref{def:quantumstate-bhist-carrier}, all vector, norm, and inner-product
rows used by the $\QuantumStateUp$ interface are projected from the consumed
$\NameCert_{\HilbertUp}$ row $\mathcal{H}$. In particular, the visible vector
endpoint $v$, the norm-one ledger $n$, and every inner-product row used to test
unit length are read through $\mathcal{H}$ and the inherited classifier
$\lambda_H$, not through an ambient Hilbert-space carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The traditional target is a Hilbert-space unit vector carrying the usual norm
and inner-product data. The BEDC source is the consumed
$\NameCert_{\HilbertUp}$ row $\mathcal{H}$, and the carrier choice is the finite
packet $\mathcal{Q}$ of \autoref{def:quantumstate-bhist-carrier}.

Unpack the definition of $\mathcal{Q}$. The only fields naming Hilbert-side
data are $\mathcal{H}$, $v$, $n$, and $\lambda_H$, together with the provenance
row $\rho$. Thus the classifier choice is the inherited Hilbert classifier
$\lambda_H$, stability is $\hsame$ transport through that classifier, and the
ledger is the norm-one row $n$.

The core theorem list for this boundary consists of the visible endpoint
projection, the norm-one ledger projection, and the inner-product projection.
Exactness says that every Hilbert-side row visible to $\QuantumStateUp$ is one
of those projected rows. There is no standard bridge supplied here, and the
status is a paper obligation for the $\NameCert_{\QuantumStateUp}$ Hilbert
source boundary.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[QuantumStateUp projective phase classifier]
\label{thm:quantumstate-projective-phase-classifier}
The classified $\QuantumStateUp$ rows are exactly the Hilbert unit-vector rows
$v$ whose projective endpoints $p$ are related by the global-phase row $\phi$
and the $\ProjectiveSpaceUp$ classifier $\lambda_P$. Equivalently, two carrier
packets
$$
\mathcal{Q}=(\mathcal{H},\mathcal{P},v,n,\phi,p,\lambda_H,\lambda_P,\rho)
$$
and
$$
\mathcal{Q}'=(\mathcal{H}',\mathcal{P}',v',n',\phi',p',\lambda'_H,\lambda'_P,\rho')
$$
classify the same quantum state precisely when the Hilbert endpoint rows are
unit-vector rows and the projective endpoints are identified by the displayed
global-phase/projective-space classifier rows. The classifier records no host
vector equality, no independent Hilbert-space quotient, and no primitive
quotient object beyond the projective scaling classifier supplied by
$\mathcal{P}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The traditional target is equality of pure quantum states modulo global phase.
The BEDC source is the pair of consumed certificates
$\NameCert_{\HilbertUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\ProjectiveSpaceUp}$. The carrier
choice is the packet $\mathcal{Q}$, while the classifier choice is the pair
formed by the global-phase row $\phi$ and the projective classifier
$\lambda_P$.

For the forward reading, a classified $\QuantumStateUp$ row first exposes a
Hilbert endpoint $v$ and its norm-one ledger $n$ through
\autoref{thm:quantumstate-hilbert-source-boundary}. It then exposes the
projective endpoint $p$ through $\mathcal{P}$. The row $\phi$ records the
allowed phase change, and $\lambda_P$ records the projective classifier that
accepts the resulting endpoint comparison.

For the reverse reading, a Hilbert unit-vector endpoint with a norm-one ledger
and a projective endpoint accepted by $\phi$ and $\lambda_P$ supplies all fields
of \autoref{def:quantumstate-bhist-carrier}. Stability is componentwise
$\hsame$ transport through $\lambda_H$ and $\lambda_P$, the ledger is $n$
together with the phase row $\phi$, and exactness says that no host quotient is
read. The core theorem list is this classifier row together with the Hilbert
source boundary. The standard bridge is absent, and the status is a paper
obligation for the $\NameCert_{\QuantumStateUp}$ classifier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[QuantumStateUp global phase stability]
\label{thm:quantumstate-global-phase-stability}
Let $\mathcal{Q}$ and $\mathcal{Q}'$ be two carrier packets of
\autoref{def:quantumstate-bhist-carrier}. If the Hilbert rows
$\mathcal{H},v,n,\lambda_H$ and the projective rows
$\mathcal{P},p,\lambda_P$ are transported componentwise by $\hsame$, then the
global-phase classifier row is preserved and the norm-one ledger remains a
valid $\QuantumStateUp$ ledger row. Thus $\hsame$ transport of the consumed
Hilbert and projective certificates preserves the phase classifier and the
norm-one condition.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The traditional target is invariance of a pure state under change of certified
presentation. The BEDC source is the pair of source certificates
$\mathcal{H}$ and $\mathcal{P}$, the carrier choice is the packet
$\mathcal{Q}$, and the classifier choice is $\lambda_H$, $\lambda_P$, and the
phase row $\phi$.

Transport the Hilbert side first. The $\hsame$ witnesses for
$\mathcal{H}$, $v$, $n$, and $\lambda_H$ move the unit-vector endpoint and the
norm-one ledger through the Hilbert classifier without opening any host vector
equality.

Transport the projective side next. The $\hsame$ witnesses for
$\mathcal{P}$, $p$, and $\lambda_P$ move the projective endpoint and its
classifier row. The phase row $\phi$ is preserved because its source and target
endpoints are exactly the transported Hilbert and projective endpoints.

The ledger consists of the norm-one row, the phase row, and the provenance
row. The core theorem list is the Hilbert source boundary, the projective phase
classifier, and this transport row. Exactness says that every preserved row is
one of the displayed packet fields. No standard bridge is supplied, and the
status is a paper obligation for $\NameCert_{\QuantumStateUp}$ stability.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[QuantumStateUp public obligation surface]
\label{thm:quantumstate-namecert-obligation-surface}
The public $\NameCert_{\QuantumStateUp}$ surface consists exactly of the
carrier packet of \autoref{def:quantumstate-bhist-carrier}, the projective
phase classifier of \autoref{thm:quantumstate-projective-phase-classifier}, the
stability row of \autoref{thm:quantumstate-global-phase-stability}, the
norm-one ledger, the projective exactness predicate, and the hidden
global-phase rows needed to witness the endpoint comparison. These rows form
the carrier, classifier, stability, ledger, exactness, and hidden
global-phase obligations exported by $\QuantumStateUp$. The surface contains
no density-matrix row, measurement row, channel row, or spectral row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The traditional target is the pure-state naming certificate. The BEDC source
is the pair $\NameCert_{\HilbertUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\ProjectiveSpaceUp}$, and
the carrier choice is the displayed packet $\mathcal{Q}$. The classifier choice
is the global-phase/projective classifier, while stability is
\autoref{thm:quantumstate-global-phase-stability}.

The ledger is finite: it contains the norm-one row $n$, the phase row $\phi$,
the two inherited classifier rows $\lambda_H$ and $\lambda_P$, and the
provenance row $\rho$. The core theorem list is the Hilbert source boundary,
the projective phase classifier, and the global-phase stability row.

Exactness says that every public $\QuantumStateUp$ row is assembled from those
fields and that every hidden phase row is used only to classify projective
endpoints. The named dependency certificates expose no density-matrix,
measurement, channel, or spectral row at this site. The standard bridge field
is empty. The status is a paper obligation for the $\NameCert_{\QuantumStateUp}$
public surface.
\end{proof}
