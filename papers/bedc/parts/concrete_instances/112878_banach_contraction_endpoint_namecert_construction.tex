\begin{definition}[Banach contraction endpoint carrier]
\label{def:banach-contraction-endpoint-carrier}
A $\BanachContractionEndpointUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
E=(X,F,O,M,L,U,Q,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the complete separated metric source row consumed by the contraction.
\item $F$ is the contraction source row, naming the displayed $\BanachContractionUp$ and $\ContractionMappingUp$ data.
\item $O$ is the $\PicardIterationUp$ orbit row generated from the displayed source and contraction data.
\item $M$ is the Cauchy modulus row for the orbit, recording the finite tail budget used by the completion service.
\item $L$ is the complete-metric endpoint row reached from the displayed orbit and modulus.
\item $U$ is the separated uniqueness row, showing that any accepted endpoint read is forced through the same completion boundary.
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row attached to the endpoint observations.
\item $R$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row, available only after $X,F,O,M,L,U,Q$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for source, contraction, orbit, modulus, endpoint, uniqueness, readback, seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay for the same endpoint route.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BanachContractionEndpointUp$, $\BanachContractionUp$, $\ContractionMappingUp$, $\PicardIterationUp$, $\CompleteMetricUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\BanachContractionEndpointUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $X,F,O,M,L,U,Q,R,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for a host-selected fixed point, quotient completion, ambient real completeness, countable choice, or a fixed-point theorem detached from the displayed orbit and endpoint seal.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Banach contraction endpoint NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:banach-contraction-endpoint-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\BanachContractionEndpointUp$ carrier
$$
E=(X,F,O,M,L,U,Q,R,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\BanachContractionEndpointUp}$ surface consists exactly of complete separated metric admission through $X$, contraction-source exposure through $F$, Picard-orbit exposure through $O$, Cauchy-modulus exposure through $M$, complete-metric endpoint exposure through $L$, separated uniqueness through $U$, regular rational readback through $Q$, real seal exposure through $R$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:banach-contraction-endpoint-carrier}. The endpoint-facing rows are $X,F,O,M,L,U,Q,R$, and the structural rows are $H,C,P,N$.

The contraction route is read in order. Rows $X$ and $F$ provide the complete separated metric source and contraction data, $O$ records the Picard orbit, and $M$ supplies the finite Cauchy budget that lets the completion service expose $L$.

The endpoint is then sealed only through $U,Q,R,H,C,P,N$. Separated uniqueness, regular rational readback, and the terminal real seal remain displayed rows of the same packet, so the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Banach contraction endpoint nonescape]
\label{thm:banach-contraction-endpoint-nonescape}
Every $\RealUp$ or fixed-endpoint consumer of an accepted $\BanachContractionEndpointUp$ packet reaches the endpoint only through
$$
\CompleteMetricUp,\ \BanachContractionUp,\ \ContractionMappingUp,\ \PicardIterationUp,\ \RegSeqRatUp,\ \RealUp

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply the obligations theorem \autoref{thm:banach-contraction-endpoint-namecert-obligations}. It requires every endpoint read to expose the complete source, contraction source, Picard orbit, Cauchy modulus, completion endpoint, separated uniqueness, readback, and seal rows.

The terminal real seal $R$ is downstream of $Q$, and $Q$ is downstream of the displayed endpoint row $L$. Thus a Real-facing consumer cannot reach $R$ without first reading the orbit and completion route carried by $O,M,L$.

Since the classifier has no coordinate for host-selected fixed points, quotient completion, countable choice, ambient completeness, or detached fixed-point theorems, those reads cannot be exported by an accepted packet.
\end{proof}
