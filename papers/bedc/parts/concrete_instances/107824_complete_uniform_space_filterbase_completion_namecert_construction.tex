\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CompleteUniformSpaceFilterbaseCompletionUp$}

Following Bourbaki and Kelley on uniform spaces and filter convergence, and Bishop--Bridges on constructive completion, the $\CompleteUniformSpaceFilterbaseCompletionUp$ packet records the finite filterbase-completion refinement of the complete uniform-space surface. It sits after the uniform entourage data of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformspace-namecert}, the saturated Cauchy-filter route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyfilter-namecert}, the finite basis generator of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-filter-basis-namecert}, the limit-facing boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyfilterlimit-namecert}, the window and readback surfaces of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the terminal seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, and the completion handoffs of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformcompletion-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyfiltercompletion-namecert}. The packet names a displayed filterbase route to completion consumers; it is not a quotient filter, selected completion point, countable-choice selector, host equality, ambient completeness theorem, or public standard bridge.

\begin{definition}[Complete uniform-space filterbase-completion carrier]
\label{def:complete-uniform-space-filterbase-completion-carrier}
A $\CompleteUniformSpaceFilterbaseCompletionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(U,F,B,L,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $U$ is the $\UniformSpaceUp$ entourage row, displaying the finite entourage filterbase, diagonal, refinement, symmetry, and composition data consumed by the completion request.
\item $F$ is the $\CauchyFilterUp$ row, recording the saturated Cauchy-filter request over the uniform surface of $U$.
\item $B$ is the $\CauchyFilterBasisUp$ generator row, exposing the finite basis elements and common-refinement data through which $F$ is read.
\item $L$ is the $\CauchyFilterLimitUp$ boundary row, reached only after the basis and saturated filter rows have been replayed.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window row attached to the filterbase convergence request.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row extracted from the same windows.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row read only after $U,F,B,L,S,R$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the entourage, filter, basis, limit boundary, window, readback, seal, replay, provenance, and naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from the uniform entourage request through the filterbase generator, Cauchy saturation, limit boundary, finite windows, regular readback, and Real seal.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CompleteUniformSpaceFilterbaseCompletionUp$, $\CompleteUniformSpaceUp$, $\UniformSpaceUp$, $\CauchyFilterUp$, $\CauchyFilterBasisUp$, $\CauchyFilterLimitUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\UniformCompletionUp$, $\CauchyFilterCompletionUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CompleteUniformSpaceFilterbaseCompletionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $U,F,B,L,S,R,E,H,C,P,N$. The carrier has no coordinate for quotient filters, selected completion points, countable-choice schedules, host equality, ambient completeness, or a detached completion object.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Filterbase convergence obligations]
\label{thm:complete-uniform-space-filterbase-convergence-obligations}
For every accepted $\CompleteUniformSpaceFilterbaseCompletionUp$ carrier $Q=(U,F,B,L,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CompleteUniformSpaceFilterbaseCompletionUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed uniform-space entourage data, Cauchy-filter row, CauchyFilterBasis generator, CauchyFilterLimit boundary, StreamName windows, RegSeqRat readbacks, Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item filterbase convergence exposure, requiring every completion-facing request to pass through $U$, $B$, and $F$ before the limit boundary $L$ is inspected;
\item readback exactness, requiring every accepted boundary observation to pass from $L$ through the matching $S$ and $R$ rows before the Real seal $E$ is available;
\item completion-handoff stability, requiring $\UniformCompletionUp$ and $\CauchyFilterCompletionUp$ consumers named by $P$ to read the same displayed route $U,B,F,L,S,R,E$;
\item ledger nonescape, requiring $H,C,P,N$ to export no quotient filter, selected completion point, countable-choice selector, host equality, ambient completeness theorem, or detached completion object.
\end{enumerate}
No local NameCert obligation is stored outside those rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:complete-uniform-space-filterbase-completion-carrier}. Its public rows are exactly $U,F,B,L,S,R,E,H,C,P,N$, so carrier admission exposes only the displayed uniform, filterbase, convergence, seal, and structural data.

The filterbase convergence exposure is forced by the row order. A completion-facing request first reads the finite entourage data $U$, then the basis generator $B$, and only then the saturated Cauchy-filter row $F$ before the limit boundary $L$ is available.

Readback exactness is downstream of the boundary row. The row $L$ can expose observations only through the StreamName windows $S$ and the RegSeqRat readback $R$, and the terminal Real seal $E$ is available only after those rows have been replayed.

The completion-handoff and ledger clauses are structural. Uniform-completion and Cauchy-filter-completion consumers are named in $P$ as readers of the same displayed route, while $H,C,N$ transport, replay, and name only that route. Hence the excluded quotient, selector, host-equality, choice, completeness, and detached-completion objects have no coordinate in the packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy-filter completion handoff]
\label{thm:complete-uniform-space-cauchy-filter-completion-handoff}
Let $Q=(U,F,B,L,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CompleteUniformSpaceFilterbaseCompletionUp$ packet. Every $\UniformCompletionUp$ or $\CauchyFilterCompletionUp$ consumer that reads $Q$ receives the finite handoff
$$
U \longmapsto B \longmapsto F \longmapsto L \longmapsto S \longmapsto R \longmapsto E \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
Thus the completion handoff is filterbase-first: the consumer cannot inspect a completion-facing boundary before the uniform entourage row, the CauchyFilterBasis generator, and the saturated CauchyFilter row have been displayed.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a completion-facing consumer. The provenance row $P$ may mention $\UniformCompletionUp$ and $\CauchyFilterCompletionUp$, but it does so only as a row of the accepted carrier $Q$.

The source of the handoff is therefore the displayed route. The uniform-space row $U$ supplies the entourage filterbase, $B$ supplies the finite basis generator, and $F$ supplies the saturated Cauchy-filter request over the same data.

The boundary and readback rows are downstream. The consumer reaches $L$ only after $U,B,F$ have been replayed, then reads $S$ and $R$ before the terminal seal $E$ is available.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name this route. Since the carrier has no separate completion object, selected point, quotient filter, choice schedule, host equality, or ambient completeness row, every accepted completion handoff is confined to the displayed path.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Filterbase Real-seal nonescape]
\label{thm:complete-uniform-space-filterbase-real-seal-nonescape}
Let $Q=(U,F,B,L,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CompleteUniformSpaceFilterbaseCompletionUp$ carrier. Every consumer that reaches the displayed $\RealUp$ seal row $E$ must first pass through the uniform entourage row $U$, the CauchyFilterBasis row $B$, the saturated CauchyFilter row $F$, the CauchyFilterLimit boundary row $L$, the StreamName windows $S$, and the RegSeqRat readback row $R$. The rows $H,C,P,N$ then preserve and name the same route, so no accepted read can escape from $E$ into a selected completion point, quotient filter, host equality, countable-choice selector, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, or detached $\UniformCompletionUp$ object.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier definition. The terminal row $E$ appears only as the Real seal of the tuple, and the preceding public coordinates are precisely $U,F,B,L,S,R$.

Apply the obligation surface of \autoref{thm:complete-uniform-space-filterbase-convergence-obligations}. It requires the filterbase convergence request to consume $U,B,F$, then the limit boundary $L$, then the window and readback rows $S,R$ before $E$ can be read.

The structural rows cannot introduce a second route. Componentwise transport $H$, replay $C$, provenance $P$, and local naming $N$ preserve the same displayed packet, while the excluded selector, quotient, host-equality, choice, ambient-completeness, and detached-completion objects are absent from the carrier.
\end{proof}
