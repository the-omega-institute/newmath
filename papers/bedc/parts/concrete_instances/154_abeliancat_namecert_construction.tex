\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\AbelianCatUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-abeliancat-namecert}

The abelian-category interface $\AbelianCatUp$ packages an additive category in which every morphism has a kernel and cokernel and the canonical epi-mono factorisation is an isomorphism. It depends on $\NameCert_{\CategoryUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-category-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\AbGroupUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-abgroup-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:abeliancat-carrier}

\begin{definition}[AbelianCatUp additive kernel-cokernel carrier]
\label{def:abeliancat-additive-kernel-cokernel-carrier}
An $\AbelianCatUp$ additive kernel-cokernel carrier is a concrete
$\mathsf{BHist}$ package
$$
\mathcal A=(\mathcal C,\mathcal H,\mathcal Z,\mathcal B,\mathcal G,\mathcal K,
\mathcal Q,\mathcal F)
$$
with the following displayed rows.
\begin{enumerate}
  \item $\mathcal C$ is a $\CategoryUp$ carrier and classifier row, with object
  carrier, morphism carrier, identity rows, composition rows, associativity,
  unit laws, and componentwise $\hsame$ transport.
  \item $\mathcal H(A,B)$ is the visible hom carrier row for each displayed pair
  of objects $A,B$, classified by the morphism classifier inherited from
  $\mathcal C$.
  \item $\mathcal Z(A,B)$ gives the visible zero morphism row $0_{A,B}:A\to B$.
  \item $\mathcal B(A,B)$ gives the visible binary biproduct rows for $A$ and
  $B$, including the object $A\oplus B$, projections, injections, and their
  four classifier equations.
  \item $\mathcal G(A,B)$ is an $\AbGroupUp$ enrichment row on
  $\mathcal H(A,B)$, with addition, zero, inverse, commutativity, and classifier
  congruence displayed in the hom row.
  \item $\mathcal K(f)$ gives, for every visible morphism $f:A\to B$, an object
  $\ker(f)$, a morphism $k_f:\ker(f)\to A$, a zero-composite row, and the
  universal factorisation ledger through $k_f$.
  \item $\mathcal Q(f)$ gives, for every visible morphism $f:A\to B$, an object
  $\operatorname{coker}(f)$, a morphism $q_f:B\to\operatorname{coker}(f)$, a
  zero-composite row, and the universal cofactorisation ledger through $q_f$.
  \item $\mathcal F(f)$ is the visible epi--mono factorisation ledger for $f$,
  displaying image and coimage objects, the quotient-side arrow, the
  inclusion-side arrow, the canonical comparison, and the classifier row saying
  that the recomposed morphism is $f$.
\end{enumerate}
No object in this carrier is supplied by an external quotient or hidden
subobject classifier. The kernel, cokernel, image, coimage, and comparison
objects are selected rows of the same $\mathsf{BHist}$ package.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[AbelianCatUp carrier classifier obligation]
\label{thm:abeliancat-carrier-classifier-obligation}
Let $\mathcal A$ be an $\AbelianCatUp$ additive kernel-cokernel carrier. If two
presentations of $\mathcal A$ are related by componentwise $\hsame$ on the
$\CategoryUp$ carrier, on every hom carrier, on every zero, biproduct,
$\AbGroupUp$ enrichment, kernel, cokernel, image, coimage, comparison, and
factorisation row, then the package carrier and every displayed row are
transported to a classified presentation of the same $\AbelianCatUp$ carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the $\CategoryUp$ carrier row. Its object, morphism, identity, and
composition classifiers are part of $\mathcal C$, so componentwise $\hsame$
transports the category carrier and the source and target data of each visible
morphism.

For each pair of transported objects $A,B$, the hom row
$\mathcal H(A,B)$ is classified by the transported morphism classifier. The
zero row, biproduct object, projections, and injections are displayed rows over
the same endpoints, so the $\CategoryUp$ identity, composition, and classifier
stability fields transport their typing and equations.

It remains to transport the additive and exactness rows. The
$\AbGroupUp$ enrichment supplies $\hsame$-stable addition, zero, inverse, and
commutativity on each hom row. The kernel, cokernel, image, coimage, comparison,
and factorisation ledgers are rows indexed by visible morphisms, so transporting
the morphism row transports the indexed ledger row and its displayed
composition equations. Repacking these transported projections gives the same
$\AbelianCatUp$ carrier presentation.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[AbelianCatUp additive zero-biproduct obligation]
\label{thm:abeliancat-additive-zero-biproduct-obligation}
For an $\AbelianCatUp$ additive kernel-cokernel carrier $\mathcal A$, the zero
morphisms, hom-row addition, and binary biproduct projections and injections
are exactly the visible additive rows inherited from
$\NameCert_{\AbGroupUp}$ on each hom carrier and from
$\NameCert_{\CategoryUp}$ on identities, composition, and biproduct equations.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Fix displayed objects $A$ and $B$. The hom row $\mathcal H(A,B)$ carries the
$\AbGroupUp$ enrichment row $\mathcal G(A,B)$, so its zero element is the
displayed zero morphism $0_{A,B}$ and its binary operation is the displayed
hom-addition row. The $\AbGroupUp$ classifier congruence field supplies the
transport of these rows under hom-classifier equivalence.

The biproduct row $\mathcal B(A,B)$ is a $\CategoryUp$ row: it supplies the
object $A\oplus B$, projections, injections, and the four equations expressed
by the category morphism classifier. The zero morphisms appearing in those
equations are the same $\mathcal Z$ rows selected by the hom
$\AbGroupUp$ zero fields.

Thus the additive part is not an extra semantic layer. The hom addition and
zero rows are the $\AbGroupUp$ visible operation rows, and the biproduct
typing and equations are the $\CategoryUp$ visible composition rows applied to
the displayed projections and injections.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[AbelianCatUp kernel-cokernel factorisation obligation]
\label{thm:abeliancat-kernel-cokernel-factorization-obligation}
For every visible morphism $f:A\to B$ in an $\AbelianCatUp$ additive
kernel-cokernel carrier, the package exposes kernel, cokernel, image, coimage,
canonical comparison, and epi--mono factorisation rows. The classified
factorisation of $f$ is obtained from these rows and no hidden quotient object
or external subobject is used.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AbelianCatUp.AbelianCatKernelCokernelCarrier\_factorization\_rows}
\begin{proof}
Project the kernel row $\mathcal K(f)$. It contains $\ker(f)$, the morphism
$k_f:\ker(f)\to A$, the zero-composite classifier for $f\circ k_f$, and the
factorisation ledger for every displayed morphism whose composite with $f$ is
classified as zero.

Project the cokernel row $\mathcal Q(f)$. It contains
$\operatorname{coker}(f)$, the morphism
$q_f:B\to\operatorname{coker}(f)$, the zero-composite classifier for
$q_f\circ f$, and the cofactorisation ledger for every displayed morphism out
of $B$ whose precomposition with $f$ is classified as zero.

The image, coimage, comparison, and epi--mono factorisation data are the
visible rows of $\mathcal F(f)$. Category composition stability types the
recomposition, while the factorisation classifier row identifies that
recomposition with $f$. Since every object and arrow used in the argument is a
projection of $\mathcal K(f)$, $\mathcal Q(f)$, or $\mathcal F(f)$, the proof
does not appeal to any object outside the displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ package.
\end{proof}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:abeliancat-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[AbelianCatUp NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:abeliancat-namecert-obligation-surface}
An $\AbelianCatUp$ additive kernel-cokernel carrier $\mathcal A$ satisfying
\autoref{thm:abeliancat-carrier-classifier-obligation},
\autoref{thm:abeliancat-additive-zero-biproduct-obligation}, and
\autoref{thm:abeliancat-kernel-cokernel-factorization-obligation} supplies the
following ten-field $\NameCert_{\AbelianCatUp}$ obligation surface.
\begin{enumerate}
  \item Traditional target: an abelian category with additive hom groups,
  binary biproducts, kernels, cokernels, and canonical epi--mono
  factorisations.
  \item BEDC source: the concrete $\mathsf{BHist}$ package of
  \autoref{def:abeliancat-additive-kernel-cokernel-carrier}.
  \item Carrier: the displayed object and morphism rows of the
  $\CategoryUp$ component together with the visible hom carriers.
  \item Classifier: the componentwise $\hsame$ classifier on objects,
  morphisms, hom elements, additive rows, kernel rows, cokernel rows, and
  factorisation rows.
  \item Stability obligations: $\CategoryUp$ composition and identity
  stability, $\AbGroupUp$ hom-addition stability, biproduct equation
  stability, and componentwise $\hsame$ transport.
  \item Ledger: source, hom, zero, addition, biproduct, kernel, cokernel,
  image, coimage, comparison, and epi--mono factorisation provenance rows.
  \item Core theorem list:
  \autoref{thm:abeliancat-carrier-classifier-obligation},
  \autoref{thm:abeliancat-additive-zero-biproduct-obligation}, and
  \autoref{thm:abeliancat-kernel-cokernel-factorization-obligation}.
  \item Exactness predicate: every visible morphism has displayed kernel,
  cokernel, image, coimage, comparison, and classified recomposition rows.
  \item Absent standard bridge: no external category of abelian groups,
  modules, sheaves, or complexes is identified here.
  \item Status: $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$ for a later Lean theorem in
  $\mathsf{BEDC.Derived.AbelianCatUp}$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier, classifier, and stability fields are projections of
\autoref{def:abeliancat-additive-kernel-cokernel-carrier}. The transport
requirement is exactly
\autoref{thm:abeliancat-carrier-classifier-obligation}, which repacks
componentwise $\hsame$ transport across the category, hom, additive, kernel,
cokernel, and factorisation rows.

The additive entries of the surface are supplied by
\autoref{thm:abeliancat-additive-zero-biproduct-obligation}. That theorem
identifies the hom addition and zero morphism rows with the
$\NameCert_{\AbGroupUp}$ visible operation rows and identifies the biproduct
typing and equations with the $\NameCert_{\CategoryUp}$ visible composition
rows.

The exactness and ledger entries are supplied by
\autoref{thm:abeliancat-kernel-cokernel-factorization-obligation}. For each
visible morphism it projects kernel, cokernel, image, coimage, comparison, and
recomposition rows from the package and records that no hidden quotient object
is used. These three projections assemble the stated ten-field
$\NameCert_{\AbelianCatUp}$ obligation surface.
\end{proof}
