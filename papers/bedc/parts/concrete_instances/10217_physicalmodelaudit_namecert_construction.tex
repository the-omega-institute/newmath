\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\PhysicalModelAuditUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-physical-model-audit-namecert}
\origin{ai}

The $\PhysicalModelAuditUp$ packet realizes the audit protocol of \autoref{def:physical-audit-certificate} as one finite BEDC naming certificate. It is adjacent to the audit-channel surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-largemodelauditchannel-namecert}, the context-audit surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-largemodelcontextaudit-namecert}, the state-space model surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-statespacemodel-namecert}, the observer-budget support surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observer-budget-support-namecert}, the ontic-state surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-onticstate-namecert}, and the reality-constrained audit packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-reality-constrained-model-audit-namecert}. Its role is to bind source traces, target phenomenon, observed and model signatures, classifier, tower, stability and descent rows, ledger, failure surface, strength, transports, routes, provenance, and local NameCert before a physical claim is read as accepted.

\begin{definition}[PhysicalModelAudit carrier]
\label{def:physical-model-audit-carrier}
A $\PhysicalModelAuditUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
A=(Q,R,O,M,C,T,Y,D,L,F,S,H,U,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $Q$ is the finite source-trace row, recording the histories or experimental traces consumed by the audit.
\item $R$ is the target-phenomenon row, naming the physical system, phenomenon, or class of systems under audit.
\item $O$ is the observed-signature row extracted from $Q$ through the declared observation bundle.
\item $M$ is the model-signature row generated by the candidate model for the same bundle.
\item $C$ is the physical-audit classifier comparing $O$ and $M$ at the declared fit level.
\item $T$ is the approximation-tower row from source traces to model-facing signatures.
\item $Y$ is the stability row specifying the licensed reuse domain for the audited comparison.
\item $D$ is the descent row recording which predictive or explanatory operations survive the tower.
\item $L$ is the audit ledger for provenance, hidden compression, representation, idealization, and translation material.
\item $F$ is the failure-surface row naming finite defeating observations or signature mismatches.
\item $S$ is the audit-strength row, distinguishing seed support, paper fit, reproduced fit, and bridged fit.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for all content rows.
\item $U$ records displayed $\Cont$ routes through source, phenomenon, signature, classifier, tower, stability, descent, ledger, failure, and strength rows.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\PhysicalModelAuditUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\PhysicalModelAuditUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no host-level truth oracle, unbounded observation stream, unledgered compression, detached model equivalence, or physical acceptance route outside the displayed packet.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[PhysicalModelAudit classifier]
\label{def:physical-model-audit-classifier}
For accepted packets
$$
A=(Q,R,O,M,C,T,Y,D,L,F,S,H,U,P,N)
\quad\hbox{and}\quad
A'=(Q',R',O',M',C',T',Y',D',L',F',S',H',U',P',N'),
$$
the predicate $\mathsf{PhysicalModelAuditClassifier}(A,A')$ holds when $H$ and $H'$ transport source traces, target phenomena, observed signatures, model signatures, approximation towers, stability rows, descent rows, ledger rows, failure surfaces, strength rows, routes, provenance, and local names componentwise, and when $C$ and $C'$ preserve the same declared comparison between $O,M$ and $O',M'$.

Classifier agreement is therefore stronger than model-syntax agreement and weaker than host-level physical truth. It is the finite assertion that the same audit comparison is read through the same displayed source-signature-classifier-tower-ledger packet.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[PhysicalModelAudit field exhaustion]
\label{thm:physical-model-audit-field-exhaustion}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:physical-model-audit-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\PhysicalModelAuditUp}$ surface is exhausted by the following six rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for $A=(Q,R,O,M,C,T,Y,D,L,F,S,H,U,P,N)$;
\item source and target admission through $Q$ and $R$;
\item signature and classifier admission through $O,M,C$;
\item tower, stability, and descent admission through $T,Y,D$;
\item ledger, failure-surface, and strength admission through $L,F,S$;
\item componentwise transport, displayed routes, provenance, and local naming through $H,U,P,N$.
\end{enumerate}
No accepted physical-audit claim through this packet can read a field outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is the projection of the displayed tuple in \autoref{def:physical-model-audit-carrier}. The packet lists the source row, target row, signature rows, classifier row, tower row, stability row, descent row, ledger row, failure row, strength row, and the structural transport, route, provenance, and naming rows.

The classifier of \autoref{def:physical-model-audit-classifier} reads only those rows. Its comparison is not a separate model oracle; it is the relation between observed and model signatures as transported and replayed inside the same packet.

The ledger and failure-surface rows close the remaining escape points. Any compression, idealization, translation, or possible defeat used by the audit must be named by $L$ or $F$, and the evidential level must be named by $S$.

Thus the local $\NameCert$ has no further coordinate to expose. A consumer that asks for another source, signature, classifier, tower, ledger, failure surface, or strength row has left the accepted $\PhysicalModelAuditUp$ carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PhysicalModelAudit real-route non-escape]
\label{thm:physical-model-audit-real-route-nonescape}
An accepted $\PhysicalModelAuditUp$ route cannot turn a model-signature comparison into a physical acceptance claim unless the source-trace row, target-phenomenon row, observed-signature row, model-signature row, classifier row, tower row, stability row, descent row, ledger row, failure-surface row, and strength row are all present in the same finite packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with field exhaustion in \autoref{thm:physical-model-audit-field-exhaustion}. The accepted route factors through the displayed tuple
$$
Q,R,O,M,C,T,Y,D,L,F,S,H,U,P,N.
$$

The real-facing part of the route is not the model signature $M$ alone. It also requires the source traces $Q$, target phenomenon $R$, observed signatures $O$, classifier $C$, tower $T$, stability and descent rows $Y,D$, ledger $L$, failure surface $F$, and strength row $S$.

If any one of those rows is absent, the route can still describe syntax or a partial finite comparison, but it cannot project the local physical acceptance name. The missing row is outside the carrier, so the corresponding physical claim is not accepted by this NameCert packet.
\end{proof}
