The $\PhysicalModelAuditUp$ packet realizes the audit protocol of \autoref{def:physical-audit-certificate} as one finite BEDC naming certificate. It is adjacent to the audit-channel surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-largemodelauditchannel-namecert}, the trace-storage surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-modeltrace-namecert}, the state-space model surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-statespacemodel-namecert}, the ontic-state surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-onticstate-namecert}, and the observation-reflection routing surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observationreflectionpacket-namecert}. Its role is to bind source traces, target phenomenon, observed and model signatures, classifier, tower, stability and descent rows, ledger, failure surface, strength, transports, routes, provenance, and local NameCert before a physical claim is read as accepted.

\begin{definition}[PhysicalModelAudit carrier]
\label{def:physical-model-audit-bhist-carrier}
A $\PhysicalModelAuditUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
A=(Q,R,O,M,C,T,Y,D,L,F,S,H,U,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $Q$ is the finite source-trace row, recording the histories or experimental traces consumed by the audit.
\item $R$ is the target-phenomenon row, naming the physical system, phenomenon, or class of systems under audit.
\item $O$ is the observed-signature row extracted from $Q$ through the declared observation bundle.
\item $M$ is the model-signature row generated by the candidate model for the same bundle.
\item $C$ is the physical-audit classifier comparing $O$ and $M$ at the declared fit level.
\item $T$ is the approximation-tower row from source traces to model-facing signatures.
\item $Y$ is the stability row specifying the licensed reuse domain for the audited comparison.
\item $D$ is the descent row recording which predictive or explanatory operations survive the tower.
\item $L$ is the audit ledger for provenance, hidden compression, representation, idealization, and translation material.
\item $F$ is the failure-surface row naming finite defeating observations or signature mismatches.
\item $S$ is the audit-strength row, distinguishing seed support, paper fit, reproduced fit, and bridged fit.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for all content rows.
\item $U$ records displayed $\Cont$ routes through source, phenomenon, signature, classifier, tower, stability, descent, ledger, failure, and strength rows.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\PhysicalModelAuditUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\PhysicalModelAuditUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no host-level truth oracle, unbounded observation stream, unledgered compression, detached model equivalence, or physical acceptance route outside the displayed packet.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[PhysicalModelAudit classifier]
\label{def:physical-model-audit-classifier}
For accepted packets
$$
A=(Q,R,O,M,C,T,Y,D,L,F,S,H,U,P,N)
\quad\hbox{and}\quad
A'=(Q',R',O',M',C',T',Y',D',L',F',S',H',U',P',N'),
$$
the predicate $\mathsf{PhysicalModelAuditClassifier}(A,A')$ holds when $H$ and $H'$ transport source traces, target phenomena, observed signatures, model signatures, approximation towers, stability rows, descent rows, ledger rows, failure surfaces, strength rows, routes, provenance, and local names componentwise, and when $C$ and $C'$ preserve the same declared comparison between $O,M$ and $O',M'$.

Classifier agreement is therefore stronger than model-syntax agreement and weaker than host-level physical truth. It is the finite assertion that the same audit comparison is read through the same displayed source-signature-classifier-tower-ledger packet.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[PhysicalModelAudit NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:physical-model-audit-namecert-obligation-surface}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:physical-model-audit-bhist-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\PhysicalModelAuditUp}$ surface is exhausted by the following five obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item source admission: $Q$ and $R$ are the only source-trace and target-phenomenon rows licensed for the audit;
\item signature admission: $O$ and $M$ are the observed and model-signature rows read by the packet;
\item classifier admission: $C$ is the finite comparison row relating $O$ and $M$ at the declared fit level;
\item tower, stability, and descent admission: $T,Y,D$ are the displayed approximation, reuse, and operation-survival rows;
\item ledger, failure, and strength admission: $L,F,S$ record provenance material, defeating observations, and the evidential grade before the local name is accepted.
\end{enumerate}
No accepted physical-audit claim through this packet can read a field outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is the projection of the displayed tuple in \autoref{def:physical-model-audit-bhist-carrier}. The packet lists the source row, target row, signature rows, classifier row, tower row, stability row, descent row, ledger row, failure row, strength row, and the structural transport, route, provenance, and naming rows.

The classifier of \autoref{def:physical-model-audit-classifier} reads only those rows. Its comparison is not a separate model oracle; it is the relation between observed and model signatures as transported and replayed inside the same packet.

The ledger and failure-surface rows close the remaining escape points. Any compression, idealization, translation, or possible defeat used by the audit must be named by $L$ or $F$, and the evidential level must be named by $S$.

Thus the local $\NameCert$ has no further coordinate to expose. A consumer that asks for another source, signature, classifier, tower, ledger, failure surface, or strength row has left the accepted $\PhysicalModelAuditUp$ carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PhysicalModelAudit acceptance non-escape]
\label{thm:physical-model-audit-acceptance-nonescape}
An accepted $\PhysicalModelAuditUp$ route cannot turn a model-signature comparison into a physical acceptance claim unless the source-trace row, target-phenomenon row, observed-signature row, model-signature row, classifier row, tower row, stability row, descent row, ledger row, failure-surface row, and strength row are all present in the same finite packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the obligation surface in \autoref{thm:physical-model-audit-namecert-obligation-surface}. The accepted route factors through the displayed tuple
$$
Q,R,O,M,C,T,Y,D,L,F,S,H,U,P,N.
$$

The real-facing part of the route is not the model signature $M$ alone. It also requires the source traces $Q$, target phenomenon $R$, observed signatures $O$, classifier $C$, tower $T$, stability and descent rows $Y,D$, ledger $L$, failure surface $F$, and strength row $S$.

If any one of those rows is absent, the route can still describe syntax or a partial finite comparison, but it cannot project the local physical acceptance name. The missing row is outside the carrier, so the corresponding physical claim is not accepted by this NameCert packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PhysicalModelAudit ledger failure strength separation]
\label{thm:physical-model-audit-ledger-failure-strength-separation}
For every accepted packet
$$
A=(Q,R,O,M,C,T,Y,D,L,F,S,H,U,P,N),
$$
the ledger row $L$, failure row $F$, and strength row $S$ are separate audit coordinates. The ledger row records provenance, compression, representation, idealization, and translation material; the failure row records finite defeating observations or signature mismatches; the strength row records the evidential grade licensed by the accepted route. None of the three rows can be reconstructed from either of the other two without leaving the displayed carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier in \autoref{def:physical-model-audit-bhist-carrier}. It lists $L$, $F$, and $S$ as distinct rows of the same finite packet, with $L$ assigned to audit material, $F$ assigned to defeating observations or mismatches, and $S$ assigned to evidential strength.

Apply the classifier in \autoref{def:physical-model-audit-classifier}. The classifier transports ledger rows, failure surfaces, and strength rows componentwise; it does not collapse them into the observed signature, model signature, or comparison row.

Finally use the NameCert obligation surface and the acceptance non-escape theorem. \autoref{thm:physical-model-audit-namecert-obligation-surface} places ledger, failure, and strength admission in the local certificate, while \autoref{thm:physical-model-audit-acceptance-nonescape} requires all three rows before a physical acceptance name can be projected. Hence an accepted audit cannot substitute ledger material for a defeat surface, a defeat surface for evidential strength, or strength for either of the audit rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PhysicalModelAudit route strength determinacy]
\label{thm:physical-model-audit-route-strength-determinacy}
For an accepted packet
$$
A=(Q,R,O,M,C,T,Y,D,L,F,S,H,U,P,N),
$$
the audit strength row $S$ is determined only by the displayed source, signature, classifier, tower, stability, descent, ledger, and failure route
$$
Q,R,O,M,C,T,Y,D,L,F,H,U,P,N.
$$
No host physical truth oracle, detached model equivalence, unbounded observation stream, or hidden validation route can determine the accepted audit strength outside the displayed packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the carrier first. \autoref{def:physical-model-audit-bhist-carrier} places $S$ in the same finite tuple as the source trace $Q$, target phenomenon $R$, observed and model signatures $O,M$, classifier $C$, tower $T$, stability and descent rows $Y,D$, ledger $L$, failure surface $F$, and structural rows $H,U,P,N$.

The classifier fixes the comparison route. By \autoref{def:physical-model-audit-classifier}, accepted comparison is a relation between $O,M$ and transported copies of the same displayed rows; it is not a separate model oracle.

Use the obligation surface to bind the strength source. \autoref{thm:physical-model-audit-namecert-obligation-surface} admits $S$ only together with the source, signature, classifier, tower, stability, descent, ledger, and failure rows. Then \autoref{thm:physical-model-audit-acceptance-nonescape} blocks a physical acceptance name whenever any of those rows is absent.

Therefore the accepted strength is determined by the displayed route and by no hidden host evidence. A consumer that changes $S$ without changing the listed source, signature, classifier, tower, stability, descent, ledger, failure, transport, route, provenance, or naming rows has left the accepted $\PhysicalModelAuditUp$ packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PhysicalModelAudit failure surface exhaustion]
\label{thm:physical-model-audit-failure-surface-exhaustion}
For an accepted packet
$$
A=(Q,R,O,M,C,T,Y,D,L,F,S,H,U,P,N),
$$
every defeating observation, signature mismatch, reuse failure, descent failure, or audit-level objection available to the physical model audit must appear through the failure row $F$ and its displayed transports through $H,U,P,N$. No hidden host observation, unledgered mismatch, or detached failure witness can defeat the accepted route outside $F$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the displayed packet. The carrier of \autoref{def:physical-model-audit-bhist-carrier} assigns finite defeating observations and signature mismatches to $F$, while $Q,R,O,M,C,T,Y,D,L,S$ record the source, phenomenon, signatures, classifier, tower, stability, descent, ledger, and strength rows.

The classifier does not add a second failure store. By \autoref{def:physical-model-audit-classifier}, mismatch information is compared through the transported observed and model signatures together with the failure surface already present in the packet.

Apply \autoref{thm:physical-model-audit-namecert-obligation-surface}. The local NameCert has a ledger, failure, and strength admission row, and its failure coordinate is the only named surface for defeating observations or signature mismatches.

Finally use \autoref{thm:physical-model-audit-acceptance-nonescape}. Acceptance requires $F$ in the same finite packet as the source, signature, classifier, tower, stability, descent, ledger, and strength rows. Thus any alleged defeat that does not enter through $F$ is not a defeating observation of the accepted $\PhysicalModelAuditUp$ route; it is outside the carrier rather than hidden host evidence.
\end{proof}
