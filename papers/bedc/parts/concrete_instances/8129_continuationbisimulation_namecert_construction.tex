\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ContinuationBisimulationUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-continuation-bisimulation-namecert}
\origin{ai}

The $\ContinuationBisimulationUp$ packet realizes the behavioral-equivalence direction of \autoref{ch:visions-computation-as-continuation} as a finite paired-simulation certificate. It is adjacent to the small-step trace packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuationtrace-namecert}, the terminal-read packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuationbigstep-namecert}, and the bounded evaluator packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finitetraceevaluator-namecert}, but it stores a different public surface: two endpoint traces together with forward and backward simulation rows over the same endpoints.

\falsifiablePrediction{An accepted $\ContinuationBisimulationUp$ packet with a forward simulation row but no backward simulation row fails the local NameCert obligations.}

\independenceWitness{continuationtrace, continuationbigstep, continuationtracenormalform, continuationautomaton, finitetraceevaluator: none is bijective to $\ContinuationBisimulationUp$ because those siblings record traces, terminal reads, normal forms, automata, or bounded evaluator rows, while this carrier requires paired forward and backward simulation rows over the same endpoints.}

\begin{definition}[ContinuationBisimulation carrier]
\label{def:continuation-bisimulation-carrier}
A $\ContinuationBisimulationUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Z=(L,R,F,B,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $L$ is the left finite $\ContinuationTraceUp$ ledger, including its source, mark-and-tail, target, and trace rows;
\item $R$ is the right finite $\ContinuationTraceUp$ ledger over the comparison endpoint;
\item $F$ is the forward simulation row, reading each visible left trace step as a right trace step with matching endpoint classifier;
\item $B$ is the backward simulation row, reading each visible right trace step as a left trace step with matching endpoint classifier;
\item $E$ is the endpoint classifier row comparing the two source endpoints and the two target endpoints after the paired simulations have been read;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the two trace ledgers, the forward and backward simulation rows, endpoint classifiers, replay rows, provenance, and local naming;
\item $C$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ replay routes used to replay the left trace, the right trace, the forward simulation, the backward simulation, and the endpoint reads;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over \autoref{ch:visions-computation-as-continuation}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuationtrace-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuationbigstep-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finitetraceevaluator-namecert}, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ContinuationBisimulationUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exposes a symmetric finite behavioral-equivalence packet. It contains no host evaluator, quotient behaviour class, unbounded trace semantics, terminal-result selector, or one-sided simulation shortcut.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[ContinuationBisimulation NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:continuation-bisimulation-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\ContinuationBisimulationUp}$ surface for the carrier of \autoref{def:continuation-bisimulation-carrier} consists of seven finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed packet $Z=(L,R,F,B,E,H,C,P,N)$;
\item left trace admission for $L$ through the finite $\ContinuationTraceUp$ rows;
\item right trace admission for $R$ through the finite $\ContinuationTraceUp$ rows;
\item forward simulation exactness, requiring every visible step read from $L$ to be matched by a displayed step read from $R$ through $F$;
\item backward simulation exactness, requiring every visible step read from $R$ to be matched by a displayed step read from $L$ through $B$;
\item endpoint-classifier stability through $E,H,C$ for the two source endpoints and the two target endpoints;
\item non-escape, stating that every accepted behavioral-equivalence read factors through $L,R,F,B,E,H,C,P,N$ and cannot consult a host evaluator, quotient behaviour class, unbounded trace semantics, terminal-result selector, or one-sided simulation row.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:continuation-bisimulation-carrier}. The accepted packet displays exactly the two trace ledgers, the two simulation rows, the endpoint classifier, the transport row, the replay row, provenance, and the local naming row.

Read the two trace obligations through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuationtrace-namecert}. The rows $L$ and $R$ are finite continuation traces, so their visible steps are source, mark-and-tail, target, trace-ledger, and replay reads rather than host-level transition facts.

The simulation obligations are then paired. The forward row $F$ accounts for every visible left step by a right-step read, and the backward row $B$ accounts for every visible right step by a left-step read. Since both rows are coordinates of the same packet, the local NameCert can reject a one-sided packet before endpoint equivalence is exported.

Finally, $E,H,C,P,N$ provide endpoint comparison, componentwise transport, replay, provenance, and local naming for the paired route. No coordinate remains for a quotient behaviour class, an evaluator graph, an unbounded trace semantics, a terminal-result selector, or a hidden one-sided simulation, so the seven obligations exhaust the packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ContinuationBisimulation non-escape]
\label{thm:continuation-bisimulation-nonescape}
Every accepted $\ContinuationBisimulationUp$ consumer route factors through the displayed finite rows
$$
L,R,F,B,E,H,C,P,N.
$$
In particular, a behavioral-equivalence consumer must read both the forward simulation row $F$ and the backward simulation row $B$ before the endpoint classifier $E$ can be exported. The route cannot be replaced by a host evaluator, a quotient of behaviours, an automaton-language equivalence, a bounded evaluator verdict, or a terminal-only big-step read.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the carrier of \autoref{def:continuation-bisimulation-carrier}. The only trace data available to a consumer are the two finite ledgers $L$ and $R$, and the only simulation data are the displayed rows $F$ and $B$.

Use the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:continuation-bisimulation-namecert-obligations}. They force left-step reads through $L$, right-step reads through $R$, forward matching through $F$, backward matching through $B$, and endpoint comparison through $E,H,C$.

The surrounding siblings keep their own surfaces. \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuationbigstep-namecert} reads terminal rows, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finitetraceevaluator-namecert} records bounded evaluator rows, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuationautomaton-namecert} records automaton transition rows. None supplies the missing opposite simulation row for this packet.

Therefore every accepted behavioral-equivalence read remains inside $L,R,F,B,E,H,C,P,N$. Removing either $F$ or $B$ changes the local NameCert surface and leaves no route by which the endpoint classifier may export symmetric equivalence.
\end{proof}
