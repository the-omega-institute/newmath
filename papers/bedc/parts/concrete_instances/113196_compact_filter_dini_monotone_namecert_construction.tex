\begin{definition}[Compact filter Dini monotone carrier]
\label{def:compact-filter-dini-monotone-carrier}
A $\CompactFilterDiniMonotoneUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
D=(K,F,\epsilon,\rho,M,T,R,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $K$ is the $\CompactMetricUp$ source row, exposing only compact finite-cover and finite-net data.
\item $F$ is the finite $\ContinuousMapUp$ family row, indexed by the displayed filter stages over the same compact source.
\item $\epsilon$ is the positive tolerance request, stored before any cover or threshold row is exported.
\item $\rho$ is the shared radius or cover-budget row obtained from the compact-source and continuous-family ledgers.
\item $M$ is the monotone-filter ledger, recording the displayed comparison that later stages stay below earlier stages at the requested tolerance.
\item $T$ is the finite threshold row, giving the cover-facing stage after which every displayed center satisfies the tolerance boundary.
\item $R$ records regular rational readback through $\RegSeqRatUp$ and the permitted real-name endpoint through $\RealUp$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CompactFilterDiniMonotoneUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\UniformModulusUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.

\end{enumerate}

\begin{theorem}[Compact filter Dini monotone obligations]
\label{thm:compact-filter-dini-monotone-obligations}
For every accepted $\CompactFilterDiniMonotoneUp$ carrier
$$
D=(K,F,\epsilon,\rho,M,T,R,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\CompactFilterDiniMonotoneUp}$ surface consists exactly of compact-source admission through $K$, continuous-family admission through $F$, tolerance admission through $\epsilon$, shared radius or cover-budget admission through $\rho$, monotone-filter admission through $M$, finite threshold admission through $T$, regular rational and real-name readback through $R$, provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:compact-filter-dini-monotone-carrier}. The analytic rows are $K,F,\epsilon,\rho,M,T,R$, and the structural rows are $P,N$.

The compact and continuity obligations are exactly $K$ and $F$. The row $K$ supplies the compact finite-cover data, while $F$ supplies the continuous family over that same source.

The Dini monotone obligations are $\epsilon,\rho,M,T$. The tolerance is fixed first, $\rho$ records the shared compact-cover budget, $M$ records monotone filter comparison, and $T$ records the finite threshold stage visible to downstream consumers.

The readback and naming obligations are $R,P,N$. Since the carrier contains no host-selected limit, selected subcover, ambient compactness, quotient real equality, countable choice, or external filter-convergence coordinate, the listed rows are the whole accepted NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Compact filter Dini monotone finite-cover route]
\label{thm:compact-filter-dini-monotone-finite-cover-route}
For every accepted $\CompactFilterDiniMonotoneUp$ carrier, any consumer of the Dini finite-cover conclusion must route its read through
$$
K,F,\epsilon \longmapsto \rho,M,T \longmapsto R,P,N .
$$
Thus the compactness used by the packet is the displayed finite-cover route over $\CompactMetricUp$ and $\ContinuousMapUp$, not an ambient open-cover principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier rows. The compact source $K$, continuous family $F$, and tolerance $\epsilon$ are the only rows visible before the cover budget and threshold rows.

The middle stage is $\rho,M,T$. The row $\rho$ stores the shared radius or cover budget, $M$ stores monotone filter comparison, and $T$ stores the finite threshold stage after all displayed cover centers satisfy the requested boundary.

The final stage is $R,P,N$. Regular rational and real-name readback can inspect only the routed threshold data, and provenance and local naming preserve that same route. Therefore an accepted consumer cannot replace the route by an arbitrary open-cover compactness argument or by a selected subcover outside the packet.
\end{proof}
