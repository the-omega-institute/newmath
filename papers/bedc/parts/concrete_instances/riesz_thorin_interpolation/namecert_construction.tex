Following Riesz, Thorin, and Stein-Weiss, the $\RieszThorinInterpolationUp$ packet records the finite interpolation certificate by which two endpoint bounded-operator rows and a Hadamard three-lines strip estimate expose one interpolated norm bound. It sits after the strip-bound packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-hadamard-three-lines-namecert}, the endpoint normed-space surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-normedspace-namecert}, the bounded-operator row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-boundedlinearoperator-namecert}, the interpolation carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-interpolation-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The chapter names the displayed certificate only; it does not import a host interpolation functor, quotient operator equality, selected extremal analytic family, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[Riesz-Thorin interpolation carrier]
\label{def:riesz-thorin-interpolation-carrier}
A $\RieszThorinInterpolationUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
T=(X_0,X_1,Y_0,Y_1,A_0,A_1,E_0,E_1,S,\Theta,B,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X_0,X_1,Y_0,Y_1$ are endpoint $\NormedSpaceUp$ rows for the source and target normed spaces at the two endpoints.
\item $A_0,A_1$ are endpoint $\BoundedLinearOperatorUp$ rows, recording the same displayed operator action with endpoint norm bounds over the corresponding normed-space rows.
\item $E_0,E_1$ are endpoint exponent rows, storing the source and target exponent ledgers and their rational comparison data.
\item $S$ is the $\HadamardThreeLinesUp$ strip-estimate row used to compare the endpoint bounds along the displayed interpolation strip.
\item $\Theta$ is the interpolation-parameter row, storing the inspected parameter, its endpoint incidence, and the convex-weight ledger consumed by $S$.
\item $B$ is the $\RealUp$ norm-bound ledger exporting the interpolated bound only after $A_0,A_1,E_0,E_1,S,\Theta$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for endpoint normed spaces, bounded operators, exponent rows, strip estimate, interpolation parameter, norm-bound ledger, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from an interpolated-bound request through $X_0,X_1,Y_0,Y_1,A_0,A_1,E_0,E_1,S,\Theta,B,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RieszThorinInterpolationUp$, $\HadamardThreeLinesUp$, $\NormedSpaceUp$, $\BoundedLinearOperatorUp$, $\InterpolationUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RieszThorinInterpolationUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier compares accepted packets only through these displayed rows. It has no coordinate for a host interpolation space, quotient equality of operators, selected extremal analytic family, non-displayed exponent completion, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Riesz-Thorin interpolation NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:riesz-thorin-interpolation-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RieszThorinInterpolationUp$ carrier
$$
T=(X_0,X_1,Y_0,Y_1,A_0,A_1,E_0,E_1,S,\Theta,B,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\RieszThorinInterpolationUp}$ surface consists exactly of endpoint normed-space admission through $X_0,X_1,Y_0,Y_1$, bounded-operator endpoint admission through $A_0,A_1$, endpoint exponent admission through $E_0,E_1$, Hadamard three-lines strip-estimate compatibility through $S$, interpolation-parameter admission through $\Theta$, interpolated $\RealUp$ norm-bound export through $B$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:riesz-thorin-interpolation-carrier}. The rows $X_0,X_1,Y_0,Y_1$ provide the endpoint normed-space surfaces, and $A_0,A_1$ are the only bounded-operator coordinates available to the classifier.

The exponent and strip data are then forced by row order. The endpoint exponent rows $E_0,E_1$ and the interpolation parameter $\Theta$ determine the displayed convex weights, while the $\HadamardThreeLinesUp$ row $S$ is the sole strip-estimate route that can compare endpoint bounds.

The bound row $B$ reads only after those endpoint and strip rows are displayed. It exports the interpolated $\RealUp$ norm-bound ledger, not a selected analytic family or a host interpolation space.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite packet. Since every excluded host object lacks a carrier coordinate, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}
