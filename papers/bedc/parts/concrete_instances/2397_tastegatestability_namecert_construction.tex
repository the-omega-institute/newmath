The $\TasteGateStabilityUp$ packet turns the TasteGate discipline of \autoref{ch:visions-theory-stability-taste-gate} into a finite NameCert carrier. It records the proposed chapter site, displayed carrier surface, $\ChapterTasteGate$ obligations, ground-compiler witness rows, admission ledger, componentwise transports, replay routes, package provenance, and the local boundary that prevents a TasteGate pass from becoming hidden semantic approval.

The stability packet consumes the candidate-admission surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-tastegateadmission-namecert} and the compiler-witness route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-tastegatecompilerwitness-namecert}: admission supplies the candidate row being tested, while the compiler witness supplies the ground-compiler evidence read by $W$ and replayed through $C$.

\begin{definition}[TasteGateStability carrier]
\label{def:taste-gate-stability-carrier}
A $\TasteGateStabilityUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(X,K,T,W,A,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the candidate chapter identity row, recording the chapter label, origin row, and concrete-instance site;
\item $K$ is the candidate NameCert carrier row, exposing the finite source, classifier, pattern, ledger, and non-escape surface proposed by the chapter;
\item $T$ is the $\ChapterTasteGate$ row, displaying conservativity, no-hidden-input, round-trip, and layer-separation obligations for the same candidate;
\item $W$ is the ground-compiler witness row, listing the finite event-flow image and named compiler facts used to read the no-hidden-input, round-trip, and source-channel separation gates;
\item $A$ is the admission ledger, recording accepted and refused TasteGate reads for the displayed candidate surface;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $X,K,T,W,A,C,P,N$;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes that replay the candidate carrier, TasteGate row, ground-compiler witnesses, and admission ledger;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, $\NameCert$, $\ChapterTasteGate$, ground-compiler witnesses, admission ledgers, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and local naming;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\TasteGateStabilityUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The packet contains no hidden prompt state, no host-side theory approval, no oracle classifier, no unlisted compiler theorem, and no global horizon admission rule outside the displayed rows.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[TasteGateStability NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:taste-gate-stability-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\TasteGateStabilityUp$ carrier
$$
S=(X,K,T,W,A,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\TasteGateStabilityUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $S$, candidate identity through $X$, candidate NameCert exposure through $K$, $\ChapterTasteGate$ exposure through $T$, ground-compiler witness exposure through $W$, admission-ledger accounting through $A$, componentwise transport through $H$, replay routes through $C$, package provenance through $P$, and the local naming row $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:taste-gate-stability-carrier}. The packet exposes exactly the candidate identity, candidate carrier, TasteGate obligations, ground-compiler witness rows, admission ledger, transport rows, replay routes, provenance, and local naming row.

The internal NameCert surface is the row $K$. It is read as a displayed candidate carrier, classifier, pattern, ledger, and non-escape surface, not as an ambient guarantee that the candidate has already joined the horizon.

The TasteGate surface is the row $T$, while the compiler-facing evidence is the row $W$. These rows keep chapter-level stability claims tied to the finite compiler witnesses that display no-hidden-input, round-trip, and layer-separation checks.

Finally $A,H,C,P,N$ account for accepted and refused reads, transport the same rows, replay them for consumers, record provenance, and name the packet locally. No other NameCert obligation is exposed by the carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[TasteGateStability ground-compiler witness route]
\label{thm:taste-gate-stability-ground-compiler-witness-route}
Every accepted $\TasteGateStabilityUp$ route that reads the $\ChapterTasteGate$ row $T$ must pass through the ground-compiler witness row $W$ and the replay routes in $C$. The route may report the displayed no-hidden-input, round-trip, and layer-separation gates for the candidate, but it cannot cite an unlisted compiler theorem or an external semantic judgment.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:taste-gate-stability-carrier}. The only row that carries chapter-level TasteGate obligations is $T$, and the only row that records compiler evidence for those obligations is $W$.

The replay routes in $C$ bind the read of $T$ to the displayed witness row $W$. Thus a consumer cannot detach a no-hidden-input, round-trip, or layer-separation claim from the finite event-flow image and named compiler facts that support it.

Since the carrier has no coordinate for an unlisted compiler theorem or external semantic judgment, every accepted TasteGate route remains inside the displayed witness rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[TasteGateStability non-escape boundary]
\label{thm:taste-gate-stability-non-escape-boundary}
No consumer of an accepted $\TasteGateStabilityUp$ packet can extract hidden prompt state, host-side theory approval, an oracle classifier, an unlisted compiler theorem, or a global horizon admission rule. Every consumer read factors through
$$
(X,K,T,W,A,H,C,P,N).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier inventory. Its coordinates are the candidate identity row, candidate NameCert carrier row, TasteGate row, ground-compiler witness row, admission ledger, transport rows, replay routes, package provenance, and local naming row.

The transport and continuation rows preserve exactly those coordinates. They can replay a displayed candidate surface and its TasteGate evidence, but they do not introduce host approval, hidden prompt state, oracle classification, or an additional compiler fact.

Therefore the forbidden objects have no projection target in the packet. Any consumer read seeking one of them has to leave the displayed rows, so it is not a read of the accepted $\TasteGateStabilityUp$ carrier.
\end{proof}
