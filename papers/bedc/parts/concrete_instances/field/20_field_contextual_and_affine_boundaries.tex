\begin{theorem}[Field affine automorphism cancellation exactness]
\label{thm:field-affine-automorphism-cancellation-exactness}
Under the same hypotheses and notation as
\autoref{thm:field-affine-automorphism-cancellation}, for certified affine
automorphism histories $S,T,U$ in $\mathsf{AffAut}_F$, cancellation by the
common affine factor is exact on both sides:
$$
(S\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}T)\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}(U\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}T)
\Longleftrightarrow S\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}U,
$$
and
$$
(T\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}S)\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}(T\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}U)
\Longleftrightarrow S\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}U.
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the forward directions, apply
\autoref{thm:field-affine-automorphism-cancellation}. Its first clause cancels
the common right factor $T$, and its second clause cancels the common left
factor $T$. In both cases the resulting classifier is precisely
$S\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}U$.

For the reverse directions, assume $S\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}U$. The common
factor $T$ is reflexively related to itself by the affine pointwise classifier.
Apply
\autoref{thm:field-affine-pointwise-classifier-operation-congruence} to the
classifier between $S$ and $U$ together with reflexivity of $T$. With the common
factor placed on the right this gives
$(S\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}T)\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}(U\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}T)$; with
the common factor placed on the left it gives
$(T\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}S)\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}(T\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}U)$.
\end{proof}
