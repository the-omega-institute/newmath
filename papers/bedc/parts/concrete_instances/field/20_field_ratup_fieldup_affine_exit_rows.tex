\begin{theorem}[RatUp FieldUp affine automorphism cancellation exactness exit]
\label{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-automorphism-cancellation-exactness-exit}
For Definition~\ref{def:ratup-fieldup-concrete-exit-object}, let
$S,T,U:\mathsf{AffAut}_F$ be certified affine automorphism histories in the
concrete $\FieldUp(\RatUp)$ exit object. With the concrete affine composition
$\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}$ and classifier $\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}$ supplied by
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-automorphism-action-certificate}, common
right affine factors cancel exactly:
$$
(S\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}T)\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}(U\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}T)
\Longleftrightarrow S\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}U.
$$
Common left affine factors cancel exactly:
$$
(T\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}S)\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}(T\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}U)
\Longleftrightarrow S\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}U.
$$
Thus affine cancellation exactness is a concrete row of the canonical
$\FieldUp(\RatUp)$ exit object, not an external schematic field parameter.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First fix the concrete exit object by
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-concrete-exit-object-complete}. This anchors the
carrier $\mathsf{BHist}$, the source $\mathsf{RatUpFieldSource}_F$, and the
operation rows used by the affine automorphism certificate.

Next apply
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-automorphism-action-certificate}. It
identifies the affine carrier $\mathsf{AffAut}_F$, composition
$\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}$, identity, inverse, and classifier
$\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}$ as rows exported by the same concrete exit object.

Finally apply
\autoref{thm:field-affine-automorphism-cancellation-exactness}. Read its
classifier as the concrete affine classifier just identified, and read its two
common-factor cancellation clauses as the displayed right and left
equivalences.
\end{proof}
