\begin{theorem}[RatUp FieldUp semantic certificate assembly]
\label{thm:ratup-fieldup-semantic-certificate-assembly}
For the concrete $\mathsf{BHist}$ data
$\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}$, $\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$,
$\mathsf{RatDenomUnitCarrier}$, $\mathsf{RatDenomUnitClassifier}$,
$\mathsf{append}$, $\Cont$, $\emp$, and strict support
$\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}$, the row-location invariant of
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-certificate-rows-source-anchored} reads the
$\CommRingUp$ base coordinate, the FieldUp-specific inverse and apart-zero rows,
the two-sided zero-product and nonzero-product rows, the denominator
multiplicative rows, the denominator-action envelope rows, contextual
denominator rows, the denominator support-neutrality rows, the denominator
unit-pair support-neutrality row, the denominator context-pair action rows, the
context-pair strict-support commutation row, and
the packaged affine $\GroupUp$ consequence over the same concrete
$\mathsf{BHist}$ source. These rows supply the carrier, source, pattern,
classifier, stability, ledger, and exactness rows of
\autoref{def:field-certificate-obligations} for the concrete target
$\FieldUp(\mathsf{RatUp})$. More explicitly,
Definition~\ref{def:concrete-ratup-fieldup-certificate-instance} supplies the
concrete $\FieldUp(\mathsf{RatUp})$ certificate rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Bind the rational carrier and classifier first. The object carrier is
$\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}$, the object classifier is
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$, and the source pattern is the BEDC
continuation pattern $\Cont$ over $\mathsf{BHist}$. The denominator tuple is
$(\mathsf{RatDenomUnitCarrier},\mathsf{RatDenomUnitClassifier},
\mathsf{append},\emp,\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier})$, with contextual action
$\Gamma$ and affine coordinate $\mathsf{AffAut}_F$ as fixed in
Definition~\ref{def:concrete-ratup-fieldup-certificate-instance}.

The source anchor is
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-certificate-rows-source-anchored}. It reads the
$\CommRingUp$ base coordinate through
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-assembled-certificate-boundary} and pins the field
carrier, field classifier, denominator tuple, contextual action, context-pair
monoid, and affine coordinate to the same concrete source tuple.

The inverse rows are the apart-zero rows. Theorem~\ref{thm:field-apart-zero-inverse-classifier-exact-from-apartness}
supplies classifier exactness for proof-indexed inverses over the concrete
apart-zero predicate, so the inverse classifier, inverse stability, and
nonzero transport entries are read from the displayed apartness witnesses.

The zero-product rows come from
\autoref{thm:field-two-sided-zero-product-cancel-from-apartness}. Its
two-sided multiplication equivalence gives exact zero classification after
certified nonzero left and right factors, and the same witness data supply the
nonzero-product contradiction row in the per-object certificate record.

For the denominator part, use
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-fieldup-multiplicative-obligation-coverage}
for the multiplicative denominator rows and
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-denominator-package-closure} for the closed
denominator-side package.

The denominator-action envelope component is supplied by
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-denominator-action-envelope-certificate-component}.
It records the multiplicative coverage, contextual boundary, normalized row
coverage, and context-pair product and action rows over the concrete tuple
fixed above.

The contextual denominator action is supplied by
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-denominator-context-pair-action-certificate},
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-denominator-context-pair-unit-support-neutrality},
and
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-denominator-total-normalization-support-collapse-row}.
They package the pair classifier, pair product, unit-pair action, product-pair
composition law, collected-action exactness, unit-pair support neutrality, and
strict-support readback over the same BHist denominator tuple, so the contextual
exactness and support-neutrality rows align with the denominator ledger entries.

\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-assembled-support-neutrality-certificate} supplies
the carried nested contextual action, the collected-action classifier
comparison, the strict-support equivalence, and the collected classifier
exactness consumed by the final FieldUp record. Thus the support-neutrality row
appearing in
Definition~\ref{def:concrete-ratup-fieldup-certificate-instance} is a concrete
certificate dependency rather than a detached denominator fact.

Separately,
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-context-pair-product-strict-support-commutes}
supplies the strict-support commutation row for the context-pair product. This
is the support-transport row consumed by
Definition~\ref{def:concrete-ratup-fieldup-certificate-instance} between the
context-pair action certificate and the final FieldUp record.

Finally, \autoref{thm:field-affine-automorphism-group-certificate} supplies
the packaged affine $\GroupUp$ consequence consumed by the field certificate.

Together the $\CommRingUp$ base rows, the inverse rows, the zero-product rows,
the denominator multiplicative rows, the denominator-action envelope component,
the contextual denominator rows, the support-neutrality row, the unit-pair
support-neutrality row, the context-pair action rows, the strict-support
commutation row, and the affine $\GroupUp$ row
exhaust the ten-entry per-object template of
Definition~\ref{def:concrete-ratup-fieldup-certificate-instance}: target,
source, carrier, classifier, stability obligations, ledger, core theorem list,
exactness predicate, standard bridge, and status.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RatUp FieldUp semantic certificate is non-singleton]
\label{thm:ratup-fieldup-semantic-certificate-non-singleton}
Let $d_1:=\Eone(\emp)$. The concrete certificate rows of
Definition~\ref{def:concrete-ratup-fieldup-certificate-instance} and
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-semantic-certificate-assembly} are carried by the
single source tuple $\mathsf{RatUpFieldSource}_F$. This same source has the
diagonal witnesses
$$
\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(d_1),
$$
$$
\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(d_1,d_1),
$$
and
$$
\mathsf{RatHistoryLedgerPolicy}(d_1,d_1).
$$
Each of the following singleton-boundary coverage maps is contradictory:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item carrier coverage
  $$
  \forall h:\mathsf{BHist},\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(h)\to
  \mathsf{fieldSingletonEmptyCarrier}(h);
  $$
  \item classifier coverage
  $$
  \forall h k:\mathsf{BHist},\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(h,k)\to
  \mathsf{FieldSingletonClassifier}(h,k);
  $$
  \item ledger endpoint coverage
  $$
  \forall \rho v:\mathsf{BHist},\mathsf{RatHistoryLedgerPolicy}(\rho,v)\to
  \mathsf{FieldSingletonCarrier}(\rho)\land
  \mathsf{FieldSingletonCarrier}(v).
  $$
\end{enumerate}
Consequently the semantic certificate rows for $\FieldUp(\RatUp)$ are sourced
by the concrete $\mathsf{BHist}$ tuple and cannot collapse to the singleton
$\FieldUp$ boundary in carrier, classifier, or ledger coordinates.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The one-source assertion is
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-certificate-rows-source-anchored}. It identifies the
field carrier, field classifier, denominator tuple, contextual action,
context-pair monoid, and affine coordinate as rows read over the single source
tuple $\mathsf{RatUpFieldSource}_F$.

Next use \autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-semantic-certificate-assembly}. It says
that those rows are exactly the concrete certificate rows consumed by
Definition~\ref{def:concrete-ratup-fieldup-certificate-instance} for the
target $\FieldUp(\RatUp)$.

Finally apply
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-source-coordinate-coverage-obstruction} at
$d_1=\Eone(\emp)$. That theorem supplies the three diagonal witnesses and
separately rules out carrier, classifier, and ledger endpoint coverage maps to
the singleton $\FieldUp$ boundary.

Combining the source-anchored certificate rows with these three coordinate
obstructions gives the claimed non-singleton semantic certificate boundary.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RatUp FieldUp assembled support-neutrality certificate]
\label{thm:ratup-fieldup-assembled-support-neutrality-certificate}
For the concrete denominator tuple $(C_D^u,R_D^u,\mu_D^u,e_D^u,N_D^u)$, the
contextual action $\Gamma$ of
\autoref{def:ratup-fieldup-denominator-contextual-action-instance}, and the
named source tuple $\mathsf{RatUpFieldSource}_F$, the assembled
$\FieldUp(\mathsf{RatUp})$ certificate includes the support-neutrality row
below. Under the hypotheses of
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-denominator-total-normalization-support-collapse-row},
the nested action is carried:
$$
C_D^u(\Gamma_{p',q',l',r'}(\Gamma_{p,q,l,r}(h))).
$$
It is classified by the collected action:
$$
R_D^u(\Gamma_{p',q',l',r'}(\Gamma_{p,q,l,r}(h)),
\Gamma_{p',q',\mu_D^u(l',l),\mu_D^u(r,r')}(h)).
$$
It satisfies strict-support neutrality:
$$
N_D^u(\Gamma_{p',q',l',r'}(\Gamma_{p,q,l,r}(h)))
\Longleftrightarrow
N_D^u(h).
$$
For any second endpoint $k$ under the same collected carried contexts, and for
$s,t,s',t'$ satisfying $s\hsame\emp$, $t\hsame\emp$, $s'\hsame\emp$, and
$t'\hsame\emp$, the collected action remains classifier-exact against that
endpoint:
$$
R_D^u(\Gamma_{s,t,\mu_D^u(l',l),\mu_D^u(r,r')}(h),
\Gamma_{s',t',\mu_D^u(l',l),\mu_D^u(r,r')}(k))
\Longleftrightarrow R_D^u(h,k).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The assembled boundary is fixed by
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-assembled-certificate-boundary}. It places
$\mathsf{RatUpFieldSource}_F$, the displayed denominator tuple, and the action
$\Gamma$ inside the concrete $\FieldUp(\mathsf{RatUp})$ certificate governed by
\autoref{def:field-certificate-obligations}.

Within that boundary, use
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-denominator-total-normalization-support-collapse-row}.
Its hypotheses are exactly the carried-denominator, empty external-context, and
strict-support hypotheses named in the statement. The theorem supplies the
carried nested action and its classifier comparison with the collected action.

The same row supplies the strict-support equivalence
$N_D^u(\Gamma_{p',q',l',r'}(\Gamma_{p,q,l,r}(h)))\Longleftrightarrow N_D^u(h)$.
It also supplies classifier exactness for the collected action against the
second endpoint $k$. These four conclusions are therefore available as one
support-neutrality row of the assembled FieldUp certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RatUp FieldUp support-neutrality row completeness]
\label{thm:ratup-fieldup-support-neutrality-row-completeness}
For the concrete source tuple $\mathsf{RatUpFieldSource}_F$ and
Definition~\ref{def:concrete-ratup-fieldup-certificate-instance}, the
support-sensitive certificate rows are exactly the concrete $\mathsf{BHist}$
rows supplied by the following sources:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item strict product support exactness for the denominator product;
  \item context-pair unit support neutrality;
  \item total-normalization support collapse;
  \item contextual action carrier, classifier, and support neutrality;
  \item context-pair strict-support commutation.
\end{enumerate}
Thus the named certificate instance contains every support row consumed by the
assembled semantic $\FieldUp(\mathsf{RatUp})$ certificate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The denominator product support row is the concrete $\mathsf{BHist}$ row
supplied by
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-empty-unit-strict-product-support-exactness}.
It fixes the strict product support exactness entry over the same denominator
carrier, classifier, product, and empty endpoint used by
$\mathsf{RatUpFieldSource}_F$.

The unit-pair and total-normalization rows are supplied by
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-denominator-context-pair-unit-support-neutrality}
and
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-denominator-total-normalization-support-collapse-row}.
Together they give the unit-pair support-neutrality clause, the
total-normalization support collapse clause, and the contextual action carrier
and classifier clauses for the concrete action $\Gamma$.

The contextual strict-support transport is the context-pair product row of
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-context-pair-product-strict-support-commutes}.
It uses the same context-pair product and the same strict support predicate as
the certificate instance, so it is the remaining support-sensitive transport
row.

Finally
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-assembled-support-neutrality-certificate} repacks
these concrete rows as the assembled support-neutrality certificate for
$\mathsf{RatUpFieldSource}_F$. Since
Definition~\ref{def:concrete-ratup-fieldup-certificate-instance} names exactly
these stability, ledger, core theorem, and exactness entries, no additional
support-sensitive row is required for the semantic assembly.
\end{proof}
