\label{thm:field-rat-denominator-unit-envelope-strict-support-laws}

  \item $N_D^\ast(\mu_D^\ast(h,k))\Longleftrightarrow
  N_D^\ast(h)\lor N_D^\ast(k)$.

  \autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-unit-envelope-commutative-monoid-laws},
  these clauses give a concrete $\mathsf{BHist}$ multiplicative-support
  boundary for $\FieldUp(\mathsf{RatUp})$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

assumptions split each input into a genuine Rat denominator endpoint or an
external empty endpoint.

$R_D^\ast(h,k)$. In the guarded carried branch, both strict-support predicates
are exactly the displayed $\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}$ witnesses, so the two
directions are projections. In the empty-empty branch, any strict-support
witness for either endpoint contradicts $\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier\_not\_empty}$
after composing its endpoint comparison with the branch comparison to $\emp$.

For product support, first consider the carried/carried branch. The canonical
continuation witness for $\mathsf{append}(h,k)$ and carried denominator closure
give $\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(\mu_D^\ast(h,k))$, while the right side holds
by either input support.

In the carried/empty branch, the append right-unit law identifies
$\mu_D^\ast(h,k)$ with $h$. Transport

$\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier\_hsame\_transport}$, and the left disjunct supplies
the reverse implication. The empty/carried branch is symmetric, using the
append left-unit law and the right strict-support disjunct.

In the empty/empty branch, the append unit laws identify
$\mu_D^\ast(h,k)$ with $\emp$. Therefore product strict support is impossible
by $\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier\_not\_empty}$, and both input strict-support
predicates are impossible by the same theorem. This proves the displayed
product equivalence.

The final clause only packages these two support laws with the already stated
commutative monoid laws: the monoid theorem supplies the algebraic envelope,
and the present theorem supplies the exact descent of genuine denominator
support through classifier transport and multiplication.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Rat denominator external-unit envelope support-unit no-confusion]
\label{thm:field-rat-denominator-unit-envelope-support-unit-noconfusion}
For every history $h:\mathsf{BHist}$ with $C_D^\ast(h)$,
$$
N_D^\ast(h)\Longleftrightarrow
(R_D^\ast(h,e_D^\ast)\to\mathsf{False}).
$$
For histories $h,k:\mathsf{BHist}$ with $C_D^\ast(h)$ and $C_D^\ast(k)$,
$$
(R_D^\ast(\mu_D^\ast(h,k),e_D^\ast)\to\mathsf{False})
\Longleftrightarrow N_D^\ast(h)\lor N_D^\ast(k).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold the carrier $C_D^\ast$ at $h$. In the carried branch, $N_D^\ast(h)$ is
exactly the displayed $\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(h)$ witness. A classifier
comparison $R_D^\ast(h,e_D^\ast)$ would be a mixed carried-empty endpoint
comparison, because $e_D^\ast=\emp$; by
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-unit-envelope-classifier-exactness} and
\autoref{thm:rat-history-carrier-not-empty} this branch is impossible.

In the empty branch, $h\hsame\emp$. Since $e_D^\ast=\emp$, the empty-empty
classifier branch gives $R_D^\ast(h,e_D^\ast)$. The same endpoint comparison
also rules out $N_D^\ast(h)$ by
\autoref{thm:rat-history-carrier-not-empty}. Thus the strict support predicate
is exactly the failure of classification as the adjoined unit.

For the product clause, first use the carrier closure in
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-unit-envelope-commutative-monoid-laws} to
view $\mu_D^\ast(h,k)$ as an envelope-carried history. Applying the first clause
to this product rewrites failure of
$R_D^\ast(\mu_D^\ast(h,k),e_D^\ast)$ as
$N_D^\ast(\mu_D^\ast(h,k))$.

Finally apply the second support law of
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-unit-envelope-strict-support-laws}, which
identifies $N_D^\ast(\mu_D^\ast(h,k))$ with
$N_D^\ast(h)\lor N_D^\ast(k)$. The two equivalences compose to give the
displayed product no-confusion statement.
\end{proof}
