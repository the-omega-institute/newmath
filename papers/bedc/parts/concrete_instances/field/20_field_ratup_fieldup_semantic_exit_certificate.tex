\begin{theorem}[RatUp FieldUp transported nondegenerate semantic exit]
\label{thm:ratup-fieldup-transported-nondegenerate-semantic-exit}
Let $d_1:=\Eone(\emp)$. Under the hypotheses of
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-transported-support-neutrality-row}, the concrete
$\FieldUp(\mathsf{RatUp})$ exit source over $\mathsf{RatUpFieldSource}_F$
simultaneously has the assembled semantic certificate rows of
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-semantic-certificate-assembly}, the endpoint
separation
$$
\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(d_1)
$$
with
$$
\mathsf{fieldSingletonEmptyCarrier}(d_1)\to\bot,
$$
the transported nested action carrier conclusion
$$
C_D^u(\Gamma_{p',q',s',t'}(\Gamma_{p,q,s,t}(h))),
$$
and the transported strict-support neutrality
$$
N_D^u(\Gamma_{p',q',s',t'}(\Gamma_{p,q,s,t}(h)))
\Longleftrightarrow
N_D^u(h).
$$
Thus the non-singleton RatUp source separation and the context-pair transported
support-neutrality row are available in one $\FieldUp(\mathsf{RatUp})$ exit
certificate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-nondegenerate-semantic-exit}. It supplies the
assembled semantic certificate rows, the common source anchoring, the original
support-neutrality row, and the endpoint separation at
$d_1=\Eone(\emp)$.

Apply \autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-transported-support-neutrality-row} under its
stated context-pair hypotheses. This gives the carried transported nested
action and the strict-support equivalence from that transported representative
back to the original denominator history $h$.

The source and certificate assembly facts are
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-semantic-certificate-assembly} and
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-certificate-rows-source-anchored}. They identify all
displayed rows as entries of the same concrete $\mathsf{RatUpFieldSource}_F$
certificate package.

Combining these three ingredients records the endpoint obstruction and the
transported support-neutrality row as simultaneous parts of the current
$\FieldUp(\mathsf{RatUp})$ semantic exit certificate.
\end{proof}
