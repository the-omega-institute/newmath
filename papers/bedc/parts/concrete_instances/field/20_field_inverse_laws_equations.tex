\begin{theorem}[Field right-multiplication empty equation solutions are unique from apartness]
\label{thm:field-right-mul-empty-equation-solution-unique-from-apartness}
For a BEDC history-level $\FieldUp$ candidate with proof-indexed inverses, if
$p_b:\mathsf{NonZero}_F(b)$ and both $x$ and $y$ solve the empty endpoint
right-multiplication equation
$$
x b\hsame \emp
\qquad\text{and}\qquad
y b\hsame \emp,
$$
then $x\hsame y$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply the right-multiplication solution theorem to both displayed equations at
the concrete endpoint $\emp$. Both solutions are classified with the same
normal form $\emp\,\mathsf{inv}(b,p_b)$. Symmetry on the second classification
and transitivity of $\hsame$ identify $x$ and $y$.
\end{proof}

the head to the unit, and the left identity law leaves $b$.
