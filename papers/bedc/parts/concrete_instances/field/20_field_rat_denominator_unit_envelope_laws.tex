\paragraph{Rat denominator external-unit envelope law spine.}
The denominator envelope used at the $\FieldUp(\mathsf{RatUp})$ boundary is the
concrete history carrier
$C_D^\ast(h)\equiv \mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(h)\lor h\hsame\emp$, with
classifier $R_D^\ast$ given by the guarded carried Rat classifier branch or the
empty-empty endpoint branch. The product is the BEDC history append operation,
and the displayed endpoint is $e_D^\ast=\emp$.

The checked law package for this envelope consists of carrier inhabitance,
append closure, associativity under $R_D^\ast$, the left and right endpoint unit
laws, and binary classifier congruence. On carried denominators the guarded
branch restricts back to the positive-denominator Rat classifier, while the
empty branch supplies the external endpoint. The one-sided unit uniqueness and
unit equivalence clauses recorded below use this same law spine together with
the nonemptiness of carried Rat denominators.
