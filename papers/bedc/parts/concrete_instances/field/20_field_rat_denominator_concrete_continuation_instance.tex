\section{Concrete Rat denominator continuation instance}
\label{sec:field-rat-denominator-concrete-continuation-instance}

\begin{definition}[Concrete Rat denominator continuation instance]
\label{def:field-rat-denominator-concrete-continuation-instance}
The concrete denominator-side continuation instance for
$\FieldUp(\mathsf{RatUp})$ has carrier, classifier, operation, continuation
witness, and ledger predicate
$$
C_D(h)\Longleftrightarrow\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(h),
$$
$$
R_D(h,k)\Longleftrightarrow\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(h,k),
$$
$$
\mu_D(h,k):=\mathsf{append}(h,k),
$$
with the canonical continuation witness
$\Cont(h,k,\mu_D(h,k))$ and ledger predicate
$\mathsf{RatHistoryLedgerPolicy}$. The empty history $\emp$ is an external
classifier-unit endpoint for the operation and is not a carried denominator.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Rat denominator continuation concrete instance laws]
\label{thm:field-rat-denominator-concrete-continuation-instance-laws}
For histories $d,d',e,e',f,\rho,v:\mathsf{BHist}$, the concrete data of
\autoref{def:field-rat-denominator-concrete-continuation-instance} satisfy the
following laws.
\begin{enumerate}
  \item If $C_D(d)$ and $C_D(e)$, then $C_D(\mu_D(d,e))$.
  \item If $C_D(d)$ and $C_D(e)$, then $R_D(\mu_D(d,e),\mu_D(e,d))$.
  \item If $C_D(d)$, $C_D(e)$, and $C_D(f)$, then
  $R_D(\mu_D(\mu_D(d,e),f),\mu_D(d,\mu_D(e,f)))$.
  \item If $R_D(d,d')$ and $R_D(e,e')$, then
  $R_D(\mu_D(d,e),\mu_D(d',e'))$.
  \item If $\mathsf{RatHistoryLedgerPolicy}(\rho,v)$, $C_D(e)$, and
  $R_D(e,e')$, then
  $\mathsf{RatHistoryLedgerPolicy}(\mu_D(\rho,e),\mu_D(v,e'))$.
  \item No carried history $u:\mathsf{BHist}$ can satisfy either one-sided
  classifier-unit law

  $$
  \forall x,r:\mathsf{BHist}.\;
  C_D(x)\to
  \Cont(x,u,r)\to
  R_D(r,x)
  $$

  or

  $$
  \forall x,r:\mathsf{BHist}.\;
  C_D(x)\to
  \Cont(u,x,r)\to
  R_D(r,x).
  $$
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold $C_D$, $R_D$, and $\mu_D$. The canonical witness
$\Cont(h,k,\mu_D(h,k))$ is the append continuation constructor, so every law
reduces to the Rat denominator continuation theorems already established in
the FieldUp boundary.

Carrier closure is exactly
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-continuation-carrier-closure} applied to
the canonical continuation. The commutative classifier law is
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-continuation-commutative-classifier} with
the two canonical continuations for $\mu_D(d,e)$ and $\mu_D(e,d)$.

Associativity of the classifier follows from
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-continuation-semigroup-laws} and
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-continuation-associative-classifier-congruence}
applied to the displayed bracketings. These theorems supply both the carried
outer results and the classifier comparison between the two bracketings.

For binary classifier congruence, unpack $R_D(d,d')$ and $R_D(e,e')$. The
second classifier supplies the matching denominator comparison needed by
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-continuation-classifier-closure}; applying
that theorem to the two canonical continuations gives
$R_D(\mu_D(d,e),\mu_D(d',e'))$.

The ledger clause uses the same matching continuation pattern. Read $C_D(e)$
and $R_D(e,e')$ as the right-tail carrier and classifier data, then apply
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-continuation-ledger-closure} to the two
canonical continuations for $\mu_D(\rho,e)$ and $\mu_D(v,e')$.

Finally,
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-continuation-unit-endpoint-empty} says that
any one-sided classifier-unit endpoint is $\hsame$ to $\emp$, and its carried
endpoint clause rules out such a history inside $\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}$.
Thus $\emp$ remains only the external classifier-unit endpoint named in the
definition, not a carried denominator.
\end{proof}
