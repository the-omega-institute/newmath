\section{Product factor equation exactness}
\label{sec:field-product-right-equation-exactness}

\begin{theorem}[Field product right-multiplication equation exactness from apartness]
\label{thm:field-product-right-mul-equation-exact-from-apartness}
For a BEDC history-level $\FieldUp$ candidate with proof-indexed inverses,
multiplication associativity, left and right unit laws, multiplication
congruence under $\hsame$, and left and right inverse laws indexed by nonzero
witnesses, explicit witnesses
$p_{ab}:\mathsf{NonZero}_F(\mathsf{mul}(a,b))$,
$p_a:\mathsf{NonZero}_F(a)$, and $p_b:\mathsf{NonZero}_F(b)$ make every
$x,c:\mathsf{BHist}$ satisfy
$$
\mathsf{mul}(x,\mathsf{mul}(a,b))\hsame c
\Longleftrightarrow
x\hsame \mathsf{mul}(c,\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{inv}(b,p_b),\mathsf{inv}(a,p_a))).
$$
Traditional target product-factor right equation solving; BEDC source
$\FieldUp$ over $\mathsf{BHist}$; carrier $\mathsf{BHist}$; classifier
$\hsame$; stability obligations right equation exactness, inverse product
reversal, multiplication congruence, associativity and unit laws; ledger the
three nonzero witnesses and the reversed inverse product; core theorem this
block; exactness predicate the displayed equivalence.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply
\autoref{thm:field-right-mul-equation-exact-from-apartness} to the active
right factor $\mathsf{mul}(a,b)$ with witness $p_{ab}$. This gives the
equivalence between the displayed equation and
$x\hsame\mathsf{mul}(c,\mathsf{inv}(\mathsf{mul}(a,b),p_{ab}))$.

Next apply
\autoref{thm:field-inverse-product-reverse-from-apartness} with the three
witnesses $p_{ab}$, $p_a$, and $p_b$. It identifies the certified inverse of
the product with the reversed product
$\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{inv}(b,p_b),\mathsf{inv}(a,p_a))$.

For the forward direction, use multiplication congruence on the right factor
of $\mathsf{mul}(c,-)$ to transport the one-factor solution along the inverse
product reversal. The transported right side is exactly the displayed reversed
product solution.

For the reverse direction, use symmetry of the inverse product reversal and
the same multiplication congruence to transport the displayed reversed product
solution back to
$x\hsame\mathsf{mul}(c,\mathsf{inv}(\mathsf{mul}(a,b),p_{ab}))$. The reverse
half of the one-factor exactness theorem then recovers
$\mathsf{mul}(x,\mathsf{mul}(a,b))\hsame c$.
\end{proof}

\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FieldUp.field\_product\_right\_mul\_empty\_equation\_exact\_from\_apartness}
\begin{proof}

$\mathsf{mul}(a,b)$ at target $\emp$. This identifies the displayed equation
with the solution using $\mathsf{inv}(\mathsf{mul}(a,b),p_{ab})$. Product
inverse reversal identifies that inverse with
$\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{inv}(b,p_b),\mathsf{inv}(a,p_a))$, and multiplication
congruence transports the solution across the right factor of
$\mathsf{mul}(\emp,-)$ in both directions.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Field three-factor product equation exactness from apartness]
\label{thm:field-three-factor-product-equation-exact-from-apartness}
For a BEDC history-level $\FieldUp$ candidate with proof-indexed inverses,
multiplication associativity, left and right unit laws, multiplication
congruence under $\hsame$, left and right inverse laws indexed by nonzero
witnesses, product nonzero exclusion, and the apartness-to-nonzero conversion
maps used in
\autoref{thm:field-inverse-three-factor-product-reversal-from-apartness}, any
witness
$$
p_{abc}:\mathsf{NonZero}_F(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a,b),c))
$$
induces witnesses $p_a:\mathsf{NonZero}_F(a)$,
$p_b:\mathsf{NonZero}_F(b)$, and $p_c:\mathsf{NonZero}_F(c)$ such that every
$x,q:\mathsf{BHist}$ satisfies
$$
\mathsf{mul}(x,\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a,b),c))\hsame q
\Longleftrightarrow
x\hsame
\mathsf{mul}(q,\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{inv}(c,p_c),
\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{inv}(b,p_b),\mathsf{inv}(a,p_a)))).
$$
It also satisfies
$$
\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a,b),c),x)\hsame q
\Longleftrightarrow
x\hsame
\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{inv}(c,p_c),
\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{inv}(b,p_b),\mathsf{inv}(a,p_a))),q).
$$
Traditional target three-factor product equation solving; BEDC source
$\FieldUp$ over $\mathsf{BHist}$; carrier $\mathsf{BHist}$; classifier
$\hsame$; stability obligations one-factor right and left equation exactness,
three-factor inverse reversal, multiplication congruence, symmetry, and
transitivity; ledger $p_{abc}$ and the induced factor witnesses
$p_a,p_b,p_c$; core theorem this block; exactness predicate the two displayed
equivalences; standard bridge deferred; downstream use finite product equation
solving for $\FieldUp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply
\autoref{thm:field-right-mul-equation-exact-from-apartness} to the active
right factor $\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a,b),c)$ with witness $p_{abc}$. This
gives the right-multiplication equation equivalence with the one-factor
solution
$x\hsame\mathsf{mul}(q,
\mathsf{inv}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a,b),c),p_{abc}))$.

Apply
\autoref{thm:field-inverse-three-factor-product-reversal-from-apartness} to
$p_{abc}$. It produces the factor witnesses $p_a$, $p_b$, and $p_c$ and
identifies the indexed inverse of
$\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a,b),c)$ with the reversed product
$\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{inv}(c,p_c),
\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{inv}(b,p_b),\mathsf{inv}(a,p_a)))$.

For the forward direction of the right equation, use multiplication
congruence on the right factor of $\mathsf{mul}(q,-)$ to transport the
one-factor solution along that inverse equality.

For the reverse direction of the right equation, use symmetry of the same
inverse equality, the same congruence, and
transitivity to return to the indexed-inverse solution before applying the
reverse half of the one-factor exactness theorem.

The left equation is identical with the active factor on the left. Apply
\autoref{thm:field-left-mul-equation-exact-from-apartness} to
$\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a,b),c)$ with witness $p_{abc}$, then transport
the indexed inverse through the first factor of $\mathsf{mul}(-,q)$ by
multiplication congruence.

Symmetry and transitivity give the reverse transport, and the reverse half of
the one-factor left exactness theorem recovers
$\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a,b),c),x)\hsame q$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Field four-factor product equation exactness from apartness]
\label{thm:field-four-factor-product-equation-exact-from-apartness}
For a BEDC history-level $\FieldUp$ candidate over $\mathsf{BHist}$ with
proof-indexed inverses, multiplication associativity, left and right unit laws,
multiplication congruence under $\hsame$, and left and right inverse laws
indexed by nonzero witnesses, let
$p_{ab}:\mathsf{NonZero}_F(\mathsf{mul}(a,b))$,
$p_{abc}:\mathsf{NonZero}_F(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a,b),c))$,
$p_{abcd}:\mathsf{NonZero}_F(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a,b),c),d))$,
$p_a:\mathsf{NonZero}_F(a)$, $p_b:\mathsf{NonZero}_F(b)$,
$p_c:\mathsf{NonZero}_F(c)$, and $p_d:\mathsf{NonZero}_F(d)$ be explicit
witnesses. Define the reversed inverse spine
$$
R:=
\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{inv}(d,p_d),
\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{inv}(c,p_c),
\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{inv}(b,p_b),\mathsf{inv}(a,p_a)))).
$$
Then every $x,r:\mathsf{BHist}$ satisfies both clauses
$$
\mathsf{mul}(x,\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a,b),c),d))\hsame r
\Longleftrightarrow
x\hsame \mathsf{mul}(r,R)
$$
and
$$
\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a,b),c),d),x)\hsame r
\Longleftrightarrow
x\hsame \mathsf{mul}(R,r).
$$
Traditional target finite product equation solving; BEDC source $\FieldUp$ over
$\mathsf{BHist}$; carrier $\mathsf{BHist}$; classifier $\hsame$; stability
obligations one-factor equation exactness, inverse-product reversal,
congruence, associativity, and unit laws; ledger the seven displayed witnesses;
core theorem this block; exactness predicate the two displayed equivalences;
standard bridge deferred; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:field-right-mul-equation-exact-from-apartness} to the
active fourfold right factor
$\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a,b),c),d)$ with witness
$p_{abcd}$. This gives the right-multiplication equation in terms of the
certified inverse of the whole product.

Apply \autoref{thm:field-left-mul-equation-exact-from-apartness} to the same
fourfold product with witness $p_{abcd}$. This gives the left-multiplication
equation in terms of the same certified inverse, now placed on the left of
$r$.

We next identify that certified inverse with $R$. Use
\autoref{thm:field-inverse-product-reverse-from-apartness} first along
$p_{abcd}$, then along $p_{abc}$, and finally along $p_{ab}$. Associativity
and the unit laws put the resulting nested product into the displayed reversed
spine.

For the right equation, transport the solution side through multiplication
congruence for $\mathsf{mul}(r,-)$. Symmetry and transitivity of $\hsame$
move between the certified inverse of the active product and $R$, yielding
$x\hsame\mathsf{mul}(r,R)$ in the forward direction and returning to the
one-factor exactness statement in the reverse direction.

For the left equation, use the same transport with multiplication congruence
for $\mathsf{mul}(-,r)$. The identified spine now appears on the left, so the
transported solution is $x\hsame\mathsf{mul}(R,r)$, and the reverse transport
recovers the original fourfold left equation.
\end{proof}
