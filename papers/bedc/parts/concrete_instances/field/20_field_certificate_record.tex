\begin{definition}[Field ledger policy]
\label{def:field-ledger-policy}
Record the ring ledger and the $\mathsf{NonZero}_F$ witness used for each inverse.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[$\FieldUp$ certificate obligations]
\label{def:field-certificate-obligations}
For the concrete target $\FieldUp(\RatUp)$, a certificate must provide the
following rows as a single obligation record.
\begin{enumerate}
  \item Source and carrier: the rational-history source on
  $\mathsf{BHist}$ with carrier $\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}$.
  \item Classifier and pattern: $\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$ and the
  continuation pattern $\Cont$ over $\mathsf{BHist}$.
  \item Ring base: the $\CommRingUp$ additive, multiplicative,
  commutativity, distributivity, operation-congruence, and ring-ledger rows.
  \item Apartness and inverse data: $\mathsf{NonZero}_F$ apartness from zero,
  transport of nonzero evidence, inverse closure, inverse classifier
  exactness, and left and right inverse laws indexed by nonzero witnesses.
  \item Product data: the two-sided zero-product rows and the certified
  nonzero-product contradiction rows.
  \item Denominator tuple:
  $(\mathsf{RatDenomUnitCarrier},\mathsf{RatDenomUnitClassifier},
  \mathsf{append},\emp,\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier})$ with its multiplicative,
  unit, closure, classifier, and ledger rows.
  \item Contextual denominator data: the action $\Gamma$, its envelope and
  normalized-support rows, and the context-pair monoid rows for pair product,
  unit-pair action, composition, and strict-support readback.
  \item Affine source: $\mathsf{AffAut}_F$ with the packaged affine
  $\GroupUp$ rows used by the field certificate.
  \item Ledger and exactness data: ring expression ledger rows, inverse
  witness ledger rows, denominator ledger rows, contextual-pair ledger rows,
  affine coefficient ledger rows, coverage, soundness, completeness, exact
  zero classification, denominator exactness, contextual-action exactness,
  context-pair exactness, and affine classifier exactness.
  \item Status: the record status field, instantiated here as
  $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[RatUp FieldUp certificate record absorbs selector-independent exit]
\label{thm:ratup-fieldup-certificate-record-absorbs-selector-independent-exit}
For the certificate-obligation record of
Definition~\ref{def:field-certificate-obligations}, for
Definition~\ref{def:ratup-fieldup-concrete-exit-object}, for the concrete
denominator tuple $(C_D^u,R_D^u,\mu_D^u,e_D^u,N_D^u)$, for histories
$h,k,\ell:\mathsf{BHist}$ with $C_D^u(h)$, $C_D^u(k)$, and $C_D^u(\ell)$, for
selectors $s,t,u,v:\mathsf{BMark}$, and for contexts
$L,R,r:\mathsf{BHist}$, if $\mathsf{ThreeSupp}_D^u(h,k,\ell)$ holds, then the
concrete $\FieldUp(\RatUp)$ certificate record contains, as one record-level
row family, every row of
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-complete-selector-exit-obstruction-certificate} and
every row of
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-selector-independent-final-boundary}. This family
includes the selector-pair strict exit rows, the bracketing obstruction rows,
both selected final boundaries, and the componentwise
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$ selector rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Instantiate
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-complete-selector-exit-obstruction-certificate} with
the displayed denominator tuple, histories, carrier witnesses, selectors
$s,t,u,v$, contexts $L,R,r$, and the given
$\mathsf{ThreeSupp}_D^u(h,k,\ell)$ witness. This supplies the selector-pair
strict exit rows, the bracketing obstruction rows, and the selected
final-boundary rows for the concrete exit object.

Instantiate
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-selector-independent-final-boundary} with the same
tuple, witnesses, selectors, contexts, and support witness. This supplies both
selected final boundaries together with the componentwise
$R_D^u$ and $\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$ selector rows.

The ledger fields of Definition~\ref{def:field-certificate-obligations} already
name the denominator ledger, contextual-pair ledger, and coverage rows. The
exactness fields name denominator exactness, contextual-action exactness,
context-pair exactness, and classifier exactness. Package the two instantiated
row families under those fields, using the selector pairs and contexts as the
ledger indices.

The result is a single $\FieldUp(\RatUp)$ record-level final exit certificate:
the traditional target is the $\FieldUp(\RatUp)$ record-level final exit
certificate, the BEDC source is $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\mathsf{BMark}$, and
$\mathsf{RatUp}$, the carrier and classifier rows are the denominator and
rational classifier rows, the ledger is selector pairs plus contexts, and the
downstream consumer is the Phase Algebra Hierarchy exit criterion.
\end{proof}

\paragraph{Primitive boundary.}
$\FieldUp$ is not a BEDC primitive. It is admitted only when those obligations are supplied.
