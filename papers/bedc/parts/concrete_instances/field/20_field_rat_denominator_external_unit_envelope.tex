\begin{theorem}[Rat denominator external-unit envelope unit equivalence]
\label{thm:field-rat-denominator-unit-envelope-unit-iff}
For every history $u:\mathsf{BHist}$ with $C_D^\ast(u)$,
$$
R_D^\ast(u,e_D^\ast)\Longleftrightarrow
\left(\forall h:\mathsf{BHist}.\;
C_D^\ast(h)\to R_D^\ast(\mu_D^\ast(h,u),h)\right)
\land
\left(\forall h:\mathsf{BHist}.\;
C_D^\ast(h)\to R_D^\ast(\mu_D^\ast(u,h),h)\right).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the forward direction, assume $R_D^\ast(u,e_D^\ast)$ and split the carrier
witness $C_D^\ast(u)$. The carried branch is impossible: the mixed
carried-empty clause of
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-unit-envelope-classifier-exactness} rejects
$R_D^\ast(u,e_D^\ast)$ because $e_D^\ast=\emp$.

In the external-empty branch, $u\hsame\emp$. For any $h$ with $C_D^\ast(h)$,
split $C_D^\ast(h)$. If $h$ is carried, the append unit laws identify
$\mu_D^\ast(h,u)$ and $\mu_D^\ast(u,h)$ with $h$; transport the carried
witnesses by $\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier\_hsame\_transport}$ and repack through
the guarded carried branch of $R_D^\ast$. If $h\hsame\emp$, both products are
$\hsame$ to $\emp$, so the empty-empty branch gives the right and left laws.

For the reverse direction, project the conjunction into the displayed right and
left envelope unit laws. Applying
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-unit-envelope-unit-unique} to either
projection gives $R_D^\ast(u,e_D^\ast)$, and the two projections therefore give
the same endpoint classifier.
\end{proof}
