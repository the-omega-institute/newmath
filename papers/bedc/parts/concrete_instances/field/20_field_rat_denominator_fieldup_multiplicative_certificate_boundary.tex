\begin{theorem}[Rat denominator FieldUp multiplicative certificate boundary]
\label{thm:field-rat-denominator-fieldup-multiplicative-certificate-boundary}
Let the denominator carrier, classifier, multiplication, unit, and strict
support be the concrete $\mathsf{BHist}$ data
$$
C_D^\ast(h),\qquad
R_D^\ast(h,k),\qquad
\mu_D^\ast(h,k):=\mathsf{append}(h,k),\qquad
e_D^\ast:=\emp,\qquad
N_D^\ast(h):=\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(h).
$$
This tuple supplies the denominator-side multiplicative certificate boundary
for $\FieldUp(\mathsf{RatUp})$: envelope classifier exactness, monoid laws,
commutativity, strict-support transport, product-support exactness, empty-unit
identity uniqueness, and carried-endpoint cancellation all hold for the
displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ denominator package.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier, classifier, operation, and unit are the external-unit envelope
data of \autoref{def:field-rat-denominator-unit-envelope}. Its classifier
boundary is exactly
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-unit-envelope-classifier-exactness}.

The algebraic envelope comes from two checked components. The monoid laws are
those of \autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-unit-envelope-monoid-laws}, and the
commutative law is supplied by
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-unit-envelope-commutative-monoid-laws}.

The strict support named in the statement is the support predicate of
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-unit-envelope-strict-support-laws}. That
theorem gives both classifier transport for $N_D^\ast$ and the exact product
law
$$
N_D^\ast(\mu_D^\ast(h,k))\Longleftrightarrow
N_D^\ast(h)\lor N_D^\ast(k).
$$

The empty endpoint is controlled by
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-empty-unit-identity-unique}, which identifies
any carried two-sided identity with $e_D^\ast=\emp$ through the endpoint
classifier. The carried cancellation part is the cancellation field of
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-empty-unit-cancellative-commutative-monoid-package},
read on the same denominator append operation and the same empty endpoint.

Combining these clauses gives a single concrete denominator-side
multiplicative boundary for $\FieldUp(\mathsf{RatUp})$, with no abstract carrier
or classifier left to choose.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Rat denominator FieldUp contextual multiplicative boundary]
\label{thm:field-rat-denominator-fieldup-contextual-multiplicative-boundary}
Let the denominator-side empty-unit tuple for $\FieldUp(\mathsf{RatUp})$ be
the concrete $\mathsf{BHist}$ data
$$
(C_D^u,R_D^u,\mu_D^u,e_D^u,N_D^u)
=
(\mathsf{RatDenomUnitCarrier},\mathsf{RatDenomUnitClassifier},
\mathsf{append},\emp,\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}).
$$
Equivalently, $\mu_D^u(h,k)=\mathsf{append}(h,k)$,
$e_D^u=\emp$, and $N_D^u(h)=\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(h)$. The
FieldUp denominator-side contextual multiplicative boundary holds for this
tuple:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item empty external contexts preserve and reflect $R_D^u$;
  \item right multiplication by any carried denominator factor is
  classifier-exact;
  \item left multiplication by any carried denominator factor is
  classifier-exact;
  \item the combined two-sided contextual statement is exactly
  \autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-empty-unit-contextual-multiplication-classifier-exactness}.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The displayed tuple is the concrete cancellation-exact denominator instance of
\autoref{def:field-rat-denominator-empty-unit-cancellation-exact-instance}.
Thus the carrier, classifier, append multiplication, empty endpoint, and strict
support are already fixed by the $\mathsf{BHist}$ denominator package.

For empty external contexts, apply
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-unit-classifier-empty-context-exactness}.
It strips and restores empty histories on both sides of the denominator
classifier, so context insertion by $\emp$ preserves and reflects $R_D^u$.

For fixed-factor multiplication, apply
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-empty-unit-right-multiplication-classifier-exactness}
and
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-empty-unit-left-multiplication-classifier-exactness}.
These give classifier exactness after right and left multiplication by any
carried denominator factor.

Finally, combine the empty-context exactness with the two fixed-factor
exactness laws through
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-empty-unit-contextual-multiplication-classifier-exactness}.
That theorem is precisely the packaged two-sided contextual multiplication
boundary for the displayed FieldUp denominator tuple.
\end{proof}

\textbf{Traditional target}: contextual multiplicative denominator boundary for
$\FieldUp(\mathsf{RatUp})$; \textbf{BEDC source}: $\mathsf{BHist}$,
$\mathsf{RatDenomUnitCarrier}$, $\mathsf{RatDenomUnitClassifier}$, $\Cont$, and
$\mathsf{append}$; \textbf{carrier}: $C_D^u$; \textbf{classifier}: $R_D^u$;
\textbf{stability obligations}: empty-context classifier exactness plus
right- and left-factor multiplication exactness; \textbf{ledger}: empty
external context witnesses and one carried denominator factor; \textbf{core
theorem}:
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-empty-unit-contextual-multiplication-classifier-exactness};
\textbf{exactness predicate}: classifier preservation and reflection after
fixed denominator multiplication inside empty external contexts;
\textbf{standard bridge}: ordinary denominator multiplication with an adjoined
empty unit and empty endpoint contexts; \textbf{status}:
$\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.

\begin{theorem}[Rat denominator FieldUp multiplicative certificate obligation coverage]
\label{thm:field-rat-denominator-fieldup-multiplicative-obligation-coverage}
For the concrete tuple
$(C_D^\ast,R_D^\ast,\mu_D^\ast,e_D^\ast,N_D^\ast)$ of
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-fieldup-multiplicative-certificate-boundary},
the multiplicative $\FieldUp(\mathsf{RatUp})$ certificate rows are covered by
the displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ denominator data:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item carrier closure and unit inhabitation for $C_D^\ast$ and
  $e_D^\ast$;
  \item associativity, commutativity, and binary classifier congruence for
  $\mu_D^\ast$ under $R_D^\ast$;
  \item strict-support classifier transport and product-support exactness for
  $N_D^\ast$;
  \item empty-unit uniqueness for the external endpoint $e_D^\ast$;
  \item two-sided cancellation over the empty-unit cancellative commutative
  monoid package;
  \item empty-product reflection through
  \autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-unit-envelope-empty-product-reflection}.
\end{enumerate}
Thus the boundary theorem is a concrete $\mathsf{BHist}$ tuple law for later
Lean formalization rather than abstract carrier/classifier transport.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-fieldup-multiplicative-certificate-boundary}.
It fixes the five objects $C_D^\ast$, $R_D^\ast$, $\mu_D^\ast$, $e_D^\ast$,
and $N_D^\ast$ and identifies them with the external-unit denominator envelope
used at the $\FieldUp(\mathsf{RatUp})$ boundary.

The carrier rows, the unit row, associativity, binary classifier congruence,
restriction to the carried denominator fragment, mixed-endpoint separation,
and empty-unit uniqueness are supplied by
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-unit-envelope-certificate}. The
commutative row is the commutative part already packaged into the boundary
theorem from the same envelope data.

The strict-support rows are exactly
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-unit-envelope-strict-support-laws}: its
first clause transports $N_D^\ast$ across $R_D^\ast$-classified endpoints, and
its second clause computes strict support of $\mu_D^\ast(h,k)$ from the two
input supports.

The cancellation row is the denominator endpoint cancellation field of
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-empty-unit-cancellative-commutative-monoid-package},
read through the same empty-unit carrier, classifier, append operation, and
endpoint. The empty-product row is precisely
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-unit-envelope-empty-product-reflection}.

These cited rows exhaust the multiplicative denominator obligations listed for
the concrete $\FieldUp(\mathsf{RatUp})$ certificate, so the certificate is
NameCert-ready as a single concrete tuple over $\mathsf{BHist}$.
\end{proof}
