\section{Rat denominator strict monoid boundary}
\label{sec:field-rat-denominator-strict-monoid-boundary}

\begin{theorem}[Rat denominator strict monoid boundary package]
\label{thm:field-rat-denominator-envelope-strict-monoid-boundary}
For the concrete carried denominator data $(C_D,R_D,\mu_D)$ of
\autoref{def:field-rat-denominator-concrete-continuation-instance}, the
external-unit envelope $(C_D^\ast,R_D^\ast,\mu_D^\ast,e_D^\ast)$ of
\autoref{def:field-rat-denominator-unit-envelope}, and the empty-unit tuple
$(C_D^u,R_D^u,\mu_D^u,e_D^u)$ of
\autoref{def:field-rat-denominator-empty-unit-continuation-monoid-instance},
the denominator boundary consumed by $\FieldUp(\mathsf{RatUp})$ has the
following concrete role.
\begin{enumerate}
  \item The envelope supplies the concrete $\MonoidUp$ naming certificate for
  the unit-bearing denominator fragment.
  \item On carried inputs, the envelope restricts exactly to $C_D$, $R_D$, and
  native append through $\mu_D$.
  \item The endpoint $e_D^\ast$ is carried by $C_D^\ast$ but not by $C_D$, and
  no $C_D$ endpoint is classified with $e_D^\ast$ by $R_D^\ast$ on either
  side.
  \item The empty-unit classifier $R_D^u$ has no mixed carried-empty endpoint
  branch.
  \item Every envelope-carried one-sided unit endpoint is classified with
  $e_D^\ast$ by $R_D^\ast$, while every carried denominator one-sided unit law
  for $C_D$, $R_D$, and $\mu_D$ is impossible.
\end{enumerate}
Thus the exported monoid object is the strict external-unit envelope, and the
carried Rat denominator multiplication remains a unitless carried semigroup at
the $\FieldUp(\mathsf{RatUp})$ boundary.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The exported $\MonoidUp$ naming certificate is exactly
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-unit-envelope-monoid-namecert}. Its
certificate fields are supplied by the envelope laws collected in
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-unit-envelope-certificate}.

The carried restriction and strict externality are read from
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-unit-envelope-strict}. This theorem gives
the recovery of $C_D$, $R_D$, and native append on carried inputs, records
$C_D^\ast(e_D^\ast)$ with $\neg C_D(e_D^\ast)$, and excludes
$R_D^\ast$-classification between any carried endpoint and $e_D^\ast$ on both
sides.

The empty-unit tuple has no mixed carried-empty classifier branch by
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-unit-envelope-mixed-endpoint-separation}.
That separation is the boundary check preventing the adjoined unit clause from
classifying a carried denominator endpoint against an empty endpoint.

For uniqueness of an envelope-carried one-sided unit, apply
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-unit-envelope-unit-unique}. It sends either
one-sided envelope unit law to the classifier comparison with $e_D^\ast$.

Finally, the carried semigroup role and the carried-unit obstruction are the
denominator-side clauses of
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-concrete-external-unit-package}. Together
these cited packages show that $\FieldUp(\mathsf{RatUp})$ uses the
unit-bearing $\MonoidUp$ certificate only at the envelope layer, while the
native carried denominator fragment keeps exactly its strict unitless
boundary.
\end{proof}
