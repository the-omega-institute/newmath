\begin{theorem}[Field singleton nonzero append context cancellation]
\label{thm:field-singleton-nonzero-append-context-cancel}
For histories $L,R,Q:\mathsf{BHist}$, if $L$ and $R$ lie in the singleton
carrier $A_{\emp}$, then surrounding $Q$ by those append contexts preserves
and reflects the singleton nonzero endpoint predicate:
$$
\mathsf{FieldSingletonCarrier}(L)\Rightarrow
\mathsf{FieldSingletonCarrier}(R)\Rightarrow
\bigl(
\mathsf{FieldSingletonNonZero}(\mathsf{append}(L,\mathsf{append}(Q,R)))
\Longleftrightarrow
\mathsf{FieldSingletonNonZero}(Q)
\bigr).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The two context carrier witnesses identify $L$ and $R$ with $\emp$. Thus
$\mathsf{append}(L,\mathsf{append}(Q,R))$ is history-same to $Q$ by the left
and right empty append laws. In the forward direction, empty-result inversion
for append extracts the singleton carrier witness for $Q$, and the history
comparison transports the displayed $\Ezero(\emp)$ endpoint witness back to
$Q$.

Conversely, combine the singleton carrier witness for $Q$ with the two context
carrier witnesses to rebuild the nested append carrier by empty-result
inversion. The same empty-context comparison transports the nonzero endpoint
witness from $Q$ to the nested append endpoint.
\end{proof}
