\section{Rat denominator append-continuation semigroup ledger bridge}
\label{sec:field-rat-denominator-append-continuation-strict-semigroup-nonmonoid-bridge}

\begin{theorem}[Rat denominator append-continuation strict semigroup non-monoid bridge]
\label{thm:field-rat-denominator-append-continuation-strict-semigroup-nonmonoid-bridge}
For the concrete data of
\autoref{def:field-rat-denominator-concrete-continuation-instance}, the append
presentation $(C_D,R_D,\mu_D)$ and the relational $\Cont$ presentation determine
the same strict commutative semigroup-with-ledger over
$\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}$, not an internal carried monoid. The common
structure has the following form.
\begin{enumerate}
  \item The carrier is $C_D(h)$, equivalently
  $\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(h)$, and the classifier is $R_D(h,k)$,
  equivalently $\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(h,k)$.
  \item The append presentation supplies carrier closure, commutative
  classifier, associativity up to $R_D$, binary classifier congruence, and
  ledger closure for $\mathsf{RatHistoryLedgerPolicy}$ under right carried
  tails.
  \item The relational $\Cont$ presentation supplies the same five rows with
  every product endpoint named by a continuation witness.
  \item The endpoint bridge identifies every relational product endpoint with
  the corresponding append endpoint at the $R_D$ classifier surface, so the five
  append rows and the five relational rows transport into one another.
  \item For every carried history $u:\mathsf{BHist}$ with $C_D(u)$, neither
  presentation admits a one-sided classifier unit. In the append presentation,
  neither

  $$
  \forall x:\mathsf{BHist}.\;
  C_D(x)\to R_D(\mu_D(x,u),x)
  $$

  nor

  $$
  \forall x:\mathsf{BHist}.\;
  C_D(x)\to R_D(\mu_D(u,x),x)
  $$

  can hold. In the relational presentation, neither

  $$
  \forall x,r:\mathsf{BHist}.\;
  C_D(x)\to \Cont(x,u,r)\to R_D(r,x)
  $$

  nor

  $$
  \forall x,r:\mathsf{BHist}.\;
  C_D(x)\to \Cont(u,x,r)\to R_D(r,x)
  $$

  can hold.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The append rows are exactly the first five clauses of
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-append-concrete-instance-laws}: closure of
$C_D$, commutativity at the classifier, associativity at the classifier, binary
classifier congruence, and ledger closure for
$\mathsf{RatHistoryLedgerPolicy}$.

The relational rows are the matching five clauses of
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-concrete-continuation-instance-laws}. They
use the same carrier, classifier, and ledger predicate, but leave each product
endpoint as a named $\Cont$ result.

The identification of the two presentations is
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-functional-relational-instance-equivalence}.
Its endpoint bridge transports each append output to the corresponding
continuation endpoint and transports each continuation endpoint back to the
append output at the $R_D$ classifier surface.

It remains to record why the common object is strict. The one-sided unit
clauses in the append and relational packages both feed into
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-continuation-unit-endpoint-empty}, which
forces any such endpoint to be $\hsame\emp$.
\autoref{thm:rat-history-carrier-not-empty} then excludes this endpoint from
$\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}$. Hence no carried denominator supplies an internal
one-sided classifier unit, so the shared denominator structure is a strict
semigroup-with-ledger rather than a carried monoid.
\end{proof}

Per-object template: traditional target strict denominator multiplication
support for $\FieldUp(\mathsf{RatUp})$; BEDC source $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\Cont$,
$\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}$, $\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$, and
$\mathsf{RatHistoryLedgerPolicy}$; carrier choice: $C_D$; classifier choice:
$R_D$; stability obligations: carrier closure, commutative classifier,
associativity, binary classifier congruence, ledger closure, and carried unit
obstruction; ledger: denominator multiplication provenance through
$\mathsf{RatHistoryLedgerPolicy}$; core theorem:
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-append-continuation-strict-semigroup-nonmonoid-bridge};
exactness predicate: append and $\Cont$ endpoint bridge; standard bridge:
deferred; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.

\begin{theorem}[Rat denominator append-continuation final-exit obstruction guard]
\label{thm:rat-denominator-append-continuation-final-exit-obstruction-guard}
For the concrete data of
Definition~\ref{def:field-rat-denominator-concrete-continuation-instance} and
the final row inventory package of
Theorem~\ref{thm:ratup-fieldup-final-row-inventory-coverage}, the Phase
Algebra Hierarchy consumer receives both the final selector-row inventory and
the strict denominator guard. Explicitly, for every carried denominator
$u:\mathsf{BHist}$ with $C_D(u)$, the append presentation gives both

$$
(\forall x:\mathsf{BHist}.\;
C_D(x)\to R_D(\mu_D(x,u),x))\to \mathsf{False}
$$

and

$$
(\forall x:\mathsf{BHist}.\;
C_D(x)\to R_D(\mu_D(u,x),x))\to \mathsf{False}.
$$

The relational presentation gives both

$$
(\forall x,r:\mathsf{BHist}.\;
C_D(x)\to \Cont(x,u,r)\to R_D(r,x))\to \mathsf{False}
$$

and

$$
(\forall x,r:\mathsf{BHist}.\;
C_D(x)\to \Cont(u,x,r)\to R_D(r,x))\to \mathsf{False}.
$$

Thus the final $\FieldUp(\RatUp)$ row inventory and the denominator obstruction
arrive as one consumable guard: later algebra may use the selector exit rows,
but may not read the denominator semigroup as a carried multiplication with a
one-sided internal classifier unit.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The positive inventory is supplied by
Theorem~\ref{thm:ratup-fieldup-final-row-inventory-coverage}. It packages the
selector inverse-domain coverage row, the selector row-stability row, the
complete selector obstruction certificate, and the selector-chain boundary rows
for the concrete $\FieldUp(\RatUp)$ exit object.

The strict denominator rows are supplied by
Theorem~\ref{thm:field-rat-denominator-append-continuation-strict-semigroup-nonmonoid-bridge}.
Its append half gives closure, commutative classifier, associativity,
congruence, ledger closure, and the two displayed denials of one-sided append
classifier units.

For the relational half, use the same bridge theorem together with the
no-one-sided-unit rows formalized by
$\mathsf{BEDC.Derived.FieldUp.field\_rat\_denominator\_continuation\_commutative\_semigroup\_obstruction\_package}$.
Those rows deny both carried relational laws with endpoints named by $\Cont$.

Combining these two cited packages gives the advertised guard. The Phase
Algebra Hierarchy consumer may carry the final selector-row inventory forward,
while the denominator side remains a strict commutative semigroup-with-ledger
and not a carried monoid.
\end{proof}
