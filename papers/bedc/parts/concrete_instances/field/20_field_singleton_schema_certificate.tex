\begin{theorem}[Singleton empty-history FieldUp schema certificate]
\label{thm:singleton-empty-history-field-schema-certificate}
The tuple from
Definition~\ref{def:singleton-empty-history-field-schema-instance}, together
with the law package from
Theorem~\ref{thm:singleton-empty-history-field-schema-laws}, supplies the
carrier, source, pattern, classifier, stability, and ledger fields required by
Definition~\ref{def:field-certificate-obligations} over the concrete
$\mathsf{BHist}$ singleton source. Its ten-field certificate template is:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item Traditional target: the singleton proof-indexed $\FieldUp$ schema.
  \item BEDC source: $\mathsf{BHist}$ with $A_{\emp}$.
  \item Carrier choice: $A_{\emp}$.
  \item Classifier choice: $\rho_{\emp}$.
  \item Stability obligations: commutative-ring laws plus vacuous nonzero
  transport, inverse closure, and inverse laws.
  \item Ledger: the singleton endpoint and the empty nonzero witness domain.
  \item Core theorem list:
  Theorem~\ref{thm:singleton-empty-history-field-schema-laws} and the
  semantic certificate theorem for the same singleton carrier.
  \item Exactness predicate: singleton endpoint classification together with
  the $\mathsf{SemanticNameCert}$ source, pattern, and ledger fields.
  \item Standard bridge: the terminal proof-indexed schema.
  \item Status: CheckedCert.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The source and carrier fields are the singleton history source
$A_{\emp}$ from Definition~\ref{def:singleton-empty-history-field-schema-instance}.
The classifier field is $\rho_{\emp}$, whose only endpoints are the empty
histories admitted by $A_{\emp}$.

For the algebraic stability fields, use
Theorem~\ref{thm:singleton-empty-history-commring-laws}. It supplies the
commutative-ring part for the constant empty-history operations on the same
carrier and classifier.

For the field-specific clauses, apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:singleton-empty-history-field-schema-laws}. Nonzero transport,
inverse closure, inverse congruence, and the left and right inverse laws all
begin with a witness $p:\mathsf{FieldSingletonNonZero}(h)$.

Theorem~\ref{thm:field-singleton-nonzero-absurd} shows that
no such witness exists, while the inverse operation itself still has the total
proof-indexed form $\mathsf{inv}(h,p):=\emp$. The ledger therefore records only
the singleton endpoint and the empty witness domain, giving the required
concrete $\mathsf{BHist}$ FieldUp naming certificate template.
\end{proof}
