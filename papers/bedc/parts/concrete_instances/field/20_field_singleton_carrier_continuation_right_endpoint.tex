\begin{theorem}[Field singleton carrier continuation right endpoint iff]
\label{thm:field-singleton-carrier-continuation-right-endpoint-iff}
For histories $P,Q,R:\mathsf{BHist}$, if $P$ is a singleton field carrier
endpoint and $\ContR(P,Q,R)$, then $R$ is a singleton field carrier endpoint
exactly when $Q$ is a singleton field carrier endpoint:
$$
\mathsf{FieldSingletonCarrier}(P)\Rightarrow
\ContR(P,Q,R)\Rightarrow
\bigl(\mathsf{FieldSingletonCarrier}(R)\Longleftrightarrow
\mathsf{FieldSingletonCarrier}(Q)\bigr).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier witness for $P$ identifies the left endpoint with $\emp$. The
displayed continuation therefore has the same result as the right endpoint
$Q$. Transporting singleton-carrier membership along this equality gives the
forward implication, while singleton carrier continuation closure gives the
reverse implication.
\end{proof}
