\begin{theorem}[RatUp FieldUp restricted normalizer action automorphism and kernel exactness]
\label{thm:ratup-fieldup-restricted-normalizer-action-automorphism-kernel-exactness}
For Definition~\ref{def:ratup-fieldup-concrete-exit-object}, fix a certified
affine automorphism $A:\mathsf{AffAut}_F$ in the concrete
$\FieldUp(\RatUp)$ exit object. Let $C_F(A)$ be the affine centralizer carrier
from \autoref{def:ratup-fieldup-affine-centralizer-carrier}, and let
$N_F(C_F(A))$ be the normalizer carrier from
\autoref{def:ratup-fieldup-affine-centralizer-normalizer-carrier}. For
$T:\mathsf{AffAut}_F$ define
$$
\mathrm{Act}_T(X):=(T\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}X)\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}\iota_F(T).
$$
If $N_F(C_F(A))(T)$ and $C_F(A)(X)$, then $\mathrm{Act}_T$ is an
automorphism of the centralizer carrier with inverse action
$\mathrm{Act}_{\iota_F(T)}$:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $C_F(A)(\mathrm{Act}_{\iota_F(T)}(X))$.
\item
$$
\mathrm{Act}_{\iota_F(T)}(\mathrm{Act}_T(X))\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}X.
$$
\item
$$
\mathrm{Act}_T(\mathrm{Act}_{\iota_F(T)}(X))\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}X.
$$
\end{enumerate}
For every $T$ with $N_F(C_F(A))(T)$, the kernel of the restricted action is
exactly the pointwise centralizer of $C_F(A)$:
$$
(\forall X:\mathsf{AffAut}_F,\ C_F(A)(X)\Rightarrow
\mathrm{Act}_T(X)\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}X)
\Longleftrightarrow
(\forall X:\mathsf{AffAut}_F,\ C_F(A)(X)\Rightarrow C_F(X)(T)).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The inverse parameter lies in the same normalizer by the inverse-closure row of
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-centralizer-normalizer-subgroup}. Applying
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-centralizer-normalizer-action} to
$\iota_F(T)$ and the centralizer element $X$ gives
$C_F(A)(\mathrm{Act}_{\iota_F(T)}(X))$.

For the two inverse-action identities, apply the composition law of
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-centralizer-normalizer-action} first to
$\iota_F(T)$ after $T$, and then to $T$ after $\iota_F(T)$. The affine inverse
laws from
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-canonical-exit-action-inverse-package} identify the
parameters $\iota_F(T)\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}T$ and
$T\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}\iota_F(T)$ with $\mathrm{id}_F$. The identity-action row
of the restricted action then reduces both composites to $X$ under
$\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}$.

For the kernel equivalence, fix $X$ with $C_F(A)(X)$. By
\autoref{def:ratup-fieldup-affine-centralizer-carrier}, the assertion
$C_F(X)(T)$ says that $T$ commutes with $X$ in the concrete affine classifier.
The fixed-point criterion
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-centralizer-fixed-point-criterion} identifies
that commutation condition with
$\mathrm{Act}_T(X)\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}X$. Quantifying this equivalence over
all elements of $C_F(A)$ gives the displayed exactness statement.
\end{proof}

Per-object template: traditional target restricted normalizer action kernel
exactness; BEDC source concrete $\FieldUp(\RatUp)$ affine exit plus
$\GroupUp$; carrier $C_F(A)$ acted on by $N_F(C_F(A))$; classifier
$\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}$; stability inverse action, two-sided cancellation,
and pointwise kernel test; ledger fixed element, normalizer witness, acted
element, inverse parameter, and centralizer-of-acted-element witness; core
theorem list
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-centralizer-normalizer-subgroup},
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-centralizer-normalizer-action},
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-canonical-exit-action-inverse-package}, and
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-centralizer-fixed-point-criterion};
exactness predicate action kernel iff centralizes every element of $C_F(A)$;
standard bridge ordinary restricted normalizer action kernel; status
$\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.

\begin{theorem}[RatUp FieldUp affine centralizer normalizer action representation]
\label{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-centralizer-normalizer-action-representation}
For Definition~\ref{def:ratup-fieldup-concrete-exit-object}, fix a certified
affine automorphism $A:\mathsf{AffAut}_F$ in the concrete $\FieldUp(\RatUp)$
exit object. Let $C_F(A)$ be the affine centralizer carrier from
\autoref{def:ratup-fieldup-affine-centralizer-carrier}, let $N_F(C_F(A))$ be
the normalizer carrier from
\autoref{def:ratup-fieldup-affine-centralizer-normalizer-carrier}, and define
the restricted action by
$$
\mathrm{Act}_T(X):=\mathrm{Conj}_T(X)
=(T\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}X)\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}\iota_F(T).
$$
The assignment
$$
T\longmapsto \mathrm{Act}_T
$$
from $N_F(C_F(A))$ to classifier-exact automorphisms of $C_F(A)$ is a concrete
homomorphic action representation. Concretely, if
$N_F(C_F(A))(T)$, $N_F(C_F(A))(U)$, and $C_F(A)(X),C_F(A)(Y)$, then:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $C_F(A)(\mathrm{Act}_T(X))$.
\item
$$
\mathrm{Act}_T(X)\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}\mathrm{Act}_T(Y)
\Longleftrightarrow X\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}Y.
$$
\item
$$
\mathrm{Act}_{\mathrm{id}_F}(X)\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}X.
$$
\item
$$
\mathrm{Act}_T(\mathrm{Act}_U(X))\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}
\mathrm{Act}_{T\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}U}(X).
$$
\item $N_F(C_F(A))(\iota_F(T))$, and the inverse action is two-sided:
$$
\mathrm{Act}_{\iota_F(T)}(\mathrm{Act}_T(X))\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}X
\quad\text{and}\quad
\mathrm{Act}_T(\mathrm{Act}_{\iota_F(T)}(X))\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}X.
$$
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier and homomorphism laws are supplied by
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-centralizer-normalizer-action}. Its
preservation clause gives $C_F(A)(\mathrm{Act}_T(X))$, its identity clause gives
the action of $\mathrm{id}_F$, and its composition clause gives the displayed
law for $T\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}U$.

The subgroup certificate
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-centralizer-normalizer-subgroup} supplies the
normalizer bookkeeping for the representing source: identity membership, product
closure, and the inverse witness $N_F(C_F(A))(\iota_F(T))$.

For the target automorphism attached to $T$, apply
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-centralizer-normalizer-action-automorphism}.
Its classifier-exactness clause gives the displayed equivalence on
$\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}$, and its inverse-action clauses give the two-sided
cancellation formulas.

Finally,
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-conjugation-exactness} identifies these
clauses with concrete affine conjugation in the $\FieldUp(\RatUp)$ exit object.
Thus the map $T\mapsto\mathrm{Act}_T$ lands in the classifier-exact
automorphisms of $C_F(A)$ and respects the normalizer multiplication by the
stated composition law.
\end{proof}

Per-object template: traditional target normalizer action representation; BEDC
source concrete $\FieldUp(\RatUp)$ plus $\GroupUp$; carrier source
$N_F(C_F(A))$ and acted carrier $C_F(A)$; classifier $\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}$;
stability carrier preservation, classifier exactness, identity action,
composition action, and inverse action; ledger fixed element, normalizer
witnesses, acted elements, and inverse witness; core theorem list
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-centralizer-normalizer-subgroup},
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-centralizer-normalizer-action},
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-centralizer-normalizer-action-automorphism},
and \autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-conjugation-exactness}; exactness
predicate concrete homomorphic action representation into classifier-exact
automorphisms; standard bridge ordinary normalizer representation by
automorphisms; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.

\begin{definition}[RatUp FieldUp affine centralizer automorphism package]
\label{def:ratup-fieldup-affine-centralizer-automorphism-package}
For Definition~\ref{def:ratup-fieldup-concrete-exit-object}, fix a certified
affine automorphism $A:\mathsf{AffAut}_F$ in the concrete $\FieldUp(\RatUp)$
exit object. Let $C_F(A)$ be the affine centralizer carrier from
\autoref{def:ratup-fieldup-affine-centralizer-carrier}. For maps
$f,g:\mathsf{AffAut}_F\to\mathsf{AffAut}_F$, write
$\mathsf{CentAut}_A(f,g)$ for the following classifier-exact self-automorphism
package on the concrete carrier $C_F(A)$:
\begin{enumerate}
\item if $C_F(A)(X)$, then $C_F(A)(f(X))$;
\item if $C_F(A)(X)$, then $C_F(A)(g(X))$;
\item if $C_F(A)(X)$, then $g(f(X))\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}X$;
\item if $C_F(A)(X)$, then $f(g(X))\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}X$;
\item if $C_F(A)(X)$ and $C_F(A)(Y)$, then
$$
f(X)\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}f(Y)
\Longleftrightarrow X\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}Y.
$$
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[RatUp FieldUp affine centralizer normalizer action homomorphism]
\label{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-centralizer-normalizer-action-homomorphism}
For Definition~\ref{def:ratup-fieldup-concrete-exit-object}, fix a certified
affine automorphism $A:\mathsf{AffAut}_F$ in the concrete $\FieldUp(\RatUp)$
exit object. Let $C_F(A)$ be the affine centralizer carrier from
\autoref{def:ratup-fieldup-affine-centralizer-carrier}, let $N_F(C_F(A))$ be
the normalizer carrier from
\autoref{def:ratup-fieldup-affine-centralizer-normalizer-carrier}, and set
$$
\rho_A(T):=\mathrm{Act}_T,\qquad
\mathrm{Act}_T(X):=(T\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}X)\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}\iota_F(T).
$$
Then the assignment $\rho_A$ sends every $T$ with $N_F(C_F(A))(T)$ to
$$
\mathsf{CentAut}_A(\mathrm{Act}_T,\mathrm{Act}_{\iota_F(T)}).
$$
It respects the affine classifier on normalizer elements: if
$N_F(C_F(A))(T)$, $N_F(C_F(A))(U)$, and $T\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}U$, then for
every $X$ with $C_F(A)(X)$,
$$
\rho_A(T)(X)\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}\rho_A(U)(X).
$$
It sends $\mathrm{id}_F$ to the identity action,
$$
\rho_A(\mathrm{id}_F)(X)\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}X,
$$
and sends affine products to composite actions: if $N_F(C_F(A))(T)$,
$N_F(C_F(A))(U)$, and $C_F(A)(X)$, then
$$
\rho_A(T\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}U)(X)\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}
\rho_A(T)(\rho_A(U)(X)).
$$
The inverse action in the automorphism package is
$\mathrm{Act}_{\iota_F(T)}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The automorphism package is supplied pointwise by
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-centralizer-normalizer-action-automorphism}.
For a fixed normalizer witness $N_F(C_F(A))(T)$, that theorem gives inverse
normalizer membership for $\iota_F(T)$, preservation of $C_F(A)$ by both
$\mathrm{Act}_T$ and $\mathrm{Act}_{\iota_F(T)}$, the two cancellation laws
$\mathrm{Act}_{\iota_F(T)}(\mathrm{Act}_T(X))\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}X$ and
$\mathrm{Act}_T(\mathrm{Act}_{\iota_F(T)}(X))\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}X$, and the
classifier-exact equivalence for the forward action.

The normalizer domain used by this package is the subgroup supplied by
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-centralizer-normalizer-subgroup}. Its identity,
product, inverse, and classifier-transport rows make the source of $\rho_A$ an
ordinary classifier group on the concrete normalizer carrier.

Classifier respect follows by applying
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-inner-action-composition-exit} to the two
conjugation words determined by $T$ and $U$. The affine classifier hypothesis
$T\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}U$ transports the outer conjugating parameter and its
canonical inverse through the same pointwise classifier, so the resulting
actions agree on every $C_F(A)$ element.

The identity row is exactly the identity action clause of
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-centralizer-normalizer-action}. The source
identity belongs to $N_F(C_F(A))$ by
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-centralizer-normalizer-subgroup}, and the
displayed comparison is the classifier identity on the centralizer element.

For products, first use
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-centralizer-normalizer-action} to keep
$\mathrm{Act}_U(X)$ inside $C_F(A)$. Then apply
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-inner-action-composition-exit}; it identifies
the inner action of $T\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}U$ with the composite action by $U$
followed by $T$ under $\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}$. This is the displayed homomorphism
law for $\rho_A$.

The inverse action row is the inverse half of the same automorphism package.
For every normalizer element $T$, the inverse-closure row of
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-centralizer-normalizer-subgroup} places
$\iota_F(T)$ back in the source carrier, and
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-centralizer-normalizer-action-automorphism}
identifies $\mathrm{Act}_{\iota_F(T)}$ as the inverse self-action on $C_F(A)$.
\end{proof}

Per-object template: target normalizer representation; source concrete
$\FieldUp(\RatUp)$ affine exit; acted carrier $C_F(A)$; source carrier
$N_F(C_F(A))$; action classifier $\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}$; automorphism package
$\mathsf{CentAut}_A$; identity law for $\mathrm{id}_F$; product law for
$T\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}U$; inverse action $\mathrm{Act}_{\iota_F(T)}$; standard
bridge ordinary homomorphism into centralizer automorphisms.

\begin{theorem}[RatUp FieldUp restricted normalizer action kernel normality]
\label{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-normalizer-action-kernel-normal}
For Definition~\ref{def:ratup-fieldup-affine-normalizer-action-kernel-carrier},
the carrier $K_A$ is the normal kernel of the restricted normalizer action on
$C_F(A)$. Concretely:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $K_A(\mathrm{id}_F)$.
\item If $K_A(T)$ and $K_A(U)$, then $K_A(T\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}U)$.
\item If $K_A(T)$, then $K_A(\iota_F(T))$.
\item If $K_A(T)$ and $N_F(C_F(A))(U)$, then
$$
K_A(U\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}T\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}\iota_F(U)).
$$
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The identity element belongs to $K_A$ because
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-centralizer-normalizer-subgroup} supplies
$N_F(C_F(A))(\mathrm{id}_F)$, and the representation identity row in
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-centralizer-normalizer-action-representation}
gives $\mathrm{Act}_{\mathrm{id}_F}(X)\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}X$ for every
$C_F(A)$ element.

For product closure, the same subgroup certificate gives
$N_F(C_F(A))(T\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}U)$. Given $C_F(A)(X)$, the composition row
of
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-centralizer-normalizer-action-representation}
identifies $\mathrm{Act}_{T\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}U}(X)$ with
$\mathrm{Act}_T(\mathrm{Act}_U(X))$. The fixed-point hypothesis for $K_A(U)$
reduces the inner action to $X$, and the fixed-point hypothesis for $K_A(T)$
then reduces the outer action to $X$.

For inverse closure, the inverse row of
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-centralizer-normalizer-subgroup} places
$\iota_F(T)$ in $N_F(C_F(A))$. For $X$ in $C_F(A)$, the inverse-action rows of
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-restricted-normalizer-action-automorphism-kernel-exactness}
make $\mathrm{Act}_{\iota_F(T)}$ the two-sided inverse of
$\mathrm{Act}_T$ on the centralizer carrier. Since $K_A(T)$ makes
$\mathrm{Act}_T$ pointwise equal to the identity action, its inverse action is
pointwise equal to the identity action as well.

For conjugation, subgroup closure first gives
$N_F(C_F(A))(U\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}T\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}\iota_F(U))$. Fix
$X$ with $C_F(A)(X)$. The inverse-preservation row puts
$\mathrm{Act}_{\iota_F(U)}(X)$ back in $C_F(A)$, so the hypothesis $K_A(T)$
fixes that acted element. Applying $\mathrm{Act}_U$ and then using the
two-sided inverse-action law for $U$ reduces the conjugated action to $X$.
Thus every conjugate of a kernel element by a normalizer element again lies in
$K_A$.
\end{proof}

exactness predicate pointwise fixed restricted action on $C_F(A)$; standard
bridge ordinary normal kernel of a group action representation; downstream
consumer faithful quotient representation of the normalizer on centralizer
orbits; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
