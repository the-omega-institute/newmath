\begin{theorem}[Rat denominator unit carrier transport-closure package]
\label{thm:rat-denom-unit-carrier-transport-closure-package}
For concrete histories $h,k,r:\mathsf{BHist}$, the empty-unit denominator
carrier has the following two concrete closure properties:
\begin{enumerate}
\item if $\mathsf{RatDenomUnitCarrier}(h)$ and $h\hsame k$, then
$\mathsf{RatDenomUnitCarrier}(k)$;
\item if $\mathsf{RatDenomUnitCarrier}(h)$,
$\mathsf{RatDenomUnitCarrier}(k)$, and $\Cont(h,k,r)$, then
$\mathsf{RatDenomUnitCarrier}(r)$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold Definition~\ref{def:field-rat-denominator-unit-carrier}. For the
transport clause, split the carrier witness for $h$. In the Rat branch,
\texttt{BEDC.Derived.RatUp.RatHistoryCarrier\_hsame\_transport} carries the
Rat endpoint witness across $h\hsame k$. In the empty branch, transport the
empty-endpoint comparison across $h\hsame k$; the result belongs to the left
disjunct for $k$.

For continuation closure, split the two input carrier witnesses. In the
empty/empty branch, transport the displayed continuation to the empty-left and
empty-right unit continuation at $\emp$, so the result is $\hsame$ to $\emp$.
This is the left disjunct of the unit carrier.

In the empty/Rat branch, transport the continuation to an empty-left
continuation and use the left unit result law to compare $r$ with the Rat
endpoint. Rat carrier transport then gives the Rat branch for $r$.

The Rat/empty branch is symmetric: transport to an empty-right continuation,
use the right unit result law, and carry the Rat witness across the resulting
$\hsame$ comparison.

In the Rat/Rat branch, apply the concrete Rat denominator continuation carrier
closure theorem. The result is a Rat endpoint, hence it lies in the right
disjunct of $\mathsf{RatDenomUnitCarrier}(r)$.
\end{proof}
