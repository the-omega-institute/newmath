\begin{theorem}[RatUp FieldUp normalized contextual singleton-classifier separation]
\label{thm:ratup-fieldup-normalized-contextual-singleton-classifier-separation}
For the concrete source tuple $\mathsf{RatUpFieldSource}_F$, the concrete
denominator tuple $(C_D^u,R_D^u,\mu_D^u,e_D^u,N_D^u)$, and the contextual
action $\Gamma$ of
\autoref{def:ratup-fieldup-denominator-contextual-action-instance}, suppose
$C_D^u(h)$, $C_D^u(k)$, $C_D^u(l)$, $C_D^u(r)$, $C_D^u(l')$, and
$C_D^u(r')$. Suppose also that $p\hsame\emp$, $q\hsame\emp$, $p'\hsame\emp$,
$q'\hsame\emp$, $\neg N_D^u(l)$, $\neg N_D^u(r)$, $\neg N_D^u(l')$,
$\neg N_D^u(r')$, and $N_D^u(h)$. Let
$x:=\Gamma_{p',q',l',r'}(\Gamma_{p,q,l,r}(h))$ and let
$y:=\Gamma_{p',q',\mu_D^u(l',l),\mu_D^u(r,r')}(h)$. Then for all
$L,R:\mathsf{BHist}$,
$$
\mathsf{fieldSingletonEmptyClassifier}(\mathsf{append}(L,x),\mathsf{append}(R,y))
\to\bot.
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-normalized-contextual-separation-package} to the
same source tuple, denominator tuple, contextual action, carried histories,
empty endpoint hypotheses, empty-support context hypotheses, and strict-support
witness. The package supplies the normalized classifier row $R_D^u(x,y)$ and
the strict-support transport
$N_D^u(x)\Longleftrightarrow N_D^u(h)$.

Use the displayed witness $N_D^u(h)$ and the transport equivalence to recover
$N_D^u(x)$. The denominator boundary identifies this strict support with the
genuine Rat carrier, so $x$ is carried by $\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}$.

Unfold the normalized classifier row through the same denominator boundary.
Together with the Rat carrier recovered at $x$, the row packages the concrete
classifier $\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(x,y)$ for the normalized pair.

Now fix arbitrary histories $L,R:\mathsf{BHist}$. Apply
\autoref{thm:field-singleton-empty-classifier-append-rat-classifier-absurd} to
the displayed $\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(x,y)$. It rejects every proposed
singleton empty classifier on
$\mathsf{append}(L,x)$ and $\mathsf{append}(R,y)$, which is the asserted
noncollapse.
\end{proof}
