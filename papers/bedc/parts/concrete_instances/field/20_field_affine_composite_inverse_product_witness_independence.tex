\begin{theorem}[Field affine composite inverse product-witness independence]
\label{thm:field-affine-composite-inverse-product-witness-independence}
Under the notation of
\autoref{thm:field-affine-composite-inverse-reverse-coherence}, fix the
collected coefficients
$$
A=\mathsf{mul}(a',a),
\qquad
B=\mathsf{mul}(b,b'),
\qquad
D=\mathsf{add}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a',d),b'),d').
$$
Let $p_A,p'_A:\mathsf{NonZero}_F(A)$ and
$p_B,p'_B:\mathsf{NonZero}_F(B)$ be two choices of product nonzero witnesses.
For each $q:\mathsf{BHist}$ define
$$
s_{21}^{p_A,p_B}(q)=
\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{inv}(A,p_A),
\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{add}(q,\mathsf{neg}(D)),\mathsf{inv}(B,p_B)))
$$
and define $s_{21}^{p'_A,p'_B}(q)$ by the same displayed formula with
$p'_A,p'_B$ in place of $p_A,p_B$. Then every $q:\mathsf{BHist}$ satisfies
the three comparisons
\begin{enumerate}
\item

$$
s_{21}^{p_A,p_B}(q)\hsame s_{21}^{p'_A,p'_B}(q).
$$
\item

$$
s_{21}^{p_A,p_B}(q)\hsame s_1(s_2(q)).
$$
\item

$$
s_{21}^{p'_A,p'_B}(q)\hsame s_1(s_2(q)).
$$
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
We first compare the two collected inverse terms. Apply
\autoref{thm:field-affine-product-witness-independence} to the two composite
affine members with common coefficients $A,B,D$ and witness pairs
$(p_A,p_B)$ and $(p'_A,p'_B)$. Its inverse-formula field gives the first
displayed comparison pointwise in $q$.

For agreement with the reverse-composed inverse, apply
\autoref{thm:field-affine-composite-inverse-reverse-coherence} with the
witness pair $(p_A,p_B)$ to obtain the second displayed comparison. Apply the
same theorem with $(p'_A,p'_B)$ to obtain the third displayed comparison.

The classifier $\hsame$ is transitive and symmetric, so these three
comparisons form a single certificate: changing product witnesses does not
change the collected explicit inverse, and each collected representative
agrees with the reverse-composed inverse $s_1(s_2(q))$.
\end{proof}
