\section{Field square apartness inverse descent}

\begin{theorem}[Field square-certified two-sided affine exactness]
\label{thm:field-square-certified-two-sided-affine-exact}
For a BEDC history-level $\FieldUp$ candidate satisfying the hypotheses of
\autoref{thm:field-square-nonzero-one-sided-mul-exact},
\autoref{thm:field-affine-two-sided-equation-exact-from-apartness}, and
\autoref{thm:field-affine-two-sided-map-classifier-exact-from-apartness},
assume the field interpretation identifies $\mathsf{NonZero}_F(h)$ with
$$
\mathsf{ApartZero}_F(h) := (h\hsame0\to\bot).
$$
For $a,b:\mathsf{BHist}$ with square witnesses
$$
p_{a^2}:\mathsf{NonZero}_F(\mathsf{mul}(a,a)),
\quad
p_{b^2}:\mathsf{NonZero}_F(\mathsf{mul}(b,b)),
$$
let $p_a:\mathsf{NonZero}_F(a)$ and $p_b:\mathsf{NonZero}_F(b)$ be the
witnesses induced by zero-factor exclusion and the displayed identification.
Then for every $c,d,x,y:\mathsf{BHist}$ both clauses hold:
$$
\mathsf{add}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a,x),b),d)\hsame c
\Longleftrightarrow
x\hsame
\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{inv}(a,p_a),
\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{add}(c,\mathsf{neg}(d)),\mathsf{inv}(b,p_b)))
$$
and
$$
\mathsf{add}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a,x),b),d)\hsame
\mathsf{add}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a,y),b),d)
\Longleftrightarrow
x\hsame y.
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:field-product-nonzero-excludes-zero-factors} to
$p_{a^2}$ with both product entries instantiated by $a$. As in
\autoref{thm:field-square-nonzero-one-sided-mul-exact}, the first factor
clause gives $a\hsame0\to\bot$, and the identification with
$\mathsf{NonZero}_F(a)$ supplies the witness $p_a$.

Apply the same zero-factor exclusion theorem to $p_{b^2}$ with both product
entries instantiated by $b$. The first factor clause gives
$b\hsame0\to\bot$, and the displayed identification supplies the witness
$p_b:\mathsf{NonZero}_F(b)$.

With these two induced witnesses, invoke
\autoref{thm:field-affine-two-sided-equation-exact-from-apartness}. Its
equivalence is exactly the stated solution formula for
$\mathsf{add}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a,x),b),d)\hsame c$.

For the classifier exactness clause, invoke
\autoref{thm:field-affine-two-sided-map-classifier-exact-from-apartness} with
the same witnesses $p_a$ and $p_b$ and the same offset $d$. This removes the
common certified affine map and yields precisely
$\mathsf{add}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a,x),b),d)\hsame
\mathsf{add}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a,y),b),d)$ if and only if
$x\hsame y$.
\end{proof}
