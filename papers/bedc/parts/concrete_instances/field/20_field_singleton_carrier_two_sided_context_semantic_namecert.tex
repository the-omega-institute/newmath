\begin{theorem}[Field singleton carrier two-sided context semantic name certificate]
\label{thm:field-singleton-carrier-two-sided-context-semantic-namecert}
For histories $L,R:\mathsf{BHist}$, if $L$ and $R$ lie in the field singleton
carrier, then the two-sided contextual carrier has a semantic name certificate
whose source, pattern, and ledger predicates are that same contextual carrier,
and whose classifier compares the two contextualized endpoints by the field
singleton classifier:
$$
\mathsf{FieldSingletonCarrier}(L)\Rightarrow
\mathsf{FieldSingletonCarrier}(R)\Rightarrow
\mathsf{SemanticNameCert}
\bigl(h\mapsto
  \mathsf{FieldSingletonCarrier}(\mathsf{append}(L,\mathsf{append}(h,R)))\bigr)
\bigl(h\mapsto
  \mathsf{FieldSingletonCarrier}(\mathsf{append}(L,\mathsf{append}(h,R)))\bigr)
\bigl(h\mapsto
  \mathsf{FieldSingletonCarrier}(\mathsf{append}(L,\mathsf{append}(h,R)))\bigr)
\bigl(h,k\mapsto
  \mathsf{FieldSingletonClassifier}
    (\mathsf{append}(L,\mathsf{append}(h,R)),
     \mathsf{append}(L,\mathsf{append}(k,R)))\bigr).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Let $C_{L,R}(h)$ denote the displayed two-sided contextual carrier. The
inhabited point is $h=\emp$: the carrier witnesses for $L$ and $R$, together
with $\emp\hsame\emp$, rebuild the nested empty append witness for
$\mathsf{append}(L,\mathsf{append}(\emp,R))$.

For reflexivity, symmetry, and transitivity of the classifier, unpack the
displayed singleton classifier. Its endpoint carrier fields are precisely the
instances of $C_{L,R}$ at the endpoints, and the comparison field is an
$\hsame$ witness between the two contextualized histories. The three laws are
therefore inherited from reflexivity, symmetry, and transitivity of $\hsame$.

It remains to transport carrier membership along the classifier. Apply
\autoref{thm:field-singleton-carrier-append-context-empty} to identify
$C_{L,R}(h)$ with the underlying singleton carrier of $h$, and use
\autoref{thm:field-singleton-classifier-append-hsame-common-context-cancel}
with the reflexive context comparisons for $L$ and $R$ to cancel the common
two-sided context. The transported endpoint carrier repacks into the source,
pattern, and ledger fields of the required $\mathsf{SemanticNameCert}$.
\end{proof}
