\begin{theorem}[Rat denominator bilateral strict factor cancellation]
\label{thm:field-rat-denominator-bilateral-strict-factor-cancellation}
For the concrete tuple
$(C_D^\ast,R_D^\ast,\mu_D^\ast,e_D^\ast,N_D^\ast)$ of
\autoref{def:field-rat-denominator-fieldup-multiplicative-exactness-instance},
let $h,k,l,r:\mathsf{BHist}$. If $C_D^\ast(h)$, $C_D^\ast(k)$,
$C_D^\ast(l)$, $C_D^\ast(r)$, $N_D^\ast(l)$, and $N_D^\ast(r)$, then
$$
R_D^\ast(\mu_D^\ast(l,\mu_D^\ast(h,r)),
\mu_D^\ast(l,\mu_D^\ast(k,r)))
\Longleftrightarrow R_D^\ast(h,k).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First build the products to which cancellation will be applied. The
strict-support product closure and carrier closure supplied by
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-fieldup-multiplicative-certificate-boundary}
place $\mu_D^\ast(l,h)$ and $\mu_D^\ast(l,k)$ in $C_D^\ast$, and keep the
larger products with the strict factor $r$ inside the same concrete carrier.
The associativity and congruence fields of that boundary identify the displayed
comparison with
$R_D^\ast(\mu_D^\ast(\mu_D^\ast(l,h),r),
\mu_D^\ast(\mu_D^\ast(l,k),r))$.

Apply
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-unit-envelope-cancellation-exactness} with
the strict common right factor $r$. Since $N_D^\ast(r)$ and the two left
products are carried by the preceding closure step, this gives
$$
R_D^\ast(\mu_D^\ast(\mu_D^\ast(l,h),r),
\mu_D^\ast(\mu_D^\ast(l,k),r))
\Longleftrightarrow
R_D^\ast(\mu_D^\ast(l,h),\mu_D^\ast(l,k)).
$$

Apply the same cancellation theorem with the strict common left factor $l$.
The hypotheses $C_D^\ast(h)$, $C_D^\ast(k)$, $C_D^\ast(l)$, and
$N_D^\ast(l)$ are exactly its input hypotheses for the left-cancellation
clause, so
$$
R_D^\ast(\mu_D^\ast(l,h),\mu_D^\ast(l,k))
\Longleftrightarrow R_D^\ast(h,k).
$$
Composing the two equivalences gives the bilateral strict-factor cancellation
claim.
\end{proof}
