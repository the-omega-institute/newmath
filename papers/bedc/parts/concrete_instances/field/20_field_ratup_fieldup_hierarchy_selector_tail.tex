\begin{theorem}[RatUp FieldUp five-factor product-map classifier exactness]
\label{thm:ratup-fieldup-five-factor-product-map-classifier-exactness}
For the concrete $\mathsf{RatUpFieldSource}_F$ operation rows over
$\mathsf{BHist}$, assume the proof-indexed inverse, multiplication congruence,
unit, associativity, product nonzero exclusion, and apartness-conversion rows
used by
\autoref{thm:field-five-factor-product-equation-exact-from-apartness}. For
five histories $a,b,c,d,e:\mathsf{BHist}$, set
$$
P_5:=\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a,b),c),d),e).
$$
Let $p_{abcde}:\mathsf{NonZero}_F(P_5)$ be the product ledger witness, and let
$R_5$ be the reversed inverse spine supplied by
\autoref{thm:field-inverse-five-factor-product-reversal-from-apartness}. Then
for every $x,y:\mathsf{BHist}$, multiplication by the same five-factor product
is classifier-exact on both sides:
$$
\mathsf{mul}(x,P_5)\hsame\mathsf{mul}(y,P_5)
\Longleftrightarrow
x\hsame y
$$
and
$$
\mathsf{mul}(P_5,x)\hsame\mathsf{mul}(P_5,y)
\Longleftrightarrow
x\hsame y.
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the forward direction of the right-product clause, apply
\autoref{thm:field-five-factor-product-equation-exact-from-apartness} to the
equation $\mathsf{mul}(x,P_5)\hsame\mathsf{mul}(y,P_5)$ with shared target
endpoint $\mathsf{mul}(y,P_5)$. It classifies $x$ by the solved endpoint
$\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(y,P_5),R_5)$.

Apply the same theorem to the reflexive right-product equation for $y$ at that
same target endpoint. The two solved endpoints are identical, so symmetry and
transitivity of $\hsame$ compare $x$ and $y$.

For the reverse direction of the right-product clause, use multiplication
congruence to transport $x\hsame y$ through
$z\mapsto\mathsf{mul}(z,P_5)$. This gives
$\mathsf{mul}(x,P_5)\hsame\mathsf{mul}(y,P_5)$.

For the forward direction of the left-product clause, apply
\autoref{thm:field-five-factor-product-equation-exact-from-apartness} to the
equation $\mathsf{mul}(P_5,x)\hsame\mathsf{mul}(P_5,y)$ with shared target
endpoint $\mathsf{mul}(P_5,y)$. The left equation solver classifies $x$ by
$\mathsf{mul}(R_5,\mathsf{mul}(P_5,y))$.

Apply the same left-product exactness clause to the reflexive equation for
$y$ at the same target endpoint. The solved endpoint is again identical, and
symmetry plus transitivity of $\hsame$ yields $x\hsame y$.

For the reverse direction of the left-product clause, multiplication
congruence transports $x\hsame y$ through
$z\mapsto\mathsf{mul}(P_5,z)$, producing the displayed left classifier
equality.
\end{proof}
