\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\SchwarzReflectionRouteUp$}

The $\SchwarzReflectionRouteUp$ packet realizes the reflection route isolated by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-analytic-continuation} as finite NameCert data. It is a concrete operation-route sibling of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-analyticcontinuationoperation-namecert}, using the holomorphic source interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-holomorphic-namecert}, the differentiability control of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-complex-diff-namecert}, and the complex carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-complex-namecert}.

\begin{definition}[Schwarz reflection route carrier]
\label{def:schwarz-reflection-route-carrier}
A $\SchwarzReflectionRouteUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(U,B,J,O,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $U$ is the holomorphic source row, recording the upper-side domain, the source function history, and the $\HolomorphicUp$ certificate over the visible side of the boundary;
\item $B$ is the real-boundary trace row, recording the boundary segment, the continuity trace to that segment, and the real-value readback used by the reflection route;
\item $J$ is the conjugation/reflection row, recording the domain reflection, the input conjugation $z\mapsto \overline{z}$, and the output conjugation $w\mapsto \overline{w}$ as displayed $\ComplexUp$ routes;
\item $O$ is the reflected-output row, recording the lower-side function history whose value is read as $\overline{f(\overline{z})}$ through the rows $U,B,J$;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $U,B,J,O$ and for the replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ replay routes from the source holomorphic row to the boundary trace, from the boundary trace to the conjugation/reflection row, and from that row to the reflected output;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\AnalyticContUp$, $\HolomorphicUp$, $\ComplexDiffUp$, $\ComplexUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\SchwarzReflectionRouteUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains exactly these rows. It has no coordinate for a global analytic-continuation theorem, no monodromy selector, no quotient germ, no host complex-analysis equality, and no RH-facing zero-location predicate.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Schwarz reflection route NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:schwarz-reflection-route-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\SchwarzReflectionRouteUp}$ surface for \autoref{def:schwarz-reflection-route-carrier} consists of carrier admission for
$$
S=(U,B,J,O,H,C,P,N),
$$
holomorphic source exposure, real-boundary trace exposure, conjugation/reflection exposure, reflected-output routing, componentwise $\hsame$ stability, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming. Every accepted consumer of the reflected output must first read the rows $U,B,J,H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:schwarz-reflection-route-carrier}. The packet displays the source, boundary, reflection, output, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows before any reflected value is public.

The analytic side is then read through existing sibling certificates. The row $U$ is a $\HolomorphicUp$ source row over $\ComplexUp$ histories, and the reflection test uses the complex-differentiability control named by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-complex-diff-namecert}; neither row is replaced by host complex equality.

The route rows $H$ and $C$ keep the same packet visible under transport and replay. The provenance row $P$ records the dependence on $\AnalyticContUp$, $\HolomorphicUp$, $\ComplexDiffUp$, and $\ComplexUp$, while $N$ supplies only the local name for this route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Schwarz reflection route boundary symmetry]
\label{thm:schwarz-reflection-route-boundary-symmetry}
For an accepted $\SchwarzReflectionRouteUp$ carrier $S=(U,B,J,O,H,C,P,N)$, the real-boundary trace row $B$ is the only boundary symmetry datum used by the reflected-output row $O$. If the reflected route is read at a boundary point already displayed in $B$, then the source-side and reflected-side reads are compared only by the $\hsame$ transports in $H$ and the replay routes in $C$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the boundary row $B$ from the carrier. It stores the boundary segment, the continuity trace, and the real-value readback that makes conjugation inert at the boundary.

Next project the conjugation/reflection row $J$ and the reflected-output row $O$. The output route is defined as a read through the source value followed by displayed input and output conjugation, so its boundary comparison has no extra datum beyond $B,J,H,C$.

Finally use the packet inventory. Since no carrier coordinate stores a host boundary theorem, a quotient germ, or a global analytic-continuation principle, boundary symmetry can be exported only as the displayed finite route through $B,H,C$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Schwarz reflection route public handoff]
\label{thm:schwarz-reflection-route-public-handoff}
Every $\AnalyticContUp$, $\HolomorphicUp$, contour-facing, or zeta-continuation-facing consumer of an accepted $\SchwarzReflectionRouteUp$ packet receives only the rows
$$
U,B,J,O,H,C,P,N
$$
and their displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ structure. The handoff presents a boundary-symmetric reflection route, not a global branch selector, monodromy theorem, quotient-germ construction, RH predicate, or zero locator.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with \autoref{thm:schwarz-reflection-route-namecert-obligations}. It exposes the finite carrier and forces the reflected-output read through the holomorphic source, real-boundary trace, conjugation/reflection, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

For analytic-continuation and holomorphic consumers, use the dependency rows named in $P$. They identify this packet as a route over $\AnalyticContUp$ and $\HolomorphicUp$ rather than as an independent continuation theorem.

For contour-facing and zeta-facing consumers, the same provenance row supplies only a finite operation-route dependency. The consumer can cite the reflected-output row $O$, but it cannot extract a hidden branch, global zero classifier, or RH-facing predicate from the packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Schwarz reflection route non-escape]
\label{thm:schwarz-reflection-route-nonescape}
An accepted $\SchwarzReflectionRouteUp$ carrier exports no object outside $U,B,J,O,H,C,P,N$. In particular, it exports no ambient Schwarz reflection principle, no selected analytic branch outside the reflected route, no monodromy closure, no quotient-germ equality, no host complex-analysis equality, and no non-trivial-zero locator.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier of \autoref{def:schwarz-reflection-route-carrier} is a finite tuple with eight displayed rows. Any public read must project one of those rows or move through the displayed transport and replay rows.

The excluded objects are absent from that tuple. They are not source, boundary, reflection, output, transport, replay, provenance, or local naming rows.

Thus a consumer may use the packet as a reflected continuation route, but no consumer can read it as a global analytic theorem, a branch-selection device, a quotient construction, a host equality principle, or a zero-location certificate.
\end{proof}
