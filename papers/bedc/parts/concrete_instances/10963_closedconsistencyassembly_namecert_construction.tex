\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ClosedConsistencyAssemblyUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-closed-consistency-assembly-namecert}
\origin{ai-composite}

The $\ClosedConsistencyAssemblyUp$ packet realizes the assembly view of \autoref{ch:closed-metacic-consistency-assembly} as a finite composite NameCert surface. It sits beside \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closed-normal-consistency-boundary-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-normalfalseexclusion-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closed-consistency-reduction-boundary-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-normal-form-consistency-seal-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closednormalconsistencypacket-namecert}: those siblings expose the positive normal-form exclusion and the subject-reduction boundary in separate packets, while the present chapter binds their rows into one composite naming certificate.

\begin{definition}[Closed-consistency assembly carrier]
\label{def:closed-consistency-assembly-carrier}
A $\ClosedConsistencyAssemblyUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
A=(L,T,E,F,S,O,D,R,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $L$ is the closedness row for the inspected MetaCIC proof candidate;
\item $T$ is the empty-context typing row for the same proof candidate and its displayed false type;
\item $E$ is the typed normal endpoint row, including beta-strong normality and the endpoint false-type read;
\item $F$ is the normal false-proof exclusion row consumed at the endpoint;
\item $S$ is the subject-reduction boundary row separating the arbitrary closed source from the typed-normal endpoint;
\item $O$ is the obstruction row recording the dependent-codomain reduction frontier;
\item $D$ is the assembly ledger row joining closedness, endpoint, exclusion, subject-reduction boundary, and obstruction entries;
\item $R$ records displayed $\Cont$ routes for every consumer read of the assembly packet;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over the closed-normal consistency boundary, normal false exclusion, reduction-boundary, normal-form seal, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ClosedConsistencyAssemblyUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no coordinate for full closed consistency, no hidden subject-reduction theorem, no endpoint transport outside $S$, and no host-level proof search channel.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Closed-consistency assembly classifier]
\label{def:closed-consistency-assembly-classifier}
Two $\ClosedConsistencyAssemblyUp$ carriers
$$
A=(L,T,E,F,S,O,D,R,P,N)
\quad\hbox{and}\quad
A'=(L',T',E',F',S',O',D',R',P',N')
$$
are classified by the componentwise $\hsame$ comparison of the displayed rows together with route replay through $R,R'$ and provenance comparison through $P,P'$. The classifier is undefined on any attempted comparison that omits $S$ or $O$, because the assembly route is a boundary certificate rather than a proof of closed subject reduction.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Closed-consistency assembly NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:closed-consistency-assembly-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:closed-consistency-assembly-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\ClosedConsistencyAssemblyUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $A$, classifier exactness for the rows $L,T,E,F,S,O,D,R,P,N$, closedness readback, typed-normal endpoint readback, normal false-proof exclusion, subject-reduction boundary exposure, obstruction-ledger exposure, displayed continuation replay, provenance accounting, and local naming.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is the finite row list in \autoref{def:closed-consistency-assembly-carrier}. The classifier of \autoref{def:closed-consistency-assembly-classifier} compares only those displayed rows, so no unlisted proof object or host equality can enter the NameCert surface.

The positive consistency read is inherited as rows, not as an ambient theorem. Closedness and typing are read through $L,T$; the typed normal endpoint is read through $E$; and the normal false-proof exclusion is read through $F$, matching the closed-normal packets in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closednormalconsistencypacket-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-normal-form-consistency-seal-namecert}.

The boundary rows are equally part of the certificate. The subject-reduction row $S$ and obstruction row $O$ record the frontier named in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closed-consistency-reduction-boundary-namecert}. Since $D,R,P,N$ only ledger, replay, package, and locally name the same surface, the local NameCert obligations are exhausted by the stated rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Closed-consistency assembly boundary nonescape]
\label{thm:closed-consistency-assembly-boundary-nonescape}
Every accepted $\ClosedConsistencyAssemblyUp$ carrier keeps full closed consistency outside the exported route. A consumer may read closedness, empty-context typing, the typed-normal endpoint, normal false-proof exclusion, the subject-reduction boundary, obstruction ledger, replay routes, provenance, and local naming, but it cannot export a closed subject-reduction theorem or an arbitrary closed proof contradiction detached from $S$ and $O$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier $A=(L,T,E,F,S,O,D,R,P,N)$. The positive rows $L,T,E,F$ give the closed-normal endpoint route, while $S$ and $O$ remain displayed boundary rows rather than discharged theorems.

The continuation row $R$ only replays the displayed rows, and the package row $P$ records provenance over the sibling packets already cited by the chapter. Neither row contains a coordinate for full closed consistency or for an independent subject-reduction theorem.

Thus every consumer route remains inside the finite assembly packet. Detaching the contradiction read from $S$ and $O$ would require an unlisted row, so the boundary cannot escape through this NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Closed-consistency assembly sibling routing]
\label{thm:closed-consistency-assembly-sibling-routing}
The $\ClosedConsistencyAssemblyUp$ packet routes every public read through the sibling surfaces named in its provenance row: closed-normal boundary data, normal false-proof exclusion, closed-consistency reduction boundary, normal-form consistency seal, and closed-normal consistency packet data. No public read bypasses those siblings by appealing directly to an ambient MetaCIC consistency principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the provenance row $P$. It names the sibling packet sources for the positive normal-form route and for the reduction boundary route, so the assembly consumer obtains a composite route rather than a new primitive consistency principle.

The closed-normal and normal-form siblings supply $L,T,E,F$ as finite rows. The reduction-boundary sibling supplies the displayed $S,O$ frontier. The ledger and continuation rows $D,R$ join these routes without identifying the frontier with a discharged subject-reduction theorem.

Therefore sibling routing is exhaustive for public reads of the assembly carrier. Any route that skips the sibling packet rows would have no carrier coordinate in $A$ and no local $\NameCert_{\ClosedConsistencyAssemblyUp}$ admission.
\end{proof}
