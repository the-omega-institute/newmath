\item $N$ is the $\NestedIntervalUp$ row, carrying the decreasing finite-window chain and the endpoint containment ledger.
\item $C$ is the $\CantorIntersectionUp$ handoff row, available only after the nested source and closed bounded interval rows have been displayed.

\begin{theorem}[Nested interval completeness NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:nested-interval-completeness-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\NestedIntervalCompletenessUp$ carrier
$$
L=(B,N,C,W,R,E,H,T,P,Q),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\NestedIntervalCompletenessUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item closed bounded interval admission through $B$;
\item nested interval admission through $N$, requiring monotone containment and displayed finite width windows;
\item Cantor-intersection handoff through $C$, requiring any completion-facing read to pass through the existing nested-intersection packet;
\item finite stream and regular rational readback through $W$ and $R$;
\item terminal Real-seal admission through $E$;
\item componentwise transport, replay, provenance, and local naming through $H,T,P,Q$.
\end{enumerate}
No nested interval completeness obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

The closed bounded interval row fixes the endpoint surface. The nested interval row supplies the decreasing finite-window chain, and the Cantor-intersection row consumes that chain before any completion-facing Real seal is visible.

The stream and regular rational rows expose only finite observations and their readback. Since the carrier has no host point, quotient equality, countable choice, trichotomy, host equality, or detached completeness coordinate, the displayed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
