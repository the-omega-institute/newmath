\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\HilbertUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-hilbert-namecert}

The Hilbert-space interface $\HilbertUp$ packages a Banach space whose norm is induced by an inner product. It depends on $\NameCert_{\InnerProductUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-innerproduct-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\BanachUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-banach-namecert}), and is the substrate for the spectral and orthogonal-decomposition certificates of functional analysis.

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:hilbert-carrier}

\begin{definition}[Compatible inner-product Banach carrier]
\label{def:hilbert-compatible-inner-product-banach-carrier}
A compatible inner-product Banach carrier for $\HilbertUp$ is a history
predicate $X:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ with point classifier
$\sim_X$ together with three displayed rows over the same carried histories.
The first row is a concrete $\BanachUp$ candidate of
\autoref{def:banach-concrete-carrier}. The second row is an
$\InnerProductUp$ carrier on $X$, including the vector-space structure,
sesquilinear form, conjugate symmetry, linearity, positivity, and
inner-product classifier transport. The third row is a norm-induction ledger
recording that the Banach norm and Banach metric rows are exactly the rows
displayed by the inner-product norm:
$$
\|x\|_{\BanachUp}=\sqrt{\langle x,x\rangle_{\InnerProductUp}}
$$
and
$$
d_{\BanachUp}(x,y)=\|x-y\|_{\BanachUp}
$$
for carried histories $x,y:X$.

The compatibility requirement is literal row sharing, not an isomorphism
postulate: the Banach carrier, the inner-product carrier, and the
norm-induced metric ledger expose the same point carrier $X$ and the same
classifier $\sim_X$. Every accepted Hilbert row therefore carries a visible
Banach surface, a visible inner-product surface, and a ledger explaining why
the Banach metric is the metric induced by the inner-product norm.
\end{definition}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:hilbert-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[Hilbert induced norm Banach compatibility]
\label{thm:hilbert-induced-norm-banach-compatibility}
Every accepted compatible inner-product Banach carrier exposes the same point
carrier and classifier to its $\BanachUp$ and $\InnerProductUp$ surfaces.
Moreover, $\hsame$ transport preserves the norm-induced Banach metric witness:
if $x\sim_X x'$ and $y\sim_X y'$, then a displayed
$\mathsf{BanachMetricWitness}_X(x,y,d)$ row transports to the corresponding
$\mathsf{BanachMetricWitness}_X(x',y',d')$ row classified by the same
inner-product norm ledger.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the compatible carrier of
\autoref{def:hilbert-compatible-inner-product-banach-carrier}. The Banach row
and inner-product row have the same endpoint predicate $X$ and classifier
$\sim_X$ by definition, so no change of carrier or classifier is introduced.

Apply \autoref{thm:banach-norm-induced-metric-compatibility} to read the
Banach metric through the displayed norm row. The Hilbert ledger identifies
that norm row with the inner-product norm row, so the metric witness remains
the norm-induced Banach witness after this reading.

Finally use the componentwise $\hsame$ transport rows supplied by the Banach
surface and the inner-product surface. They move the vector representative,
difference row, inner-product norm row, and RealUp distance endpoint together,
and repack the transported rows as
$\mathsf{BanachMetricWitness}_X(x',y',d')$ over the same classifier
$\sim_X$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Hilbert complete inner-product limit stability]
\label{thm:hilbert-complete-inner-product-limit-stability}
Let $H$ be a compatible inner-product Banach carrier. For any regular Cauchy
stream $s$ over the Banach metric, any displayed
$\mathsf{CompleteMetricLimitWitness}(X,s,\mu,\ell,L)$ supplied by the Banach
row, and any proof that the stream observations remain in the inner-product
carrier, there is a Hilbert limit witness with the same selected limit
$\ell$, the same limit name $L$, and the same classifier $\sim_X$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the Banach row contained in $H$. Its completeness field supplies
the displayed $\mathsf{CompleteMetricLimitWitness}(X,s,\mu,\ell,L)$ and its
carrier endpoint data through \autoref{def:banach-concrete-carrier}.

The observation-stability hypothesis says that the same stream is carried by
the inner-product surface of $H$. Use the inner-product carrier stability row
to transport all observation representatives through $\sim_X$ while retaining
the displayed inner-product norm rows named by the Hilbert ledger.

Apply \autoref{thm:hilbert-induced-norm-banach-compatibility} to identify the
transported Banach distance witnesses with the inner-product norm-induced
ones. The selected limit endpoint $\ell$ and limit name $L$ are therefore not
changed; they are merely read as Hilbert rows over the same carrier,
classifier, and visible metric ledger.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Hilbert NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:hilbert-namecert-obligation-surface}
The compatible carrier of
\autoref{def:hilbert-compatible-inner-product-banach-carrier}, its induced
classifier $\sim_X$, the norm-metric compatibility of
\autoref{thm:hilbert-induced-norm-banach-compatibility}, the complete-limit
stability of \autoref{thm:hilbert-complete-inner-product-limit-stability}, and
the visible ledger joining the Banach and inner-product rows assemble the
paper-side $\NameCert_{\HilbertUp}$ obligation surface. This surface records
the Hilbert carrier, classifier, norm-induced Banach metric, complete-limit
stability, and ledger provenance; it does not assert a standard
Hilbert-space bridge.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the compatible carrier definition for the public carrier field. It
already contains the Banach row, the inner-product row, and the row-sharing
ledger binding both surfaces to the same $X$ and $\sim_X$.

Use \autoref{thm:hilbert-induced-norm-banach-compatibility} for the
norm-metric field. It identifies the Banach metric witness as the metric
induced by the inner-product norm and records that $\hsame$ transport
preserves that witness componentwise.

Use \autoref{thm:hilbert-complete-inner-product-limit-stability} for the
limit-stability field. It consumes the Banach
$\mathsf{CompleteMetricLimitWitness}$ row together with the inner-product
observation-stability row and returns the same selected limit as a Hilbert
limit witness.

The remaining fields are inherited from the source rows: the Banach surface
provides the complete normed metric obligations, the inner-product surface
provides the sesquilinear, symmetry, positivity, and classifier-stability
obligations, and the ledger records exactly which displayed rows are shared.
Thus the paper-side $\NameCert_{\HilbertUp}$ obligation surface is assembled
without adding any standard bridge claim.
\end{proof}
