\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\HilbertUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-hilbert-namecert}

The Hilbert-space interface $\HilbertUp$ packages a Banach space whose norm is induced by an inner product. It depends on $\NameCert_{\InnerProductUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-innerproduct-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\BanachUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-banach-namecert}), and is the substrate for the spectral and orthogonal-decomposition certificates of functional analysis.

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:hilbert-carrier}

\begin{definition}[Hilbert singleton empty-history carrier]
\label{def:hilbert-singleton-empty-history-carrier}
The concrete $\HilbertUp$ carrier is the singleton empty-history Banach
carrier of \autoref{def:banach-singleton-empty-history-instance}, read over
the singleton empty-history vector space of
\autoref{def:singleton-empty-history-vecspace-instance}. Thus the vector
carrier is $A_{\emp}$, the vector classifier is $\rho_{\emp}$, the normed
carrier is the singleton $\BanachUp$ carrier, and the norm is
$\nu_{\emp}$.

The inner-product row is the constant-zero RealUp endpoint
$$
\langle m,n\rangle_{\emp}:=0_{\mathsf{Real}}^{c}
$$
for all displayed vector histories $m,n:\mathsf{BHist}$. Its scalar-side
classifier is $\mathsf{RealConstantHistoryClassifier}$. The induced norm row
is read by the same constant endpoint:
$$
\|\!m\!\|_{\langle,\rangle_{\emp}}:=0_{\mathsf{Real}}^{c}.
$$
Hence the induced norm is the singleton $\NormUp$ norm $\nu_{\emp}$ from
\autoref{def:singleton-empty-history-norm-instance}. The ledger records the
singleton vector-space source, the Banach singleton carrier, and the RatUp zero
witness sealed as the RealUp constant endpoint.
\end{definition}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:hilbert-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[Hilbert singleton inner-product norm compatibility]
\label{thm:hilbert-singleton-inner-product-norm-compatibility}
For the carrier of
\autoref{def:hilbert-singleton-empty-history-carrier}, the norm induced by
$\langle-,-\rangle_{\emp}$ agrees with the singleton norm $\nu_{\emp}$:
$$
\mathsf{RealConstantHistoryClassifier}
  (\|\!m\!\|_{\langle,\rangle_{\emp}},\nu_{\emp}(m))
$$
for every carried $m:\mathsf{BHist}$. Moreover positive definiteness of the
inner-product row collapses to the singleton vector classifier:
$$
\mathsf{RealConstantHistoryClassifier}
  (\langle m,m\rangle_{\emp},0_{\mathsf{Real}}^{c})
\Longleftrightarrow
\rho_{\emp}(m,\emp).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The induced norm and $\nu_{\emp}$ are both the constant endpoint
$0_{\mathsf{Real}}^{c}$. The carrier and classifier rows of
\autoref{thm:singleton-empty-history-norm-carrier-classifier} give the RealUp
constant-history classifier for this endpoint, so the two norm presentations
are classified together.

For positive definiteness in the forward direction, the inner-product value
$\langle m,m\rangle_{\emp}$ is again $0_{\mathsf{Real}}^{c}$. Apply
\autoref{thm:singleton-empty-history-vecspace-endpoint-collapse} to the
carried vector endpoint. It identifies $m$ with $\emp$ under $\rho_{\emp}$.

For the reverse direction, assume $\rho_{\emp}(m,\emp)$. The inner-product row
does not inspect the representative; it returns $0_{\mathsf{Real}}^{c}$ for
every pair. The zero-exactness boundary of
\autoref{thm:singleton-empty-history-norm-zero-exactness}, together with the
constant-real classifier already supplied by the singleton norm carrier,
repackages the row as
$\mathsf{RealConstantHistoryClassifier}
(\langle m,m\rangle_{\emp},0_{\mathsf{Real}}^{c})$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Hilbert singleton complete normed certificate]
\label{thm:hilbert-singleton-complete-normed-certificate}
The normed part of
\autoref{def:hilbert-singleton-empty-history-carrier} is complete. Every
regular Cauchy stream carried by $A_{\emp}$ has selected limit $\emp$, every
late distance-to-limit row is classified with $0_{\mathsf{Real}}^{c}$, and any
two selected Banach limit witnesses are classified by $\rho_{\emp}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the singleton Banach carrier of
\autoref{def:banach-singleton-empty-history-instance}. Its metric is the
constant RealUp zero endpoint and its limit-selection rule sends every
certified regular Cauchy stream to $\emp$.

The complete-metric laws are supplied by
\autoref{thm:singleton-complete-metric-laws}. They give the carried singleton
limit, the empty modulus, and the late-observation ledgers for the constant
zero metric.

Finally apply
\autoref{thm:banach-singleton-limit-namecert-uniqueness} to compare any two
Banach limit witnesses for the same stream. In the singleton vector carrier,
\autoref{thm:singleton-empty-history-vecspace-endpoint-collapse} reads that
comparison as the classifier row $\rho_{\emp}$, so the complete normed
certificate is the Banach singleton certificate read through the Hilbert
carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Hilbert NameCert obligation package]
\label{thm:hilbert-namecert-obligation-package}
The carrier of
\autoref{def:hilbert-singleton-empty-history-carrier}, together with the two
certificate rows
\autoref{thm:hilbert-singleton-inner-product-norm-compatibility} and
\autoref{thm:hilbert-singleton-complete-normed-certificate}, assembles the
paper-side $\NameCert_{\HilbertUp}$ obligation package. Its carrier is
$A_{\emp}$, its classifier is $\rho_{\emp}$, its inner-product row is
$\langle-,-\rangle_{\emp}$, its induced norm is $\nu_{\emp}$, its completeness
field is the singleton Banach limit rule, its ledger is the union of the
singleton vector-space, norm, inner-product zero-row, and Banach-completion
ledgers, and its bridge status is none.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier and classifier fields are copied from the singleton empty-history
vector-space certificate. The Banach source is exactly
\autoref{def:banach-singleton-empty-history-instance}, so the normed carrier
and metric rows are already present before the Hilbert row is attached.

The inner-product compatibility field is
\autoref{thm:hilbert-singleton-inner-product-norm-compatibility}. It identifies
the induced norm with $\nu_{\emp}$ and reflects positive definiteness back to
the singleton classifier against $\emp$.

The completeness field is
\autoref{thm:hilbert-singleton-complete-normed-certificate}. It supplies the
selected limit, the convergence ledgers, and classifier uniqueness for
regular Cauchy streams in the norm induced by the inner product.

Combining these rows gives the local $\HilbertUp$ naming certificate: the
traditional target is the one-point Hilbert space, the BEDC source is
$\mathsf{BHist}$, the carrier and classifier are $A_{\emp}$ and
$\rho_{\emp}$, the stability rows are constant-zero classifier transport, the
exactness row is positive definiteness against $\emp$, the ledger records the
shared singleton provenance, and no standard bridge is claimed here.
\end{proof}
