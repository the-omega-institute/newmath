\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\DyadicDensityRealEmbeddingComparisonUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-dyadic-density-real-embedding-comparison-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges constructive analysis, the
$\DyadicDensityRealEmbeddingComparisonUp$ packet compares the dyadic-density
route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadic-density-real-namecert} with the
constant dyadic real-name route of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicembedding-namecert}. It keeps the
comparison over the finite rows already exposed by
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\DyadicDensityRealUp$, $\DyadicEmbeddingUp$,
$\RealDyadicEmbeddingUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$.
The packet is a naming certificate for route agreement at displayed windows,
not a construction of host real equality, selected dense points, trichotomy,
or completeness of $\RealUp$.

\begin{definition}[Dyadic-density real embedding comparison carrier]
\label{def:dyadic-density-real-embedding-comparison-carrier}
A $\DyadicDensityRealEmbeddingComparisonUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$
packet
$$
K=(D,E,E',S,R,Q,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicDensityRealUp$ density-witness row whose dyadic
source is read through the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger.
\item $E$ is the $\DyadicEmbeddingUp$ constant-name route from the same
dyadic source into a stream, a regular rational readback, and a real seal.
\item $E'$ is the $\RealDyadicEmbeddingUp$ comparison row that records the
Real-facing read of the embedded dyadic name at the displayed window.
\item $S$ is the shared $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row opened by both the
density witness and the embedding route.
\item $R$ is the shared $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row attached to that window.
\item $Q$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row consumed by the comparison.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the density row,
embedding rows, stream window, regular readback, real seal, continuation,
provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ stores displayed $\Cont$ replay from the dyadic source to the
density witness, from the same source through the embedding route, and from
the common window-readback pair to the $\RealUp$ seal.
\item $P$ stores $\Pkg$ provenance for $\DyadicRatCoreUp$,
$\DyadicDensityRealUp$, $\DyadicEmbeddingUp$, $\RealDyadicEmbeddingUp$,
$\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$,
and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local
$\NameCert_{\DyadicDensityRealEmbeddingComparisonUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no coordinate for host real equality, selected dense
points, trichotomy, quotient stream equality, arbitrary limit choice, or
ambient completeness of $\RealUp$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Dyadic-density real embedding comparison NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:dyadic-density-real-embedding-comparison-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\DyadicDensityRealEmbeddingComparisonUp$ carrier
$K=(D,E,E',S,R,Q,H,C,P,N)$, the local
$\NameCert_{\DyadicDensityRealEmbeddingComparisonUp}$ surface consists of six
finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the density, embedding, comparison, window,
readback, real-seal, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming
rows;
\item dyadic-source agreement, stating that the density row $D$ and the
embedding row $E$ read the same $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ source row;
\item window agreement, stating that the comparison opens the same
$\StreamNameUp$ row $S$ for the density read and for the embedded constant
name;
\item readback agreement, stating that the shared $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $R$ is
the only rational readback passed to the $\RealUp$ seal $Q$;
\item Real-facing comparison, stating that the $\RealDyadicEmbeddingUp$ row
$E'$ compares only the displayed density route with the displayed embedding
route at the common finite window;
\item non-escape, stating that host real equality, selected dense points,
trichotomy, quotient stream equality, arbitrary limit choice, and ambient
$\RealUp$ completeness are absent from the carrier.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The tuple lists exactly the density row,
embedding rows, shared stream window, shared regular readback, real seal,
transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows available to the
classifier.

Dyadic-source agreement is read from the common $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ source
named by the density and embedding routes. The carrier has one source ledger
for the comparison, so the embedded constant name cannot use a separate
dyadic point.

Window agreement and readback agreement follow from the shared rows $S$ and
$R$. Both routes must open the same finite $\StreamNameUp$ window and pass the
same $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback before the $\RealUp$ seal is consumed.

The Real-facing comparison is therefore only the row $E'$. It records
agreement of the two displayed routes at the common finite read, while the
transport and continuation rows replay the same packet under componentwise
$\hsame$.

Finally inspect the carrier inventory. No row names host real equality,
selected dense points, trichotomy, quotient stream equality, arbitrary limit
choice, or ambient completeness, so the six rows exhaust the local NameCert
surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Dyadic-density real embedding comparison route]
\label{thm:dyadic-density-real-embedding-comparison-route}
Every accepted $\DyadicDensityRealEmbeddingComparisonUp$ packet compares the
density witness route and the constant dyadic embedding route only through the
finite spine
$$
\DyadicRatCoreUp \longrightarrow
(\DyadicDensityRealUp,\DyadicEmbeddingUp,\RealDyadicEmbeddingUp)
\longrightarrow \StreamNameUp \longrightarrow \RegSeqRatUp \longrightarrow
\RealUp .
$$
The comparison is a route equality of displayed certificate rows at a common
finite window; it does not import host real equality, selected dense points,
trichotomy, quotient stream equality, arbitrary limit choice, or completeness
of $\RealUp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted packet
$K=(D,E,E',S,R,Q,H,C,P,N)$. The density route begins with the
$\DyadicDensityRealUp$ row $D$, and the constant-name route begins with the
$\DyadicEmbeddingUp$ row $E$ over the same dyadic source.

The comparison row $E'$ is downstream of those two rows. It does not add a new
source; it only records the Real-facing comparison after the shared
$\StreamNameUp$ window $S$ and the shared $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback $R$ have
been displayed.

The real seal $Q$ is therefore reached through the same finite read on both
sides. Componentwise transport $H$, continuation replay $C$, provenance $P$,
and the local name $N$ preserve the packet as a finite BEDC certificate.

Since the carrier provides no row for host real equality, selected dense
points, trichotomy, quotient stream equality, arbitrary limit choice, or
ambient completeness, no consumer can replace this finite comparison by an
external real-equality or completeness argument.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Dyadic-density real embedding comparison RegSeqRat modulus use]
\label{thm:dyadic-density-real-embedding-comparison-regseqrat-modulus-use}
Every accepted $\DyadicDensityRealEmbeddingComparisonUp$ packet reads the shared $\RegSeqRatUp$ row through the same finite modulus window before comparing the density witness route with the constant dyadic embedding route. In particular, for $K=(D,E,E',S,R,Q,H,C,P,N)$, the window row $S$ and readback row $R$ are consumed before the comparison row $E'$ can export agreement to the $\RealUp$ seal $Q$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier as $K=(D,E,E',S,R,Q,H,C,P,N)$. The carrier has one shared $\StreamNameUp$ row $S$ and one shared $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row $R$ for the density witness route and the constant dyadic embedding route.

Use the shared-window and readback agreement rows from \autoref{thm:dyadic-density-real-embedding-comparison-namecert-obligations}. They force both sides of the comparison to open the same finite window and to pass the same regular rational readback before the Real-facing comparison row is consumed.

Apply \autoref{thm:dyadic-density-real-embedding-comparison-route} to identify the comparison spine ending in the $\RealUp$ seal $Q$. Since the carrier contains no row for host real equality, selected dense points, trichotomy, quotient stream equality, arbitrary limit choice, or ambient completeness, the shared $\RegSeqRatUp$ window cannot be replaced by any external Real-side principle.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Dyadic-density real embedding comparison non-escape]
\label{thm:dyadic-density-real-embedding-comparison-nonescape}
The dependency ledger of an accepted
$\DyadicDensityRealEmbeddingComparisonUp$ carrier is exhausted by
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\DyadicDensityRealUp$, $\DyadicEmbeddingUp$,
$\RealDyadicEmbeddingUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$,
$\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$. No accepted comparison
consumer may add host real equality, a selected dense point, trichotomy,
quotient stream equality, arbitrary limit choice, or ambient $\RealUp$
completeness as an implicit dependency.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the package row $P$ and the local naming row $N$. They list exactly
the finite dependency rows displayed in
\autoref{def:dyadic-density-real-embedding-comparison-carrier}.

Apply
\autoref{thm:dyadic-density-real-embedding-comparison-namecert-obligations}
for carrier admission, source agreement, window agreement, readback
agreement, Real-facing comparison, and non-escape. Then use
\autoref{thm:dyadic-density-real-embedding-comparison-route} to identify the
only comparison spine consumed by downstream Real-facing readers.

No coordinate remains that could carry an external equality test, a chosen
dense point, a trichotomy decision, a quotient-stream equality proof, an
arbitrary limit choice, or a completeness principle. Each such object would
therefore be an off-packet dependency, not a row of the accepted comparison
certificate.
\end{proof}
