¥chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $¥ControlRiccatiUp$}

Following Kalman and Anderson--Moore, the $¥ControlRiccatiUp$ packet records the control-facing Riccati certificate surface that sits between finite Riccati ledgers and regulator or estimator consumers. It reads the displayed Riccati recursion of ¥autoref{ch:concrete-instances-riccati-namecert}, the finite regulator packet of ¥autoref{ch:concrete-instances-lqr-namecert}, the estimator packet of ¥autoref{ch:concrete-instances-kalman-filter-namecert}, the observability and controllability rows of ¥autoref{ch:concrete-instances-controlobservability-namecert} and ¥autoref{ch:concrete-instances-controlcontrollability-namecert}, the finite matrix rows of ¥autoref{ch:concrete-instances-matrix-namecert}, and the normed-state boundary of ¥autoref{ch:concrete-instances-normedspace-namecert}. The packet names a finite stabilizing residual and gain-readback route; it is not an ambient algebraic Riccati solver.

¥begin{definition}[ControlRiccati carrier]
¥label{def:control-riccati-carrier}
A $¥ControlRiccatiUp$ carrier is a finite $¥BHist$ packet
$$
C=(R,A,B,Q,S,G,L,O,K,H,T,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
¥begin{enumerate}
¥item $R$ is the source $¥RiccatiUp$ value-ledger row.
¥item $A,B$ are the displayed state-transition and control-input matrix rows read through $¥MatrixUp$ and the finite state/control carrier.
¥item $Q,S$ are the displayed state-cost and control-cost matrix rows, including the symmetry and positivity ledgers needed by the control read.
¥item $G$ is the finite gain-readback row naming the feedback matrix computed from the displayed Riccati ledger.
¥item $L$ is the $¥LQRUp$ handoff row saying the regulator consumer reads the same Riccati value and gain rows.
¥item $O$ is the $¥ControlObservabilityUp$ row and $K$ is the $¥ControlControllabilityUp$ row used to keep the residual and gain checks inside the displayed control surface.
¥item $H$ is the residual-stability row: under the displayed normed-state and matrix rows, the Riccati residual remains inside the finite tolerance ledger exported by $¥NormedSpaceUp$ and $¥MatrixUp$.
¥item $T$ is componentwise $¥hsame$ transport for the Riccati value, matrix, cost, gain, observability, controllability, and residual rows.
¥item $P$ is $¥Pkg$ provenance over $¥RiccatiUp$, $¥LQRUp$, $¥KalmanFilterUp$, $¥ControlObservabilityUp$, $¥ControlControllabilityUp$, $¥MatrixUp$, and $¥NormedSpaceUp$.
¥item $N$ is the local $¥NameCert_{¥ControlRiccatiUp}$ row.
¥end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a host matrix equation, a selected stabilizing solution, an infinite-horizon limit, a spectral separation oracle, or an unlogged controller.
¥end{definition}

¥begin{theorem}[ControlRiccati NameCert obligations]
¥label{thm:control-riccati-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of ¥autoref{def:control-riccati-carrier}, the local $¥NameCert_{¥ControlRiccatiUp}$ surface consists exactly of five obligation rows:
¥begin{enumerate}
¥item carrier admission for the finite packet $C=(R,A,B,Q,S,G,L,O,K,H,T,P,N)$;
¥item Riccati residual stability through the displayed $¥MatrixUp$ and $¥NormedSpaceUp$ rows;
¥item gain-readback exactness, saying the feedback row $G$ is read only from the displayed Riccati, transition, input, and cost rows;
¥item LQR handoff, saying the $¥LQRUp$ consumer receives the same value and gain rows through $L$;
¥item no-host-matrix nonescape, saying no accepted consumer can project an algebraic Riccati equation, selected stabilizing solution, spectral oracle, or unbounded controller from the packet.
¥end{enumerate}
¥end{theorem}
¥begin{proof}
Project the carrier of ¥autoref{def:control-riccati-carrier}. The rows $R,A,B,Q,S,G,L,O,K,H,T,P,N$ are the only visible Riccati value, matrix, cost, gain, regulator, observability, controllability, residual, transport, provenance, and local naming rows.

The residual-stability obligation reads the finite residual row $H$ together with the matrix and normed-state rows. Since the residual is compared inside the displayed $¥MatrixUp$ and $¥NormedSpaceUp$ ledgers, the check cannot call on a host spectral or convergence theorem.

The gain-readback obligation then reads $G$ from the same Riccati value, transition, input, and cost rows. The $¥LQRUp$ handoff row $L$ exports exactly this finite value-and-gain surface to the regulator packet, while the $¥KalmanFilterUp$ dependency is only the neighboring estimator use of Riccati-style covariance ledgers.

Finally inspect the excluded coordinates. A host matrix equation, selected stabilizing solution, infinite-horizon limit, spectral separation oracle, and unlogged controller would require rows not present in $C$. Therefore every accepted $¥ControlRiccatiUp$ read remains inside the displayed NameCert surface.
¥end{proof}
