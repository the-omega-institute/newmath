\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CompactOpenTopologyUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-compact-open-topology-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Kelley and Bourbaki on compact-open topologies, the $\CompactOpenTopologyUp$ packet names the finite subbasic topology surface carried by compact-domain windows and open-target tests. It sits next to the compact-open metric packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compact-open-metric-namecert}, the compact-open function-space carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compact-open-function-space-namecert}, the continuous-map graph route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuousmap-namecert}, the compact-metric source surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, and the compact-window convergence route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locally-uniform-convergence-namecert}. The packet records compact-domain rows, open-target rows, finite window rows, componentwise transport, displayed replay, provenance, and local naming; it does not install a host topology on all functions.

\begin{definition}[Compact-open topology carrier]
\label{def:compact-open-topology-carrier}
A $\CompactOpenTopologyUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
T=(K,O,W,F,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $K$ is the compact-domain row. It records the retained $\CompactMetricUp$ source windows and the compact subobjects whose images are tested.
\item $O$ is the open-target row. It records the displayed target-open tests used by the subbasic condition.
\item $W$ is the finite window row. Each entry pairs one compact-domain entry from $K$ with one open-target entry from $O$.
\item $F$ is the $\ContinuousMapUp$ graph row whose displayed values are inspected only on the windows listed by $W$.
\item $S$ is the subbasic membership ledger. It records that the displayed map sends the retained compact-domain window into the corresponding open-target row.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for compact-domain rows, open-target rows, finite windows, continuous-map graph rows, subbasic membership ledgers, replay rows, provenance, and local naming.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a compact-open topology request through compact-domain inspection, open-target inspection, graph evaluation, and subbasic membership.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CompactOpenTopologyUp$, $\CompactOpenMetricUp$, $\CompactOpenFunctionSpaceUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\LocallyUniformConvergenceUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CompactOpenTopologyUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $K,O,W,F,S,H,C,P,N$. There is no coordinate for open-cover compactness, choice-selected compact witnesses, quotient equality of host functions, or ambient completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Compact-open topology NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:compact-open-topology-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CompactOpenTopologyUp$ carrier $T=(K,O,W,F,S,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CompactOpenTopologyUp}$ surface consists exactly of these obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for compact-domain rows, open-target rows, finite windows, continuous-map graph rows, subbasic membership ledgers, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming;
\item compact-domain visibility, requiring every domain side of a subbasic request to read through the retained $\CompactMetricUp$ rows in $K$;
\item open-target visibility, requiring every target side of a subbasic request to read through the displayed open-target row $O$;
\item finite-window stability, requiring membership checks to use the paired rows in $W$ and the subbasic ledger $S$ before any consumer reads the topology packet;
\item non-escape, requiring $H,C,P,N$ to preserve the same displayed rows and to export no open-cover compactness, choice-selected compact witness, host function-space equality, or ambient completeness.
\end{enumerate}
No compact-open topology obligation is stored outside those rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier in \autoref{def:compact-open-topology-carrier}. The visible topology coordinates are exactly the compact-domain row, open-target row, finite window row, continuous-map graph row, subbasic membership ledger, and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

The compact-domain side is forced by $K$. Its reads are the same finite compact-source surface used by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert} and by the compact-open metric route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compact-open-metric-namecert}.

The function side is forced by $F$ and $S$. A subbasic membership request must inspect displayed graph values through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuousmap-namecert} and then record the result in the finite membership ledger attached to the paired window in $W$.

The provenance row places the packet beside \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compact-open-function-space-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locally-uniform-convergence-namecert}. Those siblings may consume the displayed subbasic surface, but the carrier has no row for host function-space equality, open-cover compactness, choice-selected compact witnesses, or ambient completeness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Compact-open topology finite subbase stability]
\label{thm:compact-open-topology-finite-subbase-stability}
Let $T=(K,O,W,F,S,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CompactOpenTopologyUp$ carrier. Every finite family of displayed compact-open subbasic requests admitted through $W$ remains a request on the same compact-domain, open-target, continuous-map, and membership rows after componentwise $\hsame$ transport by $H$ and displayed $\Cont$ replay by $C$. Thus finite subbase stability is internal to $K,O,W,F,S,H,C,P,N$ and cannot be replaced by open-cover compactness, choice-selected compact witnesses, host function-space equality, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a finite list of subbasic requests. Each request is already an entry of $W$, so it names one compact-domain row from $K$ and one open-target row from $O$ before it can inspect a function.

Read the continuous-map data through $F$ and the membership row through $S$. The compact-open metric and compact-open function-space siblings may reuse this windowed read, but they receive only the displayed graph and membership coordinates.

Transport by $H$ is componentwise on the listed rows. Replay by $C$ repeats the same compact-domain, open-target, graph, and membership route, so finite intersections of displayed subbasic requests remain inside the same carrier.

Since the carrier has no row for open-cover compactness, selected compact witnesses, host function equality, or ambient completeness, none of those principles can be introduced by finite subbase stability.
\end{proof}
