Following Bishop and Bridges, the $\FractionalPartUp$ packet names the finite integer-part and residual-real interface used before decimal-prefix and comparison consumers may inspect a real name. It sits after the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the integer carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-int-namecert}, the public Archimedean bound surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-archimedeanreal-namecert}, the integer-part bracket packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-archimedean-integer-part-namecert}, the decimal-prefix route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-decimalexpansion-namecert}, the finite stream windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular-rational readback surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}. The packet records the residual route as finite BEDC data; it is not a host fractional-part operation, a floor selector, a trichotomy service, a quotient-real equality witness, a selected representative, or an appeal to ambient Real completeness.

\begin{definition}[FractionalPart carrier]
\label{def:fractional-part-carrier}
A $\FractionalPartUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(R,I,F,B,D,S,G,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $R$ is the $\RealUp$ name row for the source real, exposed through the real-name seal and the regular-Cauchy readback route before any integer-part read is consumed.
\item $I$ is the $\IntUp$ integer-part row, read through the $\ArchimedeanIntegerPartUp$ bracket packet and its displayed integer candidate.
\item $F$ is the residual fractional $\RealUp$ row, recording the real-name route for the residual obtained from the source row and integer row.
\item $B$ is the Archimedean bound and bracket row, binding the source real, the integer candidate, and the residual interval confinement to the same public $\ArchimedeanRealUp$ and $\ArchimedeanIntegerPartUp$ comparison data.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row, recording the dyadic radii, slack comparisons, and refinements used to observe both the source row and residual row.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row opened for decimal-prefix and comparison reads of the residual.
\item $G$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row that reads the windows in $S$ as regular rational data before the residual reaches a decimal-prefix consumer.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the source real, integer row, residual row, Archimedean bracket, dyadic tolerance ledger, stream windows, regular readback, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a precision or decimal-prefix request through $R,I,F,B,D,S,G$, without inserting another integer search or host arithmetic operation.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\FractionalPartUp$, $\RealUp$, $\IntUp$, $\ArchimedeanRealUp$, $\ArchimedeanIntegerPartUp$, $\DecimalExpansionUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\FractionalPartUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate is exactly the assertion that these rows form one finite residual certificate. It has no coordinate for host floor, trichotomy over arbitrary real names, quotient real equality, selected representatives, countable choice, hidden Archimedean search, or ambient Real completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[FractionalPart NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:fractional-part-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\FractionalPartUp$ carrier $K=(R,I,F,B,D,S,G,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\FractionalPartUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the source real row, integer-part row, residual real row, Archimedean bracket row, dyadic tolerance row, stream-window row, regular-rational handoff row, componentwise transport, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item componentwise classifier transport, requiring the accepted status of $R,I,F,B,D,S,G$ to remain stable under the $\hsame$ transports in $H$ and the displayed $\Cont$ replay in $C$;
\item residual interval confinement, requiring the residual row $F$ to stay within the displayed unit-bracket route supplied by $B$ and observed through the tolerance ledger $D$;
\item decimal-prefix readback, requiring every decimal-prefix consumer to reach $F$ only through the finite-window row $S$ and the regular-rational handoff row $G$;
\item ledger exhaustion, excluding host floor, trichotomy, quotient real equality, selected representatives, countable choice, hidden Archimedean search, and ambient Real completeness from the exported certificate.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet $K=(R,I,F,B,D,S,G,H,C,P,N)$. The projection exposes exactly the source real row, integer-part row, residual row, Archimedean bracket, dyadic tolerance ledger, stream windows, regular readback, transport row, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming row.

Componentwise classifier transport is read from $H$ and $C$. The transports move the rows $R,I,F,B,D,S,G$ together, and replay sends each precision request back through the same displayed route rather than through a detached host operation.

Residual interval confinement is the content of the bracket row $B$ together with the tolerance row $D$. The bracket binds the source real, integer candidate, and residual to the same Archimedean comparison data, while the dyadic ledger records the finite tolerances under which the residual is observed.

Decimal-prefix readback is supplied by $S$ and $G$. A decimal consumer first opens a finite $\StreamNameUp$ window and then reads it through the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff, so the residual is never presented as an untracked completed real value.

The exclusions follow from the carrier inventory. Since no row stores host floor, trichotomy, quotient equality, selected representatives, countable choice, hidden Archimedean search, or ambient Real completeness, none of those principles can be exported by the local certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FractionalPart Real-Int handoff]
\label{thm:fractional-part-real-int-handoff}
Every consumer of the fractional residual in an accepted $\FractionalPartUp$ carrier factors through the finite route
$$
\RealUp \longmapsto \ArchimedeanRealUp \longmapsto \IntUp \longmapsto \DyadicRatCoreUp \longmapsto \StreamNameUp \longmapsto \RegSeqRatUp .
$$
Concretely, the consumer receives the displayed source real row $R$, Archimedean bracket row $B$, integer-part row $I$, dyadic tolerance row $D$, stream-window row $S$, and regular-rational handoff row $G$ before reading the residual row $F$. It receives no host floor function, trichotomy service, quotient real, selected representative, or $\RealUp$ completeness witness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Let a downstream consumer request the fractional residual from an accepted carrier. The source real row $R$ is the first analytic coordinate, so the route begins at the displayed $\RealUp$ name rather than at a host real value.

The Archimedean and integer-part data are then forced by $B$ and $I$. The bracket row exposes the public Archimedean bound and comparison route, and the integer row is read through the displayed $\IntUp$ candidate supplied by the integer-part packet.

The residual can be inspected only through the dyadic and window rows. The tolerance ledger $D$ records the finite dyadic observations of the residual, $S$ opens the finite stream windows requested by a decimal or comparison consumer, and $G$ converts those windows into the regular-rational readback surface.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ package and name the same route. They transport, replay, source, and certify the displayed rows without adding a floor function, trichotomy service, quotient real, selected representative, or $\RealUp$ completeness witness.
\end{proof}
