\begin{theorem}[Bolzano--Weierstrass public subsequence selector]
\label{thm:bolzano-weierstrass-public-subsequence-selector}
For every accepted $\BolzanoWeierstrassUp$ carrier $B=(S,K,R,Q,E,H,C,P,N)$, the public subsequence selector exported by the chapter is the finite route
$$
S,K,R,Q,H,C,P,N .
$$
The bounded $\RealUp$ sequence source is admitted through $S$, retained interval cells are admitted through $K$, monotone-cofinal subsequence windows are admitted through $R$, and the regular-rational readback is admitted through $Q$. The selector surface is therefore a displayed NameCert route, not an arbitrary subsequence choice functional, a host compactness theorem, quotient stream equality, or an ambient completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:bolzano-weierstrass-bounded-sequence-admission}. It fixes the bounded sequence source $S$ as the same source consumed by the interval-net row $K$ and the later regular-subsequence rows.

Apply \autoref{thm:bolzano-weierstrass-finite-interval-tree-induction} and \autoref{thm:bolzano-weierstrass-retained-cell-monotonicity}. They supply the retained finite interval cells and keep later windows nested inside the same displayed interval-net route.

Apply \autoref{thm:bolzano-weierstrass-cofinal-window-extraction} and \autoref{thm:bolzano-weierstrass-regular-subsequence-route}. These theorems identify $R$ and $Q$ as the monotone-cofinal window and $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback surface. The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ then transport, replay, source, and name only that finite route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Bolzano--Weierstrass public cluster witness]
\label{thm:bolzano-weierstrass-public-cluster-witness}
Every public cluster witness exported by an accepted $\BolzanoWeierstrassUp$ packet factors through
$$
S,K,R,Q,E,H,C,P,N .
$$
It reads the bounded-sequence admission, retained-window ledger, regular-subsequence extraction, $\RegSeqRatUp$ cluster readback, and terminal $\RealUp$ seal as one displayed package. It exports no least cluster point, no quotient equality of real names, no open-cover compactness object, and no host completeness row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from \autoref{thm:bolzano-weierstrass-public-subsequence-selector}. The public subsequence selector has already displayed $S,K,R,Q,H,C,P,N$ and therefore contains the bounded source, retained windows, cofinal extraction, and regular rational readback.

Apply \autoref{thm:bolzano-weierstrass-regular-cluster-seal}. It places the terminal $\RealUp$ seal $E$ downstream of $R$ and $Q$, so the cluster witness cannot be read before the regular-subsequence and readback rows are present.

Finally use \autoref{thm:bolzano-weierstrass-cluster-seal-extraction}. The extracted cluster seal is the Real boundary for that same finite route. The refused host-level objects are absent from the carrier and cannot be projected by the public witness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Bolzano--Weierstrass public nonescape package]
\label{thm:bolzano-weierstrass-public-nonescape-package}
The public $\BolzanoWeierstrassUp$ export surface is exhausted by the selector and cluster-witness rows of \autoref{thm:bolzano-weierstrass-public-subsequence-selector} and \autoref{thm:bolzano-weierstrass-public-cluster-witness}. In particular, any consumer that reads a bounded sequence, retained finite interval tree, regular subsequence, $\RegSeqRatUp$ cluster row, or $\RealUp$ seal remains inside
$$
S,K,R,Q,E,H,C,P,N .
$$
The package refuses arbitrary subsequence choice, unlisted interval nets, detached bounded sources, quotient stream equality, selected least cluster points, open-cover compactness, and ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First read the selector surface. By \autoref{thm:bolzano-weierstrass-public-subsequence-selector}, the only public subsequence route is $S,K,R,Q,H,C,P,N$.

Next read the cluster witness. By \autoref{thm:bolzano-weierstrass-public-cluster-witness}, the terminal Real seal $E$ is available only after the bounded source, retained-window, regular-subsequence, and regular-rational rows have been displayed.

It remains to check nonescape. Apply \autoref{thm:bolzano-weierstrass-cluster-seal-nonescape} and \autoref{thm:bolzano-weierstrass-window-retention-refinement}. These theorems keep every cluster-facing and retained-window read inside the same finite carrier route, so no public consumer can import a detached selector, host compactness principle, quotient equality, or ambient completeness row.
\end{proof}
