\begin{theorem}[Bolzano--Weierstrass public subsequence selector]
\label{thm:bolzano-weierstrass-public-subsequence-selector}
For every accepted $\BolzanoWeierstrassUp$ carrier $B=(S,K,R,Q,E,H,C,P,N)$, the public subsequence selector exported by the chapter is the finite route
$$
S,K,R,Q,H,C,P,N .
$$
The bounded $\RealUp$ sequence source is admitted through $S$, retained interval cells are admitted through $K$, monotone-cofinal subsequence windows are admitted through $R$, and the regular-rational readback is admitted through $Q$. The selector surface is therefore a displayed NameCert route, not an arbitrary subsequence choice functional, a host compactness theorem, quotient stream equality, or an ambient completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.BolzanoWeierstrassUp.BolzanoWeierstrassPublicSubsequenceSelector}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:bolzano-weierstrass-bounded-sequence-admission}. It fixes the bounded sequence source $S$ as the same source consumed by the interval-net row $K$ and the later regular-subsequence rows.

Apply \autoref{thm:bolzano-weierstrass-finite-interval-tree-induction} and \autoref{thm:bolzano-weierstrass-retained-cell-monotonicity}. They supply the retained finite interval cells and keep later windows nested inside the same displayed interval-net route.

Apply \autoref{thm:bolzano-weierstrass-cofinal-window-extraction} and \autoref{thm:bolzano-weierstrass-regular-subsequence-route}. These theorems identify $R$ and $Q$ as the monotone-cofinal window and $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback surface. The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ then transport, replay, source, and name only that finite route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Bolzano--Weierstrass subsequence-window publicity]
\label{thm:bolzano-weierstrass-subsequence-window-publicity}
The public subsequence-window export of an accepted $\BolzanoWeierstrassUp$ carrier exposes only bounded sequence rows, closed bounded interval finite-net rows, regular Cauchy subsequence rows, $\StreamNameUp$ windows, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledgers, $\RealUp$ seals, $\hsame$ transport, $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and $\NameCert$ data. Every such export factors through the selector route
$$
S,K,R,Q,H,C,P,N
$$
of \autoref{thm:bolzano-weierstrass-public-subsequence-selector} together with the chapter dependency route through retained intervals, regular subsequences, and terminal real seals.

The public window theorem excludes choice-selected subsequences, open-cover compactness, and ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First read the public selector theorem. It exposes the bounded source $S$, finite retained interval row $K$, regular-subsequence row $R$, regular-rational readback $Q$, and structural rows $H,C,P,N$ as the only selector route.

Next compose this route with the chapter dependency route. The retained interval and finite-net rows are supplied before $R$, the stream windows and dyadic tolerance ledgers are read during retained-window inspection, and the $\RealUp$ seal is available only after the regular Cauchy subsequence and $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback rows have been displayed.

The carrier has no row for an arbitrary choice-selected subsequence, no open-cover compactness object, and no ambient completeness principle. Therefore the public subsequence-window export is exactly the displayed bounded-sequence, finite-net, regular-Cauchy, window, readback, tolerance, seal, transport, replay, provenance, and naming surface.
\end{proof}

\label{thm:bolzano-weierstrass-public-nonescape-package}

S,K,R,Q,E,H,C,P,N .
$$

Next read the cluster witness. By \autoref{thm:bolzano-weierstrass-public-cluster-witness}, the terminal Real seal $E$ is available only after the bounded source, retained-window, regular-subsequence, and regular-rational rows have been displayed.

It remains to check nonescape. Apply \autoref{thm:bolzano-weierstrass-cluster-seal-nonescape} and \autoref{thm:bolzano-weierstrass-window-retention-refinement}. \end{proof}

$$
\BoundedRealSequenceUp,\ClosedBoundedIntervalNetUp,\RegSeqRatUp,\StreamNameUp,\RealUp
\longmapsto
S,K,R,Q,E,H,C,P,N ,
$$
and no selector consumer receives a detached subsequence choice, an unlisted interval net, quotient stream equality, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

\end{proof}
