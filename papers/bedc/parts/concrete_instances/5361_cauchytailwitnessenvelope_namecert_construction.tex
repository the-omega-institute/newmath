The $\CauchyTailWitnessEnvelopeUp$ packet is the composite finite witness envelope for the Real-completeness diagonal tail route of \autoref{ch:visions-real-completeness}. It consumes the stream window surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the finite-window envelope of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finitewindowenvelope-namecert}, and the common-tail comparison packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-tail-comparison-namecert}. The packet keeps the witness route finite by storing the selected StreamName window, the finite-window endpoint ledger, the Cauchy tail-comparison row, the RealTailAgreementSeal row, the RealUp readback row, and the structural provenance needed to replay the same route.

\begin{definition}[CauchyTailWitnessEnvelope carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-tail-witness-envelope-carrier}
A $\CauchyTailWitnessEnvelopeUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
E=(S,F,Q,A,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window selected for the diagonal tail request;
\item $F$ is the $\FiniteWindowEnvelopeUp$ row that binds the selected window to its dyadic endpoint ledger, regular-rational readback, and RealUp endpoint surface;
\item $Q$ is the $\CauchyTailComparisonUp$ row that compares the relevant late regular-name windows through the same selected window and endpoint ledger;
\item $A$ is the $\RealTailAgreementSealUp$ row reached after the finite-window and comparison rows have both been displayed;
\item $R$ is the $\RealUp$ readback row exported to the diagonal Real-completeness route;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $S,F,Q,A,R,C,P,N$;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes that replay the selected window, finite-window envelope, tail comparison, tail-agreement seal, RealUp readback, provenance, and local naming row;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\StreamNameUp$, $\FiniteWindowEnvelopeUp$, $\CauchyTailComparisonUp$, $\RealTailAgreementSealUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyTailWitnessEnvelopeUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no quotient stream equality, no host real equality, no selected host limit, no countable-choice selector, and no ambient completeness principle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyTailWitnessEnvelope NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-tail-witness-envelope-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-tail-witness-envelope-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyTailWitnessEnvelopeUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for
$$
E=(S,F,Q,A,R,H,C,P,N),
$$
selected $\StreamNameUp$ window exposure through $S$, finite-window envelope exposure through $F$, common-tail comparison exposure through $Q$, RealTailAgreementSeal exposure through $A$, RealUp readback exposure through $R$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the displayed carrier. The rows $S,F,Q,A,R,H,C,P,N$ are the only coordinates of the finite packet.

Read the analytic rows in order. The selected stream window $S$ is first bound into the finite-window envelope $F$, and the tail comparison $Q$ reads its common window and dyadic endpoint ledger through that same envelope surface.

The tail-agreement seal $A$ and RealUp readback $R$ are then reached only after $S,F,Q$ have been displayed. The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, authenticate, and name the same packet, so the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyTailWitnessEnvelope selector handoff]
\label{thm:cauchy-tail-witness-envelope-selector-handoff}
Every diagonal-tail selector read exported by an accepted $\CauchyTailWitnessEnvelopeUp$ carrier factors through the selected window $S$, the finite-window envelope $F$, and the Cauchy tail-comparison row $Q$ before the RealTailAgreementSeal row $A$ or the RealUp readback row $R$ can be consumed. The handoff supplies the finite witness route used by \autoref{ch:visions-real-completeness}, not an isolated RealUp seal or a host limit.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the selected window. The carrier has exactly one StreamName window coordinate $S$, and that coordinate is the source read by the envelope row $F$.

Use the finite-window dependency next. The row $F$ supplies the selected window and endpoint ledger consumed by the comparison row $Q$, so the comparison is not a host-level comparison detached from the envelope.

Finally read $A$ and $R$. These rows are reached after the selector, envelope, and comparison rows have been replayed through $C$ and transported through $H$. Thus the diagonal-tail handoff remains a finite witness envelope rather than a selected limit or isolated RealUp object.
\end{proof}
