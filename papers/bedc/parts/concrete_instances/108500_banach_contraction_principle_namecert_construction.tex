\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\BanachContractionPrincipleUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-banach-contraction-principle-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Banach's fixed-point theorem and Bishop--Bridges constructive
analysis, the $\BanachContractionPrincipleUp$ packet records a fixed-point
route only when the complete metric carrier, the contractive map, the
iteration schedule, and the Cauchy handoff are all explicit finite rows. It
sits downstream of the complete metric carrier of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completemetric-namecert}, the contractive map
packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-contractive-map-namecert}, the fixed
point iteration route of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-fixed-point-iteration-namecert}, the Cauchy
completion reflector of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchycompletionreflector-namecert}, and the
real-number ground of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The
certificate therefore consumes existing completion data; it does not assert
ambient completeness, global choice, or an unrecorded limit operator.

\begin{definition}[Banach contraction principle carrier]
\label{def:banach-contraction-principle-carrier}
A $\BanachContractionPrincipleUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
B=(M,F,I,Q,E,U,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the complete metric row, naming the metric carrier and the completeness seal used by the argument;
\item $F$ is the contraction map row, including the displayed modulus witness and its compatibility with the metric row;
\item $I$ is the iteration schedule row, recording the initial point and the successive iterates read by the construction;
\item $Q$ is the Cauchy witness row for the iteration sequence;
\item $E$ is the endpoint seal row, identifying the completed endpoint supplied by the complete metric data;
\item $U$ is the uniqueness row, binding any second fixed endpoint back to the same contraction ledger;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $M,F,I,Q,E,U$ together with continuation, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ replay routes by which the contraction ledger, the Cauchy witness, the endpoint seal, and uniqueness row are read;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, $\NameCert$, $\CompleteMetricUp$, $\ContractiveMapUp$, $\FixedPointIterationUp$, $\CauchyCompletionReflectorUp$, and $\RealUp$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\BanachContractionPrincipleUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier accepts exactly packets with these ten displayed rows. It has
no coordinate for an arbitrary choice of fixed point, an ambient completeness
claim outside $M$, a hidden contraction constant, or a limit witness that does
not pass through $Q$ and $E$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Banach contraction principle NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:banach-contraction-principle-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of
\autoref{def:banach-contraction-principle-carrier}, the local
$\NameCert_{\BanachContractionPrincipleUp}$ surface consists exactly of five
finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for $B=(M,F,I,Q,E,U,H,C,P,N)$;
\item contraction ledger exposure, requiring every use of the map row $F$ to read the displayed contraction data against $M$;
\item Cauchy-iteration handoff, requiring the iteration schedule $I$ to produce the witness row $Q$ before an endpoint is read;
\item complete-metric seal, requiring the endpoint row $E$ to be supplied by the completeness data in $M$ rather than by an ambient choice operator;
\item uniqueness nonescape, requiring every competing fixed endpoint to factor through $U,H,C,P,N$ with no off-packet fixed-point row.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from the displayed packet. The rows
$M,F,I,Q,E,U,H,C,P,N$ are the full inventory made available to the local
certificate.

The contraction ledger is read through $F$ against the metric data in $M$.
Thus a contraction claim with a hidden modulus or a different metric carrier
has no row in the packet.

The iteration schedule then hands off to the Cauchy witness row. The endpoint
seal can be read only after $Q$ is present and the complete metric data in $M$
supplies the endpoint row $E$.

Finally, uniqueness is confined to $U$ and the replay rows $H,C,P,N$. A second
fixed endpoint that escapes these rows would require an additional carrier
coordinate, which the classifier does not provide.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Banach contraction endpoint uniqueness]
\label{thm:banach-contraction-principle-endpoint-uniqueness}
Let $B=(M,F,I,Q,E,U,H,C,P,N)$ and $B'=(M,F,I,Q,E',U',H',C',P',N')$ be accepted $\BanachContractionPrincipleUp$ packets over the same complete metric row $M$, contraction row $F$, iteration schedule $I$, and Cauchy witness row $Q$. If the endpoint seals $E$ and $E'$ are transported through the displayed uniqueness rows $U$ and $U'$, then the metric carrier named by $M$ classifies the two endpoint seals together. The conclusion exposes endpoint uniqueness only along the displayed contraction ledger; it does not select a fixed point outside the packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project both carriers to the shared rows $M,F,I,Q$. The contraction ledger exposure forces every fixed-point read to use the same metric carrier, the same contractive map row, and the same iteration schedule before any endpoint seal can be inspected.

The Cauchy witness row $Q$ then supplies the common route into the complete metric row $M$. Both endpoint seals $E$ and $E'$ are therefore read as completions of the displayed iteration data rather than as independently selected fixed points.

Apply the uniqueness rows $U$ and $U'$ through their componentwise transports and replay routes. The rows $H,C,P,N$ and $H',C',P',N'$ keep the two endpoint reads on the same local NameCert surface, so the metric classifier of $M$ identifies the endpoint seals. Since the carriers have no coordinate for an off-packet fixed point, no such point is exported.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Banach contraction iteration Cauchy handoff]
\label{thm:banach-contraction-principle-iteration-cauchy-handoff}
Every accepted $\BanachContractionPrincipleUp$ carrier $B=(M,F,I,Q,E,U,H,C,P,N)$ routes its displayed fixed-point iteration through the Cauchy tail row $Q$ before the completion reflector reads the endpoint seal $E$. The rows named by $P$ may expose $\FixedPointIterationUp$ and $\CauchyCompletionReflectorUp$ consumers only after the contraction ledger $F$, iteration schedule $I$, and Cauchy witness $Q$ have been displayed. Thus the packet exports a completion-facing endpoint handoff, not a chosen fixed point, a hidden limit operator, a global choice rule, or completeness outside the metric row $M$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The definition places the complete metric row $M$, contraction row $F$, iteration schedule $I$, Cauchy witness $Q$, and endpoint seal $E$ in a fixed displayed order.

First read $F$ and $I$. The contraction ledger determines the successive iterate route, while the iteration schedule records the finite prefix and successor replay used by downstream fixed-point consumers.

Next read $Q$. This is the only Cauchy tail row in the carrier, so the completion reflector cannot receive an endpoint request until the iteration has been certified as Cauchy through the displayed row.

Finally replay the completion-facing handoff through $E,H,C,P,N$. Since no carrier coordinate stores a chosen endpoint or an ambient completeness witness, the exported endpoint is exactly the reflector output attached to the displayed Cauchy tail.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Banach contraction endpoint route]
\label{thm:banach-contraction-principle-endpoint-route}
Every accepted $\BanachContractionPrincipleUp$ endpoint read factors through $\CompleteMetricUp$, $\ContractiveMapUp$, $\FixedPointIterationUp$, $\CauchyCompletionReflectorUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows in the carrier order
$$
M,F,I,Q,E,U,H,C,P,N .
$$
The route exports no chosen endpoint, hidden limit operator, global choice rule, quotient completion equality, or completeness outside $M$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier of \autoref{def:banach-contraction-principle-carrier}. The endpoint row $E$ is downstream of the complete metric row $M$, contraction map row $F$, iteration schedule $I$, and Cauchy witness $Q$.

Apply \autoref{thm:banach-contraction-principle-namecert-obligations}. The NameCert surface requires carrier admission, contraction ledger exposure, Cauchy-iteration handoff, complete-metric seal, and uniqueness nonescape before a fixed-point endpoint can be read.

Use \autoref{thm:banach-contraction-principle-endpoint-uniqueness} to keep any competing endpoint inside the displayed uniqueness row $U$. Then use \autoref{thm:banach-contraction-principle-iteration-cauchy-handoff} to route the displayed iteration through $Q$ before the completion reflector reads $E$.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ provide transport, replay, provenance, and local naming over the same finite rows. Since the carrier has no coordinate for a chosen endpoint, hidden limit operator, global choice rule, quotient completion equality, or completeness outside $M$, the endpoint route is exactly the displayed carrier order.
\end{proof}
