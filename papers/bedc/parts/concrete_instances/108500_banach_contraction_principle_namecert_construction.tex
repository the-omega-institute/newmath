\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\BanachContractionPrincipleUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-banach-contraction-principle-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Banach's fixed-point theorem and Bishop--Bridges constructive
analysis, the $\BanachContractionPrincipleUp$ packet records a fixed-point
route only when the complete metric carrier, the contractive map, the
iteration schedule, and the Cauchy handoff are all explicit finite rows. It
sits downstream of the complete metric carrier of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completemetric-namecert}, the contractive map
packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-contractive-map-namecert}, the fixed
point iteration route of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-fixed-point-iteration-namecert}, the Cauchy
completion reflector of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchycompletionreflector-namecert}, and the
real-number ground of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The
certificate therefore consumes existing completion data; it does not assert
ambient completeness, global choice, or an unrecorded limit operator.

\begin{definition}[Banach contraction principle carrier]
\label{def:banach-contraction-principle-carrier}
A $\BanachContractionPrincipleUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
B=(M,F,I,Q,E,U,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the complete metric row, naming the metric carrier and the completeness seal used by the argument;
\item $F$ is the contraction map row, including the displayed modulus witness and its compatibility with the metric row;
\item $I$ is the iteration schedule row, recording the initial point and the successive iterates read by the construction;
\item $Q$ is the Cauchy witness row for the iteration sequence;
\item $E$ is the endpoint seal row, identifying the completed endpoint supplied by the complete metric data;
\item $U$ is the uniqueness row, binding any second fixed endpoint back to the same contraction ledger;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $M,F,I,Q,E,U$ together with continuation, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ replay routes by which the contraction ledger, the Cauchy witness, the endpoint seal, and uniqueness row are read;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, $\NameCert$, $\CompleteMetricUp$, $\ContractiveMapUp$, $\FixedPointIterationUp$, $\CauchyCompletionReflectorUp$, and $\RealUp$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\BanachContractionPrincipleUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier accepts exactly packets with these ten displayed rows. It has
no coordinate for an arbitrary choice of fixed point, an ambient completeness
claim outside $M$, a hidden contraction constant, or a limit witness that does
not pass through $Q$ and $E$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Banach contraction principle NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:banach-contraction-principle-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of
\autoref{def:banach-contraction-principle-carrier}, the local
$\NameCert_{\BanachContractionPrincipleUp}$ surface consists exactly of five
finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for $B=(M,F,I,Q,E,U,H,C,P,N)$;
\item contraction ledger exposure, requiring every use of the map row $F$ to read the displayed contraction data against $M$;
\item Cauchy-iteration handoff, requiring the iteration schedule $I$ to produce the witness row $Q$ before an endpoint is read;
\item complete-metric seal, requiring the endpoint row $E$ to be supplied by the completeness data in $M$ rather than by an ambient choice operator;
\item uniqueness nonescape, requiring every competing fixed endpoint to factor through $U,H,C,P,N$ with no off-packet fixed-point row.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from the displayed packet. The rows
$M,F,I,Q,E,U,H,C,P,N$ are the full inventory made available to the local
certificate.

The contraction ledger is read through $F$ against the metric data in $M$.
Thus a contraction claim with a hidden modulus or a different metric carrier
has no row in the packet.

The iteration schedule then hands off to the Cauchy witness row. The endpoint
seal can be read only after $Q$ is present and the complete metric data in $M$
supplies the endpoint row $E$.

Finally, uniqueness is confined to $U$ and the replay rows $H,C,P,N$. A second
fixed endpoint that escapes these rows would require an additional carrier
coordinate, which the classifier does not provide.
\end{proof}
