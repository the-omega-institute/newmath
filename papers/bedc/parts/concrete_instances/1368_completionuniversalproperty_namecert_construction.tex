\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-completionuniversalproperty-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$ interface records the finite universal-property surface for the constructive real-completion phase. Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, where completion is handled through explicit Cauchy data and extension control rather than by selecting host limits, the packet names the data needed to extend uniformly continuous maps out of the regular rational completion envelope. It is a certificate over $\CauchyCompletionMonadUp$, $\RealDiagonalCompletionUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.

\begin{definition}[CompletionUniversalProperty carrier]
\label{def:completion-universal-property-carrier}
A $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
U=(M,D,R,A,F,E,L,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the $\CauchyCompletionMonadUp$ completion-envelope row that supplies the completion-facing source packet;
\item $D$ is the $\RealDiagonalCompletionUp$ diagonal packet that reads regular rational Cauchy data through finite diagonal windows;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular rational input row whose stream windows provide the dense source observations;
\item $A$ is the $\RealUp$ real-name seal row through which the completed source is inspected;
\item $F$ is the $\ContinuousMapUp$ row on the dense regular rational surface, including its displayed modulus and graph-read ledger;
\item $E$ is the extension-existence ledger, a finite $\Cont$ route from $F$, $R$, $D$, and $A$ to a completion-facing $\ContinuousMapUp$ read;
\item $L$ is the uniqueness ledger, a displayed $\hsame$ comparison between two extension rows whenever they agree on the same $R$, $D$, $A$, $F$, and $E$ coordinates;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyCompletionMonadUp$, $\RealDiagonalCompletionUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, and $\ContinuousMapUp$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no quotient of regular streams, no selected host limit, no host-level complete metric space, and no equality principle outside the displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ ledgers.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CompletionUniversalProperty extension obligations]
\label{thm:completion-universal-property-extension-obligations}
Let $U=(M,D,R,A,F,E,L,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$ carrier. Every extension read exported by $U$ factors through the completion-envelope row $M$, the diagonal-completion row $D$, the regular rational source row $R$, the $\RealUp$ seal $A$, the dense $\ContinuousMapUp$ row $F$, and the finite continuation ledger $E$.

Consequently the extension obligation is finite: for each displayed precision request, the consumer reads a diagonal packet from $D$, a regular rational observation from $R$, a real-name seal from $A$, and a modulus-controlled graph row from $F$, then repacks them through $E$ as a completion-facing continuous-map row. No quotient representative, host limit, or ambient completeness theorem is introduced by the extension read.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CompletionUniversalPropertyUp.CompletionUniversalPropertyPacket\_extension\_obligations}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The completion source is the row $M$, and the only diagonal access to its real-name observations is the displayed row $D$.

The dense map data are exactly the $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $R$ and the $\ContinuousMapUp$ row $F$. For a requested precision, $F$ supplies the graph-read and modulus ledger on the dense surface, while $D$ and $A$ supply the completion-facing observation route.

The extension row $E$ is the only coordinate that repacks those observations as a completion-facing continuous map. Since the carrier has no coordinate for a quotient of streams, a selected host limit, or a host complete space, the exported extension remains a finite BEDC packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CompletionUniversalProperty uniqueness ledger]
\label{thm:completion-universal-property-uniqueness-ledger}
Let $U=(M,D,R,A,F,E,L,P,N)$ and $U'=(M,D,R,A,F,E',L',P,N')$ be accepted $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$ carriers with the same completion envelope, diagonal row, dense regular rational row, real seal, dense continuous-map row, and package provenance. If the extension ledgers $E$ and $E'$ agree on every displayed finite diagonal packet read from $D$ and every displayed regular rational source read from $R$, then the two exported extension rows are compared by the displayed $\hsame$ ledger $L$ and $L'$.

The uniqueness claim is therefore a ledger statement over visible rows. It identifies extensions only by the carried $\hsame$ comparison generated from their finite dense-source and diagonal reads, not by function extensionality over a host completion.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CompletionUniversalPropertyUp.CompletionUniversalPropertyPacket\_uniqueness\_ledger}
\begin{proof}
Read the common coordinates $M$, $D$, $R$, $A$, $F$, and $P$ from the two packets. These rows fix the completion envelope, the dense-source observations, and the continuous-map data available to both extension ledgers.

The hypothesis says that $E$ and $E'$ agree on the displayed finite reads supplied by $D$ and $R$. Transport those reads through the shared $\Cont$ routes in $F$ and through the common real-name seal $A$.

The uniqueness coordinate is precisely the displayed $\hsame$ ledger. Applying $L$ and $L'$ to the transported extension rows yields the required comparison, with no appeal to host function equality or an external completion object.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CompletionUniversalProperty NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:completion-universal-property-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp}$ surface consists of five finite rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier: an accepted object is exactly the finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet of \autoref{def:completion-universal-property-carrier};
\item classifier: two accepted packets classify together only by componentwise $\hsame$ transport of $M$, $D$, $R$, $A$, $F$, $E$, $L$, $P$, and $N$, and by the displayed $\Cont$ routes carried by the dense and extension map rows;
\item extension existence over finite diagonal packets: \autoref{thm:completion-universal-property-extension-obligations} supplies the extension row from the displayed $\RealDiagonalCompletionUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$ reads;
\item uniqueness by displayed ledger: \autoref{thm:completion-universal-property-uniqueness-ledger} compares any two extension rows only through the carried $\hsame$ uniqueness ledger;
\item non-escape: every consumer read remains inside $\CauchyCompletionMonadUp$, $\RealDiagonalCompletionUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission and classification are projections from \autoref{def:completion-universal-property-carrier}. The classifier has no fields beyond the displayed packet coordinates, their $\hsame$ transports, and the $\Cont$ ledgers attached to the dense and extension map rows.

Extension existence is the obligation of \autoref{thm:completion-universal-property-extension-obligations}. It constructs the public extension read from finite diagonal-completion packets and regular rational observations.

Uniqueness is the ledger obligation of \autoref{thm:completion-universal-property-uniqueness-ledger}. The comparison is carried by the displayed $\hsame$ row, so it does not require equality of host functions.

The non-escape row follows by inspecting the carrier. There is no coordinate for quotient streams, choice-selected limits, host completeness, or host real equality; every exported object is one of the listed BEDC rows or a finite route between them.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CompletionUniversalProperty extension-uniqueness scope]
\label{thm:completion-universal-property-extension-uniqueness-scope}
Let $U=(M,D,R,A,F,E,L,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$ carrier over $\CauchyCompletionMonadUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealDiagonalCompletionUp$, and $\RealUp$. The packet combines extension existence and uniqueness ledger stability into one $\NameCert$-consumable $\mathsf{BHist}$ row: the extension ledger $E$ reads the dense $\ContinuousMapUp$ row through the completion envelope, diagonal row, regular rational source, and real seal, while the uniqueness ledger $L$ compares any two such extension rows by displayed $\hsame$ transport over the same provenance.

Consequently the completion universal-property surface exports a single finite packet for extension existence, uniqueness, and non-escape. It does not export quotient streams, selected host limits, host complete spaces, or function extensionality outside the displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First construct the extension component. Apply \autoref{thm:completion-universal-property-extension-obligations}: every extension read factors through $M$, $D$, $R$, $A$, $F$, and the finite continuation ledger $E$.

Next bind uniqueness to the same displayed rows. Apply \autoref{thm:completion-universal-property-uniqueness-ledger}: two extension rows agreeing on the finite diagonal and regular rational reads are compared by the carried $\hsame$ uniqueness ledger.

Finally repack the two components as a local naming-certificate surface. Apply \autoref{thm:completion-universal-property-namecert-obligations}, which lists carrier admission, classifier transport, extension existence, uniqueness by displayed ledger, and non-escape over exactly the rows of $U$.

The resulting packet is therefore a single $\mathsf{BHist}$ scope for the completion universal property. Since every row is displayed in the carrier, the construction has no route to quotient streams, selected host limits, host complete spaces, or host-level function equality.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CompletionUniversalProperty classifier transport]
\label{thm:completion-universal-property-classifier-transport}
Let
$$
U=(M,D,R,A,F,E,L,P,N)
$$
and
$$
U'=(M',D',R',A',F',E',L',P',N')
$$
be accepted $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$ carriers. Suppose the classifier records componentwise comparisons
$$
\begin{aligned}
&\hsame(M,M'),\quad \hsame(D,D'),\quad \hsame(R,R'),\quad \hsame(A,A'),\quad \hsame(F,F'),\\
&\hsame(E,E'),\quad \hsame(L,L'),\quad \hsame(P,P'),\quad \hsame(N,N')
\end{aligned}
$$
and the displayed $\Cont$ routes on the completion envelope, diagonal packet, regular rational source, real seal, dense map, extension ledger, uniqueness ledger, package provenance, and local naming rows. Then accepted transport preserves the carrier of \autoref{def:completion-universal-property-carrier} and preserves the $\NameCert_{\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp}$ classifier. No transported row leaves the nine displayed coordinates.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project both accepted packets through \autoref{def:completion-universal-property-carrier}. The carrier boundary is exactly the ordered tuple of nine rows, and each row named in the transport hypothesis is one of those coordinates.

The componentwise $\hsame$ comparisons preserve the displayed row identities. The recorded $\Cont$ routes only say where the same rows are read inside the finite packet, so they do not add a new completion object, quotient stream, or host equality principle.

Therefore the classifier after transport is the same local classifier with primed coordinates. Since every transported component is still one of $M'$, $D'$, $R'$, $A'$, $F'$, $E'$, $L'$, $P'$, and $N'$, the carrier and classifier are preserved on the accepted $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$ surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CompletionUniversalProperty dense-source factorization]
\label{thm:completion-universal-property-dense-source-factorization}
Let $U=(M,D,R,A,F,E,L,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$ carrier. Every exported extension read factors through the completion envelope $M$, the finite diagonal packet $D$, the dense regular-rational source $R$, the $\RealUp$ seal $A$, the dense $\ContinuousMapUp$ row $F$, and the extension ledger $E$. In this factorization, $R$ is the dense regular-rational source and $D$ is the finite diagonal packet through which completion-facing reads are admitted.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:completion-universal-property-extension-obligations}. It already identifies the six coordinates through which every extension read passes: $M$, $D$, $R$, $A$, $F$, and $E$.

It remains only to name the roles of the two source coordinates. By \autoref{def:completion-universal-property-carrier}, $R$ is the regular rational input row whose finite stream windows provide dense source observations, while $D$ is the $\RealDiagonalCompletionUp$ row reading regular rational Cauchy data through finite diagonal windows.

Composing these coordinate projections with the extension obligation gives the stated factorization. No other carrier row can serve as a dense source or diagonal admission route, because the exported extension is repacked only by the finite continuation ledger $E$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CompletionUniversalProperty extension non-escape]
\label{thm:completion-universal-property-extension-non-escape}
For any accepted $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$ carrier, the extension-plus-uniqueness packet exposes no quotient stream, selected host limit, host complete metric space, or function extensionality outside the displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ rows. Its visible data are exactly the completion envelope, diagonal packet, dense source, real seal, dense continuous-map row, extension ledger, uniqueness ledger, package provenance, and local naming row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The extension side is controlled by \autoref{thm:completion-universal-property-extension-obligations}. Each exported extension read is routed through $M$, $D$, $R$, $A$, $F$, and $E$, all of which are displayed coordinates of the accepted carrier.

The uniqueness side is controlled by \autoref{thm:completion-universal-property-uniqueness-ledger}. Two extension rows are compared only by the carried $\hsame$ ledger over the same finite dense-source and diagonal reads.

Finally, \autoref{thm:completion-universal-property-namecert-obligations} lists the local certificate rows and its non-escape boundary. Since that list contains no quotient stream, host-selected limit, host complete metric space, or function-extensionality row, the extension-plus-uniqueness packet cannot export any of them outside the displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ routes.
\end{proof}
