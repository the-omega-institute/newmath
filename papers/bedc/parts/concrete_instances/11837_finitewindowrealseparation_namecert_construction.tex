\begin{definition}[Finite-window Real-separation carrier]
\label{def:finite-window-real-separation-carrier}
A $\FiniteWindowRealSeparationUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$

$$

\begin{enumerate}
\item $W$ is the shared finite-window row naming the common observation window for the two Real-facing reads;
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row attached to exactly that shared window;
\item $S$ is the combined $\StreamNameUp/\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row, exposing the stream-name schedule and the regular-rational readback reached from $W$ and $D$;
\item $R$ is the $\RealUp$ separation readback row reached only after $W,D,S$ have been displayed;

\begin{theorem}[Shared-window refusal for Real separation]
\label{thm:finite-window-real-separation-window-refusal}
For every accepted $\FiniteWindowRealSeparationUp$ carrier
$$
F=(W,D,S,R,H,C,P,N),
$$
no consumer can expose the RealUp separation readback row $R$ without first preserving the shared finite-window row $W$, the dyadic tolerance row $D$, and the StreamName/RegSeqRat readback row $S$. Any route that asks for host real equality, quotient-stream equality, a selected representative, or a detached separation witness is outside the accepted carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:finite-window-real-separation-carrier}. The only Real-facing coordinate is $R$, and the carrier lists it after the shared window, tolerance, and readback rows.

Read the route in dependency order. The row $W$ fixes the common finite observation window, $D$ fixes the dyadic tolerance for that window, and $S$ fixes the StreamName and RegSeqRat readback that the Real-facing separation row consumes.

The remaining rows $H,C,P,N$ only transport, replay, package, and name the same displayed packet. Since no quotient stream, host equality, selected representative, or detached separation coordinate appears in the carrier, a consumer that omits $W,D,S$ has no accepted path to $R$.
\end{proof}
