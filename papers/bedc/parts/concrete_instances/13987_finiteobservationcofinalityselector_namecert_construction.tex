\begin{definition}[Finite observation cofinality-selector carrier]
\label{def:finite-observation-cofinality-selector-carrier}
A $\FiniteObservationCofinalitySelectorUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
F=(B,Q,W,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $B$ is the accepted $\FiniteObservationBudgetSelectorUp$ budget row that bounds the visible Real-completion requests;
\item $Q$ is the cofinality-request row recording the finite diagonal requests currently served by $B$;
\item $W$ is the finite family of selected $\StreamNameUp$ windows whose indices meet every request in $Q$;
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger read from the windows in $W$;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row reached after the dyadic ledger is displayed;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached through $R$;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the budget, request, window, dyadic, regular, seal, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from $B$ and $Q$ to $W$, from $W$ to $D$, from $D$ to $R$, and from $R$ to $E$;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\FiniteObservationBudgetSelectorUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteObservationCofinalitySelectorUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through these ten rows. It has no coordinate for a countable-choice selector, quotient stream, host real equality test, hidden diagonal subsequence, detached limit value, or ambient completeness theorem.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Finite observation cofinality-selector NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:finite-observation-cofinality-selector-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\FiniteObservationCofinalitySelectorUp$ carrier
$$
F=(B,Q,W,D,R,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteObservationCofinalitySelectorUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier exposure for $F$, budget admission through $B$, finite cofinality-request exposure through $Q$, selected-window coverage through $W$, dyadic readback through $D$, $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff through $R$, $\RealUp$ seal exposure through $E$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, package provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:finite-observation-cofinality-selector-carrier}. The displayed tuple lists the accepted budget selector, the finite cofinality requests, the selected StreamName windows, the dyadic ledger, the regular handoff, the Real seal, and the structural rows.

Read the analytic rows in order. The budget row $B$ bounds the requests in $Q$, and the continuation route sends those requests to the selected windows $W$ before any dyadic or regular readback can be used.

The seal-facing rows then remain downstream from the same finite surface. The dyadic ledger $D$ is read from $W$, the handoff row $R$ is read from $D$, and the seal row $E$ is read from $R$.

Finally inspect $H,C,P,N$. These rows transport, replay, source, and name the same packet, and no further analytic coordinate appears in the carrier. Hence the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Finite observation cofinality-selector non-escape]
\label{thm:finite-observation-cofinality-selector-nonescape}
Every Real-completion, diagonal-limit, $\RegSeqRatUp$, or $\RealUp$ consumer of an accepted $\FiniteObservationCofinalitySelectorUp$ packet factors through the displayed finite route
$$
B \to Q \to W \to D \to R \to E
$$
together with $H,C,P,N$. The consumer may read the finite budget selector, cofinality-request row, selected $\StreamNameUp$ windows, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger, $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff, $\RealUp$ seal, transport rows, continuation rows, provenance, and local name, but it cannot read a countable-choice selector, quotient stream, host real equality test, hidden diagonal subsequence, detached limit value, or ambient completeness theorem through the packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the NameCert obligations. They place every accepted read on the displayed carrier rows and require the budget-to-window and window-to-seal route to pass through $B,Q,W,D,R,E$.

Use the cofinality role of the request row. The row $Q$ records only the finite diagonal requests currently served by the budget row $B$, while $W$ records the finite selected windows that meet those requests. It is therefore a finite cofinal surface, not an infinite subsequence or choice principle.

The remaining rows add no escape route. The dyadic, regular, and Real rows $D,R,E$ are downstream from the displayed windows, and $H,C,P,N$ only preserve, replay, source, and name the same route.

The excluded objects are not coordinates of the carrier and are not introduced by the structural rows. Any consumer that reads one of them has left the accepted $\FiniteObservationCofinalitySelectorUp$ packet.
\end{proof}
