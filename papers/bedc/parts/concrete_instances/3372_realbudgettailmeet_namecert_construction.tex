The $\RealBudgetTailMeetUp$ surface packages the finite observation budget used by a $\RealUp$ consumer together with the shared tail threshold used by a regular-Cauchy comparison and the sealed completion handoff. It is the concrete bridge from budgeted reads and tail-meet synchronization to the $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ route named in the real-completeness vision.

The dependency path is the finite observation budget of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-observation-budget-namecert}, the shared-tail threshold packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-tail-meet-namecert}, and the completion-facing seal handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchylimitseal-namecert}; the local carrier only synchronizes these sibling rows.

\begin{definition}[RealBudgetTailMeet carrier]
\label{def:real-budget-tail-meet-carrier}
A $\RealBudgetTailMeetUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
R=(B,T,L,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $B$ is an accepted $\RealObservationBudgetUp$ packet naming the finite precision request, selected real-facing window, regular source readback, and dyadic observation budget;
\item $T$ is an accepted $\RegularCauchyTailMeetUp$ packet naming the shared tail threshold, paired regular-Cauchy source rows, paired schedules, comparison row, and threshold ledger;
\item $L$ is an accepted $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ handoff whose source family, scheduled window, observation ledger, completion diagonal row, and $\RealUp$ seal consume the same budget and shared-threshold rows;
\item $H$ stores the displayed $\hsame$ transports relating the budget rows, shared-tail rows, and seal-handoff rows componentwise;
\item $C$ stores the $\Cont$ routes through which downstream $\RealUp$ and completion consumers read the budget, tail meet, and seal handoff;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RealObservationBudgetUp$, $\RegularCauchyTailMeetUp$, $\CauchyLimitSealUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RealBudgetTailMeetUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares carriers only by the visible rows $B,T,L,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for quotient streams, selected host limits, hidden tail oracles, host real equality, trichotomy, or ambient completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[RealBudgetTailMeet NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:real-budget-tail-meet-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:real-budget-tail-meet-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\RealBudgetTailMeetUp}$ surface consists of carrier admission for $R=(B,T,L,H,C,P,N)$, classifier stability under the displayed $\hsame$ rows in $H$, budget exactness for the $\RealObservationBudgetUp$ row $B$, shared-threshold exactness for the $\RegularCauchyTailMeetUp$ row $T$, seal-route exactness for the $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ row $L$, and non-escape through $C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}

\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RealBudgetTailMeet budget projection]
\label{thm:real-budget-tail-meet-budget-projection}
Every accepted $\RealBudgetTailMeetUp$ carrier projects to the same finite observation budget used by its $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ handoff. More precisely, if
$$
R=(B,T,L,H,C,P,N),
$$
then every $\RealUp$ consumer read through $R$ first reads the $\RealObservationBudgetUp$ row $B$, transports that budget through $H$ and $C$, and only then reaches the shared threshold packet $T$ and the seal handoff $L$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier and read the budget coordinate $B$. It records the precision request, finite window, regular source readback, and dyadic observation ledger available to the consumer.

Apply the $\RealObservationBudgetUp$ exactness surface through the displayed continuation rows. The consumer route must carry the same budget rows through $H$ and $C$ before any downstream handoff can be read.

The remaining coordinates $T$ and $L$ are attached to that route by the carrier and the package provenance. They therefore consume the projected budget rather than an independent precision source.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RealBudgetTailMeet seal handoff]
\label{thm:real-budget-tail-meet-seal-handoff}
Let $R=(B,T,L,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\RealBudgetTailMeetUp$ carrier. The completion-facing handoff from $R$ to $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ is exactly the row $L$ together with the budget projection from $B$, the shared threshold from $T$, and the displayed transports and routes in $H,C,P,N$. Thus the route to the $\RealUp$ seal factors through a finite observation budget and one shared regular-Cauchy tail threshold before the seal row is read.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the budget projection of \autoref{thm:real-budget-tail-meet-budget-projection}. It fixes the consumer precision, selected window, source readback, and dyadic ledger inside $B$.

Use the shared-threshold surface of $\RegularCauchyTailMeetUp$ for $T$. The two regular-Cauchy tails are synchronized by the displayed threshold row and by the comparison ledger already attached to the same budget route.

Finally read $L$ as the $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ handoff. Its finite-window route consumes the same regular source, scheduled window, dyadic observation ledger, completion diagonal row, and $\RealUp$ seal, with $H,C,P,N$ recording the visible transport and provenance. No separate completion channel is introduced.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RealBudgetTailMeet non-escape boundary]
\label{thm:real-budget-tail-meet-nonescape}
An accepted $\RealBudgetTailMeetUp$ carrier exports only the finite rows $B,T,L,H,C,P,N$ and their displayed $\RealObservationBudgetUp$, $\RegularCauchyTailMeetUp$, and $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ dependency surfaces. It exports no quotient stream, selected host limit, hidden tail oracle, host real equality, trichotomy row, countable-choice principle, or ambient real-completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier coordinates. The public rows are exactly the budget packet, tail-meet packet, seal handoff, transports, continuation routes, package provenance, and local naming row.

The budget coordinate contains only finite observation data, the tail-meet coordinate contains only the shared threshold and comparison rows, and the seal coordinate contains only the finite completion handoff to $\RealUp$.

Since quotient streams, selected host limits, hidden tail oracles, host real equality, trichotomy, choice, and ambient completeness have no coordinate in $R$, a downstream consumer cannot read any of them from the $\RealBudgetTailMeetUp$ surface.
\end{proof}
