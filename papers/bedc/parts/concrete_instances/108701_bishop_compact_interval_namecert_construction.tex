\begin{definition}[Bishop compact interval carrier]
\label{def:bishop-compact-interval-carrier}

$$

\begin{enumerate}
\item $I$ is the $\ClosedBoundedIntervalUp$ source row, carrying endpoint order, rational containment, dyadic containment, finite windows, regular readback, and Real sealing for the interval.
\item $L$ is the $\LocatedRealIntervalUp$ row, exposing located endpoint reads, radius data, locator responses, and metric bracket rows for the same interval source.
\item $F$ is the $\ClosedBoundedIntervalCompactUp$ finite-net row, recording the compact-interval coverage surface accepted for downstream finite-net consumers.

\begin{theorem}[Bishop compact interval NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:bishop-compact-interval-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\BishopCompactIntervalUp$ carrier $K=(I,L,F,W,R,D,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\BishopCompactIntervalUp}$ surface consists exactly of six finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed closed-bounded interval, located interval, finite-net, stream, regular, dyadic, Real, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item interval-source compatibility, requiring the located row $L$ and closed-bounded row $I$ to name the same endpoint route before any compact read is exposed;
\item finite-net admission, requiring compact-interval coverage to pass through $F$ rather than through a host open-cover principle;
\item dyadic-window exactness, requiring every mesh or tolerance request to read $D$ before the finite windows $W$ and regular readback $R$ are consumed;
\item Real-seal nonescape, requiring the endpoint and interval readback route to reach $E$ only after $I,L,F,W,R,D$ have been displayed;
\item transport, replay, provenance, and local naming exhaustion through $H,C,P,N$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:bishop-compact-interval-carrier}. The tuple lists the complete finite row inventory made available to the local certificate.

The interval source is read through $I$ and $L$. Their endpoint, locator, containment, and bracket rows must agree on the same displayed interval route before compactness-facing consumers can inspect the finite-net row.

Finite-net admission is then confined to $F$. Since the carrier has no open-cover or subcover coordinate, compactness can be exposed only as the accepted closed-bounded interval finite-net row.

The dyadic and window obligations are read through $D,W,R$. Tolerance requests first enter the dyadic ledger, then open finite stream windows, and only then reach regular rational readback.

Finally $E,H,C,P,N$ seal and replay the same route. These rows preserve the packet under transport and provenance, while row exhaustion rules out quotient representatives, selected subcovers, choice schedules, and ambient Real completeness.
\end{proof}
