\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\PremetricUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-premetric-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Kelley on uniform sources and Bishop--Bridges constructive metric data, the $\PremetricUp$ packet records the distance-comparison surface that sits before separated reflection and metric completion. It reads the finite entourage interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformspace-namecert}, supplies the source row consumed by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separated-reflection-universal-namecert}, and keeps the completion-facing boundary visible before \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metriccompletion-namecert} or the terminal $\RealUp$ seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert} can be inspected. The packet names a constructive distance witness and zero-distance classifier; it does not quotient points, select representatives, assert host equality, or import ambient completeness.

\begin{definition}[Premetric carrier]
\label{def:premetric-carrier}
A $\PremetricUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
P=(X,U,D,Z,S,M,H,C,Q,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the source carrier row whose points are admitted only through displayed BEDC windows.
\item $U$ is the $\UniformSpaceUp$ source row, exposing the finite entourage requests that the distance witness is allowed to refine.
\item $D$ is the distance-witness relation row, recording finite positive-radius comparisons between displayed point windows.
\item $Z$ is the zero-distance classifier row, comparing two displayed source windows only through the radius comparisons stored in $D$.
\item $S$ is the separated-reflection handoff row consumed by $\SeparatedReflectionUniversalUp$ after $Z$ has been displayed.
\item $M$ is the completion-facing nonescape row, binding every $\MetricCompletionUp$ read to $X,U,D,Z,S$ before any completed object is named.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for source, uniform, distance, zero-distance, reflection, completion, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a finite distance or zero-distance request through $X,U,D,Z,S,M,H$.
\item $Q$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\PremetricUp$, $\UniformSpaceUp$, $\SeparatedReflectionUniversalUp$, $\MetricCompletionUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\PremetricUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $X,U,D,Z,S,M,H,C,Q,N$. It has no coordinate for quotient point equality, selected separated representatives, chosen completion limits, host metric equality, trichotomy, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Premetric NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:premetric-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\PremetricUp$ carrier $P=(X,U,D,Z,S,M,H,C,Q,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\PremetricUp}$ surface consists exactly of source-carrier admission, uniform-source readback, distance-witness exposure, zero-distance classification, separated-reflection handoff, completion-facing nonescape, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, package provenance, and local naming. Each obligation is read through the finite route
$$
X,U,D,Z \longrightarrow S,M \longrightarrow H,C,Q,N .
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:premetric-carrier}. The source row $X$ and uniform row $U$ are the only inputs a consumer may inspect before asking for distance data.

The distance-witness relation $D$ supplies finite positive-radius comparisons between displayed source windows. The zero-distance classifier $Z$ is downstream of $D$, so equality-like reads are exposed as repeated distance comparisons rather than as host point equality.

The reflection row $S$ and completion boundary $M$ are then forced to read $X,U,D,Z$. Componentwise transport through $H$ and replay through $C$ preserve that order, while $Q,N$ record provenance and local naming.

Since quotient point equality, selected separated representatives, chosen completion limits, host metric equality, trichotomy, countable choice, and ambient Real completeness do not occur among the carrier coordinates, no NameCert obligation can be discharged outside the displayed finite route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Premetric separated-reflection route]
\label{thm:premetric-separated-reflection-route}
Let $P=(X,U,D,Z,S,M,H,C,Q,N)$ be an accepted $\PremetricUp$ carrier. Every $\SeparatedReflectionUniversalUp$ consumer of $P$ must first read the source carrier $X$, the uniform source row $U$, the distance-witness relation $D$, and the zero-distance classifier $Z$. The handoff row $S$ therefore presents the reflection source as a finite zero-distance comparison surface, not as a quotient carrier or a selected representative map.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply the obligation theorem, \autoref{thm:premetric-namecert-obligations}. It identifies $X,U,D,Z$ as the full prefix needed before the separated-reflection handoff can be consumed.

The row $U$ records the uniform-source requests, while $D$ records the finite distance comparisons refining those requests. The classifier $Z$ reads zero distance only through those comparisons.

Thus $S$ supplies the premetric source expected by the separated-reflection route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separated-reflection-universal-namecert}. The structural rows $H,C,Q,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same handoff; they do not add quotient data, selected representatives, or host equality.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Premetric completion boundary]
\label{thm:premetric-completion-boundary}
Every $\MetricCompletionUp$ or $\RealUp$ consumer of an accepted $\PremetricUp$ packet reaches completion-facing data only through the displayed route
$$
\UniformSpaceUp,\PremetricUp,\SeparatedReflectionUniversalUp \longmapsto \MetricCompletionUp,\RealUp .
$$
In particular, the completion-facing row $M$ exports finite distance-comparison and zero-distance data, not a completed point, a selected Cauchy limit, a quotient equality, or an ambient completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project an accepted carrier $P=(X,U,D,Z,S,M,H,C,Q,N)$. The completion-facing row $M$ is downstream of the source, uniform, distance, zero-distance, and separated-reflection rows.

Any completion consumer must therefore read $X,U,D,Z,S$ before it can inspect $M$. The terminal $\RealUp$ seal, when a downstream completion chapter exposes one, is reached only after that same finite route has been displayed.

The packet contains no coordinate for a completed point, selected Cauchy limit, quotient equality, host metric equality, countable choice, or ambient completeness. Hence the completion boundary is a nonescape condition over the finite premetric surface.
\end{proof}
