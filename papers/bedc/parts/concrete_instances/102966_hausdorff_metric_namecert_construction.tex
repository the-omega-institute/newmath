\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\HausdorffMetricUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-hausdorff-metric-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following the Hausdorff--Kuratowski compact-set metric reading, the $\HausdorffMetricUp$ packet records the finite-net route by which two nonempty compact-subset names expose a displayed distance bound without importing an ambient supremum or a choice-selected nearest point. It sits over the compact-metric source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, the metric carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the finite-net discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-totallybounded-namecert}, and the precompact metric surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-precompactmetric-namecert}. The packet is a NameCert interface for compact-subset distance reads; it is not an open-cover compactness theorem, a host hyperspace construction, a quotient subset equality principle, a supremum principle, or a nearest-point selector.

\begin{definition}[Hausdorff metric finite-net carrier]
\label{def:hausdorff-metric-finite-net-carrier}
A $\HausdorffMetricUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
H=(A,B,M,K,E,D,S,T,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ and $B$ are the two nonempty compact-subset name rows. Each row records the displayed subset identifier, nonempty witness address, compact-subset admission data, and the finite request for which the Hausdorff read is being exposed.
\item $M$ is the ambient $\MetricUp$ row consumed by both compact-subset names, including the point-distance reads used by the finite-net comparison.
\item $K$ records the two $\CompactMetricUp$ rows, one for each compact-subset name, together with the compact-metric admission data needed before a finite net may be inspected.
\item $E$ records finite $\ProbeBundle$ nets for $A$ and $B$ at a displayed positive $\RatUp$ tolerance, including the mesh budget, net membership rows, and coverage witnesses relative to the compact-metric rows in $K$.
\item $D$ records point-to-set distance witnesses from each displayed net point in the first compact-subset name to the second compact-subset name, and from each displayed net point in the second compact-subset name to the first compact-subset name.
\item $S$ records the two symmetric bound rows obtained from $D$, including the rational slack showing that the two directed finite-net estimates share the same tolerance budget.
\item $T$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the subset names, metric row, compact-metric rows, finite nets, point-to-set witnesses, symmetric bounds, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the route
$$
A,B,M,K,E \longmapsto D \longmapsto S .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\HausdorffMetricUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\MetricUp$, $\TotallyBoundedUp$, $\PrecompactMetricUp$, $\ProbeBundle$, $\RatUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\HausdorffMetricUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $A,B,M,K,E,D,S,T,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for open-cover compactness, host hyperspace membership, quotient subset equality, ambient supremum formation, infimum formation, or a choice-selected nearest point.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[HausdorffMetricUp NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:hausdorff-metric-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\HausdorffMetricUp$ carrier
$$
H=(A,B,M,K,E,D,S,T,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\HausdorffMetricUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the two nonempty compact-subset names, the ambient metric row, the compact-metric rows, the finite-net rows, the point-to-set witness rows, the symmetric bound rows, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item compact-subset net exactness, requiring every displayed $\ProbeBundle$ net in $E$ to be read through the corresponding compact-subset name and compact-metric row before a point-distance request can be inspected;
\item symmetric point-distance bound stability, requiring the two directed estimates in $D$ and $S$ to use the same positive $\RatUp$ tolerance budget and to survive componentwise $\hsame$ transport in $T$;
\item classifier transport, requiring any accepted comparison of packets to preserve the same subset-name rows, metric source, compact-metric witnesses, finite nets, point-to-set witnesses, and symmetric rational bounds;
\item ledger exhaustion, requiring $C,P,N$ to replay, source, and name only the displayed finite route and to export no open-cover compactness theorem, host hyperspace object, quotient subset equality, ambient supremum, infimum, or selected nearest point.
\end{enumerate}
No Hausdorff metric obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Hausdorff compact-subset distance bound]
\label{thm:hausdorff-metric-compact-subset-distance-bound}
Every Metric-facing, CompactMetric-facing, or hyperspace-facing consumer of an accepted $\HausdorffMetricUp$ carrier factors through the finite route
$$
A,B,M,K,E \longmapsto D \longmapsto S \longmapsto T,C,P,N .
$$
Consequently the consumer receives only the displayed nonempty compact-subset names, the shared metric row, the compact-metric rows, the finite $\ProbeBundle$ nets at a positive $\RatUp$ tolerance, the point-to-set distance witnesses, the symmetric finite-net bounds, componentwise transport, replay, provenance, and the local name. It cannot claim an open-cover compactness theorem, host supremum, quotient subset equality, infimum, or choice-selected nearest point from the packet.
\end{theorem}
