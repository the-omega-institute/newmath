\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\LocallyCompactMetricUp$}

Following Bourbaki and Kelley metric topology together with Bishop-style constructive metric practice, the $\LocallyCompactMetricUp$ packet records local compact neighbourhood data as finite BEDC rows around a displayed metric point. It sits over the $\MetricSpaceUp$ distance surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the $\CompactMetricUp$ compact-neighbourhood witness of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, the $\CompleteMetricUp$ limit rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completemetric-namecert}, the $\TotallyBoundedUp$ finite-net rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-totallybounded-namecert}, and the positive $\RealUp$ radius seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The chapter names a local compactness certificate for later proper-metric and compactification consumers; it does not assert arbitrary open-cover compactness, choice of neighbourhood bases, quotient completion equality, or ambient local compactness outside the displayed packet.

\begin{definition}[Locally compact metric carrier]
\label{def:locally-compact-metric-carrier}
A $\LocallyCompactMetricUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(X,x,U,K,T,C_m,r,H,D,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is a $\MetricSpaceUp$ carrier row whose point histories and distance witnesses are read from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}.
\item $x$ is the inspected point history carried by $X$.
\item $U$ is a local neighbourhood row around $x$, displayed only by metric-ball membership and finite route data.
\item $K$ is a $\CompactMetricUp$ witness for the neighbourhood closure packet.
\item $T$ is the $\TotallyBoundedUp$ finite-net component read by $K$.
\item $C_m$ is the $\CompleteMetricUp$ limit-witness component read by $K$.
\item $r$ is a positive $\RealUp$ radius seal for the local ball.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $X,x,U,K,T,C_m,r$.
\item $D$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a local query through the metric carrier, neighbourhood row, compact witness, finite-net row, completion row, radius seal, and transport row.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LocallyCompactMetricUp$, $\MetricSpaceUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\CompleteMetricUp$, $\TotallyBoundedUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LocallyCompactMetricUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for arbitrary open covers, a selected neighbourhood basis, global compactness of $X$, quotient completion equality, countable choice, or a host topological space.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Locally compact metric classifier]
\label{def:locally-compact-metric-classifier}

$$
L=(X,x,U,K,T,C_m,r,H,D,P,N)
\quad\hbox{and}\quad
L'=(X',x',U',K',T',C'_m,r',H',D',P',N')

\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Locally compact metric NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:locally-compact-metric-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\LocallyCompactMetricUp$ carrier $L=(X,x,U,K,T,C_m,r,H,D,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\LocallyCompactMetricUp}$ surface consists exactly of the metric carrier row $X$, the inspected point row $x$, the neighbourhood row $U$, the compact-metric witness $K$, the totally-bounded finite-net row $T$, the complete-metric limit row $C_m$, the positive Real radius seal $r$, componentwise transport $H$, displayed replay $D$, provenance $P$, and local naming row $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:locally-compact-metric-carrier}. The projection exposes the metric row, inspected point, local neighbourhood, compact witness, finite-net witness, completion witness, radius seal, transport, replay, provenance, and naming row.

The compact witness $K$ is read only through the sibling $\CompactMetricUp$ surface, whose own carrier consumes $\TotallyBoundedUp$ and $\CompleteMetricUp$ rows. Thus the local compactness packet records compact neighbourhood evidence without adding an open-cover coordinate.

The radius row $r$ is read through the Real seal surface and the route $D$ replays all displayed dependencies. Since the carrier has no coordinate for selected bases, global compactness, quotient completion equality, countable choice, or an ambient host topology, the listed rows exhaust the NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Locally compact metric neighbourhood non-escape]
\label{thm:locally-compact-metric-neighbourhood-nonescape}
Every consumer of an accepted $\LocallyCompactMetricUp$ packet that preserves the classifier of \autoref{def:locally-compact-metric-classifier} must preserve the same metric carrier, inspected point, neighbourhood row, compact-metric witness, total-boundedness row, completeness row, Real radius seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row. In particular, it cannot replace the displayed local neighbourhood by an unrecorded open cover, a choice-selected compact set, or a host topology.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the classifier of \autoref{def:locally-compact-metric-classifier}. It compares the accepted packets componentwise and has no residual coordinate where a different neighbourhood, compact witness, finite-net row, completion row, or radius seal could be hidden.

The continuation row preserves the same route through the metric, compact, total-boundedness, completeness, and Real-radius siblings. Therefore a consumer that keeps the classifier fixed must read the same finite packet and cannot escape to an unrecorded open cover, selected compact subset, or host topology.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Locally compact metric sibling dependency route]
\label{thm:locally-compact-metric-sibling-dependency-route}
Every accepted $\LocallyCompactMetricUp$ carrier routes its local compactness certificate through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completemetric-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-totallybounded-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. \begin{proof}

Finally $H,D,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same dependency chain. Thus the local compactness packet is a finite L10 neighbour of the metric, compact metric, complete metric, totally bounded, and Real chapters, not a stand-alone topological assertion.
\end{proof}
