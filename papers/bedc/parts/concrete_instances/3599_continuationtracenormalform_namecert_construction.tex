\begin{definition}[ContinuationTraceNormalForm carrier]
\label{def:continuation-trace-normal-form-carrier}
A $\ContinuationTraceNormalFormUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$

$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the source history row from which replay begins;
\item $T$ is the finite $\ContinuationTraceUp$ small-step ledger, including its displayed source, mark-and-tail, target, and trace rows;
\item $R$ is the terminal history row reached by replaying $T$ through the displayed $\Cont$ routes;
\item $Q$ is the terminal-read witness exposed by the $\ContinuationBigStepUp$ surface;
\item $V$ is the $\TypedReductionNormalFormUp$ row naming the normal-form classifier for the same terminal history;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $S,T,R,Q,V$, provenance, and the local name;
\item $C$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ routes that replay the trace ledger, reach the terminal row, consume the terminal-read witness, and expose the typed normal-form row;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, $\BMark$, $\ContinuationTraceUp$, $\ContinuationBigStepUp$, $\TypedReductionNormalFormUp$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ContinuationTraceNormalFormUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier is exactly this finite packet. It contains no host conversion relation, no quotient-normalization proof, no evaluator graph, no global confluence theorem, and no external normalizer.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[ContinuationTraceNormalForm NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:continuation-trace-normal-form-namecert-obligations}

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

Trace replay is read from $T$ and $C$. The $\ContinuationTraceUp$ row supplies finite small-step entries, and the displayed continuation route replays those entries from the source row $S$ to the terminal row $R$.

Terminal exposure is then read through $Q$, and typed normal-form readback is read through $V$. The transport row $H$ moves the same displayed rows componentwise, while $P$ and $N$ record package provenance and local naming.

The non-escape clauses follow from the carrier inventory. Since the packet has no coordinate for host conversion, quotient behaviour, an evaluator graph, global confluence, or an external normalizer, no local certificate projection can export one.
