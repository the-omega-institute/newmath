Following Bishop and Bridges and Troelstra--van Dalen, the $\DedekindCauchyComparisonUp$ packet records the finite comparison boundary between rounded located cut observations and regular Cauchy name observations. It reads the cut surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dedekindcut-namecert}, the Cauchy equivalence surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchysequenceequivalence-namecert}, the regular rational name surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the finite stream-window surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet is a representation comparison certificate, not a Dedekind construction of $\RealUp$ and not a completeness theorem.

\begin{definition}[DedekindCauchyComparison carrier]
\label{def:dedekind-cauchy-comparison-carrier}
A $\DedekindCauchyComparisonUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(D,R,W,T,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the $\DedekindCutUp$ row, carrying lower and upper rational-observation data together with roundedness, locatedness, and disjointness evidence;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ row, carrying the regular rational sequence surface that is allowed to answer finite tolerance requests;
\item $W$ is the family of finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows through which $R$ is read by downstream consumers;
\item $T$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger recording the dyadic radii, comparison budgets, and finite refinement rows used to compare $D$ with $R$;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal boundary row reached from the displayed cut and regular-sequence observations, with no independent completed-real coordinate;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $D,R,W,T,E$;
\item $C$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ replay routes for rereading the same finite cut observations, stream windows, tolerance rows, and seal boundary rows;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\DedekindCauchyComparisonUp$, $\DedekindCutUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DedekindCauchyComparisonUp}$ evidence.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a quotient cut, a host real equality proof, a selected limit, countable choice, a construction of $\RealUp$ from Dedekind cuts, or an ambient completeness principle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[DedekindCauchyComparison NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:dedekind-cauchy-comparison-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\DedekindCauchyComparisonUp}$ surface for the carrier of \autoref{def:dedekind-cauchy-comparison-carrier} consists of five finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission: the accepted object is exactly the packet $K=(D,R,W,T,E,H,C,P,N)$ with its displayed cut row, regular sequence row, stream windows, dyadic tolerance ledger, seal boundary, transports, continuations, package provenance, and local naming evidence;
\item classifier: comparison reads only rational cut observations in $D$, finite regular-sequence observations in $R$, the windows $W$, and the dyadic budgets $T$;
\item transport stability: $H$ and $C$ preserve the same cut observations, sequence windows, tolerance rows, and seal boundary under accepted consumer motion;
\item exactness: every comparison-facing read is supplied by the displayed rows $D,R,W,T,E$ after the componentwise transport and replay routes are fixed;
\item ledger: every downstream $\RealUp$ consumer first reads the comparison packet through $D,R,W,T,E,H,C,P,N$ and receives no hidden completion coordinate.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from the packet $K=(D,R,W,T,E,H,C,P,N)$. The displayed cut row, regular sequence row, stream-window family, dyadic tolerance ledger, seal boundary, transports, continuations, provenance, and local naming evidence are exactly the coordinates named by the carrier.

The classifier is finite because every comparison request is routed through the stored rational observations in $D$, the finite windows $W$ of the regular sequence row $R$, and the dyadic tolerance ledger $T$. No classifier step can inspect a quotient cut or an unbounded real object.

Transport stability uses $H$ componentwise and then replays the same consumer path through $C$. The replay returns to the stored rows $D,R,W,T,E$ and therefore preserves the same finite observation surface.

Exactness and the ledger clause follow from the same inventory. Since the carrier contains no selected limit, host real equality proof, countable-choice schedule, Dedekind construction row for $\RealUp$, or completeness principle, every downstream comparison is exhausted by the displayed packet rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DedekindCauchyComparison Real boundary]
\label{thm:dedekind-cauchy-comparison-real-boundary}
Every $\RealUp$-facing read exported by an accepted $\DedekindCauchyComparisonUp$ carrier is only a representation boundary between the displayed $\DedekindCutUp$ row and the displayed $\RegSeqRatUp$ row. The boundary supplies no quotient cut, no host real equality, no selected limit, no countable choice, no Dedekind construction of $\RealUp$, and no completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. Its real-facing coordinate is the seal boundary row $E$, and $E$ is stored together with the cut row $D$, regular rational row $R$, finite stream-window family $W$, and dyadic tolerance ledger $T$.

Read a comparison request in finite order. The consumer first exposes rational cut observations from $D$, then reads the corresponding finite windows $W$ of $R$, and then checks the dyadic budgets recorded in $T$.

Only after those rows have been fixed can the consumer read the seal boundary $E$. The transport and replay rows $H$ and $C$ preserve that same route, while $P$ and $N$ record provenance and local naming.

Thus the packet exports a comparison boundary only. A quotient cut, host real equality proof, selected limit, countable-choice schedule, Dedekind construction of $\RealUp$, and completeness theorem have no carrier coordinate and cannot be read from the certificate.
\end{proof}
