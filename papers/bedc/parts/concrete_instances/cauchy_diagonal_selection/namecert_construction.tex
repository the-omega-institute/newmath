Following Bishop and Bridges on constructive diagonal arguments for Cauchy families, the $\CauchyDiagonalSelectionUp$ packet records the finite row that chooses a diagonal observation window from displayed regular-Cauchy data before any Real or Completion consumer reads a seal. It sits after the finite observation schedule of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance core of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, and the Bishop Cauchy-modulus extraction route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bishop-cauchy-modulus-extraction-namecert}. The packet supplies a named L10 diagonal-selection handoff for Real and Completion routes; it does not assert a host sequence selector, quotient stream equality, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[Cauchy diagonal selection carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-diagonal-selection-carrier}
A $\CauchyDiagonalSelectionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
D=(S,R,M,W,T,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ source schedule, exposing the finite family of observation windows available to the diagonal request.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular-rational readback row attached to the displayed stream windows.
\item $M$ is the Cauchy-modulus extraction row, read through $\BishopCauchyModulusExtractionUp$ as the finite threshold discipline for the request.
\item $W$ is the diagonal-window row, selecting the single displayed window whose source index and precision index have both passed through $S,R,M$.
\item $T$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, recording the comparison between the requested tolerance, the extracted threshold, and the chosen diagonal window.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal reached only after the regular readback, modulus row, diagonal window, and dyadic ledger have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the stream schedule, regular readback, modulus extraction, diagonal window, tolerance ledger, Real seal, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from the tolerance request through $S,R,M,W,T,E$ and then through the structural rows.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyDiagonalSelectionUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\BishopCauchyModulusExtractionUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyDiagonalSelectionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $S,R,M,W,T,E,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for an external diagonal selector, quotient stream equality, hidden subsequence choice, countable choice, trichotomy, host real equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy diagonal selection NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-diagonal-selection-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CauchyDiagonalSelectionUp$ carrier $D=(S,R,M,W,T,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyDiagonalSelectionUp}$ surface consists exactly of five finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item diagonal-window selection, requiring the selected row $W$ to display both the stream source window from $S$ and the regular readback from $R$ before the diagonal observation is admitted;
\item regular-Cauchy preservation, requiring $R$ and $M$ to keep the readback inside the same regular Cauchy family under the extracted threshold;
\item dyadic tolerance control, requiring $T$ to compare the requested tolerance with the modulus threshold and the selected diagonal window;
\item Real-seal handoff, requiring $E$ to be downstream of $S,R,M,W,T$ and to export only the displayed diagonal observation;
\item ledger non-escape, requiring $H,C,P,N$ to transport, replay, source, and name only the displayed packet, with no external selector, quotient stream equality, hidden subsequence choice, countable choice, trichotomy, host equality, or ambient completeness coordinate.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-diagonal-selection-carrier}. The row $W$ is meaningful only after the stream schedule and regular readback rows have been displayed, so the diagonal observation has no source outside $S$ and $R$.

The modulus row $M$ supplies the finite threshold discipline consumed by the regular-Cauchy read. Thus the readback remains inside the same displayed family rather than becoming a selected subsequence or a host-level limit.

The tolerance ledger $T$ checks the requested dyadic scale against the extracted threshold and the chosen diagonal window. Only after that finite comparison can the Real-facing row $E$ be consumed.

Finally, $H,C,P,N$ preserve and name the same coordinates. Since none of the refused objects appears in the carrier, the five displayed obligations exhaust the local certificate surface.
\end{proof}
