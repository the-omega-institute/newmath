Following the classical Banach-algebra spectrum tradition of Gelfand theory, the $\RealSpectrumUp$ packet records the finite BEDC surface by which a real Banach-algebra element is tested against displayed real scalar windows, resolvent-boundary rows, and spectral-radius estimates without importing a host spectrum. It is a spectral neighbour of the L10 real and completion frontier: it reads the complete normed algebra surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-banachalgebra-namecert}, the power-growth boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-spectral-radius-namecert}, the bounded-operator route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-boundedlinearoperator-namecert}, the real seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the regular-rational classifier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the finite stream windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}.

\begin{definition}[Real spectrum carrier]
\label{def:real-spectrum-carrier}
A $\RealSpectrumUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(A,a,U,R,B,W,Q,E,G,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ is the $\BanachAlgebraUp$ source row carrying multiplication, norm, completion, product control, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming.
\item $a$ is the carried algebra element whose real spectral behaviour is inspected.
\item $U$ is the $\BoundedLinearOperatorUp$ or algebra-action row used when the element is read through a bounded operator surface.
\item $R$ is the $\RealUp$ scalar seal row. It admits only displayed real scalars, not a completed host scalar field.
\item $B$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row for real scalar windows. It records the regular rational classifier data used to compare a scalar probe with the element.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ spectral-window observation row. It names the finite list of scalar probes, operator-power requests, and resolvent-boundary tests that are actually displayed.
\item $Q$ is the resolvent-boundary test row. For each displayed scalar window it records the local multiplication and inverse-check surface, together with a refusal row when the inverse evidence is not displayed.
\item $E$ is the $\SpectralRadiusUp$ estimate row. It reads only the power-window and norm-growth estimates supplied by the spectral-radius packet.
\item $G$ is the real-spectrum readback row. It classifies a displayed scalar as admitted, refused, or boundary-facing only through $R,B,W,Q,E$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the Banach-algebra source, carried element, bounded-operator row, real seal, regular-rational readback, stream windows, resolvent tests, spectral-radius estimates, readback, and structural rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the route
$$
\BanachAlgebraUp,\BoundedLinearOperatorUp,\StreamNameUp,\RegSeqRatUp,\RealUp,\SpectralRadiusUp
\longmapsto \RealSpectrumUp .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RealSpectrumUp$, $\BanachAlgebraUp$, $\SpectralRadiusUp$, $\BoundedLinearOperatorUp$, $\RealUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RealSpectrumUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate admits exactly these rows. Its classifier compares accepted packets only through $A,a,U,R,B,W,Q,E,G,H,C,P,N$ and has no coordinate for a host spectrum, maximal ideals, Gelfand transform, spectral theorem, quotient completion, selected approximate eigenvectors, trichotomy over real scalars, or countable choice.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Real spectrum NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:real-spectrum-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\RealSpectrumUp$ carrier
$$
S=(A,a,U,R,B,W,Q,E,G,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\RealSpectrumUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the Banach-algebra source, carried element, bounded-operator read, real seal, regular-rational readback, stream-window observations, resolvent-boundary tests, spectral-radius estimates, readback, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item scalar-window compatibility, requiring every scalar probe in $W$ to read its $\RealUp$ seal through $R$ and its $\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier through $B$ before the probe can enter a resolvent test;
\item resolvent-boundary locality, requiring $Q$ to display the multiplication and inverse-check rows used by each accepted scalar test and to record refusal when those rows are absent;
\item spectral-radius estimate discipline, requiring $E$ to use only the power-window and norm-growth rows already certified by $\SpectralRadiusUp$;
\item classifier transport, requiring $H$ and $C$ to preserve the scalar windows, resolvent tests, radius estimates, real seal, regular-rational readback, bounded-operator row, provenance, and local naming componentwise.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows. In particular, the packet exports no completed host spectrum, no maximal ideal space, no Gelfand-transform equality, no spectral theorem, no quotient completion, no selected approximate eigenvector, no trichotomy row, and no countable-choice schedule.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:real-spectrum-carrier}. The accepted packet exposes exactly the Banach-algebra source, carried element, bounded-operator read, real seal, regular-rational classifier, stream-window observations, resolvent tests, spectral-radius estimates, readback, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

Scalar-window compatibility uses $R,B,W$. Each scalar probe is first a displayed stream-window request, then a regular-rational readback, and only then a real-seal read. Thus a scalar cannot enter the spectrum packet as an ambient real number detached from the displayed L10 rows.

Resolvent locality is the row $Q$. It names the local multiplication and inverse-check evidence used by each displayed test, and it records refusal when the evidence is absent. The row therefore supplies a boundary test rather than a host inverse or a completed spectrum.

The radius estimate discipline is the row $E$. It imports only the finite power-window and norm-growth surface already exposed by $\SpectralRadiusUp$, so no limit over all powers or selected approximate eigenvector is introduced.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name only the listed coordinates. Since the excluded spectral and choice-theoretic objects have no coordinate in the carrier, they cannot appear as local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}
