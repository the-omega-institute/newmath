\begin{definition}[GroundCompiler audit verdict carrier]
\label{def:groundcompiler-audit-verdict-carrier}
A $\GroundCompilerAuditVerdictUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
G=(M,Q,F,V,A,R,C,B,H,K,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the GroundCompiler module row naming the inspected event-flow, channel, recognizer, certificate-gate, metric, motif, bootstrap, or main-theorem surface;
\item $Q$ is the displayed query-output row for the inspected module surface;
\item $F$ is the forbidden-dependency row naming dependencies and host-side evidence forms excluded by the audit;
\item $V$ is the verdict row recording the finite comparison between $Q$ and $F$;
\item $A$ is the audit-family alignment row tying the verdict to the audit-map obstruction and frontier classifications;
\item $R$ is the generated-recognizer row exposed by the GroundCompiler layer;
\item $C$ is the certificate-gate row through which accepted exports are mediated;
\item $B$ is the no-hidden-input boundary row for code, reports, motifs, classifier quotients, source substrings, and host compiler structure;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for module, query, forbidden, verdict, alignment, recognizer, gate, boundary, provenance, and naming rows;
\item $K$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay routes for query exposure, forbidden-row comparison, verdict exposure, recognizer readback, certificate-gate readback, and boundary readback;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over the GroundCompiler audit map, sibling audit-map packets, recognizer rows, certificate-gate rows, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and local $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\GroundCompilerAuditVerdictUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exports no proof from theorem code, no certificate from a metric or motif report, no raw event-flow equality from a classifier quotient, and no hidden compiler input outside the displayed rows.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[GroundCompiler audit verdict obligations]
\label{thm:groundcompiler-audit-verdict-obligations}
For every $\GroundCompilerAuditVerdictUp$ carrier
$$
G=(M,Q,F,V,A,R,C,B,H,K,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\GroundCompilerAuditVerdictUp}$ surface has five obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier exposure: every public read first projects one of $M,Q,F,V,A,R,C,B,H,K,P,N$;
\item forbidden-row comparison: the verdict is read from the displayed comparison between $Q$ and $F$;
\item verdict determinacy: transport of $Q$ and $F$ transports the same verdict row $V$ and creates no second clean or refusal row;
\item replay provenance: every replay passes through the displayed continuation row $K$ and package provenance row $P$;
\item non-escape: recognizer, certificate-gate, and no-hidden-input boundaries are exported only through $R,C,B$ and the local naming row $N$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First project the carrier. The finite tuple displays the module, query, forbidden-dependency, verdict, alignment, recognizer, certificate-gate, boundary, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

The forbidden-row comparison is local to $Q,F,V$. The query-output row $Q$ is paired with the forbidden row $F$, and the verdict row $V$ records that finite comparison. Because the carrier has no second verdict coordinate, componentwise transport of $Q$ and $F$ through $H$ transports the same verdict.

Replay is confined to $K$ and $P$. A consumer may rerun the displayed query exposure, comparison, verdict exposure, recognizer readback, gate readback, or boundary readback only along the continuation routes recorded in $K$, with package ancestry recorded in $P$.

Finally inspect the GroundCompiler-specific rows $R,C,B$. They expose generated recognizers, certificate gates, and no-hidden-input boundaries as finite rows, not as host-side audit prose. The local $\NameCert$ row $N$ names exactly this packet surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GroundCompiler audit verdict non-escape]
\label{thm:groundcompiler-audit-verdict-nonescape}
Every accepted $\GroundCompilerAuditVerdictUp$ packet exports GroundCompiler audit evidence only through the finite rows
$$
M,Q,F,V,A,R,C,B,H,K,P,N.
$$
It exports no hidden dependency query, no unrecorded verdict row, no recognizer outside the generated-recognizer row, no certificate outside the certificate-gate row, no proof from code or reports, no raw event-flow equality from a classifier quotient, and no host compiler input beyond the no-hidden-input boundary row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet as $G=(M,Q,F,V,A,R,C,B,H,K,P,N)$. These coordinates are the whole exported $\BHist$ surface.

The audit decision is exhausted by $Q,F,V,A$. The query output, forbidden row, verdict row, and audit-family alignment row are all displayed before a consumer can treat the packet as evidence.

The compiler-facing evidence is exhausted by $R,C,B$. Recognizers are read through the generated-recognizer row, accepted exports through the certificate-gate row, and exclusions through the no-hidden-input boundary row.

Transport, replay, provenance, and naming are exhausted by $H,K,P,N$. Since no remaining coordinate stores hidden query data, an alternate verdict, host proof evidence, raw classifier equality, or compiler input outside the boundary, none can escape the packet.
\end{proof}
