\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\MontelSpaceUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-montelspace-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bourbaki and Schaefer, the $\MontelSpaceUp$ packet records the traditional locally convex and Frechet-style bounded-set compactness surface as finite BEDC NameCert data. It is the carrier companion to the normal-family packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-montel-theorem-namecert}: the Montel theorem route may consume a displayed Montel-space source when local boundedness and compact-window extraction are read from a locally convex carrier rather than from a holomorphic family alone. The packet also sits over the complete separable metric boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-complete-separable-metric-namecert}, the complete-metric and Polish readback surfaces, and the finite bornological rows used by bounded-set consumers. It names a finite bornological compactness route, not host topological vector space equality, barrelledness, nuclearity, selected compact closures, quotient completions, or classical choice.

\begin{definition}[Montel space carrier]
\label{def:montel-space-carrier}
A $\MontelSpaceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(L,F,B,Q,K,R,D,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $L$ is the locally convex source row, exposing the finite seminorm or entourage windows through which bounded sets are inspected.
\item $F$ is the Frechet-style completeness and metrizability row, read through complete metric, complete separable metric, or Polish-space surfaces when those rows are displayed.
\item $B$ is the bounded-set ledger, recording the finite family of bounded-set requests that the Montel-space consumer may inspect.
\item $Q$ is the bornological compactness row: each displayed bounded request in $B$ is routed to a compact, totally bounded, or sequentially compact readback already named by the surrounding metric packets.
\item $K$ is the compact-window row consumed by normal-family, Arzela--Ascoli, and Montel theorem packets.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular-rational readback for seminorm and metric tolerances.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger used by boundedness, compact-window, and completion reads.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the displayed locally convex, Frechet, bounded, compactness, tolerance, replay, provenance, and naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from locally convex inspection through bounded-set admission, compact-window readback, and normal-family consumption.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\MontelSpaceUp$, $\LocallyConvexSpaceUp$, $\FrechetSpaceUp$, $\CompleteSeparableMetricUp$, $\PolishSpaceUp$, $\MontelTheoremUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MontelSpaceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $L,F,B,Q,K,R,D,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for a host locally convex equality, a quotient completion, a selected compact closure, a barrelled or nuclear structure, countable choice, or open-cover compactness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Montel space NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:montel-space-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\MontelSpaceUp$ carrier $S=(L,F,B,Q,K,R,D,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\MontelSpaceUp}$ surface consists exactly of locally convex source admission, Frechet or complete-separable readback when displayed, bounded-set ledger admission, bornological compactness readback, compact-window export, regular-rational and dyadic tolerance discipline, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:montel-space-carrier}. The displayed tuple exposes the locally convex source row, Frechet or complete-separable readback row, bounded-set ledger, bornological compactness row, compact-window row, tolerance rows, and the structural rows.

The bounded-set read is finite. Each request in $B$ is inspected only through the locally convex windows in $L$ and the tolerance rows $R,D$, and the compactness answer is recorded in $Q$ before any compact-window consumer sees $K$.

The replay row $C$ then routes the same data toward normal-family and completion-facing consumers. Componentwise transport through $H$ preserves the displayed rows, while $P,N$ only source and name the packet.

Since the carrier has no coordinate for host locally convex equality, quotient completion, selected compact closures, countable choice, barrelledness, nuclearity, or open-cover compactness, the listed rows exhaust the certificate obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Montel space bornological compactness route]
\label{thm:montel-space-bornological-compactness-route}
Every bounded-set compactness consumer of an accepted $\MontelSpaceUp$ carrier factors through the finite route
$$
\text{locally convex window}
\longmapsto
\text{bounded-set ledger}
\longmapsto
\text{bornological compactness readback}
\longmapsto
\text{compact-window export}.
$$
In particular, a $\MontelTheoremUp$ or complete-separable metric consumer receives only the displayed rows $L,F,B,Q,K,R,D,H,C,P,N$ and receives no selected compact closure, quotient completion equality, host barrelledness theorem, nuclearity assertion, countable-choice schedule, or open-cover compactness proof.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the accepted carrier. The locally convex row $L$ supplies the only inspection windows, and the Frechet or complete-separable row $F$ is read only when the packet displays the corresponding metric boundary.

Next consume the bounded-set ledger $B$ together with $R,D$. This gives the finite tolerance discipline for every bounded request and prevents a consumer from importing an ambient bornology or choice-selected family.

The compactness readback $Q$ and compact-window row $K$ are then replayed by $C$ toward Montel theorem and completion-facing consumers. The route remains attached to the same rows under $H$, and $P,N$ add only provenance and local naming.

Thus every bornological compactness handoff factors through the displayed packet and cannot export host locally convex equality, selected compact closures, quotient completion equality, barrelledness, nuclearity, countable choice, or open-cover compactness.
\end{proof}
