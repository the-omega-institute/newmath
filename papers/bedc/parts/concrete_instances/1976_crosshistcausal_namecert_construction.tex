\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CrossHistCausalUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-crosshistcausal-namecert}
\origin{ai}

The $\CrossHistCausalUp$ interface records the first two-history causal-dependence packet in the inter-Hist chain of \autoref{rem:next-dependency-chain}. Its purpose is local: a consumer may inspect two displayed history rows, the unary slot ledger, the finite dependence witness row, componentwise stability data, read routes, package provenance, and the local naming row, but it receives no global frame, no synchronized clock, and no ambient geometry.

This local row is the two-history dependency surface later consumed by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-crosshistcausalrate-namecert}. In that route, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observerhistidentity-namecert} supplies the observer slots and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuationtrace-namecert} supplies the finite replay surface, while $\CrossHistCausalUp$ supplies the dependence witness row $R$ and unary slot ledger $K$ that the causal-rate packet reads before imposing its maximum-rate boundary.

\begin{definition}[CrossHistCausal carrier]
\label{def:cross-hist-causal-carrier}
A $\CrossHistCausalUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
X=(A,B,K,R,H,Q,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ and $B$ are the two displayed $\Hist$ rows whose relation is being named;
\item $K$ is the unary slot ledger marking the finite time slot at which the dependence read is evaluated;
\item $R$ is the finite dependence witness row, recording the displayed source-side and target-side observations used by the cross-Hist read;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ comparisons for $A$, $B$, $K$, $R$, the probe-stability row, the continuation row, the package row, and the local naming row;
\item $Q$ records $\psame$ probe-stability at both anchors, so the same dependence witness remains visible after probe-equivalent source and target reads;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ read routes from $A$ and $B$ into $K$, $R$, $H$, $Q$, $P$, and $N$;
\item $P$ is the package provenance naming $\mathsf{CrossHistCausal}$, $\Hist$, $\Unary$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\psame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CrossHistCausalUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier is a finite two-history naming certificate for cross-Hist causal dependence. It contains no global observer frame, no total ordering of all histories, no metric structure, no maximum-rate function, and no selected synchronization map between $A$ and $B$.
\end{definition}
\leandef{BEDC.Derived.InterHist.CrossHistCausal}

\begin{theorem}[CrossHistCausal NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cross-hist-causal-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:cross-hist-causal-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\CrossHistCausalUp}$ surface consists of five finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed packet $X=(A,B,K,R,H,Q,C,P,N)$;
\item slot locality, which requires every causal-dependence read to pass through the unary slot ledger $K$;
\item witness exactness, which requires the finite dependence row $R$ to display the source and target observations consumed by the read;
\item transport stability, which preserves the carrier under the componentwise $\hsame$ row $H$, the two-anchor $\psame$ row $Q$, and the displayed $\Cont$ routes in $C$;
\item non-escape, which excludes global frames, synchronized clocks, metric data, maximum-rate data, and ambient geometry.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CrossHistCausalUp.CrossHistCausalUp\_taste\_gate\_boundary}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is the projection of the finite tuple in \autoref{def:cross-hist-causal-carrier}. The rows $A,B,K,R,H,Q,C,P,N$ display the two histories, slot ledger, dependence witness, stability rows, read routes, package provenance, and local naming row.

Slot locality follows because the only slot coordinate is $K$. Every dependence read named by the carrier reaches the witness row through that unary ledger and cannot substitute a hidden global clock.

Witness exactness is read from $R$ together with the two anchors $A$ and $B$. The source-side and target-side observations used by the causal read are rows of the same finite packet, so the dependence witness is not imported from an ambient multi-Hist object.

Transport stability is componentwise. The $\hsame$ row $H$ transports the visible carrier rows, $Q$ records $\psame$ probe-stability at both anchors, and $C$ replays the displayed $\Cont$ routes after transport.

The non-escape clause follows from the carrier inventory. Since the packet has no coordinate for a global frame, synchronized clock, metric tensor, maximum-rate function, or ambient geometry, no local $\NameCert_{\CrossHistCausalUp}$ projection can export one.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CrossHistCausal hsame and psame stability]
\label{thm:cross-hist-causal-hsame-psame-stability}
Let $X=(A,B,K,R,H,Q,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CrossHistCausalUp$ carrier. Any transported read preserving the componentwise $\hsame$ row $H$ and the two-anchor $\psame$ row $Q$ preserves the same finite dependence witness $R$ at the same unary slot ledger $K$, with all consumer access replayed through the displayed $\Cont$ routes in $C$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the stability coordinates $H$ and $Q$ from the carrier. The row $H$ compares the two history anchors, the unary slot ledger, the dependence witness, the route row, the package row, and the local naming row componentwise.

The row $Q$ records probe-stability at both anchors. Thus a transported source read at $A$ and a transported target read at $B$ still inspect the same displayed dependence witness row rather than a witness chosen outside the packet.

Finally replay the routes in $C$. Because every consumer path to $K$ and $R$ is one of the displayed continuations, the transported read preserves the same slot-local dependence packet and adds no global synchronization or metric coordinate.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CrossHistCausalUp.CrossHistCausalUp\_slot\_witness\_route\_transport}

\begin{theorem}[CrossHistCausal slot locality]
\label{thm:cross-hist-causal-slot-locality}
For an accepted $\CrossHistCausalUp$ carrier
$$
X=(A,B,K,R,H,Q,C,P,N),
$$
every causal-dependence read of $R$ is local to the displayed unary slot ledger $K$. The read may use the componentwise $\hsame$ row $H$, the two-anchor $\psame$ row $Q$, and the displayed $\Cont$ routes in $C$, but it may not introduce a global frame, synchronized clock, metric datum, or ambient geometry.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:cross-hist-causal-namecert-obligations}. Its slot-locality clause states that every causal-dependence read passes through $K$.

Apply \autoref{thm:cross-hist-causal-hsame-psame-stability}. Transported reads preserve the same finite witness $R$ at the same unary slot ledger after using $H$, $Q$, and $C$.

The checked TasteGate marker attached to \autoref{thm:cross-hist-causal-namecert-obligations} supports this boundary as an admitted taste-gate surface. Since the carrier rows are only $A,B,K,R,H,Q,C,P,N$, there is no projection target for global frames, synchronized clocks, metric data, or ambient geometry.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CrossHistCausal witness exactness]
\label{thm:cross-hist-causal-witness-exactness}
For an accepted $\CrossHistCausalUp$ carrier $X=(A,B,K,R,H,Q,C,P,N)$, the dependence witness consumed by any cross-Hist read is exactly the displayed row $R$ together with the two history anchors $A,B$ and the slot ledger $K$. Componentwise $\hsame$ and two-anchor $\psame$ transport preserve that same witness; they do not replace it by a witness chosen from a multi-Hist frame, an external rate structure, or an ambient geometric model.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CrossHistCausalUp.CrossHistCausalUp\_witness\_exactness}
\begin{proof}
Use the witness-exactness clause of \autoref{thm:cross-hist-causal-namecert-obligations}. It requires the finite dependence row $R$ to display the source-side and target-side observations consumed by the read.

Use \autoref{thm:cross-hist-causal-hsame-psame-stability} for transport. The $\hsame$ row preserves the carrier coordinates componentwise, while the $\psame$ row preserves probe-stability at both anchors.

The checked TasteGate marker at the obligation theorem records that this finite boundary is the supported local surface. Because $X$ contains no multi-Hist frame, rate row, synchronized clock, metric row, or ambient geometry coordinate, the witness remains exactly $R$ at $A,B,K$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CrossHistCausal non-escape]
\label{thm:cross-hist-causal-non-escape}
Every consumer of an accepted $\CrossHistCausalUp$ carrier factors through the finite rows
$$
A,B,K,R,H,Q,C,P,N.
$$
In particular, the consumer may read the two history anchors, unary slot ledger, finite dependence witness, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, two-anchor $\psame$ stability, displayed $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert_{\CrossHistCausalUp}$ row, and nothing outside those rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier and apply \autoref{thm:cross-hist-causal-namecert-obligations}. The non-escape clause there excludes global frames, synchronized clocks, metric data, maximum-rate data, and ambient geometry.

Apply \autoref{thm:cross-hist-causal-hsame-psame-stability}. Any transported consumer read is still routed through $H,Q,C$ and preserves the same slot-local dependence witness.

The checked TasteGate marker associated with the obligation theorem supplies the recognized gate boundary for promoting this carrier past a mere seed reading. Since every visible consumer row is one of $A,B,K,R,H,Q,C,P,N$, no consumer can extract data outside the finite packet.
\end{proof}
