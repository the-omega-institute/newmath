\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\UpperSemicontinuousUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-uppersemicontinuous-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges constructive analysis, upper semicontinuity is
read through finite upper-bound observations and hypograph exclusion rows
rather than through a host topology or a completed real-valued function
space. The $\UpperSemicontinuousUp$ packet is the hypograph-side dual of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-lowersemicontinuous-namecert}: it consumes
the upper one-sided ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-upperreal-namecert},
the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert},
the scheduled finite windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert},
and the regular rational support rows of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}. It records only displayed
upper-readback evidence, not compact maxima, open-cover compactness,
trichotomy, quotient real equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[UpperSemicontinuous carrier]
\label{def:upper-semicontinuous-carrier}
An $\UpperSemicontinuousUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
U=(X,F,G,W,R,O,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the source carrier row whose accepted points are inspected only
  through displayed finite histories and classifier transport.
\item $F$ is the displayed graph or value-read row for the function being
  inspected.
\item $G$ is the hypograph ledger: each row records a finite source window,
  a rational threshold $q$, and the displayed evidence that the value row is
  admitted below the inspected upper bound.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule selecting the finite source and
  value windows used by each hypograph inspection.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row carrying the regular rational
  value windows used by $G$.
\item $O$ is the $\UpperRealUp$ comparison ledger, with the
  $\LowerSemicontinuousUp$ sibling retained only as the dual epigraph anchor
  for lower-bound readers.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after the scheduled stream
  windows, regular readbacks, hypograph ledger, and upper-real comparisons
  have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for source histories,
  value rows, hypograph rows, stream windows, regular readbacks, upper-real
  comparisons, Real seals, provenance, and local names.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from source admission through
  window selection, regular rational readback, hypograph inspection,
  upper-real comparison, and Real-seal handoff.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\UpperSemicontinuousUp$,
  $\LowerSemicontinuousUp$, $\UpperRealUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$,
  $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\UpperSemicontinuousUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier relates two accepted packets exactly when their source rows,
value rows, hypograph ledgers, scheduled stream windows, regular rational
readbacks, upper-real comparisons, Real seals, and provenance rows transport
componentwise along $H$ and replay through the same displayed routes in $C$.
No compact maximum, open-cover compactness datum, trichotomy branch, quotient
real equality test, or ambient completeness principle is a carrier coordinate.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[UpperSemicontinuous NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:upper-semicontinuous-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\UpperSemicontinuousUp$ carrier
$U=(X,F,G,W,R,O,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local
$\NameCert_{\UpperSemicontinuousUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following
finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed source, value, hypograph, schedule,
  readback, comparison, seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming
  rows;
\item hypograph-window exactness, requiring every upper-bound read to appear
  as a finite row in $G$ after the schedule in $W$ and readback in $R$ have
  been replayed;
\item upper-real classifier stability, requiring upper refinements and
  rational-threshold comparisons to be transported through the $\UpperRealUp$
  rows in $O$;
\item dual-boundary visibility, requiring every comparison with the
  $\LowerSemicontinuousUp$ side to pass through the displayed hypograph and
  upper-real ledgers rather than through a hidden epigraph completion;
\item $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ replay, requiring all source, value, hypograph,
  readback, comparison, and seal rows to move together under the displayed
  transport and continuation ledgers;
\item provenance and non-escape, requiring $P$ and $N$ to export only the
  named finite rows and no compact maximum, open-cover compactness,
  trichotomy branch, quotient real equality, countable-choice row, or
  $\RealUp$ completeness principle.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from
\autoref{def:upper-semicontinuous-carrier}. The accepted packet contains the
source row, value row, hypograph ledger, finite schedule, regular readback,
upper-real comparison ledger, Real seal, transport row, replay row, provenance
row, and local name.

Hypograph-window exactness is read from $G$ only after the requested source
window has passed through $W$ and the value readback through $R$. Thus an
upper-bound claim is not an external predicate; it is a displayed finite row
over the inspected source and rational threshold.

Upper-real classifier stability is carried by $O$ together with $H$. The
upper-real comparisons and rational-threshold refinements move componentwise
by $\hsame$, and the continuation row $C$ records the same inspection route
after transport.

The dual-boundary clause follows because the $\LowerSemicontinuousUp$ sibling
is only a named anchor in $P$ and $O$. It supplies the epigraph contrast needed
by lower-bound readers, but the accepted upper packet exports no hidden
epigraph completion or host topology.

Finally, $P$ and $N$ package these rows as the local certificate. Since the
carrier has no coordinate for compact maxima, open-cover compactness,
trichotomy, quotient real equality, countable choice, or $\RealUp$
completeness, no consumer can recover those principles from the packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[UpperSemicontinuous hypograph route]
\label{thm:upper-semicontinuous-hypograph-route}
Every $\UpperRealUp$ or $\RealUp$ consumer of an accepted
$\UpperSemicontinuousUp$ carrier factors through the finite hypograph route
$$
X,F,W,R,G,O,E,H,C,P,N .
$$
The consumer may inspect scheduled source windows, regular rational value
readbacks, displayed hypograph rows, upper-real refinements, componentwise
transports, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming rows. It cannot
bypass the hypograph ledger to read compact maxima, open-cover compactness,
trichotomy, quotient real equality, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$
completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a real-facing consumer of the accepted packet. The source row $X$
and value row $F$ are visible only through the finite schedule $W$, so every
value inspection begins with a displayed source window rather than an ambient
neighbourhood.

The regular rational readback row $R$ supplies the finite value window. The
hypograph ledger $G$ records the upper-bound row attached to that same
inspection, and the comparison ledger $O$ checks the corresponding
$\UpperRealUp$ refinement.

Transport and replay are exhausted by $H$ and $C$. They move the source,
value, hypograph, readback, comparison, and seal rows together, so a consumer
cannot keep the Real seal while discarding the displayed finite route.

The terminal rows $P$ and $N$ record only the named packet provenance and local
certificate. Since the carrier contains no compact maximum, open-cover
compactness, trichotomy branch, quotient equality test, choice row, or ambient
Real completeness field, the route above is the whole public boundary.
\end{proof}
