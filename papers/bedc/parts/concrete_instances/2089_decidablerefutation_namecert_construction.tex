\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\DecidableRefutationUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-decidablerefutation-namecert}
\origin{ai}

The $\DecidableRefutationUp$ surface turns the permitted and forbidden contradiction boundary of \autoref{ch:visions-reasoning-by-contradiction-boundary} into a finite naming packet. It records the proposition row being inspected, the constructive refutation witness, the optional decidability route, the exclusion bits for forbidden schemas, and the provenance rows through which a consumer may read the packet. The surface does not certify classical excluded middle, host truth, or a global decision procedure over naming certificates.

\begin{definition}[DecidableRefutation carrier]
\label{def:decidable-refutation-carrier}
A $\DecidableRefutationUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
R=(A,F,D,X,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ is the proposition row being inspected, represented as a certificate-visible predicate read over the current finite history;
\item $F$ is a permitted refutation witness, namely a displayed route from an assumed witness of $A$ to $\bot$ through $\Cont$, $\hsame$, $\msame$, and the local certificate rows;
\item $D$ is an optional decidability row for $A$, present only when the packet carries a constructive branch witness for the same proposition row;
\item $X$ records the exclusion bits for the forbidden schemas of \autoref{def:reasoning-forbidden-pattern}: unwitnessed existential extraction, double-negation elimination without $D$, and excluded-middle branching over the carrier of all naming certificates;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $A$, $F$, $D$, $X$, package rows, and local naming rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes for reading the proposition row, replaying the permitted refutation, consuming the optional decidability row, and checking the forbidden-schema exclusions;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, $\Ext$, $\Cont$, $\msame$, $\hsame$, the local proof rows, and the surrounding $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DecidableRefutationUp}$ ledger.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no host truth predicate, no global excluded-middle oracle, no selected witness for an existential obtained by contradiction, and no branch over the carrier of all naming certificates.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[DecidableRefutation classifier]
\label{def:decidable-refutation-classifier}
Two accepted $\DecidableRefutationUp$ carriers
$$
R=(A,F,D,X,H,C,P,N)
\quad\hbox{and}\quad
R'=(A',F',D',X',H',C',P',N')
$$
are classified together exactly when their visible proposition rows are matched by the displayed $\hsame$ transports, their permitted refutation witnesses replay through the displayed $\Cont$ routes, their optional decidability rows have the same displayed presence shape, and their forbidden-schema exclusion bits agree componentwise.

The classifier compares only $A,F,D,X,H,C,P,N$ and $A',F',D',X',H',C',P',N'$. It has no coordinate for ambient truth, no host-level proof search, no quotient over proofs, and no classifier over the carrier of all naming certificates.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[DecidableRefutation NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:decidable-refutation-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\DecidableRefutationUp}$ surface consists of five obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the finite packet $R=(A,F,D,X,H,C,P,N)$ of \autoref{def:decidable-refutation-carrier};
\item classifier soundness for \autoref{def:decidable-refutation-classifier}, comparing only the displayed proposition, refutation, optional decidability, exclusion, transport, continuation, package, and naming rows;
\item permitted-negation transport, saying that a refutation witness $F$ remains a proof of $\neg A$ under the stored $\hsame$ transports and displayed $\Cont$ routes;
\item decidable double-negation route, saying that $\neg\neg A$ may be consumed only when the row $D$ displays a constructive decision for the same proposition row $A$;
\item non-escape from certificate-indexed truth branches, saying that the packet cannot export excluded middle over all naming certificates, unwitnessed existential extraction, or double-negation elimination without the displayed decidability row.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:decidable-refutation-carrier}. The only available rows are the proposition row, permitted refutation witness, optional decidability row, exclusion ledger, transport rows, continuation routes, package provenance, and local naming ledger.

Classifier soundness follows from \autoref{def:decidable-refutation-classifier}. A comparison replays only the displayed proposition-row match, the refutation route, the optional decidability shape, and the exclusion bits through the stored $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ data.

For permitted-negation transport, read the refutation witness $F$ as a route from an assumed witness of $A$ to $\bot$. Transporting $A$ and $F$ along $H$ and replaying the route in $C$ yields the same certificate-level negation, because no step leaves the displayed ground-loop rows.

For the decidable double-negation route, the row $D$ is the only coordinate that supplies a constructive branch for $A$. If $D$ is absent, the exclusion ledger $X$ blocks the double-negation schema named in \autoref{def:reasoning-forbidden-pattern}; if $D$ is present, the branch is consumed inside the same finite packet.

Finally, the non-escape clause is exactly the absence of a certificate-indexed truth branch coordinate. The packet contains no row classifying all naming certificates, no witness selector for existential-by-contradiction, and no host truth predicate, so every consumer remains inside $A,F,D,X,H,C,P,N$.
\end{proof}
