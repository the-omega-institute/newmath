\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\MetacicNormalizationAuditUp$}

\origin{human}

The $\MetacicNormalizationAuditUp$ packet realizes the normalization audit map of \autoref{ch:metacic-normalization-audit-map} as finite BEDC naming data. It sits beside the confluence audit packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacic-confluence-audit-namecert} and the confluence audit witness of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacicconfluenceauditwitness-namecert}: those siblings record confluence-side boundary rows, while the present packet records the strong-normalization route, its refusal boundary, and the finite replay surface used by consistency consumers.

\begin{definition}[MetaCIC normalization audit carrier]
\label{def:metacic-normalization-audit-carrier}
A $\MetacicNormalizationAuditUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Z=(R,C,S,A,N,F,H,T,P,L)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $R$ is the reducibility-candidate row naming $\mathrm{Candidate}$, $\mathrm{ClosedTypedCandidate}$, $\mathrm{ClosedTypedPiCandidate}$, and $\mathrm{ClosedTypedCandidateSetMember}$ evidence;
\item $C$ is the checked closed-term projection row for candidate, closed-candidate, and candidate-set routes to $\mathrm{StrongNormalizable}(t)$;
\item $S$ is the finite strong-normalization scope row containing the checked candidate-to-SN, Pi adequacy, application adequacy, example SN, and no-infinite-reduction reads;
\item $A$ is the application-boundary row recording that componentwise SN of function and argument is not reread as unconditional application SN;
\item $N$ is the confluence-boundary refusal row separating this normalization packet from full closed Church--Rosser, substitution compatibility, and subject-reduction transport;
\item $F$ is the route replay row for manuscript citations that pass from the audit map to the closed-normal consistency consumer;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport across $R,C,S,A,N,F,P,L$;
\item $T$ records displayed $\Cont$ routes for replaying candidate, projection, scope, boundary, and consistency-consumer reads;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over the normalization audit map, the confluence audit sibling, the confluence audit witness sibling, and the closed-normal consistency assembly;
\item $L$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MetacicNormalizationAuditUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no row for global strong normalization of every closed typed MetaCIC term without candidate-mediated evidence, no unconditional application-SN theorem, no closed subject-reduction transport theorem, no arbitrary closed-term confluence theorem, no quotient equality over reductions, and no host evaluator.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC normalization audit obligation surface]
\label{thm:metacic-normalization-audit-obligation-surface}
Every accepted $\MetacicNormalizationAuditUp$ packet binds exactly the finite obligation surface displayed in \autoref{def:metacic-normalization-audit-carrier}: carrier admission for $Z=(R,C,S,A,N,F,H,T,P,L)$, candidate evidence visibility through $R$, closed-term projection visibility through $C$, finite SN scope visibility through $S$, application-boundary visibility through $A$, confluence and transport refusal visibility through $N$, route replay through $F$ and $T$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, provenance through $P$, and local naming through $L$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier coordinates. The positive normalization reads are $R,C,S$: a consumer first names candidate evidence, then reads the closed-term projection row, and only then reads the finite SN and no-infinite-reduction inventory.

The boundary rows $A$ and $N$ are part of the same packet. They keep application SN and confluence or subject-reduction transport from being inferred by erasing their required evidence.

The structural rows $F,H,T,P,L$ replay, transport, package, and name only those displayed reads. Since the carrier has no additional coordinate, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligation surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC normalization audit finite SN scope]
\label{thm:metacic-normalization-audit-sn-scope}
The strong-normalization content exported by an accepted $\MetacicNormalizationAuditUp$ packet is exactly the candidate-mediated scope stored in $R,C,S$: candidate-to-SN consequences, closed typed term SN from candidate, closed-candidate, or candidate-set evidence, Pi adequacy, application adequacy under candidate evidence, typed example SN facts, and the associated no-infinite-reduction consequences. It does not export a blanket theorem that every closed typed MetaCIC term is strongly normalizing without one of those displayed evidence rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read $R$ as the evidence row. It contains the candidate, closed-candidate, Pi-candidate, and candidate-set membership interfaces that the checked projection theorems consume.

Read $C$ and $S$ downstream from $R$. These rows record the closed typed term projections, adequacy facts, examples, and no-infinite-reduction consequences only along the candidate-mediated route.

The carrier contains no coordinate that constructs candidate evidence for every closed typed term. Therefore agreement on the packet preserves the finite SN scope while excluding the blanket evidence-free endpoint.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC normalization audit confluence boundary]
\label{thm:metacic-normalization-audit-confluence-boundary}
Agreement on the rows of an accepted $\MetacicNormalizationAuditUp$ packet preserves normalization-side reads, but it does not discharge the confluence or subject-reduction rows isolated by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacic-confluence-audit-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacicconfluenceauditwitness-namecert}. In particular, the packet exports no proof of full closed Church--Rosser, no proof of substitution compatibility, and no subject-reduction transport theorem for arbitrary closed proofs.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The provenance row $P$ places the packet beside the two confluence audit siblings, so a consumer can compare the three audit surfaces through finite NameCert data.

The normalization rows $R,C,S,A$ are separate from the refusal row $N$. Transport through $H$ and replay through $T$ preserve that separation instead of identifying normalization with confluence or subject reduction.

Thus the packet can feed the closed-normal branch of the consistency assembly through $F$ while leaving the confluence and subject-reduction boundaries in their own sibling packets.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC normalization audit ledger policy]
\label{thm:metacic-normalization-audit-ledger-policy}
Every manuscript route that cites the normalization audit map through $\MetacicNormalizationAuditUp$ factors through the ledger
$$
R,C,S,A,N,F,H,T,P,L.
$$
The route may replay candidate evidence, closed-term SN projections, finite no-infinite-reduction reads, confluence-boundary refusals, and local NameCert provenance, but it may not replace the packet by vision prose, a host-level termination oracle, or a hidden theorem outside the displayed rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a citation to the audit map. The finite carrier replaces that prose handle by displayed rows $R,C,S,A,N,F,H,T,P,L$.

Route replay is controlled by $F$ and $T$, while $H$ preserves componentwise agreement and $P,L$ preserve provenance and local naming. These rows are sufficient for every packet read named in the theorem.

Because the carrier has no coordinate for a host oracle, hidden theorem, quotient equality, or evidence-free global SN theorem, the ledger policy blocks any consumer from bypassing the finite packet.
\end{proof}
