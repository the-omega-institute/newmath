\begin{theorem}[Compact witness public surface bridge]\label{thm:compact-witness-public-surface-bridge}
The compact-net semantic certificate, the located and finite refinement chains,
the endpoint classifier rows, and the refinement-witness ledger determine the
conservative standard finite-net compact-witness schema for $\CompactUp$.
The public object consists of a unary center, a unary precision ledger, the
displayed answer net, the locatedness and finite-net refinement endpoints, and
the visible continuation evidence connecting those histories.

The bridge is conservative: it exposes only finite-net compact-witness data
already carried by the BEDC rows. It does not export open-cover compactness,
sequential compactness, product compactness, or function-space compactness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the compact-net semantic name certificate
\texttt{BEDC.Derived.CompactUp.CompactNetWitness\_semanticNameCert} as the
canonical certificate row. Its carrier reads a center, a precision, and a net
with the net obtained by the displayed continuation from the center and
precision, so the public finite-net witness has no coordinate outside the
BEDC histories.

Next apply the compact witness carrier and refinement-chain endpoint packages,
including \autoref{thm:compact-witness-carrier-located-finite-refinement-chain-closed},
\autoref{thm:compact-witness-carrier-located-finite-refinement-chain-final-endpoints-unary},
and \autoref{thm:compact-witness-carrier-located-finite-refinement-endpoint-package}.
They supply the located and finite refinement endpoints, their unary rows, and
the endpoint classifier equivalences used by the public witness schema.

The refinement-witness ledger is exactly the displayed continuation data in
those packages. Forgetting the BEDC wrapper yields the ordinary finite-net
compact-witness reading, while repacking the same rows returns the BEDC
certificate. Since every cited row is a finite history, classifier, or
continuation row, none of the excluded compactness principles has a carrier
slot in the bridge.
\end{proof}
