Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, the finite Lebesgue-number route used here is a displayed dyadic finite-cover modulus. The $\FiniteLebesgueNumberUp$ packet records a finite dyadic cover, a positive rational radius ledger, and the finite-window witnesses needed by total-bounded and compact-metric consumers. It is not an open-cover compactness principle and it does not choose a subcover from an arbitrary family.

The finite radius ledger is consumed by the compact-metric route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert} and is built over the explicit dyadic-cover surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadiccover-namecert}.

\begin{definition}[Finite Lebesgue-number carrier]
\label{def:finite-lebesgue-number-carrier}
A $\FiniteLebesgueNumberUp$ carrier over a displayed $\DyadicCoverUp$ row is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(D,W,\lambda,M,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the accepted $\DyadicCoverUp$ row containing the finite list of dyadic centers, rational ball rows, dyadic mesh cells, and finite coverage witnesses;
\item $W$ is the $\FiniteWindowEnvelopeUp$ row whose inspected points are covered by the dyadic cells of $D$;
\item $\lambda$ is the positive finite radius ledger, read through $\RationalBallUp$ and bounded by the radii recorded in $D$;
\item $M$ is the $\DyadicMeshUp$ mesh ledger that records which mesh cells refine into the $\lambda$-balls and into the cover cells of $D$;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $D$, $W$, $\lambda$, $M$, the $\StreamNameUp$ reads, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ approximation rows, and the $\RealUp$ consumer rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which consumers inspect finite-window membership, rational-ball containment, dyadic-mesh refinement, stream-name reads, regular-rational approximations, and real rows;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\DyadicCoverUp$, $\FiniteWindowEnvelopeUp$, $\DyadicMeshUp$, $\RationalBallUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and the local certificate rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteLebesgueNumberUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier accepts only packets whose positive finite radius ledger is displayed beside the finite dyadic cover and whose mesh witnesses keep every admitted finite-window read inside a named cover cell.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Finite Lebesgue-number NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\FiniteLebesgueNumberUp}$ surface consists of five finite rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier: the packet of \autoref{def:finite-lebesgue-number-carrier};
\item classifier: componentwise comparison of the dyadic cover row, finite-window row, positive radius ledger, mesh ledger, transports, continuations, package provenance, and local certificate row;
\item radius-modulus stability: displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ transport preserves the positive ledger $\lambda$ together with the $\RationalBallUp$ and $\DyadicMeshUp$ rows that witness its refinement into the finite cover;
\item finite-window coverage exactness: every accepted point read from $W$ factors through a $\StreamNameUp$ row, a $\RegSeqRatUp$ approximation row, a $\RealUp$ row, a mesh cell from $M$, and a named cover cell in $D$;
\item non-escape: accepted packets export no open-cover compactness theorem, no arbitrary subcover selector, no host compactness principle, and no hidden cover family outside the displayed dyadic finite-cover data.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier habitation is the displayed tuple $L=(D,W,\lambda,M,H,C,P,N)$. The dyadic cover, finite-window row, radius ledger, mesh ledger, transports, continuations, package provenance, and local certificate are all finite coordinates.

The classifier is componentwise on those coordinates. Radius-modulus stability follows by transporting the positive rational radius ledger and the mesh-refinement rows along $H$, then replaying only the routes stored in $C$.

Coverage exactness is a finite witness read. Each admitted point of $W$ is observed through its stream and regular-rational approximation rows, placed in a real row, assigned to a mesh cell of $M$, and then routed to a named cover cell in $D$.

The carrier has no coordinate for an arbitrary open family, an open-cover compactness theorem, or a subcover selector. Thus every accepted export remains inside the displayed dyadic finite-cover packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Finite Lebesgue-number dyadic cover handoff]
\label{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-dyadic-cover-handoff}
Every $\TotallyBoundedUp$ or $\CompactMetricUp$ consumer of an accepted $\FiniteLebesgueNumberUp$ carrier receives only the displayed $\DyadicCoverUp$ finite cover, the $\FiniteWindowEnvelopeUp$ coverage row, the positive finite radius ledger $\lambda$, the $\DyadicMeshUp$ refinement ledger $M$, and the listed $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ provenance rows. The handoff supplies a finite dyadic-cover modulus and a positive finite radius ledger, not open-cover compactness or subcover choice.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:finite-lebesgue-number-carrier}. The only consumer-facing cover data are $D$, $W$, $\lambda$, $M$, $H$, $C$, $P$, and $N$.

Use \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-namecert-obligations}. Its classifier, radius-modulus stability, and finite-window coverage exactness rows keep every consumer read on the displayed dyadic cover, finite-window witnesses, rational-ball radius ledger, and mesh-refinement ledger.

A total-bounded or compact-metric consumer therefore receives the finite dyadic cover together with the positive radius ledger and provenance rows. Since the carrier contains no arbitrary open family and no selected subcover coordinate, the handoff cannot assert open-cover compactness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Finite Lebesgue-number radius transport]
\label{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-radius-transport}
Let $L=(D,W,\lambda,M,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\FiniteLebesgueNumberUp$ carrier. For every componentwise $\hsame$ transport recorded in $H$, the transported packet preserves the positive finite radius ledger $\lambda$, the $\RationalBallUp$ radius readback attached to $\lambda$, and the $\DyadicMeshUp$ refinement rows in $M$ as one finite packet. In particular, a consumer cannot transport the radius coordinate without simultaneously transporting the rational-ball and mesh-refinement evidence by which the radius is read.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier of \autoref{def:finite-lebesgue-number-carrier}. The radius coordinate is not stored as an isolated scalar: it is bundled with the rational-ball readback, the mesh ledger, the componentwise transport rows, and the displayed continuation routes.

Use the radius-modulus stability row of \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-namecert-obligations}. That row transports $\lambda$ only together with the $\RationalBallUp$ and $\DyadicMeshUp$ rows witnessing refinement into the finite cover.

Therefore every transported radius read remains in the finite packet $D,W,\lambda,M,H,C,P,N$. No additional radius selector, host metric radius, or open-cover radius coordinate is exposed.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Finite Lebesgue-number finite-window coverage exactness]
\label{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-window-coverage-exactness}
For an accepted $\FiniteLebesgueNumberUp$ carrier $L=(D,W,\lambda,M,H,C,P,N)$, every accepted finite-window read factors through the displayed window $W$, then through a $\StreamNameUp$ row, a $\RegSeqRatUp$ approximation row, a $\RealUp$ row, a mesh cell of $M$, and a named cover cell of $D$. The factorization has no coordinate for an arbitrary open family, no host compactness witness, and no selected subcover outside the displayed dyadic finite-cover packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted read from $W$. By the finite-window coverage exactness row of \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-namecert-obligations}, the read is observed through a named stream row, its regular-rational approximation row, and the corresponding real row.

The same exactness row then routes the real read into a displayed mesh cell of $M$ and from that mesh cell into a named cover cell of $D$. These are coordinates of the carrier, so the factorization is finite and internal to $L$.

The non-escape row of \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-namecert-obligations} excludes open-family, compactness, and selected-subcover coordinates. Hence every accepted finite-window read is exhausted by the displayed route.
\end{proof}

The non-escape clauses in these three rows leave no coordinate for open-cover compactness, host compactness, or selected subcover data. Thus the compact consumer route is exactly the ordered carrier route.
