\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\DyadicEmbeddingUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicembedding-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following the Bishop--Bridges constructive-analysis treatment of rational and dyadic numbers inside real names, the $\DyadicEmbeddingUp$ interface records a constant dyadic real name as a finite route from a dyadic row to a constant stream, a stationary regular rational readback, and a real seal. The packet below exposes the embedding path without quotienting regular sequences, selecting a host real limit, invoking real completeness, or comparing completed real representatives by ambient equality.

\begin{definition}[DyadicEmbedding carrier]
\label{def:dyadic-embedding-carrier}
A $\DyadicEmbeddingUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
D=(d,S,R,Q,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $d$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ source row naming the dyadic rational observation;
\item $S$ is the constant $\StreamNameUp$ schedule whose every admitted read returns the same dyadic row $d$;
\item $R$ is the stationary $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback obtained from the constant stream schedule;
\item $Q$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached from the stationary regular readback;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the dyadic source, constant stream, stationary readback, real seal, package provenance, and local naming row;
\item $C$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ routes from the dyadic row to the constant stream, from the stream to the stationary regular readback, and from the readback to the real seal;
\item $P$ names $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\StationaryRationalDiagonalUp$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DyadicEmbeddingUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier is a finite naming certificate for constant dyadic real names. It contains no quotient stream, no selected limit, no host real equality test, and no completeness theorem for arbitrary regular sequences.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicEmbedding NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:dyadic-embedding-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:dyadic-embedding-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\DyadicEmbeddingUp}$ surface consists of five finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed packet $D=(d,S,R,Q,H,C,P,N)$;
\item classifier transport by componentwise $\hsame$ over the dyadic source, constant stream schedule, stationary readback, and real seal;
\item constant-stream readback, which requires every stream window to return the same $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ row before it is read as a $\RegSeqRatUp$ row;
\item real-seal handoff, which routes any $\RealUp$ consumer through the stationary readback $R$ and the package rows $P,N$;
\item non-escape, which excludes quotient regular streams, chosen limits, host equality of real representatives, trichotomy, and ambient completeness.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DyadicEmbeddingUp.DyadicEmbeddingCarrier\_namecert\_obligations}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is the direct projection of the tuple in \autoref{def:dyadic-embedding-carrier}. The coordinates name the dyadic source, stream schedule, regular readback, real seal, transports, routes, package provenance, and local certificate row in one finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet.

Classifier transport is componentwise. The rows $d,S,R,Q$ are compared only through the stored $\hsame$ entries in $H$, and each accepted route is replayed through the displayed $\Cont$ entries in $C$.

For the constant-stream and real-seal obligations, first read $d$ into the constant stream schedule $S$, then read the stationary regular row $R$ through the same schedule, and finally expose $Q$ only through $R$. This proves that all downstream real-name consumers receive the displayed finite route.

The non-escape clause follows from the carrier inventory. Since the tuple contains no coordinate for quotient streams, selected limits, host real equality, trichotomy, or completeness, no projected certificate route can export one.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicEmbedding constant stream readback]
\label{thm:dyadic-embedding-constant-stream-readback}
Every accepted $\DyadicEmbeddingUp$ carrier reads its $\StreamNameUp$ coordinate as a constant schedule over the same $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ row $d$. The resulting $\RegSeqRatUp$ row is the stationary rational diagonal named by $\StationaryRationalDiagonalUp$, and no separate rational stream or unrecorded convergence witness participates in the readback.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the dyadic source and stream coordinates from $D$. The route from $d$ to $S$ is displayed in $C$, so every admitted window of the stream schedule is read through the same source row.

The stationary readback is then the route from $S$ to $R$. Because $S$ has no coordinate other than the constant dyadic row and its finite window ledger, $R$ is the regular rational readback of that stationary schedule.

The package row $P$ names $\StationaryRationalDiagonalUp$ as the readback shape. Thus the readback is a named stationary diagonal over $d$, not a separate rational stream or a convergence object chosen outside the packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicEmbedding real seal handoff]
\label{thm:dyadic-embedding-real-seal-handoff}
Every $\RealUp$ consumer exported by a $\DyadicEmbeddingUp$ packet factors through the same dyadic source row $d$, constant $\StreamNameUp$ schedule $S$, and stationary $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback $R$ before it reaches the real seal $Q$. The seal is therefore a finite handoff from the dyadic row to a constant real name, not a proof of completeness for arbitrary regular sequences.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a consumer route to $Q$. The carrier definition records this route in $C$ as downstream of the regular readback coordinate $R$.

Apply \autoref{thm:dyadic-embedding-constant-stream-readback}. It identifies $R$ as the stationary rational diagonal read from the constant schedule $S$, and identifies $S$ as the schedule produced from the same dyadic row $d$.

Composing the displayed routes gives the handoff $d$ to $S$ to $R$ to $Q$. The packet has no row for arbitrary completeness or a selected limit, so the exported real seal is exactly the constant-name handoff.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicEmbedding ledger non-escape]
\label{thm:dyadic-embedding-ledger-non-escape}
The dependency ledger of a $\DyadicEmbeddingUp$ carrier is exhausted by $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\StationaryRationalDiagonalUp$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$. No consumer may add a quotient of streams, a selected real value, host equality of completed representatives, trichotomy, or a completeness principle as an implicit dependency.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the package coordinate $P$ and the local naming coordinate $N$. Together they name exactly the dependency list displayed in \autoref{def:dyadic-embedding-carrier}.

The readback theorem accounts for $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\StationaryRationalDiagonalUp$ through the rows $d,S,R$. The real-seal theorem accounts for the $\RealUp$ consumer through $Q$ and the same route inventory.

No further coordinate remains in $D$. Therefore any extra quotient, selected host value, equality test, trichotomy, or completeness theorem would have to be supplied from outside the accepted packet, which the local $\NameCert_{\DyadicEmbeddingUp}$ surface does not permit.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicEmbedding public scope]
\label{thm:dyadic-embedding-public-scope}
The public surface of a $\DyadicEmbeddingUp$ seed packet is precisely the finite constant-name route through $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\StationaryRationalDiagonalUp$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ named in \autoref{def:dyadic-embedding-carrier}. It exports constant dyadic real names and excludes quotient-real construction, arbitrary Cauchy completion, selected limits, host real equality, and real-order trichotomy.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the public rows of the carrier. They are exactly the dyadic source, constant stream schedule, stationary regular readback, real seal, componentwise transports, continuation routes, package provenance, and local naming row.

Use \autoref{thm:dyadic-embedding-namecert-obligations} for carrier admission and local classifier transport. Use \autoref{thm:dyadic-embedding-constant-stream-readback} for the stationary readback and \autoref{thm:dyadic-embedding-real-seal-handoff} for the real-name consumer route.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:dyadic-embedding-ledger-non-escape}. The ledger admits only the displayed dependency rows, so the public scope is exactly the constant dyadic real-name interface and none of the excluded completion or host-real structure.
\end{proof}
