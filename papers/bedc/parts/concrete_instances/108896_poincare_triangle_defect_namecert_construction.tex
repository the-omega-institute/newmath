\begin{theorem}[Poincare triangle defect NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:poincare-triangle-defect-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\PoincareTriangleDefectUp$ carrier $T=(D,G,M,X,R,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\PoincareTriangleDefectUp}$ obligations are exactly disk-point carrier admission, geodesic-side admission, hyperbolic-metric readout, cross-ratio comparison admission, $\RealUp$ defect sealing, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:poincare-triangle-defect-carrier}. The tuple displays disk points, geodesic sides, metric rows, cross-ratio rows, a real seal, and the structural rows.

The disk and geodesic rows determine the finite triangle surface. The metric and cross-ratio rows provide the only comparison data from which the defect read can be formed.

The real seal $R$ is downstream of those displayed geometric rows. The transport, replay, provenance, and local name preserve the same packet and introduce no ambient model.

Thus the listed rows exhaust the local certificate obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Poincare triangle defect transport stability]
\label{thm:poincare-triangle-defect-transport-stability}
Let $T=(D,G,M,X,R,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\PoincareTriangleDefectUp$ carrier. Replacing disk-point rows, geodesic-side rows, hyperbolic-metric rows, cross-ratio rows, and the $\RealUp$ seal by displayed componentwise $\hsame$ rows, and replaying the replacement through $C$, preserves the admissible triangle-defect route
$$
D\longmapsto G\longmapsto M\longmapsto X\longmapsto R .
$$
The transported packet exports no host hyperbolic plane, ambient angle measure, selected boundary representative, global field quotient, or unrecorded geometric equality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The accepted carrier has a single real-facing route: disk points are displayed first, geodesic sides are read from those points, the metric and cross-ratio rows compare the displayed sides, and the real seal records the defect read.

The row $H$ transports each displayed coordinate componentwise. The row $C$ then replays the same continuation from the transported disk and side rows through the transported metric, cross-ratio, and real-seal rows.

The provenance row $P$ and local name $N$ only source and name this route. Since the carrier contains no coordinate for an ambient model or selected boundary representative, the transported defect remains the same finite BEDC packet.
\end{proof}
