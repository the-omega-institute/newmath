$$

with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}

\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row attached to the same window;
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance and endpoint ledger used to compare the readback with the requested precision;

\begin{theorem}[Finite limit-seal envelope NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:finite-limit-seal-envelope-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:finite-limit-seal-envelope-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteLimitSealEnvelopeUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $E=(W,R,D,L,Q,H,C,P,N)$, finite StreamName window exposure through $W$, RegSeqRat readback through $R$, dyadic tolerance and endpoint exposure through $D$, CauchyLimitSeal handoff through $L$, terminal RealUp handoff through $Q$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, package provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier. The displayed packet lists one finite stream window, one regular-rational readback, one dyadic tolerance ledger, one completion-facing limit seal, one terminal RealUp handoff, and four structural support rows.

Read the data rows in order. The window $W$ supplies the finite observation surface, $R$ is the readback attached to that window, $D$ records the dyadic tolerance evidence for the same request, and $L$ records that these rows feed the CauchyLimitSeal route.

The terminal row $Q$ is then available only as the RealUp handoff reached through $W,R,D,L$. The rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name that same finite packet, so the listed obligations exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Finite limit-seal envelope non-choice boundary]
\label{thm:finite-limit-seal-envelope-nonchoice-boundary}
Every accepted $\FiniteLimitSealEnvelopeUp$ packet keeps the Real and Completion limit theorem on the displayed finite route
$$

It receives no quotient stream equality, host real equality, selected host limit, countable-choice selector, ambient completeness principle, or hidden Real equality rule.

\begin{proof}

Apply \autoref{thm:finite-limit-seal-envelope-namecert-obligations}. The local certificate exposes exactly the displayed rows and treats $H,C,P,N$ as support for the same packet, not as independent choice or equality data.

Inspect the excluded objects. Quotient stream equality, host real equality, selected host limits, countable-choice selectors, ambient completeness, and hidden Real equality rules would require coordinates outside $W,R,D,L,Q,H,C,P,N$. Since the carrier has no such coordinate, every accepted consumer remains on the finite route.

\begin{theorem}[Finite limit-seal envelope sibling dependency]
\label{thm:finite-limit-seal-envelope-sibling-dependency}
The $\FiniteLimitSealEnvelopeUp$ carrier depends on the joint availability of $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\CauchyLimitSealUp$, and $\RealUp$ rows. No one sibling among \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchylimitseal-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert} carries the whole tuple $W,R,D,L,Q,H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Compare the finite observation siblings first. The StreamName surface supplies window data, the RegSeqRat surface supplies regular-rational readback, and the DyadicRatCore surface supplies tolerance and endpoint evidence. None of these siblings by itself also carries the completion-facing limit seal and the terminal RealUp handoff.

Compare the seal-facing siblings next. The CauchyLimitSeal surface records the completion handoff, and the RealUp surface records the terminal real-name seal, but neither one includes the finite window, readback, and dyadic tolerance ledger as a single ordered carrier.

Thus the envelope is a composite packet. Its sibling dependence is visible in the carrier itself, and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve the combined route rather than reducing it to any one sibling surface.
