Following Kelley and Bourbaki on filters and completion, the $\FilterCompletionMonadUp$ packet records the finite monad-shaped surface by which a displayed Cauchy-filter completion route admits a unit, a flattening read, and a terminal Real handoff without quotienting filters or selecting limits. It sits after the Cauchy-filter completion boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyfiltercompletion-namecert}, the finite basis route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-filterlimitbasis-namecert}, the Cauchy-filter cluster route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-filter-cluster-namecert}, the finite stream windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet exposes the completion monad only as finite $\BHist$ and $\NameCert$ rows.

\begin{definition}[Cauchy completion filter monad carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-completion-filter-monad-carrier}
A $\FilterCompletionMonadUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
M=(Q,B,Z,S,R,D,E,U,F,A,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $Q$ is the $\CauchyFilterCompletionUp$ completion-boundary row whose accepted reads remain tied to displayed finite Cauchy-filter evidence.
\item $B$ is the $\FilterLimitBasisUp$ basis row used by the completion request before any Real-facing seal is inspected.
\item $Z$ is the $\CauchyFilterClusterUp$ cluster-handoff row, exposing the same finite support as a windowed cluster route.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window schedule read by the basis and cluster rows.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row obtained from those windows.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger attached to the same finite windows and readback rows.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row, available only downstream from $Q,B,Z,S,R,D$.
\item $U$ is the unit row, admitting a displayed source filter as a one-layer completion packet without adding a quotient filter or chosen limit.
\item $F$ is the multiplication row, flattening a displayed two-layer completion packet by replaying its inner and outer $Q,B,Z,S,R,D,E$ routes.
\item $A$ is the associativity replay row, recording that the two displayed flattening orders read the same finite rows before $E$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the completion, basis, cluster, window, readback, tolerance, seal, unit, flattening, associativity, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay for unit reads, flattening reads, associativity reads, and Real handoff reads through $Q,B,Z,S,R,D,E,U,F,A,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\FilterCompletionMonadUp$, $\CauchyFilterCompletionUp$, $\FilterLimitBasisUp$, $\CauchyFilterClusterUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\FilterCompletionMonadUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $Q,B,Z,S,R,D,E,U,F,A,H,C,P,N$. There is no coordinate for quotient filters, selected limits, ultrafilters, countable-choice schedules, host filter equality, host real equality, or ambient completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy completion filter monad laws]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-filter-monad-laws}
For every accepted $\FilterCompletionMonadUp$ carrier $M=(Q,B,Z,S,R,D,E,U,F,A,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\FilterCompletionMonadUp}$ surface consists exactly of five obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed $\CauchyFilterCompletionUp$, $\FilterLimitBasisUp$, $\CauchyFilterClusterUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, unit, multiplication, associativity, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item unit admission through $U$, requiring every source-filter read to enter the completion route through the same $Q,B,Z,S,R,D,E$ rows;
\item multiplication admission through $F$, requiring every two-layer completion read to flatten by replaying the inner and outer displayed completion routes before the terminal seal;
\item associativity of displayed replay through $A$, requiring the two flattening orders to expose the same $Q,B,Z,S,R,D,E,U,F,H,C,P,N$ rows before any Real-facing consumer reads $E$;
\item Real handoff through $E$, requiring completion, basis, cluster, window, readback, tolerance, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming to remain visible to every $\RealUp$ consumer.
\end{enumerate}
No listed obligation may be discharged by quotient filters, selected limits, ultrafilters, countable-choice schedules, host equality, detached completion points, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-completion-filter-monad-carrier}. The visible rows are exactly $Q,B,Z,S,R,D,E,U,F,A,H,C,P,N$, so carrier admission has no hidden quotient-filter, selected-limit, choice, host-equality, or ambient-completeness coordinate.

The unit row is read as a displayed admission into the existing completion boundary. It points the source filter through the $\CauchyFilterCompletionUp$ row $Q$, the $\FilterLimitBasisUp$ row $B$, the $\CauchyFilterClusterUp$ row $Z$, and the finite observation rows $S,R,D$ before $E$ is available.

The multiplication row flattens only displayed completion data. An accepted two-layer packet first replays the inner and outer $Q,B,Z,S,R,D,E$ routes, and then records the flattened route through $F,H,C,P,N$.

Associativity is the equality of the displayed replay surface, not an ambient quotient principle. The row $A$ states that the two orders of flattening expose the same finite rows before the terminal seal, so no selected representative or ultrafilter argument can enter between the two replays.

Finally apply the Real handoff. Since $E$ is downstream from the completion, basis, cluster, window, readback, tolerance, unit, flattening, associativity, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows, every $\RealUp$ consumer receives the displayed monad route and no external completion principle.
\end{proof}
