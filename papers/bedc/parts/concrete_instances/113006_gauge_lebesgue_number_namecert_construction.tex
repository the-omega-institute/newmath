Following Cousin and Bishop gauge-cover arguments, the $\GaugeLebesgueNumberUp$ packet records the finite handoff from a displayed gauge cover to a positive Lebesgue-number ledger. It sits over the reusable cover row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cousin-cover-namecert}, the tagged-subdivision theorem row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cousin-lemma-namecert}, the finite radius ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finite-lebesgue-number-namecert}, the compact metric source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, and the rational comparison surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rat-namecert}. \begin{definition}[Gauge Lebesgue number carrier]
\label{def:gauge-lebesgue-number-carrier}
A $\GaugeLebesgueNumberUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(K,G,U,F,Q,H,T,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $K$ is the $\CompactMetricUp$ source row whose finite-net and located coverage data bound the gauge request.
\item $G$ is the $\CousinCoverUp$ gauge-cover row, carrying tagged cover cells and their local gauge admissions.
\item $U$ is the $\CousinLemmaUp$ subdivision row, tying the same gauge cover to a finite tagged partition.
\item $F$ is the $\FiniteLebesgueNumberUp$ positive-radius ledger for the displayed gauge cover.
\item $Q$ is the $\RatUp$ comparison row certifying that the chosen radius is positive and below the relevant gauge radii.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the compact source, gauge cover, Cousin subdivision, finite radius ledger, rational comparison, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $T$ is the displayed $\Cont$ replay from a compact-cover request through $K,G,U,F,Q$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\GaugeLebesgueNumberUp$, $\CousinCoverUp$, $\CousinLemmaUp$, $\FiniteLebesgueNumberUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\RatUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\GaugeLebesgueNumberUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $K,G,U,F,Q,H,T,P,N$. It has no coordinate for arbitrary open covers, selected subcovers, host minima, quotient metric equality, countable choice, or detached compactness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Gauge Lebesgue number NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:gauge-lebesgue-number-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\GaugeLebesgueNumberUp$ carrier
$$
L=(K,G,U,F,Q,H,T,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\GaugeLebesgueNumberUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item compact metric admission through $K$;
\item Cousin gauge-cover admission through $G$;
\item Cousin-lemma subdivision compatibility through $U$;
\item finite Lebesgue-number radius admission through $F$;
\item rational positivity and lower-bound comparison through $Q$;
\item componentwise transport, replay, provenance, and local naming through $H,T,P,N$.
\end{enumerate}
No gauge Lebesgue-number obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier of \autoref{def:gauge-lebesgue-number-carrier}. The rows $K,G,U,F,Q$ are the mathematical handoff, while $H,T,P,N$ preserve and name the same displayed route.

The compact metric row supplies the finite coverage source. The Cousin cover and Cousin lemma rows keep the gauge data tied to a finite tagged subdivision rather than to an arbitrary open-cover argument.

The finite Lebesgue-number row and rational comparison row provide the positive lower radius. Since the carrier contains no selected subcover, host minimum, quotient equality, choice, or detached compactness coordinate, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Gauge Lebesgue number cover handoff]
\label{thm:gauge-lebesgue-number-cover-handoff}
Every compact-uniform or gauge-integration consumer of an accepted $\GaugeLebesgueNumberUp$ carrier factors through
$$
\CompactMetricUp \longmapsto \CousinCoverUp \longmapsto \CousinLemmaUp \longmapsto \FiniteLebesgueNumberUp \longmapsto \RatUp .
$$
Thus the exported radius is read as a finite gauge-cover lower-bound ledger over $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows. It is not a selected host Lebesgue number, not a general open-cover compactness proof, and not an external bridge request.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the compact metric row $K$ and the gauge-cover row $G$. Together they bind the cover request to displayed finite metric and gauge data.

Read the Cousin-lemma row $U$. It supplies the finite tagged-subdivision compatibility needed by gauge-integration consumers and keeps the cover handoff inside the displayed gauge route.

Finally read the finite radius ledger $F$ and rational comparison row $Q$. These are the only rows that export a positive radius, and they are already tied to $K,G,U$ by the carrier. The structural rows $H,T,P,N$ transport and name that path, so every consumer receives the same finite cover handoff.
\end{proof}
