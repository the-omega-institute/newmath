The certificate factors that identity through the finite pointwise-sum surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regularcauchysum-namecert}, the absolute-value surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regularcauchyabs-namecert}, the rational scaling surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regularcauchyscale-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal $\RealUp$ seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. $$

\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ and $B$ are the two source $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular rational Cauchy name rows.
\item $W_A$ and $W_B$ are the aligned $\StreamNameUp$ windows used to observe the two sources at the same precision requests.
\item $D_A$ and $D_B$ are the displayed $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint rows read from those windows.
\item $S$ is the $\RegularCauchySumUp$ row for the source sum $A+B$.
\item $\Delta$ is the displayed $\RegularCauchySumUp$ difference row for $A+(-B)$, with the negation supplied by the same rational scaling route used by the sibling operation surface.
\item $V$ is the $\RegularCauchyAbsUp$ row for $|\Delta|$.
\item $T$ is the $\RegularCauchySumUp$ row for $S+V$.
\item $Q$ is the $\RegularCauchyScaleUp$ row scaling $T$ by the displayed rational one-half.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row assembled from the scaled regular Cauchy name.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after the readback row $R$ has been exposed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the two sources, aligned windows, dyadic endpoints, sum, difference, absolute-value, second sum, half-scale, readback, seal, provenance, and local name.

\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyMaxUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for trichotomy on real names, host real order, quotient stream equality, selected limits, or a $\RealUp$ completeness principle.

\begin{theorem}[RegularCauchyMax sum-abs factorization]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-max-sum-abs-factorization}
For every accepted $\RegularCauchyMaxUp$ carrier $M=(A,B,W_A,W_B,D_A,D_B,S,\Delta,V,T,Q,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, every maximum-facing consumer factors through the displayed route
$$
(A,B,W_A,W_B,D_A,D_B)
\longrightarrow
(S,\Delta,V,T)
\longrightarrow
Q
\longrightarrow
R .
$$
Thus the maximum readback is the regular Cauchy name obtained from $max(x,y)=(x+y+|x-y|)/2$ by finite sum, absolute-value, and one-half scaling rows, not by a comparison oracle for completed reals.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a maximum-facing consumer of the accepted packet. The only source reads available to it are the two $\RegSeqRatUp$ rows $A,B$, the aligned windows $W_A,W_B$, and the endpoint rows $D_A,D_B$.

Next build the algebraic route. The row $S$ displays the source sum, $\Delta$ displays the difference route, $V$ displays the absolute value of that difference, and $T$ displays the sum of $S$ with $V$. Each row is a finite sibling NameCert row over the same visible window and endpoint data.

Finally apply the half-scale row $Q$ and expose the readback $R$. Since $R$ is downstream of $Q$, and $Q$ is downstream of $S,\Delta,V,T$, the consumer route is exactly the stated sum-absolute-value factorization.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegularCauchyMax Real seal boundary]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-max-real-seal-boundary}

The real-facing boundary exposes no trichotomy witness, host real order, quotient stream equality, selected limit, or ambient completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a real-facing consumer. By \autoref{thm:regular-cauchy-max-sum-abs-factorization}, the consumer cannot reach the maximum readback $R$ until the finite sum, difference, absolute-value, post-absolute-value sum, and one-half scale rows have all been displayed.

The seal row $E$ is downstream of $R$. The continuation rows first replay the aligned source windows and dyadic endpoints, then the sibling operation rows, then the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback consumed by the terminal $\RealUp$ seal.

Since \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-max-carrier} contains no coordinate for trichotomy, host real order, quotient stream equality, selected limits, or completeness, the real-facing boundary is exhausted by the displayed finite route.
\end{proof}
