\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\UniformContinuousExtensionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-uniform-continuous-extension-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, the $\UniformContinuousExtensionUp$ packet records the finite BEDC surface for extending a uniformly continuous map across displayed metric completions. It reads the completion carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completion-namecert}, the metric-completion route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metriccompletion-namecert}, the complete-target convergence interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completemetric-namecert}, the continuous-map graph and local-modulus rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuousmap-namecert}, and the separated-target uniqueness boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separatedmetric-namecert}. The packet is an extension ledger over finite modulus and Cauchy evidence rows; it is not a quotient-completion construction and it does not select representatives.

\begin{definition}[UniformContinuousExtension carrier]
\label{def:uniform-continuous-extension-carrier}
A $\UniformContinuousExtensionUp$ carrier is a finite packet
$$
(X,Y,f,\omega,C_X,C_Y,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the source $\MetricUp$ row together with the $\CompletionUp$ and $\MetricCompletionUp$ evidence used to read completion names over the same point histories.
\item $Y$ is the target $\CompleteMetricUp$ row equipped with the $\SeparatedMetricUp$ uniqueness boundary for carried limits.
\item $f$ is the $\ContinuousMapUp$ graph row on the dense source, and $\omega$ is the finite uniform-modulus row that transports Cauchy closeness in $X$ to Cauchy closeness in $Y$.
\item $C_X$ is the source completion evidence: regular-Cauchy input windows, source observations, and the route from source names to completion names.
\item $C_Y$ is the target convergence handoff: the complete-metric limit witness used after applying $f$ through the transported modulus.
\item $E$ is the extension row assigning a target completion read to each admitted source-completion read.
\item $H$ is the componentwise $\hsame$ transport ledger for the source metric, target complete metric, map graph, modulus, completion evidence, convergence handoff, and extension row.
\item $C$ is the displayed $\Cont$ replay route that forces every extension read to pass through $X,f,\omega,C_X,C_Y,E$ in that order.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance row over $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\MetricUp$, $\CompletionUp$, $\MetricCompletionUp$, $\CompleteMetricUp$, $\SeparatedMetricUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, and the local completion and convergence rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\UniformContinuousExtensionUp}$ row naming this packet and no additional completion principle.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[UniformContinuousExtension NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:uniform-continuous-extension-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:uniform-continuous-extension-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\UniformContinuousExtensionUp}$ surface consists of six finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed packet $(X,Y,f,\omega,C_X,C_Y,E,H,C,P,N)$;
\item modulus transport, sending the source Cauchy evidence in $C_X$ through $\omega$ and the graph row $f$;
\item Cauchy-source compatibility, requiring every extension read to use the same source completion route recorded in $X$ and $C_X$;
\item complete-target convergence, requiring the transported image sequence to reach the target limit only through $Y$ and $C_Y$;
\item separated uniqueness, comparing two candidate extension reads only by the $\SeparatedMetricUp$ boundary on the same complete target;
\item ledger non-escape, stating that $H,C,P,N$ expose no quotient completion, selected representative, countable-choice schedule, open-cover compactness, or completeness of $\RealUp$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the displayed packet. Carrier admission is the statement that all rows listed in \autoref{def:uniform-continuous-extension-carrier} are present over the same source and target histories, with $P$ and $N$ naming the local package.

The modulus and source-compatibility clauses are read next. The row $\omega$ is the only route from source Cauchy evidence to target Cauchy evidence, and $C_X$ is the only route from source observations to completion names, so every admitted extension read must consume both rows before reaching the target side.

The target convergence and uniqueness clauses then follow from the target rows. The complete-metric handoff $C_Y$ supplies the carried target limit, while the separated boundary in $Y$ is the only comparison row available for two displayed target limits.

Finally inspect the structural rows. Transport in $H$ is componentwise, replay in $C$ follows the same ordered route, and provenance in $P,N$ records only the finite BEDC rows already listed. No excluded principle is a carrier coordinate, so the local NameCert obligations are exhausted by the six clauses above.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[UniformContinuousExtension completion handoff]
\label{thm:uniform-continuous-extension-completion-handoff}
Every extension read exported by an accepted $\UniformContinuousExtensionUp$ carrier factors through the displayed route
$$
X,C_X\longmapsto f,\omega\longmapsto Y,C_Y\longmapsto E .
$$
Thus a completion consumer can read the extended value only after the source completion evidence, uniform modulus, continuous-map graph row, complete-target convergence handoff, and separated uniqueness boundary have been replayed inside the same packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the source side. The completion name is admitted through $X$ and $C_X$, so the packet gives no direct access to a source-completion value before the displayed regular-Cauchy evidence has been read.

Apply the map route next. The graph row $f$ and the modulus row $\omega$ transport the source Cauchy evidence into target Cauchy evidence. This is the only target-facing route in the carrier.

The complete target row then supplies the limit handoff. The row $C_Y$ records convergence in the complete metric target, and the separated part of $Y$ records uniqueness for the displayed target read.

The extension row $E$ is therefore reached only after the route $X,C_X,f,\omega,Y,C_Y$ has been replayed. The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve that route under componentwise transport and expose no quotient completion, selected representative, countable-choice schedule, open-cover compactness, or completeness of $\RealUp$.
\end{proof}
