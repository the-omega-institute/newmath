Following Bishop and Bridges on constructive completion and reflector discipline, the $\CauchyCompletionUniversalArrowUp$ packet records the universal arrow of the L10 Cauchy completion route as finite NameCert data. It ties the reflector surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchycompletionreflector-namecert}, the regular unit of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-completion-unit-namecert}, the counit of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-completion-counit-namecert}, the idempotent comparison of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-completion-idempotent-namecert}, and the metric universal property of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-completion-universal-property-namecert} to the regular rational and real seals of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet names the universal-arrow boundary without quotient completions, selected limits, host function extensionality, ambient completeness, or bridge checking.

\begin{definition}[CauchyCompletionUniversalArrow carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-completion-universal-arrow-carrier}
A $\CauchyCompletionUniversalArrowUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
U=(M,R,\eta,F,\widehat{F},Q,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the source metric row opened by the universal-arrow request.
\item $R$ is the $\CauchyCompletionReflectorUp$ row naming the completion reflector over $M$.
\item $\eta$ is the $\RegularCauchyCompletionUnitUp$ row exposing the source-to-completion unit.
\item $F$ is the uniformly continuous map row from the source metric into the displayed complete target.
\item $\widehat{F}$ is the factor arrow row from the reflected completion to the target named by $F$.
\item $Q$ is the uniqueness comparison row, requiring any competing factor arrow with the same precomposition along $\eta$ to compare through the displayed NameCert route.
\item $S$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ and $\RealUp$ seal row reached after $R,\eta,F,\widehat{F},Q$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for source metrics, reflector rows, units, uniformly continuous maps, factor arrows, uniqueness comparisons, seal rows, replay rows, provenance rows, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the route
$$
M,R,\eta,F \longmapsto \widehat{F},Q,S .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyCompletionUniversalArrowUp$, $\CauchyCompletionReflectorUp$, $\RegularCauchyCompletionUnitUp$, $\CauchyCompletionCounitUp$, $\CauchyCompletionIdempotentUp$, $\MetricCompletionUniversalPropertyUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyCompletionUniversalArrowUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through these rows. It has no coordinate for quotient completions, selected Cauchy limits, host function extensionality, ambient completeness of a target space, or bridge-checked equality.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyCompletionUniversalArrow NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-universal-arrow-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CauchyCompletionUniversalArrowUp$ carrier $U=(M,R,\eta,F,\widehat{F},Q,S,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyCompletionUniversalArrowUp}$ surface consists exactly of six finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the source metric, completion reflector, unit, uniformly continuous map, factor arrow, uniqueness comparison, RegSeqRat/Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item reflector anchoring, requiring the rows $R$ and $\eta$ to be displayed before any universal-arrow factor can be inspected;
\item map compatibility, requiring the source map row $F$ to be read as uniformly continuous along the same source metric row $M$;
\item factorization visibility, requiring $\widehat{F}$ to be exported only as the factor arrow whose precomposition along $\eta$ replays $F$ through $C$;
\item uniqueness comparison, requiring $Q$ to compare all competing factor rows by the same displayed route rather than by host function extensionality;
\item RegSeqRat/Real seal nonescape, requiring $S,H,C,P,N$ to exhaust the regular rational seal, real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming surface.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier $U=(M,R,\eta,F,\widehat{F},Q,S,H,C,P,N)$. Carrier admission exposes exactly the rows listed in \autoref{def:cauchy-completion-universal-arrow-carrier}.

The universal-arrow request is anchored by $M,R,\eta$. The source metric is visible before the reflector row is consumed, and the unit row is the only source-to-completion boundary available to the factorization read.

The map and factor rows are then read together. The row $F$ supplies the uniformly continuous source map, while $\widehat{F}$ is exported only through the continuation replay that identifies precomposition with $\eta$ as the displayed source map route.

Finally $Q,S,H,C,P,N$ close the comparison and sealing surface. Since quotient completions, selected limits, host function extensionality, ambient completeness, and bridge checking are absent from the carrier, the listed rows exhaust the local certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}

The route terminates at $S,H,C,P,N$. Regular rational and Real sealing, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming preserve the ordered handoff and exclude quotient completions, selected limits, ambient completeness, and bridge checking from the exported packet.
