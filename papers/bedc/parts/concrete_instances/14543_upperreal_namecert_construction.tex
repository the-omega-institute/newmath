\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\UpperRealUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-upperreal-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop--Bridges constructive analysis, upper real data are read as finite upper rational or dyadic observations with explicit approximation windows, not as a Dedekind completion, quotient real, trichotomy branch, selected infimum, or host-completeness object. The $\UpperRealUp$ packet is the upper-ledger dual of the lower observation surface in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-lowerreal-namecert}; it supplies the one-sided upper data consumed by the located real interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedreal-namecert}, the located cut discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedcut-namecert}, the finite stream-window surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the terminal $\RealUp$ seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}.

\begin{definition}[UpperReal carrier]
\label{def:upper-real-carrier}
An $\UpperRealUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
U=(U_0,L,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $U_0$ is the upper rational or dyadic ledger: each row records a rational candidate, a dyadic comparison budget, and the evidence that the candidate is admitted by the upper side of the represented real.
\item $L$ is the sibling $\LowerRealUp$ comparison window, used only to expose a finite lower-upper apartness row when a located consumer asks for one-sided separation data.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row through which the displayed upper observations are inspected.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row attaching those windows to a regular rational name surface.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after $U_0,W,R$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ is the componentwise $\hsame$ transport row for the upper ledger, the lower comparison window, the finite windows, the regular rational handoff, the Real seal, and the structural rows.
\item $C$ is the displayed $\Cont$ replay route by which a consumer rereads the upper ledger through the same windows and handoff.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\UpperRealUp$, $\LowerRealUp$, $\LocatedRealUp$, $\LocatedCutUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\UpperRealUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exports no lower cut, no located cut completion, no quotient real equality, no trichotomy branch, no selected infimum, no countable-choice row, and no host-completeness coordinate.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[UpperReal NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:upper-real-obligation-surface}
For an accepted $\UpperRealUp$ carrier $U=(U_0,L,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\UpperRealUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following seven finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the upper rational or dyadic ledger, the sibling lower comparison window, the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff, the $\RealUp$ seal, componentwise transports, continuation replay, package provenance, and local naming row;
\item upper-ledger transport, stating that replacement through $H$ and replay through $C$ preserve the same displayed upper candidates and their dyadic comparison budgets;
\item lower-upper apartness scope, stating that every comparison with $\LowerRealUp$ or $\LocatedRealUp$ reads only the displayed lower comparison window $L$ together with the upper candidates in $U_0$;
\item window scope, stating that every upper observation is read through the displayed finite windows $W$ and no hidden stream tail;
\item regular-rational handoff, stating that each accepted upper-window read is attached to the displayed $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $R$ before the Real seal is consumed;
\item real-seal nonescape, stating that $E$ can be reached only after $U_0,L,W,R,H,C,P,N$ have been displayed;
\item ledger exactness, stating that every accepted upper-real read uses exactly the rows recorded in $U_0$ and no second upper ledger, lower cut, located cut completion, quotient equality, selected infimum, countable-choice row, or ambient completeness coordinate.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is the tuple displayed in \autoref{def:upper-real-carrier}. The packet has one upper ledger, one lower comparison window, one finite-window surface, one regular rational handoff, one Real seal, and the structural rows for transport, replay, provenance, and local naming.

Upper-ledger transport is componentwise over $H$ and $C$. A classified replacement may carry the same rational candidates, dyadic budgets, lower comparison window, observation windows, handoff, and seal through the displayed routes, but it cannot add a second upper ledger or any hidden completion coordinate.

The lower-upper apartness scope is supplied by $L$ together with $U_0$. A located or lower-real consumer can compare only those displayed rows, so the comparison window is finite and does not become a Dedekind cut, quotient equality test, or selected infimum.

Window scope and the regular-rational handoff are the roles of $W$ and $R$. Every upper observation in $U_0$ is read through the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window row, and the corresponding $\RegSeqRatUp$ row records the regular rational source before the terminal $\RealUp$ seal is available.

Real-seal nonescape and ledger exactness follow from the row inventory. The only route to $E$ passes through $U_0,L,W,R,H,C,P,N$, and the provenance row records the lower-real, located-real, and located-cut neighbors only as dependency anchors. The carrier exports no lower cut, no located cut completion, no quotient real equality, no trichotomy branch, no selected infimum, no countable-choice row, and no host-completeness coordinate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[UpperReal lower-upper apartness window]
\label{thm:upper-real-lower-upper-apartness-window}
Every $\LowerRealUp$-, $\LocatedRealUp$-, or $\LocatedCutUp$-facing comparison read of an accepted $\UpperRealUp$ packet factors through the displayed lower comparison window $L$, the upper ledger $U_0$, and the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window row $W$. The comparison may expose a visible dyadic gap between the lower-side witness and an upper candidate, transported by $H$ and replayed by $C$, but it cannot introduce lower-cut completion data, quotient real equality, a trichotomy branch, a selected infimum, countable choice, or host completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier $U=(U_0,L,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)$. A comparison consumer sees the lower-side window $L$ and the upper rational or dyadic ledger $U_0$, so the available apartness information is exactly the displayed finite list of lower-side witnesses, upper candidates, and comparison budgets.

Next the consumer reads the finite observation route. The row $W$ supplies the $\StreamNameUp$ window, and $R$ supplies the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff for the same displayed observations before any terminal seal is reachable.

Transport is internal to the packet. Moving along $H$ and replaying $C$ changes the presented row names but not the finite dependency route from $L$ and $U_0$ through $W$ and $R$.

The carrier exports no lower cut, no located cut completion, no quotient real equality, no trichotomy branch, no selected infimum, no countable-choice row, and no host-completeness coordinate. Therefore the comparison remains a finite lower-upper apartness window over the displayed rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[UpperReal public certificate]
\label{thm:upper-real-public-certificate}

\begin{proof}
Read the carrier route in order. The upper ledger $U_0$ supplies the finite rational or dyadic observations, $L$ supplies the lower-side comparison window, $W$ supplies the window discipline, and $R$ supplies the regular rational handoff for those same observations.

The terminal seal $E$ is downstream of that finite route. Its continuation in $C$ can replay the displayed rows, while $H$ transports only the componentwise names already present in the packet.
