\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\IntervalDomainUp$}

Following Bishop and Bridges' constructive completion through located intervals and Scott's interval-domain reading of real approximation, the $\IntervalDomainUp$ packet records a finite approximation-domain certificate for the Real-completion boundary. It sits between the endpoint-cell packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedinterval-namecert}, the nested-window packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-nestedinterval-namecert}, the rational-cell packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rationalinterval-namecert}, the stream-window packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The certificate names the directed finite route by which completion-facing consumers read approximants before a $\RealUp$ seal is exposed; it is not a quotient-real construction, a host equality principle, an open-cover compactness theorem, or ambient completeness of $\RealUp$.

\begin{definition}[IntervalDomain carrier]
\label{def:interval-domain-carrier}
An $\IntervalDomainUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
D=(L,R,N,W,Q,E,H,C,P,A)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $L$ is a finite family of $\LocatedIntervalUp$ cells whose endpoint windows are already named by the underlying real-name interface.
\item $R$ is the $\RationalIntervalUp$ refinement ledger attached to those cells, including endpoint order, containment, and width-budget rows.
\item $N$ is the $\NestedIntervalUp$ directed nesting row recording which later cells refine earlier cells under the displayed containment route.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window family through which endpoint requests and refinement checks are inspected.
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback family serving the same windows with regular rational observations.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after the located cells, rational refinements, nesting row, stream windows, and regular readbacks have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $L,R,N,W,Q,E$ and the structural rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ routes for replaying endpoint reads, refinement reads, nesting reads, window reads, regular readbacks, and seal reads in that order.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\IntervalDomainUp$, $\LocatedIntervalUp$, $\RationalIntervalUp$, $\NestedIntervalUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $A$ is the local $\NameCert_{\IntervalDomainUp}$ evidence row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exports only the displayed finite approximation domain. It contains no coordinate for quotient real equality, host real equality, open-cover compactness, a selected limiting representative, or ambient completeness of $\RealUp$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[IntervalDomain NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:interval-domain-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\IntervalDomainUp$ carrier $D=(L,R,N,W,Q,E,H,C,P,A)$, the local $\NameCert_{\IntervalDomainUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission, refinement stability, directedness, endpoint-window transport, and real-seal non-escape:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission exposes precisely the ten rows $L,R,N,W,Q,E,H,C,P,A$;
\item refinement stability keeps every $\RationalIntervalUp$ refinement row attached to its transported $\LocatedIntervalUp$ cell and its transported window data;
\item directedness requires every pair of displayed cells admitted for a common completion read to pass through a later $\NestedIntervalUp$ refinement row before the seal row is inspected;
\item endpoint-window transport moves endpoint reads only through the paired $\StreamNameUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$ rows named by the carrier;
\item real-seal non-escape states that any $\RealUp$ consumer of $E$ must first read the located cells, rational refinements, directed nesting row, stream windows, regular readbacks, and structural rows $H,C,P,A$.
\end{enumerate}
These obligations do not export quotient reals, host real equality, open-cover compactness, selected limiting representatives, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:interval-domain-carrier}. The packet lists the located interval cells, rational refinement ledger, nesting row, stream windows, regular readbacks, real seal, transports, continuations, provenance, and local naming row as its whole visible surface.

Refinement stability reads $L,R,H,C$. A transported located cell carries its rational interval ledger with it, and the displayed continuation route keeps the refinement read attached to the same endpoint-window data.

Directedness reads $N$ after the refinement ledger. For a common completion-facing request, two visible cells cannot be consumed independently; the nesting row supplies the later common refinement before the real seal can be exposed.

Endpoint-window transport reads $W$ and $Q$ as a pair. The stream row names the finite endpoint request, and the regular rational row supplies the readback for that request, so a transported endpoint read has no route outside the displayed window and readback rows.

Finally, real-seal non-escape follows because $E$ is downstream of $L,R,N,W,Q$ and the structural rows. Since the carrier has no quotient, host-equality, compactness, selected-limit, or completeness coordinate, none of those principles can be read from the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[IntervalDomain completion approximation scope]
\label{thm:interval-domain-completion-approximation-scope}
Let $D=(L,R,N,W,Q,E,H,C,P,A)$ be an accepted $\IntervalDomainUp$ carrier. Every completion-facing consumer of the displayed $\RealUp$ seal factors through the finite route
$$
\LocatedIntervalUp\longmapsto\RationalIntervalUp\longmapsto\NestedIntervalUp\longmapsto\StreamNameUp\longmapsto\RegSeqRatUp\longmapsto\RealUp\longmapsto(H,C,P,A).
$$
Thus the completion-facing interface is an interval-domain approximation route over finite NameCert data, not a quotient of real names, not host real equality, not open-cover compactness, and not an ambient completeness theorem for $\RealUp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the endpoint cells. The row $L$ supplies the visible $\LocatedIntervalUp$ data, and $R$ attaches the $\RationalIntervalUp$ containment and width ledger used by each cell.

Next read the directed approximation layer. The row $N$ records the $\NestedIntervalUp$ refinement order, so a completion-facing consumer moves from finite cells to a common directed refinement before any seal-facing request is available.

Then read the observation layer. The stream windows $W$ and regular rational readbacks $Q$ supply the finite endpoint observations for the same directed refinement route.

The seal row $E$ is reached only after those rows have been consumed. The structural rows $H,C,P,A$ transport, replay, source, and name the same route, so every completion-facing consumer factors through the displayed chain and receives no additional quotient, host-equality, compactness, selected-limit, or completeness data.
\end{proof}
