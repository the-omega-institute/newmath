The $\QuotientStreamRefusalUp$ packet records the finite Real-completion boundary that refuses quotient-stream equality unless the request first factors through displayed $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\CauchyLimitSealUp$, and $\RealUp$ seal rows. It is a non-escape certificate for completion-facing consumers, not a quotient construction and not a selected-limit principle.

The refusal packet is a consumer of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchylimitseal-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert} in that order. Finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows feed $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ binds the regular readback to a seal route, and $\RealUp$ receives only that displayed seal surface. This dependency is a horizontal L10 lattice edge, not a quotient construction, selected-limit principle, host real equality, or ambient completeness claim.

\begin{definition}[QuotientStreamRefusal carrier]
\label{def:quotient-stream-refusal-carrier}
A $\QuotientStreamRefusalUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(S,R,L,E,F,H,C,P,N)
$$
with five displayed route rows and four structural rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window row requested by the completion-facing consumer;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row served only after the window row $S$ has been exposed;
\item $L$ is the $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ route row binding the regular readback to the seal surface;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row available only after $S,R,L$ have been consumed;
\item $F$ is the refusal ledger row recording that quotient-stream equality, selected limits, host real equality, and ambient completeness are not exported by this packet;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $S,R,L,E,F$ and the local naming rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes from $S$ to $R$, from $R$ to $L$, from $L$ to $E$, and from any quotient-stream request to the refusal row $F$;
\item $P$ records $\Pkg$ provenance over $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\CauchyLimitSealUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\QuotientStreamRefusalUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[QuotientStreamRefusal NameCert surface]
\label{thm:quotient-stream-refusal-namecert-surface}
For every accepted $\QuotientStreamRefusalUp$ carrier $Q=(S,R,L,E,F,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\QuotientStreamRefusalUp}$ surface has exactly five route obligations: finite $\StreamNameUp$ window exposure through $S$, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback through $R$, $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ route exposure through $L$, $\RealUp$ seal exposure through $E$, and quotient-stream refusal through $F$. The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, package, and name those five obligations, and no quotient stream identity, selected limit, host real equality, hidden family limit, hidden tail oracle, or ambient completeness coordinate is exported.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:quotient-stream-refusal-carrier}. The only route rows are $S,R,L,E,F$, and every structural read is attached to them through $H,C,P,N$.

Read the completion route in order. The $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $R$ is exposed only after the $\StreamNameUp$ window $S$, the $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ row $L$ is exposed only after $R$, and the $\RealUp$ seal row $E$ is exposed only after $L$.

Now inspect a quotient-stream request. The displayed continuation route sends that request to $F$, whose ledger records refusal rather than an equality witness. Since the packet has no quotient, selected-limit, host-equality, hidden-tail, or ambient-completeness coordinate, the listed five obligations exhaust the local certificate surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[QuotientStreamRefusal non-escape route]
\label{thm:quotient-stream-refusal-nonescape-route}
Any Real-completion consumer accepted through a $\QuotientStreamRefusalUp$ packet must factor through the finite route
$$
\StreamNameUp \longmapsto \RegSeqRatUp \longmapsto \CauchyLimitSealUp \longmapsto \RealUp.
$$
If the consumer asks instead for quotient-stream equality, selected limits, host real equality, hidden family limits, hidden tail oracles, or ambient completeness, the request is routed to the refusal ledger $F$ and no completion coordinate is exported.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use \autoref{thm:quotient-stream-refusal-namecert-surface}. It orders the accepted route through $S,R,L,E$ and attaches all transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming data to that route.

The refusal row handles every off-route request. A quotient-stream equality request has no carrier coordinate except $F$, and $F$ is a ledger of refusal rather than a stream identity witness.

Thus every accepted Real-completion read remains on the displayed finite route, while every non-escape boundary request is recorded without exporting quotient equality, selected limits, host real equality, hidden family limits, hidden tail oracles, or ambient completeness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[QuotientStreamRefusal route exposure]
\label{thm:quotient-stream-refusal-route-exposure}
Every accepted $\QuotientStreamRefusalUp$ consumer reads the displayed route rows in the order
$$

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:quotient-stream-refusal-carrier}. The route coordinates are listed as the finite window row $S$, regular readback row $R$, limit-seal route row $L$, and Real seal row $E$.

Apply \autoref{thm:quotient-stream-refusal-namecert-surface}. It identifies these four route obligations as the only accepted Real-completion exposure path before the refusal row is consulted.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ are attached to the same displayed coordinates. They preserve and replay the route already exposed by $S,R,L,E$, so no structural projection can publish the $\RealUp$ seal before the ordered route has been read.

\begin{theorem}[QuotientStreamRefusal ledger exactness]
\label{thm:quotient-stream-refusal-ledger-exactness}

The rows $H,C,P,N$ only transport, route, package, and name that refusal; they do not turn the refusal ledger into any of the requested completion coordinates.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

Read the remaining rows through the carrier. The transport row $H$ can compare displayed rows componentwise, $C$ can route the request to $F$, $P$ can record package provenance, and $N$ can name the local packet.

None of those rows is a completion coordinate. Therefore each listed request ends exactly at the refusal ledger, with structural rows recording the refusal rather than discharging it.
