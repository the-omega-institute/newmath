$$

\begin{theorem}[Punctured cube skeleton NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:punctured-cube-skeleton-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:punctured-cube-skeleton-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\PuncturedCubeSkeletonUp}$ surface consists exactly of WNA cube admission, puncture admission, CUN square admission, bridge-edge admission, articulation-gate admission, edge-decomposition admission, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, TasteGate nontriviality and field-faithfulness requirements, and local naming. The future Lean-facing TasteGate surface must distinguish the puncture row $A$ from the unpunctured cube row $W$ and must preserve every field $W,A,C,B,G,E,H,T,Q,R,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier. The rows $W,A,C,B,G,E$ display the finite support formula, the puncture, the CUN square, the bridge edges, the articulation gate, and the induced edge-decomposition ledger.

Transport and replay are structural. The rows $H,T,Q,N$ preserve and name the same finite graph support data without adding a host quotient or a biological mechanism.

The TasteGate requirement row $R$ records the exact Lean obligations: nontriviality must separate the punctured packet from a collapsed graph, and field faithfulness must preserve every displayed vertex, puncture, bridge, gate, and decomposition row. These obligations are part of the carrier surface and do not assert that the Lean instances already exist.
\end{proof}

\label{thm:punctured-cube-skeleton-boundary-nonescape}

$$

before reading the punctured cube skeleton as finite NameCert data. \end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

Now use componentwise transport and replay. The rows $H$ and $T$ preserve those coordinates as finite $\BHist$ data, while $Q$ and $N$ record provenance and local naming for the same route.

Finally read the TasteGate row. Because $R$ is displayed before a downstream carrier can be admitted, any later Lean realization must keep nontriviality and field faithfulness visible. Thus no consumer can erase the puncture, collapse the support graph, or bypass the stated gate requirements.
\end{proof}
