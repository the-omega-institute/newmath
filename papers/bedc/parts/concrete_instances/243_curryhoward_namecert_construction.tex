\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CurryHowardUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-curryhoward-namecert}

The Curry-Howard interface $\CurryHowardUp$ packages the bijective correspondence between intuitionistic propositions and types, between proofs and programs, and between cut-elimination and reduction. It depends on $\NameCert_{\FirstOrderUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-firstorder-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\LambdaCalcUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-lambdacalc-namecert}), and bridges logic and computation through the same inductive carrier.

\begin{definition}[CurryHowardUp shared cut-beta packet]\label{def:curryhoward-shared-cut-beta-packet}
A shared cut-beta packet for $\CurryHowardUp$ is a displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ tuple
$$
\mathcal H=(P,A,B,D_1,D_2,D_{\mathsf{cut}},x,a,b,u,L,r,s,\chi,\rho)
$$
with the following rows. The histories $P,A,B$ are the shared proof-program, cut-formula/type, and conclusion-type endpoints. The rows $D_1$ and $D_2$ are accepted finite $\FirstOrderUp$ deduction ledgers over one carried syntax packet from \autoref{def:firstorder-bhist-syntax-carrier}; $D_1$ has final endpoint $A$, and $D_2$ has $A$ as the displayed initial premise row, so the pair is composable in the sense of \autoref{def:firstorder-deduction-ledger-concatenation-source}. The row $D_{\mathsf{cut}}$ is an accepted $\FirstOrderUp$ deduction ledger with the same open-premise rows as $D_1$ followed by $D_2$ and final endpoint $B$, and the comparison between $D_1 \mathbin{+\!\!+} D_2$ and $D_{\mathsf{cut}}$ is carried only by the $\FirstOrderUp$ deduction classifier of \autoref{def:firstorder-deduction-ledger-classifier}.

The row $L$ is an accepted $\LambdaCalcUp$ term packet in the sense of \autoref{def:lambdacalc-term-packet-carrier}. The history $x$ is a displayed unary binder row, $a$ is the lambda-term endpoint classified with the proof ledger $D_1$, $\mathsf{lamAbs}(x,b)$ is the lambda-term endpoint classified with the proof ledger $D_2$, and $u$ is the lambda-term endpoint classified with $D_{\mathsf{cut}}$. The row $r$ is the displayed beta-redex source
$$
\mathsf{lamApp}(\mathsf{lamAbs}(x,b),a),
$$
and $s$ is the capture-avoiding substitution ledger that substitutes $a$ for the unary binder $x$ in $b$ with output endpoint $u$. The classifier row $\chi$ is shared by the two sides: it relates $D_1$ with $a$, $D_2$ with $\mathsf{lamAbs}(x,b)$, and $D_{\mathsf{cut}}$ with $u$ by the same displayed $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and provenance rows. The provenance row $\rho$ ties these rows to $\NameCert_{\FirstOrderUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\LambdaCalcUp}$. No host proof tree, host lambda syntax equality, or quotient of alpha classes is a field of the packet.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CurryHowardUp cut-beta bridge obligation]\label{thm:curryhoward-cut-beta-bridge-obligation}
Let $\mathcal H$ be an accepted shared cut-beta packet for $\CurryHowardUp$ as in \autoref{def:curryhoward-shared-cut-beta-packet}. If the $\FirstOrderUp$ component displays the cut-elimination step from $D_1 \mathbin{+\!\!+} D_2$ to $D_{\mathsf{cut}}$, then the $\LambdaCalcUp$ component displays an accepted beta-reduction row whose source is $\mathsf{lamApp}(\mathsf{lamAbs}(x,b),a)$ and whose substitution endpoint is $u$. Under the shared classifier $\chi$, this beta/substitution endpoint is the same proof-program endpoint as the cut-eliminated deduction ledger $D_{\mathsf{cut}}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the accepted packet $\mathcal H$. The $\FirstOrderUp$ side supplies two accepted ledgers $D_1$ and $D_2$ over the same syntax carrier, with the final endpoint of $D_1$ displayed as the initial premise row of $D_2$. By \autoref{def:firstorder-deduction-ledger-concatenation-source}, this is exactly the composability datum for the concatenated ledger $D_1 \mathbin{+\!\!+} D_2$.

Apply \autoref{thm:firstorder-deduction-ledger-concatenation-closure}. The concatenation is an accepted finite deduction ledger over the same $\SetUp$ and $\TreeUp$ dependency certificates, and the join reads no extra cut formula outside the shared endpoint row $A$. The displayed cut-elimination row in $\mathcal H$ then compares this concatenated ledger with $D_{\mathsf{cut}}$ through the deduction classifier. By \autoref{thm:firstorder-deduction-endpoint-exactness} and \autoref{thm:firstorder-classifier-transport-obligation}, every premise endpoint, conclusion endpoint, and transport row of $D_{\mathsf{cut}}$ is therefore a visible $\FirstOrderUp$ carrier or ledger row.

Now read the $\LambdaCalcUp$ side. The term packet $L$ exposes the carried syntax row, binder-index row, beta-redex ledger, substitution ledger, continuation ledger, and provenance listed in \autoref{def:lambdacalc-term-packet-carrier}. The redex row $r$ has the displayed source $\mathsf{lamApp}(\mathsf{lamAbs}(x,b),a)$, where the shared classifier $\chi$ identifies $a$ with $D_1$ and $\mathsf{lamAbs}(x,b)$ with $D_2$.

Apply \autoref{thm:lambdacalc-substitution-ledger-obligation} to the substitution row $s$. It may read only the displayed $\TreeUp$ syntax row, the $\NatUp$ binder-index row, and the capture-avoidance $\Cont$ ledger, so substituting $a$ for $x$ in $b$ produces the displayed endpoint $u$ without importing a host substitution function. By \autoref{thm:lambdacalc-substitution-carrier-preservation}, the output endpoint $u$ remains a carried LambdaCalc term endpoint.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:lambdacalc-beta-reduction-classifier-obligation}. The redex row $r$, the substitution ledger $s$, the endpoint $u$, and the provenance row $\rho$ repack as an accepted beta-reduction classifier row, stable under the displayed $\hsame$ transport. The shared classifier $\chi$ identifies this endpoint $u$ with the proof-program endpoint carried by $D_{\mathsf{cut}}$, while its other two clauses identify the redex components with $D_1$ and $D_2$. Hence the displayed deduction-side cut-elimination step is carried to the displayed beta-reduction/substitution ledger under the shared $\CurryHowardUp$ classifier, and no host proof equality or host lambda-term equality enters the bridge.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[CurryHowardUp endpoint classifier]\label{def:curryhoward-endpoint-classifier}
For accepted shared cut-beta packets $\mathcal H$ and $\mathcal H'$ as in \autoref{def:curryhoward-shared-cut-beta-packet}, the CurryHowardUp endpoint classifier relates $\mathcal H$ and $\mathcal H'$ when the following rows hold simultaneously. The $\FirstOrderUp$ deduction classifier relates the displayed deduction ledgers $D_1,D_2,D_{\mathsf{cut}}$ of $\mathcal H$ with the corresponding ledgers $D'_1,D'_2,D'_{\mathsf{cut}}$ of $\mathcal H'$. The $\LambdaCalcUp$ beta/substitution classifier relates the displayed term packets, beta-redex rows, substitution ledgers, and output endpoints $u,u'$. The shared endpoint rows satisfy the displayed $\hsame$ clauses for the proof-program endpoint $P$, the cut-formula/type endpoint $A$, and the conclusion-type endpoint $B$. The finite provenance row $\rho$ records the common dependence on $\NameCert_{\FirstOrderUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\LambdaCalcUp}$, with no host proof equality, host lambda equality, or quotient of alpha classes.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CurryHowardUp classifier endpoint transport]\label{thm:curryhoward-classifier-endpoint-transport}
Let $\mathcal H$ and $\mathcal H'$ be accepted shared cut-beta packets for $\CurryHowardUp$. If the CurryHowardUp endpoint classifier of \autoref{def:curryhoward-endpoint-classifier} relates $\mathcal H$ and $\mathcal H'$, then the cut-eliminated deduction endpoint and the beta-substitution endpoint transport to the same proof-program endpoint under the shared $\hsame$ rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the endpoint classifier. Its $\FirstOrderUp$ row gives deduction-classifier agreement for the two cut sources, the two cut targets, and the two cut-eliminated ledgers. Apply \autoref{thm:firstorder-deduction-endpoint-exactness} to read the displayed endpoints of $D_{\mathsf{cut}}$ and $D'_{\mathsf{cut}}$, and then apply \autoref{thm:firstorder-classifier-transport-obligation} to transport those endpoints through the deduction classifier.

Next use the $\LambdaCalcUp$ row. Apply \autoref{thm:lambdacalc-substitution-carrier-preservation} to the two substitution ledgers, so both substitution outputs remain carried LambdaCalc term endpoints. Apply \autoref{thm:lambdacalc-beta-reduction-classifier-obligation} to the related beta-redex and substitution rows; the classifier transports $u$ and $u'$ through the same displayed beta/substitution endpoint relation.

It remains to compare the two sides of the bridge. The shared $\hsame$ clauses in the endpoint classifier identify the proof-program endpoint, the cut-formula/type endpoint, and the conclusion-type endpoint on the two packets, while the finite $\Cont$ provenance row $\rho$ keeps both transports over $\NameCert_{\FirstOrderUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\LambdaCalcUp}$. Repacking these clauses gives one proof-program endpoint for the cut-eliminated deduction side and the beta-substitution side.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CurryHowardUp public NameCert boundary]\label{thm:curryhoward-public-namecert-boundary}
The shared cut-beta packet of \autoref{def:curryhoward-shared-cut-beta-packet}, the endpoint classifier of \autoref{def:curryhoward-endpoint-classifier}, the endpoint transport theorem \autoref{thm:curryhoward-classifier-endpoint-transport}, and the no-host-equality ledger in the packet definition form the public CurryHowardUp certificate boundary over $\NameCert_{\FirstOrderUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\LambdaCalcUp}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier part of the boundary is the accepted shared cut-beta packet. It lists the $\FirstOrderUp$ deduction ledgers, the $\LambdaCalcUp$ term packet, the beta-redex row, the substitution ledger, the shared classifier row, and the provenance row as displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ data.

The classifier part is \autoref{def:curryhoward-endpoint-classifier}. It requires agreement on the deduction classifier, beta/substitution classifier, shared $\hsame$ endpoint rows, and the finite $\Cont$ provenance row. These clauses are all stated over the two source certificates $\NameCert_{\FirstOrderUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\LambdaCalcUp}$.

The stability and endpoint-exactness part is \autoref{thm:curryhoward-classifier-endpoint-transport}. The theorem transports the cut-eliminated deduction endpoint and the beta-substitution endpoint to the same proof-program endpoint, so the public boundary has a certificate-level endpoint row rather than a host equality assertion.

Finally, the packet definition explicitly excludes host proof trees, host lambda syntax equality, and quotients of alpha classes. This no-host-equality ledger is the remaining boundary clause, and the four clauses together give the stated public CurryHowardUp certificate boundary.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CurryHoward cut-beta proof-program determinacy]\label{thm:curryhoward-cut-beta-proof-program-determinacy}
Let $\mathcal H$ and $\mathcal H'$ be accepted shared cut-beta packets for $\CurryHowardUp$ as in \autoref{def:curryhoward-shared-cut-beta-packet}. Suppose their $\FirstOrderUp$ cut-elimination rows, $\LambdaCalcUp$ beta-substitution rows, shared $\hsame$ endpoint rows, and finite $\Cont$ provenance rows are the same displayed rows under the CurryHoward endpoint classifier of \autoref{def:curryhoward-endpoint-classifier}. Then the cut-eliminated deduction endpoint and the beta-substitution endpoint determine one proof-program endpoint:
$$
(\mathsf{BHist}_{D_{\mathsf{cut}}},\mathsf{BHist}_{u},\hsame_{P},\Cont_{\rho}).
$$
This endpoint is a displayed CurryHoward certificate row, not a host equality between proof trees or lambda terms.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the two accepted packets by \autoref{def:curryhoward-shared-cut-beta-packet}. The displayed cut-elimination rows fix the $\FirstOrderUp$ ledgers $D_1,D_2,D_{\mathsf{cut}}$ and $D'_1,D'_2,D'_{\mathsf{cut}}$, while the displayed beta-substitution rows fix the redex, substitution ledger, and output endpoints $u,u'$.

Apply \autoref{thm:firstorder-deduction-endpoint-exactness} to the two cut-eliminated ledgers. Their endpoints are read from the visible deduction rows, so no external proof-tree equality is available or needed. Apply \autoref{thm:lambdacalc-beta-reduction-classifier-obligation} to the two beta-substitution rows. Their substitution outputs are accepted beta-reduction classifier endpoints, not host lambda-term quotients.

Now apply \autoref{thm:curryhoward-classifier-endpoint-transport}. The CurryHoward endpoint classifier transports the deduction endpoint and the beta-substitution endpoint through the same shared $\hsame$ proof-program row and the same finite $\Cont$ provenance over $\NameCert_{\FirstOrderUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\LambdaCalcUp}$.

The common endpoint is therefore obtained by repacking the displayed rows as
$$
(\mathsf{BHist}_{D_{\mathsf{cut}}},\mathsf{BHist}_{u},\hsame_{P},\Cont_{\rho}).
$$
This is the single proof-program endpoint determined by both readings. The conclusion is a statement about the CurryHoward classifier data itself, so it does not assert equality in the host proof language.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CurryHoward cut-beta classifier exhaustion]\label{thm:curryhoward-cut-beta-classifier-exhaustion}
Every accepted CurryHoward cut-beta endpoint classifier witness of
\autoref{def:curryhoward-endpoint-classifier} projects exactly to the
$\FirstOrderUp$ deduction classifier rows, the $\LambdaCalcUp$ beta/substitution
classifier rows, the shared $\mathsf{BHist}$ proof-program endpoint rows, the
displayed $\hsame$ transports, finite $\Cont$ provenance, and the two source
certificates $\NameCert_{\FirstOrderUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\LambdaCalcUp}$.
Conversely, those rows repack into the same accepted CurryHoward endpoint
classifier witness.

Thus the cut-beta classifier surface is exhausted by displayed deduction,
program, endpoint, transport, provenance, and source-certificate rows. It does
not read host proof equality, host lambda-term equality, or an alpha-quotient.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project an accepted witness through
\autoref{def:curryhoward-endpoint-classifier}. Its first clause is precisely the
$\FirstOrderUp$ deduction classifier comparison for $D_1,D_2,D_{\mathsf{cut}}$
and the corresponding rows of the second packet. Its second clause is the
$\LambdaCalcUp$ beta/substitution classifier comparison for the term packets,
redex rows, substitution ledgers, and output endpoints.

The remaining clauses of the same definition expose the shared
$\mathsf{BHist}$ proof-program endpoint rows, the displayed $\hsame$ transports
for $P,A,B$, and the finite $\Cont$ provenance row $\rho$. The provenance row
names exactly $\NameCert_{\FirstOrderUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\LambdaCalcUp}$ as the
source certificates for the comparison.

Apply \autoref{thm:curryhoward-classifier-endpoint-transport}. The projected
deduction and beta/substitution rows transport to the same proof-program
endpoint under those displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ rows.

Apply \autoref{thm:curryhoward-cut-beta-proof-program-determinacy}. The
transported endpoint is the displayed CurryHoward proof-program row, so the
classifier has no further host equality component to project.

Conversely, given exactly the rows listed above, rebuild the witness by the
clauses of \autoref{def:curryhoward-endpoint-classifier}. The transport theorem
and determinacy theorem verify that the repacked deduction-side and
program-side endpoints are the same displayed proof-program endpoint, which
closes the classifier without adding any external quotient.
\end{proof}
