It sits over $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}), $\StreamNameUp$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}), $\RegSeqRatUp$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}), $\RealObservationBudgetUp$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-observation-budget-namecert}), and $\RealUp$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}). The certificate is a finite ledger for unary precision and tail windows; it is not a quotient of streams, a host real equality witness, a selected limit, an unbounded observation schedule, or an ambient completeness principle.

\begin{definition}[Dyadic tail-radius ledger carrier]
\label{def:dyadic-tail-radius-ledger-carrier}
A $\DyadicTailRadiusLedgerUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(E,T,W,D,R,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $E$ is the unary precision request whose tolerance selects the tail-radius read;
\item $T$ is the finite tail-window row, including the requested tail threshold and the radius budget attached to that threshold;
\item $W$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window family opened by $E$ and $T$;
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ readback row carrying the dyadic observations and displayed radii for the selected windows;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row reached from the dyadic readback after the tail-radius ledger has been checked;
\item $S$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row available to completion-facing consumers only after $R$ has been displayed;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $E,T,W,D,R,S$ and the structural rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes from precision request to tail window, StreamName windows, dyadic readback, regular-rational handoff, and real seal;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\NameCert$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealObservationBudgetUp$, and $\RealUp$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DyadicTailRadiusLedgerUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The local classifier compares accepted carriers only through the displayed rows $E,T,W,D,R,S,H,C,P,N$.
\end{definition}
