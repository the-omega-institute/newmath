\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CauchyFilterSpaceUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-filter-space-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop--Bridges constructive completion and Kelley filter-space practice, the $\CauchyFilterSpaceUp$ packet records the finite NameCert surface of a Cauchy filter space before a regularization or completion consumer reads it. It sits over the Cauchy filter carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyfilter-namecert}, next to the regularized completion-facing surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-filter-space-namecert}, and routes finite observations through the stream windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet is a human-known completion-facing object: it names the displayed filter-space rows that a completion route may inspect without adding quotient filters, selected limits, countable choice, separated completion, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[Cauchy filter space carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-filter-space-carrier}
A $\CauchyFilterSpaceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
F=(X,B,Q,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the point and basic-neighborhood carrier row, retaining classifier-distinct point and neighborhood names without quotienting them by convergence or host equality.
\item $B$ is the $\CauchyFilterUp$ basis row, exposing finite filter-membership requests, directed refinements, and Cauchy pressure witnesses available to the packet.
\item $Q$ is the filter-space exactness row, tying each accepted Cauchy request to the same point classifier and the same displayed filter-basis rows in $X$ and $B$.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ inspection row for finite observation windows requested by completion-facing consumers.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row obtained from the displayed windows and Cauchy pressure rows, before any real seal is public.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ sealing row reached only after $S,R$ and the filter-space exactness row $Q$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for all carrier, basis, exactness, window, readback, seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay through the same finite route.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyFilterSpaceUp$, $\CauchyFilterUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and local naming rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyFilterSpaceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for quotient filter equality, a selected representative filter, a selected limit point, countable choice, separated completion, or host equality of point names.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy filter space NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-filter-space-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CauchyFilterSpaceUp$ carrier
$$
F=(X,B,Q,S,R,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyFilterSpaceUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following five obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the point/neighborhood carrier $X$, Cauchy filter basis $B$, filter-space exactness row $Q$, stream windows $S$, regular rational readback $R$, real seal $E$, transport $H$, replay $C$, provenance $P$, and local naming $N$;
\item filter-space exactness, requiring every accepted read to use the displayed point classifier and Cauchy filter rows of $X,B,Q$;
\item window and readback stability, requiring finite stream observations, rational readback, and real sealing to move only componentwise through $H$ and to replay through $C$;
\item ledger nonescape, excluding quotient filters, host equality, selected representatives, selected limits, countable choice, separated completion, and ambient real completeness from the packet;
\item completion-facing handoff, exporting only $X,B,Q,S,R,E,H,C,P,N$ to regular Cauchy filter space or completion consumers before those consumers add their own regularity or completion rows.
\end{enumerate}
No other NameCert obligation is available from the Cauchy filter space packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is the projection of \autoref{def:cauchy-filter-space-carrier}. The tuple lists the point/neighborhood carrier, Cauchy filter basis, exactness row, finite stream windows, regular rational readback, Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

Filter-space exactness is carried by $Q$. A consumer first fixes the point classifier in $X$ and the Cauchy filter basis in $B$, then reads finite requests through $Q,S$ before asking for rational or real data.

Window and readback stability are componentwise. The row $H$ transports the displayed coordinates, and the row $C$ replays the same route for consumers, so Cauchy pressure and readback cannot be replaced by host equality or by a detached separated-completion fact.

Ledger nonescape follows from the carrier inventory. There is no coordinate for quotient filter equality, selected representatives, selected limits, countable choice, separated completion, or ambient real completeness.

Finally the completion-facing handoff is only the displayed tuple $X,B,Q,S,R,E,H,C,P,N$. A $\RegularCauchyFilterSpaceUp$ or completion consumer may use that finite filter-space surface as input, but any regularity witness, completion object, quotient, or limit belongs to the consumer packet rather than to this carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy filter space completion handoff]
\label{thm:cauchy-filter-space-completion-handoff}
Let $F=(X,B,Q,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyFilterSpaceUp$ carrier. Every $\RegularCauchyFilterSpaceUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, or $\RealUp$ consumer route exported by $F$ factors through the displayed Cauchy filter basis, filter-space exactness row, stream-window inspection row, rational readback row, real-seal row, componentwise transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows. The route exports no quotient filter, selected limit, countable-choice selector, separated completion, host point equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with any completion-facing read of $F$. By \autoref{def:cauchy-filter-space-carrier}, the only source rows available to that read are the displayed point/neighborhood carrier $X$, Cauchy filter basis $B$, and exactness row $Q$.

The read then passes through the finite observation interface. The stream row $S$ exposes the requested windows, the rational row $R$ supplies the regular readback attached to those windows, and the seal row $E$ is reached only after the same displayed route has been replayed.

The structural rows do not add new completion data. Transport is componentwise through $H$, replay is through $C$, provenance is through $P$, and local naming is through $N$.

Thus a downstream regular Cauchy filter space or completion packet can consume the finite boundary of $F$, but it cannot extract a quotient filter, selected limit, countable-choice selector, separated completion, host point equality, or ambient real-completeness witness from this carrier.
\end{proof}
