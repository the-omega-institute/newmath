\section{The certificate}

\begin{definition}[POSet source specification]
\label{def:poset-source-specification}
The source is a certified preorder carrier plus an antisymmetry field.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[POSet pattern specification]
\label{def:poset-pattern-specification}
The pattern is preorder relation formation with classifier collapse from bidirectional comparison.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[POSet classifier specification]
\label{def:poset-classifier-specification}
The classifier is the preorder carrier classifier.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Prefix antisymmetry]
\label{thm:poset-prefix-antisymmetry}
For the concrete unary history prefix preorder, if \(h\le k\) and \(k\le h\),
then \(h\) and \(k\) are classified the same by the preorder classifier.
\end{theorem}
\leanvariant{BEDC.Derived.PreorderUp.PreorderPrefixLE\_antisymm\_hsame}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the two prefix witnesses. Substituting the first continuation into the
second gives a loop from \(h\) through the appended forward and backward tails.
Right-unit uniqueness and append-empty inversion force the forward tail to be
\(\emp\), so the first continuation reduces to \(\hsame(h,k)\), which is the
classifier specification.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Prefix classifier exactness for concrete POSet]
\label{thm:poset-prefix-classifier-exactness}
For carrier histories $h,k$ in $\mathsf{PreorderCarrier}$, the carrier-aware
classifier relation
$\mathsf{PreorderCarrierClassifierSpec}(\mathsf{PreorderCarrier})(h,k)$ holds
exactly when $\mathsf{PreorderPrefixLE}(h,k)$ and
$\mathsf{PreorderPrefixLE}(k,h)$ both hold.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Assume first that the carrier-aware classifier holds. Its equality content is
$\hsame(h,k)$, and symmetry gives $\hsame(k,h)$. Applying
\autoref{thm:preorder-prefix-le-of-hsame} in the two orientations gives
$\mathsf{PreorderPrefixLE}(h,k)$ and $\mathsf{PreorderPrefixLE}(k,h)$.

Conversely, assume both prefix comparisons and keep the carrier hypotheses for
$h$ and $k$. By \autoref{thm:poset-prefix-antisymmetry}, the opposing
comparisons collapse to $\hsame(h,k)$. Combining this equality content with the
two carrier witnesses reassembles
$\mathsf{PreorderCarrierClassifierSpec}(\mathsf{PreorderCarrier})(h,k)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Prefix interval classifier collapse for concrete POSet]
\label{thm:poset-prefix-interval-classifier-collapse}
For carrier histories $h,k,r$ in $\mathsf{PreorderCarrier}$, if
$\mathsf{PreorderPrefixLE}(h,k)$, $\mathsf{PreorderPrefixLE}(k,r)$, and
$\mathsf{PreorderCarrierClassifierSpec}(\mathsf{PreorderCarrier})(h,r)$ hold,
then both
$\mathsf{PreorderCarrierClassifierSpec}(\mathsf{PreorderCarrier})(h,k)$ and
$\mathsf{PreorderCarrierClassifierSpec}(\mathsf{PreorderCarrier})(k,r)$ hold.

The per-object fields are as follows: the traditional target is interval
collapse for order-convex consumers; the BEDC source is the unary
$\mathsf{BHist}$ prefix order; the carrier is $\mathsf{PreorderCarrier}$; the
classifier is $\mathsf{PreorderCarrierClassifierSpec}$; stability comes from
prefix transitivity and antisymmetry; the ledger records the chains
$h\le k\le r$ and $r\le h\le k$; the core theorem is this interval classifier
collapse; exactness is inherited from
\autoref{thm:poset-prefix-classifier-exactness}; the standard bridge is the
finite unary prefix order; and the status is $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the endpoint classifier
$\mathsf{PreorderCarrierClassifierSpec}(\mathsf{PreorderCarrier})(h,r)$. It
provides carrier data for $h$ and $r$ together with $\hsame(h,r)$. The displayed
comparison $\mathsf{PreorderPrefixLE}(h,k)$ and
\autoref{thm:preorder-prefix-preserves-carrier} give the carrier witness for
$k$.

Symmetry of $\hsame(h,r)$ and \autoref{thm:preorder-prefix-le-of-hsame} give
$\mathsf{PreorderPrefixLE}(r,h)$. Compose this with
$\mathsf{PreorderPrefixLE}(k,r)$ by
\autoref{thm:preorder-prefix-transitivity} to obtain
$\mathsf{PreorderPrefixLE}(k,h)$. Now
\autoref{thm:poset-prefix-antisymmetry} applies to
$\mathsf{PreorderPrefixLE}(h,k)$ and $\mathsf{PreorderPrefixLE}(k,h)$, yielding
$\hsame(h,k)$.

For the right subinterval, compose
$\mathsf{PreorderPrefixLE}(r,h)$ with the displayed comparison
$\mathsf{PreorderPrefixLE}(h,k)$ to get $\mathsf{PreorderPrefixLE}(r,k)$.
$\mathsf{PreorderPrefixLE}(k,r)$ is already available, so
\autoref{thm:poset-prefix-antisymmetry} gives $\hsame(k,r)$.

Finally repackage the two classifier witnesses. Combine the carrier witnesses
for $h$ and $k$ with $\hsame(h,k)$ to obtain
$\mathsf{PreorderCarrierClassifierSpec}(\mathsf{PreorderCarrier})(h,k)$, and
combine the carrier witnesses for $k$ and $r$ with $\hsame(k,r)$ to obtain
$\mathsf{PreorderCarrierClassifierSpec}(\mathsf{PreorderCarrier})(k,r)$.
\end{proof}

Thus \autoref{thm:poset-prefix-classifier-exactness} supplies the exactness
predicate named in item 9 of
\autoref{def:concrete-unary-prefix-poset-instance}.

\begin{theorem}[Concrete $\POSetUp$ naming certificate]
\label{thm:concrete-unary-prefix-poset-namecert}
The tuple of \autoref{def:concrete-unary-prefix-poset-instance} supplies the
source, pattern, classifier, stability, and ledger data of a concrete
$\NameCert$ licensing $\POSetUp$ over
$\mathsf{PreorderCarrier}=\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$, classifier
$\mathsf{PreorderCarrierClassifierSpec}(\mathsf{PreorderCarrier})$, and
relation $\mathsf{PreorderPrefixLE}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier, preorder, relation-congruence, and antisymmetry fields are exactly
the fields certified in \autoref{thm:concrete-unary-prefix-poset-laws}. They
identify the carrier with unary histories, use the carrier-aware preorder
classifier, and close the partial-order law by prefix antisymmetry.

The classifier exactness field is
\autoref{thm:poset-prefix-classifier-exactness}. It identifies the carrier
classifier with the two opposing prefix comparisons required by the concrete
partial-order presentation.

The ledger component records the two comparison witnesses, as specified by
\autoref{def:poset-ledger-policy}. Repacking these source, pattern,
classifier, stability, and ledger components gives the displayed concrete
$\NameCert$ for $\POSetUp$.
\end{proof}

This theorem fits the per-object template as follows: the traditional target is
the finite unary-word prefix partial order; the BEDC source is $\mathsf{BHist}$
restricted to unary histories; the carrier is
$\mathsf{PreorderCarrier}=\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$; the classifier is
$\mathsf{PreorderCarrierClassifierSpec}(\mathsf{PreorderCarrier})$; the
stability obligations are the concrete preorder laws, classifier congruence,
and prefix antisymmetry; the ledger records the two comparison-tail witnesses;
the core theorem is \autoref{thm:concrete-unary-prefix-poset-laws}; the
exactness predicate is \autoref{thm:poset-prefix-classifier-exactness}; the
standard bridge is the finite unary-word initial-segment order; and the status
is $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$. This concrete tuple is the schema instance consumed
by the POSet certificate, so Lean can target the BHist prefix construction
rather than an abstract relation schema.

\begin{theorem}[POSet public prefix closure boundary]
\label{thm:poset-public-prefix-closure-boundary}
For the concrete unary-prefix POSet tuple of
\autoref{def:concrete-unary-prefix-poset-instance}, the public surface is
exactly the following $\mathsf{BHist}$-anchored data:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item carrier $\mathsf{PreorderCarrier}=\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$;
  \item classifier
  $\mathsf{PreorderCarrierClassifierSpec}(\mathsf{PreorderCarrier})$;
  \item relation $\mathsf{PreorderPrefixLE}$.
\end{enumerate}
Its preorder laws, endpoint congruence, antisymmetry, exactness, and ledger
rows are supplied by
\autoref{thm:concrete-unary-prefix-poset-laws},
\autoref{thm:poset-prefix-classifier-exactness}, and
\autoref{thm:concrete-unary-prefix-poset-namecert}. The package introduces no
abstract carrier, abstract classifier, quotient, host equality, or
parameterized order relation.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold the tuple of
\autoref{def:concrete-unary-prefix-poset-instance}. Its carrier coordinate is
$\mathsf{PreorderCarrier}$, which is identified there with
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ inside $\mathsf{BHist}$; its classifier coordinate is
$\mathsf{PreorderCarrierClassifierSpec}(\mathsf{PreorderCarrier})$; and its
relation coordinate is $\mathsf{PreorderPrefixLE}$.

The stability rows come from
\autoref{thm:concrete-unary-prefix-poset-laws}. That theorem supplies the
preorder fields for $\mathsf{PreorderPrefixLE}$, endpoint congruence for the
carrier-aware classifier, and the antisymmetry collapse under that classifier.

The bidirectional-collapse row is
\autoref{thm:poset-prefix-classifier-exactness}. It states that the
carrier-aware classifier is exact for the two opposing prefix comparisons, so
the public exactness field uses no quotient or external equality relation.

Finally, \autoref{thm:concrete-unary-prefix-poset-namecert} repacks the source,
pattern, classifier, stability, and ledger rows into the concrete $\NameCert$.
Since each repacked row names the same carrier, classifier, and prefix
relation, the public $\POSetUp$ surface is anchored in $\mathsf{BHist}$ rather
than in an abstract carrier or parameterized order schema.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[POSet antichain subcarrier certificate]
\label{thm:poset-antichain-subcarrier-certificate}
For a concrete $\POSetUp$ carrier $(C,\sim_C,\le_C)$ over $\mathsf{BHist}$,
define the antichain subcarrier $A_C$ to consist of carried endpoints
$a:\mathsf{BHist}$ such that every carried endpoint $b:\mathsf{BHist}$ with
$a\le_C b$ or $b\le_C a$ collapses to classifier equality $a\sim_C b$. Then
$A_C$ is a displayed BEDC subcarrier: its carrier row is inherited from $C$,
its comparison reads are the two stored $\le_C$ ledger rows, and membership in
$A_C$ is stable under $\hsame$ transport and the classifier-congruence fields
of \autoref{thm:poset-public-prefix-closure-boundary}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the public prefix closure boundary. It exposes the carrier
$\mathsf{PreorderCarrier}$, the carrier classifier, the prefix relation, the
preorder laws, relation congruence, antisymmetry, exactness, and the ledger
rows for comparison witnesses.

To build $A_C$, keep only carried endpoints of the same $\mathsf{BHist}$
carrier and attach the displayed antichain clause. No subset quotient is
formed: the subcarrier membership row is a package field whose inputs are the
carried endpoint, the comparison ledger rows, and the classifier conclusion.

For stability, transport the carried endpoint along $\hsame$ and use the
relation-congruence rows from the $\POSetUp$ certificate to transport any
comparison with a carried target. The antichain clause then returns the
classifier equality at the source, and classifier transport repacks it at the
target endpoint.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[POSet antichain discrete NameCert]
\label{thm:poset-antichain-discrete-namecert}
The relation induced by $\le_C$ on the antichain subcarrier
$A_C$ of \autoref{thm:poset-antichain-subcarrier-certificate} is the discrete
$\POSetUp$ relation. More precisely, for $a,b\in A_C$, a displayed comparison
$a\le_C b$ holds in the induced relation exactly when the carrier classifier
collapses the two endpoints, and the resulting $\NameCert$ has reflexivity
from the inherited preorder row and antisymmetry from the antichain clause.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the induced relation on $A_C$. Its comparison ledger is the same
$\le_C$ ledger stored by the ambient $\POSetUp$ package, but both endpoints now
carry the antichain membership row.

Reflexivity is inherited directly from the preorder component of
\autoref{thm:concrete-unary-prefix-poset-laws}. Thus every endpoint of $A_C$
has the displayed comparison to itself required by the discrete relation.

For antisymmetry, suppose $a,b\in A_C$ and $a\le_C b$. The antichain clause for
$a$ applies to the carried endpoint $b$ and collapses the comparison to
$a\sim_C b$. Conversely, classifier equality transports the reflexive
comparison on $a$ to a comparison with $b$ by the relation-congruence fields,
so the induced order has no non-discrete comparison content.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[POSet antichain functor consumer boundary]
\label{thm:poset-antichain-functor-consumer-boundary}
Any downstream finite-set or functor consumer of the antichain construction may
read only the antichain carrier row of
\autoref{thm:poset-antichain-subcarrier-certificate}, the inherited classifier,
the comparison ledger rows, and the $\NameCert$ provenance of
\autoref{thm:poset-antichain-discrete-namecert}. It may not read a host-level
subset quotient, an external equality relation, or a parameterized order
schema beyond the displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ package rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The construction of $A_C$ is package-local. Its carrier, classifier, relation,
and comparison ledger are inherited rows from the concrete $\POSetUp$
certificate, while the antichain clause is an additional displayed membership
field over carried endpoints.

The discrete $\NameCert$ of
\autoref{thm:poset-antichain-discrete-namecert} repacks those rows into the
consumer-facing order surface. Finite-set consumers therefore see a finite
list of carried endpoints and comparison witnesses; functor consumers see the
same classifier and relation-congruence provenance.

Since every field is read from $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, the comparison
ledger, and $\NameCert$ provenance, the consumer boundary has no access to a
host subset quotient or an unrecorded ambient relation.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[POSet stability certificate]
\label{def:poset-stability-certificate}
The stability certificate consists of:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item all preorder stability fields
  \item antisymmetry: \(a\le b\) and \(b\le a\) imply \(a\sim_C b\)
  \item compatibility of antisymmetry with relation congruence
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[POSet ledger policy]
\label{def:poset-ledger-policy}
Record both comparison derivations when antisymmetry is used.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[\(\POSetUp\) certificate obligations]
\label{def:poset-certificate-obligations}
The carrier, source, pattern, classifier, stability, and ledger data listed above are the obligations required for the interface name \(\POSetUp\).
\end{definition}

\paragraph{Primitive boundary.}
\(\POSetUp\) is not a BEDC primitive. It is admitted only when those obligations are supplied.

\begin{proposition}[$\POSetUp$ forgets to $\PreorderUp$ certificate]
\label{prop:poset-forgets-preorder-certificate}
Let $C$ carry a $\POSetUp(C)$ certificate with classifier $\sim_C$ and relation $\le_C$. If the antisymmetry field and the compatibility data whose only role is to transport that antisymmetry field across relation congruence are omitted, the retained carrier, source, pattern, classifier, preorder stability fields, and ledger form a $\PreorderUp(C)$ certificate. The carrier $C$, classifier $\sim_C$, and relation $\le_C$ are unchanged.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
By \autoref{def:poset-certificate-obligations}, the given certificate supplies carrier, source, pattern, classifier, stability, and ledger data for $\POSetUp$. The construction keeps exactly the coordinates that do not require the partial-order antisymmetry clause.

By \autoref{def:poset-carrier}, a $\POSetUp$ carrier is a preorder setup together with antisymmetry under the carrier classifier. Removing the antisymmetry coordinate leaves the carrier, classifier, and relation data required by \autoref{def:preorder-carrier}. Hence $C$, $\sim_C$, and $\le_C$ are copied without replacement.

By \autoref{def:poset-source-specification}, the source is a certified preorder carrier plus an antisymmetry field. Omitting only the antisymmetry field leaves the source described in \autoref{def:preorder-source-specification}. By \autoref{def:poset-classifier-specification}, the classifier is the preorder carrier classifier, so it is exactly the classifier specified in \autoref{def:preorder-classifier-specification}.

By \autoref{def:poset-pattern-specification}, the pattern is preorder relation formation together with classifier collapse from bidirectional comparison. Retaining the one-direction judgment formation $a\le_C b$ and suppressing only the bidirectional-collapse rule leaves the relational judgment formation of \autoref{def:preorder-pattern-specification}.

By \autoref{def:poset-stability-certificate}, the first stability component is all preorder stability fields. These are precisely the classifier reflexivity, classifier transitivity, preorder reflexivity, preorder transitivity, and relation-congruence rows listed in \autoref{def:preorder-stability-certificate}. The remaining $\POSetUp$ rows, namely antisymmetry and compatibility of antisymmetry with relation congruence, are not obligations for $\PreorderUp$. Relation congruence itself is retained because it belongs to the preorder stability component.

Finally, \autoref{def:preorder-ledger-policy} asks for the compared carrier witnesses and the relation derivation. The $\POSetUp$ ledger of \autoref{def:poset-ledger-policy} adds the paired comparison record only when antisymmetry is used. With that antisymmetry use omitted, the remaining comparison provenance is the preorder ledger for the same relation $\le_C$.

The retained carrier, source, pattern, classifier, stability, and ledger fields are therefore exactly the obligations named in \autoref{def:preorder-certificate-obligations}, giving a $\PreorderUp(C)$ certificate with unchanged carrier, classifier, and relation.
\end{proof}
