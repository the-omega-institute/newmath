\section{Poset root obligation surface}
\label{sec:poset-root-obligation-surface}

\begin{definition}[Poset root obligation surface]\label{def:poset-root-obligation-surface}
The concrete root surface for $\PosetUp$ is the $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet read
from \autoref{def:concrete-unary-prefix-poset-instance}. Its endpoints are
carried unary histories $h,k:\mathsf{BHist}$; its inherited $\PreorderUp$
comparison row is $\mathsf{PreorderPrefixLE}(h,k)$; its classifier row is the
carrier-aware $\hsame$ classifier
$\mathsf{PreorderCarrierClassifierSpec}(\mathsf{PreorderCarrier})(h,k)$; its
partial-order row is the antisymmetry collapse for the two directed prefix
comparisons; and its ledger is the finite $\Cont$ record consisting of the
two comparison-tail witnesses, their carried endpoints, and the resulting
classifier readback.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Poset root antisymmetry obligation]\label{thm:poset-root-antisymmetry-obligation}
For carried unary $\mathsf{BHist}$ endpoints $h$ and $k$ in the surface of
\autoref{def:poset-root-obligation-surface}, the bidirectional inherited
prefix comparison
$$
\mathsf{PreorderPrefixLE}(h,k)\wedge
\mathsf{PreorderPrefixLE}(k,h)
$$
collapses to the carried classifier row
$\mathsf{PreorderCarrierClassifierSpec}(\mathsf{PreorderCarrier})(h,k)$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the two inherited comparison rows from the root surface. They are the
same directed prefix rows used by the concrete unary-prefix preorder package,
and their endpoints are the carried $\mathsf{BHist}$ histories named in
\autoref{def:poset-root-obligation-surface}.

Apply \autoref{thm:poset-prefix-antisymmetry} to the two opposite prefix
comparisons. This gives the $\hsame(h,k)$ classifier content without changing
the endpoint carrier.

Finally reassemble the carrier-aware classifier with the two carried endpoint
witnesses from the root surface. By
\autoref{thm:poset-prefix-classifier-exactness}, this is exactly the
classifier row of the concrete $\PosetUp$ root obligation package.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Poset root certificate readback]\label{thm:poset-root-certificate-readback}
The concrete $\NameCert$ fields for the root surface of
\autoref{def:poset-root-obligation-surface} are read directly from
\autoref{def:concrete-unary-prefix-poset-instance} and
\autoref{thm:concrete-unary-prefix-poset-namecert}. The source is
$\mathsf{BHist}$ restricted to unary histories, the carrier is
$\mathsf{PreorderCarrier}=\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$, the classifier is the
carrier-aware $\hsame$ row, the relation is $\mathsf{PreorderPrefixLE}$, the
antisymmetry and exactness rows are the prefix collapse rows, and the ledger is
the two-comparison finite $\Cont$ record.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack \autoref{def:concrete-unary-prefix-poset-instance}. Its displayed
coordinates name the source, carrier, classifier, relation, stability rows,
exactness row, and comparison ledger used by the root surface.

Apply \autoref{thm:concrete-unary-prefix-poset-namecert}. It repacks those
coordinates as the concrete $\NameCert$ licensing $\POSetUp$ over the same
$\mathsf{BHist}$ carrier and prefix relation.

The antisymmetry component is the row isolated in
\autoref{thm:poset-root-antisymmetry-obligation}. Therefore the root
certificate contains no additional carrier, quotient representative, or
external equality witness beyond the fields already read from the concrete
unary-prefix certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Poset root downstream unblock package]\label{thm:poset-root-downstream-unblock-package}
The root package of \autoref{thm:poset-root-certificate-readback} supplies the
rows a $\TotalOrderUp$ or $\LatticeUp$ consumer may inherit from $\PosetUp$:
carrier, classifier, antisymmetry, exactness, and finite $\Cont$ ledger. A
downstream consumer may append its own total-comparison selector or meet-join
bound rows, but it cannot replace the inherited $\mathsf{BHist}$ carrier,
$\hsame$ classifier, prefix exactness, or antisymmetry ledger.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the certificate readback of
\autoref{thm:poset-root-certificate-readback}. It exposes the carrier,
classifier, relation, stability, exactness, and finite ledger rows of the
concrete root surface.

For a $\TotalOrderUp$ consumer, keep the inherited carrier and classifier and
add only the selector row deciding a directed comparison between displayed
endpoints. The antisymmetry and exactness rows still come from
\autoref{thm:poset-root-antisymmetry-obligation} and
\autoref{thm:poset-prefix-classifier-exactness}.

For a $\LatticeUp$ consumer, keep the same inherited rows and add only the
meet and join bound-characterization rows. The lower-bound and upper-bound
comparisons are ledger entries over the same prefix relation, so their
endpoint collapse still reads through the root $\PosetUp$ certificate.
\end{proof}
