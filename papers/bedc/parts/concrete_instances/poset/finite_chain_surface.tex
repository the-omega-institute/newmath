
\begin{theorem}[POSet finite-chain carrier closure]
\label{thm:poset-finite-chain-carrier-closure}
Let
$$
h=h_0,\ h_1,\ldots,h_n=k
$$
be a finite chain of carried unary-prefix histories in the concrete
$\POSetUp$ source of \autoref{def:concrete-unary-prefix-poset-instance}. If
each adjacent comparison $h_i\leq h_{i+1}$ is recorded by
$\mathsf{PreorderPrefixLE}$ in the finite $\Cont$ ledger, then every endpoint
$h_i$ in the chain remains inside
$\mathsf{PreorderCarrier}=\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$, and the composite comparison
from $h$ to $k$ is again a carried unary-prefix comparison recorded by that
finite ledger.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Argue by induction on the finite chain. The base chain has only the carried
endpoint $h$, so carrier closure is immediate from the displayed source row.

For the step, assume the prefix subchain from $h$ to $h_i$ has already stayed
inside $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ and has produced a carried composite comparison.
The adjacent row $h_i\leq h_{i+1}$ is a displayed
$\mathsf{PreorderPrefixLE}$ comparison between carried histories, so the
prefix transitivity row cited in
\autoref{thm:concrete-unary-prefix-poset-namecert} composes it with the
subchain comparison.

The same finite $\Cont$ ledger records the old subchain, the new adjacent
comparison, and their composite. Thus the next endpoint remains in
$\mathsf{PreorderCarrier}$ and the chain closes inside the concrete unary
prefix carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[POSet finite-chain antisymmetry collapse]
\label{thm:poset-finite-chain-antisymmetry-collapse}
Let $h$ and $k$ be carried unary-prefix histories. If the finite $\Cont$ ledger
records a finite $\mathsf{PreorderPrefixLE}$ chain from $h$ to $k$ and another
finite $\mathsf{PreorderPrefixLE}$ chain from $k$ to $h$, then the two endpoints
collapse to the carrier classifier:
$$
\mathsf{PreorderCarrier}(h)\wedge\mathsf{PreorderCarrier}(k)
\wedge h\leq k\wedge k\leq h
\Longrightarrow \hsame(h,k).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply \autoref{thm:poset-finite-chain-carrier-closure} to the chain from
$h$ to $k$. It gives a carried composite comparison $h\leq k$ recorded by the
finite ledger.

Apply the same closure theorem to the reverse chain from $k$ to $h$. This
gives the carried composite comparison $k\leq h$ over the same unary-prefix
carrier.

Now apply \autoref{thm:poset-prefix-antisymmetry} to the opposing composite
prefix comparisons. The result is $\hsame(h,k)$, and
\autoref{thm:poset-prefix-classifier-exactness} identifies this history
sameness with the carrier classifier on the displayed unary-prefix endpoints.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[POSet finite-chain NameCert surface]
\label{thm:poset-finite-chain-namecert-surface}
The finite-chain carrier closure of
\autoref{thm:poset-finite-chain-carrier-closure} and the finite-chain
antisymmetry collapse of
\autoref{thm:poset-finite-chain-antisymmetry-collapse} package as a
$\POSetUp$ $\NameCert$ obligation surface. Its rows are exactly the concrete
unary-prefix carrier, the $\hsame$ classifier, the
$\mathsf{PreorderPrefixLE}$ relation, prefix stability under finite
composition, the finite $\Cont$ ledger for adjacent and composite comparisons,
and the exactness row of
\autoref{thm:poset-prefix-classifier-exactness}. It adds no quotient carrier,
opposite presentation, totality field, lattice operation, or standard bridge.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the concrete unary-prefix certificate of
\autoref{thm:concrete-unary-prefix-poset-namecert}. It supplies the carrier,
classifier, relation, stability, ledger, and exactness rows for the
$\POSetUp$ surface.

The finite-chain carrier row is supplied by
\autoref{thm:poset-finite-chain-carrier-closure}. Its induction uses only
prefix transitivity and finite $\Cont$ composition already present in the
certificate ledger.

The finite-chain antisymmetry row is supplied by
\autoref{thm:poset-finite-chain-antisymmetry-collapse}. It reduces two finite
chains to opposing composite prefix comparisons, applies
\autoref{thm:poset-prefix-antisymmetry}, and then reads the result through
\autoref{thm:poset-prefix-classifier-exactness}.

Repacking these rows with the source certificate gives a single
$\POSetUp$ NameCert obligation surface. Since every row is drawn from the
concrete unary-prefix packet and its finite ledger, no additional order
structure or bridge datum is exported.
\end{proof}
