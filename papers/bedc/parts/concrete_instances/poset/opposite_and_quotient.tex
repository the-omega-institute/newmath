\begin{theorem}[POSet quotient classifier exhaustion]
\label{thm:poset-quotient-classifier-exhaustion}
In the quotient presentation of \autoref{def:preorder-quotient-presentation},
every classifier witness for quotient classes is generated by the displayed
finite pair of preorder comparisons
$$
x\le_C y
\qquad\text{and}\qquad
y\le_C x.
$$
The quotient carrier, quotient classifier, and comparison ledger are read
through the original $\mathsf{BHist}$ endpoints, the induced $\hsame$ class
row, and the finite $\Cont$ comparison ledger; no abstract carrier-equivalence
transport supplies an additional classifier witness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the quotient presentation of
\autoref{def:preorder-quotient-presentation}. Its class relation is the
bidirectional comparison row on carried representatives, so the displayed pair
$x\le_C y$ and $y\le_C x$ is the only generator of the quotient classifier.

Apply \autoref{thm:preorder-quotient-yields-poset}. The theorem builds the
$\POSetUp$ quotient certificate from that finite comparison pair, the inherited
carrier row, and the induced order on classes. Its antisymmetry clause is
therefore read from the same pair of comparisons, now recorded as the
quotient $\hsame$ class row.

The remaining data are ledger data. The finite $\Cont$ comparison ledger
records exactly the two comparison witnesses and their endpoint support in
$\mathsf{BHist}$. Since neither \autoref{def:preorder-quotient-presentation} nor
\autoref{thm:preorder-quotient-yields-poset} introduces a separate carrier
equivalence field, every quotient classifier witness is exhausted by this
displayed comparison ledger.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[POSet opposite and quotient compatibility]
\label{thm:poset-opposite-quotient-compatibility}
For a $\PreorderUp$ certificate with carrier $C$, classifier $\sim_C$, and
relation $\le_C$, taking the opposite relation before forming the quotient
presentation gives the same quotient classifier and restored comparison ledger
as forming the quotient first and then reversing the induced order. In both
routes, a class witness between representatives $x$ and $y$ is the same finite
pair $x\le_C y$ and $y\le_C x$ with the displayed comparison slots exchanged
twice.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the quotient route. By
\autoref{def:preorder-quotient-presentation}, the quotient classifier is
generated by the pair $x\le_C y$ and $y\le_C x$, and
\autoref{thm:preorder-quotient-yields-poset} records that pair as the
antisymmetry ledger of the induced $\POSetUp$ certificate.

For the opposite-first route, unfold
\autoref{def:opposite-poset-certificate-data}. Reversal exchanges the two
comparison slots, so the quotient classifier for the opposite relation is
generated by $y\le_C x$ and $x\le_C y$. This is the same finite comparison
family with the ordered slots exchanged.

Now reverse the induced quotient order. The data identity of
\autoref{thm:double-opposite-poset-certificate-data-identity} says that a
second reversal restores the carrier, classifier, order relation, and ledger
slots componentwise. Hence the opposite-first quotient and the quotient-first
opposite expose the same quotient classifier and the same restored finite
$\Cont$ comparison ledger, without invoking any abstract carrier-equivalence
transport.
\end{proof}
