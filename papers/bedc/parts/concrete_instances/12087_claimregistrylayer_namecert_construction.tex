\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ClaimRegistryLayerUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-claimregistrylayer-namecert}

The $\ClaimRegistryLayerUp$ packet realizes the registry layer of \autoref{sec:visions-philosophy-machine-interface} as a finite export-control carrier for philosophy-package claims. It sits beside the unit calibration ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-unit-calibration-ledger-namecert} and the external-supply boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-external-supply-boundary-namecert}: those siblings control empirical-value export and external-supply requests, while this packet controls whether a claim is admitted as a package-level exported claim at all.

\begin{definition}[Claim registry layer carrier]
\label{def:claim-registry-layer-carrier}
A $\ClaimRegistryLayerUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(E,R,B,C,F,V,H,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $E$ is the claim-entry row, naming the exported philosophy-package claim, its local subject index, and its intended package surface.
\item $R$ is the refusal-boundary row, recording the registry entry that blocks claims with no accepted export surface.
\item $B$ is the closure-status row, recording the theory-closure and verification axes used by the claim site.
\item $C$ is the formal-target row, recording the Lean target, statement-only site, encoded definition, or absence of a formal target for the same claim.
\item $F$ is the provenance row, tying the claim to the finite source file, section, carrier, theorem, definition, or cannot-claim row from which it is exported.
\item $V$ is the local verification-readback row, recording whether the package claim is read as checked theorem, encoded definition, scoped paper claim, or explicit refusal.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for claim entry, refusal boundary, closure status, formal target, provenance, verification readback, package provenance, and local naming.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\AxisCarryRefusalLedgerUp$, $\RealityConstrainedMethodologyLedgerUp$, $\FiniteObservationResidueBoundaryUp$, $\ExternalSupplyBoundaryUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ClaimRegistryLayerUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for an exported package claim outside the displayed entry, refusal boundary, closure status, formal target, provenance, verification readback, transport, package, and local NameCert rows.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Claim registry layer export control]
\label{thm:claim-registry-layer-export-control}
Every exported philosophy-package claim admitted by $\ClaimRegistryLayerUp$ must display the claim-entry row $E$, refusal-boundary row $R$, closure-status row $B$, formal-target row $C$, provenance row $F$, verification-readback row $V$, componentwise transport $H$, package provenance $P$, and local naming row $N$ in the same finite carrier. A consumer may cite the claim only through that packet.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ClaimRegistryLayerUp.ClaimRegistryLayer\_export\_control\_certificate}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:claim-registry-layer-carrier}. The exported claim is not just the prose entry $E$; it is the full packet $Q=(E,R,B,C,F,V,H,P,N)$.

The registry layer of \autoref{sec:visions-philosophy-machine-interface} supplies the export-control reading: a claim must either appear in a registry entry or be blocked by a registry entry. Rows $E$ and $R$ encode those two possibilities inside the same finite packet.

The remaining rows make the export auditable. Closure status $B$, formal target $C$, provenance $F$, and verification readback $V$ identify the claim's status, while $H,P,N$ preserve and name the displayed rows. Since no other export coordinate exists, the consumer can cite the claim only through the packet above.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ClaimRegistryLayerUp.TasteGate.ClaimRegistryLayer\_export\_control\_certificate}

\begin{theorem}[Claim registry layer no unregistered export]
\label{thm:claim-registry-layer-no-unregistered-export}
An exported philosophy-package claim with no $\ClaimRegistryLayerUp$ entry row and no refusal-boundary row is rejected by the carrier. In particular, a prose claim cannot become a package-level BEDC claim merely by appearing near a theorem, definition, closure-status block, or formal target; it must be registered or refused by the same finite export-control surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with export control, \autoref{thm:claim-registry-layer-export-control}. Any admitted package-level export must be read through $Q=(E,R,B,C,F,V,H,P,N)$.

If both the entry row $E$ and refusal-boundary row $R$ are absent, the packet required by \autoref{def:claim-registry-layer-carrier} is not present. The remaining rows cannot manufacture claim admission because closure status, formal targets, provenance, verification readback, transport, package provenance, and local naming are structural rows for an already displayed entry or refusal.

Thus an unregistered prose claim has no carrier coordinate from which export can be read. It remains outside the package-level claim surface until the registry layer supplies either an entry row or a refusal row.
\end{proof}
