\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\PrecompactMetricUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-precompactmetric-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges constructive analysis, where precompact metric
data is read through explicit finite nets rather than through open-cover
compactness, the $\PrecompactMetricUp$ packet records the finite bridge from
total boundedness toward completion-facing Cauchy filters. It connects the
net surface of $\TotallyBoundedUp$
(\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-totallybounded-namecert}) with the finite
filterbase and regular-filter surfaces of $\CauchyFilterUp$
(\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyfilter-namecert}),
$\RegularCauchyFilterUp$
(\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regularcauchyfilter-namecert}), and
$\CauchyFilterBasisUp$
(\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-filter-basis-namecert}), using
$\RegularityModulusUp$
(\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regularitymodulus-namecert}) as the visible
rate ledger.

\begin{definition}[Precompact metric finite packet carrier]
\label{def:precompact-metric-carrier}
A $\PrecompactMetricUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
P=(X,D,N,F,R,M,H,C,G,Q)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the $\MetricSpaceUp$ carrier row, including the accepted point
  histories and the metric-distance classifier rows used by downstream reads;
\item $D$ records the metric carrier comparison data, so every endpoint,
  distance history, and rational bound used below is transported by displayed
  $\hsame$ rows and by the metric classifier;
\item $N$ assigns to each positive $\RatUp$ tolerance history $\varepsilon$ a
  closed finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ of center names, visible
  $\mathsf{InBundle}$ membership rows, center-carrier rows, distance-witness
  rows, and rational comparison ledgers, as in the totally bounded carrier of
  \autoref{def:totallybounded-probebundle-net-carrier};
\item $F$ is the $\CauchyFilterUp$ inspection row exposing the finite
  filterbase packets, refinement rows, regular Cauchy observation rows,
  dyadic neighbourhood witnesses, and precision-to-tail reads;
\item $R$ is the $\RegularCauchyFilterUp$ inspection row naming the regular
  tail windows and their componentwise regularity checks;
\item $M$ is a $\RegularityModulusUp$ row whose dyadic windows control the
  net tolerance and the Cauchy-filter tail requests through the same displayed
  rate ledger;
\item $H$ is the evidence ledger of componentwise $\hsame$ transports for
  $X,D,N,F,R,M$ and for every center, tail, distance, dyadic radius, and
  regular readback row exposed by those coordinates;
\item $C$ is the finite $\Cont$ route ledger: net reads route from $N$ to
  $X,D,M$, filter reads route from $F,R$ to $M$, and basis reads route through
  the finite generator of $\CauchyFilterBasisUp$ before any completion-facing
  consumer can inspect them;
\item $G$ is the handoff row saying that a $\TotallyBoundedUp$ consumer reads
  only the finite net rows in $N$, the metric rows in $X,D$, and the modulus
  row in $M$;
\item $Q$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance and local $\NameCert$ row naming the
  source boundaries $\MetricSpaceUp$, $\RatUp$, $\TotallyBoundedUp$,
  $\CauchyFilterUp$, $\RegularCauchyFilterUp$, $\CauchyFilterBasisUp$, and
  $\RegularityModulusUp$.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier on such packets is componentwise transport of these ten rows
through $H$ and the displayed $\Cont$ routes in $C$. No host finite set,
selected sequence, quotient completion, ambient complete space, or choice
operator is a coordinate of the carrier.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[PrecompactMetricUp NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:precompact-metric-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\PrecompactMetricUp}$ surface of
\autoref{def:precompact-metric-carrier} consists exactly of five obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission: the ten displayed rows
  $P=(X,D,N,F,R,M,H,C,G,Q)$ are present and every row cited by a consumer is
  one of their finite subrows;
\item classifier transport: packet equality is componentwise $\hsame$
  transport of metric endpoints, center histories, filterbase tails, regular
  observations, dyadic radii, modulus windows, provenance rows, and local
  naming rows, with every read following a displayed $\Cont$ route;
\item finite-net exactness: for each positive tolerance history,
  \autoref{def:totallybounded-probebundle-net-carrier} supplies the closed
  $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, membership constructors, center rows, distance
  witnesses, and rational comparison ledgers recorded in $N$;
\item Cauchy-filter inspection: the rows $F$ and $R$ expose only finite
  filterbase packets, regular tail windows, refinement ledgers, regular
  Cauchy readbacks, and dyadic neighbourhood witnesses, with the rate read
  controlled by $M$;
\item provenance and handoff: $Q$ names the source certificates and $G$ states
  that every total-boundedness or completion-facing consumer reaches the
  packet through the displayed finite net, filter, regularity, and modulus
  rows.
\end{enumerate}
These obligations define a constructive precompact metric packet as a
finite-row interface between total boundedness and Cauchy-filter completion
data.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is \autoref{def:precompact-metric-carrier}. It lists the
metric row, finite-net row, Cauchy-filter inspection row, regular-filter
inspection row, modulus row, transport ledger, route ledger, handoff row,
provenance row, and local naming row.

Classifier transport is also read from the carrier. The evidence ledger $H$
moves each displayed history by $\hsame$, while $C$ records the continuation
route used by each net, filter, basis, and modulus read. Thus two packets
classify together only when the same finite rows can be replayed after
transport.

Finite-net exactness is supplied by the $\TotallyBoundedUp$ component named in
$N$. The packet keeps the closed $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ spine and its
$\mathsf{InBundle}$ constructors visible, so a consumer never replaces the
net by an ambient finite set of representatives.

Cauchy-filter inspection is the combined role of $F$, $R$, and $M$. The
filter rows expose finite tails, refinements, regular Cauchy observations,
dyadic neighbourhoods, and regularity windows; the modulus row supplies the
shared rate ledger that connects a net tolerance with a later Cauchy-tail
request.

Finally, $Q$ and $G$ package the same rows as the local $\NameCert$ surface.
They do not add a completed point, a quotient real, or a selected subsequence;
they merely state which source certificates and which finite rows may be read
by downstream consumers.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PrecompactMetricUp totally bounded handoff]
\label{thm:precompact-metric-totally-bounded-handoff}
Every $\TotallyBoundedUp$ consumer of an accepted $\PrecompactMetricUp$ packet
$P=(X,D,N,F,R,M,H,C,G,Q)$ reads only the displayed finite net, modulus, and
filter-inspection rows:
$$
X,D,N,M,H,C,G,Q .
$$
In particular, the consumer may inspect the metric carrier, the closed
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ nets, the $\mathsf{InBundle}$ membership rows, the
center histories, the $\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}$ rows, the rational
bound ledgers, the $\RegularityModulusUp$ windows, the componentwise $\hsame$
transports, the displayed $\Cont$ routes, and the provenance row, but it
cannot read an open-cover compactness proof, a selected subsequence, a
quotient completion, a host choice function, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the handoff coordinate $G$ from
\autoref{def:precompact-metric-carrier}. It explicitly names the
$\TotallyBoundedUp$ read route through the metric rows $X,D$, the finite-net
row $N$, the modulus row $M$, the transport ledger $H$, the continuation
ledger $C$, and the provenance row $Q$.

Now replay the route. A total-boundedness request first reads the accepted
metric carrier $X$ and its classifier data $D$. It then selects a positive
tolerance history and follows $C$ into the finite net row $N$, where the
closed $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, membership constructors, center rows, distance
witnesses, and rational comparisons are displayed.

The only rate information available to the consumer is the
$\RegularityModulusUp$ row $M$. The same row also governs the later
$\CauchyFilterUp$ and $\RegularCauchyFilterUp$ inspection coordinates, but
those coordinates are read only as finite filterbase and regular-tail
evidence; they do not expose a completed object.

Therefore the handoff is exhausted by the displayed finite rows
$X,D,N,M,H,C,G,Q$. The excluded objects are absent from the carrier
coordinates, absent from the route ledger, and absent from the provenance row,
so they cannot be produced by this local NameCert read.
\end{proof}
