\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\PrecompactMetricUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-precompactmetric-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges constructive analysis, where precompact metric
data is read through explicit finite nets rather than through open-cover
compactness, the $\PrecompactMetricUp$ packet records the finite bridge from
total boundedness toward completion-facing Cauchy filters. It connects the
net surface of $\TotallyBoundedUp$
(\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-totallybounded-namecert}) with the finite
filterbase and regular-filter surfaces of $\CauchyFilterUp$
(\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyfilter-namecert}),
$\RegularCauchyFilterUp$
(\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regularcauchyfilter-namecert}), and
$\CauchyFilterBasisUp$
(\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-filter-basis-namecert}), using
$\RegularityModulusUp$
(\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regularitymodulus-namecert}) as the visible
rate ledger.

\begin{definition}[Precompact metric finite packet carrier]
\label{def:precompact-metric-carrier}
A $\PrecompactMetricUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
P=(X,D,N,F,R,M,H,C,G,Q)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the $\MetricSpaceUp$ carrier row, including the accepted point
  histories and the metric-distance classifier rows used by downstream reads;
\item $D$ records the metric carrier comparison data, so every endpoint,
  distance history, and rational bound used below is transported by displayed
  $\hsame$ rows and by the metric classifier;
\item $N$ assigns to each positive $\RatUp$ tolerance history $\varepsilon$ a
  closed finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ of center names, visible
  $\mathsf{InBundle}$ membership rows, center-carrier rows, distance-witness
  rows, and rational comparison ledgers, as in the totally bounded carrier of
  \autoref{def:totallybounded-probebundle-net-carrier};
\item $F$ is the $\CauchyFilterUp$ inspection row exposing the finite
  filterbase packets, refinement rows, regular Cauchy observation rows,
  dyadic neighbourhood witnesses, and precision-to-tail reads;
\item $R$ is the $\RegularCauchyFilterUp$ inspection row naming the regular
  tail windows and their componentwise regularity checks;
\item $M$ is a $\RegularityModulusUp$ row whose dyadic windows control the
  net tolerance and the Cauchy-filter tail requests through the same displayed
  rate ledger;
\item $H$ is the evidence ledger of componentwise $\hsame$ transports for
  $X,D,N,F,R,M$ and for every center, tail, distance, dyadic radius, and
  regular readback row exposed by those coordinates;
\item $C$ is the finite $\Cont$ route ledger: net reads route from $N$ to
  $X,D,M$, filter reads route from $F,R$ to $M$, and basis reads route through
  the finite generator of $\CauchyFilterBasisUp$ before any completion-facing
  consumer can inspect them;
\item $G$ is the handoff row saying that a $\TotallyBoundedUp$ consumer reads
  only the finite net rows in $N$, the metric rows in $X,D$, and the modulus
  row in $M$;
\item $Q$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance and local $\NameCert$ row naming the
  source boundaries $\MetricSpaceUp$, $\RatUp$, $\TotallyBoundedUp$,
  $\CauchyFilterUp$, $\RegularCauchyFilterUp$, $\CauchyFilterBasisUp$, and
  $\RegularityModulusUp$.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier on such packets is componentwise transport of these ten rows
through $H$ and the displayed $\Cont$ routes in $C$. No host finite set,
selected sequence, quotient completion, ambient complete space, or choice
operator is a coordinate of the carrier.
\end{definition}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.PrecompactMetricUp.PrecompactMetricTasteGate\_single\_carrier\_alignment}

\begin{theorem}[PrecompactMetricUp NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:precompact-metric-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\PrecompactMetricUp}$ surface of
\autoref{def:precompact-metric-carrier} consists exactly of five obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission: the ten displayed rows
  $P=(X,D,N,F,R,M,H,C,G,Q)$ are present and every row cited by a consumer is
  one of their finite subrows;
\item classifier transport: packet equality is componentwise $\hsame$
  transport of metric endpoints, center histories, filterbase tails, regular
  observations, dyadic radii, modulus windows, provenance rows, and local
  naming rows, with every read following a displayed $\Cont$ route;
\item finite-net exactness: for each positive tolerance history,
  \autoref{def:totallybounded-probebundle-net-carrier} supplies the closed
  $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, membership constructors, center rows, distance
  witnesses, and rational comparison ledgers recorded in $N$;
\item Cauchy-filter inspection: the rows $F$ and $R$ expose only finite
  filterbase packets, regular tail windows, refinement ledgers, regular
  Cauchy readbacks, and dyadic neighbourhood witnesses, with the rate read
  controlled by $M$;
\item provenance and handoff: $Q$ names the source certificates and $G$ states
  that every total-boundedness or completion-facing consumer reaches the
  packet through the displayed finite net, filter, regularity, and modulus
  rows.
\end{enumerate}
These obligations define a constructive precompact metric packet as a
finite-row interface between total boundedness and Cauchy-filter completion
data.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.PrecompactMetricUp.PrecompactMetricNameCert\_obligations}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is \autoref{def:precompact-metric-carrier}. It lists the
metric row, finite-net row, Cauchy-filter inspection row, regular-filter
inspection row, modulus row, transport ledger, route ledger, handoff row,
provenance row, and local naming row.

Classifier transport is also read from the carrier. The evidence ledger $H$
moves each displayed history by $\hsame$, while $C$ records the continuation
route used by each net, filter, basis, and modulus read. Thus two packets
classify together only when the same finite rows can be replayed after
transport.

Finite-net exactness is supplied by the $\TotallyBoundedUp$ component named in
$N$. The packet keeps the closed $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ spine and its
$\mathsf{InBundle}$ constructors visible, so a consumer never replaces the
net by an ambient finite set of representatives.

Cauchy-filter inspection is the combined role of $F$, $R$, and $M$. The
filter rows expose finite tails, refinements, regular Cauchy observations,
dyadic neighbourhoods, and regularity windows; the modulus row supplies the
shared rate ledger that connects a net tolerance with a later Cauchy-tail
request.

Finally, $Q$ and $G$ package the same rows as the local $\NameCert$ surface.
They do not add a completed point, a quotient real, or a selected subsequence;
they merely state which source certificates and which finite rows may be read
by downstream consumers.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PrecompactMetricUp finite-net root obligations]
\label{thm:precompactmetric-root-finite-net-obligations}
For every accepted $\PrecompactMetricUp$ packet $P=(X,D,N,F,R,M,H,C,G,Q)$, the root finite-net obligations are exactly the carrier admission of $X,D,N,M,H,C,G,Q$, the $\TotallyBoundedUp$ finite-net rows in $N$, the $\RegularityModulusUp$ tolerance ledger in $M$, and the provenance row $Q$. A downstream total-boundedness consumer may read the closed $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ nets, $\mathsf{InBundle}$ memberships, center histories, metric-distance witness rows, and rational comparison ledgers only through those displayed coordinates.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.PrecompactMetricUp.PrecompactMetricCarrier\_root\_finite\_net\_obligations}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:precompact-metric-carrier}. The finite-net coordinate is $N$, and the only metric, tolerance, transport, route, handoff, provenance, and naming rows available with it are $X,D,M,H,C,G,Q$.

Apply \autoref{thm:precompact-metric-namecert-obligations}. Its finite-net exactness clause identifies the $\TotallyBoundedUp$ net surface with the closed $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, membership constructors, center rows, distance witnesses, and rational comparison ledgers already recorded in $N$.

The same theorem's carrier-admission, classifier-transport, and provenance clauses keep the read inside $X,D,N,M,H,C,G,Q$. Thus root finite-net obligations expose total-boundedness data as finite rows and do not export open-cover compactness, selected subsequences, quotient completion, host choice, or ambient completeness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PrecompactMetricUp Cauchy-filter handoff obligations]
\label{thm:precompactmetric-cauchy-filter-handoff-obligations}
For every accepted $\PrecompactMetricUp$ packet $P=(X,D,N,F,R,M,H,C,G,Q)$, every Cauchy-filter or regular-Cauchy-filter handoff reads $F$ and $R$ only after the metric rows $X,D$, finite-net row $N$, regularity-modulus row $M$, transport row $H$, continuation row $C$, handoff row $G$, and provenance row $Q$ have fixed the same packet. The handoff reaches the $\CauchyFilterUp$, $\RegularCauchyFilterUp$, and $\CauchyFilterBasisUp$ sibling surfaces as finite inspection rows, not as a completed point or quotient filter.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from \autoref{def:precompact-metric-carrier}. The rows $F$ and $R$ are the only Cauchy-filter and regular-filter inspection coordinates, while $C$ records that basis reads route through the finite generator of $\CauchyFilterBasisUp$.

Use \autoref{thm:precompact-metric-namecert-obligations}. Its Cauchy-filter inspection clause says that $F$ and $R$ expose finite filterbase packets, regular tail windows, refinement ledgers, regular Cauchy readbacks, and dyadic neighbourhood witnesses, all controlled by $M$.

The carrier-admission and provenance clauses force the same packet rows $X,D,N,F,R,M,H,C,G,Q$ to remain visible for the handoff. Therefore no downstream consumer can detach a completed point, a quotient filter, a selected subsequence, or ambient completeness from the Cauchy-filter route.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.PrecompactMetricUp.PrecompactMetric\_cauchy\_filter\_handoff\_obligations}

\begin{theorem}[PrecompactMetricUp filter-net modulus handoff]
\label{thm:precompactmetric-filter-net-modulus-handoff}
For every accepted $\PrecompactMetricUp$ packet $P=(X,D,N,F,R,M,H,C,G,Q)$, a completion-facing handoff that consumes both finite nets and Cauchy-filter tails must read the finite-net coordinate $N$, the Cauchy-filter coordinates $F,R$, and the $\RegularityModulusUp$ coordinate $M$ inside the same packet before it can expose any basis row. The handoff is replayed only through $H,C,G,Q$, so the finite-net radius, filter tail, and modulus window are one displayed NameCert route rather than a quotient completion, selected subsequence, host finite set, or ambient complete space.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the packet $P=(X,D,N,F,R,M,H,C,G,Q)$. The row $N$ is the only finite-net coordinate, $F$ and $R$ are the only Cauchy-filter and regular-Cauchy-filter coordinates, and $M$ is the only visible regularity-modulus coordinate.

Use \autoref{thm:precompactmetric-root-finite-net-obligations} for the root net surface. It identifies the readable net data with the closed $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ rows, $\mathsf{InBundle}$ memberships, center histories, metric-distance witnesses, and rational comparison ledgers stored in $N$.

Apply \autoref{thm:precompactmetric-cauchy-filter-handoff-obligations} to the filter side. It keeps every filterbase packet, regular tail window, refinement ledger, regular Cauchy readback, and dyadic neighbourhood witness inside $F,R$ and under the same modulus row $M$.

The remaining rows $H,C,G,Q$ provide transport, replay, handoff, and provenance. Since these rows mention only the displayed carrier coordinates, the combined handoff exports no quotient completion, selected subsequence, host finite set, or ambient complete space.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.PrecompactMetricUp.PrecompactMetric\_filter\_net\_modulus\_handoff}

\begin{theorem}[PrecompactMetricUp completion consumer non-escape]
\label{thm:precompactmetric-completion-consumer-nonescape}
Let $P=(X,D,N,F,R,M,H,C,G,Q)$ be an accepted $\PrecompactMetricUp$ packet. Any completion-facing consumer that reads $P$ can inspect only the metric rows $X,D$, finite-net row $N$, Cauchy-filter rows $F,R$, regularity-modulus row $M$, transport and route rows $H,C$, handoff row $G$, and provenance row $Q$. In particular, such a consumer cannot obtain a quotient completion point, a selected convergent subsequence, an external finite set of centers, a host complete-space witness, or a choice operator from the packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with carrier admission in \autoref{def:precompact-metric-carrier}. The carrier has exactly the ten coordinates $X,D,N,F,R,M,H,C,G,Q$, so every admissible consumer read must name one of those rows or a finite subrow displayed by them.

The finite-net part is controlled by \autoref{thm:precompactmetric-root-finite-net-obligations}. It exposes centers and distance witnesses only as $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ and $\mathsf{InBundle}$ data recorded in $N$, not as an external host finite set.

The filter part is controlled by \autoref{thm:precompactmetric-cauchy-filter-handoff-obligations}. It exposes Cauchy-filter and regular-filter data only through $F,R$ with the shared modulus row $M$, not through a completed point, quotient filter, or selected subsequence.

Finally $H,C,G,Q$ only transport, replay, hand off, and name these same displayed rows. Therefore no completion-facing consumer can escape from the finite NameCert surface to a quotient completion, host finite set, selected sequence, ambient complete space, or choice operator.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PrecompactMetricUp regular filter finite-net route]
\label{thm:precompactmetric-regular-filter-finite-net-route}
For every accepted $\PrecompactMetricUp$ packet $P=(X,D,N,F,R,M,H,C,G,Q)$, the regular-filter route from finite nets to completion-facing basis data is the displayed path
$$
N \longrightarrow M \longrightarrow F,R \longrightarrow C,G,Q
$$
inside the fixed metric context $X,D,H$. The route first reads the finite net row, then the regularity-modulus row, then the Cauchy-filter and regular-Cauchy-filter rows, and finally the continuation, handoff, and provenance rows; it does not pass through quotient completion, selected subsequence, host finite-set enumeration, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the metric context first. The rows $X,D,H$ fix the metric carrier, metric comparison data, and componentwise transport for every later net, filter, and modulus read.

Next use the finite-net theorem \autoref{thm:precompactmetric-root-finite-net-obligations}. It makes $N$ the displayed source of the finite centers, membership rows, distance witnesses, and rational comparison ledgers used by the route.

Then use the modulus and filter handoff theorems \autoref{thm:precompactmetric-regularity-modulus-scope} and \autoref{thm:precompactmetric-filter-net-modulus-handoff}. The row $M$ scopes the tolerance request shared by $N$ and the filter rows, while $F,R$ expose only finite filterbase and regular-tail inspection data.

The route terminates through $C,G,Q$. These rows replay the path, hand it to the relevant total-boundedness or completion-facing consumer, and record provenance without adding quotient completion, a selected subsequence, host finite-set enumeration, or ambient completeness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PrecompactMetricUp regularity-modulus scope]
\label{thm:precompactmetric-regularity-modulus-scope}
For every accepted $\PrecompactMetricUp$ packet $P=(X,D,N,F,R,M,H,C,G,Q)$, the regularity-modulus row $M$ is scoped simultaneously over finite-net tolerance requests in $N$ and Cauchy-filter tail requests in $F,R$. The scope is transported through $H$, replayed through $C$, handed to total-boundedness consumers through $G$, and named by $Q$; it does not introduce an independent convergence rate, ambient compactness theorem, choice-selected tail, or completed-space coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read $M$ from \autoref{def:precompact-metric-carrier}. The carrier says that its dyadic windows control both the net tolerance and the Cauchy-filter tail requests through one displayed rate ledger.

Apply \autoref{thm:precompact-metric-namecert-obligations}. Finite-net exactness uses $M$ with $N$, and Cauchy-filter inspection uses $M$ with $F$ and $R$; the provenance and handoff clauses keep the same row available to $G$ and $Q$.

Finally use classifier transport from the same theorem. The row $H$ transports modulus windows componentwise and $C$ records the replay route, so the modulus scope is local to $P=(X,D,N,F,R,M,H,C,G,Q)$ and exports no independent rate object or completed ambient space.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PrecompactMetricUp totally bounded handoff]
\label{thm:precompact-metric-totally-bounded-handoff}
Every $\TotallyBoundedUp$ consumer of an accepted $\PrecompactMetricUp$ packet
$P=(X,D,N,F,R,M,H,C,G,Q)$ reads only the displayed finite net, modulus, and
filter-inspection rows:
$$
X,D,N,M,H,C,G,Q .
$$
In particular, the consumer may inspect the metric carrier, the closed
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ nets, the $\mathsf{InBundle}$ membership rows, the
center histories, the $\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}$ rows, the rational
bound ledgers, the $\RegularityModulusUp$ windows, the componentwise $\hsame$
transports, the displayed $\Cont$ routes, and the provenance row, but it
cannot read an open-cover compactness proof, a selected subsequence, a
quotient completion, a host choice function, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.PrecompactMetricUp.PrecompactMetric\_totally\_bounded\_handoff}
\begin{proof}
Project the handoff coordinate $G$ from
\autoref{def:precompact-metric-carrier}. It explicitly names the
$\TotallyBoundedUp$ read route through the metric rows $X,D$, the finite-net
row $N$, the modulus row $M$, the transport ledger $H$, the continuation
ledger $C$, and the provenance row $Q$.

Now replay the route. A total-boundedness request first reads the accepted
metric carrier $X$ and its classifier data $D$. It then selects a positive
tolerance history and follows $C$ into the finite net row $N$, where the
closed $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, membership constructors, center rows, distance
witnesses, and rational comparisons are displayed.

The only rate information available to the consumer is the
$\RegularityModulusUp$ row $M$. The same row also governs the later
$\CauchyFilterUp$ and $\RegularCauchyFilterUp$ inspection coordinates, but
those coordinates are read only as finite filterbase and regular-tail
evidence; they do not expose a completed object.

Therefore the handoff is exhausted by the displayed finite rows
$X,D,N,M,H,C,G,Q$. The excluded objects are absent from the carrier
coordinates, absent from the route ledger, and absent from the provenance row,
so they cannot be produced by this local NameCert read.
\end{proof}
