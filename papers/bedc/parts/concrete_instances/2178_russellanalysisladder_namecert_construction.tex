\begin{theorem}[RussellAnalysisLadder NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:russell-analysis-ladder-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\RussellAnalysisLadderUp}$ surface consists of five finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item description exposure: every accepted carrier exposes the description row $D$ together with the displayed obligations that license name use;
\item contextual-class locality: every class-like read factors through the context row $Q$ and does not export a class object;
\item relation-first classifier stability: every classifier read factors through the relation row $A$ and is transported only by the displayed $\hsame$ data;
\item stratification accountability: every cross-level use is routed through $S$, $C$, $P$, and $N$ rather than through a level collapse;
\item witnessed descent: every downward consumer of an upward name presents the evidence row $W$ and the local naming row $N$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier exposure is projection from the finite tuple of \autoref{def:russell-analysis-ladder-carrier}. The rows $D,Q,A,S,W,H,C,P,N$ are the only data admitted to the packet.

The contextual and relation-first obligations are read from $Q$ and $A$. A consumer of a class-like phrase must pass through the displayed context, and a consumer of the name's behaviour must pass through the relation row before obtaining any object-like public use.

Stratification and witnessed descent are then read from $S$ and $W$. The only routes available to a consumer are the displayed $\Cont$ rows in $C$, transported through $H$ and recorded by the provenance and local naming rows $P$ and $N$.

Since the carrier has no coordinate beyond these rows, the certificate cannot export a primitive class, unanalysed description, unary replacement of the relation, level collapse, or unwitnessed downward use.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RussellAnalysisLadder description factorization]
\label{thm:russell-analysis-ladder-description-factorization}
Every public read of a description in an accepted $\RussellAnalysisLadderUp$ carrier factors through the finite rows $D,Q,A,S,W,H,C,P,N$. In particular, an apparent denoting phrase is consumed only after the source description, classifying context, relation row, stratification row, witnessed-descent evidence, transport rows, continuation routes, provenance row, and local naming row have been displayed.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the carrier of \autoref{def:russell-analysis-ladder-carrier}. The description row $D$ is present only together with $Q,A,S,W,H,C,P,N$.

Apply the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:russell-analysis-ladder-namecert-obligations}. Description exposure gives $D$, contextual-class locality routes class-like uses through $Q$, and relation-first classifier stability routes behavioural uses through $A$.

The remaining public read is accountable only through $S$ and $W$. Transport and continuation rows $H$ and $C$ record how the consumer reaches the displayed name, while $P$ and $N$ record the package and local certificate.

Thus the denoting phrase is not a primitive coordinate. Its public use factors through the complete finite packet.
\end{proof}
