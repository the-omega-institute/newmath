The $\HaltingDistinctionTraceUp$ surface refines the halting-as-distinction interface of \autoref{ch:visions-halting-as-form-of-distinction-limit} to the finite trace actually inspected by a certificate-level diagonal reader. It records the visible continuation segment, the distinction classifier row, the self-application row, and the finite certificate routes through which the fixed-point obstruction is read.

The trace packet consumes the broader $\HaltingDistinctionUp$ certificate-history pair and diagonal obstruction surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-haltingdistinction-namecert}, while restricting the read to the finite trace rows $A,T,D,E,H,C,P,N$.

\begin{definition}[HaltingDistinctionTrace carrier]
\label{def:halting-distinction-trace-carrier}
A $\HaltingDistinctionTraceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
R=(A,T,D,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ is the admitted certificate-history input row, naming the program certificate and the history at which its visible behavior is read;
\item $T$ is the finite $\Cont$ trace row, listing the displayed marks and histories available before any halting-facing distinction is asserted;
\item $D$ is the distinction classifier row, reading only whether the trace exposes the displayed termination mark or remains inside the finite open trace;
\item $E$ is the diagonal self-application row, asking the classifier in $D$ about the same certificate-history row named by $A$;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for input, trace, distinction, diagonal, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item $C$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ routes for replaying $T$, entering $D$, reaching $E$, and returning to the local ledger;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, $\Ext$, $\Cont$, $\hsame$, $\Pkg$, the distinction row, the diagonal row, and the surrounding $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\HaltingDistinctionTraceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no total halting predicate, no global truth predicate, no divergence detector, no host evaluator, and no classifier outside the displayed rows of $R$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[HaltingDistinctionTrace diagonal obstruction package]
\label{thm:halting-distinction-diagonal-obstruction-package}
For an accepted $\HaltingDistinctionTraceUp$ carrier $R=(A,T,D,E,H,C,P,N)$, every certificate-level diagonal read of the halting-as-distinction obstruction factors through the finite route
$$
A \longmapsto T \longmapsto D \longmapsto E \longmapsto H \longmapsto C \longmapsto P \longmapsto N.
$$
The route may expose that the classifier row $D$ is queried at its own certificate-history input through $E$, but it never exports a total halting predicate, a global truth predicate, a divergence detector, or a host-level evaluator. Thus the fixed-point obstruction is realized as finite $\Cont$, $\Ext$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.HaltingDistinctionTraceUp.HaltingDistinctionTraceDiagonalObstructionPackage}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier of \autoref{def:halting-distinction-trace-carrier}. The only rows that can name the input, the visible execution segment, the distinction classifier, and the diagonal query are $A,T,D,E$.

Replay of the trace is controlled by the $\Cont$ routes in $C$. These routes inspect the displayed marks and histories in $T$ before the distinction row $D$ is read, so a consumer cannot replace the finite trace by an ambient evaluator.

The diagonal point is the row $E$. Componentwise transport is supplied by $H$, while $P$ records provenance and $N$ records the local naming certificate. These rows can identify the self-application site, but they do not add a truth predicate or a total decision principle.

Therefore every diagonal read remains inside the displayed route through $A,T,D,E,H,C,P,N$. The obstruction is the finite certificate-level self-query itself, carried by $\Cont$, $\Ext$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ data rather than by any global halting classifier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[HaltingDistinctionTrace diagonal readback]
\label{thm:halting-distinction-trace-diagonal-readback}
For an accepted $\HaltingDistinctionTraceUp$ carrier $R=(A,T,D,E,H,C,P,N)$, the diagonal row $E$ can be read only after the finite trace row $T$, the distinction row $D$, and the continuation route $C$ have been displayed. Thus the packet exposes a finite certificate-level self-query, not a direct global halting classifier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the carrier of \autoref{def:halting-distinction-trace-carrier}. The diagonal row $E$ is a displayed coordinate of the same finite packet as $A,T,D,H,C,P,N$, and the carrier has no coordinate for a global classifier.

Apply the diagonal obstruction package of \autoref{thm:halting-distinction-diagonal-obstruction-package}. It factors every diagonal read through the finite route from the admitted input to the trace row, then to the distinction row, then to the diagonal self-application row.

The surrounding $\HaltingDistinctionUp$ surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-haltingdistinction-namecert} supplies the distinction context, but the trace packet keeps the read inside $T,D,E,C$. Therefore a consumer reads the diagonal row only through the displayed finite trace route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[HaltingDistinctionTrace provenance non-escape]
\label{thm:halting-distinction-trace-provenance-nonescape}
For an accepted $\HaltingDistinctionTraceUp$ carrier $R=(A,T,D,E,H,C,P,N)$, the provenance row $P$ and local $\NameCert_{\HaltingDistinctionTraceUp}$ row $N$ package only the displayed rows $A,T,D,E,H,C$. \begin{proof}
Read $P$ and $N$ in the carrier definition. Their provenance and naming scope is the finite packet $A,T,D,E,H,C,P,N$, with $P$ naming the displayed BEDC objects and $N$ naming the local certificate.

Use \autoref{thm:halting-distinction-trace-diagonal-readback}. The only diagonal information available to $P,N$ is the self-query route through the finite trace, distinction row, and continuation route.

Finally apply the non-export clause of \autoref{thm:halting-distinction-diagonal-obstruction-package}. \end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[HaltingDistinctionTrace scoped consumer factorization]

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

Use diagonal readback and provenance non-escape. They place every diagonal self-query behind the finite trace row $T$, the distinction row $D$, and the continuation route $C$, and they keep provenance and local naming inside the displayed packet.

Now insert the surrounding $\HaltingDistinctionUp$ context through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-haltingdistinction-namecert}. That context supplies the certificate-history and distinction surface read by $A,D,E$, but the trace packet exposes it only through those displayed rows. Hence every consumer read factors through $A,T,D,E,H,C,P,N$ and cannot import global halting content.

\begin{theorem}[HaltingDistinctionTrace scoped transport non-escape]
\label{thm:halting-distinction-trace-scoped-transport-nonescape}

If the componentwise $\hsame$ rows in $H$ and $H'$ identify the admitted input, finite trace, distinction classifier, diagonal self-application, replay, provenance, and local naming rows, then transport preserves the route from trace to distinction to diagonal self-query. The transported packet still exports no total halting predicate, truth predicate, divergence detector, or host evaluation row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the componentwise transport rows. Their scope is exactly the displayed coordinates $A,T,D,E,C,P,N$ of the two accepted trace packets, with no coordinate for an external evaluator or global classifier.

Apply scoped consumer factorization to both packets. Each consumer route first reads the admitted input and finite trace, then the distinction classifier and diagonal self-application, and finally the transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

The $\hsame$ rows preserve those coordinates component by component. Therefore the transported read has the same trace, distinction, and diagonal route as the original packet. Since neither packet contains total halting, truth, divergence, or host-evaluation coordinates, transport cannot export them.
\end{proof}
