It consumes the visible real-and-completion primitives $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ without forming quotient reals, selecting limits by choice, or appealing to host completeness. The finite completion transformer reads the window discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the dyadic observation ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the regular-rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the seal boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}.

\begin{definition}[CauchyCompletionMonad carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-completion-monad-carrier}
A $\CauchyCompletionMonadUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
M=(X,W,D,\mu,\Delta,E,L,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the source regular-Cauchy family over $\RegSeqRatUp$;
\item $W$ records the finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows inspected from the members of $X$;
\item $D$ records the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observations read through the windows in $W$;
\item $\mu$ is a unary modulus and schedule row naming which family member and which window answer each requested precision;
\item $\Delta$ is the diagonal transport ledger that repacks the scheduled observations as a regular rational readback;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal-facing row attached to that diagonal readback;
\item $L$ records the componentwise $\hsame$ comparisons for source-family rows, windows, dyadic observations, schedule rows, diagonal rows, and seal rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes through which consumers reach the schedule, window, observation, diagonal, and real-seal coordinates;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, and the local dependency certificates;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyCompletionMonadUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through these finite coordinates and their displayed transports.
\end{definition}
