\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CauchyCompletionMonadUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-cauchycompletionmonad-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\CauchyCompletionMonadUp$ interface names the finite completion transformer that reads regular rational Cauchy data and returns a $\RealUp$ seal route. It consumes the visible real-and-completion primitives $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ without forming quotient reals, selecting limits by choice, or appealing to host completeness.

\begin{definition}[CauchyCompletionMonad carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-completion-monad-carrier}
A $\CauchyCompletionMonadUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
M=(X,W,D,\mu,\Delta,E,L,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the source regular-Cauchy family over $\RegSeqRatUp$;
\item $W$ records the finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows inspected from the members of $X$;
\item $D$ records the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observations read through the windows in $W$;
\item $\mu$ is a unary modulus and schedule row naming which family member and which window answer each requested precision;
\item $\Delta$ is the diagonal transport ledger that repacks the scheduled observations as a regular rational readback;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal-facing row attached to that diagonal readback;
\item $L$ records the componentwise $\hsame$ comparisons for source-family rows, windows, dyadic observations, schedule rows, diagonal rows, and seal rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes through which consumers reach the schedule, window, observation, diagonal, and real-seal coordinates;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, and the local dependency certificates;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyCompletionMonadUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through these finite coordinates and their displayed transports.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyCompletionMonad NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-monad-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\CauchyCompletionMonadUp}$ obligation surface consists of five rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier habitation by constant regular rational data, using constant $\RegSeqRatUp$ rows, stationary $\StreamNameUp$ windows, constant $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observations, the zero schedule, the diagonal readback of that stationary packet, and the corresponding $\RealUp$ seal row;
\item classifier transport for schedule rows, window rows, dyadic observation rows, diagonal rows, and seal rows by componentwise $\hsame$ and the displayed $\Cont$ routes;
\item diagonal handoff exactness: each real-facing precision read factors through the schedule row, the selected finite window, the displayed dyadic observation, and the diagonal readback row before reaching the $\RealUp$ seal;
\item non-escape of quotient reals, host real equality, choice-selected limits, ambient completeness principles, or hidden stream representatives;
\item public consumer boundary for $\RealUp$ completion reads, exposing only $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ coordinates.
\end{enumerate}
These obligations specify the finite completion-interface surface and do not assert that $\RealUp$ is complete as an ambient real object.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For habitation, take a constant regular rational family. Its displayed stream windows are stationary, every dyadic observation is the same rational row, the unary schedule may select the initial member for each precision request, and the diagonal ledger repacks the same rows as the real-facing readback.

Classifier transport is coordinatewise. Transport the schedule, selected windows, dyadic observations, diagonal ledger, and real-seal row along the stored $\hsame$ comparisons, then compose with the displayed $\Cont$ read routes for the same coordinates.

For diagonal handoff exactness, project the carrier coordinates $X$, $W$, $D$, $\mu$, $\Delta$, and $E$. A consumer precision request first reads $\mu$, then the chosen window in $W$, then the dyadic observation in $D$, and only then the diagonal readback $\Delta$ and seal row $E$.

The non-escape and public-boundary rows follow from the same projection. Since the packet contains no coordinate for a quotient real, host equality proof, selected limit, or ambient completeness theorem, a public $\RealUp$ read cannot export such data.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyCompletionMonad unit boundary]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-monad-unit-boundary}
For every displayed rational row accepted by $\RegSeqRatUp$, the constant regular-rational family determines a $\CauchyCompletionMonadUp$ unit packet. Its $\StreamNameUp$ windows are stationary, its $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observations are the constant rational observations, its schedule row selects the constant family member, its diagonal readback is the same regular-rational row, and its $\RealUp$ seal row is reached through that constant packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Choose the constant regular-rational family described in the habitation row of \autoref{thm:cauchy-completion-monad-namecert-obligations}. The finite windows and dyadic observations are stationary because every requested precision reads the same rational source row.

The schedule row may select the displayed constant member at each request. The diagonal ledger then repacks the same finite observations as a regular-rational readback, without creating a new limit object.

Finally attach the $\RealUp$ seal row after the diagonal readback. The carrier coordinates of \autoref{def:cauchy-completion-monad-carrier} contain exactly these rows, so the unit packet is a visible finite BEDC packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyCompletionMonad diagonal-bind stability]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-monad-diagonal-bind-stability}
Let $M$ and $M'$ be accepted $\CauchyCompletionMonadUp$ packets. If their source-family rows, schedules, finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observation rows, diagonal readbacks, and $\RealUp$ seal rows are transported componentwise by displayed $\hsame$ routes and read through the corresponding $\Cont$ routes, then the diagonal-bind classifier is stable: every consumer precision read of $M$ and $M'$ follows matched schedule, window, dyadic-observation, diagonal-readback, and seal coordinates.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold the classifier of \autoref{def:cauchy-completion-monad-carrier}. It compares packets through the finite coordinates $X$, $W$, $D$, $\mu$, $\Delta$, $E$, $L$, $C$, $P$, and $N$ and their displayed transports.

Move the source-family row, schedule, selected windows, dyadic observations, diagonal ledger, and seal row along the stored $\hsame$ comparisons. The $\Cont$ coordinates keep the same read order from a precision request to the schedule, then to the window and observation rows.

Apply the diagonal handoff row of \autoref{thm:cauchy-completion-monad-namecert-obligations}. It forces each real-facing read through the transported schedule, finite window, dyadic observation, and diagonal readback before the seal, so the classifier remains stable under the componentwise transport.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyCompletionMonad public Real seal factorization]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-monad-public-real-seal-factorization}
Every public $\RealUp$ read of an accepted $\CauchyCompletionMonadUp$ packet factors through the schedule row, the selected finite $\StreamNameUp$ window, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observation row, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ diagonal readback, and the final seal row. The packet exports no quotient real, selected limit, host real equality, ambient completeness theorem, or hidden stream representative.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from a public precision request. The carrier makes the schedule row $\mu$ the first coordinate that can choose a family member and window for that request.

The chosen window is read from $W$, its dyadic observation from $D$, and the corresponding regular-rational readback from $\Delta$. This is exactly the diagonal handoff route stated in \autoref{thm:cauchy-completion-monad-namecert-obligations}.

The $\RealUp$ seal row $E$ is reached only after that route. Since the carrier has no coordinate for quotient reals, selected limits, host equality, completeness, or hidden representatives, the listed finite rows exhaust every public real-seal factorization.
\end{proof}
