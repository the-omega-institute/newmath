Following Bishop and Bridges' constructive treatment of completions by regular Cauchy data, the $\BishopCompletionUniversalCompositionUp$ packet records the finite composition surface for two Bishop completion universal-property extensions. It consumes the boundary route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bishop-completion-universal-boundary-namecert} and the extension-plus-uniqueness ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completionuniversalproperty-namecert}. The shared source rows are the $\StreamNameUp$ windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the terminal $\RealUp$ seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}.

The packet composes two already displayed extension ledgers. It does not select representatives of quotient streams, choose host limits, use countable choice, or appeal to ambient completeness of $\RealUp$.

\begin{definition}[Bishop completion universal composition carrier]
\label{def:bishop-completion-universal-composition-carrier}
A $\BishopCompletionUniversalCompositionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
C=(U,V,E,F,R,S,D,H,T,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $U$ is the first $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$ ledger, restricted to the shared Bishop regular Cauchy completion source;
\item $V$ is the second $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$ ledger, whose dense-source read begins at the extension surface exported by $U$;
\item $E$ is the first extension row exposed by $U$ through the Bishop completion universal boundary;
\item $F$ is the second extension row exposed by $V$ after $E$ has fixed the intermediate completion-facing map;
\item $R$ is the shared $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row used by both extension ledgers;
\item $S$ is the shared $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row for the source regular Cauchy name;
\item $D$ is the shared $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger used to align the two precision schedules;
\item $H$ records the terminal $\RealUp$ seal reached by the source route and preserved by both extensions;
\item $T$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport and displayed $\Cont$ replay for $U,V,E,F,R,S,D,H$;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BishopCompletionUniversalBoundaryUp$, $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\BishopCompletionUniversalCompositionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier compares accepted packets only through the displayed extension ledgers, shared source rows, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming data. It contains no quotient representative, selected limit, countable-choice schedule, host real equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness row.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Bishop completion universal composition obligations]
\label{thm:bishop-completion-universal-composition-obligations}
For every accepted $\BishopCompletionUniversalCompositionUp$ carrier $C=(U,V,E,F,R,S,D,H,T,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\BishopCompletionUniversalCompositionUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission, first-extension exactness through $U,E$, second-extension exactness through $V,F$, shared-source exactness through $S,R,D,H$, componentwise transport and replay through $T$, provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.

The two extension reads must share the same $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, and $\RealUp$ rows before the composed extension row is exported. No local obligation can be discharged by quotient representatives, selected limits, countable choice, host equality of extensions, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:bishop-completion-universal-composition-carrier}. The visible coordinates are exactly the two universal-property ledgers, their two extension rows, the shared source and seal rows, and the structural rows $T,P,N$.

Use the Bishop completion universal boundary route for the first extension. It requires the source windows, regular readback, dyadic tolerance ledger, and terminal Real seal to be displayed before the extension row $E$ can be read.

Use the CompletionUniversalProperty obligations for both ledgers. The first ledger exports $E$ from $U$ over the shared source surface; the second ledger exports $F$ from $V$ only after the same source and seal rows have been transported and replayed through $T$.

The package and local naming rows then record the composed route as a single NameCert surface. Since the carrier has no quotient representative, selected-limit coordinate, choice schedule, host equality row, or ambient-completeness field, none of these can discharge an obligation.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Bishop completion universal composition route]
\label{thm:bishop-completion-universal-composition-route}
Let $C=(U,V,E,F,R,S,D,H,T,P,N)$ be an accepted $\BishopCompletionUniversalCompositionUp$ carrier. Every composed completion-universal read factors through the finite route
$$
S,R,D,H \longmapsto U,E \longmapsto T \longmapsto V,F \longmapsto P,N .
$$
The first leg is the Bishop completion universal boundary extension route, the second leg is the CompletionUniversalProperty extension-and-uniqueness route over the transported intermediate extension, and the handoff between the legs is only the displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ replay stored in $T$.

Consequently the composed read exports a finite NameCert packet over the shared source rows. It does not export quotient representatives, selected limits, countable choice, host equality of maps, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the shared source rows. The $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, and $\RealUp$ coordinates $S,R,D,H$ are the only source and seal data carried by $C$.

Apply the Bishop completion universal boundary extension route to $U$ and $E$. This gives the first extension only after the shared finite windows, regular rational readback, dyadic tolerance ledger, and terminal Real seal have been displayed.

Next apply the CompletionUniversalProperty extension-uniqueness scope to $V$ and $F$. The intermediate extension is available to the second ledger only through the transported and replayed row $T$, so uniqueness is read as a displayed ledger comparison rather than as host function equality.

Finally package the composed route with $P$ and $N$. The carrier inventory has no quotient stream, selected limit, countable-choice schedule, host equality row, or ambient completeness theorem, so the route cannot leave the finite NameCert surface.
\end{proof}
