\begin{theorem}[Sum history classifier branch partition]
\label{thm:sum-history-classifier-branch-partition}
For branch carriers $Left,Right:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$, branch
classifiers $LeftEq,RightEq:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$,
and histories $h,k$, the history-level sum classifier is exactly the visible
left-or-right constructor analysis:
$$
\mathsf{SumHistoryClassifier}(Left,Right,LeftEq,RightEq,h,k)
\Longleftrightarrow
\bigl(\exists l,l'.\;
\hsame(h,\Ezero(l))\land
\hsame(k,\Ezero(l'))\land
LeftEq(l,l')\bigr)
\lor
\bigl(\exists r,r'.\;
\hsame(h,\Eone(r))\land
\hsame(k,\Eone(r'))\land
RightEq(r,r')\bigr).
$$
In the ten-field fit, the traditional target is disjoint-sum branch readback,
the BEDC source is $\mathsf{BHist}$, the classifier is
$\mathsf{SumHistoryClassifier}$, the ledger records the visible tag plus
payload, and the status is $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the forward direction, case-analyze the classifier witness. The left
constructor supplies the two $\Ezero$ endpoint links and the stored $LeftEq$
payload relation, yielding the left disjunct. The right constructor supplies
the two $\Eone$ endpoint links and the stored $RightEq$ payload relation,
yielding the right disjunct.

For the reverse direction, split the displayed disjunction. In the left branch,
unpack $l,l'$ together with the two visible left endpoint witnesses and the
stored $LeftEq(l,l')$ witness, then rebuild the left classifier constructor.
In the right branch, unpack $r,r'$ together with the two visible right endpoint
witnesses and the stored $RightEq(r,r')$ witness, then rebuild the right
classifier constructor.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Sum history classifier carrier-aware branch partition]
\label{thm:sum-history-classifier-carrier-aware-branch-partition}
Let $Left,Right:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be source carriers, let
$LeftEq,RightEq:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be source
classifiers. Assume source-carrier closure under history sameness:
$$
\forall x\,y.\; Left(x)\to \hsame(x,y)\to Left(y)
$$
and
$$
\forall x\,y.\; Right(x)\to \hsame(x,y)\to Right(y).
$$
If $\mathsf{SumHistoryCarrier}(Left,Right,h)$,
$\mathsf{SumHistoryCarrier}(Left,Right,k)$, and
$\mathsf{SumHistoryClassifier}(Left,Right,LeftEq,RightEq,h,k)$ hold, then a
visible classifier side supplies the branch data used by the carrier-backed
certificate analysis:
$$
\begin{array}{l}
\exists a,b.\; Left(a)\land Left(b)\land
\hsame(h,\Ezero(a))\land \hsame(k,\Ezero(b))\land LeftEq(a,b)
\end{array}
$$
or
$$
\begin{array}{l}
\exists a,b.\; Right(a)\land Right(b)\land
\hsame(h,\Eone(a))\land \hsame(k,\Eone(b))\land RightEq(a,b).
\end{array}
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Split the displayed $\mathsf{SumHistoryClassifier}$ witness into its visible
left or right constructor branch. In the left branch, let the classifier
payloads be $a,b$. Compose the carrier endpoint for $h$ with the classifier
endpoint $\hsame(h,\Ezero(a))$ and apply
$\mathsf{SumHistoryCarrier\_e0\_inversion}$ to obtain a source payload $l$,
$Left(l)$, and a payload link $\hsame(l,a)$; the same argument at $k$ gives
$l'$, $Left(l')$, and $\hsame(l',b)$. The left source-carrier closure turns
these into $Left(a)$ and $Left(b)$. Together with the stored $LeftEq(a,b)$
witness and the two classifier endpoint links, these data give the left
disjunct. In the right branch, apply the analogous $\Eone$ endpoint
composition and $\mathsf{SumHistoryCarrier\_e1\_inversion}$ argument to obtain
$Right(a)$ and $Right(b)$ from the right source-carrier closure, then pair them
with the stored $RightEq(a,b)$ witness and the two right endpoint links.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Sum history source weakening]
\label{thm:sum-history-source-weakening}
Let $Left,Right,Left',Right':\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be source
carriers, and let
$LeftEq,RightEq,LeftEq',RightEq':\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{BHist}\to
\mathsf{Prop}$ be source classifiers. The two refinements have separate
premises. Source-carrier inclusion gives carrier refinement:
$$
\bigl((\forall h.\; Left(h)\to Left'(h))\land
(\forall h.\; Right(h)\to Right'(h))\bigr)
\to
\bigl(\mathsf{SumHistoryCarrier}(Left,Right,h)\to
\mathsf{SumHistoryCarrier}(Left',Right',h)\bigr).
$$
Component-relation weakening gives classifier refinement:
$$
\bigl((\forall a\,b.\; LeftEq(a,b)\to LeftEq'(a,b))\land
(\forall a\,b.\; RightEq(a,b)\to RightEq'(a,b))\bigr)
\to
\bigl(\mathsf{SumHistoryClassifier}(Left,Right,LeftEq,RightEq,h,k)\to
\mathsf{SumHistoryClassifier}(Left',Right',LeftEq',RightEq',h,k)\bigr).
$$
In the ten-field fit, the traditional target is disjoint-sum stability under
source refinement, the BEDC source is the two $\mathsf{BHist}$ carriers, the
carrier is $\mathsf{SumHistoryCarrier}$, the component classifier is
$\mathsf{SumHistoryClassifier}$, the stability datum is source inclusion plus
component-relation weakening, the ledger keeps the visible tag unchanged, and
the core theorem separates carrier weakening from classifier weakening. The
classifier part uses only component-relation weakening because source
membership is not a field of $\mathsf{SumHistoryClassifier}$. Exactness is
preserved by the same endpoint witnesses, the standard bridge is the
$\mathsf{inl}$ and $\mathsf{inr}$ restriction, and the status is
$\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the carrier claim, assume the displayed pair of source-carrier inclusions
and split the $\mathsf{SumHistoryCarrier}$ witness into its left and right
constructor cases. The endpoint history witness is reused unchanged, while the
matching source-carrier inclusion maps the payload membership proof into the
refined source carrier. For the classifier claim, assume the displayed pair of
component-relation weakenings and split the $\mathsf{SumHistoryClassifier}$
witness into its left and right constructor cases. Reuse the same two visible
endpoints and apply only the matching component-relation weakening hypothesis
to the stored $LeftEq$ or $RightEq$ witness. Source membership is absent from
$\mathsf{SumHistoryClassifier}$, so no source-carrier inclusion is needed for
the classifier paragraph.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Sum history source equivalence]
\label{thm:sum-history-source-equivalence}
Let $Left,Right,Left',Right':\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be source
carriers, and let
$LeftEq,RightEq,LeftEq',RightEq':\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{BHist}\to
\mathsf{Prop}$ be source classifiers. Assume source-carrier equivalence
$$
\forall h.\; Left(h)\leftrightarrow Left'(h)
$$
and
$$
\forall h.\; Right(h)\leftrightarrow Right'(h),
$$
and assume component-relation equivalence
$$
\forall a\,b.\; LeftEq(a,b)\leftrightarrow LeftEq'(a,b)
$$
and
$$
\forall a\,b.\; RightEq(a,b)\leftrightarrow RightEq'(a,b).
$$
Then the sum carrier and classifier are invariant under the equivalent source
presentation:
$$
(\forall h.\; \mathsf{SumHistoryCarrier}(Left,Right,h)\leftrightarrow
\mathsf{SumHistoryCarrier}(Left',Right',h))
\land
(\forall h\,k.\; \mathsf{SumHistoryClassifier}(Left,Right,LeftEq,RightEq,h,k)
\leftrightarrow
\mathsf{SumHistoryClassifier}(Left',Right',LeftEq',RightEq',h,k)).
$$
In the ten-field fit, the traditional target is disjoint-sum
source-presentation invariance, the BEDC source is the two $\mathsf{BHist}$
carriers, the carrier is $\mathsf{SumHistoryCarrier}$, the classifier is
$\mathsf{SumHistoryClassifier}$, the stability datum is source and relation
equivalence, the ledger keeps the visible tag unchanged, and the core theorem is
carrier and classifier equivalence. Exactness uses the unchanged branch
witnesses, the standard bridge is $\mathsf{inl}$ and $\mathsf{inr}$ readback,
and the status is $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:sum-history-source-weakening} from left to right using the
forward halves of the two source-carrier equivalences for the carrier clause
and the forward halves of the two component-relation equivalences for the
classifier clause. This gives the implications from $(Left,Right,LeftEq,RightEq)$
to $(Left',Right',LeftEq',RightEq')$, with the same visible tag and the same
branch witnesses. Apply the same weakening theorem from right to left using the
reverse halves of the four equivalence hypotheses. Pair the two carrier
implications and pair the two classifier implications.
\end{proof}
