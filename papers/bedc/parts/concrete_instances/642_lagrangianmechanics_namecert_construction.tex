\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\LagrangianMechanicsUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-lagrangianmechanics-namecert}

The $\LagrangianMechanicsUp$ interface names the finite mechanics surface
whose action ledger can be consumed by Euler--Lagrange and Noether-facing
certificates. Following Arnold's \emph{Mathematical Methods of Classical
Mechanics} and Abraham--Marsden's \emph{Foundations of Mechanics}, the
standard mathematical story starts from configurations, velocities, an action
functional, admissible variations, endpoint policy, Euler--Lagrange equations,
symplectic flow, and conserved-current reads. The BEDC packet below records
only the finite rows needed to cite that surface.

\begin{definition}[LagrangianMechanicsUp BHist carrier]
\label{def:lagrangianmechanics-bhist-carrier}
A $\LagrangianMechanicsUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
L=(Q,V,A,R,E,G,S,J,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $Q$ is the configuration row naming the finite position or field entries
available to the local mechanics packet;
\item $V$ is the velocity row naming the displayed tangent or rate entries
paired with $Q$;
\item $A$ is the action-functional row recording the finite Lagrangian
expression, integration route, scalar read, and dependency certificates used
to evaluate action values;
\item $R$ is the variation row naming the admissible finite variation family
and the routes through which the first action variation is read;
\item $E$ is the endpoint row recording fixed, vanishing, or exposed endpoint
terms;
\item $G$ is the Euler--Lagrange row that receives the residual equation
surface obtained from $A$, $R$, and $E$;
\item $S$ is the symplectic-flow row naming the finite phase-flow read
available after the action ledger has selected the displayed dynamics;
\item $J$ is the Noether-current row naming the finite conserved-current read
available to a Noether-facing consumer;
\item $H$ records the $\hsame$ provenance rows for configuration, velocity,
action, variation, endpoint, Euler--Lagrange, symplectic-flow, and
Noether-current data;
\item $C$ records the $\Cont$ provenance routes by which the displayed rows
are read;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance row naming the action, variation,
Euler--Lagrange, symplectic, and Noether dependency certificates used by the
packet;
\item $N$ is the $\NameCert$ provenance row registering the packet under
$\LagrangianMechanicsUp$.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate is
$\mathsf{LagrangianMechanicsPacket}(Q,V,A,R,E,G,S,J,H,C,P,N)$. Two accepted
packets classify together only when the twelve displayed rows transport
componentwise by the $\hsame$ witnesses and the same $\Cont$ routes named in
$C$ and $P$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[LagrangianMechanicsUp action ledger obligation]
\label{thm:lagrangianmechanics-action-ledger-obligation}
Every accepted $\LagrangianMechanicsUp$ carrier exposes a finite action ledger
consisting exactly of the configuration row $Q$, velocity row $V$,
action-functional row $A$, variation row $R$, endpoint row $E$, and the
displayed $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ provenance rows that
connect them. A consumer may read the Lagrangian expression, scalar action
value, admissible variation family, and endpoint policy only through those
rows.

The ledger asserts no minimizer existence, no global solution theory, no
ambient smooth manifold of curves, and no unrecorded regularity condition.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.LagrangianMechanicsUp.LagrangianMechanicsPacket\_action\_ledger\_obligation}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of
\autoref{def:lagrangianmechanics-bhist-carrier}. The coordinates $Q$, $V$,
$A$, $R$, and $E$ are the only rows that store the displayed configuration,
velocity, action, variation, and endpoint data.

The transport and continuation coordinates $H$ and $C$ specify how these rows
move under $\hsame$ and how they are read by consumers. The package and name
certificate rows $P$ and $N$ record provenance, but they do not add analytic
objects beyond the visible packet.

Thus the action ledger is exhausted by the listed finite rows. Any minimizer,
global dynamics theorem, smooth host manifold, or hidden regularity principle
would require an additional coordinate absent from the carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LagrangianMechanicsUp Euler-Lagrange interface]
\label{thm:lagrangianmechanics-eulerlagrange-interface}
For an accepted carrier $L=(Q,V,A,R,E,G,S,J,H,C,P,N)$, the
Euler--Lagrange-facing interface is the single residual-equation row $G$
together with the action, variation, endpoint, transport, continuation, and
provenance rows that feed it. An $\EulerLagrangeUp$ consumer may read only the
equation surface displayed by $G$ and the finite action ledger named in
\autoref{thm:lagrangianmechanics-action-ledger-obligation}.

The interface does not claim variational completeness, integration by parts in
a host calculus, boundary-condition completeness, or equivalence with a
standard analytic equation model.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.LagrangianMechanicsUp.LagrangianMechanicsPacket\_eulerlagrange\_interface}
\begin{proof}
Start from the action ledger supplied by
\autoref{thm:lagrangianmechanics-action-ledger-obligation}. The variation row
$R$ and endpoint row $E$ determine which first-variation terms remain visible
to the packet.

The coordinate $G$ records the residual equation surface obtained from those
rows. Since $G$ is a finite row of the same carrier, its reads are constrained
by the displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ provenance in $H$ and $C$.

No coordinate stores an ambient variational calculus, a boundary completeness
principle, or a standard analytic equation model. Therefore the
Euler--Lagrange-facing interface is exactly the finite row $G$ together with
the action ledger that feeds it.
\end{proof}

The boundary exports no symmetry classification, no conservation-law
completeness theorem, no Hamiltonian equivalence, and no standard bridge to a

\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.LagrangianMechanicsUp.LagrangianMechanicsPacket\_noether\_consumer\_boundary}
\begin{proof}
Read the accepted packet through
\autoref{def:lagrangianmechanics-bhist-carrier}. Its Noether-facing

symplectic-flow row, the current row, and the four provenance rows.

Apply \autoref{thm:lagrangianmechanics-action-ledger-obligation} to identify

only Euler--Lagrange residual read.

The remaining rows $S$ and $J$ are finite ledgers for phase-flow and current

\end{proof}
