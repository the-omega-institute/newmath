\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\MetricCompletionReflectorUp$}

Following Bourbaki, Kelley, and Bishop--Bridges on metric and uniform completion, the $\MetricCompletionReflectorUp$ packet records the metric-specialized reflector surface for the open L10 completion route. It sits after the metric-completion carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metriccompletion-namecert}, the uniform-completion route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformcompletion-namecert}, the separated metric boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separatedmetric-namecert}, and the broader reflector surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchycompletionreflector-namecert}. The packet exposes completion reflection through finite metric, uniform, separated, Cauchy-reflector, real-seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local NameCert rows without importing quotient completion, selected limits, host equality, countable choice, ambient completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.

\begin{definition}[MetricCompletionReflector carrier]
\label{def:metric-completion-reflector-carrier}
A $\MetricCompletionReflectorUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
R=(M,U,S,K,A,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the $\MetricCompletionUp$ row. It records the displayed metric-completion source, Cauchy evidence, completion-facing reads, and local object boundary inspected by the reflector.
\item $U$ is the $\UniformCompletionUp$ comparison row. It records the uniform completion route that agrees with the metric completion on the displayed entourages and Cauchy data.
\item $S$ is the $\SeparatedMetricUp$ row. It supplies the zero-distance uniqueness boundary used before any completed read may be treated as reflector-stable.
\item $K$ is the $\CauchyCompletionReflectorUp$ row. It records the unit, counit, idempotent replay, universal-property, and reflection surface from which the metric-specialized reflector is read.
\item $A$ is the real-seal handoff row. It records the terminal $\RealUp$-facing windows reached by metric-completion consumers only after $M,U,S,K$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for metric completion, uniform completion, separated metric evidence, the Cauchy reflector row, the real-seal handoff, replay, provenance, and local naming.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the route
$$
M,U,S,K \longmapsto A \longmapsto H,P,N .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\MetricCompletionReflectorUp$, $\MetricCompletionUp$, $\UniformCompletionUp$, $\SeparatedMetricUp$, $\CauchyCompletionReflectorUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MetricCompletionReflectorUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate admits exactly these nine rows. Its classifier compares accepted packets only through $M,U,S,K,A,H,C,P,N$ and has no coordinate for quotient completion, selected representatives, host equality, countable choice, ambient completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[MetricCompletionReflector NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:metric-completion-reflector-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\MetricCompletionReflectorUp$ carrier
$$
R=(M,U,S,K,A,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\MetricCompletionReflectorUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the metric-completion row, uniform-completion comparison row, separated metric boundary, Cauchy reflector row, real-seal handoff, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item metric-reflector source exactness, requiring every reflector-facing read to project $M$ before the uniform comparison row or Cauchy reflector row can be consumed;
\item uniform compatibility, requiring $U$ to compare only the displayed metric entourages and Cauchy data already available through $M$;
\item separated uniqueness, requiring $S$ before any completed read is reused by the reflector as a stable metric-completion object;
\item reflector route compatibility, requiring $K$ to supply the unit, counit, idempotent replay, universal-property, and reflection reads over the same $M,U,S,A$ surface;
\item classifier transport, requiring $\hsame$ comparison to preserve the metric, uniform, separated, Cauchy-reflector, real-seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows componentwise;
\item non-escape, excluding quotient completion, selected limits, host equality, countable choice, ambient completeness, Lean verification, and bridge checking.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored outside those rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:metric-completion-reflector-carrier}. The accepted packet exposes exactly the metric-completion, uniform-completion, separated metric, Cauchy-reflector, real-seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

Metric-reflector source exactness starts at $M$. A consumer cannot inspect the uniform comparison row $U$ or the Cauchy reflector row $K$ until the metric-completion source and completion-facing route in $M$ have been displayed.

Uniform compatibility is confined to $U$. It compares the displayed metric entourages and Cauchy data already named by $M$, so it cannot introduce a separate host uniform completion or a selected completed point.

Separated uniqueness is supplied by $S$. The reflector may reuse a completed read only after the separated metric boundary has classified the zero-distance evidence visible in the same packet.

Finally $K,A,H,C,P,N$ preserve the same finite route. The Cauchy reflector row supplies unit, counit, idempotent, universal-property, and reflection faces over $M,U,S,A$, while the structural rows transport, replay, source, and name only displayed coordinates. The excluded host principles have no carrier row, so the listed obligations exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetricCompletionReflector real-seal handoff]
\label{thm:metric-completion-reflector-real-seal-handoff}
Let
$$
R=(M,U,S,K,A,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\MetricCompletionReflectorUp$ carrier. Every real-facing reflector consumer reaches the terminal seal row $A$ only through the finite route
$$
M\longmapsto U,S\longmapsto K\longmapsto A\longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
The handoff specializes the broader $\CauchyCompletionReflectorUp$ surface to the displayed metric-completion and separated-metric rows. It does not export quotient completion, selected limits, host equality, countable choice, ambient completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with an accepted carrier $R$. The metric-completion row $M$ is the only completion-object entry point, so every real-facing reflector consumer first reads the displayed metric-completion route.

The uniform and separated rows are then read as constraints on the same completion data. The row $U$ supplies the uniform-completion comparison over the displayed entourages, and $S$ supplies the separated metric boundary before reuse of a completed read.

The Cauchy reflector row $K$ consumes those rows as its metric-specialized input. Its unit, counit, idempotent replay, universal-property, and reflection faces are therefore attached to the same $M,U,S$ surface rather than to a detached quotient object.

The real-seal row $A$ is downstream of this reflector route. Componentwise transport by $H$, displayed replay by $C$, provenance by $P$, and local naming by $N$ preserve that order, so no excluded host-side principle is exported.
\end{proof}
