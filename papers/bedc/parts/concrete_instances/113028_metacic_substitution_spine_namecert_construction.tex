\begin{definition}[MetaCIC substitution spine carrier]
\label{def:metacic-substitution-spine-carrier}
A $\MetaCICSubstitutionSpineUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(T,V,D,Q,R,L,A,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $T$ is the closed MetaCIC term row, listing the displayed source, payload, and boundary terms.
\item $V$ is the de Bruijn variable ledger for the finite indices inspected by shift and substitution.
\item $D$ is the cutoff-depth row recording the boundary at which closedness is tested.
\item $Q$ is the closedness classifier row for the source and payload terms.
\item $R$ is the closed shift row, exposed only when the closedness classifier has already been read.
\item $L$ is the closed substitution row, exposed only through the same closedness classifier and depth row.
\item $A$ is the closed composition audit row, recording the checked closed-fragment composition and swap surfaces.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for terms, indices, depth, closedness, shift, substitution, composition, replay, provenance, and local naming.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay for closed substitution requests through the same rows.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\MetaCICSubstitutionSpineUp$, $\ClosedTermSubstitutionBoundaryUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MetaCICSubstitutionSpineUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only through $T,V,D,Q,R,L,A,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for unrestricted substitution algebra, typing preservation, subject reduction, host syntax equality, quotient terms, hidden evaluator state, or compiler correctness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC substitution spine NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:metacic-substitution-spine-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\MetaCICSubstitutionSpineUp$ carrier $S=(T,V,D,Q,R,L,A,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\MetaCICSubstitutionSpineUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for displayed terms, de Bruijn indices, cutoff depth, closedness, closed shift, closed substitution, closed composition audit, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming; shift and substitution admission requiring $Q$ and $D$ before $R$ or $L$ is visible; closed-composition admission requiring $A$ to read only the checked closed-fragment rows; and nonescape excluding unrestricted substitution composition, subject reduction, host syntax quotients, hidden evaluator state, and compiler correctness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:metacic-substitution-spine-carrier}. The accepted packet displays exactly $T,V,D,Q,R,L,A,H,C,P,N$.

Closedness gates the operation rows. The rows $Q$ and $D$ must be exposed before the closed shift row $R$ or the closed substitution row $L$ can be read.

The audit row $A$ records only the closed composition surface. It reads the already displayed operation rows and therefore cannot be reinterpreted as unrestricted substitution algebra or as a subject-reduction theorem.

Finally, $H,C,P,N$ preserve, replay, source, and name the same finite spine. Since the carrier has no coordinate for host syntax quotients, hidden evaluator state, or compiler correctness, the listed obligations exhaust the NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC substitution spine closed-term consumer]
\label{thm:metacic-substitution-spine-closed-term-consumer}
Every closed-term consumer of an accepted $\MetaCICSubstitutionSpineUp$ carrier factors through the finite route
$$
\ClosedTermSubstitutionBoundaryUp \longmapsto
T,V,D,Q,R,L,A,H,C,P,N .
$$
Thus a consumer may read closed shift, closed substitution, and closed composition audit data only after the closed boundary, de Bruijn index, cutoff-depth, and closedness rows have been displayed, and cannot import subject reduction, host syntax equality, quotient terms, hidden evaluator state, or compiler correctness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the closed boundary supplied by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closedtermsubstitutionboundary-namecert}. It gives the displayed term, closedness, shift, substitution, ledger, audit, transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows that this spine specializes.

Project the carrier rows $T,V,D,Q,R,L,A$. These rows force a closed-term consumer to read the de Bruijn index and cutoff-depth data before the operation rows are exposed.

Transport, replay, provenance, and local naming are then read through $H,C,P,N$. They preserve the same finite route, so the consumer receives only closed-fragment substitution spine data and none of the excluded host-level principles.
\end{proof}
