\begin{definition}[Real completion selector-seal carrier]
\label{def:real-completion-selector-seal-carrier}
A $\RealCompletionSelectorSealUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(B,W,R,L,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $B$ is the finite selector-budget row naming the precision request, selector index, and admitted budget surface for the completion handoff;
\item $W$ is the selected $\StreamNameUp$ window row produced by $B$;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row obtained from the selected window;
\item $L$ is the $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ row reached only after the selector budget, selected window, and regular-rational readback are displayed;
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal route consumed by the Real and Completion handoff;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the selector budget, selected window, readback, limit seal, terminal Real seal, route, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ replay route from $B$ to $W$, from $W$ to $R$, from $R$ to $L$, and from $L$ to $E$;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over selector-budget packets, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\CauchyLimitSealUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RealCompletionSelectorSealUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares carriers only by the displayed rows $B,W,R,L,E,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for quotient stream equality, host real equality, a hidden selector, a detached limit object, a second completion route, or ambient completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Real completion selector-seal NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:real-completion-selector-seal-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:real-completion-selector-seal-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\RealCompletionSelectorSealUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for
$$
S=(B,W,R,L,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
selector-budget exposure through $B$, selected-window exposure through $W$, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback through $R$, $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ handoff through $L$, terminal $\RealUp$ seal routing through $E$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:real-completion-selector-seal-carrier}. Its public surface is exactly the selector budget, selected window, readback row, limit seal, terminal Real seal, transports, continuations, provenance, and local naming row.

Read the route in dependency order. The selector budget $B$ admits the requested finite observation budget, the window row $W$ records the chosen finite window, the readback row $R$ exposes the regular-rational data in that window, and the limit-seal row $L$ is available only after those rows are displayed.

The terminal seal row $E$ is then a route from the same displayed packet. The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, record, and name only this finite route, so the certificate has no remaining coordinate for quotient equality, host equality, hidden selection, detached limits, or ambient completeness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real completion selector-seal route determinacy]
\label{thm:real-completion-selector-seal-route-determinacy}

No second selected window, hidden selector, detached limit row, quotient stream equality, host real equality, or ambient completeness coordinate can distinguish the two reads.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the carrier definition to compare the two accepted reads row by row. The rows $B,W,R,L,E,H,C,P,N$ are the whole carrier surface, and the classifier has no additional comparison coordinate.

Apply the NameCert obligations. They force every seal-facing read through the same selector budget, selected window, regular-rational readback, limit seal, and terminal Real seal before the structural rows can package the route.

Since both consumers expose the same displayed rows, their componentwise $\hsame$ transports and $\Cont$ replays name the same finite route. The excluded objects are absent carrier coordinates, so they cannot produce a second handoff.
\end{proof}
