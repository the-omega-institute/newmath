\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\AnalyticContinuationSocketUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-analytic-continuation-socket-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\AnalyticContinuationSocketUp$ interface turns the analytic-continuation operation of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-analytic-continuation} into a finite operation-socket certificate. It does not assert a global monodromy theorem or a host analytic continuation principle. It names the local source rows, overlap witnesses, continuation output rows, transport routes, and branch-obstruction ledger that a downstream $\HolomorphicUp$ or Riemann-hypothesis-facing consumer may inspect.

\begin{definition}[AnalyticContinuationSocket carrier]
\label{def:analytic-continuation-socket-carrier}
An $\AnalyticContinuationSocketUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(F,G,U,A,O,B,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $F$ is the source $\HolomorphicUp$ row on the first $\ComplexUp$ domain;
\item $G$ is the overlapping $\HolomorphicUp$ row on the second $\ComplexUp$ domain;
\item $U$ is the finite overlap witness row, including the displayed nontrivial overlap and agreement evidence needed by the local identity route;
\item $A$ is the analytic-continuation operation row that reads $F,G,U$ and proposes an output continuation;
\item $O$ is the output $\HolomorphicUp$ row on the displayed union domain;
\item $B$ is the branch-obstruction ledger, recording every requested continuation route that leaves the displayed overlap-and-output packet as a $\mathsf{GAP}$ boundary;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $F,G,U,A,O,B$ and the local naming rows;
\item $C$ stores the finite $\Cont$ routes by which consumers read the source rows, overlap witness, output row, branch ledger, package provenance, and local naming row;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over the same finite operation-socket packet;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\AnalyticContinuationSocketUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exposes exactly this local socket. It contains no unrestricted analytic-continuation operator, no global branch selector, no quotient of germs, and no host complex-analysis theorem.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[AnalyticContinuationSocket NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:analytic-continuation-socket-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\AnalyticContinuationSocketUp}$ surface for \autoref{def:analytic-continuation-socket-carrier} consists of five finite rows: carrier admission for $S$; classifier stability for the two holomorphic source rows, the overlap witness, the operation row, and the output row; ledger exactness for the branch-obstruction coordinate $B$; $\Pkg$ provenance over $F,G,U,A,O,B,H,C$; and local naming exactness for $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection of the finite tuple $S$. The source, overlap, operation, output, obstruction, transport, continuation, package, and local naming rows are all visible coordinates of the same $\BHist$ packet.

Classifier stability is componentwise. Transport of $F,G,U$ preserves the two source rows and the overlap agreement evidence, transport of $A,O$ preserves the proposed continuation output, and transport of $B$ preserves the recorded non-escape boundary.

The package and local naming rows repack these coordinates without adding a host continuation principle. Thus the socket certificate consists exactly of the five displayed obligation rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[AnalyticContinuationSocket ledger non-escape]
\label{thm:analytic-continuation-socket-ledger-nonescape}
Every consumer read exported by an accepted $\AnalyticContinuationSocketUp$ carrier factors through the displayed rows $F,G,U,A,O,B,H,C,P,N$. If a read asks for continuation data outside the source rows, overlap witness, proposed output, and displayed transport routes, the request is recorded in $B$ as a $\mathsf{GAP}$ boundary and is not exported as a holomorphic continuation row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the consumer request through the displayed $\Cont$ route $C$. If the request lands in the two source rows, the overlap witness, the operation row, the output row, or the package and naming rows, the carrier supplies exactly that coordinate.

If the request leaves those coordinates, no row of $S$ can supply a continuation witness. The branch-obstruction ledger $B$ is the only remaining classifier-visible coordinate for such a request, so the read is recorded as non-escape evidence rather than as an output continuation.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[AnalyticContinuationSocket holomorphic handoff]
\label{thm:analytic-continuation-socket-holomorphic-handoff}
For an accepted $\AnalyticContinuationSocketUp$ carrier $S=(F,G,U,A,O,B,H,C,P,N)$, every downstream $\HolomorphicUp$ consumer of the output row $O$ also reads the source rows $F,G$, the overlap witness $U$, the operation row $A$, and the transport, continuation, package, and local naming rows $H,C,P,N$. The handoff exposes no standalone continuation function and no branch choice outside the displayed packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the output row $O$. In the carrier definition it appears only as the result coordinate of the operation row $A$ over the displayed source rows $F,G$ and overlap witness $U$.

Transport through $H$ preserves the same source-overlap-output boundary, and the continuation routes in $C$ expose the output only through that boundary. The package and local naming rows $P,N$ then identify the same finite packet for downstream consumers.

The branch ledger $B$ rules out the remaining escape case. A consumer that asks for a branch choice or continuation function outside the displayed packet reaches $B$, not another $\HolomorphicUp$ row.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}

The ledger non-escape theorem \autoref{thm:analytic-continuation-socket-ledger-nonescape} rules out any continuation read outside these rows. Therefore the admitted carrier supplies the stated source surface and no host analytic-continuation principle.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[AnalyticContinuationSocket classifier stability obligation]
\label{thm:analytic-continuation-socket-classifier-stability-obligation}
Componentwise $\hsame$ transport for accepted $\AnalyticContinuationSocketUp$ packets preserves the source rows $F,G$, the overlap witness $U$, the operation row $A$, the output row $O$, and the branch-obstruction ledger $B$. Under this transport the classifier may replay the displayed $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local \NameCert{} row, but it cannot replace them with an ambient analytic-continuation theorem or a hidden branch choice.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the transport row $H$ in \autoref{def:analytic-continuation-socket-carrier}. It stores the componentwise $\hsame$ data for the source, overlap, operation, output, obstruction, and local naming rows.

Use the classifier-stability clause of \autoref{thm:analytic-continuation-socket-namecert-obligations}. Transport of $F,G,U$ preserves the source and overlap evidence, transport of $A,O$ preserves the operation-output boundary, and transport of $B$ preserves the recorded obstruction ledger.

The holomorphic handoff of \autoref{thm:analytic-continuation-socket-holomorphic-handoff} then keeps downstream $\HolomorphicUp$ consumers on the same displayed packet. Since the carrier has no branch selector or host continuation theorem, the transported classifier cannot export one.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[AnalyticContinuationSocket ledger exactness obligation]
\label{thm:analytic-continuation-socket-ledger-exactness-obligation}
Every branch-obstruction read of an accepted $\AnalyticContinuationSocketUp$ packet is recorded in the ledger row $B$. Such a read may be transported by $H$, replayed by $C$, named by $P$ and $N$, and compared with the source, overlap, operation, and output rows, but it cannot become a new $\HolomorphicUp$ output row or a continuation output outside $O$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the branch-obstruction coordinate $B$ from \autoref{def:analytic-continuation-socket-carrier}. It is the only displayed row assigned to continuation requests that leave the source, overlap, operation, and output packet.

Apply the ledger non-escape theorem \autoref{thm:analytic-continuation-socket-ledger-nonescape}. Reads outside the displayed continuation packet are recorded as $\mathsf{GAP}$ boundary data in $B$ rather than exported as holomorphic continuation rows.

Finally use the holomorphic handoff of \autoref{thm:analytic-continuation-socket-holomorphic-handoff}. Downstream $\HolomorphicUp$ consumers receive $O$ only through $F,G,U,A,H,C,P,N$, so a branch-obstruction read remains ledger data and cannot turn into another output row.
\end{proof}
