\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\StoneCechCompactificationUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-stone-cech-compactification-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Gillman--Jerison and Engelking on compactifications, the $\StoneCechCompactificationUp$ packet records the finite BEDC surface by which bounded continuous probes from a displayed completely regular source are read through compact Hausdorff target seals. It sits over the generated-open topology rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-topology-namecert}, the compact-metric witness discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, the graph and continuity surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuousmap-namecert}, the endpoint-stable limiting route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformlimit-namecert}, and the real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet names finite bounded-continuous-probe windows and compact-target seal rows only; it does not construct an ambient function algebra, choose ultrafilters, identify quotient extensions, or assert an external compactification theorem.

\begin{definition}[Stone--Cech finite probe carrier]
\label{def:stone-cech-compactification-finite-probe-carrier}
A $\StoneCechCompactificationUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(X,O,B,W,F,K,T,U,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the source-object row, read only through the displayed topology classifier of $\TopologyUp$.
\item $O$ is the finite generated-open row for the inspected source neighborhoods and separation tests.
\item $B$ is the bounded-real range row, recording the finite real-name bounds consumed by bounded continuous probes.
\item $W$ is the finite probe-window row, listing the bounded continuous probes whose values are inspected.
\item $F$ is the $\ContinuousMapUp$ graph and continuity row for each probe in $W$.
\item $K$ is the compact-target seal row, read through $\CompactMetricUp$ or the displayed compact target surface available to the surrounding route.
\item $T$ is the compact Hausdorff target-separation row for finite pairs of target observations.
\item $U$ is the $\UniformLimitUp$ handoff row for replaying finite probe windows through stable target seals.
\item $R$ is the $\RealUp$ value row attached to the bounded target observations.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for source topology, bounded range, probe window, continuous-map graph, compact-target seal, target separation, uniform handoff, real-value, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a finite bounded-probe request through $O,B,W,F,K,T,U,R$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\StoneCechCompactificationUp$, $\TopologyUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\UniformLimitUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\StoneCechCompactificationUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $X,O,B,W,F,K,T,U,R,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for ultrafilter choice, ambient bounded-function algebra, quotient extension equality, host compactification points, excluded middle, or external compact Hausdorff completion.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Stone--Cech compactification NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:stone-cech-compactification-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\StoneCechCompactificationUp$ carrier
$$
S=(X,O,B,W,F,K,T,U,R,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\StoneCechCompactificationUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the source topology row, generated-open inspections, bounded-real range row, finite probe window, continuous-map graph rows, compact-target seal, target separation rows, uniform handoff, real-value rows, transport, replay, provenance, and naming;
\item probe-window classifier, requiring every bounded continuous probe read to factor through $W,F,B,R$ and the displayed $\ContinuousMapUp$ and $\RealUp$ rows;
\item compact-target handoff, requiring $K,T$ to expose only the displayed compact target and target-separation rows before any compactification-facing consumer reads the seal;
\item transport and replay, requiring $H,C$ to preserve the same finite packet under $\hsame$ comparison and displayed $\Cont$ observation replay;
\item ledger non-escape, requiring every topology, compactness, continuous-map, uniform-limit, and real-value consumer to read through $X,O,B,W,F,K,T,U,R,H,C,P,N$ rather than through ultrafilters, host compactification points, quotient extension equality, or an ambient bounded-function algebra.
\end{enumerate}
No Stone--Cech compactification obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:stone-cech-compactification-finite-probe-carrier}. The accepted tuple exposes exactly the source topology, generated-open, bounded-range, probe-window, continuous-map, compact-target, target-separation, uniform-handoff, real-value, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows listed in the statement.

The probe-window classifier is finite. A probe request first selects an entry of $W$, then reads its graph and continuity data through $F$, its bounded range through $B$, and its displayed real values through $R$. No function algebra or selected infinite probe family is part of the carrier.

The compact-target handoff is read from $K,T,U$. The compact target seal and its finite separation rows are displayed before the uniform replay row can export a compactification-facing observation, so the handoff remains inside the target seal.

Transport, replay, provenance, and naming are supplied by $H,C,P,N$. Since the carrier has no coordinate for ultrafilter choice, quotient extension equality, host compactification points, excluded middle, or an external compact Hausdorff completion, the listed rows exhaust the local certificate surface.
\end{proof}
