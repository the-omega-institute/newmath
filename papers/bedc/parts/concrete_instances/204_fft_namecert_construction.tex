\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\FftUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-fft-namecert}

The Fast-Fourier-transform interface $\FftUp$ packages the $O(n \log n)$ divide-and-conquer evaluation of the discrete Fourier transform on length-$n$ data when $n$ has small prime factors. It depends on $\NameCert_{\FourierUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-fourier-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\ComplexUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-complex-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:fft-carrier}

\begin{definition}[FFT BHist source packet]
\label{def:fft-bhist-source-packet}
An $\FftUp$ source packet is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ schedule carrying a
$\ComplexUp$ scalar row, a $\FourierUp$ transform row, a finite
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ of butterfly stages, $\mathsf{InBundle}$ membership
witnesses for every scheduled stage, and a factorization ledger naming the
split of the transform length. The carrier accepts exactly those packets whose
complex scalar rows and Fourier rows are accepted by their dependency
certificates and whose stage rows transport through the same $\hsame$ and
$\Cont$ evidence.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[FFT carrier butterfly obligation]
\label{thm:fft-carrier-butterfly-obligation}
Every accepted $\FftUp$ carrier row projects to a finite butterfly schedule:
each stage is a $\mathsf{BHist}$ row in the displayed
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, each membership witness is an
$\mathsf{InBundle}$ row, and each butterfly continuation is carried by
$\Cont$ from the $\ComplexUp$ scalar dependency and the $\FourierUp$ transform
dependency. Conversely, those rows repack as the FFT carrier.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FftUp.FftBHistSourcePacket\_carrier\_butterfly\_obligation}
\begin{proof}
Project the $\ComplexUp$ and $\FourierUp$ dependency rows from the packet.
They supply the scalar and transform histories used by every scheduled stage.

Read the finite schedule through the $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ field. Each
butterfly row is certified by an $\mathsf{InBundle}$ witness, so the schedule
is finite at the BEDC surface and does not depend on a host array traversal.

For the converse direction, place the dependency rows, stage membership rows,
and butterfly continuations into the packet constructor. The common $\hsame$
and $\Cont$ evidence transports the stage rows and yields the carried
$\FftUp$ endpoint.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FFT factorization classifier stability obligation]
\label{thm:fft-factorization-classifier-stability-obligation}
The factorization classifier row of an $\FftUp$ packet is stable under
accepted replacement of the complex scalar, Fourier transform, and finite
butterfly schedule rows. If two packets compare by the displayed $\hsame$
evidence, then the split-length ledger, the stage membership witnesses, and
the butterfly continuations classify the same FFT factorization surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Transport the scalar row through $\NameCert_{\ComplexUp}$ and the transform
row through $\NameCert_{\FourierUp}$. These two dependency transports fix the
source histories on which the finite schedule is read.

Next transport every scheduled butterfly stage along the same packet
comparison. The $\mathsf{InBundle}$ witnesses keep the stage list inside the
finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, while $\Cont$ transports the butterfly output
rows.

The factorization ledger is stored with those stage rows. Repacking the
transported dependencies, schedule, and ledger gives the same classifier
surface for the target FFT packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FFT ledger exactness obligation]
\label{thm:fft-ledger-exactness-obligation}
The $\FftUp$ ledger consists exactly of the $\NameCert_{\ComplexUp}$
dependency row, the $\NameCert_{\FourierUp}$ dependency row, the finite
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ schedule, the $\mathsf{InBundle}$ stage witnesses, the
butterfly continuation rows, the factorization ledger rows, and their
$\hsame$ and $\Cont$ transports. It excludes analytic error estimates,
floating-point bounds, asymptotic runtime claims, and standard bridge rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Coverage reads any accepted FFT packet into the two dependency rows, the
finite schedule, the stage membership witnesses, the butterfly
continuations, and the factorization ledger.

Soundness repacks exactly those rows. The scalar and transform dependencies
justify the input histories, while $\mathsf{InBundle}$ and $\Cont$ justify the
finite schedule and every butterfly output.

Completeness is the absence of further ledger coordinates. Error estimates,
floating-point bounds, asymptotic analysis, and standard bridge data are not
fields of the packet, so they cannot be consumed by this certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FFT root threshold unblock surface]
\label{thm:fft-root-threshold-unblock-surface}
The rows of \autoref{def:fft-bhist-source-packet},
\autoref{thm:fft-carrier-butterfly-obligation},
\autoref{thm:fft-factorization-classifier-stability-obligation}, and
\autoref{thm:fft-ledger-exactness-obligation} form the dependency-ready
finite-history surface for $\FftUp$. Downstream numerical-analysis
certificates may consume the complex dependency, Fourier dependency, finite
butterfly schedule, factorization ledger, and exact exclusion of analytic
error or bridge data, but must provide any such estimates or bridges outside
the FFT packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The definition fixes the finite packet and the two dependency certificates.
The butterfly obligation turns the packet into carried $\mathsf{BHist}$ stage
rows over a certified finite schedule.

The factorization stability obligation supplies the classifier transport for
the split-length ledger and butterfly continuations. The ledger exactness
obligation then states that no analytic estimate or standard bridge row is
available inside the packet.

Together these rows expose the concrete FFT source surface needed by later
certificates while keeping numerical-analysis obligations separate.
\end{proof}
