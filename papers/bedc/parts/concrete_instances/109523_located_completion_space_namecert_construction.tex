\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\LocatedCompletionSpaceUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-located-completion-space-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges on located metric completion, the $\LocatedCompletionSpaceUp$ packet records the finite interface by which located metric data become completion-facing space data without quotient representatives or countable-choice selectors. It sits after the $\PseudometricUp$ surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-pseudometric-namecert}, the separated boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separatedcompletion-namecert}, the located distance route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedmetric-namecert}, the dense entry row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completionembedding-namecert}, the finite windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the terminal real seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The chapter names a human-origin L10 completion interface whose visible rows are finite BEDC data only.

\begin{definition}[LocatedCompletionSpace carrier]
\label{def:located-completion-space-carrier}
A $\LocatedCompletionSpaceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(P,S,M,E,W,R,D,A,H,C,Q,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $P$ is the source $\PseudometricUp$ row, exposing pre-separated distances, zero-distance evidence, finite windows, regular readback, and a Real distance seal.
\item $S$ is the $\SeparatedCompletionUp$ boundary row, read before any completed point may be compared as separated.
\item $M$ is the $\LocatedMetricUp$ row, naming the located distance comparison used by completion-facing consumers.
\item $E$ is the $\CompletionEmbeddingUp$ row by which source observations enter the completion interface.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row inspected before regular rational readback or terminal seal access.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row obtained from the displayed finite windows.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger controlling located comparison, Cauchy tail requests, and separation checks.
\item $A$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal reached only after $P,S,M,E,W,R,D$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the source, separated boundary, located metric, embedding, window, readback, tolerance, seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a completion-facing request through the same ordered rows.
\item $Q$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LocatedCompletionSpaceUp$, $\PseudometricUp$, $\SeparatedCompletionUp$, $\LocatedMetricUp$, $\CompletionEmbeddingUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedCompletionSpaceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for quotient representatives, selected completion points, countable choice, trichotomy, host equality of completed points, open-cover compactness, or ambient completeness of $\RealUp$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[LocatedCompletionSpace NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:located-completion-space-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\LocatedCompletionSpaceUp$ carrier $L=(P,S,M,E,W,R,D,A,H,C,Q,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedCompletionSpaceUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the pseudometric source, separated-completion boundary, located metric row, completion embedding, stream window, regular rational readback, dyadic tolerance ledger, terminal Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item located source exposure, requiring $P$ and $M$ to be displayed before the embedding row $E$ is consumed;
\item separated completion exposure, requiring $S$ to be read before any completed-point comparison is public;
\item finite readback exactness, requiring $W,D$ to precede $R$ and requiring $R$ to precede the seal row $A$;
\item ledger refusal for quotient representatives, selected completion points, countable choice, trichotomy, host equality of completed points, open-cover compactness, and ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{enumerate}
No obligation is stored outside these finite rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:located-completion-space-carrier}. The projection exposes the pseudometric source, separated-completion boundary, located metric row, completion embedding, finite windows, regular readback, dyadic ledger, Real seal, and structural rows.

Read the located source before the completion entry. The rows $P$ and $M$ supply the pre-separated distance data and located comparison route, while $E$ is the only coordinate that carries source observations into the completion-facing interface.

The separated boundary is local. A completed-point comparison must pass through $S$, so it cannot be replaced by quotient representatives, selected points, or host equality of completed objects.

Finally read the numerical route $W,D,R,A$ and attach $H,C,Q,N$. Transport, replay, provenance, and local naming preserve the same finite order, and no remaining coordinate names choice, trichotomy, compactness, or ambient Real completeness. Hence the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LocatedCompletionSpace finite completion route]
\label{thm:located-completion-space-finite-route}
Every completion-facing consumer of an accepted $\LocatedCompletionSpaceUp$ carrier factors through the finite route
$$
P,M\longmapsto E\longmapsto S\longmapsto W,D\longmapsto R\longmapsto A\longmapsto H,C,Q,N .
$$
In particular, the consumer reads pseudometric evidence, located metric comparison, dense completion entry, separated completion boundary, finite windows, dyadic tolerance data, regular rational readback, and the Real seal before any local name is available.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with $P$ and $M$. These rows expose the pre-separated distance surface and the located metric comparison used by the completion-facing request.

The request enters the completion interface through $E$ and then reads the separated-completion boundary $S$. Thus source entry and separated comparison are both displayed before any terminal seal is used.

The numerical part of the route is finite. The window row $W$ and tolerance ledger $D$ determine the regular rational readback $R$, and only then may the terminal Real seal $A$ be inspected.

The structural rows $H,C,Q,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same ordered route. Since the carrier contains no further analytic coordinates, every completion-facing consumer has the displayed factorization.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LocatedCompletionSpace representative refusal]
\label{thm:located-completion-space-representative-refusal}
For every accepted $\LocatedCompletionSpaceUp$ carrier $L=(P,S,M,E,W,R,D,A,H,C,Q,N)$, every separated completion read and every Real-seal read is exhausted by the rows
$$
P,S,M,E,W,R,D,A,H,C,Q,N .
$$
Consequently the packet exports no quotient representative, selected completion point, countable-choice selector, trichotomy split, host equality of completed points, open-cover compactness datum, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:located-completion-space-finite-route}. Any completion-facing consumer first exposes the source, located metric, embedding, separated boundary, finite window, tolerance ledger, readback, and Real seal rows.

The remaining rows are structural. They carry componentwise transport, replay the same route, attach provenance, and provide the local name; none of them adds a completion representative or a selector.

The carrier of \autoref{def:located-completion-space-carrier} lists no coordinate for quotient representatives, choice, trichotomy, host equality, compactness, or ambient Real completeness. Therefore those principles cannot be exported by a separated completion or Real-seal read from the packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LocatedCompletionSpace L10 neighbour handoff]
\label{thm:located-completion-space-l10-neighbour-handoff}

Any downstream consumer omitting one of these sibling rows receives only an incomplete route and cannot name the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedCompletionSpaceUp}$ surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier definition lists each sibling row as a visible coordinate. Projecting the packet therefore exposes all eight analytic neighbours before the transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows are read.

The obligation theorem identifies the role of each row: source exposure, separated comparison, located distance, completion entry, finite windowing, regular readback, dyadic tolerance accounting, and Real sealing.

If a downstream consumer omits one of those rows, one of the listed obligations cannot be discharged. The local name $N$ is attached only to the full route, so the incomplete consumer cannot name the $\LocatedCompletionSpaceUp$ surface.
\end{proof}
