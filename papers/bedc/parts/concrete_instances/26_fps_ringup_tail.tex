\begin{lemma}[FPS threefold Cauchy split reassociation over RingUp scalars]
\label{lem:fps-threefold-cauchy-split-reassociation-ringup}
Let $R$ carry a $\RingUp(R)$ certificate with scalar classifier $\sim_R$, zero endpoint $0_R$, addition $+_R$, multiplication $\cdot_R$, and finite additive fold $\mathsf{fold}_{+,R}$. Assume the scalar classifier equivalence fields, multiplication closure, multiplication congruence, and multiplicative associativity rows retained in \autoref{def:ring-stability-certificate}. Assume also the split-spine fold-swap field for the triangular finite reindexing of \autoref{def:fps-threefold-cauchy-split-spines}: the left enumeration by $((a,k),(i,j))$ with $a+k=n$ and $i+j=a$ and the right enumeration by $((i,c),(j,k))$ with $i+c=n$ and $j+k=c$ are fold-swappable after transport to the common triple spine $(i,j,k)$. For carried coefficient sequences $F,G,H:\NatUp\to R$ and unary index $n$, the two threefold split folds are scalar-classified: $\mathsf{fold}_{+,R}\bigl(\mathsf{TripSp}^{\ell}_n(F,G,H)\bigr)\sim_R\mathsf{fold}_{+,R}\bigl(\mathsf{TripSp}^{r}_n(F,G,H)\bigr)$.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
The split spine $\mathsf{Split}(n)$ from \autoref{def:fps-cauchy-coefficient-spine} is finite. Therefore both threefold spines of \autoref{def:fps-threefold-cauchy-split-spines} are finite concatenations of finite coefficient spines. Each displayed summand is carried by the coefficient-carriedness of $F$, $G$, and $H$, followed twice by the multiplication closure row of \autoref{def:ring-stability-certificate}.
The left spine indexes a summand by data $((a,k),(i,j))$ with $a+k=n$ and $i+j=a$. Sending this data to $(i,j,k)$ gives the triple equation $(i+j)+k=n$. The right spine indexes a summand by data $((i,c),(j,k))$ with $i+c=n$ and $j+k=c$. Sending this data to $(i,j,k)$ gives $i+(j+k)=n$. Unary additive associativity in the split recursion identifies these two triple predicates, and the displayed reconstructions $(i,j,k)\mapsto((i+j,k),(i,j))$ and $(i,j,k)\mapsto((i,j+k),(j,k))$ are inverse on the corresponding split witnesses.
Apply the assumed split-spine instance of the fold-swap certificate from \autoref{def:two-index-finite-fold-swap-certificate} to this finite reindexing. The remaining pointwise scalar comparison at a fixed triple $(i,j,k)$ is exactly the multiplicative associativity row from \autoref{def:ring-stability-certificate}: $(F_i\cdot_R G_j)\cdot_R H_k\sim_R F_i\cdot_R(G_j\cdot_R H_k)$. The fold-swap field and the finite additive fold congruence theorem \autoref{thm:finite-additive-fold-congruence-coefficient-lists} lift these pointwise comparisons through the finite spines, yielding the displayed scalar classification.
\end{proof}
\begin{theorem}[FPS Cauchy product associativity over RingUp scalars]
\label{thm:fps-cauchy-product-associativity-over-ringup-scalars}
Let $R$ carry a $\RingUp(R)$ certificate with scalar classifier $\sim_R$, zero endpoint $0_R$, addition $+_R$, multiplication $\cdot_R$, and finite additive fold $\mathsf{fold}_{+,R}$. Assume the scalar classifier equivalence fields, additive fold congruence, multiplication congruence, multiplicative associativity, left and right distributivity, and zero multiplication rows retained in \autoref{def:ring-stability-certificate}. Assume the scalar product fold-distribution lemma \autoref{lem:scalar-product-distributes-across-finite-additive-folds} and the split-spine fold-swap field used in \autoref{lem:fps-threefold-cauchy-split-reassociation-ringup}. Let the $\FormalPowerSeriesUp(R)$ interface use the pointwise classifier $\sim_{\mathrm{fps}}$ of \autoref{def:fps-classifier-specification}. Assume its Cauchy product coefficient at each unary index is the finite-spine rule of \autoref{def:fps-cauchy-coefficient-spine}, read over these $\RingUp$ operations: $(F\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}G)_n:=\mathsf{fold}_{+,R}(\mathsf{CauchySp}_n(F,G))$. For carried coefficient sequences $F,G,H:\NatUp\to R$, the displayed products are carried and $\forall n:\NatUp,\quad \bigl((F\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}G)\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}H\bigr)_n\sim_R\bigl(F\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}(G\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}H)\bigr)_n$. Consequently, $(F\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}G)\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}H\sim_{\mathrm{fps}}F\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}(G\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}H)$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FpsUp.FpsSingletonCauchyProduct\_assoc\_classifier}
\begin{proof}
The Cauchy-product closure row in \autoref{def:fps-stability-certificate} carries $F\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}G$ and $G\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}H$, and then carries the two outer products. By the pointwise classifier of \autoref{def:fps-classifier-specification}, it remains to compare the scalar coefficients at an arbitrary unary index.
Fix $n:\NatUp$. The left-associated coefficient unfolds to $\mathsf{fold}_{+,R}\bigl(\mathsf{CauchySp}_n(F\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}G,H)\bigr)$. For a split $(a,k)\in\mathsf{Split}(n)$, the corresponding summand is $(F\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}G)_a\cdot_R H_k$. The inner product coefficient at $a$ is $\mathsf{fold}_{+,R}\bigl(\mathsf{CauchySp}_a(F,G)\bigr)$. Applying the right-multiplication half of \autoref{lem:scalar-product-distributes-across-finite-additive-folds} classifies the summand with the inner finite fold over $\mathsf{map}_{(i,j)\in\mathsf{Split}(a)}\bigl((F_i\cdot_R G_j)\cdot_R H_k\bigr)$. The finite additive fold congruence theorem \autoref{thm:finite-additive-fold-congruence-coefficient-lists} lifts these inner comparisons through the outer split spine, giving $\bigl((F\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}G)\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}H\bigr)_n\sim_R\mathsf{fold}_{+,R}\bigl(\mathsf{TripSp}^{\ell}_n(F,G,H)\bigr)$.
The right-associated coefficient unfolds to $\mathsf{fold}_{+,R}\bigl(\mathsf{CauchySp}_n(F,G\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}H)\bigr)$. For a split $(i,c)\in\mathsf{Split}(n)$, the summand is $F_i\cdot_R(G\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}H)_c$. The inner product coefficient at $c$ is $\mathsf{fold}_{+,R}\bigl(\mathsf{CauchySp}_c(G,H)\bigr)$. Applying the left-multiplication half of \autoref{lem:scalar-product-distributes-across-finite-additive-folds} classifies this summand with the inner finite fold over $\mathsf{map}_{(j,k)\in\mathsf{Split}(c)}\bigl(F_i\cdot_R(G_j\cdot_R H_k)\bigr)$. Again \autoref{thm:finite-additive-fold-congruence-coefficient-lists} lifts through the outer split spine and gives $\bigl(F\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}(G\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}H)\bigr)_n\sim_R\mathsf{fold}_{+,R}\bigl(\mathsf{TripSp}^{r}_n(F,G,H)\bigr)$.
By \autoref{lem:fps-threefold-cauchy-split-reassociation-ringup}, $\mathsf{fold}_{+,R}\bigl(\mathsf{TripSp}^{\ell}_n(F,G,H)\bigr)\sim_R\mathsf{fold}_{+,R}\bigl(\mathsf{TripSp}^{r}_n(F,G,H)\bigr)$. Classifier transitivity composes the left expansion, this threefold split comparison, and the symmetry of the right expansion, giving $\bigl((F\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}G)\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}H\bigr)_n\sim_R\bigl(F\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}(G\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}H)\bigr)_n$. Since $n$ was arbitrary, \autoref{def:fps-classifier-specification} packages these coefficientwise scalar comparisons as the displayed $\FormalPowerSeriesUp$ classifier judgment.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[FPS zero-index Cauchy spine]\label{lem:fps-zero-index-cauchy-spine}
Let $R$ carry a $\RingUp(R)$ certificate with multiplication $\cdot_R$. For coefficient sequences $F,G:\NatUp\to R$, the split spine used by \autoref{def:fps-cauchy-coefficient-spine} at the zero index is the singleton split $\mathsf{Split}(0)=\mathsf{cons}((0,0),\mathsf{nil})$. Consequently, $\mathsf{CauchySp}_0(F,G)=\mathsf{cons}(F_0\cdot_R G_0,\mathsf{nil})$.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
The split spine is generated by the $\NatUp$ recursion in \autoref{def:fps-cauchy-coefficient-spine}. The zero branch has no predecessor index and contains only the decomposition of $0$ as $0+0$. Applying the displayed map defining $\mathsf{CauchySp}_n(F,G)$ to this singleton split replaces the split entry by its scalar product $F_0\cdot_R G_0$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FpsUp.FpsSingletonCauchyProduct\_zero\_index\_spine}
\begin{theorem}[FPS Cauchy product constant coefficient]\label{thm:fps-cauchy-product-constant-coefficient}
Let $R$ carry a $\RingUp(R)$ certificate with scalar classifier $\sim_R$, zero endpoint $0_R$, addition $+_R$, multiplication $\cdot_R$, and finite additive fold $\mathsf{fold}_{+,R}$. Let the $\FormalPowerSeriesUp(R)$ interface use the Cauchy product coefficient rule of \autoref{def:fps-cauchy-coefficient-spine}, read over these $\RingUp$ operations. For carried coefficient sequences $F,G:\NatUp\to R$, $(F\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}G)_0\sim_R F_0\cdot_R G_0$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Put $x:=F_0\cdot_R G_0$. Since $F$ and $G$ are carried coefficient sequences, the multiplication closure row retained in \autoref{def:ring-stability-certificate} carries $x$. By \autoref{lem:fps-zero-index-cauchy-spine} and the product coefficient rule in \autoref{def:fps-cauchy-coefficient-spine}, $(F\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}G)_0=\mathsf{fold}_{+,R}(\mathsf{cons}(x,\mathsf{nil}))$.
The finite fold clauses displayed in \autoref{thm:finite-additive-fold-congruence-coefficient-lists} identify this endpoint with $x+_R0_R$. The additive right-zero theorem \autoref{thm:ring-add-right-zero}, applied in the scalar ring to $x$, gives $x+_R0_R\sim_R x$. Substituting $x=F_0\cdot_R G_0$ gives the scalar classifier comparison.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FpsUp.FpsSingletonCauchyProduct\_constant\_coefficient}

\begin{definition}[FPS pointwise additive monoid instance]
\label{def:fps-pointwise-additive-monoid-instance}
Let $R$ carry a $\RingUp(R)$ certificate with scalar classifier $\sim_R$, zero endpoint $0_R$, addition $+_R$, and the scalar stability rows retained in \autoref{def:ring-stability-certificate}. Using the coefficientwise carrier of \autoref{def:fps-carrier}, the additive carrier for the formal-power-series layer is
$$
\mathsf{FPSCarrier}_R:=\{F:\NatUp\to R\mid F\text{ is carried coefficientwise}\}.
$$
Its classifier is the pointwise classifier $\sim_{\mathrm{fps}}$ of \autoref{def:fps-classifier-specification}. Its binary operation is the pointwise operation $\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}$ of \autoref{def:fps-pointwise-addition-coefficient}:
$$
(F\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}G)_n:=F_n+_R G_n.
$$
Its zero endpoint is the zero series $0_{\mathrm{fps}}$ with coefficient rule $(0_{\mathrm{fps}})_n:=0_R$ for every unary index $n:\NatUp$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[FPS pointwise additive monoid laws]
\label{thm:fps-pointwise-additive-monoid-laws}
Let $R$ carry a $\RingUp(R)$ certificate with scalar classifier $\sim_R$, zero endpoint $0_R$, addition $+_R$, and the scalar classifier equivalence, zero self-classification, addition closure, addition congruence, additive associativity, and additive zero rows retained in \autoref{def:ring-stability-certificate}. For the concrete data of \autoref{def:fps-pointwise-additive-monoid-instance}, the following laws hold for carried coefficient sequences:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item $0_{\mathrm{fps}}$ is carried, and $F\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}G$ is carried whenever $F$ and $G$ are carried.
  \item If $F\sim_{\mathrm{fps}}F'$ and $G\sim_{\mathrm{fps}}G'$, then $F\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}G\sim_{\mathrm{fps}}F'\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}G'$.
  \item $(F\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}G)\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}H\sim_{\mathrm{fps}}F\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}(G\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}H)$.
  \item $0_{\mathrm{fps}}\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}F\sim_{\mathrm{fps}}F$ and $F\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}0_{\mathrm{fps}}\sim_{\mathrm{fps}}F$.
\end{enumerate}
Thus $\mathsf{FPSCarrier}_R$ with $\sim_{\mathrm{fps}}$, $\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}$, and $0_{\mathrm{fps}}$ is the concrete additive monoid interface consumed by the formal-power-series algebra layer.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FpsUp.FpsSingletonPointwiseAdditionCoeff\_zero\_laws}
\begin{proof}
The carrier clause follows coefficientwise from \autoref{def:fps-carrier}. The zero series is carried because each coefficient is $0_R$, and the scalar zero self-classification row in \autoref{def:ring-stability-certificate} supplies the carried scalar endpoint. If $F$ and $G$ are carried, then each coefficient $F_n+_R G_n$ is carried by scalar addition closure. By \autoref{def:fps-pointwise-addition-coefficient}, these coefficients are exactly the coefficients of $F\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}G$.

For congruence, fix $n:\NatUp$. The hypotheses $F\sim_{\mathrm{fps}}F'$ and $G\sim_{\mathrm{fps}}G'$ become scalar comparisons $F_n\sim_R F'_n$ and $G_n\sim_R G'_n$ by \autoref{def:fps-classifier-specification}. Scalar addition congruence from \autoref{def:ring-stability-certificate} gives
$$
F_n+_R G_n\sim_R F'_n+_R G'_n.
$$
Using \autoref{def:fps-pointwise-addition-coefficient} on both sides and then \autoref{def:fps-classifier-specification} packages the coefficientwise comparisons as $F\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}G\sim_{\mathrm{fps}}F'\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}G'$.

Associativity is the existing pointwise associativity theorem \autoref{thm:fps-pointwise-addition-associativity}, read with the same carrier, classifier, operation, and scalar associativity row.

It remains to check the zero laws. For the left zero law, fix $n:\NatUp$. The definitions give
$$
(0_{\mathrm{fps}}\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}F)_n=0_R+_R F_n.
$$
The scalar additive left-zero row retained in \autoref{def:ring-stability-certificate} classifies this endpoint with $F_n$. Since $n$ was arbitrary, \autoref{def:fps-classifier-specification} yields $0_{\mathrm{fps}}\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}F\sim_{\mathrm{fps}}F$.

For the right zero law, fix $n:\NatUp$. The definitions give
$$
(F\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}0_{\mathrm{fps}})_n=F_n+_R0_R.
$$
The scalar right-zero theorem \autoref{thm:ring-add-right-zero}, applied in the scalar ring to $F_n$, classifies this endpoint with $F_n$. Again \autoref{def:fps-classifier-specification} packages the coefficientwise comparisons as $F\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}0_{\mathrm{fps}}\sim_{\mathrm{fps}}F$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FpsUp.FpsSingletonPointwiseAdditiveMonoid\_laws}

\begin{definition}[FPS coefficientwise additive inverse]
\label{def:fps-coefficientwise-additive-inverse}
Let $R$ carry a $\RingUp(R)$ certificate with scalar classifier $\sim_R$, zero endpoint $0_R$, addition $+_R$, and scalar additive inverse operation $-_R$. For a carried coefficient sequence $F:\NatUp\to R$, the coefficientwise FPS inverse $-_{\mathrm{fps}}F$ is the coefficient sequence determined by
$$
(-_{\mathrm{fps}}F)_n:= -_R(F_n)
$$
for every unary index $n:\NatUp$. More generally, a sequence $N:\NatUp\to R$ is coefficientwise inverse data for $F$ when $N$ is carried and, for every $n:\NatUp$,
$$
N_n\sim_R -_R(F_n).
$$
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[FPS coefficientwise additive inverse zero laws]
\label{thm:fps-coefficientwise-additive-inverse-zero-laws}
Let $R$ carry a $\RingUp(R)$ certificate with scalar classifier $\sim_R$, zero endpoint $0_R$, addition $+_R$, and scalar additive inverse operation $-_R$. Assume the scalar classifier equivalence, zero self-classification, addition closure, addition congruence, additive inverse closure, and additive left inverse rows retained in \autoref{def:ring-stability-certificate}; assume also the scalar right inverse law of \autoref{thm:ring-add-right-inverse} for this addition and inverse. Let the $\FormalPowerSeriesUp(R)$ interface use the pointwise classifier $\sim_{\mathrm{fps}}$ of \autoref{def:fps-classifier-specification}, pointwise addition from \autoref{def:fps-pointwise-addition-coefficient}, and zero series $0_{\mathrm{fps}}$ from \autoref{def:fps-pointwise-additive-monoid-instance}. If $F:\NatUp\to R$ is carried and $N:\NatUp\to R$ is coefficientwise inverse data for $F$ in the sense of \autoref{def:fps-coefficientwise-additive-inverse}, then $F\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}N$ and $N\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}F$ are carried and
$$
F\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}N\sim_{\mathrm{fps}}0_{\mathrm{fps}}
\qquad\text{and}\qquad
N\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}F\sim_{\mathrm{fps}}0_{\mathrm{fps}}.
$$
Equivalently, for every unary coefficient index $n:\NatUp$,
$$
(F\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}N)_n\sim_R(0_{\mathrm{fps}})_n
\qquad\text{and}\qquad
(N\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}F)_n\sim_R(0_{\mathrm{fps}})_n.
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The definition of coefficientwise inverse data carries $N$. The pointwise addition closure row in \autoref{def:fps-stability-certificate} carries $F\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}N$ and $N\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}F$, and \autoref{thm:fps-pointwise-additive-monoid-laws} carries the zero series.

Fix $n:\NatUp$. The inverse-data hypothesis gives
$$
N_n\sim_R -_R(F_n).
$$
By \autoref{def:fps-pointwise-addition-coefficient},
$$
(F\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}N)_n=F_n+_R N_n.
$$
Scalar classifier reflexivity at $F_n$ and scalar addition congruence transport the inverse-data comparison under the common left summand:
$$
F_n+_R N_n\sim_R F_n+_R(-_R(F_n)).
$$
The scalar right inverse law \autoref{thm:ring-add-right-inverse} classifies the right endpoint with $0_R$. Since \autoref{def:fps-pointwise-additive-monoid-instance} gives $(0_{\mathrm{fps}})_n:=0_R$, transitivity gives
$$
(F\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}N)_n\sim_R(0_{\mathrm{fps}})_n.
$$

For the other order, \autoref{def:fps-pointwise-addition-coefficient} gives
$$
(N\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}F)_n=N_n+_R F_n.
$$
Scalar addition congruence transports the same inverse-data comparison under the common right summand:
$$
N_n+_R F_n\sim_R(-_R(F_n))+_R F_n.
$$
The scalar additive left inverse row retained in \autoref{def:ring-stability-certificate} classifies this endpoint with $0_R$, and the zero-series coefficient rule again gives $(0_{\mathrm{fps}})_n:=0_R$. Thus
$$
(N\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}F)_n\sim_R(0_{\mathrm{fps}})_n.
$$

Since $n$ was arbitrary, \autoref{def:fps-classifier-specification} packages the two coefficientwise scalar comparisons as the two displayed $\FormalPowerSeriesUp$ classifier judgments.
\end{proof}
