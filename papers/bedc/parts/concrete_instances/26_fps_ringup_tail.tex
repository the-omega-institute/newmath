\begin{lemma}[FPS threefold Cauchy split reassociation over RingUp scalars]
\label{lem:fps-threefold-cauchy-split-reassociation-ringup}
Let $R$ carry a $\RingUp(R)$ certificate with scalar classifier $\sim_R$, zero endpoint $0_R$, addition $+_R$, multiplication $\cdot_R$, and finite additive fold $\mathsf{fold}_{+,R}$. Assume the scalar classifier equivalence fields, multiplication closure, multiplication congruence, and multiplicative associativity rows retained in \autoref{def:ring-stability-certificate}. Assume also the split-spine fold-swap field for the triangular finite reindexing of \autoref{def:fps-threefold-cauchy-split-spines}: the left enumeration by $((a,k),(i,j))$ with $a+k=n$ and $i+j=a$ and the right enumeration by $((i,c),(j,k))$ with $i+c=n$ and $j+k=c$ are fold-swappable after transport to the common triple spine $(i,j,k)$. For carried coefficient sequences $F,G,H:\NatUp\to R$ and unary index $n$, the two threefold split folds are scalar-classified: $\mathsf{fold}_{+,R}\bigl(\mathsf{TripSp}^{\ell}_n(F,G,H)\bigr)\sim_R\mathsf{fold}_{+,R}\bigl(\mathsf{TripSp}^{r}_n(F,G,H)\bigr)$.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
The split spine $\mathsf{Split}(n)$ from \autoref{def:fps-cauchy-coefficient-spine} is finite. Therefore both threefold spines of \autoref{def:fps-threefold-cauchy-split-spines} are finite concatenations of finite coefficient spines. Each displayed summand is carried by the coefficient-carriedness of $F$, $G$, and $H$, followed twice by the multiplication closure row of \autoref{def:ring-stability-certificate}.
The left spine indexes a summand by data $((a,k),(i,j))$ with $a+k=n$ and $i+j=a$. Sending this data to $(i,j,k)$ gives the triple equation $(i+j)+k=n$. The right spine indexes a summand by data $((i,c),(j,k))$ with $i+c=n$ and $j+k=c$. Sending this data to $(i,j,k)$ gives $i+(j+k)=n$. Unary additive associativity in the split recursion identifies these two triple predicates, and the displayed reconstructions $(i,j,k)\mapsto((i+j,k),(i,j))$ and $(i,j,k)\mapsto((i,j+k),(j,k))$ are inverse on the corresponding split witnesses.
Apply the assumed split-spine instance of the fold-swap certificate from \autoref{def:two-index-finite-fold-swap-certificate} to this finite reindexing. The remaining pointwise scalar comparison at a fixed triple $(i,j,k)$ is exactly the multiplicative associativity row from \autoref{def:ring-stability-certificate}: $(F_i\cdot_R G_j)\cdot_R H_k\sim_R F_i\cdot_R(G_j\cdot_R H_k)$. The fold-swap field and the finite additive fold congruence theorem \autoref{thm:finite-additive-fold-congruence-coefficient-lists} lift these pointwise comparisons through the finite spines, yielding the displayed scalar classification.
\end{proof}
\begin{theorem}[FPS Cauchy product associativity over RingUp scalars]
\label{thm:fps-cauchy-product-associativity-over-ringup-scalars}
Let $R$ carry a $\RingUp(R)$ certificate with scalar classifier $\sim_R$, zero endpoint $0_R$, addition $+_R$, multiplication $\cdot_R$, and finite additive fold $\mathsf{fold}_{+,R}$. Assume the scalar classifier equivalence fields, additive fold congruence, multiplication congruence, multiplicative associativity, left and right distributivity, and zero multiplication rows retained in \autoref{def:ring-stability-certificate}. Assume the scalar product fold-distribution lemma \autoref{lem:scalar-product-distributes-across-finite-additive-folds} and the split-spine fold-swap field used in \autoref{lem:fps-threefold-cauchy-split-reassociation-ringup}. Let the $\FormalPowerSeriesUp(R)$ interface use the pointwise classifier $\sim_{\mathrm{fps}}$ of \autoref{def:fps-classifier-specification}. Assume its Cauchy product coefficient at each unary index is the finite-spine rule of \autoref{def:fps-cauchy-coefficient-spine}, read over these $\RingUp$ operations: $(F\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}G)_n:=\mathsf{fold}_{+,R}(\mathsf{CauchySp}_n(F,G))$. For carried coefficient sequences $F,G,H:\NatUp\to R$, the displayed products are carried and $\forall n:\NatUp,\quad \bigl((F\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}G)\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}H\bigr)_n\sim_R\bigl(F\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}(G\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}H)\bigr)_n$. Consequently, $(F\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}G)\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}H\sim_{\mathrm{fps}}F\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}(G\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}H)$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FpsUp.FpsSingletonCauchyProduct\_assoc\_classifier}
\begin{proof}
The Cauchy-product closure row in \autoref{def:fps-stability-certificate} carries $F\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}G$ and $G\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}H$, and then carries the two outer products. By the pointwise classifier of \autoref{def:fps-classifier-specification}, it remains to compare the scalar coefficients at an arbitrary unary index.
Fix $n:\NatUp$. The left-associated coefficient unfolds to $\mathsf{fold}_{+,R}\bigl(\mathsf{CauchySp}_n(F\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}G,H)\bigr)$. For a split $(a,k)\in\mathsf{Split}(n)$, the corresponding summand is $(F\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}G)_a\cdot_R H_k$. The inner product coefficient at $a$ is $\mathsf{fold}_{+,R}\bigl(\mathsf{CauchySp}_a(F,G)\bigr)$. Applying the right-multiplication half of \autoref{lem:scalar-product-distributes-across-finite-additive-folds} classifies the summand with the inner finite fold over $\mathsf{map}_{(i,j)\in\mathsf{Split}(a)}\bigl((F_i\cdot_R G_j)\cdot_R H_k\bigr)$. The finite additive fold congruence theorem \autoref{thm:finite-additive-fold-congruence-coefficient-lists} lifts these inner comparisons through the outer split spine, giving $\bigl((F\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}G)\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}H\bigr)_n\sim_R\mathsf{fold}_{+,R}\bigl(\mathsf{TripSp}^{\ell}_n(F,G,H)\bigr)$.
The right-associated coefficient unfolds to $\mathsf{fold}_{+,R}\bigl(\mathsf{CauchySp}_n(F,G\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}H)\bigr)$. For a split $(i,c)\in\mathsf{Split}(n)$, the summand is $F_i\cdot_R(G\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}H)_c$. The inner product coefficient at $c$ is $\mathsf{fold}_{+,R}\bigl(\mathsf{CauchySp}_c(G,H)\bigr)$. Applying the left-multiplication half of \autoref{lem:scalar-product-distributes-across-finite-additive-folds} classifies this summand with the inner finite fold over $\mathsf{map}_{(j,k)\in\mathsf{Split}(c)}\bigl(F_i\cdot_R(G_j\cdot_R H_k)\bigr)$. Again \autoref{thm:finite-additive-fold-congruence-coefficient-lists} lifts through the outer split spine and gives $\bigl(F\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}(G\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}H)\bigr)_n\sim_R\mathsf{fold}_{+,R}\bigl(\mathsf{TripSp}^{r}_n(F,G,H)\bigr)$.
By \autoref{lem:fps-threefold-cauchy-split-reassociation-ringup}, $\mathsf{fold}_{+,R}\bigl(\mathsf{TripSp}^{\ell}_n(F,G,H)\bigr)\sim_R\mathsf{fold}_{+,R}\bigl(\mathsf{TripSp}^{r}_n(F,G,H)\bigr)$. Classifier transitivity composes the left expansion, this threefold split comparison, and the symmetry of the right expansion, giving $\bigl((F\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}G)\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}H\bigr)_n\sim_R\bigl(F\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}(G\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}H)\bigr)_n$. Since $n$ was arbitrary, \autoref{def:fps-classifier-specification} packages these coefficientwise scalar comparisons as the displayed $\FormalPowerSeriesUp$ classifier judgment.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FPS Cauchy product multiplicative semigroup laws]
\label{thm:fps-cauchy-product-multiplicative-semigroup-laws}
Let $R$ carry a $\RingUp(R)$ certificate with scalar classifier $\sim_R$, zero endpoint $0_R$, addition $+_R$, multiplication $\cdot_R$, and finite additive fold $\mathsf{fold}_{+,R}$. \begin{enumerate}

  \item If $F\sim_{\mathrm{fps}}F'$ and $G\sim_{\mathrm{fps}}G'$, then $F\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}G\sim_{\mathrm{fps}}F'\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}G'$.
  \item $(F\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}G)\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}H\sim_{\mathrm{fps}}F\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}(G\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}H)$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier part is exactly the Cauchy-product closure row retained by \autoref{def:fps-stability-certificate}. With $F$ and $G$ carried, that row returns a carried product $F\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}G$ in the carrier of \autoref{def:fps-carrier}.

For compatibility with the classifier, apply \autoref{thm:fps-cauchy-product-classifier-congruence}. Its hypotheses are precisely the carried coefficient sequences together with the two pointwise classifier comparisons supplied by \autoref{def:fps-classifier-specification}, and its conclusion is the displayed congruence of Cauchy products.

The associativity law is \autoref{thm:fps-cauchy-product-associativity-over-ringup-scalars}, read over the same scalar certificate, pointwise classifier, and coefficient rule. The theorem first carries the two intermediate products by \autoref{def:fps-stability-certificate}, then identifies the two associated products under $\sim_{\mathrm{fps}}$. These three clauses are exactly the multiplicative semigroup interface required downstream.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FPS Cauchy product unit laws]
\label{thm:fps-cauchy-product-unit-laws}
Let $R$ carry a $\RingUp(R)$ certificate with scalar classifier $\sim_R$, zero endpoint $0_R$, multiplicative unit endpoint $1_R$, addition $+_R$, multiplication $\cdot_R$, and finite additive fold $\mathsf{fold}_{+,R}$. Assume the scalar classifier equivalence fields, zero self-classification, additive congruence, additive zero rows, multiplication closure, multiplication congruence, left and right multiplicative unit rows, and left and right zero multiplication rows retained in \autoref{def:ring-stability-certificate}. Let the $\FormalPowerSeriesUp(R)$ interface use the pointwise classifier $\sim_{\mathrm{fps}}$ of \autoref{def:fps-classifier-specification} and the Cauchy coefficient rule of \autoref{def:fps-cauchy-coefficient-spine}, read over these $\RingUp$ operations. If $F:\NatUp\to R$ is carried, then $1_{\mathrm{fps}}$ is carried, the products $1_{\mathrm{fps}}\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}F$ and $F\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}1_{\mathrm{fps}}$ are carried, and
$$
1_{\mathrm{fps}}\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}F\sim_{\mathrm{fps}}F
\qquad\text{and}\qquad
F\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}1_{\mathrm{fps}}\sim_{\mathrm{fps}}F.
$$
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FpsUp.FpsSingletonCauchyProduct\_unit\_laws}
\begin{proof}
The unit series is carried coefficientwise. At index $0$, the coefficient is $1_R$, carried by the multiplicative monoid source inside the $\RingUp$ certificate. At every successor index, the coefficient is $0_R$, carried by the zero endpoint row retained in \autoref{def:ring-stability-certificate}. The Cauchy-product closure row in \autoref{def:fps-stability-certificate} then carries both products.

For the left unit law, fix a unary index $n$. If $n=0$, \autoref{lem:fps-zero-index-cauchy-spine} reduces the product coefficient to the singleton fold on $(1_{\mathrm{fps}})_0\cdot_R F_0$. The coefficient rule of \autoref{def:fps-cauchy-product-unit-series} reads this as $1_R\cdot_R F_0$, and the scalar multiplicative left-unit row from \autoref{def:ring-stability-certificate} classifies it with $F_0$. The singleton finite-fold endpoint is transported by the scalar additive zero row and classifier transitivity.

Now let $n=\mathsf{succ}(m)$. The finite split recursion for $\mathsf{Split}(n)$ has exactly one summand whose left index is $0$ and all remaining summands have successor left index. The head summand is $1_R\cdot_R F_n$ and is classified with $F_n$ by the scalar left-unit row. Every remaining summand has the form $(1_{\mathrm{fps}})_{\mathsf{succ}(i)}\cdot_R F_j$, hence $0_R\cdot_R F_j$, and the left zero multiplication row classifies it with $0_R$.

The zero-summand part of the tail is folded to $0_R$ by \autoref{thm:zero-convolution-summands-fold-zero}. Additive fold congruence transports the pointwise comparisons through the finite split spine, and the additive right-zero row removes the folded zero tail. Thus $(1_{\mathrm{fps}}\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}F)_n\sim_R F_n$.

For the right unit law, the zero index is analogous: \autoref{lem:fps-zero-index-cauchy-spine} gives the singleton fold on $F_0\cdot_R(1_{\mathrm{fps}})_0$, the unit-series coefficient rule reads the right factor as $1_R$, and the scalar right-unit row classifies the coefficient with $F_0$.

At $n=\mathsf{succ}(m)$, the finite split recursion has exactly one summand whose right index is $0$ and all remaining summands have successor right index. The head summand is $F_n\cdot_R1_R$ and is classified with $F_n$ by the scalar right-unit row. Each remaining summand is $F_i\cdot_R0_R$, classified with $0_R$ by the right zero multiplication row. The same zero-summand fold theorem, additive fold congruence, and additive zero row collapse the tail.

Since $n$ was arbitrary in both directions, \autoref{def:fps-classifier-specification} packages the coefficientwise scalar comparisons as the two displayed $\FormalPowerSeriesUp$ classifier judgments.
\end{proof}

(F\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}N)_n\sim_R(0_{\mathrm{fps}})_n
\qquad\text{and}\qquad
(N\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}F)_n\sim_R(0_{\mathrm{fps}})_n.
$$
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FpsUp.FpsSingletonCoeffwiseInverse\_zero\_laws}
\begin{proof}
The definition of coefficientwise inverse data carries $N$. The pointwise addition closure row in \autoref{def:fps-stability-certificate} carries $F\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}N$ and $N\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}F$, and \autoref{thm:fps-pointwise-additive-monoid-laws} carries the zero series.

Fix $n:\NatUp$. The inverse-data hypothesis gives
$$
N_n\sim_R -_R(F_n).
$$
By \autoref{def:fps-pointwise-addition-coefficient},
$$
(F\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}N)_n=F_n+_R N_n.
$$
Scalar classifier reflexivity at $F_n$ and scalar addition congruence transport the inverse-data comparison under the common left summand:
$$
F_n+_R N_n\sim_R F_n+_R(-_R(F_n)).
$$
The scalar right inverse law \autoref{thm:ring-add-right-inverse} classifies the right endpoint with $0_R$. Since \autoref{def:fps-pointwise-additive-monoid-instance} gives $(0_{\mathrm{fps}})_n:=0_R$, transitivity gives
$$
(F\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}N)_n\sim_R(0_{\mathrm{fps}})_n.
$$

For the other order, \autoref{def:fps-pointwise-addition-coefficient} gives
$$
(N\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}F)_n=N_n+_R F_n.
$$
Scalar addition congruence transports the same inverse-data comparison under the common right summand:
$$
N_n+_R F_n\sim_R(-_R(F_n))+_R F_n.
$$
The scalar additive left inverse row retained in \autoref{def:ring-stability-certificate} classifies this endpoint with $0_R$, and the zero-series coefficient rule again gives $(0_{\mathrm{fps}})_n:=0_R$. Thus
$$
(N\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}F)_n\sim_R(0_{\mathrm{fps}})_n.
$$

Since $n$ was arbitrary, \autoref{def:fps-classifier-specification} packages the two coefficientwise scalar comparisons as the two displayed $\FormalPowerSeriesUp$ classifier judgments.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FPS pointwise additive group laws]
\label{thm:fps-pointwise-additive-group-laws}
Let $R$ carry a $\RingUp(R)$ certificate with scalar classifier $\sim_R$, zero endpoint $0_R$, addition $+_R$, and scalar additive inverse operation $-_R$. Assume the scalar classifier equivalence, zero self-classification, addition closure, addition congruence, additive associativity, additive zero rows, additive inverse closure, and both scalar additive inverse laws retained in \autoref{def:ring-stability-certificate} and \autoref{thm:ring-add-right-inverse}. Then the data of \autoref{def:fps-pointwise-additive-monoid-instance}, together with the coefficientwise inverse operation $-_{\mathrm{fps}}$ of \autoref{def:fps-coefficientwise-additive-inverse}, form the concrete additive group interface on $\mathsf{FPSCarrier}_R$: $0_{\mathrm{fps}}$ is carried, $F\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}G$ is carried whenever $F$ and $G$ are carried, $\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}$ is pointwise congruent, $\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}$ is associative under $\sim_{\mathrm{fps}}$, both zero laws hold under $\sim_{\mathrm{fps}}$, $-_{\mathrm{fps}}F$ is carried whenever $F$ is carried, $-_{\mathrm{fps}}F$ is coefficientwise inverse data for $F$, and both inverse-zero laws
$$
F\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}(-_{\mathrm{fps}}F)\sim_{\mathrm{fps}}0_{\mathrm{fps}}
\qquad\text{and}\qquad
(-_{\mathrm{fps}}F)\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}F\sim_{\mathrm{fps}}0_{\mathrm{fps}}
$$
hold for every carried coefficient sequence $F:\NatUp\to R$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FpsUp.FpsSingletonPointwiseAdditiveGroup\_laws}
\begin{proof}
The carrier, congruence, associativity, and zero-law fields are exactly the fields supplied by \autoref{thm:fps-pointwise-additive-monoid-laws}, read over the carrier, classifier, pointwise addition, and zero series fixed in \autoref{def:fps-pointwise-additive-monoid-instance}.

It remains to fit the inverse operation into the same public algebra package. Let $F:\NatUp\to R$ be carried. For each coefficient index $n:\NatUp$, scalar additive inverse closure carries $-_R(F_n)$, so the coefficient rule $(-_{\mathrm{fps}}F)_n:= -_R(F_n)$ carries the whole sequence $-_{\mathrm{fps}}F$. The same coefficient rule, together with scalar classifier reflexivity at $-_R(F_n)$, makes $-_{\mathrm{fps}}F$ coefficientwise inverse data for $F$ in the sense of \autoref{def:fps-coefficientwise-additive-inverse}.

Apply \autoref{thm:fps-coefficientwise-additive-inverse-zero-laws} with $N=-_{\mathrm{fps}}F$. Its hypotheses are precisely the retained scalar inverse, congruence, closure, and zero rows, plus the right inverse law \autoref{thm:ring-add-right-inverse}. The conclusion gives the two displayed inverse-zero laws under $\sim_{\mathrm{fps}}$.

Thus the ten fields just listed form a single concrete additive group interface for the downstream formal-power-series algebra layer: zero carrier, sum carrier, pointwise congruence, associativity, left zero, right zero, inverse carrier, inverse-data fit, right inverse-zero, and left inverse-zero.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FPS zero series absorbs Cauchy product over RingUp scalars]
\label{thm:fps-zero-series-cauchy-product-absorption-over-ringup-scalars}
Let $R$ carry a $\RingUp(R)$ certificate with scalar classifier $\sim_R$, zero endpoint $0_R$, addition $+_R$, multiplication $\cdot_R$, and finite additive fold $\mathsf{fold}_{+,R}$. Assume the scalar classifier equivalence fields, zero self-classification, additive congruence, additive left-zero, multiplication closure, and left and right zero multiplication rows retained in \autoref{def:ring-stability-certificate}. Let the $\FormalPowerSeriesUp(R)$ interface use the pointwise classifier $\sim_{\mathrm{fps}}$ of \autoref{def:fps-classifier-specification}, the zero series $Z:=0_{\mathrm{fps}}$ of \autoref{def:fps-pointwise-additive-monoid-instance}, and the Cauchy coefficient rule of \autoref{def:fps-cauchy-coefficient-spine}, read over these $\RingUp$ operations. For every carried coefficient sequence $F:\NatUp\to R$, the two Cauchy products $Z\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}F$ and $F\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}Z$ are carried and
$$
Z\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}F\sim_{\mathrm{fps}}Z
\qquad\text{and}\qquad
F\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}Z\sim_{\mathrm{fps}}Z.
$$
Equivalently, for every unary coefficient index $n:\NatUp$,
$$
(Z\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}F)_n\sim_R Z_n
\qquad\text{and}\qquad
(F\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}Z)_n\sim_R Z_n.
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The zero series is carried coefficientwise because \autoref{def:fps-pointwise-additive-monoid-instance} gives $Z_n=0_R$ for every $n$, and the scalar zero self-classification row supplies the carried scalar endpoint. The Cauchy-product closure row in \autoref{def:fps-stability-certificate} therefore carries $Z\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}F$ and $F\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}Z$ from the carriedness of $Z$ and $F$.

Fix $n:\NatUp$, and write $\mathsf{ZeroSp}_n$ for the finite spine indexed by $\mathsf{Split}(n)$ whose every displayed entry is $0_R$. For the left product, each split $(i,j)\in\mathsf{Split}(n)$ contributes the scalar summand
$$
Z_i\cdot_R F_j.
$$
The zero-series coefficient rule gives the scalar comparison $Z_i\sim_R0_R$. Together with scalar classifier reflexivity at $F_j$, the multiplication-congruence row retained in \autoref{def:ring-stability-certificate} compares $Z_i\cdot_R F_j$ with $0_R\cdot_R F_j$; the left zero multiplication row retained in \autoref{def:ring-stability-certificate} then classifies $0_R\cdot_R F_j$ with $0_R$. If the coefficient comparison, multiplication-congruence step, or zero multiplication row is read in the opposite orientation, classifier symmetry supplies the displayed orientation. Structural induction on the finite split spine, using the nil and cons clauses of \autoref{def:list-classifier-specification}, assembles these pointwise scalar comparisons into
$$
\mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}\bigl(\sim_R,\mathsf{CauchySp}_n(Z,F),\mathsf{ZeroSp}_n\bigr).
$$
Applying \autoref{thm:zero-convolution-summands-fold-zero} to this all-zero Cauchy summand witness gives
$$
\mathsf{fold}_{+,R}(\mathsf{CauchySp}_n(Z,F))\sim_R0_R.
$$
By the Cauchy coefficient rule in \autoref{def:fps-cauchy-coefficient-spine}, the left endpoint is $(Z\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}F)_n$. By the zero-series coefficient rule, $Z_n=0_R$. Hence
$$
(Z\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}F)_n\sim_RZ_n.
$$

For the right product, each split $(i,j)\in\mathsf{Split}(n)$ contributes
$$
F_i\cdot_R Z_j.
$$
The zero-series coefficient rule gives the scalar comparison $Z_j\sim_R0_R$. Together with scalar classifier reflexivity at $F_i$, the multiplication-congruence row retained in \autoref{def:ring-stability-certificate} compares $F_i\cdot_R Z_j$ with $F_i\cdot_R0_R$; the right zero multiplication row classifies $F_i\cdot_R0_R$ with $0_R$, again using classifier symmetry if a row is oriented oppositely. The same finite-spine induction over \autoref{def:list-classifier-specification} yields
$$
\mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}\bigl(\sim_R,\mathsf{CauchySp}_n(F,Z),\mathsf{ZeroSp}_n\bigr).
$$
A second application of \autoref{thm:zero-convolution-summands-fold-zero} gives
$$
\mathsf{fold}_{+,R}(\mathsf{CauchySp}_n(F,Z))\sim_R0_R,
$$
which is exactly
$$
(F\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}Z)_n\sim_RZ_n
$$
after unfolding \autoref{def:fps-cauchy-coefficient-spine} and the coefficient rule for $Z$.

Since $n$ was arbitrary, the pointwise classifier specification in \autoref{def:fps-classifier-specification} packages the two coefficientwise scalar comparisons as
$$
Z\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}F\sim_{\mathrm{fps}}Z
\qquad\text{and}\qquad
F\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}Z\sim_{\mathrm{fps}}Z.
$$
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FpsUp.FpsSingletonAddFold\_zero\_series\_absorption}

\begin{theorem}[FPS Cauchy product distributes over pointwise addition]
\label{thm:fps-cauchy-product-distributes-over-pointwise-addition}
In the concrete singleton formal-power-series carrier, let $F$, $G$, and $H$ be singleton coefficient histories. Write $\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}$ for pointwise addition and $\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}$ for the Cauchy product defined by the singleton coefficient spine. Then both distributed product comparisons are classified by $\mathsf{FpsSingletonClassifier}$:
$$
F\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}(G\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}H)
\sim_{\mathsf{FpsSingletonClassifier}}
(F\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}G)\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}(F\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}H)
$$
and
$$
(F\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}G)\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}H
\sim_{\mathsf{FpsSingletonClassifier}}
(F\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}H)\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}(G\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}H).
$$
Moreover, the two undistributed Cauchy-product endpoints
$$
F\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}(G\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}H)
\qquad\text{and}\qquad
(F\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}G)\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}H
$$
are each classified with the empty history endpoint.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The pointwise addition interface in \autoref{def:fps-pointwise-additive-monoid-instance} supplies the singleton addition endpoints, and the Cauchy-product coefficient rule in \autoref{def:fps-cauchy-coefficient-spine} expresses each product coefficient as the finite additive fold over the split spine.

In the singleton carrier, every displayed coefficient spine collapses to the empty history endpoint. The checked zero-series absorption row above gives the same empty endpoint for Cauchy products whose split spine has only empty scalar contributions, and the continuation endpoint rows identify the folded singleton coefficients with that endpoint.

Applying these endpoint identifications on the left-distributive spine gives a classifier comparison between $F\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}(G\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}H)$ and $(F\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}G)\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}(F\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}H)$. The same argument on the right-distributive spine gives the comparison between $(F\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}G)\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}H$ and $(F\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}H)\oplus_{\mathrm{fps}}(G\odot_{\mathrm{fps}}H)$. The singleton empty-carrier collapse also records the two undistributed product endpoints as classified with $\mathsf{BHist.Empty}$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FpsUp.FpsSingletonCauchyProduct\_distributes\_over\_addition\_classifier}
