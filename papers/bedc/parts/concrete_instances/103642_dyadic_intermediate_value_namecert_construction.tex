Following Bishop and Bridges constructive analysis, the $\DyadicIntermediateValueUp$ packet records the finite dyadic bisection surface that sits between a displayed intermediate-value sign route and the later real-name seal. It is a neighbour of the $\IntermediateValueUp$ route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-intermediatevalue-namecert}: the packet consumes the finite branch discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicbisection-namecert}, the endpoint carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedinterval-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the finite observation windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal real-name boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The chapter names the finite bisection certificate consumed by Real and Completion phases; it does not select a root, quotient real names, decide real trichotomy, invoke open-cover compactness, or assert ambient completeness of $\RealUp$.

\begin{definition}[Dyadic intermediate-value carrier]
\label{def:dyadic-intermediate-value-carrier}
A $\DyadicIntermediateValueUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
D=(I,S,B,Q,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $I$ is the $\LocatedIntervalUp$ endpoint packet, including lower and upper endpoint windows, rational cells, and dyadic refinements visible to the bisection route.
\item $S$ is the displayed sign-change ledger inherited from an $\IntermediateValueUp$ sign route; it records only the inspected endpoint signs and the midpoint signs used by the finite branch prefix.
\item $B$ is the $\DyadicBisectionUp$ branch ledger, retaining the finite sequence of dyadic child intervals whose displayed signs preserve the intermediate-value boundary.
\item $Q$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row. It records the dyadic radius, midpoint arithmetic, and width comparison used to stop the branch prefix at the requested precision.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window family for the retained endpoint names and midpoint observations.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row obtained from $W$ after the dyadic tolerance row $Q$ has fixed the finite window.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal-facing row available only after $I,S,B,Q,W,R$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the interval, sign ledger, bisection ledger, dyadic tolerance, stream windows, regular readback, real seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the route
$$
I,S \longmapsto B,Q \longmapsto W,R \longmapsto E .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\DyadicIntermediateValueUp$, $\IntermediateValueUp$, $\DyadicBisectionUp$, $\LocatedIntervalUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DyadicIntermediateValueUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate admits exactly these rows. Its classifier compares accepted packets only through $I,S,B,Q,W,R,E,H,C,P,N$ and has no coordinate for a selected root, quotient real equality, trichotomy, open-cover compactness, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Dyadic intermediate-value NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:dyadic-intermediate-value-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\DyadicIntermediateValueUp$ carrier
$$
D=(I,S,B,Q,W,R,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\DyadicIntermediateValueUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed located interval, sign ledger, bisection ledger, dyadic tolerance row, stream windows, regular readback, Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item sign-ledger visibility, requiring every endpoint or midpoint sign consumed by the packet to pass through $I$ and $S$ before a branch row in $B$ is read;
\item dyadic branch exactness, requiring every retained child interval in $B$ to expose its midpoint arithmetic and width comparison through $Q$;
\item finite-window discipline, requiring every retained endpoint read to pass through $W$ and then through $R$ before the Real-facing row $E$ is exposed;
\item transport and replay stability through $H$ and $C$;
\item provenance exhaustion through $P$ and the local naming row $N$.
\end{enumerate}
No $\DyadicIntermediateValueUp$ obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:dyadic-intermediate-value-carrier}. The projection exposes exactly the located interval, sign ledger, dyadic branch ledger, tolerance row, finite windows, regular readback, Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

Sign-ledger visibility reads $I$ and $S$ before $B$. Endpoint and midpoint sign rows are therefore attached to the displayed located interval cells before any retained child interval is named.

Dyadic branch exactness reads $B$ together with $Q$. Each branch step carries a midpoint and a width comparison through the dyadic arithmetic row, so the finite prefix cannot be replaced by an unrecorded host search.

The finite-window and Real-facing stages are $W,R,E$. The retained endpoints are first observed through StreamName windows, then repacked as regular rational data, and only then offered to the Real seal row.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same route. Since the carrier has no coordinate for selected roots, quotient real equality, trichotomy, open-cover compactness, or ambient completeness, those principles cannot appear as local obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Dyadic intermediate-value bisection route]
\label{thm:dyadic-intermediate-value-bisection-route}
Every branch read exported by an accepted $\DyadicIntermediateValueUp$ packet factors through
$$
\IntermediateValueUp \longmapsto I,S \longmapsto B,Q \longmapsto W,R .
$$
Thus the packet consumes the intermediate-value sign route only as displayed finite sign data, refines it through a $\DyadicBisectionUp$ branch ledger and a $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row, and exposes retained windows only through $\StreamNameUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with an exported branch read. The only sign data visible to the packet are the rows $I$ and $S$, so the read first passes through the located interval endpoint carrier and the displayed sign-change ledger inherited from the neighbouring intermediate-value route.

Next the retained child interval is read from $B$. The dyadic tolerance row $Q$ supplies the midpoint arithmetic and width comparison attached to the branch prefix, so the branch read remains a finite dyadic bisection route.

Finally the retained endpoints are exposed through $W$ and $R$. The StreamName windows record the finite observations, and the RegSeqRat row repacks them as regular rational readback. No selected root, quotient real, trichotomy decision, compactness argument, or ambient completeness theorem is used by the branch route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Dyadic intermediate-value sign-branch exhaustion]
\label{thm:dyadic-intermediate-value-sign-branch-exhaustion}

Finally apply \autoref{thm:dyadic-intermediate-value-real-seal-handoff}. The handoff to $E$ is downstream from $I,S,B,Q,W,R$ and is preserved by $H,C,P,N$, so no hidden search or root-selection coordinate can be inserted after the branch row.
