\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\UnitaryGroupUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-unitarygroup-namecert}

The unitary-group interface $\UnitaryGroupUp$ packages the Lie group of inner-product-preserving automorphisms of a Hilbert space carrier. It depends on $\NameCert_{\HilbertUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-hilbert-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\LieGroupUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-liegroup-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:unitarygroup-carrier}

\begin{theorem}[UnitaryGroup source classifier obligation]
\label{thm:unitarygroup-source-classifier-obligation}
The source carrier for $\UnitaryGroupUp$ is a $\HilbertUp$ endpoint equipped with a $\LieGroupUp$ operation row and a displayed inner-product-preservation witness. Its classifier is the combined row obtained from the $\HilbertUp$ endpoint classifier, the $\LieGroupUp$ operation classifier, and transport of the preservation row along the accepted histories.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Unpack the two dependency certificates named in the introduction. The $\HilbertUp$ source supplies the carried endpoint row and the Hilbert classifier, while the $\LieGroupUp$ source supplies the multiplication, inverse, and unit operation rows over the same visible history surface.

The unitary carrier is then the $\mathsf{BHist}$ package that stores those two source projections together with a preservation witness for the Hilbert inner product. A comparison is accepted only when the Hilbert endpoint classifier, the Lie-group operation classifier, and the preservation witness are all transported through the same $\hsame$ evidence.

Repacking these rows through $\Cont$ and $\NameCert$ gives the required classifier surface. No host equality of operators is consumed; the accepted comparison is exactly the displayed BEDC row transport.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[UnitaryGroup operation preservation obligation]
\label{thm:unitarygroup-operation-preservation-obligation}
The multiplication, inverse, and unit rows of $\UnitaryGroupUp$ are inherited from the $\LieGroupUp$ source certificate, and each inherited row preserves the $\HilbertUp$ inner-product classifier on every carried endpoint. In particular, a product, inverse, or unit endpoint is admitted by the unitary carrier only when the corresponding $\LieGroupUp$ operation row repacks with the displayed Hilbert inner-product-preservation witness.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Project the operation rows from $\NameCert_{\LieGroupUp}$. The product row supplies a carried composite endpoint, the inverse row supplies a carried inverse endpoint, and the unit row supplies the identity endpoint of the same source group package.

Next project the Hilbert classifier and inner-product rows from $\NameCert_{\HilbertUp}$. For each Lie-group operation, transport the input endpoint histories through $\hsame$, apply the operation row through $\Cont$, and carry the inner-product-preservation witness along the resulting endpoint comparison.

The resulting endpoint is repacked as a $\UnitaryGroupUp$ row precisely because it contains both pieces: the inherited Lie-group operation evidence and the Hilbert preservation witness. Thus operation closure does not extend the source group structure; it restricts it to the inner-product-preserving subcarrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[UnitaryGroup stability ledger exactness obligation]
\label{thm:unitarygroup-stability-ledger-exactness-obligation}
The $\UnitaryGroupUp$ certificate has coverage, soundness, and completeness for exactly the Hilbert carrier row, the $\LieGroupUp$ operation rows, the inner-product preservation rows, the classifier transport rows, and the dependency ledger naming $\NameCert_{\HilbertUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\LieGroupUp}$. No additional operator, spectral, measure, or channel row is part of this ledger.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Coverage reads any carried unitary endpoint back into its visible components: the $\HilbertUp$ carrier row, the $\LieGroupUp$ operation rows, the preservation witness, the classifier transport evidence, and the two source $\NameCert$ dependencies.

Soundness is the forward projection. Given those ledger entries, the $\mathsf{BHist}$ carrier repacks the endpoint, $\hsame$ supplies the classifier transport, and $\Cont$ supplies the operation-composition rows needed by multiplication, inverse, and unit.

Completeness is the reverse reading. Any visible row accepted by the unitary carrier must be one of the listed Hilbert, Lie-group, preservation, transport, or dependency rows, because the certificate exposes no other ledger field. Hence the exactness boundary is precisely the displayed ledger and not a larger analytic operator surface.
\end{proof}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:unitarygroup-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[UnitaryGroup carrier and classifier obligation]\label{thm:unitarygroup-carrier-classifier-obligation}
The carrier for $\UnitaryGroupUp$ is the $\HilbertUp$ carrier together with the $\LieGroupUp$ automorphism rows whose source and target Hilbert carriers are classifier-compatible. Its classifier is inherited componentwise from the $\HilbertUp$ carrier classifier and the $\LieGroupUp$ automorphism classifier, restricted to histories that preserve the displayed Hilbert source and target rows.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.UnitaryGroupUp.UnitaryGroupCarrier\_classifier\_obligation}
\leanvariant{BEDC.Derived.UnitaryGroupUp.UnitaryGroupCarrierClassifier\_obligation}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the carrier, classifier, source, and dependency fields of the ten-field roadmap template. The introduction supplies exactly the two certificate dependencies: $\NameCert_{\HilbertUp}$ for Hilbert carriers and classifiers, and $\NameCert_{\LieGroupUp}$ for automorphism rows and their group surface.

The carrier is obtained by pairing a Hilbert carrier row with a Lie-group automorphism row whose source and target projections land back in the Hilbert carrier and are related by the Hilbert classifier. Repacking these projections gives the displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ carrier predicate for $\UnitaryGroupUp$.

The classifier field is then the restricted componentwise classifier. The Hilbert component is transported by $\NameCert_{\HilbertUp}$, the automorphism component by $\NameCert_{\LieGroupUp}$, and the restriction records that both transported endpoints remain classifier-compatible Hilbert carriers.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[UnitaryGroup operation stability obligation]\label{thm:unitarygroup-operation-stability-obligation}
The product, inverse, and identity rows of $\UnitaryGroupUp$ are the $\LieGroupUp$ operation rows restricted to the carrier of \autoref{thm:unitarygroup-carrier-classifier-obligation}. Product, inverse, and identity witnesses are stable under the displayed classifier because their source, target, and result rows remain Hilbert-carrier-preserving automorphisms.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.UnitaryGroupUp.UnitaryGroupOperation\_stability\_obligation}
\begin{proof}
Project the operation fields of the ten-field roadmap template after the carrier has been fixed. The only available product, inverse, and identity operations are the rows exposed by $\NameCert_{\LieGroupUp}$.

For product, compose two accepted automorphism rows through the $\LieGroupUp$ product row and then project the Hilbert source and target rows. Because each factor preserves the Hilbert carrier, the composite also lands in the same classifier-compatible Hilbert carrier rows.

For inverse and identity, use the corresponding $\LieGroupUp$ rows and apply the same restriction. The inverse row swaps source and target while preserving classifier compatibility, and the identity row is the Hilbert-carrier-preserving automorphism already exposed by the restricted carrier.

Stability follows by transporting every source, target, and result row along the classifier built in \autoref{thm:unitarygroup-carrier-classifier-obligation}. Repacking those transported rows gives the same product, inverse, and identity witnesses in the restricted $\UnitaryGroupUp$ package.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[UnitaryGroup inner-product preservation obligation]\label{thm:unitarygroup-inner-product-preservation-obligation}
Every automorphism carried by $\UnitaryGroupUp$ transports the $\HilbertUp$ inner-product classifier to itself. The preservation row is stable under classifier replacement: if the carried automorphism is replaced by an equivalent restricted $\LieGroupUp$ automorphism and the Hilbert carrier is replaced by an equivalent $\HilbertUp$ carrier, the transported inner-product row is the same $\HilbertUp$ classifier row.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.UnitaryGroupUp.UnitaryGroupInnerProduct\_preservation\_obligation}
\begin{proof}
Read the inner-product field of the ten-field roadmap template through the $\HilbertUp$ dependency. The Hilbert certificate supplies the inner-product classifier row, while the restricted automorphism carrier supplies the source and target histories on which that classifier is evaluated.

For a carried automorphism, apply its endpoint-preservation row to the Hilbert source and target projections. The result says that transporting an inner-product observation along the automorphism returns an observation classified by the same $\HilbertUp$ inner-product row.

For classifier replacement, first transport the Hilbert carrier by the $\HilbertUp$ classifier and the automorphism by the restricted $\LieGroupUp$ classifier. Then apply the preservation row to the transported endpoints. Repacking gives the identical inner-product-preservation witness for the classified $\UnitaryGroupUp$ row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[UnitaryGroupUp public NameCert export]
\label{thm:unitarygroup-public-namecert-export}
The carrier-classifier row of \autoref{thm:unitarygroup-carrier-classifier-obligation}, the product, inverse, and identity stability rows of \autoref{thm:unitarygroup-operation-stability-obligation}, the inner-product preservation row of \autoref{thm:unitarygroup-inner-product-preservation-obligation}, and the ledger exactness row of \autoref{thm:unitarygroup-ledger-exactness-obligation} assemble the public $\NameCert_{\UnitaryGroupUp}$ export over the source certificates $\NameCert_{\HilbertUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\LieGroupUp}$. The exported surface consists exactly of restricted Hilbert-carrier-preserving Lie-group automorphisms, their inherited group-operation rows, the displayed Hilbert inner-product-preservation witness, classifier transport for those rows, and the two dependency rows. It exports no spectral theorem, operator-algebra theorem, quantum-channel row, or standard bridge claim.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:unitarygroup-carrier-classifier-obligation}. This fixes the public carrier as the $\HilbertUp$ carrier together with the restricted $\LieGroupUp$ automorphism row whose source and target remain classifier-compatible Hilbert carriers. It also fixes the public classifier componentwise from the Hilbert carrier classifier and the Lie-group automorphism classifier, with the Hilbert source and target restrictions retained as visible rows.

Apply \autoref{thm:unitarygroup-operation-stability-obligation}. The only product, inverse, and identity rows available to the exported object are the inherited $\LieGroupUp$ operation rows after restriction to the carrier just fixed. Their stability witnesses say precisely that each operation result remains a Hilbert-carrier-preserving automorphism and that the operation witnesses transport through the public classifier.

Apply \autoref{thm:unitarygroup-inner-product-preservation-obligation}. This supplies the additional unitary condition: every carried automorphism transports the $\HilbertUp$ inner-product classifier to itself, and the preservation row is stable when the Hilbert carrier and the restricted Lie-group automorphism are replaced by classifier-equivalent representatives. Thus the public group operations do not merely preserve the carrier; they preserve the displayed Hilbert inner-product row that defines the unitary subcarrier.

Apply \autoref{thm:unitarygroup-ledger-exactness-obligation}. Coverage projects any accepted $\UnitaryGroupUp$ row to the Hilbert carrier and classifier rows, the restricted Lie-group operation rows, the product, inverse, identity, inner-product-preservation, classifier-transport, and dependency rows. Soundness repacks exactly those rows as an accepted unitary-group package. Completeness excludes every row not listed in that ledger. Therefore the four obligation rows assemble the public $\NameCert_{\UnitaryGroupUp}$ export surface over $\NameCert_{\HilbertUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\LieGroupUp}$, with no additional analytic or bridge component.
\end{proof}
