\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ExteriorAlgebraUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-exterior-algebra-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bourbaki and Mac Lane, the $\ExteriorAlgebraUp$ packet records the alternating algebra generated by a displayed module or vector-space carrier through finite tensor words with signed transposition refusals. It is grounded in the finite tensor-word source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-tensor-algebra-namecert}, the bilinear-form boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bilinform-namecert}, the module carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-module-namecert}, the vector-space specialization of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-vecspace-namecert}, and the quadratic-boundary comparison surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-clifford-namecert}. The packet is a BEDC NameCert carrier for alternating finite words and wedge multiplication; it is not a host quotient algebra, a basis-dependent determinant construction, or an ambient differential-form object.

\begin{definition}[Exterior algebra carrier]
\label{def:exterior-algebra-carrier}
An $\ExteriorAlgebraUp$ carrier over a displayed $\ModuleUp$ row $V$ and scalar $\RingUp$ row $R$ is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
E=(R,V,T,A,S,W,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $R$ is the scalar source row and $V$ is the module or vector-space source row whose carried endpoints supply the letters.
\item $T$ is the consumed $\TensorAlgebraUp$ finite-word row over $R$ and $V$.
\item $A$ is the alternating ledger: whenever two adjacent letters are classifier-equal through the source row, the corresponding repeated-letter word is refused as a nonzero exterior word.
\item $S$ is the signed adjacent-transposition ledger, recording that swapping two adjacent carried letters changes the exterior classifier by the displayed sign row.
\item $W$ is the wedge product row, formed by concatenating two finite tensor words and then reading the result through the alternating and signed-transposition ledgers.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for scalar rows, source letters, tensor words, alternating rows, signed-transposition rows, wedge products, and local classifiers.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from source endpoints through tensor-word formation, alternating reduction, signed transposition, and wedge multiplication.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ExteriorAlgebraUp$, $\TensorAlgebraUp$, $\BilinFormUp$, $\ModuleUp$, $\VecSpaceUp$, $\CliffordUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ExteriorAlgebraUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exports only alternating finite tensor-word data. It introduces no quotient primitive, no selected basis, no determinant expansion, no ambient graded-commutative algebra object, and no differential-form endpoint.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Exterior algebra NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:exterior-algebra-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\ExteriorAlgebraUp$ carrier
$$
E=(R,V,T,A,S,W,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\ExteriorAlgebraUp}$ surface consists exactly of scalar admission through $R$, module or vector-space endpoint admission through $V$, finite tensor-word admission through $T$, alternating repeated-letter refusal through $A$, signed adjacent-transposition transport through $S$, wedge multiplication through $W$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier rows of \autoref{def:exterior-algebra-carrier}. The scalar, source-letter, tensor-word, alternating, signed-transposition, and wedge rows are all displayed before a consumer receives an exterior word.

The tensor source is read through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-tensor-algebra-namecert}. Thus every exterior word first appears as a finite tensor word, and only then passes through the alternating ledger $A$ and the signed adjacent-transposition ledger $S$.

The structural rows close the certificate. Transport by $H$, replay by $C$, provenance by $P$, and naming by $N$ preserve exactly the displayed packet. Because the carrier has no quotient coordinate, selected basis, determinant coordinate, host graded algebra, or differential-form endpoint, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Exterior algebra wedge alternation]
\label{thm:exterior-algebra-wedge-alternation}
Let $x$ and $y$ be carried degree-one letters in the same $\ExteriorAlgebraUp$ carrier $E=(R,V,T,A,S,W,H,C,P,N)$. The wedge rows satisfy the displayed alternating boundary
$$
x \wedge x \sim_E 0
$$
and the signed transposition boundary
$$
x \wedge y \sim_E -\, y \wedge x
$$
whenever the corresponding source-letter rows are accepted by $V$. These boundaries are read through the finite tensor-word row $T$, the alternating ledger $A$, the signed-transposition ledger $S$, and the wedge row $W$; they do not use a quotient algebra, a selected basis, or a host determinant identity.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First read $x$ and $y$ as carried source-letter rows of $V$. The tensor source $T$ admits the length-two words $x\otimes x$, $x\otimes y$, and $y\otimes x$ as finite tensor words.

For the repeated-letter boundary, apply the alternating ledger $A$ to the adjacent pair $x,x$. This row records that the repeated-letter word is classified with the zero exterior word before any ambient quotient is available.

For the mixed boundary, apply the signed adjacent-transposition ledger $S$ to the two tensor words $x\otimes y$ and $y\otimes x$. The sign row is part of the same finite packet, so the comparison is a displayed classifier row rather than a host graded-commutativity theorem.

Finally use the wedge row $W$ to identify each length-two tensor word as the product of the corresponding degree-one exterior words. Transport by $H$ and replay by $C$ keep the source letters, tensor words, alternating rows, sign rows, and wedge products inside the same local NameCert packet.
\end{proof}
