\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\PinGroupUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-pingroup-namecert}

The Pin-group interface $\PinGroupUp$ packages the double cover of the full orthogonal group, extending Spin by a reflection generator. It depends on $\NameCert_{\SpinGroupUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-spingroup-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\CliffordUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-clifford-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:pingroup-carrier}

\begin{definition}[PinGroupUp reflection-extension carrier]
\label{def:pingroup-reflection-extension-carrier}
The $\PinGroupUp$ reflection-extension carrier is the finite $\mathsf{BHist}$
carrier obtained from the $\SpinGroupUp$ carrier of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-spingroup-namecert} by adjoining one displayed
reflection-generator row inside the $\CliffordUp$ carrier of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-clifford-namecert}.  Its classifier is inherited
from $\hsame$-compatible Clifford transport: two displayed pin rows are
classified exactly when the underlying Clifford carrier rows are connected by
the finite $\Cont$ ledger accepted by the source $\NameCert_{\CliffordUp}$ and
their spin subrows are classified by the inherited $\NameCert_{\SpinGroupUp}$
surface.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[PinGroup BHist carrier obligation]
\label{thm:pingroup-bhist-carrier-obligation}
Every $\NameCert_{\PinGroupUp}$ carrier is a $\mathsf{BHist}$ predicate built
from the consumed $\SpinGroupUp$ and $\CliffordUp$ certificate rows. Its
accepted histories record the Spin carrier row, the Clifford multiplication
and grading rows, a finite reflection-generator ledger, and the classifier
boundary used by the displayed orthogonal-action surface. It adds no host
Pin-group carrier, host orthogonal group, or external Clifford algebra object.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the ten-field $\NameCert$ template. The source rows are the
$\SpinGroupUp$ certificate of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-spingroup-namecert} and the $\CliffordUp$
certificate of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-clifford-namecert}, so the
carrier field is read as a predicate on $\mathsf{BHist}$ histories already
accepted by those rows.

The remaining fields attach only classifier, stability, exactness, and ledger
information. The reflection generator and orthogonal-action boundary therefore
enter as certificate rows inside the same history predicate, not as a host
carrier.

Thus the Pin carrier is the displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ predicate generated by
Spin rows, Clifford rows, and the finite generator ledger. Since the template
has no further carrier slot, no external Pin object is exported.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PinGroup reflection-generator obligation]
\label{thm:pingroup-reflection-generator-obligation}
The reflection-generator row of a $\NameCert_{\PinGroupUp}$ certificate is
carried by a finite $\mathsf{Cont}$ ledger witness over the $\CliffordUp$
certificate rows. The row records a unary generator, its classifier-stable
Clifford action, and its compatibility with the Pin carrier. It is not a host
orthogonal transformation and does not import a host reflection group.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the generator row inside the certificate ledger supplied by the
$\CliffordUp$ source chapter
(\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-clifford-namecert}). The ledger witness is a
finite $\mathsf{Cont}$ entry whose endpoints are carried histories, so the
generator is available only through BEDC rows.

Stability is then the stability field of the same ten-field template: replacing
endpoints by $\hsame$ histories preserves the Clifford action row and the Pin
carrier predicate. Exactness says that the generator row is accepted precisely
when those carried Clifford and ledger witnesses are present.

Therefore the reflection generator is a finite ledger witness internal to the
certificate. The displayed orthogonal meaning is a classifier boundary read
from that row, not an imported host transformation.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PinGroup Spin-extension obligation]
\label{thm:pingroup-spin-extension-obligation}
The $\PinGroupUp$ carrier includes the consumed $\SpinGroupUp$ carrier as its
even subcarrier. A Spin witness accepted by
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-spingroup-namecert} embeds into the Pin carrier
through the Clifford even-grade rows and the shared classifier, and its image
uses the identity reflection-generator ledger entry.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the source field for the $\SpinGroupUp$ dependency. Its carrier,
classifier, and exactness rows are already BEDC rows over $\mathsf{BHist}$, so
they may be copied into the Pin carrier field without changing their
classifier.

Use the $\CliffordUp$ grading rows to identify these copied histories as the
even subcarrier inside the Pin predicate. The generator ledger contributes the
identity row, so no odd reflection generator is required for Spin witnesses.

The template's exactness field then states that the embedded Spin rows are
accepted by the Pin carrier exactly through those even Clifford and identity
ledger witnesses. This gives the Spin inclusion as a certificate row rather
than a separate host subgroup.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PinGroup full-orthogonal projection obligation]
\label{thm:pingroup-full-orthogonal-projection-obligation}
The full-orthogonal projection row of a $\NameCert_{\PinGroupUp}$ certificate
records the classifier boundary from the Pin carrier to the displayed
orthogonal-action surface. The row is sourced from the Clifford action and the
finite reflection-generator ledger, and it exports only the carried action
classifier, not a host orthogonal group element.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a Pin carrier witness. By
\autoref{thm:pingroup-bhist-carrier-obligation}, it consists of Spin and
Clifford source rows together with the finite reflection-generator ledger.

Apply the classifier field of the ten-field template to read the displayed
orthogonal-action surface from those rows. The Clifford action supplies the
even part, and \autoref{thm:pingroup-reflection-generator-obligation} supplies
the finite reflection-generator part.

The exactness field identifies the projection row with that classifier
boundary. Hence the public projection records only the BEDC action classifier
and its ledger provenance, while excluding a host orthogonal-group carrier.
\end{proof}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:pingroup-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[PinGroupUp reflection-generator admission]
\label{thm:pingroup-reflection-generator-carrier}
The reflection-generator row displayed in
\autoref{def:pingroup-reflection-extension-carrier} is admitted by the
$\PinGroupUp$ carrier precisely as a $\mathsf{BHist}$ row already carried by the
source $\NameCert_{\CliffordUp}$, together with the finite $\Cont$ provenance
ledger identifying it as the non-spin generator row of the pin surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the generator from the Clifford carrier in
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-clifford-namecert}.  The row is a displayed
$\mathsf{BHist}$ carrier entry, so it is available to the pin carrier without
forming any host orthogonal-group element.

The $\hsame$ classifier and finite $\Cont$ ledger carried by the Clifford
certificate provide the admissibility certificate for transporting the
generator row.  Repacking those rows with the source $\NameCert_{\CliffordUp}$
provenance gives the reflection-generator field of
\autoref{def:pingroup-reflection-extension-carrier}.

No quotient of Clifford units and no equality in a host orthogonal group is
used; the admitted object is only the displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ generator row
and its $\NameCert$ provenance.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SpinGroupUp inclusion surface for PinGroupUp]
\label{thm:pingroup-spingroup-inclusion-surface}
Every $\mathsf{BHist}$ carrier row supplied by
$\NameCert_{\SpinGroupUp}$ embeds into the
\autoref{def:pingroup-reflection-extension-carrier} as the spin subrow of the
$\PinGroupUp$ carrier.  Its classifier, $\Cont$ transport ledger, and
$\NameCert$ provenance are the inherited $\SpinGroupUp$ rows, read inside the
ambient Clifford carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a carried spin row from
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-spingroup-namecert}.  By the construction of
\autoref{def:pingroup-reflection-extension-carrier}, the pin carrier keeps the
whole spin carrier and adds only the displayed reflection-generator row.

The spin classifier is not recomputed.  It is copied as the inherited
$\hsame$-compatible classifier row and transported by the same finite $\Cont$
ledger already accepted by $\NameCert_{\SpinGroupUp}$.

The Clifford dependency supplies the ambient carrier in which both the spin
subrow and the reflection-generator row live.  Therefore the inclusion surface
is a kernel-level $\mathsf{BHist}$ inclusion with $\NameCert$ provenance, not an
identification with a host orthogonal subgroup.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PinGroupUp Clifford conjugation ledger]
\label{thm:pingroup-clifford-conjugation-ledger}
The Clifford conjugation data visible to $\PinGroupUp$ is exactly the finite
ledger consisting of the spin subrow, the reflection-generator row, the
$\hsame$ classifier transports between their Clifford carrier representatives,
and the $\Cont$ provenance rows supplied by
$\NameCert_{\CliffordUp}$.  This ledger records provenance for pin conjugation
without exporting a quotient action or host orthogonal-group equality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the two source rows from
\autoref{def:pingroup-reflection-extension-carrier}: the inherited
$\SpinGroupUp$ carrier row and the displayed Clifford reflection-generator row.
Both are already $\mathsf{BHist}$ rows in the Clifford carrier.

Apply the $\hsame$-compatible transport surface of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-clifford-namecert}.  Its finite $\Cont$ ledger
records how the Clifford representatives are transported under the displayed
conjugation rows, and its $\NameCert$ provenance records the source of each
projection.

Combining these projections gives the exact conjugation ledger exposed by
$\PinGroupUp$.  Since the ledger consists only of $\mathsf{BHist}$,
$\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows, it exports no quotient of Clifford
representatives and no equality in a host orthogonal group.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PinGroupUp Clifford-reflection product closure]
\label{thm:pingroup-clifford-reflection-product-closure}
Let a displayed $\PinGroupUp$ carrier expose the inherited spin row $S$, the
Clifford carrier row $C$, the reflection-generator row $R$, and the Clifford
product ledger $\mu$ supplied by the source $\NameCert_{\CliffordUp}$. If $x$
is carried by $S$ and $R$ is carried by $C$, then the displayed product row
$\mu(x,R)$ is again a carried $\PinGroupUp$ row. Its Clifford source is $C$,
its reflection source is $R$, and its transport is exhausted by the $\hsame$
comparisons and $\Cont$ entries already present in the displayed carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the spin carrier row, Clifford carrier row, reflection row, product
ledger, continuation ledger, and provenance row from
\autoref{def:pingroup-reflection-extension-carrier} and the source
$\NameCert_{\CliffordUp}$ certificate. These projections give all endpoints
used by the product row $\mu(x,R)$.

The Clifford endpoint is read through $C$, and the reflection endpoint is read
through $R$. The public classifier compares both endpoints only by $\hsame$,
so transported product endpoints remain in the displayed finite packet.

Repack the product row by appending the product entry already named by $\mu$ to
the finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ ledger and transporting the same entry by the
displayed $\Cont$ row. The resulting packet has the same source rows,
reflection row, and provenance row, hence it is again a $\PinGroupUp$ carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PinGroup odd reflection coset exhaustion]
\label{thm:pingroup-odd-reflection-coset-exhaustion}
Every carried non-spin $\PinGroupUp$ row is generated by the displayed
Clifford reflection row, a $\SpinGroupUp$ source row, and the finite $\Cont$
ledger entries of \autoref{def:pingroup-reflection-extension-carrier}.  The
odd side contains no additional source row beyond the reflection generator
and the already displayed Clifford product ledger.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a carried row whose classifier is not read through the inherited
spin subcarrier.  By \autoref{def:pingroup-reflection-extension-carrier}, the
available non-spin data are the displayed Clifford reflection row, the
inherited $\SpinGroupUp$ source row, and the finite $\Cont$ ledger.

Apply \autoref{thm:pingroup-reflection-generator-carrier} to identify the
reflection endpoint as the generator row.  Apply
\autoref{thm:pingroup-spingroup-inclusion-surface} to keep the spin endpoint
inside the inherited source row.

Finally apply
\autoref{thm:pingroup-clifford-reflection-product-closure}.  It repacks the
spin endpoint, the reflection endpoint, and the Clifford product ledger as a
carried $\PinGroupUp$ row, so every non-spin row is exhausted by those finite
entries.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PinGroup reflection parity determinacy]
\label{thm:pingroup-reflection-parity-determinacy}
The finite reflection ledger of
\autoref{def:pingroup-reflection-extension-carrier} determines whether a
carried $\PinGroupUp$ row is read through the $\SpinGroupUp$ subcarrier or
through the odd reflection side.  This parity reading is invariant under
$\hsame$ transport of the displayed Clifford representatives.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ ledger in
\autoref{def:pingroup-reflection-extension-carrier}.  A row with no active
reflection-generator entry is read through the spin subcarrier by
\autoref{thm:pingroup-spingroup-inclusion-surface}.

When the reflection-generator entry is active, apply
\autoref{thm:pingroup-reflection-generator-carrier}.  The row is then read on
the odd reflection side, and
\autoref{thm:pingroup-clifford-reflection-product-closure} records the same
choice after multiplying by the displayed Clifford product row.

It remains to check transport.  The classifier only compares Clifford
representatives by $\hsame$, and the continuation data are finite $\Cont$
entries already present in the same ledger.  Transport therefore preserves
which of the two displayed readings the row uses.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PinGroup source ledger coverage]
\label{thm:pingroup-source-ledger-coverage}
Every public $\PinGroupUp$ source projection is covered by exactly the
displayed $\SpinGroupUp$ and $\CliffordUp$ source rows together with the
reflection-generator ledger of
\autoref{def:pingroup-reflection-extension-carrier}.  No further public
source certificate is exported by the $\PinGroupUp$ boundary.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the public source projections from
\autoref{def:pingroup-reflection-extension-carrier}.  The displayed source
rows are the inherited $\SpinGroupUp$ row and the $\CliffordUp$ row carrying
the reflection generator and Clifford product ledger.

Apply \autoref{thm:pingroup-reflection-generator-carrier} for the generator
projection and \autoref{thm:pingroup-spingroup-inclusion-surface} for the
spin projection.  Apply
\autoref{thm:pingroup-clifford-reflection-product-closure} to cover the
product rows that combine those two displayed sources.

The remaining public entries are $\hsame$ comparisons and finite $\Cont$
provenance rows over the same ledger.  They do not introduce a new source
projection, so the listed SpinGroupUp, CliffordUp, and reflection-generator
rows cover the whole public boundary.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PinGroupUp Spin and Clifford source scope]
\label{thm:pingroup-spin-clifford-source-scope}
Every source row admitted by a $\PinGroupUp$ carrier is one of the displayed
$\SpinGroupUp$ or $\CliffordUp$ source rows of
\autoref{def:pingroup-reflection-extension-carrier}. The reflection-generator
row, the Clifford-product row, and the spin-inclusion row are internal rows
over those two sources and do not introduce a third source certificate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Induct over the finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ ledger underlying the displayed
carrier. The initial source entries are exactly the inherited spin row and the
Clifford row from \autoref{def:pingroup-reflection-extension-carrier}.

For a reflection-generator entry, the carrier definition reads its source and
target through the Clifford row, so the entry contributes no new source. For a
spin inclusion entry, the domain is the inherited spin row and the codomain is
the displayed Clifford row.

For a Clifford-product entry, the ledger row is $\mu$ and its endpoints are the
already carried spin and reflection rows. The continuation step preserves the
same finite source list by $\Cont$, so the induction closes with only the two
displayed source certificates.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PinGroup NameCert boundary surface]
\label{thm:pingroup-namecert-boundary-surface}
The $\NameCert_{\PinGroupUp}$ boundary packages the carrier, classifier,
reflection-generator row, Spin inclusion, full-orthogonal projection,
exactness, and ledger rows into one certificate surface. The carrier is the
$\mathsf{BHist}$ predicate of
\autoref{thm:pingroup-bhist-carrier-obligation}; the reflection row is the
finite $\mathsf{Cont}$ witness of
\autoref{thm:pingroup-reflection-generator-obligation}; the Spin row is the
even-subcarrier embedding of
\autoref{thm:pingroup-spin-extension-obligation}; and the projection row is
the classifier boundary of
\autoref{thm:pingroup-full-orthogonal-projection-obligation}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Collect the four obligation rows. The carrier row supplies the predicate over
$\mathsf{BHist}$, the reflection row supplies the finite generator ledger, the
Spin-extension row supplies the even subcarrier, and the projection row
supplies the classifier boundary to the orthogonal-action surface.

The ten-field template repacks these rows by placing the consumed
$\SpinGroupUp$ and $\CliffordUp$ certificates in the source field, the Pin
classifier in the classifier field, the displayed transport clauses in the
stability field, and the finite generator and projection witnesses in the
ledger field.

Exactness is then the statement that a history is accepted exactly when the
listed source, classifier, reflection, Spin-inclusion, projection, and ledger
rows are present. This is the required PinGroup boundary surface and contains
no host Pin group or host orthogonal transformation.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PinGroupUp NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:pingroup-namecert-obligation-surface}
The public $\NameCert_{\PinGroupUp}$ obligation surface consists exactly of
the reflection-extension carrier of
\autoref{def:pingroup-reflection-extension-carrier}, its componentwise
$\hsame$ classifier, the reflection-generator row, the Clifford-product ledger,
the spin-inclusion surface, and the finite $\Cont$/$\Pkg$ ledger scope. It
claims no data beyond the displayed $\SpinGroupUp$ and $\CliffordUp$ source
certificates.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the carrier packet. The projections expose the spin source, Clifford
source, reflection generator, Clifford product, spin inclusion, continuation
ledger, and provenance row.

Apply \autoref{thm:pingroup-reflection-generator-carrier} to identify the
admitted reflection row. Apply
\autoref{thm:pingroup-clifford-reflection-product-closure} to identify the
product row as a carried $\PinGroupUp$ row. Apply
\autoref{thm:pingroup-spin-clifford-source-scope} to restrict all source rows
to the displayed spin and Clifford surfaces.

The classifier contains only componentwise $\hsame$ transport and $\Cont$
transport of the finite ledger. Repacking those projections gives precisely
the named obligation surface, with no additional carrier or source certificate
available to downstream consumers.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[PinGroupUp reflection-parity carrier]
\label{def:pingroup-reflection-parity-carrier}
The visible reflection-parity carrier of $\PinGroupUp$ is the
$\mathsf{BHist}$ predicate generated by two displayed packets: the
Spin-inclusion row of \autoref{thm:pingroup-spin-extension-obligation} and
the finite reflection-generator ledger of
\autoref{thm:pingroup-reflection-generator-obligation}. A public row lies in
the inherited Spin branch when its source projection factors through the
Spin-inclusion row. It lies in the reflection branch when its source
projection factors through one listed Clifford reflection generator and the
Clifford-product ledger of
\autoref{thm:pingroup-clifford-reflection-product-closure}. The carrier
contains no third parity source and no host-level parity predicate.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[PinGroupUp reflection-parity stability]
\label{thm:pingroup-reflection-parity-stability}
The reflection-parity carrier of
\autoref{def:pingroup-reflection-parity-carrier} is stable under the
transport rows already present in the public $\NameCert_{\PinGroupUp}$
surface. Transporting the Spin row by $\hsame$ preserves the inherited Spin
branch, and transporting the finite reflection ledger by $\Cont$ preserves the
reflection branch.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.PinGroupUp.PinGroupReflectionParityCarrier\_stability}
\begin{proof}
For the inherited branch, unpack the Spin-inclusion row from
\autoref{thm:pingroup-namecert-obligation-surface}. Its public comparison is
componentwise $\hsame$, so an $\hsame$ transport of the Spin source and target
is again read through the same Spin-inclusion row.

For the reflection branch, unpack the reflection generator and product ledger.
Apply \autoref{thm:pingroup-reflection-generator-carrier} to identify the
admitted generator row, then apply
\autoref{thm:pingroup-clifford-reflection-product-closure} to keep products
inside the displayed finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ ledger.

The continuation row is exactly the $\Cont$ field already exposed by
\autoref{thm:pingroup-namecert-obligation-surface}. Repacking the transported
Spin row and the transported reflection ledger gives the same two-branch
carrier of \autoref{def:pingroup-reflection-parity-carrier}.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PinGroupUp reflection-parity exactness]
\label{thm:pingroup-reflection-parity-exactness}
The public $\PinGroupUp$ classifier distinguishes exactly the two branches of
\autoref{def:pingroup-reflection-parity-carrier}. A public row is classified
as inherited precisely when the listed $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$,
$\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows factor through the Spin-inclusion source, and it
is classified as reflective precisely when those same rows factor through the
Clifford reflection-generator source and its product ledger.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.PinGroupUp.PinGroupReflectionParityCarrier\_exactness}
\begin{proof}
Start from the public obligation packet of
\autoref{thm:pingroup-namecert-obligation-surface}. Its source fields are
only the displayed $\SpinGroupUp$ and $\CliffordUp$ certificates, and its
classifier fields are componentwise $\hsame$ comparisons plus the finite
$\Cont$ ledger.

If the row factors through the Spin-inclusion source, the classifier reads the
row through the inherited Spin branch. The $\Pkg$ and $\NameCert$ projections
name the same source certificate, so no reflection-generator witness is used.

If the row factors through a Clifford reflection generator, apply
\autoref{thm:pingroup-reflection-generator-carrier} to obtain the generator
row and \autoref{thm:pingroup-clifford-reflection-product-closure} to obtain
the closed product row. The classifier then reads the row through the
reflection branch, with the same $\Cont$ ledger and provenance row.

Conversely, any row classified by the public packet has one of those two
factorizations, because \autoref{thm:pingroup-spin-clifford-source-scope}
exhausts all available source rows. Therefore the classifier is exact for the
two visible parity branches.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PinGroupUp source exhaustion]
\label{thm:pingroup-source-exhaustion}
Every public $\PinGroupUp$ row is generated either by the inherited
$\SpinGroupUp$ source or by the $\CliffordUp$ reflection-generator source
listed in \autoref{def:pingroup-reflection-extension-carrier}. No public row
of the $\NameCert_{\PinGroupUp}$ surface has a source outside these two
displayed origins.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack a public $\PinGroupUp$ row through
\autoref{thm:pingroup-namecert-obligation-surface}. The carrier, classifier,
ledger, and provenance projections are exactly the projections of
\autoref{def:pingroup-reflection-extension-carrier}.

Apply \autoref{thm:pingroup-spin-clifford-source-scope}. It states that the
only admitted source rows are the inherited Spin source and the Clifford
source, while reflection-generator, product, and Spin-inclusion rows are
internal rows over those sources.

The reflection side is closed under the finite product ledger by
\autoref{thm:pingroup-clifford-reflection-product-closure}. Repacking the two
cases gives source exhaustion for the whole public surface.
\end{proof}

Use the Clifford source certificate recorded in
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-clifford-namecert}. Its carrier and classifier
rows are $\mathsf{BHist}$ rows stable under $\hsame$ and certificate-level
transport, so the Pin reflection ledger inherits the same stability.

Repack the transported rows through the public packet of
\autoref{thm:pingroup-namecert-obligation-surface}. The result is exactly the
finite reflection ledger visible to $\PinGroupUp$ consumers.

\begin{theorem}[PinGroup Spin boundary exhaustion]

The $\PinGroupUp$ carrier reads the Spin subcarrier exactly through the
consumed $\SpinGroupUp$ row together with the even-grade Clifford rows already

$\PinGroupUp$ is exhausted by those consumed $\mathsf{BHist}$ rows and their

\begin{theorem}[PinGroup consumer readback packet soundness]
\label{thm:pingroup-consumer-readback-packet-sound}
Every accepted public $\PinGroupUp$ consumer row projects to the

\autoref{def:pingroup-consumer-readback-packet}.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[PinGroup downstream unblock package]
\label{thm:pingroup-downstream-unblock-package}

\autoref{thm:pingroup-namecert-obligation-surface}. The reflection ledger,
Clifford action row, and Spin boundary are exactly the three rows retained in
\autoref{def:pingroup-consumer-readback-packet}.

\autoref{thm:pingroup-consumer-readback-packet-sound}; exact readback and
exclusion of host data are supplied by

provenance, transport, and witness fields are the packet's $\Pkg$, $\Cont$,
and $\hsame$ rows.

Thus a later Clifford-family or Spin-family consumer may cite this single
package instead of separately collecting the carrier, source, ledger,
transport, and exclusion rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PinGroup reflection-parity boundary]
\label{thm:pingroup-reflection-parity-boundary}
Every public $\PinGroupUp$ row factors through exactly one branch of
\autoref{def:pingroup-reflection-parity-carrier}: either the inherited
$\SpinGroupUp$ branch or the Clifford reflection branch. No public row factors
through both branches except by the inherited Spin classification already
recorded in the carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

ledger as the two displayed sources and their transported histories. Apply
\autoref{thm:pingroup-reflection-parity-exactness} to classify each public row
by the reflection-parity carrier.

For a row classified by the inherited Spin branch, the carrier records exactly
the $\SpinGroupUp$ inclusion and its $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\Pkg$ rows. For a
row classified by the Clifford reflection branch, the carrier records the
reflection generator and Clifford product ledger.

Apply \autoref{thm:pingroup-source-exhaustion} to rule out a third branch.
Then apply \autoref{thm:pingroup-downstream-unblock-package} to see that the
consumer-visible package exports precisely these two branches and the Spin
overlap already classified by the inherited branch.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PinGroup source projection separation]
\label{thm:pingroup-source-projection-no-confusion}

$\CliffordUp$ are disjoint except for rows classified by the inherited Spin

separates inherited Spin rows from Clifford reflection rows, and it identifies
the only common public classification as the inherited Spin branch already
present in the reflection-parity carrier.

Apply \autoref{thm:pingroup-source-exhaustion} to exclude any remaining public
source projection. The downstream package of
\autoref{thm:pingroup-downstream-unblock-package} then repacks exactly the two
source projections, their transports, witnesses, and provenance for later
consumers.
\end{proof}
