\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for \(\GroupUp\)}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-group-namecert}

The group interface \(\GroupUp\) extends \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-monoid-namecert} with a unary inverse operation \({}^{-1} : C \to C\) satisfying left and right inverse laws under the carrier's classifier. It is not a primitive structure; it is licensed only by composing the monoid certificate with the inverse field.

\begin{remark}[Roadmap chapter]
\label{rem:concrete-instances-group-namecert-roadmap}
This chapter records the licensing target. Lean markers identify the
algebraic fields and derived laws that are formalized for the certificate.
\end{remark}

\section{Carrier definition}

\begin{definition}[Group carrier]
\label{def:group-carrier}
Use a monoid setup extended with inverse operation \(a^{-1}\).
\end{definition}

\section{Concrete singleton history group instance}
\label{sec:group-concrete-singleton-history-instance}

\begin{definition}[Concrete singleton history group instance]
\label{def:concrete-singleton-history-group-instance}
Let $A_{\emp}$ be the singleton history source of
Definition~\ref{def:list-singleton-history-source} at $a_0=\emp$. Define
the concrete history operations and classifier by
$$
\mathsf{mul}(h,k):=\mathsf{append}(h,k),\qquad
\mathsf{inv}(h):=\emp,\qquad
e:=\emp,
$$
and
$$
R_{\emp}(h,k)\Longleftrightarrow
A_{\emp}(h)\land A_{\emp}(k)\land \hsame(h,k).
$$
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Concrete singleton history group laws]
\label{thm:concrete-singleton-history-group-laws}
The tuple $(A_{\emp},R_{\emp},\mathsf{mul},e,\mathsf{inv})$ satisfies carrier
inhabitance, multiplication closure, inverse closure, left and right unit
laws, associativity up to $\hsame$, multiplication congruence, inverse
congruence, and left and right inverse laws.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier inhabitance is the reflexive endpoint witness
$\hsame(\emp,\emp)$, which is exactly the singleton carrier condition from
Definition~\ref{def:list-singleton-history-source} at $a_0=\emp$.

For multiplication closure, suppose $A_{\emp}(h)$ and $A_{\emp}(k)$. The two
carrier facts identify $h$ and $k$ with $\emp$. The BEDC continuation unit
laws used in Theorem~\ref{thm:concrete-history-monoid-laws} reduce
$\mathsf{append}(h,k)$ to the same endpoint class as $\emp$, using symmetry
and transitivity of $\hsame$ to compose the displayed witnesses.

Inverse closure is immediate because $\mathsf{inv}(h)$ is definitionally
$\emp$. The singleton carrier witness is again $\hsame(\emp,\emp)$.

The left unit law reduces $\mathsf{append}(\emp,h)$ to $h$ by the continuation
left-unit law, and the right unit law reduces $\mathsf{append}(h,\emp)$ to
$h$ by the continuation right-unit law. The endpoint witnesses required by
$R_{\emp}$ are supplied by the carrier facts for $\emp$ and $h$.

Associativity is the continuation associativity theorem cited in
Theorem~\ref{thm:concrete-history-monoid-laws}: the histories
$\mathsf{append}(\mathsf{append}(h,k),l)$ and
$\mathsf{append}(h,\mathsf{append}(k,l))$ are $\hsame$-equivalent. Closure of
the two products follows from the multiplication-closure clause.

Multiplication congruence follows by transporting the two input $\hsame$
witnesses across $\mathsf{append}$ and then composing with the same
continuation associativity and unit laws. Since all carrier elements are in
the singleton class of $\emp$, the source and target products both lie in
$A_{\emp}$ and are related by $\hsame$.

Inverse congruence is constant: both inverses are $\emp$, so the classifier
witness is the pair of singleton carrier witnesses together with
$\hsame(\emp,\emp)$.

For the inverse laws, $\mathsf{append}(\mathsf{inv}(h),h)$ is
$\mathsf{append}(\emp,h)$ and $\mathsf{append}(h,\mathsf{inv}(h))$ is
$\mathsf{append}(h,\emp)$. The continuation unit laws reduce these products to
$h$, and the carrier fact $A_{\emp}(h)$ identifies $h$ with $e=\emp$.
\end{proof}

\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.GroupUp.GroupSingletonHistory\_laws}

\begin{theorem}[Group singleton classifier append context cancellation]
\label{thm:group-singleton-classifier-append-context-cancel}
For histories $L,R,Q,S:\mathsf{BHist}$, if the displayed left contexts are in
the singleton group carrier $A_{\emp}$, then appending those contexts to the
two classifier endpoints preserves and reflects the singleton classifier:
$$
A_{\emp}(L)\Rightarrow A_{\emp}(R)\Rightarrow
\bigl(
R_{\emp}(\mathsf{append}(L,Q),\mathsf{append}(R,S))
\Longleftrightarrow
R_{\emp}(Q,S)
\bigr).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
In the forward direction, the two appended endpoints are classified as
singleton endpoints. Empty-result inversion for append splits each endpoint
witness, giving $Q\hsame\emp$ and $S\hsame\emp$. These two carrier witnesses,
together with transitivity through $\emp$, assemble $R_{\emp}(Q,S)$.

Conversely, combine the supplied singleton witnesses for $L$ and $R$ with the
endpoint carrier fields in $R_{\emp}(Q,S)$. Empty-result construction for
append gives singleton witnesses for $\mathsf{append}(L,Q)$ and
$\mathsf{append}(R,S)$, and transitivity through $\emp$ gives the classifier
comparison between the two appended endpoints.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.GroupUp.GroupSingletonClassifier\_append\_context\_cancel\_iff}

The $\GroupUp$ schema is instantiated by this concrete $BHist$ carrier and
operations, so subsequent formalization can prove $BHist$-anchored group laws
instead of relying only on an abstract operator schema.

\begin{theorem}[Group inverse of identity]
\label{thm:group-inverse-identity}
For a history-level group operation with right identity and left inverses, the
inverse of the identity is internally the same as the identity:
\(\hsame(e^{-1},e)\).
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Chain \(e^{-1}\) backward through the right identity
\(e^{-1}\cdot e\hsame e^{-1}\), then apply the left inverse law at \(e\).
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Left inverse involutivity]
\label{thm:group-left-inverse-involutive}
For a history-level group operation with associativity, left and right
identity, multiplication congruence, and left inverses, the left inverse
operation is involutive: \((x^{-1})^{-1}\hsame x\).
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Rewrite \((x^{-1})^{-1}\) through the right identity. Replace the identity
by \(x^{-1}x\), reassociate, collapse
\((x^{-1})^{-1}x^{-1}\) by the left inverse law, and finish with the left
identity.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Right inverse involutivity]
\label{thm:group-right-inverse-involutive}
For a history-level group operation with associativity, left and right
identity, multiplication congruence, and right inverses, the right inverse
operation is involutive: $(x^{-1})^{-1}\hsame x$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Rewrite $(x^{-1})^{-1}$ through the left identity. Replace the identity
by $xx^{-1}$, reassociate, collapse $x^{-1}(x^{-1})^{-1}$ by the right

\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Left and right inverse witnesses agree]
\label{thm:group-left-right-inverse-uniqueness}
For a history-level group operation with associativity, left and right identity,
and multiplication congruence, if histories $y$ and $z$ satisfy
$$
\hsame(yx,e)
$$
and
$$
\hsame(xz,e),
$$
then $\hsame(y,z)$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Chain $y$ through the right identity, replace the identity $e$ by $xz$,
reassociate to $(yx)z$, replace $yx$ by $e$, and finish with the left identity
on $z$. Each replacement is licensed by multiplication congruence and the
displayed inverse hypotheses.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.GroupUp.group\_left\_right\_inverse\_uniqueness}

\begin{theorem}[Group inverse congruence from laws]
\label{thm:group-inverse-congruence-from-laws}
For a history-level group operation with associativity, left and right identity,
multiplication congruence, and left and right inverses, \(\hsame(x,y)\) implies
\(\hsame(x^{-1},y^{-1})\).
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Transport the left inverse witness for \(x^{-1}\) from \(x\) to \(y\) using
multiplication congruence. Combine it with the right inverse witness for
\(y^{-1}\) at \(y\), and apply uniqueness of left and right inverse witnesses.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Group inverse cancellation from laws]
\label{thm:group-inverse-cancel-from-laws}
For a history-level group operation with associativity, left and right
identity, multiplication congruence, and left and right inverses, equality of
inverses under $\hsame$ implies equality of the original histories:
$$
\hsame(x^{-1},y^{-1})\to\hsame(x,y).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply the derived inverse congruence theorem to the displayed equality of
inverses, yielding $\hsame((x^{-1})^{-1},(y^{-1})^{-1})$. Chain the left
inverse involutivity law backward at $x$, across this double-inverse equality,
and forward at $y$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Group multiplication by a right inverse cancels]
\label{thm:group-mul-right-inverse-cancel}
For a history-level group operation with associativity, right identity,
multiplication congruence, and right inverses, every pair of histories satisfies
$$
(ab)b^{-1}\hsame a.
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Reassociate $(ab)b^{-1}$ to $a(bb^{-1})$. The right inverse law collapses
$bb^{-1}$ to the identity, and the right identity law leaves $a$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Group inverse reverses multiplication]
\label{thm:group-inverse-mul-reverse}
For a history-level group operation with associativity, left and right identity,
multiplication congruence, and left and right inverses, the inverse of a product
is internally the same as the reversed product of inverses:
$$
\hsame((xy)^{-1},y^{-1}x^{-1}).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The product $y^{-1}x^{-1}$ is a right inverse of $xy$: reassociate
$(xy)(y^{-1}x^{-1})$ to $x(y(y^{-1}x^{-1}))$, reassociate the inner product to
$(yy^{-1})x^{-1}$, collapse $yy^{-1}$ to the identity, and finish with the
left identity followed by the right inverse law for $x$. The left inverse law
for $xy$ and uniqueness of left and right inverse witnesses identify
$(xy)^{-1}$ with $y^{-1}x^{-1}$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Group left cancellation]
\label{thm:group-left-cancellation}
For a history-level group operation with associativity, left identity,
multiplication congruence, and left inverses, equality of \(ab\) and \(ac\)
under \(\hsame\) implies equality of \(b\) and \(c\) under \(\hsame\).
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Chain \(b\) through the left identity, replace that identity by
\(a^{-1}a\), reassociate to \(a^{-1}(ab)\), use multiplication congruence on
the assumed product equality, reassociate back to \((a^{-1}a)c\), collapse the
left inverse, and finish by the left identity on \(c\).
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Group left-multiplication equation exactness]
\label{thm:group-left-mul-equation-exactness}
For a history-level group operation with associativity, left identity,
multiplication congruence, and two-sided inverses, left multiplication by $a$
classifies the original equation:
$$
ax\hsame b \Longleftrightarrow x\hsame a^{-1}b
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The forward implication is Theorem~\ref{thm:group-left-mul-equation-solution}.

For the reverse direction, transport $x\hsame a^{-1}b$ by left multiplication
with $a$. Reassociate $a(a^{-1}b)$ to $(aa^{-1})b$, collapse $aa^{-1}$ by the
right inverse law, and use the left identity on $b$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Group conjugation equation exactness]
\label{thm:group-conjugation-equation-exactness}
For a history-level group operation with associativity, left and right
identities, multiplication congruence, and two-sided inverses, conjugation by
$a$ classifies the middle factor exactly:
$$
\hsame((a x)a^{-1},b) \Longleftrightarrow \hsame(x,(a^{-1}b)a).
$$
The ten-field fit is: traditional target conjugation equation solving; BEDC
source $\GroupUp$ over $\mathsf{BHist}$; carrier $\mathsf{BHist}$; classifier
$\hsame$; stability associativity, inverse laws, and multiplication
congruence; ledger conjugation provenance; core theorem this exactness iff;
standard bridge group conjugacy equations; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the forward direction, transport the displayed equation by right
multiplication with $a$. Reassociate $((a x)a^{-1})a$ to
$(a x)(a^{-1}a)$, collapse $a^{-1}a$ by the left inverse law, and use the
right identity to obtain $a x\hsame b a$.

Then transport by left multiplication with $a^{-1}$. Reassociate
$a^{-1}(a x)$ to $(a^{-1}a)x$, collapse $a^{-1}a$, and use the left identity
to obtain $x\hsame (a^{-1}b)a$.

For the reverse direction, transport $x\hsame (a^{-1}b)a$ by left
multiplication with $a$. Reassociate $a((a^{-1}b)a)$ through
$(aa^{-1})b$ and then back to $b a$, using the right inverse law for
$aa^{-1}$ and the left identity on $b$.

Finally transport by right multiplication with $a^{-1}$. Reassociate
$(a x)a^{-1}$ as the transported left side and reassociate
$(b a)a^{-1}$ to $b(aa^{-1})$, collapse $aa^{-1}$ by the right inverse law,
and finish with the right identity on $b$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Group commutator detects commutation]
\label{thm:group-commutator-trivial-iff-commutes}
For a BEDC history-level group operation over $\mathsf{BHist}$ with
associativity, left and right identities, multiplication congruence, and
two-sided inverse laws, define
$$
\mathsf{comm}(a,b):=\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a,b),
\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{inv}(a),\mathsf{inv}(b))).
$$
Then every $a,b:\mathsf{BHist}$ satisfy
$$
\mathsf{comm}(a,b)\hsame e \Longleftrightarrow
\mathsf{mul}(a,b)\hsame \mathsf{mul}(b,a).
$$
The ten-field fit is: traditional target group commutator criterion; BEDC
source $\GroupUp$ over $\mathsf{BHist}$; carrier $\mathsf{BHist}$; classifier
$\hsame$; stability obligations inverse-product reversal, multiplication
congruence, right cancellation, and inverse laws; ledger the two factors and
inverse-product spine; core theorem this block; exactness predicate trivial
commutator iff commutation; standard bridge ordinary group commutator
criterion; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use Theorem~\ref{thm:group-inverse-mul-reverse} with the ordered product
$ba$ to identify $a^{-1}b^{-1}$ as an inverse-product presentation of
$(ba)^{-1}$. Thus the displayed commutator is the product of $ab$ with an
inverse witness for $ba$.

For the forward direction, multiply the trivial commutator equation on the
right by $ba$. The right side becomes $ba$ by the left identity, while the
left side reassociates to $ab((ba)^{-1}(ba))$; the left inverse law for $ba$
collapses the final factor to $e$, and the right identity leaves $ab$. This
is exactly the right-cancellation comparison between $ab$ and $ba$.

For the reverse direction, transport $ab\hsame ba$ through multiplication
congruence by the common right factor $(ba)^{-1}$. The right-hand product
collapses to $e$ by the right-inverse law, and the inverse-product reversal
identification rewrites the left-hand product as
$\mathsf{comm}(a,b)$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.GroupUp.group\_commutator\_trivial\_iff\_commutes\_from\_empty\_unit}
