\begin{theorem}[Group singleton continuation endpoint equivalence]
\label{thm:group-singleton-continuation-result-endpoint-equivalence}
For histories $P,Q,R:\mathsf{BHist}$ with $\ContR(P,Q,R)$, the concrete
singleton group classifier compares the continuation result with either input
endpoint equivalently:
$$
R_{\emp}(R,P)\Longleftrightarrow R_{\emp}(R,Q).
$$
The ten-field fit is: traditional target terminal-group endpoint agreement
under continuation; BEDC source the concrete singleton $\GroupUp$ over
$\mathsf{BHist}$ and $\Cont$; carrier $A_{\emp}$; classifier $R_{\emp}$;
stability from result-left and result-right classifier exactness; ledger the
continuation witness; core theorem list
Theorem~\ref{thm:group-singleton-classifier-continuation-result-left} and
Theorem~\ref{thm:group-singleton-classifier-continuation-result-right};
exactness predicate the displayed iff; standard bridge the one-element group;
and status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use Theorem~\ref{thm:group-singleton-classifier-continuation-result-left} to
identify $R_{\emp}(R,P)$ with the input classifier $R_{\emp}(P,Q)$ under the
given continuation witness.

Use Theorem~\ref{thm:group-singleton-classifier-continuation-result-right} to
identify the same input classifier $R_{\emp}(P,Q)$ with $R_{\emp}(R,Q)$.
Composing these two equivalences gives the displayed endpoint-to-endpoint
equivalence.
\end{proof}
