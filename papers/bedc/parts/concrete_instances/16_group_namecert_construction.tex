\label{thm:group-singleton-terminal-classifier-package}

\begin{enumerate}
\item The classifier is exactly the product carrier relation:
$$
R_{\emp}(h,k)\Longleftrightarrow A_{\emp}(h)\land A_{\emp}(k).
$$

\item With $\mathsf{mul}=\mathsf{append}$, multiplication is exactly closed on
the singleton carrier:
$$
A_{\emp}(\mathsf{mul}(p,q))\Longleftrightarrow A_{\emp}(p)\land A_{\emp}(q).
$$

\item If $A_{\emp}(h)$, then the constant inverse has both endpoint
classifications:
$$
A_{\emp}(\mathsf{inv}(h))\land R_{\emp}(\mathsf{inv}(h),h)\land R_{\emp}(h,\mathsf{inv}(h)).
$$
\end{enumerate}
The per-object template names the traditional target as the terminal group; the
BEDC source as the concrete singleton $\GroupUp$ over $\mathsf{BHist}$; carrier
$A_{\emp}$; classifier $R_{\emp}$; stability as $\hsame$ symmetry/transitivity
and append-empty exactness; ledger the hidden singleton endpoint witnesses; core
theorem this block; exactness predicate the displayed iff rows; and status
$\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold $R_{\emp}$ and $A_{\emp}$. For the first row, the forward direction
forgets only the displayed $\hsame(h,k)$ witness. Conversely, singleton carrier
witnesses identify $h$ and $k$ with $\emp$; symmetry and transitivity of
$\hsame$ compose those endpoint identifications through $\emp$ and supply the
classifier comparison.

For the second row, use Definition~\ref{def:concrete-singleton-history-group-instance}
to replace $\mathsf{mul}$ by $\mathsf{append}$. From $A_{\emp}(p)$ and
$A_{\emp}(q)$, append empty-result construction gives
$A_{\emp}(\mathsf{append}(p,q))$. In the reverse direction, append empty-result
inversion splits the singleton endpoint witness for $\mathsf{append}(p,q)$ into
the two factor witnesses.

For the third row, Definition~\ref{def:concrete-singleton-history-group-instance}
gives the definitional equation $\mathsf{inv}(h)=\emp$. Hence
$A_{\emp}(\mathsf{inv}(h))$ is the reflexive singleton endpoint witness, and the
first row applied to $\mathsf{inv}(h)$ and $h$ packages both classifier
orientations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Group product-unit inverse correspondence]
\label{thm:group-product-unit-inverse-correspondence}
For a BEDC history-level group operation over $\mathsf{BHist}$ with
associativity, left and right identity laws for $\emp$, multiplication
congruence, and two-sided inverse laws, write $xy$ for
$\mathsf{mul}(x,y)$ and $x^{-1}$ for $\mathsf{inv}(x)$. Then every
$a,b:\mathsf{BHist}$ satisfies
$$
\hsame(ab,\emp)
\Longleftrightarrow
\hsame(a,b^{-1})
\Longleftrightarrow
\hsame(b,a^{-1}).
$$
The ten-field fit is: traditional target product-equals-empty inverse
extraction; BEDC source $\GroupUp$ over $\mathsf{BHist}$; carrier
$\mathsf{BHist}$; classifier $\hsame$; stability obligations left and right
equation exactness, unit laws, inverse laws, inverse uniqueness, inverse
involutivity, and multiplication congruence; ledger the two factors and the
two extracted inverse witnesses; core theorem this block; exactness predicate
the displayed chain of equivalences; standard bridge ordinary group inverse
extraction from $ab=\emp$; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply Theorem~\ref{thm:group-right-mul-equation-exactness} to the equation
$\hsame(ab,\emp)$ with right factor $b$. It classifies the same equation by
$\hsame(a,\emp b^{-1})$. The left identity law reduces $\emp b^{-1}$ to $b^{-1}$,
so the product-empty equation is equivalent to $\hsame(a,b^{-1})$.

Apply Theorem~\ref{thm:group-left-mul-equation-exactness} to the same equation
with left factor $a$. It classifies the equation by $\hsame(b,a^{-1}\emp)$. The
right identity law reduces $a^{-1}\emp$ to $a^{-1}$, giving the equivalence with
$\hsame(b,a^{-1})$.

The two extracted descriptions are compatible because a witness
$\hsame(a,b^{-1})$ makes $a$ a right-inverse presentation for $b$, while
$\hsame(b,a^{-1})$ makes $b$ a left-inverse presentation for $a$. The inverse
uniqueness theorem identifies the two presentations, and inverse involutivity
returns either double-inverse comparison to the original factor.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}

endpoint carrier facts available once $Z_a(y)$ is supplied.

For the forward direction, unpack a witness $s$ for $x\sim_{N_a}y$. Centrality
of $x$ commutes $x$ past $s$ and past $\mathsf{inv}(s)$. Associativity, the
two-sided inverse laws, unit laws, multiplication congruence, and inverse
congruence then identify
$\mathsf{Conj}_s(x)=\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(s,x),\mathsf{inv}(s))$ with
$x$ under $\hsame$.

Composing this fixedness comparison with the orbit endpoint witness
$\hsame(\mathsf{Conj}_s(x),y)$ gives $\hsame(x,y)$ by symmetry and
transitivity of $\hsame$.

For the reverse direction, use the identity element as the normalizer witness.

\autoref{thm:group-centralizer-normalizer-conjugation-action} gives
$\hsame(\mathsf{Conj}_e(x),x)$.

Together with the center-to-centralizer carrier fact for $x$, the supplied
$Z_a(y)$, and the assumed $\hsame(x,y)$, these data package the orbit witness
