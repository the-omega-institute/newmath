\section{Concrete singleton normalizer action certificate}
\label{sec:group-singleton-normalizer-action-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[Group singleton normalizer action certificate]
\label{thm:group-singleton-normalizer-action-certificate}
For the concrete singleton history group instance, define
$$
\mathsf{Conj}_s(x):=\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(s,x),\mathsf{inv}(s))
$$
and take the terminal normalizer carrier to be
$$
N_{\emp}(s):\Longleftrightarrow A_{\emp}(s).
$$
If $A_{\emp}(s)$ and $A_{\emp}(x)$, then:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A_{\emp}(\mathsf{Conj}_s(x))$.
\item $A_{\emp}(\mathsf{Conj}_{\mathsf{inv}(s)}(x))$.
\item $R_{\emp}(\mathsf{Conj}_s(x),x)$.
\item $R_{\emp}(\mathsf{Conj}_{\mathsf{inv}(s)}
(\mathsf{Conj}_s(x)),x)$.
\end{enumerate}
The ten-field fit is: traditional target terminal normalizer action; BEDC
source concrete singleton $\GroupUp$ over $\mathsf{BHist}$; carrier
$A_{\emp}$; classifier $R_{\emp}$; stability singleton multiplication
closure, inverse closure, and terminal classifier exactness; ledger the
conjugating singleton endpoint and the acted-on singleton endpoint; core
theorem this certificate; exactness terminal normalizer preservation;
downstream consumer $\QuotientGroupUp$; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold the concrete singleton definitions. The inverse operation is constant,
so $\mathsf{inv}(s)=\emp$, and the two conjugation words are formed only by
multiplying histories already in the singleton carrier with $\emp$.

Apply the multiplication-closure row of
Theorem~\ref{thm:group-singleton-terminal-classifier-package} to $s$ and $x$ to put
$\mathsf{mul}(s,x)$ in $A_{\emp}$. Apply the same row again with
$\mathsf{inv}(s)=\emp$ to obtain
$A_{\emp}(\mathsf{Conj}_s(x))$.

For the inverse conjugating word, inverse closure gives
$A_{\emp}(\mathsf{inv}(s))$. The same two multiplication-closure applications
place $\mathsf{Conj}_{\mathsf{inv}(s)}(x)$ in $A_{\emp}$.

It remains to read the classifier. The terminal classifier exactness row of
Theorem~\ref{thm:group-singleton-terminal-classifier-package} classifies any two
singleton endpoints by $R_{\emp}$, so it gives
$R_{\emp}(\mathsf{Conj}_s(x),x)$ from the carrier facts already built.

Applying the closure argument once more to the singleton endpoint
$\mathsf{Conj}_s(x)$ gives
$A_{\emp}(\mathsf{Conj}_{\mathsf{inv}(s)}(\mathsf{Conj}_s(x)))$. The same
classifier row then yields
$R_{\emp}(\mathsf{Conj}_{\mathsf{inv}(s)}(\mathsf{Conj}_s(x)),x)$.
\end{proof}
