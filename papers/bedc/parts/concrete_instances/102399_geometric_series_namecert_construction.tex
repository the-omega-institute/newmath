\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\GeometricSeriesUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-geometric-series-namecert}

Following Cauchy and Bishop--Bridges constructive analysis, the $\GeometricSeriesUp$ packet records the finite geometric majorant route used by Real and Completion-facing certificates before a tail estimate is handed to a regular rational or Real seal. It sits beside the dyadic finite-tail ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadic-geometric-tail-namecert}, the ratio and root-test routes of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-ratio-test-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-root-test-namecert}, the radius transport of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-convergence-radius-namecert}, and the complex partial-sum surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-complex-series-namecert}. The packet names a displayed majorant discipline; it does not assert selected infinite sums, quotient stream equality, countable choice, host real equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[Geometric series carrier]
\label{def:geometric-series-carrier}
A $\GeometricSeriesUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
G=(a,q,n,W,T,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $a$ is the initial-term row, recording the finite magnitude budget that enters the majorant.
\item $q$ is the dyadic ratio row, displaying a ratio strictly below the unit tolerance scale together with the dyadic slack used by later tail requests.
\item $n$ is the unary exponent-window row, recording the finite exponent range and successor action on which the powers of $q$ are inspected.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window family that exposes the source tail and the finite power windows used by the packet.
\item $T$ is the $\DyadicGeometricTailUp$ ledger that turns $a$, $q$, and $n$ into a finite geometric tail budget.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row obtained only from the displayed tail ledger and the inspected stream windows.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal-facing row reached only after the regular rational readback has been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the initial term, ratio, exponent window, stream windows, dyadic tail ledger, regular readback, Real seal, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a precision request through
$$
a,q,n\longmapsto W\longmapsto T\longmapsto R\longmapsto E .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\GeometricSeriesUp$, $\DyadicGeometricTailUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\GeometricSeriesUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only through these displayed rows. It has no coordinate for a selected infinite sum, quotient stream equality, countable-choice selector, host real equality, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness theorem, or bridge credential.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Geometric series NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:geometric-series-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\GeometricSeriesUp$ carrier $G=(a,q,n,W,T,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\GeometricSeriesUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed eleven-row packet;
\item initial-term exposure for $a$ and dyadic ratio exposure for $q$, including the explicit strict-below-unit slack;
\item unary exponent-window coverage for $n$ and the finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows in $W$;
\item tail-ledger exactness, requiring $T$ to be read from $a,q,n,W$ through the $\DyadicGeometricTailUp$ surface;
\item $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback and $\RealUp$ seal non-escape, requiring $R$ and $E$ to factor through the displayed tail ledger;
\item transport, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming for exactly the rows recorded in $H,C,P,N$.
\end{enumerate}
No geometric-series NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier $G=(a,q,n,W,T,R,E,H,C,P,N)$. The projection exposes the initial term, ratio, exponent window, stream windows, tail ledger, regular readback, Real seal, and structural rows.

The numeric part of the certificate is finite. The rows $a$ and $q$ display the starting magnitude and the strict subunit dyadic ratio, while $n$ and $W$ specify the only exponent and source windows admitted for inspection.

The row $T$ is then the sole tail-producing coordinate. It sends the displayed data through the $\DyadicGeometricTailUp$ ledger, so the geometric majorant is a finite BEDC row rather than an appeal to a host infinite sum.

Finally $R$ and $E$ are downstream of $T$. Componentwise transport in $H$ preserves the same displayed route, and $C,P,N$ only replay, source, and name that route. Therefore the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Geometric series tail modulus]
\label{thm:geometric-series-tail-modulus}
Let $G=(a,q,n,W,T,R,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\GeometricSeriesUp$ carrier. Every precision request consumed by $G$ receives a finite tail threshold by the route
$$
a,q,n,W\longmapsto T\longmapsto R .
$$
The resulting modulus is the one displayed by the $\DyadicGeometricTailUp$ ledger and then read back through $\RegSeqRatUp$; it is not a selected limit of the infinite geometric series.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Fix a displayed precision request. The rows $a$ and $q$ provide the starting budget and subunit ratio, while $n$ selects the finite exponent window in which the request is evaluated.

Open the stream windows in $W$. These windows are the only source observations visible to the packet, so the request cannot inspect an unrecorded tail or a quotient stream representative.

Apply the tail ledger $T$. The ledger computes the dyadic geometric budget and returns the finite threshold required by the request. The row $R$ reads that threshold as a $\RegSeqRatUp$ modulus, and the structural rows preserve the same route under transport and replay.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Geometric series majorant handoff]
\label{thm:geometric-series-majorant-handoff}
Every accepted $\GeometricSeriesUp$ majorant exported to a ratio-test, root-test, convergence-radius, complex-series, or Real-facing consumer factors through the finite handoff
$$
a,q,n\longmapsto W,T\longmapsto R,E,H,C,P,N .
$$
Consequently the consumer receives only the displayed initial-term row, subunit dyadic ratio row, unary exponent window, stream windows, $\DyadicGeometricTailUp$ ledger, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, $\RealUp$ seal row, transport package, replay route, provenance, and local naming row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an exported majorant. Carrier projection shows that the only available numerical rows are $a$, $q$, and $n$, and the only admitted observations of the source tail lie in $W$.

The majorant must then pass through $T$. Since $T$ is the displayed $\DyadicGeometricTailUp$ ledger, every downstream comparison receives a finite dyadic tail budget before it can read the regular rational handoff.

The handoff to $R$ and $E$ does not add analytic power. It merely presents the displayed tail budget as a $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback and a Real-facing seal row.

The remaining structural rows preserve the same route. Thus a consumer of the ratio-test, root-test, convergence-radius, complex-series, or Real-facing surface cannot read a selected infinite sum, quotient stream equality, countable-choice selector, host real equality, or ambient completeness theorem from the $\GeometricSeriesUp$ packet.
\end{proof}
