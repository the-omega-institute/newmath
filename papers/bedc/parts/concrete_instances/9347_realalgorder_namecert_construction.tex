\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RealAlgOrderUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-real-alg-order-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\RealAlgOrderUp$ interface packages the algebra and order rows of displayed constructive real names. It sits above the real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the regular rational source rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic operation ledgers of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the finite windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, and the positive-separation rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-apartnessreal-namecert}. These sibling chapters supply the endpoint, readback, operation, window, and apartness data consumed by the local packet.

The certificate is the constructive algebra/order surface for downstream metric, measure, and probability consumers. It records finite addition, negation, multiplication, absolute-value, nonnegative-order, and strict-apartness evidence as named rows; it does not import trichotomy of reals, quotient equality of Cauchy presentations, a host real carrier, or ambient completeness.

\begin{definition}[RealAlgOrder carrier]
\label{def:real-alg-order-carrier}
A $\RealAlgOrderUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
R=(E,G,D,S,A,O,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $E$ is the finite family of $\RealUp$ endpoint rows consumed by the algebra or order request;
\item $G$ is the family of $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback rows attached to those endpoints before the real-seal handoff;
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ operation ledger recording the finite dyadic reads used for addition, negation, multiplication, absolute value, and nonnegative comparison;
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window schedule through which the endpoint readbacks are inspected;
\item $A$ is the optional $\ApartnessRealUp$ finite-window separation row used when the request is strict apartness rather than nonnegative comparison;
\item $O$ is the local algebra/order ledger whose rows are the displayed sum, product, negation, absolute-value, nonnegative-order, and strict-apartness conclusions licensed by $E,G,D,S,A$;
\item $H$ stores the componentwise $\hsame$ transports for endpoint rows, readbacks, dyadic ledgers, windows, apartness rows, and the local order ledger;
\item $C$ stores the $\Cont$ routes exposing finite readback and operation rows to downstream consumers;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RealUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\ApartnessRealUp$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RealAlgOrderUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier is a finite certificate for algebra and constructive order on displayed real names. It is not a host ordered field, not a quotient of real-name presentations, and not a completeness object.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[RealAlgOrder NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:real-alg-order-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\RealAlgOrderUp}$ surface for the carrier of \autoref{def:real-alg-order-carrier} consists of five finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission: the accepted object is exactly the displayed packet $R=(E,G,D,S,A,O,H,C,P,N)$ with endpoint, readback, dyadic-operation, finite-window, apartness, ledger, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming coordinates;
\item classifier transport: classification transports every endpoint row in $E$, every $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback in $G$, every dyadic operation row in $D$, every finite window in $S$, every apartness row in $A$, and the algebra/order ledger $O$ componentwise through the stored $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ routes;
\item algebra stability: addition, negation, multiplication, and absolute-value rows in $O$ remain accepted after replacing their inputs by classified $\RealUp$ endpoint rows and transporting the corresponding $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, and $\StreamNameUp$ witnesses;
\item order stability: nonnegative-order and strict-apartness rows in $O$ are read only from the displayed finite comparison and separation ledgers, and they transport across the same endpoint and window classifiers;
\item ledger exactness: every downstream algebra or order read is one of the displayed rows in $E,G,D,S,A,O,H,C,P,N$ before any consumer receives it.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from the tuple in \autoref{def:real-alg-order-carrier}. The endpoint rows, regular-rational readbacks, dyadic operation ledger, finite-window schedule, apartness row, local ledger, transport table, continuation table, package provenance, and local naming row are all explicit coordinates.

Classifier transport is componentwise. The $\RealUp$ endpoints transport together with their $\RegSeqRatUp$ source readbacks and $\StreamNameUp$ windows, while the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ operation rows and $\ApartnessRealUp$ separation rows transport through their own sibling classifiers.

The algebra and order stability rows then repack the transported coordinates into the same ledger shape $O$. Addition, negation, multiplication, and absolute value use only the dyadic operation reads exposed by $D$; nonnegative order and strict apartness use only the finite comparison and separation rows exposed by $D$ and $A$.

Ledger exactness follows because every consumer-facing row is a coordinate of $R$ or a row of $O$ generated from those coordinates. Since no coordinate stores trichotomy, quotient real equality, a host real carrier, or ambient completeness, none of those principles can be projected from the local $\NameCert_{\RealAlgOrderUp}$ surface.
\end{proof}

Thus every metric, probability, measure, or algebra-facing consumer receives a finite order or separation row with visible $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ provenance. None of the excluded host principles is a carrier coordinate or a ledger row, so none can escape through this handoff.
