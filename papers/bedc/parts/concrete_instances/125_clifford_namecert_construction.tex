\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CliffordUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-clifford-namecert}
\origin{human}

The Clifford-algebra interface $\CliffordUp$ packages the universal associative algebra generated by a vector-space carrier and a quadratic form $q$, modulo the relation $v \cdot v = q(v)$. It depends on $\NameCert_{\TensorProductUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-tensorproduct-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\BilinFormUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bilinform-namecert}), and is the substrate for the Spin and Pin certificates.

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:clifford-carrier}

\begin{definition}[Clifford carrier package]
\label{def:clifford-carrier-package}
Fix a $\VecSpaceUp$ source carrier, a $\BilinFormUp$ source certificate whose diagonal row presents the quadratic form $q$, and the $\TensorProductUp$ tensor-word source over the same vector histories. The $\CliffordUp$ carrier package is the $\mathsf{BHist}$ presentation whose accepted rows are generated by four constructors: the unit row, vector-injection rows for accepted vector histories, product rows formed by $\Cont$-composing tensor-word histories, and boundary rows identifying the product of a vector row with itself with the scalar endpoint classified by $q$.

Thus a carried Clifford row is not a host quotient algebra. It is a finite BEDC ledger containing the tensor-word $\mathsf{BHist}$ spine, the bilinear-form diagonal row, and the $\hsame$ or $\Cont$ witnesses that place each quadratic boundary inside the same generated history package.
\end{definition}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CliffordUp.CliffordTasteGate\_single\_carrier\_alignment}

\begin{definition}[Clifford classifier package]
\label{def:clifford-classifier-package}
The $\CliffordUp$ classifier is the least BEDC classifier on the carrier of \autoref{def:clifford-carrier-package} generated by the $\TensorProductUp$ classifier rows, product congruence for $\Cont$-composed tensor-word products, and the quadratic-relation row
$$
v \cdot v \sim q(v) \cdot 1
$$
whenever the vector endpoint $v$ is accepted by the $\VecSpaceUp$ source and the scalar endpoint $q(v)$ is classified by the diagonal row of $\NameCert_{\BilinFormUp}$.

Classifier transport is NameCert-level transport: replacing a tensor row by a $\TensorProductUp$-classified row, a vector row by a source-classified row, or a diagonal scalar row by a $\BilinFormUp$-classified row preserves the Clifford classifier through the displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ witnesses.
\end{definition}
\leandef{BEDC.Derived.CliffordUp.CliffordClassifierPackage}

\begin{theorem}[Clifford carrier and classifier obligations]\label{thm:clifford-carrier-classifier-obligations}
The Clifford word carrier has visible $\mathsf{BHist}$ endpoints for every accepted
finite vector-letter history, and the quadratic-relation classifier of
\autoref{def:clifford-classifier-package} is an equivalence
relation on these endpoints. Its constructors are exactly tensor-word
congruence, bilinear-form transport, local quadratic rows, and the three
equivalence closures.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier coverage is read from \autoref{def:clifford-carrier-package}. Each
letter is already a $\VecSpaceUp$-carried endpoint, and each finite word carries its
$\TensorProductUp$ bracketing witness, so the endpoint is a concrete $\mathsf{BHist}$
history rather than an external algebra element.

For the classifier, induct on the generated relation. The generator cases are
the three displayed rows in
\autoref{def:clifford-classifier-package}. Tensor congruence keeps
the same vector-letter ledger up to the $\TensorProductUp$ classifier, bilinear-form
transport keeps the $\BilinFormUp$ row, and the local quadratic row supplies the single
visible reduction $v \cdot v \sim q(v) \cdot 1$.

The reflexive, symmetric, and transitive cases are the closure constructors
of the least relation. Because every constructor targets the same Clifford
word carrier, the induction also proves that no classifier endpoint leaves
the accepted $\mathsf{BHist}$ carrier.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CliffordUp.CliffordCarrierPackage\_carrier\_classifier\_obligations}

\begin{theorem}[Clifford product relation stability obligations]\label{thm:clifford-product-relation-stability-obligations}
If two Clifford word endpoints are related by the quadratic-relation
classifier, then their products with any accepted left or right Clifford word
context are again related. In particular, tensor-word concatenation preserves
carrier membership and the classifier is stable under the two context
operations induced by $\TensorProductUp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First build the product carrier. Concatenating an accepted context word with
an accepted endpoint concatenates two finite vector-letter ledgers and uses
the $\TensorProductUp$ row to record the new bracketing witness. Hence both
left and right products remain Clifford word endpoints.

Now induct on the given classifier derivation. Tensor congruence is stable
because the context is another tensor word read by the same $\TensorProductUp$ source.
Bilinear-form transport is stable because the transported row remains a
$\BilinFormUp$-classified scalar row after the context is attached outside the local
letter pair.

For a local quadratic row, the context constructors record exactly the same
replacement inside a larger finite word: the subword $v \cdot v$ is replaced
by $q(v)$, while the surrounding left and right word ledgers are retained.
The equivalence closure steps are preserved by applying the same context
operation to each premise in the induction.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Clifford universal ledger exactness obligations]\label{thm:clifford-universal-ledger-exactness-obligations}
The Clifford obligation ledger records exactly four kinds of witnesses:
vector-letter membership in the $\VecSpaceUp$ source, tensor bracketing rows
from $\TensorProductUp$, bilinear and quadratic rows from $\BilinFormUp$, and
relation-generation steps for the quadratic classifier. Therefore the
Clifford certificate imports no host quotient, no external associative
algebra, and no equality principle outside the displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ ledger.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Coverage follows by unpacking an accepted Clifford word. Its data are a
finite vector-letter ledger, the tensor bracketing row for that ledger, and
the endpoint $\mathsf{BHist}$. These are the first two witness kinds in the stated
ledger.

Soundness of scalar and quadratic information is supplied by the
$\NameCert_{\BilinFormUp}$ source. The bilinear transport row and the local
quadratic row $v \cdot v \sim q(v)$ are recorded as rows of the generated
classifier, so the scalar endpoint is never read from a host quotient.

Exactness is the induction principle for the least classifier relation. Every
accepted relation step is either one of the listed generators or one of the
equivalence and context closures already recorded by the relation-generation
ledger. Thus any purported Clifford equality must expose one of these rows,
which rules out imported algebraic equality as a hidden source.
\end{proof}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:clifford-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[Clifford quadratic relation obligation]
\label{thm:clifford-quadratic-relation-obligation}
For every accepted vector $\mathsf{BHist}$ row $v$ in the $\VecSpaceUp$ source and every diagonal scalar row classified by $\NameCert_{\BilinFormUp}$ as $q(v)$, the $\CliffordUp$ certificate contains a classifier witness identifying the product history $v \cdot v$ with the scalar-unit endpoint $q(v) \cdot 1$. The witness is transported along $\hsame$, $\Cont$, the $\TensorProductUp$ tensor-word classifier, and the $\BilinFormUp$ diagonal classifier; no host-level quotient equality is part of the obligation.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CliffordUp.CliffordClassifierPackage\_quadratic\_relation\_obligation}
\begin{proof}
Start with the vector-injection constructor of \autoref{def:clifford-carrier-package}. Applying it twice gives two tensor-word endpoints in the $\TensorProductUp$ source, and the product constructor records their product as a $\mathsf{BHist}$ row through $\Cont$.

Use the $\BilinFormUp$ dependency to read the diagonal scalar row $q(v)$. The scalar endpoint is inserted into the Clifford carrier by the unit constructor and the scalar-row classifier supplied by the source certificate.

	The quadratic boundary constructor then contributes the displayed classifier row. Transport of the vector representative uses $\hsame$ and the $\TensorProductUp$ classifier; transport of the scalar endpoint uses the $\BilinFormUp$ classifier. Induction on the generated boundary witness has the unit and vector cases empty, the product case closed by congruence, and the boundary case exactly the relation above.
	\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Clifford quadratic boundary confluence]
\label{thm:clifford-quadratic-boundary-confluence}
Suppose two accepted Clifford word histories are obtained from the same accepted
tensor-word context by applying quadratic-boundary rows to two visible
vector-square subwords, possibly in different orders, with accepted left and
right tensor contexts around each local square. Then the two resulting endpoints
are related by the generated $\CliffordUp$ classifier. The comparison uses only
the local quadratic rows, tensor-context stability, and transitivity of the
generated classifier; it does not use host quotient-algebra equality.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CliffordUp.CliffordClassifierPackage\_quadratic\_boundary\_confluence}
\begin{proof}
First isolate the two local visible squares inside the accepted tensor word. For
each square, \autoref{thm:clifford-quadratic-relation-obligation} supplies the
classifier row from $v \cdot v$ to the corresponding scalar-unit endpoint
$q(v) \cdot 1$.

Next place each local classifier row back inside its accepted left and right
tensor contexts. Apply
\autoref{thm:clifford-product-relation-stability-obligations} to close the row
under the surrounding product contexts, so each local reduction becomes a
classifier row between accepted whole-word histories.

If the two reductions are disjoint, perform them in either order by applying the
same context-stability step to the remaining visible square. If one context is
nested in the other, first regard the inner reduction as a classifier row inside
the outer tensor context, and then apply the outer quadratic row with the inner
endpoint already transported.

In both cases the generated classifier contains the two contextualized local
steps. Transitivity of the generated relation composes the first-order path with
the reverse of the second-order path, yielding a classifier witness between the
two endpoints. Since every step is generated inside the Clifford
$\mathsf{BHist}$ carrier, no host quotient equality is read.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Clifford product stability obligation]
\label{thm:clifford-product-stability-obligation}
If $a,a',b,b'$ are carried Clifford $\mathsf{BHist}$ rows, $a$ is classified with $a'$, and $b$ is classified with $b'$, then every product row classified from $a \cdot b$ is transported to a product row classified from $a' \cdot b'$. The transport uses $\Cont$ for product composition, $\hsame$ for endpoint replacement, the $\TensorProductUp$ classifier on tensor-word spines, and NameCert-level classifier transport for every $\BilinFormUp$ quadratic boundary appearing in the witnesses.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CliffordUp.CliffordCarrierPackage\_product\_stability\_obligation}
\begin{proof}
Induct on the classifier witness for the left factor. The reflexive generator keeps the product ledger unchanged, the tensor-product generator is transported by the $\TensorProductUp$ classifier row, and the quadratic generator is transported by \autoref{thm:clifford-quadratic-relation-obligation}.

Induct on the classifier witness for the right factor in the same product context. The $\Cont$ product row is held fixed while the endpoint is replaced by $\hsame$, and product congruence repacks the resulting history as a Clifford product row.

For composite classifier witnesses, concatenate the two transported product ledgers. Associativity of the visible $\Cont$ spine supplies the endpoint comparison needed to read the concatenation as the same product history, so the final row remains inside the least classifier of \autoref{def:clifford-classifier-package}.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Clifford universal factorization obligation]
\label{thm:clifford-universal-factorization-obligation}
Let $A$ be any BEDC algebra package whose multiplication is recorded by $\mathsf{BHist}$ product rows and whose classifier is stable under $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and NameCert-level transport. If a vector-row map from the shared $\VecSpaceUp$ source into $A$ sends each accepted vector $v$ to an endpoint whose square is classified with the scalar endpoint $q(v)$ supplied by $\NameCert_{\BilinFormUp}$, then every Clifford carrier row has a unique induced classified image in $A$ obtained by recursion on the generated tensor-word presentation. The recursion factors through the $\TensorProductUp$ tensor-word source and respects the quadratic-relation classifier of \autoref{def:clifford-classifier-package}.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CliffordUp.CliffordCarrierPackage\_universal\_factorization\_obligation}
\begin{proof}
Define the image on generators first. The unit row maps to the unit row of $A$, and a vector-injection row maps by the given vector-row map. These two clauses are compatible with the source classifiers by the assumed NameCert-level transport for $A$.

For a product row, use the multiplication row of $A$ and the $\Cont$ ledger that composes the already constructed images of the two factors. The induction hypothesis supplies classified images for the factors, and product stability in $A$ transports the composite image across representative changes.

For a quadratic boundary row, use the hypothesis that the square of the vector image is classified with the scalar endpoint $q(v)$. The $\BilinFormUp$ diagonal classifier transports the scalar representative, and \autoref{thm:clifford-quadratic-relation-obligation} supplies the corresponding Clifford-side boundary.

Uniqueness is by induction on the same generated presentation. Any factorization must agree on the unit and vector constructors, must preserve product rows because its multiplication is read through $\Cont$, and must preserve the quadratic boundary because its square clause is exactly the diagonal $\BilinFormUp$ clause.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Clifford ledger exactness obligation]
\label{thm:clifford-ledger-exactness-obligation}
The $\CliffordUp$ certificate ledger contains exactly the shared $\VecSpaceUp$ source rows, the $\TensorProductUp$ tensor-word rows, the $\BilinFormUp$ diagonal scalar rows, the unit, vector-injection, product, and quadratic-boundary constructors of \autoref{def:clifford-carrier-package}, and the classifier witnesses generated in \autoref{def:clifford-classifier-package}. Every accepted Clifford $\mathsf{BHist}$ row and every accepted classifier transport is obtained from this list by finite induction on the generated carrier and classifier witnesses.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Soundness follows by reading each constructor. Unit and vector-injection rows are listed directly, product rows are listed with their $\Cont$ composition witness, and quadratic-boundary rows are listed with their $\TensorProductUp$ and $\BilinFormUp$ source witnesses.

Classifier soundness is also by induction. Tensor-product classifier rows, product congruence, and the quadratic-relation row are exactly the three generators in \autoref{def:clifford-classifier-package}; reflexive, symmetric, and transitive uses only repack these finite witnesses through $\hsame$.

For exactness, take any accepted Clifford ledger row. Inverting its generated carrier witness gives one of the four carrier constructors. Inverting any accepted classifier witness gives either a source classifier row, a product-congruence row, or a quadratic-boundary row. Hence no additional host quotient, analytic algebra, Spin-row, or Pin-row datum is present in the Clifford certificate.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CliffordUp.CliffordCarrierPackage\_ledger\_exactness\_obligation}
