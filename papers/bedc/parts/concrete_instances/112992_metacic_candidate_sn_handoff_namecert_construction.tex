\label{thm:metacic-candidate-sn-handoff-obligations}

Read the no-infinite-reduction row from the same candidate evidence. This keeps the termination consequence on the candidate-mediated route named by \autoref{ch:metacic-normalization-audit-map}.

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC candidate SN nonescape]
\label{thm:metacic-candidate-sn-handoff-nonescape}
Every normalization-audit read of an accepted $\MetacicCandidateSnHandoffUp$ packet must pass through the supplied candidate row $A$ before it may cite the strong-normalization row $S$ or the no-infinite-reduction row $N$. The handoff may route that finite evidence to the subject-reduction obstruction, finite-window diamond envelope, confluence audit packet, and typed conversion residual frontier, but it does not promote the evidence to candidate-free strong normalization, closed subject reduction, unrestricted confluence, full substitution compatibility, typed conversion decidability, or closed consistency.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier rows $T,A,S,N$. The closed typed term row $T$ is visible, but the termination rows $S$ and $N$ are displayed as consequences of $A$.

Use componentwise transport $H$ and replay $C$ to move the same evidence through a consumer request. Transport preserves the attachment of $S$ and $N$ to $A$, so a replayed request cannot drop the candidate row.

Finally inspect the sibling routes $O,D,F$. They hand the finite candidate-mediated evidence to the subject-reduction, diamond-envelope, confluence, and typed-conversion boundaries as audit data only. Those siblings are boundary packets, so the handoff exports no candidate-free SN theorem, no unconditional subject-reduction theorem, no unrestricted confluence theorem, no full substitution-compatibility theorem, no typed conversion decider, and no closed-consistency endpoint.
\end{proof}
