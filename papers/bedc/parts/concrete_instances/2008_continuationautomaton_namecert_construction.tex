\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ContinuationAutomatonUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-continuationautomaton-namecert}
\origin{ai}

The $\ContinuationAutomatonUp$ interface realizes computation as a finite $\Cont$-labelled transition surface. It packages a small-step automaton directly over $\BHist$ rows, behaviour classifiers, acceptance rows, package provenance, and a local naming row, without adding a host state space or an external operational semantics.

The automaton transition rows are the step-level input later repacked by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-unary-continuation-category-namecert}, while \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuationmonad-namecert} supplies the unit and associativity ledger for the same continuation routes.

\begin{definition}[ContinuationAutomaton carrier]\label{def:continuationautomaton-carrier}
A $\ContinuationAutomatonUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
A=(Q,q_0,F,T,B,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $Q$ is the finite list of reachable state rows, each row being a $\BHist$ entry;
\item $q_0$ is the displayed initial state row and $F$ is the finite accepting-state subledger of $Q$;
\item $T$ is the finite transition table whose rows have the form $(q,m,q',c)$ with $q,q'\in Q$, $m:\Mark$, and $c$ a displayed $\Cont(q,m,q')$ witness;
\item $B$ is the behaviour classifier ledger, read only from initial reachability, accepting membership, and the displayed transition rows;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for state rows, transition endpoints, accepting rows, behaviour rows, provenance, and local name;
\item $C$ stores the displayed continuation routes by which consumers inspect transition steps and behaviour reads;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, $\Mark$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, behaviour classifiers, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ContinuationAutomatonUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier accepts behaviour claims only through this finite state, transition, acceptance, transport, continuation, provenance, and naming surface. The carrier has no coordinate for host configurations, an external evaluator, a quotient of traces, or an unlisted transition rule.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[ContinuationAutomaton step-continuation correspondence]\label{thm:continuationautomaton-step-continuation-correspondence}
For an accepted $\ContinuationAutomatonUp$ carrier $A=(Q,q_0,F,T,B,H,C,P,N)$, every displayed automaton step $q \xrightarrow{m} q'$ is exactly a row of $T$ carrying a $\Cont(q,m,q')$ witness, and every such displayed $\Cont(q,m,q')$ row reconstructs the automaton step from $q$ to $q'$ labelled by $m$. Consequently the behaviour classifier $B$ reads transition behaviour only through $\Cont$ rows already present in the finite carrier.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ContinuationAutomatonUp.ContinuationAutomatonTasteGate\_single\_carrier\_alignment}
\begin{proof}
For the forward direction, project the carrier of \autoref{def:continuationautomaton-carrier}. A displayed automaton step is not an independent predicate in the packet; it is a row of the finite transition table $T$. Reading that row exposes endpoints $q,q'$, a mark $m$, and the stored witness $c:\Cont(q,m,q')$.

For the reverse direction, begin with a displayed transition row $(q,m,q',c)$ in $T$. The endpoints are members of the finite state ledger $Q$, and the row itself records that the automaton has a transition from $q$ to $q'$ labelled by $m$. Thus the same row reconstructs the step.

Finally read the classifier row $B$. Its behaviour observations are licensed only by initial reachability, accepting membership in $F$, the transition table $T$, and the routes in $C$. Since the only transition evidence in $T$ is the displayed $\Cont$ field, the classifier cannot consult a host evaluator, quotient trace, or unlisted transition rule.
\end{proof}
