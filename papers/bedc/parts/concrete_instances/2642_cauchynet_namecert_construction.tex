\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CauchyNetUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-cauchynet-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\CauchyNetUp$ surface records directed convergence data as finite observation packets. Kelley-style Cauchy nets and uniform-space convergence provide the mathematical precedent, but the BEDC packet below is not a quotient net, a selected limit, or an ambient completeness theorem. It is a finite $\BHist$ boundary that exposes the directed windows actually inspected by downstream $\RegSeqRatUp$ and $\RealUp$ consumers.

\begin{definition}[CauchyNet carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-net-carrier}
A $\CauchyNetUp$ carrier is a finite directed-window $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(D,S,R,W,T,L,H,C,P,N)
$$
over $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the finite directed index-window ledger, naming the inspected vertices and the common upper-window witnesses used by the packet;
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule that turns each requested directed window into displayed observation names;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ row read from the scheduled rational observations on those windows;
\item $W$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observation row for the dyadic tolerances and rational comparisons used inside the finite windows;
\item $T$ is the eventual common-window classifier row, asserting that each displayed tolerance request is witnessed by one finite directed window in $D$;
\item $L$ is the $\RealUp$ handoff row through which downstream real consumers read the regular rational data;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $D,S,R,W,T,L$ and the local naming rows;
\item $C$ stores the finite $\Cont$ routes that expose directed windows, observations, common-window witnesses, classifier reads, and real handoffs;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over the listed BEDC dependencies;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyNetUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exports only this finite directed-window packet. It contains no quotient of nets, no selected limit point, no ambient uniform-space completeness theorem, and no choice operator selecting cofinal windows.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyNet NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:cauchy-net-namecert-obligation-surface}
The local $\NameCert_{\CauchyNetUp}$ surface for \autoref{def:cauchy-net-carrier} consists of five finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed directed-window packet $K$;
\item eventual common-window classification through $T$, stating that each exposed tolerance request is routed to a common upper window recorded in $D$;
\item directed-window transport along $H$ and $C$, preserving the scheduled $\StreamNameUp$ observations, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ comparisons, and $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff rows;
\item ledger non-escape, stating that a consumer read without a displayed common-window witness is recorded as outside the packet rather than exported as convergence data;
\item $\RealUp$ handoff through $L$, so real-facing consumers see only the regular rational data certified by the same finite directed-window rows.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from the finite tuple in \autoref{def:cauchy-net-carrier}. The directed index-window ledger, stream schedule, dyadic observations, regular rational handoff, classifier row, real handoff, transports, routes, package provenance, and local naming row are all visible coordinates of one $\BHist$ packet.

The eventual common-window classifier reads $T$ only after the finite directed ledger $D$ has supplied a displayed upper-window witness. The schedule $S$ and observation row $W$ then provide the rational data consumed by $R$.

Directed-window transport is componentwise through $H$ and replayed through $C$. These rows preserve the same windows, observations, classifier reads, and handoff rows; they do not add a cofinal choice function or a quotient relation on nets.

If a consumer asks for convergence data without a displayed common-window witness, no coordinate of $K$ can supply it. The package provenance records the request as outside the finite packet boundary. The only real-facing route is therefore the handoff $L$ through the same $\RegSeqRatUp$ row, which gives the stated $\RealUp$ boundary.
\end{proof}
