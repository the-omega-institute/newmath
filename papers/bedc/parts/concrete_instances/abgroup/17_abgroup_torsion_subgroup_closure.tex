\begin{definition}[AbGroup unary scalar multiple and torsion]
\label{def:abgroup-unary-scalar-torsion}
Fix an $\AbGroupUp$ source package with carrier predicate $A$, classifier $\rho$, binary operation $+$, identity $0$, and inverse operation $-$. Write $x\sim_A y$ for $\rho(x,y)$. The unary scalar-action relation $\mathsf{AbNatAct}_A(n,a,x)$ is the closed relation generated by:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item for every carried $a$, $\mathsf{AbNatAct}_A(\emp,a,0)$;
  \item if $\mathsf{AbNatAct}_A(n,a,x)$ and $A(a)$, then $\mathsf{AbNatAct}_A(\Eone(n),a,x+a)$.
\end{enumerate}
Endpoint replacement in this relation is read through the classifier $\sim_A$ using the congruence fields of the carrier.

A unary history $n$ is positive, written $\mathsf{PosUnary}(n)$, when
$$
\mathsf{PosUnary}(n)\Longleftrightarrow \exists t\, (\Unary(t)\land n=\Eone(t)).
$$
A multiplier $n$ annihilates a carried element $a$, written $\mathsf{Ann}_A(n,a)$, when $A(a)$ and there is an endpoint $x$ such that $\mathsf{AbNatAct}_A(n,a,x)$ and $x\sim_A0$. The torsion predicate is
$$
\mathsf{Tor}_A(a)\Longleftrightarrow
\exists n\, (\mathsf{PosUnary}(n)\land \mathsf{Ann}_A(n,a)).
$$
\end{definition}

\begin{lemma}[Positive unary common product]
\label{lem:positive-unary-common-product}
If $m$ and $n$ are positive unary histories, then there is a positive unary history $p$ and unary quotient witnesses $q_m,q_n$ such that
$$
\mathsf{NatMul}(m,q_m,p)\qquad\hbox{and}\qquad \mathsf{NatMul}(n,q_n,p).
$$
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Use totality of unary multiplication from \autoref{lem:nat-mul-total} to form a row product $p_{mn}$ with $\mathsf{NatMul}(m,n,p_{mn})$ and a column product $p_{nm}$ with $\mathsf{NatMul}(n,m,p_{nm})$.

A rectangular induction over the unary generators for $m$ and $n$ identifies these two products. The empty row and empty column are the zero constructor of \autoref{def:nat-multiplication}. In the one-step cases, unary continuation associativity and unary continuation commutativity from \autoref{lem:unary-continuation-associativity} and \autoref{thm:unary-commutativity-refined} move the last row past the last column. Functionality of $\mathsf{NatMul}$ from \autoref{lem:nat-mul-functional} then classifies the two resulting endpoints, giving $p_{mn}\hsame p_{nm}$.

Transport the column multiplication witness along this endpoint sameness by \autoref{lem:nat-mul-result-hsame-transport}, and take $p=p_{mn}$, $q_m=n$, and $q_n=m$. The result $p$ is unary by \autoref{lem:nat-mul-result-unary}. Since $m$ and $n$ are positive, neither is history-same to $\emp$; if $p\hsame\emp$, then \autoref{thm:natmul-nonempty-multiplicand-empty-result-iff} applied to $\mathsf{NatMul}(m,n,p)$ would force $n\hsame\emp$, a contradiction. Closed unary generation in \autoref{def:unary-history-judgment} therefore makes $p$ a one-headed unary history, hence positive.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[AbGroup annihilator transport laws]
\label{lem:abgroup-annihilator-transport-laws}
For the torsion data of \autoref{def:abgroup-unary-scalar-torsion}:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item if $\mathsf{Ann}_A(m,a)$ and $\mathsf{NatMul}(m,q,p)$, then $\mathsf{Ann}_A(p,a)$;
  \item if $\mathsf{Ann}_A(p,a)$ and $\mathsf{Ann}_A(p,b)$, then $\mathsf{Ann}_A(p,a+b)$;
  \item if $\mathsf{Ann}_A(p,a)$, then $\mathsf{Ann}_A(p,-a)$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
For the first row, induct on the $\mathsf{NatMul}(m,q,p)$ witness. The empty quotient gives the zero scalar, which annihilates every carried element by the zero row of $\mathsf{AbNatAct}_A$. In the successor case, the scalar action over the appended unary multiplier decomposes into the previous block plus one $m$-block. The induction hypothesis classifies the previous block with $0$, and the hypothesis $\mathsf{Ann}_A(m,a)$ classifies the new block with $0$. The unit, associativity, and congruence fields inherited from \autoref{def:abgroup-carrier} collapse the sum of the two zero-classified blocks to $0$.

For the second row, induct on the scalar-action witness for $p$. At the zero multiplier, both sides are the identity. In the successor case, the induction hypothesis gives the distributive comparison
$$
p\cdot(a+b)\sim_A (p\cdot a)+(p\cdot b),
$$
where $p\cdot a$ denotes the endpoint generated by $\mathsf{AbNatAct}_A(p,a,-)$. The next step adds one copy of $a+b$. Associativity, commutativity, and congruence regroup the endpoint as
$$
(p\cdot a+a)+(p\cdot b+b),
$$
which is the pair of successor scalar endpoints for $a$ and $b$. The two annihilator hypotheses classify those endpoints with $0$, and the unit law classifies their sum with $0$.

For the third row, induct on the scalar-action witness for $p$. The zero multiplier gives $0$. In the successor case, \autoref{thm:abgroup-inverse-mul} and commutativity identify the scalar endpoint for $-a$ with the inverse of the scalar endpoint for $a$. If the latter is classified with $0$, inverse congruence classifies its inverse with $-0$. The inverse of $0$ is classified with $0$ by the group inverse uniqueness theorem \autoref{thm:group-left-right-inverse-uniqueness}, using the group package retained by \autoref{prop:abgroup-forgets-group-certificate}. Thus the scalar endpoint for $-a$ is classified with $0$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[AbGroup torsion subgroup closure]
\label{thm:abgroup-torsion-subgroup-closure}
Let an $\AbGroupUp$ carrier be written additively as in \autoref{def:abgroup-unary-scalar-torsion}. Then its torsion predicate contains $0$ and is closed under addition and inverse: if $\mathsf{Tor}_A(a)$ and $\mathsf{Tor}_A(b)$, then $\mathsf{Tor}_A(a+b)$ and $\mathsf{Tor}_A(-a)$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AbGroupUp.AbGroupTorsionSubgroup\_singleton\_closure}
\begin{proof}
The zero element is torsion: choose the positive unit multiplier $\Eone(\emp)$. The one-step scalar action sends $0$ to $0+0$, and the unit law classifies this endpoint with $0$.

Now assume $\mathsf{Tor}_A(a)$ and $\mathsf{Tor}_A(b)$. Unpack \autoref{def:abgroup-unary-scalar-torsion}. Choose positive unary annihilators $m$ and $n$ with $\mathsf{Ann}_A(m,a)$ and $\mathsf{Ann}_A(n,b)$. By \autoref{lem:positive-unary-common-product}, choose a positive common product $p$ with multiplication witnesses $\mathsf{NatMul}(m,q_m,p)$ and $\mathsf{NatMul}(n,q_n,p)$.

Apply the first row of \autoref{lem:abgroup-annihilator-transport-laws} to transport the annihilator of $a$ from $m$ to $p$, and again to transport the annihilator of $b$ from $n$ to $p$. The second row of the same lemma then gives $\mathsf{Ann}_A(p,a+b)$. Since $p$ is positive, the definition of torsion gives $\mathsf{Tor}_A(a+b)$.

For inverses, apply the third row of \autoref{lem:abgroup-annihilator-transport-laws} directly to $\mathsf{Ann}_A(m,a)$. The original multiplier $m$ is positive, so the resulting annihilator witness gives $\mathsf{Tor}_A(-a)$.
\end{proof}
