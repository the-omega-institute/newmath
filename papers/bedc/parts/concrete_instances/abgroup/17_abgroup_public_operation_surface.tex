\section{Public closure record}
\label{sec:abgroup-public-closure-record}

\begin{theorem}[AbGroup public operation surface]
\label{thm:abgroup-public-operation-surface}
The public $\AbGroupUp$ surface consists of the singleton empty-history carrier,
classifier, and operation tuple from
\autoref{def:singleton-empty-history-abgroup-instance}; the checked singleton
laws from \autoref{thm:singleton-empty-history-abgroup-laws}; the forgetful
group reduct from \autoref{prop:abgroup-forgets-group-certificate}; the
abelian normalizer-orbit collapse package from
\autoref{thm:abgroup-total-centralizer-normalizer-orbit-collapse}; and the
middle-four and double-cancellation normal-form rows represented by
\autoref{thm:abgroup-middle-four-interchange},
\autoref{thm:abgroup-middle-four-double-cancel}, and
\autoref{thm:abgroup-middle-four-transported-double-cancel}. No host equality,
quotient carrier, subgroup primitive, or module equivalence is part of this
surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier, classifier, multiplication, identity, inverse, and commutativity
data are the singleton empty-history data of
\autoref{def:singleton-empty-history-abgroup-instance}. The checked laws in
\autoref{thm:singleton-empty-history-abgroup-laws} make those operations the
displayed $\AbGroupUp$ singleton package.

The group-facing part is obtained by applying
\autoref{prop:abgroup-forgets-group-certificate}; it keeps the same carrier,
classifier, multiplication, identity, and inverse while only dropping the
commutativity field as an extra obligation. The abelian operation consequences
then remain visible through
\autoref{thm:abgroup-total-centralizer-normalizer-orbit-collapse} and the
normal-form rows
\autoref{thm:abgroup-middle-four-interchange},
\autoref{thm:abgroup-middle-four-double-cancel}, and
\autoref{thm:abgroup-middle-four-transported-double-cancel}.

These cited rows exhaust the public operation surface asserted here: they
expose the singleton carrier and laws, the group reduct, the centralizer and
normalizer collapse, and the middle-four cancellation forms. Since none of the
cited rows constructs host equality, a quotient carrier, a subgroup primitive,
or a module equivalence, the public surface adds none of those structures.
\end{proof}
