\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RealityConstrainedObjectivityUp$}

The $\RealityConstrainedObjectivityUp$ packet realizes the relational objectivity discipline of \autoref{sec:objectivity-without-privileged-observer} as a finite multi-observer NameCert surface. It consumes the physical signature and descent rows of the $\PhysicalFitTowerUp$ role, the observation handoff rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observationreflectionpacket-namecert}, the observer-facing approximation route of the $\ObserverApproximationTowerUp$ role, and the model-audit rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-reality-constrained-model-audit-namecert}. Those siblings provide fit, observation handoff, approximation, and audit carriers; this packet adds the symmetric anchor-change classifier and the refusal row that prevents any one observer history from becoming a global observer.

\begin{definition}[Reality-constrained objectivity carrier]
\label{def:reality-constrained-objectivity-carrier}
A $\RealityConstrainedObjectivityUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
O=(H,A,I,F,R,T,P,L,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $H$ is the finite multi-observer history family, containing the observer histories whose relation is being certified;
\item $A$ is the anchor row, naming the currently selected observer standpoint and the admissible anchor-change schedule;
\item $I$ is the inter-observer invariant packet, containing the relation, classifier, and invariant value transported across the histories;
\item $F$ is the $\PhysicalFitTowerUp$ fit row that exposes the reality-facing signature discipline consumed by the invariant;
\item $R$ is the $\ObservationReflectionPacketUp$ handoff row that records how observation and reflection data enter the invariant packet;
\item $T$ is the $\ObserverApproximationTowerUp$ row that bounds the observer-facing approximation route used by terminal consumers;
\item $P$ is the $\RealityConstrainedModelAuditUp$ row that records the model-facing audit surface and failure rows;
\item $L$ is the no-global-observer ledger, recording anchor changes, rejected privileged observers, and the finite routes by which invariant stability is checked;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RealityConstrainedObjectivityUp}$ row naming the whole packet.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a view from nowhere, no hidden synchronizing observer, and no objectivity claim outside the displayed multi-observer, invariant, fit, handoff, approximation, audit, ledger, and naming rows.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Reality-constrained objectivity certificate]
\label{def:reality-constrained-objectivity-certificate}
For accepted packets
$$
O=(H,A,I,F,R,T,P,L,N)
\quad\hbox{and}\quad
O'=(H',A',I',F',R',T',P',L',N'),
$$
the certificate classifier $\mathsf{RealityConstrainedObjectivityClassifier}(O,O')$ holds exactly when the multi-observer histories are related by displayed $\hsame$ transports, the anchor-change rows carry $A$ to $A'$ and back without selecting a privileged history, the invariant packets $I$ and $I'$ preserve their relation and value under the same classifier, and the fit, handoff, approximation, audit, ledger, and naming rows transport componentwise.

Thus certificate agreement is not local certainty inside one observer history. It is symmetric preservation of the invariant packet under admissible anchor change, with every physical-fit, observation-handoff, approximation, model-audit, and no-global-observer row still visible.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Reality-constrained objectivity anchor stability]
\label{thm:reality-constrained-objectivity-anchor-stability}
Let $O=(H,A,I,F,R,T,P,L,N)$ be an accepted $\RealityConstrainedObjectivityUp$ packet. If an objectivity claim is exported by $O$, then the claim must be read from the invariant packet $I$ through an anchor-change route recorded in $A$ and $L$. A claim depending on one observer history without such a symmetric route is local content, not a $\RealityConstrainedObjectivityUp$ export.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the carrier of \autoref{def:reality-constrained-objectivity-carrier}. The only rows that can export an objectivity claim are the multi-observer family $H$, anchor row $A$, invariant packet $I$, sibling carrier rows $F,R,T,P$, ledger $L$, and local NameCert row $N$.

Now use the certificate classifier of \autoref{def:reality-constrained-objectivity-certificate}. It requires anchor-change transport in both directions and preservation of the invariant packet under the same classifier; it does not accept one observer history as a distinguished frame.

Therefore a route through one history alone lacks the anchor-change row and the no-global-observer ledger. It remains local observational content even if it is internally stable for that history.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Reality-constrained objectivity NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:reality-constrained-objectivity-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:reality-constrained-objectivity-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\RealityConstrainedObjectivityUp}$ surface consists exactly of five obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission: the accepted object is exactly $O=(H,A,I,F,R,T,P,L,N)$;
\item classifier stability: every accepted comparison uses the certificate classifier of \autoref{def:reality-constrained-objectivity-certificate};
\item anchor-stability exactness: objectivity exports must preserve the invariant packet $I$ under the anchor-change rows recorded in $A$ and $L$;
\item sibling-carrier accountability: the physical-fit, observation-handoff, observer-approximation, and model-audit rows are consumed only through $F,R,T,P$;
\item non-escape: no consumer may infer objectivity from a single observer history, hidden global observer, detached model audit, unbounded observation route, or row outside $H,A,I,F,R,T,P,L,N$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:reality-constrained-objectivity-carrier}. The packet lists the only rows available to the local certificate.

Classifier stability follows from \autoref{def:reality-constrained-objectivity-certificate}. Comparisons transport the observer histories, anchor rows, invariant packet, sibling carriers, ledger, and naming row as one finite object rather than as detached local claims.

Anchor-stability exactness is the distinguished use of $A,I,L$. The invariant packet can be exported only when the anchor-change schedule and no-global-observer ledger record the symmetric route that preserves it.

Sibling-carrier accountability is forced by the rows $F,R,T,P$. Physical fit, observation handoff, approximation, and model audit data are consumed through the displayed sibling carriers, so no hidden adequacy or reflection route can be inserted after admission.

The non-escape row is the finite-packet conclusion. A claim requiring a single privileged observer, unbounded observation, detached audit, or absent coordinate has no projection from $O$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Reality-constrained objectivity falsifiable separation]
\label{thm:reality-constrained-objectivity-falsifiable-separation}
An accepted inter-observer invariant packet that factors every admitted comparison through one anchor row is rejected by the $\RealityConstrainedObjectivityUp$ classifier as non-objective. Such a packet may still carry local or model-audit content, but it does not export relational objectivity because the no-global-observer ledger cannot certify symmetric anchor change.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Assume the admitted comparisons all factor through one anchor row. Then the route has a distinguished observer standpoint that orders or evaluates the other histories from that row.

Apply the anchor-stability theorem. A $\RealityConstrainedObjectivityUp$ export must carry the invariant through the anchor-change schedule and no-global-observer ledger, not through a single privileged row.

The certificate classifier therefore rejects the packet as an objectivity certificate. The remaining rows may still support local content, fit comparison, observation handoff, approximation, or model audit, but they do not supply the symmetric anchor-change surface required for relational objectivity.
\end{proof}
