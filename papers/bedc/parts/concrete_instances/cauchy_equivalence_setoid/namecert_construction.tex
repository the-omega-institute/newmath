\begin{definition}[CauchyEquivalenceSetoid carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-equivalence-setoid-carrier}

$$
Q=(S_0,S_1,R_0,R_1,D,T,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S_0$ and $S_1$ are the two $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window rows. Each row lists only the visible observation windows that a classifier request may inspect.
\item $R_0$ and $R_1$ are the corresponding $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback rows. They expose the regular rational values attached to the stream windows before any real-name seal is consumed.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger. It records the admitted dyadic precision requests, comparison witnesses, and refinement rows used to compare the two regular names.
\item $T$ is the setoid-test row. It stores reflexivity, symmetry, and transitivity checks only as finite classifier rows over $S_0,S_1,R_0,R_1,D$.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal-boundary row reached after the stream windows, readback rows, dyadic ledger, and setoid-test row have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the two stream rows, the two readback rows, the dyadic ledger, the setoid-test row, the seal row, replay, provenance, and local naming.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from an equality-facing tolerance request through $S_0,S_1,R_0,R_1,D,T,E,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyEquivalenceSetoidUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyEquivalenceSetoidUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through the displayed tuple $S_0,S_1,R_0,R_1,D,T,E,H,C,P,N$. The carrier has no coordinate for quotient stream equality, host real equality, selected limits, countable choice, ambient completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyEquivalenceSetoid classifier laws]
\label{thm:cauchy-equivalence-setoid-classifier-laws}
For every accepted $\CauchyEquivalenceSetoidUp$ carrier
$$
Q=(S_0,S_1,R_0,R_1,D,T,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the classifier row $T$ supplies exactly the reflexive, symmetric, and transitive finite tests visible to the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyEquivalenceSetoidUp}$ surface:
\begin{enumerate}
\item reflexivity tests compare a stream window with itself through the matching $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row and the dyadic zero-tolerance entries of $D$;
\item symmetry tests reverse the two displayed stream-readback sides and reuse the same dyadic comparison ledger $D$ with no second equality relation;
\item transitivity tests concatenate two displayed comparison witnesses through a common readback side and discharge the resulting tolerance request by the refinement rows of $D$;
\item every test is replayed by $C$, transported componentwise by $H$, sourced by $P$, and named by $N$ before the $\RealUp$ seal row $E$ is consumed.
\end{enumerate}
No classifier law is stored outside the finite rows of $Q$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-equivalence-setoid-carrier}. The projection exposes two finite StreamName windows, two RegSeqRat readback rows, one DyadicRatCore tolerance ledger, one setoid-test row, the Real seal boundary, and the structural rows.

For reflexivity, use the same stream row and the same readback row on both sides. The dyadic ledger supplies the displayed zero-tolerance and refinement entries, so the test is a row of $T$ rather than a quotient assertion.

For symmetry, exchange the two stream-readback sides. The comparison witness remains in the same ledger $D$, and $T$ records the reversed finite row as the visible classifier test.

For transitivity, concatenate the two displayed comparison rows through their shared middle readback side. The resulting tolerance request is discharged by the refinement entries already listed in $D$.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name these finite rows, while $E$ is available only after the rows have been displayed. Since $Q$ has no quotient or host-equality coordinate, the local certificate contains exactly the listed classifier laws.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyEquivalenceSetoid transport]
\label{thm:cauchy-equivalence-setoid-transport}

$$

$$
Thus transport preserves the StreamName finite windows, RegSeqRat readback, DyadicRatCore tolerance ledger, Real seal boundary, displayed replay, package provenance, and local NameCert rows before any equality consumer may inspect the result.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a classifier read and its displayed $\hsame$ witness in $H$. By the carrier definition, $H$ has no global equality coordinate; it stores only componentwise transports for the rows of $Q$.

Apply the transport row to $S_0,S_1,R_0,R_1,D,T,E$ in order. The stream windows remain finite, the readback rows remain the same regular rational interface, the tolerance ledger remains the same dyadic comparison surface, and the seal row remains downstream of those rows.

The replay, provenance, and local naming rows are transported last. Since $C,P,N$ only replay, source, and name the displayed route, the transported read still factors through the same finite classifier surface before any equality-facing consumer can inspect it.
\end{proof}

\label{thm:cauchy-equivalence-setoid-namecert-surface}

\item StreamName window exactness, requiring every equality-facing observation to be one of the displayed windows in $S_0$ or $S_1$;
