\begin{theorem}[CauchyEquivalenceSetoid sibling route]
\label{thm:cauchy-equivalence-setoid-sibling-route}
Every setoid-classifier read exported by an accepted $\CauchyEquivalenceSetoidUp$ packet consumes the finite sibling route through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert} before any $\RealEqualityUp$ or $\MetricCompletionUp$ consumer may inspect it. The route reads finite stream windows, regular rational readbacks, the dyadic tolerance ledger, and the terminal real-name seal as displayed rows; it does not export host real equality, quotient setoid classes, selected representatives, countable choice, or ambient completion.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted setoid packet to its classifier route. The sequence-facing entry is a displayed $\StreamNameUp$ row, so a consumer first reads finite windows rather than a completed stream or selected representative.

The regular rational readback is then consumed through the $\RegSeqRatUp$ sibling route. Its tolerance comparisons are read only through the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger, which supplies the finite dyadic budget used by the classifier.

Only after those rows are fixed can the terminal $\RealUp$ seal be inspected. Thus RealEquality and MetricCompletion consumers receive a routed setoid classifier over displayed rows, not host real equality or a quotient completion object.
\end{proof}
