\begin{theorem}[Cauchy equivalence setoid Real-separability sibling route]
\label{thm:cauchy-equivalence-setoid-real-separability-sibling-route}
Every $\RealSeparabilityUp$ dense-route consumer that reads an accepted $\CauchyEquivalenceSetoidUp$ packet must first traverse the finite sibling route
$$
\StreamNameUp \longrightarrow \RegSeqRatUp \longrightarrow \DyadicRatCoreUp \longrightarrow \CauchyEquivalenceSetoidUp \longrightarrow \RealUp \longrightarrow \RealSeparabilityUp .
$$
Concretely, the consumer reads the two $\StreamNameUp$ windows, the two $\RegSeqRatUp$ readbacks, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, the setoid-test row, the terminal $\RealUp$ seal, and then the componentwise $\hsame$, displayed $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ rows before density is public through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-separability-namecert}. The route exports no quotient stream equality, selected representative, host real equality, countable-choice selector, or ambient completeness row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier and surface theorems \autoref{def:cauchy-equivalence-setoid-carrier} and \autoref{thm:cauchy-equivalence-setoid-namecert-surface}. They list exactly the two stream windows, two regular-rational readbacks, dyadic ledger, setoid-test row, Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

Apply the classifier laws and transport discipline, \autoref{thm:cauchy-equivalence-setoid-classifier-laws} and \autoref{thm:cauchy-equivalence-setoid-transport}. These keep the setoid-test row finite over the displayed StreamName, RegSeqRat, and DyadicRatCore rows before the Real seal is consumed.

Now use the existing Real-separability routes \autoref{thm:cauchy-equivalence-setoid-real-separability-route}, \autoref{thm:cauchy-equivalence-setoid-real-separability-forward-link}, and \autoref{thm:cauchy-equivalence-setoid-real-separability-stable-density}. They place the dense-route consumer of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-separability-namecert} after the same classifier and Real-seal surface.

Thus the public density read is available only after the sibling route has displayed every row in order. Since none of the cited carrier rows contains quotient equality, selected representatives, host equality, choice, or ambient completeness, the Real-separability consumer receives only the finite sibling edge.
\end{proof}
