\begin{proposition}[Cauchy equivalence setoid dense-readback consumer route]
\label{prop:cauchy-equivalence-setoid-real-separability-dense-consumer-route}
The $\CauchyEquivalenceSetoidUp$ packet supplies the classifier boundary consumed by the dense-readback routes of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-separability-namecert}. In particular, every dense $\RealSeparabilityUp$ read that identifies a rational or regular-rational approximation through Cauchy-tail agreement must keep the equivalence-setoid row upstream of the $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, and $\RealUp$ readback rows, so the dense consumer receives a finite classifier boundary rather than quotient stream equality, host real equality, or a selected completion representative.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
Project the $\CauchyEquivalenceSetoidUp$ carrier through the existing real-classifier and uniform-completion routes. Those routes expose the Cauchy-tail classifier before any regular rational or Real-facing seal is consumed.

The dense-readback packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-separability-namecert} uses that same boundary when it names approximating rationals. Since the setoid row is already upstream of the StreamName, RegSeqRat, DyadicRatCore, and Real rows, the dense consumer cannot replace it by quotient stream equality or by a host-selected representative.
\end{proof}
