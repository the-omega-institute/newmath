A $\RealUp$ or completion consumer first sees $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\CauchySequenceEquivalenceUp$, and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ rows, then the classifier laws and terminal seal; only after that finite dependency route has been displayed can the public equality read be consumed.

Therefore the sibling route is substantive and non-quotient: the setoid packet exposes equality only after the RegSeqRat and CauchySequenceEquivalence dependencies have been consumed, and no detached host equality coordinate is present.
\end{proof}
