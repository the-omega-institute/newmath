\begin{theorem}[Cauchy equivalence setoid open-tail forward link]
\label{thm:cauchy-equivalence-setoid-open-tail-forward-link}
Every accepted $\CauchyEquivalenceSetoidUp$ packet that is consumed by an open-tail classifier route sends its setoid comparison forward through the displayed route of \autoref{thm:cauchy-equivalence-setoid-open-tail-classifier}. The consumer first reads the two $\StreamNameUp$ finite windows, then the corresponding $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular-rational readback rows, then the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger and local setoid-test row, and only then reaches the $\RealUp$ completion seal. Thus the open-tail route is a child dependency of the setoid packet rather than a detached completion oracle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier from \autoref{def:cauchy-equivalence-setoid-carrier}. Its visible comparison data are the two stream names, their regular-rational readbacks, the dyadic tolerance ledger, the local setoid-test row, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming.

Apply the open-tail classifier route of \autoref{thm:cauchy-equivalence-setoid-open-tail-classifier}. That route already orders the finite windows before the $\RegSeqRatUp$ rows and the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger before the terminal $\RealUp$ seal.

Consequently a consumer that asks for the open-tail classifier consumes the same setoid comparison surface. Since the carrier has no coordinate for quotient stream equality, selected representatives, host real equality, or a detached completion point, the forward link is exactly the displayed child route.
\end{proof}
