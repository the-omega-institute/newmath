\begin{theorem}[Open-tail Cauchy equivalence classifier]
\label{thm:cauchy-equivalence-setoid-open-tail-classifier}
Every open-tail classifier read from an accepted $\CauchyEquivalenceSetoidUp$ packet is the exact sibling route consumed by the $\RegSeqRatUp$ completion readback and the terminal $\RealUp$ classifier boundary. It first exposes the two $\StreamNameUp$ finite windows, then the corresponding $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, then the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger and setoid-test row, and only then reaches the $\RealUp$ seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. Completion-facing consumers may read that route through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metriccompletion-namecert}, but they receive no quotient stream equality, selected representative, host real equality, or detached completion oracle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-equivalence-setoid-carrier}. An open-tail read begins with the two visible stream windows and their regular rational readback rows.

Use the RegSeqRat completion classifier route of \autoref{thm:cauchy-equivalence-setoid-regseqrat-completion-classifier}. That route places the regular rational readbacks before the metric-completion tolerance and completion rows, so the comparison is already a displayed finite classifier before any completion consumer can inspect it.

Finally use the Real classifier consumer of \autoref{thm:cauchy-equivalence-setoid-real-classifier-consumer}. The terminal Real boundary is reached only after the dyadic tolerance ledger and setoid-test row are visible, and the packet has no coordinate for quotient equality or selected representatives.
\end{proof}
