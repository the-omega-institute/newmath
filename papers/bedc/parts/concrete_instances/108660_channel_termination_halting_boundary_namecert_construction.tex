It records source-event rows, legal channel-event rows, terminator-position rows, decoder-state rows, halting-boundary refusal rows, trace rows, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming in one displayed packet. The point of the packet is the stratum separation: a legal channel event can end locally, while certificate-level halting remains a refusal boundary rather than a global total classifier.

\begin{definition}[ChannelTerminationHaltingBoundary carrier]
\label{def:channel-termination-halting-boundary-carrier}
A $\ChannelTerminationHaltingBoundaryUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
T=(S,E,B,D,L,R,F,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the source-event row, naming the inspected finite binary source word and the source-layer reading of its glyphs.
\item $E$ is the legal channel-event row, recording the encoded body over fragments $0$ and $10$ together with the displayed channel terminator $11$.
\item $B$ is the body-safety row, recording that the encoded body contains no adjacent $11$ before the terminator row is exposed.
\item $D$ is the decoder-state row, naming the left-to-right scan, the first displayed $11$, and the recovered source event.
\item $L$ is the legal-channel-event classifier row, accepting only event reads that pass through $S,E,B,D$ before any boundary claim is made.
\item $R$ is the halting-boundary refusal row. It records that local event termination is not a total classifier for arbitrary $\NameCert$ continuation chains.
\item $F$ is the finite trace row, carrying the prompt-response and bounded evaluator evidence used to display the refusal without solving certificate-level halting.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for source events, legal channel events, body safety, decoder state, classifier exposure, refusal, trace evidence, replay, provenance, and local naming.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from each boundary-facing request through $S,E,B,D,L,R,F,H,P,N$.

\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ChannelTerminationHaltingBoundaryUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $S,E,B,D,L,R,F,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for a bare bitstream parser, a host global halting oracle, an unrestricted evaluator, a certificate-of-certificates total classifier, or a meta-stop proof.

\begin{theorem}[ChannelTerminationHaltingBoundary NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:channel-termination-halting-boundary-namecert-obligations}

$$

the local $\NameCert_{\ChannelTerminationHaltingBoundaryUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the displayed packet; source-event exposure through $S$; legal channel-event exposure through $E$; body-safety exposure through $B$; decoder-state exposure through $D$; legal-channel-event classification through $L$; halting-boundary refusal through $R$; finite trace evidence through $F$; componentwise transport through $H$; displayed replay through $C$; provenance through $P$; and local naming through $N$.

No obligation is discharged by a bare bitstream parser, a host global halting oracle, an unrestricted evaluator, a total certificate-level halting classifier, or a meta-stop proof.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:channel-termination-halting-boundary-carrier}. The accepted packet displays exactly the source event, legal channel event, body-safety, decoder-state, classifier, refusal, trace, and structural rows.

The local channel obligations are exhausted by $S,E,B,D,L$. The source row names the event being encoded, the legal channel-event row displays the body and terminator, the body-safety row excludes an earlier terminator pattern, and the decoder-state row reads the first $11$ as the local endpoint.

The halting-boundary obligation is exhausted by $R$ and $F$. The refusal row states that the local endpoint is not a certificate-level total halting classifier, while the finite trace row records only bounded evidence around prompt-response and evaluator routes.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name that same finite route. Since the carrier has no coordinate for a global halting oracle or meta-stop proof, no local $\NameCert_{\ChannelTerminationHaltingBoundaryUp}$ obligation can export one.
\end{proof}

\end{proof}
