\begin{definition}[Finite-fold matrix identity data]
\label{def:finite-fold-matrix-identity-data}
Let $R$ be a scalar $\RingUp$ carrier with classifier $\sim_R$, zero endpoint
$0_R$, unit endpoint $1_R$, addition $+_R$, multiplication $\cdot_R$, and finite
additive folds from \autoref{thm:finite-additive-fold-congruence-coefficient-lists}. For each finite
dimension $n:\NatUp$, the finite-fold identity matrix $I_n$ is the carried
$n\times n$ matrix whose $(i,j)$ entry is $1_R$ when the finite indices agree
and $0_R$ otherwise.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Finite-fold matrix identity law]
\label{thm:finite-fold-matrix-identity-law}
Let $R$ be a scalar $\RingUp$ carrier with classifier $\sim_R$, zero endpoint
$0_R$, unit endpoint $1_R$, addition $+_R$, multiplication $\cdot_R$, and finite
additive folds over finite index spines as in
\autoref{thm:finite-additive-fold-congruence-coefficient-lists}. Let $A$ be a carried $n\times m$
$\MatrixUp$ matrix. Assume the scalar classifier equivalence fields, additive
zero laws, multiplication congruence, zero multiplication laws, and left and
right unit laws retained in \autoref{def:ring-stability-certificate}. Then the
finite-fold products with the identity matrices of
\autoref{def:finite-fold-matrix-identity-data} are carried and
$$
I_n\cdot_{\mathrm{Mat}} A\sim_{\mathrm{Mat}}A
\qquad\text{and}\qquad
A\cdot_{\mathrm{Mat}} I_m\sim_{\mathrm{Mat}}A.
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The multiplication closure row of \autoref{def:matrix-stability-certificate}
carries both products. By \autoref{def:matrix-classifier-specification}, it is
enough to compare scalar entries.

For the left identity, fix $i:\mathsf{Fin}(n)$ and $j:\mathsf{Fin}(m)$. The
finite fold for $(I_n\cdot_{\mathrm{Mat}}A)_{i,j}$ has one diagonal summand
$1_R\cdot_R A_{i,j}$ and all other summands $0_R\cdot_R A_{k,j}$. The unit law
classifies the diagonal summand with $A_{i,j}$, and the zero multiplication law
classifies every off-diagonal summand with $0_R$.

The finite additive fold over a spine with one carried scalar and all remaining
entries classified with $0_R$ reduces, by the additive zero laws and fold
congruence from \autoref{thm:finite-additive-fold-congruence-coefficient-lists},
to that single scalar. Hence $(I_n\cdot_{\mathrm{Mat}}A)_{i,j}\sim_R A_{i,j}$.

The right identity is symmetric. The fold for
$(A\cdot_{\mathrm{Mat}}I_m)_{i,j}$ has diagonal summand
$A_{i,j}\cdot_R1_R$ and off-diagonal summands classified with $0_R$. The right
unit law, zero multiplication laws, additive zero laws, and fold congruence
again collapse the fold to $A_{i,j}$. Packaging the two entrywise comparisons
gives the displayed matrix classifier laws.
\end{proof}
