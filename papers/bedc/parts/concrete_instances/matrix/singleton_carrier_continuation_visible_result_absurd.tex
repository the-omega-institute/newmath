\begin{theorem}[Matrix singleton visible continuation result exclusion]
\label{thm:matrix-singleton-carrier-continuation-visible-result-absurd}
Let $M,N:\mathsf{BHist}$ be carried by the singleton matrix carrier
$A_{\emp}$. Then a continuation \(\ContR(M,N,-)\) cannot have a visible
\(\mathsf{e0}\)-headed or \(\mathsf{e1}\)-headed result:
$$
\ContR(M,N,\mathsf{e0}(r))\to\bot
\qquad\text{and}\qquad
\ContR(M,N,\mathsf{e1}(r))\to\bot.
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier witnesses identify both inputs with \(\emp\). With those
identifications, any continuation result must be the BEDC append endpoint
\(\mathsf{append}(\emp,\emp)=\emp\). A visible-headed result cannot be that
empty endpoint, so both visible cases are impossible.
\end{proof}
