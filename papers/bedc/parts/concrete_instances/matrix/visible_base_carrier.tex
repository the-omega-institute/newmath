\begin{theorem}[Matrix singleton power visible-base carrier exactness]
\label{thm:matrix-singleton-pow-visible-base-carrier-iff}
For any history $m$ and exponent $e$, a singleton power with visible base
$\Ezero(m)$ or $\Eone(m)$ is carried exactly in the empty-exponent or zero-head
exponent cases:
$$
\begin{aligned}
A_{\emp}(\mathsf{MatrixSingletonPow}(\Ezero(m),e))
&\Longleftrightarrow
e\hsame\emp\ \lor\ \exists t.\ e=\Ezero(t),\\
A_{\emp}(\mathsf{MatrixSingletonPow}(\Eone(m),e))
&\Longleftrightarrow
e\hsame\emp\ \lor\ \exists t.\ e=\Ezero(t).
\end{aligned}
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Split on the exponent. For $\emp$, the power is the singleton identity
endpoint, hence carried. For $\Ezero(t)$, the recursive power is the singleton
zero endpoint, hence carried. For $\Eone(t)$, the power is an append product
whose right factor is the visible base; append empty-result inversion would
force that visible base to be history-same to $\emp$, contradicting BEDC
constructor separation. The reverse implication is immediate in the two
carried exponent cases, and impossible in the one-headed exponent case.
\end{proof}
