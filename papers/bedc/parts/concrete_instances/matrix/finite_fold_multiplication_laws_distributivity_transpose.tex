\begin{theorem}[Matrix multiplication left-distributes over matrix addition]
\label{thm:matrix-multiplication-left-distributes-over-addition}
Let $R$ be a scalar $\RingUp$ carrier with classifier $\sim_R$, zero endpoint
$0_R$, addition $+_R$, multiplication $\cdot_R$, and finite additive folds over
finite index spines as in \autoref{thm:finite-additive-fold-congruence-coefficient-lists}. Let
$n,m,p:\NatUp$, and consider the $\MatrixUp$ carriers of
\autoref{def:matrix-carrier} with pointwise classifier from
\autoref{def:matrix-classifier-specification}. Let
$$
A:\mathsf{Fin}(n)\times\mathsf{Fin}(m)\to R,
\qquad
B,C:\mathsf{Fin}(m)\times\mathsf{Fin}(p)\to R
$$
be carried matrices. Assume matrix addition is pointwise and matrix
multiplication is the finite-fold operation, using the addition and
multiplication closure rows of \autoref{def:matrix-stability-certificate}.
Assume the scalar ring stability fields of \autoref{def:ring-stability-certificate}:
additive abelian-group laws, addition and multiplication congruence, and left
distributivity. Then the displayed matrices are carried and
$$
A\cdot_{\mathrm{Mat}}(B+C)\sim_{\mathrm{Mat}}(A\cdot_{\mathrm{Mat}}B)+(A\cdot_{\mathrm{Mat}}C).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Closure follows from the matrix addition and multiplication rows of
\autoref{def:matrix-stability-certificate}. By
\autoref{def:matrix-classifier-specification}, compare entries.

Fix $i:\mathsf{Fin}(n)$ and $k:\mathsf{Fin}(p)$. Over the finite spine
enumerating $\mathsf{Fin}(m)$, define
$$
xs(j):=A_{i,j}\cdot_RB_{j,k},
\qquad
ys(j):=A_{i,j}\cdot_RC_{j,k}.
$$
The pointwise addition definition and scalar left distributivity classify each
summand $A_{i,j}\cdot_R(B+C)_{j,k}$ with $xs(j)+_Rys(j)$.

Fold congruence lifts these pointwise comparisons through the finite fold. Then
\autoref{lem:finite-additive-fold-separates-pointwise-scalar-sums} separates the
fold of pointwise sums into the sum of the two finite folds. Unfolding matrix
multiplication and pointwise matrix addition identifies the endpoint with
$((A\cdot_{\mathrm{Mat}}B)+(A\cdot_{\mathrm{Mat}}C))_{i,k}$. Since $i$ and $k$
were arbitrary, the pointwise classifier packages the displayed law.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Finite-fold matrix transpose reverses multiplication]
\label{thm:finite-fold-matrix-transpose-reverses-multiplication}
Let $R$ be a scalar $\RingUp$ carrier with opposite multiplication data as in
\autoref{def:opposite-ring-certificate-data}. Let $A$ be a carried $n\times m$
matrix and $B$ a carried $m\times p$ matrix. Assume matrix multiplication is the
finite scalar-fold operation and matrix transpose swaps the two finite index
coordinates pointwise. Then
$$
(A\cdot_{\mathrm{Mat}}B)^{T}
\sim_{\mathrm{Mat}}
B^{T}\cdot_{\mathrm{Mat}}A^{T}
$$
over the opposite scalar multiplication.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The transpose carrier row swaps the row and column witnesses, and multiplication
closure carries both displayed matrices. Entrywise, the left side at
$(k,i)$ is the fold of $A_{i,j}\cdot_RB_{j,k}$ over the shared inner finite
spine.

The right side in the opposite scalar multiplication is the fold of
$B_{j,k}\cdot_{R^{\mathrm{op}}}A_{i,j}$ over the same finite spine. By
\autoref{def:opposite-ring-certificate-data}, each opposite product is the same
scalar endpoint as $A_{i,j}\cdot_RB_{j,k}$. Fold congruence from
\autoref{thm:finite-additive-fold-congruence-coefficient-lists} compares the two
folds, and the pointwise matrix classifier packages the transpose law.
\end{proof}
