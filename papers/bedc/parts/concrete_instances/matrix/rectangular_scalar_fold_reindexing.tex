\begin{lemma}[Rectangular scalar fold reindexing]
\label{lem:rectangular-scalar-fold-reindexing}
Let $R$ be a scalar $\RingUp$ carrier with classifier $\sim_R$, zero endpoint
$0_R$, addition $+_R$, and finite additive folds over finite index spines as in
\autoref{thm:finite-additive-fold-congruence-coefficient-lists}. Let $J$ and $K$ be finite index
carriers with chosen finite enumerating spines, and let
$s:J\times K\to R$ be a pointwise carried scalar family. Assume the scalar
classifier equivalence fields and the additive associativity, commutativity,
zero, and congruence rows retained in \autoref{def:ring-stability-certificate}.
Then the two rectangular iterated folds are scalar-classified:
$$
\Sigma_{j:J}\Sigma_{k:K}s(j,k)
\sim_R
\Sigma_{k:K}\Sigma_{j:J}s(j,k).
$$
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Flatten both iterated folds to finite spines over the product index carrier
$J\times K$. For the left fold, use the enumeration that lists the $K$-fiber
inside each $j:J$; for the right fold, use the enumeration that lists the
$J$-fiber inside each $k:K$.

The two flattened spines contain the same carried scalar entries, related by the
coordinate-swap bijection $(j,k)\mapsto(k,j)$. Additive associativity and
commutativity make the finite additive fold invariant under this finite
permutation, while fold congruence transports the pointwise reflexive scalar
classifier witnesses through the two folds.

Equivalently, apply the two-index fold swap certificate of
\autoref{def:two-index-finite-fold-swap-certificate} to the family $s$ and to
the transpose family $s^{\tau}(k,j):=s(j,k)$. The transpose compatibility
witness is reflexive at every scalar entry, so the certificate gives exactly the
displayed reindexing comparison.
\end{proof}
