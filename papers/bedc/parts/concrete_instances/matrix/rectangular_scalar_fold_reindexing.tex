\begin{lemma}[Rectangular scalar fold reindexing]
\label{lem:rectangular-scalar-fold-reindexing}
Let $R$ be a scalar $\RingUp$ carrier with classifier $\sim_R$, zero endpoint
$0_R$, addition $+_R$, and finite additive fold $\mathsf{fold}_{+,R}$ from
\autoref{thm:finite-additive-fold-congruence-coefficient-lists}. Assume the scalar classifier
equivalence fields and the additive associativity, commutativity, zero, and
congruence rows retained in \autoref{def:ring-stability-certificate}. Let $J$
and $K$ be finite index carriers with chosen finite enumerating spines, and
write $\Sigma_J$ and $\Sigma_K$ for the corresponding finite folds. For every
carried scalar family $s:J\times K\to R$,
$$
\Sigma_J(j\mapsto \Sigma_K(k\mapsto s(j,k)))
\sim_R
\Sigma_K(k\mapsto \Sigma_J(j\mapsto s(j,k))).
$$
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Use \autoref{thm:two-index-fold-swap-from-finite-permutation-invariance} with
the transpose family $s^\tau(k,j):=s(j,k)$. Pointwise classifier reflexivity
supplies the matched-entry witness required by the swap certificate.

The conclusion of the swap theorem is exactly the displayed comparison after
unfolding the notational abbreviations $\Sigma_J$ and $\Sigma_K$ for the chosen
finite enumerations.
\end{proof}
