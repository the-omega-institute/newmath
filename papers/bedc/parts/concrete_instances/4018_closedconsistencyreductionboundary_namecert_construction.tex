\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ClosedConsistencyReductionBoundaryUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-closed-consistency-reduction-boundary-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\ClosedConsistencyReductionBoundaryUp$ packet turns the frontier of
\autoref{ch:closed-metacic-consistency-assembly} into a finite NameCert object.
It is downstream of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-subjectreduction-namecert}:
the packet names the rows that would be consumed by a closed-consistency
reduction path, while keeping the blocked subject-reduction discharge as a
public obstruction row rather than as an available theorem.

\begin{definition}[Closed-consistency reduction-boundary carrier]
\label{def:closed-consistency-reduction-boundary-carrier}
A $\ClosedConsistencyReductionBoundaryUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
R=(S,Q,E,D,K,O,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the closed source proof row, naming the empty-context proof
candidate and its displayed false type;
\item $Q$ is the reduction-route row, recording the finite beta route from the
source proof row toward a normal endpoint;
\item $E$ is the normal endpoint row, naming the reached term together with its
closedness and beta-strong normality data;
\item $D$ is the subject-reduction discharge row, exposing the beta,
application-argument, lambda-domain, and Pi-domain obligations required before
the false type may be transported along $Q$;
\item $K$ is the closed-normal consistency row consumed after the endpoint row
has retained the displayed false type;
\item $O$ is the obstruction ledger row, recording that the present packet does
not contain the missing closed subject-reduction discharge;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the source, route,
endpoint, discharge, closed-normal consistency, obstruction, package, and local
naming rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which consistency consumers
read $S,Q,E,D,K,O$ inside the finite packet;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, MetaCIC closed typing,
beta-reduction routes, beta-strong normal endpoints, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, subject
reduction, closed-normal consistency, obstruction rows, and local
$\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local
$\NameCert_{\ClosedConsistencyReductionBoundaryUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no full closed consistency theorem, no unparameterized
closed subject-reduction theorem, and no route that transports the displayed
false type from $S$ to $E$ without the discharge row $D$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Closed-consistency reduction-boundary NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:closed-consistency-reduction-boundary-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of
\autoref{def:closed-consistency-reduction-boundary-carrier}, the local
$\NameCert_{\ClosedConsistencyReductionBoundaryUp}$ surface consists of five
finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item source exposure: every accepted packet displays the closed source proof
row $S$ together with the false type it claims to inhabit;
\item route exposure: the beta route $Q$ and normal endpoint $E$ are public
rows, not ambient normalization data;
\item discharge exposure: the subject-reduction discharge row $D$ is the only
row that may preserve the displayed false type along $Q$;
\item normal-consistency handoff: the closed-normal consistency row $K$ is read
only after the endpoint row $E$ has retained the displayed false type;
\item obstruction accounting: the ledger row $O$ records the missing closed
subject-reduction discharge and prevents $K$ from being repacked as full closed
consistency.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the displayed packet $R$. The rows $S,Q,E,D,K,O,H,C,P,N$ are finite
coordinates, so source exposure, route exposure, discharge exposure, normal
handoff, and obstruction accounting are all read from the same carrier.

The subject-reduction sibling
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-subjectreduction-namecert} supplies the
discipline for reading preservation as a named row rather than as an ambient
conversion principle. Here that row is $D$, and the packet gives no second
coordinate that can preserve the false type along $Q$.

The closed-normal consistency row $K$ is therefore downstream of $E$ only under
the displayed discharge boundary. Since $O$ records the missing closed
discharge, the local certificate exposes the reduction boundary without closing
full closed consistency.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Closed-consistency reduction-boundary obstruction readback]
\label{thm:closed-consistency-reduction-boundary-obstruction-readback}
Every downstream read of an accepted
$\ClosedConsistencyReductionBoundaryUp$ packet factors through
$$
S,Q,E,D,K,O,H,C,P,N.
$$
A consumer may inspect the source proof row, reduction route, normal endpoint,
subject-reduction discharge row, closed-normal consistency row, obstruction
ledger, transports, continuations, provenance, and local naming certificate.
It cannot read full closed consistency, a closed subject-reduction theorem, an
undisplayed false-type transport, or an endpoint consistency result detached
from the discharge row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the classifier clause of
\autoref{def:closed-consistency-reduction-boundary-carrier}. Public comparison
and downstream reading are restricted to the displayed rows and their
$\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ provenance.

The normal endpoint can be consumed by the closed-normal consistency row only
through the route and discharge rows. Thus the readback exposes the precise
place where arbitrary closed proof reduction would need subject reduction, and
it does not supply that missing theorem.

The non-readable objects are exactly absent from the carrier. Full closed
consistency, a closed subject-reduction theorem, hidden false-type transport,
and detached endpoint consistency have no coordinate in $R$ and no continuation
route in $C$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Closed-consistency reduction-boundary vision handoff]
\label{thm:closed-consistency-reduction-boundary-vision-handoff}
The $\ClosedConsistencyReductionBoundaryUp$ packet realizes the frontier of
\autoref{ch:closed-metacic-consistency-assembly} as finite BEDC data. It
connects the closed source proof row, beta route, normal endpoint,
subject-reduction discharge boundary, closed-normal consistency row, and
obstruction ledger without claiming that arbitrary closed proofs can already be
transported to beta-strong normal endpoints of the same displayed false type.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the frontier in
\autoref{sec:closed-metacic-consistency-assembly-frontier}. Full closed
consistency is blocked at the step where an arbitrary closed proof must retain
its displayed false type along reduction to a beta-strong normal endpoint.

The carrier of
\autoref{def:closed-consistency-reduction-boundary-carrier} names exactly that
interface. The source row $S$, route row $Q$, endpoint row $E$, discharge row
$D$, closed-normal consistency row $K$, and obstruction row $O$ are separate
finite coordinates with displayed transport, continuation, package, and naming
data.

Therefore the packet is a handoff from the vision frontier to the
concrete-instance layer. It records the boundary that a later formal target may
consume, while the obstruction ledger keeps the manuscript from citing the
packet as a proof of full closed consistency.
\end{proof}
