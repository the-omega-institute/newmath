Following Bishop and Bridges constructive analysis, the $\LocatedRealIntervalBisectionUp$ packet records finite dyadic interval bisection over displayed $\RealUp$ endpoints without importing an interval-completeness theorem. It sits beside the real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the dyadic arithmetic core of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the regular rational readback route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the closed bounded interval carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closedboundedinterval-namecert}. The packet is a finite bisection ledger for located interval reads; it is not a Dedekind completion, quotient-real equality, trichotomy on arbitrary reals, a selected infinite branch, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[Located real interval bisection carrier]
\label{def:located-real-interval-bisection-carrier}
A $\LocatedRealIntervalBisectionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
B=(I,L,M,D,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $I$ is the $\ClosedBoundedIntervalUp$ source row, carrying lower and upper $\RealUp$ endpoint names, endpoint order, and interval-containment data.
\item $L$ is the locatedness row, recording the finite apartness or locator evidence used to decide which inspected subinterval remains visible at the requested stage.
\item $M$ is the dyadic midpoint row, exposing the midpoint construction for each retained interval cell.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ diameter and tolerance ledger attached to the same midpoint rows.
\item $W$ is the finite endpoint-window row, recording which endpoint and midpoint windows are inspected by the bisection request.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row obtained from $W$ and $D$.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal available only after the source interval, locatedness, midpoint, dyadic, window, and readback rows have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the interval, locatedness, midpoint, dyadic, window, readback, seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a bisection request through $I,L,M,D,W,R,E,H$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LocatedRealIntervalBisectionUp$, $\ClosedBoundedIntervalUp$, $\RealUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedRealIntervalBisectionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $I,L,M,D,W,R,E,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for a completed nested-interval limit, quotient equality of real names, excluded-middle order trichotomy, a selected infinite branch, countable choice, or ambient real completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Located real interval bisection obligation surface]
\label{thm:located-real-interval-bisection-obligation-surface}
For every accepted $\LocatedRealIntervalBisectionUp$ carrier
$$
B=(I,L,M,D,W,R,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedRealIntervalBisectionUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the closed bounded interval source, locatedness row, midpoint row, dyadic tolerance ledger, endpoint windows, regular rational readback, terminal real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming;
\item source visibility, requiring every bisection read to expose the $\ClosedBoundedIntervalUp$ source row $I$ before any midpoint, diameter, branch, or seal row is consumed;
\item located branch discipline, requiring $L$ to record the finite locator evidence for the retained cell without adding global trichotomy on $\RealUp$;
\item dyadic midpoint exactness, requiring $M$ and $D$ to supply the displayed midpoint and diameter budgets at the requested finite depth;
\item readback handoff, requiring $W,R,E$ to route every Real-facing output through finite endpoint windows and a $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback;
\item non-escape, excluding completed nested-interval limits, quotient-real equality, selected infinite branches, countable choice, host endpoint equality, and ambient $\RealUp$ completeness from the carrier.
\end{enumerate}
No LocatedRealIntervalBisection obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:located-real-interval-bisection-carrier}. The accepted packet displays exactly the closed bounded interval source, locator row, midpoint row, dyadic ledger, endpoint windows, regular readback, terminal real seal, and structural rows.

The source stage is $I,L$. A request first reads the displayed endpoints and interval containment through $I$, then reads the finite locator evidence in $L$ before any retained child interval is available.

The bisection stage is $M,D,W$. Induction on the finite retained-depth ledger reads one midpoint row at a time. At each step the dyadic tolerance entries in $D$ bound the retained child, while $W$ records the inspected endpoint and midpoint windows.

The Real-facing handoff is $R,E,H,C,P,N$. The regular rational readback $R$ is computed from the displayed windows and dyadic budgets, and the terminal seal $E$ is downstream from that readback. The structural rows only transport, replay, source, and name the same finite route.

Since the carrier contains no completed nested-interval limit, quotient-real equality, global trichotomy, selected infinite branch, countable-choice row, or ambient completeness coordinate, the listed rows exhaust the local obligation surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Located real interval bisection finite handoff]
\label{thm:located-real-interval-bisection-finite-handoff}

$$
\ClosedBoundedIntervalUp \longmapsto L,M,D,W \longmapsto \RegSeqRatUp \longmapsto \RealUp .
$$
The consumer receives one finite located interval bisection ledger with dyadic midpoint and diameter rows. It receives no completed intersection point, quotient representative, selected branch, countable-choice witness, or ambient completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the carrier projection and the obligation surface of \autoref{thm:located-real-interval-bisection-obligation-surface}. Source visibility places every read below the row $I$ before midpoint or readback data are available.

Located branch discipline then exposes only the finite locator row $L$. Dyadic midpoint exactness exposes $M,D,W$ for the requested depth, so the retained cell is read as a finite ledger rather than as an infinite branch.

Finally $R$ repacks the displayed windows as regular rational readback data, and $E,H,C,P,N$ seal and name the same route for $\RealUp$ consumers. The absent coordinates listed in the carrier definition cannot be projected from the packet.
\end{proof}
