Following Bishop--Bridges constructive metric completion and Kelley's net and filter language, the $\MetricCofinalCompletionUp$ packet records the finite cofinal-evidence surface that lets a completion consumer compare Cauchy-filter and Cauchy-net presentations before reading a Real-facing or separated-metric readback. It sits beside the metric source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the Cauchy-filter branch of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyfilter-namecert}, the Cauchy-net branch of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchynet-namecert}, the regular rational and real readbacks of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the separated comparison of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separatedmetric-namecert}, the uniform completion route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformcompletion-namecert}, and the metric completion surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metriccompletion-namecert}.

\begin{definition}[Metric cofinal completion carrier]
\label{def:metric-cofinal-completion-carrier}
A $\MetricCofinalCompletionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(X,F,D,R,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the source $\MetricUp$ row. It records the metric carrier, distance observation, and finite request boundary consumed by the completion-facing route.
\item $F$ is the $\CauchyFilterUp$ branch. It records filter-neighborhood evidence and the finite Cauchy request surface over $X$.
\item $D$ is the $\CauchyNetUp$ branch. It records directed-index evidence, net values, and the finite Cauchy request surface over the same $X$.
\item $R$ is the readback row. It records either the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback needed by regular rational names or the $\RealUp$ seal reached from that readback.
\item $S$ is the $\SeparatedMetricUp$ comparison row. It records the equality-facing separated metric comparison available after both cofinal branches have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the metric source, filter branch, net branch, readback, separated comparison, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the route
$$
X,F,D \longmapsto R,S \longmapsto H,P,N .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\MetricCofinalCompletionUp$, $\MetricUp$, $\CauchyFilterUp$, $\CauchyNetUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\SeparatedMetricUp$, $\UniformCompletionUp$, $\MetricCompletionUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MetricCofinalCompletionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for quotient completion, selected representatives, countable choice, open-cover compactness, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Metric cofinal completion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:metric-cofinal-completion-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\MetricCofinalCompletionUp$ carrier $Q=(X,F,D,R,S,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\MetricCofinalCompletionUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission, metric-source exactness, Cauchy-filter branch exposure, Cauchy-net branch exposure, cofinal comparison of the two branches, readback exactness through $\RegSeqRatUp$ or $\RealUp$, separated comparison through $\SeparatedMetricUp$, transport and replay stability, provenance, local naming, and non-escape of quotient completion, selected representatives, countable choice, open-cover compactness, and ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:metric-cofinal-completion-carrier}. The metric source is the row $X$, and it is the only row that supplies the underlying metric request boundary.

The Cauchy-filter and Cauchy-net branches are the rows $F$ and $D$. They are both read over $X$, so a completion-facing consumer can compare their request surfaces without introducing a host quotient or a selected representative.

The readback and separated comparison are the rows $R$ and $S$. The row $R$ keeps regular rational and real-name evidence as displayed readback data, while $S$ records separated-metric comparison after the two Cauchy branches have been exposed.

The rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same route. Since no other coordinate exists, the excluded host-side completion principles cannot enter the accepted packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Metric cofinal completion filter-net cofinality]
\label{thm:metric-cofinal-completion-filter-net-cofinality}
Every completion-facing read of an accepted $\MetricCofinalCompletionUp$ packet factors the Cauchy-filter and Cauchy-net branches through the same metric source row:
$$
X \longmapsto F,D \longmapsto R,S .
$$
Thus the packet records cofinal evidence between the $\CauchyFilterUp$ and $\CauchyNetUp$ presentations before any $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\SeparatedMetricUp$, $\UniformCompletionUp$, or $\MetricCompletionUp$ consumer can inspect the route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier and read $X$ first. Both branch rows name Cauchy evidence over this same metric source, so neither branch can change the metric carrier or distance request boundary.

The filter branch $F$ supplies neighborhood evidence, and the net branch $D$ supplies directed-index evidence. Cofinality is recorded by requiring both rows to be visible before the readback row $R$ or the separated comparison row $S$ is consumed.

The downstream completion chapters read only the displayed route. The uniform and metric completion surfaces may consume $X,F,D,R,S,H,C,P,N$, but the packet gives them no separate quotient object, representative selector, choice principle, compact open-cover argument, or ambient completeness seal.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Metric cofinal completion nonescape]
\label{thm:metric-cofinal-completion-nonescape}
Every accepted $\MetricCofinalCompletionUp$ read remains inside the finite route
$$
X,F,D,R,S,H,C,P,N .
$$
In particular, no consumer may export quotient completion equality, selected representatives, countable choice, open-cover compactness, or $\RealUp$ completeness merely from the cofinal filter-net evidence.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier inventory of \autoref{def:metric-cofinal-completion-carrier} lists the whole packet. Rows $X,F,D,R,S$ give the metric source, two Cauchy branches, finite readback, and separated comparison.

Rows $H$ and $C$ preserve that inventory under $\hsame$ transport and $\Cont$ replay. Rows $P$ and $N$ record provenance and local naming for the same displayed route.

None of the excluded principles has a coordinate in the packet or a replay edge out of it. Hence any accepted consumer remains inside the finite cofinal-evidence surface.
\end{proof}
