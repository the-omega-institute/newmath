\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\GeomQuantizationUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-geomquantization-namecert}

The geometric-quantization interface $\GeomQuantizationUp$ packages the construction of a Hilbert space from a symplectic manifold via a prequantum line bundle, polarisation, and metaplectic correction. It depends on $\NameCert_{\SymplecticUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-symplectic-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\HilbertUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-hilbert-namecert}), and bridges geometric and operator-algebraic certificates through a common polarisation classifier.

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:geomquantization-carrier}

\begin{definition}[GeomQuantization BHist source packet]
\label{def:geomquantization-bhist-source-packet}
A $\GeomQuantizationUp$ source packet is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ package
$$
\mathcal{G}=(\mathcal{S},\mathcal{H},L,\Pi,\mu,\rho,\lambda,\tau)
$$
where $\mathcal{S}$ is a dependency row certified by $\NameCert_{\SymplecticUp}$, $\mathcal{H}$ is a dependency row certified by $\NameCert_{\HilbertUp}$, $L:\mathsf{BHist}$ is the prequantum line-bundle row over the symplectic source, $\Pi:\mathsf{BHist}$ is the polarisation classifier row, $\mu:\mathsf{BHist}$ is the metaplectic-correction ledger row, $\rho$ records the $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance of the symplectic and Hilbert dependency packets, $\lambda$ is the finite $\Cont$ readback from $(\mathcal{S},L,\Pi,\mu)$ to the Hilbert carrier row, and $\tau$ is the family of $\hsame$ transports preserving the source, line-bundle, polarisation, metaplectic, and Hilbert endpoint rows.

The packet is accepted only when all listed rows project from one displayed $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance package. Thus $\GeomQuantizationUp$ carries a finite history surface from a certified symplectic source to a certified Hilbert target; it does not export an ambient quantization functor, a host Hilbert construction, or uncarried symplectic geometry.
\end{definition}
\leandef{BEDC.Derived.GeomQuantizationUp.GeomQuantizationBHistSourcePacket}

\begin{theorem}[GeomQuantization source dependency surface]
\label{thm:geomquantization-source-dependency-surface}
For every packet of \autoref{def:geomquantization-bhist-source-packet}, the $\NameCert_{\SymplecticUp}$ projection and the $\NameCert_{\HilbertUp}$ projection are rows of the same $\mathsf{BHist}$ package. The prequantum line-bundle row, polarisation classifier row, metaplectic ledger row, finite $\Cont$ readback, $\hsame$ transports, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance are therefore shared dependency data rather than separate certificates.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the tuple $\mathcal{G}$. Its first two components are the symplectic and Hilbert dependency rows, and its provenance component $\rho$ records both rows inside one package.

The remaining components are read from that package: $L$ gives the prequantum line-bundle row, $\Pi$ gives the polarisation classifier, $\mu$ gives the metaplectic ledger, $\lambda$ gives the finite continuation to the Hilbert endpoint, and $\tau$ gives the history transports.

Repacking these projections returns the same source packet. Since the construction never leaves the displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ package, the two dependency projections share one BEDC carrier surface.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.GeomQuantizationUp.GeomQuantizationBHistSourcePacket\_source\_dependency\_surface}

\begin{theorem}[GeomQuantization polarisation classifier stability]
\label{thm:geomquantization-polarisation-classifier-stability}
If two packets of \autoref{def:geomquantization-bhist-source-packet} are related by the carried $\hsame$ transports on their $\NameCert_{\SymplecticUp}$ source rows and by the same $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance, then transport of the polarisation classifier preserves the prequantum line-bundle row and the polarisation row, and the finite $\Cont$ readback lands in the transported $\NameCert_{\HilbertUp}$ row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the transported symplectic source rows. The packet lists $\tau$ as the transport family, so the source comparison is a BEDC history comparison rather than an equality of external symplectic manifolds.

Apply the same transport family to the line-bundle row $L$ and the polarisation row $\Pi$. Because both rows are components of the same package, their transported versions remain attached to the transported source.

Finally read the ledger $\lambda$. It is the finite $\Cont$ path from the transported source, line-bundle, polarisation, and metaplectic rows to the Hilbert endpoint. Repacking that endpoint through the $\NameCert_{\HilbertUp}$ dependency row proves classifier stability.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.GeomQuantizationUp.GeomQuantizationBHistSourcePacket\_polarisation\_classifier\_stability}

\begin{theorem}[GeomQuantization ledger exactness]
\label{thm:geomquantization-ledger-exactness}
The $\GeomQuantizationUp$ ledger is exact for exactly the rows listed in \autoref{def:geomquantization-bhist-source-packet}: the $\NameCert_{\SymplecticUp}$ source row, the $\NameCert_{\HilbertUp}$ endpoint row, the prequantum line-bundle row, the polarisation classifier row, the metaplectic-correction ledger row, the finite $\Cont$ readback, the $\hsame$ transports, and the $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance. Coverage, soundness, and completeness range only over those rows and exclude host Hilbert-space construction and uncarried symplectic geometry.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Coverage is projection from an accepted packet. Each accepted packet displays the two dependency rows, the line-bundle row, the polarisation row, the metaplectic ledger, the continuation readback, the transport family, and the provenance row.

For soundness, take exactly such displayed rows with the dependency certificates attached. Repack them as the tuple $\mathcal{G}$, and the carrier definition accepts the resulting package.

For completeness, suppose a public $\GeomQuantizationUp$ row is accepted. The carrier definition has no projection for a host Hilbert construction and no projection for uncarried symplectic data. Hence every public row is generated by the listed source, classifier, ledger, transport, continuation, and provenance rows.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.GeomQuantizationUp.GeomQuantizationBHistSourcePacket\_ledger\_exactness}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.GeomQuantizationUp.GeomQuantizationBHistSourcePacket\_line\_ledger\_transport\_determinacy}

\begin{theorem}[GeomQuantization NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:geomquantization-namecert-obligation-surface}
The paper-side $\NameCert_{\GeomQuantizationUp}$ obligation surface consists of the carrier packet of \autoref{def:geomquantization-bhist-source-packet}, the shared dependency surface of \autoref{thm:geomquantization-source-dependency-surface}, the polarisation classifier stability row of \autoref{thm:geomquantization-polarisation-classifier-stability}, and the ledger exactness row of \autoref{thm:geomquantization-ledger-exactness}. Downstream consumers may project the symplectic dependency, Hilbert dependency, prequantum, polarisation, metaplectic, $\Cont$, $\hsame$, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ rows, but no stronger geometric-quantization bridge is part of this surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Assemble the carrier first. The definition supplies a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet with the two dependency certificates, the prequantum row, the polarisation row, the metaplectic row, continuation readback, transport data, and provenance.

Next use \autoref{thm:geomquantization-source-dependency-surface} to bind the $\NameCert_{\SymplecticUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\HilbertUp}$ projections to the same package. Use \autoref{thm:geomquantization-polarisation-classifier-stability} to keep the classifier stable under the carried $\hsame$ transports.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:geomquantization-ledger-exactness}. Its coverage, soundness, and completeness clauses say that the public certificate is exhausted by the displayed rows. Repacking these rows gives the $\NameCert_{\GeomQuantizationUp}$ obligation surface and rules out any unlisted bridge data.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.GeomQuantizationUp.GeomQuantizationBHistSourcePacket\_namecert\_obligation\_surface}

\begin{theorem}[GeomQuantization shared-provenance readback exactness]
\label{thm:geomquantization-shared-provenance-readback-exactness}
Any public readback of a $\GeomQuantizationUp$ packet that consumes both the
$\NameCert_{\SymplecticUp}$ source row and the $\NameCert_{\HilbertUp}$
endpoint row factors through the same displayed $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance row,
finite $\Cont$ readback, and carried $\hsame$ transport family of
\autoref{def:geomquantization-bhist-source-packet}. The readback may also
project the prequantum line-bundle row, polarisation classifier row, and
metaplectic-correction ledger row named by that packet, but it cannot read an
ambient quantization functor or host Hilbert construction.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the accepted source packet. By
\autoref{thm:geomquantization-source-dependency-surface}, the symplectic source
row and Hilbert endpoint row are projections of one finite $\mathsf{BHist}$
package, and their provenance is the single displayed $\mathsf{Pkg}$ row
$\rho$.

Next transport the classifier data. The polarisation classifier stability
theorem keeps the prequantum line-bundle and polarisation rows attached to the
same carried $\hsame$ transport family, and it sends the finite $\Cont$ ledger
to the transported $\NameCert_{\HilbertUp}$ endpoint row.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:geomquantization-ledger-exactness} together with
\autoref{thm:geomquantization-namecert-obligation-surface}. These results
exhaust the public certificate by the displayed source, endpoint, classifier,
ledger, continuation, transport, and provenance rows. Since no accepted packet
field names a host quantization functor or an ambient Hilbert construction,
the public readback has no route to either structure.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.GeomQuantizationUp.GeomQuantizationBHistSourcePacket\_shared\_provenance\_readback\_exactness}

\begin{theorem}[GeomQuantization public certificate handoff]
\label{thm:geomquantization-public-certificate-handoff}
A public $\NameCert_{\GeomQuantizationUp}$ consumer may read exactly the shared
$\NameCert_{\SymplecticUp}$ source row, the $\NameCert_{\HilbertUp}$ endpoint
row, the prequantum line-bundle row, the polarisation classifier row, the
metaplectic ledger row, the finite $\Cont$ readback, the $\hsame$ transport
family, and the $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance supplied by
\autoref{def:geomquantization-bhist-source-packet} and exhausted by
\autoref{thm:geomquantization-namecert-obligation-surface}. The handoff exports
no host quantization functor, no ambient Hilbert construction, and no
uncarried symplectic geometry.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.GeomQuantizationUp.GeomQuantizationBHistSourcePacket\_public\_certificate\_handoff}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted source packet. Its displayed coordinates supply the
symplectic dependency row, Hilbert endpoint row, prequantum line-bundle row,
polarisation classifier, metaplectic ledger, finite continuation readback,
transport family, and package provenance.

Apply \autoref{thm:geomquantization-source-dependency-surface} to bind the
source and endpoint rows to one provenance package. Then use
\autoref{thm:geomquantization-polarisation-classifier-stability} for the
classifier transport and \autoref{thm:geomquantization-ledger-exactness} for
the exact public ledger rows.

Finally repack exactly those projected fields as the consumer handoff. The
obligation surface of \autoref{thm:geomquantization-namecert-obligation-surface}
exhausts the public projections, so there is no coordinate from which to read a
host quantization functor, an ambient Hilbert construction, or uncarried
symplectic geometry.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[GeomQuantization public-surface standard presentation]
\label{def:geomquantization-public-surface-standard-presentation}
A standard geometric-quantization presentation is public-surface over a $\GeomQuantizationUp$ source packet $\mathcal{G}$ when its admitted BEDC readback is a tuple
$$
\mathcal{R}=(S,H,L,\Pi,\mu,\lambda,\tau,\rho)
$$
whose entries are, respectively, the $\NameCert_{\SymplecticUp}$ source row, the $\NameCert_{\HilbertUp}$ endpoint row, the prequantum line-bundle row, the polarisation classifier row, the metaplectic-correction ledger row, the finite $\Cont$ readback, the carried $\hsame$ transport family, and the $\Pkg$ provenance row projected from $\mathcal{G}$ in the sense of \autoref{def:geomquantization-bhist-source-packet}.

The readback is conservative when every consumer-visible row factors through those eight projections and their public certificate handoff in \autoref{thm:geomquantization-public-certificate-handoff}. In particular, the presentation may name the ordinary geometric-quantization words attached to $S$, $H$, $L$, $\Pi$, and $\mu$, but it has no separate coordinate for an ambient quantization functor, no separate coordinate for a host Hilbert construction, and no separate coordinate for uncarried symplectic geometry.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[GeomQuantization public-surface standard bridge]
\label{thm:geomquantization-public-surface-standard-bridge}
Every public-surface standard presentation over a $\GeomQuantizationUp$ source packet conservatively bridges to the public $\NameCert_{\GeomQuantizationUp}$ certificate. More explicitly, if a standard geometric-quantization presentation is read only through the $\NameCert_{\SymplecticUp}$ source row, the $\NameCert_{\HilbertUp}$ endpoint row, the prequantum line-bundle row, the polarisation classifier row, the metaplectic-correction ledger row, the finite $\Cont$ readback, the carried $\hsame$ transport family, and the $\Pkg$ provenance row of \autoref{def:geomquantization-public-surface-standard-presentation}, then its BEDC export is exactly the public handoff of \autoref{thm:geomquantization-public-certificate-handoff}. Consequently the bridge cannot expose an ambient quantization functor, a host Hilbert construction, or uncarried symplectic geometry.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Let $\mathcal{R}=(S,H,L,\Pi,\mu,\lambda,\tau,\rho)$ be the admitted readback of a public-surface standard presentation over a source packet $\mathcal{G}$. By the definition of the public-surface presentation, each coordinate of $\mathcal{R}$ is a projection from the same packet named in \autoref{def:geomquantization-bhist-source-packet}.

The source and endpoint rows are not independent standard data. By \autoref{thm:geomquantization-source-dependency-surface}, $S$ and $H$ are rows of the same finite $\BHist$ package, with the same displayed $\Pkg$ provenance row $\rho$. The line-bundle, polarisation, metaplectic, continuation, and transport entries are therefore shared dependency data rather than a second certificate.

The classifier part is stable under the carried history transport. By \autoref{thm:geomquantization-polarisation-classifier-stability}, transporting the polarisation classifier keeps the line-bundle and polarisation rows attached to the transported source and sends the finite $\Cont$ readback to the transported $\NameCert_{\HilbertUp}$ endpoint row. Thus the standard presentation has no additional route from the symplectic row to the Hilbert row beyond the displayed public transport and continuation rows.

It remains to identify the public export. By \autoref{thm:geomquantization-ledger-exactness}, the ledger of $\GeomQuantizationUp$ is exact for precisely the listed rows. By \autoref{thm:geomquantization-namecert-obligation-surface}, those rows are the local $\NameCert_{\GeomQuantizationUp}$ obligation surface. Finally, \autoref{thm:geomquantization-public-certificate-handoff} says that a public consumer may read exactly those eight rows and nothing stronger.

Repacking $\mathcal{R}$ through that handoff gives the public $\NameCert_{\GeomQuantizationUp}$ certificate. Since the public-surface presentation has no coordinate outside $S$, $H$, $L$, $\Pi$, $\mu$, $\lambda$, $\tau$, and $\rho$, there is no BEDC projection from which to read an ambient quantization functor, a host Hilbert construction, or uncarried symplectic geometry. This is exactly the claimed conservative bridge.
\end{proof}
