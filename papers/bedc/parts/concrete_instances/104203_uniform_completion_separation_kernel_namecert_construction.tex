Following Bishop--Bridges constructive completion and Kelley's separated uniform reflection, the $\UniformCompletionSeparationKernelUp$ packet names the finite interface that lets a completion-facing consumer pass from displayed Cauchy data to a separated comparison boundary. It sits between the metric completion surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metriccompletion-namecert}, the uniform completion route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformcompletion-namecert}, and the zero-distance exactness boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separatedmetric-namecert}.

\begin{definition}[UniformCompletionSeparationKernel carrier]
\label{def:uniform-completion-separation-kernel-carrier}
A $\UniformCompletionSeparationKernelUp$ carrier is a tuple
$$
K=(M,U,S,F,N,R,H,C,P,L)
$$
with the following displayed rows.
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the $\MetricCompletionUp$ row read by metric completion consumers before any separated comparison is requested.
\item $U$ is the $\UniformCompletionUp$ row recording the uniform-completion handoff surface over the same displayed Cauchy data.
\item $S$ is the $\SeparatedMetricUp$ zero-distance row used as the exact separated-kernel boundary.
\item $F$ is the $\CauchyFilterUp$ branch, exposing finite filter evidence for completion-facing reads.
\item $N$ is the $\CauchyNetUp$ branch, exposing finite net evidence over the same completion route.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ or $\RealUp$ readback row through which terminal precision claims are sealed.
\item $H$ is componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $M,U,S,F,N,R$.
\item $C$ is the displayed $\Cont$ replay from Cauchy evidence to the completion handoff and separated comparison.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance row over $\MetricCompletionUp$, $\UniformCompletionUp$, $\SeparatedMetricUp$, $\CauchyFilterUp$, $\CauchyNetUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $L$ is the local $\NameCert_{\UniformCompletionSeparationKernelUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[UniformCompletionSeparationKernel NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:uniform-completion-separation-kernel-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted carrier $K=(M,U,S,F,N,R,H,C,P,L)$, the local $\NameCert_{\UniformCompletionSeparationKernelUp}$ obligation surface consists of carrier admission, uniform-completion handoff, separated-kernel exactness, filter and net branch visibility, RegSeqRat or Real seal nonescape, and componentwise transport. Every completion-facing read must pass through the displayed route
$$
M \to U \to S \to F,N \to R \to H,C,P,L .
$$
The packet exports no quotient completion, selected representative, countable choice schedule, host equality, open-cover compactness theorem, or Real completeness row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is read directly from \autoref{def:uniform-completion-separation-kernel-carrier}. The tuple displays the metric-completion source, the uniform-completion handoff, the separated zero-distance boundary, both Cauchy evidence branches, the terminal readback row, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming.

The uniform-completion handoff uses $U$ only after $M$ has been projected. Thus a completion-facing consumer cannot bypass the metric-completion source or insert a separate quotient-completion object before the uniform route is exposed.

Separated-kernel exactness is the role of $S$. Any comparison of two displayed completion reads is forced through the $\SeparatedMetricUp$ zero-distance row, so the packet never supplies host equality or selected representatives.

The filter and net branches are visible as the separate rows $F$ and $N$. They can be inspected as finite BEDC evidence, but neither branch introduces countable choice, an unbounded selector, open-cover compactness, or an ambient completeness theorem.

Finally $R,H,C,P,L$ seal the route componentwise. The RegSeqRat or Real readback is reached only after the displayed completion and separated-boundary rows, while transport, replay, provenance, and local naming preserve the same finite route for every consumer.
\end{proof}
