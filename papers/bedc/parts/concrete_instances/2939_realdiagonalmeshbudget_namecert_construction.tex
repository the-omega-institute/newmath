\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RealDiagonalMeshBudgetUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-realdiagonalmeshbudget-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\RealDiagonalMeshBudgetUp$ interface records the finite mesh-budget route by which a diagonal Real-completion consumer turns a precision request into a bounded family of inspected windows and then into a $\RealUp$ seal read. It refines the selected-window surface of $\RealDiagonalWindowCoverageUp$ by making the mesh bound, dyadic tolerance rows, and packet budget visible before the completion-facing handoff.

\begin{definition}[Real diagonal mesh-budget carrier]
\label{def:real-diagonal-mesh-budget-carrier}
A $\RealDiagonalMeshBudgetUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
M=(q,b,\omega,\rho,\delta,\theta,e,h,c,p,n)
$$
with the following visible coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $q$ is the rational precision request read through the displayed $\RatUp$ and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance rows;
\item $b$ is the finite mesh budget, listing the inspected diagonal indices and the dyadic radii assigned to them;
\item $\omega$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window family selected by $b$;
\item $\rho$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback family attached to those selected windows;
\item $\delta$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger recording the tolerance split, endpoint rows, radius comparisons, and mesh-bound checks;
\item $\theta$ is the diagonal aggregation row stating that the finite windows in $\omega$ cover the requested diagonal packet under the budget $b$;
\item $e$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after $q,b,\omega,\rho,\delta,\theta$ have been displayed;
\item $h$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the precision request, mesh budget, selected windows, readback family, dyadic ledger, aggregation row, seal row, provenance, and local name;
\item $c$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes from $q$ to $b$, from $b$ to $\omega$, from $\omega$ to $\rho$, from $\rho$ to $\delta$, from $\delta$ to $\theta$, and from $\theta$ to $e$;
\item $p$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealDiagonalWindowCoverageUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $n$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RealDiagonalMeshBudgetUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no infinite mesh, no quotient stream, no host real equality, no ambient completeness theorem, no selected limiting representative, and no open-cover compactness row.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Real diagonal mesh-budget NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:real-diagonal-mesh-budget-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\RealDiagonalMeshBudgetUp}$ surface for \autoref{def:real-diagonal-mesh-budget-carrier} consists exactly of carrier admission for $M$, precision exposure through $q$, finite mesh-budget exposure through $b$, selected $\StreamNameUp$ window coverage through $\omega$, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback through $\rho$, dyadic tolerance-ledger exactness through $\delta$, diagonal packet aggregation through $\theta$, $\RealUp$ seal handoff through $e$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $h$, displayed $\Cont$ route replay through $c$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $p$, and local naming through $n$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from the displayed packet $M$. The rows $q,b,\omega,\rho,\delta,\theta,e,h,c,p,n$ list the precision request, finite mesh budget, selected windows, regular rational readbacks, dyadic tolerance ledger, aggregation row, real seal, transports, continuations, provenance, and local name.

The mesh-budget obligations are finite. A consumer reads $q$, then reads the budget $b$, inspects only the window family $\omega$ selected by that budget, and then reads the rational family $\rho$ together with the dyadic ledger $\delta$.

The aggregation row $\theta$ records that the displayed windows cover the requested diagonal packet before the seal row $e$ is exported. The rows $h,c,p,n$ account for transport, route replay, provenance, and local naming, so the local surface is exhausted by the displayed packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real diagonal mesh-budget window coverage]
\label{thm:real-diagonal-mesh-budget-window-coverage}
Every diagonal observation exported from an accepted $\RealDiagonalMeshBudgetUp$ carrier factors through the finite mesh budget $b$, the selected $\StreamNameUp$ window family $\omega$, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback family $\rho$, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger $\delta$, and the aggregation row $\theta$.

No exported observation may range over an infinite mesh, inspect an unlisted stream window, use quotient equality of completed names, or appeal to ambient Real completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier rows $b,\omega,\rho,\delta,\theta$. The budget $b$ lists the inspected diagonal indices and radii, while $\omega$ and $\rho$ provide the selected windows and rational readbacks.

Apply the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:real-diagonal-mesh-budget-namecert-obligations}. They route every observation through the precision request, budget, selected-window family, readback family, dyadic ledger, and aggregation row before any seal-facing read is available.

Since the carrier has no coordinate for an infinite mesh, unlisted stream window, quotient equality, or ambient completeness, a downstream diagonal observation can only use the displayed finite coverage route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real diagonal mesh-budget RealUp handoff]
\label{thm:real-diagonal-mesh-budget-realup-handoff}
Every $\RealUp$ completion or continuous-map consumer of an accepted $\RealDiagonalMeshBudgetUp$ carrier receives the seal row $e$ only through the displayed route
$$
q,b,\omega,\rho,\delta,\theta,h,c,p,n.
$$
Thus the handoff consumes a rational precision request, a finite mesh budget, selected $\StreamNameUp$ windows, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readbacks, a $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, a diagonal aggregation row, componentwise transport, route replay, package provenance, and local naming before the $\RealUp$ seal is read.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the seal coordinate $e$. It is the only $\RealUp$ row in the carrier and is listed after the precision, mesh-budget, window, readback, dyadic-ledger, and aggregation rows.

Use \autoref{thm:real-diagonal-mesh-budget-window-coverage}. It shows that the diagonal packet presented to a real-facing consumer has already been confined to the finite windows and dyadic tolerances selected by $b$.

The rows $h$ and $c$ replay the displayed transports and continuations, while $p$ and $n$ provide provenance and local naming. Therefore the seal is reached exactly through the displayed route and not through a hidden completeness or quotient-equality coordinate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real diagonal mesh-budget non-escape]
\label{thm:real-diagonal-mesh-budget-nonescape}
Every downstream consumer of an accepted $\RealDiagonalMeshBudgetUp$ packet factors through the finite rows
$$
q,b,\omega,\rho,\delta,\theta,e,h,c,p,n.
$$
The consumer may read the rational precision request, finite mesh budget, selected $\StreamNameUp$ windows, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readbacks, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger, diagonal aggregation row, $\RealUp$ seal, $\hsame$ transports, $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ row, and it cannot read infinite-mesh data, quotient streams, host real equality, ambient completeness, selected limits, or open-cover compactness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier and the local NameCert surface. The rows $q,b,\omega,\rho,\delta,\theta,e,h,c,p,n$ are the complete displayed packet.

Use the window-coverage theorem for observation-facing consumers. It confines every diagonal read to the finite mesh budget, selected windows, regular rational readbacks, dyadic tolerance ledger, and aggregation row.

Use the RealUp handoff theorem for seal-facing consumers. It routes every completion or continuous-map read through $q,b,\omega,\rho,\delta,\theta,h,c,p,n$ before $e$ is consumed.

No remaining carrier coordinate exists beyond those rows. Hence no downstream consumer can extract infinite-mesh data, quotient streams, host real equality, ambient completeness, selected limits, or open-cover compactness from the packet.
\end{proof}
