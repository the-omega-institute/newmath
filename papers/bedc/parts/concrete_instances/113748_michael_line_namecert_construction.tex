\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\MichaelLineUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-michael-line-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Michael and Kelley topology, the $\MichaelLineUp$ packet records the classical Michael-line presentation as a finite topology-adjacent NameCert object. It reads the real-order topology rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-order-topology-namecert}, the indexed open-row discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-topology-namecert}, and the observable-open truth classifier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-sierpinski-space-namecert}. The packet names only the displayed real-order, distinguished-row, isolated-point, and observable-open handoff data needed by topology consumers; it does not import a host powerset, a choice-selected irrational subset, a quotient point equality, or an ambient completeness theorem for the real line.

\begin{definition}[Michael line carrier]
\label{def:michael-line-carrier}
A $\MichaelLineUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
M=(R,T,D,O,S,L,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $R$ is the $\RealOrderTopologyUp$ source row, carrying the named real-order intervals and endpoint comparisons available from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-order-topology-namecert}.
\item $T$ is the $\TopologyUp$ source row, exposing indexed open rows, finite neighbourhood membership reads, and topology closure witnesses from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-topology-namecert}.
\item $D$ is the distinguished-row ledger marking the points whose singleton neighbourhood row is admitted by the Michael-line presentation.
\item $O$ is the ordinary real-order open row used for points outside the distinguished ledger.
\item $S$ is the $\SierpinskiSpaceUp$ observable-open truth row, recording whether the currently inspected named point is read through the ordinary open row or through the isolated singleton row.
\item $L$ is the topology handoff row that exports the ordinary and isolated neighbourhood reads back to the indexed open surface of $T$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the real-order source, topology source, distinguished ledger, ordinary open row, observable truth row, handoff row, continuation row, provenance row, and local naming row.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from point inspection to distinguished-row lookup, from lookup to observable-open truth, and from observable truth to the topology handoff.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\MichaelLineUp$, $\RealOrderTopologyUp$, $\TopologyUp$, $\SierpinskiSpaceUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MichaelLineUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The local classifier compares accepted packets only through $R,T,D,O,S,L,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for an ambient powerset of reals, a selected subset outside the distinguished ledger, quotient equality of points, open-cover compactness, real completeness, or a standard bridge.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Michael line NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:michael-line-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\MichaelLineUp$ carrier $M=(R,T,D,O,S,L,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\MichaelLineUp}$ surface has five obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier exposure for the displayed tuple $R,T,D,O,S,L,H,C,P,N$;
\item real-order source discipline, requiring ordinary neighbourhood reads to be supplied by $R$ and its interval rows;
\item distinguished-row discipline, requiring isolated singleton reads to pass through the finite ledger $D$;
\item observable-open truth, requiring the choice between ordinary and isolated reads to be witnessed by the $\SierpinskiSpaceUp$ row $S$;
\item topology handoff and non-escape, requiring all exported open rows to pass through $L,H,C,P,N$ without host powersets, selected subsets, quotient point equality, open-cover compactness, real completeness, or a standard bridge.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier exposure is projection from \autoref{def:michael-line-carrier}. The real-order source $R$ and topology source $T$ are the only ambient sibling rows available to the packet.

For an ordinary point read, the open neighbourhood is supplied by the real-order row $R$ and then routed through the topology handoff $L$. For an isolated read, the distinguished ledger $D$ must first display the singleton neighbourhood row.

The $\SierpinskiSpaceUp$ row $S$ records the observable-open truth value separating those two routes. By \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-sierpinski-space-namecert}, this is a finite classifier read rather than a host-level predicate over all subsets of reals.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same handoff. Since the carrier has no coordinate for host powersets, selected subsets, quotient point equality, open-cover compactness, real completeness, or bridge data, the five obligations exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Michael line Sierpinski topology handoff]
\label{thm:michael-line-sierpinski-topology-handoff}
Every accepted $\MichaelLineUp$ topology read reaches the public $\TopologyUp$ surface only after the $\SierpinskiSpaceUp$ observable-open row has selected one of the displayed real-order or isolated-neighbourhood routes. For
$$
M=(R,T,D,O,S,L,H,C,P,N),
$$
the exported open row is computed through
$$
R,D,O,S,H,C,P,N \longrightarrow L \longrightarrow T .
$$
No host powerset, choice-selected distinguished subset, quotient point equality, open-cover compactness, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, or standard bridge supplies the handoff.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier rows from \autoref{def:michael-line-carrier}. The only routes to a neighbourhood read are the ordinary real-order row $O$ sourced by $R$ and the isolated singleton route sourced by the distinguished ledger $D$.

The observable-open row $S$ is read before the topology handoff. It records which finite route is visible for the inspected point and gives the handoff a displayed $\SierpinskiSpaceUp$ truth row instead of an ambient subset predicate.

The row $L$ then exports the selected route to the indexed open surface $T$ of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-topology-namecert}. Componentwise transport through $H$ and continuation replay through $C$ preserve the same ordinary-or-isolated decision, while $P$ and $N$ record provenance and local naming.

Thus the handoff is a finite BEDC route through the sibling certificates \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-order-topology-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-sierpinski-space-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-topology-namecert}. The excluded host objects have no carrier coordinate, so they cannot supply topology data to $\MichaelLineUp$.
\end{proof}
