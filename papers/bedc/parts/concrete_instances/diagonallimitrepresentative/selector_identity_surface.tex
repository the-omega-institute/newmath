\begin{definition}[Diagonal-limit representative selector identity surface]
\label{def:diagonal-limit-representative-selector-identity-surface}
For an accepted $\DiagonalLimitRepresentativeUp$ packet
$$
(X,\mu,n,k,i,w,r,d,e,H,C,P,N),
$$
the selector identity surface is the displayed finite row
$$
\Sigma_i=(X,\mu,n,k,i,w,r,d,e,H,C,P,N)
$$
subject to the equations $k=\mu(1/(n+1))$ and $i=k+n+1$, where the same selected index $i$ is read through the cofinal modulus route, the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window $w$, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback $r$, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger $d$, and the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row $e$. The support rows $H,C,P,N$ are only the displayed $\BHist$, componentwise $\hsame$, $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert_{\DiagonalLimitRepresentativeUp}$ rows for that same identity surface.

The surface is the selector row exposed by the root-index readiness package, the root-window obligation surface, and the terminal-modulus coherence surface. It is not a quotient stream, a host Real limit, a selected completion point, a countable-choice row, an ambient $\RealUp$ completeness field, or a second terminal selector.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Diagonal-limit representative selector identity totality]
\label{thm:diagonal-limit-representative-selector-identity-totality}
Every accepted $\DiagonalLimitRepresentativeUp$ carrier exposes exactly one selector identity surface of \autoref{def:diagonal-limit-representative-selector-identity-surface}. The exposed surface is total on the selected cofinal index and simultaneously determines the finite window, regular readback, dyadic ledger, and terminal Real seal route
$$
i \rightsquigarrow w \rightsquigarrow r \rightsquigarrow d \rightsquigarrow e
$$
inside the recorded $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert_{\DiagonalLimitRepresentativeUp}$ support.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the accepted carrier of \autoref{def:diagonal-limit-representative-carrier}. It records $X,\mu,n,k,i,w,r,d,e,H,C,P,N$ and the two selector equations $k=\mu(1/(n+1))$ and $i=k+n+1$ as finite carrier data, so existence of the displayed row is obtained by projection rather than by choosing a tail outside the packet.

Root-index coverage and the phase-root window totality theorem, \autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-representative-root-index-coverage} and \autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-representative-phase-root-window-totality}, attach the selected index to the same finite StreamName, RegSeqRat, and DyadicRatCore route. Thus the selector identity row is total before the terminal seal is read.

For uniqueness, apply selected-window determinacy through the modulus-window-readback package and terminal-route uniqueness, \autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-representative-modulus-window-readback-package} and \autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-representative-terminal-route-uniqueness}. Once the carrier and support rows are fixed, a second selected index, window, readback, ledger, or seal would have to be a second terminal route, which those results exclude.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Diagonal-limit representative selector identity transport]
\label{thm:diagonal-limit-representative-selector-identity-transport}
Let two accepted $\DiagonalLimitRepresentativeUp$ selector identity surfaces be classified componentwise by the recorded $\hsame$ rows and by displayed $\Cont$ replay on their carrier, cofinal, window, readback, ledger, seal, provenance, and local naming rows. Then transport preserves the same selector identity through the window/readback/ledger/seal route. In particular, the transported surface has the same selected cofinal index route and the same ordered public path through
$$
\StreamNameUp,\quad \RegSeqRatUp,\quad \DyadicRatCoreUp,\quad \CofinalModulusSealUp,\quad \RealUp.
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with classifier transport. The classifier of \autoref{def:diagonal-limit-representative-classifier} and the $\hsame$--$\Cont$ scope theorem \autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-representative-hsame-cont-scope} allow transport only through the recorded component rows and displayed replay of the same finite packet.

Next use cofinal selector transport, \autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-representative-cofinal-selector-transport}. It keeps the selected equations, selected window, readback, dyadic ledger, cofinal modulus rows, and terminal seal inside the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ containers.

Finally apply finite-tail selector uniqueness, \autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-representative-finite-tail-selector-uniqueness}. Since the finite tail admits only one displayed selector route, componentwise transport cannot exchange the window/readback/ledger/seal path for another index, another tail, or another terminal Real seal.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Diagonal-limit representative selector identity Real-seal exhaustion]
\label{thm:diagonal-limit-representative-selector-identity-real-seal-exhaustion}
Every $\RealUp$ seal consumer of an accepted $\DiagonalLimitRepresentativeUp$ carrier factors through the selector identity surface of \autoref{def:diagonal-limit-representative-selector-identity-surface}. Such a consumer may read the terminal seal only after the selected cofinal index, finite window, RegSeqRat readback, and DyadicRatCore ledger have appeared on the displayed surface; it cannot replace that surface by a hidden selector, quotient stream, host limit, countable-choice row, selected completion point, or ambient completeness coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the positive factorization first. The Real-seal factorization theorem and Real-seal handoff theorem, \autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-representative-real-seal-factorization} and \autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-representative-real-seal-handoff}, place every terminal Real read after the selected window, readback, dyadic ledger, and local support rows.

The selector identity totality theorem identifies those rows with the same selected index $i$ and the same cofinal route. Therefore a seal consumer reaches $e$ only through $\Sigma_i$, with no separate entry point for the Real endpoint.

The boundary is supplied by the nonchoice results, especially \autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-representative-nonchoice-boundary}, \autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-representative-phase-root-nonchoice-boundary}, and \autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-representative-real-seal-nonchoice-export}. These results remove hidden selectors, quotient streams, host limits, selected completion points, choice rows, and ambient completeness fields from the carrier surface, so the selector identity surface exhausts every Real-seal consumer route.
\end{proof}
