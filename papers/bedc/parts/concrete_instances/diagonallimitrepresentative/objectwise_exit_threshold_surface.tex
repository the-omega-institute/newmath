\begin{theorem}[Diagonal-limit representative face threshold readiness]
\label{thm:diagonal-limit-representative-face-threshold-readiness}
Every accepted completion-selector ledger for $\DiagonalLimitRepresentativeUp$ exposes a finite objectwise threshold package whose faces are exactly
$$
\DyadicRatCoreUp,\qquad \StreamNameUp,\qquad \RegSeqRatUp,\qquad \RealUp.
$$
The $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ face records the dyadic tolerance ledger, the $\StreamNameUp$ face records the finite observation window, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ face records the regular-rational readback, and the $\RealUp$ face records the terminal seal. These four faces are read from the ledger only through its displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert_{\DiagonalLimitRepresentativeUp}$ support.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply four-object source exhaustion and the Phase Real and Completion exit certificate, \autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-representative-four-object-source-exhaustion} and \autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-representative-phase-real-completion-exit-certificate}. They identify the same four rows as the complete exit packet for Phase Real and Completion consumers.

Next apply phase-source projection totality, \autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-representative-phase-source-projection-totality}. It sends every accepted ledger to the dyadic ledger, finite window, regular-rational readback, and terminal seal, while the local support rows serve only as transport, replay, sourcing, and naming data.

Thus the threshold package is objectwise: it exposes the four named faces and no extra route coordinate beyond the displayed finite ledger support.
\end{proof}

\label{thm:diagonal-limit-representative-objectwise-audit-threshold-handoff}

In particular, an audit read may cite the four faces as a finite route surface, but it may not replace the supporting rows by detached quotient, host-limit, selector, choice, completeness, trichotomy, compactness, or bridge data.

\begin{proof}

It confines every Real-and-Completion consumer read to the finite frontier through $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, and $\RealUp$ together with the displayed local support.

Combining these two reads gives the audit handoff. The consumer sees the four face records as a finite source surface, and every permitted audit step remains anchored in $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ support.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Diagonal-limit representative exit threshold consumer boundary]
\label{thm:diagonal-limit-representative-exit-threshold-consumer-boundary}
The objectwise threshold package for $\DiagonalLimitRepresentativeUp$ supplies no route-level formalstatus, no quotient stream, no selected host limit, no countable choice row, no ambient $\RealUp$ completeness principle, no trichotomy principle, no open-cover compactness principle, and no standard bridge row. \end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply the Phase Real and Completion exit certificate, \autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-representative-phase-real-completion-exit-certificate}. It excludes quotient equality, choice, ambient completeness, trichotomy, and open-cover compactness from the finite exit packet.

Next apply phase-source projection totality and completion-frontier readback coverage, \autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-representative-phase-source-projection-totality} and \autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-representative-completion-frontier-readback-coverage}. \end{proof}
