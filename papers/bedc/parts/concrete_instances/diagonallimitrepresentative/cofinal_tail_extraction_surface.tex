\begin{definition}[Diagonal-limit representative cofinal-tail extraction]
\label{def:diagonal-limit-representative-cofinal-tail-extraction}
For an accepted shared-terminal $\DiagonalLimitRepresentativeUp$ carrier, the cofinal-tail extraction surface is the finite packet
$$
T=(\mu,n,k,i,w,r,d,e,Q,B,W,D_F,R,S,E_F,H,C,P,N).
$$
The first four rows record the root-index equations $k=\mu(1/(n+1))$ and $i=k+n+1$ from the root-readiness and cofinal-index readiness surfaces. The rows $w,r,d,e$ are respectively the selected $\StreamNameUp$ window, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger, and terminal $\RealUp$ seal. The rows $Q,B,W,D_F,R,S,E_F$ are the finite-prefix budget rows inherited from $\FinitePrefixLimitBudgetUp$, with the cofinal modulus source supplied by $\CofinalModulusSealUp$ through the cofinal-terminal budget bridge. The rows $H,C,P,N$ are the componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert_{\DiagonalLimitRepresentativeUp}$ row for the same extraction.

This packet is sourced by $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\FinitePrefixLimitBudgetUp$, $\CofinalModulusSealUp$, and $\RealUp$ through \autoref{def:diagonal-limit-representative-carrier}, \autoref{def:diagonal-limit-representative-classifier}, \autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-representative-root-readiness-exactness}, \autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-representative-cofinal-index-terminal-readiness}, and \autoref{def:diagonal-limit-representative-cofinal-terminal-budget-bridge}. It records only finite cofinal-tail data; it exports no quotient stream equality, host limit, selected limit, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness field.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Diagonal-limit representative cofinal-tail coverage]
\label{thm:diagonal-limit-representative-cofinal-tail-coverage}
Every Real-and-Completion consumer of an accepted $\DiagonalLimitRepresentativeUp$ terminal route factors through the cofinal-tail extraction packet of \autoref{def:diagonal-limit-representative-cofinal-tail-extraction}. In particular, the consumer must read the selected root-index rows $\mu,n,k,i$, the selected window $w$, readback $r$, dyadic ledger $d$, terminal seal $e$, finite-prefix budget rows $Q,B,W,D_F,R,S,E_F$, and support rows $H,C,P,N$ as one finite $\BHist$ surface before it can consume the terminal $\RealUp$ seal.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier and classifier of \autoref{def:diagonal-limit-representative-carrier} and \autoref{def:diagonal-limit-representative-classifier}. They admit only the selected representative rows and their componentwise support rows, so every downstream read begins on a finite $\BHist$ surface rather than on a host stream or completed Real object.

Next use the root-readiness exactness theorem \autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-representative-root-readiness-exactness} and the cofinal-index readiness package \autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-representative-cofinal-index-terminal-readiness}. These results place $\mu,n,k,i,w,r,d,e$ on the same route as the finite-prefix and cofinal modulus rows, with the equations for $k$ and $i$ visible before terminal consumption.

Finally attach the cofinal-terminal budget bridge \autoref{def:diagonal-limit-representative-cofinal-terminal-budget-bridge} and the terminal-pullback obligation surface \autoref{def:diagonal-limit-representative-terminal-pullback-obligation-surface}. The bridge supplies the finite-prefix and cofinal-tail budget rows, while the pullback surface forces terminal consumption back through the displayed StreamName, RegSeqRat, DyadicRatCore, and Real sources. Hence every Real-and-Completion consumer factors through the packet $T$ and no off-packet quotient, host limit, selected limit, choice row, or completeness principle is available.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Diagonal-limit representative cofinal-tail classifier stability]
\label{thm:diagonal-limit-representative-cofinal-tail-classifier-stability}
Let two accepted cofinal-tail extraction packets for $\DiagonalLimitRepresentativeUp$ be classified together by the displayed componentwise $\hsame$ rows and $\Cont$ replay. Then the selected rows $\mu,n,k,i,w,r,d,e$, the finite-prefix budget rows $Q,B,W,D_F,R,S,E_F$, and the support rows $H,C,P,N$ are preserved componentwise. The preservation uses only the packet of \autoref{def:diagonal-limit-representative-cofinal-tail-extraction}; it does not add a quotient stream equality, host $\RealUp$ equality, selected limit, hidden tail schedule, countable choice row, or ambient completeness witness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the classifier of \autoref{def:diagonal-limit-representative-classifier}. It compares accepted carriers componentwise, and the only transport rows available to the extraction packet are the recorded $\hsame$ rows together with displayed $\Cont$ replay.

The cofinal-index readiness package fixes the rows that must be preserved. It attaches the root-index equations, the selected window and readback, the dyadic ledger, the finite-prefix budget rows, and the terminal seal to the same cofinal source route.

It remains to check that no stronger transport is introduced by the extraction. The cofinal-terminal budget bridge and the terminal-pullback obligation surface preserve the same displayed terminal rows, while the formal handoff boundary \autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-representative-formal-handoff-boundary} permits later work to encode only the visible $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, $\NameCert$, and sibling rows. Therefore classifier stability is exactly componentwise stability of the finite packet $T$.
\end{proof}
