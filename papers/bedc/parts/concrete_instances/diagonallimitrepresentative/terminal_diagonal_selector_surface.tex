\label{thm:diagonal-limit-representative-terminal-diagonal-coverage}

$$
(X,\mu,n,k,i,w,r,d,e)
$$

The exposed row is exactly the row routed by the root-index coverage theorem, cofinal-tail coverage theorem, finite selector exhaustion theorem, terminal-pullback source coverage theorem, and Real-completion consumer exactness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First read the root-index coverage theorem \autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-representative-root-index-coverage}. It makes the displayed equations for $k$ and $i$ visible before any terminal consumer reads the selected window, readback, dyadic ledger, or Real seal.

These two surfaces place the cofinal modulus seal, finite-prefix budget rows, selected finite prefix window, and fixed selector rows on the same finite $\BHist$ route.

Finally use terminal-pullback source coverage and Real-completion consumer exactness, \autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-representative-terminal-pullback-source-coverage} and \autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-representative-real-completion-consumer-exactness}. \begin{theorem}[Diagonal-limit representative terminal-diagonal transport]
\label{thm:diagonal-limit-representative-terminal-diagonal-transport}
Let $\Delta$ and $\Delta'$ be accepted terminal-diagonal selector packets for $\DiagonalLimitRepresentativeUp$ whose corresponding rows are related by the componentwise $\hsame$ rows and displayed $\Cont$ replay of \autoref{def:diagonal-limit-representative-classifier}. Then the selected terminal diagonal route
$$
(M,Q,X,\mu,n,k,i,w,r,d,e,B,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
is preserved componentwise. In particular, transport cannot replace the selected terminal row by a second cofinal index row, a hidden diagonal selector, quotient stream equality, host $\RealUp$ equality, countable choice, or ambient completeness data.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from terminal-diagonal coverage. By \autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-representative-terminal-diagonal-coverage}, each accepted selector packet has concrete rows for the cofinal modulus seal, finite-prefix budget, selected index equations, terminal representative row, and support data.

Now apply the classifier stability already available on the cofinal-tail and terminal-pullback surfaces. The cofinal-tail classifier stability theorem preserves the selected cofinal rows and budget rows, while the terminal-pullback classifier stability theorem preserves the StreamName, RegSeqRat, DyadicRatCore, and Real rows through the displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ data.

The support rows close the transport claim. Since $H,C,P,N$ only record componentwise history, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming for the same finite packet, transport has no coordinate in which to insert an alternate terminal row or a stronger equality principle. Thus the selected terminal diagonal route is preserved exactly componentwise.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}

\end{proof}
