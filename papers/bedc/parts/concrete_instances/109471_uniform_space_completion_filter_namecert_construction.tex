\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\UniformSpaceCompletionFilterUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-uniform-space-completion-filter-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bourbaki and Kelley, the $\UniformSpaceCompletionFilterUp$ packet records the completion route in which a displayed Cauchy filter is read through a uniform-space entourage basis before any quotient completion or selected limit is available. It sits after the uniform-space surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformspace-namecert}, the filter-basis generator of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-filter-basis-namecert}, the saturated Cauchy-filter packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyfilter-namecert}, and the completion boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completion-namecert}. Its finite readback route uses the stream windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}.

\begin{definition}[Uniform-space completion-filter carrier]
\label{def:uniform-space-completion-filter-carrier}
A $\UniformSpaceCompletionFilterUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(U,B,F,S,R,D,A,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $U$ is the $\UniformSpaceUp$ entourage row, including the finite diagonal, refinement, symmetry, and composition data visible to the packet.
\item $B$ is the $\CauchyFilterBasisUp$ generator row, listing the basis elements and common-refinement witnesses read from $U$.
\item $F$ is the $\CauchyFilterUp$ saturated filter row generated from $B$.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window schedule controlling which filter and entourage reads may be inspected.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row attached to the displayed windows and tolerance requests.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger for entourage shrinkage, Cauchy refinement, and completion-radius comparison.
\item $A$ is the $\RealUp$ completion-admission seal reached only after $U,B,F,S,R,D$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for all displayed rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a completion request through the uniform, basis, filter, window, readback, tolerance, and Real-seal rows.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\UniformSpaceCompletionFilterUp$, $\UniformSpaceUp$, $\CauchyFilterBasisUp$, $\CauchyFilterUp$, $\CompletionUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\UniformSpaceCompletionFilterUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $U,B,F,S,R,D,A,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for quotient filters, host equality of filters, selected representatives, countable choice, a chosen completion point, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Uniform-space completion-filter NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:uniform-space-completion-filter-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\UniformSpaceCompletionFilterUp$ carrier $Q=(U,B,F,S,R,D,A,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\UniformSpaceCompletionFilterUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the displayed uniform-space entourage row, Cauchy-filter basis row, saturated Cauchy-filter row, stream-window row, regular-rational readback row, dyadic tolerance ledger, Real completion-admission seal, componentwise transport, continuation replay, package provenance, and local naming row. Every completion-facing read of $Q$ must pass through
$$
\UniformSpaceUp \longmapsto \CauchyFilterBasisUp \longmapsto \CauchyFilterUp \longmapsto \StreamNameUp \longmapsto \DyadicRatCoreUp \longmapsto \RegSeqRatUp \longmapsto \RealUp
$$
before the completion boundary of $\CompletionUp$ may consume the packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:uniform-space-completion-filter-carrier}. The coordinates list exactly the uniform, basis, filter, window, readback, tolerance, Real-seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

The completion-facing order is forced by $C$. A consumer first reads the entourage data $U$, then the basis row $B$ and saturated filter row $F$, and only then inspects the finite windows $S$, dyadic ledger $D$, regular rational readback $R$, and Real seal $A$.

The structural rows do not add a second source. The transport row $H$ moves only the displayed coordinates componentwise, while $P$ and $N$ name the same finite packet as provenance and local certificate data.

Since no coordinate of $Q$ names quotient filters, host filter equality, selected representatives, countable choice, a chosen completion point, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, those objects cannot be exported by the local NameCert surface. The listed rows therefore exhaust the obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Uniform-space completion-filter boundary]
\label{thm:uniform-space-completion-filter-boundary}
Let $Q=(U,B,F,S,R,D,A,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\UniformSpaceCompletionFilterUp$ packet. Any $\CompletionUp$ consumer of $Q$ receives a finite filter-completion route through the displayed rows $U,B,F,S,R,D,A,H,C,P,N$ and receives no quotient-filter class, selected completion point, host filter equality, countable-choice selector, hidden cofinal subfilter, or ambient completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the NameCert obligation theorem, \autoref{thm:uniform-space-completion-filter-namecert-obligations}. It routes completion-facing reads through the uniform row, Cauchy-filter basis, Cauchy filter, finite windows, dyadic tolerance ledger, regular readback, and Real seal before the completion boundary consumes the packet.

The replay and transport rows preserve the same route. They do not add representatives, quotient classes, hidden cofinal filters, or selected limits; they only replay and transport the displayed coordinates.

Finally inspect the carrier definition. The excluded objects are absent from $Q$, so a $\CompletionUp$ consumer cannot extract them from the packet.
\end{proof}
