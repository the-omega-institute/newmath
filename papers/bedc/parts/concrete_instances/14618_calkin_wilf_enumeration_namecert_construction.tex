\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CalkinWilfEnumerationUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-calkin-wilf-enumeration-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Calkin and Wilf, the $\CalkinWilfEnumerationUp$ packet records the
positive-rational enumeration surface generated by the binary tree rule
$a/b \mapsto a/(a+b)$ and $a/b \mapsto (a+b)/b$ as finite BEDC naming data.
Its dependency lattice starts from the unary source of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-nat-namecert}, the rational carrier of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rat-namecert}, the rational-stream interface of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rationalstream-namecert}, the finite schedule
surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular
rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and
the terminal $\RealUp$ seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}.
It is the breadth-first positive-rational sibling of the continued-fraction
route in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuedfraction-namecert} and the
Stern--Brocot approximation route in
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-stern-brocot-approximation-namecert}. The
packet is not quotient rational equality, not an ambient host enumeration, not
an arbitrary choice of representatives, and not $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[Calkin-Wilf enumeration carrier]
\label{def:calkin-wilf-enumeration-carrier}
A $\CalkinWilfEnumerationUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
W=(U,Q,T,B,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $U$ is the unary $\NatUp$ address row naming the finite breadth-first
tree position.
\item $Q$ is the $\RatUp$ numerator-denominator row exposed at that address,
with both coordinates positive unary histories.
\item $T$ is the local tree-step row recording whether the next finite edge is
left, $a/b \mapsto a/(a+b)$, or right, $a/b \mapsto (a+b)/b$.
\item $B$ is the breadth-first schedule row ordering finite addresses before
they are read as a stream.
\item $S$ is the $\RationalStreamUp$ output row whose $U$-indexed finite
window is filled by the displayed $Q,T,B$ data.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row used when the enumeration is
consumed as a rational source for Cauchy-style real-name packets.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ handoff row, exposed only as a downstream consumer
of the rational stream and regular readback rows.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $U,Q,T,B,S,R,E$.
\item $C$ stores displayed $\Cont$ replay for opening an address, computing its
tree edge, filling the stream window, and passing the selected rational row to
regular-rational and real-name consumers.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\NatUp$, $\RatUp$,
$\RationalStreamUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$,
$\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CalkinWilfEnumerationUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for quotient rational equality, host-side
enumeration of all rationals, choice-selected representatives, signed-rational
coverage, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Calkin-Wilf enumeration NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:calkin-wilf-enumeration-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\CalkinWilfEnumerationUp}$ surface for
\autoref{def:calkin-wilf-enumeration-carrier} consists of five finite
obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed packet
$W=(U,Q,T,B,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)$;
\item tree-step exactness, requiring every exposed child rational to be
obtained from its parent by the displayed left or right Calkin-Wilf rule;
\item breadth-first stream discipline, requiring stream windows to read the
finite address order in $B$ before exposing the corresponding $Q$ row;
\item regular-rational handoff, requiring every $\RegSeqRatUp$ and $\RealUp$
consumer to pass through $S$ and $R$ before the real-name seal $E$ is public;
\item non-escape, stating that quotient rational equality, ambient host
enumeration, arbitrary representative choice, signed-rational coverage, and
$\RealUp$ completeness are absent from the packet.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from the finite tuple
$W=(U,Q,T,B,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)$. Each coordinate is a displayed BEDC row, and the
local naming row $N$ records that exactly these coordinates form the public
$\CalkinWilfEnumerationUp$ surface.

Tree-step exactness is local to $T$. A left edge replaces $a/b$ by
$a/(a+b)$, while a right edge replaces $a/b$ by $(a+b)/b$; both operations
stay inside positive unary numerator-denominator data and therefore feed the
same $\RatUp$ row format.

Breadth-first stream discipline is supplied by $B$ and $S$. The schedule row
orders finite addresses before they are exposed as rational-stream windows, so
the stream cannot be read as a host enumeration detached from the displayed
tree.

Finally, $R$ and $E$ give the downstream handoff. Componentwise transport,
continuation replay, provenance, and local naming are exactly $H,C,P,N$, so no
additional quotient equality, representative selector, signed-rational cover,
or ambient completeness coordinate remains available.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Calkin-Wilf positive-rational coverage]
\label{thm:calkin-wilf-enumeration-positive-rational-coverage}
For every accepted $\CalkinWilfEnumerationUp$ carrier, the public stream row
$S$ covers the positive $\RatUp$ rows generated from $1/1$ by finite
Calkin-Wilf left and right edges, and every public coverage read factors
through the displayed address, tree-step, breadth-first schedule, transport,
continuation, provenance, and local naming rows. The coverage claim is only
for the generated positive numerator-denominator surface; it does not identify
quotient-equal rational presentations, choose representatives in a host
enumeration, cover signed rationals, or assert completeness of $\RealUp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier and start from the root rational $1/1$ in the
$Q$ row. A finite address in $U$ is read by repeated use of the displayed
tree-step row $T$, so every addressed output remains a positive rational
presentation.

The breadth-first row $B$ then orders these addressed presentations before
they enter the rational-stream row $S$. Thus a coverage read of $S$ is a read
of finite Calkin-Wilf address data, not a read of an external list of host
rationals.

The statement is intentionally presentation-level. Since the packet has no
quotient rational equality coordinate and no selector for preferred host
representatives, it covers exactly the positive generated surface available
through $U,Q,T,B,S,H,C,P,N$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Calkin-Wilf Real completion handoff]
\label{thm:calkin-wilf-enumeration-real-completion-handoff}
Every $\RealUp$-facing consumer of an accepted
$\CalkinWilfEnumerationUp$ carrier factors through the displayed
$\RationalStreamUp$ row $S$ and the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row $R$ before the
handoff row $E$ can be exposed. Consequently the enumeration can feed L10
rational streams and regular Cauchy real-name readback without exporting
quotient rational equality, an ambient host enumeration, arbitrary choice of
representatives, or $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Let a real-facing consumer request data from an accepted carrier. The only
stream coordinate in the packet is $S$, so the consumer first sees a finite
window of positive rational presentations generated by the Calkin-Wilf tree
and ordered by $B$.

The next completion-facing coordinate is $R$. It is the regular-rational
readback row attached to the same stream window, and it is the only row that
connects the enumeration surface to the regular Cauchy route.

Only after $S$ and $R$ have been consumed can $E$ expose a real-name handoff.
The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ replay and name this same finite path, so the
consumer receives a rational-stream source for L10 completion work without
obtaining quotient equality, host enumeration data, arbitrary representatives,
or a completeness principle.
\end{proof}
