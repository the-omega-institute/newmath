\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for \(\ListUp\)}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-list-namecert}

The list interface \(\ListUp\) is licensed over a source carrier endowed with a \(\NameCert\). The carrier is the inductive sequence built from a nil constructor and a cons constructor consuming one source witness and one shorter list. The classifier is structural sameness lifted from the source classifier: nil-with-nil and cons-with-cons under componentwise sameness. It is not a primitive sequence type; it is licensed only as a derived inductive over the source.

\section{Roadmap template status}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Traditional target}: finite lists over a source object.
  \item \textbf{BEDC source}: one licensed source carrier $A$ with its name certificate.
  \item \textbf{Carrier choice}: the carrier is generated by $\mathsf{nil}$ and $\mathsf{cons}(a,xs)$.
  \item \textbf{Classifier choice}: the public semantic classifier is $\mathsf{ListHistoryClassifier}_{S,\sim_S}$ on BEDC histories, relating nil endpoints to nil endpoints and cons endpoints to cons endpoints by source head classification, recursive tail classification, and continuation witnesses; the finite-spine helper $\mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}$ remains the checked structural model.
  \item \textbf{Stability obligations}: structural symmetry from the source $\NameCert$, structural transitivity, list-carrier transport, nil/cons separation, map stability, append stability, reverse stability, and cancellation facts.
  \item \textbf{Ledger}: list length and constructor shape remain visible; payload classification is delegated to the source certificate.
  \item \textbf{Core theorem list}: history carrier case analysis, history classifier constructor exactness, classifier transitivity, classifier symmetry from the source $\NameCert$, cons inversion, singleton equivalence, map stability, append cancellation, reverse stability, and length preservation for arbitrary source classifiers.
  \item \textbf{Exactness predicate}: classification holds exactly when both lists have the same constructor shape and all corresponding entries classify through the source.
  \item \textbf{Standard bridge}: the generated carrier reads back as the ordinary finite list syntax for $A$.
  \item \textbf{Status}: $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$ for the full semantic certificate, with finite-spine classifier components checked in Lean.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Carrier definition}

\begin{definition}[List carrier]
\label{def:list-carrier}
For a licensed source carrier \(A\), define the inductive list carrier by \(\mathsf{nil}\) and \(\mathsf{cons}(a,\ell)\).
\end{definition}

The checked $\ListUp$ lemmas below operate on a finite-spine classifier model, while the $\NameCert$ carrier remains the generated nil/cons BEDC carrier described in this definition.

\begin{definition}[List history carrier]
\label{def:list-history-carrier}
For a source carrier $S:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$, define $\mathsf{ListHistoryCarrier}_S(h)$ as the least history predicate generated by two clauses:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item nil: if $\hsame(h,\emp)$, then $\mathsf{ListHistoryCarrier}_S(h)$;
  \item cons: if there are histories $a,t,p$ with $S(a)$, $\mathsf{ListHistoryCarrier}_S(t)$, $\ContR(a,t,p)$, and $\hsame(h,\Eone(p))$, then $\mathsf{ListHistoryCarrier}_S(h)$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[List history classifier]
\label{def:list-history-classifier}
For a source carrier $S:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ and source classifier
$\sim_S:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$, define
$\mathsf{ListHistoryClassifier}_{S,\sim_S}(h,k)$ as the least relation
generated by two clauses:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item nil-nil: if $\hsame(h,\emp)$ and $\hsame(k,\emp)$, then
  $\mathsf{ListHistoryClassifier}_{S,\sim_S}(h,k)$;
  \item cons-cons: if there are histories $a,b,t,u,p,q$ such that $S(a)$,
  $S(b)$, $a\sim_S b$, $\mathsf{ListHistoryClassifier}_{S,\sim_S}(t,u)$,
  $\ContR(a,t,p)$, $\ContR(b,u,q)$, $\hsame(h,\Eone(p))$, and
  $\hsame(k,\Eone(q))$, then
  $\mathsf{ListHistoryClassifier}_{S,\sim_S}(h,k)$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[List history classifier carrier endpoints]
\label{thm:list-history-classifier-carrier-endpoints}
For any source carrier $S:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ and source classifier
$\sim_S:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$, if
$\mathsf{ListHistoryClassifier}_{S,\sim_S}(h,k)$ holds, then both
$\mathsf{ListHistoryCarrier}_S(h)$ and $\mathsf{ListHistoryCarrier}_S(k)$
hold.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Induct on the displayed classifier witness. In the nil-nil clause, the two
endpoint witnesses rebuild the nil clause of $\mathsf{ListHistoryCarrier}_S$
on both sides. In the cons-cons clause, the induction hypothesis gives carrier
witnesses for the two tails $t$ and $u$; combine those with the source carrier
witnesses $S(a)$ and $S(b)$, the continuation witnesses $\ContR(a,t,p)$ and
$\ContR(b,u,q)$, and the endpoint witnesses $\hsame(h,\Eone(p))$ and
$\hsame(k,\Eone(q))$ to rebuild the two cons carrier clauses.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[List history classifier constructor exactness]
\label{thm:list-history-classifier-constructor-exactness}
For any source carrier $S:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$, source classifier
$\sim_S:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$, and histories $h,k$,
every witness of $\mathsf{ListHistoryClassifier}_{S,\sim_S}(h,k)$ is exactly one
of the two displayed constructor cases: either nil-nil endpoint witnesses
$\hsame(h,\emp)$ and $\hsame(k,\emp)$, or cons-cons witnesses $a,b,t,u,p,q$ with
$S(a)$, $S(b)$, $a\sim_S b$, $\mathsf{ListHistoryClassifier}_{S,\sim_S}(t,u)$,
$\ContR(a,t,p)$, $\ContR(b,u,q)$, $\hsame(h,\Eone(p))$, and
$\hsame(k,\Eone(q))$. A nil endpoint classified against a cons endpoint, or a
cons endpoint classified against a nil endpoint, has no classifier case.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Induct on the generated classifier witness. The nil-nil constructor gives
exactly the two empty endpoint witnesses. The cons-cons constructor gives
exactly the source head witnesses, the recursive tail-classifier witness, the
two continuation witnesses, and the two visible $e1$ endpoint witnesses. The
mixed endpoint claims reduce to BEDC history no-confusion:
$\mathsf{not\_hsame\_emp\_e1}$ eliminates nil against cons, and
$\mathsf{not\_hsame\_e1\_empty}$ eliminates cons against nil.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[List history carrier generated cases]
\label{thm:list-history-carrier-generated-cases}
For any source carrier $S:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ and any history $h$, if $\mathsf{ListHistoryCarrier}_S(h)$, then either $\hsame(h,\emp)$, or there exist histories $a,t,p$ such that $S(a)$, $\mathsf{ListHistoryCarrier}_S(t)$, $\ContR(a,t,p)$, and $\hsame(h,\Eone(p))$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply the induction principle for the least predicate displayed in the preceding definition. The nil constructor gives the left disjunct directly. The cons constructor exposes its witnesses $a,t,p$ together with $S(a)$, the recursive carrier witness for $t$, the continuation witness $\ContR(a,t,p)$, and the endpoint witness $\hsame(h,\Eone(p))$, which are exactly the data required for the right disjunct.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[List history carrier transport]
\label{thm:list-history-carrier-hsame-transport}
For any source carrier $S:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$, if $\hsame(h,k)$ and $\mathsf{ListHistoryCarrier}_S(h)$, then $\mathsf{ListHistoryCarrier}_S(k)$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Proceed by cases on the displayed generation of $\mathsf{ListHistoryCarrier}_S(h)$. In the nil case, compose the symmetry of $\hsame(h,k)$ with the endpoint witness $\hsame(h,\emp)$ to obtain $\hsame(k,\emp)$, then use the nil clause. In the cons case, keep the witnesses $a,t,p$, the source witness $S(a)$, the recursive carrier witness for $t$, and the continuation witness $\ContR(a,t,p)$. Compose the symmetry of $\hsame(h,k)$ with the endpoint witness $\hsame(h,\Eone(p))$ to obtain $\hsame(k,\Eone(p))$, then rebuild the cons clause for $k$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[List classifier case exactness]
\label{thm:list-classifier-case-exactness}
For any source relation $\mathsf{sameA}$ on $A$ and any generated lists $xs,ys : \ListUp(A)$, $\mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(\mathsf{sameA},xs,ys)$ holds exactly in the matching constructor cases: both lists are $\mathsf{nil}$, or both lists are $\mathsf{cons}$ lists with a source-head witness $\mathsf{sameA}(a,b)$ and a recursive tail witness $\mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(\mathsf{sameA},xs',ys')$. It is false for the mixed shapes $\mathsf{nil}$ against $\mathsf{cons}(b,ys')$ and $\mathsf{cons}(a,xs')$ against $\mathsf{nil}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Case-analyze the two generated list constructors. In the nil-nil case the classifier is the nil clause. In the cons-cons case the classifier unfolds to its head source witness together with the recursive tail classifier witness, and conversely those two witnesses rebuild the cons classifier clause. The nil-cons and cons-nil cases have no classifier clause, so any assumed witness is eliminated by constructor case analysis.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[List classifier nil-cons no-confusion]
\label{lem:list-classifier-nil-cons-no-confusion}
For any source classifier on $A$, $\mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}$ never relates
$\mathsf{nil}$ to $\mathsf{cons}(a,xs)$, and never relates
$\mathsf{cons}(a,xs)$ to $\mathsf{nil}$.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Unfold the structural classifier by list constructors. The only nil clause is
the nil-nil clause, and the only cons clause is the cons-cons clause. The two
mixed constructor shapes therefore have no classifier constructor, so both
directions are discharged directly by case analysis on the impossible witness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[History list classifier constructor-map embeddings]
\label{thm:list-classifier-constructor-map-embeddings}
For BEDC history lists $xs$ and $ys$, structural classification by $\hsame$ is
equivalent to structural classification after mapping both lists through the
$e0$ history constructor, and likewise equivalent to structural classification
after mapping both lists through the $e1$ history constructor.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the $e0$ equivalence, the forward implication is exactly the checked
preservation theorem for $e0$-mapping, and the reverse implication is exactly
the checked $e0$-mapping inversion theorem. The $e1$ equivalence is obtained in
the same way from the checked preservation theorem for $e1$-mapping and the
checked $e1$-mapping inversion theorem. Thus each BEDC history constructor
preserves and reflects the list classifier, giving a classifier embedding in
both constructor-map cases.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[List classifier preserves length]
\label{thm:list-classifier-length-eq}
For any source relation $sameA$ on BEDC history entries and finite list spines
$xs,ys$, if $\mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(sameA,xs,ys)$ holds, then $xs$ and

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Induct on the first list spine. The nil case can classify only against nil by

the classifier can classify only against a cons spine; its tail witness is again
a $\mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}$ witness for the source relation $sameA$, so the
induction hypothesis gives equality of tail lengths, and the two cons lengths
are equal.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[List stability certificate]
\label{def:list-stability-certificate}
The stability certificate consists of:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item classifier reflexivity by list induction, symmetry from the source $\NameCert$, and transitivity by paired source/list induction
  \item nil/cons no-confusion together with head and tail inversion for classified cons values
  \item constructor-map preservation and reflection for $e0$ and $e1$, with mixed-tag separation
  \item append stability, left prefix cancellation, right suffix cancellation, and two-sided cancellation
  \item reverse preservation, reverse reflection, and reverse equivalence
  \item length preservation for structurally classified BEDC history lists
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\end{theorem}

\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[List classifier stability under append]
\label{thm:list-classifier-append-hsame}
For BEDC histories, if two list pairs are structurally classified by \(\HSame\),
then appending the first components and appending the second components
preserves structural classification.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ListUp.ListClassifierSpec\_append\_hsame}

\begin{proof}
Induct on the first list. The empty case returns the classification of the
second pair. In the cons case, invert the second list and the structural
classifier; the head \(\HSame\) witness is reused and the tail follows from
the induction hypothesis.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[History list classifier left append cancellation]

\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Classified list prefix cancellation]
\label{thm:list-classifier-hsame-append-left-cancel-classified}
For BEDC history lists, if \(p\) and \(p'\) are structurally classified by
\(\hsame\), and \(p{++}x\) is structurally classified with \(p'{++}y\), then

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Induct on \(p\). The empty case forces \(p'\) to be empty by the prefix
classifier, leaving the appended classifier. In the cons case, the prefix
classifier and appended classifier both expose matching classified tails, and
the induction hypothesis applies to those tails.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ListUp.ListClassifierSpec\_hsame\_append\_left\_cancel\_classified}

\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[List classifier reverse inversion]
\label{thm:list-classifier-reverse-hsame-inversion}
For BEDC history lists, if the reversed lists are structurally classified by
\(\HSame\), then the original lists are structurally classified by \(\HSame\).
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply reverse stability to the classified reversed lists. The double reverse of
each list is definitionally identified with the original list by list
reverse-reverse induction, giving the desired structural classifier.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ListUp.ListClassifierSpec\_reverse\_hsame\_inversion}

\begin{theorem}[List classifier reverse equivalence]
\label{thm:list-classifier-reverse-hsame-equiv}
For BEDC history lists $xs$ and $ys$, structural classification by $\HSame$ is equivalent before and after reversing both lists:
$$
  \mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(\hsame,xs,ys)
  \Longleftrightarrow
  \mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(\hsame,\mathsf{reverse}(xs),\mathsf{reverse}(ys)).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The forward direction is the reverse-stability theorem. The reverse direction is the reverse-inversion theorem applied to the classified reversed lists.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[List classifier append right cancellation]
\label{thm:list-classifier-append-right-cancel-hsame}
For BEDC history lists, if adding the same right suffix to two lists gives a
structural \(\HSame\)-classification, then the original lists are structurally
classified.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Reverse the classified append witnesses. The common right suffix becomes a
common left prefix, so left-append cancellation removes it. Reversing the
remaining classifier witness returns the original list order.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ListUp.ListClassifierSpec\_append\_right\_cancel\_hsame}

\begin{theorem}[List classifier right cancellation with equivalent suffixes]
\label{thm:list-classifier-append-right-cancel-with-hsame-suffix}
For BEDC history lists, if two right suffixes are structurally classified by
\(\HSame\), and appending those suffixes to \(x\) and \(y\) gives a structural
\(\HSame\)-classification, then \(x\) and \(y\) are structurally classified.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use symmetry on the suffix classifier and append stability with reflexivity on
the left list \(y\) to classify \(y{++}\mathit{suffix}'\) with
\(y{++}\mathit{suffix}\). Transitivity aligns the appended classification to a
common right suffix, and ordinary right-append cancellation removes it.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ListUp.ListClassifierSpec\_append\_right\_cancel\_with\_hsame\_suffix}

\begin{theorem}[List classifier two-sided cancellation with classified boundaries]
\label{thm:list-classifier-append-two-sided-cancel-classified}
For BEDC history lists $p$, $p'$, $x$, $y$, $s$, and $s'$, if
$$
  \mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(\hsame,p,p'),
  \quad
  \mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(\hsame,s,s'),
  \quad
  \mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(\hsame,p{++}x{++}s,p'{++}y{++}s')
$$
hold, then $\mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(\hsame,x,y)$ holds.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Reassociate both appended histories as $(p{++}x){++}s$ and
$(p'{++}y){++}s'$. Apply right cancellation with the classified suffixes to
obtain $\mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(\hsame,p{++}x,p'{++}y)$. Then apply
classified left cancellation with the classified prefixes $p$ and $p'$ to
obtain $\mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(\hsame,x,y)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[List classifier classified append context equivalence]
\label{thm:list-classifier-append-context-equiv}
For BEDC history lists $p$, $p'$, $x$, $y$, $s$, and $s'$, if the prefix
contexts $p$ and $p'$ are structurally classified by $\HSame$ and the suffix
contexts $s$ and $s'$ are structurally classified by $\HSame$, then
$$
  \mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(\hsame,x,y)
  \quad\text{if and only if}\quad
  \mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(\hsame,p{++}x{++}s,p'{++}y{++}s')
$$
holds.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the forward direction, apply append stability first to the classified
prefixes and middle lists, obtaining a classification of $p{++}x$ with
$p'{++}y$, and then apply append stability again with the classified suffixes.
For the reverse direction, apply the preceding two-sided cancellation theorem
to the classified bordered histories, the classified prefixes, and the
classified suffixes.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[List classifier transitivity from a source certificate]
\label{thm:list-classifier-trans-from-namecert}
If the source classifier is supplied by a \(\NameCert\), then the lifted list classifier is transitive on finite list spines.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ListUp.ListClassifierSpec\_trans\_from\_nameCert}

\begin{proof}
Proceed by induction on the first list. The nil case forces the middle list to be nil, then the final list is nil. In the cons case, nil/cons mismatches are impossible by classifier inversion. For matching cons constructors, transitivity of the source certificate handles the heads and the induction hypothesis handles the tails.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[List classifier symmetry from a source certificate]
\label{thm:list-classifier-symm-from-namecert}
For a BEDC history carrier $A : \mathsf{BHist} \to \mathsf{Prop}$, source classifier $\sim_A$, and source certificate $\mathsf{cert} : \NameCert(A,\sim_A)$, if $\mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(\sim_A,xs,ys)$ holds for finite list spines $xs$ and $ys$, then $\mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(\sim_A,ys,xs)$ holds.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Induct on the structural list-classifier witness. The nil clause reverses to nil. In the cons clause, apply $\mathsf{cert.equiv\_symm}$ to the head witness and apply the induction hypothesis to the tail witness; the cons constructor of the lifted classifier rebuilds the reversed structural witness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[List carrier transport from a source certificate]
\label{thm:list-carrier-transport-from-namecert}
Let $C$ be a source carrier predicate and let $\sim_A$ be a source classifier equipped with $\NameCert(C,\sim_A)$. For finite list spines $xs,ys : \ListUp(A)$, if every entry of $xs$ satisfies $C$ and $\mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(\sim_A,xs,ys)$ holds, then every entry of $ys$ satisfies $C$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Induct on the structural classifier derivation. The nil clause has no entries to transport. In the cons clause, the source certificate field $\mathsf{NameCert.carrier\_respects\_equiv}$ transports the head carrier witness across the head classifier witness, and the induction hypothesis transports all tail entries across the tail classifier witness.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[List ledger policy]
\label{def:list-ledger-policy}
Record the finite constructor spine and the source ledger for each element.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[List history ledger]
\label{def:list-history-ledger}
For a source carrier $S:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$,
$\mathsf{ListHistoryLedger}_S(h)$ records the visible history endpoint as a
finite nil/cons spine: the empty endpoint is recorded by $\hsame(h,\emp)$, and
a cons endpoint is recorded by histories $a,t,p$ with $S(a)$,
$\mathsf{ListHistoryLedger}_S(t)$, $\ContR(a,t,p)$, and
$\hsame(h,\Eone(p))$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[List spine representation]
\label{def:list-spine-representation}
For a BEDC history source carrier $A:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$, the relation $\mathsf{ListSpineRep}_A(h,xs)$ says that the history $h$ represents the finite list spine $xs$ by the same nil and cons clauses used by $\mathsf{ListHistoryCarrier}_A$:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item nil: $xs=\mathsf{nil}$ and $\hsame(h,\emp)$;
  \item cons: $xs=\mathsf{cons}(ah,xs')$ and there are histories $ah,t,p$ with $A(ah)$, $\mathsf{ListSpineRep}_A(t,xs')$, $\ContR(ah,t,p)$, and $\hsame(h,\Eone(p))$.
\end{enumerate}
The history-level classifier used by the certificate is
$$
  \mathsf{ListHistoryClassifier}_{\sim_A}(h,k)
  \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
  \exists xs,ys.\;
  \mathsf{ListSpineRep}_A(h,xs)\land
  \mathsf{ListSpineRep}_A(k,ys)\land
  \mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(\sim_A,xs,ys).
$$
Thus $\mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}$ remains the finite-spine classifier, and $\mathsf{ListHistoryClassifier}_{\sim_A}$ is the classifier whose endpoints are BEDC histories.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Concrete $\ListUp$ certificate target]
\label{thm:concrete-list-namecert}
Given a BEDC history source carrier $A : \mathsf{BHist} \to \mathsf{Prop}$, source classifier $\sim_A$, and source certificate $\mathsf{cert} : \NameCert(A,\sim_A)$, define $\mathsf{ListSource}(A)$ as the history carrier $\mathsf{ListHistoryCarrier}_A$, define $\mathsf{ListHistoryClassifier}_{\sim_A}$ by Definition~\ref{def:list-spine-representation}, define $\mathsf{ListPattern}(A)$ as the nil-or-cons constructor-shape predicate over represented histories, and define $\mathsf{ListLedger}(A)$ as the visible length together with the source ledger read from a represented finite spine. These fields form the paper-side target $\mathsf{SemanticNameCert}(\mathsf{ListHistoryCarrier}_A,\mathsf{ListPattern}(A),\mathsf{ListLedger}(A),\mathsf{ListHistoryClassifier}_{\sim_A})$ for $\ListUp(A)$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The source $\NameCert$ supplies the head-level reflexivity, symmetry, transitivity, and carrier transport fields. The generated nil/cons carrier supplies the BEDC histories, and Definition~\ref{def:list-spine-representation} supplies the finite spine through which the checked structural classifier is applied. Classifier reflexivity chooses the representing spine for the same history and applies induction on that spine. Symmetry is obtained by keeping the two representation witnesses and reversing the finite-spine classifier with Theorem~\ref{thm:list-classifier-symm-from-namecert}. Transitivity composes the finite-spine classifier witnesses with Theorem~\ref{thm:list-classifier-trans-from-namecert} after matching the middle represented history. Carrier transport follows by induction over the represented spine, with nil transported to nil and cons transported componentwise by the source certificate and the tail induction hypothesis. Nil/cons case analysis uses Theorem~\ref{thm:list-history-carrier-generated-cases} for the generated history carrier and Lemma~\ref{lem:list-classifier-nil-cons-no-confusion} inside the finite-spine bridge, length and source-ledger policy are read from the representing spine, and exactness is the matching-shape statement of Theorem~\ref{thm:list-classifier-case-exactness} transported through the representation relation.
\end{proof}

\(\ListUp\) is licensed as a finite inductive sequence over \(A\).
\end{corollary}
