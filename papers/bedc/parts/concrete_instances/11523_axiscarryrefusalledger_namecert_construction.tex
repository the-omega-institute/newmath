\begin{definition}[Axis carry-refusal ledger carrier]
\label{def:axis-carry-refusal-ledger-carrier}
An $\AxisCarryRefusalLedgerUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
R=(G,Z,F,A,B,L,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $G$ is the accepted $\AxisZeckendorfCannotClaimUp$ registry row containing the checked negative claims and horizon refusals;
\item $Z$ is the checked row witnessing that a displayed $\ZCarryUp$ edge is not an $\hsame$ identification;
\item $F$ is the horizon row refusing to read $\FullAxisUp$ as $\RealUp$ or as a complete metric object;
\item $A$ is the horizon row refusing to read $\AxisNatUp$ as a replacement for $\NatUp$;
\item $B$ records the $\UnaryDirectionBridgeUp$ boundary through which any comparison between axis and unary naturals must pass;
\item $L$ is the refusal ledger binding the negative rows $Z,F,A$ to their registry source $G$ and bridge boundary $B$;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $G,Z,F,A,B,L,C,P,N$ without converting a refused identification into equality;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay for the registry row, refusal rows, bridge boundary, ledger, transports, provenance, and local naming;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\AxisZeckendorfCannotClaimUp$, $\ZCarryUp$, $\FullAxisUp$, $\RealUp$, $\AxisNatUp$, $\NatUp$, $\UnaryDirectionBridgeUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\AxisCarryRefusalLedgerUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for turning $\ZCarryUp$ into $\hsame$, promoting $\FullAxisUp$ to $\RealUp$, replacing $\NatUp$ by $\AxisNatUp$, or bypassing the bridge boundary $B$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Axis carry-refusal ledger realizes registry refusals]
\label{thm:axis-carry-refusal-ledger-realizes-registry-refusals}
Every accepted $\AxisCarryRefusalLedgerUp$ packet $R=(G,Z,F,A,B,L,H,C,P,N)$ exposes the cannot-claim registry as a finite ledger of negative rows. The carry refusal $Z$, full-axis horizon refusal $F$, axis-natural refusal $A$, bridge boundary $B$, ledger $L$, transport $H$, continuation replay $C$, provenance $P$, and local naming row $N$ must be read together before a consumer may cite the registry as a transportable refusal packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:axis-carry-refusal-ledger-carrier}. The registry row $G$ supplies the source cannot-claim packet, while $Z,F,A$ are the displayed refusal rows selected from it.

Read the bridge and ledger rows next. The bridge boundary $B$ records where any $\AxisNatUp$--$\NatUp$ comparison must pass, and $L$ binds the carry, full-axis, and axis-natural refusals to the same registry source.

Finally replay the support rows. Transport through $H$ preserves the displayed refusal components, $C$ replays only those components, $P$ records their provenance, and $N$ names the finite packet. Since no excluded identification is a coordinate of $R$, every accepted consumer remains inside the ledger above.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Axis carry-refusal ledger non-identification]
\label{thm:axis-carry-refusal-ledger-nonidentification}
For an accepted $\AxisCarryRefusalLedgerUp$ packet, no consumer can derive $\hsame$ from the $\ZCarryUp$ row, a $\RealUp$ field surface from the $\FullAxisUp$ row, or a replacement of $\NatUp$ by $\AxisNatUp$ from the axis-natural row. Each such reading is rejected unless its corresponding refusal row $Z$, $F$, or $A$ is absent, in which case the packet is not the accepted carrier of \autoref{def:axis-carry-refusal-ledger-carrier}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use \autoref{thm:axis-carry-refusal-ledger-realizes-registry-refusals}. The accepted carrier exposes $Z,F,A$ as part of the same finite refusal ledger, so none of the three rows is optional for this packet.

The structural rows do not add hidden identifications. The transport row $H$ preserves displayed components, $C$ replays the same refusal route, and $P,N$ only record provenance and local naming.

Coordinate exhaustion gives the claim. The carrier contains no host equality, no Real field-operation surface for $\FullAxisUp$, and no replacement map from $\AxisNatUp$ to $\NatUp$; therefore the proposed readings are rejected by the ledger classifier.
\end{proof}
