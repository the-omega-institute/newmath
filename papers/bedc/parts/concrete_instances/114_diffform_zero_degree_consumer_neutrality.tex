\begin{definition}[Differential-form zero-degree consumer-neutrality surface]
\label{def:diffform-zero-degree-consumer-neutrality-surface}
Fix $\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\TensorProductUp}$ sources
$M,T$ as in \autoref{def:diffform-bhist-form-carrier}. The differential-form
zero-degree consumer-neutrality surface is the zero-degree scalar boundary row
of \autoref{def:diffform-zero-degree-scalar-boundary}, read together with the
shared rows visible to every downstream consumer:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item the carried $BHist$ row, its $\hsame$ ledger, and the displayed
  $\Cont$ unit rows on the empty degree history;
  \item the empty probe spine $\mathsf{ProbeBundle.Bnil}$ and its absence of
  probe membership rows;
  \item the $\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\TensorProductUp}$
  source ledgers supplying the manifold and tensor endpoints;
  \item the retained scalar endpoint classifier row;
  \item the empty antisymmetry ledger and the source ledger rows recorded by
  the boundary coordinate $\lambda_0$.
\end{enumerate}
This surface is a shared root-face package only. It contains the data needed to
reuse the scalar $0$-form boundary, but it does not add a consumer-specific
law row.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Differential-form zero-degree shared consumer face]
\label{thm:diffform-zero-degree-shared-consumer-face}
The zero-degree consumer-neutrality surface of
\autoref{def:diffform-zero-degree-consumer-neutrality-surface} lies wholly in
the shared root face consumed by $\DeRhamUp$, $\SymplecticUp$, and
$\ContactUp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the surface through
\autoref{def:diffform-zero-degree-consumer-neutrality-surface}. Its probe
coordinate is $\mathsf{ProbeBundle.Bnil}$, so
\autoref{thm:diffform-zero-degree-empty-probe-exhaustion} removes every
probe-indexed membership row from the exported data.

The remaining classifier information is exactly the retained scalar endpoint
and the visible source-ledger comparisons by
\autoref{thm:diffform-zero-degree-scalar-classifier-readback}. These rows are
carried $BHist$, classifier, scalar endpoint, empty antisymmetry, wedge-unit,
and source-ledger rows.

Apply \autoref{thm:diffform-root-consumer-face-coverage}. The shared root
face is precisely the face that may be consumed by the three downstream
chapters without assigning a row to a consumer-specific law. Hence the whole
zero-degree surface lies in that shared face.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Differential-form zero-degree consumer law separation]
\label{thm:diffform-zero-degree-consumer-law-separation}
The zero-degree consumer-neutrality surface exports no $d^2=0$ row, no
closedness row, no nondegeneracy row, and no contact nonintegrability row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
By \autoref{thm:diffform-zero-degree-shared-consumer-face}, the surface lies
inside the shared root face. Apply
\autoref{thm:diffform-root-consumer-face-disjointness}: nilpotence is owned by
$\DeRhamUp$, closedness and nondegeneracy by $\SymplecticUp$, and contact
nonintegrability by $\ContactUp$.

It remains to check that the wedge contribution does not smuggle in one of
those laws. The wedge row available at zero degree is only the $\Cont$ unit
boundary of
\autoref{thm:diffform-zero-degree-wedge-cont-unit-boundary}. That theorem
identifies the empty probe and empty degree contribution of the scalar
endpoint; it supplies no exterior-derivative square, no symplectic closedness
or nondegeneracy clause, and no contact nonintegrability clause.

Therefore every law row named in the statement remains outside the exported
zero-degree surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Differential-form zero-degree deps-ready consumer seal]
\label{thm:diffform-zero-degree-deps-ready-consumer-seal}
The definition
\autoref{def:diffform-zero-degree-consumer-neutrality-surface}, the shared
consumer-face theorem
\autoref{thm:diffform-zero-degree-shared-consumer-face}, and the consumer law
separation theorem
\autoref{thm:diffform-zero-degree-consumer-law-separation} form the citable
zero-degree shared package for the $\DeRhamUp$, $\SymplecticUp$, and
$\ContactUp$ consumers.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The definition names the exact rows carried by the scalar $0$-form boundary.
The shared consumer-face theorem places those rows in the common root face
seen by the three downstream chapters.

The consumer law separation theorem records the complementary exclusion:
nilpotence, closedness, nondegeneracy, and contact nonintegrability are not
part of the zero-degree root package. Thus downstream consumers may cite this
package for the shared zero-degree boundary while keeping their own laws
separate.
\end{proof}
