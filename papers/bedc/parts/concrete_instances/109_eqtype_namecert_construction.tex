\begin{theorem}[EqType identity semantic name certificate]
\label{thm:eqtype-identity-semantic-namecert}
The identity classifier on histories forms a semantic name certificate when
source, pattern, and ledger are all the self-identity predicate:
$$
\mathsf{SemanticNameCert}
(\lambda h.\ h\hsame h,\lambda h.\ h\hsame h,\lambda h.\ h\hsame h,\hsame).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The empty history supplies inhabitation. Reflexivity, symmetry, transitivity,
and carrier transport are exactly the corresponding closure laws of $\hsame$.
The pattern and ledger maps are the identity maps on the self-identity
predicate.
\end{proof}
