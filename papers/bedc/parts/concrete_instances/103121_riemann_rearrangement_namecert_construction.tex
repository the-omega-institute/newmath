\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RiemannRearrangementUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-riemann-rearrangement-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Riemann and Bishop--Bridges constructive analysis, the $\RiemannRearrangementUp$ packet records the finite boundary of rearranging a conditionally convergent real or complex series by visible positive and negative partial-window ledgers and a displayed permutation schedule. It sits over the complex-series surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-complex-series-namecert}, the terminal real-name boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the finite stream observations of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the dyadic budget rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the fast tail-control surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-fastconvergentseries-namecert}. The packet names the finite admission surface for a rearrangement read; it is not a host theorem asserting an arbitrary selected sum, a quotient equality of infinite series, a countable-choice principle, trichotomy on arbitrary terms, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[Riemann rearrangement carrier]
\label{def:riemann-rearrangement-carrier}
A $\RiemannRearrangementUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
R=(S,Q,P,M,\Pi,B,G,E,H,C,K,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the signed source-series row, read as a $\RealUp$ or $\ComplexSeriesUp$ term stream with visible finite partial sums.
\item $Q$ is the conditional-convergence evidence row, exposing convergence of the signed source route together with the absence of an absolute-majorant seal in the displayed budget.
\item $P$ is the positive-window ledger, listing the finite windows whose partial sums raise the running rearranged read above the requested lower gate.
\item $M$ is the negative-window ledger, listing the finite windows whose partial sums lower the running rearranged read below the requested upper gate.
\item $\Pi$ is the finite permutation schedule row, recording only the displayed source indices consumed by the current alternation of positive and negative windows.
\item $B$ is the dyadic budget row, carrying the requested tolerance, comparison scale, and finite overshoot bounds through $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ data.
\item $G$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row obtained from the displayed stream windows, ledgers, permutation schedule, and dyadic budget.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ or $\ComplexSeriesUp$ seal row for the accepted rearrangement read, reached only after $S,Q,P,M,\Pi,B,G$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the source series, conditional-convergence evidence, positive and negative ledgers, permutation schedule, dyadic budget, regular readback, terminal seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the route
$$
S,Q \longmapsto P,M,\Pi \longmapsto B \longmapsto G \longmapsto E .
$$
\item $K$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RiemannRearrangementUp$, $\ComplexSeriesUp$, $\RealUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\FastConvergentSeriesUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RiemannRearrangementUp}$ registration row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate compares accepted packets only through $S,Q,P,M,\Pi,B,G,E,H,C,K,N$. It has no coordinate for an arbitrary permutation selector, selected target sum, quotient equality of infinite series, countable choice, trichotomy on arbitrary terms, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Riemann rearrangement NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:riemann-rearrangement-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RiemannRearrangementUp$ carrier
$$
R=(S,Q,P,M,\Pi,B,G,E,H,C,K,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\RiemannRearrangementUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed packet of \autoref{def:riemann-rearrangement-carrier};
\item positive and negative ledger exposure, requiring both $P$ and $M$ to be visible before any target-facing seal is read;
\item finite permutation-window transport, requiring the schedule $\Pi$ to list only displayed source indices consumed by the current positive or negative window;
\item regular-readback handoff, requiring $B$ and $G$ to mediate every tolerance and $\RegSeqRatUp$ read before $E$ is visible;
\item selector nonescape, requiring every accepted consumer to factor through $S,Q,P,M,\Pi,B,G,H,C,K,N$ before inspecting a terminal $\RealUp$ or $\ComplexSeriesUp$ seal.
\end{enumerate}
No rearrangement obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:riemann-rearrangement-carrier}. The displayed packet lists the signed source series, conditional-convergence evidence, positive-window ledger, negative-window ledger, finite permutation schedule, dyadic budget, regular rational readback, terminal seal, and structural BEDC rows.

The ledger clauses are read before the seal. The row $P$ records the finite positive windows, while $M$ records the finite negative windows. Since $E$ occurs only after both rows in the carrier route, a consumer cannot inspect a rearranged read without seeing both signed ledgers.

Permutation-window transport is the role of $\Pi$. It exposes the finite source indices consumed in the current window alternation and has no coordinate for a global selector over all natural numbers.

The readback handoff is then forced through $B$ and $G$. The dyadic row supplies the tolerance budget, and the regular rational row exports the finite stream read before any terminal $\RealUp$ or $\ComplexSeriesUp$ seal can be consumed.

Finally, $H$ and $C$ preserve and replay the displayed route componentwise, while $K$ and $N$ source and name the same packet. Since the carrier contains no excluded selector, quotient, choice, trichotomy, or completeness row, the five obligation rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Riemann rearrangement positive-negative ledger]
\label{thm:riemann-rearrangement-positive-negative-ledger}
For every accepted $\RiemannRearrangementUp$ carrier $R=(S,Q,P,M,\Pi,B,G,E,H,C,K,N)$, every accepted rearrangement read factors through both the positive-window ledger $P$ and the negative-window ledger $M$ before any terminal $\RealUp$ or $\ComplexSeriesUp$ seal $E$ is visible.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted read of the terminal seal $E$. By the carrier definition, $E$ is not stored as an independent infinite-sum object; it is downstream of the displayed source row, conditional-convergence evidence, both signed window ledgers, the finite permutation schedule, the dyadic budget, and the regular readback.

The positive route is exposed by $P$. It records the finite windows whose partial sums move the running read above the requested lower gate.

The negative route is exposed by $M$. It records the finite windows whose partial sums move the running read below the requested upper gate.

The schedule $\Pi$ only alternates between these displayed ledgers, and the rows $B,G,H,C,K,N$ transport, read back, replay, source, and name that same route. Hence every accepted read reaches $E$ only after both signed ledgers have been inspected.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Riemann rearrangement selector nonescape]
\label{thm:riemann-rearrangement-selector-nonescape}
An accepted $\RiemannRearrangementUp$ carrier exports no arbitrary permutation selector, no selected target sum, no quotient equality of infinite series, no countable-choice row, no trichotomy on arbitrary source terms, and no ambient $\RealUp$ completeness witness. Its public consumers receive only the displayed source-series row, conditional-convergence evidence, positive and negative ledgers, finite permutation-window schedule, dyadic budget, regular readback, terminal seal, componentwise transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. Its coordinates are exactly $S,Q,P,M,\Pi,B,G,E,H,C,K,N$.

The only permutation data is the finite schedule $\Pi$. It records displayed source indices for the current positive or negative window and does not contain a total selector for all source positions.

The only target-facing data is the downstream seal $E$, reached through $B$ and $G$ after both signed ledgers have been read. Thus the carrier supplies a finite readback surface, not a selected target sum or a quotient equality of infinite series.

The structural rows $H,C,K,N$ preserve, replay, source, and name the same finite packet. None of the listed coordinates can store countable choice, trichotomy, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, so those principles do not escape through the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}
