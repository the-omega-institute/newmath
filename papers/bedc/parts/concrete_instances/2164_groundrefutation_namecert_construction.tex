\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\GroundRefutationUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-groundrefutation-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\GroundRefutationUp$ surface records the permitted contradiction pattern of \autoref{ch:visions-reasoning-by-contradiction-boundary} as a closed ground-loop naming packet. Its refutation is not a meta rule over arbitrary certificates: it is a finite $\BHist$ row whose assumption, falsity route, transports, continuation routes, provenance, and local $\NameCert$ row are all displayed before any negation may be exported.

It is the closed ground-loop specialization of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-refutationboundary-namecert}. It consumes the permitted-refutation route and forbidden truth-branch exclusion from that broader boundary, but removes the optional decidability and certificate-indexed boundary rows.

\begin{definition}[Ground refutation carrier]
\label{def:ground-refutation-carrier}
A $\GroundRefutationUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
G=(A,F,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ is the assumption row admitted only inside the fixed ground loop;
\item $F$ is the falsity-producing route that sends the displayed assumption row through recorded $\Cont$ steps to the bottom row;
\item $H$ records the componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the assumption row, falsity route, package provenance, and local naming row;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes used to read $A$, replay $F$, transport the bottom row, and return the exported negation to the same finite packet;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, $\Cont$, $\hsame$, the bottom row, and the surrounding certificate surface;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\GroundRefutationUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no coordinate for a certificate-indexed truth branch, no excluded-middle split, no undecidable double-negation eliminator, and no existential witness selected by contradiction.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[GroundRefutation NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:ground-refutation-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\GroundRefutationUp}$ surface for the carrier $G=(A,F,H,C,P,N)$ consists of five finite rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier exposure: an accepted object exposes exactly the packet of \autoref{def:ground-refutation-carrier};
\item classifier row: two accepted packets classify together only when their assumption rows, falsity routes, transports, continuation routes, package provenance, and local naming rows are matched by the displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ data;
\item stability row: the exported negation remains tied to the same assumption row after every stored $\hsame$ transport;
\item ledger row: the bottom row reached by $F$ is accountable to the displayed $\Cont$ route and package provenance;
\item non-escape row: no consumer may read from $G$ a certificate-indexed truth branch, excluded-middle split, undecidable double-negation elimination, or existential witness by contradiction.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.GroundRefutationUp.GroundRefutationNameCertObligations}
\begin{proof}
Carrier exposure is projection from the finite packet of \autoref{def:ground-refutation-carrier}. The assumption row, falsity route, transport ledger, continuation routes, provenance, and local naming row are the complete public inventory.

The classifier row compares only those coordinates. A classified pair must replay the same assumption read and the same falsity-producing route through the displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ data.

Stability follows because the exported negation is defined by applying the transported falsity route to the transported assumption row. The route never leaves $A,F,H,C,P,N$, so the negation remains a ground-loop output rather than a meta rule.

The ledger row is the accountability of $F$ inside $C$ and $P$. A bottom row can be consumed only together with the route and provenance that produced it.

Finally, the non-escape row follows from the carrier inventory. None of the six coordinates stores a truth predicate over certificates, an excluded-middle branch, an undecided double-negation eliminator, or an existential selector, so no consumer can project such data from the packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Ground-loop refutation boundary]
\label{thm:ground-refutation-ground-loop-boundary}
Every negation exported by an accepted $\GroundRefutationUp$ packet factors through the fixed certificate's displayed $\Cont$ route and $\hsame$ transport. The packet therefore realizes only the ground-loop rule that the displayed assumption row $A$ leads by the recorded route $F$ to bottom. It does not export a certificate-indexed truth branch, excluded-middle split, undecidable double-negation elimination, or existential witness by contradiction.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.GroundRefutationUp.GroundRefutationCarrier\_ground\_loop\_boundary}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the accepted packet as $G=(A,F,H,C,P,N)$. The only route from an assumed witness of $A$ to bottom is the displayed falsity-producing route $F$ read through $C$.

Transporting the packet uses $H$ componentwise. The assumption row and falsity route therefore move together, and the exported negation is still the same finite route from the transported assumption row to the transported bottom row.

The remaining coordinates $P$ and $N$ record provenance and the local naming ledger for this route. Since the carrier has no field for truth over certificates, excluded middle, undecided double-negation elimination, or witness selection by contradiction, every exported negation stays inside the closed ground loop.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GroundRefutation classifier transport]
\label{thm:ground-refutation-classifier-transport}
Let $G=(A,F,H,C,P,N)$ and $G'=(A',F',H',C',P',N')$ be accepted $\GroundRefutationUp$ carriers, each displayed as a finite $\BHist$ packet with its $\hsame$ ledger, $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert_{\GroundRefutationUp}$ row. If the classifier row matches $A$ with $A'$, $F$ with $F'$, $H$ with $H'$, $C$ with $C'$, $P$ with $P'$, and $N$ with $N'$ by the displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ data, then every classified negation exported from $G$ transports to the corresponding classified negation exported from $G'$. The transport has no coordinate that can introduce a certificate-indexed truth branch, excluded-middle split, undecided double-negation eliminator, or existential witness by contradiction.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.GroundRefutationUp.GroundRefutationCarrier\_classifier\_transport}
\begin{proof}
Read both carriers through \autoref{def:ground-refutation-carrier}. The classifier has only the six displayed coordinates, so its transport data is the componentwise comparison of assumption row, falsity route, transport ledger, continuation ledger, provenance, and naming row.

Apply the $\hsame$ entries to move the assumption row and the falsity route together. Apply the $\Cont$ entries to replay the assumption read, falsity production, bottom transport, and exported-negation return inside the second packet.

The $\Pkg$ row and local $\NameCert$ row account for the same finite route after transport. Since no remaining carrier coordinate stores a truth branch, excluded-middle split, undecided double-negation eliminator, or witness selector, the transported classifier remains a ground-loop classifier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GroundRefutation route stability]
\label{thm:ground-refutation-route-stability}
For an accepted $\GroundRefutationUp$ carrier $G=(A,F,H,C,P,N)$ displayed as a finite $\BHist$ packet, every stored $\hsame$ transport in $H$ preserves the $\Cont$ route in $C$ that reads $A$, replays $F$, transports the bottom row, and returns the exported negation. Thus the exported negation is stable under the recorded carrier transports, with accountability still carried by $P$ and by the local $\NameCert_{\GroundRefutationUp}$ row $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the transport ledger $H$. Its entries are attached to the assumption row, falsity route, provenance row, and naming row rather than to an ambient meta rule.

The continuation ledger $C$ is read in the same order before and after transport: read the assumption row, replay $F$, move the bottom row, and return the negation. Componentwise $\hsame$ therefore cannot detach the conclusion from its ground assumption.

The provenance row $P$ and the local $\NameCert$ row $N$ remain part of the transported packet. The route stability claim is exactly that the transported output is still produced by these displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GroundRefutation ledger accountability]
\label{thm:ground-refutation-ledger-accountability}
Let $G=(A,F,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\GroundRefutationUp$ carrier. Any consumer that reads the bottom row produced by $F$ must also read the displayed $\BHist$ assumption row $A$, the $\hsame$ transport ledger $H$, the $\Cont$ replay ledger $C$, the $\Pkg$ provenance row $P$, and the local $\NameCert_{\GroundRefutationUp}$ row $N$. Consequently the bottom row is not available as an unaccounted contradiction token outside the packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier inventory has no standalone bottom-token coordinate. The bottom row appears only through the falsity-producing route $F$ and the continuation entries in $C$.

A consumer of that row therefore consumes the route that produced it. The $\hsame$ ledger records how the route and assumption move, while the $\Pkg$ provenance and local $\NameCert$ row record why the route belongs to this finite packet.

It follows that the contradiction cannot be exported as anonymous evidence. It is always accompanied by the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows of $G$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GroundRefutation consumer readiness]
\label{thm:ground-refutation-consumer-readiness}
An accepted $\GroundRefutationUp$ consumer may use the carrier $G=(A,F,H,C,P,N)$ only through the displayed finite $\BHist$ rows, the componentwise $\hsame$ transports, the $\Cont$ routes, the $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local $\NameCert_{\GroundRefutationUp}$ row. Such a consumer is ready for the ground-loop negation output exactly when it requests no certificate-indexed truth branch, excluded-middle split, undecided double-negation eliminator, existential witness by contradiction, or route not listed in $C$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Consumer readiness is a projection discipline on the six-coordinate packet. The available data are precisely $A,F,H,C,P,N$, read with their $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ structure.

If a consumer asks for the ground-loop negation, the packet supplies the assumption row, the falsity route, the transport ledger, the continuation replay, the provenance row, and the naming row. These are exactly the rows required by the prior boundary and accountability statements.

If a consumer asks for a truth branch, excluded-middle split, undecided double-negation eliminator, existential selector, or unlisted route, the carrier has no such coordinate to project. The readiness condition therefore keeps every valid consumer inside the displayed ground-loop surface.
\end{proof}
