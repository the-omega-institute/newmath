\begin{theorem}[Picard--Lindelof local-flow NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:picard-lindelof-local-flow-namecert-obligations}

$$

the local $\NameCert_{\PicardLindelofLocalFlowUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the displayed rows, ODE source compatibility for $O$, Lipschitz graph stability for $F$, contraction packaging for $K$, finite Picard iteration for $L$, complete-metric handoff for $M$, stream-name visibility for $S$, rational budget visibility for $R$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, package provenance through $G$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

The source rows $O,F,R$ fix the local window. The ODE row supplies the initial time-state cell and endpoint row, the Lipschitz row supplies the graph reads and bound, and the rational control row supplies the time-step and radius budgets that keep the later rows inside the same window.

The iteration rows $K,L,S$ are finite. Finally $H,C,G,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite surface. Since all exported rows are fields of $P$, the certificate cannot assert host function equality, choose a solution curve, or extend the local window beyond the displayed nonescape data.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}

Use $S$ and $R$ to keep the iteration visible. The stream-name row names the finite prefix and its continuing ledger, and the rational control row supplies the budgets used to keep the prefix inside the nonescape window.
