\begin{definition}[Constructive Dini modulus carrier]
\label{def:constructive-dini-modulus-carrier}
A $\ConstructiveDiniModulusUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
D=(K,F,L,N,Q,W,S,R,E,G,M,H,C,P,A)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $K$ is the $\CompactMetricUp$ source row, exposing only the finite-net rows used by the modulus extraction.
\item $F$ is the monotone real-function sequence row, listing the inspected function names, their pointwise order comparisons, and their target $\RealUp$ value rows.
\item $L$ is the pointwise-limit row, naming the displayed limit function and the finite pointwise tail requests that compare $F_n(x)$ with $L(x)$.
\item $N$ is the compact finite-net row selected from $K$ for the current tolerance.
\item $Q$ is the $\ContinuousMapUp$ continuity ledger for the finitely many functions and the limit row inspected at the net centers.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window schedule for function values, limit values, and net-center observations.
\item $S$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row obtained from the windows in $W$.
\item $R$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, recording the requested tolerance, the smaller comparison tolerances, and the finite dyadic descent used by the chain.
\item $E$ is the finite chain comparison row. It lists the net-center tail index, monotonicity step, continuity transfer, and target estimate used for each displayed center.
\item $G$ is the global modulus row obtained by taking the finite maximum of the center indices recorded in $E$.
\item $M$ is the exported Dini-modulus witness for the monotone sequence and limit row.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $K,F,L,N,Q,W,S,R,E,G,M$ and the administrative rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from compact finite-net data through pointwise-limit windows, dyadic tolerances, finite chain comparisons, and the exported modulus.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ConstructiveDiniModulusUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\HeineCantorUp$, $\RealUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $A$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ConstructiveDiniModulusUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exposes only monotone real-function sequence rows, pointwise-limit rows, compact finite-net rows, finite chain comparison rows, dyadic tolerance ledgers, regular rational and stream-name finite windows, componentwise transport, replay, provenance, and local naming. It has no coordinate for open-cover compactness, selected subcovers, choice, quotient equality, trichotomy, host function extensionality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Constructive Dini finite-chain modulus]
\label{thm:constructive-dini-modulus-finite-chain}
For every accepted $\ConstructiveDiniModulusUp$ carrier
$$
D=(K,F,L,N,Q,W,S,R,E,G,M,H,C,P,A),
$$
the exported row $M$ is a finite Dini-modulus witness for the monotone sequence row $F$ and the pointwise-limit row $L$ at the tolerance recorded by $R$. Every consumer of $M$ must factor through the displayed route
$$
K,F,L,R \longmapsto N,Q,W,S,E \longmapsto G,M \longmapsto H,C,P,A.
$$
Thus compact finite nets, pointwise limit windows, $\ContinuousMapUp$ continuity ledgers, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance descent, $\RegSeqRatUp$ and $\StreamNameUp$ readbacks, finite chain comparisons, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local $\NameCert$ ledger suffice to export the constructive Dini modulus.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:constructive-dini-modulus-carrier}. The tolerance ledger $R$ determines the comparison scale, while $K$ supplies the finite net row $N$ at the source scale consumed by the chain comparison.

At each center of $N$, read the pointwise-limit row $L$ through the finite windows $W$ and the regular rational readback $S$. The monotone function sequence row $F$ supplies the ordered tail comparisons, and $Q$ supplies the continuity rows needed to move from centers to neighboring source points.

The chain row $E$ records the only comparison route used by the packet. It combines the center tail estimate, monotonicity, continuity transfer, and the dyadic tolerance descent of $R$ into a displayed estimate at the requested tolerance.

Take the finite maximum of the center indices in $E$ to obtain $G$, and read $M$ as the exported modulus row. Componentwise transport in $H$ preserves the compact source, function sequence, limit, window, tolerance, comparison, and modulus rows, while $C$ replays the same route for downstream consumers.

Finally $P$ and $A$ record provenance and local naming. Since no carrier coordinate contains an open cover, selected subcover, choice functional, quotient equality, or ambient completeness principle, no consumer can extract any of those stronger readings from the modulus witness.
\end{proof}
