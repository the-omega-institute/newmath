\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RegularCauchyAbsUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-regularcauchyabs-namecert}

Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, the $\RegularCauchyAbsUp$ packet lifts the finite $\DyadicAbsoluteValueUp$ endpoint-and-tolerance route through $\StreamNameUp$ windows, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, and the $\RealUp$ seal. It sits after the dyadic absolute-value surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicabsolutevalue-namecert} and beside the regular Cauchy operation surfaces of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regularcauchysum-namecert}. The packet exposes absolute value for regular Cauchy names as finite NameCert data; it is not trichotomy, quotient streams, a host absolute-value operation, selected limits, or ambient completeness.

\begin{definition}[RegularCauchyAbs carrier]
\label{def:regular-cauchy-abs-carrier}
A $\RegularCauchyAbsUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
A=(X,W,D,V,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the source $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular rational Cauchy name row;
\item $W$ is the scheduled family of $\StreamNameUp$ finite windows through which $X$ is observed;
\item $D$ is the family of $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint rows read from those windows;
\item $V$ is the family of $\DyadicAbsoluteValueUp$ endpoint-and-tolerance rows applied to the displayed dyadic endpoints;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row assembled from the absolute-value windows and their finite tolerance ledger;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after the readback row $R$ has been exposed;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the source, windows, dyadic endpoints, absolute-value rows, readback, seal, provenance, and local name;
\item $C$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ routes from $X$ to $W$ and $D$, from $D$ to $V$, from $V$ to $R$, and from $R$ to $E$;
\item $P$ records $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\DyadicAbsoluteValueUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyAbsUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for quotient streams, trichotomy of reals, a host absolute-value operation, selected limits, or a $\RealUp$ completeness principle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[RegularCauchyAbs NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-abs-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyAbsUp}$ surface for \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-abs-carrier} consists exactly of source $\RegSeqRatUp$ admission, scheduled $\StreamNameUp$ window exposure, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint readback, $\DyadicAbsoluteValueUp$ endpoint-and-tolerance application, $\RegSeqRatUp$ absolute-value handoff, $\RealUp$ seal boundary, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local naming row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from the displayed packet $A$. The coordinates list the regular source name, stream windows, dyadic endpoint rows, dyadic absolute-value rows, regular readback, real seal, transport, continuation, provenance, and local name.

The endpoint-and-tolerance obligation is inherited from the finite dyadic packet named in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicabsolutevalue-namecert}. Each observed endpoint in $D$ is routed through a visible $\DyadicAbsoluteValueUp$ row in $V$, and no order oracle or completed-real absolute value is consulted.

The remaining obligations are the regular and real-facing handoffs. The rows $H,C,P,N$ transport the same finite route, replay it for consumers, record the package provenance, and bind the local naming surface without adding quotient streams, selected limits, trichotomy, host absolute value, or ambient completeness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegularCauchyAbs RegSeqRat handoff]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-abs-regseqrat-handoff}
For every accepted $\RegularCauchyAbsUp$ carrier $A=(X,W,D,V,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $R$ is assembled only from the displayed source row $X$, the scheduled $\StreamNameUp$ windows $W$, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint rows $D$, and the $\DyadicAbsoluteValueUp$ tolerance rows $V$. A $\RegSeqRatUp$ consumer can read the absolute-value name only through those rows and through the stored $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ provenance.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The source regular name is visible only as $X$, and the finite observations of that name are the scheduled windows $W$ together with the endpoint rows $D$.

Apply the dyadic absolute-value route to each displayed endpoint row. The rows in $V$ supply the nonnegative endpoint and tolerance ledger for the same window schedule, so they are the only data available for building the absolute-value readback.

Repack the displayed absolute-value windows as the readback row $R$. The continuation routes in $C$ and package row $P$ record this handoff over the same $\BHist$ support, and the transport rows in $H$ preserve it componentwise for consumers.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegularCauchyAbs Real seal boundary]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-abs-real-seal-boundary}
Every $\RealUp$ consumer of an accepted $\RegularCauchyAbsUp$ carrier reaches the seal row $E$ only after the $\RegSeqRatUp$ absolute-value handoff $R$ has been produced from $X,W,D,V$. The real-facing boundary exposes the route through $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\DyadicAbsoluteValueUp$, and $\RegSeqRatUp$, and it exposes no quotient stream, host real absolute value, selected limit, trichotomy witness, or ambient completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a real-facing consumer. By \autoref{thm:regular-cauchy-abs-regseqrat-handoff}, the consumer cannot reach a regular absolute-value readback until the finite window route $X,W,D,V$ has been displayed.

The seal row $E$ is downstream of that readback. The stored continuation routes first replay the source windows, then the dyadic endpoint observations, then the $\DyadicAbsoluteValueUp$ tolerance rows, and only then the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff $R$ consumed by the real seal.

Since \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-abs-carrier} contains no separate coordinate for quotient streams, trichotomy, host absolute value, selected limits, or completeness, the $\RealUp$ seal boundary is exactly the displayed finite route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegularCauchyAbs non-escape boundary]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-abs-non-escape}
No accepted $\RegularCauchyAbsUp$ packet exports data outside the displayed tuple $X,W,D,V,R,E,H,C,P,N$. In particular, it cannot be used to recover a quotient of regular Cauchy streams, a host absolute-value operation, a trichotomy proof for real names, a selected limiting real, or a $\RealUp$ completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier of \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-abs-carrier}. Its only source data are the regular name, finite stream windows, dyadic endpoints, dyadic absolute-value rows, readback, seal, and structural rows.

Apply \autoref{thm:regular-cauchy-abs-namecert-obligations}. Every local obligation is a projection of those rows or a transport, continuation, provenance, or naming route for those rows.

Finally combine \autoref{thm:regular-cauchy-abs-regseqrat-handoff} with \autoref{thm:regular-cauchy-abs-real-seal-boundary}. All regular and real-facing consumers pass through $X,W,D,V,R,E,H,C,P,N$, so no unlisted analytic or quotient object can be extracted from the certificate.
\end{proof}
