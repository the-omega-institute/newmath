\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CauchyModulusExtractionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-cauchymodulusextraction-namecert}

The $\CauchyModulusExtractionUp$ packet records the finite observation-budget surface that a Real-completeness consumer uses before applying the diagonal route of \autoref{ch:visions-real-completeness}. It turns a regular Cauchy input over $\RegSeqRatUp$ into explicit witness schedules, selected stream windows, and dyadic tolerance rows, while keeping the resulting modulus read inside displayed $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, and $\NameCert$ data.

\begin{definition}[Cauchy modulus-extraction carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-modulus-extraction-carrier}
A $\CauchyModulusExtractionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
M=(E,B,W,S,\mathsf{Mu},Q,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $E$ is the positive tolerance request read through the $\RatUp$ and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ precision ledgers;
\item $B$ is the finite observation budget selected for the requested tolerance and diagonal scale;
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window family inspected under that budget;
\item $S$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ schedule row naming the regular Cauchy witnesses consumed by the diagonal route;
\item $\mathsf{Mu}$ is the extracted modulus row, recording the natural threshold produced from $E,B,W,S$;
\item $Q$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ comparison and half-radius ledger used to justify the threshold;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the tolerance request, budget, window family, schedule row, modulus row, dyadic ledger, package provenance, and local name;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes from tolerance to budget, from budget to windows, from windows to schedule reads, from schedule reads to $\mathsf{Mu}$, and from $\mathsf{Mu}$ to the $\RealUp$ diagonal consumer;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyModulusExtractionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only after transporting the same tolerance request, finite budget, inspected windows, schedule row, extracted modulus, and dyadic ledger to a common Real-completion surface. The carrier contains no host-level limit, no quotient stream equality, no ambient completeness theorem, and no modulus chosen outside the displayed finite budget.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy modulus-extraction NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-modulus-extraction-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\CauchyModulusExtractionUp}$ surface for \autoref{def:cauchy-modulus-extraction-carrier} consists exactly of five finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed packet $M=(E,B,W,S,\mathsf{Mu},Q,H,C,P,N)$;
\item budget exposure stating that $B$ is the only finite observation budget available for the tolerance request $E$;
\item schedule extraction stating that $W$ and $S$ are read through $\StreamNameUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$ rows before the modulus row $\mathsf{Mu}$ is exported;
\item dyadic soundness stating that $Q$ supplies the positive half-radius and comparison ledgers needed by the extracted threshold;
\item ledger exactness stating that all transport, continuation, package, and naming reads factor through $H,C,P,N$ and no external modulus selector is present.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:cauchy-modulus-extraction-carrier}. The tuple lists the tolerance request, finite budget, inspected windows, regular Cauchy schedule, extracted modulus, dyadic ledger, transports, continuations, provenance, and local name.

Budget exposure uses the row $B$. A consumer may inspect only the finite windows listed by that budget, so the modulus row is tied to displayed observation data rather than to an ambient convergence choice.

Schedule extraction uses $W$ and $S$. The $\StreamNameUp$ windows identify the inspected rational observations, while the $\RegSeqRatUp$ schedule row records the regular Cauchy witnesses consumed by the diagonal Real-completion route.

Dyadic soundness is the role of $Q$. It stores the half-radius split and comparison ledger that justify the threshold recorded in $\mathsf{Mu}$ for the requested tolerance.

Finally, $H,C,P,N$ repack the same rows under componentwise transport, route replay, package provenance, and local naming. Since no remaining coordinate contains an external modulus selector, these five obligations exhaust the local certificate surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy modulus-extraction budget soundness]
\label{thm:cauchy-modulus-extraction-budget-soundness}
For an accepted $\CauchyModulusExtractionUp$ carrier
$$
M=(E,B,W,S,\mathsf{Mu},Q,H,C,P,N),
$$
the extracted modulus row $\mathsf{Mu}$ is sound for every diagonal Real-completion consumer that reads the same finite budget $B$, $\StreamNameUp$ windows $W$, $\RegSeqRatUp$ witness schedule $S$, and dyadic comparison ledger $Q$. The consumer receives a natural threshold justified by the displayed observation budget and receives no host-selected convergence modulus, quotient stream representative, or ambient completeness witness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the tolerance request $E$ and the budget row $B$. The budget determines the finite observation family available to the extraction packet.

Read the window and schedule rows $W,S$. They identify the StreamName observations and RegSeqRat witnesses that the real-completeness diagonal route may consume for the requested scale.

Use the dyadic ledger $Q$ to justify the half-radius comparisons attached to those reads. The extracted row $\mathsf{Mu}$ records the resulting natural threshold and is transported by $H$ along the continuation routes in $C$.

The package and naming rows $P,N$ expose exactly that finite packet. Because the carrier has no coordinate for an external modulus, a host-selected limit, or quotient equality, the handoff to the $\RealUp$ diagonal consumer is precisely the displayed budget-sound modulus row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy modulus-extraction window schedule exhaustion]
\label{thm:cauchy-modulus-extraction-window-schedule-exhaustion}
For an accepted $\CauchyModulusExtractionUp$ carrier
$$

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

The window row $W$ is the only StreamName row in the packet. By the StreamName naming surface cited in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, window reads available to the consumer are therefore the reads displayed in $W$.

The schedule row $S$ is the only RegSeqRat schedule row in the packet. By the RegSeqRat naming surface cited in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, regular Cauchy witness reads available to the consumer are therefore the reads displayed in $S$.

The Real carrier surface cited in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert} receives the diagonal handoff through the continuation rows in $C$. Because those continuations start from the displayed budget, windows, and schedule row, no further schedule data can be introduced at the Real-completion boundary.

The carrier has no coordinate for a quotient stream, host-selected convergence modulus, selected limit, host real equality, or ambient completeness witness. Hence none of those objects can escape through the modulus row.
