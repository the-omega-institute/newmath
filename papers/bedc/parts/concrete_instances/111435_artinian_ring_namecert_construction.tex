\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ArtinianRingUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-artinian-ring-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Hopkins--Levitzki and the Atiyah--Macdonald Artinian ring tradition, the $\ArtinianRingUp$ packet records finite descending ideal-chain stabilization data over a ring, commutative-ring, and ideal source. In BEDC form it sits after $\NameCert_{\RingUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-ring-namecert}), $\NameCert_{\CommRingUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-commring-namecert}), and $\NameCert_{\IdealUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-ideal-namecert}), while complementing the ascending-chain sibling $\NameCert_{\NoetherianRingUp}$ of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-noetherian-ring-namecert}. The packet supplies a descending-chain source row for later algebra consumers; it does not assert module finite length, semisimplicity, quotient ideal spaces, maximal ideal choice, or the Hopkins--Levitzki theorem itself.

\section{Carrier Definition}
\label{sec:artinian-ring-carrier}

\begin{definition}[Artinian ring carrier]\label{def:artinian-ring-carrier}
An $\ArtinianRingUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
\begin{gathered}
\mathsf{ArtRing}(R,C,I,D,L,H,Q,P,N).
\end{gathered}
$$
The row $R$ is the ambient $\RingUp$ source surface. The row $C$ is the $\CommRingUp$ source surface when the consumer reads the commutative specialization. The row $I$ is the $\IdealUp$ source surface over the same classifier. The row $D$ is the descending-chain stabilization witness: every displayed descending chain of ideal rows over $R,C,I$ carries a finite stage after which all later ideal endpoints classify with that stage. The row $L$ is the finite-length ledger used by algebraic consumers that read descending stabilization together with the ascending-chain sibling of $\NoetherianRingUp$. The row $H$ is componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the ring, commutative-ring, ideal, stabilization, and finite-length rows. The row $Q$ is displayed $\Cont$ replay for the chain and length certificates. The row $P$ is $\Pkg$ provenance, and $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ArtinianRingUp}$ naming row.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Artinian ring NameCert obligations]\label{thm:artinian-ring-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\ArtinianRingUp$ carrier $\mathsf{ArtRing}(R,C,I,D,L,H,Q,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\ArtinianRingUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the nine displayed rows, source compatibility with $\RingUp$, $\CommRingUp$, and $\IdealUp$, descending-chain stabilization access through $D$, finite-length ledger access through $L$, componentwise classifier transport through $H$, displayed continuation replay through $Q$, provenance non-escape through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the rows of \autoref{def:artinian-ring-carrier}. The source rows $R,C,I$ fix the ring, commutative-ring, and ideal classifiers before any chain is inspected.

The stabilization row $D$ is then read over that shared source surface. The finite-length ledger $L$ is available only as a displayed ledger attached to the same classifier, so it cannot replace the descending-chain witness by a host theorem.

Finally $H,Q,P,N$ provide componentwise transport, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming. Since no quotient ideal space, maximal ideal selector, semisimplicity proof, or Hopkins--Levitzki theorem row is present, the listed coordinates exhaust the NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Artinian descending-chain stability]\label{thm:artinian-ring-descending-chain-stability}
Let $\mathsf{ArtRing}(R,C,I,D,L,H,Q,P,N)$ be an accepted $\ArtinianRingUp$ carrier. For every displayed descending chain of ideal rows over the shared $\RingUp$, $\CommRingUp$, and $\IdealUp$ classifier, the witness $D$ supplies a finite stabilization stage, and $H$ transports that stabilization along the same classifier. Any downstream algebra consumer may therefore use $\ArtinianRingUp$ as a finite descending ideal-chain certificate without re-proving stabilization inside its own packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the source compatibility rows from \autoref{thm:artinian-ring-namecert-obligations}. They force the descending ideal chain to be read over the ring, commutative-ring, and ideal classifiers already present in $R,C,I$.

Apply the stabilization witness $D$ to obtain a finite stage for the displayed descending chain. If the accepted packet is transported through a classified replacement, use the componentwise transport row $H$ to move both the chain entries and the stabilization witness through the shared classifier.

The transported stage remains a stage for the same finite chain surface because $Q$ replays only the displayed continuation data and $P$ prevents provenance escape. The conclusion is therefore a reusable finite descending ideal-chain certificate that can be paired with the ascending-chain surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-noetherian-ring-namecert}.
\end{proof}
