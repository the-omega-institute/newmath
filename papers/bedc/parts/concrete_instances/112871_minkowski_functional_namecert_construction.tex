\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\MinkowskiFunctionalUp$}

Following Bourbaki and Rockafellar on locally convex spaces, the $\MinkowskiFunctionalUp$ packet records the gauge functional of a displayed absorbing balanced convex neighbourhood as finite NameCert data. It sits over the convex-set source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-convexset-namecert}, the topological-vector-space operations of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-topvecspace-namecert}, the locally convex neighbourhood ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locally-convex-space-namecert}, the seminormed-space consumer surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-seminormed-space-namecert}, and the terminal scalar seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet names the finite route by which a gauge-bound row is exposed; it does not import host real infima, quotient vector equality, choice of a neighbourhood basis, Hahn--Banach separation, or ambient completeness.

\begin{definition}[Minkowski functional carrier]
\label{def:minkowski-functional-carrier}
A $\MinkowskiFunctionalUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
G=(X,V,A,B,L,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the $\ConvexSetUp$ source row naming the displayed convex subset whose gauge is being read.
\item $V$ is the $\TopVecSpaceUp$ operation row for addition, zero, negation, scalar action, and topological transport on the displayed vector carrier.
\item $A$ is the absorbing-neighbourhood ledger row, recording the finite scale witnesses by which each inspected vector is admitted into a scalar multiple of $X$.
\item $B$ is the balanced-neighbourhood ledger row, recording the scalar-stability witnesses needed before the gauge read may be transported.
\item $L$ is the $\LocallyConvexSpaceUp$ neighbourhood row tying $X,A,B$ to the displayed locally convex source surface.
\item $R$ is the $\RealUp$ gauge-bound row, exposing the finite scalar bound obtained from the displayed absorbing and balanced ledgers.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the convex source, vector-space operations, absorbing ledger, balanced ledger, locally convex row, scalar bound, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a vector request through $V,X,A,B,L,R$ and back to the public gauge-bound boundary.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\MinkowskiFunctionalUp$, $\ConvexSetUp$, $\TopVecSpaceUp$, $\LocallyConvexSpaceUp$, $\SeminormedSpaceUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MinkowskiFunctionalUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $X,V,A,B,L,R,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for a host infimum, quotient-vector equality, selected neighbourhood basis, separation theorem, ambient topological-vector-space completion, or standard bridge.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Minkowski functional NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:minkowski-functional-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\MinkowskiFunctionalUp$ carrier $G=(X,V,A,B,L,R,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\MinkowskiFunctionalUp}$ surface consists exactly of convex-source admission through $X$, topological-vector-space operation exposure through $V$, absorbing-ledger exposure through $A$, balanced-ledger exposure through $B$, locally convex neighbourhood routing through $L$, scalar gauge-bound sealing through $R$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.

No NameCert obligation is stored outside those displayed rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier. The analytic source rows are precisely the convex source $X$, the topological-vector-space operations $V$, the absorbing ledger $A$, the balanced ledger $B$, the locally convex neighbourhood row $L$, and the real scalar bound $R$.

The gauge route first reads $X$ inside the vector-space operation surface $V$. Absorption and balancedness are then visible as finite ledger rows, so the scalar bound is read only after the neighbourhood evidence has been displayed.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite route. Since no excluded host analytic object is a coordinate of the packet, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Minkowski functional gauge route]
\label{thm:minkowski-functional-gauge-route}
Every $\SeminormedSpaceUp$ or locally convex consumer of an accepted $\MinkowskiFunctionalUp$ packet receives the gauge-bound read only through the finite route
$$
\ConvexSetUp,\TopVecSpaceUp
\longmapsto A,B
\longmapsto \LocallyConvexSpaceUp
\longmapsto \RealUp
\longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
Thus the public boundary is a displayed RealUp scalar-bound row generated from the convex, absorbing, and balanced neighbourhood ledgers; it is not a host real infimum, quotient-vector equality, selected basis element, Hahn--Banach separation result, or completed seminorm theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the convex and vector-space rows. The source set $X$ is read as a displayed convex packet, while $V$ supplies only the finite operation and topology rows needed by the inspected vector request.

Next read the neighbourhood ledgers. The absorbing row $A$ records the available scale witnesses, and the balanced row $B$ records scalar stability. The locally convex row $L$ ties those two ledgers to the same neighbourhood surface.

The scalar read is then sealed by $R$. It is a $\RealUp$ gauge-bound row obtained from the displayed ledgers, not an imported infimum over all host scalars.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same route for downstream locally convex and seminormed consumers. None of the excluded analytic objects appears as a carrier coordinate, so no consumer can read them from this packet.
\end{proof}
