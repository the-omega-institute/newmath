\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RegularCauchySubsequenceStabilityUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-subsequence-stability-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges' constructive analysis, the $\RegularCauchySubsequenceStabilityUp$ packet records the finite certificate surface for the standard fact that a monotone cofinal subsequence of a regular Cauchy rational name remains regular and presents the same real-name boundary. It consumes the subsequence interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-subsequence-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the finite stream windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the terminal seal surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet is a finite BEDC handoff for Phase Real and Completion consumers; it does not choose a subsequence by countable choice, quotient streams, or invoke ambient completeness.

\begin{definition}[RegularCauchySubsequenceStability carrier]
\label{def:regular-cauchy-subsequence-stability-carrier}
A $\RegularCauchySubsequenceStabilityUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
G=(S,T,W,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the source regular Cauchy packet, carrying the accepted $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback rows and the source finite stream observations.
\item $T$ is a monotone-cofinal schedule row, recording the unary reindexing route by which each selected subsequence window is reached from an inspected source window.
\item $W$ records the finite $\StreamNameUp$ source and subsequence windows compared by the packet.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, carrying dyadic radii, endpoint comparisons, and the transported regularity budgets.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row for the subsequence window after transport along $T$.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal boundary exposed only after the source, schedule, window, dyadic, and readback rows have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the source packet, schedule, windows, tolerance ledger, readback row, seal boundary, continuation routes, provenance, and local certificate.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the same finite route.
\item $P$ records the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RegularCauchySubsequenceStabilityUp$, $\RegularCauchySubsequenceUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchySubsequenceStabilityUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only by $S,T,W,D,R,E,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for quotient stream equality, a choice-selected infinite subsequence, host sequence equality, host real equality, or a completeness principle for $\RealUp$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[RegularCauchySubsequenceStability NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-subsequence-stability-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RegularCauchySubsequenceStabilityUp$ carrier
$$
G=(S,T,W,D,R,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchySubsequenceStabilityUp}$ surface consists exactly of the source regular Cauchy rows $S$, the monotone-cofinal schedule $T$, the finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows $W$, the transported $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger $D$, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ subsequence readback $R$, the $\RealUp$ seal boundary $E$, the componentwise transport row $H$, the continuation replay row $C$, the package provenance row $P$, and the local naming row $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier exposure is projection from \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-subsequence-stability-carrier}. Each row in the statement is one displayed coordinate of the finite packet, and the definition lists no hidden coordinate for an externally selected subsequence or completion object.

The source and schedule obligations are $S$ and $T$. The finite-window obligation is $W$, while the dyadic tolerance and regular rational readback obligations are $D$ and $R$. These are the rows that express the usual cofinal-subsequence regularity argument inside the BEDC packet.

Transport, replay, provenance, and local naming are supplied by $H,C,P,N$. Repacking these projections yields the displayed $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchySubsequenceStabilityUp}$ surface, with no quotient stream, host equality, or ambient completeness datum.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegularCauchySubsequenceStability Real seal boundary]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-subsequence-stability-real-seal-boundary}
An accepted $\RegularCauchySubsequenceStabilityUp$ carrier may expose its $\RealUp$ seal row $E$ only after the source regular Cauchy data $S$, the monotone-cofinal schedule $T$, the finite stream windows $W$, the transported dyadic tolerance ledger $D$, and the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row $R$ have been displayed through $H$ and $C$. Thus cofinal subsequence transport preserves the real-name seal surface as finite BEDC data and does not export quotient streams, a choice-selected subsequence, host sequence equality, or ambient real completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted carrier. The seal row $E$ is not a source coordinate; it is reached after the source, schedule, window, dyadic, readback, transport, continuation, provenance, and naming rows have been projected.

Apply the NameCert obligation surface of \autoref{thm:regular-cauchy-subsequence-stability-namecert-obligations}. It identifies the monotone-cofinal schedule, the inspected and selected finite windows, the transported dyadic tolerance ledger, and the regular rational readback as the only data available before the seal boundary.

The ordinary constructive subsequence argument is therefore recorded as carrier projection followed by cofinal schedule transport and finite-window replay. Since the classifier has no coordinate for quotient streams, choice-selected subsequences, host sequence equality, host real equality, or a completeness principle, none of those data can escape through the $\RealUp$ seal row.
\end{proof}
