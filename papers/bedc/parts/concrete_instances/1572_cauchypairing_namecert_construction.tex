The $\CauchyPairingUp$ interface records the Bishop--Bridges constructive-analysis pairing of two regular Cauchy names as one synchronized finite-window packet. It is a name-certificate source for downstream diagonal, completion, and real-seal chapters: consumers may inspect shared windows, dyadic ledgers, modulus schedules, and the paired $\RealUp$ seal, but they do not receive quotient equality of reals, selected host limits, or ambient completeness.

\begin{definition}[Cauchy pairing NameCert source]
\label{def:cauchy-pairing-namecert-source}
A $\CauchyPairingUp$ source packet is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ row
$$
K=(A,B,W_A,W_B,L_A,L_B,\mu_A,\mu_B,\mu,E_A,E_B,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ and $B$ are the two $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular Cauchy names being paired;
\item $W_A$ and $W_B$ are finite $\StreamNameUp$ window schedules for the two names, recorded on a common unary request ledger;
\item $L_A$ and $L_B$ are the corresponding $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observation ledgers, read through the displayed $\RegSeqRatUp$ rows;
\item $\mu_A$ and $\mu_B$ are the two $\CauchyModulusUp$ schedules, and $\mu$ is the synchronized schedule that requests a common finite window large enough for both sides;
\item $E_A$ and $E_B$ are the source $\RealUp$ seal rows attached to the two regular names;
\item $E$ is the paired $\RealUp$ seal row reached only through the synchronized windows and the two dyadic ledgers;
\item $H$ stores the componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the names, windows, dyadic ledgers, modulus rows, and seal rows;
\item $C$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which a consumer request reaches the synchronized schedule, both selected windows, both dyadic ledgers, and the paired seal;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\CauchyModulusUp$, $\RealUp$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyPairingUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through these displayed rows and their stored transports.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy pairing NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-pairing-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\CauchyPairingUp}$ surface for the source packet of \autoref{def:cauchy-pairing-namecert-source} consists of five rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission: the accepted object is the displayed finite packet $K$;
\item classifier stability: paired packets are compared componentwise by the two $\RegSeqRatUp$ names, the synchronized $\StreamNameUp$ windows, the two $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledgers, the three $\CauchyModulusUp$ schedules, and the three $\RealUp$ seal rows;
\item synchronized-window exactness: every consumer precision request first reads $\mu$, then reads the corresponding windows in $W_A$ and $W_B$, and only then reads the dyadic ledgers $L_A$ and $L_B$;
\item modulus compatibility: the synchronized schedule $\mu$ is accepted only as a finite ledger row that dominates the displayed side schedules $\mu_A$ and $\mu_B$ on the inspected requests;
\item seal non-escape: the paired $\RealUp$ seal $E$ is consumed only after both source readbacks, their finite windows, and their dyadic ledgers have been displayed.
\end{enumerate}
This surface specifies synchronized finite-window pairing and does not assert quotient equality of completed reals or completeness of $\RealUp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is the projection of the packet $K$. All names, windows, ledgers, modulus schedules, seal rows, transports, continuation routes, package provenance, and local naming data are stored coordinates.

Classifier stability is componentwise. The displayed $\hsame$ rows transport the two regular names, the two window schedules, the two dyadic ledgers, the three modulus rows, and the three seal rows, while the displayed $\Cont$ rows preserve the consumer read order.

For synchronized-window exactness, a precision request must pass through $\mu$ before either side can be inspected. The two selected window rows are then read through $W_A$ and $W_B$, and their rational observations are the dyadic ledger rows $L_A$ and $L_B$.

The modulus compatibility and seal rows use the same finite route. Because the packet has no coordinate for quotient equality, selected host limits, host real equality, or ambient completeness, the paired $\RealUp$ seal cannot export data outside the displayed certificate surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy pairing synchronized-window stability]
\label{thm:cauchy-pairing-synchronized-window-stability}
Let $K$ and $K'$ be accepted $\CauchyPairingUp$ source packets. If their two $\RegSeqRatUp$ names, synchronized $\StreamNameUp$ windows, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledgers, $\CauchyModulusUp$ schedules, $\RealUp$ seal rows, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ rows are transported componentwise by displayed $\hsame$ rows and read through the displayed $\Cont$ routes, then every finite consumer request follows matched synchronized windows on both sides and reaches matched paired $\RealUp$ seal rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project both accepted packets by \autoref{def:cauchy-pairing-namecert-source}. The classifier can inspect only the two regular names, the selected finite windows, the dyadic ledgers, the modulus rows, the seal rows, and the recorded package and naming provenance.

Read a consumer request through the synchronized schedule. The request selects a common window budget before either source name is inspected, so the two side reads remain aligned under the same finite $\StreamNameUp$ ledger.

Transport the selected side rows along the stored $\hsame$ comparisons and $\Cont$ routes. The two dyadic ledgers remain attached to their selected windows, and the paired seal remains downstream of those two readbacks.

Thus matched packets expose matched finite-window routes for every inspected request. The conclusion is a stability statement about the displayed BEDC certificate rows and does not introduce quotient equality, host limits, or an ambient completeness claim.
\end{proof}
