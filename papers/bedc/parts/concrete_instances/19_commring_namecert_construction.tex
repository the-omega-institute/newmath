\begin{theorem}[CommRing zero linear factor forces equal squares]
\label{thm:commring-zero-linear-factor-equal-squares}
For BEDC history operations $add$, $mul$, $neg$, and $zero$ satisfying the
$\RingUp$ additive abelian-group laws, addition and multiplication congruence,
left distributivity, the $\RingUp$ negative-product laws, and the
$\CommRingUp$ multiplicative commutativity field used in
\autoref{thm:commring-difference-of-squares-zero-exact}, every
$a,b : \mathsf{BHist}$ satisfies
$$
\bigl((a+b)\hsame0 \lor (a+(-b))\hsame0\bigr)
\Longrightarrow
a\cdot a\hsame b\cdot b .
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The commutative-ring source first supplies the right distributive law from the
left distributive law by \autoref{thm:commring-right-distrib-from-left}. Thus
the zero-factor absorption theorem may be read with both distributive inputs
available.

If the chosen zero factor is $a+b$, apply
\autoref{thm:ring-zero-classifier-factor-absorption} with $x=a+b$ and
$y=a+(-b)$. Its left-product conclusion gives
$(a+b)\cdot(a+(-b))\hsame0$.

If the chosen zero factor is $a+(-b)$, apply the same theorem with
$x=a+(-b)$ and $y=a+b$. Its right-product conclusion gives the same oriented
product-zero statement $(a+b)\cdot(a+(-b))\hsame0$.

In either case the forward direction of
\autoref{thm:commring-difference-of-squares-zero-exact} sends that product-zero
witness to $a\cdot a\hsame b\cdot b$.
\end{proof}

The ten-field fit is: traditional target zero linear factor implies equal
squares in a commutative ring; BEDC source $\RingUp$ plus $\CommRingUp$ over
$\mathsf{BHist}$; carrier $\mathsf{BHist}$; classifier $\hsame$; stability
from zero-factor absorption and difference-of-squares exactness; ledger the
chosen zero factor and product-zero witness; core theorem this block; exactness
equality of squares; standard bridge ordinary commutative-ring factor argument;
status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.

\begin{theorem}[CommRing equal squares force signed factor zero]
\label{thm:commring-equal-squares-signed-factor-zero}
For BEDC history operations $add$, $mul$, $neg$, and $zero$ satisfying the
$\RingUp$ additive abelian-group laws, addition and multiplication
congruence, left distributivity, multiplicative commutativity, and the
$\RingUp$ negative-product laws, every $a,b : \mathsf{BHist}$ satisfies
$$
a\cdot a\hsame b\cdot b
\Longrightarrow
(a+b)\cdot(a+(-b))\hsame0 .
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply Theorem~\ref{thm:commring-difference-of-squares} to identify the signed
factor product with $(a\cdot a)+(-(b\cdot b))$.

Transport the assumed square equality $a\cdot a\hsame b\cdot b$ through
additive congruence while keeping the right summand $-(b\cdot b)$ fixed. The
right endpoint becomes $(b\cdot b)+(-(b\cdot b))$.

Theorem~\ref{thm:ring-add-right-inverse} collapses this additive inverse pair
to $0$. Transitivity of $\hsame$ composes the difference-of-squares
identification, the transported square equality, and the inverse collapse.
\end{proof}
