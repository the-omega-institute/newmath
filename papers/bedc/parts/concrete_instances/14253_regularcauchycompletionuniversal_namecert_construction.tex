\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RegularCauchyCompletionUniversalUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-completion-universal-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges, Constructive Analysis, the $\RegularCauchyCompletionUniversalUp$ packet isolates the regular-Cauchy universal-property seed for the L10 Real and Completion route. It refines the general $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$ surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completionuniversalproperty-namecert} by forcing every extension and uniqueness read through the finite regular-name chain already exposed by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-realcompletionwitnessextractor-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regularcauchyequivalence-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}.

The purpose of the packet is local. It names the rows needed for extending a regular-Cauchy source route into the terminal $\RealUp$ seal and for comparing any two such extensions by the displayed $\hsame$ ledger. It does not claim the full ambient completeness of $\RealUp$, nor does it replace the mature general completion chapter.

\begin{definition}[RegularCauchyCompletionUniversal carrier]
\label{def:regular-cauchy-completion-universal-carrier}
A $\RegularCauchyCompletionUniversalUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
U=(S,W,D,R,A,E,L,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the regular source row whose admissible names are read as $\RegSeqRatUp$ data;
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window schedule used by the same regular source;
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger attached to those windows;
\item $R$ is the $\RegularCauchyEquivalenceUp$ comparison row for replacing regular names without leaving the finite route;
\item $A$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal reached through the displayed regular source, window, tolerance, and equivalence rows;
\item $E$ is the extension route row, read as the $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$ extension face restricted to $S,W,D,R,A$;
\item $L$ is the uniqueness $\hsame$ ledger comparing two extension routes over the same regular source, finite windows, dyadic ledger, equivalence row, and terminal seal;
\item $H$ records the componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $S,W,D,R,A,E,L$;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes that replay the extension and uniqueness reads;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$, $\RealCompletionWitnessExtractorUp$, $\RegularCauchyEquivalenceUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyCompletionUniversalUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The accepted carrier stores no quotient stream, no selected host limit, no host real equality row, no countable-choice selector, and no ambient $\RealUp$ completeness coordinate.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Regular extension route]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-completion-universal-extension-route}
Let $U=(S,W,D,R,A,E,L,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\RegularCauchyCompletionUniversalUp$ packet. Every extension consumer of $E$ first reads the regular source $S$, then the finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows $W$, then the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger $D$, then the $\RegularCauchyEquivalenceUp$ comparison row $R$, and only then reaches the terminal $\RealUp$ seal $A$ and the restricted $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$ extension face.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-completion-universal-carrier}. The extension row $E$ is not stored as a detached completion theorem; it appears after the regular source, the scheduled finite windows, the dyadic ledger, the equivalence row, and the terminal Real seal.

Read the sibling surfaces in the displayed order. The StreamName, RegSeqRat, DyadicRatCore, RegularCauchyEquivalence, and Real chapters provide exactly the finite route named by $S,W,D,R,A$, while the general completion chapter supplies the extension face only after that route has been exposed.

The continuation row $C$ can replay only those displayed reads, and the provenance row $P$ records no selected limit or host completion object. Therefore every extension consumer factors through the regular-Cauchy route before it reaches the universal-property face.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Regular uniqueness ledger]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-completion-universal-uniqueness-ledger}
For accepted packets $U=(S,W,D,R,A,E,L,H,C,P,N)$ and $U'=(S,W,D,R,A,E',L',H',C',P,N')$ over the same regular source, finite windows, dyadic ledger, equivalence row, terminal seal, and provenance, any comparison between the exported extension routes is exhausted by the displayed uniqueness ledgers $L$ and $L'$ together with their componentwise $\hsame$ transports. No quotient-stream equality, selected host limit, host real equality, countable choice, or ambient completeness row can contribute to the comparison.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the shared coordinates $S,W,D,R,A,P$. They fix the regular source, scheduled windows, tolerance ledger, regular-Cauchy comparison row, and terminal Real seal for both packets.

The only coordinates that can distinguish the two extension exports are $E,L,H,C,N$ and $E',L',H',C',N'$. The ledgers $L$ and $L'$ compare the extension rows over the same shared route, while $H,H'$ and $C,C'$ display the permitted transports and replays.

Since neither carrier contains a quotient stream, selected host limit, host real equality row, countable-choice selector, or ambient completeness coordinate, no external equality principle can enter the comparison. The uniqueness judgement is exactly the local $\hsame$ ledger over the shared regular-Cauchy route.
\end{proof}
