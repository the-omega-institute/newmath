It reads the nested interval carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-nestedinterval-namecert}, the located endpoint surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedinterval-namecert}, the dyadic interval ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicinterval-namecert}, finite stream observations from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, regular rational readback from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal real seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. $$

\begin{enumerate}

\item $L$ is the $\LocatedIntervalUp$ endpoint-location row for the same displayed prefix.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicIntervalUp$ row containing the dyadic endpoint boxes, inclusion checks, and width comparisons used by the finite prefix.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window row through which candidate real-name observations are read at the displayed precisions.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row extracted from the windows and dyadic boxes.

The classifier compares accepted packets only through these rows and their replay paths. \begin{theorem}[NestedIntervalCompactness finite-intersection window]
\label{thm:nested-interval-compactness-finite-intersection-window}
Let $K=(I,L,D,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\NestedIntervalCompactnessUp$ carrier. Every finite prefix request for the nested family reads the displayed route
$$
I,L,D \longmapsto W \longmapsto R \longmapsto E \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
Consequently the finite-window intersection witness available to a Real-facing consumer is the regular-rational readback over the same displayed dyadic and located interval rows. The witness is not a selected point of the full countable intersection and is not an open-cover compactness certificate.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.NestedIntervalCompactnessUp.NestedIntervalCompactnessCarrier\_finite\_intersection\_window}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:nested-interval-compactness-carrier}. The prefix request first exposes $I$, then the located endpoint row $L$, and then the dyadic interval row $D$ for the same finite prefix.

The continuation row $C$ routes the request from those interval rows into the stream windows $W$ and the regular rational readback $R$. Thus the Real-facing observation is read from displayed finite data rather than from an ambient completeness or compactness principle.

Finally $E,H,P,N$ seal, transport, source, and name that route. Since no row contains a selected endpoint of the full intersection, quotient real equality, countable choice, or open-cover data, the finite prefix witness is exactly the displayed finite-window route.
\end{proof}
