\begin{definition}[Axis boundary limit-refusal carrier]
\label{def:axis-boundary-limit-refusal-carrier}
An $\AxisBoundaryLimitRefusalUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
B=(Z,L,R,Q,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $Z$ is the AxisZeckendorf boundary-token row, including the
$\mathtt{boundary\_01}$ address from the cannot-claim registry;
\item $L$ is the limit-refusal row, recording that the boundary token is not a
limit or supremum carrier;
\item $R$ is the Real-facing refusal row, recording that the same boundary
does not provide $\RealUp$ equality, a Cauchy limit, or a completion row;
\item $Q$ is the completeness-refusal row, recording that no total completeness
principle is exported by the packet;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports preserving the boundary,
limit-refusal, Real-facing refusal, completeness-refusal, provenance, and
local naming rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which a consumer reads
$Z,L,R,Q,H,P,N$ without leaving the finite packet;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\AxisZeckendorfCannotClaimUp$,
$\DimLiftBoundaryUp$, $\FullAxisRealRefusalUp$, $\AxisNatReplacementRefusalUp$,
$\ZeckendorfCarryNormalizationUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and
$\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\AxisBoundaryLimitRefusalUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exports only this boundary-token refusal packet. It contains no
limit, supremum, $\RealUp$ equality, Cauchy completion, completeness principle,
or bridge to an ambient real object.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Axis boundary limit-refusal NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:axis-boundary-limit-refusal-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:axis-boundary-limit-refusal-carrier}, the
local $\NameCert_{\AxisBoundaryLimitRefusalUp}$ surface consists exactly of
five finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed packet $B=(Z,L,R,Q,H,C,P,N)$;
\item boundary-token exposure, stating that $Z$ is the only AxisZeckendorf
boundary row exported by the packet;
\item limit-refusal exactness, stating that $L$ refuses limit and supremum
readings of the boundary token;
\item Real and completeness refusal, stating that $R,Q$ refuse $\RealUp$
equality, Cauchy completion, and completeness readings;
\item local naming exhaustion, stating that $H,C,P,N$ preserve and name only
the displayed refusal rows.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection of the tuple in
\autoref{def:axis-boundary-limit-refusal-carrier}. The rows
$Z,L,R,Q,H,C,P,N$ are all displayed coordinates of one finite packet.

Boundary-token exposure reads $Z$ against the cannot-claim registry of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-axis-zeckendorf-cannot-claim}. The packet has no
second boundary coordinate from which a limit object could be selected.

The refusal rows are separate. The row $L$ blocks limit and supremum readings,
while $R$ and $Q$ block $\RealUp$ equality, Cauchy completion, and
completeness readings.

Finally, $H,C,P,N$ transport, route, cite, and name those same rows. Since no
coordinate names a limit, supremum, real equality, completion, or completeness
object, the five obligations exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Axis boundary limit-refusal non-escape]
\label{thm:axis-boundary-limit-refusal-nonescape}
Every consumer read of an accepted $\AxisBoundaryLimitRefusalUp$ packet
factors through
$$
Z \to L \to R \to Q \to H \to C \to P \to N.
$$
The read may expose the boundary token and its refusal rows, but it admits no
consumer route to a limit, supremum, $\RealUp$ equality, Cauchy completion, or
completeness row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier of \autoref{def:axis-boundary-limit-refusal-carrier}.
It lists the boundary token, limit-refusal row, Real-facing refusal row,
completeness-refusal row, transport, continuation, provenance, and local name
as the whole packet.

Apply \autoref{thm:axis-boundary-limit-refusal-namecert-obligations}. The
boundary-token exposure and refusal exactness clauses force every consumer
read through $Z,L,R,Q$ before the transport and provenance rows are consumed.

The sibling packets
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dimliftboundary-namecert} and
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-fullaxisrealrefusal-namecert} provide broader
refusal contexts, but they do not add a hidden limit or real-completion row to
this carrier. Thus the displayed route exhausts the accepted packet.
\end{proof}
