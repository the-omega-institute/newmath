It sits beside the export surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-registry-export-surface-namecert}, the cannot-claim registry of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-axiszeckendorfcannotclaim-namecert}, the verifier boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-largemodeloutputverifier-namecert}, the focused ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-focused-multi-hist-ledger-namecert}, and the GroundCompiler recognition packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-groundcompilerrecognition-namecert}. \begin{definition}[Core registry index carrier]
\label{def:core-registry-index-carrier}
A $\CoreRegistryIndexUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
R=(C,T,G,D,S,F,U,O,A,H,K,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $C$ is the concept-registry row, naming BEDC concepts and their package surfaces;
\item $T$ is the theorem-registry row, naming theorem entries, proof sites, and checked or unclosed status;
\item $G$ is the gap-registry row, naming open gaps, refusal sites, and boundary items;
\item $D$ is the tradition-registry row, recording inherited mathematical or philosophical entry surfaces;
\item $S$ is the scientific-claim row, recording empirical or model-facing claims with their audit boundaries;
\item $F$ is the refusal row, recording blocked export, cannot-claim, unavailable-evidence, or policy refusal entries;
\item $U$ is the route and upgrade row, recording how a registry entry may move to a stronger closure surface;
\item $O$ is the ordinary-language row, recording entry points by which a reader reaches a finite registry entry;
\item $A$ is the audit row, recording export checks, consistency gates, and machine-readable review data;
\item $H$ is the componentwise $\hsame$ transport row for all displayed registry rows;
\item $K$ is the displayed $\Cont$ continuation row through the concept, theorem, gap, tradition, scientific-claim, refusal, route, ordinary-language, and audit entries;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RegistryExportSurfaceUp$, $\AxisZeckendorfCannotClaimUp$, $\LargeModelOutputVerifierUp$, $\mathsf{FocusedMultiHistLedger}^{\uparrow}$, $\GroundCompilerRecognitionUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CoreRegistryIndexUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for an unlicensed export, hidden total index, theorem-content synthesis, erased refusal, audit-free route, or ordinary-language entry point outside the finite packet $R=(C,T,G,D,S,F,U,O,A,H,K,P,N)$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Core registry index NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:core-registry-index-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:core-registry-index-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\CoreRegistryIndexUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $R=(C,T,G,D,S,F,U,O,A,H,K,P,N)$; registry exposure for concept, theorem, gap, tradition, scientific-claim, refusal, route, and ordinary-language rows; audit exposure through $A$; componentwise transport through $H$; displayed continuation through $K$; package provenance through $P$; and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CoreRegistryIndexUp.CoreRegistryIndexTasteGate\_single\_carrier\_alignment}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier definition. The finite packet displays every registry table named by the core-registry surface together with audit, transport, continuation, package, and local naming rows.

The sibling chapters provide only bounded surfaces. The export surface controls public registry export, the cannot-claim packet supplies refusal discipline, the large-model verifier controls checked machine output, the focused multi-Hist ledger supplies finite history focus, and the GroundCompiler recognition packet supplies recognized machine-facing rows.

Rows $A,H,K,P,N$ bind those registry rows as one NameCert surface. Since no row names a hidden total index or theorem-content generator, the obligations are exactly the displayed finite registry packet and its audit, transport, continuation, provenance, and naming data.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Core registry index nonescape]
\label{thm:core-registry-index-nonescape}
Every public consumer of an accepted $\CoreRegistryIndexUp$ packet must read the concept, theorem, gap, tradition, scientific-claim, refusal, route, ordinary-language, audit, transport, continuation, provenance, and local NameCert rows together. Removing the refusal row $F$ or audit row $A$ exits the accepted registry index and cannot be repaired by transport, continuation, package provenance, or local naming alone.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:core-registry-index-namecert-obligations}. They require the registry rows $C,T,G,D,S,F,U,O$ and the audit row $A$ before the packet may be exported as $\CoreRegistryIndexUp$.

The export surface and cannot-claim sibling chapters keep exported rows and blocked rows distinct. The verifier, focused-ledger, and recognition siblings keep machine-output, history-focus, and recognition data bounded by their displayed rows.

Thus a consumer that removes $F$ loses the refusal boundary, and a consumer that removes $A$ loses the export audit. The support rows $H,K,P,N$ preserve an admitted packet; they do not synthesize the missing refusal or audit surface.
\end{proof}
