\begin{definition}[FiniteRecognizerCarrierObstruction carrier]
\label{def:finite-recognizer-carrier-obstruction-carrier}

$$
O=(A,G,Q,F,R,T,K,H,C,P,N)

with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ is the recognizer-acceptance row consumed from $\GroundCompilerRecognizerFlowUp$.
\item $G$ is the generated-recognizer mediation row, recording that the accepted recognizer is the generated object read by the compiler-facing flow.
\item $Q$ is the certificate-gate row, recording that the recognizer output has passed the displayed gate before any carrier comparison is attempted.
\item $F$ is the finite tuple extraction row consumed through $\FiniteReflectionTupleUp$.
\item $R$ is the component readback row, exposing the $\BHist$ components recovered from the finite tuple image.
\item $T$ is the componentwise $\hsame$ and $\NameCert$ transport row for the recovered components.

\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteRecognizerCarrierObstructionUp}$ row.

The carrier does not construct a recognizer-to-carrier map, does not assert unrestricted beta-conversion, and does not supply a $\StdBridge$ theorem. It only records the finite landing rows that a later construction must expose before those questions are reached.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[FiniteRecognizerCarrierObstruction NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:finite-recognizer-carrier-obstruction-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\FiniteRecognizerCarrierObstructionUp$ carrier $O=(A,G,Q,F,R,T,K,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteRecognizerCarrierObstructionUp}$ surface consists exactly of recognizer acceptance, generated-recognizer mediation, certificate-gate admission, finite tuple extraction, component readback, componentwise $\hsame$ and $\NameCert$ transport, $\ChapterTasteGate$ exposure, componentwise transport, replay, provenance, and local naming.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier. The first seven rows $A,G,Q,F,R,T,K$ are the recognizer-to-carrier landing surface, while $H,C,P,N$ are the structural rows that preserve and expose that surface.

Each obligation is read from its displayed row. Acceptance comes from $A$, generated-recognizer mediation from $G$, gate admission from $Q$, tuple extraction from $F$, readback from $R$, componentwise transport from $T$, and chapter-facing gate exposure from $K$.

No obligation row supplies a recognizer-to-carrier map or a standard bridge. The packet therefore isolates the obstruction boundary rather than resolving it.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FiniteRecognizerCarrierObstruction missing-row refusal]
\label{thm:finite-recognizer-carrier-obstruction-missing-row-refusal}
An attempted recognizer-to-carrier read that omits any one of $A,G,Q,F,R,T,K$ fails inside the $\FiniteRecognizerCarrierObstructionUp$ packet before the $\StdBridge$ question is reached. The failure is a missing landing row, not a failed bridge theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the obligation surface of \autoref{thm:finite-recognizer-carrier-obstruction-namecert-obligations}. It lists the seven landing rows as jointly required before the carrier-facing surface is displayed.

If one row is absent, the replay route $C$ cannot traverse the full path from recognizer acceptance to $\ChapterTasteGate$. The packet then records the absence as an obstruction at the landing surface.

Since the packet has no $\StdBridge$ theorem coordinate, such a failure occurs before bridge comparison and cannot be read as a bridge counterexample.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FiniteRecognizerCarrierObstruction sibling nonbijection]
\label{thm:finite-recognizer-carrier-obstruction-sibling-nonbijection}
The carrier of \autoref{def:finite-recognizer-carrier-obstruction-carrier} is not bijective, row for row, with $\GroundCompilerRecognizerFlowUp$, $\FiniteReflectionTupleUp$, $\MetaCICNormalizationCertificateRouterUp$, or $\KernelInductiveAcceptanceUp$. Any row-preserving bijection would have to preserve recognizer acceptance, generated-recognizer mediation, certificate-gate passage, tuple extraction, component readback, componentwise $\hsame$ and $\NameCert$ transport, and $\ChapterTasteGate$ exposure at once, and no listed sibling carries that whole surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Compare first with $\GroundCompilerRecognizerFlowUp$. It supplies recognizer acceptance, generated-recognizer mediation, and certificate-gate rows, but it does not carry finite tuple extraction, tuple readback, componentwise $\NameCert$ transport, and $\ChapterTasteGate$ exposure as one carrier.

Compare next with $\FiniteReflectionTupleUp$. It supplies tuple image and readback rows, but it does not carry recognizer acceptance, generated-recognizer mediation, or certificate-gate passage.

The normalization router and inductive-acceptance packet have different endpoints. They record normalization evidence routing or inductive declaration acceptance, not the simultaneous seven-row recognizer-to-carrier landing surface. Hence no row-preserving bijection can identify them with this obstruction packet.
\end{proof}
