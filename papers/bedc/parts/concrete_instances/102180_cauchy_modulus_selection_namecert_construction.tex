\begin{theorem}[Cauchy modulus-selection NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-modulus-selection-namecert-obligations}

$$
X=(R,D,W,M,T,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyModulusSelectionUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the $\RegSeqRatUp$ source row, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance request, finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows, selected unary threshold, exactness ledger, Real request boundary, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming;
\item selected-threshold exactness, requiring $T$ to bind the selected row $M$ to the displayed tolerance request in $D$ over the source row $R$;
\item dyadic tolerance compatibility, requiring every comparison and slack row used by $M$ and $T$ to be read through $D$;
\item finite-window handoff, requiring every source observation used by the selection to occur inside the displayed window family $W$;
\item $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, requiring the source data consumed by the selector to be read through $R$ before $M$ is exported;

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

Dyadic compatibility is forced by the absence of any second tolerance coordinate. Every radius, slack row, and comparison used by the selection is therefore read through $D$.

The handoff to Real-facing consumers is terminal. The row $E$ is downstream of $R,D,W,M,T$, while $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite route.
