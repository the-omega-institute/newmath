\begin{definition}[Closed-normal endpoint transport carrier]
\label{def:closed-normal-endpoint-transport-carrier}
A $\ClosedNormalEndpointTransportUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$

$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the source proof row naming the closed proof candidate before the endpoint handoff;
\item $B$ is the beta-route row naming the finite reduction route available to the packet;
\item $T$ is the typed normal endpoint row consumed by the closed-normal consistency main result;
\item $F$ is the false-type preservation socket, displayed as a socket rather than as a discharged subject-reduction theorem;
\item $O$ is the subject-reduction obstruction row that prevents the transport from being reread as full closed consistency;
\item $L$ is the non-escape ledger recording that arbitrary closed proofs are not exported beyond the typed normal endpoint boundary;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for source proof, beta-route, typed endpoint, socket, obstruction, and non-escape rows;
\item $C$ stores displayed $\Cont$ routes from the source proof and beta-route rows to the endpoint row and from the endpoint row to the closed-normal route certificate;

\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ClosedNormalEndpointTransportUp}$ row.

No coordinate contains full closed consistency, a closed subject-reduction theorem, unrestricted normalization, quotient conversion, host evaluation, or an ambient proof that every closed false proof reaches a typed normal endpoint.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Closed-normal endpoint transport NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:closed-normal-endpoint-transport-namecert-obligations}
Every accepted $\ClosedNormalEndpointTransportUp$ packet binds exactly the finite obligation surface of \autoref{def:closed-normal-endpoint-transport-carrier}: carrier admission for $E=(S,B,T,F,O,L,H,C,P,N)$, source proof row exactness through $S$, beta-route exactness through $B$, typed normal endpoint exactness through $T$, false-type preservation socket visibility through $F$, obstruction visibility through $O$, non-escape through $L$, componentwise classifier stability through $H$, displayed continuation stability through $C$, package provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. Admission exposes only the finite tuple $E=(S,B,T,F,O,L,H,C,P,N)$, so every public read starts from the displayed source proof, beta-route, endpoint, socket, obstruction, and non-escape rows.

The endpoint transport is read in two stages. The rows $S$ and $B$ name the source proof and beta route, while $T$ names the typed normal endpoint already at the boundary consumed by \autoref{ch:closed-normal-consistency-main-result}. The socket $F$ is visible but not discharged into a closed subject-reduction theorem.

The rows $O$ and $L$ record the negative boundary. They keep the subject-reduction obstruction and non-escape ledger in the same packet as the positive endpoint row, so a consumer cannot erase the missing first arrow from arbitrary closed proof to typed normal endpoint.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, package, and name only this finite surface. Since no coordinate supplies full closed consistency, unrestricted normalization, quotient conversion, host evaluation, or ambient consistency, the accepted packet exports only the displayed endpoint transport boundary.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Closed-normal endpoint transport boundary]
\label{thm:closed-normal-endpoint-transport-boundary}
Every consumer of an accepted $\ClosedNormalEndpointTransportUp$ packet that reaches a beta-strong false endpoint must factor through either the displayed transport row from $S,B$ to $T$ together with the socket $F$, or through the displayed obstruction row $O$ and non-escape ledger $L$. A consumer that asks for full closed consistency, closed subject reduction, unrestricted normalization, quotient proof conversion, host evaluation, or an unlisted route from arbitrary closed proofs to typed normal endpoints is outside the carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a consumer read of the packet and project the carrier. The only endpoint coordinate is $T$, and the only rows leading to it are the displayed source proof row $S$, beta-route row $B$, and socket row $F$.

If the read uses the positive endpoint route, then it is already a finite closed-normal endpoint read and can be handed to the route certificate of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closed-normal-route-certificate-namecert}. The rows $H$ and $C$ preserve that route componentwise and do not add a subject-reduction discharge.

If the read asks for the missing first arrow, it must encounter $O$ and $L$. Those rows record that the transport surface is an obstruction-aware endpoint packet, not a proof of arbitrary closed consistency.

Thus every accepted read either stays on the displayed endpoint transport row or stops at the displayed obstruction boundary. The package and naming rows $P,N$ make that boundary public without adding any unlisted consistency principle.
\end{proof}
