\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for \(\ContinuousFunctionUp\)}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-continuous-namecert}

The continuous function interface \(\ContinuousFunctionUp\) is licensed between two metric spaces from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}. The carrier is the predicate "\(f : X \to Y\) is uniformly continuous on every totally bounded subset of \(X\), with explicit modulus of continuity \(\delta : \mathbb{N} \to \mathbb{N}\)". Bishop's choice of uniform continuity over pointwise continuity is required for 0-axiom workability: pointwise continuity in BEDC would require LEM to discharge the standard \(\epsilon\)-\(\delta\) argument.
