\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ContinuousMapUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-continuous-namecert}

The continuous-map interface $\ContinuousMapUp$ has certificate obligations between two metric spaces from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}. The carrier is a BEDC graph witness between certified source and target point histories, together with a unary precision-indexed modulus relation. Totality over classifier-admitted source histories and single-valuedness up to the target classifier are certificate fields, so the public object is a relation-plus-ledger certificate rather than a host-level map or host-level numeric modulus.

\section{Carrier definition}

\begin{definition}[ContinuousMap carrier]
\label{def:continuous-carrier}
Use a graph relation between source point histories and target point histories, equipped with source and target metric ledgers, a totally bounded source witness, a unary precision ledger, and a modulus ledger relating requested source precision to target precision. The carrier includes totality for every source history accepted by the source classifier and single-valuedness up to the target classifier for any two target histories related to the same classifier-admitted source history.
\end{definition}
\leandef{BEDC.Derived.ContinuousUp.ContinuousFunctionCarrier}

\begin{theorem}[Continuous function carrier composition closure]
\label{thm:continuous-function-carrier-comp-closed}
If $f$ carries a source history to a middle history with modulus $modF$, and
$g$ carries the middle history to a target history with modulus $modG$, then a
continuation composite $fg$ with modulus composite $modFG$ carries the source
history to the target history.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The two carrier witnesses give unary histories for the source, target, maps,
and moduli, plus continuation ledgers for the source-to-middle and
middle-to-target maps. Unary continuation closure gives unary histories for
the composite map and composite modulus. Case analysis on the continuation
constructors reduces the source-to-target continuation to associativity of
history append, while the supplied target-to-certificate ledger records the
composite modulus certificate.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ContinuousUp.ContinuousFunctionCarrier\_comp\_closed}

\begin{theorem}[Continuous function carrier prefix closure]
\label{thm:continuous-function-carrier-prefix-closed}
For histories $p$, $source$, $map$, $targetHist$, $modulus$, and $certHist$, a
visible unary prefix on the source, target, and certificate ledgers preserves
the full continuous-function carrier:

$$
\begin{array}{l}
\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(p)
\Longrightarrow
\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(source,map,targetHist,modulus,certHist)
\Longrightarrow
\\
\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(p\cdot source,map,p\cdot targetHist,modulus,
p\cdot certHist).
\end{array}
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the carrier into unary histories for the source, target, map, and
modulus, together with continuations $targetHist=source\cdot map$ and
$certHist=targetHist\cdot modulus$.

Unary append closure supplies the prefixed source and target histories
$p\cdot source$ and $p\cdot targetHist$. The map and modulus histories are
kept unchanged, so their unary witnesses are reused directly.

The graph ledger for the prefixed carrier follows by applying the prefix $p$ to
the continuation and reassociating append:

$$
(p\cdot source)\cdot map
=
p\cdot(source\cdot map)
=
p\cdot targetHist.
$$

The modulus-certificate ledger is transported in the same way:

$$
(p\cdot targetHist)\cdot modulus
=
p\cdot(targetHist\cdot modulus)
=
p\cdot certHist.
$$

The ten-field certificate fit is the following. The traditional target is
prefix-stable continuous graph evidence. The BEDC source is $BHist$ equipped
with $\mathsf{Cont}$. The carrier is $\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}$ and
the classifier is $\mathsf{hsame}$. The stability obligation is prefix closure
of the graph and modulus-certificate ledgers. The ledger consists of source,
map, target, modulus, and certificate histories. The core theorem is this
prefix closure, exactness is the displayed carrier witness, the standard bridge
is deferred, and the status is $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ContinuousUp.ContinuousFunctionCarrier\_prefix\_closed}

\begin{theorem}[Continuous function carrier prefix exactness]
\label{thm:continuous-function-carrier-prefix-exact}
For histories $p,source,map,target,modulus,cert$, a visible unary prefix is an
exact invariant of the full continuous-function carrier:

$$
\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(p)
\Longrightarrow
\left(
\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(p\cdot source,map,p\cdot target,modulus,p\cdot cert)
\Longleftrightarrow
\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(source,map,target,modulus,cert)
\right).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the reverse direction, use
\autoref{thm:continuous-function-carrier-prefix-closed}. It prefixes the
source, target, and certificate ledgers by the unary history $p$ while reusing
the same unary map and unary modulus witnesses.

For the forward direction, unpack the prefixed carrier. Unary append
factorization recovers unary witnesses for $source$ and $target$ from the
unary histories $p\cdot source$ and $p\cdot target$ together with
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(p)$. The map and modulus unary witnesses are already
part of the prefixed carrier, so no transport is needed for those fields.

The prefixed graph ledger has the form

$$
(p\cdot source)\cdot map=p\cdot target.
$$

Associativity rewrites its left side to $p\cdot(source\cdot map)$, and
continuation prefix cancellation removes the common visible prefix $p$. This
recovers the unprefixed graph ledger $source\cdot map=target$.

The modulus-certificate ledger is treated in the same way. From

$$
(p\cdot target)\cdot modulus=p\cdot cert
$$

associativity and the same prefix cancellation principle recover
$target\cdot modulus=cert$. The recovered unary endpoint histories, the reused
unary map and modulus histories, and the two cancelled ledgers reassemble
$\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(source,map,target,modulus,cert)$.
\end{proof}

\section{The certificate}

\begin{definition}[ContinuousMap source specification]
\label{def:continuous-source-specification}
The source is a pair of certified metric spaces and the totally bounded subset discipline on the domain.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[ContinuousMap pattern specification]
\label{def:continuous-pattern-specification}
The pattern is graph-with-modulus formation over certified metric spaces and totally bounded source witnesses. A witness supplies the graph relation, the domain and codomain classifier ledgers, and the unary modulus relation that converts a target precision request into the source precision obligation needed for the graph.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[ContinuousMap classifier specification]
\label{def:continuous-classifier-specification}
The classifier relates two continuous-map witnesses when their graph outputs agree under the codomain classifier on every pair of source histories related by the source classifier, and when their unary modulus ledgers mutually refine each other for every precision history admitted by the precision classifier.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[ContinuousMap stability certificate]
\label{def:continuous-stability-certificate}
The stability certificate consists of:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item continuous-map classifier reflexivity
  \item continuous-map classifier symmetry
  \item continuous-map classifier transitivity
  \item modulus validity for uniform continuity
  \item graph and modulus replacement under the continuous-map classifier
  \item closure under identity
  \item closure under composition by composing moduli
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Continuous modulus chain factorization]
\label{thm:continuous-modulus-chain-factorizes}
For histories $s,\delta_1,\delta_2,t$, assume the two-link chain
$$
\mathsf{ContinuousModulusChain}(s,\delta_1,\delta_2,t).
$$
Then there is a history $m$ such that both one-step links are certified:
$$
\mathsf{ContinuousModulusWitness}(s,\delta_1,m)
\quad\text{and}\quad
\mathsf{ContinuousModulusWitness}(m,\delta_2,t).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the chain into unary data for $s$, $\delta_1$, $\delta_2$, and $t$,
together with a middle history $m$ and continuation ledgers
$s\cdot\delta_1=m$ and $m\cdot\delta_2=t$.

The first ledger and the unary source and modulus data assemble
$$
\mathsf{ContinuousModulusWitness}(s,\delta_1,m).
$$

Unary continuation closure then makes the middle history unary. The second
ledger and the unary $\delta_2$ and target data assemble
$$
\mathsf{ContinuousModulusWitness}(m,\delta_2,t).
$$

Taking this middle history as the existential witness gives the displayed
factorization of the chain into its two certified one-step modulus witnesses.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ContinuousUp.ContinuousModulusChain\_factorizes}

\begin{theorem}[Continuous modulus chain composite closure]
\label{thm:continuous-modulus-chain-composite-closed}
For unary histories $s,\delta_1,\delta_2,t$, a two-link modulus chain from
$s$ through $\delta_1$ and $\delta_2$ to $t$, together with a continuation
ledger composing $\delta_1$ and $\delta_2$ into $\delta$, determines a single
continuous modulus witness from $s$ to $t$ using $\delta$:
$$
\mathsf{ContinuousModulusChain}(s,\delta_1,\delta_2,t)
\Longrightarrow
\mathsf{Cont}(\delta_1,\delta_2,\delta)
\Longrightarrow
\mathsf{ContinuousModulusWitness}(s,\delta,t).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the chain into unary endpoint and modulus data plus a middle history
with ledgers $m=s\cdot\delta_1$ and $t=m\cdot\delta_2$. The composite modulus
is unary by closure of unary continuation. The source-to-target continuation is
the associativity equality $(s\cdot\delta_1)\cdot\delta_2
=s\cdot(\delta_1\cdot\delta_2)$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ContinuousUp.ContinuousModulusChain\_composite\_closed}

\begin{theorem}[Prefixed continuous modulus composite closure]
\label{thm:continuous-modulus-prefixed-composite-closed}
For unary histories $p,s,\delta_1,\delta_2,t,\delta$, a unary prefix $p$, a
two-link modulus chain from $s$ through $\delta_1$ and $\delta_2$ to $t$, and a
continuation ledger composing $\delta_1$ and $\delta_2$ into $\delta$ determine
a prefixed continuous modulus witness:

$$
\begin{array}{l}
\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(p)
\Longrightarrow
\mathsf{ContinuousModulusChain}(s,\delta_1,\delta_2,t)
\Longrightarrow
\\
\mathsf{Cont}(\delta_1,\delta_2,\delta)
\Longrightarrow
\mathsf{ContinuousModulusWitness}(p\cdot s,\delta,p\cdot t).
\end{array}
$$

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the chain into unary endpoint and modulus histories, together with the
continuation ledgers from $s$ to the middle history and from the middle history
to $t$.

The prefixed source and target histories are unary by unary append closure
applied to $p$ with $s$ and $t$. The composite modulus history is unary by unary
continuation closure applied to the two modulus histories and their composition
ledger.

The remaining continuation ledger is obtained by associating the concatenation
spine:

$$
(p\cdot s)\cdot\delta
=
p\cdot(s\cdot\delta)
=
p\cdot t.
$$

Thus the prefixed endpoints and the same composite modulus satisfy the public
continuous modulus witness predicate.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ContinuousUp.ContinuousModulusWitness\_prefixed\_composite\_closed}

\begin{theorem}[Continuous modulus chain prefix closure]
\label{thm:continuous-modulus-chain-prefix-closed}
For histories $p,s,\delta_1,\delta_2,t$, a unary prefix on the source and
target preserves a two-link continuous modulus chain:
$$
\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(p)
\Longrightarrow
\mathsf{ContinuousModulusChain}(s,\delta_1,\delta_2,t)
\Longrightarrow
\mathsf{ContinuousModulusChain}(p \cdot s,\delta_1,\delta_2,p \cdot t).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the chain into unary endpoint data and a middle history $m$ with
continuations $m=s\cdot\delta_1$ and $t=m\cdot\delta_2$. Use $p\cdot m$ as the
middle history for the prefixed chain. Unary append closure supplies the
prefixed endpoints, and continuation associativity prefixes both ledger
equalities.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ContinuousUp.ContinuousModulusChain\_prefix\_closed}

\begin{theorem}[Continuous modulus chain prefix exactness]
\label{thm:continuous-modulus-chain-prefix-iff}
For histories $p,s,\delta_1,\delta_2,t$, prefixing both endpoints of a
two-link continuous modulus chain is exact:
$$
\mathsf{ContinuousModulusChain}(p\cdot s,\delta_1,\delta_2,p\cdot t)
\Longleftrightarrow
\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(p)\wedge
\mathsf{ContinuousModulusChain}(s,\delta_1,\delta_2,t).
$$
The traditional target is prefix-stable uniform-modulus data. The BEDC source
is $BHist$ with $\mathsf{Cont}$, the carrier is
$\mathsf{ContinuousModulusChain}$, and the classifier is $\mathsf{hsame}$. The
stability obligation is prefix exactness. The ledger records source, two
precision histories, middle, and target continuations. The core theorem is
this exactness statement, the exactness predicate is the displayed iff, the
standard bridge is deferred, and the status is $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the forward direction, unpack the prefixed chain. Unary append
factorization gives unary $p$ and unary $s$. Let the unprefixed middle be
$s\cdot\delta_1$.

The first continuation ledger, together with associativity, identifies the
prefixed middle as $p\cdot(s\cdot\delta_1)$. The second continuation ledger
then has a common visible prefix $p$ on both sides. Associativity and left
cancellation recover the unprefixed second continuation from
$s\cdot\delta_1$ through $\delta_2$ to $t$. These data assemble
$\mathsf{ContinuousModulusChain}(s,\delta_1,\delta_2,t)$, paired with unary
$p$.

For the reverse direction, take unary $p$ together with the unprefixed chain
and apply Theorem~\ref{thm:continuous-modulus-chain-prefix-closed}. This gives
the prefixed chain from $p\cdot s$ through the same two precision histories to
$p\cdot t$.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[ContinuousMap ledger policy]
\label{def:continuous-ledger-policy}
Record the graph relation, source and target metric ledgers, the totally bounded subset witness, the unary precision ledger, and the modulus ledger.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[$\ContinuousMapUp$ certificate obligations]
\label{def:continuous-certificate-obligations}
The carrier, source, pattern, classifier, stability, and ledger data listed above are the obligations required for the interface name $\ContinuousMapUp$.
\end{definition}

\paragraph{Primitive boundary.}
$\ContinuousMapUp$ is not a BEDC primitive. It is admitted only when those obligations are supplied.

\begin{remark}[Zero-axiom encoding note]
\label{rem:continuous-zero-axiom-note}
The encoding avoids extensional equality of maps and host $\texttt{Nat}$ by using graph classifier sameness and unary precision histories. The modulus object is a certified relation between precision histories, so no host-level numeric modulus is exposed at the certificate boundary.
\end{remark}
