\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ContinuousMapUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-continuous-namecert}

The continuous-map interface $\ContinuousMapUp$ has certificate obligations between two metric spaces from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}. The carrier is a BEDC graph witness between certified source and target point histories, together with a unary precision-indexed modulus relation. Totality over classifier-admitted source histories and single-valuedness up to the target classifier are certificate fields, so the public object is a relation-plus-ledger certificate rather than a host-level map or host-level numeric modulus.

\section{Carrier definition}

\begin{definition}[ContinuousMap carrier]
\label{def:continuous-carrier}
Use a graph relation between source point histories and target point histories, equipped with source and target metric ledgers, a totally bounded source witness, a unary precision ledger, and a modulus ledger relating requested source precision to target precision. The carrier includes totality for every source history accepted by the source classifier and single-valuedness up to the target classifier for any two target histories related to the same classifier-admitted source history.
\end{definition}
\leandef{BEDC.Derived.ContinuousUp.ContinuousFunctionCarrier}

\begin{definition}[Continuous modulus witness]
\label{def:continuous-modulus-witness}
For histories $source$, $modulus$, and $cert$,
$\mathsf{ContinuousModulusWitness}(source,modulus,cert)$ records the four
components
$$
\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(source),\quad
\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(modulus),\quad
\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(cert),\quad
\mathsf{Cont}(source,modulus,cert).
$$
\end{definition}
\leandef{BEDC.Derived.ContinuousUp.ContinuousModulusWitness}

\begin{theorem}[Continuous function carrier composition closure]
\label{thm:continuous-function-carrier-comp-closed}
If $f$ carries a source history to a middle history with modulus $modF$, and
$g$ carries the middle history to a target history with modulus $modG$, then a
continuation composite $fg$ with modulus composite $modFG$ carries the source
history to the target history.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The two carrier witnesses give unary histories for the source, target, maps,
and moduli, plus continuation ledgers for the source-to-middle and
middle-to-target maps. Unary continuation closure gives unary histories for
the composite map and composite modulus. Case analysis on the continuation
constructors reduces the source-to-target continuation to associativity of
history append, while the supplied target-to-certificate ledger records the
composite modulus certificate.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ContinuousUp.ContinuousFunctionCarrier\_comp\_closed}

\begin{theorem}[Continuous function carrier empty-map identity]
\label{thm:continuous-function-carrier-empty-map-identity}
For histories $source$, $modulus$, and $cert$, a certified modulus witness over
the source ledger determines the identity continuous-function carrier:
$$
\mathsf{ContinuousModulusWitness}(source,modulus,cert)
\Longrightarrow
\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(source,\emp,source,modulus,cert).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the modulus witness. It supplies unary histories for $source$, $modulus$,
and $cert$, together with the supplied certificate continuation
$\mathsf{Cont}(source,modulus,cert)$.

The empty map ledger is unary by the empty-history constructor. The graph part of
the identity carrier is the right-unit continuation
$\mathsf{Cont}(source,\emp,source)$.

Reassemble $\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}$ from the unary source ledger
used for both source and target slots, the unary empty map ledger, the unary
modulus ledger, the right-unit graph continuation, and the supplied certificate
continuation.

The ten-field certificate fit is the following. The traditional target is the
identity uniformly continuous graph. The BEDC source is $BHist$ with
$\mathsf{Cont}$. The carrier is $\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}$ and the
classifier is $\mathsf{hsame}$. The stability obligation is identity closure.
The ledger consists of source, empty map, modulus, and certificate histories.
The core theorem is this identity carrier, exactness is the displayed carrier,
the standard bridge is deferred, and the status is $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ContinuousUp.ContinuousFunctionCarrier\_empty\_map\_identity}

\begin{theorem}[Continuous function carrier target-certificate determinacy]
\label{thm:continuous-function-carrier-target-cert-deterministic}
For histories $source,map,target,target',modulus,cert,cert'$, if
$\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(source,map,target,modulus,cert)$ and
$\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(source,map,target',modulus,cert')$, then
both the target ledger and the certificate ledger are determined up to the
history classifier:

$$
\mathsf{hsame}(target,target')\quad\text{and}\quad
\mathsf{hsame}(cert,cert').
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the two carrier witnesses. The first carrier supplies the graph ledger
$\mathsf{Cont}(source,map,target)$ and the certificate ledger
$\mathsf{Cont}(target,modulus,cert)$; the second supplies
$\mathsf{Cont}(source,map,target')$ and
$\mathsf{Cont}(target',modulus,cert')$.

Continuation determinacy applied to the two graph ledgers gives
$\mathsf{hsame}(target,target')$. This is the target part of the displayed
conclusion.

For the certificate part, keep the common modulus ledger fixed and transport
the first certificate continuation across the recovered target sameness. The
transported ledger and the second certificate ledger now have the same source
history up to $\mathsf{hsame}$ and the same modulus history, so continuation
respect for $\mathsf{hsame}$ gives $\mathsf{hsame}(cert,cert')$.

The ten-field certificate fit is the following. The traditional target is
single-valued graph-with-modulus evidence. The BEDC source is $BHist$ equipped
with $\mathsf{Cont}$. The carrier is $\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}$ and
the classifier is $\mathsf{hsame}$. The stability obligation is target and
certificate determinacy. The ledger is source, map, target, modulus, and
certificate histories. The core theorem is this determinacy result. The
exactness predicate is the pair of displayed $\mathsf{hsame}$ conclusions. The
standard bridge is deferred, and the status is $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ContinuousUp.ContinuousFunctionCarrier\_target\_cert\_deterministic}

\begin{theorem}[Continuous function carrier modulus determinacy]
\label{thm:continuous-function-carrier-modulus-deterministic}
For histories $source,map,target,modulus,modulus',cert$, if two
continuous-function carrier witnesses share the same graph target and terminal
certificate, then their modulus histories are determined up to $\hsame$:
$$
\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(source,map,target,modulus,cert)
\Longrightarrow
\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(source,map,target,modulus',cert)
\Longrightarrow
\hsame(modulus,modulus').
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack both carrier witnesses. Their certificate ledgers are
$\mathsf{Cont}(target,modulus,cert)$ and
$\mathsf{Cont}(target,modulus',cert)$. Continuation left cancellation with the
common target history and common certificate history yields
$\hsame(modulus,modulus')$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Continuous function carrier prefix closure]
\label{thm:continuous-function-carrier-prefix-closed}
For histories $p$, $source$, $map$, $targetHist$, $modulus$, and $certHist$, a
visible unary prefix on the source, target, and certificate ledgers preserves
the full continuous-function carrier:

$$
\begin{array}{l}
\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(p)
\Longrightarrow
\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(source,map,targetHist,modulus,certHist)
\Longrightarrow
\\
\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(p\cdot source,map,p\cdot targetHist,modulus,
p\cdot certHist).
\end{array}
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the carrier into unary histories for the source, target, map, and
modulus, together with continuations $targetHist=source\cdot map$ and
$certHist=targetHist\cdot modulus$.

Unary append closure supplies the prefixed source and target histories
$p\cdot source$ and $p\cdot targetHist$. The map and modulus histories are
kept unchanged, so their unary witnesses are reused directly.

The graph ledger for the prefixed carrier follows by applying the prefix $p$ to
the continuation and reassociating append:

$$
(p\cdot source)\cdot map
=
p\cdot(source\cdot map)
=
p\cdot targetHist.
$$

The modulus-certificate ledger is transported in the same way:

$$
(p\cdot targetHist)\cdot modulus
=
p\cdot(targetHist\cdot modulus)
=
p\cdot certHist.
$$

The ten-field certificate fit is the following. The traditional target is
prefix-stable continuous graph evidence. The BEDC source is $BHist$ equipped
with $\mathsf{Cont}$. The carrier is $\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}$ and
the classifier is $\mathsf{hsame}$. The stability obligation is prefix closure
of the graph and modulus-certificate ledgers. The ledger consists of source,
map, target, modulus, and certificate histories. The core theorem is this
prefix closure, exactness is the displayed carrier witness, the standard bridge
is deferred, and the status is $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ContinuousUp.ContinuousFunctionCarrier\_prefix\_closed}

\begin{theorem}[Continuous modulus witness prefix exactness]
\label{thm:continuous-modulus-witness-prefix-iff}
For histories $p,s,\delta,t$, a common visible unary prefix on the source and
target ledgers is exactly the same as unary evidence for the prefix together
with the unprefixed one-step modulus witness:

$$
\mathsf{ContinuousModulusWitness}(p\cdot s,\delta,p\cdot t)
\Longleftrightarrow
\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(p)\wedge
\mathsf{ContinuousModulusWitness}(s,\delta,t).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the forward direction, unpack the prefixed witness into unary histories for
$p\cdot s$, $\delta$, and $p\cdot t$, together with the continuation ledger

$$
(p\cdot s)\cdot\delta=p\cdot t.
$$

Unary append factorization on $p\cdot s$ recovers
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(p)$ and $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(s)$. The same
factorization on $p\cdot t$ recovers $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(t)$. Associativity
rewrites the displayed continuation ledger as
$p\cdot(s\cdot\delta)=p\cdot t$, and left cancellation on the continuation
ledger recovers $s\cdot\delta=t$. These data reassemble
$\mathsf{ContinuousModulusWitness}(s,\delta,t)$.

For the reverse direction, project $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(p)$ and the
unprefixed witness from the conjunction. Unpacking the witness supplies unary
histories for $s$, $\delta$, and $t$, and the continuation ledger
$s\cdot\delta=t$.

Unary append closure gives unary histories for $p\cdot s$ and $p\cdot t$.
Prefixed continuation associativity transports the unprefixed ledger to
$(p\cdot s)\cdot\delta=p\cdot t$, so the prefixed witness is reconstructed.

The ten-field certificate fit is the following. The traditional target is
one-step modulus readback. The BEDC source is $BHist$ equipped with
$\mathsf{Cont}$. The carrier is $\mathsf{ContinuousModulusWitness}$ and the
classifier is $\mathsf{hsame}$. The stability obligation is prefix exactness.
The ledger consists of source, modulus, target, and prefix histories. The core
theorem is this iff, exactness is the displayed iff, the standard bridge is
deferred, and the status is $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ContinuousUp.ContinuousModulusWitness\_prefix\_iff}

\begin{theorem}[Continuous function carrier prefix exactness]
\label{thm:continuous-function-carrier-prefix-iff}
For histories $p,source,map,target,modulus,cert$, prefixing the visible source,
target, and certificate ledgers is conservative for the continuous-function
carrier:

$$
\begin{array}{l}
\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(p\cdot source,map,p\cdot target,modulus,p\cdot cert)
\Longleftrightarrow
\\
\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(p)\wedge
\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(source,map,target,modulus,cert).
\end{array}
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the forward direction, unpack the prefixed carrier. It supplies unary
histories for $p\cdot source$, $p\cdot target$, $map$, and $modulus$, together
with the two continuation ledgers
$\mathsf{Cont}(p\cdot source,map,p\cdot target)$ and
$\mathsf{Cont}(p\cdot target,modulus,p\cdot cert)$.

Unary append factorization applied to the source ledger recovers
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(p)$ and $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(source)$. Applied to the
target ledger, it recovers $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(target)$. Prefix cancellation
on the graph continuation ledger gives $\mathsf{Cont}(source,map,target)$, and
prefix cancellation on the modulus-certificate continuation ledger gives
$\mathsf{Cont}(target,modulus,cert)$. Repacking these witnesses gives
$\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(source,map,target,modulus,cert)$.

For the reverse direction, project $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(p)$ and the
unprefixed carrier from the conjunction. Apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:continuous-function-carrier-prefix-closed} to obtain the
displayed prefixed carrier.

The ten-field certificate fit is the following. The traditional target is
prefix-invariant continuous graph evidence. The BEDC source is $BHist$ equipped
with $\mathsf{Cont}$. The carrier is $\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}$ and
the classifier is $\mathsf{hsame}$. The stability obligation is exact prefix
cancellation for graph and modulus-certificate ledgers. The ledger consists of
source, map, target, modulus, and certificate histories. The core theorem is
prefix exactness, the exactness predicate is the displayed iff, the standard
bridge is deferred, and the status is $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{proof}

\section{The certificate}

\begin{definition}[ContinuousMap source specification]
\label{def:continuous-source-specification}
The source is a pair of certified metric spaces and the totally bounded subset discipline on the domain.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[ContinuousMap pattern specification]
\label{def:continuous-pattern-specification}
The pattern is graph-with-modulus formation over certified metric spaces and totally bounded source witnesses. A witness supplies the graph relation, the domain and codomain classifier ledgers, and the unary modulus relation that converts a target precision request into the source precision obligation needed for the graph.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[ContinuousMap classifier specification]
\label{def:continuous-classifier-specification}
The classifier relates two continuous-map witnesses when their graph outputs agree under the codomain classifier on every pair of source histories related by the source classifier, and when their unary modulus ledgers mutually refine each other for every precision history admitted by the precision classifier.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[ContinuousMap stability certificate]
\label{def:continuous-stability-certificate}
The stability certificate consists of:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item continuous-map classifier reflexivity
  \item continuous-map classifier symmetry
  \item continuous-map classifier transitivity
  \item modulus validity for uniform continuity
  \item graph and modulus replacement under the continuous-map classifier
  \item closure under identity
  \item closure under composition by composing moduli
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Continuous modulus chain factorization]
\label{thm:continuous-modulus-chain-factorizes}
For histories $s,\delta_1,\delta_2,t$, assume the two-link chain
$$
\mathsf{ContinuousModulusChain}(s,\delta_1,\delta_2,t).
$$
Then there is a history $m$ such that both one-step links are certified:
$$
\mathsf{ContinuousModulusWitness}(s,\delta_1,m)
\quad\text{and}\quad
\mathsf{ContinuousModulusWitness}(m,\delta_2,t).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the chain into unary data for $s$, $\delta_1$, $\delta_2$, and $t$,
together with a middle history $m$ and continuation ledgers
$s\cdot\delta_1=m$ and $m\cdot\delta_2=t$.

The first ledger and the unary source and modulus data assemble
$$
\mathsf{ContinuousModulusWitness}(s,\delta_1,m).
$$

Unary continuation closure then makes the middle history unary. The second
ledger and the unary $\delta_2$ and target data assemble
$$
\mathsf{ContinuousModulusWitness}(m,\delta_2,t).
$$

Taking this middle history as the existential witness gives the displayed
factorization of the chain into its two certified one-step modulus witnesses.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ContinuousUp.ContinuousModulusChain\_factorizes}

\begin{theorem}[Continuous modulus chain middle deterministic factorization]
\label{thm:continuous-modulus-chain-middle-deterministic-factorization}
For histories $s,\delta_1,\delta_2,t$, a continuous modulus chain determines a
middle history $m$ such that the two one-step modulus witnesses factor the
chain, and every other first-leg middle endpoint is the same BEDC history:
$$
\mathsf{ContinuousModulusChain}(s,\delta_1,\delta_2,t)
\Longrightarrow
\exists m.\;
\mathsf{ContinuousModulusWitness}(s,\delta_1,m)\land
\mathsf{ContinuousModulusWitness}(m,\delta_2,t)
$$
$$
\land\;
\forall m'.\;
\ContR(s,\delta_1,m')\Longrightarrow
\ContR(m',\delta_2,t)\Longrightarrow
\hsame(m,m').
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the chain into unary data and a displayed middle history $m$ with
continuation ledgers $s\cdot\delta_1=m$ and $m\cdot\delta_2=t$. As in
Theorem~\ref{thm:continuous-modulus-chain-factorizes}, the first ledger gives
the first witness and unary continuation closure supplies unary data for $m$,
so the second ledger gives the second witness. If another $m'$ satisfies the
same first continuation ledger, $\mathsf{Cont}$ determinacy for
$s\cdot\delta_1$ yields $\hsame(m,m')$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ContinuousUp.ContinuousModulusChain\_middle\_deterministic\_factorization}

\begin{theorem}[Continuous modulus chain target determinacy]
\label{thm:continuous-modulus-chain-target-deterministic}
For histories $source,first,second,target,target'$, two modulus-chain witnesses
with the same source and the same two modulus histories determine the same
target up to $\hsame$:
$$
\mathsf{ContinuousModulusChain}(source,first,second,target)
\Longrightarrow
\mathsf{ContinuousModulusChain}(source,first,second,target')
\Longrightarrow
\hsame(target,target').
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack both chains. The first continuation ledgers share $source$ and $first$,
so continuation determinacy identifies the two middle histories up to
$\hsame$. Transport the second continuation comparison along this middle
sameness, keeping the common $second$ history fixed; continuation respect for
$\hsame$ then gives $\hsame(target,target')$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ContinuousUp.ContinuousModulusChain\_target\_deterministic}

\begin{theorem}[Continuous modulus chain result determinacy]
\label{thm:continuous-modulus-chain-result-deterministic}
For histories $s,\delta_1,\delta_2,t,t'$, two continuous modulus chains with the
same source and modulus ledgers determine the same target history:
$$
\mathsf{ContinuousModulusChain}(s,\delta_1,\delta_2,t)
\Longrightarrow
\mathsf{ContinuousModulusChain}(s,\delta_1,\delta_2,t')
\Longrightarrow
\hsame(t,t').
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the two chains into middle histories $m$ and $m'$ with first ledgers
$s\cdot\delta_1=m$ and $s\cdot\delta_1=m'$, and second ledgers
$m\cdot\delta_2=t$ and $m'\cdot\delta_2=t'$. Determinacy of the first
continuation gives $\hsame(m,m')$. Continuation compatibility with this middle
history equality and reflexivity of $\delta_2$ transports the two second ledgers
to obtain $\hsame(t,t')$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ContinuousUp.ContinuousModulusChain\_result\_deterministic}

\begin{theorem}[Continuous modulus chain composite closure]
\label{thm:continuous-modulus-chain-composite-closed}
For unary histories $s,\delta_1,\delta_2,t$, a two-link modulus chain from
$s$ through $\delta_1$ and $\delta_2$ to $t$, together with a continuation
ledger composing $\delta_1$ and $\delta_2$ into $\delta$, determines a single
continuous modulus witness from $s$ to $t$ using $\delta$:
$$
\mathsf{ContinuousModulusChain}(s,\delta_1,\delta_2,t)
\Longrightarrow
\mathsf{Cont}(\delta_1,\delta_2,\delta)
\Longrightarrow
\mathsf{ContinuousModulusWitness}(s,\delta,t).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the chain into unary endpoint and modulus data plus a middle history
with ledgers $m=s\cdot\delta_1$ and $t=m\cdot\delta_2$. The composite modulus
is unary by closure of unary continuation. The source-to-target continuation is
the associativity equality $(s\cdot\delta_1)\cdot\delta_2
=s\cdot(\delta_1\cdot\delta_2)$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ContinuousUp.ContinuousModulusChain\_composite\_closed}

\begin{theorem}[Continuous function carrier modulus-chain replacement]
\label{thm:continuous-function-carrier-modulus-chain-replacement}
For histories $source,map,target,oldMod,oldCert,\delta_1,\delta_2,\delta,cert$,
assume an existing continuous-function carrier, a two-step modulus chain from
the same target history to a certificate history, and a continuation ledger
composing the two modulus steps:

$$
\begin{array}{l}
\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(source,map,target,oldMod,oldCert),
\\
\mathsf{ContinuousModulusChain}(target,\delta_1,\delta_2,cert),
\\
\mathsf{Cont}(\delta_1,\delta_2,\delta).
\end{array}
$$

Then the carrier admits the composite modulus and the chain certificate:

$$
\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(source,map,target,\delta,cert).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the given carrier into unary data for $source$, $map$, $target$,
$oldMod$, and $oldCert$. Keep the graph ledger
$\mathsf{Cont}(source,map,target)$; the old modulus and certificate are used
only to expose the source, map, and target carrier data.

Apply Theorem~\ref{thm:continuous-modulus-chain-composite-closed} to the chain
$\mathsf{ContinuousModulusChain}(target,\delta_1,\delta_2,cert)$ and the
composition ledger $\mathsf{Cont}(\delta_1,\delta_2,\delta)$. This yields the
replacement modulus witness

$$
\mathsf{ContinuousModulusWitness}(target,\delta,cert).
$$

Unpack this witness to obtain unary data for $\delta$ and $cert$, together with
the modulus-certificate ledger $\mathsf{Cont}(target,\delta,cert)$. Reassemble
$\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(source,map,target,\delta,cert)$ from the
preserved source, map, and target data, the preserved graph ledger, and this new
modulus witness.

The ten-field certificate fit is the following. The traditional target is
uniform-continuity data whose modulus certificate may be recomputed through a
two-link modulus chain. The BEDC source is $BHist$ equipped with $\mathsf{Cont}$.
The carrier is $\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}$ and the classifier is
$\mathsf{hsame}$. The stability obligation is carrier preservation under
modulus-chain replacement. The ledger consists of source, map, target, old
modulus, old certificate, two modulus steps, the composite modulus, and the
terminal certificate. The core theorem is this carrier-level replacement,
exactness is the displayed carrier conclusion, the standard bridge is deferred,
and the status is $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ContinuousUp.ContinuousFunctionCarrier\_modulus\_chain\_replacement}

\begin{theorem}[Prefixed continuous function carrier graph-chain closure]
\label{thm:continuous-function-carrier-prefixed-graph-chain-closed}
For histories $p,source,map,target,\delta_1,\delta_2,\delta,cert$, unary
source, map, target, and visible-prefix histories, a graph continuation ledger,
a two-step modulus chain from the target history to a certificate history, and a
continuation ledger composing the two modulus steps determine the prefixed graph
carrier with the composite modulus certificate:

$$
\begin{array}{l}
\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(p)
\Longrightarrow
\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(source)
\Longrightarrow
\\
\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(target)
\Longrightarrow
\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(map)
\Longrightarrow
\\
\mathsf{Cont}(source,map,target)
\Longrightarrow
\mathsf{ContinuousModulusChain}(target,\delta_1,\delta_2,cert)
\Longrightarrow
\mathsf{Cont}(\delta_1,\delta_2,\delta)
\Longrightarrow
\\
\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(p\cdot source,map,p\cdot target,\delta,p\cdot cert).
\end{array}
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply Theorem~\ref{thm:continuous-modulus-prefixed-composite-closed} to
the prefix $p$, the chain
$\mathsf{ContinuousModulusChain}(target,\delta_1,\delta_2,cert)$, and the
composition ledger $\mathsf{Cont}(\delta_1,\delta_2,\delta)$. This gives the
modulus witness
$$
\mathsf{ContinuousModulusWitness}(p\cdot target,\delta,p\cdot cert).
$$

Unpack this witness into unary data for $p\cdot target$, the composite modulus
$\delta$, and $p\cdot cert$, together with the certificate ledger
$\mathsf{Cont}(p\cdot target,\delta,p\cdot cert)$.

The graph ledger is prefixed directly from
$\mathsf{Cont}(source,map,target)$. Associativity of history append gives

$$
(p\cdot source)\cdot map
=
p\cdot(source\cdot map)
=
p\cdot target.
$$

Unary append closure applied to $p$ and $source$ supplies the unary source
history $p\cdot source$. The unary map hypothesis is reused, and the prefixed
target, composite modulus, and prefixed certificate data come from the modulus
witness. These fields assemble
$\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(p\cdot source,map,p\cdot target,\delta,
p\cdot cert)$.

The ten-field certificate fit is the following. The traditional target is
uniformly continuous map composition under visible finite-prefix evidence. The
BEDC source is $BHist$ equipped with $\mathsf{Cont}$. The carrier is
$\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}$ and the classifier is $\mathsf{hsame}$. The
stability obligation is carrier preservation from graph data and a prefixed
two-link modulus chain. The ledger consists of source, map, target, two modulus
steps, the composite modulus, the terminal certificate, and the visible prefix.
The core theorem is this carrier-level closure, exactness is the displayed
carrier conclusion, the standard bridge is deferred, and the status is
$\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Prefixed continuous modulus composite closure]
\label{thm:continuous-modulus-prefixed-composite-closed}
For unary histories $p,s,\delta_1,\delta_2,t,\delta$, a unary prefix $p$, a
two-link modulus chain from $s$ through $\delta_1$ and $\delta_2$ to $t$, and a
continuation ledger composing $\delta_1$ and $\delta_2$ into $\delta$ determine
a prefixed continuous modulus witness:

$$
\begin{array}{l}
\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(p)
\Longrightarrow
\mathsf{ContinuousModulusChain}(s,\delta_1,\delta_2,t)
\Longrightarrow
\\
\mathsf{Cont}(\delta_1,\delta_2,\delta)
\Longrightarrow
\mathsf{ContinuousModulusWitness}(p\cdot s,\delta,p\cdot t).
\end{array}
$$

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the chain into unary endpoint and modulus histories, together with the
continuation ledgers from $s$ to the middle history and from the middle history
to $t$.

The prefixed source and target histories are unary by unary append closure
applied to $p$ with $s$ and $t$. The composite modulus history is unary by unary
continuation closure applied to the two modulus histories and their composition
ledger.

The remaining continuation ledger is obtained by associating the concatenation
spine:

$$
(p\cdot s)\cdot\delta
=
p\cdot(s\cdot\delta)
=
p\cdot t.
$$

Thus the prefixed endpoints and the same composite modulus satisfy the public
continuous modulus witness predicate.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ContinuousUp.ContinuousModulusWitness\_prefixed\_composite\_closed}

\begin{theorem}[Continuous modulus chain prefix closure]
\label{thm:continuous-modulus-chain-prefix-closed}
For histories $p,s,\delta_1,\delta_2,t$, a unary prefix on the source and
target preserves a two-link continuous modulus chain:
$$
\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(p)
\Longrightarrow
\mathsf{ContinuousModulusChain}(s,\delta_1,\delta_2,t)
\Longrightarrow
\mathsf{ContinuousModulusChain}(p \cdot s,\delta_1,\delta_2,p \cdot t).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the chain into unary endpoint data and a middle history $m$ with
continuations $m=s\cdot\delta_1$ and $t=m\cdot\delta_2$. Use $p\cdot m$ as the
middle history for the prefixed chain. Unary append closure supplies the
prefixed endpoints, and continuation associativity prefixes both ledger
equalities.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ContinuousUp.ContinuousModulusChain\_prefix\_closed}

\begin{theorem}[Continuous modulus chain prefix exactness]
\label{thm:continuous-modulus-chain-prefix-iff}
For histories $p,s,\delta_1,\delta_2,t$, prefixing both endpoints of a
two-link continuous modulus chain is exact:
$$
\mathsf{ContinuousModulusChain}(p\cdot s,\delta_1,\delta_2,p\cdot t)
\Longleftrightarrow
\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(p)\wedge
\mathsf{ContinuousModulusChain}(s,\delta_1,\delta_2,t).
$$
The traditional target is prefix-stable uniform-modulus data. The BEDC source
is $BHist$ with $\mathsf{Cont}$, the carrier is
$\mathsf{ContinuousModulusChain}$, and the classifier is $\mathsf{hsame}$. The
stability obligation is prefix exactness. The ledger records source, two
precision histories, middle, and target continuations. The core theorem is
this exactness statement, the exactness predicate is the displayed iff, the
standard bridge is deferred, and the status is $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the forward direction, unpack the prefixed chain. Unary append
factorization gives unary $p$ and unary $s$. Let the unprefixed middle be
$s\cdot\delta_1$.

The first continuation ledger, together with associativity, identifies the
prefixed middle as $p\cdot(s\cdot\delta_1)$. The second continuation ledger
then has a common visible prefix $p$ on both sides. Associativity and left
cancellation recover the unprefixed second continuation from
$s\cdot\delta_1$ through $\delta_2$ to $t$. These data assemble
$\mathsf{ContinuousModulusChain}(s,\delta_1,\delta_2,t)$, paired with unary
$p$.

For the reverse direction, take unary $p$ together with the unprefixed chain
and apply Theorem~\ref{thm:continuous-modulus-chain-prefix-closed}. This gives
the prefixed chain from $p\cdot s$ through the same two precision histories to
$p\cdot t$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ContinuousUp.ContinuousModulusChain\_prefix\_iff}

\begin{remark}[Zero-axiom encoding note]
\label{rem:continuous-zero-axiom-note}
The encoding avoids extensional equality of maps and host $\texttt{Nat}$ by using graph classifier sameness and unary precision histories. The modulus object is a certified relation between precision histories, so no host-level numeric modulus is exposed at the certificate boundary.
\end{remark}
