\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\UniformSequentialLimitUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-uniform-sequential-limit-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, the $\UniformSequentialLimitUp$ packet records the standard passage from uniform convergence of a sequence of real-valued observations to sequential real-limit consumers as a finite BEDC route. It sits beside the uniform-tail certificate of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniform-convergence-namecert}, the sequence-source surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedsequence-namecert}, the sup-norm comparison boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuous-sup-norm-namecert}, the finite stream and regular rational readback rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal real seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The chapter names the finite handoff by which a uniform tail schedule may be consumed by Real-limit readers without selecting a limit function, quotienting functions, invoking countable choice, asserting ambient completeness, or importing open-cover compactness.

\begin{definition}[Uniform sequential limit carrier]\label{def:uniform-sequential-limit-carrier}
A $\UniformSequentialLimitUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
U=(S,M,V,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the sequence source row. It records the accepted $\LocatedSequenceUp$ observations and the finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows from which the displayed sequence entries are read.
\item $M$ is the uniform tail schedule row. It is read through the $\UniformConvergenceUp$ packet and assigns one displayed tail bound to each accepted tolerance request.
\item $V$ is the sup-norm comparison row. It consumes the $\ContinuousSupNormUp$ boundary used to compare all displayed source windows under the same tolerance.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row for the regular rational observations exposed by $S,M,V$.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after the sequence source, uniform tail schedule, sup-norm comparison, and regular rational readback have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $S,M,V,R,E,C,P,N$.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a sequential Real-limit request through the source, tail, comparison, readback, seal, transport, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\UniformSequentialLimitUp$, $\UniformConvergenceUp$, $\LocatedSequenceUp$, $\ContinuousSupNormUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\UniformSequentialLimitUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $S,M,V,R,E,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for a selected limit, quotient equality of functions, countable choice, ambient completeness of $\RealUp$, open-cover compactness, Lean verification, or a bridge credential.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Uniform sequential limit NameCert obligations]\label{thm:uniform-sequential-limit-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\UniformSequentialLimitUp$ carrier
$$
U=(S,M,V,R,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\UniformSequentialLimitUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the sequence source, uniform tail schedule, sup-norm comparison row, regular rational readback, Real seal, componentwise transport, displayed replay, package provenance, and local name. Every sequential $\RealUp$-limit consumer of $U$ factors through
$$
\LocatedSequenceUp \longmapsto \UniformConvergenceUp \longmapsto \ContinuousSupNormUp \longmapsto \StreamNameUp \longmapsto \RegSeqRatUp \longmapsto \RealUp
$$
as displayed by $S,M,V,R,E,H,C,P,N$.

Consequently the packet exports a finite uniform-limit handoff for Real-limit readers and exports no selected limit, quotient equality of functions, countable-choice schedule, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, or open-cover compactness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier through \autoref{def:uniform-sequential-limit-carrier}. The source row $S$ supplies only displayed $\LocatedSequenceUp$ observations and finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows, while $M$ records the uniform tail schedule supplied by the $\UniformConvergenceUp$ boundary.

Read the comparison row $V$ next. Its $\ContinuousSupNormUp$ surface applies the same displayed tolerance to all source windows admitted by $S$ and $M$, so the consumer receives a uniform comparison ledger rather than a pointwise host-limit selector.

Now read $R$ and $E$. The regular rational observations pass through $\RegSeqRatUp$ before the $\RealUp$ seal is exposed, and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name exactly that route.

The excluded principles are absent from the carrier inventory. Therefore a sequential Real-limit consumer can use only the displayed finite uniform route and cannot extract a selected limit, quotient function equality, countable choice, ambient completeness, or open-cover compactness witness.
\end{proof}
