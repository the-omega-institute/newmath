Following Bishop--Bridges constructive analysis, the $\LocatedBoundedRealSequenceUp$ packet records a bounded located sequence of real names as finite BEDC NameCert data before any compactness, supremum, or completeness principle is available. It sits after the real seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the located real boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedreal-namecert}, the regular rational readback surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the finite stream-name discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, and the dyadic rational core of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}. The packet gives L10 Real and Completion consumers one named sequence-boundary object whose rows can later feed limit, Cauchy, and boundedness arguments without importing Heine--Borel compactness, sequential compactness, host suprema, countable choice, or $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[Located bounded real sequence carrier]
\label{def:located-bounded-real-sequence-carrier}
A $\LocatedBoundedRealSequenceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(I,L,U,D,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $I$ is the inspected-index row. It records the finite sequence positions and tail windows opened by the current consumer.
\item $L$ is the located-real row. It records the $\LocatedRealUp$ membership and apartness windows attached to the inspected entries.
\item $U$ is the boundedness row. It records the displayed lower and upper real-boundary windows that bound every inspected entry.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ comparison ledger. It records the dyadic radii, shrink steps, lower-bound comparisons, upper-bound comparisons, and tolerance budgets used by $L$ and $U$.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ observation row. It names the finite stream windows for the inspected indices, located-real reads, bound reads, and dyadic comparisons.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row. It records the regular rational observations obtained from $W$ and $D$ before any real-facing seal is exported.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row. It exposes only the real-name boundary reached through $L,U,D,W,R$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the index row, located-real row, boundedness row, dyadic ledger, stream windows, regular readbacks, real seal, replay, provenance, and local naming row.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the finite route
$$
I,L,U \longmapsto D,W \longmapsto R \longmapsto E .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LocatedBoundedRealSequenceUp$, $\RealUp$, $\LocatedRealUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedBoundedRealSequenceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate admits exactly these eleven rows. Its classifier compares accepted packets only through $I,L,U,D,W,R,E,H,C,P,N$ and has no coordinate for Heine--Borel compactness, sequential compactness, host suprema, countable choice, selected subsequences, or $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Located bounded real sequence NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:located-bounded-real-sequence-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\LocatedBoundedRealSequenceUp$ carrier
$$
S=(I,L,U,D,W,R,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedBoundedRealSequenceUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the inspected-index row, located-real row, boundedness row, dyadic comparison ledger, stream observation row, regular rational readback row, real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item located-entry exposure, requiring every inspected sequence entry to pass through the $\LocatedRealUp$ windows in $L$ and the finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows in $W$;
\item boundedness exposure, requiring every lower-bound or upper-bound read to be displayed in $U$ and compared through the dyadic ledger $D$;
\item dyadic exactness, requiring every tolerance, shrink, and comparison step used by an inspected entry to be recorded in $D$;
\item regular readback discipline, requiring $R$ to be the only route from the displayed stream and dyadic rows into regular rational observations;
\item real-seal nonescape, requiring $E$ to remain downstream of $I,L,U,D,W,R$ and to export no host supremum, compactness theorem, selected subsequence, or ambient completeness witness;
\item transport, continuation, provenance, and naming stability, requiring $H,C,P,N$ to preserve and source exactly the displayed rows componentwise.
\end{enumerate}
No located-bounded-real-sequence obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet $S=(I,L,U,D,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)$. The carrier exposes the inspected indices, located-real entry windows, boundedness windows, dyadic comparison ledger, stream observations, regular readbacks, and terminal real seal as the whole analytic surface.

An entry read starts with $I,L,W$. The row $I$ fixes the finite positions under inspection, $L$ supplies the located-real windows for those positions, and $W$ names the stream windows through which the observations are read.

The boundedness read then passes through $U$ and $D$. The row $U$ supplies the displayed lower and upper real-boundary windows, while $D$ supplies the dyadic radii, shrink steps, and comparison cells used to relate the inspected entries to those bounds.

The readback and seal rows are downstream. The row $R$ is the only regular rational handoff from the displayed stream and dyadic data, and the row $E$ is exposed only after the index, located-entry, boundedness, dyadic, stream, and readback rows have been retained.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite packet. Since compactness, selected subsequences, host suprema, countable choice, and $\RealUp$ completeness have no carrier coordinate or replay edge, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Located bounded real sequence Real seal handoff]
\label{thm:located-bounded-real-sequence-real-seal-handoff}
Every $\RealUp$ or $\RegSeqRatUp$ consumer read of an accepted $\LocatedBoundedRealSequenceUp$ carrier factors through the finite route
$$
I,L,U \longmapsto D,W \longmapsto R \longmapsto E \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
Thus the consumer receives inspected sequence indices, located real entry windows, displayed lower and upper real-boundary windows, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ comparison rows, finite $\StreamNameUp$ observation windows, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readbacks, the terminal $\RealUp$ seal, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ naming. It receives no Heine--Borel compactness, sequential compactness, host supremum, countable-choice schedule, selected subsequence, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Start with a real-facing or regular-rational consumer read of the accepted packet. The carrier contains no global sequence object outside the displayed finite rows, so the read first enters through the inspected-index row $I$ and the located-entry row $L$.

The bounded sequence boundary is then finite. The row $U$ records the displayed lower and upper real-boundary windows, and $D$ records the dyadic comparisons that bind the inspected entries to those windows. The row $W$ names the exact stream observations used by both the entry reads and the bound reads.

The public handoff is $R$ followed by $E$. The $\RegSeqRatUp$ row translates the displayed stream and dyadic rows into regular rational observations, and the $\RealUp$ seal exposes only the real-name boundary reached through that readback.

The structural rows preserve the same dependency order. Componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local $\NameCert$ row can preserve or source the route, but they cannot introduce compactness, selected subsequences, host suprema, countable choice, or completeness data absent from the carrier.
\end{proof}
