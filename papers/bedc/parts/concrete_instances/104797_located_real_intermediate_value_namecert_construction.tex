Following Bishop--Bridges constructive analysis, the $\LocatedRealIntermediateValueUp$ packet records the finite route from located endpoint signs through dyadic bisection windows to the $\RealUp$ seal. It sits next to the sign-bracket certificate of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-intermediatevalue-namecert} and the dyadic branch certificate of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadic-intermediate-value-namecert}, but it keeps the located real output as a sealed finite read rather than a selected root or a quotient real.

The packet is an L10 Real and Completion neighbour. Its source cells are located interval endpoints, its branch rows are dyadic, and its consumer boundary is the same finite $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ route used by the regular-real seal.

\begin{definition}[Located real intermediate-value carrier]
\label{def:located-real-intermediate-value-carrier}
A $\LocatedRealIntermediateValueUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(I,S,B,Q,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $I$ is the $\LocatedIntervalUp$ endpoint row, including the lower endpoint cell, upper endpoint cell, located interval comparison data, and the dyadic endpoint refinements consumed by the branch route;
\item $S$ is the $\IntermediateValueUp$ sign ledger, recording the accepted negative-side and positive-side endpoint reads and the displayed midpoint sign rows carried along the route of \autoref{thm:intermediate-value-located-sign-window-exactness};
\item $B$ is the $\DyadicIntermediateValueUp$ branch data, retaining the finite sequence of accepted dyadic child intervals and the sign boundary that travels with each branch row;
\item $Q$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row whose radius and midpoint arithmetic determine the displayed stopping window;
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window family opened for the retained endpoints and midpoint observations;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row obtained from the same windows after $Q$ fixes the finite tolerance request;
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row available only after $I,S,B,Q,W,R$ have been displayed;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the located interval, sign ledger, dyadic branch data, tolerance row, window rows, readback row, seal row, replay row, provenance row, and local naming row;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the route
$$
I,S \longmapsto B,Q \longmapsto W,R \longmapsto E ;
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LocatedRealIntermediateValueUp$, $\LocatedIntervalUp$, $\IntermediateValueUp$, $\DyadicIntermediateValueUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedRealIntermediateValueUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate admits exactly these rows. It has no coordinate for a selected root, quotient real, real trichotomy decision, open-cover compactness argument, or ambient completeness principle for $\RealUp$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Located real intermediate-value NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:located-real-intermediate-value-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\LocatedRealIntermediateValueUp$ carrier
$$
L=(I,S,B,Q,W,R,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedRealIntermediateValueUp}$ surface consists of carrier admission for the displayed located interval, sign ledger, dyadic branch, tolerance, window, readback, seal, transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows; sign-ledger visibility through $I$ and $S$; dyadic branch exactness through $B$ and $Q$; finite-window readback through $W$ and $R$; Real-seal exposure through $E$; and structural stability through $H,C,P,N$.

Every obligation is internal to the displayed finite packet. In particular, the certificate cannot be discharged by selecting a root, passing to a quotient real, deciding trichotomy, invoking open-cover compactness, or using an ambient completeness theorem for $\RealUp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:located-real-intermediate-value-carrier}. The projection exposes exactly the located interval, sign, branch, tolerance, window, readback, seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

Sign-ledger visibility follows by reading $S$ only over the located endpoint row $I$. The dyadic branch stage then reads $B$ together with the tolerance row $Q$, so each accepted child interval is tied to displayed midpoint arithmetic and a visible sign boundary.

The finite-window stage reads $W$ and then $R$. The real-facing row $E$ is downstream of those windows, while $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same route.

Since none of the carrier coordinates stores a root selector, quotient-real equality, trichotomy oracle, compactness datum, or Real-completeness principle, none of these objects can serve as a local obligation.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Located real sign-branch route]
\label{thm:located-real-intermediate-value-sign-branch-route}
Every Real-facing read from an accepted $\LocatedRealIntermediateValueUp$ packet factors through
$$
\LocatedIntervalUp \longmapsto \IntermediateValueUp \longmapsto \DyadicIntermediateValueUp \longmapsto \StreamNameUp \longmapsto \RegSeqRatUp \longmapsto \RealUp .
$$
The route reads the located endpoint row $I$, the sign ledger $S$, the branch data $B$, the tolerance row $Q$, the windows $W$, the readback row $R$, and only then the seal row $E$, with $H,C,P,N$ preserving the same finite packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier $L=(I,S,B,Q,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)$. The endpoint and sign stages are $I$ followed by $S$, so the read begins with located signs rather than a host predicate over all real points.

Apply the dyadic bisection route of \autoref{thm:dyadic-intermediate-value-bisection-route}. The branch row $B$ and tolerance row $Q$ determine the finite child-interval route before any window data is consumed.

Next read the finite windows and regular-rational readback. The rows $W$ and $R$ are upstream of the seal row $E$, so a Real-facing consumer reaches the seal only through the displayed finite route.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name that same route. Therefore no selected root, quotient real, real trichotomy decision, open-cover compactness argument, or ambient completeness principle can be exported by the packet.
\end{proof}
