Following Bishop and Bridges and Bourbaki--Kelley completion terminology, the $\PseudometricCompletionUniversalPropertyUp$ packet records the finite universal-property route for completing a pre-separated distance surface. It sits after the $\PseudometricUp$ carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-pseudometric-namecert}, the separated classifier route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separated-quotient-metric-namecert}, the separated-completion handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separatedpseudometriccompletion-namecert}, the metric-completion surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metriccompletion-namecert}, and the extension ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completionuniversalproperty-namecert}. Its finite observations are read through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the terminal seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The chapter supplies the L10 bridge from pre-separated distance data to extension uniqueness without quotient completions, selected representatives, selected limits, host function equality, or countable choice.

\begin{definition}[PseudometricCompletionUniversalProperty carrier]
\label{def:pseudometric-completion-universal-property-carrier}
A $\PseudometricCompletionUniversalPropertyUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
U=(P,Q,S,M,V,W,R,D,A,F,E,L,H,C,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $P$ is the source $\PseudometricUp$ packet, carrying the point rows, displayed distance witness, zero-distance boundary, dyadic tolerance ledger, finite windows, regular rational readback, and terminal real distance seal.
\item $Q$ is the $\SeparatedQuotientMetricUp$ classifier row that consumes zero-distance evidence from $P$ before any separated metric read is exposed.
\item $S$ is the $\SeparatedPseudometricCompletionUp$ handoff row that routes the source pseudometric through separated completion data while keeping the pre-separated source visible.
\item $M$ is the $\MetricCompletionUp$ row naming the completion-facing separated metric packet consumed by extension readers.
\item $V$ is the $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$ row that supplies the finite extension-existence and uniqueness ledgers over completion data.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window schedule through which source points, distance approximants, and dense observations are inspected.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row attached to the windows in $W$ and to the dense source observations used by the extension route.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row used for zero-distance, triangle, and extension-precision comparisons before the real seal is read.
\item $A$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal reached only after $W,R,D$ display the requested approximation data.
\item $F$ is the dense-source map row whose finite graph and modulus reads are to be extended from the pseudometric source.
\item $E$ is the extension-existence ledger, a displayed route from $P,Q,S,M,V,W,R,D,A,F$ to a completion-facing map row.
\item $L$ is the uniqueness ledger comparing two extension rows whenever they agree on the same separated classifier, dense-source windows, regular readback, dyadic tolerance, real seal, and dense map rows.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the source pseudometric, separated classifier, separated completion, metric completion, universal-property row, window schedule, regular readback, dyadic tolerance, real seal, map row, extension ledger, uniqueness ledger, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the route
$$
P,Q,S,M,V,W,R,D,A,F\longmapsto E\longmapsto L .
$$
\item $N$ is both the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\PseudometricUp$, $\SeparatedQuotientMetricUp$, $\SeparatedPseudometricCompletionUp$, $\MetricCompletionUp$, $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows and the local $\NameCert_{\PseudometricCompletionUniversalPropertyUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for quotient completion classes, selected representatives, selected limits, host function equality, countable-choice schedules, trichotomy, or ambient completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[PseudometricCompletionUniversalProperty NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:pseudometric-completion-universal-property-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\PseudometricCompletionUniversalPropertyUp$ carrier $U=(P,Q,S,M,V,W,R,D,A,F,E,L,H,C,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\PseudometricCompletionUniversalPropertyUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the source pseudometric, separated classifier, separated completion handoff, metric completion row, universal-property row, finite windows, regular readback, dyadic tolerance, real seal, dense map, extension ledger, uniqueness ledger, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item separated-source exposure, requiring zero-distance evidence from $P$ to pass through $Q$ and $S$ before the metric-completion row $M$ is read;
\item dense-readback exposure, requiring every extension request to display $W,R,D,A$ before the dense map row $F$ is repacked as an extension;
\item extension existence, requiring $E$ to be read only after $P,Q,S,M,V,W,R,D,A,F$ have been displayed;
\item extension uniqueness, requiring $L$ to compare candidate extension rows over the same separated source, dense windows, readback rows, real seal, and dense map data;
\item non-escape, excluding quotient completions, selected representatives, selected host limits, host function equality, countable choice, trichotomy, and ambient completeness from every exported consumer route.
\end{enumerate}
No pseudometric-completion universal-property obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:pseudometric-completion-universal-property-carrier}. The accepted packet lists the source pseudometric, separated classifier, separated completion handoff, completion row, universal-property row, dense readback rows, extension and uniqueness ledgers, and structural support rows.

Separated-source exposure follows from the order $P,Q,S,M$. The source distance surface remains pre-separated in $P$ until the zero-distance evidence is consumed by $Q$ and the separated-completion handoff $S$.

Dense-readback exposure follows from $W,R,D,A,F$. The stream schedule, regular rational row, dyadic tolerance row, and real seal are displayed before the dense map row is used by an extension request.

Extension existence is the role of $E$, and uniqueness is the role of $L$. The replay row $C$ places those ledgers after the same source, separated, completion, dense-readback, and map rows, while $H$ transports only the displayed coordinates.

The non-escape clause is an inventory check on the carrier. Since no quotient completion class, selected representative, selected host limit, host function equality, countable-choice schedule, trichotomy row, or ambient completeness theorem appears among the coordinates, none can be exported by the local naming certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PseudometricCompletionUniversalProperty extension uniqueness]
\label{thm:pseudometric-completion-universal-property-extension-uniqueness}
Let $U=(P,Q,S,M,V,W,R,D,A,F,E,L,H,C,N)$ and $U'=(P,Q,S,M,V,W,R,D,A,F,E',L',H',C',N')$ be accepted $\PseudometricCompletionUniversalPropertyUp$ carriers with the same source pseudometric, separated classifier, separated completion handoff, metric completion row, universal-property row, finite windows, regular readback, dyadic tolerance, real seal, dense map row, and package provenance. If $E$ and $E'$ agree on every displayed finite read from $W,R,D,A$ after the separated-source route $P,Q,S$ has been consumed, then the two completion-facing extension rows are compared by the uniqueness ledgers $L$ and $L'$.

The comparison is a displayed $\hsame$ ledger over the finite carrier rows. It is not host equality of maps, not equality of quotient-completion representatives, and not a selected-limit principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the common source coordinates from the two accepted packets. The rows $P,Q,S$ fix the pre-separated distance surface, the separated classifier, and the separated-completion handoff available to both extensions.

Next read the common completion and dense-source coordinates. The rows $M,V,W,R,D,A,F$ fix the completion-facing target, the universal-property ledger, the finite window schedule, regular rational readback, dyadic tolerances, real seal, and dense map data.

The hypothesis compares $E$ and $E'$ on the displayed finite reads from $W,R,D,A$ after the separated-source route has been consumed. Transport those reads through $H,H'$ and replay them through $C,C'$ so both extension rows are presented over the same source, completion, dense-readback, real-seal, and dense-map coordinates.

The uniqueness coordinates are exactly $L$ and $L'$. Applying them gives the required displayed comparison of extension rows, with no appeal to host function equality, quotient representatives, selected limits, or countable choice.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PseudometricCompletionUniversalProperty non-escape]
\label{thm:pseudometric-completion-universal-property-nonescape}
Every consumer read exported by an accepted $\PseudometricCompletionUniversalPropertyUp$ carrier factors through the finite route
$$
\PseudometricUp,\SeparatedQuotientMetricUp,\SeparatedPseudometricCompletionUp,\MetricCompletionUp,\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp,\StreamNameUp,\RegSeqRatUp,\DyadicRatCoreUp,\RealUp .
$$
The packet exposes extension existence through $E$ and extension uniqueness through $L$ only after that route has been displayed. It exports no quotient completion class, selected representative, selected host limit, countable-choice schedule, host function equality, trichotomy decision, or ambient completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:pseudometric-completion-universal-property-carrier}. Its source and completion rows are $P,Q,S,M,V$, and its dense-readback rows are $W,R,D,A$.

Apply \autoref{thm:pseudometric-completion-universal-property-namecert-obligations}. The separated-source obligation forces a consumer through $P,Q,S$ before $M$ is read, and the dense-readback obligation forces a consumer through $W,R,D,A$ before $F,E,L$ are read.

Extension existence is then confined to the ledger $E$, while extension uniqueness is confined to $L$. The transport and replay rows $H,C$ move only the displayed coordinates and preserve the same order.

The exclusions follow from the carrier inventory. Since the packet contains no coordinate for quotient completion classes, selected representatives, selected host limits, countable-choice schedules, host function equality, trichotomy, or ambient completeness, none of those objects can escape along a consumer route.
\end{proof}
