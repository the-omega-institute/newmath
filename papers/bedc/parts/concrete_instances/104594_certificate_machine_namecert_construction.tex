\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CertificateMachineUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-certificate-machine-namecert}
\origin{ai}

The $\CertificateMachineUp$ packet realizes the certificate-machine branch of \autoref{subsec:philosophy-certificate-machine-allegory-branch} as finite BEDC carrier data. It names the surface on which a machine-like object is admitted only through a finite kernel row, accepted-observation rows, branch-refusal rows, audit replay, provenance, and local NameCert evidence. The packet is not an ordinary Turing-machine identity claim, not a semantic truth oracle, not a host execution trace, and not a replacement for the kernel and NameCert obligations it records.

\begin{definition}[CertificateMachine carrier]
\label{def:certificate-machine-carrier}
A $\CertificateMachineUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
M=(K,O,B,A,R,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $K$ is the finite certificate-kernel row. It lists the admitted constructors, candidate rows, acceptance stamps, and refusal slots visible to this machine surface.
\item $O$ is the accepted-observation row, recording the finite observations the machine has admitted as certificate-bearing rows.
\item $B$ is the branch-refusal row, recording rejected or unlicensed branches together with the local reason each branch cannot be read as accepted data.
\item $A$ is the audit-replay row, recording the finite replay route from generated candidate through accepted observation or branch refusal.
\item $R$ is the replay-result row, naming the public result that may be consumed after the audit replay finishes.
\item $S$ is the soundness-boundary row saying that the machine exports certificate acceptance and refusal data, not a host truth predicate or an external operational semantics.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $K,O,B,A,R,S,P,N$.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from kernel row to observation or refusal row, from that row to audit replay, and from audit replay to the public result.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CertificateMachineUp$, finite certificate kernels, accepted observations, branch refusals, audit replay, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CertificateMachineUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $K,O,B,A,R,S,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for host machine equality, ordinary Turing-machine identity, semantic truth, unbounded execution logs, unlicensed branch acceptance, or external proof authority.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CertificateMachine NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:certificate-machine-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CertificateMachineUp$ carrier
$$
M=(K,O,B,A,R,S,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\CertificateMachineUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item kernel admission, requiring every candidate, constructor, acceptance stamp, and refusal slot to pass through $K$;
\item accepted-observation exactness, requiring every admitted observation to be one of the rows displayed in $O$;
\item branch-refusal exactness, requiring every rejected branch read to pass through $B$ with its local refusal reason;
\item audit-replay determinacy, requiring every public result to be replayed through $A$ before $R$ is consumed;
\item soundness-boundary non-escape, requiring $S$ to exclude semantic truth, ordinary Turing-machine identity, host execution logs, and external proof authority;
\item transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming stability through $H,C,P,N$.
\end{enumerate}
Lean follow-up must provide $\mathsf{Nontrivial}(\CertificateMachineUp)$ and $\mathsf{FieldFaithful}(\CertificateMachineUp)$ instances before the object may be consumed as a nondegenerate field-facing certificate machine.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:certificate-machine-carrier}. The only kernel coordinate is $K$, so every candidate and acceptance stamp begins from that finite certificate-kernel row.

Accepted observations and refusals then split through $O$ and $B$. The accepted side records only admitted observation rows, while the refusal side records branch-local reasons for rejection.

The audit route passes through $A$ before the public result $R$ is available. Thus a consumer cannot bypass replay by citing a host execution log or an unbounded operational trace.

Finally $S,H,C,P,N$ bind the boundary, transport the same rows, replay the displayed route, record provenance, and name the local certificate. Since no coordinate adds semantic truth, ordinary Turing-machine identity, unlicensed branch acceptance, or external proof authority, the listed obligations exhaust the NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CertificateMachine audit-replay non-escape]
\label{thm:certificate-machine-audit-replay-nonescape}
Every public read of an accepted $\CertificateMachineUp$ carrier factors through
$$
K,O,B,A,R,S,H,C,P,N .
$$
An accepted observation reaches the public result only by the route $K,O,A,R$, and a rejected branch reaches the public refusal surface only by the route $K,B,A,R$. Both routes keep the soundness boundary $S$ visible and preserve the same transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows. No read exports host machine equality, semantic truth, unbounded execution, unlicensed branch acceptance, or an external proof oracle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the displayed carrier $M$. There are exactly two observation-facing routes after the kernel row: the accepted-observation row $O$ and the branch-refusal row $B$.

In the accepted case, continuation replay through $C$ sends $K$ and $O$ into the audit row $A$ before the public result $R$ can be read. In the refusal case, the same replay discipline sends $K$ and $B$ into $A$ before the refusal result is visible.

The soundness boundary $S$ is carried with both routes. It excludes the stronger readings that would identify the packet with an ordinary Turing machine, a semantic truth oracle, a host execution log, or an external proof source.

The remaining rows $H,P,N$ only transport, source, and name the packet. Hence every public read remains inside the displayed finite surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CertificateMachine branch-refusal determinacy]
\label{thm:certificate-machine-branch-refusal-determinacy}
Let $M=(K,O,B,A,R,S,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CertificateMachineUp$ packet. Every refused branch of $M$ is determined by the finite kernel row $K$, the branch-refusal row $B$, the audit-replay row $A$, and the soundness-boundary row $S$. Before the refused branch can be consumed as a MetaCIC audit row, its read must pass through the accepted-kernel surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-kernelacceptancewitness-namecert}, the axiom-query ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-axiomqueryledger-namecert}, and the closed-generator audit surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closedgeneratoraudit-namecert}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:certificate-machine-carrier}. The refusal side has only the kernel row $K$, the branch-refusal row $B$, the audit replay row $A$, and the soundness boundary $S$ before the public result row can be read.

Apply audit-replay non-escape, \autoref{thm:certificate-machine-audit-replay-nonescape}. A rejected branch reaches the public refusal surface only by the route $K,B,A,R$, with $S$ still visible as the boundary against host truth, unbounded execution, and external proof authority.

The MetaCIC sibling lattice supplies the concrete audit route for those rows. Kernel admission is read through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-kernelacceptancewitness-namecert}, proof-supply cleanliness through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-axiomqueryledger-namecert}, and closed-generator replay through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closedgeneratoraudit-namecert}.

Thus the refused branch is determined by the displayed finite rows of the same packet and by the named sibling ledgers. No separate kernel-only or host-execution route can consume it as a MetaCIC audit row.
\end{proof}
