\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CauchyModulusEnvelopeUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-modulus-envelope-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges on constructive Cauchy moduli, the $\CauchyModulusEnvelopeUp$ packet records a finite majorant envelope for the modulus rows used by regular rational and real-name convergence arguments. It sits over the dyadic arithmetic of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the finite stream windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet names cofinal modulus majorants and finite-family merges before any $\RealUp$ seal is read; it is not a quotient of Cauchy streams, a selected completion point, countable choice, or an ambient real-completeness theorem.

\begin{definition}[Cauchy modulus envelope carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-modulus-envelope-carrier}
A $\CauchyModulusEnvelopeUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
E=(D,W,R,F,M,U,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row used to compare requested precision levels and dyadic tail bounds.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite observation window containing the named sequence entries inspected by the envelope.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular-rational readback row attached to the same window.
\item $F$ is a finite family of displayed Cauchy-modulus rows whose thresholds are all read through $D,W,R$.
\item $M$ is the finite majorant row assigning a single envelope threshold to each requested dyadic precision.
\item $U$ is the finite merge row that combines a displayed finite subfamily of modulus rows into the same majorant envelope.
\item $S$ is the downstream $\RealUp$ seal handoff available only after $D,W,R,F,M,U$ are accepted.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for dyadic tolerances, stream windows, regular-rational readback, modulus rows, envelope majorants, finite merges, seal handoffs, replay, provenance, and naming.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from precision requests through the finite envelope route and then to the real-name seal.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyModulusEnvelopeUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyModulusEnvelopeUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
Accepted carriers are compared only through $D,W,R,F,M,U,S,H,C,P,N$. There is no coordinate for an infinite modulus choice, a quotient stream equality, a selected limit, or an ambient completeness principle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy modulus envelope NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-modulus-envelope-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CauchyModulusEnvelopeUp$ carrier $E=(D,W,R,F,M,U,S,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyModulusEnvelopeUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the dyadic tolerance row, finite stream window, regular-rational readback row, finite family of modulus rows, envelope majorant row, finite merge row, real-name seal handoff, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, provenance, and local naming.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-modulus-envelope-carrier}. Its analytic coordinates are exactly $D,W,R,F,M,U,S$, and its structural coordinates are exactly $H,C,P,N$.

The dyadic, stream, and regular-rational rows supply the only observable precision and tail-window data. The modulus family $F$ and majorant row $M$ are read after those rows, so an envelope threshold cannot be supplied by an external modulus selector.

The merge row $U$ is finite and stays attached to the same displayed family. Rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same packet, with no additional coordinate for quotient streams, selected limits, countable choice, or ambient real completeness. Hence the listed rows exhaust the NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy modulus envelope majorization]
\label{thm:cauchy-modulus-envelope-modulus-majorization}
Let $E=(D,W,R,F,M,U,S,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyModulusEnvelopeUp$ carrier. Every modulus row in the displayed finite family $F$ is majorized only through the envelope row $M$ over the same dyadic tolerance row $D$, stream window $W$, and regular-rational readback row $R$. A consumer cannot read a threshold from $F$ without also reading the common envelope row $M$ and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the carrier. The finite family $F$ is not a free list of host functions; it is a displayed family of modulus rows attached to $D,W,R$.

The envelope row $M$ is the only row that assigns a common threshold to the requested dyadic precision. Thus a majorization read must inspect $D,W,R$ first, then read the relevant modulus row from $F$, and then compare it with $M$ inside the same packet.

Transport and replay through $H$ and $C$ preserve this dependency, while $P,N$ only source and name it. Since the carrier has no external modulus coordinate, every accepted majorization remains inside the finite envelope route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy modulus envelope finite-family merge]
\label{thm:cauchy-modulus-envelope-finite-family-merge}
For every accepted $\CauchyModulusEnvelopeUp$ carrier $E=(D,W,R,F,M,U,S,H,C,P,N)$, the merge row $U$ combines only finite displayed subfamilies of $F$ into the same envelope row $M$. The merge may reuse dyadic tolerance comparisons from $D$ and tail-window reads from $W,R$, but it exports no infinite family choice, no quotient equality of modulus functions, and no selected completion witness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read $F$ and $U$ from the carrier. The family row is finite, and the merge row is listed as a row of that same finite packet rather than as an ambient operation over all moduli.

Use the majorization theorem \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-envelope-modulus-majorization}. Each component modulus in the displayed family is compared with $M$ only after $D,W,R$ are visible.

The merge row therefore combines already displayed comparisons. The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve and name the finite merge route, but they add no infinite selector, quotient equality, selected limit, or completion object.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy modulus envelope Real-seal nonescape]
\label{thm:cauchy-modulus-envelope-real-seal-nonescape}
Let $E=(D,W,R,F,M,U,S,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyModulusEnvelopeUp$ carrier. Every downstream $\RealUp$ consumer of the seal row $S$ must first expose the dyadic tolerance row $D$, stream window $W$, regular-rational readback row $R$, finite modulus family $F$, envelope majorant row $M$, and finite merge row $U$. The seal exports no quotient Cauchy stream, selected real value, countable-choice modulus selection, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness claim.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the seal row $S$ from the carrier. It appears only after $D,W,R,F,M,U$, so it is a handoff reached from the accepted finite envelope packet rather than an independent real object.

Apply \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-envelope-modulus-majorization}. It keeps every threshold read tied to the same dyadic tolerance, stream window, regular-rational readback, and envelope row.

Apply \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-envelope-finite-family-merge}. The merge route remains finite and displays the family members whose thresholds are being bounded.

Rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name only that finite route. Thus a real-name consumer must pass through the envelope packet before reading $S$, and none of the refused quotient, choice, selected-limit, or ambient-completeness data is exported.
\end{proof}
