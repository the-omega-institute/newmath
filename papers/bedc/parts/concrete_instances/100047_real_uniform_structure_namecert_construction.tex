\begin{definition}[Real uniform-structure carrier]
\label{def:real-uniform-structure-carrier}
A $\RealUniformStructureUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
U_{\Real}=(R,M,U,F,D,S,Q,H,C,P,N)

\begin{enumerate}
\item $R$ is the $\RealUp$ endpoint and seal row whose named points supply the source of every ball request.
\item $M$ is the $\MetricUp$ distance witness row read from the Real metric surface before any entourage is generated.
\item $U$ is the $\UniformSpaceUp$ entourage row generated by positive dyadic radius requests around the displayed metric balls.
\item $F$ is the $\CauchyFilterUp$ compatibility row for completion-facing consumers that read Cauchy data through the induced entourage surface.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ positive-radius ledger used by the metric-ball generator.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row inspected by Real and Cauchy-filter consumers.
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular rational readback row attached to those windows.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the Real, metric, uniform, Cauchy-filter, dyadic, window, readback, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

The classifier compares accepted packets only through $R,M,U,F,D,S,Q,H,C,P,N$. The carrier has no coordinate for quotient real equality, selected limits, trichotomy, countable choice, open-cover compactness, or ambient Real completeness.

\begin{theorem}[Real uniform-structure NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:real-uniform-structure-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RealUniformStructureUp$ carrier $U_{\Real}=(R,M,U,F,D,S,Q,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\RealUniformStructureUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed Real endpoint and seal row, metric distance witness, uniform entourage row, Cauchy-filter compatibility row, dyadic radius ledger, StreamName windows, RegSeqRat readback, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item metric-ball entourage generation, requiring every entourage request in $U$ to be generated from $M$ by a positive dyadic radius read from $D$ around endpoints displayed by $R$;
\item Cauchy-filter compatibility, requiring every completion-facing Cauchy-filter read in $F$ to consume the induced entourage row $U$ before inspecting stream windows or regular rational readbacks;
\item classifier transport, requiring componentwise $\hsame$ transport $H$ and displayed $\Cont$ replay $C$ to preserve the route $R,M,D,U,F,S,Q$;
\item ledger non-escape, requiring $P$ and $N$ to export no quotient real equality, selected limit, trichotomy, countable-choice selector, open-cover compactness, or ambient Real completeness.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored outside those rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:real-uniform-structure-carrier}. The projection exposes exactly the Real endpoint and seal row, the metric distance witness, the generated uniform entourage row, the Cauchy-filter compatibility row, the dyadic radius ledger, the finite-window and readback rows, and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

Metric-ball entourage generation is forced by the displayed route. An entourage request must first read endpoints from $R$, then read the distance witness in $M$, and then choose a positive dyadic radius from $D$ before it can be recorded in the uniform row $U$.

Cauchy-filter compatibility is downstream of the induced uniformity. The row $F$ is available only as a consumer of $U$, and the finite observations $S$ and $Q$ are read after that uniform row has been displayed.

Transport and replay are structural. Componentwise $\hsame$ in $H$ preserves each displayed coordinate, and $\Cont$ replay in $C$ repeats the same route $R,M,D,U,F,S,Q$ for every accepted request.

Finally, provenance is exhausted by $P$ and $N$. Since quotient real equality, selected limits, trichotomy, countable choice, open-cover compactness, and ambient Real completeness are not rows of $U_{\Real}$, the local NameCert surface cannot export them.
\end{proof}
