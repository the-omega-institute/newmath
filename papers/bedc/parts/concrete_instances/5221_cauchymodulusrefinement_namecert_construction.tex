\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CauchyModulusRefinementUp$}

The $\CauchyModulusRefinementUp$ packet concretizes the Real-completeness diagonal-modulus route of \autoref{ch:visions-real-completeness}. It is assembled from the comparison and meet surfaces of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchymoduluscomparison-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchymodulusmeet-namecert}, then routes extraction, selector-budget, finite-window, rational-readback, dyadic-tolerance, and $\RealUp$ seal data through one displayed NameCert carrier.

\begin{definition}[Cauchy modulus refinement carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-modulus-refinement-carrier}
A $\CauchyModulusRefinementUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
R=(M_0,M_1,U,V,T,W,Q,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M_0$ and $M_1$ are the two source $\CauchyModulusUp$ schedules whose refinement is being compared;
\item $U$ is the shared $\CauchyModulusMeetUp$ row consumed by both source schedules;
\item $V$ is the $\CauchyModulusExtractionUp$ refinement witness row, read only after $M_0,M_1$ and $U$ have been displayed;
\item $T$ is the $\RegularCauchySelectorBudgetUp$ row that budgets the selected finite windows for the refinement handoff;
\item $W$ is the shared tail-threshold and finite $\StreamNameUp$ window ledger selected by $U,V,T$;
\item $Q$ stores the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance rows and $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback ledger attached to the same selected windows;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached after the shared meet, refinement witness, selector budget, windows, tolerance rows, and regular readbacks have been displayed;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $M_0,M_1,U,V,T,W,Q,E,P,N$ and for the local packet shape;
\item $C$ stores displayed $\Cont$ routes from the source schedules to $U$, from $U$ to $V$, from $V$ to $T$, from $T$ to $W$, from $W$ to $Q$, from $Q$ to $E$, and from the packet rows to the local naming row;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyModulusUp$, $\CauchyModulusComparisonUp$, $\CauchyModulusMeetUp$, $\CauchyModulusExtractionUp$, $\RegularCauchySelectorBudgetUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyModulusRefinementUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no quotient stream, no selected limit, no host real equality, no hidden selector, no second meet row, and no ambient completeness coordinate.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy modulus refinement NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\CauchyModulusRefinementUp}$ surface for \autoref{def:cauchy-modulus-refinement-carrier} consists of five finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for $R=(M_0,M_1,U,V,T,W,Q,E,H,C,P,N)$;
\item classifier stability under the displayed $\hsame$ transports and $\Cont$ routes;
\item refinement-source exactness stating that the row $V$ is read only from the two source schedules $M_0,M_1$ through the shared meet row $U$;
\item shared-window tolerance exactness forcing every selected-window read through $T,W,Q$;
\item $\RealUp$ seal non-escape stating that $E$ is reached only after the shared meet, refinement witness, selector budget, windows, dyadic tolerance rows, regular readbacks, transports, routes, provenance, and local name have been displayed.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from the finite packet in \autoref{def:cauchy-modulus-refinement-carrier}. The tuple lists the two source schedules, shared meet row, refinement witness, selector-budget row, selected windows, tolerance and readback ledger, Real seal, transports, routes, provenance, and local name.

Classifier stability uses $H$ and $C$. These rows are componentwise over the packet coordinates, so a transported refinement read remains a read of the same source schedules, meet row, witness row, budget row, window ledger, tolerance ledger, seal row, package row, and local certificate.

Refinement-source exactness follows from the order of the displayed coordinates and routes. The witness $V$ is downstream of $M_0,M_1,U$, and no carrier coordinate stores an external modulus ordering, a second meet, or a hidden selector that could replace that source.

Finally, the shared-window and Real-seal clauses use $T,W,Q,E$. Every Real-facing read reaches $E$ only through the selector-budget row, the shared finite-window ledger, and the tolerance-readback ledger, so quotient streams, selected limits, host real equality, and ambient completeness remain outside the packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy modulus refinement non-escape]
\label{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-nonescape}
An accepted $\CauchyModulusRefinementUp$ packet exposes only the finite route
$$
M_0,M_1,U,V,T,W,Q,E,H,C,P,N.
$$
If the refinement packet is accepted, every $\RealUp$-facing read must factor through the displayed shared meet and selector-budget rows. Thus a counterpacket with the same seal row $E$ but no shared meet row $U$ refutes the chapter's concrete carrier claim.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the carrier inventory. The only route from the two source schedules to the Real-facing seal passes through $U,V,T,W,Q,E$ and is stabilized by $H,C,P,N$.

The shared meet row $U$ is required before the refinement witness $V$ can be read, and the selector-budget row $T$ is required before the selected window ledger $W$ and tolerance-readback ledger $Q$ can be consumed. Hence a Real-facing read cannot bypass either $U$ or $T$.

If a packet presents the same seal row but omits the shared meet row, its displayed coordinate list is not the carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-modulus-refinement-carrier}. Such a packet therefore witnesses failure of the concrete carrier claim rather than a second accepted refinement surface.
\end{proof}
