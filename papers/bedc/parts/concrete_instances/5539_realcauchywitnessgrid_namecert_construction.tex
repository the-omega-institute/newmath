\begin{theorem}[Real Cauchy witness grid NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:real-cauchy-witness-grid-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:real-cauchy-witness-grid-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\RealCauchyWitnessGridUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for
$$
G=(B,L,K,T,S,R,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
selector-budget exposure through $B$, witness-ledger exposure through $L$, completion-seal classifier exposure through $K$, tail-witness exposure through $T$, shared StreamName window exposure through $S$, RegSeqRat witness exposure through $R$, RealUp seal exposure through $E$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ route replay through $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.RealCauchyWitnessGridUp.RealCauchyWitnessGridUp\_obligation\_surface}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from the displayed tuple. The rows $B,L,K,T,S,R,E,H,C,P,N$ are all public coordinates of the grid.

The analytic rows are read in order. The selector-budget row $B$ supplies the finite modulus and window request; the witness-ledger row $L$ stores the diagonal budget, observation budget, synchronizer, and classifier route; the classifier row $K$ reads the completion-seal boundary; and the tail-witness row $T$ stores the selected tail envelope.

The shared rows $S,R,E$ expose the StreamName, RegSeqRat, and RealUp handoff data consumed by those four packets. The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, authenticate, and name the same grid. Since the carrier has no forbidden analytic coordinate, these rows exhaust the NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real Cauchy witness grid selector exactness]
\label{thm:real-cauchy-witness-grid-selector-exactness}
Every selector read admitted by an accepted $\RealCauchyWitnessGridUp$ carrier factors through the displayed finite route
$$
B \longmapsto (S,R) \longmapsto (L,T)
$$
before the completion-seal classifier $K$ or RealUp seal row $E$ can be consumed. Thus the selected modulus and window are the only selector source for both the ledger side and the tail-witness side of the grid.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier to $G=(B,L,K,T,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)$. The only selector-budget coordinate is $B$, and the only shared observation and regular-witness coordinates are $S$ and $R$.

Apply the NameCert obligation surface. It exposes selector-budget data through $B$, shared StreamName data through $S$, and RegSeqRat witness data through $R$ before either the ledger row $L$ or tail-witness row $T$ is consumed.

The route rows in $C$ replay exactly this order, while $H$ preserves the same coordinates under componentwise transport. No second selector or hidden window coordinate remains in the carrier, so the selector route is exact.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real Cauchy witness grid Real handoff]
\label{thm:real-cauchy-witness-grid-real-handoff}
Every Real-completion consumer of an accepted $\RealCauchyWitnessGridUp$ carrier reaches $\RealUp$ only through the finite route
$$
B,S,R,L,T,K,E,H,C,P,N.
$$
In particular, the consumer first reads the selector-budget row, the shared StreamName and RegSeqRat rows, the ledger and tail-witness rows, and the completion-seal classifier before receiving the RealUp seal or readback row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with selector exactness. By \autoref{thm:real-cauchy-witness-grid-selector-exactness}, the selected window and RegSeqRat witness are fixed by $B,S,R$ before the ledger row $L$ or tail-witness row $T$ can be used.

Read the sibling packets. The witness ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-witness-ledger-namecert} supplies the diagonal budget and synchronizer route, while the tail-witness envelope of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-tail-witness-envelope-namecert} supplies the finite tail readback route.

The completion-seal classifier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-completion-seal-classifier-namecert} is then reached through $K$, and the RealUp seal or readback row is $E$. The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve and name this route, so no Real-completion consumer receives a different handoff.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real Cauchy witness grid non-escape]
\label{thm:real-cauchy-witness-grid-nonescape}
An accepted $\RealCauchyWitnessGridUp$ carrier exports only the displayed rows $B,L,K,T,S,R,E,H,C,P,N$. It exports no quotient stream equality, host real equality, selected external limit, countable-choice row, hidden selector enlargement, unlisted tail witness, or ambient Real completeness theorem. \begin{proof}

The excluded objects have no coordinate in the tuple. Therefore no accepted consumer can recover them from the grid, and the Lean-facing nontriviality and field-faithfulness tasks must be witnessed over the displayed fields.
\end{proof}
