\begin{theorem}[GapClosureBoundary source-refusal admission triad]
\label{thm:gap-closure-boundary-source-refusal-admission-triad}
For every accepted $\GapClosureBoundaryUp$ carrier $B=(G,S,R,H,C,P,N)$, a downstream recognizer-facing request is admitted only through the displayed source-refusal triad: the request row $G$, the supported source route $G \longrightarrow S$, and the unsupported refusal route $G \longrightarrow R$, all transported, replayed, packaged, and named by $H,C,P,N$. The triad is read through the source-refusal decision surface, the consumer exactness package, the recognizer landing obligations, and the route completion surface. It exports no hidden closure row, hidden $\mathsf{T}$ supply, private source route, host equality row, quotient datum, direct aspect-reduction row, or off-packet continuation.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with \autoref{thm:gap-closure-boundary-source-refusal-decision-surface}. It separates each accepted read of $B$ into the supported route $G \longrightarrow S$ or the unsupported route $G \longrightarrow R$.

Next apply the consumer exactness package of \autoref{thm:gap-closure-boundary-consumer-exactness-package}. The listed hard-problem consumers read the shared carrier and receive no consumer-specific source, refusal, transport, continuation, provenance, or naming coordinate.

Use the recognizer landing obligations, \autoref{thm:gap-closure-boundary-recognizer-source-admission} and \autoref{thm:gap-closure-boundary-recognizer-refusal-stability}. Successful recognizer landings are consumed as the displayed source branch, and failed or unsupported landings are consumed as the displayed refusal branch.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:gap-closure-boundary-consumer-nonescape}. The completed route remains $G,S,R,H,C,P,N$, so the excluded hidden, host, quotient, aspect, and off-packet rows have no admitted coordinate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GapClosureBoundary refusal ledger exhaustion]
\label{thm:gap-closure-boundary-refusal-ledger-exhaustion}
For every accepted $\GapClosureBoundaryUp$ carrier $B=(G,S,R,H,C,P,N)$, the refusal ledger for downstream recognizer-facing requests is exhausted by the displayed refusal route $G \longrightarrow R$ with structural rows $H,C,P,N$. Any request that cannot enter the source branch through the recognizer landing surface remains at this refusal boundary, and the ledger supplies no hidden closure theorem, hidden $\mathsf{T}$ source, host equality, quotient object, private selector, or sibling-specific recognizer row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First read the unsupported side of the source-refusal decision surface. Without the displayed source route $G \longrightarrow S$, the accepted branch is the refusal route $G \longrightarrow R$.

Apply \autoref{thm:gap-closure-boundary-refusal-route-exactness} and \autoref{thm:gap-closure-boundary-refusal-ledger-stability}. These theorems identify $R$ as the refusal endpoint and preserve it under the structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

Then use recognizer refusal stability from \autoref{thm:gap-closure-boundary-recognizer-refusal-stability}. A failed landing after the $\GroundCompilerRecognizerFlowUp$ and $\FiniteReflectionTupleUp$ surfaces cannot be reread as source evidence.

The consumer non-escape boundary of \autoref{thm:gap-closure-boundary-consumer-nonescape} removes hidden closure, hidden $\mathsf{T}$ supply, host equality, quotient data, private selectors, and sibling-specific recognizer rows. Hence the refusal ledger is exhausted by the displayed refusal route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GapClosureBoundary downstream consumer boundary]
\label{thm:gap-closure-boundary-downstream-consumer-boundary}
Every downstream recognizer-facing consumer of an accepted $\GapClosureBoundaryUp$ packet either factors through the source-refusal admission triad of \autoref{thm:gap-closure-boundary-source-refusal-admission-triad} or remains at the refusal ledger boundary of \autoref{thm:gap-closure-boundary-refusal-ledger-exhaustion}. In particular, the consumers $\CausalCommitmentUp$, $\FiniteWitnessedRefutationUp$, $\OtherMindsCommitmentUp$, $\AspectChainUp$, and $\SolipsismGapRefusalUp$ receive only the displayed packet $G,S,R,H,C,P,N$ and the two visible source-refusal routes. They receive no bypass around the displayed refusal package and no hidden recognizer, solver, host equality, quotient, closure, or off-packet continuation.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with recognizer consumer exhaustion, \autoref{thm:gap-closure-boundary-recognizer-consumer-exhaustion}. It says successful recognizer landings are consumed as $G \longrightarrow S$ and failed or unsupported landings are consumed as $G \longrightarrow R$.

Apply the source-refusal admission triad to the successful side. The admitted route reads the shared request, source, refusal, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows before any downstream consumer receives the packet.

Apply refusal ledger exhaustion to the unsupported side. Requests not passing through the displayed source branch remain at $R$ with the same structural rows and cannot use a bypassing recognizer or solver route.

Finally combine supported-route totality with consumer non-escape, \autoref{thm:gap-closure-boundary-supported-route-totality} and \autoref{thm:gap-closure-boundary-consumer-nonescape}. The completed downstream boundary is exactly $G,S,R,H,C,P,N$, and every hidden recognizer, solver, host equality, quotient, closure, or off-packet continuation request is refused.
\end{proof}
