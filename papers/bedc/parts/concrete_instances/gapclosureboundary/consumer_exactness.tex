\begin{theorem}[GapClosureBoundary downstream branch determinacy]
\label{thm:gap-closure-boundary-downstream-branch-determinacy}
For every accepted $\GapClosureBoundaryUp$ carrier $B=(G,S,R,H,C,P,N)$ and every hard-problem consumer among $\CausalCommitmentUp$, $\FiniteWitnessedRefutationUp$, $\OtherMindsCommitmentUp$, $\AspectChainUp$, and $\SolipsismGapRefusalUp$, the consumer chooses exactly one of the displayed root branches: the source route
$$
G \longrightarrow S
$$
or the refusal route
$$
G \longrightarrow R.
$$
The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ carry the chosen branch and do not create a consumer-specific third route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with hard-problem consumer factorization and closure-request determinacy, \autoref{thm:gap-closure-boundary-hard-problem-consumer-factorization} and \autoref{thm:gap-closure-boundary-closure-request-determinacy}. They place every listed consumer inside the packet $G,S,R,H,C,P,N$ and determine whether a root request is source-supported or refusal-routed.

For the source case, apply sibling source totality. A supported consumer first displays the route $G \longrightarrow S$ before $H,C,P,N$ can transport, route, package, or name the read.

For the refusal case, apply refusal route determinacy. When the source route is absent, the same consumer is routed to $R$ over $H,C,P,N$ and cannot choose a hidden closure row, private source row, or hidden $\mathsf{T}$ supply.

Structural row conservation then excludes a third branch. The rows $H,C,P,N$ conserve the selected source/refusal branch rather than adding another consumer route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GapClosureBoundary refusal source coverage]
\label{thm:gap-closure-boundary-refusal-source-coverage}
The source and refusal branches of an accepted $\GapClosureBoundaryUp$ packet jointly cover all accepted hard-problem reads by $\CausalCommitmentUp$, $\FiniteWitnessedRefutationUp$, $\OtherMindsCommitmentUp$, $\AspectChainUp$, and $\SolipsismGapRefusalUp$. A listed read is accepted only through $G \longrightarrow S$ with $H,C,P,N$ or through $G \longrightarrow R$ with $H,C,P,N$; there is no accepted third route outside $G,S,R,H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First expose the shared boundary carrier by \autoref{def:gap-closure-boundary-carrier} and \autoref{thm:gap-closure-boundary-namecert-obligations}. The accepted packet has exactly the request, source, refusal, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.

Use source-refusal orthogonality to separate the two possible reads. The source branch carries a displayed source row, while the refusal branch carries a refusal ledger rather than concealed closure supply.

Apply downstream branch determinacy. Each listed hard-problem consumer is assigned to one of the two branches already displayed by the packet.

Finally apply structural row conservation and refusal non-escape. They rule out hidden closure, hidden $\mathsf{T}$ supply, private access, causal necessity, direct aspect reduction, and off-packet continuation, so the two branches jointly cover the accepted reads.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GapClosureBoundary consumer exactness package]
\label{thm:gap-closure-boundary-consumer-exactness-package}
The consumer exactness surface of $\GapClosureBoundaryUp$ is the finite package consisting of carrier admission for $B=(G,S,R,H,C,P,N)$, downstream branch determinacy, refusal-source coverage, structural row conservation, and the root-unblock package. This surface is exact for the listed hard-problem consumers: it exports the source branch, the refusal branch, and the rows $H,C,P,N$, but it exports no hidden closure, hidden $\mathsf{T}$ supply, private access route, causal-necessity row, direct aspect-reduction row, or off-packet continuation.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is given by \autoref{def:gap-closure-boundary-carrier} and \autoref{thm:gap-closure-boundary-namecert-obligations}. These results fix the finite packet $G,S,R,H,C,P,N$ before any listed consumer reads the boundary.

Next use downstream branch determinacy. It assigns each accepted consumer read to the displayed source route or the displayed refusal route over the same structural rows.

Then use refusal-source coverage. The two displayed branches cover all accepted hard-problem reads, so no consumer needs an extra route outside the finite packet.

Finally combine structural row conservation with the root-unblock package of \autoref{thm:gap-closure-boundary-root-unblock-package}. The structural rows carry only the selected branch, and the root package records the same source/refusal surface for $\CausalCommitmentUp$, $\FiniteWitnessedRefutationUp$, $\OtherMindsCommitmentUp$, $\AspectChainUp$, and $\SolipsismGapRefusalUp$.
\end{proof}
