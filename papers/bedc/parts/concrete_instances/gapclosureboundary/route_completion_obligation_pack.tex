\begin{theorem}[GapClosureBoundary NameCert route completion]
\label{thm:gap-closure-boundary-namecert-route-completion}
For every accepted $\GapClosureBoundaryUp$ carrier
$$
B=(G,S,R,H,C,P,N),
$$
the obligations of \autoref{thm:gap-closure-boundary-namecert-obligations} complete the route surface visible to causal commitment, finite witnessed refutation, other-minds commitment, aspect-chain, and solipsism-refusal consumers. A supported consumer reads the request row $G$ through the displayed source row $S$; an unsupported consumer reads $G$ through the refusal row $R$; and both routes are transported by $H$, replayed by $C$, packaged by $P$, and locally named by $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:gap-closure-boundary-carrier}. Its full consumer-visible inventory is $G,S,R,H,C,P,N$, with $H$ assigned to componentwise $\hsame$ transport, $C$ to displayed $\Cont$ replay, $P$ to $\Pkg$ provenance, and $N$ to the local $\NameCert$ row.

Apply \autoref{thm:gap-closure-boundary-namecert-obligations}. Carrier exposure gives the finite packet, source-route exactness sends supported reads through $S$, and refusal-ledger routing sends unsupported reads through $R$.

Finally use \autoref{thm:gap-closure-boundary-refusal-nonescape} together with the sibling consumer surface recorded in the carrier chapter. The causal commitment, finite witnessed refutation, other-minds commitment, aspect-chain, and solipsism-refusal consumers receive only the completed source-or-refusal route, so no consumer reads a hidden closure coordinate or a parameter-transport equivalence schema.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GapClosureBoundary refusal totality readback]
\label{thm:gap-closure-boundary-refusal-totality-readback}
For every accepted $\GapClosureBoundaryUp$ carrier $B=(G,S,R,H,C,P,N)$, every hard-problem consumer readback that cannot exhibit the source branch $G \longrightarrow S$ is total on the refusal branch $G \longrightarrow R$. The readback keeps the refusal row under the displayed $\hsame$ transports, $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and $\NameCert$ naming rows, and it exports no private other-minds access, causal necessity row, direct aspect-reduction row, hidden $\mathsf{T}$ supply, or off-packet continuation.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the source/refusal split supplied by \autoref{thm:gap-closure-boundary-namecert-route-completion}. A consumer readback is either supported by the displayed source row $S$ or is unsupported at the boundary.

In the unsupported case, apply \autoref{thm:gap-closure-boundary-refusal-nonescape}. The only available endpoint is the refusal ledger $R$, and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve that endpoint rather than replacing it with a source witness.

The listed sibling consumers do not add further coordinates. Causal commitment, finite witnessed refutation, other-minds commitment, aspect-chain, and solipsism-refusal consumers therefore read the same refusal branch when $S$ is absent, with no host-side completion route beyond $B$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GapClosureBoundary ledger exhaustion]
\label{thm:gap-closure-boundary-ledger-exhaustion}
For every accepted $\GapClosureBoundaryUp$ carrier $B=(G,S,R,H,C,P,N)$, the route ledger is exhausted by the source branch $G \longrightarrow S$, the refusal branch $G \longrightarrow R$, the componentwise $\hsame$ transport row $H$, the displayed $\Cont$ route row $C$, the $\Pkg$ provenance row $P$, and the local $\NameCert$ row $N$. No downstream consumer can extend the ledger to an internal universal closure row or to a separate parameter-transport equivalence surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First read the route-completion theorem. It identifies the only two consumer routes from $G$: the displayed source route and the displayed refusal route.

Next read refusal totality. Whenever the source route is absent, the readback is already total at $R$ under $H,C,P,N$.

It remains to check consumer exhaustion. The carrier of \autoref{def:gap-closure-boundary-carrier} and the obligation theorem of \autoref{thm:gap-closure-boundary-namecert-obligations} leave no row beyond $G,S,R,H,C,P,N$. Thus every downstream hard-problem consumer receives a finite ledger route and no extra closure, hidden source, private access, causal, aspect, or off-packet continuation coordinate.
\end{proof}
