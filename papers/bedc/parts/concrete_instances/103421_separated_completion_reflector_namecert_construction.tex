\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\SeparatedCompletionReflectorUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-separated-completion-reflector-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop--Bridges on constructive metric completion and Kelley on separated uniform reflection, the $\SeparatedCompletionReflectorUp$ packet records the L10 boundary where a displayed completion route may be read as separated before later complete-metric and real-completion consumers use it. It sits after the metric-completion carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metriccompletion-namecert}, the separated metric boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separatedmetric-namecert}, the uniform-completion route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformcompletion-namecert}, the completion universal-property row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformcompletion-namecert}, the Cauchy-filter surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyfilter-namecert}, the Cauchy-net surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchynet-namecert}, the regular-rational readback surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the real seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet is a finite reflector boundary, not a quotient completion, selected-limit principle, ambient complete space, host-equality bridge, or countable-choice schedule.

\begin{definition}[SeparatedCompletionReflector carrier]
\label{def:separated-completion-reflector-carrier}
A $\SeparatedCompletionReflectorUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
R=(M,S,U,Q,F,D,T,A,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the $\MetricCompletionUp$ row exposing the displayed metric-completion source and completion-facing read route.
\item $S$ is the $\SeparatedMetricUp$ row supplying the zero-distance classifier boundary before any completed read is treated as reusable.
\item $U$ is the $\UniformCompletionUp$ row comparing the same Cauchy data through the displayed uniform route.
\item $Q$ is the $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$ handoff row read only after $M,S,U$ have been displayed.
\item $F$ is the $\CauchyFilterUp$ branch row when the completion evidence is filter-presented.
\item $D$ is the $\CauchyNetUp$ branch row when the completion evidence is net-presented.
\item $T$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ tolerance-readback row used by numerical consumers.
\item $A$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row reached by real-completion consumers.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the completion, separation, uniform, universal-property, Cauchy, tolerance, real-seal, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the route
$$
M,S,U,F,D,T \longmapsto Q \longmapsto A \longmapsto H,P,N .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\SeparatedCompletionReflectorUp$, $\MetricCompletionUp$, $\SeparatedMetricUp$, $\UniformCompletionUp$, $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$, $\CauchyFilterUp$, $\CauchyNetUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\SeparatedCompletionReflectorUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate admits exactly these twelve rows. Its classifier compares accepted reflector packets only through $M,S,U,Q,F,D,T,A,H,C,P,N$ and has no coordinate for quotient completion, selected limits, host equality, countable choice, ambient completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[SeparatedCompletionReflector NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:separated-completion-reflector-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\SeparatedCompletionReflectorUp$ carrier
$$
R=(M,S,U,Q,F,D,T,A,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\SeparatedCompletionReflectorUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the metric-completion, separated-metric, uniform-completion, universal-property, Cauchy-filter, Cauchy-net, regular-rational, real-seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item separated-source exactness, requiring every reflector-facing completion read to project $M$ and then $S$ before $U,Q,A$ can be consumed;
\item branch visibility, requiring every Cauchy read to use the displayed filter branch $F$ or net branch $D$ before tolerance data in $T$ may be read;
\item universal handoff locality, requiring $Q$ to consume only the displayed $M,S,U,F,D,T$ surface and to export only the real-seal route $A$ plus structural rows;
\item classifier transport, requiring $\hsame$ comparison to preserve the completion, separation, uniform, universal-property, Cauchy, tolerance, real-seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows componentwise;
\item non-escape, excluding quotient completion, selected limits, host equality, countable choice, ambient completeness, Lean verification, and bridge checking.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored outside those rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:separated-completion-reflector-carrier}. The accepted packet exposes exactly the metric-completion, separated-metric, uniform-completion, universal-property, Cauchy-branch, tolerance, real-seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

Separated-source exactness starts at $M$ and $S$. A consumer cannot read the uniform route $U$, the universal-property handoff $Q$, or the terminal real seal $A$ until the displayed completion source and separated zero-distance classifier boundary have both been exposed.

Branch visibility is confined to $F,D,T$. The packet records whether the Cauchy evidence is filter-presented or net-presented before any regular-rational tolerance row can be inspected.

Finally $Q,A,H,C,P,N$ preserve the same finite route. The universal-property row consumes only the displayed completion, separation, uniform, Cauchy, and tolerance rows; the real-seal row is downstream of that handoff; and the structural rows transport, replay, source, and name only displayed coordinates. Since no excluded host principle has a carrier row, the listed obligations exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SeparatedCompletionReflector universal handoff]
\label{thm:separated-completion-reflector-universal-handoff}
Let
$$
R=(M,S,U,Q,F,D,T,A,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\SeparatedCompletionReflectorUp$ carrier. Every complete-metric or real-completion consumer reaches the universal-property row $Q$ and the terminal real-seal row $A$ only through the finite route
$$
M\longmapsto S,U\longmapsto F,D,T\longmapsto Q\longmapsto A\longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
The handoff refines the $\MetricCompletionUp$ and $\UniformCompletionUp$ surfaces by inserting the $\SeparatedMetricUp$ boundary before universal extension data are exported. It does not export quotient completion, selected limits, host equality, countable choice, ambient completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with an accepted carrier $R$. The metric-completion row $M$ is the only completion-object entry point, and the separated metric row $S$ is the only row that authorizes reuse of completed reads through zero-distance classifier evidence.

The uniform row $U$ and Cauchy branch rows $F,D$ are then read as constraints on the same completion data. The tolerance row $T$ supplies finite regular-rational readback before the universal-property row is allowed to export a consumer-facing extension route.

The row $Q$ is therefore downstream of the displayed completion, separation, uniform, Cauchy, and tolerance data. The real-seal row $A$ and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve that order, so the handoff reaches consumers without introducing any excluded host-side principle.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SeparatedCompletionReflector non-escape]
\label{thm:separated-completion-reflector-nonescape}
Every consumer of an accepted $\SeparatedCompletionReflectorUp$ carrier may inspect completion reflection only through the displayed rows
$$
\MetricCompletionUp,\ \SeparatedMetricUp,\ \UniformCompletionUp,\ \CompletionUniversalPropertyUp,\ \CauchyFilterUp,\ \CauchyNetUp,\ \RegSeqRatUp,\ \RealUp,\ \BHist,\ \hsame,\ \Cont,\ \Pkg,\ \NameCert .
$$
No consumer route can read a quotient completion, selected limit, host equality of completed points, countable-choice schedule, ambient complete object, Lean verification certificate, or standard bridge from this packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The non-escape claim is field exclusion. The carrier of \autoref{def:separated-completion-reflector-carrier} lists the only rows admitted by the packet, and the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:separated-completion-reflector-namecert-obligations} require every accepted read to project through those rows.

Any route asking for a quotient completion, selected limit, host equality, countable choice, ambient completeness, Lean verification, or standard bridge would require an additional coordinate. Since no such coordinate occurs in $R$, the local classifier has no accepted read for that request.
\end{proof}
