\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ManifoldUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-manifold-namecert}

The manifold interface $\ManifoldUp$ packages a topological carrier with a finite atlas of charts into $(\RealUp)^n$ whose transition maps are constructively smooth. It depends on $\NameCert_{\TopologyUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-topology-namecert}) and the real-analytic certificate (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-analytic-namecert}), and supplies the substrate for the bundle and de~Rham certificates.

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:manifold-carrier}

\begin{definition}[Charted manifold carrier]
\label{def:manifold-charted-carrier}
A charted $\ManifoldUp$ carrier package over a $\TopologyUp$ source is a tuple
$$
\mathcal{M}=(X,\mathcal{T},A,\mathsf{Dom},\mathsf{domIx},\mathsf{chart},\mathsf{coord},\mathsf{Trans})
$$
with the following displayed data. The point carrier is a predicate
$X:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ on BEDC histories, and
$\mathcal{T}$ is an indexed open topology carrier for $X$ in the sense of
\autoref{def:topology-indexed-open-carrier}. The atlas index family $A$ is
finite. For every $a:A$, the chart-domain predicate
$\mathsf{Dom}_a:X\to\mathsf{Prop}$ is carried by a witnessed open
$\mathsf{domIx}(a):\mathsf{OpenIx}$:
$$
\mathsf{CarriesOpen}_{\mathcal{T}}(\mathsf{domIx}(a),\mathsf{Dom}_a).
$$
The chart map $\mathsf{chart}_a$ sends a point $x$ with
$\mathsf{Dom}_a(x)$ to a finite list of coordinate histories
$$
\mathsf{coord}_a(x)=(r_1,\ldots,r_{n(a)})
$$
whose entries satisfy the $\RealUp$ carrier and are classified by the
$\RealUp$ classifier under replacement of $x$ along the topology carrier
classifier.

For every overlap row $a,b:A$ and every point $x$ lying in
$\mathsf{Dom}_a\cap\mathsf{Dom}_b$, the ledger includes a transition entry
$$
\mathsf{Trans}_{a,b}(x):
\mathsf{coord}_a(x)\longmapsto \mathsf{coord}_b(x)
$$
whose source and target coordinate lists are finite $\RealUp$ histories and
whose local transport row is certified by the $\RealAnalyticUp$ boundary of
\autoref{thm:real-analytic-certificate-boundary}. Thus the manifold carrier
does not add a new equality principle: point sameness is the topology carrier
classifier, coordinate sameness is the $\RealUp$ classifier, and overlap
smoothness is ledgered by supplied real-analytic rows.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Chart-atlas manifold carrier]
\label{def:manifold-chart-atlas-carrier}
A chart-atlas manifold carrier for $\ManifoldUp$ is a predicate
$\mathsf{ManAtlasPkg}(P)$ on $\mathsf{BHist}$ packages $P$ whose fields are the
following rows.
\begin{enumerate}
\item The traditional target is a finite-atlas topological manifold.
\item The BEDC source is $\TopologyUp$ together with $\RealUp$ coordinate
histories and real-analytic transition rows from
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-analytic-namecert}.
\item The carrier row contains a topological carrier history $M$ with its
$\TopologyUp$ certificate, a finite chart-index history $I$, chart-domain
predicates $\mathsf{Dom}_i$ on $M$, chart maps
$\varphi_i:\mathsf{Dom}_i\to(\RealUp)^{n_i}$, and overlap transition ledgers
$\mathsf{Trans}_{ij}$ for each chart pair $i,j$.
\item The classifier row is componentwise $\hsame$ on $M$, on $I$, on every
chart-domain row, on every chart-map row, and on every transition ledger.
\item The stability row says that componentwise $\hsame$ transport of those
fields carries $\mathsf{ManAtlasPkg}$ packages to
$\mathsf{ManAtlasPkg}$ packages.
\item The ledger records topology rows, real-coordinate rows, overlap rows, and
constructively smooth transition certificates for each nonempty chart overlap.
\item The standard bridge row is absent: this carrier names the BEDC
obligation surface and does not assert an external manifold equivalence.
\end{enumerate}
Equivalently, $\mathsf{ManAtlasPkg}(P)$ is the ten-field
$\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$ source object whose public carrier is the finite
chart atlas over $M$, whose classifier is the displayed componentwise
$\hsame$ classifier, and whose visible ledger is the topology-coordinate-overlap
ledger just listed.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[ManifoldUp chart cover coverage]
\label{thm:manifoldup-chart-cover-coverage}
For every package $P$ with $\mathsf{ManAtlasPkg}(P)$ and every carried point
history $x$ in the underlying $\TopologyUp$ carrier, the visible atlas ledger
exhibits a chart index $i$ together with the displayed chart-domain row
$\mathsf{Dom}_i(x)$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack $\mathsf{ManAtlasPkg}(P)$ by
\autoref{def:manifold-chart-atlas-carrier}. The carrier row says that $x$ is a
$\mathsf{BHist}$ point carried by the same $\TopologyUp$ carrier that supports
every chart domain.

The visible atlas ledger is part of the charted source object. Its chart-cover
row is indexed by the finite chart history and states that every carried point
is displayed in at least one domain row.

Project the cover witness for $x$. The projection returns a chart index $i$
and the corresponding membership evidence $\mathsf{Dom}_i(x)$, both living in
the same $\TopologyUp$ carrier row as $x$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ManifoldUp chart domain carrier transport]
\label{thm:manifoldup-chart-domain-carrier-transport}
Let $P$ be a package with $\mathsf{ManAtlasPkg}(P)$. If carried point histories
$x$ and $y$ are related by the $\TopologyUp$ carrier classifier and
$\mathsf{Dom}_i(x)$ holds for a displayed chart-domain row, then
$\mathsf{Dom}_i(y)$ holds after transporting the row along the classifier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The chart-domain row $\mathsf{Dom}_i$ is an indexed-open witness supplied by
the $\TopologyUp$ part of
\autoref{def:manifold-chart-atlas-carrier}. Its membership predicate is
therefore classified by the same carrier comparison as the underlying
topological points.

Apply the carrier classifier to the evidence that $x$ and $y$ are the same
carried point for the topology row. The indexed-open stability clause transports
membership in $\mathsf{Dom}_i$ from $x$ to $y$.

The transported domain row is still the displayed row for chart $i$, because
the chart classifier is componentwise $\hsame$ on chart-domain histories. Thus
the transported membership is exactly $\mathsf{Dom}_i(y)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ManifoldUp atlas transition composition]
\label{thm:manifoldup-atlas-transition-composition}
Let $P$ be a package with $\mathsf{ManAtlasPkg}(P)$. For chart indices
$i,j,k$ and a carried point history $x$ lying in the three displayed domains,
the overlap transition from $i$ to $k$ is witnessed by composing the supplied
$\RealAnalyticUp$ transition ledgers from $i$ to $j$ and from $j$ to $k$ on the
common overlap.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the transition rows of
\autoref{def:manifold-chart-atlas-carrier}. The hypotheses
$\mathsf{Dom}_i(x)$, $\mathsf{Dom}_j(x)$, and $\mathsf{Dom}_k(x)$ place $x$ in
both overlaps $(i,j)$ and $(j,k)$.

Project the two visible ledgers $\mathsf{Trans}_{ij}$ and
$\mathsf{Trans}_{jk}$. Each supplies a coordinate-change history between finite
$\RealUp$ windows together with a $\RealAnalyticUp$ local transition
certificate on its overlap.

Compose the coordinate histories at the shared $j$-coordinates. The
$\RealAnalyticUp$ local transition row is closed under this displayed
composition boundary, so the composed history carries a real-analytic
certificate on the triple-overlap row.

Record the composed certificate as the transition witness from $i$ to $k$ on
the common overlap. This witness is the atlas-transition composition required
by the $\ManifoldUp$ ledger.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ManifoldUp chart-domain intersection open]
\label{thm:manifoldup-chart-domain-intersection-open}
Let $P$ be a package with $\mathsf{ManAtlasPkg}(P)$. For chart indices $i,j$,
the $\TopologyUp$ index
$\mathsf{domIx}(i)\wedge_{\TopologyUp}\mathsf{domIx}(j)$ carries the overlap
predicate
$$
x\longmapsto \mathsf{Dom}_i(x)\wedge\mathsf{Dom}_j(x)
$$
on the underlying topological carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack $\mathsf{ManAtlasPkg}(P)$ by
\autoref{def:manifold-chart-atlas-carrier}. The carrier row exposes the common
$\TopologyUp$ carrier and, for every chart index, a displayed open index
$\mathsf{domIx}(-)$ carrying the corresponding chart-domain predicate.

Project the two rows
$\mathsf{CarriesOpen}_{\mathcal{T}}(\mathsf{domIx}(i),\mathsf{Dom}_i)$ and
$\mathsf{CarriesOpen}_{\mathcal{T}}(\mathsf{domIx}(j),\mathsf{Dom}_j)$. They
live over the same carrier because both chart domains are fields of the same
atlas package.

Apply \autoref{thm:topology-indexed-open-finite-intersection} to these two
carried opens. The resulting finite-intersection index is
$\mathsf{domIx}(i)\wedge_{\TopologyUp}\mathsf{domIx}(j)$, and its pointwise
readback is exactly the displayed conjunction of the two chart-domain
memberships.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ManifoldUp overlap transition source carried]
\label{thm:manifoldup-overlap-transition-source-carried}
Let $P$ be a package with $\mathsf{ManAtlasPkg}(P)$. Every displayed transition
row $\mathsf{Trans}_{ij}$ is sourced on the carried open overlap supplied by
\autoref{thm:manifoldup-chart-domain-intersection-open} and exposes finite
$\RealUp$ source and target coordinate rows
$\mathsf{coord}_i(x)$ and $\mathsf{coord}_j(x)$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Fix the chart pair $i,j$ and unpack the transition ledger of
\autoref{def:manifold-chart-atlas-carrier}. The ledger row for
$\mathsf{Trans}_{ij}$ is indexed by the same chart-domain predicates
$\mathsf{Dom}_i$ and $\mathsf{Dom}_j$ that appear in the carrier row.

Use \autoref{thm:manifoldup-chart-domain-intersection-open} to replace the
informal pairwise overlap by the explicit carried-open index
$\mathsf{domIx}(i)\wedge_{\TopologyUp}\mathsf{domIx}(j)$. Thus the source
predicate for the transition is not a hidden set-theoretic intersection; it is
the $\TopologyUp$ certificate carrying
$\mathsf{Dom}_i(x)\wedge\mathsf{Dom}_j(x)$.

Project the coordinate fields from the same atlas package. The source list
$\mathsf{coord}_i(x)$ and target list $\mathsf{coord}_j(x)$ are finite lists of
$\RealUp$ histories, and the transition row records the real-analytic
certificate between those two finite coordinate rows on the carried overlap.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ManifoldUp triple-overlap composition scope]
\label{thm:manifoldup-triple-overlap-open-composition-scope}
Let $P$ be a package with $\mathsf{ManAtlasPkg}(P)$. For chart indices $i,j,k$,
the triple-overlap predicate
$$
x\longmapsto
\mathsf{Dom}_i(x)\wedge\mathsf{Dom}_j(x)\wedge\mathsf{Dom}_k(x)
$$
is carried by iterating the $\TopologyUp$ finite-intersection index, and this
carried triple overlap is the scoped source on which
\autoref{thm:manifoldup-atlas-transition-composition} composes the two
real-analytic transition ledgers.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First build the pairwise overlap for $i$ and $j$ using
\autoref{thm:manifoldup-chart-domain-intersection-open}. This gives a
$\TopologyUp$ carried-open index for
$\mathsf{Dom}_i(x)\wedge\mathsf{Dom}_j(x)$ over the package carrier.

Project the chart-domain row for $k$ from
\autoref{def:manifold-chart-atlas-carrier}. Apply
\autoref{thm:topology-indexed-open-finite-intersection} to the carried
pairwise overlap and the carried open $\mathsf{Dom}_k$. The resulting iterated
index carries the displayed triple conjunction, with the parenthesizing read
through the pointwise conjunction witness.

Now restrict the transition ledgers $\mathsf{Trans}_{ij}$ and
$\mathsf{Trans}_{jk}$ to this carried triple overlap. The source and target
coordinate rows are finite $\RealUp$ lists by
\autoref{thm:manifoldup-overlap-transition-source-carried}, and the shared
$j$-coordinate row is exposed on the same carried source.

Only after this source has been carried, apply
\autoref{thm:manifoldup-atlas-transition-composition}. Its composition witness
therefore lives on the explicit $\TopologyUp$ triple-overlap certificate, not
on an implicit common-overlap predicate.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[Singleton empty-chart manifold package]
\label{def:manifold-singleton-empty-chart-package}
Let the BEDC manifold carrier be the singleton history predicate
$$
C_{\emp}(h)\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad \hsame(h,\emp)
$$
on $\mathsf{BHist}$. Its carrier classifier is $\hsame$ restricted to
$C_{\emp}$: two carried histories classify when they are internally the same,
and the carrier side conditions are transported along $\hsame$.

The topology surface is the inherited indexed $\TopologyUp$ boundary surface on
this carrier. The distinguished top index carries $C_{\emp}$ itself, and the
distinguished bottom index carries the empty predicate. The membership ledger
records the chosen history, its $C_{\emp}$ witness, the classifier row, and the
boundary open witness supplied by $\TopologyUp$.

The atlas has one chart $\chi_{\emp}$. Its domain is the top open of the
displayed topology surface. Its target is the constant zero endpoint of
$\RealUp$, read through the real-analytic local constant stream boundary. For
every carried $h$, the chart value is the same constant endpoint, and the chart
ledger records $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$,
$\TopologyUp$, $\RealUp$, and the $\RealAnalyticUp$ constant-map boundary.

Thus the singleton package supplies the following ten certificate rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item the traditional target is a one-point smooth manifold;
\item the BEDC source is $\mathsf{BHist}$ with $\emp$ as the displayed point;
\item the carrier choice is $C_{\emp}$;
\item the classifier choice is $\hsame$ restricted to $C_{\emp}$;
\item the stability obligations are carrier transport, top-domain transport,
bottom-domain exclusion, chart-value transport, and transition transport;
\item the ledger is the history, unary-history row, classifier row,
continuation row, topology boundary row, real constant endpoint row, and
real-analytic constant-map row;
\item the core theorem list is carrier topology scope, chart coverage,
transition smoothness, and the assembled $\ManifoldUp$ obligation package;
\item the exactness predicate says every chart-domain witness is exactly a
$C_{\emp}$ witness and every transition witness is the sole self-overlap row;
\item the standard bridge is absent;
\item the status is paper-side obligation target.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}
