\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\MetricCompletionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-metriccompletion-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, and Kelley-style metric completion, the $\MetricCompletionUp$ packet records the L10 metric-completion interface as finite Cauchy-filter or Cauchy-net evidence over a displayed metric source. It sits between the metric carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the filter surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyfilter-namecert}, the directed-net surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchynet-namecert}, the separated-limit uniqueness surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separatedmetric-namecert}, and the uniform-completion boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformcompletion-namecert}. The packet gives later complete-metric and real-completion consumers a named landing surface without quotienting Cauchy names, selecting representatives, assuming an ambient complete space, invoking open-cover compactness, using countable choice, or asserting completeness of $\RealUp$.

\begin{definition}[MetricCompletion carrier]
\label{def:metric-completion-carrier}
A $\MetricCompletionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
M=(X,F,D,R,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the $\MetricUp$ source row, including its point classifier, metric-distance witness surface, and displayed finite observation histories.
\item $F$ is either a $\CauchyFilterUp$ cofinal packet or an empty marker when the completion route is presented by directed nets.
\item $D$ is either a $\CauchyNetUp$ directed-window packet or an empty marker when the completion route is presented by Cauchy filters.
\item $R$ is the finite $\RegSeqRatUp$ or $\RealUp$ readback row used when a completion-facing consumer asks for numerical tolerance data.
\item $S$ is the $\SeparatedMetricUp$ uniqueness row, used only to identify two displayed limit readings through zero-distance classifier evidence.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the source metric, cofinal Cauchy packet, finite numerical readback, separatedness row, replay row, package row, and local naming row.
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ route replaying metric admission, Cauchy cofinality, readback extraction, separated-limit comparison, and completion handoff.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\MetricUp$, $\CauchyFilterUp$, $\CauchyNetUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\SeparatedMetricUp$, $\UniformCompletionUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MetricCompletionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for quotient completion equality, selected representatives, an ambient complete metric space, open-cover compactness, countable choice, or completeness of $\RealUp$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[MetricCompletion classifier]
\label{def:metric-completion-classifier}
The classifier of an accepted $\MetricCompletionUp$ packet relates two completion-facing reads precisely when their source metric row is the same displayed $X$, their cofinal Cauchy evidence is read from the same $F$ or $D$ branch, their finite $\RegSeqRatUp$ or $\RealUp$ tolerance rows agree after the componentwise $\hsame$ transport $H$, and the separatedness row $S$ identifies any two displayed limit readings through the zero-distance route already available in $\SeparatedMetricUp$.

Equivalently, the classifier is the finite replay path
$$
X,(F\ \hbox{or}\ D),R,S,H,C,P,N .
$$
It is not a quotient of Cauchy names, not a choice of canonical representatives, and not a claim that a host metric space is complete.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[MetricCompletion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:metric-completion-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\MetricCompletionUp$ carrier $M=(X,F,D,R,S,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\MetricCompletionUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed $\MetricUp$ source, $\CauchyFilterUp$ or $\CauchyNetUp$ cofinal packet, finite $\RegSeqRatUp$ or $\RealUp$ readback, $\SeparatedMetricUp$ uniqueness row, componentwise transport, continuation replay, provenance row, and local naming row;
\item cofinal evidence selection, requiring each completion-facing read to pass through exactly one visible branch, either $F$ or $D$;
\item finite readback compatibility, requiring numerical tolerance requests to factor through $R$ before any completion handoff is consumed;
\item separated-limit uniqueness, requiring equality of two displayed limit readings to pass through the zero-distance classifier route stored in $S$;
\item ledger non-escape, stating that no quotient completion, selected representative, ambient complete metric space, open-cover compactness principle, countable-choice schedule, or $\RealUp$ completeness theorem is exported by the packet.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:metric-completion-carrier}. The tuple lists the source metric row, the two possible Cauchy-evidence branches, the finite numerical readback, the separatedness row, and the structural support rows.

Cofinal evidence selection is local because the accepted carrier records the chosen branch in $F$ or $D$. A filter-presented route consumes the $\CauchyFilterUp$ row before any completion handoff, while a net-presented route consumes the $\CauchyNetUp$ directed-window row in the same position.

Finite readback compatibility is the role of $R$. Every tolerance-facing consumer reads the displayed $\RegSeqRatUp$ or $\RealUp$ data before it can compare completion endpoints.

Separated-limit uniqueness is supplied by $S$ and replayed by $C$ after transport by $H$. The provenance row $P$ and local naming row $N$ record that no further coordinate is available, so the excluded quotient, choice, compactness, ambient completeness, and real-completeness principles cannot be read from the certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetricCompletion Cauchy-filter handoff]
\label{thm:metric-completion-cauchy-filter-handoff}
Let $M=(X,F,D,R,S,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\MetricCompletionUp$ packet whose cofinal evidence is presented by the $\CauchyFilterUp$ branch $F$. Then every completion-facing consumer of $M$ factors first through the source metric row $X$, then through the displayed Cauchy-filter row $F$, then through the finite readback row $R$ when numerical tolerance data are requested, and only then through the separatedness row $S$ for comparison of displayed limit readings.

The handoff is therefore the finite route
$$
X\longmapsto F\longmapsto R\longmapsto S\longmapsto H,C,P,N ,
$$
not a quotient completion, not a selected representative, not an ambient complete metric space, and not an appeal to countable choice or $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet and choose the filter branch. The first row available to a completion consumer is the metric source $X$, so point admission and distance evidence are read before any completion-facing operation.

The next row is $F$. It is already a $\CauchyFilterUp$ finite-filterbase and late-window packet, so cofinality is consumed as displayed filter evidence rather than as a selected Cauchy representative.

When the consumer asks for tolerance data, the route passes through $R$. The replay row $C$ then carries the same source, filter, and readback rows to $S$, where separated-limit comparison is performed only through the local zero-distance classifier route.

The remaining rows $H,P,N$ transport and name the same finite packet. Since the route has no coordinate for quotient equality, selected representatives, ambient completeness, countable choice, or $\RealUp$ completeness, none of those principles is exported by the handoff.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetricCompletion separated limit uniqueness]
\label{thm:metric-completion-separated-limit-uniqueness}
Let $M=(X,F,D,R,S,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\MetricCompletionUp$ packet, and suppose two completion-facing reads of the same source metric packet use the same displayed cofinal branch $F$ or $D$ and the same finite readback row $R$ after transport by $H$. If the separatedness row $S$ displays zero distance between the two resulting limit readings, then the $\MetricCompletionUp$ classifier identifies the two reads.

This identification is internal to the displayed $\MetricUp$, $\CauchyFilterUp$ or $\CauchyNetUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$ or $\RealUp$, and $\SeparatedMetricUp$ rows. It is not host equality, not equality of quotient classes, and not a selected-limit principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the two completion-facing paths through the common source metric row $X$. The shared cofinal branch, either $F$ or $D$, keeps both paths on the same displayed Cauchy evidence.

Transport by $H$ aligns the finite readback row $R$ for the two paths. The replay row $C$ then presents the two limit readings to the separatedness row $S$.

By the meaning of $S$, zero distance between the two displayed limit readings yields classifier equivalence inside the underlying metric carrier. The definition of \autoref{def:metric-completion-classifier} uses exactly this route as the comparison criterion.

No quotient class, host equality, selected representative, ambient completeness row, countable-choice schedule, or $\RealUp$ completeness theorem appears among the fields of $M$, so the uniqueness conclusion remains a local classifier identification.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetricCompletion filter-net branch determinacy]
\label{thm:metric-completion-filter-net-branch-determinacy}
Let $M=(X,F,D,R,S,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\MetricCompletionUp$ packet. Every completion-facing read of $M$ uses exactly one visible cofinal branch, either the $\CauchyFilterUp$ row $F$ or the $\CauchyNetUp$ row $D$. A downstream read cannot mix filter evidence and net evidence unless an additional branch-conversion row is added, and no such row occurs in the carrier of \autoref{def:metric-completion-carrier}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The classifier of \autoref{def:metric-completion-classifier} reads the cofinal evidence from the single displayed branch recorded in the packet, written there as $F$ or $D$. Thus the accepted route selects one branch before the finite readback row $R$ and separatedness row $S$ are inspected.

In the filter case, every downstream comparison factors through the row $F$ named in the finite route. In the net case, the corresponding route factors through $D$ in the same position.

A mixed read would require a new row relating the filter and net evidence before $R$ and $S$ could be shared as one classifier path. The carrier has no coordinate for such a row, so the classifier route cannot manufacture it downstream.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetricCompletion finite-readback ledger non-escape]
\label{thm:metric-completion-finite-readback-ledger-nonescape}
For an accepted $\MetricCompletionUp$ carrier $M=(X,F,D,R,S,H,C,P,N)$, every tolerance read, readback request, and limit-comparison read exported by $M$ is exhausted by the rows
$$
X,F,D,R,S,H,C,P,N .
$$
Consequently the packet cannot introduce quotient classes, host equality, selected limits, open-cover compactness, countable choice, or completeness of $\RealUp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Tolerance-facing reads pass through the finite readback row $R$ after the source metric row $X$ and the chosen cofinal branch $F$ or $D$ have been displayed. The componentwise transport row $H$ and replay row $C$ preserve that same route.

Limit-comparison reads then pass through $S$. The separatedness row supplies only local zero-distance classifier evidence, so it compares displayed limit readings without changing the carrier into a host equality or quotient equality.

The provenance row $P$ and local naming row $N$ close the ledger at exactly the listed rows. Since no remaining coordinate names quotient classes, selected limits, open-cover compactness, countable choice, host equality, ambient completeness, or $\RealUp$ completeness, none of those data can escape from the finite readback ledger.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetricCompletion branch coverage]
\label{thm:metric-completion-branch-coverage}
Let
$$
M=(X,F,D,R,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\MetricCompletionUp$ packet. Every completion-facing read of $M$ uses either the $\CauchyFilterUp$ branch $F$ or the $\CauchyNetUp$ branch $D$ before any finite readback, separated-limit comparison, or completion handoff is inspected. A consumer that has not chosen one of these displayed branches has no route to $R$, $S$, $H$, $C$, $P$, or $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:metric-completion-carrier}. The only cofinal evidence fields are $F$ and $D$, and the classifier of \autoref{def:metric-completion-classifier} reads completion data through one of those fields before it reaches the readback row.

The existing handoff theorems identify the two possible routes. In the filter case, \autoref{thm:metric-completion-cauchy-filter-handoff} forces the path through $F$ before $R$ and $S$ are read. In the net case, \autoref{thm:metric-completion-cauchy-net-handoff} forces the same position to be occupied by $D$.

The remaining rows are structural. Transport, replay, provenance, and local naming preserve the chosen branch; they do not create a third Cauchy-evidence coordinate. Hence branch coverage is exactly the displayed filter-or-net dichotomy.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetricCompletion filter-net compatibility]
\label{thm:metric-completion-filter-net-compatibility}
Let $M=(X,F,D,R,S,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\MetricCompletionUp$ packet. Any consumer comparing filter and net presentations of the same completion-facing read must pass through the same source $\MetricUp$ row $X$, the same finite readback row $R$, the same $\SeparatedMetricUp$ row $S$, the componentwise $\hsame$ transport row $H$, the displayed $\Cont$ replay row $C$, the $\Pkg$ provenance row $P$, and the local $\NameCert_{\MetricCompletionUp}$ row $N$. The comparison cannot be made by quotienting the two Cauchy presentations or by selecting a representative common limit.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the branch coverage of \autoref{thm:metric-completion-branch-coverage}. A filter presentation and a net presentation are readable only as the two visible cofinal branches of the same carrier, so any comparison between them must keep the source row $X$ fixed.

Finite numerical data are then compared through $R$. The finite-readback ledger non-escape theorem, \autoref{thm:metric-completion-finite-readback-ledger-nonescape}, prevents a consumer from replacing this row by an external tolerance oracle or a quotient-class read.

Separated comparison is performed at $S$. The separated-limit uniqueness theorem, \autoref{thm:metric-completion-separated-limit-uniqueness}, supplies only local zero-distance classifier evidence, and $H,C,P,N$ preserve and name that same route.

Since no carrier coordinate stores quotient equality or a selected representative, the filter-net comparison is exhausted by the listed rows.
\end{proof}

The excluded completion principles are absent from \autoref{def:metric-completion-carrier} and are barred again by \autoref{thm:metric-completion-finite-readback-ledger-nonescape}. \begin{theorem}[MetricCompletion branch-conversion refusal]
\label{thm:metric-completion-branch-conversion-refusal}

\begin{proof}

Now apply \autoref{thm:metric-completion-branch-coverage}. A consumer that reads $F$ follows the filter handoff, while a consumer that reads $D$ follows the net handoff. To combine the two as one route, the consumer would need an additional row witnessing conversion from one displayed branch to the other.

The carrier inventory in \autoref{def:metric-completion-carrier} lists only $X,F,D,R,S,H,C,P,N$. The structural rows transport, replay, source, and name the selected route; they do not add a conversion coordinate. Hence a mixed read has no projection from the accepted packet.
\end{proof}
