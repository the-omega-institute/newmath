The $\DigestFiberLedgerUp$ surface realizes the hash-like apophatic fixed-point discipline of \autoref{ch:visions-hash-like-apophatic-fixed-point} as a finite NameCert packet. It sits beside the hash certificate of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-hash-namecert}, the apophatic naming certificate of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-apophaticname-namecert}, and the supply socket ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-supplysocketledger-namecert}. The packet keeps digest visibility, hidden fiber membership, GAP provenance, apophatic refusal, and consumer non-escape in one displayed carrier, so digest equality cannot silently become source equality.

\begin{definition}[DigestFiberLedger carrier]
\label{def:digest-fiber-ledger-carrier}
A $\DigestFiberLedgerUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
D=(V,F,G,A,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $V$ is the visible digest row, read through the local $\HashUp$ certificate surface;
\item $F$ is the fiber-membership row recording which source histories are carried behind the visible digest without identifying them with the digest;
\item $G$ is the $\mathsf{GAP}$ provenance row that makes the fiber membership auditable;
\item $A$ is the apophatic refusal row that blocks treating the far end of the fiber as a kernel object, source payload, or global digest;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the digest, fiber, GAP, refusal, continuation, provenance, and local certificate rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which consumers read $V$, $F$, $G$, and $A$ without obtaining source equality or an internal $\mathsf{T}$ object;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\HashUp$, $\ApophaticNameUp$, $\SupplySocketLedgerUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DigestFiberLedgerUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no coordinate for source equality derived from digest equality, no hidden preimage selector, no global universe digest, and no internal object representing the apophatic far end.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[DigestFiberLedger NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:digest-fiber-ledger-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:digest-fiber-ledger-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\DigestFiberLedgerUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for
$$
D=(V,F,G,A,H,C,P,N),
$$
visible digest exposure through $V$, fiber-membership exposure through $F$, GAP provenance through $G$, apophatic refusal through $A$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed continuation replay through $C$, package provenance through $P$, local naming through $N$, and non-escape for any consumer that tries to identify digest equality with source equality without the fiber and GAP rows.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DigestFiberLedgerUp.DigestFiberLedgerNameCertObligations}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier in \autoref{def:digest-fiber-ledger-carrier}. Its displayed coordinates are precisely the visible digest, fiber-membership, GAP provenance, apophatic refusal, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.

Read the three nearest sources through their displayed roles. The $\HashUp$ certificate supplies the digest-facing row, $\ApophaticNameUp$ supplies the refusal discipline for the far end of the fiber, and $\SupplySocketLedgerUp$ supplies the socket ledger that keeps the missing source from becoming an internal theorem.

The NameCert obligations are therefore exhausted by the rows of $D$. Since the carrier has no coordinate for source equality from digest equality, no hidden preimage selector, and no object-level far end, any accepted consumer must retain $F$, $G$, $A$, $H$, $C$, $P$, and $N$ when it cites the visible digest.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DigestFiberLedger non-escape]
\label{thm:digest-fiber-ledger-nonescape}
Every accepted $\DigestFiberLedgerUp$ consumer of a visible digest row factors through the displayed fiber-membership row $F$, GAP provenance row $G$, apophatic refusal row $A$, componentwise transport row $H$, continuation routes $C$, package provenance $P$, and local naming row $N$. No consumer can use equality of visible digest rows as equality of source histories unless an additional exactness certificate is displayed outside the $\DigestFiberLedgerUp$ packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted packet $D=(V,F,G,A,H,C,P,N)$. The visible row $V$ is only the digest-facing surface, while $F$ and $G$ record the fiber and its provenance.

Apply the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:digest-fiber-ledger-namecert-obligations}. A consumer route may cite the digest only through the same carrier that displays fiber membership, GAP provenance, apophatic refusal, componentwise transport, continuation replay, package provenance, and local naming.

The absence claim follows from the carrier inventory. No coordinate in $D$ turns digest equality into source equality, selects a preimage, exposes a global universe digest, or internalizes the apophatic far end; any such use requires a separate displayed exactness certificate.
\end{proof}
