It opens only finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows, reads dyadic endpoint and radius data through $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, exposes the regular-Cauchy source through $\RegSeqRatUp$, and seals the reciprocal candidate through $\RealUp$. \begin{definition}[CauchyInverseBudget carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-inverse-budget-carrier}
A $\CauchyInverseBudgetUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
B=(X,A,M,W,D,R,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the accepted $\RegSeqRatUp$ source whose reciprocal window is being read;
\item $A$ is the nonzero-apartness budget row, recording the displayed finite lower bound that keeps the reciprocal denominator away from the zero boundary;
\item $M$ is the finite modulus row selecting the observation precision required by the reciprocal read;
\item $W$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window selected from $X$ at the requested budget;
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint, radius, and reciprocal arithmetic row read from that window;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row obtained from $D$ together with the budget rows $A$ and $M$;
\item $S$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row through which completion-facing consumers read the reciprocal candidate;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $X,A,M,W,D,R,S$ and for the package and local naming rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes for source inspection, apartness-budget exposure, finite-window coverage, dyadic reciprocal readback, regular-sequence handoff, and RealUp seal handoff;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyInverseBudgetUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The local classifier compares accepted carriers only through the displayed rows $X,A,M,W,D,R,S,H,C,P,N$.
\end{definition}

The dyadic row $D$ is then a read of that window, not a read of an unbounded stream. Its endpoint, radius, and reciprocal arithmetic rows are all routed through the same $W$ coordinate and the same displayed continuation row $C$.

The support rows $H,P,N$ preserve, package, and name that finite route. Therefore downstream consumers reach $R$ and $S$ only after the displayed source, budget, modulus, window, and dyadic rows have been consumed.
