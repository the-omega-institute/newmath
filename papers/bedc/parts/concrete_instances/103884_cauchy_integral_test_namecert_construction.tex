\begin{theorem}[CauchyIntegralTest NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-integral-test-namecert-obligations}

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The row $F$ exposes only the nonnegative monotone real-name observations needed for the integral-test comparison, while the row $I$ exposes the inspected integral tail windows.

The row $S$ records the series-tail windows and their comparison to $I$. The rows $W$ and $R$ are the only routes through which the finite observations and rational readbacks are inspected.

\begin{theorem}[CauchyIntegralTest tail boundary]
\label{thm:cauchy-integral-test-tail-boundary}

$$

$$
before it can be used as convergence-test evidence. Thus the packet exports only a displayed tail-window boundary, not an ambient theorem that all monotone real functions have improper integrals.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

The integral row $I$ and readback row $R$ expose the finite Darboux or Riemann bounds attached to those windows. Only after those rows are visible may the series-tail row $S$ be compared against them.

Therefore a consumer that bypasses $F,W,I,R$ or treats the route as a host improper-integral theorem leaves the accepted carrier surface.
