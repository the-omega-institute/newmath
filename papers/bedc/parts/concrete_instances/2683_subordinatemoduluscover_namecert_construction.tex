The $\SubordinateModulusCoverUp$ packet names the finite cover returned before the compact-uniform-continuity rational lower-bound fold. It sits between the compact finite-net carrier and the $\UniformModulusUp$ consumer: the packet records bundled centers, pointwise-modulus precision rows, subordinate positive-radius rows, half-radius coverage witnesses, rational comparison ledgers, and the local provenance needed to hand the bundle to the uniform-modulus fold.

\begin{definition}[SubordinateModulusCover carrier]
\label{def:subordinate-modulus-cover-carrier}
A $\SubordinateModulusCoverUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(E,\mathcal{P},C,R,D,M,H,F,L,Q,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $E$ is the positive $\RatUp$ tolerance history requested by the compact-continuous consumer;
\item $\mathcal{P}$ is the nonempty $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ spine returned by the compact finite-net certificate;
\item $C$ assigns to each bundled probe its displayed center history;
\item $R$ assigns to each bundled probe a positive subordinate radius history;
\item $D$ assigns to each bundled probe the pointwise-modulus precision history supplied at its center;
\item $M$ records the accepted pointwise-modulus row for the tolerance fraction read from $E$;
\item $H$ records the half-radius coverage row saying that every source history is covered by a bundled center at the displayed subordinate radius;
\item $F$ records the rational comparison ledger witnessing that every subordinate radius row is below its pointwise precision row;
\item $L$ records componentwise $\hsame$ stability for the tolerance, bundle, centers, radius rows, precision rows, pointwise rows, coverage rows, and rational comparisons;
\item $Q$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes through which compact and continuous consumers read the packet;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CompactUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\MetricUp$, $\RatUp$, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and the local naming rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\SubordinateModulusCoverUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exports no open cover, no external radius selector, no host minimum, and no uniform precision row. Its role is exactly the finite subordinate bundle consumed before the $\UniformModulusUp$ lower-bound fold.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[SubordinateModulusCover NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:subordinate-modulus-cover-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\SubordinateModulusCoverUp}$ surface for \autoref{def:subordinate-modulus-cover-carrier} consists of carrier admission for $S=(E,\mathcal{P},C,R,D,M,H,F,L,Q,P,N)$, classifier stability under the componentwise $\hsame$ rows in $L$, compact coverage exactness for the half-radius rows in $H$, pointwise-modulus compatibility for the rows in $M$ and $D$, rational subordination exactness for the comparison rows in $F$, and consumer non-escape through the displayed $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local naming rows.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is the displayed finite packet. The tolerance row, nonempty bundle, centers, subordinate radii, pointwise precision rows, pointwise-modulus rows, coverage witnesses, rational comparisons, transport ledger, continuation routes, package provenance, and local naming row are all visible coordinates.

Classifier stability is componentwise over those coordinates. The transport ledger $L$ can rename bundled probes, centers, radius histories, precision histories, coverage rows, and comparison rows only through the displayed $\hsame$ entries, and the routes in $Q$ preserve the compact and continuous reads attached to the same bundle.

Coverage exactness is the half-radius row $H$. Pointwise compatibility is the row $M$ at each center together with the precision row in $D$. Rational subordination is the row $F$, which binds each radius row in $R$ below the corresponding precision row in $D$.

These rows exhaust the consumer surface. Since no coordinate stores an open cover, an external selector, a host minimum, or a uniform precision history, the local naming certificate cannot export any of those objects.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SubordinateModulusCover uniform-modulus handoff]
\label{thm:subordinate-modulus-cover-uniform-handoff}
Every accepted $\SubordinateModulusCoverUp$ packet hands a $\UniformModulusUp$ consumer exactly the nonempty $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ spine, bundled centers, positive subordinate radius rows, pointwise-modulus precision rows, half-radius coverage rows, and rational subordination comparisons needed by the finite lower-bound fold. The handoff does not yet choose the uniform precision; it supplies the finite input ledger from which the $\UniformModulusUp$ fold computes that precision.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet through \autoref{def:subordinate-modulus-cover-carrier}. The consumer receives $\mathcal{P}$, $C$, $R$, $D$, $M$, $H$, and $F$ through the displayed routes in $Q$ and the provenance rows $P,N$.

The nonempty bundle and coverage rows are the compact side of the handoff. The pointwise-modulus and precision rows are the continuous side. The rational comparison ledger records the subordination that permits the later lower-bound fold to stay below every bundled half-radius row.

No fold output is present in the carrier. Therefore the handoff is precisely the finite subordinate-cover input to the $\UniformModulusUp$ rational fold, with no external modulus selector and no host-level compactness object.
\end{proof}
