Following Bishop and Bridges, the $\CompactIntervalFixedPointUp$ packet records the constructive one-dimensional Brouwer fixed-point theorem as a finite interval-return route. A candidate continuous self-map on a located compact interval is first read through the $\LocatedIntervalUp$ endpoint surface and the $\ContinuousMapUp$ graph and modulus rows; its residual $g(x)=f(x)-x$ is then consumed by the intermediate-value surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-intermediatevalue-namecert} and the bisection route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-intermediate-value-bisection-namecert}; the resulting finite windows are handed to the stream, regular-rational, and real-name surfaces, including the seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The certificate names only this displayed finite route. It does not claim the general Brouwer fixed-point theorem, Heine--Borel compactness, host completeness, quotient equality of streams, real trichotomy, or a choice-selected fixed point.

\begin{definition}[Compact interval fixed-point carrier]
\label{def:compact-interval-fixed-point-carrier}
A $\CompactIntervalFixedPointUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
F=(J,G,R,B,W,Q,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $J$ is the $\LocatedIntervalUp$ interval-endpoint packet, including the lower and upper endpoint names, rational cells, and located-order witness used for every later read;
\item $G$ is the $\ContinuousMapUp$ graph and pointwise-modulus evidence for the displayed interval-returning map, together with the residual read route for $g(x)=f(x)-x$;
\item $R$ is the interval-return row asserting that every candidate read of the map is routed back into the same located interval packet $J$ before residual signs are inspected;
\item $B$ is the $\IntermediateValueBisectionUp$ residual-bisection row obtained from the endpoint residual signs, the modulus evidence in $G$, and the interval-return row $R$;
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window surface for the retained bisection interval and the displayed candidate fixed-point name;
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row translating the retained windows into regular rational approximants at the requested dyadic tolerance;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal exposed to downstream consumers only after the retained windows and regular rational readback have been displayed;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $J,G,R,B,W,Q,E$ and the local certificate row;
\item $C$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ replay routes for interval-endpoint reads, continuous-map reads, interval-return reads, residual bisection, finite windows, regular readback, and real-seal consumption;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LocatedIntervalUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\IntermediateValueUp$, $\IntermediateValueBisectionUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CompactIntervalFixedPointUp}$ registration row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate says that these rows form one finite fixed-point certificate for a displayed interval-returning continuous map. The packet has no coordinate for a host fixed point, open-cover compactness, general Brouwer fixed points, real trichotomy, quotient equality of streams, selected limits, or ambient completeness of $\RealUp$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CompactIntervalFixedPoint NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:compact-interval-fixed-point-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted packet $F=(J,G,R,B,W,Q,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CompactIntervalFixedPointUp}$ obligations are exhausted by the carrier rows of \autoref{def:compact-interval-fixed-point-carrier}. They consist of carrier admission, interval-return exactness, continuous-map modulus replay, residual-sign bisection, stream-window handoff, regular-rational readback, real-seal nonescape, componentwise transport, displayed continuation replay, provenance, and local naming. No obligation may call on a host fixed point, open-cover compactness, general Brouwer fixed points, real trichotomy, quotient stream equality, selected limits, countable choice, or ambient Real completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from the displayed packet. The rows $J,G,R,B,W,Q,E,H,C,P,N$ are exactly the data listed in \autoref{def:compact-interval-fixed-point-carrier}.

The analytic obligations are routed in order. The interval-return row $R$ binds every map read back to the same located interval packet $J$; the continuous-map row $G$ supplies the graph and modulus evidence; and the residual bisection row $B$ reads endpoint signs only after those two rows have been fixed.

The consumer-facing obligations then read the retained windows through $W$, the regular rational approximants through $Q$, and the real seal through $E$. The rows $H,C,P$, and $N$ transport, replay, record provenance, and register the same finite route.

Since no coordinate outside this route is present, the listed classical, host-completion, quotient, trichotomy, choice, and selected-limit principles cannot enter the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Interval-return fixed-point route]
\label{thm:compact-interval-fixed-point-interval-return-route}
Every candidate fixed-point read accepted by a $\CompactIntervalFixedPointUp$ carrier first consumes the interval-return row $R$ and the continuous-map row $G$ before it can reach the residual bisection row $B$. Consequently the read factors through
$$
J,G,R,B,W,Q,E,H,C,P,N .
$$
It cannot inspect a candidate real name as a fixed point unless the same route has already tied the map value, residual sign data, retained bisection window, stream windows, regular readback, and real seal to the displayed located interval.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted carrier $F=(J,G,R,B,W,Q,E,H,C,P,N)$. The continuation row $C$ provides no direct path from a consumer to $E$ or to a candidate fixed-point name; it first replays the endpoint and map reads through $J$ and $G$.

The interval-return row $R$ is then mandatory. It records that every displayed value of the map is read back inside the same located interval packet, so the residual sign test is a row over $J$ and $G$, not a host-level comparison.

Only after that read does the residual bisection row $B$ become available. The retained interval supplies windows $W$, the windows supply regular rational readback $Q$, and the readback supplies the Real-facing seal $E$.

The transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows preserve this order. Thus every accepted fixed-point read factors through the displayed route and has no bypass through a host fixed point or external equality principle.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CompactIntervalFixedPoint Real-seal nonescape]
\label{thm:compact-interval-fixed-point-real-seal-nonescape}
The $\RealUp$ seal exported by an accepted $\CompactIntervalFixedPointUp$ packet is reachable only through $J,G,R,B,W,Q,E,H,C,P,N$. The packet exports no host fixed point, no open-cover compactness, no general Brouwer fixed-point theorem, no real trichotomy, no quotient stream equality, no selected limit, and no ambient completeness principle for $\RealUp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The only Real-facing coordinate is $E$. By definition of the carrier, $E$ is paired with the windows $W$ and the regular rational readback $Q$, and both are paired with the residual bisection row $B$.

The bisection row itself is not free-standing. It is generated from the interval-return and continuous-map rows $R$ and $G$ over the same located interval packet $J$, with transports and replay stored in $H$ and $C$.

Package provenance $P$ and the local naming row $N$ register precisely this finite route. Because no other Real-facing or compactness-facing coordinate exists in the packet, a consumer receives only the displayed real-name seal and none of the excluded classical or host principles.
\end{proof}
