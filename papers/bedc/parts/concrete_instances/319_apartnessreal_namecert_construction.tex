\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ApartnessRealUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-apartnessreal-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\ApartnessRealUp$ interface names constructive apartness evidence between displayed real-name presentations. Following the Bishop--Bridges 1985 constructive-analysis treatment, it records positive finite separation witnesses in place of a trichotomy principle. It is a companion to $\RealUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$, not a quotient construction and not a completeness theorem.

Its certificate consumes the sealed real-name surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the regular Cauchy source route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic positive-radius rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the finite observation windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}. These four sibling chapters supply, respectively, the endpoint names, source classifier, positive radius, and finite window rows used by the apartness packet, so the positive-separation certificate is routed through the real-completion lattice rather than through an isolated kernel packet.

\section{Carrier and classifier}
\label{sec:apartnessreal-carrier-classifier}

\begin{definition}[ApartnessReal carrier]
\label{def:apartnessreal-carrier}
An $\ApartnessRealUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet containing two displayed $\RealUp$ names, or equivalently two displayed $\RegSeqRatUp$ source packets at the real-seal boundary, together with the finite rows needed to witness positive separation. The packet carries:
\begin{enumerate}
\item the left and right real-name rows, with their visible $\RealUp$ or $\RegSeqRatUp$ provenance;
\item a positive $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ radius row $\rho$;
\item a finite $\StreamNameUp$ observation window shared by the two presentations;
\item two endpoint readbacks $a$ and $b$ from that window;
\item a separation ledger displaying that the readbacks are separated by the radius $\rho$.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier accepts only the displayed positive-separation packet. It does not decide whether arbitrary real names are equal or apart, does not assert trichotomy, and does not add quotient classes of real-name presentations.
\end{definition}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ApartnessRealUp.ApartnessRealTasteGate\_single\_carrier\_alignment}

\begin{definition}[ApartnessReal classifier]
\label{def:apartnessreal-classifier}
For accepted $\ApartnessRealUp$ packets $P$ and $Q$, the classifier $\mathsf{ApartnessRealClassifier}(P,Q)$ holds when every visible component of the separation packet transports componentwise through the available classifiers. The two real names transport by the existing $\RealUp$ classifier, or by the $\RegSeqRatUp$ source classifier before the real-seal handoff; the radius row transports by the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ classifier; the finite observation window transports by the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule classifier; the two endpoint readbacks transport through the same $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ routes; and the separation ledger is repacked after those componentwise transports.

Thus the classifier identifies two presentations only as presentations of the same finite apartness evidence. It supplies no host real inequality, no quotient equality of Cauchy streams, and no law deciding equality versus apartness for arbitrary real names.
\end{definition}

\section{Certificate obligations}

\begin{theorem}[ApartnessReal obligation surface]
\label{thm:apartnessreal-namecert-obligation-surface}
The local $\NameCert_{\ApartnessRealUp}$ obligation surface has five finite rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier acceptance for the packet of \autoref{def:apartnessreal-carrier};
\item classifier stability for the componentwise transport relation of \autoref{def:apartnessreal-classifier};
\item symmetry, obtained by swapping the two endpoint readbacks and reversing the displayed separation ledger;
\item exactness, read as the finite-window separation witness exposed by the $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ rows;
\item ledger visibility, asserting that the positive radius, window, endpoint readbacks, and separation ledger remain visible through the displayed $\Cont$ and $\Pkg$ rows.
\end{enumerate}
The surface supplies apartness evidence only. It does not supply trichotomy of constructive reals, completeness of real names, a metric-completion object, or a quotient carrier.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ApartnessRealUp.ApartnessRealNameCertObligationSurface\_rows}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ApartnessRealUp.ApartnessRealCarrier\_namecert\_obligation\_surface}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier packet. Its rows are exactly the two real-name presentations, the positive dyadic radius, the finite stream window, the two endpoint readbacks, and the displayed separation ledger.

Project the classifier next. Componentwise transport of the two real-name rows, radius row, stream window, endpoint rows, and ledger is precisely the stability face of the certificate.

For symmetry, swap the left and right endpoint readbacks while keeping the same positive radius and observation window. The separation ledger is read in the reverse direction, and the componentwise $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ routes repack the swapped packet.

Exactness and visibility are finite-readback claims: every apartness witness consumed downstream is one of the displayed finite rows, and every such row remains inspectable through its $\Cont$ and $\Pkg$ provenance. Since the packet has no coordinate for trichotomy, completeness, or quotient classes, those principles cannot be exported.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ApartnessRealUp.ApartnessRealSeparationPacket\_namecert\_obligation\_surface}

\begin{theorem}[ApartnessReal symmetry stability]
\label{thm:apartnessreal-symmetry-stability}
If an accepted $\ApartnessRealUp$ packet witnesses positive separation from a displayed real name $x$ to a displayed real name $y$, then swapping the two real-name rows and the two endpoint readbacks produces an accepted $\ApartnessRealUp$ packet witnessing positive separation from $y$ to $x$. The swapped packet preserves the same positive $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ radius row, the same finite $\StreamNameUp$ observation window, and the same $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and $\NameCert$ provenance.

This symmetry is stability of the displayed apartness evidence. It is not a decision procedure for whether $x$ and $y$ are equal or apart.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ApartnessRealUp.ApartnessRealCarrier\_symmetry\_stability}
\begin{proof}
Start from the accepted carrier of \autoref{def:apartnessreal-carrier}. It contains the two real-name rows, the shared finite observation window, the positive radius, the two endpoint readbacks, and the separation ledger.

Exchange the left and right real-name rows and exchange the two endpoint readbacks. The finite observation window and the positive radius row are unchanged.

The ledger is repacked by reading the separation row in the opposite endpoint order and carrying along the same $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and $\NameCert$ provenance. The classifier of \autoref{def:apartnessreal-classifier} accepts this componentwise transport, so the swapped packet is again an accepted apartness witness. No equality-or-apartness case split is introduced.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ApartnessRealUp.ApartnessRealSeparationPacket\_symmetry\_stability}

\begin{definition}[ApartnessReal finite-window separation row]
\label{def:apartnessreal-finite-window-separation-row}
An $\ApartnessRealUp$ finite-window separation row is the displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ tuple consisting of:
\begin{enumerate}
\item a left endpoint row and a right endpoint row, each read either as a displayed $\RealUp$ name or as its $\RegSeqRatUp$ source row at the real-seal boundary;
\item one positive $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ radius row $\rho$;
\item one finite $\StreamNameUp$ observation window shared by the two endpoint presentations;
\item two endpoint readbacks from that window, one for each endpoint row;
\item one separation ledger witnessing that the two readbacks are separated by the radius $\rho$.
\end{enumerate}
The tuple is accepted only when these six rows are visible through the same $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and $\NameCert$ provenance used by \autoref{def:apartnessreal-carrier} and \autoref{def:apartnessreal-classifier}. It is a finite certificate row, not a host-level inequality between real numbers and not a quotient class of real-name presentations.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[ApartnessReal finite-window separation transport]
\label{thm:apartnessreal-finite-window-separation-transport}
Let $R$ and $S$ be accepted $\ApartnessRealUp$ finite-window separation rows in the sense of \autoref{def:apartnessreal-finite-window-separation-row}. If the two endpoint rows, the positive radius row, the finite observation window, the two endpoint readbacks, and the separation ledger of $R$ transport componentwise to the corresponding rows of $S$ through the displayed $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and $\NameCert$ routes, then $S$ repacks as an accepted finite-window separation row.

This transport adds no trichotomy principle, quotient carrier, total order, or completeness datum for constructive reals.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ApartnessRealUp.ApartnessRealSeparationPacket\_finite\_window\_transport}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier data from \autoref{def:apartnessreal-carrier}. The only coordinates present are the two endpoint presentations, the positive dyadic radius, the finite stream window, the two endpoint readbacks, and the separation ledger.

Use \autoref{def:apartnessreal-classifier} to transport those coordinates one by one. The real-name endpoints transport either through their displayed $\RealUp$ classifiers or through their $\RegSeqRatUp$ source classifiers before the real-seal boundary; the radius and window rows transport through their own classifiers; the endpoint readbacks follow the same $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ routes.

It remains to repack the ledger. Since the ledger is assumed to transport componentwise through the same visible $\mathsf{Pkg}$ and $\NameCert$ provenance, the target tuple has exactly the six rows required by \autoref{def:apartnessreal-finite-window-separation-row}. No additional coordinate can be read from this repacking, so no excluded-order, quotient, or completion principle is exported.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ApartnessReal located-window positive gap handoff]
\label{thm:apartnessreal-located-window-positive-gap-handoff}
Let $L$ and $R$ be displayed $\LocatedRealUp$ packets. Suppose they share a finite $\StreamNameUp$ observation window and their $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint ledgers display two disjoint enclosures with positive dyadic gap row $\rho$. If the two endpoint real-name rows, the common finite window, the two endpoint readbacks, and the visible gap ledger are all carried by the displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and $\NameCert$ provenance of the two packets, then the tuple consisting of those two endpoint real-name rows, $\rho$, the common finite window, the two endpoint readbacks, and the visible separation ledger repacks as an accepted $\ApartnessRealUp$ finite-window separation row in the sense of \autoref{def:apartnessreal-finite-window-separation-row}.

The handoff exports finite positive-gap evidence only. It does not decide equality of real names, does not supply trichotomy or a total order, and does not identify located real packets by a quotient or host real equality.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ApartnessRealUp.ApartnessRealSeparationPacket\_located\_window\_positive\_gap\_handoff}
\begin{proof}
Project both located packets through \autoref{def:locatedreal-carrier-surface}. The carrier surface exposes the $\RegSeqRatUp$ source rows, the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule row, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint ledger, and the locatedness classifier rows, all tied to the visible provenance.

Apply \autoref{thm:locatedreal-scope-regseqrat-dyadic-window} to read the shared observation window as a finite scheduled window. Apply \autoref{thm:locatedreal-interval-ledger-exactness} to identify the two endpoint readbacks with rows of the displayed dyadic endpoint ledgers.

The disjoint-enclosure hypothesis supplies the positive dyadic gap row $\rho$. Repack that row as the separation ledger, using the same common window, endpoint readbacks, and provenance rows already projected from the two located packets.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:apartnessreal-finite-window-separation-transport} and then \autoref{thm:apartnessreal-metric-consumer-separation-boundary}. The first theorem identifies the repacked tuple as a stable finite-window separation row, and the second theorem restricts every later metric or order consumer to exactly that finite apartness evidence.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Localized Cauchy apartness bridge]
\label{thm:localized-cauchy-apartness-bridge}
Let $L$ and $R$ be displayed $\LocatedRealUp$ packets whose finite $\StreamNameUp$ observation window, $\RegSeqRatUp$ source rows, positive $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ gap row, endpoint readbacks, and $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and $\NameCert$ provenance agree componentwise. Suppose the displayed endpoint ledgers exhibit a positive located-window gap in the sense of \autoref{thm:apartnessreal-located-window-positive-gap-handoff}. Then the resulting $\ApartnessRealUp$ finite-window separation row is stable under the $\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier routes and may be consumed by $\RealUp$ metric or order interfaces only as positive apartness evidence.

The bridge exports no host inequality, quotient equality, trichotomy decision, total order, or completeness datum over the real or completion substrate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:apartnessreal-located-window-positive-gap-handoff} to the two displayed located-real packets. The shared finite window, regular Cauchy source rows, positive dyadic gap row, endpoint readbacks, and visible provenance repack as the finite separation row of \autoref{def:apartnessreal-finite-window-separation-row}.

Next read the packet through \autoref{def:apartnessreal-classifier}. The componentwise agreement hypotheses are exactly the classifier coordinates needed to transport the two endpoint rows, the common window, the gap row, the two readbacks, and the separation ledger along the displayed $\RegSeqRatUp$ routes.

Apply \autoref{thm:apartnessreal-finite-window-separation-transport} to make that transport stable. The transported packet is still the same finite positive-gap row, now presented over the regular Cauchy classifiers feeding the real-name substrate.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:apartnessreal-metric-consumer-separation-boundary}. It restricts every $\RealUp$ metric or order consumer to the finite positive-apartness row and excludes any readback of host inequality, quotient equality, trichotomy, total order, or completion data.
\end{proof}
