\begin{theorem}[DyadicIntervalBasis NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:dyadic-interval-basis-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\DyadicIntervalBasisUp$ carrier
$$
D=(I,E,O,R,B,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\DyadicIntervalBasisUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the dyadic interval cell, dyadic endpoint ledger, rational containment row, finite readback row, order-topology basis row, transport, replay, provenance, and local name;
\item endpoint exactness, requiring each lower and upper endpoint read of $I$ to be sourced by the dyadic ledger $E$ and bounded by the rational containment row $O$;
\item basis generation, requiring every basis read in $B$ to arise from the displayed dyadic cell together with the stream-window, regular-rational, and real-seal data in $R$;
\item refinement routing, requiring any accepted subcell or containment refinement to pass through the same $I,E,O,R,B$ rows before it reaches a consumer;
\item structural stability, requiring $H$ and $C$ to preserve and replay only the displayed packet rows.
\end{enumerate}
No DyadicIntervalBasis obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:dyadic-interval-basis-carrier}. The visible coordinates are exactly the dyadic interval cell, endpoint ledger, rational containment row, finite readback row, order-topology basis row, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

Endpoint exactness is forced by the displayed order. The dyadic cell $I$ has no endpoint source except the dyadic ledger $E$, and the rational containment row $O$ is the only interval bound attached to those endpoints.

Basis generation then reads through $R$ and $B$. The stream windows, regular-rational observations, and real seal in $R$ are the only real-facing data available before the order-topology basis row $B$ is exposed.

Finally, refinement routing and structural stability are exhausted by $H,C,P,N$. These rows can transport, replay, source, and name the displayed packet, but they add no coordinate for Dedekind completeness, open-cover compactness, quotient interval equality, host real equality, countable choice, or a selected completion point.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicIntervalBasis refinement stability]
\label{thm:dyadic-interval-basis-refinement-stability}
Let $D=(I,E,O,R,B,H,C,P,N)$ and $D'=(I',E',O',R',B',H',C',P',N')$ be accepted $\DyadicIntervalBasisUp$ carriers. If $D'$ is obtained from $D$ by a displayed dyadic subcell refinement whose endpoints are transported by the stored $\hsame$ rows and replayed through the stored $\Cont$ routes, then $D'$ is accepted by the same local classifier. The transported packet preserves the $\DyadicIntervalUp$ cell, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint ledger, $\RationalIntervalUp$ containment row, $\StreamNameUp$ finite windows, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readbacks, $\RealUp$ seal rows, $\RealOrderTopologyUp$ basis read, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert_{\DyadicIntervalBasisUp}$ row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the accepted packet $D$ and a displayed dyadic subcell refinement. The refinement changes the interval cell only through the $\DyadicIntervalUp$ coordinate $I$, and its endpoints remain sourced by the transported $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger $E$.

The rational containment row is transported next. Because $O$ is the only displayed $\RationalIntervalUp$ bound, the refined cell remains attached to the same containment route rather than to an unrecorded host interval.

The readback row $R$ and basis row $B$ then replay along the stored $\Cont$ routes. The $\StreamNameUp$ windows, $\RegSeqRatUp$ observations, $\RealUp$ seal rows, and $\RealOrderTopologyUp$ basis read are transported componentwise, so the consumer sees the refined cell through the same finite order.

The structural rows finish the classification. The provenance and local name are transported with the packet, and the carrier has no extra coordinate from which a quotient interval equality, host real equality, open-cover compactness theorem, Dedekind completeness principle, countable-choice witness, or selected completion point could be read.
\end{proof}
