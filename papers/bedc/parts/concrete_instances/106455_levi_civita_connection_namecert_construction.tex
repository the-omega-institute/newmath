\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\LeviCivitaConnectionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-levi-civita-connection-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Kobayashi and Nomizu, the $\LeviCivitaConnectionUp$ packet records the finite NameCert surface that ties a Riemannian metric to the unique torsion-free metric-compatible connection visible to curvature consumers. It is grounded in the source-fibre metric packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-riemannianmetric-namecert}, the connection packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-connection-namecert}, and the curvature boundary surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-curvature-namecert}. The packet exports only displayed rows and certificate ledgers; it does not import an ambient smooth connection operator, coordinate Christoffel symbols, or a host-level uniqueness theorem.

\begin{definition}[Levi-Civita connection carrier]
\label{def:levi-civita-connection-carrier}
A $\LeviCivitaConnectionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(G,C,T,M,R,H,K,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $G$ is the $\RiemannianMetricUp$ row whose source-fibre surface supplies the metric data on the displayed tangent fibres.
\item $C$ is the $\ConnectionUp$ row carrying the base, fibre, section, tangent-direction, derivative endpoint, and connection provenance visible to the packet.
\item $T$ is the torsion-free boundary row, recording that the two displayed tangent-direction orders have no residual antisymmetric endpoint outside the connection ledger.
\item $M$ is the metric-compatibility row, recording that the connection derivative preserves the displayed $\RiemannianMetricUp$ inner-product row along the inspected tangent window.
\item $R$ is the $\CurvatureUp$ compatibility row, exposing the curvature boundary obtained from the connection row after the torsion-free and metric-compatibility rows have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $G,C,T,M,R$ and their dependency rows.
\item $K$ records the displayed $\Cont$ replay from the metric row and connection row through torsion-free verification, metric compatibility, and curvature compatibility.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LeviCivitaConnectionUp$, $\RiemannianMetricUp$, $\ConnectionUp$, $\CurvatureUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LeviCivitaConnectionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier therefore packages a displayed Riemannian metric together with a displayed connection whose torsion-free, metric-compatible, and curvature-facing rows are all visible in the same finite certificate. It does not contain a coordinate formula for Christoffel symbols, a selected smooth atlas, a global geodesic theorem, or a host-level equality between possible connections.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Levi-Civita connection NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:levi-civita-connection-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\LeviCivitaConnectionUp$ carrier $L=(G,C,T,M,R,H,K,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\LeviCivitaConnectionUp}$ surface is exhausted by the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed metric and connection rows $G$ and $C$;
\item torsion-free exposure for the boundary row $T$;
\item metric-compatibility exposure for the row $M$;
\item curvature-facing compatibility for the row $R$;
\item componentwise transport for $G,C,T,M,R$ through $H$;
\item replay exactness for the route recorded by $K$;
\item provenance and local naming through $P$ and $N$.
\end{enumerate}
No obligation asks for an unlogged covariant derivative, a coordinate chart calculation, a selected geodesic flow, or a host-level uniqueness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:levi-civita-connection-carrier}. The rows $G$ and $C$ are the only metric and connection sources available to the local certificate, and their dependency surfaces are already the public rows of $\RiemannianMetricUp$ and $\ConnectionUp$.

The torsion-free and metric-compatibility clauses are the two displayed boundary rows $T$ and $M$. They are read after the connection row has exposed tangent-direction and derivative-endpoint data and after the metric row has exposed the fibrewise inner-product surface.

The curvature-facing clause is exactly $R$. It can be consumed only after the connection and compatibility rows have been replayed by $K$, so the downstream $\CurvatureUp$ read receives a finite boundary route rather than a detached tensor object.

Transport and replay exactness are then componentwise projections from $H$ and $K$. The package row $P$ and local name row $N$ repack these rows as the whole local NameCert surface, leaving no coordinate for the excluded ambient objects.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Levi-Civita connection curvature compatibility]
\label{thm:levi-civita-connection-curvature-compatibility}
Every $\CurvatureUp$ consumer of an accepted $\LeviCivitaConnectionUp$ carrier reads curvature through the finite route
$$
G \longmapsto C \longmapsto T \longmapsto M \longmapsto R .
$$
Thus the curvature row is downstream of the displayed $\RiemannianMetricUp$ metric packet, the displayed $\ConnectionUp$ derivative packet, the torsion-free boundary, and the metric-compatibility row. It cannot be read as an independent coordinate curvature tensor, a host connection form, or a curvature object detached from the metric-compatible connection certificate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a curvature-facing request. The carrier has only one curvature coordinate, namely $R$, and $R$ is declared as a compatibility row over the displayed connection row $C$.

The connection row $C$ is not detached from the metric. The torsion-free row $T$ and metric-compatibility row $M$ sit between $C$ and $R$ in the replay row $K$, while $G$ supplies the metric source that $M$ preserves.

Apply the connection curvature-boundary surface from \autoref{thm:connection-curvature-boundary-obligation} and the curvature source obligations of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-curvature-namecert}. These existing rows explain how a connection boundary becomes consumable by $\CurvatureUp$.

Repacking the projected rows gives the displayed route $G \mapsto C \mapsto T \mapsto M \mapsto R$. Since no coordinate curvature tensor, host connection form, or detached curvature object appears in the carrier, no such object is exported to the consumer.
\end{proof}
