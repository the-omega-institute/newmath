\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ConstructiveLipschitzFlowUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-constructive-lipschitz-flow-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Picard and Lindelof, local flow data are treated here as a finite
constructive package: a metric phase row, a rational time-window row, a local
Lipschitz budget, an Euler-style approximation schedule, and a replay ledger
that records how each requested window is inspected. The
$\ConstructiveLipschitzFlowUp$ packet names the local existence interface for
ODE consumers without importing host flows, selected maximal intervals,
ambient completeness, or global continuation. Its local-flow route consumes
the Lipschitz budget surface of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-lipschitz-modulus-namecert} together with the
map boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-lipschitzmap-namecert}, the
phase metric row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the
regular time-window readback of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the finite schedule
surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}.

\begin{definition}[Constructive Lipschitz flow carrier]
\label{def:constructive-lipschitz-flow-carrier}
A $\ConstructiveLipschitzFlowUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
F=(M,T,L,E,W,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the $\MetricSpaceUp$ phase row containing the point-history
carrier, metric readbacks, distance classifier, and local metric
$\NameCert$.
\item $T$ is the rational time-window row, read through $\RegSeqRatUp$
windows and not through a host interval object.
\item $L$ is the local Lipschitz budget row; it records the radius,
time-length, and contraction budget inspected by the packet.
\item $E$ is the finite approximation schedule, read as a $\StreamNameUp$
sequence of local Euler windows.
\item $W$ is the observation ledger joining metric-radius requests, time
windows, Lipschitz-budget checks, and approximation-window readbacks.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $M,T,L,E,W$ and the
local certificate rows.
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ replay from a requested local flow
window to its metric, time, Lipschitz, and approximation rows.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\MetricSpaceUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$,
$\StreamNameUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ConstructiveLipschitzFlowUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares two accepted carriers only by transporting these
displayed rows to a common metric, time-window, Lipschitz-budget, and
approximation-schedule surface. It has no coordinate for a selected host
solution curve, maximal interval, quotient trajectory, or ambient
completeness theorem.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Constructive Lipschitz flow NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:constructive-lipschitz-flow-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\ConstructiveLipschitzFlowUp$ carrier
$F=(M,T,L,E,W,H,C,P,N)$, the local
$\NameCert_{\ConstructiveLipschitzFlowUp}$ surface consists exactly of the
carrier admission row, classifier-transport row, local Lipschitz-budget row,
finite approximation-schedule row, observation-ledger exactness row,
continuation-replay row, package-provenance row, and local naming row. These
rows bind $\MetricSpaceUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\BHist$,
$\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ as the whole public obligation
surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of
\autoref{def:constructive-lipschitz-flow-carrier}. The projection displays
the phase metric row $M$, time-window row $T$, Lipschitz budget $L$,
approximation schedule $E$, observation ledger $W$, transport row $H$,
continuation row $C$, provenance row $P$, and local name $N$.

Classifier transport uses $H$ to keep all rows over one metric and one
time-window surface. The Lipschitz budget is therefore read with the same
metric-radius and rational time data as the approximation schedule.

The ledger exactness clause is exhausted by $W$. Every local-flow request
must pass through the displayed metric, time, budget, and schedule windows
before it reaches the $\Cont$ replay row.

Finally attach $P$ and $N$. These rows source and name the displayed packet;
they add no host solution curve, selected maximal interval, quotient
trajectory, or ambient completeness principle. Hence the listed rows exhaust
the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Constructive Lipschitz flow local-window boundary]
\label{thm:constructive-lipschitz-flow-local-window-boundary}
Every downstream ODE consumer of an accepted
$\ConstructiveLipschitzFlowUp$ carrier
$F=(M,T,L,E,W,H,C,P,N)$ must read local flow data through the displayed
metric row $M$, rational time-window row $T$, Lipschitz budget $L$,
approximation schedule $E$, observation ledger $W$, componentwise transport
$H$, continuation replay $C$, package provenance $P$, and local $\NameCert$
row $N$. The packet supplies no global flow, no selected maximal interval,
no quotient trajectory equality, no closed completeness of the phase space,
and no host-level uniqueness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the NameCert obligation surface of
\autoref{thm:constructive-lipschitz-flow-namecert-obligations}. It exposes
only the metric, time, budget, schedule, ledger, transport, replay,
provenance, and naming rows.

A local-window request first fixes its rational time window and its metric
radius through $T$ and $M$. The Lipschitz budget $L$ and approximation
schedule $E$ can then be inspected only through the ledger $W$.

The continuation row $C$ replays this finite inspection route, while $H$
keeps the rows componentwise stable and $P,N$ record provenance and naming.
Since no carrier coordinate stores a host flow or maximal interval, the
downstream consumer receives only the displayed local-window data.
\end{proof}
