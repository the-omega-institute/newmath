\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\PrefixObserverUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-prefixobserver-namecert}
\origin{ai}

The $\PrefixObserverUp$ surface realizes the finite-observation side of
\autoref{ch:visions-inscription-event-as-concrete-instance}. It packages an
inscription-facing prefix read as a finite $\BHist$ packet with a displayed
prefix row, an accepted observation route, a check row, a consumer ledger,
and local $\NameCert$ provenance. It is adjacent to the accepted inscription
packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-inscriptionevent-namecert}, but it
stops one step earlier: it certifies the prefix read that a downstream
$\InscriptionEventUp$ handoff may consume.

\begin{definition}[PrefixObserver carrier]
\label{def:prefix-observer-carrier}
A $\PrefixObserverUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
P=(S,W,R,K,U,L,H,C,Q,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the source observation-history row whose finite prefix is being read;
\item $W$ is the displayed prefix-window row selected from $S$;
\item $R$ is the accepted observation-route row from $S$ to $W$;
\item $K$ is the accepted check row certifying that the route really reads the displayed prefix;
\item $U$ is the consumer row naming the inscription-facing reader of the prefix;
\item $L$ is the consumer-ledger row recording what the reader may carry forward from the prefix;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the source, prefix, route, check, consumer, ledger, package, and local naming rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which consumers read $S,W,R,K,U,L$ without leaving the finite packet;
\item $Q$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance row over $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, accepted-prefix checks, consumer-ledger data, and local certificate dependencies;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\PrefixObserverUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier for two accepted prefix observers compares only the displayed
source row, prefix row, accepted route, check row, consumer row, ledger row,
and their $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ provenance.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[PrefixObserver NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:prefix-observer-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\PrefixObserverUp}$ surface for the carrier of
\autoref{def:prefix-observer-carrier} consists of five finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier exposure: every accepted prefix observer exposes exactly the packet $P=(S,W,R,K,U,L,H,C,Q,N)$;
\item prefix locality: public comparison is restricted to the source, displayed prefix, accepted route, check, consumer, ledger, transport, continuation, package, and local naming rows;
\item check acceptance: the route row $R$ reaches the displayed prefix $W$ from the source row $S$ only through the accepted check row $K$;
\item consumer accountability: every inscription-facing use first reads the consumer row $U$, the ledger row $L$, and the displayed $\Cont$ routes in $C$;
\item provenance exposure: $Q$ and $N$ bind the prefix read to finite $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ data rather than to a hidden observation oracle.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.PrefixObserverUp.PrefixObserverNameCert\_obligations}
\begin{proof}
Carrier exposure is projection from \autoref{def:prefix-observer-carrier}. The
source, prefix window, accepted route, check, consumer, ledger, transport,
continuation, package, and local naming rows are all displayed coordinates.

Prefix locality follows from the classifier clause in the carrier definition.
The comparison has no coordinate beyond the listed public rows and their
recorded $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ routes, so it cannot inspect an undisplayed
observation stream.

Check acceptance and consumer accountability are read from $R,K,U,L$, and
$C$. The route reaches the displayed prefix only after the checker row accepts
it, and every downstream consumer receives the ledgered prefix through the
displayed continuation surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PrefixObserver finite-route non-escape]
\label{thm:prefix-observer-finite-route-nonescape}
For an accepted $\PrefixObserverUp$ packet
$$
P=(S,W,R,K,U,L,H,C,Q,N),
$$
every public route to the observed prefix factors through $S,W,R,K,U,L,H,C,Q,N$.
The route may expose the selected prefix and the consumer ledger, but it
cannot export an infinite stream, an undisplayed observation row, an unchecked
route, a private observer state, or a host-level inspection oracle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the carrier inventory. The only rows available to a public read
are the source history, displayed prefix, route, check, consumer, ledger,
transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.

Apply the check-acceptance obligation from
\autoref{thm:prefix-observer-namecert-obligations}. It forces any public read
of $W$ through $R$ and $K$, so a consumer cannot bypass the finite accepted
route or replace it with an unchecked stream read.

Finally use consumer accountability and provenance exposure. The ledger $L$,
routes $C$, package row $Q$, and local name row $N$ carry only the displayed
prefix packet, which leaves no coordinate for an infinite stream, private
observer state, or host-level inspection oracle.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PrefixObserver inscription handoff]
\label{thm:prefix-observer-inscription-handoff}
Every accepted inscription-facing prefix read hands data to
$\InscriptionEventUp$ consumers only through the displayed prefix and check
rows before the inscription-event handoff. In particular, a consumer may pass
$S,W,R,K,U,L,H,C,Q,N$ toward the inscription surface of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-inscriptionevent-namecert}, but it receives no
unselected history suffix, unchecked route, hidden observation source, private
agency predicate, or raw host-event trace.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the packet $P$ from \autoref{def:prefix-observer-carrier}. The
displayed prefix row $W$ and accepted check row $K$ are explicit coordinates,
and $U$ names the inscription-facing consumer.

Use finite-route non-escape from
\autoref{thm:prefix-observer-finite-route-nonescape}. The consumer reaches the
prefix only through the accepted route and the displayed check, then reads the
consumer ledger through $C,Q,N$.

The handoff to \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-inscriptionevent-namecert} is
therefore a finite packet handoff rather than a stream handoff. The inscription
event may consume the prefix, route, check, and ledger rows, but no coordinate
supplies an unselected suffix, hidden observer source, private agency
predicate, or raw host trace.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PrefixObserver StreamName schedule link]
\label{thm:prefix-observer-streamname-schedule-link}
Every accepted $\PrefixObserverUp$ prefix window $W$ may be read as a finite observation schedule row compatible with the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window discipline before it is handed to $\InscriptionEventUp$. For
$$
P=(S,W,R,K,U,L,H,C,Q,N),
$$
the rows $S,W,R,K,U,L,H,C,Q,N$ expose the source, displayed prefix window, accepted route, check, consumer, ledger, transport, continuation, package, and local name needed for that finite schedule. The link exports no infinite stream, hidden suffix, unchecked route, private observer state, or host-level inspection oracle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:prefix-observer-carrier}. The displayed prefix window $W$ is a finite coordinate of the accepted packet, and the rows $R,K$ record the accepted route and check by which it is read from the source row $S$.

Use finite-route non-escape from \autoref{thm:prefix-observer-finite-route-nonescape}. It confines every public prefix read to $S,W,R,K,U,L,H,C,Q,N$, so the schedule row cannot inspect an unbounded observation stream or an undisplayed suffix.

Compare the finite schedule with the sibling discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}. The $\StreamNameUp$ chapter supplies the finite-window reading convention, and the $\PrefixObserverUp$ packet supplies the concrete prefix window and inscription-facing ledger that instantiate it.

Finally apply the inscription handoff. The downstream $\InscriptionEventUp$ consumer receives only the displayed prefix, route, check, ledger, and certificate rows, so no unchecked route, private observer state, or host-level inspection oracle is exported.
\end{proof}
