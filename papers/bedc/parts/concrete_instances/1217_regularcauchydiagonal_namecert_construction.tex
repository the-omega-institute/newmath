\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RegularCauchyDiagonalUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-regularcauchydiagonal-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\RegularCauchyDiagonalUp$ packet names the stationary and finite-window diagonal data used by the real-completeness construction before it is repacked into the broader $\RealDiagonalCompletionUp$ surface. Its carrier is deliberately smaller than a completion theorem: it records the inspected regular Cauchy rows, the diagonal selector, the stationary rational rows, and the local naming-certificate provenance through $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$.

\begin{definition}[Regular Cauchy diagonal source]
\label{def:regular-cauchy-diagonal-source}
A $\RegularCauchyDiagonalUp$ source row is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
D=(Q,S,R,L,W,P,N)
$$
whose displayed coordinates are as follows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $Q$ is the visible $\RatUp$ seed row used for stationary rational inputs;
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ row naming the stream windows read by the diagonal selector;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ row naming the regular rational sequence selected at the requested diagonal precision;
\item $L$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row receiving the selected diagonal representative;
\item $W$ is the finite list of requested diagonal windows and their $\Cont$ routes through the inspected regular Cauchy family;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ row naming the $\RatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, and local certificate dependencies;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyDiagonalUp}$ provenance row.
\end{enumerate}
The source row contains no quotient of regular streams, no selected host limit, and no ambient completeness field.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Regular Cauchy diagonal carrier]
\label{def:regular-cauchy-diagonal-carrier}
The carrier of $\RegularCauchyDiagonalUp$ consists of the source rows of \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-diagonal-source} whose diagonal windows are all read from displayed $\RegSeqRatUp$ rows and whose stationary rational rows factor through the same $\RatUp$ seed, the same $\StreamNameUp$ constant-window witness, and the same $\RealUp$ seal row. The classifier relates two carrier rows exactly when their visible rational seeds, stream windows, regular-sequence reads, real-seal rows, and package provenance are transported componentwise by the displayed $\hsame$ comparisons and $\Cont$ routes.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy diagonal window coverage]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-diagonal-window-coverage}
Every consumer read of an accepted $\RegularCauchyDiagonalUp$ carrier factors through one displayed diagonal window, one $\StreamNameUp$ window row, one $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row, the associated $\RatUp$ seed evidence for stationary rational rows, and the visible $\Pkg$ and $\NameCert$ provenance.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier through \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-diagonal-carrier}. The only consumer-facing coordinates are the diagonal-window list, the stream-window row, the regular rational readback, the real-seal row, and the local provenance rows.

For a requested precision, the diagonal selector chooses a displayed member of the regular Cauchy family and a displayed window inside that member. The $\Cont$ route in the source packet records that this window is one of the finite rows already named by the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule.

The selected value is then read through the $\RegSeqRatUp$ row and, in the stationary case, through the same $\RatUp$ seed row. Repacking these visible rows with $P$ and $N$ gives the claimed factorization, and there is no additional carrier coordinate from which a host limit or quotient equality could be read.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy diagonal stationary compatibility]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-diagonal-stationary-compatibility}
If a $\RegularCauchyDiagonalUp$ carrier row is generated by a stationary rational seed $Q:\RatUp$, then every diagonal window reads the same rational seed through the constant $\StreamNameUp$ row, and the resulting $\RealUp$ seal is classified with the constant rational embedding of $Q$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
In the stationary packet, every inspected member of the regular Cauchy family is the same regular rational row. The diagonal selector may vary the requested window, but the stream row is constant and each selected observation is transported by the same displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ witnesses.

Thus the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row carries the seed $Q$ at every diagonal window. The $\RealUp$ seal coordinate is attached to exactly those readbacks, so the constant rational embedding receives the same rational seed and the same local provenance.

The classifier of \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-diagonal-carrier} compares only the displayed seed, window, readback, seal, and package rows. It therefore identifies the stationary diagonal carrier with the constant rational $\RealUp$ embedding of $Q$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy diagonal NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-diagonal-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyDiagonalUp}$ obligation surface consists of carrier habitation, componentwise classifier transport through $\hsame$ and $\Cont$, diagonal-window coverage, stationary rational compatibility, and ledger non-escape over $\RatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier habitation is the packaging operation of \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-diagonal-carrier}: the source row supplies the rational seed, stream windows, regular-sequence readbacks, real seal, finite diagonal-window routes, and provenance rows.

Classifier transport is componentwise. Each accepted replacement is an $\hsame$ comparison of one displayed row or a $\Cont$ route identifying where that row is read inside the finite diagonal packet, so the transported packet remains within the same carrier definition.

Window coverage is exactly \autoref{thm:regular-cauchy-diagonal-window-coverage}, and stationary rational compatibility is exactly \autoref{thm:regular-cauchy-diagonal-stationary-compatibility}. The ledger non-escape claim follows by inspecting the listed source coordinates: none is a quotient carrier, host-limit selector, ambient completeness theorem, or hidden real endpoint.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy diagonal zero seed]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-diagonal-zero-seed}

Its $\RealUp$ seal row $L$, finite window ledger $W$, package row $P$, and local $\NameCert$ row $N$ are all displayed finite rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the stationary rational seed. The zero $\RatUp$ row supplies the same displayed rational observation at every requested diagonal precision, and the constant $\StreamNameUp$ row supplies the same finite window witness for each read.

The $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback is therefore the constant regular rational row. By \autoref{thm:regular-cauchy-diagonal-stationary-compatibility}, the resulting $\RealUp$ seal is classified with the constant rational embedding of the same seed.

Finally package the constant window routes with $P$ and $N$. The carrier definition in \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-diagonal-carrier} requires no quotient stream, selected host limit, or ambient completeness field, so these displayed rows inhabit the diagonal carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy diagonal selector stability]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-diagonal-selector-stability}
Let $D=(Q,S,R,L,W,P,N)$ be an accepted $\RegularCauchyDiagonalUp$ source row. Transporting $Q$, $S$, $R$, $L$, $W$, $P$, and $N$ componentwise by the displayed $\hsame$ comparisons and $\Cont$ routes preserves the diagonal selector classifier through the $\RatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, and $\RegSeqRatUp$ rows.

\begin{proof}

The selector uses the window list $W$ to choose a displayed window inside the inspected regular Cauchy family. Componentwise $\hsame$ comparisons and $\Cont$ routes move exactly those displayed windows and readback rows, so the selected diagonal row remains inside the same finite packet.

Apply \autoref{thm:regular-cauchy-diagonal-window-coverage} for the consumer-facing route and \autoref{thm:regular-cauchy-diagonal-namecert-obligations} for the local certificate surface.
