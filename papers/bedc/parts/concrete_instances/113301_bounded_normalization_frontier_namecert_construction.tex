\begin{definition}[Bounded normalization frontier carrier]
\label{def:bounded-normalization-frontier-carrier}
A $\BoundedNormalizationFrontierUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
F=(T,U,B,X,Y,E,O,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $T$ is the typed term row, carrying the closed MetaCIC terms and their displayed typing context boundary.
\item $U$ is the shared finite fuel row used by the bounded normalizer.
\item $B$ is the \texttt{normalizeBounded} trace row for both terms at fuel $U$.
\item $X$ is the finished-normal witness row, present only when the bounded traces are certified as closed finished normal forms.
\item $Y$ is the bounded \texttt{Term.eq} comparison row over the two bounded normalizer outputs.
\item $E$ is the bounded conversion result row exposed by \texttt{decideClosedBetaConv} at the displayed fuel.
\item $O$ is the refusal and obstruction row. It records the missing endpoint data whenever the packet cannot promote a bounded comparison to unrestricted beta-conversion.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for typed terms, fuel, bounded traces, finished-normal witnesses, bounded equality, refusal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a conversion request through $T,U,B,X,Y,E,O,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BoundedNormalizationFrontierUp$, MetaCIC typed terms, \texttt{normalizeBounded}, \texttt{Term.eq}, \texttt{decideClosedBetaConv}, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\BoundedNormalizationFrontierUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $T,U,B,X,Y,E,O,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for global normalization, unrestricted beta-conversion decidability, fuel-erased confluence, selected normal forms outside the trace, or a host equality oracle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Bounded normalization frontier NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:bounded-normalization-frontier-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\BoundedNormalizationFrontierUp$ carrier
$$
F=(T,U,B,X,Y,E,O,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\BoundedNormalizationFrontierUp}$ surface consists exactly of typed-term admission, finite-fuel admission, bounded-normalizer trace replay, finished-normal witness exposure, bounded \texttt{Term.eq} comparison, bounded conversion transport, refusal-row nonescape, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The typed terms are read through $T$, the computation budget through $U$, and the bounded normalizer traces through $B$.

Finished-normal evidence is separate. The row $X$ records the endpoint witnesses that make the bounded traces closed normal forms; if those witnesses are absent, the refusal row $O$ is the only exposed endpoint information.

The bounded comparison uses $Y$ and $E$. It compares the two displayed bounded normalizer outputs by \texttt{Term.eq} and exposes only the finite result at fuel $U$.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and locally name the same route. Since the carrier contains no row for global normalization, unrestricted beta-conversion decidability, fuel-erased confluence, selected external normal forms, or host equality, the listed obligations exhaust the NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Bounded conversion frontier handoff]
\label{thm:bounded-normalization-frontier-bounded-conversion-handoff}
Every conversion consumer of an accepted $\BoundedNormalizationFrontierUp$ packet receives a bounded frontier certificate, not an unrestricted beta-conversion decider. For
$$
F=(T,U,B,X,Y,E,O,H,C,P,N),
$$
the consumer must read typed terms $T$, finite fuel $U$, bounded traces $B$, finished-normal witnesses $X$ when present, bounded \texttt{Term.eq} comparison $Y$, bounded conversion result $E$, and the refusal row $O$ before any conversion-facing claim is exposed through $H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the carrier inventory in \autoref{def:bounded-normalization-frontier-carrier}. It stores a concrete fuel value and concrete bounded traces, so every conversion request is already a finite-fuel request.

Apply \autoref{thm:bounded-normalization-frontier-namecert-obligations}. The comparison route is $T,U,B,X,Y,E,O$ in that order: typed terms and fuel determine the traces, finished-normal witnesses mark the closed endpoint case, the bounded equality row compares the displayed outputs, and the refusal row records the missing endpoint data when no global conclusion is available.

The rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve that finite route. Because unrestricted normalization and fuel-erased beta-conversion are absent from the carrier, a consumer cannot bypass the bounded frontier by appealing to a host conversion theorem or selected normal form.
\end{proof}
