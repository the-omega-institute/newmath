\begin{definition}[Real approximation scheme carrier]
\label{def:real-approximation-scheme-carrier}
A $\RealApproximationSchemeUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
A=(S,D,R,Q,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ observation row. It records the finite schedule of requested observations, the visible stream-name windows, and the order in which a consumer may read them.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row. It records the dyadic radii, comparison cells, shrink steps, and budget rows attached to each displayed observation request.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row. It records the regular rational sequence data that consumes $S$ and $D$ before any real-facing seal is exposed.
\item $Q$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row. It records only the finite real-boundary interface reached through the regular readback, not a host real value.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the stream observations, dyadic tolerance rows, regular readback, real seal row, continuation route, package provenance, and local naming row.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the route
$$
S,D \longmapsto R \longmapsto Q \longmapsto H,P,N .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RealApproximationSchemeUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RealApproximationSchemeUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $S,D,R,Q,H,C,P,N$. The carrier has no coordinate for host real equality, quotient streams, selected limits, trichotomy, ambient completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Real approximation scheme NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:real-approximation-scheme-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RealApproximationSchemeUp$ carrier
$$
A=(S,D,R,Q,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\RealApproximationSchemeUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the stream observations, dyadic tolerance ledger, regular readback, real seal boundary, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item stream observability, requiring every approximation request to enter through the finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows in $S$;
\item dyadic tolerance exactness, requiring every precision demand to be witnessed by a displayed $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ radius, shrink, comparison, or budget row in $D$;
\item regular readback routing, requiring the $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $R$ to consume the stream and dyadic rows before a real-facing consumer may read the packet;
\item real seal nonescape, requiring the $\RealUp$ seal row $Q$ to be reached only through $R$ and never through a host real representative;
\item provenance exhaustion, requiring $H,C,P,N$ to transport, replay, source, and name exactly the rows listed above and no host real equality, quotient stream, selected limit, trichotomy, ambient completeness, Lean verification, or bridge credential.
\end{enumerate}
No real-approximation-scheme obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier $A=(S,D,R,Q,H,C,P,N)$. The definition lists the whole visible packet, so carrier admission is just the displayed finite $\BHist$ data together with its transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows.

The observation side is forced by $S$. Every approximation request first reads the $\StreamNameUp$ windows and schedule before any tolerance or readback row can be consumed.

The precision side is forced by $D$. The dyadic radii, shrink steps, comparison cells, and budget rows are the only tolerance coordinates available to the packet, so a consumer cannot replace them by an ambient metric or a host real estimate.

The real-facing route passes through $R$ and then $Q$. The $\RegSeqRatUp$ row reads the stream and dyadic data as a regular rational sequence, and the $\RealUp$ seal row exposes only the finite boundary supplied by that readback.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same rows componentwise. Since the refused principles have no coordinate and no replay edge, the listed obligations exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real approximation scheme RegSeqRat handoff]
\label{thm:real-approximation-scheme-regseqrat-handoff}
Every $\RegSeqRatUp$ or $\RealUp$ consumer read of an accepted $\RealApproximationSchemeUp$ carrier factors through the finite route
$$
S,D \longmapsto R \longmapsto Q \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
Thus the consumer receives the $\StreamNameUp$ observation windows, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, the $\RealUp$ seal boundary, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ row. It receives no host real equality, quotient stream, selected limit, trichotomy, ambient completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Start with a consumer read of the accepted packet. The stream observation row $S$ supplies the finite request schedule and visible windows, so the read begins with a named $\StreamNameUp$ surface rather than an unbounded stream.

Next read the dyadic tolerance row $D$. The row provides the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ radii, shrink steps, comparison cells, and budget entries attached to the same observations, so the read has a finite precision ledger before it reaches a regular sequence.

The handoff is then the row $R$. It packages the observed stream and tolerance data as the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback consumed by later real-facing certificates.

The real boundary is last. The row $Q$ records only the $\RealUp$ seal reached through $R$, and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve exactly this route by componentwise transport, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming.
\end{proof}
