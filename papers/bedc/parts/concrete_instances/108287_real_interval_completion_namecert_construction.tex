\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RealIntervalCompletionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-real-interval-completion-namecert}

Following Bishop and Bridges constructive analysis, the $\RealIntervalCompletionUp$ packet records the finite completion-facing boundary at which located interval data, nested refinements, regular rational readbacks, and Real seals are read as one interval-completion carrier. It sits beside the located endpoint surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedinterval-namecert}, the nested interval carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-nestedinterval-namecert}, and the public completion reflector route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchycompletionreflector-namecert}. The packet names the L10 Real and Completion handoff without importing Dedekind cuts, quotient completions, selected points, open-cover compactness, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[RealIntervalCompletion carrier]
\label{def:real-interval-completion-carrier}
A $\RealIntervalCompletionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
I=(L,N,D,W,R,E,H,C,P,Q)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $L$ is the $\LocatedIntervalUp$ endpoint row, carrying lower and upper located endpoint windows and their visible interval-order classifier.
\item $N$ is the $\NestedIntervalUp$ refinement row, recording the displayed nesting discipline and the dependency of each later interval on the earlier endpoint rows.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ radius row, carrying the dyadic shrinkage scale used by the refinement windows.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window row that exposes only finite interval windows before any regular rational readback is consumed.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row, reading the displayed interval windows as regular rational approximants.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row attached to the completed interval-facing read.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $L,N,D,W,R,E$ and the structural rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from located endpoints through nested refinements, dyadic shrinkage, stream windows, regular rational readback, and the Real seal.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RealIntervalCompletionUp$, $\LocatedIntervalUp$, $\NestedIntervalUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $Q$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RealIntervalCompletionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for Dedekind cuts, quotient interval completions, selected interval points, open-cover compactness, countable choice, host real equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[RealIntervalCompletion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:real-interval-completion-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RealIntervalCompletionUp$ carrier $I=(L,N,D,W,R,E,H,C,P,Q)$, the local $\NameCert_{\RealIntervalCompletionUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed located endpoint, nested refinement, dyadic radius, StreamName window, RegSeqRat readback, Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item interval-refinement classification, requiring every refinement read to pass through the $\LocatedIntervalUp$ endpoint row and the $\NestedIntervalUp$ nesting row before any completion-facing seal is visible;
\item diameter-shrinkage stability, requiring the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ radii to decrease along the displayed nested interval route and to remain stable under the classifier;
\item Real seal compatibility, requiring the $\StreamNameUp$ windows and $\RegSeqRatUp$ readbacks to reach the $\RealUp$ seal only through the same finite interval route;
\item refusal of quotient intervals, selected limit points, countable-choice schedules, open-cover compactness, and ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is the projection of \autoref{def:real-interval-completion-carrier}. The displayed tuple lists the located endpoints, nested refinements, dyadic radii, finite windows, regular rational readbacks, Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row.

The interval-refinement classifier is the route $L,N$. A consumer must read located endpoint data before it can inspect the nested refinement row, so the packet cannot replace the route by a host interval completion or by selected endpoint representatives.

Diameter-shrinkage stability is carried by $D$ together with the nesting row $N$. The dyadic radii are read as finite $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ data, and componentwise $\hsame$ transport in $H$ preserves the same shrinkage classifier.

Real seal compatibility is the route $W,R,E$. The stream windows feed the regular rational readback before the Real seal is exposed, and $\Cont$ replay in $C$ preserves this order for downstream completion consumers.

Finally, $P$ and $Q$ name exactly the listed rows. Since the carrier contains no Dedekind-cut, quotient-interval, selected-point, choice, open-cover compactness, or ambient-completeness coordinate, those objects cannot be exported from an accepted packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RealIntervalCompletion completion handoff]
\label{thm:real-interval-completion-handoff}
Every $\CauchyCompletionReflectorUp$ or $\RealUp$ consumer of an accepted $\RealIntervalCompletionUp$ carrier receives the finite route
$$
L,N,D,W,R,E,H,C,P,Q
$$
and no independent completion object. The completion-facing read factors through located interval endpoints, nested interval refinement, dyadic diameter shrinkage, finite stream windows, regular rational readback, and the Real seal.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted consumer read. Carrier admission exposes only the rows $L,N,D,W,R,E,H,C,P,Q$.

The interval part is $L,N,D$. It fixes located endpoints, the displayed refinement order, and the dyadic shrinkage scale before any Real-facing data are available.

The completion-facing part is $W,R,E$. It reads finite windows, regular rational approximants, and the Real seal along the same route used by the public $\CauchyCompletionReflectorUp$ surface.

Transport, replay, provenance, and local naming are the structural rows $H,C,P,Q$. They preserve the route but add no quotient completion, selected point, countable-choice schedule, open-cover compactness, or ambient Real completeness principle.
\end{proof}
