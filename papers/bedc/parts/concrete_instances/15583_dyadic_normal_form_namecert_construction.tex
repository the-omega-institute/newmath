\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\DyadicNormalFormUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-dyadic-normal-form-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop--Bridges constructive analysis, the $\DyadicNormalFormUp$ packet names the finite canonical presentation of a dyadic rational before the open Real and Completion route passes through regular rational streams. It sits downstream of the signed rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-int-namecert}, the rational rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rat-namecert}, and the dyadic core boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}; it supplies the displayed normal-form surface read by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the $\RealUp$ seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet records canonical finite dyadic representation data, not quotient rational equality, completed real equality, order, completeness, or a standard bridge.

\begin{definition}[Dyadic normal-form carrier]
\label{def:dyadic-normal-form-carrier}
A $\DyadicNormalFormUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
D=(S,M,E,Z,R,L,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the sign row, read through the signed $\IntUp$ surface and recording positive, negative, or zero orientation for the displayed dyadic.
\item $M$ is the signed mantissa row, an $\IntUp$-read finite integer whose absolute part is visible before scale inspection.
\item $E$ is the unary exponent row, carrying the nonnegative dyadic scale attached to the denominator $2^E$.
\item $Z$ is the zero classifier row, recording that the zero packet is represented by the distinguished row $M=0$ with terminal scale $E=0$.
\item $R$ is the scale-minimality row, recording that a nonzero mantissa is odd at the displayed scale and hence cannot be divided by $2$ while lowering $E$.
\item $L$ is the dyadic readback ledger, exposing the finite rational row $M/2^E$ through $\RatUp$ and the accepted $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ boundary.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the sign, mantissa, exponent, zero classifier, scale-minimality row, readback ledger, continuation row, provenance row, and local naming row.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a dyadic input through sign extraction, mantissa readback, unary scale inspection, zero handling, scale-minimality verification, and dyadic readback.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\IntUp$, $\RatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DyadicNormalFormUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through the displayed rows $S,M,E,Z,R,L,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for quotient rational equality, host real equality, order, completion, a choice-selected representative, or a standard bridge.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Dyadic normal-form NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:dyadic-normal-form-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\DyadicNormalFormUp$ carrier $D=(S,M,E,Z,R,L,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\DyadicNormalFormUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the sign, signed mantissa, unary exponent, zero classifier, scale-minimality row, dyadic readback ledger, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming row;
\item source compatibility, requiring $M$ and $E$ to be read from an accepted $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ packet through the displayed $\IntUp$ and $\RatUp$ surfaces;
\item zero exactness, requiring $Z$ to identify the unique zero normal row $M=0,E=0$ before any downstream readback is exposed;
\item scale-minimality, requiring every nonzero accepted row to expose an odd mantissa at the displayed scale, so that no lower unary exponent represents the same dyadic row inside the packet;
\item dyadic readback stability, requiring $L,H,C$ to preserve the rational readback $M/2^E$ under componentwise $\hsame$ transport and displayed $\Cont$ replay;
\item downstream handoff, requiring $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ consumers to inspect only the finite normal-form row and its dyadic readback ledger;
\item ledger refusal, excluding quotient rational equality, host real equality, order, completion, choice-selected representatives, and standard-bridge data.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored outside those rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:dyadic-normal-form-carrier}. The projection exposes exactly the sign row, mantissa row, unary exponent row, zero classifier, scale-minimality row, dyadic readback ledger, and structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

Source compatibility follows from the attachment to \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}. The mantissa is read through $\IntUp$, the rational readback is read through $\RatUp$, and both rows are consumed only as finite dyadic-core data.

Zero exactness and scale-minimality are the two classifier rows. The zero case is forced by $Z$, while the nonzero case is forced by $R$, which records that no displayed factor of $2$ remains in the mantissa at the chosen scale.

Replay $C$ and then transport by $H$. The route preserves the same sign, mantissa, exponent, zero classifier, minimality row, and readback ledger before any $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, or $\RealUp$ consumer can inspect the packet.

The exclusions are immediate from the carrier inventory. Since quotient rational equality, host real equality, order, completion, choice-selected representatives, and bridge rows are absent, the local NameCert surface cannot export them.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Dyadic normal-form canonical readback]
\label{thm:dyadic-normal-form-canonical-readback}
Let $D=(S,M,E,Z,R,L,H,C,P,N)$ and $D'=(S',M',E',Z',R',L',H',C',P',N')$ be accepted $\DyadicNormalFormUp$ carriers whose dyadic readback ledgers expose the same $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ row through the displayed $\RatUp$ surface. If both carriers satisfy the same zero classifier and scale-minimality rows, then their sign, mantissa, unary exponent, and dyadic readback rows agree under componentwise $\hsame$ transport. Consequently every $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ consumer reads a single canonical finite normal form rather than a family of scale-shifted dyadic representatives.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Compare the two accepted packets through the displayed readback ledgers $L$ and $L'$. The hypothesis says that the comparison is made inside the same $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ boundary and through the same $\RatUp$ readback surface.

If the common readback is zero, the classifier rows $Z$ and $Z'$ force both packets to use the distinguished zero row. Hence $M=M'=0$ and $E=E'=0$ after componentwise transport, and the sign rows agree with the zero orientation.

In the nonzero case, the scale-minimality rows $R$ and $R'$ force both mantissas to be odd at their displayed scales. A dyadic equality between two odd mantissas at different unary exponents would exhibit a remaining factor of $2$ on one side after transporting to a common scale, contradicting the corresponding minimality row.

Thus the unary exponent rows agree, and then the displayed rational readback forces the signed mantissa rows and sign rows to agree. Transport by $H$ and $H'$ carries this agreement to the local naming rows, while $C$ and $C'$ replay the same finite route for downstream consumers.

The $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ handoff reads only the rows just compared. Therefore downstream consumers see the canonical finite normal form and not a separate scale-shifted representative.
\end{proof}
