\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\QuasiMetricUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-quasimetric-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Kelly and Lowen, the $\QuasiMetricUp$ packet records the finite asymmetric-distance surface that lies between the symmetric distance certificate of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the zero-distance boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-pseudometric-namecert}, and the entourage discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformspace-namecert}. Its Real-valued distance rows read the terminal seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert} through the dyadic tolerance source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, so Real and Completion consumers see an oriented distance ledger before any uniform-entourage handoff is exposed. The chapter names only that asymmetric NameCert surface; it does not assert symmetry, separatedness, metric completeness, open-cover compactness, quotient distance, or a standard bridge.

\begin{definition}[QuasiMetric carrier surface]
\label{def:quasimetric-carrier-surface}
A $\QuasiMetricUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(S,X,D,Z,T,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the source row. It names the traditional quasi-metric source and the finite observation context in which oriented distance requests are admitted.
\item $X$ is the point carrier row. It records the admitted point names and their classifier, but it does not identify points by zero distance.
\item $D$ is the asymmetric $\RealUp$-valued distance row. It records oriented requests $d(x,y)$ and keeps the reverse request $d(y,x)$ as a separate row when that reverse request is present.
\item $Z$ is the zero diagonal row. It records $d(x,x)=0$ as a displayed boundary condition on oriented distance requests.
\item $T$ is the triangle row. It records the oriented comparison from $d(x,z)$ through the pair of rows $d(x,y)$ and $d(y,z)$.
\item $E$ is the symmetrized comparison and entourage row. It records the finite handoff from the oriented distance ledger to entourage requests such as the common-boundary comparison of $d(x,y)$ and $d(y,x)$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for source, point-carrier, asymmetric distance, zero diagonal, triangle, entourage, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a consumer request through point admission, oriented distance readback, zero diagonal or triangle evidence, symmetrized entourage comparison, provenance, and local naming.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\QuasiMetricUp$, $\MetricUp$, $\PseudometricUp$, $\UniformSpaceUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\QuasiMetricUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $S,X,D,Z,T,E,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for symmetry, separated point identity, metric completeness, open-cover compactness, quotient distance, selected limits, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[QuasiMetric NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:quasimetric-namecert-obligation-surface}
For every accepted $\QuasiMetricUp$ carrier $Q=(S,X,D,Z,T,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\QuasiMetricUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the source, point carrier, oriented $\RealUp$-valued distance row, zero diagonal row, triangle row, symmetrized comparison and entourage row, componentwise transport, displayed replay, provenance, and local name;
\item asymmetric distance readback, requiring every exported distance request to keep the ordered pair of point rows visible before the $\RealUp$ distance seal is read;
\item zero diagonal stability, requiring self-distance to be stored in $Z$ rather than imported from a host metric law;
\item oriented triangle stability, requiring each triangle comparison to be displayed in $T$ with its ordered endpoints;
\item classifier transport, requiring accepted packets to move only by the componentwise $\hsame$ row $H$ and the replay row $C$;
\item entourage handoff, requiring every uniform-facing request to pass through $E$ after the oriented distance rows have been displayed;
\item ledger non-escape, excluding symmetry, separatedness, metric completeness, open-cover compactness, quotient distance, selected limits, and any standard bridge.
\end{enumerate}
No quasi-metric NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:quasimetric-carrier-surface}. The tuple lists exactly the source, point carrier, oriented distance, zero diagonal, triangle, entourage, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

Asymmetric distance readback is forced by $D$. A request for $d(x,y)$ keeps $x$ and $y$ in that order, and a request for $d(y,x)$ is a distinct displayed row unless the carrier separately records a comparison between them.

The zero diagonal and triangle obligations are the displayed rows $Z$ and $T$. Thus reflexive zero and triangle comparison are local quasi-metric data, not imported symmetry or host metric structure.

Classifier transport is componentwise through $H$, while $C$ replays the same ordered route for consumers. The entourage handoff uses $E$ only after the oriented distance ledger has been read.

The remaining exclusions follow from the carrier inventory. Since no coordinate names symmetry, separated identity, metric completeness, open-cover compactness, quotient distance, selected limits, or a standard bridge, none of those data can be exported by the local certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[QuasiMetric uniform entourage handoff]
\label{thm:quasimetric-uniform-entourage-handoff}
Let $Q=(S,X,D,Z,T,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\QuasiMetricUp$ packet. Every $\UniformSpaceUp$ consumer of $Q$ reaches entourage data only through the finite route
$$
S,X,D,Z,T \longmapsto E \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
The handoff may read the symmetrized comparison row $E$ and the oriented $\RealUp$ distance ledger $D$, but it receives no symmetry theorem, no separated point identity, no metric completeness, no open-cover compactness, no quotient distance, and no standard bridge.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the accepted carrier. The consumer first reads the source and point rows $S,X$, then the oriented distance ledger $D$.

The diagonal and triangle rows $Z,T$ are read before any uniform-facing handoff. They supply the quasi-metric boundary conditions needed to form entourage requests without turning the oriented distance into a symmetric metric.

Only after those rows are displayed does the consumer reach $E$. This row records the symmetrized comparison or entourage request, while the support rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, package, and name the same finite route.

The excluded principles are absent from the carrier. Therefore the uniform-facing handoff exports finite oriented-distance and entourage data, not symmetry, separatedness, metric completeness, open-cover compactness, quotient distance, or bridge evidence.
\end{proof}
