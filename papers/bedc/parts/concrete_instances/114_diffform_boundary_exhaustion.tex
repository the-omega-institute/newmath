\section{Boundary exhaustion for DiffFormUp}
\label{sec:diffform-boundary-exhaustion}

\begin{definition}[Differential-form boundary exhaustion source]
\label{def:diffform-boundary-exhaustion-source}
The boundary exhaustion source for $\DiffFormUp$ is the complete paper-side
obligation surface displayed by the chapter. It consists of the following ten
fields:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item the traditional target surface of differential forms;
  \item the BEDC sources $BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$,
  $\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$, and $\NameCert_{\TensorProductUp}$;
  \item the carrier and classifier rows of
  \autoref{def:diffform-root-unblock-source-surface},
  \autoref{def:diffform-root-obligation-source}, and
  \autoref{thm:diffform-root-carrier-classifier-obligation};
  \item the wedge ledger rows visible through
  \autoref{thm:diffform-root-wedge-antisymmetry-obligation};
  \item the exterior-derivative candidate row of
  \autoref{def:diffform-exterior-derivative-candidate-row};
  \item the input-output exactness row of
  \autoref{thm:diffform-exterior-derivative-input-output-ledger-exactness};
  \item the downstream boundary rows for $\DeRhamUp$, $\SymplecticUp$, and
  $\ContactUp$;
  \item the exactness scope asserting that every displayed row is read as one
  of the named carrier, classifier, wedge, antisymmetry, exterior-input,
  transport, source-ledger, or downstream rows;
  \item the absent standard bridge slot;
  \item the status row saying that this is a paper-side obligation surface,
  not a standard-object bridge and not a Lean-checked theorem package.
\end{enumerate}
The source contains no row for $d^2=0$, closedness, nondegeneracy, contact
nonintegrability, a de Rham quotient, or a standard bridge.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Differential-form boundary carrier coverage]
\label{thm:diffform-boundary-exhaustion-carrier-coverage}
The carrier and classifier rows of
\autoref{def:diffform-boundary-exhaustion-source} are exactly the root source
rows already displayed in
\autoref{def:diffform-root-unblock-source-surface},
\autoref{def:diffform-root-obligation-source}, and
\autoref{thm:diffform-root-carrier-classifier-obligation}. No additional
carrier over tangent spaces, alternating maps, quotient forms, or external
classifier is part of the $\DiffFormUp$ boundary exhaustion source.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DiffFormUp.DiffFormBoundaryExhaustion\_carrier\_coverage}
\begin{proof}
Read the source surface first. The ten-field root-unblock surface lists the
ambient $BHist$ carrier, the $\hsame$ comparisons, the $\Cont$ rows, the
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ spine, the two upstream NameCert sources, the displayed
six-coordinate carrier, and the displayed $\DiffFormUp$ classifier.

Next read the root obligation source. It selects the same carried rows from
\autoref{def:diffform-bhist-form-carrier} and the same classifier rows from
\autoref{def:diffform-bhist-form-classifier}, with the retained
$\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\TensorProductUp}$ sources.

Finally apply
\autoref{thm:diffform-root-carrier-classifier-obligation}. That theorem
repackages the displayed carrier and classifier obligations without adding a
host tangent-space carrier, an alternating-map carrier, or a quotient
classifier. Therefore the carrier and classifier entries of
\autoref{def:diffform-boundary-exhaustion-source} are exactly the displayed
root source entries.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Differential-form wedge and derivative routing]
\label{thm:diffform-boundary-exhaustion-wedge-derivative-routing}
The wedge, antisymmetry, degree, and exterior-derivative candidate rows of
\autoref{def:diffform-boundary-exhaustion-source} are exactly the rows
supplied by
\autoref{thm:diffform-root-wedge-antisymmetry-obligation},
\autoref{thm:diffform-root-exterior-derivative-boundary-obligation},
\autoref{def:diffform-exterior-derivative-candidate-row}, and
\autoref{thm:diffform-exterior-derivative-input-output-ledger-exactness}.
The boundary source routes those rows as visible ledger data and does not add
a Leibniz rule, a nilpotence row, or a closedness row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the wedge and antisymmetry side. The root wedge theorem supplies
the admitted tensor concatenation ledger, the probe concatenation classifier
stability row, the associator row, and the finite antisymmetry chain ledger.
These are the only multiplicative and permutation rows named by the boundary
source.

Now read the exterior-derivative side. The root exterior-derivative boundary
obligation keeps only the candidate derivative row, the displayed
input-output ledger, and the wedge-side boundary row consumed by downstream
wedge formulas.

Unfold the candidate row. Its fields are the input carried form, the output
carried form, the one-step degree-raise $\Cont$ ledger, the transported
probe, tensor, scalar endpoint, and source ledgers, all over the same BEDC
sources named in the boundary source.

Finally apply the input-output exactness theorem. It says that the candidate
row exposes exactly those fields and no closedness, nondegeneracy, contact
condition, or second-derivative identity. Hence the wedge, antisymmetry,
degree, and derivative entries of the boundary source are precisely the cited
ledger rows.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DiffFormUp.DiffFormBoundaryExhaustion\_wedge\_derivative\_routing}

\begin{theorem}[Differential-form downstream export exhaustion]
\label{thm:diffform-boundary-exhaustion-downstream-export}
The downstream chapters $\DeRhamUp$, $\SymplecticUp$, and $\ContactUp$ may
consume $\DiffFormUp$ only through the rows named by
\autoref{def:diffform-boundary-exhaustion-source}: carrier rows, classifier
rows, wedge rows, antisymmetry rows, the exterior-derivative candidate and
input-output rows, transport rows, source-ledger rows, and the displayed
downstream boundary rows. They receive no $d^2=0$, closedness,
nondegeneracy, contact nonintegrability, quotient, or standard-bridge row
from $\DiffFormUp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the common input first. By
\autoref{thm:diffform-boundary-exhaustion-carrier-coverage}, every downstream
read begins with the same carried $\DiffFormUp$ rows and the same
$\sim_{\DiffFormUp}$ classifier rows already displayed at the root boundary.

Project the operation rows next. By
\autoref{thm:diffform-boundary-exhaustion-wedge-derivative-routing}, the only
available wedge, antisymmetry, degree, and exterior-derivative entries are the
visible ledger rows named by the root obligation and input-output exactness
statements.

Now use the downstream boundary package. Its scope rows name $\DeRhamUp$,
$\SymplecticUp$, and $\ContactUp$ as consumers of the same carrier,
classifier, wedge, antisymmetry, exterior-input, transport, and source-ledger
surface, and they exclude nilpotence and geometric laws from the exported
DiffForm boundary.

Repack these projections as
\autoref{def:diffform-boundary-exhaustion-source}. Every downstream row is one
of the displayed source fields, and none of the cited projections contains a
row for $d^2=0$, closedness, nondegeneracy, contact nonintegrability, a
quotient, or a standard bridge. Thus the displayed rows exhaust the
paper-side $\DiffFormUp$ obligation surface available downstream.
\end{proof}
