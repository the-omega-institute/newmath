\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CWComplexUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-cw-complex-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Whitehead and Hatcher, the $\CWComplexUp$ packet records the finite cell-attachment surface used by algebraic-topology and homology consumers before any quotient-space, weak-topology, or homotopy-extension theorem is imported. It sits after the open-neighbourhood interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-topology-namecert}, the finite face inventory of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-simplicialcomplex-namecert}, the boundary and cycle consumer of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-homology-namecert}, and the finite subcover discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compact-namecert}. The chapter names cellular strata, attaching maps, boundary incidence, and homology handoff as displayed $\BHist$ data rather than as an ambient topological construction.

\begin{definition}[CW-complex carrier]\label{def:cw-complex-carrier}
A $\CWComplexUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
X=(T,K,E,A,B,C,H,R,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $T$ is the $\TopologyUp$ source row, naming the open-neighbourhood interface on which cells are inspected.
\item $K$ is the $\SimplicialComplexUp$ comparison row for finite face-style reads and cellular approximation consumers.
\item $E$ is the finite cell inventory, with each displayed cell carrying a dimension, a name, and an incidence window.
\item $A$ is the attaching ledger, recording the finite boundary route by which an $n$-cell reads the lower-dimensional skeleton.
\item $B$ is the cellular boundary ledger, listing incidence coefficients only through displayed attaching data.
\item $C$ is the $\CompactUp$ inspection row for finite subcomplex and compact-closure reads.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for topology, simplicial comparison, cell inventory, attaching, boundary, compact inspection, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $R$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from $T,K,E,A,B,C,H$ to cellular and homology-facing consumers.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CWComplexUp$, $\TopologyUp$, $\SimplicialComplexUp$, $\HomologyUp$, $\CompactUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CWComplexUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through these rows. It has no coordinate for a host quotient topology, arbitrary weak topology, selected characteristic maps, infinite cellular chains, global homotopy extension, or completed homology computation.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CW-complex NameCert obligations]\label{thm:cw-complex-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\CWComplexUp$ carrier $X=(T,K,E,A,B,C,H,R,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CWComplexUp}$ surface consists exactly of the finite carrier admission, cell-inventory coverage, attaching-ledger replay, cellular-boundary exposure, compact finite-subcomplex inspection, componentwise transport, displayed replay, provenance, and local naming obligations recorded by $T,K,E,A,B,C,H,R,P,N$. A consumer cannot replace these rows by quotient-space semantics, weak-topology closure, selected characteristic maps, arbitrary infinite cellular chains, host homotopy extension, or an ambient homology computation.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:cw-complex-carrier}. The topology and comparison rows are $T,K$, the cellular data are $E,A,B$, the compact inspection row is $C$, and the structural rows are $H,R,P,N$.

Cell-inventory coverage is read from $E$. Each cell has a displayed dimension and incidence window before an attaching route is considered, so the packet never starts from an unbounded class of cells.

The attaching and boundary obligations are then read from $A$ and $B$. The boundary ledger can inspect only the lower-skeleton data named by the attaching ledger, while compact finite-subcomplex reads pass through $C$.

Finally $H$ and $R$ transport and replay the same rows, and $P,N$ source and name them. Since no coordinate stores quotient topology, weak topology, selected characteristic maps, infinite cellular chains, homotopy extension, or completed homology, those endpoints are outside the certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CW-complex cellular-boundary handoff]\label{thm:cw-complex-cellular-boundary-handoff}
Every $\HomologyUp$ consumer of an accepted $\CWComplexUp$ packet reaches cellular boundary data only through the finite route
$$
\TopologyUp,\;\SimplicialComplexUp,\;E,A,B,C \longmapsto \HomologyUp .
$$
The handoff exposes the topological source, simplicial comparison surface, finite cell inventory, attaching ledger, cellular boundary ledger, compact finite-subcomplex inspection, componentwise transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows. It does not export quotient-space equality, a weak-topology oracle, selected characteristic maps, unrestricted cellular chains, host homotopy extension, or computed homology groups.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:cw-complex-namecert-obligations}. They force the consumer to read the topology row $T$, the simplicial comparison row $K$, and the finite cell inventory $E$ before boundary data is available.

Next use the attaching ledger $A$. A cellular boundary read through $B$ is valid only after the lower-skeleton route named by $A$ is displayed, and compact finite-subcomplex inspection remains the separate row $C$.

The structural rows $H,R,P,N$ repack this route for homology-facing consumers. Therefore the $\HomologyUp$ handoff is exactly the displayed finite cellular boundary route and cannot import quotient spaces, weak topology, selected characteristic maps, unrestricted chain complexes, host homotopy extension, or completed homology.
\end{proof}
