\begin{definition}[Phenomenological frame carrier]
\label{def:phenomenological-frame-carrier}
A $\PhenomenologicalFrameUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
F=(L,M,U,O,G,A,T,H,C,P,R,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $L$ is the $\FocusedLedgerUp$ row exposing selected histories, focus, selector, digest, gap, relation, invariant, dependence, audit, replay, provenance, and local naming data;
\item $M$ is the $\FocusedMultiHistLedgerUp$ row exposing the finite multi-history support used by the frame;
\item $U$ is the consciousness/universe row, read through the finite universe-slice discipline of $\ConsciousUniverseSpaceUp$ rather than through a global universe object;
\item $O$ is the $\ObserverSubjectBoundaryUp$ row that keeps observation on finite $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows without adding observer substance;
\item $G$ is the $\GapClosureBoundaryUp$ row recording gap requests, source rows, refusal ledgers, and non-escape data;
\item $A$ is the hard-problem and action row, consuming the gap boundary and the $\CausalCommitmentUp$ socket for action, causality, freedom, other minds, mind--matter relation, and self-reference readings;
\item $T$ is the local time and continuation ledger, exposing only displayed continuation steps and no object-level time carrier;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $L,M,U,O,G,A,T,C,P,R,N$;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay for focused ledger reads, multi-history restriction, universe-slice access, observer-boundary inspection, gap refusal, causal commitment, and local time steps;
\item $P$ records $\Pkg$ provenance over $\PhenomenologicalFrameUp$, $\FocusedLedgerUp$, $\FocusedMultiHistLedgerUp$, $\ConsciousUniverseSpaceUp$, $\ObserverSubjectBoundaryUp$, $\GapClosureBoundaryUp$, $\CausalCommitmentUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $R$ is the audit-refusal row excluding subject substance, global universe object, hidden chooser, object-level $T$, host equality, quotient history equality, and off-packet continuation;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\PhenomenologicalFrameUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for private consciousness substance, ambient world container, global time object, unledgered agency selector, hidden solution of the hard problem, or causal necessity outside the displayed rows.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Phenomenological frame NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:phenomenological-frame-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:phenomenological-frame-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\PhenomenologicalFrameUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $F=(L,M,U,O,G,A,T,H,C,P,R,N)$, focused-ledger admission through $L$, multi-history support through $M$, consciousness/universe slicing through $U$, no-subject observation through $O$, gap and refusal routing through $G$, hard-problem and action commitment through $A$, local time and continuation exposure through $T$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, package provenance through $P$, audit refusal through $R$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier into its twelve rows. The rows $L,M,U,O$ expose focused records, multi-history support, finite universe slicing, and no-subject observation before the frame can be consumed as a phenomenological packet.

Read the gap and action rows next. The gap boundary $G$ supplies source/refusal routing, while $A$ records hard-problem and causal-commitment readings only as displayed socketed commitments.

Finally use the structural rows $T,H,C,P,R,N$. Local time remains a continuation ledger, transport and replay preserve only displayed rows, provenance names the sibling sources, audit refusal excludes hidden objects, and local naming registers the packet. Since no further coordinate exists, these obligations exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\label{thm:phenomenological-frame-audit-nonescape}

$$
It receives no subject substance, global universe object, hidden chooser, object-level $T$, host equality, quotient history equality, hidden hard-problem solution, causal necessity row, or off-packet continuation.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply the NameCert obligation surface. It identifies $L,M,U,O,G,A,T,H,C,P,R,N$ as the complete accepted packet and supplies no additional public coordinate.

Inspect the sibling rows. $\FocusedLedgerUp$ and $\FocusedMultiHistLedgerUp$ supply finite focused histories; $\ObserverSubjectBoundaryUp$ refuses a separate subject carrier; $\GapClosureBoundaryUp$ routes unsupported closure requests to visible refusal; and $\CausalCommitmentUp$ exposes causal reading as a forward socket rather than as necessity.

\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Phenomenological frame dependency package]
\label{thm:phenomenological-frame-dependency-package}
The dependency surface of an accepted $\PhenomenologicalFrameUp$ carrier is the ordered package consisting of focused-ledger rows, focused multi-Hist rows, consciousness/universe-slice rows, observer-subject-boundary rows, gap-closure rows, causal-commitment action rows, local-time continuation rows, transport, replay, provenance, audit refusal, and local naming. Dropping any one of the sibling rows $L,M,O,G,A$ changes the accepted dependency package.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier definition assigns one displayed coordinate to each sibling role. The package therefore has distinct rows for focus, multi-history support, observer boundary, shared gap closure, and causal commitment.

If $L$ is absent, the frame has no focused record route. If $M$ is absent, it has no finite multi-history support. If $O$ is absent, observation can no longer be certified without a subject-substance gap. If $G$ is absent, unsupported closure requests have no refusal ledger. If $A$ is absent, action and causal commitment are not socketed into the frame.

The remaining structural rows cannot reconstruct a missing sibling row. They preserve, replay, source, refuse, and name only what is already displayed, so the dependency package changes whenever any listed sibling coordinate is removed.
\end{proof}
