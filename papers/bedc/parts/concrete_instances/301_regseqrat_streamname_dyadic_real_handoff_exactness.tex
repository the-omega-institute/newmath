\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat StreamName Dyadic Real handoff exactness]
\label{thm:regseqrat-streamname-dyadic-real-handoff-exactness}
For every accepted $\RegSeqRatUp$ carrier, any public $\RealUp$ seal consumer
of that carrier factors exactly through the finite route
\begin{enumerate}
\item the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule rows selecting the visible finite windows;
\item the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint, radius, and dyadic-distance rows read
from those windows;
\item the $\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier rows over the same scheduled dyadic
observations;
\item the $\RealUp$ seal rows consuming that finite classifier surface.
\end{enumerate}
The factorization preserves the displayed $\hsame$ comparisons, $\Cont$
routes, $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ rows at every stage.
No quotient stream, host real equality, selected limit, metric-completion
object, ambient completeness datum, or extra seal coordinate can enter the
handoff.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:regseqrat-stream-carrier} and the
classifier of \autoref{def:regseqrat-classifier}. Their visible rows are the
scheduled $\StreamNameUp$ windows, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint and radius
reads, the finite dyadic-distance rows, regularity rows, classifier rows,
and the attached $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and local $\NameCert$
provenance.

Apply \autoref{thm:regseqrat-window-regularity-ledger} to the schedule side
and \autoref{thm:regseqrat-dyadic-radius-observation} to the classifier side.
Together they identify the endpoint, radius, distance, and regularity rows as
rows of the same finite scheduled observation package.

Transport that package through
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-finite-window-transport-obligation}. The transport
keeps the schedule, endpoint, radius, distance, regularity, classifier,
$\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and $\NameCert$ rows componentwise, so the
consumer has no alternative finite source surface available.

Finally use \autoref{thm:regseqrat-real-seal-handoff},
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-real-seal-classifier-factorization}, and
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-real-seal-public-handoff-determinacy}. The first
theorem routes the accepted regular-rational source to the $\RealUp$ seal,
the second makes the classifier factorization explicit, and the third locks
the public consumer to the same finite schedule and replay ledger. Since the
cited surfaces expose only the displayed finite rows, there is no coordinate
for quotient streams, host real equality, selected limits, completion
objects, ambient completeness data, or an additional seal route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat StreamName Dyadic Real handoff single-source lock]
\label{thm:regseqrat-streamname-dyadic-real-handoff-single-source-lock}
Let two public $\RealUp$ seal consumers read the same accepted
$\RegSeqRatUp$ carrier through displayed $\StreamNameUp$ schedules and
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint and radius ledgers. Then both seal reads factor
through one finite source route: the schedule rows, dyadic endpoint rows,
dyadic radius rows, $\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier rows, regularity rows,
$\hsame$ transports, $\Cont$ routes, $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance, and local
$\NameCert$ rows attached to that carrier.

The lock exposes no quotient stream, host real equality, selected limit,
metric-completeness datum, or extra $\RealUp$ seal coordinate. A consumer that
requests any of those objects has left the displayed source route rather than
reading a second seal source.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with \autoref{thm:regseqrat-streamname-dyadic-real-handoff-exactness}.
It routes each public $\RealUp$ seal read through the same four-stage
StreamName, DyadicRatCore, RegSeqRat, and Real seal path, preserving the
displayed $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and $\NameCert$ rows.

Next apply \autoref{thm:regseqrat-l10-source-lattice-pointer}. The L10 source
lattice records the finite schedule rows, dyadic endpoint and radius rows,
classifier rows, regularity rows, transports, routes, provenance, and naming
rows as one visible source packet. Since both consumers start from the same
accepted carrier, their displayed schedules and dyadic ledgers point into that
same packet.

Finally use \autoref{thm:regseqrat-real-seal-handoff},
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-real-seal-classifier-factorization}, and
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-real-seal-public-handoff-determinacy}. These seal
handoff results identify the public read with the regular-rational classifier
surface and rule out a second public seal source. The excluded quotient,
host-equality, selected-limit, completeness, and extra-coordinate data have no
row in the route just assembled, so the two seal consumers are locked to one
finite source route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat common dyadic tail ledger]
\label{thm:regseqrat-common-dyadic-tail-ledger}
Let $P$ be an accepted $\RegSeqRatUp$ carrier. Suppose two finite
$\StreamNameUp$ tail-window reads of $P$ feed public $\RealUp$ seal consumers,
and suppose the two reads admit a common cofinal dyadic refinement. Then the
two seal routes factor through one common finite ledger. This ledger consists
exactly of the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule rows, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint,
radius, and distance rows on the common refinement, the $\RegSeqRatUp$
classifier and regularity rows, componentwise $\hsame$ rows, displayed $\Cont$
routes, $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ rows attached to $P$.

The common ledger is a displayed finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ route. It is not a
quotient stream equality, host real equality, selected limit, choice datum,
ambient completeness witness, or second $\RealUp$ seal coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:regseqrat-streamname-dyadic-real-handoff-exactness} to each
of the two tail-window reads. Each public seal consumer is thereby routed
through the same four surfaces: the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule rows,
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint, radius, and distance rows, $\RegSeqRatUp$
classifier and regularity rows, and the $\RealUp$ seal rows consuming that
finite source surface.

Next apply
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-streamname-dyadic-real-handoff-single-source-lock}. Since
both reads start from the same accepted carrier $P$, the two public seal
consumers have one displayed source route, with the same componentwise
$\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and local $\NameCert$ rows.

It remains to identify the shared cofinal refinement as finite ledger data.
Use \autoref{thm:regseqrat-l10-source-lattice-pointer} to place the schedule,
endpoint, radius, classifier, regularity, transport, route, provenance, and
naming rows inside one visible L10 source packet.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:regseqrat-window-regularity-ledger},
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-dyadic-radius-observation}, and
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-dyadic-radius-ledger-readback}. These facts read the
cofinal dyadic refinement as displayed endpoint, radius, distance, and
regularity rows of the same finite packet. No step creates a host completion
object, selected limit, choice datum, quotient stream equality, host real
equality, or second seal coordinate, so the common tail route is exactly the
finite ledger stated above.
\end{proof}
