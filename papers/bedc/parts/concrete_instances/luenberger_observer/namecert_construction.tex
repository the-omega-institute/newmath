Following Luenberger's observer construction for finite-dimensional linear systems, the $\LuenbergerObserverUp$ packet records a deterministic state-estimate correction row rather than a stochastic filter. It consumes the observed-output surface of $\ControlObservabilityUp$ in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-controlobservability-namecert}, the reachability-side finite-control surface of $\ControlControllabilityUp$ in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-controlcontrollability-namecert}, the finite dual readback of $\ObservabilityGramianUp$ in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observability-gramian-namecert}, the estimator boundary of $\KalmanFilterUp$ in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-kalman-filter-namecert}, and the linear-map carrier of $\LinearMapUp$ in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-linearmap-namecert}. The chapter names the displayed observer ledger used by downstream estimator and observer-based-control packets; it does not identify the deterministic observer with a Kalman update.

\begin{definition}[LuenbergerObserverUp BHist carrier]
\label{def:luenberger-observer-carrier}
A $\LuenbergerObserverUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
\begin{gathered}
L=(A,C,Y,G,R,E,H,T,P,N).
\end{gathered}
$$
Here $A$ is the plant-dynamics row, read as the finite transition surface shared with $\LinearMapUp$ and the control-system certificates; $C$ is the output-map row consumed through $\ControlObservabilityUp$; $Y$ is the displayed measured-output and predicted-output comparison row; $G$ is the observer-gain row, with its finite matrix or linear-map provenance separated from any Kalman covariance update; $R$ is the residual row naming $y-C\widehat{x}$ as a finite output discrepancy; $E$ is the error-propagation ledger recording the closed observer error route governed by the displayed plant, output, and gain rows; $H$ is componentwise $\hsame$ transport for all observer rows; $T$ is displayed $\Cont$ replay for residual formation and error-propagation reads; $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LuenbergerObserverUp$, $\ControlObservabilityUp$, $\ControlControllabilityUp$, $\ObservabilityGramianUp$, $\KalmanFilterUp$, $\LinearMapUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$; and $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LuenbergerObserverUp}$ row.

The classifier compares observer packets only through $A,C,Y,G,R,E,H,T,P,N$. It has no coordinate for stochastic covariance recursion, optimal filtering, asymptotic convergence rate, pole-placement completeness, unobserved trajectory semantics, or a hidden analytic state space.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[LuenbergerObserverUp NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:luenberger-observer-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\LuenbergerObserverUp$ carrier $L=(A,C,Y,G,R,E,H,T,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\LuenbergerObserverUp}$ surface consists exactly of plant-dynamics admission through $A$, output-map admission through $C$, measured/predicted output comparison through $Y$, observer-gain admission through $G$, residual formation through $R$, error-propagation exposure through $E$, componentwise transport through $H$, displayed continuation replay through $T$, package provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.

A downstream deterministic estimator, observer-based controller, or control-diagnostic packet may read this surface only after the plant dynamics, output map, observer gain, residual, and error-propagation ledger have all been displayed. The packet exports no Kalman covariance update, no stochastic innovation law, no host convergence theorem, no pole-placement theorem, and no analytic state-trajectory object.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier of \autoref{def:luenberger-observer-carrier}. The rows $A,C,Y,G,R,E$ are the observer-facing data, while $H,T,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite packet.

The output row is read through $\ControlObservabilityUp$ and the transition/gain rows through the displayed finite linear-map surface. The observability Gramian and controllability packets can be used only as named provenance for finite control rows; they do not add an unrecorded analytic observer theorem.

The Kalman-filter sibling is present only as an estimator boundary. Since the carrier separates the deterministic gain row $G$ and residual row $R$ from any covariance update, a consumer cannot reinterpret the observer certificate as stochastic filtering data.

Thus the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface. Any read that demands a convergence theorem, pole-placement completeness theorem, covariance recursion, or host trajectory semantics leaves the accepted carrier.
\end{proof}
