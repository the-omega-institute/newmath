
\begin{theorem}[Continuous function carrier fourfold left-right synchronization]
\label{thm:continuous-function-carrier-fourfold-left-right-synchronization}
For BHist histories
$source,mid_1,mid_2,mid_3,target,f,g,h,k,fg,fgh,hk,ghk,map_L,map_R,
mod_F,mod_G,mod_H,mod_K,mod_{FG},mod_{FGH},mod_{HK},mod_{GHK},
mod_L,mod_R,cert_F,cert_G,cert_H,cert_K,cert_{FG},cert_{FGH},
cert_{HK},cert_{GHK},cert_L,cert_R$, suppose the four one-step carriers
$$
\begin{array}{l}
\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(source,f,mid_1,mod_F,cert_F),\\
\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(mid_1,g,mid_2,mod_G,cert_G),\\
\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(mid_2,h,mid_3,mod_H,cert_H),\\
\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(mid_3,k,target,mod_K,cert_K)
\end{array}
$$
hold. Suppose also that the left-deep ledgers
$$
\begin{array}{lll}
\mathsf{Cont}(f,g,fg),&
\mathsf{Cont}(mod_F,mod_G,mod_{FG}),&
\mathsf{Cont}(mid_2,mod_{FG},cert_{FG}),\\
\mathsf{Cont}(fg,h,fgh),&
\mathsf{Cont}(mod_{FG},mod_H,mod_{FGH}),&
\mathsf{Cont}(mid_3,mod_{FGH},cert_{FGH}),\\
\mathsf{Cont}(fgh,k,map_L),&
\mathsf{Cont}(mod_{FGH},mod_K,mod_L),&
\mathsf{Cont}(target,mod_L,cert_L)
\end{array}
$$
hold, and that the right-deep ledgers
$$
\begin{array}{lll}
\mathsf{Cont}(h,k,hk),&
\mathsf{Cont}(mod_H,mod_K,mod_{HK}),&
\mathsf{Cont}(target,mod_{HK},cert_{HK}),\\
\mathsf{Cont}(g,hk,ghk),&
\mathsf{Cont}(mod_G,mod_{HK},mod_{GHK}),&
\mathsf{Cont}(target,mod_{GHK},cert_{GHK}),\\
\mathsf{Cont}(f,ghk,map_R),&
\mathsf{Cont}(mod_F,mod_{GHK},mod_R),&
\mathsf{Cont}(target,mod_R,cert_R)
\end{array}
$$
hold. Then the left-deep and right-deep composite carriers agree at the
public graph, modulus, and certificate histories:
$$
\begin{array}{l}
\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(source,map_L,target,mod_L,cert_L)\land\\
\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(source,map_R,target,mod_R,cert_R)\land\\
\hsame(map_L,map_R)\land\hsame(mod_L,mod_R)\land\hsame(cert_L,cert_R).
\end{array}
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First build the left-deep carrier. Apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:continuous-function-carrier-comp-closed} to the $f$ and $g$
carriers with the displayed $fg$, $mod_{FG}$, and $cert_{FG}$ ledgers. Apply
the same closure theorem to compose that result with the $h$ carrier, then
again with the $k$ carrier. This gives
$\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(source,map_L,target,mod_L,cert_L)$.

The right-deep carrier is obtained by the symmetric closure chain. Compose the
$h$ and $k$ carriers through $hk$, $mod_{HK}$, and $cert_{HK}$; compose the
$g$ carrier with that composite through $ghk$, $mod_{GHK}$, and $cert_{GHK}$;
then compose the $f$ carrier with the resulting carrier to obtain
$\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(source,map_R,target,mod_R,cert_R)$.

For graph synchronization, apply the BEDC fourfold continuation associativity
theorem $\mathsf{cont\_assoc\_four}$ to the common graph spine
$f,g,h,k$. The left spine is presented by
$\mathsf{Cont}(f,g,fg)$, $\mathsf{Cont}(fg,h,fgh)$, and
$\mathsf{Cont}(fgh,k,map_L)$, while the right spine is presented by
$\mathsf{Cont}(h,k,hk)$, $\mathsf{Cont}(g,hk,ghk)$, and
$\mathsf{Cont}(f,ghk,map_R)$. The underlying append-associativity spine
$\mathsf{append\_assoc}$ yields $\hsame(map_L,map_R)$.

For modulus synchronization, apply the same fourfold continuation
associativity theorem to $mod_F,mod_G,mod_H,mod_K$. The left modulus ledger
ends at $mod_L$ and the right modulus ledger ends at $mod_R$, so the common
append spine yields $\hsame(mod_L,mod_R)$.

It remains to compare certificates. The ledgers
$\mathsf{Cont}(target,mod_L,cert_L)$ and
$\mathsf{Cont}(target,mod_R,cert_R)$ have the same target source. Continuation
respect for $\hsame$, using reflexivity on $target$ and the recovered modulus
synchronization, transports the certificate ledger to give
$\hsame(cert_L,cert_R)$.

The ten-field certificate fit is the following. The traditional target is
fourfold continuous composition coherence. The BEDC source is $BHist$ with
$\mathsf{Cont}$. The carrier is $\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}$ and the
classifier is $\hsame$. The ledger data are the graph, modulus, and
certificate histories displayed above. The stability field is fourfold
left-right bracketing synchronization. The exactness field is the pair of
constructed carriers together with the three $\hsame$ conclusions. The
downstream use is ContinuousMapUp associativity coherence and the later
category bridge.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Continuous function carrier fivefold left-right synchronization]
\label{thm:continuous-function-carrier-fivefold-left-right-synchronization}
For BHist histories
$source,mid_1,mid_2,mid_3,mid_4,target,f,g,h,k,l,fg,fgh,fghk,kl,hkl,ghkl,
map_L,map_R,mod_F,mod_G,mod_H,mod_K,mod_l,mod_{FG},mod_{FGH},mod_{FGHK},
mod_{KL},mod_{HKL},mod_{GHKL},mod_L,mod_R,cert_F,cert_G,cert_H,cert_K,
cert_l,cert_{FG},cert_{FGH},cert_{FGHK},cert_{KL},cert_{HKL},cert_{GHKL},
cert_L,cert_R$, suppose the five one-step carriers
$$
\begin{array}{l}
\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(source,f,mid_1,mod_F,cert_F),\\
\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(mid_1,g,mid_2,mod_G,cert_G),\\
\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(mid_2,h,mid_3,mod_H,cert_H),\\
\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(mid_3,k,mid_4,mod_K,cert_K),\\
\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(mid_4,l,target,mod_l,cert_l)
\end{array}
$$
hold. Suppose also that the left-deep ledgers
$$
\begin{array}{lll}
\mathsf{Cont}(f,g,fg),&
\mathsf{Cont}(mod_F,mod_G,mod_{FG}),&
\mathsf{Cont}(mid_2,mod_{FG},cert_{FG}),\\
\mathsf{Cont}(fg,h,fgh),&
\mathsf{Cont}(mod_{FG},mod_H,mod_{FGH}),&
\mathsf{Cont}(mid_3,mod_{FGH},cert_{FGH}),\\
\mathsf{Cont}(fgh,k,fghk),&
\mathsf{Cont}(mod_{FGH},mod_K,mod_{FGHK}),&
\mathsf{Cont}(mid_4,mod_{FGHK},cert_{FGHK}),\\
\mathsf{Cont}(fghk,l,map_L),&
\mathsf{Cont}(mod_{FGHK},mod_l,mod_L),&
\mathsf{Cont}(target,mod_L,cert_L)
\end{array}
$$
hold, and that the right-deep ledgers
$$
\begin{array}{lll}
\mathsf{Cont}(k,l,kl),&
\mathsf{Cont}(mod_K,mod_l,mod_{KL}),&
\mathsf{Cont}(target,mod_{KL},cert_{KL}),\\
\mathsf{Cont}(h,kl,hkl),&
\mathsf{Cont}(mod_H,mod_{KL},mod_{HKL}),&
\mathsf{Cont}(target,mod_{HKL},cert_{HKL}),\\
\mathsf{Cont}(g,hkl,ghkl),&
\mathsf{Cont}(mod_G,mod_{HKL},mod_{GHKL}),&
\mathsf{Cont}(target,mod_{GHKL},cert_{GHKL}),\\
\mathsf{Cont}(f,ghkl,map_R),&
\mathsf{Cont}(mod_F,mod_{GHKL},mod_R),&
\mathsf{Cont}(target,mod_R,cert_R)
\end{array}
$$
hold. Then the left-deep and right-deep composite carriers agree at the
public graph, modulus, and certificate histories:
$$
\begin{array}{l}
\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(source,map_L,target,mod_L,cert_L)\land\\
\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(source,map_R,target,mod_R,cert_R)\land\\
\hsame(map_L,map_R)\land\hsame(mod_L,mod_R)\land\hsame(cert_L,cert_R).
\end{array}
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First build the left-deep carrier. Apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:continuous-function-carrier-comp-closed} to the $f$ and $g$
carriers with the displayed $fg$, $mod_{FG}$, and $cert_{FG}$ ledgers. Apply
the same closure theorem to compose that result with the $h$ carrier, then
with the $k$ carrier, and finally with the $l$ carrier. This gives
$\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(source,map_L,target,mod_L,cert_L)$.

The right-deep carrier is obtained by the matching closure chain. Compose the
$k$ and $l$ carriers through $kl$, $mod_{KL}$, and $cert_{KL}$; compose the
$h$ carrier with that composite through $hkl$, $mod_{HKL}$, and $cert_{HKL}$;
compose the $g$ carrier with the resulting carrier through $ghkl$,
$mod_{GHKL}$, and $cert_{GHKL}$; then compose the $f$ carrier with that final
right-deep carrier. This gives
$\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(source,map_R,target,mod_R,cert_R)$.

For graph synchronization, apply
$\mathsf{BEDC.FKernel.Cont.cont\_assoc\_five}$ to the common graph spine
$f,g,h,k,l$. The left spine is presented by
$\mathsf{Cont}(f,g,fg)$, $\mathsf{Cont}(fg,h,fgh)$,
$\mathsf{Cont}(fgh,k,fghk)$, and $\mathsf{Cont}(fghk,l,map_L)$, while the
right spine is presented by $\mathsf{Cont}(k,l,kl)$,
$\mathsf{Cont}(h,kl,hkl)$, $\mathsf{Cont}(g,hkl,ghkl)$, and
$\mathsf{Cont}(f,ghkl,map_R)$. The fivefold continuation reassociation gives
$\hsame(map_L,map_R)$.

For modulus synchronization, apply the same fivefold reassociation theorem to
$mod_F,mod_G,mod_H,mod_K,mod_l$. The left modulus ledger ends at $mod_L$ and
the right modulus ledger ends at $mod_R$, so the common continuation spine
gives $\hsame(mod_L,mod_R)$.

It remains to compare the terminal certificates. The ledgers
$\mathsf{Cont}(target,mod_L,cert_L)$ and
$\mathsf{Cont}(target,mod_R,cert_R)$ have the same target source.
Continuation respect for $\hsame$, using reflexivity on $target$ and the
recovered modulus synchronization, transports the terminal certificate ledger
and gives $\hsame(cert_L,cert_R)$.

The ten-field certificate fit is the following. The traditional target is
pentagon-style fivefold continuous composition coherence. The BEDC source is
$BHist$ with $\mathsf{Cont}$. The carrier is
$\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}$ and the classifier is $\hsame$. The
ledger data are the five one-step graph histories, their modulus histories,
their target-certificate histories, and the displayed left-deep and right-deep
composite ledgers. The stability field is fivefold left-right bracketing
synchronization. The exactness field is the pair of constructed carriers
together with the three $\hsame$ conclusions. The downstream use is
ContinuousMapUp pentagon coherence and the category bridge.
\end{proof}
