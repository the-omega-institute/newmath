\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\BundleUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-bundle-namecert}

The bundle interface $\BundleUp$ packages a fibred carrier $E \to B$ with locally trivial fibres of a fixed model type. It depends on $\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-manifold-namecert}) for the base and total-space topology, and supplies the substrate for the tangent, cotangent, and frame bundles.

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:bundle-carrier}

\begin{definition}[Bundle local-trivialization obligation surface]
\label{def:bundle-local-trivialization-obligation-surface}
The local-trivialization obligation surface for $\BundleUp$ is the ten-field
$\NameCert_{\BundleUp}$ template whose rows are fixed as follows.
\begin{enumerate}
\item The traditional target is a fibred carrier $\pi:E\to B$ whose fibres are
locally trivial over a fixed fibre model $F$.
\item The BEDC source is $\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$ together with visible
$\mathsf{BHist}$ histories for the base space, the total space, the projection,
local trivializations, transition data, and the source ledger.
\item The carrier is a $\mathsf{BHist}$ package
$\mathsf{BundleLocalTrivPkg}(P)$ exposing a base manifold row $B$, a total
manifold row $E$, a projection row $\pi$, a fibre-model row $F$, finite
chart-trivialization $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ rows, transition rows on overlaps,
and a ledger row recording the source of each displayed field.
\item The retained manifold classifier from $\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$ classifies
the base and total manifold rows.
\item The remaining classifier is componentwise $\hsame$ on projection
histories, fibre-model histories, trivialization $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ rows,
transition rows, and source-ledger rows.
\item Carrier stability transports $\mathsf{BundleLocalTrivPkg}$ packages along
the retained manifold classifier on $B$ and $E$ and along componentwise
$\hsame$ on every displayed bundle row.
\item Projection compatibility says that transport of $\pi$ along the base and
total classifiers remains the displayed projection row between the transported
manifold rows.
\item Transition congruence says that if two overlapping trivialization rows
are transported by componentwise $\hsame$, then the corresponding transition
row is transported to the displayed transition row over the transported
overlap.
\item Ledger descent says that every transported carrier, projection,
trivialization, fibre-model, and transition row keeps the same source entry
after replacing its fields by classified histories.
\item Exactness is visible local-trivialization readback: every accepted
$\NameCert_{\BundleUp}$ field is one of the carrier, classifier, stability,
projection, transition, ledger, and readback rows above. The standard bridge
row is absent.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:bundle-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[Bundle carrier projection obligation]
\label{thm:bundle-carrier-projection-obligation}
Every carried row of $\mathsf{BundleLocalTrivPkg}(P)$ exposes exactly the
base manifold row, total manifold row, projection row, fibre-model row,
trivialization $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ row, transition row, and source-ledger
row displayed in
\autoref{def:bundle-local-trivialization-obligation-surface}. There is no
other carrier case in the $\BundleUp$ obligation surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack $\mathsf{BundleLocalTrivPkg}(P)$ by
\autoref{def:bundle-local-trivialization-obligation-surface}. The carrier
slot is a $\mathsf{BHist}$ package whose displayed fields are the base row,
total row, projection row, fibre-model row, trivialization
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ rows, transition rows, and source ledger.

Projecting a carried bundle row therefore reads one of those listed fields.
The base and total projections are manifold rows classified by the upstream
$\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$ row, while the projection, fibre, trivialization,
transition, and ledger projections are the componentwise $\hsame$ fields of
the same package.

No additional branch is available: the carrier predicate is the displayed
package predicate itself, and its final readback row accepts only the listed
fields. Thus every carried row is one of the named rows, and the carrier has no
second case.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Bundle trivialization transition stability]
\label{thm:bundle-trivialization-transition-stability}
For packages satisfying $\mathsf{BundleLocalTrivPkg}$, componentwise $\hsame$
transport preserves local trivialization rows and transition compatibility
through the retained $\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$ classifier on the base and total
manifold rows.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.BundleUp.BundleLocalTrivPkg\_transition\_stability}
\begin{proof}
Start with a carried package and a componentwise transport witness. The base
and total manifold rows are transported by the classifier retained from
$\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$, so their chart and overlap histories remain accepted
manifold histories.

The trivialization row is a finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ of local witnesses.
Induct on that closed probe bundle. The empty probe row is preserved by the
carrier-stability clause, and each bundled probe is transported by the
componentwise $\hsame$ classifier on its trivialization history.

For an overlap row, the transition congruence field of
\autoref{def:bundle-local-trivialization-obligation-surface} compares the two
transported trivialization rows and the displayed transition row. Projection
compatibility supplies the same base and total rows after transport, so the
transition still relates fibres over the transported base overlap.

Ledger descent then transports the provenance row for the trivialization and
transition witnesses. Hence local trivialization rows and transition
compatibility are preserved by the displayed $\hsame$ transport.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Bundle transition cocycle ledger closure]
\label{thm:bundle-transition-cocycle-ledger-closure}
Let $P$ be carried by $\mathsf{BundleLocalTrivPkg}(P)$, and let
$\tau_i,\tau_j,\tau_k$ be three local trivialization rows in its finite
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ row. Suppose the pairwise overlaps
$\mathsf{Overlap}_{ij}$ and $\mathsf{Overlap}_{jk}$ are accepted by the same
trivialization $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, and suppose their common base
restriction carries the direct transition row from $\tau_i$ to $\tau_k$ and
the composed route through $\tau_j$ with continuation witness
$\Cont(\mathsf{Trans}_{ij},\mathsf{Trans}_{jk},\mathsf{Trans}_{ijk})$. Then,
after transporting the base and total manifold rows by the retained
$\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$ classifier, the direct transition row and the
composed transition row are componentwise $\hsame$. The source-ledger row for
this comparison is exactly the row displaying
$\tau_i,\tau_j,\tau_k$, the two overlap witnesses, the displayed $\Cont$
composition witness, and the transition congruence witnesses for the three
transition edges.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Work inside the carrier displayed in
\autoref{def:bundle-local-trivialization-obligation-surface}. The three
trivialization rows and the two accepted overlap witnesses live in the closed
finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ row of $P$.

Induct on the finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ of overlap witnesses. In the empty
case there is no additional overlap branch to inspect: ledger descent keeps
the displayed provenance row, and reflexivity of $\hsame$ identifies the
direct and composed transition histories on the vacuous carried overlap.

For the cons step, transport the head transition by componentwise $\hsame$.
Projection compatibility keeps the transported base and total manifold rows
aligned with the displayed projection row, so all three transition edges still
have the same source and target bundle rows on the common triple restriction.

Apply transition congruence to the $i,j$, $j,k$, and $i,k$ transition edges.
Continuation associativity for $\Cont$, followed by transitivity of $\hsame$,
identifies the direct $i,k$ transition history with the route composed through
the middle trivialization row $\tau_j$.

Finally, ledger descent repacks the same displayed provenance: the three
trivialization rows, the two overlap witnesses, the $\Cont$ composition
witness, and the transition congruence witnesses. This is the claimed
cocycle-style ledger row for the bundle transition comparison.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Bundle NameCert exactness obligation]
\label{thm:bundle-namecert-exactness-obligation}
The public fields of $\NameCert_{\BundleUp}$ are exactly the carrier,
classifier, stability, transition ledger, visible exactness readback, and
absent bridge row specified by
\autoref{def:bundle-local-trivialization-obligation-surface}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First project the carrier by
\autoref{thm:bundle-carrier-projection-obligation}. This accounts for the base
manifold row, total manifold row, projection row, fibre-model row,
trivialization $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ row, transition row, and source-ledger
row.

Next read the classifier and stability fields from the definition. The
classifier is the retained $\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$ classifier on base and
total rows together with componentwise $\hsame$ on all bundle-specific
histories. The stability fields are carrier transport, projection
compatibility, transition congruence, and ledger descent.

Finally apply the visible local-trivialization readback row. It accepts only
the displayed carrier fields, the displayed classifier and stability fields,
the transition ledger, and the explicit statement that the standard bridge row
is absent. Therefore the public $\NameCert_{\BundleUp}$ surface is exhausted
by exactly those fields.
\end{proof}
