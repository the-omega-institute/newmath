\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\OnePointCompactificationUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-one-point-compactification-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Alexandroff compactification and Kelley topology, the $\OnePointCompactificationUp$ packet records the one-point compactification boundary of a displayed locally compact metric source as finite BEDC NameCert data. It reads the local compact-neighbourhood surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locallycompact-namecert}, the radius-window closed-ball ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-propermetric-namecert}, the center-radius membership row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metricclosedball-namecert}, the compact witness surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, and the open-index handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-topology-namecert}. The packet names the added infinity row and the compact-complement neighbourhood route without importing open-cover compactness or a host compactification space.

\begin{definition}[OnePointCompactification carrier]
\label{def:one-point-compactification-carrier}
A $\OnePointCompactificationUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
A=(X,L,B,\infty,Q,T,K,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the locally compact source row. It supplies the point classifier, local compact-neighbourhood windows, and source transport through the $\LocallyCompactUp$ surface.
\item $L$ is the proper or closed-ball neighbourhood ledger. It records the displayed $\ProperMetricUp$ radius-window rows and the $\MetricClosedBallUp$ membership rows used to present compact complements around infinity.
\item $B$ is the closed-ball boundary row. It exposes only center-radius non-strict membership comparisons already admitted by $\MetricClosedBallUp$.
\item $\infty$ is the added infinity point row, disjoint from the displayed source-point rows by the local classifier and available only through the compactification packet.
\item $Q$ is the compact-complement row. A neighbourhood of $\infty$ is read as the complement of a displayed compact closed-ball or compact metric window, with the compact witness carried by the rows $L$ and $B$.
\item $T$ is the topology handoff row. It sends source-open rows and infinity-neighbourhood rows into the indexed open surface of $\TopologyUp$ without adding a second open-set carrier.
\item $K$ is the compact-metric handoff row. It records that compactness consumers of the displayed complement window read the $\CompactMetricUp$ witness already attached to $L$ and $B$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the source row, neighbourhood ledger, closed-ball row, infinity point row, compact-complement row, topology handoff, compact-metric handoff, replay row, provenance row, and local naming row.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay through
$$
X,L,B,\infty \longmapsto Q \longmapsto T,K \longmapsto H,P,N .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\OnePointCompactificationUp$, $\LocallyCompactUp$, $\ProperMetricUp$, $\MetricClosedBallUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\TopologyUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\OnePointCompactificationUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $X,L,B,\infty,Q,T,K,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for open-cover compactness, a host compactification, selected points, quotient spaces, choice, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, or a standard bridge.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[OnePointCompactification NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:one-point-compactification-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\OnePointCompactificationUp$ carrier
$$
A=(X,L,B,\infty,Q,T,K,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\OnePointCompactificationUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the locally compact source, proper or closed-ball neighbourhood ledger, closed-ball boundary row, infinity point row, compact-complement row, topology handoff, compact-metric handoff, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item source-local exactness, requiring every ordinary-point neighbourhood read to factor through $X$ and the cited $\LocallyCompactUp$ neighbourhood surface;
\item compact-complement exactness, requiring every infinity-neighbourhood read to open $L$ and $B$ before it reads the compact-complement row $Q$;
\item topology handoff, requiring the ordinary and infinity neighbourhood rows to be exported only through the indexed open surface $T$ of $\TopologyUp$;
\item compact-metric handoff, requiring compactness consumers to read only the displayed $\CompactMetricUp$ witness row $K$ attached to the same compact complement window;
\item provenance exhaustion, requiring $H,C,P,N$ to transport, replay, source, and name only the listed route.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored outside those rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:one-point-compactification-carrier}. The accepted packet displays the locally compact source $X$, the proper or closed-ball ledger $L$, the closed-ball row $B$, the infinity point row, the compact-complement row $Q$, the topology and compact-metric handoffs $T,K$, and the structural rows.

Source-local exactness follows from the first entry of the route. An ordinary-point neighbourhood read starts in $X$, so it can use only the local compact-neighbourhood rows supplied by $\LocallyCompactUp$.

Infinity-neighbourhood exactness is the compact-complement clause. The request first opens the neighbourhood ledger $L$ and the closed-ball row $B$, then reads $Q$ as the complement of the displayed compact closed-ball or compact metric window.

The two handoffs are separate. The row $T$ exports the ordinary and infinity neighbourhood rows to $\TopologyUp$, while $K$ exposes the compact witness already attached to the compact complement. Neither row creates an open-cover compactness theorem.

Finally, $H,C,P,N$ preserve, replay, source, and name the same row inventory. Since the carrier has no other coordinate, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface and cannot export a host compactification, selected point, quotient space, choice principle, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness theorem, Lean verification, or bridge credential.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[OnePointCompactification compact-neighbourhood handoff]
\label{thm:one-point-compactification-compact-neighborhood-handoff}
Every compactness, topology, or completion-facing consumer of an accepted $\OnePointCompactificationUp$ carrier factors its infinity-neighbourhood read through
$$
X,L,B,\infty \longmapsto Q \longmapsto T,K \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
Thus the consumer receives a compact-complement neighbourhood of $\infty$ only as a displayed proper or closed-ball window with its compact metric witness and topology handoff, and receives no open-cover compactness proof, host compactification space, selected source point, quotient-space construction, choice principle, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a consumer request at the added point. The only admitted added point is the row $\infty$, so the request must read the source row $X$ together with the neighbourhood ledger $L$ and closed-ball row $B$ before any compact complement can be named.

The compact-complement row is exactly $Q$. It records the complement of the displayed compact closed-ball or compact metric window; the compact witness remains the one already routed through $L,B$ and then through the compact-metric handoff $K$.

The topology export is exact. The row $T$ sends the ordinary source neighbourhood rows and the infinity-neighbourhood rows into the indexed $\TopologyUp$ surface, while $H$ and $C$ transport and replay the same data componentwise.

The provenance row $P$ names only the cited sibling certificates, and $N$ names only the local $\OnePointCompactificationUp$ packet. Therefore every downstream compactness, topology, or completion-facing read factors through the displayed route and cannot recover any excluded host or choice-based datum.
\end{proof}
