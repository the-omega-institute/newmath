Following Evans and Friedman on heat kernels and Bishop--Bridges constructive
analysis, the $\HeatKernelUp$ packet records only the finite kernel-window
surface inspected by BEDC consumers. It is adjacent to the finite heat-equation
stencil of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-heat-equation-finite-stencil-namecert}: the
stencil sibling names displayed difference rows, while this chapter names the
finite kernel-window packet that a downstream $\PdeUp$ consumer may inspect
after the kernel source, semigroup row, rational readback, $\RealUp$ seal,
$\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local
$\NameCert$ row have all been displayed. The packet uses $\RegSeqRatUp$,
$\CauchyUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ anchors for finite
readback and tolerance discipline; it does not export global analytic

countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[HeatKernel carrier]
\label{def:heat-kernel-carrier}
A $\HeatKernelUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(W,S,G,Q,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $W$ is the inspected kernel-window row, listing the finite time windows,
spatial windows, boundary tags, and dyadic tolerances requested by the
consumer.
\item $S$ is the finite heat-equation stencil source row, linked to the
$\HeatEquationFiniteStencilUp$ sibling and exposing only displayed source,
mesh, time-step, and boundary reads.
\item $G$ is the semigroup-composition row, recording the finite pair of
kernel windows and the displayed intermediate-window budget used to compose
them.
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row for every kernel value,
intermediate value, and stencil value inspected by $W,S,G$.
\item $R$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after the regular rational,
$\CauchyUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ rows have been read.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $W,S,G,Q,R,C,P,N$ and
for the local certificate rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from the requested kernel windows
through source-stencil, semigroup, readback, and real-seal rows.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\HeatKernelUp$,
$\HeatEquationFiniteStencilUp$, $\PdeUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$,
$\CauchyUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$,
and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\HeatKernelUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only through $W,S,G,Q,R,H,C,P,N$.
It has no coordinate for global analytic kernel existence, host integral
operators, function-space quotient equality, selected infinite partitions,
countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[HeatKernel NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:heat-kernel-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\HeatKernelUp$ carrier
$K=(W,S,G,Q,R,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\HeatKernelUp}$ surface consists
exactly of carrier admission, kernel-window admission, finite stencil-source
admission, semigroup-row exactness, regular rational readback, real-seal
nonescape, componentwise transport, displayed continuation replay, provenance,
and local naming. These obligations are discharged along the finite route
$$
W,S,G\longmapsto Q\longmapsto R
$$
together with $H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:heat-kernel-carrier}. The
projection exposes the inspected kernel windows, finite stencil source,
semigroup row, readback row, real seal, transport rows, replay rows,
provenance row, and local name.

The kernel-window and stencil-source obligations are $W$ and $S$. The row $W$
contains only the finite time, spatial, boundary, and tolerance windows that
the consumer requested, and $S$ routes those requests through the displayed
finite heat-equation stencil surface of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-heat-equation-finite-stencil-namecert}.

The semigroup obligation is $G$. It records the finite pair of kernel windows,
the displayed intermediate-window budget, and the requested composition row;
it is not an integral over a host continuum and does not select an infinite
partition.

The readback and seal obligations are downstream. The row $Q$ reads the
displayed values through $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\CauchyUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, and
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$, and $R$ exposes the $\RealUp$ seal only after those finite
rows have been displayed.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same route. Since
the carrier contains no row for global analytic kernel existence, host
integral operators, quotient function equality, selected infinite partitions,
countable choice, or ambient real completeness, the listed rows exhaust the
local certificate surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[HeatKernel semigroup handoff]
\label{thm:heat-kernel-semigroup-handoff}
Every $\PdeUp$ consumer of an accepted $\HeatKernelUp$ packet reaches a
semigroup-composition read only through
$$
\HeatEquationFiniteStencilUp \longmapsto \HeatKernelUp
\longmapsto \RegSeqRatUp \longmapsto \RealUp
$$
inside the displayed finite window package. The consumer receives no global
fundamental solution, host integral kernel, quotient equality of functions,
countable-choice schedule, selected limiting partition, or ambient $\RealUp$
completeness claim.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the finite stencil-source row $S$. It names the source, mesh,
time-step, and boundary data that the sibling
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-heat-equation-finite-stencil-namecert} exposes,
so the $\PdeUp$ consumer cannot read a kernel row detached from the finite
heat-equation source.

Next read the inspected kernel windows $W$ and the semigroup row $G$. The
handoff composes only the displayed pair of windows over the displayed
intermediate-window budget, with every requested value still recorded as a
finite row.

The route then passes through $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback $Q$ and terminal
$\RealUp$ seal $R$, while $H,C,P,N$ preserve the same packet under transport,
replay, provenance, and local naming. Because the excluded analytic objects
are absent from the carrier, every semigroup handoff remains a finite
NameCert route rather than a claim of global heat-kernel existence.
\end{proof}
