\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\LiminfUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-liminf-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Hardy and Bishop--Bridges, $\LiminfUp$ records the finite tail-lower-cut companion to the tail-upper-cut surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-limsup-namecert}. The packet consumes the bounded sequence rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-boundedrealsequence-namecert}, the lower-cut observation discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bounded-real-lower-cut-namecert}, the upper-cut comparison discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bounded-real-upper-cut-namecert}, and the terminal $\RealUp$ seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. It gives Real-and-Completion consumers a finite lower-tail boundary for bounded real sequences without importing Real completeness, trichotomy, a host infimum, quotient stream equality, selected subsequences, or open-cover compactness.

\begin{definition}[Liminf carrier]
\label{def:liminf-carrier}
A $\LiminfUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
I=(S,L,D,T,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the bounded $\BoundedRealSequenceUp$ row whose finite windows, bound ledger, and displayed $\RealUp$ entries are being inspected.
\item $L$ is the tail lower-cut schedule, recording for each displayed tail index the bounded lower-cut observations read through $\BoundedRealLowerCutUp$.
\item $D$ is the dyadic tolerance ledger, recording the finite rational tolerances used to compare tail lower cuts with the upper observation surface.
\item $T$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row named as the liminf candidate exposed by the packet.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $S,L,D,T$ and the structural rows.
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which consumers replay the bounded sequence row, tail lower-cut schedule, dyadic tolerance ledger, terminal real seal, transports, provenance, and local naming row.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LiminfUp$, $\BoundedRealSequenceUp$, $\BoundedRealLowerCutUp$, $\BoundedRealUpperCutUp$, $\RealUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LiminfUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier stores a finite lower-tail cut surface for a bounded real sequence. It has no coordinate for Real completeness, trichotomy, a host infimum, quotient stream equality, selected subsequences, or open-cover compactness.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Liminf classifier]
\label{def:liminf-classifier}
Two $\LiminfUp$ carriers
$$
I=(S,L,D,T,H,C,P,N)
\qquad\text{and}\qquad
I'=(S',L',D',T',H',C',P',N')
$$
are classified as the same displayed liminf packet exactly when the bounded sequence rows match componentwise, the tail lower-cut schedules expose the same finite tail observations, the dyadic tolerance ledgers carry the same rational comparison budgets, the terminal $\RealUp$ seals are transported by the displayed $\hsame$ data, the continuation replays preserve the same consumer routes, and the provenance and local NameCert rows name the same $\LiminfUp$ surface. The classifier is therefore rowwise over $S,L,D,T,H,C,P,N$ and does not identify carriers by host sequence equality, quotient streams, selected cofinal subsequences, or a completed infimum construction.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Liminf NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:liminf-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\LiminfUp$ carrier $I=(S,L,D,T,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\LiminfUp}$ surface consists of six finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier formation for the bounded real sequence row, tail lower-cut schedule, dyadic tolerance ledger, terminal $\RealUp$ seal, transports, continuations, provenance, and local naming data;
\item classifier stability for the componentwise comparison of \autoref{def:liminf-classifier};
\item lower-tail exactness, stating that every lower observation in $L$ is read from a finite tail of $S$ through the bounded lower-cut surface;
\item upper-cut compatibility, stating that every tolerance row in $D$ compares the same tail data against the bounded upper-cut observation surface;
\item terminal seal routing, stating that the candidate row $T$ is consumed only as a $\RealUp$ seal after the displayed tail-cut checks have been replayed;
\item ledger non-escape, excluding Real completeness, trichotomy, host infimum selection, quotient stream equality, selected subsequences, and open-cover compactness.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier formation is projection from \autoref{def:liminf-carrier}. The packet displays exactly the bounded sequence row, tail lower-cut schedule, dyadic tolerance ledger, terminal real seal, componentwise transports, continuation routes, provenance, and local name.

Classifier stability is the componentwise comparison of \autoref{def:liminf-classifier}. Transport along $H$ may replace the visible rows only by rows with the same scheduled tail observations, the same dyadic budgets, and the same terminal seal route, while $C$ preserves the consumer replay order.

Lower-tail exactness is supplied by $L$ together with the bounded sequence row $S$. Each accepted lower observation names the finite tail index, the inspected sequence windows, and the bounded lower-cut certificate through which the read is made.

Upper-cut compatibility is supplied by $D$ and the sibling bounded upper-cut discipline. The tolerance ledger records only finite dyadic comparisons between the same tail data and the upper observation surface, so it does not become a host supremum or a completeness witness.

Terminal seal routing is the role of $T$. A consumer may read the candidate liminf only after the sequence tail, lower-cut, upper-cut, and tolerance rows have been replayed; the terminal coordinate remains a $\RealUp$ seal rather than a completed infimum object.

The non-escape row follows from the carrier inventory. No displayed field carries Real completeness, trichotomy, a host infimum, quotient stream equality, selected subsequences, or open-cover compactness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Liminf tail-cut route]
\label{thm:liminf-tail-cut-route}
Every Real-and-Completion consumer of an accepted $\LiminfUp$ packet factors through the finite route
$$
S\longmapsto L\longmapsto D\longmapsto T\longmapsto H\longmapsto C\longmapsto P\longmapsto N
$$
before using the displayed liminf surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the bounded sequence row $S$. It supplies the only displayed source of sequence entries, finite windows, and boundedness evidence available to the packet.

The tail lower-cut schedule $L$ then names which tails and lower observations are inspected. The dyadic ledger $D$ records the tolerance comparisons that align those tail-lower observations with the bounded upper-cut surface.

Only after those rows are replayed can a consumer inspect the terminal real seal $T$. The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ then transport, replay, source, and name the same finite route, so the consumer cannot bypass the tail-cut surface by reading a host infimum or a selected subsequence.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Liminf Real seal non-escape]
\label{thm:liminf-real-seal-nonescape}
An accepted $\LiminfUp$ packet exposes its candidate value only as the terminal $\RealUp$ row $T$ reached through the displayed tail-cut route. In particular, it does not export Real completeness, trichotomy, a host infimum, quotient stream equality, selected subsequences, or open-cover compactness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The only value-facing coordinate in the carrier is $T$. All sequence-facing information is confined to $S,L,D$, and all structural access is confined to $H,C,P,N$.

A consumer that asks for the candidate value must therefore follow the continuation replay from the bounded sequence row through the tail lower-cut and tolerance ledgers before reaching $T$. The replay exposes a sealed real name, not a construction by infimum, compactness, or subsequence selection.

Since no carrier field stores the excluded principles, componentwise transport and continuation replay cannot introduce them. The $\RealUp$ seal remains terminal for the packet, and the liminf surface remains a finite tail-cut certificate.
\end{proof}
