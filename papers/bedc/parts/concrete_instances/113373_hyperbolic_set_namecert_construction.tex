\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\HyperbolicSetUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-hyperbolic-set-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Smale and Katok--Hasselblatt, the $\HyperbolicSetUp$ packet records
the finite BEDC surface for an invariant set whose displayed tangent data split
into stable and unstable rows with bounded contraction and expansion budgets.
It sits over the finite routed dynamics of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dynsystem-namecert}, the metric observation
surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the compact-metric
source discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert},
and the stable-manifold consumer boundary of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-stablemanifold-namecert}. The packet names a
finite hyperbolic-set certificate; it does not assert global structural
stability, shadowing, measure recurrence, or complete stable-manifold theory.

\begin{definition}[Hyperbolic set carrier]
\label{def:hyperbolic-set-carrier}
A $\HyperbolicSetUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(X,D,M,K,E^s,E^u,\Lambda_s,\Lambda_u,A,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the invariant-set carrier row, naming the inspected subset and
the finite membership windows through which it is read.
\item $D$ is the $\DynSystemUp$ row whose displayed transition data preserve
the invariant-set row $X$.
\item $M$ is the $\MetricUp$ observation row for the source distances used by
the finite estimates.
\item $K$ is the $\CompactMetricUp$ support row, recording the compact metric
windows on which the estimates are certified.
\item $E^s$ and $E^u$ are the stable and unstable splitting rows, displaying
the two named directions over the inspected invariant-set windows.
\item $\Lambda_s$ is the finite contraction-budget row for stable directions,
and $\Lambda_u$ is the finite expansion-budget row for unstable directions.
\item $A$ is the $\StableManifoldUp$ handoff row, exposing only the local
splitting data that a stable-manifold consumer may read.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for invariant-set,
dynamical-system, metric, compact-metric, splitting, budget, handoff,
provenance, replay, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a hyperbolic-set request
through $X,D,M,K,E^s,E^u,\Lambda_s,\Lambda_u,A,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\HyperbolicSetUp$, $\DynSystemUp$,
$\MetricUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\StableManifoldUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$,
$\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\HyperbolicSetUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through these rows. It has no
coordinate for global structural stability, shadowing, measure recurrence,
complete stable-manifold theory, a host tangent bundle, or an ambient smooth
manifold theorem.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Hyperbolic set NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:hyperbolic-set-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\HyperbolicSetUp$ carrier
$Q=(X,D,M,K,E^s,E^u,\Lambda_s,\Lambda_u,A,H,C,P,N)$, the local
$\NameCert_{\HyperbolicSetUp}$ surface is exhausted by the following
obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for invariant-set, dynamical-system, metric,
compact-metric, stable-splitting, unstable-splitting, contraction-budget,
expansion-budget, handoff, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming
rows;
\item invariant-set preservation, requiring the displayed $\DynSystemUp$ row
to preserve the inspected membership windows of $X$;
\item metric and compact support, requiring $M$ and $K$ to expose the finite
distance and compact-window data before any splitting estimate is read;
\item splitting visibility, requiring $E^s$ and $E^u$ to remain separate named
rows rather than aliases of the invariant-set or metric rows;
\item budget exactness, requiring $\Lambda_s$ and $\Lambda_u$ to record finite
contraction and expansion estimates for the displayed splitting rows;
\item stable-manifold handoff, requiring $A$ to expose only the local
splitting-and-budget data consumed by $\StableManifoldUp$;
\item ledger nonescape, requiring $H,C,P,N$ to export no global structural
stability, shadowing theorem, measure recurrence theorem, complete
stable-manifold theory, host tangent bundle, or ambient smooth-manifold
principle.
\end{enumerate}
No local NameCert obligation is stored outside those rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:hyperbolic-set-carrier}. The accepted
rows are exactly $X,D,M,K,E^s,E^u,\Lambda_s,\Lambda_u,A,H,C,P,N$, so carrier
admission exposes only finite invariant-set, dynamical-system, metric,
compact-support, splitting, budget, handoff, transport, replay, provenance,
and local naming data.

The route first reads $X$ through $D$. Thus invariant-set preservation is a
displayed $\DynSystemUp$ obligation, not a host theorem about all orbits of an
ambient dynamical system.

The metric and compact rows $M,K$ are then read before the splitting rows.
They bind the stable and unstable estimates to finite distance windows and
compact-metric support.

Finally the separate rows $E^s,E^u,\Lambda_s,\Lambda_u$ provide the splitting
and its budgets, while $A,H,C,P,N$ only hand off, transport, replay, source,
and name that same finite packet. Since none of these rows adds global
structural stability, shadowing, measure recurrence, complete stable-manifold
theory, a host tangent bundle, or an ambient smooth-manifold theorem, the
listed obligations exhaust the NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Hyperbolic set splitting transport]
\label{thm:hyperbolic-set-splitting-transport}
Every stable-manifold or dynamical-systems consumer of an accepted
$\HyperbolicSetUp$ packet factors through the displayed route
$$
\DynSystemUp,\MetricUp,\CompactMetricUp
\longmapsto
E^s,E^u,\Lambda_s,\Lambda_u
\longmapsto
\StableManifoldUp
\longmapsto
H,C,P,N .
$$
Thus the consumer may transport stable and unstable splitting data, finite
contraction and expansion budgets, compact-metric support, componentwise
$\hsame$, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming. It
obtains no global structural stability theorem, shadowing theorem, measure
recurrence theorem, or complete stable-manifold theory.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a downstream consumer. By
\autoref{thm:hyperbolic-set-namecert-obligations}, the consumer must first
read the invariant-set and $\DynSystemUp$ preservation rows, then the
$\MetricUp$ and $\CompactMetricUp$ support rows.

Next read $E^s,E^u,\Lambda_s,\Lambda_u$. These rows carry the stable and
unstable splitting together with their finite budgets, so transport along
$\hsame$ preserves the displayed splitting fields rather than replacing them
by a host tangent-bundle construction.

The $\StableManifoldUp$ handoff row $A$ is downstream of those displayed
fields. It exposes local splitting-and-budget data only, while $C,P,N$ replay,
source, and name the same packet.

No row in the carrier is a global structural-stability argument, a shadowing
principle, a recurrence theorem, or a complete stable-manifold construction.
The transported surface is therefore exactly the finite BEDC route displayed
above.
\end{proof}
