\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\LocatedIntervalCompactnessUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-located-interval-compactness-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges, the $\LocatedIntervalCompactnessUp$ packet records the finite-cover compactness step available for a displayed located real interval when the cover has already been reduced to explicit dyadic mesh data. It sits over the located endpoint surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedinterval-namecert}, the dyadic rational arithmetic of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the terminal real-name rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the finite-net compact metric surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, and the finite refinement route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finite-cover-refinement-namecert}. The packet names only endpoint rows, mesh cells, finite cover rows, refinement witnesses, compact-metric handoff, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming evidence; it is not a general Heine--Borel theorem and does not import arbitrary open-cover compactness.

\begin{definition}[Located interval compactness carrier]
\label{def:located-interval-compactness-carrier}
A $\LocatedIntervalCompactnessUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
I=(L,E,M,F,W,K,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $L$ is the $\LocatedIntervalUp$ source row, exposing the lower and upper endpoint names, rational cell ledger, dyadic endpoint refinements, finite endpoint windows, and the downstream $\RealUp$ seals.
\item $E$ is the endpoint exposure row that records the two endpoint rows as the boundary read consumed by the finite mesh.
\item $M$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ mesh row, listing a finite dyadic subdivision with the endpoint cells and mesh-step witnesses used by the interval packet.
\item $F$ is the finite cover row, containing only displayed finite cover members indexed by the mesh cells.
\item $W$ is the refinement witness row, pairing each mesh cell with a retained finite cover member and the displayed inclusion or overlap comparison.
\item $K$ is the $\CompactMetricUp$ handoff row read after the mesh and finite cover rows have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the located interval, endpoint exposure, mesh, finite cover, refinement witness, compact-metric handoff, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from endpoint inspection through dyadic mesh construction, finite cover lookup, refinement witness replay, compact-metric handoff, and local naming.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LocatedIntervalCompactnessUp$, $\LocatedIntervalUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\FiniteCoverRefinementUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedIntervalCompactnessUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for an arbitrary open cover, selected subcover functional, sequential compactness principle, countable choice, quotient real equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Located interval compactness carrier exposure]
\label{thm:located-interval-compactness-carrier}
For every accepted $\LocatedIntervalCompactnessUp$ packet
$$
I=(L,E,M,F,W,K,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local certificate exposes exactly the located endpoint rows, the dyadic mesh row, the finite cover row, the refinement witness, the compact-metric handoff, componentwise transport, displayed replay, provenance, and local NameCert rows. Every finite-cover compactness read factors through
$$
L\longmapsto E\longmapsto M\longmapsto F\longmapsto W\longmapsto K\longmapsto(H,C,P,N)
$$
before any compact-metric or completion-facing consumer can use the packet. The exported route contains no general open-cover compactness theorem, no sequential compactness principle, no choice selector, and no ambient $\RealUp$ completeness field.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:located-interval-compactness-carrier}. The interval source is the row $L$, and the only endpoint-facing data available to the compactness route are the displayed endpoint rows named by $E$.

The finite construction then proceeds by dyadic mesh induction over $M$. The base cell reads the endpoint exposure row and the corresponding finite cover entry in $F$. The induction step appends the next displayed dyadic cell, reads its cover member from the same finite cover row, and records the retained overlap or inclusion comparison in $W$.

Apply the finite refinement route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finite-cover-refinement-namecert}. The witness row $W$ is the only refinement surface exported to the compact-metric handoff $K$, so a compact-facing consumer receives a finite mesh-and-cover comparison rather than an arbitrary open cover.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same displayed route. Since the carrier has no coordinate for a selected subcover functional, sequential compactness, countable choice, quotient real equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, none of those principles can be exported by the local certificate.
\end{proof}
