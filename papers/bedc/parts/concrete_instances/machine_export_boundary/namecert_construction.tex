\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\MachineExportBoundaryUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-machine-export-boundary-namecert}
\origin{ai}

The $\MachineExportBoundaryUp$ packet realizes the machine-facing export side of \autoref{sec:visions-philosophy-machine-interface} as one finite NameCert boundary. It sits beside the registry export surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-registry-export-surface-namecert}, the machine-readable audit interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-machine-readable-audit-interface-namecert}, the cannot-claim export gate of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cannot-claim-export-gate-namecert}, and the certificate-preserving morphism route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-namecertmorphism-namecert}. Those siblings expose registry status, audit readback, refusal, and morphism preservation separately; this packet binds target, audit, refusal, transport, replay, provenance, and local NameCert rows before a machine export may be read.

\begin{definition}[Machine export boundary carrier]
\label{def:machine-export-boundary-carrier}
A $\MachineExportBoundaryUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
E=(R,T,A,F,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $R$ is the $\RegistryExportSurfaceUp$ row, recording the exported, blocked, horizon-facing, or formal-target registry status.
\item $T$ is the machine target row, recording the exact machine-facing target that may read the export.
\item $A$ is the $\MachineReadableAuditInterfaceUp$ row, recording the schema, checker, audit report, and non-evidence boundary for the export.
\item $F$ is the $\CannotClaimExportGateUp$ refusal row, recording any blocked claim that must remain visible before export.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for registry status, target, audit, refusal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ stores displayed $\Cont$ replay from registry lookup to target routing, audit readback, refusal inspection, and local NameCert exposure.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\MachineExportBoundaryUp$, $\RegistryExportSurfaceUp$, $\MachineReadableAuditInterfaceUp$, $\CannotClaimExportGateUp$, $\NameCertMorphismUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MachineExportBoundaryUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no coordinate for an unregistered export, unchecked theorem evidence, refused-claim erasure, hidden transport, replay outside the displayed continuation row, provenance outside $P$, or a local name not carried by $N$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Machine export boundary NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:machine-export-boundary-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted packet $E=(R,T,A,F,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\MachineExportBoundaryUp}$ obligations are exhausted by registry export admission through $R$, target exposure through $T$, audit readback through $A$, refusal preservation through $F$, componentwise transport through $H$, displayed replay through $C$, package provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:machine-export-boundary-carrier}. The registry export row $R$, target row $T$, audit row $A$, and refusal row $F$ are displayed before a machine consumer reaches local naming.

Read the sibling routes in order. The registry export surface fixes the public status, the machine-readable audit interface fixes the audit report and non-evidence boundary, and the cannot-claim export gate keeps refused rows visible.

Finally use $H,C,P,N$. These rows transport, replay, package, and name the same export route, with $\NameCertMorphismUp$ present only as provenance for certificate-preserving handoff. Since the carrier has no hidden export or proof-producing report coordinate, the listed rows exhaust the NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}
