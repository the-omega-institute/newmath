\begin{definition}[Pointwise modulus ledger carrier]
\label{def:pointwise-modulus-ledger-carrier}
A $\PointwiseModulusLedgerUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
L=(X,Y,F,C,E,R,I,H,Q,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the source $\MetricSpaceUp$ row whose distance comparisons are tested near the displayed center;
\item $Y$ is the target $\MetricSpaceUp$ row whose distance comparisons read the requested tolerance;
\item $F$ is the $\ContinuousMapUp$ graph row over the same source and target metrics;
\item $C$ is the center history at which the local continuity row is requested;
\item $E$ is the positive $\RatUp$ tolerance history;
\item $R$ is the positive $\RatUp$ local radius history attached to $C$ and $E$;
\item $I$ is the implication certificate saying that every source history within radius $R$ of $C$ is mapped by $F$ within tolerance $E$ in $Y$;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the metric, graph, center, tolerance, radius, implication, route, provenance, and naming rows;
\item $Q$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes through which continuous-map and compact-net consumers read the packet;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\MetricSpaceUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\RatUp$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and local naming rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\PointwiseModulusLedgerUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exports only the displayed source metric, target metric, graph, center, tolerance, radius, implication, transport, route, provenance, and local naming rows. It has no coordinate for compactness, a finite-net selector, a folded lower bound, a host continuity theorem, a chosen global modulus, or an open-cover argument.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Pointwise modulus ledger NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:pointwise-modulus-ledger-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\PointwiseModulusLedgerUp}$ surface consists exactly of five finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the packet of \autoref{def:pointwise-modulus-ledger-carrier};
\item graph compatibility, requiring $F$ to be read as a $\ContinuousMapUp$ row from the source metric $X$ to the target metric $Y$;
\item center-tolerance-radius exactness, requiring $C$, $E$, and $R$ to be displayed together as the local pointwise request;
\item implication exactness, requiring $I$ to consume only the source-distance row in $X$, the target-distance row in $Y$, the graph row $F$, and the displayed $C,E,R$ rows;
\item non-escape, requiring every compact-net consumer read to pass through $X,Y,F,C,E,R,I,H,Q,P,N$ and forbidding compactness data, finite selector output, folded lower bounds, host continuity, chosen global moduli, and open-cover subcovers.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:pointwise-modulus-ledger-carrier}. The packet displays the source and target metrics, graph row, center, tolerance, radius, implication, transports, routes, provenance, and local name.

Graph compatibility reads $F$ only over $X$ and $Y$. The pointwise row is therefore attached to the same metric surfaces later used by a compact-net selector.

Center-tolerance-radius exactness binds $C,E,R$ as one local request. The radius row is not reconstructed from compactness or a folded minimum; it is the displayed positive $\RatUp$ radius carried by the packet.

Implication exactness reads $I$ as the local continuity certificate. Its antecedent is a source-metric comparison to $C$ below $R$, and its conclusion is a target-metric comparison below $E$ after applying the graph row $F$.

The non-escape clause follows from the carrier inventory and the displayed route rows. Since no compactness, finite selector, lower-bound fold, host theorem, global modulus, or open-cover coordinate is present, no accepted projection can export such data.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Pointwise modulus ledger center-radius handoff]
\label{thm:pointwise-modulus-ledger-center-radius-handoff}
Let
$$
L=(X,Y,F,C,E,R,I,H,Q,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\PointwiseModulusLedgerUp$ carrier. Any downstream $\CompactNetModulusSelectorUp$ read of the center-specific pointwise modulus factors through the displayed center row $C$, local radius row $R$, tolerance row $E$, graph row $F$, source and target metric rows $X,Y$, and implication certificate $I$. The selector receives a center-radius implication packet, not a compact finite-net fold or a host modulus.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The center, tolerance, and radius rows are visible as $C,E,R$, and the graph and metric rows are visible as $F,X,Y$.

Apply \autoref{thm:pointwise-modulus-ledger-namecert-obligations}. Its graph compatibility and center-tolerance-radius exactness clauses bind these rows before any consumer can read the implication certificate.

Now read $I$. The implication certificate consumes exactly the source-distance comparison to $C$ below $R$ and returns exactly the target-distance comparison below $E$ through $F$ and $Y$.

The compact-net selector can therefore use the row only as a center-radius handoff. It must still supply its own finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ spine and lower-bound fold, because neither coordinate appears in the accepted $\PointwiseModulusLedgerUp$ packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Pointwise modulus ledger non-escape]
\label{thm:pointwise-modulus-ledger-nonescape}
Every consumer read of an accepted $\PointwiseModulusLedgerUp$ packet factors through the displayed source metric, target metric, graph, center, tolerance, radius, implication, transport, route, provenance, and local naming rows of \autoref{def:pointwise-modulus-ledger-carrier}. The packet exports no compact finite-net row, no selected precision, no lower-bound fold, no open-cover compactness, no host continuity theorem, and no external modulus function.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Read the carrier coordinates $X,Y,F,C,E,R,I,H,Q,P,N$. These are the only rows available to the local $\NameCert_{\PointwiseModulusLedgerUp}$ projection.

Use \autoref{thm:pointwise-modulus-ledger-namecert-obligations}. Its exactness clauses route graph reads through $F$, local request reads through $C,E,R$, and continuity implication reads through $I$.

The transport, continuation, provenance, and naming rows $H,Q,P,N$ replay that same finite route componentwise. No coordinate remains from which a consumer could read compact finite-net data, a selected precision, a lower-bound fold, open-cover compactness, host continuity, or an external modulus function.
\end{proof}
