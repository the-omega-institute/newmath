\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ClosedUnitIntervalUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-closed-unit-interval-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, the $\ClosedUnitIntervalUp$ packet records the displayed $[0,1]$ real-name interval as finite dyadic windows, regular-rational readback, rational-density witnesses, and a visible $\RealUp$ seal. It is a closed endpoint packet over \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bishop-interval-compact-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rational-density-real-namecert}; it is not an open-cover compactness theorem, a selected subsequence, quotient real equality, or host real interval.

\begin{definition}[Closed unit interval carrier]
\label{def:closed-unit-interval-carrier}
A $\ClosedUnitIntervalUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
U=(Z,O,W,Q,D,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $Z$ is the displayed zero endpoint row, read through the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ rational and dyadic ledger and then through the visible $\RealUp$ seal;
\item $O$ is the displayed one endpoint row, read through the same $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ rational and dyadic ledger and the same $\RealUp$ seal;
\item $W$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window ledger used for the endpoint names and for any inspected interior regular name;
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row translating those finite windows into regular rational approximants;
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger recording the dyadic scales at which endpoint, window, and boundary comparisons are inspected;
\item $R$ is the rational-density or $\BishopIntervalCompactUp$ route row that records the finite witness surface by which an inspected regular-rational name lies between the displayed endpoints;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for endpoints, windows, readbacks, dyadic tolerances, density witnesses, interval routes, continuation rows, package provenance, and the local naming row;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from endpoint rows to dyadic windows, from windows to regular-rational readback, from readback to rational-density or finite interval-net witnesses, and from those rows to the visible $\RealUp$ seal;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ClosedUnitIntervalUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BishopIntervalCompactUp$, $\RationalDensityRealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ClosedUnitIntervalUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier classifier compares two accepted packets only by replaying the displayed rows $Z,O,W,Q,D,R,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for an arbitrary open cover, Heine--Borel compactness, sequential compactness, quotient real equality, selected limits, host real equality, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Closed unit interval NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:closed-unit-interval-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\ClosedUnitIntervalUp$ packet $U=(Z,O,W,Q,D,R,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\ClosedUnitIntervalUp}$ obligation surface consists exactly of endpoint admission for $Z$ and $O$, finite-window admission through $W$, regular-rational readback through $Q$, dyadic tolerance admission through $D$, rational-density or finite interval-net routing through $R$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed continuation replay through $C$, package provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:closed-unit-interval-carrier}. The zero endpoint, one endpoint, window, readback, dyadic tolerance, interval-route, transport, continuation, provenance, and naming rows are the complete displayed coordinates of the finite packet.

Read the endpoint and real-name side first. The rows $Z$ and $O$ expose the two displayed endpoints, while $W,Q$, and $D$ expose only finite windows, regular-rational approximants, and dyadic tolerances for those endpoints and inspected names.

Read the interval route next. The row $R$ records the rational-density or finite interval-net witness surface that places the inspected read between the endpoints at the displayed tolerance. It does not select a subsequence, a limit, or a subcover.

Finally $H,C,P$, and $N$ transport, replay, source, and name exactly these rows. Since the carrier has no coordinate for open covers, Heine--Borel compactness, quotient equality, host equality, choice, or ambient completeness, no such obligation can be read from the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Closed unit interval dyadic window boundary]
\label{thm:closed-unit-interval-dyadic-window-boundary}
Every accepted $\ClosedUnitIntervalUp$ consumer read of a point between the displayed endpoints factors through the finite boundary surface
$$
Z,O,W,Q,D,R.
$$
The read first inspects the zero and one endpoint rows, then the finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows, then the $\RegSeqRatUp$ rational readback and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, and only then the rational-density or finite interval-net route named by $R$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the endpoint rows. The packet exposes the displayed zero row $Z$ and one row $O$ before any interior or boundary membership read is available.

Next inspect the finite windows and rational readback. The rows $W$ and $Q$ provide only the scheduled $\StreamNameUp$ observations and regular-rational approximants that the dyadic tolerance ledger $D$ can compare.

Finally read the interval route $R$. It is attached to those same windows and tolerances, so a consumer cannot bypass $Z,O,W,Q$, and $D$ to obtain an ambient interval object or host real comparison.

The boundary is therefore a finite dyadic-window boundary. Later tails of a real name, quotient equality, selected limits, countable choice, and real completeness are absent from the packet and cannot affect the read.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Closed unit interval Real seal scope]
\label{thm:closed-unit-interval-real-seal-scope}
Every $\RealUp$-facing consumer of an accepted $\ClosedUnitIntervalUp$ packet must first consume the endpoint, finite-window, regular-rational, dyadic-tolerance, and rational-density or finite interval-net rows named by $Z,O,W,Q,D$, and $R$. The visible $\RealUp$ seal confirms the displayed endpoint packet and no stronger real interval principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Expose the real-facing rows by projection from \autoref{def:closed-unit-interval-carrier}. The packet reaches the visible $\RealUp$ seal only after the endpoint rows and their finite window, readback, tolerance, and route rows have been displayed.

Use \autoref{thm:closed-unit-interval-namecert-obligations} to exhaust the local certificate surface. Its real-facing obligations are exactly the listed endpoint, window, readback, dyadic, interval-route, transport, continuation, provenance, and naming rows.

Use \autoref{thm:closed-unit-interval-dyadic-window-boundary} for the consumer boundary. Any read that treats the packet as an interval first passes through the finite dyadic-window surface, so the seal cannot be reinterpreted as quotient real equality, host equality, open-cover compactness, selected limits, countable choice, or ambient real completeness.
\end{proof}
