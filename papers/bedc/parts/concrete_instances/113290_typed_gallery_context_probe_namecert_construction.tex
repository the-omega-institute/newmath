\begin{definition}[Typed gallery context-probe carrier]
\label{def:typed-gallery-context-probe-carrier}
A $\TypedGalleryContextProbeUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
G=(S,B,A,R,C,K,D,Q,H,T,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the sort-indexed context row, listing the contexts used by the gallery examples.
\item $B$ is the binder-shape row for Pi forms, shifted variables, dependent identity examples, and polymorphic identity examples.
\item $A$ is the application row for ordinary applications, application tails, and projected applications.
\item $R$ is the beta-redex row for the displayed reductions used by the gallery.
\item $C$ is the Church-example row for booleans, natural numbers, pairs, options, lists, sums, and equality examples.
\item $K$ is the checker-read row consumed by the MetaCIC decidability boundary and bounded typechecking budget surfaces.
\item $D$ is the discharge-facing row consumed by the substitution spine and subject-reduction boundary surfaces.
\item $Q$ records the boundary refusal: the probe supplies typed context data but does not supply a global preservation theorem.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for gallery rows and structural rows.
\item $T$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from the typed gallery audit map through $S,B,A,R,C,K,D,Q,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\TypedGalleryContextProbeUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows together with the MetaCIC typed-gallery audit surface.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\TypedGalleryContextProbeUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted probes only through $S,B,A,R,C,K,D,Q,H,T,P,N$. It has no coordinate for unrestricted typechecking completeness, unconditional subject reduction, global substitution soundness, confluence, normalization, conversion decidability, or a hidden checker oracle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Typed gallery context-probe NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:typed-gallery-context-probe-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\TypedGalleryContextProbeUp$ carrier $G=(S,B,A,R,C,K,D,Q,H,T,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\TypedGalleryContextProbeUp}$ surface consists exactly of sort-context admission, binder-shape admission, application admission, beta-redex admission, Church-example admission, checker-read exposure, discharge-facing exposure, boundary refusal, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming. The proof-search and typechecking consumers receive a finite gallery probe, not an unrestricted metatheorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet $G=(S,B,A,R,C,K,D,Q,H,T,P,N)$. The rows $S,B,A,R,C$ are the gallery-facing coordinates, so they give the only admitted context, binder, application, beta, and Church-example data.

The consumer-facing rows are $K,D,Q$. The checker-read row $K$ is the route to decidability and bounded typechecking budget consumers, while $D$ is the route to substitution-spine and subject-reduction boundary consumers. The refusal row $Q$ records that these routes do not close the global metatheorems.

Finally $H,T,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite probe. Since no hidden checker oracle or unrestricted theorem coordinate is present, the listed rows exhaust the NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Typed gallery context-probe binder handoff]
\label{thm:typed-gallery-context-probe-binder-handoff}
Every decidability, substitution-spine, or subject-reduction audit consumer of an accepted $\TypedGalleryContextProbeUp$ carrier receives binder data only through the route
$$
S,B,A,R,C
\longmapsto
K,D,Q
\longmapsto
\hsame,\Cont,\Pkg,\NameCert .
$$
In particular, sort-indexed contexts and Pi/application examples are exposed before the checker-read and discharge-facing rows are consumed.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with $S$ and $B$. These rows display the sort-indexed contexts and binder shapes before any checker or discharge request is available.

Read $A,R,C$ next. Application forms, beta redexes, and Church examples become probe data only as listed gallery rows, so the consumer cannot appeal to an ambient collection of examples.

The handoff then passes through $K,D,Q$. The checker-facing row and discharge-facing row are both guarded by the refusal row, which prevents the probe from being read as a proof of global subject reduction or global decidability.

Finally $H,T,P,N$ preserve the same route for replay and naming. Thus binder-sensitive audit consumers receive finite context probes with explicit boundaries.
\end{proof}
