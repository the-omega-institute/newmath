\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\MetaCICRedexFrontierUp$}

\origin{ai}

The $\MetaCICRedexFrontierUp$ packet realizes the beta-redex part of the
MetaCIC subject-reduction frontier as finite certificate data. It reads the
open-problem inventory of \autoref{ch:metacic-open-problems}, the
finite row catalogue of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacicopenproblemledger-namecert}, and the
normalization boundary of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacicnormalizationfrontier-namecert}. Its
purpose is narrower than those ledgers: it binds the four subject-reduction
discharge rows to a beta-boundary route and to an obstruction ledger, so a
consumer can distinguish an incomplete beta-redex packet from a complete
frontier carrier.

The packet is composite over the MetaCIC open-problem ledger, MetaCIC
normalization frontier, beta-boundary route, and obstruction-ledger siblings;
those rows jointly supply the carrier surface.

\falsifiablePrediction{A packet lacking the beta-redex row but carrying the other three discharge rows is rejected by the redex-frontier carrier.}

\independenceWitness{metacicopenproblemledger, metacicnormalizationfrontier, metacic-substitution-audit-packet, beta-critical-pair, apophaticname: those siblings either inventory open rows, record normalization boundaries, isolate substitution windows, expose critical pairs, or refuse external supply; none is bijective with the complete redex-frontier carrier because this packet requires all four discharge rows plus the obstruction ledger in one carrier.}

\begin{definition}[MetaCIC redex-frontier carrier]
\label{def:metacic-redex-frontier-carrier}
A $\MetaCICRedexFrontierUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
R=(B,A,L,P,O,E,H,C,G,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $B$ is the beta-redex discharge row, carrying $\mathrm{BetaSubstitutionPreservation}$ for the beta case;
\item $A$ is the application-argument discharge row, carrying $\mathrm{AppArgTypeStable}$ for reduction under the argument of an application;
\item $L$ is the lambda-domain discharge row, carrying $\mathrm{LamDomainSubjectReduction}$ for binder-domain reduction inside lambda formation;
\item $P$ is the Pi-domain discharge row, carrying $\mathrm{PiDomainSubjectReduction}$ for binder-domain reduction inside Pi formation;
\item $O$ is the obstruction ledger row recording that the four rows are required because closed subject reduction is not exported at this boundary;
\item $E$ is the beta-boundary route row that places beta-redex consumption before any subject-reduction handoff;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $B,A,L,P,O,E$ together with provenance and local naming rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes from the four discharge rows to the beta-boundary route and from that route to subject-reduction consumers;
\item $G$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, $\NameCert$, $\SubjectReductionDischargeBundle$, $\SubjectReductionSetup$, $\MetaCICSubjectReductionFrontierUp$, $\MetaCICNormalizationFrontierUp$, $\BetaCriticalPairUp$, and the open-problem ledger rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MetaCICRedexFrontierUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier accepts exactly packets with all four discharge rows
$B,A,L,P$ and the obstruction ledger $O$ present in the same carrier. It has no
coordinate for a beta-only shortcut, a three-row discharge interface, a hidden
substitution oracle, a closed subject-reduction theorem, or a consumer route
that bypasses $E$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCICRedexFrontier NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:metacic-redex-frontier-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:metacic-redex-frontier-carrier}, the local
$\NameCert_{\MetaCICRedexFrontierUp}$ surface consists exactly of six finite
obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for $R=(B,A,L,P,O,E,H,C,G,N)$;
\item four-row discharge coverage for beta-redex, application-argument, lambda-domain, and Pi-domain subject-reduction cases;
\item beta-boundary routing through $E$ before any subject-reduction consumer can read the discharge packet;
\item obstruction ledger exposure through $O$, separating the finite route from a closed subject-reduction theorem;
\item componentwise transport and replay through $H$ and $C$;
\item provenance and local naming exhaustion through $G,N$, with no beta-only shortcut, hidden substitution oracle, or off-packet consumer route.
\end{enumerate}
The Lean follow-up must provide \texttt{Nontrivial MetaCICRedexFrontierUp} and
\texttt{FieldFaithful MetaCICRedexFrontierUp} in
\texttt{lean4/BEDC/Derived/MetaCICRedexFrontierUp/TasteGate.lean}.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.MetaCICRedexFrontierUp.MetaCICRedexFrontierCarrier\_namecert\_obligations}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from
\autoref{def:metacic-redex-frontier-carrier}. The displayed tuple
$R=(B,A,L,P,O,E,H,C,G,N)$ is the full row inventory available to an accepted
redex-frontier reader.

The four discharge rows are read next. The beta row $B$ supplies the
substitution-preservation case, while $A,L,P$ supply the application-argument,
lambda-domain, and Pi-domain cases. Since the classifier requires all four
rows, a packet that omits $B$ is not the displayed carrier even if $A,L,P$ are
present.

The boundary and obstruction rows are then read through $E$ and $O$. The row
$E$ is the only route by which beta-redex consumption reaches a
subject-reduction consumer, and $O$ records why this finite route is not being
replaced by a closed subject-reduction theorem.

Finally, $H,C,G,N$ transport, replay, package, and name the same finite
surface. The carrier has no coordinate for a beta-only shortcut, hidden
substitution oracle, or off-packet consumer path, so the six obligations
exhaust the local certificate boundary.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCICRedexFrontier beta-boundary routing]
\label{thm:metacic-redex-frontier-beta-boundary}
Every accepted $\MetaCICRedexFrontierUp$ carrier routes beta-redex consumption
through
$$
B \to E \to C
$$
before a subject-reduction consumer may read the packet. The remaining
discharge rows $A,L,P$ travel with the same carrier, but none of them replaces
the beta row $B$ or the beta-boundary route $E$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier. The beta-redex row is $B$, the beta-boundary route row is
$E$, and the consumer replay row is $C$.

Use the obligation theorem for the same packet. It makes four-row discharge
coverage and beta-boundary routing simultaneous requirements of the local
$\NameCert_{\MetaCICRedexFrontierUp}$ surface.

The rows $A,L,P$ remain necessary discharge rows, but their branch content is
application-argument, lambda-domain, and Pi-domain preservation. They do not
contain the beta-substitution premise-conclusion shape carried by $B$.
Therefore the only accepted beta-facing route is the displayed path from $B$
through $E$ to $C$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.MetaCICRedexFrontierUp.MetaCICRedexFrontierCarrier\_beta\_boundary\_route}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCICRedexFrontier obstruction non-escape]
\label{thm:metacic-redex-frontier-nonescape}
No consumer of an accepted $\MetaCICRedexFrontierUp$ carrier can extract a
closed MetaCIC subject-reduction theorem, a hidden substitution oracle, or a
three-row subject-reduction interface from the packet. Every consumer read
factors through the displayed rows
$$
B,A,L,P,O,E,H,C,G,N.
$$
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.MetaCICRedexFrontierUp.MetaCICRedexFrontierCarrier\_obstruction\_nonescape}
\begin{proof}
Read the carrier definition. It exposes the four discharge rows, the
obstruction ledger, the beta-boundary route, transport, replay, provenance, and
local naming. It lists no closed theorem coordinate and no hidden oracle
coordinate.

Apply the NameCert obligations. The obstruction row $O$ keeps the packet tied
to the finite discharge route described by
\autoref{ch:metacic-open-problems}, rather than to a closed
subject-reduction theorem. The beta-boundary row $E$ also prevents a consumer
from reading the beta case without the named route.

The remaining structural rows $H,C,G,N$ preserve the same packet. Since the
classifier requires all four discharge rows $B,A,L,P$, a three-row interface
is rejected rather than completed by transport, continuation replay,
provenance, or local naming.
\end{proof}
