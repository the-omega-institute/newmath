\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\PointedMetricSpaceUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-pointed-metric-space-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bourbaki and Kelley on based metric spaces, the
$\PointedMetricSpaceUp$ packet records a metric carrier together with a named
basepoint route before completion, loop-space, or embedding consumers are
allowed to inspect point-level data. It sits over the $\MetricSpaceUp$ carrier
of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, reads names through the
$\StreamNameUp$ windows of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, passes regular rational
distance observations through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}
and the $\RealUp$ seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, and
offers a based source for the distance-preserving consumer route of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-isometric-embedding-namecert}. The certificate
names only finite BEDC rows; it does not import host points, quotient metric
equality, selected representatives, or ambient completeness.

\begin{definition}[Pointed metric space carrier]
\label{def:pointed-metric-space-carrier}
A $\PointedMetricSpaceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
P=(M,b,D,S,R,E,H,C,P_0,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the $\MetricSpaceUp$ metric carrier row.
\item $b$ is the basepoint name row, stored as finite BEDC data attached to
$M$ rather than as a host-level point.
\item $D$ is the basepoint distance ledger, recording the finite requests
that compare $b$ with inspected metric names.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window row through which basepoint and metric
names are inspected.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row for the displayed distance
windows.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row for the basepoint-distance observations.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $M,b,D,S,R,E$ and the
local certificate rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a based metric request through
the stream windows, regular rational readback, and real-distance seal.
\item $P_0$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\PointedMetricSpaceUp$,
$\MetricSpaceUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$,
$\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\PointedMetricSpaceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only after transporting the metric
carrier, basepoint name, distance ledger, stream windows, regular readback,
real seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows together.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Pointed metric space NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:pointed-metric-space-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\PointedMetricSpaceUp$ carrier
$P=(M,b,D,S,R,E,H,C,P_0,N)$, the local
$\NameCert_{\PointedMetricSpaceUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following
obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the metric row, basepoint name, basepoint distance
ledger, stream windows, regular rational readback, real-distance seal,
transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item common-carrier classification, requiring every comparison of basepoint
data to be read over the transported metric row $M$;
\item basepoint-distance exposure, requiring every basepoint comparison to
pass through $D,S,R,E$ before any completion-facing or embedding-facing
consumer reads it;
\item package provenance and local naming for exactly the displayed finite
packet.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored in a host point, quotient metric equality, a
selected representative, or an ambient completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:pointed-metric-space-carrier}. The
projection exposes the metric row, basepoint name, distance ledger, stream
windows, regular readback, real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local
name as the only available coordinates.

The classifier route is common-carrier. Componentwise transport in $H$ moves
$M,b,D,S,R,E$ together, so a consumer cannot compare a basepoint name over one
metric carrier with distance observations over another.

The distance ledger fixes the read order. A basepoint comparison is first a
finite request in $D$, then a scheduled stream observation in $S$, then a
regular rational readback in $R$, and only then a $\RealUp$ distance seal in
$E$.

Finally replay $C$ and attach $P_0,N$. Since the carrier has no coordinate for
host points, quotient equality, selected representatives, or ambient
completeness, the listed rows exhaust the local naming surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Pointed metric space basepoint nonescape]
\label{thm:pointed-metric-space-basepoint-nonescape}
Let $P=(M,b,D,S,R,E,H,C,P_0,N)$ be an accepted
$\PointedMetricSpaceUp$ carrier. Every completion-facing, loop-space-facing,
or isometric-embedding-facing consumer of the basepoint row $b$ must read it
through the displayed route
$$
M,b,D \longmapsto S \longmapsto R \longmapsto E \longmapsto H,C,P_0,N .
$$
Thus the basepoint is exported only as finite named metric data over $M$, not
as a host point, quotient representative, selected completion point, or
ambient metric-equality witness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with carrier admission. The only row naming the basepoint is $b$, and it
is stored beside the metric carrier $M$ and distance ledger $D$.

Read the inspection route next. The stream windows $S$ and regular rational
readback $R$ display every observed distance from the basepoint before the
$\RealUp$ seal $E$ is available to downstream consumers.

Transport and replay preserve that same route. The rows $H,C,P_0,N$ move,
replay, source, and name the displayed packet; they do not add host points,
quotient representatives, selected completion points, or ambient metric
equality.

Therefore every based consumer receives exactly the finite route displayed in
the statement.
\end{proof}
