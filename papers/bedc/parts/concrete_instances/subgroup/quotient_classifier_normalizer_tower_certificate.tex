\begin{theorem}[Centralizer normalizer SubgroupUp certificate]
\label{thm:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-subgroup-certificate}
For a BEDC history-level $\GroupUp$ operation over $\mathsf{BHist}$ satisfying
the same associativity, unit, multiplication-congruence, and two-sided inverse
laws as in \autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-certificate-target}, the carrier
$\mathsf{Norm}_{Z_a}$ of
\autoref{def:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-witness} supplies the concrete
$\SubgroupUp$ certificate rows for the normalizer of $Z_a$:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $\mathsf{Norm}_{Z_a}(e)$.
\item If $\mathsf{Norm}_{Z_a}(s)$ and $\mathsf{Norm}_{Z_a}(t)$, then
$\mathsf{Norm}_{Z_a}(\mathsf{mul}(s,t))$.
\item If $\mathsf{Norm}_{Z_a}(t)$, then
$\mathsf{Norm}_{Z_a}(\mathsf{inv}(t))$.
\item If $\mathsf{Norm}_{Z_a}(s)$ and $s\hsame t$, then
$\mathsf{Norm}_{Z_a}(t)$.
\item If $Z_a(t)$, then $\mathsf{Norm}_{Z_a}(t)$.
\end{enumerate}
Thus the two-sided normalizer carrier has identity membership, product closure,
inverse closure, $\hsame$-transport, and the inclusion row from the centralizer.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For identity membership, unfold
\autoref{def:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-witness}. The unit laws make
conjugation by $e$ act as the identity on every carried $x$, and the two-sided
inverse laws identify $\mathsf{inv}(e)$ with $e$. Carrier transport from
\autoref{thm:group-centralizer-subgroup} returns both clauses.

For product closure, assume $\mathsf{Norm}_{Z_a}(s)$ and
$\mathsf{Norm}_{Z_a}(t)$, reading these as expanded $N_a$ witnesses. Apply
\autoref{thm:group-normalizer-conjugation-action-composition}: its forward
composition row sends $Z_a(x)$ first through the $t$ clause and then through the
$s$ clause, while its inverse-action row sends $Z_a(x)$ first through the
inverse clause for $s$ and then through the inverse clause for $t$. These are
the two clauses of $\mathsf{Norm}_{Z_a}(\mathsf{mul}(s,t))$.

For inverse closure, the first clause of
$\mathsf{Norm}_{Z_a}(\mathsf{inv}(t))$ is exactly the inverse-conjugation half
of $\mathsf{Norm}_{Z_a}(t)$. Its second clause conjugates by
$\mathsf{inv}(\mathsf{inv}(t))$; the two-sided inverse laws identify this word
with the original conjugation by $t$, and multiplication congruence plus
carrier transport move the first clause across the comparison.

For $\hsame$-transport, assume $\mathsf{Norm}_{Z_a}(s)$ and $s\hsame t$.
Multiplication congruence transports the forward conjugation word with $s$ to
the corresponding word with $t$, and inverse congruence transports the inverse
word. Carrier transport from \autoref{thm:group-centralizer-subgroup} therefore
gives both clauses of $\mathsf{Norm}_{Z_a}(t)$.

The inclusion row is exactly
\autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-self-normalizer}: every $Z_a(t)$ supplies both
normalizer clauses. Repacking the five rows with the restricted $\hsame$
classifier gives the advertised concrete $\SubgroupUp$ certificate for the
normalizer carrier.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SubgroupUp.SubgroupCentralizerNormalizer\_certificate\_from\_empty\_unit}

Per-object template: traditional target normalizer subgroup certificate; BEDC
source $\GroupUp$ over $\mathsf{BHist}$ plus centralizer $\SubgroupUp$; carrier
$\mathsf{Norm}_{Z_a}$; classifier restricted $\hsame$; stability identity,
product, inverse, and carrier transport; ledger unit conjugation, product
conjugation composition, inverse conjugation, and endpoint classifier witnesses;
core theorem list \autoref{thm:group-normalizer-conjugation-action-composition},
\autoref{thm:group-centralizer-subgroup}, and
\autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-self-normalizer}; exactness predicate
two-sided preservation of $Z_a$ under conjugation; standard bridge ordinary

\begin{theorem}[Centralizer normalizer hsame transport]
\label{thm:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-hsame-transport}
Under the same BEDC history-level $\GroupUp$ laws, if $N_a(s)$ and
$s\hsame t$, then $N_a(t)$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For every centralizer input $x$, the normalizer witness for $s$ gives carried
membership of the conjugation word
$\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(s,x),\mathsf{inv}(s))$. Multiplication congruence
and inverse congruence transport this word to
$\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(t,x),\mathsf{inv}(t))$. The centralizer
$\hsame$-transport row from the $\SubgroupUp$ certificate for $Z_a$ gives the
required carried membership for $t$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Centralizer normalizer SubgroupUp tower certificate]
\label{thm:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-tower-certificate}
For a BEDC history-level $\GroupUp$ operation over $\mathsf{BHist}$ satisfying
the same associativity, unit, multiplication-congruence, and two-sided inverse
laws as in \autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-certificate-target}, let $Z_a$
be the centralizer carrier of \autoref{def:group-centralizer-carrier} and let
$N_a$ be the centralizer normalizer carrier of
\autoref{def:group-centralizer-normalizer-carrier}. The pair $Z_a\leq N_a$
forms a quotient-ready $\SubgroupUp$ tower:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $Z_a$ satisfies the identity, product, inverse, $\hsame$-transport, and
commutator-fiber rows of the centralizer $\SubgroupUp$ certificate.
\item $N_a$ satisfies the identity, product, inverse, and $\hsame$-transport
subgroup rows over the same ambient classifier.
\item Every carried centralizer element is carried by the normalizer: if
$Z_a(t)$, then $N_a(t)$.
\item If $N_a(s)$ and $Z_a(x)$, then
$Z_a(\mathsf{Conj}_s(x))$ and
$Z_a(\mathsf{Conj}_{\mathsf{inv}(s)}(x))$.
\item The two conjugation-closure rows are stable under the ambient
classifier: if $\hsame(s,t)$, then the corresponding forward and inverse
conjugation words define the same carried $Z_a$ membership after transport.
\end{enumerate}
Equivalently, the ten-field $\SubgroupUp$ certificate for $Z_a$, the
four-field subgroup certificate for $N_a$, the inclusion $Z_a\subseteq N_a$,
the two conjugation rows, and their classifier stability are packaged as the
normal-subgroup input used by quotient-group construction.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The $Z_a$ certificate is exactly
\autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-certificate-target}. It supplies the
identity carrier, product closure, inverse closure, $\hsame$ transport, and
commutator-fiber exactness rows for the centralizer object viewed as a
$\SubgroupUp$ instance.

The $N_a$ subgroup rows come from
\autoref{thm:group-centralizer-normalizer-subgroup}. Its identity, product,
inverse, and $\hsame$-transport clauses give the normalizer carrier the same
ambient operation, unit, inverse, and classifier interface required for the
upper subgroup object.

The inclusion row has two equivalent sources. The fifth row of
\autoref{thm:group-centralizer-normalizer-subgroup} states directly that
$Z_a(t)$ implies $N_a(t)$, while
\autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-self-normalizer} gives the same witness by
showing that every centralizer element preserves $Z_a$ under conjugation.

Finally,
\autoref{thm:group-centralizer-normal-in-normalizer} supplies the forward
conjugation row, the inverse-conjugation row, and the classifier transport of
those rows. Repacking these data in the order displayed above gives the
quotient-ready tower certificate without introducing any host quotient or
external parameter transport.
\end{proof}

Per-object template: traditional target normal centralizer subgroup inside
its normalizer for quotient groups; BEDC source $\GroupUp$ over
$\mathsf{BHist}$ plus $\SubgroupUp$; lower carrier $Z_a$; upper carrier
$N_a$; classifier $\hsame$; stability identity, product, inverse, classifier
transport, inclusion, forward conjugation, inverse conjugation, and
conjugation-word transport; ledger centralizing element, normalizing element,
acted element, inverse normalizer witness, and carried conjugation witnesses;
core theorem list \autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-certificate-target},
\autoref{thm:group-centralizer-normalizer-subgroup},
\autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-self-normalizer}, and
\autoref{thm:group-centralizer-normal-in-normalizer}; exactness predicate
normalizer action on the centralizer; standard bridge normal-subgroup input
for quotient groups; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.

\begin{theorem}[Subgroup normalizer orbit quotient classifier]
\label{thm:subgroup-normalizer-orbit-quotient-classifier}
For a BEDC history-level $\GroupUp$ operation over $\mathsf{BHist}$ satisfying
the BEDC group laws used by
\autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-tower-certificate} and
\autoref{thm:group-centralizer-normalizer-orbit-equivalence}, including
associativity, unit, multiplication-congruence, inverse-congruence, and
two-sided inverse laws, let $Z_a$ be the centralizer carrier, let $N_a$ be the
centralizer normalizer carrier, let
$\mathsf{Conj}_s(x):=\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(s,x),\mathsf{inv}(s))$, and let
$x\sim_{N_a}y$ be the normalizer-orbit relation of
\autoref{def:group-centralizer-normalizer-orbit-relation}. For
$x,y,z:\mathsf{BHist}$ with $Z_a(x)$, $Z_a(y)$, and $Z_a(z)$, the data of
\autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-tower-certificate} together with
\autoref{thm:group-centralizer-normalizer-orbit-equivalence} supply the
quotient-free classifier input for $\QuotientGroupUp$:
\begin{enumerate}
\item the lower $\SubgroupUp$ certificate rows for $Z_a$;
\item the upper subgroup rows for $N_a$ over the same history operation;
\item the inclusion row $Z_a\subseteq N_a$;
\item the forward and inverse conjugation stability of $Z_a$ under histories
carried by $N_a$;
\item reflexivity, symmetry, and transitivity of $\sim_{N_a}$ on carried
histories of $Z_a$.
\end{enumerate}
Thus the normalizer action on the centralizer carries exactly the
quotient-free orbit classifier required before any quotient-group carrier is
introduced.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply
\autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-tower-certificate}. Its displayed
rows give the lower certificate for $Z_a$, the upper subgroup rows for $N_a$,
the inclusion $Z_a\subseteq N_a$, and the two conjugation-closure rows for
$\mathsf{Conj}_s$ and $\mathsf{Conj}_{\mathsf{inv}(s)}$.

Next use
\autoref{def:group-centralizer-normalizer-orbit-relation} to read
$\sim_{N_a}$ as reachability by a carried normalizer history with an $\hsame$
endpoint comparison. This keeps the classifier entirely at the history level.

Finally apply
\autoref{thm:group-centralizer-normalizer-orbit-equivalence} to the carried
endpoints $x$, $y$, and $z$. Its three laws give reflexivity, symmetry, and
transitivity for the same relation $\sim_{N_a}$ on $Z_a$, so the tower rows and
the orbit laws combine into the advertised $\QuotientGroupUp$ classifier input.
\end{proof}

Per-object template: traditional target normalizer-orbit quotient classifier;
BEDC source $\GroupUp$ plus $\SubgroupUp$ tower; carrier $Z_a$; classifier
$\sim_{N_a}$; stability equivalence plus conjugation closure; ledger normalizer
witness and conjugation endpoint; exactness quotient-free orbit reachability;
standard bridge ordinary normal-subgroup orbit quotient input; status
$\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.

\begin{definition}[Abstract two-subgroup intersection carrier]
\label{def:subgroup-abstract-two-intersection-carrier}
Fix a BEDC history-level $\GroupUp$ source over $\mathsf{BHist}$ with operation $\mathsf{mul}$, unit $e$, inverse operation $\mathsf{inv}$, and ambient classifier $\hsame$. Let $H_1,H_2:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be two predicates read as $\SubgroupUp$ carriers over this same source. Define their two-subgroup intersection carrier by
$$
H_{1,2}(x) :\Longleftrightarrow H_1(x)\land H_2(x).
$$
The inherited restricted classifier on this carrier is
$$
R_{1,2}(x,y) :\Longleftrightarrow H_{1,2}(x)\land H_{1,2}(y)\land x\hsame y.
$$
The operation, unit, inverse, and history classifier are inherited from the ambient $\GroupUp$ source.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Two-subgroup intersection SubgroupUp certificate]
\label{thm:subgroup-abstract-two-intersection-certificate}
For a BEDC history-level $\GroupUp$ operation over $\mathsf{BHist}$ with operation $\mathsf{mul}$, unit $e$, inverse operation $\mathsf{inv}$, and ambient classifier $\hsame$, assume the monoid stability rows of \autoref{def:monoid-stability-certificate}, the inverse rows of \autoref{def:group-stability-certificate}, and the history-sameness equivalence of \autoref{thm:history-sameness-equivalence}. Let $H_1,H_2:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be two carrier predicates, and let $H_{1,2}$ and $R_{1,2}$ be the carrier and restricted classifier of \autoref{def:subgroup-abstract-two-intersection-carrier}. Assume that each $H_i$, for $i\in\{1,2\}$, satisfies the ambient $\SubgroupUp$ carrier rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $H_i(e)$.
\item If $H_i(x)$ and $H_i(y)$, then $H_i(\mathsf{mul}(x,y))$.
\item If $H_i(x)$, then $H_i(\mathsf{inv}(x))$.
\item If $H_i(x)$ and $x\hsame y$, then $H_i(y)$.
\end{enumerate}
Then the carrier $H_{1,2}$ with classifier $R_{1,2}$ satisfies the concrete $\SubgroupUp$ certificate rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $R_{1,2}$ is a semantic naming classifier on $H_{1,2}$.
\item $H_{1,2}(e)$.
\item If $H_{1,2}(x)$ and $H_{1,2}(y)$, then $H_{1,2}(\mathsf{mul}(x,y))$.
\item If $H_{1,2}(x)$, then $H_{1,2}(\mathsf{inv}(x))$.
\item If $H_{1,2}(x)$ and $x\hsame y$, then $H_{1,2}(y)$.
\item If $R_{1,2}(x,x')$ and $R_{1,2}(y,y')$, then
$$
R_{1,2}(\mathsf{mul}(x,y),\mathsf{mul}(x',y')).
$$
\item If $R_{1,2}(x,y)$, then
$$
R_{1,2}(\mathsf{inv}(x),\mathsf{inv}(y)).
$$
\end{enumerate}
Thus the intersection of two carried $\SubgroupUp$ predicates over the same ambient $\GroupUp$ source is again a concrete $\SubgroupUp$ certificate target over the restricted $\hsame$ classifier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First verify the hypotheses of \autoref{thm:subgroup-one-step-criterion} for the predicate $P=H_{1,2}$. The inhabitance row is $H_{1,2}(e)$, obtained by pairing the two assumed unit rows $H_1(e)$ and $H_2(e)$.

For carrier stability, assume $H_{1,2}(x)$ and $x\hsame y$. Unfolding $H_{1,2}(x)$ gives $H_1(x)$ and $H_2(x)$. The transport row for $H_1$ gives $H_1(y)$, and the transport row for $H_2$ gives $H_2(y)$. Pairing these conclusions gives $H_{1,2}(y)$.

For one-step relative-product closure, assume $H_{1,2}(x)$ and $H_{1,2}(y)$. Unfolding gives $H_i(x)$ and $H_i(y)$ for each $i\in\{1,2\}$. The inverse row for $H_i$ gives $H_i(\mathsf{inv}(y))$, and then the product row for $H_i$ gives
$$
H_i(\mathsf{mul}(x,\mathsf{inv}(y))).
$$
Doing this for $i=1$ and $i=2$ and pairing the two results proves
$$
H_{1,2}(\mathsf{mul}(x,\mathsf{inv}(y))).
$$
Therefore \autoref{thm:subgroup-one-step-criterion} applies to $H_{1,2}$ and yields the identity, product, inverse, and carrier-transport rows listed above.

It remains to record the restricted-classifier rows. For semantic naming, unfold $R_{1,2}$. If $H_{1,2}(x)$, reflexivity of $\hsame$ from \autoref{thm:history-sameness-equivalence} gives $x\hsame x$, so $R_{1,2}(x,x)$ holds. If $R_{1,2}(x,y)$ holds, keep the two endpoint carrier witnesses and reverse the stored $\hsame$ comparison by symmetry, giving $R_{1,2}(y,x)$. If $R_{1,2}(x,y)$ and $R_{1,2}(y,z)$ hold, keep the carried endpoints $H_{1,2}(x)$ and $H_{1,2}(z)$ and compose the stored comparisons by transitivity of $\hsame$, giving $R_{1,2}(x,z)$. Hence $R_{1,2}$ is a semantic naming classifier on the intersection carrier.

For multiplication compatibility, assume $R_{1,2}(x,x')$ and $R_{1,2}(y,y')$. The already proved product row for $H_{1,2}$ supplies the endpoint witnesses
$$
H_{1,2}(\mathsf{mul}(x,y))
$$
and
$$
H_{1,2}(\mathsf{mul}(x',y')).
$$
The multiplication congruence row from \autoref{def:monoid-stability-certificate} transports the stored comparisons $x\hsame x'$ and $y\hsame y'$ to
$$
\mathsf{mul}(x,y)\hsame\mathsf{mul}(x',y').
$$
These three fields are exactly $R_{1,2}(\mathsf{mul}(x,y),\mathsf{mul}(x',y'))$.

For inverse compatibility, assume $R_{1,2}(x,y)$. The already proved inverse row supplies $H_{1,2}(\mathsf{inv}(x))$ and $H_{1,2}(\mathsf{inv}(y))$. The ambient inverse congruence theorem \autoref{thm:group-inverse-congruence-from-laws} transports the stored comparison $x\hsame y$ to
$$
\mathsf{inv}(x)\hsame\mathsf{inv}(y).
$$
Repacking gives $R_{1,2}(\mathsf{inv}(x),\mathsf{inv}(y))$.

The carrier, classifier, operation, unit, and inverse rows are therefore the concrete $\SubgroupUp$ row format used in \autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-certificate-target} and \autoref{thm:subgroup-trivial-certificate}.
\end{proof}
