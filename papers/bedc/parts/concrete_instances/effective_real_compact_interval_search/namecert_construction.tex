Following Bishop--Bridges constructive analysis, the $\EffectiveRealCompactIntervalSearchUp$ packet records the finite search surface by which a continuous real-valued map on a displayed closed bounded interval is inspected through compact finite nets, uniform-modulus data, and interval bisection rows before a downstream extremum consumer reads a certified bracket. It sits over the real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the interval endpoint carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closedboundedinterval-namecert}, the compact-net selector of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactnetmodulusselector-namecert}, the graph and pointwise rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuousmap-namecert}, and the tolerance handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformmodulus-namecert}. The packet gives the constructive extreme-value route a finite searchable carrier; it does not assert classical Heine--Borel compactness, choose a host maximum, or identify quotient real functions.

The search packet consumes the compact interval finite-net route before it exports bisection or extreme-value handoffs. Its selector row $P$ is read downstream of the closed-bounded interval net of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closedboundedintervalnet-namecert} and the finite epsilon-net surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finite-epsilon-net-namecert}; the bisection ledger then refines those displayed cells instead of introducing an open-cover compactness argument or a selected supremum.

\begin{definition}[Effective real compact-interval search carrier]
\label{def:effective-real-compact-interval-search-carrier}
An $\EffectiveRealCompactIntervalSearchUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(I,F,U,P,B,V,H,C,Q,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $I$ is the $\ClosedBoundedIntervalUp$ endpoint and containment row whose endpoints are read through $\RealUp$ names.
\item $F$ is the $\ContinuousMapUp$ graph row for a real-valued map over the interval source named by $I$.
\item $U$ is the $\UniformModulusUp$ tolerance handoff for $F$ on the displayed interval source.
\item $P$ is the $\CompactNetModulusSelectorUp$ finite-net row that lists the rational mesh cells and selected centers used by the search.
\item $B$ is the bisection ledger, a finite prefix tree of interval halves whose cells refine $P$ at the requested tolerance.
\item $V$ is the value-comparison ledger storing the displayed $\RealUp$ value rows and rational error budgets attached to the cells of $B$.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the interval, continuous-map, uniform-modulus, selector, bisection, value-comparison, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ stores displayed $\Cont$ replay from an extremum-search request through $I,F,U,P,B,V,H,Q,N$.
\item $Q$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\EffectiveRealCompactIntervalSearchUp$, $\RealUp$, $\ClosedBoundedIntervalUp$, $\CompactNetModulusSelectorUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\UniformModulusUp$, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\EffectiveRealCompactIntervalSearchUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted search carriers only through $I,F,U,P,B,V,H,C,Q,N$. No open-cover compactness theorem, selected host maximum, excluded-middle comparison on all real values, quotient function equality, or ambient completeness coordinate is part of the carrier.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Effective real compact-interval search obligations]
\label{thm:effective-real-compact-interval-search-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\EffectiveRealCompactIntervalSearchUp$ carrier $S=(I,F,U,P,B,V,H,C,Q,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\EffectiveRealCompactIntervalSearchUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the closed bounded interval source, continuous-map graph, uniform-modulus handoff, compact-net selector, bisection ledger, value-comparison ledger, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming;
\item source agreement, requiring $I$, $F$, $U$, and $P$ to name the same displayed interval source before any cell is searched;
\item selector exactness, requiring the search cells in $B$ to refine the finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ centers and tolerances exposed by $P$;
\item value-row binding, requiring every value comparison in $V$ to be attached to a cell of $B$, a displayed graph row of $F$, and the error budget supplied by $U$;
\item bisection stability, requiring interval refinement to preserve endpoint containment and the selected candidate cell under componentwise $\hsame$ transport;
\item extremum handoff, requiring any lower-bound, upper-bound, near-minimum, or near-maximum read to pass through $B,V,H,C,Q,N$ rather than through a host maximum selector;
\item non-escape, excluding open-cover compactness, classical Heine--Borel selection, trichotomy on arbitrary real values, quotient function equality, and ambient $\RealUp$ completeness from the local packet.
\end{enumerate}
No effective compact-interval search obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:effective-real-compact-interval-search-carrier}. The interval source is $I$, the continuous graph is $F$, the uniform tolerance handoff is $U$, and the finite selector row is $P$.

The search may begin only after these rows agree on the same displayed interval source. The bisection ledger $B$ then refines the selector cells supplied by $P$, so each later comparison is attached to a visible interval cell rather than to an external point choice.

The value ledger $V$ reads graph values from $F$ with the error budget delivered by $U$. This makes every candidate comparison a finite statement about named cells and rational tolerances.

Finally the extremum-facing read uses $H,C,Q,N$ to replay and name the same route. Since the carrier has no coordinate for open-cover compactness, a host maximum, global real trichotomy, quotient function equality, or ambient completeness, none of those objects can enter the local certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Effective real compact-interval bisection induction]
\label{thm:effective-real-compact-interval-search-bisection-induction}
For an accepted $\EffectiveRealCompactIntervalSearchUp$ carrier $S=(I,F,U,P,B,V,H,C,Q,N)$, every finite bisection stage in $B$ is obtained by induction from the endpoint row $I$ and the selector row $P$. The induction step replaces one displayed interval cell by its two named halves, transports the corresponding $\ContinuousMapUp$ and $\UniformModulusUp$ reads through $H$, and extends $V$ only by value rows attached to those halves.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The base stage is the interval cell named by $I$ together with the first finite-net read in $P$. Its value ledger is the corresponding displayed graph read from $F$ with the tolerance supplied by $U$.

For the induction step, choose the cell already selected by the finite ledger. The midpoint and half-interval endpoints are read as interval rows below $I$, and the selector exactness obligation identifies their centers with the relevant $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ entries of $P$.

Transport along $H$ keeps the continuous-map graph and uniform-modulus handoff on the same source. The new value rows are therefore added to $V$ only for the two displayed halves, so the induction never leaves the finite bisection tree.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Effective real compact-interval extreme-value handoff]
\label{thm:effective-real-compact-interval-search-extreme-value-handoff}
Every extremum-search consumer reading an accepted $\EffectiveRealCompactIntervalSearchUp$ carrier factors through
$$
I,F,U,P \longmapsto B,V \longmapsto H,C,Q,N .
$$
The consumer receives a finite bracket or near-extremal cell family whose error budget is supplied by $\UniformModulusUp$ and whose candidates are selected by $\CompactNetModulusSelectorUp$. It receives no classical maximum point, open-cover compactness proof, quotient equality of functions, global trichotomy on $\RealUp$ values, or ambient completeness assertion.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the carrier projection. The only source data available to the consumer are the interval row $I$, continuous graph row $F$, uniform-modulus row $U$, and compact-net selector row $P$.

Apply \autoref{thm:effective-real-compact-interval-search-bisection-induction}. Every searched cell and every candidate value is produced inside the finite bisection ledger $B$ and value ledger $V$.

The final handoff reads those ledgers through $H,C,Q,N$. Applying \autoref{thm:effective-real-compact-interval-search-namecert-obligations}, any route that asks for a host maximum, open-cover compactness, quotient function equality, global real trichotomy, or ambient completeness is outside the accepted packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}

Apply \autoref{thm:effective-real-compact-interval-search-bisection-induction}. The bisection row $B$ refines the finite cells supplied by $P$, and every value comparison in $V$ is attached to a displayed graph row of $F$ with the tolerance budget from $U$.

Apply \autoref{thm:effective-real-compact-interval-search-extreme-value-handoff}. The final route reads $B,V,H,C,Q,N$ after the finite-net source rows, so the exported extreme-value handoff is a finite search certificate rather than an open-cover compactness, supremum-selection, trichotomy, quotient-equality, or completeness principle.
