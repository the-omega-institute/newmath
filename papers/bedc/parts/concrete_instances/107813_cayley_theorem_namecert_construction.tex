\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CayleyTheoremUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-cayley-theorem-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Cayley, the $\CayleyTheoremUp$ packet records the finite left-regular
action of a displayed group row as a permutation-action row. It consumes the
finite $\GroupUp$ source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-group-namecert} and
the finite-position bijection surface of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-permutation-namecert}. The packet names the
carrier, action, image, faithfulness, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and
$\NameCert$ rows needed to read the theorem as BEDC data; it does not assert a
classification of finite groups, a presentation by generators and relations,
or a representation-theoretic decomposition.

\begin{definition}[Cayley theorem carrier]
\label{def:cayley-theorem-carrier}
A $\CayleyTheoremUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
C=(G,L,I,F,H,T,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $G$ is the consumed finite $\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$ row, including the
carrier, multiplication, unit, inverse, and finite support surfaces;
\item $L$ is the left-regular action row sending a displayed group element $g$
and a displayed group endpoint $x$ to the product endpoint $gx$;
\item $I$ is the permutation-image row, reading each left-translation row as a
certified self-bijection of the finite carrier of $G$ through the
$\PermutationUp$ interface;
\item $F$ is the faithfulness row, recording that if two left-translation
rows have the same action on every displayed endpoint, then their source group
rows classify together by the $\GroupUp$ classifier;
\item $H$ is the componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $G,L,I,F$;
\item $T$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ routes through which consumers read the
left action, permutation image, and faithfulness rows;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\GroupUp$, $\PermutationUp$, $\BHist$,
$\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CayleyTheoremUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no coordinate for a host embedding into an ambient
symmetric group, no quotient by an external equality of functions, and no
representation category.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cayley theorem NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cayley-theorem-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CayleyTheoremUp$ carrier
$$
C=(G,L,I,F,H,T,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\CayleyTheoremUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier
admission for the displayed finite group row, left-regular action row,
permutation-image row, faithfulness row, componentwise transport row,
continuation routes, provenance, and local naming row. The obligation route is
finite group row, left-regular action row, permutation image row, faithfulness
row, and then the $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ packaging rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:cayley-theorem-carrier}. Carrier admission
is the displayed tuple $G,L,I,F,H,T,P,N$, so no host symmetric group, host
function equality, or representation category is available as an additional
coordinate.

Read $L$ from the multiplication row of $G$. For each displayed source row
$g$, left multiplication by $g$ sends every displayed endpoint $x$ to the
endpoint $gx$ and sends the inverse endpoint through the inverse row of $G$,
so the action has the inverse-witness data needed by the permutation surface.

Read $I$ through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-permutation-namecert}. The
finite support row of $G$ supplies the finite spine, while the left-action and
inverse rows supply the forward graph, inverse graph, inverse witnesses, and
action ledger of the carried $\PermutationUp$ row.

Finally read $F$ at the unit endpoint of $G$. If two left-translation rows act
the same on every displayed endpoint, their actions on the unit endpoint
classify the two source group rows together. Repacking this faithfulness row
with $H,T,P,N$ gives the stated local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cayley theorem permutation-action handoff]
\label{thm:cayley-theorem-permutation-action-handoff}
Every downstream consumer of a $\CayleyTheoremUp$ carrier that asks for a
permutation action of a finite group reads exactly the rows
$$
G,L,I,F,H,T,P,N
$$
of \autoref{def:cayley-theorem-carrier}. The handoff exports the consumed
$\GroupUp$ row, the left-regular action row, the $\PermutationUp$ image row,
and the faithfulness row, and then transports them through the displayed
$\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows. It does not export a bare host
function, a separate symmetric-group carrier, or any representation-theoretic
decomposition.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply \autoref{thm:cayley-theorem-namecert-obligations}. It identifies
the public packet with the finite group row, left-regular action row,
permutation-image row, faithfulness row, and the transport and provenance rows.

Next project the permutation-image coordinate through
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-permutation-namecert}. Each left-translation
row is read as a certified finite-position self-bijection; the action row is
the corresponding endpoint action, and inverse coverage is supplied by the
inverse row of the consumed group packet.

Finally use the faithfulness row. The action on the unit endpoint recovers the
source group classifier, so the consumer can read the group-to-permutation
map as faithful without importing host function equality. Since every public
field has already been listed in $G,L,I,F,H,T,P,N$, the handoff has exactly the
displayed BEDC surface and no additional symmetric-group or representation
coordinate.
\end{proof}
