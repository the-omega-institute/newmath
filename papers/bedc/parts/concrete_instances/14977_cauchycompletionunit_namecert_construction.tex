\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CauchyCompletionUnitUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-cauchycompletionunit-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges constructive completion, and the Kelley-style unit map from a metric source into its Cauchy completion, the $\CauchyCompletionUnitUp$ packet records the finite BEDC certificate for sending source data to the completion-facing surface. It sits over the finite stream-window route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, and the metric-completion boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metriccompletion-namecert}. The packet is the unit-map handoff from displayed source rows into completion consumers; it is not a quotient completion, not a selected representative, not countable choice, not open-cover compactness, and not a completeness theorem for $\RealUp$.

\begin{definition}[CauchyCompletionUnit carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-completion-unit-carrier}
A $\CauchyCompletionUnitUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
U=(X,S,D,R,E,M,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the source row whose displayed point, metric, or regular-Cauchy data are to be admitted into a completion-facing consumer.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row obtained by reading the source data as a constant stream or as an already displayed finite window schedule.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger used to answer finite precision requests before any completion handoff is accepted.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row produced from $S$ and $D$.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row available only after the displayed route through $S,D,R$ has been admitted.
\item $M$ is the $\MetricCompletionUp$ target row that consumes the source-side route as the unit image.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the source, stream-window, dyadic ledger, regular readback, real seal, metric-completion target, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from source admission through constant-window generation, tolerance readback, real sealing, and completion-unit handoff.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyCompletionUnitUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\MetricCompletionUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyCompletionUnitUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for quotient completion equality, selected representatives, countable-choice schedules, open-cover compactness, ambient completeness, or completeness of $\RealUp$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[CauchyCompletionUnit classifier]
\label{def:cauchy-completion-unit-classifier}
The classifier of two accepted $\CauchyCompletionUnitUp$ packets relates them exactly when their source rows agree after the displayed $\hsame$ transport, their finite stream windows are the same constant-window or scheduled-window reads, their dyadic tolerance ledgers and regular readbacks agree, and their completion target rows consume the same routed unit image under the stored $\Cont$ replay.

Equivalently, the classifier is the finite route
$$
X\longmapsto S\longmapsto D\longmapsto R\longmapsto E\longmapsto M\longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
It is not equality of quotient classes, not host equality of completed points, and not a choice of a canonical Cauchy representative.
\end{definition}
\leandef{BEDC.Derived.CauchyCompletionUnitUp.CauchyCompletionUnitClassifier}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyCompletionUnit NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-unit-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\CauchyCompletionUnitUp$ carrier $U=(X,S,D,R,E,M,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyCompletionUnitUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed source, stream-window, dyadic ledger, regular readback, real seal, metric-completion target, componentwise transport, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item unit-window generation, requiring each source read to enter the completion route through the constant stream or finite scheduled window stored in $S$;
\item tolerance and readback exactness, requiring every precision request to pass through $D$ and then $R$ before the real seal or completion target is consumed;
\item classifier stability, requiring transported packets with the same $X,S,D,R,E,M$ route to be identified by the classifier of \autoref{def:cauchy-completion-unit-classifier};
\item ledger non-escape, stating that no quotient completion equality, selected representative, countable-choice schedule, open-cover compactness principle, ambient completeness theorem, or $\RealUp$ completeness theorem is exported by the packet.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CauchyCompletionUnitUp.CauchyCompletionUnitNameCert\_obligations}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:cauchy-completion-unit-carrier}. The tuple lists exactly the source row, finite-window row, tolerance ledger, readback row, real seal, completion target, transport, replay, provenance, and local name.

Unit-window generation is constructive by induction on the finite request window. The empty window reads no source data, and the extension step appends the same source read to the displayed constant stream or preserves the next scheduled window already stored in $S$.

Tolerance and readback exactness then follow by tuple projection from $D$ and $R$. The continuation row $C$ replays this route before the seal $E$ or completion target $M$ can be inspected.

Classifier stability is componentwise $\hsame$ transport along $H$ followed by the classifier definition. Since the carrier fields are exhausted by $X,S,D,R,E,M,H,C,P,N$, the excluded quotient, choice, compactness, ambient completeness, and real-completeness principles have no row from which a consumer could read them.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyCompletionUnit dense embedding handoff]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-unit-dense-embedding-handoff}
Every dense-embedding or completion-facing consumer of an accepted $\CauchyCompletionUnitUp$ packet factors through the finite unit route
$$
X\longmapsto S\longmapsto D\longmapsto R\longmapsto E\longmapsto M .
$$
The source row is therefore handed to the completion boundary as displayed finite BEDC data, not as a quotient class, not as a selected Cauchy representative, not as host equality, and not as an appeal to countable choice or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CauchyCompletionUnitUp.CauchyCompletionUnitCarrier\_dense\_embedding\_handoff}
\begin{proof}
Start with the source row $X$. A consumer cannot inspect the completion target before the unit route has displayed how the source read becomes a finite stream-window request.

The window row $S$ is generated as a constant stream when the source read is a point input, or read as the existing finite schedule when the source is already presented by a regular-Cauchy name. Induction on the finite window gives the required handoff at each precision request.

The dyadic ledger $D$ and regular readback $R$ answer the request before any Real or completion-facing row is reached. The seal $E$ is terminal for the real-facing part of the route, and $M$ consumes only this displayed route as the unit image.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same packet. No field contains quotient completion equality, a selected representative, host equality, countable choice, or ambient completeness, so the dense-embedding handoff exports only the finite unit route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyCompletionUnit real-completion consumer boundary]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-unit-real-completion-consumer-boundary}
Let $U=(X,S,D,R,E,M,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyCompletionUnitUp$ packet. Any $\RealUp$ or $\MetricCompletionUp$ consumer of $U$ must read the source through $S$, account for tolerance by $D$, obtain regular rational readback by $R$, and then consume the terminal seal $E$ or the completion target $M$ through the same replay row $C$.

Thus the consumer boundary is the displayed finite route $S,D,R,E,M$ over $X$, with componentwise transport by $H$ and provenance by $P,N$. It does not close quotient completion equality, selected representatives, countable choice, open-cover compactness, ambient completeness, or completeness of $\RealUp$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CauchyCompletionUnitUp.CauchyCompletionUnitCarrier\_real\_completion\_consumer\_boundary}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet and read the consumer-facing rows. The source row $X$ is available to $\RealUp$ and $\MetricCompletionUp$ consumers only through the stream-window row $S$.

Precision requests are then routed through $D$ and $R$. The dyadic ledger records the requested scale, while the regular readback row records the finite rational response supplied by the displayed source windows.

The continuation row $C$ replays this same route to $E$ and $M$. Transport by $H$ preserves the route componentwise, and the package and local name rows $P,N$ record that the consumer is reading the same accepted unit packet.

Since every consumer-facing read has passed through $S,D,R,E,M$ over $X$, and since the carrier has no coordinate for the excluded principles, the packet supplies only the finite real-completion boundary stated above.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyCompletionUnit constant-window induction]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-unit-constant-window-induction}
Let $U=(X,S,D,R,E,M,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyCompletionUnitUp$ packet, and let a consumer request a finite source window from the unit route. Before any $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, or $\MetricCompletionUp$ row can be read, every admitted source row is generated either as the stored constant-window $\StreamNameUp$ row or as the already scheduled finite-window $\StreamNameUp$ row recorded in $S$.

Consequently the visible prefix of every consumer read has the form
$$
X\longmapsto S
$$
before the tolerance ledger, regular readback, real seal, or completion target is available.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CauchyCompletionUnitUp.CauchyCompletionUnitCarrier\_constant\_window\_induction}
\begin{proof}
Induct on the finite request window. For the empty window, no source read is exposed, so the only available consumer prefix is the stored packet projection $X,S$.

For the extension step, assume the claim for a finite window $W$. A further request appends one displayed source read. If $S$ is the constant-window row, the same source row $X$ is appended as the next constant stream entry. If $S$ is a scheduled-window row, the next scheduled entry already belongs to the finite row stored in $S$.

In both cases the appended read is still a $\StreamNameUp$ read. The tuple order in \autoref{def:cauchy-completion-unit-carrier} places $D,R,E,M$ after $S$, and the replay row $C$ records the same order. Thus no dyadic ledger, regular readback, real seal, or completion target can be inspected before the finite stream-window generation step has occurred.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyCompletionUnit classifier stability]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-unit-classifier-stability}
Let $U=(X,S,D,R,E,M,H,C,P,N)$ and $U'=(X',S',D',R',E',M',H',C',P',N')$ be accepted $\CauchyCompletionUnitUp$ packets. If componentwise $\hsame$ transport identifies the displayed source, stream-window, dyadic ledger, regular readback, real seal, completion target, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming rows, then the classifier of \autoref{def:cauchy-completion-unit-classifier} identifies $U$ and $U'$.

The transport uses only the listed tuple components. It cannot introduce quotient completion equality, selected representatives, countable-choice schedules, open-cover compactness, ambient completeness, or completeness of $\RealUp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the two accepted packets componentwise. The hypotheses give $\hsame$ transport for $X$ with $X'$, $S$ with $S'$, $D$ with $D'$, $R$ with $R'$, $E$ with $E'$, $M$ with $M'$, and likewise for $H,C,P,N$.

Apply the classifier definition. It asks exactly that source rows agree after displayed transport, stream windows agree as constant-window or scheduled-window reads, dyadic ledgers and regular readbacks agree, and the completion targets consume the same routed unit image under the stored continuation replay.

No further coordinate remains in the carrier. Since quotient completion equality, selected representatives, countable choice, compactness, ambient completeness, and real completeness are explicitly absent from \autoref{def:cauchy-completion-unit-carrier}, componentwise transport has no row from which any of those principles could be generated.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CauchyCompletionUnitUp.CauchyCompletionUnitCarrier\_classifier\_stability}
