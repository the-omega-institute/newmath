\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\SpecializationOrderUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-specialization-order-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Alexandroff and standard topology, the specialization preorder of a topological space is read through open-neighbourhood implication rather than host point equality. The $\SpecializationOrderUp$ packet sits after the indexed-open topology of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-topology-namecert}, the observable-open truth classifier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-sierpinski-space-namecert}, the membership discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-setlike-namecert}, and the separated order surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-poset-namecert}. It is also the topology-side order interface consumed by the metric-generated topology handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-topology-namecert}. The packet names only finite open-set implication data; it does not import quotient point equality, selected representatives, powerset extensionality, excluded middle over opens, or choice-based separation.

\begin{definition}[SpecializationOrder carrier]
\label{def:specialization-order-carrier}
A $\SpecializationOrderUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(T,O,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $T$ is the topology row, carrying the accepted indexed-open source, finite open-neighbourhood requests, and topology closure witnesses read from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-topology-namecert}.
\item $O$ is the open-set membership classifier, recording for each displayed point and accepted open index whether the point is observed inside that open through the local open-row interface.
\item $R$ is the specialization relation row. A displayed comparison $x \leq_{\mathsf{spec}} y$ is admitted only when every open-membership read true at $x$ in $O$ is replayed as true at $y$ through the same topology row $T$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the topology row, open-set membership classifier, specialization relation row, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from an open-neighbourhood request through $T$ and $O$ into the specialization relation row $R$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\SpecializationOrderUp$, the topology, Sierpinski, SetLike, Poset, metric-topology, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ surfaces.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\SpecializationOrderUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for host point equality, quotient points, selected separated representatives, powerset equality of opens, excluded middle over open membership, or choice-based separation.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[SpecializationOrder NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:specialization-order-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\SpecializationOrderUp$ carrier $K=(T,O,R,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\SpecializationOrderUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the topology row, open-set membership classifier, specialization relation row, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming;
\item classifier transport, requiring $H$ to preserve every open-membership read in $O$ along displayed $\hsame$ moves;
\item reflexivity, read by replaying each open-membership observation for a point back to the same point through $O$;
\item transitivity, read by composing two displayed open-neighbourhood implication rows inside $R$;
\item topology handoff, requiring every public order comparison to factor through the indexed-open row $T$ before it reaches $R$;
\item sibling nonescape, forbidding quotient point equality, host separation, selected representatives, powerset extensionality, excluded middle, and choice data not displayed in $K$;
\item local naming through the provenance row $P$ and the local certificate row $N$.
\end{enumerate}
These rows exhaust the packet obligations. A downstream T0-reflection, formal-topology, metric-topology, or separated-order consumer receives only the displayed open-neighbourhood implication route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:specialization-order-carrier}. The packet exposes only $T,O,R,H,C,P,N$, so each local obligation must be read from the topology row, the open-membership classifier, the specialization relation, or the structural rows.

Classifier transport is componentwise. The $H$ row moves topology indices, point reads, open-membership observations, and specialization comparisons together, so an accepted classifier read cannot detach from the topology row that generated it.

Reflexivity and transitivity are internal to $R$. Reflexivity reuses the same open-membership read at the same point, while transitivity composes the two displayed implication routes through the common open index. No quotient or host equality coordinate is involved.

The topology handoff and sibling nonescape follow from the carrier boundary. Every public order read passes through $T$ and $O$ before $R$, and the rows $P,N$ name only that route. Since quotient points, selected representatives, powerset extensionality, excluded middle, and choice-based separation are absent from the packet, none can be exported as a NameCert obligation.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SpecializationOrder topology handoff]
\label{thm:specialization-order-topology-handoff}
Every accepted $\SpecializationOrderUp$ packet reads its preorder only from open-set implication:
$$
x \leq_{\mathsf{spec}} y
\quad\text{means that every displayed open-neighbourhood read true at }x\text{ is replayed as true at }y.
$$
The handoff exports no quotient point equality, no selected representative, and no choice-based separation principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted carrier $K=(T,O,R,H,C,P,N)$. The topology row $T$ supplies the only accepted open indices, and $O$ supplies the only membership reads for those opens.

The specialization row $R$ is defined as the replay of those membership reads from source point to target point. Thus a preorder comparison can be inspected by checking the displayed implication from each open-neighbourhood observation at $x$ to the corresponding observation at $y$.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ preserve, replay, source, and name that same implication route. The carrier has no coordinate for quotient points, selected representatives, host equality, or choice-based separation, so the topology handoff cannot export any of them.
\end{proof}
