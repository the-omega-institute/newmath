Following Bishop and Bridges on located subsets of metric and real spaces, the $\LocatedSubsetUp$ packet records a displayed subset together with finite distance-witness rows. It sits after the real-valued metric route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-metric-namecert}, the Hausdorff comparison boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-hausdorff-metric-namecert}, the real-separability window surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-separability-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance core of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the regular rational readback surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}. The packet gives Real and Completion consumers a finite located-subset interface without host set extensionality, selected nearest points, quotient subset equality, or ambient completeness.

The located-subset distance route consumes \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-separability-namecert} as its finite search-window source and consumes \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-hausdorff-metric-namecert} only after the real-distance seal has been displayed.

\begin{definition}[LocatedSubset carrier]
\label{def:located-subset-carrier}
A $\LocatedSubsetUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(M,S,W,D,R,E,K,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the $\RealMetricUp$ source row, carrying the ambient metric distance through finite real-name observations.
\item $S$ is the subset-admission row, naming the displayed members read by the carrier and exporting no host powerset equality.
\item $W$ is the $\RealSeparabilityUp$ search-window row, recording the countable dense or finite-window probes available to a distance request.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger attached to each positive distance query.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row, packaging the rational comparison data produced from $W$ and $D$.
\item $E$ is the real distance seal row, read only after the metric source, subset admission, search windows, dyadic tolerances, and regular rational readback have been displayed.
\item $K$ is the optional $\HausdorffMetricUp$ comparison boundary for compact or hyperspace consumers; it records only the finite directed comparisons visible through the preceding rows.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the metric source, subset admission, search windows, dyadic ledger, regular readback, real seal, Hausdorff boundary, replay, provenance, and local naming.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from an ambient point and tolerance request through subset admission, separability windows, dyadic comparison, regular rational readback, real sealing, and any Hausdorff-facing comparison.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LocatedSubsetUp$, $\RealMetricUp$, $\HausdorffMetricUp$, $\RealSeparabilityUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedSubsetUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $M,S,W,D,R,E,K,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for a host subset object, extensional equality of subsets, a selected nearest point, quotient real equality, countable choice, or an ambient completion theorem.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[LocatedSubset NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:located-subset-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\LocatedSubsetUp$ carrier
$$
L=(M,S,W,D,R,E,K,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedSubsetUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission, ambient real-metric visibility, subset-admission stability, separability-window exposure, dyadic-tolerance compatibility, regular-rational readback, real-distance sealing, optional Hausdorff comparison routing, componentwise transport, displayed replay, package provenance, and local naming.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:located-subset-carrier}. The displayed packet lists exactly the rows used by an ambient point, subset-membership, tolerance, rational-readback, real-seal, or Hausdorff-facing request.

The metric and subset rows $M,S$ are read first. A distance query can inspect the ambient space only through $\RealMetricUp$, and it can inspect membership only through the displayed admission row $S$.

The search, tolerance, and readback rows $W,D,R$ then supply finite witnesses for the locatedness request. The real seal $E$ is reached only after those rows have been displayed, while $K$ is available only as a downstream comparison boundary.

The rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite packet. Since the carrier contains no host subset, selected nearest point, quotient equality, countable-choice schedule, or ambient completion coordinate, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LocatedSubset dyadic distance witness]
\label{thm:located-subset-dyadic-distance-witness}
Every distance read exported by an accepted $\LocatedSubsetUp$ packet factors through the finite route
$$
\RealMetricUp \longmapsto \RealSeparabilityUp \longmapsto \DyadicRatCoreUp \longmapsto \RegSeqRatUp \longmapsto E .
$$
The read may be handed to a $\HausdorffMetricUp$ consumer only after the same route has produced the displayed real-distance seal, transported by $H$ and replayed by $C$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a point-to-subset distance request. The ambient metric row $M$ supplies the only metric observations, and the subset-admission row $S$ supplies the only visible membership data.

The finite search window $W$ names the inspected probes. The dyadic ledger $D$ fixes the positive tolerance requests, and the regular rational row $R$ records the resulting readback windows.

The real-distance seal $E$ is therefore downstream of $M,S,W,D,R$. Any Hausdorff-facing row $K$ can compare only the finite distance data already sealed by this route.

Transport and replay do not add another distance object. The componentwise $\hsame$ row $H$, the displayed $\Cont$ route $C$, the $\Pkg$ provenance $P$, and the local $\NameCert$ row $N$ preserve the same witness surface, so no nearest-point selector or host infimum can bypass the dyadic distance route.
\end{proof}
