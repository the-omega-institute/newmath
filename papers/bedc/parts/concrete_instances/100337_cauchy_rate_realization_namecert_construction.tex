\begin{definition}[Cauchy rate realization carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-rate-realization-carrier}

$$

\begin{enumerate}
\item $p$ is the displayed precision-request row whose requested dyadic tolerance must be served.
\item $r$ is the accepted $\CauchyRateUp$ row exposing the effective threshold schedule for the request $p$.
\item $d$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger recording the rational endpoint and radius comparisons read at that threshold.
\item $s$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row opened by the rate row $r$ for the request $p$.
\item $q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row obtained from the displayed stream window and dyadic tolerance ledger.
\item $m$ is the $\RealCauchyModulusUp$ route row showing that the same rate, window, tolerance, and readback data serve the Real-facing modulus request.
\item $e$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after $p,r,d,s,q,m$ have all been displayed.

\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy rate realization NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-rate-realization-namecert-obligations}

$$

Every accepted Real-facing read is classified only by those rows, and no obligation is stored in a quotient stream, selected limit, countable-choice selector, host real equality proof, ambient completeness theorem, or detached seal row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. Its analytic coordinates are exactly $p,r,d,s,q,m,e$, and its structural coordinates are exactly $H,C,P,N$.

Read the analytic rows in dependency order. Since the carrier has no coordinate for quotient streams, selected limits, countable choice, host real equality, ambient completeness, trichotomy, or a detached seal row, none of those objects can become a local NameCert obligation.

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy rate realization Real seal route]
\label{thm:cauchy-rate-realization-real-seal-route}

$$
p\longmapsto r\longmapsto s,d\longmapsto q\longmapsto m\longmapsto e\longmapsto(H,C,P,N).
$$
Thus the seal read is the realization of explicit Cauchy-rate data through finite window, tolerance, readback, and modulus rows, not an appeal to $\RealUp$ completeness, quotient streams, selected limits, countable choice, or host real equality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

The finite observations are then forced through the scheduled window and tolerance rows. The stream row $s$ and dyadic ledger $d$ are the only coordinates from which the regular rational readback $q$ is available.

The modulus route and seal are downstream. The row $m$ records the Real-facing modulus use of the same finite data, and $e$ is reached only after $p,r,s,d,q,m$ have been displayed.

Finally replay the structural rows. The route through $H,C,P,N$ preserves and names the same packet, so no ambient completion theorem, selected host limit, quotient stream, countable-choice row, or host equality proof can substitute for the displayed chain.
