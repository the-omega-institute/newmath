\begin{theorem}[Closed-term substitution boundary source totality]
\label{thm:closed-term-substitution-boundary-source-totality}
Every accepted $\ClosedTermSubstitutionBoundaryUp$ root-unblock read used by $\AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp$, $\BinderBudgetSealUp$, or $\ClosedTermSubstitutionCompilerUp$ must pass through the complete finite source surface
$$
T,V,d,C_{\mathsf{src}},C_{\mathsf{val}},Q,R,L,A,H,C,P,N.
$$
The read exposes the $\BHist$ carrier, componentwise $\hsame$ rows, displayed $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ row for the closed source, closed value, inspected binder depth, shift boundary, substitution boundary, closedness ledger, and audit non-escape row. It exposes no open-term source coordinate, host syntax quotient, unchecked evaluator, or subject-reduction theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier and classifier of \autoref{def:closed-term-substitution-boundary-carrier} and \autoref{def:closed-term-substitution-boundary-classifier}. They list the only rows available to a root-unblock consumer and restrict movement of those rows to componentwise $\hsame$ transport and displayed $\Cont$ replay.

Use the source triad and operation route from \autoref{thm:closed-term-substitution-boundary-source-triad} and \autoref{thm:closed-term-substitution-boundary-operation-route}. These rows force the closed source, closed value, and inspected depth to be fixed before either $Q$ or $R$ is consumed by a generator, binder-budget, or compiler-facing consumer.

Finally apply the audit non-escape surface of \autoref{thm:closed-term-substitution-boundary-audit-nonescape}. Since no open source, host quotient, evaluator, or subject-reduction coordinate is present in the packet, totality of the root-unblock source surface is exactly the displayed finite route.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Closed-term substitution boundary downstream non-escape]
\label{thm:closed-term-substitution-boundary-downstream-nonescape}
Every downstream consumer that combines $\AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp$, $\BinderBudgetSealUp$, and $\ClosedTermSubstitutionCompilerUp$ dependencies receives only the displayed $\ClosedTermSubstitutionBoundaryUp$ route over
$$
T,V,d,C_{\mathsf{src}},C_{\mathsf{val}},Q,R,L,A,H,C,P,N.
$$
The $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows carry the accepted closedness, operation, ledger, and audit data across those dependencies, while the downstream surface exports no unregistered free variable, hidden binder convention, host substitution semantics, host syntax quotient, unchecked compiler correctness theorem, or ambient subject reduction.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Package the source-totality row of \autoref{thm:closed-term-substitution-boundary-source-totality}. It fixes the complete finite source surface before any downstream dependency is allowed to consume the packet.

Then package the recursive readback row of \autoref{thm:closed-term-substitution-boundary-recursive-readback}. The generator branch, binder-budget factorization, and compiler schedule all replay the same closed-boundary rows through displayed transports, continuations, provenance, and local naming data.

Apply the ledger non-escape theorem \autoref{thm:closed-term-substitution-boundary-ledger-non-escape}. The excluded objects have no coordinate in the carrier, and none can be produced by $\hsame$ transport, $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, or a local $\NameCert$ row. Hence the downstream handoff cannot leave the displayed closed-term substitution boundary.
\end{proof}
