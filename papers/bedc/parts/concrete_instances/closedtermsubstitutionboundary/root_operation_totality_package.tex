\begin{definition}[Closed-term substitution boundary root operation surface]
\label{def:closed-term-substitution-boundary-root-operation-surface}
The root operation surface of an accepted $\ClosedTermSubstitutionBoundaryUp$ packet is the finite displayed row list
$$
T,V,d,C_{\mathsf{src}},C_{\mathsf{val}},Q,R,L,A,H,C,P,N.
$$
Here $T$ is the source term row, $V$ is the substituting closed value row, $d$ is the inspected depth row, $C_{\mathsf{src}}$ and $C_{\mathsf{val}}$ are the displayed closedness witnesses, $Q$ is the closed shift boundary row, $R$ is the substitute-closed boundary row, $L$ is the ledger row, $A$ is the audit row, $H$ is the componentwise $\hsame$ transport row, $C$ is the displayed $\Cont$ route row, $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance row, and $N$ is the local $\NameCert$ row.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Closed-term substitution boundary root operation totality]
\label{thm:closed-term-substitution-boundary-root-operation-totality}
For every accepted $\ClosedTermSubstitutionBoundaryUp$ packet, the closed shift row $Q$ and the substitute-closed row $R$ are both exhausted by the root operation surface of \autoref{def:closed-term-substitution-boundary-root-operation-surface}. $$
T,V,d,C_{\mathsf{src}},C_{\mathsf{val}},Q,R,L,A,H,C,P,N.
$$
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet through \autoref{def:closed-term-substitution-boundary-carrier} and \autoref{def:closed-term-substitution-boundary-classifier}. These two rows expose the source, value, depth, closedness witnesses, operation rows, ledger, audit row, transport, route, provenance, and local naming data as one finite packet.

Apply \autoref{thm:closed-term-substitution-boundary-ledger-non-escape} and \autoref{thm:closed-term-substitution-boundary-root-audit-totality}. The ledger blocks escape from the displayed packet, while the root audit row makes every audit-visible operation read pass through $T,V,d,C_{\mathsf{src}},C_{\mathsf{val}},Q,R,L,A,H,C,P,N$.

Finally use \autoref{thm:closed-term-substitution-boundary-root-compiler-nonescape}. Since the compiler-facing read is admitted only after the same closed-boundary packet is visible, both $Q$ and $R$ are total over the displayed root operation surface and nowhere else.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Closed-term substitution boundary root audit compiler coherence]
\label{thm:closed-term-substitution-boundary-root-audit-compiler-coherence}
The audit route of \autoref{thm:closed-term-substitution-boundary-root-audit-totality} and the compiler-facing route of \autoref{thm:closed-term-substitution-boundary-root-compiler-nonescape} cite the same closed-boundary packet. Their common cited object is the surface of \autoref{def:closed-term-substitution-boundary-root-operation-surface}, not two separate substitution semantics and not a host-level compiler correctness witness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the root audit route. By \autoref{thm:closed-term-substitution-boundary-root-audit-totality}, every audit-visible consumer is already routed through the displayed closedness, operation, ledger, audit, transport, continuation, provenance, and naming rows.

Now read the compiler route through \autoref{thm:closed-term-substitution-boundary-root-compiler-nonescape}. That theorem permits a citation of $\ClosedTermSubstitutionCompilerUp$ only after the same closed shift and substitute-closed boundary rows have been exposed by the boundary packet.

The two routes therefore meet at the packet of \autoref{def:closed-term-substitution-boundary-root-operation-surface}. The audit side and compiler side share one closed-boundary citation, so no second host substitution semantics, hidden evaluator, or ambient compiler theorem is present.
\end{proof}

\label{thm:closed-term-substitution-boundary-root-compiler-route-exhaustion}

\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Closed-term substitution boundary root downstream coverage]
\label{thm:closed-term-substitution-boundary-root-downstream-coverage}
For every accepted $\ClosedTermSubstitutionBoundaryUp$ packet, every consumer-facing use of the root operation surface is covered by the finite downstream alternatives
$$
\begin{gathered}
\mathsf{operation}(Q,R),\qquad \mathsf{ledger}(L,A),\qquad \mathsf{transport}(H,C),\qquad \mathsf{provenance}(P,N),\\
\mathsf{compiler\_citation}(\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closedtermsubstitutioncompiler-namecert})\ \mathsf{after}\ T,V,d,C_{\mathsf{src}},C_{\mathsf{val}},Q,R,L,A,H,C,P,N.
\end{gathered}
$$
Equivalently, any accepted local consumer of closed shift, substitute-closed readback, audit replay, or compiler-facing citation factors through the displayed surface of \autoref{def:closed-term-substitution-boundary-root-operation-surface}. Host substitution semantics, subject reduction, host syntax quotients, hidden evaluators, hidden compiler state, detached compiler correctness, and any operation coordinate outside
$$
T,V,d,C_{\mathsf{src}},C_{\mathsf{val}},Q,R,L,A,H,C,P,N
$$
remain outside the $\ClosedTermSubstitutionBoundaryUp$ packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First consider a direct operation consumer. By \autoref{thm:closed-term-substitution-boundary-root-operation-totality}, both root operation rows $Q$ and $R$ are exhausted by the finite surface of \autoref{def:closed-term-substitution-boundary-root-operation-surface}. Thus a consumer cannot obtain closed shift or substitute-closed readback from a host substitution semantics, subject reduction theorem, host syntax quotient, hidden evaluator, hidden compiler state, or unlisted row.

Next consider a ledger, audit, transport, provenance, or naming consumer. The totality theorem routes every audit-visible operation read through the same rows $T,V,d,C_{\mathsf{src}},C_{\mathsf{val}},Q,R,L,A,H,C,P,N$, and \autoref{thm:closed-term-substitution-boundary-root-audit-totality} makes the audit side total over that finite route. The rows $L$ and $A$ record the ledger and audit boundary, while $H,C,P,N$ are only componentwise transport, displayed continuation, provenance, and local naming rows; none of them contains a coordinate for an external substitution source.

Finally consider a downstream compiler-facing consumer. By \autoref{thm:closed-term-substitution-boundary-root-compiler-route-exhaustion}, every read that cites \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closedtermsubstitutioncompiler-namecert} first factors through the same root operation surface. By \autoref{thm:closed-term-substitution-boundary-root-audit-compiler-coherence}, this compiler route and the audit route cite one closed-boundary packet rather than two separate semantics. The non-escaping compiler condition of \autoref{thm:closed-term-substitution-boundary-root-compiler-nonescape} then keeps the sibling compiler citation downstream of the displayed packet, so the excluded alternatives remain outside the BEDC packet.
\end{proof}
