\begin{theorem}[Budgeted real seal route NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:budgeted-real-seal-route-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:budgeted-real-seal-route-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\BudgetedRealSealRouteUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $B=(S,T,O,L,R,H,C,P,N)$, budget selector admission through $S$, TailMeet threshold admission through $T$, observation-budget entrance through $O$, CauchyLimitSeal route handoff through $L$, Real seal and readback exactness through $R$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the displayed packet $B=(S,T,O,L,R,H,C,P,N)$. Each obligation names one carrier coordinate or one structural route attached to that coordinate.

The budget route starts with $S$, enters the Real-facing observation surface through $O$, and then requires the threshold row $T$ before the limit seal $L$ can hand a read to $R$. Thus the Real seal read is tied to the same finite selector, threshold, observation-budget, and limit-seal rows.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, identify, and name the same finite packet. Since the carrier contains no external completion coordinate, these rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Budgeted real seal route non-escape]
\label{thm:budgeted-real-seal-route-nonescape}
Every accepted $\BudgetedRealSealRouteUp$ packet exports only the displayed rows
$$
S,T,O,L,R,H,C,P,N.
$$
In particular, no accepted packet may expose the $\RealUp$ seal row $R$ without the TailMeet threshold row $T$, and no consumer may recover a quotient stream, host equality of real names, countable-choice selector, external limit, or ambient Real-completion principle from the packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier. The only Real-facing coordinate is $R$, and it is reached through the route rows $S,T,O,L$ together with the structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

Apply the NameCert obligation theorem. It requires threshold admission through $T$ and limit-seal handoff through $L$ before Real readback exactness through $R$ is available.

The excluded objects would require coordinates not present in the carrier. Because every export must factor through the displayed rows, the packet has no route to quotient streams, host equality, choice selectors, external limits, or an ambient completeness theorem.
\end{proof}
