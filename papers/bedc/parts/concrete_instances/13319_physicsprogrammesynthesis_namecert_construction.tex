It sits after the multi-history physics route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-multi-history-physics-route-namecert}, the physics falsification surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-physics-falsification-surface-namecert}, the programme-strength ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-programme-strength-ledger-namecert}, the verification-failure roadmap of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-verification-failure-roadmap-namecert}, and the failure-certificate packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-failurecertificate-namecert}. These siblings expose route, falsification, strength, verification-failure, and failure-certificate rows separately; this carrier binds them before any consumer reads the programme as a completed physical theory or as a single-axis verification claim.

\begin{definition}[Physics programme synthesis carrier]
\label{def:physics-programme-synthesis-carrier}
A $\PhysicsProgrammeSynthesisUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(R,F,L,V,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $R$ is the $\MultiHistoryPhysicsRouteUp$ row, recording the relational physics, law bridge, scientific-model bridge, quantum-state, and falsification-boundary route;
\item $F$ is the $\PhysicsFalsificationSurfaceUp$ row, recording route-law-empirical-countermodel refusal data before completed physics may be claimed;
\item $L$ is the $\ProgrammeStrengthLedgerUp$ row, recording claim strength, dependency status, theory and verification status, bridge status, refusal, and local naming;
\item $V$ is the $\VerificationFailureRoadmapUp$ row, recording checked-report failure, downgrade, roadmap-boundary, cannot-claim, and upgrade-registry data;
\item $E$ is the $\FailureCertificateUp$ row, recording the concrete failure certificate consumed when a programme route is blocked;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $R,F,L,V,E$, package provenance, and the local naming row;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ routes for physical-route readback, falsification-surface inspection, strength-ledger inspection, verification-failure replay, and failure-certificate handoff;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\PhysicsProgrammeSynthesisUp$, $\MultiHistoryPhysicsRouteUp$, $\PhysicsFalsificationSurfaceUp$, $\ProgrammeStrengthLedgerUp$, $\VerificationFailureRoadmapUp$, $\FailureCertificateUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\PhysicsProgrammeSynthesisUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier accepts exactly packets whose visible rows are $R,F,L,V,E,H,C,P,N$. It contains no completed physical theory, no empirical value row outside the cited physics surfaces, no verification-status promotion outside the ledger, and no failure route outside the displayed certificate and roadmap rows.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Physics programme synthesis falsification non-escape]
\label{thm:physics-programme-synthesis-falsification-nonescape}
Every accepted $\PhysicsProgrammeSynthesisUp$ packet reads physical ambition through the displayed route row $R$ and falsification row $F$ before it may inspect programme strength, verification failure, or failure certificates. A consumer cannot bypass $F$ to claim completed physics, empirical adequacy, law completion, bridge completion, or axiom-pure success.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:physics-programme-synthesis-carrier}. The physical side of the packet is exactly $R$ together with $F$.

Apply the sibling route and falsification surfaces. The multi-history physics route records the candidate physical route, while the physics falsification surface records the law, empirical, countermodel, bridge-audit, axiom-purity, transport, provenance, and naming rows that can block that route.

The remaining coordinates $L,V,E,H,C,P,N$ replay or report the status of the same displayed route. None of them supplies a second physical-success coordinate. Hence a completed-physics read that omits $F$ is outside the accepted $\PhysicsProgrammeSynthesisUp$ carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Physics programme synthesis axis separation]
\label{thm:physics-programme-synthesis-axis-separation}
For an accepted $\PhysicsProgrammeSynthesisUp$ carrier $S=(R,F,L,V,E,H,C,P,N)$, the physical-route axis, falsification axis, programme-strength axis, verification-failure axis, and failure-certificate axis remain separate NameCert coordinates. In particular, a stronger programme-strength row in $L$ cannot erase a falsification row in $F$, a verification-failure row in $V$ cannot be read as a physical countermodel by itself, and a failure certificate $E$ cannot replace the route and ledger rows it diagnoses.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the displayed tuple. The rows $R,F,L,V,E$ are distinct carrier coordinates, and $H$ transports them componentwise rather than by quotienting them together.

Read the programme-strength and verification-failure siblings next. The strength ledger reports dependency, theory, formal, bridge, and refusal coordinates; the verification-failure roadmap records audit report, failure, downgrade, roadmap, upgrade registry, and cannot-claim rows. These reports consume the physical and failure surfaces but do not identify them.

Finally inspect $C,P,N$. The continuation routes replay each named coordinate, the package row records the sibling provenance, and the local NameCert row seals the composite packet. Therefore no axis can discharge another axis by prose, status promotion, or certificate substitution.
\end{proof}
