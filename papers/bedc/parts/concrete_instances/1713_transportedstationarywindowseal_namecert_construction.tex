\begin{definition}[Transported stationary window-seal carrier]
\label{def:transported-stationary-window-seal-carrier}
A $\TransportedStationaryWindowSealUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
T=(R,S,G,W,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $R$ is the transported $\RatUp$ classifier row for the two stationary rational seeds;
\item $S$ is the constant $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window classifier induced by $R$;
\item $G$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ finite-window seal row read from the same constant stream window;
\item $W$ is the $\FiniteWindowEnvelopeUp$ packet naming the selected anchor, window, dyadic ledger, regular-rational seal, and endpoint classifier;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal-facing row obtained from $G$ and $W$;
\item $H$ records the transported stationary diagonal handoff rows that identify the displayed diagonal representatives with the same constant rational route;
\item $C$ stores the displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ transports for the seed classifier, stream window, regular-rational seal, finite envelope, and real seal;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance naming $\RatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\FiniteWindowEnvelopeUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ source certificates;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\TransportedStationaryWindowSealUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The local classifier relates two carriers exactly when their transported $\RatUp$ classifier rows agree and the induced $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\FiniteWindowEnvelopeUp$, $\RealUp$, transport, package, and local certificate rows compare componentwise after the displayed routes in $C$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Transported stationary window-seal NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:transported-stationary-window-seal-namecert-obligations}
The $\NameCert_{\TransportedStationaryWindowSealUp}$ surface has five obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier: formation of the finite packet of \autoref{def:transported-stationary-window-seal-carrier};
\item classifier: componentwise comparison of the transported $\RatUp$ seed classifier, constant $\StreamNameUp$ window, $\RegSeqRatUp$ seal, $\FiniteWindowEnvelopeUp$ packet, $\RealUp$ row, transports, package provenance, and local certificate row;
\item transported window exactness: the $\RatUp$ classifier, constant $\StreamNameUp$ window, and $\RegSeqRatUp$ finite-window seal read the same transported stationary row;
\item seal exhaustion: every $\RealUp$ consumer read factors through the displayed $\FiniteWindowEnvelopeUp$ packet and the same $\RegSeqRatUp$ seal row;
\item non-escape: the carrier has no coordinate for quotient streams, host equality, choice, selected host limits, or real completeness.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Carrier formation is the finite assembly of the displayed tuple. The transported rational classifier supplies $R$, the stationary stream read supplies $S$, the regular-rational boundary supplies $G$, and the finite observation envelope supplies $W$. The real-facing row $E$, transported diagonal rows $H$, route table $C$, provenance row $P$, and local naming row $N$ are all visible coordinates of the same $\BHist$ packet.

For the classifier obligation, compare the packet component by component after applying the displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ routes. The transported $\RatUp$ row determines the constant stream window, the stream window determines the regular-rational finite seal, and the envelope ties the same window and dyadic ledger to the real-facing endpoint row.

Transported window exactness is the stationary diagonal calculation read through a finite window. The transported $\RatUp$ classifier induces the constant $\StreamNameUp$ classifier, and the $\RegSeqRatUp$ row consumes exactly that window with the displayed ledger. Thus the three public readings are the same stationary row at the window boundary.

Seal exhaustion follows by projecting $W$ and $E$. The $\FiniteWindowEnvelopeUp$ packet exposes the selected anchor, finite window, dyadic ledger, regular-rational seal, and endpoint classifier, and the $\RealUp$ row can read only those exposed fields plus the displayed transports and provenance.

The non-escape clause is constructor inspection. None of $R,S,G,W,E,H,C,P,N$ is a quotient stream, a host equality proof, a choice operator, a selected host limit, or a real-completeness witness. Therefore the certificate remains a finite transported window-seal packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Transported stationary window-seal readback determinacy]

\begin{proof}

The transported window exactness clause in \autoref{thm:transported-stationary-window-seal-namecert-obligations} states that $R$, $S$, and $G$ read the same transported stationary row. The classifier clause transports these rows only componentwise through the displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ routes.

\begin{theorem}[Transported stationary window-seal uniqueness]
\label{thm:transported-stationary-window-seal-uniqueness}
Let $T=(R,S,G,W,E,H,C,P,N)$ and $T'=(R',S',G',W',E',H',C',P',N')$ be accepted $\TransportedStationaryWindowSealUp$ carriers. Suppose they are displayed over the same transported $\RatUp$ row, the same constant $\StreamNameUp$ window, the same $\RegSeqRatUp$ finite seal, the same $\FiniteWindowEnvelopeUp$ packet, and the same $\RealUp$ seal-facing row after the $\hsame$ rows and $\Cont$ routes named by $H,H',C,C'$. Then $T$ and $T'$ expose the same public seal row.

The uniqueness claim is only about the finite public seal row. It does not identify quotient streams, host equalities, selected host limits, or real-completeness data.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply the carrier and classifier clauses of \autoref{thm:transported-stationary-window-seal-namecert-obligations}. They restrict comparison of $T$ and $T'$ to the displayed transported rational row, constant stream window, regular-rational seal, finite envelope, real-facing row, transport rows, continuation routes, provenance, and local naming rows.

Next use \autoref{thm:transported-stationary-window-seal-readback-determinacy}. Agreement of the transported $\RatUp$ row under the displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ routes fixes the constant $\StreamNameUp$ window and the $\RegSeqRatUp$ finite-window seal at the public readback boundary.

Apply \autoref{thm:transported-stationary-window-seal-real-factorization}. Any $\RealUp$ seal-facing read must factor through the same finite envelope $W$ and the same regular-rational seal row $G$, so the public seal row exposed by $T$ and $T'$ is the same row.

Finally, \autoref{thm:transported-stationary-window-seal-ledger-nonescape} blocks the only possible escapes from this finite comparison. Quotient streams, host equality, selected host limits, and real-completeness data are not carrier coordinates, so they cannot refine or split the public seal row.
\end{proof}
