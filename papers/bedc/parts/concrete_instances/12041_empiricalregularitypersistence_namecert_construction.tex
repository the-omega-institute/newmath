It sits before the law-certificate packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-law-certificate-namecert} and beside the physical-induction carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-reality-constrained-physical-induction-namecert}, the open-fit packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-open-fit-packet-namecert}, the model-audit packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-reality-constrained-model-audit-namecert}, and the induction-stability seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-physical-induction-stability-seal-namecert}. Its role is to keep observed regularity and future-persistence commitment in one packet without identifying either side with a bare equation or a total law oracle.

\begin{definition}[Empirical regularity persistence carrier]
\label{def:empirical-regularity-persistence-carrier}
An $\EmpiricalRegularityPersistenceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
E=(M,R,K,L,G,A,S,F,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the finite measurement row naming the observed cases, instruments, units, and admissible sampling window.
\item $R$ is the records-side regularity row stating the relation satisfied by the observed rows under $M$.
\item $K$ is the classifier row comparing two regularity packets only through their displayed measurements, records, tolerance ledgers, GAP rows, stability rows, failure rows, transports, continuations, provenance, and local names.
\item $L$ is the finite error-ledger row recording tolerances, idealizations, calibration residue, and excluded observations.
\item $G$ is the Type IV future-persistence GAP row asserting that the records-side regularity is offered for not-yet-observed continuations.
\item $A$ is the $\LawCertificateUp$ consumption row naming the law-certificate surface that may read the packet.
\item $S$ is the stability row, read against $\PhysicalInductionStabilitySealUp$ and $\RealityConstrainedPhysicalInductionUp$, naming the continuation class and persistence basis under which $G$ is licensed.
\item $F$ is the failure row naming observations, continuations, refined measurements, or audit rows that defeat the persistence claim.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $M,R,K,L,G,A,S,F,C,P,N$.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay through measurement, regularity, classifier, error ledger, future-persistence GAP, law-certificate consumption, stability, and failure rows.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\EmpiricalRegularityPersistenceUp$, $\LawCertificateUp$, $\RealityConstrainedPhysicalInductionUp$, $\OpenFitPacketUp$, $\RealityConstrainedModelAuditUp$, $\PhysicalInductionStabilitySealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\EmpiricalRegularityPersistenceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a bare equation, hidden measurement row, omitted tolerance ledger, persistence claim without $G$, all-future truth, final-fit oracle, host physical necessity, or law status detached from $A,S,F,H,C,P,N$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Empirical regularity persistence classifier]
\label{def:empirical-regularity-persistence-classifier}
The $\EmpiricalRegularityPersistenceUp$ classifier identifies two accepted packets only when $M,R,K,L,G,A,S,F,H,C,P,N$ agree through displayed componentwise $\hsame$ transport. Equality of equation-like syntax is not enough; agreement of finite observed rows is not enough; and a future-persistence claim not attached to the explicit GAP row $G$, stability row $S$, failure row $F$, and law-certificate consumption row $A$ is outside the accepted classifier.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Empirical regularity persistence GAP exposure]
\label{thm:empirical-regularity-persistence-gap-exposure}
For every accepted $\EmpiricalRegularityPersistenceUp$ carrier $E=(M,R,K,L,G,A,S,F,H,C,P,N)$, the future-facing part of the packet is exposed exactly by the Type IV GAP row $G$ together with stability row $S$, failure row $F$, continuation replay $C$, provenance $P$, and local name $N$. The records-side rows $M,R,K,L$ certify observed regularity only; they do not by themselves license future persistence.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:empirical-regularity-persistence-carrier}. The observed side is listed as $M,R,K,L$, while the persistence side is listed as $G,S,F$ and is replayed through $C,P,N$.

Apply the classifier of \autoref{def:empirical-regularity-persistence-classifier}. It refuses equality of equation-like syntax and finite observed rows when the GAP, stability, failure, replay, provenance, or local naming rows are missing.

Thus records-side regularity is a finite observation claim, while future persistence is the separate Type IV GAP exposure. Since the carrier has no hidden persistence coordinate, no accepted consumer can read forward commitment from $M,R,K,L$ alone.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LawCertificate consumption of empirical regularity persistence]
\label{thm:empirical-regularity-lawcertificate-consumption}
Every $\LawCertificateUp$ consumer read of an accepted $\EmpiricalRegularityPersistenceUp$ packet factors through the measurement row $M$, regularity row $R$, classifier row $K$, error-ledger row $L$, future-persistence GAP row $G$, law-certificate consumption row $A$, stability row $S$, failure row $F$, transport row $H$, replay row $C$, provenance row $P$, and local $\NameCert$ row $N$. The consumer may not read an equation-like law packet when either the finite measurement and error rows or the explicit future-persistence GAP are absent.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the consumption row $A$. It names the $\LawCertificateUp$ surface that may read the empirical packet, but $A$ is itself only one coordinate of $E=(M,R,K,L,G,A,S,F,H,C,P,N)$.

Use \autoref{thm:empirical-regularity-persistence-gap-exposure}. The theorem separates observed regularity from future persistence and requires $G,S,F,C,P,N$ for the forward commitment. The measurement row $M$, regularity row $R$, classifier row $K$, and error-ledger row $L$ remain mandatory for the records-side evidence.

Therefore a law-certificate consumer sees a complete finite packet rather than a detached equation. If either the measurement and error rows or the future-persistence GAP row is absent, the requested law read lacks one side of the carrier and is rejected by the classifier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Empirical regularity persistence nonescape]
\label{thm:empirical-regularity-persistence-nonescape}
No accepted $\EmpiricalRegularityPersistenceUp$ consumer can recover physical-law status from a bare equation, observed records alone, model syntax alone, hidden calibration assumptions, an omitted error ledger, an unlisted continuation class, or a future-persistence assertion outside the displayed Type IV GAP row. Every accepted read remains inside $M,R,K,L,G,A,S,F,H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier projection gives the complete inventory $M,R,K,L,G,A,S,F,H,C,P,N$. The bare equation, model syntax, hidden calibration assumption, omitted error ledger, unlisted continuation class, and off-packet future-persistence assertion are not coordinates of that inventory.

Apply \autoref{def:empirical-regularity-persistence-classifier}. The classifier compares accepted packets only through the displayed rows, so replacing any of those rows by an absent object changes the comparison surface rather than preserving the accepted packet.

Apply \autoref{thm:empirical-regularity-lawcertificate-consumption}. Even the law-certificate consumer must pass through both sides of the packet: finite measurement and error evidence on the records side, and GAP, stability, and failure exposure on the future-facing side. Hence no accepted consumer escapes to physical-law status outside the displayed carrier.
\end{proof}
