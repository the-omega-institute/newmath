\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CauchyCompleteRealLineUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-complete-real-line-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges' constructive account of the real line as regular Cauchy data with explicit moduli, the $\CauchyCompleteRealLineUp$ packet records the finite exit surface for the Real and Completion phase. It assembles the dyadic tolerance core of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the finite stream windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the Bishop handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bishop-regular-cauchy-completion-namecert}, the completeness modulus boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-completeness-modulus-namecert}, and the terminal $\RealUp$ seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet names a real-line completion read as finite BEDC data; it does not introduce quotient streams, selected limits, countable choice, trichotomy, host real equality, open-cover compactness, or Dedekind completeness.

\begin{definition}[Cauchy complete real line carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-complete-real-line-carrier}
A $\CauchyCompleteRealLineUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(R,S,D,B,M,L,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ source row, carrying the regular rational sequence, its finite request surface, and the rowwise regularity data accepted by the completion route.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window row, exposing the finite prefix, tail window, readback indices, and stream observations inspected by the route.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, recording positivity, refinement, and budget splitting for every displayed completion tolerance.
\item $B$ is the $\BishopRegularCauchyCompletionUp$ handoff row, opening the regular Cauchy completion boundary after $R,S,D$ have been displayed.
\item $M$ is the $\CauchyCompletenessModulusUp$ row, recording the explicit tail-modulus surface used by completion consumers before they read the terminal seal.
\item $L$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row, named only after the source, window, tolerance, Bishop handoff, and modulus rows have been opened.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the regular source, stream windows, dyadic ledger, Bishop handoff, completeness modulus, terminal real seal, replay row, provenance row, and local naming row.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay through
$$
R,S,D \longmapsto B \longmapsto M \longmapsto L \longmapsto H,P,N .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyCompleteRealLineUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\BishopRegularCauchyCompletionUp$, $\CauchyCompletenessModulusUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyCompleteRealLineUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $R,S,D,B,M,L,H,C,P,N$. The carrier has no coordinate for quotient stream equality, a selected limit, countable choice, trichotomy, host real equality, open-cover compactness, Dedekind completeness, Lean verification, or a standard bridge.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy complete real line NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-complete-real-line-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CauchyCompleteRealLineUp$ carrier
$$
Q=(R,S,D,B,M,L,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyCompleteRealLineUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the regular rational source, stream windows, dyadic tolerance ledger, Bishop handoff, completeness modulus, terminal real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item source readiness, requiring every accepted completion read to expose the same $\RegSeqRatUp$ source row $R$ before any real-line seal is named;
\item finite window readiness, requiring all inspected prefixes, tail windows, readback indices, and observations to pass through the $\StreamNameUp$ row $S$;
\item dyadic tolerance routing, requiring each tolerance request to factor through the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ positivity, refinement, and budget-splitting ledger $D$;
\item Bishop handoff, requiring the $\BishopRegularCauchyCompletionUp$ row $B$ to mediate between the regular rational source and the terminal real-line seal;
\item completeness-modulus exposure, requiring the $\CauchyCompletenessModulusUp$ row $M$ to display the tail-modulus surface before $L$ is opened;
\item Real seal discipline, requiring $L$ to be downstream of $R,S,D,B,M$ under componentwise $\hsame$ transport and displayed $\Cont$ replay;
\item provenance non-escape, requiring $P$ and $N$ to source and name only the listed route, with no quotient stream, selected limit, countable choice, trichotomy, host real equality, open-cover compactness, Dedekind completeness, Lean verification, or bridge row.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored outside those rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:cauchy-complete-real-line-carrier}. The accepted packet displays the regular source $R$, finite windows $S$, dyadic tolerance ledger $D$, Bishop handoff $B$, completeness modulus $M$, terminal seal $L$, and structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

Source readiness and finite-window readiness are the first visible constraints. A completion consumer reads the regular rational source through $R$ and the inspected finite stream data through $S$, so no request can enter the seal route from an unnamed stream.

Dyadic tolerance routing is rowwise. The ledger $D$ supplies positivity, refinement, and budget splitting before the Bishop handoff receives a tolerance request.

The Bishop handoff and completeness-modulus rows are then forced by the order of the displayed replay. The route opens $B$ after $R,S,D$, opens $M$ after $B$, and only then reaches the terminal $\RealUp$ seal $L$.

The remaining rows do not enlarge the carrier. The $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ rows transport and replay the same inventory, while $P$ and $N$ record provenance and local naming for that inventory. Since the excluded objects have no coordinate in $Q$, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy complete real line regular limit route]
\label{thm:cauchy-complete-real-line-regular-limit-route}
Every accepted regular Cauchy real-line completion read exported by a $\CauchyCompleteRealLineUp$ carrier factors through
$$
\StreamNameUp,\DyadicRatCoreUp,\RegSeqRatUp,\BishopRegularCauchyCompletionUp,\CauchyCompletenessModulusUp,\RealUp .
$$
Equivalently, a consumer can open the terminal seal row $L$ only after reading the finite stream window $S$, dyadic tolerance ledger $D$, regular rational source $R$, Bishop handoff row $B$, and completeness-modulus row $M$.

This factorization has no alternate path through quotient streams, selected limits, countable choice, trichotomy, host real equality, open-cover compactness, or Dedekind completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Let $Q=(R,S,D,B,M,L,H,C,P,N)$ be accepted. A regular Cauchy real-line completion read starts with a finite observation request, so the visible stream data are supplied by $S$.

The tolerance attached to that request is interpreted through $D$. Positivity, refinement, and budget splitting are therefore fixed before the read can be treated as a completion-facing tail claim.

The source row $R$ supplies the regular rational data read at the displayed indices. This keeps the request inside the same regular sequence surface rather than replacing it by a quotient stream or a host real value.

The route then opens the Bishop handoff $B$ and the completeness-modulus row $M$. These rows mediate the passage from regular rational Cauchy data to the completion seal while keeping the tail-modulus evidence explicit.

Only after $S,D,R,B,M$ have been displayed does the replay reach $L$. The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name that same path, so there is no remaining coordinate from which an alternate selected-limit, choice, trichotomy, compactness, or Dedekind-completeness route could be read.
\end{proof}
