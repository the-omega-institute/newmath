\begin{definition}[Philosophy synthesis audit carrier]
\label{def:philosophy-synthesis-audit-carrier}
A $\PhilosophySynthesisAuditUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(R,E,G,A,L,Q,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $R$ is the $\CoreRegistryIndexUp$ row naming the concept, theorem, gap, tradition, scientific-claim, refusal, route, ordinary-language, and audit entries that the synthesis may read;
\item $E$ is the $\RegistryExportSurfaceUp$ row recording whether a synthesized reading is exported, blocked, horizon-facing, or held as a formalization target;
\item $G$ is the $\TheoremGapRegistryUp$ row separating theorem-strength readings from gap-gate readings;
\item $A$ is the $\AuditGateBoundaryUp$ row recording the audit route by which the synthesis is checked before export;
\item $L$ is the $\FocusedLedgerUp$ row recording the finite focus, selector, digest, gap, relation, invariant, dependence, self-proxy, and audit rows consumed by the reading;
\item $Q$ is the synthesis-question row recording the philosophical reading, its allowed registry entries, its blocked readings, its audit-route requirement, and its not-claimed surface;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $R,E,G,A,L,Q$;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay through the registry index, export surface, theorem-gap registry, audit boundary, focused ledger, and synthesis-question row;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CoreRegistryIndexUp$, $\RegistryExportSurfaceUp$, $\TheoremGapRegistryUp$, $\AuditGateBoundaryUp$, $\FocusedLedgerUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\PhilosophySynthesisAuditUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for inspiration-only synthesis, hidden registry export, gate-free theorem strength, audit-free philosophical conclusion, unledgered focus selection, or a reading that bypasses the not-claimed surface in $Q$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Philosophy synthesis audit NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:philosophy-synthesis-audit-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:philosophy-synthesis-audit-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\PhilosophySynthesisAuditUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for
$$
S=(R,E,G,A,L,Q,H,C,P,N),
$$
registry-index coverage through $R$, export-status coverage through $E$, theorem-gap separation through $G$, audit-route exposure through $A$, focused-ledger support through $L$, synthesis-question accountability through $Q$, componentwise $\hsame$ stability through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, package provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the finite carrier of \autoref{def:philosophy-synthesis-audit-carrier}. The rows $R,E,G,A,L,Q$ display the registry, export, theorem-gap, audit, focus, and synthesis-question data before any public reading of auditable generativity is allowed.

Read the sibling rows in order. $\CoreRegistryIndexUp$ supplies the finite registry entries, $\RegistryExportSurfaceUp$ controls whether the reading may leave the registry, $\TheoremGapRegistryUp$ keeps theorem strength distinct from gap status, $\AuditGateBoundaryUp$ supplies the audit route, and $\FocusedLedgerUp$ records the finite focused ledger consumed by the reading.

Finally inspect $Q,H,C,P,N$. The synthesis-question row records the philosophical reading and its not-claimed surface, while the structural rows only transport, replay, source, and name the same finite packet. Hence the local NameCert obligations are exactly the displayed synthesis-audit rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Philosophy synthesis audit nonescape]
\label{thm:philosophy-synthesis-audit-nonescape}
Every exported auditable-generativity reading of an accepted $\PhilosophySynthesisAuditUp$ packet must read the registry-index row $R$, export row $E$, theorem-gap row $G$, audit-route row $A$, focused-ledger row $L$, synthesis-question row $Q$, transport row $H$, replay row $C$, provenance row $P$, and local naming row $N$. A packet without the audit-route row $A$ cannot export an auditable-generativity reading.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:philosophy-synthesis-audit-namecert-obligations}. They require the registry, export, theorem-gap, audit, focused-ledger, and synthesis-question rows as one finite packet before a reading can be exported.

If $A$ is absent, the reading still has no audit route. The registry index can name entries, the export surface can classify status, the theorem-gap registry can separate theorem and gap rows, and the focused ledger can display finite focus data, but none of those rows records the audit-route check by itself.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve an admitted packet; they do not synthesize a missing audit boundary. Thus a synthesis-audit packet without $A$ exits the accepted carrier and cannot export an auditable-generativity reading.
\end{proof}
