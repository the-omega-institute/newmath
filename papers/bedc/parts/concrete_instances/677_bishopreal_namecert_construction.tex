¥chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $¥BishopRealUp$}
¥label{ch:concrete-instances-bishopreal-namecert}

The $¥BishopRealUp$ interface records the Bishop--Bridges 1985 constructive-analysis
reading of real names inside the BEDC certificate layer. Its carrier is not a
Dedekind cut, a quotient real, or a completed host number. It is a finite
certificate surface over regular rational names, locatedness evidence,
apartness evidence, explicit modulus rows, and the visible $¥RealUp$ seal.

¥begin{definition}[BishopReal regular located carrier]
¥label{def:bishopreal-regular-located-carrier}
A $¥BishopRealUp$ packet is a finite $¥mathsf{BHist}$ row
$$
B=(S,Q,D,M,L,A,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible coordinates:
¥begin{enumerate}
¥item $S$ is a $¥StreamNameUp$ schedule naming the finite windows through which
the rational approximation stream is inspected;
¥item $Q$ is a certified $¥RegSeqRatUp$ name read on those scheduled windows;
¥item $D$ is the $¥DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint packet used for finite tolerance
and interval observations;
¥item $M$ is the explicit $¥ModulusOfConvergenceUp$ row controlling the regular
sequence windows;
¥item $L$ is the displayed $¥LocatedRealUp$ evidence row;
¥item $A$ is the displayed $¥ApartnessRealUp$ evidence row;
¥item $E$ is the $¥RealUp$ seal consumed by downstream real-facing interfaces;
¥item $H$ records componentwise $¥hsame$ transport for $S,Q,D,M,L,A$, and $E$;
¥item $C$ records the visible $¥Cont$ routes through which a consumer reaches the
finite stream windows, dyadic endpoint packets, modulus rows, locatedness rows,
apartness rows, and seal row;
¥item $P$ is the $¥Pkg$ provenance row naming $¥DyadicRatCoreUp$,
$¥StreamNameUp$, $¥RegSeqRatUp$, $¥LocatedRealUp$, $¥ApartnessRealUp$,
$¥ModulusOfConvergenceUp$, and $¥RealUp$ as the dependency anchors;
¥item $N$ is the local $¥NameCert_{¥BishopRealUp}$ ledger row.

The carrier predicate is
$¥mathsf{BishopRealPacket}(S,Q,D,M,L,A,E,H,C,P,N)$. Its classifier compares two
accepted packets only by passing through common finite stream windows, the same
dyadic endpoint packet family, the displayed modulus rows, the locatedness and
apartness evidence rows, and the visible $¥RealUp$ seal. No coordinate stores a
Dedekind cut, quotient real, trichotomy principle, selected limit, or classical
completeness witness.
¥end{definition}

¥begin{theorem}[BishopReal located-apartness transport]
¥label{thm:bishopreal-located-apartness-transport}
For any two accepted $¥BishopRealUp$ packets
$B=(S,Q,D,M,L,A,E,H,C,P,N)$ and $B'=(S',Q',D',M',L',A',E',H',C',P',N')$, a
componentwise $¥hsame$ comparison of their stream schedules, regular rational
names, dyadic endpoint packets, modulus rows, locatedness rows, apartness rows,
and $¥RealUp$ seals transports the local classifier from $B$ to $B'$.
Downstream reads of locatedness or apartness therefore factor through the same
finite stream-window and dyadic-endpoint surface, never through trichotomy or
host real equality.
¥end{theorem}
¥begin{proof}
Project the two carrier rows through
¥autoref{def:bishopreal-regular-located-carrier}. The schedule coordinates give
the only stream windows a consumer may inspect, and the dyadic endpoint
coordinates give the only finite tolerance packets available at those windows.

Transport the regular rational, modulus, locatedness, apartness, and seal rows
componentwise along the displayed $¥hsame$ witnesses. Each read is reached
through one of the visible $¥Cont$ routes in the carrier, so the transported
packet has the same finite observation shape as the source packet.

The forbidden host data are absent from the carrier. Since no coordinate stores
trichotomy, quotient equality, a cut, or a selected limit, the classifier cannot
export any such principle after transport.
¥end{proof}

¥begin{theorem}[BishopReal RegSeqRat readback]
¥label{thm:bishopreal-regseqrat-readback}
Every $¥RealUp$ seal read exported by an accepted $¥BishopRealUp$ packet factors
through the certified $¥RegSeqRatUp$ name $Q$, the scheduled finite windows $S$,
the $¥DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint packets $D$, and the explicit
$¥ModulusOfConvergenceUp$ row $M$. The readback exposes only the regular
rational name, its dyadic finite observations, and the displayed real seal.
¥end{theorem}
¥begin{proof}
Let a downstream consumer read the seal coordinate $E$ of a packet $B$. By the
carrier definition, $E$ is stored together with the schedule $S$, regular name
$Q$, dyadic packet family $D$, and modulus row $M$.

The continuation coordinate $C$ gives the finite routes by which $E$ is reached.
Each such route also names the stream window, dyadic endpoint packet, and
modulus row used for the observation. Thus the seal read has the advertised
regular-sequence handoff as part of its visible packet.

Nothing else remains to read. The packet has no coordinate for a quotient class,
cut membership predicate, choice-selected endpoint, or classical completeness
argument, so the readback is exhausted by $S,Q,D,M$, and $E$.
¥end{proof}

¥begin{theorem}[BishopReal ledger obligation surface]
¥label{thm:bishopreal-ledger-obligation-surface}
The local $¥NameCert_{¥BishopRealUp}$ ledger surface consists exactly of the
carrier, classifier, stability, readback, and dependency rows displayed by
¥autoref{def:bishopreal-regular-located-carrier},
¥autoref{thm:bishopreal-located-apartness-transport}, and
¥autoref{thm:bishopreal-regseqrat-readback}. Its dependency anchors are
$¥DyadicRatCoreUp$, $¥StreamNameUp$, $¥RegSeqRatUp$, $¥LocatedRealUp$,
$¥ApartnessRealUp$, $¥ModulusOfConvergenceUp$, and $¥RealUp$.
¥end{theorem}
¥begin{proof}
The carrier row is the packet of
¥autoref{def:bishopreal-regular-located-carrier}. It lists the stream schedule,
regular rational name, dyadic endpoint packet, modulus, locatedness, apartness,
seal, transport, continuation, provenance, and local ledger coordinates.

The classifier and stability rows are supplied by
¥autoref{thm:bishopreal-located-apartness-transport}. They compare packets only
through common finite stream windows and componentwise transport of the visible
rows.

The exactness and readback row is
¥autoref{thm:bishopreal-regseqrat-readback}. The provenance coordinate $P$ and
ledger coordinate $N$ list precisely the seven dependency anchors named in the
statement, so the ledger surface has no further exported row.
¥end{proof}

¥label{thm:bishopreal-namecert-seed-obligations}

$¥ModulusOfConvergenceUp$, and $¥RealUp$;
