¥begin{theorem}[BishopReal located-apartness transport]
¥label{thm:bishopreal-located-apartness-transport}
For any two accepted $¥BishopRealUp$ packets
$B=(S,Q,D,M,L,A,E,H,C,P,N)$ and $B'=(S',Q',D',M',L',A',E',H',C',P',N')$, a
componentwise $¥hsame$ comparison of their stream schedules, regular rational
names, dyadic endpoint packets, modulus rows, locatedness rows, apartness rows,
and $¥RealUp$ seals transports the local classifier from $B$ to $B'$.
Downstream reads of locatedness or apartness therefore factor through the same
finite stream-window and dyadic-endpoint surface, never through trichotomy or
host real equality.
¥end{theorem}
¥begin{proof}
Project the two carrier rows through
¥autoref{def:bishopreal-regular-located-carrier}. The schedule coordinates give
the only stream windows a consumer may inspect, and the dyadic endpoint
coordinates give the only finite tolerance packets available at those windows.

Transport the regular rational, modulus, locatedness, apartness, and seal rows
componentwise along the displayed $¥hsame$ witnesses. Each read is reached
through one of the visible $¥Cont$ routes in the carrier, so the transported
packet has the same finite observation shape as the source packet.

The forbidden host data are absent from the carrier. Since no coordinate stores
trichotomy, quotient equality, a cut, or a selected limit, the classifier cannot
export any such principle after transport.
¥end{proof}

¥label{thm:bishopreal-regseqrat-readback}

the $¥DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint packets $D$, and the explicit
$¥ModulusOfConvergenceUp$ row $M$. The readback exposes only the regular
rational name, its dyadic finite observations, and the displayed real seal.
¥end{theorem}
¥begin{proof}
Let a downstream consumer read the seal coordinate $E$ of a packet $B$. By the
carrier definition, $E$ is stored together with the schedule $S$, regular name
$Q$, dyadic packet family $D$, and modulus row $M$.

The continuation coordinate $C$ gives the finite routes by which $E$ is reached.
Each such route also names the stream window, dyadic endpoint packet, and
modulus row used for the observation. Thus the seal read has the advertised
regular-sequence handoff as part of its visible packet.

Nothing else remains to read. The packet has no coordinate for a quotient class,
cut membership predicate, choice-selected endpoint, or classical completeness
argument, so the readback is exhausted by $S,Q,D,M$, and $E$.
¥end{proof}
