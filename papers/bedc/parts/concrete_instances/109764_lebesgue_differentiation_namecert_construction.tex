\begin{definition}[Lebesgue differentiation carrier]
\label{def:lebesgue-differentiation-carrier}
A $\LebesgueDifferentiationUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(M,F,V,H_0,A,D,E,T,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the $\MeasureUp$ source row, exposing only the finite measurable-event and local-measure requests inspected by the packet.
\item $F$ is the displayed function-read row whose local averages are tested through the measure row.
\item $V$ is the $\FiniteVitaliCoverUp$ ledger, recording the finite disjoint subfamily, cover cells, and overlap budget used by the differentiation window.
\item $H_0$ is the $\HardyLittlewoodMaximalUp$ domination row that bounds the exceptional local-average requests before a differentiation verdict is consumed.
\item $A$ records the finite local-average rows for the inspected balls or intervals.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger used to compare radii, averages, exceptional budgets, and real-name windows.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row for the value and error-bound reads.
\item $T$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the measure, function, Vitali, maximal, average, dyadic, real, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the route from finite local-average requests through $M,F,V,H_0,A,D,E$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LebesgueDifferentiationUp$, $\MeasureUp$, $\FiniteVitaliCoverUp$, $\HardyLittlewoodMaximalUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LebesgueDifferentiationUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $M,F,V,H_0,A,D,E,T,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for a completed measure algebra, an almost-everywhere quotient, a countable family of selected radii, a selected representative of a function, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Lebesgue differentiation NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:lebesgue-differentiation-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\LebesgueDifferentiationUp$ carrier
$$
L=(M,F,V,H_0,A,D,E,T,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\LebesgueDifferentiationUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the measure row, displayed function-read row, finite Vitali-cover ledger, Hardy--Littlewood maximal domination row, local-average rows, dyadic tolerance ledger, real seal, componentwise transport, displayed continuation, package provenance, and local naming.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:lebesgue-differentiation-carrier}. The rows $M,F,V,H_0,A,D,E,T,C,P,N$ are the whole displayed packet.

The measurable and averaging data are read first. The measure row $M$ admits the inspected event and local-measure requests, while $F$ and $A$ supply the displayed function windows and average rows.

The covering and domination rows then constrain the differentiation request. The Vitali ledger $V$ supplies the finite cover selection surface, and the Hardy--Littlewood row $H_0$ supplies the maximal domination boundary before the real-valued verdict is exposed.

Finally $D,E,T,C,P,N$ compare, seal, transport, replay, source, and name the same finite route. Since no additional coordinate is present, the NameCert surface exports no measure completion, almost-everywhere quotient, countable-choice selector, selected representative, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Lebesgue differentiation Vitali-maximal handoff]
\label{thm:lebesgue-differentiation-vitali-maximal-handoff}
Every differentiation read accepted by a $\LebesgueDifferentiationUp$ packet factors through the finite Vitali-cover ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finite-vitali-cover-namecert} and the maximal-domination surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-hardy-littlewood-maximal-namecert} before it reaches the $\MeasureUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, and $\RealUp$ rows used by downstream measure and real-name consumers.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted carrier $L=(M,F,V,H_0,A,D,E,T,C,P,N)$. A differentiation read reaches the local averages only through the measure and function rows $M,F,A$.

The finite covering step is then forced through $V$. It supplies the disjoint subfamily, cover-cell ledger, and overlap budget used by the local differentiation window.

The maximal-function step is forced through $H_0$. It bounds the exceptional average requests before the dyadic comparison row $D$ and terminal real seal $E$ can be consumed.

Thus the accepted read follows the ordered route through finite Vitali cover, Hardy--Littlewood maximal domination, measure, dyadic tolerance, and real-name rows, with $T,C,P,N$ preserving the route. No countable selection, measure completion, almost-everywhere quotient, selected function representative, or ambient completeness row can be inserted into that handoff.
\end{proof}
