\begin{theorem}[CriticalLineWitness source-budget carrier]
\label{thm:critical-line-witness-source-budget-carrier}
Let $W=(Z,S,M,R,Q,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CriticalLineWitnessUp$ packet.
Every accepted packet exposes this finite tuple as the zero-strip source,
source budget, modulus and readback route, refusal row, transport,
continuation, provenance, and local $\NameCert_{\CriticalLineWitnessUp}$
rows. No accepted packet exports hidden analytic continuation, a host zero
locator, a completed $\mathsf{RH}$ proof, or an off-packet critical-line
oracle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet through the carrier description of
\autoref{def:critical-line-witness-carrier}. Coordinate exhaustion gives only
the displayed rows $Z,S,M,R,Q,H,C,P,N$: the zero-strip source $Z$, source
budget $S$, modulus and readback route $M,R,Q$, transport $H$, continuation
$C$, provenance $P$, and local naming row $N$.

The existing root-strip source totality theorem
\autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-root-strip-source-totality} places every
line-facing read on this finite route before it can be consumed. The analytic
continuation nonescape theorem
\autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-analytic-continuation-nonescape} then
removes any ambient analytic continuation, host zero locator, completed
$\mathsf{RH}$ proof, or off-packet critical-line oracle from the exported
surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CriticalLineWitness zero-strip refusal]
\label{thm:critical-line-witness-zero-strip-refusal}
Let $W=(Z,S,M,R,Q,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CriticalLineWitnessUp$ packet.
Every zero-strip consumer factors through the displayed source, budget,
modulus, readback, and refusal rows before downstream use. The consumer cannot
replace the refusal row by a theorem about $\mathsf{RH}$, an
analytic-continuation bridge, or a completed zeta-zero classifier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the tuple projection supplied by
\autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-source-budget-carrier}. It exposes the
source, budget, modulus, readback, and refusal coordinates as components of
the accepted $\BHist$ packet, rather than as host-side analytic data.

Apply the zero-strip route factorization theorem
\autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-zero-strip-route-factorization}. The
consumer must pass through $Z,S,M,R,Q$ and the refusal row before any
downstream use can read the packet.

Finally use the modulus refusal exhaustion theorem
\autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-modulus-refusal-exhaustion}. Its
nonescape clause prevents the refusal row from being replaced by an
$\mathsf{RH}$ theorem, an analytic-continuation bridge, or a completed
zeta-zero classifier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CriticalLineWitness NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:critical-line-witness-namecert-obligation-surface}
For an accepted $\CriticalLineWitnessUp$ packet $W=(Z,S,M,R,Q,H,C,P,N)$, the
local $\NameCert_{\CriticalLineWitnessUp}$ surface consists exactly of the
following five obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier exposure of $Z,S,M,R,Q,H,C,P,N$;
\item source-budget exactness for the zero-strip source and source budget;
\item determinacy of the modulus and readback route;
\item zero-strip refusal non-escape;
\item downstream stability through $H,C,P,N$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First project the accepted packet to its carrier coordinates. The
source-budget carrier theorem
\autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-source-budget-carrier} supplies carrier
exposure and fixes the zero-strip source and budget as visible packet rows.

Next read the route coordinates. The source-route totality theorem
\autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-source-route-totality} and the root bridge
readback totality theorem
\autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-root-bridge-readback-totality} determine
the modulus and readback path through the same finite packet.

It remains to isolate the refusal and downstream clauses. The zero-strip
refusal theorem
\autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-zero-strip-refusal} gives non-escape for
the refusal row, while the displayed $H,C,P,N$ coordinates carry transport,
continuation, provenance, and naming stability. These five rows exhaust the
local $\NameCert_{\CriticalLineWitnessUp}$ surface.
\end{proof}
