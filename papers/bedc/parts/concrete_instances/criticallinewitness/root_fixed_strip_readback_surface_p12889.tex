\begin{theorem}[CriticalLineWitness fixed-strip source readback totality]
\label{thm:critical-line-witness-fixed-strip-source-readback-totality}
Let $W=(Z,S,M,R,Q,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted
$\CriticalLineWitnessUp$ packet whose source is the fixed critical strip.
Before any zeta-facing or $\mathsf{RH}$-facing consumer can inspect the
packet, the read must pass through the displayed $\BHist$ source rows $Z,S$,
the rational-height and modulus rows $R,Q,M$, the zero-strip localization
row $Z$, and the local $\NameCert_{\CriticalLineWitnessUp}$ row $N$ along
the structural route $H,C,P$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CriticalLineWitnessUp.CriticalLineWitnessCarrier\_fixed\_strip\_source\_readback\_totality}
\begin{proof}
Project the packet through the root obligation carrier of
\autoref{def:critical-line-witness-root-obligation-carrier}. The carrier
exposes only $Z,S,M,R,Q,H,C,P,N$, so the fixed-strip source is read as a
displayed $\BHist$ row rather than as a host critical-line theorem.

Apply \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-root-classifier-stability}. The
same $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ route keeps the source row, rational-height row,
modulus row, and zero-strip localization row attached to the accepted packet
before downstream inspection.

Finally use the NameCert completion row
\autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-root-namecert-obligation-completion} and
the zeta dependency lattice \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-zeta-link}.
Together they force zeta and $\mathsf{RH}$ consumers to read the displayed
source, modulus, zero-strip, provenance, and local naming rows before
inspecting the witness surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CriticalLineWitness zero-strip modulus separation]
\label{thm:critical-line-witness-zero-strip-modulus-separation}
For every accepted $\CriticalLineWitnessUp$ packet
$W=(Z,S,M,R,Q,H,C,P,N)$, the zero-strip refusal row is separated from the
positive analytic-continuation payload by the displayed modulus ledger
$M,R,Q,H,C,P,N$ and by the $\hsame$-stable classifier of the root obligation
surface. Transport along $H$ and replay along $C$ therefore cannot turn a
refusal row into an analytic-zero witness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the classifier stability row
\autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-root-classifier-stability}. It keeps any
root-facing classifier inside the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$,
$\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ route.

Next apply the root ledger nonescape theorem
\autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-root-ledger-nonescape}. The ledger has no
coordinate for analytic continuation, host zero extraction, quotient
representatives, or ambient completion, so its refusal row cannot be read as
a positive analytic payload.

It remains to separate downstream zeta reads from the refusal row. Use
\autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-zeta-link} together with
\autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-rh-refusal-readback-totality}. They place
zeta and $\mathsf{RH}$ consumers behind the same finite modulus and
zero-strip readback route, leaving transport and replay unable to manufacture
an analytic-zero witness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CriticalLineWitness RH-refusal consumer nonescape]
\label{thm:critical-line-witness-rh-refusal-consumer-nonescape}
Every downstream $\mathsf{RH}$-boundary consumer of an accepted
$\CriticalLineWitnessUp$ packet may cite the fixed-strip source row $S$, the
zero-strip refusal row $Z$, the modulus ledger $M,R,Q$, the $\Cont$ route
$C$, the $\Pkg$ provenance row $P$, and the local
$\NameCert_{\CriticalLineWitnessUp}$ row $N$. Such a consumer cannot extract
a host zero, an analytic continuation theorem, a classical contradiction, a
quotient stream, or an ambient completeness datum from those rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the consumer through the completed NameCert obligation
\autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-root-namecert-obligation-completion}. It
supplies the fixed-strip, zero-strip, modulus, transport, route, provenance,
and naming rows as one finite local packet.

Apply \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-root-ledger-nonescape}. This removes
analytic-continuation theorems, $\mathsf{RH}$ proofs, host-limit data,
quotient representatives, and standard-bridge objects from the root-facing
export.

Finally combine the downstream counter-surface
\autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-downstream-counter-surface} with the
$\mathsf{RH}$-refusal readback totality row
\autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-rh-refusal-readback-totality}. The
consumer may cite the displayed rows, but every extraction request for a host
zero, analytic theorem, classical contradiction, quotient stream, or ambient
completeness datum stops at the finite refusal ledger.
\end{proof}
