\begin{theorem}[Critical-line witness root zero carrier]
\label{thm:critical-line-witness-root-zero-carrier}
Let $W=(Z,S,M,R,Q,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CriticalLineWitnessUp$ packet. A root read of $W$ exposes only the finite zero-strip carrier rows over $\BHist$, $\RatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\ZetaZerosUp$, and $\CritStripUp$: the supplied zero row $Z$, the critical-strip row $S$, the displayed modulus row $M$, the real-part row $R$, the rational comparison ledger $Q$, and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:critical-line-witness-carrier}. The rows $Z,S,M,R,Q,H,C,P,N$ are the whole accepted packet, and the root-facing rows are read as finite $\BHist$ coordinates rather than as host analytic data.

Use the supplied zero-strip route of \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-supplied-zero-strip-route} together with the root modulus-window totality of \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-root-modulus-window-totality}. The first row keeps $Z,S$ inside the $\ZetaZerosUp$ and $\CritStripUp$ boundary supplied by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-zeta-zeros-namecert}; the second keeps $M,R,Q$ inside the $\RatUp$ and $\RealUp$ comparison boundary supplied by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}.

The root refusal downstream gate of \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-root-refusal-downstream-gate} then removes every coordinate not displayed in the finite packet. Hence the root read exposes exactly the zero-strip carrier rows listed above.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Critical-line witness strip modulus readback]
\label{thm:critical-line-witness-strip-modulus-readback}
Every strip-modulus consumer of an accepted $\CriticalLineWitnessUp$ packet reads the displayed modulus and zero-strip rows through the route
$$
Z,S,M,R,Q,H,C,P,N.
$$
In particular, the consumer passes through $\Cont$, componentwise $\hsame$, $\Pkg$, and the local $\NameCert_{\CriticalLineWitnessUp}$ rows before any RH-facing boundary is available.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the root zero carrier of \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-root-zero-carrier}. It identifies the zero row, strip row, modulus row, comparison ledger, and structural rows as the only carrier data a root consumer may read.

Apply \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-root-zero-strip-route} for the $Z,S$ part of the read, and apply \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-root-modulus-ledger} for the $M,R,Q$ part. These sibling rows place the zero-strip and modulus requests under the same displayed $\hsame$ transport, $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ row.

Finally use \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-rh-nonescape-package}. It places any RH-facing read downstream of the same finite package, so the consumer cannot reach an RH boundary before the displayed strip-modulus route has been read.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Critical-line witness zero-strip consumer boundary]
\label{thm:critical-line-witness-zero-strip-consumer-boundary}
Every downstream RH or zero-count consumer of an accepted $\CriticalLineWitnessUp$ packet receives only the displayed zero-strip witness package
$$
Z,S,M,R,Q,H,C,P,N.
$$
The consumer receives no analytic continuation certificate, no host complex equality, no global zero oracle, no quotient representative, and no proof of $\mathsf{RH}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-strip-modulus-readback}. It forces every strip-modulus consumer through the displayed zero, strip, modulus, comparison, transport, continuation, provenance, and naming rows.

Next use the downstream refusal package of \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-downstream-refusal-package} and the RH refusal ledger completeness of \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-rh-refusal-ledger-completeness}. These rows exclude analytic continuation, host equality, quotient representatives, global zero classification, and RH proof data from downstream reads.

The carrier dependencies remain the finite $\ZetaZerosUp$, $\CritStripUp$, $\RatUp$, and $\RealUp$ rows described by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-zeta-zeros-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. Thus an RH or zero-count consumer receives only the displayed witness package and none of the analytic or host-level objects listed above.
\end{proof}
