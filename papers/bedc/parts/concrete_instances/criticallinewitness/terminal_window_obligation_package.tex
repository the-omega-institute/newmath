\label{thm:critical-line-witness-terminal-window-factorization}

strip, source window, modulus comparison, $\RatUp$ readback, $\RealUp$ readback,
$\hsame$ transport, $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$
rows. The factorization surface is the finite packet
$$

$$
over the terminal source and comparison ledgers; it has no entry point from a

oracle, analytic-continuation theorem, or ambient completeness principle.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Begin with terminal source exhaustion,
\autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-terminal-source-exhaustion}. It makes the
zero strip and source window rows $Z,S$ the first visible terminal surface and
keeps the route inside $H,C,P,N$.

Next apply the terminal comparison ledger lock,
\autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-terminal-comparison-ledger-lock}. The
modulus comparison and $\RatUp/\RealUp$ readback rows are exactly $M,R,Q$ over
the same transport, continuation, provenance, and naming surface.

Finally use terminal RH-refusal exactness,
\autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-terminal-rh-refusal-exactness}. It rules out
the analytic, quotient, oracle, and completeness rows, so the terminal route
factors only through the displayed finite packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Critical-line witness terminal ledger nonescape]
\label{thm:critical-line-witness-terminal-ledger-nonescape}
For every accepted terminal $\CriticalLineWitnessUp$ packet, analytic
continuation content, $\mathsf{RH}$ proof content, quotient representatives,
hidden numeric oracles, host equality, and ambient completeness are refused
unless they already occur as displayed rows of the finite terminal ledger
$Z,S,M,R,Q,H,C,P,N$. Since the ledger contains only the zero-strip source,
source-window, modulus-comparison, readback, transport, continuation,
provenance, and naming rows, none of those external objects can be recovered by
a downstream terminal consumer.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First read the source side from
\autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-terminal-source-exhaustion}. Before any
terminal consumer reaches a comparison row, the displayed zero strip and source
window have already exhausted the available source data.

Then read the comparison side from
\autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-terminal-comparison-ledger-lock}. It locks
the modulus and readback ledger to $M,R,Q,H,C,P,N$ and names no external
analytic or host-level object.

Finally apply terminal RH-refusal exactness,
\autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-terminal-rh-refusal-exactness}. The terminal
boundary is a finite witness boundary rather than an $\mathsf{RH}$ proof,
analytic-continuation certificate, quotient representative, oracle, or
completeness surface, so the ledger cannot escape its displayed rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Critical-line witness terminal consumer exhaustion]
\label{thm:critical-line-witness-terminal-consumer-exhaustion}
Every downstream RH-boundary consumer of an accepted terminal
$\CriticalLineWitnessUp$ packet receives exactly the terminal window
factorization of \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-terminal-window-factorization}

\begin{proof}

\autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-terminal-source-exhaustion} and
\autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-terminal-comparison-ledger-lock}. Together

\autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-terminal-window-factorization}. This gives
the consumer the finite packet surface $Z,S,M,R,Q,H,C,P,N$ as its only terminal

Finally attach terminal ledger nonescape and terminal RH-refusal exactness,
\autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-terminal-ledger-nonescape} and
