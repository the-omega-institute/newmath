\begin{theorem}[CriticalLineWitness Phase Real dependency totality]
\label{thm:critical-line-witness-phase-real-dependency-totality}
Let $W=(Z,S,M,R,Q,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CriticalLineWitnessUp$ carrier. Every Phase Real or Completion read that consumes $W$ first factors through the displayed supplied zero-strip route, modulus ledger entry, zeta dependency lattice, and RH refusal boundary:
$$
Z,S,M,R,Q,H,C,P,N.
$$
The read may use this packet as a finite source-modulus dependency surface, but it has no coordinate for analytic continuation, host zero search, quotient zero sets, selected host limits, ambient completeness, or an $\mathsf{RH}$ proof row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier of \autoref{def:critical-line-witness-carrier}. Its only public coordinates are the supplied zero row $Z$, critical-strip row $S$, modulus row $M$, real-part row $R$, rational-comparison row $Q$, componentwise transport $H$, displayed continuation $C$, package provenance $P$, and local naming row $N$.

Apply the supplied zero-strip route of \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-supplied-zero-strip-route} and the modulus ledger entry of \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-modulus-ledger-entry}. Together they force the source side and the Real-rational comparison side of every consumer read through the same finite packet.

Next use the zeta dependency lattice of \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-zeta-link}. It places any zero-strip or critical-line read behind the existing $\ZetaZerosUp$ and RH-boundary surfaces before a modulus witness is public.

Finally apply the RH non-escape package of \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-rh-nonescape-package}. It removes analytic continuation, host zero search, quotient representatives, ambient completeness, and $\mathsf{RH}$ proof content from the accepted route. Hence the Phase Real and Completion dependency route is exhausted by the displayed rows of $W$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CriticalLineWitness zero-source Real readback factorization]
\label{thm:critical-line-witness-zero-source-real-readback-factorization}
For an accepted $\CriticalLineWitnessUp$ carrier $W=(Z,S,M,R,Q,H,C,P,N)$, every Real-facing readback from the supplied zero source factors through the route
$$
Z,S,H,C,P,N
\quad\hbox{then}\quad
M,R,Q,H,C,P,N.
$$
Thus the zero source and the Real-rational modulus ledger are read as two faces of the same $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ packet; no consumer can identify the zero source with a Real endpoint or bypass the modulus ledger.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the supplied source side through \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-supplied-zero-strip-route}. The rows $Z,S,H,C,P,N$ are the accepted zero-strip route and are already tied to the local $\NameCert_{\CriticalLineWitnessUp}$ row.

Read the Real-facing side through \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-modulus-ledger-entry}. The rows $M,R,Q,H,C,P,N$ form the finite modulus, real-part, and rational-comparison ledger available to a downstream consumer.

The carrier of \autoref{def:critical-line-witness-carrier} stores both routes in the same packet, with shared transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows. A readback that keeps the zero source but drops the modulus ledger therefore leaves the accepted carrier surface.

The non-escape clause follows from \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-rh-nonescape-package} and \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-zeta-link}. They prevent the zero source from becoming a host Real endpoint, quotient representative, selected limit, or analytic certificate outside the displayed route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CriticalLineWitness RH refusal phase boundary]
\label{thm:critical-line-witness-rh-refusal-phase-boundary}
Every $\mathsf{RH}$-facing Phase Real or Completion request to an accepted $\CriticalLineWitnessUp$ carrier is bounded by the same finite refusal surface
$$
Z,S,M,R,Q,H,C,P,N.
$$
The boundary may expose the supplied zero-strip route, the modulus ledger entry, the zeta dependency lattice, and the local refusal package. It does not export an $\mathsf{RH}$ truth value, zero-location theorem, analytic-continuation certificate, quotient zero-set equality, selected limit, or ambient-completeness row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the carrier row of \autoref{def:critical-line-witness-carrier}. A request to the packet can inspect only the rows $Z,S,M,R,Q,H,C,P,N$.

Use \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-supplied-zero-strip-route} for the source side and \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-modulus-ledger-entry} for the modulus side. These two rows give the full positive data visible to a Phase Real or Completion consumer.

Now apply the zeta dependency lattice of \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-zeta-link}. It records that zero-strip and critical-line reads depend on existing zeta and RH boundary surfaces rather than on a standalone zeta-function object.

Finally use \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-rh-nonescape-package}. The package classifies $\mathsf{RH}$ proofs, zero searches, quotient representatives, analytic continuation, and analytic completeness as absent from the accepted carrier. The stated refusal boundary follows.
\end{proof}
