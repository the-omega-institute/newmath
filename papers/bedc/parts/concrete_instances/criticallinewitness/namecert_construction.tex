\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CriticalLineWitnessUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-criticallinewitness-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\CriticalLineWitnessUp$ interface records the finite packet a constructive Riemann Hypothesis witness would expose after a zero has already been supplied in the critical strip. It does not prove $\mathsf{RH}$ and does not produce a zero. It only names the BEDC-native surface of the witness type in \autoref{thm:rh-witness-modulus} and the restriction type in \autoref{thm:rh-witness-critical-line-restriction}.

\begin{definition}[Critical-line witness carrier]
\label{def:critical-line-witness-carrier}
A $\CriticalLineWitnessUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
W=(Z,S,M,R,Q,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $Z$ is the supplied $\ZetaZerosUp$ zero row, displaying the zero history and its zero-classifier witness;
\item $S$ is the supplied $\CritStripUp$ critical-strip row for the same complex history, displaying the left and right strip comparisons;
\item $M$ is the on-critical-line modulus row, sending each depth $n:\NatUp$ to an inspected rational depth at which the real-part representative is within $2^{-n}$ of $1/2$;
\item $R$ is the $\RealUp$ real-part row consumed by the modulus;
\item $Q$ is the $\RatUp$ comparison ledger containing the half-line rational row, dyadic tolerance row, and strict comparison row used by $M$;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the zero row, critical-strip row, real-part row, rational comparison ledger, and modulus row;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes through which consumers inspect those rows;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ZetaZerosUp$, $\CritStripUp$, $\RealUp$, $\RatUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and the supplied analytic certificates;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CriticalLineWitnessUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exposes a witness packet for a supplied zero-in-strip input. It contains no proof that every zero in the critical strip has such a packet, no zero-producing search, no host complex equality, no quotient real equality, no selected representative of a Cauchy class, and no appeal to ambient analytic completeness.
\end{definition}
