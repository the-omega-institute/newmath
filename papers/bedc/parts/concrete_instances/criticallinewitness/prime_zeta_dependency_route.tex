\begin{proposition}[CriticalLineWitness prime and zeta dependency route]
\label{prop:critical-line-witness-prime-zeta-dependency-route}
Every $\CriticalLineWitnessUp$ packet that is consumed by an RH-facing refusal route first reads the arithmetic divisibility surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-prime-namecert} and the zeta-facing zero-strip source rows displayed by the existing CriticalLineWitness lattice. The prime row supplies the finite divisibility and Euler-product-facing source discipline, while the zeta rows supply zero-strip localization, source windows, and refusal budgets. Only after those rows are present may the RH-facing consumer inspect terminal refusal, fixed-strip, or continuation data.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
Project the CriticalLineWitness hub through its source, modulus, zero-strip, and zeta sibling routes. Those routes name the arithmetic and analytic source rows before any terminal RH-facing refusal packet is read.

The prime dependency is supplied by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-prime-namecert}. Its divisibility and factorization surface is the arithmetic source for zeta-facing rows, not a consequence of a later RH refusal.

The zeta-facing rows then carry fixed-strip and zero-strip source data into the CriticalLineWitness packet. Since the carrier route exposes these rows before terminal refusal, an RH-facing consumer cannot bypass PrimeUp divisibility or the zeta source ledger.
\end{proof}
