\begin{theorem}[CriticalLineWitness finite observation route]
\label{thm:critical-line-witness-finite-observation-route}
Let $W=(Z,S,M,R,Q,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CriticalLineWitnessUp$ packet. Every downstream finite-observation read of $W$ must expose the finite zero-strip row $Z$, critical-strip row $S$, rational-height and comparison rows $R,Q$, zeta-continuation dependency surface, and root-comparison rows before any downstream consumer reads the packet. No such read can bypass those rows through a host zero classifier, global analytic continuation object, quotient representative, ambient completeness witness, or detached $\mathsf{RH}$ truth value.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the root source triad of \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-root-source-triad}. It exposes the supplied zero row, critical-strip row, and source-facing comparison ledger as finite rows before downstream access.

Use the zero-strip source completion rows, including \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-zero-strip-source-totality}, to keep the zero-strip and critical-line sources on the displayed finite route. Use the root comparison ledger of \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-root-comparison-ledger} to attach the rational-height and comparison reads through $R,Q,H,C,P,N$.

Finally apply the zeta dependency lattice of \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-zeta-link}. It places the zeta-zero and continuation-facing dependency surfaces before the modulus witness is public, so every downstream finite-observation read factors through the displayed packet rather than an analytic or host-level replacement.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CriticalLineWitness StdBridge candidate refusal]
\label{thm:critical-line-witness-stdbridge-candidate-refusal}
For an accepted $\CriticalLineWitnessUp$ packet $W=(Z,S,M,R,Q,H,C,P,N)$, a proposed standard bridge candidate that lacks the zero-strip source rows, modulus rows, or finite readback rows is a failed premise for the bridge route, not a counterinstance to the $\CriticalLineWitnessUp$ witness surface. The packet refuses the missing zero-strip, modulus, and readback demands inside its displayed finite ledger.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the candidate first through the root refusal route, \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-root-refusal-route}. That route sends off-surface demands to the displayed finite refusal boundary rather than treating them as alternate bridge data.

Next apply the zero-strip source completion and downstream readback exhaustion rows, including \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-zero-strip-source-totality} and \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-route-readback-exhaustion}. Missing zero-strip or readback data therefore means that the candidate has not supplied the premises required by the accepted packet route.

The root comparison ledger of \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-root-comparison-ledger} supplies the same conclusion for modulus and rational comparison reads. Since the candidate lacks required displayed rows, it fails before any standard bridge surface is reached and cannot witness a bridge counterinstance.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CriticalLineWitness dependency ledger exhaustion]
\label{thm:critical-line-witness-dependency-ledger-exhaustion}
Every downstream $\CriticalLineWitnessUp$ read factors through the displayed dependency rows supplied by $\CritStripUp$, $\GammaUp$, $\NatUp$, $\RatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\ZetaContinuationWitnessUp$, and $\ZetaZerosUp$. The accepted packet $W=(Z,S,M,R,Q,H,C,P,N)$ exhausts these dependencies through its finite zero-strip, modulus, rational-height, root-comparison, transport, continuation, package, and local $\NameCert$ rows, with no additional analytic, host-equality, quotient, or ambient-completeness route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the root source triad and the root comparison ledger, \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-root-source-triad} and \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-root-comparison-ledger}. They expose the $\CritStripUp$, $\NatUp$, $\RatUp$, and $\RealUp$ rows through the displayed packet coordinates.

Use the zero-strip source completion rows for the $\ZetaZerosUp$ side and the zeta dependency lattice of \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-zeta-link} for the $\ZetaContinuationWitnessUp$ side. These rows force zero-strip and continuation-facing reads to consume sibling dependency surfaces before the local modulus witness is public.

Finally combine the root refusal route with the downstream readback exhaustion theorem, \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-root-refusal-route} and \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-route-readback-exhaustion}. The remaining $\GammaUp$ and readback dependencies stay inside the finite ledger, and every forbidden analytic, host-equality, quotient, or ambient-completeness demand is refused rather than exported.
\end{proof}
