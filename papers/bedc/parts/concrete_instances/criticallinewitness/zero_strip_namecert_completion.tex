\begin{theorem}[CriticalLineWitness zero-strip carrier]
\label{thm:critical-line-witness-zero-strip-carrier}
Let $W=(Z,S,M,R,Q,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CriticalLineWitnessUp$ packet. The packet exposes a finite zero-strip source row over $\CritStripUp$, $\GammaUp$, $\NatUp$, $\RatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\ZetaContinuationWitnessUp$, and $\ZetaZerosUp$, with the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert_{\CriticalLineWitnessUp}$ coordinates. This row records the finite source surface; it does not import a proof of $\mathsf{RH}$ or any analytic continuation principle as carrier data.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier to its displayed rows $Z,S,M,R,Q,H,C,P,N$. The zero-strip source row is read through $Z$ and $S$, while the numerical and comparison rows are read through $M,R,Q$.

The surrounding root source and strip-source obligation rows already bind those coordinates to the local $\BHist$ packet. Transport through $H$, continuation through $C$, provenance through $P$, and the local naming row $N$ keep the carrier inside the finite $\CriticalLineWitnessUp$ surface.

The refusal boundary is part of the same packet. It prevents $\mathsf{RH}$ proof data, analytic continuation principles, quotient representatives, host equality, and ambient completeness from becoming carrier fields.
\end{proof}

\label{thm:critical-line-witness-zeta-comparison-ledger}

\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CriticalLineWitnessUp.CriticalLineWitnessCarrier\_zeta\_comparison\_ledger}

The ledger is read through $H,C,P,N$. Componentwise $\hsame$ transport aligns the source rows, the displayed $\Cont$ route records the admissible readback, and $\Pkg$ with $\NameCert$ authenticates the local packet surface.

No host complex equality or classical zero selection is a coordinate of the accepted packet. The ledger therefore remains a finite displayed comparison row rather than an analytic zero-selection mechanism.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CriticalLineWitness NameCert obligation package]
\label{thm:critical-line-witness-namecert-obligation-package}
For an accepted $\CriticalLineWitnessUp$ packet, the carrier row, zero-strip source row, zeta-comparison ledger, refusal boundary, and downstream readback rows form the local $\NameCert_{\CriticalLineWitnessUp}$ obligation surface. Every downstream root, strip, zeta, or $\mathsf{RH}$-facing read must pass through that surface before it can consume any displayed packet coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CriticalLineWitnessUp.CriticalLineWitnessCarrier\_namecert\_obligation\_package}
\begin{proof}
The carrier row supplies the finite packet coordinates $Z,S,M,R,Q,H,C,P,N$. The zero-strip source row and zeta-comparison ledger identify the source and comparison portions of the same packet.

The refusal boundary records what the packet does not export: $\mathsf{RH}$ proof data, analytic continuation as data, host equality, quotient representatives, global zero selection, and ambient completeness. These exclusions keep every off-route demand at the local ledger boundary.

Finally the downstream readback rows replay the same $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ coordinates. Hence the local naming certificate surface is exactly the package assembled from the carrier, source, comparison, refusal, and readback rows.
\end{proof}
