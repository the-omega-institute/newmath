\begin{theorem}[CriticalLineWitness source-signature readback]
\label{thm:critical-line-witness-source-signature-readback}
Let $W=(Z,S,M,R,Q,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CriticalLineWitnessUp$ packet. Every accepted source-signature readback of $W$ reads the critical-strip source only through the displayed finite source rows $Z,S$, the signature and gap rows of \autoref{def:critical-line-witness-signature-gap-carrier}, and the zeta, $\RealUp$, and $\RatUp$ dependency routes recorded by $M,R,Q,H,C,P,N$. No accepted readback can replace those rows by an unledgered critical-strip conclusion, analytic-continuation certificate, host zero witness, quotient representative, or ambient completeness datum.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the signature-gap carrier \autoref{def:critical-line-witness-signature-gap-carrier}. It identifies the source signature as the same finite packet $Z,S,M,R,Q,H,C,P,N$ and places the gap row between $Z,S$ and $M,R,Q$ under the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ surface.

Apply the signature-gap exactness theorem \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-signature-gap-exactness}. It forces every admitted signature-gap read to factor through the supplied zero row, critical-strip row, modulus row, real-part row, rational comparison ledger, transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows.

Finally use the zeta dependency lattice \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-zeta-link} together with the root modulus readback row \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-root-modulus-readback}. These rows place the zeta-facing, $\RealUp$-facing, and $\RatUp$-facing dependencies behind the displayed packet route, so the source-signature readback has no coordinate for a hidden critical-strip theorem or host analytic witness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CriticalLineWitness GAP-policy stability]
\label{thm:critical-line-witness-gap-policy-stability}
Let $W=(Z,S,M,R,Q,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CriticalLineWitnessUp$ packet. Transport by the displayed $\hsame$ row $H$ and replay by the displayed $\Cont$ row $C$ preserve the refusal rows and the gap-policy rows of \autoref{def:critical-line-witness-signature-gap-carrier}. They do not convert an absent zero row, a refused $\mathsf{RH}$ request, or a missing analytic-continuation route into a zero witness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the policy row from \autoref{def:critical-line-witness-signature-gap-carrier}. The gap separates the source side $Z,S$ from the modulus and comparison side $M,R,Q$ while keeping both sides under the same transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows.

Use \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-signature-gap-nonescape}. It records that the signature-gap readback cannot import an $\mathsf{RH}$ proof, zero search, analytic-continuation certificate, host equality, quotient representative, or ambient completeness witness.

Now apply the ledger route stability theorem \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-ledger-route-stability}. Since $H,C,P,N$ preserve the same $M,R,Q$ comparison route, transport and replay can only move the displayed gap-policy row along the accepted packet route.

The refusal side is therefore stable as refusal. The root unblock refusal boundary \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-root-unblock-refusal-boundary} keeps refused RH, analytic, host, quotient, and completeness requests outside the packet, so $\hsame/\Cont$ transport cannot turn those refusals into a zero witness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CriticalLineWitness NameCert route exhaustion]
\label{thm:critical-line-witness-namecert-route-exhaustion}
For an accepted $\CriticalLineWitnessUp$ packet $W=(Z,S,M,R,Q,H,C,P,N)$, every accepted consumer route factors through the displayed $\BHist$ carrier, $\Cont$ replay row, $\hsame$ transport row, $\Pkg$ provenance row, and local $\NameCert_{\CriticalLineWitnessUp}$ row. The route can consume only the source, signature, gap, modulus, real-part, and rational-comparison rows already displayed in $W$; it cannot read an unledgered $\mathsf{RH}$ conclusion or an unledgered critical-strip conclusion.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the accepted carrier and the NameCert obligation surface. The carrier \autoref{def:critical-line-witness-carrier} exposes only $Z,S,M,R,Q,H,C,P,N$, and \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-namecert-obligations} attaches the local naming obligations to those same rows.

Next combine the route readback exhaustion theorem \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-route-readback-exhaustion} with the downstream consumer unblock theorem \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-downstream-consumer-unblock}. Together they say that every downstream consumer reads the finite surface already displayed by the packet and cannot branch to host equality, analytic continuation, quotient representatives, ambient completeness, global zero classification, or an $\mathsf{RH}$ proof.

It remains to include the source-signature and gap-policy rows. The source-signature readback theorem above confines the critical-strip source to the displayed finite signature route, and the GAP-policy stability theorem above keeps refusals stable under $H$ and $C$.

Thus every accepted route factors through $\BHist$, $\Cont$, $\hsame$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ over the displayed packet rows. Any route that asks for an unledgered $\mathsf{RH}$ or critical-strip conclusion is not an accepted $\CriticalLineWitnessUp$ consumer route.
\end{proof}
