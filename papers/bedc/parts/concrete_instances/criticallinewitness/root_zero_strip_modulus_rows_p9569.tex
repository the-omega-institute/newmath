\begin{theorem}[Critical-line witness root zero-strip carrier row]
\label{thm:critical-line-witness-root-zero-strip-carrier-row}
For an accepted $\CriticalLineWitnessUp$ packet, the root-facing zero, strip, modulus, route, transport, package, and NameCert coordinates are one finite $\BHist$ carrier row:
$$
W=(Z,S,M,R,Q,H,C,P,N).
$$
Carrier admission ties $Z$ to the supplied $\ZetaZerosUp$ row, $S$ to the supplied $\CritStripUp$ row, $M$ to the on-line modulus row, $R$ to the $\RealUp$ real-part row, $Q$ to the $\RatUp$ comparison ledger, $H$ to componentwise $\hsame$ transport, $C$ to displayed $\Cont$ replay, $P$ to $\Pkg$ provenance, and $N$ to the local $\NameCert_{\CriticalLineWitnessUp}$ row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-obligation-carrier-admission-row}. It admits exactly the finite packet $Z,S,M,R,Q,H,C,P,N$ and excludes zero search, global zero classifiers, host equality, quotient representatives, and analytic-completeness coordinates.

Use the root carrier projection of \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-root-carrier-projection} and the downstream package of \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-root-downstream-package} to read the same tuple from the root-facing side. They keep the supplied zero row, critical-strip row, modulus row, comparison ledger, transport, route, provenance, and naming coordinates in one visible carrier.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-obligation-closure-package}. The closure package repacks the admitted carrier, NameCert obligations, strip-zero compatibility, modulus routing, non-escape boundary, and ledger exactness as the finite obligation surface available to downstream root consumers.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Critical-line witness root modulus route row]
\label{thm:critical-line-witness-root-modulus-route-row}
Every root-facing modulus comparison of an accepted $\CriticalLineWitnessUp$ packet passes through the displayed route
$$
M,R,Q,H,C,P,N.
$$
The comparison must use the $\Cont$ row $C$ and the componentwise $\hsame$ row $H$ before the modulus row $M$ or rational comparison ledger $Q$ can be exported; it has no route through a hidden numeric oracle, host real equality, quotient representative, analytic-continuation certificate, or ambient completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the modulus request factorization of \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-modulus-request-factorization} and the modulus comparison route totality of \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-modulus-comparison-route-totality}. They force each depth or comparison request through the displayed modulus row, real-part row, rational comparison ledger, transport row, continuation row, provenance row, and local naming row.

Next use the root comparison ledger of \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-root-comparison-ledger} and the root modulus ledger of \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-root-modulus-ledger}. These rows identify $Q$ as the root-facing rational comparison ledger and keep the depth request inside $M,R,Q,H,C,P,N$.

Apply \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-obligation-closure-package} to repack the route as part of the local obligation surface. Since that package admits no hidden oracle, host equality, quotient representative, analytic-continuation certificate, or ambient completeness row, every modulus comparison remains on the displayed $\Cont$ and $\hsame$ route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Critical-line witness root non-escape ledger row]
\label{thm:critical-line-witness-root-nonescape-ledger-row}
The root-facing ledger of an accepted $\CriticalLineWitnessUp$ packet excludes an $\mathsf{RH}$ proof, analytic-continuation certificate, quotient representative, host equality, and ambient completeness from every downstream read. A downstream consumer may cite only the finite carrier row $Z,S,M,R,Q,H,C,P,N$ together with the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ structure of that packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First read the root non-escape package of \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-root-nonescape-package} and the downstream refusal factorization of \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-downstream-refusal-factorization}. These rows state that root-facing consumers receive the supplied zero-strip route, modulus ledger route, and local BEDC rows, not RH proof data or analytic proof surfaces.

Next apply the root zero-strip carrier row established above. It identifies the downstream evidence as the finite packet $Z,S,M,R,Q,H,C,P,N$ and prevents the consumer from adding a second carrier behind the accepted witness.

Finally use \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-obligation-closure-package}. The package closes the local obligation surface around carrier admission, naming, transport, continuation, package provenance, non-escape, and ledger rows. Therefore $\mathsf{RH}$ proof data, analytic-continuation certificates, quotient representatives, host equality, and ambient completeness have no admitted ledger coordinate.
\end{proof}
