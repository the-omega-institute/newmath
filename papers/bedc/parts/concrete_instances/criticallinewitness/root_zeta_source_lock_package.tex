\begin{theorem}[CriticalLineWitness zeta source lock]
\label{thm:critical-line-witness-zeta-source-lock}
Let $W=(Z,S,M,R,Q,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CriticalLineWitnessUp$ packet.
Every zeta-facing read of $W$ factors through the supplied zero row $Z$, the
fixed critical-strip row $S$, the modulus row $M$, the rational comparison
row $Q$, and the displayed support rows $H,C,P,N$ before any
$\mathsf{RH}$-facing consumer can inspect it. The zeta-facing route is
therefore a finite source lock over $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and
the local $\NameCert_{\CriticalLineWitnessUp}$ row, not an analytic
continuation theorem or a host zero-classifier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the dependency lattice of
\autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-zeta-link}. It places every zeta or
critical-line read behind the supplied zero row and the fixed strip row before
any modulus witness is visible.

Use the carrier and local NameCert obligations in
\autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-namecert-obligations}. They bind the
modulus, rational comparison, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming
rows to the same accepted packet.

Finally apply the source-modulus public readiness surface,
\autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-source-modulus-public-readiness}. The
readiness route exposes $Z,S,M,R,Q,H,C,P,N$ together and leaves no field from
which a zeta-facing read could bypass the finite source lock before the
$\mathsf{RH}$ consumer sees it.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CriticalLineWitness source-modulus zeta readback]
\label{thm:critical-line-witness-source-modulus-zeta-readback}
For every accepted $\CriticalLineWitnessUp$ packet
$$
W=(Z,S,M,R,Q,H,C,P,N),
$$
the source surface and the modulus surface share the same displayed
$\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ support. A
zeta-facing readback may distinguish the supplied zero row, the fixed-strip
source row, the modulus row, the real-part row, and the rational comparison
row, but it cannot detach any of them from the shared support route
$H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet through the carrier and NameCert rows of
\autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-namecert-obligations}. The source and
modulus coordinates are rows of one finite packet, with $H,C,P,N$ carrying
transport, replay, provenance, and local naming.

Apply the zeta dependency lattice
\autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-zeta-link}. It keeps zero-strip and
critical-line reads on the same finite witness surface before the modulus
read is exposed.

Use \autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-source-modulus-public-readiness} to
read the source and modulus surfaces as one public route. Thus the zeta
readback can replay the individual rows only through their common
$\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CriticalLineWitness RH consumer source nonescape]
\label{thm:critical-line-witness-rh-consumer-source-nonescape}
Every $\mathsf{RH}$-facing consumer of an accepted
$\CriticalLineWitnessUp$ packet receives only the finite source, modulus, and
zeta packet
$$
Z,\quad S,\quad M,\quad R,\quad Q,\quad H,\quad C,\quad P,\quad N.
$$
The consumer receives no proof of $\mathsf{RH}$, analytic-continuation
theorem, host equality, quotient zero set, global zero classifier, ambient
completeness row, or standard bridge row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the source lock above. It forces zeta-facing inspection through
the supplied zero, fixed strip, modulus, comparison, and displayed support
rows before any $\mathsf{RH}$-facing consumer sees the packet.

Next use the source-modulus zeta readback theorem. It keeps the source and
modulus surfaces on the same $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and
$\NameCert$ route, so the consumer cannot detach a host source from the
finite readback.

Finally apply
\autoref{thm:critical-line-witness-source-modulus-consumer-nonescape}. That
consumer boundary removes analytic continuation, host equality, quotient
representatives, ambient completeness, zero search, selected host limits, and
$\mathsf{RH}$ proof content. Since the accepted packet also has no standard
bridge coordinate, the $\mathsf{RH}$-facing consumer receives only the finite
source, modulus, and zeta rows listed above.
\end{proof}
