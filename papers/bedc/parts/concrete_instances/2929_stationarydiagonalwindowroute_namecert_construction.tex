It keeps the rational seed, constant $\StreamNameUp$ row, $\RegSeqRatUp$ seal-consumption row, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observation ledger, real finite-window row, transport table, continuation routes, provenance, and local naming row in one finite boundary packet. \begin{definition}[Stationary diagonal window route carrier]
\label{def:stationary-diagonal-window-route-carrier}
A $\StationaryDiagonalWindowRouteUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
W=(Q,S,G,D,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $Q$ is the transported stationary $\RatUp$ seed row, including the two rational history carriers and their $\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$ row;
\item $S$ is the constant $\StreamNameUp$ window row induced by $Q$;
\item $G$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ finite seal-consumption row for the same window;
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observation ledger read from that window;
\item $R$ is the $\RealUp$ finite-window classifier row reached from $S,G,D$;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $Q,S,G,D,R,P,N$;
\item $C$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ routes from $Q$ to $S$, from $S$ to $G$, from $S$ to $D$, and from $D,S,G$ to $R$;
\item $P$ records $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\StationaryDiagonalWindowRouteUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier is a finite route packet for stationary window reads. It contains no coordinate for quotient streams, selected real limits, host real equality, non-stationary tails, or ambient completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Stationary diagonal window route NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:stationary-diagonal-window-route-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\StationaryDiagonalWindowRouteUp}$ surface for \autoref{def:stationary-diagonal-window-route-carrier} consists exactly of carrier admission for $W=(Q,S,G,D,R,H,C,P,N)$, stationary $\RatUp$ seed exposure, constant $\StreamNameUp$ window exposure, $\RegSeqRatUp$ seal-consumption exposure, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger exposure, $\RealUp$ finite-window classifier exposure, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ route replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local naming row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from the displayed tuple $W$. The rows $Q,S,G,D,R$ expose the stationary rational seed, constant stream window, regular-sequence seal row, dyadic observation ledger, and real-facing finite-window classifier.

The route obligations are read from $C$. They force every stream, regular-sequence, dyadic, and real-facing read to pass through the constant window induced by the same rational seed row $Q$.

The transport, provenance, and naming obligations are the rows $H,P,N$. Since the carrier has no coordinate for quotient streams, selected real limits, host equality, non-stationary tails, or ambient completeness, these displayed rows exhaust the local naming surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Stationary diagonal window route non-escape]
\label{thm:stationary-diagonal-window-route-nonescape}
Every transported stationary $\RealUp$ finite-window read exported by an accepted $\StationaryDiagonalWindowRouteUp$ carrier factors through the finite rows
$$

$$

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier and inspect its consumer-facing rows. The only route out of the stationary rational seed is the displayed continuation from $Q$ to the constant stream window $S$.

The regular-sequence and dyadic reads are then forced by the routes from $S$ to $G$ and from $S$ to $D$. The real-facing finite-window row $R$ receives only those rows together with the transport, provenance, and naming rows.

Thus any certified finite observation of the transported stationary diagonal is exhausted by the displayed route packet. No quotient stream comparison, selected limit, host real equality, non-stationary tail, or ambient completeness datum can escape from the carrier.
\end{proof}
