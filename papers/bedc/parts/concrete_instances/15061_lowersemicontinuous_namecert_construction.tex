Following Bishop and Bridges constructive analysis, lower semicontinuity is
read through lower bounds and finite epigraph witnesses rather than through a
host topology. The $\LowerSemicontinuousUp$ packet records that interface over
the lower-real surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-lowerreal-namecert},
the upper-real comparison boundary of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-upperreal-namecert}, the located readbacks of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedreal-namecert}, the modulus-bearing
map rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuousmap-namecert}, the
scheduled stream windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert},
the regular rational source rows of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal real-name
seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}.

\begin{definition}[LowerSemicontinuous carrier]
\label{def:lower-semicontinuous-carrier}
A $\LowerSemicontinuousUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
L=(X,F,E,W,R,O,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the source carrier row whose accepted points are read through
  displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ histories and their classifier transport;
\item $F$ is the function graph or map row, read through the
  $\ContinuousMapUp$ pointwise carrier only when such a map surface is present;
\item $E$ is the lower-real epigraph ledger, consisting of finite rows
  $(x,q)$ saying that the value at the displayed source point admits the
  rational lower bound $q$ at the inspected window;
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule row selecting finite source and
  value windows for each epigraph inspection;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row giving the regular rational
  value windows used by $E$;
\item $O$ is the $\LowerRealUp$, $\UpperRealUp$, and $\LocatedRealUp$
  comparison ledger used to read lower windows, upper exclusions, located
  endpoint checks, and finite lower-bound refinements;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for source histories,
  graph rows, lower-real epigraph rows, stream windows, regular readbacks,
  comparison ledgers, provenance rows, and local naming rows;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from source admission through
  window selection, regular rational readback, lower-real epigraph inspection,
  located readback, and real-seal handoff;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LowerSemicontinuousUp$,
  $\LowerRealUp$, $\UpperRealUp$, $\LocatedRealUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$,
  $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$,
  $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LowerSemicontinuousUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier relates two accepted packets exactly when their source rows,
finite epigraph windows, regular rational readbacks, lower-real comparisons,
located readbacks, $\RealUp$ seal rows, and provenance rows transport
componentwise along $H$ and replay through the same displayed routes in $C$.
No host topology, open-set lattice, quotient real equality, semantic limit
operator, or completeness principle is a carrier coordinate.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[LowerSemicontinuous NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:lower-semicontinuous-namecert-obligation-surface}
For an accepted $\LowerSemicontinuousUp$ carrier
$L=(X,F,E,W,R,O,H,C,P,N)$, the local
$\NameCert_{\LowerSemicontinuousUp}$ surface consists exactly of six
obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed source, graph, epigraph, schedule,
  readback, comparison, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item epigraph-window exactness, requiring every lower-bound read to appear
  as a finite $(x,q)$ row in $E$ after the schedule in $W$ and readback in
  $R$ have been replayed;
\item lower-real classifier stability, requiring lower refinements and
  rational-bound comparisons to be transported through the $\LowerRealUp$
  rows in $O$;
\item located readback compatibility, requiring every inspected source or
  value window to pass through the $\LocatedRealUp$ rows before a real-facing
  consumer can read it;
\item $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ replay, requiring all source, graph, epigraph,
  readback, comparison, and seal rows to move together under the displayed
  transport and continuation ledgers;
\item provenance and non-escape, stating that the package exports only the
  named finite rows and no host topology, quotient equality, semantic limit,
  open-cover compactness, countable choice, or $\RealUp$ completeness
  principle.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from
\autoref{def:lower-semicontinuous-carrier}. The packet lists exactly the
source row, graph row, epigraph ledger, finite schedule, regular readback,
comparison ledger, transport ledger, replay ledger, provenance row, and local
name.

Epigraph-window exactness is read from $E$ only after the requested source
window has passed through $W$ and the value readback through $R$. Thus a
lower-bound claim is not an external predicate; it is a displayed finite row
over the inspected source and rational bound.

Lower-real classifier stability is the role of $O$ together with $H$. The
lower-real comparisons, upper-side exclusions, and located endpoint checks
move componentwise by $\hsame$, and the continuation row $C$ records the same
inspection route after transport.

Located readback compatibility follows because every real-facing window is
read through the $\LocatedRealUp$ rows before the terminal $\RealUp$ seal is
available. The readback is therefore a finite stream and regular-rational
route, not a host limit operation.

Finally, $P$ and $N$ package those rows as the local certificate. Since the
carrier has no coordinate for the excluded principles, no consumer can obtain
them from the $\LowerSemicontinuousUp$ NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LowerSemicontinuous NameCert basis obligations]
\label{thm:lowersemicontinuous-namecert-obligation-basis}
For an accepted $\LowerSemicontinuousUp$ carrier
$L=(X,F,E,W,R,O,H,C,P,N)$, every root-unblock obligation that exposes
lower-real epigraph data to a downstream consumer is a finite row of the same
packet. The basis consists of the $\LowerRealUp$ epigraph ledger $E$, the
$\StreamNameUp$ schedule $W$, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback $R$, the
$UpperRealUp$ and $\LocatedRealUp$ comparison rows in $O$, the terminal
$\RealUp$ seal boundary, $\BHist$ histories, $\hsame$ transport, $\Cont$
replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local $\NameCert$ row.

No basis obligation is discharged by host topology, a semantic liminf
operator, quotient real equality, selected limits, countable choice, or
ambient completeness. Epigraph and comparison rows are finite carrier data
rather than host-theoretic evidence.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier in \autoref{def:lower-semicontinuous-carrier}. The only
epigraph data available to a consumer is the finite ledger $E$, and the only
inspection route to it is through the scheduled windows $W$ and regular
rational readbacks $R$.

The comparison row $O$ records lower-real, upper-real, and located-real
readbacks before the terminal $\RealUp$ seal is exposed. Thus every
root-unblock read remains inside the displayed carrier coordinates.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, package, and name the same
finite route. Since the excluded host principles are absent from
$L=(X,F,E,W,R,O,H,C,P,N)$, they cannot serve as basis obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LowerSemicontinuous Real seal nonescape]
\label{thm:lowersemicontinuous-real-seal-nonescape}
Let $L=(X,F,E,W,R,O,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted
$\LowerSemicontinuousUp$ carrier. Any $\RealUp$ readback from $L$ must pass
through the $\LowerRealUp$ epigraph row $E$, the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule
$W$, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback $R$, the $\UpperRealUp$ and
$\LocatedRealUp$ comparisons in $O$, the $\BHist$ history rows, $\hsame$
transport, $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local $\NameCert$ row.

The $\RealUp$ seal is therefore a finite carrier boundary. It is not a route
to host topology, a semantic limit, quotient real equality, selected limits,
countable choice, open-cover compactness, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a $\RealUp$-facing consumer of $L$. By
\autoref{def:lower-semicontinuous-carrier}, the consumer can inspect only the
displayed source, graph, epigraph, schedule, readback, comparison, transport,
replay, provenance, and naming rows.

Apply \autoref{thm:lower-semicontinuous-namecert-obligation-surface}. Its
non-escape obligation places the terminal real seal downstream from
epigraph-window exactness, lower-real classifier stability, located readback
compatibility, and replay.

Hence a real readback cannot detach from $E,W,R,O,H,C,P,N$. The excluded
principles have no coordinate in the carrier and no continuation row that
could introduce them.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LowerSemicontinuous UpperReal handoff]
\label{thm:lowersemicontinuous-upperreal-handoff}
For an accepted $\LowerSemicontinuousUp$ carrier
$L=(X,F,E,W,R,O,H,C,P,N)$, every $\UpperRealUp$ comparison handed to a
downstream lower-semicontinuity consumer is read from the same comparison

$\RealUp$ readbacks. The handoff preserves the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule,
$\RegSeqRatUp$ value windows, $\BHist$ histories, $\hsame$ transport, $\Cont$
replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ naming of the carrier.

Thus upper-side comparison is finite BEDC data paired with lower-real
epigraph rows. It is not an ambient order-completeness principle, a host
topology assertion, a semantic upper envelope, quotient real equality, a
selected limit, countable choice, or completeness of $\RealUp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the comparison ledger from
\autoref{def:lower-semicontinuous-carrier}. The row $O$ is the only carrier
coordinate that contains upper-real comparison data, and it is tied to the
same lower-real and located-real checks used by epigraph reads.

The schedule and readback rows $W$ and $R$ determine which finite value
windows the comparison may inspect. Transport through $H$ and replay through
$C$ preserve those windows together with the epigraph and comparison rows.

The provenance and local name rows $P,N$ certify this as a
$\LowerSemicontinuousUp$ handoff. Since the carrier has no coordinate for the
excluded host principles, the $\UpperRealUp$ consumer receives only the
finite comparison surface stated above.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Lower-real epigraph route]
\label{thm:lower-semicontinuous-lowerreal-epigraph-route}
Every lower-real or real-seal consumer of an accepted
$\LowerSemicontinuousUp$ carrier factors through the finite epigraph route
$$
X,F,W,R,E,O,H,C,P,N .
$$
The consumer may inspect scheduled source windows, regular rational value
readbacks, displayed lower-bound rows, lower-real refinements, located
endpoint checks, componentwise transports, continuation replay, provenance,
and local naming rows. It cannot bypass the epigraph ledger to read host
topological openness, quotient real equality, a semantic liminf operator,
countable choice, open-cover compactness, or completeness of $\RealUp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from a real-facing consumer of the packet. The source row $X$ and graph
row $F$ are visible only through the finite schedule $W$, so every value
inspection begins with a displayed source window rather than an ambient
neighbourhood.

The regular rational readback row $R$ then supplies the finite value window.
The epigraph ledger $E$ records the lower-bound row $(x,q)$ attached to that
same inspection, and the comparison ledger $O$ checks lower-real refinements,
upper-side exclusions, and located endpoint compatibility.

Transport and replay are exhausted by $H$ and $C$. They move the source,
graph, readback, epigraph, comparison, and seal rows together, so the route
remains the same after classifier transport.

The package and local name rows $P,N$ record exactly this route for consumers.
Since the excluded host principles are absent from the route and absent from
the carrier coordinates, the lower-real epigraph handoff exposes only the
finite BEDC rows stated above.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LowerSemicontinuous located readback stability]
\label{thm:lower-semicontinuous-located-readback-stability}
Let $L=(X,F,E,W,R,O,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted
$\LowerSemicontinuousUp$ carrier. Every epigraph-window comparison accepted by
$E$ and transported by $H$ preserves the $\LocatedRealUp$ endpoint checks in
$O$ before any $\RealUp$-facing consumer can read the transported lower bound.
Equivalently, a consumer that receives a transported row $(x,q)$ must pass
through the same scheduled window in $W$, the same regular rational readback in
$R$, and the located endpoint ledger in $O$ before the terminal real-seal route
is available.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier rows from \autoref{def:lower-semicontinuous-carrier}. The
only accepted lower-bound comparisons are the finite epigraph rows in $E$, and
the only transport available for those rows is the componentwise $\hsame$
ledger $H$.

Apply \autoref{thm:lower-semicontinuous-lowerreal-epigraph-route}. Its route
places $W$ and $R$ before $E$, and places the lower-real, upper-real, and
located-real comparison ledger $O$ before any terminal $\RealUp$ seal can be
read.

Thus the transported comparison cannot expose a bound by itself. The located
endpoint checks in $O$ remain on the route after transport, and $C$ replays the
same scheduled inspection for every Real-facing consumer.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LowerSemicontinuous real handoff]
\label{thm:lowersemicontinuous-real-handoff}
Every $\RealUp$-facing read of the root packet
$L_{\mathrm{root}}=(X,F,E,W,R,O,H,C,P,N)$ factors through the displayed
$\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\LowerRealUp$, $\LocatedRealUp$, and
$\RealUp$ rows. Concretely, a consumer first reads a stream window from $W$,
then a regular rational value row from $R$, then a lower-real epigraph witness
from $E$, then the located comparison data in $O$, and only then the terminal
real-seal route recorded by $O,H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The root packet is the carrier of
\autoref{def:lowersemicontinuous-root-carrier}, so a consumer can start only
from the rows $X,F,E,W,R,O,H,C,P,N$.

Apply \autoref{thm:lower-semicontinuous-namecert-obligation-surface}. Its
obligation surface places carrier admission, epigraph-window exactness,
lower-real classifier stability, located readback compatibility, transport
replay, provenance, and non-escape on the same local NameCert route.

Apply \autoref{thm:lower-semicontinuous-lowerreal-epigraph-route}. That route
orders the read through scheduled windows, regular rational readbacks,
displayed lower-bound rows, lower-real refinements, located endpoint checks,
transport, replay, provenance, and local naming.

Finally apply
\autoref{thm:lower-semicontinuous-located-readback-stability} and
\autoref{thm:lower-semicontinuous-real-seal-nonescape}. They keep the located
readback before the terminal real seal and exclude every host route not present
in the finite packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LowerSemicontinuous open-sublevel route]
\label{thm:lowersemicontinuous-open-sublevel-route}
Every sublevel or open-window read of the root packet
$L_{\mathrm{root}}=(X,F,E,W,R,O,H,C,P,N)$ is a displayed epigraph route through
$E,W,R,O,H,C,P,N$. It is not a read of host topological openness: the only
available witness is a finite lower-bound row transported through the
$\LowerRealUp$ and $\LocatedRealUp$ comparison ledgers and replayed by the same
continuation route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a sublevel or open-window consumer of the root packet. By
\autoref{def:lowersemicontinuous-root-carrier}, the packet has no coordinate
for a topology carrier or open-set lattice, so the consumer must read one of
the displayed finite rows.

Use \autoref{thm:lower-semicontinuous-lowerreal-epigraph-route}. The theorem
identifies the lower-real route as
$$
X,F,W,R,E,O,H,C,P,N .
$$
Thus an open-window read can only mean that a scheduled source window and a
regular rational value row have reached a displayed epigraph witness in $E$.

The located readback stability theorem keeps the comparison in $O$ on the same
route after transport. The real-seal nonescape theorem then prevents the
consumer from replacing that displayed epigraph route by host topology,
semantic liminf data, quotient real equality, choice, compactness, or ambient
completeness.
\end{proof}
