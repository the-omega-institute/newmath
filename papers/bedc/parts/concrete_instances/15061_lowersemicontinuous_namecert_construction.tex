\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\LowerSemicontinuousUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-lowersemicontinuous-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges constructive analysis, lower semicontinuity is
read through lower bounds and finite epigraph witnesses rather than through a
host topology. The $\LowerSemicontinuousUp$ packet records that interface over
the lower-real surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-lowerreal-namecert},
the upper-real comparison boundary of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-upperreal-namecert}, the located readbacks of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedreal-namecert}, the modulus-bearing
map rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuousmap-namecert}, the
scheduled stream windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert},
the regular rational source rows of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal real-name
seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}.

\begin{definition}[LowerSemicontinuous carrier]
\label{def:lower-semicontinuous-carrier}
A $\LowerSemicontinuousUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
L=(X,F,E,W,R,O,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the source carrier row whose accepted points are read through
  displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ histories and their classifier transport;
\item $F$ is the function graph or map row, read through the
  $\ContinuousMapUp$ pointwise carrier only when such a map surface is present;
\item $E$ is the lower-real epigraph ledger, consisting of finite rows
  $(x,q)$ saying that the value at the displayed source point admits the
  rational lower bound $q$ at the inspected window;
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule row selecting finite source and
  value windows for each epigraph inspection;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row giving the regular rational
  value windows used by $E$;
\item $O$ is the $\LowerRealUp$, $\UpperRealUp$, and $\LocatedRealUp$
  comparison ledger used to read lower windows, upper exclusions, located
  endpoint checks, and finite lower-bound refinements;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for source histories,
  graph rows, lower-real epigraph rows, stream windows, regular readbacks,
  comparison ledgers, provenance rows, and local naming rows;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from source admission through
  window selection, regular rational readback, lower-real epigraph inspection,
  located readback, and real-seal handoff;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LowerSemicontinuousUp$,
  $\LowerRealUp$, $\UpperRealUp$, $\LocatedRealUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$,
  $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$,
  $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LowerSemicontinuousUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier relates two accepted packets exactly when their source rows,
finite epigraph windows, regular rational readbacks, lower-real comparisons,
located readbacks, $\RealUp$ seal rows, and provenance rows transport
componentwise along $H$ and replay through the same displayed routes in $C$.
No host topology, open-set lattice, quotient real equality, semantic limit
operator, or completeness principle is a carrier coordinate.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[LowerSemicontinuous NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:lower-semicontinuous-namecert-obligation-surface}
For an accepted $\LowerSemicontinuousUp$ carrier
$L=(X,F,E,W,R,O,H,C,P,N)$, the local
$\NameCert_{\LowerSemicontinuousUp}$ surface consists exactly of six
obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed source, graph, epigraph, schedule,
  readback, comparison, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item epigraph-window exactness, requiring every lower-bound read to appear
  as a finite $(x,q)$ row in $E$ after the schedule in $W$ and readback in
  $R$ have been replayed;
\item lower-real classifier stability, requiring lower refinements and
  rational-bound comparisons to be transported through the $\LowerRealUp$
  rows in $O$;
\item located readback compatibility, requiring every inspected source or
  value window to pass through the $\LocatedRealUp$ rows before a real-facing
  consumer can read it;
\item $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ replay, requiring all source, graph, epigraph,
  readback, comparison, and seal rows to move together under the displayed
  transport and continuation ledgers;
\item provenance and non-escape, stating that the package exports only the
  named finite rows and no host topology, quotient equality, semantic limit,
  open-cover compactness, countable choice, or $\RealUp$ completeness
  principle.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from
\autoref{def:lower-semicontinuous-carrier}. The packet lists exactly the
source row, graph row, epigraph ledger, finite schedule, regular readback,
comparison ledger, transport ledger, replay ledger, provenance row, and local
name.

Epigraph-window exactness is read from $E$ only after the requested source
window has passed through $W$ and the value readback through $R$. Thus a
lower-bound claim is not an external predicate; it is a displayed finite row
over the inspected source and rational bound.

Lower-real classifier stability is the role of $O$ together with $H$. The
lower-real comparisons, upper-side exclusions, and located endpoint checks
move componentwise by $\hsame$, and the continuation row $C$ records the same
inspection route after transport.

Located readback compatibility follows because every real-facing window is
read through the $\LocatedRealUp$ rows before the terminal $\RealUp$ seal is
available. The readback is therefore a finite stream and regular-rational
route, not a host limit operation.

Finally, $P$ and $N$ package those rows as the local certificate. Since the
carrier has no coordinate for the excluded principles, no consumer can obtain
them from the $\LowerSemicontinuousUp$ NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Lower-real epigraph route]
\label{thm:lower-semicontinuous-lowerreal-epigraph-route}
Every lower-real or real-seal consumer of an accepted
$\LowerSemicontinuousUp$ carrier factors through the finite epigraph route
$$
X,F,W,R,E,O,H,C,P,N .
$$
The consumer may inspect scheduled source windows, regular rational value
readbacks, displayed lower-bound rows, lower-real refinements, located
endpoint checks, componentwise transports, continuation replay, provenance,
and local naming rows. It cannot bypass the epigraph ledger to read host
topological openness, quotient real equality, a semantic liminf operator,
countable choice, open-cover compactness, or completeness of $\RealUp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from a real-facing consumer of the packet. The source row $X$ and graph
row $F$ are visible only through the finite schedule $W$, so every value
inspection begins with a displayed source window rather than an ambient
neighbourhood.

The regular rational readback row $R$ then supplies the finite value window.
The epigraph ledger $E$ records the lower-bound row $(x,q)$ attached to that
same inspection, and the comparison ledger $O$ checks lower-real refinements,
upper-side exclusions, and located endpoint compatibility.

Transport and replay are exhausted by $H$ and $C$. They move the source,
graph, readback, epigraph, comparison, and seal rows together, so the route
remains the same after classifier transport.

The package and local name rows $P,N$ record exactly this route for consumers.
Since the excluded host principles are absent from the route and absent from
the carrier coordinates, the lower-real epigraph handoff exposes only the
finite BEDC rows stated above.
\end{proof}
