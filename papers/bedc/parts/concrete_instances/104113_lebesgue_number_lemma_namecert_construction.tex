Following Lebesgue's finite-radius lemma for compact metric covers, the $\LebesgueNumberLemmaUp$ packet records the theorem-facing route that turns a compact metric finite-cover request into a positive radius ledger. It refines, rather than duplicates, the $\FiniteLebesgueNumberUp$ surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finite-lebesgue-number-namecert}: the latter is the finite radius ledger, while this packet names the compact-cover-to-ledger lemma route consumed by compact uniform continuity. \begin{definition}[Lebesgue number lemma carrier]
\label{def:lebesgue-number-lemma-carrier}
A $\LebesgueNumberLemmaUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(K,M,F,R,U,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $K$ is the $\CompactMetricUp$ source row, including the finite-net and located coverage data used by the cover request.
\item $M$ is the $\MetricUp$ distance-observation row through which cover radii and ball-containment checks are read.
\item $F$ is the displayed finite cover or pointwise-radius family row, read through $\ContinuousMapUp$ when the consumer is compact uniform continuity.
\item $R$ is the positive $\FiniteLebesgueNumberUp$ radius ledger produced for the displayed finite cover row.
\item $U$ is the downstream $\UniformModulusUp$ handoff row that may consume $R$ after the compact and metric rows have been exposed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $K,M,F,R,U,P,N$.

\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LebesgueNumberLemmaUp}$ row.

The classifier compares accepted packets only through $K,M,F,R,U,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for an arbitrary open-cover compactness theorem, selected subcover choice, host real minima, external Lebesgue numbers, or an ambient completeness principle.

\begin{theorem}[Lebesgue number lemma NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:lebesgue-number-lemma-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\LebesgueNumberLemmaUp$ carrier $L=(K,M,F,R,U,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\LebesgueNumberLemmaUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the compact metric source, metric observation row, displayed finite cover or pointwise-radius family, finite Lebesgue-number positive radius ledger, uniform-modulus handoff, componentwise transport, continuation replay, package provenance, and local naming; compact-source lock between $K$ and $M$; cover-to-ledger exactness through $F$ and $R$; uniform-modulus handoff through $U$; and non-escape from arbitrary open covers, selected subcovers, host minima, external Lebesgue numbers, and ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:lebesgue-number-lemma-carrier}. The displayed tuple contains exactly the compact source, metric observation, finite cover, positive radius ledger, uniform handoff, and structural rows.

The finite radius is not selected externally. It is the row $R$ of the carrier, read after the compact metric and metric rows have exposed the finite coverage data for $F$.

The rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name this same route. Since no host cover or external minimum coordinate exists in the carrier, the local NameCert surface exports only the finite BEDC lemma packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Lebesgue number lemma uniform-modulus handoff]
\label{thm:lebesgue-number-lemma-uniform-modulus-handoff}
Every compact-uniform-continuity consumer of an accepted $\LebesgueNumberLemmaUp$ carrier reaches $\UniformModulusUp$ only through
$$
\CompactMetricUp,\quad \MetricUp,\quad \ContinuousMapUp,\quad \FiniteLebesgueNumberUp,\quad \UniformModulusUp .
$$
The theorem-facing row is the route from compact finite-cover data to the positive finite Lebesgue-number ledger $R$; the ledger itself remains the sibling surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finite-lebesgue-number-namecert}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read $K,M,F$ first. These rows display the compact metric source, the metric ball observations, and the finite cover or pointwise-radius family that generates the cover request.

Then read $R$. By the obligations of \autoref{thm:lebesgue-number-lemma-namecert-obligations}, this is the only positive radius ledger exported by the packet, and it is exactly the $\FiniteLebesgueNumberUp$ row for the displayed cover data.

Finally the uniform-modulus handoff $U$ may consume that ledger through $H,C,P,N$. The route therefore refines the existing finite-ledger packet without creating a synonym for it.
\end{proof}
