Following Toeplitz and Hardy on summability transforms, the $\ToeplitzLemmaUp$ packet records the finite matrix-window route by which a regular real-name sequence and a Toeplitz row ledger produce a regular transformed sequence before any host limit theorem is read. It sits between the series and uniform-limit consumers of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-realseries-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformlimit-namecert}, the finite stream windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic row-sum ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet is a finite certificate for a regular summability transform; it is not a host summability theorem, a selected limit, quotient equality of real-name streams, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[Toeplitz lemma carrier]\label{def:toeplitz-lemma-carrier}
A $\ToeplitzLemmaUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(A,W,R,D,T,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ is the matrix-coefficient window row, listing the finite Toeplitz rows and columns currently inspected together with their row-sum and entry-size requests.
\item $W$ is the selected source-window row, recording the finite $\StreamNameUp$ observations of the input regular real-name sequence used by the inspected matrix rows.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row for the selected source windows and for the transformed finite row produced by the same matrix window.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger for row sums, coefficient truncation, source-window tolerances, small-entry estimates, and accumulated arithmetic error.
\item $T$ is the transformed finite partial row, obtained by applying the displayed coefficients of $A$ to the selected source values in $W$ and reading the result through $R$ and $D$.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal reached only after the transformed row $T$, regular readback $R$, and dyadic ledger $D$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the matrix window, source windows, readback, dyadic ledger, transformed row, real seal, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ stores displayed $\Cont$ replay from matrix-row inspection to selected source windows, from source windows to regular readback, from readback and dyadic accounting to the transformed row, and from the transformed row to the real seal.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ToeplitzLemmaUp$, $\RealSeriesUp$, $\UniformLimitUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ToeplitzLemmaUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $A,W,R,D,T,E,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for an infinite matrix object, a host summability method, a selected limit of the source sequence, a quotient of real-name streams, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Toeplitz lemma NameCert obligations]\label{thm:toeplitz-lemma-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\ToeplitzLemmaUp$ carrier $K=(A,W,R,D,T,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\ToeplitzLemmaUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following five finite rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed matrix-window, source-window, readback, ledger, transformed-row, seal, transport, continuation, provenance, and naming rows;
\item row-sum boundedness, read from $A$ and checked inside the dyadic ledger $D$ before any transformed row is exposed;
\item small-entry and window-vanishing control, requiring every displayed coefficient request outside the selected finite source window to pass through the same $A,W,D$ route;
\item regular transformed handoff, requiring every transformed-sequence read to pass through $T$, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $R$, and the $\RealUp$ seal $E$;
\item ledger nonescape, excluding host summability methods, selected source limits, quotient real-name equality, countable choice, and ambient $\RealUp$ completeness from the packet surface.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:toeplitz-lemma-carrier}. Carrier admission is exactly the inventory of $A,W,R,D,T,E,H,C,P,N$, and the matrix-row information available to the classifier is confined to the finite row $A$.

The row-sum and small-entry clauses are read through the same dyadic ledger $D$. The selected source windows $W$ provide the only input sequence observations, so any window-vanishing claim must use the displayed coefficient requests in $A$ together with the finite source reads in $W$.

The transformed row $T$ is then exposed through $R$ and $E$. The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name this route, and the carrier contains no coordinate for the excluded host principles.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Toeplitz lemma regular sequence handoff]\label{thm:toeplitz-lemma-regular-sequence-handoff}
Every $\RegSeqRatUp$ or $\RealUp$ consumer of the transformed sequence exported by an accepted $\ToeplitzLemmaUp$ carrier factors through
$$
A,W,R,D,T,E,H,C,P,N.
$$
Thus the consumer reads a finite Toeplitz matrix window, selected source windows, regular rational readback, dyadic row-sum and error accounting, transformed finite partial row, real seal, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming before it can use the transformed regular sequence.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a transformed-sequence consumer. By \autoref{def:toeplitz-lemma-carrier}, the only transformed row coordinate is $T$, and it is built from the matrix-window row $A$, selected source windows $W$, regular readback $R$, and dyadic ledger $D$.

For a $\RegSeqRatUp$ read, the handoff is therefore already forced through $R$ after $A,W,D$ have supplied the coefficient, source, and error data. For a $\RealUp$ read, the same route continues to the seal row $E$.

The rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve the route under transport, replay the same continuations, record package provenance, and bind the local NameCert. Since no second transformed-sequence coordinate is present, every downstream consumer factors through the displayed packet.
\end{proof}
