Following Bishop and Bridges and Kelley, the $\SeparableMetricSpaceUp$ packet records the traditional countable-dense metric interface as finite BEDC rows. It sits between the metric carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the finite observation schedule of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal real seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet names a displayed dense enumeration and its approximation obligations; it is not quotient completion, not a selected-representative principle, not countable choice, not open-cover compactness, and not completeness of $\RealUp$.

\begin{definition}[SeparableMetricSpace carrier]
\label{def:separable-metric-space-carrier}
A $\SeparableMetricSpaceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(M,D,E,W,R,Q,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the $\MetricUp$ row, including the source point histories, metric-read classifier, and finite distance-query surface.
\item $D$ is the countable dense enumeration row, represented as a $\StreamNameUp$ schedule whose displayed entries are inspected only through finite windows.
\item $E$ is the density evidence row: for every displayed point request and positive rational tolerance request visible in $M$, it stores a finite witness index in $D$ together with the corresponding distance-read budget.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row used to read the requested dense entries and the finite witness indices.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row for regular rational distance bounds attached to the metric requests.
\item $Q$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after $M,D,E,W,R$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ is the componentwise $\hsame$ transport ledger for metric, dense stream, density evidence, finite windows, regular rational readbacks, real seals, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ is the displayed $\Cont$ replay route forcing every density read to pass through
$$
M,D \longmapsto E,W,R \longmapsto Q \longmapsto H,P,N .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\SeparableMetricSpaceUp$, $\MetricUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\SeparableMetricSpaceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for quotient completion, selected representatives, countable choice, open-cover compactness, host real equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[SeparableMetricSpace NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:separable-metric-space-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\SeparableMetricSpaceUp$ carrier $S=(M,D,E,W,R,Q,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\SeparableMetricSpaceUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the metric row, dense stream row, density evidence row, finite StreamName windows, RegSeqRat readbacks, Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item dense-stream visibility, requiring every dense witness to be read through $D$ and $W$ before it can answer a metric approximation request;
\item approximation evidence, requiring every displayed point-and-tolerance request to pass through $E$ and then through the metric row $M$ and rational readback row $R$;
\item real-seal order, requiring the terminal $\RealUp$ readback $Q$ to remain downstream of the metric, stream, density, and regular-rational rows;
\item transport stability, requiring $H$ and $C$ to preserve the same displayed metric and dense-stream coordinates componentwise;
\item ledger non-escape, stating that $P$ and $N$ export no quotient completion, selected representative, countable-choice schedule, open-cover compactness argument, host real equality, or $\RealUp$ completeness theorem.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:separable-metric-space-carrier}. The tuple exposes exactly the metric carrier, dense stream, density evidence, finite windows, regular rational readbacks, real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

Dense-stream visibility uses $D$ together with the finite window row $W$. A consumer can inspect only the displayed dense entries selected by the StreamName schedule, so the countable dense surface is a named stream row rather than a host enumeration principle.

Approximation evidence uses $E$ between the metric row $M$ and the rational readback row $R$. The witness index and distance budget are stored as finite rows before any terminal real seal is read.

The real-seal, transport, and non-escape clauses follow from the remaining coordinates. The seal row $Q$ is downstream of $M,D,E,W,R$, while $H,C,P,N$ preserve, replay, source, and name only those displayed rows. Since no excluded principle is a coordinate of the carrier, the listed obligations exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SeparableMetricSpace countable-dense stream handoff]
\label{thm:separable-metric-space-countable-dense-stream-handoff}
Let $S=(M,D,E,W,R,Q,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\SeparableMetricSpaceUp$ carrier. Every downstream $\MetricUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, or $\RealUp$ consumer of its countable-dense interface factors through
$$
M,D \longmapsto E,W,R \longmapsto Q \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
Consequently the handoff exports a displayed countable dense stream with finite approximation evidence, not quotient completion, selected representatives, countable choice, open-cover compactness, or completeness of $\RealUp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a downstream dense-approximation read. The metric row $M$ supplies the point and distance-query surface, while the dense stream row $D$ supplies only finite entries selected through the StreamName window $W$.

The density evidence row $E$ then chooses the displayed witness index already present in $D$ and attaches the rational budget read through $R$. This is the point at which the countable-dense route becomes visible to completion and Real-facing consumers.

The terminal seal $Q$ and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve that route without adding any host-side principle. Thus every consumer receives the same finite dense-stream handoff and none of the excluded completion, choice, compactness, or Real-completeness data.
\end{proof}
