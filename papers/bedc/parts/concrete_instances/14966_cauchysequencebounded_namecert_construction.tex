Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, the $\CauchySequenceBoundedUp$ packet records the boundedness surface that a Cauchy sequence exposes before any completed-real consumer is allowed to read it. It sits between the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the $\CauchyModulusUp$ threshold rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchymodulus-namecert}, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the $\RealUp$ seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, and the convergence boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-convergence-criterion-namecert}. The certificate names a finite bound packet for one displayed Cauchy schedule; it does not turn Cauchy data into a host-complete object, quotient stream, or selected limit.

\begin{definition}[Cauchy sequence bounded carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-sequence-bounded-carrier}
A $\CauchySequenceBoundedUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
X=(S,M,D,R,E,B,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ request schedule exposing the finite window of sequence reads.
\item $M$ is the $\CauchyModulusUp$ threshold row that says which tail requests are governed by the displayed Cauchy modulus.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger used to compare the window reads and tail tolerances in dyadic or rational form.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row that packages the accepted regular rational readback induced by $S,M,D$.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after the stream schedule, modulus row, dyadic tolerance ledger, regular-rational handoff, and bound ledger have been displayed.
\item $B$ is the explicit dyadic or rational bound ledger, consisting of a finite initial-window bound, a tail-bound row extracted from $M$ and $D$, and a reconciliation row saying that both windows are contained in the same displayed bound.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the stream schedule, modulus row, dyadic ledger, regular-rational handoff, real seal, bound ledger, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming row.
\item $C$ stores displayed $\Cont$ replay from stream requests to modulus thresholds, from thresholds to dyadic tolerances, from tolerances to the bound ledger, and from the bound ledger to the regular-rational and real-facing rows.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchySequenceBoundedUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\CauchyModulusUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchySequenceBoundedUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $S,M,D,R,E,B,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for host completeness, quotient streams, selected limits, real trichotomy, open-cover compactness, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy boundedness NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-sequence-bounded-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\CauchySequenceBoundedUp$ carrier $X=(S,M,D,R,E,B,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CauchySequenceBoundedUp}$ obligations are exactly carrier admission, finite-window classification, modulus-to-bound extraction, dyadic bound ledger stability, $\RealUp$ seal handoff, and ledger non-escape. These obligations are discharged by the displayed route
$$
S\longmapsto M\longmapsto D\longmapsto R\longmapsto E\longmapsto B
$$
together with $H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CauchySequenceBoundedUp.CauchySequenceBoundedCarrier\_namecert\_obligations}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is the projection of the displayed tuple in \autoref{def:cauchy-sequence-bounded-carrier}. The finite-window classifier reads the schedule $S$ and then uses $M$ to identify the tail threshold where the Cauchy comparison becomes governed by the displayed modulus row.

The modulus-to-bound extraction applies the tolerance ledger $D$ to the inspected initial window and to the tail window named by $M$. The ledger $B$ records both finite data: an initial-window bound and a tail-bound row, reconciled into one dyadic or rational bound.

The rows $H$ and $C$ transport and replay the same route componentwise, so dyadic bound stability is a property of the displayed ledger rather than of an external order principle. The rows $R$ and $E$ are downstream handoff rows; they receive a bounded sequence packet after $B$ is present.

Finally, $P$ and $N$ source and name the packet. Since the carrier has no coordinate for host completeness, quotient streams, selected limits, trichotomy, open-cover compactness, choice, or ambient real completeness, the listed obligations exhaust the NameCert surface.
\end{proof}
