\begin{definition}[Closed interval total-bounded carrier]
\label{def:closed-interval-total-bounded-carrier}
A $\ClosedIntervalTotalBoundedUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
T=(A,B,M,D,F,U,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ and $B$ are the lower and upper closed $\RealUp$ endpoint rows, exposed only through their finite stream windows and regular rational readbacks.
\item $M$ is the $\ClosedBoundedIntervalNetUp$ mesh row carrying the unary dyadic scale, displayed cells, center rows, and interval coverage ledger for the closed interval determined by $A,B$.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicIntervalBasisUp$ refinement row, recording how each displayed mesh cell is refined by dyadic basis cells at the requested positive tolerance.
\item $F$ is the $\FiniteEpsilonNetUp$ ledger obtained from the displayed mesh centers, coverage witnesses, and dyadic tolerance comparisons.
\item $U$ is the $\TotallyBoundedUp$ handoff row, read only after $M,D,F$ have displayed the finite center bundle and coverage route.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window family for endpoint, center, dyadic-cell, and coverage observations.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row for the same endpoint, center, and coverage windows.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after $S$ and $R$ have exposed the relevant finite reads.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for endpoints, interval nets, dyadic refinements, finite epsilon nets, the total-bounded handoff, stream windows, readbacks, the Real seal, replay, provenance, and local naming.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of every consumer request through
$$
A,B\longmapsto M\longmapsto D\longmapsto F\longmapsto U\longmapsto S\longmapsto R\longmapsto E .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ClosedIntervalTotalBoundedUp$, $\ClosedBoundedIntervalNetUp$, $\DyadicIntervalBasisUp$, $\TotallyBoundedUp$, $\FiniteEpsilonNetUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ClosedIntervalTotalBoundedUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only through these rows. It has no coordinate for open-cover compactness, selected finite subcovers, quotient real equality, trichotomy, countable choice, a hidden center selector, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Closed interval total-bounded NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:closed-interval-total-bounded-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\ClosedIntervalTotalBoundedUp$ carrier $T=(A,B,M,D,F,U,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\ClosedIntervalTotalBoundedUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the two endpoint rows, the closed-bounded interval net, dyadic basis refinement, finite epsilon-net ledger, total-bounded handoff, stream windows, regular readback, Real seal, and structural rows $H,C,P,N$;
\item endpoint visibility, requiring $A$ and $B$ to be read through the displayed $\StreamNameUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$ windows before any interval-total-bounded route is consumed;
\item mesh coverage, requiring $M$ to expose the dyadic scale, cell ledger, center rows, and closed-interval coverage witnesses supplied by the $\ClosedBoundedIntervalNetUp$ packet;
\item dyadic refinement exactness, requiring $D$ to refine only the displayed mesh cells by the $\DyadicIntervalBasisUp$ route at the requested tolerance;
\item finite epsilon-net formation, requiring $F$ to list exactly the displayed centers and coverage witnesses used by the tolerance request;
\item total-bounded handoff, requiring $U$ to be downstream of $M,D,F$ and to export no additional center selector or compactness theorem;
\item transport, replay, provenance, and local naming for exactly the displayed rows recorded in $H,C,P,N$.
\end{enumerate}
No closed-interval total-bounded NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet $T=(A,B,M,D,F,U,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)$. Carrier admission exposes exactly the endpoint rows, the interval mesh, the dyadic refinement row, the finite epsilon-net ledger, the total-bounded handoff, the stream windows, the regular readback, the Real seal, and the four structural rows.

The endpoint and mesh obligations are finite. The endpoints are visible only through $S$ and $R$, while $M$ supplies the displayed dyadic scale, center rows, and coverage witnesses from the closed-bounded interval-net packet.

The tolerance obligation is the route $M,D,F,U$. The dyadic basis row $D$ refines the displayed mesh cells, the finite epsilon-net ledger $F$ records the centers and coverage witnesses for the requested tolerance, and the total-bounded handoff $U$ is read only after those rows have been displayed.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same route. Since the carrier contains no open-cover, selected-subcover, quotient-equality, trichotomy, choice, hidden-selector, or ambient-completeness coordinate, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Closed interval total-bounded dyadic-net handoff]
\label{thm:closed-interval-total-bounded-dyadic-net-handoff}
For every accepted $\ClosedIntervalTotalBoundedUp$ carrier $T=(A,B,M,D,F,U,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)$ and every displayed positive tolerance request, the exported $\TotallyBoundedUp$ row factors through the finite route
$$
\ClosedBoundedIntervalNetUp \longrightarrow \DyadicIntervalBasisUp \longrightarrow \FiniteEpsilonNetUp \longrightarrow \TotallyBoundedUp .
$$
Thus the total-boundedness witness is the finite dyadic mesh and its displayed center bundle, not a compactness theorem or a selected subcover of an open family.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a tolerance request for the total-bounded handoff $U$. The carrier places $U$ downstream of the closed-bounded interval-net row $M$, so the request first reads the displayed mesh cells, centers, and coverage witnesses.

The dyadic basis row $D$ refines those mesh cells at the requested tolerance. This refinement is tied to the cells in $M$ and does not introduce a separate family of centers.

The finite epsilon-net ledger $F$ then packages the displayed centers and coverage witnesses as the finite net consumed by $U$. The rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve and replay the same sequence of reads.

Therefore every total-bounded export factors through $M,D,F,U$. Because the carrier has no open-cover coordinate and no selected-subcover coordinate, the handoff is finite dyadic-net data rather than a compactness selection principle.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Closed interval total-bounded Real-seal nonescape]
\label{thm:closed-interval-total-bounded-real-seal-nonescape}
Every $\RealUp$, compact-net, or Heine--Borel consumer of an accepted $\ClosedIntervalTotalBoundedUp$ carrier reaches endpoint and coverage data only through
$$
A,B,M,D,F,U,S,R,E,H,C,P,N .
$$
The terminal $\RealUp$ seal is downstream of the displayed stream windows and regular rational readbacks, and the consumer receives no quotient real equality, host endpoint choice, selected subcover, countable choice, or ambient completeness row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a consumer request for endpoint, coverage, or total-boundedness data. The only endpoint rows in the packet are $A$ and $B$, and the only Real-facing terminal row is $E$.

The route to $E$ passes through the finite windows $S$ and regular readback $R$. Before those rows can serve the interval-total-bounded request, the mesh row $M$, dyadic refinement row $D$, finite epsilon-net row $F$, and total-bounded handoff $U$ have already fixed the displayed center and coverage data.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ then transport, replay, source, and name the same route for compact-net and Heine--Borel consumers. Since no quotient equality, host endpoint selector, selected subcover, countable-choice schedule, or ambient-completeness row appears in the carrier, none can escape through the Real seal.
\end{proof}
