Following Bishop and Bridges constructive metric spaces and Weihrauch represented spaces, the $\EffectiveMetricSpaceUp$ packet records the finite interface needed by completion consumers before any limit object is formed. It refines the represented-name surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-representedspace-namecert}, the distance obligations of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance arithmetic of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the scheduled name windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal real seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}.

\begin{definition}[EffectiveMetricSpace carrier]
\label{def:effective-metric-space-carrier}
An $\EffectiveMetricSpaceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
E=(X,S,W,\rho,D,R,\delta,\Sigma,\Delta,T,C,P,N)
$$
with the following rows:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item a point-name row $X$ and a represented-space schedule $S,W,\rho$ for finite $\StreamNameUp$ reads;
  \item a dyadic tolerance row $D$ and a $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row $R$ for all inspected distance approximants;
  \item a $\RealUp$-valued distance seal $\delta$ whose admitted reads pass through $D$ and $R$;
  \item symmetry and triangle replay rows $\Sigma,\Delta$ for the displayed distance seal;
  \item componentwise transport $T$, replay $C$, provenance $P$, and a local $\NameCert$ row $N$.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier is accepted only when every distance request exposes a scheduled name window, a dyadic tolerance, a regular rational readback, and the resulting $\RealUp$ seal before a completion-facing consumer can inspect it.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[EffectiveMetricSpace NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:effective-metric-space-namecert-obligations}
Every accepted $\EffectiveMetricSpaceUp$ carrier $E=(X,S,W,\rho,D,R,\delta,\Sigma,\Delta,T,C,P,N)$ supplies the finite NameCert obligations needed by an $\EffectiveMetricCompletionUp$ consumer: point-name admission, represented-space scheduling, dyadic tolerance tracking, regular rational readback, Real-valued distance sealing, symmetry replay, triangle replay, componentwise transport, provenance, and local naming.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:effective-metric-space-carrier}. The only rows available to a completion-facing reader are the displayed point names, scheduled representation reads, tolerance ledger, rational readback, real seal, replay rows, transport, provenance, and local NameCert.

The represented-space part is sourced by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-representedspace-namecert}. The tolerance and readback rows then pass through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, so the distance seal cannot be opened without the finite arithmetic route.

Finally the symmetry and triangle rows replay only that displayed seal. Transport and provenance carry the same finite packet into the local $\NameCert$ row, excluding host equality of points, selected representatives, countable choice, and ambient completion data.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[EffectiveMetricSpace distance readback]
\label{thm:effective-metric-space-distance-readback}

$$
S,W,\rho \longmapsto D \longmapsto R \longmapsto \delta \longmapsto \Sigma,\Delta,T,C,P,N.

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

Next consume the dyadic and regular-rational rows. They produce the tolerance-indexed readback that feeds the $\RealUp$ distance seal, so the downstream reader sees a certified real value rather than an ambient metric value.
