\begin{definition}[Cook frontier coordinate carrier]
\label{def:cook-frontier-coordinate-carrier}
A $\CookFrontierCoordinateUp$ carrier is a finite packet
$$
K=(F,R,S,A,L,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $F=(F_{\mathrm{tag}},F_{\mathrm{compile}},F_{\mathrm{halt}})$ is the ordered stage-frontier triple naming the tag-system encoding frontier, the Rule 110 compilation frontier, and the halted-target frontier inspected by the packet;
\item $R$ is the finite Rule 110 scale row naming the cellular rule table, width budget, time budget, boundary convention, and row family used by the frontier read;
\item $S$ is the finite stage-sample ledger recording the tag-system word, compiled cellular window, and target readback rows displayed at the chosen frontier;
\item $A$ is the audit-binary row containing the replay executable name, input manifest, digest row, accepted output rows, and refusal rows for failed replay;
\item $L$ is the failure-ledger row recording absent encodings, scale overflows, replay mismatches, unread target rows, and refused universality reads;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $F,R,S,A,L,P,N$;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ routes from the stage-frontier triple through the scale row, sample ledger, audit replay, and failure ledger;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CookFrontierCoordinateUp$, \autoref{ch:visions-cook-compile-frontier-engineering-path}, $\CellularAutomatonUp$, $\BoundedUniversalTraceUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CookFrontierCoordinateUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The local classifier compares accepted coordinates only by these displayed rows. It has no coordinate for unbounded Rule 110 universality, a host-level proof that the target Turing machine halts, a hidden cellular evaluator, quotient stream equality, or a detached success claim outside the audit binary.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cook frontier coordinate NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cook-frontier-coordinate-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:cook-frontier-coordinate-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\CookFrontierCoordinateUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $K=(F,R,S,A,L,H,C,P,N)$, ordered stage-frontier accountability through $F$, Rule 110 scale accountability through $R$, finite sample exposure through $S$, audit replay exactness through $A$, failure-ledger non-escape through $L$, componentwise stability through $H$, displayed route replay through $C$, package provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:cook-frontier-coordinate-carrier}. The packet displays the stage-frontier triple, scale row, sample ledger, audit replay, failure ledger, transports, routes, provenance, and local naming row as its only coordinates.

Stage-frontier and scale accountability are read from $F$ and $R$. A consumer must name which encoding, compilation, and halted-target frontiers are being inspected, and must read the Rule 110 width, time, boundary, and row-family data from the finite scale row.

Sample exposure and audit replay are read from $S$ and $A$. The sample ledger records the tag-system, cellular, and target readback rows selected by the coordinate, while the audit-binary row supplies the replay manifest, digest, accepted outputs, and refused replay cases.

Finally use $L,H,C,P,N$. The failure ledger blocks success claims that bypass the replay rows, and the structural rows transport and package the same finite fields. Since the carrier has no unbounded universality, host halting, hidden evaluator, quotient stream, or detached success coordinate, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cook frontier coordinate witnessed-universality boundary]
\label{thm:cook-frontier-coordinate-witnessed-universality-boundary}
An accepted $\CookFrontierCoordinateUp$ packet may report witnessed operational coverage only along the finite route
$$
F \longmapsto R \longmapsto S \longmapsto A \longmapsto L \longmapsto H \longmapsto C \longmapsto P \longmapsto N.
$$
It may cite the Cook compile-frontier path of \autoref{ch:visions-cook-compile-frontier-engineering-path} as the vision being measured and may use the cellular and bounded-trace surfaces of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cellularautomaton-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bounded-universal-trace-namecert} as sibling inputs, but it does not promote finite replay into an unbounded Rule 110 universality theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the route inventory. The frontier triple $F$ names the three inspected stages, while $R$ and $S$ provide only a finite Rule 110 scale row and a finite stage-sample ledger.

The sibling chapters supply finite surfaces rather than unbounded semantic closure. The cellular chapter contributes displayed rule-table and orbit rows, and the bounded-trace chapter contributes fuel-indexed readback; neither gives this packet a host proof that every future Rule 110 row or every target Turing-machine behaviour has been covered.

The audit and failure rows determine the witnessed boundary. A replay accepted by $A$ is reported only with the refusals and missing cases recorded in $L$, then transported and packaged through $H,C,P,N$. Thus the coordinate can witness finite operational coverage without asserting unbounded universality as a new theorem.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cook frontier coordinate failure-ledger non-escape]
\label{thm:cook-frontier-coordinate-failure-ledger-nonescape}
Every rejected or incomplete $\CookFrontierCoordinateUp$ read remains inside the displayed failure ledger $L$. A missing encoding row, scale overflow, audit mismatch, unread target row, or refused universality read cannot be reclassified as success by appealing to $\CellularAutomatonUp$, $\BoundedUniversalTraceUp$, a host evaluator, quotient stream equality, or any route absent from $K=(F,R,S,A,L,H,C,P,N)$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The only rows that can record failure are the replay row $A$ and the failure ledger $L$, with $F,R,S$ providing the stage, scale, and sample context.

Inspect each failure class through the same finite coordinates. Missing encodings are failures of the stage-frontier row, scale overflows are failures of the Rule 110 scale row, replay mismatches are failures of the audit binary, and unread target rows or refused universality reads are ledger entries in $L$.

The sibling chapters do not supply a bypass. Cellular rows and bounded traces can be cited only as displayed finite inputs to this coordinate, and no host evaluator, quotient stream, or absent route is carried by $K$. Therefore every rejected or incomplete read stays recorded as a failure-ledger row.
\end{proof}
