It sits over the compact source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compact-namecert}, the metric source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the continuous-map pointwise modulus rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuous-namecert}, the rational positivity surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rat-namecert}, and the sibling subordinate-cover packets of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-subordinatemoduluscover-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-subordinate-finite-modulus-cover-namecert}. \begin{definition}[Compact-uniform subordinate-cover carrier]
\label{def:compact-uniform-subordinate-cover-namecert-surface}
A $\CompactUniformSubordinateCoverUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
U=(K,G,Q,F,L,M,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $K$ is the compact metric source row, combining the $\CompactUp$ admission and the $\MetricUp$ distance carrier over the same source.
\item $G$ is the continuous graph row, exposing pointwise modulus requests for the same source and target metric carriers.
\item $Q$ is the positive $\RatUp$ tolerance row read by the pointwise-modulus requests.
\item $F$ is the finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ subordinate-cover row, naming bundled centers, coverage witnesses, and the source-distance windows attached to those centers.
\item $L$ is the rational lower-bound fold over the displayed subordinate radii in $F$.
\item $M$ is the $\UniformModulusUp$ handoff row produced only after $K,G,Q,F,L$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $K,G,Q,F,L,M$ and for the replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from compact-source inspection through pointwise-modulus reads, finite-cover coverage, rational lower-bound folding, and uniform-modulus handoff.

\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CompactUniformSubordinateCoverUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $K,G,Q,F,L,M,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for open-cover compactness, host lower-bound extraction, a selected infinite subcover, quotient point equality, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Compact-uniform subordinate cover handoff]
\label{thm:compact-uniform-subordinate-cover-handoff}
For every accepted $\CompactUniformSubordinateCoverUp$ carrier $U=(K,G,Q,F,L,M,H,C,P,N)$, the finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ row $F$ is the unique subordinate-cover input to the uniform-modulus handoff. A consumer reaches $M$ only by first reading the compact metric source $K$, the continuous graph row $G$, the positive rational tolerance row $Q$, the bundled center and coverage row $F$, and the rational lower-bound fold $L$, with $H,C,P,N$ preserving that route.

Consequently the packet supplies the ProbeBundle modulus-fold route used in \autoref{ch:visions-compact-uniform-continuity} without exposing open-cover compactness, a host Lebesgue-number choice, a selected subcover, quotient point equality, or an external modulus selector.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:compact-uniform-subordinate-cover-namecert-surface}. The compact metric source is $K$, the continuous graph row is $G$, the tolerance row is $Q$, the finite subordinate cover is $F$, the rational lower-bound fold is $L$, and the uniform-modulus handoff is $M$.

The cover row $F$ is the only coordinate carrying bundled centers and coverage witnesses. Thus every local pointwise-modulus read used by the handoff is tied to a displayed probe name and to the same source metric row in $K$.

The fold row $L$ is read after $F$ and before $M$. It computes the tolerance-only radius through finite rational data rather than through a host minimum or an external Lebesgue-number selector.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same route. Since the carrier contains no coordinate for the excluded host objects, every $\UniformModulusUp$ consumer of $M$ factors through the displayed subordinate cover.
\end{proof}
