\begin{definition}[Cauchy completion route carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-completion-route-carrier}
A $\CauchyCompletionRouteUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
R=(D,S,Q,E,B,T,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row, recording endpoints, dyadic comparisons, precision requests, and the finite gap ledger used by the route.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ observation row, exposing only finite windows of the source stream before any regular-Cauchy read is admitted.
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regularity row, recording the rational sequence data and tail modulus visible through $D$ and $S$.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ endpoint row, read only as the terminal seal produced from the displayed regular-Cauchy data.
\item $B$ is the $\BishopRegularCauchyCompletionUp$ route row, tying $D,S,Q,E$ to the established regular-Cauchy completion packet.
\item $T$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for dyadic tolerances, stream windows, regular rational readback, terminal real seal, Bishop completion route, replay, provenance, and local naming.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a completion request through $D,S,Q,B$ and then to $E$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyCompletionRouteUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BishopRegularCauchyCompletionUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyCompletionRouteUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only by these displayed rows. It has no coordinate for quotient stream equality, selected limits, host real equality, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy completion route NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-route-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CauchyCompletionRouteUp$ carrier $R=(D,S,Q,E,B,T,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyCompletionRouteUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed dyadic tolerance, stream-window, regular rational, real seal, Bishop completion, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item dyadic observation discipline, requiring every completion-facing read to expose $D$ and $S$ before any regular rational or real seal row is consumed;
\item regularity routing, requiring the $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $Q$ to be the only passage from finite windows to a completion-facing regular sequence;
\item Bishop route compatibility, requiring $B$ to identify the same dyadic, stream, regular rational, and real rows as the Bishop regular-Cauchy completion packet;
\item terminal seal discipline, requiring the $\RealUp$ row $E$ to be read only after $D,S,Q,B$ have been displayed;
\item transport and replay non-escape, requiring $T,C,P,N$ to preserve the listed rows and to export no quotient stream equality, selected limit, host real equality, countable choice, or ambient completeness theorem.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored outside $D,S,Q,E,B,T,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-completion-route-carrier}. The finite packet exposes only the dyadic tolerance row, stream-window row, regular rational row, terminal real seal, Bishop completion route, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming.

The dyadic and stream rows are upstream. Any completion-facing consumer first reads the displayed tolerance and finite observation data, so the regular rational row is not a detached sequence or a host limit object.

The Bishop route row then checks compatibility with the established regular-Cauchy completion packet. It ties the same dyadic, stream, regular rational, and real rows to the route and prevents a second completion carrier from being inserted.

Finally $T,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the displayed route. Since the carrier lacks quotient, selected-limit, host-equality, choice, and ambient-completeness coordinates, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy completion route real boundary]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-route-real-boundary}
Every $\RealUp$-facing consumer of an accepted $\CauchyCompletionRouteUp$ carrier factors through the finite route
$$
\DyadicRatCoreUp \longmapsto \StreamNameUp \longmapsto \RegSeqRatUp \longmapsto \BishopRegularCauchyCompletionUp \longmapsto \RealUp .
$$
The handoff exposes only dyadic tolerance rows, finite stream windows, regular rational readback, the Bishop regular-Cauchy completion route, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ rows. It does not export quotient stream equality, selected limits, host real equality, countable choice, or an ambient completeness theorem for $\RealUp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with \autoref{thm:cauchy-completion-route-namecert-obligations}. It places every completion-facing read behind the displayed dyadic tolerance row $D$ and stream-window row $S$.

The regularity route is then unique inside the carrier. The row $Q$ is the only regular rational passage, and $B$ confirms that the same $D,S,Q,E$ rows are the rows consumed by the Bishop regular-Cauchy completion packet.

The terminal handoff is the row $E$. Transport by $T$, replay by $C$, provenance by $P$, and local naming by $N$ preserve the route without adding new analytic data, so the $\RealUp$ consumer receives exactly the displayed finite boundary.
\end{proof}
