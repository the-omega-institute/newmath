\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\HausdorffCompletionFilterSeparationUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-hausdorff-completion-filter-separation-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges and Bourbaki on separated completions and Hausdorff uniform filters, the $\HausdorffCompletionFilterSeparationUp$ packet records the finite route by which a Cauchy-filter completion surface becomes separated before a Real-facing completion consumer reads it. It sits after the Hausdorff completion surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-hausdorffcompletion-namecert}, the separated completion surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separatedcompletion-namecert}, the Cauchy-filter completion surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyfiltercompletion-namecert}, the metric completion surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metriccompletion-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet names the separated-filter completion handoff as finite BEDC data; it is not a quotient-filter construction, a selected representative, a countable-choice limit selector, host equality of completion points, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[Hausdorff completion filter separation carrier]
\label{def:hausdorff-completion-filter-separation-carrier}
A $\HausdorffCompletionFilterSeparationUp$ carrier is a finite packet
$$
(F,H,S,C,M,R,T,Q,P,N).
$$
Here $F$ is the Cauchy-filter completion row, $H$ is the Hausdorff separated row, $S$ is the separated-completion handoff row, $C$ is the completion-continuation row, $M$ is the $\MetricCompletionUp$ seal, $R$ is the $\RealUp$ seal, $T$ is componentwise $\hsame$ transport over the filter, separation, completion, metric, and real rows, $Q$ is displayed $\Cont$ replay, $P$ is $\Pkg$ provenance over the cited sibling certificates and BEDC structural rows, and $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\HausdorffCompletionFilterSeparationUp}$ row.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Hausdorff completion filter separation obligations]
\label{thm:hausdorff-completion-filter-separation-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:hausdorff-completion-filter-separation-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\HausdorffCompletionFilterSeparationUp}$ surface consists exactly of six finite obligations: carrier admission for the displayed Cauchy-filter completion row $F$; Hausdorff separation compatibility for $H$; separated-completion handoff exactness for $S$ and $C$; MetricCompletion and Real seal agreement for $M$ and $R$; componentwise transport and replay stability for $T$ and $Q$; and provenance plus local naming through $P$ and $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier. The only completion source row before separation is $F$, so every completion read begins on the Cauchy-filter completion surface named by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyfiltercompletion-namecert}.

Read the separation rows next. The Hausdorff row $H$ and separated-completion handoff row $S$ force zero-distance or indistinguishability comparisons through the separated surfaces of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-hausdorffcompletion-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separatedcompletion-namecert}.

Attach the completion seals. The continuation row $C$, $\MetricCompletionUp$ seal $M$, and $\RealUp$ seal $R$ keep the separated filter completion connected to the metric-completion and real-name boundaries rather than to host equality of quotient classes.

Finally transport and replay the same finite packet through $T$ and $Q$, and record provenance and local naming through $P$ and $N$. Since no coordinate stores a quotient filter, a selected representative, a countable-choice selector, host equality, or ambient completeness, the listed obligations are the whole local certificate surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Hausdorff completion filter separation]
\label{thm:hausdorff-completion-filter-separation}
Every accepted $\HausdorffCompletionFilterSeparationUp$ carrier $(F,H,S,C,M,R,T,Q,P,N)$ presents a separated Cauchy-filter completion route: a completion consumer must first read $F$, then the Hausdorff and separated handoff rows $H,S,C$, then the $\MetricCompletionUp$ and $\RealUp$ seals $M,R$, and only then the structural rows $T,Q,P,N$. The exported completion point is therefore separated at the displayed filter boundary and cannot be read through quotient filters, selected representatives, countable choice, host equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from carrier admission. The Cauchy-filter completion row $F$ supplies the only completion source, so a consumer has no route to a completed object before the filter-completion surface is displayed.

Apply the local NameCert obligations. They place $H,S,C$ between the filter source and the completion seals, making Hausdorff separation and separated-completion handoff part of the same finite route.

Read the two seals in order. The $\MetricCompletionUp$ seal $M$ records the metric-completion side, and the $\RealUp$ seal $R$ records the real-name boundary consumed by later Real and Completion rows.

The structural rows do not add new completion data. Componentwise $\hsame$ transport $T$, displayed $\Cont$ replay $Q$, $\Pkg$ provenance $P$, and local naming $N$ preserve the same packet, so the exported route is exactly the separated Cauchy-filter completion route and no stronger ambient principle is available.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Hausdorff completion filter separation stability]
\label{thm:hausdorff-completion-filter-separation-obligation-separation-stability}
For every accepted $\HausdorffCompletionFilterSeparationUp$ carrier $(F,H,S,C,M,R,T,Q,P,N)$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport preserves the displayed order
$$
F \longmapsto H,S,C \longmapsto M,R \longmapsto T,Q,P,N .
$$
Hausdorff separation therefore remains upstream of the $\MetricCompletionUp$ and $\RealUp$ seals whenever the packet is transported or replayed.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:hausdorff-completion-filter-separation-carrier}. The Cauchy-filter completion source is $F$, the separation and separated-completion rows are $H,S,C$, and the completion-facing seals are $M,R$.

Use \autoref{thm:hausdorff-completion-filter-separation-namecert-obligations}. Its transport and replay clause assigns $T$ and $Q$ to the same finite packet rather than to a new completion source.

Thus transporting the packet preserves the route from $F$ through $H,S,C$ before $M,R$ are read. The structural rows $T,Q,P,N$ record stability, replay, provenance, and naming after the separation rows have already been displayed.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Hausdorff completion filter Real seal non-escape]
\label{thm:hausdorff-completion-filter-separation-seal-nonescape}
For every accepted $\HausdorffCompletionFilterSeparationUp$ carrier $(F,H,S,C,M,R,T,Q,P,N)$, the $\RealUp$ seal row $R$ cannot be read as a quotient-filter construction, selected representative, countable-choice limit selector, host equality of completion points, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness. It seals only the displayed completion route that has already passed through $F,H,S,C,M$ and the structural rows $T,Q,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the same carrier. The only Real-facing coordinate is $R$, and it appears after the Cauchy-filter source, Hausdorff row, separated-completion handoff, and metric-completion seal.

Apply \autoref{thm:hausdorff-completion-filter-separation-namecert-obligations}. The obligations identify $R$ as a seal agreement row for the displayed packet, while the transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows preserve that packet without adding new Real data.

No coordinate stores quotient-filter construction, a selected representative, a countable-choice limit selector, host equality, or ambient Real completeness. The seal therefore cannot escape the displayed separated-filter route.
\end{proof}
