\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RegularCauchyDensityUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-density-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges on constructive real numbers, the
$\RegularCauchyDensityUp$ packet records the finite density route by which a
dyadic rational approximation is read through regular Cauchy data before a
terminal $\RealUp$ seal is exposed. It sits over the dyadic arithmetic of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the finite window
surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular
rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the
terminal Real seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, and the
separable Real boundary of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-separability-namecert}. The packet names a
displayed approximation handoff for Real and Completion consumers; it is not a
quotient equality of real names, a selected Cauchy representative, countable
choice, trichotomy, a host density theorem, or ambient completeness of
$\RealUp$.

\begin{definition}[RegularCauchyDensity carrier]
\label{def:regular-cauchy-density-carrier}
A $\RegularCauchyDensityUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
D=(Q,S,R,A,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $Q$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ row naming the dyadic rational candidate
and the dyadic tolerance budget requested by the density read.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row exposing the inspected
prefixes of the source real name at the precisions demanded by $Q$.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular-rational readback row reached from the
finite windows.
\item $A$ is the approximation row stating that $Q$ is accepted only after the
regular-rational comparison in $R$ displays the requested dyadic tolerance.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row, visible only after $Q,S,R,A$
have been displayed.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the dyadic candidate,
stream windows, regular readback, approximation row, Real seal, replay,
provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ stores displayed $\Cont$ replay from a density request through
$Q,S,R,A,E,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RegularCauchyDensityUp$,
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$,
$\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyDensityUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier stores only displayed finite rows. It has no coordinate for
quotient equality of real names, selected representatives, countable choice,
trichotomy, host density of rationals, or ambient completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[RegularCauchyDensity NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-density-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RegularCauchyDensityUp$ carrier
$$
D=(Q,S,R,A,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyDensityUp}$ surface consists exactly of
carrier admission for the dyadic candidate, finite stream windows,
regular-rational readback, approximation row, terminal Real seal,
componentwise transport, displayed replay, provenance, and local naming. The
obligation surface also requires dyadic-budget compatibility between $Q$ and
$A$, window-readback exactness between $S$ and $R$, seal nonescape through
$E$, and absence of quotient real equality, selected representatives,
countable choice, trichotomy, host density, and ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-density-carrier}. The
available analytic rows are $Q,S,R,A,E$, and the available structural rows are
$H,C,P,N$.

The budget compatibility row reads $Q$ before $A$. Thus an accepted density
request cannot claim a dyadic approximation unless the requested dyadic
tolerance is one of the displayed rows.

The readback part then runs through $S$ and $R$. Finite stream windows expose
the inspected prefixes, and the regular-rational row records the comparison
data consumed by the approximation row.

Finally $E$ seals the Real-facing output only after those rows have been
displayed. The transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows preserve the
same finite route, while the refused host objects are absent from the carrier
inventory. Hence the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegularCauchyDensity dyadic approximation]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-density-dyadic-approximation}
Every Real-facing density read exported by an accepted
$\RegularCauchyDensityUp$ carrier factors through the displayed route
$$
\DyadicRatCoreUp \longmapsto \StreamNameUp \longmapsto \RegSeqRatUp
\longmapsto \RealUp
$$
as instantiated by $Q,S,R,A,E$. In particular, the dyadic candidate and
tolerance budget are read first, the finite stream windows and
regular-rational comparison are displayed next, and the terminal Real seal is
available only after the approximation row has consumed those data.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an exported density read and project the accepted carrier. The
only dyadic candidate row is $Q$, so the read begins at the displayed
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$ surface.

The finite observation route then passes through $S$ and $R$. The stream row
opens the inspected windows, and the regular-rational row supplies the
comparison surface needed to judge the dyadic budget.

The approximation row $A$ is downstream of these rows and upstream of the
terminal seal $E$. Since the carrier has no direct edge from $Q$ to $E$, a
Real-facing consumer can receive the seal only through the displayed
dyadic-window-readback route.

The rows $H,C,P,N$ do not add another approximation source. They transport,
replay, source, and name the same finite route, so every exported density read
factors as claimed.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegularCauchyDensity RegSeqRat handoff]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-density-regseqrat-handoff}
An accepted $\RegularCauchyDensityUp$ carrier hands a dyadic approximation to
the $\RealUp$ boundary only through the $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $R$. No Real-facing
consumer of the packet receives a quotient representative, a selected Cauchy
stream, a countable-choice schedule, trichotomy, a host rational-density
theorem, or an ambient Real-completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply the dyadic approximation theorem. It forces every Real-facing density
read to pass through $Q,S,R,A,E$ in that order, with $R$ as the unique
regular-rational readback coordinate.

It remains to inspect what the handoff can export. The carrier's structural
rows $H,C,P,N$ only preserve the same displayed rows under transport, replay,
provenance, and local naming.

The refused objects are not coordinates of
\autoref{def:regular-cauchy-density-carrier}. Therefore the handoff exports a
finite RegSeqRat-mediated approximation route and none of the listed host or
quotient principles.
\end{proof}
