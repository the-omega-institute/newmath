\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\IdealClassUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-idealclass-namecert}

The ideal-class interface $\IdealClassUp$ packages the quotient group of fractional ideals modulo principal ideals in a Dedekind domain. It depends on $\NameCert_{\DedekindUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dedekind-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\QuotientGroupUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-quotientgroup-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:idealclass-carrier}

\begin{theorem}[IdealClass fractional-ideal carrier obligation]\label{thm:idealclass-obligation-fractional-carrier}
The $\IdealClassUp$ carrier is the carried history surface of fractional ideals over the fixed $\DedekindUp$ source. Its endpoints are fractional-ideal representatives whose ring, ideal, and domain rows are inherited from $\NameCert_{\DedekindUp}$, and no endpoint is admitted unless its source ledger identifies the same Dedekind-domain carrier and classifier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the opening dependency on $\NameCert_{\DedekindUp}$. The carrier obligation reads the Dedekind-domain source as the BEDC source field in the roadmap ten-field template, then chooses fractional-ideal histories as the carried target rather than a host-side class type.

The ten-field fit is: traditional target ideal classes of a Dedekind domain; BEDC source the cited $\DedekindUp$ certificate; carrier fractional-ideal histories; classifier still pending principal-ideal equivalence; stability pending quotient-group transport; ledger the source and representative rows; core theorem the carrier admission row; exactness the exclusion of non-Dedekind representatives; standard bridge none; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[IdealClass principal classifier obligation]\label{thm:idealclass-obligation-principal-classifier}
The $\IdealClassUp$ classifier is principal-ideal equivalence on fractional-ideal histories over the same $\DedekindUp$ source. Two representatives are equivalent exactly when the ledger supplies a principal fractional ideal witnessing their quotient relation; this classifier is not the host quotient relation of a quotient type.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the fractional-ideal carrier fixed by \autoref{thm:idealclass-obligation-fractional-carrier}. The classifier row is then the principal-ideal witness relation read over that same Dedekind source, so changing representatives is certified by a visible principal ideal rather than by eliminating a host quotient.

The ten-field fit is: traditional target ideal-class equality; BEDC source $\NameCert_{\DedekindUp}$; carrier fractional-ideal histories; classifier principal-ideal equivalence; stability the witness transport demanded by the quotient certificate; ledger the two representatives and the principal witness; core theorem the classifier admission row; exactness the absence of host quotient soundness; standard bridge none; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[IdealClass quotient operation stability obligation]\label{thm:idealclass-obligation-quotient-operation-stability}
Multiplication of fractional-ideal representatives, the identity representative, and the inverse representative are stable under principal-ideal equivalence. The stability data is supplied by the $\NameCert_{\QuotientGroupUp}$ certificate applied over the classifier of \autoref{thm:idealclass-obligation-principal-classifier}, so $\IdealClassUp$ inherits a quotient-group operation row without changing its Dedekind-domain source.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply \autoref{thm:idealclass-obligation-fractional-carrier} and \autoref{thm:idealclass-obligation-principal-classifier} to fix the representatives and classifier. Then use the opening dependency on $\NameCert_{\QuotientGroupUp}$ to read multiplication, identity, and inverse as quotient-group rows over that classifier.

The ten-field fit is: traditional target the ideal-class group; BEDC source the shared $\DedekindUp$ fractional-ideal carrier together with $\NameCert_{\QuotientGroupUp}$; carrier fractional-ideal histories; classifier principal-ideal equivalence; stability quotient multiplication, identity, and inverse descent; ledger the quotient-group operation row; core theorem representative independence; exactness operation visibility only modulo principal witnesses; standard bridge none; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[IdealClass ledger exactness obligation]\label{thm:idealclass-obligation-ledger-exactness}
The $\IdealClassUp$ ledger records exactly the Dedekind source, the fractional-ideal representative, the principal-ideal witness, the quotient-group operation row, and the exactness boundary. It does not record a detached host quotient, a second Dedekind source, or operation data not transported through the principal classifier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Assemble the carrier row from \autoref{thm:idealclass-obligation-fractional-carrier}, the classifier row from \autoref{thm:idealclass-obligation-principal-classifier}, and the operation row from \autoref{thm:idealclass-obligation-quotient-operation-stability}. These are the only named rows required by the opening $\NameCert_{\DedekindUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\QuotientGroupUp}$ dependencies.

The ten-field fit is: traditional target the public ideal-class ledger; BEDC source the cited Dedekind-domain certificate; carrier fractional-ideal representatives; classifier principal-ideal equivalence; stability quotient-group descent; ledger Dedekind source, representative, principal witness, operation row, and boundary; core theorem exact ledger exposure; exactness the exclusion of detached quotient data; standard bridge none; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{proof}
