\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\IdealClassUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-idealclass-namecert}

The ideal-class interface $\IdealClassUp$ packages the quotient group of fractional ideals modulo principal ideals in a Dedekind domain. It depends on $\NameCert_{\DedekindUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dedekind-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\QuotientGroupUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-quotientgroup-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:idealclass-carrier}

\begin{theorem}[IdealClass fractional-ideal carrier obligation]\label{thm:idealclass-fractional-ideal-carrier-obligation}
An $\IdealClassUp$ carrier endpoint is a $\mathsf{BHist}$ row carrying the $\DedekindUp$ fractional-ideal source, the nonzero and invertible fractional-ideal ledger, and the $\QuotientGroupUp$ source boundary. The endpoint is admitted only when its $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\NameCert$, $\hsame$, and $\Pkg$ rows identify the same Dedekind carrier and the same quotient-group boundary.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.IdealClassUp.IdealClassFractionalIdealCarrier\_obligation}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.IdealClassUp.IdealClassFractionalCarrier\_source\_obligation}
\begin{proof}
Start from the opening dependencies on $\NameCert_{\DedekindUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\QuotientGroupUp}$. The carrier obligation reads the Dedekind fractional-ideal source as the BEDC source field, chooses $\mathsf{BHist}$ rows as the carrier, and records nonzero and invertible fractional-ideal evidence in the ledger before any quotient is formed.

The ten-field fit is: traditional target ideal classes of a Dedekind domain; BEDC source the cited $\DedekindUp$ certificate together with the $\QuotientGroupUp$ source boundary; carrier $\mathsf{BHist}$ rows for fractional ideals; classifier pending principal-ideal equivalence; stability pending quotient transport; ledger the Dedekind source plus nonzero and invertible evidence; core theorem carrier endpoint admission; exactness exclusion of detached fractional-ideal representatives; standard bridge none; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[IdealClass principal classifier obligation]\label{thm:idealclass-principal-classifier-obligation}
The $\IdealClassUp$ classifier relates two carried fractional-ideal endpoints exactly when their quotient product is principal. Reflexivity, symmetry, transitivity, and carrier transport are inherited from the $\DedekindUp$ fractional-ideal rows and the $\QuotientGroupUp$ classifier boundary, so the classifier is a carried BEDC relation rather than a host quotient relation.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.IdealClassUp.IdealClassPrincipalClassifier\_semanticNameCert}
\begin{proof}
Use the fractional-ideal carrier fixed by \autoref{thm:idealclass-fractional-ideal-carrier-obligation}. The classifier row is the principal quotient-product witness relation over the same Dedekind source, so equality of endpoints is certified by a visible principal ideal and not by eliminating a quotient type.

The ten-field fit is: traditional target ideal-class equality; BEDC source $\NameCert_{\DedekindUp}$ with the $\QuotientGroupUp$ source boundary; carrier carried fractional-ideal endpoints; classifier principal quotient-product equivalence; stability reflexivity, symmetry, transitivity, and carrier transport; ledger the two endpoints and the principal witness; core theorem classifier admission and equivalence laws; exactness absence of host quotient soundness; standard bridge none; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.IdealClassUp.IdealClassPrincipalClassifier\_empty\_boundary\_laws}

\begin{theorem}[IdealClass quotient operation exactness obligation]\label{thm:idealclass-quotient-operation-exactness-obligation}
Fractional-ideal multiplication descends through the principal-class classifier of \autoref{thm:idealclass-principal-classifier-obligation}. The $\IdealClassUp$ ledger hides only the principal witness while preserving the $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\NameCert$, $\hsame$, and $\Pkg$ provenance rows for the Dedekind source, the carried representatives, and the $\QuotientGroupUp$ operation boundary.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.IdealClassUp.IdealClassQuotientOperation\_exactness\_obligation}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.IdealClassUp.IdealClassQuotientOperation\_empty\_product\_exactness}
\begin{proof}
First apply \autoref{thm:idealclass-fractional-ideal-carrier-obligation} and \autoref{thm:idealclass-principal-classifier-obligation} to fix the carried representatives and the principal classifier. Multiplication is then read as a $\QuotientGroupUp$ operation row over that classifier, with identity and inverse data transported through the same source boundary.

The ten-field fit is: traditional target the ideal-class group operation; BEDC source the shared $\DedekindUp$ fractional-ideal source and $\NameCert_{\QuotientGroupUp}$; carrier carried fractional-ideal endpoints; classifier principal quotient-product equivalence; stability multiplication descent, identity descent, inverse descent, and representative independence; ledger preserved $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\NameCert$, $\hsame$, and $\Pkg$ provenance with only the principal witness hidden; core theorem quotient operation exactness; exactness no detached quotient and no lost provenance row; standard bridge none; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{proof}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:idealclass-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[IdealClass scoped source certificate]\label{thm:idealclass-scoped-source-certificate}
The carrier obligation of \autoref{thm:idealclass-fractional-ideal-carrier-obligation}, the principal classifier obligation of \autoref{thm:idealclass-principal-classifier-obligation}, and the quotient operation exactness obligation of \autoref{thm:idealclass-quotient-operation-exactness-obligation} jointly determine the scoped $\IdealClassUp$ source certificate over the cited $\DedekindUp$ and $\QuotientGroupUp$ sources. Its carrier endpoints are $\mathsf{BHist}$ rows with Dedekind fractional-ideal provenance and quotient boundary provenance; its classifier equality is the principal quotient-product witness relation; its multiplication, identity, and inverse descent are read through the $\QuotientGroupUp$ operation boundary; and its ledger hides only the principal witness while preserving $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\NameCert$, $\hsame$, and $\Pkg$ provenance.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.IdealClassUp.IdealClassScoped\_source\_certificate}
\begin{proof}
Repack the carrier site as the carrier and ledger source of the certificate. The admitted endpoints are the $\mathsf{BHist}$ rows already tied to the $\DedekindUp$ fractional-ideal source and the $\QuotientGroupUp$ boundary, with $\NameCert$, $\hsame$, and $\Pkg$ rows kept in the same source scope.

Repack the classifier site as the equality field. The relation is not a host quotient equality: it is the principal quotient-product witness relation on the carried endpoints, and its equivalence behavior is the one transported through the named Dedekind and quotient-group sources.

Repack the operation site as the stability and exactness fields. Multiplication, identity, and inverse descent are read through the $\QuotientGroupUp$ operation boundary, and the exactness ledger hides only the principal witness while retaining the visible $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\NameCert$, $\hsame$, and $\Pkg$ provenance rows.

These three repackings fill the roadmap ten-field template for $\IdealClassUp$: traditional target, BEDC source, carrier, classifier, stability, ledger, core theorem surface, exactness exclusion, standard bridge status, and verification status. Thus the scoped source certificate is determined within exactly the $\DedekindUp$, $\QuotientGroupUp$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\NameCert$, $\hsame$, and $\Pkg$ scope recorded above.
\end{proof}
