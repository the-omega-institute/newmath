\begin{definition}[Dyadic mesh scale-normalization carrier]
\label{def:dyadic-mesh-scale-normalization-carrier}
A $\DyadicMeshScaleNormalizationUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
M=(G,A,B,S,D,T,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $G$ is the $\DyadicMeshUp$ mesh row whose cells are to be compared or consumed by a finite-net route.
\item $A$ and $B$ are the source mesh-scale rows exposed before normalization.
\item $S$ is the common-scale row, recording one dyadic scale that refines both $A$ and $B$ for the requested comparison window.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ arithmetic ledger for rescaling endpoints, widths, and cell representatives from $A$ and $B$ into $S$.
\item $T$ is the $\DyadicToleranceScaleUp$ precision row that binds the normalization scale to the requested tolerance.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row opened only after $S,D,T$ have been displayed.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row obtained from the normalized dyadic windows.
\item $E$ is the $\RealDyadicEmbeddingUp$ seal row reached after the normalized dyadic readback.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the mesh row, source scales, common scale, arithmetic ledger, tolerance row, window row, readback, seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a mesh comparison request through $G,A,B,S,D,T,W,R,E,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\DyadicMeshScaleNormalizationUp$, $\DyadicMeshUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\DyadicToleranceScaleUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealDyadicEmbeddingUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DyadicMeshScaleNormalizationUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $G,A,B,S,D,T,W,R,E,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for host real equality, quotient streams, selected representatives, hidden cofinal refinements, countable choice, trichotomy, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Dyadic mesh scale-normalization NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:dyadic-mesh-scale-normalization-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\DyadicMeshScaleNormalizationUp$ carrier
$$
M=(G,A,B,S,D,T,W,R,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\DyadicMeshScaleNormalizationUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the dyadic mesh row $G$, source scale rows $A,B$, common-scale row $S$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ rescaling ledger $D$, $\DyadicToleranceScaleUp$ precision row $T$, $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row $W$, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row $R$, $\RealDyadicEmbeddingUp$ seal row $E$, componentwise transport $H$, displayed continuation replay $C$, package provenance $P$, and local naming row $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:dyadic-mesh-scale-normalization-carrier}. The analytic rows are exactly the mesh row $G$, the two source scales $A,B$, the common scale $S$, the dyadic arithmetic ledger $D$, the tolerance-scale row $T$, the finite-window row $W$, the regular rational readback $R$, and the real-dyadic seal $E$.

The normalization obligation is read only from $S,D,T$. The row $S$ names the common dyadic scale, $D$ records the arithmetic that rewrites both source scales into that scale, and $T$ binds the scale choice to the requested tolerance.

The stream and real-facing rows are downstream of the same normalization. Thus $W$ opens only normalized finite windows, $R$ reads the corresponding regular rational data, and $E$ seals the route through $\RealDyadicEmbeddingUp$ rather than through host equality of real numbers.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite route. Since the carrier has no coordinate for quotient streams, selected representatives, hidden cofinal refinements, countable choice, trichotomy, or ambient completeness, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Dyadic mesh common-scale exhaustion]
\label{thm:dyadic-mesh-scale-normalization-common-scale-exhaustion}
Every finite-net, dyadic-approximation, or $\RealDyadicEmbeddingUp$ consumer exported by an accepted $\DyadicMeshScaleNormalizationUp$ packet factors through the displayed route
$$
\DyadicMeshUp \longmapsto \DyadicRatCoreUp \longmapsto \DyadicToleranceScaleUp \longmapsto \StreamNameUp \longmapsto \RegSeqRatUp \longmapsto \RealDyadicEmbeddingUp .
$$
Consequently such a consumer may read the original mesh, the two source scales, the common scale, the dyadic rescaling ledger, the tolerance row, normalized stream windows, regular rational readback, and real-dyadic seal, but it receives no host real equality, quotient stream, selected representative, hidden cofinal refinement, countable choice, trichotomy, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an admitted consumer read. By \autoref{def:dyadic-mesh-scale-normalization-carrier}, the only mesh-facing data before readback are $G,A,B,S,D,T,W$.

The common scale is forced through $S$. The rows $A$ and $B$ expose the two source scales, while $D$ supplies the dyadic rescaling ledger that rewrites both into $S$ under the tolerance request recorded by $T$.

Only after that scale has been displayed may the packet expose $W$, $R$, and $E$. The finite windows are therefore normalized before regular-rational readback, and the real-facing seal is the $\RealDyadicEmbeddingUp$ row downstream of that readback.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve the same dependency order under transport, replay, provenance, and local naming. Hence every exported finite-net, approximation, or real-dyadic consumer factors through the displayed common-scale route and cannot bypass it by using any excluded host, quotient, choice, or completeness datum.
\end{proof}
