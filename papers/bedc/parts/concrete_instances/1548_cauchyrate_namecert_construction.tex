\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CauchyRateUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyrate-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\CauchyRateUp$ interface names the effective-rate certificate consumed between regular Cauchy data and real-completion readers. In the constructive-analysis reading of Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, a usable real name supplies an explicit precision request, a modulus schedule, a tolerance row, and a regular rational source before any completion-facing consumer may read a real seal. The packet below records exactly that finite route through $\CauchyModulusUp$, $\DyadicTailBoundUp$, $\RegularCauchyFamilyUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$.

The same packet is the effective-rate dependency edge from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert} into the completion-facing \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert} seal boundary: a precision request selects a modulus schedule, the schedule opens a finite $\StreamNameUp$ window, the window licenses the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, and the resulting regular row is admitted through the $\RealUp$ seal gate.

The rate-facing carrier is also the consumer surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchymodulus-namecert}. Its tolerance and readback route consumes the dyadic tail-bound ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadictailbound-namecert} and the regular-rational source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert} before the packet reaches the $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ boundary.

\begin{definition}[CauchyRate BHist source]
\label{def:cauchy-rate-bhist-source}
A $\CauchyRateUp$ source packet is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ row
$$
K=(p,\mu,\tau,F,Q,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $p$ is a unary precision row naming the requested effective rate;
\item $\mu$ is the $\CauchyModulusUp$ schedule row that assigns the finite inspection window for $p$;
\item $\tau$ is the $\DyadicTailBoundUp$ tolerance row used to justify the scheduled tail comparison;
\item $F$ is the $\RegularCauchyFamilyUp$ source row whose inspected family entries are read through the schedule $\mu$;
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ source row obtained from the same scheduled family window and tolerance row;
\item $E$ is the $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ consumer row reached only through $Q$, $F$, $\tau$, and $\mu$;
\item $H$ records the componentwise $\hsame$ transports for precision, schedule, tolerance, family source, regular-sequence source, and completion-consumer rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes from the consumer request to the schedule, tolerance, source, regular readback, and completion boundary;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyModulusUp$, $\DyadicTailBoundUp$, $\RegularCauchyFamilyUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyRateUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The displayed transports and routes are the rows
$$
\begin{aligned}
&\hsame(p,p'),\quad \hsame(\mu,\mu'),\quad \hsame(\tau,\tau'),\quad
\hsame(F,F'),\quad \hsame(Q,Q'),\quad \hsame(E,E'),\\
&\Cont(p,\mu),\quad \Cont(\mu,\tau),\quad \Cont((\mu,\tau),F),\quad
\Cont((F,\tau),Q),\quad \Cont((Q,F,\tau,\mu),E).
\end{aligned}
$$
The classifier compares accepted packets only by these displayed rows, their $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyRateUp}$ row.
\end{definition}
