Following Mac Lane's monad multiplication terminology together with Bishop--Bridges and Kelley completion language, the $\CauchyCompletionMultiplicationUp$ packet records the finite BEDC join route that flattens a completion of completion into the displayed completion boundary. It sits after the monad surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchycompletionmonad-namecert}, the idempotence row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-completion-idempotence-namecert}, and the associativity ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-completion-associativity-namecert}; its public L10 face is read through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet names monad multiplication as finite carrier data; it is not a quotient completion, selected representative, host equality comparison, countable-choice construction, ambient completeness principle, or completeness theorem for $\RealUp$.

\begin{definition}[Cauchy completion multiplication carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-completion-multiplication-carrier}
A $\CauchyCompletionMultiplicationUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(M,O,I,A,S,R,D,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the $\CauchyCompletionMonadUp$ transformer row that records the completion endofunctor, unit, and bind surface consumed by the join.
\item $O$ is the outer completion row, presenting the completion-of-completion input before flattening.
\item $I$ is the $\CauchyCompletionIdempotenceUp$ row that identifies a completed completion boundary with the displayed completion boundary through finite certificate data.
\item $A$ is the $\CauchyCompletionAssociativityUp$ ledger that keeps nested bind and flattening routes in the same displayed order.
\item $S$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window row exposed only after the nested completion route has passed through $M,O,I,A$.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row attached to the same flattened stream windows.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row used by the flattened readback and terminal seal.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row reached after the displayed StreamName, RegSeqRat, and DyadicRatCore faces have been fixed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the monad, outer-completion, idempotence, associativity, window, readback, tolerance, seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from an outer completion request through the flattening route and then through $S,R,D,E$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyCompletionMultiplicationUp$, $\CauchyCompletionMonadUp$, $\CauchyCompletionIdempotenceUp$, $\CauchyCompletionAssociativityUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyCompletionMultiplicationUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier of accepted packets compares only these twelve displayed rows. It has no coordinate for quotient completion equality, selected representatives, countable choice, host equality, ambient Real completeness, completeness of $\RealUp$, or bridge credentials.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy completion multiplication NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-multiplication-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CauchyCompletionMultiplicationUp$ carrier $K=(M,O,I,A,S,R,D,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyCompletionMultiplicationUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed monad transformer, outer completion, idempotence, associativity, stream-window, readback, tolerance, real-seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item outer-to-inner flattening, requiring every completion-of-completion request to pass through $M,O,I,A$ before any public L10 row is exposed;
\item shared window, readback, and seal routing, requiring $S,R,D,E$ to be read as one flattened route rather than as detached representatives;
\item componentwise transport, requiring $H$ to preserve the twelve displayed coordinates and no additional completion witness;
\item replay, requiring $C$ to reproduce the same route from outer completion request through $M,O,I,A,S,R,D,E$;
\item provenance, requiring $P$ to name only the cited monad, idempotence, associativity, stream, regular rational, dyadic, Real, and BEDC structural sources;
\item ledger nonescape, requiring $N$ and the classifier to export no quotient completion, selected representative, countable choice, host equality, ambient Real completeness, $\RealUp$ completeness theorem, Lean verification claim, or bridge credential.
\end{enumerate}
No local obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:cauchy-completion-multiplication-carrier}. The tuple lists exactly the monad transformer, outer completion, idempotence, associativity ledger, public L10 rows, transport, replay, provenance, and local name.

The outer-to-inner flattening obligation is forced by the tuple order. A request addressed to the outer completion first reads $M$ and $O$, then uses $I$ to display the boundary as a single completion row, and uses $A$ to keep the nested bind route coherent.

Shared routing is the projection to $S,R,D,E$. These rows are downstream of $M,O,I,A$, so the packet cannot expose a stream window, regular rational readback, dyadic tolerance, or Real seal detached from the displayed flattening route.

Componentwise transport and replay are read through $H$ and $C$. The transport row identifies accepted packets coordinate by coordinate, while the continuation row reproduces the same request path from the outer completion through the public L10 face.

Finally, provenance and nonescape are exhausted by $P,N$ and by the classifier. Since the carrier has no coordinate for quotient completion equality, selected representatives, countable choice, host equality, ambient completeness, $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, or bridge credentials, none of these can be exported by a local obligation.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy completion multiplication flattening route]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-multiplication-flattening-route}
Every accepted $\CauchyCompletionMultiplicationUp$ packet flattens the outer completion through the route
$$
M \longmapsto O \longmapsto I \longmapsto A
$$
before exposing the public L10 face
$$
S \longmapsto R \longmapsto D \longmapsto E .
$$
Thus a completion-of-completion consumer can read the StreamName window, RegSeqRat readback, DyadicRatCore tolerance, and Real seal only after the monad transformer, outer completion row, idempotence row, and associativity ledger have been displayed.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted packet $K=(M,O,I,A,S,R,D,E,H,C,P,N)$. Projection gives the monad transformer $M$ and the outer completion row $O$ before any public L10 coordinate is available.

The idempotence row $I$ supplies the displayed route from the completed completion boundary back to the single completion boundary. The associativity ledger $A$ records that nested bind and flattening reads follow the same route.

Only after $M,O,I,A$ have been read does the carrier expose $S,R,D,E$. The continuation row $C$ replays this order, and componentwise transport by $H$ preserves it for transported packets.

Consequently every public read from an accepted packet factors through the stated flattening route before the StreamName, RegSeqRat, DyadicRatCore, and Real-seal rows can be consumed.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy completion multiplication Real seal nonescape]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-multiplication-real-seal-nonescape}
Let $K=(M,O,I,A,S,R,D,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyCompletionMultiplicationUp$ carrier. Every $\RealUp$-facing consumer of $K$ receives only the displayed $\StreamNameUp$ row $S$, $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $R$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ row $D$, terminal $\RealUp$ seal $E$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance $P$, and local $\NameCert$ row $N$ after the flattening route through $M,O,I,A$.

In particular, the consumer receives no quotient completion, selected representative, countable-choice selector, host equality comparison, ambient Real completeness datum, or completeness theorem for $\RealUp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet. A $\RealUp$-facing read reaches the terminal seal only through the flattened public face $S,R,D,E$.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ do not add data to that face. They transport it componentwise, replay the displayed route, record the cited sources, and name the local certificate.

The excluded objects are absent from the carrier and from the classifier. There is no coordinate from which a quotient completion, selected representative, countable-choice selector, host equality comparison, ambient Real completeness datum, or $\RealUp$ completeness theorem could be read.

Therefore the Real-facing consumer receives exactly the displayed rows listed in the statement, downstream of the flattening route through $M,O,I,A$.
\end{proof}
