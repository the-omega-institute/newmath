\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RegularCauchyPowerUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-power-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges on constructive Cauchy real arithmetic, the $\RegularCauchyPowerUp$ packet records finite integer powers of one displayed regular Cauchy rational name as a bounded product route. It sits after the regular rational source surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the finite observation windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the dyadic arithmetic core of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the binary regular product packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regularcauchyproduct-namecert}, the finite product packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-finite-product-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The carrier packages repeated multiplication by a unary exponent without importing host exponentiation, quotient stream equality, selected limits, trichotomy, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[Regular Cauchy power carrier]\label{def:regular-cauchy-power-carrier}
A $\RegularCauchyPowerUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(n,A,W,D,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $n$ is the finite unary exponent row. It records the requested integer power by a bounded successor chain, with the zeroth row reserved for the displayed unit read.
\item $A$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ source row whose scheduled rational observations are multiplied repeatedly.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window family for the source reads, intermediate product reads, and final power-facing read.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ budget and observation row, storing source dyadic observations, intermediate product observations, requested radii, unit cells, and slack cells.
\item $S$ is the displayed multiplication schedule. Each successor step cites either a $\RegularCauchyProductUp$ row for the current accumulator multiplied by $A$ or a $\RegularCauchyFiniteProductUp$ row for the finite fold named by $n$.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row for the final power, obtained only from $n,A,W,D,S$.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached after the regular readback of the power has been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the exponent, source, windows, dyadic observations, product schedule, readback, seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from any finite power request through the same rows.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RegularCauchyPowerUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegularCauchyProductUp$, $\RegularCauchyFiniteProductUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyPowerUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $n,A,W,D,S,R,E,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for host exponentiation, quotient streams, selected limits, ambient completeness, Lean verification, or bridge credentials.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy power NameCert obligations]\label{thm:regular-cauchy-power-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RegularCauchyPowerUp$ carrier
$$
K=(n,A,W,D,S,R,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyPowerUp}$ surface consists exactly of five finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the unary exponent, source $\RegSeqRatUp$ row, synchronized $\StreamNameUp$ windows, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observations and slack cells, repeated product schedule, final readback, $\RealUp$ seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local name;
\item exponent-window coverage, requiring each successor stage named by $n$ to open the source row $A$ and the accumulator window used by the displayed product step;
\item dyadic product-budget exactness, requiring each intermediate product observation to consume its assigned radius and slack cells from $D$ and the schedule row $S$;
\item RegSeqRat readback stability, requiring the final power readback $R$ to depend only on the displayed exponent, source, windows, dyadic budget rows, and product schedule;
\item Real seal non-escape, requiring $E,H,C,P,N$ to expose no host exponentiation, quotient stream equality, selected limit, trichotomy, ambient completeness theorem, Lean verification, or bridge credential.
\end{enumerate}
No Regular Cauchy power obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier of \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-power-carrier}. The displayed tuple $n,A,W,D,S,R,E,H,C,P,N$ is the entire public surface, so carrier admission is exactly the admission of those rows.

Exponent-window coverage is forced by the unary exponent row. The base stage names the displayed unit read, and each successor stage opens the source row $A$ together with the accumulator window selected by $W$.

Dyadic exactness is the joint role of $D$ and $S$. The observation row supplies the component and accumulator dyadic cells, while the schedule row records the binary product route or finite product route used at each successor stage.

The final regular readback row $R$ is downstream of $n,A,W,D,S$. Therefore readback stability follows from the carrier order: no power-facing $\RegSeqRatUp$ data can be inspected until the exponent route, synchronized windows, dyadic observations, and product budget have already been displayed.

Finally, the seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local name rows preserve only this displayed route. Since none of the excluded objects is a coordinate of the carrier, the listed five rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy power product induction]\label{thm:regular-cauchy-power-product-induction}
Let
$$
K=(n,A,W,D,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\RegularCauchyPowerUp$ carrier. Induction on the finite unary exponent row $n$ shows that every intermediate power read remains inside the displayed product schedule $S$. The base read is the unit row recorded in $D$, and each successor read is obtained from the previous accumulator and the source row $A$ through the next $\RegularCauchyProductUp$ or $\RegularCauchyFiniteProductUp$ schedule cell. Thus no stage of the power read leaves the rows $n,A,W,D,S,R,E,H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Proceed by induction over the unary exponent row. In the base case, the carrier exposes the unit cell in $D$ and the corresponding base entry in $S$, so the zeroth power read is already inside the displayed schedule.

For the successor step, assume the accumulator read for the current exponent has been displayed through $W,D,S$. The next schedule cell in $S$ cites the binary $\RegularCauchyProductUp$ route for multiplying that accumulator by the source row $A$, or the equivalent $\RegularCauchyFiniteProductUp$ fold cell named by the same finite exponent route.

The dyadic budget row $D$ supplies the observation and slack cells for that product cell, while $W$ supplies the synchronized source and accumulator windows. The successor accumulator is therefore a displayed schedule read, not a host exponentiation step.

After the last successor stage, the final accumulator is repacked as $R$ and then sealed by $E$. The remaining rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same route, so every intermediate and final power read stays inside the displayed product schedule.
\end{proof}
