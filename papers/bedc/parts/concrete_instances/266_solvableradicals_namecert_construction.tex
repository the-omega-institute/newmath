\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\SolvableRadicalsUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-solvableradicals-namecert}

The radical-tower interface $\SolvableRadicalsUp$ packages a tower of radical extensions $K=K_0\subseteq K_1\subseteq\cdots\subseteq K_r$ where each $K_{i+1}=K_i(\alpha_i)$ with $\alpha_i^{n_i}\in K_i$. It depends on $\NameCert_{\FieldExtUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-fieldext-namecert}), $\NameCert_{\GaloisGroupUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-galoisgroup-namecert}), and $\NameCert_{\NumFieldUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-numfield-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:solvableradicals-carrier}

A $\SolvableRadicalsUp$ token carries a finite tower depth $r$, a $\SeqUp$-typed sequence of intermediate field rows $K_0,\ldots,K_r$, a sequence of root indices $n_0,\ldots,n_{r-1}$, and a sequence of radicand witnesses $\alpha_i$ with $\alpha_i^{n_i}\in K_i$. The carrier accepts a polynomial $P$ when $P$ splits in $K_r$ and each step is genuinely radical. The classifier is $\hsame$ on the tower depth and intermediate-field rows; the ledger keeps the depth $r$, the root indices, the radicand witnesses, and the splitting verdict visible.

\begin{theorem}[SolvableRadicals tower-step obligation]
\label{thm:solvableradicals-tower-step-obligation}
Every accepted $\SolvableRadicalsUp$ radical-tower row exposes the finite
$\SeqUp$ tower depth, the root-index rows, the radicand witnesses, and the
$\FieldExtUp$ radical-step certificate for each adjacent pair of intermediate
field rows. For each displayed step $K_i\subseteq K_{i+1}$, the ledger records
the row $\alpha_i^{n_i}\in K_i$ and the corresponding extension row for
$K_{i+1}=K_i(\alpha_i)$.

The obligation is only the finite tower-step surface. It does not assert a
Galois-correspondence theorem, abstract solvable-group classification, or a
radical-closure bridge.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SolvableRadicalsUp.SolvableRadicalsTowerStepLedger\_assoc\_surface}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet to its tower-depth coordinate and intermediate
field sequence. These rows give the finite $\SeqUp$ spine
$K_0,\ldots,K_r$.

For each adjacent pair, read the root-index and radicand coordinates. The
$\FieldExtUp$ certificate attached to that pair records the radical-step
witness and the equation $\alpha_i^{n_i}\in K_i$ in the same ledger.

Repacking the displayed adjacent-step rows gives the tower-step obligation.
No classification of solvable groups or radical closures is invoked.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SolvableRadicalsUp.SolvableRadicalsTowerStep\_obligation}

\begin{theorem}[SolvableRadicals intermediate-field stability]
\label{thm:solvableradicals-intermediate-field-stability}
For $\SolvableRadicalsUp$, $\hsame$ transport of fixed-depth intermediate
field rows preserves the displayed radical-step ledger. If two tower
presentations have the same finite depth and their corresponding intermediate
field rows are related by the fixed classifier, then each root-index,
radicand, and $\FieldExtUp$ radical-step certificate transports to the
matching adjacent field row.

The statement is a fixed-depth stability obligation. It does not identify
abstract Galois groups or claim a full Galois correspondence.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with two accepted tower rows of the same displayed depth. The classifier
relates corresponding intermediate field rows by $\hsame$ and keeps the
finite index positions fixed.

At each adjacent position, transport the root-index, radicand, and
$\FieldExtUp$ radical-step certificate along the corresponding field-row
classifiers. Since the positions are fixed by the tower depth, no new step is
created and no step is omitted.

The transported packet therefore has the same radical-step ledger at the
matching positions. The argument is finite ledger transport rather than a
Galois-correspondence or solvable-group classification result.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SolvableRadicalsUp.SolvableRadicalsTowerPacket\_intermediate\_field\_stability}

\begin{theorem}[SolvableRadicals splitting ledger exactness]
\label{thm:solvableradicals-splitting-ledger-exactness}
In an accepted $\SolvableRadicalsUp$ packet, the accepted polynomial row is
identified with the displayed splitting verdict in the top field $K_r$. The
ledger reads the polynomial over $K_0$, transports it through the finite tower,
and records the top-field factor rows exactly as the carrier's splitting
verdict.

This exactness statement does not assert a Galois-correspondence theorem,
abstract solvable-group classification, transcendental methods, or a standard
radical-closure bridge.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the polynomial coordinate over the base field row $K_0$ and the finite
tower coordinate ending at $K_r$. Both are part of the accepted packet.

Next inspect the splitting verdict. Its factor rows live in the displayed top
field, and the ledger ties them to the original polynomial row through the
finite tower already exposed by the carrier.

Thus the accepted polynomial row and the top-field splitting verdict are the
same ledger surface. No additional correspondence theorem, solvable-group
classification, or transcendental method is projected from the packet.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SolvableRadicalsUp.SolvableRadicalsTowerPacket\_splitting\_ledger\_exactness}
