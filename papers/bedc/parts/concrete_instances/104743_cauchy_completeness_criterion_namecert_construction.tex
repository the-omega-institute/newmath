Following Bishop and Bridges constructive analysis, the $\CauchyCompletenessCriterionUp$ packet records the finite Cauchy-criterion route by which a metric source, an explicit Cauchy modulus, named stream windows, regular rational readback, dyadic tolerance accounting, and a terminal $\RealUp$ seal are presented to completion-facing consumers. It sits over the metric completion surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metriccompletion-namecert}, the regular rational rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the finite windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the terminal real seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet gives later completion chapters a single criterion object to cite instead of restating the Cauchy-modulus handoff locally.

\begin{definition}[CauchyCompletenessCriterion carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-completeness-criterion-carrier}
A $\CauchyCompletenessCriterionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(M,\mu,S,R,D,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the metric source row, carrying the named carrier, distance observations, and completion-facing request inherited by the metric completion route.
\item $\mu$ is the explicit Cauchy modulus row, displayed as finite threshold data for each inspected tolerance request.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window family naming the finite tail observations read from the source stream and the modulus thresholds.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row extracted from those windows.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger recording the rational budget, tail slack, and comparison bounds used by the readback.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row, visible only after $M,\mu,S,R,D$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the metric source, modulus, windows, readback, dyadic ledger, Real seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a completion-facing Cauchy request through $M,\mu,S,R,D,E,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyCompletenessCriterionUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyCompletenessCriterionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through the ten displayed rows. It has no coordinate for quotient completions, selected limits, countable-choice schedules, trichotomy decisions, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyCompletenessCriterion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-completeness-criterion-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CauchyCompletenessCriterionUp$ carrier $Q=(M,\mu,S,R,D,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyCompletenessCriterionUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the metric source, explicit Cauchy modulus, finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, $\RealUp$ seal, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming. Every completion-facing read must consume $M,\mu,S,R,D$ before it can inspect $E,H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-completeness-criterion-carrier}. The rows $M$ and $\mu$ expose the metric source and the Cauchy modulus as finite packet data.

Read the observation rows next. The row $S$ stores exactly the inspected tail windows, $R$ records the regular rational readback from those windows, and $D$ stores the dyadic tolerance budget attached to the same finite observations.

Finally inspect the seal and structural rows. Since $E,H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the already displayed route, the certificate has no remaining coordinate from which a quotient completion, selected limit, countable-choice schedule, trichotomy decision, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness theorem could be exported.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyCompletenessCriterion Real-completion handoff]
\label{thm:cauchy-completeness-criterion-real-completion-handoff}
Every $\RealUp$ or metric-completion consumer citing an accepted $\CauchyCompletenessCriterionUp$ packet receives only the finite route
$$
M,\mu,S,R,D,E,H,C,P,N .
$$
The handoff first displays the metric source and Cauchy modulus, then the $\StreamNameUp$ windows, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, and only then the $\RealUp$ seal together with transport, replay, provenance, and local naming.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with an accepted carrier and apply \autoref{thm:cauchy-completeness-criterion-namecert-obligations}. The consumer-facing data are exhausted by the displayed rows $M,\mu,S,R,D,E,H,C,P,N$.

The criterion route is ordered. A completion-facing read must first expose the metric source and explicit modulus, then read finite stream windows and regular rational observations, and then consume the dyadic tolerance ledger.

The Real seal is terminal with respect to this route. Because quotient completions, selected limits, countable-choice schedules, trichotomy decisions, and ambient $\RealUp$ completeness are absent from the carrier, every exported handoff is the displayed finite criterion packet.
\end{proof}
