\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\MetaCICCriticalPathUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-metaciccriticalpath-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\MetaCICCriticalPathUp$ packet realizes the critical-path reading of \autoref{ch:metacic-audit-synthesis} as a finite NameCert packet. It is narrower than the synthesis packet: it records the path from closed strong normalisation to the normal-form consistency row, keeps the subject-reduction obstruction visible, and names the substitution, confluence, decidability, discharge, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows needed by downstream MetaCIC consumers.

It sits beside the subject-reduction obstruction packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacic-subject-reduction-obstruction-namecert}, the normalization audit packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacicnormalizationaudit-namecert}, the decidability boundary packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacicdecidableboundary-namecert}, and the normal false exclusion packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-normalfalseexclusion-namecert}. Those siblings supply the named rows; the present packet records the finite route by which a reader may use them together without converting the blocked edge into a theorem.

\begin{definition}[MetaCIC critical-path packet]
\label{def:metacic-critical-path-packet}
A $\MetaCICCriticalPathUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(S,N,O,U,D,H,C,P,L)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the closed-strong-normalization route, read through the candidate-mediated normalization surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacicnormalizationaudit-namecert};
\item $N$ is the normal-form consistency row, read through the normal false exclusion packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-normalfalseexclusion-namecert};
\item $O$ is the subject-reduction obstruction row, read through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacic-subject-reduction-obstruction-namecert};
\item $U$ is the substitution, confluence, and decidability handoff row, naming the closed substitution support, conditional confluence route, and bounded checker boundary used before any consistency read is attempted;
\item $D$ is the discharge-bundle socket, naming the missing subject-reduction input as a socket rather than as a supplied theorem;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $S,N,O,U,D,P,L$;
\item $C$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ routes from the strong-normalization row to the normal-form row, from the handoff row to the checker and confluence boundaries, and from the obstruction row to the discharge socket;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over the MetaCIC audit synthesis chapter and the four sibling concrete packets cited above;
\item $L$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MetaCICCriticalPathUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for closed subject reduction, no coordinate for full closed consistency, no global beta-conversion decision procedure, and no quotient over MetaCIC terms.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC critical-path consistency handoff]
\label{thm:metacic-critical-path-consistency-handoff}
Every accepted $\MetaCICCriticalPathUp$ packet hands a consistency-facing consumer from the closed-strong-normalization row $S$ to the normal-form consistency row $N$ only through the displayed route rows $H,C,P,L$. The consumer may read the normal false exclusion sibling after the normalization route has supplied the normal-form endpoint, but it cannot read a full closed-consistency theorem unless the discharge-bundle socket $D$ is separately filled.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier $K=(S,N,O,U,D,H,C,P,L)$. The rows $S$ and $N$ are the only positive rows on the consistency path: $S$ records the closed-strong-normalization route, and $N$ records the normal-form false-exclusion endpoint.

The route row $C$ fixes the passage from $S$ to $N$, while $H,P,L$ preserve the same path under componentwise transport, provenance readback, and local naming. This gives the consumer the closed-normal consistency handoff named by the sibling packets.

The discharge socket $D$ is a separate coordinate. Since it is recorded as a socket and not as a filled subject-reduction theorem, the handoff stops at the normal-form endpoint and does not export full closed consistency.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC critical-path obstruction boundary]
\label{thm:metacic-critical-path-obstruction-boundary}
For every accepted $\MetaCICCriticalPathUp$ packet, the obstruction row $O$ remains visible under the substitution, confluence, and decidability handoff row $U$ and under all componentwise transports in $H$. Agreement on $S,N,U,D,H,C,P,L$ cannot erase $O$, cannot convert $D$ into a discharged bundle, and cannot promote the normal-form consistency route to closed subject reduction or full closed consistency.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier definition lists $O$ as a named row. It is not derived from $S$, $N$, or $U$; it is part of the displayed packet and is read through the subject-reduction obstruction sibling.

The row $U$ collects the supporting handoffs for substitution, confluence, and decidability. These handoffs explain how local syntax movement, conditional confluence, and bounded checking interact with the route, but none of them fills the subject-reduction discharge socket $D$.

Componentwise transport through $H$ and route replay through $C$ preserve the same rows. Therefore a transported packet still contains $O$ and still treats $D$ as a socket, so the excluded closed subject-reduction and full closed-consistency conclusions remain outside the packet.
\end{proof}
