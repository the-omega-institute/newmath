It sits downstream of the directed-window Cauchy-net surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchynet-namecert}, the zero-distance classifier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separatedmetric-namecert}, the completion-facing handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metriccompletion-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. \begin{definition}[CauchyNetHausdorffUniqueness carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-net-hausdorff-uniqueness-carrier}
A $\CauchyNetHausdorffUniquenessUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
U=(D,L_0,L_1,S,M,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the $\CauchyNetUp$ directed-window route, including the index preorder, late-window Cauchy rows, and finite metric-distance reads for the same net;
\item $L_0$ and $L_1$ are two displayed limit reads over $D$, written through the finite routes
$$
D \longmapsto L_0
$$
and
$$
D \longmapsto L_1;
$$
\item $S$ is the $\SeparatedMetricUp$ or Hausdorff-metric separation row that displays zero distance between the two limit reads after both are read from the same directed-window route;
\item $M$ is the $\MetricCompletionUp$ handoff row, recording that completion-facing consumers see only the Cauchy-net route, the two displayed limit reads, and the separated comparison row;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $D,L_0,L_1,S,M,C,P,N$;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay through the directed Cauchy windows, the two limit reads, the separated comparison, and the metric-completion handoff;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyNetUp$, $\SeparatedMetricUp$, $\MetricCompletionUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyNetHausdorffUniquenessUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no quotient-net equality, no selected representative limit, no host equality of points, no countable-choice schedule, and no ambient completeness theorem.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyNetHausdorffUniqueness NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-net-hausdorff-uniqueness-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-net-hausdorff-uniqueness-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyNetHausdorffUniquenessUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $U=(D,L_0,L_1,S,M,H,C,P,N)$, the shared $\CauchyNetUp$ directed-window route $D$, Hausdorff separation through $S$, endpoint uniqueness through the displayed zero-distance comparison, $\MetricCompletionUp$ handoff through $M$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.

The obligations do not include quotienting nets, choosing a canonical limit, comparing host equality, importing countable choice, or asserting ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:cauchy-net-hausdorff-uniqueness-carrier}. The rows $D,L_0,L_1,S,M,H,C,P,N$ are the complete displayed packet.

The shared Cauchy-net route is the first obligation. Both limit reads are attached to the same directed-window row $D$, so a consumer cannot compare endpoints before reading the same Cauchy evidence.

The Hausdorff step is the row $S$. It is read only after $L_0$ and $L_1$ have both been displayed over $D$, and it identifies the endpoints through separated zero-distance evidence rather than through host equality.

The rows $M,H,C,P,N$ preserve and expose that same route. The metric-completion handoff, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows add no coordinate for quotient nets, selected representatives, choice, or ambient completeness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyNetHausdorff limit separation]
\label{thm:cauchy-net-hausdorff-limit-separation}
Let
$$
U=(D,L_0,L_1,S,M,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\CauchyNetHausdorffUniquenessUp$ packet. If $L_0$ and $L_1$ are two accepted limit reads over the same $\CauchyNetUp$ directed-window route $D$, then the packet identifies them only through the displayed separated zero-distance row $S$, transported by $H$ and replayed by $C$ into the same $\MetricCompletionUp$ handoff $M$.

Thus two accepted limit reads of the same directed-window Cauchy route are equal for the exported classifier exactly at the $\SeparatedMetricUp$ row. The conclusion is not quotient-net equality, not host point equality, and not a selected-limit principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the accepted packet through the carrier definition. The two limit rows $L_0$ and $L_1$ share the same source coordinate $D$, so every comparison begins with the displayed $\CauchyNetUp$ directed-window evidence.

The only comparison row is $S$. It records separated zero-distance evidence for the two displayed limit reads and is therefore the unique route by which the exported classifier identifies them.

Transport and replay do not change the comparison target. The rows $H$ and $C$ move and replay $D,L_0,L_1,S$ componentwise, while $M,P,N$ record the completion handoff, provenance, and local name.

Since no remaining coordinate contains quotient-net equality, host equality, a selected representative, choice, or ambient completeness, the uniqueness conclusion stays at the displayed separated row.
\end{proof}
