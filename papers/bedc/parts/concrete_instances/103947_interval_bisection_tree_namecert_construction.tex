\begin{definition}[IntervalBisectionTree carrier]
\label{def:interval-bisection-tree-carrier}
An $\IntervalBisectionTreeUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
T=(I,D,B,Q,W,S,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $I$ is the source $\LocatedIntervalUp$ row carrying the visible endpoint cells, located endpoint windows, rational containment rows, and Real-facing seal coordinates.
\item $D$ is the finite $\DyadicBisectionUp$ decision ledger, recording exactly the inspected left-or-right child choices and their dyadic scales.
\item $B$ is the $\DyadicIntervalTreeUp$ finite-tree row whose nodes are the interval cells visible at the requested depth.
\item $Q$ records the containment and refinement rows that attach every child cell in $B$ to its parent interval and to the retained branch prefix in $D$.
\item $W$ is the retained $\NestedIntervalUp$ window route exported from the finite tree after the inspected refinements have been applied.
\item $S$ records the $\StreamNameUp$ windows by which a downstream consumer requests finite endpoint or retained-window observations.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback and $\RealUp$ seal route reached only after $I,D,B,Q,W,S$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the interval source, branch ledger, finite tree, containment rows, retained windows, stream windows, readback and seal rows, replay rows, provenance row, and local naming row.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a bisection-tree request through the same rows without adding an external branch selector.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\IntervalBisectionTreeUp$, $\DyadicBisectionUp$, $\LocatedIntervalUp$, $\DyadicIntervalTreeUp$, $\NestedIntervalUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\IntervalBisectionTreeUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate asserts only that these rows form one finite interval bisection-tree packet. It has no coordinate for open-cover compactness, selected infinite branches, countable choice, quotient real equality, trichotomy, or ambient Real completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[IntervalBisectionTree NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:interval-bisection-tree-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\IntervalBisectionTreeUp$ carrier $T=(I,D,B,Q,W,S,R,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\IntervalBisectionTreeUp}$ surface is exhausted by carrier admission, source located-interval exposure, dyadic branch-ledger exposure, finite tree-node exposure, parent-child containment rows, retained nested-window routing, stream-window requests, regular rational readback, Real-facing seal routing, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming. A consumer may inspect exactly those rows and no host open cover, selected branch, countable-choice witness, quotient-real representative, trichotomy row, or ambient completeness object.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:interval-bisection-tree-carrier}. The analytic coordinates are the source interval row $I$, the branch ledger $D$, the finite tree row $B$, the containment rows $Q$, the retained nested route $W$, the stream windows $S$, and the readback and seal route $R$.

The local certificate obligations are then structural over those rows. Transport through $H$ is componentwise, replay through $C$ rereads only displayed entries, provenance through $P$ names the listed sibling packets, and $N$ names the resulting local certificate.

Since no other coordinate is present, the excluded compactness, branch-selection, choice, quotient, trichotomy, and completeness principles cannot be projected from an accepted packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[IntervalBisectionTree Real handoff]
\label{thm:interval-bisection-tree-real-handoff}
Every $\RegSeqRatUp$ or $\RealUp$ consumer of an accepted $\IntervalBisectionTreeUp$ packet factors through the finite route
$$
I,D,B,Q \longmapsto W \longmapsto S \longmapsto R \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
The consumer first reads the located interval and finite bisection-tree data, then the retained nested window, then the stream and regular-rational readback rows, and only then the Real-facing seal. The handoff exports no selected branch, quotient real, countable-choice row, trichotomy premise, open-cover compactness witness, or ambient Real-completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted packet $T=(I,D,B,Q,W,S,R,H,C,P,N)$. The source and refinement stage is $I,D,B,Q$: it exposes the located interval row, finite branch decisions, finite tree nodes, and parent-child containment rows.

The retained-window stage is $W$. It is the only nested-interval route supplied by the packet, so a downstream read cannot skip the finite tree and ask for an independently selected branch.

The endpoint stage is $S,R$. Stream requests are finite, regular rational readback is displayed, and the Real-facing seal is downstream of those rows. Transport, replay, provenance, and local naming then preserve the same route and add no host-side principle.
\end{proof}
