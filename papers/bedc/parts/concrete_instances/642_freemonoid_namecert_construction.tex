\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\FreeMonoidUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-freemonoid-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\FreeMonoidUp$ interface names finite BEDC word histories over a displayed
payload row. In the standard algebraic account, as in Mac Lane's treatment of
free algebraic constructions and the usual formal-language presentation, a free
monoid is generated by finite words with empty word and concatenation. The BEDC
certificate below records that surface without importing a host free-monoid
primitive.

\begin{definition}[FreeMonoid word carrier]
\label{def:freemonoid-word-carrier}
Fix a payload history source row $A:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ and a
payload classifier $\sim_A$. A $\FreeMonoidUp$ word is a finite
$\mathsf{BHist}$ spine
$$
W=(s,\ell,p,h,c,q)
$$
whose positions are licensed by $\ListUp$, whose length row $\ell$ is licensed
by $\NatUp$, whose payload entries $p$ are carried by $A$, whose endpoint
history $h$ is represented by the displayed list spine, whose route row $c$
records the $\Cont$ reads for empty word, singleton insertion, and
concatenation, and whose provenance row $q$ records the $\Pkg$ dependencies on
$A$, $\ListUp$, $\NatUp$, and $\MonoidUp$.

Two accepted words classify together exactly when their length rows agree and
their corresponding payload positions compare by $\hsame$ over the payload
rows, transported along the displayed $\Cont$ routes. The classifier does not
identify words by an external quotient or by a host list type.
\end{definition}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FreeMonoidUp.FreeMonoidTasteGate\_single\_carrier\_alignment}

\begin{theorem}[FreeMonoid empty word unit]
\label{thm:freemonoid-empty-word-unit}
The empty $\mathsf{BHist}$ spine is an accepted $\FreeMonoidUp$ word, its
length row is the $\NatUp$ zero row, and concatenating it on either side of an
accepted word returns a word classified with the original endpoint. The unit
rows are read only through the empty-word constructor, the $\ListUp$ empty-spine
license, the $\NatUp$ zero-length row, the $\MonoidUp$ append unit surface, and
the same $\Pkg$ provenance.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FreeMonoidUp.FreeMonoidWordCarrier\_empty\_word\_unit}
\begin{proof}
Use the empty constructor of the finite list spine for the position row and the
zero row of $\NatUp$ for the length row. This gives a carried word with no
payload entry and no hidden endpoint.

For the two unit laws, read concatenation through the $\MonoidUp$ append
surface. The left and right empty-history continuation laws identify the
resulting endpoint with the original word endpoint under $\hsame$, while the
length ledger reads zero plus the displayed length and the displayed length
plus zero as the same $\NatUp$ row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FreeMonoid concatenation associativity]
\label{thm:freemonoid-concat-associativity}
For accepted $\FreeMonoidUp$ words $u$, $v$, and $w$, both concatenation
parenthesizations
$$
(u\cdot v)\cdot w
\quad\hbox{and}\quad
u\cdot (v\cdot w)
$$
are accepted and classify together. The witness is the finite
$\mathsf{BHist}$ append-associativity route together with the $\ListUp$
append-spine associativity row, the $\NatUp$ length-addition associativity row,
and componentwise $\hsame$ transport of the payload positions.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FreeMonoidUp.FreeMonoidWordCarrier\_concat\_associativity}
\begin{proof}
First concatenate the displayed list spines. The $\ListUp$ append readback
keeps the positions finite and records the same ordered payload rows in both
parenthesizations.

Next apply the $\MonoidUp$ append associativity surface to the endpoint
histories. The resulting $\hsame$ route transports the endpoint row without
changing any payload entry.

Finally compare the length rows. Both paths read the same finite addition
ledger for the three displayed lengths, so the $\NatUp$ row agrees with the
positionwise payload transport. These rows repack as the classifier witness for
the two accepted concatenation endpoints.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FreeMonoid NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:freemonoid-namecert-obligation-surface}
The $\NameCert_{\FreeMonoidUp}$ obligation surface consists of exactly five
finite rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier habitation by the empty word of
\autoref{thm:freemonoid-empty-word-unit};
\item classifier reflexivity, symmetry, and transitivity from the $\ListUp$
position classifier, the $\NatUp$ length row, and the payload $\hsame$ rows;
\item carrier transport under the classifier by componentwise payload transport
and length agreement;
\item concatenation closure by the $\MonoidUp$ append surface and the
$\ListUp$ represented-spine append route;
\item ledger exactness for empty-word, singleton, and concatenation reads.
\end{enumerate}
No host free-monoid carrier, external quotient, infinite word, or language
recognizer is part of the obligation.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FreeMonoidUp.FreeMonoidWordCarrier\_namecert\_obligation\_surface}
\begin{proof}
The habitation row is the empty finite spine with the zero length row. This is
the carried endpoint fixed by \autoref{thm:freemonoid-empty-word-unit}.

The classifier laws are inherited componentwise. $\ListUp$ supplies the finite
position comparison, $\NatUp$ supplies length agreement, and the payload
classifier supplies the $\hsame$ rows on each displayed position.

For transport and closure, move each payload position along the classifier
route and then use the displayed append readback. Concatenation remains a finite
spine, and the endpoint route is the $\MonoidUp$ append continuation.

The ledger has only three read forms: empty word, singleton insertion, and
concatenation. Each read is stored with its source provenance and length row, so
every accepted consumer read projects back to one of those displayed rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FreeMonoid ledger normal form]
\label{thm:freemonoid-ledger-normal-form}
Every accepted $\FreeMonoidUp$ consumer read has a unique displayed ledger
shape: empty word, singleton insertion of one payload row, or concatenation of
two previously accepted word rows. The normal form records the source
provenance, the $\ListUp$ position spine, the $\NatUp$ length row, the
$\MonoidUp$ append route when present, and the componentwise payload
$\hsame$ rows. It exports no separate evaluator for words.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FreeMonoidUp.FreeMonoidWordCarrier\_ledger\_normal\_form}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier row of
\autoref{def:freemonoid-word-carrier}. The only constructors recorded in the
route row are empty word, singleton insertion, and concatenation.

In the empty case, the position spine is empty and the length row is the
$\NatUp$ zero row. In the singleton case, the spine has one displayed payload
entry and one source provenance read. In the concatenation case, the two input
word rows and the append route determine the displayed output spine and the
length-addition row.

These cases exhaust the route row, so a consumer cannot read a hidden word
evaluation, host list operation, or language-level recognizer from the packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FreeMonoid public seal]
\label{thm:freemonoid-public-seal}
The public $\FreeMonoidUp$ seal is the finite packet consisting of the word
carrier of \autoref{def:freemonoid-word-carrier}, the empty-unit and
associativity rows of \autoref{thm:freemonoid-empty-word-unit} and
\autoref{thm:freemonoid-concat-associativity}, the
$\NameCert_{\FreeMonoidUp}$ obligation surface of
\autoref{thm:freemonoid-namecert-obligation-surface}, and the ledger normal
form of \autoref{thm:freemonoid-ledger-normal-form}. This packet is the
consumer-facing certificate for finite word histories over the displayed
payload source.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FreeMonoidUp.FreeMonoidWordCarrier\_public\_seal}
\begin{proof}
Package the carrier, classifier, unit, concatenation, and ledger rows named in
the preceding blocks. Each row is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$,
or $\Pkg$ dependency over the payload source, $\ListUp$, $\NatUp$, and
$\MonoidUp$.

The public seal exposes exactly those rows. Since the carrier has no coordinate
for a host free-monoid object, external quotient, infinite stream, automaton, or
language semantics, downstream chapters can consume only the displayed finite
word-history certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FreeMonoid empty concatenation inversion]
\label{thm:freemonoid-empty-concat-inversion}
Fix one payload source row $A:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ and one
payload classifier $\sim_A$. Let $u$ and $v$ be accepted $\FreeMonoidUp$ words
for this same payload, $\ListUp$, $\NatUp$, and $\MonoidUp$ setup, and let
$u\cdot v$ be their displayed concatenation word. If $u\cdot v$ is classified
with the empty $\FreeMonoidUp$ word, then $u$ is classified with the empty word
and $v$ is classified with the empty word, all under the same payload and
provenance rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the carrier of \autoref{def:freemonoid-word-carrier}. The accepted word
$u$ has a finite position spine $s_u$, a $\NatUp$ length row $\ell_u$, payload
entries over $A$, and provenance through the fixed $A$, $\ListUp$, $\NatUp$, and
$\MonoidUp$ packet. The word $v$ has the analogous rows $s_v$ and $\ell_v$. The
concatenation ledger records the output position spine as the displayed
$\ListUp$ append of $s_u$ and $s_v$, and its length row as the displayed
$\NatUp$ addition row $\ell_u+\ell_v$.

The hypothesis says that this concatenation output is classified with the empty
word. By the classifier clause in \autoref{def:freemonoid-word-carrier}, its
length row is therefore classified with the $\NatUp$ zero row, and its position
spine is classified with the empty $\ListUp$ spine. The ledger normal form of
\autoref{thm:freemonoid-ledger-normal-form} leaves no hidden word evaluator: in
the concatenation case, these two rows are exactly the append spine and the
length-addition row just displayed.

Apply empty-result inversion for the finite append surface recorded in
\autoref{thm:external-model-soundness-finite-kernel}, equivalently the
contrapositive form of \autoref{thm:append-nonempty-iff}, to the displayed
append spine $s_u\cdot s_v$ classified with the empty spine. Both factors are
classified with the empty spine. The same inversion applied to the $\NatUp$
length-addition row identifies both $\ell_u$ and $\ell_v$ with the zero length
row.

There are now no remaining payload positions in either factor: an empty
$\ListUp$ spine has no displayed position at which a payload comparison can be
stored, and the zero $\NatUp$ row agrees with that empty presentation. The
$\Pkg$ provenance has not changed, since the concatenation row in
\autoref{def:freemonoid-word-carrier} uses the same payload source, $\ListUp$,
$\NatUp$, and $\MonoidUp$ packet for the two inputs and the result. Thus the
classifier clauses for $u$ against the empty word and for $v$ against the empty
word are exactly the empty spine comparison, the zero length comparison, the
vacuous componentwise payload comparison, and the same continuation provenance.
Therefore both factors are classified with the empty $\FreeMonoidUp$ word under
the original setup.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FreeMonoidUp.FreeMonoidWordCarrier\_empty\_concat\_inversion}

\begin{theorem}[FreeMonoid singleton concatenation inversion]
\label{thm:freemonoid-singleton-concat-inversion}
Fix one payload source row $A:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ and one
payload classifier $\sim_A$. Let $u$ and $v$ be accepted $\FreeMonoidUp$ words
for this same payload, $\ListUp$, $\NatUp$, and $\MonoidUp$ setup, and let
$u\cdot v$ be their displayed concatenation word. If $u\cdot v$ is classified
with the singleton $\FreeMonoidUp$ word carrying payload row $a$, then exactly
one factor is classified with the empty word and the other factor is classified
with the singleton word carrying $a$, all under the same $\ListUp$, $\NatUp$,
$\MonoidUp$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\Pkg$ provenance rows.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FreeMonoidUp.FreeMonoidWordCarrier\_singleton\_concat\_inversion}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the carrier of \autoref{def:freemonoid-word-carrier}. The two accepted
factors have finite position spines $s_u$ and $s_v$, length rows $\ell_u$ and
$\ell_v$, componentwise payload rows over $A$, and the same displayed
provenance packet. Their concatenation ledger records the output spine as the
$\ListUp$ append of $s_u$ and $s_v$ and the output length as the $\NatUp$
addition row $\ell_u+\ell_v$.

The singleton-output hypothesis identifies that same ledger with the displayed
one-position spine and the $\NatUp$ successor-of-zero length row. By the ledger
normal form of \autoref{thm:freemonoid-ledger-normal-form}, the concatenation
case has no further evaluator: the only possible source of that singleton row
is the append spine and its paired length row.

Apply finite append singleton inversion to the displayed $\ListUp$ spine
$s_u\cdot s_v$. There are two cases. In the left-empty case, $s_u$ is the empty
spine and $s_v$ is the singleton spine carrying the unique displayed payload
position. In the right-empty case, $s_u$ is that singleton spine and $s_v$ is
the empty spine. The corresponding $\NatUp$ length row is inverted in the same
case split: the empty factor has zero length, and the singleton factor has the
one-position length row.

It remains to transport the payload row. In each case the componentwise
classifier in \autoref{def:freemonoid-word-carrier} has exactly one displayed
position on the singleton side and no displayed position on the empty side.
Thus the unique payload comparison for $a$ belongs to the singleton factor,
while the empty factor has the vacuous payload comparison.

The $\Pkg$ and $\Cont$ rows are unchanged, because the concatenation clause of
\autoref{def:freemonoid-word-carrier} uses the same payload source, $\ListUp$,
$\NatUp$, and $\MonoidUp$ packet for both inputs and the result. Repacking the
classifier clauses gives the two stated alternatives, and the finite spine
inversion makes them disjoint: the unique singleton position cannot lie in both
factors.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FreeMonoid ListUp Cont concrete instance]
\label{thm:freemonoid-list-cont-concrete-instance}
The $\FreeMonoidUp$ word carrier of \autoref{def:freemonoid-word-carrier}, with the empty word as unit and certified concatenation read through the $\ListUp$ spine and displayed $\Cont$ routes, forms a concrete $\mathsf{BHist}$-grounded $\MonoidUp$ instance. The left and right unit laws are the empty-word unit surface of \autoref{thm:freemonoid-empty-word-unit}; associativity is the certified concatenation associativity of \autoref{thm:freemonoid-concat-associativity}; and carrier and classifier stability are supplied by the existing $\NameCert_{\FreeMonoidUp}$ obligation surface over $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, $\ListUp$, $\NatUp$, and $\MonoidUp$ rows.

This theorem is the sibling lattice link to \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-monoid-namecert}: the finite word carrier supplies a concrete $\MonoidUp$ instance by using the $\ListUp$ spine, the empty word, certified concatenation, and displayed $\Cont$ routes rather than an abstract monoid carrier detached from BEDC histories.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FreeMonoidUp.FreeMonoidWordCarrier\_list\_cont\_concrete\_instance}
\begin{proof}
Read a word carrier as its finite $\ListUp$ spine, length row, componentwise payload rows, continuation routes, and package provenance. The empty word is the displayed empty spine with zero length, so \autoref{thm:freemonoid-empty-word-unit} gives both unit laws through the same $\mathsf{BHist}$ carrier rows.

Concatenation is the certified append ledger on the $\ListUp$ spine together with the corresponding length and payload ledgers. Applying \autoref{thm:freemonoid-concat-associativity} identifies the two triple-concatenation readbacks by the displayed $\hsame$ comparisons and the same $\Cont$ routes.

Finally use the $\NameCert_{\FreeMonoidUp}$ obligation surface. It is the carrier and classifier stability statement for these concrete rows, so the resulting $\MonoidUp$ instance is grounded in the listed $\mathsf{BHist}$ data rather than in abstract carrier or classifier equivalence parameters.
\end{proof}
