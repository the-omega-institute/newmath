\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\MorseTheoryUp$}

Following Milnor on Morse theory, the $\MorseTheoryUp$ packet records the finite certificate surface by which critical-point data, Hessian signatures, local chart rows, gradient-flow incidence, and filtration boundary rows are read as one displayed Morse-theory name. It sits after the charted carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-manifold-namecert}, the derivative observation rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-derivative-namecert}, the scalar comparison seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the tangent-fibre bookkeeping of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-vectorbundle-namecert}, and the fibrewise metric rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-riemannianmetric-namecert}. The packet names a local finite Morse certificate; it is not a host smooth-function space, not a Sard or transversality theorem, not a handle-decomposition completeness theorem, and not a cobordism classification principle.

\begin{definition}[Morse theory carrier]
\label{def:morse-theory-carrier}
A $\MorseTheoryUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
M=(X,F,D,R,T,G,L,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the $\ManifoldUp$ charted source row naming the local carrier on which the Morse data are read.
\item $F$ is the displayed function row, exposing only the finite function-name observations used by the certificate.
\item $D$ is the $\DerivativeUp$ first- and second-derivative observation row attached to $F$ over the charted source.
\item $R$ is the $\RealUp$ scalar comparison row used for critical values, local height comparisons, and finite ordering tests.
\item $T$ is the $\VectorBundleUp$ tangent-fibre row over $X$, carrying the finite source for Hessian and flow-incidence reads.
\item $G$ is the $\RiemannianMetricUp$ metric row used to read gradient-flow incidence only after $X,T,D$ have been displayed.
\item $L$ is the local Morse ledger row: critical-point admission, nondegenerate Hessian signature, index tag, finite flow-incidence boundary, and filtration boundary are recorded here as finite observations.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the chart, function, derivative, scalar, tangent-fibre, metric, ledger, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay through chart admission, derivative readback, real comparison, tangent-fibre transport, metric readback, and Morse ledger inspection.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\MorseTheoryUp$, $\ManifoldUp$, $\DerivativeUp$, $\RealUp$, $\VectorBundleUp$, $\RiemannianMetricUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MorseTheoryUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exports Morse-theory reads only through $X,F,D,R,T,G,L,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for a host function space, global transversality, Sard regular-value selection, complete handle decomposition, cobordism classification, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Morse theory NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:morse-theory-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\MorseTheoryUp$ carrier
$$
M=(X,F,D,R,T,G,L,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\MorseTheoryUp}$ surface consists exactly of charted manifold admission, displayed function rows, derivative readback, real scalar comparison, tangent-fibre bookkeeping, Riemannian metric readback, local Morse ledger inspection, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, package provenance, and local naming.

Every critical-point, index, or flow-incidence read exported by the packet must factor through
$$
\ManifoldUp,\ \DerivativeUp,\ \RealUp,\ \VectorBundleUp,\ \RiemannianMetricUp,\ \BHist,\ \hsame,\ \Cont,\ \Pkg,\ \NameCert .
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The charted source is row $X$, the displayed function observations are row $F$, derivative readback is row $D$, real comparison is row $R$, tangent-fibre bookkeeping is row $T$, and metric readback is row $G$.

The Morse-specific content is confined to row $L$. It records critical-point admission, nondegenerate Hessian signature, index tag, finite flow-incidence boundary, and filtration boundary only after the chart, derivative, scalar, tangent, and metric rows are available.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name exactly that displayed route. Since no row stores Sard regular values, transversality, complete handle decompositions, cobordism classification, Lean verification, or bridge checking, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}
