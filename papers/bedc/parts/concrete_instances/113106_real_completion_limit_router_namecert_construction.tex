\begin{theorem}[Real completion limit router NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:real-completion-limit-router-namecert-obligations}

The router cannot discharge an unbounded diagonal limit, quotient equality of streams, selected representative, countable-choice selector, host equality, or ambient Real completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the displayed carrier. The observation budget $O$ is the only entry point for a completion request, and the window row $W$ is the only StreamName service exposed by that budget.

Read the analytic route in order. The row $Q$ packages the accepted windows as regular rational readback, the row $D$ records the dyadic tolerance checks attached to that readback, and the row $E$ seals the result as a RealUp name.

Finally use the structural rows. Transport $H$, continuation replay $C$, provenance $P$, and local naming $N$ preserve the same finite-window route. Since no carrier coordinate names an unbounded diagonal, quotient equality, selected representative, countable-choice selector, host equality, or Real-completeness principle, the certificate cannot export those claims.
\end{proof}

\label{thm:real-completion-limit-router-nonescape}

The consumer may read the seal as a finite routed name, but it may not replace the route by an ambient Real completeness theorem, a selected diagonal limit, quotient stream equality, countable choice, host equality, or a hidden representative.

\begin{proof}
Start from the carrier definition. The seal row $E$ is downstream of $O,W,Q,D$ in the displayed packet, so a consumer cannot reach $E$ without first exposing those rows.

Apply the NameCert obligation theorem. It identifies the local surface as the finite router rows and structural replay rows, leaving no hidden coordinate that could supply a global completion principle.

The nonescape conclusion is therefore a row-exhaustion claim. Once $O,W,Q,D,E,H,C,P,N$ have been read, the packet has exported the finite router and nothing beyond it; selected limits, quotient streams, countable choice, host equality, and ambient Real completeness remain outside the carrier.
