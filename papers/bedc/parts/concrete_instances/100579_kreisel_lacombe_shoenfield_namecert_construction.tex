\begin{theorem}[Kreisel-Lacombe-Shoenfield NameCert obligations]\label{thm:kreisel-lacombe-shoenfield-namecert-obligations}

\item prefix-observation exactness, requiring every query in $O$ to be read inside the displayed prefix context $B$;

The excluded principles are absent from the carrier. Since no row names countable choice, a completed host function, quotient stream equality, a selected limit, trichotomy, or ambient Real completeness, none of them can become a local $\NameCert_{\KreiselLacombeShoenfieldUp}$ obligation.

\begin{theorem}[Kreisel-Lacombe-Shoenfield prefix continuity]\label{thm:kreisel-lacombe-shoenfield-prefix-continuity}
Every output-window read from an accepted $\KreiselLacombeShoenfieldUp$ carrier factors through a finite prefix route
$$
B,O \longmapsto M \longmapsto S,R \longmapsto L \longmapsto E,H,C,P,N .
$$
Thus two accepted carriers that agree on the displayed prefix context and choice-sequence observations up to the modulus row $M$ have the same scheduled output-window readback through $S$ and $R$ for the ledger entry in $L$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an output-window read. The schedule row $S$ is not available as a detached total output function; it is paired with the displayed prefix context $B$ and observation row $O$.

Open the modulus row $M$. It assigns the finite input-prefix length required by the requested output window, so matching prefixes and matching observations are the only data the ledger may compare.

Replay the ledger row $L$. Its accepted entries bind the prefix agreement, observation agreement, scheduled output window, and regular rational readback into one finite route.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve this route and add no hidden input or output object. Therefore agreement on the displayed finite prefix data up to $M$ determines the scheduled readback for the ledger entry under consideration.
\end{proof}
