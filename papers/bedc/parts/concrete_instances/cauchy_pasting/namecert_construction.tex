\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CauchyPastingUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-pasting-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges, compatible local Cauchy data may be pasted only through explicit moduli, overlap witnesses, and regular readback rows before a real-name seal is exposed. The $\CauchyPastingUp$ packet records that compatible-local-Cauchy route as finite NameCert data for the L10 Real-and-Completion spine. It consumes the $\RegularCauchyGluingUp$ route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-gluing-namecert}, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal $\RealUp$ seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. It names a finite pasting carrier, not quotient stream equality, selected representatives, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[Cauchy pasting carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-pasting-carrier}
A $\CauchyPastingUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
P=(G,D,S,R,E,H,C,K,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $G$ is the accepted $\RegularCauchyGluingUp$ source row for the compatible local Cauchy windows.
\item $D$ is the shared $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row used to compare the local windows on their displayed overlap.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window row recording exactly the finite observations read by the pasting consumer.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row obtained after $G,D,S$ have been displayed.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row exposed only after the regular readback has been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for source, tolerance, window, readback, seal, replay, provenance, and naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the same pasting route.
\item $K$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyPastingUp$, $\RegularCauchyGluingUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyPastingUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $G,D,S,R,E,H,C,K,N$. It has no coordinate for quotient stream equality, selected representatives, host real equality, countable choice, or a general completeness theorem for $\RealUp$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy pasting NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-pasting-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CauchyPastingUp$ carrier
$$
P=(G,D,S,R,E,H,C,K,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyPastingUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the compatible $\RegularCauchyGluingUp$ source row, shared $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance display, finite $\StreamNameUp$ window display, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, terminal $\RealUp$ seal, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming. No pasting obligation is stored outside those finite rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-pasting-carrier}. The visible route is exhausted by $G,D,S,R,E,H,C,K,N$, so carrier admission and all pasting obligations must be read from those rows.

The source row $G$ supplies the compatible local Cauchy gluing surface. The tolerance row $D$ and window row $S$ record the finite overlap data read by the consumer, while $R$ is the only regular rational readback row downstream of that finite data.

The terminal seal $E$ is exposed only after $G,D,S,R$ have been displayed. The rows $H,C,K,N$ then transport, replay, source, and name the same route.

Since the carrier has no further coordinate, quotient stream equality, selected representatives, countable choice, host equality, and ambient completeness cannot be obligations of the local certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy pasting Real-seal nonescape]
\label{thm:cauchy-pasting-real-seal-nonescape}
Every $\RealUp$-facing consumer of an accepted $\CauchyPastingUp$ packet reaches the seal row $E$ only through the route
$$
G,D,S \longmapsto R \longmapsto E \longmapsto H,C,K,N .
$$
In particular, the consumer inherits the pasting nonescape boundary of \autoref{thm:regular-cauchy-gluing-pasting-nonescape}: it cannot export quotient stream equality, selected representatives, host real equality, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness as a coordinate of the accepted pasting read.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a $\RealUp$-facing read of the accepted packet. By \autoref{def:cauchy-pasting-carrier}, the only seal coordinate is $E$, and it is downstream of the source, tolerance, window, and regular readback rows.

Apply \autoref{thm:cauchy-pasting-namecert-obligations}. The local certificate must expose $G,D,S,R$ before the seal and must preserve the same transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows $H,C,K,N$.

Finally use \autoref{thm:regular-cauchy-gluing-pasting-nonescape} on the source row $G$. The gluing source cannot contribute quotient stream equality, selected representatives, host equality, choice, or ambient completeness, and the pasting carrier adds no coordinate of those kinds.
\end{proof}
