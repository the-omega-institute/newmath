\label{def:mollifier-carrier}

\begin{enumerate}

\item $R$ is the support-radius row, recording the dyadic radius, local support inclusion, and finite locality budget.
\item $N$ is the normalization row, recording the unit-mass ledger as displayed $\RealUp$ and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ comparisons rather than as a host integral.

\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $S,R,N,P,C,L$ and the sibling rows named by the request.

\begin{theorem}[Mollifier NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:mollifier-namecert-obligations}

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

The support-locality and normalization obligations are separate displayed rows. The radius row $R$ confines every kernel read to the named local window, while $N$ exposes only the finite real and dyadic ledger needed for unit normalization.

The convolution route is the row $P$. It records the source window, target window, translation request, and scale request before any downstream consumer may treat the packet as a regularizing kernel.

Finally $C,H,L$ replay, transport, and name the same rows. \end{proof}
