Following Bishop--Bridges constructive completion and Mac Lane pushout terminology, the $\CauchyCompletionPushoutUp$ packet records the finite gluing surface for two completion legs over a shared dense source. It sits between the unit-and-bind schedule of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchycompletionmonad-namecert}, the extension uniqueness route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completionuniversalproperty-namecert}, the metric completion carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metriccompletion-namecert}, the finite stream interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet names completion gluing without quotienting Cauchy names, selecting representatives, invoking countable choice, or importing ambient completeness of $\RealUp$.

\begin{definition}[Cauchy completion pushout carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-completion-pushout-carrier}
A $\CauchyCompletionPushoutUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
X=(S,L,R,Q,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the shared source row, naming the common metric or Cauchy source and the finite requests on which both completion legs are compared.
\item $L$ is the left completion leg row, carrying the displayed dense entry, finite Cauchy windows, and completion-facing readback for the left side.
\item $R$ is the right completion leg row, carrying the analogous displayed dense entry, finite Cauchy windows, and completion-facing readback for the right side.
\item $Q$ is the compatibility-square row, recording that the two legs agree on the shared source through displayed $\StreamNameUp$ windows and $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback before any terminal seal is read.
\item $E$ is the endpoint seal row, exposing the $\RealUp$ or completion-facing endpoint reached after $S,L,R,Q$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $S,L,R,Q,E$ and for the structural rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a shared source request through the two completion legs, the compatibility square, and the endpoint seal.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyCompletionPushoutUp$, $\CauchyCompletionMonadUp$, $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$, $\MetricCompletionUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyCompletionPushoutUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through these rows. It has no coordinate for quotient completion equality, a selected pushout representative, countable choice of approximants, host equality of completed points, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy completion pushout NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-pushout-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CauchyCompletionPushoutUp$ carrier $X=(S,L,R,Q,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyCompletionPushoutUp}$ surface consists exactly of shared-source admission, left completion-leg admission, right completion-leg admission, compatibility-square admission, endpoint-seal admission, componentwise transport, displayed replay, provenance, and local naming for the rows $S,L,R,Q,E,H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-completion-pushout-carrier}. The analytic rows are $S,L,R,Q,E$, and the structural rows are $H,C,P,N$.

The shared source row is read first. The left and right completion legs are then inspected against the same finite source request, using the finite-window and regular-readback routes already named by the StreamName and RegSeqRat siblings.

The compatibility square $Q$ is available only after both legs have been displayed over $S$. The endpoint seal $E$ is downstream of that square, so a Real or Completion consumer cannot bypass the compatibility row.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same packet. Since no quotient equality, selected representative, countable-choice schedule, host equality, or ambient completeness row appears in the carrier, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy completion pushout nonescape]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-pushout-nonescape}
Every public read from an accepted $\CauchyCompletionPushoutUp$ packet factors through
$$
S,L,R \longmapsto Q \longmapsto E \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
Thus completion functoriality and Real seal consumers receive a displayed gluing route, not quotient completion equality, selected pushout representatives, countable choice, host equality of completed points, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:cauchy-completion-pushout-namecert-obligations}. They expose the source row and both completion legs before the compatibility square is readable.

The endpoint seal is read only after the square. A consumer that reaches $E$ has therefore already consumed the shared-source row, the left and right leg rows, and the square witnessing their agreement on the source.

The structural rows preserve this same order and add no analytic coordinate. Hence every public read factors through the displayed route and cannot export any of the excluded host-side or quotient data.
\end{proof}
