\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ForbiddenAxiomAncestryUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-forbidden-axiom-ancestry-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\ForbiddenAxiomAncestryUp$ packet gives the strict axiom-purity boundary a finite local certificate. It records a theorem row, its proof-ancestry report, the forbidden host-axiom dependency row, the refusal verdict, displayed audit routes, package provenance, and the local naming row. The packet is the concrete object consumed when a theorem has no source axiom under \texttt{lean4/BEDC/} but its host proof ancestry still attempts to carry \texttt{Classical.choice}, \texttt{Quot.sound}, or \texttt{propext}.

\begin{definition}[ForbiddenAxiomAncestry carrier]
\label{def:forbidden-axiom-ancestry-carrier}
A $\ForbiddenAxiomAncestryUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(T,A,F,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $T$ is the theorem row, naming the public BEDC theorem or lemma whose proof ancestry is being inspected;
\item $A$ is the proof-ancestry report returned by the host audit query for $T$;
\item $F$ is the forbidden dependency row for \texttt{Classical.choice}, \texttt{Quot.sound}, and \texttt{propext};
\item $R$ is the refusal verdict, recording whether the ancestry report is admitted as clean or refused because it meets one of the forbidden dependency rows;
\item $H$ records displayed $\hsame$ transport for theorem names, ancestry reports, forbidden rows, verdicts, continuation routes, provenance, and the local naming row;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ audit routes by which a consumer reads $T$, replays $A$, compares it with $F$, and exposes the verdict $R$;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over theorem rows, proof-ancestry reports, strict axiom-purity gates, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ForbiddenAxiomAncestryUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for admitting a forbidden host dependency as BEDC theorem content, replacing a refused theorem by a neighboring theorem, or treating source-token purity as sufficient proof-ancestry purity.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[ForbiddenAxiomAncestry NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:forbidden-axiom-ancestry-namecert-obligations}
For every $\ForbiddenAxiomAncestryUp$ carrier
$$
Q=(T,A,F,R,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\ForbiddenAxiomAncestryUp}$ surface consists of carrier exposure for the theorem row, proof-ancestry report, forbidden dependency row, refusal verdict, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ audit replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming row; exactness of the comparison between $A$ and $F$; and non-escape for \texttt{Classical.choice}, \texttt{Quot.sound}, \texttt{propext}, fuzzy theorem substitution, and source-token-only acceptance.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ForbiddenAxiomAncestryUp.ForbiddenAxiomAncestryTasteGate\_single\_carrier\_alignment}
\begin{proof}
Carrier exposure is projection from \autoref{def:forbidden-axiom-ancestry-carrier}. The packet lists exactly the rows $T,A,F,R,H,C,P,N$.

The ancestry comparison is local to the displayed audit surface. The row $A$ supplies the reported host dependencies for the theorem row $T$, and the row $F$ supplies the three forbidden dependency names.

The verdict row $R$ records the finite comparison. Transport and replay are confined to $H$ and $C$, so a consumer can inspect or move the same comparison only through the displayed routes.

Finally $P$ and $N$ record provenance and the local naming certificate. Since the excluded objects have no coordinate outside the refusal row, the NameCert surface cannot export them as BEDC theorem content.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ForbiddenAxiomAncestry non-escape]
\label{thm:forbidden-axiom-ancestry-non-escape}
For an accepted $\ForbiddenAxiomAncestryUp$ packet, any occurrence of \texttt{Classical.choice}, \texttt{Quot.sound}, or \texttt{propext} in the ancestry report $A$ is confined to the refusal verdict $R$. Downstream consumers may read the theorem row, ancestry report, forbidden dependency row, refusal row, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming certificate, but they cannot turn a refused host dependency into a BEDC theorem dependency or replace the refused row by a nearby resolving theorem name.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet as $Q=(T,A,F,R,H,C,P,N)$. The theorem row and ancestry report are visible only as the displayed rows $T$ and $A$.

Compare $A$ with the forbidden dependency row $F$. If the report contains one of the listed host dependencies, the result of that comparison is stored in $R$ as refusal content.

The remaining rows do not change that classification. The $\hsame$ row $H$ moves the displayed packet componentwise, the $\Cont$ row $C$ replays the audit read, and $P,N$ record provenance and the local name.

No coordinate stores an admission rule for a forbidden host dependency, and no coordinate stores a fuzzy replacement for the theorem row. Thus refused ancestry cannot escape the packet as BEDC theorem content.
\end{proof}
