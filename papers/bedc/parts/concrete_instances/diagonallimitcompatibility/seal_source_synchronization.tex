\begin{theorem}[Diagonal-limit seal source synchronization]
\label{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-seal-source-synchronization}
For an accepted $\DiagonalLimitCompatibilityUp$ packet
$$
K=(R,T,S,D,W,Q,L,H,C,P,N),
$$
any seal-facing read first fixes the diagonal packet $R$, the triangle row
$T$, the dyadic ledger $D$, the window ledger $W$, the regular readback row
$Q$, and the seal row $L$ through the displayed $\hsame$ transports $H$,
$\Cont$ routes $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance $P$, and local $\NameCert$ row $N$.
The read cannot inspect $Q$ or $L$ through a second source route, alternate
window ledger, host limit, or ambient completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DiagonallimitcompatibilityUp.DiagonalLimitCompatibility\_seal\_source\_synchronization}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of
\autoref{def:diagonal-limit-compatibility-carrier}. The accepted packet
exposes exactly the source rows $R,T,S,D,W,Q,L$ together with $H,C,P,N$.

Use triangle selector alignment,
\autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-triangle-selector-alignment}, to
bind the triangle row $T$ to the same dyadic ledger $D$ and window ledger $W$
already named by the diagonal packet $R$.

Now apply seal route compatibility,
\autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-seal-route-compatibility}. The
seal route can read $Q$ and $L$ only after the displayed source, triangle,
tolerance, and window rows have been fixed.

Finally apply non-escape,
\autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-non-escape}. Since no second source
route, alternate window ledger, host limit, host equality, choice datum, or
ambient completeness row is exported, the seal-facing read is synchronized to
the displayed packet rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Diagonal-limit triangle readback lock]
\label{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-triangle-readback-lock}
For an accepted $\DiagonalLimitCompatibilityUp$ packet
$$
K=(R,T,S,D,W,Q,L,H,C,P,N),
$$
the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row $Q$ is locked behind the same triangle row
$T$, dyadic ledger $D$, and window ledger $W$ that support the seal route.
After $T,D,W$ have been displayed, $Q$ cannot be selected from a second
regular readback route or from a tolerance row outside the packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with window ledger exactness,
\autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-window-ledger-exactness}. The row
$W$ is the exact shared-window ledger read by the diagonal source, triangle
row, dyadic ledger, seal route, readback row, and real seal row.

Next use the triangle seal route,
\autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-triangle-seal-route}. It places the
seal route to $Q$ behind the displayed $T,D,W$ rows.

Apply regular readback exactness,
\autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-regular-readback-exactness}. That
theorem identifies $Q$ as the readback determined by the same $T,D,W$ route
and excludes another regular readback or dyadic witness.

The non-escape boundary then removes any remaining hidden source. The packet
has no coordinate from which a second readback route or alternate tolerance
surface could be selected.
\end{proof}

\label{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-real-seal-factorization}

$\DiagonalLimitCompatibilityUp$ packet factors through

$$
The consumer receives the displayed diagonal source, triangle row, seal
route, dyadic ledger, window ledger, regular readback, real seal row,
componentwise transports, continuations, provenance, and local naming row. It
receives no quotient stream, selected host limit, host real equality, choice
datum, or ambient completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

\autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-seal-source-synchronization}. It
fixes the source, triangle, dyadic, window, readback, seal, transport, route,
provenance, and naming rows before a seal-facing consumer can proceed.

Then apply the triangle readback lock,
\autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-triangle-readback-lock}. The
regular readback row $Q$ is tied to the same $T,D,W$ route as the seal row,
so a $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ or $\RealUp$ consumer has no independent readback
surface.

Finally use completion consumer nonescape,
\autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-completion-consumer-nonescape}.
Every completion-facing read remains inside the displayed packet rows and
exports none of the omitted quotient, host, choice, or completeness data.
\end{proof}
