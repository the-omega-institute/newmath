\begin{theorem}[DiagonalLimitCompatibility functor-uniform terminal factorization]
\label{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-functor-uniform-terminal-factorization}
Let
$$
K=(R,T,S,D,W,Q,L,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\DiagonalLimitCompatibilityUp$ packet, let
$$
F=(S_F,T_F,M_F,W_{F,0},W_{F,1},R_F,D_F,K_F,E_F,H_F,C_F,P_F,N_F)
$$
be an accepted $\CauchyLimitFunctorUp$ packet, and let
$$
U=(I_U,W_U,M_U,Q_U,T_U,E_U,H_U,C_U,P_U,N_U)
$$
be an accepted $\UniformCauchyCriterionUp$ packet. These three packets name the same terminal $\RealUp$ seal route exactly when their displayed terminal rows agree componentwise: the $\StreamNameUp$ windows $W,W_{F,0},W_{F,1},W_U$, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback rows $Q,R_F$, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledgers $D,D_F,Q_U$, the $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ rows $S,S_F,T_F$, and the terminal $\RealUp$ rows $L,E_F,E_U$ are identified through the displayed $\hsame$ rows $H,H_F,H_U$, $\Cont$ rows $C,C_F,C_U$, $\Pkg$ rows $P,P_F,P_U$, and local $\NameCert$ rows $N,N_F,N_U$.

Equivalently, the diagonal compatibility route, the functor standard-reflection route, and the finite-family uniform-Cauchy consumer route factor through one terminal BEDC seal packet over those displayed rows. No quotient stream, host real equality, hidden selector, selected host limit, countable-choice schedule, detached limit object, or ambient completeness coordinate can witness another terminal route.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DiagonallimitcompatibilityUp.DiagonalLimitCompatibilityFunctorUniformTerminalFactorization}
\begin{proof}
First read the diagonal compatibility packet through \autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-selector-observer-seal-correspondence}. Its terminal route is already fixed by the displayed $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\CauchyLimitSealUp$, and $\RealUp$ rows, together with the visible $H,C,P,N$ structure.

Now attach the functor side. By \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-functor-standard-reflection-bridge-surface}, every standard-reflection read of $F$ passes through its source and target seal rows, transport row, stream windows, regular readback, dyadic tolerance ledger, classifier row, terminal $\RealUp$ endpoint, and the displayed $H_F,C_F,P_F,N_F$ rows. Under the componentwise agreements in the statement, this is the same terminal seal route as the diagonal packet.

Next attach the uniform-Cauchy consumer side. Apply \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-real-seal-consumer-factorization}. The $\RegSeqRatUp$-, $\RealUp$-, and completion-facing reads of $U$ are exhausted by the finite package $I_U,W_U,M_U,Q_U,T_U,E_U,H_U,C_U,P_U,N_U$, so the seal row $E_U$ is downstream of the same window, tolerance, readback, and structural rows.

Conversely, suppose the three accepted packets name one terminal $\RealUp$ seal route. The diagonal correspondence theorem forces the diagonal route through $W,Q,D,S,L,H,C,P,N$; the functor bridge theorem forces the standard-reflection route through $W_{F,0},W_{F,1},R_F,D_F,S_F,T_F,E_F,H_F,C_F,P_F,N_F$; and the uniform-Cauchy factorization forces the consumer route through $W_U,Q_U,T_U,E_U,H_U,C_U,P_U,N_U$. Since each cited theorem excludes off-packet quotient, equality, selector, choice, limit, and completeness coordinates, the only possible witness of sameness is componentwise agreement through the displayed transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.
\end{proof}
