\begin{theorem}[Diagonal-limit compatibility budget-selector carrier]\label{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-budget-selector-carrier}
Let $K=(R,T,S,D,W,Q,L,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\DiagonalLimitCompatibilityUp$ packet. Any completion-facing read that uses $K$ as a diagonal compatibility source consumes the $\FiniteObservationBudgetSelectorUp$ route before the $\StreamNameUp$ window row $W$, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row $Q$, and the $\RealUp$ seal row $L$ are made available to the downstream consumer.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted compatibility packet. The carrier displays the diagonal source, triangle row, seal route, dyadic ledger, stream-window row, regular readback, Real seal, transports, continuations, provenance, and local naming row.

Insert the finite observation-budget selector at the point where the diagonal route asks for its selected observation budget. The selector route supplies the finite budget-to-schedule passage that determines which window and dyadic rows may be read.

Only after that selector route is fixed may the consumer read $W$, then the dyadic and regular-rational handoff through $D,Q$, and finally the Real seal row $L$. Thus the selector is consumed before the window, readback, and seal rows are exported.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}

No further mediator is present in the carrier. Hence the selector handoff is exhausted by $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$.
\end{proof}
