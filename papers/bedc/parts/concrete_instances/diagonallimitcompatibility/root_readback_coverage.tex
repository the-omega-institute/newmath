\begin{theorem}[Diagonal-limit compatibility root readback exactness]
\label{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-root-readback-exactness}
For an accepted $\DiagonalLimitCompatibilityUp$ packet
$$
K=(R,T,S,D,W,Q,L,H,C,P,N),
$$
every root-budget readback first consumes the displayed $\StreamNameUp$ window $W$, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback $Q$, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger $D$, the $\RealUp$ seal row $L$, and the $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ boundary carried by $S$. The route is the finite L10 sibling path through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchylimitseal-namecert}, together with the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows of $K$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DiagonallimitcompatibilityUp.DiagonalLimitCompatibilityRootReadbackExactness}
\begin{proof}
Start with the root budget source and window rows. \autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-root-budget-source} fixes the root budget source as the displayed compatibility packet, and \autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-root-window-route} places the $\StreamNameUp$ window before the regular readback and Real seal handoff.

Read the seal and ledger rows next. \autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-root-seal-boundary} forces every Real-facing read through the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback and seal route, while \autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-root-budget-ledger-row} records the dyadic ledger, window, readback, seal, transport, route, provenance, and local naming rows as the exact root-budget ledger boundary.

The concrete sibling chapters supply the same route components: StreamName gives the finite window, RegSeqRat gives the regular-rational readback, DyadicRatCore gives the dyadic ledger, RealUp gives the seal row, and CauchyLimitSeal gives the seal boundary. Since \autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-root-consumer-nonescape} exports no host limit, quotient stream, selected limit, host real equality, or ambient completeness coordinate, the root readback is exactly this displayed route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Diagonal-limit compatibility root transport replay]
\label{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-root-transport-replay}
Let $K=(R,T,S,D,W,Q,L,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\DiagonalLimitCompatibilityUp$ packet, and let $K'$ be obtained by componentwise $\hsame$ transport of the displayed diagonal source, triangle row, seal route, dyadic ledger, stream window, regular readback, Real seal, provenance, and local naming rows. If the displayed $\Cont$ routes of $K'$ replay the transported rows in the same order, then $K'$ preserves the root readback route of \autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-root-readback-exactness}.
\end{theorem}
\leanvariant{BEDC.Derived.DiagonallimitcompatibilityUp.DiagonalLimitCompatibilityCarrier\_root\_transport\_replay}
\begin{proof}
First transport the packet rows componentwise. The carrier row of \autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-root-budget-carrier-row} exposes exactly the root-facing source, window, seal, dyadic, readback, transport, continuation, provenance, and local-name surface, so $\hsame$ transport has no additional coordinate to move.

Next replay the displayed continuations. \autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-root-budget-classifier-row} keeps the classifier stable under componentwise $\hsame$ movement and displayed $\Cont$ replay, and \autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-root-budget-ledger-row} keeps the replay attached to the same source, window, seal, and consumer rows.

The replayed route therefore still reads the sibling L10 windows, regular readback, dyadic ledger, Real seal, and CauchyLimitSeal boundary before any completion-facing consumer is reached. The transported packet cannot acquire a second root route, because the root carrier and consumer rows remain exhausted by the displayed finite surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Diagonal-limit compatibility root consumer coverage]
\label{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-root-consumer-coverage}
Every $\RealUp$-facing or completion-facing consumer of the root route of an accepted $\DiagonalLimitCompatibilityUp$ packet is exhausted by the rows displayed in \autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-root-readback-exactness} and preserved by \autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-root-transport-replay}. Such a consumer can read the StreamName window, RegSeqRat readback, DyadicRatCore ledger, RealUp seal row, CauchyLimitSeal boundary, and structural rows $H,C,P,N$, but it cannot read host limits, quotient streams, selected limits, host real equality, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DiagonallimitcompatibilityUp.DiagonalLimitCompatibilityRootConsumerCoverage}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a root-route consumer. \autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-root-consumer-nonescape} limits downstream $\RealUp$, $\CompletionUp$, and completion-seal reads to the displayed budget, window, diagonal, seal, transport, route, provenance, and local-name rows.

Use the readback exactness theorem above to identify those rows with the concrete L10 sibling route. The consumer reads \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert} for windows, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert} for regular readback, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert} for the dyadic ledger, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert} for the Real seal, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchylimitseal-namecert} for the seal boundary.

Finally apply transport replay. Any componentwise $\hsame$ movement and displayed $\Cont$ replay reaches the same root route, so every allowed consumer is already listed by the displayed rows. The excluded host-side objects are absent from the packet and cannot be recovered from the root readback surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Diagonal-limit compatibility root L10 spine exhaustion]
\label{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-root-l10-spine-exhaustion}
Every accepted root route
$$
K=(R,T,S,D,W,Q,L,H,C,P,N)
$$
whose consumer is $\RealUp$-facing or completion-facing is equivalent, as a displayed BEDC route, to the finite L10 spine through the $\StreamNameUp$ window, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, $\RealUp$ seal, and $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ boundary. The only structural rows available along the spine are $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows displayed by $K$. The route exports no quotient stream, selected limit, host real equality, ambient completeness, or second completion witness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First identify the displayed root readback. By \autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-root-readback-exactness}, every root-budget readback passes through the StreamName window, RegSeqRat readback, DyadicRatCore ledger, RealUp seal row, and CauchyLimitSeal boundary, with the structural rows $H,C,P,N$ attached to the same packet.

Next use transport replay. \autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-root-transport-replay} shows that componentwise $\hsame$ movement and displayed $\Cont$ replay preserve this route, so the displayed BEDC route is unchanged by the allowed structural comparisons.

Finally apply consumer coverage. \autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-root-consumer-coverage} exhausts every $\RealUp$-facing or completion-facing consumer by the same five sibling handoffs and the same structural rows. Since the carrier contains no quotient stream, selected limit, host real equality, ambient completeness, or second completion witness, every such consumer is equivalent to the finite L10 spine and nothing beyond it.
\end{proof}
