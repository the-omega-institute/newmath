\begin{theorem}[Diagonal-limit compatibility root budget coherence]
\label{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-root-budget-coherence}
For an accepted $\DiagonalLimitCompatibilityUp$ packet
$$
K=(R,T,S,D,W,Q,L,H,C,P,N),
$$
the root-budget route is the same displayed route used by the rows
$D,W,Q,L,H,C,P,N$. It reads the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger $D$, the
$\StreamNameUp$ window row $W$, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row $Q$, the
$\RealUp$ seal row $L$, the componentwise $\hsame$ transports $H$, the
displayed $\Cont$ routes $C$, the $\Pkg$ provenance $P$, and the local
$\NameCert$ row $N$ through the carrier of
\autoref{def:diagonal-limit-compatibility-carrier}. It has no root-budget
source outside that $\BHist/\hsame/\Cont/\Pkg/\NameCert$ surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The only root-facing data in the packet are the
displayed rows $R,T,S,D,W,Q,L,H,C,P,N$, with $D,W,Q,L$ carrying the budget,
window, readback, and seal coordinates and $H,C,P,N$ carrying transport,
route, provenance, and naming.

Apply the root budget source theorem,
\autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-root-budget-source}. It identifies
the budget source with the same finite structural surface used by the
selector-budget route, rather than with a hidden host-side source.

Now use the root window route and root seal boundary theorems,
\autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-root-window-route} and
\autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-root-seal-boundary}. They keep the
route through $W,Q,L$ after the displayed ledger and window rows and before
any Real-facing handoff.

Finally apply root consumer non-escape,
\autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-root-consumer-nonescape}. Since no
additional budget coordinate is exported, the root-budget route is exactly
the route already used by $D,W,Q,L,H,C,P,N$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Diagonal-limit compatibility root selector budget exhaustion]
\label{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-root-selector-budget-exhaustion}
Every selector-budget consumer of an accepted $\DiagonalLimitCompatibilityUp$
packet factors through
$$
R,T,S,D,W,Q,L.
$$
It must first read the accepted diagonal source $R$, the triangle selector row
$T$, the seal route $S$, the dyadic ledger $D$, the finite window row $W$, the
regular readback row $Q$, and the Real seal row $L$, transported by the same
$H,C,P,N$ support rows. No selector-budget consumer can introduce a second
diagonal route, alternate window route, alternate dyadic ledger, selected
host limit, quotient stream, choice datum, or ambient completeness input.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier projection. A selector-budget consumer sees one
diagonal source row $R$, one triangle selector row $T$, one seal route $S$,
one dyadic ledger $D$, one stream-window row $W$, one regular readback $Q$,
and one Real seal row $L$.

Use triangle selector alignment together with source-route exactness,
\autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-triangle-selector-alignment} and
\autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-source-route-exactness}. These
theorems attach the selector route to the same $R,T,D,W$ rows and place the
route to $Q,L$ on that displayed source path.

Next apply the root budget carrier and classifier rows,
\autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-root-budget-carrier-row} and
\autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-root-budget-classifier-row}. The
carrier row supplies the finite packet surface, and the classifier row keeps
componentwise $\hsame$ transport and $\Cont$ replay on that same route.

The exhaustion claim follows from root budget ledger row and completion
consumer non-escape,
\autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-root-budget-ledger-row} and
\autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-completion-consumer-nonescape}.
Together they leave no selector-budget coordinate outside
$R,T,S,D,W,Q,L,H,C,P,N$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Diagonal-limit compatibility Real seal readback lock]
\label{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-root-real-seal-readback-lock}
Every $\RealUp$ or $\CompletionUp$ seal read of an accepted
$\DiagonalLimitCompatibilityUp$ packet reaches $L$ only after the
$\StreamNameUp$ window row $W$, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row $Q$, the
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger $D$, and the $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ route $S$ have
been fixed on the displayed compatibility route. The read is therefore locked
to the carrier route
$$
R,T,S,D,W,Q,L,H,C,P,N
$$
and cannot be replaced by a host real equality, selected limit, quotient
stream, choice datum, or ambient completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First read the seal source synchronization theorem,
\autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-seal-source-synchronization}. It
fixes $R,T,S,D,W,Q,L$ together with $H,C,P,N$ before a seal-facing consumer
can inspect the readback or seal rows.

Next apply the triangle readback lock and regular readback exactness,
\autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-triangle-readback-lock} and
\autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-regular-readback-exactness}. These
results place $Q$ behind the same $T,D,W$ route used by the seal row and
exclude an independent regular readback source.

Now use seal readback exactness and Real seal exactness,
\autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-seal-readback-exactness} and
\autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-real-seal-exactness}. They identify
the route to $L$ with the displayed seal, dyadic, window, readback, transport,
continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.

Finally apply the completion consumer gate,
\autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-completion-consumer-gate}. Every
$\RealUp$ or $\CompletionUp$ read remains downstream of the same finite
budget-source and seal-budget routes, so no host-side completion data can
replace the locked readback route.
\end{proof}
