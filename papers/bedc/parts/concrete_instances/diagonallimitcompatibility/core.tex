\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\DiagonalLimitCompatibilityUp$}

The $\DiagonalLimitCompatibilityUp$ surface records the finite compatibility
packet between the Real completeness diagonal-limit route and the downstream
completion-seal consumer. It ties the accepted diagonal-limit packet, the
three-leg regularity estimate, the Cauchy limit seal, stream windows, regular
rational readback, dyadic tolerance bookkeeping, and the final $\RealUp$ seal
into one local naming certificate. The packet is a compatibility surface, not
a host completion theorem and not a choice of a completed real.

\begin{definition}[Diagonal-limit compatibility carrier]
\label{def:diagonal-limit-compatibility-carrier}
A $\DiagonalLimitCompatibilityUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(R,T,S,D,W,Q,L,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $R$ is the accepted $\RealCompletenessUp$ diagonal-limit packet whose
diagonal route is being consumed;
\item $T$ is the accepted $\RegularCauchyTriangleBoundUp$ three-leg estimate
packet controlling the selected diagonal windows;
\item $S$ is the $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ seal route through which downstream
completion-facing consumers read the diagonal handoff;
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger shared by the
diagonal-limit row, the triangle estimate, and the seal route;
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window family naming the finite observations
used by the diagonal selector and by the seal consumer;
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row exposed after the window and
dyadic ledger have been fixed;
\item $L$ is the $\RealUp$ seal class reached by the compatibility route;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for
$R,T,S,D,W,Q,L$ and the structural rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes from the diagonal-limit packet
through the triangle estimate, seal route, tolerance ledger, window family,
regular rational readback, and $\RealUp$ seal;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RealCompletenessUp$,
$\RegularCauchyTriangleBoundUp$, $\CauchyLimitSealUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$,
$\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$,
and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DiagonalLimitCompatibilityUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier classifier reads only these displayed rows and their $\hsame$ and
$\Cont$ transports.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Diagonal-limit compatibility NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:diagonal-limit-compatibility-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\DiagonalLimitCompatibilityUp}$ surface has five finite
obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the packet
$K=(R,T,S,D,W,Q,L,H,C,P,N)$ of
\autoref{def:diagonal-limit-compatibility-carrier};
\item triangle-bound and selector alignment, requiring the
$\RegularCauchyTriangleBoundUp$ row $T$ to consume the same selected windows
and dyadic tolerance ledger named by $R,W,D$;
\item $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ route compatibility, requiring the seal row $S$ to
read the regular-rational handoff $Q$ and the $\RealUp$ seal class $L$ only
after the displayed diagonal route has passed through $T,D,W$;
\item classifier transport over the shared $\hsame$ rows in $H$ and the
displayed continuation routes in $C$;
\item non-escape for quotient streams, selected host limits, host real
equality, choice data, and ambient completeness.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DiagonallimitcompatibilityUp.DiagonalLimitCompatibility\_namecert\_obligations}
\begin{proof}

\autoref{def:diagonal-limit-compatibility-carrier}. The rows
$R,T,S,D,W,Q,L$ are the only mathematical coordinates, and $H,C,P,N$ are the
only transport, route, provenance, and naming coordinates.

with $R,W,D$. The same window family and dyadic ledger are displayed before

selector surface.

$L$ only through the displayed continuation rows in $C$, after the

been fixed.

Finally inspect the coordinate list. Since no quotient stream, selected host
