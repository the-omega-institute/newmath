\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\EnrichedCatUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-enrichedcat-namecert}

The enriched-category interface $\EnrichedCatUp$ packages a collection of objects with hom-objects valued in a fixed monoidal category, with composition and identity morphisms in that monoidal category. It depends on $\NameCert_{\MonoidalCatUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-monoidalcat-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\CategoryUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-category-namecert}).

\begin{theorem}[EnrichedCatUp source obligation]\label{thm:enrichedcat-source-obligation}
Every public source row of an $\EnrichedCatUp$ packet is one of the displayed
$\mathsf{BHist}$ rows for the consumed $\NameCert_{\CategoryUp}$ object
boundary, the consumed $\NameCert_{\MonoidalCatUp}$ tensor boundary, the
hom-object rows, the identity rows, the composition rows, the $\hsame$
transport rows, the $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance rows, and the finite $\Cont$
readback rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the packet as the named $\mathsf{BHist}$ tuple. The first projections are
the two consumed certificates: the object boundary is the public boundary of
$\NameCert_{\CategoryUp}$, and the tensor boundary is the public boundary of
$\NameCert_{\MonoidalCatUp}$.

The remaining projections are the enriched rows themselves. They record the
hom-object assignment, the identity witnesses, composition witnesses, component
$\hsame$ transports, package provenance, and the finite continuation readbacks.

Conversely, each displayed row is inserted into the packet by the corresponding
constructor coordinate. Projection followed by repacking is judgmentally the
same named tuple, so no further public source row can appear.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[EnrichedCatUp classifier transport obligation]\label{thm:enrichedcat-classifier-transport-obligation}
The public classifier comparison for an $\EnrichedCatUp$ packet is the
componentwise $\hsame$ transport of the object-boundary, tensor-boundary,
hom-object, identity, composition, provenance, and continuation rows, and is not
host equality of enriched categories.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the classifier comparison to each displayed row. On the consumed
$\NameCert_{\CategoryUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\MonoidalCatUp}$ boundaries, the
comparison is exactly the already exported boundary $\hsame$ comparison.

For the enriched rows, transport is pointwise: hom-objects transport along the
object boundary, identity and composition rows transport along the tensor
boundary, and provenance and continuation rows transport by their named
$\mathsf{BHist}$ coordinates.

Repack the transported coordinates into the public classifier row. The
construction never compares host-level enriched categories; it compares only the
displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ rows and the $\hsame$ witnesses attached to them.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[EnrichedCatUp ledger exactness obligation]\label{thm:enrichedcat-ledger-exactness-obligation}
Projection from an $\EnrichedCatUp$ certificate to its hom-object, identity,
composition, tensor, provenance, and continuation ledger rows, followed by
repacking those same rows in the displayed order, recovers exactly the public
certificate ledger.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the public certificate ledger and project the hom-object, identity,
composition, tensor, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and $\Cont$ coordinates. These projections
are the named coordinates of the certificate tuple.

Repacking uses those coordinates in the same order and with the same boundary
certificates. The object and tensor rows are not recomputed; they are the
consumed rows already present in the tuple.

Therefore the repacked ledger has the same projections as the original public
ledger on every displayed coordinate. Since the certificate exposes no other
ledger coordinate, the projection-and-repacking operation is exact.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[EnrichedCatUp monoidal-category boundary]\label{thm:enrichedcat-monoidal-category-boundary}
The $\NameCert_{\MonoidalCatUp}$ dependency of an $\EnrichedCatUp$ certificate is
read only through tensor, unit, associator and unit-transport, and
$\mathsf{Pkg}$ rows; no other monoidal-category host data is part of the public
obligation surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the monoidal dependency coordinates in the certificate tuple. The tensor
and unit rows provide the objects used by the hom-object, identity, and
composition entries.

The associator and unit-transport rows are the only coherence transports read by
the enriched composition and identity rows. Provenance is recorded through the
displayed $\mathsf{Pkg}$ coordinate attached to the consumed
$\NameCert_{\MonoidalCatUp}$ packet.

All public reads of the monoidal dependency factor through these rows. Hence any
two monoidal witnesses with the same displayed tensor, unit, coherence
transport, and provenance rows are indistinguishable to the $\EnrichedCatUp$
certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[EnrichedCatUp consumer readback boundary]\label{thm:enrichedcat-consumer-readback-boundary}
A downstream $\FunctorUp$ or $\NatTransUp$ consumer of an $\EnrichedCatUp$
certificate reads only the displayed object, hom-object, identity, composition,
transport, provenance, and finite $\Cont$ rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Give the consumer its public readback map. By the source obligation, the map can
project only from the displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ tuple and its finite $\Cont$
readback rows.

The object and hom-object rows supply the source and target data for enriched
functors. The identity, composition, and transport rows supply the compatibility
witnesses read by functor and natural-transformation certificates.

Package provenance is available only through the $\mathsf{Pkg}$ rows, and finite
continuations are available only through the listed $\Cont$ rows. Repacking
these rows gives the same public readback packet, so the consumer boundary is
exactly the displayed surface.
\end{proof}
