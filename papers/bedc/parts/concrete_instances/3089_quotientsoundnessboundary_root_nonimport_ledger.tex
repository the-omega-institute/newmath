\begin{theorem}[QuotientSoundnessBoundary root nonimport ledger]
\label{thm:quotient-soundness-boundary-root-nonimport-ledger}
The root kernel ledger exported by a $\QuotientSoundnessBoundaryUp$ packet is exactly the refused representative surface $E,A,V$ together with the admitted $\psame$ transport surface $E,T,H,C,P,N$. It imports no quotient carrier, representative selector, quotient equality eliminator, erased derivation recovery map, host quotient recursion principle, or $\mathsf{Quot.sound}$ dependency.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First combine root representative refusal with root $\psame$ transport admission, \autoref{thm:quotient-soundness-boundary-root-representative-refusal} and \autoref{thm:quotient-soundness-boundary-root-psame-transport-admission}. These two rows identify the negative ledger side $E,A,V$ and the surviving positive side $E,T,H,C,P,N$.

Apply the kernel consumer audit triad, \autoref{thm:quotient-soundness-boundary-kernel-consumer-audit-triad}. It packages those two sides as the kernel-facing audit surface and records that no further quotient-soundness coordinate is exported.

Finally cite public bridge non-escape and psame-only descent, \autoref{thm:quotient-soundness-boundary-public-bridge-nonescape} and \autoref{thm:quotient-soundness-boundary-psame-only-descent}. The bridge and descent rows exclude quotient carriers, representative selectors, erased derivation recovery, host quotient recursion, and $\mathsf{Quot.sound}$, so the ledger is a nonimport ledger rather than a quotient extension.
\end{proof}
