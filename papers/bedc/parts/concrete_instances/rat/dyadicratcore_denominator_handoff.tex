\begin{theorem}[RatUp DyadicRatCore denominator handoff]
\label{thm:ratup-dyadicratcore-denominator-handoff}
Every $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ consumer of a signed mantissa/exponent packet first
reads the normalized $\RatUp$ positive-denominator surface through the
displayed $\IntUp$ numerator, unary positive denominator, $\hsame$ transport,
$\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and $\mathsf{NameCert}$ rows. This route
supplies the rational mantissa and denominator evidence consumed by
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, but it adds no
quotient rational equality, selected real limit, host equality, or
$\RealUp$ completeness coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the rational carrier of \autoref{def:rat-carrier}. Its visible
surface is an $\IntUp$ numerator together with a denominator history that is
unary and nonempty, equivalently a positive unary denominator by
\autoref{thm:rat-carrier-iff-positive-unary-denominator}.

The denominator rows are stable under the Rat-side transport already recorded
in \autoref{thm:rat-carrier-denominator-hsame-transport} and
\autoref{thm:rat-positive-unary-denominator-hsame-transport}. Hence a
consumer may move the displayed denominator through $\hsame$ without changing
the positive unary evidence read from the carrier.

Next follow the ledger route. The one-tail and saturated denominator readback
theorems in \autoref{thm:rat-ledger-one-tail-denominator-shape-readback}
expose the unary tail, nonempty endpoint, and zero-extension exclusions that a
finite $\Cont$ replay can inspect. The $\Pkg$ and $\mathsf{NameCert}$ rows keep
this denominator evidence attached to the Rat provenance rather than turning
it into a host rational object.

Finally cite the DyadicRatCore carrier handoff in
\autoref{def:dyadicratcore-carrier} and
\autoref{thm:dyadic-rat-core-rat-int-sibling-route}. That consumer reads only
the mantissa, exponent, denominator ledger, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and source
certificate rows. Since neither the Rat carrier nor the DyadicRatCore packet
contains a quotient rational equality, selected real limit, host equality, or
$\RealUp$ completeness coordinate, the displayed positive-denominator route is
the full handoff surface.
\end{proof}
