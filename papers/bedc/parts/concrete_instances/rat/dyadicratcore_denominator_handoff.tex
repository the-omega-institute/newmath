\begin{theorem}[RatUp DyadicRatCore denominator handoff]
\label{thm:ratup-dyadicratcore-denominator-handoff}
Every $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ consumer of a signed mantissa/exponent packet first
reads the normalized $\RatUp$ positive-denominator surface through the
displayed $\IntUp$ numerator, unary positive denominator, $\hsame$ transport,
$\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and $\mathsf{NameCert}$ rows. This route
supplies the rational mantissa and denominator evidence consumed by
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, but it adds no
quotient rational equality, selected real limit, host equality, or
$\RealUp$ completeness coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the rational carrier of \autoref{def:rat-carrier}. Its visible
surface is an $\IntUp$ numerator together with a denominator history that is
unary and nonempty, equivalently a positive unary denominator by
\autoref{thm:rat-carrier-iff-positive-unary-denominator}.

The denominator rows are stable under the Rat-side transport already recorded
in \autoref{thm:rat-carrier-denominator-hsame-transport} and
\autoref{thm:rat-positive-unary-denominator-hsame-transport}. Hence a
consumer may move the displayed denominator through $\hsame$ without changing
the positive unary evidence read from the carrier.

Next follow the ledger route. The one-tail and saturated denominator readback
theorems in \autoref{thm:rat-ledger-one-tail-denominator-shape-readback}
expose the unary tail, nonempty endpoint, and zero-extension exclusions that a
finite $\Cont$ replay can inspect. The $\Pkg$ and $\mathsf{NameCert}$ rows keep
this denominator evidence attached to the Rat provenance rather than turning
it into a host rational object.

Finally cite the DyadicRatCore carrier handoff in
\autoref{def:dyadicratcore-carrier} and
\autoref{thm:dyadic-rat-core-rat-int-sibling-route}. That consumer reads only
the mantissa, exponent, denominator ledger, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and source
certificate rows. Since neither the Rat carrier nor the DyadicRatCore packet
contains a quotient rational equality, selected real limit, host equality, or
$\RealUp$ completeness coordinate, the displayed positive-denominator route is
the full handoff surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RatUp L10 dyadic denominator gateway]
\label{thm:ratup-l10-dyadic-denominator-gateway}
Let $\mathcal E$ be a finite Phase $\RealUp$ and Completion packet whose
dyadic face reads a $\RatUp$ positive-denominator surface. Then the
denominator row consumed by $\mathcal E$ factors through the finite route
$$
\RatUp \to \DyadicRatCoreUp \to \StreamNameUp \to \RegSeqRatUp \to \RealUp .
$$
The factorization uses only the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$,
$\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows attached to the Rat, dyadic, StreamName,
RegSeqRat, and Real faces.

Consequently the gateway exports no quotient rational equality, quotient
stream equality, selected limit, host real equality, choice row,
metric-completeness datum, bridge row, or $\RealUp$ completeness claim.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with \autoref{thm:ratup-dyadicratcore-denominator-handoff}. It reads
the positive unary denominator from the $\RatUp$ carrier through the displayed
numerator, denominator ledger, $\hsame$ transport, $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$
provenance, and $\NameCert$ rows, and hands exactly that finite surface to the
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$ consumer.

Next attach the current Phase Real and Completion exit packet. By
\autoref{thm:real-l10-current-phase-exit-local-threshold}, such a packet reads
the four local faces objectwise, with support restricted to
$$
\BHist,\quad \hsame,\quad \Cont,\quad \Pkg,\quad \NameCert .
$$
Thus the dyadic face that receives the Rat denominator handoff is the same
finite dyadic row available to the Real-and-Completion exit read.

Now push the row through the terminal route. Apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-l10-four-face-ordered-exit-gate} to force the public
consumer order through $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and
$\RealUp$, and apply
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-stream-dyadic-real-section-coherence} to keep the
StreamName window, dyadic ledger, RegSeqRat readback, and Real finite-section
readback on one common finite source section.

The displayed route therefore carries the Rat positive-denominator row only as
finite BEDC support data. The exclusion clauses in the Rat handoff, ordered
StreamName gate, RegSeqRat coherence theorem, and Real current-phase threshold
remove every non-displayed coordinate: quotient rational equality, quotient
stream equality, selected limits, host real equality, choice, metric
completeness, bridge data, and $\RealUp$ completeness are not part of the
gateway surface.
\end{proof}
