The ledger keeps the raw sequence, target, and modulus data visible, and its checked rows paste moduli for constant sequences, pointwise sums, pointwise products, and componentwise assembly rather than hiding convergence behind choice. The five name-certificate obligations follow from the constant-sequence witness for nonemptiness, the componentwise $\hsame$ equivalence laws, and the stability rows saying that regularity, limit status, and modulus comparisons transport along the same component histories.
