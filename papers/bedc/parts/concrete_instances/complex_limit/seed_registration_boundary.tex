\begin{theorem}[Complex-limit seed closure register]
\label{thm:complex-limit-seed-closure-register}
The seed surface already present in the $\ComplexLimitUp$ carrier consists of the $\ComplexUp$ coordinate rows, the $\RealUp$ coordinate seals, the unary modulus row, the complex-distance rows, and the local $\NameCert_{\ComplexLimitUp}$ source, pattern, classifier, stability, ledger, replay, provenance, and naming rows. A seed consumer may read those rows before it asks for closure under constant, difference, negation, or affine-combination operations, but it cannot replace them by host complex equality, quotient-limit data, selected representatives, or an ambient completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier through the sibling route of \autoref{thm:complex-limit-real-coordinate-sibling-route}. The source sequence first exposes $\ComplexUp$ coordinates, and those coordinates are sealed componentwise by the $\RealUp$ rows before any limit-facing data is available.

The certificate file then supplies the local rows. The source and pattern specifications identify the unary modulus and complex-distance histories, while the classifier, stability certificate, ledger policy, replay, provenance, and local name are exactly the $\NameCert_{\ComplexLimitUp}$ seed rows.

All later closure files consume this seed surface. Constant, difference, negation, and affine-combination rows may extend the ledger, but they do not create a new seed source or import host complex equality, quotient limits, representatives, or completeness.
\end{proof}
