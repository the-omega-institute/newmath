\begin{definition}[MetaCIC normalization frontier public export surface]
\label{def:metacic-normalization-frontier-public-export-surface}
The public export surface of an accepted
$\MetaCICNormalizationFrontierUp$ packet is the finite row package
$$
F_{\mathrm{pub}}=(C,K,E,N,S,H,R,P,L).
$$
Here $C$ is candidate evidence, $K$ is closed-candidate placement, $E$ is
finished-normal evidence, $N$ is the normal endpoint, $S$ is the
subject-reduction obstruction, $H$ is componentwise $\hsame$ transport, $R$
is the displayed $\Cont$ replay surface, $P$ is $\Pkg$ provenance, and $L$ is
the local $\NameCert$ row. A public read of the frontier packet is a read of
this finite surface, not a read of an ambient normalization theorem.
\end{definition}

\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.MetaCICNormalizationFrontierUp.MetaCICNormalizationFrontierPublicExportSurfaceCertificate}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC normalization frontier public export totality]
\label{thm:metacic-normalization-frontier-public-export-totality}
Every public $\MetaCICNormalizationFrontierUp$ consumer factors through one of
the two displayed public routes:
\begin{enumerate}
\item the candidate surface $C,K,R,P,L$;
\item the finished-normal surface $E,N,R,P,L$.
\end{enumerate}
In both cases the row $S$ is retained as subject-reduction obstruction data,
and $H$ transports only the displayed components of the same finite public
surface.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.MetaCICNormalizationFrontierUp.MetaCICNormalizationFrontierPublicExportTotality}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier and local certificate obligations. The carrier
\autoref{def:metacic-normalization-frontier-carrier} supplies exactly the
rows $C,K,E,N,S,H,R,P,L$, and
\autoref{thm:metacic-normalization-frontier-namecert-obligations} assigns
consumer reads to the candidate boundary, the finished-normal boundary, and
the displayed handoff rows.

The positive route is then exhausted by the existing route theorems. Use
\autoref{thm:metacic-normalization-frontier-consumer-route-totality} and
\autoref{thm:metacic-normalization-frontier-evidence-choice-exhaustion}: a
public consumer must choose $C,K$ or $E,N$, with replay, provenance, and local
naming supplied by $R,P,L$.

Finally use obstruction retention. The theorem
\autoref{thm:metacic-normalization-frontier-obstruction-retention} keeps $S$
visible under both choices, and non-escape prevents $H,R,P,L$ from becoming a
third positive normalization route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC normalization frontier public export non-escape]
\label{thm:metacic-normalization-frontier-public-export-nonescape}
The public export surface of
\autoref{def:metacic-normalization-frontier-public-export-surface} cannot be
read as blanket strong normalization for all empty-context typed closed
MetaCIC terms, full closed consistency, erased subject-reduction transport, or
a third hidden normalization evidence route beyond $C,K,R,P,L$ and
$E,N,R,P,L$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the public surface to the existing carrier. The definition of
$F_{\mathrm{pub}}$ is only the displayed finite row package
$C,K,E,N,S,H,R,P,L$, so any public read must stay inside the same carrier,
transport, replay, package, and local naming coordinates.

Apply the chapter's non-escape and evidence-choice results.
\autoref{thm:metacic-normalization-frontier-nonescape} excludes blanket
normalization, closed consistency, and erased subject-reduction transport,
while \autoref{thm:metacic-normalization-frontier-evidence-choice-exhaustion}
leaves only the candidate and finished-normal evidence choices.

It remains to rule out a hidden route. Consumer-route totality gives no
public path outside $C,K,R,P,L$ and $E,N,R,P,L$, and obstruction retention
keeps $S$ as obstruction data rather than positive normalization evidence.
Therefore the public export boundary is exactly the two-route surface with
the obstruction row retained.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.MetaCICNormalizationFrontierUp.MetaCICNormalizationFrontierPublicExport\_nonescape}

\begin{definition}[MetaCIC normalization frontier visible obstruction packet]
\label{def:metacic-normalization-frontier-visible-obstruction-packet}

$$
O_{\mathrm{vis}}=(C,N,O,S,B,H,R,P,L).
$$
Here $C$ is candidate evidence, $N$ is the normal endpoint readback, $O$ is the

$R$ is the $\Cont$ replay surface, $P$ is $\Pkg$ provenance, and $L$ is the
local $\NameCert$ row. A consumer reads $O_{\mathrm{vis}}$ as obstruction
content for the public frontier, not as a proof of ambient normalization.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC normalization frontier visible obstruction admission]
\label{thm:metacic-normalization-frontier-visible-obstruction-admission}
Every accepted $\MetaCICNormalizationFrontierUp$ public export admits the
visible obstruction packet $O_{\mathrm{vis}}$ of
\autoref{def:metacic-normalization-frontier-visible-obstruction-packet}. The
packet displays candidate evidence, normal endpoint readback, obstruction
ledger, subject-reduction obstruction reference, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$,
$\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ rows on the same finite public surface.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.MetaCICNormalizationFrontierUp.MetaCICNormalizationFrontierVisibleObstructionAdmission}
\begin{proof}
Start from the public export surface
\autoref{def:metacic-normalization-frontier-public-export-surface}. It already
contains candidate evidence, finished-normal evidence, the normal endpoint,
the subject-reduction obstruction, componentwise transport, replay, provenance,
and local naming rows.

Apply public export totality,
\autoref{thm:metacic-normalization-frontier-public-export-totality}. A public
consumer is forced onto either the candidate route or the finished-normal
route, and in both cases the subject-reduction obstruction row is retained.

Finally read the retained obstruction as a visible ledger. The same public
surface supplies $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support
through the displayed rows, so repackaging those coordinates gives
$O_{\mathrm{vis}}$ without adding a third normalization route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC normalization frontier visible obstruction route factorization]
\label{thm:metacic-normalization-frontier-visible-obstruction-route-factorization}
Every public route through the visible obstruction packet factors through the
finite package $C,N,O,S,B,H,R,P,L$ and then through one of the two public
export routes $C,K,R,P,L$ or $E,N,R,P,L$. The obstruction-preserving route is
finite, and it exports no blanket strong-normalization theorem, no closed
consistency proof, and no erased subject-reduction transport.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.MetaCICNormalizationFrontierUp.MetaCICNormalizationFrontierVisibleObstruction\_route\_factorization}
\begin{proof}
First project the visible packet back to the public export surface. The rows
$C,N,O,S,B,H,R,P,L$ are read from the same carrier, obstruction, replay,
provenance, and local naming coordinates used by
\autoref{def:metacic-normalization-frontier-public-export-surface}.

Next use the public totality theorem. By
\autoref{thm:metacic-normalization-frontier-public-export-totality}, the
positive part of the route must be $C,K,R,P,L$ or $E,N,R,P,L$. The obstruction
rows $O$ and $S$ remain attached to that choice as retained data.

The finite route has no escape coordinate. The non-escape theorem
\autoref{thm:metacic-normalization-frontier-public-export-nonescape} rules out
blanket strong normalization, closed consistency, erased subject-reduction
transport, and any hidden positive route beyond the two displayed export
routes. Thus the visible obstruction route factors through the finite public
surface only.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC normalization frontier visible obstruction consumer non-escape]
\label{thm:metacic-normalization-frontier-visible-obstruction-consumer-nonescape}
A downstream consumer of $O_{\mathrm{vis}}$ may cite the candidate evidence,
normal endpoint readback, obstruction ledger, subject-reduction obstruction
reference, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ rows as
frontier obstruction content. It cannot convert that packet into ambient
normalization, full closed consistency, erased subject-reduction transport, or
a consumer-owned normalization witness outside the two public export routes.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.MetaCICNormalizationFrontierUp.MetaCICNormalizationFrontierVisibleObstructionConsumerNonescape}
\begin{proof}
Read the consumer through the packet definition. The only available rows are
$C,N,O,S,B,H,R,P,L$, so every cited coordinate is candidate, endpoint,
obstruction, support, replay, provenance, or local naming data.

Route factorization then fixes the public boundary. By
\autoref{thm:metacic-normalization-frontier-visible-obstruction-route-factorization},
the consumer's positive access factors through $C,K,R,P,L$ or $E,N,R,P,L$,
with the obstruction ledger retained as obstruction content.

Finally apply public export non-escape. The theorem
\autoref{thm:metacic-normalization-frontier-public-export-nonescape} forbids
ambient normalization, full closed consistency, erased subject-reduction
transport, and hidden normalization evidence. The visible packet therefore
serves as citable obstruction content, not as a replacement for the frontier's
finite public export discipline.
\end{proof}
