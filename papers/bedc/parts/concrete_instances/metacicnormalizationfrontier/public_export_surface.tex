\begin{definition}[MetaCIC normalization frontier public export surface]
\label{def:metacic-normalization-frontier-public-export-surface}
The public export surface of an accepted
$\MetaCICNormalizationFrontierUp$ packet is the finite row package
$$
F_{\mathrm{pub}}=(C,K,E,N,S,H,R,P,L).
$$
Here $C$ is candidate evidence, $K$ is closed-candidate placement, $E$ is
finished-normal evidence, $N$ is the normal endpoint, $S$ is the
subject-reduction obstruction, $H$ is componentwise $\hsame$ transport, $R$
is the displayed $\Cont$ replay surface, $P$ is $\Pkg$ provenance, and $L$ is
the local $\NameCert$ row. A public read of the frontier packet is a read of
this finite surface, not a read of an ambient normalization theorem.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC normalization frontier public export totality]
\label{thm:metacic-normalization-frontier-public-export-totality}
Every public $\MetaCICNormalizationFrontierUp$ consumer factors through one of
the two displayed public routes:
\begin{enumerate}
\item the candidate surface $C,K,R,P,L$;
\item the finished-normal surface $E,N,R,P,L$.
\end{enumerate}
In both cases the row $S$ is retained as subject-reduction obstruction data,
and $H$ transports only the displayed components of the same finite public
surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier and local certificate obligations. The carrier
\autoref{def:metacic-normalization-frontier-carrier} supplies exactly the
rows $C,K,E,N,S,H,R,P,L$, and
\autoref{thm:metacic-normalization-frontier-namecert-obligations} assigns
consumer reads to the candidate boundary, the finished-normal boundary, and
the displayed handoff rows.

The positive route is then exhausted by the existing route theorems. Use
\autoref{thm:metacic-normalization-frontier-consumer-route-totality} and
\autoref{thm:metacic-normalization-frontier-evidence-choice-exhaustion}: a
public consumer must choose $C,K$ or $E,N$, with replay, provenance, and local
naming supplied by $R,P,L$.

Finally use obstruction retention. The theorem
\autoref{thm:metacic-normalization-frontier-obstruction-retention} keeps $S$
visible under both choices, and non-escape prevents $H,R,P,L$ from becoming a
third positive normalization route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC normalization frontier public export non-escape]
\label{thm:metacic-normalization-frontier-public-export-nonescape}
The public export surface of
\autoref{def:metacic-normalization-frontier-public-export-surface} cannot be
read as blanket strong normalization for all empty-context typed closed
MetaCIC terms, full closed consistency, erased subject-reduction transport, or
a third hidden normalization evidence route beyond $C,K,R,P,L$ and
$E,N,R,P,L$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the public surface to the existing carrier. The definition of
$F_{\mathrm{pub}}$ is only the displayed finite row package
$C,K,E,N,S,H,R,P,L$, so any public read must stay inside the same carrier,
transport, replay, package, and local naming coordinates.

Apply the chapter's non-escape and evidence-choice results.
\autoref{thm:metacic-normalization-frontier-nonescape} excludes blanket
normalization, closed consistency, and erased subject-reduction transport,
while \autoref{thm:metacic-normalization-frontier-evidence-choice-exhaustion}
leaves only the candidate and finished-normal evidence choices.

It remains to rule out a hidden route. Consumer-route totality gives no
public path outside $C,K,R,P,L$ and $E,N,R,P,L$, and obstruction retention
keeps $S$ as obstruction data rather than positive normalization evidence.
Therefore the public export boundary is exactly the two-route surface with
the obstruction row retained.
\end{proof}
