\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC normalization frontier obligation carrier totality]
\label{thm:metacic-normalization-frontier-obligation-closure-carrier-totality}
Carrier admission for an accepted $\MetaCICNormalizationFrontierUp$ packet
$$
F=(C,K,E,N,S,H,R,P,L)
$$
is exhausted by the displayed $\BHist$ rows. The carrier of \autoref{def:metacic-normalization-frontier-carrier}, the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:metacic-normalization-frontier-namecert-obligations}, and the root carrier rows of \autoref{thm:metacic-normalization-frontier-root-carrier-rows} leave no admitted frontier coordinate outside $C,K,E,N,S,H,R,P,L$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier definition. It presents $F=(C,K,E,N,S,H,R,P,L)$ as the finite frontier packet and assigns the candidate, finished-normal, obstruction, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming roles to those rows.

Apply the NameCert obligations. Candidate reads are bound to $C,K$, finished-normal reads are bound to $E,N$, and the obstruction row $S$ remains separated from the positive normalization surfaces.

Finally use the root carrier rows. Every root consumer begins from the same displayed $\BHist$ packet, so carrier admission is total over exactly those rows and has no hidden normalization, consistency, or erased-transport coordinate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC normalization frontier obligation evidence split]
\label{thm:metacic-normalization-frontier-obligation-closure-evidence-split}
For an accepted $\MetaCICNormalizationFrontierUp$ packet, positive normalization evidence splits before any closed-normal handoff into the candidate route through $C,K$ and the finished-normal route through $E,N$. This split is the joint content of \autoref{thm:metacic-normalization-frontier-candidate-route}, \autoref{thm:metacic-normalization-frontier-finished-route}, \autoref{thm:metacic-normalization-frontier-evidence-choice-exhaustion}, and \autoref{thm:metacic-normalization-frontier-public-obligation-package}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the candidate route. It routes candidate-facing consumers through $C,K,R,P,L$ and keeps the read inside the same finite packet.

Turn to the finished-normal route. It routes endpoint-facing consumers through $E,N,R,P,L$ and makes the normal endpoint available only after the finished-normal evidence row has been read.

The evidence-choice exhaustion theorem supplies the dichotomy. It rules out a third positive normalization evidence surface between those two routes.

Repack the result through the public obligation package. Since the package records carrier admission, the two positive routes, and the retained obstruction row together, any closed-normal handoff receives one of the two displayed positive surfaces rather than an unnamed normalization source.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC normalization frontier obligation obstruction retention]
\label{thm:metacic-normalization-frontier-obligation-closure-obstruction-retention}
For an accepted $\MetaCICNormalizationFrontierUp$ packet, the subject-reduction obstruction row $S$ is retained across both positive routes and cannot be consumed as normalization evidence. The obstruction ledger of \autoref{thm:metacic-normalization-frontier-obstruction-ledger}, the retention theorem of \autoref{thm:metacic-normalization-frontier-obstruction-retention}, and the subject-reduction compatibility theorem of \autoref{thm:metacic-normalization-frontier-subject-reduction-obstruction-compatibility} keep $S$ visible beside the candidate rows $C,K$ and the finished-normal rows $E,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the obstruction ledger first. It names $S$ as a separate coordinate of the same frontier packet as $C,K,E,N,H,R,P,L$.

Apply obstruction retention along the candidate and finished-normal routes. The candidate side through $C,K$ and the endpoint side through $E,N$ transport $S$ rather than turning it into positive normalization evidence.

Use subject-reduction obstruction compatibility for downstream consumers. A SubjectReductionBoundary-facing consumer may read $S$ as an obstruction coordinate, but it cannot discharge that row by consuming either positive normalization surface.
\end{proof}
