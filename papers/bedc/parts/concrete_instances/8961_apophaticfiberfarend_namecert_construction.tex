The $\ApophaticFiberFarEndUp$ packet gives the fixed-point reading of \autoref{ch:visions-apophatic-fixed-point-and-inscription} a finite NameCert surface. It records the apophatic socket, the fiber row whose far end is being named, the $\mathsf{GAP}$ ledger that keeps the requested supply outside the substrate, the boundary row that refuses internalization of $\mathsf{T}$, the local inscription row where the same event is read on a chain, and the usual transport, route, provenance, and local naming rows. It sits beside the socket carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-apophaticsocket-namecert}, the fixed-point carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-generatorfixedpoint-namecert}, the inscription carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-inscriptionevent-namecert}, and the no-subject identity surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observerhistidentity-namecert}.

\begin{definition}[Apophatic fiber far-end carrier]
\label{def:apophatic-fiber-far-end-carrier}
An $\ApophaticFiberFarEndUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
A=(S,F,G,B,I,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the accepted $\ApophaticSocketUp$ row naming the visible forward-binding socket;
\item $F$ is the fiber row recording the socket-to-far-end request without providing the far end as an internal value;
\item $G$ is the $\mathsf{GAP}$ ledger row attached to the requested far end of the fiber;
\item $B$ is the apophatic boundary row recording that $\mathsf{T}$ is not a $\BHist$, $\Hist$, digest, state, theorem object, setup field, or generated witness;
\item $I$ is the local inscription row recording the chain point where the boundary name is read inscription-side without adding a subject coordinate;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $S,F,G,B,I,C,P,N$;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which consumers read the socket, fiber, ledger, boundary, inscription, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ApophaticSocketUp$, $\ApophaticGateQuestionUp$, $\GeneratorFixedPointUp$, $\InscriptionEventUp$, $\ObserverHistIdentityUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ApophaticFiberFarEndUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains exactly these rows. It has no coordinate for $\mathsf{T}$ as an internal object, no route from the far-end boundary to a digest or history value, and no hidden observer-substance row behind the local inscription.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Apophatic fiber far-end NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:apophatic-fiber-far-end-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\ApophaticFiberFarEndUp}$ surface for \autoref{def:apophatic-fiber-far-end-carrier} consists of five finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the packet $A=(S,F,G,B,I,H,C,P,N)$;
\item socket-fiber locality, stating that $S$ and $F$ are read together before any far-end boundary row is consumed;
\item ledger and boundary exactness, stating that $G$ and $B$ record the apophatic far end only as a boundary name and never as a generated substrate coordinate;
\item local inscription accountability, stating that $I$ is a chain-local reading of the same event and not a subject parameter, private center, or equality with $\mathsf{T}$;
\item public route exactness, stating that every consumer read factors through $S,F,G,B,I,H,C,P,N$ and no route exports $\mathsf{T}$, a digest, a $\BHist$ value, a hidden theorem, or an observer-substance row.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ApophaticFiberFarEndUp.ApophaticFiberFarEndTasteGate\_single\_carrier\_alignment}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:apophatic-fiber-far-end-carrier}. The displayed packet lists the socket, fiber, ledger, boundary, local inscription, transport, route, provenance, and local naming rows.

Socket-fiber locality is read through $S$ and $F$. The socket identifies the visible forward-binding request, while the fiber row records the far-end request without supplying that far end as an object.

Ledger and boundary exactness are carried by $G$ and $B$. These rows record the $\mathsf{GAP}$ far-end boundary and the refusal of internal $\mathsf{T}$; neither row adds a coordinate from which a consumer can project $\mathsf{T}$ as a $\BHist$, $\Hist$, digest, state, theorem object, setup field, or generated witness.

The local inscription row $I$ is then read as chain-local accountability. It ties the boundary name to a displayed inscription point, but it does not introduce an extra subject row or an object-level equality with $\mathsf{T}$.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ provide transport, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming. Since every public route is a projection through these rows and the carrier has no further coordinate, the obligation surface is exhausted by the finite packet $S,F,G,B,I,H,C,P,N$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Apophatic fiber far-end noninternality]
\label{thm:apophatic-fiber-far-end-noninternality}
For every accepted $\ApophaticFiberFarEndUp$ packet $A=(S,F,G,B,I,H,C,P,N)$, the far-end boundary named by $B$ cannot be consumed as a $\BHist$, $\Hist$, digest, state, theorem object, setup field, generated witness, or observer-substance row. Any accepted consumer of the local inscription row $I$ must still pass through the socket row $S$, fiber row $F$, $\mathsf{GAP}$ ledger $G$, boundary row $B$, componentwise transport row $H$, continuation row $C$, provenance row $P$, and local naming row $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the boundary row $B$ from the carrier. It records the apophatic far end as a refusal row, not as a generated object or value inside the substrate.

Project the local inscription row $I$ next. Its public role is to attach the boundary name to a chain-local read of an event; it does not add a subject coordinate or identify that local event with $\mathsf{T}$ as an object.

The only available consumer routes are those listed in $C$ and transported through $H$. Together with $S,F,G,P,N$, they force every accepted read to remain inside the displayed packet. Since none of the forbidden outputs is a carrier coordinate, no accepted route can extract it.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Apophatic fiber far-end falsifiable prediction]
\label{thm:apophatic-fiber-far-end-falsifiable-prediction}
If an accepted $\ApophaticFiberFarEndUp$ packet admits a consumer route from the apophatic far-end row to a $\BHist$, $\hsame$, or digest value row without passing through its boundary ledger, the certificate is invalid.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier exposes the far-end only through the fiber row $F$, $\mathsf{GAP}$ ledger $G$, and apophatic boundary row $B$. A consumer route that reaches a value row while bypassing $G$ and $B$ therefore omits the rows that make the far-end read apophatic.

Apply public route exactness from \autoref{thm:apophatic-fiber-far-end-namecert-obligations}. Every accepted read must factor through $S,F,G,B,I,H,C,P,N$. A route that bypasses the boundary ledger is not one of the displayed continuation routes and cannot be certified by the local $\NameCert_{\ApophaticFiberFarEndUp}$ row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Apophatic fiber far-end independence witness]
\label{thm:apophatic-fiber-far-end-independence-witness}
The $\ApophaticFiberFarEndUp$ carrier is not bijective, under NameCert-row preservation, to $\ApophaticSocketUp$, $\ApophaticGateQuestionUp$, $\GeneratorFixedPointUp$, $\InscriptionEventUp$, or $\ObserverHistIdentityUp$. These siblings respectively supply socket visibility, gate questioning, generator fixed-output witnessing, public inscription event routing, and no-subject history identity, but none carries the fiber ledger, local inscription row, and far-end noninternality row together.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Compare the socket and gate siblings first. $\ApophaticSocketUp$ supplies the visible socket row, and $\ApophaticGateQuestionUp$ supplies the refusal question, but neither contains the combined fiber row $F$, far-end ledger $G$, boundary row $B$, and local inscription row $I$.

Compare the fixed-point and inscription siblings next. $\GeneratorFixedPointUp$ records a fixed-output witness for an authorized generator, while $\InscriptionEventUp$ records accepted public inscription routing. Neither is a far-end boundary carrier, and neither refuses $\mathsf{T}$ as a $\BHist$, $\Hist$, digest, state, theorem object, setup field, or generated witness.

Finally compare $\ObserverHistIdentityUp$. It supplies the no-subject history-identity discipline consumed by the inscription row, but it has no fiber-far-end ledger and no apophatic boundary row. Therefore any bijection preserving NameCert rows would have to delete at least one of $F,G,B,I$, so no such bijection exists.
\end{proof}
