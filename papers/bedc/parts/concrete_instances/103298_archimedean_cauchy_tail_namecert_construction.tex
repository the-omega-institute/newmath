\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ArchimedeanCauchyTailUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-archimedean-cauchy-tail-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges constructive analysis, the $\ArchimedeanCauchyTailUp$ packet records the finite route by which an explicit Archimedean bound on a displayed real name supplies a stable Cauchy-tail threshold envelope for regular rational names. It sits after the explicit bound surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-archimedeanreal-namecert}, the threshold modulus of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-tail-modulus-namecert}, the comparison route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-tail-equivalence-namecert}, the finite observation windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet names only the finite route from a displayed Archimedean row to a regular Cauchy tail threshold; it does not select a least bound, identify quotient streams, invoke trichotomy, use countable choice, or assert ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[Archimedean Cauchy-tail carrier]
\label{def:archimedean-cauchy-tail-carrier}
An $\ArchimedeanCauchyTailUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
T=(R,A,M,E,W,Q,D,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $R$ is the displayed $\RealUp$ name whose tail request is being bounded. It records only the real-name seal visible through the BEDC carrier surface.
\item $A$ is the $\ArchimedeanRealUp$ bound row. It records the explicit integer or dyadic bound exposed for the displayed real name, together with the finite comparison budget used to read it.
\item $M$ is the $\RegularCauchyTailModulusUp$ threshold row. It records the finite threshold requested by a regular Cauchy tail budget after the bound row has been displayed.
\item $E$ is the $\CauchyTailEquivalenceUp$ comparison row. It records the finite equivalence route that transports the threshold from the displayed regular name to the requested tail formulation.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window row. It records the inspected finite stream windows, the requested tail cut, and the observation schedule consumed by $A,M,E$.
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row. It records the regular rational observations read from the same windows and threshold request.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger. It records the dyadic radius, slack, denominator budget, and comparison witnesses used by the bound and tail rows.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the real name, Archimedean bound, threshold modulus, tail equivalence, stream windows, regular rational readback, dyadic ledger, replay, provenance, and local naming row.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the route
$$
R,A,W,D \longmapsto M \longmapsto E \longmapsto Q .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ArchimedeanCauchyTailUp$, $\RealUp$, $\ArchimedeanRealUp$, $\RegularCauchyTailModulusUp$, $\CauchyTailEquivalenceUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ArchimedeanCauchyTailUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate admits exactly these eleven rows. Its classifier compares accepted packets only through $R,A,M,E,W,Q,D,H,C,P,N$ and has no coordinate for host supremum data, selected least bounds, quotient stream equality, trichotomy, countable choice, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Archimedean Cauchy-tail NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:archimedean-cauchy-tail-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\ArchimedeanCauchyTailUp$ carrier
$$
T=(R,A,M,E,W,Q,D,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\ArchimedeanCauchyTailUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following five finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed real name, Archimedean bound row, tail-threshold modulus, tail-equivalence comparison, stream windows, regular rational readback, dyadic tolerance ledger, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item bound-row exposure, requiring $A$ to display the explicit Archimedean bound for $R$ before any threshold request is answered;
\item threshold extraction, requiring $M$ to obtain the regular Cauchy tail threshold only from the displayed bound row, stream windows, and dyadic budget;
\item tail-equivalence stability, requiring $E$ to transport the extracted threshold across the displayed Cauchy-tail comparison without adding quotient stream equality or a selected limit;
\item ledger nonescape, requiring $D,H,C,P,N$ to preserve the displayed route and to export no host supremum, least-bound selector, trichotomy, countable choice, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, or bridge credential.
\end{enumerate}
No Archimedean-to-tail obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet $T=(R,A,M,E,W,Q,D,H,C,P,N)$. The carrier exposes exactly the displayed real name, the Archimedean bound row, the regular Cauchy threshold row, the Cauchy-tail comparison row, the stream-window row, the regular rational readback row, the dyadic ledger, and the structural rows.

The bound exposure is first in the route. A tail request can inspect $R$ only through the bound row $A$, and $A$ records an explicit displayed bound together with the finite comparison budget that supports it.

The threshold extraction is then local. The row $M$ reads the already displayed bound, the windows in $W$, and the tolerance data in $D$ to produce a regular Cauchy tail threshold. No detached threshold source is present in the carrier.

The comparison row $E$ transports that same threshold across the displayed Cauchy-tail equivalence. Since $E$ has only the comparison data named in the tuple, it cannot replace the route by quotient stream equality or by a selected limiting representative.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite packet. Because the classifier has no coordinate for host supremum data, selected least bounds, trichotomy, countable choice, ambient completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking, the five listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Archimedean Cauchy-tail threshold envelope]
\label{thm:archimedean-cauchy-tail-threshold-envelope}
For every accepted $\ArchimedeanCauchyTailUp$ carrier
$$
T=(R,A,M,E,W,Q,D,H,C,P,N),
$$
every budgeted tail request first reads the Archimedean bound row $A$ for the displayed real name $R$ and then obtains its Cauchy threshold through the $\RegularCauchyTailModulusUp$ row $M$ and the $\CauchyTailEquivalenceUp$ row $E$. The request may inspect only the shared $\StreamNameUp$ windows $W$, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback $Q$, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger $D$, and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Begin with a budgeted tail request for the accepted packet. The request names the displayed real row $R$, but the only row that exposes a bound for $R$ is $A$.

After $A$ is read, the dyadic budget and finite observation schedule are fixed by $D$ and $W$. The row $M$ converts exactly those displayed data into the regular Cauchy threshold required by the request.

The row $E$ then transfers the threshold across the displayed Cauchy-tail equivalence. The readback row $Q$ records the regular rational observations reached by the same windows and threshold.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve the route under transport, replay, provenance, and local naming. Hence the request factors through $A$, then $M$ and $E$, and no other threshold source is available.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Archimedean Cauchy-tail nonescape boundary]
\label{thm:archimedean-cauchy-tail-nonescape-boundary}
Every consumer of an accepted $\ArchimedeanCauchyTailUp$ carrier is confined to the displayed route
$$
R,A,W,D \longmapsto M \longmapsto E \longmapsto Q \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
Consequently the packet excludes host supremum data, a selected least bound, quotient stream equality, trichotomy, countable choice, and ambient $\RealUp$ completeness from the Archimedean-to-tail handoff.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Let a consumer read an accepted carrier. The carrier definition permits the consumer to inspect only $R,A,M,E,W,Q,D,H,C,P,N$.

The real-name and bound part of the route is $R,A,W,D$. It displays a concrete bound row and the finite windows and dyadic budget needed to read it, but it stores neither a host supremum nor a least-bound selector.

The tail part of the route is $M,E,Q$. It extracts a regular Cauchy threshold and transports it through the displayed tail-equivalence row before exposing the regular rational readback.

The structural part of the route is $H,C,P,N$. These rows preserve and source the same finite packet, so they cannot add quotient stream equality, trichotomy, countable choice, or ambient real completeness.

Thus every accepted consumer remains inside the displayed route, and each listed escape principle is absent from the carrier surface.
\end{proof}
