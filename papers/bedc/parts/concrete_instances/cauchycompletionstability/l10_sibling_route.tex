\begin{theorem}[CauchyCompletionStability L10 sibling route]
\label{thm:cauchycompletionstability-l10-sibling-route}
Every accepted $\CauchyCompletionStabilityUp$ packet reads its L10 dependency lattice through the finite-window $\StreamNameUp$ rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular-rational $\RegSeqRatUp$ rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the terminal $\RealUp$ seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, and the $\CompletionUp$ and $\SeparatedMetricUp$ boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completion-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separatedmetric-namecert} before it exports completion stability. The route is visible as a finite $\BHist$ spine whose $\Cont$ replay and $\hsame$ transports reread exactly those sibling rows; it does not identify quotient representatives, select limits, invoke countable choice, assert host equality, or import ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-completion-stability-carrier} exposes the rows $M,S,D,Q,E,H,C,P,N$. The finite windows are read from $S$, the dyadic tolerances from $D$, the regular-rational readback from $Q$, and the terminal Real seal from $E$. The completion boundary is the displayed passage from the regular readback to the terminal seal, and the separated-metric boundary is the uniqueness discipline enforced only by the visible transport rows.

The route is therefore a $\BHist$ and $\Cont$ route, not a quotient construction. The replay coordinate $C$ rereads the same windows, tolerances, readback, seal, and boundary rows, while $H$ gives only componentwise $\hsame$ transport. No coordinate of the packet stores quotient representatives, a selected limit, a choice schedule, host equality, or an ambient completeness theorem, so none of those principles can be exported by the local NameCert.
\end{proof}
