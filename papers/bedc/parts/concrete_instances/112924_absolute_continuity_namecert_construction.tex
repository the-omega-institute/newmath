\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\AbsoluteContinuityUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-absolute-continuity-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Folland and Rudin, the $\AbsoluteContinuityUp$ packet names the finite epsilon--delta partition surface used to read absolutely continuous real functions before any measure-theoretic completion is available. It sits between the regulated-function schedules of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regulated-function-namecert}, the finite partition-variation ledgers of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-boundedvariation-namecert}, the variation-measure consumers of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-variation-measure-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet records only finite interval partitions, endpoint reads, dyadic tolerance ledgers, componentwise transport, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

\begin{definition}[AbsoluteContinuity carrier]
\label{def:absolute-continuity-carrier}
An $\AbsoluteContinuityUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
A=(I,\Pi,E,\Delta,X,D,R,V,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $I$ is the interval-domain row, read through the terminal $\RealUp$ endpoint surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}.
\item $\Pi$ is the finite partition family: a displayed finite list of interval subfamilies with endpoint rows, length rows, and common-refinement rows.
\item $E$ is the epsilon-budget row, recording the finite positive tolerance request that the packet answers.
\item $\Delta$ is the delta-budget row, recording the displayed finite bound on the total length of the selected interval subfamily.
\item $X$ records the $\RealUp$ endpoint reads, or their regular rational names when the endpoint is read through a sibling regular-name route.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger comparing interval lengths, endpoint differences, epsilon rows, and delta rows by finite dyadic observations.
\item $R$ is the source row, read from either a $\RegulatedFunctionUp$ step schedule of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regulated-function-namecert} or a $\BoundedVariationUp$ finite partition ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-boundedvariation-namecert}.
\item $V$ is the finite variation-control row consumed by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-variation-measure-namecert} when the endpoint-difference ledger is repacked as variation data.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for interval domain, finite partition family, epsilon budget, delta budget, endpoint reads, dyadic tolerance ledger, source row, variation-control row, provenance, and local naming.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from an epsilon request to a delta row, then to a finite interval subfamily, endpoint reads, dyadic comparisons, source schedule, and variation-control handoff.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\AbsoluteContinuityUp$, $\RealUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegulatedFunctionUp$, $\BoundedVariationUp$, $\VariationMeasureUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\AbsoluteContinuityUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $I,\Pi,E,\Delta,X,D,R,V,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for host function quotients, measure completion, selected suprema over all partitions, ambient Lebesgue integration, or unlisted endpoint representatives.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[AbsoluteContinuity NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:absolute-continuity-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\AbsoluteContinuityUp$ carrier $A=(I,\Pi,E,\Delta,X,D,R,V,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\AbsoluteContinuityUp}$ surface consists exactly of interval-domain admission, finite partition-family admission, epsilon-budget admission, delta-budget admission, $\RealUp$ endpoint reads, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance comparisons, regulated-function or bounded-variation source routing, variation-control handoff, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, local naming, and non-escape from host function quotients, measure completion, selected suprema, ambient Lebesgue integration, and unlisted endpoint representatives.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:absolute-continuity-carrier}. The analytic coordinates are exactly the interval row $I$, finite partition family $\Pi$, epsilon row $E$, delta row $\Delta$, endpoint reads $X$, dyadic tolerance ledger $D$, source row $R$, and variation-control row $V$.

The source row is already confined to displayed BEDC data. If it is read through $\RegulatedFunctionUp$, the schedule has passed through the finite step-envelope route of \autoref{thm:regulated-function-variation-control-route}. If it is read through $\BoundedVariationUp$, it is a finite partition-variation ledger over the rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-boundedvariation-namecert}.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name those same displayed coordinates. Since no carrier row names a host quotient, completed measure object, selected supremum, ambient integral, or hidden endpoint representative, none of those objects belongs to the NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[AbsoluteContinuity finite variation control]
\label{thm:absolute-continuity-finite-variation-control}
Every accepted $\AbsoluteContinuityUp$ packet whose epsilon--delta row is read on a displayed finite interval subfamily exposes variation control only through
$$
I,\Pi,E,\Delta,X,D,R,V,H,C,P,N .
$$
The handoff to $\BoundedVariationUp$ and $\VariationMeasureUp$ is the finite endpoint-difference ledger $D,V$ over the displayed partition family $\Pi$; it is not a host total-variation read, a measure completion, a selected supremum, or a quotient of functions.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an epsilon request in $E$. The continuation row $C$ first replays the delta row $\Delta$ and the displayed interval subfamily in $\Pi$, so the request is already confined to finitely many endpoint rows.

Read the endpoint values through $X$ and the dyadic comparisons through $D$. These rows give only finite endpoint-difference observations and finite length comparisons, all over the interval domain $I$.

The source row $R$ then supplies either a regulated step schedule or a bounded-variation partition ledger. In both cases, the downstream variation handoff is repacked into $V$ before any consumer in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-boundedvariation-namecert} or \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-variation-measure-namecert} can read it.

Thus the exposed variation control is the displayed finite ledger $D,V$ over $\Pi$. The carrier has no coordinate from which a host total variation, measure completion, selected supremum, quotient function, or ambient completeness principle could be obtained.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[AbsoluteContinuity no-escape boundary]
\label{thm:absolute-continuity-no-escape-boundary}
The $\AbsoluteContinuityUp$ public boundary exports finite interval-domain rows, finite partition families, epsilon and delta budget rows, $\RealUp$ endpoint reads, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledgers, regulated-function or bounded-variation source rows, finite variation-control handoff rows, visible $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ rows. It exports no measure completion, host function quotient, selected supremum over all partitions, ambient Lebesgue integral, hidden endpoint representative, or bridge claim.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier coordinates in \autoref{def:absolute-continuity-carrier}. Every exported analytic read is one of the finite rows $I,\Pi,E,\Delta,X,D,R,V$, and every structural read is one of $H,C,P,N$.

Apply the NameCert-obligation theorem. It identifies the same rows as the complete certificate surface and keeps the source route inside the regulated-function and bounded-variation chapters.

Finally apply the finite variation-control theorem. Any variation-facing consumer reaches only the displayed endpoint-difference and partition ledger, so there is no route to a completed measure object, quotient function, selected supremum, ambient integral, hidden endpoint representative, or bridge theorem.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[AbsoluteContinuity scoped kernel route]
\label{thm:absolute-continuity-scoped-kernel-route}
The carrier, NameCert-obligation surface, finite variation-control theorem, and no-escape boundary jointly bind $\AbsoluteContinuityUp$ to the concrete BEDC route through $\RealUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegulatedFunctionUp$, $\BoundedVariationUp$, $\VariationMeasureUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$. Every accepted public read factors through the displayed carrier tuple before any downstream variation or integration-facing consumer sees it.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier tuple from \autoref{def:absolute-continuity-carrier}. It lists the interval, partition, tolerance, endpoint, source, variation, transport, continuation, provenance, and naming rows available to the packet.

Use \autoref{thm:absolute-continuity-namecert-obligations} to identify those rows as the local obligation surface. The theorem also records the exclusions that keep absent analytic objects outside the packet.

Use \autoref{thm:absolute-continuity-finite-variation-control} for downstream consumers. It forces the bounded-variation and variation-measure handoff to pass through the displayed finite partition ledger and dyadic tolerance rows.

The no-escape boundary of \autoref{thm:absolute-continuity-no-escape-boundary} then exhausts the public interface. Hence the scoped kernel route is exactly the displayed tuple and its listed BEDC dependencies.
\end{proof}
