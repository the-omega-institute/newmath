\begin{definition}[Regular Cauchy tail fiber carrier]
\label{def:regular-cauchy-tail-fiber-carrier}
A $\RegularCauchyTailFiberUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
F=(R_0,R_1,W_0,W_1,D_0,D_1,T,A,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $R_0$ and $R_1$ are the two $\RegSeqRatUp$ source rows whose late regular-rational tails are being compared;
\item $W_0$ and $W_1$ are the finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows through which the two source rows are observed;
\item $D_0$ and $D_1$ are $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tail-radius ledgers for the two observed tails;
\item $T$ is the shared tail fiber row classifying the two source tails as one finite tail boundary;
\item $A$ is the $\RealUp$ tail-agreement seal row reached through the shared fiber;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the source rows, windows, dyadic ledgers, shared fiber, seal, replay route, provenance, and local name;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ routes for reading both source tails, both windows, both dyadic ledgers, the shared fiber row, and the seal row;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealTailAgreementSealUp$, $\RegularCauchyTailMeetUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyTailFiberUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier accepts only packets whose two regular Cauchy source tails meet in the displayed shared fiber row before any $\RealUp$ seal is read. It contains no quotient stream, no host real equality, no selected limit, and no completion theorem beyond the finite seal boundary.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy tail fiber NameCert obligations]

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy tail fiber window projection]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-tail-fiber-window-projection}

$$

$$
The read cannot reach the $\RealUp$ tail-agreement seal row $A$ without first passing through the shared tail fiber row $T$ and the displayed continuation route $C$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a source-facing consumer. Projection exposes $R_0,R_1$ as the only regular-rational source rows and $W_0,W_1$ as the only finite stream-name windows attached to them.

The dyadic-radius ledgers $D_0,D_1$ are then read at those same windows. A consumer has no separate radius or tolerance row outside the displayed packet.

The seal-facing step is forced through $T$ and $C$. Since $A$ is attached to the shared fiber route, a consumer cannot bypass the common fiber and read a detached $\RealUp$ seal.
