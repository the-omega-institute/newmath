\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RealCompletenessUp$}

The $\RealCompletenessUp$ interface records the diagonal-limit target of \autoref{ch:visions-real-completeness} as a finite naming-certificate packet. It packages the displayed $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observations, $\StreamNameUp$ windows, $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular sequence rows, and $\RealUp$ seal row through which a completion consumer sees the candidate limit. The packet is a seed carrier for the Real and Completion substrate; it contains no quotient real, selected host limit, choice principle, or host completeness theorem.

\begin{definition}[Real completeness finite carrier]
\label{def:real-completeness-carrier}
A $\RealCompletenessUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
R=(X,\mu,\Delta,D,W,Q,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the displayed $\RegSeqRatUp$ family of rational regular sequences;
\item $\mu$ is the explicit Cauchy modulus row naming, for each requested rational tolerance, a finite stage after which the family is stable;
\item $\Delta$ is the diagonal selector row, choosing the finite stage and rational observation used at each precision request;
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observation ledger containing the dyadic endpoints, radii, comparison rows, and tolerance rows read by the diagonal selector;
\item $W$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window family through which the selected rational names are inspected;
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row tying the selected windows to the regular rational sequence data;
\item $S$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row that presents the same diagonal observations as the candidate limit name;
\item $H$ stores displayed $\hsame$ transports for the input family, modulus, selector, dyadic ledger, stream windows, readbacks, and seal row;
\item $C$ stores the $\Cont$ routes by which consumers read the modulus, selected diagonal observations, and sealed candidate;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance row over $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RealCompletenessUp}$ registration row.
\end{enumerate}
The local classifier compares two carriers only through these displayed rows. It does not identify quotient representatives, does not choose an external real number, and does not appeal to ambient completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Real completeness NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:real-completeness-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\RealCompletenessUp}$ surface consists of five finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier formation for the finite packet of \autoref{def:real-completeness-carrier};
\item classifier stability for the transported family $X$, modulus $\mu$, diagonal selector $\Delta$, observation ledger $D$, windows $W$, readback row $Q$, and seal row $S$;
\item modulus exactness, requiring every convergence bound consumed by the diagonal selector to be the explicit row recorded by $\mu$;
\item diagonal readback exactness, requiring every selected rational observation to pass through the displayed $\StreamNameUp$ window, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger;
\item package closure, requiring the $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows to contain exactly the provenance used by downstream $\RealUp$ consumers.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.RealCompletenessUp.RealCompletenessBHistCarrier\_namecert\_obligations}
\begin{proof}
Carrier formation is the tuple assembly of \autoref{def:real-completeness-carrier}. Each dependency row named by the diagonal-limit target is a visible coordinate of $R$.

Classifier stability is componentwise. Transport the regular sequence family and modulus first, then transport the selected stream windows and dyadic observation ledger, and finally transport the $\RealUp$ seal row reached by those observations.

The modulus and diagonal readback clauses are projections of the same packet. The row $\mu$ supplies the finite stage bounds, while $\Delta$, $W$, $Q$, and $D$ record exactly which rational observations are read at each precision request.

Package closure follows by projecting $H$, $C$, $P$, and $N$. No consumer route reaches a quotient real, external limit selector, or host completeness theorem because no such coordinate occurs in the carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real completeness diagonal limit candidate]
\label{thm:real-completeness-diagonal-limit-candidate}
Let
$$
R=(X,\mu,\Delta,D,W,Q,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\RealCompletenessUp$ carrier. The candidate limit exported to $\RealUp$ is the seal row $S$ obtained from the selected diagonal observations $\Delta$ through the finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows $W$, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row $Q$, and the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger $D$. Every convergence read used by this candidate factors through the explicit modulus row $\mu$ and the displayed $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:real-completeness-namecert-obligations}. They bind the modulus row, diagonal selector, finite windows, regular-sequence readbacks, dyadic ledger, and seal row to the same carrier.

The diagonal selector $\Delta$ chooses only recorded stages and observations. The selected reads therefore pass through $W$, $Q$, and $D$ before the seal row $S$ is formed.

The convergence-facing part is controlled by $\mu$. Since all consumer routes are stored in $H$, $C$, $P$, and $N$, the exported candidate is exactly the finite diagonal-limit surface seen by $\RealUp$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real completeness no-escape boundary]
\label{thm:real-completeness-nonescape-boundary}
The public boundary of $\RealCompletenessUp$ exports only the finite diagonal-limit candidate over $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$. It exports no quotient real, no host equality on real numbers, no selected external limit, no countable-choice principle, and no host completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier coordinates of \autoref{def:real-completeness-carrier}. The only completion-facing rows are the modulus, diagonal selector, finite stream windows, regular-sequence readbacks, dyadic observation ledger, and $\RealUp$ seal row.

Apply \autoref{thm:real-completeness-diagonal-limit-candidate}. Every candidate-limit read is downstream of those displayed rows and their transport, continuation, package, and local naming data.

The excluded objects are absent from the tuple. Therefore the boundary cannot export a quotient real, host real equality, external selected limit, choice principle, or host completeness theorem.
\end{proof}
