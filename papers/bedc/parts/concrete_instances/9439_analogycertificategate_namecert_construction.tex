The $\AnalogyCertificateGateUp$ packet concretizes the relation-certificate discipline of \autoref{ch:visions-layered-relation-certificate} as finite NameCert data. It consumes the same-schema audit rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-relationalframeaudit-namecert}, the contextual reading discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-contextualclassreading-namecert}, the description boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-definite-description-boundary-namecert}, the admission gate of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-tastegateadmission-namecert}, and the refusal router of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-supply-kind-router-namecert}. Its role is to admit analogy language only after the source rows, carrier, classifier, preservation, refusal, ledger, exactness, and failure-boundary rows are visible in one packet.

\begin{definition}[AnalogyCertificateGate carrier]
\label{def:analogy-certificate-gate-carrier}
An $\AnalogyCertificateGateUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
A=(S,K,G,R,V,U,L,E,F,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the source-row ledger for the two themes whose analogy is being tested;
\item $K$ is the finite carrier row naming the analogy packet rather than either source theme;
\item $G$ is the contextual classifier row that licenses the analogy as a relation-first reading;
\item $R$ records the relation rows shared by the sources;
\item $V$ records the preserved rows that support same-schema language;
\item $U$ records the not-preserved rows, including any identity, empirical, subject-substance, or bridge claim refused by the packet;
\item $L$ is the ledger row recording which source row supports each preserved or not-preserved entry;
\item $E$ is the exactness row saying that same-schema language reads only the displayed relation schema;
\item $F$ is the failure-boundary row for analogy requests whose source, classifier, preservation, refusal, ledger, or exactness rows are absent;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for all visible rows;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ routes for admitted same-schema reads and refused analogy reads;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\AnalogyCertificateGateUp$, $\RelationalFrameAuditUp$, $\ContextualClassReadingUp$, $\DefiniteDescriptionBoundaryUp$, $\TasteGateAdmissionUp$, $\SupplyKindRouterUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\AnalogyCertificateGateUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for object identity between the sources, global-frame comparison, empirical law, subject-substance claim, denoting description, or requested supply outside the displayed packet.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[AnalogyCertificateGate NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:analogy-certificate-gate-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:analogy-certificate-gate-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\AnalogyCertificateGateUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for
$$
A=(S,K,G,R,V,U,L,E,F,H,C,P,N),
$$
source exposure through $S$, packet-carrier exposure through $K$, contextual classifier exposure through $G$, relation-row exposure through $R$, preserved-row exposure through $V$, not-preserved-row exposure through $U$, ledger exactness through $L,E$, refusal through $F$, and transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming through $H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:analogy-certificate-gate-carrier}. The tuple displays the source ledger, packet carrier, contextual classifier, relation rows, preserved rows, not-preserved rows, ledger, exactness row, failure boundary, transport, continuation, provenance, and local name.

The admitted route first reads $S,K,G,R$. These rows make the analogy a relation-first classifier over displayed source rows, in the sense of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-contextualclassreading-namecert}, rather than a host object shared by the sources.

Next the route reads $V,U,L,E$. The preserved rows and not-preserved rows are both ledgered before exact same-schema language can be exported, matching the relation-audit discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-relationalframeaudit-namecert}.

The refused route reads $F$, with \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-definite-description-boundary-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-tastegateadmission-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-supply-kind-router-namecert} supplying sibling boundaries for denotation, admission, and missing-supply requests. Finally $H,C,P,N$ only transport, replay, package, and name the same rows, so the NameCert surface is exhausted by the displayed packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[AnalogyCertificateGate falsifiable prediction]
\label{thm:analogy-certificate-gate-falsifiable-prediction}
If an accepted $\AnalogyCertificateGateUp$ packet has no not-preserved row $U$, then it cannot export same-schema language. Equivalently, every admitted same-schema read of an analogy packet factors through both the preserved row $V$ and the not-preserved row $U$ before the exactness row $E$ is readable.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:analogy-certificate-gate-namecert-obligations}. The theorem places same-schema export on the route through $V,U,L,E$.

If $U$ is absent, the exactness row has no displayed refusal side. The request is therefore routed to the failure-boundary row $F$, because the packet cannot distinguish same-schema language from identity collapse or unlisted bridge language.

Thus an accepted analogy gate without $U$ has no admitted export route for same-schema language. The prediction is falsifiable by deleting the not-preserved row: the packet then fails the gate rather than exporting analogy language.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[AnalogyCertificateGate independence witness]
\label{thm:analogy-certificate-gate-independence-witness}
The carrier of \autoref{def:analogy-certificate-gate-carrier} is not bijective, row for row, to any of $\RelationalFrameAuditUp$, $\ContextualClassReadingUp$, $\DefiniteDescriptionBoundaryUp$, $\TasteGateAdmissionUp$, or $\SupplyKindRouterUp$. A bijection preserving local NameCert obligations would have to preserve the joint rows $S,K,G,R,V,U,L,E,F,H,C,P,N$, and no listed sibling carries exactly this source, analogy-carrier, contextual-classifier, preserved, not-preserved, ledger, exactness, failure-boundary, transport, continuation, provenance, and local-name surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Compare first with $\RelationalFrameAuditUp$. That sibling carries frame-audit and no-global-frame rows; it supplies the relation-audit discipline consumed here, but it does not carry the contextual analogy classifier $G$ together with the admission, description, and supply-refusal boundaries.

Compare next with $\ContextualClassReadingUp$ and $\DefiniteDescriptionBoundaryUp$. They control contextual class reading and denoting-description use, but neither stores the paired preserved and not-preserved analogy rows $V,U$ with the source ledger $S$ and exactness row $E$.

Finally compare with $\TasteGateAdmissionUp$ and $\SupplyKindRouterUp$. They carry admission and requested-supply refusal rows, while $\AnalogyCertificateGateUp$ carries a relation packet whose same-schema export is governed by $V,U,L,E,F$. Since each sibling omits at least one required coordinate and carries rows not present in the analogy gate, no carrier bijection can preserve all NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}
