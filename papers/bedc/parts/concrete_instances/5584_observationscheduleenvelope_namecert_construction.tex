\begin{definition}[Observation schedule envelope carrier]
\label{def:observation-schedule-envelope-carrier}
An $\ObservationScheduleEnvelopeUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
E=(Q,S,W,R,J,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $Q$ is the accepted $\CountableObservationScheduleUp$ source packet supplying observed prefixes and finite request windows;
\item $S$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ source row through which the schedule is addressed;
\item $W$ is the Real-facing request-window row read by budget and completion consumers;
\item $R$ is the joint refusal row blocking completed streams, future-law claims, hidden persistence, and unbounded observation schedules;
\item $J$ is the consumer-handoff row tying the schedule source $Q$, StreamName row $S$, and Real-facing window $W$ into one envelope;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $Q,S,W,R,J,C,P,N$;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes that expose schedule prefixes, StreamName source names, Real-facing windows, refusal data, and the handoff row;

\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ObservationScheduleEnvelopeUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier is exactly this finite envelope. It exports no completed countable stream, no unobserved future row, no hidden causal-continuity principle, no quotient schedule equality, no selected Real value, and no ambient completion theorem.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Observation schedule envelope NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:observation-schedule-envelope-namecert-obligations}

$$

schedule-source exposure, StreamName source accountability, Real-facing request-window exposure, joint refusal, consumer handoff, componentwise $\hsame$ stability, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and non-escape for completed streams, future observations, hidden persistence laws, quotient schedule equality, selected Real values, and ambient completion.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:observation-schedule-envelope-carrier}. The rows $Q,S,W,R,J,H,C,P,N$ are all displayed coordinates of one finite $\BHist$ packet.

Schedule-source exposure reads $Q$ through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-countableobservationschedule-namecert}. The refusal and handoff rows then bind the envelope. The refusal row $R$ blocks completed streams and future-law data, and the handoff row $J$ forces the consumer to carry $Q,S,W$ together before the route is authenticated by $H,C,P,N$.

\begin{theorem}[Observation schedule envelope window transport]
\label{thm:observation-schedule-envelope-window-transport}
For an accepted $\ObservationScheduleEnvelopeUp$ carrier $E=(Q,S,W,R,J,H,C,P,N)$, any displayed transport of the Real-facing request window preserves the same source schedule row $Q$, StreamName source row $S$, joint refusal row $R$, and consumer-handoff row $J$. Transport may replay the window through $H$ and $C$, but it may not enlarge $W$, replace $S$ by an unlisted source, or turn the observed prefix carried by $Q$ into a completed stream.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier and read the componentwise transport row $H$. The row records transport for the schedule source, StreamName source, Real-facing window, refusal row, handoff row, route row, provenance row, and local name.

Replay the consumer route through $C$. A transported Real-facing read still reaches $W$ only together with $Q,S,R,J$, so the same finite source and refusal boundary remain visible.

Apply non-escape from \autoref{thm:observation-schedule-envelope-namecert-obligations}. Since the envelope has no coordinate for window enlargement, an unlisted source, or a completed stream, transport cannot create any of those rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}

The refusal row and non-escape clause exclude the missing objects. A consumer can inspect the displayed envelope, but it cannot obtain an independent carrier, a completed stream, a future observation law, or ambient completion from the handoff.
