The class-field-theory interface $\ClassFieldUp$ packages the maximal abelian extension of a number field together with the Artin map sending an idele class to a Frobenius automorphism. It depends on $\NameCert_{\NumFieldUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-numfield-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\AdeleUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-adele-namecert}).

\begin{theorem}[ClassField carrier and classifier obligations]\label{thm:classfield-obligation-carrier-classifier}
A $\ClassFieldUp$ certificate must provide a $\mathsf{BHist}$ carrier packaging a $\NumFieldUp$ base source, an $\AdeleUp$ idele-class source, an abelian-extension history row, and a classifier stable under the source $\NameCert$ classifiers and $\hsame$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ClassFieldUp.ClassFieldSourceCarrier\_semantic\_name\_certificate}

\begin{proof}
Unpack the roadmap ten-field template for $\ClassFieldUp$. The visible carrier row names the base source, the idele-class source, and the abelian-extension history row.

Then read the $\NumFieldUp$ and $\AdeleUp$ certificates as the two named dependency certificates. Their classifiers supply source replacement, while the $\ClassFieldUp$ row records the remaining classifier as an obligation rather than as a closed field.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ClassField Artin and Frobenius stability obligations]\label{thm:classfield-obligation-artin-frobenius-stability}
The Artin-map relation and Frobenius endpoint rows of a $\ClassFieldUp$ certificate must transport along the $\AdeleUp$ classifier, the $\NumFieldUp$ classifier, and $\mathsf{BHist}$ $\hsame$, and they must be compatible with the chosen extension classifier.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ClassFieldUp.ClassFieldArtinFrobeniusRows\_stability}

\begin{proof}
Carrier transport in the dependency $\NameCert$ objects supplies source replacement for the $\NumFieldUp$ and $\AdeleUp$ rows. The $\mathsf{BHist}$ comparison supplies the remaining history replacement.

The Artin relation and the Frobenius endpoints are then listed as stability obligations for the class-field classifier. Compatibility with the extension classifier is part of that obligation surface, not a reciprocity theorem.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ClassField ledger and exactness obligations]\label{thm:classfield-obligation-ledger-exactness}
The $\ClassFieldUp$ ledger records the hidden idele representative, the local-prime and Frobenius representative, and the extension presentation. Exactness consists of coverage, soundness, and completeness for the Artin-Frobenius classifier, without claiming reciprocity.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Separate visible classifier data from hidden representative data. The visible side contains the classifier row and the Artin-Frobenius comparison; the hidden side records the idele representative, local-prime representative, Frobenius representative, and extension presentation.

Identify the three ExactGlobalize-style rows that must be supplied: coverage of the classifier, soundness of accepted Artin-Frobenius rows, and completeness for the visible rows that the classifier is meant to cover. These rows state the obligation surface and do not assert reciprocity.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Scoped carrier source binding]\label{thm:classfield-scoped-carrier-source-binding}
The carrier and classifier obligation of $\ClassFieldUp$ is scoped over concrete BEDC data: $\mathsf{BHist}$ rows for the base source, idele-class source, and abelian-extension row; $\hsame$ transport between history rows; and the dependency classifiers supplied by $\NameCert_{\NumFieldUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\AdeleUp}$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Unpack Theorem~\ref{thm:classfield-obligation-carrier-classifier}. Its carrier row is already a $\mathsf{BHist}$ package with the base, idele-class, and extension histories visible as rows of the certificate.

Project the source classifiers from $\NameCert_{\NumFieldUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\AdeleUp}$. These projections give the permitted replacement steps for the number-field and adele endpoints.

Use $\hsame$ for the remaining BEDC history replacement. The only unclosed class-field data is then the extension classifier row, so the theorem records a scoped primitive boundary rather than an external semantic assumption.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Scoped Artin--Frobenius transport]\label{thm:classfield-artin-frobenius-transport-scope}
The Artin--Frobenius transport obligation of $\ClassFieldUp$ is scoped by endpoint replacement along $\NameCert_{\NumFieldUp}$, endpoint replacement along $\NameCert_{\AdeleUp}$, and $\mathsf{BHist}$ $\hsame$; the extension classifier remains the recorded row controlling the transported target.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Start from Theorem~\ref{thm:classfield-obligation-artin-frobenius-stability}. It lists the Artin relation and Frobenius endpoints as stability rows attached to the visible class-field classifier.

The $\NumFieldUp$ dependency certificate supplies replacement for the base field endpoint, and the $\AdeleUp$ dependency certificate supplies replacement for the idele-class endpoint. These are concrete certificate projections, not free parameters.

Apply $\hsame$ to move between the corresponding $\mathsf{BHist}$ rows. After these three transports are accounted for, the extension classifier is exactly the remaining row that records how the Artin--Frobenius target is carried.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Scoped exact-globalize boundary]\label{thm:classfield-exactglobalize-scope}
The coverage, soundness, and completeness obligations of $\ClassFieldUp$ are exactly an $\ExactGlobalizeBase$-style boundary for the visible Artin--Frobenius classifier. This boundary does not close reciprocity, maximality of the abelian extension, or any global class-field law beyond the displayed exactness rows.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Unpack Theorem~\ref{thm:classfield-obligation-ledger-exactness}. The ledger separates hidden representatives from the visible classifier and names coverage, soundness, and completeness as the exactness rows to be provided.

Collect those three rows in the same shape as $\ExactGlobalizeBase$: coverage says the visible classifier has admitted rows, soundness says accepted Artin--Frobenius rows are legitimate for the boundary, and completeness says every row in the stated visible scope is covered.

Nothing in this packaging supplies a reciprocity proof or a maximality theorem. The result therefore closes only the scoped boundary for the visible Artin--Frobenius classifier and leaves those stronger conclusions outside the chapter scope.
\end{proof}
