\begin{definition}[Differential-form root degree-probe consumer face]
\label{def:diffform-root-degree-probe-consumer-face}
The root degree-probe consumer face of $\DiffFormUp$ is the subface of
\autoref{def:diffform-root-bhist-namecert-surface} whose rows are exactly:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item the unary degree history of a carried
  $\mathsf{DiffFormBHistCarrier}_{M,T}$ row;
  \item the visible $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ spine carried by the same row;
  \item the degree-probe alignment transport row of
  \autoref{thm:diffform-degree-probe-alignment-transport};
  \item the wedge degree routing row exposed by the wedge ledger;
  \item the exterior-derivative successor-degree input routing row of
  \autoref{thm:diffform-exterior-input-root-closure};
  \item the displayed $BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$,
  $\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$, and $\NameCert_{\TensorProductUp}$ source rows.
\end{enumerate}
The face contains no row for nilpotence, closedness, nondegeneracy, contact
nonintegrability, a de Rham quotient, or a standard bridge. It is a consumer
face for downstream reads of degree and probe data only; all consumer-specific
laws remain outside $\DiffFormUp$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Differential-form root degree-probe face coverage]
\label{thm:diffform-root-degree-probe-face-coverage}
Every $\DiffFormUp$ row read by $\DeRhamUp$, $\SymplecticUp$, or $\ContactUp$
for degree bookkeeping, visible probe bookkeeping, degree-probe alignment,
wedge degree routing, or exterior-derivative successor-degree input routing
belongs to \autoref{def:diffform-root-degree-probe-consumer-face} or to the
shared root rows named by
\autoref{def:diffform-root-consumer-face-classifier}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First read the root carrier surface. Apply
\autoref{thm:diffform-root-bhist-carrier-obligation}. It identifies the carried
degree history, the visible $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ spine, and the ManifoldUp and
TensorProductUp source rows as fields of the root $BHist$ NameCert surface.

Next isolate the degree-probe row. Apply
\autoref{thm:diffform-degree-probe-alignment-carrier-refinement}. The carried
form row refines to the unary degree history and the matching probe bundle over
the same tensor input reading, so the downstream degree/probe read is one of
the rows named in
\autoref{def:diffform-root-degree-probe-consumer-face}.

For operation routing, use
\autoref{thm:diffform-exterior-input-root-closure} and
\autoref{thm:diffform-root-bhist-downstream-boundary}. These theorems place the
successor-degree exterior input, wedge degree routing, carried row, classifier
row, source rows, and $\Cont$ histories on the root-visible surface.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:diffform-root-consumer-face-coverage}. Every
downstream exported row is in a consumer face or in the shared root face.
Intersecting that coverage with the rows listed in
\autoref{def:diffform-root-degree-probe-consumer-face} gives the stated
coverage for degree and probe reads, with no residual row outside the two
named faces.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Differential-form root degree-probe face stability]
\label{thm:diffform-root-degree-probe-face-stability}
Classifier transport along $\sim_{\DiffFormUp}$ preserves the root
degree-probe consumer face. In particular, transported downstream reads of
degree history, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ spine, degree-probe alignment, wedge
degree routing, exterior-derivative successor-degree input routing, and the
displayed source rows remain inside
\autoref{def:diffform-root-degree-probe-consumer-face}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Transport the root surface first. Apply
\autoref{thm:diffform-root-bhist-classifier-stability-obligation}; carrier
transport preserves the degree history, probe bundle, wedge routing,
exterior-input routing, and displayed source rows of
\autoref{def:diffform-root-bhist-namecert-surface}.

Now transport the aligned subrow. Apply
\autoref{thm:diffform-degree-probe-alignment-transport}. The classifier
comparison carries the $\hsame$ degree comparison and the transported
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ spine to the target endpoint, rebuilding the aligned
degree/probe row there.

It remains to keep consumer laws out of the transported face. Apply
\autoref{thm:diffform-root-consumer-face-disjointness}. Nilpotence, closedness,
nondegeneracy, and contact nonintegrability are consumer-specific law rows, and
the root degree-probe face contains no quotient or standard bridge row. Thus
classifier transport preserves the face without enlarging it.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Differential-form root degree-probe downstream factorization]
\label{thm:diffform-root-degree-probe-face-downstream-factorization}
Every read by $\DeRhamUp$, $\SymplecticUp$, or $\ContactUp$ that uses
$\DiffFormUp$ degree or probe data factors first through
\autoref{def:diffform-root-degree-probe-consumer-face}, and only after that
through the law rows owned by the downstream chapter.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an arbitrary downstream read of $\DiffFormUp$ degree or probe data.
Apply \autoref{thm:diffform-root-bhist-downstream-boundary}; downstream access
to $\DiffFormUp$ factors through the carried row, classifier row,
degree-probe alignment, wedge routing, exterior-input routing, source NameCert
rows, and $\Cont$ route histories.

Refine the degree/probe component with
\autoref{thm:diffform-degree-probe-alignment-carrier-refinement} and transport
it with \autoref{thm:diffform-degree-probe-alignment-transport}. The consumer
therefore reads the unary degree history and the $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ spine
through the face defined in
\autoref{def:diffform-root-degree-probe-consumer-face}, not through a separate
downstream carrier.

For exterior-derivative input, apply
\autoref{thm:diffform-exterior-input-root-closure}. The successor-degree input
row is repacked from the same carried row, transported probe bundle, tensor
row, scalar endpoint comparison, and source ledger. For wedge routing, use the
wedge degree routing already listed by the root $BHist$ downstream boundary.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:diffform-root-consumer-face-coverage} and
\autoref{thm:diffform-root-consumer-face-disjointness}. Coverage places all
downstream reads in the named consumer or shared root faces, while disjointness
keeps nilpotence, closedness, nondegeneracy, contact nonintegrability, quotient
data, and bridge data outside this face. Hence $\DeRhamUp$, $\SymplecticUp$,
and $\ContactUp$ first factor through the degree-probe consumer face and then
state their own laws on their own surfaces.
\end{proof}
