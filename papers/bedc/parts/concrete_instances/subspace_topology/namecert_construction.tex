\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\SubspaceTopologyUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-subspace-topology-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bourbaki and Kelley, the $\SubspaceTopologyUp$ packet records the induced topology on a displayed carrier inside an ambient topological space as finite BEDC naming data. It sits between the indexed-open topology surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-topology-namecert}, the metric-subspace restriction packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-subspace-namecert}, and the existing $\mathsf{BHist}$ subspace-open surface in \autoref{def:topology-bhist-subspace-open-surface}. The packet gives compact-subspace, restriction-of-continuity, and relative-open consumers a general topology-level carrier, without forcing them through metric distance rows, host powersets, quotient subset equality, or selected ambient points.

\begin{definition}[SubspaceTopology carrier]
\label{def:subspace-topology-carrier}
A $\SubspaceTopologyUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(X,A,O,R,B,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the ambient $\TopologyUp$ row, exposing the point classifier, indexed-open ledger, finite-intersection rows, arbitrary-union rows, boundary opens, and visible open-membership stability.
\item $A$ is the displayed admission row for the subspace carrier. A point is available to the subspace packet only when it passes the ambient point classifier of $X$ and the admission row $A$.
\item $O$ is the relative-open row. Each accepted subspace open is displayed as an ambient open row of $X$ together with restriction to admitted points.
\item $R$ is the restriction-exactness row, recording that membership in a relative open is read only as simultaneous admission by $A$ and ambient membership in the corresponding open row of $X$.
\item $B$ is the boundary and closure ledger, recording empty, whole-of-subspace, finite-intersection, and arbitrary-union rows obtained by restricting the corresponding ambient topology rows.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the ambient topology, admission row, relative-open row, restriction exactness, boundary and closure ledger, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a relative-open request through $X,A,O,R,B,H$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\SubspaceTopologyUp$, $\TopologyUp$, $\MetricSubspaceUp$, \autoref{def:topology-bhist-subspace-open-surface}, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\SubspaceTopologyUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $X,A,O,R,B,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for host powersets, quotient subset equality, selected ambient representatives, independent open selectors, metric distance data, or a replacement topology.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[SubspaceTopology NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:subspace-topology-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\SubspaceTopologyUp$ carrier $S=(X,A,O,R,B,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\SubspaceTopologyUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the ambient topology and admission rows, relative-open exposure, restriction exactness, boundary and closure rows, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming. Every relative-open consumer must read the route
$$
\TopologyUp,A,O,R,B,H,C,P,N
$$
before treating a subspace open as public.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:subspace-topology-carrier}. The available rows are the ambient topology $X$, admission predicate $A$, relative-open row $O$, restriction exactness $R$, boundary and closure ledger $B$, and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

Relative-open exposure is the role of $O$ and $R$. A public subspace membership read is not a host subset membership; it is the displayed conjunction of the admission row $A$ and an ambient open-membership row of $X$.

The boundary and closure rows are inherited from the ambient topology. Empty and whole-of-subspace opens are obtained by restricting the corresponding boundary opens of $X$, finite intersections restrict the ambient meet row, and arbitrary unions restrict the ambient union row while keeping the same admission predicate.

Transport and replay preserve the same route. The row $H$ aligns the ambient classifier and admission predicate, $C$ replays relative-open requests through the displayed rows, and $P,N$ source and name the packet.

Since host powersets, quotient subset equality, selected representatives, independent open selectors, metric distance data, and replacement topologies are absent from the carrier, no local NameCert obligation can export them.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SubspaceTopology neighbour compatibility]
\label{thm:subspace-topology-neighbour-compatibility}
Every $\SubspaceTopologyUp$ packet refines the ambient $\TopologyUp$ row by restriction to admitted points, and every $\MetricSubspaceUp$ topology consumer may read the same admission row through this topology-level packet before invoking metric distance data. In particular, the relative-open route factors as
$$
X,A \longmapsto O,R,B \longmapsto H,C,P,N ,
$$
so compact subspace, continuity restriction, and metric-subspace consumers can share the same displayed subspace carrier while keeping metric distances outside the general topology certificate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted $\SubspaceTopologyUp$ carrier and project the ambient topology row $X$ and admission row $A$. These two rows determine the carrier on which all relative-open reads take place.

Use \autoref{thm:subspace-topology-namecert-obligations}. It forces every relative-open read through $O,R,B$ after the admission row has been displayed, and it keeps transport, replay, provenance, and local naming in $H,C,P,N$.

A $\MetricSubspaceUp$ consumer supplies a compatible admission row and an inherited distance ledger, but those metric rows are not needed to form the relative opens. The topology-level packet can therefore be read first, and metric distance data can be invoked only by consumers that explicitly require it.

Thus the packet is the common neighbour of the ambient topology and metric-subspace surfaces: it restricts opens by admission, names the induced topology, and does not add an independent metric or host subset coordinate.
\end{proof}
