\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\BisectionRealUp$}

Following Bishop and Bridges constructive analysis, the $\BisectionRealUp$ packet records bisection as a finite nested-interval and bracket-refinement certificate over the L10 Real route. It sits after the dyadic tolerance surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the finite stream windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, and the nested interval route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-nested-interval-real-namecert}. The packet names the displayed bisection ledger; it is not a Dedekind cut, quotient real, trichotomy principle, selected root, or completeness theorem.

\begin{definition}[BisectionReal carrier]
\label{def:bisection-real-carrier}
A $\BisectionRealUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
B=(I,M,S,T,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $I$ is the nested interval source row, recording the lower and upper $\RealUp$ endpoint names, interval containment, and the finite retained subintervals consumed by the bisection route.
\item $M$ is the dyadic midpoint and tolerance ledger. It records the midpoint rows, branch radii, diameter budgets, and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ comparisons used at each inspected stage.
\item $S$ is the sign or bracket-preservation row, storing the displayed bracket invariant that survives each retained child interval.
\item $T$ is the tail request row, recording the finite precision request and the inspected bisection depth needed to answer it.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window row for the retained endpoint and midpoint names.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row extracted from $W$ and $M$.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row for the real name exported by the retained nested interval route.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the interval source, midpoint ledger, bracket row, tail request, stream windows, regular readback, Real seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a bisection request through interval inspection, midpoint production, bracket preservation, dyadic tolerance readback, stream-window exposure, regular readback, and Real-seal consumption.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BisectionRealUp$, $\NestedIntervalRealUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\BisectionRealUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $I,M,S,T,W,R,E,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for Dedekind cuts, quotient-real representatives, real trichotomy, selected roots, host equality of real points, countable choice, or ambient completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[BisectionReal NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:bisection-real-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\BisectionRealUp$ carrier
$$
B=(I,M,S,T,W,R,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\BisectionRealUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the nested interval source, dyadic midpoint and tolerance ledger, bracket-preservation row, tail request, stream windows, regular readback, Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming;
\item nested-source discipline, requiring every retained interval read to pass through $I$ before midpoint or Real-facing data are available;
\item midpoint and tolerance discipline, requiring $M$ to expose only dyadic midpoint, radius, and diameter rows whose budgets answer the request $T$;
\item bracket preservation, requiring $S$ to carry the displayed sign or bracket invariant across each retained child interval;
\item readback discipline, requiring $W$ and $R$ to expose the retained interval only through finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows and $\RegSeqRatUp$ rows;
\item seal nonescape, requiring $E,H,C,P,N$ to export only the displayed bisection route and no Dedekind cut, quotient real, trichotomy principle, selected root, host equality, countable choice, or completeness theorem.
\end{enumerate}
No BisectionReal obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:bisection-real-carrier}. The accepted packet displays exactly the nested interval source, midpoint ledger, bracket row, tail request, window rows, readback, Real seal, and structural rows.

The finite source stage is $I,T$. A precision request opens the source interval row and the requested bisection depth before any midpoint or retained endpoint can be inspected.

The bisection stage is $M,S$. A finite induction over the retained branch ledger reads one midpoint row at a time, uses the dyadic tolerance entries to bound the retained child, and carries the displayed sign or bracket invariant into the next interval row.

The readback stage is $W,R,E$. The retained endpoints are exposed as finite stream windows, then as regular rational readbacks, and only then as the terminal $\RealUp$ seal.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, package, and name the same route. Since the carrier contains no Dedekind-cut, quotient-real, trichotomy, selected-root, host-equality, countable-choice, or completeness coordinate, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BisectionReal nested interval factorization]
\label{thm:bisection-real-nested-interval-factorization}
Every $\BisectionRealUp$ read factors through the displayed nested interval route
$$
I,T \longmapsto M,S \longmapsto W,R \longmapsto E,H,C,P,N .
$$
In particular, a Real-facing consumer first reads the $\NestedIntervalRealUp$ source row $I$, then the finite dyadic midpoint and bracket-preservation rows $M,S$, then the finite $\StreamNameUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback rows $W,R$, and only then the terminal $\RealUp$ seal and local $\NameCert_{\BisectionRealUp}$ row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the accepted carrier $B=(I,M,S,T,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)$. The only interval source coordinate is $I$, and the only request coordinate is $T$, so a bisection read begins by exposing those rows.

Induct on the finite branch prefix recorded in the midpoint ledger. The base case reads the initial interval and its bracket row. In the successor step, the midpoint row in $M$ supplies the dyadic split, the tolerance entry supplies the diameter budget, and $S$ records that the displayed bracket invariant is preserved for the retained child.

After the last retained child, the endpoint windows are read through $W$ and the rational data through $R$. The Real-facing seal $E$ is therefore downstream from the nested interval, midpoint, bracket, stream, and regular readback rows.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ do not add a second route. They transport componentwise $\hsame$, replay the same displayed $\Cont$ chain, record $\Pkg$ provenance, and supply the local name. Thus every read factors through the stated finite route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BisectionReal regular Cauchy handoff]
\label{thm:bisection-real-regular-cauchy-handoff}
For every accepted $\BisectionRealUp$ carrier $B=(I,M,S,T,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, the handoff to $\RegSeqRatUp$ and $\RealUp$ is available only after the retained nested-interval rows, dyadic midpoint and tolerance rows, sign or bracket-preservation rows, and finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows have been replayed through the same $\BHist$ packet. The exported object is a regular Cauchy readback with a Real seal, not a selected root, quotient representative, trichotomy decision, or completeness witness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the nested interval factorization of \autoref{thm:bisection-real-nested-interval-factorization}. It places $W,R,E$ strictly after the source row $I$, request row $T$, midpoint ledger $M$, and bracket row $S$.

The $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff is the row $R$. It is computed from the finite stream windows in $W$ and the dyadic tolerance entries in $M$, so no downstream consumer can read $R$ without replaying the retained bisection ledger.

The $\RealUp$ handoff is the row $E$. It seals the regular readback already obtained from $R$, while $H,C,P,N$ preserve the same packet by componentwise $\hsame$, displayed $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ data.

Since the carrier has no selected-root, quotient-representative, trichotomy-decision, host-equality, countable-choice, Dedekind-cut, or completeness coordinate, the handoff exposes only the regular Cauchy route stated in the theorem.
\end{proof}
