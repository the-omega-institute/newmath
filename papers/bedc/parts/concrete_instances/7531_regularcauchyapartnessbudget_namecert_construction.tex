\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy apartness-budget NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-apartness-budget-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-apartness-budget-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyApartnessBudgetUp}$ surface consists of carrier admission, classifier stability, apartness-budget exposure, dyadic lower-bound soundness, and Real-seal non-escape for the same finite packet $B=(X,A,M,W,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)$. Carrier admission exposes exactly the listed rows. Classifier stability transports those rows componentwise through $H$ and replays the same routes through $C$. Apartness-budget exposure requires $A$ before any lower-bound or seal read. Dyadic lower-bound soundness requires $D$ to be read from the finite window $W$ selected by $M$. Real-seal non-escape requires every $\RealUp$ consumer to reach $E$ only after the regular readback $R$ has been displayed.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the displayed packet. The carrier rows are exactly $X,A,M,W,D,R,E,H,C,P,N$, so admission has no hidden coordinate outside the tuple.

Classifier stability is componentwise. The transport row $H$ compares the same source, budget, modulus, window, lower-bound, readback, seal, provenance, and name fields, while $C$ replays the displayed route in the transported packet.

The apartness and dyadic clauses follow from the order of the finite route. The budget row $A$ is read before the selected window and lower-bound row, and $D$ is a read of the finite StreamName window $W$ under the modulus row $M$.

The Real-seal clause then uses the regular readback row $R$. Since $E$ is the only Real seal coordinate and it is reached only through $R,H,C,P,N$, a seal-facing consumer cannot bypass the displayed apartness-budget and dyadic lower-bound rows.
\end{proof}
