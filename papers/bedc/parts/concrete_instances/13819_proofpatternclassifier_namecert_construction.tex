It sits beside the proof witness chain of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-proofwitnesschain-namecert}, the forward-binding GAP ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-forward-binding-gap-ledger-namecert}, the GAP socket boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-gap-socket-boundary-namecert}, the registry no-smuggling predicate of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-registrynosmugglingpredicate-namecert}, and the typed failure certificate of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-typedfailurecertificate-namecert}. Those siblings expose proof traces, forward commitments, missing-supply boundaries, export refusal, and typed failure status separately; this packet classifies proof-pattern routes only when positive witness construction, local refutation, decidable double-negation, and prohibited positive extraction remain separated.

\begin{definition}[Proof-pattern classifier carrier]
\label{def:proof-pattern-classifier-carrier}
A $\ProofPatternClassifierUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
P=(W,L,D,R,E,G,H,C,Q,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $W$ is the proof-witness-chain row, recording the accepted finite proof trace, source carrier, classifier witness, ledger, refusal, audit verdict, provenance, and local name consumed from $\ProofWitnessChainUp$;
\item $L$ is the local carrier and classifier context, recording the fixed object row, predicate row, classifier row, and local certificate surface against which the proof pattern is read;
\item $D$ is the displayed decidability evidence, present exactly when a local $\mathsf{Decidable}(P(h))$ route is available for the named predicate and history row;
\item $R$ is the local refutation and inversion evidence, recording carrier no-confusion, classifier inversion, certificate-field contradiction, and typed failure rows;
\item $E$ is the forbidden positive-extraction refusal row, blocking extraction of existential or positive witness content from $\neg\forall h,\neg P(h)$, undecided $\neg\neg P(h)$, arbitrary excluded middle, quotient collapse, host equality, or hidden choice;
\item $G$ is the GAP row for forward-binding commitments, recording any unsupported universal, future, or missing-supply component through $\ForwardBindingGapLedgerUp$ and $\GapSocketBoundaryUp$ rather than as proof content;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for proof witness, local classifier, decidability, refutation, refusal, GAP, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay for positive witness construction, local refutation, decidable double-negation, positive-extraction refusal, GAP handoff, and downstream audit;
\item $Q$ records $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ProofPatternClassifierUp$, $\ProofWitnessChainUp$, $\ForwardBindingGapLedgerUp$, $\GapSocketBoundaryUp$, $\RegistryNoSmugglingPredicateUp$, $\TypedFailureCertificateUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ProofPatternClassifierUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exports no route from refutation to positive content, no double-negation eliminator without $D$, no witness for an existential claim without a displayed witness row in $W$ or $L$, and no registry export that bypasses $E,G,Q,N$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Proof-pattern classifier NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:proof-pattern-classifier-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:proof-pattern-classifier-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\ProofPatternClassifierUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following five obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier exposure for $P=(W,L,D,R,E,G,H,C,Q,N)$;
\item classifier stability for the proof-witness row $W$ against the local carrier and classifier context $L$, preserved under $H$ and $C$;
\item local-refutation boundary through $R$, with typed failure and certificate-field contradiction kept inside the displayed carrier and classifier rows;
\item decidable double-negation gate through $D$, allowing $\neg\neg P(h)$ to be consumed as positive content only when the same local predicate row has displayed decidability;
\item positive-extraction refusal through $E,G,Q,N$, blocking positive witness export from negative or undecided premises and preserving the GAP and no-smuggling audit route.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the packet of \autoref{def:proof-pattern-classifier-carrier}. The local certificate can read only $W,L,D,R,E,G,H,C,Q,N$, so carrier exposure fixes the proof witness, classifier context, decision row, refutation row, refusal row, GAP row, transport, replay, provenance, and local name.

Classifier stability is then read through $W,L,H,C$. The proof witness row supplies the finite proof chain, the local row supplies the predicate and classifier surface, and transport plus replay preserve that same pairing for downstream use.

The remaining obligations separate the three proof-pattern exits. The refutation route uses $R$ only as local inversion against displayed rows; the double-negation route uses $D$ only as displayed decidability; and the refusal route uses $E,G,Q,N$ to prevent missing witnesses or unsupported universal commitments from becoming positive proof content.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Proof-pattern classifier local-refutation boundary]
\label{thm:proof-pattern-classifier-local-refutation-boundary}
An accepted $\ProofPatternClassifierUp$ packet accepts a negative proof only when its refutation factors through the displayed carrier row $L$, proof-witness row $W$, local inversion row $R$, transport row $H$, replay row $C$, provenance row $Q$, and local NameCert row $N$. A contradiction that requires a hidden truth predicate, unrecorded classifier crossing, host equality, quotient collapse, or missing certificate field is outside the accepted local-refutation route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the obligation surface of \autoref{thm:proof-pattern-classifier-namecert-obligations}. A negative proof is accepted through the local-refutation row, not through a global truth classifier.

The row $R$ must therefore consume a purported witness through the displayed carrier and classifier context $L$ and the proof-witness chain $W$. Transport and replay are limited to $H$ and $C$, while provenance and local naming remain $Q$ and $N$.

If the contradiction uses hidden truth, unrecorded classifier crossing, host equality, quotient collapse, or an absent certificate field, the factorization through $L,W,R,H,C,Q,N$ fails. The packet then records no accepted negative proof; it records either a refusal route through $E$ or an exposed GAP route through $G$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Proof-pattern classifier positive-extraction refusal]
\label{thm:proof-pattern-classifier-positive-extraction-refusal}
No accepted $\ProofPatternClassifierUp$ packet can turn $\neg\forall h,\neg P(h)$ or undecided $\neg\neg P(h)$ into positive content without the displayed decidability row $D$ or a displayed witness row in $W$ or $L$. In particular, the packet refuses existential export, theorem-evidence export, and local NameCert export for any route whose only positive source is negative pressure, undecided double negation, arbitrary excluded middle, or a hidden registry entry.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the carrier rows. Positive content must come from a witness already displayed in $W$ or $L$, or from a decidable double-negation route whose decision evidence is displayed in $D$.

The premise $\neg\forall h,\neg P(h)$ supplies no named $h$ and no row witnessing $P(h)$. The premise $\neg\neg P(h)$ supplies no constructor for $P(h)$ unless the same local predicate row is accompanied by $D$.

The refusal row $E$ and GAP row $G$ record exactly these missing-source cases. Provenance and local naming through $Q,N$ preserve the refusal, while the registry no-smuggling sibling prevents a hidden entry from being exported as theorem evidence. Hence the accepted packet cannot convert negative pressure or undecided double negation into positive content.
\end{proof}
