\begin{theorem}[Cauchy witness ledger source sibling readback]
\label{thm:cauchy-witness-ledger-source-sibling-readback}
Let $L=(Q,B,S,K,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted
$\CauchyWitnessLedgerUp$ packet. Every completion-facing read of $L$ exposes
the four sibling source rows in the order
$$
\CauchyDiagonalBudgetUp \longmapsto
\RealObservationBudgetUp \longmapsto
\CauchySealBudgetSynchronizerUp \longmapsto
\RealCompletionSealClassifierUp
$$
before any completion-seal classifier consumer reads $K$.

The consumer receives the displayed source rows, the componentwise transport
row $H$, route replay $C$, package provenance $P$, and the local naming row
$N$. It receives no quotient stream, host real equality, selected external
limit, countable-choice row, or ambient completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-witness-ledger-carrier}. Its
completion-facing source rows are exactly $Q,B,S,K$, with $H,C,P,N$ carrying
transport, replay, provenance, and local naming for those rows.

The budget factorization and source coherence are the displayed route
constraints. The diagonal-observation route of
\autoref{thm:cauchy-witness-ledger-diagonal-observation-route} first reads the
$\CauchyDiagonalBudgetUp$ row $Q$ and the $\RealObservationBudgetUp$ row $B$.
The synchronizer-classifier lock of
\autoref{thm:cauchy-witness-ledger-synchronizer-classifier-lock} then carries
the read through $S$ before the classifier row $K$ is consumed.

Classifier consumer non-escape and forbidden-coordinate exclusion close the
boundary. Apply \autoref{thm:cauchy-witness-ledger-real-consumer-nonescape}
and \autoref{thm:cauchy-witness-ledger-real-seal-refusal-boundary}. Any
request for a quotient stream, host equality, selected limit, countable-choice
row, or ambient completeness theorem has no carrier coordinate and is refused
outside the source-sibling route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy witness ledger Real-and-Completion sibling route]
\label{thm:cauchy-witness-ledger-real-completion-sibling-route}
Let $L=(Q,B,S,K,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyWitnessLedgerUp$
packet. Every Real-and-Completion consumer route that reads $L$ and reaches
the completion-seal classifier must pass through the finite-window surface of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular readback of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic ledger of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the seal boundary of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, and the public route of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchylimitseal-namecert}. The route exports no
quotient stream, host equality, selected limit, hidden tail oracle, or ambient
completeness coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-witness-ledger-carrier}. The
accepted packet supplies exactly the rows $Q,B,S,K,H,C,P,N$; thus any route to
the completion-seal classifier must be read through the displayed
diagonal-budget, observation-budget, synchronizer, classifier, transport,
replay, provenance, and naming rows.

First use \autoref{thm:cauchy-witness-ledger-source-sibling-readback}. It
orders the local route through $\CauchyDiagonalBudgetUp$,
$\RealObservationBudgetUp$, $\CauchySealBudgetSynchronizerUp$, and
$\RealCompletionSealClassifierUp$ before the classifier row $K$ is consumed.

Next attach the CauchyLimitSeal root L10 sibling route
\autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-root-l10-sibling-route} and its non-escape
boundary \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-no-unbudgeted-limit}. Those results
force the completion-facing seal route through the displayed
$\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, and $\RealUp$ sibling
surface before any public limit-facing read is admitted.

Combining the two routes places the witness ledger inside the same
Real-and-Completion dependency lattice as the seal route. The structural rows
$H,C,P,N$ only transport, replay, package, and name that finite path; they add
no quotient stream, host equality, selected limit, hidden tail oracle, or
ambient completeness coordinate.
\end{proof}
