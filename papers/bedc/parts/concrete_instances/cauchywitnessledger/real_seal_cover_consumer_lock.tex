\begin{theorem}[Cauchy witness ledger Real seal cover consumer lock]
\label{thm:cauchy-witness-ledger-real-seal-cover-consumer-lock}
Every $\RealUp$ completion-facing consumer that reads a $\CauchyWitnessLedgerUp$ packet over a finite cover must pass through the displayed diagonal-budget row $Q$, observation-budget row $B$, seal synchronizer row $S$, completion-seal classifier row $K$, and the support rows $H,C,P,N$. Cover and tail refinements are delegated to the $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, and $\RealUp$ handoff surface together with $\RegularCauchyTailFusionUp$; the ledger exports no quotient stream, host equality, selected external limit, countable-choice row, ambient completeness principle, or second seal coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with budget factorization and the Real completion handoff. By \autoref{thm:cauchy-witness-ledger-budget-factorization} and \autoref{thm:cauchy-witness-ledger-real-completion-handoff}, any completion-facing read reaches $K$ only through the ordered route $Q \longmapsto B \longmapsto S \longmapsto K$ with support from $H,C,P,N$.

Use route determinacy and ledger exhaustion next. \autoref{thm:cauchy-witness-ledger-route-determinacy} fixes the completion route once the displayed rows agree, and \autoref{thm:cauchy-witness-ledger-ledger-exhaustion} leaves no public coordinate outside $Q,B,S,K,H,C,P,N$.

For finite-cover and tail refinements, cite the L10 consumer route through \autoref{thm:cauchy-witness-ledger-completion-consumer-factorization} and \autoref{thm:cauchy-witness-ledger-root-streamname-pullback}. These results delegate the consumer-side refinement to the $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, and $\RealUp$ handoff route.

Finally apply the tail-fusion surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regularcauchytailfusion-namecert}. The $\RegularCauchyTailFusionUp$ route supplies the finite cofinal tail consumer without adding quotient streams, host equality, selected external limits, countable choice, ambient completeness, or a second seal coordinate to the witness ledger.
\end{proof}
