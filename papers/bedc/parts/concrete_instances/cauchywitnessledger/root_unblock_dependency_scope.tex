\begin{theorem}[Cauchy witness ledger completion consumer factorization]
\label{thm:cauchy-witness-ledger-completion-consumer-factorization}
Let $L=(Q,B,S,K,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyWitnessLedgerUp$ packet. Every completion-facing consumer of $L$ factors through the displayed sibling route
$$
\DyadicRatCoreUp \longmapsto \StreamNameUp \longmapsto \RegSeqRatUp \longmapsto \RealUp
$$
and through the internal ledger route
$$
Q \longmapsto B \longmapsto S \longmapsto K.
$$
The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve the same two routes as componentwise transport, displayed continuation, package provenance, and local naming data; no consumer reaches a completion-facing read through a detached dyadic core, a second stream name, an unregistered regular rational readback, or an external Real completion selector.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CauchyWitnessLedgerUp.CauchyWitnessLedgerCarrier\_completion\_consumer\_factorization}
\begin{proof}
Start with \autoref{thm:cauchy-witness-ledger-streamname-regseq-factorization}. It places the $\StreamNameUp$ window source and $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback on the same $Q \longmapsto B$ budget source, with $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ and $\RealUp$ already named in the shared source surface.

Next apply \autoref{thm:cauchy-witness-ledger-real-completion-nonescape}. Completion-facing reads reach $K$ only through $Q \longmapsto B \longmapsto S \longmapsto K$ and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

Finally use \autoref{thm:cauchy-witness-ledger-forbidden-coordinate-exclusion}. Since the carrier has no detached dyadic, stream, regular-rational, or external Real-completion coordinate, the sibling route and the internal ledger route are the only public factorization surfaces available to completion consumers.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Cauchy witness ledger completion consumer strict local obstruction]
\label{cor:cauchy-witness-ledger-completion-consumer-strict-local-obstruction}
Let $L=(Q,B,S,K,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyWitnessLedgerUp$ packet. Any displayed failure, refusal, separation, or countermodel row attached to the completion-consumer surface of $L$ is a strict local obstruction. It blocks only a proposed converse read, detached reuse, second stream-name route, unregistered regular-rational readback, external $\RealUp$ completion selector, or bridge-shaped completion request that tries to bypass the displayed sibling route
$$
\DyadicRatCoreUp \longmapsto \StreamNameUp \longmapsto \RegSeqRatUp \longmapsto \RealUp
$$
and the internal ledger route
$$
Q \longmapsto B \longmapsto S \longmapsto K.
$$
Every admitted completion-facing consumer still factors through those two routes together with the structural rows $H,C,P,N$. A request using an unlisted coordinate cannot be reinterpreted as a global impossibility theorem for $\RealUp$ completion, as a host theorem about Real completeness, or as hidden validation by an external completion principle.
\end{corollary}
\begin{proof}
Begin with \autoref{thm:cauchy-witness-ledger-completion-consumer-factorization}. It states that every completion-facing consumer of $L$ factors through the sibling route from $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ to $\RealUp$ and through the internal route $Q \longmapsto B \longmapsto S \longmapsto K$. The same theorem also fixes the role of $H,C,P,N$: they preserve those routes as componentwise transport, displayed continuation, package provenance, and local naming data, and they do not create a detached dyadic core, a second stream name, an unregistered regular-rational readback, or an external Real completion selector.

Now apply \autoref{thm:cauchy-witness-ledger-root-real-completion-nonescape}. The terminal pullback exports no quotient stream, selected limit, host equality, choice row, ambient completeness principle, metric-completion datum, or standard bridge row. Its public data are exactly the four-object sibling route, the ledger route $Q \longmapsto B \longmapsto S \longmapsto K$, and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

These two statements leave only two consumer-facing cases. In the admitted case, the consumer factors through the displayed routes and the structural rows by completion-consumer factorization. In the refused case, the attempted coordinate is one of the unlisted completion, quotient, host, choice, bridge, or detached readback coordinates excluded by root completion nonescape. Such a coordinate has no exported carrier row, no structural replay through $H,C,P,N$, and no terminal pullback datum from which a host theorem or hidden external validation route could be read. Hence the displayed obstruction is strict and local: it rejects only the named converse or reuse attempt outside the ledger packet, while leaving the admitted completion-consumer factorization intact.
\end{proof}
