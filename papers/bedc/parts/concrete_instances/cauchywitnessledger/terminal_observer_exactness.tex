\section{Cauchy witness ledger terminal observer exactness}
\label{sec:cauchy-witness-ledger-terminal-observer-exactness}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy witness ledger terminal observer exactness]
\label{thm:cauchy-witness-ledger-terminal-observer-exactness}
Let $L=(Q,B,S,K,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyWitnessLedgerUp$ packet. Any observer that reads the same $\StreamNameUp$ window face, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger face, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback face, $\RealUp$ seal face, and displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support rows determines the same terminal Real-completion observer surface.

Consequently, the observer has no residual quotient stream, selected limit, host sequence equality, ambient Real completeness, metric-completeness datum, countable-choice row, second completion route, or standard bridge row to read after the four faces and support rows have been fixed.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CauchyWitnessLedgerUp.CauchyWitnessLedgerCarrier\_terminal\_observer\_exactness}
\begin{proof}
Begin with \autoref{thm:cauchy-witness-ledger-selector-seal-terminality}. It places every Phase Real and Completion consumer of $L$ on the selector-seal route ending at $\RealUp$, with only the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows as support.

Next use \autoref{thm:cauchy-witness-ledger-open-phase-budget-route-handoff} and \autoref{thm:cauchy-witness-ledger-budget-selector-four-face-exhaustion}. The handoff supplies the shared dyadic budget, named stream window, regular-rational readback, and Real seal coordinates, while the selector exhaustion shows that an observation-budget mediator exports the same four terminal faces and no off-route completion coordinate.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:streamname-open-phase-four-face-terminality}. Once two observers agree on the $\StreamNameUp$ window face, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger face, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback face, $\RealUp$ seal face, and the displayed support rows, the open Phase Real and Completion surface is terminal. The exclusions in the cited Cauchy ledger route and StreamName terminality theorem leave no quotient stream, selected limit, host sequence equality, ambient Real completeness, metric-completeness datum, countable-choice row, second completion route, or standard bridge row outside that surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy witness ledger observer-filter budget-selector lock]
\label{thm:cauchy-witness-ledger-observer-filter-budget-selector-lock}
Let $L=(Q,B,S,K,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyWitnessLedgerUp$ packet, and let $G=(F,I,S_{\mathrm{obs}},O,B_{\mathrm{obs}},W,D,R,E,H_{\mathrm{obs}},C_{\mathrm{obs}},P_{\mathrm{obs}},N_{\mathrm{obs}})$ be an accepted $\ObserverFilterBudgetSelectorUp$ packet. Suppose the selected $\StreamNameUp$ window family $W$ of $G$ agrees with the terminal $\StreamNameUp$ window face of $L$, together with the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support rows. Then every Real-completion observer whose finite observations are selected by $G$ factors through the single route
$$
\ObserverHistoryIdentityUp \to
\ObserverFilterUp \to
\ObserverFilterBudgetSelectorUp \to
\StreamNameUp \to
\DyadicRatCoreUp \to
\RegSeqRatUp \to
\RealUp.
$$
The omitted rows of $G$ and every off-route completion datum remain outside this factorization.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CauchyWitnessLedgerUp.CauchyWitnessLedgerCarrier\_observer\_filter\_budget\_selector\_lock}
\begin{proof}
Begin with \autoref{thm:cauchy-witness-ledger-terminal-observer-exactness}. The agreement hypothesis identifies the terminal Cauchy ledger window face with the selected $\StreamNameUp$ window family exported by $G$, and it fixes the same support rows for the dyadic ledger, regular-rational readback, and Real seal.

Next apply \autoref{thm:observer-filter-budget-selector-streamname-handoff}. It forces every finite observation consumed by $G$ through the observer-history identity row, the observer filter, the selected row, the finite budget selector, and then the $\StreamNameUp$ window family before any dyadic, regular-rational, or Real-completion row is exposed.

The NameCert surface of \autoref{thm:observer-filter-budget-selector-namecert-surface} supplies the boundary check for the omitted ledger. Its coordinates place omitted rows only in the boundary and support rows, while $W,D,R,E$ are the sole observation and completion coordinates available on the selected route.

Coordinate exhaustion now gives the claimed lock. Since the Cauchy terminal observer has no residual quotient stream, host real equality, selected limit, countable-choice row, ambient completeness, second completion route, metric-completion datum, or standard bridge row to read, and since the observer-filter budget selector has no coordinate that can turn an omitted row into $W,D,R$, or $E$, every Real-completion observer selected by $G$ factors through the displayed route and nowhere else.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy witness ledger observer-filter omitted-row boundary]
\label{thm:cauchy-witness-ledger-observer-filter-omitted-row-boundary}
Let $L=(Q,B,S,K,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyWitnessLedgerUp$ packet, and let
$$
G=(F,I,S_{\mathrm{obs}},O,B_{\mathrm{obs}},W,D,R,E,H_{\mathrm{obs}},C_{\mathrm{obs}},P_{\mathrm{obs}},N_{\mathrm{obs}})
$$
be an accepted $\ObserverFilterBudgetSelectorUp$ packet. Suppose $G$ selects the same $\StreamNameUp$ window family, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger row, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row, and $\RealUp$ seal row as the terminal Cauchy ledger route of $L$, with the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support rows identified. Then every Real-and-Completion observer factors through the selected route
$$
\ObserverHistoryIdentityUp \to
\ObserverFilterUp \to
\ObserverFilterBudgetSelectorUp \to
\StreamNameUp \to
\DyadicRatCoreUp \to
\RegSeqRatUp \to
\RealUp,
$$
and the omitted ledger $O$ of $G$ remains boundary-only data. No omitted row can be repacked as a $\StreamNameUp$ window, dyadic ledger, $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff, $\RealUp$ seal, quotient stream, selected limit, host equality, choice row, ambient completeness principle, or second completion route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First use terminal observer exactness, \autoref{thm:cauchy-witness-ledger-terminal-observer-exactness}. The shared terminal faces force every Real-and-Completion observer of $L$ onto the displayed $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ surface, with no residual quotient stream, selected limit, host equality, choice row, ambient completeness principle, metric-completion datum, or second completion route.

Next apply the observer-filter budget-selector lock, \autoref{thm:cauchy-witness-ledger-observer-filter-budget-selector-lock}. The agreement with $G$ places the same observer on the route through $\ObserverHistoryIdentityUp$, $\ObserverFilterUp$, $\ObserverFilterBudgetSelectorUp$, and then the selected $W,D,R,E$ coordinates.

Finally invoke omitted-row exclusion for the observer-filter budget selector, \autoref{thm:observer-filter-budget-selector-omitted-row-exclusion}. It keeps $O$ outside the selected StreamName window, dyadic ledger, regular-rational readback, and Real seal coordinates. Combining this boundary exclusion with the terminal Cauchy exhaustion leaves no carrier position where an omitted row could be repacked as any of the off-route completion objects listed in the statement.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy witness ledger terminal selector-observer uniqueness]
\label{thm:cauchy-witness-ledger-terminal-selector-observer-uniqueness}
Let $L_{\mathrm{cw}}=(Q,B_{\mathrm{cw}},S,K,H_{\mathrm{cw}},C_{\mathrm{cw}},P_{\mathrm{cw}},N_{\mathrm{cw}})$ be an accepted $\CauchyWitnessLedgerUp$ packet, let
$$
G=(F,I,S_{\mathrm{obs}},O,B_{\mathrm{obs}},W,D,R_{\mathrm{obs}},E_{\mathrm{obs}},H_{\mathrm{obs}},C_{\mathrm{obs}},P_{\mathrm{obs}},N_{\mathrm{obs}})
$$
be an accepted $\ObserverFilterBudgetSelectorUp$ packet, and let
$$
T=(B,W,R,L,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\RealCompletionSelectorSealUp$ packet. Suppose these three packets agree on the selected $\StreamNameUp$ window family, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger face, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback face, the $\RealUp$ seal face, and the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support rows. Then every terminal Real-completion observer selected through the ledger route of $L_{\mathrm{cw}}$ and every terminal Real-completion observer selected through the observer-filter route of $G$ is the same finite selector-observer route through $T$.

The route has no quotient stream coordinate, selected limit object, host equality row, ambient completeness principle, metric-completeness datum, countable-choice row, second completion route, or standard bridge row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply \autoref{thm:cauchy-witness-ledger-terminal-observer-exactness}. The shared $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ faces identify every terminal observer read from $L_{\mathrm{cw}}$ with the displayed Cauchy witness ledger surface and its support rows.

Next apply \autoref{thm:cauchy-witness-ledger-observer-filter-budget-selector-lock}. The observer-filter packet $G$ sends every selected finite observation through the observer-history identity, observer filter, budget selector, selected stream window, dyadic ledger face, regular-rational readback, and Real seal. Thus the observer-filter route reaches the same four terminal faces fixed by the ledger route.

Now use \autoref{thm:real-completion-selector-seal-route-determinacy}. Since the accepted selector-seal packet $T$ displays the same selector budget, selected window, readback row, limit-seal handoff, Real endpoint, and structural rows, any completion consumer exposed by either upstream route determines the same finite Real and Completion handoff route.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:real-completion-selector-seal-nonescape}. Non-escape removes the remaining possible bypasses: quotient streams, selected or detached limits, host equality, ambient completeness, metric-completeness data, countable choice, second completion routes, and standard bridge rows are not carrier coordinates of the shared packet.

Coordinate exhaustion over the shared finite faces therefore leaves only one selector-observer route. It is simultaneously the terminal observer route read from $L_{\mathrm{cw}}$, the selected observer-filter route read from $G$, and the selector-seal route read from $T$.
\end{proof}

Then every terminal selector-observer read exposed by these packets is exhausted by exactly those four shared faces and the displayed support rows.

No quotient stream, selected limit, host equality, ambient completeness principle, metric-completeness datum, countable-choice row, second completion route, standard bridge row, or extra support row remains after the shared $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ inventory is fixed.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:real-completion-selector-seal-nonescape}. Non-escape removes the possible residual rows named in the statement from the accepted selector-seal carrier. The only remaining readable surface is therefore the shared $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ faces with the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support rows.

\begin{corollary}[Cauchy witness ledger terminal observer exactness determinacy]
\label{cor:cauchy-witness-ledger-terminal-observer-exactness-determinacy}
Let $L_{\mathrm{cw}}=(Q,B_{\mathrm{cw}},S,K,H_{\mathrm{cw}},C_{\mathrm{cw}},P_{\mathrm{cw}},N_{\mathrm{cw}})$ be an accepted $\CauchyWitnessLedgerUp$ packet, let
$$
G=(F,I,S_{\mathrm{obs}},O,B_{\mathrm{obs}},W,D,R_{\mathrm{obs}},E_{\mathrm{obs}},H_{\mathrm{obs}},C_{\mathrm{obs}},P_{\mathrm{obs}},N_{\mathrm{obs}})
$$
be an accepted $\ObserverFilterBudgetSelectorUp$ packet, and let
$$
T=(B,W,R,L,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\RealCompletionSelectorSealUp$ packet. Suppose the three packets agree on the selected $\StreamNameUp$ window family, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger face, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback face, the $\RealUp$ seal face, and the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support rows. Then any accepted consumer-facing use of the terminal observer exactness surface has a determined finite read: it must choose the route, normal form, and exactness decision read from the single chain
$$
\CauchyWitnessLedgerUp \to
\ObserverHistoryIdentityUp \to
\ObserverFilterUp \to
\ObserverFilterBudgetSelectorUp \to
\RealCompletionSelectorSealUp \to
\StreamNameUp \to
\DyadicRatCoreUp \to
\RegSeqRatUp \to
\RealUp.
$$
No accepted use can choose an alternate hidden coordinate, omitted-row classifier, duplicate classifier source, quotient stream, selected limit, host equality row, ambient completeness principle, metric-completeness datum, countable-choice row, second completion route, or standard bridge row after those local rows have been fixed.
\end{corollary}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:cauchy-witness-ledger-terminal-observer-exactness}. The shared $\StreamNameUp$ window face, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger face, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback face, $\RealUp$ seal face, and displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows determine one terminal Real-completion observer surface for the accepted Cauchy witness ledger packet. Thus an accepted consumer-facing use of terminal observer exactness has no remaining Real-completion coordinate outside that surface from which to read a different exactness decision.

Now apply \autoref{thm:cauchy-witness-ledger-terminal-selector-observer-uniqueness}. The ledger route, the observer-filter route, and the selector-seal route determine the same finite selector-observer route through $T$. In particular, the observer-filter part of the read passes through $\ObserverHistoryIdentityUp$, $\ObserverFilterUp$, $\ObserverFilterBudgetSelectorUp$, the selected $\StreamNameUp$ window, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger face, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback face, and the $\RealUp$ seal face; the selector-seal part reads the same selector budget, selected window, readback row, seal handoff, Real endpoint, and structural rows.

The two cited results exhaust both possible sources of variation. Exactness fixes the terminal observer surface once the four faces and support rows are fixed, while uniqueness, with the componentwise transport reference \autoref{thm:cauchy-witness-ledger-componentwise-transport}, identifies every selected terminal observer with the same finite route through the displayed packets. Hence the consumer cannot replace the route by an omitted-row classifier, a second NameCert source, or any of the excluded completion coordinates named in the statement. The route, its normal form as a finite read, and the exactness decision exposed to the consumer are therefore determined by the displayed local rows alone.
\end{proof}
