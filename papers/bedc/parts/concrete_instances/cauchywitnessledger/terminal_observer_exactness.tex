\section{Cauchy witness ledger terminal observer exactness}
\label{sec:cauchy-witness-ledger-terminal-observer-exactness}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy witness ledger terminal observer exactness]
\label{thm:cauchy-witness-ledger-terminal-observer-exactness}
Let $L=(Q,B,S,K,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyWitnessLedgerUp$ packet. Any observer that reads the same $\StreamNameUp$ window face, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger face, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback face, $\RealUp$ seal face, and displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support rows determines the same terminal Real-completion observer surface.

Consequently, the observer has no residual quotient stream, selected limit, host sequence equality, ambient Real completeness, metric-completeness datum, countable-choice row, second completion route, or standard bridge row to read after the four faces and support rows have been fixed.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with \autoref{thm:cauchy-witness-ledger-selector-seal-terminality}. It places every Phase Real and Completion consumer of $L$ on the selector-seal route ending at $\RealUp$, with only the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows as support.

Next use \autoref{thm:cauchy-witness-ledger-open-phase-budget-route-handoff} and \autoref{thm:cauchy-witness-ledger-budget-selector-four-face-exhaustion}. The handoff supplies the shared dyadic budget, named stream window, regular-rational readback, and Real seal coordinates, while the selector exhaustion shows that an observation-budget mediator exports the same four terminal faces and no off-route completion coordinate.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:streamname-open-phase-four-face-terminality}. Once two observers agree on the $\StreamNameUp$ window face, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger face, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback face, $\RealUp$ seal face, and the displayed support rows, the open Phase Real and Completion surface is terminal. The exclusions in the cited Cauchy ledger route and StreamName terminality theorem leave no quotient stream, selected limit, host sequence equality, ambient Real completeness, metric-completeness datum, countable-choice row, second completion route, or standard bridge row outside that surface.
\end{proof}
