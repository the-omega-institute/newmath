\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CauchyModulusMajorantUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-modulus-majorant-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges on explicit moduli in constructive analysis, the $\CauchyModulusMajorantUp$ packet records the finite certificate that turns an arbitrary displayed Cauchy threshold schedule into a monotone majorant before completion consumers inspect the seal. It sits after the modulus rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchymodulus-namecert}, reads dyadic tolerances from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, reads finite windows from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, supplies regular rational schedules for \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and keeps the terminal real read public only through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}.

\begin{definition}[Cauchy modulus majorant carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-modulus-majorant-carrier}
A $\CauchyModulusMajorantUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
M=(A,D,S,R,E,G,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ is the incoming $\CauchyModulusUp$ threshold schedule;
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger used to compare requested precisions and majorant bounds;
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row naming which threshold windows may be inspected;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row consumed by regular rational sequence certificates;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal reached only after the finite schedule and dyadic comparison rows have been displayed;
\item $G$ is the monotone majorant graph row, recording a displayed bound for every inspected threshold window and recording monotonicity as finite comparison data;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the incoming modulus, dyadic ledger, stream windows, regular readback, real seal, majorant graph, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay through the same rows in the order seen by consumers;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyModulusUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyModulusMajorantUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for countable choice, hidden convergence witnesses, host-level supremum selection, or a real-number read that bypasses the displayed majorant route.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy modulus majorant NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-modulus-majorant-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CauchyModulusMajorantUp$ carrier $M=(A,D,S,R,E,G,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyModulusMajorantUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission, incoming modulus exposure through $A$, dyadic tolerance exposure through $D$, finite-window exposure through $S$, regular readback through $R$, real sealing through $E$, majorant graph exposure through $G$, componentwise transport through $H$, replay through $C$, provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-modulus-majorant-carrier}. The tuple lists exactly the incoming modulus schedule, dyadic tolerance ledger, stream windows, regular readback, real seal, majorant graph, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

The majorant obligation is displayed by $G$. It records a finite bound for each inspected threshold window and records monotonicity as comparison data against the dyadic ledger $D$.

The consumer route is then forced by $C$. A RegSeqRat or Real consumer reads $S,D,G,R$ before reaching $E$, and $H$ transports only the displayed coordinates componentwise.

Since no coordinate for countable choice, hidden convergence witnesses, host supremum selection, or bypassed real reads appears in the carrier, the listed rows exhaust the NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy modulus majorant normalization route]
\label{thm:cauchy-modulus-majorant-normalization-route}
Let $M=(A,D,S,R,E,G,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyModulusMajorantUp$ packet. Every consumer that replaces an arbitrary Cauchy threshold by a monotone schedule must route the replacement through the displayed graph row $G$ after reading the finite windows $S$ and the dyadic tolerance ledger $D$, and before exposing the $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $R$ or the terminal $\RealUp$ seal $E$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the carrier definition. The only row that records the monotone majorant is $G$, while $S$ supplies the inspected windows and $D$ supplies the tolerance comparisons.

The replay row $C$ orders the consumer route through $S,D,G,R,E$. Thus a regular rational sequence consumer sees the majorant schedule before it receives the readback row $R$, and a real-number consumer sees the same route before the seal $E$ is opened.

The transport row $H$ is componentwise, so it cannot replace the displayed graph by a host-level monotone choice. The provenance row $P$ records the same dependencies, and $N$ names the local certificate.
\end{proof}
