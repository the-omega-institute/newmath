The $\ObserverAccumulationClockUp$ packet makes the time-as-accumulation reading of \autoref{ch:visions-consciousness-universe-observer-in-bedc} into a finite NameCert carrier. It reads its state side through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-consciousobserverstate-namecert}, its observer-history identity side through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observerhistoryidentity-namecert}, and its order side through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observation-time-order-namecert}. Unlike the state, identity, locality, order, or frame siblings, the packet stores a named transition witness that produces the next public clock tick only when the accumulated-record edge is displayed.

\begin{definition}[ObserverAccumulationClock carrier]
\label{def:observer-accumulation-clock-carrier}
An $\ObserverAccumulationClockUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
C=(S_0,S_1,A,G,H,R,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S_0$ is the present observer-state row, read through the $\ConsciousObserverStateUp$ surface;
\item $S_1$ is the successor observer-state row, carrying the records visible after the next accepted accumulation step;
\item $A$ is the accumulation-edge row, naming the retained-record inclusion from $S_0$ to $S_1$ and producing the public tick;
\item $G$ is the $\Gap$ residue row for demanded tick reads whose accumulation edge, retained-record comparison, or successor state is absent;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for present state, successor state, accumulation edge, gap residue, route, package, and local naming rows;
\item $R$ records displayed $\Cont$ routes by which consumers read $S_0$, then $S_1$, then $A$, and only then the clock-read result;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ConsciousObserverStateUp$, $\ObserverHistoryIdentityUp$, $\ObservationTimeOrderUp$, $\BHist$, $\Gap$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ObserverAccumulationClockUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a host scalar clock, global synchronization map, direct state-to-clock read, reversible tick, or time read detached from the displayed accumulation edge.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[ObserverAccumulationClock NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:observer-accumulation-clock-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:observer-accumulation-clock-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\ObserverAccumulationClockUp}$ surface consists exactly of five obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the finite packet $C=(S_0,S_1,A,G,H,R,P,N)$;
\item successor-record exposure, requiring $S_1$ to be read as a successor observer-state row of the same observer-history identity surface as $S_0$;
\item accumulation-edge exactness, requiring $A$ to witness retained-record accumulation from $S_0$ to $S_1$ before any tick can be exported;
\item $\Gap$ residue exactness, requiring $G$ to classify any demanded tick lacking $S_1$ or $A$ as outside the accepted clock surface;
\item stability and non-escape under the componentwise $\hsame$ row $H$, displayed route row $R$, package provenance $P$, and local naming row $N$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:observer-accumulation-clock-carrier}. The displayed packet exposes the two observer-state rows, the accumulation edge, the gap residue, transport, route, provenance, and local name.

The successor-record clause uses the conscious-state and observer-history identity siblings. The row $S_1$ is not a free clock value; it is another observer-state row on the same identity surface as $S_0$.

The accumulation-edge clause then reads $A$. By the observation-time-order sibling, a time comparison is record retention between displayed states, so a public tick is obtained only after the route has exposed the retained-record edge from $S_0$ to $S_1$.

The gap-residue row $G$ handles failed tick demands. If a consumer asks for a tick without a successor state or without the accumulation edge, the request remains a residue rather than an accepted clock read.

Finally $H,R,P,N$ transport, replay, package, and name the same finite packet. Since no displayed row stores a host scalar clock, direct state-to-clock function, global synchronization map, or reversible tick, the local certificate exports none.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ObserverAccumulationClock falsifiable boundary]
\label{thm:observer-accumulation-clock-falsifiable-boundary}
Every accepted $\ObserverAccumulationClockUp$ consumer that claims a time tick factors through the displayed route
$$
S_0 \to S_1 \to A
$$
and then through the transport, package, and local naming rows $H,R,P,N$. A direct read from a single observer-state row to a clock value, or a tick read that bypasses the accumulation-edge row $A$, is a counter-witness to the accepted packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier rows. The only two state coordinates are $S_0$ and $S_1$, and the only row that relates them as a tick-producing transition is $A$.

The displayed route row $R$ fixes the consumer order: read the present state, read the successor state, then read the accumulation edge. The transport row $H$ preserves those coordinates componentwise, while $P$ and $N$ bind the route to the local package and name.

Thus a claimed clock read that starts and ends at a single state has no carrier coordinate that can justify it. Likewise a read from $S_0$ to $S_1$ that omits $A$ has not displayed the accumulation witness required by the packet.
\end{proof}
