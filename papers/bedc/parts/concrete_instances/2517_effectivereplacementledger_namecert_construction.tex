\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\EffectiveReplacementLedgerUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-effective-replacement-ledger-namecert}
\origin{ai}

The $\EffectiveReplacementLedgerUp$ interface records the finite certificate packet that realizes the three replacement directions of \autoref{sec:three-axioms-replacements}. It gathers explicit choice witnesses, classifier-respecting quotient transport, direction-indexed proposition transport, and the axiom-purity refusal row into one local $\NameCert$ surface, so later purity and boundary arguments can consume a concrete ledger rather than a schematic discipline statement.

When a replacement row is used as a finite refusal witness, $\EffectiveReplacementLedgerUp$ consumes the bounded finite-refutation boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-boundedsearchrefutation-namecert}. When a proposed replacement would become a truth-total or validation-as-row query, it consumes the reflection-limit boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-reflectionlimitboundary-namecert}. These sibling links are dependency rows only; they add no proof search, truth principle, quotient principle, choice principle, or propext principle beyond the displayed ledger.

\begin{definition}[EffectiveReplacementLedger carrier]
\label{def:effective-replacement-ledger-carrier}
An $\EffectiveReplacementLedgerUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
E=(W,Q,T,A,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $W$ is the choice-witness row, recording countable witness functions, dependent-step witnesses along displayed $\Cont$ chains, and finite-recursion witnesses over closed inductive lists;
\item $Q$ is the quotient-transport row, recording classifier-respecting transport through typed $\NameCert$ data without choosing a canonical representative;
\item $T$ is the propext-transport row, recording bidirectional implication as direction-indexed $\hsame$ and $\psame$ transport rather than as a proposition-equality token;
\item $A$ is the axiom-purity refusal row, recording that no consumer may request $\mathsf{Classical.choice}$, $\mathsf{Quot.sound}$, $\mathsf{propext}$, or a host rewrite through those operations;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ and $\psame$ transports for the witness, quotient, proposition, refusal, provenance, and naming rows;
\item $C$ stores the finite $\Cont$ routes by which consumers read each replacement row and receive only its displayed output;
\item $P$ records $\Pkg$ provenance over the ThreeAxiomsOneClosure schema, typed $\NameCert$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\psame$, $\Cont$, and the axiom-purity audit surface;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\EffectiveReplacementLedgerUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no hidden coordinate for an unwitnessed selector, a quotient representative section, a proposition equality $P=Q$, or an ignored axiom-purity gate.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[EffectiveReplacementLedger choice row]
\label{thm:effective-replacement-ledger-choice-row}
For every accepted carrier $E=(W,Q,T,A,H,C,P,N)$, the choice replacement surface is exactly the witness row $W$ together with the continuation routes in $C$ that expose its entries. Countable choice is read as an explicit witness function over a displayed $\mathbb{N}$-indexed family, dependent choice is read as displayed step data along a $\Cont$ chain, and finite choice is read as primitive recursion over a closed inductive list.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier to $W$ and $C$. The witness row names the three admitted forms of choice replacement, and the continuation row gives the only routes by which a consumer may read them.

Each route returns a displayed witness: a function for the countable case, a step witness for the dependent case, or a finite recursive witness for the closed-list case. None of these reads supplies an oracle body outside the carrier.

The refusal row $A$ blocks any request for an unwitnessed selector. Therefore the choice surface of the packet is exhausted by $W$ and its displayed routes in $C$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[EffectiveReplacementLedger quotient transport row]
\label{thm:effective-replacement-ledger-quot-transport-row}
For every accepted carrier $E=(W,Q,T,A,H,C,P,N)$, the quotient replacement surface is exactly the classifier-respecting transport row $Q$ together with the $\hsame$ and $\psame$ transport data in $H$. A consumer may move certified content across the displayed classifier, but it may not extract a canonical representative, a quotient section, or a host-side collapse of carrier elements.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read $Q$ as the quotient-facing coordinate of the packet. It names classifier-respecting transport through typed $\NameCert$ data and has no field for a representative-selection function.

The transport row $H$ supplies the componentwise movement required to compare two certified presentations. It moves witness rows, classifier rows, provenance rows, and naming rows through displayed sameness data.

If a consumer asks for a quotient representative or a type-level collapse, the request is not routed through $Q$ or $H$ and is therefore refused by $A$. Thus quotient use remains transport-only inside the finite packet.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.EffectiveReplacementLedgerUp.EffectiveReplacementLedgerQuotTransportRow}

\begin{theorem}[EffectiveReplacementLedger propext transport row]
\label{thm:effective-replacement-ledger-propext-transport-row}
For every accepted carrier $E=(W,Q,T,A,H,C,P,N)$, the proposition-stratum replacement surface is exactly the direction-indexed transport row $T$ together with the continuation routes in $C$ and the componentwise $\hsame$ and $\psame$ rows in $H$. Bidirectional implication may be consumed in a named direction, but the carrier exports no proposition equality and no host rewrite through $P=Q$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the packet to $T$. This row records bidirectional implication as directed transport data, so a consumer must name which direction it reads before using the implication evidence.

The route row $C$ exposes only those directed reads, and the transport row $H$ preserves them componentwise across certified presentations. Together they provide exactly the replacement behavior named in \autoref{def:three-axioms-replacement-propext}.

The refusal row $A$ excludes proposition equality, propositional extensionality, and host rewrite. Since none of these is a coordinate of $E$, they cannot be exported by certificate projection.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.EffectiveReplacementLedgerUp.EffectiveReplacementLedgerPropextTransportRow}

\begin{theorem}[EffectiveReplacementLedger axiom-purity boundary]
\label{thm:effective-replacement-ledger-axiom-purity-boundary}
For every accepted carrier $E=(W,Q,T,A,H,C,P,N)$, the axiom-purity refusal row $A$ is a boundary on the whole packet: any requested read that requires $\mathsf{Classical.choice}$, $\mathsf{Quot.sound}$, $\mathsf{propext}$, an unwitnessed selector, a quotient representative section, or proposition equality is rejected rather than transported through $W$, $Q$, $T$, $H$, or $C$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier inventory. The positive replacement rows are $W$, $Q$, and $T$, while $H$ and $C$ only transport and replay those displayed rows.

The refusal row $A$ is attached to the same package provenance $P$ and local name $N$. It therefore constrains every public read of the packet, not merely one replacement direction.

A request for one of the excluded operations has no coordinate to project and no continuation route to replay. The only available response is the refusal recorded in $A$, so the finite ledger preserves the axiom-purity boundary.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.EffectiveReplacementLedgerUp.EffectiveReplacementLedgerAxiomPurityBoundary}

\begin{theorem}[EffectiveReplacementLedger NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:effective-replacement-ledger-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\EffectiveReplacementLedgerUp}$ surface for \autoref{def:effective-replacement-ledger-carrier} consists of five finite obligations: carrier exposure for $E$, choice-witness exactness for $W$, quotient-transport exactness for $Q$, propext-transport exactness for $T$, and axiom-purity non-escape for $A$ over the shared transport, continuation, provenance, and naming rows $H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier exposure is projection of the displayed tuple $E=(W,Q,T,A,H,C,P,N)$. The packet lists every replacement, transport, continuation, provenance, and naming row as a finite coordinate.

The three exactness obligations are supplied by the row theorems above. \autoref{thm:effective-replacement-ledger-choice-row} binds $W$ to explicit witnesses, \autoref{thm:effective-replacement-ledger-quot-transport-row} binds $Q$ to classifier-respecting transport, and \autoref{thm:effective-replacement-ledger-propext-transport-row} binds $T$ to direction-indexed proposition transport.

The final obligation is the non-escape boundary of \autoref{thm:effective-replacement-ledger-axiom-purity-boundary}. Repacking these rows with $H,C,P,N$ gives one finite NameCert packet whose public surface contains replacement data and refusal data, but no classical axiom dependency.
\end{proof}
