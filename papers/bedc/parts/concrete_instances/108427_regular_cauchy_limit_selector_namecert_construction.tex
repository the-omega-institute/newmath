\begin{definition}[Regular Cauchy limit selector carrier]
\label{def:regular-cauchy-limit-selector-carrier}
A $\RegularCauchyLimitSelectorUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(A,W,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ is the selector schedule row, listing the finite diagonal stages and the requested precision levels.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window row naming the inspected source entries at each stage of $A$.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row that records the dyadic error budget attached to the selected stages.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row that turns the selected stream windows and dyadic tolerances into regular rational observations.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row exposed only after the selector schedule, stream windows, dyadic tolerances, and regular rational readback have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $A,W,D,R,E$ and for the replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a finite limit request through
$$
A,W,D,R \longmapsto E \longmapsto H,P,N .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RegularCauchyLimitSelectorUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyLimitSelectorUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $A,W,D,R,E,H,C,P,N$. The carrier has no coordinate for countable choice, selected host limits, quotient equality of stream names, hidden tail schedules, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, or bridge credentials.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy limit selector NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-limit-selector-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RegularCauchyLimitSelectorUp$ carrier $S=(A,W,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyLimitSelectorUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the selector schedule, $\StreamNameUp$ windows, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, $\RealUp$ seal, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming row. Every Real or Completion consumer of the selector reads the diagonal-limit handoff through these rows before using the representative surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-diagonallimitrepresentative-namecert}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-limit-selector-carrier}. The analytic rows are exactly the selector schedule $A$, finite stream window $W$, dyadic tolerance row $D$, regular rational readback $R$, and terminal seal $E$.

The selector route is finite. A consumer first reads the stages in $A$, then inspects only the windows named by $W$, then checks the dyadic tolerances recorded in $D$ before any regular rational readback is available.

The readback row $R$ is the only route from selected finite windows to the terminal seal $E$. Thus the Real-facing endpoint is reached through $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, and $\RegSeqRatUp$ data before it can be consumed by the diagonal-limit representative boundary.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same rows. Since the carrier contains no coordinate for countable choice, selected host limits, quotient-stream equality, hidden tail schedules, ambient Real completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}
