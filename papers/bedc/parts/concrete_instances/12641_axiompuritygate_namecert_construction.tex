\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\AxiomPurityGateUp$}

The $\AxiomPurityGateUp$ packet realizes the axiom-purity section of \autoref{subsec:philosophy-axiom-purity} inside the auditable-generativity programme of \autoref{ch:visions-philosophy-auditable-generativity}. It sits beside the forbidden-schema obstruction of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metaclosureobstruction-namecert}, the finite choice-output refusal ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-choice-recipe-ledger-namecert}, the quotient-boundary packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-quotientsoundnessboundary-namecert}, and the proposition-transport boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-propexttransportboundary-namecert}. Those siblings expose forbidden schemas, term-stratum choice demand, quotient soundness, and proposition transport; this carrier binds the dependency-audit gate that accepts only finite reports with visible forbidden-dependency refusals.

\begin{definition}[AxiomPurityGate carrier]
\label{def:axiom-purity-gate-carrier}
An $\AxiomPurityGateUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
G=(T,D,F,R,L,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $T$ is the theorem-target row naming the declaration whose dependency report is being consumed;
\item $D$ is the finite dependency-report row, listing the transitive proof dependencies visible to the audit consumer;
\item $F$ is the forbidden-dependency refusal row for $\mathsf{Classical.choice}$, quotient soundness, and propositional extensionality;
\item $R$ is the replay row recording the command-level route by which the dependency report can be re-read;
\item $L$ is the replacement row naming the constructive BEDC route used when a forbidden dependency request is refused;
\item $S$ is the transport row recording that theorem-target, dependency-report, refusal, replay, and replacement reads are consumed componentwise;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $T,D,F,R,L,S,P,N$;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which consumers read the report, refusals, replay route, replacement route, and transport row;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\MetaClosureObstructionUp$, $\UnwitnessedExistenceClosureUp$, $\ChoiceRecipeLedgerUp$, $\QuotientSoundnessBoundaryUp$, $\PropextTransportBoundaryUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\AxiomPurityGateUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only by the displayed rows $T,D,F,R,L,S,H,C,P,N$. A packet missing any forbidden-dependency refusal row in $F$ is not accepted as an $\AxiomPurityGateUp$ carrier.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[AxiomPurityGate NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:axiom-purity-gate-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:axiom-purity-gate-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\AxiomPurityGateUp}$ surface consists of carrier admission for
$$
G=(T,D,F,R,L,S,H,C,P,N),
$$
theorem-target exposure through $T$, dependency-report exposure through $D$, forbidden-dependency refusal through $F$, replay exposure through $R$, constructive replacement exposure through $L$, transport exposure through $S$, componentwise $\hsame$ stability through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, package provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:axiom-purity-gate-carrier}. The tuple displays the theorem target, dependency report, forbidden-dependency refusal, replay route, replacement route, transport row, componentwise stability, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming.

The rows $T,D,F$ are the audit-facing core. Row $T$ names the declaration being read, row $D$ exposes the finite dependency report, and row $F$ records the refusal of each forbidden dependency class rather than treating purity as prose outside the carrier.

The rows $R,L,S$ make the refusal replayable and consumable. Row $R$ records the report replay route, row $L$ records the constructive replacement route, and row $S$ records transport of the visible rows without adding a hidden dependency or host equality.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ exhaust stability, continuation replay, provenance, and naming. Since no further coordinate exists, the listed rows are exactly the local NameCert obligations for the gate packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[AxiomPurityGate non-escape]
\label{thm:axiom-purity-gate-nonescape}
Every accepted $\AxiomPurityGateUp$ packet exports only the finite rows
$$
T,D,F,R,L,S,H,C,P,N.
$$
No consumer can extract from such a packet an unreported theorem dependency, a $\mathsf{Classical.choice}$ dependency, a quotient-soundness dependency, a propositional-extensionality dependency, a hidden replay oracle, a host equality transport, or a proof of meta closure. A packet missing any forbidden-dependency refusal row cannot be accepted as an $\AxiomPurityGateUp$ carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet to the carrier inventory of \autoref{def:axiom-purity-gate-carrier}. The only dependency information available to a consumer is the theorem target $T$, the finite report $D$, and the forbidden-dependency refusal row $F$.

Apply the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:axiom-purity-gate-namecert-obligations}. Every replay, replacement, and transport read must pass through $R,L,S,H,C,P,N$, so a consumer may inspect the displayed audit packet but cannot bypass it to obtain a hidden dependency or host-level equality route.

The last claim is part of carrier admission. Row $F$ contains the refusal records for the forbidden dependency classes; removing one of those records removes a displayed coordinate required by the classifier and therefore removes acceptance as an $\AxiomPurityGateUp$ carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[AxiomPurityGate witness-preserving consumer]
\label{thm:axiom-purity-gate-witness-preserving-consumer}
Every downstream consumer of an accepted $\AxiomPurityGateUp$ packet that asks for a theorem-target audit must read the dependency report, forbidden-dependency refusal, replay route, constructive replacement route, transport row, stability row, continuation route, provenance row, and local naming row through
$$
T,D,F,R,L,S,H,C,P,N.
$$
Such a consumer preserves the displayed witness boundary: accepted theorem reads may cite the finite report and replacement rows, while rejected reads remain blocked by $F$. The consumer cannot turn the gate into a selector for witnesses, a quotient of dependency reports, a proposition-equality transport, a second replay surface, or a meta-closure proof.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First expose the theorem-target audit through $T,D,F$. The dependency report is visible only as the finite row $D$, and each forbidden dependency class is handled by its refusal record in $F$.

Next route replay and replacement through $R,L,S$. The replay row lets the report be re-read, the replacement row records the constructive BEDC route available after refusal, and the transport row keeps those reads componentwise rather than quotienting dependency reports.

Finally apply $H,C,P,N$. These rows preserve stability, continuation replay, package provenance, and the local name of the packet. They do not add a selector, quotient, proposition-equality transport, second replay surface, or meta-closure theorem, so the consumer remains witness-preserving.
\end{proof}
