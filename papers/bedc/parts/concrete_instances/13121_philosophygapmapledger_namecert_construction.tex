\begin{definition}[Philosophy gap-map ledger carrier]
\label{def:philosophy-gap-map-ledger-carrier}
A $\PhilosophyGapMapLedgerUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
G=(E,S,K,T,U,F,A,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $E$ is the gap-entry row, recording the name, BEDC site, requested supply, and claim surface for each gap entry;
\item $S$ is the socket-kind row, recording whether the entry requests theorem supply, bridge supply, empirical supply, reflection supply, or package-audit supply;
\item $K$ is the gate row, recording the local gate that must be passed before the entry may move from reported gap to accepted theorem, bridge, or package row;
\item $T$ is the status row, recording open, blocked, scaffolded, checked, bridged, or refused status without collapsing theory closure into formal verification;
\item $U$ is the upgrade-route row, recording the finite route from the current status to the next admissible BEDC obligation;
\item $F$ is the forbidden-internalization row, recording that the gap may not be exported as a closed theorem, primitive truth predicate, host equality, quotient import, choice principle, or hidden subject-substance reading;
\item $A$ is the audit-route row, recording the philosophy-map target, package carrier, theorem-gap registry, gap-failure bridge audit, and claim registry layer consulted by the export;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for entries, sockets, gates, statuses, upgrade routes, forbidden-internalization rows, audit routes, provenance, and local naming;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay for the same gap-map ledger rows;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\PhilosophyMapTargetUp$, $\BedcPhilosophyPackageUp$, theorem-gap registry rows, $\GapFailureBridgeAuditUp$, $\ClaimRegistryLayerUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\PhilosophyGapMapLedgerUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for prose adjacency, unchecked theorem closure, implicit bridge success, host-level semantic completion, or a status row that erases the forbidden-internalization boundary.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Philosophy gap-map ledger exactness]
\label{thm:philosophy-gap-map-ledger-exactness}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:philosophy-gap-map-ledger-carrier}, every public gap-map export is read from exactly the displayed rows $E,S,K,T,U,F,A,H,C,P,N$. In particular, the entry name, socket kind, gate, status, upgrade route, and forbidden-internalization row are separate finite coordinates and cannot be recovered from prose placement or from a sibling package route alone.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the finite packet $G=(E,S,K,T,U,F,A,H,C,P,N)$. The gap entry, socket kind, gate, status, upgrade route, and forbidden-internalization rows are displayed before the audit route or local name is consumed.

The audit route $A$ may cite the philosophy-map target, package carrier, theorem-gap registry, gap-failure bridge audit, and claim registry layer. Those citations only explain where the displayed ledger is checked; they do not supply a replacement coordinate for any of $E,S,K,T,U,F$.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite packet. Since the carrier contains no prose-only coordinate and no hidden closure coordinate, every public export factors through the listed rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Philosophy gap-map ledger nonescape]
\label{thm:philosophy-gap-map-ledger-nonescape}
Every accepted $\PhilosophyGapMapLedgerUp$ export must pass through the forbidden-internalization row $F$. If $F$ is absent, a gap-map export has no displayed coordinate that refuses the reading of a boundary question as a closed theorem, primitive truth predicate, host equality, quotient import, choice principle, or hidden subject-substance claim.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the carrier of \autoref{def:philosophy-gap-map-ledger-carrier}. The rows $E,S,K,T,U$ identify the gap, socket, gate, status, and next route, but none of them states the internalization refusal.

The audit rows $A,H,C,P,N$ preserve and name only what the carrier already displays. They can route the ledger through sibling packets and replay the same packet for a consumer, but they cannot manufacture a missing refusal row.

Therefore an accepted export that blocks closure illusion must read $F$ explicitly. Without $F$, the packet may still list an open gap, but it does not certify nonescape for the forbidden closed readings.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Philosophy gap-map ledger sibling independence]
\label{thm:philosophy-gap-map-ledger-sibling-independence}
The sibling packets $\PhilosophyMapTargetUp$, $\BedcPhilosophyPackageUp$, theorem-gap registry rows, $\GapFailureBridgeAuditUp$, and $\ClaimRegistryLayerUp$ are dependencies of $\PhilosophyGapMapLedgerUp$ only through displayed routes in $A$ and provenance in $P$. None is bijective with the carrier $G=(E,S,K,T,U,F,A,H,C,P,N)$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier. It stores the gap entry, socket kind, gate, status, upgrade route, forbidden-internalization row, audit route, transport, replay, provenance, and local name together.

Each sibling stores a narrower surface. The philosophy-map target names map obligations, the package carrier stores package rows, the theorem-gap registry stores theorem/gap entries, the gap-failure bridge audit stores typed gap and bridge audit rows, and the claim registry layer stores claim admission and refusal rows.

The ledger may cite those siblings through $A$ and $P$. But no one sibling reconstructs all eleven displayed coordinates, and the gap-map ledger cannot be projected back to any one sibling without losing the forbidden-internalization row or local ledger name.
\end{proof}
