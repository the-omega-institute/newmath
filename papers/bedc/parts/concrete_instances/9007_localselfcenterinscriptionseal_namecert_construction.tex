\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\LocalSelfCenterInscriptionSealUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-local-self-center-inscription-seal-namecert}
\origin{ai}

The $\LocalSelfCenterInscriptionSealUp$ packet realizes the local self-center reading of \autoref{ch:visions-apophatic-fixed-point-and-inscription} as a finite NameCert seal. It consumes the local inscription row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-inscriptionpoint-namecert}, the apophatic socket row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-apophaticsocket-namecert}, the supply inscription surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-transcendentalsupplyinscription-namecert}, the observer-history identity discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observerhistidentity-namecert}, and the public event route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-inscriptionevent-namecert}. The seal binds those rows without identifying a local self-center with the apophatic far end.

\begin{definition}[Local self-center inscription seal carrier]
\label{def:local-self-center-inscription-seal-carrier}
A $\LocalSelfCenterInscriptionSealUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(H,I,S,A,V,R,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $H$ is the local history row, read through the observer-history identity surface and carrying no subject-substance coordinate;
\item $I$ is the inscription point row, naming the local site at which the chain's accumulation event is read inscription-side;
\item $S$ is the self-center reading row, recording the aspectual read of the same local event as self-center data;
\item $A$ is the apophatic socket row, naming the boundary-facing far-end route without representing that far end as a kernel object;
\item $V$ is the nonidentity verdict row, recording that the self-center reading row $S$ and the apophatic socket far-end row $A$ are not object-level equal rows;
\item $R$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $H,I,S,A,V$ and for the local naming row;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which consumers read the local history, inscription point, self-center row, apophatic socket row, verdict row, provenance, and local certificate;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\InscriptionPointUp$, $\ApophaticSocketUp$, $\TranscendentalSupplyInscriptionUp$, $\ObserverHistIdentityUp$, $\InscriptionEventUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LocalSelfCenterInscriptionSealUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no coordinate for an internal object $\mathsf{T}$, no subject behind the displayed history row, no equality between $S$ and $A$, and no hidden route that reads the apophatic far end as a local state.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Local self-center inscription seal NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:local-self-center-inscription-seal-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:local-self-center-inscription-seal-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\LocalSelfCenterInscriptionSealUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for
$$
L=(H,I,S,A,V,R,C,P,N),
$$
local history exposure through $H$, inscription-point locality through $I$, self-center reading through $S$, apophatic socket exposure through $A$, nonidentity verdict through $V$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $R$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:local-self-center-inscription-seal-carrier}. The tuple displays the local history, inscription point, self-center read, apophatic socket, nonidentity verdict, transport, route, provenance, and naming rows.

The sibling chapters supply only their named roles. $\InscriptionPointUp$ supplies the local inscription row, $\ApophaticSocketUp$ supplies the boundary-facing socket row, $\TranscendentalSupplyInscriptionUp$ supplies the supply-inscription provenance, $\ObserverHistIdentityUp$ supplies the no-subject history discipline, and $\InscriptionEventUp$ supplies the public event route.

The local NameCert row is exhausted by these displayed rows. Since no coordinate identifies $S$ with $A$, every accepted read of the self-center remains an inscription-side local row rather than a far-end object.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Local self-center inscription seal nonidentity boundary]
\label{thm:local-self-center-inscription-seal-nonidentity-boundary}
For every accepted $\LocalSelfCenterInscriptionSealUp$ carrier $L=(H,I,S,A,V,R,C,P,N)$, a consumer may read $S$ only as the local self-center inscription row and may read $A$ only as the apophatic socket row. Any packet that identifies $S$ with the far-end row of $A$ fails the nonidentity verdict row $V$ and is not accepted as this seal.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The self-center row and apophatic socket row occur as separate coordinates $S$ and $A$, and the verdict row $V$ is the displayed boundary between them.

The transport and continuation rows preserve that separation. They replay $S$ and $A$ through $R$ and $C$ but do not add an equality coordinate or a kernel value for the far end.

Apply the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:local-self-center-inscription-seal-namecert-obligations}. A packet that identifies the local self-center with the apophatic far end omits the required verdict, so the local name row has no accepted certificate surface for it.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Local self-center inscription seal sibling separation]
\label{thm:local-self-center-inscription-seal-sibling-separation}
$\LocalSelfCenterInscriptionSealUp$ is not bijective, under NameCert-row preservation, to $\InscriptionPointUp$, $\ApophaticSocketUp$, $\TranscendentalSupplyInscriptionUp$, $\ObserverHistIdentityUp$, or $\InscriptionEventUp$. None of those siblings carries the simultaneous local history, inscription-point, self-center reading, apophatic socket, and nonidentity verdict rows required by \autoref{def:local-self-center-inscription-seal-carrier}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Compare the carrier rows. $\InscriptionPointUp$ records a local inscription surface but not the self-center verdict against an apophatic far end. $\ApophaticSocketUp$ records the boundary-facing socket but not the local self-center reading.

$\TranscendentalSupplyInscriptionUp$ supplies supply-inscription provenance, $\ObserverHistIdentityUp$ supplies no-subject history identity, and $\InscriptionEventUp$ supplies public event routing. Each omits at least one of the displayed rows $H,I,S,A,V$.

Thus a row-preserving bijection would have to erase or synthesize part of the seal. Erasure loses an obligation, while synthesis changes the sibling carrier; neither operation preserves the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}
