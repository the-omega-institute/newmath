\begin{definition}[FourierTransform carrier]\label{def:fourier-transform-carrier}
A $\FourierTransformUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
T=(W,K,I,R,V,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $W$ is the transform-sample window row, listing the finite source windows, frequency addresses, and retained sample requests read from the $\FourierUp$ finite-observation surface.
\item $K$ is the kernel/probe row, recording the displayed oscillatory kernel tags, inner-product probes, and source-domain probes used by the retained transform samples.
\item $I$ is the integral-witness row, requiring each retained sample to name its $\IntegralUp$ witness and the finite approximation ledger used by that witness.
\item $R$ is the real-seal row, reached only after the transform-sample window, kernel/probe row, and integral-witness row have been replayed.
\item $V$ is the convolution handoff row, reading $\CauchyConvolutionUp$ only through the finite product-tail packet needed by convolution-facing transform consumers.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for transform windows, kernel/probe rows, integral witnesses, real seals, convolution handoff rows, provenance, and local naming.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a finite transform request through $W,K,I,R,V$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\FourierTransformUp$, $\FourierUp$, $\IntegralUp$, $\InnerProductUp$, $\RealUp$, $\CauchyConvolutionUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\FourierTransformUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through the component rows $W,K,I,R,V,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for equality of host transforms, inversion, Plancherel, convergence closure, selected limiting integrals, or ambient completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[FourierTransform NameCert obligations]\label{thm:fourier-transform-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\FourierTransformUp$ carrier $T=(W,K,I,R,V,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\FourierTransformUp}$ surface consists exactly of transform-sample window admission through $W$, kernel and probe admission through $K$, integral-witness readback through $I$, terminal $\RealUp$ seal admission through $R$, convolution handoff through $V$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:fourier-transform-carrier}. Its public rows are exactly $W,K,I,R,V,H,C,P,N$, so every local naming duty begins with a finite transform-sample window rather than with a host transform.

The analytic source rows are then read in order. The row $K$ exposes the kernel/probe data and inner-product probes, while $I$ attaches each retained sample to an integral witness. The real seal row $R$ is reached only after those rows have been displayed.

The convolution-facing part is confined to $V$. It imports only the finite product-tail handoff already named by $\CauchyConvolutionUp$, and it does not add an inversion theorem, Plancherel identity, convergence closure, or quotient equality of functions.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite packet. Since no carrier coordinate stores a selected ambient transform or a stronger analytic theorem, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FourierTransform convolution handoff]\label{thm:fourier-transform-convolution-handoff}
Every convolution-facing read from an accepted $\FourierTransformUp$ carrier factors through
$$
W,K,I \longmapsto R \longmapsto V \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
The read may consume $\FourierUp$ finite observations, $\IntegralUp$ witnesses, $\InnerProductUp$ probes, $\RealUp$ seals, and the $\CauchyConvolutionUp$ product-tail handoff, but it exports only the finite transform-sample route displayed by the carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the transform sample. The window row $W$ fixes the retained source and frequency addresses, and the kernel/probe row $K$ records the oscillatory kernel tags and inner-product probes used at those addresses.

Attach the integral and real rows next. The integral-witness row $I$ supplies the finite approximation ledger for the retained sample, and the real-seal row $R$ records the terminal real read obtained from that ledger.

Only after this route is displayed can the convolution row $V$ be consumed. Its source is the finite product-tail handoff of $\CauchyConvolutionUp$, so convolution-facing consumers receive a named handoff row rather than a host multiplication theorem or an equality of transforms.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve the same factorization under componentwise transport, replay, provenance, and local naming. Therefore the handoff exports the displayed transform-sample route only, with Plancherel, inversion, convergence closure, quotient function equality, and selected ambient transforms outside the packet.
\end{proof}
