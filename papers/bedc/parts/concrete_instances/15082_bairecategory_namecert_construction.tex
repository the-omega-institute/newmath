\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\BaireCategoryUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-bairecategory-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Baire and Bishop--Bridges constructive analysis, the
$\BaireCategoryUp$ packet records the finite certificate surface for
complete-metric dense-open intersection data. It depends on the prefix
interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bairespace-namecert}, the
limit-witness row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completemetric-namecert},
the distance and ball classifier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert},
the finite schedule of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert},
the regular rational readback of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal real seal
of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The chapter names the
finite BEDC handoff for displayed dense-open rows; it does not assert the full
classical Baire category theorem as a host theorem.

The dense-open schedule is the sibling row consumed by
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compact-baire-namecert} and
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-meagre-set-namecert}: $\CompactBaireUp$
restricts the $\BaireCategoryUp$ row to a compact-metric source, while
$\MeagreSetUp$ hands complementary dense-open rows to the same
$\BaireCategoryUp$ packet.

The same dense-open schedule is consumed through the Baire-space source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bairespace-namecert} and the complete-metric Cauchy-thread row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completemetric-namecert}.

\begin{definition}[BaireCategory carrier]
\label{def:baire-category-carrier}
A $\BaireCategoryUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
Q=(B,M,D,O,R,T,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $B$ is the $\BaireSpaceUp$ prefix packet supplying the finite stream
window through which dense-open requests are named.
\item $M$ is the $\CompleteMetricUp$ row supplying the metric carrier, ball
classifier, displayed Cauchy modulus, and limit-witness route used by the
intersection construction.
\item $D$ is the finite dense-open schedule. Each listed row contains an
open-ball admission datum, a density witness for the displayed predecessor
ball, and the regular-rational tolerance read needed to shrink the next ball.
\item $O$ is the open-row ledger recording the displayed ball inclusions and
the finite meet of the dense-open rows in $D$.
\item $R$ is the refinement row. It records the nested-ball shrinkage,
positive-radius comparisons, and metric-distance witnesses that keep each next
ball inside the previous ball and inside the next dense-open row.
\item $T$ is the Cauchy thread produced by the refinement row. It carries the
finite list of centers, radii, metric comparison rows, and the terminal
$\RegSeqRatUp/\RealUp$ handoff for the displayed limit read.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $B,M,D,O,R,T$ and the
structural rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the prefix schedule, metric
ball reads, dense-open admissions, refinement route, Cauchy thread, and
terminal real-name handoff.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BaireCategoryUp$, $\BaireSpaceUp$,
$\CompleteMetricUp$, $\MetricUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$,
$\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\BaireCategoryUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for countable choice, a host set intersection,
open-cover compactness, ambient completeness, or a completeness theorem for
$\RealUp$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[BaireCategory NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:baire-category-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\BaireCategoryUp$ carrier
$Q=(B,M,D,O,R,T,H,C,P,N)$, the local
$\NameCert_{\BaireCategoryUp}$ surface consists exactly of five finite
obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the Baire-space prefix packet, complete-metric
row, dense-open finite schedule, open-row ledger, refinement row, Cauchy
thread, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item dense-open classifier exposure, requiring every displayed dense-open
row to present an open-ball admission datum and a density witness over the
current predecessor ball;
\item refinement stability, requiring the nested-ball shrinkage, positive
radius comparison, and metric-distance rows to transport componentwise by
$\hsame$ and replay by $\Cont$;
\item Cauchy-thread exactness, requiring the terminal $\RegSeqRatUp/\RealUp$
read to pass through the displayed center list, radius ledger, and
complete-metric limit-witness route in $M$;
\item ledger non-escape, stating that no countable-choice selector, host set
intersection, open-cover compactness theorem, ambient-completeness theorem,
or $\RealUp$ completeness theorem is exported by the packet.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from
\autoref{def:baire-category-carrier}. The tuple lists exactly the prefix,
metric, dense-open, open-ledger, refinement, Cauchy-thread, transport, replay,
provenance, and local naming rows.

The dense-open classifier exposure is read from $D$ and $O$. Each displayed
row in the finite schedule supplies an open-ball admission and a density
witness for the predecessor ball already present in the ledger.

Refinement stability is componentwise. The row $R$ stores the nested-ball
shrinkage, radius comparison, and metric-distance witnesses, while $H$ and $C$
transport and replay those rows without changing the carrier shape.

Cauchy-thread exactness is then forced by $T$ and $M$. The thread lists the
centers and radii generated by the finite refinement route, and the
complete-metric row supplies the displayed limit-witness route consumed by the
$\RegSeqRatUp/\RealUp$ handoff.

No remaining packet coordinate names a selector over all dense-open rows, a
host intersection object, an open-cover compactness principle, ambient
completeness, or completeness of $\RealUp$. The five rows therefore exhaust
the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BaireCategory finite meet density handoff]
\label{thm:baire-category-finite-meet-density-handoff}
Let $Q=(B,M,D,O,R,T,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\BaireCategoryUp$ carrier. The
finite meet of the dense-open rows displayed by $D$ routes through the
complete-metric Cauchy thread $T$: each successive density witness refines the
current displayed ball, the row $R$ records the nested shrinkage and positive
radius comparison, and the thread $T$ presents the terminal regular-rational
and real-name handoff through $M$.

This handoff uses only the listed finite rows. It does not use countable
choice, a host set intersection, open-cover compactness, ambient completeness,
or completeness of $\RealUp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the finite schedule $D$. Its first dense-open row is applied to the
initial displayed ball supplied by the metric and prefix data, producing an
open subball and a density witness recorded in $O$.

For the next listed row, use the predecessor ball supplied by the previous
refinement step. The refinement row $R$ records the new center, the smaller
positive radius, the inclusion into the predecessor ball, and the
metric-distance witnesses showing that the new ball lies inside the next
dense-open row.

Iterating over the displayed finite list yields the thread $T$. The thread is
not a choice sequence: every center, radius, inclusion row, and comparison row
appears in the finite packet, and each step is replayed by the displayed
$\Cont$ data.

The complete-metric row $M$ is then read only along this thread. It supplies
the Cauchy limit-witness route for the displayed centers and radii, after
which the terminal read is handed to the $\RegSeqRatUp$ and $\RealUp$ rows.

Since the construction consumes a finite list of dense-open rows and records
each refinement explicitly, there is no call to countable choice. Since the
carrier exposes only ball, refinement, thread, and handoff rows, it also has no
host intersection object, open-cover compactness principle, ambient
completeness theorem, or $\RealUp$ completeness theorem available for the
handoff.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BaireCategory complete-metric thread extraction]
\label{thm:baire-category-complete-metric-thread-extraction}
Let $Q=(B,M,D,O,R,T,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\BaireCategoryUp$ carrier. The
finite dense-open schedule in $D$, the open ledger $O$, and the refinement row
$R$ determine a displayed nested-ball Cauchy thread $T$. Every terminal
$\RegSeqRatUp/\RealUp$ read from the packet factors through the
$\CompleteMetricUp$ limit-witness route in $M$, the $\BaireSpaceUp$ prefix
packet $B$, and the visible $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and
$\NameCert$ rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier along \autoref{def:baire-category-carrier}. The rows
$B,M,D,O,R,T,H,C,P,N$ are the only available coordinates, so the extraction
must be assembled from the displayed schedule, ledger, refinement data, thread,
transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row.

Apply \autoref{thm:baire-category-finite-meet-density-handoff} to the finite
schedule $D$. At each listed dense-open row, the current predecessor ball is
already present in $O$, and the handoff supplies a successor subball admitted by
the dense-open witness for that row.

Read the positive-radius comparison, nested inclusion, and metric-distance
witnesses from $R$. These rows give the center and radius data for the next
displayed ball and certify that it sits inside the predecessor ball and inside
the next dense-open row.

Assembling these finitely many center and radius rows gives the thread $T$.
The componentwise $\hsame$ data in $H$ and displayed $\Cont$ replay in $C$ keep
the thread stable under the accepted packet identifications, while $P$ and $N$
record that the same data are the named $\BaireCategoryUp$ surface.

Finally, consume $M$ only through this thread. The complete-metric row supplies
the displayed limit-witness route for the nested centers and radii, and the
terminal read is then handed through the recorded $\RegSeqRatUp$ and $\RealUp$
rows. No terminal real-name read bypasses $T$ or appeals to an ambient
completeness theorem.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BaireCategory dense-open thread obligations]
\label{thm:baire-category-dense-open-thread-obligations}
Let $Q=(B,M,D,O,R,T,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\BaireCategoryUp$ carrier. The dense-open thread surface consists of the following obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item dense-open schedule exposure through the finite row $D$;
\item predecessor-ball density witnessing through the open ledger $O$;
\item nested refinement through the refinement row $R$;
\item Cauchy-thread extraction through $T$ using the $\CompleteMetricUp$ row $M$;
\item ledger refusal of countable choice, host set intersection, open-cover compactness, ambient completeness, and completeness of $\RealUp$.
\end{enumerate}
No dense-open thread obligation is stored outside $B,M,D,O,R,T,H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:baire-category-carrier}. The dense-open schedule is visible only in $D$, and the predecessor-ball density witnesses are recorded by the open ledger $O$.

Apply \autoref{thm:baire-category-complete-metric-thread-extraction}. The refinement row $R$ supplies the nested successor balls, and the thread row $T$ is assembled from those finitely displayed refinements before the complete-metric row $M$ is consumed.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve and package the same route. Since the carrier inventory has no coordinate for countable choice, host set intersection, open-cover compactness, ambient completeness, or $\RealUp$ completeness, the thread obligations are exhausted by the displayed finite rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BaireCategory complete-metric refinement scope]
\label{thm:baire-category-complete-metric-refinement-scope}
Let $Q=(B,M,D,O,R,T,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\BaireCategoryUp$ carrier. Every refinement read exported by $Q$ factors through the displayed $\CompleteMetricUp$, $\MetricUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, and $\RealUp$ rows. Concretely, the predecessor ball is read through the metric classifier in $M$, the finite schedule is named by the stream-prefix row $B$, the positive-radius and tolerance entries are read through the regular-rational row of $T$, and the terminal scalar seal is read through the $\RealUp$ handoff recorded by the same thread.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:baire-category-carrier}. The refinement row $R$ is not an independent object; it is tied to the complete-metric row $M$, the finite dense-open schedule $D$, the open ledger $O$, and the thread $T$.

Apply the dense-open thread obligations of \autoref{thm:baire-category-dense-open-thread-obligations}. They force each predecessor-ball request to pass through the displayed open ledger and then through the nested refinement row.

Now use the complete-metric thread extraction theorem. The extracted thread reads the metric carrier and ball classifier from $M$, reads the finite schedule through the prefix packet $B$, and records the regular-rational tolerance and terminal real handoff in $T$.

Thus every refinement read reaches the complete-metric, metric, regular-rational, stream-name, and real rows as displayed carrier rows. There is no separate refinement surface outside those rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BaireCategory dense-open schedule scope]
\label{thm:baire-category-dense-open-schedule-scope}
Let $Q=(B,M,D,O,R,T,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\BaireCategoryUp$ carrier. Finite dense-open schedules are consumed only through the displayed ball-admission rows in $D$ and the predecessor-density rows recorded in $O$. A downstream consumer may replay the schedule through $R$ and $T$, but it cannot consume an unlisted dense-open family, an external intersection object, or a host enumeration of opens.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the finite schedule row $D$. By the carrier definition, each schedule entry contains its own open-ball admission datum and is not a pointer to an unrecorded family of dense-open sets.

The predecessor-density data are held in $O$. Applying the finite meet density handoff shows that each listed dense-open row is used only after the previous displayed ball has been recorded in the open ledger.

The replay through $R$ and $T$ preserves that order. The refinement row records the successor ball and the thread row records the resulting center and radius data, while $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite list.

Since no coordinate stores an external enumeration, a host intersection, or a family of opens outside $D$, the dense-open schedule is consumed exactly through the displayed ball-admission and predecessor-density rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BaireCategory countable-choice boundary]
\label{thm:baire-category-countable-choice-boundary}
The $\BaireCategoryUp$ packet exports no countable-choice selector, host intersection, open-cover compactness theorem, or $\RealUp$ completeness theorem. Every accepted consumer read is confined to the carrier rows of \autoref{def:baire-category-carrier}, the five NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:baire-category-namecert-obligations}, the dense-open handoff of \autoref{thm:baire-category-finite-meet-density-handoff}, and the complete-metric thread extraction of \autoref{thm:baire-category-complete-metric-thread-extraction}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the carrier inventory. The rows $B,M,D,O,R,T,H,C,P,N$ contain a prefix packet, complete-metric row, finite schedule, open ledger, refinement row, Cauchy thread, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row.

The NameCert obligations include ledger non-escape. That row explicitly refuses countable-choice selectors, host set intersections, open-cover compactness, ambient completeness, and completeness of $\RealUp$ as exported coordinates.

The finite meet density handoff and complete-metric thread extraction describe the accepted route that remains: finite dense-open rows refine displayed predecessor balls, assemble a finite Cauchy thread, and hand terminal reads through the regular-rational and real-name surface.

Therefore a consumer asking for a selector, host intersection, open-cover compactness theorem, or real completeness theorem asks for data that are not carried by the packet. The boundary is exactly the displayed finite route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BaireCategory countable-choice refusal]
\label{thm:baire-category-countable-choice-refusal}
Let $Q=(B,M,D,O,R,T,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\BaireCategoryUp$ carrier. Any
$\BaireCategoryUp$ consumer that asks for a countable selector, a host
intersection, an open-cover compactness proof, an ambient completeness theorem,
or a $\RealUp$ completeness row lies outside the carrier surface. Accepted
reads are exhausted by $B,M,D,O,R,T,H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier inventory in \autoref{def:baire-category-carrier} lists the entire
accepted surface. Its coordinates are the prefix packet, complete-metric row,
finite dense-open schedule, open ledger, refinement row, Cauchy thread,
transport row, replay row, provenance row, and local naming row.

The ledger non-escape clause in
\autoref{thm:baire-category-namecert-obligations} rules out extra exported
coordinates for countable selectors, host intersections, open-cover compactness,
ambient completeness, and completeness of $\RealUp$. Thus a consumer requesting
one of those objects has left the named packet.

The finite meet density handoff,
\autoref{thm:baire-category-finite-meet-density-handoff}, explains the only
accepted dense-open route: displayed finite rows are refined into nested balls
and then handed to the thread. The extraction theorem
\autoref{thm:baire-category-complete-metric-thread-extraction} identifies that
thread as the sole complete-metric path to the terminal
$\RegSeqRatUp/\RealUp$ read.

Combining the inventory, non-escape clause, finite handoff, and thread
extraction leaves no remaining carrier coordinate through which a countable
choice principle or host-level completeness theorem could be read. Therefore
the accepted reads are exactly the displayed rows
$B,M,D,O,R,T,H,C,P,N$.
\end{proof}
