\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\BaireCategoryUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-bairecategory-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Baire and Bishop--Bridges constructive analysis, the
$\BaireCategoryUp$ packet records the finite certificate surface for
complete-metric dense-open intersection data. It depends on the prefix
interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bairespace-namecert}, the
limit-witness row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completemetric-namecert},
the distance and ball classifier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert},
the finite schedule of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert},
the regular rational readback of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal real seal
of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The chapter names the
finite BEDC handoff for displayed dense-open rows; it does not assert the full
classical Baire category theorem as a host theorem.

The dense-open schedule is the sibling row consumed by
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compact-baire-namecert} and
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-meagre-set-namecert}: $\CompactBaireUp$
restricts the $\BaireCategoryUp$ row to a compact-metric source, while
$\MeagreSetUp$ hands complementary dense-open rows to the same
$\BaireCategoryUp$ packet.

The same dense-open schedule is consumed through the Baire-space source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bairespace-namecert} and the complete-metric Cauchy-thread row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completemetric-namecert}.

\begin{definition}[BaireCategory carrier]
\label{def:baire-category-carrier}
A $\BaireCategoryUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
Q=(B,M,D,O,R,T,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $B$ is the $\BaireSpaceUp$ prefix packet supplying the finite stream
window through which dense-open requests are named.
\item $M$ is the $\CompleteMetricUp$ row supplying the metric carrier, ball
classifier, displayed Cauchy modulus, and limit-witness route used by the
intersection construction.
\item $D$ is the finite dense-open schedule. Each listed row contains an
open-ball admission datum, a density witness for the displayed predecessor
ball, and the regular-rational tolerance read needed to shrink the next ball.
\item $O$ is the open-row ledger recording the displayed ball inclusions and
the finite meet of the dense-open rows in $D$.
\item $R$ is the refinement row. It records the nested-ball shrinkage,
positive-radius comparisons, and metric-distance witnesses that keep each next
ball inside the previous ball and inside the next dense-open row.
\item $T$ is the Cauchy thread produced by the refinement row. It carries the
finite list of centers, radii, metric comparison rows, and the terminal
$\RegSeqRatUp/\RealUp$ handoff for the displayed limit read.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $B,M,D,O,R,T$ and the
structural rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the prefix schedule, metric
ball reads, dense-open admissions, refinement route, Cauchy thread, and
terminal real-name handoff.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BaireCategoryUp$, $\BaireSpaceUp$,
$\CompleteMetricUp$, $\MetricUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$,
$\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\BaireCategoryUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for countable choice, a host set intersection,
open-cover compactness, ambient completeness, or a completeness theorem for
$\RealUp$.
\end{definition}
