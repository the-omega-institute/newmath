Following Stern--Brocot and Farey rational approximation, the $\SternBrocotApproximationUp$ packet records finite rational bracketing as displayed BEDC data. It reads a pair of rational interval endpoints through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rationalinterval-namecert}, a finite mediant path through dyadic tolerance bookkeeping from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, a $\StreamNameUp$ observation window from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, a $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and a terminal $\RealUp$ seal from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet is a finite bracketing neighbor of the decimal and continued-fraction readback surfaces in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-decimalexpansion-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuedfraction-namecert}; it does not store an ambient real order or a quotient-real representative.

\begin{definition}[Stern--Brocot approximation carrier]\label{def:stern-brocot-approximation-carrier}
A $\SternBrocotApproximationUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(L,U,M,T,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $L$ is the lower rational endpoint row, carrying the left side of the displayed $\RationalIntervalUp$ bracket.
\item $U$ is the upper rational endpoint row, carrying the right side of the displayed $\RationalIntervalUp$ bracket.
\item $M$ is the finite mediant-path ledger, whose rows record the left/right Stern--Brocot choices and the rational mediants actually inspected.
\item $T$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row, including the dyadic precision requested by the consumer and the displayed bound showing that the bracket width is below that tolerance.
\item $W$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window used to request and replay the inspected rational rows.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row obtained from the same endpoint and mediant data.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row exposed only after the rational interval, tolerance, stream-window, and regular-rational readback rows have been displayed.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for endpoint, mediant-path, tolerance, stream-window, readback, and seal rows.
\item $C$ stores the $\Cont$ replay from stream windows to mediant inspection, from mediant inspection to rational bracketing, from bracketing to regular readback, and from readback to the real seal.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\SternBrocotApproximationUp$, $\RationalIntervalUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and the local naming row.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\SternBrocotApproximationUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier stores only a finite rational bracketing certificate. It has no coordinate for Dedekind cuts, trichotomy, quotient reals, selected representatives, or RealUp completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Stern--Brocot NameCert obligations]\label{thm:stern-brocot-approximation-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\SternBrocotApproximationUp$ carrier $K=(L,U,M,T,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\SternBrocotApproximationUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the lower endpoint, upper endpoint, mediant-path, dyadic tolerance, stream-window, regular-rational readback, real-seal, transport, continuation, provenance, and naming rows;
\item classifier transport along the stored $\hsame$ rows and displayed $\Cont$ routes;
\item bracketing exactness, stating that every exported rational bracket is one of the endpoint or mediant rows displayed by $L,U,M$ and checked against $T$;
\item readback discipline, stating that every $\RegSeqRatUp$ or $\RealUp$ consumer reads through the finite window $W$ and the regular readback row $R$ before receiving the seal row $E$;
\item ledger exclusion, stating that Dedekind cuts, trichotomy, quotient reals, selected representatives, and RealUp completeness are not exported by the packet.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from the tuple in \autoref{def:stern-brocot-approximation-carrier}. The rational endpoints, mediant path, dyadic tolerance, stream window, regular readback, real seal, transports, continuations, package row, and local naming row are precisely the displayed coordinates.

Classifier transport is componentwise. The endpoint and mediant rows move through their stored $\hsame$ witnesses, while $C$ replays the same window-to-mediant and bracket-to-readback route after transport.

Bracketing exactness uses the sibling interval and dyadic arithmetic surfaces. The endpoint rows are the displayed $\RationalIntervalUp$ bounds from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rationalinterval-namecert}, and the tolerance row is the displayed $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ comparison row from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}.

The readback discipline then follows the stored route through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The excluded principles are absent from the carrier inventory, so the local certificate cannot export them.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Finite rational bracketing]\label{thm:stern-brocot-approximation-finite-rational-bracketing}
Every accepted $\SternBrocotApproximationUp$ packet exposes a finite rational bracket
$$
L \leq M_i \leq U
$$
at each inspected mediant row $M_i$ of the finite ledger $M$, and the final displayed bracket has width bounded by the dyadic tolerance row $T$. Every route to the bracket factors through
$$
\RationalIntervalUp,\ \DyadicRatCoreUp,\ \StreamNameUp .
$$
The packet supplies no host trichotomy, no quotient-real equality test, and no choice of a distinguished representative for the sealed real.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the mediant ledger $M$ in order. Each row records a finite left/right choice and the rational mediant computed from the currently displayed endpoints, so the next bracket is formed by replacing exactly one endpoint with that mediant.

The interval side is supplied by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rationalinterval-namecert}. It records the endpoint rows and the local comparison data needed to keep the displayed lower endpoint below the displayed upper endpoint at the inspected scale.

The tolerance side is supplied by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}. The row $T$ records the dyadic precision and the displayed comparison showing that the final interval width is below that precision.

The window side is supplied by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}. Since the carrier has no other observation coordinate, every bracket read factors through the displayed finite window and the rational rows it exposes.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Stern--Brocot Real seal handoff]\label{thm:stern-brocot-approximation-real-seal-handoff}
Every $\RealUp$ read of an accepted $\SternBrocotApproximationUp$ packet factors through the finite handoff
$$
\SternBrocotApproximationUp,\ \RationalIntervalUp,\ \DyadicRatCoreUp,\ \StreamNameUp,\ \RegSeqRatUp,\ \RealUp .
$$
The Stern--Brocot carrier provides the finite rational bracket; the decimal and continued-fraction siblings of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-decimalexpansion-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuedfraction-namecert} provide comparison readback neighbors; and the real-facing consumer receives only the displayed regular rational row $R$ and seal row $E$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the accepted packet $K=(L,U,M,T,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)$. The carrier exposes the bracket through $L,U,M,T$ and exposes the observation request through $W$.

The regular-rational handoff is the row $R$. By the readback discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, a real-facing consumer reads the finite rational data through the regular sequence surface before reading a real seal.

The seal row $E$ is therefore downstream of $R$ and the displayed window route. The transports $H$, continuations $C$, and provenance row $P$ preserve the same route for classified packets and for repeated finite-window reads.

The comparison with decimal and continued-fraction siblings is only a readback comparison: all three chapters present finite rational observation surfaces before the same $\RegSeqRatUp$ and $\RealUp$ consumers. None supplies Dedekind cuts, trichotomy, quotient reals, selected representatives, or RealUp completeness.
\end{proof}
