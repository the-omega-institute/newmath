\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\TopVecSpaceUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-topvecspace-namecert}

The topological-vector-space interface $\TopVecSpaceUp$ packages a vector-space carrier with a Hausdorff topology making addition and scalar multiplication continuous. It depends on $\NameCert_{\VecSpaceUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-vecspace-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\TopologyUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-topology-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:topvecspace-carrier}

\begin{definition}[TopVecSpace BHist carrier]
\label{def:topvecspace-bhist-carrier}
A $\TopVecSpaceUp$ BHist carrier is a finite displayed packet
$$
(V,\mathcal T,A,S,\lambda,\rho)
$$
whose fields are as follows.
\begin{enumerate}
\item $V$ is the $\VecSpaceUp$ carrier row, including the displayed zero, addition, negation, scalar-source, scalar-action, and vector-space classifier rows.
\item $\mathcal T$ is the $\TopologyUp$ open-neighbourhood row over the same displayed carrier, naming the open rows used to test continuity.
\item $A$ is the finite addition-continuity ledger: each entry records source neighbourhood rows for two vector inputs, the displayed addition endpoint, and the target neighbourhood row.
\item $S$ is the finite scalar-continuity ledger: each entry records a scalar-source row, a vector-source row, the displayed scalar-action endpoint, and the target neighbourhood row.
\item $\lambda$ is the finite $\Cont$ ledger transporting the vector-space, topology, addition-continuity, and scalar-continuity rows.
\item $\rho$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance row naming the $\NameCert_{\VecSpaceUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\TopologyUp}$ dependency certificates.
\end{enumerate}
Two packets classify together exactly when the vector-space row, topology row, continuity ledgers, continuation rows, and package provenance are transported componentwise by the available $\hsame$ rows. The carrier exports no host topological-vector-space equality, ambient topology, or analytic continuity predicate outside the displayed ledgers.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[TopVecSpace vector-space source obligation]
\label{thm:topvecspace-vecspace-source-obligation}
Every accepted $\TopVecSpaceUp$ packet projects its algebraic source exactly to the $\VecSpaceUp$ row $V$ of \autoref{def:topvecspace-bhist-carrier}. The zero, addition, negation, scalar-source, scalar-action, and vector-space classifier rows are consumed through $\NameCert_{\VecSpaceUp}$ and transported only by the carried $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\Pkg$ rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project an accepted packet to its first field $V$. By the carrier definition, $V$ is the only row that exposes vector-space operations and the vector-space classifier.

The addition and scalar-continuity ledgers may refer to those operations, but they do so by naming the rows already present in $V$. Their references are routed through $\lambda$ and the provenance row $\rho$.

Since no host vector space or ambient equality row is available in the packet, the algebraic source obligation is exactly the displayed $\VecSpaceUp$ source row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[TopVecSpace topology source obligation]
\label{thm:topvecspace-topology-source-obligation}
Every accepted $\TopVecSpaceUp$ packet projects its topological source exactly to the $\TopologyUp$ row $\mathcal T$ of \autoref{def:topvecspace-bhist-carrier}. Open-neighbourhood membership, neighbourhood transport, and topological classifier data are consumed through $\NameCert_{\TopologyUp}$ and remain tied to the vector-space carrier only through displayed $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\Pkg$ rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the packet to $\mathcal T$. This row records the open-neighbourhood data used by the continuity ledgers, and it is the only field that consumes the topology certificate.

The vector-space row $V$ supplies the carrier on which those opens are displayed. The connection between $V$ and $\mathcal T$ is not host equality of underlying sets; it is the componentwise transport recorded by $\lambda$ and named by $\rho$.

Thus every topological readback factors through $\NameCert_{\TopologyUp}$ and the finite packet rows. No ambient topology or unlogged neighbourhood filter is exported.
\end{proof}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:topvecspace-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[TopVecSpace continuous addition obligation]
\label{thm:topvecspace-continuous-addition-obligation}
For a $\TopVecSpaceUp$ BHist carrier, every addition-continuity claim is a row of the finite ledger $A$. Each such row reads two vector-source neighbourhoods from $\mathcal T$, the displayed addition endpoint from $V$, and the target neighbourhood row from $\mathcal T$, with the route and provenance recorded by $\lambda$ and $\rho$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read an entry of the addition-continuity ledger $A$. Its input neighbourhoods and target neighbourhood are topology rows, while its operation endpoint is the addition row exposed by $V$.

The comparison data for transported packets are supplied componentwise by $\hsame$. The finite continuation ledger $\lambda$ records how the two input neighbourhoods, the addition endpoint, and the target neighbourhood remain aligned after transport.

Repacking these projections gives the addition-continuity obligation. Because the packet contains no host continuity predicate, no external topological-vector-space equality is used.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[TopVecSpace continuous scalar obligation]
\label{thm:topvecspace-continuous-scalar-obligation}
For a $\TopVecSpaceUp$ BHist carrier, every scalar-continuity claim is a row of the finite ledger $S$. Each such row reads the scalar-source and vector-source coordinates from $V$, the relevant neighbourhood rows from $\mathcal T$, and the scalar-action endpoint from $V$, with transport and provenance recorded by $\lambda$ and $\rho$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read an entry of the scalar-continuity ledger $S$. The scalar coordinate, vector coordinate, and scalar-action endpoint are projected from the $\VecSpaceUp$ row $V$.

Read the neighbourhood coordinates from $\mathcal T$. Their stability under replacement is witnessed by the carried $\hsame$ comparisons and the finite continuation route in $\lambda$.

The provenance row $\rho$ records that the algebraic and topological reads come from the two named dependency certificates. Therefore the scalar-continuity obligation is internal to the displayed packet and does not invoke host topological-vector-space equality.
\end{proof}
