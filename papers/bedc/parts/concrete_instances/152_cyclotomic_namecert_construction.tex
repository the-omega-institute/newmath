\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CyclotomicUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-cyclotomic-namecert}

The cyclotomic-field interface $\CyclotomicUp$ packages the splitting field of $X^n - 1$ over the rational-number certificate. It depends on $\NameCert_{\NumFieldUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-numfield-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\SplittingFieldUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-splittingfield-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:cyclotomic-carrier}

\begin{definition}[Cyclotomic root carrier]
\label{def:cyclotomic-root-carrier}
A $\CyclotomicUp$ carrier is a $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
\mathcal{C}_{\zeta}=(\mathcal{N},n,p,\mathcal{S},\zeta,\alpha,\lambda,\rho)
$$
where $\mathcal{N}$ is a source $\NameCert_{\NumFieldUp}$ row, $n:\mathsf{BHist}$ is a positive unary exponent row, $p:\mathsf{BHist}$ is the rational polynomial ledger for $X^n-1$, $\mathcal{S}$ is a source $\NameCert_{\SplittingFieldUp}$ row over that polynomial ledger, $\zeta:\mathsf{BHist}$ is the chosen primitive-root endpoint, $\alpha$ is the finite $\Cont$ row accepting $\zeta$ as a root and recording the factorization rows inside $\mathcal{S}$, $\lambda$ is the $\hsame$ comparison tying the NumFieldUp source and the SplittingFieldUp base row, and $\rho$ records the $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance of the two source certificates.

The accepted carrier is exactly this displayed history tuple. It carries no quotient of primitive roots and no host equality of fields; every endpoint read is mediated by $\mathsf{BHist}$ rows, $\hsame$ transport, $\Cont$ ledgers, and the paired $\NameCert_{\NumFieldUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\SplittingFieldUp}$ provenance rows.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Cyclotomic rational polynomial classifier]
\label{def:cyclotomic-rational-polynomial-classifier}
The $\CyclotomicUp$ classifier relates two carriers of \autoref{def:cyclotomic-root-carrier} when their $\NameCert_{\NumFieldUp}$ source rows, positive unary exponent rows, rational polynomial ledgers for $X^n-1$, $\NameCert_{\SplittingFieldUp}$ source rows, and primitive-root endpoints are connected by $\hsame$ rows whose endpoints are supported by the displayed $\Cont$ and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance ledgers. The classifier transports the NumFieldUp source, exponent, polynomial row, splitting-field row, and primitive-root endpoint as carried $\mathsf{BHist}$ data, not as equality of host fields or as a quotient of roots.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cyclotomic classifier reflexivity]
\label{thm:cyclotomic-classifier-reflexive}
For every accepted $\CyclotomicUp$ carrier
$$
\mathcal{C}_{\zeta}=(\mathcal{N},n,p,\mathcal{S},\zeta,\alpha,\lambda,\rho)
$$
of \autoref{def:cyclotomic-root-carrier}, the classifier of \autoref{def:cyclotomic-rational-polynomial-classifier} relates $\mathcal{C}_{\zeta}$ to itself. The witnessing row is the componentwise reflexive $\hsame$ support on the $\NameCert_{\NumFieldUp}$ source $\mathcal{N}$, the exponent row $n$, the rational polynomial ledger $p$ for $X^n-1$, the $\NameCert_{\SplittingFieldUp}$ source $\mathcal{S}$, and the primitive-root endpoint $\zeta$, together with the unchanged $\Cont$ row $\alpha$, base comparison row $\lambda$, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance row $\rho$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the displayed carrier. Each carried $\mathsf{BHist}$ component has the reflexive $\hsame$ row at its own endpoint: $\mathcal{N}$, $n$, $p$, $\mathcal{S}$, and $\zeta$ are therefore transported to themselves.

The finite $\Cont$ row $\alpha$ already accepts $\zeta$ as a root and records the factorization rows inside $\mathcal{S}$. The comparison row $\lambda$ already ties the NumFieldUp source to the SplittingFieldUp base row, and $\rho$ is already the provenance package for the two source certificates.

Repacking these five reflexive $\hsame$ rows with the displayed $\Cont$ and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ ledgers gives exactly the classifier row from $\mathcal{C}_{\zeta}$ to $\mathcal{C}_{\zeta}$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cyclotomic classifier symmetry]
\label{thm:cyclotomic-classifier-symmetric}
Let
$$
\mathcal{C}_{\zeta}=(\mathcal{N},n,p,\mathcal{S},\zeta,\alpha,\lambda,\rho)
$$
and
$$
\mathcal{C}'_{\eta}=(\mathcal{N}',n',p',\mathcal{S}',\eta,\alpha',\lambda',\rho')
$$
be accepted $\CyclotomicUp$ carriers. If the classifier of \autoref{def:cyclotomic-rational-polynomial-classifier} relates $\mathcal{C}_{\zeta}$ to $\mathcal{C}'_{\eta}$, then it also relates $\mathcal{C}'_{\eta}$ to $\mathcal{C}_{\zeta}$ by reversing the carried $\hsame$ rows for the NumFieldUp sources, exponent rows, polynomial ledgers, SplittingFieldUp sources, and primitive-root endpoints, while reading the same $\Cont$ acceptance rows and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance rows from the opposite side.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the given classifier row to its five $\hsame$ components. These components compare $\mathcal{N}$ with $\mathcal{N}'$, $n$ with $n'$, $p$ with $p'$, $\mathcal{S}$ with $\mathcal{S}'$, and $\zeta$ with $\eta$.

Reverse each projected $\hsame$ row. The reversed rows compare the same carried histories in the order required for a classifier from $\mathcal{C}'_{\eta}$ to $\mathcal{C}_{\zeta}$.

It remains to account for the ledgers. The acceptance rows $\alpha'$ and $\alpha$ still support the endpoint and factorization reads at their respective sides, while $\lambda'$, $\lambda$, $\rho'$, and $\rho$ provide the same base-comparison and provenance evidence with source and target interchanged. Repacking these rows gives the opposite classifier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cyclotomic classifier transitivity]
\label{thm:cyclotomic-classifier-transitive}
Let
$$
\mathcal{C}_{0}=(\mathcal{N}_{0},n_{0},p_{0},\mathcal{S}_{0},\zeta_{0},\alpha_{0},\lambda_{0},\rho_{0}),\quad
\mathcal{C}_{1}=(\mathcal{N}_{1},n_{1},p_{1},\mathcal{S}_{1},\zeta_{1},\alpha_{1},\lambda_{1},\rho_{1}),\quad
\mathcal{C}_{2}=(\mathcal{N}_{2},n_{2},p_{2},\mathcal{S}_{2},\zeta_{2},\alpha_{2},\lambda_{2},\rho_{2})
$$
be accepted $\CyclotomicUp$ carriers. If the classifier relates $\mathcal{C}_{0}$ to $\mathcal{C}_{1}$ and also relates $\mathcal{C}_{1}$ to $\mathcal{C}_{2}$, then it relates $\mathcal{C}_{0}$ to $\mathcal{C}_{2}$ by composing the $\hsame$ rows componentwise through the shared middle carrier for the NumFieldUp source, exponent, polynomial ledger, SplittingFieldUp source, and primitive-root endpoint, and by repacking the shared $\Cont$ acceptance rows and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the first classifier row to the comparisons from $\mathcal{C}_{0}$ to $\mathcal{C}_{1}$, and project the second classifier row to the comparisons from $\mathcal{C}_{1}$ to $\mathcal{C}_{2}$. The middle carrier supplies the common endpoint for each pair of projected $\hsame$ rows.

Compose the NumFieldUp source comparisons through $\mathcal{N}_{1}$, the exponent comparisons through $n_{1}$, the polynomial-ledger comparisons through $p_{1}$, the SplittingFieldUp source comparisons through $\mathcal{S}_{1}$, and the primitive-endpoint comparisons through $\zeta_{1}$.

The $\Cont$ rows $\alpha_{0}$, $\alpha_{1}$, and $\alpha_{2}$ keep the endpoint and factorization reads attached to the three carriers, and the provenance rows $\rho_{0}$, $\rho_{1}$, and $\rho_{2}$ keep the source packages aligned. Repacking the composed $\hsame$ rows with these ledgers yields the classifier from $\mathcal{C}_{0}$ to $\mathcal{C}_{2}$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cyclotomic source-triad obligation]
\label{thm:cyclotomic-source-triad-obligation}
For every accepted $\CyclotomicUp$ carrier
$$
\mathcal{C}_{\zeta}=(\mathcal{N},n,p,\mathcal{S},\zeta,\alpha,\lambda,\rho)
$$
of \autoref{def:cyclotomic-root-carrier}, the $\NameCert_{\CyclotomicUp}$ source triad is exactly the $\NameCert_{\NumFieldUp}$ row $\mathcal{N}$, the $\NameCert_{\SplittingFieldUp}$ row $\mathcal{S}$ over the rational polynomial ledger $p$ for $X^n-1$, and the primitive-root endpoint row $\zeta$ accepted by $\alpha$. The triad is covered by the displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ rows, transported by the classifier of \autoref{def:cyclotomic-rational-polynomial-classifier}, and closed under the $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ rows $\lambda$, $\alpha$, and $\rho$; no fourth source row is exported by the certificate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier to $\mathcal{N}$, $\mathcal{S}$, and $\zeta$. The exponent row $n$ and polynomial ledger $p$ locate $\mathcal{S}$ at the displayed equation $X^n-1$, while the finite $\Cont$ row $\alpha$ attaches the primitive endpoint to the root and factorization surface.

Use \autoref{thm:cyclotomic-splittingfield-stability} and \autoref{thm:cyclotomic-numfield-embedding-ledger} to transport the NumFieldUp and SplittingFieldUp rows through $\hsame$. Use \autoref{thm:cyclotomic-primitive-root-readback-exactness} for the endpoint component.

Finally repack the three projected source rows with $\lambda$, $\alpha$, and $\rho$. The obligation surface of \autoref{thm:cyclotomic-namecert-obligation-surface} lists no additional source component, so the triad is both covered and complete.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cyclotomic p-adic consumer obligation]
\label{thm:cyclotomic-padic-consumer-obligation}
A $p$-adic consumer of $\NameCert_{\CyclotomicUp}$ may read only the carrier rows $\mathcal{N}$, $n$, $p$, $\mathcal{S}$, $\zeta$, $\alpha$, $\lambda$, and $\rho$ from \autoref{def:cyclotomic-root-carrier}, with $p$ interpreted as the rational polynomial ledger for $X^n-1$ and not as a valuation or completion row. Any valuation, completion, or measure row used downstream must be supplied by a separate $\PadicUp$ or $\IwasawaUp$ certificate and may enter only through its own $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, or $\NameCert$ interface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the displayed cyclotomic carrier. Its rows provide a NumFieldUp source, a positive exponent, the rational polynomial ledger, the SplittingFieldUp source, the primitive endpoint, the acceptance ledger, the base comparison, and the provenance package.

Apply \autoref{thm:cyclotomic-polynomial-ledger-exactness} to identify the polynomial row as the $X^n-1$ ledger and \autoref{thm:cyclotomic-factorization-ledger-exactness} to identify the accepted factorization rows. Apply \autoref{thm:cyclotomic-padic-completion-input-boundary} for the downstream completion boundary.

The carrier and classifier contain no valuation, completion, or measure field. Therefore any $p$-adic consumer starts from the cyclotomic source package alone and must cite the separate certificate that provides the missing analytic rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cyclotomic Iwasawa tower obligation]
\label{thm:cyclotomic-iwasawa-tower-obligation}
An $\IwasawaUp$ tower that consumes $\NameCert_{\CyclotomicUp}$ receives exactly the cyclotomic layer formed by the $\NameCert_{\NumFieldUp}$ row $\mathcal{N}$, the exponent row $n$, the primitive-root endpoint $\zeta$, the splitting-field factorization rows over $p$, the acceptance row $\alpha$, and the provenance row $\rho$. The tower may transport these rows through the carried $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ structure, but $\NameCert_{\CyclotomicUp}$ alone supplies no tower transition map, no $p$-adic measure row, no class-group row, and no host-field equality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier to the source, exponent, polynomial, splitting-field, endpoint, acceptance, comparison, and provenance rows. These are the only rows available before an Iwasawa-specific certificate is attached.

Use \autoref{thm:cyclotomic-iwasawa-consumer-boundary} and \autoref{thm:cyclotomic-iwasawa-source-boundary} to restrict the tower input surface to the projected cyclotomic source rows. Use \autoref{thm:cyclotomic-iwasawa-tower-source-readback} to repack those rows as the cyclotomic layer of the tower.

The excluded tower maps and analytic rows do not occur in the carrier, the classifier, or the obligation surface. They must therefore be supplied by later $\NameCert$ rows rather than inferred from the cyclotomic packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cyclotomic class-field source obligation]
\label{thm:cyclotomic-classfield-source-obligation}
A $\ClassFieldUp$ source interface may cite $\NameCert_{\CyclotomicUp}$ only through the displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ rows $\mathcal{N}$, $n$, $p$, $\mathcal{S}$, and $\zeta$, the $\Cont$ row $\alpha$, the $\hsame$ comparison $\lambda$, and the $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance row $\rho$ of \autoref{def:cyclotomic-root-carrier}. \begin{proof}
Project the carrier rows and read them through the classifier of \autoref{def:cyclotomic-rational-polynomial-classifier}. The only transport available is the carried $\hsame$ support on the source, exponent, polynomial, splitting-field, and endpoint rows, together with the displayed $\Cont$ and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance.

Apply \autoref{thm:cyclotomic-classfield-source-interface}. Its interface is the repacking of the cyclotomic source boundary, the $p$-adic input boundary, and the classifier transport already present in this chapter.

Since the named class-field operations are not entries of the cyclotomic carrier and are not produced by its classifier, the class-field consumer receives a source interface only. Any class-field operation must arrive with a separate $\NameCert$ row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cyclotomic Galois transport obligation]
\label{thm:cyclotomic-galois-transport-obligation}
Every Galois-facing transport read from $\NameCert_{\CyclotomicUp}$ is a transport of the primitive-root endpoint and the splitting-field root and factorization rows along the $\hsame$ support carried by the classifier of \autoref{def:cyclotomic-rational-polynomial-classifier}. \end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the classifier to the source, exponent, polynomial, splitting-field, and endpoint comparisons. These $\hsame$ rows are the only Galois-facing transport data available from the cyclotomic certificate.

Use \autoref{thm:cyclotomic-galois-action-boundary} and \autoref{thm:cyclotomic-galois-action-namecert-boundary} to keep the readback inside endpoint and factorization transport.

The proof closes by inspecting the carrier and classifier fields. Neither contains an automorphism group, a quotient-root representative, or a host-field equality datum, so no downstream Galois consumer can read such data from $\NameCert_{\CyclotomicUp}$ alone.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cyclotomic splitting-field stability]
\label{thm:cyclotomic-splittingfield-stability}
For any two $\CyclotomicUp$ carriers related by the classifier of \autoref{def:cyclotomic-rational-polynomial-classifier}, $\hsame$ transport along the $\NameCert_{\SplittingFieldUp}$ classifier preserves the accepted primitive-root endpoint, the root row for $X^n-1$, and the factorization rows carried by the $\mathsf{BHist}$ splitting-field ledger. The transported certificate keeps the same $\NameCert_{\NumFieldUp}$ source comparison, the same positive exponent comparison, and the same $\Cont$ and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the two cyclotomic packets to their $\NameCert_{\SplittingFieldUp}$ components. The splitting-field classifier supplies the transported root row and the transported factorization rows for the polynomial ledger classified with $X^n-1$.

Use the carrier comparison to identify the exponent row and the rational polynomial row by $\hsame$. The primitive-root endpoint is then read through the same $\Cont$ acceptance ledger, so its root status and the listed factorization rows remain attached to the transported splitting-field package.

The $\NameCert_{\NumFieldUp}$ comparison and the $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance row are projected unchanged from the cyclotomic carrier. Repacking these projected rows gives another accepted $\CyclotomicUp$ carrier with the same public root and factorization evidence.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cyclotomic number-field embedding ledger]
\label{thm:cyclotomic-numfield-embedding-ledger}
When a $\CyclotomicUp$ carrier is read as a number-field extension, its embedding and provenance surface is the $\mathsf{BHist}$ ledger inherited from $\NameCert_{\NumFieldUp}$ together with the $\hsame$ bridge to the $\NameCert_{\SplittingFieldUp}$ base row, the rational polynomial ledger, and the primitive-root endpoint. \end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the NumFieldUp source row in the carrier. Its embedding and provenance ledger is already a public row of $\NameCert_{\NumFieldUp}$, so reading the cyclotomic packet as a number-field extension first projects that ledger.

Next attach the $\hsame$ comparison tying the NumFieldUp row to the base row consumed by $\NameCert_{\SplittingFieldUp}$. The polynomial row and primitive-root endpoint are carried in the same tuple, and the $\Cont$ acceptance row records that the endpoint is read from the splitting-field root surface.

Thus the number-field readback retains both sources of provenance. It does not infer an embedding from an ambient field equality; it projects the displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ rows and the carried $\mathsf{Pkg}$ ledger.
\end{proof}

Exactness is by projection from this list. Every public cyclotomic move uses one of the displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, or $\mathsf{Pkg}$ rows, and no consumer receives a stronger analytic, quotient, or host-field equality principle from $\NameCert_{\CyclotomicUp}$ alone.

By \autoref{thm:cyclotomic-splittingfield-stability}, classifier transport preserves that attachment through $\hsame$ rows rather than through an ambient equality of fields.

For the converse direction, start with the same projected exponent, polynomial, splitting-field, endpoint, and acceptance rows, then reattach the NumFieldUp source comparison and the $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance supplied by the carrier. \autoref{thm:cyclotomic-numfield-embedding-ledger} identifies these as the number-field readback rows, so the repacked certificate has exactly the primitive-root readback advertised by $\NameCert_{\CyclotomicUp}$.

\begin{theorem}[Cyclotomic primitive-root endpoint determinacy]
\label{thm:cyclotomic-primitive-root-endpoint-determinacy}
For any two $\CyclotomicUp$ carriers related by the classifier of \autoref{def:cyclotomic-rational-polynomial-classifier}, the displayed primitive-root endpoints are the same up to the carried $\hsame$ row. The determining data are exactly the positive exponent row, the rational polynomial ledger for $X^n-1$, the $\NameCert_{\SplittingFieldUp}$ source row over that ledger, and the finite $\Cont$ acceptance row that records the endpoint as a primitive root inside the splitting-field factorization surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the classifier to the exponent component and the rational polynomial component. These two $\hsame$ rows identify the displayed $X^n-1$ ledger as a carried history row, so the endpoint is not read from an ambient field but from the same polynomial surface.
