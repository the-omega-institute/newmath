\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\NullSequenceUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-nullsequence-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\NullSequenceUp$ interface records finite evidence that a rational stream has a displayed tail-to-zero schedule. Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, 1985, convergence to zero is treated as carried rate data: each requested precision is answered by a visible tail threshold, dyadic zero-bound rows, regular-rational readback, and the $\RealUp$ zero seal. The packet is consumed by $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\CauchyRegularizationUp$, and $\FastConvergentSeriesUp$ without forming quotient streams, importing host limits, invoking trichotomy, or selecting witnesses by choice.

\begin{definition}[NullSequence carrier]
\label{def:nullsequence-carrier}
A $\NullSequenceUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
\nu=(S,\mu,D,Z,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is a $\RationalStreamUp$ row presented through finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows;
\item $\mu$ is the displayed $\CauchyModulusUp$ tail schedule assigning a threshold to each unary precision request;
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observation ledger used to read rational entries and dyadic radii inside each scheduled tail;
\item $Z$ is the displayed dyadic zero-bound row asserting that every inspected entry beyond the scheduled threshold lies within the requested dyadic radius of zero;
\item $E$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row obtained from the same stream windows, tail thresholds, and dyadic ledgers;
\item $H$ is the $\RealUp$ zero seal row attached to the regular-rational handoff;
\item $C$ is the displayed collection of $\hsame$ transports and $\Cont$ routes carrying schedules, windows, zero-bound rows, handoffs, and seals;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance row naming the $\RationalStreamUp$, $\CauchyModulusUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ source certificates;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\NullSequenceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for quotient stream equality, host real equality, an external limit, a trichotomy decision, or a choice-selected tail witness.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[NullSequence classifier]
\label{def:nullsequence-classifier}
Two $\NullSequenceUp$ carriers are classified together exactly when the displayed tail schedules, tail thresholds, dyadic zero-bound rows, $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff rows, $\RealUp$ zero seal rows, transports, routes, package provenance, and local certificate rows compare componentwise through the recorded $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ data. The classifier never compares hidden stream tails, quotient representatives, host limits, or undecorated rational functions.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[NullSequence zero seed]
\label{thm:nullsequence-zero-seed}
There is an accepted $\NullSequenceUp$ seed carrier whose rational stream row is constantly zero on every displayed $\StreamNameUp$ window, whose tail schedule may answer each precision by the initial threshold, and whose dyadic observation ledger records only zero-bound rows. Its $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff is the constant regular rational name and its $\RealUp$ seal is the displayed zero seal.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Take $S$ to be the displayed constant-zero rational stream and let $\mu$ return the initial threshold for every precision request. Each finite window of $S$ has the same dyadic observation: the value row is zero and the requested dyadic radius contains zero.

Package those observations as $D$ and $Z$. The $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff reads the same constant entry in every inspected window, so the regularity and tail-to-zero rows are witnessed by the displayed zero ledger rather than by any external convergence theorem.

Attach the $\RealUp$ zero seal, the identity $\hsame$ transports, the evident $\Cont$ routes, the package provenance, and the local certificate row. This finite packet is accepted by the carrier definition and contains no hidden limit object.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[NullSequence RegSeqRat tail boundary]
\label{thm:nullsequence-regseqrat-tail-boundary}
For every accepted $\NullSequenceUp$ carrier and every displayed precision request, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ consumer read factors through the stored tail threshold, one finite $\StreamNameUp$ window, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observation ledger, and the dyadic zero-bound row for that request. The handoff exports only these finite rows and the recorded $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ provenance.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:nullsequence-carrier}. The modulus coordinate selects the tail threshold for the requested precision before any regular-rational consumer reads the stream.

The chosen threshold determines the displayed finite window, and the dyadic ledger supplies the rational observation rows in that window. The zero-bound coordinate records that the inspected tail entries are within the requested dyadic radius of zero.

The $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff is therefore exhausted by those rows. Since the route is mediated by the stored transports, continuation routes, package provenance, and local certificate, no quotient stream or host limit is exported to the consumer.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[NullSequence NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:nullsequence-namecert-obligation-surface}
The local $\NameCert_{\NullSequenceUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier habitation, displayed classifier transport, zero-seed habitation, finite tail-to-zero handoff into $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$ zero-seal non-escape, and dependency stability over $\RationalStreamUp$, $\CauchyModulusUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier habitation is \autoref{def:nullsequence-carrier}, and classifier transport is \autoref{def:nullsequence-classifier}. These two rows fix the visible packet and the only comparisons allowed between packets.

The zero seed is supplied by \autoref{thm:nullsequence-zero-seed}. It gives a canonical inhabitant whose schedule, dyadic rows, regular-rational handoff, and real seal are all displayed.

The downstream tail boundary is \autoref{thm:nullsequence-regseqrat-tail-boundary}. It forces every $\RegSeqRatUp$ read through the finite schedule, stream window, dyadic ledger, and zero-bound row, after which the $\RealUp$ zero seal may be consumed only through the same recorded route.

Combining these rows gives the stated local naming-certificate surface. The exclusions follow because no carrier coordinate stores quotient stream equality, a host limit, trichotomy, choice, or an ambient real-completeness principle.
\end{proof}
