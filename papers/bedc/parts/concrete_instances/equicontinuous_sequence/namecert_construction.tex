\begin{definition}[Equicontinuous sequence carrier]
\label{def:equicontinuous-sequence-carrier}
An $\EquicontinuousSequenceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
E=(K,F,S,W,Q,R,A,T,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $K$ is the $\CompactMetricUp$ source row whose finite cover and metric inspection data bound the common domain.
\item $F$ is the $\ContinuousMapUp$ row naming the displayed maps inspected by the sequence.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row that records which sequence indices and domain windows are read.
\item $W$ is the shared equicontinuity-window row, displayed as a finite request schedule over $K,F,S$.
\item $Q$ is the $\EquicontinuityUp$ row asserting that the schedule $W$ is common to the inspected sequence window.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row for endpoint and modulus values reached through the same finite windows.
\item $A$ is the $\ArzelaAscoliFiniteModulusUp$ handoff row, available only after $K,F,S,W,Q,R$ have been displayed.
\item $T$ is the $\EquicontinuityFiniteNetSelectorUp$ selector row, recording the finite net queries permitted by $K$ and $W$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for compact source, map row, stream windows, common modulus schedule, equicontinuity row, regular readback, Arzela--Ascoli handoff, finite-net selector, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a sequence-window request through $K,F,S,W,Q,R,A,T,H,P,N$.

\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\EquicontinuousSequenceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only through $K,F,S,W,Q,R,A,T,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for a host function sequence, quotient equality of maps, countable choice, open-cover compactness, selected subsequences, or ambient completeness of $\RealUp$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Equicontinuous sequence NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:equicontinuous-sequence-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\EquicontinuousSequenceUp$ carrier
$$
E=(K,F,S,W,Q,R,A,T,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\EquicontinuousSequenceUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the compact metric source, displayed continuous-map row, finite stream windows, shared modulus schedule, equicontinuity row, regular rational readback, Arzela--Ascoli finite-modulus handoff, finite-net selector, componentwise transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows. No obligation may replace these rows by a host function-space object, quotient map equality, a selected subsequence, countable choice, open-cover compactness, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier of \autoref{def:equicontinuous-sequence-carrier}. The analytic rows are exactly $K,F,S,W,Q,R,A,T$: compact source, displayed maps, finite sequence windows, the common schedule, the equicontinuity row, regular readback, the finite-modulus handoff, and the finite-net selector.

The common modulus is visible only as $W$ and $Q$. Since the packet stores no host function sequence and no quotient equality of maps, every local obligation must read the displayed continuous-map and stream-window rows before it can compare sequence entries.

The compactness-facing rows are $A$ and $T$. They are downstream of $K,F,S,W,Q,R$ and therefore cannot supply an independent subsequence selection, countable-choice principle, open-cover compactness theorem, or ambient completeness theorem for $\RealUp$.

Finally, $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite packet. These rows preserve the displayed route but do not add any excluded object, so the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}
