\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\NontrivialZeroClassifierUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-nontrivial-zero-classifier-namecert}
\origin{ai}

The $\NontrivialZeroClassifierUp$ packet realizes the RH witness-catalogue classifier as a finite boundary object. It separates supplied critical-strip zero rows from trivial-zero rows by displaying the $\ZetaZerosUp$ row, the $\CritStripUp$ row, the critical-line or critical-strip witness row already available to an RH-facing consumer, the real and rational comparison ledgers, componentwise transports, continuation routes, package provenance, and a local naming row. It is not an RH proof, a zero search procedure, a host complex equality, a quotient zero-set equality, or a selected representative.

The $\NontrivialZeroClassifierUp$ route consumes the public boundary discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-classifierboundary-namecert} and the negative-even exclusion route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-trivialzeroclassifier-namecert} before exposing an RH-facing nontrivial-zero row. These sibling rows supply the classifier boundary and trivial-zero separation used by downstream RH-facing packets.

\begin{definition}[Nontrivial-zero-classifier carrier]
\label{def:nontrivial-zero-classifier-carrier}
A $\NontrivialZeroClassifierUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
Q=(Z,S,W,T,R,A,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $Z$ is the supplied $\ZetaZerosUp$ row, carrying the zeta-zero source history and zero-pattern evidence;
\item $S$ is the supplied $\CritStripUp$ row for the same displayed source when the row is read as a critical-strip zero;
\item $W$ is the RH-facing witness row, read either as a $\CriticalLineWitnessUp$ packet or as a $\CriticalStripZetaZeroWitnessUp$ packet;
\item $T$ is the trivial-zero ledger row naming the negative-even-integer source route through $\IntUp$, $\RatUp$, $\RealUp$, and the carried zeta-zero row;
\item $R$ is the real-part row used by the strip and trivial-zero comparisons;
\item $A$ is the rational comparison ledger recording the critical-strip interval side and the negative-even side as distinct displayed rows;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $Z,S,W,T,R,A$ and for the package and local naming coordinates;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes from the zeta-zero row to the strip row, from the trivial-zero row to the real and rational ledgers, and from the witness row to the RH-facing consumer boundary;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ZetaZerosUp$, $\CritStripUp$, $\CriticalLineWitnessUp$, $\CriticalStripZetaZeroWitnessUp$, $\RealUp$, $\RatUp$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\NontrivialZeroClassifierUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier classifies a supplied zero as non-trivial only through the displayed critical-strip row $S$ and the witness row $W$. It classifies a supplied zero as trivial only through the displayed negative-even ledger $T$ and the rational comparison ledger $A$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[NontrivialZeroClassifier NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:nontrivial-zero-classifier-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\NontrivialZeroClassifierUp}$ surface for \autoref{def:nontrivial-zero-classifier-carrier} consists exactly of six finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for $Q=(Z,S,W,T,R,A,H,C,P,N)$;
\item shared-source binding between the $\ZetaZerosUp$ row $Z$ and the $\CritStripUp$ row $S$ through the displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ routes;
\item witness handoff through either $\CriticalLineWitnessUp$ or $\CriticalStripZetaZeroWitnessUp$ without adding a proof of $\mathsf{RH}$;
\item trivial-zero ledger exposure through the negative-even route $T$ and the rational comparison ledger $A$;
\item componentwise classifier stability under the displayed transports in $H$ and replay through the routes in $C$;
\item non-escape from analytic zero search, host complex equality, quotient zero-set equality, selected representatives, and an RH conclusion.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:nontrivial-zero-classifier-carrier}. The packet exposes exactly the zero row, strip row, witness row, trivial-zero ledger, real-part row, rational comparison ledger, transports, continuations, provenance, and local name.

The shared-source binding is read from $H$ and $C$. These rows are the only coordinates that compare the source of $Z$ with the source of $S$, so the classifier never replaces the displayed route by host complex equality.

The witness handoff is read through $W$. If $W$ is consumed as a $\CriticalLineWitnessUp$ packet, it supplies only the displayed critical-line witness surface; if it is consumed as a $\CriticalStripZetaZeroWitnessUp$ packet, it supplies only the critical-strip zeta-zero premise surface.

The trivial-zero side is read through $T,R,A$. Those rows route the negative-even source through the displayed real and rational ledgers, while the non-trivial side remains the supplied strip row $S$ attached to $Z$.

Finally, $P$ and $N$ package these obligations under the local certificate. Since no carrier coordinate names a zero search, a quotient zero set, a host equality, a representative selector, or an RH proof, the six listed obligations exhaust the available surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[NontrivialZeroClassifier critical-strip handoff]
\label{thm:nontrivial-zero-classifier-critical-strip-handoff}
For an accepted $\NontrivialZeroClassifierUp$ carrier $Q=(Z,S,W,T,R,A,H,C,P,N)$, every RH-facing non-trivial-zero read factors through the supplied $\ZetaZerosUp$ row $Z$, the supplied $\CritStripUp$ row $S$, the witness row $W$, the componentwise $\hsame$ transport row $H$, the displayed $\Cont$ route row $C$, package provenance $P$, and the local $\NameCert_{\NontrivialZeroClassifierUp}$ row $N$. The read receives a critical-strip zero boundary and receives no analytic zero search, host complex equality, quotient zero-set equality, selected representative, or proof of $\mathsf{RH}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted carrier and project the rows $Z,S,W,H,C,P,N$. The rows $Z$ and $S$ supply the zero and critical-strip premise, while $H$ and $C$ bind them as one displayed source route.

Read the witness row $W$ only after that premise route is present. The witness is therefore a consumer of the supplied zero-in-strip packet, not a constructor of new zeros and not a proof that all such zeros are on the critical line.

The package and local naming rows close the handoff. No unlisted coordinate can provide host complex equality, a quotient zero-set comparison, a representative selector, or an RH conclusion.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[NontrivialZeroClassifier trivial-zero exclusion ledger]
\label{thm:nontrivial-zero-classifier-trivial-zero-exclusion-ledger}
For an accepted $\NontrivialZeroClassifierUp$ carrier $Q=(Z,S,W,T,R,A,H,C,P,N)$, every trivial-zero read factors through the negative-even ledger $T$, the real-part row $R$, the rational comparison ledger $A$, the displayed transports $H$, the displayed continuations $C$, the package row $P$, and the local name $N$. A row classified through $T,R,A$ is a trivial-zero row of the packet and cannot simultaneously be read as a critical-strip non-trivial-zero row except through a separately displayed strip row $S$ tied to $Z$ by $H$ and $C$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the trivial-zero side of the carrier. The only rows available for such a read are $T,R,A,H,C,P,N$.

The ledger $T$ supplies the negative-even route, and $R,A$ record the real and rational comparisons used by that route. These rows are distinct from the critical-strip row $S$ and the witness row $W$.

The transport and continuation rows preserve this separation componentwise. Thus a trivial-zero consumer receives the finite exclusion ledger and cannot obtain a critical-strip non-trivial-zero reading unless the carrier also displays the separate $S,Z$ route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[NontrivialZeroClassifier RH consumer boundary]
\label{thm:nontrivial-zero-classifier-rh-consumer-boundary}
Every RH-facing consumer of an accepted $\NontrivialZeroClassifierUp$ packet receives exactly the finite classifier boundary
$$
Z,S,W,T,R,A,H,C,P,N.
$$
It may branch between the critical-strip zero route $Z,S,W,H,C,P,N$ and the trivial-zero ledger route $T,R,A,H,C,P,N$, but it receives no proof of $\mathsf{RH}$, no analytic zero search, no host complex equality, no quotient zero-set equality, and no selected representative of a zero class.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission displays the ten rows of the packet. The NameCert obligations identify which rows belong to the critical-strip handoff and which rows belong to the trivial-zero ledger.

Use the critical-strip handoff theorem for the $Z,S,W,H,C,P,N$ route. Use the trivial-zero exclusion ledger theorem for the $T,R,A,H,C,P,N$ route.

The two routes share only the displayed transport, continuation, provenance, and naming rows. Since the carrier has no field for RH proof, analytic search, host equality, quotient zero-set equality, or representative selection, every RH-facing consumer remains inside the stated finite boundary.
\end{proof}
