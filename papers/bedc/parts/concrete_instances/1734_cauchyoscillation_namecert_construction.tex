Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, the Cauchy oscillation criterion is read here as a finite tail-window object: a real-facing consumer may inspect named rational observations, an explicit modulus schedule, dyadic tolerances, and a displayed ledger of tail comparisons, but it never receives a quotient stream, a chosen limit, host real equality, or an ambient completeness principle. The $\CauchyOscillationUp$ packet is the finite oscillation surface consumed by $\CauchyCriterionUp$, $\UniformCauchyCriterionUp$, and later $\RealUp$ completion certificates.

The $\CauchyOscillationUp$ packet is the single-tail finite-window sibling of $\UniformCauchyCriterionUp$. Its window, modulus, dyadic tolerance, and oscillation ledger rows are consumed by the finite-family route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformcauchycriterion-namecert} before any $\RealUp$ seal can be read.

The oscillation packet consumes the $\RegSeqRatUp$ core of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the threshold schedule of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchymodulus-namecert}, and the dyadic-to-Real handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}.

Its oscillation ledger is therefore consumed through the $\RegSeqRatUp$ route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert} and the $\DyadicTailBoundUp$ dependency before any $\RealUp$ seal consumer inspects the finite tail comparison.

\begin{definition}[CauchyOscillation carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-oscillation-carrier}

$$

$$

\begin{enumerate}

\item $M$ is the $\CauchyModulusUp$ threshold schedule selecting the tail region inspected for each precision request;
\item $Q$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row whose dyadic budget is used by the oscillation test;
\item $T$ is the $\DyadicTailBoundUp$ oscillation ledger comparing all named tail observations beyond the threshold supplied by $M$ under the tolerance supplied by $Q$;
\item $S$ is the $\RealUp$ seal handoff reached only after the finite oscillation ledger has been accepted;
\item $H$ records the componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the tail window, modulus schedule, dyadic tolerance, oscillation ledger, seal handoff, package row, and local certificate row;

\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyOscillationUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only by these displayed rows, their componentwise $\hsame$ transports, their $\Cont$ read routes, their package provenance, and their local naming rows.
