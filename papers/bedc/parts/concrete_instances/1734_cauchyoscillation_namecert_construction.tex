\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CauchyOscillationUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyoscillation-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, the Cauchy oscillation criterion is read here as a finite tail-window object: a real-facing consumer may inspect named rational observations, an explicit modulus schedule, dyadic tolerances, and a displayed ledger of tail comparisons, but it never receives a quotient stream, a chosen limit, host real equality, or an ambient completeness principle. The $\CauchyOscillationUp$ packet is the finite oscillation surface consumed by $\CauchyCriterionUp$, $\UniformCauchyCriterionUp$, and later $\RealUp$ completion certificates.

The $\CauchyOscillationUp$ packet is the single-tail finite-window sibling of $\UniformCauchyCriterionUp$. Its window, modulus, dyadic tolerance, and oscillation ledger rows are consumed by the finite-family route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformcauchycriterion-namecert} before any $\RealUp$ seal can be read.

The oscillation packet consumes the $\RegSeqRatUp$ core of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the threshold schedule of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchymodulus-namecert}, and the dyadic-to-Real handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}.

\begin{definition}[CauchyOscillation carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-oscillation-carrier}
A $\CauchyOscillationUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
O=(W,M,Q,T,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $W$ is a $\RegSeqRatUp$ or $\StreamNameUp$ finite tail-window row, displaying the named rational observations available to the consumer;
\item $M$ is the $\CauchyModulusUp$ threshold schedule selecting the tail region inspected for each precision request;
\item $Q$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row whose dyadic budget is used by the oscillation test;
\item $T$ is the $\DyadicTailBoundUp$ oscillation ledger comparing all named tail observations beyond the threshold supplied by $M$ under the tolerance supplied by $Q$;
\item $S$ is the $\RealUp$ seal handoff reached only after the finite oscillation ledger has been accepted;
\item $H$ records the componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the tail window, modulus schedule, dyadic tolerance, oscillation ledger, seal handoff, package row, and local certificate row;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ read routes by which consumers inspect $W,M,Q,T,S,H,P,N$;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyCriterionUp$, $\UniformCauchyCriterionUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\DyadicTailBoundUp$, and $\RealUp$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyOscillationUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only by these displayed rows, their componentwise $\hsame$ transports, their $\Cont$ read routes, their package provenance, and their local naming rows.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyOscillation NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-oscillation-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\CauchyOscillationUp}$ surface consists of five obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier habitation: the packet of \autoref{def:cauchy-oscillation-carrier} displays a finite tail-window row, a modulus threshold schedule, a dyadic tolerance row, an oscillation ledger, a $\RealUp$ seal handoff, transport routes, package provenance, and local naming provenance;
\item common tail-window classifier: accepted packets are compared only by componentwise $\hsame$ and displayed $\Cont$ routes over $W,M,Q,T,S,H,C,P,N$;
\item oscillation-bound exactness: every accepted comparison in $T$ uses the threshold row $M$, the tolerance row $Q$, and the finite tail observations in $W$ from the same packet;
\item stability under $\hsame$ and $\Cont$: transported carriers preserve the selected tail window, threshold, tolerance, oscillation ledger, and seal handoff as one finite dependency packet;
\item ledger non-escape: accepted carriers export no quotient stream, no selected limit, no host real equality, and no ambient completeness principle.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CauchyOscillationUp.CauchyOscillationCarrier\_namecert\_obligations}
\leanvariant{BEDC.Derived.CauchyOscillationUp.CauchyOscillationPacket\_semantic\_name\_certificate}
\begin{proof}
Carrier habitation is the projection of the finite tuple
$$
O=(W,M,Q,T,S,H,C,P,N).
$$
Each coordinate is a displayed $\BHist$ row or a displayed route between such rows, so the packet supplies the advertised tail window, threshold schedule, tolerance row, oscillation ledger, seal handoff, provenance, and local certificate data.

For the common classifier, compare two accepted packets componentwise through the stored $\hsame$ rows and replay only the $\Cont$ routes recorded in $C$. Because the classifier has no further coordinate, it can read only $W,M,Q,T,S,H,C,P,N$ and cannot substitute an external tail object.

Oscillation-bound exactness is read from the paired rows $M,Q,T$ together with $W$. The threshold selected by $M$ determines which tail observations in $W$ are compared, the dyadic budget in $Q$ determines the comparison radius, and the ledger $T$ records exactly those finite comparisons.

Stability follows by transporting the same rows through $H$ and then replaying the same read routes in $C$. The package row $P$ records the $\CauchyCriterionUp$, $\UniformCauchyCriterionUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\DyadicTailBoundUp$, and $\RealUp$ source certificates, while $N$ records the local certificate row.

Finally, the carrier has no coordinate for quotient streams, selected limits, host real equality, or ambient completeness. Repacking the projections therefore gives the five NameCert obligations and the stated non-escape boundary.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyOscillation seal-handoff factorization]
\label{thm:cauchy-oscillation-seal-handoff-factorization}
Let
$$
O=(W,M,Q,T,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\CauchyOscillationUp$ carrier, and suppose that $S$ is the exported $\RealUp$ seal handoff of $O$. Every displayed consumer read in $C$ whose terminal row is $S$ factors through the same finite oscillation surface
$$
(W,M,Q,T)
$$
of that packet. More explicitly, such a read has a finite prefix reading the tail-window row $W$, the threshold schedule $M$, and the dyadic tolerance row $Q$; it then reads the oscillation ledger $T$ whose comparisons are made exactly from those three rows; only after that ledger prefix is accepted may the terminal projection expose $S$. No consumer read of $S$ may replace this prefix by a quotient stream, a selected limit, host real equality, an ambient completeness principle, or rows taken from a different $\CauchyOscillationUp$ packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
By \autoref{def:cauchy-oscillation-carrier}, the carrier has exactly the displayed rows
$$
W,M,Q,T,S,H,C,P,N.
$$
The row $S$ is not an independent real object: it is the $\RealUp$ seal handoff reached only after the finite oscillation ledger has been accepted. Thus any consumer read whose terminal row is $S$ must be one of the displayed $\Cont$ routes recorded in $C$, and its final step is a projection from the accepted packet rather than an external construction of a real.

It remains to identify the prefix that makes the seal available. The ledger row $T$ is specified as the $\DyadicTailBoundUp$ oscillation ledger comparing the named tail observations in $W$ beyond the threshold supplied by $M$ under the dyadic tolerance supplied by $Q$. Hence a read of $T$ cannot be separated from the same $W$, $M$, and $Q$ coordinates of $O$: the observations, cutoff schedule, and tolerance budget are precisely the data from which the ledger comparisons are recorded.

The NameCert obligations in \autoref{thm:cauchy-oscillation-namecert-obligations} make this dependence stable and exhaustive. Oscillation-bound exactness says that every accepted comparison in $T$ uses the threshold row $M$, the tolerance row $Q$, and the finite tail observations in $W$ from the same packet. Stability under $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ says that transported reads preserve the selected tail window, threshold, tolerance, ledger, and seal handoff as one finite dependency packet. Ledger non-escape says that the accepted carrier exports no quotient stream, selected limit, host real equality, or ambient completeness principle.

Therefore a displayed route to $S$ has no available input other than the accepted packet rows and cannot bypass the accepted ledger. Its terminal seal read factors through the finite prefix $W,M,Q,T$ of the same carrier, with $T$ itself determined by $W$, $M$, and $Q$. This is the asserted handoff factorization.
\end{proof}
