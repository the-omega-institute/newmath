\section{DiffForm consumer boundary obligations}
\label{sec:diffform-consumer-boundary-obligations}

\begin{theorem}[DiffForm consumer source scope]
\label{thm:diffform-consumer-source-scope}
Every $\DeRhamUp$, $\ContactUp$, or $\SymplecticUp$ consumer row exported
from $\DiffFormUp$ is a projection of the root-unblock source surface of
\autoref{def:diffform-root-unblock-source-surface}. Hence such a row may read
only $BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, the
$\ManifoldUp$ and $\TensorProductUp$ $\NameCert$ sources, the
six-coordinate carrier, and the $\DiffFormUp$ classifier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the displayed root-unblock source surface. Its entries list the
only histories and certificates made available to downstream consumers, so a
consumer row must be obtained by forgetting part of that displayed surface.

Apply \autoref{thm:diffform-root-unblock-carrier-classifier-scope} to restrict
the visible carrier and classifier data to the six-coordinate carrier and the
$\DiffFormUp$ classifier.

Finally apply
\autoref{thm:diffform-root-bhist-ledger-exactness-obligation}. It identifies
the exposed source ledger with the stated $BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$,
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, $\ManifoldUp$, and $\TensorProductUp$ sources, leaving
no additional source row for a downstream consumer to read.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DiffForm wedge degree ledger consumption]
\label{thm:diffform-wedge-degree-ledger-consumption}
Any $\DeRhamUp$, $\ContactUp$, or $\SymplecticUp$ consumer using a
$\DiffFormUp$ wedge may consume the wedge degree ledger only through its
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ endpoints and the displayed
$\Cont(d_{\omega},d_{\eta},d_{\mathrm{out}})$ row. This consumption rule is
stable under $\hsame$ transport of the two input degree histories and the
output degree history.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DiffFormUp.DiffFormWedgeDegreeLedger\_consumer\_boundary}
\begin{proof}
Read the ledger in \autoref{def:diffform-wedge-degree-ledger}. The displayed
data consist of the two input unary histories, the output unary history, and
the single continuation row joining them.

Classifier stability is then exactly
\autoref{thm:diffform-wedge-degree-classifier-stability}: transporting the
input form rows by $\hsame$ transports the degree histories and preserves the
wedge classifier row.

The root-unblock wedge closure of
\autoref{thm:diffform-root-unblock-wedge-cont-closure} identifies the same
continuation row as the wedge-visible $\Cont$ entry. Therefore a downstream
consumer can use the wedge degree ledger only through those unary endpoints
and that displayed continuation row, and the rule is invariant under
$\hsame$ transport.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DiffForm exterior consumer boundary]
\label{thm:diffform-exterior-consumer-boundary}
The chapters $\DeRhamUp$, $\ContactUp$, and $\SymplecticUp$ must supply
nilpotence, closedness, nondegeneracy, and contact nonintegrability in their
own certificate surfaces. The $\DiffFormUp$ export supplies only
carrier/classifier rows, wedge rows, degree rows, source rows, and the
ledger data made visible by the preceding consumer-source and wedge-degree
obligations.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply \autoref{thm:diffform-consumer-source-scope}. This bounds every
downstream consumer source to the displayed root-unblock carrier, classifier,
history, continuation, probe, and NameCert rows.

Next apply \autoref{thm:diffform-wedge-degree-ledger-consumption}. It bounds
all wedge-degree use to unary-history endpoints and the visible continuation
row, with no extra operator law.

Finally combine these two bounds with
\autoref{thm:diffform-root-unblock-derham-input-consumption} and
\autoref{thm:diffform-derham-input-boundary}. Those rows already separate
exterior-derivative input consumption from $d^2=0$, closedness,
nondegeneracy, and contact nonintegrability. The listed geometric laws
therefore remain obligations of the $\DeRhamUp$, $\ContactUp$, and
$\SymplecticUp$ certificate surfaces rather than exports of $\DiffFormUp$.
\end{proof}
