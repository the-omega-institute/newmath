\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\BishopLocatedCompletionBoundaryUp$}

Following Bishop and Bridges constructive analysis, the $\BishopLocatedCompletionBoundaryUp$ packet names the finite boundary at which a regular Cauchy rational source, locatedness evidence, and the displayed limit route are all visible before a $\RealUp$ completion-facing seal is read. It sits after the regular located Cauchy surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regular-located-cauchy-real-namecert}, the located-limit route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedlimit-namecert}, the located-real source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedreal-namecert}, the real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the regular rational source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the stream-window source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, and the dyadic arithmetic core of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}. The chapter records only the NameCert boundary for located completion data; it does not assert that $\RealUp$ is complete.

\begin{definition}[Bishop located completion boundary carrier]
\label{def:bishop-located-completion-boundary-carrier}
A $\BishopLocatedCompletionBoundaryUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
B=(S,Q,D,R,L,E,T,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window family naming the finite observations of the rational Cauchy source.
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular rational source row read only through the windows in $S$.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance, endpoint, and radius ledger attached to those windows.
\item $R$ is the $\RegularLocatedCauchyRealUp$ row that binds the regular source, dyadic ledger, locatedness evidence, and terminal real-name seal as one visible route.
\item $L$ is the $\LocatedLimitUp$ route by which the same displayed source is read as a located limit candidate.
\item $E$ is the $\LocatedRealUp$ enclosure row used at the location boundary before any terminal real-name consumer is allowed to inspect a seal.
\item $T$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after $S,Q,D,R,L,E$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the stream, regular-source, dyadic, regular-located, located-limit, located-real, Real-seal, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ replay for every completion-facing request through the same rows.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BishopLocatedCompletionBoundaryUp$, $\RegularLocatedCauchyRealUp$, $\LocatedLimitUp$, $\LocatedRealUp$, $\RealUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\BishopLocatedCompletionBoundaryUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a proof of $\RealUp$ completeness, quotient equality of Cauchy names, real trichotomy, countable choice, open-cover compactness, or a host-selected limit.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy extraction at the located boundary]
\label{thm:bishop-located-completion-boundary-regular-cauchy-extraction}
Every accepted $\BishopLocatedCompletionBoundaryUp$ packet $B=(S,Q,D,R,L,E,T,H,C,P,N)$ exposes its regular Cauchy source before any located completion read. The extracted source surface is exactly the finite route
$$
S,\quad Q,\quad D,\quad R
$$
with the visible transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows $H,C,P,N$. No consumer can obtain a completion-facing $\RealUp$ seal from $B$ without first reading the displayed StreamName windows, RegSeqRat source, DyadicRatCore tolerance ledger, and RegularLocatedCauchyReal route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:bishop-located-completion-boundary-carrier}. The only regular Cauchy data present are the stream windows $S$, regular rational source $Q$, dyadic tolerance ledger $D$, and regular located Cauchy row $R$.

Now read any completion-facing request. The row $T$ is stored after the located-limit and located-real rows, and those rows are themselves attached to $S,Q,D,R$. Thus the request must first pass through the extracted regular Cauchy source route.

The transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows preserve this same route. Since the carrier contains no independent completion coordinate, the displayed extraction exhausts the regular Cauchy surface available to the consumer.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Located limit route at the completion boundary]
\label{thm:bishop-located-completion-boundary-located-limit-route}
For every accepted $\BishopLocatedCompletionBoundaryUp$ packet $B=(S,Q,D,R,L,E,T,H,C,P,N)$, every located completion read factors through
$$
S\longmapsto Q\longmapsto D\longmapsto R\longmapsto L\longmapsto E\longmapsto T .
$$
The route uses the located-limit row $L$ and located-real enclosure row $E$ as visible finite data before the $\RealUp$ seal row $T$ is read, and it exports no separate host limit, quotient equality row, or choice-selected subsequence.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the extraction theorem \autoref{thm:bishop-located-completion-boundary-regular-cauchy-extraction}. It places the stream, regular rational, dyadic tolerance, and regular located Cauchy rows before any completion-facing seal.

The carrier definition then places $L$ and $E$ between that extracted source and the row $T$. Hence a located completion read must first display the located-limit route and the located-real enclosure row attached to the same finite source.

Finally apply the structural rows. Componentwise transport and replay keep the consumer on $S,Q,D,R,L,E,T$, while provenance and local naming record the same packet. No coordinate outside this route can supply a host limit or a quotient equality proof.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Bishop located completion boundary NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:bishop-located-completion-boundary-namecert-obligations}
The seed $\NameCert_{\BishopLocatedCompletionBoundaryUp}$ surface for \autoref{def:bishop-located-completion-boundary-carrier} consists exactly of carrier exposure for $B$, regular Cauchy extraction through $S,Q,D,R$, located-limit routing through $L$, located-real enclosure through $E$, terminal $\RealUp$ seal exposure through $T$, componentwise transport through $H$, displayed continuation replay through $C$, package provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$. These obligations bind the finite located completion boundary without adding $\RealUp$ completeness, quotient equality, trichotomy, countable choice, open-cover compactness, or a host-selected limit.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier exposure is projection from the displayed tuple $B=(S,Q,D,R,L,E,T,H,C,P,N)$. The analytic rows are exactly the stream, regular rational, dyadic, regular located Cauchy, located-limit, located-real, and Real-seal rows named in the carrier.

Regular Cauchy extraction is supplied by \autoref{thm:bishop-located-completion-boundary-regular-cauchy-extraction}. Located routing is supplied by \autoref{thm:bishop-located-completion-boundary-located-limit-route}. Together they put every completion-facing read on one finite route through $S,Q,D,R,L,E,T$.

The remaining rows are structural. The $\hsame$ transports and $\Cont$ replay preserve the same finite route, while $\Pkg$ provenance and the local $\NameCert$ row name the packet. Since no excluded principle appears as a carrier coordinate, the listed rows exhaust the seed obligation surface.
\end{proof}
