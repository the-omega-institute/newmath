\begin{definition}[Cofinal tail-equivalence carrier]
\label{def:cofinal-tail-equivalence-carrier}
A $\CofinalTailEquivalenceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
E=(R_0,R_1,W,Q,D,A,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $R_0$ and $R_1$ are the two cofinal tail-route rows being compared.
\item $W$ is the shared $\StreamNameUp$ readback row through which both routes expose the same finite observation window.
\item $Q$ is the shared $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row attached to that window on both tail routes.
\item $D$ is the shared $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger recording the tolerance and endpoint observations consumed by the comparison.
\item $A$ is the $\RealTailAgreementSealUp$ consumer row reached only after both cofinal routes, the shared window, the regular readback, and the dyadic ledger are displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $R_0,R_1,W,Q,D,A$ and the local certificate row.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from each cofinal route through the shared StreamName, RegSeqRat, DyadicRatCore, and RealTailAgreementSeal rows.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CofinalTailEquivalenceUp$, $\CofinalTailAgreementUp$, $\TailCofinalityBudgetUp$, $\DyadicCofinalTailSelectorUp$, $\RealTailAgreementSealUp$, $\DiagonalLimitCompatibilityUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CofinalTailEquivalenceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exports only the two displayed cofinal routes, their shared finite readback rows, the Real-tail agreement seal consumer row, and the structural transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cofinal tail-equivalence NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cofinal-tail-equivalence-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:cofinal-tail-equivalence-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\CofinalTailEquivalenceUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $E=(R_0,R_1,W,Q,D,A,H,C,P,N)$, two cofinal route exposures through $R_0$ and $R_1$, shared $\StreamNameUp$ window exposure through $W$, shared $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback exposure through $Q$, shared $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger exposure through $D$, $\RealTailAgreementSealUp$ consumer exposure through $A$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier. Its coordinates display exactly the two cofinal route rows, the shared StreamName window, the regular-rational readback, the dyadic ledger, the Real-tail agreement seal consumer row, and the structural support rows.

Read the sibling sources in order. The $\CofinalTailAgreementUp$ row supplies the one-sided agreement surface, the $\TailCofinalityBudgetUp$ row supplies the shared threshold discipline, and the $\DyadicCofinalTailSelectorUp$ row supplies the finite selector route that may feed either side.

Finally read the seal consumer. The $\RealTailAgreementSealUp$ row $A$ is downstream of both routes and the shared readback rows, while $H,C,P,N$ only transport, replay, source, and name those displayed rows. These clauses exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cofinal tail-equivalence tail-agreement transport]
\label{thm:cofinal-tail-equivalence-tail-agreement-transport}
Let $E=(R_0,R_1,W,Q,D,A,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CofinalTailEquivalenceUp$ carrier. If the first cofinal route $R_0$ is transported to the second route $R_1$ through the displayed $H$ and $C$ rows, then the tail-agreement read remains on the same shared finite surface
$$
W,Q,D,A,H,C,P,N.
$$
The transport preserves the StreamName readback, RegSeqRat readback, DyadicRatCore ledger, and RealTailAgreementSeal consumer row, and it exports no quotient stream equality, host real equality, selected limit, hidden tail selector, countable-choice row, or ambient completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the two route coordinates of \autoref{def:cofinal-tail-equivalence-carrier}. The only relation between them inside the packet is the componentwise $\hsame$ table $H$ and the displayed continuation replay $C$.

Apply the NameCert obligations. They force both $R_0$ and $R_1$ to read the same $W,Q,D,A$ rows before any tail-agreement consumer can inspect the packet.

Thus transporting the tail-agreement read from $R_0$ to $R_1$ changes only the displayed route coordinate. The shared readback and seal rows remain $W,Q,D,A,H,C,P,N$, and the excluded external equalities and selectors have no carrier coordinate from which they could be projected.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cofinal tail-equivalence Real-seal consumer]
\label{thm:cofinal-tail-equivalence-real-seal-consumer}
Every $\RealUp$ or completion-facing consumer of an accepted $\CofinalTailEquivalenceUp$ packet reaches the $\RealTailAgreementSealUp$ row $A$ only after both cofinal routes $R_0,R_1$, the shared $\StreamNameUp$ window $W$, the shared $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback $Q$, and the shared $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger $D$ have been displayed. The consumer route is exhausted by
$$
R_0,R_1,W,Q,D,A,H,C,P,N.
$$
No consumer of this packet receives a one-route agreement in place of two-route equivalence, a host equality of completed reals, a quotient-stream comparison, a selected limit, a hidden tail oracle, or an ambient completeness witness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier inventory. The Real-facing coordinate is the $\RealTailAgreementSealUp$ row $A$, and it appears only after the two route rows and the three shared readback rows.

Use \autoref{thm:cofinal-tail-equivalence-namecert-obligations}. It makes $R_0,R_1,W,Q,D$ mandatory before $A$ can be consumed, while $H,C,P,N$ authenticate the same finite route.

Use \autoref{thm:cofinal-tail-equivalence-tail-agreement-transport}. Any transport between the two routes preserves the shared tail-agreement surface and therefore cannot collapse the carrier to a one-route packet or import a host-level equality. The listed finite route is the whole Real-seal consumer surface.
\end{proof}
