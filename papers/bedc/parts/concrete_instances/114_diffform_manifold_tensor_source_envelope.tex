\begin{definition}[Differential-form manifold-tensor source envelope]
\label{def:diffform-manifold-tensor-source-envelope}
The differential-form manifold-tensor source envelope is the visible BEDC
package
$$
\mathsf{DiffFormSourceEnvelope}_{M,T}.
$$
Its rows are exactly the $BHist$ carrier rows of
\autoref{def:diffform-bhist-form-carrier}, the classifier rows of
\autoref{def:diffform-bhist-form-classifier}, the $\hsame$ transport rows used
by the classifier, the $\Cont$ rows used by degree and wedge continuation, the
displayed $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ spines, the tangent-probe rows certified by
$\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$, and the multilinear tensor rows certified by
$\NameCert_{\TensorProductUp}$. It also contains the wedge probe concatenation
ledger of \autoref{def:diffform-wedge-probe-concatenation-ledger}, the
degree-probe alignment rows of
\autoref{def:diffform-degree-probe-alignment-row}, and the exterior-derivative
input rows of \autoref{def:diffform-exterior-derivative-input-row}. The
envelope has no field for nilpotence, closedness, nondegeneracy, contact
nonintegrability, or a standard bridge.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Differential-form manifold-tensor source scope]
\label{thm:diffform-manifold-tensor-source-scope}
Every carrier witness and every classifier witness read from
$\mathsf{DiffFormSourceEnvelope}_{M,T}$ is obtained only from the displayed
$BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$,
$\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$, and $\NameCert_{\TensorProductUp}$ rows of
\autoref{def:diffform-manifold-tensor-source-envelope}. No downstream
$\DeRhamUp$, $\SymplecticUp$, or $\ContactUp$ law is a source for the
$\DiffFormUp$ carrier or classifier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the envelope definition. Its carrier part is exactly the six-coordinate
$BHist$ row of \autoref{def:diffform-bhist-form-carrier}; its classifier part
is exactly the componentwise comparison of
\autoref{def:diffform-bhist-form-classifier}. Those two rows expose the unary
degree history, the visible probe bundle, the tensor row, the scalar endpoint,
the antisymmetry ledger, and the source ledger.

Project the manifold and tensor inputs. Tangent probes are read through the
displayed $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ spine and the
$\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$ source. Multilinear tensor rows are read through the
same probe spine and the $\NameCert_{\TensorProductUp}$ source. The remaining
classifier comparisons are $\hsame$ and retained $\Cont$ rows over the same
BHist provenance.

Finally compare this list with the downstream consumer face classifier of
\autoref{def:diffform-root-consumer-face-classifier}. That classifier places
nilpotence, closedness, nondegeneracy, and contact nonintegrability outside
the shared root face. Hence carrier and classifier witnesses are sourced only
from the envelope rows named above.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Differential-form wedge continuation input closure]
\label{thm:diffform-wedge-continuation-input-closure}
Let $\omega$ and $\eta$ be carried rows in
$\mathsf{DiffFormSourceEnvelope}_{M,T}$. If their admitted wedge row is
obtained through the visible continuation and probe append data, then the
wedge input and output rows remain inside
$\mathsf{DiffFormSourceEnvelope}_{M,T}$. The retained rows are the wedge probe
concatenation ledger, the coverage row for its output probe bundle, and the
degree-probe alignment row for the wedge output.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the two carried input rows. By
\autoref{def:diffform-manifold-tensor-source-envelope}, each input row already
carries its $BHist$ carrier witness, classifier witness, probe bundle,
Manifold source row, TensorProduct source row, and degree-probe alignment row.

Apply \autoref{def:diffform-wedge-probe-concatenation-ledger}. The wedge input
is recorded by the two input ledgers, the two input probe spines, the output
probe spine, and the tensor-concatenation provenance row. These are all rows
named in the source envelope.

Next apply \autoref{thm:diffform-wedge-probe-concatenation-coverage}. It
identifies the output probe bundle as the visible append of the two input
bundles and shows that every output probe comes from one of the two input
spines. Thus the manifold and tensor source readings remain the same displayed
source readings.

Finally apply
\autoref{thm:diffform-degree-probe-alignment-wedge-closure}. The output degree
row and output probe bundle are aligned by the wedge continuation row. Repack
the two input rows, the output append row, the tensor provenance, and the
aligned degree-probe row into
\autoref{def:diffform-manifold-tensor-source-envelope}. The wedge continuation
therefore stays inside the source envelope.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Differential-form exterior-derivative consumer coverage]
\label{thm:diffform-exterior-derivative-consumer-coverage}
Every witness in $\mathsf{DiffFormSourceEnvelope}_{M,T}$ that satisfies the
exterior-derivative input boundary supplies the downstream exterior-derivative
consumer face for $\DeRhamUp$, $\SymplecticUp$, and $\ContactUp$. The supplied
face is exactly the carrier, classifier, degree-shift, wedge-input,
endpoint-transport, source-ledger, and downstream boundary surface already
listed for those consumers, with no downstream law imported into
$\DiffFormUp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with an envelope witness satisfying the exterior-derivative input
boundary. The input boundary row is the visible package of
\autoref{def:diffform-exterior-derivative-input-row}, and its downstream
visible form is the package of
\autoref{def:diffform-exterior-derivative-visible-input-package}.

Use \autoref{thm:diffform-exterior-input-root-closure}. It repacks the source
carrier, output carrier, successor-degree continuation, transported probe and
tensor rows, scalar endpoint comparison, and restricted source ledger into
$\mathsf{DiffFormDownstreamBoundary}_{M,T}$.

Now combine the downstream boundary rows. The exterior-derivative scope row
\autoref{thm:diffform-exterior-derivative-downstream-scope} names the carrier,
classifier, degree-raise candidate, endpoint transport, and exterior-algebra
substrate consumed downstream. The consumer boundary theorem
\autoref{thm:diffform-downstream-consumer-boundary} identifies
$\DeRhamUp$, $\SymplecticUp$, and $\ContactUp$ as the downstream consumers of
that surface.

Finally apply
\autoref{thm:diffform-boundary-exhaustion-downstream-export}. It says the
downstream export is exhausted by the carrier, classifier, wedge,
antisymmetry, exterior-input, transport, source-ledger, and downstream rows.
The consumer face classifier of
\autoref{def:diffform-root-consumer-face-classifier} assigns those rows to the
de Rham, symplectic, contact, or shared root faces. Thus every exterior-input
envelope witness supplies the advertised downstream consumer face and still
leaves nilpotence, closedness, nondegeneracy, and contact nonintegrability to
the consuming chapters.
\end{proof}
