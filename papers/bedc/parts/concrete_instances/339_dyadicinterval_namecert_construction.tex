\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\DyadicIntervalUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicinterval-namecert}

The $\DyadicIntervalUp$ interface names finite dyadic interval packets in the
sense of Moore--Kearfott--Cloud interval analysis. It sits between
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$, which supplies visible dyadic endpoint and radius ledgers,
and $\RegSeqRatUp$ or $\RealUp$, which consume finite approximation packets
without importing quotient reals or completeness principles.

\section{Carrier and refinement ledger}
\label{sec:dyadicinterval-carrier-refinement}

\begin{definition}[DyadicInterval BHist carrier]
\label{def:dyadicinterval-carrier}
A $\DyadicIntervalUp$ carrier row is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
I=(L,R,W,O,M,\rho,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item left and right endpoint rows $L$ and $R$ accepted by
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$;
\item a unary nonnegative width row $W$ naming the displayed dyadic
width bound;
\item an endpoint-order ledger $O$ proving $L\leq R$ by a
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$ common-exponent comparison;
\item midpoint and radius rows $M$ and $\rho$ computed from the same
endpoint window;
\item componentwise $\hsame$ rows $H$ and displayed $\Cont$ routes $C$ for
endpoint, order, midpoint, radius, and width transport;
\item a $\Pkg$ provenance row $P$ naming the consumed
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RatUp$, $\IntUp$, and $\StreamNameUp$ source rows;
\item a $\NameCert$ row $N$ recording that the public interval packet is
classified only by endpointwise finite dyadic tolerance.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for Dedekind cuts, open-cover compactness,
quotient intervals, host real endpoints, or real-completeness data.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicInterval nested refinement ledger]
\label{thm:dyadicinterval-nested-refinement-ledger}
Let $I=(L,R,W,O,M,\rho,H,C,P,N)$ and
$I'=(L',R',W',O',M',\rho',H',C',P',N')$ be accepted
$\DyadicIntervalUp$ carriers. Suppose $I'$ is obtained from $I$ by a nested
refinement: the transported endpoint rows satisfy $L\leq L'\leq R'\leq R$ in
the displayed $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ common-exponent ledger, the width row $W'$
is bounded by the transported width row $W$, and the midpoint and radius rows
are recomputed from the refined endpoint window. Then $I'$ repacks the same
kind of endpoint, radius, width, order, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and
$\NameCert$ provenance rows as $I$.

The finite source consumed by $\RegSeqRatUp$ is preserved: a scheduled
regular-stream window may read the refined endpoint rows, radius row, width
row, and provenance through the same visible finite dyadic interval surface,
and it receives no quotient sequence, real-completion object, or hidden
host-real endpoint.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DyadicIntervalUp.DyadicIntervalPacket\_nested\_refinement\_ledger}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:dyadicinterval-carrier}. The endpoint
rows, width row, order ledger, midpoint row, radius row, componentwise
$\hsame$ data, continuation routes, package provenance, and naming row are
all visible finite rows.

Apply the nested-refinement hypotheses to the endpoint-order ledger. The
refined endpoints are still accepted $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ rows, and the order
comparisons are transported through the same common-exponent dyadic ledger.

Recompute the midpoint and radius rows from the refined endpoint window and
transport the width row along the displayed nonnegative dyadic bound. These
operations only repack the finite endpoint and arithmetic ledgers already
named by the carrier.

Finally restrict the consumer view to the rows a $\RegSeqRatUp$ scheduled
window can read. That view consists exactly of endpoint, radius, width,
regular dyadic comparison, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and $\NameCert$
provenance rows, so refinement preserves the finite source surface.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DyadicIntervalUp.DyadicIntervalEndpointPacket\_nested\_refinement\_ledger}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicInterval nested window composition]
\label{thm:dyadicinterval-nested-window-composition}
Let $I_0=(L_0,R_0,W_0,O_0,M_0,\rho_0,H_0,C_0,P_0,N_0)$,
$I_1=(L_1,R_1,W_1,O_1,M_1,\rho_1,H_1,C_1,P_1,N_1)$, and
$I_2=(L_2,R_2,W_2,O_2,M_2,\rho_2,H_2,C_2,P_2,N_2)$ be accepted
$\DyadicIntervalUp$ carriers. Suppose $I_1$ is a nested refinement of $I_0$
and $I_2$ is a nested refinement of $I_1$ in the endpoint-order, width,
midpoint, and radius ledgers of \autoref{thm:dyadicinterval-nested-refinement-ledger}.
Then $I_2$ is a nested refinement of $I_0$.

Moreover, the seal-source packet obtained by refining first from $I_0$ to
$I_1$ and then from $I_1$ to $I_2$ exposes exactly the same endpoint, width,
radius, order, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and $\NameCert$ rows as the
direct refinement from $I_0$ to $I_2$. It exposes no Dedekind cut, quotient
interval, host-real endpoint, open-cover compactness witness, completeness
datum, or standard bridge.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DyadicIntervalUp.DyadicIntervalPacket\_nested\_window\_composition}
\begin{proof}
Project the three accepted packets through \autoref{def:dyadicinterval-carrier}.
The only coordinates available for comparison are the endpoint rows, width
row, order ledger, midpoint row, radius row, componentwise $\hsame$ rows,
displayed $\Cont$ routes, $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance, and $\NameCert$ row.

Compose the endpoint-order ledgers. The hypotheses give
$L_0\leq L_1\leq R_1\leq R_0$ and $L_1\leq L_2\leq R_2\leq R_1$ in the
displayed $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ common-exponent comparisons, so transitivity of
the finite comparison ledger gives $L_0\leq L_2\leq R_2\leq R_0$. The
width and radius transports compose in the same visible dyadic arithmetic
rows, and the midpoint row is recomputed from the final endpoint window.

Apply \autoref{thm:dyadicinterval-nested-refinement-ledger} to the first
refinement and then to the second refinement. The two applications repack the
same endpoint, width, radius, order, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and
$\NameCert$ row kinds that the direct $I_0$ to $I_2$ refinement uses.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:dyadicinterval-real-seal-source-boundary}. That
boundary exhausts the rows visible to a $\RealUp$ or $\RegSeqRatUp$
finite-window consumer, so the two-step seal source and the direct seal source
have the same consumer-visible rows. Since the carrier has no coordinate for
Dedekind cuts, quotient intervals, host-real endpoints, open-cover compactness,
completeness data, or standard bridges, neither refinement path can expose
one.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DyadicIntervalUp.DyadicIntervalPacket\_nested\_window\_seal\_source\_composition}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DyadicIntervalUp.DyadicIntervalPacket\_nested\_window\_name\_certificate}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicInterval NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:dyadicinterval-namecert-obligation}
The local $\NameCert_{\DyadicIntervalUp}$ obligation surface consists of five
finite rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier: the $\mathsf{BHist}$ interval packet of
\autoref{def:dyadicinterval-carrier};
\item classifier: endpointwise dyadic tolerance, comparing left endpoints
and right endpoints by $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ common-exponent windows while
preserving the displayed width and radius rows;
\item endpoint-order stability: componentwise $\hsame$ and $\Cont$
transport preserve the visible $L\leq R$ ledger;
\item nested refinement transport: the refinement ledger of
\autoref{thm:dyadicinterval-nested-refinement-ledger} repacks the same finite
endpoint, midpoint, radius, width, provenance, and naming rows;
\item $\RealUp$ seal handoff boundary: a real-name seal may consume only the
finite interval endpoint, radius, width, order, and provenance rows and gains
no real-completeness, quotient-interval, or standard-bridge datum.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier row is \autoref{def:dyadicinterval-carrier}. It lists the only
public coordinates exposed by an accepted $\DyadicIntervalUp$ packet.

The classifier row is endpointwise because the carrier has separate left and
right $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint rows. A dyadic-tolerance witness therefore
uses two common-exponent comparisons, one for each endpoint, together with
the displayed width and radius rows.

Endpoint-order stability follows by transporting the order ledger through
the componentwise $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ rows named in the carrier. Nested
refinement transport is exactly the repacking statement of
\autoref{thm:dyadicinterval-nested-refinement-ledger}.

At the $\RealUp$ seal boundary, the available source rows are exhausted by
the carrier, classifier, order, and refinement rows above. Since none of
those rows contains a Dedekind cut, an open-cover compactness object, a
quotient interval, or completeness data, the handoff remains a finite BEDC
source surface.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DyadicIntervalUp.DyadicIntervalPacket\_namecert\_obligation\_surface}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicInterval endpoint classifier transport]
\label{thm:dyadicinterval-endpoint-classifier-transport}
Let $I=(L,R,W,O,M,\rho,H,C,P,N)$ and
$I'=(L',R',W',O',M',\rho',H',C',P',N')$ be accepted
$\DyadicIntervalUp$ carriers. Suppose the displayed $H$ rows provide
componentwise $\hsame$ witnesses and the displayed $C$ rows provide
$\Cont$ routes from the coordinates of $I$ to the corresponding coordinates
of $I'$. Then every endpointwise dyadic-tolerance witness for the left and
right endpoint rows of $I$ transports componentwise to $I'$, and the same
transport repacks the order, midpoint, radius, width, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and
$\NameCert$ rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the two carriers through \autoref{def:dyadicinterval-carrier}. The
left endpoint, right endpoint, width, order, midpoint, radius, package, and
naming coordinates are separate finite rows, and the carrier exposes the
matching $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ rows for each coordinate.

Transport the classifier in the endpoint coordinates first. The
dyadic-tolerance witness is endpointwise, so the left-endpoint comparison
follows the left endpoint route and the right-endpoint comparison follows
the right endpoint route.

It remains to repack the non-endpoint rows. The order, midpoint, radius, and
width data are read from the same displayed carrier coordinates, while the
$\mathsf{Pkg}$ and $\NameCert$ rows are transported by their own visible
routes. This gives the componentwise target classifier without adding any
extra source datum.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DyadicIntervalUp.DyadicIntervalEndpointPacket\_endpoint\_classifier\_transport}
\leanvariant{BEDC.Derived.DyadicIntervalUp.DyadicIntervalPacket\_endpoint\_classifier\_transport}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicInterval width-radius ledger exactness]
\label{thm:dyadicinterval-width-radius-ledger-exactness}
Every width, midpoint, and radius readback used by the
$\DyadicIntervalUp$ endpoint classifier is projected from the carrier rows
of \autoref{def:dyadicinterval-carrier} and, under nested refinement, from
the transported rows supplied by
\autoref{thm:dyadicinterval-nested-refinement-ledger}. No classifier
readback may use an external host-real radius, quotient-interval width, or
completion-side midpoint.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DyadicIntervalUp.DyadicIntervalPacket\_width\_radius\_ledger\_exactness}
\begin{proof}
The carrier projection exposes exactly the width row $W$, midpoint row $M$,
and radius row $\rho$. These are the only rows named in
\autoref{def:dyadicinterval-carrier} for width, midpoint, and radius
readback.

Under nested refinement, apply
\autoref{thm:dyadicinterval-nested-refinement-ledger}. The refined endpoint
window repacks the transported width row and recomputes the midpoint and
radius rows from that finite window.

Thus every classifier readback is a projection from either the original
carrier or its nested-refinement transport. Since neither source contains a
host-real, quotient-interval, or completion coordinate, no such coordinate
can participate in the readback.
\end{proof}
