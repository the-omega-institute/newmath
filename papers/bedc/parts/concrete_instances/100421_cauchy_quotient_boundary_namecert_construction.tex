Following Bishop and Bridges' constructive completion, the $\CauchyQuotientBoundaryUp$ packet records quotient-facing Cauchy completion requests only as finite BEDC boundary data. It sits where the $\CauchySequenceSpaceUp$ common-window route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchysequencespace-namecert} meets the $\QuotientStreamRefusalUp$ refusal surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-quotientstreamrefusal-namecert}, downstream of the $\StreamNameUp$ windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the $\BishopRegularCauchyCompletionUp$ regular-completion route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bishop-regular-cauchy-completion-namecert}, and the terminal $\RealUp$ seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet is a finite refusal boundary for quotient-style completion reads; it is not a quotient construction, selected representative, host equality, countable-choice principle, or ambient completeness theorem for $\RealUp$.

\begin{definition}[Cauchy quotient boundary carrier]\label{def:cauchy-quotient-boundary-carrier}
A $\CauchyQuotientBoundaryUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
B=(S,Q,F,D,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the $\CauchySequenceSpaceUp$ source row. It records the finite family, common-window schedule, cofinal Cauchy-window ledger, tolerance row, completion handoff, and local classifier data that are already visible before any quotient-facing request is inspected.
\item $Q$ is the quotient-request row. It names the requested equality of Cauchy streams, quotient class, representative, or quotient-completion read without admitting any of those objects as carrier coordinates.
\item $F$ is the $\QuotientStreamRefusalUp$ route row. It records the refusal path used when $Q$ asks for a quotient-style completion datum outside the displayed finite route.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger. It stores dyadic radii, slack rows, comparison budgets, and tolerance conversions consumed by the source and refusal routes.
\item $W$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window row. It records the inspected source windows that make the Cauchy request visible before refusal is issued.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row. It records the regular rational observations available after the finite windows and dyadic ledger have been displayed.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal boundary row. It is visible only downstream of $S,W,D,R,F$ and does not identify quotient streams or choose a representative.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the source, quotient request, refusal route, dyadic ledger, stream windows, regular readback, Real seal boundary, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a quotient-facing completion request through $S,Q,F,D,W,R,E,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyQuotientBoundaryUp$, $\CauchySequenceSpaceUp$, $\QuotientStreamRefusalUp$, $\BishopRegularCauchyCompletionUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyQuotientBoundaryUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate asserts exactly that these rows form one finite quotient-facing refusal-boundary packet. It has no coordinate for quotient stream equality, quotient classes, selected representatives, host equality, countable choice, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy quotient boundary NameCert obligations]\label{thm:cauchy-quotient-boundary-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CauchyQuotientBoundaryUp$ carrier $B=(S,Q,F,D,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyQuotientBoundaryUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the Cauchy-window source, quotient request, refusal route, dyadic tolerance ledger, finite stream windows, regular rational readback, Real seal boundary, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item quotient-request routing, requiring every quotient-facing completion request to expose $Q$ and then enter the displayed refusal route $F$ unless it is already a read of the finite Cauchy source route $S,W,D,R$;
\item refusal factorization, requiring $F$ to route off-route quotient-style requests through the $\QuotientStreamRefusalUp$ surface before any $\RealUp$ seal boundary is visible;
\item Real-seal non-escape, requiring $E$ to remain downstream of the CauchySequenceSpace and refusal rows and never to serve as quotient stream equality, a quotient class, selected representative, host equality, countable choice, or ambient completeness;
\item transport and replay stability, requiring $H$ and $C$ to preserve the displayed row order $S,Q,F,D,W,R,E,P,N$;
\item provenance exhaustion, requiring $P$ and $N$ to name exactly the displayed packet and no quotient object outside it.
\end{enumerate}
No Cauchy quotient boundary obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is the projection of \autoref{def:cauchy-quotient-boundary-carrier}. The accepted packet exposes only the CauchySequenceSpace source row, quotient-request row, refusal route, dyadic ledger, finite windows, regular readback, Real seal boundary, and structural rows.

Quotient-request routing begins at $Q$. If the request is a finite Cauchy-window read, it remains inside the displayed source route $S,W,D,R$. If it asks for quotient equality, a quotient class, a representative, or quotient-completion data beyond that route, the only displayed next row is the refusal row $F$.

The refusal factorization is supplied by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-quotientstreamrefusal-namecert}. That chapter routes off-route quotient-style requests through the refusal ledger before any Real-completion read can reach a seal, so $E$ stays downstream of $S,W,D,R,F$.

Finally, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, provenance, and local naming preserve the same rows. Since no excluded quotient or completeness coordinate occurs in $B$, the listed obligations exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy quotient boundary refusal factorization]\label{thm:cauchy-quotient-boundary-refusal-factorization}
Every quotient-facing Cauchy completion read of an accepted $\CauchyQuotientBoundaryUp$ carrier $B=(S,Q,F,D,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)$ factors through the displayed route
$$
\CauchySequenceSpaceUp \longmapsto \StreamNameUp \longmapsto \DyadicRatCoreUp \longmapsto \RegSeqRatUp \longmapsto \QuotientStreamRefusalUp \longmapsto \RealUp .
$$
Thus the read consumes the source row $S$, finite windows $W$, dyadic ledger $D$, regular readback $R$, refusal route $F$, and only then the Real seal boundary $E$. It exports no quotient class, selected representative, host equality, countable choice, or $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a quotient-facing completion read. The carrier gives the request only as $Q$, and \autoref{def:cauchy-quotient-boundary-carrier} gives no coordinate where quotient stream equality, a quotient class, or a selected representative could already live.

The Cauchy source part of the read is forced through $S$. By the $\CauchySequenceSpaceUp$ route, the consumer then opens finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows, reads the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, and obtains the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback before any completion-facing handoff is visible.

When the same consumer asks for quotient-style data beyond those rows, the next displayed row is $F$. The $\QuotientStreamRefusalUp$ route sends that request through the refusal ledger, and only after that route is fixed can the terminal $\RealUp$ seal boundary $E$ be inspected.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, package, and name this route. They add no quotient class, selected representative, host equality, countable-choice schedule, or ambient completeness witness, so the stated factorization is exhaustive.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy quotient boundary Real-seal non-escape]\label{thm:cauchy-quotient-boundary-real-seal-nonescape}
Let $B=(S,Q,F,D,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyQuotientBoundaryUp$ carrier. Any consumer that reaches the $\RealUp$ seal boundary $E$ has already consumed the displayed CauchySequenceSpace source, quotient-request row, QuotientStreamRefusal route, StreamName windows, DyadicRatCore tolerance ledger, RegSeqRat readback, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows. No consumer can use $E$ to recover quotient stream equality, a quotient class, a selected representative, host equality, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The Real seal boundary appears only as the row $E$ in the carrier. Its position in the packet is downstream of $S,Q,F,D,W,R$, so a consumer cannot reach it before the Cauchy source route and the refusal route have both been displayed.

Replay the request through $C$. The replay first exposes the source and quotient request, then the finite-window, dyadic-ledger, regular-readback, and refusal rows. Componentwise transport in $H$ preserves those rows rather than replacing them by quotient data.

The provenance and local naming rows $P,N$ name the same finite boundary. Since quotient stream equality, quotient classes, selected representatives, host equality, countable choice, and ambient Real completeness are absent from the carrier, $E$ cannot export any of them.
\end{proof}
