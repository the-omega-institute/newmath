Following Holder and Bishop--Bridges constructive analysis, the $\HolderContinuityUp$ packet records the classical power-law continuity surface as finite BEDC data. It sits between the displayed metric source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the terminal real coefficient route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the graph-facing map surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuousmap-namecert}, the exponent-one boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-lipschitzmap-namecert}, and the explicit modulus routes of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-moduluscontinuity-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformmodulus-namecert}. The packet gives later Real and Completion consumers a finite power-law modulus layer without forcing all continuity data to be either Lipschitz or arbitrary-modulus data.

\begin{definition}[Holder continuity carrier]
\label{def:holder-continuity-carrier}
A $\HolderContinuityUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(X,Y,G,\alpha,K,M,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the source $\MetricUp$ row, carrying the source point classifier, source distance classifier, and the finite source-distance observations inspected by the packet.
\item $Y$ is the target $\MetricUp$ row, carrying the target point classifier, target distance classifier, and the finite target-distance observations exposed by the graph row.
\item $G$ is the $\ContinuousMapUp$ graph row from $X$ to $Y$, including only those finite source and target observations consumed by the continuity read.
\item $\alpha$ is the rational exponent row. It records a positive rational exponent, its displayed comparison data, and the finite power-law read discipline used by the packet.
\item $K$ is the $\RealUp$ coefficient ledger for the displayed Holder constant and the real-valued distance estimates used by the power-law bound.
\item $M$ is the induced $\ModulusContinuityUp$ or $\UniformModulusUp$ row obtained from $X,Y,G,\alpha,K$ by reading the power-law budget as a displayed modulus.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the two metric rows, graph row, rational exponent, coefficient ledger, induced modulus row, continuation route, provenance, and local naming.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a finite source-distance request through $G,\alpha,K,M,H,P,N$ to the target-distance estimate.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\HolderContinuityUp$, $\MetricUp$, $\RealUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\ModulusContinuityUp$, $\UniformModulusUp$, $\LipschitzMapUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\HolderContinuityUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $X,Y,G,\alpha,K,M,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for a host real power function, unrestricted exponent calculus, ambient metric completion, quotient metric equality, selected limit, choice sequence, or a continuity principle detached from the displayed graph.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Holder continuity NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:holder-continuity-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\HolderContinuityUp$ carrier $Q=(X,Y,G,\alpha,K,M,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\HolderContinuityUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the source and target $\MetricUp$ rows, $\ContinuousMapUp$ graph row, rational exponent row, $\RealUp$ coefficient ledger, induced modulus row, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item exponent and coefficient stability, requiring $\alpha$ and $K$ to be transported only componentwise and to remain attached to the same graph row $G$;
\item power-law metric-bound read, requiring every accepted source-distance request to read the target-distance estimate through $X,Y,G,\alpha,K$ before any modulus row is exported;
\item induced modulus handoff, requiring $M$ to be consumed as the displayed $\ModulusContinuityUp$ or $\UniformModulusUp$ row generated by the power-law budget;
\item transport and replay, requiring $H$ and $C$ to preserve the same displayed dependency order under $\hsame$ transport and $\Cont$ replay;
\item ledger nonescape, excluding host real power functions, unrestricted exponent calculus, ambient metric completion, quotient metric equality, selected limits, choice sequences, and detached continuity principles as local certificate data.
\end{enumerate}
No Holder continuity obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:holder-continuity-carrier}. Carrier admission exposes exactly the two metric rows $X,Y$, the continuous graph row $G$, the exponent row $\alpha$, the coefficient ledger $K$, the induced modulus row $M$, and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

Exponent and coefficient stability are componentwise clauses. The row $\alpha$ is read only as the displayed rational exponent, while $K$ is read only as the Real coefficient ledger attached to the same graph row $G$.

The power-law metric-bound read is forced by the order of the rows. A source-distance request first reads the metric source $X$, then the graph row $G$, then the target metric row $Y$, and only then consults $\alpha$ and $K$ for the displayed power-law estimate.

The modulus handoff is the row $M$. Since $M$ is downstream of $X,Y,G,\alpha,K$, every $\ModulusContinuityUp$ or $\UniformModulusUp$ consumer receives the displayed power-law budget rather than an arbitrary continuity witness.

Finally, $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite packet. The excluded objects have no coordinate in the carrier, so they cannot become local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Holder continuity modulus handoff]
\label{thm:holder-continuity-modulus-handoff}
Every $\ModulusContinuityUp$ or $\UniformModulusUp$ consumer of an accepted $\HolderContinuityUp$ carrier reads the Holder packet only through $\MetricUp$, $\RealUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\ModulusContinuityUp$, $\UniformModulusUp$, $\LipschitzMapUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows. Equivalently, the consumer factors through the displayed route
$$
X,Y,G,\alpha,K \longmapsto M \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
The handoff exports no host real power function, unrestricted exponent calculus, ambient metric completion, quotient metric equality, selected limit, choice sequence, or detached continuity principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a modulus consumer of $Q$. By \autoref{def:holder-continuity-carrier}, the only modulus-facing coordinate is $M$, and $M$ is displayed only after the two metric rows, graph row, exponent row, and coefficient ledger have been read.

The first part of the route is analytic. The source and target distance classifiers come from $X$ and $Y$, the graph-facing continuity data come from $G$, the rational exponent comes from $\alpha$, and the Real coefficient and distance estimates come from $K$.

The second part of the route is the continuity handoff. The row $M$ is read as a $\ModulusContinuityUp$ or $\UniformModulusUp$ row, while the $\LipschitzMapUp$ dependency is visible only as the exponent-one boundary named in provenance, not as an independent replacement for the Holder packet.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve the same factorization under transport, replay, provenance, and local naming. Since every consumer-facing read has already passed through those rows, none of the excluded host-level or choice-level objects can be recovered from the packet.
\end{proof}
