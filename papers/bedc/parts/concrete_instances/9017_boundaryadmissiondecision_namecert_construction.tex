The $\BoundaryAdmissionDecisionUp$ packet realizes the admission-decision rule of \autoref{ch:visions-external-supply-without-axioms} as a finite NameCert object. It sits beside the external boundary packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-external-supply-boundary-namecert}, the router of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-supply-kind-router-namecert}, and the apophatic naming surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-apophaticname-namecert}. Those siblings expose a boundary, route a supply kind, or name an external position; this packet binds the decision row that chooses the strongest permitted non-axiom form for a proposed dependency.

\begin{definition}[BoundaryAdmissionDecision carrier]
\label{def:boundary-admission-decision-carrier}
A $\BoundaryAdmissionDecisionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
D=(X,F,R,A,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the proposed-dependency row, naming the object, operation, theorem, or interface component under admission;
\item $F$ is the strongest permitted non-axiom form row, choosing one of closed inductive syntax, computed definition, setup field, mature NameCert row, GAP ledger, bridge slot, or apophatic socket;
\item $R$ is the requested-shape row, recording the exact supply shape the consumer wants to read;
\item $A$ is the audit/refusal row, recording the local decision when the requested shape cannot be exported as internal substrate truth;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $X,F,R,A$;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay routes through the same admission rows;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ExternalSupplyBoundaryUp$, $\SupplyKindRouterUp$, $\ApophaticNameUp$, $\SocketKindClassifierUp$, $\AuditGateCompositionUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\BoundaryAdmissionDecisionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for an anonymous axiom, hidden host selector, detached consumer permission, quotient collapse, or supplied-content theorem.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[BoundaryAdmissionDecision NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:boundary-admission-decision-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:boundary-admission-decision-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\BoundaryAdmissionDecisionUp}$ surface consists exactly of five obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for $D=(X,F,R,A,H,C,P,N)$;
\item proposed-dependency exposure through $X$;
\item strongest permitted non-axiom form exactness through $F$;
\item requested-shape exactness through $R$;
\item audit/refusal exactness through $A$, with componentwise $\hsame$ stability, displayed $\Cont$ replay, package provenance, and local naming supplied by $H,C,P,N$.
\end{enumerate}
No consumer read of the certificate may bypass $F$ and reach $R$ as an admitted internal dependency.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the displayed carrier. The accepted packet lists $X,F,R,A,H,C,P,N$, so the local certificate cannot introduce a second dependency, hidden permitted form, detached requested shape, unrecorded refusal, transport row, replay route, provenance row, or local name.

Read the decision rows in order. The proposed-dependency row $X$ names the object under admission, and the chosen-form row $F$ displays the strongest permitted non-axiom form before any consumer reaches the requested-shape row $R$.

Use the audit/refusal row $A$. If the requested shape cannot be exported through $F$, the row $A$ records refusal rather than turning the request into an axiom, hidden selector, quotient collapse, or host permission.

Finally inspect $H,C,P,N$. These rows only transport, replay, package, and name the same finite decision packet. Therefore the NameCert obligations are exhausted by the five displayed obligation rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BoundaryAdmissionDecision router exhaustion]
\label{thm:boundary-admission-decision-router-exhaustion}
Every accepted $\BoundaryAdmissionDecisionUp$ packet forces a consumer route through $F$ before the requested-shape row $R$ can be read. If $F$ is absent, the route cannot distinguish a closed inductive form, computed definition, setup field, NameCert row, GAP ledger, bridge slot, or apophatic socket, and the admission decision is not auditable.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with carrier admission for $D=(X,F,R,A,H,C,P,N)$. The requested-shape row $R$ is part of the same finite packet as the chosen-form row $F$.

Apply the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:boundary-admission-decision-namecert-obligations}. They expose $F$ before any consumer reads $R$ as a decision result, so a route reaching $R$ without $F$ omits the row that records which non-axiom form carries the request.

The support rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve and name only the displayed packet. They cannot reconstruct an omitted chosen-form row or replace it by host convention. Hence router exhaustion fails when $F$ is absent.
\end{proof}
