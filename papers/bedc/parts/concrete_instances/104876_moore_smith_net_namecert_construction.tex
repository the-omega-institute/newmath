\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\MooreSmithNetUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-moore-smith-net-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Moore--Smith and Kelley nets, the $\MooreSmithNetUp$ packet records the finite directed-net carrier that completion and convergence chapters can inspect before any limit, quotient net, or ambient completeness assertion is named. It sits over the directed-index substrate of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-directedset-namecert}, the ordinary net-convergence route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-netconvergence-namecert}, the uniform-completion consumer of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformcompletion-namecert}, and the filter-base surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-filterbase-namecert}. The packet is a completion-side neighbour for the L10 directed evidence corridor; it supplies a finite net row, not a selected subnet, not a cofinal-choice operator, and not a host-level limit theorem.

\begin{definition}[Moore--Smith net carrier]\label{def:moore-smith-net-carrier}
A $\MooreSmithNetUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
M=(D,V,T,F,U,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the $\DirectedSetUp$ index surface, naming finite index vertices, displayed preorder comparisons, and upper-window witnesses consumed by every net read.
\item $V$ is the net-value window, recording the finite values visible at the indices named by $D$.
\item $T$ is the tail ledger, recording the finite late-index requests and the displayed upper-window witnesses that answer them.
\item $F$ is the $\FilterBaseUp$ handoff row obtained from the displayed tails of $D$ and $T$.
\item $U$ is the completion-facing consumer row for $\NetConvergenceUp$ and $\UniformCompletionUp$ reads that inspect the same directed tail surface.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the directed index row, value window, tail ledger, filter-base handoff, completion consumer row, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a net request through $D,V,T,F,U$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\MooreSmithNetUp$, $\DirectedSetUp$, $\NetConvergenceUp$, $\UniformCompletionUp$, $\FilterBaseUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MooreSmithNetUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exports only this finite directed-net surface. It has no coordinate for quotient nets, selected limits, cofinal choice functions, ambient completeness, or host equality of net values.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Moore--Smith net NameCert obligations]\label{thm:moore-smith-net-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\MooreSmithNetUp$ carrier $M=(D,V,T,F,U,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\MooreSmithNetUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the directed-index row, value window, tail ledger, filter-base handoff, completion consumer row, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item directed-tail coverage, requiring every late-index request to read an upper-window witness from $D$ and $T$ before any value in $V$ is consumed;
\item filter-base exposure, requiring the $\FilterBaseUp$ row $F$ to be assembled only from the displayed directed tails;
\item convergence and completion handoff, requiring $\NetConvergenceUp$ and $\UniformCompletionUp$ consumers to inspect the same $D,V,T,F$ route before reaching $U$;
\item ledger refusal, stating that quotient nets, selected limits, cofinal-choice functions, ambient completeness, and host equality are not packet coordinates.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier of \autoref{def:moore-smith-net-carrier}. Its analytic rows are $D,V,T,F,U$, and its structural rows are $H,C,P,N$.

Directed-tail coverage is read from $D$ and $T$. A consumer first names a displayed index request, then reads the upper-window witness in the directed set, and only then inspects the corresponding value window $V$.

The filter-base row $F$ is downstream of the same directed tails. Since $F$ has no independent source coordinate, the filter-facing consumer receives only the finite tail family already displayed by $D,T,V$.

The completion row $U$ is reached after this route. Componentwise transport through $H$ and replay through $C$ preserve the same rows, while $P$ and $N$ name them as the local certificate surface. No listed coordinate can export quotient nets, selected limits, cofinal choice, ambient completeness, or host equality.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Moore--Smith net uniform-completion handoff]\label{thm:moore-smith-net-uniform-completion-handoff}
Every $\UniformCompletionUp$ consumer of an accepted $\MooreSmithNetUp$ packet factors through the finite route
$$
\DirectedSetUp,\; V,\; T \longmapsto \FilterBaseUp \longmapsto \NetConvergenceUp \longmapsto \UniformCompletionUp .
$$
The handoff exposes directed indices, displayed value windows, finite tail requests, filter-base data, and convergence-facing replay only through $D,V,T,F,U,H,C,P,N$. It does not supply a quotient of nets, a selected completion point, a cofinal-choice function, host equality of values, or ambient uniform completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:moore-smith-net-namecert-obligations}. The directed-tail coverage row forces the consumer to read $D$ and $T$ before any value window can be used.

Next apply the filter-base exposure row. The handoff to $\FilterBaseUp$ is assembled from those displayed tails, so it cannot introduce an external filter, ultrafilter, or quotient of net tails.

The convergence and completion handoff row then sends the same finite route to $\NetConvergenceUp$ and $\UniformCompletionUp$ consumers. Transport and replay remain componentwise through $H$ and $C$, while $P,N$ record provenance and naming for the same packet. Thus the uniform-completion read is exhausted by the displayed directed-net carrier and cannot import selected limits, cofinal choice, host equality, or ambient completeness.
\end{proof}
