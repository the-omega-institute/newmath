\begin{definition}[OnticTower carrier]
\label{def:ontic-tower-carrier}
An $\OnticTowerUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
T=(O,A,S,R,B,L,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $O$ is the $\OnticStateUp$ source row naming the stable reality-facing signatures at the base of the tower.
\item $A$ is the $\RealityConstrainedApproximationTowerUp$ stage row, recording the accepted finite approximation stages.
\item $S$ is the $\RealityConstrainedSignatureFitUp$ classifier row, recording the physical-signature fit used at each stage.
\item $R$ is the $\ResidueUp$ ledger row, recording content preserved, withheld, or still unresolved between stages.
\item $B$ is the $\ObserverBudgetSupportUp$ observer-budget row, recording which observer-facing supports may read the stage data.
\item $L$ is the ledger-continuity row, recording each stage transition as preservation, refinement, or explicit correction of earlier signature rows.
\item $H$ is componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $O,A,S,R,B,L$.
\item $C$ is the displayed $\Cont$ replay route through the stage and ledger rows.
\item $P$ is $\Pkg$ provenance over $\OnticTowerUp$, the five cited sibling packets, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\OnticTowerUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for an imported limit object, hidden final ontology, host equality of physical states, quotient stream, countable-choice selector, or observer-independent access route.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[OnticTower classifier]
\label{def:ontic-tower-classifier}
Two accepted $\OnticTowerUp$ packets are classified as the same only when their $\OnticStateUp$ source rows agree through the same stage row, their $\RealityConstrainedSignatureFitUp$ classifiers preserve the same physical-signature comparisons, their $\ResidueUp$ ledgers record the same hidden and unresolved content, their $\ObserverBudgetSupportUp$ rows admit the same observer-facing reads, and their ledger-continuity rows identify the same sequence of preservation, refinement, and correction transitions under componentwise $\hsame$ transport. Agreement of a single terminal $\OnticStateUp$ row is not enough to classify tower packets.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[OnticTower stage refinement]
\label{thm:ontic-tower-stage-refinement}
Let $T=(O,A,S,R,B,L,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\OnticTowerUp$ packet. Every accepted transition between two tower stages factors through the displayed approximation row $A$, signature-fit row $S$, residue ledger $R$, observer-budget row $B$, and ledger-continuity row $L$. A transition that preserves only the terminal $\OnticStateUp$ source row $O$ while dropping any one of $A,S,R,B,L$ is not an accepted stage refinement.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the carrier of \autoref{def:ontic-tower-carrier}. The stage data are not read as a bare source row; they are carried by $O,A,S,R,B,L$ together with transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

Use the classifier of \autoref{def:ontic-tower-classifier}. It compares packets by the joint source, approximation-stage, physical-signature, residue, observer-budget, and ledger-continuity rows, so a transition missing one of these rows cannot be classified as the same tower route.

The sibling chapters supply the individual surfaces: $\OnticStateUp$ supplies the source, $\RealityConstrainedApproximationTowerUp$ supplies indexed stage structure, $\RealityConstrainedSignatureFitUp$ supplies physical-signature comparison, $\ResidueUp$ supplies ledgered residue, and $\ObserverBudgetSupportUp$ supplies observer-facing support. Repacking them through $H,C,P,N$ gives the accepted refinement route and no omitted coordinate remains.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[OnticTower NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:ontic-tower-namecert-obligation-surface}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:ontic-tower-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\OnticTowerUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission, classifier exposure, stage-refinement preservation, ledger-continuity exposure, observer-budget support, componentwise transport, displayed replay, provenance, and local naming. It excludes imported tower limits, final total descriptions, quotient streams, selected host limits, host real equality, countable-choice selectors, and second ledger sources.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is the displayed tuple of \autoref{def:ontic-tower-carrier}. Classifier exposure is \autoref{def:ontic-tower-classifier}, which requires the joint source, stage, signature-fit, residue, support, and continuity rows.

Stage-refinement preservation is \autoref{thm:ontic-tower-stage-refinement}. The theorem forces every transition through the same finite packet before a later stage can be read as preserving or refining an earlier stage.

Ledger-continuity and observer support are visible rows of the carrier, and the remaining $H,C,P,N$ rows transport, replay, source, and name the packet. Since the carrier has no additional coordinate, the excluded limit, selector, quotient, host-equality, and second-ledger surfaces cannot be exported from the accepted NameCert.
\end{proof}
