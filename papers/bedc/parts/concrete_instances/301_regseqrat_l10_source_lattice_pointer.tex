\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat L10 source lattice pointer]
\label{thm:regseqrat-l10-source-lattice-pointer}
Every accepted $\RegSeqRatUp$ carrier consumes the finite observation schedules
of $\StreamNameUp$ from
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert} and the endpoint and
radius ledgers of $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ from
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert} before it can be
consumed by the $\RealUp$ seal surface of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The source lattice is therefore
the visible route
$$
\StreamNameUp,\ \DyadicRatCoreUp \longrightarrow \RegSeqRatUp \longrightarrow \RealUp .
$$
Along this route the accepted packet carries only scheduled finite stream
windows, dyadic endpoint rows, dyadic radius rows, finite dyadic-distance
ledgers, regularity ledgers, classifier rows, $\hsame$ comparisons, displayed
$\Cont$ routes, $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ rows. It
does not expose quotient streams, host real equality, selected limits, ambient
completeness, or a hidden completion carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the carrier definition in \autoref{def:regseqrat-stream-carrier}.
Its stored coordinates are the scheduled finite windows supplied by
$\StreamNameUp$ together with the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint and radius rows
attached to those windows.

Next read the classifier in \autoref{def:regseqrat-classifier}. The classifier
adds only finite dyadic-tolerance rows over the same scheduled observations:
endpoint rows, radius rows, finite distance ledgers, regularity rows, and the
displayed provenance needed to transport them.

The exact handoff theorem
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-streamname-dyadic-real-handoff-exactness} identifies
the complete route from the stream-name schedule and dyadic ledgers through
the $\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier to the $\RealUp$ seal rows. Applying
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-real-seal-handoff} then shows that a seal consumer
reads this regular-rational source surface itself.

Since all cited surfaces are finite BEDC rows, no quotient stream, host real
equality, selected limit, ambient completeness datum, or hidden completion
carrier can be consumed along the route.
\end{proof}

Finally use the Real seal handoff rows. They make the $\RealUp$ seal the terminal row of the same route, so the ownership list is exhausted by the four displayed chapters and cannot be enlarged by quotient streams, host equality, selected limits, completion witnesses, or ambient completeness.

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat L10 row owner noncollapse]
\label{thm:regseqrat-l10-row-owner-noncollapse}
Every accepted L10 handoff from $\StreamNameUp$ and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ through
$\RegSeqRatUp$ to the public $\RealUp$ seal has four pairwise noncollapsible
row owners. The schedule rows belong to $\StreamNameUp$; the endpoint,
radius, and finite dyadic-distance ledgers belong to $\DyadicRatCoreUp$; the
regularity, classifier, and transport rows belong to $\RegSeqRatUp$; and the
terminal seal row belongs to $\RealUp$.

Removing any owner, or identifying any owner with another, breaks the accepted
finite route or introduces one of the excluded coordinates: quotient streams,
host real equality, selected limits, completion witnesses, ambient
completeness data, or choice data.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The source lattice pointer
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-l10-source-lattice-pointer} fixes the visible route
from $\StreamNameUp$ and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ through $\RegSeqRatUp$ to
$\RealUp$. The source lattice exhaustion theorem
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-l10-source-lattice-exhaustion} assigns the route rows
to exactly these four owners and excludes off-route owners.

The exact StreamName--dyadic--Real handoff
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-streamname-dyadic-real-handoff-exactness} then reads
the schedule rows, dyadic endpoint and radius rows, dyadic-distance ledgers,
regularity rows, classifier rows, transport rows, and public seal row as one
finite route. Thus the four owners are not alternative names for one store:
each contributes a different face of the displayed packet.

Apply the four-face necessity theorem
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-l10-four-face-necessity}. If the schedule owner is
removed, there is no finite source index; if the dyadic owner is removed,
there are no endpoint, radius, or distance ledgers; if the RegSeqRat owner is
removed, the classifier and regularity rows disappear; and if the Real owner
is removed, the public seal has no terminal row. Identifying any two owners
therefore erases one of these required faces rather than preserving the same
accepted route.

Finally use finite-window saturation,
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-real-seal-finite-window-saturation}. It says that the
accepted Real seal exit is already saturated by the four displayed finite
faces. A collapse of owners can only be repaired by importing an off-route
coordinate, and those coordinates are precisely the quotient streams, host
real equality, selected limits, completion witnesses, ambient completeness
data, and choice data excluded by the handoff.
\end{proof}
