\begin{theorem}[Dense isometry extension NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:dense-isometry-extension-namecert-obligations}

$$

$$
Thus the extension is read as finite NameCert data over the dense source and its completion route, not as a host-level function on a quotient completion.

Next use the universal-property row $U$. It repacks those displayed observations as an extension route, while the separated comparison row $S$ supplies the zero-distance classifier boundary needed for uniqueness of the extended isometry.

Finally transport and replay are confined to $H$ and $C$, with $P$ and $N$ recording provenance and local naming. Since the carrier has no coordinate for a quotient completion, selected representative, host function equality, ambient completeness theorem, or choice schedule, every accepted extension consumer is forced through the displayed finite route.
\end{proof}

The handoff is therefore a finite completion-extension service. It exports no quotient completion equality, selected host limit, host function equality, countable-choice schedule, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness theorem, or bridge-checking credential.

Therefore the completion handoff is exactly the stated finite route, with no coordinate from which a quotient completion, selected limit, host function, choice schedule, ambient completeness theorem, or bridge credential could be read.
\end{proof}
