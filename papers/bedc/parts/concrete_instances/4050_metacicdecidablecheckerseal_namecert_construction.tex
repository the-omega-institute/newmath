The $\MetacicDecidableCheckerSealUp$ packet is the concrete NameCert surface drawn from the decidability audit map of \autoref{ch:metacic-decidable-audit-map}. It packages the checked typing-inference rows, the same-term decision rows, and the bounded normal-equality rows as one finite consumer-facing seal. The packet sits beside the normal-form carriers of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-typedreductionnormalform-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuationtracenormalform-namecert}: those chapters expose terminal normal-form readback, while this chapter exposes the decidable checker rows that may be safely consumed without claiming a global closed-term conversion decider.

\begin{definition}[Metacic decidable checker seal carrier]
\label{def:metacic-decidable-checker-seal-carrier}
A $\MetacicDecidableCheckerSealUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
D=(E,T,S,N,H,C,P,L)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $E$ is the structural term-equality row for encoded MetaCIC syntax;
\item $T$ is the typing-inference row, including the checked soundness and completeness surfaces for the encoded \texttt{HasType} relation;
\item $S$ is the same-term checker row for the displayed syntax layer;
\item $N$ is the bounded normal-equality row, including the finished-normalisation consequences recorded by the audit map;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $E,T,S,N$, route data, provenance, and local naming;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which a consumer reads structural equality, typing inference, same-term checking, and bounded normal equality;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, $\BMark$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, $\NameCert$, and the MetaCIC decidability audit rows named in \autoref{ch:metacic-decidable-audit-map};
\item $L$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MetacicDecidableCheckerSealUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no row for unrestricted closedness decidability, no row for global closed-term beta-conversion decidability, and no row turning bounded normal equality into an unbounded conversion theorem.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Metacic decidable checker seal NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:metacic-decidable-checker-seal-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\MetacicDecidableCheckerSealUp$ carrier
$$
D=(E,T,S,N,H,C,P,L),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\MetacicDecidableCheckerSealUp}$ surface consists exactly of structural term-equality admission through $E$, typing-inference soundness and completeness through $T$, same-term checking through $S$, bounded normal-equality soundness through $N$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, package provenance through $P$, and local naming through $L$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:metacic-decidable-checker-seal-carrier}. Its checker coordinates are exactly $E,T,S,N$, so the visible decision surface is structural equality, typing inference, same-term checking, and bounded normal equality.

The transport row $H$ preserves those four coordinates componentwise. The continuation row $C$ supplies the only routes by which a downstream consumer may read them, and those routes end inside the same packet.

Package provenance through $P$ records the audit-map dependency, while the local row $L$ names the packet as a $\NameCert_{\MetacicDecidableCheckerSealUp}$ surface. Since the carrier has no further checker coordinate, the NameCert obligations are exactly the displayed finite rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Metacic decidable checker seal non-escape]
\label{thm:metacic-decidable-checker-seal-non-escape}
Every downstream consumer of an accepted $\MetacicDecidableCheckerSealUp$ carrier factors through
$$
E,T,S,N,H,C,P,L.
$$
The consumer may read structural term equality, typing-inference soundness and completeness, same-term checking, bounded normal-equality soundness, componentwise transports, displayed continuation routes, provenance, and the local naming row. It cannot read unrestricted closedness decidability, global closed-term beta-conversion decidability, a confluence theorem, a subject-reduction theorem, or an external normalizer from the checker seal.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the displayed continuation row $C$. It names consumer routes only for $E,T,S,N$ and has no endpoint for a closedness decider, a global conversion decider, confluence, subject reduction, or an external normalizer.

Next use componentwise transport through $H$. Transport moves the same four checker rows and cannot manufacture a theorem coordinate absent from the carrier.

Finally package the result through $P$ and $L$. Provenance and local naming certify the finite checker surface described by \autoref{ch:metacic-decidable-audit-map}, while the missing coordinates rule out escape beyond $E,T,S,N,H,C,P,L$.
\end{proof}
