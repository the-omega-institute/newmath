It sits beside the finite observation route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finite-observation-interface-namecert}, the closed observation packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closedobservationsystem-namecert}, the observation conservation ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observation-conservation-ledger-namecert}, and the runtime checkpoint packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-reflection-runtime-checkpoint-namecert}. \begin{definition}[ObservationReflectionPacket carrier]
\label{def:observation-reflection-packet-carrier}
An $\ObservationReflectionPacketUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(S,H,\Sigma,C,P,L,E,T,R,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the source trace row, recording the reality-facing or MetaCIC-facing source access that starts the inquiry;
\item $H$ is the finite $\BHist$ carrier of observations, generated traces, and replayable inquiry events;
\item $\Sigma$ is the signature extraction row, listing the finite signature read from $S$ through $H$;
\item $C$ is the classifier row comparing the extracted signature with the displayed model, package, or checkpoint claim;
\item $P$ is the package row naming the finite $\Pkg$ provenance for the source, trace, signature, classifier, ledger, and route rows;
\item $L$ is the ledger row recording accepted matches, open gaps, rejected identity claims, and conservation constraints;
\item $E$ is the open-continuation row, recording the prediction horizon or open meta-continuation that remains after the finite certificate is read;
\item $T$ is the transport row carrying componentwise $\hsame$ comparisons between the observation side and the reflection side;
\item $R$ is the route row of displayed $\Cont$ consumers through $S,H,\Sigma,C,P,L,E,T$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ObservationReflectionPacketUp}$ row naming exactly this packet.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for an ontological identity between physical reality and MetaCIC, no final truth oracle, and no host-level physical law beyond the finite inquiry rows shown above.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[ObservationReflectionPacket NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:observation-reflection-packet-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:observation-reflection-packet-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\ObservationReflectionPacketUp}$ surface consists exactly of six finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed packet $Q=(S,H,\Sigma,C,P,L,E,T,R,N)$;
\item source-trace exactness, requiring every inquiry read to start from $S$ and pass through the finite history row $H$;
\item signature-classifier fit, requiring every accepted comparison to expose $\Sigma$ and $C$ before any package read;
\item package and ledger exactness, requiring $P$ and $L$ to name the finite provenance, accepted matches, open gaps, and rejected identity claims;
\item open-continuation preservation, requiring $E$ to remain an open continuation rather than a final oracle;
\item transport, route, and local naming closure through $T,R,N$, requiring all observation-side and reflection-side reads to remain inside the displayed packet.
\end{enumerate}
Thus the observation--reflection thesis is certified as a finite role correspondence and not as an identity of source domains.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:observation-reflection-packet-carrier}. The ten rows $S,H,\Sigma,C,P,L,E,T,R,N$ are the complete finite packet visible to a consumer.

Source-trace exactness uses the first two rows. The source row $S$ records the inquiry access point, while $H$ records only the finite trace available from that source. This matches the finite-trace side of \autoref{ch:visions-reality-constrained-approximation-towers} and the finite observation interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finite-observation-interface-namecert}.

Signature-classifier fit is then read through $\Sigma$ and $C$. The packet does not classify a hidden source directly; it classifies only the displayed signature extracted from the finite trace. Package and ledger exactness attach $P$ and $L$ to that same read, so accepted matches, open gaps, and rejected identity claims remain part of the local NameCert packet.

The open-continuation row $E$ records the non-final horizon. It agrees with the reflection checkpoint boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-reflection-runtime-checkpoint-namecert}: checkpoint and inquiry rows can be inspected, but neither side exports total truth or host validation from the finite packet.

Finally $T,R,N$ transport, route, and name the same rows. Since those rows add no source-domain equality, final oracle, or host physical-law coordinate, the six obligations exhaust the local certificate surface.
\end{proof}

\label{thm:observation-reflection-packet-nonescape}

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

Apply the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:observation-reflection-packet-namecert-obligations}. Every legal read starts with source-trace exactness, passes through signature-classifier fit, and then records package provenance, ledger status, and open continuation before transport and local naming.

Thus any demand for source-domain identity, final truth, host physical law, unbounded observation, or runtime validation has no coordinate in the accepted packet. It is recorded as a non-exported demand at the ledger or continuation boundary rather than as a readable row.
