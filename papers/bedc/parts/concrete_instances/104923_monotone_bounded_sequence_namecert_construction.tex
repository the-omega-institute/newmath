Following Bishop and Bridges constructive analysis, the $\MonotoneBoundedSequenceUp$ packet records the finite carrier surface for a monotone sequence of constructive real names together with an explicit boundedness ledger. It sits between the $\RealUp$ rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the bounded sequence discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-boundedrealsequence-namecert}, the limit interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-sequence-limit-namecert}, the tail-modulus handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-convergence-modulus-namecert}, and the located monotone convergence route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-located-monotone-convergence-namecert}. The packet names the data available before a constructive monotone-convergence consumer asks for a limit; it does not smuggle a completed limit into the source carrier.

\begin{definition}[MonotoneBoundedSequence carrier]
\label{def:monotone-bounded-sequence-carrier}
A $\MonotoneBoundedSequenceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
M=(S,W,R,O,B,T,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the $\RealUp$ source-row family for the sequence values, read only through the finite sequence schedule admitted by the packet.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window ledger recording the inspected indices and observation windows for each displayed sequence value.
\item $R$ is the real-readback row that ties each scheduled observation in $W$ to the corresponding $\RealUp$ source row in $S$.
\item $O$ is the monotonicity ledger, listing the finite comparison rows by which the packet certifies that later scheduled values do not move below earlier scheduled values.
\item $B$ is the boundedness ledger, read through the $\BoundedRealSequenceUp$ surface, which records the common real bound and the finite comparisons keeping every inspected source row below that bound.
\item $T$ is the $\CauchyConvergenceModulusUp$ handoff row exposed to downstream convergence consumers when the monotonicity and boundedness ledgers have been inspected together.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the source rows, stream windows, real readbacks, monotonicity ledger, boundedness ledger, modulus handoff, replay route, provenance, and local naming row.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a convergence request through $S,W,R,O,B,T,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\MonotoneBoundedSequenceUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BoundedRealSequenceUp$, $\RealSequenceLimitUp$, $\CauchyConvergenceModulusUp$, $\LocatedMonotoneConvergenceUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MonotoneBoundedSequenceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through
$$
S,W,R,O,B,T,H,C,P,N.
$$
It has no coordinate for quotient limits, countable choice, trichotomy, selected representatives, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[MonotoneBoundedSequence NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:monotone-bounded-sequence-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\MonotoneBoundedSequenceUp$ carrier
$$
M=(S,W,R,O,B,T,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\MonotoneBoundedSequenceUp}$ surface consists exactly of the finite rows for carrier admission, source-row scheduling, stream-window readback, monotonicity-ledger admission, boundedness-ledger admission, $\CauchyConvergenceModulusUp$ handoff, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming. Each obligation reads the same $\RealUp$ source rows and the same $\StreamNameUp$ windows before the boundedness ledger or modulus handoff is exposed. No obligation may appeal to quotient limits, countable choice, trichotomy, selected representatives, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier in \autoref{def:monotone-bounded-sequence-carrier}. Carrier admission exposes precisely the rows $S,W,R,O,B,T,H,C,P,N$, so every source value is read as a scheduled $\RealUp$ row through the displayed stream windows.

The order-facing rows are then forced to use the same source schedule. The monotonicity ledger $O$ compares only scheduled values from $S,W,R$, while the boundedness ledger $B$ is the $\BoundedRealSequenceUp$ read of the same scheduled source rows against a common bound.

The convergence-facing handoff comes last. The $\CauchyConvergenceModulusUp$ row $T$ may be exposed only after the monotonicity and boundedness ledgers have both been inspected, and the $\RealSequenceLimitUp$ and $\LocatedMonotoneConvergenceUp$ consumers can read only that displayed handoff.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same packet. Since the excluded quotient, choice, trichotomy, representative-selection, and ambient-completeness coordinates do not occur in the carrier, they cannot occur among the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MonotoneBoundedSequence finite limit boundary]
\label{thm:monotone-bounded-sequence-finite-limit-boundary}
Let
$$
M=(S,W,R,O,B,T,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\MonotoneBoundedSequenceUp$ carrier. Every $\RealSequenceLimitUp$ or $\LocatedMonotoneConvergenceUp$ consumer of $M$ must first read the $\RealUp$ source rows, $\StreamNameUp$ windows, monotonicity ledger, boundedness ledger, $\CauchyConvergenceModulusUp$ handoff, $\hsame$ transport, $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ through the same finite surface. The consumer receives a limit-boundary route for monotone bounded data, not a quotient limit, countable-choice-selected tail, trichotomy decision, selected representative, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a limit-facing consumer. By \autoref{def:monotone-bounded-sequence-carrier}, the only available sequence data are the scheduled $\RealUp$ source rows $S$, their $\StreamNameUp$ windows $W$, and the readback row $R$.

Apply \autoref{thm:monotone-bounded-sequence-namecert-obligations}. The monotonicity ledger $O$ and boundedness ledger $B$ are attached to that same scheduled source surface, and the modulus row $T$ is available only after both ledgers have been displayed.

The $\RealSequenceLimitUp$ route may therefore inspect a finite tail-boundary surface, and the $\LocatedMonotoneConvergenceUp$ route may inspect the same bounded monotone handoff before reaching its located-supremum data. Neither route can replace the packet by a completed real limit or a host-level sequence theorem.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve this boundary under transport, replay, provenance, and naming. The absent quotient-limit, choice, trichotomy, representative, and ambient-completeness coordinates remain absent for every downstream consumer.
\end{proof}
