\begin{theorem}[Category empty morphism between one-headed endpoints]
\label{thm:category-hom-carrier-e1-source-e1-target-empty-morphism-iff}

$$
\mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier}(\Eone(a),\Eone(r),\emp)

$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

empty morphism. The visible morphism branch contradicts constructor
no-confusion, and the empty branch exposes unary membership of $a$ and

carriers, the tail classifier lifts through $\Eone$, and empty-identity
exactness rebuilds the hom-carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Category empty one-headed endpoint consumer determinacy]
\label{cor:category-empty-e1-endpoint-consumer-determinacy}
For histories $a,r,x,b,c,f,g,k,l',m':\BHist$, suppose the nonempty composite
surface fixes the displayed factor rows
$$
\begin{aligned}
&\mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier}(x,b,f)\land
\mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier}(b,c,g)\land
\ContR(f,g,\Eone(k))\\
&\quad\land (f\hsame\emp\to\mathsf{False})\land
(g\hsame\emp\to\mathsf{False})\land f=\Eone(l')\land g=\Eone(m').
\end{aligned}
$$
Then the empty morphism between the one-headed endpoints has the single
chapter exactness decision
$$
\mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier}(\Eone(a),\Eone(r),\emp)
\Longleftrightarrow
\UnaryHistory(a)\land\HSame(a,r),
$$
and the nonempty visible-composite readback has the forced finite factor route
$$
\exists u,v:\BHist.\;
b=\Eone(u)\land c=\Eone(v)\land
\mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier}(x,u,l')
$$
$$
{}\land
\mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier}(\Eone(u),v,m')\land
\ContR(\Eone(l'),\Eone(m'),\Eone(k)).
$$
Consequently, once these rows are fixed, every accepted consumer-facing route,
normal-form read, or exactness decision for the empty one-headed endpoint and
its attached nonempty visible composite factors through the same listed rows:
the empty row supplies only $\UnaryHistory(a)$ and $\HSame(a,r)$, while
the nonempty row supplies only the displayed tails $l',m'$ and the recovered
endpoints $u,v$. There is no alternate hidden coordinate and no duplicate
classifier source for this packet.
\end{corollary}
\begin{proof}
The empty endpoint part is exactly
\autoref{thm:category-hom-carrier-e1-source-e1-target-empty-morphism-iff}.
It reads the carrier
$\mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier}(\Eone(a),\Eone(r),\emp)$ as unary membership of
the exposed source tail together with the endpoint classifier $\HSame(a,r)$,
and it provides the converse reconstruction from precisely those two rows.
Thus the empty morphism exposes no visible tail, middle endpoint, or second
classifier coordinate.

For the nonempty visible-composite part, apply
\autoref{thm:category-comp-nonempty-e1-morphism-factor-tail-unique} to the
fixed hom-carrier rows, the fixed continuation row, the two internal
nonemptiness assumptions, and the displayed identities $f=\Eone(l')$ and
$g=\Eone(m')$. The theorem returns histories $u$ and $v$ with
$b=\Eone(u)$ and $c=\Eone(v)$, together with the descended carrier rows
$\mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier}(x,u,l')$ and
$\mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier}(\Eone(u),v,m')$, and the visible composite
continuation $\ContR(\Eone(l'),\Eone(m'),\Eone(k))$.

These are exactly the route and readback rows displayed in the conclusion. An
accepted consumer use that keeps the hypotheses fixed cannot choose a different
empty exactness decision without contradicting the first iff, and cannot choose
different visible factor tails without contradicting the factor-tail uniqueness
readback, because the displayed equalities have already identified the exposed
tails with $l'$ and $m'$. Hence every accepted use factors through the listed
finite rows and no unlisted coordinate remains available.
\end{proof}
