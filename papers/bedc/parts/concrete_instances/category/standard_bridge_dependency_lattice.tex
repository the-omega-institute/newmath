\begin{proposition}[Category standard bridge dependency lattice]
\label{prop:category-standard-bridge-dependency-lattice}
The $\CategoryUp$ Hom carrier, composition row, identity row, and checked
standard bridge surface are the shared substrate consumed by
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-functor-namecert} and
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-nattrans-namecert}. Their downstream reads use
the category rows as dependency anchors: functor and natural-transformation
certificates receive the displayed $\mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier}$ arrows,
empty-tail identities, continuation composition ledger, and the
$\mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier\_comp\_result\_semanticNameCert}$ bridge-facing
certificate, rather than a host-category object, host-category equality, or
external morphism primitive.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
The CategoryUp certificate in
\autoref{thm:category-unary-continuation-stability-law-package} supplies the
identity, composition, associativity, unit, and endpoint-transport rows over
the unary-continuation Hom carrier. The standard bridge of
\autoref{thm:category-standard-unary-continuation-bridge} reads the same
surface through $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$, $\mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier}$, and
$\hsame$, and the closure block of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-category-namecert} records this package at
$\matureClosure$ with $\bridgeCheckedV$ status.

The FunctorUp and NatTransUp chapters cite the CategoryUp chapter by
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-functor-namecert} and
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-nattrans-namecert}. Their certificate rows
therefore attach to the displayed object, Hom, identity, composition, and
bridge-facing semantic-certificate rows already exported by CategoryUp. No
downstream clause needs a host-category carrier or primitive host equality:
the dependency lattice is the BEDC row package itself, and the bridge reads
that package without replacing it.
\end{proof}
