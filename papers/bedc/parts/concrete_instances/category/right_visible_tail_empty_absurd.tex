\begin{theorem}[Continuation morphism composite right visible tail empty absurd]
\label{thm:continuation-morphism-comp-right-visible-tail-empty-absurd}
For histories $a,b,c,m$, let $left:\mathsf{ContinuationMorphism}(a,b)$ and
$right:\mathsf{ContinuationMorphism}(b,c)$. If the right displayed tail is a
visible constructor, then the canonical composite displayed tail cannot be
history-same to $\emp$:
$$
\hsame(\mathsf{tail}(right),\mathsf{e0}(m))
\Longrightarrow
\hsame(\mathsf{tail}(left\circ right),\emp)\Longrightarrow\mathsf{False}
$$
and
$$
\hsame(\mathsf{tail}(right),\mathsf{e1}(m))
\Longrightarrow
\hsame(\mathsf{tail}(left\circ right),\emp)\Longrightarrow\mathsf{False}.
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack both continuation morphism records. The composite tail is the kernel
append of the left displayed tail with the right displayed tail. If this append
were empty, append-empty inversion would force the right displayed tail itself
to be $\emp$. This contradicts constructor separation for either
$\mathsf{e0}(m)$ or $\mathsf{e1}(m)$.
\end{proof}
