\begin{theorem}[Continuation morphism composite right visible tail empty absurd]
\label{thm:continuation-morphism-comp-right-visible-tail-empty-absurd}
For histories $a,b,c,m$, let $left:\mathsf{ContinuationMorphism}(a,b)$ and
$right:\mathsf{ContinuationMorphism}(b,c)$. If the right displayed tail is a
visible constructor, then the canonical composite displayed tail cannot be
history-same to $\emp$:
$$
\hsame(\mathsf{tail}(right),\mathsf{e0}(m))
\Longrightarrow
\hsame(\mathsf{tail}(left\circ right),\emp)\Longrightarrow\mathsf{False}
$$
and
$$
\hsame(\mathsf{tail}(right),\mathsf{e1}(m))
\Longrightarrow
\hsame(\mathsf{tail}(left\circ right),\emp)\Longrightarrow\mathsf{False}.
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack both continuation morphism records. The composite tail is the kernel
append of the left displayed tail with the right displayed tail. If this append
were empty, append-empty inversion would force the right displayed tail itself
to be $\emp$. This contradicts constructor separation for either
$\mathsf{e0}(m)$ or $\mathsf{e1}(m)$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CategoryUp.ContinuationMorphism\_comp\_right\_visible\_tail\_empty\_absurd}

\begin{theorem}[Continuation morphism right visible tail target not empty]
\label{thm:continuation-morphism-right-visible-tail-target-not-empty}
For histories $b,c,m$ and a continuation morphism
$right:\mathsf{ContinuationMorphism}(b,c)$, if the displayed tail of $right$ is
one-headed, then the target cannot be history-same to $\emp$:
$$
\hsame(\mathsf{tail}(right),\mathsf{e1}(m))
\Longrightarrow
\hsame(c,\emp)
\Longrightarrow
\mathsf{False}.
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the continuation morphism record. Its relation identifies $c$ with
$\mathsf{append}(b,\mathsf{e1}(m))$. If $c$ were history-same to $\emp$,
append-empty inversion would force $\mathsf{e1}(m)$ itself to be history-same to
$\emp$, contradicting one-headed constructor separation.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CategoryUp.ContinuationMorphism\_right\_visible\_tail\_target\_not\_empty}

\begin{theorem}[Continuation morphism zero-tail target not empty]
\label{thm:continuation-morphism-e0-tail-target-not-empty}
For histories $a,b,m$ and a continuation morphism
$morph:\mathsf{ContinuationMorphism}(a,b)$, if the displayed tail of $morph$
is zero-headed, then the target cannot be history-same to $\emp$:
$$
\hsame(\mathsf{tail}(morph),\mathsf{e0}(m))
\Longrightarrow
\hsame(b,\emp)
\Longrightarrow
\mathsf{False}.
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The zero-headed tail case exposes a zero-headed target presentation
$b=\mathsf{e0}(r)$ for some residual history $r$. If $b$ were history-same to
$\emp$, constructor separation would force $\mathsf{e0}(r)\hsame\emp$, which is
impossible.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Continuation morphism composite right visible bridge determinacy]
\label{lem:continuation-morphism-comp-right-visible-bridge-determinacy}
For histories $a,b,c,m$, let $left$ and $right$ be continuation morphisms from
$a$ to $b$ and from $b$ to $c$, respectively. Choose local history witnesses
$z,o,r_0,r_1,q$ such that $z$ is the zero-headed history with payload $m$, $o$
is the one-headed history with payload $m$, $r_0$ names the zero-headed right
displayed tail of $right$, $r_1$ names the one-headed right displayed tail of
$right$, and $q$ names the displayed tail of the composite $left\circ right$.
The consumer-facing surface for a composite whose right displayed tail is
visible is determined by the following finite local rows:
$$
\begin{gathered}
\hsame(r_0,z)
\Longrightarrow
\hsame(q,\emp)
\Longrightarrow \bot,\\
\hsame(r_1,o)
\Longrightarrow
\hsame(q,\emp)
\Longrightarrow \bot,\\
\hsame(r_1,o)
\Longrightarrow
\hsame(c,\emp)
\Longrightarrow \bot.
\end{gathered}
$$
Consequently every consumer-facing use of the composite-tail empty coordinate
is certified by either the zero-headed or one-headed composite-tail refusal row
of \autoref{thm:continuation-morphism-comp-right-visible-tail-empty-absurd},
and the one-headed target case is certified by
\autoref{thm:continuation-morphism-right-visible-tail-target-not-empty}. No
consumer-facing bridge coordinate is available for a detached empty composite
tail, a detached empty target, a hidden payload classifier, an independent
left-tail inspection, host equality of composites, or an off-packet boundary.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
The visible right-tail packet has two displayed constructor branches, and the
chosen witnesses expose those branches as bound history names. In the
zero-headed branch, the available row is
$$
\hsame(r_0,z).
$$
The first projection of
\autoref{thm:continuation-morphism-comp-right-visible-tail-empty-absurd}, read
with witness $r_0$ for the right displayed tail, witness $z$ for its
zero-headed shape, and witness $q$ for the composite displayed tail, is exactly
the refusal
$$
\hsame(q,\emp)
\Longrightarrow \bot.
$$
Thus any consumer use that tries to consume the zero-headed right-tail branch as
an empty composite tail is blocked by that local refusal. The row exposes no
target-empty conclusion and no additional payload coordinate.

In the one-headed branch, the available row is
$$
\hsame(r_1,o).
$$
The second projection of
\autoref{thm:continuation-morphism-comp-right-visible-tail-empty-absurd}, read
with witness $r_1$ for the right displayed tail, witness $o$ for its
one-headed shape, and witness $q$ for the composite displayed tail, is exactly
the composite-tail refusal
$$
\hsame(q,\emp)
\Longrightarrow \bot,
$$
so every consumer use of the one-headed branch as a composite-tail empty claim
is blocked by that row. The same one-headed right-tail row is also the exact
input of
\autoref{thm:continuation-morphism-right-visible-tail-target-not-empty}, which
produces the target-empty refusal
$$
\hsame(c,\emp)
\Longrightarrow \bot.
$$
Thus every one-headed target-empty claim is blocked by the cited
target-not-empty row.

These three implications exhaust the bridge surface named by the local rows.
The first two rows cover all visible right-tail cases leading to the
composite-tail empty obstruction, and the third row covers the only target-empty
obstruction available from the one-headed right-tail branch. None of the cited
rows has a premise or conclusion mentioning a detached empty composite-tail
coordinate, a detached empty target coordinate, a hidden payload classifier, an
independent left-tail inspection, host equality of composites, or an off-packet
boundary. Such a coordinate therefore has no local row through which a consumer
can read the composite right-visible bridge.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Continuation morphism composite right visible consumer determinacy]
\label{cor:continuation-morphism-comp-right-visible-consumer-determinacy}
For histories $a,b,c,m$, let $left$ and $right$ be continuation morphisms from
$a$ to $b$ and from $b$ to $c$, respectively. Choose local history witnesses
$z,o,r_0,r_1,q$ as in
\autoref{lem:continuation-morphism-comp-right-visible-bridge-determinacy}. Once
the right-visible local rows
$$
\begin{gathered}
\hsame(r_0,z)
\Longrightarrow
\hsame(q,\emp)
\Longrightarrow \bot,\\
\hsame(r_1,o)
\Longrightarrow
\hsame(q,\emp)
\Longrightarrow \bot,\\
\hsame(r_1,o)
\Longrightarrow
\hsame(c,\emp)
\Longrightarrow \bot
\end{gathered}
$$
are fixed, every accepted consumer-facing use of the composite right-visible
surface has the same chapter-named finite data. A composite-tail empty use must
factor through the zero-headed or one-headed refusal supplied by
\autoref{thm:continuation-morphism-comp-right-visible-tail-empty-absurd}; a
one-headed target-empty use must factor through
\autoref{thm:continuation-morphism-right-visible-tail-target-not-empty}; and
any normal form or exactness decision exposed to a consumer is one of those
three displayed refusals. Thus two accepted consumers over the same fixed rows
cannot choose different finite routes, different right-visible normal forms, or
different exactness decisions, and no accepted use can introduce a detached
empty composite tail, detached empty target, alternate hidden coordinate,
duplicate classifier source, independent left-tail inspection, host equality of
composites, or off-packet boundary.
\end{corollary}
\begin{proof}
Apply
\autoref{lem:continuation-morphism-comp-right-visible-bridge-determinacy}. The
lemma lists the complete consumer-facing bridge surface for a composite whose
right displayed tail is visible. Its first two rows are exactly the
zero-headed and one-headed routes from a right-tail constructor row to the
refusal of $\hsame(q,\emp)$, and those two routes are the two projections of
\autoref{thm:continuation-morphism-comp-right-visible-tail-empty-absurd}. Hence
any accepted consumer use that asks for an empty composite-tail coordinate has
one of these two finite routes as its normal form.

The same bridge determinacy lemma also identifies the only target-empty route
available from the visible right-tail packet. It is the one-headed row
$\hsame(r_1,o)$ followed by
\autoref{thm:continuation-morphism-right-visible-tail-target-not-empty}, which
refuses $\hsame(c,\emp)$. With the three displayed rows fixed, this fixes the
one-headed target-empty exactness decision as the third displayed refusal, and
there is no separate target-empty route in the zero-headed branch.

It remains to rule out competing consumer choices. The cited determinacy lemma
states that the bridge exports no detached empty composite-tail coordinate, no
detached empty target coordinate, no hidden payload classifier, no independent
left-tail inspection, no host equality of composites, and no off-packet
boundary. A duplicate classifier source or any further hidden coordinate would
therefore have to be an additional row outside the three displayed refusals.
Such a row is not a premise or conclusion of the local bridge surface and has
no factor through the cited tail-empty or target-not-empty theorems. It is
therefore not an accepted route, normal form, or exactness decision for this
composite right-visible surface.
\end{proof}
