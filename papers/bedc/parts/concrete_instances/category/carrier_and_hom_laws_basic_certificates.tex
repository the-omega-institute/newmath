\begin{theorem}[Category hom carrier identity semantic name certificate]
\label{thm:category-hom-carrier-identity-semantic-namecert}
For a unary object history $a$, the identity hom-carrier predicate
$f\mapsto\mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier}(a,a,f)$ carries a semantic naming
certificate whose source, pattern, and ledger predicates are this hom-carrier
predicate and whose classifier is $\hsame$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier witness is the empty history, using the empty identity hom-carrier
at $a$. The classifier laws are the reflexive, symmetric, and transitive laws
for $\hsame$. Carrier transport follows by transporting the concrete
hom-carrier witness across history sameness.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CategoryUp.CategoryHomCarrier\_identity\_semanticNameCert}

For displayed unary-continuation hom data
$\mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier}(a,b,f)$, the hom-carrier predicate

certificate whose source, pattern, and ledger predicates are this hom-carrier

are reflexivity, symmetry, and transitivity of $\hsame$. If a classified
morphism is transported across $\hsame$, endpoint histories stay fixed and
\autoref{thm:category-hom-carrier-hsame-transport} rebuilds the
hom-carrier witness. Pattern and ledger soundness are identities because the
three semantic predicates are the same hom-carrier predicate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Category hom carrier unary suffix semantic boundary]
\label{thm:category-hom-carrier-unary-suffix-semantic-boundary}
For a unary suffix $q$ and displayed hom carrier
$\mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier}(a,b,f)$, the suffixed hom-carrier predicate
$g\mapsto\mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier}(\mathsf{append}(a,q),\mathsf{append}(b,q),g)$
carries a semantic naming certificate with classifier $\hsame$. The displayed
endpoint is exact:
$$
\mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier}(\mathsf{append}(a,q),\mathsf{append}(b,q),f)
\Longleftrightarrow
\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(q)\land\mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier}(a,b,f).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The unary suffix equivalence rebuilds the displayed suffixed hom carrier from
$q$ and the base hom carrier. The existing hom-carrier semantic certificate is
then applied at the suffixed endpoints. The same unary suffix equivalence gives
the exact endpoint readback.
\end{proof}

h\mapsto \mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier}(a,c,h)\land h\hsame fg

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Composition closure gives the carrier witness at $fg$, and reflexivity gives
the displayed comparison $fg\hsame fg$. The classifier laws are the reflexive,
symmetric, and transitive laws for $\hsame$. Transport along a classifier proof
uses hom-carrier transport for the fixed endpoints $a,c$ and transitivity with
the inverse classifier proof to keep the displayed composite result aligned
with $fg$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Category hom carrier composite-result endpoint semantic name certificate]
\label{thm:category-hom-carrier-composite-result-endpoint-semantic-name-certificate}
For displayed hom carriers
$\mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier}(a,b,f)$ and
$\mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier}(b,c,g)$ together with $\ContR(f,g,fg)$, if
$a\hsame a'$ and $c\hsame c'$, then the transported predicate
$$
h\mapsto \mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier}(a',c',h)\land h\hsame fg

classifier is $\hsame$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Composition closure first gives the concrete composite hom carrier at
endpoints $(a,c)$ and result $fg$. Endpoint transport along $a\hsame a'$ and
$c\hsame c'$ moves this carrier to $(a',c')$, giving inhabitation at $fg$.
The classifier laws are reflexivity, symmetry, and transitivity of $\hsame$.
Carrier transport keeps endpoints fixed at $(a',c')$, transports the displayed
hom carrier along the classifier proof, and composes the inverse classifier
proof with the stored comparison to $fg$. Pattern and ledger soundness are
identities because all three semantic predicates coincide.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Category hom identity semantic name certificate bridge determinacy]
\label{lem:category-hom-identity-semantic-namecert-bridge-determinacy}
Let $a$ be a unary object history, and consider the identity hom-carrier naming
surface of \autoref{thm:category-hom-carrier-identity-semantic-namecert}. Any
consumer-facing use of this identity surface as a route, transport, readback, or
handoff factors through the following displayed local rows: the identity
hom-carrier predicate $f\mapsto\mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier}(a,a,f)$, the
classifier row $\hsame$, carrier transport for
$\mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier}$, and the continuation-source certificate row of
\autoref{thm:continuation-morphism-source-semantic-namecert} applied to the
continuation morphism encoded by the identity carrier. Thus an accepted consumer
cannot obtain a primitive host identity arrow, a second morphism classifier, an
off-carrier readback, a detached continuation source, or any unlisted bridge
coordinate from the identity name certificate.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
The identity certificate of
\autoref{thm:category-hom-carrier-identity-semantic-namecert} names exactly one
source, pattern, and ledger predicate, namely
$f\mapsto\mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier}(a,a,f)$, and it names exactly one
classifier, namely $\hsame$. Therefore every route that reads the identity
surface first lands in the displayed hom-carrier predicate over the fixed
endpoints $(a,a)$. The identity witness itself is the empty history supplied by
\autoref{thm:category-hom-carrier-empty-identity}, so the route has no separate
host identity coordinate beyond the BEDC carrier row.

A transport use is fixed by the same certificate. If the displayed morphism
history is changed only by $\hsame$, then
\autoref{thm:category-hom-carrier-hsame-transport} rebuilds the transported
hom-carrier witness over the same identity endpoints. This is the full
transport surface of the identity name certificate: the certificate has no
second classifier and no independent transport predicate outside
$\mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier}$ and $\hsame$.

A readback or handoff to continuation data also remains local. By the
hom-carrier and continuation-morphism tail correspondence of
\autoref{thm:category-hom-carrier-continuation-morphism-tail-correspondence}, a
read of $\mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier}(a,a,f)$ is exactly a unary endpoint and
unary tail presentation of a continuation morphism $m:\mathsf{ContinuationMorphism}(a,a)$
whose displayed tail is classified with $f$. Applying
\autoref{thm:continuation-morphism-source-semantic-namecert} to this displayed
continuation morphism shows that a consumer may read or transport the source
only through the source predicate
$src\mapsto\mathsf{Nonempty}(\mathsf{ContinuationMorphism}(src,a))$ and its
same classifier $\hsame$. Hence the continuation handoff is not an additional
bridge coordinate; it is the source row already determined by the identity
hom-carrier readback.

The listed rows exhaust the consumer-facing bridge. Routes are the fixed
identity hom-carrier predicate, transports are the $\hsame$ hom-carrier
transports, readbacks are the hom-carrier-to-continuation tail correspondence,
and continuation handoffs are the continuation-source semantic certificate for
the resulting morphism. Since none of these rows contains a primitive host
identity arrow, a second morphism classifier, an off-carrier readback, a
detached continuation source, or an extra bridge field, such a coordinate has no
row through which an accepted consumer could read the identity semantic name
certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Category hom identity semantic name certificate consumer determinacy]
\label{cor:category-hom-identity-semantic-namecert-consumer-determinacy}
Let $a$ be a unary object history, and fix the identity hom-carrier semantic
name certificate of \autoref{thm:category-hom-carrier-identity-semantic-namecert}.
Once the displayed identity rows are fixed, every accepted consumer-facing use
of this surface as a finite route, transport, readback normal form, or exactness
decision is determined by the same local data:
$$
\begin{gathered}
f\mapsto\mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier}(a,a,f),\qquad
\hsame,\\
\text{hom-carrier transport across }\hsame,\qquad
\text{the continuation-source row reached from the hom-carrier readback.}
\end{gathered}
$$
Thus two accepted consumers over these rows cannot choose different identity
routes, different morphism classifiers, different readback normal forms, or
different exactness decisions. Any proposed primitive host identity arrow,
off-carrier coordinate, detached continuation source, unlisted bridge row, or
duplicate classifier source is outside the accepted identity semantic name
surface.
\end{corollary}
\begin{proof}
The identity certificate in
\autoref{thm:category-hom-carrier-identity-semantic-namecert} fixes the
semantic name surface before any consumer reads it. Its source, pattern, and
ledger predicates are all the single predicate
$f\mapsto\mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier}(a,a,f)$, and its classifier is the single
history classifier $\hsame$. Therefore a consumer route has no independent
choice of carrier predicate or classifier: reading the identity name means
reading the displayed hom-carrier predicate over the fixed endpoints $(a,a)$.

Apply
\autoref{lem:category-hom-identity-semantic-namecert-bridge-determinacy}. It
states that every accepted route, transport, readback, or handoff through this
identity surface factors through exactly the displayed local rows: the identity
hom-carrier predicate, the classifier $\hsame$, hom-carrier transport for
$\mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier}$, and the continuation-source certificate obtained
from the continuation morphism encoded by the identity carrier. Hence the
consumer-facing finite route is not a further datum; it is the ordered use of
those rows.

The same factorization fixes the normal-form and exactness readings. A
transport read can only be the $\hsame$ hom-carrier transport over the fixed
identity endpoints. A readback can only pass through the hom-carrier to
continuation-tail correspondence named in the bridge-determinacy lemma, and its
continuation handoff can only use the continuation-source semantic certificate
for the resulting morphism. Consequently an exactness decision about the
identity name is a decision about membership in the displayed hom-carrier row
and its $\hsame$ readback, not a decision supplied by a second host arrow,
second classifier, or detached continuation coordinate.

Suppose two accepted consumers over the fixed rows produced different identity
routes, different readback normal forms, or different exactness decisions. The
difference would have to enter through a row not among the identity
hom-carrier predicate, $\hsame$, the hom-carrier transport row, or the
continuation-source row reached from the readback. But the bridge-determinacy
lemma excludes exactly those unlisted coordinates: primitive host identity
arrows, second morphism classifiers, off-carrier readbacks, detached
continuation sources, and extra bridge fields have no exported row in the
identity semantic name certificate. Thus every accepted consumer over the fixed
rows determines the same chapter-named finite route, normal-form readback, and
exactness decision.
\end{proof}
