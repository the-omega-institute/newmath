\begin{theorem}[Continuation morphism composite middle identity tail result]
\label{thm:continuation-morphism-comp-middle-identity-tail-result}
For continuation morphisms $left:a\to b$, $identity:b\to b$, and
$right:b\to c$, the middle identity tail is empty, and inserting it between
$left$ and $right$ leaves the composite tail history-same to the direct
composite:
$$
\HSame(identity.tail,\emp)\wedge
\HSame((left\circ identity)\circ right,\; left\circ right).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Compare $identity$ with the canonical empty continuation morphism at $b$ by
tail determinacy, giving $\HSame(identity.tail,\emp)$. Unfolding the two
composite tails reduces the comparison to append histories. The middle tail
is transported to $\emp$, and the right-unit law
$\mathsf{append}(left.tail,\emp)\hsame left.tail$ collapses the displayed
middle factor.
\end{proof}
