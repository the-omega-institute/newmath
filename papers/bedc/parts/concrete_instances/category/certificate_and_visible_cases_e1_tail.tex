\begin{theorem}[Continuation morphism one-headed source to one-headed target tail cases]
\label{thm:continuation-morphism-e1-source-e1-target-tail-cases}
For histories $a,r:\mathsf{BHist}$, every continuation morphism
$m:\mathsf{ContinuationMorphism}(\Eone(a),\Eone(r))$ has either the empty-tail
case with the exposed endpoints classified together,
$$
\mathsf{tail}(m)=\emp\land\HSame(a,r),
$$
or a one-headed tail whose payload still continues from $\Eone(a)$ to $r$:
$$
\exists k:\mathsf{BHist}.\;
\mathsf{tail}(m)=\Eone(k)\land \ContR(\Eone(a),k,r).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the displayed continuation tail. The empty-tail branch is the right-unit
continuation, so constructor injectivity exposes $\HSame(a,r)$. A zero-headed
tail would force a one-headed result to be zero-headed, contradicting
constructor disjointness. In the one-headed branch, constructor injectivity on
the continuation result removes the outer one constructor and leaves
$\ContR(\Eone(a),k,r)$.
\end{proof}
\leanvariant{BEDC.Derived.CategoryUp.ContinuationMorphism\_e1\_source\_e1\_target\_tail\_cases}

\begin{theorem}[Continuation morphism one-headed endpoints reject zero-headed tail]
\label{thm:continuation-morphism-e1-source-e1-target-tail-e0-absurd}
For histories $a,r,k:\mathsf{BHist}$ and
$m:\mathsf{ContinuationMorphism}(\Eone(a),\Eone(r))$,
$$
\HSame(\mathsf{tail}(m),\Ezero(k))\to\mathsf{False}.
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply the preceding one-headed endpoint tail cases. The empty-tail branch gives
$\emp\hsame\Ezero(k)$, and the visible branch gives
$\Eone(t)\hsame\Ezero(k)$ for some $t$; both contradict constructor separation.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CategoryUp.ContinuationMorphism\_e1\_source\_e1\_target\_tail\_e0\_absurd}

\begin{theorem}[Continuation morphism one-headed endpoints one-tail exactness]
\label{thm:continuation-morphism-e1-source-e1-target-tail-e1-iff}
For histories $a,r,k:\mathsf{BHist}$ and
$m:\mathsf{ContinuationMorphism}(\Eone(a),\Eone(r))$, the displayed tail is
one-headed with payload $k$ exactly when the payload continues from the
one-headed source to the target payload:
$$
\HSame(\mathsf{tail}(m),\Eone(k))
\Longleftrightarrow
\ContR(\Eone(a),k,r).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the forward direction, apply the one-headed endpoint tail cases. The
empty-tail branch contradicts the assumed one-headed tail comparison. In the
visible branch, the exposed one-headed tail payload and the assumed tail
comparison identify the two payloads, so continuation transport gives
$\ContR(\Eone(a),k,r)$. Conversely, step the displayed payload continuation
through the one constructor and compare it with the record continuation by
left cancellation.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CategoryUp.ContinuationMorphism\_e1\_source\_e1\_target\_tail\_e1\_iff}

\begin{lemma}[Continuation morphism one-headed endpoints bridge determinacy]
\label{lem:continuation-morphism-e1-source-e1-target-bridge-determinacy}
For histories $a,r:\BHist$ and a continuation morphism
$m:\mathsf{ContinuationMorphism}(\Eone(a),\Eone(r))$, the consumer-facing
bridge surface of $m$ is exhausted by the empty endpoint-collapse row and the
one-headed payload-continuation row:
$$
\bigl(\mathsf{tail}(m)=\emp\land\HSame(a,r)\bigr)
\lor
\exists k:\BHist.\;
\mathsf{tail}(m)=\Eone(k)\land\ContR(\Eone(a),k,r).
$$
Every zero-headed bridge coordinate is unavailable:
$$
\forall q:\BHist,\;
\HSame(\mathsf{tail}(m),\Ezero(q))\to\mathsf{False}.
$$
Consequently any accepted route, transport, readback, or handoff for this
one-headed endpoint packet factors either through the empty endpoint-collapse
row or through the one-headed payload-continuation row; no unlisted bridge
coordinate supplies a zero-headed tail, detached payload route, or alternate
bridge read.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:continuation-morphism-e1-source-e1-target-tail-cases} to
$m$. The first branch is exactly the empty endpoint-collapse row
$$
\mathsf{tail}(m)=\emp\land\HSame(a,r).
$$
It carries no payload coordinate, so any consumer use that reaches this branch
has only the endpoint transport supplied by $\HSame(a,r)$.

The second branch supplies a history $k:\BHist$ with
$$
\mathsf{tail}(m)=\Eone(k)\land\ContR(\Eone(a),k,r).
$$
This is the only payload-bearing local row exposed by the split, and its route
data is exactly the continuation ledger $\ContR(\Eone(a),k,r)$.

For zero-headed coordinates, fix $q:\BHist$. The refusal theorem
\autoref{thm:continuation-morphism-e1-source-e1-target-tail-e0-absurd} gives
$$
\HSame(\mathsf{tail}(m),\Ezero(q))\to\mathsf{False}.
$$
Thus a purported zero-headed route cannot be a consumer-facing bridge row for
the same packet.

These clauses exhaust the bridge surface. A route or readback that asks for the
displayed tail first passes through the case split, hence lands in one of the
two rows above. A transport in the empty row can only use the exposed endpoint
classifier, while a handoff in the visible row can only use the exposed
one-headed payload and its continuation ledger. The zero-headed coordinate is
refused by the absurd row, and the case split displays no further branch.
Therefore an unlisted bridge coordinate has no local row through which a
consumer could obtain a tail, payload, route, transport, readback, or handoff.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Continuation morphism one-headed endpoints consumer determinacy]
\label{cor:continuation-morphism-e1-source-e1-target-consumer-determinacy}
For histories $a,r:\BHist$ and a continuation morphism
$m:\mathsf{ContinuationMorphism}(\Eone(a),\Eone(r))$, once the two rows
exposed in \autoref{lem:continuation-morphism-e1-source-e1-target-bridge-determinacy}
are fixed, every accepted consumer-facing use of the same one-headed endpoint
packet is determined by the displayed finite split of
\autoref{thm:continuation-morphism-e1-source-e1-target-tail-cases}. Its normal
form is either the empty endpoint-collapse row
$$
\mathsf{tail}(m)=\emp\land\HSame(a,r),
$$
or a one-headed payload-continuation row
$$
\mathsf{tail}(m)=\Eone(k)\land\ContR(\Eone(a),k,r)
$$
for a displayed payload $k:\BHist$. A zero-headed normal form is impossible:
$$
\forall q:\BHist,\;
\HSame(\mathsf{tail}(m),\Ezero(q))\to\mathsf{False}.
$$
If two accepted one-headed uses expose payloads $k,k':\BHist$ with
$$
\mathsf{tail}(m)=\Eone(k)\land\ContR(\Eone(a),k,r)
$$
and
$$
\mathsf{tail}(m)=\Eone(k')\land\ContR(\Eone(a),k',r),
$$
then constructor injectivity gives the payload classifier $\HSame(k,k')$, and
both exactness decisions are the continuation rows displayed by the same tail
case. Consequently every accepted route, normal-form read, classifier
comparison, handoff, or exactness decision for $m$ factors through the empty
endpoint-collapse row or through that displayed one-headed
payload-continuation row; no alternate hidden coordinate, zero-headed route,
detached exactness decision, or duplicate classifier source is exported by this
chapter surface.
\end{corollary}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:continuation-morphism-e1-source-e1-target-tail-cases} to
$m$. The first branch gives exactly the empty endpoint-collapse row
$$
\mathsf{tail}(m)=\emp\land\HSame(a,r),
$$
so a consumer that reads the empty normal form obtains only the endpoint
classifier already displayed in that row. There is no payload coordinate in
this branch, hence no separate route or exactness decision can be selected
there.

In the second branch, the case split supplies a displayed payload $k:\BHist$
with
$$
\mathsf{tail}(m)=\Eone(k)\land\ContR(\Eone(a),k,r).
$$
Thus the one-headed normal form and the exactness decision are carried by the
same row: the payload is the one occurring in the displayed tail, and the
available continuation decision is precisely $\ContR(\Eone(a),k,r)$.

The bridge determinacy lemma
\autoref{lem:continuation-morphism-e1-source-e1-target-bridge-determinacy}
adds the consumer-surface exhaustion. It states that every route, classifier
comparison, readback, or handoff for the packet factors through one of these
two rows and that every zero-headed coordinate is refused. Hence for each
$q:\BHist$,
$$
\HSame(\mathsf{tail}(m),\Ezero(q))\to\mathsf{False},
$$
so a zero-headed normal form cannot be an accepted consumer route for the same
morphism.

It remains to compare two accepted one-headed reads. Suppose they expose
payloads $k$ and $k'$ through the two displayed one-headed rows. Both rows name
the same tail of the same morphism $m$, so the equalities
$\mathsf{tail}(m)=\Eone(k)$ and $\mathsf{tail}(m)=\Eone(k')$ make the two
one-headed tail displays the same displayed normal form. Constructor
injectivity for the one-headed history constructor gives $\HSame(k,k')$. The
continuation data attached to the two reads are therefore exactly the
continuation rows already displayed in the two tail cases, not an additional
source of exactness data.

The cited bridge lemma exposes no branch beyond the empty endpoint-collapse row
and the one-headed payload-continuation row, while the tail case theorem gives
no further normal form for the one-headed endpoint packet. Therefore every
accepted consumer-facing use of $m$ chooses the same finite route determined by
those displayed rows, and an unlisted coordinate has no local row from which to
supply a hidden tail, alternate normal form, detached exactness decision, or
duplicate classifier source.
\end{proof}
