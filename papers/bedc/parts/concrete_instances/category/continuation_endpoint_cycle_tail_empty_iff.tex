\begin{theorem}[Continuation morphism composite endpoint cycle tail empty iff]
\label{thm:continuation-morphism-comp-endpoint-cycle-tail-empty-iff}
For histories $a,b,c$, let $left:\mathsf{ContinuationMorphism}(a,b)$ and
$right:\mathsf{ContinuationMorphism}(b,c)$ be continuation morphisms. Then the
outer endpoints close exactly when the canonical composite displayed tail is
empty:
$$
\hsame(a,c)\Longleftrightarrow
\hsame(\mathsf{tail}(left\circ right),\emp).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The forward implication is the endpoint-cycle empty-tail theorem. Conversely,
if the composite tail is empty, transport the stored continuation relation
$\Cont(a,\mathsf{tail}(left\circ right),c)$ across that empty-tail comparison
to obtain $\Cont(a,\emp,c)$. Determinacy against the right-unit continuation
$\Cont(a,\emp,a)$ identifies $c$ with $a$, hence $a\hsame c$ by symmetry.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CategoryUp.ContinuationMorphism\_comp\_endpoint\_cycle\_tail\_empty\_iff}

\begin{theorem}[Continuation morphism composite source empty tail target iff]
\label{thm:continuation-morphism-comp-source-empty-tail-target-iff}
For histories $a,b,c$, let $left:\mathsf{ContinuationMorphism}(a,b)$ and
$right:\mathsf{ContinuationMorphism}(b,c)$ be continuation morphisms. The
source is empty exactly when the canonical composite displayed tail is the
target:
$$
\hsame(a,\emp)\Longleftrightarrow
\hsame(\mathsf{tail}(left\circ right),c).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
If $a\hsame\emp$, the stored composite continuation ledger has empty source.
The left-unit result law therefore identifies the target with the composite
tail, and symmetry gives the displayed tail-target comparison.

Conversely, transport the stored composite continuation ledger along the
tail-target comparison to obtain $\ContR(a,c,c)$. Comparing this ledger with
the canonical left-unit continuation $\ContR(\emp,c,c)$ by right cancellation
identifies $a$ with $\emp$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CategoryUp.ContinuationMorphism\_comp\_source\_empty\_tail\_target\_iff}

\begin{lemma}[Continuation morphism composite endpoint cycle bridge determinacy]
\label{lem:continuation-morphism-comp-endpoint-cycle-bridge-determinacy}
For histories $a,b,c$, let $left:\mathsf{ContinuationMorphism}(a,b)$ and
$right:\mathsf{ContinuationMorphism}(b,c)$ be continuation morphisms, and write
$T=\mathsf{tail}(left\circ right)$ for the canonical composite displayed tail.
The consumer-facing endpoint-cycle bridge for this composite is determined by
exactly the two local rows
$$
\hsame(a,c)\Longleftrightarrow\hsame(T,\emp)
$$
and
$$
\hsame(a,\emp)\Longleftrightarrow\hsame(T,c).
$$
Thus every accepted route, transport, readback, or handoff through the composite
endpoint-cycle surface factors through either
\autoref{thm:continuation-morphism-comp-endpoint-cycle-tail-empty-iff} or
\autoref{thm:continuation-morphism-comp-source-empty-tail-target-iff}, together
with the stored composite continuation ledger $\ContR(a,T,c)$. No accepted
consumer obtains an independent endpoint-cycle flag, an independent
source-empty flag, a primitive host composite, a second tail classifier, or any
bridge coordinate not listed by these two rows.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
The displayed composite contributes one tail coordinate, namely $T$, and one
stored continuation ledger, namely $\ContR(a,T,c)$. The endpoint-cycle row of
\autoref{thm:continuation-morphism-comp-endpoint-cycle-tail-empty-iff} says that
reading the outer endpoint comparison $\hsame(a,c)$ is exactly the same
consumer operation as reading the empty-tail comparison $\hsame(T,\emp)$. Hence
a route that starts from endpoint closure factors by applying the forward
implication of that row, while a route that starts from an empty composite tail
factors by applying the reverse implication. A transport or handoff along this
branch has no further datum: the stored ledger $\ContR(a,T,c)$ is transported
across $\hsame(T,\emp)$ by \autoref{thm:cont-hsame-transport} to
$\ContR(a,\emp,c)$, and determinacy against the right-unit continuation is
precisely the reverse direction already recorded by the same row.

The source-empty row of
\autoref{thm:continuation-morphism-comp-source-empty-tail-target-iff} gives the
second and only other consumer-facing comparison. Reading $\hsame(a,\emp)$ is
exactly the same operation as reading the tail-target comparison
$\hsame(T,c)$. Thus a source-empty route factors through the forward implication
of that row, and a tail-target readback factors through the reverse implication.
The corresponding handoff again uses only the stored composite ledger: transport
$\ContR(a,T,c)$ along $\hsame(T,c)$ by \autoref{thm:cont-hsame-transport} to
obtain $\ContR(a,c,c)$, then compare with the left-unit continuation as in the
cited row.

These two cases exhaust the bridge surface. The first row exposes the
endpoint-cycle/empty-tail coordinate, and the second row exposes the
source-empty/tail-target coordinate. Both use the same displayed tail $T$ and
the same stored composite continuation ledger. None of the rows contains a
primitive host composite, a second classifier for $T$, an endpoint-cycle flag
separate from $\hsame(T,\emp)$, a source-empty flag separate from $\hsame(T,c)$,
or any detached bridge coordinate. Therefore every accepted consumer-facing
route, transport, readback, or handoff factors through the listed local rows,
and unlisted bridge coordinates remain unavailable.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Continuation morphism composite endpoint cycle consumer determinacy]
\label{cor:continuation-morphism-comp-endpoint-cycle-consumer-determinacy}
For histories $a,b,c$, let $left:\mathsf{ContinuationMorphism}(a,b)$ and
$right:\mathsf{ContinuationMorphism}(b,c)$ be continuation morphisms, and write
$T=\mathsf{tail}(left\circ right)$ for the canonical composite displayed tail.
Once the endpoint-cycle bridge rows of
\autoref{lem:continuation-morphism-comp-endpoint-cycle-bridge-determinacy} are
fixed, every accepted consumer-facing use of the composite endpoint-cycle
surface has only the chapter-named finite route, normal-form read, and exactness
decision:
$$
\begin{gathered}
\hsame(a,c)\Longleftrightarrow\hsame(T,\emp),\\
\hsame(a,\emp)\Longleftrightarrow\hsame(T,c).
\end{gathered}
$$
Thus the endpoint-cycle decision is read through the empty-tail normal form
$\hsame(T,\emp)$, the source-empty decision is read through the tail-target
normal form $\hsame(T,c)$, and no accepted consumer can introduce an alternate
hidden coordinate or a duplicate classifier source for the composite.
\end{corollary}
\begin{proof}
By \autoref{lem:continuation-morphism-comp-endpoint-cycle-bridge-determinacy},
the composite exposes exactly one displayed tail coordinate $T$ and one stored
composite continuation ledger $\ContR(a,T,c)$ to the endpoint-cycle bridge. The
same lemma lists the complete bridge interface as the two rows
$\hsame(a,c)\Longleftrightarrow\hsame(T,\emp)$ and
$\hsame(a,\emp)\Longleftrightarrow\hsame(T,c)$, and states that every accepted
route, transport, readback, or handoff through the composite factors through
one of those rows together with the stored ledger.

The first row is the finite endpoint-cycle route. Its normal-form read is the
empty-tail comparison $\hsame(T,\emp)$, and its exactness decision is precisely
the equivalence with the outer endpoint comparison $\hsame(a,c)$ supplied by
\autoref{thm:continuation-morphism-comp-endpoint-cycle-tail-empty-iff}. Hence
two accepted consumers of the endpoint-cycle decision cannot disagree without
leaving that row: both must read the same displayed tail $T$, transport the
same ledger $\ContR(a,T,c)$, and decide the same comparison
$\hsame(T,\emp)$.

The second row is the finite source-empty route. By
\autoref{thm:continuation-morphism-comp-source-empty-tail-target-iff}, its
normal-form read is the tail-target comparison $\hsame(T,c)$, and its exactness
decision is precisely the equivalence with $\hsame(a,\emp)$. Therefore any
accepted consumer that claims a source-empty readback also factors through the
same displayed tail $T$ and the same ledger $\ContR(a,T,c)$, with no second
source from which the classifier could be recomputed.

These two rows exhaust the bridge surface by
\autoref{lem:continuation-morphism-comp-endpoint-cycle-bridge-determinacy}. A
consumer-facing use that selects a finite route must select one of the two
chapter routes; a use that selects a normal form must select either
$\hsame(T,\emp)$ or $\hsame(T,c)$; and a use that selects an exactness decision
must select the corresponding displayed equivalence. Since the bridge exports
no primitive host composite, no second tail classifier, and no coordinate
outside those rows, alternate hidden coordinates and duplicate classifier
sources are not accepted.
\end{proof}
