\begin{theorem}[Continuation morphism composite endpoint cycle tail empty iff]
\label{thm:continuation-morphism-comp-endpoint-cycle-tail-empty-iff}
For histories $a,b,c$, let $left:\mathsf{ContinuationMorphism}(a,b)$ and
$right:\mathsf{ContinuationMorphism}(b,c)$ be continuation morphisms. Then the
outer endpoints close exactly when the canonical composite displayed tail is
empty:
$$
\hsame(a,c)\Longleftrightarrow
\hsame(\mathsf{tail}(left\circ right),\emp).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The forward implication is the endpoint-cycle empty-tail theorem. Conversely,
if the composite tail is empty, transport the stored continuation relation
$\Cont(a,\mathsf{tail}(left\circ right),c)$ across that empty-tail comparison
to obtain $\Cont(a,\emp,c)$. Determinacy against the right-unit continuation
$\Cont(a,\emp,a)$ identifies $c$ with $a$, hence $a\hsame c$ by symmetry.
\end{proof}
