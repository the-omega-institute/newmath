\begin{theorem}[Functor hom-carrier codomain evaluation]
\label{thm:functor-hom-carrier-codomain-evaluation}
Let \(C\) and \(E\) be certified categories, and let \(H:C\to E\) be a certified functor with object map \(H_0\). Write \(\mathsf{FunctorHomCarrier}_{H}(x,y)\) for the predicate on codomain morphism candidates \(m\) abbreviated by
$$
\mathsf{FunctorHomCarrier}_{H}(x,y,m)
\equiv
\mathsf{HomCarrier}_{E}(H_0(x),H_0(y),m).
$$
Then for every pair of source objects \(x,y\) and every codomain morphism candidate \(m\), the functor-indexed hom-carrier classifier at \(x,y\) evaluates to the codomain category hom-carrier classifier at the image endpoints:
$$
\mathsf{FunctorHomCarrier}_{H}(x,y,m)
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
\mathsf{HomCarrier}_{E}(H_0(x),H_0(y),m).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The functor carrier of \autoref{def:functor-carrier} supplies the object map \(H_0\). The category carrier of \autoref{def:category-carrier} supplies, in the codomain category \(E\), a hom-carrier classifier for each ordered pair of codomain objects. Applying that category classifier to the image pair \(H_0(x),H_0(y)\) is exactly the displayed abbreviation for \(\mathsf{FunctorHomCarrier}_{H}(x,y,-)\). The pointwise classifier discipline in \autoref{def:functor-classifier-specification} and \autoref{rem:functor-zero-axiom-note} reads classifier equality at the morphism argument \(m\), so unfolding the abbreviation gives the stated biconditional.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Functor composite preservation witnesses]
\label{thm:functor-composite-preservation-witnesses}
Let \(C,D,E\) be certified categories. Let \(F:C\to D\) and \(G:D\to E\) be certified \(\FunctorUp\) carriers with object maps \(F_0,G_0\) and morphism maps \(F_1,G_1\). Write \(H\) for the composite pair with
$$
H_0(x)=G_0(F_0(x)),
\qquad
H_1(u)=G_1(F_1(u)).
$$
Then \(H\) has identity and composition preservation witnesses under the morphism classifier of \(E\): for every object \(x\) of \(C\), \(H_1(\mathsf{id}_x)\) is classified with \(\mathsf{id}_{H_0(x)}\), and for every composable pair \(u:x\to y\), \(v:y\to z\) in \(C\), \(H_1(v\circ_C u)\) is classified with \(H_1(v)\circ_E H_1(u)\).
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The functor carrier of \autoref{def:functor-carrier} supplies the object and morphism maps for \(F\) and \(G\). The stability certificate in \autoref{def:functor-stability-certificate} supplies identity preservation, composition preservation, and classifier-replacement stability, all read through the codomain morphism classifier. By \autoref{thm:functor-hom-carrier-codomain-evaluation}, the hom-carrier classifier for the composite pair \(H\) is the hom-carrier classifier of \(E\) at the image endpoints \(H_0(x)\) and \(H_0(y)\).

Fix an object \(x\) of \(C\). The identity field of \(F\) classifies \(F_1(\mathsf{id}_x)\) with \(\mathsf{id}_{F_0(x)}\) in \(D\). The classifier-replacement stability field of \(G\) transports that comparison through \(G_1\), giving a classifier witness in \(E\) between \(G_1(F_1(\mathsf{id}_x))\) and \(G_1(\mathsf{id}_{F_0(x)})\). The identity field of \(G\), applied at \(F_0(x)\), classifies \(G_1(\mathsf{id}_{F_0(x)})\) with \(\mathsf{id}_{G_0(F_0(x))}\). Morphism-classifier transitivity from the category stability certificate in \autoref{def:category-stability-certificate} composes the two comparisons, giving the identity witness for \(H\).

Fix composable morphisms \(u:x\to y\) and \(v:y\to z\) in \(C\). The composition field of \(F\) classifies \(F_1(v\circ_C u)\) with \(F_1(v)\circ_D F_1(u)\) in \(D\). The classifier-replacement stability field of \(G\) transports this comparison through \(G_1\), giving a classifier witness in \(E\) between \(G_1(F_1(v\circ_C u))\) and \(G_1(F_1(v)\circ_D F_1(u))\). The composition field of \(G\), applied to the composable pair \(F_1(u),F_1(v)\), classifies \(G_1(F_1(v)\circ_D F_1(u))\) with \(G_1(F_1(v))\circ_E G_1(F_1(u))\). Transitivity in \(E\) gives the composition witness for \(H\), and \autoref{def:functor-ledger-policy} records these two preservation ledgers as the composite functor ledger data.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Functor composition carries the hom-carrier classifier]
\label{thm:functor-composite-hom-carrier-classifier}
Let $C,D,E$ be certified categories. Let $F:C\to D$ and $G:D\to E$ be certified $\FunctorUp$ carriers with object maps $F_0,G_0$ and morphism maps $F_1,G_1$. Let $H=G\circ F$ be the composite carrier whose object and morphism maps are
$$
H_0(x)=G_0(F_0(x)),
\qquad
H_1(u)=G_1(F_1(u)).
$$
For every pair of source objects $x,y$ and every codomain morphism candidate $m$, the hom-carrier classifier carried by $H$ is pointwise the codomain hom-carrier classifier at the composed image endpoints:
$$
\mathsf{FunctorHomCarrier}_{G\circ F}(x,y,m)
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
\mathsf{HomCarrier}_{E}(G_0(F_0(x)),G_0(F_0(y)),m).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier fields in \autoref{def:functor-carrier} give the maps $F_0,F_1,G_0,G_1$, and \autoref{thm:functor-composite-preservation-witnesses} supplies the identity and composition preservation witnesses for the composite pair $H$ under the morphism classifier of $E$. Thus $H$ is read as the certified functor $C\to E$ with object map $H_0$.

Apply \autoref{thm:functor-hom-carrier-codomain-evaluation} to this functor $H$. It gives
$$
\mathsf{FunctorHomCarrier}_{H}(x,y,m)
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
\mathsf{HomCarrier}_{E}(H_0(x),H_0(y),m).
$$
By the displayed definition of the composite object map, the right-hand side is exactly $\mathsf{HomCarrier}_{E}(G_0(F_0(x)),G_0(F_0(y)),m)$. The classifier discipline of \autoref{def:functor-classifier-specification} and the pointwise reading in \autoref{rem:functor-zero-axiom-note} identify classifier sameness by this morphism-argument equivalence, so the composite functor carries precisely the composed hom-carrier classifier.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Functor composition identity needs morphism-sameness respect]
\label{prop:functor-composition-identity-sameness-respect-required}
Let $C,D,E$ be finite certified categories, and let $F:C\to D$ and $G:D\to E$ be carrier pairs with object maps $F_0,G_0$ and morphism maps $F_1,G_1$. Write $m\sim_D n$ and $r\sim_E s$ for the endpoint-indexed morphism classifiers, and suppose the $E$-classifier is symmetric and transitive. Assume the raw identity and raw composition preservation ledgers for $F$ and $G$ are present in the sense of \autoref{def:functor-carrier}. Fix an object $x$ of $C$ and set $H=G\circ F$ with $H_0(x)=G_0(F_0(x))$ and $H_1(u)=G_1(F_1(u))$. If
$$
F_1(\mathsf{id}_x)\sim_D \mathsf{id}_{F_0(x)}
$$
and
$$
G_1(\mathsf{id}_{F_0(x)})\sim_E \mathsf{id}_{G_0(F_0(x))}
$$
but
$$
\neg\bigl(G_1(F_1(\mathsf{id}_x))\sim_E G_1(\mathsf{id}_{F_0(x)})\bigr),
$$
then $H$ has no identity preservation witness at $x$:
$$
\neg\bigl(H_1(\mathsf{id}_x)\sim_E \mathsf{id}_{H_0(x)}\bigr).
$$
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
Assume that the displayed composite identity witness exists. Unfolding the composite carrier gives
$$
G_1(F_1(\mathsf{id}_x))\sim_E \mathsf{id}_{G_0(F_0(x))}.
$$
The raw identity ledger for $G$ gives
$$
G_1(\mathsf{id}_{F_0(x)})\sim_E \mathsf{id}_{G_0(F_0(x))}.
$$
Symmetry reverses the second comparison, and transitivity composes it with the first. Hence
$$
G_1(F_1(\mathsf{id}_x))\sim_E G_1(\mathsf{id}_{F_0(x)}),
$$
contradicting the displayed non-respect premise. Thus the composite identity witness cannot exist.

The comparison supplied by $F$ is only a $D$-morphism classifier witness between $F_1(\mathsf{id}_x)$ and $\mathsf{id}_{F_0(x)}$. To use the raw identity ledger for $G$, that witness must be transported through $G_1$. This is exactly the classifier-replacement stability field in \autoref{def:functor-stability-certificate} and the step used in \autoref{thm:functor-composite-preservation-witnesses}. Raw composition preservation does not supply this transport, so it cannot restore identity preservation for $G\circ F$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FunctorUp.FunctorComposition\_identity\_sameness\_respect\_required}

\begin{theorem}[Raw functor hom-carrier landing obstruction]\label{thm:raw-functor-hom-carrier-landing-obstruction}
There are finite two-object certified categories $C$ and $D$, together with an object map $F_0$, a raw morphism map $F_1$, and strict raw identity and composition preservation ledgers, such that a displayed source hom is not carried to the endpoint-indexed codomain hom-carrier required by $\FunctorUp$. Take object and morphism classifiers in both categories to be literal equality.
Let $C$ have objects $0,1$, raw morphisms $\mathsf{id}_0,\mathsf{id}_1,a$, and hom-carrier table $\mathsf{HomCarrier}_{C}(0,0,\mathsf{id}_0)$, $\mathsf{HomCarrier}_{C}(1,1,\mathsf{id}_1)$, and $\mathsf{HomCarrier}_{C}(0,1,a)$, with no other hom-carrier witnesses. Its legal composites are $\mathsf{id}_0\circ_C\mathsf{id}_0=\mathsf{id}_0$, $\mathsf{id}_1\circ_C\mathsf{id}_1=\mathsf{id}_1$, $a\circ_C\mathsf{id}_0=a$, and $\mathsf{id}_1\circ_C a=a$.
Let $D$ have objects $0,1$, raw morphisms $\mathsf{id}_0,\mathsf{id}_1,b$, and hom-carrier table $\mathsf{HomCarrier}_{D}(0,0,\mathsf{id}_0)$, $\mathsf{HomCarrier}_{D}(1,1,\mathsf{id}_1)$, and $\mathsf{HomCarrier}_{D}(1,0,b)$, with no other hom-carrier witnesses. Its legal composites are $\mathsf{id}_0\circ_D\mathsf{id}_0=\mathsf{id}_0$, $\mathsf{id}_1\circ_D\mathsf{id}_1=\mathsf{id}_1$, $b\circ_D\mathsf{id}_1=b$, and $\mathsf{id}_0\circ_D b=b$.
Define $F_0(0)=0$, $F_0(1)=1$, $F_1(\mathsf{id}_0)=\mathsf{id}_0$, $F_1(\mathsf{id}_1)=\mathsf{id}_1$, and $F_1(a)=b$. Equip the raw morphism carrier of $D$ with an auxiliary total raw operation $\star_D$ whose values on the image pairs of source composites are $\mathsf{id}_0\star_D\mathsf{id}_0=\mathsf{id}_0$, $\mathsf{id}_1\star_D\mathsf{id}_1=\mathsf{id}_1$, $b\star_D\mathsf{id}_0=b$, and $\mathsf{id}_1\star_D b=b$.
Then the object-map field, raw morphism-map field, identity preservation field, and raw composition preservation field are all satisfied. Nevertheless $\mathsf{HomCarrier}_{C}(0,1,a)$ and $\neg\,\mathsf{HomCarrier}_{D}(F_0(0),F_0(1),F_1(a))$. Thus endpoint-indexed hom-carrier landing is not entailed by those raw fields.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The displayed hom-carrier and composite tables make $C$ and $D$ certified finite categories in the sense of \autoref{def:category-carrier}. In each category every legal composite contains only identity morphisms except for the single displayed nonidentity arrow, so the left and right identity laws hold strictly and every legal threefold composite reduces to the same displayed morphism on both associations. This supplies the category stability fields of \autoref{def:category-stability-certificate}.
The map $F_0$ sends the two source objects to objects of $D$, and $F_1$ sends each raw source morphism to a raw morphism of $D$, giving the two carrier maps named in \autoref{def:functor-carrier}. Identity preservation is strict because $F_1(\mathsf{id}_0)=\mathsf{id}_0=\mathsf{id}_{F_0(0)}$ and $F_1(\mathsf{id}_1)=\mathsf{id}_1=\mathsf{id}_{F_0(1)}$.
For raw composition preservation, the only composable pairs in $C$ are the four listed pairs. Applying $F_1$ to their composites gives respectively $\mathsf{id}_0$, $\mathsf{id}_1$, $b$, and $b$. The displayed values of $\star_D$ give exactly the same four results, so the raw composition preservation field from \autoref{def:functor-stability-certificate} is satisfied on all source composites.
The source hom assertion $\mathsf{HomCarrier}_{C}(0,1,a)$ is one of the constructors in the table for $C$. On the codomain side, $F_0(0)=0$, $F_0(1)=1$, and $F_1(a)=b$, so the required endpoint-indexed landing assertion is $\mathsf{HomCarrier}_{D}(0,1,b)$. The table for $D$ has no such generator; by the closed-generation discipline of \autoref{ch:proof-standing-internal-sameness-no-confusion} and the indexed hom-carrier reading in \autoref{def:category-classifier-specification}, no hidden witness is available. Hence $\neg\,\mathsf{HomCarrier}_{D}(F_0(0),F_0(1),F_1(a))$.
By \autoref{thm:functor-hom-carrier-codomain-evaluation}, a certified $\FunctorUp$ carrier reads the source-indexed functor hom predicate as the codomain hom-carrier at the image endpoints. The finite table above satisfies the raw preservation data while failing exactly that predicate at $(0,1,a)$, so the endpoint-indexed hom-carrier landing field is a separate obligation.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FunctorUp.RawFunctorHomCarrier\_landing\_obstruction}

\begin{theorem}[Functor composition identity unit laws]
\label{thm:functor-composition-identity-unit-laws}
Let $\mathcal C$ and $\mathcal D$ be $\CategoryUp$ certificates satisfying
\autoref{def:category-certificate-obligations}, and let
$F:\mathcal C\to\mathcal D$ be a $\FunctorUp$ certificate satisfying
\autoref{def:functor-certificate-obligations}. Write $F_0$ and $F_1$ for its
object and morphism maps. Let $1_{\mathcal C}$ and $1_{\mathcal D}$ be the
identity functor certificates supplied by
\autoref{def:identity-functor-certificate-data} and
\autoref{thm:identity-functor-namecert}, and write
$$
R=F\circ 1_{\mathcal C},
\qquad
L=1_{\mathcal D}\circ F.
$$
Then $R$ and $L$ are certified $\FunctorUp$ carriers from $\mathcal C$ to
$\mathcal D$, and both are classified with $F$ by the $\FunctorUp$ classifier.
Equivalently, for every source object $x$ and every displayed source morphism
$u:a\to b$,
$$
R_0(x)\sim_{\mathcal O_{\mathcal D}}F_0(x),
\qquad
L_0(x)\sim_{\mathcal O_{\mathcal D}}F_0(x),
$$
and
$$
R_1(u)\sim_{\mathcal H_{\mathcal D}(F_0(a),F_0(b))}F_1(u),
\qquad
L_1(u)\sim_{\mathcal H_{\mathcal D}(F_0(a),F_0(b))}F_1(u).
$$
Consequently, for every pair of source objects $a,b$ and every codomain
morphism candidate $m$,
$$
\mathsf{FunctorHomCarrier}_{R}(a,b,m)
\Longleftrightarrow
\mathsf{FunctorHomCarrier}_{F}(a,b,m)
\Longleftrightarrow
\mathsf{FunctorHomCarrier}_{L}(a,b,m).
$$
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FunctorUp.PrefixFunctorCarrier\_empty\_prefix\_identity\_unit\_laws}
\begin{proof}
The identity certificates are available by
\autoref{thm:identity-functor-namecert}. Applying
\autoref{thm:functor-composite-preservation-witnesses} to the pair
$(1_{\mathcal C},F)$ gives the identity and composition preservation witnesses
for $R=F\circ 1_{\mathcal C}$, and applying the same theorem to the pair
$(F,1_{\mathcal D})$ gives the corresponding witnesses for
$L=1_{\mathcal D}\circ F$. Together with the source, pattern, classifier, and
ledger rows listed in \autoref{def:functor-certificate-obligations}, these are
certified $\FunctorUp$ carriers from $\mathcal C$ to $\mathcal D$.

Unfold the identity functor data of
\autoref{def:identity-functor-certificate-data}. For every source object $x$,
$$
(F\circ 1_{\mathcal C})_0(x)=F_0((1_{\mathcal C})_0(x))=F_0(x),
$$
and
$$
(1_{\mathcal D}\circ F)_0(x)=(1_{\mathcal D})_0(F_0(x))=F_0(x).
$$
Object-classifier reflexivity in the codomain category, recorded by
\autoref{def:category-stability-certificate}, gives the two displayed object
comparisons.

For a displayed source morphism $u:a\to b$, the same unfolding gives
$$
(F\circ 1_{\mathcal C})_1(u)=F_1((1_{\mathcal C})_1(u))=F_1(u),
$$
and
$$
(1_{\mathcal D}\circ F)_1(u)=(1_{\mathcal D})_1(F_1(u))=F_1(u).
$$
Morphism-classifier reflexivity in the endpoint-indexed hom classifier of
$\mathcal D$ gives both displayed morphism comparisons. By the pointwise
classifier specification in \autoref{def:functor-classifier-specification} and
the reflexivity row in \autoref{def:functor-stability-certificate}, these
object and morphism comparisons are exactly the $\FunctorUp$ classifier
witnesses $R\sim F$ and $L\sim F$.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:functor-hom-carrier-codomain-evaluation} to $F$ and
apply \autoref{thm:functor-composite-hom-carrier-classifier} to the two
composites. Since the identity functor has identity object map on both
$\mathcal C$ and $\mathcal D$, all three functor-indexed hom-carrier
predicates reduce to
$$
\mathsf{HomCarrier}_{\mathcal D}(F_0(a),F_0(b),m).
$$
This gives the displayed biconditional chain.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Functor composition associativity classifier law]

A=(H\circ G)\circ F,
\qquad
B=H\circ(G\circ F).
$$

$\mathcal L$, and they are classified together by the $\FunctorUp$ classifier.
Explicitly, for every source object $x$,
$$
A_0(x)\sim_{\mathcal O_{\mathcal L}}B_0(x).
$$
For every displayed source morphism $u:a\to b$,
$$
A_1(u)\sim_{\mathcal H_{\mathcal L}(H_0(G_0(F_0(a))),H_0(G_0(F_0(b))))}B_1(u).
$$
Moreover, the functor-indexed hom-carrier classifiers agree pointwise: for
every pair of source objects $a,b$ and every codomain morphism candidate $m$,
$$
\mathsf{FunctorHomCarrier}_{A}(a,b,m)
\Longleftrightarrow
\mathsf{FunctorHomCarrier}_{B}(a,b,m).
$$
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Functor composition classifier category package]
\label{thm:functor-composition-classifier-category-package}
Let $\mathcal C,\mathcal D,\mathcal E,\mathcal L$ be certified
$\CategoryUp$ packages satisfying \autoref{def:category-certificate-obligations}.
Let $F:\mathcal C\to\mathcal D$, $G:\mathcal D\to\mathcal E$, and
$H:\mathcal E\to\mathcal L$ be certified $\FunctorUp$ carriers satisfying
\autoref{def:functor-certificate-obligations}, with object maps
$F_0,G_0,H_0$ and morphism maps $F_1,G_1,H_1$. Then the certified
functor carriers form a classifier-respected composition package with the
following fields.
\begin{enumerate}
\item For every composable pair $F:\mathcal C\to\mathcal D$ and
$G:\mathcal D\to\mathcal E$, the composite $G\circ F$ is a certified
$\FunctorUp$ carrier from $\mathcal C$ to $\mathcal E$, and its
$\mathsf{FunctorHomCarrier}$ is the codomain hom-carrier classifier at
$G_0(F_0(-))$.
\item For every certified $\CategoryUp$ package $\mathcal A$, the identity
carrier $1_{\mathcal A}$ is a certified $\FunctorUp$ carrier on
$\mathcal A$.
\item For every certified $K:\mathcal A\to\mathcal B$, the composites
$K\circ 1_{\mathcal A}$ and $1_{\mathcal B}\circ K$ are classified with
$K$ by the $\FunctorUp$ classifier, and the three
$\mathsf{FunctorHomCarrier}$ predicates are pointwise the same.
\item For every certified triple $F,G,H$ as above, the two triple composites
$A=(H\circ G)\circ F$ and $B=H\circ(G\circ F)$ are classified together by
the $\FunctorUp$ classifier, and for every pair of source objects $a,b$ and
every codomain morphism candidate $m$,
$$
\mathsf{FunctorHomCarrier}_{A}(a,b,m)
\Longleftrightarrow
\mathsf{FunctorHomCarrier}_{B}(a,b,m).
$$
\end{enumerate}
This is the composition structure used by the $\NatTransUp$ and $\YonedaUp$
interfaces whenever their source functors are composed.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The proof uses the following ten-field roadmap.
\begin{enumerate}
\item traditional target = category-style composition of certified functors;
\item BEDC source = $\CategoryUp$ and $\FunctorUp$;
\item carrier = certified functor carriers;
\item classifier = pointwise $\FunctorUp$ classifier;
\item stability = closure, unit, associativity, and hom-carrier transport;
\item ledger = source and codomain category ledgers plus preservation witnesses;
\item core theorem list = \autoref{thm:functor-composite-preservation-witnesses},
\autoref{thm:functor-composite-hom-carrier-classifier},
\autoref{thm:identity-functor-namecert},
\autoref{thm:functor-composition-identity-unit-laws}, and
\autoref{thm:functor-composition-associativity-classifier-law};
\item exactness = hom-carrier pointwise classifier agreement;
\item standard bridge row = not claimed by this composition package; ordinary
functor-category laws are only the external comparison target named by the
upgrade path;
\item status = paper proof target.
\end{enumerate}

For closure under composition, apply
\autoref{thm:functor-composite-preservation-witnesses} to a composable pair
of certified functors. It supplies the identity and composition preservation
witnesses for the composite carrier. Applying
\autoref{thm:functor-composite-hom-carrier-classifier} reads the resulting
$\mathsf{FunctorHomCarrier}$ pointwise as the codomain hom-carrier classifier
at the composed image endpoints.

For identities, \autoref{thm:identity-functor-namecert} supplies the certified
identity functor on each certified $\CategoryUp$ package. The carrier,
classifier, stability, and ledger rows named in
\autoref{def:functor-certificate-obligations} are therefore available for
$1_{\mathcal A}$ without adding any extra source data.

For the unit laws, apply
\autoref{thm:functor-composition-identity-unit-laws}. It identifies
$K\circ 1_{\mathcal A}$ and $1_{\mathcal B}\circ K$ with $K$ under the
object and morphism components of the $\FunctorUp$ classifier, and it gives
the same pointwise $\mathsf{FunctorHomCarrier}$ predicate for all three
functors.

For associativity, apply
\autoref{thm:functor-composition-associativity-classifier-law}. It certifies
both triple composites, gives the pointwise object and morphism classifier
witnesses between them, and gives the displayed agreement of their
functor-indexed hom-carrier classifiers.

The four fields are exactly the composition data demanded downstream: closed
composition, certified identities, unit classifiers, associativity
classifiers, and exact hom-carrier transport. Repacking those fields gives the
claimed classifier-respected composition package for certified $\FunctorUp$
carriers.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[Two-point classifier-transport obstruction data]
\label{def:two-point-classifier-transport-obstruction-data}
Let $M=\{1,u\}$ be the idempotent monoid with unit $1$ and $u\circ u=u$. Let $T=\{I,P,Q\}$ be the transformation monoid on $\{0,1\}$ generated by the identity $I$ and the constant maps $P(0)=P(1)=0$ and $Q(0)=Q(1)=1$, with composition of functions. Thus $P\circ P=P$, $Q\circ Q=Q$, $Q\circ I=Q$, $I\circ P=P$, and $Q\ne P$.

Let $\mathcal C$ be the one-object category of $M$ with equality as its morphism classifier. Let $\mathcal D$ be the one-object category of $T$ with the total morphism classifier, so every two carried endomorphisms are classified. Let $\mathcal E$ be the one-object category of $T$ with equality as its morphism classifier. Hom-carriers are total in each one-object category, and object classifiers are equality on the unique object.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[FunctorUp composition obstruction without classifier replacement]
\label{thm:functorup-composition-obstruction-without-classifier-replacement}
The finite data of \autoref{def:two-point-classifier-transport-obstruction-data} exhibit a raw functor-like map $H:\mathcal D\to\mathcal E$ preserving identities and raw composition, certified $\FunctorUp$ carriers $F,G:\mathcal C\to\mathcal D$, and a certified $\NatTransUp$ carrier $\alpha:F\Rightarrow G$ such that the functor-whiskering naturality row for $H_{\ast}\alpha$ is impossible. Consequently the classifier-replacement row in \autoref{def:functor-stability-certificate} is not entailed by the raw carrier, identity, and composition rows of \autoref{def:functor-carrier}, and $\FunctorUp$ composition certification cannot be derived from those raw rows alone.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The categories are certified in the sense of \autoref{def:category-certificate-obligations}. For $\mathcal C$ and $\mathcal E$, equality supplies the object and morphism classifier laws and is compatible with composition. For $\mathcal D$, the total morphism classifier is reflexive, symmetric, transitive, and compatible with composition. The identity, composition closure, associativity, and unit laws are the monoid laws of $M$ and $T$, matching \autoref{def:category-stability-certificate}.

Define $F,G:\mathcal C\to\mathcal D$ on the unique object by the unique object map and on morphisms by
$$
F_1(1)=I,\qquad F_1(u)=P,
\qquad
G_1(1)=I,\qquad G_1(u)=Q.
$$
The equations $P\circ P=P$ and $Q\circ Q=Q$ make identity and composition preservation strict. Since the codomain classifier of $\mathcal D$ is total, the pointwise classifier rows and the source-comparison transport rows listed in \autoref{def:functor-stability-certificate} are automatic. Thus $F$ and $G$ satisfy the $\FunctorUp$ obligations of \autoref{def:functor-certificate-obligations}.

Let $\alpha:F\Rightarrow G$ have the single component $\alpha_{\ast}=I$. The component carrier exists because $\mathcal D$ has one object. Naturality at the identity morphism is immediate. Naturality at $u$ is the $\mathcal D$-classifier comparison
$$
G_1(u)\circ \alpha_{\ast}
=
Q\circ I
=
Q
\sim_{\mathcal D}
P
=
I\circ P
=
\alpha_{\ast}\circ F_1(u),
$$
which is supplied by the total classifier of $\mathcal D$. The remaining component classifier, naturality-stability, vertical-composition, and identity rows of \autoref{def:nattrans-stability-certificate} are again immediate in the total classifier, so $\alpha$ satisfies \autoref{def:nattrans-certificate-obligations}.

Now define $H:\mathcal D\to\mathcal E$ by the unique object map and the identity map on the raw monoid $T$. It preserves the identity and every raw composite strictly because the two categories have the same raw monoid. It does not preserve the morphism classifier: $\mathcal D$ classifies $Q\sim_{\mathcal D}P$, while the equality classifier of $\mathcal E$ gives $Q\not\sim_{\mathcal E}P$.

The component prescribed by \autoref{def:functor-whiskered-nattrans-certificate-data} is
$$
(H_{\ast}\alpha)_{\ast}=H_1(I)=I,
$$
so the component carrier itself exists in $\mathcal E$. The naturality row at $u$, however, would have to be
$$
H_1(G_1(u))\circ H_1(I)
\sim_{\mathcal E}
H_1(I)\circ H_1(F_1(u)).
$$
Unfolding the displayed maps and the monoid products turns this into
$$
Q\circ I
=
Q
\sim_{\mathcal E}
P
=
I\circ P.
$$
This is impossible because $\mathcal E$ uses equality as its morphism classifier and $Q\ne P$ as transformations of $\{0,1\}$.

Thus the naturality classifier for $\alpha$ in $\mathcal D$ cannot be transported through $H_1$. This is exactly the transport step used in \autoref{thm:functor-whiskering-nattrans-certificate} and, at the functor level, in \autoref{thm:functor-composite-preservation-witnesses}. If raw identity and raw composition preservation were enough for $\FunctorUp$ composition certification, applying that rule to $H\circ 1_{\mathcal D}$, with $1_{\mathcal D}$ supplied by \autoref{thm:identity-functor-namecert}, would certify the same raw map $H$ and hence the missing classifier transport $Q\sim_{\mathcal D}P\Rightarrow H_1(Q)\sim_{\mathcal E}H_1(P)$. The displayed failure in $\mathcal E$ contradicts that conclusion.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Split monomorphisms are left-cancellative]
\label{thm:category-split-monomorphism-left-cancellative}
Let $\mathcal C$ be a $\CategoryUp$ certificate satisfying
\autoref{def:category-certificate-obligations}. Let $f:a\to b$ be a split
monomorphism in $\mathcal C$ with left-inverse witness $g:b\to a$ in the
sense of \autoref{def:category-split-monomorphism}. If $u,v:x\to a$ are
displayed morphisms and $p,q:x\to b$ are displayed composites with
$$
\mathsf{Comp}_{\mathcal C}(u,f,p),
\qquad
\mathsf{Comp}_{\mathcal C}(v,f,q),
$$
and if $p\sim_{\mathcal C}q$ in the morphism classifier at $(x,b)$, then
$u\sim_{\mathcal C}v$ in the morphism classifier at $(x,a)$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the split-monomorphism witness. It supplies displayed hom-carriers for
$f$ and $g$, an identity display $i_a:a\to a$, and a composite display
$c:a\to a$ such that
$$
\mathsf{Comp}_{\mathcal C}(f,g,c)
\qquad\text{and}\qquad
c\sim_{\mathcal C} i_a.
$$
The displayed morphisms $u$ and $v$, together with composition closure in
\autoref{def:category-stability-certificate}, make $p$ and $q$ hom-carriers
from $x$ to $b$.

Let $r_u:x\to a$ and $r_v:x\to a$ be composites of $p$ and $q$ followed by
$g$:
$$
\mathsf{Comp}_{\mathcal C}(p,g,r_u),
\qquad
\mathsf{Comp}_{\mathcal C}(q,g,r_v).
$$
Composition congruence with the fixed factor $g$ transports the hypothesis
$p\sim_{\mathcal C}q$ to
$$
r_u\sim_{\mathcal C} r_v.
$$

Apply associativity to the chain
$$
x\xrightarrow{u}a\xrightarrow{f}b\xrightarrow{g}a.
$$
It compares $r_u=g\circ_{\mathcal C}(f\circ_{\mathcal C}u)$ with the
composite of $u$ followed by $c=g\circ_{\mathcal C}f$. Since
$c\sim_{\mathcal C}i_a$, composition congruence with the fixed factor $u$
compares this latter composite with the composite of $u$ followed by $i_a$.
The target-identity law then classifies that composite with $u$. Hence
$$
r_u\sim_{\mathcal C}u.
$$
The same argument applied to
$$
x\xrightarrow{v}a\xrightarrow{f}b\xrightarrow{g}a
$$
gives
$$
r_v\sim_{\mathcal C}v.
$$

The morphism-classifier equivalence laws, recorded in the classifier and
stability rows of the $\CategoryUp$ certificate, compose the comparison
$u\sim_{\mathcal C}r_u$, the transported comparison
$r_u\sim_{\mathcal C}r_v$, and the comparison $r_v\sim_{\mathcal C}v$.
Therefore $u\sim_{\mathcal C}v$ at the endpoint pair $(x,a)$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FunctorUp.CategorySplitMonomorphism\_left\_cancellative}
