\begin{theorem}[Split isomorphism inverse witnesses coincide]
\label{thm:category-split-isomorphism-inverse-witnesses-coincide}
Let $\mathcal C$ be a $\CategoryUp$ certificate satisfying the obligations of
\autoref{def:category-certificate-obligations}. Let $f:a\to b$ carry a split
isomorphism witness pair in the sense of
\autoref{def:category-split-isomorphism-witness-pair}, with left-inverse
witness $g_L:b\to a$ and right-inverse witness $g_R:b\to a$. Then the two
inverse-side witnesses are classified together in the hom carrier from $b$ to
$a$:
$$
g_L\sim_{\mathcal C}g_R.
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the split-isomorphism witness pair. The left-oriented field supplies a
displayed composite $c_a:a\to a$ for $g_L\circ_{\mathcal C} f$ and a
classifier comparison
$$
c_a\sim_{\mathcal C}\mathsf{id}_a.
$$
The right-oriented field supplies a displayed composite $c_b:b\to b$ for
$f\circ_{\mathcal C}g_R$ and a classifier comparison
$$
c_b\sim_{\mathcal C}\mathsf{id}_b.
$$

Choose the displayed composites needed to compare both inverse witnesses
through the common three-factor string
$$
b\xrightarrow{g_R}a\xrightarrow{f}b\xrightarrow{g_L}a.
$$
Associativity for the $\CategoryUp$ composition relation classifies the
right-associated composite $g_L\circ_{\mathcal C}(f\circ_{\mathcal C}g_R)$
with the left-associated composite $(g_L\circ_{\mathcal C}f)\circ_{\mathcal C}
g_R$, expressed in the paper's composition orientation as the two bracketings
of $g_R,f,g_L$.

On the right-associated side, replace the displayed composite
$f\circ_{\mathcal C}g_R$ by the target identity $\mathsf{id}_b$ using the
right-inverse comparison and classifier-respecting composition with the fixed
factor $g_L$. The right-unit law then classifies the resulting composite with
$g_L$.

On the left-associated side, replace the displayed composite
$g_L\circ_{\mathcal C}f$ by the source identity $\mathsf{id}_a$ using the
left-inverse comparison and classifier-respecting composition with the fixed
factor $g_R$. The left-unit law then classifies the resulting composite with
$g_R$.

The two reductions start from associativity-related bracketings of the same
three displayed factors. Transitivity of the morphism classifier therefore
compares $g_L$ with $g_R$; symmetry orients the comparison as displayed.
\end{proof}
