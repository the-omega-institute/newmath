\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\InscriptionPointUp$}
\origin{ai}

The $\InscriptionPointUp$ surface makes the inscription-point direction of \autoref{sec:transcendental-input-inscription-point} finite. It records the local observation-history row, the displayed supply socket, the actuality row where an inscription is received as public record data, the GAP boundary for content not supplied internally, transport and read routes, provenance through the neighboring socket chapters, and the local naming certificate. The packet is a certificate surface for the local supply position; it is not a positive predicate asserting that the missing supply has become substrate-internal.

The $\InscriptionPointUp$ packet consumes the supply socket of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-transcendentalinductionsocket-namecert}, the ledger row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-supplysocketledger-namecert}, and the public record event of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-inscriptionevent-namecert}. This dependency is limited to finite $\BHist$, $\mathsf{GAP}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows, so the inscription point remains a displayed packet rather than a hidden supply source.

\begin{definition}[InscriptionPoint carrier]
\label{def:inscription-point-namecert-carrier}
An $\InscriptionPointUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
I=(O,S,A,G,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $O$ is the local observation-history row, naming the finite record surface through which the inscription point is read;
\item $S$ is the displayed supply-socket row, naming the requested supply position and its local consumer route;
\item $A$ is the local actuality and inscription row, recording the public point at which the observation history receives the inscription as record data;
\item $G$ is the GAP boundary row for content not internally supplied by the finite observation history or by BEDC substrate rules;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the observation-history, supply-socket, actuality, GAP, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which consumers read $O$, inspect $S$, expose $G$, and reach $A$ without leaving the finite packet;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance row over $\BHist$, $\mathsf{GAP}$ rows, $\TranscendentalInductionSocketUp$, $\SupplySocketLedgerUp$, $\InscriptionEventUp$, and $\NameCert$ dependencies;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\InscriptionPointUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for an internally supplied induction rule, witness selector, extensional quotient equality, hidden receiver object, private source predicate, or completed supply theorem.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[InscriptionPoint local supply socket]
\label{thm:inscription-point-local-supply-socket}
For an accepted $\InscriptionPointUp$ carrier $I=(O,S,A,G,H,C,P,N)$, every read of the local supply position factors through the displayed supply-socket row $S$ and the GAP boundary row $G$. The route may expose where the requested supply is consumed, but it does not convert the requested supply into an internal BEDC theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:inscription-point-namecert-carrier}. The only supply-facing coordinate is $S$, and the only row naming content not internally supplied is $G$.

The continuation row $C$ gives the consumer route from observation-history inspection to the supply socket and then to the GAP boundary. Componentwise transport through $H$ preserves the same displayed rows when accepted packets are compared.

The provenance row $P$ ties the route to $\TranscendentalInductionSocketUp$ and $\SupplySocketLedgerUp$. Since the carrier has no coordinate for a supplied induction rule, selector, quotient equality, or completed supply theorem, the read remains a finite socket read through $S$ and $G$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[InscriptionPoint observation-history actuality]
\label{thm:inscription-point-observation-history-actuality}
Every inscription read of an accepted $\InscriptionPointUp$ carrier is tied to the local observation-history row $O$ and the actuality row $A$. The packet does not introduce a separate receiver object outside the displayed observation history and inscription surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the carrier of \autoref{def:inscription-point-namecert-carrier}. The observation side is $O$, and the public actuality of inscription is $A$.

The displayed routes in $C$ carry an inscription read from $O$ to $A$ after the supply socket and GAP rows have been exposed. The transports in $H$ compare those rows as coordinates of the same finite packet, not as a relation to an external receiver.

The provenance row $P$ may name $\InscriptionEventUp$ as the neighboring public-event surface. That provenance supplies a route into record data, but it adds no hidden observer carrier, private source predicate, or receiver object beyond $O$ and $A$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[InscriptionPoint certificate surface]
\label{thm:inscription-point-certificate-surface}
The local $\NameCert_{\InscriptionPointUp}$ surface has five rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier exposure for the finite packet $I=(O,S,A,G,H,C,P,N)$ of \autoref{def:inscription-point-namecert-carrier};
\item supply-socket locality, requiring every supply read to pass through $S$ and $G$;
\item observation-actuality binding, requiring every inscription read to pass through $O$ and $A$;
\item transport and continuation locality through $H$ and $C$ for the displayed rows only;
\item provenance non-escape through $P$ and $N$ over $\BHist$, $\mathsf{GAP}$, $\TranscendentalInductionSocketUp$, $\SupplySocketLedgerUp$, $\InscriptionEventUp$, and $\NameCert$ dependencies.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier exposure is the tuple displayed in \autoref{def:inscription-point-namecert-carrier}.

Supply-socket locality is \autoref{thm:inscription-point-local-supply-socket}. It forces every supply-position read through the displayed socket and GAP rows, so the certificate cannot smuggle a supplied theorem into the carrier.

Observation-actuality binding is \autoref{thm:inscription-point-observation-history-actuality}. It identifies the public inscription read with $O$ and $A$ rather than with a separate receiver object.

The remaining rows are $H$, $C$, $P$, and $N$. They transport, route, record provenance, and name the packet over the listed BEDC dependencies, and no coordinate remains from which a hidden supply source or receiver object could be projected.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[InscriptionPoint GAP boundary non-escape]
\label{thm:inscription-point-gap-boundary-nonescape}
For an accepted $\InscriptionPointUp$ carrier $I=(O,S,A,G,H,C,P,N)$, every supply-facing read remains routed through the displayed supply-socket row $S$ and the GAP boundary row $G$. It cannot escape to a substrate-internal supply theorem, a private source predicate, an external witness selector, or a hidden receiver route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:inscription-point-namecert-carrier}. The only row that names the requested supply position is $S$, and the only row that marks content not supplied internally is $G$.

Apply \autoref{thm:inscription-point-local-supply-socket}. It forces every supply-position read through those two rows and rules out conversion of the request into an internal BEDC theorem.

The transport, continuation, provenance, and naming rows preserve the same displayed packet. Since the carrier has no coordinate for a private source predicate, external witness selector, supplied theorem, or hidden receiver route, the read cannot escape the $S,G$ boundary.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[InscriptionPoint supply-actuality factorization]
\label{thm:inscription-point-supply-actuality-factorization}
Every accepted public inscription read of an $\InscriptionPointUp$ carrier factors through the displayed route
$$
O,S,G,A,H,C,P,N .
$$
The observation row is read first, the supply socket and GAP boundary expose the missing-supply side, the actuality row records the public inscription event, and the structural rows transport, replay, package, and name only that route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier order in \autoref{def:inscription-point-namecert-carrier}. The observation-history row is $O$, the supply-facing rows are $S$ and $G$, and the public inscription row is $A$.

Use \autoref{thm:inscription-point-gap-boundary-nonescape}. It confines the supply-facing part of the read to $S$ and $G$ before any hidden source or supplied theorem can be used.

Apply \autoref{thm:inscription-point-observation-history-actuality}. It binds the public inscription event to $O$ and $A$. The remaining structural rows $H,C,P,N$ then transport, replay, record provenance, and name the same finite route, giving the displayed factorization.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[InscriptionPoint obligation closure package]
\label{thm:inscription-point-obligation-closure-package}
The paper-side $\NameCert_{\InscriptionPointUp}$ obligation surface for an accepted carrier $I=(O,S,A,G,H,C,P,N)$ is assembled from carrier exposure, supply-socket locality, observation-actuality binding, GAP non-escape, transport locality, and provenance non-escape over the neighboring socket chapters. It contains no internally supplied induction rule, selected witness, extensional quotient equality, hidden receiver object, private source predicate, or completed supply theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the carrier of \autoref{def:inscription-point-namecert-carrier}. It lists exactly the rows $O,S,A,G,H,C,P,N$, so carrier exposure supplies no further coordinate from which an additional obligation could be read.

Apply \autoref{thm:inscription-point-local-supply-socket} and \autoref{thm:inscription-point-observation-history-actuality}. These two results bind the supply read to $S,G$ and the public inscription read to $O,A$.

Next use \autoref{thm:inscription-point-gap-boundary-nonescape} and \autoref{thm:inscription-point-supply-actuality-factorization}. The first theorem keeps every supply-facing read inside $S,G$, while the second gives the public inscription route through $O,S,G,A,H,C,P,N$.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:inscription-point-certificate-surface}. Its five certificate rows assemble carrier exposure, socket locality, observation actuality, transport and continuation locality, and provenance non-escape, so the listed obligations form the paper-side package and exclude every hidden supply or receiver coordinate.
\end{proof}
