\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\UniformModulusUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-uniformmodulus-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\UniformModulusUp$ interface packages the finite rational-fold ledger used
by the compact-uniform-continuity argument of
\autoref{ch:visions-compact-uniform-continuity}. Its carrier is not an external
modulus function. It is a finite BEDC certificate row that starts from a
positive tolerance history, folds the compact $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ of local
continuity radii, and exposes a positive precision history through local
$\NameCert$ provenance.

\begin{definition}[Uniform modulus ledger carrier]
\label{def:uniform-modulus-ledger-carrier}
A $\UniformModulusUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ row
$$
U=(\varepsilon,\delta,\mathsf{u},P,N)
$$
with the following visible coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $\varepsilon$ is a positive $\RatUp$ tolerance history;
\item $\delta$ is a positive $\RatUp$ precision history returned by the finite
lower-bound fold;
\item $\mathsf{u}$ records the $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ fold provenance, including
the bundled center rows, local pointwise-modulus rows, subordinate radius rows,
half-radius comparisons, and the proof that $\delta$ is below every folded
radius ledger;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance naming exactly the $\RatUp$, $\MetricUp$,
$\ContinuousUp$, $\CompactUp$, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, $\mathsf{BHist}$,
$\hsame$, $\Cont$, and source NameCert dependency rows used by the fold;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\UniformModulusUp}$ provenance row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers by the displayed $\RatUp$ tolerance
and precision rows, the same finite bundle fold spine, and the same visible
$\Pkg$ and local $\NameCert$ provenance, transported only through stored
$\hsame$ and $\Cont$ rows.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Uniform modulus NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:uniform-modulus-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\UniformModulusUp}$ surface consists exactly of five
obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier formation: the finite row of
\autoref{def:uniform-modulus-ledger-carrier} carries positive $\RatUp$
tolerance and precision histories and one finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ fold
certificate;
\item classifier laws: two accepted rows agree precisely when their displayed
$\RatUp$ rows, bundle-fold provenance, $\Pkg$ row, and local certificate row
agree under the public classifiers;
\item transport: componentwise $\hsame$ transport and visible $\Cont$ routing
preserve the tolerance row, precision row, finite fold spine, and dependency
ledger;
\item finite fold exactness: the $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ fold certifies that the
precision history is below every bundled local radius used by the compact cover;
\item ledger exhaustion: every consumer read factors through the listed
$\RatUp$, $\MetricUp$, $\ContinuousUp$, $\CompactUp$, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$,
$\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the carrier of
\autoref{def:uniform-modulus-ledger-carrier}. The formation row is the displayed
finite tuple, with positivity supplied by the two $\RatUp$ histories and fold
provenance supplied by $\mathsf{u}$.

The classifier row is read componentwise. It compares the tolerance and
precision through their $\RatUp$ classifiers, compares the fold spine through
the stored bundle provenance, and compares the package and local certificate
rows through their public rows.

Transport is also componentwise. The $\hsame$ rows replace histories without
changing the accepted classifier class, and the $\Cont$ rows name the only
routes by which the compact, metric, and continuity ledgers are consulted.

For exactness, unfold the finite bundle provenance in $\mathsf{u}$. Each
successor step of the fold keeps a certified lower bound, so the terminal
precision history is below every local radius row used by the compact cover.

The ledger is exhausted by $P$ and $N$. Since the carrier contains no coordinate
for an external selector, ambient compactness principle, or host real
continuity theorem, the local NameCert surface exports only the listed finite
dependency packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Uniform modulus finite bundle fold stability]
\label{thm:uniform-modulus-finite-bundle-fold-stability}
Let $U=(\varepsilon,\delta,\mathsf{u},P,N)$ be an accepted
$\UniformModulusUp$ carrier. If the compact cover bundle, its local radius
histories, and its pointwise-continuity rows are transported along displayed
$\hsame$ rows and then read through the displayed $\Cont$ routes, the resulting
finite fold accepts the transported precision history in the same classifier
class as $\delta$. In particular, no re-enumeration of the bundle and no
external minimum selector is exposed.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the finite fold spine from $\mathsf{u}$. The base bundle row carries the
initial positive radius ledger, and every successor row carries the comparison
between the accumulator and the next bundled radius.

Transport each radius, center, and pointwise-modulus row along its displayed
$\hsame$ witness. The $\Cont$ routes then recover the same comparison rows at
the transported histories, so the primitive-recursive fold performs the same
accepted lower-bound step at each bundled probe.

The terminal accumulator is therefore transported to a precision history in the
same public classifier class as $\delta$. Because all fold steps are recorded in
the finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ provenance, the certificate does not expose an
external ordering procedure or a second bundle enumeration.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Uniform modulus compact-continuous handoff]
\label{thm:uniform-modulus-compact-continuous-handoff}
Every $\UniformModulusUp$ carrier produced from a compact $\MetricUp$ source and
a $\ContinuousUp$ graph supplies exactly the ledger consumed by
\autoref{thm:compact-uniform-continuity}. For each accepted tolerance history
$\varepsilon$, the precision history $\delta$ and fold certificate
$\mathsf{u}$ route through the compact finite-net rows, the pointwise continuity
rows, the metric triangle and comparison rows, and the local $\NameCert$
provenance, yielding the certified
$\mathsf{UniformModulusLedger}(\varepsilon,\delta,\mathsf{u})$ relation.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The compact certificate supplies the finite cover bundle, center histories,
subordinate radius histories, and half-radius coverage rows. The continuity
certificate supplies the local pointwise-modulus rows at the bundled centers.

Apply the finite fold exactness obligation from
\autoref{thm:uniform-modulus-namecert-obligations}. It gives a positive
precision history below every bundled local radius, with the full fold route
recorded in $\mathsf{u}$.

Now read the metric and continuity dependencies through the package row $P$.
For any two source histories whose source distance is bounded by $\delta$, the
compact cover chooses a bundled center for the first point, the fold certificate
places the second point within the same local continuity radius, and the metric
triangle rows supply the two local comparisons needed by the graph ledger.

The target metric triangle row then gives the tolerance bound
$\varepsilon$. This is exactly the
$\mathsf{UniformModulusLedger}(\varepsilon,\delta,\mathsf{u})$ consumed by
\autoref{thm:compact-uniform-continuity}, and every dependency has been read
through the displayed finite packet.
\end{proof}
