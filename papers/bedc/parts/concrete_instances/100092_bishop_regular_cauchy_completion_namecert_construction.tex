Following Bishop and Bridges' constructive regular Cauchy completion, the $\BishopRegularCauchyCompletionUp$ packet names the finite route by which dyadic tolerances, stream observations, regular rational sequences, and the terminal Real seal are assembled into a completion-facing object. It sits on the L10 spine supplied by the dyadic endpoint ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the finite observation interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational sequence route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the displayed real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet records a regular Cauchy completion boundary for Real and Completion consumers; it does not assert completeness of $\RealUp$, identify quotient streams, select limits, invoke countable choice, or compare host real values.

\begin{definition}[Bishop regular Cauchy completion carrier]
\label{def:bishop-regular-cauchy-completion-carrier}
A $\BishopRegularCauchyCompletionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
B=(E,S,R,D,W,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $E$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint row, containing the dyadic source endpoints and tolerance comparisons used by the regular Cauchy reading.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-observation family opened from the named stream before any completion-facing consumer reads a tail claim.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regularity row, recording the regular rational sequence data exposed by the stream windows and dyadic endpoint ledger.
\item $D$ is the dyadic tail modulus row, assigning each displayed dyadic tolerance a finite tail index and the dyadic comparison budget used at that tail.
\item $W$ is the common-tail window row, recording the shared finite window in which two accepted regular readings may be compared without quotienting their streams.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for endpoints, observations, regularity, tail modulus, common-tail windows, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a Real or Completion tolerance request through $D$, then through $W,S,R,E$, and finally into the displayed $\RealUp$ seal.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BishopRegularCauchyCompletionUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\BishopRegularCauchyCompletionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $E,S,R,D,W,H,C,P,N$. The carrier has no coordinate for quotient stream equality, host real equality, selected limits, countable choice, or ambient completeness of $\RealUp$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Bishop regular Cauchy completion regularity envelope]
\label{thm:bishop-regular-cauchy-completion-regularity-envelope}
For every accepted $\BishopRegularCauchyCompletionUp$ carrier $B=(E,S,R,D,W,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\BishopRegularCauchyCompletionUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the dyadic endpoint row, finite stream observations, regular rational sequence row, dyadic tail modulus, common-tail window, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item classifier transport, requiring componentwise $\hsame$ transport in $H$ and displayed $\Cont$ replay in $C$ to preserve the ordered route $D,W,S,R,E$;
\item regularity envelope, requiring every regular Cauchy read to pass through the displayed $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $R$ and the dyadic endpoint ledger $E$ before reaching the $\RealUp$ seal;
\item common-tail modulus stability, requiring any comparison of two accepted readings to expose the same displayed tail budget in $D$ and the same common-tail window in $W$;
\item Real-seal handoff, requiring any Real consumer to read the terminal $\RealUp$ seal only after $S,R,E,D,W$ have been displayed;
\item ledger non-escape, requiring $P$ and $N$ to export no quotient stream equality, host real equality, selected limit, countable choice, or ambient Real completeness.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:bishop-regular-cauchy-completion-carrier}. The visible packet consists of the endpoint ledger $E$, finite observations $S$, regularity row $R$, dyadic tail modulus $D$, common-tail window $W$, and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

Classifier transport is structural. Componentwise $\hsame$ in $H$ preserves each displayed coordinate, and displayed $\Cont$ replay in $C$ repeats the same ordered route $D,W,S,R,E$ for every accepted tolerance request.

The regularity envelope is forced by the placement of $R$ and $E$. A consumer cannot read the completion boundary until the finite stream windows have exposed regular rational data and the dyadic ledger has supplied the corresponding endpoint and tolerance comparisons.

Common-tail stability is the row $W$ governed by the tail modulus $D$. Two accepted readings are compared only inside the displayed shared window and under the same dyadic budget, so the packet never identifies their underlying streams by quotient equality.

Finally, the Real-seal handoff and ledger boundary are exhausted by $C,P,N$. Since no excluded quotient, host-equality, selected-limit, choice, or Real-completeness coordinate occurs in the carrier, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Bishop regular Cauchy completion seal stability]
\label{thm:bishop-regular-cauchy-completion-seal-stability}

$$
\DyadicRatCoreUp,\ \StreamNameUp,\ \RegSeqRatUp,\ \RealUp,\ \BHist,\ \hsame,\ \Cont,\ \Pkg,\ \NameCert .
$$
Consequently the consumer receives finite endpoint, observation, regularity, tail-modulus, common-tail, transport, replay, provenance, and naming data, and receives no quotient stream equality, host real equality, selected limit, countable-choice schedule, or ambient Real completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Let a $\RealUp$ consumer read an accepted packet $B=(E,S,R,D,W,H,C,P,N)$. Carrier projection gives access only to the displayed rows of \autoref{def:bishop-regular-cauchy-completion-carrier}.

The Real-facing route starts with the tolerance request. The dyadic tail modulus $D$ fixes the tail budget, $W$ identifies the common window, $S$ supplies finite observations, $R$ records regular rational readback, and $E$ supplies the dyadic endpoint comparisons before the terminal $\RealUp$ seal is reached.

The remaining rows transport and replay the same route. The $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ coordinates preserve the finite packet boundary and add no quotient, host-equality, selected-limit, choice, or completeness object.
\end{proof}
