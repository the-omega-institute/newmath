\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\AuditSystemUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-auditsystem-namecert}
\origin{ai}

The $\AuditSystemUp$ packet realizes the total audit theory of \autoref{subsec:philosophy-total-audit} as a finite NameCert surface. It sits beside the kernel-acceptance audit witness of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-kernelacceptanceauditwitness-namecert}, the claim registry layer of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-claimregistrylayer-namecert}, and the failure-certificate packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-failurecertificate-namecert}. Those siblings expose kernel acceptance, positive claim registration, and typed refusal separately; this packet binds admissible claims, positive certificates, failure certificates, cannot-claim rows, export grades, and ledger closure in one finite audit carrier.

\begin{definition}[Audit system carrier]
\label{def:audit-system-carrier}
An $\AuditSystemUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
A=(C,P,F,R,E,L,H,K,Q,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $C$ is the admissible-claim row, recording the claim name, subject surface, carrier, classifier, and intended export site;
\item $P$ is the positive-certificate row, recording the certificate that licenses the claim when the carrier, classifier, ledger, status, and surface fields are present;
\item $F$ is the failure-certificate row, recording the missing, invalid, overstated, unchecked, or axis-confused field when export is blocked;
\item $R$ is the cannot-claim row, recording a typed refusal when the packet is required to expose non-export rather than positive admission;
\item $E$ is the export-grade row, recording whether the claim is read as paper-scoped, theorem-checked, audit-clean, axiom-clean, bridge-checked, or explicitly non-exportable;
\item $L$ is the ledger-closure row, recording that the claim, certificate, failure certificate, refusal row, export grade, provenance, and local naming are closed as one audit packet;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for admissible claim, positive certificate, failure certificate, cannot-claim row, export grade, ledger closure, replay, provenance, and local naming;
\item $K$ stores displayed $\Cont$ replay for reading $C,P,F,R,E,L$ without adding a host-side repair route;
\item $Q$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\AuditSystemUp$, $\KernelAcceptanceAuditWitnessUp$, $\ClaimRegistryLayerUp$, $\FailureCertificateUp$, $\RegistryExportConsistencyGateUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\AuditSystemUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for exporting a claim outside the displayed positive certificate, failure certificate, refusal row, export grade, and ledger-closure surface.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Audit system NameCert surface]
\label{thm:audit-system-namecert-surface}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:audit-system-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\AuditSystemUp}$ surface consists exactly of the admissible-claim row $C$, positive-certificate row $P$, failure-certificate row $F$, cannot-claim row $R$, export-grade row $E$, ledger-closure row $L$, componentwise transport row $H$, replay row $K$, provenance row $Q$, and local naming row $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the finite packet $A=(C,P,F,R,E,L,H,K,Q,N)$. The admissible claim is read first through $C$, but it is not an exported BEDC claim until the certificate and boundary rows are read in the same carrier.

Rows $P,F,R,E$ supply the positive and negative audit faces. The positive row licenses export, the failure row blocks export by naming the failed field, the cannot-claim row records explicit refusal, and the export-grade row records the public strength of the same claim site.

Rows $L,H,K,Q,N$ then close, transport, replay, package, and name the displayed audit data. Since the carrier contains no additional export coordinate, these ten rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Audit system export refusal]
\label{thm:audit-system-export-refusal}
If an accepted $\AuditSystemUp$ packet attempts to export the claim row $C$ while its failure-certificate row $F$ or cannot-claim row $R$ is active for the same claim site, then the packet produces a certificate conflict rather than an exportable claim.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Assume an accepted packet $A=(C,P,F,R,E,L,H,K,Q,N)$. The export-grade row $E$ is read together with the positive certificate $P$, failure certificate $F$, and cannot-claim row $R$.

If $F$ is active for the same claim site, the packet records a missing, invalid, overstated, unchecked, or axis-confused field. The positive certificate cannot erase that negative row, because both rows are displayed before ledger closure is read.

If $R$ is active, the packet records explicit refusal. Again $P$ cannot by itself create an export route, since the carrier admits no coordinate for export outside $P,F,R,E,L$.

Thus the accepted packet exposes a certificate conflict. It may be audited as a blocked or refused claim, but it is not an exportable claim.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Audit system ledger closure]
\label{thm:audit-system-ledger-closure}
Every exported claim admitted by $\AuditSystemUp$ is ledger-closed by the same finite packet that displays its admissible-claim row, certificate row, negative rows, export grade, transport, replay, provenance, and local NameCert row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
By \autoref{def:audit-system-carrier}, the only ledger-closure coordinate is $L$ inside $A=(C,P,F,R,E,L,H,K,Q,N)$. The packet therefore cannot close a claim by citing an external ledger while omitting $C,P,F,R,E$.

The transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows $H,K,Q,N$ preserve the same displayed rows. They do not add a second claim, a second export grade, or a hidden ledger.

Consequently any admitted export is closed by the same finite packet that names its positive and negative audit data.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Audit system independence witness]
\label{thm:audit-system-independence-witness}
The $\AuditSystemUp$ carrier is not bijective, under NameCert-row preservation, to $\ClaimRegistryLayerUp$, $\FailureCertificateUp$, $\RegistryExportConsistencyGateUp$, or $\KernelAcceptanceAuditWitnessUp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Compare the displayed rows. $\ClaimRegistryLayerUp$ registers or refuses package claims, $\FailureCertificateUp$ supplies a typed negative certificate, $\RegistryExportConsistencyGateUp$ blocks inconsistent export, and $\KernelAcceptanceAuditWitnessUp$ records kernel-side acceptance audit data.

None of those siblings carries the simultaneous positive-certificate row $P$, failure-certificate row $F$, cannot-claim row $R$, export-grade row $E$, and ledger-closure row $L$ for the same admissible claim $C$. A NameCert-row-preserving bijection would have to preserve that joint positive and negative audit surface, so no such bijection exists.
\end{proof}
