\begin{definition}[Located sequence modulus carrier]
\label{def:located-sequence-modulus-carrier}

$$
B=(L,M,D,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $L$ is the located sequence-window row. It records the finite rational interval or ball witnesses available at each inspected sequence index, together with the BEDC marks that say which windows are presently exposed.
\item $M$ is the modulus schedule row. It assigns each displayed tolerance request to a finite index bound and records monotone reuse when a tighter or later request is inspected.
\item $D$ is the dyadic tolerance-scale row, read through $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ and carrying the rational error budgets against which the sequence windows are compared.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window row. It names exactly the finite stream coordinates that may be queried before the regular rational or real-facing read is formed.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row. It records the regular Cauchy-facing rational sequence obtained from the located windows, the schedule, the dyadic tolerance scale, and the stream-name window.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row. It packages the real-facing consumer boundary after the regular rational readback has been displayed, without adding a selected limit or quotient-stream coordinate.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the located windows, modulus schedule, dyadic tolerance scale, stream-name window, regular rational readback, real seal, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.

\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedSequenceModulusUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exposes only the finite located-window, modulus, tolerance, stream, readback, and seal boundary. It contains no quotient stream, selected limit, trichotomy principle, countable-choice selector, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness theorem, Lean verification certificate, or checked bridge.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Located sequence modulus NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:located-sequence-modulus-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\LocatedSequenceModulusUp$ carrier $B=(L,M,D,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedSequenceModulusUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed located sequence windows, modulus schedule, dyadic tolerance scale, stream-name window, regular rational readback, real seal, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item located-window coverage, requiring every sequence coordinate used by a consumer to be witnessed in $L$ before it can be compared against the tolerance scale;
\item monotone modulus stability, requiring a bound stored in $M$ to remain valid under later index requests and tighter finite tolerance requests displayed by the packet;
\item dyadic tolerance compatibility, requiring each comparison between sequence windows and scheduled bounds to use the row $D$ rather than an ambient rational approximation convention;
\item $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff, requiring the readback row $R$ to be formed only after the located-window, modulus, tolerance, and stream-name rows have been projected;
\item real-seal non-escape, requiring $E,P,N$ to export no quotient stream, selected limit, trichotomy, countable choice, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness theorem, Lean verification certificate, or checked bridge.
\end{enumerate}
No local obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:located-sequence-modulus-carrier}. The packet displays the located-window row $L$, modulus schedule $M$, dyadic tolerance row $D$, stream-name row $S$, regular rational readback $R$, real seal $E$, and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

Located-window coverage is forced before any readback can occur. A consumer that inspects a sequence coordinate must obtain the corresponding finite witness from $L$, and the witness is compared only after the tolerance request has been routed through $D$.

Monotone modulus stability is stored in the schedule row. The same scheduled bound may be reused for later index windows or tighter displayed tolerances only through $M$, so no external choice of a bound is available to the consumer.

The $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff is then determined by the visible route $L,M,D,S,R$. The readback row $R$ cannot be projected until the located windows, schedule, dyadic tolerance scale, and stream-name window have already been displayed.

Finally, the real seal $E$ is downstream of that readback. Since the remaining rows only transport, replay, package, and name the displayed finite data, the local NameCert has no coordinate from which a quotient stream, selected limit, trichotomy, countable choice, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness theorem, Lean verification certificate, or checked bridge could be obtained.
\end{proof}
