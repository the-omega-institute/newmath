\begin{theorem}[Equicontinuity modulus selection NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:equicontinuity-modulus-selection-namecert-obligations}

$$

the local $\NameCert_{\EquicontinuityModulusSelectionUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the compact finite-net, equicontinuity, rational-fold, metric, uniform-modulus, Real-seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item compact-net exposure, requiring every selected modulus read to display $K$ before the family-radius fold is inspected;
\item family-radius exposure, requiring $E$ to supply the shared equicontinuity radius rows consumed by the finite fold;
\item rational-fold exactness, requiring $Q$ to record the positive lower bound chosen by finite recursion over the displayed net and family rows;
\item uniform-modulus handoff, requiring $U$ and then $R$ to be downstream of $K,E,Q,M$;
\item structural stability, requiring $H,C,P,N$ to preserve, replay, source, and name the same finite selection packet.

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

The rational-fold row then records the finite minimum route. The metric row transports the center estimate to the displayed source comparison, and the uniform-modulus and Real rows receive only that folded ledger.

The structural rows add no selector data. They transport, replay, source, and name the same packet, so the six obligation rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
