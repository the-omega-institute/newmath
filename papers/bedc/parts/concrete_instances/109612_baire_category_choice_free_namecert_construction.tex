Following Baire and Bishop--Bridges constructive analysis, the $\BaireCategoryChoiceFreeUp$ packet records the finite choice-free boundary that Real and Completion consumers may use before any dense-open or Baire-style argument reaches a sealed real name. It sits between the dense-open packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bairecategory-namecert}, the complete metric source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completemetric-namecert}, the finite window interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, and the Specker obstruction surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-speckersequence-namecert}. The packet exposes a displayed finite thread through those rows; it does not assert a host Baire theorem, countable choice, quotient stream equality, selected limits, or ambient completeness for $\RealUp$.

\begin{definition}[BaireCategoryChoiceFree carrier]
\label{def:baire-category-choice-free-carrier}
A $\BaireCategoryChoiceFreeUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(B,M,W,R,S,K,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $B$ is the $\BaireCategoryUp$ dense-open boundary row, read only through the finite schedule and nested-ball thread already named in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bairecategory-namecert}.
\item $M$ is the $\CompleteMetricUp$ source row supplying the displayed complete-metric carrier, ball classifier, Cauchy-thread route, and limit-witness boundary.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row listing the inspected dense-open requests, metric balls, and terminal real-name observations before any consumer reads the boundary.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row converting the displayed windows into regular-rational tolerance data.
\item $S$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after $B,M,W,R$ have been displayed.
\item $K$ is the Specker boundary row. It records that nonconstructive selection or completeness requests are refused by comparison with the finite Specker-style obstruction surface, rather than discharged by countable choice.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $B,M,W,R,S,K$ and the structural rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from dense-open requests through finite windows, regular-rational readback, the real seal, and the refusal row.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BaireCategoryChoiceFreeUp$, $\BaireCategoryUp$, $\CompleteMetricUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\SpeckerSequenceUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\BaireCategoryChoiceFreeUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier compares accepted packets only through $B,M,W,R,S,K,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for countable choice, an infinite selector, host set intersection, quotient stream equality, a selected limit, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[BaireCategoryChoiceFree NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:baire-category-choice-free-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\BaireCategoryChoiceFreeUp$ carrier $Q=(B,M,W,R,S,K,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\BaireCategoryChoiceFreeUp}$ surface consists exactly of the finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the dense-open Baire row, complete-metric source row, finite windows, regular-rational readback, real seal, Specker refusal row, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming;
\item window coherence, requiring every dense-open request consumed from $B$ to pass through the same $\StreamNameUp$ window row $W$ before the readback row $R$ is visible;
\item metric-source coherence, requiring the complete-metric row $M$ to supply the only Cauchy-thread and limit-witness route available to the packet;
\item real-seal exactness, requiring $S$ to be downstream of $B,M,W,R$ and not a detached host real object;
\item choice-free refusal, requiring $K$ to block countable choice, hidden selectors, quotient stream equality, selected limits, and ambient completeness as local certificate data.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:baire-category-choice-free-carrier}. Its rows list the dense-open Baire boundary, complete metric source, finite windows, readback, real seal, Specker refusal row, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming, and no other row is available to the local certificate.

Window coherence follows by reading the $\BaireCategoryUp$ schedule only through $W$. The regular-rational row $R$ is downstream of that same finite window surface, so the carrier cannot replace it with an unlisted stream representative or a selector over all dense-open requests.

Metric-source coherence is supplied by the complete-metric row $M$. The Cauchy thread and limit-witness route are the displayed rows consumed from $M$, and the transport and replay rows $H,C$ preserve that source without creating a second completeness datum.

Finally read the seal and refusal rows. The $\RealUp$ row $S$ is exposed only after $B,M,W,R$, while $K$ records the Specker-style obstruction to turning the packet into countable choice, quotient equality, a selected limit, or ambient completeness. Thus the five obligations exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BaireCategoryChoiceFree ProbeBundle induction]
\label{thm:baire-category-choice-free-probebundle-induction}
Every accepted probe-bundle consumer of a $\BaireCategoryChoiceFreeUp$ packet advances by finite induction over the displayed window row $W$. At each step the consumer reads the current dense-open request from $B$, the complete-metric source row $M$, the regular-rational tolerance row $R$, and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$ before it may inspect the next request. The induction has no branch that selects an infinite sequence of witnesses, identifies quotient streams, chooses a host limit, or invokes ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the finite window row $W$. It is the only row listing the requests visible to the consumer, so the induction domain is a displayed finite packet rather than a countable family.

For the base step, the consumer reads the initial dense-open and metric rows through $B$ and $M$. The readback row $R$ supplies the tolerance data for the inspected window, and $H,C,P,N$ carry only transport, replay, provenance, and local naming.

For the inductive step, the next request is another entry of the same finite $W$ row. The same $B,M,R,H,C,P,N$ route is replayed for that entry, and the refusal row $K$ prevents the step from being interpreted as a hidden selector or a host completeness argument.

Since every step is indexed by an entry already displayed in $W$, the probe-bundle induction remains finite and choice-free. The excluded analytic alternatives have no coordinate in the carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BaireCategoryChoiceFree Real and Completion boundary]
\label{thm:baire-category-choice-free-real-completion-boundary}
Every $\RealUp$ or Completion-facing consumer of an accepted $\BaireCategoryChoiceFreeUp$ packet must pass through the displayed route
$$

It exports no countable choice principle, quotient stream, selected limit, host equality test, or ambient Real completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the carrier first. The only Real-facing endpoint is the row $S$, and $S$ is downstream of the Baire boundary $B$, complete metric row $M$, finite windows $W$, and regular-rational readback $R$.

Apply the NameCert obligations. They force every local naming read through the same carrier, window, metric-source, real-seal, and refusal rows, so a Completion-facing consumer cannot bypass the finite route by citing an ambient completed object.

Use the probe-bundle induction for consumers that inspect several dense-open requests. The induction is finite over $W$ and reuses the same $B,M,R,H,C,P,N$ route at every inspected entry.

The Specker refusal row closes the boundary. It records that requests for countable choice, quotient stream equality, selected limits, host equality, or ambient Real completeness remain outside the packet. Therefore every Real or Completion consumer reads exactly the displayed finite choice-free route before the local NameCert is available.
\end{proof}
