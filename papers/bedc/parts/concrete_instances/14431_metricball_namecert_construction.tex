\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\MetricBallUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-metricball-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Kelley and Bishop--Bridges, the $\MetricBallUp$ packet records the center-radius open ball used by metric-generated topology, but as finite BEDC NameCert rows rather than as an ambient subset. It extracts the singleton-ball datum used inside $\TopologyUp$ into a reusable surface for topology, continuous maps, compact nets, and metric arguments that need the same carrier before generated-open exactness is invoked.

\begin{definition}[MetricBall carrier]
\label{def:metric-ball-carrier}
A $\MetricBallUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
B=(X,d,c,r,\rho,m,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the $\MetricSpaceUp$ carrier row with its point classifier and metric source data.
\item $d$ is the $\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}$ row read from $X$ and valued in the $\RealUp$ distance surface.
\item $c$ is the center row, classified as a point of the carrier named by $X$.
\item $r$ is a $\RatUp$ or $\RealUp$ radius row, according to the chosen radius ledger.
\item $\rho$ is the positive-radius ledger for $r$, transported through the ordered-real comparison used by the metric source.
\item $m$ is the membership and distance-comparison row. For a displayed point $x$, it records the comparison $d(c,x)<_{\mathsf R}r$ and names this comparison as the $\mathsf{MetricOpenBall}_d(c,r,\rho)$ membership read.
\item $H$ records the $\hsame$ transports for the carrier, distance witness, center, radius, positive-radius ledger, and membership-comparison row.
\item $C$ records the $\Cont$ routes that replay distance evaluation, radius comparison, membership transport, finite-base refinement, and singleton-ball handoff.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\MetricBallUp$, $\MetricSpaceUp$, $\RealUp$, $\RatUp$, $\TopologyUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MetricBallUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The accepted carrier contains no ambient powerset coordinate, no unindexed open predicate, no quotient equality test on points or radii, and no generated topology closure field.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[MetricBall NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:metric-ball-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted packet $B=(X,d,c,r,\rho,m,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\MetricBallUp}$ obligation surface consists exactly of carrier admission, classifier transport, radius positivity stability, membership transport, finite refinement and intersection readiness, and ledger non-escape for the displayed rows $X,d,c,r,\rho,m,H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission reads $X$ as the metric carrier and $d$ as the distance witness over that carrier. The center row $c$ and radius row $r$ are admitted only relative to those source rows, and $\rho$ supplies the displayed positive-radius ledger.

Classifier transport is supplied by $H$. Transporting the carrier, distance witness, center, radius, and positive-radius rows moves the comparison data in $m$ to the transported copy without introducing a second point classifier or a second radius equality test.

Membership transport is the distance-comparison part of the same ledger. The row $m$ names the predicate $d(c,x)<_{\mathsf R}r$, while $C$ replays the route from distance evaluation to ordered-real comparison.

Finite refinement and intersection readiness are only readiness claims. They expose the singleton-ball row and its replayable comparison so that a finite list of such rows can later be concatenated by $\TopologyUp$, but they do not themselves close arbitrary generated opens.

Ledger non-escape follows by inspecting the carrier. Every exported read is one of $X,d,c,r,\rho,m,H,C,P,N$, so no ambient powerset, unindexed open predicate, quotient equality test, or generated topology closure field can be recovered from the packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetricBall topology handoff]
\label{thm:metric-ball-topology-handoff}
Every $\TopologyUp$ metric-generated singleton-ball open read factors through a $\MetricBallUp$ packet before it enters generated-open exactness. In particular, the singleton ball datum of \autoref{def:metric-finite-ball-indexed-topology} and the singleton carrying theorem of \autoref{thm:metric-balls-instantiate-indexed-topology-opens} are read as the $X,d,c,r,\rho,m,H,C,P,N$ rows of $\MetricBallUp$ and only then handed to the indexed topology carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a metric-generated singleton-ball open in $\TopologyUp$. By \autoref{def:metric-finite-ball-indexed-topology}, its datum is a center, a positive radius, and the open-ball predicate obtained from the distance comparison.

Package these entries as $X,d,c,r,\rho,m$. The metric carrier and distance witness supply $X$ and $d$, the singleton datum supplies $c,r,\rho$, and the predicate $\mathsf{MetricOpenBall}_d$ supplies the membership-comparison row $m$.

Add the structural rows $H,C,P,N$. They are exactly the transports, continuation replays, provenance, and local name row required by \autoref{def:metric-ball-carrier}.

Now apply \autoref{thm:metric-balls-instantiate-indexed-topology-opens}. Its singleton carrying equation reads the same membership row and places the singleton index inside $\mathcal{T}_{d,\mathsf{ball}}$. Thus the generated-open exactness package receives a carried singleton-ball index only after the $\MetricBallUp$ packet has exposed the center-radius NameCert surface.
\end{proof}
