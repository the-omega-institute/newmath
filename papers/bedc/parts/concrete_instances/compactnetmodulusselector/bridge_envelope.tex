\begin{theorem}[Compact net modulus selector bridge envelope]
\label{thm:compact-net-modulus-selector-bridge-envelope}
The public compact-net selector handoff of \autoref{thm:compactnetmodulusselector-public-modulus-handoff} is the paper-side bridge envelope from compact finite-net rows, pointwise-modulus rows, lower-bound fold rows, selected precision, and $\UniformModulusUp$ consumer rows to the standard acceptance schema. For every accepted $\CompactNetModulusSelectorUp$ packet $S=(X,Y,E,\mathcal{P},C,R,M,F,D,H,Q,P,N)$, the envelope reads the compact source rows $X,\mathcal{P},C,R$, pointwise rows $M$, fold row $F$, selected precision $D$, and consumer rows $H,Q,P,N$ in that order, and exports no open-cover compactness, host minimum, selected center outside $\mathcal{P}$, quotient point equality, sequential compactness, or external modulus function.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the accepted selector carrier and the public handoff theorem. The finite compact source is already displayed as $X,\mathcal{P},C,R$, so the envelope begins with the compact finite-net rows rather than an open-cover compactness principle.

Next read the pointwise-modulus and fold rows. The rows $M,F,D$ bind each pointwise radius ledger to the selected precision by the lower-bound fold, so the handoff cannot be reinterpreted as a host minimum or an external modulus.

Finally pass to the consumer rows $H,Q,P,N$. They provide componentwise transport, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming for the same packet, matching the acceptance schema's demand for a single visible source route.

The excluded rows are absent from the carrier and from the public handoff. Hence the displayed handoff is precisely the bridge envelope from the compact selector packet to the standard acceptance boundary.
\end{proof}
