\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\BaireSpaceUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-bairespace-namecert}

The $\BaireSpaceUp$ interface names Brouwer-style Baire space as a certificate
for natural-number-indexed streams whose observations are displayed finite
unary histories. The BEDC surface does not treat infinite sequences as
primitive histories. It records finite prefix windows, a prefix-cylinder
classifier, stream-name scheduling rows, and package provenance that can be
read by $\RegSeqRatUp$ and $\RealUp$ consumers.

\begin{definition}[BaireSpace stream carrier]
\label{def:baire-space-stream-carrier}
A $\BaireSpaceUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
B=(s,w,c,l,p)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $s$ is a $\StreamNameUp$ schedule row whose $n$th displayed request
returns a finite $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ answer row;
\item $w$ is a finite prefix-window ledger listing the requested natural
indices and the unary answer rows displayed for those indices;
\item $c$ is the prefix-cylinder classifier row: two packets classify together
over a displayed window exactly when their listed unary answer rows are paired
componentwise by $\hsame$ through the same scheduled indices;
\item $l$ is the local ledger exposing the $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet, the
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ answer rows, the finite index window, the
componentwise $\hsame$ comparisons, and the $\Cont$ routes used by prefix
restriction;
\item $p$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance row naming the $\NatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$,
$\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ certificate boundaries
available to consumers.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate is $\mathsf{BairePrefix}(s,w,c,l,p)$. It accepts only
finite prefix displays of a natural-number-indexed stream; every public read
is routed through the selected window and the displayed classifier row.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[BaireSpace prefix-cylinder stability]
\label{thm:baire-space-prefix-cylinder-stability}
Let $B=(s,w,c,l,p)$ be an accepted $\BaireSpaceUp$ carrier. If $B'$ is obtained
from $B$ by transporting the stream schedule, every displayed unary answer in a
finite prefix window, the classifier row, the local ledger, and the provenance
row by the $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ data recorded in $l$, then $B'$ is again an
accepted carrier. The prefix-cylinder classifier for the transported window
reads exactly the transported unary rows and no tail beyond that window.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:baire-space-stream-carrier}. The only
data visible to the classifier are the stream schedule, the finite index
window, and the unary answer rows listed by that window.

Transport each listed row through the displayed $\hsame$ comparisons in the
ledger. The $\Cont$ routes in the same ledger restrict the stream readback to
the transported prefix, so the repacked tuple has the same carrier shape.

The classifier row is a cylinder classifier. It compares exactly the displayed
prefix entries, and no coordinate in the carrier stores an infinite tail as a
history object. Thus the transported packet is accepted with the transported
prefix-cylinder read.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BaireSpace prefix-restriction composition]
\label{thm:baire-space-prefix-restriction-composition}
Let $B=(s,w,c,l,p)$ be an accepted $\BaireSpaceUp$ carrier. Suppose the ledger
$l$ displays a prefix restriction from $B$ to $B_1$ and then a second prefix
restriction from $B_1$ to $B_2$, each recorded by the corresponding $\hsame$
comparisons and $\Cont$ routes on the stream schedule, finite index window,
unary answer rows, classifier row, and provenance row. Then the composite
restriction is again an accepted $\BaireSpaceUp$ prefix packet.

Its visible unary rows are exactly the twice-restricted subwindow of $w$, its
classifier is the componentwise $\hsame$ restriction of the original
prefix-cylinder classifier $c$ to that subwindow, and its provenance is still
the same $\Pkg$ row $p$. The composite packet introduces no hidden infinite
tail and no host sequence equality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet $B$ through
\autoref{def:baire-space-stream-carrier}. The first displayed restriction is a
transport of the schedule, window, unary rows, classifier, ledger, and
provenance along rows already recorded in $l$.

Use \autoref{thm:baire-space-prefix-cylinder-stability} on this first
restriction. The result is an accepted prefix packet $B_1$ whose classifier
reads only the first restricted finite subwindow.

Apply the same stability theorem to the second displayed restriction out of
$B_1$. The $\Cont$ routes compose inside the displayed ledger, so the resulting
packet reads precisely the subwindow obtained by restricting the original
prefix window twice.

It remains to identify the classifier and provenance. The classifier at each
listed coordinate is obtained by the componentwise $\hsame$ restriction of the
original cylinder row $c$, and the $\Pkg$ coordinate is transported as the same
provenance row $p$.

Finally, the ledger coverage statement of
\autoref{thm:baire-space-ledger-coverage} rules out every consumer read not
listed in the finite window. Hence the composite construction cannot expose an
infinite tail or an equality of host sequences.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BaireSpace NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:baire-space-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\BaireSpaceUp}$ obligation surface consists of five
finite rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier: the stream carrier of
\autoref{def:baire-space-stream-carrier};
\item classifier: the prefix-cylinder row comparing displayed unary answers
componentwise over a finite index window;
\item stability: prefix-window transport as in
\autoref{thm:baire-space-prefix-cylinder-stability};
\item exactness: a public observation is readable from the certificate iff it
is one of the finite unary rows listed by the selected prefix window;
\item provenance: downstream reads factor through the displayed $\NatUp$,
$\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ package
rows carried by $p$.
\end{enumerate}
These rows are the complete seed obligation for naming countable stream space
data over BEDC histories.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier and classifier rows are the first two projections of
\autoref{def:baire-space-stream-carrier}. They expose the scheduled stream
read, the finite index window, and the unary answer rows over that window.

Stability is supplied by
\autoref{thm:baire-space-prefix-cylinder-stability}. Transporting the
schedule, window, answer rows, ledger, and provenance repacks the same finite
prefix surface.

For exactness, inspect the coordinates of the packet. A public observation can
only be read from the prefix ledger $w$ through the classifier row $c$ and the
routes in $l$. The provenance row $p$ names the available consumers but does
not add a second observation channel.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BaireSpace real-name handoff]
\label{thm:baire-space-real-name-handoff}
Every $\RegSeqRatUp$ or $\RealUp$ consumer of an accepted $\BaireSpaceUp$
packet reads the stream only through finite prefix windows. A regular rational
sequence consumer may read a requested unary index and its scheduled answer
row, and a real-name consumer may combine that finite read with the
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$ provenance named by $p$. Neither consumer receives a
primitive infinite history, a completed real, or an unlogged tail equality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet through
\autoref{def:baire-space-stream-carrier}. The stream schedule is present only
through finite request rows, and the answers are the unary histories displayed
in $w$.

A $\RegSeqRatUp$ consumer therefore obtains a finite index request and the
corresponding unary answer row. Transport of that read is controlled by the
prefix-cylinder classifier and the ledger routes.

A $\RealUp$ consumer can additionally use the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ provenance
named by $p$ when interpreting the finite answer as a dyadic or regular
sequence component. The packet still exposes only the selected prefix rows, so
no completed infinite object is exported.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BaireSpace ledger coverage]
\label{thm:baire-space-ledger-coverage}
For an accepted carrier $B=(s,w,c,l,p)$, the ledger $l$ covers every public
row of the $\BaireSpaceUp$ packet: the $\mathsf{BHist}$ stream packet, the
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ answer rows, the finite prefix window, the
prefix-cylinder classifier, the componentwise $\hsame$ comparisons, the
prefix-restriction $\Cont$ routes, and the package provenance. The ledger
exports no apartness principle, choice functional, host sequence equality, or
real-completeness assertion.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the packet coordinates one by one. The stream schedule $s$, prefix window
$w$, classifier $c$, and provenance $p$ are all named fields of the carrier,
and the ledger $l$ records the comparison and restriction routes among them.

Any public row must be read from one of those fields. Prefix restriction uses
the displayed $\Cont$ route, while equality of observations uses the displayed
componentwise $\hsame$ rows in the cylinder classifier.

The omitted objects would require extra coordinates: an apartness principle,
a choice functional, a host equality on full sequences, or a completeness
theorem for real names. No such coordinate occurs in the carrier, so the
ledger coverage is exactly the listed finite surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BaireSpace cylinder-tail coverage]
\label{thm:baire-space-cylinder-tail-coverage}
Every finite prefix-cylinder consumer read from an accepted $\BaireSpaceUp$
packet factors through the displayed $\StreamNameUp$ schedule, the
prefix-restriction ledger, $\hsame$ transport, $\Cont$ routes, $\mathsf{Pkg}$
provenance, and the $\RealUp$ handoff boundary. The read exposes no completed
infinite sequence, no classical topology, and no choice-selected branch.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the stream carrier of \autoref{def:baire-space-stream-carrier}. Its public
coordinates are the schedule, finite prefix window, prefix-cylinder classifier,
ledger, and provenance rows, so a cylinder-tail consumer begins with a finite
window named by that carrier.

Use \autoref{thm:baire-space-prefix-cylinder-stability} to keep the cylinder
answer stable under displayed prefix reads, and
\autoref{thm:baire-space-prefix-restriction-composition} to compose the
restriction ledger. These steps route the consumer through the same $\hsame$
transport and $\Cont$ paths already recorded in the packet.

The real-facing boundary is exactly the finite handoff of
\autoref{thm:baire-space-real-name-handoff}, and
\autoref{thm:baire-space-ledger-coverage} exhausts the remaining public rows.
No carrier coordinate stores a completed sequence, a topological completion, or
a choice-selected branch, so such data cannot be exported by a prefix-cylinder
read.
\end{proof}
