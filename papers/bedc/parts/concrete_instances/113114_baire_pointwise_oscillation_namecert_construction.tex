\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\BairePointwiseOscillationUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-baire-pointwise-oscillation-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Baire and Lebesgue on pointwise oscillation, the $\BairePointwiseOscillationUp$ packet records the finite value-window evidence by which a pointwise-limit ledger exposes an oscillation bound at named source points. It reads the source and approximant windows supplied by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-baireonefunction-namecert}, checks the rational value windows through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, seals the endpoint observations through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, keeps the inspection schedule visible through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, and exports only the epigraph-facing handoff that \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-lowersemicontinuous-namecert} may consume. The packet is a local certificate for displayed oscillation data, not a topology of function spaces and not a theorem about all discontinuity sets.

\begin{definition}[Baire pointwise oscillation carrier]
\label{def:baire-pointwise-oscillation-carrier}
A $\BairePointwiseOscillationUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(X,F,S,Q^-,Q^+,R^-,R^+,B,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the source-context row inherited from the $\BaireOneFunctionUp$ packet, naming the source points and basic-open evidence inspected by the oscillation certificate.
\item $F$ is the pointwise-limit family ledger of $\BaireOneFunctionUp$ approximant observations whose two displayed value windows are being compared.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule row selecting the two finite inspection windows, the source point, and the precision request.
\item $Q^-$ and $Q^+$ are the lower and upper $\RegSeqRatUp$ rational readback rows for the two displayed value windows.
\item $R^-$ and $R^+$ are the corresponding $\RealUp$ seals, reached only after $X,F,S,Q^-,Q^+$ have been displayed.
\item $B$ is the rational oscillation-bound row recording a displayed $b\in\mathbb{Q}_{\ge 0}$ with the certified window inequality $R^+ - R^- \leq b$ at the named source point.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the source context, pointwise-limit ledger, stream schedule, rational readbacks, real seals, oscillation bound, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a source-point request through the $\BaireOneFunctionUp$ value windows, the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readbacks, the $\RealUp$ seals, the rational oscillation bound, and the lower-semicontinuous handoff surface.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BairePointwiseOscillationUp$, $\BaireOneFunctionUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\LowerSemicontinuousUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\BairePointwiseOscillationUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exports no ambient topology on a function space, no arbitrary selector of subsequences, no quotient equality of real names, no global discontinuity classification, and no completeness principle for $\RealUp$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Baire pointwise oscillation NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:baire-pointwise-oscillation-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\BairePointwiseOscillationUp$ carrier $K=(X,F,S,Q^-,Q^+,R^-,R^+,B,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\BairePointwiseOscillationUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the source context, pointwise-limit family ledger, stream schedule, lower and upper rational readbacks, lower and upper real seals, rational oscillation bound, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item value-window admission, requiring both displayed value windows to be read from the same $\BaireOneFunctionUp$ pointwise-limit ledger over the same source row $X$;
\item schedule coherence, requiring the $\StreamNameUp$ row $S$ to name the source point, precision request, and two finite approximant windows used by $Q^-$ and $Q^+$;
\item rational readback exactness, requiring $Q^-$ and $Q^+$ to feed the real seals $R^-$ and $R^+$ before the bound row $B$ is available;
\item oscillation-bound stability, requiring the displayed inequality $R^+ - R^- \leq b$ in $B$ to be preserved by the componentwise $\hsame$ rows in $H$;
\item handoff compatibility, allowing $\LowerSemicontinuousUp$ consumers to read only the displayed oscillation-bound row and the route that produced it;
\item ledger refusal, excluding host function-space topology, arbitrary subsequence selection, quotient equality of real names, global discontinuity theorems, and completeness of $\RealUp$.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:baire-pointwise-oscillation-carrier}. The accepted packet exposes exactly the source context, pointwise-limit family ledger, stream schedule, two rational readbacks, two real seals, rational bound, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.

Value-window admission and schedule coherence come from the rows $F$ and $S$. Both value windows are read from the same $\BaireOneFunctionUp$ ledger, and $S$ records the finite inspection windows before any rational readback can be used.

Rational readback exactness is forced by the order of the route. The rows $Q^-$ and $Q^+$ feed the real seals $R^-$ and $R^+$, and the bound row $B$ is downstream of those seals.

Transport stability is componentwise. The $\hsame$ rows in $H$ identify the displayed source, ledger, schedule, readback, seal, bound, replay, provenance, and naming coordinates, so the displayed inequality is preserved along accepted classifier moves.

The lower-semicontinuous handoff is a read of the same route. Since $P$ and $N$ contain no coordinate for ambient topology, arbitrary subsequences, quotient equality, global discontinuity classification, or $\RealUp$ completeness, the listed obligations exhaust the certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BaireOneFunction oscillation handoff]
\label{thm:baire-pointwise-oscillation-baireone-handoff}
Every $\BairePointwiseOscillationUp$ consumer row exported by an accepted carrier factors through the displayed $\BaireOneFunctionUp$ value-window route
$$
X,F,S,Q^-,Q^+ \longmapsto R^-,R^+ \longmapsto B .
$$
Consequently a downstream $\LowerSemicontinuousUp$ consumer may use the rational oscillation bound and the displayed provenance of its two value windows, but it receives no host function-space topology, no arbitrary subsequence selector, no quotient equality of real names, and no global theorem about the discontinuity set of a Baire-one function.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a consumer of the oscillation-bound row $B$. By carrier admission, $B$ appears only after the source row $X$, the $\BaireOneFunctionUp$ family ledger $F$, the schedule $S$, the rational readbacks $Q^-,Q^+$, and the real seals $R^-,R^+$.

Replay the continuation row $C$. The source-point request first reads the pointwise-limit ledger, then selects the two finite value windows through $S$, checks their rational windows through $Q^-$ and $Q^+$, and seals the endpoints as $R^-$ and $R^+$.

The bound row is therefore a downstream finite certificate, not an independent analytic object. Transport by $H$ preserves the same coordinates, and the provenance row $P$ records only the named packets in the displayed route.

A $\LowerSemicontinuousUp$ consumer can read the handoff only through that route. Since no excluded coordinate is present in the packet, the handoff exports neither ambient topology nor any global discontinuity theorem.
\end{proof}
