\begin{definition}[Heine--Borel net compactness carrier]
\label{def:heine-borel-net-compactness-carrier}

$$
K=(I,F,R,W,D,E,H,C,P,N)

\begin{enumerate}
\item $I$ is the located closed bounded interval row, recording the endpoint order, interval membership classifier, and interval-source readback.
\item $F$ is the finite net row, read through $\HeineBorelFiniteNetUp$ and $\CompactMetricUp$, naming the displayed centers, mesh radius, cell coverage witnesses, and compact-metric provenance.
\item $R$ is the positive-radius ledger, recording the requested positive tolerance, its dyadic lower witness, and the comparison surface used by cover readers.

\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row used to compare mesh radius, endpoint error, cover radius, and finite-net budget.

\begin{theorem}[Heine--Borel net compactness obligations]
\label{thm:heine-borel-net-compactness-obligations}
For every accepted $\HeineBorelNetCompactnessUp$ carrier $K=(I,F,R,W,D,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\HeineBorelNetCompactnessUp}$ surface consists exactly of located interval admission, finite-net admission, positive-radius ledger exposure, finite cover-read witnesses, dyadic tolerance readback, Real-seal readback, componentwise transport, displayed continuation replay, provenance, and local naming. Every compactness-facing read must pass through the displayed rows before using a finite-net compactness conclusion.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:heine-borel-net-compactness-carrier}. The tuple displays exactly the rows $I,F,R,W,D,E,H,C,P,N$, so the local classifier has no access to data outside that packet.

The interval and finite-net sides are read first. The located interval row $I$ supplies the endpoint and membership surface, while $F$ supplies the finite centers and coverage witnesses inherited from the finite-net and compact-metric siblings.

The cover-facing side is finite as well. The positive-radius ledger $R$ fixes the requested tolerance, $W$ records the inspected cover witnesses, $D$ gives the dyadic comparison budget, and $E$ seals only the endpoint and radius reads that are already attached to those rows.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same route. Since no row contains open covers as an unrestricted object, selected subcovers, quotient interval equality, countable choice, or ambient real completeness, the listed rows exhaust the NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\label{thm:heine-borel-net-compactness-finite-subcover-route}

\begin{proof}

Next read the tolerance side. The positive-radius ledger $R$ and dyadic tolerance row $D$ control the requested mesh and cover radius, while $W$ exposes only the finite cover reads attached to the displayed centers and interval boundary cells.

The terminal Real row $E$ seals those endpoint and radius reads without adding a completeness theorem. The transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve the same finite route for each sibling consumer.

Therefore the public handoff is exactly the finite-subcover route displayed in the statement. The excluded open-cover, selected-subcover, quotient, choice, and ambient-completeness data have no carrier row through which they could be exported.
\end{proof}
