\begin{theorem}[Tail budget coherence NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:tail-budget-coherence-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:tail-budget-coherence-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\TailBudgetCoherenceUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $T=(M,O,S,A,L,W,R,D,H,C,P,N)$, TailMeet threshold exposure through $M$, observation-budget exposure through $O$, selector-budget exposure through $S$, agreement-seal exposure through $A$, limit-seal exposure through $L$, finite window service through $W$, regular readback through $R$, dyadic tolerance accounting through $D$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the displayed packet. Its coordinates are exactly $M,O,S,A,L,W,R,D,H,C,P,N$, so carrier admission and local naming cannot introduce a second tail threshold, budget, readback, agreement seal, or limit seal.

Read the route rows in dependency order. The threshold row $M$ fixes the shared tail boundary; $O$ and $S$ bound the observation and selector requests; $W,D,R$ display the finite windows, tolerance ledger, and regular-rational readback available after those budget rows.

The seal rows are then read through the same route. The agreement row $A$ and limit row $L$ are attached to the displayed readback surface, while $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, package, and name only that finite packet.

Thus the NameCert surface is composite rather than atomic. It packages the displayed sibling rows from the tail-meet, observation-budget, selector-budget, agreement-seal, limit-seal, stream-window, readback, and dyadic-ledger chapters, and it exports no quotient streams, host real equality, selected limits, or ambient completeness.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}
