\chapter{A Conditional Certificate Boundary for $\RealAnalyticUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-real-analytic-namecert}

This chapter records the proof obligations for the constructive transcendental package $\RealAnalyticUp$ over $\RatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRat$, and $\RealUp$ without asserting a completed $\NameCert(\RealAnalyticUp)$. The package is organized around five interrelated objects on $\RealUp$-history representations: the constant $\pi$, the exponential $\exp$, the logarithm $\log$ on positive arguments, and the trigonometric pair $\sin / \cos$. The construction boundary sits above $\RatUp$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rat-namecert}) for the rational base, $\StreamNameUp$ for explicit unary streams, $\RegSeqRat$ for regular Cauchy data, and $\RealUp$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}) for the eventual real-history carrier. Every series below is given by an explicit unary-history term, every modulus is a total function on the unary index domain, and every passage from regular rational data to a real-history value requires a supplied limit witness from the Phase Real and Completion obligations.

\section{Constructive \(\pi\) via Leibniz acceleration}
\label{sec:real-analytic-pi}

\begin{definition}[Leibniz partial sum]
\label{def:real-analytic-leibniz-partial-sum}
For unary index \(n\), the Leibniz partial sum predicate \(\mathsf{LeibPart}(n, S_n)\) holds when \(S_n\) is the explicit \(\RatUp\)-history obtained by summing the first \(n\) terms of \(\sum_{k \geq 0} (-1)^k / (2k+1)\) via unary recursion using \(\RatUp\) addition and signed-fraction subtraction.
\end{definition}

\begin{lemma}[Leibniz alternating-series Cauchy modulus]
\label{lem:real-analytic-leibniz-cauchy}
The sequence of partial sums \(S_n\) is a \(\RegSeqRat\)-Cauchy sequence with explicit modulus \(N(k) := 2^{k+1}\): for all unary \(m, n \geq N(k)\), \(|4 S_m - 4 S_n| < 2^{-k}\).
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Pair adjacent terms after index \(N(k)\); each pair has magnitude bounded by the next omitted term \(1/(2N(k)+1)\). The alternating bound gives \(|4 S_m - 4 S_n| \leq 4/(2N(k)+1) < 2^{-k}\) for the chosen \(N(k)\).
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[Constructive \(\pi\)]
\label{def:real-analytic-pi}
$\mathsf{Pi}$ is a $\RealUp$-history candidate only together with a supplied limit witness from the Phase Real and Completion obligations, applied to the $\RegSeqRat$ Cauchy data of \autoref{lem:real-analytic-leibniz-cauchy} for the rescaled sequence $(4 S_n)_n$. The local data of this chapter is the $\RegSeqRat$ name and its explicit modulus inherited from the alternating-series bound.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}
The Leibniz series is the simplest constructive presentation; faster-converging series (Machin, Chudnovsky) define the same \(\RealUp\)-history up to \(\RegSeqRat\)-classifier equivalence, with smaller explicit moduli. Only the existence of \emph{some} explicit modulus is required by the certificate.
\end{remark}

\section{Constructive exponential}
\label{sec:real-analytic-exp}

\begin{definition}[Exponential partial sum]
\label{def:real-analytic-exp-part}
For \(\RealUp\)-history \(x\) and unary index \(n\), the predicate \(\mathsf{ExpPart}(x, n, S_n)\) holds when \(S_n\) is the partial sum \(\sum_{k=0}^{n} x^k / k!\) computed by unary recursion: powers \(x^k\) via iterated \(\RealUp\) multiplication, factorials \(k!\) via the unary factorial of \(\PrimeUp\) (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-prime-namecert}), and division as \(\RealUp\)-multiplicative inverse against the positive unary denominator.
\end{definition}

\begin{lemma}[Exponential Cauchy modulus on bounded inputs]
\label{lem:real-analytic-exp-cauchy}
For every \(\RealUp\)-history \(x\) with \(|x| \leq B\) (explicit unary upper bound \(B\)), the partial sums \(\mathsf{ExpPart}(x, n, S_n)\) form a \(\RegSeqRat\)-Cauchy sequence with explicit modulus \(N(k) := 2 B + k\): for all \(m, n \geq N(k)\), \(|S_m - S_n| < 2^{-k}\).
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
For \(n \geq 2B\), the ratio of successive terms \(|x|/(n+1)\) is bounded by \(1/2\), so the tail majorizes a geometric series with ratio \(1/2\). The geometric-series modulus \(2^{-(n - 2B)}\) gives the explicit \(N(k)\) above.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[Constructive \(\exp\)]
\label{def:real-analytic-exp}
For a $\RealUp$-history $x$, $\mathsf{Exp}(x, y)$ holds in this chapter only relative to a supplied $\RealUp$ limit witness from the Phase Real and Completion obligations for the partial sums $\mathsf{ExpPart}(x, \cdot, \cdot)$ and the Cauchy modulus of \autoref{lem:real-analytic-exp-cauchy}. The local witness includes the explicit input bound $B$ and the modulus $N$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Exponential additivity]
\label{thm:real-analytic-exp-add}
For all \(\RealUp\)-histories \(x, y\) and corresponding \(\mathsf{Exp}(x, e_x)\), \(\mathsf{Exp}(y, e_y)\), \(\mathsf{Exp}(x + y, e_{x+y})\), the equality \(\mathsf{RealClassifier}(e_{x+y}, e_x \cdot e_y)\) holds.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Compare the partial sums of \(\exp(x+y)\) and \(\exp(x) \cdot \exp(y)\) by Cauchy product; the tail bound is dominated by the geometric tail of \autoref{lem:real-analytic-exp-cauchy} applied at \(B = |x| + |y|\). The classifier equality then follows from limit uniqueness.
\end{proof}

\section{Constructive logarithm on positives}
\label{sec:real-analytic-log}

\begin{definition}[Logarithm via inverse-of-\(\exp\) bisection]
\label{def:real-analytic-log}
For a positive $\RealUp$-history $x$ (i.e.\ apartness witness $x \neq 0$ with positive sign), $\mathsf{Log}(x, y)$ holds in this chapter only relative to a supplied $\RealUp$ limit witness for the dyadic bisection sequence on unary precision $k$: at each precision, locate the dyadic-rational interval $[y_k, y_k + 2^{-k}]$ such that $\exp(y_k) \leq x < \exp(y_k + 2^{-k})$ (decidable on rational representations using \autoref{def:real-analytic-exp}), and assemble the local $\RegSeqRat$ data with modulus $N(k) = k + \lceil \log_2(1/x) \rceil$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Logarithm is the inverse of \(\exp\) on positives]
\label{thm:real-analytic-log-exp-inverse}
For positive \(\RealUp\)-history \(x\) with \(\mathsf{Log}(x, y)\) and \(\mathsf{Exp}(y, e_y)\), \(\mathsf{RealClassifier}(e_y, x)\) holds. Conversely, for \(\RealUp\)-history \(z\) with \(\mathsf{Exp}(z, w)\), \(\mathsf{Log}(w, z')\) gives \(\mathsf{RealClassifier}(z', z)\).
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The bisection construction in \autoref{def:real-analytic-log} explicitly enforces \(\exp(y_k) \to x\) at every precision; limit uniqueness gives the forward direction. The reverse direction is the same construction applied to \(\exp(z)\).
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Logarithm of a unary natural]
\label{thm:real-analytic-log-unary}
For positive unary natural \(n\), \(\mathsf{Log}(\Eone^n(\emp), y_n)\) gives a constructive \(\log n\) used by the Dirichlet term construction (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dirichlet-series-namecert}).
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Specialize \autoref{def:real-analytic-log} to the rational embedding of the unary natural; the bisection terminates with explicit modulus determined by the unary length of \(n\).
\end{proof}

\section{Constructive sine and cosine}
\label{sec:real-analytic-trig}

\begin{definition}[Sine and cosine partial sums]
\label{def:real-analytic-trig-part}
For \(\RealUp\)-history \(x\) and unary index \(n\), \(\mathsf{SinPart}(x, n, S_n)\) and \(\mathsf{CosPart}(x, n, C_n)\) are the partial sums \(\sum_{k=0}^{n} (-1)^k x^{2k+1}/(2k+1)!\) and \(\sum_{k=0}^{n} (-1)^k x^{2k}/(2k)!\) respectively, computed by unary recursion as in \autoref{def:real-analytic-exp-part}.
\end{definition}

\begin{lemma}[Trigonometric Cauchy modulus]
\label{lem:real-analytic-trig-cauchy}
For every \(\RealUp\)-history \(x\) with \(|x| \leq B\), the partial sums \(\mathsf{SinPart}\) and \(\mathsf{CosPart}\) form \(\RegSeqRat\)-Cauchy sequences with explicit modulus \(N(k) := B^2 + k\). The bound is the alternating-series majorant: each term has magnitude bounded by the previous one once \(2k(2k+1) > B^2\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{definition}[Constructive \(\sin\) and \(\cos\)]
\label{def:real-analytic-sin-cos}
$\mathsf{Sin}(x, s)$ and $\mathsf{Cos}(x, c)$ hold in this chapter only relative to supplied $\RealUp$ limit witnesses from the Phase Real and Completion obligations for $\mathsf{SinPart}(x, \cdot, \cdot)$ and $\mathsf{CosPart}(x, \cdot, \cdot)$ respectively, using the explicit Cauchy moduli of \autoref{lem:real-analytic-trig-cauchy}.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Pythagorean identity]
\label{thm:real-analytic-pythagorean}
For all \(\RealUp\)-history \(x\) with \(\mathsf{Sin}(x, s)\) and \(\mathsf{Cos}(x, c)\), \(\mathsf{RealClassifier}(s^2 + c^2, \Eone(\emp))\) holds.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Compare partial sums \(S_n^2 + C_n^2\) against the constant \(1\) using the Cauchy product with explicit cancellation between odd-power and even-power terms. The tail bound is the squared geometric tail of \autoref{lem:real-analytic-trig-cauchy}.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Sine vanishes at integer multiples of \(\pi\)]
\label{thm:real-analytic-sin-pi-multiples}
For every signed unary integer \(n\), \(\mathsf{Sin}(n \cdot \mathsf{Pi}, s)\) gives \(\mathsf{RealClassifier}(s, \emp)\). In particular, \(\sin(-n\pi) = 0\) for every unary \(n \geq \emp\), supplying the trivial-zero witness used in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-zeta-zeros-namecert}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
By induction on \(n\) using addition formula \(\sin((n+1)\pi) = \sin(n\pi)\cos(\pi) + \cos(n\pi)\sin(\pi)\), with base cases \(\sin(0) = 0\) and \(\sin(\pi) = 0\). The base \(\sin(\pi) = 0\) is established by comparing the alternating partial sums of \(\sin(\mathsf{Pi})\) against the explicit cancellation pattern visible in the \(\mathsf{LeibPart}\) construction.
\end{proof}

\section{Certificate target}
\label{sec:real-analytic-cert-target}

\begin{theorem}[$\RealAnalyticUp$ certificate boundary]
\label{thm:real-analytic-certificate-boundary}
Suppose the following machine-checked rows are supplied for the analytic construction over $\RatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRat$, and $\RealUp$:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Dyadic rational core}: $\DyadicRatCore$ arithmetic, signed fractions, factorial denominators, powers, and positivity comparisons for the displayed series and bisection predicates.
  \item \textbf{Stream carrier}: $\StreamNameUp$ and $\RegSeqRat$ data for every displayed partial-sum family, including the explicit unary moduli of \autoref{lem:real-analytic-leibniz-cauchy}, \autoref{lem:real-analytic-exp-cauchy}, and \autoref{lem:real-analytic-trig-cauchy}.
  \item \textbf{Real carrier}: $\RealUp$ limit witnesses for $\mathsf{Pi}$, $\mathsf{Exp}$, $\mathsf{Log}$, $\mathsf{Sin}$, and $\mathsf{Cos}$, supplied from the Phase Real and Completion obligations.
  \item \textbf{Classifier}: limit uniqueness for the supplied $\RealUp$ witnesses, stated up to $\mathsf{RealClassifier}$.
  \item \textbf{Stability}: additivity of $\exp$ (\autoref{thm:real-analytic-exp-add}), inverse relation $\log = \exp^{-1}$ (\autoref{thm:real-analytic-log-exp-inverse}), Pythagorean identity (\autoref{thm:real-analytic-pythagorean}), and $\pi$-multiple zeros of $\sin$ (\autoref{thm:real-analytic-sin-pi-multiples}).
\end{enumerate}
Under these supplied rows, the tuple $(\mathsf{Pi}, \mathsf{Exp}, \mathsf{Log}, \mathsf{Sin}, \mathsf{Cos})$, with sources, classifiers, stability obligations, ledgers, and standard bridges, is a $\NameCert(\RealAnalyticUp)$ candidate over $\RealUp$. Without those supplied rows, this chapter exports only the displayed proof obligations and explicit-modulus scaffolds.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The local constructions provide regular rational stream data: the Leibniz rescaling for $\pi$, the exponential power-series family, the bisection presentation of $\log$, and the sine and cosine power-series families. Their moduli are explicit unary functions, so they can be passed to the $\StreamNameUp$ and $\RegSeqRat$ rows once those rows are available.

The supplied $\RealUp$ limit witnesses then assign real-history candidates to the five analytic objects. Limit uniqueness supplies the classifier row, and the four stability obligations listed in the statement provide the algebraic and trigonometric preservation clauses required by the $\NameCert$ template.

Thus the tuple has the shape of a naming-certificate candidate exactly when the rows in the statement are present. In their absence, the chapter has not produced the carrier, classifier, or stability package needed for a completed $\NameCert(\RealAnalyticUp)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[$\RealAnalyticUp$ primitive boundary]
\label{cor:real-analytic-primitive-boundary}
$\RealAnalyticUp$ is not a BEDC primitive, and this chapter alone does not license it as an available naming certificate. Its admissible use requires the supplied Real and Completion rows named in \autoref{thm:real-analytic-certificate-boundary}.
\end{corollary}
