\chapter{A Conditional Certificate Boundary for $\RealAnalyticUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-real-analytic-namecert}

This chapter records the proof obligations for the constructive transcendental package $\RealAnalyticUp$ over $\RatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRat$, and $\RealUp$ without asserting a completed $\NameCert(\RealAnalyticUp)$. The package is organized around five interrelated objects on $\RealUp$-history representations: the constant $\pi$, the exponential $\exp$, the logarithm $\log$ on positive arguments, and the trigonometric pair $\sin / \cos$. The construction boundary sits above $\RatUp$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rat-namecert}) for the rational base, $\StreamNameUp$ for explicit unary streams, $\RegSeqRat$ for regular Cauchy data, and $\RealUp$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}) for the eventual real-history carrier. Every series below is given by an explicit unary-history term, every modulus is a total function on the unary index domain, and every passage from regular rational data to a real-history value requires a supplied limit witness from the Phase Real and Completion obligations.

\section{Constructive \(\pi\) via Leibniz acceleration}
\label{sec:real-analytic-pi}

\begin{definition}[Leibniz partial sum]
\label{def:real-analytic-leibniz-partial-sum}
For unary index \(n\), the Leibniz partial sum predicate \(\mathsf{LeibPart}(n, S_n)\) holds when \(S_n\) is the explicit \(\RatUp\)-history obtained by summing the first \(n\) terms of \(\sum_{k \geq 0} (-1)^k / (2k+1)\) via unary recursion using \(\RatUp\) addition and signed-fraction subtraction.
\end{definition}

\begin{lemma}[Leibniz alternating-series Cauchy modulus]
\label{lem:real-analytic-leibniz-cauchy}
The sequence of partial sums \(S_n\) is a \(\RegSeqRat\)-Cauchy sequence with explicit modulus \(N(k) := 2^{k+1}\): for all unary \(m, n \geq N(k)\), \(|4 S_m - 4 S_n| < 2^{-k}\).
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Pair adjacent terms after index \(N(k)\); each pair has magnitude bounded by the next omitted term \(1/(2N(k)+1)\). The alternating bound gives \(|4 S_m - 4 S_n| \leq 4/(2N(k)+1) < 2^{-k}\) for the chosen \(N(k)\).
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[Constructive \(\pi\)]
\label{def:real-analytic-pi}
$\mathsf{Pi}$ is a $\RealUp$-history candidate only together with a supplied limit witness from the Phase Real and Completion obligations, applied to the $\RegSeqRat$ Cauchy data of \autoref{lem:real-analytic-leibniz-cauchy} for the rescaled sequence $(4 S_n)_n$. The local data of this chapter is the $\RegSeqRat$ name and its explicit modulus inherited from the alternating-series bound.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Series variant equivalence boundary]
\label{thm:real-analytic-series-variant-equivalence-boundary}
Let $p_1$ and $p_2$ be two explicit $\pi$ stream presentations, such as the
Leibniz rescaling and a faster Machin or Chudnovsky presentation. A conclusion
$\mathsf{RealClassifier}(r_1,r_2)$ is available for their $\RealUp$ histories
only under supplied rows: both $p_1$ and $p_2$ satisfy the relevant
$\RegSeqRat$ carrier obligations, a $\RegSeqRat$ classifier witness relates the
two stream names, both streams have supplied $\RealUp$ limit witnesses with
targets $r_1$ and $r_2$, and the supplied $\RealUp$ limit-uniqueness row
transports the stream classifier to the real-history classifier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the supplied $\RegSeqRat$ classifier witness first. The two
carrier-obligation rows make this witness a relation between legitimate regular
rational stream names, so it is eligible for transport by the real-completion
interface.

Apply the two supplied $\RealUp$ limit witnesses next. They place $p_1$ and
$p_2$ in the real-history carrier as $r_1$ and $r_2$, without adding any
independent equality claim between the analytic presentations.

Finally invoke the supplied limit-uniqueness row. Its transport clause takes the
$\RegSeqRat$ classifier witness between the source streams and the two limit
witnesses, and returns the displayed conclusion
$\mathsf{RealClassifier}(r_1,r_2)$.
\end{proof}

\section{Constructive exponential}

\begin{definition}[Exponential partial sum]
\label{def:real-analytic-exp-part}
For \(\RealUp\)-history \(x\) and unary index \(n\), the predicate \(\mathsf{ExpPart}(x, n, S_n)\) holds when \(S_n\) is the partial sum \(\sum_{k=0}^{n} x^k / k!\) computed by unary recursion: powers \(x^k\) via iterated \(\RealUp\) multiplication, factorials \(k!\) via the unary factorial of \(\PrimeUp\) (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-prime-namecert}), and division as \(\RealUp\)-multiplicative inverse against the positive unary denominator.
\end{definition}

\begin{lemma}[Real-analytic complex partial-sum indices are unary]
\label{lem:real-analytic-complex-part-sum-index-unary}
For the real-analytic series interface, every closed complex partial-sum
witness has unary index.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Induct on the closed complex partial-sum witness. The zero constructor has the
empty index, and the successor constructor applies the unary successor closure
to the predecessor index.
\end{proof}

For the real-analytic series interface, if the chosen zero history is unary and

unary zero history. In the successor case, an inner induction on the
predecessor partial-sum witness supplies unaryness of the predecessor index,
the term hypothesis supplies unaryness of the next term, and unary continuation
closure gives the successor result.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Exponential Cauchy modulus on bounded inputs]
\label{lem:real-analytic-exp-cauchy}
For every \(\RealUp\)-history \(x\) with \(|x| \leq B\) (explicit unary upper bound \(B\)), the partial sums \(\mathsf{ExpPart}(x, n, S_n)\) form a \(\RegSeqRat\)-Cauchy sequence with explicit modulus \(N(k) := 2 B + k\): for all \(m, n \geq N(k)\), \(|S_m - S_n| < 2^{-k}\).
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
For \(n \geq 2B\), the ratio of successive terms \(|x|/(n+1)\) is bounded by \(1/2\), so the tail majorizes a geometric series with ratio \(1/2\). The geometric-series modulus \(2^{-(n - 2B)}\) gives the explicit \(N(k)\) above.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[Constructive \(\exp\)]
\label{def:real-analytic-exp}
For a $\RealUp$-history $x$, $\mathsf{Exp}(x, y)$ holds in this chapter only relative to a supplied $\RealUp$ limit witness from the Phase Real and Completion obligations for the partial sums $\mathsf{ExpPart}(x, \cdot, \cdot)$ and the Cauchy modulus of \autoref{lem:real-analytic-exp-cauchy}. The local witness includes the explicit input bound $B$ and the modulus $N$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Exponential additivity]
\label{thm:real-analytic-exp-add}
For $\RealUp$-histories $x$ and $y$, assume supplied $\RealAlgOrder$ rows for $x+y$, products in $\RealUp$, powers $x^k$, $y^k$, and $(x+y)^k$, the unary factorial-denominator inverses, and the Cauchy-product comparison between the partial sums of $\mathsf{ExpPart}(x+y,\cdot,\cdot)$ and the product of the partial sums for $x$ and $y$. Assume also supplied $\RealUp$ limit witnesses and their limit-uniqueness classifier rows for $\mathsf{Exp}(x,e_x)$, $\mathsf{Exp}(y,e_y)$, and $\mathsf{Exp}(x+y,e_{x+y})$. Under exactly these rows, $\mathsf{RealClassifier}(e_{x+y}, e_x \cdot e_y)$ holds.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The $\RealAlgOrder$ rows make the finite algebraic comparison meaningful: addition supplies $x+y$, multiplication supplies the product side, powers and factorial-denominator inverses supply the displayed exponential terms, and the Cauchy-product row compares the two regular rational partial-sum families.

The three supplied $\RealUp$ limit witnesses place those regular rational families in the real-history carrier. Their supplied classifier uniqueness rows identify the common limit, giving $\mathsf{RealClassifier}(e_{x+y}, e_x \cdot e_y)$.
\end{proof}

\section{Constructive logarithm on positives}
\label{sec:real-analytic-log}

\begin{definition}[Logarithm via inverse-of-\(\exp\) bisection]
\label{def:real-analytic-log}
For a positive $\RealUp$-history $x$ (i.e.\ apartness witness $x \neq 0$ with positive sign), $\mathsf{Log}(x, y)$ holds in this chapter only relative to a supplied $\RealUp$ limit witness for the dyadic bisection sequence on unary precision $k$: at each precision, locate the dyadic-rational interval $[y_k, y_k + 2^{-k}]$ such that $\exp(y_k) \leq x < \exp(y_k + 2^{-k})$ (decidable on rational representations using \autoref{def:real-analytic-exp}), and assemble the local $\RegSeqRat$ data with modulus $N(k) = k + \lceil \log_2(1/x) \rceil$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Logarithm is the inverse of \(\exp\) on positives]
\label{thm:real-analytic-log-exp-inverse}
For a $\RealUp$-history $x$, assume supplied positivity and apartness rows for $x$, supplied dyadic bisection rows locating rational intervals $[y_k,y_k+2^{-k}]$ with $\exp(y_k) \leq x < \exp(y_k+2^{-k})$, supplied rational-embedding rows for the dyadic endpoints and exponential comparisons, and supplied $\RealUp$ limit witnesses with limit-uniqueness classifier rows for $\mathsf{Log}(x,y)$ and $\mathsf{Exp}(y,e_y)$. Under these rows, $\mathsf{RealClassifier}(e_y,x)$ holds. Conversely, for a $\RealUp$-history $z$, assume the supplied $\mathsf{Exp}(z,w)$ limit witness, the supplied positivity row for $w$, the same bisection and rational-embedding rows applied to $w$, and the supplied uniqueness rows for $\mathsf{Log}(w,z')$; then $\mathsf{RealClassifier}(z',z)$ holds.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the forward direction, the supplied positivity row permits the bisection search, and the dyadic and rational-embedding rows provide the rational comparisons used at every precision. The supplied limit witness for $\mathsf{Log}(x,y)$ records the bisection limit, while the supplied witness for $\mathsf{Exp}(y,e_y)$ records the exponential value of that limit.

The supplied uniqueness rows then identify the real-history value of $\exp(y)$ with the original positive input $x$. The reverse direction uses the supplied $\mathsf{Exp}(z,w)$ row as the positive input for the same bisection package, and the uniqueness row for $\mathsf{Log}(w,z')$ identifies the recovered logarithm with $z$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Logarithm of a unary natural]
\label{thm:real-analytic-log-unary}
For positive unary natural $n$, assume supplied rational-embedding rows sending the unary numeral $\Eone^n(\emp)$ into the positive $\RealUp$ carrier, supplied positivity and apartness rows for that embedded value, supplied dyadic bisection rows with the modulus determined by the unary length of $n$, and supplied $\RealUp$ limit uniqueness rows for the resulting $\mathsf{Log}(\Eone^n(\emp),y_n)$ witness. Under these rows, $y_n$ is the constructive $\log n$ input used by the Dirichlet term construction (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dirichlet-series-namecert}).
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The rational-embedding rows place the unary numeral inside $\RealUp$ with the required positive sign data. The bisection rows then specialize \autoref{def:real-analytic-log} to this embedded value and supply the precision modulus determined by the unary length of $n$.

The supplied limit witness and uniqueness rows turn the bisection stream into the real-history value $y_n$. No logarithm classifier conclusion is available before these rows are present.
\end{proof}

\section{Constructive sine and cosine}
\label{sec:real-analytic-trig}

\begin{definition}[Sine and cosine partial sums]
\label{def:real-analytic-trig-part}
For \(\RealUp\)-history \(x\) and unary index \(n\), \(\mathsf{SinPart}(x, n, S_n)\) and \(\mathsf{CosPart}(x, n, C_n)\) are the partial sums \(\sum_{k=0}^{n} (-1)^k x^{2k+1}/(2k+1)!\) and \(\sum_{k=0}^{n} (-1)^k x^{2k}/(2k)!\) respectively, computed by unary recursion as in \autoref{def:real-analytic-exp-part}.
\end{definition}

\begin{lemma}[Trigonometric Cauchy modulus]
\label{lem:real-analytic-trig-cauchy}
For every \(\RealUp\)-history \(x\) with \(|x| \leq B\), the partial sums \(\mathsf{SinPart}\) and \(\mathsf{CosPart}\) form \(\RegSeqRat\)-Cauchy sequences with explicit modulus \(N(k) := B^2 + k\). The bound is the alternating-series majorant: each term has magnitude bounded by the previous one once \(2k(2k+1) > B^2\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{definition}[Constructive \(\sin\) and \(\cos\)]
\label{def:real-analytic-sin-cos}
$\mathsf{Sin}(x, s)$ and $\mathsf{Cos}(x, c)$ hold in this chapter only relative to supplied $\RealUp$ limit witnesses from the Phase Real and Completion obligations for $\mathsf{SinPart}(x, \cdot, \cdot)$ and $\mathsf{CosPart}(x, \cdot, \cdot)$ respectively, using the explicit Cauchy moduli of \autoref{lem:real-analytic-trig-cauchy}.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Pythagorean identity]
\label{thm:real-analytic-pythagorean}
For a $\RealUp$-history $x$, assume supplied $\RealAlgOrder$ rows for products, squares, addition, and the constant $\Eone(\emp)$, supplied product-stability rows for multiplying supplied sine and cosine limits, supplied trigonometric limit witnesses for $\mathsf{Sin}(x,s)$ and $\mathsf{Cos}(x,c)$, and supplied limit-uniqueness classifier rows comparing the regular rational partial sums for $s^2+c^2$ with the constant-one witness. Under these rows, $\mathsf{RealClassifier}(s^2 + c^2, \Eone(\emp))$ holds.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The supplied algebraic rows make $s^2+c^2$ and the constant-one comparison available in $\RealUp$. Product stability transports the supplied sine and cosine limit witnesses through the finite products needed to form the square terms.

The regular rational comparison is the usual cancellation between the odd-power and even-power partial sums, bounded by the trigonometric Cauchy modulus of \autoref{lem:real-analytic-trig-cauchy}. The supplied uniqueness classifier rows then identify the resulting value with $\Eone(\emp)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Sine vanishes at integer multiples of \(\pi\)]
\label{thm:real-analytic-sin-pi-multiples}
For every signed unary integer $n$, assume supplied $\RealAlgOrder$ rows for integer scalar multiplication $n \cdot \mathsf{Pi}$, addition of consecutive multiples of $\mathsf{Pi}$, products in the sine addition formula, and the zero constant $\emp$. Assume also a supplied constant $\mathsf{Pi}$ limit witness, supplied trigonometric limit witnesses for the required sine and cosine terms, supplied addition-formula rows for $\sin(a+b)$ and $\cos(a+b)$ at these arguments, supplied product-stability rows for the formula terms, and supplied limit-uniqueness classifier rows for the base values $\sin(0)$ and $\sin(\mathsf{Pi})$. Under these rows, $\mathsf{Sin}(n \cdot \mathsf{Pi},s)$ gives $\mathsf{RealClassifier}(s,\emp)$. In particular, the negative signed multiples supply the zero witness used in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-zeta-zeros-namecert}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The supplied $\mathsf{Pi}$ limit witness and scalar-multiplication rows make each integer multiple $n \cdot \mathsf{Pi}$ a legitimate $\RealUp$ argument. The trigonometric witnesses and classifier rows supply the base values $\sin(0)$ and $\sin(\mathsf{Pi})$ as zero.

For the induction step, the supplied addition-formula row rewrites $\sin((n+1)\mathsf{Pi})$ through $\sin(n\mathsf{Pi})\cos(\mathsf{Pi})+\cos(n\mathsf{Pi})\sin(\mathsf{Pi})$. Product stability transports the induction hypothesis and the base zero classifier through the product terms, and the supplied uniqueness row returns the classifier $\mathsf{RealClassifier}(s,\emp)$.
\end{proof}

\section{Certificate target}
\label{sec:real-analytic-cert-target}

\begin{theorem}[$\RealAnalyticUp$ certificate boundary]
\label{thm:real-analytic-certificate-boundary}
Suppose the following machine-checked rows are supplied for the analytic construction over $\RatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRat$, and $\RealUp$:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Dyadic rational core}: $\DyadicRatCore$ arithmetic, signed fractions, factorial denominators, powers, and positivity comparisons for the displayed series and bisection predicates.
  \item \textbf{Stream carrier}: $\StreamNameUp$ and $\RegSeqRat$ data for every displayed partial-sum family, including the explicit unary moduli of \autoref{lem:real-analytic-leibniz-cauchy}, \autoref{lem:real-analytic-exp-cauchy}, and \autoref{lem:real-analytic-trig-cauchy}.
  \item \textbf{Real carrier}: $\RealUp$ limit witnesses for $\mathsf{Pi}$, $\mathsf{Exp}$, $\mathsf{Log}$, $\mathsf{Sin}$, and $\mathsf{Cos}$, supplied from the Phase Real and Completion obligations.
  \item \textbf{Classifier}: limit uniqueness for the supplied $\RealUp$ witnesses, stated up to $\mathsf{RealClassifier}$.
  \item \textbf{Stability}: supplied conditional rows for additivity of $\exp$ (\autoref{thm:real-analytic-exp-add}), inverse relation $\log = \exp^{-1}$ (\autoref{thm:real-analytic-log-exp-inverse}), Pythagorean identity (\autoref{thm:real-analytic-pythagorean}), and $\pi$-multiple zeros of $\sin$ (\autoref{thm:real-analytic-sin-pi-multiples}), each with its theorem-site hypotheses for algebraic operations, bisection or Cauchy-product comparison, limit witnesses, classifiers, addition formulas, and product stability.
\end{enumerate}
Under these supplied rows, the tuple $(\mathsf{Pi}, \mathsf{Exp}, \mathsf{Log}, \mathsf{Sin}, \mathsf{Cos})$, with sources, classifiers, stability obligations, ledgers, and standard bridges, is a $\NameCert(\RealAnalyticUp)$ candidate over $\RealUp$. Without those supplied rows, this chapter exports only the displayed proof obligations and explicit-modulus scaffolds.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The local constructions provide regular rational stream data: the Leibniz rescaling for $\pi$, the exponential power-series family, the bisection presentation of $\log$, and the sine and cosine power-series families. Their moduli are explicit unary functions, so they can be passed to the $\StreamNameUp$ and $\RegSeqRat$ rows once those rows are available.

The supplied $\RealUp$ limit witnesses then assign real-history candidates to the five analytic objects. Limit uniqueness supplies the classifier row, and the four stability obligations listed in the statement provide the algebraic and trigonometric preservation clauses required by the $\NameCert$ template.

Thus the tuple has the shape of a naming-certificate candidate exactly when the rows in the statement are present. In their absence, the chapter has not produced the carrier, classifier, or stability package needed for a completed $\NameCert(\RealAnalyticUp)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[$\RealAnalyticUp$ primitive boundary]
\label{cor:real-analytic-primitive-boundary}
$\RealAnalyticUp$ is not a BEDC primitive, and this chapter alone does not license it as an available naming certificate. Its admissible use requires the supplied Real and Completion rows named in \autoref{thm:real-analytic-certificate-boundary}.
\end{corollary}
