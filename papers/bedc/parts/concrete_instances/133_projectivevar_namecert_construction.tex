\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ProjectiveVarUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-projectivevar-namecert}

The projective-variety interface $\ProjectiveVarUp$ packages the zero-locus of a homogeneous polynomial ideal in the projective $n$-space of a commutative-ring carrier. It depends on $\NameCert_{\AffineVarUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-affinevar-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\PolynomialUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-polynomial-namecert}).

\begin{theorem}[ProjectiveVarUp carrier obligation]
\label{thm:projectivevar-carrier-obligation}
The $\ProjectiveVarUp$ carrier is a $\mathsf{BHist}$ predicate assembled from the visible $\AffineVarUp$ chart zero-locus source, the $\PolynomialUp$ homogeneous-family rows, the projective-space carrier row, and the zero-evaluation package. A carried endpoint is accepted exactly when those four rows are present with their $\Pkg$ provenance and probe reads; no host quotient, host orbit set, or external projective point equality is part of the carrier predicate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the carrier row through the roadmap ten-field template:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item traditional target: the projective zero-locus of a homogeneous polynomial family;
  \item BEDC source: a $\mathsf{BHist}$ endpoint carrying chart, homogeneous-family, projective-space, and zero-evaluation rows;
  \item carrier field: the visible predicate formed by those four rows and their $\Pkg$ provenance;
  \item dependency fields: $\NameCert_{\AffineVarUp}$ from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-affinevar-namecert} and $\NameCert_{\PolynomialUp}$ from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-polynomial-namecert};
  \item classifier field: reserved for projective scaling and chart-compatible $\hsame$ transport;
  \item stability field: chart zero-locus rows and homogeneous rows are transported together;
  \item exactness field: zero evaluation is read only through the displayed package;
  \item ledger field: chart choice, family presentation, scale witness, and routing evidence remain hidden;
  \item theorem-list field: the carrier, classifier, exactness, and ledger obligations named in this section;
  \item boundary field: downstream consumers see only the projective carrier and classifier.
\end{enumerate}

The carrier projection is therefore just the third entry of the template. If an endpoint supplies the four displayed rows, it repacks as a carried $\ProjectiveVarUp$ history; if a carried endpoint is projected back out, the same rows are recovered. Host quotients and orbit representatives occur only as ledger readings and cannot enlarge the predicate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ProjectiveVarUp classifier stability obligation]
\label{thm:projectivevar-classifier-stability-obligation}
The $\ProjectiveVarUp$ classifier is projective scaling together with chart-compatible $\hsame$ transport. Whenever two carried endpoints are related by a scale witness and their chart zero-locus rows transport through the corresponding $\AffineVarUp$ chart classifiers, the projective carrier is preserved and each homogeneous $\PolynomialUp$ row is transported to a row with the same zero-evaluation outcome.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the classifier row through the roadmap ten-field template:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item traditional target: equality of projective zero-locus points up to nonzero scalar multiplication;
  \item BEDC source: two carried $\mathsf{BHist}$ endpoints with chart rows, homogeneous-family rows, projective-space rows, and zero-evaluation packages;
  \item carrier field: the carrier predicate of \autoref{thm:projectivevar-carrier-obligation} on both endpoints;
  \item dependency fields: $\NameCert_{\AffineVarUp}$ from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-affinevar-namecert} and $\NameCert_{\PolynomialUp}$ from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-polynomial-namecert};
  \item classifier field: scale witness plus chart-compatible $\hsame$ transport;
  \item stability field: transported chart rows preserve carrier membership;
  \item exactness field: homogeneous zero-evaluation is invariant under the transported presentation;
  \item ledger field: the chosen scale and chart route remain hidden provenance;
  \item theorem-list field: this classifier row feeds the exactness and ledger obligations below;
  \item boundary field: consumers may use only the resulting carrier-preserving classifier.
\end{enumerate}

Apply the scale witness to the projective-space row and the $\hsame$ transport to the chart row. The homogeneous-family rows are then read through the $\PolynomialUp$ source certificate, so evaluation at transported representatives remains a zero-evaluation package fact rather than a host equality fact.

Thus the classifier preserves the carrier predicate. Conversely, any classifier witness visible at the $\ProjectiveVarUp$ boundary must decompose into exactly the scale route, chart transport, homogeneous-row transport, and provenance entries listed above.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ProjectiveVarUp homogeneous zero-locus exactness obligation]
\label{thm:projectivevar-homogeneous-zero-locus-exactness-obligation}
The visible zero-locus package for $\ProjectiveVarUp$ is exact relative to the homogeneous $\PolynomialUp$ family and the $\AffineVarUp$ chart exactness rows: coverage says every carried projective endpoint has a chart read and homogeneous zero-evaluation read; soundness says every accepted read satisfies all displayed homogeneous rows on every compatible chart; completeness says every compatible chart-family zero-locus read repacks as a carried projective endpoint.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the exactness row through the roadmap ten-field template:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item traditional target: a projective homogeneous-family zero-locus;
  \item BEDC source: chart-local zero-locus reads tied to homogeneous polynomial-code rows;
  \item carrier field: the projective carrier predicate of \autoref{thm:projectivevar-carrier-obligation};
  \item dependency fields: $\NameCert_{\AffineVarUp}$ from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-affinevar-namecert} and $\NameCert_{\PolynomialUp}$ from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-polynomial-namecert};
  \item classifier field: the scaling and chart-transport row of \autoref{thm:projectivevar-classifier-stability-obligation};
  \item stability field: compatible affine charts carry the same homogeneous-family zero tests;
  \item exactness field: coverage, soundness, and completeness for visible zero-locus reads;
  \item ledger field: chart selections and evaluation routing remain provenance data;
  \item theorem-list field: carrier and classifier obligations supply the projections consumed here;
  \item boundary field: downstream consumers receive an exact visible zero-locus package, not host algebraic geometry data.
\end{enumerate}

For coverage, project a carried endpoint to its chart read and homogeneous-family zero-evaluation read. Both are already visible components of the carrier row, so no extra witness is introduced.

For soundness, use the $\AffineVarUp$ chart exactness rows to interpret the chart zero-locus source and the $\PolynomialUp$ homogeneous-family rows to interpret each zero-evaluation entry. The classifier stability obligation transports these entries across compatible charts.

For completeness, begin with a compatible chart-family read satisfying the listed zero-evaluation rows. Repack the chart source, homogeneous family, projective-space row, and routing package into the carrier predicate, then attach the ledger entries for chart choice and scale routing. The result is a carried $\ProjectiveVarUp$ endpoint with exactly the advertised visible zero-locus package.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ProjectiveVarUp scaling ledger obligation]
\label{thm:projectivevar-scaling-ledger-obligation}
The $\ProjectiveVarUp$ ledger hides exactly chart choice, homogeneous-family presentation, scale witnesses, and zero-evaluation package routing. It exposes only the projective carrier of \autoref{thm:projectivevar-carrier-obligation} and the projective classifier of \autoref{thm:projectivevar-classifier-stability-obligation}; no hidden chart, polynomial presentation, scalar representative, or evaluation route becomes public carrier data.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the ledger row through the roadmap ten-field template:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item traditional target: presentation independence for a projective homogeneous zero-locus;
  \item BEDC source: the carrier and classifier rows already displayed at the $\ProjectiveVarUp$ boundary;
  \item carrier field: the projective carrier predicate;
  \item dependency fields: $\NameCert_{\AffineVarUp}$ from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-affinevar-namecert} and $\NameCert_{\PolynomialUp}$ from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-polynomial-namecert};
  \item classifier field: projective scaling with chart-compatible $\hsame$ transport;
  \item stability field: hidden presentation changes preserve the same visible carrier and classifier;
  \item exactness field: zero-locus coverage, soundness, and completeness remain routed through the visible package;
  \item ledger field: chart choice, homogeneous-family presentation, scale witness, and zero-evaluation routing are the hidden entries;
  \item theorem-list field: the four obligations of this section are the whole certificate surface at this grade;
  \item boundary field: consumers cannot inspect anything beyond the projective carrier and classifier.
\end{enumerate}

Projecting the ledger reveals only the entries listed in the eighth template field. Chart choices are justified by the affine source certificate, homogeneous-family presentations by the polynomial source certificate, scale witnesses by the classifier row, and evaluation routing by the zero-locus package.

Since each hidden entry is consumed by one of those visible rows, exposing it would duplicate a presentation witness rather than add a BEDC carrier field. The public boundary is therefore exactly the carrier and classifier named in the statement.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ProjectiveVarUp scoped primitive binding]
\label{thm:projectivevar-scoped-primitive-binding}
The four rows in \autoref{thm:projectivevar-carrier-obligation}, \autoref{thm:projectivevar-classifier-stability-obligation}, \autoref{thm:projectivevar-homogeneous-zero-locus-exactness-obligation}, and \autoref{thm:projectivevar-scaling-ledger-obligation} are scoped exactly by the BEDC primitives $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\Pkg$, probe reads, $\hsame$ transport, the projective scaling witness, $\NameCert_{\AffineVarUp}$, and $\NameCert_{\PolynomialUp}$. Chart choice, homogeneous-family presentation, scale witness, and zero-evaluation routing remain ledger data, and no host quotient or external projective-point equality enters the public carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier row first. Its roadmap dependency fields expose only the $\mathsf{BHist}$ predicate, the visible $\Pkg$ provenance, the relevant probe reads, and the upstream $\AffineVarUp$ and $\PolynomialUp$ certificates named in the statement.

Project the classifier row next. Its dependency and classifier fields bind the projective scaling witness to chart-compatible $\hsame$ transport, while the carrier field remains the predicate supplied by \autoref{thm:projectivevar-carrier-obligation}.

Project the exactness row. Coverage, soundness, and completeness consume the same chart reads, homogeneous-family rows, zero-evaluation package, and upstream certificate exactness already present in \autoref{thm:projectivevar-homogeneous-zero-locus-exactness-obligation}; they do not add a host quotient or an external equality relation.

Finally project the ledger row. The ledger field names exactly chart choice, homogeneous-family presentation, scale witness, and zero-evaluation routing as hidden provenance, so those witnesses can be transported and consumed but not promoted into the public carrier. Combining the four projections gives the reusable scoped boundary for $\ProjectiveVarUp$.
\end{proof}
