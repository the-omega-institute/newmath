\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RingedSpaceUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-ringedspace-namecert}

The ringed-space interface $\RingedSpaceUp$ packages a topological carrier with a sheaf of commutative rings on it, certifying that stalks form local rings. It depends on $\NameCert_{\SheafUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-sheaf-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\CommRingUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-commring-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:ringedspace-carrier}

\begin{theorem}[RingedSpace carrier and classifier obligations]\label{thm:ringedspace-carrier-classifier-obligation}
A $\RingedSpaceUp$ certificate must expose a $\TopologyUp$ base carrier over $\mathsf{BHist}$, a $\SheafUp$ row on that topology, a $\CommRingUp$ row on every displayed open of the topology, and a classifier row combining base-open classification, sheaf-section classification, and commutative-ring endpoint classification. The carrier part records the open index, membership row, section carrier, restriction row, and commutative-ring operation tuple for each displayed open. The classifier part reads equality only through the displayed base classifier, the section classifier of $\SheafUp$, and the endpoint classifier of the relevant $\CommRingUp$ row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The traditional target is a topological space equipped with a sheaf of commutative rings. The BEDC source is therefore the joint surface supplied by $\TopologyUp$, $\SheafUp$, and $\CommRingUp$, all read over $\mathsf{BHist}$ histories and $\hsame$ transport.

The carrier field is the indexed-open carrier of $\TopologyUp$ together with the $\SheafUp$ section carrier over each displayed open. The commutative-ring coordinate is not a host algebra attached after the fact; it is a row on each displayed section carrier and is indexed by the same open ledger used by the sheaf.

The classifier field combines three already visible comparisons: base-open classification for transported opens, section classification for transported sections, and $\CommRingUp$ endpoint classification for zero, one, addition, multiplication, negation, and unit-detection endpoints. Stability says that the three classifier rows transport together along $\hsame$, restriction, and operation congruence.

The ledger contains the topology row, the sheaf row, and the per-open commutative-ring operation row. The core theorem list is exactly carrier exposure, classifier exposure, restriction compatibility, stalk-locality readback, transport, and ledger exactness. The exactness predicate is equality of the displayed ringed-space row under these classifiers. No standard bridge is claimed, and the status is paper-side $\obligationClosure$ with Lean-side $\unformalizedV$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RingedSpace sheaf and commutative-ring stalk locality obligation]\label{thm:ringedspace-sheaf-commring-stalk-locality-obligation}
For a $\RingedSpaceUp$ certificate over a $\TopologyUp$ carrier, every displayed inclusion $j\le_{\mathcal{T}}i$ must use the $\SheafUp$ restriction row $\rho_{i,j}$ to transport the $\CommRingUp$ operations from sections over $i$ to sections over $j$. For every displayed point history $b:\mathsf{BHist}$ and every displayed neighbourhood row of $b$, the stalk-local row is read only through the displayed germ classifier supplied by the sheaf point-germ ledger and the $\CommRingUp$ endpoint classifier; it is not read through a host quotient of sections.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The traditional target is the local-ring condition on the stalks of the structure sheaf. The BEDC source is the $\SheafUp$ point-germ ledger together with the $\CommRingUp$ operation rows carried by sections on displayed opens and the $\TopologyUp$ membership row naming each neighbourhood.

For the carrier, choose an open index containing the point history, a section carrier over that open, the restriction route to a smaller displayed open, and the commutative-ring operation tuple on both opens. The classifier is the displayed germ classifier: two section rows agree at the stalk only when the ledger supplies a common displayed neighbourhood and restriction comparison through $\SheafUp$.

Stability is the compatibility of restriction with addition, multiplication, zero, one, negation, units, and maximal-ideal detection. The ledger records the chosen neighbourhood, the restriction route, the two operation rows, and the germ comparison witness; it does not record an external quotient constructor.

The core theorem list is restriction transport of operations, germ classifier readback, local-ring row exposure, and exclusion of host section quotients. The exactness predicate says that every stalk-local operation read is represented by displayed restrictions and $\CommRingUp$ endpoint classifiers. No standard bridge is claimed, and the status remains $\obligationClosure$ with $\unformalizedV$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RingedSpace transport exact ledger obligation]\label{thm:ringedspace-transport-exact-ledger-obligation}
In a $\RingedSpaceUp$ certificate, $\hsame$ transport of the base histories, $\SheafUp$ restriction transport, and $\CommRingUp$ classifier transport must preserve the displayed ringed-space row. The exact ledger records precisely the topology row, the sheaf restriction witness, the commutative-ring operation witness, and the stalk-locality witness used by the transported row, and it records no additional host open equality, section quotient, stalk quotient, or standard bridge witness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The traditional target is invariance of a ringed-space certificate under change of displayed presentation. The BEDC source is $\TopologyUp$ for base-open transport, $\SheafUp$ for restriction and germ transport, and $\CommRingUp$ for operation and endpoint classifier transport.

The carrier before and after transport is the same displayed ringed-space row viewed through transported open membership, transported restriction routes, and transported operation endpoints. The classifier is the triple classifier from \autoref{thm:ringedspace-carrier-classifier-obligation}, so every comparison remains inside named BEDC rows.

Stability follows by applying the $\hsame$ transport row to the topology coordinate, the sheaf restriction-transport row to section coordinates, and the $\CommRingUp$ congruence rows to operation endpoints. These witnesses are then repacked as the transported ringed-space row.

The ledger is exact because it has four entries and each is consumed once: topology row, sheaf restriction witness, commutative-ring operation witness, and stalk-locality witness. The core theorem list is preservation of the displayed row, exact ledger projection, and exclusion of hidden host equalities. The exactness predicate is preservation of the displayed $\RingedSpaceUp$ row under the three transports. No standard bridge is claimed, and the status is $\obligationClosure$ with $\unformalizedV$.
\end{proof}
