\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RingedSpaceUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-ringedspace-namecert}

The ringed-space interface $\RingedSpaceUp$ packages a topological carrier with a sheaf of commutative rings on it, certifying that stalks form local rings. It depends on $\NameCert_{\SheafUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-sheaf-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\CommRingUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-commring-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:ringedspace-carrier}

\begin{theorem}[RingedSpaceUp carrier and sheaf obligation]\label{thm:ringedspace-carrier-sheaf-obligation}
A $\RingedSpaceUp$ $\NameCert$ is sourced by a $\mathsf{BHist}$ topological carrier together with a $\SheafUp$ row assigning to each carried open witness a $\CommRingUp$ section carrier. Its classifier is inherited only from $\hsame$, the $\TopologyUp$ open classifier, the $\SheafUp$ restriction classifier, and the $\CommRingUp$ classifier. The carrier ledger records the open witness, membership row, section carrier, restriction row, and commutative-ring endpoint surface for each displayed open.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the ten-field $\NameCert$ template. The source row supplies the $\mathsf{BHist}$ topological carrier, so every open witness is read through the carried $\TopologyUp$ classifier and transported only by $\hsame$.

Read the $\SheafUp$ $\NameCert$ over the indexed opens. For each displayed open, its section row gives the section carrier and the restriction classifier visible along displayed inclusions.

Finally restrict each section value to the $\CommRingUp$ certificate surface. The result is the displayed carrier and classifier row needed by $\RingedSpaceUp$, with no extra host algebra or standard bridge data.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RingedSpaceUp stalk-locality obligation]\label{thm:ringedspace-stalk-locality-obligation}
For every carried point history and every admissible neighbourhood-filter row of a $\RingedSpaceUp$ certificate, the stalk projection is a local $\CommRingUp$ row. Restriction-compatible germs have a unique maximal non-unit classifier branch, and germ comparison is stable under $\hsame$ transport of the point and under $\SheafUp$ refinement. Local-ring status is recorded as an obligation row of the displayed certificate rather than as an external model.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the $\SheafUp$ restriction and refinement exactness rows to compare two representatives of a germ on a common displayed neighbourhood. The common row supplies the section classifier needed to identify the restricted section endpoints.

Apply the $\CommRingUp$ carrier and classifier rows to those section endpoints. Unit detection, non-unit comparison, and maximal-branch uniqueness are then read from the displayed commutative-ring row attached to the germ endpoint.

Transport stability follows because the point history is moved by $\hsame$ and the neighbourhood row is refined through $\SheafUp$. Repacking these witnesses gives the stalk-local $\CommRingUp$ obligation row without introducing a host stalk quotient.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RingedSpaceUp stability, exactness, and ledger obligation]\label{thm:ringedspace-stability-ledger-obligation}
Carrier transport, restriction compatibility, stalk transport, and exactness for visible $\RingedSpaceUp$ packages are exactly the conjunction of $\TopologyUp$ carrier transport, $\SheafUp$ restriction and gluing classifier transport, and $\CommRingUp$ algebraic classifier transport. The ledger records the open witness, restriction route, section endpoint, and stalk-germ provenance, and it records no $\SchemeUp$, affine-cover, spectrum, or standard bridge data.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Compose the three named certificate surfaces through the $\NameCert$ stability field. The $\TopologyUp$ surface transports open witnesses, the $\SheafUp$ surface transports restrictions and gluing classifiers, and the $\CommRingUp$ surface transports algebraic endpoints.

Project the ledger coordinates after this composition. The open witness comes from the topology row, the restriction route and section endpoint come from the sheaf row, and the stalk-germ provenance is obtained by the stalk-locality row of \autoref{thm:ringedspace-stalk-locality-obligation}.

Exactness is therefore the conjunction of those three transport surfaces as seen by the visible package. Since every ledger coordinate has already been projected, no scheme, affine-cover, spectrum, or standard bridge row is present in the $\RingedSpaceUp$ certificate.
\end{proof}
