\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\UniformCompletionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-uniformcompletion-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges constructive analysis and Bourbaki uniform spaces,
the $\UniformCompletionUp$ packet records the finite completion surface for a
uniform space before any host completed uniform space is selected. It sits over
the entourage carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformspace-namecert},
the late-window filter of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyfilter-finite-observation-filter}, the
finite schedule of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the
regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert},
the seal route of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-cauchy-completion-namecert}, the
extension ledger of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completionuniversalproperty-namecert}, and the
functor route of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchycompletionfunctor-namecert}. The
certificate keeps uniform completion as a displayed $\BHist$ packet rather than
as quotient completion equality, a selected limit object, or ambient
completeness.

\begin{definition}[UniformCompletion carrier]
\label{def:uniformcompletion-carrier}
A $\UniformCompletionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
U=(U_0,F,S,R,C,E,H,K,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $U_0$ is the $\UniformSpaceUp$ entourage row, including the displayed
filterbase, diagonal, symmetry, composition, and refinement ledgers;
\item $F$ is the $\CauchyFilterUp$ finite late-window row whose directed
windows read the Cauchy side of the completion request;
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule row exposing the finite observation
windows used by $F$;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row that records regular rational
access to those windows;
\item $C$ is the $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ seal row reached from the same
uniform, window, and readback data;
\item $E$ is the extension ledger that hands uniformly continuous completion
routes to the $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$ surface;
\item $H$ is the componentwise $\hsame$ transport row for $U_0,F,S,R,C,E$;
\item $K$ is the displayed $\Cont$ replay row used to re-read finite windows,
entourage refinements, completion seals, and extension ledgers;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\UniformSpaceUp$, $\CauchyFilterUp$,
$\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$,
$\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$, $\CauchyCompletionFunctorUp$, $\BHist$,
$\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\UniformCompletionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no quotient of Cauchy filters, no selected limit point, no
host completed uniform space, and no completeness principle outside the listed
finite rows.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[UniformCompletion NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:uniformcompletion-namecert-obligation-surface}
The local $\NameCert_{\UniformCompletionUp}$ surface of
\autoref{def:uniformcompletion-carrier} consists of five finite rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed packet
$U=(U_0,F,S,R,C,E,H,K,P,N)$;
\item classifier transport through the componentwise $\hsame$ row $H$ and the
displayed $\Cont$ replay row $K$;
\item Cauchy-filter seal compatibility, requiring every completion-facing read
to pass through $U_0,F,S,R,C,H,K,P,N$;
\item universal-extension compatibility, requiring every uniformly continuous
extension route to pass through $E$ and the
$\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$ and $\CauchyCompletionFunctorUp$ rows named in
$P$;
\item non-escape, stating that quotient completions, selected limits, ambient
complete spaces, and host equality are absent from the packet.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection of the tuple in
\autoref{def:uniformcompletion-carrier}. The classifier row compares only the
displayed coordinates and then replays the finite windows and entourage
refinements through $K$.

The completion-facing row is the seal coordinate $C$. To read it, a consumer
must first pass through the uniform entourage row, the Cauchy-filter row, the
stream schedule, the regular rational readback, and the transport and
provenance rows.

The extension row is $E$. Its declared consumers are the universal-property and
functor surfaces named in the provenance ledger, so no extension route can skip
the displayed finite completion packet.

The non-escape clause follows by inspection of the carrier. There is no
coordinate for a quotient completion, selected limit, host completed uniform
space, or host-level equality principle.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[UniformCompletion Cauchy-filter seal boundary]
\label{thm:uniformcompletion-cauchy-filter-seal-boundary}
Every completion-facing read exported by an accepted $\UniformCompletionUp$
carrier factors through the $\UniformSpaceUp$ row $U_0$, the
$\CauchyFilterUp$ row $F$, the $\StreamNameUp$ row $S$, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ row
$R$, the $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ seal row $C$, and the displayed $H,K,P,N$
transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows. The boundary exports no
second filter, quotient stream, chosen representative, selected limit, host
real equality, or ambient completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a completion-facing consumer and project the accepted carrier. The
uniform side is the displayed entourage row $U_0$, and the Cauchy side is the
finite late-window row $F$.

The consumer next reads finite observations through $S$ and their regular
rational readback through $R$. These are the only schedule and readback rows
listed in the packet.

The seal row $C$ is then reached over the same displayed route, with $H$ and
$K$ recording transport and replay of the consumed rows. Since the carrier has
no further completion coordinate, every exported completion read is exhausted
by $U_0,F,S,R,C,H,K,P,N$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[UniformCompletion universal extension route]
\label{thm:uniformcompletion-universal-extension-route}
Every uniformly continuous extension route exported by an accepted
$\UniformCompletionUp$ carrier factors through the extension ledger $E$, the
$\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$ row named by $P$, and the
$\CauchyCompletionFunctorUp$ row named by $P$. The route may replay the same
uniform, Cauchy-filter, stream, regular-readback, and completion-seal rows
through $H$ and $K$, but it cannot introduce a separate host completion, a
selected limit, quotient completion equality, or an ambient complete uniform
space.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the extension coordinate $E$ from the carrier. Its source rows are the
uniform space, Cauchy-filter, finite schedule, regular readback, and completion
seal coordinates already visible in the packet.

The universal-property surface reads $E$ as the finite extension ledger, while
the functor surface records that the same ledger is compatible with completion
routes. Both are named in $P$ and replayed through the displayed $H$ and $K$
rows.

Thus an extension consumer has exactly the rows
$U_0,F,S,R,C,E,H,K,P,N$. No coordinate remains from which to read a host
completed uniform space, selected limit object, quotient completion equality,
or ambient completeness theorem.
\end{proof}
