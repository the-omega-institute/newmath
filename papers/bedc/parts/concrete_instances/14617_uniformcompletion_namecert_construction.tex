\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\UniformCompletionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-uniformcompletion-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Kelley, \emph{General Topology}, and Bishop--Bridges constructive
completion, the $\UniformCompletionUp$ packet records uniform completion as
finite Cauchy-filter and Cauchy-net evidence over BEDC rows, not quotient
completion, selected limits, ambient completeness, or choice. The packet
threads the filter boundary of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyfilter-namecert}, the directed witness
boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchynet-namecert}, the extension
surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completionuniversalproperty-namecert},
the regular rational handoff of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the real readback
boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}.

\begin{definition}[UniformCompletion carrier]
\label{def:uniform-completion-carrier}
A $\UniformCompletionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
(F,D,U,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $F$ is the $\CauchyFilterUp$ data row recording the finite filterbase,
late-window, and cofinality evidence consumed by completion-facing readers;
\item $D$ is the directed $\CauchyNetUp$ witness row recording the finite net
indices, common-window witnesses, and extracted directed subwindows;
\item $U$ is the $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$ universal-extension row for
uniformly continuous consumers served by the same $F$ and $D$ rows;
\item $E$ is the completion equality classifier row, comparing two packet reads
only through the displayed filter, net, and universal-extension coordinates;
\item $H$ is the componentwise $\hsame$ row over $F,D,U,E$;
\item $C$ is the displayed $\Cont$ route used to replay the Cauchy-filter,
directed-net, extension, and classifier rows under finite observation;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyFilterUp$, $\CauchyNetUp$,
$\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$,
$\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\UniformCompletionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for quotient completion equality, selected
representatives, ambient complete uniform spaces, open-cover compactness, or
choice.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[UniformCompletion NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:uniform-completion-namecert-obligation-surface}
The local $\NameCert_{\UniformCompletionUp}$ surface of
\autoref{def:uniform-completion-carrier} consists of five obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed packet $(F,D,U,E,H,C,P,N)$;
\item Cauchy-filter factorization, requiring every completion-facing consumer
to read the displayed $\CauchyFilterUp$ row $F$ before any completion handoff;
\item directed-net witness extraction, requiring directed witnesses to be
projected from the $\CauchyNetUp$ row $D$ rather than from a selected limit;
\item universal-extension uniqueness, requiring uniformly continuous extension
comparisons to pass through the $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$ row $U$ and
the classifier row $E$;
\item ledger non-escape, stating that the packet exports only the rows named in
$P$ and never a quotient completion, selected representative, ambient
complete uniform space, open-cover compactness principle, or choice principle.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.UniformCompletionUp.TasteGate.UniformCompletion\_namecert\_obligation\_surface}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from the finite tuple
$(F,D,U,E,H,C,P,N)$ in \autoref{def:uniform-completion-carrier}. Each named
coordinate is already a BEDC row, and the local naming row $N$ records that
these coordinates are the public $\UniformCompletionUp$ surface.

Cauchy-filter factorization is the obligation that completion-facing reads
start with $F$. Directed-net witness extraction is the parallel obligation
that directed witnesses start with $D$, so neither route can introduce a
selected representative or an ambient limit point.

Universal-extension uniqueness is then confined to $U$ and $E$. The
componentwise $\hsame$ row $H$ and displayed $\Cont$ route $C$ replay these
same finite coordinates, while $P$ records that all downstream handoffs stay
inside the named Cauchy-filter, Cauchy-net, universal-property, RegSeqRat, and
Real rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[UniformCompletion Cauchy-filter factorization]
\label{thm:uniform-completion-cauchy-filter-factorization}
For an accepted $\UniformCompletionUp$ carrier
$(F,D,U,E,H,C,P,N)$, every completion-facing consumer factors through the
displayed $\CauchyFilterUp$ row $F$ and the $\CauchyNetUp$ witness row $D$
before reading any $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$ extension data or
$\RealUp$ handoff data. Thus the completion boundary is exhausted by
$F,D,U,E,H,C,P,N$ and exports no quotient completion, selected representative,
ambient complete uniform space, open-cover compactness principle, or choice
principle.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.UniformCompletionUp.TasteGate.UniformCompletion\_cauchy\_filter\_factorization}
\begin{proof}
Project an accepted carrier $(F,D,U,E,H,C,P,N)$. The first completion-facing
coordinate is $F$, so a consumer reaches Cauchy-filter evidence before it can
ask for any completion extension or real handoff.

The next witness coordinate is $D$. Its directed Cauchy-net subwindows provide
the directed evidence used by the completion route, and the packet contains no
separate selected-limit coordinate from which such witnesses could be read.

Only after $F$ and $D$ have been consumed can the route read the
$\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$ row $U$, the classifier row $E$, or the
$\RealUp$ handoff data named by $P$. The transport and replay rows $H$ and $C$
preserve this order because they are componentwise rows over the same finite
packet.
\end{proof}
