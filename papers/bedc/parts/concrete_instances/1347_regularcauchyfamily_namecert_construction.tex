\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RegularCauchyFamilyUp$}

\origin{human}

The $\RegularCauchyFamilyUp$ interface is the named input certificate consumed by $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$. Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, 1985, it presents a regular Cauchy family as visible regular-rational member rows with an explicit Cauchy modulus, finite stream windows, dyadic observation ledgers, and diagonal-completion routes. The packet exports no quotient stream, no choice-selected limit, no host real carrier, and no ambient completeness object.

Each family member is read first through the regular-name discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regularcauchyname-namecert}. Only after that sibling row has fixed the member surface do the modulus schedule, stream window, dyadic ledger, and diagonal route become consumable by the family packet, so the completion-facing handoff never treats a member as an unstructured rational stream.

\begin{definition}[RegularCauchyFamily carrier]
\label{def:regular-cauchy-family-carrier}
A $\RegularCauchyFamilyUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
F=(I,X,\mu,W,D,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $I$ is the finite $\RegSeqRatUp$ member-index ledger inspected by the family consumer;
\item $X$ assigns to each inspected row of $I$ a $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular rational member;
\item $\mu$ is the explicit $\CauchyModulusUp$ schedule that selects a late member row for each requested precision;
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window catalogue used to read the selected members;
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observation ledger containing the rational endpoints, radius rows, comparison rows, and precision rows used by the member reads;
\item $R$ is the $\RealDiagonalCompletionUp$ diagonal-route ledger that records how the selected member windows feed the completion-facing diagonal representative;
\item $H$ and $C$ are respectively the displayed $\hsame$ comparisons and $\Cont$ routes used to transport the member, modulus, stream, dyadic, and diagonal rows;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance naming $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\CauchyModulusUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealDiagonalCompletionUp$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, and $\Cont$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyFamilyUp}$ provenance row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier deliberately has no coordinate for a quotient of streams, a selected limiting real, host sequence equality, or a choice principle. Every completion-facing datum is one of the displayed rows of $F$.
\end{definition}
