\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\PackageExportDisciplineUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-package-export-discipline-namecert}
\origin{ai}

The $\PackageExportDisciplineUp$ packet realizes the export discipline of \autoref{sec:visions-philosophy-package-governance} as a finite NameCert carrier for philosophy-package exports. It sits beside the package-map boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-packagemapboundary-namecert}, the cannot-claim registry of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-philosophy-cannot-claim-registry-namecert}, the registry export surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-registry-export-surface-namecert}, the cannot-claim export gate of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cannot-claim-export-gate-namecert}, and the formal-target ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-philosophy-formal-target-ledger-namecert}. Those siblings expose map boundaries, refusal status, registry export rows, export-grade gates, and formal-target ledgers separately; this carrier binds them into one package export packet before a philosophy reading can leave the package.

\begin{definition}[Package export discipline carrier]
\label{def:package-export-discipline-carrier}
A $\PackageExportDisciplineUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
E=(M,R,S,G,T,U,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the package-map row, recording theorem-map, gap-map, tradition-map, scientific-map, and axis-boundary data from $\PackageMapBoundaryUp$;
\item $R$ is the refusal-registry row, recording cannot-claim status, exclusion reason, upgrade boundary, and blocking gate from $\PhilosophyCannotClaimRegistryUp$;
\item $S$ is the registry-export row, recording registration, blocking, status, machine target, audit route, and non-smuggling data from $\RegistryExportSurfaceUp$;
\item $G$ is the export-gate row, recording refusal, export decision, export grade, target route, and audit readback from $\CannotClaimExportGateUp$;
\item $T$ is the formal-target row, recording claim entry, target skeleton, no-smuggling, closure status, refusal boundary, and machine-readable audit route from $\PhilosophyFormalTargetLedgerUp$;
\item $U$ is the package non-smuggling row, recording that no export may bypass any of $M,R,S,G,T$ by prose adjacency, hidden registry state, or an unchecked machine target;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the five sibling rows, non-smuggling row, replay, provenance, and local naming;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay for package-map inspection, refusal inspection, registry-export inspection, gate inspection, formal-target inspection, and non-smuggling readback;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\PackageMapBoundaryUp$, $\PhilosophyCannotClaimRegistryUp$, $\RegistryExportSurfaceUp$, $\CannotClaimExportGateUp$, $\PhilosophyFormalTargetLedgerUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\PackageExportDisciplineUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for an export that preserves only a package map, only a registry row, only a refusal, only an export gate, only a formal target, or a hidden package-level synthesis outside the displayed packet.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Package export discipline NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:package-export-discipline-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:package-export-discipline-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\PackageExportDisciplineUp}$ obligation surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $E=(M,R,S,G,T,U,H,C,P,N)$, package-map coverage through $M$, refusal-registry coverage through $R$, registry-export coverage through $S$, cannot-claim export-gate coverage through $G$, formal-target coverage through $T$, non-smuggling coverage through $U$, componentwise transport through $H$, displayed replay through $C$, package provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the finite packet $E=(M,R,S,G,T,U,H,C,P,N)$. The first five rows are read as sibling NameCert surfaces: $M$ supplies the package-map boundary, $R$ supplies the cannot-claim registry, $S$ supplies the registry-export surface, $G$ supplies the cannot-claim export gate, and $T$ supplies the formal-target ledger.

Read the non-smuggling row next. It states that a package export is not admitted through prose adjacency, hidden registry state, or unchecked target routing unless the same packet carries $M,R,S,G,T$.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and locally name the displayed rows. Since the carrier has no further coordinate, these rows exhaust the local $\NameCert_{\PackageExportDisciplineUp}$ obligation surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Package export discipline non-smuggling boundary]
\label{thm:package-export-discipline-non-smuggling-boundary}
Every accepted $\PackageExportDisciplineUp$ export factors through the package-map row $M$, refusal-registry row $R$, registry-export row $S$, export-gate row $G$, formal-target row $T$, and non-smuggling row $U$ before local naming. Removing either the cannot-claim gate row $G$ or the registry-export row $S$ permits only a partial package record, not an accepted package export discipline packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the obligation theorem. The export reads $M,R,S,G,T$ before any package-level export can be named, and $U$ blocks routes that skip one of those rows.

If $S$ is absent, the packet has no displayed registry-export row recording registration, blocking, status, machine target, audit route, and non-smuggling data. A consumer may still inspect map or refusal data, but it cannot export a registry reading through $\PackageExportDisciplineUp$.

If $G$ is absent, the packet has no displayed cannot-claim export gate. A refused claim can no longer be checked against an export decision, export grade, target route, and audit readback inside the same package export carrier.

The rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve and name the accepted packet only after $M,R,S,G,T,U$ have been supplied. Hence a route missing $S$ or $G$ leaves the accepted carrier rather than producing a package export discipline packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Package export discipline cross-reference handoff]
\label{thm:package-export-discipline-cross-reference-handoff}
The Lean-facing handoff for $\PackageExportDisciplineUp$ must preserve package-map, registry-export, cannot-claim, export-gate, formal-target, non-smuggling, transport, replay, provenance, and local NameCert rows as distinct fields. A consumer that collapses any of $M,R,S,G,T$ cannot recover the cross-reference discipline required by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-packagemapboundary-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-philosophy-cannot-claim-registry-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-registry-export-surface-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cannot-claim-export-gate-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-philosophy-formal-target-ledger-namecert}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The five sibling rows are cited for different reasons. The package-map row supplies boundary coordinates, the refusal-registry row supplies cannot-claim status, the registry-export row supplies public export control, the export-gate row supplies the refused-claim gate, and the formal-target row supplies the machine-readable target ledger.

The non-smuggling, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows keep those citations tied to the same finite packet. They do not identify the rows with one another and do not create a replacement route when a sibling row is missing.

Therefore the handoff to Lean must use a field-faithful carrier. The proposed Lean follow-up must provide explicit $\Nontrivial\ \PackageExportDisciplineUp$ and $\FieldFaithful\ \PackageExportDisciplineUp$ instances in \texttt{lean4/BEDC/Derived/PackageExportDisciplineUp/TasteGate.lean}, with a target shaped for \texttt{BEDC.Derived.PackageExportDisciplineUp.PackageExportDisciplineTasteGate\_single\_carrier\_alignment}.
\end{proof}
