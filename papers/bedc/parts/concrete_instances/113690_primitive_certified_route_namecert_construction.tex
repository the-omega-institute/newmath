\begin{definition}[Primitive-certified route carrier]
\label{def:primitive-certified-route-carrier}
A $\PrimitiveCertifiedRouteUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
R=(D,H_0,A,Q,N_0,L,Z,T,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the generated distinction row, recording the primitive same-and-different boundary being certified.
\item $H_0$ is the history row, displaying the finite route by which the distinction entered the packet.
\item $A$ is the record row, naming the generated record inspected by the classifier.
\item $Q$ is the classifier row, assigning the record to the stable classifier surface consumed by the certificate.
\item $N_0$ is the inner $\NameCert$ row for the classified record.
\item $L$ is the ledger row, recording the certificate-facing audit trail from $D,H_0,A,Q,N_0$.
\item $Z$ is the CertifiedObject seal row exported only after the previous rows are displayed.
\item $T$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for distinction, history, record, classifier, certificate, ledger, seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay through the ordered route $D,H_0,A,Q,N_0,L,Z,T,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\PrimitiveCertifiedRouteUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\PrimitiveCertifiedRouteUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through the ordered rows $D,H_0,A,Q,N_0,L,Z,T,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for a certified-object seal detached from the distinction, history, record, classifier, certificate, ledger, transport, replay, provenance, or local naming rows.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Primitive-certified route NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:primitive-certified-route-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\PrimitiveCertifiedRouteUp$ carrier $R=(D,H_0,A,Q,N_0,L,Z,T,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\PrimitiveCertifiedRouteUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed distinction, history, record, classifier, certificate, ledger, seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item primitive order, requiring $D,H_0,A$ to be read before the classifier row $Q$ is available;
\item classifier certification, requiring $Q$ and $N_0$ to certify the same record before the ledger row $L$ is accepted;
\item ledger seal ordering, requiring $Z$ to be downstream of $L$ rather than a standalone certified-object export;
\item structural non-escape, requiring $T,C,P,N$ to transport, replay, source, and name only the ordered route already displayed.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:primitive-certified-route-carrier}. The listed rows are the whole surface available to an accepted read.

The primitive order obligation forces a read through $D,H_0,A$ before the classifier row $Q$ can be consumed. Thus the classifier is attached to a displayed generated record rather than to an unnamed object.

Classifier certification then binds $Q$ to the inner NameCert row $N_0$. The ledger row $L$ records that certification before the certified-object seal $Z$ can be exported.

Finally $T,C,P,N$ only preserve this ordered route. Because the carrier has no detached seal coordinate, every accepted certified-object read remains inside the displayed primitive-to-certified packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Primitive-certified route classifier nonescape]
\label{thm:primitive-certified-route-classifier-nonescape}
Every classifier-facing read of an accepted $\PrimitiveCertifiedRouteUp$ packet factors through
$$
D,H_0,A,Q,N_0,L,Z,T,C,P,N .
$$
In particular, the classifier row cannot export a CertifiedObject seal without the preceding distinction, history, record, NameCert, ledger, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows, and no sibling packet supplies a bijective replacement for that ordered route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier row of \autoref{def:primitive-certified-route-carrier}. A classifier-facing read reaches $Q$ only after the distinction, history, and record rows have been displayed.

Use the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:primitive-certified-route-namecert-obligations}. They force certification through $N_0$, ledger recording through $L$, and seal export through $Z$ in that order.

The structural rows $T,C,P,N$ preserve the route but do not add a detached classifier or certified-object coordinate. The independence witness separates this packet from sibling NameCert, audit-map, interface, compiler, and gate packets because none of them stores the same ordered primitive-to-certified carrier.
\end{proof}
