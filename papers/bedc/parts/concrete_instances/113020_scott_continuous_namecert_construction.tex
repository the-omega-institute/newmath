\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ScottContinuousUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-scott-continuous-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Scott's domain-theoretic treatment of continuous maps and the later domain-theory accounts of directed-complete orders, the $\ScottContinuousUp$ packet records morphisms that preserve directed suprema and Scott-open observation as finite BEDC naming data. It sits over the domain carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-scott-domain-namecert}, the directed-complete order surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dcpo-namecert}, the lattice-facing supremum discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-complete-lattice-namecert}, and the Scott-open interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-scott-topology-namecert}. The packet names a displayed continuity carrier; it does not assert a host function space, quotient map equality, selected pointwise supremum proof, arbitrary topological inverse image, or semantic completeness theorem.

\begin{definition}[ScottContinuous carrier]
\label{def:scott-continuous-carrier}
A $\ScottContinuousUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
F=(S,T,G,D,O,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the source $\DcpoUp$ or $\ScottDomainUp$ row, exposing the source order, directed-family schedule, supremum row, compact-basis data when present, and local NameCert surface.
\item $T$ is the target $\DcpoUp$ or $\ScottDomainUp$ row, exposing the target order, directed-family schedule, supremum row, compact-basis data when present, and local NameCert surface.
\item $G$ is the graph row, recording the finite source-target read used by the continuity packet rather than a host function object.
\item $D$ is the directed-supremum preservation ledger. For every displayed directed schedule admitted by $S$, it records that the graph read in $G$ carries the source supremum row to the target supremum row visible in $T$.
\item $O$ is the Scott-open inverse-image row. Every accepted Scott-open observation on the target is replayed through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-scott-topology-namecert} as a Scott-open observation on the source.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the source row, target row, graph row, directed-supremum ledger, inverse-image row, replay, provenance, and naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a source read, target read, directed-supremum preservation request, or Scott-open inverse-image request through $S,T,G,D,O,H$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ScottContinuousUp$, $\ScottDomainUp$, $\DcpoUp$, $\CompleteLatticeUp$, $\ScottTopologyUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ScottContinuousUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $S,T,G,D,O,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for a host exponential object, quotient equality of maps, pointwise choice of directed suprema, arbitrary inverse-image topology, or unrecorded semantic continuity proof.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[ScottContinuous NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:scott-continuous-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\ScottContinuousUp$ carrier $F=(S,T,G,D,O,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\ScottContinuousUp}$ surface consists exactly of source admission, target admission, graph-row admission, directed-supremum preservation through $D$, Scott-open inverse-image replay through $O$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming. Every continuity consumer must read $S,T,G,D,O,H,C,P,N$ before treating the morphism as public.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:scott-continuous-carrier}. The source and target order data are $S$ and $T$, the finite morphism presentation is $G$, the continuity ledgers are $D$ and $O$, and the structural rows are $H,C,P,N$.

The directed-supremum obligation reads the directed schedule and supremum row exposed by $S$, then checks the target supremum row exposed by $T$ through the graph row $G$. Thus preservation is attached to displayed dcpo or domain data and not to a host function space.

The Scott-open obligation reads target opens through the Scott-topology packet and replays their inverse image as source Scott-open data. The replay uses the same graph row and the same directed-supremum ledger, so it cannot introduce an arbitrary topological inverse-image operation.

Rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name this finite packet. Since quotient map equality, selected supremum witnesses, host exponentials, and semantic completeness are absent from the carrier, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ScottContinuous directed-supremum stability]
\label{thm:scott-continuous-directed-sup-stability}
Every accepted $\ScottContinuousUp$ packet preserves directed-supremum visibility under componentwise transport and continuation replay. If a consumer reads a directed source schedule through $S$ and the preservation ledger $D$, any transported or replayed read of the same continuity packet factors again through
$$
S,T,G,D,O,H,C,P,N.
$$
Thus directed-supremum preservation remains a finite ledger over displayed dcpo or domain rows and never exposes a host function equality, selected pointwise lub proof, or hidden target supremum oracle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a directed-supremum preservation read. By \autoref{def:scott-continuous-carrier}, the read reaches $D$ only after the source row $S$, target row $T$, and graph row $G$ are displayed.

Use \autoref{thm:scott-continuous-namecert-obligations}. Componentwise transport through $H$ preserves the same carrier rows, and $\Cont$ replay through $C$ repeats the displayed route rather than generating a second continuity source.

The provenance and local naming rows $P,N$ identify the same packet after the replay. Because selected pointwise lub proofs, host function equality, and hidden supremum oracles are not coordinates of $S,T,G,D,O,H,C,P,N$, the transported read remains the same finite directed-supremum ledger.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ScottContinuous domain handoff]
\label{thm:scott-continuous-domain-handoff}
Let $F=(S,T,G,D,O,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\ScottContinuousUp$ carrier whose source and target rows are $\ScottDomainUp$ packets. Any downstream domain-semantic consumer of $F$ must first read the source domain row, target domain row, graph row, directed-supremum preservation ledger, Scott-open inverse-image row, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows. The handoff exports no host domain morphism, quotient map equality, selected basis enumeration, or ambient semantic interpretation.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the source and target coordinates. The domain-facing information available to the consumer is precisely the pair of $\ScottDomainUp$ rows $S,T$ plus the graph and continuity rows $G,D,O$.

Apply the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:scott-continuous-namecert-obligations}. They force every public continuity read through $S,T,G,D,O,H,C,P,N$ before a downstream consumer can use it as a morphism of domain packets.

Finally use the Scott-domain and Scott-topology sibling surfaces cited at the chapter start. They provide directed-supremum and Scott-open observation rows as finite NameCert data. Since the continuity carrier merely links those rows and lacks a host morphism coordinate, the handoff is the displayed finite packet rather than an ambient semantic interpretation.
\end{proof}
