$$

\begin{enumerate}

\item $A$ is the coefficient-window row, displaying the scalar coefficients read at the same finite endpoints.
\item $D$ is the finite-difference row, displaying the adjacent coefficient differences used by the summation-by-parts calculation.
\item $B$ is the boundary-term row, displaying the two endpoint products and the finite interior products determined by $S,A,D$.
\item $T$ is the tail-estimate row, assigning a requested dyadic tolerance to a finite source window, coefficient window, and difference window.

\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[AbelSummation NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:abel-summation-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\AbelSummationUp$ carrier $Q=(S,A,D,B,T,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\AbelSummationUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed eleven-row packet;
\item partial-sum exposure for $S$, preserving the inspected source values and cumulative sums along componentwise $\hsame$ transport;
\item coefficient-window exposure for $A$, preserving the endpoint coefficients read by the finite identity;
\item finite-difference exposure for $D$, preserving the adjacent coefficient differences used by the interior products;
\item boundary exactness for $B$, stating that the displayed endpoint and interior terms are the finite summation-by-parts row determined by $S,A,D$;
\item tail-handoff exactness for $T,R,E$, forcing every completion-facing consumer through the displayed boundary row and regular rational handoff;
\item transport stability, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming for exactly the route recorded by $H,C,P,N$.
\end{enumerate}
These are obligations over displayed finite rows, not obligations over a host summation operator or a selected completed series.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet $Q=(S,A,D,B,T,R,E,H,C,P,N)$. The projection exposes exactly the partial-sum row, coefficient-window row, finite-difference row, boundary-term row, tail-estimate row, regular handoff, real seal, transport rows, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming row.

Read the finite identity through $S,A,D,B$. The row $S$ supplies cumulative sums on the inspected interval, $A$ supplies the endpoint coefficients, and $D$ supplies the adjacent coefficient differences. The boundary row $B$ records the endpoint products and interior products obtained from those rows.

Now read a precision request through $T$. The schedule names only finite windows, so the handoff row $R$ receives a regular rational estimate whose data are already displayed in $S,A,D,B,T$.

Finally apply the structural rows. Componentwise transport in $H$ preserves the analytic rows together, while $C,P,N$ replay, source, and name that same route. Since the carrier has no further coordinate, the seven listed clauses exhaust the local $\NameCert_{\AbelSummationUp}$ surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[AbelSummation partial-sum handoff]
\label{thm:abel-summation-partial-sum-handoff}
Every accepted $\AbelSummationUp$ carrier sends a displayed coefficient-weighted finite sum to a completion-facing consumer only through
$$
S,A,D,B,T \longmapsto R \longmapsto E,H,C,P,N.
$$
Thus a $\RegSeqRatUp$ or $\RealUp$ consumer may read the summation-by-parts calculation only after reading the finite partial sums, coefficient windows, adjacent differences, boundary terms, and tail estimate that determine the handoff.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with an accepted carrier and a completion-facing read. By the carrier definition, the only rows visible to such a read are the regular handoff row $R$, the seal row $E$, and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

Trace $R$ backward through the packet. Its input is the tail-estimate row $T$, and the estimate row is defined over the displayed finite rows $S,A,D,B$.

The partial sums, coefficient values, finite differences, and boundary terms are therefore read before any regular rational or real seal row can be consumed. No separate coordinate supplies a host sum, a quotient series equality, or a choice-selected limit.

Transport and replay preserve this route componentwise. Hence every completion-facing read factors through the displayed chain and cannot bypass the finite summation-by-parts packet.
\end{proof}
