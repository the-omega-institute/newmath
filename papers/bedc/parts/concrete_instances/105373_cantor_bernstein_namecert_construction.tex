\label{def:cantor-bernstein-carrier}

$$

$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ is the first classifier surface, naming the observed source-side rows and their equality test before any target-side identification is read.
\item $B$ is the second classifier surface, naming the observed target-side rows and their equality test before any source-side identification is read.
\item $I$ is the displayed injection row from $A$ into $B$, including source classifier preservation and target classifier reflection for every inspected finite row.
\item $J$ is the displayed injection row from $B$ into $A$, including target classifier preservation and source classifier reflection for every inspected finite row.
\item $X$ is the finite zig-zag decomposition ledger. It separates forward chains, backward chains, anchored components, and unresolved boundary components using only the two displayed injection rows.
\item $Y$ is the classifier-equivalence route read from $X$. It records which side supplies the representative for each finite zig-zag component without identifying host quotient classes.

\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the two classifier surfaces, the two injection rows, the zig-zag decomposition ledger, the classifier-equivalence route, and the exported boundary row.

Build the finite zig-zag ledger $X$ from the displayed reachability rows. The ledger separates chain components by finite inspection and hands the representative-side decision to $Y$ without invoking an ambient chain selector.

\end{proof}
