\begin{theorem}[TopGroupUp operation ledger obligation]
\label{thm:topgroup-operation-ledger-obligation}
The multiplication and inverse continuity obligations of a $\TopGroupUp$
certificate are exactly the two operation-continuity ledger families supplied
by the finite $\Cont$ ledger: product-neighbourhood transport and
inverse-neighbourhood transport. There is no third operation row in the public
certificate boundary.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.TopGroupUp.TopGroupRootThresholdPackage\_operation\_ledger\_semantic\_certificate}
\begin{proof}
Use \autoref{thm:topgroup-continuity-ledger-coverage}. Every public continuity
row over the accepted packet is covered by the finite $\Cont$ ledger and is one
of the product or inverse neighbourhood transport rows.

\autoref{thm:topgroup-cont-ledger-operation-exhaustion} identifies those two
families with the product and inverse components of
\autoref{def:topgroup-bhist-continuity-obligation-triple}. It also excludes
ambient continuity predicates and any operation outside the carried product and
inverse graphs.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:topgroup-product-inverse-obligation-triad}. The
public operation surface repacks the same two continuity families with the
shared carrier compatibility row. Therefore the operation ledger obligation has
exactly two operation families and no third operation row.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.TopGroupUp.TopGroupRootThresholdPackage\_operation\_ledger\_consumer\_boundary}
