\begin{definition}[TopGroupUp root shared source packet]
\label{def:topgroup-root-shared-source-packet}
The $\TopGroupUp$ root shared source packet is the projection of the carrier
of \autoref{def:topgroup-bhist-carrier} consisting of one displayed
$\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$ source row, one displayed $\NameCert_{\TopologyUp}$
source row, the shared $\mathsf{BHist}$ carrier endpoints, the componentwise
$\hsame$ classifier rows, the product and inverse operation rows, the finite
$\Cont$ ledger, and the $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance row. A downstream root
consumer reads the two source certificates only through this packet.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[TopGroupUp root shared carrier obligation]
\label{thm:topgroup-root-shared-carrier-obligation}
For every accepted $\TopGroupUp$ root shared source packet, the
$\GroupUp$ and $\TopologyUp$ source rows share the same $\mathsf{BHist}$
carrier of \autoref{def:topgroup-bhist-carrier}. Their endpoint classifiers
are the same componentwise $\hsame$ rows, their operation-continuity transport
is recorded in the same finite $\Cont$ ledger, and their provenance is the
same $\mathsf{Pkg}$ row. No root consumer can split these fields into two
independent source packets.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the shared packet of
\autoref{def:topgroup-root-shared-source-packet}. Its coordinates are exactly
the group source, topology source, carried endpoints, classifier rows, finite
continuity ledger, and provenance row projected from the $\TopGroupUp$
carrier.

Apply \autoref{thm:topgroup-source-fiber-product-carrier}. It identifies the
two source certificate rows, the product graph, inverse graph, neighbourhood
row, finite $\Cont$ ledger, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance as projections of
one $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:topgroup-public-consumer-minimality}. Since a
public consumer receives only those source, operation, ledger, classifier, and
provenance rows, there is no available second carrier or second provenance
packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[TopGroupUp root continuity classifier stability]
\label{thm:topgroup-root-continuity-classifier-stability}
For an accepted $\TopGroupUp$ root shared source packet, product continuity
and inverse continuity transport under the same componentwise classifier. The
product row transports its two input endpoints, product endpoint, target
neighbourhood, and $\Cont$ entry through the carried $\hsame$ rows, and the
inverse row transports its input endpoint, inverse endpoint, target
neighbourhood, and $\Cont$ entry through the same classifier packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the root shared source packet. The projection exposes the common
$\hsame$ endpoint classifier rows and the finite $\Cont$ ledger attached to
the product and inverse operation rows.

Use \autoref{thm:topgroup-product-cont-ledger-soundness} for the product
continuity row. It makes the row sound exactly through the two input
endpoints, product endpoint, target neighbourhood row, componentwise
$\hsame$ transports, and finite ledger entry projected from the shared packet.

Use \autoref{thm:topgroup-inverse-cont-ledger-soundness} for the inverse
continuity row. It gives the same classifier form for the input endpoint,
inverse endpoint, target neighbourhood row, and ledger entry.

The two rows therefore transport under one classifier packet: their endpoint
lists differ by arity, but their carrier, $\hsame$ source, $\Cont$ ledger, and
$\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance are the shared rows of the root packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[TopGroupUp root ledger exactness obligation]
\label{thm:topgroup-root-ledger-exactness-obligation}
For an accepted $\TopGroupUp$ root shared source packet, the finite $\Cont$
ledger exhausts exactly the product-continuity rows and inverse-continuity
rows. Every accepted continuity read is one of those two families, each listed
entry is sound through the shared $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet, and no host
topological-group continuity primitive is available outside the ledger.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the shared carrier obligation of
\autoref{thm:topgroup-root-shared-carrier-obligation}. It binds both source
certificates, the classifier rows, the continuity ledger, and the provenance
row to the same $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet.

Apply \autoref{thm:topgroup-ledger-exactness-obligation}. It states that the
finite $\Cont$ ledger covers every accepted product-neighbourhood and
inverse-neighbourhood continuity read, and that the two listed families are
sound through the shared packet.

Apply \autoref{thm:topgroup-public-consumer-minimality}. The public root
consumer receives no continuity row beyond the displayed source certificates,
operation rows, classifier rows, finite ledger, and provenance. Hence the
ledger is exact for precisely the product and inverse continuity obligations.
\end{proof}
