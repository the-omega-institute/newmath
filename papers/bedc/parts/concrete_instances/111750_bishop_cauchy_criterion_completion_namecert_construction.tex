It sits between the criterion carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bishop-cauchy-criterion-namecert}, the modulus family of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-modulus-space-namecert}, the left-exact completion boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-completion-left-exactness-namecert}, the finite windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the terminal real seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. \begin{definition}[Bishop Cauchy criterion completion carrier]
\label{def:bishop-cauchy-criterion-completion-carrier}
A $\BishopCauchyCriterionCompletionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(Q,M,W,R,D,E,L,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $Q$ is the source $\BishopCauchyCriterionUp$ row, naming the finite Cauchy criterion request that will be consumed by completion-facing data.
\item $M$ is the $\CauchyModulusSpaceUp$ row, recording the explicit modulus schedules and their precision requests for the same criterion.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window row through which the source sequence, modulus requests, and finite tail comparisons are inspected.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row assembled from the windows in $W$ and the tolerance schedule in $M$.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, recording the dyadic radii and comparison budgets used by the criterion and readback rows.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after $Q,M,W,R,D$ have been displayed.
\item $L$ is the $\CauchyCompletionLeftExactnessUp$ handoff row, recording that completion-facing finite-limit reads consume the same criterion, modulus, window, readback, tolerance, and real-seal rows.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $Q,M,W,R,D,E,L,C,P,N$.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a completion request through the criterion, modulus schedule, stream windows, rational readback, dyadic tolerance ledger, real seal, and left-exactness handoff.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BishopCauchyCriterionCompletionUp$, $\BishopCauchyCriterionUp$, $\CauchyModulusSpaceUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\CauchyCompletionLeftExactnessUp$, $\CompletionUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\BishopCauchyCriterionCompletionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $Q,M,W,R,D,E,L,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for quotient stream equality, selected Cauchy representatives, selected limits, countable choice, trichotomy, host real equality, or ambient completeness of $\RealUp$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Bishop Cauchy criterion completion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:bishop-cauchy-criterion-completion-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\BishopCauchyCriterionCompletionUp$ carrier
$$
K=(Q,M,W,R,D,E,L,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\BishopCauchyCriterionCompletionUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the Bishop Cauchy criterion row, explicit Cauchy modulus schedules, finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance exposure, terminal $\RealUp$ seal exposure, $\CauchyCompletionLeftExactnessUp$ handoff, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming. Every completion-facing read must expose $Q,M,W,R,D,E,L$ before $H,C,P,N$ can be consumed.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:bishop-cauchy-criterion-completion-carrier}. The analytic rows are exactly $Q,M,W,R,D,E,L$, and the structural rows are exactly $H,C,P,N$.

The source criterion row $Q$ supplies the Bishop Cauchy request, while the modulus row $M$ makes the precision schedule explicit. The finite windows $W$ are therefore the only observation surface through which the criterion can be read.

Next the readback and tolerance rows are forced through $R$ and $D$. The terminal real seal $E$ is downstream of those rational and dyadic rows, so no real-facing consumer can bypass the displayed criterion data.

Finally the completion handoff $L$ records the left-exact completion-facing use of the same route, and $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name it. Since no excluded host object is present as a carrier coordinate, these rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}
