\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\UnaryContMonoidUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-unarycontmonoid-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\UnaryContMonoidUp$ packet is the concrete monoid-shaped reading of the BEDC unary continuation calculus. It realizes the continuation-as-computation vision of \autoref{ch:visions-computation-as-continuation} on the finite $\BHist$ surface: unary histories are the carrier, $\Cont$ composition is the operation, the empty history is the unit, $\hsame$ is the classifier, and the local ledger records only displayed continuation rows.

It is the monoid sibling of the Kleisli-style $\ContinuationMonadUp$ packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuationmonad-namecert} and the category-shaped $\CategoryUp$ packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-category-namecert}. The chapter does not introduce a host monoid primitive, quotient equality, or external algebraic carrier.

\begin{definition}[Unary Cont monoid carrier]
\label{def:unary-cont-monoid-carrier}
A $\UnaryContMonoidUp$ carrier is a finite packet
$$
M=(U,\mu,e,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $U$ is the unary carrier row: a displayed history $h$ is admitted exactly through $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(h)$.
\item $\mu$ is the binary continuation operation row: for unary histories $h,k,r$, the product $\mu(h,k)=r$ is displayed by the continuation ledger $\ContR(h,k,r)$.
\item $e$ is the unit row, fixed to the empty history $\emp$ with the displayed left and right unit continuations.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for unary histories, continuation results, unit rows, and ledger entries.
\item $C$ records the associativity and unit continuation ledgers for $\mu$, including the two parenthesized continuation routes over triples of unary histories.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$, $\Cont$, $\hsame$, $\Pkg$, $\NameCert$, $\ContinuationMonadUp$, and $\CategoryUp$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\UnaryContMonoidUp}$ row for carrier admission, classifier stability, operation closure, unit and associativity exactness, and ledger non-escape.
\end{enumerate}
Thus the carrier is the tuple $(U,\mu,e,H,C,P,N)$ with $U=\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$, $\mu$ given by $\Cont$ composition, and $e=\emp$. Every readable algebraic row is a displayed BEDC history row.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Unary Cont monoid NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:unary-cont-monoid-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\UnaryContMonoidUp}$ surface consists exactly of five finite obligations: carrier admission for the unary history row $U$; classifier stability under $\hsame$ transport; closure of $\mu$ under displayed $\Cont$ composition; left-unit, right-unit, and associativity exactness for the empty row and the continuation ledgers in $C$; and non-escape of the ledger through the provenance and local naming rows $P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is the first coordinate of \autoref{def:unary-cont-monoid-carrier}. It accepts precisely unary histories and uses no carrier outside $\BHist$.

Classifier stability is componentwise transport through $H$. The transported carrier row, operation row, unit row, and ledger row remain the same kind of displayed history data.

Closure and exactness are the continuation laws already read by the sibling packets. The unit clauses are the empty-history laws of \autoref{lem:unary-continuation-unit}, and the associativity clause is the continuation associativity law of \autoref{lem:unary-continuation-associativity}, equivalently the monoid ledger summarized by \autoref{thm:history-continuation-monoid-laws}.

Finally, $P$ and $N$ record only the named BEDC rows. Since no coordinate names a host monoid object, quotient product, or external equality, the local ledger cannot export anything beyond the unary continuation packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Unary Cont associativity]
\label{thm:unary-cont-monoid-cont-associativity}
For unary histories $a,b,c$ and displayed continuation results
$$
\ContR(a,b,ab)
\quad\text{and}\quad
\ContR(b,c,bc),
$$
the two continuation products
$$
\mu(\mu(a,b),c)
\quad\text{and}\quad
\mu(a,\mu(b,c))
$$
are classified together by $\hsame$ whenever both are read through the carrier of \autoref{def:unary-cont-monoid-carrier}. The empty history $\emp$ is a two-sided unit for the same operation.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read $\mu$ as displayed $\Cont$ composition. The left-associated product first consumes the ledger for $\ContR(a,b,ab)$ and then composes with $c$; the right-associated product first consumes the ledger for $\ContR(b,c,bc)$ and then composes from $a$.

Apply the core continuation associativity row cited in \autoref{lem:unary-continuation-associativity}. It compares exactly these two parenthesized displayed routes by $\hsame$, so the two carrier results are classified together.

For the unit rows, apply \autoref{lem:unary-continuation-unit}. The left and right products with $\emp$ read back to the original unary history under the same classifier. These facts are ledger entries in $C$, not host monoid equalities.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Unary Cont monoid operation closure]
\label{thm:unary-cont-monoid-operation-closure}
For unary histories $a,b$ and a displayed continuation result $r$ with $\ContR(a,b,r)$, the product row $\mu(a,b)=r$ remains inside the $\UnaryContMonoidUp$ carrier. It is transported componentwise by $\hsame$ and packaged by the same $\Pkg$ provenance and $\NameCert$ rows as the carrier packet.

No quotient product, host monoid multiplication, or external carrier equality is introduced by this operation row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:unary-cont-monoid-carrier}. Its unary row admits histories through $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$, and its operation row is exactly displayed $\ContR$ composition.

Use the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:unary-cont-monoid-namecert-obligations}. They include closure of $\mu$ under displayed $\Cont$ composition, componentwise classifier stability under $\hsame$, and packaging by the provenance and local naming rows $P,N$.

The associativity and unit theorem \autoref{thm:unary-cont-monoid-cont-associativity} supplies the operation ledger used by later algebraic reads. That ledger remains a continuation row inside the same carrier, so it adds neither a quotient product nor a host monoid multiplication nor an external equality.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Unary Cont sibling dependency]
\label{thm:unary-cont-monoid-sibling-dependency}
The $\UnaryContMonoidUp$ packet is the monoid-shaped dependency sibling of $\ContinuationMonadUp$ and $\CategoryUp$: it consumes the continuation unit and associativity rows named by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuationmonad-namecert}, agrees with the tail-composition reading used by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-category-namecert}, and realizes the operational interpretation of \autoref{ch:visions-computation-as-continuation} without adding any host monoid primitive, quotient carrier, or non-BEDC operation.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The continuation-as-computation chapter reads $\Cont$ as the small-step route and identifies tail append as the operational composite. The carrier of \autoref{def:unary-cont-monoid-carrier} stores exactly that route as $\mu$.

The $\ContinuationMonadUp$ sibling supplies the same unit and associativity ledger for Kleisli-style two-step continuation reads. Forgetting source, middle, and target notation leaves the unary carrier, the binary continuation result, and the empty unit row.

The $\CategoryUp$ sibling reads the same tails as hom-carrier composition. Forgetting object and arrow packaging leaves precisely the monoid-shaped operation on unary histories. Thus the dependency is a repackaging of existing continuation rows, with no additional algebraic primitive.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[Unary Cont monoid kernel scope]
\label{def:unary-cont-monoid-kernel-scope}
The $\UnaryContMonoidUp$ kernel scope is the finite row package
$$
\mathsf{UnaryHistory},\quad \ContR,\quad \hsame,\quad \emp,\quad \Pkg,\quad \NameCert,\quad \ContinuationMonadUp,\quad \CategoryUp.
$$
It binds the carrier to unary histories, the operation to displayed $\ContR$ composition, the unit to the empty-history row, classifier transport to $\hsame$, and sibling provenance to the continuation-monad and category packets.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Unary Cont monoid scoped grounding]
\label{thm:unary-cont-monoid-scoped-grounding}
For every accepted $\UnaryContMonoidUp$ carrier, associativity, left and right unit exactness, carrier transport, and ledger non-escape all factor through the rows of \autoref{def:unary-cont-monoid-kernel-scope}. The scoped grounding cannot invoke a host monoid, a quotient product, or any carrier equality not displayed by $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$, $\ContR$, $\hsame$, $\emp$, $\Pkg$, $\NameCert$, $\ContinuationMonadUp$, and $\CategoryUp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission reads unary histories through $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ and reads the operation through displayed $\ContR$ composition. The unit row is the empty history $\emp$, so the carrier has no separate algebraic object.

Associativity and unit exactness are inherited as continuation ledger rows from $\ContinuationMonadUp$ and $\CategoryUp$. Transport is componentwise $\hsame$ transport of the same displayed histories and continuation results.

The provenance and local naming rows package those finite facts for consumers. Since every algebraic read has already factored through the listed kernel rows, the ledger cannot escape to host monoids, quotient products, or external carrier equality.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Unary Cont monoid public NameCert export]
\label{thm:unary-cont-monoid-public-namecert-export}
The carrier of \autoref{def:unary-cont-monoid-carrier}, the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:unary-cont-monoid-namecert-obligations}, the associativity and unit exactness of \autoref{thm:unary-cont-monoid-cont-associativity}, the sibling dependency of \autoref{thm:unary-cont-monoid-sibling-dependency}, the kernel scope of \autoref{def:unary-cont-monoid-kernel-scope}, and the scoped grounding of \autoref{thm:unary-cont-monoid-scoped-grounding} assemble a public $\NameCert_{\UnaryContMonoidUp}$ export over
$$
\mathsf{UnaryHistory},\quad \ContR,\quad \hsame,\quad \emp,\quad \Pkg,\quad \NameCert,\quad \ContinuationMonadUp,\quad \CategoryUp .
$$
The export excludes host monoids, quotient products, arbitrary carrier monoids, and external algebraic equality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier and obligation rows. The carrier of \autoref{def:unary-cont-monoid-carrier} exposes unary histories, displayed continuation composition, the empty unit, transport, route, provenance, and local naming rows; \autoref{thm:unary-cont-monoid-namecert-obligations} states that these rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.

The algebraic rows are already internal to the continuation ledger. Associativity and the two unit laws are supplied by \autoref{thm:unary-cont-monoid-cont-associativity}, while the sibling dependency of \autoref{thm:unary-cont-monoid-sibling-dependency} records that the same rows are consumed from $\ContinuationMonadUp$ and $\CategoryUp$.

The public export surface is therefore the finite kernel scope of \autoref{def:unary-cont-monoid-kernel-scope}. The scoped grounding theorem \autoref{thm:unary-cont-monoid-scoped-grounding} says that every accepted read factors through exactly those rows.

Since no listed row names a host monoid, quotient product, arbitrary carrier monoid, or external equality relation, the public $\NameCert_{\UnaryContMonoidUp}$ export is a BEDC continuation packet and not an imported algebraic object.
\end{proof}
