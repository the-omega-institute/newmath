\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CountableMetricCompletionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-countable-metric-completion-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges and Kelley, the $\CountableMetricCompletionUp$ packet names the L10 completion route by which a countably displayed metric source exposes completion names through stream schedules, regular rational readbacks, dyadic tolerance ledgers, and terminal real seals. It refines the finite-window completion surfaces of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bishop-metric-completion-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-sequential-completion-namecert}, and it routes its observable data through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separatedcompletion-namecert}. The packet records only the displayed countable source, enumerated metric windows, regular sequence route, separated comparison, transport, replay, provenance, and local NameCert rows.

\begin{definition}[Countable metric completion carrier]
\label{def:countable-metric-completion-carrier}
A $\CountableMetricCompletionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
C=(M,A,S,Q,D,Z,R,H,K,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the metric source row, naming the source point reads, distance comparison windows, and metric ledger offered to the completion request.
\item $A$ is the countable admission row, exposing the finite portion of the source enumeration that the request actually inspects.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule row for the selected finite prefixes of candidate completion names.
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row obtained from the displayed stream windows.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger for the requested radii, slack rows, and comparison budgets.
\item $Z$ is the separated comparison row inherited from the separated-completion route after the same $M,A,S,Q,D$ rows have been displayed.
\item $R$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row for metric distances and completion-facing comparison readbacks.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the metric source, countable admission, stream schedule, regular readback, dyadic ledger, separated comparison, Real seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $K$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a completion request through $M,A,S,Q,D,Z,R,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CountableMetricCompletionUp$, $\BishopMetricCompletionUp$, $\SequentialCompletionUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\SeparatedCompletionUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CountableMetricCompletionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $M,A,S,Q,D,Z,R,H,K,P,N$. It has no coordinate for quotient completions, selected limits, countable choice, host powersets, host equality, trichotomy, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Countable metric completion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:countable-metric-completion-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\CountableMetricCompletionUp$ packet $C=(M,A,S,Q,D,Z,R,H,K,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CountableMetricCompletionUp}$ surface consists of carrier admission, countable source exposure, finite stream scheduling, regular rational readback, dyadic tolerance checking, separated comparison, terminal Real sealing, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, provenance, and ledger non-escape. Every obligation is discharged through the displayed route
$$
M\longmapsto A\longmapsto S\longmapsto Q\longmapsto D\longmapsto Z\longmapsto R
$$
together with $H,K,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:countable-metric-completion-carrier}. The metric source row $M$ and countable admission row $A$ must be visible before any completion schedule is inspected.

The schedule row $S$ exposes only finite stream prefixes. The regular readback row $Q$ and dyadic ledger $D$ then provide the rational observations and tolerance budgets used by the separated comparison row $Z$.

The terminal real seal $R$ is reached only after that finite route. Componentwise transport through $H$ and replay through $K$ preserve the same rows, while $P$ and $N$ source and name the packet.

Since quotient completions, selected limits, countable choice, host powersets, host equality, trichotomy, and ambient completeness are absent from the carrier, the displayed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Countable metric completion Cauchy sequence route]
\label{thm:countable-metric-completion-cauchy-sequence-route}
Every Cauchy-sequence read of an accepted $\CountableMetricCompletionUp$ packet factors through the displayed source metric row, countable admission row, $\StreamNameUp$ schedule, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, separated comparison row, transport, replay, provenance, and local NameCert rows. The route exports finite Cauchy evidence and separated comparison evidence; it does not export a quotient class, a selected representative, a countable-choice selector, or a host powerset of all sequences.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use \autoref{thm:countable-metric-completion-namecert-obligations}. It places every accepted completion read on the route $M,A,S,Q,D,Z,R,H,K,P,N$.

A Cauchy-facing consumer first receives only the finite source enumeration visible in $A$. It then reads stream prefixes through $S$, rational regular-sequence observations through $Q$, and tolerance budgets through $D$.

The separated comparison row $Z$ is downstream of those rows, so equality-facing or distance-facing reads cannot bypass the displayed Cauchy evidence. The structural rows $H,K,P,N$ preserve and name that route.

No carrier row contains a quotient class, representative selector, choice function, or host powerset, so the Cauchy-sequence consumer boundary is exactly the finite displayed route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Countable metric completion Real handoff]
\label{thm:countable-metric-completion-real-handoff}
Every $\RealUp$-facing handoff from an accepted $\CountableMetricCompletionUp$ packet reaches the terminal real seal only after the metric source, countable admission, stream schedule, regular rational readback, dyadic tolerance ledger, and separated comparison rows have been displayed. Thus the handoff can reuse the Real row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert} for named distance readbacks without asserting ambient Real completeness, selected limits, quotient completion equality, trichotomy, or countable choice.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the carrier route in \autoref{def:countable-metric-completion-carrier}. The row $R$ is not an independent source of completion data; it is downstream of $M,A,S,Q,D,Z$.

Replay through $K$ therefore reaches the terminal $\RealUp$ seal only after the stream schedule, regular rational readback, dyadic tolerance ledger, and separated comparison rows have been inspected. Componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$ keeps the same dependence under name change.

The Real-facing consumer receives a seal for the displayed readback, not a completeness principle. Since no excluded object is a coordinate of $C$, the handoff cannot introduce selected limits, quotient equality, trichotomy, countable choice, or ambient Real completeness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Countable metric completion ledger non-escape]
\label{thm:countable-metric-completion-ledger-nonescape}
The provenance and local naming rows of an accepted $\CountableMetricCompletionUp$ packet prevent downstream completion consumers from reading outside the displayed L10 ledger. Any consumer of the packet receives only $M,A,S,Q,D,Z,R,H,K,P,N$ and the cited Bishop metric completion, sequential completion, StreamName, RegSeqRat, DyadicRatCore, separated-completion, and Real rows; no host completion, quotient universe, selected limit operation, countable-choice principle, or ambient Real-completeness theorem is exported.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:countable-metric-completion-namecert-obligations} to expose the local NameCert surface. The provenance row $P$ cites the completion and numerical chapters only through their displayed packet rows, and the local name $N$ records the same finite carrier.

Replay through $K$ cannot add a new coordinate. It can only revisit the source, countable admission, stream schedule, regular readback, dyadic ledger, separated comparison, Real seal, and transport rows already present in $C$.

Consequently every downstream consumer is confined to the L10 ledger named by the packet. Host completions, quotient universes, selected limits, countable choice, and ambient Real completeness are not exported because no row of the carrier stores them.
\end{proof}
