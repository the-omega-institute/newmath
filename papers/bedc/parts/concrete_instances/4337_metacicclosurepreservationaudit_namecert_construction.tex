\begin{definition}[MetaCIC closure preservation audit carrier]
\label{def:metacic-closure-preservation-audit-carrier}
A $\MetaCICClosurePreservationAuditUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(T,G,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $T$ is the five-row theorem ledger for $\texttt{shift\_one\_preserves\_closed\_succ}$, $\texttt{substitute\_var\_preserves\_closed\_at}$, $\texttt{substitute\_preserves\_closed\_at}$, $\texttt{betaStep\_preserves\_closed}$, and $\texttt{betaStarStep\_preserves\_closed}$;
\item $G$ is the generator-closure ledger for the closed-under-shift and closed-under-substitute classifier rows named in \autoref{sec:metacic-closure-preservation-audit-map-generator-classifier};
\item $S$ is the handoff ledger that records how the substitution rows feed the beta-step row and how the beta-step row feeds the finite beta-chain row;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for theorem rows, generator-closure rows, handoff rows, continuation rows, provenance rows, and local naming rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes for reading shift closedness, variable substitution closedness, full substitution closedness, one-step beta closedness, and beta-chain closedness in dependency order;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over \autoref{ch:metacic-closure-preservation-audit-map}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-kernelacceptancetrace-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-substitutionauditmap-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacicnormalizationauditseal-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-subjectreduction-namecert};
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MetaCICClosurePreservationAuditUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for typing preservation, conversion typing, closed subject reduction, host reduction semantics, quotient equality of terms, or an external proof oracle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC closure preservation audit NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:metacic-closure-preservation-audit-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:metacic-closure-preservation-audit-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\MetaCICClosurePreservationAuditUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $Q=(T,G,S,H,C,P,N)$, theorem-ledger readback through $T$, generator-closure readback through $G$, substitution-to-beta handoff readback through $S$, componentwise transport through $H$, displayed route replay through $C$, package provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The five theorem rows in $T$ are the complete checked closedness-preservation chain named by \autoref{ch:metacic-closure-preservation-audit-map}.

Read $G$ and $S$ next. The generator rows authorize shift and substitution only relative to the closedness classifier, while the handoff row orders the substitution endpoint before beta-step and beta-chain reads.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, locate, and name the same packet. Since no further coordinate is present, these rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC closure preservation substitution-to-beta handoff]
\label{thm:metacic-closure-preservation-audit-substitution-beta-chain}
Every accepted $\MetaCICClosurePreservationAuditUp$ carrier routes closedness preservation from substitution to beta movement through the ordered ledger
$$
\texttt{substitute\_preserves\_closed\_at}
\longrightarrow
\texttt{betaStep\_preserves\_closed}
\longrightarrow
\texttt{betaStarStep\_preserves\_closed}.
$$
The route may read the shift and variable-substitution rows as prerequisites of the full substitution row, but it cannot bypass the displayed substitution row when certifying the beta-redex case or bypass the one-step row when certifying a finite beta chain.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the handoff coordinate $S$. Its first dependency records that the full substitution theorem is obtained from the shift and variable-substitution rows in the theorem ledger.

The beta-step read then consumes that substitution row only at the beta-redex boundary. Congruence cases stay inside the same theorem ledger and do not create a typing or conversion route.

Finally the finite beta-chain read consumes the one-step beta row. The continuation row $C$ preserves this order for downstream consumers, so the packet exposes a dependency chain rather than an unstructured collection of theorem names.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC closure preservation subject-reduction boundary]
\label{thm:metacic-closure-preservation-audit-subject-reduction-boundary}
An accepted $\MetaCICClosurePreservationAuditUp$ packet may be consumed by the subject-reduction surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-subjectreduction-namecert} only as closedness-stability data. It exports the displayed rows $T,G,S,H,C,P,N$ and no row for $\mathsf{HasType}$ preservation, dependent-codomain transport, binder-domain preservation, beta-substitution typing, conversion typing, or closed subject reduction.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier rows. The theorem ledger $T$ is stated over the closedness classifier, and $G$ records generator closure for that classifier rather than for typing membership.

The handoff row $S$ reaches beta-step and beta-chain closedness, not target typing. Its routes are therefore usable around the preservation-facing packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-subjectreduction-namecert} without supplying any of the typed discharge rows named there.

The exclusion follows from the missing coordinates. The transport, continuation, provenance, and naming rows replay $T,G,S$ componentwise, so they cannot introduce a hidden subject-reduction theorem.
\end{proof}
