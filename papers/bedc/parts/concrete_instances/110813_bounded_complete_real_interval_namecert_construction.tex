Following Bishop and Bridges on constructive real intervals, the $\BoundedCompleteRealIntervalUp$ packet records a bounded interval together with the finite nested-window route used by Real and completion consumers. It sits over the dyadic rational arithmetic of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the stream-window interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal Real seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet names a bounded interval principle for displayed nested windows; it does not assert open-cover compactness, a global supremum operator, quotient real equality, countable choice, trichotomy, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[BoundedCompleteRealInterval carrier]
\label{def:bounded-complete-real-interval-carrier}
A $\BoundedCompleteRealIntervalUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
I=(B,D,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $B$ is the bounded interval row. It records the inspected lower and upper $\RealUp$ endpoint names, the boundedness witness visible to the packet, and the local containment predicate for interval windows.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint ledger. It records the dyadic rational cuts used to compare successive windows and to certify the displayed diameter bound.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window row. It lists exactly the finite endpoint and interval observations that may be inspected by the nested-window route.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row attached to the same dyadic cuts and stream windows.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row reached by every Real-facing extraction from $B,D,W,R$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for bounded interval rows, dyadic ledgers, stream windows, regular rational readbacks, Real seals, replay, provenance, and the local name.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a nested-window request through $B,D,W,R,E$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BoundedCompleteRealIntervalUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\BoundedCompleteRealIntervalUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $B,D,W,R,E,H,C,P,N$. There is no coordinate for open-cover compactness, a selected supremum, host interval equality, quotient equality of Real names, countable choice, trichotomy, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[BoundedCompleteRealInterval NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:bounded-complete-real-interval-obligations}
For every accepted $\BoundedCompleteRealIntervalUp$ carrier
$$
I=(B,D,W,R,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\BoundedCompleteRealIntervalUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the bounded interval row, dyadic endpoint ledger, stream windows, regular rational readback, Real seal, componentwise transport, continuation replay, package provenance, and local naming row;
\item bounded-window visibility, requiring every interval read to pass through the displayed lower-upper row $B$ and its local containment predicate;
\item dyadic diameter discipline, requiring $D$ to witness the inspected endpoint comparisons and the shrinking-width estimates used by the extraction route;
\item stream-readback exactness, requiring $W$ and $R$ to reuse the same finite endpoint observations and dyadic cuts rather than selecting a second Real representative;
\item Real-seal nonescape, requiring every Real-facing output to pass through $E$ after $B,D,W,R$ have all been projected;
\item structural preservation through $H,C,P,N$, with no exported row for open-cover compactness, selected suprema, host interval equality, quotient Real equality, countable choice, trichotomy, or ambient Real completeness.
\end{enumerate}
No local obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:bounded-complete-real-interval-carrier}. The accepted packet displays exactly the bounded interval row, dyadic endpoint ledger, stream windows, regular rational readback, Real seal, and the structural rows.

Bounded-window visibility is forced by $B$. A consumer can inspect only the finite lower-upper interval data and the local containment predicate already present in the carrier.

Dyadic diameter discipline is forced by $D$. The endpoint comparisons and shrinking-width estimates used by the packet are the only numerical rows available to the later stream and readback rows.

The rows $W$ and $R$ are downstream of the same dyadic ledger. They schedule finite observations and repack them as regular rational readback without replacing the bounded interval by a second Real representative.

Finally, $E,H,C,P,N$ close the route. The Real seal is reached only after $B,D,W,R$, while transport, replay, provenance, and naming preserve the same displayed data. Since no excluded coordinate occurs in the carrier, the local NameCert surface has exactly the listed obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BoundedCompleteRealInterval nested-interval extraction]
\label{thm:bounded-complete-real-interval-nested-interval-extraction}
Let $I=(B,D,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\BoundedCompleteRealIntervalUp$ carrier. Every nested-window extraction exported by $I$ factors through the finite route
$$
B\longmapsto D\longmapsto W\longmapsto R\longmapsto E
$$
before it can inspect transport, replay, provenance, or local naming rows. The extracted Real-facing read is therefore determined by the bounded interval row, the dyadic diameter ledger, the stream windows, and the regular rational readback visible in the packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a nested-window extraction from $I$. The carrier gives no source row before the bounded interval row $B$, so the lower-upper interval data and local containment predicate are inspected first.

The extraction then projects the dyadic ledger $D$. This fixes the endpoint comparisons and diameter estimates available to the finite window schedule.

Next the extraction passes through $W$ and $R$. The stream row exposes only the finite endpoint observations allowed by $B,D$, and the regular rational row repacks those same observations as the readback consumed by the Real seal.

Only after this route has been displayed can the extraction reach $E$. The remaining rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve, replay, source, and name the same route, so the exported extraction is exhausted by the displayed bounded-window data.
\end{proof}
