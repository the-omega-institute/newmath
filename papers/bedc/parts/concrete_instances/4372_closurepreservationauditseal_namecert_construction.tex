$$

\item $R$ is the shift row for $\mathsf{shift\_one\_preserves\_closed\_succ}$;
\item $V$ is the variable-substitution row for $\mathsf{substitute\_var\_preserves\_closed\_at}$;

\begin{theorem}[Closure-preservation audit seal NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:closure-preservation-audit-seal-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:closure-preservation-audit-seal-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\ClosurePreservationAuditSealUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for
$$
S=(A,R,V,U,B,Z,G,H,C,P,N),
$$
audit-anchor visibility through $A$, closed shift visibility through $R$, variable-substitution visibility through $V$, full-substitution visibility through $U$, beta-step visibility through $B$, beta-star visibility through $Z$, the displayed $\mathsf{GAP}$ refusal through $G$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, package provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the displayed $\BHist$ packet. The audit anchor $A$ fixes the source chapter, while $R,V,U,B,Z$ are exactly the five closure-preservation theorem rows listed there.

Read the boundary row $G$ next. It records that the seal is a closedness-classifier object: the packet may expose shift, substitution, beta-step, and beta-star closedness, but it does not expose typing preservation, conversion transport, normalization, confluence, or subject reduction.

Finally inspect $H,C,P,N$. Transport, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming preserve the same finite rows and add no hidden coordinate. Hence the displayed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligation surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Closure-preservation audit seal route exactness]
\label{thm:closure-preservation-audit-seal-route-exactness}
Every downstream read of an accepted $\ClosurePreservationAuditSealUp$ packet that claims a closedness-preservation route factors through one of the displayed theorem rows $R,V,U,B,Z$, then through the matching $\Cont$ route in $C$, and then through the same $\hsame$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows $H,P,N$. In particular, a closed-normal or subject-reduction-boundary consumer may cite beta-star closedness only through $Z$ together with the finite replay and provenance rows; it may not replace that route by a direct appeal to vision prose or by an unrecorded typing-preservation step.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted packet and project the route component $C$. Its displayed continuations have sources only in $R,V,U,B,Z$ and in the sibling-facing rows named by $P$.

For a shift or substitution read, the route reaches $R,V,U$ and then uses the same transport row $H$ before it is reported to a consumer. This is the route used by the substitution audit packet, so the consumer receives a finite closed-syntax movement row rather than an unrestricted substitution calculus.

For a beta-step or beta-star read, the route reaches $B$ or $Z$ and then passes through $H,P,N$. The closed-normal consistency boundary can therefore cite a finite beta closedness route, while the $\mathsf{GAP}$ row keeps subject-reduction discharge and typed conversion outside the route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Closure-preservation audit seal non-escape]
\label{thm:closure-preservation-audit-seal-nonescape}
Every public read of an accepted $\ClosurePreservationAuditSealUp$ packet remains inside the finite rows
$$
A,R,V,U,B,Z,G,H,C,P,N.
$$
The seal exports no $\mathsf{HasType}$ preservation theorem, no closed subject-reduction theorem, no discharge of beta-substitution typing, application-argument typing, lambda-domain typing, or Pi-domain typing obligations, no quotient equality over reductions, and no host semantic conversion principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The only positive theorem rows in the carrier are $R,V,U,B,Z$. They are closedness-preservation rows over the closedness classifier, and their replay routes are the displayed continuations in $C$.

The $\mathsf{GAP}$ row names the excluded crossings. It separates the finite closedness theorem surface from typing preservation, subject-reduction discharge, quotient equality, and host conversion.

Componentwise transport and provenance cannot create an omitted coordinate. Since $H,P,N$ only preserve and name the rows already projected from $S$, every public read remains inside the displayed finite packet.
\end{proof}
