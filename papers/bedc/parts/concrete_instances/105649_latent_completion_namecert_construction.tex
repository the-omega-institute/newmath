\begin{definition}[Latent completion carrier]
\label{def:latent-completion-carrier}
A $\LatentCompletionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
\mathsf{LatentCompletion}(O,E,L,D,M,K,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $O$ is the observed module-state ledger containing the sampled endpoints and their module-value rows.
\item $E$ is the exception ledger for the target-monotone failures represented by table identifiers $9$, $14$, and $28$.
\item $L$ is the latent-state row containing one deep-branch prefix and one partial-boundary prefix from \autoref{def:philosophy-latent-completion-states}.
\item $D$ is the directed rewrite-edge row for target-monotone one-module changes.
\item $M$ is the monotone-path coverage row verifying that every observed endpoint is reachable after the latent rows are admitted.
\item $K$ is the exception-kill row recording that the two latent rows cover the two independent missing squares and no third exception branch is created.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for observed states, latent states, rewrite edges, coverage rows, exception rows, replay, provenance, and local naming.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of a coverage request through $O,E,L,D,M,K$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LatentCompletionUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows, with the module-state source tied to \autoref{def:philosophy-latent-completion-states}.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LatentCompletionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $O,E,L,D,M,K,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for a continuous biological transition, a complete empirical state space, a graph oracle, unbounded latent search, or a module-state equality principle outside the displayed rows.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Latent completion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:latent-completion-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\LatentCompletionUp$ carrier $\mathsf{LatentCompletion}(O,E,L,D,M,K,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\LatentCompletionUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for observed module states, exception rows, latent-prefix rows, directed rewrite edges, monotone-path coverage, exception-kill accounting, componentwise transport, displayed replay, provenance, and local naming.

Every accepted coverage read must identify the two independent missing squares from $E$, consume the two latent rows in $L$, rebuild the directed rewrite graph through $D$, and then expose coverage through $M,K,H,C,P,N$. No obligation is stored in a host graph-completion oracle, a biological-transition claim, an unbounded latent search, or a complete empirical state space.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:latent-completion-carrier}. The tuple displays the observed ledger, exception ledger, latent rows, rewrite edges, coverage row, exception-kill row, and structural support.

The exception analysis is read from $E$ and $L$. The deep-branch exception rows require one deep prefix, and the partial-boundary exception row requires one partial-boundary prefix.

The coverage route is $D,M,K$. The directed rewrite row rebuilds target-monotone one-module steps, the coverage row records reachability of the observed endpoints, and the exception-kill row records that no third missing square remains.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite route. Since the carrier contains no graph oracle, biological transition row, unbounded search row, or complete empirical state-space row, the stated surface exhausts the NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Latent completion monotone-path coverage]
\label{thm:latent-completion-monotone-path-coverage}
Every accepted $\LatentCompletionUp$ packet covers the finite monotone-path exception geometry by routing each observed endpoint through the displayed rewrite graph after adjoining exactly one deep-branch latent prefix and exactly one partial-boundary latent prefix. The public coverage claim is the finite route
$$
O,E,L,D,M,K,H,C,P,N
$$
and it exports no stronger temporal, mechanistic, or empirical completeness claim.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the observed ledger $O$ and exception ledger $E$. They identify the endpoints and the three target-monotone failures that must be covered.

Use the latent row $L$. It supplies one deep-branch prefix for the table-$9$ and table-$14$ square and one partial-boundary prefix for the table-$28$ square.

Now apply the rewrite-edge and coverage rows $D,M$. These rows rebuild the finite directed graph and record reachability of every observed endpoint from the standard state.

The exception-kill and support rows $K,H,C,P,N$ preserve and name the same coverage route. Since no temporal mechanism or empirical-completeness coordinate is present, the exported conclusion is exactly monotone-path coverage of the finite module-state packet.
\end{proof}
