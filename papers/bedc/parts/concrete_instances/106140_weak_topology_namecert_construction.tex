\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\WeakTopologyUp$}

\origin{human}

Following Bourbaki and Kelley, the $\WeakTopologyUp$ packet records a weak topology only through finite windows of scalar linear-functional observations. It sits over the source carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-normedspace-namecert}, the functional-analysis surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-functionalanalysis-namecert}, the continuous-map rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuousmap-namecert}, the open-neighbourhood classifier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-topology-namecert}, and the scalar seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The chapter names the finite observation-window certificate used by weak-convergence consumers; it does not choose dual elements, assert Banach--Alaoglu, compactness, quotient function spaces, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[WeakTopology carrier]
\label{def:weak-topology-carrier}
A $\WeakTopologyUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
W=(V,F,U,T,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $V$ is the source vector or normed-space carrier, read through the $\NormedSpaceUp$ and $\FunctionalAnalysisUp$ sibling rows.
\item $F$ is the finite family of linear-functional observation rows. Each row names a scalar-valued functional already admitted by the functional-analysis surface, not a selected element of a completed dual space.
\item $U$ is the weak-neighbourhood classifier row. It records finite scalar constraints around a displayed source point and sends them to the $\TopologyUp$ open-neighbourhood surface.
\item $T$ is the finite test-window row. It lists the scalar probes, tolerances, source points, and comparison budgets inspected by a consumer.
\item $R$ is the $\RealUp$ scalar readback row for the observed functional values and their finite tolerance comparisons.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the source carrier, functional rows, neighbourhood classifier, test windows, scalar readbacks, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from source reads through finite functional observations, from observations to weak-neighbourhood rows, and from those rows to scalar readbacks.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\WeakTopologyUp$, $\NormedSpaceUp$, $\FunctionalAnalysisUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\TopologyUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\WeakTopologyUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $V,F,U,T,R,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for an unrestricted dual object, a selected family of all functionals, compactness, quotient function spaces, choice, or ambient real completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[WeakTopology NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:weak-topology-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\WeakTopologyUp$ carrier $W=(V,F,U,T,R,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\WeakTopologyUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the source vector or normed-space row $V$, finite functional-observation family $F$, weak-neighbourhood classifier $U$, finite test windows $T$, scalar readback row $R$, componentwise transport $H$, displayed replay $C$, provenance $P$, and local naming $N$. Every weak-neighbourhood read must cite the sibling routes named above before it reaches a public topology row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:weak-topology-carrier}. The only source-facing coordinate is $V$, the only functional-facing coordinate is the finite row $F$, and the only topology-facing coordinate is the neighbourhood classifier $U$.

The test-window row $T$ records which finitely many probes and tolerances are inspected. Scalar comparison then passes through $R$, so the public read receives finite $\RealUp$ observations rather than a selected dual object.

Finally transport, replay, provenance, and naming are supplied by $H,C,P,N$. Since the carrier contains no further coordinates, the listed rows exhaust the local certificate obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[WeakTopology functional-window stability]
\label{thm:weak-topology-functional-window-stability}
Let $W=(V,F,U,T,R,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\WeakTopologyUp$ carrier. Replacing a test request by a finite subwindow already recorded in $T$ preserves the same source carrier $V$, the same displayed functional family $F$, the same weak-neighbourhood classifier $U$, and the same scalar readback route $R$. The transport and replay rows $H,C$ carry the subwindow through the same provenance and local naming rows $P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the finite test-window clause of \autoref{def:weak-topology-carrier}. A subwindow of $T$ still names only scalar probes from $F$, source-point reads from $V$, and finite tolerance comparisons.

Replay through $C$. The subwindow reaches the weak-neighbourhood classifier $U$ and scalar row $R$ along the same displayed path, so it cannot add a new functional, a hidden dual element, or a host function-space quotient.

Transport through $H$ and repack through $P,N$. The packet therefore preserves the weak-topology observation surface while narrowing the inspected finite window.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[WeakTopology scalar observation nonescape]
\label{thm:weak-topology-scalar-observation-nonescape}
Every scalar observation exported by an accepted $\WeakTopologyUp$ carrier factors through
$$
V,F,T,U,R,H,C,P,N .
$$
Thus weak-convergence, neighbourhood, or continuity consumers may inspect only finite functional windows and their $\RealUp$ scalar comparisons. They receive no Banach--Alaoglu compactness claim, no selected dual element, no quotient function space, no countable-choice selector, and no ambient $\RealUp$ completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with an exported scalar observation. By \autoref{thm:weak-topology-namecert-obligations}, the observation must first expose the source row $V$, the finite functional family $F$, the test windows $T$, and the weak-neighbourhood classifier $U$.

The scalar row $R$ is then the only route to $\RealUp$ readback. It records finite observed values and tolerance comparisons, not a completed dual-space object or a host function-space equality.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve and name that same route. Since the excluded analytic principles have no carrier coordinate, they cannot be exported by the accepted packet.
\end{proof}
