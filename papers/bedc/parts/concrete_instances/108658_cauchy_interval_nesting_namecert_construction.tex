\begin{theorem}[Cauchy interval nesting RegSeqRat route]
\label{thm:cauchy-interval-nesting-regseqrat-route}
Every accepted $\CauchyIntervalNestingUp$ carrier reaches the $\RegSeqRatUp$ row only after reading the interval source, retained bisection branch, nested inclusion ledger, $\StreamNameUp$ finite windows, and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row. Equivalently, every regular rational readback factors through the displayed finite route
$$
I,B,N,S,D \longmapsto R \longmapsto E,H,C,P,L .
$$
The route is a Cauchy-facing finite readback route; it is not a selected infinite branch, a quotient real, a countable-choice schedule, or an ambient completeness argument.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier $X=(I,B,N,S,D,R,E,H,C,P,L)$ from \autoref{def:cauchy-interval-nesting-carrier}. The source row $I$ and branch row $B$ expose the only retained closed-interval bisection data available to the request.

Next read the nested ledger $N$, the stream windows $S$, and the dyadic tolerance row $D$. These rows supply the finite prefix, displayed observations, and tolerance controls needed before the regular rational row can be inspected.

The row $R$ is therefore downstream of $I,B,N,S,D$. The terminal seal and structural rows $E,H,C,P,L$ preserve the same order by real sealing, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming.

Since the carrier has no coordinate for completed branches, quotient equality, choice, trichotomy, compactness, or ambient completeness, no consumer can bypass the displayed route.
\end{proof}
