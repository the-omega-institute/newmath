\begin{theorem}[AuthorizedGeneratorRecursor root signature row totality]
\label{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-root-signature-row-totality}
For every accepted $\AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp$ packet, each public root
read exposes the entire row family
$$
I,E,M,B,D,O,A,H,C,P,G,N
$$
as the local $\BHist$ packet with componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed
$\Cont$ routes, and the local $\NameCert_{\AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp}$
surface. No public root read may expose only a partial signature, branch,
descent, audit, or output row while suppressing the remaining displayed rows.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp.AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorRootSignatureRowTotality}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of
\autoref{def:authorized-generator-recursor-carrier}. It names the finite
$\BHist$ packet $R=(I,E,M,B,D,O,A,H,C,P,G,N)$ and gives no coordinate for a
second root signature or a detached public route.

Use the local surface of
\autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-namecert-surface}. The
$\NameCert_{\AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp}$ row reads the accepted signature,
recursor name, motive, branch package, descent row, output name, audit
provenance, transports, routes, package provenance, boundary, and local naming
row as one public packet.

Branch and recursive-read coverage are supplied by
\autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-branch-exhaustion} and
\autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-structural-descent-locality}. The
audit and handoff side is supplied by
\autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-audit-provenance-factorization} and
\autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-ground-compiler-handoff}. Together
these results make every root read total over the displayed $\BHist$,
$\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[AuthorizedGeneratorRecursor root descent audit coupling]
\label{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-root-descent-audit-coupling}
For every accepted $\AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp$ packet, the structural
descent row $D$ and audit row $A$ must be transported together before the
output row $O$ is public. A public output exposure cannot retain $D$ while
dropping $A$, cannot retain $A$ while dropping $D$, and cannot route $O$ through
an audit surface detached from the accepted structural-descent row.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp.AuthorizedGeneratorRecursor\_root\_descent\_audit\_coupling}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier and the structural locality theorem. The displayed
packet of \autoref{def:authorized-generator-recursor-carrier} places $D$ in
the same tuple as $I,E,M,B,O,A,H,C,P,G,N$, while
\autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-structural-descent-locality}
confines public recursive reads to the accepted signature, eliminator, motive,
branch package, and descent row.

Next apply the audit boundary and audit-provenance factorization.
\autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-audit-boundary} requires public
output to pass through the accepted signature, checked motive, complete branch
package, structural descent, and audit provenance.
\autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-audit-provenance-factorization}
keeps $O$ attached to $A,H,C,P,G,N$ before exposure.

Finally use branch exhaustion and the TasteGate handoff. By
\autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-branch-exhaustion}, no branch read
can escape the accepted constructor list; by
\autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-ground-compiler-handoff}, the
checked alignment witness consumes only the same finite carrier. Hence the
descent row and audit row are coupled before $O$ becomes public.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[AuthorizedGeneratorRecursor root output NameCert saturation]
\label{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-root-output-namecert-saturation}
For every accepted $\AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp$ packet, public output
exposure is saturated by the local $\NameCert_{\AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp}$
surface over
$$
I,E,M,B,D,O,A,H,C,P,G,N.
$$
The exposure cannot add an unlisted constructor, an extra branch, a second
recursion channel, an axiom row, or a hidden generator outside the displayed
packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the carrier definition and the local NameCert surface. The carrier in
\autoref{def:authorized-generator-recursor-carrier} is exactly the displayed
tuple, and \autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-namecert-surface} makes
$O$ public only through that tuple.

Branch and descent saturation follow from
\autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-branch-exhaustion} and
\autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-structural-descent-locality}. These
results rule out an unlisted constructor, an extra branch, or a recursive read
whose descent is absent from $D$.

Audit saturation follows from
\autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-audit-boundary} and
\autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-audit-provenance-factorization}.
They keep $O$ behind $A,H,C,P,G,N$ and refuse an unaudited dependency channel.
The TasteGate handoff of
\autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-ground-compiler-handoff} consumes
only the same aligned carrier, so no axiom row or hidden generator can be
introduced by the output exposure.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp.AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorRootOutputNameCertSaturation}

\begin{theorem}[AuthorizedGeneratorRecursor root signature downstream coverage]
\label{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-root-signature-downstream-coverage}
For every accepted $\AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp$ packet, every accepted local consumer downstream of a public root signature read is exhausted by the finite root rows
$$
I,E,M,B,D,O,A,H,C,P,G,N.
$$
More precisely, such a consumer can only use the signature, eliminator, motive, branch, and descent alternatives $I,E,M,B,D$, the output and audit ledger rows $O,A,H,C,P,G,N$, or the coverage surface obtained by reading the displayed $\BHist$, componentwise $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and local $\NameCert_{\AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp}$ rows together. A request for an unlisted constructor, extra branch, second recursion channel, detached audit route, axiom row, hidden generator, or off-packet naming certificate remains outside the accepted $\BEDC$ packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Fix an accepted packet and a public root signature read. By \autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-root-signature-row-totality}, the read exposes the full row family
$$
I,E,M,B,D,O,A,H,C,P,G,N
$$
as one local $\BHist$ packet with componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ routes, and the local $\NameCert_{\AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp}$ surface. The same theorem rules out a partial public root read: no consumer can keep only a signature, branch, descent, audit, or output row while suppressing the remaining displayed rows.

The possible downstream uses therefore have only the displayed coordinates available. The root signature side supplies $I,E,M,B,D$ as the accepted signature, emitted eliminator, checked motive, branch package, and structural-descent alternatives. The output side supplies $O,A,H,C,P,G,N$ as the output, audit provenance, transport, continuation, package provenance, boundary, and local naming ledger. The coverage surface is not a further coordinate; it is the simultaneous exposure of the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows forced by the root signature totality theorem.

Apply \autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-root-output-namecert-saturation} to the output-facing part of the same packet. Public output exposure is saturated by the local $\NameCert_{\AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp}$ surface over exactly the displayed rows, and cannot add an unlisted constructor, an extra branch, a second recursion channel, an axiom row, or a hidden generator outside the packet. Combining this saturation with root signature row totality leaves no coordinate for a detached audit route or off-packet naming certificate either: any such request would require either a partial root read or an output exposure beyond the saturated local NameCert surface. Hence every accepted local downstream consumer is exhausted by the finite alternatives, ledger rows, and coverage rows displayed above, while every excluded alternative remains outside the accepted $\BEDC$ packet.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp.AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorRootSignatureDownstreamCoverage}
