\begin{theorem}[AuthorizedGeneratorRecursor boundary audit exhaustion]
\label{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-boundary-audit-exhaustion}
Every accepted $\AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp$ packet that exposes the boundary row $G$ must retain the audit row $A$, componentwise transport row $H$, displayed $\Cont$ row $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance row $P$, and local $\NameCert_{\AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp}$ row $N$ before any output row $O$ is public. The retained root-boundary packet is exactly the finite $\BHist$ surface

$$
I,E,M,B,D,O,A,H,C,P,G,N.
$$

Thus an output-facing consumer may reach $G$ only through the same audited carrier, not through a detached boundary witness or a public output name alone.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the carrier definition \autoref{def:authorized-generator-recursor-carrier}. It places $G$ in one finite $\BHist$ packet with $O,A,H,C,P$ and the local certificate row $N$, and it gives no separate coordinate for a boundary witness outside that packet.

Apply the local surface theorem \autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-namecert-surface}. It allows a downstream consumer to treat $O$ as recursor-generated only through the displayed packet, while \autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-branch-exhaustion} and \autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-structural-descent-locality} keep the signature, branch package, and descent rows attached to that read.

Finally use the root-unblock rows already available in this chapter. Theorems~\ref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-root-unblock-carrier-admission} and~\ref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-root-unblock-route-totality} force every output-facing route through $I,E,M,B,D,O,A,H,C,P,G,N$, and \autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-root-unblock-ledger-nonescape} excludes a hidden authorization route. Therefore the audit, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows are retained before $O$ becomes public.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp.AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorBoundaryAuditExhaustion}

\begin{theorem}[AuthorizedGeneratorRecursor recursion-channel refusal]
\label{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-recursion-channel-refusal}
Any attempted use of an accepted $\AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp$ packet as a non-structural recursion channel, missing-branch repair, detached substitution step, or hidden proof-producing generator is routed to the displayed boundary row $G$ and the audited rows $A,H,C,P,N$ rather than exported as a public output. In particular, the packet exports only the structural recursor route witnessed by $I,E,M,B,D,O,A,H,C,P,G,N$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp.AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorRecursionChannelRefusal}
\begin{proof}
Start with branch exhaustion and structural descent locality. Theorems~\ref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-branch-exhaustion} and~\ref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-structural-descent-locality} leave no public branch outside $B$ and no recursive call whose descent is absent from $D$.

Next read the carrier and NameCert surface. By \autoref{def:authorized-generator-recursor-carrier} and \autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-namecert-surface}, the boundary row $G$ is the displayed place where unlisted branches, non-structural recursion, external axioms, and hidden proof generators are refused.

Detached substitution is handled by the root-unblock packet rows. Theorems~\ref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-root-unblock-route-totality} and~\ref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-root-unblock-ledger-nonescape} force output-facing and ledger-facing reads to stay inside the same $\BHist$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows, so a substitution step cannot become an off-packet generator.

Finally apply boundary audit exhaustion and output provenance coverage above. They keep $A,H,C,P,G,N$ attached before $O$ is public. Therefore every rejected recursion channel is recorded at the displayed boundary rather than exported as a public proof-producing route.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[AuthorizedGeneratorRecursor boundary audit strict obstruction]
\label{cor:authorized-generator-recursor-boundary-audit-strict-obstruction}
For an accepted $\AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp$ packet, the obstruction recorded by the boundary-audit exhaustion and recursion-channel refusal is strictly local. It blocks only the attempted non-structural recursion channel, missing-branch repair, detached substitution step, or hidden proof-producing generator named in \autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-recursion-channel-refusal}. Its consumer-facing use must factor through the displayed packet

$$
I,E,M,B,D,O,A,H,C,P,G,N.
$$

Consequently the same obstruction cannot be reread as a global impossibility theorem, a host theorem, or an external validation route for any coordinate not displayed in that packet.
\end{corollary}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-boundary-audit-exhaustion}. It identifies the output-facing boundary audit with the finite $\BHist$ surface $I,E,M,B,D,O,A,H,C,P,G,N$ and says that a consumer may reach the boundary row $G$ only through the audited carrier retaining $A,H,C,P$ and the local $\NameCert_{\AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp}$ row $N$. Thus the boundary row is not a detached witness and is not available through the public output name alone.

Now apply \autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-recursion-channel-refusal}. The only failed uses routed to $G$ are the named attempts: non-structural recursion, missing-branch repair, detached substitution, and hidden proof production. The theorem sends those attempts back to the audited rows $A,H,C,P,N$ and states that the packet exports only the structural recursor route witnessed by the same displayed rows.

These two statements exhaust the consumer-facing cases. A listed failed route factors through $G$ together with $A,H,C,P,N$, so it is blocked as a local misuse of the accepted recursor packet. An unlisted use would require an additional public coordinate, such as a global impossibility certificate, host theorem row, or external validation row. The carrier displayed in \autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-boundary-audit-exhaustion} has no such coordinate, and \autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-recursion-channel-refusal} exports no route besides the structural recursor route. Therefore the obstruction is exactly the local obstruction stated above.
\end{proof}
