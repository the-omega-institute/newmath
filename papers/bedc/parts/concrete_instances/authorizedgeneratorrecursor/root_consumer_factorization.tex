Finally apply root boundary nonescape, \autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-root-boundary-nonescape}. The boundary row $G$ exports only the accepted recursor call over the finite packet, so the downstream consumer has no off-packet generator authority.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[AuthorizedGeneratorRecursor root consumer fact downstream coverage]
\label{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-root-consumer-fact-downstream-coverage}
For every accepted $\AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp$ root packet, every accepted local downstream consumer fact whose handoff is closed-normal or generator-audit is exhausted by the displayed finite surface
$$
(I,E,M,B,D,O,A,H,C,P,G,N).
$$
The constructor alternatives and recursive calls are covered by $I,E,M,B,D$; the output, audit, transport, continuation, provenance, boundary, and local naming ledgers are covered by $O,A,H,C,P,G,N$; and any remaining readiness demand is covered by root output audit totality, root boundary nonescape, root NameCert consumer readiness, and the obligation triad. \leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp.AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorRootConsumerFactDownstreamCoverage}

Thus the consumer has no independent read surface beyond the accepted signature, eliminator, motive, branch package, descent row, output name, audit row, transports, continuations, package provenance, boundary row, and local name.

Therefore every accepted local consumer fact is exhausted by the finite alternatives, ledgers, or coverage rows already displayed, while all excluded alternatives remain outside the BEDC packet.
\end{proof}
