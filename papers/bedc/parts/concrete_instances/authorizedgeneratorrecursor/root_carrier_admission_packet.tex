\begin{theorem}[AuthorizedGeneratorRecursor root carrier admission]
\label{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-root-carrier-admission}
Every accepted $\AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp$ root packet exposes only the carrier rows

$$
I,E,M,B,D,O,A,H,C,P,G,N
$$

before any root-unblock, ground-compiler, or output-facing consumer may read it. Here $I$ is the accepted signature, $E$ the emitted eliminator, $M$ the motive, $B$ the branch package, $D$ the structural descent row, $O$ the output row, $A$ the audit row, $H$ the componentwise transport row, $C$ the displayed continuation route, $P$ the package provenance, $G$ the boundary row, and $N$ the local $\NameCert$ row.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp.AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorRootCarrierAdmission}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:authorized-generator-recursor-carrier}. Its displayed $\BHist$ packet is exactly $I,E,M,B,D,O,A,H,C,P,G,N$, and the definition gives no coordinate for an off-packet generator, hidden branch source, or detached output name.

Branch and descent exposure are already exhausted by the local root obligations. Theorems~\ref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-branch-exhaustion} and~\ref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-structural-descent-locality} keep every public recursive read on the signature, emitted eliminator, motive, branch package, and structural descent rows.

Finally attach audit and handoff data. The audit boundary of \autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-audit-boundary} keeps output exposure behind audit provenance, and \autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-ground-compiler-handoff} reads the checked carrier alignment only as the same finite packet. Hence the accepted root carrier exposes no rows beyond the displayed list.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[AuthorizedGeneratorRecursor ground-compiler route totality]
\label{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-ground-compiler-route-totality}
Every ground-compiler or TasteGate-facing route out of an accepted $\AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp$ packet factors through the signature row $I$, eliminator row $E$, motive row $M$, branch row $B$, descent row $D$, audit row $A$, continuation row $C$, package row $P$, boundary row $G$, and local certificate row $N$, together with the output row $O$ and componentwise transport row $H$ of the same carrier.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp.AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorGroundCompilerRouteTotality}
\begin{proof}
Start from root carrier admission above. The ground-compiler route begins with the same finite packet $I,E,M,B,D,O,A,H,C,P,G,N$ and has no separate compiler-facing carrier.

The computational part is forced through the signature, eliminator, motive, branch, and descent rows. Apply \autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-branch-exhaustion} and \autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-structural-descent-locality}; together they rule out an unlisted constructor case, detached branch table, or non-structural recursion channel.

The public handoff is then routed through audit, continuation, package, boundary, and naming rows. Theorems~\ref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-audit-boundary} and~\ref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-ground-compiler-handoff} place the TasteGate-facing read behind the finite NameCert packet rather than behind a separate proof-producing generator. Thus every ground-compiler route is total over the displayed rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[AuthorizedGeneratorRecursor ledger provenance closure]
\label{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-ledger-provenance-closure}
For every accepted $\AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp$ packet, the audit ledger and output provenance close inside $A,H,C,P,G,N$ before any $\ClosedTermSubstitutionBoundaryUp$, $\MetaCICNormalizationFrontierUp$, or $\OperationalMembraneUp$ consumer reads $O$. Such a consumer receives the output only as a row of the finite packet $I,E,M,B,D,O,A,H,C,P,G,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the root carrier admission theorem. It places the output row $O$ inside the admitted carrier and keeps the audit, transport, continuation, provenance, boundary, and local certificate rows attached to that output.

Next use the branch and descent obligations. Branch exhaustion and structural-descent locality keep the recursive source of $O$ on $I,E,M,B,D$, so a downstream consumer cannot replace the output by a detached generator or hidden substitution step.

Finally apply audit boundary and ground-compiler handoff. The audit ledger is closed by \autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-audit-boundary}, while \autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-ground-compiler-handoff} confines the compiler-facing handoff to the same NameCert carrier. Therefore closed-substitution, normalization-frontier, and operational-membrane consumers read only the closed provenance packet, not an off-route output.
\end{proof}
