\begin{theorem}[AuthorizedGeneratorRecursor L10 source triad]
\label{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-l10-open-phase-source-triad}
Let
$$
R=(I,E,M,B,D,O,A,H,C,P,G,N)
$$
be an accepted $\AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp$ packet. Any L10-facing finite-source consumer for $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, or $\RealUp$ may read the packet only through the displayed rows $I,E,M,B,D,O,A,H,C,P,G,N$: signature admission in $I$, emitted recursor naming in $E$, motive checking in $M$, branch coverage in $B$, structural descent in $D$, audit provenance in $A$, boundary refusal in $G$, continuation locality in $C$, package provenance in $P$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport in $H$, and the local $\NameCert_{\AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp}$ row $N$. No L10 source route may read the packet as a host choice, a detached induction rule, a selected real witness, a quotient stream, or an external proof producer.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp.AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorL10OpenPhaseSourceTriad}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:authorized-generator-recursor-carrier}. The displayed packet lists exactly the signature, eliminator, motive, branch, descent, output, audit, transport, continuation, provenance, boundary, and local naming rows available to a consumer.

Use branch exhaustion, \autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-branch-exhaustion}, together with structural descent locality, \autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-structural-descent-locality}. These keep every recursive read on the accepted constructor signature, complete branch package, and displayed descent row before any finite-source route can use the output name.

Attach the output and audit locks. Theorems~\ref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-output-route-determinacy}, \ref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-boundary-audit-exhaustion}, and \ref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-root-output-totality-lock} keep $O$ with $A,H,C,P,G,N$ on the same finite route.

The $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ consumers therefore receive only a $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ route over the displayed packet. The carrier has no coordinate for host choice, detached induction, selected real data, quotient stream data, or an external proof producer.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[AuthorizedGeneratorRecursor consumer handoff]
\label{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-open-phase-consumer-handoff}
Every downstream $\RealUp$ or completion-facing consumer of an accepted $\AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp$ packet must preserve the output row $O$ together with $A,H,C,P,G,N$. The consumer may specialize the packet to a finite real-source or completion-source route, but it cannot reinterpret the recursor packet as a host choice principle, hidden induction principle, selected limit, quotient stream, or external proof-producing channel.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp.AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorOpenPhaseConsumerHandoff}
\begin{proof}
Start with the L10 source triad above. It gives downstream consumers access to $O$ only through the same finite packet rows, with the audit, transport, continuation, provenance, boundary, and local naming rows still attached.

Read the existing downstream locks. Theorems~\ref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-output-route-determinacy}, \ref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-closed-substitution-consumer-lock}, and \ref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-normalization-frontier-consumer-lock} treat downstream consumers as readers of the authorized output route rather than as producers of a second generator result.

Now specialize the route to real and completion consumers. A $\RealUp$ or completion-facing route may consume finite $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ evidence, but the row $O$ remains attached to $A,H,C,P,G,N$ by output provenance coverage and root-output totality.

The excluded interpretations would require a separate host choice coordinate, hidden induction row, selected-limit witness, quotient-stream equality, or proof-producing channel. None is present in the carrier, and the boundary row $G$ records their refusal.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[AuthorizedGeneratorRecursor ledger non-escape]
\label{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-open-phase-ledger-nonescape}
For L10-facing use of an accepted $\AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp$ packet, the audit row $A$ and boundary row $G$ exhaust the ledger exported to consumers. Every consumer route is a finite $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ route over
$$
I,E,M,B,D,O,A,H,C,P,G,N.
$$
No route exports a completion object, a real-completeness datum, a quotient completion equality, a selected limit, or a host proof of completeness.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp.AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorOpenPhaseLedgerNonescape}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier and retain the audit and boundary rows. The definition of the carrier places $A$ and $G$ in the same finite packet as the branch, descent, output, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.

Use the local boundary results. Theorems~\ref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-audit-boundary}, \ref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-boundary-audit-exhaustion}, and \ref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-recursion-channel-refusal} route every rejected generator, recursion, or dependency request to the displayed boundary and audit rows.

Next use root-output totality and output provenance coverage. They keep L10-facing reads inside the same $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ surface before the output row becomes public.

Thus the ledger exported to $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, and completion-facing consumers is finite and local to the packet. Completion objects, real-completeness data, quotient completion equality, selected limits, and host completeness proofs are not rows of $R$, so they cannot escape from the ledger.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[AuthorizedGeneratorRecursor L10 source-triad strict obstruction]
\label{cor:authorized-generator-recursor-l10-source-triad-strict-obstruction}
Let
$$
R=(I,E,M,B,D,O,A,H,C,P,G,N)
$$
be an accepted $\AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp$ packet, and let a $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, or completion-facing L10 consumer display a failure, refusal, separation, or countermodel row against a named converse or reuse route for the source triad. Then that row is a strict local obstruction: it blocks only the named route whose consumer-facing read factors through the displayed finite route, transport, and readback rows
$$
I,E,M,B,D,O,A,H,C,P,G,N
$$
with $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ access. It cannot be reinterpreted as a global impossibility theorem, a host theorem about recursors or real completion, hidden external validation of the failed route, a selected real witness, a quotient stream equality, a completion object, or a proof-producing channel outside the accepted packet.
\end{corollary}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp.AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorL10SourceTriadStrictObstruction}
\begin{proof}
Start with the L10 source triad in \autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-l10-open-phase-source-triad}. It identifies the only rows available to an L10-facing finite-source consumer: signature admission $I$, emitted recursor naming $E$, motive checking $M$, branch coverage $B$, structural descent $D$, output naming $O$, audit provenance $A$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport $H$, continuation locality $C$, package provenance $P$, boundary refusal $G$, and the local $\NameCert_{\AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp}$ row $N$. The same theorem excludes host choice, detached induction, selected real data, quotient stream data, and external proof production as possible source reads.

Now apply ledger non-escape, \autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-open-phase-ledger-nonescape}. For L10-facing use, the exported ledger is exhausted by the audit row $A$ and boundary row $G$, and every consumer route is a finite $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ route over the displayed packet. The ledger exports no completion object, real-completeness datum, quotient completion equality, selected limit, or host proof of completeness.

Consider the displayed failure, refusal, separation, or countermodel row. If it is used by the named consumer against the authorized source triad, the two cited results force that use to pass through exactly the displayed packet rows and their $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ readback surface. Thus the row can obstruct only the advertised converse or reuse route at the packet coordinate where that route asks to read, replace, or bypass one of those rows.

Any stronger interpretation asks for an unlisted coordinate. A global impossibility theorem would require a theorem surface beyond the finite packet; a host theorem would require host choice, detached induction, or a host completeness proof; hidden external validation would require an external certificate route; a selected real witness, quotient stream equality, completion object, or proof-producing channel would require one of the objects that the triad and the ledger explicitly refuse. Since none of those coordinates is exported by $R$, the obstruction remains strict and local to the named L10 source-triad route.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp.AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorL10SourceTriadStrictObstruction}
