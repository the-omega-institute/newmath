\begin{theorem}[AuthorizedGeneratorRecursor output-route determinacy]
\label{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-output-route-determinacy}
For every accepted $\AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp$ packet
$$
R=(I,E,M,B,D,O,A,H,C,P,G,N),
$$
any accepted output read of $O$ is determined by the displayed signature row $I$, eliminator row $E$, motive row $M$, branch row $B$, descent row $D$, audit row $A$, route row $C$, package row $P$, boundary row $G$, and local $\NameCert_{\AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp}$ row $N$. There is no accepted output route for $O$ detached from those rows, and no downstream consumer may recover a second output meaning from the packet.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp.AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorCarrier\_output\_route\_determinacy}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the branch and descent rows. Theorems~\ref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-branch-exhaustion} and~\ref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-structural-descent-locality} force the output route to keep the accepted constructor signature, emitted eliminator, checked motive, complete branch package, and displayed structural descent.

Next attach audit and package provenance. Theorem~\ref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-audit-provenance-factorization} retains $A,H,C,P,G,N$ before $O$ is exposed, while \autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-ground-compiler-handoff} identifies the TasteGate handoff only as carrier alignment rather than as another generator route.

Finally apply admission exhaustion, \autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-admission-exhaustion}. It exhausts accepted output reads by the finite packet $R$ and its readiness view. Since neither view has a coordinate for a detached output channel, the route to $O$ is determined by the displayed rows.
\end{proof}

\label{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-closed-substitution-consumer-lock}

$$

$$
and cannot reinterpret closed substitution as an off-packet generator result, an unrestricted substitution calculus, or a proof-producing branch outside the authorized recursor packet.

\begin{proof}
Project the output route determinacy theorem. It gives the closed-substitution consumer the output row only as part of the accepted signature, eliminator, motive, branch, descent, audit, route, package, boundary, and local naming rows.

Read the closed-substitution chapters as consumers, not as producers of the recursor output. Their boundary surface can specialize finite $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ data, but it receives no coordinate from which to manufacture a generator result outside $R$.

The exclusion follows by row projection. If a consumer tries to interpret substitution as an off-packet generator result, the interpretation would need a second output route or branch source. \end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[AuthorizedGeneratorRecursor output-route strict local obstruction]
\label{cor:authorized-generator-recursor-output-route-strict-local-obstruction}
Let an accepted $\AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp$ packet
$$
R=(I,E,M,B,D,O,A,H,C,P,G,N)
$$
be consumed by a normalization-frontier or recursor-generator downstream route as in \autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-normalization-frontier-consumer-lock}. If that consumer displays a failure, refusal, separation, or countermodel row against a converse output reading, a reuse of the recursor output, or a second generator interpretation, then the displayed row obstructs only that named consumer-facing route through
$$
I,E,M,B,D,O,A,H,C,P,G,N.
$$
It cannot be reinterpreted as a global impossibility theorem, a host theorem about all recursors, a hidden external validation of the failed route, or a second meaning of $O$ outside the authorized packet.
\end{corollary}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-output-route-determinacy} first. Any accepted read of $O$ is determined by the displayed signature, eliminator, motive, branch, descent, audit, route, package, boundary, and local $\NameCert_{\AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp}$ rows. In particular, there is no accepted output route detached from these rows and no downstream consumer may recover a second output meaning from the packet.

Now pass to the normalization-frontier and recursor-generator consumers. By \autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-normalization-frontier-consumer-lock}, every such route consumes $O$ only through the same finite packet and cannot add a second generator, hidden induction principle, or proof-producing branch source beyond the displayed rows.

Consider a displayed failure, refusal, separation, or countermodel row in such a consumer. For the row to bear on the authorized output, the two cited theorems force its consumer-facing use to factor through the named packet coordinates. Therefore the row can block the advertised converse reading, reuse route, or second-generator interpretation only at those coordinates. Reading it as a global impossibility would require a theorem coordinate outside the packet; reading it as a host theorem would require a host-facing induction or proof row outside the packet; reading it as hidden external validation would require an external certificate route outside the packet. The determinacy theorem and the consumer lock exclude all three additional coordinates, so the obstruction remains strict and local to the named route.
\end{proof}
