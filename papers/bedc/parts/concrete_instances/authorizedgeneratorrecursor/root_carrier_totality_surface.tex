\begin{theorem}[AuthorizedGeneratorRecursor root carrier totality]
\label{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-root-carrier-totality}
Every accepted $\AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp$ readiness route exposes its root carrier only through the finite $\BHist$ rows
$$
I,E,M,B,D,O,A,H,C,P,G,N
$$
and through the repackaged readiness rows
$$
U,J,K,O,L,H,C,P,N.
$$
The accepted carrier, generator authorization row, boundary row, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert_{\AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp}$ row are therefore read together. No hidden generator, external axiom, host induction principle, detached branch table, unchecked recursive call, or off-packet substitution source is a carrier coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp.AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorRootCarrierTotality}
\begin{proof}
Start with root carrier admission. By \autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-root-carrier-admission}, the admitted packet is exactly the finite route through $I,E,M,B,D,O,A,H,C,P,G,N$ before any compiler, normalization, or closed-substitution consumer may read the output.

Next use the readiness route definition. \autoref{def:authorized-generator-recursor-readiness-route} repackages the signature, emitted eliminator, motive, branch, descent, output, audit, boundary, transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows as $U,J,K,O,L,H,C,P,N$ without adding a further carrier source.

Finally apply NameCert obligation exhaustion, \autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-namecert-obligation-exhaustion}. The carrier, classifier, stability, refusal-ledger, and non-escape clauses are all discharged by the displayed rows, so any route omitting one of them remains outside the accepted root carrier.
\end{proof}

Finally use output refusal and closed-substitution admission, \autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-output-refusal} and \autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-closed-substitution-admission}. \begin{theorem}[AuthorizedGeneratorRecursor terminal output non-escape]
\label{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-terminal-output-nonescape}

\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp.AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorTerminalOutputNonescape}
\begin{proof}

Now attach public output coverage. \autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-public-output-coverage} routes normalization, closed-substitution, and ground-compiler public consumers through the same readiness packet before any of them reads $O$.

Finally combine terminal authorized output cover with root output route saturation, \autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-terminal-authorized-output-cover} and \autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-root-output-route-saturation}. These theorems keep terminal output consumption on the displayed audit, boundary, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows, leaving no carrier coordinate for an escaping output route.
\end{proof}
