\section{AuthorizedGeneratorRecursor displayed route surface}
\label{sec:authorized-generator-recursor-displayed-route-surface}

\begin{definition}[AuthorizedGeneratorRecursor displayed route]
\label{def:authorized-generator-recursor-displayed-route}
An $\AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp$ displayed route is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
R=(A,G,F,K,P,N)
$$
whose rows are:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$, the authorization row for the accepted generator signature;
\item $G$, the generator row naming the generator whose recursor is being read;
\item $F$, the recursion-fuel row bounding the displayed recursive descent;
\item $K$, the continuation route that exposes each branch, recursive call, and output row through displayed $\Cont$ data;
\item $P$, the package provenance over the accepted signature, generator row, fuel row, branch package, output route, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$, the local $\NameCert_{\AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp}$ row for this displayed route.
\end{enumerate}
The route has no coordinate for an unrecorded host recursor, an unbounded recursion principle, an extra generator branch, or a detached output channel.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[AuthorizedGeneratorRecursor ledger exhaustion]
\label{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-ledger-exhaustion}
Every consumer read of an accepted $\AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp$ displayed route factors through the authorization row $A$, generator row $G$, recursion-fuel row $F$, continuation route $K$, package provenance $P$, and local naming row $N$. No consumer can import an unrecorded host recursor, bypass the displayed fuel row, or read a generator output detached from these rows.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp.AuthorizedGeneratorRecursor\_ledger\_exhaustion}
\begin{proof}
Project the displayed route of \autoref{def:authorized-generator-recursor-displayed-route}. Its only public coordinates are $A,G,F,K,P,N$, and the route contains no second recursor channel.

The authorization and generator rows must be read first. They identify the accepted generator signature and the generator whose recursor output is public, so a consumer that lacks either row has no accepted root route to consume.

The recursion-fuel and continuation rows then exhaust recursive access. Every branch, recursive call, and output exposure is routed through $F$ and $K$, with provenance and local naming supplied by $P,N$.

Therefore an unrecorded host recursor, an unbounded recursion principle, or a detached output channel would require a coordinate outside $R$, and no such coordinate is present.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp.AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorDisplayedRoute\_ledger\_exhaustion}

\begin{theorem}[AuthorizedGeneratorRecursor NameCert route]
\label{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-namecert-route}
For an accepted $\AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp$ displayed route, the local $\NameCert$ obligation surface consists exactly of carrier exposure for $R=(A,G,F,K,P,N)$, classifier transport of these six rows by the displayed $\hsame$ data, route determinacy through $K$, ledger exhaustion through \autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-ledger-exhaustion}, and non-escape from unrecorded host recursors or detached generator outputs.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier exposure is the finite route packet of \autoref{def:authorized-generator-recursor-displayed-route}. It displays the authorization, generator, fuel, continuation, provenance, and naming rows as one $\BHist$ surface.

Classifier transport is componentwise: an accepted comparison preserves $A,G,F,K,P,N$ through the stored $\hsame$ rows and replays the same public reads through the displayed $\Cont$ route $K$.

Route determinacy follows because $K$ is the only continuation coordinate. Once $A,G,F,P,N$ are fixed, every branch, recursive call, and output read must pass through that route.

Ledger exhaustion and non-escape are exactly the content of \autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-ledger-exhaustion}. The route therefore packages the carrier, classifier, deterministic continuation, exhaustive ledger, and refusal of unrecorded host recursors into the local $\NameCert$ surface.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp.AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorNameCertRoute}

\begin{lemma}[AuthorizedGeneratorRecursor displayed route downstream coverage]
\label{lem:authorized-generator-recursor-displayed-route-downstream-coverage}
Let $c$ be an accepted local consumer of an $\AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp$ displayed route. Then the consumer read is exhausted by the finite displayed-route packet
$$
R=(A,G,F,K,P,N)
$$
of \autoref{def:authorized-generator-recursor-displayed-route}. More precisely, $c$ factors through one of the following local alternatives:
\begin{enumerate}
\item the authorization row $A$ for the accepted generator signature;
\item the generator row $G$ naming the generator whose recursor is being read;
\item the recursion-fuel row $F$ bounding the displayed recursive descent;
\item the continuation route $K$ exposing each branch, recursive call, and output row through displayed $\Cont$ data;
\item the package provenance row $P$ over the accepted signature, generator row, fuel row, branch package, output route, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item the local $\NameCert_{\AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp}$ row $N$ for the displayed route.
\end{enumerate}
Consequently every request for an unrecorded host recursor, an unbounded recursion principle, an extra generator branch, or a detached output channel has no accepted coordinate in the $\AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp$ displayed-route packet.
\end{lemma}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp.AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorDisplayedRoute\_downstream\_coverage}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted local consumer read of the displayed route. By \autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-ledger-exhaustion}, every consumer read of an accepted $\AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp$ displayed route factors through the authorization row $A$, generator row $G$, recursion-fuel row $F$, continuation route $K$, package provenance row $P$, and local naming row $N$.

Project those rows through \autoref{def:authorized-generator-recursor-displayed-route}. The displayed route is the finite $\BHist$ packet $R=(A,G,F,K,P,N)$, and the definition assigns each public coordinate a fixed role: $A$ admits the accepted generator signature, $G$ names the generator whose recursor is public, $F$ bounds the recursive descent, $K$ carries the displayed continuation data for branches, recursive calls, and output rows, $P$ records the package provenance for the route data, and $N$ supplies the local $\NameCert_{\AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp}$ row. Thus the factorization supplied by ledger exhaustion lands in exactly the six alternatives listed in the statement.

It remains to exclude requests not listed among those alternatives. An unrecorded host recursor would require a second recursor coordinate, but \autoref{def:authorized-generator-recursor-displayed-route} states that the route has no coordinate for one and \autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-ledger-exhaustion} blocks importing one through a consumer read. An unbounded recursion principle would have to bypass $F$, while ledger exhaustion requires every recursive access to factor through the displayed fuel row. An extra generator branch would require a branch coordinate outside the continuation route $K$, but the displayed route exposes branches only through $K$. A detached output channel would require an output read not attached to $A,G,F,K,P,N$, while ledger exhaustion states that no consumer can read such an output. Hence every accepted local consumer is covered by the displayed finite rows, and every unlisted alternative remains outside the BEDC packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[AuthorizedGeneratorRecursor public route]
\label{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-public-route}
For an accepted $\AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp$ displayed route
$$
R=(A,G,F,K,P,N),
$$
the public finite export surface for generator-recursor consumers is exactly the displayed route of \autoref{def:authorized-generator-recursor-displayed-route}, the ledger exhaustion of \autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-ledger-exhaustion}, the NameCert route of \autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-namecert-route}, and the downstream coverage of \autoref{lem:authorized-generator-recursor-displayed-route-downstream-coverage}. A consumer may read authorization, generator identity, recursion fuel, continuation data, provenance, and local naming from this surface, but it receives no unrecorded host recursor, unbounded recursion principle, extra generator branch, or detached output channel.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp.AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorPublicRoute}
\begin{proof}
First expose the displayed route. The definition fixes the six public rows $A,G,F,K,P,N$ and rules out any second recursor coordinate.

Next apply ledger exhaustion. Every consumer read factors through those six rows, with recursive access routed through the fuel and continuation rows.

Apply the NameCert route to package carrier exposure, classifier transport, route determinacy, ledger exhaustion, and non-escape as the local certificate surface.

Finally apply downstream coverage. It identifies the accepted local consumer alternatives with the same six rows and excludes every request outside them, so the public export surface is exactly the displayed route together with its ledger and NameCert route.
\end{proof}
