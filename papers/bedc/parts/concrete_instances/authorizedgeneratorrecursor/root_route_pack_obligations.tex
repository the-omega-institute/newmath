\begin{theorem}[AuthorizedGeneratorRecursor route-pack exactness]
\label{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-route-pack-exactness}
For every accepted $\AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp$ packet
$$
R=(I,E,M,B,D,O,A,H,C,P,G,N),
$$
every accepted recursor route factors through the finite packet rows displayed
in \autoref{def:authorized-generator-recursor-carrier}. The route may expose
the accepted constructor signature, emitted eliminator, checked motive, branch
package, structural descent, output name, audit provenance, componentwise
$\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ route, $\Pkg$ provenance, $\mathsf{GAP}$
boundary, and local $\NameCert$ row, but it cannot add a row outside that
packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of
\autoref{def:authorized-generator-recursor-carrier}. The displayed tuple is the
whole route package, and each public recursor read is one of the listed rows.

Use the local NameCert surface of
\autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-namecert-surface}. It identifies the
same rows as the naming certificate for the authorized recursor output and
requires a consumer to read $O$ only through the packet.

Finally apply the audit and boundary results. The audit boundary of
\autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-audit-boundary} and the kernel
acceptance scope of
\autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-kernel-acceptance-scope} keep the
host-kernel handoff at $G$ on the displayed tuple. Since no carrier coordinate
exists beyond $I,E,M,B,D,O,A,H,C,P,G,N$, route-pack exactness follows.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp.AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorRoutePackExactness}

\begin{theorem}[AuthorizedGeneratorRecursor branch-budget exhaustion]
\label{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-branch-budget-exhaustion}
For every accepted $\AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp$ packet, the branch package
$B$ and structural-descent row $D$ exhaust every public branch read. A public
consumer may read only constructor branches listed by $I$ and authorized
through $E,M,B,D$; it cannot import an unlisted constructor branch, a detached
recursive call, or a second branch budget outside the displayed carrier.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp.AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorBranchBudgetExhaustion}
\begin{proof}
Start with branch exhaustion from
\autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-branch-exhaustion}. The row $B$
contains exactly the accepted branch surface for the constructors displayed by
$I$, and downstream access reaches it only through the displayed continuation
route.

Add structural locality from
\autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-structural-descent-locality} and
\autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-branch-descent-totality}. These rows
bind public recursive reads to the checked motive and structural-descent row,
so every recursive branch read remains inside $E,M,B,D$.

The refusal side is supplied by
\autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-hidden-generator-exclusion} and
\autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-gap-boundary-refusal}. The carrier
has no coordinate for an imported constructor branch, non-structural recursion
channel, or hidden proof generator, so the displayed branch budget is
exhausted.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[AuthorizedGeneratorRecursor consumer ledger cover]
\label{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-consumer-ledger-cover}
Every downstream $\RecursorGeneratorUp$,
$\ClosedTermSubstitutionBoundaryUp$, and $\TasteGate$ consumer of an
accepted $\AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp$ packet receives only the carrier,
branch, descent, audit, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, $\mathsf{GAP}$, and local
$\NameCert$ rows displayed by
$$
(I,E,M,B,D,O,A,H,C,P,G,N).
$$
No such consumer receives an extra generator row, substitution authority,
unlisted branch, unaudited dependency channel, or naming certificate outside
the packet.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp.AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorConsumerLedgerCover}
\begin{proof}
The carrier side is fixed by
\autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-route-pack-exactness}. It makes the
finite tuple the exact route package available to any consumer.

The branch and descent side is fixed by
\autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-branch-budget-exhaustion}. Thus a
$\RecursorGeneratorUp$ consumer can read only the accepted signature,
eliminator, motive, branch package, and structural-descent rows already
present in the packet.

The audit and ledger side is fixed by
\autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-root-namecert-consumer-readiness}
and \autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-obligation-triad}. They attach
the output, audit row, transports, continuation routes, package provenance,
boundary row, and local certificate before downstream closed-normal or
generator-audit consumers can cite the surface.

Finally the TasteGate handoff of
\autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-ground-compiler-handoff} reads only
the same single carrier alignment witness. Since the ledger has no coordinate
for substitution authority, an extra generator, or an outside naming
certificate, the listed consumers receive exactly the displayed rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[AuthorizedGeneratorRecursor route-pack downstream coverage]
\label{lem:authorized-generator-recursor-route-pack-downstream-coverage}
Let $c$ be an accepted local consumer of an accepted $\AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp$ packet with displayed route pack
$$
R=(I,E,M,B,D,O,A,H,C,P,G,N).
$$
Then the read made by $c$ is exhausted by the finite alternatives already displayed in the packet. Its branch-facing part is one of the constructor, eliminator, motive, branch-package, or structural-descent rows $I,E,M,B,D$; its output-facing ledger part is one of $O,A,H,C,P,G,N$; and its consumer-facing coverage row is one of the carrier, branch, descent, audit, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, $\mathsf{GAP}$, or local $\NameCert$ rows enumerated for the packet. Consequently an unlisted constructor alternative, detached recursive call, second branch budget, extra generator row, off-packet substitution authority, unaudited dependency channel, or outside naming certificate remains outside the BEDC packet and has no accepted local consumer route.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Fix an accepted local consumer $c$ of the displayed packet. By \autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-route-pack-exactness}, every accepted recursor route factors through the finite carrier of \autoref{def:authorized-generator-recursor-carrier}. Thus the consumer-facing route of $c$ has no coordinate except the displayed rows $I,E,M,B,D,O,A,H,C,P,G,N$.

Apply \autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-branch-budget-exhaustion} to the branch-facing part of the read. Any public branch read must use only the constructor branches listed by $I$ and authorized through $E,M,B,D$. Hence the alternatives available to $c$ on the branch side are exactly the displayed constructor, eliminator, motive, branch-package, and structural-descent rows, and there is no accepted coordinate for an unlisted constructor branch, detached recursive call, or second branch budget.

For the downstream ledger side, use \autoref{thm:authorized-generator-recursor-consumer-ledger-cover}. It confines every downstream $\RecursorGeneratorUp$, $\ClosedTermSubstitutionBoundaryUp$, and $\TasteGate$ consumer to the carrier, branch, descent, audit, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, $\mathsf{GAP}$, and local $\NameCert$ rows displayed by the same tuple. Therefore the output-facing part of $c$ can reach only $O,A,H,C,P,G,N$ after the admitted branch and descent rows have been read.

Combining the route exactness, branch-budget exhaustion, and consumer-ledger cover leaves no remaining packet coordinate from which $c$ could obtain an extra generator row, off-packet substitution authority, unaudited dependency channel, or outside naming certificate. All excluded alternatives are therefore refused by absence of a displayed BEDC row, and every accepted local consumer read is exhausted by the finite route pack.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorUp.AuthorizedGeneratorRecursorRoutePackDownstreamCoverage}
