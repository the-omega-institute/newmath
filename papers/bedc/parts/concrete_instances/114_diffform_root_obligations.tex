\begin{theorem}[Differential-form root source obligation]
\label{thm:diffform-root-source-obligation}
The root $\DiffFormUp$ chapter reads every displayed differential-form row
only from $BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$,
$\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$, and $\NameCert_{\TensorProductUp}$, as witnessed by
the carrier of \autoref{def:diffform-bhist-form-carrier}. In particular, the
root surface has no source row outside the six displayed entries
$$
(d,\mathcal{P},\tau,\sigma,\alpha,\lambda).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the root chapter through the carrier definition. The row
$(d,\mathcal{P},\tau,\sigma,\alpha,\lambda)$ displays the degree history, the
probe bundle, the tensor row, the scalar endpoint, the antisymmetry ledger,
and the source ledger.

Each coordinate is sourced by one of the named BEDC rows. The degree,
endpoint, antisymmetry, and provenance entries are $BHist$ rows; comparison
and transport use $\hsame$; tensor concatenation uses $\Cont$; probe exposure
uses $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$; and the only upstream certificates are
$\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\TensorProductUp}$.

No clause of \autoref{def:diffform-bhist-form-carrier} allocates another
source. Therefore the root chapter may be scanned as exactly this displayed
source surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Differential-form root carrier and classifier obligation]
\label{thm:diffform-root-carrier-classifier-obligation}
The carrier and classifier obligations visible at the $\DiffFormUp$ root are
exactly the carrier of \autoref{def:diffform-bhist-form-carrier}, the
classifier of \autoref{def:diffform-bhist-form-classifier}, carrier transport
of \autoref{thm:diffform-carrier-hsame-transport}, and classifier stability
of \autoref{thm:diffform-form-classifier-stability}. No further root
classifier or carrier transport principle is exposed.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier obligation is the accepted BHist row of
\autoref{def:diffform-bhist-form-carrier}. Its six displayed coordinates give
the complete carrier data used by all later wedge and boundary rows.

The classifier obligation is the comparison of
\autoref{def:diffform-bhist-form-classifier}. It compares degrees by
$\hsame$, matched tensor and ledger rows by visible BHist comparisons, and
scalar endpoints by the retained scalar classifier from the upstream sources.

The two stability obligations are precisely the transport theorem
\autoref{thm:diffform-carrier-hsame-transport} and the classifier theorem
\autoref{thm:diffform-form-classifier-stability}. Those results repack the
same displayed rows after componentwise $\hsame$ transport, so the root
obligation set contains no additional carrier or classifier field.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Differential-form root wedge ledger obligation]
\label{thm:diffform-root-wedge-ledger-obligation}
The wedge surface visible at the $\DiffFormUp$ root is exhausted by wedge
ledger coverage in \autoref{thm:diffform-wedge-ledger-coverage}, the
probe-concatenation ledger of
\autoref{def:diffform-wedge-probe-concatenation-ledger}, the wedge-degree
ledger of \autoref{def:diffform-wedge-degree-ledger}, and the associator
ledger of \autoref{def:diffform-wedge-associator-ledger}. All four ledgers
are rows over the displayed BEDC carrier and classifier surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an admitted wedge row. By
\autoref{thm:diffform-wedge-ledger-coverage}, admission requires a visible
$\TensorProductUp$ concatenation row joining the tensor components of the two
carried inputs.

The output probe data are then bounded by
\autoref{def:diffform-wedge-probe-concatenation-ledger}; its coverage theorem
identifies the output bundle as the displayed append of the two input probe
spines. The degree data are bounded by
\autoref{def:diffform-wedge-degree-ledger}, which records only the two input
degree rows, the concatenation provenance, and the output unary degree row.

Associativity is not a hidden algebraic law at the root. It is the displayed
ledger of \autoref{def:diffform-wedge-associator-ledger}, with closure stated
by \autoref{thm:diffform-wedge-associator-closure}. These four rows exhaust
the wedge surface because each refers back to the same carried form rows and
their visible $\hsame$, $\Cont$, probe, tensor, scalar, and antisymmetry
ledgers.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Differential-form root exterior boundary obligation]
\label{thm:diffform-root-exterior-boundary-obligation}
Exterior-derivative consumers of $\DiffFormUp$ may use only the candidate
ledger of \autoref{def:diffform-exterior-derivative-candidate-ledger}, the
input row of \autoref{def:diffform-exterior-derivative-input-row}, and the
boundary theorems already displayed for degree shift, scalar transport,
wedge-input stability, and downstream scope. The identities $d^2=0$,
closedness, nondegeneracy, and contact nonintegrability are outside this
$\DiffFormUp$ root surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First read the candidate ledger. It exposes an input carrier row, an output
carrier row, the successor-degree continuation, and transported probe, tensor,
and scalar endpoint rows. Its degree-raise theorem records only this
degree-raising candidate data.

Next read the accepted input row. The row of
\autoref{def:diffform-exterior-derivative-input-row} supplies the source and
target carried forms, the source and target degree rows, the one-step
continuation, and the carrier ledgers exposed by the de Rham input boundary.

Finally apply the boundary theorems already present in the chapter:
\autoref{thm:diffform-exterior-derivative-wedge-input-stability},
\autoref{thm:diffform-exterior-derivative-degree-shift-boundary},
\autoref{thm:diffform-exterior-derivative-scalar-transport-boundary}, and
\autoref{thm:diffform-exterior-derivative-downstream-scope}. These theorems
permit downstream consumers to cite the displayed candidate and input
ledgers, while leaving nilpotence, closedness, nondegeneracy, and contact
nonintegrability to the downstream certificate surfaces.
\end{proof}
