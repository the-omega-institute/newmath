\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\DirichletUnitUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-dirichletunit-namecert}

The Dirichlet-unit interface $\DirichletUnitUp$ packages the structure of the unit group of a ring of integers as a finitely generated abelian group of rank $r_1 + r_2 - 1$. It depends on $\NameCert_{\RingOfIntegersUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-ringofintegers-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\AbGroupUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-abgroup-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:dirichletunit-carrier}

\begin{definition}[Dirichlet-unit history carrier]
\label{def:dirichletunit-bhist-carrier}
A $\DirichletUnitUp$ carrier is a displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ row
$$
\mathcal{D}=(\mathcal{O},u,u^{-1},\mathcal{A},\lambda,\mu,\rho)
$$
where $\mathcal{O}$ is a source $\NameCert_{\RingOfIntegersUp}$ row, $u:\mathsf{BHist}$ is the visible unit row in the ring of integers, $u^{-1}:\mathsf{BHist}$ is the displayed inverse row, $\mathcal{A}$ is the source $\NameCert_{\AbGroupUp}$ row recording the additive presentation and the multiplicative unit-group law surface, $\lambda$ is the finite $\Cont$ ledger reading the unit and inverse rows from $\mathcal{O}$, $\mu$ is the finite $\Cont$ ledger reading the abelian-group law row from $\mathcal{A}$, and $\rho$ records the paired $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance.

The accepted carrier is exactly this history row. It does not assert an ambient equality of rings, a quotient of units, or an analytic logarithm map; the public endpoint is the carried unit together with its inverse, abelian-group law row, finite readbacks, and provenance.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Dirichlet-unit row classifier]
\label{def:dirichletunit-unit-classifier}
Two $\DirichletUnitUp$ carriers $\mathcal{D}$ and $\mathcal{D}'$ are classified when their visible unit rows, inverse rows, abelian-group law rows, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance projections are related by $\hsame$ after finite $\Cont$ readback from the same four projection slots of \autoref{def:dirichletunit-bhist-carrier}. The classifier therefore compares the ring-of-integers unit projection, the inverse projection, the abelian-group projection, and the provenance projection as carried $\mathsf{BHist}$ data, not as equality of host unit groups.
\end{definition}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:dirichletunit-certificate}
\closureat{DirichletUnitUp}{seedStr}

\begin{theorem}[Dirichlet-unit carrier transport]
\label{thm:dirichletunit-carrier-hsame-transport}
If two $\DirichletUnitUp$ carriers are classified by \autoref{def:dirichletunit-unit-classifier}, then $\hsame$ transports each visible carrier projection: the $\RingOfIntegersUp$ unit row, the inverse row, the $\AbGroupUp$ law row, and the paired $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance row. The transported packet is again a carrier of \autoref{def:dirichletunit-bhist-carrier} with the same finite $\Cont$ readback pattern.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the two carrier packets. The classifier definition supplies exactly four displayed comparisons: unit row, inverse row, abelian-group law row, and provenance row, each read through the corresponding finite $\Cont$ projection.

Transport the unit and inverse projections first. Since both are $\mathsf{BHist}$ rows read from the same $\RingOfIntegersUp$ source slot, $\hsame$ carries the accepted unit endpoint and its inverse endpoint to the second carrier without changing the source ledger.

Next transport the $\AbGroupUp$ law projection and the paired provenance row. Repack the four transported projections with the original readback ledgers, giving a new Dirichlet-unit carrier whose public surface is componentwise the transported one.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Dirichlet-unit boundary continuation stability]
\label{thm:dirichletunit-boundary-continuation-stability}
For a classified pair of $\DirichletUnitUp$ carriers, the finite $\Cont$ ledgers reading the unit row from $\NameCert_{\RingOfIntegersUp}$ and the abelian-group law row from $\NameCert_{\AbGroupUp}$ remain stable under the classifier. The source, inverse, law, and provenance endpoints are preserved by $\hsame$ through those ledgers, so boundary readback exposes the same Dirichlet-unit certificate surface after transport.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the ring-of-integers side through the ledger $\lambda$. It lists the source certificate, the unit row, and the inverse row, so the classifier transport from \autoref{thm:dirichletunit-carrier-hsame-transport} gives endpoint comparisons for every boundary component on that side.

Read the abelian-group side through the ledger $\mu$. The law row is transported by the same classifier, and its source certificate remains the $\AbGroupUp$ projection of the carrier packet.

Finally compose the two finite readbacks inside the displayed provenance row. The boundary after transport is therefore the same finite list of source, unit, inverse, law, and provenance projections, with $\hsame$ witnesses attached componentwise.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Dirichlet-unit ledger exactness]
\label{thm:dirichletunit-ledger-exactness}
Every public row exported by $\NameCert_{\DirichletUnitUp}$ is one of the rows displayed in \autoref{def:dirichletunit-bhist-carrier}: the source $\RingOfIntegersUp$ row, the unit row, the inverse row, the source $\AbGroupUp$ law row, one of the two finite $\Cont$ readbacks, or the paired $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance row. Conversely, those displayed rows repack as the visible Dirichlet-unit ledger.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the forward direction, project an accepted Dirichlet-unit packet to its carrier tuple. The definition names the complete list of visible entries, so any exported row must be the ring-of-integers source, unit, inverse, abelian-group source, readback ledger, or provenance entry.

For the reverse direction, start with the displayed entries over matching dependency certificates. The two finite readbacks witness that the unit and inverse rows come from the ring-of-integers source and that the abelian-group law row comes from the group source.

Repack those entries as the carrier tuple and apply the classifier definition when comparisons are required. Exactness follows because the certificate has no further exported slot beyond these displayed rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Dirichlet-unit NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:dirichletunit-namecert-obligation-surface}
The paper-side $\NameCert_{\DirichletUnitUp}$ surface consists of the $\mathsf{BHist}$ carrier of \autoref{def:dirichletunit-bhist-carrier}, the row classifier of \autoref{def:dirichletunit-unit-classifier}, carrier $\hsame$ transport, boundary continuation stability, ledger exactness, the two dependency certificates $\NameCert_{\RingOfIntegersUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\AbGroupUp}$, the finite $\Cont$ readbacks, and the paired $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance. A downstream $\RegulatorUp$ certificate may cite exactly this surface as its Dirichlet-unit input.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Collect the ten certificate roles from the displayed rows. The traditional target is the unit group of the ring of integers, the BEDC sources are the two dependency certificates, the carrier is the tuple $\mathcal{D}$, the classifier is componentwise $\hsame$, stability is supplied by carrier transport and boundary continuation, the ledger is the pair of finite readbacks, and exactness is \autoref{thm:dirichletunit-ledger-exactness}.

The core theorem list is precisely the three preceding transport, stability, and exactness obligations together with the carrier and classifier definitions. The standard bridge slot is absent, and the status is a paper-side proof target for the displayed $\NameCert_{\DirichletUnitUp}$ surface.

Thus a consumer receives a concrete BHist-facing certificate boundary. It may read the unit, inverse, law, provenance, and dependency rows, but it may not assume a logarithmic regulator formula, finite-rank basis choice, or host-level equality of unit groups from this certificate alone.
\end{proof}
