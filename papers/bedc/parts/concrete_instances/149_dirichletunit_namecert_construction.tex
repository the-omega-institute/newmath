\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\DirichletUnitUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-dirichletunit-namecert}

The Dirichlet-unit interface $\DirichletUnitUp$ packages the structure of the unit group of a ring of integers as a finitely generated abelian group of rank $r_1 + r_2 - 1$. It depends on $\NameCert_{\RingOfIntegersUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-ringofintegers-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\AbGroupUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-abgroup-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:dirichletunit-carrier}

\begin{definition}[Dirichlet unit BHist carrier packet]
\label{def:dirichletunit-bhist-unit-carrier}
A $\DirichletUnitUp$ carrier packet is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ tuple
$$
\mathcal{U}=(K,R,u,u^{-1},r,l)
$$
where $K$ is the source certificate row projected from
$\NameCert_{\RingOfIntegersUp}$, $R$ is the inherited $\AbGroupUp$ carrier row,
$u$ and $u^{-1}$ are $\mathsf{BHist}$ endpoints for a unit and its inverse, and
$r,l$ are finite $\Cont$ ledgers witnessing the right and left unit products
inside the ring-of-integers source row. The packet is accepted only when the
unit endpoints, the two product ledgers, the inherited abelian-group row, and
the $\RingOfIntegersUp$ source row are all readable from the same finite
package.

Two packets classify together precisely when the inherited $\hsame$ classifier
preserves the displayed endpoints and the $\RingOfIntegersUp$ unit-row
classifier identifies the two source rows. Thus the carrier is a BEDC history
packet for a named unit row, not an ambient equality in a host number-field
model.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Dirichlet unit carrier transport]
\label{thm:dirichletunit-unit-carrier-transport}
Let $\mathcal{U}=(K,R,u,u^{-1},r,l)$ be a packet of
\autoref{def:dirichletunit-bhist-unit-carrier}. If a second displayed tuple
$\mathcal{U}'=(K',R',v,v^{-1},r',l')$ is obtained by $\hsame$ transport of
$u$, $u^{-1}$, $r$, and $l$, while the $\RingOfIntegersUp$ unit-row classifier
identifies $K$ with $K'$ and the inherited $\AbGroupUp$ classifier identifies
$R$ with $R'$, then $\mathcal{U}'$ is again an accepted
$\DirichletUnitUp$ carrier packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the carrier packet. The projections give the source
$\RingOfIntegersUp$ row, the inherited $\AbGroupUp$ row, the two unit
endpoints, and the two finite product ledgers.

Transport the endpoint rows and the two ledgers along the displayed $\hsame$
comparisons. The $\Cont$ readback is finite, so transporting a ledger preserves
its source and target rows inside the same package.

The dependency classifiers supply the remaining rows: the
$\RingOfIntegersUp$ classifier transports the source certificate and the
$\AbGroupUp$ classifier transports the inherited abelian-group carrier. Repack
these transported projections in the tuple
$(K',R',v,v^{-1},r',l')$ to obtain an accepted carrier packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Dirichlet unit rank ledger]
\label{thm:dirichletunit-unit-rank-ledger}
Every accepted packet of \autoref{def:dirichletunit-bhist-unit-carrier}
contains a rank witness as a $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ row recording the visible
ledger expression $r_1+r_2-1$. This row is stable under the same inherited
$\hsame$ classifier and $\RingOfIntegersUp$ unit-row classifier used for the
carrier packet, and it asserts no host arithmetic beyond finite ledger
readback.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the finite package underlying the carrier. The rank row is read as a
unary history from the same source certificate that supplies the ring of
integers and its real and complex place-count ledgers.

The visible expression $r_1+r_2-1$ is therefore a ledger label: its content is
the displayed finite readback row, not an assertion about an external
arithmetic object. The $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ witness records exactly that
visible row.

Apply the carrier transport of
\autoref{thm:dirichletunit-unit-carrier-transport} to the unit endpoints and
the two product ledgers. The inherited classifier keeps the unary rank row in
the transported source package, so the same rank ledger is available for every
classified packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Dirichlet unit AbGroup projection]
\label{thm:dirichletunit-unit-abgroup-projection}
Every accepted $\DirichletUnitUp$ packet projects to the $\AbGroupUp$
operation, inverse, identity, and classifier rows carried by its dependency
certificate. The Dirichlet-unit carrier adds the ring-of-integers unit source
and the two unit-product ledgers, but it does not replace the inherited
abelian-group interface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the tuple of \autoref{def:dirichletunit-bhist-unit-carrier}. Its second
projection is the dependency row $R$ from $\NameCert_{\AbGroupUp}$.

Read $R$ through the abelian-group certificate. This exposes the operation
row, inverse row, identity row, and classifier row for the inherited finitely
generated abelian carrier.

The remaining projections $K,u,u^{-1},r,l$ witness that the inherited row is
being used as a unit group for the ring-of-integers source. Repacking with $R$
therefore gives exactly the $\AbGroupUp$ projection fields, with the unit rows
retained as additional dependency data.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Dirichlet unit certificate obligation surface]
\label{thm:dirichletunit-certificate-obligation-surface}
The public $\NameCert_{\DirichletUnitUp}$ obligation surface consists exactly
of the carrier packet of \autoref{def:dirichletunit-bhist-unit-carrier}, the
classifier transport of \autoref{thm:dirichletunit-unit-carrier-transport}, the
rank ledger of \autoref{thm:dirichletunit-unit-rank-ledger}, the inherited
$\AbGroupUp$ projection of
\autoref{thm:dirichletunit-unit-abgroup-projection}, and the
$\RingOfIntegersUp$ source rows for the unit, inverse, and two unit-product
ledgers.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier definition supplies the finite tuple and its classifier. The
transport theorem states the exact stability fields for the unit endpoints and
for the two product ledgers.

The rank theorem supplies the unary ledger row for the visible
$r_1+r_2-1$ witness, while the abelian-group projection theorem supplies the
operation, inverse, identity, and classifier rows inherited from
$\NameCert_{\AbGroupUp}$.

Conversely, every listed consumer reads one of these rows: unit membership and
unit products read from $\NameCert_{\RingOfIntegersUp}$, group operations read
from $\NameCert_{\AbGroupUp}$, and classifier stability reads from $\hsame$
transport plus the dependency classifiers. Hence the displayed rows are
exactly the NameCert surface and no stronger number-field arithmetic or
standard bridge is included.
\end{proof}
