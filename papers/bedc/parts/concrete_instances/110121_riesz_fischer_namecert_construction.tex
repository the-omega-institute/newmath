$$

\begin{enumerate}
\item $W$ is the square-summable coefficient-window row, recording finite coefficient blocks, tail bounds, and the window schedule used by the packet.
\item $I$ is the $\InnerProductUp$ row that reads coefficient-window comparisons as inner-product estimates.
\item $H$ is the $\HilbertUp$ source row naming the Hilbert-facing endpoint surface over which the coefficient windows are interpreted.
\item $N$ is the $\NormUp$ row induced by the displayed inner-product estimates.

\begin{theorem}[RieszFischer NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:riesz-fischer-namecert-obligations}

\item carrier admission for the square-summable coefficient windows, $\InnerProductUp$ comparison row, $\HilbertUp$ source row, $\NormUp$ metric row, $\CompleteMetricUp$ Cauchy-service row, $\CauchyCompletionUnitUp$ handoff row, componentwise transport, replay, provenance, and local naming;
\item square-summable exhaustion, requiring every Hilbert-facing completion read to begin with the displayed coefficient-window row $W$;
\item inner-product and norm compatibility, requiring $I$ and $N$ to expose the same finite comparison route before any metric-completion row is consumed;
\item completion handoff, requiring $M$ and $U$ to route the same Cauchy datum and not a selected external limit;
\item transport replay, requiring $T,C,P,L$ to preserve the ordered route under $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$;
\item ledger non-escape, excluding quotient $L^{2}$ classes, choice-selected limits, ambient Hilbert completeness, host equality of limits, and hidden measure-space representatives.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The packet displays the square-summable windows, inner-product estimates, Hilbert-facing source, norm metric, complete-metric service, completion-unit handoff, transport, replay, provenance, and local name as the whole available surface.

Read the analytic route in order. The coefficient window $W$ supplies the finite square-summability data; the rows $I$ and $N$ turn those windows into inner-product and norm comparisons; the row $M$ consumes the resulting Cauchy datum; and $U$ exposes the same datum to completion consumers.

Finally attach the structural rows. Componentwise transport and displayed replay preserve only $W,I,H,N,M,U$, while $P$ and $L$ source and name the packet. Since no carrier coordinate contains quotient classes, selected limits, ambient completeness, host equality, or hidden representatives, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}
