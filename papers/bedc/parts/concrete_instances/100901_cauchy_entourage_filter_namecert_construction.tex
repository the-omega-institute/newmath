\begin{definition}[Cauchy entourage-filter carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-entourage-filter-carrier}
A $\CauchyEntourageFilterUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(U,B,S,R,D,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $U$ is the $\UniformSpaceUp$ entourage row. It records the finite entourage filterbase, diagonal, refinement, symmetry, and composition data from which every Cauchy-entourage request starts.
\item $B$ is the $\CauchyFilterBasisUp$ generator row. It turns displayed entourage requests into finite Cauchy-filter basis elements before any completion-facing consumer can read them.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window row inspected by those generated basis elements.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row attached to the finite windows in $S$.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger used to compare entourage radii with the regular rational readbacks.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after $U,B,S,R,D$ have been replayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the entourage row, basis generator, finite windows, regular readback, dyadic ledger, Real seal, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from an entourage request through $U$, then through $B,S,R,D$, and finally to the seal row $E$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyEntourageFilterUp$, $\UniformSpaceUp$, $\CauchyFilterBasisUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\CompleteUniformSpaceUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyEntourageFilterUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through the displayed route $U,B,S,R,D,E,H,C,P,N$. The carrier has no coordinate for quotient filters, selected limits, countable-choice selectors, host equality, ambient completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy entourage-filter NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-entourage-filter-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CauchyEntourageFilterUp$ carrier
$$
Q=(U,B,S,R,D,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyEntourageFilterUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier exposure for the displayed uniform entourage row, CauchyFilterBasis generator, StreamName windows, RegSeqRat readbacks, DyadicRatCore tolerance ledger, Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item entourage-to-basis routing, requiring every Cauchy request read from $U$ to pass through the finite generator row $B$ before any window or rational readback is available;
\item dyadic readback comparison, requiring every generated basis element to inspect $S$ and $R$ before the tolerance ledger $D$ compares the requested entourage scale;
\item completion handoff, requiring every $\CompleteUniformSpaceUp$ or $\RealUp$ consumer named by $P$ to read the seal boundary $E$ only after the route $U,B,S,R,D$ has been replayed;
\item transport, replay, and provenance exhaustion, requiring $H,C,P,N$ to preserve and name exactly this finite route and to export no quotient filter, selected limit, countable-choice selector, host equality, ambient completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{enumerate}
No local NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier exposure is projection from \autoref{def:cauchy-entourage-filter-carrier}. The projection exposes exactly the uniform entourage row, the basis generator, the finite windows, the regular readback, the dyadic tolerance ledger, the Real seal, and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

The entourage-to-basis route is fixed by the displayed row order. A Cauchy request begins at the finite entourage filterbase in $U$ and is admitted to completion-facing use only after the generator row $B$ has produced a displayed basis element.

The dyadic comparison is downstream of that generator. The basis element can inspect windows only through $S$, can read regular rational data only through $R$, and can compare the requested scale only through the ledger $D$.

The completion handoff is terminal for this packet. The provenance row $P$ may mention $\CompleteUniformSpaceUp$ and $\RealUp$ only as consumers of the already replayed route $U,B,S,R,D\longmapsto E$.

Finally, $H$ transports each listed coordinate componentwise and $C$ replays the same dependency order. Since the carrier has no quotient, choice, selected-limit, host-equality, completeness, verification, or bridge coordinate, $P$ and $N$ exhaust the local certificate surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy entourage-filter dyadic basis handoff]
\label{thm:cauchy-entourage-filter-dyadic-basis-handoff}
Let $Q=(U,B,S,R,D,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyEntourageFilterUp$ carrier. Every generated Cauchy-filter basis read factors through the finite route
$$
U \longmapsto B \longmapsto S \longmapsto R \longmapsto D \longmapsto E \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
Thus an entourage request reaches the Cauchy-filter basis surface only with its StreamName windows, RegSeqRat readbacks, and DyadicRatCore tolerance ledger displayed. It cannot bypass $D$ by selecting a real representative, quotienting a filter, choosing a limit, or invoking ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

The row $B$ consumes that demand as a finite Cauchy-filter basis generator. It does not create a completed filter object and does not expose a limit by itself.

The regular readback route is then forced. The generated basis element inspects finite windows through $S$, reads regular rational observations through $R$, and checks the dyadic scale and inclusion data through $D$.

Only after this route has been replayed can the seal row $E$ and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$ be read. Hence no selected representative, quotient filter, selected limit, choice schedule, host equality, or ambient completeness witness is available as a bypass.
