\begin{theorem}[RegularCauchyName terminal finite-cover refinement reflection]
\label{thm:regularcauchyname-terminal-finite-cover-refinement-reflection}
Let $W$ and $W'$ be accepted terminal finite-cover witnesses for the same $\RegularCauchyNameUp$ packet with displayed root dependency manifest, and suppose that $W$ and $W'$ admit an accepted common refinement $R$. If the pullbacks of $W$ and $W'$ to $R$ agree on the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, componentwise $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows named by \autoref{def:regularcauchyname-root-dependency-manifest}, then $W$ and $W'$ present the same terminal $\RealUp$ read and the same completion-facing read. Conversely, sameness of those terminal reads reflects, after pullback to $R$, agreement on exactly those finite rows.

The reflection is only a statement about the finite terminal support of $\RegularCauchyNameUp$. It does not identify quotient streams, host sequences, selected limits, countable-choice selectors, ambient completeness, metric completeness, aggregate formalstatus rows, bridge rows, or any row outside the root dependency manifest.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First reduce each presentation to the terminal finite-cover witness surface. By \autoref{thm:regularcauchyname-terminal-finite-cover-witness}, both $W$ and $W'$ factor every terminal $\RealUp$ read and completion-facing read through the finite support listed in \autoref{def:regularcauchyname-root-dependency-manifest}. The four-face support minimality theorem \autoref{thm:regularcauchyname-four-face-support-minimality} pins that support to $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ with the displayed $\BHist$, componentwise $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.

For the forward direction, pull $W$ and $W'$ back to the accepted common refinement $R$. The StreamName finite-cover support bicriterion \autoref{thm:streamname-finite-cover-support-bicriterion} reads agreement on the displayed finite rows as sameness of the descended $\RealUp$ seal-visible source. The terminal no-extra-support theorem \autoref{thm:regularcauchyname-terminal-exit-no-extra-support} then transfers that sameness to the terminal $\RealUp$ read and to the completion-facing read, because no fifth support coordinate can change the exit.

For the reverse direction, assume the two witnesses present the same terminal $\RealUp$ read and the same completion-facing read. Apply \autoref{thm:regularcauchyname-terminal-finite-cover-witness} to express both reads through the two finite-cover witnesses, and use \autoref{thm:regularcauchyname-terminal-exit-no-extra-support} to remove any possible contribution from rows outside the four-face terminal surface.

It remains only to reflect agreement back to the refinement. The StreamName finite-cover support bicriterion \autoref{thm:streamname-finite-cover-support-bicriterion} reflects equality of the descended terminal support packet to agreement, after pullback to $R$, on $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, componentwise $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$. Since \autoref{thm:regularcauchyname-four-face-support-minimality} identifies exactly those rows as the RegularCauchyName support, this is precisely the reflected row agreement claimed.

Finally record the refusal boundary. The witness theorem and the terminal no-extra-support theorem refuse quotient stream equality, host sequence equality, selected limits, independent real carriers, ambient completeness, metric completeness, countable choice, and bridge rows; the StreamName bicriterion also refuses aggregate formalstatus rows. Therefore neither direction imports quotient stream equality, host sequence equality, selected limits, countable choice, ambient completeness, metric completeness, aggregate formalstatus, bridge data, or any row not named by the root dependency manifest.
\end{proof}
