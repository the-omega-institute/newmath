\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\EquicontinuityUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-equicontinuity-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges constructive analysis and Kelley-style compact-metric reasoning, the $\EquicontinuityUp$ packet records the finite family-modulus surface by which a compact source, a finite continuous-family ledger, and a shared tolerance-radius schedule are presented before any finite-net selector or Arzela--Ascoli consumer may read the family. It sits over the compact source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, the continuous graph and pointwise-modulus rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuousmap-namecert}, the tolerance-only modulus consumer of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformmodulus-namecert}, and the finite-net family route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finite-net-equicontinuity-namecert}.

The packet exposes a shared family modulus. It does not assert compactness, select subsequences, provide a finite net, choose an Arzela--Ascoli limit, or import host-level continuity.

\begin{definition}[Equicontinuity carrier]
\label{def:equicontinuity-carrier}
An $\EquicontinuityUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
E=(K,F,\epsilon,\rho,M,T,R,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $K$ is the compact metric source row whose finite-cover and metric inspection data are read through $\CompactMetricUp$.
\item $F$ is the finite continuous-family row, each member of which is presented through $\ContinuousMapUp$ graph and pointwise-modulus data over the same source.
\item $\epsilon$ is the tolerance row consumed by every family member before a radius is exported.
\item $\rho$ is the shared radius row, read as a single family modulus rather than as one radius per function.
\item $M$ is the $\UniformEquicontinuityModulusUp$ or $\UniformModulusUp$ consumer row that records the shared tolerance-radius handoff.
\item $T$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $K,F,\epsilon,\rho,M$.
\item $R$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from compact-source observations through continuous-family rows into the shared-modulus handoff.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CompactMetricUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\UniformEquicontinuityModulusUp$, $\UniformModulusUp$, $\FiniteNetEquicontinuityUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\EquicontinuityUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a selected finite net, selected subsequence, compactness theorem, host continuity predicate, pointwise-only modulus family, or ambient completeness principle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Equicontinuity NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:equicontinuity-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\EquicontinuityUp$ carrier $E=(K,F,\epsilon,\rho,M,T,R,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\EquicontinuityUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the compact metric source, continuous-family ledger, tolerance row, shared radius row, modulus handoff, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming; shared-modulus exactness requiring the same $\rho$ to serve every member of $F$ at tolerance $\epsilon$; compact-source compatibility requiring any finite-net consumer to read $K$ before using the modulus row; and nonescape excluding compactness assertions, selected finite nets, selected subsequences, host continuity, and ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:equicontinuity-carrier}. The tuple displays exactly $K,F,\epsilon,\rho,M,T,R,P,N$.

Shared-modulus exactness is the role of $\rho$ and $M$. The radius row is a single displayed row for the family, so a consumer cannot replace it by independently chosen radii for different members of $F$.

Compact-source compatibility is the route through $K$. A finite-net or compact-uniform consumer must read the compact metric source before it can consume the shared family modulus.

Finally, $T,R,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same displayed rows. Since the carrier has no selected net, selected subsequence, compactness theorem, host-continuity predicate, or ambient-completeness coordinate, the listed obligations exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Equicontinuity compact-metric route]
\label{thm:equicontinuity-compact-metric-route}
Every compact-uniform or finite-net consumer of an accepted $\EquicontinuityUp$ carrier factors through
$$
K,F,\epsilon,\rho,M \longmapsto T,R,P,N .
$$
The route first reads the compact metric source $K$, then the continuous-family ledger $F$, then the shared tolerance-radius handoff $\epsilon,\rho,M$. It may be consumed by the finite-net equicontinuity surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finite-net-equicontinuity-namecert}, but it does not itself supply a finite net or compactness proof.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The only source coordinate is $K$, so any compact-uniform consumer starts from the displayed compact metric row.

Read the family row $F$ next. Its entries are continuous-map rows over the same source, so the family cannot change source data while the modulus is being consumed.

The shared handoff is $\epsilon,\rho,M$. It records a tolerance and one family radius before a downstream finite-net selector reads the packet. Transport and replay through $T,R$ preserve this order, while $P,N$ merely package and name it.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Equicontinuity nonescape]
\label{thm:equicontinuity-nonescape}
An accepted $\EquicontinuityUp$ carrier exports only the finite family-modulus rows $K,F,\epsilon,\rho,M,T,R,P,N$. It cannot export compactness, a selected finite net, a selected subsequence, Arzela--Ascoli convergence, host-level continuity, pointwise-only radius choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier definition lists the entire public inventory. The analytic rows are the compact source, continuous family, tolerance, shared radius, and modulus handoff.

The structural rows transport and replay those same rows. They do not add a selector, a compactness principle, a subsequence object, a convergence row, or an external continuity predicate.

Thus every downstream route must obtain finite nets, compactness conclusions, and convergence statements from separate consumer chapters. The $\EquicontinuityUp$ packet supplies only the displayed family-modulus boundary.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Equicontinuity shared-radius stability]
\label{thm:equicontinuity-shared-radius-stability}
Let $E=(K,F,\epsilon,\rho,M,T,R,P,N)$ be an accepted $\EquicontinuityUp$ carrier. Any transport of the compact source $K$, finite family row $F$, tolerance $\epsilon$, and modulus handoff $M$ through $T$ preserves the same shared radius row $\rho$. Thus a consumer reading $\CompactMetricUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\UniformModulusUp$, or $\FiniteNetEquicontinuityUp$ through $R,P,N$ cannot replace $\rho$ by pointwise radii depending on members of $F$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:equicontinuity-carrier}. The row $\rho$ is displayed once, between the tolerance row $\epsilon$ and the modulus handoff row $M$.

Transport through $T$ is componentwise on the displayed rows. It may identify the compact source, the family row, the tolerance row, and the handoff row with transported copies, but it has no coordinate for a family-indexed list of radii.

Replay through $R$ and provenance through $P,N$ therefore reread the same shared-radius row. The compact metric, continuous-map, uniform-modulus, and finite-net consumers inherit the single $\rho$ boundary rather than a pointwise-radius surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Equicontinuity shared-radius obligation]
\label{thm:equicontinuity-shared-radius-obligation}
For every accepted $\EquicontinuityUp$ carrier $E=(K,F,\epsilon,\rho,M,T,R,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\EquicontinuityUp}$ obligation requires the single radius row $\rho$ to serve every displayed member of $F$ at tolerance $\epsilon$ before any $\FiniteNetEquicontinuityUp$ or Arzela--Ascoli consumer may read the packet. No consumer may replace $\rho$ by memberwise radii, a pointwise-only modulus family, or a selected finite-net radius outside the carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:equicontinuity-carrier}. The family row $F$ is finite and displayed over the compact metric source $K$, while $\rho$ appears as one row between the tolerance $\epsilon$ and the modulus handoff $M$.

Use the compact-metric route of \autoref{thm:equicontinuity-compact-metric-route}. It forces consumers to read $K,F,\epsilon,\rho,M$ before transport, replay, provenance, and local naming can expose the packet.

Apply shared-radius stability and finite-net readiness, \autoref{thm:equicontinuity-shared-radius-stability} and \autoref{thm:equicontinuity-finite-net-readiness}. Together they keep $\rho$ stable under transport and make it available before any finite-net or Arzela--Ascoli-facing read, so pointwise-radius leakage is outside the accepted carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Equicontinuity finite-net readiness]
\label{thm:equicontinuity-finite-net-readiness}
For every accepted $\EquicontinuityUp$ carrier $E=(K,F,\epsilon,\rho,M,T,R,P,N)$, a $\FiniteNetEquicontinuityUp$ consumer may begin only after the compact source $K$, the continuous family ledger $F$, the tolerance row $\epsilon$, the shared radius row $\rho$, and the modulus handoff $M$ have all been exposed. The rows $T,R,P,N$ preserve this order and do not themselves select a finite net.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the carrier in the order stated by \autoref{thm:equicontinuity-compact-metric-route}. The compact source $K$ is the only source row, and the family ledger $F$ is the only row naming the continuous maps over that source.

The finite-net consumer then reads $\epsilon,\rho,M$. These rows record a tolerance, a shared radius, and the handoff to the uniform-modulus surface before any finite-net selector can inspect the packet.

The structural rows $T,R,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same displayed route. Since \autoref{def:equicontinuity-carrier} has no finite-net coordinate, readiness for $\FiniteNetEquicontinuityUp$ is a boundary condition, not a selected net.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Equicontinuity modulus-ledger exactness]
\label{thm:equicontinuity-modulus-ledger-exactness}
Let $E=(K,F,\epsilon,\rho,M,T,R,P,N)$ be accepted. The modulus ledger exported by $\EquicontinuityUp$ is exactly the handoff from the compact source $K$ and finite family $F$ at tolerance $\epsilon$ to the shared radius $\rho$ recorded by $M$, transported by $T$, replayed by $R$, sourced by $P$, and named by $N$. It has no additional ledger row for host continuity, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, selected covers, or an Arzela--Ascoli limit.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The analytic part of the carrier is $K,F,\epsilon,\rho,M$. The compact-metric row supplies the source, the continuous-map row supplies the finite family, and the uniform-modulus row records the tolerance-to-radius handoff.

The ledger is exact because $T,R,P,N$ refer back to those same rows. They cannot add a second modulus, a selected cover, a limit candidate, or a host-level continuity predicate; they only preserve and name the displayed handoff.

The exclusions are also forced by \autoref{thm:equicontinuity-nonescape}. Any consumer needing compactness output, selected nets, subsequences, or completeness must obtain those data from a separate chapter after reading the exact modulus ledger here.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Equicontinuity compact-source admission]
\label{thm:equicontinuity-compact-source-admission}
For every accepted $\EquicontinuityUp$ carrier $E=(K,F,\epsilon,\rho,M,T,R,P,N)$, the compact source row $K$ is admitted through $\CompactMetricUp$ before any finite-net, shared-radius, or modulus consumer may inspect $F,\epsilon,\rho,M$. No downstream $\FiniteNetEquicontinuityUp$, $\UniformModulusUp$, or Arzela--Ascoli-facing read can bypass $K$ or replace it by an ambient compactness assertion.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:equicontinuity-carrier}. The row $K$ is the only compact-source coordinate, and the introduction fixes it as the same compact metric surface used by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}.

Use the compact-metric route and finite-net readiness theorems, \autoref{thm:equicontinuity-compact-metric-route} and \autoref{thm:equicontinuity-finite-net-readiness}. They order every consumer read through $K$ before the family ledger, tolerance, shared radius, and handoff rows.

The nonescape theorem then excludes an ambient compactness replacement. Since selected finite nets and compactness conclusions are absent from the carrier, the admitted compact source is precisely the displayed $\CompactMetricUp$ row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Equicontinuity continuous-family admission]
\label{thm:equicontinuity-continuous-family-admission}
For every accepted $\EquicontinuityUp$ carrier $E=(K,F,\epsilon,\rho,M,T,R,P,N)$, the family row $F$ is a finite ledger of $\ContinuousMapUp$ graph and pointwise-modulus rows over the same source $K$. Transport through $T$ and replay through $R$ preserve this common source, so a consumer cannot read the family as host-level continuity, source-changing maps, or pointwise-only radius data detached from $K$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read $F$ from \autoref{def:equicontinuity-carrier}. Its entries are continuous-map rows over the compact source row $K$, matching the continuous-map sibling cited in the chapter introduction.

Apply shared-radius stability. The theorem keeps $\rho$ attached to the transported compact source, finite family, tolerance, and modulus handoff, so $F$ cannot be split into unrelated pointwise-radius ledgers.

Finally apply nonescape. Host-level continuity and source-changing maps are not coordinates of $E$, and $T,R,P,N$ only transport, replay, source, and name the displayed family ledger.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Equicontinuity modulus-handoff admission]
\label{thm:equicontinuity-modulus-handoff-admission}
For every accepted $\EquicontinuityUp$ carrier $E=(K,F,\epsilon,\rho,M,T,R,P,N)$, the shared tolerance-radius row $\epsilon,\rho,M$ is the only $\UniformModulusUp$ or $\UniformEquicontinuityModulusUp$ handoff available to downstream finite-net consumers. A consumer that reads $\FiniteNetEquicontinuityUp$ must receive the handoff through this row and cannot add a second modulus ledger, a memberwise radius schedule, or a selected finite-net radius outside the carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the handoff rows $\epsilon,\rho,M$. The carrier displays one tolerance, one shared radius, and one modulus consumer row.

Use the shared-radius obligation and finite-net readiness theorems. They force every finite-net or Arzela--Ascoli-facing consumer to read $\epsilon,\rho,M$ after $K,F$ and before any finite-net action can begin.

The modulus-ledger exactness theorem identifies this handoff as the whole exported modulus ledger. Since there is no coordinate for a second modulus, a memberwise radius family, or an outside selected radius, the downstream read is confined to the displayed $\UniformModulusUp$ or $\UniformEquicontinuityModulusUp$ handoff.
\end{proof}
