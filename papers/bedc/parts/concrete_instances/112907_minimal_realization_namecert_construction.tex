\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\MinimalRealizationUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-minimal-realization-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Kalman on finite-dimensional realization theory, the $\MinimalRealizationUp$ packet records the displayed finite control-system rows that witness a realization with no smaller state ledger carrying the same input-output behavior. It sits after the observation surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-controlobservability-namecert}, the reachability surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-controlcontrollability-namecert}, the reachability Gramian of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-reachabilitygramian-namecert}, the observability Gramian of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observability-gramian-namecert}, and the finite $\LinearMapUp$ and $\MatrixUp$ rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-linearmap-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-matrix-namecert}. The packet names only finite plant, input, output, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows; it does not import a host quotient state space, a canonical Kalman decomposition, or an analytic transfer-function equality.

\begin{definition}[MinimalRealization carrier]
\label{def:minimal-realization-carrier}
A $\MinimalRealizationUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
R=(X,U,Y,A,B,C,D,Q,O,G_o,G_r,n,m,I,L,T,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the finite plant state row, $U$ is the input row, and $Y$ is the output row, each read through displayed $\MatrixUp$ or $\LinearMapUp$ coordinates;
\item $A:X\to X$, $B:U\to X$, $C:X\to Y$, and $D:U\to Y$ are the finite transition, input, output, and feedthrough rows of the plant;
\item $Q$ is the reachability row consumed from $\ControlControllabilityUp$ and $\ReachabilityGramianUp$;
\item $O$ is the observability row consumed from $\ControlObservabilityUp$ and $\ObservabilityGramianUp$;
\item $G_o$ is the state-dimension ledger and $G_r$ is the reduced-state ledger, both recorded as finite natural rows with matrix-rank readback;
\item $n$ is the displayed state-dimension row and $m$ is the reduced-state candidate row compared against it;
\item $I$ is the isomorphism witness between any accepted reduced realization at dimension $n$ and the displayed realization rows;
\item $L$ records the minimality ledger saying that every realization with the same displayed finite input-output rows has reduced-state ledger $m$ admitting a transport into $n$;
\item $T$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport and displayed $\Cont$ replay for the plant, input, output, reachability, observability, dimension, reduced-state, isomorphism, and minimality rows;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\MinimalRealizationUp$, $\ControlObservabilityUp$, $\ControlControllabilityUp$, $\ReachabilityGramianUp$, $\ObservabilityGramianUp$, $\LinearMapUp$, $\MatrixUp$, $\BHist$, $\msame$, $\hsame$, $\psame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MinimalRealizationUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[MinimalRealization NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:minimal-realization-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:minimal-realization-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\MinimalRealizationUp}$ surface consists exactly of finite plant admission for $X,U,Y,A,B,C,D$, reachability admission through $Q$, observability admission through $O$, state-dimension and reduced-state ledger admission through $G_o,G_r,n,m$, isomorphism-witness admission through $I$, minimality-ledger admission through $L$, componentwise $\hsame$ and $\msame$ transport through $T$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $T$, package provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:minimal-realization-carrier}. The plant rows $X,U,Y,A,B,C,D$ are finite $\MatrixUp$ and $\LinearMapUp$ reads, so the local certificate begins with displayed finite control-system data.

The rows $Q$ and $O$ are consumed from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-controlcontrollability-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-controlobservability-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-reachabilitygramian-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observability-gramian-namecert}. They provide the reachability and observability witnesses used by the minimality ledger without introducing a host reachable quotient or hidden unobservable subspace.

Finally $G_o,G_r,n,m,I,L,T,P,N$ record finite dimension comparison, reduced-state comparison, the displayed isomorphism witness, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming. Repacking those rows gives the stated NameCert obligation surface and no additional coordinate is available.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Controllability-observability minimality]
\label{thm:minimal-realization-controllability-observability-minimality}
Let $R=(X,U,Y,A,B,C,D,Q,O,G_o,G_r,n,m,I,L,T,P,N)$ be an accepted $\MinimalRealizationUp$ packet. Any downstream realization consumer that reads $R$ as a minimal finite realization must pass first through the displayed $\ControlControllabilityUp$ row $Q$, the displayed $\ControlObservabilityUp$ row $O$, the finite Gramian rows named by $\ReachabilityGramianUp$ and $\ObservabilityGramianUp$, the state-dimension and reduced-state ledgers $G_o,G_r,n,m$, the isomorphism witness $I$, the minimality ledger $L$, componentwise transport and replay through $T$, package provenance through $P$, and the local $\NameCert_{\MinimalRealizationUp}$ row $N$.

The exported minimality claim supplies no host quotient state space, no selected canonical realization, no unbounded trajectory semantics, and no transfer-function equality outside the displayed finite plant rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with \autoref{thm:minimal-realization-namecert-obligations}. It exposes exactly the plant, reachability, observability, dimension, reduced-state, isomorphism, minimality, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

The controllability side is read through $Q$ and the reachability Gramian route, while the observability side is read through $O$ and the observability Gramian route. These are finite $\BHist$ rows over $\MatrixUp$ and $\LinearMapUp$, so the consumer cannot replace them by an ambient state-space quotient.

The ledger $L$ compares every same-input-output realization against the displayed dimension rows $n,m$, and $I$ is the only isomorphism witness exported at the same dimension. Therefore the minimality read is the finite BEDC route named by $R$, not a host canonical-form assertion or analytic transfer-function identification.
\end{proof}
