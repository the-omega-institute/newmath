\begin{theorem}[Materiality orbit escape NameCert obligations]\label{thm:materiality-orbit-escape-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\MaterialityOrbitEscapeUp$ carrier $M=(O,F,I,R,S,T,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\MaterialityOrbitEscapeUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the observational context, feedback prefix, identity-closure witnesses, finite rule family, separation witnesses, indexed judgment, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming;
\item context exactness, requiring every observer, probe, candidate interface, classifier, rule-family, and ledger read to pass through $O$;
\item prefix exactness, requiring every feedback response to pass through the finite prefix row $F$;
\item identity-closure exactness, requiring the sameness of the observed datum to be witnessed by $I$ before any material or freedom-like judgment is exposed;
\item rule-family and separation exactness, requiring material appearance to read a displayed closing rule from $R$ and freedom-like appearance to read a positive separation row from $S$ for each rule in $R$;
\item structural transport, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming through $H,C,P,N$.
\end{enumerate}
No obligation outside these rows is part of the packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier of \autoref{def:materiality-orbit-escape-carrier}. The observational context row $O$ fixes the observer history, probe pattern, candidate interface, classifiers, finite rule family, and ledger source. Thus no judgment can be read independently of its context.

The feedback prefix is $F$, and identity closure is $I$. A material or freedom-like read must first expose the finite response prefix and the classifier witnesses that keep it traceable as one datum.

The rule-family row $R$ and separation row $S$ distinguish the two public cases. Material appearance reads a closing rule from $R$; freedom-like appearance reads, for each displayed rule in $R$, a positive separation witness in $S$.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ only transport, replay, source, and name the same finite route. They add no global observer, completed stream, context-free predicate, hidden rule family, or universal predictor, so the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Materiality orbit escape rule-family nonescape]\label{thm:materiality-orbit-escape-rule-family-nonescape}
Every public $\MaterialityOrbitEscapeUp$ read is indexed by the same observational context and finite rule family that appear in its carrier. A consumer may project a material-appearance read only through $O,F,I,R,T,H,C,P,N$ with an orbit-closing rule in $R$, and may project a freedom-like read only through $O,F,I,R,S,T,H,C,P,N$ with a displayed separation witness for each rule in $R$. The packet exports no judgment after replacing $R$ by a hidden larger family, deleting $I$, or erasing the observer and prefix indices.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

In the material case, $T$ records a rule from $R$ together with the orbit-closing ledger for the prefix. Removing $R$ or replacing it by a hidden family changes the source row and therefore changes the carrier.

In the freedom-like case, $T$ records certified escape from the same family $R$ by using the separation row $S$. Each rule admitted by $R$ must have a corresponding positive separation witness before the public read is exposed.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve this indexing under transport and replay. Hence the consumer cannot de-index the judgment, drop identity closure, or read escape relative to an undisplayed rule family.
\end{proof}
