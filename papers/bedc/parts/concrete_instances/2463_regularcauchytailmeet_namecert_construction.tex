The $\RegularCauchyTailMeetUp$ interface packages the finite rows needed when several regular Cauchy consumers must share one dyadic tail threshold. It is a concrete naming certificate for the tail-meet packet used by diagonal real-completion routes; it does not assert quotient equality of streams, a selected limit, trichotomy, or an ambient completeness theorem.

\begin{definition}[RegularCauchyTailMeet carrier]
\label{def:regular-cauchy-tail-meet-carrier}
A $\RegularCauchyTailMeetUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
T=(R_0,R_1,W_0,W_1,M_0,M_1,\tau,Q,H,C,L,N)
$$
with the following displayed coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $R_0$ and $R_1$ are the two $\RegularCauchyNameUp$ source rows whose tails must be read through one threshold;
\item $W_0$ and $W_1$ are the paired $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window schedules exposing the two rational tails;
\item $M_0$ and $M_1$ are the source $\CauchyModulusUp$ or dyadic-radius rows attached to the two regular Cauchy names;
\item $\tau$ is the shared tail threshold row, displayed as a $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger sufficient for both source rows;
\item $Q$ is the paired tail comparison row recording the two scheduled observations at the shared threshold;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the two source names, windows, source moduli, shared threshold, paired comparison, provenance, and local name;
\item $C$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ routes for reading source windows, source modulus rows, the shared threshold, the paired tail comparison, and the real-facing handoff;
\item $L$ is the finite ledger over $\RegularCauchyNameUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\CauchyModulusUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyTailMeetUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier is only the finite shared-tail packet. It has no coordinate for quotient streams, host real equality, selected limits, trichotomy, or ambient completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[RegularCauchyTailMeet NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-tail-meet-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyTailMeetUp}$ surface for the carrier of \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-tail-meet-carrier} consists exactly of carrier admission, paired $\RegularCauchyNameUp$ source admission, paired $\StreamNameUp$ scheduling, source modulus readback, shared dyadic tail-threshold exposure, paired tail comparison, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ routes, finite ledger exactness, package provenance, and the local naming row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from the displayed packet $T$. Its coordinates list the two regular Cauchy sources, their windows, their source modulus rows, the shared tail threshold, the comparison row, transport, continuation, ledger, and local name.

The shared threshold is finite. The two schedules expose the rational tail windows before $\tau$ and $Q$ can be read, and the source modulus rows certify that the same tolerance ledger is sufficient for both sides.

The remaining obligations are provenance and routing obligations. The rows $H,C,L,N$ transport the displayed coordinates, route the reads, record package provenance, and name the packet; none of them introduces quotient equality, a selected limit, trichotomy, host real equality, or completeness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegularCauchyTailMeet classifier stability]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-tail-meet-classifier-stability}
For accepted $\RegularCauchyTailMeetUp$ carriers whose displayed coordinates are related componentwise by the stored $\hsame$ rows, the paired tail comparison row $Q$ remains readable through the same shared threshold $\tau$. The classifier is stable under transport of the two source names, two window schedules, two source modulus rows, the threshold ledger, and the local certificate rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the two accepted carriers. The only rows that determine the tail classifier are the two source names, their scheduled windows, their source modulus rows, the shared threshold, and the comparison row.

Use the componentwise transport entries in $H$ to move these rows to a common displayed surface. The continuation rows in $C$ then replay the reads from sources to windows, from windows and source bounds to $\tau$, and from $\tau$ to $Q$.

Since the carrier contains no independent classifier coordinate, transported reads must use the same threshold ledger and paired comparison route. Stability follows from the displayed packet itself, not from host real equality or quotient stream equality.
\end{proof}

\label{thm:regular-cauchy-tail-meet-shared-threshold-cofinality}

If a displayed dyadic threshold row $\tau'$ is at least as strong as $\tau$ for both source modulus rows and is transported through the same paired window schedules, then the paired comparison row $Q$ and the $\RealUp$ handoff factor through $\tau'$ using the existing $\hsame$ transports, displayed $\Cont$ routes, finite ledger, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyTailMeetUp}$ row.

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The two source modulus rows $M_0$ and $M_1$ are the only rows that certify threshold sufficiency for the paired tails, and the paired schedules $W_0$ and $W_1$ are the only rows that expose the corresponding observations.

Read the displayed strengthening $\tau'$ through those same source modulus rows. The hypothesis that $\tau'$ is at least as strong as $\tau$ for both sources lets each scheduled tail read use $\tau'$ without changing the source rows or window schedules.

Replay the paired schedules through the displayed continuation routes. Transport the comparison row through the componentwise $\hsame$ entries already stored in $H$, so the comparison remains the same carrier-facing row $Q$ with the threshold coordinate refined to $\tau'$.

Finally apply ledger exactness and the Real seal handoff. The finite ledger and seal route already contain the required transports, continuations, provenance, and local name, so the strengthened threshold factors through the same public handoff and adds no unlisted analytic coordinate.
\end{proof}
