\begin{definition}[RegularCauchyTailMeet carrier]
\label{def:regular-cauchy-tail-meet-carrier}

$$
T=(R_0,R_1,W_0,W_1,M_0,M_1,\tau,Q,H,C,L,N)

with the following displayed coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $R_0$ and $R_1$ are the two $\RegularCauchyNameUp$ source rows whose tails must be read through one threshold;
\item $W_0$ and $W_1$ are the paired $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window schedules exposing the two rational tails;
\item $M_0$ and $M_1$ are the source $\CauchyModulusUp$ or dyadic-radius rows attached to the two regular Cauchy names;
\item $\tau$ is the shared tail threshold row, displayed as a $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger sufficient for both source rows;
\item $Q$ is the paired tail comparison row recording the two scheduled observations at the shared threshold;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the two source names, windows, source moduli, shared threshold, paired comparison, provenance, and local name;
\item $C$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ routes for reading source windows, source modulus rows, the shared threshold, the paired tail comparison, and the real-facing handoff;
\item $L$ is the finite ledger over $\RegularCauchyNameUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\CauchyModulusUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$;

The carrier is only the finite shared-tail packet. It has no coordinate for quotient streams, host real equality, selected limits, trichotomy, or ambient completeness.
