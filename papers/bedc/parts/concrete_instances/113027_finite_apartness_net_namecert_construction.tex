\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\FiniteApartnessNetUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-finite-apartness-net-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges constructive analysis, the $\FiniteApartnessNetUp$ packet records the finite apartness-net certificate used when a Real or completion-facing argument needs finite local separation without open-cover compactness. It reads the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the finite observation schedule of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet is a finite certificate surface; it does not assert host compactness, quotient equality of real names, selected representatives, countable choice, or ambient completeness.

\begin{definition}[Finite apartness-net carrier]
\label{def:finite-apartness-net-carrier}
A $\FiniteApartnessNetUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
F=(C,D,A,V,H,K,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $C$ is the finite center row, listing the displayed centers through $\StreamNameUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback data.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ radius row, recording dyadic radii, tolerance comparisons, and the positive-radius ledger used by the net.
\item $A$ is the pairwise apartness ledger, certifying apartness between distinct displayed centers at the requested dyadic scale.
\item $V$ is the coverage ledger, recording that every accepted finite request is routed to a displayed center and radius before any Real-facing readback is exported.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for centers, dyadic radii, apartness rows, coverage rows, replay, provenance, and local naming.
\item $K$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a tolerance request through the same center, radius, apartness, and coverage rows.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\FiniteApartnessNetUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteApartnessNetUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only through $C,D,A,V,H,K,P,N$. It has no coordinate for an open cover, a finite-subcover theorem, quotient equality of real names, a selected representative, countable choice, host real equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Finite apartness-net NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:finite-apartness-net-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\FiniteApartnessNetUp$ carrier $F=(C,D,A,V,H,K,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteApartnessNetUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for finite centers, dyadic radii, pairwise apartness, coverage, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming; radius admission through $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ before apartness is read; coverage admission requiring each accepted request to expose a center and radius before a $\RealUp$ consumer sees the result; and nonescape excluding open-cover compactness, selected representatives, quotient real equality, countable choice, host real equality, and ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:finite-apartness-net-carrier}. The accepted packet displays exactly $C,D,A,V,H,K,P,N$.

The radius and apartness obligations are read before coverage. The dyadic row $D$ supplies the visible scale, while the apartness ledger $A$ compares only the displayed finite centers in $C$ at that scale.

Coverage is the row $V$. It can answer a finite request only by pointing back to a displayed center and dyadic radius, so a Real-facing consumer cannot read a hidden finite-subcover theorem or a selected host representative.

Finally, $H,K,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same data. Since the carrier has no coordinate for open covers, quotient real equality, countable choice, host real equality, or ambient completeness, the listed obligations exhaust the certificate surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Finite apartness-net RegSeqRat consumer]
\label{thm:finite-apartness-net-regseqrat-consumer}
Every $\RegSeqRatUp$ or $\RealUp$ consumer of an accepted $\FiniteApartnessNetUp$ carrier must pass through the finite route
$$
\DyadicRatCoreUp,\ \StreamNameUp,\ \RegSeqRatUp,\ \RealUp .
$$
In particular, finite centers are read as stream names with regular rational windows, dyadic radii supply the tolerance scale, the pairwise apartness and coverage ledgers are exposed before the Real seal, and no consumer receives open-cover compactness, countable choice, quotient equality, selected representatives, host real equality, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from an accepted carrier $F=(C,D,A,V,H,K,P,N)$. A regular-rational consumer can see only the centers named by $C$ and the dyadic scale named by $D$.

The route then exposes the finite ledgers. Pairwise apartness is read through $A$, and coverage is read through $V$ only after the displayed center and radius rows have fixed the current tolerance request.

Transport and replay through $H$ and $K$ preserve that route, while $P$ and $N$ record provenance and local naming. Therefore every RegSeqRat or Real-facing read is generated by the displayed finite-net data and cannot import the excluded host principles.
\end{proof}
