\begin{definition}[RegularCauchyTelescopingBudget carrier]
\label{def:regular-cauchy-telescoping-budget-carrier}
A $\RegularCauchyTelescopingBudgetUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
B=(E,W,D,R,S,T,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $E$ is the precision request whose requested Real-completion tolerance is being budgeted;
\item $W$ is a finite chain of selected $\StreamNameUp$ windows opened from the displayed regular Cauchy sources;
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ error ledger read from the successive windows in $W$;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row obtained after the selected dyadic errors have been composed;
\item $S$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached from the handoff $R$ at the requested precision;
\item $T$ is the telescoping composition ledger, recording the finite sum of adjacent-window errors and the remaining budget after each handoff;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $E,W,D,R,S,T$ and for the structural rows of the packet;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes for precision exposure, window-chain admission, dyadic-ledger reads, telescoping composition, $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff, and $\RealUp$ seal delivery;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\RegularCauchySequenceUp$, $\RegularCauchyWindowFusionUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyTelescopingBudgetUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The local classifier compares accepted carriers only through the visible rows $E,W,D,R,S,T,H,C,P,N$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[RegularCauchyTelescopingBudget window chain]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-telescoping-budget-window-chain}
For every accepted $\RegularCauchyTelescopingBudgetUp$ carrier
$$
B=(E,W,D,R,S,T,H,C,P,N),
$$
the selected windows form a finite chain read through $\RegularCauchySequenceUp$ and $\RegularCauchyWindowFusionUp$ surfaces. Each adjacent-window error consumed by the chain is recorded in $D$, and each transfer of remaining precision is recorded in $T$ before the handoff row $R$ is available.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project $E,W,D,T$ from the carrier. The precision row $E$ fixes the requested tolerance, and the window row $W$ is the only coordinate that opens selected $\StreamNameUp$ windows.

Use the existing $\RegularCauchySequenceUp$ and $\RegularCauchyWindowFusionUp$ surfaces as the displayed sources of the chain. They expose finite selected windows and shared-window Real-facing routes without selecting a host limit or quotient representative.

Now read the dyadic and telescoping rows. The ledger $D$ stores the adjacent-window error rows, while $T$ composes them and records the remaining budget passed to the next window. Since $R$ appears only after $D$ and $T$, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff is downstream of the finite chain.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegularCauchyTelescopingBudget Real seal handoff]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-telescoping-budget-real-seal-handoff}
Every $\RealUp$ seal read exported by an accepted $\RegularCauchyTelescopingBudgetUp$ carrier factors through the route
$$
E,W,D,T,R,S,H,C,P,N.
$$
The route first consumes the requested precision, then the selected window chain, then the dyadic error ledger, then the telescoping composition ledger, then the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff, and only then the $\RealUp$ seal row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the window-chain theorem. It places the selected windows, dyadic error rows, telescoping composition, and $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff in the displayed order $E,W,D,T,R$.

Read the seal coordinate. The row $S$ is the only $\RealUp$ seal row in the carrier, and the continuation row $C$ exposes it only after the route through $E,W,D,T,R$ has been replayed.

The rows $H,P,N$ transport, package, and name the same route. Therefore every Real-facing consumer receives the seal through $E,W,D,T,R,S,H,C,P,N$ and through no independent completion object.
\end{proof}
