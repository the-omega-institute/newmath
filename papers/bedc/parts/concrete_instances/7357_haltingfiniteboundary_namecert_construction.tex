\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\HaltingFiniteBoundaryUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-halting-finite-boundary-namecert}
\origin{ai}

The $\HaltingFiniteBoundaryUp$ packet realizes the halting fixed-point obstruction of \autoref{ch:visions-halting-as-form-of-distinction-limit} as a finite boundary certificate. It sits beside the distinction packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-haltingdistinction-namecert}, the finite trace packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-haltingdistinctiontrace-namecert}, the positive trace classifier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-haltingtraceclassifier-namecert}, the terminal trace-normal-form surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuationtracenormalform-namecert}, and the public inscription record of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-inscriptionevent-namecert}. These dependencies are row consumption: the carrier reads finite trace evidence, terminal trace shape, and public record accountability as displayed packet rows, not as an external halting ontology.

\begin{definition}[Halting finite-boundary carrier]
\label{def:halting-finite-boundary-carrier}
A $\HaltingFiniteBoundaryUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
B=(A,F,R,D,T,I,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ is the admitted certificate-history input row whose visible behavior is being inspected;
\item $F$ is the finite positive $\Cont$ trace row, recording only the displayed segment available to the packet;
\item $R$ is the Real or trace-consumer row that may read $F$ as finite evidence but not as a total decision;
\item $D$ is the diagonal refusal row, rejecting any request to turn absence of a displayed trace into divergence, non-termination, or a negative halting verdict;
\item $T$ is the terminal trace-normal-form row consumed from the continuation-trace normal-form surface when a displayed terminal boundary is present;
\item $I$ is the inscription-event row naming the public record in which the finite trace and refusal boundary are consumed;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the input, trace, consumer, refusal, terminal, inscription, package, and local naming rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes that replay the finite trace, enter the trace consumer, expose the diagonal refusal, and return the terminal and inscription rows to the same packet;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, $\NameCert$, $\HaltingDistinctionUp$, $\HaltingDistinctionTraceUp$, $\HaltingTraceClassifierUp$, $\ContinuationTraceNormalFormUp$, $\InscriptionEventUp$, and $\RealUp$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\HaltingFiniteBoundaryUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a total halting predicate, a divergence oracle, an unbounded search certificate, a host evaluator, or a proof that every absent future continuation is absent.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Halting finite-boundary NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:halting-finite-boundary-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\HaltingFiniteBoundaryUp}$ surface for \autoref{def:halting-finite-boundary-carrier} consists of six finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier exposure for the displayed packet $B=(A,F,R,D,T,I,H,C,P,N)$;
\item finite trace evidence, requiring every accepted Real or trace-consumer read to expose the finite $\Cont$ trace row $F$;
\item diagonal refusal, requiring every total-halting, divergence, or non-termination request outside $F$ to be rejected by $D$;
\item terminal trace locality, requiring any normal-form or terminal read to pass through $T$ and the displayed routes $C$;
\item public record accountability, requiring downstream inscription use to pass through $I,P,N$ without adding private host evidence;
\item transport and ledger exactness, requiring $A,F,R,D,T,I,P,N$ to be transported componentwise through $H$ and replayed only through $C$.
\end{enumerate}
The later Lean \texttt{TasteGate.lean} development must include $\mathsf{Nontrivial}\ \HaltingFiniteBoundaryUp$ and $\mathsf{FieldFaithful}\ \HaltingFiniteBoundaryUp$ instances with an explicit \texttt{witness\_pair} and \texttt{mk}-derived field projection, so the gate cannot identify the finite trace row with the diagonal refusal row or forget either row from the carrier.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.HaltingFiniteBoundaryUp.HaltingFiniteBoundaryTasteGate\_single\_carrier\_alignment}
\begin{proof}
Carrier exposure is projection from \autoref{def:halting-finite-boundary-carrier}. The rows list the inspected input, finite trace, consumer read, diagonal refusal, terminal trace boundary, inscription record, transports, replay routes, package provenance, and local name.

Finite trace evidence is the $F$ row consumed by $R$. The route data $C$ may replay that finite trace and transport it through $H$, but no coordinate supplies an unbounded search over future continuations.

Diagonal refusal is the $D$ row. It is transported with $F$ and $R$, so a consumer cannot turn absence of a displayed continuation into divergence or a negative verdict after transport.

Terminal trace locality and public record accountability are the $T$ and $I$ rows. They allow a consumer to read terminal trace shape and public inscription use only through the same finite packet, with provenance and local naming recorded by $P$ and $N$.

Since every accepted read factors through $A,F,R,D,T,I,H,C,P,N$, the packet separates positive finite trace evidence from forbidden total halting decisions.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Halting finite-boundary consumer inversion]
\label{thm:halting-finite-boundary-consumer-inversion}
Any accepted $\HaltingFiniteBoundaryUp$ packet with a Real or trace consumer exposes both a finite $\Cont$ trace row and a diagonal refusal row. A packet lacking either row is not a carrier of \autoref{def:halting-finite-boundary-carrier}, and therefore cannot supply the local $\NameCert_{\HaltingFiniteBoundaryUp}$ obligations.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.HaltingFiniteBoundaryUp.HaltingFiniteBoundaryCarrier\_consumer\_inversion}
\begin{proof}
Let an accepted packet be presented to a Real or trace consumer. By carrier exposure it has the displayed form $B=(A,F,R,D,T,I,H,C,P,N)$.

The consumer row is $R$, and its finite evidence is $F$. By the finite trace obligation, any accepted read through $R$ must expose $F$ before the consumer may classify the observed behavior.

The boundary row is $D$. By diagonal refusal, the same accepted packet must expose $D$ before any request outside the displayed finite trace is rejected. If $F$ or $D$ is missing, the tuple is not the carrier named by the definition, so the local certificate cannot be formed.
\end{proof}
