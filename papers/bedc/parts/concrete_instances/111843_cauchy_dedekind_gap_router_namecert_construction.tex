\begin{theorem}[Cauchy--Dedekind gap-router NameCert obligations]\label{thm:cauchy-dedekind-gap-router-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\CauchyDedekindGapRouterUp$ carrier
$$
G=(D,S,R,E,L,B,J,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyDedekindGapRouterUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the dyadic tolerance ledger, stream-window schedule, regular rational readback, real seal, located-cut ledger, Cauchy--Dedekind boundary row, gap-routing judgment, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item window exactness, requiring every public comparison request to be addressed by $S$ before either the Cauchy-facing readback $R$ or the located-cut ledger $L$ can be inspected;
\item dyadic gap exactness, requiring every routed comparison to consume a displayed positive dyadic cell from $D$;
\item Cauchy-side handoff, requiring the $\RealUp$ seal $E$ to be downstream of $D,S,R$;
\item cut-side handoff, requiring the boundary row $B$ to compare only the displayed $R$ and $L$ rows;
\item routing determinacy, requiring $J$ to name the gap cell and side of the comparison without adding a second equality principle;
\item classifier transport and replay, requiring $H$ and $C$ to preserve exactly the listed rows;
\item non-escape, excluding quotient stream equality, primitive cut equality, selected limits, trichotomy, countable choice, and ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored outside those rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:cauchy-dedekind-gap-router-carrier}. The accepted packet exposes the dyadic, stream, regular readback, real-seal, located-cut, boundary, judgment, and structural rows as one finite display.

Window exactness follows from the shared row $S$. A public request cannot reach either the Cauchy-facing row $R$ or the located-cut row $L$ until its finite observation window has been named.

Dyadic gap exactness is supplied by $D$. The same positive dyadic cell is the comparison budget used by the regular readback and by the located boundary row, so the router cannot introduce an unbudgeted comparison.

The Cauchy-side handoff reaches $E$ only after $D,S,R$ have been displayed. The cut-side handoff reaches $B$ only after $D,S,L$ and the common readback row have been displayed.

The routing row $J$ then records which displayed gap cell and side of the comparison is used. Since $J$ is a row of the carrier rather than an external equality principle, it cannot export quotient stream equality or primitive cut equality.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name only the listed rows. The excluded completeness and choice principles have no carrier coordinate and therefore cannot become local obligations.
\end{proof}
