\begin{theorem}[Cauchy regular completion adjunction NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-regular-completion-adjunction-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CauchyRegularCompletionAdjunctionUp$ carrier $A=(F,R,W,D,L,U,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyRegularCompletionAdjunctionUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $A$, regular-readback compatibility through $R$, finite-window budget compatibility through $W,D$, terminal Real seal determinacy through $L$, universal-property handoff through $U$, functorial completion source exposure through $F$, and structural transport, replay, provenance, and local naming through $H,C,P,N$.

The ledger refuses every route that would discharge an obligation by quotient completion equality, a selected limit, a host completed metric space, an ambient completeness principle, a hidden cofinal stream family, or a host-level equality of real names.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-regular-completion-adjunction-carrier}. The visible rows are exactly $F,R,W,D,L,U,H,C,P,N$, and each listed NameCert obligation is a projection of one of those rows.

The adjunction-facing side is downstream from that route. The functor row $F$ records the completion-facing source packet, while the universal-property row $U$ records extension and uniqueness only after the same $R,W,D,L$ rows have been read.

Finally, $H,C,P,N$ preserve componentwise comparison, replay, provenance, and local naming for the displayed rows. Since the carrier contains no quotient-completion, selection, host-completion, hidden-completeness, cofinal-family, or host-equality coordinate, the ledger refusal clauses follow from exhaustion of the tuple.
\end{proof}
