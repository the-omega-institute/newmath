Following Bishop and Bridges, the $\LocatedApartnessRealBridgeUp$ packet records the finite comparison route by which located-radius readback and positive apartness evidence can be consumed together without promoting the result to classical real order. It sits between the located-real surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedreal-namecert}, the Cauchy real apartness surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-real-apartness-namecert}, the real apartness order bridge of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-apartness-order-bridge-namecert}, and the cotransitivity route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-apartness-cotransitivity-namecert}. The packet is a constructive comparison bridge for Real and Completion consumers; it is not a trichotomy theorem, quotient-real equality principle, host real equality test, selected-limit operator, or completeness claim.

\begin{definition}[Located apartness real bridge carrier]
\label{def:located-apartness-real-bridge-carrier}
A $\LocatedApartnessRealBridgeUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
J=(L_0,L_1,R_0,R_1,A,O,T,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $L_0$ and $L_1$ are accepted $\LocatedRealUp$ endpoint packets whose finite located-radius windows are being compared.
\item $R_0$ and $R_1$ are the located-radius readback rows, each exposing a $\StreamNameUp$ window, a $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, and a $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ radius ledger before any terminal $\RealUp$ seal is inspected.
\item $A$ is the positive apartness witness row, read through the $\CauchyRealApartnessUp$ surface and tied to the same endpoint windows.
\item $O$ is the order-facing bridge row supplied by $\RealApartnessOrderBridgeUp$, used only after $A$ has exposed positive apartness.
\item $T$ is the cotransitivity route row, pointing to the existing $\ApartnessCotransitivityUp$ surface when a third displayed real name is inspected.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the endpoint packets, readbacks, apartness witness, order bridge, cotransitivity route, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a located-apartness bridge request through $L_0,L_1,R_0,R_1,A,O,T,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LocatedApartnessRealBridgeUp$, $\LocatedRealUp$, $\CauchyRealApartnessUp$, $\RealApartnessOrderBridgeUp$, $\ApartnessCotransitivityUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedApartnessRealBridgeUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier admits exactly the finite packet in which located-radius readback and apartness evidence share the displayed endpoint windows. It has no coordinate for real trichotomy, quotient equality, host real equality, selected limits, countable choice, ambient completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Located real apartness cotransitive bridge]
\label{thm:located-real-apartness-cotransitive-bridge}
For every accepted $\LocatedApartnessRealBridgeUp$ carrier
$$
J=(L_0,L_1,R_0,R_1,A,O,T,H,C,P,N),
$$
any comparison read that uses the located-radius readbacks $R_0,R_1$ together with the positive apartness witness $A$ reaches a Real-facing consumer only through the existing apartness-cotransitivity surface. More precisely, the read first opens the two $\LocatedRealUp$ endpoint packets, then the shared finite readback route
$$
\StreamNameUp \longmapsto \RegSeqRatUp \longmapsto \DyadicRatCoreUp \longmapsto \RealUp,
$$
then the $\CauchyRealApartnessUp$ witness, then the $\RealApartnessOrderBridgeUp$ order-facing handoff, and finally the $\ApartnessCotransitivityUp$ row $T$ when a third real name is present. The export is finite constructive comparison evidence only. It exports no trichotomy, quotient real equality, host real equality, selected limit, countable-choice branch selection, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, or completion principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:located-apartness-real-bridge-carrier}. The endpoint information is exactly the two located real packets $L_0,L_1$ and the two finite located-radius readbacks $R_0,R_1$.

The apartness row is opened only after those finite readbacks are visible. Thus $A$ is tied to the same $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, and terminal $\RealUp$ route as the endpoint data.

The order-facing handoff is downstream of apartness. The row $O$ may expose the order bridge supplied by $\RealApartnessOrderBridgeUp$, but it receives positive separation from $A$ and cannot introduce an equality test or a trichotomy branch.

When a third real name is inspected, the route must pass through $T$. That row is precisely the cotransitivity surface of $\ApartnessCotransitivityUp$, so the consumer receives a displayed cotransitive apartness route rather than a classical comparison principle.

The remaining rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, package, and name the same finite route. Since the carrier lists no quotient equality, host equality, selected limit, choice, or completeness row, none can be exported by the local certificate.
\end{proof}
