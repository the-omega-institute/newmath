\begin{lemma}[Located apartness real bridge forward lattice]
Every downstream Real and Completion consumer of a $\LocatedApartnessRealBridgeUp$ packet reads the local bridge only after the located-real, Cauchy-apartness, order-bridge, and cotransitivity sibling surfaces of \autoref{lem:located-apartness-real-bridge-sibling-dependency-route} have been exposed. \end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Project the dependency route already recorded in \autoref{lem:located-apartness-real-bridge-sibling-dependency-route}. The local bridge has no independent downstream coordinate before the located endpoint rows, positive Cauchy apartness evidence, order-facing handoff rows, and cotransitivity routing have been read.

A separated-completion consumer may therefore receive only the finite bridge packet produced by those sibling surfaces. The consumer obtains a displayed Real and Completion handoff, while the excluded host principles remain outside the carrier.
\end{proof}
