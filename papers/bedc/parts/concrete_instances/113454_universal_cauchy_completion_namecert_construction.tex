\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\UniversalCauchyCompletionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-universal-cauchy-completion-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges on constructive completion, the $\UniversalCauchyCompletionUp$ packet records the universal-property boundary of a Cauchy completion as finite NameCert data. It sits between the Cauchy-filter carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyfiltercompletion-namecert}, the universal extension surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-completion-universal-property-namecert}, the metric completion route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metriccompletion-namecert}, the separated boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separatedcompletion-namecert}, and the displayed readback chain through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet gives downstream completion chapters one named carrier for dense extension and uniqueness without importing quotient completion equality, selected representatives, countable choice, or ambient Real completeness.

\begin{definition}[Universal Cauchy completion carrier]
\label{def:universal-cauchy-completion-carrier}
A $\UniversalCauchyCompletionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
U=(S,D,E,Q,Z,W,R,L,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the source metric and Cauchy packet row, naming the metric source, its displayed Cauchy evidence, and the finite tolerance requests read by the completion construction.
\item $D$ is the dense embedding row from the source carrier into the completion-facing carrier.
\item $E$ is the extension-arrow row, carrying the displayed continuous arrow out of the completed carrier into a separated target.
\item $Q$ is the uniqueness row, recording that two accepted extension arrows with the same source action are identified by the stored NameCert route rather than by quotient equality.
\item $Z$ is the separated-reflection row, exposing the separated target boundary before uniqueness is read.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row used to inspect the source and extension data at the displayed tolerance.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row obtained from the same windows.
\item $L$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after the StreamName windows and RegSeqRat readback are visible.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the source, embedding, extension, uniqueness, separated-reflection, window, readback, real-seal, replay, provenance, and naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a source tolerance request through the dense embedding, extension arrow, separated boundary, finite windows, regular rational readback, and real seal.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\UniversalCauchyCompletionUp$, $\CauchyFilterCompletionUp$, $\CauchyCompletionUniversalPropertyUp$, $\MetricCompletionUp$, $\SeparatedCompletionUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\UniversalCauchyCompletionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through these rows. It has no coordinate for quotient completion equality, selected representatives, selected limits, countable choice, host equality of completed points, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Universal Cauchy completion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:universal-cauchy-completion-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\UniversalCauchyCompletionUp$ carrier $U=(S,D,E,Q,Z,W,R,L,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\UniversalCauchyCompletionUp}$ surface consists exactly of source metric and Cauchy admission, dense embedding admission, extension-arrow admission, uniqueness-row admission, separated-reflection admission, StreamName finite-window admission, RegSeqRat readback, Real seal admission, componentwise transport, displayed replay, provenance, and local naming for the displayed rows $S,D,E,Q,Z,W,R,L,H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier of \autoref{def:universal-cauchy-completion-carrier}. The analytic rows are the source metric and Cauchy row $S$, the dense embedding $D$, the extension arrow $E$, the uniqueness row $Q$, the separated-reflection boundary $Z$, the finite windows $W$, the regular rational readback $R$, and the terminal real seal $L$.

The certificate reads these rows in order. A completion-facing request first sees the source and dense embedding, then the extension arrow into the separated boundary, then the finite windows and regular rational readback used by the real-facing seal.

The rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same packet. Since the carrier has no quotient equality, selected representative, selected limit, countable-choice schedule, host equality, or ambient completeness coordinate, the listed rows exhaust the local naming obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Universal Cauchy completion density route]
\label{thm:universal-cauchy-completion-density-route}
Every density-facing read of an accepted $\UniversalCauchyCompletionUp$ carrier factors through the finite route
$$
\CauchyFilterCompletionUp \longmapsto \StreamNameUp \longmapsto \RegSeqRatUp \longmapsto \RealUp .
$$
More explicitly, the consumer reads the source metric and Cauchy packet $S$, the dense embedding $D$, the StreamName windows $W$, the RegSeqRat readback $R$, and finally the Real seal $L$, with $H,C,P,N$ preserving the same route. The route supplies a density handoff for $\MetricCompletionUp$ and $\SeparatedCompletionUp$ consumers without quotient completion equality, selected representatives, selected limits, countable choice, host equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the obligation surface of \autoref{thm:universal-cauchy-completion-namecert-obligations}. It exposes the source metric and Cauchy packet before any dense embedding or completion-facing read is available.

The dense embedding row then fixes the route from source observations to completion observations. The StreamName row supplies finite windows, and the RegSeqRat row repacks those windows as regular rational readback before the terminal Real seal is visible.

Transport, replay, provenance, and local naming keep the same packet throughout the route. Thus a density-facing consumer can cite the completion route only through $S,D,W,R,L,H,C,P,N$, and no excluded host-side datum is introduced.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Universal Cauchy completion extension uniqueness]
\label{thm:universal-cauchy-completion-universal-property}
Let $U=(S,D,E,Q,Z,W,R,L,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\UniversalCauchyCompletionUp$ carrier. Any two accepted extension-arrow reads over the same source action and the same separated target boundary are forced through the stored uniqueness row $Q$, after first exposing the dense embedding $D$, separated-reflection row $Z$, StreamName windows $W$, RegSeqRat readback $R$, Real seal $L$, and structural rows $H,C,P,N$. The conclusion is an equality of accepted NameCert routes, not a quotient equality of completed points or a selected representative.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Expose the two extension-arrow reads through the carrier of \autoref{def:universal-cauchy-completion-carrier}. The shared source action is read through $S$ and the dense embedding row $D$, while the common separated target boundary is read through $Z$.

The uniqueness row $Q$ is only available after these rows have been displayed. Its replay uses the same finite windows $W$, the same regular rational readback $R$, and the same Real seal $L$ for both extension arrows.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport and name the comparison as an accepted route comparison. Because the carrier contains no quotient equality of completed points, selected representative, selected limit, host equality, countable-choice selector, or ambient completeness coordinate, the conclusion is exactly the displayed route uniqueness.
\end{proof}
