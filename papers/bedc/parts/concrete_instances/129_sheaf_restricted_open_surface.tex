\begin{definition}[Sheaf restricted-open carrier]
\label{def:sheaf-restricted-open-carrier}
Let $\mathcal{T}$ be a $\mathsf{BHist}$-scoped indexed open topology carrier for $\TopologyUp$, and let $\mathcal{S}$ be an indexed sheaf carrier for $\SheafUp$ over $\mathcal{T}$. Fix an open index $i:\mathsf{OpenIx}$. The restricted-open carrier $\mathcal{S}|_i$ is the carrier indexed by pairs $(j,\eta_j)$ with
$$
\eta_j:j\le_{\mathcal{T}}i.
$$
On such an index it inherits
$$
\mathsf{Sec}_{|i}(j,\eta_j):=\mathsf{Sec}(j),
\qquad
\sim_{|i,(j,\eta_j)}:=\sim_j.
$$
For an inclusion $\theta:k\le_{\mathcal{T}}j$ between restricted indices, its restriction map is the ambient sheaf restriction
$$
\rho^{|i}_{(j,\eta_j),(k,\eta_k)}:=\rho_{j,k}.
$$
The target witness $\eta_k:k\le_{\mathcal{T}}i$ is the composite of $\theta$ with $\eta_j$, so every carrier, classifier, and restriction row stays inside the fixed open $i$ without adding a host subspace, powerset restriction, quotient, or sheafification primitive.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Sheaf restricted-open restriction laws]
\label{thm:sheaf-restricted-open-restriction-laws}
Let $\mathcal{S}|_i$ be the restricted-open carrier of \autoref{def:sheaf-restricted-open-carrier}. For every restricted index $(j,\eta_j)$ and section $s:\mathsf{Sec}_{|i}(j,\eta_j)$, the inherited identity row satisfies
$$
\rho^{|i}_{(j,\eta_j),(j,\eta_j)}(s)\sim_{|i,(j,\eta_j)}s.
$$
For every chain of restricted indices
$$
(k,\eta_k)\le_{\mathcal{T}}(j,\eta_j)\le_{\mathcal{T}}(l,\eta_l)
$$
and every $s:\mathsf{Sec}_{|i}(l,\eta_l)$, the inherited composition row satisfies
$$
\rho^{|i}_{(j,\eta_j),(k,\eta_k)}
\bigl(\rho^{|i}_{(l,\eta_l),(j,\eta_j)}(s)\bigr)
\sim_{|i,(k,\eta_k)}
\rho^{|i}_{(l,\eta_l),(k,\eta_k)}(s).
$$
Moreover, for every restricted inclusion $(k,\eta_k)\le_{\mathcal{T}}(j,\eta_j)$, classifier equality transports along the inherited restriction:
$$
s\sim_{|i,(j,\eta_j)}t
\Longrightarrow
\rho^{|i}_{(j,\eta_j),(k,\eta_k)}(s)
\sim_{|i,(k,\eta_k)}
\rho^{|i}_{(j,\eta_j),(k,\eta_k)}(t).
$$
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SheafUp.SheafRestrictedOpenCarrier\_restriction\_laws}
\begin{proof}
Unfold \autoref{def:sheaf-restricted-open-carrier}. The section carrier and classifier at $(j,\eta_j)$ are exactly $\mathsf{Sec}(j)$ and $\sim_j$, and the identity restriction is the ambient row $\rho_{j,j}$.

Apply the identity field of the indexed section presheaf carrier in \autoref{def:sheaf-indexed-section-presheaf-carrier}. This gives the first displayed classifier equality.

For the composition row, forget only the fixed target witness to $i$. The displayed restricted chain is an ambient chain $k\le_{\mathcal{T}}j\le_{\mathcal{T}}l$, so the composition field of \autoref{def:sheaf-indexed-section-presheaf-carrier} gives the second displayed equality after replacing each restricted restriction map by the corresponding ambient $\rho$.

The classifier-transport row is identical. A restricted classifier equality is an ambient equality $s\sim_j t$, and the restricted map from $(j,\eta_j)$ to $(k,\eta_k)$ is $\rho_{j,k}$. The restriction classifier-stability field of \autoref{def:sheaf-indexed-section-presheaf-carrier} therefore gives the final displayed implication.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Sheaf restricted-open locality and gluing]
\label{thm:sheaf-restricted-open-locality-gluing}
Let $\mathcal{S}|_i$ be the restricted-open carrier of \autoref{def:sheaf-restricted-open-carrier}. Let $(j,\eta_j)$ be a restricted index and let $\mathcal{U}=(A,\iota,h,\gamma)$ be an indexed open cover of $j$ whose member inclusions satisfy $h_a:\iota(a)\le_{\mathcal{T}}j$. Regard it as a cover inside $\mathcal{S}|_i$ by assigning each member the composite witness
$$
\eta_{\iota(a)}:\iota(a)\le_{\mathcal{T}}i.
$$
Then locality and gluing hold in the restricted carrier. Thus, if $g,h':\mathsf{Sec}_{|i}(j,\eta_j)$ and
$$
\forall a:A,\quad
\rho^{|i}_{(j,\eta_j),(\iota(a),\eta_{\iota(a)})}(g)
\sim_{|i,(\iota(a),\eta_{\iota(a)})}
\rho^{|i}_{(j,\eta_j),(\iota(a),\eta_{\iota(a)})}(h'),
$$
then $g\sim_{|i,(j,\eta_j)}h'$. If $s_a:\mathsf{Sec}_{|i}(\iota(a),\eta_{\iota(a)})$ is compatible on the restricted cover, then there is $g:\mathsf{Sec}_{|i}(j,\eta_j)$ with
$$
\forall a:A,\quad
\rho^{|i}_{(j,\eta_j),(\iota(a),\eta_{\iota(a)})}(g)
\sim_{|i,(\iota(a),\eta_{\iota(a)})}
s_a.
$$
Compatible local families remain compatible after every further restriction inside $\mathcal{S}|_i$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
View the restricted cover as the ambient indexed open cover of $j$ with the same index type $A$, the same open-index map $\iota$, the same inclusion witnesses $h_a$, and the same cover law $\gamma$. The extra witnesses $\eta_{\iota(a)}$ only record that each member also lies below the fixed open $i$.

After unfolding \autoref{def:sheaf-restricted-open-carrier}, the displayed memberwise equality is exactly the ambient antecedent for the locality field of \autoref{def:sheaf-indexed-cover-compatibility-locality}. Applying that field to the ambient cover of $j$ gives $g\sim_j h'$, which is the restricted classifier equality at $(j,\eta_j)$.

For gluing, a compatible family in the restricted carrier is the same family $s_a:\mathsf{Sec}(\iota(a))$ with the same overlap equalities as in the ambient sheaf. Apply the gluing field of \autoref{def:sheaf-indexed-cover-compatibility-locality} to obtain $g:\mathsf{Sec}(j)$ whose ambient restrictions classify as $s_a$. Unfolding the restricted carrier turns those rows into the displayed restricted gluing rows.

It remains to check that compatibility is stable under further restriction inside the fixed open. Fix two local members and restrict them to a smaller restricted index below their overlap. The finite-intersection row of \autoref{def:topology-indexed-open-carrier} supplies the overlap inclusions, and \autoref{thm:sheaf-restricted-open-restriction-laws} supplies restriction composition along the chains through that overlap. Transporting the original compatibility row by the same theorem and composing the resulting classifier equalities proves that the further-restricted family is still compatible.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Sheaf restricted-open NameCert surface]
\label{thm:sheaf-restricted-open-namecert-surface}
For every $\mathsf{BHist}$-scoped indexed open topology carrier $\mathcal{T}$, indexed sheaf carrier $\mathcal{S}$ over $\mathcal{T}$, and open index $i:\mathsf{OpenIx}$, the restricted-open carrier $\mathcal{S}|_i$ supplies the paper-side $\NameCert_{\SheafUp}$ surface used by open-local consumers such as ringed-space and scheme certificates. Its rows are:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier: restricted indices $(j,\eta_j)$ with $j\le_{\mathcal{T}}i$ and inherited section carriers $\mathsf{Sec}(j)$;
\item classifier: the inherited section classifiers $\sim_j$;
\item stability: identity, composition, and classifier transport from \autoref{thm:sheaf-restricted-open-restriction-laws}, with classifier transport agreeing with \autoref{thm:sheaf-restriction-classifier-transport};
\item ledger: open-cover indices below $i$, cover-member inclusions, finite-intersection overlap rows, refinement maps, and restriction paths;
\item exactness: restricted locality and gluing from \autoref{thm:sheaf-restricted-open-locality-gluing}, stable under refinement by \autoref{thm:sheaf-refinement-compatible-family-pullback} and \autoref{thm:sheaf-refinement-gluing-descent};
\item standard bridge: absent, as in the global obligation surface of \autoref{thm:sheaf-namecert-obligation-surface}.
\end{enumerate}
Thus an open-local consumer may work entirely below $i$ while reusing the global $\NameCert_{\SheafUp}$ carrier, classifier, stability, ledger, exactness, and absent-standard-bridge rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier and classifier rows are immediate from \autoref{def:sheaf-restricted-open-carrier}: every restricted index names an ambient open below $i$, and no section carrier or classifier is rebuilt.

The stability row is supplied by \autoref{thm:sheaf-restricted-open-restriction-laws}. Its classifier-transport clause is the restricted form of \autoref{thm:sheaf-restriction-classifier-transport}, because both statements use the same ambient restriction map $\rho_{j,k}$ and the same classifier family $\sim$.

The ledger row is inherited from the ambient cover and refinement ledgers. Covers below $i$ are ambient covers whose target open also carries a witness to $i$, while refinement pullback and gluing descent are exactly the rows of \autoref{thm:sheaf-refinement-compatible-family-pullback} and \autoref{thm:sheaf-refinement-gluing-descent} read inside the fixed-open slice.

Finally, \autoref{thm:sheaf-restricted-open-locality-gluing} gives the exactness row for locality and gluing below $i$. The global obligation surface of \autoref{thm:sheaf-namecert-obligation-surface} already records that no standard bridge is attached, and restricting to a fixed open adds no such bridge. These rows are precisely the $\NameCert_{\SheafUp}$ surface required by open-local consumers.
\end{proof}
