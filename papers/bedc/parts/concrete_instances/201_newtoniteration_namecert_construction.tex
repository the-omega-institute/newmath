\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\NewtonIterationUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-newtoniteration-namecert}

The Newton-iteration interface $\NewtonIterationUp$ packages the iterative scheme $x_{n+1} = x_n - F(x_n)/F'(x_n)$ for finding zeros of a differentiable map, with quadratic convergence on a contraction neighbourhood. It depends on $\NameCert_{\DerivativeUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-derivative-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\BanachUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-banach-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:newtoniteration-carrier}

\begin{definition}[Newton-iteration finite step carrier]
\label{def:newtoniteration-bhist-carrier}
Fix a $\NameCert_{\DerivativeUp}$ derivative source row and a $\NameCert_{\BanachUp}$ normed-source row for the same displayed point context. A $\NewtonIterationUp$ carrier row is the finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ ledger recording the source point answer, the derivative answer row at that point, an inverse-row witness for the displayed derivative answer, the next-step answer row, and the $\Cont$ witnesses that place the step computation inside the derivative and Banach source ledgers.

The carrier records one finite Newton step or a finite list of such displayed steps. It does not assert convergence, completeness of the ambient space, a contraction theorem, or a radius of validity.
\end{definition}
\leandef{BEDC.Derived.NewtonIterationUp.NewtonIterationBHistCarrier}

\begin{definition}[Newton-iteration contraction classifier]
\label{def:newtoniteration-contraction-classifier}
Two rows of \autoref{def:newtoniteration-bhist-carrier} are classified as the same $\NewtonIterationUp$ step exactly when they have the same source point up to $\hsame$, the derivative rows are transported through $\NameCert_{\DerivativeUp}$, the inverse-row witnesses are matched as visible answer histories, and the next-step answer rows are related by $\hsame$ inside the same $\NameCert_{\BanachUp}$ source context.

The classifier is generated by derivative-source transport, Banach-source transport, inverse-row transport, next-step history transport, and finite concatenation of displayed step rows. It contains no convergence or completeness generator.
\end{definition}
\leandef{BEDC.Derived.NewtonIterationUp.NewtonIterationContractionClassifier}

\section{The certificate}

\begin{theorem}[Newton-iteration Banach and derivative source scope]
\label{thm:newtoniteration-banach-derivative-source-scope}
Every $\NewtonIterationUp$ carrier row has exactly two source certificates: the derivative source row from $\NameCert_{\DerivativeUp}$ and the normed-source row from $\NameCert_{\BanachUp}$. The point, derivative, inverse-row, and next-step data are finite answer histories attached to those sources by $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\Pkg$ witnesses.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack \autoref{def:newtoniteration-bhist-carrier}. The derivative and Banach certificates are explicit source fields, while the source point, derivative answer, inverse-row witness, and next-step answer are the finite displayed step ledger.

The classifier of \autoref{def:newtoniteration-contraction-classifier} can transport only those fields. Its source moves use the two named $\NameCert$ classifiers, and its computational moves use the displayed $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\Pkg$ witnesses, so no convergence or completeness datum enters the scope.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.NewtonIterationUp.NewtonIterationBHistCarrier\_banach\_derivative\_source\_scope}

\begin{theorem}[Newton-iteration step stability]
\label{thm:newtoniteration-step-stability}
Classified $\NewtonIterationUp$ step rows remain classified after transporting the derivative source through $\NameCert_{\DerivativeUp}$, transporting the Banach source through $\NameCert_{\BanachUp}$, and replacing the visible source point, inverse-row witness, or next-step answer by an $\hsame$-related row.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.NewtonIterationUp.NewtonIterationBHistCarrier\_step\_endpoint\_transport}
\leanvariant{BEDC.Derived.NewtonIterationUp.NewtonIterationStep\_stability}
\begin{proof}
First transport the source certificates. The derivative source gives the derivative-row comparison, and the Banach source gives the point and next-step context in which the row is read.

Next transport the displayed step data. The source point and next-step rows use $\hsame$ endpoint replacement; the inverse-row witness is carried as a visible answer history; and $\Cont$ repacks the transported fields into the same finite computation ledger. The generated classifier then supplies the classified step row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Newton-iteration obligation package]
\label{thm:newtoniteration-obligation-package}
The $\NewtonIterationUp$ chapter exports a finite obligation package consisting of the step carrier, the contraction classifier, the derivative and Banach source certificates, and ledger exactness for the displayed Newton step row. It does not claim convergence or completeness.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.NewtonIterationUp.NewtonIterationBHistCarrier\_semantic\_name\_certificate}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.NewtonIterationUp.NewtonIterationCarrier\_obligation\_package}
\begin{proof}
Build the package from a carried step row and a classifier witness. The carrier supplies the source point, derivative answer, inverse-row witness, next-step answer, and the $\Cont$ rows placing them under the two source certificates.

Ledger exactness follows by inverting the carrier and classifier constructors. Every accepted datum is one of the two source certificates, one of the finite step answer histories, or one of the named $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, or $\NameCert$ transport witnesses. Hence the package exposes only the displayed finite Newton step obligation.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Newton-iteration scoped kernel dependency boundary]
\label{thm:newtoniteration-scoped-kernel-dependency-boundary}
The $\NewtonIterationUp$ obligation package is scoped exactly by the $\DerivativeUp$ source row, the $\BanachUp$ source row, the finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ step ledger, the inverse-row witness, the next-step answer row, and the displayed $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ transport witnesses.

Equivalently, every obligation row exported by \autoref{thm:newtoniteration-obligation-package} is read from the carrier of \autoref{def:newtoniteration-bhist-carrier}, classified by \autoref{def:newtoniteration-contraction-classifier}, and bounded by the source-scope and stability rows of \autoref{thm:newtoniteration-banach-derivative-source-scope} and \autoref{thm:newtoniteration-step-stability}. Convergence, completeness, radius data, and host Newton data are outside this carrier.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.NewtonIterationUp.NewtonIterationBHistCarrier\_scoped\_kernel\_dependency\_boundary}
\begin{proof}
Start with an exported obligation row. By \autoref{thm:newtoniteration-obligation-package}, it is either one of the two source certificates, one of the finite step answer histories, or one of the displayed transport witnesses used to read that finite step.

Apply \autoref{def:newtoniteration-bhist-carrier} to identify the only carrier fields: the derivative source row, the Banach source row, the source point answer, the derivative answer, the inverse-row witness, the next-step answer, and the finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ ledger connecting those rows by $\Cont$. Apply \autoref{def:newtoniteration-contraction-classifier} to identify the only classifier moves: source transport, inverse-row transport, next-step transport, and finite concatenation of displayed step rows.

The source boundary is then fixed by \autoref{thm:newtoniteration-banach-derivative-source-scope}; there are no further source certificates beyond $\DerivativeUp$ and $\BanachUp$. The admissible replacements are fixed by \autoref{thm:newtoniteration-step-stability}; they use only $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ transport witnesses already visible in the carrier.

Therefore the dependency boundary is exactly the named kernel surface. Any convergence theorem, ambient completeness field, contraction radius, or host-level Newton recurrence would require data not present in the carrier or classifier, so those rows are not part of the scoped obligation package.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Newton-iteration finite-step concatenation closure]
\label{thm:newtoniteration-finite-step-concatenation-closure}
Fix a $\NameCert_{\DerivativeUp}$ derivative source row $D$ and a $\NameCert_{\BanachUp}$ normed-source row $B$ for the same displayed point context. Let $R_1$ and $R_2$ be accepted $\NewtonIterationUp$ carrier rows of \autoref{def:newtoniteration-bhist-carrier} over this same source pair. Write the source and next-step answer rows of $R_1$ as $x_0$ and $x_1$, and those of $R_2$ as $y_1$ and $x_2$. Let $\Lambda_1$ and $\Lambda_2$ be their displayed finite step ledgers, including the derivative-answer rows, inverse-row witnesses, and the $\Cont$ witnesses placing each displayed Newton step inside $D$ and $B$.

If $\hsame(x_1,y_1)$ and $\Lambda_{12}:\mathsf{BHist}$ is displayed with
$$
\Cont(\Lambda_1,\Lambda_2,\Lambda_{12}),
$$
then the displayed concatenated row $R_{12}$ over $D$ and $B$ is an accepted $\NewtonIterationUp$ carrier row. Its source-point answer is $x_0$, its next-step answer is $x_2$, and its finite step ledger is exactly the concatenated ledger $\Lambda_{12}$. The derivative-answer and inverse-witness subledgers of $R_{12}$ are the visible finite lists obtained by keeping the $R_1$ subledgers before the $R_2$ subledgers after transporting the splice endpoint along $\hsame(x_1,y_1)$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the carrier of \autoref{def:newtoniteration-bhist-carrier}. The row $R_1$ supplies a finite displayed Newton-step ledger from $x_0$ to $x_1$ under the derivative source $D$ and the Banach source $B$. The row $R_2$ supplies a finite displayed Newton-step ledger from $y_1$ to $x_2$ under the same two sources. Both ledgers are finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ rows, and their derivative-answer rows, inverse-row witnesses, next-step rows, and source-containment witnesses are already among the accepted carrier fields.

Use the splice comparison $\hsame(x_1,y_1)$ to read the source endpoint of $R_2$ as the next-step endpoint of $R_1$. This is exactly the endpoint replacement allowed by \autoref{thm:newtoniteration-step-stability}: the derivative and Banach source certificates remain $D$ and $B$, while the visible source point of the second row is transported through the displayed $\hsame$ witness. No analytic Newton equation, convergence datum, completeness datum, or radius condition is introduced by this transport.

Now concatenate the two finite ledgers by the displayed continuation row $\Cont(\Lambda_1,\Lambda_2,\Lambda_{12})$. Since $\Cont$ is the finite continuation relation on $\mathsf{BHist}$, the result is again a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ ledger. The first segment records exactly the step data already accepted in $R_1$, and the second segment records exactly the transported step data already accepted in $R_2$. At the splice, the outgoing answer of the first segment and the incoming answer of the second segment are identified by the given $\hsame(x_1,y_1)$ witness, so the displayed list has a single source endpoint $x_0$ and a single final next-step endpoint $x_2$.

The internal $\Cont$ witnesses remain valid after concatenation. Where a witness is wholly inside one segment, it is inherited from the corresponding accepted carrier row. Where a witness crosses the displayed grouping of the two ledgers, \autoref{thm:continuation-associativity} identifies the two possible groupings up to $\hsame$, and continuation result transport repacks the same finite history as $\Lambda_{12}$. Thus every derivative-source and Banach-source containment row required by the carrier definition is present in the concatenated display.

Finally apply the finite-concatenation generator in the classifier of \autoref{def:newtoniteration-contraction-classifier}. It classifies the two accepted displayed step rows, after the splice transport, as one accepted finite Newton-iteration row. Repacking these fields gives $R_{12}$ with source point $x_0$, next-step answer $x_2$, and finite step ledger $\Lambda_{12}$, as claimed.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.NewtonIterationUp.NewtonIterationBHistCarrier\_finite\_step\_concatenation\_closure}
\leanvariant{BEDC.Derived.NewtonIterationUp.NewtonIterationBHistCarrier\_finite\_step\_ledger\_concat\_closure}

\begin{theorem}[Newton-iteration splice source determinacy]
\label{thm:newtoniteration-splice-source-determinacy}
Let $R_{12}$ and $R'_{12}$ be accepted concatenated $\NewtonIterationUp$ carrier rows over the same $\NameCert_{\DerivativeUp}$ source row $D$, the same $\NameCert_{\BanachUp}$ source row $B$, the same initial endpoint $x_0$, the same final endpoint $x_2$, and the same displayed splice witness $\sigma:\hsame(x_1,y_1)$. Suppose their concatenation ledgers $\Lambda_{12}$ and $\Lambda'_{12}$ are related by $\hsame$ and arise from displayed continuation rows
$$
\Cont(\Lambda_1,\Lambda_2,\Lambda_{12})
\qquad\text{and}\qquad
\Cont(\Lambda'_1,\Lambda'_2,\Lambda'_{12})
$$
whose two segments are accepted Newton step ledgers over $D$ and $B$.

Then $R_{12}$ and $R'_{12}$ have the same source-boundary projection. Their derivative source is $D$, their Banach source is $B$, their inverse-row subledger lists are read as the same displayed finite lists up to the $\hsame$ transport of the concatenation ledgers, their next-step endpoint is $x_2$, and their finite ledger provenance is classified by the Newton-iteration contraction classifier of \autoref{def:newtoniteration-contraction-classifier}.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.NewtonIterationUp.NewtonIterationBHistCarrier\_splice\_source\_determinacy}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:newtoniteration-finite-step-concatenation-closure} to both displayed concatenations. Each accepted concatenated row is therefore obtained by repacking the same derivative source $D$, the same Banach source $B$, the same initial endpoint $x_0$, the same final endpoint $x_2$, and the same splice comparison $\sigma$ into the carrier of \autoref{def:newtoniteration-bhist-carrier}.

The carrier definition exposes no hidden source boundary. Its source projection consists only of the derivative source, the Banach source, the inverse-row answer histories, the next-step endpoint, and the finite $\Cont$ provenance attached to those rows. Thus two rows with the same displayed sources and endpoints can differ at the source boundary only by the listed ledger rows.

Use the hypothesis $\hsame(\Lambda_{12},\Lambda'_{12})$ to transport the continuation result. The continuation result transport repacks the $\Cont$ witnesses for the two displayed concatenations into the same finite history boundary, and \autoref{thm:newtoniteration-step-stability} transports the segment data through the shared splice witness $\sigma$.

Finally read the transported rows through the classifier of \autoref{def:newtoniteration-contraction-classifier}. Its generators identify derivative-source transport, Banach-source transport, inverse-row transport, next-step history transport, and finite concatenation, so the derivative source, Banach source, inverse-row subledger list, next-step endpoint, and finite ledger provenance have the same classified source-boundary projection.

No convergence datum, completeness datum, contraction radius, or analytic Newton theorem is introduced in this comparison. The statement is only a determinacy result for the finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ source boundary already present in the accepted carrier rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Newton-iteration finite-prefix restriction carrier]
\label{thm:newtoniteration-finite-prefix-restriction-carrier}
Fix a $\NameCert_{\DerivativeUp}$ derivative source row $D$ and a $\NameCert_{\BanachUp}$ normed-source row $B$ for the same displayed point context. Let $R_{n+k}$ be an accepted $\NewtonIterationUp$ carrier row of \autoref{def:newtoniteration-bhist-carrier} over $D$ and $B$ whose finite Newton ledger $\Lambda_{n+k}:\mathsf{BHist}$ is displayed as a prefix ledger followed by a tail ledger:
$$
\Cont(\Lambda_n,\Lambda_k,\Lambda_{n+k}).
$$
Assume that the displayed prefix ledger $\Lambda_n$ contains exactly the first $n$ source-point rows, next-step rows, function-value rows, derivative-answer rows, inverse-row witnesses, and derivative-nonzero denominator witnesses read from $R_{n+k}$, with endpoint rows $x_0$ and $x_n$. Then the row $R_n$ obtained by keeping $D$, $B$, $x_0$, $x_n$, and the displayed prefix subledgers is an accepted $\NewtonIterationUp$ carrier row. In particular, finite restriction to the first $n$ Newton recurrence steps preserves the iteration rows, the function-value rows, and the derivative-nonzero ledger without adding convergence, completeness, a contraction theorem, or a radius datum.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.NewtonIterationUp.NewtonIterationBHistCarrier\_finite\_prefix\_restriction}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the accepted carrier row $R_{n+k}$ using \autoref{def:newtoniteration-bhist-carrier}. Its public data are the two source certificates $D$ and $B$, the visible source-point and next-step histories, the derivative answer rows, the inverse-row witnesses for the displayed derivative answers, and the $\Cont$ witnesses placing the displayed Newton computations inside the derivative and Banach source ledgers. The accepted row contains no analytic convergence field and no ambient completeness field.

The displayed continuation
$$
\Cont(\Lambda_n,\Lambda_k,\Lambda_{n+k})
$$
exhibits $\Lambda_n$ as a finite initial subledger of the accepted finite ledger. Since $\Cont$ is generated only by the finite right-recursive continuation rules of \autoref{def:continuation-relation}, every row of $\Lambda_n$ is one of the visible rows already appearing in $\Lambda_{n+k}$ before the tail $\Lambda_k$ is read. Thus the first $n$ source-point rows, next-step rows, function-value rows, derivative-answer rows, inverse-row witnesses, and derivative-nonzero denominator witnesses are inherited as finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ rows from the accepted packet.

The source certificates do not change under restriction. The derivative rows in the prefix are still scoped by $D$, because they are the same derivative-answer and nonzero-denominator rows that $R_{n+k}$ placed under the derivative source. The point and next-step rows in the prefix are still scoped by $B$, because the Banach source containment witnesses are among the same retained $\Cont$ rows. If an endpoint of the retained prefix is represented by an $\hsame$-transported history, \autoref{thm:newtoniteration-step-stability} repacks the same visible source point, inverse-row witness, and next-step answer without changing the two source certificates.

It remains only to check that the retained ledger is a Newton-iteration carrier ledger rather than an arbitrary subhistory. By the hypothesis on $\Lambda_n$, each retained step is one of the first $n$ displayed Newton recurrence steps from $R_{n+k}$: it carries its source point, function value, derivative answer, inverse-row witness, nonzero-denominator witness, and next-step answer. The containment witnesses for those rows are inherited from the longer carrier, and no witness from the discarded tail $\Lambda_k$ is required to read a retained recurrence step. Repacking these retained fields gives exactly the carrier row described in \autoref{def:newtoniteration-bhist-carrier} over the same pair $D,B$.

The classifier of \autoref{def:newtoniteration-contraction-classifier} permits only source transport, inverse-row transport, next-step history transport, and finite concatenation of displayed step rows. Restricting to the first displayed segment uses none of the tail-specific data and introduces no additional generator. Therefore the prefix row $R_n$ is accepted as a $\NewtonIterationUp$ carrier row, with finite ledger $\Lambda_n$ and endpoints $x_0,x_n$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.NewtonIterationUp.NewtonIterationBHistCarrier\_finite\_prefix\_restriction\_carrier}
