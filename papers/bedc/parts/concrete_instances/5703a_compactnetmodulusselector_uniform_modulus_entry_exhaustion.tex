\begin{theorem}[Compact net modulus selector root-budget admission]
\label{thm:compact-net-modulus-selector-root-budget-admission}
For every accepted root-ready $\CompactNetModulusSelectorUp$ packet
$$
S=(X,Y,E,\mathcal{P},C,R,M,F,D,H,Q,P,N),
$$
the selector budget $D$ is admitted for downstream use only with the displayed finite probe spine $\mathcal{P}$, compact coverage row $C$, positive radius rows $R$, pointwise-modulus rows $M$, finite fold row $F$, selected precision row $D$, transport rows $H$, route rows $Q$, provenance row $P$, and local $\NameCert$ row $N$. Thus the budget is a finite root-ready handoff and not a parameter-only compactness promise.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the packet through \autoref{def:compact-net-modulus-selector-carrier} and \autoref{def:compact-net-modulus-selector-root-readiness}. These two displays enumerate exactly the root rows $X,Y,E,\mathcal{P},C,R,M,F,D,H,Q,P,N$ and assign the budget role to the selected precision row $D$.

Apply \autoref{thm:compact-net-modulus-selector-finite-net-exposure}. It binds $D$ to the nonempty finite probe spine, compact coverage rows, subordinate positive radius ledger, pointwise-modulus rows, and selected fold output.

Use \autoref{thm:compact-net-modulus-selector-totallybounded-handoff} to pass the same rows to the downstream compact-net and uniform-modulus consumers. The handoff keeps the finite fold and selected precision visible together with the transport, route, provenance, and local naming rows.

Finally invoke \autoref{thm:compact-net-modulus-selector-consumer-nonescape}. Since no consumer can leave the displayed packet, a budget read without any one of the root rows is not an admitted selector read.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Compact net modulus selector uniform-modulus entry exhaustion]
\label{thm:compact-net-modulus-selector-uniform-modulus-entry-exhaustion}
Every $\UniformModulusUp$ entry through a root-ready $\CompactNetModulusSelectorUp$ packet exhausts the visible route through $\CompactUp$, $\ContinuousFunctionUp$, $\MetricSpaceUp$, $\RatUp$, $\FiniteNetMinimumFoldUp$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$. No additional entry point supplies compactness, continuity, metric control, rational positivity, finite selection, history transport, package provenance, or local naming data.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with \autoref{def:compact-net-modulus-selector-carrier}. It lists the compact source rows, continuous pointwise-modulus rows, metric rows, rational tolerance and radius rows, finite fold data, history transport, continuation route, package provenance, and local name certificate.

\autoref{def:compact-net-modulus-selector-root-readiness} turns that carrier list into the root-ready packet. The displayed roles specify how $X,\mathcal{P},C,R,M,F,D,H,Q,P,N$ become the finite selector handoff.

Use \autoref{thm:compact-net-modulus-selector-finite-net-exposure} and \autoref{thm:compact-net-modulus-selector-totallybounded-handoff}. Together they force the selected precision read through the compact finite-net spine, pointwise-modulus rows, positive rational ledger, finite fold, and route rows before it reaches $\UniformModulusUp$.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:compact-net-modulus-selector-consumer-nonescape}. Since every downstream read factors through the root-ready tuple, the visible route is exhausted and no separate entry point can supply the omitted compactness, continuity, metric, rational, fold, history, transport, provenance, or naming data.
\end{proof}
