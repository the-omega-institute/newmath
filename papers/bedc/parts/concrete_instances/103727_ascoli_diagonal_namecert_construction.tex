\begin{definition}[AscoliDiagonal carrier]
\label{def:ascoli-diagonal-carrier}
An $\AscoliDiagonalUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
D=(X,E,Y,\mathcal{W},R,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ records compact source-net rows from the $\CompactMetricUp$ source,
including finite probe windows and their coverage witnesses;
\item $E$ records the $\EquicontinuityFamilyUp$ ledger that binds each target
tolerance request to a displayed source-radius row uniformly over the family
ledger;
\item $Y$ records the $\CompleteMetricUp$ target rows through which diagonal
pointwise reads are sealed;
\item $\mathcal{W}$ records finite diagonal $\StreamNameUp$ windows, with each
window naming the source-net points and family indices currently inspected;
\item $R$ records $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback rows for the target-distance and
tolerance witnesses visible on $\mathcal{W}$;
\item $S$ records the $\RealUp$ seal that repacks the window readbacks into the
target-side regular Cauchy boundary;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $X,E,Y,\mathcal{W},R,S$
and the structural rows;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay through compact source nets,
equicontinuity rows, target complete-metric rows, StreamName windows,
RegSeqRat readback, and the Real seal;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\AscoliDiagonalUp$,
$\ArzelaAscoliUp$, $\EquicontinuityFamilyUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$,
$\CompleteMetricUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$,
$\hsame$, and $\Cont$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\AscoliDiagonalUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exports only finite-window diagonal data. It has no coordinate for
an unbounded subsequence selector, an ambient compact-open topology, or a
choice principle over all family members.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[AscoliDiagonal NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:ascoli-diagonal-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\AscoliDiagonalUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier
admission for the packet of \autoref{def:ascoli-diagonal-carrier}; classifier
transport for compact source-net rows, equicontinuity-family ledgers,
complete-metric target rows, finite $\StreamNameUp$ diagonal windows,
$\RegSeqRatUp$ readbacks, and the $\RealUp$ seal; finite-window replay through
$H,C,P,N$; and non-escape from unbounded subsequence selection, ambient
function-space topology, and open-cover compactness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from
\autoref{def:ascoli-diagonal-carrier}. The compact source rows, family
equicontinuity rows, target completion rows, finite diagonal windows, readback
rows, Real seal, transport rows, continuation routes, provenance, and local
name are all displayed.

Classifier transport is componentwise. The source rows are transported through
the compact-metric finite nets, the family rows through the
EquicontinuityFamily ledger, and the target rows through the complete-metric
and Real readback boundaries.

Finite-window replay uses $\mathcal{W}$ and $R$. Each consumer sees only the
currently displayed diagonal window, its RegSeqRat readback rows, and the Real
seal $S$ carried through $H,C,P,N$.

The non-escape clauses follow by inspecting the carrier. No coordinate names an
unbounded subsequence selector, ambient compact-open topology, or open-cover
compactness object.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[AscoliDiagonal finite-window extraction]
\label{thm:ascoli-diagonal-finite-window-extraction}
Every public extraction read from an accepted $\AscoliDiagonalUp$ carrier
factors through the finite route
$$
\CompactMetricUp,\ \EquicontinuityFamilyUp,\ \CompleteMetricUp,\ \StreamNameUp,\ \RegSeqRatUp,\ \RealUp,\ \NameCert_{\AscoliDiagonalUp}.
$$
The route first reads compact source nets, then equicontinuity-family modulus
rows, then target complete-metric rows, then finite diagonal StreamName
windows, then RegSeqRat readback and the Real seal. It exports a finite-window
diagonal extraction surface and no choice-selected subsequence or compact-open
topological completion.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted carrier $D=(X,E,Y,\mathcal{W},R,S,H,C,P,N)$. The compact
source row $X$ supplies the finite source-net windows, and the equicontinuity
row $E$ supplies the shared modulus ledger for those windows.

The target row $Y$ supplies the complete-metric readback surface. Each diagonal
inspection is then restricted to the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window
$\mathcal{W}$, so the consumer sees only the displayed source-net points and
family indices.

The RegSeqRat row $R$ records the finite readback values for that window, and
the Real seal $S$ repacks the readback as the visible target boundary. Transport
by $H$ and replay through $C$ preserve the same rows, while $P$ and $N$ record
provenance and local naming.

Thus extraction is finite-window diagonal replay. Since the carrier contains no
unbounded subsequence selector, no ambient compact-open topology, and no
topological completion row, none of those objects is exported.
\end{proof}
