\label{def:mean-ergodic-carrier}

$$

\item $D$ is the $\DynSystemUp$ step row for the same transformation, recording the source point row, successor row, and finite iterate endpoints used by the orbit window.

\begin{theorem}[Mean ergodic NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:mean-ergodic-namecert-obligations}

\item carrier admission for the displayed ergodic source, dynamic step row, finite orbit windows, transfer handoff, Hardy--Cesaro averaging row, averaged endpoint rows, measure-preservation row, transport, replay, provenance, and local name;
\item orbit-window exactness, requiring every entry of $W$ to be read from the displayed $\DynSystemUp$ step ledger and the measure-preserving $\ErgodicUp$ source row;
\item Cesaro-average exactness, requiring every exposed average in $A$ to be computed by the $\HardyCesaroMeanUp$ row from the displayed finite orbit window and denominator row;
\item preservation stability, requiring the $\MeasureUp$ preservation row to be transported with the same $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ routes as the orbit-window source;
\item nonescape, requiring the packet to export no Hilbert projection, no pointwise convergence theorem, no invariant-function quotient, no completed orbit space, and no ambient spectral or Banach-space limit.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:mean-ergodic-carrier}. The visible packet lists exactly the ergodic source, dynamic steps, orbit windows, transfer rows, Cesaro averaging row, averaged endpoints, preservation row, transport rows, replay rows, provenance, and local name.

Orbit-window exactness is read from $D$ and $W$. Each window entry is generated by replaying the displayed $\DynSystemUp$ step row over the transformation already carried by $E$, so there is no independent trajectory object.

Cesaro-average exactness is read from $H$ and $A$. The averaging row consumes the finite window $W$, prefix sums, the visible denominator, and dyadic division data supplied by $\HardyCesaroMeanUp$ before an averaged endpoint can be exposed.

Preservation stability is then read from $P,R,C$. The same measure-preservation row is transported along the displayed $\hsame$ and replay routes used by the orbit-window source, so a measure claim cannot detach from the carried transformation.

Finally, nonescape follows from the carrier inventory. Since none of the coordinates names a Hilbert projection, pointwise convergence theorem, invariant quotient, completed orbit space, spectral theorem, or Banach limit, the local NameCert obligations are exhausted by the finite rows above.
\end{proof}
