\begin{theorem}[Helly theorem convex-hull forward link]
\label{thm:helly-theorem-convex-hull-forward-link}
Every convex-hull consumer of an accepted $\HellyTheoremUp$ packet must read the displayed finite family row, full-family intersection witness, Caratheodory-style finite subfamily row, $\ConvexSetUp$ classifier rows, and local $\NameCert$ provenance before it uses the Helly packet. The forward link factors through the Helly construction of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-helly-theorem-namecert}, the Caratheodory dependency route in \autoref{thm:helly-theorem-finite-subfamily-ledger-route}, and the convex-hull surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-caratheodory-convex-hull-namecert}. It supplies no full compact convexity theorem and no host separation theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the Helly carrier. Its finite family row is $F$, its full-family intersection witness is $I$, and its local naming and provenance are carried by $P,N$.

Use the existing Caratheodory forward dependency route. That route reads $\ConvexSetUp$ membership rows, $\PolytopeUp$ finite witnesses, affine and vector ambient rows, and compact finite-read boundaries before a Caratheodory convex-hull consumer can read a bounded support surface.

The present forward link names the same boundary for convex-hull consumers. The consumer may use the Helly packet only after the finite family, intersection witness, finite subfamily ledger, convex-set classifier rows, and local NameCert provenance have all been displayed.

Since the Helly carrier has no coordinate for compact convexity as a host theorem and no coordinate for a separation theorem, neither principle is exported by this link.
\end{proof}
