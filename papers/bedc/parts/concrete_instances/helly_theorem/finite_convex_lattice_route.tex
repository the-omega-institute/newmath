The finite subfamily ledger of $\HellyTheoremUp$ is read through the horizontal convex lattice before any convex-hull consumer may use it. The route first fixes the convex-family rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-convexset-namecert}, the finite witness rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-polytope-namecert}, the affine and vector ambient rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-affinespace-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-vecspace-namecert}, and the support-reduction boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-caratheodory-convex-hull-namecert}; only after those rows are displayed may the Helly finite subfamily ledger be consumed as convex-hull input.
