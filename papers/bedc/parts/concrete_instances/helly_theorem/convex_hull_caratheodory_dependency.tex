\paragraph{Convex-hull and Caratheodory dependency.}
The $\HellyTheoremUp$ packet consumes its finite convex-family carrier through the convex-set surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-convexset-namecert} and its affine-combination reduction through the Caratheodory convex-hull surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-caratheodory-convex-hull-namecert} before it exports an intersection certificate. Thus the Helly read is not an isolated incidence claim: it first exposes the convex-family rows, then the Caratheodory finite-combination rows, and only then packages the common-intersection row through its local $\NameCert_{\HellyTheoremUp}$ surface.
