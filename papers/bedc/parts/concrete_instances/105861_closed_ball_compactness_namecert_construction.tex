\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ClosedBallCompactnessUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-closed-ball-compactness-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges and Kelley, the $\ClosedBallCompactnessUp$ packet records the theorem-facing finite certificate that every displayed closed ball in a proper metric carrier is compact. It sits between the closed-ball membership surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metricclosedball-namecert}, the proper-metric witness of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-propermetric-namecert}, the nested-ball completion route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-nestedclosedball-namecert}, the compact-metric certificate of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, and the total-bounded plus complete-metric route through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-totallybounded-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completemetric-namecert}. The packet names the finite closed-ball compactness handoff only; it is not an open-cover compactness theorem, not a host Heine--Borel proof, not an arbitrary properness oracle, and not an ambient $\RealUp$ completeness principle.

\begin{definition}[Closed-ball compactness carrier]
\label{def:closed-ball-compactness-carrier}
A $\ClosedBallCompactnessUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
C=(B,P,N,K,T,M,R,H,D,Q,A)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $B$ is the $\MetricClosedBallUp$ row naming the center, radius, metric source, and closed membership comparison.
\item $P$ is the $\ProperMetricUp$ row whose displayed closed-ball family contains the same center-radius window.
\item $N$ is the $\NestedClosedBallUp$ route available for Cauchy-center and descending-ball handoff reads.
\item $K$ is the $\CompactMetricUp$ witness row attached to the displayed closed ball.
\item $T$ is the $\TotallyBoundedUp$ finite-net row exposed by the compact metric witness.
\item $M$ is the $\CompleteMetricUp$ limit-service row exposed by the compact metric witness and by the proper-metric completion handoff.
\item $R$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ readback row for center, radius, distance, and compactness-window comparisons.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the closed-ball, proper-metric, nested-ball, compact-metric, totally-bounded, complete-metric, real-readback, replay, provenance, and naming rows.
\item $D$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a closed-ball compactness request through $B,P,N,K,T,M,R$.
\item $Q$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ClosedBallCompactnessUp$, $\MetricClosedBallUp$, $\ProperMetricUp$, $\NestedClosedBallUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\TotallyBoundedUp$, $\CompleteMetricUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $A$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ClosedBallCompactnessUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through these rows, their displayed transports, their continuation routes, provenance, and local naming. It has no coordinate for open covers, selected subcovers, quotient metric points, a host compactness theorem, arbitrary properness beyond the shown ball, or ambient real completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Closed-ball compactness NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:closed-ball-compactness-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\ClosedBallCompactnessUp$ carrier
$$
C=(B,P,N,K,T,M,R,H,D,Q,A),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\ClosedBallCompactnessUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the metric closed-ball row, proper-metric row, nested-ball route, compact-metric witness, totally-bounded row, complete-metric row, real-readback row, componentwise transport, displayed continuation replay, package provenance, and local naming; center-radius compatibility between $B$ and the closed-ball family displayed by $P$; compact witness exactness through $K$; finite-net and complete-limit exposure through $T$ and $M$; and ledger refusal for open-cover compactness, selected subcovers, quotient metric points, host compactness, arbitrary properness outside the displayed ball, and ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:closed-ball-compactness-carrier}. The accepted packet displays the closed-ball row, the proper-metric source, the nested-ball route, the compact witness, the total-bounded and complete-metric witnesses, the real readback, and the structural rows.

Center-radius compatibility is forced before compactness is read. The row $B$ names the closed-ball membership comparison, and $P$ exposes the same center-radius window in its displayed proper-metric family, so a consumer cannot switch to another ball.

Compact witness exactness is the route through $K$. The finite-net face $T$ and the limit-service face $M$ are read only after $K$ has named the compact metric witness attached to $B$.

The structural rows $H,D,Q,A$ transport, replay, source, and name the same route. Since no excluded coordinate occurs in the carrier, open-cover compactness, selected subcovers, quotient point equality, host compactness, arbitrary properness outside the displayed ball, and ambient real completeness cannot enter the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Closed-ball compactness nested-ball handoff]
\label{thm:closed-ball-compactness-nested-ball-handoff}
Every nested-ball or completion-facing consumer of an accepted $\ClosedBallCompactnessUp$ carrier reads the closed ball through
$$
\MetricClosedBallUp,\quad \ProperMetricUp,\quad \NestedClosedBallUp,\quad \CompactMetricUp,\quad \TotallyBoundedUp,\quad \CompleteMetricUp,\quad \RealUp .
$$
The handoff therefore exposes the displayed closed-ball membership row, the proper-metric compact witness, the nested-ball Cauchy-center route, the finite-net face, and the complete-metric face, and it exports no open-cover compactness proof or choice-selected center.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the accepted carrier $C=(B,P,N,K,T,M,R,H,D,Q,A)$. The closed-ball request first reads $B$ and the matching proper-metric row $P$.

The nested-ball route $N$ is then available only as a displayed handoff. It supplies the descending-ball and Cauchy-center route used by completion consumers, while $K$ supplies the compact metric witness attached to the same closed ball.

Finally $T,M,R,H,D,Q,A$ expose the finite-net, limit-service, real-readback, transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows. Because the carrier has no selector coordinate, the consumer receives no open-cover compactness proof and no choice-selected center.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Closed-ball compactness nonescape]
\label{thm:closed-ball-compactness-nonescape}
Every public read of an accepted $\ClosedBallCompactnessUp$ packet factors through the carrier of \autoref{def:closed-ball-compactness-carrier} and the obligations of \autoref{thm:closed-ball-compactness-namecert-obligations}. In particular, the packet can export compactness for the displayed closed ball only as the route
$$
B,P,N,K,T,M,R,H,D,Q,A,
$$
and cannot export open-cover compactness, a selected finite subcover, quotient equality of metric points, a host Heine--Borel theorem, arbitrary properness beyond the displayed center-radius window, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The rows $B,P,N,K,T,M,R$ are the only mathematical rows available before structural replay, and the rows $H,D,Q,A$ are the only transport, continuation, provenance, and naming rows.

Apply \autoref{thm:closed-ball-compactness-namecert-obligations}. The local NameCert surface requires the closed-ball row, proper-metric row, nested-ball route, compact witness, total-bounded face, complete-metric face, and real-readback face to be admitted together.

Any public compactness read must therefore pass through the same finite tuple. Since the tuple has no coordinate for the excluded objects, those objects cannot escape through the packet.
\end{proof}
