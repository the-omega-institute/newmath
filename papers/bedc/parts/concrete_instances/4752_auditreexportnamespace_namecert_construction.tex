\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\AuditReexportNamespaceUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-auditreexportnamespace-namecert}
\origin{ai}

The $\AuditReexportNamespaceUp$ packet realizes the audit reexport namespace layer of \autoref{ch:twin-substrate-synthesis} as a finite NameCert carrier. It sits beside the twin-substrate packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-twinsubstratesynthesis-namecert} and the audit-map interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-auditmapinterface-namecert}: those siblings name the two-substrate architecture and the generic audit-map consumer surface, while this chapter names the concrete namespace-addressing packet through which downstream audit readers cite the two reexport namespaces without collapsing them.

\begin{definition}[Audit reexport namespace carrier]
\label{def:audit-reexport-namespace-carrier}
An $\AuditReexportNamespaceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
A=(M,G,S,L,B,R,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the \texttt{BEDC.MetaCIC.Audit} namespace row, recording the MetaCIC audit exports that may be cited as theorem-side audit addresses;
\item $G$ is the \texttt{BEDC.GroundCompiler.Audit} namespace row, recording the GroundCompiler audit exports that may be cited as compiler-side audit addresses;
\item $S$ records the substrate tag rows that classify each namespace address as MetaCIC-facing, GroundCompiler-facing, or a paired project-level citation;
\item $L$ is the export-address ledger, listing the finite public addresses exposed by $M$ and $G$ and the local citation names by which consumers reach them;
\item $B$ is the separation witness, marking compiler audit rows as unavailable as MetaCIC theorem rows and MetaCIC theorem rows as unavailable as compiler audit rows;
\item $R$ is the consumer route, recording which downstream audit-map, twin-substrate, or self-compilation reader consumes the namespace packet and through which displayed continuation;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over \autoref{ch:twin-substrate-synthesis}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-twinsubstratesynthesis-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-auditmapinterface-namecert}, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\AuditReexportNamespaceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate is $\mathsf{AuditReexportNamespacePacket}(A)$. Its public rows are exactly the two namespace rows, substrate tags, export-address ledger, separation witness, consumer route, package provenance, and local naming row.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Audit reexport namespace NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:audit-reexport-namespace-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\AuditReexportNamespaceUp$ carrier
$$
A=(M,G,S,L,B,R,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\AuditReexportNamespaceUp}$ surface consists of five displayed obligation rows: carrier admission for the finite packet of \autoref{def:audit-reexport-namespace-carrier}; namespace address readback for $M,G,L$; substrate-tag exactness for $S$; separation and nonconversion readback for $B$; and consumer-route, package, and local-name stability for $R,P,N$ under the displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the finite carrier. The tuple exposes the two namespace rows, the tag row, the ledger, the separation row, the consumer route, provenance, and local naming.

The first obligation is carrier admission for exactly those rows. The second reads the public namespace addresses through $M,G,L$, so a citation has a displayed export address before a consumer can use it.

The third and fourth obligations inspect $S$ and $B$. The tag row classifies each address by substrate, and the separation witness records that the two address classes are not conversion rules.

Finally $R,P,N$ bind the downstream route, package provenance, and local name to the same packet. Componentwise transport and continuation replay these rows without adding a hidden namespace equality, so the five displayed obligations exhaust the NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Audit reexport namespace non-collapse]
\label{thm:audit-reexport-namespace-noncollapse}
Every consumer of an accepted $\AuditReexportNamespaceUp$ carrier may cite \texttt{BEDC.MetaCIC.Audit} through $M$ and \texttt{BEDC.GroundCompiler.Audit} through $G$ only along the displayed address ledger $L$, substrate tag row $S$, separation witness $B$, and route $R$. Such a citation cannot convert a compiler audit row into a MetaCIC theorem row, cannot convert a MetaCIC theorem row into a compiler audit row, and cannot export a shared hidden equality between the two namespace layers.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the NameCert obligations. They force every consumer citation to pass through the visible namespace rows and export-address ledger.

If the cited address is MetaCIC-facing, $S$ tags it as a MetaCIC address and $B$ records that compiler audit evidence is not available as a theorem row. The consumer route $R$ may attach paired compiler context, but only as a ledgered citation through $G,L$.

If the cited address is GroundCompiler-facing, $S$ tags it as a compiler-side audit address and $B$ records that theorem rows are not compiler audit evidence. The paired MetaCIC context remains a cited namespace row, not a compiler artifact.

Because the packet has no coordinate identifying $M$ with $G$ and no conversion row between their evidence classes, the namespace layer gives two citeable audit surfaces while preserving the noncollapse boundary.
\end{proof}
