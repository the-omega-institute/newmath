\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ArchimedeanCompletionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-archimedean-completion-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges' constructive treatment of Archimedean ordered fields and regular Cauchy completions, the $\ArchimedeanCompletionUp$ packet records the finite naming surface that carries a dyadic ordered-field source into the Real and Completion route. It sits after the dyadic ordered core of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the ordered cut comparison of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bishop-order-completion-namecert}, and the completion witness of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-completeness-witness-namecert}. The packet names an Archimedean completion interface; it is not quotient equality of Cauchy streams, a selected host limit, a trichotomy oracle, countable choice, or ambient completeness of $\RealUp$.

\begin{definition}[Archimedean completion carrier]
\label{def:archimedean-completion-carrier}
An $\ArchimedeanCompletionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
A=(F,K,R,D,O,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $F$ is the dyadic ordered-field source row, including the finite addition, multiplication, order, unit, and sign-comparison data read from the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger.
\item $K$ is the Archimedean cofinality row: every displayed field element is bounded by a named dyadic scale already present in the same packet.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular-Cauchy readback row generated from the source and cofinality data.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger used to compare finite approximants and dyadic scales.
\item $O$ is the order-density comparison row linking the regular readback to the order-completion surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bishop-order-completion-namecert}.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal reached only after $F,K,R,D,O$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the ordered-field source, cofinality witness, regular readback, dyadic ledger, order comparison, Real seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from an Archimedean-completion request through $F,K,R,D,O,E$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ArchimedeanCompletionUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BishopOrderCompletionUp$, $\CauchyCompletenessWitnessUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ArchimedeanCompletionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $F,K,R,D,O,E,H,C,P,N$ and the displayed replay path. It contains no coordinate for quotient stream equality, selected representatives, host-limit choice, countable choice, trichotomy, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Archimedean completion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:archimedean-completion-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\ArchimedeanCompletionUp$ carrier $A=(F,K,R,D,O,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\ArchimedeanCompletionUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following five rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the finite dyadic ordered-field packet $F$ together with the Archimedean cofinality witness $K$;
\item regular-Cauchy readback through $R$ and dyadic tolerance accounting through $D$;
\item order-density comparison through $O$, with no independent located-cut or trichotomy oracle;
\item terminal $\RealUp$ handoff through $E$, reached only after the source, cofinality, readback, ledger, and order-comparison rows have been displayed;
\item componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming through $H,C,P,N$.
\end{enumerate}
No local obligation can be discharged by quotient Cauchy equality, selected host limits, countable choice, a detached order-completion theorem, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:archimedean-completion-carrier}. Its analytic rows are exactly the dyadic ordered-field source, the Archimedean cofinality witness, the regular-Cauchy readback, the dyadic tolerance ledger, the order-density comparison, and the terminal Real seal.

Carrier admission is the first row: $F$ supplies the finite ordered-field operations and comparisons, while $K$ supplies only the displayed cofinal dyadic scales. The readback row $R$ and ledger row $D$ then account for every regular-Cauchy and tolerance datum consumed by the packet.

The order-facing route is forced through $O$. It compares the same finite readback with the Bishop order-completion surface, but it cannot introduce a located-cut selector, trichotomy oracle, or host order equality because no such row belongs to the carrier.

Finally $E,H,C,P,N$ route the terminal Real handoff and structural support. Since the carrier has no quotient stream equality, selected limit, countable-choice schedule, detached completion theorem, or ambient-completeness coordinate, none of those objects belongs to the NameCert obligation surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Archimedean completion Real handoff]
\label{thm:archimedean-completion-real-handoff}
Every $\RealUp$ handoff exported by an accepted $\ArchimedeanCompletionUp$ carrier factors through the displayed dyadic, regular, and order-completion rows
$$
F,K \longmapsto R,D \longmapsto O \longmapsto E \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
The dyadic ordered-field source and Archimedean cofinality rows determine the regular-Cauchy readback; the dyadic ledger and order-density row compare that readback with the Bishop order-completion interface; the terminal Real seal is available only after those rows have been replayed.

Consequently a Real-facing consumer receives a finite NameCert route from dyadic ordered-field data to regular Cauchy and order-completion data. It receives no quotient stream equality, selected representative, host-limit operator, trichotomy oracle, countable-choice selector, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a consumer that reaches the terminal Real row. In the carrier of \autoref{def:archimedean-completion-carrier}, the only Real-facing coordinate is $E$, and $E$ is placed after $F,K,R,D,O$.

The consumer must therefore read the dyadic ordered-field source and cofinality rows before any regular-Cauchy data is available. The readback row $R$ and ledger row $D$ expose the finite approximation route, while the order-density row $O$ aligns that route with the Bishop order-completion packet.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ then transport, replay, source, and locally certify the same route. They preserve the displayed handoff, but they cannot add quotient equality, selected limits, trichotomy, countable choice, detached completion, or ambient Real completeness because the accepted packet has no such coordinate.
\end{proof}
