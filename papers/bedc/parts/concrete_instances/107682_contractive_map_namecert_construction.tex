\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ContractiveMapUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-contractive-map-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Banach and Bishop--Bridges constructive analysis, the $\ContractiveMapUp$ packet records a displayed contraction between metric carriers as finite NameCert data. It sits over the source and target metric rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the complete-metric consumer boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completemetric-namecert}, the tolerance export surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformmodulus-namecert}, the endpoint discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-banach-fixed-point-namecert}, and the finite orbit route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-picard-iteration-namecert}. The packet names the map-level shrink surface that Banach and Picard consumers can inspect before any fixed point, completed limit, quotient equality, or host function extensionality is available.

\begin{definition}[Contractive map carrier]
\label{def:contractive-map-carrier}
A $\ContractiveMapUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(X,Y,f,k,D,G,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the source $\MetricSpaceUp$ row and $Y$ is the target $\MetricSpaceUp$ row.
\item $f$ is the finite map-code row from carried source histories to carried target histories.
\item $k$ is the contraction-constant row, with displayed nonnegativity and strict unit-bound evidence.
\item $D$ is the metric-distance witness row recording source-distance and target-distance requests for the accepted map reads.
\item $G$ is the graph row witnessing that every accepted application of $f$ is a finite BEDC graph read.
\item $S$ is the shrink ledger recording each displayed comparison
$$
d_Y(f(x),f(y))\leq k\,d_X(x,y)
$$
for source histories carried by $X$ and graph reads carried by $G$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the source and target metric rows, map-code row, contraction constant, distance witnesses, graph row, shrink ledger, continuation rows, provenance row, and local naming row.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from source metric requests through $f,k,D,G,S$ to target metric responses, uniform-modulus handoff, Picard iteration reads, and fixed-point consumers.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ContractiveMapUp$, $\MetricSpaceUp$, $\CompleteMetricUp$, $\UniformModulusUp$, $\BanachFixedPointUp$, $\PicardIterationUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ContractiveMapUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a selected fixed point, a completed Picard limit, quotient equality of maps, host function extensionality, ambient completeness, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Contractive map NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:contractive-map-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\ContractiveMapUp$ carrier $K=(X,Y,f,k,D,G,S,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\ContractiveMapUp}$ surface consists exactly of source and target metric exposure, map-code admission, contraction-constant exactness, distance-witness exposure, graph replay, shrink-ledger replay, componentwise transport, displayed continuation, package provenance, and local naming through the rows $X,Y,f,k,D,G,S,H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier. The analytic rows are precisely the source metric $X$, target metric $Y$, map code $f$, contraction constant $k$, distance witnesses $D$, graph row $G$, and shrink ledger $S$.

The contraction obligation is replayed from the same rows. A target-distance read must pass through $G$, use the distance witnesses in $D$, and cite the comparison stored in $S$ against the same constant row $k$.

The structural rows preserve that packet and add no analytic coordinate. Transport through $H$, replay through $C$, provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$ keep the displayed route intact, so the listed rows exhaust the NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Contractive map Picard consumer handoff]
\label{thm:contractive-map-picard-consumer-handoff}
Every $\PicardIterationUp$ or $\BanachFixedPointUp$ consumer of an accepted $\ContractiveMapUp$ carrier receives contraction evidence only through
$$
X,Y,f,k,D,G,S,H,C,P,N.
$$
The handoff exports metric-distance shrink replay and the strict unit-bound row for $k$; it does not export fixed-point existence, complete-metric convergence, quotient equality of maps, a selected endpoint, or host function extensionality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:contractive-map-namecert-obligations}. They make the shrink ledger $S$ the only row that can justify a Picard or Banach contraction read.

A Picard consumer therefore reads the graph row $G$, the distance witnesses $D$, and the contraction constant $k$ before it can replay a one-step or finite-orbit shrink comparison. The continuation row $C$ transports this order to uniform-modulus, iteration, and fixed-point consumers without adding a limit point.

Completeness and endpoint selection remain outside the packet. A Banach consumer must attach its own complete-metric and endpoint rows, while the $\ContractiveMapUp$ handoff supplies only the finite map-level shrink data.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Contractive map constant-row exactness]
\label{thm:contractive-map-constant-row-exactness}
For every accepted $\ContractiveMapUp$ carrier $K=(X,Y,f,k,D,G,S,H,C,P,N)$, every contraction read exposed by $\NameCert_{\ContractiveMapUp}$ uses the displayed constant row $k$ together with its nonnegativity and strict unit-bound evidence. No Picard, Banach, or uniform-modulus consumer may replace $k$ by an unstored scalar or by a scalar recovered from ambient host data.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier and apply the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:contractive-map-namecert-obligations}. The obligation list has a single contraction-constant coordinate, namely the row $k$, and it records the strict unit-bound evidence as part of that row.

The Picard handoff of \autoref{thm:contractive-map-picard-consumer-handoff} exports the same row to downstream consumers. Since the handoff excludes host function extensionality, quotient equality of maps, completed limits, and selected endpoints, there is no second route from which a different contraction scalar can be read.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Contractive map distance-witness stability]
\label{thm:contractive-map-distance-witness-stability}
For every accepted $\ContractiveMapUp$ carrier $K=(X,Y,f,k,D,G,S,H,C,P,N)$, each source-distance or target-distance request used in a contraction comparison factors through the distance-witness row $D$. If the request involves a displayed application of $f$, the same read is tied to the graph row $G$ before it can be used by the shrink ledger $S$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier. The obligations in \autoref{thm:contractive-map-namecert-obligations} list distance-witness exposure and graph replay as separate rows, and the proof of that theorem identifies $D$ as the row carrying source-distance and target-distance requests.

A contraction comparison in $S$ is therefore not a free metric assertion. It first reads the relevant source and target distance witnesses through $D$, and when the comparison evaluates $f$ it reads the corresponding finite graph entry through $G$. The continuation row $C$ can replay this order, but it cannot bypass either displayed row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Contractive map shrink-ledger replay]
\label{thm:contractive-map-shrink-ledger-replay}
For every accepted $\ContractiveMapUp$ carrier $K=(X,Y,f,k,D,G,S,H,C,P,N)$, every shrink comparison available to $\PicardIterationUp$, $\BanachFixedPointUp$, or $\UniformModulusUp$ consumers is one of the comparisons stored in $S$ and transported, replayed, provenanced, and locally named through $H,C,P,N$. The carrier exposes no additional contraction inequality outside those rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier and read the shrink-ledger clause of \autoref{thm:contractive-map-namecert-obligations}. That clause makes $S$ the row recording the displayed comparisons
$$
d_Y(f(x),f(y))\leq k\,d_X(x,y)
$$
for carried source histories and graph reads.

The consumer handoff in \autoref{thm:contractive-map-picard-consumer-handoff} sends the same finite shrink data to Picard and Banach consumers, and the carrier definition routes uniform-modulus handoff through the same continuation surface. Componentwise transport through $H$, displayed replay through $C$, provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$ preserve the stored ledger entries rather than generating fresh analytic inequalities.
\end{proof}
