The $\TowerEquivalenceUp$ packet realizes the tower-equivalence boundary of \autoref{sec:approximation-towers-ledgered-compression} as a finite BEDC NameCert carrier. It consumes the tower packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-realityconstrainedtowerpacket-namecert}, the indexed approximation tower of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-reality-constrained-approximation-tower-namecert}, the $\PhysicalFitTowerUp$ physical-fit descent surface, the open-fit refutation surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-reality-constrained-open-fit-namecert}, and the observer-invariant surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-reality-constrained-objectivity-namecert}. Those siblings supply tower carriers, indexed approximation rows, physical descent data, open refutation, and observer-stability rows; this packet binds endpoint agreement to ledger and descent compatibility before any consumer treats two towers as equivalent.

\begin{definition}[TowerEquivalence carrier]
\label{def:tower-equivalence-carrier}
A $\TowerEquivalenceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
E=(T,T',A,A',P,P',O,O',B,L,D,R,H,Q,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $T$ and $T'$ are the two $\RealityConstrainedTowerPacketUp$ rows whose endpoint packages are being compared.
\item $A$ and $A'$ are the $\RealityConstrainedApproximationTowerUp$ rows exposing the indexed approximation levels of the two towers.
\item $P$ and $P'$ are the $\PhysicalFitTowerUp$ rows recording source-facing signatures, hidden-source ledgers, and descent policies.
\item $O$ and $O'$ are the $\RealityConstrainedOpenFitUp$ rows recording open-continuation and finite-refutation surfaces for the two endpoint reads.
\item $B$ is the $\RealityConstrainedObjectivityUp$ row that transports the comparison across the admitted observer anchors without selecting a global observer.
\item $L$ is the paired ledger row. It records source-history agreement, hidden-residue agreement, omitted-row exposure, and every open adequacy gap that remains visible after endpoint comparison.
\item $D$ is the descent-compatibility row. It lists exactly which operations descend through both towers and records refusal for any operation lacking a paired displayed route.
\item $R$ is the endpoint-agreement row, comparing the terminal packages only under the displayed classifier bundle.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $T,T',A,A',P,P',O,O',B,L,D,R,Q,N$.
\item $Q$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over the sibling carriers, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\TowerEquivalenceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for endpoint-only equivalence, no hidden route from terminal agreement to source identity, no descent outside $D$, and no observer-stable claim outside $B,L,H$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[TowerEquivalence NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:tower-equivalence-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:tower-equivalence-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\TowerEquivalenceUp}$ surface consists exactly of five obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for $E=(T,T',A,A',P,P',O,O',B,L,D,R,H,Q,N)$;
\item endpoint agreement through $R$ together with the two tower-packet rows $T,T'$;
\item ledger compatibility through $L$ together with the approximation rows $A,A'$, physical-fit rows $P,P'$, and open-fit rows $O,O'$;
\item descent compatibility through $D$ and observer-stable transport through $B,H$;
\item provenance and local naming closure through $Q,N$.
\end{enumerate}
Every accepted tower-equivalence read must display these five rows before exporting equivalence between the two towers.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.TowerEquivalenceUp.TowerEquivalence\_namecert\_obligations}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:tower-equivalence-carrier}. The admitted packet lists the two tower rows, the two approximation rows, the two physical-fit rows, the two open-fit rows, the objectivity row, the paired ledger, the descent row, endpoint agreement, transport, provenance, and local naming.

Endpoint agreement is therefore only one coordinate of the packet. The local certificate reads it through $R$ together with $T$ and $T'$, so terminal package agreement cannot by itself certify the two towers.

Ledger compatibility is read next. The rows $A,A',P,P',O,O'$ expose indexed approximation, physical signature, descent policy, open continuation, and finite refutation data on both sides, while $L$ records source-history and hidden-residue agreement across those rows.

Descent and observer stability close the comparison. The row $D$ lists exactly the operations that descend through both towers, and $B,H$ preserve the comparison across observer anchors and componentwise transport. The provenance and local name rows then name the same displayed packet, giving exactly the five listed obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[TowerEquivalence endpoint refusal]
\label{thm:tower-equivalence-endpoint-refusal}
Let
$$
E=(T,T',A,A',P,P',O,O',B,L,D,R,H,Q,N)
$$
be an accepted $\TowerEquivalenceUp$ packet. Endpoint agreement through $R$ does not by itself export tower equivalence. A consumer route is accepted only when the paired ledger $L$, descent row $D$, objectivity row $B$, and componentwise transport row $H$ travel with the same endpoint comparison.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:tower-equivalence-namecert-obligations}. The endpoint row $R$ appears only as one row of a five-row local surface.

The paired ledger $L$ records the source histories, hidden residues, omitted rows, and open adequacy gaps that endpoint comparison may hide. Removing $L$ leaves no displayed evidence that the two towers agree below the terminal package.

The descent row $D$ and objectivity row $B$ are also mandatory. Without $D$, an operation may descend through one tower but not the other; without $B,H$, the comparison may depend on a privileged observer anchor or an untransported component. Thus endpoint agreement alone is refused as tower equivalence.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.TowerEquivalenceUp.TowerEquivalenceCarrier\_endpoint\_refusal}

\begin{theorem}[TowerEquivalence ledger-descent stability]
\label{thm:tower-equivalence-ledger-descent-stability}
Every accepted $\TowerEquivalenceUp$ transport preserves ledger and descent compatibility componentwise. If $H$ transports $E$ to another accepted carrier, then the transported paired ledger still records the same source-history, hidden-residue, omitted-row, and adequacy-gap comparison, and the transported descent row still refuses exactly the operations not displayed on both towers.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the carrier definition. The transport row $H$ is componentwise over the tower rows, approximation rows, physical-fit rows, open-fit rows, objectivity row, ledger, descent row, endpoint row, provenance, and local name.

The ledger row $L$ is one of those transported components, so source-history agreement, hidden-residue agreement, omitted-row exposure, and open adequacy gaps cannot be dropped by transport. They remain attached to the same comparison after transport.

The descent row $D$ is transported in the same packet. Since $D$ lists only paired displayed descent routes, any operation missing on either side remains outside the accepted transported descent row.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.TowerEquivalenceUp.TowerEquivalence\_ledger\_descent\_stability}

\begin{theorem}[TowerEquivalence refinement separation]
\label{thm:tower-equivalence-refinement-separation}
Two $\TowerEquivalenceUp$ candidates may agree at the endpoint row $R$ and still be separated by a refinement that exposes a difference in $L$ or $D$. Such a separation does not contradict endpoint agreement; it shows that the endpoint comparison was not a complete tower-equivalence certificate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with \autoref{thm:tower-equivalence-endpoint-refusal}. Endpoint agreement is not the accepted certificate unless the paired ledger, descent row, objectivity row, and transport row are present in the same packet.

A refinement may inspect a source-history residue, omitted physical-signature row, open-fit refutation row, or operation descent route that was invisible to the endpoint classifier. Those data live in $L$ and $D$, not in $R$ alone.

If the refinement separates the candidates through $L$ or $D$, the result is exactly the refusal boundary: the endpoint comparison remains an endpoint comparison, while the full tower-equivalence carrier is not accepted.
\end{proof}
