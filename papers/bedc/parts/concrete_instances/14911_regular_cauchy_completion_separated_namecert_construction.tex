It reads the regular source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the finite-window schedule of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the metric-completion handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metriccompletion-namecert}, the zero-distance classifier route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separatedmetric-namecert}, the completion uniqueness ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completion-uniqueness-namecert}, and the regular universal-property surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-completion-universal-namecert}.

The packet is a local L10 bridge. It names the rows by which regular Cauchy names enter a completion-facing metric route, get compared through separated zero-distance evidence, and then feed the uniqueness ledger. It does not turn regular names into quotient streams or assert completeness of an ambient host real line.

\begin{theorem}[RegularCauchyCompletionSeparated NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-completion-separated-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\RegularCauchyCompletionSeparatedUp$ carrier $Q=(S,W,D,R,M,Z,U,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyCompletionSeparatedUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed regular source, finite windows, dyadic tolerance ledger, regular comparison row, metric-completion handoff, separatedness row, completion uniqueness ledger, transports, replay routes, provenance, and local naming row;
\item classifier stability under the componentwise $\hsame$ transports $H$ and the replay routes $C$;
\item regular-source compatibility, requiring completion consumers to read $S,W,D,R$ before the metric handoff $M$;
\item separatedness compatibility, requiring any identification of displayed completion readings to pass through the zero-distance row $Z$;
\item uniqueness-ledger exactness, requiring comparison of two completion-facing presentations to be exhausted by the ledger $U$ over the same displayed route.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-completion-separated-carrier}. The tuple displays the regular source, stream windows, dyadic tolerances, regular comparison row, metric-completion handoff, separatedness row, uniqueness ledger, and the structural support rows.

Classifier stability is componentwise. The transports in $H$ move each displayed coordinate without changing the order of dependency, and the continuation row $C$ replays the same route after transport.

The remaining obligations are sibling handoffs. The regular-source compatibility clause reads through $S,W,D,R$ before $M$; the separatedness clause reads through $Z$ after the metric-completion handoff; and the uniqueness exactness clause reads through $U$ only after the same finite route has been projected.

No carrier coordinate contains quotient streams, selected host limits, host real equality, countable choice, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, open-cover compactness, or trichotomy. The listed obligations therefore exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Regular separated completion route]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-completion-separated-separation-route}
Let $Q=(S,W,D,R,M,Z,U,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\RegularCauchyCompletionSeparatedUp$ packet. Every separated comparison exported by $Q$ factors through the route
$$
S,\quad W,\quad D,\quad R,\quad M,\quad Z .
$$
Thus a completion-facing consumer identifies two displayed readings only by projecting the regular source, scheduled windows, tolerance ledger, comparison row, metric-completion handoff, and zero-distance separation row. The conclusion is classifier equivalence inside the displayed separated metric surface, not equality of quotient streams or equality in a host real line.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The first four rows $S,W,D,R$ keep the comparison attached to a regular Cauchy name inspected through finite windows and dyadic tolerances.

Read the metric-completion handoff only after those rows have been fixed. The handoff $M$ supplies completion-facing evidence over the displayed regular route, so a consumer cannot replace it by a selected host limit.

Apply the separatedness row $Z$ to the displayed zero-distance reading. The replay row $C$ exposes exactly this route, and the transport row $H$ moves it componentwise without adding a host equality coordinate.

Since the packet carries no quotient stream, host real equality, trichotomy, countable choice, open-cover compactness, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness field, every separated comparison is the displayed finite route.
\end{proof}
