\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CauchyModulusArithmeticUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-cauchymodulusarithmetic-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, explicit Cauchy
moduli are part of the data by which regular rational sequences are consumed:
addition, multiplication, and common-tail comparison require visible schedules
rather than a hidden appeal to completed real equality. The
$\CauchyModulusArithmeticUp$ packet records this arithmetic surface as a finite
NameCert bridge between the modulus chapters
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchymodulus-namecert} and
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchymodulusmeet-namecert}, the regular
operation chapters \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regularcauchysum-namecert}
and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regularcauchyproduct-namecert}, and the
real seal surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}.

\begin{definition}[Cauchy-modulus arithmetic carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-modulus-arithmetic-carrier}
A $\CauchyModulusArithmeticUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
A=(S_0,S_1,\mu_0,\mu_1,\mu_{\wedge},\sigma,\pi,D,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S_0$ and $S_1$ are the two $\StreamNameUp$ windows whose rational
observations are read back through $\RegSeqRatUp$ rows.
\item $\mu_0$ and $\mu_1$ are the two source $\CauchyModulusUp$ schedules,
each carrying its own precision request, threshold row, and late-pair ledger.
\item $\mu_{\wedge}$ is the shared-threshold $\CauchyModulusMeetUp$ row that
places the two source schedules on one dyadic tolerance surface.
\item $\sigma$ and $\pi$ are the $\RegularCauchySumUp$ and
$\RegularCauchyProductUp$ consumer rows; they read only the two source
schedules, the shared meet row, and the regular rational readbacks.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, recording the split
tolerances for the sum route, the product route, and the shared meet route.
\item $W$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window ledger that names the inspected
late observations for both source streams and both arithmetic consumers.
\item $R$ stores the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readbacks obtained from the displayed
windows, tolerance rows, and modulus schedules.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached only through the regular rational
sum or product handoff.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the two streams, two
source schedules, shared meet row, sum row, product row, dyadic tolerance
ledger, window ledger, regular readbacks, real seal rows, continuation rows,
provenance, and local name.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a precision request through
the two source schedules and the shared meet row, then through $D,W,R$ to
$\sigma$, $\pi$, and $E$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyModulusUp$,
$\CauchyModulusMeetUp$, $\RegularCauchySumUp$,
$\RegularCauchyProductUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$,
$\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyModulusArithmeticUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only by transporting these displayed
rows to a common finite surface. It has no coordinate for quotient streams,
selected limits, host real equality, or ambient completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy-modulus arithmetic NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-modulus-arithmetic-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CauchyModulusArithmeticUp$ carrier
$A=(S_0,S_1,\mu_0,\mu_1,\mu_{\wedge},\sigma,\pi,D,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local
$\NameCert_{\CauchyModulusArithmeticUp}$ surface consists exactly of five
obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed packet with two source modulus
schedules, one shared meet row, two arithmetic consumer rows, tolerance
ledgers, windows, readbacks, seal rows, transport, continuation, provenance,
and local name;
\item classifier transport to a common $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$,
$\CauchyModulusUp$, $\CauchyModulusMeetUp$, and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ surface;
\item stability of $\mu_0$, $\mu_1$, $\mu_{\wedge}$, $\sigma$, and $\pi$
under the componentwise $\hsame$ rows recorded in $H$;
\item exactness of the dyadic tolerance ledger $D$ as the only source of
split-tolerance and shared-threshold arithmetic reads;
\item $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, $\RealUp$ handoff, and local
NameCert rows for exactly the displayed packet.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored outside those five rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-modulus-arithmetic-carrier}. The
projection exposes precisely the two stream windows, two source modulus
schedules, shared meet row, sum and product consumers, tolerance ledger,
regular readbacks, real seal rows, transport rows, continuation rows,
provenance row, and local name.

Classifier transport is componentwise. The rows in $H$ move the two source
schedules, the shared meet schedule, and the two arithmetic consumers to the
same displayed stream, regular rational, and dyadic tolerance surface.

The dyadic exactness row is exhausted by $D$. The sum route reads split
tolerances from $D$, the product route reads product bounds from $D$, and the
meet route reads its shared tolerance from the same finite ledger.

Finally replay $C$ and attach $P,N$. These rows route, source, and name the
already displayed packet; they do not introduce quotient streams, selected
limits, host real equality, or ambient completeness. Hence the five listed
rows exhaust the local naming obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy-modulus arithmetic sum and meet closure]
\label{thm:cauchy-modulus-arithmetic-sum-meet-closure}
Let $A$ be an accepted $\CauchyModulusArithmeticUp$ carrier. Every displayed
precision request for the $\RegularCauchySumUp$ row $\sigma$ is answered by
first reading the two source schedules $\mu_0,\mu_1$, then reading the shared
meet row $\mu_{\wedge}$, and then reading the split dyadic tolerance ledger
$D$. The resulting sum schedule is a finite $\CauchyModulusUp$ read over the
same $\StreamNameUp$ windows and $\RegSeqRatUp$ readbacks, and its shared
threshold is stable under the $\CauchyModulusMeetUp$ transport rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a precision request for $\sigma$. Carrier admission exposes the two
source schedules and the shared meet row before the sum row can be consumed.

Read $D$ at the requested tolerance. Its split-tolerance rows provide the
dyadic requests sent to $\mu_0$ and $\mu_1$, while its shared-threshold row is
the tolerance read by $\mu_{\wedge}$.

Replay the $\Cont$ rows from $C$. They inspect only the windows in $W$ and the
regular rational readbacks in $R$, so the produced sum schedule remains a
finite modulus read over the displayed packet.

Stability follows from $H$ and the meet obligation surface. The shared meet
row transports the two source schedules together, and the sum consumer can
therefore reuse the same threshold packet after componentwise $\hsame$
transport.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy-modulus arithmetic Real consumer boundary]
\label{thm:cauchy-modulus-arithmetic-real-consumer-boundary}
Every $\RealUp$ consumer of a $\CauchyModulusArithmeticUp$ packet must factor
through the displayed route
$$
S_0,S_1,\mu_0,\mu_1,\mu_{\wedge},\sigma,\pi,D,W,R,E,H,C,P,N.
$$
The consumer may read the regular sum or product handoff and then the real
seal row $E$, but it cannot extract quotient stream equality, selected limits,
host real equality, or ambient completeness from the arithmetic carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a $\RealUp$ consumer route. The carrier has no real-facing
coordinate before the regular rational readback rows $R$ and the seal row $E$.

For the sum route, \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-arithmetic-sum-meet-closure}
forces the consumer through $\mu_0,\mu_1,\mu_{\wedge},D,W,R$ before $\sigma$
can be sealed. For the product route, the carrier definition gives the same
source schedules, shared meet row, dyadic ledger, windows, and readbacks before
$\pi$ can be sealed.

The remaining rows $H,C,P,N$ only transport, replay, source, and name that
finite route. Since the carrier exposes no quotient stream row, selected-limit
row, host real equality row, or ambient-completeness row, no $\RealUp$ consumer
can extract such data from the arithmetic packet.
\end{proof}
