\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CausalContinuitySocketUp$}

\origin{ai}

The $\CausalContinuitySocketUp$ packet realizes the Type IV causal-continuity socket as a finite certificate surface. It keeps record-side regularity, proposed prediction shape, and the audit gate in separate rows, so a successful finite record cannot be read as an internal future-law theorem.

The Type IV gate row $G$ is the causal-continuity socket-kind row consumed from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-transcendentalsupplytaxonomy-namecert} before the local socket site $S$ is read. The taxonomy supplies the socket-kind ledger; $\CausalContinuitySocketUp$ specializes that row to the record, prediction, gate, and site packet below.

The Type IV gate row should be read as a causal-continuity specialization of the finite refusal boundary in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-negativecertificateboundary-namecert}, with $R,P,G,S$ replacing socket, refused-internalizer, $\mathsf{GAP}$, and audit rows for the record-to-prediction passage.

This socket is consumed by the inter-Hist causal-rate chain, especially \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-crosshistcausalrate-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-interhistinvariant-namecert}, because those chapters need finite record and prediction audit rows before any rate or invariant consumer may read a future-facing route.

\begin{definition}[Causal continuity socket carrier]
\label{def:causal-continuity-socket-carrier}
A $\CausalContinuitySocketUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
X=(R,P,G,S,H,C,Q,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $R$ is the bounded record-regularity row, recording the finite observation window, its accepted regularity summary, and the last record boundary;
\item $P$ is the proposed prediction-shape row, recording the not-yet-observed continuation pattern as a request shape rather than as a theorem of persistence;
\item $G$ is the $\mathsf{GAP}$ and audit-gate row, recording the Type IV question that separates record support from causal-continuity commitment;
\item $S$ is the socket-site row, recording the manuscript, observation, or certificate location where the prediction shape is requested from the record;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $R,P,G,S$, the continuation row, the provenance row, and the local naming row;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which consumers inspect the record row, prediction-shape row, audit gate, socket site, provenance, and local certificate;
\item $Q$ records $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, bounded records, prediction-shape ledgers, $\mathsf{GAP}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and \NameCert{};
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CausalContinuitySocketUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a universal future law, selected unseen observation, completed empirical stream, host causality principle, or theorem converting finite regularity into persistence.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Causal continuity record-prediction separation]
\label{thm:causal-continuity-socket-record-prediction-separation}
For an accepted $\CausalContinuitySocketUp$ carrier, the record-side row $R$ and prediction-shape row $P$ remain distinct displayed coordinates. A downstream consumer may cite $R$ as bounded regularity evidence and may cite $P$ as a requested continuation shape, but no consumer may convert the pair $(R,P)$ into a future-law commitment without passing through the Type IV gate row $G$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier $X$. The record evidence, prediction shape, and gate are the separate rows $R,P,G$.

The row $R$ records only bounded observation data and the accepted finite regularity summary. The row $P$ records only the requested continuation shape, not its fulfillment.

Any route from record support to prediction use must therefore pass through $G$ and the socket-site row $S$. Since the carrier contains no future-law coordinate, the pair $(R,P)$ cannot itself supply causal continuity.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Causal continuity audit gate]
\label{thm:causal-continuity-socket-audit-gate}
The audit gate of an accepted $\CausalContinuitySocketUp$ packet is exactly the row $G$ attached to the record row $R$, prediction row $P$, and socket site $S$. A use of record regularity for not-yet-observed history is visible precisely when it factors through
$$
R,P,G,S,H,C,Q,N.
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the record and prediction sides first. The row $R$ supplies the bounded regularity evidence, and $P$ supplies the proposed continuation shape.

The gate row $G$ is the displayed Type IV audit question linking those two rows at the site $S$. Thus visible use of the prediction route is use through the gate and site rows.

The rows $H,C,Q,N$ transport, replay, package, and name the same visible gate. A route omitting $G$ has no accepted coordinate that marks record-to-prediction passage, so it is not an accepted visible use.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Causal continuity socket non-escape]
\label{thm:causal-continuity-socket-nonescape}
Every downstream consumer of an accepted $\CausalContinuitySocketUp$ packet factors through the finite rows
$$
R,P,G,S,H,C,Q,N.
$$
The consumer may inspect bounded record regularity, proposed prediction shape, the Type IV gate, socket site, transport rows, continuation routes, package provenance, and the local $\NameCert$ row. It cannot extract a universal future law, selected unseen observation, completed empirical stream, host causality principle, or persistence theorem from the packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the carrier definition. The packet exposes exactly the rows $R,P,G,S,H,C,Q,N$.

The record-prediction separation theorem confines the evidential read to $R$ and the requested continuation shape to $P$. The audit-gate theorem then requires $G$ and $S$ for any visible route from one side to the other.

The remaining rows only transport, replay, package, and name this same finite packet. Because no future-law or unseen-observation coordinate exists in the carrier, no downstream consumer can project such a coordinate from it.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Causal continuity socket NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:causal-continuity-socket-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\CausalContinuitySocketUp}$ surface for \autoref{def:causal-continuity-socket-carrier} consists exactly of carrier admission for
$$
X=(R,P,G,S,H,C,Q,N),
$$
bounded record-regularity exposure, prediction-shape display, Type IV audit-gate exactness, socket-site locality, componentwise $\hsame$ stability, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, local naming, and refusal to internalize causal continuity as a theorem of the record.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from the displayed packet. The rows $R,P,G,S,H,C,Q,N$ are the complete local inventory.

The record and prediction obligations are read from $R$ and $P$. They expose bounded regularity and proposed continuation shape while keeping them separate.

The gate and site obligations are read from $G$ and $S$, and the transport and replay obligations are read from $H$ and $C$. Finally $Q$ and $N$ record provenance and local naming.

Together these rows give the NameCert surface. Since the inventory contains no causal-continuity theorem or completed future-history coordinate, the certificate names the socket without internalizing the requested persistence.
\end{proof}
