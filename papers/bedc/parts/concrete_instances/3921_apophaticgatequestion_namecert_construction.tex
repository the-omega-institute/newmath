\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ApophaticGateQuestionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-apophaticgatequestion-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\ApophaticGateQuestionUp$ packet realizes the name-to-gate correspondence as a finite question object. It starts from an apophatic socket name, records the gate question that audits the named outside-supply position, keeps the refusal ledger visible, and exposes only the readback rows needed by downstream audit gates.

The socket row is consumed from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-apophaticsocket-namecert}, and the named refusal row remains compatible with \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-apophaticname-namecert}. Thus the gate-question packet reads the same sibling socket-name surfaces before it exposes its audit-readback row.

\begin{definition}[Apophatic gate question carrier]
\label{def:apophatic-gate-question-carrier}
An $\ApophaticGateQuestionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
G=(S,Q,R,A,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the visible $\ApophaticSocketUp$ or $\ApophaticNameUp$ row naming the external supply position;
\item $Q$ is the gate-question row asking whether a consumer route used the named socket, which supply kind it requested, and whether the use remained externally marked;
\item $R$ is the refusal-ledger row excluding hidden axioms, forbidden host ancestry, quotient collapse, proposition-extensionality collapse, unmarked selectors, and unrecorded inscription novelty;
\item $A$ is the audit-readback row that records the answer to $Q$ as a finite ledger entry rather than as a host-level judgment;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the socket row, question row, refusal ledger, readback row, provenance row, and local naming row;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which audit, paper-Lean, axiom-purity, and conservativity consumers may read $S,Q,R,A,H,P,N$;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, $\ApophaticSocketUp$, $\ApophaticNameUp$, audit gates, refusal ledgers, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and \NameCert{} rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ApophaticGateQuestionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for the requested outside supply itself, a hidden theorem premise, an axiom channel, a host proof of semantic harmlessness, or an unmarked route that internalizes the socket.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Apophatic gate question NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:apophatic-gate-question-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:apophatic-gate-question-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\ApophaticGateQuestionUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for
$$
G=(S,Q,R,A,H,C,P,N),
$$
socket-name exposure through $S$, gate-question exactness through $Q$, refusal-ledger coverage through $R$, audit-readback exactness through $A$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ route replay through $C$, dependency provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from the displayed packet $G$. The rows $S,Q,R,A,H,C,P,N$ list the socket name, gate question, refusal ledger, audit readback, transports, continuations, provenance, and local certificate.

The question obligation reads $S$ before $Q$. This forces the gate question to target the named socket rather than a free-form host predicate, and it records the supply kind and marked-use condition as finite rows.

The refusal and readback obligations read $R$ and $A$. The refusal ledger excludes the hidden channels named in the statement, while the readback row stores the gate answer as a BEDC ledger entry.

The rows $H,C,P,N$ provide the only transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming routes. Since the carrier has no further coordinate, the local $\NameCert_{\ApophaticGateQuestionUp}$ obligations are exhausted by the displayed packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Apophatic gate question visible socket non-escape]
\label{thm:apophatic-gate-question-visible-socket-nonescape}
Every consumer read of an accepted $\ApophaticGateQuestionUp$ packet factors through the displayed socket row, gate-question row, refusal ledger, audit-readback row, componentwise $\hsame$ transports, $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ row. The read cannot export the requested outside supply, a hidden axiom, a quotient equality, a proposition-extensionality collapse, an unmarked selector, an unrecorded inscription novelty, or a host-level replacement for the gate answer.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with \autoref{thm:apophatic-gate-question-namecert-obligations}. It identifies the accepted packet with the finite rows $S,Q,R,A,H,C,P,N$ and gives the complete local certificate inventory.

For the visible socket route, read $S$ and $Q$. The socket row names the outside-supply position, and the question row asks only whether a route used that named socket and kept the use externally marked.

For non-escape, read $R$ and $A$. The refusal ledger excludes hidden axiom channels, quotient collapse, proposition-extensionality collapse, unmarked selectors, and unrecorded inscription novelty; the audit-readback row prevents replacing the ledger answer by a host judgment.

Finally route through $H,C,P,N$. These rows account for transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming, and the carrier contains no coordinate for the excluded objects. Hence the consumer surface remains the finite visible-socket gate question.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Apophatic gate question socket-name dependency]
\label{thm:apophatic-gate-question-socket-name-dependency}
Every accepted $\ApophaticGateQuestionUp$ packet reads its socket row through the sibling $\ApophaticSocketUp$ surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-apophaticsocket-namecert} and keeps its named refusal row compatible with the $\ApophaticNameUp$ surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-apophaticname-namecert} before the audit-readback row is available. The audit answer therefore depends on the visible socket-name route rather than on a hidden host predicate, unmarked selector, or detached outside-supply witness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier of \autoref{def:apophatic-gate-question-carrier}. Its first row $S$ is explicitly the visible $\ApophaticSocketUp$ or $\ApophaticNameUp$ row naming the external supply position, while $A$ is only the later audit-readback row.

Apply \autoref{thm:apophatic-gate-question-visible-socket-nonescape}. Every consumer read of the packet factors through $S,Q,R,A,H,C,P,N$, and the visible socket route cannot be replaced by a hidden axiom, quotient equality, proposition-extensionality collapse, unmarked selector, or host-level gate answer.

Consequently the socket-name dependency is read before audit readback. The sibling socket and name surfaces provide the visible rows that $S$ may reference, while $A$ records only the finite answer to the already displayed gate question.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Apophatic gate question source exposure]
\label{thm:apophatic-gate-question-source-exposure}
Every accepted $\ApophaticGateQuestionUp$ packet exposes the visible socket-name
source row $S$ before the gate-question row $Q$ is read. Thus the question is
asked about an already displayed $\ApophaticSocketUp$ or $\ApophaticNameUp$
surface, not about a hidden outside-supply witness, host predicate, or detached
gate answer.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier from \autoref{def:apophatic-gate-question-carrier}. The
packet order displays $S$ as the visible socket-name source row and $Q$ as the
later question row.

Apply the NameCert obligations and socket-name dependency theorems. They force
carrier admission and every audit route to read the socket-name surface before
the gate question can be answered.

The non-escape theorem removes the remaining alternatives. Since the packet has
no coordinate for a hidden outside-supply witness, host predicate, or detached
gate answer, exposure of $S$ is the unique entrance to $Q$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Apophatic gate question visible socket coverage]
\label{thm:apophatic-gate-question-visible-socket-coverage}
Every visible socket route of an accepted $\ApophaticGateQuestionUp$ packet is
read through $\ApophaticSocketUp$ and $\ApophaticNameUp$ before audit readback
at $A$. The route passes through $S,Q,R,H,C,P,N$ and then records the finite
readback row $A$, without replacing that readback by an unmarked selector,
hidden axiom, quotient collapse, or host gate answer.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with source exposure. It places the visible socket-name row $S$ before the
question row $Q$ and therefore before the readback row $A$.

Use socket-name dependency. The sibling $\ApophaticSocketUp$ and
$\ApophaticNameUp$ surfaces provide the only visible rows that $S$ may read, so
the audit route is already attached to those surfaces when $A$ is recorded.

Finally apply visible socket non-escape. The route through $R,H,C,P,N$ accounts
for refusal, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming, and prevents
an unmarked selector, hidden axiom, quotient collapse, or host gate answer from
substituting for the displayed readback.
\end{proof}

a hidden axiom, quotient collapse, unmarked selector, or host gate answer, the

\end{proof}
