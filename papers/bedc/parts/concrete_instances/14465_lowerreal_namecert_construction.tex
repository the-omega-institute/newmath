\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\LowerRealUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-lowerreal-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges, lower real data are read as inhabited lower rational information with finite approximation windows, not as a completed Dedekind cut, a quotient real, or a host-completeness object. The $\LowerRealUp$ packet sits between the located cut discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedcut-namecert}, the located real interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedreal-namecert}, the finite stream-window surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the terminal $\RealUp$ seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. It is the one-sided lower-real observation surface consumed by bound-shaped packets such as \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedsupremum-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedinfimum-namecert}.

\begin{definition}[LowerReal carrier]
\label{def:lower-real-carrier}
A $\LowerRealUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(L_0,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $L_0$ is the lower rational or dyadic ledger: each row records a rational candidate, a dyadic comparison budget, and the evidence that the candidate is admitted by the lower side of the represented real.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row through which the displayed lower observations are inspected.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row attaching those windows to a regular rational name surface.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after $L_0,W,R$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ is the componentwise $\hsame$ transport row for the lower ledger, the finite windows, the regular rational handoff, the Real seal, and the structural rows.
\item $C$ is the displayed $\Cont$ replay route by which a consumer rereads the lower ledger through the same windows and handoff.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LowerRealUp$, $\LocatedCutUp$, $\LocatedRealUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LowerRealUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exports no upper cut, no located cut completion, no quotient real equality, no trichotomy branch, no selected supremum, no countable-choice row, and no host-completeness coordinate.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[LowerReal NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:lower-real-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\LowerRealUp$ carrier $L=(L_0,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\LowerRealUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the lower rational or dyadic ledger, the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff, the $\RealUp$ seal, componentwise transports, continuation replay, package provenance, and local naming row;
\item lower-ledger transport, stating that replacement through $H$ and replay through $C$ preserve the same displayed lower candidates and their dyadic comparison budgets;
\item window scope, stating that every lower observation is read through the displayed finite windows $W$ and no hidden stream tail;
\item regular-rational handoff, stating that each accepted lower-window read is attached to the displayed $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $R$ before the Real seal is consumed;
\item real-seal nonescape, stating that $E$ can be reached only after $L_0,W,R,H,C,P,N$ have been displayed;
\item ledger exactness, stating that every accepted lower-real read uses exactly the rows recorded in $L_0$ and no second lower ledger, quotient equality, selected supremum, or ambient completeness coordinate.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is the tuple displayed in \autoref{def:lower-real-carrier}. The packet has one lower ledger, one finite-window surface, one regular rational handoff, one Real seal, and the structural rows for transport, replay, provenance, and local naming.

Lower-ledger transport is componentwise over $H$ and $C$. A classified replacement may carry the same rational candidates, dyadic budgets, windows, handoff, and seal through the displayed routes, but it cannot add an upper-cut coordinate, a second lower ledger, or a host equality witness.

Window scope and the regular-rational handoff are the roles of $W$ and $R$. Every lower observation in $L_0$ is read through the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window row, and the corresponding $\RegSeqRatUp$ row records the regular rational source before the terminal $\RealUp$ seal is available.

Real-seal nonescape and ledger exactness follow from the row inventory. The only route to $E$ passes through $L_0,W,R,H,C,P,N$, and the provenance row records the located-cut and located-real neighbors only as dependency anchors. No row stores a Dedekind cut completion, quotient real, trichotomy branch, selected supremum, countable choice, or host completeness principle.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LowerReal Real handoff]
\label{thm:lower-real-realup-handoff}
Every $\RealUp$-facing read of an accepted $\LowerRealUp$ packet factors through the displayed lower ledger $L_0$, the $\StreamNameUp$ window row $W$, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row $R$, and the terminal $\RealUp$ seal $E$, with $H,C,P,N$ carrying only componentwise transport, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming. The route exports no Dedekind cut completion, quotient real, trichotomy branch, selected supremum, countable-choice selector, or host-completeness coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. A consumer first sees the lower rational or dyadic ledger $L_0$, so the lower-real information available to it is exactly the displayed finite list of candidates and comparison budgets.

Next the consumer reads the finite observation route. The row $W$ supplies the $\StreamNameUp$ window, and $R$ supplies the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff for the same displayed observations before any terminal seal is reachable.

Finally the read reaches $E$ through the structural rows $H,C,P,N$. Since those rows only transport, replay, package, and name the already displayed ledger route, they cannot insert a Dedekind completion, quotient real equality, trichotomy branch, selected supremum, countable-choice selector, or host-completeness object.
\end{proof}
