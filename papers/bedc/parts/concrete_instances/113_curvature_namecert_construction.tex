\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CurvatureUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-curvature-namecert}

The curvature interface $\CurvatureUp$ packages the antisymmetric bracket of a connection, certifying the Bianchi identities and the tensorial behaviour under section change. It depends on $\NameCert_{\ConnectionUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-connection-namecert}) and supplies the local invariants on which Riemannian and gauge-field certificates rest.

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:curvature-carrier}

\begin{definition}[Curvature connection source row]
\label{def:curvature-connection-source-row}
A $\CurvatureUp$ source row over $\NameCert_{\ConnectionUp}$ is a displayed carrier packet
$$
\mathcal{R}=(\mathcal{C},b,f,s,u,v,d_{uv},d_{vu},\kappa,\rho,\pi_u,\pi_v,\pi_{uv},\pi_{vu})
$$
where $\mathcal{C}$ is a connection carrier packet accepted by $\NameCert_{\ConnectionUp}$, $b:\mathsf{BHist}$ is its base row, $f:\mathsf{BHist}$ is its fibre row, and $s:\mathsf{BHist}$ is its section row. The rows $u,v:\mathsf{BHist}$ are the two displayed tangent directions, $d_{uv},d_{vu}:\mathsf{BHist}$ are the two derivative endpoint rows obtained by reading the directions in the two orders, and $\kappa:\mathsf{BHist}$ is the commutator boundary row supplied by \autoref{thm:connection-curvature-boundary-obligation}.

The entry $\rho$ is the carried $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance row of the source connection packet. The entries $\pi_u,\pi_v,\pi_{uv},\pi_{vu}$ are $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ witnesses reading the two tangent rows and the two derivative endpoint rows from the same finite connection ledger. Acceptance of the source row means that all displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ entries are compared only by the inherited $\hsame$, $\Cont$, classifier, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and probe witnesses of $\NameCert_{\ConnectionUp}$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Curvature carrier boundary row]
\label{thm:curvature-carrier-boundary-row}
Every accepted $\CurvatureUp$ endpoint is exactly a displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ commutator-boundary row inherited from \autoref{thm:connection-curvature-boundary-obligation}. Conversely, every connection source row of \autoref{def:curvature-connection-source-row} whose two derivative endpoints are accepted by the source $\NameCert_{\ConnectionUp}$ determines an accepted curvature endpoint with the same base, fibre, section, tangent-direction, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ provenance rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project an accepted curvature endpoint to its source row. By the carrier definition, the endpoint component is the row $\kappa$, and $\kappa$ is declared only as the commutator boundary read from the two derivative orders of the source connection packet.

Apply \autoref{thm:connection-curvature-boundary-obligation} to the displayed tangent rows $u$ and $v$ of that packet. The theorem supplies a $\mathsf{BHist}$ boundary whose source data are precisely the base, fibre, section, tangent-pair, derivative-endpoint, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ rows already present in the curvature source row.

For the converse, start with such a connection source row and the two accepted derivative endpoints. The connection boundary theorem produces $\kappa$; repack $\kappa$ together with the unchanged $\mathsf{Pkg}$ and $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ witnesses as the curvature endpoint. No coordinate curvature object is introduced, because the endpoint is only the displayed boundary row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Curvature classifier transport row]
\label{thm:curvature-classifier-transport-row}
Let $\mathcal{R}$ and $\mathcal{R}'$ be curvature source rows over connection packets $\mathcal{C}$ and $\mathcal{C}'$. If the inherited $\NameCert_{\ConnectionUp}$ classifier transports the base, fibre, section, tangent-direction, and derivative-endpoint rows of $\mathcal{C}$ to those of $\mathcal{C}'$, if the two commutator boundaries are related by $\hsame$, and if both rows carry the same $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance through a finite $\Cont$ reassociation ledger, then the $\CurvatureUp$ classifier transports the curvature endpoint of $\mathcal{R}$ to the curvature endpoint of $\mathcal{R}'$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the two curvature source rows. The connection classifier supplies the comparisons for the base, fibre, section, tangent-direction, and derivative-endpoint components, while the common provenance row supplies the package comparison.

Reassociate the finite $\Cont$ ledger so that the two derivative orders are read with the same source grouping. This reassociation does not change any endpoint; it only exposes the two commutator-boundary rows as comparable $\mathsf{BHist}$ entries in the same provenance context.

Use the assumed $\hsame$ comparison between the two boundary rows. Repack the transported connection classifier rows, the boundary $\hsame$ witness, and the provenance ledger as the curvature classifier witness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Curvature Bianchi ledger row]
\label{thm:curvature-bianchi-ledger-row}
For any cyclic triple of tangent-direction rows $u,v,w:\mathsf{BHist}$ over the same curvature source row, the three commutator boundaries for $(u,v)$, $(v,w)$, and $(w,u)$ carry a visible $\mathsf{SigRel}$ ledger witnessing the Bianchi boundary. This ledger is a finite $\Cont$ consequence of the three inherited connection boundary rows and their shared $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance, and it does not assert coordinate equality, exterior-calculus equality, or a host-level curvature identity.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read $u$, $v$, and $w$ through the same source connection packet and the same $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ spine. For each cyclic ordered pair, apply \autoref{thm:connection-curvature-boundary-obligation} to obtain the displayed commutator boundary row with the common base, fibre, section, and provenance projections.

Collect the three boundary rows into the signature ledger whose entries are the cyclic boundary readings. The $\mathsf{SigRel}$ witness records that the three readings belong to one finite certificate surface, while the $\Cont$ ledger records the append order of the boundary rows.

The Bianchi row is this ledger. It is visible because every entry is a carried $\mathsf{BHist}$ or $\mathsf{SigRel}$ row, and it is conservative because no equality outside those displayed rows is used.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Curvature tensorial section-change row]
\label{thm:curvature-tensorial-section-change-row}
Replacing the section row or fibre representative of a curvature source row through the inherited $\NameCert_{\ConnectionUp}$ classifier transports the curvature endpoint through the same $\mathsf{BHist}$ boundary row and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ ledger. The derivative endpoint rows, the commutator boundary, and the cyclic $\mathsf{SigRel}$ ledger are carried along by the corresponding $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ witnesses, so the transported source row presents the same $\CurvatureUp$ classifier class.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the source connection classifier witness for the section or fibre replacement. It identifies the displayed base, fibre, section, tangent, and derivative endpoint rows by the inherited component classifiers and exposes the needed $\hsame$ rows.

Transport the two derivative endpoint rows along those component comparisons. Applying the connection curvature-boundary theorem to the transported derivative orders gives the same boundary construction in the transported provenance context.

Finally carry the cyclic $\mathsf{SigRel}$ ledger through the same $\Cont$ and $\hsame$ witnesses. Repacking these rows yields the transported curvature source row, and the endpoint remains in the same classifier class because the boundary and package ledgers have not changed their source certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Curvature public obligation surface]
\label{thm:curvature-public-obligation-surface}
The public $\NameCert_{\CurvatureUp}$ obligation surface over $\NameCert_{\ConnectionUp}$ consists exactly of the connection source row of \autoref{def:curvature-connection-source-row}, the boundary row of \autoref{thm:curvature-carrier-boundary-row}, the classifier transport row of \autoref{thm:curvature-classifier-transport-row}, the Bianchi ledger row of \autoref{thm:curvature-bianchi-ledger-row}, and the tensorial section-change row of \autoref{thm:curvature-tensorial-section-change-row}. A downstream certificate such as $\ChernWeilUp$ may consume these rows as $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\Cont$, $\hsame$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, and $\mathsf{SigRel}$ data, but it may not assume any additional coordinate curvature form or standard bridge from $\CurvatureUp$ alone.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Assemble the carrier by projecting the source connection packet, the tangent rows, the derivative endpoint rows, the commutator boundary, the package provenance, and the probe witnesses from \autoref{def:curvature-connection-source-row}.

The boundary theorem identifies the accepted endpoint; the classifier theorem transports it under inherited connection classification; the Bianchi theorem records the cyclic signature ledger; and the section-change theorem carries the endpoint through replacement of the section or fibre representative.

Conversely, every row exposed by the public surface appears in one of those five clauses. Thus the obligation surface is exact: it provides a concrete certificate surface for downstream geometry while leaving coordinate curvature forms, de Rham representatives, and standard bridges to their own certificate chapters.
\end{proof}
