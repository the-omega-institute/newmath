\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CurvatureUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-curvature-namecert}

The curvature interface $\CurvatureUp$ packages the antisymmetric bracket of a connection, certifying the Bianchi identities and the tensorial behaviour under section change. It depends on $\NameCert_{\ConnectionUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-connection-namecert}) and supplies the local invariants on which Riemannian and gauge-field certificates rest.

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:curvature-carrier}

\begin{definition}[Curvature BHist bracket source]
\label{def:curvature-bhist-bracket-source}
A $\CurvatureUp$ bracket source is a displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ tuple
$$
\mathcal{K}=(\mathcal{C},t_1,t_2,s,d_{12},d_{21},\partial_{\nabla},R_{\nabla},\rho_{\mathcal{C}})
$$
where $\mathcal{C}$ is a $\mathsf{ConnectionCarrierPacket}$ row, $t_1$ and $t_2$ are tangent-direction rows over the base row of $\mathcal{C}$, $s$ is the section row projected from $\mathcal{C}$, $d_{12}$ and $d_{21}$ are the two iterated derivative endpoint rows, $\partial_{\nabla}$ is the commutator boundary row supplied by \autoref{thm:connection-curvature-boundary-obligation}, and $R_{\nabla}$ is the curvature endpoint row read from that boundary.

Two such sources $\mathcal{K}$ and $\mathcal{K}'$ are classified by $\mathsf{CurvCls}$ when their connection packets are related by the $\ConnectionUp$ classifier $\mathsf{ConnCls}$, their two tangent rows and section rows are transported along that same connection classifier, and the rows $d_{12}$, $d_{21}$, $\partial_{\nabla}$, and $R_{\nabla}$ are related componentwise by $\hsame$. The witness $\rho_{\mathcal{C}}$ records that no curvature row is admitted without the visible $\ConnectionUp$ provenance and the finite $\Cont$ ledger connecting the commutator boundary to the displayed endpoint.
\end{definition}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:curvature-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[Curvature connection-carrier scope]
\label{thm:curvature-connection-carrier-scope}
Every $\CurvatureUp$ bracket source of \autoref{def:curvature-bhist-bracket-source} has a visible $\ConnectionUp$ carrier packet as its only dependency source. The base row, fibre row, section row, tangent-pair rows, iterated derivative rows, commutator boundary row, curvature endpoint row, and provenance row are obtained by projection from that packet together with the boundary row of \autoref{thm:connection-curvature-boundary-obligation}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the connection packet $\mathcal{C}$ from the displayed tuple. Its base, fibre, section, tangent, derivative, dependency, and provenance rows are already named by \autoref{def:connection-carrier-packet}.

Apply \autoref{thm:connection-curvature-boundary-obligation} to the two tangent-direction rows. This gives the commutator boundary as a $\mathsf{BHist}$ row whose source data is the same base, fibre, section, tangent-pair, derivative-endpoint, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance data.

Read $R_{\nabla}$ from that boundary row and repack the projected rows into the curvature tuple. Since every row used in the repacking is either a projection from $\mathcal{C}$ or the displayed commutator boundary, the carrier scope contains no additional geometric object.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Curvature antisymmetric bracket obligation]
\label{thm:curvature-antisymmetric-bracket-obligation}
For a bracket source $\mathcal{K}$, swapping the tangent-direction rows $t_1$ and $t_2$ swaps the iterated derivative endpoint rows $d_{12}$ and $d_{21}$ and reverses the displayed commutator boundary. The two curvature endpoints are classified by $\mathsf{CurvCls}$ through the same $\ConnectionUp$ packet and the $\hsame$ transport of the reversed boundary row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the shared connection packet and the two tangent rows from $\mathcal{K}$. The connection classifier keeps the base, fibre, section, and provenance rows fixed, so the swapped tuple has the same visible dependency source.

Apply \autoref{thm:connection-curvature-boundary-obligation} first to $(t_1,t_2)$ and then to $(t_2,t_1)$. The two constructions expose the same iterated derivative rows in opposite order and therefore display opposite commutator boundaries.

Transport the reversed boundary endpoint along $\hsame$ and retain the $\mathsf{ConnCls}$ witness for the connection component. Repacking these transported rows gives the required $\mathsf{CurvCls}$ comparison for the two curvature endpoints.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Curvature Bianchi boundary obligation]
\label{thm:curvature-bianchi-boundary-obligation}
Given three tangent-direction rows over the same $\ConnectionUp$ carrier packet, the cyclic sum of the three curvature bracket sources has a displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ boundary row. That boundary is accepted by $\CurvatureUp$ exactly as a finite $\Cont$ ledger assembled from the three commutator boundary rows supplied by \autoref{thm:connection-curvature-boundary-obligation}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the common connection packet and the three tangent-direction rows. For each cyclic pair, apply \autoref{thm:connection-curvature-boundary-obligation} to obtain the corresponding commutator boundary row and its curvature endpoint.

Use $\Cont$ composition to concatenate the three visible boundary rows in cyclic order. The connection packet supplies the common base, fibre, section, derivative, and provenance rows for each piece of the cycle.

Transport adjacent endpoints by $\hsame$ wherever the displayed order changes from one cyclic boundary to the next. The resulting finite ledger is the Bianchi boundary row, and repacking it with the three curvature endpoints gives the claimed $\CurvatureUp$ obligation surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Curvature tensorial section-change obligation]
\label{thm:curvature-tensorial-section-change-obligation}
Let two section rows over the same $\ConnectionUp$ carrier packet be related by the section classifier inherited by $\mathsf{ConnCls}$. Then the curvature endpoints obtained from the same tangent-pair rows are related by $\mathsf{CurvCls}$, with the endpoint comparison witnessed by $\hsame$ transport along the displayed section-change ledger.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the two section rows and the shared base, fibre, tangent-pair, dependency, and provenance rows from the connection packet. The $\mathsf{ConnCls}$ witness supplies the classifier comparison between the two section components.

Apply $\Cont$ transport to the two iterated derivative endpoint rows along the section-change ledger. The commutator boundary row is transported by the same $\hsame$ comparison because its source endpoints are precisely those iterated derivative rows.

Repack the transported derivative endpoints, boundary row, and curvature endpoint with the unchanged connection provenance. This is componentwise $\mathsf{CurvCls}$, so the curvature row is tensorial with respect to the certified section change.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Curvature certificate obligation surface]
\label{thm:curvature-certificate-obligation-surface}
The visible $\CurvatureUp$ certificate surface consists exactly of the bracket-source carrier of \autoref{def:curvature-bhist-bracket-source}, the componentwise $\mathsf{CurvCls}$ classifier, connection-carrier scope, antisymmetric bracket transport, Bianchi boundary transport, tensorial section-change transport, and the ledger readback rows for the connection packet, tangent pair, section, iterated derivative endpoints, commutator boundary, curvature endpoint, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance. It contains no coordinate chart, metric, differential-form complex, characteristic-class row, or standard bridge.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier readback is by projection from $\mathcal{K}$. The tuple exposes the connection packet, tangent pair, section, two iterated derivative endpoints, commutator boundary, curvature endpoint, and connection provenance.

Classifier stability is componentwise. The connection component is governed by $\mathsf{ConnCls}$, while the iterated derivative endpoints, boundary row, and curvature endpoint are transported by $\hsame$ inside the same finite $\Cont$ ledger.

The four obligation families listed in the statement are exactly the constructive uses of those rows: scope projects the source packet, antisymmetry reverses the tangent pair, Bianchi composes the cyclic boundary ledgers, and tensoriality transports along a certified section change. Since each step consumes only projected rows and each projected row appears in one of these steps, the displayed surface is exact.
\end{proof}
