The packet records before and after classifier profiles as a ledgered finite object, rather than as a prose score or a marker-only label change.

\begin{definition}[Classifier novelty ledger carrier]
\label{def:classifier-novelty-ledger-carrier}
A $\ClassifierNoveltyLedgerUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(B,A,S,J,D,U,Q,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $B$ is the before-profile row, naming the source scope, classifier relation, stability mode, ledger status, and seal claim before the inspected shift.
\item $A$ is the after-profile row, naming the corresponding source scope, classifier relation, stability mode, ledger status, and seal claim after the inspected shift.
\item $S$ is the shared-source row, proving that $B$ and $A$ are compared over the same displayed behavior or source family.
\item $J$ is the classifier-novelty witness row, exposing the before and after classifier disagreement inside the shared source scope.
\item $D$ is the debt row, separating classifier debt, semantic debt, ledger debt, verification debt, intervention debt, and generalization debt.
\item $U$ is the downstream-use row, recording which report, certificate, or consumer reads the after profile rather than merely naming it.
\item $Q$ is the public audit row, recording the finite evidence needed to replay the disagreement, debt accounting, and downstream use.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $B,A,S,J,D,U,Q$ and for the structural rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from the shared source through the before profile, after profile, novelty witness, debt row, downstream use, and audit row.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ClassifierNoveltyLedgerUp$, $\PositiveInterpretabilityClassifierUp$, $\ClassifierNoveltyWitnessUp$, $\PositiveInterpretabilityClassifierPracticeUp$, $\DiscoveryPipelineCalculusUp$, $\EffectiveReplacementLedgerUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ClassifierNoveltyLedgerUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a marker-only classifier change, a detached score, a prose-only novelty claim, a hidden source switch, or a positive-discovery verdict without displayed classifier disagreement and ledger rows.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Classifier novelty ledger NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:classifier-novelty-ledger-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\ClassifierNoveltyLedgerUp$ carrier $L=(B,A,S,J,D,U,Q,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\ClassifierNoveltyLedgerUp}$ surface consists exactly of before-profile admission, after-profile admission, shared-source admission, classifier-novelty witness admission, debt-row admission, downstream-use admission, public-audit admission, componentwise transport, displayed replay, provenance, and local naming.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:classifier-novelty-ledger-carrier}. The profile rows are $B,A$, the comparison rows are $S,J$, the ledger rows are $D,U,Q$, and the structural rows are $H,C,P,N$.

The shared-source row is read before classifier disagreement. Thus the novelty witness cannot be manufactured by changing the source family or observation protocol.

The debt, downstream-use, and public-audit rows are read next. They record why the classifier shift matters to a consumer and which finite evidence replays the disagreement.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same packet. Since marker-only labels, detached scores, hidden source switches, prose-only novelty claims, and positive-discovery verdicts have no carrier coordinate, the listed rows are exactly the local obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Classifier novelty ledger rejects marker-only shifts]
\label{thm:classifier-novelty-ledger-rejects-marker-only-shifts}
An accepted $\ClassifierNoveltyLedgerUp$ packet cannot be obtained by changing only an external marker attached to an unchanged classifier profile. If $B$ and $A$ agree on shared source, classifier relation, stability mode, ledger status, debt row, downstream use, and public audit row, then the packet has no classifier-novelty witness row $J$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the obligation surface of \autoref{thm:classifier-novelty-ledger-namecert-obligations}. The novelty witness $J$ is a displayed classifier-disagreement row over the shared source $S$.

If the before and after profiles agree on the classifier relation and the supporting rows $D,U,Q$, then a marker change is outside the carrier inventory. It is not a row that $J$ can read.

The structural rows preserve the displayed coordinates and do not create a new disagreement. Hence an external marker change alone cannot satisfy the novelty ledger.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Classifier novelty ledger handoff]
\label{thm:classifier-novelty-ledger-handoff}
Every downstream read from an accepted $\ClassifierNoveltyLedgerUp$ packet factors through
$$
B,A,S \longmapsto J \longmapsto D,U,Q \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
The consumer receives before and after classifier-profile disagreement as finite certificate data, not a detached theorem-DNA score or an unledgered interpretability label.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the carrier. The before and after profiles and the shared source must be visible before $J$ can certify classifier disagreement.

The rows $D,U,Q$ then bind the disagreement to debt accounting, downstream use, and replayable audit evidence. Without those rows, the classifier shift has no public ledger surface.

Transport, replay, provenance, and local naming preserve that route. Therefore every downstream read factors through the displayed finite packet and cannot replace it by a detached score or unledgered label.
\end{proof}
