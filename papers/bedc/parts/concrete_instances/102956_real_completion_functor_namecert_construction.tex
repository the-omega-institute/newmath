\begin{definition}[RealCompletionFunctor carrier]
\label{def:real-completion-functor-carrier}
A $\RealCompletionFunctorUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
R=(D,W,Q,E,F,U,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, including the inspected dyadic endpoints, radius requests, and comparison rows used by every downstream completion read;
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row selecting the objectwise requests that the completion route is allowed to inspect;
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row that converts the selected windows into regular rational Cauchy evidence;
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row reached from the same $D,W,Q$ route;
\item $F$ is the $\CauchyCompletionFunctorUp$ unit-and-bind row, read only after the four L10 faces $D,W,Q,E$ have been displayed;
\item $U$ is the $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$ extension-uniqueness row attached to the same completion functor row and the same terminal real seal;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the dyadic ledger, stream windows, regular readback, real seal, functor row, universal-property row, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the route
$$
D,W,Q \longmapsto E \longmapsto F \longmapsto U .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RealCompletionFunctorUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\CauchyCompletionFunctorUp$, $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RealCompletionFunctorUp}$ ledger.

The classifier compares accepted packets only through $D,W,Q,E,F,U,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for quotient completion classes, selected limit representatives, countable choice, host-level equality of real names, or ambient completeness of $\RealUp$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[RealCompletionFunctor NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:real-completion-functor-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RealCompletionFunctorUp$ carrier
$$
R=(D,W,Q,E,F,U,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\RealCompletionFunctorUp}$ surface consists exactly of seven finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the dyadic tolerance ledger, stream-window row, regular rational readback, terminal real seal, completion-functor row, universal-property row, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item four-face visibility, requiring every completion consumer to display the $D,W,Q,E$ route before reading the functor or universal-property rows;
\item dyadic tolerance exactness, requiring all admitted radius and comparison requests to be read from $D$ through the same displayed windows $W$;
\item stream and regular readback compatibility, requiring the $W$ requests and $Q$ readbacks to name the same finite regular-Cauchy route;
\item terminal real seal compatibility, requiring $E$ to be consumed only as the terminal seal of the displayed $D,W,Q$ route;
\item functorial handoff, requiring $F$ to supply the $\CauchyCompletionFunctorUp$ unit-and-bind surface only after $D,W,Q,E$ have been displayed;
\item universal-property exactness, requiring $U$ to expose extension and uniqueness only for the same $E,F$ route and only through $H,C,P,N$.
\end{enumerate}
No $\RealCompletionFunctorUp$ obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:real-completion-functor-carrier}. The accepted packet displays exactly the dyadic, stream-window, regular-readback, terminal-seal, functorial, universal-property, and structural rows.

Four-face visibility is read from $D,W,Q,E$. The dyadic tolerance row supplies the allowed radius requests, the stream row supplies finite windows, the regular row supplies rational Cauchy readback, and the terminal real row seals that route before the completion functor is inspected.

The functorial handoff is read from $F$. Since $F$ is downstream of the same four faces, the unit and bind schedules cannot be replaced by a quotient completion object or by a selected host limit.

The universal-property row is then read from $U$. Its extension and uniqueness data are tied to the displayed real seal and functor row, while $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name only those visible rows.

These projections exhaust the carrier coordinates, so no additional obligation can be read from the packet.
\end{proof}

The route exports no quotient completion, selected limit, countable-choice selector, host real equality, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, or hidden completion object.

\begin{proof}
Start with a terminal-seal or completion-facing consumer and project the accepted carrier. The only terminal real seal is $E$, and it is named only after the dyadic tolerance row $D$, stream-window row $W$, and regular rational readback row $Q$ have been displayed.

The extension-uniqueness consumer must then pass through $U$. This row is attached to the same $E,F$ pair, so existence and uniqueness are read as finite universal-property data over the displayed route.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ preserve only componentwise transport, replay, provenance, and local naming. Since no carrier coordinate names a quotient completion, a selected limit, host equality, countable choice, ambient completeness, or a hidden completion object, the displayed route is the whole exported terminal seal surface.
