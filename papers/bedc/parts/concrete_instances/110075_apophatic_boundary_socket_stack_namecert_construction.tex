\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ApophaticBoundarySocketStackUp$}

The $\ApophaticBoundarySocketStackUp$ packet realizes the apophatic socket-stack branch of \autoref{ch:visions-subhub-apophatic} as finite boundary/refusal NameCert data. It sits over the typed socket taxonomy of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-typedgapsockettaxonomy-namecert}, the socket-kind classifier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-socketkindclassifier-namecert}, the supply ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-supplysocketledger-namecert}, the far-end refusal socket of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-apophaticfarendsocket-namecert}, and the named externality surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-apophaticname-namecert}. The packet names a stacked visible-socket boundary; it does not supply the far end, discharge the refusal row, collapse socket kinds, or replace the named boundary by a host predicate.

\begin{definition}[Apophatic boundary socket stack carrier]
\label{def:apophatic-boundary-socket-stack-carrier}
An $\ApophaticBoundarySocketStackUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(T,K_D,L,F,A,V,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $T$ is the $\TypedGapSocketTaxonomyUp$ row naming the typed boundary class of each socket in the stack.
\item $K_D$ is the $\SocketKindClassifierUp$ row classifying the visible socket kind at each stack layer.
\item $L$ is the $\SupplySocketLedgerUp$ row recording which visible supply request is being ledgered.
\item $F$ is the $\ApophaticFarEndSocketUp$ row marking the far-end refusal boundary without supplying its content.
\item $A$ is the $\ApophaticNameUp$ row naming the externality position that the stack refuses to internalize.
\item $V$ is the visible stack-order row, listing the finite order in which typed socket, classifier, ledger, far-end, and name rows are read.
\item $R$ is the refusal row, recording that a consumer may factor through the visible socket and refusal rows but may not replace them by hidden host supply.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $T,K_D,L,F,A,V,R,C,P,N$.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay through the visible stack order and refusal row.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\TypedGapSocketTaxonomyUp$, $\SocketKindClassifierUp$, $\SupplySocketLedgerUp$, $\ApophaticFarEndSocketUp$, $\ApophaticNameUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ApophaticBoundarySocketStackUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $T,K_D,L,F,A,V,R,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for supplied far-end content, untyped sockets, anonymous supply, hidden host predicates, quotient equality, or discharged apophatic refusal.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Apophatic boundary socket stack NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:apophatic-boundary-socket-stack-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\ApophaticBoundarySocketStackUp$ carrier $K=(T,K_D,L,F,A,V,R,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\ApophaticBoundarySocketStackUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission, typed socket exposure, kind-classifier exposure, supply-ledger exposure, far-end refusal exposure, apophatic name exposure, visible stack-order determinacy, refusal-row exactness, componentwise transport, displayed replay, provenance, and local naming. Every accepted stack consumer factors through the visible socket rows $T,K_D,L,V$ and the refusal/name rows $F,A,R$ before any public read is available.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:apophatic-boundary-socket-stack-carrier}. The socket-facing rows are $T,K_D,L,V$, and the refusal/name rows are $F,A,R$.

The stack order row $V$ forces those rows to be read in a finite visible order. Thus a consumer cannot inspect the supply ledger or far-end boundary without first carrying the typed socket and classifier rows that identify the visible socket.

The refusal row $R$ is downstream of $F$ and $A$. It records refusal of internalization, so the public read is a boundary read rather than a supplied far-end witness or a hidden host predicate.

Transport, replay, provenance, and naming preserve the same finite packet. Since the refused objects have no carrier coordinate, the accepted stack factors through exactly the visible socket and refusal rows.
\end{proof}
