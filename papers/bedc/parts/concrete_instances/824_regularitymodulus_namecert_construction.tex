\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RegularityModulusUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-regularitymodulus-namecert}

The $\RegularityModulusUp$ interface names the finite rate witness used by
regular dyadic and rational Cauchy data before the $\RegSeqRatUp$ and
$\RealUp$ carriers consume it. In the Bishop-style constructive reading of
regular Cauchy moduli, a precision request is not answered by an invisible
choice of a tail. It is answered by a displayed rate row together with the
finite observations and transport rows that certify the requested dyadic
window.

\begin{definition}[RegularityModulus dyadic-window carrier]
\label{def:regularitymodulus-dyadic-window-carrier}
A $\RegularityModulusUp$ carrier packet is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
R=(k,m,w,h,c,p,n)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $k$ is a unary precision row naming the requested dyadic scale;
\item $m$ is a unary modulus row naming the threshold or window size supplied
  for that precision;
\item $w$ is a dyadic window witness over $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observations,
  including the displayed endpoint, radius, and comparison rows read by the
  regularity test;
\item $h$ records the componentwise $\hsame$ transport rows for the precision,
  modulus, dyadic-window, and consumer-facing coordinates;
\item $c$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which a precision request,
  a modulus row, and a dyadic observation window are read in the same finite
  packet;
\item $p$ records the $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance over $\DyadicRatCoreUp$,
  $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, and $\CauchyModulusUp$;
\item $n$ is the local $\NameCert$ row exposing the accepted carrier and its
  classifier surface.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier accepts only packets in which these seven rows are displayed in
one finite history and the consumer reads follow the recorded $\Cont$ routes.
It contains no quotient of streams, no selected real endpoint, and no
unrecorded tail choice.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[RegularityModulus NameCert seed obligation surface]
\label{thm:regularitymodulus-namecert-seed-obligation-surface}
The local $\NameCert_{\RegularityModulusUp}$ seed obligation surface consists
of five finite rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier habitation: the zero precision request is carried by the zero
  dyadic window, with unary rows $k$ and $m$ and a displayed
  $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ zero endpoint ledger;
\item classifier laws: two packets classify together by componentwise
  $\hsame$ on $k$, $m$, $w$, $h$, $c$, $p$, and $n$;
\item monotone precision transport: increasing the requested precision
  transports the modulus row and refines the dyadic window through the
  displayed $\Cont$ routes;
\item ledger exactness: every visible dyadic window read by a consumer is
  exactly the finite $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger named by $w$ and routed by $c$;
\item downstream consumption: $\RegSeqRatUp$ and $\RealUp$ read the regularity
  rate through the same finite packet, without quotienting streams or choosing
  a tail outside the carrier.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier row is \autoref{def:regularitymodulus-dyadic-window-carrier}.
For the zero precision request, take the displayed unary zero rows and the
zero dyadic endpoint ledger supplied by $\DyadicRatCoreUp$; these rows inhabit
the packet without using a nonconstructive selector.

Classifier laws are componentwise. Transport each row of the seven-coordinate
packet by the corresponding $\hsame$ witness and then replay the same
$\Cont$ routes. The accepted carrier shape is unchanged because every row
read by the classifier is already visible.

For monotone precision, a refined request replaces $k$ by a transported unary
row and uses the modulus row $m$ to expose the refined window. Ledger exactness
then says that the only dyadic observations available to consumers are the
endpoint, radius, and comparison rows in $w$.

Finally, the provenance row $p$ names exactly the downstream consumers:
$\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, and $\CauchyModulusUp$ over the
$\StreamNameUp/\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observation surface. Those consumers can
therefore read only the finite window and rate rows carried by $R$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegularityModulus window monotonicity]
\label{thm:regularitymodulus-window-monotonicity}
Let $R=(k,m,w,h,c,p,n)$ be an accepted $\RegularityModulusUp$ carrier packet.
If a precision row $k'$ refines $k$ through the displayed unary comparison
and the carrier records the corresponding transport in $h$ and $c$, then the
dyadic window read at $k'$ is a refinement of $w$ using the same modulus
provenance $m$ and the same $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint family.

The refined read remains a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet with transported
$\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and $\NameCert$ rows, and it introduces no
additional stream quotient or hidden modulus selector.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.RegularityModulusUp.RegularityModulusDyadicWindowCarrier\_window\_monotonicity}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.RegularityModulusUp.RegularityModulusPacket\_window\_monotonicity}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet through
\autoref{def:regularitymodulus-dyadic-window-carrier}. The precision row, the
modulus row, the dyadic window, and the transport ledger are all coordinates
of the same finite history.

Follow the displayed comparison from $k$ to $k'$. The row $h$ transports the
precision and observation coordinates, while $c$ gives the continuation route
from the refined precision request to the refined dyadic window.

Repack the transported endpoint, radius, and comparison rows with the same
$m$, $p$, and $n$ rows. The construction has no coordinate from which a
quotient stream or hidden selector could be read.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegularityModulus dyadic-window exactness]

packet factors through one common finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ window carrying the
shared precision row, modulus row, dyadic endpoint rows, transported window,

The common readback surface exports no quotient stream, selected limit, hidden
