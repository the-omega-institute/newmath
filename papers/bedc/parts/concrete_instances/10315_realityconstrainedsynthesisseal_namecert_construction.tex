\item $F$ is the $\RealityConstrainedOpenFitUp$ row recording finite fit, open continuation, and finite refutation.

\begin{theorem}[Reality-constrained synthesis seal NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:reality-constrained-synthesis-seal-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:reality-constrained-synthesis-seal-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\RealityConstrainedSynthesisSealUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $S=(A,F,O,Q,E,T,L,R,H,C,P,N)$, audit exposure through $A$, open-fit exposure through $F$, observer-invariant exposure through $O$, observation-reflection exposure through $Q$, explanatory-descent exposure through $E$, tower transport through $T$, ledger exactness through $L$, failure-surface exposure through $R$, componentwise transport through $H$, displayed continuation replay through $C$, provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission projects the finite packet of \autoref{def:reality-constrained-synthesis-seal-carrier}. No row outside $A,F,O,Q,E,T,L,R,H,C,P,N$ can be read by the local certificate.

The five sibling rows supply the public synthesis surface. The audit row $A$ constrains model-facing comparison, $F$ keeps finite fit open to continuation and refutation, $O$ prevents one observer history from becoming the public claim, $Q$ exposes the observation handoff, and $E$ records explanatory descent.

The tower, ledger, and failure rows bind the same surface to physical use. The tower row $T$ licenses operations through the approximation route, $L$ records hidden residue and provenance, and $R$ names defeat conditions and refused finality.

Finally the transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve exactly the displayed seal. Thus the local NameCert surface is exhausted by the listed obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Reality-constrained synthesis seal non-escape]
\label{thm:reality-constrained-synthesis-seal-nonescape}

No consumer may replace that route by model--reality identity, a final-fit oracle, a private observer anchor, an unledgered explanation, host equality, or a bare model row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:reality-constrained-synthesis-seal-namecert-obligations}. They force every accepted route to expose $A,F,O,Q,E,T,L,R,H,C,P,N$ before constrained synthesis is read.

The open-fit, objectivity, and observation-reflection rows prevent the familiar escape routes. The fit row $F$ is finite and defeasible, the objectivity row $O$ requires an invariant anchor-change route, and the observation row $Q$ keeps the claim tied to displayed readback.

The explanation, tower, ledger, and failure rows close the remaining routes. A consumer that omits $E,T,L,R$ loses descent, hidden residue, and defeat information, so it no longer reads the accepted seal.

Therefore a synthesis read can only be the displayed route through the full carrier. The excluded identity, finality, private-anchor, unledgered, host-equality, and bare-model routes have no row from which to be exported.

\begin{theorem}[Reality-constrained synthesis seal independence boundary]
\label{thm:reality-constrained-synthesis-seal-independence-boundary}
The $\RealityConstrainedSynthesisSealUp$ carrier is independent of each single sibling surface named by $\RealityConstrainedModelAuditUp$, $\RealityConstrainedOpenFitUp$, $\RealityConstrainedObjectivityUp$, $\ObservationReflectionPacketUp$, and $\RealityConstrainedExplanationUp$. \end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.RealityConstrainedSynthesisSealUp.RealityConstrainedSynthesisSealUp\_independence\_boundary}
\begin{proof}
Read the carrier definition componentwise. The audit sibling supplies $A$, the open-fit sibling supplies $F$, the objectivity sibling supplies $O$, the observation-reflection sibling supplies $Q$, and the explanation sibling supplies $E$.

The non-escape theorem depends on the full displayed packet. Since each single sibling lacks at least one of the audit, open-fit, objectivity, observation, explanation, tower, ledger, or failure coordinates, no single sibling can be bijective with the seal.
\end{proof}
