\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\TranscendentalSupplyLedgerUp$}

The $\TranscendentalSupplyLedgerUp$ surface turns a reported seven-kind $\Gap$ socket into a finite ledger packet that can be consumed by NameCert readers without treating the vision taxonomy itself as a certificate. It records the socket site, requested supply, seven-kind classifier, audit gate, transport, continuation, provenance, and local name. The packet is a ledger for the request boundary; it does not provide the requested supply.

\begin{definition}[TranscendentalSupplyLedger carrier]
\label{def:transcendental-supply-ledger-carrier}
A $\TranscendentalSupplyLedgerUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
\Lambda=(S,R,T,G,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the socket-site row naming the concrete paper or formal site at which a $\Gap$ request is reported;
\item $R$ is the requested-supply row, stated as the exact missing discharge the site would consume;
\item $T$ is the seven-kind classifier row, choosing one of the socket kinds of \autoref{sec:transcendental-supply-unified-table};
\item $G$ is the audit-gate row that prevents the request from being read as an internal BEDC theorem;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the site, requested supply, classifier, audit gate, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes through which consumers read the site row, classify the socket, inspect the requested supply, and return the audit gate;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, $\Gap$, the seven socket-kind rows, requested supply rows, audit-gate rows, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\TranscendentalSupplyLedgerUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exports no row for an unrestricted induction rule, hidden witness, quotient equality, empirical persistence law, shared first-person history, inscription-source explanation, or truth-total self-validator. Each of those shapes may be named in $R$ and classified by $T$, but none is discharged by the packet.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[TranscendentalSupplyLedger taxonomy exhaustion]
\label{thm:transcendental-supply-taxonomy-exhaustion}
For every accepted $\TranscendentalSupplyLedgerUp$ carrier
$$
\Lambda=(S,R,T,G,H,C,P,N),
$$
the classifier row $T$ exhausts the socket taxonomy by routing the site row $S$ and requested-supply row $R$ through exactly one of the seven displayed kinds: free-floating induction, unwitnessed witness, extensional identification, causal continuity, inter-history coordination, inscription novelty, or self-reference closure. Every downstream taxonomy read factors through
$$
S \to R \to T \to G \to H \to C \to P \to N
$$
and no consumer receives an eighth classifier coordinate or a discharge of the request named in $R$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:transcendental-supply-ledger-carrier}. The only classifier-facing row is $T$, and the only request-facing row is $R$ at the socket site $S$.

Read $T$ against the seven rows of \autoref{sec:transcendental-supply-unified-table}. Since $T$ is a displayed finite row, accepting the packet selects one of those seven cases and provides no further case coordinate.

Now route the accepted row through $G,H,C,P,N$. The audit gate records why the requested supply remains outside the closed substrate, while transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming preserve the same finite packet for downstream readers.

Because the carrier contains no row for the requested discharge itself, taxonomy exhaustion is classificatory only. The packet can say which socket kind has appeared, but it cannot provide the witness, rule, equality token, law, recognition, inscription source, or self-reference closure named by the request.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[TranscendentalSupplyLedger NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:transcendental-supply-namecert-obligation-surface}
The local $\NameCert_{\TranscendentalSupplyLedgerUp}$ surface consists exactly of the carrier row of \autoref{def:transcendental-supply-ledger-carrier}, the socket-site row $S$, the requested-supply row $R$, the seven-kind classifier row $T$, the audit-gate row $G$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport $H$, displayed $\Cont$ routes $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance $P$, and local naming row $N$.

Thus a consumer may accept the ledger only by reading the finite packet
$$
(S,R,T,G,H,C,P,N)
$$
as one NameCert boundary. It may not replace the audit gate with an internal theorem, replace the classifier with a hidden source object, or read the requested supply as available substrate data.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier exposure comes first. The packet in \autoref{def:transcendental-supply-ledger-carrier} lists the exact coordinates available to the local classifier and to any downstream NameCert consumer.

The site and request rows are $S$ and $R$. They identify where the socket appears and what missing discharge is being requested, without adding a row that supplies that discharge.

The classifier and audit rows are $T$ and $G$. By \autoref{thm:transcendental-supply-taxonomy-exhaustion}, $T$ selects exactly one of the seven socket kinds, while $G$ keeps the selected request behind the corresponding audit gate.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ bind the transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming boundaries. Repacking these rows gives the whole local $\NameCert_{\TranscendentalSupplyLedgerUp}$ surface, and there is no remaining coordinate from which a consumer could import an internal theorem, hidden source object, or supplied request.
\end{proof}
