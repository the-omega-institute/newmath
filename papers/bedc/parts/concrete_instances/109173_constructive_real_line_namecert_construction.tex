\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ConstructiveRealLineUp$}

Following Bishop and Bridges, the $\ConstructiveRealLineUp$ packet records the textbook-facing L10 real-line interface as finite BEDC NameCert data rather than as a primitive host continuum. It sits over the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the finite observation windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the terminal seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the located real surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedreal-namecert}, and the apartness boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-located-apartness-namecert}. The chapter names the interface on which later real-analysis packets can depend without claiming real completeness, trichotomy, quotient streams, selected limits, or host real equality.

\begin{definition}[Constructive real line carrier]
\label{def:constructive-real-line-carrier}
A $\ConstructiveRealLineUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(D,S,R,E,L,A,\Delta,I,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger storing requested dyadic precision, comparison slack, and density margins.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window schedule through which every requested observation of a real name is inspected.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row that turns the inspected stream windows and dyadic tolerance rows into rational approximants.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ terminal seal row exported only after the $D,S,R$ route has been displayed.
\item $L$ is the $\LocatedRealUp$ boundary row recording finite interval-location evidence for the same readback surface.
\item $A$ is the $\LocatedApartnessUp$ boundary row recording positive separatedness evidence when a consumer asks for apartness.
\item $\Delta$ is the dyadic-density handoff row from rational or dyadic samples to the inspected real-name windows.
\item $I$ is the interval readback row naming rational intervals whose endpoints are read through $D,S,R$ and whose locatedness is checked through $L$ and $A$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $D,S,R,E,L,A,\Delta,I$ and for the structural certificate rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a real-line request to the finite window, tolerance, readback, seal, locatedness, apartness, density, and interval rows.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ConstructiveRealLineUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\LocatedRealUp$, $\LocatedApartnessUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ConstructiveRealLineUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only through the rows $D,S,R,E,L,A,\Delta,I,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for real completeness, trichotomy, quotient streams, selected limits, or host real equality.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Constructive real line NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:constructive-real-line-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\ConstructiveRealLineUp$ carrier
$$
Q=(D,S,R,E,L,A,\Delta,I,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\ConstructiveRealLineUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the dyadic tolerance ledger, stream windows, regular rational readback, terminal real seal, locatedness boundary, apartness boundary, density handoff, interval readback, transport, replay, provenance, and local-name rows;
\item window-readback compatibility, requiring each $S$ window consumed by a real-line request to be read by $R$ under the tolerance ledger $D$;
\item seal discipline, requiring every $\RealUp$ export through $E$ to pass first through the displayed $D,S,R$ route;
\item locatedness discipline, requiring every located interval read to pass through $L$ and $I$ before a real-line consumer can inspect it;
\item apartness discipline, requiring every positive separatedness read to pass through $A$ and the interval readback row $I$;
\item dyadic-density handoff, requiring each rational or dyadic sample exposed by $\Delta$ to be tied to the same $D,S,R$ windows;
\item componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $D,S,R,E,L,A,\Delta,I,C,P,N$ through $H$;
\item displayed $\Cont$ replay of each real-line request through the finite route named by $C$;
\item $\Pkg$ provenance for exactly the displayed packet;
\item ledger non-escape, requiring every consumer to remain inside $D,S,R,E,L,A,\Delta,I,H,C,P,N$.
\end{enumerate}
Consequently the packet exports a textbook-facing constructive real-line interface, not real completeness, trichotomy, quotient streams, selected limits, or host real equality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:constructive-real-line-carrier}. The projection exposes exactly the dyadic tolerance, finite window, regular readback, real seal, locatedness, apartness, density, interval, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

The readback obligations are forced by the displayed route $D,S,R$. A consumer first names the finite stream windows, then reads the corresponding regular rational data, and only then reaches the terminal $\RealUp$ seal $E$.

The located and apartness obligations are separate boundary reads. The interval row $I$ supplies the rational interval readback, $L$ supplies locatedness, and $A$ supplies positive separatedness when the request needs it.

The density, transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows do not add a host continuum coordinate. Since every listed consumer remains inside $D,S,R,E,L,A,\Delta,I,H,C,P,N$, the packet cannot export real completeness, trichotomy, quotient streams, selected limits, or host real equality.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Constructive real line L10 seal route]
\label{thm:constructive-real-line-l10-seal-route}
Every $\RealUp$ read exported by an accepted $\ConstructiveRealLineUp$ carrier factors through the L10 route
$$
\DyadicRatCoreUp \longmapsto \StreamNameUp \longmapsto \RegSeqRatUp \longmapsto \RealUp
$$
with located and apartness observations read only through the sibling $\LocatedRealUp$ and $\LocatedApartnessUp$ boundary rows. No accepted real-line consumer can bypass this route through quotient streams, a selected limit, host real equality, trichotomy, or ambient real completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the carrier route in \autoref{def:constructive-real-line-carrier}. Its only terminal real-name coordinate is $E$, and $E$ is listed after the dyadic tolerance ledger $D$, the stream window schedule $S$, and the regular rational readback $R$.

The located and apartness reads remain boundary reads. A located request must use $L$ and $I$, while a positive separatedness request must use $A$ and the same interval readback row.

Finally replay the request through $C$ and transport it through $H$. The provenance row $P$ and local certificate row $N$ name the same displayed packet, so the route has no hidden coordinate from which quotient stream equality, a selected host limit, trichotomy, host real equality, or real completeness could be read.
\end{proof}
