The $\RealityConstrainedMethodologyLedgerUp$ packet realizes the scientific-methodology discipline of \autoref{sec:scientific-methodology-consequences} as a finite BEDC NameCert carrier. It consumes the explanation rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-reality-constrained-explanation-namecert}, the model-audit rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-reality-constrained-model-audit-namecert}, the multi-observer rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-reality-constrained-objectivity-namecert}, the tower-compression rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-reality-constrained-tower-compression-namecert}, and the open-fit rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-open-fit-packet-namecert}. Those siblings separately expose explanation scope, fit audit, observer symmetry, compression residue, and open refutation surfaces; this packet binds them into the finite ledger by which a scientific-methodology claim is admitted only with effective-region bounds, idealization residue, domain-leakage refusal, upgrade routes, provenance, and local naming rows visible.

\begin{definition}[Reality-constrained methodology-ledger carrier]
\label{def:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-carrier}
A $\RealityConstrainedMethodologyLedgerUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(X,A,O,T,I,S,D,U,F,H,C,Q,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the explanation row, recording the observation, model, phenomenon, signature-comparison, descent, residue, and failure-surface material inherited from $\RealityConstrainedExplanationUp$;
\item $A$ is the audit row, recording source traces, observed signatures, model-generated signatures, classifier comparisons, ledgers, failure surfaces, and strength rows inherited from $\RealityConstrainedModelAuditUp$;
\item $O$ is the objectivity row, recording multi-observer histories, anchor-change schedules, invariant packets, and no-global-observer refusals inherited from $\RealityConstrainedObjectivityUp$;
\item $T$ is the tower-compression row, recording source, indexed tower, observer tower, physical-fit tower, endpoint, composite ledger, replay, and provenance data inherited from $\RealityConstrainedTowerCompressionUp$;
\item $I$ is the open-fit row, recording finite observation traces, admissible observation bundles, current fit, open continuation, and finite refutation surface inherited from $\OpenFitPacketUp$;
\item $S$ is the scope row, naming the domain, observation budget, admissible consumers, and physical-signature interface for the methodology claim;
\item $D$ is the domain-leakage refusal row, naming attempted exports beyond $S$ and the finite reason each export is refused;
\item $U$ is the upgrade-route row, naming the additional observations, classifier refinements, bridge rows, or ledger entries needed to extend the claim without deleting the present carrier;
\item $F$ is the methodology failure-surface row, collecting finite tests, signature mismatches, observer-anchor failures, tower-residue conflicts, and open-fit defeats;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the explanation, audit, objectivity, tower-compression, open-fit, scope, refusal, upgrade, and failure rows;
\item $C$ records the accepted consumer route through the displayed methodology ledger;
\item $Q$ records $\Pkg$ provenance over the sibling carriers, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RealityConstrainedMethodologyLedgerUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for unbounded explanation, final model truth, hidden observer privilege, endpoint/source identity, closed empirical fit, or methodological export outside the displayed packet.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Reality-constrained methodology-ledger certificate]
\label{def:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-certificate}
For accepted packets
$$
L=(X,A,O,T,I,S,D,U,F,H,C,Q,N)
\quad\hbox{and}\quad
L'=(X',A',O',T',I',S',D',U',F',H',C',Q',N'),
$$
the certificate classifier $\mathsf{RealityConstrainedMethodologyLedgerClassifier}(L,L')$ holds exactly when the explanation, audit, objectivity, tower-compression, and open-fit rows transport componentwise, the scope rows name the same admissible domain after $\hsame$ transport, the domain-leakage refusal rows preserve the refused exports, the upgrade-route rows preserve the admitted extension paths, the methodology failure surfaces preserve the defeating finite tests, and the consumer, provenance, and local naming rows transport through the same displayed route.

Thus certificate agreement is not sameness of theories, models, or observers. It is preservation of the finite methodology ledger that records where the claim explains, where it fits, where it is objective, where it is compressed, where it remains open, where it refuses export, and how it may be extended.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Reality-constrained methodology-ledger NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\RealityConstrainedMethodologyLedgerUp}$ surface consists exactly of six obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission: the accepted object is exactly $L=(X,A,O,T,I,S,D,U,F,H,C,Q,N)$;
\item sibling accountability: explanation, audit, objectivity, tower-compression, and open-fit reads must pass through $X,A,O,T,I$;
\item scope exactness: every methodology claim must display the scope row $S$ before it is exported;
\item domain-leakage refusal: every attempted export beyond $S$ must be recorded in $D$ as refused or routed through $U$ as a declared extension path;
\item failure-surface exposure: finite defeats from explanation, audit, objectivity, tower compression, and open fit must appear in $F$ before the consumer route $C$ is accepted;
\item ledger non-escape: no consumer may infer scientific-methodology authority from rows outside $X,A,O,T,I,S,D,U,F,H,C,Q,N$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-carrier}. The packet lists the only rows available to the local certificate.

Sibling accountability follows from the distinguished roles of $X,A,O,T,I$. Explanation scope, model audit, objectivity, tower compression, and open fit are not reconstructed by host prose; they enter through the five displayed sibling rows.

Scope exactness and domain-leakage refusal are forced by $S$ and $D$. A methodology claim is admitted only after its domain and budget are named, and an attempted export beyond that boundary is either refused in $D$ or sent to the declared extension route $U$.

Failure exposure is forced by $F$. The consumer route may read the methodology ledger only after the finite tests, signature mismatches, anchor failures, tower-residue conflicts, and open-fit defeats are visible.

Ledger non-escape follows because the carrier contains no coordinate outside $X,A,O,T,I,S,D,U,F,H,C,Q,N$. A route depending on an absent row is not a route in the accepted local naming certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Reality-constrained methodology-ledger domain-leakage refusal]
\label{thm:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-domain-leakage-refusal}
Let $L=(X,A,O,T,I,S,D,U,F,H,C,Q,N)$ be an accepted $\RealityConstrainedMethodologyLedgerUp$ packet. A claim that uses the explanation, audit, objectivity, tower-compression, or open-fit rows while exporting beyond the displayed scope row $S$ is rejected unless the same packet records the attempted export in $D$ and supplies an upgrade route in $U$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the scope-exactness and domain-leakage obligations of \autoref{thm:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-namecert-obligations}. They require the scope row before export and require every export beyond that scope to appear in the refusal or upgrade surface.

If a claim exports beyond $S$ without a row in $D$, the packet lacks the displayed refusal record. If it also lacks a route in $U$, the packet gives no accepted extension path.

The carrier has no remaining coordinate through which the export could be licensed. The claim may remain a local explanation, audit read, objectivity read, tower-compression read, or open-fit read, but it is not a $\RealityConstrainedMethodologyLedgerUp$ methodology export.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Reality-constrained methodology-ledger sibling independence]
\label{thm:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-sibling-independence}
The $\RealityConstrainedMethodologyLedgerUp$ carrier is not bijective to any one of $\RealityConstrainedExplanationUp$, $\RealityConstrainedModelAuditUp$, $\RealityConstrainedObjectivityUp$, $\RealityConstrainedTowerCompressionUp$, or $\OpenFitPacketUp$. Each sibling lacks at least one of the joined effective-region, idealization-residue, domain-leakage, upgrade-route, and methodology-ledger rows required by \autoref{def:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-carrier}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Compare the carrier fields. $\RealityConstrainedExplanationUp$ supplies explanation scope and residue but lacks the joined audit, objectivity, tower-compression, open-fit, domain-leakage, and upgrade-route rows.

$\RealityConstrainedModelAuditUp$ supplies audit and failure rows but lacks the explanation, no-global-observer, tower-compression endpoint, open-continuation, methodology scope, and upgrade-route rows. $\RealityConstrainedObjectivityUp$ supplies anchor-change and no-global-observer refusal but lacks the effective-region, idealization-residue, open-fit, and methodology-upgrade rows.

$\RealityConstrainedTowerCompressionUp$ supplies compression residue and endpoint/source separation but lacks the methodology domain-leakage refusal and open-fit refutation surface. $\OpenFitPacketUp$ supplies finite fit and open continuation but lacks the joined explanation, audit, objectivity, tower-compression, and methodology-ledger rows.

Since each sibling omits at least one required field of $L$, no bijection can preserve all carrier fields of \autoref{def:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-carrier}.
\end{proof}
