\begin{theorem}[Tagged option source weakening]
\label{thm:tagged-option-source-weakening}
This theorem fits the per-object template as follows: the traditional target is
tagged option source weakening; the BEDC source is $\mathsf{BHist}$ with
$\hsame$; the carrier is $\mathsf{TaggedOptionHistoryCarrier}$; the classifier
is $\mathsf{TaggedOptionHistoryClassifier}$; the stability field is source
monotonicity for tagged endpoints; the ledger is the visible absent/present tag
with present payload evidence; the core theorem is tagged source weakening;
exactness is endpoint witness preservation; the standard bridge is constructor
tagged option syntax; and the status is $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$. Let
$S,T:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be BEDC history source carriers, and let
$\sim_S$ and $\sim_T$ be source classifiers. Assume
$$
\forall h.\; S(h)\to T(h)
$$
and
$$
\forall a\,b.\; S(a)\to S(b)\to a\sim_S b\to a\sim_T b.
$$
Then for every history $h$,
$$
\mathsf{TaggedOptionHistoryCarrier}_S(h)\to
\mathsf{TaggedOptionHistoryCarrier}_T(h),
$$
and for every pair of histories $h,k$,
$$
\mathsf{TaggedOptionHistoryClassifier}_{S,\sim_S}(h,k)\to
\mathsf{TaggedOptionHistoryClassifier}_{T,\sim_T}(h,k).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the carrier implication, split the tagged carrier witness. The absent case
has no source payload and is reused unchanged. The present case exposes a
payload history $a$, a source witness $S(a)$, and the endpoint witness
$\hsame(h,\Eone(a))$; apply the source-carrier implication to obtain $T(a)$ and
reuse the same endpoint witness.

For the classifier implication, split the tagged classifier witness. The
absent-absent case is reused unchanged. In the present-present case, keep the
payload histories $a,b$ and both endpoint witnesses. Apply the source-carrier
implication to the two source witnesses to obtain $T(a)$ and $T(b)$, and apply
the source-classifier implication to the same two source witnesses and the
given $a\sim_S b$ witness to obtain $a\sim_T b$. These fields rebuild the
tagged classifier witness for $T$ and $\sim_T$.
\end{proof}

\mathsf{TaggedOptionHistoryCarrier}_S(h)\to
\mathsf{TaggedOptionHistoryCarrier}_T(h),
$$

\mathsf{TaggedOptionHistoryLedger}_S(h)\to

$$
\mathsf{TaggedOptionHistoryClassifier}_{S,\sim_S}(h,k)\to

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply Theorem~\ref{thm:tagged-option-source-weakening} to the displayed

$\mathsf{TaggedOptionHistoryPattern}_T(h)$. The ledger field is identical, using

\begin{definition}[Tagged option payload descent relation]
\label{def:tagged-option-payload-descent-relation}
Let $S,T:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be BEDC history source carriers, let
$\sim_S$ and $\sim_T$ be source classifiers, let
$\delta:\mathsf{DescentCertificate}(\mathsf{BHist},\mathsf{BHist},\sim_S,\sim_T)$,
and assume payload-carrier preservation
$$
\rho_{\delta}:\forall a.\;S(a)\to T(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a)).
$$
The relation $\mathsf{TaggedOptionMapRel}_{\delta}(S,T,\rho_{\delta};h,k)$
means that the tagged endpoint $h$ over $S$ is carried to the tagged endpoint
$k$ over $T$ by preserving the visible option tag:
$$
\mathsf{TaggedOptionMapRel}_{\delta}(S,T,\rho_{\delta};h,k)
\Longleftrightarrow
\bigl(\hsame(h,\emp)\land\hsame(k,\emp)\bigr)
\lor
\bigl(\exists a:\mathsf{BHist}.\;
S(a)\land T(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a))\land
\hsame(h,\Eone(a))\land
\hsame(k,\Eone(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a)))\bigr).
$$
The displayed $T(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a))$ witness is the carrier preservation
field $\rho_{\delta}$ applied to the exposed source payload.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Tagged option map relation visible branch equivalence]
\label{thm:tagged-option-maprel-visible-branch-equivalence}
This theorem fits the per-object template as follows: the traditional target is
constructor-tagged option payload descent readback; the BEDC source is
$\mathsf{BHist}$ with a
$\mathsf{DescentCertificate}$; the carrier is tagged option histories; the
classifier is the transported tagged option classifier; the stability obligation
is visible branch preservation; the ledger is the absent/present tag with the
transported payload; the core theorem is this branch equivalence; exactness is
branch inversion; the standard bridge is none/some map readback; and the status
is $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$. Let $S,T:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be BEDC
history source carriers, let $\sim_S,\sim_T$ be source classifiers, let
$\delta:\mathsf{DescentCertificate}(\mathsf{BHist},\mathsf{BHist},\sim_S,\sim_T)$,
and let
$$
\rho_{\delta}:\forall a.\;S(a)\to T(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a)).
$$
For all histories $h,k$, every witness
$\mathsf{TaggedOptionMapRel}_{\delta}(S,T,\rho_{\delta};h,k)$ implies both
$$
\hsame(h,\emp)\Longleftrightarrow\hsame(k,\emp)
$$
and
$$
(\exists a:\mathsf{BHist}.\;S(a)\land\hsame(h,\Eone(a)))
\Longleftrightarrow
(\exists b:\mathsf{BHist}.\;T(b)\land\hsame(k,\Eone(b))).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Split the map-relation witness into its visible branches.

In the absent branch, both endpoint presentations are $\emp$. Thus absentness is
preserved in either direction, and any present payload witness would identify an
empty presentation with a one-constructor presentation, contradicting
constructor no-confusion.

In the present branch, the exposed source payload $a$ supplies the left present
witness, while $\delta.\mathsf{map}(a)$ together with $\rho_{\delta}$ supplies
the right present witness. Either endpoint being absent would again identify
$\emp$ with a stored one-constructor presentation, so absentness agrees in both
directions.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Tagged option identity payload descent exactness]
\label{thm:tagged-option-payload-descent-identity-exactness}
This theorem fits the per-object template as follows: the traditional target is
constructor-tagged option identity map; the BEDC source is $\mathsf{BHist}$ with
$\hsame$; the carrier is $\mathsf{TaggedOptionHistoryCarrier}$; the classifier
is $\mathsf{TaggedOptionHistoryClassifier}$ specialized to $\hsame$; the
stability obligation is identity preservation of visible absent and present
endpoints; the ledger is the visible tag and unchanged payload; the core theorem
is identity exactness for $\mathsf{TaggedOptionMapRel}$; exactness is branch
analysis; the standard bridge is none/some identity readback; and the status is
$\mathsf{ProofTarget}$. Let $S:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$. Write
$\delta_{\mathsf{id}}$ for the descent certificate
$$
\delta_{\mathsf{id}}:
\mathsf{DescentCertificate}(\mathsf{BHist},\mathsf{BHist},\hsame,\hsame)
$$
whose map field is $a\mapsto a$ and whose respects field sends $p$ to $p$. Let
$$
\rho_{\mathsf{id}}:\forall a.\;S(a)\to S(\delta_{\mathsf{id}}.\mathsf{map}(a))
$$
be the preservation field that reuses the source witness. Then for all histories
$h,k$,
$$
\mathsf{TaggedOptionMapRel}_{\delta_{\mathsf{id}}}
(S,S,\rho_{\mathsf{id}};h,k)
\Longleftrightarrow
\mathsf{TaggedOptionHistoryCarrier}_S(h)\land\hsame(h,k).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the forward direction, split the map relation by its visible branch.

In the absent branch, the witness gives $\hsame(h,\emp)$ and $\hsame(k,\emp)$.
The first field builds the absent carrier for $h$. Composing $\hsame(h,\emp)$
with symmetry of $\hsame(k,\emp)$ gives $\hsame(h,k)$.

In the present branch, the witness exposes a payload $a$ with $S(a)$,
$S(\delta_{\mathsf{id}}.\mathsf{map}(a))$, $\hsame(h,\Eone(a))$, and
$\hsame(k,\Eone(\delta_{\mathsf{id}}.\mathsf{map}(a)))$. Since
$\delta_{\mathsf{id}}.\mathsf{map}(a)$ is $a$, the first payload witness and the
source endpoint presentation build the present carrier for $h$, and composing
the two endpoint presentations gives $\hsame(h,k)$.

For the reverse direction, split the carrier of $h$ by its tagged branch and use
the assumed $\hsame(h,k)$.

In the absent branch, compose symmetry of $\hsame(h,k)$ with the absent
presentation of $h$ to obtain $\hsame(k,\emp)$. Together with the original absent
presentation, this is the absent branch of
$\mathsf{TaggedOptionMapRel}_{\delta_{\mathsf{id}}}$.

In the present branch, keep the exposed payload $a$ and the witness $S(a)$.
The field $\rho_{\mathsf{id}}$ supplies the target payload witness, and
composing symmetry of $\hsame(h,k)$ with $\hsame(h,\Eone(a))$ gives
$\hsame(k,\Eone(a))$. Because the identity descent leaves $a$ unchanged, these
fields build the present branch of
$\mathsf{TaggedOptionMapRel}_{\delta_{\mathsf{id}}}$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Tagged option payload descent preserves classification]
\label{thm:tagged-option-payload-descent-preserves-classification}
Let $S,T:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be BEDC history source carriers, let
$\sim_S$ and $\sim_T$ be source classifiers, let
$\delta:\mathsf{DescentCertificate}(\mathsf{BHist},\mathsf{BHist},\sim_S,\sim_T)$,
and assume
$$
\rho_{\delta}:\forall a.\;S(a)\to T(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a)).
$$
For histories $h,h',k,k'$, if
$$
\mathsf{TaggedOptionHistoryClassifier}_{S,\sim_S}(h,h'),
$$
$$
\mathsf{TaggedOptionMapRel}_{\delta}(S,T,\rho_{\delta};h,k),
$$
$$
\mathsf{TaggedOptionMapRel}_{\delta}(S,T,\rho_{\delta};h',k'),
$$
$$
\mathsf{cert}:\NameCert(S,\sim_S),
$$
and
$$
\mathsf{TaggedOptionSourceHsameCompatible}(S,\sim_S),
$$
then
$$
\mathsf{TaggedOptionHistoryClassifier}_{T,\sim_T}(k,k').
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Split the source classifier witness by its tagged branch and split the two
endpoint map-relation witnesses. In the absent classifier branch, both source
endpoints are presented as $\emp$. A present map-relation branch would also
present one of those endpoints as $\Eone(a)$, contradicting empty-versus-one
no-confusion; hence both map-relation witnesses are absent branches, and the
target classifier is rebuilt by the absent-absent constructor for $k$ and $k'$.

In the present classifier branch, the source witness exposes payloads $a,b$ with
$S(a)$, $S(b)$, endpoint presentations
$\hsame(h,\Eone(a))$ and $\hsame(h',\Eone(b))$, and classifier witness
$a\sim_S b$. The absent map-relation branch is impossible by the same
constructor no-confusion argument, so the two map-relation witnesses expose
payloads $c,d$ with target endpoints
$\hsame(k,\Eone(\delta.\mathsf{map}(c)))$ and
$\hsame(k',\Eone(\delta.\mathsf{map}(d)))$. Endpoint inversion between
$\Eone(a)$ and $\Eone(c)$, and between $\Eone(b)$ and $\Eone(d)$, gives source
history sameness between the corresponding payloads. Source hsame compatibility
turns these sameness witnesses into source classifier witnesses; symmetry and
transitivity from $\mathsf{cert}$ compose them with $a\sim_S b$ to obtain
$c\sim_S d$. Applying $\delta.\mathsf{respects}$ yields
$\delta.\mathsf{map}(c)\sim_T\delta.\mathsf{map}(d)$, while
$\rho_{\delta}$ supplies both target carrier witnesses. These fields rebuild the
present-present target classifier for $k$ and $k'$.
\end{proof}

$\delta.\mathsf{map}(a)\sim_T\delta.\mathsf{map}(b)$. Together with the stored

  then
  $$
  \mathsf{TaggedOptionHistoryClassifier}_{T,\sim_T}(k,k').
  $$
  \item For histories $h,h',k,k'$, if $h$ maps to $k$, $h'$ maps to $k'$, and
  the source endpoints are classified together, so that
  $$
  \mathsf{TaggedOptionMapRel}_{\delta}(S,T,\rho_{\delta};h,k),
  $$
  $$
  \mathsf{TaggedOptionMapRel}_{\delta}(S,T,\rho_{\delta};h',k'),
  $$
  and
  $$
  \mathsf{TaggedOptionHistoryClassifier}_{S,\sim_S}(h,h'),
  $$
  then
  $$
  \mathsf{TaggedOptionHistoryClassifier}_{T,\sim_T}(k,k').
  $$
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the endpoint totality clause, apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:tagged-option-payload-descent-total-endpoint-transport} to the
source carrier witness. It supplies the target endpoint, the
$\mathsf{TaggedOptionMapRel}$ witness, and the target carrier, pattern, and
ledger fields.

For the same-source target classification clause, apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:tagged-option-maprel-common-source-target-classification} to
the two map-relation witnesses and the source hsame compatibility hypothesis.
This gives the target classifier directly.

For the classifier preservation clause, apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:tagged-option-payload-descent-preserves-classification} to the
source classifier witness, the two map-relation witnesses, the source
certificate $\mathsf{cert}$, and the source hsame compatibility hypothesis.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Tagged option payload descent composition]
\label{thm:tagged-option-payload-descent-composition}
This theorem fits the per-object template as follows: the traditional target is
constructor-tagged option map composition; the BEDC source is $\mathsf{BHist}$
with two $\mathsf{DescentCertificate}$ maps; the carrier is
$\mathsf{TaggedOptionHistoryCarrier}$; the classifier is
$\mathsf{TaggedOptionHistoryClassifier}$; the stability obligation is closure of
$\mathsf{TaggedOptionMapRel}$ under composition; the ledger is visible
absent/present tags with transported payloads; exactness is branch analysis; and
the status is $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$. Let $S,T,U:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$
be BEDC history source carriers, let $\sim_S$, $\sim_T$, and $\sim_U$ be source
classifiers, and let
$$
\delta:\mathsf{DescentCertificate}(\mathsf{BHist},\mathsf{BHist},\sim_S,\sim_T)
$$
and
$$
\epsilon:\mathsf{DescentCertificate}(\mathsf{BHist},\mathsf{BHist},\sim_T,\sim_U).
$$
Assume payload preservation fields
$$
\rho_{\delta}:\forall a.\;S(a)\to T(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a))
$$
and
$$
\rho_{\epsilon}:\forall b.\;T(b)\to U(\epsilon.\mathsf{map}(b)).
$$
Assume also endpoint stability for the second descent:
$$
\eta_{\epsilon}:\forall x\,y.\;
T(x)\to T(y)\to \hsame(x,y)\to
\hsame(\epsilon.\mathsf{map}(x),\epsilon.\mathsf{map}(y)).
$$
Write $\epsilon\circ\delta$ for the descent certificate whose map field is
$a\mapsto\epsilon.\mathsf{map}(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a))$ and whose respects field
sends $p:a\sim_S b$ to
$\epsilon.\mathsf{respects}(\delta.\mathsf{respects}(p))$. Define
$$
\rho_{\epsilon\circ\delta}(a,Sa)=
\rho_{\epsilon}(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a),\rho_{\delta}(a,Sa)).
$$
For histories $h,k,m$, if
$$
\mathsf{TaggedOptionMapRel}_{\delta}(S,T,\rho_{\delta};h,k)
$$
and
$$
\mathsf{TaggedOptionMapRel}_{\epsilon}(T,U,\rho_{\epsilon};k,m),
$$
then
$$
\mathsf{TaggedOptionMapRel}_{\epsilon\circ\delta}
(S,U,\rho_{\epsilon\circ\delta};h,m).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Split the first map-relation witness. In the absent case, the source endpoint
is presented as $\emp$ and the intermediate endpoint is presented as $\emp$.
The second map-relation witness cannot be present, since that would also
present the same intermediate endpoint as $\Eone(b)$ and would contradict
empty-versus-one no-confusion at $k$. Hence the second witness is absent, and
the composed witness is the absent branch from $h$ to $m$.

In the present case, the first witness exposes a payload $a$ with $S(a)$,
$T(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a))$, source endpoint
$\hsame(h,\Eone(a))$, and intermediate endpoint
$\hsame(k,\Eone(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a)))$. The second map-relation witness
cannot be absent, since that would present $k$ as both a one-constructor
endpoint and $\emp$.

The remaining second witness is present. It exposes a payload $b$ with $T(b)$,
$U(\epsilon.\mathsf{map}(b))$, intermediate endpoint $\hsame(k,\Eone(b))$, and
final endpoint $\hsame(m,\Eone(\epsilon.\mathsf{map}(b)))$.

Invert the two presentations of $k$ to obtain
$\hsame(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a),b)$, and apply $\eta_{\epsilon}$ to the two
target payload witnesses. This gives
$\hsame(\epsilon.\mathsf{map}(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a)),
\epsilon.\mathsf{map}(b))$.

By one-constructor congruence and transitivity of $\hsame$, the final endpoint
presentation for $m$ rewrites from $\Eone(\epsilon.\mathsf{map}(b))$ to
$\Eone(\epsilon.\mathsf{map}(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a)))$.

Together with $\rho_{\epsilon\circ\delta}(a,Sa)$ and the original source
endpoint witness, these fields build the present branch of
$\mathsf{TaggedOptionMapRel}_{\epsilon\circ\delta}
(S,U,\rho_{\epsilon\circ\delta};h,m)$.
\end{proof}
\leandef{BEDC.Derived.OptionUp.TaggedOptionDescentComp}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.OptionUp.TaggedOptionMapRel\_composition\_closed}

\begin{theorem}[Tagged option payload descent composition exactness]
\label{thm:tagged-option-payload-descent-composition-exactness}
Let $S,T,U:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be BEDC history source carriers,
let $\sim_S$, $\sim_T$, and $\sim_U$ be source classifiers, and let
$$
\delta:\mathsf{DescentCertificate}(\mathsf{BHist},\mathsf{BHist},\sim_S,\sim_T)
$$
and
$$
\epsilon:\mathsf{DescentCertificate}(\mathsf{BHist},\mathsf{BHist},\sim_T,\sim_U).
$$
Assume payload preservation fields
$$
\rho_{\delta}:\forall a.\;S(a)\to T(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a))
$$
and
$$
\rho_{\epsilon}:\forall b.\;T(b)\to U(\epsilon.\mathsf{map}(b)).
$$
Assume also endpoint stability for the second descent:
$$
\eta_{\epsilon}:\forall x\,y.\;
T(x)\to T(y)\to \hsame(x,y)\to
\hsame(\epsilon.\mathsf{map}(x),\epsilon.\mathsf{map}(y)).
$$
Write $\epsilon\circ\delta$ for the descent certificate whose map field is
$a\mapsto\epsilon.\mathsf{map}(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a))$ and whose respects field
sends $p:a\sim_S b$ to
$\epsilon.\mathsf{respects}(\delta.\mathsf{respects}(p))$. Define
$$
\rho_{\epsilon\circ\delta}(a,Sa)=
\rho_{\epsilon}(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a),\rho_{\delta}(a,Sa)).
$$
For histories $h,m$, the composite relation holds exactly when it factors
through a canonical intermediate endpoint:
$$
\mathsf{TaggedOptionMapRel}_{\epsilon\circ\delta}
(S,U,\rho_{\epsilon\circ\delta};h,m)
\Longleftrightarrow
\exists k:\mathsf{BHist}.\;
\mathsf{TaggedOptionMapRel}_{\delta}(S,T,\rho_{\delta};h,k)\land
\mathsf{TaggedOptionMapRel}_{\epsilon}(T,U,\rho_{\epsilon};k,m).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the left-to-right direction, split the composite map-relation witness.

In the absent branch, the composite witness presents both endpoints as
$\emp$. Choose $k=\emp$. The first factor is the absent branch of
$\mathsf{TaggedOptionMapRel}_{\delta}$ using the displayed source endpoint
presentation and reflexivity at $\emp$, and the second factor is the absent
branch of $\mathsf{TaggedOptionMapRel}_{\epsilon}$ using reflexivity at $\emp$
and the displayed final endpoint presentation.

In the present branch, the composite witness exposes a payload $a$, a source
witness $S(a)$, the composite payload witness
$\rho_{\epsilon\circ\delta}(a,Sa)$, the source endpoint presentation
$\hsame(h,\Eone(a))$, and the final endpoint presentation
$\hsame(m,\Eone(\epsilon.\mathsf{map}(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a))))$. Choose
$k=\Eone(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a))$.

The first factor is the present branch for $\delta$, using $S(a)$,
$\rho_{\delta}(a,Sa)$, the source endpoint presentation, and reflexivity at
$\Eone(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a))$. The second factor is the present branch for
$\epsilon$, using $\rho_{\delta}(a,Sa)$,
$\rho_{\epsilon}(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a),\rho_{\delta}(a,Sa))$, reflexivity at
the intermediate endpoint, and the displayed final endpoint presentation.

For the right-to-left direction, take an intermediate history $k$ and the two
displayed factor witnesses. Apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:tagged-option-payload-descent-composition} with that
intermediate witness and the same endpoint-stability hypothesis
$\eta_{\epsilon}$ to obtain the composite
$\mathsf{TaggedOptionMapRel}_{\epsilon\circ\delta}$ witness.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.OptionUp.TaggedOptionMapRel\_composition\_factorization\_iff}
