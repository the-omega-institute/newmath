\begin{theorem}[Tagged option history classifier absent-branch equivalence]
\label{thm:tagged-option-history-classifier-absent-branch-equivalence}
This theorem fits the per-object template as follows: the traditional target is
option absent-branch agreement; the BEDC source is $\mathsf{BHist}$ with
$\hsame$; the carrier is $\mathsf{TaggedOptionHistoryCarrier}$; the classifier
is $\mathsf{TaggedOptionHistoryClassifier}$; the stability obligation is branch
determinism; the ledger is the visible absent/present tag; the core theorem is
absentness equivalence; exactness is absent endpoints agree under classifier;
the standard bridge is $\mathsf{none}$/$\mathsf{some}$; and the status is
$\mathsf{ProofTarget}$. For every BEDC history source carrier
$S:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$, source classifier $\sim_S$, and histories
$h,k$, classified endpoints agree on absentness:
$$
\mathsf{TaggedOptionHistoryClassifier}_{S,\sim_S}(h,k)\to
\bigl(\hsame(h,\emp)\Longleftrightarrow\hsame(k,\emp)\bigr).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Assume a classifier witness between $h$ and $k$. The forward implication is
exactly the absent clause of
Theorem~\ref{thm:tagged-option-history-classifier-visible-branch-inversion}:
from $\hsame(h,\emp)$, left visible-branch inversion gives
$\hsame(k,\emp)$. The reverse implication is the absent clause of
Theorem~\ref{thm:tagged-option-history-classifier-right-visible-branch-inversion}:
from $\hsame(k,\emp)$, right visible-branch inversion gives
$\hsame(h,\emp)$. These two implications assemble the displayed
$\Longleftrightarrow$.
\end{proof}
\leandef{BEDC.Derived.OptionUp.TaggedOptionHistoryClassifier}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.OptionUp.TaggedOptionHistoryClassifier\_absent\_branch\_equivalence}

$$
\bigl(\exists a:\mathsf{BHist}.\; S(a)\land\hsame(h,\Eone(a))\bigr)
\Longleftrightarrow
\bigl(\exists b:\mathsf{BHist}.\; S(b)\land\hsame(k,\Eone(b))\bigr).

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

$\hsame(k,\Eone(b))$, which is the required present witness for $k$. For the
reverse implication, unpack a right payload $b$ with $S(b)$ and
$\hsame(k,\Eone(b))$. Apply the present clause of
Theorem~\ref{thm:tagged-option-history-classifier-right-visible-branch-inversion}

\end{proof}
