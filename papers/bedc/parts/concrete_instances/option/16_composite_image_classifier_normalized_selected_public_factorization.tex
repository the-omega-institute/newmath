\begin{theorem}[Tagged option composite image classifier normalized selected visible public pair canonicality]
\label{thm:tagged-option-composite-image-classifier-normalized-selected-visible-public-pair-canonicality}
This theorem fits the per-object template as follows: the traditional target is
endpoint-normalized functorial $\mathsf{OptionUp}$ selected visible public-pair
canonicality; the BEDC source is $\mathsf{BHist}$ with two composable
$\mathsf{DescentCertificate}$ maps; the carrier is
$\mathsf{TaggedOptionPayloadDescentImageCarrier}$; the classifier is
$\mathsf{TaggedOptionPayloadDescentImageClassifier}$; the stability obligations
are composite descent, endpoint $\hsame$ transport, normalized target-payload
classification, visible-payload determinacy, and empty-versus-present
no-confusion; the ledger is one selected normalized visible public pair
together with all one-sided competing readings; the core theorem is selected
pair classification, same-side uniqueness, cross-side target classification,
and non-absence; exactness says normalized visible readback is single-valued
around the selected public pair; the standard bridge is $\mathsf{none}$/
$\mathsf{some}$ normalized public readback; and the status is
$\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.

Let $S,T,U:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be BEDC history source,
intermediate, and target carriers, let $\sim_S$, $\sim_T$, and $\sim_U$ be their
classifiers, and let
$$
\delta:\mathsf{DescentCertificate}(\mathsf{BHist},\mathsf{BHist},\sim_S,\sim_T)
$$
and
$$
\epsilon:\mathsf{DescentCertificate}(\mathsf{BHist},\mathsf{BHist},\sim_T,\sim_U).
$$
Assume payload preservation fields
$$
\rho_{\delta}:\forall a.\;S(a)\to T(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a))
$$
and
$$
\rho_{\epsilon}:\forall b.\;T(b)\to U(\epsilon.\mathsf{map}(b)).
$$
Assume endpoint stability for the second descent:
$$
\eta_{\epsilon}:\forall x\,y.\;
T(x)\to T(y)\to \hsame(x,y)\to
\hsame(\epsilon.\mathsf{map}(x),\epsilon.\mathsf{map}(y)).
$$
Write $\epsilon\circ\delta$ for the descent certificate whose map field is
$a\mapsto\epsilon.\mathsf{map}(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a))$ and whose respects field
sends $r:a\sim_S b$ to
$\epsilon.\mathsf{respects}(\delta.\mathsf{respects}(r))$. Define
$$
\rho_{\epsilon\circ\delta}(a,Sa)=
\rho_{\epsilon}(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a),\rho_{\delta}(a,Sa)).
$$
Let $\mathsf{cert}_S:\NameCert(S,\sim_S)$,
$\mathsf{cert}_T:\NameCert(T,\sim_T)$, and
$\mathsf{cert}_U:\NameCert(U,\sim_U)$. Assume
$\mathsf{TaggedOptionSourceHsameCompatible}(S,\sim_S)$,
$\mathsf{TaggedOptionSourceHsameCompatible}(T,\sim_T)$, and
$\mathsf{TaggedOptionSourceHsameCompatible}(U,\sim_U)$. For final endpoints
$m,m':\mathsf{BHist}$, normalized endpoints $u,u':\mathsf{BHist}$, and selected
visible target payloads $x,y:\mathsf{BHist}$, if
$$
\mathsf{TaggedOptionPayloadDescentImageClassifier}_{\epsilon\circ\delta}
(S,U,\rho_{\epsilon\circ\delta};m,m')
$$
and
$$
\hsame(m,u)\land \hsame(m',u')
$$
and
$$
U(x)\land U(y)\land
\hsame(u,\Eone(x))\land \hsame(u',\Eone(y)),
$$
then
$$
x\sim_U y
$$
and
$$
\forall x':\mathsf{BHist}.\;
U(x')\to
\hsame(u,\Eone(x'))\to
\hsame(x,x')\land x'\sim_U y
$$
and
$$
\forall y':\mathsf{BHist}.\;
U(y')\to
\hsame(u',\Eone(y'))\to
\hsame(y,y')\land x\sim_U y'
$$
and
$$
\neg\hsame(u,\emp)\land \neg\hsame(u',\emp).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:tagged-option-composite-image-classifier-normalized-visible-target-payload-classification}
to the composite image-classifier witness, the endpoint normalization witnesses,
and the selected visible presentation $U(x)$, $U(y)$,
$\hsame(u,\Eone(x))$, and $\hsame(u',\Eone(y))$. This gives the selected target
classification $x\sim_U y$.

Let $x'$ be a competing left visible payload with $U(x')$ and
$\hsame(u,\Eone(x'))$. Apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:tagged-option-composite-image-classifier-normalized-visible-payload-determinacy}
to the two complete visible pairs $(x,y)$ and $(x',y)$. The returned
conjunction contains $\hsame(x,x')$ and $x'\sim_U y$, which are exactly the
left-side canonicality and target-classification conclusions for $x'$.

Let $y'$ be a competing right visible payload with $U(y')$ and
$\hsame(u',\Eone(y'))$. Apply the same determinacy theorem to the two complete
visible pairs $(x,y)$ and $(x,y')$. The returned conjunction contains
$\hsame(y,y')$ and $x\sim_U y'$, which are exactly the right-side canonicality
and target-classification conclusions for $y'$.

It remains to record that the selected public endpoints are not absent. If
$\hsame(u,\emp)$ held, composing its symmetry with $\hsame(u,\Eone(x))$ would
give $\hsame(\emp,\Eone(x))$, contradicting BEDC history no-confusion via
$not\_hsame\_emp\_e1$.

Likewise, if $\hsame(u',\emp)$ held, composing its symmetry with
$\hsame(u',\Eone(y))$ would give $\hsame(\emp,\Eone(y))$, again contradicting
BEDC history no-confusion via $not\_hsame\_emp\_e1$.

Collect the selected target classification, the two universal one-sided
canonicality clauses, and the two non-absence conclusions.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Tagged option composite image classifier normalized one-sided visible public factorization]
\label{thm:tagged-option-composite-image-classifier-normalized-one-sided-visible-public-factorization}
This theorem fits the per-object template as follows: the traditional target is
endpoint-normalized functorial $\mathsf{OptionUp}$ one-sided visible public
factorization; the BEDC source is $\mathsf{BHist}$ with two composable
$\mathsf{DescentCertificate}$ maps; the carrier is
$\mathsf{TaggedOptionPayloadDescentImageCarrier}$; the classifier is
$\mathsf{TaggedOptionPayloadDescentImageClassifier}$; the stability obligations
are endpoint transport, branch synchronization, visible-payload factorization,
and intermediate classifier transport; the ledger is one specified normalized
visible endpoint together with the opposite public payload, intermediate tagged
endpoints, and source payload witnesses; the core theorem is one-sided visible
readback with factorization and uniqueness against all competing visible payloads
on the other side; exactness says the public endpoint already read by a consumer
exposes the concrete source and intermediate payloads behind the synchronized
public branch; the standard bridge is $\mathsf{none}$/$\mathsf{some}$ normalized
public readback; and the status is $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.

Let $S,T,U:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be BEDC history source,
intermediate, and target carriers, let $\sim_S$, $\sim_T$, and $\sim_U$ be their
classifiers, and let
$$
\delta:\mathsf{DescentCertificate}(\mathsf{BHist},\mathsf{BHist},\sim_S,\sim_T)
$$
and
$$
\epsilon:\mathsf{DescentCertificate}(\mathsf{BHist},\mathsf{BHist},\sim_T,\sim_U).
$$
Assume payload preservation fields
$$
\rho_{\delta}:\forall a.\;S(a)\to T(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a))
$$
and
$$
\rho_{\epsilon}:\forall b.\;T(b)\to U(\epsilon.\mathsf{map}(b)).
$$
Assume endpoint stability for the second descent:
$$
\eta_{\epsilon}:\forall x\,y.\;
T(x)\to T(y)\to \hsame(x,y)\to
\hsame(\epsilon.\mathsf{map}(x),\epsilon.\mathsf{map}(y)).
$$
Write $\epsilon\circ\delta$ for the descent certificate whose map field is
$a\mapsto\epsilon.\mathsf{map}(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a))$ and whose respects field
sends $r:a\sim_S b$ to
$\epsilon.\mathsf{respects}(\delta.\mathsf{respects}(r))$. Define
$$
\rho_{\epsilon\circ\delta}(a,Sa)=
\rho_{\epsilon}(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a),\rho_{\delta}(a,Sa)).
$$
Let $\mathsf{cert}_S:\NameCert(S,\sim_S)$,
$\mathsf{cert}_T:\NameCert(T,\sim_T)$, and
$\mathsf{cert}_U:\NameCert(U,\sim_U)$. Assume
$\mathsf{TaggedOptionSourceHsameCompatible}(S,\sim_S)$,
$\mathsf{TaggedOptionSourceHsameCompatible}(T,\sim_T)$, and
$\mathsf{TaggedOptionSourceHsameCompatible}(U,\sim_U)$. For final endpoints
$m,m':\mathsf{BHist}$ and normalized endpoints $u,u':\mathsf{BHist}$, if
$$
\mathsf{TaggedOptionPayloadDescentImageClassifier}_{\epsilon\circ\delta}
(S,U,\rho_{\epsilon\circ\delta};m,m')
$$
and
$$
\hsame(m,u)\land \hsame(m',u'),
$$
then the following two one-sided visible public factorization clauses hold:
\begin{enumerate}
\item For every $x:\mathsf{BHist}$, if $U(x)$ and $\hsame(u,\Eone(x))$, then
there exist $y,k,k',p,q,a,b:\mathsf{BHist}$ such that
\begin{enumerate}
\item $U(y)$, $\hsame(u',\Eone(y))$, and $x\sim_U y$;
\item $\mathsf{TaggedOptionPayloadDescentImageClassifier}_{\delta}
(S,T,\rho_{\delta};k,k')$;
\item $\mathsf{TaggedOptionMapRel}_{\epsilon}(T,U,\rho_{\epsilon};k,u)$ and
$\mathsf{TaggedOptionMapRel}_{\epsilon}(T,U,\rho_{\epsilon};k',u')$;
\item $T(p)$, $T(q)$, $U(\epsilon.\mathsf{map}(p))$, and
$U(\epsilon.\mathsf{map}(q))$;
\item $\hsame(k,\Eone(p))$, $\hsame(k',\Eone(q))$,
$\hsame(x,\epsilon.\mathsf{map}(p))$, and
$\hsame(y,\epsilon.\mathsf{map}(q))$;
\item $S(a)$, $S(b)$, $a\sim_S b$, $T(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a))$, and
$T(\delta.\mathsf{map}(b))$;
\item $\hsame(p,\delta.\mathsf{map}(a))$ and
$\hsame(q,\delta.\mathsf{map}(b))$;
\item $p\sim_T q$;
\item $\neg\hsame(u,\emp)$ and $\neg\hsame(u',\emp)$;
\item for every $y':\mathsf{BHist}$, if $U(y')$ and
$\hsame(u',\Eone(y'))$, then $\hsame(y,y')$ and $x\sim_U y'$.
\end{enumerate}
\item For every $y:\mathsf{BHist}$, if $U(y)$ and $\hsame(u',\Eone(y))$, then
there exist $x,k,k',p,q,a,b:\mathsf{BHist}$ such that
\begin{enumerate}
\item $U(x)$, $\hsame(u,\Eone(x))$, and $x\sim_U y$;
\item $\mathsf{TaggedOptionPayloadDescentImageClassifier}_{\delta}
(S,T,\rho_{\delta};k,k')$;
\item $\mathsf{TaggedOptionMapRel}_{\epsilon}(T,U,\rho_{\epsilon};k,u)$ and
$\mathsf{TaggedOptionMapRel}_{\epsilon}(T,U,\rho_{\epsilon};k',u')$;
\item $T(p)$, $T(q)$, $U(\epsilon.\mathsf{map}(p))$, and
$U(\epsilon.\mathsf{map}(q))$;
\item $\hsame(k,\Eone(p))$, $\hsame(k',\Eone(q))$,
$\hsame(x,\epsilon.\mathsf{map}(p))$, and
$\hsame(y,\epsilon.\mathsf{map}(q))$;
\item $S(a)$, $S(b)$, $a\sim_S b$, $T(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a))$, and
$T(\delta.\mathsf{map}(b))$;
\item $\hsame(p,\delta.\mathsf{map}(a))$ and
$\hsame(q,\delta.\mathsf{map}(b))$;
\item $p\sim_T q$;
\item $\neg\hsame(u,\emp)$ and $\neg\hsame(u',\emp)$;
\item for every $x':\mathsf{BHist}$, if $U(x')$ and
$\hsame(u,\Eone(x'))$, then $\hsame(x,x')$ and $x'\sim_U y$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Fix a left visible payload $x$ with $U(x)$ and $\hsame(u,\Eone(x))$. Apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:tagged-option-composite-image-classifier-normalized-one-sided-visible-branch-synchronization}
to the composite image-classifier witness, the endpoint normalization witnesses,
and the displayed descent, preservation, stability, certificate, and
compatibility data. The left-to-right branch supplies a right payload $y$ with
$U(y)$, $\hsame(u',\Eone(y))$, $x\sim_U y$, the two non-absence facts, and the
universal competing-right-payload uniqueness clause.

Apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:tagged-option-composite-image-classifier-normalized-visible-payload-factorization}
to the same composite classifier, the same endpoint normalization witnesses, and
the specified visible pair $(x,y)$. This supplies intermediate endpoints
$k,k'$, intermediate payloads $p,q$, and source payloads $a,b$ with the
factorization clauses through $\delta$ and $\epsilon$.

It remains to identify the intermediate payloads as classified. Apply
$\delta.\mathsf{respects}$ to $a\sim_S b$. Compatibility for $T$ turns
$\hsame(p,\delta.\mathsf{map}(a))$ and
$\hsame(q,\delta.\mathsf{map}(b))$ into classifier links, and symmetry and
transitivity from $\mathsf{cert}_T$ compose these links into $p\sim_T q$.

Package $y,k,k',p,q,a,b$ with the visible target facts, the factorization data,
$p\sim_T q$, the retained non-absence facts, and the competing-right-payload
uniqueness clause. This proves the left one-sided factorization clause.

For the right visible payload $y$ with $U(y)$ and $\hsame(u',\Eone(y))$, apply
the same branch-synchronization theorem and use its right-to-left branch. It
supplies a left payload $x$ with $U(x)$, $\hsame(u,\Eone(x))$, $x\sim_U y$, the
two non-absence facts, and the universal competing-left-payload uniqueness
clause.

Apply the normalized visible payload factorization theorem to the visible pair
$(x,y)$. The same factorization data $k,k',p,q,a,b$ is obtained, because the
specified right payload has now been paired with the synchronized left payload.

As in the left clause, $\delta.\mathsf{respects}$, compatibility for $T$, and
symmetry and transitivity from $\mathsf{cert}_T$ compose the source classifier
fact and the two readback links into $p\sim_T q$.

Package $x,k,k',p,q,a,b$ with the visible target facts, the factorization data,
$p\sim_T q$, the retained non-absence facts, and the competing-left-payload
uniqueness clause. This proves the symmetric right one-sided factorization
clause.
\end{proof}
