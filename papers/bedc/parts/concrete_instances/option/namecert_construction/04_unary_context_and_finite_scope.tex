\section{Option history classifier unary two-sided context}
\label{sec:option-history-classifier-unary-two-sided-context}

\begin{theorem}[Option history carrier unary two-sided context]
\label{thm:option-history-carrier-unary-two-sided-context}
Let $p,q,h:\mathsf{BHist}$. If $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(p)$ and
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(q)$ hold, then
$$
\mathsf{OptionHistoryCarrier}_{\mathsf{UnaryHistory}}(h)
\Longleftrightarrow
\mathsf{OptionHistoryCarrier}_{\mathsf{UnaryHistory}}
  (\mathsf{append}(p,\mathsf{append}(h,q))).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the forward direction, first apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:option-history-carrier-unary-suffix-closed} with suffix $q$.
Then apply Theorem~\ref{thm:option-history-carrier-unary-prefix-closed} with
prefix $p$.

For the reverse direction, split the contextual nullable carrier witness.

In the absent branch, use $\mathsf{hsame}\_\mathsf{iff}\_\mathsf{eq}$ to turn
the empty contextual endpoint witness into equality. Apply
$\mathsf{append}\_\mathsf{eq}\_\mathsf{empty}\_\mathsf{iff}$ to the outer
append, and then again to the inner append, recovering $\hsame(h,\emp)$.

In the source branch, use
$\mathsf{unary}\_\mathsf{append}\_\mathsf{right}\_\mathsf{factor}$ on the
outer append to recover $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(\mathsf{append}(h,q))$. Then
use $\mathsf{unary}\_\mathsf{append}\_\mathsf{left}\_\mathsf{factor}$ on the
inner append to recover $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(h)$.

These two recovered branches are exactly the nullable carrier alternatives for
$h$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.OptionUp.OptionHistoryCarrier\_unary\_two\_sided\_context\_iff}

\begin{theorem}[Option history classifier unary two-sided context]
\label{thm:option-history-classifier-unary-two-sided-context}
This theorem fits the ten-field template as follows: the traditional target is
context-stable nullable optional history classification; the BEDC source is
$\mathsf{BHist}$ with $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$, $\mathsf{append}$, and
$\hsame$; the carrier is
$\mathsf{OptionHistoryCarrier}_{\mathsf{UnaryHistory}}$; the classifier is
$\mathsf{OptionHistoryClassifier}_{\mathsf{UnaryHistory}}$; the stability
obligations are unary prefix and suffix closure together with cancellation;
the ledger is the hidden common unary context; the core theorem is the
displayed equivalence; exactness is recovery of the uncloaked classifier by
append cancellation and unary factor readback; the standard bridge is option
values under common unary context; and the status is $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.

Let $p,q,h,k:\mathsf{BHist}$. If $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(p)$ and
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(q)$ hold, then
$$
\mathsf{OptionHistoryClassifier}_{\mathsf{UnaryHistory}}(h,k)
\Longleftrightarrow
\mathsf{OptionHistoryClassifier}_{\mathsf{UnaryHistory}}
  (\mathsf{append}(p,\mathsf{append}(h,q)),
   \mathsf{append}(p,\mathsf{append}(k,q))).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the forward direction, first apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:option-history-classifier-unary-suffix-closed} with suffix
$q$ to place the common unary right context on both endpoints. Then apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:option-history-classifier-unary-prefix-closed} with prefix
$p$ to place the common unary left context.

For the reverse direction, unpack the contextual nullable classifier into its
two endpoint carrier witnesses and the contextual $\hsame$ field. Each
endpoint carrier is read back from the surrounding context. In an absent
branch, use $\hsame\_\mathsf{iff}\_\mathsf{eq}$ and
$\mathsf{append}\_\mathsf{eq}\_\mathsf{empty}\_\mathsf{iff}$ twice to recover
the corresponding uncloaked empty endpoint witness.

In a source branch, use
$\mathsf{unary}\_\mathsf{append}\_\mathsf{right}\_\mathsf{factor}$ on the
outer append and
$\mathsf{unary}\_\mathsf{append}\_\mathsf{left}\_\mathsf{factor}$ on the inner
append to recover $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(h)$ or
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(k)$ as required by the nullable carrier.

It remains to recover the uncloaked classifier relation. Convert the
contextual $\hsame$ field to equality, cancel the common left prefix by
$\mathsf{append}\_\mathsf{left}\_\mathsf{cancel}$, and then cancel the common
right suffix by $\mathsf{append}\_\mathsf{right}\_\mathsf{cancel}$. Repack the
two recovered endpoint carriers together with the recovered $\hsame(h,k)$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.OptionUp.OptionHistoryClassifier\_unary\_two\_sided\_context\_iff}

\begin{theorem}[Option unary context composition law]
\label{thm:option-unary-context-composition}
Let $p,q,r,s,h,k:\mathsf{BHist}$. For histories $u,v,x$, write
$C_{u,v}(x):=\mathsf{append}(u,\mathsf{append}(x,v))$. If
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(p)$, $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(q)$,
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(r)$, and $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(s)$ hold, then
$$
\mathsf{OptionHistoryClassifier}_{\mathsf{UnaryHistory}}(h,k)
\Longleftrightarrow
\mathsf{OptionHistoryClassifier}_{\mathsf{UnaryHistory}}
  (C_{p,q}(C_{r,s}(h)),
   C_{\mathsf{append}(p,r),\mathsf{append}(s,q)}(k)).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Set $L:=\mathsf{append}(p,r)$ and $R:=\mathsf{append}(s,q)$. Unary append
closure applied to $p,r$ and to $s,q$ gives $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(L)$ and
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(R)$. The associativity laws for BEDC append identify
$C_{p,q}(C_{r,s}(x))$ with $C_{L,R}(x)$ for every history $x$; in particular,
they provide the $\hsame$ witnesses needed to move classifiers between the
nested source endpoint and the normalized endpoint.

For the forward direction, apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:option-history-classifier-unary-two-sided-context} to the
classifier between $h$ and $k$ with unary contexts $L$ and $R$. This gives
$$
\mathsf{OptionHistoryClassifier}_{\mathsf{UnaryHistory}}
  (C_{L,R}(h),C_{L,R}(k)).
$$

Transport the source endpoint along the associativity $\hsame$ witness from
$C_{L,R}(h)$ to $C_{p,q}(C_{r,s}(h))$. The target endpoint is already
$C_{L,R}(k)=C_{\mathsf{append}(p,r),\mathsf{append}(s,q)}(k)$ by the
definitions of $L$ and $R$.

For the reverse direction, start with the displayed classifier and transport
its source endpoint back across the same associativity witness. This produces
$$
\mathsf{OptionHistoryClassifier}_{\mathsf{UnaryHistory}}
  (C_{L,R}(h),C_{L,R}(k)).
$$

Apply the reverse implication of
Theorem~\ref{thm:option-history-classifier-unary-two-sided-context} with
contexts $L$ and $R$. Append cancellation inside that theorem removes the
common normalized ledger and recovers
$\mathsf{OptionHistoryClassifier}_{\mathsf{UnaryHistory}}(h,k)$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.OptionUp.OptionHistoryClassifier\_unary\_context\_composition\_iff}

\begin{theorem}[Option finite-data scoped row exhaustion]
\label{thm:option-finite-data-scoped-row-exhaustion}
The $\OptionUp$ finite-data public surface exports only the following rows:
nullable and tagged option carrier rows; absent and present branch readbacks;
payload-descent image carrier and image-classifier rows; componentwise
$\hsame$ transports; $\Cont$ routes induced by the displayed branch data;
source $\NameCert$ rows; and the visible $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance already
listed by the unary-context, tagged-option semantic, and payload-descent public
packages. It exports no host $\mathsf{Option}$ object, quotient row, hidden
payload equality row, or unlogged branch eliminator.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The nullable side is exhausted by the moved unary-context theorems above. They
only transport $\mathsf{OptionHistoryCarrier}_{\mathsf{UnaryHistory}}$ and
$\mathsf{OptionHistoryClassifier}_{\mathsf{UnaryHistory}}$ through common
unary ledgers, and their proofs recover the uncloaked rows by append
cancellation and constructor-branch readback.

The tagged semantic rows are the explicit carrier, classifier, exactness, and
$\NameCert$ rows recorded in the tagged option semantic package. Their branch
analysis has only the absent constructor and the present constructor carrying
a source witness, so projecting any public row yields either $\emp$ readback
or an $\Eone(a)$ readback together with the source certificate data.

The payload-descent image rows are exhausted by the public readback and image
classifier blocks. A target endpoint is public only through a source tagged
endpoint and a displayed $\mathsf{TaggedOptionMapRel}$ route; a classified
target pair is public only through two such routes and a source tagged
classifier witness.

Combining these projections gives row exhaustion. Constructor branch
exhaustion accounts for absent and present cases, componentwise $\hsame$
transport accounts for endpoint replacement, $\Cont$ records only the displayed
routes, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance records only the listed certificates and
descent data. None of these projections produces a host option value, a
quotient representative, a payload equality outside $\hsame$ or the source
classifier, or a branch eliminator without a logged constructor case.
\end{proof}
