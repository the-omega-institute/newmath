\section{Tagged option map relation constructor normal form}
\label{sec:option-maprel-constructor-normal-form}

\begin{theorem}[Tagged option map relation constructor normal form]
\label{thm:tagged-option-maprel-constructor-normal-form}
This theorem fits the per-object template as follows: the traditional target is
constructor-facing readback for the tagged option map relation; the BEDC source
is $\mathsf{BHist}$ with one $\mathsf{DescentCertificate}$ map; the carrier is
the visible tagged option endpoint shape; the classifier is the source and
target history classifiers carried by the descent certificate; the stability
obligation is branch preservation, payload readback, and zero-tag exclusion; the
ledger records the absent branch, the present branch, and the absence of
zero-tag endpoints; the core theorem is the displayed constructor normal form;
exactness says a map-relation witness has exactly the empty branch or a
transported one-payload branch; the standard bridge is $\mathsf{none}$/$\mathsf{some}$
map readback; and the status is $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.

Let $S,T:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be BEDC history source and target
carriers, let $\sim_S$ and $\sim_T$ be their classifiers, let
$$
\delta:\mathsf{DescentCertificate}(\mathsf{BHist},\mathsf{BHist},\sim_S,\sim_T),
$$
and assume payload preservation
$$
\rho_{\delta}:\forall a.\;S(a)\to T(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a)).
$$
For histories $h,k:\mathsf{BHist}$, every witness
$$
\mathsf{TaggedOptionMapRel}_{\delta}(S,T,\rho_{\delta};h,k)
$$
implies the following clauses simultaneously.
\begin{enumerate}
  \item Every zero-tag presentation is impossible at either endpoint:
  $$
  \forall t:\mathsf{BHist}.\;
  (\hsame(h,\Ezero(t))\to\bot)\land
  (\hsame(k,\Ezero(t))\to\bot).
  $$

  \item The absent branch is visible on both sides:
  $$
  \hsame(h,\emp)\Longleftrightarrow\hsame(k,\emp).
  $$

  \item The present branch is visible on both sides:
  $$
  (\exists a:\mathsf{BHist}.\;S(a)\land\hsame(h,\Eone(a)))
  \Longleftrightarrow
  (\exists b:\mathsf{BHist}.\;T(b)\land\hsame(k,\Eone(b))).
  $$

  \item Visible source and target payloads read back to one transported
  payload:
  $$
  \forall u\,v:\mathsf{BHist}.\;
  S(u)\land\hsame(h,\Eone(u))\land
  T(v)\land\hsame(k,\Eone(v))
  \to
  \exists a:\mathsf{BHist}.\;
  S(a)\land T(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a))\land
  \hsame(u,a)\land\hsame(v,\delta.\mathsf{map}(a)).
  $$
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Split the map-relation witness into its absent and present branches. In the
absent branch, the stored endpoint presentations are $\hsame(h,\emp)$ and
$\hsame(k,\emp)$; in the present branch, the stored presentations are
$\hsame(h,\Eone(a))$ and
$\hsame(k,\Eone(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a)))$ for the exposed payload $a$.

First prove zero-tag exclusion. Fix $t:\mathsf{BHist}$. In the absent branch,
any presentation $\hsame(h,\Ezero(t))$ or $\hsame(k,\Ezero(t))$ composes with
the corresponding empty presentation and contradicts generated-history
no-confusion between $\emp$ and $\Ezero(t)$. In the present branch, any such
presentation composes with the corresponding one-tag presentation and
contradicts generated-history no-confusion between $\Eone$ and $\Ezero$.

Next obtain the visible branch clauses by applying
Theorem~\ref{thm:tagged-option-maprel-visible-branch-equivalence} to the same
map-relation witness. Its two projections are exactly the absent-branch
equivalence and the present-branch equivalence in the statement.

Finally let $u,v:\mathsf{BHist}$ be visible source and target payloads with the
displayed endpoint presentations. Apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:tagged-option-maprel-visible-payload-readback} to the
map-relation witness and to these four visible payload facts. The produced
payload $a$ carries $S(a)$, $T(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a))$, and the two readback
witnesses required by the last clause.
\end{proof}

$\hsame(k',\Eone(\delta.\mathsf{map}(b)))$.

$\delta.\mathsf{map}(a)\sim_T\delta.\mathsf{map}(b)$.

The two target carrier witnesses, the two target endpoint presentations, and

$\mathsf{TaggedOptionHistoryClassifier}_{T,\sim_T}(k,k')$. Package $a$ and $b$
with the retained source facts, target facts, endpoint presentations, source
classification, and target classifier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Tagged option map relation exclusive visible readback is single-valued]
\label{thm:tagged-option-maprel-exclusive-visible-readback-single-valued}
This theorem fits the per-object template as follows: the traditional target is
constructor-facing option map readback; the BEDC source is $\mathsf{BHist}$ with
one $\mathsf{DescentCertificate}$; the carrier is the visible tagged option
endpoint shape; the classifiers are the source and target classifiers
transported by the descent certificate; the stability obligations are branch
preservation, no-confusion, and one-constructor inversion; the ledger separates
the absent branch from the transported present branch; the core theorem is
exclusive visible readback with uniqueness; exactness is a disjoint
absent-or-present map-relation package; the standard bridge is
$\mathsf{none}$/$\mathsf{some}$ map readback; and the status is
$\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.

Let $S,T:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be BEDC history source and target
carriers, let $\sim_S$ and $\sim_T$ be their classifiers, let
$$
\delta:\mathsf{DescentCertificate}(\mathsf{BHist},\mathsf{BHist},\sim_S,\sim_T),
$$
and assume payload preservation
$$
\rho_{\delta}:\forall a.\;S(a)\to T(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a)).
$$
For histories $h,k:\mathsf{BHist}$, every witness
$$
\mathsf{TaggedOptionMapRel}_{\delta}(S,T,\rho_{\delta};h,k)
$$
implies exactly one of the following visible readback branches.
\begin{enumerate}
  \item The absent branch holds on both endpoints:
  $$
  \hsame(h,\emp)\land
  \hsame(k,\emp)\land
  (\forall a':\mathsf{BHist}.\;S(a')\to\hsame(h,\Eone(a'))\to\bot)\land
  (\forall b:\mathsf{BHist}.\;T(b)\to\hsame(k,\Eone(b))\to\bot).
  $$

  \item The present branch has one visible source payload and its transported
  target payload:
  $$
  \exists a:\mathsf{BHist}.\;
  S(a)\land
  T(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a))\land
  \hsame(h,\Eone(a))\land
  \hsame(k,\Eone(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a)))\land
  (\hsame(h,\emp)\to\bot)\land
  (\hsame(k,\emp)\to\bot)\land
  (\forall a':\mathsf{BHist}.\;S(a')\to\hsame(h,\Eone(a'))\to\hsame(a,a'))\land
  (\forall b:\mathsf{BHist}.\;T(b)\to
    \hsame(k,\Eone(b))\to\hsame(b,\delta.\mathsf{map}(a))).
  $$
\end{enumerate}
The two displayed alternatives are disjoint, so the map-relation witness packages
the absent case and the single-valued visible present readback without any
additional endpoint choice.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Case-analyze the
$\mathsf{TaggedOptionMapRel}_{\delta}(S,T,\rho_{\delta};h,k)$ witness.

In the absent branch, the constructor supplies $\hsame(h,\emp)$ and
$\hsame(k,\emp)$. If a source presentation $\hsame(h,\Eone(a'))$ were also
visible, generated-history no-confusion between $\emp$ and $\Eone(a')$ gives
$\bot$; the same argument at the target endpoint rules out every visible
$\Eone(b)$ presentation. These four facts assemble the absent alternative.

In the present branch, retain the constructor payload $a$. Payload preservation
gives $T(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a))$, and the constructor gives the endpoint
presentations $\hsame(h,\Eone(a))$ and
$\hsame(k,\Eone(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a)))$.

It remains to show non-absence and uniqueness. A competing
$\hsame(h,\emp)$ contradicts $\hsame(h,\Eone(a))$ by one-versus-empty
no-confusion, and the target endpoint is identical with
$\delta.\mathsf{map}(a)$ in place of $a$.

For any competing source presentation $\hsame(h,\Eone(a'))$, one-constructor
inversion for $\Eone$ yields $\hsame(a,a')$. For any competing target
presentation $\hsame(k,\Eone(b))$, the same inversion applied to the stored
presentation $\hsame(k,\Eone(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a)))$ yields
$\hsame(b,\delta.\mathsf{map}(a))$. These clauses assemble the present
alternative, and empty-versus-one no-confusion also proves that the two displayed
alternatives cannot overlap.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Tagged option composite factorization has a canonical intermediate endpoint]
\label{thm:tagged-option-maprel-composite-factorization-canonical-intermediate}
Let $S,T,U:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be BEDC history source,
intermediate, and target carriers, let $\sim_S$, $\sim_T$, and $\sim_U$ be their
classifiers, and let
$$
\delta:\mathsf{DescentCertificate}(\mathsf{BHist},\mathsf{BHist},\sim_S,\sim_T)
$$
and
$$
\epsilon:\mathsf{DescentCertificate}(\mathsf{BHist},\mathsf{BHist},\sim_T,\sim_U).
$$
Assume payload preservation fields
$$
\rho_{\delta}:\forall a.\;S(a)\to T(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a))
$$
and
$$
\rho_{\epsilon}:\forall b.\;T(b)\to U(\epsilon.\mathsf{map}(b)).
$$
Assume endpoint stability for both descents:
$$
\eta_{\delta}:\forall x\,y.\;
S(x)\to S(y)\to \hsame(x,y)\to
\hsame(\delta.\mathsf{map}(x),\delta.\mathsf{map}(y))
$$
and
$$
\eta_{\epsilon}:\forall x\,y.\;
T(x)\to T(y)\to \hsame(x,y)\to
\hsame(\epsilon.\mathsf{map}(x),\epsilon.\mathsf{map}(y)).
$$
For histories $h,k,k',m:\mathsf{BHist}$, suppose the same composed endpoint pair
factors through both $k$ and $k'$:
$$
\mathsf{TaggedOptionMapRel}_{\delta}(S,T,\rho_{\delta};h,k)
\land
\mathsf{TaggedOptionMapRel}_{\epsilon}(T,U,\rho_{\epsilon};k,m)
$$
and
$$
\mathsf{TaggedOptionMapRel}_{\delta}(S,T,\rho_{\delta};h,k')
\land
\mathsf{TaggedOptionMapRel}_{\epsilon}(T,U,\rho_{\epsilon};k',m).
$$
Then either all four visible endpoints are absent,
$$
\hsame(h,\emp)\land
\hsame(k,\emp)\land
\hsame(k',\emp)\land
\hsame(m,\emp),
$$
or there is one source payload $a:\mathsf{BHist}$ such that
$$
S(a)\land
T(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a))\land
U(\epsilon.\mathsf{map}(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a)))\land
\hsame(h,\Eone(a))\land
\hsame(k,\Eone(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a)))\land
\hsame(k',\Eone(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a)))\land
\hsame(m,\Eone(\epsilon.\mathsf{map}(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a)))).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Split the first $\delta$ factor through $k$.

In the absent branch, $h$ and $k$ are visibly $\emp$. The second $\delta$ factor
through $k'$ cannot be present, because it would present the same source
endpoint $h$ as both $\emp$ and $\Eone(a)$. Hence the second $\delta$ factor is
also absent, giving $\hsame(k',\emp)$. The first $\epsilon$ factor cannot be
present for the same no-confusion reason at $k$, so it is absent and gives
$\hsame(m,\emp)$. These four presentations assemble the absent alternative.

In the present branch, the first $\delta$ factor exposes a source payload $a$
with $S(a)$, $T(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a))$,
$\hsame(h,\Eone(a))$, and
$\hsame(k,\Eone(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a)))$.

Split the second $\delta$ factor. Its absent branch would present $h$ as
$\emp$, contradicting the stored one-constructor presentation of $h$. In its
present branch it exposes a payload $a'$ with $S(a')$ and
$\hsame(h,\Eone(a'))$. One-constructor inversion at $h$ yields
$\hsame(a,a')$, and $\eta_{\delta}$ transports this to
$\hsame(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a),\delta.\mathsf{map}(a'))$. Rewriting the
intermediate endpoint presentation for $k'$ gives
$\hsame(k',\Eone(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a)))$.

Now split the first $\epsilon$ factor. Its absent branch would present $k$ as
$\emp$, contradicting the stored presentation
$\hsame(k,\Eone(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a)))$. In the present branch it exposes an
intermediate payload $b$ with $T(b)$ and final endpoint
$\hsame(m,\Eone(\epsilon.\mathsf{map}(b)))$. One-constructor inversion at $k$
gives $\hsame(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a),b)$, and $\eta_{\epsilon}$ transports the
map endpoint to
$\hsame(\epsilon.\mathsf{map}(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a)),
\epsilon.\mathsf{map}(b))$.

By one-constructor congruence and transitivity of $\hsame$, the final endpoint
presentation rewrites to

\end{proof}
