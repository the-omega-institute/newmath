\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\SobolevEmbeddingUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-sobolev-embedding-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Adams and Fournier on Sobolev spaces, the $\SobolevEmbeddingUp$ packet records the finite BEDC naming surface for an embedding assertion read from a Sobolev source certificate into a normed, Banach, Hilbert, metric, or compact-metric target certificate. It is a recognised analysis neighbour of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-sobolev-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-norm-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-banach-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-hilbert-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The chapter does not import a host function space; it names the displayed dependency rows that later PDE and functional-analysis certificates can cite.

\begin{definition}[Sobolev embedding carrier]
\label{def:sobolev-embedding-carrier}
A $\SobolevEmbeddingUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(D,S,N,E,T,R,L,P)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ records the domain regularity and scalar $\RealUp$ boundary on which the source observations are read;
\item $S$ is the $\SobolevUp$ source row, including the visible weak-derivative, integrability, and base-metric rows supplied by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-sobolev-namecert};
\item $N$ is the target norm row, read through $\NormUp$, $\BanachUp$, or $\HilbertUp$ according to the named target surface;
\item $E$ records the exponent and regularity comparison rows that license the displayed embedding estimate;
\item $T$ is the target readback row, which may point to normed, Banach, Hilbert, metric, or compact-metric evidence but never to a hidden host function;
\item $R$ stores the componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the domain, Sobolev source, norm target, exponent rows, target readback, ledger, and provenance rows;
\item $L$ is the finite ledger stating the norm-bound budget and the source-to-target observations consumed by the embedding read;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\SobolevEmbeddingUp$, $\SobolevUp$, $\NormUp$, $\BanachUp$, $\HilbertUp$, $\MetricSpaceUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$, together with the local naming row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a host function representative, almost-everywhere quotient equality, Rellich compactness, full PDE regularity, open-cover compactness, countable choice, or a selected subsequence.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Sobolev embedding NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:sobolev-embedding-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\SobolevEmbeddingUp}$ surface for the carrier of \autoref{def:sobolev-embedding-carrier} consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission: an accepted packet exposes only $Q=(D,S,N,E,T,R,L,P)$ and admits no hidden analytic coordinate;
\item classifier stability: two accepted packets compare only through componentwise $\hsame$ transport of the domain, Sobolev source, norm target, exponent, target readback, ledger, provenance, and naming rows;
\item derivative-row transport: the weak-derivative observations inherited from $S$ remain attached to the same embedding read whenever $R$ transports the displayed rows;
\item norm-bound ledger preservation: the estimate recorded in $L$ is preserved under the same displayed transport and cannot be strengthened by an unrecorded bound;
\item target readback: every target observation factors through $T$ and through the named $\NormUp$, $\BanachUp$, $\HilbertUp$, $\MetricSpaceUp$, or $\CompactMetricUp$ surface;
\item ledger non-escape: no consumer can recover Rellich compactness, full PDE regularity, open-cover compactness, host function equality, choice, or a selected representative from the packet.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from the eight displayed rows of $Q$. The source and target data are visible certificates, and the local packet contains no row for host functions or external compactness.

For classifier stability, compare two packets row by row using the stored $\hsame$ routes in $R$. The Sobolev source, norm target, exponent rows, target readback, ledger, provenance, and local naming row are transported componentwise.

Derivative-row transport and norm-bound preservation are ledger facts. The derivative observations are those already exposed by $S$, while the bound is the row stored in $L$; the replay can reread those rows but cannot create an additional derivative or a sharper estimate.

Finally, target readback is forced through $T$ and the named neighbouring certificates. Since the carrier has no coordinate for the excluded analytic principles, ledger exhaustion gives the non-escape boundary.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Sobolev embedding finite-energy localization]
\label{thm:sobolev-embedding-finite-energy-localization}
Every accepted $\SobolevEmbeddingUp$ packet exposes only the displayed local Sobolev, norm, metric, exponent, transport, and ledger data in $D,S,N,E,T,R,L,P$. The finite source rows may localize an energy or derivative budget to the named embedding read, but they do not assert Rellich compactness, full PDE regularity, open-cover compactness, host function equality, countable choice, or a selected representative.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect a downstream consumer of the embedding packet. It can read the domain row $D$, the Sobolev source row $S$, the norm target row $N$, the exponent and regularity rows $E$, the target readback $T$, the transport ledger $R$, the norm-bound ledger $L$, and the provenance row $P$.

This is enough to localize the finite energy or weak-derivative budget named by the source rows. It is not enough to produce compactness, regularity, host equality, choice, or representatives, because none of those objects appears as a row of the carrier or as an output of the componentwise transport.
\end{proof}
