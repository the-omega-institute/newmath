The $\ScienceBridgeUp$ packet realizes the science-bridge site of \autoref{sec:visions-philosophy-science-bridge} as one finite NameCert surface. It sits after the scientific-object carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-scientificobject-namecert}, the physical-model audit of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-physical-model-audit-namecert}, the reality-constrained truth certificate of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-reality-constrained-truth-cert-namecert}, the standard-bridge audit packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-standardbridgeauditpacket-namecert}, and the gap/failure bridge audit of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-gap-failure-bridge-audit-namecert}. Those siblings expose object records, physical audit, truth rows, bridge preservation, and typed gap/failure rows; this packet binds them before any consumer reads a scientific bridge as an accepted BEDC object.

\begin{definition}[Science bridge carrier]
\label{def:science-bridge-carrier}
A $\ScienceBridgeUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(R,O,A,T,B,G,F,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $R$ is the records row, storing the source histories, observation traces, instrument records, and finite ledgers from which the bridge is read.
\item $O$ is the $\ScientificObjectUp$ row exposing the object record, classifier, invariant, empirical ledger, bridge row, gap report, audit, truth, open-fit, domain, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming surfaces.
\item $A$ is the $\PhysicalModelAuditUp$ row exposing source traces, target phenomenon, observed signatures, model signatures, audit classifier, tower data, failure surface, and audit strength.
\item $T$ is the $\RealityConstrainedTruthCertUp$ row exposing the truth-certificate source, signature, classifier, tower, stability, descent, invariant, ledger, and failure fields.
\item $B$ is the $\StandardBridgeAuditPacketUp$ row exposing encoding, decoding, round-trip, theorem preservation, no-host-leak, transport, provenance, and local naming data for a proposed bridge.
\item $G$ is the $\GapFailureBridgeAuditUp$ row exposing the typed gap, failure branch, bridge row, no-host-leak row, axis-separation row, audit route, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming data.
\item $F$ is the failure-surface row, recording falsifying observations, open prediction gaps, bridge defeats, and domain exits that remain visible to downstream consumers.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $R,O,A,T,B,G,F,C,P,N$.
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which records, objects, audits, truth certificates, bridge audits, gaps, failure surfaces, transports, provenance, and local naming are replayed.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ScienceBridgeUp$, $\ScientificObjectUp$, $\PhysicalModelAuditUp$, $\RealityConstrainedTruthCertUp$, $\StandardBridgeAuditPacketUp$, $\GapFailureBridgeAuditUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ScienceBridgeUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares carriers only through these rows. It has no coordinate for host scientific-object identity, unledgered physical truth, standard-model authority outside the bridge audit, a bridge-checked export without a gap/falsification row, or a repair of a failed bridge by local naming alone.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Science bridge NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:science-bridge-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:science-bridge-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\ScienceBridgeUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $S=(R,O,A,T,B,G,F,H,C,P,N)$, records exposure through $R$, scientific-object exposure through $O$, physical-model audit exposure through $A$, reality-constrained truth exposure through $T$, standard-bridge audit exposure through $B$, gap/falsification exposure through $G$, failure-surface exposure through $F$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ScienceBridgeUp.TasteGate.ScienceBridgeTasteGate\_single\_carrier\_alignment}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet to its displayed rows. The records row $R$ supplies the finite source data, while $O,A,T,B,G,F$ supply the scientific-object, physical-audit, truth-certificate, standard-bridge-audit, gap/falsification, and failure-surface coordinates.

Read the transport and replay rows next. The componentwise $\hsame$ row $H$ preserves only the displayed coordinates, and the continuation row $C$ replays only the routes listed by the packet.

Finally apply provenance and local naming. The package row $P$ records the sibling surfaces used by the carrier, and $N$ names the resulting finite packet. Since the carrier has no further coordinate, the listed rows exhaust the local $\NameCert$ obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Science bridge axis separation]
\label{thm:science-bridge-axis-separation}
An accepted $\ScienceBridgeUp$ carrier cannot read scientific-object maturity, physical-model audit strength, reality-constrained truth, standard-bridge preservation, and gap/falsification visibility as one axis. The rows $O,A,T,B,G,F$ remain distinct under the same $R,H,C,P,N$ support, so a consumer may not promote a bridge row to physical truth, erase the failure surface, or treat the gap/falsification row as discharged by local naming.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the carrier of \autoref{def:science-bridge-carrier}. The rows $O,A,T,B,G,F$ are separate coordinates, and each is transported and replayed through $H$ and $C$ without being identified with another coordinate.

Use \autoref{thm:science-bridge-namecert-obligations}. The local certificate admits scientific-object, physical-audit, truth-certificate, standard-bridge-audit, gap/falsification, and failure-surface rows separately before provenance or naming can be consumed.

Thus a bridge-preservation read cannot become physical truth, and a local name cannot erase an open gap or failure surface. Any such reading would collapse coordinates that the accepted packet stores separately.
\end{proof}
