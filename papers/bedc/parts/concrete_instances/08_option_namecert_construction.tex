\begin{theorem}[Tagged option map relation constructor normal form]
\label{thm:tagged-option-maprel-constructor-normal-form}
This theorem fits the per-object template as follows: the traditional target is
constructor-facing readback for the tagged option map relation; the BEDC source
is $\mathsf{BHist}$ with one $\mathsf{DescentCertificate}$ map; the carrier is
the visible tagged option endpoint shape; the classifier is the source and
target history classifiers carried by the descent certificate; the stability
obligation is branch preservation, payload readback, and zero-tag exclusion; the
ledger records the absent branch, the present branch, and the absence of
zero-tag endpoints; the core theorem is the displayed constructor normal form;
exactness says a map-relation witness has exactly the empty branch or a
transported one-payload branch; the standard bridge is $\mathsf{none}$/$\mathsf{some}$
map readback; and the status is $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.

Let $S,T:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be BEDC history source and target
carriers, let $\sim_S$ and $\sim_T$ be their classifiers, let
$$
\delta:\mathsf{DescentCertificate}(\mathsf{BHist},\mathsf{BHist},\sim_S,\sim_T),
$$
and assume payload preservation
$$
\rho_{\delta}:\forall a.\;S(a)\to T(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a)).
$$
For histories $h,k:\mathsf{BHist}$, every witness
$$
\mathsf{TaggedOptionMapRel}_{\delta}(S,T,\rho_{\delta};h,k)
$$
implies the following clauses simultaneously.
\begin{enumerate}
  \item Every zero-tag presentation is impossible at either endpoint:
  $$
  \forall t:\mathsf{BHist}.\;
  (\hsame(h,\Ezero(t))\to\bot)\land
  (\hsame(k,\Ezero(t))\to\bot).
  $$

  \item The absent branch is visible on both sides:
  $$
  \hsame(h,\emp)\Longleftrightarrow\hsame(k,\emp).
  $$

  \item The present branch is visible on both sides:
  $$
  (\exists a:\mathsf{BHist}.\;S(a)\land\hsame(h,\Eone(a)))
  \Longleftrightarrow
  (\exists b:\mathsf{BHist}.\;T(b)\land\hsame(k,\Eone(b))).
  $$

  \item Visible source and target payloads read back to one transported
  payload:
  $$
  \forall u\,v:\mathsf{BHist}.\;
  S(u)\land\hsame(h,\Eone(u))\land
  T(v)\land\hsame(k,\Eone(v))
  \to
  \exists a:\mathsf{BHist}.\;
  S(a)\land T(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a))\land
  \hsame(u,a)\land\hsame(v,\delta.\mathsf{map}(a)).
  $$
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Split the map-relation witness into its absent and present branches. In the
absent branch, the stored endpoint presentations are $\hsame(h,\emp)$ and
$\hsame(k,\emp)$; in the present branch, the stored presentations are
$\hsame(h,\Eone(a))$ and
$\hsame(k,\Eone(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a)))$ for the exposed payload $a$.

First prove zero-tag exclusion. Fix $t:\mathsf{BHist}$. In the absent branch,
any presentation $\hsame(h,\Ezero(t))$ or $\hsame(k,\Ezero(t))$ composes with
the corresponding empty presentation and contradicts generated-history
no-confusion between $\emp$ and $\Ezero(t)$. In the present branch, any such
presentation composes with the corresponding one-tag presentation and
contradicts generated-history no-confusion between $\Eone$ and $\Ezero$.

Next obtain the visible branch clauses by applying
Theorem~\ref{thm:tagged-option-maprel-visible-branch-equivalence} to the same
map-relation witness. Its two projections are exactly the absent-branch
equivalence and the present-branch equivalence in the statement.

Finally let $u,v:\mathsf{BHist}$ be visible source and target payloads with the
displayed endpoint presentations. Apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:tagged-option-maprel-visible-payload-readback} to the
map-relation witness and to these four visible payload facts. The produced
payload $a$ carries $S(a)$, $T(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a))$, and the two readback
witnesses required by the last clause.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Tagged option map relation shared-source target normal form]
\label{thm:tagged-option-maprel-shared-source-target-normal-form}

$$

$$

\land T(\delta.\mathsf{map}(b))
\land \hsame(h,\Eone(a))
\land \hsame(h,\Eone(b))
\land \hsame(k,\Eone(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a)))
\land \hsame(k',\Eone(\delta.\mathsf{map}(b)))
\land \mathsf{TaggedOptionHistoryClassifier}_{T,\sim_T}(k,k').
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Split the first map-relation witness.

In the absent branch, the first witness presents the shared source endpoint as
$\hsame(h,\emp)$ and the target endpoint as $\hsame(k,\emp)$. Split the second
map-relation witness. Its absent branch contributes $\hsame(k',\emp)$, so the
absent alternative follows. Its present branch would present the same source
endpoint as $\hsame(h,\Eone(b))$ for some payload $b$. Composing the two source
presentations contradicts generated-history empty-versus-one no-confusion, so
that branch is impossible.

In the present branch of the first witness, expose a payload $a$ with $S(a)$,
$T(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a))$, source presentation $\hsame(h,\Eone(a))$, and
target presentation $\hsame(k,\Eone(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a)))$. Split the
second map-relation witness. Its absent branch presents the shared source as
$\hsame(h,\emp)$, which again contradicts empty-versus-one no-confusion after
composition with $\hsame(h,\Eone(a))$.

It remains to handle the present/present branch. The second witness exposes a
payload $b$ with $S(b)$, $T(\delta.\mathsf{map}(b))$, source presentation
$\hsame(h,\Eone(b))$, and target presentation
$\hsame(k',\Eone(\delta.\mathsf{map}(b)))$.

Invert the two one-tag presentations of the shared source endpoint to obtain
$\hsame(a,b)$. Source hsame compatibility converts $S(a)$, $S(b)$, and
$\hsame(a,b)$ into $a\sim_S b$. Applying $\delta.\mathsf{respects}$ gives
$\delta.\mathsf{map}(a)\sim_T\delta.\mathsf{map}(b)$.

The two target carrier witnesses, the two target endpoint presentations, and
this transported payload relation assemble the present-present branch of
$\mathsf{TaggedOptionHistoryClassifier}_{T,\sim_T}(k,k')$. Package $a$ and $b$
with the retained source facts, target facts, endpoint presentations, source
classification, and target classifier.
\end{proof}
