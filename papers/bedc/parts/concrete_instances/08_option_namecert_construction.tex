\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for \(\OptionUp\)}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-option-namecert}

The optional value interface \(\OptionUp\) is licensed over any source carrier already endowed with a \(\NameCert\). The carrier is the inductive sum of an absent constructor and a present constructor wrapping a witness of the source. It is not a primitive type former; it is licensed only via its underlying inductive carrier and the classifier that lifts the source classifier through the present constructor.

\section{Roadmap template status}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Traditional target}: optional data with one absent value and present values over a source object.
  \item \textbf{BEDC source}: any licensed source carrier $A$ equipped with its existing name certificate.
  \item \textbf{Carrier choice}: the typed $\mathsf{OptionCarrier}$ has $\mathsf{none}$ and $\mathsf{some}$ constructors, while the checked history carrier is the nullable envelope $\mathsf{OptionHistoryCarrier}_S(h)\equiv \hsame(h,\emp)\lor S(h)$.
  \item \textbf{Classifier choice}: the typed $\mathsf{OptionClassifierSpec}$ relates only matching option tags through the source classifier; $\mathsf{OptionHistoryClassifier}$ is the nullable-history classifier consisting of endpoint envelope-carrier witnesses and endpoint $\hsame$.
  \item \textbf{Stability obligations}: typed optional carrier transport, typed absent/present separation, lifted typed classifier symmetry and transitivity, nullable-history endpoint transport, nullable-history classifier symmetry and transitivity, and for the constructor-tagged optional-history certificate a payload coherence condition converting source-carrier $\hsame$ between middle payloads into the source classifier.
  \item \textbf{Ledger}: the option tag is explicit; present payload evidence remains the source certificate evidence.
  \item \textbf{Core theorem list}: typed carrier cases, typed absent/present no-confusion, present inversion, typed classifier equivalence, nullable-history carrier transport, nullable-history classifier transport, and the nullable-history semantic certificate.
  \item \textbf{Exactness predicate}: matching-tag exactness belongs to $\mathsf{OptionClassifierSpec}$; the checked history classifier exactness is endpoint envelope membership plus endpoint $\hsame$ and carries no present constructor tag.
  \item \textbf{Standard bridge}: the absent branch reads as $\mathsf{none}$ and the present branch reads as $\mathsf{some}(a)$.
  \item \textbf{Status}: the nullable history-envelope $\mathsf{SemanticNameCert}$ is $\mathsf{CheckedCert}$; the constructor-tagged optional-history certificate is $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Carrier definition}

\begin{definition}[Option carrier]
\label{def:option-carrier}
For a licensed source carrier \(A\), define an inductive carrier with constructors \(\mathsf{none}\) and \(\mathsf{some}(a)\).
\end{definition}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.OptionUp.OptionCarrier\_cases}

The checked BEDC-history carrier used below is the nullable semantic carrier $\mathsf{OptionHistoryCarrier}_S(h)\equiv \hsame(h,\emp)\lor S(h)$, not a tagged history carrier.

\begin{theorem}[Option history carrier transport]
\label{thm:option-history-carrier-hsame-transport}
If two BEDC histories are internally the same, then optional history-carrier
membership transfers from the first history to the second.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Invert the optional carrier witness. In the absent branch, compose the
symmetric history-sameness link with the stored empty-history witness. In the
source branch, eliminate the $\hsame(h,k)$ witness, so $k$ is definitionally
the same history as $h$, and reuse the source witness.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.OptionUp.OptionHistoryCarrier\_hsame\_transport}

\begin{definition}[Option pattern specification]

\begin{definition}[Tagged option history carrier]
\label{def:tagged-option-history-carrier}
For a BEDC history source carrier $S:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$, define $\mathsf{TaggedOptionHistoryCarrier}_S(h)$ by the two constructor-shaped cases:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item absent: $\hsame(h,\emp)$;
  \item present: there is a payload history $a$ such that $S(a)$ and $\hsame(h,\Eone(a))$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Tagged option history present inversion]
\label{thm:tagged-option-history-present-inversion}
For any BEDC history source carrier $S:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ and
history $h$, a present endpoint in the tagged carrier exposes a source payload:
$$
\mathsf{TaggedOptionHistoryCarrier}_S(\Eone(h))
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
\exists a:\mathsf{BHist}.\; S(a)\land \hsame(h,a).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Case-analyze the tagged carrier witness for $\Eone(h)$. The absent branch gives
$\hsame(\Eone(h),\emp)$ and is eliminated by generated-history no-confusion
between a present endpoint and the empty endpoint. The present branch gives a
payload history $a$, a source witness $S(a)$, and
$\hsame(\Eone(h),\Eone(a))$; applying the one-constructor inversion theorem for
$\hsame$ strips the visible present tags and yields $\hsame(h,a)$, so $a$ is the
required payload.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[Tagged option history classifier]
\label{def:tagged-option-history-classifier}
For a BEDC history source carrier $S:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ and source classifier $\sim_S$, define $\mathsf{TaggedOptionHistoryClassifier}_{S,\sim_S}(h,k)$ by the two constructor-matching cases:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item absent-absent: $\hsame(h,\emp)$ and $\hsame(k,\emp)$;
  \item present-present: there are payload histories $a,b$ such that $S(a)$, $S(b)$, $a\sim_S b$, $\hsame(h,\Eone(a))$, and $\hsame(k,\Eone(b))$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Tagged option source hsame compatibility]
\label{def:tagged-option-source-hsame-compatibility}
For a BEDC history source carrier $S:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ and source classifier $\sim_S$, define $\mathsf{TaggedOptionSourceHsameCompatible}(S,\sim_S)$ to be the property that for all payload histories $a,b$, if $S(a)$, $S(b)$, and $\hsame(a,b)$, then $a\sim_S b$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Tagged option history pattern]
\label{def:tagged-option-history-pattern}
For a BEDC history source carrier $S:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$, the visible tag pattern $\mathsf{TaggedOptionHistoryPattern}_S(h)$ holds exactly when $h$ is either an absent endpoint with $\hsame(h,\emp)$ or a present endpoint with payload history $a$ satisfying $S(a)$ and $\hsame(h,\Eone(a))$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Tagged option history ledger]
\label{def:tagged-option-history-ledger}
For a BEDC history source carrier $S:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$, the visible tag ledger $\mathsf{TaggedOptionHistoryLedger}_S(h)$ records the same two endpoint forms as certificate data: the absent record stores $\hsame(h,\emp)$, and the present record stores a payload history $a$ together with $S(a)$ and $\hsame(h,\Eone(a))$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Tagged option history carrier, pattern, and ledger equivalence]
\label{thm:tagged-option-history-carrier-pattern-ledger-equivalence}
For every BEDC history source carrier $S:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ and history $h$, the tagged option history carrier is equivalent to both visible certificate presentations:
$$
\mathsf{TaggedOptionHistoryCarrier}_S(h)
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
\mathsf{TaggedOptionHistoryPattern}_S(h)
$$
and
$$
\mathsf{TaggedOptionHistoryCarrier}_S(h)
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
\mathsf{TaggedOptionHistoryLedger}_S(h).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold the three tagged option predicates. In the absent case, each presentation stores exactly the witness $\hsame(h,\emp)$, so the witness is reused unchanged in both directions. In the present case, each presentation stores the same payload history $a$ together with $S(a)$ and $\hsame(h,\Eone(a))$, so the same triple rebuilds the carrier, pattern, and ledger witnesses. The two cases exhaust the tagged option history carrier and the two visible certificate presentations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Tagged option history carrier transport]
\label{thm:tagged-option-history-carrier-hsame-transport}
For any BEDC history source carrier $S:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$, if $\mathsf{TaggedOptionHistoryCarrier}_S(h)$ and $\hsame(h,k)$, then $\mathsf{TaggedOptionHistoryCarrier}_S(k)$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Case-analyze the tagged carrier witness for $h$. In the absent case, compose the symmetry of $\hsame(h,k)$ with the stored endpoint witness $\hsame(h,\emp)$ to obtain $\hsame(k,\emp)$, then rebuild the absent clause. In the present case, keep the same payload $a$ and source witness $S(a)$. Compose the symmetry of $\hsame(h,k)$ with the stored endpoint witness $\hsame(h,\Eone(a))$ to obtain $\hsame(k,\Eone(a))$, then rebuild the present clause.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Tagged option history constructor exactness]
\label{thm:tagged-option-history-constructor-exactness}
For a BEDC history source carrier $S:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$, source classifier $\sim_S$, source certificate $\mathsf{cert}:\NameCert(S,\sim_S)$, and $\mathsf{TaggedOptionSourceHsameCompatible}(S,\sim_S)$, the classifier $\mathsf{TaggedOptionHistoryClassifier}_{S,\sim_S}$ from Definition~\ref{def:tagged-option-history-classifier} separates the absent and present constructors at the BEDC history level. An absent-present or present-absent classification is impossible; every present-present classification inverts to payload histories $a,b$ with $S(a)$, $S(b)$, $a\sim_S b$, and endpoint witnesses $\hsame(h,\Eone(a))$ and $\hsame(k,\Eone(b))$; and classifier reflexivity, symmetry, transitivity, and carrier transport are induced by the corresponding fields of $\mathsf{cert}$ together with the source hsame-compatibility bridge for middle payload presentations.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The mixed cases reduce to BEDC history constructor no-confusion: $\mathsf{not\_hsame\_emp\_e1}$ eliminates an empty history classified with an $e1$-tagged present history, and $\mathsf{not\_hsame\_e1\_empty}$ eliminates the reverse direction. In the present-present case, $\mathsf{hsame\_e1\_iff}$ strips the visible $e1$ tags and exposes the payload histories, so the source certificate supplies $S(a)$, $S(b)$, the payload classification, and the fields $\mathsf{cert.equiv\_refl}$, $\mathsf{cert.equiv\_symm}$, $\mathsf{cert.equiv\_trans}$, and $\mathsf{cert.carrier\_respects\_equiv}$. For present-present transitivity, invert the two classifier witnesses around the shared middle history to obtain endpoint witnesses for $\Eone(b)$ and $\Eone(b')$. Symmetry and transitivity of $\hsame$ yield $\hsame(\Eone(b),\Eone(b'))$, $e1$-constructor inversion yields $\hsame(b,b')$, and source hsame compatibility gives $b\sim_S b'$. The field $\mathsf{cert.equiv\_trans}$ then composes $a\sim_S b$, $b\sim_S b'$, and $b'\sim_S c$ into $a\sim_S c$. The absent-absent case is the empty-history classifier clause.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Tagged option history same-tag exactness]
\label{thm:tagged-option-history-same-tag-exactness}
For a BEDC history source carrier $S:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ and source classifier $\sim_S$, the classifier $\mathsf{TaggedOptionHistoryClassifier}_{S,\sim_S}$ from Definition~\ref{def:tagged-option-history-classifier} is exact on equal visible tags:
$$
\mathsf{TaggedOptionHistoryClassifier}_{S,\sim_S}(\emp,k)
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
\hsame(k,\emp)
$$
and
$$
\mathsf{TaggedOptionHistoryClassifier}_{S,\sim_S}(\Eone(h),\Eone(k))
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
\exists a,b.\; S(a)\land S(b)\land \hsame(h,a)\land \hsame(k,b)\land a\sim_S b.
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the absent endpoint, case-analyze the tagged classifier. The absent-absent branch stores $\hsame(k,\emp)$, while the present-present branch would contain $\hsame(\emp,\Eone(a))$ and is eliminated by empty-versus-one no-confusion. Conversely, $\hsame(k,\emp)$ combines with reflexivity of $\hsame(\emp,\emp)$ to build the absent-absent classifier branch.

For the present endpoint, the absent-absent branch would contain $\hsame(\Eone(h),\emp)$ and is eliminated by one-versus-empty no-confusion. The present-present branch gives payload histories $a,b$, source witnesses $S(a)$ and $S(b)$, a source classifier witness $a\sim_S b$, and endpoint witnesses $\hsame(\Eone(h),\Eone(a))$ and $\hsame(\Eone(k),\Eone(b))$; one-constructor inversion turns these endpoint witnesses into $\hsame(h,a)$ and $\hsame(k,b)$. Conversely, such payload data rebuilds the present-present classifier branch after applying the one-constructor congruence for $\hsame$ to the two payload endpoint witnesses.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Tagged option history absent exactness]
\label{thm:tagged-option-history-absent-exactness}
For every BEDC history source carrier $S:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$, source classifier $\sim_S$, and history $k$, the tagged option history classifier has the exact absent endpoint:
$$
\mathsf{TaggedOptionHistoryClassifier}_{S,\sim_S}(\emp,k)
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
\hsame(k,\emp).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the forward direction, case-analyze the classifier witness. The absent-absent case directly provides the endpoint witness $\hsame(k,\emp)$. The present-present case would classify $\emp$ with some $\Eone(a)$ endpoint, contradicting the BEDC empty-vs-$\Eone$ no-confusion theorem. For the reverse direction, combine the assumed $\hsame(k,\emp)$ with reflexivity of $\hsame$ at $\emp$ and rebuild the absent-absent classifier clause.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Tagged option history semantic certificate target]
\label{thm:tagged-option-history-semantic-certificate}
Let $S:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be a source carrier, let $\sim_S$ be a source classifier on source histories, and assume a source $\NameCert(S,\sim_S)$ together with $\mathsf{TaggedOptionSourceHsameCompatible}(S,\sim_S)$. Then $\mathsf{TaggedOptionHistoryCarrier}_S$, $\mathsf{TaggedOptionHistoryClassifier}_{S,\sim_S}$, $\mathsf{TaggedOptionHistoryPattern}_S$, and $\mathsf{TaggedOptionHistoryLedger}_S$ form a $\mathsf{SemanticNameCert}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The absent branch supplies inhabitation through the empty history. Present reflexivity and symmetry are obtained by inverting the present classifier witness and applying the corresponding source certificate fields; the absent branch uses reflexivity, symmetry, and transitivity of $\hsame$ at $\emp$. For present-present transitivity, unpack the two classifier witnesses into payload data $(a,b)$ and $(b',c)$. The shared middle history has endpoint witnesses $\hsame(m,\Eone(b))$ and $\hsame(m,\Eone(b'))$, so symmetry and transitivity of $\hsame$ give $\hsame(\Eone(b),\Eone(b'))$. Applying $e1$-constructor inversion gives $\hsame(b,b')$; source hsame compatibility classifies the two middle payload presentations as $b\sim_S b'$. The source certificate transitivity field first composes $a\sim_S b$ with $b\sim_S b'$, then composes the result with $b'\sim_S c$. Constructor exactness eliminates mixed absent-present classifications and inverts present-present classifications to payload histories. Carrier transport follows by cases on $\mathsf{TaggedOptionHistoryClassifier}_{S,\sim_S}$: absent transport uses the empty endpoint case, and present transport uses the $S(b)$ field already stored in the present-present classifier case. The visible tag pattern and visible tag ledger fields are supplied by Theorem~\ref{thm:tagged-option-history-carrier-pattern-ledger-equivalence}, which identifies both certificate presentations with the tagged option history carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Option history classifier endpoint transport]
\label{thm:option-history-classifier-hsame-transport}
If the optional history classifier relates \(h\) to \(k\), and
\(\hsame(h,h')\) and \(\hsame(k,k')\), then it relates \(h'\) to \(k'\).
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the classifier into its endpoint carrier witnesses and the stored
\(\hsame(h,k)\) link. Transport both carriers along the endpoint sameness
links, then compose symmetry of \(\hsame(h,h')\), the stored link, and
\(\hsame(k,k')\).
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[Option stability certificate]
\label{def:option-stability-certificate}
The stability certificate separates the typed optional classifier fields from
the nullable history-envelope fields.

For the typed $\mathsf{OptionClassifierSpec}$ classifier, the fields are:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item reflexivity for $\mathsf{none}$;
  \item reflexivity for $\mathsf{some}(a)$ from source reflexivity;
  \item transitivity from a source-transitivity hypothesis;
  \item no-confusion between $\mathsf{none}$ and $\mathsf{some}(a)$ in either order;
  \item present inversion, exposing the source classifier witness from a $\mathsf{some}$--$\mathsf{some}$ classification.
\end{enumerate}
Symmetry for this typed classifier is supplied only from a source-symmetry
hypothesis; the checked optional-history instance specializes that principle to
heads classified by $\hsame$.

For the nullable $\mathsf{OptionHistoryCarrier}$ envelope and its
$\mathsf{OptionHistoryClassifier}$, the certificate fields are:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item carrier transport across $\hsame$;
  \item endpoint transport for the classifier across $\hsame$ on both endpoints;
  \item classifier symmetry;
  \item classifier transitivity;
  \item ledger transport across raw and visible endpoint $\hsame$ links.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Option classifier transitivity]
\label{thm:option-classifier-transitivity}
Let $R : A \to A \to \mathsf{Prop}$ be a source classifier relation.  If $R$
is transitive, then for optional carriers $x$, $y$, and $z$,
$$
  \mathsf{OptionClassifierSpec}(R,x,y)
  \quad\text{and}\quad
  \mathsf{OptionClassifierSpec}(R,y,z)
$$
imply $\mathsf{OptionClassifierSpec}(R,x,z)$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.OptionUp.OptionClassifierSpec\_trans}
\begin{proof}
Proceed by constructor case analysis on $x$, $y$, and $z$.  The none--none--none
case is immediate, mixed none--some cases contradict the classifier clauses, and
the some--some--some case is exactly the supplied transitivity of $R$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Concrete optional-history semantic certificate]
\label{thm:concrete-option-namecert}
For any source predicate on BEDC histories, $\mathsf{OptionHistoryCarrier}$ and $\mathsf{OptionHistoryClassifier}$ form the checked $\mathsf{SemanticNameCert}$ for the nullable empty-or-source history envelope. This semantic certificate does not assert constructor-discriminating history tags; that stronger tagged interface is the preceding paper-side target.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The history carrier contains the empty history and any source history. The classifier records both endpoint envelope-carrier witnesses and the BEDC history equality witness. Reflexivity, symmetry, transitivity, and carrier transport are the corresponding \(\hsame\) fields, while pattern and ledger soundness are carrier projections for the envelope.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.OptionUp.option\_history\_semantic\_name\_certificate}

\begin{corollary}[OptionUp certificate scope]
\label{cor:option-certificate-scope}
The checked envelope semantic certificate licenses the empty-or-source history
envelope only. It is not a checked constructor-tagged $\OptionUp$ certificate:
citations to Theorem~\ref{thm:concrete-option-namecert} assert the nullable
history-envelope certificate, while the explicit optional value interface is
licensed only when the typed tag classifier $\mathsf{OptionClassifierSpec}$,
none/some no-confusion, some-inversion, and the tag-plus-source ledger are
supplied together with that envelope.
\end{corollary}
