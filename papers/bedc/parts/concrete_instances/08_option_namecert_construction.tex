\begin{theorem}[Tagged option composite image classifier normalized absent public factorization]
\label{thm:tagged-option-composite-image-classifier-normalized-absent-public-factorization}
This theorem fits the per-object template as follows: the traditional target is
endpoint-normalized functorial $\mathsf{OptionUp}$ absent public factorization;
the BEDC source is $\mathsf{BHist}$ with two composable
$\mathsf{DescentCertificate}$ maps; the carrier is
$\mathsf{TaggedOptionPayloadDescentImageCarrier}$; the classifier is
$\mathsf{TaggedOptionPayloadDescentImageClassifier}$; the stability obligations
are endpoint $\hsame$ transport, map-relation composition factorization,
empty-branch preservation, and empty-versus-present no-confusion; the ledger is
the normalized absent public pair together with its intermediate endpoints; the
core theorem is absent public factorization through the second descent stage;
exactness says the intermediate public pair is also absent and has no visible
present presentation; the standard bridge is $\mathsf{none}$ normalized public
readback; and the status is $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.

Let $S,T,U:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be BEDC history source,
intermediate, and target carriers, let $\sim_S$, $\sim_T$, and $\sim_U$ be their
classifiers, and let
$$
\delta:\mathsf{DescentCertificate}(\mathsf{BHist},\mathsf{BHist},\sim_S,\sim_T)
$$
and
$$
\epsilon:\mathsf{DescentCertificate}(\mathsf{BHist},\mathsf{BHist},\sim_T,\sim_U).
$$
Assume payload preservation fields
$$
\rho_{\delta}:\forall a.\;S(a)\to T(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a))
$$
and
$$
\rho_{\epsilon}:\forall b.\;T(b)\to U(\epsilon.\mathsf{map}(b)).
$$
Assume endpoint stability for the second descent:
$$
\eta_{\epsilon}:\forall x\,y.\;
T(x)\to T(y)\to \hsame(x,y)\to
\hsame(\epsilon.\mathsf{map}(x),\epsilon.\mathsf{map}(y)).
$$
Write $\epsilon\circ\delta$ for the descent certificate whose map field is
$a\mapsto\epsilon.\mathsf{map}(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a))$ and whose respects field
sends $r:a\sim_S b$ to
$\epsilon.\mathsf{respects}(\delta.\mathsf{respects}(r))$. Define
$$
\rho_{\epsilon\circ\delta}(a,Sa)=
\rho_{\epsilon}(\delta.\mathsf{map}(a),\rho_{\delta}(a,Sa)).
$$
Let $\mathsf{cert}_S:\NameCert(S,\sim_S)$,
$\mathsf{cert}_T:\NameCert(T,\sim_T)$, and
$\mathsf{cert}_U:\NameCert(U,\sim_U)$. Assume
$\mathsf{TaggedOptionSourceHsameCompatible}(S,\sim_S)$,
$\mathsf{TaggedOptionSourceHsameCompatible}(T,\sim_T)$, and
$\mathsf{TaggedOptionSourceHsameCompatible}(U,\sim_U)$. For final endpoints
$m,m':\mathsf{BHist}$ and normalized endpoints $u,u':\mathsf{BHist}$, if
$$
\mathsf{TaggedOptionPayloadDescentImageClassifier}_{\epsilon\circ\delta}
(S,U,\rho_{\epsilon\circ\delta};m,m')
$$
and
$$
\hsame(m,u)\land \hsame(m',u')
$$
and
$$
\hsame(u,\emp)\land \hsame(u',\emp),
$$
then there exist $k,k':\mathsf{BHist}$ such that
\begin{enumerate}
\item $\mathsf{TaggedOptionPayloadDescentImageClassifier}_{\delta}
(S,T,\rho_{\delta};k,k')$;
\item $\mathsf{TaggedOptionMapRel}_{\epsilon}(T,U,\rho_{\epsilon};k,u)$ and
$\mathsf{TaggedOptionMapRel}_{\epsilon}(T,U,\rho_{\epsilon};k',u')$;
\item $\hsame(k,\emp)\land \hsame(k',\emp)$;
\item $\neg\exists p,q:\mathsf{BHist}.\;
T(p)\land T(q)\land
\hsame(k,\Eone(p))\land
\hsame(k',\Eone(q))$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First transport the composite image-classifier witness from $(m,m')$ to
$(u,u')$. Apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:tagged-option-payload-descent-image-classifier-hsame-transport}
with the endpoint normalization witnesses $\hsame(m,u)$ and $\hsame(m',u')$.

Next apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:tagged-option-payload-descent-image-classifier-composition-factorization}
to the transported composite classifier. This supplies intermediate endpoints
$k,k'$, the $\delta$ image-classifier factor at $(k,k')$, and the two
$\epsilon$ map-relation witnesses from $k$ to $u$ and from $k'$ to $u'$.

Use Theorem~\ref{thm:tagged-option-maprel-visible-branch-equivalence} on the
left $\epsilon$ map-relation witness. The normalized absent presentation
$\hsame(u,\emp)$ forces $\hsame(k,\emp)$. Apply the same branch equivalence to
the right $\epsilon$ map-relation witness and $\hsame(u',\emp)$ to obtain
$\hsame(k',\emp)$.

It remains to rule out an intermediate visible present pair. If such $p,q$
existed, the presentation $\hsame(k,\Eone(p))$ would compose with
$\hsame(k,\emp)$ to identify $\emp$ with $\Eone(p)$, contradicting BEDC history
empty-versus-one no-confusion.

Package the witnesses $k,k'$ with the retained $\delta$ image-classifier
factor, the retained $\epsilon$ map-relation witnesses, the two intermediate
absent presentations, and the excluded visible-pair clause.
\end{proof}
