\begin{theorem}[Covering dimension Real separability forward bound]
\label{thm:coveringdimension-real-separability-forward-bound}
For every accepted $\CoveringDimensionUp$ carrier, a $\RealUp$ separability read supplies a finite cover-bound ledger when it is combined with the metric-completion dependency route. Concretely, the rational dense windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-separability-namecert} and the completion-facing rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metriccompletion-namecert} determine a finite ledger of sampled balls, refinement witnesses, and order-bound rows for the $\CoveringDimensionUp$ NameCert surface. The conclusion is only this finite NameCert ledger; it is not a classical open-cover compactness theorem or a selected infinite refinement.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the $\CoveringDimensionUp$ carrier of \autoref{def:covering-dimension-carrier}. Its public data are finite cover rows, refinement maps, order-bound witnesses, compact Lebesgue ledger rows, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, provenance, and local naming.

Read the $\RealUp$ separability route through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-separability-namecert}. The rational dense windows supply the sampled centers and mesh witnesses that can be attached to the finite cover rows.

Next use the metric-completion dependency route through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metriccompletion-namecert}. Completion-facing data may refine the sampled balls, but it must do so through displayed completion rows and the same structural transport and replay rows.

Combining the two routes yields a finite cover-bound ledger: sampled balls from separability, completion-compatible refinement rows, and order-bound witnesses inside the covering-dimension packet. Since every row is displayed and finite, the theorem exports no open-cover compactness principle and no selected infinite refinement.
\end{proof}
