\begin{theorem}[CoveringDimension Cauchy-filter completion route]
\label{thm:coveringdimension-cauchy-filter-completion-route}
Every $\MetricCompletionUp$-facing consumer of an accepted $\CoveringDimensionUp$ carrier must read the finite cover-refinement rows of \autoref{def:covering-dimension-carrier} through the compact metric source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, the finite sampling row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finite-epsilon-net-namecert}, the compact cover Lebesgue ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compact-cover-lebesgue-ledger-namecert}, the rational-window surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-separability-namecert}, and then the Cauchy-filter completion boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyfiltercompletion-namecert} before any completion-facing readback is exported.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier $D=(K,E,C,R,O,L,H,T,P,N)$ of \autoref{def:covering-dimension-carrier}. The cover row $C$, refinement row $R$, order-bound row $O$, and Lebesgue ledger $L$ are available only after the compact metric source $K$ and finite epsilon-net row $E$ have been displayed.

Use the metric-completion dependency route already carried by the $\CoveringDimensionUp$ sibling lattice. It sends completion-facing reads through compact metric finite covers, $\RealSeparabilityUp$ rational windows, finite Lebesgue ledger rows, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local $\NameCert$ row.

Finally route the completion boundary through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyfiltercompletion-namecert}. The Cauchy-filter completion packet exposes the source filter, finite windows, tolerance, regular readback, and Real seal rows before completion consumers inspect the handoff, so the dimension route cannot bypass the displayed finite-cover sampling surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CoveringDimension finite refinement window]
\label{thm:coveringdimension-finite-refinement-window}
Under the completion handoff of \autoref{thm:coveringdimension-cauchy-filter-completion-route}, the finite refinement and order-bound window of an accepted $\CoveringDimensionUp$ packet remains the displayed $\BHist$ route
$$
K,E,C \longmapsto R,O,L \longmapsto H,T,P,N .
$$
The handoff exports no arbitrary open-cover theorem, no choice-selected refinement, no hidden completion point, and no detached multiplicity oracle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from \autoref{thm:covering-dimension-refinement-order}. It factors every dimension consumer through the compact metric row $K$, finite epsilon-net row $E$, finite cover row $C$, refinement row $R$, order-bound witness $O$, and compact Lebesgue ledger $L$ before the structural rows are read.

Apply \autoref{thm:coveringdimension-cauchy-filter-completion-route}. Completion-facing consumers inherit the same finite route through $\RealSeparabilityUp$ and $\CauchyFilterCompletionUp$, but those sibling rows add only rational windows, Cauchy-filter windows, regular readback, and completion-facing seal data.

Now use \autoref{thm:covering-dimension-ledger-non-escape}. Since the local carrier has no coordinate for arbitrary open covers, selected refinements, hidden completion points, or external multiplicity oracles, transporting the route through completion boundaries preserves the displayed $\BHist$ refinement window.
\end{proof}
