Following Bishop and Bridges, the $\DyadicFastCauchyUp$ packet records the finite fast-Cauchy interface by which dyadic tolerance schedules become regular rational data before a $\RealUp$ seal is read. It sits over the dyadic arithmetic ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the finite stream window surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the terminal real-name boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, and the displayed modulus discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-fast-cauchy-modulus-namecert}. The chapter names the L10 Real and Completion handoff as finite NameCert data, not as a host completion theorem.

\begin{definition}[Dyadic fast Cauchy carrier]\label{def:dyadic-fast-cauchy-carrier}
A $\DyadicFastCauchyUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
F=(D,S,M,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, storing dyadic radii, slack comparisons, addition rows, and containment checks consumed by the fast Cauchy route.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row selected at the visible dyadic requests.
\item $M$ is the $\FastCauchyModulusUp$ row, recording the displayed rule that a request at level $k$ supplies windows whose mutual distance is bounded by the dyadic tolerance named by $D$.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row obtained from $D,S,M$.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row exposed only after the dyadic ledger, stream windows, modulus row, and regular rational readback have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the dyadic ledger, stream windows, modulus row, readback, seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from dyadic requests through $D,S,M,R,E,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\DyadicFastCauchyUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\FastCauchyModulusUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DyadicFastCauchyUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $D,S,M,R,E,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for quotient streams, selected host limits, countable choice, trichotomy, host real equality, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Dyadic fast Cauchy NameCert obligations]\label{thm:dyadic-fast-cauchy-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\DyadicFastCauchyUp$ carrier $F=(D,S,M,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\DyadicFastCauchyUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the dyadic ledger, finite stream windows, displayed fast-Cauchy modulus, regular rational readback, terminal Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item dyadic tolerance exposure, requiring every requested bound to be read from $D$ before a stream window or readback row is consumed;
\item finite-window coverage, requiring $S$ to contain precisely the displayed windows selected for the visible dyadic requests;
\item modulus stability, requiring $M$ to bind each visible request to the pairwise window comparison budget named in $D$;
\item RegSeqRat handoff, requiring $R$ to be downstream of the same $D,S,M$ route;
\item Real-seal boundary, requiring $E$ to remain terminal after $R$ and to export no quotient stream, selected host limit, countable-choice selector, trichotomy row, host real equality, ambient completeness theorem, Lean evidence, or checked bridge;
\item provenance and local naming, requiring $P$ and $N$ to package exactly the displayed fast-Cauchy route.
\end{enumerate}
No DyadicFastCauchy NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:dyadic-fast-cauchy-carrier}. The analytic coordinates are exactly the dyadic ledger $D$, finite windows $S$, displayed modulus row $M$, regular readback $R$, and terminal Real seal $E$.

The tolerance and window clauses are finite projections. A requested bound enters through $D$, the visible windows are stored in $S$, and the modulus row $M$ is the only row that relates the request to the pairwise window comparison budget.

The handoff clauses follow the displayed order of the packet. The readback row $R$ is downstream of $D,S,M$, while $E$ appears only after $R$ and therefore cannot be read as an independent host limit or ambient completeness witness.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite route. Since none of the excluded objects appears as a carrier coordinate, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligation surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Dyadic fast Cauchy RegSeqRat handoff]\label{thm:dyadic-fast-cauchy-regseqrat-handoff}
For every accepted $\DyadicFastCauchyUp$ carrier $F=(D,S,M,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, a $\RegSeqRatUp$ consumer reaches the readback row $R$ only through the finite route
$$
D\longmapsto S\longmapsto M\longmapsto R .
$$
Thus the handoff from dyadic fast-Cauchy data to regular rational data cannot bypass the displayed tolerance ledger, the selected stream windows, or the fast-Cauchy modulus row by quotient streams, selected host limits, countable choice, trichotomy, host real equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the accepted packet. The only regular rational coordinate is $R$, and it is placed after the dyadic ledger $D$, finite windows $S$, and displayed modulus row $M$.

The transport and replay rows preserve this ordering componentwise. Provenance and local naming then package the same route without adding a second source for readback data.

Consequently any RegSeqRat consumer of the packet must pass through $D,S,M$ before reading $R$. The excluded quotient, selector, choice, order, equality, and completeness objects are absent from the carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Dyadic fast Cauchy Real seal boundary]\label{thm:dyadic-fast-cauchy-real-seal-boundary}
For every accepted $\DyadicFastCauchyUp$ carrier $F=(D,S,M,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, the $\RealUp$ seal row $E$ is terminal over the displayed dyadic fast-Cauchy route. A Real consumer may read the seal only after the DyadicRatCore ledger, StreamName windows, FastCauchyModulus row, and RegSeqRat readback have been displayed, and the seal exports no quotient stream equality, selected host limit, countable choice, trichotomy, host real equality, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the terminal coordinate $E$ from the accepted carrier. It is downstream of $D,S,M,R$ and has no independent host-limit coordinate.

The componentwise transport row $H$ and replay row $C$ preserve the route from the dyadic ledger through stream windows, modulus, readback, and seal. The provenance and local naming rows only record that route as finite certificate data.

Therefore a downstream Real consumer cannot use $E$ as a hidden quotient, selected limit, choice principle, trichotomy claim, host equality, ambient completeness result, Lean theorem, or checked bridge. The seal is exactly the terminal boundary of the displayed route.
\end{proof}
