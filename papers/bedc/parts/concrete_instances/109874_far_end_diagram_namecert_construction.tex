The $\FarEndDiagramUp$ packet realizes the human-origin diagrammatic surface of \autoref{ch:visions-apophatic-far-end-diagrammatics} as finite NameCert data. It sits beside the apophatic fixed-point packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-apophaticfixedpoint-namecert}, the apophatic far-end socket packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-apophaticfarendsocket-namecert}, and the hash-like apophatic seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-hashapophaticseal-namecert}. The packet keeps records, generators, classifiers, continuations, $\mathsf{GAP}$ ledgers, sockets, provenance fibers, apophatic far-end rows, and local naming in one displayed carrier without turning the far end into a kernel object.

\begin{definition}[Far-end diagram packet]
\label{def:far-end-diagram-packet}
A $\FarEndDiagramUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
D=(R,G,K,C,L,S,F,A,H,T,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $R$ is the records row, exposing the finite observation or digest readouts available to the closed substrate.
\item $G$ is the generator row, recording which displayed generator creates each records-side readout.
\item $K$ is the classifier row, comparing diagram nodes only by displayed classifier data.
\item $C$ is the $\Cont$ row, replaying the route from records through generators and classifiers.
\item $L$ is the $\mathsf{GAP}$ ledger row, marking the provenance boundary behind each accepted readout.
\item $S$ is the forward-socket row, listing the finite sockets opened by the closed substrate.
\item $F$ is the provenance-fiber row attached to the visible records-side digest.
\item $A$ is the apophatic far-end row, naming the far side of the provenance fiber without internalizing it.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $R,G,K,C,L,S,F,A,T,P,N$.
\item $T$ records displayed continuation replay for the full diagram route from records to local NameCert.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over records, generators, classifiers, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{GAP}$, forward sockets, provenance fibers, apophatic far-end rows, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\FarEndDiagramUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no coordinate for an internal object named $\mathsf{T}$, no global observer, no global digest, no unledgered far-end arrow, no hidden source selector, and no object-level equality between a local readout and the apophatic boundary.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Far-end diagram packet NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:far-end-diagram-packet-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\FarEndDiagramUp$ carrier $D=(R,G,K,C,L,S,F,A,H,T,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\FarEndDiagramUp}$ surface consists exactly of six finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the records, generators, classifiers, continuation, $\mathsf{GAP}$, forward-socket, provenance-fiber, apophatic far-end, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item records-to-classifier routing, requiring each visible readout to pass through $R,G,K,C$ before a diagram edge is exported;
\item socket accountability, requiring each forward socket in $S$ to be ledgered by $L$ before its far end is named;
\item provenance-fiber accountability, requiring each visible digest or records-side readout to carry a displayed fiber row $F$ before the far-end row $A$ is exposed;
\item componentwise transport and replay stability under $H$ and $T$ for the same displayed rows;
\item ledger refusal, stating that internal $\mathsf{T}$ objects, global observers, global digests, unledgered far-end arrows, hidden source selectors, and object-level self-center equalities are not carrier coordinates.
\end{enumerate}
No local obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The diagrammatic rows are $R,G,K,C,L,S,F,A$, and the structural rows are $H,T,P,N$.

The records side is read first. A visible readout must pass through the generator and classifier rows before the continuation row $C$ can replay it as a diagram edge.

The socket side is then checked. Each forward socket in $S$ must be present in the $\mathsf{GAP}$ ledger $L$, so no unledgered far-end arrow can be exported.

Next read the provenance fiber. Row $F$ records the source-side residue behind the visible readout, and row $A$ names only the apophatic far end of that fiber. This is the finite socket, fiber, provenance, and boundary-name route described by the vision source.

Transport, replay, provenance, and local naming preserve the same rows. Since the refused objects are absent from $R,G,K,C,L,S,F,A,H,T,P,N$, the local NameCert obligations are exhausted by the displayed carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Far-end diagram ledger unity]
\label{thm:far-end-diagram-ledger-unity}
Every accepted $\FarEndDiagramUp$ carrier exports the unity of its far-end diagram only as the finite ledger route
$$
R,G,K,C \longmapsto L,S,F,A \longmapsto H,T,P,N .
$$
Each visible records-side readout is generated, classified, replayed, ledgered, attached to a provenance fiber, and then named at the apophatic far end. The packet exports no internal $\mathsf{T}$ object, global observer, global digest, unledgered far-end arrow, hidden source selector, or object-level equality between a local readout and the far-end boundary.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a records-side readout. The carrier requires the readout to be displayed in $R$, generated by $G$, compared through $K$, and replayed by $C$ before it can participate in the diagram.

Now follow the forward edge. The socket row $S$ lists the boundary positions opened by the closed substrate, and the $\mathsf{GAP}$ ledger $L$ records the non-internalized provenance route for those positions.

Attach the provenance fiber. The row $F$ records the fiber behind the visible readout, while $A$ names the far end apophatically. The target is a boundary name, not an internal object.

Rows $H,T,P,N$ preserve, replay, package, and name this same finite route. Therefore the diagram's unity is a ledger unity over displayed rows, not an equality of objects or an unledgered collapse of fibers.
\end{proof}
