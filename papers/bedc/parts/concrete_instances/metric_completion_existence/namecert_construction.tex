Following Bishop and Bridges and Kelley, the $\MetricCompletionExistenceUp$ packet records the finite BEDC surface by which a displayed metric source receives a completion-facing existence certificate without selecting quotient classes or host representatives. It sits between the metric carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-space-namecert}, the Cauchy route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-namecert}, the finite stream windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the real seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the completion object surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metriccompletion-namecert}, and the complete-metric consumer row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completemetric-namecert}. The packet names existence as finite certificate data; it is not an ambient completeness theorem for $\RealUp$.

\begin{definition}[Metric completion existence carrier]
\label{def:metric-completion-existence-carrier}
A $\MetricCompletionExistenceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
E=(M,S,D,W,R,Y,U,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the source $\MetricSpaceUp$ row whose metric observations are to be completed.
\item $S$ is the $\CauchyUp$ schedule row naming the finite Cauchy requests admitted from $M$.
\item $D$ is the dense-embedding row that presents source points inside the completion-facing surface without choosing representatives.
\item $W$ is the selected $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row through which the schedule is inspected.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row attached to the inspected windows.
\item $Y$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger that bounds the readback by finite dyadic budgets.
\item $U$ is the completion seal row, tying the preceding route to $\MetricCompletionUp$ and $\CompleteMetricUp$ consumers while preserving the displayed $\RealUp$ distance boundary.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the source metric, Cauchy schedule, dense embedding, windows, readback, dyadic ledger, completion seal, uniqueness ledger, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the route
$$
M,S,D \longmapsto W \longmapsto R \longmapsto Y \longmapsto U .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\MetricCompletionExistenceUp$, $\MetricSpaceUp$, $\CauchyUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\MetricCompletionUp$, $\CompleteMetricUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MetricCompletionExistenceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through these rows, their uniqueness ledger inside $H$, and the replay path $C$. It has no coordinate for quotient completion, selected representatives, countable choice, host equality, trichotomy, open-cover compactness, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Metric completion existence NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:metric-completion-existence-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\MetricCompletionExistenceUp$ carrier $E=(M,S,D,W,R,Y,U,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\MetricCompletionExistenceUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the displayed source metric, Cauchy schedule, dense embedding, selected finite stream windows, regular rational readback, dyadic tolerance ledger, completion seal, uniqueness ledger, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming. The certificate refuses quotient completion, selected representatives, countable choice, host equality, trichotomy, open-cover compactness, and ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:metric-completion-existence-carrier}. Its rows expose the source $\MetricSpaceUp$ data, the $\CauchyUp$ schedule, the dense embedding, selected $\StreamNameUp$ windows, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, completion seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local name.

The NameCert obligations follow the displayed order. The schedule is read from $M$, the embedding row $D$ is inspected before any window, $W$ and $R$ provide finite observations and regular rational readback, and $Y$ bounds them by dyadic tolerances before the completion seal $U$ is available.

Transport and replay supply the uniqueness ledger. The row $H$ moves the same coordinates componentwise, and $C$ repeats the same inspection route for each consumer request; neither row introduces a host equality test or a chosen quotient representative.

Finally, $P$ and $N$ source and name the packet. Since the carrier contains no quotient-completion coordinate, selected representative, choice schedule, host equality oracle, trichotomy row, open-cover compactness row, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness theorem, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}
