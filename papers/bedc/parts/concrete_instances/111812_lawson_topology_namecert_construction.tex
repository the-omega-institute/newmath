\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\LawsonTopologyUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-lawson-topology-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Lawson, Scott, and domain-theoretic topology, the $\LawsonTopologyUp$ packet records the finite interface where Scott-open observation rows and order-dual compact neighbourhood rows are read through a displayed topology. It sits over the domain/order/topology source chapters \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-scott-domain-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-sierpinski-space-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-topology-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-preorder-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-poset-namecert}. The packet names the Lawson carrier without importing an ambient powerset topology, a quotient of opens, or a host dcpo completion.

\begin{definition}[Lawson topology carrier]
\label{def:lawson-topology-carrier}
A $\LawsonTopologyUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(D,O,K,S,T,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the domain source row, exposing the $\ScottDomainUp$ carrier, directed-join observation interface, and order comparison data consumed by the topology.
\item $O$ is the Scott-open row, recording open observations that are upward stable and directed-inaccessible through the displayed domain interface.
\item $K$ is the lower-compact row, recording finite order-dual neighbourhood tests generated from principal lower observations and compact exclusions.
\item $S$ is the Sierpinski classifier row, reading open predicates through the $\SierpinskiSpaceUp$ observation interface.
\item $T$ is the $\TopologyUp$ handoff row generated by the subbasis consisting of the Scott-open rows in $O$ and the lower-compact rows in $K$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the domain row, Scott-open row, lower-compact row, Sierpinski row, topology handoff, replay, provenance, and local naming.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from domain and order observations to the Lawson topology handoff.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LawsonTopologyUp$, $\ScottDomainUp$, $\SierpinskiSpaceUp$, $\TopologyUp$, $\PreorderUp$, $\PosetUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LawsonTopologyUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $D,O,K,S,T,H,C,P,N$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Lawson topology NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:lawson-topology-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\LawsonTopologyUp$ carrier
$$
L=(D,O,K,S,T,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\LawsonTopologyUp}$ surface consists exactly of the displayed Scott-domain source through $D$, Scott-open observation through $O$, lower-compact observation through $K$, Sierpinski classifier reading through $S$, topology handoff through $T$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:lawson-topology-carrier}. The domain row supplies the order carrier inherited from the preorder and poset source chapters, while the Scott-open row supplies the directed-observation side.

The lower-compact row is separate from the Scott-open row. It records the order-dual neighbourhood tests used by the Lawson topology and prevents the packet from collapsing into the Scott topology alone.

The Sierpinski row reads open predicates as observations, and the topology row packages the generated subbasis. Transport, replay, provenance, and local naming are exactly $H,C,P,N$, so no additional host topology or quotient-open coordinate is available.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Lawson topology Scott-specialization handoff]
\label{thm:lawson-topology-scott-specialization-handoff}
Every specialization-order read exported by an accepted $\LawsonTopologyUp$ carrier factors through the displayed $\ScottDomainUp$ source, $\PreorderUp$ comparison, $\PosetUp$ antisymmetry row, $\SierpinskiSpaceUp$ open-observation classifier, and $\TopologyUp$ handoff. The Lawson packet may combine Scott-open and lower-compact observations, but it cannot replace the specialization route by host equality of points, an ambient powerset of opens, or an unrecorded dcpo completion.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with \autoref{thm:lawson-topology-namecert-obligations}. The only order-facing source is the displayed domain row $D$, and its comparison data are read through the preorder and poset source surfaces.

Open observations are read through $O$ and then through the Sierpinski classifier row $S$. The topology handoff $T$ records the Lawson subbasis generated from the Scott-open and lower-compact rows.

Since specialization is recovered from the displayed topology observations, the route must pass through $D,O,K,S,T$ and then through $H,C,P,N$. None of the carrier rows stores host point equality, a powerset topology, or a completion theorem.
\end{proof}
