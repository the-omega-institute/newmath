\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\DecimalEndpointNormalizationUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-decimal-endpoint-normalization-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, the $\DecimalEndpointNormalizationUp$ packet records the finite convention that relates terminating decimal prefixes to repeating-nine endpoint prefixes. It refines the finite decimal readback surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-decimalexpansion-namecert} without selecting a host representative for a real number. The packet compares its decimal-window rows with the binary and continued-fraction readback neighbors of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-binaryexpansion-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuedfraction-namecert}, and it hands all real-facing reads through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}.

\begin{definition}[Decimal endpoint normalization carrier]\label{def:decimal-endpoint-normalization-carrier}
A $\DecimalEndpointNormalizationUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(D,E,L,A,W,Q,R,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the finite digit-prefix ledger. Its rows list the inspected decimal prefixes, their signs or integral prefixes when present, and the finite place window in which the prefix is read.
\item $E$ is the terminating-versus-repeating-nine endpoint ledger. Each row records a displayed finite terminating prefix, the corresponding repeating-nine tail convention visible inside the same finite window, and the endpoint side on which the convention is invoked.
\item $L$ is the decimal carry and normalization row. It records the finite carry propagation, the last changed digit, and the normalized prefix produced by that carry.
\item $A$ is the base-ten rational place-value row for the prefixes displayed by $D,E,L$.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row used to request and replay exactly the inspected prefix and endpoint-convention entries.
\item $Q$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance readback row comparing the base-ten place-value rows with the dyadic tolerance demanded by the consumer.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row reached from the same window, place-value, carry, and dyadic-tolerance rows.
\item $S$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row exposed only after $D,E,L,A,W,Q,R$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for digit-prefix, endpoint-ledger, carry-normalization, place-value, stream-window, dyadic-tolerance, regular-readback, seal, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ replay from prefix windows to endpoint rows, from endpoint rows to carry normalization, from normalization to dyadic tolerance, from tolerance to regular readback, and from readback to the real seal.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\DecimalEndpointNormalizationUp$, $\DecimalExpansionUp$, $\BinaryExpansionUp$, $\ContinuedFractionUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and the local naming row.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DecimalEndpointNormalizationUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier stores only the finite endpoint-convention certificate just displayed. It has no row for host decimal equality, quotient-real equality, selected representatives, trichotomy, countable choice, $\RealUp$ completeness, or a standard bridge.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Decimal endpoint normalization classifier]\label{def:decimal-endpoint-normalization-classifier}
The local classifier for an accepted packet $K=(D,E,L,A,W,Q,R,S,H,C,P,N)$ accepts exactly those finite reads that pass through the displayed rows in the order
$$
D,E,L,A,W,Q,R,S .
$$
It treats a terminating endpoint row and its repeating-nine companion as two named endpoint conventions inside the same finite ledger $E$; it then reads the carry row $L$ to produce the normalized finite prefix inspected by $A$. Classifier transport is componentwise through $H$, continuation is replay through $C$, and package admissibility is the single provenance row $P$ together with the local naming row $N$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Decimal endpoint normalization NameCert obligations]\label{thm:decimal-endpoint-normalization-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\DecimalEndpointNormalizationUp$ carrier $K=(D,E,L,A,W,Q,R,S,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\DecimalEndpointNormalizationUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the digit-prefix, endpoint-ledger, carry-normalization, place-value, stream-window, dyadic-tolerance, regular-readback, real-seal, transport, continuation, provenance, and naming rows;
\item classifier transport along the stored $\hsame$ coordinates and the displayed $\Cont$ route;
\item endpoint-ledger exactness, stating that every terminating-versus-repeating-nine comparison exported by the packet is one of the rows listed in $E$ and normalized through $L$;
\item readback discipline, stating that every $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, or $\RealUp$ consumer reads the same finite window before receiving the corresponding tolerance, handoff, or seal row;
\item ledger exclusion, stating that host decimal equality, quotient-real equality, selected representatives, trichotomy, countable choice, $\RealUp$ completeness, and bridge data are not exported by the packet.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:decimal-endpoint-normalization-carrier}. The finite digit-prefix rows, endpoint ledger, carry-normalization row, place-value row, stream window, dyadic tolerance, regular readback, real seal, transports, continuations, package row, and local naming row are precisely the displayed coordinates.

Classifier transport is componentwise. The digit and endpoint rows move through their stored $\hsame$ witnesses, while $C$ replays the same window-to-endpoint, endpoint-to-carry, carry-to-tolerance, and tolerance-to-readback route after transport.

Endpoint-ledger exactness is local to $E$ and $L$. A terminating prefix and a repeating-nine companion are compared only when the finite ledger lists that pair, and the only exported normalization is the carry row displayed for that pair.

The readback discipline then follows the sibling surfaces. Windows are read through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, dyadic tolerances through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, regular rational handoff through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal seal through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}.

The excluded principles are absent from the carrier inventory, so the local certificate cannot export them.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Decimal endpoint Real readback boundary]\label{thm:decimal-endpoint-normalization-real-readback-boundary}
Every $\RealUp$ read of an accepted $\DecimalEndpointNormalizationUp$ packet factors through the finite route
$$
\DecimalExpansionUp,\ \DecimalEndpointNormalizationUp,\ \StreamNameUp,\ \DyadicRatCoreUp,\ \RegSeqRatUp,\ \RealUp .
$$
The decimal packet supplies finite prefix rows, the endpoint-normalization packet supplies the displayed terminating-versus-repeating-nine ledger and carry row, the stream window supplies the inspected observation, the dyadic row supplies tolerance readback, the regular row supplies the rational handoff, and the real-facing consumer receives only the displayed seal. The binary and continued-fraction neighbors from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-binaryexpansion-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuedfraction-namecert} are comparison readback surfaces, not additional representative selectors.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the accepted packet $K=(D,E,L,A,W,Q,R,S,H,C,P,N)$. The decimal-facing data are $D,E,L,A$: the prefix ledger, endpoint-convention ledger, carry-normalization row, and base-ten place-value row.

The stream-facing data are exactly the windows in $W$. A consumer can inspect only the finite prefix and endpoint rows named by that window, so no infinite decimal stream enters the read.

The dyadic row $Q$ converts the same finite normalized prefix into the tolerance language used by downstream real consumers. The regular handoff row $R$ is reached only after $D,E,L,A,W,Q$ are fixed.

The seal row $S$ is downstream of $R$, and the rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve the same route under transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming. Thus the real readback has the displayed finite factorization and cannot add a selected representative or quotient-real equality test.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Decimal endpoint nonescape]\label{thm:decimal-endpoint-normalization-nonescape}
Let $K=(D,E,L,A,W,Q,R,S,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\DecimalEndpointNormalizationUp$ packet. Any consumer that reads an endpoint convention, a normalized decimal prefix, a dyadic tolerance, a regular rational handoff, or a visible $\RealUp$ seal is exhausted by the displayed rows of $K$. In particular, no route from $K$ escapes to endpoint uniqueness as host equality, quotient reals, selected representatives, trichotomy, countable choice, $\RealUp$ completeness, or a bridge.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the requested datum by cases. Endpoint conventions use $E$, normalized decimal prefixes use $L$ together with the prefix rows $D$, dyadic tolerances use $Q$, regular rational handoffs use $R$, and visible real seals use $S$.

The shared observation window is $W$, and all replay is mediated by $C$. Hence every case remains inside the finite window and the rows already displayed in the carrier.

Transport and provenance do not add new data. The row $H$ transports equal displayed coordinates, while $P$ records the dependence on the decimal, binary, continued-fraction, stream, dyadic, regular, and real sibling surfaces already cited in the chapter.

The listed escaped objects are not rows of $K$. Therefore the packet cannot supply them to any consumer of its endpoint-normalization certificate.
\end{proof}
