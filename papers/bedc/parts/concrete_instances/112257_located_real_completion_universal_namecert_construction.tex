\begin{theorem}[LocatedRealCompletionUniversal NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:located-real-completion-universal-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\LocatedRealCompletionUniversalUp$ carrier
$$
U=(D,S,R,E,L,F,X,Q,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedRealCompletionUniversalUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for dyadic rational probes, stream windows, regular rational readbacks, Real seals, located comparisons, located completion functor handoffs, extension rows, universal-property comparison rows, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming;
\item rational-source density, requiring every extension request to expose $D,S,R$ before the terminal $\RealUp$ seal $E$ can be consumed;
\item located functoriality, requiring $F$ to read the displayed located comparison row $L$ together with the same rational-source and Real-seal rows;
\item extension formation, requiring $X$ to be built from $D,S,R,E,L,F$ and not from a quotient class or selected completion point;
\item uniqueness comparison, requiring $Q$ to compare extension routes only after agreement on the displayed dense rational source has been named;
\item structural stability under $H,C,P,N$, which transports, replays, sources, and names only the displayed packet.
\end{enumerate}
No obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:located-real-completion-universal-carrier}. The source-facing rows are $D,S,R,E$: dyadic probes, stream windows, regular rational readback, and the Real seal.

The located route is then read through $L$ and $F$. The located comparison row supplies the finite tests used by the functor handoff, and $F$ cannot act before the source-facing rows have been displayed.

The extension row $X$ is downstream of those data. Since the carrier has no quotient-class or selected-limit coordinate, an accepted extension route is a displayed route through $D,S,R,E,L,F$ rather than a host completion map.

Finally read the comparison and structural rows. The row $Q$ compares extension routes only by their displayed agreement on the dense rational source, while $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite packet. These projections exhaust the listed NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LocatedRealCompletionUniversal extension route]
\label{thm:located-real-completion-universal-extension-route}
Every extension read exported by an accepted $\LocatedRealCompletionUniversalUp$ carrier factors through the finite route
$$

It does not export quotient classes of Cauchy names, choice-selected limits, ambient completeness, global equality of completed objects, or an extension principle outside the displayed packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with an extension request. By carrier admission the only rational source available to the request is the displayed triple $D,S,R$, and the only real-facing endpoint attached to that source is $E$.

The located part of the request is read next. The row $L$ supplies the finite located comparisons, and the $\LocatedCompletionFunctorUp$ handoff $F$ consumes that row together with the already displayed rational and Real data.

The extension and uniqueness rows are downstream. The row $X$ records the extension route built from the finite source and located functor rows, while $Q$ compares two such routes only through displayed agreement on the dense rational source, as in the $\CauchyCompletionUniversalPropertyUp$ packet.

Transport through $H$, replay through $C$, provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$ preserve the same route. Since none of the refused objects is a coordinate of the carrier, no extension read can bypass the displayed finite surface.
\end{proof}
