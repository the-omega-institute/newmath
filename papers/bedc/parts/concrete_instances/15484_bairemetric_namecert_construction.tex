\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\BaireMetricUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-bairemetric-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Brouwer's finite-prefix presentation of choice sequences, the Bishop--Bridges metric treatment of countable product spaces, and Kelley's prefix topology, the $\BaireMetricUp$ packet records the standard prefix ultrametric on $\BaireSpaceUp$ as finite BEDC rows. It sits between the prefix carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bairespace-namecert}, the metric parent surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, and the strong-triangle interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-ultrametricspace-namecert}. The packet names only displayed first-disagreement windows, dyadic radius comparisons, stream-name schedules, transport, replay, provenance, and a local certificate; it does not import completed streams, quotient equality of infinite names, real-valued host metrics, or external topology.

\begin{definition}[BaireMetric prefix-distance carrier]
\label{def:bairemetric-prefix-distance-carrier}
A $\BaireMetricUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
M=(B,W,D,R,U,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
over $\BaireSpaceUp$, $\NatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\MetricSpaceUp$, $\UltrametricSpaceUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $B$ is the accepted $\BaireSpaceUp$ prefix packet whose finite stream windows supply the two names under comparison.
\item $W$ is the $\NatUp$ first-disagreement window. It records the inspected prefix length, the equality rows below that length, and the first displayed coordinate where the two prefix windows separate, when such a coordinate is present.
\item $D$ is the dyadic radius row. It records the radius $2^{-k}$ attached to the first displayed disagreement at $k$, together with the zero-radius branch for displayed equality across the inspected window.
\item $R$ is the parent $\MetricSpaceUp$ row that reads the displayed point names, zero-distance boundary, symmetry row, and ordinary triangle route from $B,W,D$.
\item $U$ is the $\UltrametricSpaceUp$ witness whose strong max-radius comparison is read from the same first-disagreement window and dyadic radius row.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule controlling which finite prefixes of the two Baire names may be inspected by a consumer.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the BaireSpace row, Nat window, dyadic radius row, parent metric row, ultrametric witness, stream schedule, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a requested prefix comparison through $S$, then through $B,W,D$, and only then into the parent metric row $R$ and ultrametric witness $U$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over the listed BEDC dependencies.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\BaireMetricUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier accepts the packet only when every distance comparison is read through the displayed first-disagreement window and dyadic radius row. It has no coordinate for a completed infinite stream, a quotient of Baire names, a host real-valued distance function, countable choice, or an external topological space.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[BaireMetric NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:bairemetric-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\BaireMetricUp$ carrier $M=(B,W,D,R,U,S,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\BaireMetricUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following five rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed BaireSpace prefix packet, Nat first-disagreement window, dyadic radius row, parent MetricSpace row, UltrametricSpace witness, StreamName schedule, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item prefix-distance admission, requiring every positive radius claim to name the first displayed disagreement in $W$ and every zero-radius claim to remain inside the inspected equality window;
\item metric compatibility, requiring the parent $\MetricSpaceUp$ row $R$ to read zero-distance, symmetry, and ordinary triangle evidence only from $B,W,D$;
\item ultrametric compatibility, requiring the witness $U$ to read its strong max-radius comparison from the same first-disagreement and dyadic-radius rows;
\item ledger refusal, excluding completed streams, quotient equality of Baire names, host real-valued metrics, countable-choice selectors, and external topology.
\end{enumerate}
No obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:bairemetric-prefix-distance-carrier}. The tuple lists exactly the BaireSpace prefix packet, first-disagreement window, dyadic radius row, parent metric row, ultrametric witness, stream schedule, transport, continuation, provenance, and local name.

Prefix-distance admission is read from $W$ and $D$. A positive comparison names the least displayed coordinate where the two scheduled prefixes disagree, while the zero-radius branch records only equality across the inspected finite window.

Metric compatibility is then replayed through $R$. The zero-distance boundary, symmetry row, and ordinary triangle route do not inspect a separate distance function; they use the same prefix windows and dyadic radius comparisons already displayed by $B,W,D$.

Ultrametric compatibility is read from $U$. Since the controlling radius is determined by the earliest displayed disagreement, the strong max-radius comparison factors through the same window and cannot bypass the dyadic ledger.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name only those coordinates. Hence no completed stream, quotient equality of infinite names, host real metric, countable-choice selector, or external topological datum can appear as a NameCert obligation.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BaireMetric prefix-window admission]
\label{thm:bairemetric-prefix-window-admission}
Let $M=(B,W,D,R,U,S,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\BaireMetricUp$ carrier. Every accepted distance read admits a finite prefix window: before the dyadic radius row is inspected, the continuation row displays a $\StreamNameUp$ schedule $S$, an accepted $\BaireSpaceUp$ prefix packet $B$, and a $\NatUp$ window $W$ that records the inspected length and the equality rows below the first displayed separation.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:bairemetric-prefix-distance-carrier}. The continuation row $C$ routes every comparison through the stream schedule $S$ and the BaireSpace prefix packet $B$ before reaching any radius coordinate.

The window row $W$ is the only Nat-valued inspection coordinate in the carrier. Thus the accepted comparison must name the inspected finite length and the displayed equality rows below any first disagreement before $D,R,$ or $U$ can be read.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BaireMetric first-disagreement dyadic exactness]
\label{thm:bairemetric-first-disagreement-dyadic-radius-exactness}
For an accepted $\BaireMetricUp$ carrier $M=(B,W,D,R,U,S,H,C,P,N)$, the dyadic radius row $D$ is exact for the displayed first-disagreement window. If $W$ records first disagreement at $k$, then $D$ records the radius branch $2^{-k}$; if $W$ records only displayed equality across the inspected window, then $D$ records the zero-radius branch for that finite read. No other radius source is available to the packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier admits dyadic radius data only through the row $D$. The row is downstream of $S,B,W$, so its positive branch is read from the first displayed disagreement already named by $W$.

In the equality branch, $W$ exposes only the inspected finite equality window. The corresponding radius branch in $D$ is therefore the displayed zero-radius read for that window, not a claim about completed infinite stream equality.

Because $R$ and $U$ are downstream consumers of $D$, neither can introduce an independent radius value. Exactness is consequently a projection of the single dyadic row attached to the first-disagreement window.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BaireMetric prefix radius monotonicity]
\label{thm:bairemetric-prefix-radius-monotonicity}
Let $M=(B,W,D,R,U,S,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\BaireMetricUp$ carrier. Extending the displayed inspected prefix through the same $\StreamNameUp$ schedule can only preserve the dyadic radius branch already read from $W,D$ or move it to a smaller displayed dyadic branch. The extension cannot replace the first-disagreement route by a larger radius, a host metric, or a completed-stream comparison.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use carrier admission from \autoref{def:bairemetric-prefix-distance-carrier} and the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:bairemetric-namecert-obligations}. Every distance read is routed through the scheduled prefix packet $S,B$ and the finite window $W$ before the dyadic row $D$ is inspected.

By prefix-window admission in \autoref{thm:bairemetric-prefix-window-admission}, an extended read only adds inspected coordinates after the displayed window already exposed. If the earlier first disagreement remains visible, first-disagreement exactness in \autoref{thm:bairemetric-first-disagreement-dyadic-radius-exactness} keeps the same $2^{-k}$ branch.

If the extension exposes a later first displayed disagreement after an equality branch, the dyadic branch read from $D$ moves to that later index and is therefore no larger than the previous displayed prefix bound. Since $R$ and $U$ consume the same $D$ row, no downstream metric or ultrametric consumer can enlarge the radius.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BaireMetric zero-radius branch confinement]
\label{thm:bairemetric-zero-radius-branch}
For an accepted $\BaireMetricUp$ carrier $M=(B,W,D,R,U,S,H,C,P,N)$, the zero-radius branch of $D$ is confined to displayed finite inspected-window equality. It is never a quotient equality of completed Baire streams, a global extensional equality theorem, or an ambient topological identification.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the zero branch through the carrier of \autoref{def:bairemetric-prefix-distance-carrier}. The only equality evidence before $D$ is the displayed finite equality row in $W$, scheduled by $S$ and admitted through $B$.

Apply the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:bairemetric-namecert-obligations}. The prefix-distance row requires every zero-radius read to remain inside the inspected equality window, and the ledger-refusal row excludes completed streams, quotient equality of Baire names, host real metrics, and external topology.

Finally use \autoref{thm:bairemetric-first-disagreement-dyadic-radius-exactness}. Its equality branch identifies the zero-radius read with that finite window alone. Transport by $H$ and replay by $C$ preserve the same row family and cannot turn finite inspected equality into equality of completed streams.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BaireMetric prefix radius transport]
\label{thm:bairemetric-prefix-radius-transport}
Let $M=(B,W,D,R,U,S,H,C,P,N)$ and $M'=(B',W',D',R',U',S',H',C',P',N')$ be accepted $\BaireMetricUp$ carriers. If componentwise $\hsame$ transports the displayed Baire prefix packets, stream schedules, first-disagreement windows, dyadic rows, parent metric rows, ultrametric rows, provenance, and local names, and the displayed $\Cont$ replay preserves the prefix-read order, then $M'$ exposes the same route
$$
S,B,W,D,R,U
$$
as $M$ for every accepted prefix-radius read.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the accepted read in $M$. Prefix-window admission supplies the schedule, prefix packet, first-disagreement window, and dyadic row before any parent metric or ultrametric row is consumed.

Transport each displayed coordinate through the componentwise $\hsame$ rows recorded in $H$ and $H'$. The carrier definition gives matching coordinates $S',B',W',D',R',U'$ and the NameCert obligations require those coordinates to remain the only distance, metric, and ultrametric sources.

Replay the transported read through $C'$. First-disagreement exactness then reads $D'$ from the transported $W'$ branch, while the parent handoff and ultrametric handoff consume $R'$ and $U'$ downstream of that same branch. Thus the transported packet preserves the route and admits no alternate radius source.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BaireMetric parent MetricSpace handoff]
\label{thm:bairemetric-parent-metricspace-handoff}
Every parent $\MetricSpaceUp$ read exported by an accepted $\BaireMetricUp$ packet factors through
$$
S,B,W,D \longmapsto R .
$$
The zero-distance boundary, symmetry row, and ordinary triangle route in $R$ may read only the scheduled prefix packet, first-disagreement window, and dyadic radius row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a parent metric read. Carrier admission exposes the parent row only as $R$, and the definition places $R$ downstream of the scheduled prefix and dyadic comparison rows.

The zero-distance, symmetry, and ordinary triangle components of $R$ therefore receive their data from $S,B,W,D$. Componentwise transport by $H$ and replay by $C$ preserve this order, so the handoff cannot inspect a host metric on completed streams.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BaireMetric UltrametricSpace strong-triangle handoff]
\label{thm:bairemetric-ultrametricspace-strong-triangle-handoff}
Every $\UltrametricSpaceUp$ strong-triangle read exported by an accepted $\BaireMetricUp$ carrier factors through the same first-disagreement and dyadic-radius rows used by the parent metric handoff:
$$
S,B,W,D,R \longmapsto U .

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

Thus a strong-triangle read must first replay the finite prefix comparisons through $S,B,W,D$ and the parent handoff $R$. The row $U$ then packages the max-radius comparison over those displayed radii, with no coordinate for external topology or completed Baire names.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BaireMetric stream-schedule nonescape]
\label{thm:bairemetric-stream-schedule-nonescape}
For every accepted $\BaireMetricUp$ carrier $M=(B,W,D,R,U,S,H,C,P,N)$, a consumer cannot read a distance, metric, or ultrametric conclusion outside the displayed $\StreamNameUp$ schedule. Every such conclusion is replayed by $C$ through $S$ before it reaches $B,W,D,R,$ or $U$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The stream schedule $S$ is the carrier coordinate controlling which finite prefixes may be inspected. The continuation row $C$ names the replay order from a requested comparison through $S$ before any BaireSpace, window, radius, metric, or ultrametric row is consumed.

Transport, provenance, and local naming preserve that replay order. Consequently a consumer has no slot from which to read an unscheduled prefix, completed stream, quotient equality of names, or external topological neighborhood.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BaireMetric ultrametric prefix route]
\label{thm:bairemetric-ultrametric-prefix-route}
Let $M=(B,W,D,R,U,S,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\BaireMetricUp$ packet. Every accepted prefix-distance comparison exported by $M$ factors through the displayed route
$$
B,S,W,D,R,U .
$$
Equivalently, a consumer reads first the $\BaireSpaceUp$ prefix packet, then the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule, the $\NatUp$ first-disagreement window, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ radius row, the parent $\MetricSpaceUp$ row, and finally the $\UltrametricSpaceUp$ strong-triangle witness. The route is a finite BEDC replay over prefix rows, not a host metric on completed streams.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted prefix-distance comparison exported by $M$. Carrier admission exposes the comparison only through the coordinates $B,S,W,D,R,U$ together with the structural rows.

The continuation row $C$ first schedules finite prefix reads through $S$ and then reads the accepted $\BaireSpaceUp$ packet $B$. The $\NatUp$ window $W$ records how far the comparison has inspected the two prefixes and, when the positive branch is taken, which displayed coordinate is the first disagreement.

The dyadic radius row $D$ converts that window into the displayed radius comparison. The parent $\MetricSpaceUp$ row $R$ receives only this radius evidence when supplying zero-distance, symmetry, and ordinary triangle data.

Finally the $\UltrametricSpaceUp$ witness $U$ reads the same first-disagreement and radius rows to supply the strong max-radius route. Componentwise transport by $H$ and provenance by $P$ preserve this order, so no consumer-facing path bypasses the displayed BaireSpace, Nat, StreamName, DyadicRatCore, MetricSpace, and UltrametricSpace rows.
\end{proof}

\label{thm:bairemetric-root-prefix-ultrametric-package}

$$

$$
No consumer-facing read may replace this row family by completed streams, quotient equality of names, host real metrics, countable choice, or external topology.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:bairemetric-prefix-window-admission}. It forces every distance read to expose the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule $S$, the $\BaireSpaceUp$ prefix packet $B$, and the $\NatUp$ first-disagreement window $W$ before any radius or metric row is inspected.

Apply \autoref{thm:bairemetric-first-disagreement-dyadic-radius-exactness}. The dyadic radius row $D$ is then determined by the displayed first-disagreement window, with the zero-radius branch remaining inside the inspected finite equality window.

Apply \autoref{thm:bairemetric-parent-metricspace-handoff} and \autoref{thm:bairemetric-ultrametricspace-strong-triangle-handoff}. The parent $\MetricSpaceUp$ row $R$ and the $\UltrametricSpaceUp$ row $U$ both read the same scheduled prefix, window, and dyadic-radius data.

Finally use \autoref{thm:bairemetric-stream-schedule-nonescape} and \autoref{thm:bairemetric-ultrametric-prefix-route}. They place every metric, ultrametric, PolishSpace, BaireSpace, or downstream prefix consumer behind the same replay row $C$, with componentwise transport $H$, provenance $P$, and local naming $N$. Since the carrier has no coordinate for completed streams, quotient equality of names, host real metrics, countable choice, or external topology, the displayed root package is the whole exported surface.
