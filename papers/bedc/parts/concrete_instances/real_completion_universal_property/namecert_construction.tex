\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RealCompletionUniversalPropertyUp$}

Following Bishop and Bridges on constructive completions, the $\RealCompletionUniversalPropertyUp$ packet records the L10 universal-property neighbour of the Real completion interface as finite NameCert data. It consumes the dyadic tolerance core of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the finite stream windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the completion interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-real-completion-interface-namecert}, and the Bishop regular Cauchy completion route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bishop-regular-cauchy-completion-namecert}. The packet names extension and uniqueness rows for completion-facing consumers without asserting quotient streams, selected limits, host equality, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[Real completion universal-property carrier]
\label{def:real-completion-universal-property-carrier}
A $\RealCompletionUniversalPropertyUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
G=(D,S,R,B,E,U,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ dense-source row, exposing only named dyadic tolerances and dyadic source observations.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row through which completion-facing source reads are scheduled.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular-readback row attached to the same stream windows and dyadic tolerances.
\item $B$ is the $\BishopRegularCauchyCompletionUp$ handoff row, recording the regular Cauchy completion route before any terminal Real seal is consumed.
\item $E$ is the $\CauchyRealCompletionInterfaceUp$ extension row, displaying the completion-interface read that receives $D,S,R,B$.
\item $U$ is the universal-property row: it records existence and uniqueness of the displayed extension only as a finite route over $D,S,R,B,E$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the dyadic, stream, regular-readback, completion, interface, universal-property, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a dense-source request through $D,S,R,B,E,U,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RealCompletionUniversalPropertyUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\BishopRegularCauchyCompletionUp$, $\CauchyRealCompletionInterfaceUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RealCompletionUniversalPropertyUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only through $D,S,R,B,E,U,H,C,P,N$. It contains no coordinate for quotient streams, selected limits, host equality, countable choice, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, or bridge credentials.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Real completion universal-property NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:real-completion-universal-property-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RealCompletionUniversalPropertyUp$ carrier $G=(D,S,R,B,E,U,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\RealCompletionUniversalPropertyUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed dyadic source, stream-window, regular-readback, Bishop regular Cauchy completion, completion-interface, universal-property, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item dense-source exposure, requiring every extension read to project $D$ before inspecting the completion-interface row;
\item stream-window exactness, requiring $S$ to schedule the finite observations consumed by the regular-readback and completion handoff rows;
\item regular-readback handoff, requiring $R$ and then $B$ to mediate between the finite windows and the completion-facing interface;
\item extension existence, requiring $E$ and $U$ to display a completion-interface extension over the same $D,S,R,B$ coordinates;
\item extension uniqueness, requiring any two accepted extension reads over the same displayed dyadic, stream, regular-readback, and terminal Real-seal rows to share the same completion-facing route;
\item non-escape, requiring $H,C,P,N$ to transport, replay, source, and name no quotient stream, selected limit, host equality, countable-choice schedule, ambient Real-completeness theorem, Lean verification, or bridge credential.
\end{enumerate}
No local obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:real-completion-universal-property-carrier}. The accepted tuple displays exactly the dyadic source, stream-window, regular-readback, completion, interface, universal-property, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

The dense-source and stream-window clauses follow from the order of the displayed route. An extension read must first expose $D$ and then schedule finite observations through $S$ before the regular readback or completion interface can be consumed.

The regular-readback handoff is carried by $R$ and $B$. These rows bind the stream windows and dyadic tolerances to the Bishop regular Cauchy completion route, so the interface cannot choose a quotient representative or host limit.

The extension rows $E$ and $U$ then record existence and uniqueness only over the fixed $D,S,R,B$ coordinates. Transport, replay, provenance, and local naming through $H,C,P,N$ preserve that route and provide no coordinate for the excluded principles.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real completion universal-property Real-seal uniqueness]
\label{thm:real-completion-universal-property-real-seal-uniqueness}
Let $G=(D,S,R,B,E,U,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\RealCompletionUniversalPropertyUp$ carrier. If two accepted extension reads display the same dyadic row $D$, stream row $S$, regular-readback row $R$, Bishop regular Cauchy completion row $B$, and terminal $\RealUp$ seal row named by the completion interface, then they have the same completion-facing route through $E,U,H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier and the two accepted reads. The hypotheses identify the dyadic source, finite windows, regular readback, Bishop regular Cauchy handoff, and terminal Real seal rows.

Apply the extension uniqueness row of \autoref{thm:real-completion-universal-property-namecert-obligations}. Since uniqueness is recorded over exactly the displayed $D,S,R,B,E$ coordinates, the two reads cannot diverge into separate completion-interface routes.

The remaining structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same accepted route. Because the carrier has no quotient-stream, selected-limit, host-equality, countable-choice, ambient-completeness, or bridge coordinate, the common displayed seal determines the completion-facing route.
\end{proof}
