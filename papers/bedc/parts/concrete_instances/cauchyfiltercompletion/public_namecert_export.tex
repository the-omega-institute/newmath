\begin{theorem}[CauchyFilterCompletion public NameCert export]
\label{thm:cauchyfiltercompletion-public-namecert}
Let $F=(C,W,T,R,L,H,K,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyFilterCompletionUp$ carrier. The public $\NameCert_{\CauchyFilterCompletionUp}$ export is exactly the route $C,W,T,R,L,H,K,P,N$ isolated by the carrier, the completion handoff, the completion-functor factorization, the cofinal regular readback, and source/ledger exhaustion. A public consumer receives the displayed Cauchy-filter source, finite windows, dyadic tolerance ledger, regular readback, terminal Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows, and receives no quotient-filter, selected-limit, host-equality, choice, or ambient-completeness object.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-filter-completion-carrier}. The visible packet is $C,W,T,R,L,H,K,P,N$, with $C$ as source, $W,T,R$ as finite readback rows, $L$ as terminal seal, and $H,K,P,N$ as transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

Apply completion handoff and completion-functor factorization, \autoref{thm:cauchy-filter-completion-completion-handoff} and \autoref{thm:cauchy-filter-completion-completion-functor-factorization}. They place every completion-facing, metric-facing, uniform-facing, and functor-facing read on the same displayed route.

Use cofinal regular readback together with source exactness, \autoref{thm:cauchy-filter-completion-cofinal-regular-readback} and \autoref{thm:cauchy-filter-completion-source-exactness}. These theorems identify the cofinal public readback through the $\CauchyFilterUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ rows already carried by $F$.

Finally apply ledger exhaustion, \autoref{thm:cauchy-filter-completion-ledger-exhaustion}. The quotient-filter, selected-limit, host-equality, choice, and ambient-completeness alternatives have no carrier coordinate, so the public NameCert export is exactly the displayed route.
\end{proof}
