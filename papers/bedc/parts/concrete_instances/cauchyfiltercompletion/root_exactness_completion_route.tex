\begin{theorem}[CauchyFilterCompletion root exactness]
\label{thm:cauchy-filter-completion-root-exactness}
Let $F=(C,W,T,R,L,H,K,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyFilterCompletionUp$ carrier. The accepted root reads of $F$ are exhausted by the $\FilterBaseUp/\CauchyFilterUp$ source row $C$, $\StreamNameUp$ windows $W$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger $T$, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback $R$, terminal $\RealUp$ seal $L$, $\UniformCompletionUp$ provenance carried by $P$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay $K$, $\Pkg$ provenance $P$, and local $\NameCert_{\CauchyFilterCompletionUp}$ rows $N$. No root read adds a quotient filter, selected limit, detached completion carrier, host equality, countable-choice selector, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-filter-completion-carrier}. Its root completion route is the displayed tuple $C,W,T,R,L,H,K,P,N$.

Apply \autoref{thm:cauchy-filter-completion-filterbase-window-minimality} and \autoref{thm:cauchy-filter-completion-tail-readback-admission}. These place every source and tail read behind the $\FilterBaseUp/\CauchyFilterUp$ row, the finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, and the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback.

Use \autoref{thm:cauchy-filter-completion-real-seal-source-exhaustion} and \autoref{thm:cauchy-filter-completion-uniform-completion-universal-property}. The Real seal and uniform-completion consumers inherit the same finite route and cannot introduce detached completion data.

Finally the structural rows $H,K,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name exactly those reads. Since the refused quotient, selected-limit, host-equality, choice, and ambient-completeness objects have no coordinate in the carrier, the listed rows exhaust the root completion surface.
\end{proof}
