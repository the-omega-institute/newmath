Following Kelley, \emph{General Topology}, and Bishop--Bridges constructive analysis, the $\RealOrderTopologyUp$ packet records the finite order-topology route on BEDC real names. It binds the real-name carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the comparison rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-alg-order-namecert}, the generated-open surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-topology-namecert}, the ball-basis packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metricball-namecert}, and the observable-open truth interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-sierpinski-space-namecert}. The packet gives later L10 consumers a BEDC-native topology interface for $\RealUp$ without treating host subsets, completeness, trichotomy, Dedekind cuts, or open-cover compactness as primitive structure.

\begin{definition}[Real order topology carrier]
\label{def:real-order-topology-carrier}
A $\RealOrderTopologyUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
O=(R,A,B,S,T,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $R$ is the $\RealUp$ point-name surface, exposing only finite real-name windows and their admitted seal rows.
\item $A$ is the $\RealAlgOrderUp$ comparison row used to read rational and real-name order probes against the displayed point windows.
\item $B$ is the interval-or-ball basis row, recording finite rays, bounded intervals, and $\MetricBallUp$ cells that are generated from the comparison service.
\item $S$ is the $\SierpinskiSpaceUp$ observable-open truth row, recording which displayed point-window probes certify membership in a basic open.
\item $T$ is the $\TopologyUp$ generated-open row, closing only under the finite generated-open operations displayed by $B$ and $S$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the real point rows, comparison rows, basis rows, observable truth rows, generated-open rows, replay row, provenance row, and local naming row.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a real-name observation through comparison, basis generation, observable-open truth, generated-open formation, transport, provenance, and local naming.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RealOrderTopologyUp$, $\RealUp$, $\RealAlgOrderUp$, $\MetricBallUp$, $\SierpinskiSpaceUp$, $\TopologyUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RealOrderTopologyUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for Real completeness, order trichotomy, Dedekind-cut construction, host subset equality, open-cover compactness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Real order topology classifier]
\label{def:real-order-topology-classifier}
Two accepted $\RealOrderTopologyUp$ carriers
$$
O=(R,A,B,S,T,H,C,P,N)
\quad\text{and}\quad
O'=(R',A',B',S',T',H',C',P',N')
$$
are classified by $\msame_{\RealOrderTopologyUp}$ exactly when their displayed real point rows, order-comparison rows, interval-or-ball basis rows, Sierpinski observable-open truth rows, generated-open rows, transports, replay routes, provenance rows, and local naming rows are matched componentwise by the visible $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ evidence. The classifier cannot inspect hidden host subsets, completeness witnesses, trichotomy decisions, Dedekind cuts, open-cover compactness data, Lean proof terms, or bridge certificates.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Real order topology NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:real-order-topology-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\RealOrderTopologyUp$ carrier $O=(R,A,B,S,T,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\RealOrderTopologyUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the real point-name rows, order-comparison rows, interval-or-ball basis rows, observable-open truth rows, generated-open topology rows, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming;
\item comparison-source exactness, requiring every interval endpoint, ray boundary, and ball-center comparison used by $B$ to pass through the displayed $\RealAlgOrderUp$ row $A$ and the displayed $\RealUp$ row $R$;
\item basis-generation exactness, requiring every basic open to be generated from the displayed interval-or-ball row $B$ and not from a host subset predicate;
\item observable-open compatibility, requiring every membership read exported to $\SierpinskiSpaceUp$ to be replayed through the same rows $R,A,B$ before the truth row $S$ is accepted;
\item topology-row stability, requiring the $\TopologyUp$ row $T$ to close only the generated-open operations displayed by $B$ and $S$;
\item transport and replay stability, requiring $H$ and $C$ to preserve the route $R,A,B,S,T,P,N$ without adding hidden completeness, trichotomy, Dedekind-cut, compactness, Lean, or bridge data;
\item ledger exhaustion, stating that no Real completeness, trichotomy, Dedekind-cut construction, open-cover compactness, Lean verification, or bridge checking is exported.
\end{enumerate}
No order-topology obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:real-order-topology-carrier}. The accepted packet displays exactly one real point-name surface, one comparison row, one basis row, one observable-open truth row, one generated-open row, and the structural rows.

Comparison-source exactness follows from the pair $R,A$. Every endpoint, ray, and ball-center comparison consumed by the basis row has no second comparison coordinate and no hidden host-order field.

Basis generation and observable-open compatibility are forced by $B$ and $S$. The basis row lists only finite interval-or-ball cells, and the observable truth row reads those cells through the same real-name and comparison rows.

Topology-row stability is the role of $T$. It records the generated-open operations admitted from $B$ and $S$, so it cannot replace the generated surface with an ambient subset predicate.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite route. Since the excluded completeness, trichotomy, Dedekind-cut, compactness, Lean, and bridge data are absent from the carrier inventory, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real order topology Cauchy-window basis]
\label{thm:real-order-topology-cauchy-window-basis}
Every basic-open read of an accepted $\RealOrderTopologyUp$ carrier factors through finite $\RealUp$ windows, $\RealAlgOrderUp$ comparisons, and an interval-or-ball basis row before it reaches either the $\SierpinskiSpaceUp$ observable-open truth row or the $\TopologyUp$ generated-open row. In particular, a Cauchy-window consumer of real names can use the displayed route
$$
R,A,B \longmapsto S,T,H,C,P,N
$$
without importing a host subset predicate, a selected limit, real-order trichotomy, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted carrier $O=(R,A,B,S,T,H,C,P,N)$. The only point-name coordinate is $R$, so any real-name window opened by a Cauchy-style consumer must begin with that finite surface.

The only order service is $A$. Thus endpoint, ray, and ball-center tests used to recognize a basic open are read through the displayed comparison row.

The basis row $B$ receives those finite comparison reads and records the interval-or-ball cells. The observable truth row $S$ and the generated-open row $T$ are downstream of that basis row, so neither can introduce an independent host subset.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve the same route. Because the packet has no selected-limit coordinate, trichotomy field, or completeness witness, the Cauchy-window consumer remains inside the stated finite basis route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real order topology Real seal nonescape]
\label{thm:real-order-topology-real-seal-nonescape}
For an accepted $\RealOrderTopologyUp$ carrier $O=(R,A,B,S,T,H,C,P,N)$, every read that reaches a $\RealUp$ seal or a generated-open seal has already passed through the finite route
$$
R,A,B,S,T,H,C,P,N.
$$
The read exports no Real completeness principle, no trichotomy decision, no Dedekind-cut construction, no open-cover compactness, no Lean verification, and no bridge checking.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
A $\RealUp$ seal read can only inspect the point-name coordinate $R$ and the downstream route displayed by the carrier. The packet contains no second real-name surface and no host equality coordinate.

A generated-open seal read must pass through $A,B,S,T$. The comparison row supplies finite order probes, the basis row supplies intervals or balls, the observable truth row supplies Sierpinski membership reads, and the topology row records generated-open formation.

Transport and replay are then supplied by $H$ and $C$, with provenance and local naming supplied by $P$ and $N$. These rows preserve the displayed route and cannot add the excluded completeness, trichotomy, Dedekind-cut, compactness, Lean, or bridge data.
\end{proof}
