\begin{theorem}[Sobolev trace-boundary obligation]
\label{thm:sobolev-trace-boundary-obligation}
Every trace-facing, PDE-facing, variational, or boundary-value consumer of an accepted $\SobolevUp$ packet must first read the displayed $\RealUp$ domain row, metric or complete-metric base row, norm or Hilbert codomain row, $\IntegralUp$ magnitude ledger, $\DerivativeUp$ weak-gradient row, $\BHist$ carrier rows, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ row. The packet does not supply a trace theorem, boundary restriction operator, quotient function equality, density theorem, compact embedding, selected representative, or completed Sobolev space.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:sobolev-bhist-carrier}. A boundary-facing consumer sees only the rows $D,M,V,L,G,H,C,P,N$, with $D$ carrying the Real domain, $M$ carrying the metric or complete-metric base, $V$ carrying the norm or Hilbert codomain, and $L,G$ carrying the integral and weak-gradient ledgers.

Apply the weak-derivative ledger, completion-boundary, classifier-locality, and integral-derivative handoff theorems. These results order every derivative-facing read through $D,M,V,L,G$ and keep sameness, transport, and replay local to the displayed carrier rows.

Finally repack the route through $H,C,P,N$. Those rows preserve, replay, source, and name the same Sobolev packet; they do not add a trace theorem, boundary restriction operator, quotient function equality, density theorem, compact embedding, selected representative, or completed Sobolev space. Coordinate exhaustion gives the stated boundary obligation.
\end{proof}
