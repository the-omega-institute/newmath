\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\SubjectIndexRouteUp$}

The $\SubjectIndexRouteUp$ packet realizes the subject-index route of \autoref{thm:philosophy-registry-synthesis} as a finite NameCert carrier. It sits beside the core registry index of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-core-registry-index-namecert}, the concept registry surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-concept-registry-surface-namecert}, the registry export surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-registry-export-surface-namecert}, the registry export consistency gate of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-registry-export-consistency-gate-namecert}, and the tradition/science bridge of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-tradition-science-registry-bridge-namecert}. Those siblings expose registry tables, concept entries, export rows, consistency gates, and tradition/science rows; this packet stores the route by which ordinary subject vocabulary reaches that lattice.

\begin{definition}[SubjectIndexRoute carrier]
\label{def:subject-index-route-carrier}
A $\SubjectIndexRouteUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(O,C,R,E,G,T,H,K,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $O$ is the ordinary subject-entry row, recording the public subject vocabulary, local reader entry point, and displayed registry destination.
\item $C$ is the core-registry route row, recording the $\CoreRegistryIndexUp$ table position reached by the ordinary entry.
\item $R$ is the concept-registry route row, recording the $\ConceptRegistrySurfaceUp$ concept entry when the subject term names a concept-facing route.
\item $E$ is the registry-export route row, recording the $\RegistryExportSurfaceUp$ export surface through which a public subject entry may leave the registry lattice.
\item $G$ is the consistency-gate route row, recording the $\RegistryExportConsistencyGateUp$ check that keeps registry entry, export grade, closure status, and formal target aligned.
\item $T$ is the tradition/science bridge row, recording the $\TraditionScienceRegistryBridgeUp$ route when the subject term points into inherited comparison or science-roadmap vocabulary.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for ordinary subject entries, core registry rows, concept rows, export rows, consistency-gate rows, tradition/science rows, replay, provenance, and local naming.
\item $K$ stores displayed $\Cont$ replay through ordinary subject lookup, core registry indexing, concept registry lookup, registry export, consistency-gate inspection, and tradition/science bridge handoff.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\SubjectIndexRouteUp$, $\CoreRegistryIndexUp$, $\ConceptRegistrySurfaceUp$, $\RegistryExportSurfaceUp$, $\RegistryExportConsistencyGateUp$, $\TraditionScienceRegistryBridgeUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\SubjectIndexRouteUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a public subject entry without a displayed registry row, for a hidden ordinary-language index, for a route outside the registry lattice, or for prose synthesis that bypasses $S=(O,C,R,E,G,T,H,K,P,N)$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[SubjectIndexRoute NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:subject-index-route-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:subject-index-route-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\SubjectIndexRouteUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $S=(O,C,R,E,G,T,H,K,P,N)$; ordinary subject-entry exposure through $O$; core registry routing through $C$; concept registry routing through $R$; registry export routing through $E$; consistency-gate routing through $G$; tradition/science bridge routing through $T$; componentwise transport through $H$; displayed continuation replay through $K$; package provenance through $P$; and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SubjectIndexRouteUp.SubjectIndexRouteUp\_single\_carrier\_alignment}
\begin{proof}
Project the finite packet of \autoref{def:subject-index-route-carrier}. The ordinary subject row $O$ gives the reader-facing entry point, while $C,R,E,G,T$ display the registry lattice positions reached by that entry.

The sibling chapters supply the table, concept, export, consistency, and tradition/science surfaces. $\SubjectIndexRouteUp$ does not copy any one sibling alone; it binds the route from ordinary vocabulary into the same finite registry lattice.

Finally $H,K,P,N$ transport, replay, package, and name the same packet. Since the carrier contains no hidden subject index or prose-only route, the listed rows exhaust the local $\NameCert_{\SubjectIndexRouteUp}$ surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SubjectIndexRoute registry sibling handoff]
\label{thm:subject-index-route-registry-sibling-handoff}
Every accepted $\SubjectIndexRouteUp$ packet hands an ordinary subject entry to at least one displayed sibling row among \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-core-registry-index-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-concept-registry-surface-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-registry-export-surface-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-registry-export-consistency-gate-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-tradition-science-registry-bridge-namecert}. A consumer cannot replace that handoff by ordinary prose, host semantics, hidden registry state, or local naming alone.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the obligations of \autoref{thm:subject-index-route-namecert-obligations}. An accepted packet must display the ordinary subject-entry row $O$ together with the sibling route rows $C,R,E,G,T$ and the support rows $H,K,P,N$.

If none of $C,R,E,G,T$ is displayed, then $O$ has no registry destination and the packet is not an accepted subject-index route. If a consumer keeps only local naming, the same destination data is still absent.

Thus each accepted subject entry reaches the registry lattice through a displayed sibling row. The route may pass through the core index, concept surface, export surface, consistency gate, or tradition/science bridge, but it cannot bypass the displayed handoff.
\end{proof}
