\begin{theorem}[DyadicMax NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:dyadic-max-namecert-obligations}

\begin{enumerate}

\item classifier stability under componentwise $\hsame$ transport and displayed $\Cont$ replay of $x,y,n,m,L,U$;
\item selected endpoint determinacy, stating that $m$ is one of the two displayed $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint rows at precision $n$;
\item upper-bound ledger exactness, requiring the two endpoint-bound reads to be precisely the rows stored in $U$ and justified by $L$;
\item $\RealUp$-facing non-escape, requiring every real or completion consumer to read the maximum through the displayed dyadic, rounding, floor, absolute-value, transport, continuation, package, and local naming rows.
\end{enumerate}
No further obligation can be read from the carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier exposure is projection from \autoref{def:dyadic-max-carrier}. The two dyadic endpoint rows, shared precision row, selected endpoint, comparison ledger, upper-bound ledger, transport, replay, provenance, and local name are explicit coordinates of $M$.

Classifier stability is componentwise. The rows $H$ and $C$ transport and replay exactly the displayed endpoint, precision, selection, comparison, and bound data, so the selected endpoint cannot detach from the grid on which it was certified.

Determinacy and upper-bound exactness are finite ledger reads. The selected row $m$ is named as one of the two endpoint rows, while $L$ records the inspected comparison and $U$ records both upper-bound checks at the same precision.

Finally inspect the carrier inventory. Since no coordinate stores arbitrary real trichotomy, quotient equality, a host maximum, completeness, or a supremum argument, every $\RealUp$ or completion-facing consumer receives only the displayed finite route.
\end{proof}
