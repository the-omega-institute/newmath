\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RealSequentialCompletenessUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-real-sequential-completeness-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges constructive analysis and Kelley-style sequential completeness, the $\RealSequentialCompletenessUp$ packet records the real-line sequential-completeness face as finite NameCert data. It reads a regular Cauchy sequence of real names through the $\RegSeqRatUp$ and $\StreamNameUp$ surfaces of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, carries the real Cauchy modulus of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-realcauchymodulus-namecert}, and exposes the displayed limit only through the $\RealUp$ surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The chapter is a real-facing sibling of the metric sequence bridge in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-sequentiallycompletemetric-namecert}; it does not claim quotient completion, countable choice, open-cover compactness, or a classical ambient completeness principle.

\begin{definition}[RealSequentialCompleteness carrier]
\label{def:real-sequential-completeness-carrier}
A $\RealSequentialCompletenessUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(R,S,M,L,D,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $R$ is the $\RealUp$ source row naming the real surface on which the sequence and limit are displayed.
\item $S$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ sequence data read through $\StreamNameUp$ finite windows for the real names under consideration.
\item $M$ is the displayed real Cauchy modulus row, assigning each requested real-facing tolerance a late window for the sequence.
\item $L$ is a displayed $\RealUp$ limit row, available to consumers only as a named real surface rather than as a host real or selected quotient representative.
\item $D$ is the late-distance ledger; for every tolerance request admitted by $M$, it records the sufficiently late $\RealUp$ distance bounds from the rows of $S$ to $L$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $R,S,M,L,D$, the replay row, the provenance row, and the local naming row.
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ replay from real-source admission to stream windows, regular rational readbacks, modulus thresholds, late-distance bounds, and the real-limit handoff.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RealSequentialCompletenessUp$, $\RealUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RealCauchyModulusUp$, $\SequentiallyCompleteMetricUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RealSequentialCompletenessUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for quotient completion equality, countable choice, open-cover compactness, a selected host limit, or a classical completeness theorem for the ambient real line.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[RealSequentialCompleteness NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:real-sequential-completeness-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\RealSequentialCompletenessUp$ carrier $Q=(R,S,M,L,D,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\RealSequentialCompletenessUp}$ surface consists exactly of five obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed $\RealUp$ source, regular Cauchy real-name sequence, real Cauchy modulus, displayed limit, late-distance ledger, componentwise transport, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item classifier stability, requiring any accepted comparison of carriers to transport $R,S,M,L,D$ componentwise by $H$ before the local classifier identifies the two packets;
\item regular-Cauchy convergence, requiring each tolerance request to read a late threshold from $M$ and then read the corresponding late-distance rows from $D$ through the $\StreamNameUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$ sequence surface;
\item real-limit handoff, requiring the same finite rows $R,S,M,L,D,H,C,P,N$ to present $L$ as the real-facing limit surface for the displayed sequence;
\item ledger non-escape, stating that no quotient completion, countable-choice schedule, open-cover compactness principle, selected host limit, or ambient completeness proof is exported by the packet.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:real-sequential-completeness-carrier}. The packet exposes exactly the real source, sequence row, modulus, limit row, late-distance ledger, transport, replay, provenance, and local name listed there.

Classifier stability is componentwise. A comparison of two accepted packets must first transport the real source, stream windows, regular rational readbacks, modulus thresholds, displayed limit rows, and late-distance ledger by $H$; the local classifier then reads only the transported rows.

Regular-Cauchy convergence is the joint content of $S,M,D$. The sequence data is observed through $\StreamNameUp$ windows and $\RegSeqRatUp$ readbacks, the modulus row supplies the late threshold for each tolerance, and the ledger row supplies the corresponding real-facing distance bounds to $L$.

The real-limit handoff uses no second limit object. The replay row $C$ presents the same rows $R,S,M,L,D$ to the real-facing consumer, while $P$ and $N$ record that the route is sourced and named by this finite packet.

The non-escape clause follows because the displayed rows are exhausted by $R,S,M,L,D,H,C,P,N$. None stores quotient completion equality, a countable-choice schedule, open-cover compactness, a selected host limit, or an ambient completeness proof.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RealSequentialCompleteness regular-Cauchy handoff]
\label{thm:real-sequential-completeness-regular-cauchy-handoff}
Let $Q=(R,S,M,L,D,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\RealSequentialCompletenessUp$ packet. If the real source $R$, the sequence data $S$, the modulus $M$, the displayed limit $L$, and the late-distance ledger $D$ are displayed, then the same finite packet supplies the real-facing limit surface for the regular Cauchy sequence.

The handoff route is
$$
R\longmapsto S\longmapsto M\longmapsto L\longmapsto D\longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
It exports sequential completeness only as the displayed $\RealUp$ row $L$ reached from regular Cauchy real-name data, not as quotient completion, countable choice, open-cover compactness, selected host limits, or a classical ambient completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the accepted carrier and project $R$. The real-facing classifier and observation surface used by the handoff are read from this displayed $\RealUp$ row, so the limit claim remains internal to the named real surface.

Next read the sequence and modulus rows. The row $S$ supplies the $\StreamNameUp$ windows and $\RegSeqRatUp$ readbacks for the regular Cauchy real-name sequence, while $M$ supplies the late threshold requested by each tolerance.

Now read $L$ and $D$. The row $L$ is already a displayed $\RealUp$ limit row, and $D$ records the late-distance bounds that connect sufficiently late sequence windows to $L$ under the thresholds supplied by $M$.

Finally replay the route through $C$ and attach $H,P,N$. These rows transport, source, and name exactly the data required for the real-facing limit surface, and they introduce no coordinate for quotient completion, countable choice, open-cover compactness, selected host limits, or ambient completeness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RealSequentialCompleteness CauchyCriterion handoff]
\label{thm:real-sequential-completeness-cauchycriterion-handoff}
Let $K$ be an accepted $\RealCauchyCriterionUp$ packet and let $Q=(R,S,M,L,D,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\RealSequentialCompletenessUp$ packet. Suppose the two packets expose the same displayed $\RealUp$ source, the same $\StreamNameUp$ finite windows, the same $\RegSeqRatUp$ readbacks, the same $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance and Cauchy ledgers, and the same terminal $\RealUp$ seal for the displayed limit row $L$.

Then the Cauchy-criterion certificate of $K$ determines one finite BEDC route to the displayed sequential-completeness limit in $Q$:
$$
K\longmapsto R,S\longmapsto \StreamNameUp,\RegSeqRatUp\longmapsto \DyadicRatCoreUp\longmapsto L,D,H,C,P,N .
$$
The route consumes the Cauchy-criterion certificate only through the matching finite rows and exports no quotient streams, selected host limits, choice principle, trichotomy, open-cover compactness, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First read the Cauchy-criterion certificate. By \autoref{thm:real-cauchy-criterion-namecert-obligations}, the accepted $\RealCauchyCriterionUp$ packet exposes exactly the carrier admission, window-readback compatibility, dyadic exactness, transport and replay stability, and provenance exhaustion needed for its finite certificate surface.

Next follow the readback and seal rows. Apply \autoref{thm:real-cauchy-criterion-regular-readback-route} to obtain the $\StreamNameUp$ windows, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readbacks, and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance and Cauchy ledgers from the same certificate. Then use \autoref{thm:real-cauchy-criterion-seal-factorization} to factor the terminal real seal through those rows rather than through an independent completion object.

Now compare the resulting rows with the sequential-completeness packet. The hypotheses identify the real source, stream windows, regular rational readbacks, dyadic ledgers, and terminal real seal of $K$ with the corresponding rows of $Q$, so \autoref{thm:real-sequential-completeness-namecert-obligations} supplies the accepted $\RealSequentialCompletenessUp$ admission, classifier stability, regular-Cauchy convergence, real-limit handoff, and ledger non-escape clauses for the same finite data.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:real-sequential-completeness-regular-cauchy-handoff}. It reads the displayed limit row $L$ and the late-distance ledger $D$ from $Q$ and replays them through $H,C,P,N$, giving the advertised finite route from the Cauchy-criterion certificate to the displayed sequential-completeness limit.

The non-export claim is inherited from both packets' exhaustion clauses. Neither cited certificate adds quotient streams, selected host limits, choice, trichotomy, open-cover compactness, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness to the displayed BEDC route.
\end{proof}
