\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\BousfieldLocalizationUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-bousfieldlocalization-namecert}

The $\BousfieldLocalizationUp$ interface names the finite certificate surface for a displayed left Bousfield-localization packet over a model-category row. In Hirschhorn 2003 and in Goerss--Jardine 1999, local objects and inverted maps are controlled by a model-category background, a selected class of morphisms, and fibrant replacement tests; the BEDC packet below records only the displayed rows that a downstream consumer may inspect.

The dependency boundary is $\ModelCatUp$, $\CategoryUp$, and $\FunctorUp$. The model-category row supplies weak-equivalence, cofibration, fibration, and fibrant-object faces; the category row types objects and arrows; and the functor row records classifier transport and readback routes without exporting a homotopy-category quotient.

\section{Finite localizing packet}
\label{sec:bousfieldlocalization-finite-localizing-packet}

\begin{definition}[BousfieldLocalization carrier]
\label{def:bousfieldlocalization-carrier}
A $\BousfieldLocalizationUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
B=(M,S,L,T,P,N)
$$
whose visible rows have the following shape:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is a displayed $\ModelCatUp$ row, typed through the displayed $\CategoryUp$ object and morphism rows;
\item $S$ is a finite selected morphism-class row whose arrows are the maps to be read as inverted by the localizing certificate;
\item $L$ is a finite list of fibrant-local-object rows, each recording a displayed fibrant object together with the mapping-test rows by which $S$ reads locally;
\item $T$ is the classifier-transport ledger, consisting of the componentwise $\hsame$ witnesses and $\Cont$ routes that carry the model-category, selected-map, and local-object rows;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance row naming the $\ModelCatUp$, $\CategoryUp$, and $\FunctorUp$ dependency certificates used by the packet;
\item $N$ is the $\NameCert$ ledger for the carrier, classifier, stability, exactness, and consumer-readback obligations.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate is $\mathsf{BousfieldLocalizationPacket}(M,S,L,T,P,N)$. Two accepted packets classify together only when the model-category row, selected morphism-class row, fibrant-local-object rows, classifier-transport ledger, package provenance, and $\NameCert$ ledger transport by the displayed $\hsame$ witnesses along the same $\Cont$ routes named in $T$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[BousfieldLocalization NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:bousfieldlocalization-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\BousfieldLocalizationUp}$ seed surface consists of the following five finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier row: the $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet of \autoref{def:bousfieldlocalization-carrier}, including the model-category row, selected morphism-class row, fibrant-local-object rows, classifier-transport ledger, $\Pkg$ provenance, and $\NameCert$ ledger;
\item classifier row: componentwise $\hsame$ on exactly those displayed coordinates, with no comparison of hidden model structures, external homotopy categories, or unrecorded mapping-space objects;
\item stability row: whenever the displayed coordinates are transported along the $\hsame$ witnesses and $\Cont$ routes named by $T$, the transported packet is again an accepted $\BousfieldLocalizationUp$ carrier;
\item exactness row: every local-object test, inverted-map read, and fibrant-row readback obtained by a consumer must be one of the rows recorded in $M$, $S$, $L$, $T$, $P$, and $N$ or a finite readback through their named $\Pkg$ provenance;
\item ledger row: the $\NameCert$ ledger records the carrier, classifier, stability, exactness, and dependency-boundary checks without storing a global localization functor, a quotient homotopy category, or a choice of representatives.
\end{enumerate}
These obligations define only the BEDC-kernel naming surface for a displayed Bousfield-localization packet; they do not assert the existence theorem for a localization model structure.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.BousfieldLocalizationUp.BousfieldLocalizationFiniteLocalizingPacket\_namecert\_obligations}
\begin{proof}
Read the carrier from \autoref{def:bousfieldlocalization-carrier}. Its six coordinates list exactly the finite rows a Bousfield-localization consumer can inspect: a model-category row, a selected class of maps, fibrant-local-object rows, classifier transport, package provenance, and the local certificate ledger.

The classifier and stability obligations are componentwise. Transporting an accepted packet along the displayed $\hsame$ witnesses replaces representatives only inside the visible rows, and the $\Cont$ routes in $T$ keep the reads inside the named $\ModelCatUp$, $\CategoryUp$, and $\FunctorUp$ boundaries.

Exactness is then a projection statement. A consumer obtains a local-object test, an inverted-map read, or a fibrant-row readback only from the recorded rows or from the finite provenance named by $P$ and $N$.

No coordinate stores a localization functor, a host quotient category, a derived mapping object, or a representative choice. Those classical outputs therefore remain outside the seed surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BousfieldLocalization local-object handoff]
\label{thm:bousfieldlocalization-local-object-handoff}
Every downstream homotopy consumer of an accepted $\BousfieldLocalizationUp$ packet may read only the certified fibrant-local-object rows in $L$, the selected maps in $S$ as inverted-map rows through the displayed model-category ledger $M$, and the finite $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ provenance recorded in $T$, $P$, and $N$.

The handoff exports no unlicensed quotient localization, no homotopy-category object outside the displayed rows, no global functorial localization, and no choice of representatives for local equivalence classes.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet $B=(M,S,L,T,P,N)$. The local-object data are exactly the finite rows in $L$, and the inverted-map data are exactly the selected arrows in $S$ interpreted through the displayed model-category ledger $M$.

Transport and readback use only the visible continuation ledger and provenance rows. The $\hsame$ witnesses and $\Cont$ routes in $T$ carry the selected-map and local-object rows componentwise, while $P$ and $N$ name the dependency certificates and the finite readback surface.

It remains to check that nothing else is exported. Since the tuple contains no quotient-localization coordinate, no homotopy-category object coordinate, no global localization functor, and no representative-choice row, the consumer boundary is exhausted by the certified local-object and inverted-map rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BousfieldLocalization empty selected-map boundary]
\label{thm:bousfieldlocalization-empty-selected-map-boundary}
Let $B=(M,S,L,T,P,N)$ be an accepted $\BousfieldLocalizationUp$ carrier in the sense of \autoref{def:bousfieldlocalization-carrier}. If the selected morphism-class row $S$ is empty, then no downstream consumer has an inverted-map row to read from $B$. The remaining certified handoff surface is confined to the displayed model-category row $M$, the fibrant-local-object rows $L$, the classifier-transport ledger $T$, the $\Pkg$ provenance row $P$, and the $\NameCert$ ledger $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
By \autoref{def:bousfieldlocalization-carrier}, the only coordinate of $B$ whose entries are maps read as inverted by the localizing certificate is the selected morphism-class row $S$. The local-object rows in $L$ record fibrant objects and their mapping-test rows, while $M$, $T$, $P$, and $N$ record the model-category ledger, transport routes, package provenance, and certificate ledger. None of those five coordinates is a second selected morphism-class row.

Now assume that $S$ is empty. If a downstream consumer had an inverted-map row to read, then \autoref{thm:bousfieldlocalization-local-object-handoff} would force that read to be one of the selected maps in $S$, interpreted through the displayed model-category ledger $M$. Since $S$ has no displayed arrow entry, there is no such selected map. Hence every inverted-map consumer read is absent.

It remains only to identify what the consumer may still inspect. The exactness row in \autoref{thm:bousfieldlocalization-namecert-obligations} allows consumer reads only from the recorded rows of the packet or from their finite named provenance. After the empty row $S$ contributes no inverted-map read, the visible nonempty handoff coordinates are exactly the possible reads from $M$, $L$, $T$, $P$, and $N$. This also preserves the exclusions in \autoref{thm:bousfieldlocalization-local-object-handoff}: no quotient localization, homotopy-category object outside the displayed rows, global functorial localization, or representative-choice row is exported.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.BousfieldLocalizationUp.BousfieldLocalizationEmptySelectedMap\_boundary}

\begin{theorem}[BousfieldLocalization two-stage local-object readback]
\label{thm:bousfieldlocalization-two-stage-local-object-readback}
Let $B=(M,S,L,T,P,N)$ be an accepted $\BousfieldLocalizationUp$ carrier. Every
downstream local-object test read obtained after choosing a selected map row in
$S$ and a fibrant-local-object row in $L$ factors first through the displayed
model-category row $M$ and then through the transport and provenance rows
$T$, $P$, and $N$.

The readback exports no homotopy-category quotient, localization functor,
derived mapping object, local-equivalence representative choice, or row outside
the finite packet $B$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project $B$ through \autoref{def:bousfieldlocalization-carrier}. The selected
map rows live only in $S$, the fibrant-local-object rows live only in $L$, and
both are typed against the displayed model-category coordinate $M$.

Apply the exactness row in
\autoref{thm:bousfieldlocalization-namecert-obligations}. A consumer read
after selecting one row from $S$ and one row from $L$ must be a read from
$M,S,L,T,P,N$ or from their finite named provenance.

Use \autoref{thm:bousfieldlocalization-local-object-handoff} to order that
readback. The selected map is interpreted through $M$, the fibrant-local-object
test is read through $L$, and the allowed transport and naming data are exactly
the $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and $\NameCert$ rows recorded in
$T$, $P$, and $N$.

In the degenerate selected-map case,
\autoref{thm:bousfieldlocalization-empty-selected-map-boundary} removes the
first-stage selected-map read. The remaining local-object reads still pass
through $M$, $L$, $T$, $P$, and $N$, so no quotient, global localization
functor, derived mapping object, or representative-choice row is exported.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BousfieldLocalization scoped kernel boundary]
\label{thm:bousfieldlocalization-scoped-kernel-boundary}
For any accepted $\BousfieldLocalizationUp$ packet in the sense of \autoref{def:bousfieldlocalization-carrier}, every selected morphism-class row, fibrant-local-object row, classifier transport row, exact readback row, ledger-boundary row, and local-object handoff row factors through the displayed $\ModelCatUp$, $\CategoryUp$, $\FunctorUp$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and $\NameCert$ rows listed in \autoref{thm:bousfieldlocalization-namecert-obligations}.

The scoped boundary exports no localized model structure, no global functorial localization, no homotopy-category quotient, no derived mapping object, and no representative choice.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet $B=(M,S,L,T,P,N)$ through \autoref{def:bousfieldlocalization-carrier}. The selected morphism-class rows are exactly the entries of $S$, the fibrant-local-object rows are exactly the entries of $L$, and their model-category typing passes through the displayed row $M$.

Apply the five clauses of \autoref{thm:bousfieldlocalization-namecert-obligations}. The carrier clause identifies the $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet; the classifier clause restricts comparison to componentwise $\hsame$; the stability clause keeps transported packets inside the same displayed coordinates; the exactness clause confines consumer reads to $M,S,L,T,P,N$ and their finite provenance; and the ledger clause records the certificate boundary without adding an external localization object.

Use \autoref{thm:bousfieldlocalization-local-object-handoff} and \autoref{thm:bousfieldlocalization-two-stage-local-object-readback} for the consumer-facing rows. They route selected maps, fibrant-local-object tests, and two-stage readbacks through $M$, $S$, $L$, $T$, $P$, and $N$, so each displayed handoff row factors through the same $\ModelCatUp$, $\CategoryUp$, $\FunctorUp$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and $\NameCert$ boundary.

The excluded objects would require coordinates not present in the carrier or ledger. There is no localized model-structure coordinate, no global localization-functor coordinate, no homotopy-category quotient coordinate, no derived mapping-object coordinate, and no representative-choice coordinate, so the scoped boundary exports none of them.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BousfieldLocalization public export]
\label{thm:bousfieldlocalization-public-export}
The public $\BousfieldLocalizationUp$ export is exactly the finite packet rows
$(M,S,L,T,P,N)$ of \autoref{def:bousfieldlocalization-carrier}, routed through
the scoped kernel boundary of
\autoref{thm:bousfieldlocalization-scoped-kernel-boundary}. A public consumer
may read selected-map rows, fibrant-local-object rows, classifier transport by
$\hsame$ and $\Cont$, $\Pkg$ provenance, and the $\NameCert$ ledger, and no
other coordinate is part of the exported object.

The export does not include localized model structures, global localization
functors, homotopy-category quotients, derived mapping objects, or representative
choice.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier of \autoref{def:bousfieldlocalization-carrier}. The six
coordinates name the only displayed rows available to the packet: model-category
data, selected maps, fibrant-local-object reads, classifier transport,
provenance, and the certificate ledger.

Apply \autoref{thm:bousfieldlocalization-namecert-obligations} to identify the
carrier, classifier, stability, exactness, and ledger obligations on those same
coordinates. Then use \autoref{thm:bousfieldlocalization-local-object-handoff}
and \autoref{thm:bousfieldlocalization-two-stage-local-object-readback} to route
the selected-map and fibrant-local-object reads through the displayed
$M,S,L,T,P,N$ rows.

Finally invoke \autoref{thm:bousfieldlocalization-scoped-kernel-boundary}. It
packages the finite rows as the scoped consumer boundary, so the public export is
the same boundary viewed as the citable $\BousfieldLocalizationUp$ object. Since
the carrier has no coordinate for a localized model structure, global
localization functor, homotopy-category quotient, derived mapping object, or
representative choice, none of those data can be exported.
\end{proof}

For an accepted finite public packet

B=(M,S,L,T,P,N)
$$

\autoref{thm:bousfieldlocalization-public-export} determines the bridge boundary
for $\BousfieldLocalizationUp$ by routing selected-map rows, fibrant-local-object
rows, classifier transport, provenance, and $\NameCert$ rows through the
displayed $\ModelCatUp$, $\CategoryUp$, $\FunctorUp$, $\mathsf{BHist}$,
$\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and $\NameCert$ surfaces named by the packet.

The bridge boundary exports no localized model structure, global localization
functor, homotopy-category quotient, derived mapping object, or representative

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier $B=(M,S,L,T,P,N)$. The six coordinates give the model

ledger, package provenance, and certificate ledger used by the public packet.

Invoke \autoref{thm:bousfieldlocalization-namecert-obligations} to identify the
carrier, classifier, stability, exactness, and ledger clauses. The selected-map
and fibrant-local-object rows are therefore read only as displayed
$\mathsf{BHist}$ data typed through the $\ModelCatUp$, $\CategoryUp$, and
$\FunctorUp$ dependency surfaces.

Use \autoref{thm:bousfieldlocalization-scoped-kernel-boundary} to pass from the

\autoref{thm:bousfieldlocalization-local-object-handoff} together with
\autoref{thm:bousfieldlocalization-two-stage-local-object-readback} for the

$\Cont$, and route provenance through $\mathsf{Pkg}$ and $\NameCert$.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:bousfieldlocalization-public-export}. The public
object is the same finite packet viewed as a consumer-facing boundary. Since the

\begin{theorem}[BousfieldLocalization displayed packet bridge exactness]
\label{thm:bousfieldlocalization-displayed-packet-bridge-exactness}
For an accepted $\BousfieldLocalizationUp$ packet
$$
B=(M,S,L,T,P,N),
$$
the displayed bridge boundary is exact exactly when every consumer read factors
through the selected-map row $S$, fibrant-local-object row $L$,
model-category row $M$, classifier-transport row $T$, package row $P$, and
$\NameCert$ row $N$. Equivalently, the bridge exports precisely those six
packet rows as the finite localizing boundary and exports no localized model
structure, global localization functor, homotopy-category quotient, derived
mapping object, or representative choice.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For exactness to consumer factorization, apply
\autoref{thm:bousfieldlocalization-namecert-obligations}. It identifies the
accepted packet carrier, componentwise classifier, stability rows, exact
readback rows, and ledger coordinates over $M$, $S$, $L$, $T$, $P$, and $N$.

Use \autoref{thm:bousfieldlocalization-local-object-handoff} and
\autoref{thm:bousfieldlocalization-two-stage-local-object-readback} to route
the local-object reads through the selected-map row, fibrant-local-object row,
and classifier-transport ledger. The readback route therefore factors through
the displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and
$\NameCert$ surfaces named by the packet.

For consumer factorization to exactness, apply
\autoref{thm:bousfieldlocalization-scoped-kernel-boundary} and
\autoref{thm:bousfieldlocalization-public-export}. A consumer read that factors
through exactly the six displayed rows is the same scoped finite packet viewed
as the public $\BousfieldLocalizationUp$ boundary.

Finally invoke \autoref{thm:bousfieldlocalization-bridge-boundary}. Its bridge
boundary is precisely the finite packet over $M$, $S$, $L$, $T$, $P$, and $N$.
Since none of those rows contains a localized model structure, global
localization functor, homotopy-category quotient, derived mapping object, or
representative choice, the displayed bridge packet is exact with exactly the
advertised exclusions.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BousfieldLocalization mature finite localizing-packet consumer catalogue]
\label{thm:bousfieldlocalization-mature-consumer-catalogue}
For every accepted $\BousfieldLocalizationUp$ packet
$$
B=(M,S,L,T,P,N),
$$
every mature consumer read is one of the following five displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item selected-map rows from $S$ typed through the model-category row $M$;
\item fibrant-local-object test rows from $L$;
\item two-stage local-object readbacks through the classifier-transport ledger $T$;
\item dependency provenance through the package row $P$;
\item certificate and finite-ledger rows through $N$.
\end{enumerate}
The catalogue exports no localized model structure, global localization
functor, homotopy-category quotient, derived mapping object, or representative
choice.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier through
\autoref{def:bousfieldlocalization-carrier}. The coordinates $M$, $S$, $L$,
$T$, $P$, and $N$ are the only rows a consumer can inspect.

Use \autoref{thm:bousfieldlocalization-local-object-handoff} for the selected
maps and fibrant-local-object tests, and use
\autoref{thm:bousfieldlocalization-two-stage-local-object-readback} for the
two-stage readbacks through $T$. These theorems place all localizing reads in
the displayed $M$, $S$, $L$, and $T$ rows.

Next apply \autoref{thm:bousfieldlocalization-scoped-kernel-boundary} and
\autoref{thm:bousfieldlocalization-public-export}. They identify the remaining
dependency and certificate reads with the displayed $\Pkg$ row $P$ and
$\NameCert$ ledger $N$.

Finally invoke \autoref{thm:bousfieldlocalization-bridge-boundary}. The bridge
boundary is the same finite packet viewed as a consumer surface, so every
mature read is exhausted by the five listed rows. Since the carrier has no
coordinate for a localized model structure, global localization functor,
homotopy-category quotient, derived mapping object, or representative choice,
none of those objects is exported.
\end{proof}
