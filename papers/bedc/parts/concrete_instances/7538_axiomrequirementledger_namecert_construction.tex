The $\AxiomRequirementLedgerUp$ packet realizes the Required-Axiom-Set coordinate of \autoref{ch:bedc-axiom-dependency-map} as a finite consumer-facing ledger. It sits beside the dependency-map packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-axiomdependencymap-namecert}, the audit boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-auditgateboundary-namecert}, the forbidden-host ancestry boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-forbidden-axiom-ancestry-namecert}, the apophatic refusal surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-apophaticname-namecert}, and the common audit-map interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-auditmapinterface-namecert}. Those siblings expose mode rows, audit refusals, forbidden dependency ancestry, named externality refusal, and cross-map reads; this chapter packages the finite row by which a required axiom supply is carried without being confused with a zero-axiom witness.

\begin{definition}[AxiomRequirementLedger carrier]
\label{def:axiom-requirement-ledger-carrier}
An $\AxiomRequirementLedgerUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(\Phi,M,W,A,R,T,U,V,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $\Phi$ is the claim row whose dependency tuple is being read;
\item $M$ is the Mode A, Mode B, or Mode C row inherited from the dependency-map entry;
\item $W$ is the witness row, either a zero-axiom proof, a restricted proof, a counterexample, or a named socket;
\item $A$ is the finite required-axiom-set row displayed for the entry;
\item $R$ is the refusal ledger that prevents a nonempty $A$ row from being read as substrate-internal proof content;
\item $T$ is the transport row connecting the required-supply row to audit-map consumers;
\item $U$ is the route row from the dependency tuple to the audit boundary and forbidden-ancestry checks;
\item $V$ is the provenance row for apophatic names, audit-map interface reads, and dependency-map siblings;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the claim, mode, witness, required-supply, refusal, transport, route, provenance, and local name;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ routes by which consumers inspect $\Phi,M,W,A,R,T,U,V,H$;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\AxiomDependencyMapUp$, $\AuditGateBoundaryUp$, $\ForbiddenAxiomAncestryUp$, $\ApophaticNameUp$, $\AuditMapInterfaceUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\AxiomRequirementLedgerUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate that turns a nonempty required-axiom row into a Mode A proof, imports a refused host axiom as BEDC theorem content, hides a selected witness, or bypasses the displayed audit route.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[AxiomRequirementLedger NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:axiom-requirement-ledger-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:axiom-requirement-ledger-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\AxiomRequirementLedgerUp}$ surface consists exactly of claim exposure through $\Phi$, mode exposure through $M$, witness exposure through $W$, required-supply exposure through $A$, refusal preservation through $R$, transport through $T,H$, route replay through $U,C$, provenance through $V,P$, and local naming through $N$. A downstream audit-map consumer can read a nonempty required-axiom-set row only as required supply, refusal data, or named socket data, never as an accepted zero-axiom witness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier. The accepted packet exposes the rows $\Phi,M,W,A,R,T,U,V,H,C,P,N$ and no other supply row.

The dependency-map sibling supplies the tuple order: claim, mode, witness, and required-axiom set are read before the ledger is exported. The audit boundary and forbidden-ancestry siblings then read the same required-supply row through $R,T,U,H,C$ as a finite refusal or ancestry boundary.

The apophatic-name and audit-map-interface siblings supply the public consumer shape. They allow a named socket or refusal row to be cited, routed, and transported, but they do not convert it into a generated BEDC witness.

Thus the local NameCert surface is exactly the displayed ledger. Since no carrier coordinate imports the required axiom or manufactures a Mode A proof, a nonempty $A$ row remains required supply rather than substrate-internal proof content.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[AxiomRequirementLedger non-escape]
\label{thm:axiom-requirement-ledger-nonescape}
Every accepted $\AxiomRequirementLedgerUp$ packet separates required axiom supply from zero-axiom witnesses. If the required-supply row $A$ is nonempty, any consumer that reads the packet must factor through
$$
\Phi,M,W,A,R,T,U,V,H,C,P,N.
$$
It cannot export the entry as a Mode A witness unless the witness row $W$ is itself a checked zero-axiom proof row and the required-supply row is empty on the displayed route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the carrier definition. The only rows available to a consumer are the displayed finite rows, and the required-supply row $A$ is separate from the witness row $W$.

Use the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:axiom-requirement-ledger-namecert-obligations}. They require every consumer read of $A$ to pass through the refusal, transport, route, provenance, and local naming rows $R,T,U,V,H,C,P,N$.

Now compare with the dependency-map mode row. A Mode A read is permitted only where the witness row is a checked zero-axiom proof and the required-supply row is empty on the displayed route. When $A$ is nonempty, the accepted packet records supply still required; it does not export a substrate theorem.
\end{proof}
