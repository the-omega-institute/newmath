\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\UpperDarbouxSumUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-upper-darboux-sum-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Darboux and Riemann, the $\UpperDarbouxSumUp$ packet isolates the finite upper-sum side of the Darboux interface before a consumer asks for a full upper/lower gap. It sits between the dyadic step-function surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicstepfunction-namecert}, the tagged finite-sum consumer of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-riemannsum-namecert}, the Darboux integration handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-darbouxintegral-namecert}, and the tagged-mesh integral boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-riemannintegral-namecert}. The packet supplies a finite upper-bound ledger for later Darboux and Riemann criteria without importing host suprema, open-cover compactness, quotient function equality, selected mesh limits, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[Upper Darboux sum carrier]\label{def:upper-darboux-sum-carrier}
An $\UpperDarbouxSumUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
U=(P,B,D,S,I,R,H,C,Q,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $P$ is the partition row, naming the finite ordered cell list, cell-width ledger, endpoint order, and refinement handle used by every later read.
\item $B$ is the cell upper-bound ledger. For each displayed cell it records the chosen upper value row and the comparison proof that every admitted sample value in that cell is below the carried bound.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicStepFunctionUp$ row over the same partition cells, carrying the step value used to display the upper envelope.
\item $S$ is the $\RiemannSumUp$ row computed from the displayed widths of $P$ and the upper values exposed by $B$ and $D$.
\item $I$ is the $\DarbouxIntegralUp$ provenance row, recording that the upper-sum read is available to the Darboux handoff before any lower-sum or gap row is requested.
\item $R$ is the $\RealUp$ comparison row, exposing the finite real-name comparison used by upper-bound consumers.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the partition, upper-bound ledger, dyadic step row, Riemann-sum row, Darboux handoff, comparison row, replay, provenance, and local naming row.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from partition cells to upper-bound reads, from upper-bound reads to the dyadic step row, from the dyadic step row to the Riemann-sum row, and from the sum row to the Darboux handoff.
\item $Q$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\UpperDarbouxSumUp$, $\DyadicStepFunctionUp$, $\RiemannSumUp$, $\DarbouxIntegralUp$, $\RiemannIntegralUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\UpperDarbouxSumUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier reads accepted packets only through $P,B,D,S,I,R,H,C,Q,N$. It has no coordinate for a host supremum, an open-cover compactness theorem, a selected mesh limit, a lower Darboux sum, quotient equality of functions, or ambient real completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Upper Darboux sum NameCert obligations]\label{thm:upper-darboux-sum-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\UpperDarbouxSumUp$ carrier
$$
U=(P,B,D,S,I,R,H,C,Q,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\UpperDarbouxSumUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following five finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed partition row, cell upper-bound ledger, dyadic step row, Riemann-sum row, Darboux handoff, comparison row, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item partition-refinement stability, requiring each transported refinement of $P$ to carry the same cell-width ledger and to route upper-bound reads through the displayed refinement handle;
\item upper-bound ledger transport, requiring $B$ and $D$ to move componentwise by $\hsame$ while preserving the stated upper relation on every displayed cell;
\item Darboux handoff, requiring the row $I$ to expose the upper-sum read to $\DarbouxIntegralUp$ without producing a lower sum, a gap witness, or a host supremum;
\item Riemann-sum consumer boundary, requiring every $\RiemannSumUp$ or $\RiemannIntegralUp$ consumer to read the finite widths, upper values, replay, provenance, and local name before it can use the upper-sum row.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is the projection of \autoref{def:upper-darboux-sum-carrier}. The accepted packet displays exactly the partition, upper-bound, dyadic step, finite sum, Darboux handoff, comparison, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

Partition-refinement stability comes next. The partition row $P$ names the finite cells and widths, so a transported refinement can only expose an upper read through the same displayed cell ledger and refinement handle.

Upper-bound ledger transport is then componentwise. The rows $B$ and $D$ move together under $H$, and replay through $C$ recomputes the dyadic upper envelope before the finite sum row $S$ is available.

The Darboux and Riemann boundaries are exhausted by $S$ and $I$. The Darboux consumer receives only the upper-sum handoff, while a Riemann-facing consumer must read the same finite widths, upper values, replay, provenance, and local name before using the sum.

The excluded analytic objects are absent from the carrier. No row stores a host supremum, open-cover compactness, selected mesh limit, quotient function equality, lower-sum datum, or ambient completeness principle.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Upper-sum consumer boundary]\label{thm:upper-darboux-sum-consumer-boundary}
Every $\DarbouxIntegralUp$, $\RiemannSumUp$, or $\RiemannIntegralUp$ consumer of an accepted $\UpperDarbouxSumUp$ carrier $U=(P,B,D,S,I,R,H,C,Q,N)$ must first read the partition row $P$, the upper-bound ledger $B$, the $\DyadicStepFunctionUp$ row $D$, the finite sum row $S$, the Darboux handoff $I$, and the comparison row $R$, with transport, replay, provenance, and local naming supplied by $H,C,Q,N$. The consumer therefore receives a finite upper-sum route rather than a host supremum, an open-cover compactness proof, a selected limiting mesh, or a quotient function-equality principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The rows upstream of every exported upper-sum read are $P,B,D,S,I,R$ with structural support $H,C,Q,N$.

For a Darboux-facing read, the handoff row $I$ is reachable only after the partition, upper-bound ledger, dyadic step row, and finite sum row have been replayed. For a Riemann-facing read, the row $S$ is already the displayed finite sum built from those same cells and upper values.

Since the packet has no additional analytic coordinate, every listed consumer reads the same finite route and cannot bypass it through a host supremum, compactness theorem, selected mesh limit, quotient equality, or ambient completeness.
\end{proof}
