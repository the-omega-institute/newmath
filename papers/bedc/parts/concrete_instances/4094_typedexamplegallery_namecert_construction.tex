\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\TypedExampleGalleryUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-typedexamplegallery-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\TypedExampleGalleryUp$ packet realizes the audit reading of \autoref{ch:metacic-typedexamples-audit-map} as finite BEDC data. Its source material is the existing MetaCIC typed-example corpus: Church booleans and naturals, pairs and options, lists and sums, identity and polymorphic identity rows, application forms, beta redexes, projections, Pi forms, constants, and sort-indexed context examples. The packet sits after the closed substitution boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closedtermsubstitutionboundary-namecert}, the derivation route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-typecheckingderivationtrace-namecert}, and the proof-erasure route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-prooftermerasuretrace-namecert}. It names the gallery as a finite regression surface without turning the examples into subject reduction, normalisation, confluence, or global conversion theorems.

\begin{definition}[Typed example gallery carrier]
\label{def:typed-example-gallery-carrier}
A $\TypedExampleGalleryUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
G=(E,T,C,B,R,H,K,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $E$ is the finite gallery-entry row for the accepted Church, identity, application, beta-redex, projection, Pi, constant, and sort-context examples;
\item $T$ is the type-checking row recording each displayed $\mathsf{HasType}$ or checker-accepted example;
\item $C$ is the closedness row recording the $\mathsf{ClosedAt}$ inputs consumed by the typed examples and computation rows;
\item $B$ is the beta-computation row for the selected redex, eliminator, boolean, natural-number, list, sum, and equality examples that compute inside the gallery;
\item $R$ is the regression-surface row recording the finite families whose preservation is required when checker, closedness, substitution, beta, projection, or binder code is changed;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport across $E,T,C,B,R,K,P,N$;
\item $K$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which consumers replay gallery entry reads, checker reads, closedness reads, beta reads, and regression-surface reads;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over \autoref{ch:metacic-typedexamples-audit-map}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closedtermsubstitutionboundary-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-typecheckingderivationtrace-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-prooftermerasuretrace-namecert};
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\TypedExampleGalleryUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for an arbitrary MetaCIC term, no global type-safety theorem, no closed-term normalisation theorem, no confluence theorem, no proof-erased compiler theorem, and no host evaluator.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Typed example gallery NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:typed-example-gallery-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:typed-example-gallery-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\TypedExampleGalleryUp}$ surface consists of five finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for $G=(E,T,C,B,R,H,K,P,N)$;
\item checker visibility through the displayed type-checking row $T$ for every accepted gallery entry in $E$;
\item closedness visibility through $C$ for each example whose typing or computation row consumes a $\mathsf{ClosedAt}$ input;
\item beta visibility through $B$ for the selected computation rows and no other conversion surface;
\item regression-surface visibility through $R$, transported only by $H,K,P,N$ and grounded in the sibling provenance listed in $P$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the finite packet $G$. Carrier admission supplies the displayed entry, checker, closedness, beta, regression, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

The checker obligation reads $T$ only against entries already present in $E$. The closedness obligation reads $C$ only for entries whose displayed theorem rows require $\mathsf{ClosedAt}$ inputs. The beta obligation reads $B$ only for the selected computations in the gallery.

Finally read $R,H,K,P,N$. The regression row names the finite families whose checked behavior is preserved as a gallery surface, while transport, replay, provenance, and local naming keep every consumer inside the same displayed packet. No row adds a general subject-reduction, normalisation, confluence, global conversion, proof-erasure, or host-evaluation endpoint.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Typed example gallery regression surface]
\label{thm:typed-example-gallery-regression-surface}
Every consumer of an accepted $\TypedExampleGalleryUp$ packet receives a finite regression surface for the typed-example corpus: accepted typing rows, closedness inputs, selected beta computations, and sibling provenance through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closedtermsubstitutionboundary-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-typecheckingderivationtrace-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-prooftermerasuretrace-namecert}. The packet cannot be reread as a theorem about all represented terms, all closed substitutions, all beta steps, all erased proof terms, or all checker results.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The regression surface is the row $R$ together with the entry, checker, closedness, and beta rows $E,T,C,B$. These rows are finite and are indexed by the gallery families recorded in \autoref{ch:metacic-typedexamples-audit-map}.

The sibling references in $P$ locate the required substrate: closed substitution supplies the closed-term boundary, derivation traces supply finite checker routes, and proof-erasure traces supply the erased-readback discipline. The packet records dependence on those surfaces without merging their theorem rows into the gallery.

Since the carrier contains no row for an arbitrary represented term, arbitrary closed substitution, arbitrary beta step, arbitrary proof erasure, or arbitrary checker result, the consumer cannot export a global metatheorem. The accepted reading is exactly the finite gallery regression surface.
\end{proof}
