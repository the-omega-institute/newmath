\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CauchyStabilityUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-stability-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges on constructive real numbers by regular Cauchy data, the $\CauchyStabilityUp$ packet names the finite stability route used when displayed transport changes a Cauchy presentation but not the Real-facing read. It sits on the StreamName windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance core of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the regular rational sequence route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the Cauchy equivalence boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-equivalence-setoid-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet records stability of displayed Cauchy data; it is not quotient equality, selected representatives, countable choice over tails, host sequence equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[CauchyStability carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-stability-carrier}
A $\CauchyStabilityUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(A,B,M,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ and $B$ are two regular Cauchy source rows whose rational observations are opened only through $\RegSeqRatUp$ and finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows.
\item $M$ is the shared tail-modulus comparison row relating the two source rows at each displayed precision request.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger used to compare the transported source observations.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row recording the regular rational readback after $A,B,M,D$ have been displayed.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row reached through the displayed $\CauchyEquivalenceSetoidUp$ boundary.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the source, modulus, dyadic, regular-readback, equivalence, seal, replay, package, and naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a Cauchy-stability request through $A,B,M,D,R,E,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyStabilityUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\CauchyEquivalenceSetoidUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyStabilityUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for quotient sequence equality, selected limits, hidden representatives, countable-choice tail selection, host real equality, or an ambient completeness theorem.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyStability NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-stability-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CauchyStabilityUp$ carrier $S=(A,B,M,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyStabilityUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the two regular Cauchy source rows, shared tail-modulus comparison, dyadic tolerance exposure, $\RegSeqRatUp$ stability of the transported readback, $\RealUp$ seal nonescape through $\CauchyEquivalenceSetoidUp$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, package provenance, and local naming through the rows $A,B,M,D,R,E,H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-stability-carrier}. The only source data are the rows $A$ and $B$, and the only comparison data between them are the shared tail-modulus row $M$ and dyadic tolerance ledger $D$.

The regular-rational readback $R$ is downstream from those rows, so stability is read as a finite $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff rather than as equality of host sequences. The displayed $\CauchyEquivalenceSetoidUp$ boundary is consumed before the terminal seal $E$ can be read.

Transport and replay use $H$ and $C$ componentwise. The package and naming rows $P,N$ source the same finite route, and the excluded quotient, selected-limit, hidden-representative, choice, host-equality, and completeness data have no carrier coordinate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyStability Real seal handoff]
\label{thm:cauchy-stability-real-seal-handoff}
Every $\RealUp$-facing read of an accepted $\CauchyStabilityUp$ packet factors through
$$
\StreamNameUp \longmapsto \DyadicRatCoreUp \longmapsto \RegSeqRatUp \longmapsto \CauchyEquivalenceSetoidUp \longmapsto \RealUp
$$
before the terminal seal $E$ is consumed. Thus displayed transport may change the finite Cauchy presentation only through the stored $A,B,M,D,R,H,C,P,N$ rows, and the Real-facing boundary receives no quotient equality, selected representative, hidden tail family, countable-choice selector, host real equality, or ambient completeness coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a Real-facing request. The carrier first exposes the finite StreamName windows attached to $A$ and $B$, together with the shared modulus comparison $M$.

The dyadic ledger $D$ translates those windows into displayed tolerance observations. The regular-rational handoff $R$ then repacks the inspected observations as $\RegSeqRatUp$ data.

Only after this readback does the packet enter the displayed $\CauchyEquivalenceSetoidUp$ boundary and then the seal $E$. The rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name that same route, leaving no hidden Real-facing coordinate beyond the listed sibling surfaces.
\end{proof}
