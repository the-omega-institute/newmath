\begin{definition}[CodonTransitionMatrix carrier]

$$

\item $Q$ is the finite-state row listing scan, elongate, terminate, readthrough, repair, and mutate states as displayed classifier entries.
\item $F$ is the stop-face row naming the local $\mathrm{UAA}$, $\mathrm{UAG}$, $\mathrm{UGA}$, and $\mathrm{UGG}$ suffix reads inside the codon-window surface.

$$

\delta(\mathsf{elongate},\mathrm{UAG}) &= \mathsf{terminate},\\

\begin{theorem}[CodonTransitionMatrix NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:codon-transition-matrix-namecert-obligations}

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

The stop-face row $F$ selects the four local suffix reads named by the prototype. The deterministic row $D$ then displays exactly the three terminating rows and the one $\mathrm{UGG}$ elongation row.

No stochastic probability, global table, amino-acid classifier, quotient source equality, or biological inevitability row appears in the carrier. Hence the listed rows exhaust the NameCert obligation surface.
\end{proof}

\label{thm:codon-transition-matrix-stop-boundary-exactness}

$$

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

The stop-face row $F$ narrows the read to the four local suffix rows. The deterministic row $D$ then fixes the three stop transitions and the $\mathrm{UGG}$ elongation transition.

Finally $T$ checks exactness against the displayed matrix. Since $H,C,P,N$ only preserve the same route, changing the $\mathrm{UGG}$ row to terminate changes $D$ and breaks $T$.
\end{proof}
