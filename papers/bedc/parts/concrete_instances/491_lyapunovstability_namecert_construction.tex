\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\LyapunovStabilityUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-lyapunovstability-namecert}

The $\LyapunovStabilityUp$ interface records the finite certificate surface used by standard Lyapunov stability arguments for displayed dynamical systems. The classical reference point is Khalil, \emph{Nonlinear Systems}, together with the LaSalle-style Lyapunov stability tradition; the BEDC chapter names only the finite descent packet and its consumer boundary.

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:lyapunovstability-carrier}

\begin{definition}[LyapunovStability finite BHist carrier]
\label{def:lyapunovstability-bhist-carrier}
A $\LyapunovStabilityUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet over displayed $\DynSystemUp$, $\VecSpaceUp$, $\MatrixUp$, and $\RealUp$ rows. It consists of a state-carrier row $X$, a displayed state-transition row $\Phi$ from $\DynSystemUp$, a base state row $x_0$, a scalar-valued or matrix-valued energy row $V$, nonnegative comparison rows in $\RealUp$, a finite level row $\alpha$, a finite descent ledger along the displayed transition row, $\hsame$ transports between corresponding state and energy rows, $\Cont$ routes for every transition and energy readback, $\Pkg$ provenance naming the dependency certificates, and a $\NameCert_{\LyapunovStabilityUp}$ row for the packet.

The classifier compares two accepted packets only by these displayed rows: state rows through $\VecSpaceUp$, transition rows through $\DynSystemUp$, energy and nonnegative comparison rows through $\MatrixUp$ or $\RealUp$, and the finite descent ledger through the named $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\Pkg$ coordinates. It exports no analytic flow, invariant measure, asymptotic convergence theorem, or ambient normed-space completion.
\end{definition}

\section{Finite stability surface}
\label{sec:lyapunovstability-stability-surface}

\begin{theorem}[LyapunovStability energy descent obligation]
\label{thm:lyapunovstability-energy-descent-obligation}
For an accepted $\LyapunovStabilityUp$ carrier $P$ as in \autoref{def:lyapunovstability-bhist-carrier}, every descent assertion visible to a consumer is one row of the finite ledger. Each such row reads a displayed transition face $x\mapsto \Phi(x)$, the two energy rows $V(x)$ and $V(\Phi(x))$, a $\RealUp$ comparison row saying that the successor energy is no larger than the source energy, and the $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\Pkg$ provenance that transports those rows inside $P$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier to its finite descent ledger. By the carrier definition, each ledger entry is attached to a displayed transition face and to two energy readbacks.

The nonnegative comparison row is read through $\RealUp$, while a matrix-valued energy row first passes through the displayed $\MatrixUp$ or scalar readback route named in the packet. The same entry also carries the $\Cont$ route explaining how the successor state and the successor energy are obtained.

No other descent source appears in the carrier. Therefore every visible descent assertion is one of the finite rows listed in the theorem.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LyapunovStability level-set containment]
\label{thm:lyapunovstability-level-set-containment}
Let $P$ be an accepted $\LyapunovStabilityUp$ carrier with finite level row $\alpha$. If a displayed state row $x$ satisfies the finite comparison row $V(x)\leq \alpha$ and the transition face from $x$ is recorded in the descent ledger of $P$, then the successor state row $\Phi(x)$ has a displayed comparison row $V(\Phi(x))\leq \alpha$ assembled from the same $\RealUp$ nonnegative comparison rows, energy ledger, $\hsame$ transports, and $\Cont$ provenance.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.LyapunovStabilityUp.LyapunovStabilityCarrier\_level\_set\_containment}
\begin{proof}
Read the finite descent row for the transition face from $x$ to $\Phi(x)$. It supplies the comparison from the successor energy to the source energy.

Compose that comparison with the displayed level comparison for $x$. The composition is a finite $\RealUp$ comparison route, and the carrier records the required $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ provenance for both energy readbacks.

Repack the composed comparison as the level row for the successor state. The construction uses only rows already present in the accepted packet, so it does not introduce an analytic invariant set or an external flow object.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LyapunovStability NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:lyapunovstability-namecert-obligation-surface}
The local $\NameCert_{\LyapunovStabilityUp}$ obligation surface consists of five rows: the finite carrier of \autoref{def:lyapunovstability-bhist-carrier}; the classifier that transports state, transition, energy, level, comparison, descent, $\Cont$, and $\Pkg$ rows componentwise; the energy descent obligation of \autoref{thm:lyapunovstability-energy-descent-obligation}; the level-set containment row of \autoref{thm:lyapunovstability-level-set-containment}; and the exact consumer boundary over the displayed $\DynSystemUp$, $\VecSpaceUp$, $\MatrixUp$, $\RealUp$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier and classifier. They enumerate the finite packet coordinates and the componentwise transport relation.

Add the descent row and the level-set containment row. Both are projections from the same finite ledger and both use only the displayed transition, energy, comparison, continuation, and provenance rows.

The consumer boundary is then a closure of the displayed surface under projection. Since the packet has no row for an analytic flow, invariant measure, asymptotic convergence theorem, or ambient completion, those objects cannot be exported by the $\NameCert_{\LyapunovStabilityUp}$ surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LyapunovStability consumer boundary]
\label{thm:lyapunovstability-consumer-boundary}
Every downstream consumer of $\LyapunovStabilityUp$, including $\DynSystemUp$, $\LQRUp$, $\RiccatiUp$, and control-stability packets, may read only the displayed state-transition row, state-carrier row, scalar or matrix energy row, $\RealUp$ nonnegative comparison rows, finite descent ledger, $\hsame$ transports, $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and $\NameCert$ row of \autoref{def:lyapunovstability-bhist-carrier}. No consumer can require completeness, an asymptotic convergence theorem, invariant-measure data, or an external analytic flow bridge from this chapter.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Take a consumer-visible readback from an accepted packet. The classifier lets the consumer compare only the displayed carrier coordinates and the finite ledger rows.

Apply the energy descent and level-set containment theorems to identify the stability-facing rows. These rows are exactly the transition, state, energy, comparison, continuation, transport, and package coordinates named in the carrier.

Because none of the excluded objects is a coordinate of the packet, no downstream consumer can project such an object from the certificate. The boundary is therefore the finite BEDC Lyapunov-stability packet itself.
\end{proof}
