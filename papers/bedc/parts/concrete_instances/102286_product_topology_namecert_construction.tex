\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ProductTopologyUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-product-topology-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Kelley and Munkres on product topologies, the $\ProductTopologyUp$ packet records the finite BEDC route by which two displayed topological carriers are paired and read through projection-continuity rows. It sits after the indexed-open surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-topology-namecert}, the binary product carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-prod-namecert}, and the graph-and-modulus interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuousmap-namecert}. The packet names only the finite binary product-topology certificate needed by later product, compact-metric, and continuous-map consumers; it is not a box topology, quotient topology, host powerset construction, selected open family, choice principle, or ambient set-theoretic product.

\begin{definition}[ProductTopology carrier]
\label{def:product-topology-carrier}
A $\ProductTopologyUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(X,Y,O_X,O_Y,\Pi,\pi_X,\pi_Y,B,T,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ and $Y$ are the two component carrier histories whose classifiers are transported only through displayed $\hsame$ rows.
\item $O_X$ and $O_Y$ are the two $\TopologyUp$ indexed-open rows, exposing component open indices, component membership reads, and finite intersection ledgers.
\item $\Pi$ is the $\ProdUp$ pairing row whose first and second projection readbacks expose paired histories only through the two component rows.
\item $\pi_X$ and $\pi_Y$ are the two $\ContinuousMapUp$ projection rows from the product packet to the component topology rows.
\item $B$ is the rectangular subbasis row, recording finite pairs of component-open indices and their paired membership route through $\Pi,O_X,O_Y$.
\item $T$ is the generated $\TopologyUp$ row for the product carrier, admitting exactly the finite generated-open operations displayed by $B$ and no host powerset of pairs.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for component carriers, component topology rows, the $\ProdUp$ readback row, both projection-continuity rows, the rectangular subbasis row, the generated topology row, replay, provenance, and local naming.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a product observation through pairing, component readback, rectangular subbasis membership, generated-open formation, projection continuity, transport, provenance, and local naming.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ProductTopologyUp$, $\TopologyUp$, $\ProdUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ProductTopologyUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through these displayed rows. It has no coordinate for box-topology opens, quotient topology, an external powerset of pairs, arbitrary host-indexed open families, choice-selected bases, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[ProductTopology NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:product-topology-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\ProductTopologyUp$ carrier
$$
Q=(X,Y,O_X,O_Y,\Pi,\pi_X,\pi_Y,B,T,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\ProductTopologyUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for that thirteen-row packet, component-topology exposure through $O_X,O_Y$, product readback through $\Pi$, projection-continuity exposure through $\pi_X,\pi_Y$, rectangular subbasis generation through $B$, generated topology admission through $T$, and transport, replay, provenance, and local naming through $H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet. The carrier displays exactly two component histories, two component topology rows, one product readback row, two projection-continuity rows, one rectangular subbasis row, one generated topology row, and the structural rows.

Read the topology rows before the product row. The component open indices and membership reads are supplied by $O_X$ and $O_Y$, while $\Pi$ supplies only the first and second component readbacks for paired histories.

The subbasis row $B$ then binds a component-open pair to the corresponding product observation, and $T$ records the generated-open operations admitted from that rectangular row. The projection rows $\pi_X,\pi_Y$ are downstream records that the same paired readbacks are continuous into the component topology rows.

Finally, $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name that same finite route. Since box opens, quotient opens, host powersets, arbitrary host-indexed open families, selected bases, Lean proof terms, and bridge certificates are absent from the carrier inventory, the listed rows exhaust the local certificate surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ProductTopology projection continuity]
\label{thm:product-topology-projection-continuity}
Every projection-continuity read exported by an accepted $\ProductTopologyUp$ carrier factors through
$$
\Pi,O_X,O_Y,B,T,H,C,P,N.
$$
The first projection reads the first component through $\Pi$ and $O_X$, and the second projection reads the second component through $\Pi$ and $O_Y$; neither route can bypass the rectangular subbasis row or the generated $\TopologyUp$ row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a projection-continuity request. The only product readback coordinate is $\Pi$, so the request must choose the first or second displayed projection route of that row.

On the first route, the component open evidence is read from $O_X$ and the continuity row is $\pi_X$. On the second route, the component open evidence is read from $O_Y$ and the continuity row is $\pi_Y$.

In both cases the rectangular subbasis row $B$ records how the component-open observation is represented as a product-open observation, and $T$ admits that row as part of the generated product topology. The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve the same route, so a projection consumer receives no host pair equality, box topology, quotient topology, or external powerset data.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ProductTopology universal route]
\label{thm:product-topology-universal-route}
Let a displayed $\ContinuousMapUp$ packet into an accepted $\ProductTopologyUp$ carrier have component reads that factor through the two projection rows. Then its product-facing route is the finite ledger
$$
O_X,O_Y,\Pi,\pi_X,\pi_Y,B,T,H,C,P,N.
$$
Thus the binary product-topology universal property is recorded only as a BEDC route through paired histories, component topology rows, projection-continuity rows, rectangular subbasis generation, and generated-open replay; it does not assert a host function-space theorem, a box-topology comparison, quotient topology, choice of arbitrary opens, or ambient set product.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the displayed map packet and its two component reads. Each component read is a $\ContinuousMapUp$ route into one of the topology rows $O_X$ or $O_Y$, and the only product carrier available for pairing those reads is $\Pi$.

The projection rows $\pi_X,\pi_Y$ identify the component reads as the two product projections. The rectangular subbasis row $B$ is therefore the only row that can turn paired component-open observations into product-open observations.

The generated topology row $T$ admits exactly the finite generated-open operations exposed by $B$. Transport and replay through $H$ and $C$, together with provenance and local naming through $P,N$, repack the displayed component routes into the product-facing ledger.

No further universal-property coordinate is present. The carrier contains no host function-space theorem, box-topology comparison, quotient topology, choice-selected open family, or ambient set product, so the stated finite ledger is the entire exported route.
\end{proof}
