\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\AsymptoticEquivalenceUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-asymptotic-equivalence-namecert}

Following Hardy on divergent series and Bishop--Bridges constructive analysis, an $\AsymptoticEquivalenceUp$ packet names tail-equivalence data for real-series and regular-Cauchy comparison routes by finite thresholds, displayed tail windows, and terminal Real-facing seals. It sits after the partial-sum and tail-budget surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-realseries-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the finite stream-window interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet gives Real and Completion consumers a reusable comparison certificate for two displayed tails; it is not a quotient of series, not a selected sum, not a host limit, and not an ambient completeness principle.

\begin{definition}[Asymptotic equivalence carrier]
\label{def:asymptotic-equivalence-carrier}
An $\AsymptoticEquivalenceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
A=(S_0,S_1,R_0,R_1,D,T,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S_0,S_1$ are the two $\RealSeriesUp$ tail-source rows whose partial-sum or term tails are compared.
\item $R_0,R_1$ are the matching $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback rows used by the two tail names.
\item $D$ is the shared $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger for ratio, difference, and tail-budget inspections.
\item $T$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ tail-window schedule assigning each requested precision a pair of inspected tail windows.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ comparison-seal row reached after the two readbacks and dyadic ledger have been consumed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the two series rows, two regular readbacks, dyadic ledger, stream windows, terminal seal, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ replay from each precision request through $T,D,R_0,R_1,E,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RealSeriesUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\AsymptoticEquivalenceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exposes only finite tail-comparison data. It has no coordinate for a selected infinite sum, quotient equality of series, host limit selection, trichotomy, countable choice, ambient Real completeness, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Asymptotic equivalence NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:asymptotic-equivalence-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\AsymptoticEquivalenceUp$ carrier $A=(S_0,S_1,R_0,R_1,D,T,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\AsymptoticEquivalenceUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier exposure, two real-series tail-source rows, two regular-Cauchy readback rows, the shared dyadic tolerance ledger, the stream-indexed tail-window schedule, the terminal Real comparison seal, componentwise classifier transport, continuation replay, package provenance, and local naming. Each accepted precision request is classified by the finite tail window in $T$ and the tolerance ledger in $D$ before either regular readback may expose the terminal row $E$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier tuple in \autoref{def:asymptotic-equivalence-carrier}. The analytic rows are $S_0,S_1,R_0,R_1,D,T,E$, and the structural rows are $H,C,P,N$.

For a precision request, first read the scheduled finite windows in $T$ and the dyadic tolerance ledger $D$. These rows determine which tails of the two $\RealSeriesUp$ sources and which regular-Cauchy readbacks may be inspected.

The classifier transport is componentwise through $H$, while $C$ replays the same route from the request to the two readbacks and then to $E$. The provenance row $P$ and local naming row $N$ record only those displayed dependencies, so the NameCert surface cannot import a selected sum, quotient equality, host limit, choice schedule, or ambient completeness proof.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Asymptotic equivalence tail transfer]
\label{thm:asymptotic-equivalence-tail-transfer}
Let $A=(S_0,S_1,R_0,R_1,D,T,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\AsymptoticEquivalenceUp$ carrier. If a Real or Completion consumer reads a finite tail estimate for $S_0$ through the schedule $T$ and ledger $D$, then the same accepted packet exposes the matching finite tail estimate for $S_1$ through the componentwise readback rows $R_0,R_1$ and the terminal comparison seal $E$. The transfer is stable under $\hsame$ transport of the carrier rows and exports no selected real representative, quotient stream equality, host convergence principle, or completed infinite tail.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the accepted schedule row $T$. It stores a pair of finite tail windows for each precision request, so a consumer that reads the first source tail has already displayed the matching second-source window.

Use the dyadic ledger $D$ to compare the two inspected tails. The readback rows $R_0$ and $R_1$ then expose the regular-Cauchy data attached to the two windows, and the terminal row $E$ records the Real-facing comparison seal reached after both readbacks are displayed.

Transport along $H$ preserves the two series rows, two readbacks, ledger, windows, and terminal seal component by component. Replaying $C$ therefore gives the same tail-transfer route to every downstream consumer, while the excluded selected representatives, quotient equalities, host convergence principles, and completed infinite tails remain absent from the carrier.
\end{proof}
