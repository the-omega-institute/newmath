\begin{theorem}[Presburger closed-observation NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:presburger-closed-observation-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:presburger-subuniversal-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\PresburgerSubUniversalUp}$ surface consists exactly of six finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier exposure for $P=(S,A,Q,D,K,H,C,L,N)$;
\item additive-syntax accountability through $S$ and $A$;
\item finite decision accountability through $Q$ and $D$;
\item closed-observation accountability through $K$;
\item componentwise stability through $H$ and displayed replay through $C$;
\item non-escape through $L$ and $N$, excluding multiplication, oracle import, Turing-universal simulation, quotient equality of names, and external semantic ratification.
\end{enumerate}
No consumer can cite Presburger arithmetic as the sub-universal closed quadrant witness unless the additive syntax, finite decision trace, and closure row are present in the same finite packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier exposure is projection from \autoref{def:presburger-subuniversal-carrier}. The packet displays syntax, additive arithmetic, quantifier-elimination or normal-form decision data, decision trace, closure, transports, replay routes, provenance, and the local name.

The arithmetic and decision obligations are separate reads. The rows $S$ and $A$ identify the first-order additive language, while $Q$ and $D$ record the finite decision trace for the displayed formula.

Closure is read through $K$. The replay routes in $C$ may inspect the syntax, additive readback, decision trace, and closure readback, but they do not import multiplication, a Turing-machine simulator, or an oracle row.

Finally, $H,L,N$ transport, package, and name the same finite packet. Since every exported read is tied to the displayed Presburger decision trace, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}
