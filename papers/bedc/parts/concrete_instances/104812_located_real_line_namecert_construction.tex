\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\LocatedRealLineUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-located-real-line-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges, the located real line is presented as a finite interface over located real names, apartness evidence, and regular Cauchy readback, rather than as a host real-line object or quotient completion. The $\LocatedRealLineUp$ packet binds the located-name surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-located-real-namecert}, the positive-separation evidence of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-apartnessreal-namecert}, the terminal real seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the regular rational source route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the finite observation windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}. It also reads the dyadic endpoint and tolerance ledgers of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert} so L10 real-completion routes can cite one non-isolated line interface.

\begin{definition}[LocatedRealLine carrier]
\label{def:located-real-line-carrier}
A $\LocatedRealLineUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
\Lambda=(L,A,Q,R,S,D,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $L$ is the $\LocatedRealUp$ row presenting the point-facing located real-name surface.
\item $A$ is the $\ApartnessRealUp$ row presenting positive finite-window separation evidence available to line-facing order and metric consumers.
\item $Q$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row reached from the same regular rational source route.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ source row carrying the regular Cauchy readback used by $L$ and $Q$.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-observation schedule naming the windows inspected by the line interface.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint, radius, and tolerance ledger used for located windows, apartness gaps, and regular Cauchy bounds.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $L,A,Q,R,S,D$ and for the structural replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a line request through $L,A,Q,R,S,D,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LocatedRealLineUp$, $\LocatedRealUp$, $\ApartnessRealUp$, $\RealUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedRealLineUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate asserts that these rows form one finite located real-line surface. It contains no quotient real equality, trichotomy decision, host real-line primitive, ambient completeness theorem, selected limit, countable-choice schedule, or bridge credential.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[LocatedRealLine NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:located-real-line-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\LocatedRealLineUp$ carrier $\Lambda=(L,A,Q,R,S,D,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedRealLineUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the located-real row, apartness row, real seal, regular rational source, stream schedule, dyadic ledger, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item classifier stability, requiring sameness to be componentwise transport of $L,A,Q,R,S,D$ through the displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ routes;
\item located point exactness, requiring every point read to pass through the $\LocatedRealUp$ row $L$ and its scheduled regular Cauchy source rows $R,S,D$;
\item apartness visibility, requiring every positive gap exposed to an order or metric consumer to pass through the $\ApartnessRealUp$ row $A$ and the same finite dyadic ledger $D$;
\item real-seal compatibility, requiring the $\RealUp$ row $Q$ to retain the same $\RegSeqRatUp$ and $\StreamNameUp$ source surface seen by $L$;
\item non-escape, excluding quotient real equality, trichotomy, host real-line primitives, ambient completeness, selected limits, countable choice, Lean verification, and bridge checking as local data.
\end{enumerate}
No located real-line obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:located-real-line-carrier}. The analytic rows are precisely the located-real row $L$, apartness row $A$, real seal $Q$, regular rational source $R$, stream schedule $S$, and dyadic ledger $D$.

Classifier stability is componentwise. The rows $H$ and $C$ transport and replay the six analytic rows, while $P$ and $N$ source and name the same displayed packet.

Located point exactness follows by reading $L$ through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-located-real-namecert}. Apartness visibility follows by reading $A$ through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-apartnessreal-namecert}. In both cases the only line-facing observations available are the scheduled regular Cauchy windows and dyadic ledgers already stored in $R,S,D$.

The seal row $Q$ is terminal and shares the same $R,S$ source route. Since no remaining coordinate contains quotient equality, trichotomy, a host real line, ambient completeness, selected limits, or countable choice, the listed rows exhaust the local certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LocatedRealLine regular Cauchy real handoff]
\label{thm:located-real-line-regular-cauchy-real-handoff}
Every L10 real-completion consumer of an accepted $\LocatedRealLineUp$ carrier receives the finite route
$$
L,A,R,S,D \longmapsto Q \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
The handoff first exposes located real-name data, apartness-gap data, scheduled regular rational readback, stream windows, and dyadic tolerance accounting. Only after those rows are displayed does it expose the terminal $\RealUp$ seal and the structural transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted carrier $\Lambda=(L,A,Q,R,S,D,H,C,P,N)$. By \autoref{thm:located-real-line-namecert-obligations}, any consumer must read the point surface, positive-gap surface, regular Cauchy source, stream schedule, and dyadic ledger before the seal row is visible.

The located-real component $L$ supplies the point-facing readback through the finite windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert} and the regular source rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}. The apartness component $A$ supplies positive finite-window gap evidence through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-apartnessreal-namecert}.

The real seal $Q$ is then the same terminal seal route cited by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The dyadic ledger $D$ remains the only endpoint, radius, and tolerance account available from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}.

Repacking through $H,C,P,N$ preserves exactly this displayed route. The carrier has no coordinate for quotient real equality, trichotomy, a host real-line primitive, ambient completeness, selected limits, countable choice, or bridge data, so the L10 handoff is exhausted by the finite packet above.
\end{proof}
