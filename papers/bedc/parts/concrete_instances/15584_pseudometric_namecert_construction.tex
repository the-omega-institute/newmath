\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\PseudometricUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-pseudometric-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, the $\PseudometricUp$ packet records the pre-separated distance surface used by the constructive metric and completion route. It sits before the metric carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the separated reflection of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separatedmetric-namecert}, the located-distance interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedmetric-namecert}, and the completion boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completion-namecert}. The packet reads dyadic tolerance rows from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, finite windows from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, regular rational readback from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal distance seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. Its purpose is to expose distance-zero evidence as finite NameCert data before any consumer turns that evidence into point identity.

\begin{definition}[Pseudometric carrier]
\label{def:pseudometric-carrier}
A $\PseudometricUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
P=(X,M,D,S,R,E,Z,H,C,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the point carrier row. It records the admitted point names and their classifier, but it does not quotient points by zero distance.
\item $M$ is the displayed distance witness. It names the finite comparison surface used to request a distance between two admitted point rows.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance and triangle ledger. It records dyadic bounds, reflexive zero requests, symmetry swaps, and triangle estimates as displayed comparison rows.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row controlling which point-name and distance-name windows may be inspected.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row attached to the distance witness, giving regular rational approximants for requested windows.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ distance seal. It is reachable only after $S,R,D$ have displayed the finite readback and tolerance evidence.
\item $Z$ is the zero-distance boundary row. It records that zero distance is a boundary condition on the distance ledger, not an identification of point rows.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the point carrier, distance witness, dyadic ledger, stream windows, regular readback, Real seal, zero-distance boundary, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a consumer request through point admission, stream windows, regular readback, dyadic tolerance and triangle rows, Real distance sealing, and the zero-distance boundary.
\item $N$ is both the $\Pkg$ provenance over the listed BEDC dependencies and the local $\NameCert_{\PseudometricUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for host equality of points, quotient completion, selected limits, countable choice, trichotomy, open-cover compactness, or a separated reflection.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Pseudometric NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:pseudometric-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\PseudometricUp$ carrier $P=(X,M,D,S,R,E,Z,H,C,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\PseudometricUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the point carrier, displayed distance witness, dyadic tolerance and triangle ledger, StreamName windows, RegSeqRat readback, Real distance seal, zero-distance boundary, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item distance readback, requiring every exported distance request to pass through $S,R,D$ before the Real seal $E$ is read;
\item reflexive, symmetric, and triangle stability, requiring zero self-distance, symmetry transport, and triangle estimates to be displayed as rows of $D$ rather than imported as host metric laws;
\item classifier transport, requiring point and distance classifiers to move only by the componentwise $\hsame$ row $H$ and replay row $C$;
\item zero-distance non-identification, requiring the row $Z$ to record zero distance without equating the two point rows;
\item ledger non-escape, excluding quotient completion, host equality, selected limits, countable-choice selectors, trichotomy, open-cover compactness, and any separated reflection not routed through $\SeparatedMetricUp$.
\end{enumerate}
No pseudometric NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:pseudometric-carrier}. The tuple lists exactly the point carrier, distance witness, dyadic ledger, stream windows, regular readback, Real seal, zero-distance boundary, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.

Distance readback follows the replay order in $C$. A consumer first reads the point names and finite stream windows, then the regular rational readback $R$ and dyadic tolerance ledger $D$, and only then reaches the Real seal $E$.

Reflexive, symmetric, and triangle stability are ledger rows. The packet stores self-distance, symmetry, and triangle estimates inside $D$, so no host-level metric law can enter as a detached premise.

Classifier transport is componentwise. The row $H$ transports exactly the displayed coordinates, while $C$ replays them in the same order for consumers.

The zero-distance row $Z$ is separate from point identity. Since $X$ keeps point admission and $Z$ keeps the boundary condition, the certificate exposes zero-distance evidence without identifying the point rows.

The remaining exclusions are immediate from the carrier coordinates. No quotient completion, host equality, selected limit, countable-choice selector, trichotomy, open-cover compactness, or separated reflection appears in $P$, so the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Pseudometric separated reflection handoff]
\label{thm:pseudometric-separated-reflection-handoff}
Let $P=(X,M,D,S,R,E,Z,H,C,N)$ be an accepted $\PseudometricUp$ packet. Any consumer that wants point identity from zero distance must pass through the separated reflection boundary named by $\SeparatedMetricUp$. Consumers for $\CompletionUp$ and $\LocatedMetricUp$ receive only the displayed pseudometric rows $X,M,D,S,R,E,Z,H,C,N$; they receive no quotient completion, host equality, selected limit, countable-choice selector, trichotomy, or open-cover compactness from the pseudometric packet itself.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a consumer of $P$. Carrier admission exposes the point carrier and distance rows, but the zero-distance row $Z$ is only a boundary row on the displayed distance ledger.

If the consumer asks for point identity, the request is not answered inside $P$. The only named route from zero distance to point identity is the separated reflection interface of $\SeparatedMetricUp$.

Completion and located-distance consumers follow a different boundary. They may read the displayed point, distance, tolerance, stream, readback, Real seal, zero-distance, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows, but those rows do not identify points.

Because quotient completion, host equality, selected limits, countable-choice selectors, trichotomy, and open-cover compactness are absent from the carrier, no such datum can be exported along the completion or located-metric route.
\end{proof}
