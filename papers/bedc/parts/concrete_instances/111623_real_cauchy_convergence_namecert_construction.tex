\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RealCauchyConvergenceUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-real-cauchy-convergence-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges' constructive treatment of real convergence by explicit Cauchy moduli, the $\RealCauchyConvergenceUp$ packet records the finite route by which a displayed tail schedule for regular rational names is consumed before the terminal $\RealUp$ seal is read. It sits over the dyadic tolerance core of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the finite observation interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, and the completion-facing regular Cauchy boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bishop-regular-cauchy-completion-namecert}. The packet names a convergence certificate for L10 Real and Completion consumers; it does not assert ambient completeness of $\RealUp$, select a host limit, quotient Cauchy streams, invoke countable choice, or compare completed host real values.

\begin{definition}[RealCauchyConvergence carrier]
\label{def:real-cauchy-convergence-carrier}
A $\RealCauchyConvergenceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
G=(D,S,R,T,B,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, containing the dyadic error requests and comparison budgets inspected by the convergence consumer.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window schedule opened at the tail thresholds named for those dyadic requests.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row obtained from the scheduled windows and dyadic comparisons.
\item $T$ is the displayed Cauchy modulus tail row, assigning each dyadic tolerance a finite tail index and the late-window reuse condition needed by convergence checks.
\item $B$ is the $\BishopRegularCauchyCompletionUp$ boundary row, recording that the same $D,S,R,T$ data are read as a completion-facing regular Cauchy packet before any Real seal is exposed.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after the tail modulus, finite windows, regular readback, and completion boundary have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the tolerance ledger, window schedule, regular readback, tail modulus, completion boundary, Real seal, replay row, provenance row, and local naming row.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a convergence request through $T$, then through $D,S,R,B$, and finally into the terminal $\RealUp$ seal.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RealCauchyConvergenceUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\BishopRegularCauchyCompletionUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RealCauchyConvergenceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $D,S,R,T,B,E,H,C,P,N$. The carrier has no coordinate for quotient stream equality, selected limits, countable-choice schedules, host real equality, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[RealCauchyConvergence NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:real-cauchy-convergence-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RealCauchyConvergenceUp$ carrier
$$
G=(D,S,R,T,B,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\RealCauchyConvergenceUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission, dyadic tolerance exposure, finite tail-window inspection, regular rational readback, displayed Cauchy modulus tail control, completion-boundary handoff, Real-seal routing, componentwise transport, displayed replay, provenance, local naming, and non-escape from the displayed rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:real-cauchy-convergence-carrier}. The accepted packet exposes the tolerance ledger $D$, finite windows $S$, regular readback $R$, tail modulus row $T$, completion boundary $B$, terminal Real seal $E$, and structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

The convergence obligations are then read in order. The dyadic core of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert} supplies the tolerance requests, the stream interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert} supplies the finite inspected windows, and the regular rational route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert} packages those windows as the displayed readback row.

The tail and completion rows are the only convergence-specific data. The row $T$ fixes the late-window threshold for each displayed tolerance, while the boundary row $B$ routes the same data through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bishop-regular-cauchy-completion-namecert} before the $\RealUp$ seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert} is consumed.

Finally $H$ and $C$ transport and replay the same coordinates, while $P$ and $N$ record provenance and local naming. Since no other coordinate occurs in the carrier, the NameCert surface exports none of the excluded quotient, selected-limit, choice, host-equality, completeness, verification, or bridge data.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RealCauchyConvergence regular seal route]
\label{thm:real-cauchy-convergence-regular-seal}
Every $\RealUp$-facing read exported by an accepted $\RealCauchyConvergenceUp$ carrier factors through the finite route
$$
T,D,S,R \longmapsto B \longmapsto E
$$
before the terminal $\RealUp$ seal can be consumed. Thus a consumer receives the displayed Cauchy modulus tail threshold, dyadic tolerance rows, finite stream windows, regular rational readback, and the Bishop regular Cauchy completion boundary before any Real seal is visible.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted carrier $G=(D,S,R,T,B,E,H,C,P,N)$. By \autoref{thm:real-cauchy-convergence-namecert-obligations}, the only Real-facing row is $E$, and it is downstream of the displayed tail, dyadic, stream, regular-readback, and completion-boundary rows.

The route cannot skip the tail row because $T$ is the only coordinate assigning late thresholds to dyadic tolerance requests. It cannot skip $D$ or $S$ because the dyadic ledger and finite windows are the only displayed sources for the inspected regular rational reads.

The completion boundary is also forced. The row $B$ is the sole $\BishopRegularCauchyCompletionUp$ handoff available to a Real or Completion consumer, so the terminal seal is read only after the regular readback $R$ has been routed through that boundary.

Transport, replay, provenance, and local naming preserve the same sequence of rows. Since $H,C,P,N$ add no quotient stream equality, selected limit, countable-choice schedule, host equality, or ambient completeness theorem, every $\RealUp$-facing read reaches $E$ only through the displayed convergence packet.
\end{proof}
