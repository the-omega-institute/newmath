Following Bishop-style constructive analysis, the $\CountableChoiceBoundaryUp$ packet separates finite witness choice from countable choice in the Real and Completion corridor. It sits after the finite stream-window surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the terminal Real seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the no-choice diagonal boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-choice-boundary-namecert}, and the deterministic relation readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-unique-choice-principle-namecert}.

The packet records the point at which finite windows may carry displayed witnesses while no global selector, host sequence choice, selected limit, quotient stream equality, or ambient completion principle is admitted. It is a human-origin boundary object because the distinction between finite witness extraction and countable choice is part of constructive analysis rather than a BEDC-native discovery.

\begin{definition}[Countable choice boundary carrier]
\label{def:countable-choice-boundary-carrier}
A $\CountableChoiceBoundaryUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
B=(Q,W,R,E,F,L,U,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $Q$ is the finite request row naming the observation window and tolerance demand being served.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window row opened by $Q$.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row attached to the same finite window.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after $Q,W,R$ have been displayed.
\item $F$ is the finite witness row. It records witnesses for the displayed window and no data outside that window.
\item $L$ is the local no-selected-limit row. It refuses a global selector, host sequence choice, countable choice, quotient stream equality, and ambient Real-completion evidence.
\item $U$ is the deterministic unique-readback row, inherited from $\UniqueChoicePrincipleUp$ only for already displayed finite relation witnesses.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for request, window, readback, seal, finite witnesses, refusal, unique readback, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from $Q$ through $W,R,E,F,L,U,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CountableChoiceBoundaryUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\CauchyChoiceBoundaryUp$, $\UniqueChoicePrincipleUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CountableChoiceBoundaryUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through these eleven rows. It has no coordinate for a global selector, a host sequence-choice function, a countable-choice principle, quotient stream equality, selected limits, ambient completion, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Countable choice boundary finite witness row]
\label{thm:countable-choice-boundary-finite-witness-row}
Let $B=(Q,W,R,E,F,L,U,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CountableChoiceBoundaryUp$ carrier. Every witness exported by $B$ factors through the displayed finite route
$$
Q \longmapsto W \longmapsto R \longmapsto F .
$$
Thus finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows may be witnessed, and their $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback may be inspected, only inside the finite request already named by $Q$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier of \autoref{def:countable-choice-boundary-carrier}. The only witness coordinate is $F$, and it appears after the finite request, stream window, and regular rational readback rows.

The $\BHist$ shape keeps the witness inside the displayed finite packet. Componentwise $\hsame$ transport in $H$ moves the same rows together, while the $\Cont$ replay row $C$ follows the ordered route from $Q$ through $W,R,F$.

The provenance row $P$ cites $\StreamNameUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$ as finite-window sources, not as a host family of all choices. Hence the packet permits witnessed finite windows while preserving the refusal of any global selector or host sequence-choice function.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Countable choice boundary no selected limit]
\label{thm:countable-choice-boundary-no-selected-limit}
No accepted $\CountableChoiceBoundaryUp$ carrier exports a selected limit, a global stream selector, host sequence choice, countable choice, quotient stream equality, or ambient completion datum. The only Real-facing data exported by
$$
B=(Q,W,R,E,F,L,U,H,C,P,N)
$$
are the displayed finite request, $\StreamNameUp$ window, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, $\RealUp$ seal, finite witness row, refusal row, deterministic finite readback, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier inventory. The refusal row $L$ explicitly excludes selected limits, global stream selectors, host sequence choice, countable choice, quotient stream equality, and ambient completion data, and the tuple has no additional coordinate from which such data could be reconstructed.

The seal row $E$ is terminal for the displayed finite route. A consumer reaches it only after reading $Q,W,R$, so the $\RealUp$ face is attached to the visible finite packet rather than to a selected host limit.

The unique-readback row $U$ does not add a choice principle. By the role inherited from $\UniqueChoicePrincipleUp$, it compares already displayed finite relation witnesses and therefore cannot choose a sequence of witnesses over all requests.

Transport, replay, and provenance preserve the same boundary. The rows $H,C,P,N$ move, replay, source, and name only the finite packet, while the no-choice sibling $\CauchyChoiceBoundaryUp$ blocks the diagonal route from being read as a global selector.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Countable choice boundary Real-completion discipline]
\label{thm:countable-choice-boundary-real-completion-discipline}
Every Real and Completion consumer of an accepted $\CountableChoiceBoundaryUp$ carrier must pass through the finite witness row of \autoref{thm:countable-choice-boundary-finite-witness-row} and the no-selected-limit boundary of \autoref{thm:countable-choice-boundary-no-selected-limit}. Consequently $\RealUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$ arguments may use finite-window witnesses but may not import countable choice, host sequence choice, selected limits, quotient stream equality, or ambient Real-completion evidence.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a Real and Completion consumer of $B$. The carrier exposes the request row $Q$, the $\StreamNameUp$ window $W$, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback $R$, and the $\RealUp$ seal $E$ before any structural row is consumed.

Apply \autoref{thm:countable-choice-boundary-finite-witness-row}. The only witness surface is the finite row $F$, so the consumer may use witnesses for the displayed finite window and no larger family.

Apply \autoref{thm:countable-choice-boundary-no-selected-limit}. The refusal row $L$ and the sibling $\CauchyChoiceBoundaryUp$ exclude selected limits, global selectors, host sequence choice, countable choice, quotient stream equality, and ambient completion data.

Finally read $U,H,C,P,N$. The deterministic finite readback inherited from $\UniqueChoicePrincipleUp$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming rows preserve the same finite route, so the Real-completion consumer remains inside the finite witness boundary.
\end{proof}
