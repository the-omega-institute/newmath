\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\LocatedCutUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-locatedcut-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\LocatedCutUp$ interface records the constructive real described by located lower and upper rational information while keeping the completion-facing readback visible as finite rows. Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, locatedness is represented as explicit interval evidence at each unary precision, not as a quotient of cuts or a selected supremum. The packet depends on $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, dyadic interval ledgers, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.

\begin{definition}[LocatedCut carrier]
\label{def:located-cut-carrier}
A $\LocatedCutUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(D^{-},D^{+},W,R,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D^{-}$ is the lower dyadic interval ledger, recording finite witnesses that a dyadic rational lies below the presented cut;
\item $D^{+}$ is the upper dyadic interval ledger, recording finite witnesses that a dyadic rational lies above the presented cut;
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window row listing, for each unary precision visible in the packet, the finite lower and upper ledger entries exposed to consumers;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row extracted from the displayed located windows;
\item $S$ is the $\RealUp$ seal-facing row reached through the regular rational handoff;
\item $H$ records the componentwise $\hsame$ transports for lower ledger, upper ledger, stream-window, regular-sequence, and real-seal rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes through the same rows;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over the displayed dyadic, stream, regular-sequence, and real-seal dependencies;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedCutUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no quotient cut, no host real equality, no selected supremum, and no use of $\mathsf{Classical.choice}$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[LocatedCut NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:located-cut-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\LocatedCutUp}$ surface consists of six finite rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier habitation by lower and upper dyadic ledgers, $\StreamNameUp$ windows, a $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff, a $\RealUp$ seal row, displayed transports, continuations, package provenance, and local naming;
\item lower-ledger and upper-ledger stability under the stored $\hsame$ transports;
\item locatedness evidence at each unary precision, witnessed by a finite pair of dyadic bounds in the corresponding $\StreamNameUp$ window row;
\item compatibility of those windows with the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row;
\item factorization of the $\RealUp$ seal through the handoff row and displayed provenance;
\item exactness of the $\Pkg$ and local $\NameCert$ rows for the listed dependencies.
\end{enumerate}
These rows certify a located constructive real without quotient cuts, host real equality, selected suprema, or $\mathsf{Classical.choice}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier habitation is projection of \autoref{def:located-cut-carrier}. The lower ledger, upper ledger, stream-window, regular-sequence, real-seal, transport, continuation, package, and local naming rows are finite coordinates of the same $\BHist$ packet.

Ledger stability is componentwise. Move the lower and upper dyadic rows along the stored $\hsame$ transports, then replay the displayed $\Cont$ routes for the same window entries. Since each unary precision appears only through a finite window row, locatedness is a displayed witness rather than an ambient order-completeness claim.

The compatibility and seal rows use the same finite path. The $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff reads only the located dyadic windows, and the $\RealUp$ seal reads only that handoff together with the package and local naming rows.

The exclusions follow from the carrier shape. No coordinate stores a quotient cut, host real equality proof, selected supremum, or classical choice term, so the listed finite rows exhaust the certificate surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LocatedCut regular Cauchy handoff]
\label{thm:located-cut-regular-cauchy-handoff}
Every accepted $\LocatedCutUp$ packet exports a $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff whose $n$th visible approximation is selected from the finite lower and upper dyadic ledgers displayed in the $\StreamNameUp$ row for unary precision $n$. The handoff is transported only by the stored $\hsame$ rows and displayed $\Cont$ routes, and it introduces no quotient cut, host real equality, selected supremum, or $\mathsf{Classical.choice}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Fix an accepted carrier $L=(D^{-},D^{+},W,R,S,H,C,P,N)$. For a visible unary precision $n$, the window row $W$ supplies the finite lower and upper dyadic ledger entries associated with $n$.

The handoff row $R$ is one of the carrier coordinates. Its readback is constrained by the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:located-cut-namecert-obligations}, so it may inspect only the displayed ledger entries in $W$ and the stored transport and continuation rows.

Transport is therefore internal to the packet. Moving along $H$ and replaying $C$ changes the presented row names but not the finite dependency route from located windows to $R$.

Because neither the carrier nor the obligations contain a quotient-cut coordinate, a host real equality test, a supremum selector, or a classical choice row, the resulting regular Cauchy handoff is constructive and finite-row based.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LocatedCut dyadic interval exhaustion]
\label{thm:located-cut-dyadic-interval-exhaustion}
For every accepted $\LocatedCutUp$ packet and every unary precision row visible in its $\StreamNameUp$ window ledger, the lower and upper dyadic interval rows supply a finite located interval whose width is bounded by that precision row and whose endpoints remain attached to the same $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ provenance. No appeal is made to a quotient of cuts, host real equality, selected supremum, or $\mathsf{Classical.choice}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the visible unary precision row from $W$. By the locatedness clause in \autoref{thm:located-cut-namecert-obligations}, the same window carries a finite lower dyadic witness from $D^{-}$ and a finite upper dyadic witness from $D^{+}$.

The interval bound is a displayed row of the packet, not a completeness theorem. Its width claim is checked against the unary precision row inside the finite window ledger, and the endpoint names remain tied to the same package and local naming coordinates.

Stability follows by replaying the stored $\hsame$ transports and $\Cont$ routes for the lower endpoint, upper endpoint, precision row, and window row. The transported interval is still read from the same finite packet.

Thus the visible precision rows are exhausted by displayed dyadic interval evidence. The construction never forms a cut quotient, never compares host real equalities, never selects a supremum, and never invokes classical choice.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LocatedCut Real seal non-escape]
\label{thm:located-cut-real-seal-non-escape}
Every $\RealUp$ seal-facing read exported by an accepted $\LocatedCutUp$ carrier factors through the lower and upper dyadic ledgers, the $\StreamNameUp$ located window rows, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff, and the displayed $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ provenance rows. The exported seal cannot bypass those rows by using quotient cuts, host real equality, selected suprema, or $\mathsf{Classical.choice}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted carrier $L=(D^{-},D^{+},W,R,S,H,C,P,N)$. The only real-facing coordinate is $S$, and the only handoff coordinate feeding it is $R$.

Apply \autoref{thm:located-cut-regular-cauchy-handoff}. The handoff $R$ is reached through the located dyadic windows in $W$, with lower and upper evidence read from $D^{-}$ and $D^{+}$.

Apply \autoref{thm:located-cut-dyadic-interval-exhaustion}. Each visible precision row is exhausted by finite interval evidence carrying the same transport, continuation, package, and local naming provenance.

The $\RealUp$ seal therefore has no escape route outside the displayed chain
$$
(D^{-},D^{+}) \longrightarrow W \longrightarrow R \longrightarrow S.
$$
Since the carrier has no quotient, host equality, supremum selector, or classical choice coordinate, the listed rows give the complete seal boundary.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LocatedCut classifier transport]
\label{thm:located-cut-classifier-transport}
Let $L=(D^{-},D^{+},W,R,S,H,C,P,N)$ and
$L'=(D'^{-},D'^{+},W',R',S',H',C',P',N')$ be accepted
$\LocatedCutUp$ packets. Classifier transport is componentwise over the lower
ledger, upper ledger, stream-window row, $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff, $\RealUp$
seal, displayed $\hsame$ rows, displayed $\Cont$ routes, package provenance,
and local $\NameCert_{\LocatedCutUp}$ row. It transports no quotient cut,
host real equality, selected supremum, or ambient order-completeness datum.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier tuple of \autoref{def:located-cut-carrier}. Its visible
coordinates are exactly the two dyadic ledgers, window row, handoff row, seal
row, transport rows, continuation rows, provenance, and local naming row.

Apply the finite-row clauses of
\autoref{thm:located-cut-namecert-obligations}. The lower and upper ledgers
move along the stored $\hsame$ transports, while the displayed $\Cont$ routes
replay the same window, handoff, and seal reads.

The non-escape part is immediate from the carrier shape. None of the
transported coordinates stores a quotient cut, host real equality proof,
supremum selector, or order-completeness object.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LocatedCut located-window exactness]
\label{thm:located-cut-located-window-exactness}
For every accepted packet $L=(D^{-},D^{+},W,R,S,H,C,P,N)$ and every visible
unary precision row, the stream-window row $W$ reads a finite lower dyadic
entry from $D^{-}$ and a finite upper dyadic entry from $D^{+}$. The
corresponding $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff is computed from that same displayed
window and not from a quotient cut or selected supremum.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the locatedness row of
\autoref{thm:located-cut-namecert-obligations}. For each visible precision it
requires a finite lower witness and a finite upper witness in the same
$\StreamNameUp$ window row.

Apply \autoref{thm:located-cut-dyadic-interval-exhaustion}. The dyadic
endpoints and the precision row remain attached to the displayed $\BHist$,
$\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ provenance.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:located-cut-regular-cauchy-handoff}. The handoff
row $R$ reads the located window supplied by $W$, so the visible precision is
answered by finite dyadic data rather than by an ambient cut object.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LocatedCut seal-boundary exactness]
\label{thm:located-cut-seal-boundary-exactness}
Every $\RealUp$ seal read exported by an accepted
$\LocatedCutUp$ packet factors through the displayed chain
$$
(D^{-},D^{+}) \longrightarrow W \longrightarrow R \longrightarrow S.
$$
The seal boundary exposes no quotient cut, host real equality, selected
supremum, or ambient completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the packet $L=(D^{-},D^{+},W,R,S,H,C,P,N)$ of
\autoref{def:located-cut-carrier}. The only seal-facing row is $S$, and its
only regular handoff input is $R$.

Use \autoref{thm:located-cut-located-window-exactness} to identify the window
read feeding $R$. Each visible precision reaches a finite lower and upper
dyadic pair in $W$.

Apply \autoref{thm:located-cut-real-seal-non-escape}. It gives the same
factorization from the dyadic ledgers through $W$ and $R$ to $S$, and its
non-escape clause excludes quotient cuts, host equality, selected suprema, and
ambient completeness.
\end{proof}
