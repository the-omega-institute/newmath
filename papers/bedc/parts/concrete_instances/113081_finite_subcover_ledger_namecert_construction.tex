The $\FiniteSubcoverLedgerUp$ packet records the finite subcover handoff used by the compact-uniform-continuity route of \autoref{ch:visions-compact-uniform-continuity}. It sits over a $\CompactMetricUp$ source row, a $\TotallyBoundedUp$ finite-net schedule, the dyadic radius rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the downstream $\UniformModulusUp$ consumer, packaging the finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ cover-selection route as NameCert data rather than as an open-cover compactness theorem.

\begin{definition}[Finite subcover ledger carrier]
\label{def:finite-subcover-ledger-carrier}
A $\FiniteSubcoverLedgerUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(K,T,B,D,S,U,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $K$ is the $\CompactMetricUp$ source row whose metric windows and compact source provenance are visible to the cover request.
\item $T$ is the $\TotallyBoundedUp$ finite-net schedule naming the centers and mesh tolerance used to inspect the cover.
\item $B$ is the finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ cover spine whose entries are the only cover candidates admitted by the ledger.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ radius and comparison ledger attached to the same finite cover spine.
\item $S$ is the selected finite subcover row, displayed as a sublist of $B$ with its inherited radius data.
\item $U$ is the $\UniformModulusUp$ handoff row that consumes the finite subcover ledger for compact-continuity arguments.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the compact source, net schedule, cover spine, dyadic ledger, subcover row, uniform-modulus handoff, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ stores displayed $\Cont$ replay from a compact-uniform request through the finite cover spine and into the uniform-modulus handoff.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\FiniteSubcoverLedgerUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\TotallyBoundedUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\UniformModulusUp$, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteSubcoverLedgerUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for arbitrary open covers, selected infinite subcovers, host compactness, quotient point equality, countable choice, or ambient completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Finite subcover ledger obligations]
\label{thm:finite-subcover-ledger-obligations}
For every accepted $\FiniteSubcoverLedgerUp$ carrier
$$
L=(K,T,B,D,S,U,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteSubcoverLedgerUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the ten displayed rows, compact-source exposure through $K$, finite-net exposure through $T$, finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ cover admission through $B$, dyadic radius accounting through $D$, finite subcover display through $S$, $\UniformModulusUp$ handoff through $U$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $P$, local naming through $N$, and non-escape from host open-cover compactness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:finite-subcover-ledger-carrier}. The only cover candidates available to the packet are the finite entries of $B$, and the only source coverage data are the compact source $K$, finite-net schedule $T$, and dyadic radius ledger $D$.

Read the selected subcover row $S$ as a displayed finite sublist of the same $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ spine. Because $S$ inherits its radii from $D$ and its source coverage from $K,T$, the ledger never invokes an arbitrary open cover or a selected infinite subcover.

Finally route the ledger into $U$ and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$. The $\UniformModulusUp$ consumer receives exactly the finite subcover handoff, while host compactness, quotient point equality, countable choice, and ambient completeness remain outside the packet.
\end{proof}
