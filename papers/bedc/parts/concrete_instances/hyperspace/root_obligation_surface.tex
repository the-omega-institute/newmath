\begin{theorem}[Hyperspace compact-subset root admission]
\label{thm:hyperspace-compact-subset-root-admission}
Every root consumer admitted by an accepted $\HyperspaceUp$ carrier must first read compact-source admission through \autoref{def:hyperspace-carrier} and the local obligation rows of \autoref{thm:hyperspace-namecert-obligations}. The compact-subset request is therefore represented by the concrete BEDC rows $X,K_0,K_1,N_0,N_1$ together with finite subset-net schedules, tolerance readback, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local $\NameCert_{\HyperspaceUp}$ row. It is not a host powerset element, quotient compactum, selected compact limit, open-cover compactness proof, or choice-selected representative.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.HyperspaceUp.Hyperspace\_compact\_subset\_root\_admission}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier. The source row $X$ supplies the displayed compact metric surface, while $K_0,K_1,N_0,N_1$ provide the finite compact-subset presentations and resolution schedules.

Apply \autoref{thm:hyperspace-namecert-obligations}. Its compact-source and finite subset-net clauses force every root consumer to read the subset request through those displayed rows before any Hausdorff or Vietoris data is public.

The remaining rows $R,H_s,C,P,M$ account for tolerance readback, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming. Since the excluded host and quotient objects are absent from the carrier, compact-subset admission is exactly the concrete BEDC row surface listed above.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Hyperspace directed Hausdorff root coverage]
\label{thm:hyperspace-directed-hausdorff-root-coverage}
Every directed Hausdorff root read exported by an accepted $\HyperspaceUp$ packet is covered by the two displayed ledgers $D_0$ and $D_1$, the finite net rows $K_0,K_1,N_0,N_1$, and the tolerance readback row $R$. The coverage must be bidirectional before a symmetric Hausdorff-distance consumer receives data, and the read remains transport-stable through $H_s$, replayed through $C$, packaged by $P$, and named by $M$. No directed coverage row may be replaced by a selected nearest point, quotient compact-subset equality, host Hausdorff metric, open-cover compactness, or ambient complete-hyperspace object.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.HyperspaceUp.Hyperspace\_directed\_hausdorff\_root\_coverage}
\begin{proof}
Use \autoref{thm:hyperspace-finite-net-hit}. It routes each displayed hit through the compact source and the finite subset-net schedules before the tolerance row can be consumed.

Apply \autoref{thm:hyperspace-finite-hit-directed-cover}. That theorem requires both $D_0$ and $D_1$ before a symmetric Hausdorff read is public, so a one-sided host selection cannot stand in for the missing direction.

Finally use the transport and provenance clauses of \autoref{thm:hyperspace-namecert-obligations}. The rows $H_s,C,P,M$ preserve the same finite coverage route and add no selected point, quotient equality, host metric, open-cover proof, or ambient completion coordinate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Hyperspace finite Hausdorff root obligations]
\label{thm:hyperspace-finite-hausdorff-root-obligations}
The finite Hausdorff root surface of $\HyperspaceUp$ is exhausted by compact-source admission, finite subset-net schedules, bidirectional directed Hausdorff coverage, tolerance readback, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local $\NameCert$ rows carried by
$$
X,K_0,K_1,N_0,N_1,D_0,D_1,R,H_s,C,P,M .
$$
Equivalently, the root surface cited by \autoref{thm:hyperspace-finite-hausdorff-carrier} cannot be strengthened by host powersets, quotient compacta, selected limits, open-cover compactness, selected nearest points, or choice-selected representatives.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with \autoref{thm:hyperspace-finite-hausdorff-carrier}. It identifies the public Hausdorff read as the two directed finite ledgers together with the tolerance row over the displayed compact source and net schedules.

Use \autoref{thm:hyperspace-compact-subset-root-admission} to account for the root carrier and finite subset-net admission, and use \autoref{thm:hyperspace-directed-hausdorff-root-coverage} for the bidirectional directed coverage and tolerance readback.

The structural rows are then fixed by \autoref{thm:hyperspace-namecert-obligations}. Componentwise transport, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming are the only remaining public rows, so the excluded host and quotient objects cannot add a further root obligation.
\end{proof}
