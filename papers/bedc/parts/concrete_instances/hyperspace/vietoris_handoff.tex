\begin{theorem}[Hyperspace Vietoris handoff]
\label{thm:hyperspace-vietoris-handoff}
Every Vietoris-topology or Hausdorff-distance consumer of an accepted $\HyperspaceUp$ packet must first read the compact source row, the finite subset-net schedules, both directed coverage ledgers, and the tolerance readback row. In the notation of \autoref{def:hyperspace-carrier}, the route is
$$
X,K_0,K_1,N_0,N_1,D_0,D_1,R \longmapsto H_s,C,P,M .
$$
Thus the downstream interfaces of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-vietoris-topology-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-hausdorff-distance-namecert} consume the same compact-source and finite-net boundary supplied by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}; they receive no host powerset topology, quotient compactum, selected compact limit, open-cover compactness proof, or untracked Hausdorff metric.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted $\HyperspaceUp$ carrier. The source row $X$ is the only compact metric source, and the finite subset-net schedules are displayed by $K_0,K_1,N_0,N_1$ before any directed distance or topology read is public.

Use the Hausdorff-distance ledger stability and finite Hausdorff carrier theorems of \autoref{thm:hyperspace-hausdorff-distance-stability} and \autoref{thm:hyperspace-finite-hausdorff-carrier}. They force the two directed coverage ledgers $D_0,D_1$ and the tolerance readback row $R$ to be present before a symmetric Hausdorff consumer can read a distance value.

For Vietoris consumers, use the root Vietoris net basis and refinement factorization in \autoref{thm:hyperspace-root-vietoris-net-basis} and \autoref{thm:hyperspace-vietoris-refinement-factorization}. Those results route hit, miss, and refinement reads through the same compact source and finite subset-net schedules.

The structural rows $H_s,C,P,M$ only transport, replay, source, and name this displayed packet. Since no forbidden host powerset, quotient compactum, selected compact limit, open-cover compactness proof, or untracked Hausdorff metric appears in the carrier, every Vietoris or Hausdorff consumer must use the stated handoff.
\end{proof}
