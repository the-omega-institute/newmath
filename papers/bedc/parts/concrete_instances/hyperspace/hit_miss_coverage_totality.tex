\begin{theorem}[Hyperspace hit-miss coverage totality]
\label{thm:hyperspace-hit-miss-coverage-totality}
Every hit/miss coverage read from an accepted $\HyperspaceUp$ carrier factors through the displayed $\CompactMetricUp$, $\TotallyBoundedUp$, $\CompleteMetricUp$, $\MetricDiameterUp$, $\NetConvergenceUp$, and $\CauchyUniformNetUp$ rows before it is public. The coverage read is total over the accepted packet precisely because each available route is one of the finite-net, Vietoris, directed Hausdorff, metric-diameter, net-convergence, or Cauchy-uniform handoff rows already stored in the carrier.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.HyperspaceUp.HyperspaceHitMissCoverageTotality}
\begin{proof}
Start with \autoref{thm:hyperspace-compactmetric-vietoris-unblock}. It lists the compact-source, total-bounded, complete-metric, metric-diameter, net-convergence, and Cauchy-uniform-net rows that must be displayed before a Vietoris-facing consumer can read hit/miss data.

Next use \autoref{thm:hyperspace-vietoris-finite-net-obligations} and \autoref{thm:hyperspace-cauchy-uniform-net-handoff}. The first theorem routes the compact-subset topology read through finite-net and Vietoris-hit rows, while the second routes the completion-facing handoff through the same finite carrier.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:hyperspace-finite-cover-selector-refusal}. Since the finite-cover surface has no selector, host powerset, quotient compactum, or open-cover oracle, every public hit/miss coverage answer is accounted for by the displayed finite rows. That is the asserted totality of coverage reads over the accepted packet.
\end{proof}
