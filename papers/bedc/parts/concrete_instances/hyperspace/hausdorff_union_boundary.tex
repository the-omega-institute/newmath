\begin{theorem}[Hyperspace Hausdorff union boundary]
\label{thm:hyperspace-hausdorff-union-boundary}
Every Hausdorff read of the finite union of two accepted $\HyperspaceUp$ packets is bounded only by the displayed finite subset-net rows and directed coverage ledgers of the two packets. A downstream consumer may inspect the union through the common compact source, the net rows $K_0,K_1,N_0,N_1,D_0,D_1$, the terminal $\RealUp$ tolerance readback, and the structural rows, but it receives no host powerset metric, quotient hyperspace equality, selected nearest-point operation, open-cover compactness, or ambient complete-hyperspace object.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.HyperspaceUp.HyperspaceHausdorffUnionBoundary}
\begin{proof}
Start from the Hausdorff classifier transport of \autoref{thm:hyperspace-root-hausdorff-finite-window}. It routes every comparison through the displayed compact source, finite net rows, directed Hausdorff ledgers, and Real tolerance readback.

Apply the finite-net exhaustion used by \autoref{thm:hyperspace-root-complete-totallybounded-handoff}. The union read can only combine the two displayed net families and their coverage ledgers; it cannot select a new host-level compact subset.

Then use the Vietoris union stability theorem above. Since the union packet is already expressed through displayed hit, miss, net, and refinement rows, the Hausdorff read is bounded by those same rows and exports none of the excluded host or quotient data.
\end{proof}
