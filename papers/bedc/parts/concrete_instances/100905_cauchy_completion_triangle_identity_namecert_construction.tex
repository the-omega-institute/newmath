\begin{theorem}[CauchyCompletionTriangleIdentity NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-triangle-identity-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CauchyCompletionTriangleIdentityUp$ carrier $T=(M,U,V,A,I,L,R,S,D,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyCompletionTriangleIdentityUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed monad, unit, counit, adjunction, idempotence, left route, right route, stream, dyadic, real-regular, transport, continuation, provenance, and naming rows;
\item left triangle exactness, requiring every source-side triangle read to pass through $U,M,V,A,I,L$ before it can be exported;
\item right triangle exactness, requiring every completed-side triangle read to pass through $M,U,V,A,I,R$ before it can be exported;
\item handoff discipline, requiring all finite observations to use $S$, all dyadic budgets to use $D$, and all real or regular rational reads to use $E$;
\item route exhaustion, requiring $H,C,P,N$ to replay and name no row except the displayed carrier rows;
\item non-escape, requiring the packet to export no quotient completion, selected representative, host function equality, countable choice, ambient real completeness, or bridge-checked coherence theorem.
\end{enumerate}
No local obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:cauchy-completion-triangle-identity-carrier}. The packet displays the monad, unit, counit, adjunction, idempotence, two route ledgers, three handoff rows, transport, continuation, provenance, and local name.

The left and right triangle obligations are read separately. The left route ledger $L$ starts from source admission through $U$, then reads the completion surface through $M$, returns through $V$, compares through $A$, and reuses completion through $I$.

The right route ledger $R$ starts from completed input, replays unit and bind through $M$ and $U$, returns through $V$, compares through $A$, and records repeated completion through $I$. Thus each triangle composite has a named finite route before any consumer inspects it.

The handoff rows are then forced by projection. Finite stream observations are in $S$, dyadic tolerances are in $D$, and real or regular rational reads are in $E$.

Finally, $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, package, and name only the displayed rows. Since quotient completions, selected representatives, host function equality, countable choice, ambient real completeness, and bridge checking are absent from those rows, the non-escape obligation follows by route exhaustion.
\end{proof}
