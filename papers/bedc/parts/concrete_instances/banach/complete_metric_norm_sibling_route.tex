\begin{theorem}[Banach complete-metric norm sibling route]
\label{thm:banach-complete-metric-norm-sibling-route}
Every $\BanachUp$ certificate route consumes the norm carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-norm-namecert}, the completion interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completion-namecert}, and the complete metric surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completemetric-namecert} before exporting bounded-linear-operator or limit rows. The Banach packet therefore reaches bounded-linear-operator and limit consumers through a finite ledger factorization, not through an unlisted analytic completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the Banach hub through its child files. The carrier and root NameCert rows expose a normed vector-space source, while the norm-completion certificate identifies the metric distance and regular Cauchy readback rows that the source must carry.

The completion interface is then consumed through the complete-metric NameCert interface. That child file binds the completion read to the complete metric surface before any limit certificate can be projected.

Finally the bounded-linear-operator and limit child files use those same rows as inputs. Their exports are therefore ledger factors over the displayed norm, completion, and complete-metric siblings, not separate claims of analytic completeness.
\end{proof}
