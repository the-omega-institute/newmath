\begin{definition}[Banach bounded linear operator limit comparison]
\label{def:banach-bounded-linear-operator-limit-comparison}
Let $C,D$ be $\BanachUp$ candidates, let
$\mathsf{BanachBLOp}(C,D,T,K,\Lambda)$ be a carried bounded-linear operator
row, and let $s$ be a regular Cauchy $\StreamNameUp$ stream over the
norm-induced metric of $C$ with observations $X_n$. A bounded-linear operator
limit comparison datum is the BEDC package
$$
\mathsf{BanachBLOpLimit}(C,D,T,K,\Lambda,s,\mu,\ell,L,s_T,\mu_T,\ell_T,L_T)
$$
consisting of the source complete-metric limit witness
$$
\mathsf{CompleteMetricLimitWitness}(C,s,\mu,\ell,L),
$$
the pointwise target stream $s_T$ with observations $T(X_n)$, the displayed
target limit history $\ell_T:=T(\ell)$, a target complete-metric limit witness
$$
\mathsf{CompleteMetricLimitWitness}(D,s_T,\mu_T,\ell_T,L_T),
$$
and the visible operator ledger $\Lambda$ recording the source endpoint row,
target endpoint row, source and target norm rows, additive and scalar
compatibility rows, bound endpoint $K$, and bound-comparison rows used to
turn source late-distance estimates into target late-distance estimates.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Bounded linear operator transports limit witnesses]
\label{thm:banach-bounded-linear-operator-limit-witness-transport}
Let $C,D$ be $\BanachUp$ candidates, let
$\mathsf{BanachBLOp}(C,D,T,K,\Lambda)$ be carried, and let
$$
\mathsf{CompleteMetricLimitWitness}(C,s,\mu,\ell,L)
$$
be a source limit witness for a regular Cauchy $\StreamNameUp$ stream $s$ over
the norm-induced metric of $C$. Then the pointwise stream $s_T$ with
observations $T(s(n))$ admits a complete-metric limit witness over $D$ with
limit history $T(\ell)$. The witness is the target component of
\autoref{def:banach-bounded-linear-operator-limit-comparison}.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.BanachUp.BanachSingletonBoundedLinearOperator\_limit\_witness\_transport}
\begin{proof}
First build the target stream. Apply
\autoref{thm:banach-bounded-linear-operator-cauchy-transport} to the carried
operator row and the source regular Cauchy stream. Its conclusion supplies the
regular Cauchy $\StreamNameUp$ stream whose observations are $T(s(n))$, with
target endpoint rows read from $\Lambda$.

Next transport the candidate limit endpoint. The source witness carries
$\ell$ as a point of $C$, and the target endpoint row of
\autoref{def:banach-bounded-linear-operator-carrier} carries $T(\ell)$ as a
point of $D$. The same ledger supplies the target norm row and all classifier
transport needed to read $T(\ell)$ at the Banach point surface.

It remains to assemble convergence. Fix a positive target tolerance. Use the
RealUp bound row for $K$ to choose a positive source tolerance whose product
with $K$ is below the requested target tolerance. The source
$\mathsf{CompleteMetricLimitWitness}$ gives a threshold after which
$d_C(s(n),\ell)$ is below that source tolerance.

For every late observation, apply
\autoref{thm:banach-bounded-linear-operator-lipschitz} to $s(n)$ and $\ell$.
The displayed estimate
$$
d_D(T(s(n)),T(\ell))\le K\cdot d_C(s(n),\ell)
$$
combines with the source convergence bound and the RealUp comparison chosen
above. Repacking the target stream, target limit, late-distance rows, and
comparison ledgers gives the required
$\mathsf{CompleteMetricLimitWitness}(D,s_T,\mu_T,T(\ell),L_T)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Bounded linear operator limit commutation]
\label{thm:banach-bounded-linear-operator-limit-commutation}
Let $C,D$ be $\BanachUp$ candidates, let
$\mathsf{BanachBLOp}(C,D,T,K,\Lambda)$ be carried, and suppose $s$ has source
limit witness
$$
\mathsf{CompleteMetricLimitWitness}(C,s,\mu,\ell,L).
$$
If a target complete-metric witness
$$
\mathsf{CompleteMetricLimitWitness}(D,s_T,\mu',\ell',L')
$$
is supplied for the pointwise stream $s_T(n)=T(s(n))$, then the Banach point
classifier of $D$ identifies $\ell'$ with $T(\ell)$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply
\autoref{thm:banach-bounded-linear-operator-limit-witness-transport} to the
source limit witness. This constructs a target complete-metric limit witness
for the same pointwise stream $s_T$ whose displayed limit history is
$T(\ell)$.

Now compare the two target witnesses. The supplied witness has limit
$\ell'$, while the transported witness has limit $T(\ell)$; both are
complete-metric limit witnesses for the same target stream over the same
Banach carrier $D$.

Apply \autoref{thm:banach-limit-uniqueness}. Its separatedness input is the
Banach norm-distance zero exactness already recorded for the target carrier,
and its conclusion is exactly the $D$-point classifier relation between
$\ell'$ and $T(\ell)$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.BanachUp.BanachSingletonBoundedLinearOperator\_limit\_commutation}

\begin{theorem}[Bounded linear operator limit NameCert surface]
\label{thm:banach-bounded-linear-operator-limit-namecert-surface}
For $\BanachUp$ candidates $C,D$, the comparison datum of
\autoref{def:banach-bounded-linear-operator-limit-comparison}, together with
\autoref{thm:banach-bounded-linear-operator-limit-witness-transport} and
\autoref{thm:banach-bounded-linear-operator-limit-commutation}, assembles the
paper-side naming-certificate surface for bounded-linear operator limits. The
carrier is the displayed pair of source and target
$\mathsf{CompleteMetricLimitWitness}$ rows connected by the operator ledger
$\Lambda$; the classifier is componentwise $\hsame$ transport of the source
stream, source limit, pointwise target stream, target limit, bound row, and
late-distance ledgers; stability is inherited from the Banach,
$\CompleteMetricUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RealUp$, and $\NormUp$ rows; exactness
is the classification of any supplied target limit with $T(\ell)$; and the
ledger fields are the visible operator, norm, stream, limit, distance, and
bound-comparison rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier field is the package displayed in
\autoref{def:banach-bounded-linear-operator-limit-comparison}. It names the
source Banach carrier, target Banach carrier, bounded-linear operator row,
source complete-metric witness, pointwise target stream, transported limit,
target complete-metric witness, and the visible operator ledger.

The classifier field is componentwise transport. Banach endpoint transport
moves source and target carried points, $\NormUp$ transport moves norm and
metric rows, $\StreamNameUp$ transport moves observations and moduli, RealUp
transport moves the bound and order comparisons, and
$\CompleteMetricUp$ transport repacks the transported late-distance ledgers as
complete-metric witnesses.

The stability field is the witness-transport theorem. Apply
\autoref{thm:banach-bounded-linear-operator-limit-witness-transport}; it
turns the source convergence row, the Cauchy transport row, and the Lipschitz
estimate into the target complete-metric witness with limit $T(\ell)$.

The exactness field is the commutation theorem. Apply
\autoref{thm:banach-bounded-linear-operator-limit-commutation}; it compares
any supplied target witness for the pointwise stream with the transported
witness and returns the target Banach classifier relation to $T(\ell)$.

Thus the bounded-linear operator surface now exposes the standard limit
transport obligation using only the existing Banach, CompleteMetricUp,
StreamNameUp, RealUp, and NormUp certificate rows, with no additional
parameter-transport schema.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Bounded linear operator limit comparison minimal witness]
\label{lem:banach-bounded-linear-operator-limit-comparison-minimal-witness}
Let $C,D$ be $\BanachUp$ candidates, let
$\mathsf{BanachBLOp}(C,D,T,K,\Lambda)$ be carried, and let
$$
\mathsf{CompleteMetricLimitWitness}(C,s,\mu,\ell,L)
$$
be a source limit witness for a regular Cauchy $\StreamNameUp$ stream $s$.
For the comparison datum
$$
\mathsf{BanachBLOpLimit}(C,D,T,K,\Lambda,s,\mu,\ell,L,s_T,\mu_T,T(\ell),L_T)
$$
of \autoref{def:banach-bounded-linear-operator-limit-comparison}, the
minimal consumer-facing witness is the displayed target component supplied by
\autoref{thm:banach-bounded-linear-operator-limit-witness-transport}: the
pointwise stream row $s_T(n)=T(s(n))$, the selected target endpoint $T(\ell)$,
the transported modulus $\mu_T$, the late-distance ledger $L_T$, and the
operator ledger $\Lambda$. Every accepted local admission, consumption,
route, transport, or readback of the bounded-linear-operator limit comparison
factors through these rows and through the rows listed by
\autoref{thm:banach-bounded-linear-operator-limit-namecert-surface}. It cannot
add an ambient limit selector, an off-ledger continuity principle, a quotient
completion object, a compactness row, or any unlisted coordinate outside the
same displayed packet.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Unfold \autoref{def:banach-bounded-linear-operator-limit-comparison}. The
comparison datum contains exactly the source complete-metric witness, the
operator row with ledger $\Lambda$, the pointwise target stream, the displayed
target endpoint $T(\ell)$, the target complete-metric witness, and the visible
operator, norm, stream, limit, distance, and bound-comparison ledgers. Thus a
consumer of the comparison datum has only these named fields available at the
local packet boundary.

Apply \autoref{thm:banach-bounded-linear-operator-limit-witness-transport} to
the carried operator row and the source witness. The theorem constructs the
target witness by first applying
\autoref{thm:banach-bounded-linear-operator-cauchy-transport} to obtain the
pointwise stream $s_T$ from the target endpoint rows in $\Lambda$, then reading
$T(\ell)$ through the target endpoint row of the same operator ledger. For a
requested positive target tolerance, its proof uses the $\RealUp$ bound row for
$K$ to select the source tolerance, feeds that tolerance into the displayed
source convergence modulus $\mu$, and applies
\autoref{thm:banach-bounded-linear-operator-lipschitz} to each late pair
$s(n),\ell$. The whole late-distance handoff is therefore the finite chain
$$
\mu
\quad\longrightarrow\quad
 d_C(s(n),\ell)
\quad\longrightarrow\quad
 K\cdot d_C(s(n),\ell)
\quad\longrightarrow\quad
 d_D(T(s(n)),T(\ell))
\quad\longrightarrow\quad
 L_T.
$$
No step in this chain asks for a target limit search: the endpoint is the
named endpoint $T(\ell)$, and the tolerance selector is the one obtained from
the displayed $K$-comparison rows.

Now read the same package through
\autoref{thm:banach-bounded-linear-operator-limit-namecert-surface}. Its
carrier is the pair of source and target $\mathsf{CompleteMetricLimitWitness}$
rows connected by $\Lambda$; its classifier is componentwise $\hsame$ transport
of the source stream, source limit, target stream, target limit, bound row, and
late-distance ledgers; its stability field is precisely the witness-transport
theorem; and its exactness field is
\autoref{thm:banach-bounded-linear-operator-limit-commutation}. Hence any
consumer-facing use that claims to admit or consume the limit comparison must
first project to the same source witness, $\Lambda$, $s_T$, $T(\ell)$,
$\mu_T$, and $L_T$, and any replacement of representatives is mediated only by
those componentwise classifier rows.

Suppose a stronger reading attempted to add an independent target-limit
selector, a host continuity theorem, a quotient completion object, a compactness
row, or another coordinate not present in the displayed comparison datum. Such
a coordinate is not a field of
\autoref{def:banach-bounded-linear-operator-limit-comparison}, is not produced
by \autoref{thm:banach-bounded-linear-operator-limit-witness-transport}, and
is not part of the carrier, classifier, stability, exactness, or ledger fields
listed by \autoref{thm:banach-bounded-linear-operator-limit-namecert-surface}.
Therefore it cannot participate in an accepted local admission or consumption
claim for this comparison. Every accepted use factors back to the displayed
finite witness and selector rows, as claimed.
\end{proof}
