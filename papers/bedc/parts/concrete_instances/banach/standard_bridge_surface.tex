\begin{definition}[Singleton Banach standard bridge surface]
\label{def:banach-singleton-standard-bridge-surface}
The standard comparison object for the singleton Banach certificate is the
one-point complete normed vector space $B_{\star}=\{\star\}$ over the one-point
scalar action, with zero vector $\star$, zero norm
$\lVert\star\rVert=0_{\mathsf{Real}}^{c}$, zero metric
$d_{\star}(\star,\star)=0_{\mathsf{Real}}^{c}$, and the unique complete-metric
limit operation sending every regular Cauchy stream to $\star$.

The BEDC side is the singleton empty-history carrier of
\autoref{def:banach-singleton-empty-history-instance}. Its carrier is
$A_{\emp}$, its classifier is $\rho_{\emp}$, its norm and metric endpoints are
the constant RealUp zero endpoint, and its convergence rows are the displayed
$\mathsf{CompleteMetricLimitWitness}$ rows supplied by
\autoref{thm:banach-regular-cauchy-limit-witness}. The bridge maps every
carried BEDC vector history to $\star$, and maps $\star$ back to $\emp$ with
the carrier, norm, metric, and limit ledgers read through the singleton
classifier.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Singleton Banach bridge soundness]
\label{thm:banach-singleton-standard-bridge-soundness}
Every carried BEDC singleton Banach row of
\autoref{def:banach-singleton-empty-history-instance} maps to the unique point
$\star$ of $B_{\star}$. Under this map, the displayed vector is classified by
$\rho_{\emp}$, its norm and induced metric distance are the constant RealUp
zero endpoint, and every regular Cauchy stream carries complete-metric
convergence data to the constant standard limit $\star$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First collapse the carried vector row. By
\autoref{thm:banach-singleton-empty-history-carrier-classifier}, every carried
Banach vector lies in $A_{\emp}$ and every pair of carried singleton vectors is
$\rho_{\emp}$-classified. The bridge therefore has only one possible standard
image, namely $\star$.

Next read the metric data. The norm-induced compatibility theorem
\autoref{thm:banach-norm-induced-metric-compatibility} identifies the
singleton Banach metric row with the constant RealUp zero endpoint exactly on
the same classifier row. Thus the standard norm and standard distance both
receive the zero value required by $B_{\star}$.

Finally transport the convergence ledger. For a regular Cauchy stream,
\autoref{thm:banach-regular-cauchy-limit-witness} supplies a displayed
$\mathsf{CompleteMetricLimitWitness}$ row whose selected limit, modulus
history, and late-distance rows are all singleton rows. Applying the bridge to
each component sends that row to the unique complete-metric limit datum of the
one-point standard object.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Singleton Banach bridge completeness]
\label{thm:banach-singleton-standard-bridge-completeness}
The unique standard point $\star$ maps back to $\emp$ and reconstructs the
singleton BEDC Banach surface: carrier $A_{\emp}$, classifier $\rho_{\emp}$,
constant zero norm, constant zero metric, and the singleton
$\mathsf{CompleteMetricLimitWitness}$ family.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the bridge surface of
\autoref{def:banach-singleton-standard-bridge-surface}. Its reverse map sends
the only standard point to $\emp$, and the BEDC carrier row is precisely the
singleton empty-history carrier of
\autoref{def:banach-singleton-empty-history-instance}.

The reconstructed completeness data are the singleton data. Apply
\autoref{thm:banach-singleton-empty-history-cauchy-completeness}: every
regular Cauchy stream in the singleton Banach metric has selected limit
$\emp$, and every distance to that limit is classified by the constant RealUp
zero endpoint.

It remains to identify the displayed limit row chosen by the bridge with any
other singleton limit presentation. Use
\autoref{thm:banach-singleton-limit-namecert-uniqueness}; it gives
$\hsame$-equality of singleton Banach limits, and the singleton classifier
repackages that equality as $\rho_{\emp}$. Hence the reverse image reconstructs
the same carrier, norm, metric, and limit witness surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Singleton Banach bridge roundtrip]
\label{thm:banach-singleton-standard-bridge-roundtrip}
For the bridge of
\autoref{def:banach-singleton-standard-bridge-surface}, the composite
BEDC-to-standard-to-BEDC is $\rho_{\emp}$ on carried singleton histories, and
the composite standard-to-BEDC-to-standard is the identity on the one-point
complete normed vector space $B_{\star}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the BEDC composite, start with a carried singleton Banach history. The
soundness theorem
\autoref{thm:banach-singleton-standard-bridge-soundness} sends it to the
unique standard point $\star$, and the completeness theorem
\autoref{thm:banach-singleton-standard-bridge-completeness} sends $\star$ back
to $\emp$ with the singleton carrier and zero ledgers.

The classifier comparison is exactly the singleton collapse. By
\autoref{thm:banach-singleton-empty-history-carrier-classifier}, every carried
source history is $\rho_{\emp}$-classified with $\emp$, so the BEDC composite
acts as $\rho_{\emp}$ on carried histories.

For the standard composite, there is only one point of $B_{\star}$. The reverse
map selects $\emp$, and soundness maps that BEDC point back to $\star$. Since
$\star$ is the unique standard point, the composite is the identity on the
one-point complete normed vector space.
\end{proof}
