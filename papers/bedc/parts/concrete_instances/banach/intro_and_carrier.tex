\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\BanachUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-banach-namecert}
\origin{human}

The Banach-space interface $\BanachUp$ packages a normed vector space carrier together with a regular Cauchy completeness witness in the metric induced by the norm. It depends on $\NameCert_{\NormUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-norm-namecert}) and the real-completeness capstone (\autoref{ch:visions-real-completeness}), and supplies the substrate for $\NameCert_{\HilbertUp}$ and the operator certificates downstream. The concrete Banach carrier reads its vector source through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-vecspace-namecert}, its norm through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-norm-namecert}, its induced distance through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, and its Cauchy-limit and completeness row through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completion-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completemetric-namecert} before operator or limit rows are exported downstream.

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:banach-carrier}

\begin{definition}[Concrete Banach carrier]\label{def:banach-concrete-carrier}
A concrete Banach carrier is a history predicate $X:\Hist\to\mathsf{Prop}$
together with the $\NormUp$ carrier and classifier data supplied by
$\NameCert_{\NormUp}$, a displayed norm value row for each carried history,
and the metric predicate induced by that norm:
$$
\mathsf{BanachMetricWitness}_X(x,y,d)
$$
means that $x$ and $y$ are carried by $X$, their difference history is a
$\NormUp$ vector-history row, and $d$ is the $\RealUp$ norm value assigned to
that difference. The point classifier of the carrier is not a quotient:
carried representatives are identified by $\hsame$ transport of the displayed
vector, norm-value, and metric rows.

The completeness field is a family which assigns, to every regular Cauchy
$\StreamNameUp$ stream $s$ over $X$ for the norm-induced metric, histories
$\mu_s,\ell_s,L_s$ such that
$$
\mathsf{CompleteMetricLimitWitness}(X,s,\mu_s,\ell_s,L_s)
$$
holds. Thus the Banach datum is the tuple consisting of the carried
$\NormUp$ structure, the induced metric ledger, the $\hsame$ classifier on
representatives, and the displayed limit-witness family for all regular
Cauchy stream names over $X$.
\end{definition}
\leandef{BEDC.Derived.BanachUp.BanachConcreteCarrier}

\begin{definition}[Complete normed-history Banach carrier]
\label{def:banach-complete-normed-carrier}
A $\BanachUp$ candidate is a $\VecSpaceUp$ carrier $C_V$ with vector
classifier $\sim_V$, zero endpoint $0_V$, additive difference operation
$x-_V y$, and scalar source read over $\NameCert_{\RealUp}$, together with a
$\NormUp$ norm
$$
\nu:C_V\to \RealUp
$$
whose classifier is the RealUp classifier. Its induced distance predicate is
the RealUp value
$$
d_\nu(x,y):=\nu(x-_V y)
$$
on carried vector endpoints $x,y:C_V$. A regular Cauchy completion witness for
the candidate is a row assigning to every regular Cauchy sequence
$(X,\mu):\mathbb{N}\to C_V$ for $d_\nu$ a carried limit endpoint
$\lim_\nu(X,\mu):C_V$, a convergence modulus, and a uniqueness certificate up
to $\sim_V$.

The BEDC source is $\NameCert_{\NormUp}$ together with the real-completeness
capstone of \autoref{ch:visions-real-completeness}. The carrier choice is
$C_V$, the classifier is $\sim_V$, the stability obligations are norm transport
and Cauchy-sequence transport across $\sim_V$, the ledger records all moduli and
proof-index data used by the norm and completion rows, and the status is
$\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Singleton empty-history Banach carrier]
\label{def:banach-singleton-empty-history-instance}
The concrete $\BanachUp$ carrier is the singleton empty-history norm instance
of \autoref{def:singleton-empty-history-norm-instance}. Its vector carrier is
$A_{\emp}$, its vector classifier is $\rho_{\emp}$, its norm is
$\nu_{\emp}$, and its metric distance is the constant RealUp zero endpoint
$$
d_{\emp}(m,n):=0_{\mathsf{Real}}^{c}
$$
for all displayed vector histories $m,n:\mathsf{BHist}$. The metric
classifier on all displayed distances is
$\mathsf{RealConstantHistoryClassifier}$.

A certified regular Cauchy stream $s$ in this metric is assigned the limit
$\emp$. Its convergence witness sends every requested real precision to the
same singleton carrier endpoint: each term of $s$ is $\rho_{\emp}$-classified
with $\emp$, and each displayed distance to the limit is
$\mathsf{RealConstantHistoryClassifier}$-classified with
$0_{\mathsf{Real}}^{c}$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Banach singleton carrier semantic NameCert]
\label{thm:banach-singleton-carrier-semantic-namecert}
The singleton Banach carrier of
\autoref{def:banach-singleton-empty-history-instance}, equipped with the
classifier which relates two carried histories exactly when their carried
rows are present and the histories are $\hsame$-classified, forms a
$\mathsf{SemanticNameCert}$ for the singleton Banach surface. Its inhabited
carrier is witnessed by $\emp$; its reflexive, symmetric, and transitive
classifier fields are the corresponding $\hsame$ rows; its carrier transport
field is the carrier projection of the classifier triple; and its pattern and
ledger soundness fields are identity reads of the carried singleton Banach
rows.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.BanachUp.BanachSingletonCarrier\_semanticNameCert}
\begin{proof}
Use \autoref{def:banach-singleton-empty-history-instance} to read the carried
object as the singleton empty-history norm carrier together with its
norm-induced empty-distance metric row. The inhabited carrier row is the
history $\emp$, with its singleton vector row and the
$\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}(\emp,\emp,\emp)$ row supplied by empty-distance
exactness.

The classifier row is the carried pair together with $\hsame$. Hence
reflexivity, symmetry, and transitivity are the corresponding $\hsame$
operations, with the carried rows retained on each side of the comparison.
Carrier preservation is projection from the classifier triple: an accepted
comparison already contains both endpoint carrier witnesses.

Finally apply
\autoref{thm:banach-singleton-empty-history-carrier-classifier}. It identifies
the singleton Banach carrier, classifier, norm rows, and constant-zero metric
rows. The pattern and ledger soundness clauses therefore read back the same
carried singleton rows, with no additional transport beyond the displayed
$\hsame$ comparison and the empty-distance metric witness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Banach norm-induced metric compatibility]
\label{thm:banach-norm-induced-metric-compatibility}
For the singleton empty-history $\NormUp$ instance of
\autoref{def:singleton-empty-history-norm-instance}, read through the
singleton metric carrier and the relation
$\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}$, the induced Banach metric row is the
constant RealUp zero endpoint row. Concretely, for carried vector histories
$m,n:\mathsf{BHist}$, the Banach distance between $m$ and $n$ is classified by
$0_{\mathsf{Real}}^{c}$ exactly when
$$
\rho_{\emp}(m,n)
$$
holds.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.BanachUp.BanachSingleton\_metric\_distance\_empty\_iff\_classifier}
\begin{proof}
First identify the singleton vector endpoints. By
\autoref{thm:singleton-empty-history-norm-carrier-classifier}, any two carried
vectors in the singleton norm instance are related by $\rho_{\emp}$, and their
constant norm endpoints are related by the RealUp constant-history
classifier.

The zero side is exact by
\autoref{thm:singleton-empty-history-norm-zero-exactness}. Thus the displayed
norm value of the singleton difference is classified by
$0_{\mathsf{Real}}^{c}$ exactly when the underlying vector endpoint is
classified with the singleton zero vector.

Finally read the same endpoint row through the metric carrier. The metric row
with empty distance is equivalent, by
\autoref{thm:metric-distance-witness-empty-distance-iff}, to both endpoints
being $\hsame$ to $\emp$. The singleton vector classifier is precisely the
collapse relation on those carried endpoints, so the norm-zero row and the
empty-distance metric row classify the same pairs $m,n$.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Concrete Banach downstream coverage]\label{lem:banach-downstream-coverage}
For the concrete $\BanachUp$ packet whose complete normed carrier is specified in
\autoref{def:banach-complete-normed-carrier} and whose singleton semantic
certificate is supplied by
\autoref{thm:banach-singleton-carrier-semantic-namecert}, every accepted local
consumer visible before a Hilbert, bounded-operator, or limit export is
exhausted by the following displayed Banach rows:
$$
C_V,\ \sim_V,\ 0_V,\ x-_V y,\ \nu,\ d_\nu(x,y),\
\mathsf{BanachMetricWitness},\ \lim_\nu,\ \text{modulus},\ \text{uniqueness},\
\text{and the ledger rows}.
$$
In the singleton instance these rows reduce to $\emp$, $\rho_{\emp}$,
$\nu_{\emp}$, the constant zero distance $d_{\emp}$, the
$\mathsf{RealConstantHistoryClassifier}$ row, and the identity pattern and
ledger reads of the semantic NameCert. Consequently a request for an anonymous
metric row, host normed-space equality, quotient representative, selected limit
outside the completion row, compactness argument, choice principle, ordinary
non-singleton Banach bridge, or off-packet proof channel is not an accepted
consumer of this BEDC packet.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the complete normed carrier in
\autoref{def:banach-complete-normed-carrier}. Its coordinates are exactly the
vector carrier $C_V$, the classifier $\sim_V$, the zero endpoint $0_V$, the
difference operation $x-_V y$, the scalar source over $\NameCert_{\RealUp}$,
the norm $\nu$, and the norm-induced distance
$$
d_\nu(x,y):=\nu(x-_V y).
$$
The same definition also names the completion surface: for each regular Cauchy
stream over $d_\nu$ it supplies a carried limit $\lim_\nu$, a convergence
modulus, uniqueness up to $\sim_V$, and a ledger containing the norm rows,
stream observations, moduli, proof indices, and rational comparison data.
Thus a local consumer whose input is the complete normed carrier can only read
one of these carrier, metric, completion, or ledger coordinates.

Now specialize this carrier to the singleton surface certified in
\autoref{thm:banach-singleton-carrier-semantic-namecert}. The theorem,
together with the transport theorem \autoref{thm:banach-hsame-transport}, reads
the inhabited carrier as $\emp$, the classifier fields as the displayed
$\hsame$ rows, carrier transport as projection from the classifier triple, and
pattern and ledger soundness as identity reads of the carried singleton Banach
rows. Hence every accepted NameCert consumer is a read of the same singleton
carrier, classifier, norm, metric, stream, modulus, limit, or ledger row; there
is no additional semantic field through which a different analytic object could
enter.

The finite alternatives in the statement are therefore just the possible local
read positions of the two cited objects. A carrier or classifier consumer lands
in $C_V,\sim_V,0_V,x-_V y$ and, in the singleton case, in $\emp$ and
$\rho_{\emp}$. A metric consumer lands in $\nu$, $d_\nu$, and the displayed
$\mathsf{BanachMetricWitness}$ row, which becomes $\nu_{\emp}$ and the constant
zero distance $d_{\emp}$. A completion or limit consumer lands in the regular
Cauchy stream row, selected limit, modulus, uniqueness certificate, and ledger
rows named by the complete normed carrier, which the singleton semantic
certificate reads back by identity.

All excluded alternatives ask for a coordinate not present in this list. An
anonymous metric would bypass $d_\nu$ and
$\mathsf{BanachMetricWitness}$; host equality or quotient representatives would
bypass $\sim_V$, $\rho_{\emp}$, and $\hsame$; a selected limit outside the
completion row would bypass $\lim_\nu$ and its modulus and uniqueness ledger;
compactness, choice, an ordinary non-singleton Banach bridge, or an off-packet
proof channel would add a proof source not named by either the complete normed
carrier or the singleton semantic NameCert. Since acceptance at this local
surface is acceptance through those displayed rows, such requests remain
outside the BEDC packet.
\end{proof}
