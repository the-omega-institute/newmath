\begin{definition}[Banach bounded linear operator composition carrier]
\label{def:banach-bounded-linear-operator-composition-carrier}
Let $C,D,E$ be $\BanachUp$ candidates with vector carriers $C_V,D_V,E_V$,
classifiers $\sim_C,\sim_D,\sim_E$, norms $\nu_C,\nu_D,\nu_E$, and
norm-induced distances $d_C,d_D,d_E$. Given carried rows
$$
\mathsf{BanachBLOp}(C,D,T,K,\Lambda)
$$
and
$$
\mathsf{BanachBLOp}(D,E,S,L,\Gamma),
$$
their composition carrier is
$$
\mathsf{BanachBLOp}(C,E,S\circ T,L\cdot K,\Gamma\star\Lambda).
$$
Here $S\circ T$ is the endpoint map $x\mapsto S(Tx)$, the RealUp bound row is
the product endpoint $L\cdot K$, and the visible ledger
$\Gamma\star\Lambda$ is the ordered concatenation of the source-to-middle
operator rows, the middle-to-target operator rows, and the RealUp
multiplication and order rows used to compare the composite bound.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Bounded linear operator composition Lipschitz]
\label{thm:banach-bounded-linear-operator-composition-lipschitz}
For $\BanachUp$ candidates $C,D,E$, carried rows
$\mathsf{BanachBLOp}(C,D,T,K,\Lambda)$ and
$\mathsf{BanachBLOp}(D,E,S,L,\Gamma)$ satisfy the composite estimate
$$
d_E(S(Tx),S(Ty))\le (L\cdot K)\cdot d_C(x,y)
$$
for all carried source endpoints $x,y:C_V$. The distance rows are the
norm-induced rows of \autoref{thm:banach-metric-induced-by-norm}, and the
final comparison is the RealUp multiplication/order row associating the two
operator bounds with the source distance.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:banach-bounded-linear-operator-lipschitz} to the carried
row $\mathsf{BanachBLOp}(D,E,S,L,\Gamma)$ and the middle endpoints $Tx,Ty$.
This gives the target estimate
$$
d_E(S(Tx),S(Ty))\le L\cdot d_D(Tx,Ty).
$$

Apply \autoref{thm:banach-bounded-linear-operator-lipschitz} again to
$\mathsf{BanachBLOp}(C,D,T,K,\Lambda)$ and the source endpoints $x,y$. The
middle distance is bounded by
$$
d_D(Tx,Ty)\le K\cdot d_C(x,y).
$$

Use the nonnegative bound rows for $L$ and $K$, monotonicity of RealUp
multiplication, and associativity of the displayed product endpoint. The
ordered comparison transports
$L\cdot d_D(Tx,Ty)$ across the second estimate and rewrites the resulting
$L\cdot(K\cdot d_C(x,y))$ as $(L\cdot K)\cdot d_C(x,y)$. Repacking the two
operator ledgers gives the displayed composite Lipschitz row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Bounded linear operator composition preserves regular Cauchy streams]
\label{thm:banach-bounded-linear-operator-composition-cauchy-transport}
For $\BanachUp$ candidates $C,D,E$, carried rows
$\mathsf{BanachBLOp}(C,D,T,K,\Lambda)$ and
$\mathsf{BanachBLOp}(D,E,S,L,\Gamma)$ transport every regular Cauchy
$\StreamNameUp$ stream over $C$ to a regular Cauchy $\StreamNameUp$ stream
over $E$ by pointwise application of $S\circ T$. The transported observation
rows and modulus ledger are the result of first applying the $T$ transport
and then applying the $S$ transport.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a regular Cauchy stream $(X,\mu):\mathbb{N}\to C_V$. Apply
\autoref{thm:banach-bounded-linear-operator-cauchy-transport} to
$\mathsf{BanachBLOp}(C,D,T,K,\Lambda)$. The result is the pointwise stream
$T\circ X$ over $D$, with carried observation rows and a modulus transformed
through the RealUp bound row for $K$.

Apply \autoref{thm:banach-bounded-linear-operator-cauchy-transport} a second
time to $\mathsf{BanachBLOp}(D,E,S,L,\Gamma)$ and the transported middle
stream. The result is the stream $S\circ(T\circ X)$ over $E$, with its
observation rows transported through the target endpoint fields of
$\Gamma$.

Finally identify $S\circ(T\circ X)$ with $(S\circ T)\circ X$ by endpoint
definition, and concatenate the two visible ledgers. The RealUp
multiplication/order rows combine the two modulus transformations into the
single composite bound $L\cdot K$, so the final stream is regular Cauchy for
the target distance $d_E$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Banach bounded linear operator composition NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:banach-bounded-linear-operator-composition-namecert-obligations}
For $\BanachUp$ candidates $C,D,E$, the carrier of
\autoref{def:banach-bounded-linear-operator-composition-carrier}, together
with
\autoref{thm:banach-bounded-linear-operator-composition-lipschitz} and
\autoref{thm:banach-bounded-linear-operator-composition-cauchy-transport},
assembles the paper-side $\NameCert$ obligation set for composed Banach
bounded linear operators. The carrier is
$\mathsf{BanachBLOp}(C,E,S\circ T,L\cdot K,\Gamma\star\Lambda)$, the
classifier is componentwise $\hsame$ transport of maps and ledgers together
with RealUp transport of the product bound, stability is inherited through
the two operator carriers, exactness is the equivalence between the displayed
composite rows and the two-stage endpoint action, and the ledger records the
source, middle, target, classifier, compatibility, bound, and stream rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier field is exactly
\autoref{def:banach-bounded-linear-operator-composition-carrier}. Its endpoint
map is $S\circ T$, its bound endpoint is $L\cdot K$, and its ledger is the
ordered concatenation $\Gamma\star\Lambda$.

Classifier transport is obtained in two stages. The first operator row moves
source classifier comparisons through $T$, and the second operator row moves
the resulting middle comparisons through $S$. RealUp classifier transport
keeps the product bound $L\cdot K$ stable, while componentwise $\hsame$
transport keeps the concatenated ledger aligned with the two endpoint maps.

The Lipschitz field is
\autoref{thm:banach-bounded-linear-operator-composition-lipschitz}, and the
regular-Cauchy field is
\autoref{thm:banach-bounded-linear-operator-composition-cauchy-transport}.
These are the metric and stream obligations required for downstream operator
certificates.

Exactness follows by unfolding the composite carrier. Any accepted composite
row contains precisely the two accepted operator rows, the composed endpoint
map, the RealUp product bound, and the visible concatenated ledger. Conversely,
those fields repack as
$\mathsf{BanachBLOp}(C,E,S\circ T,L\cdot K,\Gamma\star\Lambda)$, so the
composition surface adds no host-level quotient or unrecorded operation row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Banach bounded linear operator composition closure]
\label{thm:banach-bounded-linear-operator-composition-closure}
Let $C,D,E$ be $\BanachUp$ candidates with vector carriers $C_V,D_V,E_V$,
classifiers $\sim_C,\sim_D,\sim_E$, norms $\nu_C,\nu_D,\nu_E$, and
norm-induced distances $d_C,d_D,d_E$. If
$$
\mathsf{BanachBLOp}(C,D,T,K,\Lambda)
$$
and
$$
\mathsf{BanachBLOp}(D,E,S,L,\Gamma)
$$
are carried, then the endpoint composite $S\circ T:C_V\to E_V$ is carried by
$$
\mathsf{BanachBLOp}(C,E,S\circ T,L\cdot K,\Gamma\star\Lambda).
$$
The composed ledger $\Gamma\star\Lambda$ records the two operator ledgers and
the RealUp multiplication, monotonicity, associativity, and non-negativity
rows needed for the product bound $L\cdot K$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.BanachUp.BanachSingletonBoundedLinearOperator\_composition\_closure}
\begin{proof}
Unfold the operator carrier of
\autoref{def:banach-bounded-linear-operator-carrier}. For a carried source
endpoint $x:C_V$, the target endpoint row of $\Lambda$ carries $T(x):D_V$.
The target endpoint row of $\Gamma$ then carries $S(Tx):E_V$, which is the
endpoint value of $(S\circ T)(x)$.

For classifier-respect, suppose $x\sim_C x'$ with both source endpoints
carried. The classifier row of $\Lambda$ gives $T(x)\sim_D T(x')$. Applying
the classifier row of $\Gamma$ gives $S(Tx)\sim_E S(Tx')$, which is the
classifier row for the composite endpoint map.

For additivity, let $x,y:C_V$ be carried. The additive row of $\Lambda$ gives
$$
T(x+_C y)\sim_D T(x)+_D T(y).
$$
Both middle endpoints in this comparison are carried by the $D$ vector
carrier, so the classifier-respect row of $\Gamma$ transports it to
$$
S(T(x+_C y))\sim_E S(T(x)+_D T(y)).
$$
The additive row of $\Gamma$, applied to the carried middle endpoints $T(x)$
and $T(y)$, gives
$$
S(T(x)+_D T(y))\sim_E S(Tx)+_E S(Ty).
$$
Transitivity of the target vector classifier yields
$$
(S\circ T)(x+_C y)\sim_E (S\circ T)(x)+_E(S\circ T)(y).
$$

For homogeneity, let $a$ be a carried RealUp scalar endpoint and let
$x:C_V$ be carried. The scalar row of $\Lambda$ gives
$$
T(a\cdot_C x)\sim_D a\cdot_D T(x).
$$
Transport this comparison through the classifier row of $\Gamma$, then apply
the scalar row of $\Gamma$ to the carried middle endpoint $T(x)$. The target
classifier transitivity row gives
$$
(S\circ T)(a\cdot_C x)\sim_E a\cdot_E(S\circ T)(x).
$$

It remains to verify the product bound. The non-negativity rows in the two
operator carriers give $K\ge 0$ and $L\ge 0$, so the RealUp multiplication row
carries $L\cdot K\ge 0$. For a carried source endpoint $x$, the bound row of
$\Gamma$ applied to the carried middle endpoint $T(x)$ gives
$$
\nu_E(S(Tx))\le L\cdot\nu_D(Tx).
$$
The bound row of $\Lambda$ gives
$$
\nu_D(Tx)\le K\cdot\nu_C(x).
$$
Using $L\ge 0$, RealUp multiplication monotonicity transports the second
comparison to
$$
L\cdot\nu_D(Tx)\le L\cdot(K\cdot\nu_C(x)).
$$
RealUp associativity identifies the right-hand side with
$(L\cdot K)\cdot\nu_C(x)$. Composing the displayed inequalities gives
$$
\nu_E((S\circ T)(x))\le (L\cdot K)\cdot\nu_C(x),
$$
which is the bound row required for
$\mathsf{BanachBLOp}(C,E,S\circ T,L\cdot K,\Gamma\star\Lambda)$.

The ledger clause is exactly the ordered concatenation described in
\autoref{def:banach-bounded-linear-operator-composition-carrier}: it keeps the
source-to-middle endpoint, classifier, linearity, and bound rows from
$\Lambda$, the middle-to-target rows from $\Gamma$, and the RealUp product
rows used above. These rows are precisely the fields of
\autoref{def:banach-bounded-linear-operator-carrier} for the composite
endpoint map, so the displayed composite operator carrier is carried.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Banach bounded linear operator composition associativity]
\label{thm:banach-bounded-linear-operator-composition-associative}
Let $B,C,D,E$ be $\BanachUp$ candidates with vector carriers
$B_V,C_V,D_V,E_V$ and carried bounded linear operator rows
$$
\mathsf{BanachBLOp}(B,C,T,K,\Lambda),
$$
$$
\mathsf{BanachBLOp}(C,D,S,L,\Gamma),
$$
and
$$
\mathsf{BanachBLOp}(D,E,R,M,\Omega).
$$
Then the two iterated composite carriers
$$
\mathsf{BanachBLOp}(B,E,(R\circ S)\circ T,(M\cdot L)\cdot K,
(\Omega\star\Gamma)\star\Lambda)
$$
and
$$
\mathsf{BanachBLOp}(B,E,R\circ(S\circ T),M\cdot(L\cdot K),
\Omega\star(\Gamma\star\Lambda))
$$
classify the same carried Banach bounded linear operator row after endpoint
composition associativity identifies $((R\circ S)\circ T)(x)$ with
$(R\circ(S\circ T))(x)$ for every carried source endpoint $x$, RealUp
multiplication associativity identifies the two bound endpoints, and ledger
associativity identifies the two ordered concatenations of the
source-to-middle, middle-to-target, and target-to-final rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First form the left-associated carrier. Apply
\autoref{thm:banach-bounded-linear-operator-composition-closure} to the rows
for $S$ and $R$, obtaining
$\mathsf{BanachBLOp}(C,E,R\circ S,M\cdot L,\Omega\star\Gamma)$. Applying the
same closure theorem to this row and the row for $T$ gives the displayed
carrier with endpoint map $(R\circ S)\circ T$, bound $(M\cdot L)\cdot K$, and
ledger $(\Omega\star\Gamma)\star\Lambda$.

Next form the right-associated carrier. Apply
\autoref{thm:banach-bounded-linear-operator-composition-closure} to the rows
for $T$ and $S$, obtaining
$\mathsf{BanachBLOp}(B,D,S\circ T,L\cdot K,\Gamma\star\Lambda)$. Applying the
closure theorem to this row and the row for $R$ gives the displayed carrier
with endpoint map $R\circ(S\circ T)$, bound $M\cdot(L\cdot K)$, and ledger
$\Omega\star(\Gamma\star\Lambda)$.

Unfold the composition carrier of
\autoref{def:banach-bounded-linear-operator-composition-carrier}. For a
carried source endpoint $x:B_V$, both endpoint maps compute to $R(S(Tx))$.
Thus the endpoint classifiers compare the same target endpoint after
definitionally reassociating composition.

The metric and stream fields are the same obligations in the two bracketings.
\autoref{thm:banach-bounded-linear-operator-composition-lipschitz} supplies
the composite Lipschitz row at each binary step, and RealUp multiplication
associativity rewrites $((M\cdot L)\cdot K)\cdot d_B(x,y)$ to
$(M\cdot(L\cdot K))\cdot d_B(x,y)$. Likewise,
\autoref{thm:banach-bounded-linear-operator-composition-cauchy-transport}
transports regular Cauchy streams by pointwise application of the same
endpoint map $x\mapsto R(S(Tx))$.

Finally, the visible ledgers contain exactly the rows from $\Lambda$,
$\Gamma$, and $\Omega$ together with the RealUp product and order rows used
by the two applications of composition closure. Ledger associativity
identifies $(\Omega\star\Gamma)\star\Lambda$ with
$\Omega\star(\Gamma\star\Lambda)$ as the same ordered source-middle-target
record, so the two displayed carriers classify the same Banach bounded linear
operator row.
\end{proof}
