\begin{definition}[Banach bounded linear operator carrier]
\label{def:banach-bounded-linear-operator-carrier}
Let $C$ and $D$ be $\BanachUp$ candidates with vector carriers $C_V,D_V$,
classifiers $\sim_C,\sim_D$, norms $\nu_C,\nu_D$, and induced distances
$d_C,d_D$. A Banach bounded linear operator carrier from $C$ to $D$ is a BEDC
history predicate
$$
\mathsf{BanachBLOp}(C,D,T,K,\Lambda)
$$
on endpoint maps $T:C_V\to D_V$, RealUp bound endpoints $K$, and visible
ledger histories $\Lambda$ such that the following rows are carried.
\begin{enumerate}
\item The map respects vector classifiers: if $x\sim_C x'$ and both
endpoints are carried by $C_V$, then $T(x)\sim_D T(x')$.
\item The map respects the vector operations:
$T(x+_C y)\sim_D T(x)+_D T(y)$ and
$T(a\cdot_C x)\sim_D a\cdot_D T(x)$ for every carried RealUp scalar endpoint
$a$ and carried source endpoints $x,y$.
\item The RealUp bound row records $K\ge 0$ and
$$
\nu_D(T(x))\le K\cdot\nu_C(x)
$$
for every carried source endpoint $x:C_V$.
\item The ledger $\Lambda$ records the source endpoint row, target endpoint
row, source and target norm rows, scalar row, additive and homogeneity
compatibility rows, and the bound witness used for each carried input.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier on operator representatives is $\hsame$ transport of the
endpoint map together with $\RealUp$ transport of the bound and componentwise
transport of the ledger rows.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Bounded linear operator Lipschitz obligation]
\label{thm:banach-bounded-linear-operator-lipschitz}
For $\BanachUp$ candidates $C,D$, any carried row
$\mathsf{BanachBLOp}(C,D,T,K,\Lambda)$ satisfies the metric Lipschitz
obligation
$$
d_D(Tx,Ty)\le K\cdot d_C(x,y)
$$
for all carried source endpoints $x,y:C_V$. The distance rows are the
norm-induced rows of \autoref{thm:banach-metric-induced-by-norm}.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.BanachUp.BanachSingletonBoundedLinearOperator\_lipschitz}
\begin{proof}
First read the two distances through
\autoref{thm:banach-metric-induced-by-norm}. The source distance is the
RealUp norm endpoint $\nu_C(x-_C y)$, and the target distance is
$\nu_D(Tx-_D Ty)$.

Use the additive compatibility row in
\autoref{def:banach-bounded-linear-operator-carrier}. It transports the target
difference $Tx-_D Ty$ to the endpoint $T(x-_C y)$ under the target vector
classifier, and the $\NormUp$ transport row moves $\nu_D$ across that
classifier comparison.

Apply the bound row of the operator carrier to the carried source difference
$x-_C y$. This gives
$$
\nu_D(T(x-_C y))\le K\cdot\nu_C(x-_C y).
$$
Substituting the norm-induced distance rows gives the displayed Lipschitz
obligation.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Bounded linear operator preserves regular Cauchy streams]
\label{thm:banach-bounded-linear-operator-cauchy-transport}
Let $C,D$ be $\BanachUp$ candidates and let
$\mathsf{BanachBLOp}(C,D,T,K,\Lambda)$ be carried. If
$(X,\mu):\mathbb{N}\to C_V$ is a regular Cauchy $\StreamNameUp$ stream for the
source distance $d_C$, then the pointwise stream $T\circ X$ is a regular
Cauchy $\StreamNameUp$ stream for the target distance $d_D$. Its carried
observation rows are obtained by applying the target endpoint field of
$\Lambda$, and its modulus is obtained from $\mu$ by feeding each target
tolerance through the RealUp bound comparison for $K$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The observation rows are immediate from the operator ledger: for each carried
source endpoint $X_n$, the target endpoint row records that $T(X_n)$ is
carried by $D_V$. Classifier-respect transports any source stream
identifications to target stream identifications, so the pointwise stream is
accepted by the target vector classifier.

It remains to show the Cauchy estimate. For a requested positive target
tolerance, use the RealUp order and multiplication rows to choose a positive
source tolerance whose product with $K$ is bounded by the requested target
tolerance. Feed that source tolerance into the source modulus $\mu$.

For indices past the returned bound, the source Cauchy row gives a bound on
$d_C(X_m,X_n)$. Apply
\autoref{thm:banach-bounded-linear-operator-lipschitz} to $X_m$ and $X_n$,
then compose the RealUp inequalities. The resulting target distance row is
$$
d_D(TX_m,TX_n)\le K\cdot d_C(X_m,X_n),
$$
and the chosen tolerance comparison turns it into the regular Cauchy row for
the transported stream.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Banach bounded linear operator pointwise sum closure]
\label{thm:banach-bounded-linear-operator-pointwise-sum-closure}
Let $C,D$ be $\BanachUp$ candidates as in
\autoref{def:banach-complete-normed-carrier}, with vector carriers $C_V,D_V$,
classifiers $\sim_C,\sim_D$, target addition $+_D$, scalar action
$\cdot_D$, norms $\nu_C,\nu_D$, and norm-induced distances $d_C,d_D$. If
$$
\mathsf{BanachBLOp}(C,D,T,K,\Lambda)
$$
and
$$
\mathsf{BanachBLOp}(C,D,S,L,\Gamma)
$$
are carried, then the pointwise sum map of
\autoref{def:banach-bounded-linear-operator-pointwise-sum-carrier} is carried
by
$$
\mathsf{BanachBLOp}(C,D,T\boxplus_D S,K+L,\Lambda\boxplus\Gamma).
$$
For every carried source endpoint $x:C_V$, its bound row is the
$\RealAlgOrder$ additive-order composite
$$
\nu_D((T\boxplus_D S)(x))\le (K+L)\cdot\nu_C(x),
$$
and the displayed operator-bound endpoint is classified by the RealUp order
with the scalar sum of the two input bounds:
$$
K+L\le K+L.
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold the operator carrier of
\autoref{def:banach-bounded-linear-operator-carrier}. For a carried source
endpoint $x:C_V$, the target endpoint rows of $\Lambda$ and $\Gamma$ carry
$T(x)$ and $S(x)$ in $D_V$. The target additive carrier row from the
$\AbGroupUp$ reduct of the target vector space carries
$$
(T\boxplus_D S)(x)=T(x)+_D S(x),
$$
so the endpoint row required for the sum map is present.

For classifier-respect, suppose $x\sim_Cx'$ with all source endpoints carried.
The classifier rows in $\Lambda$ and $\Gamma$ give
$$
T(x)\sim_D T(x')
\qquad\text{and}\qquad
S(x)\sim_D S(x').
$$
Target additive congruence transports these two comparisons to
$$
T(x)+_D S(x)\sim_D T(x')+_D S(x'),
$$
which is exactly
$$
(T\boxplus_D S)(x)\sim_D(T\boxplus_D S)(x').
$$

For additivity, let $x,y:C_V$ be carried. The additive rows of the two input
operator ledgers give
$$
T(x+_C y)\sim_D T(x)+_D T(y)
$$
and
$$
S(x+_C y)\sim_D S(x)+_D S(y).
$$
Applying target additive congruence to these two comparisons gives
$$
T(x+_C y)+_D S(x+_C y)
\sim_D
(T(x)+_D T(y))+_D(S(x)+_D S(y)).
$$
The target $\AbGroupUp$ associativity and commutativity rows reorder the four
carried summands as
$$
(T(x)+_D T(y))+_D(S(x)+_D S(y))
\sim_D
(T(x)+_D S(x))+_D(T(y)+_D S(y)).
$$
Transitivity of $\sim_D$ yields
$$
(T\boxplus_D S)(x+_C y)
\sim_D
(T\boxplus_D S)(x)+_D(T\boxplus_D S)(y).
$$

For homogeneity, let $a$ be a carried RealUp scalar endpoint and let
$x:C_V$ be carried. The homogeneous rows of $\Lambda$ and $\Gamma$ give
$$
T(a\cdot_C x)\sim_D a\cdot_D T(x)
$$
and
$$
S(a\cdot_C x)\sim_D a\cdot_D S(x).
$$
Target additive congruence combines them as
$$
T(a\cdot_C x)+_D S(a\cdot_C x)
\sim_D
(a\cdot_D T(x))+_D(a\cdot_D S(x)).
$$
The scalar-distributivity row of the target vector-space structure classifies
the right endpoint with
$$
a\cdot_D(T(x)+_D S(x)).
$$
Composing these comparisons gives
$$
(T\boxplus_D S)(a\cdot_C x)
\sim_D
 a\cdot_D(T\boxplus_D S)(x).
$$

It remains to verify the bound row. The non-negativity rows in the two
operator carriers give $K\ge0$ and $L\ge0$. The $\RealAlgOrder$ additive
order row carries $K+L\ge0$, so the proposed sum bound is non-negative.
For a carried source endpoint $x:C_V$, the target $\NormUp$ triangle row gives
$$
\nu_D(T(x)+_D S(x))\le \nu_D(T(x))+\nu_D(S(x)).
$$
The two input bound rows give
$$
\nu_D(T(x))\le K\cdot\nu_C(x)
$$
and
$$
\nu_D(S(x))\le L\cdot\nu_C(x).
$$
The additive order-transport row of $\RealAlgOrder$ adds these two comparisons:
$$
\nu_D(T(x))+\nu_D(S(x))
\le
(K\cdot\nu_C(x))+(L\cdot\nu_C(x)).
$$
The RealUp distributivity row rewrites the right endpoint as
$$
(K\cdot\nu_C(x))+(L\cdot\nu_C(x))
\sim_{\RealUp}
(K+L)\cdot\nu_C(x).
$$
Order transport across this RealUp classifier comparison and transitivity with
the triangle inequality give
$$
\nu_D((T\boxplus_D S)(x))\le (K+L)\cdot\nu_C(x).
$$

The bound-classification statement is the RealUp order reflexivity row at the
same addition endpoint $K+L$, after the $\RealAlgOrder$ addition row has
carried that endpoint from the displayed input bounds $K$ and $L$:
$$
K+L\le K+L.
$$
The endpoint, classifier, additive, homogeneous, non-negative, bound, and
ledger rows just assembled are precisely the rows required by
\autoref{def:banach-bounded-linear-operator-carrier}. Repacking them with the
pointwise sum endpoint map, the bound endpoint $K+L$, and the visible ledger
$\Lambda\boxplus\Gamma$ yields the carried row
$$
\mathsf{BanachBLOp}(C,D,T\boxplus_D S,K+L,\Lambda\boxplus\Gamma).
$$
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Banach bounded linear operator scalar multiple closure]
\label{thm:banach-bounded-linear-operator-scalar-multiple-closure}
Let $C,D$ be $\BanachUp$ candidates as in \autoref{def:banach-complete-normed-carrier}, with vector carriers $C_V,D_V$, classifiers $\sim_C,\sim_D$, target scalar action $\cdot_D$, norms $\nu_C,\nu_D$, and norm-induced distances $d_C,d_D$. If
$$
\mathsf{BanachBLOp}(C,D,T,M,\Lambda)
$$
is carried and $a$ is a carried RealUp scalar endpoint with carried absolute-value endpoint $|a|_{\RealUp}$, then the scalar-multiple endpoint map of \autoref{def:banach-bounded-linear-operator-scalar-multiple-carrier} is carried by
$$
\mathsf{BanachBLOp}(C,D,a\boxtimes_D T,|a|_{\RealUp}\cdot M,\langle a\rangle\boxtimes\Lambda).
$$
For every carried source endpoint $x:C_V$, its bound row is
$$
\nu_D((a\boxtimes_D T)(x))\le (|a|_{\RealUp}\cdot M)\cdot\nu_C(x).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold the operator carrier of \autoref{def:banach-bounded-linear-operator-carrier}. For a carried source endpoint $x:C_V$, the target endpoint row of $\Lambda$ carries $T(x)$ in $D_V$. Since $a$ is a carried RealUp scalar endpoint, the target vector-space scalar-action closure row carries
$$
(a\boxtimes_D T)(x)=a\cdot_D T(x)
$$
in $D_V$.

For classifier-respect, suppose $x\sim_Cx'$ with both source endpoints carried. The classifier row of $\Lambda$ gives
$$
T(x)\sim_D T(x').
$$
Target scalar-action congruence with the fixed carried scalar $a$ transports this comparison to
$$
a\cdot_DT(x)\sim_D a\cdot_DT(x'),
$$
which is exactly
$$
(a\boxtimes_D T)(x)\sim_D(a\boxtimes_D T)(x').
$$

For additivity, let $x,y:C_V$ be carried. The additive row of $\Lambda$ gives
$$
T(x+_C y)\sim_D T(x)+_D T(y).
$$
Scalar-action congruence with $a$ gives
$$
a\cdot_D T(x+_C y)\sim_D a\cdot_D(T(x)+_D T(y)).
$$
The target vector-space distributivity row classifies the right endpoint with
$$
a\cdot_D T(x)+_D a\cdot_D T(y).
$$
Composing these target classifier comparisons yields
$$
(a\boxtimes_D T)(x+_C y)
\sim_D
(a\boxtimes_D T)(x)+_D(a\boxtimes_D T)(y).
$$

For homogeneity, let $r$ be a carried RealUp scalar endpoint and let $x:C_V$ be carried. The homogeneous row of $\Lambda$ gives
$$
T(r\cdot_C x)\sim_D r\cdot_D T(x).
$$
Scalar-action congruence with $a$ transports it to
$$
a\cdot_D T(r\cdot_C x)\sim_D a\cdot_D(r\cdot_D T(x)).
$$
Target scalar associativity classifies the right endpoint with
$$
(a\cdot r)\cdot_D T(x).
$$
The RealUp multiplication commutativity row classifies $a\cdot r$ with $r\cdot a$, and target scalar-action congruence transports this scalar comparison to
$$
(a\cdot r)\cdot_D T(x)\sim_D(r\cdot a)\cdot_D T(x).
$$
Applying target scalar associativity in the reverse direction gives
$$
(r\cdot a)\cdot_D T(x)\sim_D r\cdot_D(a\cdot_D T(x)).
$$
Transitivity of $\sim_D$ gives
$$
(a\boxtimes_D T)(r\cdot_C x)
\sim_D
r\cdot_D(a\boxtimes_D T)(x).
$$

It remains to verify the bound row. The operator carrier supplies $M\ge0$. The RealAlgOrder absolute-value non-negativity row supplies $|a|_{\RealUp}\ge0$. Hence RealUp multiplication non-negativity carries
$$
|a|_{\RealUp}\cdot M\ge0,
$$
so the proposed bound endpoint is non-negative.

For a carried source endpoint $x:C_V$, the target $\NormUp$ absolute-homogeneity row gives the RealUp classifier comparison
$$
\nu_D(a\cdot_D T(x))\sim_{\RealUp}|a|_{\RealUp}\cdot\nu_D(T(x)).
$$
The bound row of $\Lambda$ gives
$$
\nu_D(T(x))\le M\cdot\nu_C(x).
$$
Since $|a|_{\RealUp}\ge0$, RealUp multiplication monotonicity transports this comparison to
$$
|a|_{\RealUp}\cdot\nu_D(T(x))
\le
|a|_{\RealUp}\cdot(M\cdot\nu_C(x)).
$$
RealUp associativity classifies the right endpoint with
$$
(|a|_{\RealUp}\cdot M)\cdot\nu_C(x).
$$
Order transport across the absolute-homogeneity and associativity classifier comparisons, followed by order transitivity, gives
$$
\nu_D((a\boxtimes_D T)(x))\le (|a|_{\RealUp}\cdot M)\cdot\nu_C(x).
$$

The endpoint, classifier, additive, homogeneous, non-negative, bound, and ledger rows assembled above are precisely the rows demanded by \autoref{def:banach-bounded-linear-operator-carrier}. Repacking them with the scalar-multiple endpoint map, the bound endpoint $|a|_{\RealUp}\cdot M$, and the visible ledger $\langle a\rangle\boxtimes\Lambda$ yields the carried row
$$
\mathsf{BanachBLOp}(C,D,a\boxtimes_D T,|a|_{\RealUp}\cdot M,\langle a\rangle\boxtimes\Lambda).
$$
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Banach identity bounded linear operator carrier]
\label{thm:banach-identity-bounded-linear-operator-carrier}

$$
\operatorname{id}_C:C_V\to C_V,\qquad \operatorname{id}_C(x):=x.
$$
Let $1_{\RealUp}$ be the RealUp multiplicative-unit endpoint exposed by the scalar source. Let $\mathcal{I}_C$ be the visible identity ledger containing the source endpoint row, the same row read as the target endpoint row, the classifier equivalence rows of $\sim_C$, the vector operation rows for $+_C$ and $\cdot_C$, the $\NormUp$ norm rows for $\nu_C$, the RealUp non-negativity row
$$
1_{\RealUp}\ge 0,
$$
the RealUp multiplication-unit row
$$
1_{\RealUp}\cdot r\sim_{\RealUp} r
$$
for every carried norm endpoint $r$, and the RealUp order-reflexivity and order-transport rows. Then
$$
\mathsf{BanachBLOp}(C,C,\operatorname{id}_C,1_{\RealUp},\mathcal{I}_C)
$$
is carried.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold \autoref{def:banach-bounded-linear-operator-carrier}. For every carried source endpoint $x:C_V$, the endpoint value of the displayed map is $\operatorname{id}_C(x)=x$. The source carrier row for $x$ is therefore also the target endpoint row, since source and target are both the same candidate $C$.

For classifier-respect, suppose $x\sim_C x'$ and both endpoints are carried. The identity endpoint map changes neither side, so the same classifier witness gives
$$
\operatorname{id}_C(x)\sim_C\operatorname{id}_C(x').
$$

For additivity, let $x,y:C_V$ be carried. The vector-space addition row of the $\BanachUp$ candidate carries $x+_C y$. Both sides of the required comparison compute to this carried endpoint:
$$
\operatorname{id}_C(x+_C y)=x+_C y
$$
and
$$
\operatorname{id}_C(x)+_C\operatorname{id}_C(y)=x+_C y.
$$
Classifier reflexivity at the carried sum gives
$$
\operatorname{id}_C(x+_C y)\sim_C\operatorname{id}_C(x)+_C\operatorname{id}_C(y).
$$

For homogeneity, let $a$ be a carried RealUp scalar endpoint and let $x:C_V$ be carried. The scalar-action row carries $a\cdot_C x$. Again both sides compute to the same endpoint:
$$
\operatorname{id}_C(a\cdot_C x)=a\cdot_C x
$$
and
$$
a\cdot_C\operatorname{id}_C(x)=a\cdot_C x.
$$
Classifier reflexivity at the carried scalar-action endpoint gives
$$
\operatorname{id}_C(a\cdot_C x)\sim_C a\cdot_C\operatorname{id}_C(x).
$$

It remains to verify the bound row. The ledger gives $1_{\RealUp}\ge0$. For a carried source endpoint $x$, the norm row gives the carried RealUp endpoint $\nu_C(x)$. Since $\operatorname{id}_C(x)=x$, the left side of the operator bound is $\nu_C(x)$. RealUp order reflexivity gives
$$
\nu_C(x)\le \nu_C(x).
$$
The RealUp multiplication-unit row classifies $1_{\RealUp}\cdot\nu_C(x)$ with $\nu_C(x)$. Transporting the right side of the reflexive order comparison across this RealUp classifier comparison gives
$$
\nu_C(\operatorname{id}_C(x))\le 1_{\RealUp}\cdot\nu_C(x).
$$

The rows just assembled are exactly the target endpoint, classifier-respect, additive compatibility, scalar compatibility, RealUp bound, and visible ledger rows demanded by \autoref{def:banach-bounded-linear-operator-carrier}. Repacking them with the displayed endpoint map and the bound endpoint $1_{\RealUp}$ yields the carried row
$$
\mathsf{BanachBLOp}(C,C,\operatorname{id}_C,1_{\RealUp},\mathcal{I}_C).
$$
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Banach bounded linear operator Cauchy bridge determinacy]
\label{lem:banach-bounded-linear-operator-cauchy-bridge-determinacy}
Let $C,D$ be $\BanachUp$ candidates and let
$\mathsf{BanachBLOp}(C,D,T,K,\Lambda)$ be carried. For every regular Cauchy
$\StreamNameUp$ stream $(X,\mu):\mathbb{N}\to C_V$ over the source distance
$d_C$, the consumer-facing bridge from the source stream to the transported
stream $T\circ X$ over $d_D$ is determined by the following displayed rows:
the source endpoint rows for $X_n$, the target endpoint rows for $T(X_n)$ in
$\Lambda$, the classifier-respect row of
\autoref{def:banach-bounded-linear-operator-carrier}, the norm-induced
distance rows of \autoref{thm:banach-metric-induced-by-norm}, the RealUp
non-negativity and bound-comparison rows for $K$, the source modulus row
$\mu$, the source Cauchy row, and the Lipschitz row of
\autoref{thm:banach-bounded-linear-operator-lipschitz}. Consequently every
accepted route, transport, readback, or handoff used by a downstream consumer
of \autoref{thm:banach-bounded-linear-operator-cauchy-transport} factors
through those rows. No further coordinate is available for a hidden inverse
operator, an ambient compactness principle, a selected limit, a quotient stream
equality, a host continuity theorem, or a detached tolerance selector.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Unfold the transported-stream theorem
\autoref{thm:banach-bounded-linear-operator-cauchy-transport}. Its first public
row is the observation row for the target stream. For each carried source
endpoint $X_n$, the operator ledger $\Lambda$ supplies the target endpoint row
for $T(X_n)$. If a consumer changes a displayed source representative, the
source stream identification passes to the target stream identification by the
observation-stability clause of
\autoref{thm:banach-bounded-linear-operator-cauchy-transport}; that theorem
consumes exactly the classifier-respect row named in
\autoref{def:banach-bounded-linear-operator-carrier}.

The distance readback is also fixed. By
\autoref{thm:banach-metric-induced-by-norm}, the source and target distances
are read as the norm rows
$$
d_C(X_m,X_n)=\nu_C(X_m-_C X_n)
$$
and
$$
d_D(TX_m,TX_n)=\nu_D(TX_m-_D TX_n).
$$
Apply \autoref{thm:banach-bounded-linear-operator-lipschitz}. In its adjacent
Lipschitz transport step, the additive compatibility row of
\autoref{def:banach-bounded-linear-operator-carrier} identifies the target
difference with the displayed endpoint $T(X_m-_C X_n)$ under the target vector
classifier, and the norm-transport row used by
\autoref{thm:banach-bounded-linear-operator-lipschitz} moves the target norm
across that classifier comparison. The theorem then gives the unique metric
handoff exposed by the packet:
$$
d_D(TX_m,TX_n)\le K\cdot d_C(X_m,X_n).
$$

Now fix a requested positive target tolerance. The Cauchy-transport theorem
uses only the RealUp order and multiplication rows for the displayed bound
$K$ to choose a source tolerance whose product with $K$ is below the requested
target tolerance. It then feeds that source tolerance into the displayed source
modulus $\mu$. For all indices beyond the returned bound, the source Cauchy
row gives the source distance estimate; composing it with the Lipschitz row
and the chosen RealUp comparison gives the target Cauchy estimate. Thus the
consumer handoff is exactly the chain
$$
\mu
\quad\longrightarrow\quad
 d_C(X_m,X_n)
\quad\longrightarrow\quad
 K\cdot d_C(X_m,X_n)
\quad\longrightarrow\quad
 d_D(TX_m,TX_n).
$$

Every row used in this chain has already been named in the carrier, the
norm-induced metric theorem, the Lipschitz theorem, or the Cauchy-transport
theorem. The accepted packet has no field naming an inverse operator, a compact
subcover, a target limit choice, a quotient equality between streams, an
external continuity theorem, or a tolerance selector detached from the RealUp
bound comparison. Such a request therefore has no source coordinate in the
Banach bounded-linear-operator bridge. Hence every accepted consumer-facing
route factors through the listed local rows, and every other bridge coordinate
remains outside the displayed packet.
\end{proof}
