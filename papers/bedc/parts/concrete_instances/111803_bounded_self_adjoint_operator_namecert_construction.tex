\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\BoundedSelfAdjointOperatorUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-bounded-self-adjoint-operator-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Reed--Simon and Conway on bounded self-adjoint operators on Hilbert spaces, the $\BoundedSelfAdjointOperatorUp$ packet records the finite BEDC surface on which a bounded operator can be read together with its adjoint witness and symmetry row. It sits after the Hilbert carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-hilbert-namecert}, the inner-product row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-innerproduct-namecert}, the operator-norm boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-operator-norm-namecert}, the normal-operator packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-normal-operator-namecert}, and the spectral-radius consumer boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-spectral-radius-namecert}. The packet names bounded self-adjoint operator data; it does not assert spectral-measure existence, functional calculus, compactness, diagonalization, or a host operator-space equality.

\begin{definition}[Bounded self-adjoint operator carrier]
\label{def:bounded-self-adjoint-operator-carrier}
A $\BoundedSelfAdjointOperatorUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
A=(H,I,G,N,J,S,R,T,C,P,L)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $H$ is the $\HilbertUp$ carrier row for the source and target Hilbert endpoint.
\item $I$ is the $\InnerProductUp$ row through which adjoint comparisons are read.
\item $G$ is the bounded operator graph row, recording the displayed action on accepted vectors.
\item $N$ is the $\OperatorNormUp$ bound row attached to the same graph.
\item $J$ is the adjoint witness row, pairing the action row with the inner-product transpose read.
\item $S$ is the self-adjoint symmetry row, identifying the displayed operator row with its adjoint witness through the same $\hsame$ route.
\item $R$ is the spectral-consumer boundary row read by $\NormalOperatorUp$ and $\SpectralRadiusUp$ consumers before any spectral request is allowed.
\item $T$ records componentwise transport for the Hilbert carrier, inner-product row, graph, norm bound, adjoint witness, symmetry, spectral boundary, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a finite operator observation through graph inspection, norm-bound readback, adjoint comparison, symmetry checking, and spectral-consumer boundary exposure.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BoundedSelfAdjointOperatorUp$, $\HilbertUp$, $\InnerProductUp$, $\BoundedLinearOperatorUp$, $\OperatorNormUp$, $\NormalOperatorUp$, $\SpectralRadiusUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $L$ is the local $\NameCert_{\BoundedSelfAdjointOperatorUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $H,I,G,N,J,S,R,T,C,P,L$. The carrier has no coordinate for spectral measures, eigenbasis selection, compactness, quotient operator equality, unbounded domains, or an ambient functional calculus.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Bounded self-adjoint operator NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:bounded-self-adjoint-operator-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\BoundedSelfAdjointOperatorUp$ carrier
$$
A=(H,I,G,N,J,S,R,T,C,P,L),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\BoundedSelfAdjointOperatorUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission, Hilbert-source exposure, inner-product readback, bounded graph admission, operator-norm control, adjoint witness display, self-adjoint symmetry, spectral-consumer boundary exposure, componentwise transport, displayed replay, provenance, and local naming.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:bounded-self-adjoint-operator-carrier}. The accepted packet exposes exactly the Hilbert, inner-product, graph, norm, adjoint, symmetry, spectral-boundary, transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows.

The boundedness obligation is read from $G,N$. The graph row records the displayed operator action, and the operator-norm row supplies the finite bound consumed by downstream operator-theory packets.

The adjoint and symmetry obligations are read from $I,J,S$. The inner-product row provides the comparison surface, the adjoint witness row records the transpose read, and the self-adjoint row identifies the accepted operator with that witness.

Finally $R,T,C,P,L$ keep every normal-operator or spectral-radius consumer behind the same finite packet. Since no carrier row stores a spectral measure, eigenbasis, compactness assertion, quotient operator equality, unbounded-domain extension, or functional calculus, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Bounded self-adjoint operator adjoint symmetry]
\label{thm:bounded-self-adjoint-operator-adjoint-symmetry}
For every accepted $\BoundedSelfAdjointOperatorUp$ carrier $A=(H,I,G,N,J,S,R,T,C,P,L)$, any consumer that reads the operator graph through $G$ and the adjoint witness through $J$ must read the self-adjoint symmetry row $S$ over the same Hilbert and inner-product rows $H,I$ before it can export a $\NormalOperatorUp$ or $\SpectralRadiusUp$ boundary read.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the graph and adjoint rows. The graph row $G$ is typed over the Hilbert carrier $H$, while the adjoint witness $J$ is read through the inner-product row $I$ over the same source.

Apply the NameCert obligations above. They require $S$ to be displayed as the row binding the operator graph and adjoint witness before the packet is consumed as self-adjoint data.

The spectral boundary row $R$ is downstream of this symmetry check. Transport, replay, provenance, and local naming through $T,C,P,L$ preserve the same route, so no consumer can replace the displayed adjoint symmetry by a host operator equality, spectral decomposition, compactness claim, or functional-calculus object.
\end{proof}
