\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RationalBallUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-rational-ball-namecert}

The $\RationalBallUp$ interface names finite rational-radius balls on the
path from rational intervals and dyadic radius rows to metric and real-name
consumer windows. In the constructive analysis tradition of Bishop and
Bridges, a metric ball used here is a located finite witness: a center row,
a visible nonnegative radius row, and the finite ledgers by which rational
samples are certified inside the radius. It is not an open-cover topology
object and it carries no hidden completed real.

\begin{definition}[RationalBall BHist carrier]
\label{def:rational-ball-bhist-carrier}
A $\RationalBallUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
B=(c,r,o,h,k,p,n)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $c$ is the center row, a $\RatUp$ carrier displayed in the packet;
\item $r$ is the dyadic or rational radius row, read through
  $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ as the radius budget for the ball;
\item $o$ is the nonnegative radius and order witness row, certifying that
  the displayed radius is usable as a finite metric bound;
\item $h$ records the componentwise $\hsame$ transport rows for the center,
  radius, order witness, containment ledger, consumer-facing metric rows, and
  real-window handoff coordinates;
\item $k$ is the containment ledger recording exactly which finite rational
  sample rows are certified to lie within the displayed radius of $c$;
\item $p$ is the visible $\Pkg$ provenance row naming the consumed
  $\RatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RationalIntervalUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$,
  $\MetricSpaceUp$, and $\RealUp$ certificate surfaces;
\item $n$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RationalBallUp}$ registration row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate is
$\mathsf{RationalBallPacket}(c,r,o,h,k,p,n)$. Its classifier compares two
accepted packets only by transporting the displayed center and radius rows,
then replaying the finite containment ledger through the same displayed
$\mathsf{BHist}$ surface.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[RationalBall NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:rational-ball-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\RationalBallUp}$ seed obligation surface consists of
six finite rows: the carrier of
\autoref{def:rational-ball-bhist-carrier}, center equality through the
displayed $\RatUp$ classifier, radius compatibility through
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$, the nonnegative radius witness, containment ledger
exactness, and $\hsame$ stability for the $\Cont$ routes that expose the
$\RationalIntervalUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\MetricSpaceUp$, and $\RealUp$
consumer windows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the seven carrier coordinates. The center row $c$, radius row $r$,
and order witness $o$ provide the finite rational-radius data.

The containment surface is exactly the ledger $k$, interpreted with the
transport rows in $h$. The package row $p$ names the downstream interval,
regular-sequence, metric, and real-name surfaces, while $n$ supplies the local
registration row.

These rows account for the stated seed obligations. No coordinate remains for
an open cover, a completed metric space point, or an ambient real ball.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RationalBall containment exactness]
\label{thm:rational-ball-containment-exactness}
For an accepted $\RationalBallUp$ packet
$B=(c,r,o,h,k,p,n)$, every rational containment read is exactly one row of
$k$ transported by $h$ and interpreted against the displayed center $c$,
radius $r$, and nonnegative radius witness $o$. Conversely, any row of $k$
with those carrier, transport, package, and local naming coordinates is
accepted as a $\RationalBallUp$ containment read.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project $B$ through \autoref{def:rational-ball-bhist-carrier}. The only
containment coordinate is the ledger $k$, and each row of $k$ is read with
the displayed center, radius, and order witness.

For the forward direction, a consumer route can only access a row named by
the carrier. Transporting that row uses the componentwise $\hsame$ data in
$h$, so the read remains inside the same finite rational-radius packet.

For the reverse direction, start with a displayed row of $k$ together with
$c$, $r$, $o$, $h$, $p$, and $n$. These are precisely the carrier coordinates
needed to interpret the row as a rational containment read.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RationalBall refinement transport]
\label{thm:rational-ball-refinement-transport}
Suppose two accepted $\RationalBallUp$ packets are connected by displayed
$\hsame$ rows for their centers, radius rows, nonnegative radius witnesses,
and finite containment ledgers. Then any rational sample accepted by the
first packet transports to a rational sample accepted by the second packet
through the same $\Cont$ route surface. The transport introduces no completed
metric point, no total real equality, and no open-cover refinement principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use \autoref{thm:rational-ball-containment-exactness} to identify the sample
read as one displayed ledger row of the first packet. The hypotheses provide
the matching $\hsame$ rows for the center, radius, order witness, and ledger.

Move the ledger row along those displayed transports. The package row keeps
the same $\RatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RationalIntervalUp$,
$\RegSeqRatUp$, $\MetricSpaceUp$, and $\RealUp$ surfaces visible to the
consumer.

Apply the reverse direction of
\autoref{thm:rational-ball-containment-exactness}. The transported row has
exactly the coordinates required by the second packet, so it is accepted
there and no external metric completion datum is introduced.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RationalBall RealUp window handoff]
\label{thm:rational-ball-real-window-handoff}
A $\RealUp$ consumer of an accepted $\RationalBallUp$ packet may read only the
finite rational-radius window recorded by the carrier: the $\RatUp$ center,
the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ radius row, the nonnegative radius witness, finite
rational containment ledger, displayed $\hsame$ transports, $\Cont$ routes,
$\Pkg$ provenance over $\RatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RationalIntervalUp$,
$\RegSeqRatUp$, $\MetricSpaceUp$, and $\RealUp$, and the local
$\NameCert_{\RationalBallUp}$ row.

The handoff supplies a finite rational-radius seed for real-name consumer
windows. It does not assert completeness of $\RealUp$, compactness of balls,
or an open-cover topology theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:rational-ball-bhist-carrier}. The center,
radius, order witness, containment ledger, transport rows, provenance, and
local naming row are the only coordinates exposed by the packet.

Use \autoref{thm:rational-ball-containment-exactness} to exhaust the rational
sample reads available to a downstream consumer. Use
\autoref{thm:rational-ball-refinement-transport} to see that every such read
is stable under displayed finite refinements.

Thus a $\RealUp$ consumer receives exactly the finite rational-radius window
and its provenance through the named BEDC surfaces. Since the packet has no
coordinate for a completed real, an open cover, or compactness data, none can
be exported at the handoff.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RationalBall carrier-radius obligation]
\label{thm:rationalball-carrier-radius-obligation}
Every admitted $\RationalBallUp$ packet consists of a $\RatUp$ center history,
a positive rational radius row, a containment classifier row over
$\mathsf{BHist}$ observations, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance for the displayed
$\RatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RationalIntervalUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$,
$\MetricSpaceUp$, and $\RealUp$ surfaces.

These are the only carrier-radius rows visible to downstream consumers.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the admitted packet through \autoref{def:rational-ball-bhist-carrier}.
The carrier lists the center, radius, order witness, containment ledger,
transport rows, package provenance, and local naming row.

Apply \autoref{thm:rational-ball-namecert-obligations}. It isolates the
center and radius rows as the finite data required by the local
$\NameCert_{\RationalBallUp}$ surface.

Use \autoref{thm:rational-ball-containment-exactness} to identify the
containment classifier as the whole ledger over $\mathsf{BHist}$ observations.
The remaining visible dependency rows are precisely the package coordinates
named in the carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RationalBall center transport obligation]
\label{thm:rationalball-center-transport-obligation}
Transporting the center history and radius row along the displayed $\RatUp$
classifier preserves the $\RationalBallUp$ containment classifier under
componentwise $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ routes.

The transported packet exposes the same finite rational-radius consumer
surface and no completed metric point.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the center and radius rows supplied by
\autoref{def:rational-ball-bhist-carrier}. The displayed $\RatUp$ classifier
provides the transport route for the center, and the radius row travels with
the same finite rational-radius packet.

Apply \autoref{thm:rational-ball-refinement-transport}. It moves a containment
sample through the matching $\hsame$ rows and the displayed $\Cont$ route
without introducing an external metric completion datum.

Finally use \autoref{thm:rational-ball-containment-exactness} in the target
packet. The transported center and radius rows determine exactly the same
containment classifier shape, so the $\RationalBallUp$ consumer surface is
preserved.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RationalBall ledger exactness obligation]
\label{thm:rationalball-ledger-exactness-obligation}
The $\RationalBallUp$ ledger exposes exactly the center, radius, containment,
refinement, $\RealUp$ window handoff, and dependency provenance rows of the
finite rational-radius packet.

It exposes no compactness theorem, open-cover topology, quotient real
equality, or completed metric-space point.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the ledger coordinates from
\autoref{def:rational-ball-bhist-carrier}. The center and radius rows are the
carrier entries, and the dependency provenance is the displayed
$\mathsf{Pkg}$ row.

Apply \autoref{thm:rational-ball-namecert-obligations} to bind those rows to
the local naming surface. Then use
\autoref{thm:rational-ball-containment-exactness} and
\autoref{thm:rational-ball-refinement-transport} to exhaust the containment
and refinement reads.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:rational-ball-real-window-handoff}. The only
real-facing consumer data are the finite rational-radius window and its
provenance, so compactness, open-cover topology, quotient real equality, and
completed metric points have no ledger coordinate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RationalBall RegSeqRat window obligations]
\label{thm:rationalball-regseqrat-window-obligations}
A $\RegSeqRatUp$ consumer of an accepted $\RationalBallUp$ packet reads exactly
the $\RatUp$ center, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ radius row, nonnegative-radius witness,
finite containment ledger, $\hsame$ transports, $\Cont$ routes, $\mathsf{Pkg}$
provenance over $\RationalIntervalUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\MetricSpaceUp$, and
$\RealUp$, and the local $\NameCert_{\RationalBallUp}$ row.

The obligation surface exports no real completeness theorem, compactness of
metric balls, open-cover topology, quotient real equality, or completed metric
point.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier rows from \autoref{def:rational-ball-bhist-carrier}. The
visible data are the rational center, dyadic radius, nonnegative-radius witness,
finite containment ledger, transport rows, continuation routes, package
provenance, and local naming row.

Apply \autoref{thm:rational-ball-namecert-obligations} to bind these rows to
the accepted $\RationalBallUp$ packet. Then use
\autoref{thm:rational-ball-containment-exactness} to exhaust the finite
containment ledger.

Next apply \autoref{thm:rational-ball-refinement-transport}. It preserves the
same rational-radius window under the displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ routes,
with package provenance over the rational-interval, regular-sequence, metric,
and real-facing consumers.

Finally use \autoref{thm:rational-ball-real-window-handoff}. The real-facing
boundary is only the finite window read by the $\RegSeqRatUp$ consumer, so real
completeness, compactness, open-cover topology, quotient real equality, and
completed metric points have no exported row.
\end{proof}
