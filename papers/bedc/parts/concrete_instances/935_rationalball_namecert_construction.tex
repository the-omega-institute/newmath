\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RationalBallUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-rational-ball-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\RationalBallUp$ interface names finite rational-radius balls on the
path from rational intervals and dyadic radius rows to metric and real-name
consumer windows. In the constructive analysis tradition of Bishop and
Bridges, a metric ball used here is a located finite witness: a center row,
a visible nonnegative radius row, and the finite ledgers by which rational
samples are certified inside the radius. It is not an open-cover topology
object and it carries no hidden completed real.

$\RationalBallUp$ consumes the finite endpoint-and-containment discipline of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rationalinterval-namecert}. The ball ledger
refines interval containment by adding the visible center row, nonnegative
radius row, and rational-radius comparison route.

\begin{definition}[RationalBall BHist carrier]
\label{def:rational-ball-bhist-carrier}
A $\RationalBallUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
B=(c,r,o,h,k,p,n)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $c$ is the center row, a $\RatUp$ carrier displayed in the packet;
\item $r$ is the dyadic or rational radius row, read through
  $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ as the radius budget for the ball;
\item $o$ is the nonnegative radius and order witness row, certifying that
  the displayed radius is usable as a finite metric bound;
\item $h$ records the componentwise $\hsame$ transport rows for the center,
  radius, order witness, containment ledger, consumer-facing metric rows, and
  real-window handoff coordinates;
\item $k$ is the containment ledger recording exactly which finite rational
  sample rows are certified to lie within the displayed radius of $c$;
\item $p$ is the visible $\Pkg$ provenance row naming the consumed
  $\RatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RationalIntervalUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$,
  $\MetricSpaceUp$, and $\RealUp$ certificate surfaces;
\item $n$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RationalBallUp}$ registration row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate is
$\mathsf{RationalBallPacket}(c,r,o,h,k,p,n)$. Its classifier compares two
accepted packets only by transporting the displayed center and radius rows,
then replaying the finite containment ledger through the same displayed
$\mathsf{BHist}$ surface.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[RationalBall containment exactness]
\label{thm:rational-ball-containment-exactness}
For an accepted $\RationalBallUp$ packet
$B=(c,r,o,h,k,p,n)$, every rational containment read is exactly one row of
$k$ transported by $h$ and interpreted against the displayed center $c$,
radius $r$, and nonnegative radius witness $o$. Conversely, any row of $k$
with those carrier, transport, package, and local naming coordinates is
accepted as a $\RationalBallUp$ containment read.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.RationalBallUp.RationalBallPacket\_containment\_exactness}

For the reverse direction, start with a displayed row of $k$ together with

\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RationalBall nested containment consumer composition]
\label{thm:rationalball-nested-containment-consumer-composition}
Let $B_0=(c_0,r_0,H_0,R_0,P_0,C_0)$ and
$B_1=(c_1,r_1,H_1,R_1,P_1,C_1)$ be accepted $\RationalBallUp$ packets, and
let $q$ be a query history. Suppose the displayed
$\RationalIntervalUp$ and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ refinement row says that the
finite rational ball of $B_1$ is contained in the finite rational ball of
$B_0$. If $q$ is accepted by the containment row of $B_1$, then $q$ is accepted
by the containment row of $B_0$.

Moreover every $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, or $\MetricSpaceUp$ consumer receives
the composed finite containment ledger formed from the two packet ledgers, the
displayed $\hsame$ transports, the $\Cont$ routes, the $\mathsf{Pkg}$
provenance over $\RatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RationalIntervalUp$,
$\RegSeqRatUp$, $\MetricSpaceUp$, and $\RealUp$, and the two local
$\NameCert_{\RationalBallUp}$ rows. The composition introduces no host metric,
choice-selected point, real-completeness row, compactness datum, topology row,
or quotient real equality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply \autoref{thm:rational-ball-refinement-transport}. It transports
the inner packet $B_1$ through the displayed outer containment row of $B_0$ and
keeps the containment witness as a finite $\RationalIntervalUp$ and
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger row.

Next use \autoref{thm:rationalball-interval-center-containment}. The query
history $q$ is already accepted by the containment row of $B_1$, and the
transported refinement row places that accepted read inside the containment row
of $B_0$.

For regular and real consumers, pass the composed ledger through
\autoref{thm:rationalball-regseqrat-window-obligations} and
\autoref{thm:rationalball-standard-finite-window-bridge}. These theorems keep
the same finite observation windows and standard rational-radius bridge rows,
so the consumer sees the composed containment ledger rather than a rebuilt host
argument.

Finally apply
\autoref{thm:rationalball-metric-positive-containment-transport}. The
$\MetricSpaceUp$ read is the same positive containment row transported through
the finite packet data. Since every step used only displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$,
$\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and $\NameCert$ rows, no external metric
structure or choice data enters the composed handoff.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RationalBall DyadicApproximation nested window readback]

\begin{theorem}[RationalBall nested containment refinement monotonicity]
\label{thm:rationalball-nested-containment-refinement-monotonicity}
For any finite chain $B_0,\ldots,B_n$ of accepted $\RationalBallUp$ packets
whose adjacent $\RationalIntervalUp$ and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ containment rows
witness that each displayed ball is contained in the preceding displayed ball,
every query history accepted by the containment row of $B_n$ is accepted by
the containment row of every earlier $B_i$.

Every $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, or $\MetricSpaceUp$ consumer receives the
iterated finite containment ledger assembled from the adjacent packet ledgers,
displayed $\hsame$ transports, displayed $\Cont$ routes, $\mathsf{Pkg}$
provenance, and local $\NameCert_{\RationalBallUp}$ rows. The finite chain
exports no host metric, choice-selected point, compactness datum, topology row,
completed real, or quotient real equality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Induct on the finite chain. For the singleton chain, the terminal packet is
already the initial packet, and the containment read is the packet's displayed
finite containment row.

For the extension step, assume the statement for a chain ending in $B_n$ and
append an accepted packet $B_{n+1}$ whose adjacent containment row places it
inside $B_n$. Apply
\autoref{thm:rationalball-nested-containment-consumer-composition} to compose
the accepted read of $B_{n+1}$ with the containment row of $B_n$.

The induction hypothesis then transports the resulting accepted read through
every earlier containment row. The composed ledger is the finite concatenation
of the adjacent packet ledgers together with the displayed $\hsame$, $\Cont$,
$\mathsf{Pkg}$, and local $\NameCert$ rows used at each step.

For consumer handoff, use
\autoref{thm:rationalball-regseqrat-window-obligations},
\autoref{thm:rationalball-standard-finite-window-bridge}, and
\autoref{thm:rationalball-metric-positive-containment-transport}. These
theorems read the same iterated containment ledger for $\RegSeqRatUp$,
$\RealUp$, and $\MetricSpaceUp$, without adding any host metric, selected
point, compactness, topology, completed-real, or quotient-equality row.
\end{proof}
