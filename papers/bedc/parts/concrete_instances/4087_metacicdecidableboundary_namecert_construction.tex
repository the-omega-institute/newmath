\begin{definition}[MetaCIC decidable boundary carrier]
\label{def:metacic-decidable-boundary-carrier}
A $\MetaCICDecidableBoundaryUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
D=(T,S,B,F,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $T$ is the $\mathsf{inferTypeCtx}$ checker row for the encoded $\mathsf{HasType}$ relation;
\item $S$ is the $\mathsf{checkSameTerm}$ structural-equality row used by the checker;
\item $B$ is the bounded normal-equality row for $\mathsf{normalizeBounded}$ and $\mathsf{decideClosedBetaConv}$;
\item $F$ is the finished-normal-form side-condition row consumed by the bounded comparison soundness theorems;
\item $R$ records the refusal rows for a standalone $\mathsf{ClosedAt}$ decision theorem and for global closed beta-conversion decidability;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport across $T,S,B,F,R,P,N$;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which consumers replay checker, comparison, refusal, and side-condition reads;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over the decidability audit map, the normal-form exclusion sibling, the closed-normal confluence seal sibling, and the subject-reduction obstruction sibling;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MetaCICDecidableBoundaryUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no row for a global $\mathsf{ClosedAt}$ decider, no unrestricted beta-conversion oracle, no fuel-erased normalisation theorem, no quotient of represented terms, and no host evaluator.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC decidable boundary NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:metacic-decidable-boundary-namecert-obligations}
Every accepted $\MetaCICDecidableBoundaryUp$ packet binds exactly the finite obligation surface displayed in \autoref{def:metacic-decidable-boundary-carrier}: carrier admission for $D=(T,S,B,F,R,H,C,P,N)$, checker visibility for $T$, structural equality visibility for $S$, bounded normal comparison visibility for $B$, finished-normal side-condition visibility for $F$, refusal visibility for $R$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, finite $\Cont$ replay through $C$, sibling provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier. The positive checker rows are $T,S,B,F$: the typing checker reads $T$ together with the structural equality row $S$, and the conversion checker reads the bounded normal comparison row $B$ together with the finished-normal side-condition row $F$.

The refusal row $R$ is part of the same finite packet. It records that the carrier does not contain a separate $\mathsf{ClosedAt}$ decision theorem and does not contain an unrestricted beta-conversion decider for arbitrary closed terms.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ only transport, replay, package, and name those displayed reads. Since no further coordinate is present, the local NameCert surface is exhausted by the listed obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC decidable boundary bounded comparison non-escape]
\label{thm:metacic-decidable-boundary-bounded-comparison-nonescape}
Any consumer of an accepted $\MetaCICDecidableBoundaryUp$ packet may read bounded normal equality only through $B$ and $F$. The read can expose equality or beta-convertibility of the bounded normalizer outputs under the finished-normal-form side conditions, but it cannot export a global closed beta-conversion decision procedure, a fuel-erased normalisation theorem, or a quotient equality over represented terms.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the bounded comparison coordinates. The carrier separates $B$, the bounded normalizer comparison row, from $F$, the row that supplies the finished-normal-form side conditions.

The refusal coordinate $R$ records the missing promotion from bounded comparison to global closed beta conversion. Thus a consumer cannot skip $F$ or erase the fuel boundary by rereading $B$ as an unrestricted oracle.

Finally use the structural rows. The $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ routes in $H,C$ replay the same finite comparison route, while $P,N$ only record provenance and local naming. None of these rows adds a quotient equality, host evaluator, or global normalisation endpoint.
\end{proof}
