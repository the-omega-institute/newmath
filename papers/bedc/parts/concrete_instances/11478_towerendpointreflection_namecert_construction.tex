\begin{definition}[TowerEndpointReflection carrier]
\label{def:tower-endpoint-reflection-carrier}
A $\TowerEndpointReflectionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
R=(E,S,K,Q,F,D,O,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $E$ is the endpoint row, naming the terminal visible package whose reflection is being queried.
\item $S$ is the source row, naming the base system, trace family, or observation process to which reflection is proposed.
\item $K$ is the $\RealityConstrainedTowerCompressionUp$ row, exposing the source, tower, classifier, endpoint, and composite-ledger surface.
\item $Q$ is the $\TowerEquivalenceUp$ row, recording endpoint agreement together with ledger and descent compatibility.
\item $F$ is the $\CompressionLedgerFunctorUp$ row, recording the composite-ledger route assigned to the displayed tower arrows.
\item $D$ is the $\CompressionDescentAuditUp$ row, recording the accepted or refused descent route for the endpoint operation under reflection.
\item $O$ is the obstruction row, listing the finite reasons that block reflection, including missing composite ledger, failed descent audit, endpoint-only equivalence, source mismatch, and model-facing non-descent.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $E,S,K,Q,F,D,O$.
\item $C$ is the displayed $\Cont$ replay route through the same endpoint, source, ledger, equivalence, descent, and obstruction rows.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\TowerEndpointReflectionUp$, the four cited tower siblings, $\RealWindowBudgetUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\TowerEndpointReflectionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for endpoint truth as source truth, source identity from endpoint equivalence, unledgered reflection, private descent repair, or a model-facing acceptance route outside $R$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[TowerEndpointReflection descent boundary]
\label{thm:tower-endpoint-reflection-descent-boundary}
Let
$$
R=(E,S,K,Q,F,D,O,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\TowerEndpointReflectionUp$ packet. Any endpoint/source reflection exported from $E$ to $S$ requires both the composite-ledger row carried by $K,F$ and the descent-audit row $D$. A consumer that keeps endpoint equivalence through $Q$ while omitting either the composite ledger or the descent audit has an endpoint comparison, not a source-facing reflection.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:tower-endpoint-reflection-carrier}. The endpoint row $E$ and source row $S$ are displayed together with tower compression $K$, tower equivalence $Q$, ledger functor $F$, descent audit $D$, and obstruction row $O$.

The tower-compression row exposes the source, classifier, endpoint, and composite-ledger discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-reality-constrained-tower-compression-namecert}. The ledger-functor row then records the composite route by which the tower arrows transport hidden-source material to the endpoint-facing ledger.

The descent-audit row supplies the separate decision surface required by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compression-descent-audit-namecert}. It is not implied by endpoint equivalence, because \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-towerequivalence-namecert} binds agreement only when ledger and descent compatibility are also displayed.

Thus endpoint/source reflection is licensed only through the joint route $K,F,D$ transported by $H$ and replayed by $C$. If the composite-ledger row or the descent-audit row is absent, the packet has no carrier coordinate that can move an endpoint claim from $E$ to $S$.
\end{proof}

\label{thm:tower-endpoint-reflection-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:tower-endpoint-reflection-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\TowerEndpointReflectionUp}$ surface consists exactly of seven obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for $R=(E,S,K,Q,F,D,O,H,C,P,N)$;
\item endpoint and source exposure through $E,S$;
\item tower-compression exposure through $K$;
\item endpoint-equivalence exposure through $Q$;
\item composite-ledger exposure through $F$;
\item descent-audit and obstruction exposure through $D,O$;
\item componentwise transport, replay, provenance, and local naming closure through $H,C,P,N$.
\end{enumerate}
Every accepted endpoint/source reflection must display these rows before a consumer may treat an endpoint operation as source-facing.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

The obstruction row $O$ records the finite refusal surface whenever a ledger, descent, equivalence, or source-facing requirement is missing. The rows $H,C,P,N$ keep the same packet stable under transport, replay, provenance, and local naming.

Since the carrier admits no further coordinate, the seven displayed obligations exhaust the NameCert surface. A consumer that lacks one of them is reading a different packet rather than an accepted $\TowerEndpointReflectionUp$ carrier.
\end{proof}
