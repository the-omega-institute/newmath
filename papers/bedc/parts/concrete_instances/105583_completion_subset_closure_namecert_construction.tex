\begin{theorem}[CompletionSubsetClosure NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:completion-subset-closure-namecert-obligations}

Every completion-facing read must therefore pass through
$$
M\longmapsto E\longmapsto D\longmapsto P\longmapsto S\longmapsto W\longmapsto Q\longmapsto R\longmapsto L\longmapsto(H,C,G,N)
$$
before it can treat a Cauchy read in the subset as closed under completion. The route exports no open-cover compactness theorem, no quotient equality of completion classes, no selected limit, no countable-choice schedule, and no ambient $\RealUp$ completeness row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:completion-subset-closure-carrier}. The rows $M,E,D,P$ expose the complete separable source, dense completion image, dense Cauchy handoff, and Polish consumer boundary before the subset ledger can be read.

Next inspect the closed-subset and finite observation rows. The ledger $S$ is read only through finite windows $W$, regular rational data $Q$, and Real seals $R$, so a subset Cauchy request has a displayed membership and boundary route before a limit row is exposed.

Finally read the structural rows. The limit handoff $L$ is transported by $H$, replayed by $C$, sourced by $G$, and named by $N$. Since the carrier has no row for open covers, quotient completion equality, selected limits, countable choice, trichotomy, or ambient Real completeness, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}
