\begin{definition}[Ideal intersection predicate]
\label{def:ideal-intersection-predicate}
Let $R$ carry a $\RingUp$ certificate with carrier $C$, and let $I$ and $J$ be $\IdealUp$ carried predicates over $R$ in the sense of \autoref{sec:ideal-carrier}. The intersection predicate $I\cap_RJ$ is defined by
$$
(I\cap_RJ)(x)\Longleftrightarrow I(x)\land J(x).
$$
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Ideal intersection closure]
\label{thm:ideal-intersection-closure}
Let $R$ carry a $\RingUp$ certificate as in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-ring-namecert}, with carrier $C$, classifier $\sim_R$, addition $+_R$, additive inverse $-_R$, zero $0_R$, and multiplication $\cdot_R$. Let $I$ and $J$ be $\IdealUp$ predicates over $R$. Assume the two ideal certificates provide the rows specified in \autoref{sec:ideal-certificate}: carrier support, zero membership, additive closure, additive inverse closure, internal multiplication closure, classifier transport, and left and right absorption by every carried ring endpoint. For $K:=I\cap_RJ$ as in \autoref{def:ideal-intersection-predicate}, $K$ is an $\IdealUp$ predicate over $R$. Explicitly, $K$ has carrier support and the rows
$$
K(0_R),\qquad
K(x)\land K(y)\Longrightarrow K(x+_R y),\qquad
K(x)\Longrightarrow K(-_R x),
$$
$$
K(x)\land K(y)\Longrightarrow K(x\cdot_R y),\qquad
K(x)\land x\sim_R y\Longrightarrow K(y),
$$
and, for every $r$ with $C(r)$,
$$
K(x)\Longrightarrow K(r\cdot_R x)\land K(x\cdot_R r).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier support row is immediate. If $K(x)$, then $I(x)$ and $J(x)$; the carrier-support row of either ideal gives $C(x)$.

For zero, the zero rows for $I$ and $J$ give $I(0_R)$ and $J(0_R)$, hence $K(0_R)$.

For additive closure, assume $K(x)$ and $K(y)$. Then $I(x)$, $I(y)$, $J(x)$, and $J(y)$. The additive-closure row for $I$ gives $I(x+_R y)$, and the additive-closure row for $J$ gives $J(x+_R y)$. Together these are $K(x+_R y)$.

For additive inverse closure, assume $K(x)$. The inverse row for $I$ gives $I(-_R x)$, and the inverse row for $J$ gives $J(-_R x)$. Thus $K(-_R x)$.

For internal multiplication closure, assume $K(x)$ and $K(y)$. Carrier support gives $C(y)$. Right absorption in $I$, applied to $I(x)$ and $C(y)$, gives $I(x\cdot_R y)$. Right absorption in $J$, applied to $J(x)$ and $C(y)$, gives $J(x\cdot_R y)$. Hence $K(x\cdot_R y)$, which supplies the multiplicative closure row required by the sub-rng part of $\IdealUp$.

For classifier transport, assume $K(x)$ and $x\sim_R y$. The transport row for $I$ gives $I(y)$, and the transport row for $J$ gives $J(y)$. Therefore $K(y)$.

For right absorption, assume $K(x)$ and $C(r)$. Right absorption in the two input ideal certificates gives $I(x\cdot_R r)$ and $J(x\cdot_R r)$, so $K(x\cdot_R r)$. For left absorption, the same argument with the left absorption rows gives $I(r\cdot_R x)$ and $J(r\cdot_R x)$, hence $K(r\cdot_R x)$.

These rows are exactly the carrier, additive sub-rng, classifier-stability, and ambient two-sided absorption rows required of an $\IdealUp$ predicate over the given $\RingUp$ certificate.
\end{proof}
