\begin{theorem}[CauchyCriterion finite-window Real seal handoff]
\label{thm:cauchy-criterion-finite-window-real-seal-handoff}
Let $K=(W,M,T,Q,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyCriterionUp$ packet. Every
accepted finite-window budget-selector read of $K$ that is handed to a
$\RealUp$-facing consumer, a $\CompletionUp$-facing consumer, a
$\RealCauchyCompletionUp$-facing consumer, or a $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ consumer
factors through the displayed route
$$
\StreamNameUp
\longmapsto \RegSeqRatUp
\longmapsto \DyadicRatCoreUp
\longmapsto \RealUp
\longmapsto \CauchyLimitSealUp.
$$
The route reads the CauchyCriterion rows $M,Q,T,W,E$ before the consumer sees
the selected finite window, the regular-rational readback, the dyadic
tolerance ledger, the Real seal row, and the limit-seal boundary. Its support
rows are precisely the visible $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and
$\NameCert$ rows carried by $H,C,P,N$.

No accepted handoff exports a quotient stream, a selected limit, host real
equality, a hidden tail oracle, ambient completeness, or a completion endpoint
outside the displayed finite route.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CauchyCriterionUp.CauchyCriterionCarrier\_finite\_window\_real\_seal\_handoff}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted CauchyCriterion carrier. The finite selector and budget
rows of the chapter expose the route $M,Q,T,W,E$ together with the structural
rows $H,C,P,N$; no consumer can read beyond those rows before the selector
surface has been displayed.

Next use the StreamName and RegSeqRat handoff route. The
CauchyCriterion-to-StreamName theorem
\autoref{thm:cauchycriterion-regseqrat-streamname-realup-handoff} places the
finite window, regular-rational readback, dyadic ledger, and Real seal on the
same packet. The selector boundary
\autoref{thm:cauchycriterion-streamname-real-budget-selector-boundary} then
forces every finite-window budget-selector read through that route before a
Real-facing or completion-facing consumer receives it.

Now attach the completion and seal boundary. The completion-boundary theorem
\autoref{thm:cauchycriterion-tail-budget-real-completion-boundary} confines
$\CompletionUp$ and $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ consumers to the same
$M,Q,T,W,E,H,C,P,N$ surface, while the StreamName CauchyLimitSeal pullback
\autoref{thm:streamname-budget-cauchy-limit-seal-pullback} and the public
CauchyLimitSeal route boundary
\autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-public-route-boundary} put the limit-seal
consumer after the displayed StreamName window, RegSeqRat readback,
DyadicRatCore tolerance ledger, and Real seal row.

Finally apply the non-escape clauses of the cited boundaries. The carrier has
no coordinate for quotient streams, selected host limits, host real equality,
hidden tail oracles, ambient completeness, or an extra completion endpoint, so
each accepted handoff factors through the displayed finite route and nowhere
else.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyCriterion limit-seal route stability]
\label{thm:cauchy-criterion-limit-seal-route-stability}
Let $K=(W,M,T,Q,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyCriterionUp$ packet, and let
$K'$ be any displayed replay of the same finite-window handoff that preserves
the rows $M,Q,T,W,E,H,C,P,N$ through the visible $\BHist$ packet, componentwise
$\hsame$ transports, and $\Cont$ replay. Then the replayed handoff preserves the
same route
$$
\StreamNameUp
\longmapsto \RegSeqRatUp
\longmapsto \DyadicRatCoreUp
\longmapsto \RealUp
\longmapsto \CauchyLimitSealUp .
$$
No displayed replay of this route can substitute a quotient stream, hidden tail
oracle, host real equality, selected limit, ambient completeness principle, or
completion endpoint outside the rows already carried by $K$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CauchyCriterionUp.CauchyCriterionCarrier\_limit\_seal\_route\_stability}
\begin{proof}
Start with the finite handoff theorem. By
\autoref{thm:cauchy-criterion-finite-window-real-seal-handoff}, every accepted
finite-window read of $K$ reaches the limit-seal boundary only through
$M,Q,T,W,E,H,C,P,N$ and the displayed
$\StreamNameUp \to \RegSeqRatUp \to \DyadicRatCoreUp \to \RealUp \to
\CauchyLimitSealUp$ route.

Next identify the route coordinates. The RegSeqRat and StreamName handoff
\autoref{thm:cauchycriterion-regseqrat-streamname-realup-handoff} binds the
regular readback to the StreamName window, while
\autoref{thm:cauchycriterion-streamname-real-budget-selector-boundary} and
\autoref{thm:cauchycriterion-tail-budget-real-completion-boundary} keep the
budget selector and completion-facing reads on the same finite rows.

Finally attach the seal side.
\autoref{thm:streamname-budget-cauchy-limit-seal-pullback} pulls the seal read
back to the StreamName budget surface, and
\autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-public-route-boundary} confines public
CauchyLimitSeal consumption to that surface. Since $K'$ preserves exactly the
displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, and $\Cont$ rows, carrier-coordinate exhaustion
leaves no coordinate from which a quotient stream, hidden tail oracle, host
real equality, selected limit, ambient completeness principle, or off-route
completion endpoint could be read.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyCriterion Real L10 terminal-window sufficiency]
\label{thm:cauchycriterion-real-l10-terminal-window-sufficiency}
Let $K=(W,M,T,Q,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyCriterionUp$ packet whose
finite-window budget-selector read follows the route
$$
\StreamNameUp
\longmapsto \RegSeqRatUp
\longmapsto \DyadicRatCoreUp
\longmapsto \RealUp
\longmapsto \CauchyLimitSealUp .
$$
Then the same displayed rows $M,Q,T,W,E,H,C,P,N$, together with their visible
$\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ coordinates, suffice to
supply the Real L10 finite-window witness surface consumed by the public
boundary.

Conversely, any Real L10 public-boundary read sourced through $K$ recovers
exactly the selector rows $M,Q,T,W,E,H,C,P,N$ and no additional coordinate. The
sufficiency and recovery clauses exclude quotient streams, host real equality,
selected limits, countable choice, ambient completeness, metric-completeness
rows, arithmetic packages, order packages, and bridge rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First use \autoref{thm:cauchy-criterion-finite-window-real-seal-handoff}. It
places every accepted finite-window budget-selector read of $K$ on the
displayed route and exposes the rows $M,Q,T,W,E,H,C,P,N$ before any Real-facing,
completion-facing, or limit-seal consumer can read the packet.

Next apply \autoref{thm:cauchy-criterion-limit-seal-route-stability}. The
route remains fixed under displayed replay of the same $\BHist$, $\hsame$, and
$\Cont$ rows, so the terminal-window read cannot be replaced by a quotient
stream, hidden tail oracle, host equality row, selected limit, or completeness
principle.

Now attach the Real L10 boundary. The lattice budget cover
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-realup-lattice-budget-cover} identifies the
StreamName, RegSeqRat, DyadicRatCore, and Real seal faces as one finite route,
with only the displayed support coordinates. Feeding that route into
\autoref{thm:real-l10-finite-window-witness-determinacy} supplies exactly the
finite-window witness surface consumed by the Real L10 public boundary.

For the converse, start from a Real L10 public-boundary read sourced through
$K$. By \autoref{thm:real-l10-source-route-exactness}, the boundary can be
sourced only through the ordered finite-window route displayed by the Real L10
lattice. Comparing this route with the CauchyCriterion handoff and its replay
stability recovers precisely $M,Q,T,W,E,H,C,P,N$.

The exclusion clause is inherited from the cited route and L10 boundary
theorems. None of those packets carries quotient streams, host real equality,
selected limits, countable choice, ambient completeness, metric-completeness
rows, arithmetic packages, order packages, or bridge rows, so no such row can be
introduced at the terminal public boundary.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CauchyCriterionUp.CauchyCriterionCarrier\_real\_l10\_terminal\_window\_sufficiency}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyCriterion Real seal finite-route uniqueness]
\label{thm:cauchy-criterion-real-seal-unique-finite-route}
Let $K=(W,M,T,Q,E,H,C,P,N)$ and
$K'=(W',M',T',Q',E',H',C',P',N')$ be accepted $\CauchyCriterionUp$ packets.
Suppose their public-boundary reads expose the same rows
$M,Q,T,W,E,H,C,P,N$ through the same visible $\BHist$ packet, componentwise
$\hsame$ transports, $\Cont$ replay, and matching $\Pkg$ and $\NameCert$
coordinates. Then the two Real L10 public-boundary reads determine the same
terminal finite route
$$
\StreamNameUp
\longmapsto \RegSeqRatUp
\longmapsto \DyadicRatCoreUp
\longmapsto \RealUp
\longmapsto \CauchyLimitSealUp .
$$
No quotient stream, host equality, selected limit, countable-choice row,
ambient completeness principle, or bridge row can distinguish the two reads at
the terminal public boundary.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First expose the common route. By
\autoref{thm:cauchy-criterion-finite-window-real-seal-handoff}, each accepted
read reaches the Real and limit-seal boundary only through the displayed rows
$M,Q,T,W,E,H,C,P,N$ and the finite route from $\StreamNameUp$ to
$\CauchyLimitSealUp$.

Next compare the two displayed reads under replay. The hypothesis identifies
the visible $\BHist$, componentwise $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$
coordinates, so \autoref{thm:cauchy-criterion-limit-seal-route-stability} keeps
both reads on the same replayed route rather than on two independently selected
completion surfaces.

Now attach the public Real face. The lattice budget cover
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-realup-lattice-budget-cover} makes the
StreamName, RegSeqRat, DyadicRatCore, and Real faces a single finite support
surface. The Real L10 witness determinacy theorem
\autoref{thm:real-l10-finite-window-witness-determinacy} then identifies the
public-boundary witnesses carried by the two reads on that shared surface.

Finally use source-route exactness. By
\autoref{thm:real-l10-source-route-exactness}, a Real L10 public-boundary read
has no source outside the ordered finite-window route already exposed by the
CauchyCriterion handoff. Hence the two reads determine the same terminal finite
route, and the absent rows listed in the statement have no coordinate from
which a distinction could be read.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CauchyCriterionUp.CauchyCriterionCarrier\_real\_seal\_unique\_finite\_route}

\begin{corollary}[CauchyCriterion finite-window Real seal handoff determinacy]
\label{cor:cauchy-criterion-finite-window-real-seal-handoff-determinacy}
Let $K=(W,M,T,Q,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyCriterionUp$ packet. Fix
the displayed rows $M,Q,T,W,E,H,C,P,N$ through the visible $\BHist$ packet,
componentwise $\hsame$ transports, $\Cont$ replay, and matching $\Pkg$ and
$\NameCert$ coordinates. Then every accepted consumer-facing use of the
finite-window Real seal handoff of $K$ determines the same terminal route
$$
\StreamNameUp
\longmapsto \RegSeqRatUp
\longmapsto \DyadicRatCoreUp
\longmapsto \RealUp
\longmapsto \CauchyLimitSealUp .
$$
In particular, a $\RealUp$-facing, $\CompletionUp$-facing,
$\RealCauchyCompletionUp$-facing, or $\CauchyLimitSealUp$-facing consumer must
use the same finite-route normal form and the same source-route exactness
verdict named by the chapter. No accepted use can introduce an alternate
hidden coordinate, a duplicate classifier source, a quotient stream, host real
equality, a selected limit, countable choice, ambient completeness, a bridge
row, or a completion endpoint outside the fixed rows.
\end{corollary}
\begin{proof}
Take two accepted consumer-facing uses of the handoff after the rows
$M,Q,T,W,E,H,C,P,N$ have been fixed. The needed transport comparison is the
finite-route uniqueness result, \autoref{thm:cauchy-criterion-real-seal-unique-finite-route}: its hypotheses are precisely the common visible $\BHist$ packet,
componentwise $\hsame$ transports, $\Cont$ replay, and matching $\Pkg$ and
$\NameCert$ coordinates over the fixed rows. Regard the second use as a
displayed replay of the first along that cited comparison surface. By
\autoref{thm:cauchy-criterion-limit-seal-route-stability}, the replay cannot
move the handoff away from the displayed
$\StreamNameUp \to \RegSeqRatUp \to \DyadicRatCoreUp \to \RealUp \to
\CauchyLimitSealUp$ route, and it cannot substitute any quotient stream,
hidden tail oracle, host real equality, selected limit, ambient completeness
principle, or off-route completion endpoint.

Now compare the two public-boundary reads. The hypotheses are exactly the row
identifications required by
\autoref{thm:cauchy-criterion-real-seal-unique-finite-route}. Hence the two
reads determine one terminal finite route, rather than two independently chosen
consumer surfaces. The same comparison fixes the chapter's terminal
finite-route normal form, because the normal form is read only after the
StreamName, RegSeqRat, DyadicRatCore, Real, and CauchyLimitSeal faces have been
placed on that route.

For the exactness decision, use the same exhaustion of sources. The unique
finite route already excludes any source outside the ordered finite-window
route, while route stability keeps every replay on the same rows. Therefore an
accepted consumer use has no coordinate from which to read a second classifier,
a hidden exactness source, or an alternate completion endpoint. The only
available decision is the source-route exactness verdict attached to the fixed
finite-window Real seal handoff.
\end{proof}
