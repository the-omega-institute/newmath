\begin{theorem}[CauchyCriterion finite-window Real seal handoff]
\label{thm:cauchy-criterion-finite-window-real-seal-handoff}
Let $K=(W,M,T,Q,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyCriterionUp$ packet. Every
accepted finite-window budget-selector read of $K$ that is handed to a
$\RealUp$-facing consumer, a $\CompletionUp$-facing consumer, a
$\RealCauchyCompletionUp$-facing consumer, or a $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ consumer
factors through the displayed route
$$
\StreamNameUp
\longmapsto \RegSeqRatUp
\longmapsto \DyadicRatCoreUp
\longmapsto \RealUp
\longmapsto \CauchyLimitSealUp.
$$
The route reads the CauchyCriterion rows $M,Q,T,W,E$ before the consumer sees
the selected finite window, the regular-rational readback, the dyadic
tolerance ledger, the Real seal row, and the limit-seal boundary. Its support
rows are precisely the visible $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and
$\NameCert$ rows carried by $H,C,P,N$.

No accepted handoff exports a quotient stream, a selected limit, host real
equality, a hidden tail oracle, ambient completeness, or a completion endpoint
outside the displayed finite route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted CauchyCriterion carrier. The finite selector and budget
rows of the chapter expose the route $M,Q,T,W,E$ together with the structural
rows $H,C,P,N$; no consumer can read beyond those rows before the selector
surface has been displayed.

Next use the StreamName and RegSeqRat handoff route. The
CauchyCriterion-to-StreamName theorem
\autoref{thm:cauchycriterion-regseqrat-streamname-realup-handoff} places the
finite window, regular-rational readback, dyadic ledger, and Real seal on the
same packet. The selector boundary
\autoref{thm:cauchycriterion-streamname-real-budget-selector-boundary} then
forces every finite-window budget-selector read through that route before a
Real-facing or completion-facing consumer receives it.

Now attach the completion and seal boundary. The completion-boundary theorem
\autoref{thm:cauchycriterion-tail-budget-real-completion-boundary} confines
$\CompletionUp$ and $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ consumers to the same
$M,Q,T,W,E,H,C,P,N$ surface, while the StreamName CauchyLimitSeal pullback
\autoref{thm:streamname-budget-cauchy-limit-seal-pullback} and the public
CauchyLimitSeal route boundary
\autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-public-route-boundary} put the limit-seal
consumer after the displayed StreamName window, RegSeqRat readback,
DyadicRatCore tolerance ledger, and Real seal row.

Finally apply the non-escape clauses of the cited boundaries. The carrier has
no coordinate for quotient streams, selected host limits, host real equality,
hidden tail oracles, ambient completeness, or an extra completion endpoint, so
each accepted handoff factors through the displayed finite route and nowhere
else.
\end{proof}
