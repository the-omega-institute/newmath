\begin{theorem}[CauchyCriterion StreamName Real budget-selector boundary]
\label{thm:cauchycriterion-streamname-real-budget-selector-boundary}
Let $K=(W,M,T,Q,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyCriterionUp$ packet. Any
$\RealUp$-completion consumer or finite-window budget selector sharing the
displayed
$$
\StreamNameUp,\quad \RegSeqRatUp,\quad \DyadicRatCoreUp,\quad \RealUp
$$
rows must read through the CauchyCriterion route
$$
M,\quad Q,\quad T,\quad W,\quad E,\quad H,\quad C,\quad P,\quad N
$$
before exposing a selector output. Conversely, a selector that displays this
route has exactly the finite CauchyCriterion budget surface available for the
shared StreamName--RegSeqRat--DyadicRatCore--Real read.

The boundary exports no quotient stream, selected limit, host real equality,
ambient completeness principle, unrecorded completion object, or selector
choice independent of $M,Q,T,W,E,H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the CauchyCriterion handoff
\autoref{thm:cauchycriterion-regseqrat-streamname-realup-handoff}. It places
the $\StreamNameUp$ finite window, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ source row, the
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$ budget rows, and the $\RealUp$ seal on the displayed
CauchyCriterion carrier rows.

Next apply the completion boundary
\autoref{thm:cauchycriterion-tail-budget-real-completion-boundary}. It says
that any $\CompletionUp$ or $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ consumer sharing those
four faces must factor through $M,Q,T,W,E,H,C,P,N$ before it can read a
completion seal.

Now use the finite-window selector surfaces from the StreamName and Real
siblings. The request and coverage theorems
\autoref{thm:streamname-tail-compatible-window-request} and
\autoref{thm:streamname-tail-compatible-window-coverage} identify a
finite-window selector by its selected observation, bundle membership,
tail-selector route, finite budget surface, pointwise $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff,
and the support rows $\hsame,\Cont,\Pkg,\NameCert$. The budget handoff
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-regseq-finite-window-budget-handoff} and the
boundary \autoref{thm:streamname-realup-finite-window-seal-boundary} then
force the $\RealUp$ read to use the same finite surface.

Finally read the completion-facing exit through
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-completion-four-object-exit-route}. Its four
faces are exactly $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and
$\RealUp$, with no fifth completion coordinate. Combining this exit route with
the CauchyCriterion handoff and tail-budget boundary gives the stated
factorization before any selector output is exposed, and the excluded objects
have no row in the resulting packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyCriterion uniform selector-seal compatibility]
\label{thm:cauchycriterion-uniform-selector-seal-compatibility}
Let $K=(W,M,T,Q,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyCriterionUp$ packet, and
let $U=(I,W_U,M_U,Q_U,T_U,E_U,H_U,C_U,P_U,N_U)$ be an accepted
$\UniformCauchyCriterionUp$ packet. Suppose the two presentations display the
same $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, and $\RealUp$ finite
rows at the completion-facing boundary. Then the budget-selector output of the
$\CauchyCriterionUp$ route and the shared-threshold seal output of the
$\UniformCauchyCriterionUp$ route factor through the same finite packet before
any $\RealUp$ seal is exposed.

The common packet is the displayed StreamName--RegSeqRat--DyadicRatCore--Real
surface with its $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support
rows. It contains no quotient stream, selected limit, host real equality,
detached family threshold, ambient completeness principle, or second
completion seal.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the CauchyCriterion side. Apply
\autoref{thm:cauchycriterion-regseqrat-streamname-realup-handoff} and
\autoref{thm:cauchycriterion-tail-budget-real-completion-boundary}. These
place the selector-facing read on the displayed $M,Q,T,W,E,H,C,P,N$ packet
before any Real or Completion seal can be consumed.

Next expose the StreamName exit route. The finite-window request and coverage
rows, together with
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-regseq-finite-window-budget-handoff},
\autoref{thm:streamname-realup-finite-window-seal-boundary}, and
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-completion-four-object-exit-route}, identify the
four public faces as $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and
$\RealUp$ with no fifth completion coordinate.

Now read the uniform presentation. Apply
\autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-shared-threshold-transport}. The family
threshold request is transported as one finite route through the displayed
window, threshold, tolerance, and tail rows, with the support rows supplied by
$H_U,C_U,P_U,N_U$.

Finally apply
\autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-real-seal-handoff},
\autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-real-seal-consumer-factorization}, and
\autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-non-escape-boundary}. The uniform seal
is downstream of the same finite packet, and the non-escape boundary removes
quotient streams, selected limits, host equality, detached thresholds, ambient
completeness, and any second completion seal. Hence the selector route and the
shared-threshold seal route agree on the finite packet before the $\RealUp$
seal is exposed.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyCriterion selector-seal synchronizer totality]
\label{thm:cauchycriterion-selector-seal-synchronizer-totality}
Let $K=(W,M,T,Q,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyCriterionUp$ packet, and let $U=(I,W_U,M_U,Q_U,T_U,E_U,H_U,C_U,P_U,N_U)$ be an accepted $\UniformCauchyCriterionUp$ packet. Suppose the two packets share the displayed $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, and finite-window selector rows at the completion-facing boundary. Then there is a total synchronizer route between the CauchyCriterion budget-selector surface and the UniformCauchyCriterion shared-threshold surface before any $\RealUp$ seal is consumed.

The synchronizer route is the common StreamName--RegSeqRat--DyadicRatCore--Real finite packet with its selector rows, $\BHist$ support, $\hsame$ transports, $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and $\NameCert$ rows. It exposes no quotient stream, selected limit, host real equality, detached threshold family, ambient completeness principle, or second completion seal.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with \autoref{thm:cauchycriterion-streamname-real-budget-selector-boundary}. It places every CauchyCriterion selector-facing read on the displayed $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, and $\RealUp$ route before exposing selector output.

Apply \autoref{thm:cauchycriterion-uniform-selector-seal-compatibility}. The CauchyCriterion selector route and the UniformCauchyCriterion shared-threshold seal route factor through the same finite packet before a $\RealUp$ seal is exposed.

Use the StreamName finite-window request and coverage rows together with the shared Real-facing handoff. They make the selector rows total over the displayed finite windows rather than over an ambient completion object.

Finally read the UniformCauchyCriterion shared-threshold transport and non-escape boundary. The uniform route contributes no detached threshold family or second seal, so the common packet gives the total synchronizer route claimed above.
\end{proof}
