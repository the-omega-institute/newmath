\begin{theorem}[CauchyCriterion StreamName Real budget-selector boundary]
\label{thm:cauchycriterion-streamname-real-budget-selector-boundary}
Let $K=(W,M,T,Q,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyCriterionUp$ packet. Any
$\RealUp$-completion consumer or finite-window budget selector sharing the
displayed
$$
\StreamNameUp,\quad \RegSeqRatUp,\quad \DyadicRatCoreUp,\quad \RealUp
$$
rows must read through the CauchyCriterion route
$$
M,\quad Q,\quad T,\quad W,\quad E,\quad H,\quad C,\quad P,\quad N
$$
before exposing a selector output. Conversely, a selector that displays this
route has exactly the finite CauchyCriterion budget surface available for the
shared StreamName--RegSeqRat--DyadicRatCore--Real read.

The boundary exports no quotient stream, selected limit, host real equality,
ambient completeness principle, unrecorded completion object, or selector
choice independent of $M,Q,T,W,E,H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the CauchyCriterion handoff
\autoref{thm:cauchycriterion-regseqrat-streamname-realup-handoff}. It places
the $\StreamNameUp$ finite window, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ source row, the
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$ budget rows, and the $\RealUp$ seal on the displayed
CauchyCriterion carrier rows.

Next apply the completion boundary
\autoref{thm:cauchycriterion-tail-budget-real-completion-boundary}. It says
that any $\CompletionUp$ or $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ consumer sharing those
four faces must factor through $M,Q,T,W,E,H,C,P,N$ before it can read a
completion seal.

Now use the finite-window selector surfaces from the StreamName and Real
siblings. The request and coverage theorems
\autoref{thm:streamname-tail-compatible-window-request} and
\autoref{thm:streamname-tail-compatible-window-coverage} identify a
finite-window selector by its selected observation, bundle membership,
tail-selector route, finite budget surface, pointwise $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff,
and the support rows $\hsame,\Cont,\Pkg,\NameCert$. The budget handoff
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-regseq-finite-window-budget-handoff} and the
boundary \autoref{thm:streamname-realup-finite-window-seal-boundary} then
force the $\RealUp$ read to use the same finite surface.

Finally read the completion-facing exit through
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-completion-four-object-exit-route}. Its four
faces are exactly $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and
$\RealUp$, with no fifth completion coordinate. Combining this exit route with
the CauchyCriterion handoff and tail-budget boundary gives the stated
factorization before any selector output is exposed, and the excluded objects
have no row in the resulting packet.
\end{proof}
