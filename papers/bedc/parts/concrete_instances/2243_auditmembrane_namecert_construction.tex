\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\AuditMembraneUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-auditmembrane-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\AuditMembraneUp$ packet turns the audit-gate boundary into a finite local certificate. It records the gate surface, the content refused at that surface, the visible result of the refusal, the route by which paper-Lean drift is resolved, the transport and replay rows, the package provenance, and the local naming certificate available to downstream discipline chapters.

It consumes the audit-gate refusal surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-auditgateboundary-namecert}: the membrane carrier repackages that boundary as the downstream gate, boundary-content, refusal-result, drift-route, transport, replay, provenance, and local NameCert rows used below.

\begin{definition}[AuditMembrane carrier]
\label{def:audit-membrane-carrier}
An $\AuditMembraneUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
M=(G,B,R,D,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $G$ is the gate-surface row, recording the source-token scan, dependency ancestry scan, marker-resolution scan, and origin-flow scan as one finite audit face;
\item $B$ is the boundary-content row, recording the proposed crossings through source axioms, forbidden host dependencies, unresolved Lean names, and untracked origin/import flow;
\item $R$ is the refusal-result row, recording for each boundary item whether the membrane admits it as displayed certificate content or refuses it as non-substrate supply;
\item $D$ is the drift-resolution route row, recording the named paper marker, the candidate Lean declaration, and the finite resolve-or-refuse result;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $G,B,R,D$, the continuation row, the package row, and the local naming row;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which consumers inspect the gate surface, boundary content, refusal result, drift route, provenance, and local certificate;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, $\Hist$, $\mathsf{GAP}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, $\NameCert$, audit rows, marker names, and origin ledgers;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\AuditMembraneUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains exactly these rows. It has no coordinate for a hidden global audit theorem, no permission to replace an unresolved name by a nearby theorem, no semantic proof that every import is conservative, and no route by which refused boundary content becomes substrate-internal theorem content.
\end{definition}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AuditMembraneUp.AuditMembraneTasteGate\_single\_carrier\_alignment}

\begin{theorem}[AuditMembrane NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:audit-membrane-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\AuditMembraneUp}$ surface for the carrier of \autoref{def:audit-membrane-carrier} consists of carrier admission for
$$
M=(G,B,R,D,H,C,P,N),
$$
gate-surface exactness, boundary-content display, refusal-result determinacy, paper-Lean drift resolution, componentwise $\hsame$ stability, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and non-escape for hidden global correctness, fuzzy target resolution, source-axiom admission, forbidden host-dependency admission, and untracked origin flow.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AuditMembraneUp.AuditMembraneCarrier\_namecert\_obligations}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:audit-membrane-carrier}. The packet admits exactly the rows $G,B,R,D,H,C,P,N$.

The audit obligations are then read row by row. The gate face is supplied by $G$, the boundary content by $B$, the refusal result by $R$, and the paper-Lean drift route by $D$.

Transport and consumer access are confined by $H$ and $C$. These rows replay the displayed audit packet under componentwise $\hsame$ movement and expose only the recorded routes.

Finally $P$ and $N$ record provenance and the local naming certificate. Since the excluded objects have no coordinate in the packet, the NameCert surface cannot export them.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[AuditMembrane boundary refusal]
\label{thm:audit-membrane-boundary-refusal}
For an accepted $\AuditMembraneUp$ packet, the refusal row $R$ is the complete public result of applying the gate surface $G$ to the boundary-content row $B$. A consumer may read admitted certificate content only through $G,B,R,H,C,P,N$, and any source axiom, forbidden host dependency, unresolved target name, or untracked origin edge marked as refused in $R$ has no exported route into substrate theorem content.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AuditMembraneUp.AuditMembraneCarrier\_refusal\_replay\_closure}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier and read the gate surface $G$ together with the boundary-content row $B$. These rows list the finite audit face and the finite items presented to that face.

The refusal result is the row $R$. Because $R$ is part of the same finite packet, every admitted or refused item remains tied to the displayed gate surface that classified it.

The replay rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve and expose the classification. They transport, route, record provenance, and name the packet, but they do not add a new coordinate by which a refused item can be read as theorem content.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[AuditMembrane drift resolution]
\label{thm:audit-membrane-drift-resolution}
For an accepted $\AuditMembraneUp$ packet, the drift-resolution row $D$ is the only route by which a paper marker becomes a local naming certificate. The route must display the marker text, the proposed Lean target name, the finite resolution result, the componentwise $\hsame$ transport row, the $\Cont$ replay row, the $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local $\NameCert_{\AuditMembraneUp}$ row.

If $D$ records refusal, the packet exports no replacement by similar spelling, neighboring namespace, prose interpretation, or consumer preference.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AuditMembraneUp.AuditMembraneCarrier\_drift\_resolution}
\begin{proof}
Project $D$ from the carrier. It contains the paper marker, the candidate Lean declaration, and the finite resolve-or-refuse result.

Move the row only through the displayed transport and replay data. The rows $H$ and $C$ preserve the same marker and the same resolution result, while $P$ and $N$ record provenance and the local name.

No carrier coordinate stores fuzzy matching, namespace guessing, prose reinterpretation, or consumer-selected substitution. A refused drift row therefore remains refused at every exported read.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[AuditMembrane consumer boundary]
\label{thm:audit-membrane-consumer-boundary}
Every downstream consumer of an accepted $\AuditMembraneUp$ packet factors through the finite rows
$$
G,B,R,D,H,C,P,N.
$$
Such a consumer may inspect the gate surface, boundary content, refusal result, paper-Lean resolution route, transport rows, replay rows, provenance, and local naming certificate, but it cannot extract a global audit-completeness theorem, a semantic conservativity theorem for arbitrary imports, a host-kernel completeness proof, or a theorem object for refused boundary content.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AuditMembraneUp.AuditMembraneCarrier\_consumer\_boundary}
\begin{proof}
Let a downstream consumer route be given. By \autoref{def:audit-membrane-carrier}, the source carrier is the finite tuple $G,B,R,D,H,C,P,N$.

The consumer first reads the gate and boundary rows, then the refusal and drift-resolution rows. The rows $H$ and $C$ supply all permitted transport and replay, and $P,N$ supply provenance and the local certificate.

The non-escape claim follows from the carrier inventory. None of the forbidden objects appears among the displayed rows, so no consumer route out of the packet can project it.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[AuditGateBoundary to AuditMembrane refusal bridge]
\label{thm:audit-gate-boundary-to-audit-membrane-refusal-bridge}
Let
$$
A=(S,D_A,L,O,H_A,C_A,P_A,G_A,N_A)
$$
be an accepted $\AuditGateBoundaryUp$ packet, and let
$$
M=(G_M,B,R,D_M,H_M,C_M,P_M,N_M)
$$
be an accepted $\AuditMembraneUp$ packet. Suppose that the membrane gate surface $G_M$ is the finite audit face obtained from the source-token row $S$, the dependency row $D_A$, the paper-Lean target-resolution row $L$, the origin/import row $O$, and the $\mathsf{GAP}$ row $G_A$ of $A$. Then every boundary item refused by the corresponding $\AuditGateBoundaryUp$ row remains refused by the membrane row $R$. In particular, a refused source axiom, forbidden host dependency, unresolved target name, untracked origin/import edge, or hidden global-correctness crossing has no exported route from $M$ into substrate theorem content.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AuditMembraneUp.AuditMembraneUp\_StdBridge}
\begin{proof}
Project the gate-boundary packet by \autoref{def:audit-gate-boundary-carrier}. Its displayed audit face consists of the source-token row $S$, the dependency row $D_A$, the paper-Lean target row $L$, the origin/import row $O$, and the $\mathsf{GAP}$ row $G_A$, together with transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.

The refusal content of these rows is fixed inside the gate-boundary packet. By \autoref{thm:audit-gate-boundary-namecert-obligations}, source-axiom admission, forbidden host-dependency admission, unresolved marker names, untracked origin flow, global semantic conservativity, and host-kernel completeness are non-escaping objects for $A$. By \autoref{thm:audit-gate-boundary-axiom-purity-soundness}, the rows $S$ and $D_A$ decide the source-token and forbidden-dependency boundary displayed by $A$. By \autoref{thm:audit-gate-boundary-paper-lean-drift-exactness}, $L$ permits only the named finite resolution result and gives no route through a nearby declaration or unresolved string. By \autoref{thm:audit-gate-boundary-origin-conservativity}, $O$ exposes only the finite origin/import ledger and gives no semantic conservativity theorem for arbitrary import flow. The row $G_A$ records the matching $\mathsf{GAP}$ boundary: the gate refuses the displayed boundary crossings and does not provide a hidden theorem of global correctness.

Now project the membrane packet by \autoref{def:audit-membrane-carrier}. The hypothesis identifies its gate surface $G_M$ with precisely the displayed audit face $S,D_A,L,O,G_A$ read from $A$. Hence an item refused at the gate-boundary side is the same displayed boundary item when it is presented in the membrane boundary-content row $B$: source axioms are read through $S$, forbidden dependencies through $D_A$, target names through $L$, origin/import edges through $O$, and hidden global-correctness crossings through $G_A$.

By \autoref{thm:audit-membrane-boundary-refusal}, the membrane refusal row $R$ is the complete public result of applying $G_M$ to $B$. Therefore the classification already refused by the corresponding row of $A$ cannot be changed by the membrane packet; it appears in $R$ as refused boundary content rather than admitted substrate supply.

Finally, \autoref{thm:audit-membrane-consumer-boundary} confines every downstream consumer of $M$ to the finite rows $G_M,B,R,D_M,H_M,C_M,P_M,N_M$. These rows allow inspection, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming, but none of them exports a theorem object for refused boundary content. Thus every item refused by the instantiated $\AuditGateBoundaryUp$ surface remains refused at the $\AuditMembraneUp$ boundary and cannot become substrate theorem content.
\end{proof}
