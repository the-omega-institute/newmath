\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\AuditMapFrontierIndexUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-audit-map-frontier-index-namecert}
\origin{ai}

The $\AuditMapFrontierIndexUp$ packet realizes the audit-map family methodology of \autoref{ch:audit-map-family-methodology} as finite frontier-index routing data. It sits beside the family ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-auditmapfamilyledger-namecert}, the substitution audit map of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-substitutionauditmap-namecert}, and the synthesis packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacicauditsynthesispacket-namecert}: those chapters expose local rows and family ledgers, while this packet records how frontier entries are routed between local maps, neighbouring-map rows, obstruction rows, and synthesis consumers without closing the frontier itself.

\begin{definition}[Audit-map frontier index carrier]
\label{def:audit-map-frontier-index-carrier}
An $\AuditMapFrontierIndexUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
I=(T,A,E,P,R,O,F,S,H,C,K,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $T$ is the map tag naming the local audit-map module and its BEDC substrate;
\item $A$ is the local audit row whose frontier is being indexed;
\item $E$ is the neighbouring-map row listing the sibling audit packets that may supply or consume frontier entries;
\item $P$ is the positive row recording entries already available in the local map;
\item $R$ is the conditional route row naming the displayed discharge route required before a conditional entry may be consumed;
\item $O$ is the obstruction row whose named boundary must remain visible to every route;
\item $F$ is the frontier row offered to downstream consumers as a named boundary and not as a closed theorem;
\item $S$ is the synthesis-consumer row naming the finite reader that consumes the indexed frontier together with neighbouring-map links;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the map tag, local audit row, neighbouring-map row, positive row, conditional route row, obstruction row, frontier row, synthesis-consumer row, provenance row, and local naming row;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes from the local map to neighbouring maps, from conditional rows to their named routes, from obstruction rows to frontier rows, and from frontier rows to synthesis consumers;
\item $K$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance row over the audit-map family ledger, local audit maps, neighbouring-map links, frontier rows, synthesis consumers, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\AuditMapFrontierIndexUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier is the finite frontier index. It contains no coordinate for a hidden discharge of the obstruction row, a closed proof of a frontier entry, an unlisted neighbouring map, a global subject-reduction theorem, full closed consistency, ambient confluence, or host semantic equality.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Audit-map frontier index NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:audit-map-frontier-index-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:audit-map-frontier-index-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\AuditMapFrontierIndexUp}$ surface consists exactly of five obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for $I=(T,A,E,P,R,O,F,S,H,C,K,N)$ as finite $\BHist$ data;
\item family-map readback through the map tag $T$, local audit row $A$, and neighbouring-map row $E$;
\item frontier-row exactness saying that $F$ is read only as the displayed frontier row associated to $A,E,P,R,O$;
\item obstruction non-escape saying that no consumer route can bypass $O$ or read $F$ as a discharge of $O$;
\item synthesis-consumer handoff saying that $S$ receives only the displayed frontier row, neighbouring-map links, transports, continuations, provenance, and local name through $H,C,K,N$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AuditMapFrontierIndexUp.AuditMapFrontierIndexTasteGate\_single\_carrier\_alignment}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier $I$. The rows $T,A,E$ identify the local audit map and its neighbouring-map surface, while $P,R,O,F$ separate positive entries, conditional routes, obstruction entries, and frontier entries.

The frontier exactness obligation is then finite row inspection. The only frontier coordinate in the packet is $F$, and the displayed continuation row $C$ routes it together with the local row, neighbouring-map row, positive row, conditional route row, and obstruction row.

For non-escape, inspect the obstruction and consumer rows. Since $O$ is a separate carrier coordinate and $F$ is only a frontier coordinate, a consumer cannot turn the frontier row into a proof of the obstruction or bypass the named conditional route $R$.

Finally read $S,H,C,K,N$. These rows hand the indexed frontier to synthesis consumers, preserve the same component rows under $\hsame$, replay the displayed $\Cont$ routes, record package provenance, and bind the local name. No remaining coordinate can supply an unlisted neighbouring map, hidden discharge, global subject-reduction theorem, full closed consistency, ambient confluence, or host semantic equality.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[Displayed frontier-index restriction]
\label{def:audit-map-frontier-index-displayed-restriction}
Let
$$
I=(T,A,E,P,R,O,F,S,H,C,K,N)
$$
be an accepted $\AuditMapFrontierIndexUp$ carrier in the sense of \autoref{def:audit-map-frontier-index-carrier} and \autoref{thm:audit-map-frontier-index-namecert-obligations}. A displayed subfamily restriction of $I$ is a finite row $E_0$ together with an inclusion certificate $E_0\subseteq E$ and the coordinatewise retained packet
$$
I|_{E_0}=(T,A,E_0,P|_{E_0},R|_{E_0},O|_{E_0},F|_{E_0},S|_{E_0},H|_{E_0},C|_{E_0},K|_{E_0},N|_{E_0}),
$$
where each restricted row is obtained by deleting exactly the entries whose neighbouring-map coordinate is not displayed in $E_0$, while keeping the local audit row $A$, the map tag $T$, the local positive row over $A$, and every retained transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming certificate attached to an entry of $E_0$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Audit-map frontier-index neighbouring restriction]
\label{thm:audit-map-frontier-index-neighbour-restriction}
Let
$$
I=(T,A,E,P,R,O,F,S,H,C,K,N)
$$
be an accepted $\AuditMapFrontierIndexUp$ carrier. Let $E_0\subseteq E$ be a displayed finite neighbouring-map subfamily, and suppose the restricted rows
$$
P|_{E_0},\ R|_{E_0},\ O|_{E_0},\ F|_{E_0},\ S|_{E_0},\ H|_{E_0},\ C|_{E_0},\ K|_{E_0},\ N|_{E_0}
$$
retain exactly the positive rows, conditional route rows, obstruction rows, frontier rows, synthesis-consumer rows, transports, continuations, provenance, and local $\NameCert$ rows whose neighbouring-map coordinate lies in $E_0$, while retaining the same local audit row $A$ and map tag $T$. Then the displayed restriction
$$
I|_{E_0}=(T,A,E_0,P|_{E_0},R|_{E_0},O|_{E_0},F|_{E_0},S|_{E_0},H|_{E_0},C|_{E_0},K|_{E_0},N|_{E_0})
$$
is an accepted $\AuditMapFrontierIndexUp$ carrier for the restricted neighbouring-map row. Moreover, if a neighbouring map $e$ is not displayed in $E_0$, then $e$ supplies no frontier entry of $I|_{E_0}$ and no synthesis consumer of $I|_{E_0}$ can read $e$ as a frontier source.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
By \autoref{def:audit-map-frontier-index-carrier}, an $\AuditMapFrontierIndexUp$ carrier is exhausted by the twelve displayed rows $T,A,E,P,R,O,F,S,H,C,K,N$. The restriction keeps the map tag $T$ and local audit row $A$, replaces only the neighbouring-map row $E$ by the displayed finite row $E_0$, and restricts every row whose entries are indexed by neighbouring maps to those coordinates lying in $E_0$. Since $E_0$ is finite and every retained coordinate is a coordinate already present in the finite packet $I$, the displayed tuple $I|_{E_0}$ is again finite $\BHist$ data.

It remains to check that the five local $\NameCert_{\AuditMapFrontierIndexUp}$ obligations of \autoref{thm:audit-map-frontier-index-namecert-obligations} survive this deletion. Carrier admission has just been checked by finite subrow formation. Family-map readback is local in $T,A,E$: after restriction the readback row is $T,A,E_0$, and each neighbour named there is named because $E_0\subseteq E$. Frontier-row exactness is also coordinatewise: $F|_{E_0}$ is read only as the displayed frontier row associated to $A,E_0,P|_{E_0},R|_{E_0},O|_{E_0}$, because no other frontier coordinate is inserted by the restriction.

For obstruction non-escape, inspect the retained obstruction and continuation rows. The row $O|_{E_0}$ is the only obstruction row of the restricted packet, and $C|_{E_0}$ contains only the displayed routes from the local map to neighbours in $E_0$, from retained conditional rows to their named routes, from retained obstruction rows to retained frontier rows, and from retained frontier rows to retained synthesis consumers. Thus a consumer of $I|_{E_0}$ cannot bypass $O|_{E_0}$, cannot discharge $O|_{E_0}$ through a deleted neighbour, and cannot read $F|_{E_0}$ as a proof of the obstruction.

For synthesis-consumer handoff, the consumer row is exactly $S|_{E_0}$, and its available data are exactly the retained frontier row, retained neighbouring-map links, retained transports $H|_{E_0}$, retained continuations $C|_{E_0}$, retained provenance $K|_{E_0}$, and retained local name $N|_{E_0}$. Hence the restricted packet satisfies the same five obligations with $E_0$ in place of $E$, so it is an accepted frontier index.

Finally let $e$ be a neighbouring map not displayed in $E_0$. A frontier entry of $I|_{E_0}$ must occur in the sole frontier coordinate $F|_{E_0}$, and every such coordinate was retained only when its neighbouring-map coordinate lay in $E_0$. Likewise every synthesis read in $I|_{E_0}$ factors through $S|_{E_0}$ and $C|_{E_0}$, whose entries were retained only over $E_0$. Therefore $e$ has no coordinate in the restricted frontier row and no displayed continuation into a restricted synthesis consumer, so it cannot supply a frontier entry of $I|_{E_0}$.
\end{proof}
