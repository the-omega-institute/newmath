\label{def:finite-lebesgue-number-real-completion-terminal-radius-ledger}

$$

\begin{theorem}[Finite Lebesgue-number terminal-radius choice refusal]
\label{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-terminal-radius-choice-refusal}
Let a downstream $\CompactMetricUp$, $\CompactNetModulusSelectorUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, or $\UniformModulusUp$ consumer preserve the Real-completion terminal-radius ledger
$$

of \autoref{def:finite-lebesgue-number-real-completion-terminal-radius-ledger}. Then the displayed rational-ball radius $\lambda$ cannot be replaced, at that terminal boundary, by a host minimum, a selected subcover radius, a choice-selected modulus, quotient $\RealUp$ equality, a selected limit, or an external compactness witness.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FiniteLebesgueNumberUp.FiniteLebesgueNumberTerminalRadiusChoiceRefusal}
\begin{proof}

By \autoref{def:finite-lebesgue-number-real-completion-terminal-radius-ledger}, the terminal packet exposes only $D,W,\lambda,M,S,Q,R,K,U,H,C,P,N$, with $K$ and $U$ serving as consumer rows and with no coordinate for open-cover compactness, a host minimum, quotient equality, selected limits, choice, or an ambient Real-completeness primitive.

Finally use the root consumer boundary cited in that proof. \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-root-radius-window-consumer-exactness} excludes open-cover compactness, host minima, quotient equality, selected limits, choice, and Real-completeness coordinates from exactly these root consumers. \end{proof}
