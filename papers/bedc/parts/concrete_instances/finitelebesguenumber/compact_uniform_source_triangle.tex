\begin{theorem}[Compact-uniform source triangle]
\label{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-compact-uniform-source-triangle}
Every accepted compact, continuous-map, or uniform-modulus consumer of a $\FiniteLebesgueNumberUp$ positive-radius row factors through exactly the displayed Phase Real source triangle
$$
\begin{gathered}
\DyadicRatCoreUp,\quad \StreamNameUp,\quad \RegSeqRatUp,\quad \RealUp
\end{gathered}
$$
before the route reaches $\CompactMetricUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, or $\UniformModulusUp$. The four source faces are read through the same $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows tied to the visible $\DyadicCoverUp$, $\RationalBallUp$, and $\DyadicMeshUp$ packet, and no accepted downstream consumer may read a detached radius before that triangle has been consumed.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Start at the open Phase boundary. \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-open-phase-source-nonescape} keeps every public source read inside the four-face route, and \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-open-phase-radius-classifier-pullback} identifies the positive-radius classifier with the same $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ source packet.

Next pass through the sibling route. \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-l10-sibling-route} supplies the finite-name chain that carries the radius through $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows, while \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-uniform-continuity-handoff} makes the compact, continuous-map, and uniform-modulus consumers read that carried packet rather than an independent consumer datum.

The source chapters \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert} give the four faces their public NameCert readings. Combining those readings with the cited finite-lebesgue surfaces yields the displayed source triangle before any $\CompactMetricUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, or $\UniformModulusUp$ endpoint is available.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FiniteLebesgueNumberUp.FiniteLebesgueNumberCompactUniformSourceTriangle}

\begin{theorem}[Open-phase nonchoice cover]
\label{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-open-phase-nonchoice-cover}
The open Phase route used by $\FiniteLebesgueNumberUp$ contains no coordinate for open-cover compactness, selected subcovers, quotient equality, host minima, or external modulus data. Any compact-uniform consumer admitted by the route receives only the finite $\DyadicCoverUp$, $\RationalBallUp$, $\DyadicMeshUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows displayed by the source triangle of \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-compact-uniform-source-triangle}.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FiniteLebesgueNumberUp.FiniteLebesgueNumberOpenPhaseNonchoiceCover}

\begin{proof}
The nonescape theorem rules out host $\RealUp$ equality, open-cover compactness, selected subcovers, and external modulus reads before the four-face passage. The classifier pullback then fixes the positive-radius read to the finite dyadic cover, rational-ball, dyadic mesh, and four source faces.

The L10 sibling route and uniform-continuity handoff preserve only the displayed transport and naming rows. Since none of those rows has a field for a chosen subcover, quotient equality, host minimum, or detached modulus, the downstream compact-uniform consumer cannot obtain such data from the open Phase route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Uniform consumer radius cover]
\label{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-uniform-consumer-radius-cover}
For later formalization under $\mathsf{BEDC.Derived.FiniteLebesgueNumberUp}$, the downstream radius-cover handoff of $\FiniteLebesgueNumberUp$ is the single consumer statement that a $\CompactMetricUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, or $\UniformModulusUp$ endpoint may consume the positive radius only through the source triangle of \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-compact-uniform-source-triangle} and the nonchoice cover of \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-open-phase-nonchoice-cover}. Thus the consumer-visible radius cover is finite, named, and exhausted by the Phase Real and Completion route.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Apply the compact-uniform source triangle to obtain the required order: $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ are consumed before the compact, continuous-map, or uniform-modulus endpoint can read the radius.

Then apply the open-phase nonchoice cover. It removes open-cover compactness, selected subcovers, quotient equality, host minima, and external modulus data from the same handoff, so the endpoint receives exactly the finite named radius-cover route and no alternate source.
\end{proof}
