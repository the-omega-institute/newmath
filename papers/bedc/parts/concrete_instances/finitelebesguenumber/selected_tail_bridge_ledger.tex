\begin{definition}[Finite Lebesgue-number selected-tail bridge ledger]
\label{def:finite-lebesgue-number-selected-tail-bridge-ledger}
The selected-tail bridge ledger for an accepted $\FiniteLebesgueNumberUp$ carrier is the finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
J=(D,W,\lambda,M,A_T,W_T,K_T,S_T,R_T,H,C,P,N).
$$
Here $D,W,\lambda,M$ are the displayed $\FiniteLebesgueNumberUp$ dyadic-cover, finite-window, rational-ball radius, and dyadic-mesh rows. The rows $A_T,W_T,K_T,S_T,R_T$ are the selected finite-tail admission, stream-window, regular-rational readback, dyadic tolerance ledger, and Real seal rows supplied by the current $\SelectedTailSeedUp$ seed. The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ record componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and the compatible local $\NameCert$ rows.

The ledger is accepted only when the finite Lebesgue radius read and the selected-tail Real-window read are carried by the same structural support. Thus the compact-uniform route may consume $\lambda$ only together with $D,W,M,A_T,W_T,K_T,S_T,R_T,H,C,P,N$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Selected-tail radius admission]
\label{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-selected-tail-radius-admission}
Any accepted finite Lebesgue radius read entering the compact-uniform route factors through $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, and the selected-tail seed handoff before $\CompactNetModulusSelectorUp$ consumes the radius. More precisely, if the read is admitted by the ledger $J$ of \autoref{def:finite-lebesgue-number-selected-tail-bridge-ledger}, then the consumer-facing radius is the same finite packet route
$$
D,W,\lambda,M,A_T,W_T,K_T,S_T,R_T,H,C,P,N
$$
and not a radius obtained from open-cover compactness, host real equality, selected subcovers, or ambient Real completeness.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FiniteLebesgueNumberUp.FiniteLebesgueNumberSelectedTailRadiusAdmission}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the $\FiniteLebesgueNumberUp$ carrier. The carrier definition \autoref{def:finite-lebesgue-number-carrier} and the root package theorem \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-root-unblock-package} identify the radius read as the displayed dyadic-cover, finite-window, rational-ball, and mesh packet $D,W,\lambda,M$ with the same $H,C,P,N$ support used by compact consumers.

Next read the selected-tail seed scope. The stream-regular readback theorem \autoref{thm:selected-tail-seed-stream-regular-readback-scope} binds the $W_T,K_T$ part of the route to $\StreamNameUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$. The dyadic Real-seal theorem \autoref{thm:selected-tail-seed-dyadic-real-seal-scope} keeps $S_T,R_T$ as the finite $\DyadicRatCoreUp$-to-$\RealUp$ seal.

Finally apply the finite-tail Real-boundary theorem \autoref{thm:selected-tail-seed-finite-tail-real-boundary-scope}. It identifies $A_T,W_T,K_T,S_T,R_T$ as the selected-tail boundary consumed downstream. Since the bridge ledger stores this boundary beside $D,W,\lambda,M$, the compact-net selector receives the finite radius only after the selected-tail source route has been displayed.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Selected-tail uniform consumer route]
\label{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-selected-tail-uniform-consumer-route}
Every $\UniformModulusUp$ consumer downstream of an accepted selected-tail bridge ledger receives the same finite radius packet as $\CompactNetModulusSelectorUp$:
$$
D,W,\lambda,M,A_T,W_T,K_T,S_T,R_T,H,C,P,N .
$$
The consumer cannot replace this packet by open-cover compactness, host minima, selected subcovers, quotient equality, choice, or ambient Real completeness.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FiniteLebesgueNumberUp.FiniteLebesgueNumberSelectedTailUniformConsumerRoute}
\begin{proof}
Use \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-selected-tail-radius-admission} to fix the compact-net input. The admitted radius already factors through the $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, and $\RealUp$ selected-tail route before the compact-net selector can consume it.

The compact-uniform handoff \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-compact-uniform-handoff-scope} and the consumer nonescape triad \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-consumer-nonescape-triad} then pass the same displayed radius packet from $\CompactNetModulusSelectorUp$ to $\UniformModulusUp$. These rows expose the finite cover, mesh, rational-ball radius, selected-tail source boundary, and structural support; they do not expose a second compactness or modulus interface.

The exclusions are coordinate exclusions. Open-cover compactness, host minima, selected subcovers, quotient equality, choice, and ambient Real completeness are absent from $J$, while $H,C,P,N$ only transport, replay, source, and name the rows already listed. Hence the uniform consumer route is the same finite selected-tail bridge packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Finite Lebesgue-number total-bounded radius source lock]
\label{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-total-bounded-radius-source-lock}
Any $\TotallyBoundedUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\CompactNetModulusSelectorUp$, or $\UniformModulusUp$ consumer that preserves the selected-tail bridge ledger of \autoref{def:finite-lebesgue-number-selected-tail-bridge-ledger} can read the positive radius only through the same finite packet
$$
D,W,\lambda,M,A_T,W_T,K_T,S_T,R_T,H,C,P,N .
$$
Equivalently, the consumer-facing radius is unavailable unless the selected finite-tail admission, stream-window row, regular-rational readback, dyadic tolerance ledger, Real-seal row, finite Lebesgue radius row, dyadic-cover row, finite-window row, mesh row, componentwise transport, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming rows are all present. The route contains no open-cover compactness, host minimum, selected subcover, quotient equality, choice, or ambient Real completeness coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FiniteLebesgueNumberUp.FiniteLebesgueNumberTotalBoundedRadiusSourceLock}
\begin{proof}
Start with the selected-tail radius admission theorem, \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-selected-tail-radius-admission}. It identifies the compact-net input radius with the displayed selected-tail packet and prevents the radius from being read before the stream, regular-rational, dyadic tolerance, and Real-seal rows have appeared.

Next use the selected-tail uniform consumer route, \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-selected-tail-uniform-consumer-route}. That theorem passes the same packet from $\CompactNetModulusSelectorUp$ to $\UniformModulusUp$ and records that the uniform consumer receives no second compactness or modulus interface.

The root finite Lebesgue-number NameCert obligations, \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-namecert-obligations}, together with the root unblock package \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-root-unblock-package}, supply the finite dyadic-cover, finite-window, rational-ball radius, mesh, transport, continuation, provenance, and naming rows. These obligations keep $\lambda$ attached to $D,W,M,H,C,P,N$ before the selected-tail rows are joined to the route.

Finally apply the compact consumer nonchoice boundary, \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-compact-consumer-nonchoice-boundary}. It keeps $\TotallyBoundedUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, and $\CompactNetModulusSelectorUp$ consumers inside the displayed finite route and excludes open-cover compactness, host minima, selected subcovers, quotient equality, choice, and ambient Real completeness. Therefore any preserving compact or uniform consumer reads the positive radius exactly through $D,W,\lambda,M,A_T,W_T,K_T,S_T,R_T,H,C,P,N$.
\end{proof}
