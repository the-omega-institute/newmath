\begin{theorem}[Finite Lebesgue-number L10 phase consumer readiness]
\label{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-l10-phase-consumer-readiness}
Every accepted $\FiniteLebesgueNumberUp$ consumer route used at the Phase Real or Completion boundary factors through the finite packet over $\DyadicCoverUp$, $\RationalBallUp$, $\DyadicMeshUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\CompactNetModulusSelectorUp$, $\UniformModulusUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$. Continuous-map and totally-bounded consumers read the same route through the displayed compact-uniform handoff. The route exports no open-cover compactness, selected subcover, host minimum, quotient equality, choice, external modulus, or $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the compact-uniform export. \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-public-compact-uniform-export} identifies the public handoff as the finite dyadic-cover, rational-ball, mesh, stream, regular-rational, Real, transport, replay, provenance, naming, and compact-net selector packet.

Next attach the L10 source chain. \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-dyadic-cover-real-route} sends each finite-window read through $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ before mesh and cover-cell readback, and \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-source-chain-admission} places the displayed dyadic rows in the same $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ packet.

Use ledger transport to keep the route stable. \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-ledger-transport} preserves the positive radius ledger together with the cover, mesh, stream, regular-rational, and Real rows under $\hsame$ transport, so later compact, continuous-map, totally-bounded, uniform-modulus, or Completion-facing consumers cannot detach a scalar or host-side witness from the packet.

Finally apply the nonescape boundary. \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-consumer-nonescape-triad} excludes open-cover compactness, host minima, selected subcovers, quotient equality, choice, and external modulus data from the compact, compact-net, and uniform consumer endpoints. Since the Phase Real and Completion handoffs read through those endpoints and the same L10 source packet, their consumer-ready route is exactly the displayed finite packet and not an additional compactness, choice, modulus, or completeness principle.
\end{proof}
