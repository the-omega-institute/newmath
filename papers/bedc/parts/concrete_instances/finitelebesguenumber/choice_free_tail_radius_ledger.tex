\begin{definition}[Finite Lebesgue-number choice-free tail-radius ledger]
\label{def:finite-lebesgue-number-choice-free-tail-radius-ledger}
For an accepted $\FiniteLebesgueNumberUp$ route, the choice-free tail-radius ledger is the finite $\BHist$ row family
$$
\Theta_{\lambda}^{\mathrm{tail}}=(D,B,\lambda,M,A_T,S_T,Q_T,R_T,H,C,P,N).
$$
Here $D$ is the displayed $\DyadicCoverUp$ cover row, $B$ is the $\RationalBallUp$ radius readback row for the positive radius $\lambda$, $M$ is the $\DyadicMeshUp$ mesh row, and $A_T$ is the selected finite-tail admission row. The rows $S_T,Q_T,R_T$ record the $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ tail endpoint route; $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport, $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ replay, $P$ records $\Pkg$ provenance, and $N$ records the compatible local $\NameCert$ rows.

The ledger is accepted only when the dyadic cover, rational-ball radius, dyadic mesh, selected finite-tail admission, stream-name row, regular-rational row, Real endpoint row, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows are read as one finite packet. A compact-uniform consumer may inspect $\lambda$ only as this packet and may not introduce a separate open-cover compactness, selected-subcover, host-minimum, quotient-stream, countable-choice, detached-completeness, or external-modulus coordinate.
\end{definition}
\leandef{BEDC.Derived.FiniteLebesgueNumberUp.FiniteLebesgueNumberChoiceFreeTailRadiusLedger}

\begin{theorem}[Choice-free tail-radius admission]
\label{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-choice-free-tail-radius-admission}
Every $\CompactMetricUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\TotallyBoundedUp$, or $\UniformModulusUp$ consumer admitted by the L10 route of an accepted $\FiniteLebesgueNumberUp$ carrier factors its tail-radius read through the ledger $\Theta_{\lambda}^{\mathrm{tail}}$ of \autoref{def:finite-lebesgue-number-choice-free-tail-radius-ledger}. Thus the consumer-facing radius is the finite row family
$$
D,B,\lambda,M,A_T,S_T,Q_T,R_T,H,C,P,N
$$
and no admitted read bypasses the selected finite-tail admission, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, or $\NameCert$ rows.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FiniteLebesgueNumberUp.FiniteLebesgueNumberChoiceFreeTailRadiusAdmission}
\begin{proof}
Start with selected-tail exhaustion. \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-selected-tail-modulus-exhaustion} forces every selected-tail witness and every finite-window witness visible to a modulus consumer through the displayed finite packet, with no hidden selector, quotient stream, countable choice coordinate, host real equality, or detached $\RealUp$ representative.

Next use the root-radius coherence package. \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-root-radius-coherence} keeps the positive radius stable under the displayed $\hsame$ transports and $\Cont$ replays, while \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-compact-uniform-radius-admission} makes compact, compact-net, continuous-map, and uniform-modulus consumers read that coherent finite ledger rather than an outside compactness datum.

Finally read the L10 handoff. \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-l10-phase-consumer-readiness} places the compact-uniform endpoints on the Phase Real route, and \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-compact-uniform-source-triangle} identifies the source faces as $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ tied to the dyadic cover, rational-ball, and mesh rows. Combining these cited rows gives exactly $\Theta_{\lambda}^{\mathrm{tail}}$, so every admitted tail-radius read factors through the stated finite ledger.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Tail-radius transport determinacy]
\label{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-tail-radius-transport-determinacy}
Let two accepted $\FiniteLebesgueNumberUp$ routes carry choice-free tail-radius ledgers $\Theta_{\lambda}^{\mathrm{tail}}$ and $\Theta_{\mu}^{\mathrm{tail}}$. If their $\DyadicCoverUp$, $\RationalBallUp$, $\DyadicMeshUp$, selected finite-tail admission, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ rows agree componentwise under the recorded $\hsame$ transports, and their $\Cont$ replays use the same $\Pkg$ provenance and local $\NameCert$ support, then they expose the same radius to every $\CompactMetricUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\TotallyBoundedUp$, and $\UniformModulusUp$ consumer admitted by the L10 route.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FiniteLebesgueNumberUp.FiniteLebesgueNumberTailRadiusTransportDeterminacy}
\begin{proof}
First identify the two ledgers row by row. The hypotheses make the cover, rational-ball radius, mesh, selected finite-tail admission, stream-name, regular-rational, and Real endpoint rows agree under $\hsame$, and the shared $\Cont$ replay fixes the order in which a consumer can inspect them.

Now apply root-radius coherence. \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-root-radius-coherence} says that accepted routes agreeing on the radius, source, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming support expose the same root-radius obligation to downstream consumers.

It remains to pass from root radius to tail radius. By \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-selected-tail-modulus-exhaustion}, the selected finite-tail side has no second modulus selector, quotient stream, choice coordinate, host equality, or detached Real representative. By \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-l10-phase-consumer-readiness}, the compact-uniform consumers read only the L10 packet. Therefore the two accepted routes expose the same consumer radius across $\CompactMetricUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\TotallyBoundedUp$, and $\UniformModulusUp$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Compact-uniform choice refusal]
\label{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-compact-uniform-choice-refusal}
The compact-uniform handoff of an accepted $\FiniteLebesgueNumberUp$ route cannot replace the choice-free tail-radius ledger of \autoref{def:finite-lebesgue-number-choice-free-tail-radius-ledger} by open-cover compactness, selected subcovers, host minima, quotient stream equality, countable choice, detached $\RealUp$ completeness, or external modulus data. Any radius visible to $\CompactMetricUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\TotallyBoundedUp$, or $\UniformModulusUp$ is read only from
$$
\Theta_{\lambda}^{\mathrm{tail}}=(D,B,\lambda,M,A_T,S_T,Q_T,R_T,H,C,P,N).
$$
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FiniteLebesgueNumberUp.FiniteLebesgueNumberCompactUniformChoiceRefusal}
\begin{proof}
Read the ledger definition first. \autoref{def:finite-lebesgue-number-choice-free-tail-radius-ledger} lists the only rows available at the tail-radius handoff: dyadic cover, rational-ball radius, dyadic mesh, selected finite-tail admission, stream-name, regular-rational, Real endpoint, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming.

The selected-tail and source-triangle theorems remove the proposed outside coordinates. \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-selected-tail-modulus-exhaustion} rules out hidden modulus selectors, quotient streams, countable choice, host real equality, and detached Real representatives, while \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-compact-uniform-source-triangle} requires the compact-uniform consumer to pass through the named Phase Real source faces before the radius is visible.

Finally apply compact-uniform admission along the L10 route. \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-l10-phase-consumer-readiness} and \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-compact-uniform-radius-admission} leave no consumer coordinate outside the finite ledger. Since open-cover compactness, selected subcovers, host minima, quotient stream equality, countable choice, detached completeness, and external modulus data are absent from that ledger, the handoff refuses each of those readings.
\end{proof}
