\begin{definition}[Finite Lebesgue-number compact-continuous unblock ledger]
\label{def:finite-lebesgue-number-compact-continuous-unblock-ledger}
For an accepted $\FiniteLebesgueNumberUp$ carrier, the compact-continuous unblock ledger is the displayed finite packet
$$
U_{\mathrm{cc}}=(D,W,\lambda,M,H,C,P,N;K,F,Q)
$$
where $D,W,\lambda,M,H,C,P,N$ are the dyadic cover row, finite-window row, positive rational-ball radius ledger, dyadic mesh ledger, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ row of the finite Lebesgue-number carrier. The row $K$ is the compact-net consumer row over $\CompactMetricUp$ and $\CompactNetModulusSelectorUp$, $F$ is the $\ContinuousMapUp$ pointwise-modulus consumer row over the same finite-window source, and $Q$ is the $\UniformModulusUp$ handoff row consuming the radius read from $\lambda$.

The ledger is accepted only when $K,F,Q$ read the same finite dyadic-radius packet $D,W,\lambda,M,H,C,P,N$ through the same $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support. It has no coordinate for open-cover compactness, selected subcovers, host minima, quotient equality, choice, detached $\RealUp$ completeness, or an external modulus selector.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Finite Lebesgue-number compact-continuous triad]
\label{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-compact-continuous-triad}
Every $\CompactMetricUp$, $\CompactNetModulusSelectorUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, and $\UniformModulusUp$ consumer admitted by the compact-continuous unblock ledger of \autoref{def:finite-lebesgue-number-compact-continuous-unblock-ledger} reads the same finite dyadic-radius packet
$$
D,W,\lambda,M,H,C,P,N .
$$
The compact row, compact-net row, continuous-map pointwise row, and uniform-modulus handoff row are therefore a single $\BHist$ route rather than four independent parameter surfaces.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FiniteLebesgueNumberUp.FiniteLebesgueNumberCompactContinuousTriad}
\begin{proof}
Begin with finite-window coverage exactness. \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-window-coverage-exactness} sends every accepted window read through $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, the mesh row $M$, and the named dyadic cover row $D$ before any consumer can inspect the radius ledger.

Next attach the compact consumer nonchoice boundary and compact-uniform selector handoff. \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-compact-consumer-nonchoice-boundary} keeps compact and compact-net consumers inside the displayed finite packet, while \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-compact-uniform-selector-handoff} sends the same positive radius ledger from that packet to the compact-net and uniform endpoints.

Use source-chain admission and ledger transport for the shared support. \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-source-chain-admission} admits the route only through the common $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows, and \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-ledger-transport} prevents $\lambda$ from separating from the dyadic cover, finite window, mesh, stream, regular-rational, and real rows.

Finally apply the consumer nonescape triad. \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-consumer-nonescape-triad} excludes open-cover compactness, host minima, selected subcovers, quotient equality, choice, and external modulus data. The continuous-map row $F$ in the ledger is therefore read only as a pointwise consumer of the same finite radius packet, and the four consumer rows form the stated compact-continuous triad.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Finite Lebesgue-number source-radius unblock]
\label{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-source-radius-unblock}
In every accepted compact-continuous unblock ledger $U_{\mathrm{cc}}$, a source-side radius request reaches $\lambda$ only after the displayed finite-window source has passed through $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\DyadicMeshUp$, and $\DyadicCoverUp$ rows carried by $H,C,P,N$. Thus the root unblock surface for compact, compact-net, continuous-map, and uniform-modulus consumers is the finite dyadic-radius packet itself, not an open-cover or host-completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FiniteLebesgueNumberUp.FiniteLebesgueNumberSourceRadiusUnblock}
\begin{proof}
Start with the source-chain admission theorem. It requires the dyadic cover, rational-ball radius, and mesh rows to pass through the stream-name, regular-rational, and real rows before the route is admitted into the shared support.

Now use finite-window coverage exactness. Each accepted read from $W$ lands in a named mesh and cover cell before the radius ledger is visible, so a source request cannot enter through a detached real representative or an arbitrary open family.

Transport the radius with ledger transport. The $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ rows preserve the positive radius together with the cover, mesh, stream, regular-rational, and real rows; $\lambda$ is never a scalar outside the finite packet.

The compact consumer nonchoice boundary supplies the negative side. It refuses open-cover compactness, selected subcovers, host minima, quotient equality, choice, detached $\RealUp$ completeness, and external selectors, leaving the finite dyadic-radius packet as the only root unblock surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Finite Lebesgue-number uniform-continuity handoff]
\label{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-uniform-continuity-handoff}
Every $\UniformModulusUp$ handoff accepted by the compact-continuous unblock ledger consumes the $\ContinuousMapUp$ pointwise row only after $\CompactMetricUp$ and $\CompactNetModulusSelectorUp$ have exposed the same positive finite-radius packet $D,W,\lambda,M,H,C,P,N$. \end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FiniteLebesgueNumberUp.FiniteLebesgueNumberUniformContinuityHandoff}
\begin{proof}
First use compact-uniform selector handoff. It places the uniform-modulus read downstream of the positive radius ledger already carried by $D,W,\lambda,M,H,C,P,N$.

Next use the compact radius source-accountability theorem. \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-compact-radius-source-accountability} identifies the compact and compact-net source of the radius as the displayed dyadic cover, mesh, stream-name, regular-rational, real, transport, continuation, provenance, and naming rows.

Read the continuous-map row through the ledger definition. The pointwise row $F$ is admitted only as a consumer row over the same finite-window source and the same $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support, so it cannot supply an independent global modulus surface.

Finally invoke uniform modulus source factorization and consumer nonescape. \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-uniform-modulus-source-factorization} sends the uniform consumer through the same source packet, and \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-consumer-nonescape-triad} removes open-cover compactness, selected subcovers, host minima, quotient equality, choice, detached real completeness, and external modulus selectors from the handoff.
