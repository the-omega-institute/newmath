\begin{theorem}[Compact radius source-accountability]
\label{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-compact-radius-source-accountability}
Every $\CompactMetricUp$ or $\CompactNetModulusSelectorUp$ radius read from an accepted $\FiniteLebesgueNumberUp$ carrier factors through the displayed source-accountability surface
$$
\DyadicCoverUp,\ \DyadicMeshUp,\ \StreamNameUp,\ \RegSeqRatUp,\ \RealUp,\ \hsame,\ \Cont,\ \Pkg,\ \NameCert .
$$
The compact or compact-net consumer may read the positive radius only as part of that finite dyadic packet. It receives no open-cover compactness, selected subcover, host minimum, quotient equality, choice, or radius selector outside the displayed $\BHist$ ledger.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the compact-uniform selector handoff. \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-compact-uniform-selector-handoff} sends every compact-net and uniform-facing radius read through the displayed finite cover, finite window, rational radius, dyadic mesh, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.

Now apply the L10 source-lock. \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-l10-source-lock} forces the consumer-facing radius route through $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\DyadicMeshUp$, and $\DyadicCoverUp$ before any compact or compact-net consumer can inspect the positive ledger.

Finally use the compact-route dyadic modulus package. \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-compact-route-dyadic-modulus-package} identifies the compact-facing packet with the finite dyadic modulus route carried by $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$. Since the packet has no open-cover, subcover-selection, host-minimum, quotient-equality, choice, or detached selector coordinate, every compact radius read has the stated source-accountability surface.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FiniteLebesgueNumberUp.FiniteLebesgueNumberCompactRadiusSourceAccountability}

\begin{theorem}[Uniform modulus source factorization]
\label{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-uniform-modulus-source-factorization}
Every $\UniformModulusUp$ consumer of an accepted $\FiniteLebesgueNumberUp$ packet reads the same positive finite-radius ledger used by $\CompactMetricUp$ and $\CompactNetModulusSelectorUp$ consumers. The factorization runs through the displayed $\DyadicCoverUp$, $\DyadicMeshUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows, rather than through an external modulus function or an independently supplied continuity surface.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FiniteLebesgueNumberUp.FiniteLebesgueNumberUniformModulusSourceFactorization}
\begin{proof}
First read the compact radius source-accountability theorem above. It fixes the compact and compact-net radius source as the displayed dyadic packet and forbids a detached radius selector before the uniform consumer appears.

Next apply the compact-uniform selector handoff and the compact-route dyadic modulus package. These rows pass the same positive ledger from the compact route to the compact-net selector and keep the ledger inside the finite dyadic modulus packet.

Use the consumer nonescape triad for the final boundary. \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-consumer-nonescape-triad} states that $\CompactMetricUp$, $\CompactNetModulusSelectorUp$, and $\UniformModulusUp$ consumers receive only the displayed finite route. Hence the uniform modulus consumer reads the already displayed positive radius ledger and cannot import an external modulus function, a separate continuous-map modulus, open-cover compactness, quotient equality, or choice.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Total-bounded ledger readback]
\label{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-total-bounded-ledger-readback}
Every $\TotallyBoundedUp$ handoff downstream of an accepted $\FiniteLebesgueNumberUp$ carrier receives the displayed finite dyadic cover ledger
$$
D,W,\lambda,M,H,C,P,N .
$$
Its readback may inspect the $\DyadicCoverUp$, $\DyadicMeshUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows already present in that ledger, but it cannot recover open-cover compactness, selected subcovers, quotient equality, choice, host compactness, or an external modulus surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the total-bounded handoff obligation. \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-total-bounded-handoff} identifies $D,W,\lambda,M,H,C,P,N$ as the exact packet delivered to $\TotallyBoundedUp$ and compact-metric consumers.

Attach the L10 source-lock and the compact radius source-accountability theorem above. Together they force the radius, mesh, stream, regular-rational, real, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming reads to occur inside the displayed finite dyadic ledger before any total-bounded or compact-facing consumer can use the handoff.

Finally invoke the consumer nonescape triad. \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-consumer-nonescape-triad} excludes open-cover compactness, host minima, selected subcovers, quotient equality, choice, and external modulus data from the downstream consumer route. Therefore the total-bounded handoff reads back exactly the displayed ledger and cannot reconstruct any stronger compactness or selection principle from it.
\end{proof}
