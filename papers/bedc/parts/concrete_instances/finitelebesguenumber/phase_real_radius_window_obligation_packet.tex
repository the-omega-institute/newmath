\begin{definition}[Finite Lebesgue-number Phase Real radius-window carrier]
\label{def:finite-lebesgue-number-phase-real-radius-window-carrier}
For an accepted $\FiniteLebesgueNumberUp$ route, the Phase Real radius-window carrier is the finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
\mathcal{W}_{\lambda}^{\mathrm{PhaseReal}}=(E,R,D,W,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
where $E$ is the finite Lebesgue-number request row, $R$ is the rational-ball radius row, $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ radius readback row, $W$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window row, $S$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$-to-$\RealUp$ seal route, and $H,C,P,N$ are the componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and compatible local $\NameCert$ rows. \end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Finite Lebesgue-number dyadic radius-window admission]
\label{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-dyadic-radius-window-admission}
Every admitted radius-window read of an accepted $\FiniteLebesgueNumberUp$ Phase Real carrier exposes the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ radius readback row $D$ before the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window row $W$. Equivalently, a downstream compact, continuous, totally bounded, or uniform consumer may read $W$ only as part of the packet $\mathcal{W}_{\lambda}^{\mathrm{PhaseReal}}$ of \autoref{def:finite-lebesgue-number-phase-real-radius-window-carrier}, with the displayed dyadic radius read already present.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier definition. The packet $\mathcal{W}_{\lambda}^{\mathrm{PhaseReal}}$ places the finite Lebesgue-number request $E$, the rational-ball radius row $R$, and the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ readback row $D$ before the finite stream window $W$ is available to any consumer.

The existing root and open Phase surfaces give the same order at the chapter boundary. \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-root-radius-window-obligation-surface} identifies the positive radius and finite-window obligations with the dyadic, stream, regular-rational, Real, history, transport, continuation, provenance, and naming rows. \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-open-phase-root-unblock-radius-coverage} then exports the root radius read through the finite dyadic and four-face Real packet.

Finally use the Phase Real audit and source exactness. \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-phase-real-radius-audit-carrier} and \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-phase-real-four-face-source-exactness} keep the consumer-visible radius inside $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ with the same $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support. Since $W$ has no row outside this packet, an admitted window read can occur only after $D$ has been displayed.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Finite Lebesgue-number Real seal radius readback]
\label{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-real-seal-radius-readback}
Any $\RealUp$ seal read used by an accepted $\FiniteLebesgueNumberUp$ Phase Real radius-window carrier factors through the ordered rows
$$
D,\quad W,\quad S
$$
of \autoref{def:finite-lebesgue-number-phase-real-radius-window-carrier}. Thus the Real-side radius is read back only through the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ radius row, the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window row, and the $\RegSeqRatUp$-to-$\RealUp$ seal route, together with the shared $H,C,P,N$ support.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First expose the dyadic admission. By \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-dyadic-radius-window-admission}, an admitted radius-window read displays $D$ before $W$. The dyadic readback is therefore already part of the carrier before the stream window is inspected.

Next attach the regular-rational and Real faces. \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-phase-real-four-face-source-exactness} routes every consumer-visible radius through $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$. The definition of $\mathcal{W}_{\lambda}^{\mathrm{PhaseReal}}$ records the $\RegSeqRatUp$-to-$\RealUp$ part of this route as $S$.

The terminal boundary supplies the readback exclusion. \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-real-completion-terminal-radius-exhaustion} keeps terminal radius reads on the same L10 packet and denies host minima, quotient equality, selected limits, countable choice, and detached Real completeness at the Real boundary. Hence any Real seal read factors through $D,W,S$ and the common $H,C,P,N$ rows, rather than through an off-packet Real source.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Finite Lebesgue-number open Phase radius-window nonescape]
\label{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-open-phase-nonescape}
No accepted $\FiniteLebesgueNumberUp$ open Phase radius-window carrier can escape to quotient streams, host limits, countable choice, open-cover compactness, or ambient Real completeness. Every admitted compact, continuous, totally bounded, compact-net, or uniform radius read remains inside the finite packet
$$
E,\quad R,\quad D,\quad W,\quad S,\quad H,\quad C,\quad P,\quad N
$$
of \autoref{def:finite-lebesgue-number-phase-real-radius-window-carrier}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the two positive readback gates. \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-dyadic-radius-window-admission} forces the window side to expose the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ row before $\StreamNameUp$ can be consumed. \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-real-seal-radius-readback} then factors each Real seal read through $D,W,S$ with the shared support rows.

Now read the existing nonescape boundaries. \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-open-phase-source-nonescape} keeps the open Phase source from bypassing the stream and regular-rational rows through host sequences, quotient stream equality, selected representatives, choice, or ambient completeness. \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-regseq-streamname-nonescape} gives the same refusal for the $\StreamNameUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$ faces.

Finally apply terminal and consumer exhaustion. \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-terminal-radius-phase-consumer-nonescape} and \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-l10-consumer-radius-exhaustion} keep compact, compact-net, continuous, totally bounded, and uniform consumers inside the four-face Phase Real and Completion packet. Since the carrier itself has no coordinate for quotient streams, host limits, countable choice, open-cover compactness, or ambient Real completeness, none of those rows can be exported from an admitted radius-window read.
\end{proof}
