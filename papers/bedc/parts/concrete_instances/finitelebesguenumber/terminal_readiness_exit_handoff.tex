\begin{theorem}[Finite Lebesgue-number terminal-readiness face projection]
\label{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-terminal-readiness-face-projection}
Every accepted $\FiniteLebesgueNumberUp$ terminal readiness ledger projects componentwise to the four displayed Phase Real exit faces
$$
\DyadicRatCoreUp,\quad \StreamNameUp,\quad \RegSeqRatUp,\quad \RealUp
$$
and to the support rows $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$. The projection keeps the dyadic-cover, rational-ball, dyadic-mesh, compact, continuous, totally bounded, compact-net, and uniform endpoint rows only as coordinates of the same finite terminal packet; it does not introduce a second radius source.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FiniteLebesgueNumberUp.FiniteLebesgueNumberTerminalReadinessFaceProjection}
\begin{proof}
Read the terminal ledger first. \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-phase-real-terminal-radius-readiness} makes every accepted compact, continuous, totally bounded, compact-net, or uniform terminal consumer factor through the displayed packet $D,B,M,E,S,Q,R,K,F,T,U,H,C,P,N$.

The four source faces are then forced by the exit package. \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-four-face-radius-exit-coverage} covers the accepted root radius read by the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ faces, and \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-phase-real-four-face-source-exactness} identifies those faces together with $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ as the exact source packet for consumer-visible reads.

Finally compare transported ledgers. \autoref{thm:finite-lebesgue-number-phase-real-terminal-radius-stability} says terminal verdict stability is exactly componentwise stability of these displayed coordinates. Hence every accepted terminal readiness ledger has the stated componentwise projection, and the consumer rows remain downstream coordinates of the same packet rather than private positive-radius sources.
\end{proof}
