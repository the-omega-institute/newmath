\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\MetricCompletionDenseEmbeddingUp$}

Following Kelley and Bourbaki on metric completion, the $\MetricCompletionDenseEmbeddingUp$ packet records the standard dense embedding of a metric carrier into its completion as finite BEDC data. It reads the metric source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the completion row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metriccompletion-namecert}, the separated comparison surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separatedmetric-namecert}, the uniform completion handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformcompletion-namecert}, and the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}. The packet gives completion-uniqueness and universal-property consumers a visible dense-image route without quotient representatives, selected limits, countable choice, host equality of completion classes, or an ambient completion object.

\begin{definition}[MetricCompletionDenseEmbedding carrier]
\label{def:metric-completion-dense-embedding-carrier}
A $\MetricCompletionDenseEmbeddingUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
D=(X,C,E,S,U,R,H,K,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the $\mathsf{Metric}^{\uparrow}$ source row, carrying the source carrier, distance classifier, and finite metric reads inherited from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}.
\item $C$ is the $\mathsf{MetricCompletion}^{\uparrow}$ completion row, carrying the completion-facing Cauchy surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metriccompletion-namecert}.
\item $E$ is the dense-image row, recording the displayed embedding route from a source point row in $X$ to its completion name in $C$ and the finite witness rows saying that every inspected completion neighbourhood is reached by a source image.
\item $S$ is the $\mathsf{SeparatedMetric}^{\uparrow}$ comparison row from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separatedmetric-namecert}, used only after the source and completion rows have been displayed.
\item $U$ is the $\mathsf{UniformCompletion}^{\uparrow}$ handoff row from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformcompletion-namecert}, recording the uniform completion consumer route that reads the same dense image surface.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, exposing the finite regular-rational observations attached to completion-neighbourhood and distance-budget reads.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $X,C,E,S,U,R,K,P,N$.
\item $K$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from source metric reads through completion names, dense-image witnesses, separated comparisons, uniform completion handoff, and regular rational readback.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\MetricCompletionDenseEmbeddingUp$, the five sibling chapters named above, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MetricCompletionDenseEmbeddingUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through these rows and their replay paths. It has no coordinate for quotient representatives, selected Cauchy limits, countable choice, host equality of completion classes, or a detached completion uniqueness theorem.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[MetricCompletionDenseEmbedding NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:metric-completion-dense-embedding-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\MetricCompletionDenseEmbeddingUp$ carrier $D=(X,C,E,S,U,R,H,K,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\MetricCompletionDenseEmbeddingUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the source metric row, completion row, dense-image row, separated comparison row, uniform completion handoff, regular rational readback, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming. Every completion-facing read must consume $X,C,E$ before it can read $S,U,R,H,K,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:metric-completion-dense-embedding-carrier}. The rows $X,C,E$ expose the source metric, completion carrier, and dense image surface as displayed finite packet data.

The comparison and handoff rows are downstream. The separated comparison $S$ reads the same source and completion rows, the uniform completion handoff $U$ follows the same dense-image route, and the regular rational readback $R$ exposes only the finite observation data attached to those reads.

Finally $H,K,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same packet. Since no carrier coordinate stores quotient representatives, selected limits, countable choice, or host completion equality, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetricCompletionDenseEmbedding handoff]
\label{thm:metric-completion-dense-embedding-handoff}

$$
\mathsf{Metric}^{\uparrow},\ \mathsf{MetricCompletion}^{\uparrow},\ \mathsf{DenseImage}^{\uparrow},\ \mathsf{SeparatedMetric}^{\uparrow},\ \mathsf{UniformCompletion}^{\uparrow},\ \RegSeqRatUp .
$$
The exported handoff is the displayed dense embedding of the metric source into its completion; it is not a quotient-representative selector, an ambient completion theorem, a host equality comparison, or a choice principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a completion-facing consumer. By \autoref{thm:metric-completion-dense-embedding-namecert-obligations}, the consumer first reads the source metric row $X$, the completion row $C$, and the dense-image row $E$.

The sibling chapters then determine the downstream route. The separated metric comparison is read through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separatedmetric-namecert}, the uniform handoff through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformcompletion-namecert}, and the regular rational windows through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}.

The structural rows $H,K,P,N$ preserve that same finite route under transport, replay, provenance, and naming. Because the carrier has no row for representatives, selected limits, countable choice, host equality, or an ambient completion theorem, every accepted handoff remains the displayed dense embedding surface.
\end{proof}
