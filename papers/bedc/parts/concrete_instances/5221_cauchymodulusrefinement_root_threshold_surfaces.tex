\begin{theorem}[Cauchy modulus refinement root carrier threshold admission]
\label{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-root-carrier-threshold-admission}
For every accepted $\CauchyModulusRefinementUp$ packet
$$
R=(M_0,M_1,U,V,T,W,Q,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the root carrier admits the rows $M_0,M_1,U,V,T,W,Q,E,H,C,P,N$ only after the shared meet row $U$ and the selector-budget row $T$ are displayed on the same route. In particular, the Real-completion root threshold surface may read $V,W,Q,E,H,C,P,N$ only as descendants of the source schedules $M_0,M_1$, the shared meet $U$, and the selector budget $T$ already shown in \autoref{def:cauchy-modulus-refinement-carrier}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-modulus-refinement-carrier}. Its finite packet lists the two source schedules, the shared meet, the extraction witness, the selector budget, the selected-window ledger, the tolerance-readback ledger, the Real seal, and the structural rows in one $\BHist$ tuple.

Apply the local NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-namecert-obligations}. Carrier admission is attached to the same tuple, while the source exactness and shared-window clauses require $V$ to be read after $M_0,M_1,U$ and require $W,Q$ to be read through $T$.

Use source-meet exactness, selector-budget totality, and window-tolerance exactness, \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-source-meet-exactness}, \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-selector-budget-totality}, and \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-window-tolerance-exactness}. These identify the root threshold route as the displayed chain through $U$ and $T$ before any Real-facing seal read can consume $E$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy modulus refinement root classifier transport exactness]
\label{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-root-classifier-transport-exactness}
For every accepted $\CauchyModulusRefinementUp$ packet $R=(M_0,M_1,U,V,T,W,Q,E,H,C,P,N)$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport preserves the same source-meet-selector-window-readback route through $H$ and $C$. A transported classifier read of $R$ is therefore still a read of $M_0,M_1,U,V,T,W,Q,E$ in the displayed order, authenticated by the same package row $P$ and the same local $\NameCert$ row $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with classifier transport from \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-classifier-transport}. The row $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the carrier coordinates, and $C$ stores the displayed continuation route between them.

Next bind the source portion. Source-meet exactness, \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-source-meet-exactness}, keeps the witness $V$ on the route from $M_0,M_1$ through $U$, so transport cannot replace the shared meet by a second source row.

Finally bind the selected-window and Real-facing portion. Selector-budget totality, window-tolerance exactness, and Real seal factorization, \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-selector-budget-totality}, \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-window-tolerance-exactness}, and \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-real-seal-factorization}, keep $T,W,Q,E$ on the same readback chain. The package and local name rows then certify that the transported classifier is still the displayed root route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy modulus refinement root ledger non-escape totality]
\label{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-root-ledger-nonescape-totality}
Every $\RealUp$ or Completion consumer of an accepted $\CauchyModulusRefinementUp$ root ledger receives only the displayed rows
$$
M_0,M_1,U,V,T,W,Q,E,H,C,P,N.
$$
It receives no quotient stream, selected limit, host real equality, hidden selector, second meet row, or ambient completeness datum outside those carrier rows, and the completion-facing ledger is total exactly because every accepted consumer read is already accounted for by that displayed route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the carrier and the Real seal factorization theorem, \autoref{def:cauchy-modulus-refinement-carrier} and \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-real-seal-factorization}. The seal row $E$ is reached only after the shared meet, extraction witness, selector budget, windows, tolerance rows, and regular readbacks have been displayed.

Apply the Real-consumer coverage theorem, \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-real-consumer-coverage}. It states that every Real or Completion consumer reads only the selector-budget, window, readback, and seal route together with the structural rows.

Finally apply non-escape, \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-nonescape}. Since quotient streams, selected limits, host real equality, hidden selectors, second meet rows, and ambient completeness coordinates are absent from the carrier, the displayed ledger exhausts the root consumer surface.
\end{proof}
