The $\EmptyFableMachineUp$ interface is the finite NameCert packet for the Empty Fable Machine of \autoref{ch:visions-empty-fable-machine}. It sits above the boundary packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-fable-machine-boundary-namecert}, the local clock packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-fablemachineclock-namecert}, and the observer-history identity packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observerhistidentity-namecert}. Its carrier keeps the empty-boundary source, selector-step witness, trace row, fable ledger, transport, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming row in one displayed tuple.

\begin{definition}[EmptyFableMachine NameCert carrier]
\label{def:empty-fable-machine-carrier}
An $\EmptyFableMachineUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
E=(Z,S,T,L,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $Z$ is the empty-boundary source row, read through the boundary discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-fable-machine-boundary-namecert};
\item $S$ is the selector-step witness row, including the enacted mark, its displayed $\Ext$ edge, and the local branch certificate;
\item $T$ is the finite trace row read from the constructor spine generated by the displayed selector step;
\item $L$ is the fable-ledger row identifying the same trace as the Empty-fable story ledger;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $Z,S,T,L$ and for the local naming row;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ replay routes by which a consumer reads the source, selector witness, trace, ledger, transport, and local name;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\EmptyFableMachineUp$, $\FableMachineBoundaryUp$, $\FableMachineClockUp$, $\ObserverHistIdentityUp$, $\BHist$, $\Ext$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\EmptyFableMachineUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only by these rows. It has no coordinate for an undisplayed selector function, observer-substance, host trace quotient, global clock, or truth oracle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[EmptyFableMachine NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:empty-fable-machine-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\EmptyFableMachineUp}$ surface for the carrier of \autoref{def:empty-fable-machine-carrier} consists exactly of carrier admission for $E=(Z,S,T,L,H,C,P,N)$, empty-boundary admission for $Z$, selector-step admission for $S$, trace admission for $T$, fable-ledger admission for $L$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, package provenance through $P$, and the local naming row $N$.

The boundary row is supplied by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-fable-machine-boundary-namecert}; the step-order reading is supplied by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-fablemachineclock-namecert}; and the observer-facing history read is constrained by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observerhistidentity-namecert}. The chapter therefore binds the source, selected branch, trace, ledger, transport, replay, provenance, and naming obligations without adding a hidden chooser.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier rows. The empty-boundary source $Z$, selector witness $S$, trace row $T$, fable ledger $L$, transport row $H$, continuation row $C$, provenance row $P$, and local name $N$ are separate coordinates of the finite packet.

Read $Z$ through the boundary packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-fable-machine-boundary-namecert}. Read the local order and enacted selected mark through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-fablemachineclock-namecert}. Read the observer-facing history discipline through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observerhistidentity-namecert}.

The trace row and fable-ledger row then refer to the same displayed constructor spine named in \autoref{ch:visions-empty-fable-machine}. The transport, continuation, package, and local naming rows repack that spine for consumers, so no further selector, subject, quotient, clock, or oracle coordinate is introduced.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[EmptyFableMachine selector-ledger non-escape]
\label{thm:empty-fable-machine-nonescape}
Every public selector-ledger read of an accepted $\EmptyFableMachineUp$ packet factors through
$$
Z,S,T,L,H,C,P,N.
$$
In particular, a branch mark can be exposed only with its displayed selector-step witness $S$, its trace row $T$, and its fable-ledger row $L$. The packet exports no branch mark detached from selector evidence, no hidden chooser, no observer-substance coordinate, no global clock, and no host trace equality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the accepted carrier. The only branch-facing row is $S$, and that row contains the selected mark together with the displayed $\Ext$ edge and local branch certificate.

Trace and ledger reads are confined to $T$ and $L$. The hsame transport row $H$ and continuation row $C$ move only those displayed rows, while the provenance row $P$ names the boundary, clock, and observer-history identity surfaces that license the read.

A public branch mark without $S$ would require an additional selector coordinate. No such coordinate appears in $E=(Z,S,T,L,H,C,P,N)$, so every selector-ledger read remains inside the finite NameCert packet.
\end{proof}
