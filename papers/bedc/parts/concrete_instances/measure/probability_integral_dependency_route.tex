\begin{theorem}[Measure probability and integral dependency route]
\label{thm:measure-probability-integral-dependency-route}
Every accepted $\MeasureUp$ packet sends its finite event rows and $\RealUp$-valued mass rows to \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-probspace-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-integral-namecert} only through displayed $\MeasureUp$ rows. The $\ProbSpaceUp$ consumer reads normalization and finite additivity from the event-value surface, while the $\IntegralUp$ consumer reads premeasure and value rows from the same finite $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the dependency lattice of \autoref{thm:measure-dependency-lattice}. It ties probability and integral consumers to the same real-valued measure rows rather than to detached host functions.

For the probability side, read the $\ProbSpaceUp$ consumer rows through the displayed finite event boundary. Normalization and finite additivity are therefore projections of the $\MeasureUp$ packet.

For the integral side, read the $\IntegralUp$ premeasure route through the displayed value rows. Since both consumers share the same structural transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows, neither receives a mass row outside the shown $\MeasureUp$ carrier surface.
\end{proof}
