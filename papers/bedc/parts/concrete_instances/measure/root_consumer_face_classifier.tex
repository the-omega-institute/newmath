\begin{definition}[Measure root consumer face classifier]
\label{def:measure-root-consumer-face-classifier}
The consumer face classifier of the $\MeasureUp$ root threshold is the
three-face partition of the consumer rows in
\autoref{def:measure-root-bhist-consumer-threshold}. The integral face is the
$\IntegralUp$ projection of \autoref{thm:measure-integral-consumer-rows}: it
contains covered measurable-event rows, $\RealUp$ endpoint classifier rows,
countable-sum endpoint rows, head-tail readback rows, and finite
disjoint-union readback rows. The probability face is the $\ProbSpaceUp$
projection of \autoref{thm:measure-probability-consumer-rows}: it contains
the total-event normalization row, empty-zero row, and finite
disjoint-union row. The functional-analysis face is the
$\FunctionalAnalysisUp$ projection of
\autoref{thm:measure-functionalanalysis-consumer-rows}: it contains
nonnegative endpoint rows, monotone-inclusion rows, relative-difference rows,
and countable-sum classifier rows.

Rows common to more than one face are classified as shared root rows only
when they are the displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ event-ledger rows or the
$\RealUp$ endpoint classifier rows already present at the root
$\MeasureUp$ surface. No face classifier row is a host set equality,
quotient event equality, analytic completion object, or $\StdBridge$ row.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Measure root consumer face coverage]
\label{thm:measure-root-consumer-face-coverage}
Every consumer row projected by
\autoref{def:measure-root-consumer-package} belongs to one of the three faces
of \autoref{def:measure-root-consumer-face-classifier}. Thus each outgoing
consumer row is classified as integral, probability, functional-analysis, or
as a shared root $\mathsf{BHist}$ event-ledger or $\RealUp$ endpoint row
seen through those faces.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from \autoref{def:measure-root-consumer-package}. Its consumer rows are
the public root surface together with the downstream package of
\autoref{thm:measure-root-downstream-unblock-package}.

Apply \autoref{thm:measure-integral-consumer-rows},
\autoref{thm:measure-probability-consumer-rows}, and
\autoref{thm:measure-functionalanalysis-consumer-rows}. These three theorems
list the complete rows consumed by $\IntegralUp$, $\ProbSpaceUp$, and
$\FunctionalAnalysisUp$ from that same root package.

Finally use \autoref{def:measure-root-consumer-face-classifier}. The
classifier names exactly those three outgoing projections and assigns their
common event-ledger and endpoint rows to the shared root part, so every row
projected by the consumer package has one of the displayed face labels.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Measure root consumer face disjointness]
\label{thm:measure-root-consumer-face-disjointness}
The integral, probability, and functional-analysis faces of
\autoref{def:measure-root-consumer-face-classifier} overlap only on the
shared root $\mathsf{BHist}$ event ledger and the shared $\RealUp$ endpoint
classifier. The total-event probability-normalization row remains specific
to the probability face, the countable-sum endpoint and head-tail readback
rows remain specific to the integral face, and the relative-difference and
monotone-inclusion rows remain specific to the functional-analysis face.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First read the three outgoing projections through the consumer-row theorems.
\autoref{thm:measure-integral-consumer-rows} gives the integral rows,
\autoref{thm:measure-probability-consumer-rows} gives the probability rows,
and \autoref{thm:measure-functionalanalysis-consumer-rows} gives the
functional-analysis rows.

The only rows those projections are allowed to share are the displayed
covered event rows and $\RealUp$ endpoint classifier rows already present in
the root $\MeasureUp$ package. This is exactly the shared-root clause in
\autoref{def:measure-root-consumer-face-classifier}.

Now apply
\autoref{thm:measure-root-consumer-projection-separation}. The separation
theorem makes the three consumer projections one-way outputs from the root
surface and prevents any consumer-specific row from becoming a root
hypothesis.

The exclusions in the three consumer-row theorems then keep the remaining
rows face-specific: probability normalization is absent from the integral and
functional-analysis surfaces, countable-sum endpoint and head-tail readback
rows are absent from the probability surface, and relative-difference plus
monotone-inclusion rows are absent from the probability consumer surface and
do not become integral premeasure rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Measure root cross-consumer threshold package]
\label{thm:measure-root-cross-consumer-threshold-package}
The face classifier of
\autoref{def:measure-root-consumer-face-classifier}, the coverage result of
\autoref{thm:measure-root-consumer-face-coverage}, the disjointness result of
\autoref{thm:measure-root-consumer-face-disjointness}, and the deps-ready
package of \autoref{thm:measure-root-deps-ready-public-package} form the root
consumer threshold for $\IntegralUp$, $\ProbSpaceUp$, and
$\FunctionalAnalysisUp$. This threshold exposes the shared $\mathsf{BHist}$
event ledger and shared $\RealUp$ endpoint classifier once, exposes each
consumer-specific row only on its own face, and keeps all outgoing faces from
serving as inputs to the root $\MeasureUp$ obligations.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use \autoref{thm:measure-root-deps-ready-public-package} to fix the public
root package. It supplies the orientation surface, the $\mathsf{BHist}$
threshold, and the downstream projections needed by the three consumers.

Next apply \autoref{thm:measure-root-consumer-face-coverage}. This assigns
every outgoing row of the consumer package to the integral, probability, or
functional-analysis face, with shared event-ledger and endpoint rows read at
the root.

Then apply \autoref{thm:measure-root-consumer-face-disjointness}. The
face-specific probability-normalization, countable-sum, head-tail,
relative-difference, and monotone-inclusion rows remain on their named
consumer faces.

Finally apply
\autoref{thm:measure-root-consumer-projection-separation}. Since the
consumer rows are outgoing projections and not hypotheses for the root
carrier, classifier, coverage, additivity, continuity, or endpoint-family
obligations, the packaged classifier is a root consumer threshold rather than
a justification loop for $\MeasureUp$.
\end{proof}
