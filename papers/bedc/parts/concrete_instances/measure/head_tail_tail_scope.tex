\begin{definition}[Measure head-tail tail scope]
\label{def:measure-head-tail-tail-scope}
Let $\mathcal{M}$ be a $\MeasureUp$ certificate surface as in
\autoref{def:measure-certificate-surface}, and let $E_\bullet$ be a measurable
sequence in that surface. The head-tail tail scope of $E_\bullet$ is the
four-row package consisting of:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item the shifted measurable sequence $E_{\bullet+1}$, read as the family
  $n\mapsto E_{n+1}$ by the same measurable-family row;
  \item pointwise $\sim_M$ transport for the shifted event presentations, with
  endpoint movement read through the $\RealUp$ classifier $\sim_R$;
  \item the countable endpoint $\Sigma_\mu(E_{\bullet+1})$ attached to the
  shifted measurable sequence;
  \item admissibility of $E_{\bullet+1}$ as an input to the same head-tail
  readback row and countable-sum classifier row that classify
  $\Sigma_\mu(E_\bullet)$.
\end{enumerate}
This scope records only certificate-surface rows. It does not identify shifted
host sequences by ambient equality, and it does not introduce a separate
measure construction outside the displayed $\MeasureUp$ rows.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Measure head-tail tail measurable-sequence scope]
\label{thm:measure-head-tail-tail-measurable-sequence-scope}
Let $\mathcal{M}$ be a $\MeasureUp$ certificate surface as in
\autoref{def:measure-certificate-surface}, and let $E_\bullet$ be a measurable
sequence in that surface. Then the shifted family $E_{\bullet+1}$ is an
admitted measurable sequence for the same certificate surface. Consequently
every head-tail readback later applied to $\Sigma_\mu(E_{\bullet+1})$ is an
application of the displayed measurable-family row of $\mathcal{M}$, not an
implicit host-level shift.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the measurable-family row from
\autoref{def:measure-certificate-surface}. Since $E_\bullet$ is supplied as a
measurable sequence, each displayed event $E_n$ carries the certificate
membership row required by $\mathsf{MeasFam}$.

Form the shifted family by reindexing the same displayed rows: at index $n$ the
new family reads the old row at $n+1$. This uses no new event carrier and no
extra endpoint; it repacks the existing measurable witnesses into the
sequence-indexed slot demanded by the surface.

The carrier inventory of
\autoref{def:measureup-carrier-obligation-inventory} lists measurable sequence
rows and countable-sum rows as ledger entries. Therefore the shifted family is
admitted exactly as another measurable sequence over the same certificate
surface, and the head-tail row may be projected for it.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Measure head-tail tail countable-sum scope]
\label{thm:measure-head-tail-tail-countable-sum-scope}
Let $\mathcal{M}$ be a $\MeasureUp$ certificate surface as in
\autoref{def:measure-certificate-surface}, and let $E_\bullet$ be a measurable
sequence with head-tail tail scope as in
\autoref{def:measure-head-tail-tail-scope}. Then
$\Sigma_\mu(E_{\bullet+1})$ is the countable-sum endpoint consumed by the
head-tail comparison for $E_\bullet$:
$$
\Sigma_\mu(E_\bullet)\sim_R\mu(E_0)+\Sigma_\mu(E_{\bullet+1}).
$$
If $F_\bullet$ is another admitted tail sequence and every index satisfies
$\mu(E_{n+1})\sim_R\mu(F_n)$, then the countable endpoint transported into the
head-tail comparison satisfies
$$
\Sigma_\mu(E_{\bullet+1})\sim_R\Sigma_\mu(F_\bullet).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use \autoref{thm:measure-head-tail-tail-measurable-sequence-scope} to read
$E_{\bullet+1}$ as a measurable sequence in the same surface. The countable-sum
field of \autoref{def:measure-certificate-surface} therefore supplies the
endpoint $\Sigma_\mu(E_{\bullet+1})$ as a certificate row.

Project the head-tail readback row of the same surface and apply it to
$E_\bullet$. The displayed comparison is exactly the row named in
\autoref{def:measure-head-tail-tail-scope}: its tail term is the endpoint
attached to the admitted shifted family.

For classifier transport, apply
\autoref{thm:measure-obligation-countable-sum-classifier-stability} to the two
admitted measurable sequences $E_{\bullet+1}$ and $F_\bullet$. The pointwise
comparison $\mu(E_{n+1})\sim_R\mu(F_n)$ is precisely the hypothesis required by
the countable-sum classifier row, so the transported endpoint is accepted by the
same head-tail comparison.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Measure head-tail consumer exhaustion]
\label{thm:measure-head-tail-consumer-exhaustion}
Every later finite-support, continuity-from-below, integral, probability, or
functional-analysis use of a shifted countable tail in the $\MeasureUp$ carrier
consumes exactly the following four certificate rows:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item shifted measurable-sequence scope for $E_{\bullet+1}$;
  \item the shifted endpoint $\Sigma_\mu(E_{\bullet+1})$;
  \item head-tail readback classifying $\Sigma_\mu(E_\bullet)$ with
  $\mu(E_0)+\Sigma_\mu(E_{\bullet+1})$;
  \item countable-sum classifier stability transporting pointwise tail
  equality through $\sim_R$.
\end{enumerate}
In particular, the finite-support readbacks that repeatedly apply head-tail to
shifted sequences, including
\autoref{thm:measure-finite-three-support-countable-readback} and
\autoref{thm:measure-four-support-countable-readback}, are audited by these
four rows together with the sigma-additive input of
\autoref{thm:measure-obligation-sigma-additive-row}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The first two rows are exactly the tail package of
\autoref{def:measure-head-tail-tail-scope}, with admissibility supplied by
\autoref{thm:measure-head-tail-tail-measurable-sequence-scope}. They account for
the measurable-sequence witness and the countable endpoint before any additive
readback is used.

The third row is the head-tail projection of
\autoref{def:measure-certificate-surface}. It is the row consumed in the
finite-support readbacks when the original sequence, once-shifted sequence, and
twice-shifted sequence are exposed successively.

The fourth row is the countable-sum classifier stability obligation
\autoref{thm:measure-obligation-countable-sum-classifier-stability}. It is the
only transport principle used when a shifted tail is compared with the canonical
empty tail or with a downstream tail carrying pointwise equal $\RealUp$
endpoints.

The remaining downstream consumers named in the statement receive the same
surface through the carrier inventory of
\autoref{def:measureup-carrier-obligation-inventory}. Since the inventory lists
measurable sequence rows, countable-sum rows, head-tail readback, and classifier
stability as the available rows, there is no fifth certificate input for a
shifted countable tail.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[Measure finite-prefix head-tail readback spine]
\label{def:measure-finite-prefix-head-tail-readback-spine}
Let $\mathcal{M}$ be a $\MeasureUp$ certificate surface as in
\autoref{def:measure-certificate-surface}, and let $E_\bullet$ be a measurable
sequence in that surface. For a unary history $n$ with $\Unary(n)$, define the
iterated shifted family $E_{\bullet+n}$ and the finite-prefix head-tail endpoint
$\mathsf{HT}_n(E_\bullet)$ by unary recursion:
$$
\begin{aligned}
E_{\bullet+\emp} &:= E_\bullet, \\
E_{\bullet+\Eone(n)} &:= (E_{\bullet+n})_{\bullet+1},
\end{aligned}
$$
and
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathsf{HT}_{\emp}(E_\bullet) &:= \Sigma_\mu(E_\bullet), \\
\mathsf{HT}_{\Eone(n)}(E_\bullet) &:= \mu(E_0)+_R\mathsf{HT}_n(E_{\bullet+1}).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus $\mathsf{HT}_n(E_\bullet)$ is the right-nested endpoint obtained by
reading the first $n$ heads and then stopping at the countable endpoint of the
$n$-shifted tail. The spine records only the measurable-family row, the
countable-sum endpoint row, the head-tail row, and the additive $\RealUp$ row;
it does not identify shifted host sequences by ambient equality.
\end{definition}

\begin{lemma}[Measure iterated shifted-tail admissibility]
\label{lem:measure-iterated-shifted-tail-admissibility}
Let $\mathcal{M}$ be a $\MeasureUp$ certificate surface as in
\autoref{def:measure-certificate-surface}, let $E_\bullet$ be a measurable
sequence in that surface, and let $n$ be a unary history with $\Unary(n)$. Then
$E_{\bullet+n}$, as defined in
\autoref{def:measure-finite-prefix-head-tail-readback-spine}, is an admitted
measurable sequence for the same certificate surface.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Induct on the unary generation of $n$. In the empty case,
$E_{\bullet+\emp}$ is $E_\bullet$, which is measurable by hypothesis.

For the one-step case, assume $E_{\bullet+n}$ is an admitted measurable
sequence. Apply
\autoref{thm:measure-head-tail-tail-measurable-sequence-scope} to the admitted
sequence $E_{\bullet+n}$. Its shifted family
$(E_{\bullet+n})_{\bullet+1}$ is admitted as a measurable sequence in the same
certificate surface. By the defining recursion, this shifted family is
$E_{\bullet+\Eone(n)}$, so the successor case follows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Measure finite-prefix head-tail readback]
\label{thm:measure-finite-prefix-head-tail-readback}
Let $\mathcal{M}$ be a $\MeasureUp$ certificate surface as in
\autoref{def:measure-certificate-surface}, let $E_\bullet$ be a measurable
sequence in that surface, and let $n$ be a unary history with $\Unary(n)$. Then
the countable endpoint of $E_\bullet$ is classified by the finite-prefix
head-tail endpoint:
$$
\Sigma_\mu(E_\bullet)\sim_R\mathsf{HT}_n(E_\bullet).
$$
Equivalently, expanding the recursion of
\autoref{def:measure-finite-prefix-head-tail-readback-spine}, this is the
right-nested finite-prefix readback
$$
\Sigma_\mu(E_\bullet)\sim_R
\mu(E_0)+_R\mu(E_1)+_R\cdots+_R\mu(E_{n-1})+_R\Sigma_\mu(E_{\bullet+n}),
$$
with the final tail endpoint omitted only in the empty-prefix case by the first
recursion clause.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Prove the stronger assertion uniformly for every admitted measurable input
sequence $G_\bullet$: for every unary $n$,
$\Sigma_\mu(G_\bullet)\sim_R\mathsf{HT}_n(G_\bullet)$. The claimed statement is
the specialization $G_\bullet=E_\bullet$.

In the empty case, the definition gives
$\mathsf{HT}_{\emp}(G_\bullet)=\Sigma_\mu(G_\bullet)$. Reflexivity of the
$\RealUp$ classifier from \autoref{def:real-classifier-specification} gives
$$
\Sigma_\mu(G_\bullet)\sim_R\mathsf{HT}_{\emp}(G_\bullet).
$$

For the one-step case, let $n=\Eone(m)$ and assume the uniform induction
hypothesis at $m$. Equip $G_\bullet$ with the head-tail tail scope of
\autoref{def:measure-head-tail-tail-scope}. By
\autoref{thm:measure-head-tail-tail-countable-sum-scope}, the single-step
readback gives
$$
\Sigma_\mu(G_\bullet)\sim_R
\mu(G_0)+_R\Sigma_\mu(G_{\bullet+1}).
$$
By \autoref{thm:measure-head-tail-tail-measurable-sequence-scope}, the shifted
family $G_{\bullet+1}$ is again an admitted measurable sequence. Apply the
induction hypothesis to this shifted sequence:
$$
\Sigma_\mu(G_{\bullet+1})\sim_R\mathsf{HT}_m(G_{\bullet+1}).
$$
The additive congruence row of the target $\RealUp$ additive $\AbGroupUp$
certificate, cited in \autoref{def:measure-certificate-surface} and
\autoref{def:abgroup-carrier}, transports this tail comparison through the
fixed left summand $\mu(G_0)$:
$$
\mu(G_0)+_R\Sigma_\mu(G_{\bullet+1})\sim_R
\mu(G_0)+_R\mathsf{HT}_m(G_{\bullet+1}).
$$
The right-hand endpoint is exactly
$\mathsf{HT}_{\Eone(m)}(G_\bullet)$ by the successor clause of
\autoref{def:measure-finite-prefix-head-tail-readback-spine}. Transitivity of
$\sim_R$ composes the single-step head-tail comparison with this transported
induction comparison, yielding
$$
\Sigma_\mu(G_\bullet)\sim_R\mathsf{HT}_{\Eone(m)}(G_\bullet).
$$
This closes the unary induction and therefore the finite-prefix readback for
$E_\bullet$.
\end{proof}
