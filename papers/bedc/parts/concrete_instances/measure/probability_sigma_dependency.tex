\begin{theorem}[Measure probability sigma dependency]
\label{thm:measure-probability-sigma-dependency}
Every accepted $\MeasureUp$ packet exposes its event algebra, sigma-additive rows, finite disjoint-union readback, and $\RealUp$-valued mass rows before any integration, random-variable, distribution, or probability consumer receives a measure row. In particular, the $\ProbSpaceUp$ consumer of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-probspace-namecert} reads its total-event normalization and probability-total-mass boundary through the same displayed $\MeasureUp$ event family and value route used by the integral and random-variable surfaces, not through a detached host measure or an independent probability primitive.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the root event boundary and sigma-additive obligation rows of \autoref{thm:measure-root-event-boundary-carrier} and \autoref{thm:measure-root-threshold-sigma-additive-obligation}. They expose the measurable-event family, event classifier, empty-event row, finite disjoint-union readback, and countable additivity surface as carried $\MeasureUp$ data.

Apply the probability substrate obligations of \autoref{thm:measure-probability-substrate-obligations}. The $\ProbSpaceUp$ row adds the total event and the unit endpoint only after those measure rows have already supplied the event algebra and mass values.

Thus every downstream probability, integration, random-variable, or distribution read sees the same finite event-and-value route. The Measure packet exports a shared dependency boundary, not separate host-side measure and probability objects.
\end{proof}
