\section{Continuity from below row}
\label{sec:measure-continuity-from-below}

\begin{theorem}[Measure monotone partial-union chain]
\label{thm:measure-monotone-partial-union-chain}
Let $M$ be a $\MeasureUp$ carrier as in \autoref{sec:measure-carrier}, with
designated measurable family $\mathcal{A}$, measure map $\mu$, and event
classifier $\sim_M$. Let $E_\bullet$ be a displayed increasing measurable
sequence in one carried $\mathsf{BHist}$ fiber: every $E_n$ is in
$\mathcal{A}$, and the public event readback displays $E_n\subseteq E_{n+1}$.
Then the $\MeasureUp$ sigma-algebra and relative-difference rows supply
measurable finite partial unions $P_n$ and pairwise disjoint measurable layers
$D_n$ such that
$$
D_0=E_0,\qquad D_{n+1}=E_{n+1}\setminus E_n,
\qquad P_n\sim_M E_n.
$$
Moreover $P_n$ is classified as the displayed disjoint union
$D_0\sqcup\cdots\sqcup D_n$, and the public monotone row classifies
$\mu(E_n)\le_R\mu(E_{n+1})$ for every $n$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with $D_0:=E_0$ and $P_0:=E_0$. The displayed measurability of $E_0$
gives both rows, and the classifier comparison $P_0\sim_M E_0$ is reflexivity
of the $\MeasureUp$ event classifier.

For the successor step, apply
\autoref{thm:measure-relative-difference-disjoint-decomposition} to the
displayed inclusion $E_n\subseteq E_{n+1}$. It supplies the measurable layer
$D_{n+1}:=E_{n+1}\setminus E_n$, its disjointness from $E_n$, and the
binary-union readback
$$
E_n\sqcup D_{n+1}\sim_M E_{n+1}.
$$

Use the induction readback $P_n\sim_M E_n$ to transport this binary-union row
from $E_n$ to $P_n$. The sigma-algebra binary-union row then supplies
$P_{n+1}:=P_n\sqcup D_{n+1}$ as a measurable event, classified with
$E_{n+1}$ by the composed event classifier comparison.

The disjointness of the accumulated layers follows by induction from the
displayed chain: earlier layers lie inside $E_n$ by the previous partial-union
readback, while the new layer is disjoint from $E_n$. Finally apply
\autoref{thm:measure-monotone-under-measurable-inclusion} to each displayed
inclusion $E_n\subseteq E_{n+1}$ to obtain
$\mu(E_n)\le_R\mu(E_{n+1})$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Measure continuity from below package]
\label{thm:measure-continuity-from-below-package}
Let $M$ be a $\MeasureUp$ carrier as in \autoref{sec:measure-carrier}. For a
displayed increasing measurable sequence $E_\bullet$, let
$D_0:=E_0$ and $D_{n+1}:=E_{n+1}\setminus E_n$ be the measurable disjoint
layers supplied by
\autoref{thm:measure-monotone-partial-union-chain}. If
$U:=\bigcup_{n:\mathbb{N}}E_n$ is the countable-union row of the same
measurable sequence, then the $\sigma$-additivity row classifies
$$
\mu(U)\sim_R\Sigma_\mu(D_\bullet).
$$
For every finite prefix, the finite disjoint-union readback classifies
$$
\Sigma_\mu(D_0,\ldots,D_n)\sim_R\mu(E_n),
$$
where the left endpoint denotes the visible finite-support countable sum
associated to the first $n+1$ layers and an empty tail.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:measure-monotone-partial-union-chain} to obtain the layer
sequence $D_\bullet$, the partial unions $P_n$, and the classifier readbacks
$P_n\sim_M E_n$. The same theorem gives pairwise disjointness of the layers and
measurability of every layer.

The countable union of the layers is classified with the countable union
$U:=\bigcup_nE_n$: every layer lies in the corresponding $E_n$, and every
element visible in some $E_n$ lies in the finite partial union
$D_0\sqcup\cdots\sqcup D_n$ by the partial-union readback. This is an event
classifier comparison, not a host-level equality of subsets.

Apply the $\sigma$-additivity row of
\autoref{thm:measureup-sigma-additive-certificate-obligations} to the
pairwise-disjoint measurable sequence $D_\bullet$. It gives the comparison
between the measure of the displayed countable union of the layers and
$\Sigma_\mu(D_\bullet)$, and event-classifier transport carries the left
endpoint to $\mu(U)$.

For a fixed $n$, apply finite disjoint-union additivity along the visible
binary-union tree for $P_n=D_0\sqcup\cdots\sqcup D_n$. The finite-support
countable readback rows of
\autoref{thm:measure-finite-support-countable-readback} and
\autoref{thm:measure-finite-three-support-countable-readback}, iterated over
the displayed finite tree, identify that finite-support countable endpoint
with the measure of $P_n$. Transport along $P_n\sim_M E_n$ gives the displayed
classification with $\mu(E_n)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Measure continuity from below integral scope]
\label{thm:measure-continuity-from-below-integral-scope}
The $\IntegralUp$ premeasure interface may consume the continuity-from-below
package of \autoref{thm:measure-continuity-from-below-package} exactly through
the public $\MeasureUp$ rows: displayed measurable events, relative-difference
layers, finite disjoint-union readbacks, $\sigma$-additivity for the disjoint
layer sequence, countable-sum classifier stability, and $\RealUp$ endpoint
transport. It receives no standard bridge row and no host set equality row from
this paper site.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the premeasure rows named in
\autoref{thm:measure-integral-premeasure-consumption}. These are the
measurable-event ledger, endpoint classifier, countable-sum endpoint,
head-tail readback, and finite disjoint-union rows exposed by the public
$\MeasureUp$ package.

The layer construction uses only the relative-difference row from
\autoref{thm:measure-relative-difference-disjoint-decomposition} and the
monotone inclusion row from
\autoref{thm:measure-monotone-under-measurable-inclusion}. The endpoint
classification uses only the $\sigma$-additivity and countable-sum rows named
by \autoref{thm:measureup-sigma-additive-certificate-obligations}.

Thus an $\IntegralUp$ consumer receives the named continuity package as a
premeasure obligation over visible $\mathsf{BHist}$ event rows. The only
identifications available to that consumer are $\MeasureUp$ event classifier
comparisons and $\RealUp$ endpoint classifier comparisons, so no standard
bridge or host equality is imported.
\end{proof}
