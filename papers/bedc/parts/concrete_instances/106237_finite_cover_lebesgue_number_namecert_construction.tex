\item $M$ is the $\MetricUp$ ball-observation row through which distances, rational balls, and containment checks are read.

\begin{theorem}[Finite-cover Lebesgue-number NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:finite-cover-lebesgue-number-namecert-obligations}

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier of \autoref{def:finite-cover-lebesgue-number-carrier}. The displayed tuple contains exactly the compact metric source, metric observation row, finite cover, positive radius ledger, lemma route, uniform handoff, and structural rows.

Cover-to-radius exactness is read through $V$ and $R$. The finite cover is not replaced by an arbitrary open family, and the radius is not selected by a host minimum; it is the displayed positive ledger attached to the cover row.

The uniform handoff $U$ can be reached only after this finite route has been transported by $H$ and replayed by $C$, with provenance and local naming stored in $P$ and $N$.
