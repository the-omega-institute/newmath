\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RegularCauchyRateExtractionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-rate-extraction-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges constructive analysis, the $\RegularCauchyRateExtractionUp$ packet records the finite route by which a regular Cauchy rational name exposes an explicit precision rate before a $\RealUp$ consumer reads the seal. It sits over the finite window surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the generic modulus row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchymodulus-namecert}, the Real-facing modulus boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-realcauchymodulus-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet makes the rate read part of the named certificate rather than a host-side inference from Cauchy convergence prose.

\begin{definition}[Regular Cauchy rate extraction carrier]
\label{def:regular-cauchy-rate-extraction-carrier}
A $\RegularCauchyRateExtractionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
X=(S,D,R,M,U,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window row opened by the source regular Cauchy name and by every downstream precision request.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, carrying requested radii, comparison cells, slack rows, and endpoint observations.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row obtained from the displayed windows and dyadic observations.
\item $M$ is the $\CauchyModulusUp$ threshold row. A requested precision is sent to a displayed finite stage through $M$ before any rate-bearing handoff is available.
\item $U$ is the $\RealCauchyModulusUp$ readback row binding the same $S,D,R,M$ data to the Real-facing modulus surface.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row, reachable only after $S,D,R,M,U$ have all been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the window, dyadic, regular readback, modulus, Real-facing modulus, seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the rate route
$$
S,D,R \longmapsto M \longmapsto U \longmapsto E .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RegularCauchyRateExtractionUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\CauchyModulusUp$, $\RealCauchyModulusUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyRateExtractionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $S,D,R,M,U,E,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for a selected limit, quotient equality of Cauchy streams, countable choice, trichotomy, host real equality, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy rate extraction NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-rate-extraction-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RegularCauchyRateExtractionUp$ carrier
$$
X=(S,D,R,M,U,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyRateExtractionUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the StreamName windows, DyadicRatCore tolerances, RegSeqRat readback, CauchyModulus threshold, RealCauchyModulus readback, Real seal, componentwise transport, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item window-readback exactness, requiring $R$ to read only windows displayed in $S$ and tolerance data displayed in $D$;
\item threshold extraction exactness, requiring every precision request to pass through the displayed $\CauchyModulusUp$ row $M$ before the Real-facing modulus row is available;
\item Real-modulus compatibility, requiring $U$ to bind the same $S,D,R,M$ route and to expose no independent convergence oracle;
\item Real-seal nonescape, requiring $E$ to be downstream of $S,D,R,M,U$ and to export no selected limit, quotient stream equality, countable choice, trichotomy, host real equality, or ambient completeness claim;
\item structural stability, requiring $H,C,P,N$ to transport, replay, source, and name only the displayed rate-extraction route.
\end{enumerate}
No $\RegularCauchyRateExtractionUp$ NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-rate-extraction-carrier}. The accepted packet displays exactly the StreamName, DyadicRatCore, RegSeqRat, CauchyModulus, RealCauchyModulus, Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

Window-readback exactness is read from $S,D,R$. The regular rational readback has access only to the displayed finite windows and dyadic tolerance cells, so it cannot inspect an unbounded stream representative or a quotient equality class.

Threshold extraction exactness is the role of $M$. A precision request reaches a finite stage only through the displayed $\CauchyModulusUp$ row, and no other carrier coordinate can supply a detached selector.

The Real-facing modulus row $U$ then binds the same source route. Since $U$ is downstream of $S,D,R,M$, it records the rate as certificate data rather than as an external convergence principle.

Finally, $E,H,C,P,N$ close the finite route. The seal and structural rows preserve only the displayed coordinates, so the excluded objects are absent from every accepted packet and the listed obligations exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy rate extraction modulus readback]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-rate-extraction-modulus-readback}
Let
$$
X=(S,D,R,M,U,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\RegularCauchyRateExtractionUp$ carrier. Every modulus or Real-facing consumer of $X$ reads a precision rate through the finite route
$$
S,D,R \longmapsto M \longmapsto U \longmapsto E \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
Consequently the consumer receives a displayed StreamName window row, DyadicRatCore tolerance ledger, RegSeqRat readback, CauchyModulus threshold, RealCauchyModulus handoff, Real seal, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ data, and receives no selected limit, quotient stream equality, countable choice, trichotomy, host real equality, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, or bridge credential.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a modulus or Real-facing read of an accepted packet. Projection exposes the ordered carrier rows $S,D,R,M,U,E,H,C,P,N$.

The readback starts at the finite observation surface. The row $S$ displays the windows, $D$ displays the tolerance ledger, and $R$ displays the regular rational values available to the request.

The precision rate is then read from $M$. Because $M$ is the only threshold row in the carrier, a consumer cannot replace the rate by countable choice, a hidden selector, or a host convergence argument.

The Real-facing handoff is $U$, and the terminal seal is $E$. These rows are downstream of the same finite threshold route, so the rate used by the Real seal is the displayed CauchyModulus threshold already named in the packet.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name that same route. Therefore every modulus or Real-facing consumer factors through the displayed packet and receives none of the excluded escape objects.
\end{proof}
