The sibling lattice for $\ContinuousMapUp$ is horizontal rather than
kernel-isolated. It consumes the $\MetricUp$ distance witness from
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the $\ContinuousUp$
pointwise-modulus rows from
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuous-namecert}, and the $\CategoryUp$
graph, identity, and composition rows from
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-category-namecert}. Downstream
compact-uniform packets consume this $\ContinuousMapUp$ surface as the common
entry point for finite metric rows, pointwise continuity certificates, and
categorical composition data.

\begin{theorem}[ContinuousMap sibling lattice exactness]
\label{thm:continuousmap-sibling-lattice-exactness}
An accepted $\ContinuousMapUp$ packet is admitted exactly through the
$\MetricUp$ distance witness, the $\ContinuousUp$ pointwise-modulus rows, and
the $\CategoryUp$ graph, identity, and composition rows, together with the
visible $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support rows.

The admitted surface excludes host functions, function-space topology,
pointwise convergence, uniform convergence, and standard bridge rows. In
particular, downstream compact, complete, and uniform consumers may read only
the horizontal sibling lattice determined by the metric, continuity, and
category coordinates plus the displayed BEDC support rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply
\autoref{thm:continuousmap-carrier-category-metric-decomposition} to split the
carrier into the categorical hom row, the target-side modulus row, and the
metric-distance row. These are precisely the $\CategoryUp$, $\ContinuousUp$,
and $\MetricUp$ sibling coordinates named in the theorem statement.

Apply \autoref{thm:continuousmapup-metric-certificate-bridge} to read the same
surface through the certificate packet. The bridge exposes the visible
$\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ bookkeeping without adding
a host function or a function-space topology.

Apply
\autoref{thm:continuousmap-composition-category-metric-package} to the
composition rows. The package shows that identities and composites remain
inside the same category-metric lattice and that the displayed distance ledger
is fixed by the finite carrier data.

Finally apply
\autoref{thm:continuousmap-completemetric-compactmetric-uniform-route}. Its
consumer route uses the ContinuousMap surface as input while refusing
pointwise convergence, uniform convergence as an ambient topology, and
standard bridge rows. Hence the exact admitted surface is the stated
horizontal lattice and no larger host-level structure is exported.
\end{proof}
