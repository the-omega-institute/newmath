\begin{theorem}[ContinuousMapUp public export surface]
\label{thm:continuousmap-public-export-surface}
The metric certificate bridge of
\autoref{thm:continuousmapup-metric-certificate-bridge}, together with the
existing identity, composition, canonical-distance, field-transport, and
associativity rows, exports the public $\ContinuousMapUp$ surface over
$\mathsf{BHist}$. The exported data are exactly the source row, pattern row,
componentwise classifier, stability rows, canonical distance ledger, identity
rows, and composition rows.

This public surface does not claim host functions, function-space topology,
standard topological-space bridges, or pointwise or uniform convergence.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The source row, pattern row, componentwise classifier, stability rows, and
canonical distance ledger are the five certificate rows packaged by
\autoref{thm:continuousmapup-metric-certificate-bridge}. The componentwise
classifier and its stability are transported by
\autoref{thm:continuousmap-carrier-hsame-field-transport-depth}, while the
canonical distance ledger is the endpoint append ledger used by the same
bridge.

The public identity rows are the empty source and target carriers of
\autoref{thm:continuousmap-empty-identity-carriers}. Their two-sided behavior
inside composition is supplied by
\autoref{thm:continuousmap-empty-identities-two-sided-composition-units}.

The public composition rows are closed by
\autoref{thm:continuousmap-carrier-comp-closed} and repackaged by
\autoref{thm:continuousmap-composition-canonical-certificate-package}. The
associative bracketing row is the canonical package of
\autoref{thm:continuousmap-carrier-composition-associative-canonical-package}.

These cited rows exhaust the displayed $\ContinuousMapUp$ public surface over
$\mathsf{BHist}$. Since none of them introduces host functions, a
function-space topology, a standard topological-space bridge, or a
pointwise/uniform convergence interface, the theorem exports only the stated
BEDC surface.
\end{proof}
