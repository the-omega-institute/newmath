\begin{theorem}[Empty identity maps reflect metric witnesses]
\label{thm:continuousmap-empty-identity-metric-witness-reflects}
For histories $x,y,x',y',d,m_x,m_y,c_x,c_y:\mathsf{BHist}$, if
$\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(x,\emp,x',m_x,c_x)$ and
$\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(y,\emp,y',m_y,c_y)$ are empty-map
continuous carriers, then every displayed metric witness
$\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}(x',y',d)$ reflects to a source-side witness
$\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}(x,y,d)$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The empty-map carrier readback identifies $x'$ with $x$ and $y'$ with $y$.
Unary endpoint membership and the continuation field of the metric witness
transport along these two $\hsame$ witnesses, while the distance history is
held fixed.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Empty identity maps transport metric witnesses exactly]
\label{thm:continuousmap-empty-identity-metric-witness-iff}
For histories $x,y,x',y',d,m_x,m_y,c_x,c_y:\mathsf{BHist}$, if
$\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(x,\emp,x',m_x,c_x)$ and
$\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(y,\emp,y',m_y,c_y)$ are empty-map
continuous carriers, then the image-side and source-side metric witnesses are
equivalent:
$$
\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}(x',y',d)\Longleftrightarrow
\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}(x,y,d).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the forward direction, apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:continuousmap-empty-identity-metric-witness-reflects} to the
two empty-map continuous carriers and the displayed image-side metric witness.

For the reverse direction, apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:continuous-function-carrier-empty-map-iff} to the two
carriers. It reads back $x'\hsame x$ and $y'\hsame y$; symmetry orients these
witnesses as $x\hsame x'$ and $y\hsame y'$. Combine them with reflexivity
$d\hsame d$ and apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:metric-distance-witness-hsame-fields-transport} to transport
the source-side metric fields. Repacking those transported unary fields and
the transported continuation ledger gives
$\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}(x',y',d)$.
\end{proof}

BEDC source ContinuousMapUp, carrier $\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}$,
classifier $\hsame$, empty-map graph ledger $\emp$, distance ledger $d$,
metric certificate $\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}$, core theorem
Theorem~\ref{thm:continuousmap-empty-identity-metric-witness-iff}, exactness
the displayed iff, and downstream consumer compact-continuous metric readback.

\begin{theorem}[ContinuousMap target, certificate, and distance determinacy]
\label{thm:continuousmap-target-cert-distance-deterministic}

$distance$, and $distance'$, two concrete continuous-map carriers with the same
source, map, and modulus determine the target, certificate, and distance

$$
\begin{array}{l}
\mathsf{ContinuousMapCarrier}(source,map,target,modulus,cert,distance)
\Longrightarrow
\mathsf{ContinuousMapCarrier}(source,map,target',modulus,cert',distance')
\\
\Longrightarrow
\hsame(target,target')\land\hsame(cert,cert')\land
\hsame(distance,distance')\land\ContR(source,target,distance).

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The continuous-function components have the same source, map, and modulus, so
continuous-function target-certificate determinacy gives

witnesses then share source histories and target histories up to $\hsame$;

continuation ledger $\ContR(source,target,distance)$ is the final field of the
left metric distance witness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ContinuousMap carrier canonical distance exactness]
\label{thm:continuousmap-carrier-canonical-distance-exactness}
For histories $source,map,target,modulus,cert,distance:\mathsf{BHist}$,
the concrete continuous-map carrier is exactly the continuous-function carrier
together with the canonical source-target distance classifier:
$$
\mathsf{ContinuousMapCarrier}(source,map,target,modulus,cert,distance)
\Longleftrightarrow
\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(source,map,target,modulus,cert)\land
\hsame(distance,\mathsf{append}(source,target)).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
In the forward direction, project the continuous-function carrier from the
given continuous-map carrier. Theorem~\ref{thm:continuousmap-carrier-canonical-distance-iff}
then supplies the carrier with distance ledger
$\mathsf{append}(source,target)$, and
Theorem~\ref{thm:continuousmap-target-cert-distance-deterministic} compares
the two carriers with the same source, map, target, modulus, and certificate,
identifying $distance$ with $\mathsf{append}(source,target)$.

Conversely, start from the displayed continuous-function carrier and the
classifier $\hsame(distance,\mathsf{append}(source,target))$.
Theorem~\ref{thm:continuousmap-carrier-canonical-distance-iff} builds the
canonical-distance carrier. Its metric component contains the continuation
$\ContR(source,target,\mathsf{append}(source,target))$ and unary membership of
the canonical distance ledger. Transport the continuation by
$\mathsf{BEDC.Derived.MetricUp.MetricDistanceWitness\_cont\_hsame\_transport}$,
transport unary membership of the distance ledger along $\hsame$, and repack
these fields as the required $\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}(source,target,distance)$.
Together with the original continuous-function carrier this is precisely
$\mathsf{ContinuousMapCarrier}(source,map,target,modulus,cert,distance)$.

The ten-field fit is: traditional target normalized continuous-map distance
ledger; BEDC source $\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}$,
$\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}$, and $\ContR$; carrier
$\mathsf{ContinuousMapCarrier}$; classifier $\hsame$; stability obligations
distance determinacy and metric hsame transport; ledger the hidden distance
history; core theorem this iff; exactness the displayed iff; standard bridge
deferred; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{proof}
