\begin{theorem}[ContinuousMap visible-context canonical distance exactness]
\label{thm:continuousmap-visible-context-canonical-distance-exactness}
For histories $p,q,source,map,target,modulus,cert,distance:\mathsf{BHist}$,
the visible continuous-map carrier is exactly the central continuous-function
carrier together with the canonical source-target distance classifier:
$$
\begin{array}{l}
\mathsf{ContinuousMapCarrier}(p\cdot source,map,p\cdot target,modulus\cdot q,
(p\cdot cert)\cdot q,p\cdot distance)
\\[2pt]
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(p)\land\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(q)\land
\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(source,map,target,modulus,cert)\land
\hsame(distance,\mathsf{append}(source,target)).
\end{array}
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the forward direction, apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:continuousmap-carrier-visible-context-category-metric-decomposition}
to the displayed carrier. This produces unary evidence for $p$ and $q$, the
central category hom row, the central modulus row, and
$\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}(source,target,distance)$.

The category hom row and the modulus row repack as
$\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(source,map,target,modulus,cert)$. Compare
the resulting central continuous-map carrier with its canonical-distance form
by Theorem~\ref{thm:continuousmap-carrier-canonical-distance-exactness}. This
replaces the central metric witness by the classifier
$\hsame(distance,\mathsf{append}(source,target))$.

For the reverse direction, start from the two unary context histories, the
central continuous-function carrier, and the displayed canonical classifier.
Theorem~\ref{thm:continuousmap-carrier-canonical-distance-exactness} builds
the central continuous-map carrier, equivalently transporting the canonical
metric witness for $\mathsf{append}(source,target)$ along
$\hsame(distance,\mathsf{append}(source,target))$.

Project the central carrier into its category hom, modulus, and metric rows.
Then apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:continuousmap-carrier-visible-context-category-metric-decomposition}
from right to left with the two unary context histories. The result is
precisely the displayed visible-context
$\mathsf{ContinuousMapCarrier}$.
\end{proof}
