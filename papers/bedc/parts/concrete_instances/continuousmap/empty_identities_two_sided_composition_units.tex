\begin{theorem}[ContinuousMap empty identities are two-sided composition units]
\label{thm:continuousmap-empty-identities-two-sided-composition-units}
For histories
$source,target,map,modulus,cert,distance,displayed_L,displayed_R:\mathsf{BHist}$,
assume
$$
\mathsf{ContinuousMapCarrier}(source,map,target,modulus,cert,distance).
$$
Then the empty-map identities at the source and target are continuous-map
carriers, and they are two-sided units for composition:
\begin{enumerate}
\item the source identity carrier is
$$
\mathsf{ContinuousMapCarrier}
(source,\emp,source,\emp,source,\mathsf{append}(source,source));
$$

\item the target identity carrier is
$$
\mathsf{ContinuousMapCarrier}
(target,\emp,target,\emp,target,\mathsf{append}(target,target));
$$

\item composing the source identity on the left with the displayed carrier,
using
$$
\ContR(\emp,map,map),\qquad
\ContR(\emp,modulus,modulus),\qquad
\ContR(target,modulus,cert),
$$
closes as the canonical carrier
$$
\mathsf{ContinuousMapCarrier}
(source,map,target,modulus,cert,\mathsf{append}(source,target));
$$

\item composing the displayed carrier with the target identity on the right,
using
$$
\ContR(map,\emp,map),\qquad
\ContR(modulus,\emp,modulus),\qquad
\ContR(target,modulus,cert),
$$
closes as the same canonical carrier
$$
\mathsf{ContinuousMapCarrier}
(source,map,target,modulus,cert,\mathsf{append}(source,target));
$$

\item if the left and right unit composites are displayed with distances
$displayed_L$ and $displayed_R$, respectively, then
$$
\hsame(displayed_L,\mathsf{append}(source,target))\land
\hsame(displayed_R,\mathsf{append}(source,target)).
$$
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the continuous-function carrier from the given
$\mathsf{ContinuousMapCarrier}$. Its fields supply unary endpoint data and the
certificate continuation $\ContR(target,modulus,cert)$.

For the source identity, use the unary source field and the left-unit
continuation $\ContR(source,\emp,source)$ to form
$\mathsf{ContinuousModulusWitness}(source,\emp,source)$. Apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:continuous-function-carrier-empty-map-identity}, then package
the canonical distance by
Theorem~\ref{thm:continuousmap-carrier-canonical-distance-iff}.

For the target identity, the same construction uses the unary target field and
the left-unit continuation $\ContR(target,\emp,target)$. The empty-map identity
theorem builds the continuous-function carrier at $target$, and canonical
distance packaging gives the displayed target identity carrier.

Now apply Theorem~\ref{thm:continuousmap-carrier-comp-closed} to the source
identity carrier and the displayed carrier, with the BEDC left-unit laws
$\ContR(\emp,map,map)$ and $\ContR(\emp,modulus,modulus)$ and the projected
certificate ledger $\ContR(target,modulus,cert)$. This gives the left unit
composite over $\mathsf{append}(source,target)$.

Apply the same composition-closure theorem to the displayed carrier and the
target identity carrier, using the right-unit laws $\ContR(map,\emp,map)$ and
$\ContR(modulus,\emp,modulus)$ together with the same certificate ledger. This
gives the right unit composite over the same canonical distance.

Finally, apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:continuousmap-carrier-comp-distance-deterministic} to each unit
composite presentation. Comparing any displayed left or right unit-composite
distance with the corresponding canonical composite identifies it by $\hsame$
with $\mathsf{append}(source,target)$.
\end{proof}
