\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\LipschitzModulusUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-lipschitz-modulus-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop--Bridges constructive metric analysis and Kelley-style uniform-space bookkeeping, the $\LipschitzModulusUp$ packet records the finite tolerance object carried by a displayed Lipschitz bound. It sits over the source and target metric rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the real-bound seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the Lipschitz map and constant surfaces of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-lipschitzmap-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-lipschitz-constant-namecert}, the division and rounding ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the exported modulus and continuity consumers of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformmodulus-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformcontinuity-namecert}. The packet names a finite tolerance-only route; it is not a host proof of continuity, a quotient of maps, a selected inverse for a real bound, or an ambient completeness theorem.

\begin{definition}[LipschitzModulus carrier]\label{def:lipschitz-modulus-carrier}
A $\LipschitzModulusUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
M=(X,Y,F,K,E,D,U,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the source $\MetricUp$ row and $Y$ is the target $\MetricUp$ row.
\item $F$ is the $\LipschitzMapUp$ row whose graph and source-distance reads use $X$ and $Y$.
\item $K$ is the $\LipschitzConstantUp$ row for the same source, target, and graph data.
\item $E$ is the positive target-tolerance row, read through $\RealUp$ rather than through a host scalar.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ division and rounding ledger that turns $E$ and the displayed bound in $K$ into a source-tolerance request.
\item $U$ is the exported $\UniformModulusUp$ row consumed by $\UniformContinuityUp$ and $\ContinuousMapUp$ routes.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for metric rows, map rows, constant rows, tolerance rows, dyadic division and rounding rows, the exported modulus, continuation, provenance, and local naming.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a target-tolerance request through $E,K,D$ to the source-tolerance row exported by $U$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LipschitzModulusUp$, $\MetricUp$, $\LipschitzMapUp$, $\LipschitzConstantUp$, $\RealUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\UniformModulusUp$, $\UniformContinuityUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LipschitzModulusUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier accepts only packets with these rows. It has no coordinate for an unbounded host Lipschitz proof, a quotient-function equality, a hidden choice of reciprocal, a completed function space, or an ambient real-completeness argument.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[LipschitzModulus NameCert obligations]\label{thm:lipschitz-modulus-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\LipschitzModulusUp$ carrier $M=(X,Y,F,K,E,D,U,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\LipschitzModulusUp}$ surface consists exactly of five finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the source metric, target metric, Lipschitz map, Lipschitz constant, positive tolerance, dyadic division and rounding, exported modulus, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item bound alignment, requiring $F$ and $K$ to read the same source, target, and graph rows before a tolerance conversion is exported;
\item tolerance positivity, requiring $E$ to be displayed before the dyadic ledger is read;
\item modulus export, requiring every $\UniformModulusUp$, $\UniformContinuityUp$, or $\ContinuousMapUp$ consumer to pass through $E,K,D,U$ in that order;
\item ledger non-escape, excluding host Lipschitz proofs, quotient map equality, hidden reciprocal choice, completed function spaces, selected limits, countable choice, and ambient completeness.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:lipschitz-modulus-carrier}. The displayed packet exposes exactly the metric, Lipschitz-map, Lipschitz-constant, positive-tolerance, dyadic-ledger, modulus, transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows.

The bound-alignment and positivity obligations are row-order checks. The map row $F$ and constant row $K$ share the source and target metric rows, and the tolerance row $E$ is the only positive target request available to the dyadic ledger $D$.

The export obligation follows because $U$ is the only $\UniformModulusUp$ row in the carrier. A continuity consumer therefore reaches $U$ only after reading the displayed tolerance, constant, and dyadic division and rounding route.

The refusal clause is an inventory check on the carrier. None of the displayed rows supplies host continuity, quotient map equality, hidden reciprocal selection, completed function-space data, selected limits, choice, or ambient completeness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LipschitzModulus uniform-continuity handoff]\label{thm:lipschitz-modulus-uniform-continuity-handoff}
Let $M=(X,Y,F,K,E,D,U,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\LipschitzModulusUp$ carrier. Every $\UniformContinuityUp$ or $\ContinuousMapUp$ consumer that requests a target tolerance receives only the source-tolerance route exported by $U$ after the packet has displayed the target tolerance $E$, the aligned Lipschitz constant $K$, and the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ division and rounding ledger $D$. Thus the handoff is a finite modulus route, not a rereading of a host Lipschitz proof.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the carrier of \autoref{def:lipschitz-modulus-carrier}. The source and target metrics are $X$ and $Y$, the graph and bound rows are $F$ and $K$, and the only target-tolerance request is $E$.

The dyadic ledger $D$ performs the finite division and rounding step from the displayed tolerance and bound data. Since $D$ is the only arithmetic route in the packet, no consumer can replace it by a host reciprocal or by a selected real approximation.

The exported row $U$ is then read by the $\UniformModulusUp$ surface and by downstream $\UniformContinuityUp$ and $\ContinuousMapUp$ consumers. Transport through $H$ and replay through $C$ preserve this order, so the consumer receives only the displayed finite modulus route.
\end{proof}
