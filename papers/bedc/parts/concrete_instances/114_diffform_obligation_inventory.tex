\begin{definition}[Differential-form obligation inventory]
\label{def:diffform-obligation-inventory}
The paper-side obligation inventory for $\DiffFormUp$ is the following
ten-field roadmap:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item the traditional target is the certificate surface for antisymmetric
  multilinear forms on tangent probes;
  \item the BEDC sources are $\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$ and
  $\NameCert_{\TensorProductUp}$;
  \item the carrier is
  $\mathsf{DiffFormBHistCarrier}_{M,T}$ from
  \autoref{def:diffform-bhist-form-carrier};
  \item the classifier is $\sim_{\DiffFormUp}$ from
  \autoref{def:diffform-bhist-form-classifier};
  \item carrier stability is the $\hsame$ transport row of
  \autoref{thm:diffform-carrier-hsame-transport};
  \item classifier stability is
  \autoref{thm:diffform-form-classifier-stability};
  \item the ledger rows are exactly the degree, probe, tensor, scalar,
  antisymmetry, and source-provenance rows;
  \item the core theorem list consists of carrier exhaustion, classifier
  reuse, ledger exactness, transport stability, wedge coverage, and the
  exterior-input boundary theorems already displayed in this chapter;
  \item exactness is row exhaustion: every admitted downstream use must factor
  through one of the displayed rows;
  \item no standard bridge is supplied here, so the inventory is the displayed
  paper-side obligation surface and not a bridge certificate.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Differential-form carrier support obligation]
\label{thm:diffform-carrier-support-obligation}
The carrier support promised by the $\DiffFormUp$ obligation inventory is
exactly the six-coordinate surface
$$
(d,\mathcal{P},\tau,\sigma,\alpha,\lambda)
$$
of \autoref{def:diffform-bhist-form-carrier}. It is therefore exhausted by the
degree row, probe-bundle row, tensor row, scalar endpoint row, antisymmetry
ledger, and source-provenance ledger, as projected by
\autoref{thm:diffform-bhist-carrier-obligation}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold \autoref{def:diffform-obligation-inventory}. Its carrier field points
to $\mathsf{DiffFormBHistCarrier}_{M,T}$ and introduces no second carrier
predicate.

Apply \autoref{def:diffform-bhist-form-carrier}. A carried row is displayed as
the tuple $(d,\mathcal{P},\tau,\sigma,\alpha,\lambda)$, with one acceptance
clause for each of its six coordinates.

Now apply \autoref{thm:diffform-bhist-carrier-obligation}. That theorem
states that carrier acceptance adds no seventh coordinate and no hidden source
outside the displayed histories. The support field of the inventory is
therefore exactly the six-coordinate carrier surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Differential-form classifier transport obligation]
\label{thm:diffform-classifier-transport-obligation}
The classifier obligation recorded by
\autoref{def:diffform-obligation-inventory} consists of reflexivity, symmetry,
transitivity, carrier transport, and classifier stability for the displayed
$\DiffFormUp$ rows. These obligations are projections of
\autoref{def:diffform-bhist-form-classifier},
\autoref{thm:diffform-bhist-classifier-obligation},
\autoref{thm:diffform-carrier-hsame-transport}, and
\autoref{thm:diffform-form-classifier-stability}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For reflexivity, read each classifier component in
\autoref{def:diffform-bhist-form-classifier} against itself. The degree,
tensor, antisymmetry, and source-ledger clauses are reflexive $\hsame$ rows,
and the scalar endpoint clause uses the retained source classifier on the
same endpoint row.

For symmetry and transitivity, compose the same displayed clauses
componentwise. The $\hsame$ clauses supply the ledger-side comparisons, while
the scalar endpoint comparison stays inside the upstream scalar classifier
named in the definition.

It remains to identify the transport obligations. Carrier transport is
exactly \autoref{thm:diffform-carrier-hsame-transport}. Once transported
rows are accepted, classifier stability is exactly
\autoref{thm:diffform-form-classifier-stability}. Thus the inventory records
no classifier law beyond the existing displayed classifier and transport
theorems.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Differential-form exterior input boundary obligation]
\label{thm:diffform-exterior-input-boundary-obligation}
Downstream $\DeRhamUp$, $\SymplecticUp$, and $\ContactUp$ consumers of
$\DiffFormUp$ may use only the listed $BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$,
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, wedge-ledger, and exterior-input rows. In particular,
their access is bounded by \autoref{thm:diffform-derham-input-boundary} and
\autoref{thm:diffform-exterior-derivative-downstream-scope}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First unfold the source and carrier entries of
\autoref{def:diffform-obligation-inventory}. They name only
$\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$, $\NameCert_{\TensorProductUp}$, the carried
$BHist$ rows, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, $\hsame$, and the $\Cont$ ledgers
attached to tensor concatenation.

Next apply \autoref{thm:diffform-derham-input-boundary}. It permits
exterior-derivative consumers to read the probe bundle, degree row, tensor
row, scalar endpoint classifier, antisymmetry ledger, wedge coverage, wedge
probe concatenation ledger, and wedge degree ledger, and it excludes $d^2=0$,
closedness, nondegeneracy, and contact nonintegrability from the
$\DiffFormUp$ source.

Finally apply
\autoref{thm:diffform-exterior-derivative-downstream-scope}. The downstream
scope theorem adds the exterior-derivative candidate ledger and confirms that
the three named consumer chapters must state any operator or geometric laws
inside their own surfaces. The obligation boundary is therefore exactly the
listed rows and no broader import surface.
\end{proof}
