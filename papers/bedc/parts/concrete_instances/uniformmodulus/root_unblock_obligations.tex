\begin{theorem}[UniformModulus compact-cover root row totality]
\label{thm:uniformmodulus-root-compact-cover-row-totality}
For an accepted $\UniformModulusUp$ packet
$$
U=(E,D,\mathcal{P},R,C,M,F,L,Q,P,N),
$$
every compact-cover read made by a compact-continuous downstream consumer is exhausted by the displayed $\CompactMetricUp$ finite-net row $C$, the finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ spine $\mathcal{P}$, the positive $\RatUp$ radius rows $R$, and the boundary rows $L,Q,P,N$.

Thus the root compact-cover obligation is a finite packet obligation over the carrier of \autoref{def:uniformmodulus-bhist-carrier}. It has no coordinate for open-cover compactness, host finite-subcover selection, choice-selected centers, or an external cover selector.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.UniformModulusUp.UniformModulusPacket\_root\_compact\_cover\_row\_totality}
\begin{proof}
Read the accepted carrier through \autoref{def:uniformmodulus-bhist-carrier}. The compact-cover coordinates visible to the consumer are exactly the bundled centers $\mathcal{P}$, the radius ledger $R$, the compact finite-net row $C$, and the transport, route, provenance, and naming boundary $L,Q,P,N$.

Apply \autoref{thm:uniformmodulus-compact-net-coverage-obligation}. It binds every compact-net coverage read to a center already present in $\mathcal{P}$ and to the radius rows already recorded in $R$.

Apply \autoref{thm:uniformmodulus-finite-probebundle-fold} to keep the precision threshold tied to the same finite bundle and radius ledger. Then use \autoref{thm:uniformmodulus-compact-continuous-nonescape} to exclude any compactness or center-selection channel outside $C,\mathcal{P},R,L,Q,P,N$.
\end{proof}

Use \autoref{thm:uniformmodulus-finite-probebundle-fold} to keep the folded threshold $D$ below the radius rows attached to those centers. The metric witnesses read by the pointwise row therefore remain on the displayed $\MetricUp$ route rather than on a host selector.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:uniformmodulus-compact-continuous-nonescape}. The rows $L,Q,P,N$ are the only boundary through which the pointwise data can leave the packet, so $M$ has no external modulus or undisplayed center coordinate.
