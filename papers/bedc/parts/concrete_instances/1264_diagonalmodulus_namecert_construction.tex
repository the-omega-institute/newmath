\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\DiagonalModulusUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-diagonalmodulus-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\DiagonalModulusUp$ packet names the selector modulus used when regular rational streams are read along a diagonal and then sealed as real-name data. Following Bishop--Bridges constructive analysis, the packet keeps the modulus explicit: a consumer precision is converted into a displayed threshold, a finite stream window, a $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, a $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observation ledger, and a $\RealUp$ seal row. It does not classify by quotient streams, host real equality, selected limits, or ambient completeness.

Its dependency list is $\DyadicRatCore$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRat$, $\RealUp$, $\CauchyModulusUp$, $\RegularityModulusUp$, $\RegularCauchyDiagonalUp$, and $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$.

\begin{definition}[DiagonalModulus carrier]
\label{def:diagonalmodulus-carrier}
A $\DiagonalModulusUp$ carrier row is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
\Delta=(n,\mu,w,r,d,e,P,N)
$$
with the following visible coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $n$ is the unary precision request issued by the downstream real-name reader;
\item $\mu$ is the selected modulus threshold witnessing how far the diagonal read must inspect regular rational data at precision $n$;
\item $w$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window selected from $\mu$ and $n$;
\item $r$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row obtained inside the window $w$;
\item $d$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observation ledger recording the dyadic rational bounds used by that readback;
\item $e$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row exposed to the consumer after the readback is displayed;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance row naming the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\CauchyModulusUp$, $\RegularityModulusUp$, $\RegularCauchyDiagonalUp$, and $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ dependencies;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DiagonalModulusUp}$ provenance row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no quotient of streams, no host-level equality of reals, no selected limit object, and no completeness theorem field.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[DiagonalModulus classifier]
\label{def:diagonalmodulus-classifier}
Two $\DiagonalModulusUp$ carrier rows are classified together exactly when their displayed precision requests, modulus thresholds, stream windows, regular-sequence readbacks, dyadic observation ledgers, real-seal rows, package rows, and local certificate rows are transported componentwise by the recorded $\hsame$ comparisons and $\Cont$ routes. The classifier is not allowed to compare hidden sequences, quotient representatives, host real endpoints, or unrecorded limit selections.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[DiagonalModulus window selector totality]
\label{thm:diagonalmodulus-window-selector-total}
For every accepted $\DiagonalModulusUp$ carrier row and every displayed unary precision request $n$, the selected modulus threshold $\mu$ determines a finite $\StreamNameUp$ window $w$ and a $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row $r$ inside that window. The selector therefore returns visible finite data for every consumer precision request.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DiagonalModulusUp.DiagonalModulusPacket\_window\_selector\_total}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier row of \autoref{def:diagonalmodulus-carrier}. The precision coordinate $n$ and the threshold coordinate $\mu$ are stored before any readback, so the window selection is read from displayed data rather than from an external sequence object.

The $\StreamNameUp$ coordinate $w$ records the finite window selected by that threshold. The readback coordinate $r$ is then attached to the same window, and the allowed transport routes are exactly the $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ entries recorded by the carrier provenance.

Thus a consumer precision request always lands in a visible stream window and a visible regular rational row. No hidden limit selector is consulted.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DiagonalModulus RegSeqRat handoff]
\label{thm:diagonalmodulus-regseqrat-handoff}
Every $\DiagonalModulusUp$ read that reaches $\RegSeqRatUp$ factors through the selected $\StreamNameUp$ window $w$, the displayed readback row $r$, and the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger $d$ attached to that readback. The handoff exports only finite rational evidence and the recorded classifier routes.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from an accepted carrier and a selected window. By \autoref{thm:diagonalmodulus-window-selector-total}, the window and the regular rational readback are already displayed as coordinates of the same finite packet.

The dyadic ledger $d$ is stored beside $r$. It records the rational observations and radius data used by the readback, so transport of the readback also transports the corresponding $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ evidence.

Consequently the handoff into $\RegSeqRatUp$ is a finite route through $w$, $r$, and $d$. It does not expose host sequence equality or quotient equality of regular streams.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DiagonalModulus RealUp seal non-escape]
\label{thm:diagonalmodulus-real-seal-nonescape}
The $\RealUp$ seal row $e$ of an accepted $\DiagonalModulusUp$ packet can be consumed only after the precision request, modulus threshold, stream window, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger have been displayed. The seal therefore exports a real-name surface without exporting selected limits, quotient equality, host real equality, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the coordinates of \autoref{def:diagonalmodulus-carrier} in order. The seal row appears after the precision, threshold, window, readback, and dyadic ledger rows, so any consumer route to $e$ must pass through those displayed coordinates.

The handoff theorem identifies the preceding rational evidence. Once that evidence is displayed, the seal row records only the $\RealUp$ consumer boundary attached to it.

Therefore the seal cannot be used as an escape to an unrecorded completion object. It withholds selected limits, quotient stream equality, host real equality, and ambient completeness data.
\end{proof}
