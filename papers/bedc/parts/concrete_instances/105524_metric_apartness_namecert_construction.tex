\chapter{A Constructive Apartness Certificate for $\MetricApartnessUp$}

Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, the $\MetricApartnessUp$ packet records the positive-distance separation surface of a constructive metric space as finite BEDC NameCert data. It sits after the metric carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the separated zero-distance classifier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separatedmetric-namecert}, and the completion boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metriccompletion-namecert}. The packet supplies a finite apartness interface for metric and completion consumers; it does not import host inequality, real trichotomy, quotient classes, selected representatives, or ambient completeness.

\begin{definition}[MetricApartness carrier]
\label{def:metric-apartness-carrier}
A $\MetricApartnessUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
A=(M,x,y,D,R,Z,S,Q,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the $\MetricUp$ source row supplying the displayed distance-read interface.
\item $x$ and $y$ are the two inspected point rows over the same metric source.
\item $D$ is the metric-distance witness row for the finite read $d(x,y)$.
\item $R$ is the $\RealUp$ positive-distance row, recording that the displayed distance has a positive real witness.
\item $Z$ is the $\RealUp$ zero-distance refusal row, recording that the accepted apartness read rejects the zero-distance branch by displayed real data.
\item $S$ is the $\SeparatedMetricUp$ classifier-comparison row, used only to compare the zero-distance boundary with the displayed point classifier.
\item $Q$ is the $\MetricCompletionUp$ and $\CauchySequenceEquivalenceUp$ finite-window transport row for completion-facing consumers.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the metric source, point rows, distance witness, real positivity row, zero-distance refusal row, separated comparison, finite-window transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from an apartness request through $M,x,y,D,R,Z,S,Q,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\MetricApartnessUp$, $\MetricUp$, $\RealUp$, $\SeparatedMetricUp$, $\MetricCompletionUp$, $\CauchySequenceEquivalenceUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MetricApartnessUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted apartness packets only through these displayed rows. It contains no host inequality predicate, no trichotomy of real numbers, no quotient-completion class, no selected representative, and no ambient completeness witness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[MetricApartness NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:metric-apartness-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\MetricApartnessUp$ carrier $A=(M,x,y,D,R,Z,S,Q,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\MetricApartnessUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the displayed metric source and point rows, distance-witness visibility through $D$, positive-distance readback through $R$, zero-distance refusal through $Z$, separated classifier comparison through $S$, completion-facing finite-window transport through $Q$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$. Every apartness read therefore follows the route
$$
M,x,y\longmapsto D\longmapsto R,Z\longmapsto S,Q\longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
No additional coordinate can decide apartness by host inequality, real trichotomy, quotient class comparison, selected representative equality, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:metric-apartness-carrier}. The rows $M,x,y$ expose the metric source and the two inspected points, and $D$ is the only row from which their displayed distance can be read.

The real-facing branch is then forced through $R$ and $Z$. The row $R$ supplies positive-distance evidence, while $Z$ records refusal of the zero-distance branch by the same $\RealUp$ readback surface.

Separated and completion-facing consumers are downstream. The row $S$ compares the zero-distance boundary with the separated classifier, and $Q$ transports finite-window completion reads through $\MetricCompletionUp$ and $\CauchySequenceEquivalenceUp$ without creating quotient classes or selected representatives.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ preserve, replay, source, and name the displayed route. Since no row stores host inequality, real trichotomy, quotient comparison, selected representative equality, or ambient completeness, the listed coordinates exhaust the NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetricApartness completion handoff]
\label{thm:metric-apartness-completion-handoff}
Every $\MetricCompletionUp$ or $\CauchySequenceEquivalenceUp$ consumer of an accepted $\MetricApartnessUp$ packet must read the finite route $M,x,y,D,R,Z,S,Q,H,C,P,N$ before treating the two displayed names as apart. The exported handoff is only the positive-distance certificate together with zero-distance refusal, separated-classifier comparison, finite-window transport, componentwise transport, replay, provenance, and local naming. It does not export host inequality, real trichotomy, quotient-completion equality, selected representatives, or an ambient completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a completion-facing apartness request. The metric source and inspected point rows $M,x,y$ must be displayed before the distance witness $D$ can be consumed.

The rows $R$ and $Z$ bind the constructive apartness decision to real readback: one row supplies positive-distance evidence, and the other refuses the zero-distance branch. This gives the separated row $S$ a displayed classifier boundary to compare.

The finite-window row $Q$ is then the only completion-facing handoff. It transports the same distance and refusal data through $\MetricCompletionUp$ and $\CauchySequenceEquivalenceUp$ consumers, while $H,C,P,N$ record transport, replay, provenance, and local naming.

Thus the completion consumer receives exactly the finite apartness route described above, with no hidden route through host inequality, trichotomy, quotient classes, selected representatives, or ambient completeness.
\end{proof}
