The $\DiagonalIndexCofinalityUp$ packet realizes the finite modulus-threshold row used by the Real completeness proof target of \autoref{sec:real-completeness-limit-theorem}. For a regular Cauchy input and precision request $n$, it records the displayed inequality that the selected diagonal index is at least $\mu(1/(n+1))$ before any $\RealUp$ completion-facing consumer reads the diagonal representative. The packet sits between the representative route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-diagonallimitrepresentative-namecert}, the cofinal modulus seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cofinal-modulus-seal-namecert}, the tail budget of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-tail-cofinality-budget-namecert}, the stream naming surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular-rational row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic observation core of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the terminal Real surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}.

The certificate is deliberately finite. It names the threshold comparison, the selected diagonal window, the regular-rational readback, the dyadic tolerance row, the Real handoff, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming. It does not export quotient stream equality, a host-selected limit, countable choice, an unbounded search principle, or an ambient completeness theorem.

\begin{definition}[Diagonal index cofinality carrier]
\label{def:diagonal-index-cofinality-carrier}
A $\DiagonalIndexCofinalityUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
I=(X,\mu,n,k,i,W,R,D,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the accepted regular Cauchy input family over $\RegSeqRatUp$;
\item $\mu$ is the displayed modulus row whose precision read at $1/(n+1)$ returns $k$;
\item $n$ is the finite precision request made by the Real-completion consumer;
\item $k$ is the threshold row $k=\mu(1/(n+1))$;
\item $i$ is the selected diagonal index row with $i=k+n+1$ and hence $i\geq k$;
\item $W$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window opened at the selected diagonal index;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row for the selected member and index;
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger attached to the same finite read;
\item $E$ is the downstream $\RealUp$ handoff row reached only after $W,R,D$ and the threshold comparison have been displayed;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the input family, modulus row, request, threshold, index, window, readback, dyadic ledger, Real handoff, route, provenance, and naming rows;

\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DiagonalIndexCofinalityUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The local classifier compares accepted carriers only through the displayed rows $X,\mu,n,k,i,W,R,D,E,H,C,P,N$ and the visible threshold proof $i\geq k$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Diagonal index cofinality window monotonicity]
\label{thm:diagonal-index-cofinality-window-monotonicity}
For an accepted $\DiagonalIndexCofinalityUp$ carrier $I=(X,\mu,n,k,i,W,R,D,E,H,C,P,N)$, every downstream finite-window read exposed through $W,R,D,E$ is indexed at $i$ and therefore lies at or beyond the modulus threshold $k=\mu(1/(n+1))$. If the same packet is transported by the componentwise $\hsame$ row $H$, the transported window preserves the same threshold comparison before the $\RealUp$ handoff can be consumed.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The selected window row $W$ is attached to the index coordinate $i$ of the carrier. By \autoref{def:diagonal-index-cofinality-carrier}, this coordinate is not independent of the modulus row: it is the displayed value $k+n+1$ with $k=\mu(1/(n+1))$.

Apply the obligation surface of \autoref{thm:diagonal-index-cofinality-namecert-obligation-surface}. It requires any accepted read of $W,R,D,E$ to preserve the threshold row, selected-index row, and cofinality comparison before the readback or handoff row is public.

Transport along $H$ is componentwise. Hence it carries the request, threshold, selected index, window, readback, dyadic ledger, and Real handoff together, and it cannot move the finite window to a pre-threshold index without changing one of the displayed coordinates.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Diagonal index cofinality Real-completion handoff]
\label{thm:diagonal-index-cofinality-real-completion-handoff}

$$

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the route displayed by the carrier. The precision request $n$ first reads the modulus row $\mu$ to obtain $k$, then forms the selected diagonal index $i=k+n+1$.

Next apply \autoref{thm:diagonal-index-cofinality-window-monotonicity}. It shows that the finite StreamName, RegSeqRat, dyadic, and Real rows exposed by the packet all remain at or beyond the modulus threshold.

Finally read the Real handoff through $E$ and the support rows $H,C,P,N$. Since the carrier has no coordinate for a direct host limit, quotient stream equality, unbounded selection, countable choice, or ambient completeness, every consumer of this packet receives exactly the finite cofinality handoff displayed above.
\end{proof}
