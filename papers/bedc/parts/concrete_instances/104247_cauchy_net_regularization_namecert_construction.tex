\begin{definition}[CauchyNetRegularization carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-net-regularization-carrier}
A $\CauchyNetRegularizationUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
G=(D,S,R,T,A,E,M,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the accepted $\CauchyNetUp$ directed-window row whose inspected vertices and common upper-window witnesses provide the source evidence.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule exposing the finite observation windows read from $D$.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row attached to the scheduled observations.
\item $T$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger carrying the dyadic radii, rational comparison cells, and late-window bounds used by the regularization.
\item $A$ is the finite regularization row: for each displayed tolerance request it chooses a named regular window already justified by the directed common-window witness in $D$ and the tolerance ledger $T$.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row available only after the route $D,S,R,T,A$ has been displayed.
\item $M$ is the $\MetricCompletionUp$ handoff row available only after the same regularized route has been displayed.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the directed evidence, schedules, readbacks, tolerances, regularization row, seal row, completion handoff, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ stores displayed $\Cont$ replay from directed windows to scheduled observations, regular readback, dyadic tolerance checks, regularized windows, and then to the real and metric-completion consumers.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyNetRegularizationUp$, $\CauchyNetUp$, $\CauchyRegularizationUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\MetricCompletionUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyNetRegularizationUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through these rows. It has no coordinate for quotient-net equality, selected representatives, cofinal choice functions, host metric completeness, open-cover compactness, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyNetRegularization NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-net-regularization-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CauchyNetRegularizationUp$ carrier $G=(D,S,R,T,A,E,M,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyNetRegularizationUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the eleven displayed rows, directed-window evidence through $D$, finite schedule exposure through $S$, regular rational readback through $R$, dyadic tolerance and comparison exactness through $T$, regularized-window selection through $A$, terminal real sealing through $E$, metric-completion handoff through $M$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:cauchy-net-regularization-carrier}. The accepted packet exposes the directed-window evidence, schedule, readback, tolerance ledger, regularization row, two consumer rows, and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

Read the evidence route in order. The directed row $D$ supplies only displayed finite common-window witnesses; the schedule $S$ and readback row $R$ then expose the observations that are available to a regularization consumer. The dyadic row $T$ records the tolerance and comparison cells attached to those observations.

The regularization row $A$ is therefore not an independent selector. It chooses only among windows already justified by $D,S,R,T$, and the terminal rows $E$ and $M$ may be read only after that route has been displayed.

Rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite packet. Since the carrier has no quotient-net, selected-subnet, cofinal-choice, host-completeness, open-cover, or ambient real-completeness coordinate, none of those principles is part of the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyNetRegularization ledger exactness]
\label{thm:cauchy-net-regularization-ledger-exactness}
Every $\RealUp$ or $\MetricCompletionUp$ consumer read exported by an accepted $\CauchyNetRegularizationUp$ packet factors through exactly the regularized directed route
$$
D\longmapsto S\longmapsto R\longmapsto T\longmapsto A\longmapsto (E,M)
$$
together with $H,C,P,N$. Consequently the consumer sees finite Cauchy-net evidence regularized into displayed StreamName, RegSeqRat, and DyadicRatCore rows before reaching either terminal surface, and it receives no quotient net, selected subnet, cofinal choice function, host completion theorem, open-cover compactness proof, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the source row $D$. By \autoref{thm:cauchy-net-namecert-obligation-surface}, Cauchy-net evidence is available only as displayed directed-window data with common-window witnesses.

Next read the regularization route. The scheduled windows $S$, regular readback $R$, and dyadic tolerance ledger $T$ are the only rows from which $A$ may form a regularized window. Thus every regularized observation is tied to the same finite directed witness data rather than to an external subnet or host net.

Finally inspect the two terminal rows. The real seal $E$ and metric-completion handoff $M$ are downstream of $A$ and share the same transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows. Removing any row from $D,S,R,T,A,H,C,P,N$ breaks the displayed route, so the ledger is exact for the stated consumer surface.
\end{proof}
