Bishop-Bridges constructive analysis treats real names through explicit regularity data: a precision request is answered by a finite stage, the finite stage is read through rational observations, and the terminal real object is consumed only after the regularity route has been displayed. The $\RealRegularityModulusUp$ packet records this route between the Cauchy-modulus surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchymodulus-namecert}, the finite observations of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the dyadic endpoint ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. It gives Real and Completion consumers a named finite modulus route rather than a parameter echo of Cauchy convergence.

\begin{definition}[RealRegularityModulus carrier]
\label{def:real-regularity-modulus-carrier}
A $\RealRegularityModulusUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(S,M,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule row, exposing exactly the finite observation windows inspected by the modulus route.
\item $M$ is the $\CauchyModulusUp$ threshold row, answering each dyadic precision request with a displayed finite stage.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger read by $M$ before any rational-window comparison is accepted.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular rational readback row attached to the finite windows selected by $S$ and $M$.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row, available only after $S,M,D,R$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the schedule, modulus, dyadic, readback, seal, continuation, provenance, and naming rows.
\item $C$ stores displayed $\Cont$ replay from a Real or Completion precision request through $D,M,S,R$ and then into $E$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RealRegularityModulusUp$, $\CauchyModulusUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RealRegularityModulusUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $S,M,D,R,E,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for quotient stream equality, selected limits, host real equality, countable choice, trichotomy, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[RealRegularityModulus NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:real-regularity-modulus-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RealRegularityModulusUp$ carrier $Q=(S,M,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\RealRegularityModulusUp}$ surface consists exactly of finite schedule exposure, Cauchy-modulus threshold exposure, dyadic tolerance exposure, regular rational readback, terminal Real seal exposure, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming. Any Real or Completion consumer of $Q$ must read those rows before consuming the seal row $E$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier to the displayed rows $S,M,D,R,E,H,C,P,N$. The stream schedule and modulus row provide the only finite-stage route, while the dyadic ledger supplies the tolerance data consumed by that route.

Next read the regular rational handoff. The row $R$ is attached to the windows selected by $S$ and $M$, so a consumer reaches rational readback only through the displayed finite schedule and tolerance ledger.

Finally, the seal row $E$ is downstream of $S,M,D,R$, and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$ only transport, replay, source, and name that same route. Since no excluded coordinate appears in the carrier, the local NameCert surface is exactly the displayed regularity-modulus surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RealRegularityModulus RegSeqRat handoff]
\label{thm:real-regularity-modulus-regseqrat-handoff}
Every accepted $\RealRegularityModulusUp$ packet hands a precision request to $\RegSeqRatUp$ only after the request has been read through the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger $D$, the $\CauchyModulusUp$ threshold row $M$, and the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule row $S$. The handoff route is
$$
D,M,S\longmapsto R
$$
together with $H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a precision request. The dyadic ledger $D$ records the requested tolerance before the modulus route can choose a finite stage.

The row $M$ then supplies the displayed threshold, and $S$ exposes the finite windows available at that threshold. Only those displayed windows are passed to the regular rational readback row $R$.

Componentwise transport and continuation replay preserve the same row order. Thus the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff cannot bypass $D,M,S$, and the structural rows cannot introduce a selected limit or quotient-stream comparison.
\end{proof}

\label{thm:real-regularity-modulus-real-seal-boundary}

$$

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

Apply \autoref{thm:real-regularity-modulus-regseqrat-handoff} to the analytic part of the route. It places regular rational readback after the dyadic tolerance, threshold, and finite-window rows.

The terminal seal is therefore downstream of the explicit regularity route. Since the carrier has no coordinate for the excluded principles, the seal boundary exports only the finite route recorded by $D,M,S,R,H,C,P,N$.
\end{proof}
