\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RepresentedSpaceUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-representedspace-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Weihrauch-style represented spaces and Bishop--Bridges constructive
analysis, the $\RepresentedSpaceUp$ packet records the interface at which a
computable name is allowed to present a real-facing object. It sits over the
finite stream windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert},
the regular rational readback surface of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger
of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the terminal
real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The carrier
below treats representation as a finite NameCert interface rather than as a
quotient of names or a selected host representative.

\begin{definition}[RepresentedSpace carrier]
\label{def:representedspace-carrier}
A $\RepresentedSpaceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
R=(S,W,\rho,X,T,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the name carrier: a finite source row whose observations are read
through the $\StreamNameUp$ interface.
\item $W$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ schedule recording the inspected
precision requests, stream positions, and replay windows.
\item $\rho$ is the representation relation row; it relates the displayed
stream observations to the target row only through the local finite schedule.
\item $X$ is the target carrier row, with the real-facing case routed through
$\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, and $\RealUp$ before any consumer reads it.
\item $T$ is classifier transport from the name row, schedule row,
representation relation, and target row to a common finite comparison surface.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $S,W,\rho,X,T,C,P,N$.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a precision request through the
finite schedule, representation relation, target row, and classifier transport.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$,
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and the local
NameCert row.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RepresentedSpaceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only by transporting these displayed
rows to a common finite surface. It has no coordinate for quotient names,
selected representatives, host real equality, an ambient completeness row, or a
choice principle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[RepresentedSpace NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:representedspace-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RepresentedSpaceUp$ carrier
$R=(S,W,\rho,X,T,H,C,P,N)$, the local
$\NameCert_{\RepresentedSpaceUp}$ surface consists exactly of five obligation
rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed name carrier, finite
$\StreamNameUp$ schedule, representation relation, target carrier, classifier
transport, continuation, provenance, and local name;
\item classifier transport to a common finite surface on which the name row,
schedule row, representation row, and target row can be compared without adding
host equality;
\item stability of the name, schedule, representation relation, target row, and
classifier transport under the componentwise $\hsame$ rows recorded in $H$;
\item exactness of the representation relation row $\rho$ as the only permitted
route from stream observations to the target carrier;
\item $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local NameCert rows for exactly the
displayed packet.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored outside those five rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:representedspace-carrier}. The projection
exposes the name carrier, finite stream schedule, representation relation,
target carrier, transport rows, continuation row, provenance row, and local
name.

Classifier transport is componentwise. The rows in $H$ move the name, schedule,
representation relation, target row, and transport row to the same finite
comparison surface, while $C$ replays only the displayed requests and windows.

Representation exactness is exhausted by $\rho$. A consumer can reach $X$ from
the stream observations only through the stored relation row, and the real-facing
case must then pass through the regular rational, dyadic, and real seal rows
already named in the carrier.

Finally attach $P,N$. These rows source and name the displayed packet; they add
no quotient name, selected representative, host real equality, completeness row,
or choice principle. Hence the five listed rows exhaust the local naming
obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RepresentedSpace StreamName Real handoff]
\label{thm:representedspace-streamname-real-handoff}
Every $\RealUp$-facing represented-space read through an accepted
$\RepresentedSpaceUp$ carrier factors through
$$
\StreamNameUp,\ \RegSeqRatUp,\ \DyadicRatCoreUp,\ \RealUp,\ \BHist,\ \hsame,\ \Cont,\ \Pkg,\ \NameCert .
$$
Concretely, a consumer first reads the finite $\StreamNameUp$ schedule $W$, then
the regular rational readback and dyadic tolerance rows attached to the target
carrier $X$, then the terminal $\RealUp$ seal, and only then consumes the local
$\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows. The route contains no
quotient name, selected representative, host real equality, completeness row, or
choice principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a real-facing read of the target row $X$. Carrier admission exposes
the finite schedule $W$ before the representation relation $\rho$ can be
consumed, so the first visible route is the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}.

Next read the analytic handoff rows. The target carrier is real-facing only by
passing through the $\RegSeqRatUp$ surface of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$
tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and
the terminal $\RealUp$ seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}.

Finally replay $H,C,P,N$. These rows keep the handoff inside the local
$\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ data. Since the carrier has
no coordinate for quotient names, selected representatives, host real equality,
completeness, or choice, no such route can be read by a consumer.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RepresentedSpace schedule representation exactness]
\label{thm:representedspace-schedule-representation-exactness}
Every target read from an accepted $\RepresentedSpaceUp$ carrier $R=(S,W,\rho,X,T,H,C,P,N)$ must expose the finite $\StreamNameUp$ schedule $W$ before the representation relation row $\rho$ can be used. The representation relation is therefore exact only as a displayed route from the inspected stream observations to the target row $X$, with $\Cont$ replay and $\Pkg$ provenance preserving that schedule.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:representedspace-carrier}. The representation relation $\rho$ is stored after the name carrier $S$ and the finite schedule $W$, so no target read can consume $\rho$ without first displaying the inspected stream positions and precision requests.

Replay through $C$ preserves this order. The continuation row starts from the precision request, exposes the schedule, and only then reaches the representation relation and target row.

Finally attach $P$ and $N$. Provenance and local naming source the same scheduled route, and the carrier has no coordinate for an unscheduled representation oracle.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RepresentedSpace classifier transport]
\label{thm:representedspace-classifier-transport}
Accepted $\RepresentedSpaceUp$ carrier sameness is transported componentwise through $S,W,\rho,X,T,H,C,P,N$. The transport compares the name carrier, schedule, representation relation, target row, classifier transport, structural replay, provenance, and local naming rows; it never appeals to host equality of names, target objects, real representatives, or quotient classes.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the classifier row $T$ together with the transport data $H$. These rows compare accepted packets only after their displayed components have been moved to a common finite surface.

The representation and target rows move with the same schedule. Thus $\rho$ and $X$ are compared only through the corresponding scheduled carrier rows, not by host equality.

The structural rows $C,P,N$ then replay, source, and name the transported packet. Since no host equality, quotient class, or selected representative is present in the carrier, transport is exhausted by the listed components.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RepresentedSpace target seal nonescape]
\label{thm:representedspace-target-seal-nonescape}
Every $\RealUp$-facing target read from an accepted $\RepresentedSpaceUp$ packet must pass through $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows. The read cannot bypass the regular rational readback, dyadic tolerance ledger, terminal Real seal, componentwise transport, replay, provenance, or local naming evidence.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the target carrier $X$. In the real-facing case, the carrier definition routes $X$ through the regular rational readback surface, the dyadic tolerance ledger, and the terminal Real seal.

Apply the schedule exactness theorem. The target read has already exposed the finite StreamName schedule before $\rho$ is consumed, so the analytic rows are downstream of a displayed name schedule rather than an ambient target object.

Finally replay the structural rows $H,C,P,N$. They keep the read inside $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ data. Coordinate exhaustion blocks any route that skips RegSeqRat, DyadicRatCore, the Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, or local naming.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RepresentedSpace admissible name coverage]
\label{thm:representedspace-admissible-name-coverage}
Every admissible $\RepresentedSpaceUp$ name accepted by the displayed representation row is covered by the finite route
$$
S,W,\rho,X,T,H,C,P,N.
$$
In particular, the read starts with the $\StreamNameUp$ finite windows in $W$, passes through the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback and $\RealUp$ seal exposed by the target row $X$, transports classifier data by the componentwise $\hsame$ rows in $H$, replays the requested observations by $C$, and ends in the $\Pkg$ provenance and local $\NameCert$ row $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:representedspace-carrier}. The admissible name is not a free stream; it is read through the displayed name carrier $S$ and finite schedule $W$, so the only inspected observations are the $\StreamNameUp$ windows stored in the packet.

Use the representation relation row $\rho$ next. It is the sole route from those stream observations to the target row $X$, and the real-facing case of $X$ exposes the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback and terminal $\RealUp$ seal before any consumer receives a target value.

Finally transport and replay the route. The componentwise $\hsame$ rows in $H$ keep $S,W,\rho,X,T,C,P,N$ on the same finite comparison surface, while $C$ replays the inspected windows and $P,N$ source the resulting NameCert row. Thus every admissible name covered by the representation row is covered by the displayed finite route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RepresentedSpace completion-boundary refusal]
\label{thm:representedspace-completion-boundary-refusal}
An accepted $\RepresentedSpaceUp$ packet does not by itself certify a completion boundary, a quotient completion, or a host-real representative. Any completion-facing consumer must first display the representation row, the $\StreamNameUp$ finite windows, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback path, the $\RealUp$ seal, the $\hsame$ transport, $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and the same local $\NameCert$ row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier coordinates. They include a name carrier, finite windows, a representation row, a target row, classifier transport, structural transport, replay, provenance, and local naming, but no coordinate for a completed ambient space, quotient completion, or selected host-real representative.

The refusal is stable under the analytic handoff. A completion-facing read still has to pass through $\rho$, the finite $\StreamNameUp$ schedule, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback exposed by $X$, and the terminal $\RealUp$ seal; none of those rows adds a completion object.

It remains to check transport and provenance. The $\hsame$ rows, $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ only preserve and source the displayed route. Since the packet never stores an additional completion boundary, the represented-space certificate refuses to export one.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RepresentedSpace realization transport]
\label{thm:representedspace-realization-transport}
If two accepted $\RepresentedSpaceUp$ packets compare by the displayed classifier, then every realized target read transported from the first packet to the second is transported through the same finite representation route: $\StreamNameUp$ windows, $\rho$, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, $\RealUp$ seal, $\hsame$ comparison, $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ naming.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the classifier transport theorem. Equality of accepted packets is not host equality of names or targets; it is the componentwise $\hsame$ comparison of $S,W,\rho,X,T,H,C,P,N$ on a common finite surface.

Transport the realized read along those rows. The finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows move with $W$, the representation relation moves with $\rho$, and the target read moves with $X$, where the real-facing route still passes through the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback and $\RealUp$ seal.

Finally replay the structural evidence on the second packet. The $\Cont$ row reproduces the same inspected windows, while $\Pkg$ provenance and the local $\NameCert$ row source the transported certificate. No unrecorded realization map, quotient equality, or host representative is introduced by the transport.
\end{proof}
