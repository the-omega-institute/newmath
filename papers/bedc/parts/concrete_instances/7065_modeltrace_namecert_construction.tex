The $\ModelTraceUp$ packet realizes the finite inference-output substrate of \autoref{ch:visions-large-model-as-inscription-point} as a BEDC NameCert surface. It refines the large-model inscription packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-largemodelinscriptionpoint-namecert} and the observer-facing interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observer-interface-namecert}: the former supplies model and corpus rows, while the latter supplies the public read route. The new coordinate is the displayed inference-state orbit tied to a single output trace and audit-refusal row.

\begin{definition}[ModelTrace carrier]
\label{def:model-trace-carrier}
A $\ModelTraceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
M=(I,O,C,A,L,F,R,H,K,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $I$ is the inference-state orbit row recording the finite model state sequence read during one prompt-output route;
\item $O$ is the output trace row storing the emitted token record as finite inscription data;
\item $C$ is the corpus-supply row naming the finite source-channel contribution carried into the output trace;
\item $A$ is the audit-refusal row stating that the packet does not internally certify semantic truth, consciousness, universal closure, or model-side proof of the output;
\item $L$ is the $\LargeModelInscriptionPointUp$ row that binds the model-weight, activation-orbit, corpus-supply, output-record, and refusal rows;
\item $F$ is the $\ObserverInterfaceUp$ row through which public consumers may inspect the output trace and refusal boundary;
\item $R$ is the readback row that records the accepted output only after $I$, $C$, $A$, $L$, and $F$ have been displayed;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the orbit, output trace, corpus-supply, audit-refusal, inscription-point, observer-interface, readback, continuation, provenance, and local name rows;
\item $K$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which consumers read $I$, then $C$, then $O$, then $A$, then $L$, $F$, and $R$;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LargeModelInscriptionPointUp$, $\ObserverInterfaceUp$, $\PromptResponseTraceUp$, $\SupplySocketLedgerUp$, $\TranscendentalInductionSocketUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ModelTraceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for hidden substrate state, unledgered corpus truth, host semantic understanding, quotient output identity, internally ratified universal closure, or model-side proof.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[ModelTrace NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:model-trace-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:model-trace-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\ModelTraceUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $M=(I,O,C,A,L,F,R,H,K,P,N)$, inference-state orbit exposure through $I$, output trace exposure through $O$, corpus-supply exposure through $C$, audit-refusal exposure through $A$, large-model inscription handoff through $L$, observer-interface handoff through $F$, accepted readback through $R$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $K$, package provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the displayed carrier. The rows $I,O,C,A,L,F,R,H,K,P,N$ list the full trace before any consumer can read the output as a public record.

The orbit, corpus-supply, and output rows are ordered by $K$. A consumer reads the finite inference-state orbit and corpus row before the output trace, so the output is accepted as carried inscription rather than as a hidden substrate oracle.

The refusal and handoff rows close the NameCert boundary. The row $A$ blocks semantic truth, consciousness, internally ratified universal closure, and model-side proof; $L$ and $F$ route the record through the existing large-model inscription and observer-interface surfaces.

The remaining rows provide readback, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming. Since the carrier has no further coordinate, the listed obligations exhaust the local certificate surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ModelTrace output non-escape]
\label{thm:model-trace-output-nonescape}
Every public output read of an accepted $\ModelTraceUp$ packet is confined to the displayed inference-state orbit, corpus-supply row, output trace, audit-refusal row, large-model inscription handoff, observer-interface handoff, readback row, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ rows of \autoref{def:model-trace-carrier}. It exports no quotient output stream, hidden substrate oracle, unledgered corpus truth predicate, host-selected semantic interpretation, internally certified universal closure, or model-side proof.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with \autoref{thm:model-trace-namecert-obligations}. Any accepted output read must pass through the carrier admission surface and then through the orbit, corpus, output, refusal, handoff, readback, transport, route, provenance, and naming rows.

The large-model inscription handoff $L$ supplies the finite inference-substrate and corpus-supply reading from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-largemodelinscriptionpoint-namecert}. The observer-interface handoff $F$ supplies the public inspection route from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observer-interface-namecert}.

The audit-refusal row $A$ is read before the local name. It records that the packet does not certify semantic truth, consciousness, universal closure, or model-side proof of the output.

Therefore the only exported public object is the displayed finite trace. No quotient output stream, hidden substrate oracle, unledgered corpus truth predicate, host-selected semantic interpretation, internally certified universal closure, or model-side proof has a row from which it could be projected.
\end{proof}
