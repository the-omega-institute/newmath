The carrier sits in the Phase Real and Completion frontier between the regular rational names of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the stream schedules of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledgers of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the finite real seal boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, and the selector discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-monotone-subsequence-namecert}.

\begin{definition}[SpeckerSequence carrier]
\label{def:speckersequence-carrier}
A $\SpeckerSequenceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(R,W,D,M,B,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ source row naming the computable rational stream by regular finite windows.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule that exposes the inspected windows of $R$.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger carrying the rational bounds and tolerances read from those windows.
\item $M$ is the finite monotone ledger, recording that each displayed inspected value is below the later displayed values in the schedule.
\item $B$ is the boundedness ledger, recording a displayed lower rational row and upper rational row for every inspected window.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row available only as a finite readback boundary for displayed regular windows, not as a located supremum.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the source, schedule, dyadic, monotone, boundedness, seal, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ stores displayed $\Cont$ replay from $R,W$ into $D$, from $D$ into $M$ and $B$, and from the regular readback surface into $E$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\SpeckerSequenceUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\MonotoneSubsequenceUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\SpeckerSequenceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through these rows. It has no coordinate for located supremum data, Dedekind cuts, countable choice, trichotomy, ambient completeness, or a selected limiting real.
\end{definition}

The route is finite at every stage. The stream is read through $R$ and $W$, rational bounds are read through $D$, monotonicity and boundedness are read through $M$ and $B$, and the seal row $E$ is reached only after those rows have been displayed.

Since the carrier lists no located supremum, Dedekind cut, selected limit, countable-choice selector, trichotomy branch, or completeness row, those objects are not part of the exposed packet.

\begin{theorem}[SpeckerSequence monotone bounded ledger]
\label{thm:speckersequence-monotone-bounded-ledger}
In every accepted $\SpeckerSequenceUp$ packet, the monotone ledger $M$ and boundedness ledger $B$ are read before any $\RealUp$ seal consumer can inspect $E$. Thus every seal-facing route factors through
$$
R,W\longmapsto D\longmapsto (M,B)\longmapsto E
$$
together with $H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the source route. The $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $R$ and $\StreamNameUp$ schedule $W$ select the finite rational observations inspected by the packet.

Next read the dyadic ledger. The row $D$ supplies the rational bounds and tolerance data used to compare the selected observations.

Now read the monotone and boundedness ledgers. The row $M$ records the displayed monotone comparisons, while $B$ records the displayed lower and upper rational bounds for the same inspected windows.

Only after those rows have been exposed can the finite $\RealUp$ seal row $E$ be consumed. The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve this route and do not add a selected supremum or an ambient completeness witness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SpeckerSequence finite-stage induction]
\label{thm:speckersequence-finite-stage-induction}
For every accepted $\SpeckerSequenceUp$ packet $S=(R,W,D,M,B,E,H,C,P,N)$, induction along the displayed finite $\StreamNameUp$ schedule $W$ lets any consumer read only the current finite rational window of $R$, the corresponding dyadic tolerance row of $D$, the monotone ledger prefix of $M$, the boundedness ledger prefix of $B$, and the transported $\BHist$ provenance before the consumer reaches the $\RealUp$ seal row $E$. The induction route introduces no located supremum, selected limit, countable choice, trichotomy, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Induct on the finite schedule prefix displayed by $W$. In the base case, the consumer sees only the initial inspected window of $R$, the matching tolerance and bound row in $D$, the empty or initial monotone and boundedness ledger entries in $M$ and $B$, and the provenance rows carried by $H,C,P,N$.

For the step case, append one visible rational observation and its tolerance row. Componentwise $\hsame$ in $H$ transports the source, schedule, dyadic, monotone, boundedness, and seal coordinates without changing their row types. The displayed $\Cont$ replay in $C$ appends the new dyadic observation before replaying the corresponding monotone comparison in $M$ and the corresponding lower and upper bound in $B$.

Thus each prefix consumer receives exactly the finite window and ledger data already displayed at that stage. Passing from one prefix to the next preserves the route of \autoref{thm:speckersequence-monotone-bounded-ledger}; the $\RealUp$ seal remains downstream of the visible rational, tolerance, monotone, boundedness, and provenance rows. Since no induction clause contains a coordinate for located supremum data, selected limits, countable choice, trichotomy, or ambient completeness, none is introduced before the seal.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SpeckerSequence non-located supremum boundary]
\label{thm:speckersequence-nonlocated-supremum-boundary}
An accepted $\SpeckerSequenceUp$ packet may certify a monotone bounded computable rational stream, but it does not certify a located supremum. Any downstream route that treats the packet as a located $\RealUp$ datum must supply an external location witness not contained in $R,W,D,M,B,E,H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use \autoref{thm:speckersequence-carrier-exposure} to identify the packet inventory. The available analytic rows are the regular rational source, stream schedule, dyadic observations, monotone ledger, boundedness ledger, and finite seal boundary.

Use \autoref{thm:speckersequence-monotone-bounded-ledger} for the seal route. It records that $E$ is reached through finite windows and ledgers, not through a located-supremum construction.

A located supremum requires a location witness comparing rational cuts around the supremum. No such witness is a row of the carrier, and the structural rows merely transport, replay, source, and name the displayed packet. Hence the packet sets the non-located boundary rather than closing located completeness.
\end{proof}

Finally apply \autoref{thm:speckersequence-realup-seal-nonescape}. The finite $\RealUp$ seal exports only the displayed finite-window read.
