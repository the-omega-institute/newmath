Following Bishop and Bridges, located continuity is recorded as finite precision-response data over located real names, not as a host function on completed reals. The $\LocatedRealContinuityUp$ packet sits over the located-real carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedreal-namecert}, the dyadic precision ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the finite stream-window surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, and the located modulus surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-located-real-modulus-namecert}. It gives the L10 Real and Completion route a named continuity consumer whose public data are precision requests and finite responses rather than quotient-real functions, trichotomy, countable choice, or ambient completeness.

\begin{definition}[Located real continuity carrier]
\label{def:located-real-continuity-carrier}
A $\LocatedRealContinuityUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(X,D,W,R,M,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the located input row, read through the $\LocatedRealUp$ surface.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ precision ledger for requested input and output tolerances.
\item $W$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window used to inspect the located input at the requested tolerance.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row that repacks the inspected window as regular rational data.
\item $M$ is the modulus-response row, returning a finite precision response for the displayed input tolerance.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row for the output name, visible only after $X,D,W,R,M$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the located input, dyadic ledger, stream window, regular readback, modulus response, real seal, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a located precision request through $X,D,W,R,M,E,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LocatedRealContinuityUp$, $\LocatedRealUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\LocatedRealModulusUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedRealContinuityUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a host real function, quotient-real equality test, trichotomy oracle, countable-choice schedule, selected representative, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness principle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Located real continuity modulus stability]
\label{thm:located-real-continuity-modulus-stability}
Let $K=(X,D,W,R,M,E,H,C,P,N)$ and $K'=(X',D',W',R',M',E',H',C',P',N')$ be accepted $\LocatedRealContinuityUp$ carriers. If the located input rows, dyadic tolerance ledgers, finite windows, and regular-rational readbacks agree componentwise under $\hsame$, then the modulus-response rows $M$ and $M'$ answer the same displayed dyadic tolerance requests, and every $\RealUp$ seal consumer reaches the same response route
$$
X,D,W,R \longmapsto M \longmapsto E \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
Thus modulus-response stability is a finite packet property: it is preserved by $\hsame$ transport and by the displayed dyadic tolerance rows, without reading quotient-real equality, trichotomy, countable choice, selected representatives, host function extensionality, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the two accepted carriers from \autoref{def:located-real-continuity-carrier}. The only precision source rows are $X,D,W,R$ and their transported counterparts.

Use the assumed componentwise $\hsame$ agreement on those rows. The dyadic ledger $D$ fixes the tolerance request, the window $W$ fixes the finite observations, and $R$ fixes the regular-rational readback. Therefore the accepted modulus rows $M$ and $M'$ respond to the same displayed precision request.

The seal row is downstream of the response row. Since $E$ and $E'$ can be consumed only after the displayed route through the input, tolerance, window, readback, and response rows, any $\RealUp$ consumer reads the same finite route before seeing a real-name seal.

The excluded analytic principles have no carrier coordinate in either packet. They cannot alter the transported modulus response or add a second continuity channel.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Located real continuity NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:located-real-continuity-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\LocatedRealContinuityUp$ carrier $K=(X,D,W,R,M,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedRealContinuityUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission, classifier stability for the located input and precision-response rows, componentwise transport, the continuity-modulus ledger, terminal real-seal routing, package provenance, and local naming. The obligation surface does not include quotient reals, trichotomy, countable choice, selected representatives, host function extensionality, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:located-real-continuity-carrier}. The accepted packet displays exactly the located input, dyadic tolerance ledger, finite window, regular readback, modulus response, real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

Classifier stability is supplied by the same inventory. An accepted continuity read must keep the rows $X,D,W,R,M$ attached to one displayed precision route, so changing any of these rows changes the carrier rather than producing a hidden function on completed reals.

Transport and replay are the rows $H$ and $C$. They preserve the displayed route through the dyadic tolerance and modulus-response ledger before any terminal $\RealUp$ seal can be read.

Finally $P$ and $N$ source and name the same finite packet. Since quotient reals, trichotomy, countable choice, selected representatives, host function extensionality, and ambient completeness have no carrier rows, they are not local NameCert obligations for $\LocatedRealContinuityUp$.
\end{proof}
