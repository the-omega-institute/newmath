\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\GaloisExtUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-galoisext-namecert}

The Galois-extension interface $\GaloisExtUp$ packages a normal and separable field extension. It depends on $\NameCert_{\FieldExtUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-fieldext-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\SeparableExtUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separableext-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:galoisext-carrier}

\begin{definition}[GaloisExtUp BHist source packet]
\label{def:galoisext-bhist-source-packet}
A $\GaloisExtUp$ source packet is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ tuple
$(F,S,n,s,c,\rho,\lambda)$. The row $F$ is the consumed
$\NameCert_{\FieldExtUp}$ dependency packet, and the row $S$ is the consumed
$\NameCert_{\SeparableExtUp}$ dependency packet over the same displayed field
extension carrier. The row $n$ is the normality and root-splitting row: each
conjugate endpoint and each displayed root-splitting endpoint is a
$\mathsf{BHist}$ endpoint read through the carrier, classifier, exactness, and
ledger rows of $F$. The row $s$ is the separability row: it is the
minimal-polynomial simple-root row of $S$ after transport into the carrier
displayed by $F$.

The row $c$ is the classifier row binding the FieldExtUp and SeparableExtUp
dependency packets. It records that their endpoint comparisons are read by
$\hsame$ on the same finite history endpoints, with no host-field equality and
no quotient endpoint. The row $\rho$ is the $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance showing
which dependency packet supplies each displayed face. The finite ledger
$\lambda$ is the $\mathsf{Cont}$ list containing exactly the FieldExtUp
dependency row, the SeparableExtUp dependency row, the normality row, the
separability row, the classifier row, the stability rows, and the exactness
rows consumed by the public $\NameCert_{\GaloisExtUp}$ interface.
\end{definition}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:galoisext-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[GaloisExtUp normality obligation row]
\label{thm:galoisext-normality-obligation-row}
For a source packet $(F,S,n,s,c,\rho,\lambda)$ of
\autoref{def:galoisext-bhist-source-packet}, the normality face of
$\NameCert_{\GaloisExtUp}$ is accepted exactly when every displayed conjugate
witness and every displayed root-splitting witness in $n$ is read from the
$\NameCert_{\FieldExtUp}$ packet $F$ through finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ endpoints,
$\mathsf{Cont}$ ledger rows, $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance rows, and the classifier
rows retained by $F$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First read the normality row $n$ from the source packet. By
\autoref{def:galoisext-bhist-source-packet}, its endpoints are not independent
field elements; they are displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ endpoints whose carrier,
classifier, exactness, and ledger provenance are supplied by $F$.

For the forward direction, acceptance of the normality face exposes exactly the
conjugate and root-splitting entries carried by $n$. Project each entry to its
$\mathsf{Cont}$ row and then to its $\mathsf{Pkg}$ source. The definition of
the packet sends those projections back to the FieldExtUp dependency row, so
the accepted face contains only rows read from $F$.

For the reverse direction, assume every displayed conjugate and root-splitting
witness is read from $F$ with the stated history, ledger, and provenance
support. Repack those rows as the tuple entry $n$, attach the classifier row
$c$, and use the same finite ledger $\lambda$. This constructs the normality
face required by $\NameCert_{\GaloisExtUp}$ without introducing any endpoint
outside the FieldExtUp packet.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.GaloisExtUp.GaloisExtSourcePacket\_normality\_obligation\_row}

\begin{theorem}[GaloisExtUp separability obligation row]
\label{thm:galoisext-separability-obligation-row}
For a source packet $(F,S,n,s,c,\rho,\lambda)$ of
\autoref{def:galoisext-bhist-source-packet}, the separability face of
$\NameCert_{\GaloisExtUp}$ is precisely the minimal-polynomial simple-root row
of the consumed $\NameCert_{\SeparableExtUp}$ packet $S$, transported by the
classifier row $c$ into the same FieldExtUp carrier displayed by $F$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the row $s$ from the source packet. The definition states that $s$ is the
simple-root row of $S$ after it has been placed over the carrier displayed by
$F$.

The classifier row $c$ supplies the needed transport. It compares the
FieldExtUp and SeparableExtUp endpoints by $\hsame$ on the common
$\mathsf{BHist}$ source, so the minimal-polynomial endpoint, the root endpoint,
and the simple-root witness remain the same finite history rows after
transport.

Thus the separability face has no independent certificate data. Projecting it
recovers the simple-root row of $S$, and repacking that row with $c$, $\rho$,
and $\lambda$ gives the separability face consumed by
$\NameCert_{\GaloisExtUp}$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GaloisExtUp classifier transport row]
\label{thm:galoisext-classifier-transport-row}
Let $(F,S,n,s,c,\rho,\lambda)$ and
$(F',S',n',s',c',\rho',\lambda')$ be two $\GaloisExtUp$ source packets. If the
FieldExtUp dependency rows of $F$ and $F'$ are transported by $\hsame$, and the
SeparableExtUp dependency rows of $S$ and $S'$ are transported by $\hsame$ over
the same finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ endpoints, then the normality and separability
faces are transported from $(n,s)$ to $(n',s')$ by the classifier rows $c$ and
$c'$ without host field equality or quotienting.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the FieldExtUp transport. The hypothesis identifies the carrier,
classifier, exactness, and ledger endpoints of $F$ and $F'$ by $\hsame$.
Therefore every conjugate and root-splitting witness read from $F$ has the
corresponding displayed endpoint in $F'$.

Next use the SeparableExtUp transport. It identifies the minimal-polynomial
simple-root rows of $S$ and $S'$ over the same history endpoints. The
classifier rows $c$ and $c'$ place those rows in the FieldExtUp carriers
already matched in the previous paragraph.

Combining the two transports sends $n$ to $n'$ and $s$ to $s'$. Since all
comparisons are $\hsame$ comparisons on finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ endpoints, the
argument never requires equality of ambient host fields, quotient
representatives, or an external field carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GaloisExtUp normal separable stability]
\label{thm:galoisext-normal-separable-stability}
Every $\NameCert_{\GaloisExtUp}$ source packet of
\autoref{def:galoisext-bhist-source-packet} is stable under replacing its
displayed rows by $\hsame$-classified $\mathsf{BHist}$ rows through the same
$\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance. The stable rows are exactly the
$\NameCert_{\FieldExtUp}$ field-extension dependency, the
$\NameCert_{\SeparableExtUp}$ separability dependency, the finite normality
and root-splitting row, and the finite $\mathsf{Cont}$ ledgers belonging to the
enclosing $\NameCert$ packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply the classifier transport row of
\autoref{thm:galoisext-classifier-transport-row} to the transported FieldExtUp
and SeparableExtUp dependency rows. This handles the carrier, classifier,
exactness, and ledger endpoints through the inherited classifiers of the two
dependency packets.

For the normality component, transport every conjugate and root-splitting row
along the displayed $\hsame$ comparisons and retain the finite $\mathsf{Cont}$
ledger that reads those rows from the field-extension source $F$.

For the separability component, use the displayed
$\NameCert_{\SeparableExtUp}$ dependency and its simple-root row. The shared
$\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance row $\rho$ is carried through the repacking, so the
transported packet has the same certificate surface as the original packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GaloisExtUp public obligation boundary]
\label{thm:galoisext-public-obligation-boundary}
For a source packet $(F,S,n,s,c,\rho,\lambda)$ of
\autoref{def:galoisext-bhist-source-packet}, the public
$\NameCert_{\GaloisExtUp}$ obligation surface is exactly the following finite
row family: the carrier row from $F$, the normality row $n$, the separability
row $s$, the classifier row $c$, the stability rows inherited from the
FieldExtUp and SeparableExtUp packets, the finite $\mathsf{Cont}$ ledger
$\lambda$, and the exactness rows named by those packets. This is the complete
surface consumed by $\GaloisGroupUp$ through
$\NameCert_{\GaloisExtUp}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the packet to its dependency rows. The FieldExtUp packet supplies the
carrier, field-extension classifier, stability, exactness, and ledger rows.
The SeparableExtUp packet supplies the minimal-polynomial simple-root row, and
\autoref{thm:galoisext-separability-obligation-row} identifies that row with
the separability face of the GaloisExtUp surface.

The normality face is supplied by
\autoref{thm:galoisext-normality-obligation-row}. The classifier compatibility
needed to place both faces over one carrier is supplied by
\autoref{thm:galoisext-classifier-transport-row}. Thus every public row is a
projection of $F$, $S$, $n$, $s$, $c$, $\rho$, or $\lambda$.

Conversely, each listed row is part of the source packet and is named by the
public GaloisExtUp interface: carrier, normality, separability, classifier,
stability, ledger, and exactness. Repacking these rows gives exactly the
$\NameCert_{\GaloisExtUp}$ surface available to the downstream
$\GaloisGroupUp$ carrier, so no additional obligation row remains hidden.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GaloisExtUp dependency exactness ledger]
\label{thm:galoisext-dependency-exactness-ledger}
For the source packet $(F,S,n,s,c,\rho,\lambda)$ of
\autoref{def:galoisext-bhist-source-packet}, the dependency ledger for
$\NameCert_{\GaloisExtUp}$ has coverage, soundness, and completeness for
exactly these displayed rows: the carrier, classifier, stability, exactness,
and ledger rows from $\NameCert_{\FieldExtUp}$; the simple-root row from
$\NameCert_{\SeparableExtUp}$; the finite normality and root-splitting row;
the $\hsame$ classifier rows; the finite $\mathsf{Cont}$ readback rows; and
the shared $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance row. No other $\NameCert$ row is exported
by the $\GaloisExtUp$ packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Coverage follows by projecting the tuple in
\autoref{def:galoisext-bhist-source-packet}. Each field listed in the ledger
is one of the packet entries or one of the dependency rows displayed by
$\NameCert_{\FieldExtUp}$ or $\NameCert_{\SeparableExtUp}$.

Soundness follows from the preceding rows. The normality projection is
\autoref{thm:galoisext-normality-obligation-row}, the separability consumption
is \autoref{thm:galoisext-separability-obligation-row}, and the classifier and
stability steps are \autoref{thm:galoisext-classifier-transport-row} and
\autoref{thm:galoisext-normal-separable-stability}. Each step uses only
$\mathsf{BHist}$ projection, $\hsame$ comparison, finite $\mathsf{Cont}$
readback, and the recorded $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance.

Completeness follows because a downstream consumer can project only those rows
from the packet. The tuple contains no separate host field equality, ambient
algebraic-closure object, or hidden separability witness. Therefore every
accepted $\GaloisExtUp$ dependency read factors through exactly the displayed
ledger rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[GaloisExtUp automorphism consumer surface]
\label{def:galoisext-automorphism-consumer-surface}
The $\GaloisExtUp$ automorphism consumer surface exported to
$\GaloisGroupUp$ is the finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ row family read from a source
packet $(F,S,n,s,c,\rho,\lambda)$ of
\autoref{def:galoisext-bhist-source-packet}. It consists of the FieldExtUp
carrier and classifier rows, the SeparableExtUp simple-root rows transported
into that carrier, the normality and root-splitting rows $n$, the base-endpoint
fixed rows obtained from the FieldExtUp base endpoints by $\hsame$ comparison,
the automorphism-action rows whose source and target extension endpoints and
transported root-splitting endpoints are read through the same packet, the
finite $\mathsf{Cont}$ ledgers that read those rows, and the shared
$\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance row $\rho$.

No row of this surface is an ambient automorphism object. Each row is either a
dependency projection from $F$ or $S$, a normality or separability face of the
GaloisExtUp packet, an $\hsame$ comparison between displayed endpoints, a
finite $\mathsf{Cont}$ readback row, or the package provenance recording that
source.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[GaloisExtUp fixed-base automorphism source]
\label{thm:galoisext-fixed-base-automorphism-source}
For a source packet $(F,S,n,s,c,\rho,\lambda)$ of
\autoref{def:galoisext-bhist-source-packet}, every fixed-base automorphism row
accepted by
\autoref{def:galoisext-automorphism-consumer-surface} is projected only from
the FieldExtUp base endpoints of $F$ together with the $\hsame$ comparisons
recorded in the finite GaloisExtUp ledger. Conversely, any such finite family
of FieldExtUp base endpoints and $\hsame$ comparisons repacks as a fixed-base
row of the automorphism consumer surface, with the same $\mathsf{Pkg}$
provenance and no host-field equality clause.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the source packet through
\autoref{def:galoisext-bhist-source-packet}. The FieldExtUp row $F$ supplies
the displayed extension carrier, its base endpoints, classifier, exactness
rows, and finite ledger. The classifier transport row of
\autoref{thm:galoisext-classifier-transport-row} keeps those endpoints inside
the same finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ comparison system.

For the forward direction, read an accepted fixed-base row from
\autoref{def:galoisext-automorphism-consumer-surface}. Its base endpoint is
one of the endpoints exposed by $F$, and its fixedness assertion is an
$\hsame$ comparison in the GaloisExtUp ledger $\lambda$. The dependency
exactness ledger of \autoref{thm:galoisext-dependency-exactness-ledger}
excludes any further $\NameCert$ row, so the projection has no additional
source.

For the reverse direction, start with FieldExtUp base endpoints and the
displayed $\hsame$ comparisons. Repack them as the base-endpoint fixed rows of
the consumer surface, attach the same $\mathsf{Cont}$ readback rows and
$\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance $\rho$, and use the public obligation boundary of
\autoref{thm:galoisext-public-obligation-boundary} to place the result inside
the exported GaloisExtUp surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GaloisExtUp automorphism action source]
\label{thm:galoisext-automorphism-action-source}
For a source packet $(F,S,n,s,c,\rho,\lambda)$ of
\autoref{def:galoisext-bhist-source-packet}, an automorphism-action row is
accepted by
\autoref{def:galoisext-automorphism-consumer-surface} only when its source
extension endpoint, target extension endpoint, and transported root-splitting
endpoints are all read through the same GaloisExtUp packet. The action row is
therefore a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ row over $F$, $S$, $n$, $s$, $c$, $\rho$,
and $\lambda$, not a row imported from an ambient algebraic-closure carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with an accepted action row. Its source and target endpoints are
extension endpoints of the FieldExtUp carrier displayed by $F$. Its
root-splitting endpoints are the normality endpoints of $n$, and
\autoref{thm:galoisext-normality-obligation-row} reads those endpoints from
$F$ by finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ projections.

The separability face contributes only the transported simple-root rows of
$S$. By \autoref{thm:galoisext-separability-obligation-row}, these rows enter
the action surface after transport by $c$ into the FieldExtUp carrier. Thus
the action source, target, and transported splitting data all share the same
packet provenance.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:galoisext-dependency-exactness-ledger}. The ledger
has no row for an ambient algebraic-closure object or external automorphism
carrier. Repacking the displayed source endpoint, target endpoint,
root-splitting endpoints, $\mathsf{Cont}$ rows, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance
therefore gives exactly the accepted automorphism-action row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GaloisExtUp GaloisGroupUp consumer automorphism surface]
\label{thm:galoisext-galoisgroup-consumer-automorphism-surface}
For every source packet $(F,S,n,s,c,\rho,\lambda)$ of
\autoref{def:galoisext-bhist-source-packet}, the surface of
\autoref{def:galoisext-automorphism-consumer-surface} is exactly the
$\GaloisExtUp$ consumer surface available to $\GaloisGroupUp$. It exposes the
FieldExtUp carrier and classifier rows, SeparableExtUp simple-root rows,
normality and root-splitting rows, fixed-base endpoint rows,
automorphism-action rows, finite $\mathsf{Cont}$ ledgers, and
$\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance. It exposes no host automorphism equality, no
quotient of automorphisms, and no ambient algebraic-closure object.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First project the public GaloisExtUp packet. The public obligation boundary of
\autoref{thm:galoisext-public-obligation-boundary} gives the extension
carrier, classifier, normality, separability, stability, ledger, exactness,
and provenance rows. The dependency exactness ledger of
\autoref{thm:galoisext-dependency-exactness-ledger} says these are all the
dependency rows exported by the packet.

Next add the automorphism-facing rows. Fixed-base rows are exactly the
projections described in
\autoref{thm:galoisext-fixed-base-automorphism-source}; automorphism-action
rows are exactly the projections described in
\autoref{thm:galoisext-automorphism-action-source}. Both constructions use
only $\mathsf{BHist}$ endpoints, $\hsame$ comparisons, finite $\mathsf{Cont}$
readback, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance already present in the GaloisExtUp
packet.

Conversely, the GaloisGroupUp carrier definition consumes no more than these
rows from its GaloisExtUp dependency: its fixed-base and action clauses are
read through displayed endpoints and ledgers. Since the GaloisExtUp packet
contains no host automorphism equality, no automorphism quotient, and no
ambient algebraic-closure object, the listed row family is exactly the
consumer automorphism surface available downstream.
\end{proof}
