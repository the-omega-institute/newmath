\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\GaloisExtUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-galoisext-namecert}

The Galois-extension interface $\GaloisExtUp$ packages a normal and separable field extension. It depends on $\NameCert_{\FieldExtUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-fieldext-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\SeparableExtUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separableext-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:galoisext-carrier}

\begin{definition}[Normal separable Galois-extension carrier packet]
\label{def:galoisext-normal-separable-carrier}
A concrete $\GaloisExtUp$ carrier packet is a finite family of
$\mathsf{BHist}$ rows
$$
\mathcal{G}=(\mathcal{F},\mathcal{S},b,e,\iota,\nu,\sigma,\pi,\lambda,\rho)
$$
with the following displayed fields. The row $\mathcal{F}$ is the
$\NameCert$ dependency written as $\mathsf{NameCert\_FieldExtUp}$; it supplies
the base row $b:\mathsf{BHist}$, extension row $e:\mathsf{BHist}$, embedding
row $\iota:\mathsf{BHist}$, and the inherited $\FieldExtUp$ classifier rows.
The row $\mathcal{S}$ is the separability dependency written as
$\mathsf{NameCert\_SeparableExtUp}$ over the same field-extension source; it
supplies the simple-root row $\sigma:\mathsf{BHist}$.

The normality field $\nu:\mathsf{BHist}$ is not a host equality of fields. It
is a finite conjugate/root ledger: each displayed conjugate of a carried
algebraic element has a root row in the extension side, and the membership
comparison is recorded by $\hsame$ together with a finite $\Cont$ readback.
The row $\pi$ records that the normality row and the separability row use the
same $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance. The row $\lambda$ records the componentwise
classifier comparison between two such packets. Thus the carrier is exactly a
$\NameCert_{\GaloisExtUp}$ packet over $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$,
$\mathsf{Pkg}$, $\mathsf{NameCert\_FieldExtUp}$, and
$\mathsf{NameCert\_SeparableExtUp}$.
\end{definition}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:galoisext-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[GaloisExt normality row projection]
\label{thm:galoisext-normality-row}
For every carrier packet $\mathcal{G}$ of
\autoref{def:galoisext-normal-separable-carrier}, the
$\NameCert_{\GaloisExtUp}$ normality obligation projects to the displayed
$\mathsf{BHist}$ row $\nu$. This row is read by $\hsame$ and finite $\Cont$
from the same $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance as the
$\mathsf{NameCert\_FieldExtUp}$ dependency, and it records exactly the
finite conjugate/root rows carried by the packet. The projection uses the
$\NameCert$ source and the $\mathsf{NameCert\_SeparableExtUp}$ dependency only
as displayed rows; it does not identify the base and extension fields by host
field equality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the tuple of
\autoref{def:galoisext-normal-separable-carrier}. The normality component is
the row $\nu$, and its source data are the base row, extension row, embedding
row, finite conjugate rows, root rows, and provenance row already listed in
the carrier packet.

The $\hsame$ comparisons are part of the same displayed normality ledger.
Their finite $\Cont$ readback witnesses that each conjugate/root endpoint is
obtained from the field-extension source rows under the recorded
$\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance.

The separability dependency is merely retained as the row $\mathcal{S}$ in
the packet. Since the normality projection never replaces the two field
endpoints by host equality, the displayed normality obligation is exactly the
finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ row $\nu$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GaloisExt separability row consumption]
\label{thm:galoisext-separability-row}
For every carrier packet $\mathcal{G}$ of
\autoref{def:galoisext-normal-separable-carrier}, the
$\NameCert_{\GaloisExtUp}$ separability obligation is consumed from the
displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ row $\sigma$ supplied by
$\mathsf{NameCert\_SeparableExtUp}$. The consumption keeps the
$\mathsf{NameCert\_FieldExtUp}$ base and extension rows, compares endpoints
only by $\hsame$, reads the dependency through finite $\Cont$, and preserves
the same $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance inside the enclosing $\NameCert$ packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the separability dependency $\mathcal{S}$ from the carrier packet.
By definition, its visible contribution to $\GaloisExtUp$ is the simple-root
row $\sigma:\mathsf{BHist}$ over the displayed field-extension source.

The field-extension source is not recomputed. The base row, extension row,
and embedding row remain the ones supplied by
$\mathsf{NameCert\_FieldExtUp}$, and the finite $\Cont$ readback records how
the separability row is attached to those same rows.

The packet also carries $\pi$, the shared provenance row. Repacking
$\sigma$ with $\pi$ gives the separability part of the
$\NameCert_{\GaloisExtUp}$ certificate, with all endpoint comparison mediated
by $\hsame$ rather than by host equality.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GaloisExt fixed-base classifier]
\label{thm:galoisext-fixed-base-classifier}
Let $\mathcal{G}$ and $\mathcal{G}'$ be carrier packets of
\autoref{def:galoisext-normal-separable-carrier}. If their
$\mathsf{NameCert\_FieldExtUp}$ base rows are componentwise related by
$\hsame$, their normality rows and separability rows are transported by finite
$\Cont$ ledgers, and both packets retain the same $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance,
then the $\NameCert_{\GaloisExtUp}$ classifier relates the two
$\mathsf{BHist}$ packets. This fixed-base classification also keeps the
$\mathsf{NameCert\_SeparableExtUp}$ dependency as a displayed $\NameCert$ row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the $\mathsf{NameCert\_FieldExtUp}$ projection. The fixed-base
hypothesis supplies the componentwise $\hsame$ comparisons for the base row,
extension row, and embedding row, so the field-extension face of the two
packets is already classified over the same visible source.

Next transport the normality row. The finite $\Cont$ ledger carries the
conjugate/root rows from $\nu$ to the corresponding rows of $\nu'$ while
preserving the same $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance.

Finally transport the separability row through
$\mathsf{NameCert\_SeparableExtUp}$. Since the separability dependency is a
displayed row over the already classified field-extension source, repacking
the transported normality and separability rows gives the
$\NameCert_{\GaloisExtUp}$ classifier relation.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GaloisExt normal separable stability]
\label{thm:galoisext-normal-separable-stability}
Every $\NameCert_{\GaloisExtUp}$ carrier packet of
\autoref{def:galoisext-normal-separable-carrier} is stable under replacing
its displayed rows by $\hsame$-classified $\mathsf{BHist}$ rows through the
same $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance. The stable rows are exactly the
$\mathsf{NameCert\_FieldExtUp}$ field-extension dependency, the
$\mathsf{NameCert\_SeparableExtUp}$ separability dependency, the finite
normality conjugate/root row, and the finite $\Cont$ ledgers belonging to
the enclosing $\NameCert$ packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply the fixed-base classifier of
\autoref{thm:galoisext-fixed-base-classifier} to the transported field
extension rows. This handles the base, extension, and embedding components
through the inherited classifier of $\mathsf{NameCert\_FieldExtUp}$.

For the normality component, transport every conjugate/root row along the
displayed $\hsame$ comparisons and retain the finite $\Cont$ ledger that
reads those rows from the field-extension source.

For the separability component, use the displayed
$\mathsf{NameCert\_SeparableExtUp}$ dependency and its simple-root row. The
shared $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance row is copied through the repacking, so the
transported packet has the same certificate surface as the original packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GaloisExt dependency exactness ledger]
\label{thm:galoisext-dependency-exactness-ledger}
For the carrier packet of
\autoref{def:galoisext-normal-separable-carrier}, the dependency ledger for
$\NameCert_{\GaloisExtUp}$ has coverage, soundness, and completeness for
exactly these displayed rows: the $\mathsf{BHist}$ base, extension, and
embedding rows from $\mathsf{NameCert\_FieldExtUp}$; the
$\mathsf{BHist}$ simple-root row from $\mathsf{NameCert\_SeparableExtUp}$;
the finite conjugate/root normality row; the $\hsame$ classifier rows; the
finite $\Cont$ readback rows; and the shared $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance row.
No other $\NameCert$ row is exported by the $\GaloisExtUp$ packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Coverage follows by projecting the tuple in
\autoref{def:galoisext-normal-separable-carrier}. Each field listed in the
ledger is one of the carrier packet entries or one of the dependency rows
displayed by $\mathsf{NameCert\_FieldExtUp}$ or
$\mathsf{NameCert\_SeparableExtUp}$.

Soundness follows from the preceding rows. The normality projection is
\autoref{thm:galoisext-normality-row}, the separability consumption is
\autoref{thm:galoisext-separability-row}, and the classifier and stability
steps are \autoref{thm:galoisext-fixed-base-classifier} and
\autoref{thm:galoisext-normal-separable-stability}. Each step uses only
$\mathsf{BHist}$ projection, $\hsame$ comparison, finite $\Cont$ readback, and
the recorded $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance.

Completeness follows because a downstream consumer can project only those
rows from the packet. The tuple contains no separate host field equality,
ambient algebraic-closure object, or hidden separability witness. Therefore
every accepted $\GaloisExtUp$ dependency read factors through exactly the
displayed ledger rows.
\end{proof}
