\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\UniformCompletionCommonWindowUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-uniform-completion-common-window-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Kelley on uniform spaces and Bishop--Bridges on completion by explicit regular data, the $\UniformCompletionCommonWindowUp$ packet records the finite common-window route by which uniform-completion requests share one observation window before any $\RealUp$ seal is read. It concretizes the Real and Completion vision of \autoref{ch:visions-real-completeness} by tying the $\UniformCompletionFunctorUp$ surface to $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, and $\RealUp$ rows without quotient completion equality. Its concrete dependency route reads the functor source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformcompletionfunctor-namecert}, the stream windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic budget of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}.

\begin{definition}[Uniform completion common-window carrier]
\label{def:uniform-completion-common-window-carrier}
A $\UniformCompletionCommonWindowUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
W=(U,E,S,R,D,L,F,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $U$ is the $\UniformCompletionFunctorUp$ source row naming the completion-facing request, entourage scale, and functorial handoff boundary.
\item $E$ is the finite entourage-window row. It records the common uniform scale used by all source observations in the accepted request.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ common-window row. It synchronizes the finite observation windows before any regular rational readback is exposed.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ source-readback row generated from the synchronized window.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ budget row, recording the dyadic tolerance, slack, comparison, and endpoint-conversion entries used by the readback.
\item $L$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row. It is exposed only after $S,R,D$ have displayed the shared window and regular rational readback.
\item $F$ is the common-window functoriality row. It states that completion-facing consumers of $U$ and Real-facing consumers of $L$ read the same $E,S,R,D$ route.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the uniform-completion source, common entourage window, StreamName window, RegSeqRat readback, DyadicRatCore budget, Real seal, functoriality, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a completion request through $U,E,S,R,D,L,F,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\UniformCompletionCommonWindowUp$, $\UniformCompletionFunctorUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\UniformCompletionCommonWindowUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for quotient completion equality, selected completion points, host metric completeness, countable choice, or a second private observation window.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Uniform completion common-window NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:uniform-completion-common-window-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\UniformCompletionCommonWindowUp$ carrier
$$
W=(U,E,S,R,D,L,F,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\UniformCompletionCommonWindowUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the displayed rows, uniform-completion source compatibility through $U$, common entourage-window admission through $E$, synchronized $\StreamNameUp$ admission through $S$, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback exactness through $R$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ budget exactness through $D$, $\RealUp$ seal handoff through $L$, common-window functoriality through $F$, componentwise transport through $H$, displayed replay through $C$, package provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.

Every completion-facing or Real-facing consumer must use the same $E,S,R,D$ route. It receives no quotient completion equality, no selected completion point, no host metric-completeness principle, no countable-choice schedule, and no private window not displayed by the packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:uniform-completion-common-window-carrier}. The displayed rows are exactly $U,E,S,R,D,L,F,H,C,P,N$.

The uniform-completion source row $U$ is read first, and the common entourage window $E$ fixes the finite scale for the request. The synchronized StreamName row $S$ then supplies the only observation window available to the packet.

The regular rational and dyadic rows $R,D$ are downstream of that shared window. The Real seal $L$ can be exposed only after those rows display the readback and budget entries, so no consumer can substitute a quotient completion class or selected limit.

The row $F$ ties the completion-facing and Real-facing reads to the same route. Transport, replay, provenance, and local naming in $H,C,P,N$ preserve the route without adding host completeness, choice, or a second private window.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Uniform completion common-window Real seal handoff]
\label{thm:uniform-completion-common-window-real-seal-handoff}
Every $\UniformCompletionFunctorUp$ or $\RealUp$ consumer of an accepted $\UniformCompletionCommonWindowUp$ carrier reaches the terminal Real seal only through
$$
U \longmapsto E \longmapsto S \longmapsto R,D \longmapsto L \longmapsto F,H,C,P,N .
$$
Thus the completion request, common entourage window, synchronized StreamName window, RegSeqRat readback, DyadicRatCore budget, RealUp seal, common-window functoriality, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming are jointly visible, and quotient completion equality, selected completion points, host metric completeness, countable choice, and private observation windows are not exported.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a completion-facing or Real-facing read. The source request is $U$, and the only uniform scale that may be consumed is the common row $E$.

The observation side is forced through $S$. Once that StreamName window is displayed, the regular rational readback $R$ and dyadic budget $D$ provide the finite data needed by the Real seal.

The seal row $L$ is therefore downstream of the shared completion window, not of a quotient completion object. The functoriality row $F$ records that both the completion consumer and the Real consumer read the same route.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the packet. Since no coordinate stores selected completion points, host completeness, choice, or private windows, the handoff exports only the displayed common-window route.
\end{proof}
