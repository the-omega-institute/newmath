The $\InterHistInvariantUp$ interface packages a finite two-history invariant readback for the Inter-Hist locality chain. It consumes the multi-history packet rows already exposed by $\FiniteMultiHistPacketUp$, the causal-witness rows supplied by $\CrossHistCausalUp$ when present, and the unary maximum-rate boundary consumed by $\MaxCausalRateUp$, while keeping the whole read inside $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ provenance. The causal row is consumed through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-crosshistcausal-namecert}, and the downstream unary-rate boundary is the consumer surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-maxcausalrate-namecert}. The packet is not a global frame, a synchronized clock, a metric geometry, a host real equality, or a quotient of histories.

\begin{definition}[InterHistInvariant carrier]
\label{def:interhist-invariant-carrier}
An $\InterHistInvariantUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
I=(A,B,R,V,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ and $B$ are the two history rows being compared;
\item $R$ is either the displayed relation row supplied by $\FiniteMultiHistPacketUp$ or the causal-witness row supplied by $\CrossHistCausalUp$;
\item $V$ is the unary invariant readback row obtained from the transported pair and the relation row;
\item $S$ is the observer-symmetry row, recording that swapping the two history rows transports to the same unary readback surface;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the two histories, relation row, invariant readback, symmetry row, continuation routes, package row, and local name row;
\item $C$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ routes for two-history projection, relation inspection, unary readback, symmetry comparison, and downstream consumer handoff;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\FiniteMultiHistPacketUp$, $\CrossHistCausalUp$, $\MaxCausalRateUp$, $\Hist$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\InterHistInvariantUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only after transporting the two histories, the relation or causal-witness row, the unary readback row, and the observer-symmetry row to a common finite two-history surface. The carrier has no coordinate for a global frame, synchronized clock, metric geometry, host real equality, quotient construction, or observer-independent ambient object.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[InterHistInvariant NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:interhist-invariant-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:interhist-invariant-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\InterHistInvariantUp}$ surface consists of five finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed packet $I=(A,B,R,V,S,H,C,P,N)$;
\item two-history projection through $A,B$ and the relation or causal-witness row $R$;
\item unary invariant readback through $V$ after componentwise $\hsame$ transport;
\item observer-symmetry through the row $S$, with the swapped packet returning to the same readback surface;
\item consumer non-escape for global frames, synchronized clocks, metric geometry, host real equality, and quotient constructions.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is the projection of \autoref{def:interhist-invariant-carrier}. The rows $A,B,R,V,S,H,C,P,N$ display exactly the two histories, the relation or causal-witness row, the unary invariant readback, the symmetry row, transport, route, provenance, and local name.

The two-history projection uses $C$ to read $A$ and $B$ before it reads $R$. The relation row is therefore part of a finite multi-history or cross-history packet, not an ambient relation over host objects.

The unary readback is the row $V$ transported by $H$. Observer symmetry is then the row $S$, which compares the original and swapped two-history presentations after the same displayed transport.

Since no carrier coordinate names a global frame, synchronized clock, metric geometry, host real equality, or quotient construction, the local $\NameCert$ obligations cannot export any such object.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[InterHistInvariant hsame stability]
\label{thm:interhist-invariant-hsame-stability}
For accepted $\InterHistInvariantUp$ packets $I=(A,B,R,V,S,H,C,P,N)$ and $I'=(A',B',R',V',S',H',C',P',N')$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport of the two history rows, relation or causal-witness rows, unary readback rows, and observer-symmetry rows preserves the $\InterHistInvariantUp$ classifier. The transported packet still reads only the displayed two-history surface and its local invariant row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project both carriers and transport the history rows through $H$ and $H'$. The hypotheses put $A,B$ and $A',B'$ on a common two-history surface before either invariant row is read.

Next transport the relation or causal-witness rows $R$ and $R'$. The continuation routes in $C$ and $C'$ replay the same finite relation inspection after transport, so the classifier has no alternate source for inter-history content.

Finally read the unary rows $V,V'$ and the symmetry rows $S,S'$. Because these rows are part of the same transported packet, the classifier preserves the accepted invariant surface and does not introduce a global frame, clock, metric, host equality, or quotient.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[InterHistInvariant observer symmetry]
\label{thm:interhist-invariant-observer-symmetry}
For an accepted $\InterHistInvariantUp$ carrier $I=(A,B,R,V,S,H,C,P,N)$, swapping the two history rows produces a readback that factors through the same relation or causal-witness row, the same unary invariant row, and the same observer-symmetry row after displayed $\hsame$ transport. The invariant is observer-symmetric as a finite packet read, not as an equality inside a global frame.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the two projected history rows $A$ and $B$. The swapped presentation uses the same carrier rows with the two history projections interchanged.

Apply the componentwise transport row $H$ and the routes in $C$. These rows carry both the original and swapped presentations to the common finite two-history surface on which $R$ and $V$ are read.

The row $S$ is the displayed symmetry witness between those two presentations. It identifies the invariant readback at the packet surface and supplies no synchronized clock, metric comparison, host real equality, or quotient of the two histories.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[InterHistInvariant maximum-rate consumer boundary]
\label{thm:interhist-invariant-max-rate-consumer-boundary}

The consumer receives a finite invariant packet over $\FiniteMultiHistPacketUp$ and $\CrossHistCausalUp$ rows; it receives no global frame, synchronized clock, metric geometry, host real-valued speed, host real equality, or quotient construction.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier. A maximum-rate consumer first sees the two history rows, the relation or causal-witness row, the invariant readback, and the symmetry row because those are the only coordinates that can name inter-history content.

Use \autoref{thm:interhist-invariant-namecert-obligations} to order the read through carrier admission, two-history projection, unary readback, and observer symmetry. Then use \autoref{thm:interhist-invariant-hsame-stability} to transport the same rows to the consumer surface.

The observer-symmetry theorem supplies the packet read that a maximum-rate boundary may consume. Since the packet exports only $A,B,R,V,S,H,C,P,N$, the consumer has no source for global frames, synchronized clocks, metric geometry, host real-valued speeds, host real equality, or quotient constructions.
\end{proof}
