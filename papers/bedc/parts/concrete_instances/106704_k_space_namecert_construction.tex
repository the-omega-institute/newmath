\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\KSpaceUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-k-space-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Kelley's compactly generated spaces, the $\KSpaceUp$ packet records the topology whose public open rows are tested by compact probes. It sits after the topology carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-topology-namecert}, the compact-space certificate surface, and the compact-open function-space consumer of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compact-open-function-space-namecert}. The packet names compact-test topology rows for downstream continuity and mapping-space consumers; it does not import arbitrary open-cover choice, a host category of spaces, quotient equality of opens, or an ambient set-theoretic coreflection.

\begin{definition}[K-space carrier]
\label{def:k-space-carrier}
A $\KSpaceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ tuple
$$
K=(X,T,Q,A,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the underlying point-name carrier whose observations are read only through $\BHist$ rows.
\item $T$ is the ambient $\TopologyUp$ row listing the candidate open-name classifier.
\item $Q$ is the compact-probe ledger: each accepted row records a compact source certificate, a probe map into $X$, and its compact-domain provenance.
\item $A$ is the compact-test topology ledger, saying that an open-name row $U$ is admitted precisely when every compact probe in $Q$ pulls $U$ back to an open row on its compact source.
\item $E$ records exactness between $T$ and $A$ for the displayed open names, so the packet exports the compactly generated topology rather than a second unrelated topology.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $X,T,Q,A,E,C,P,N$.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from topology requests and compact-probe requests through the same rows.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\KSpaceUp$, $\TopologyUp$, compact probes, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\KSpaceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exposes compact-test topology data only through $X,T,Q,A,E,H,C,P,N$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[K-space classifier]
\label{def:k-space-classifier}
The $\KSpaceUp$ classifier accepts two carrier rows as the same public packet exactly when their point carrier, ambient topology row, compact-probe ledger, compact-test topology ledger, exactness row, transport row, replay row, provenance row, and local naming row are componentwise related by the displayed $\hsame$ evidence. It has no coordinate for selected subcovers, host open families, quotient-open representatives, choice of compact probes outside $Q$, or an external coreflection functor.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[K-space NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:k-space-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\KSpaceUp$ carrier $K=(X,T,Q,A,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\KSpaceUp}$ surface consists exactly of five obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed point carrier, topology row, compact-probe ledger, compact-test topology ledger, exactness row, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item compact-probe accountability, requiring every probe inspected by $A$ to be named by the compact-probe ledger $Q$;
\item compact-test exactness, requiring the admitted open rows to be exactly those whose pullbacks along all displayed compact probes are open on their compact sources;
\item componentwise transport and replay stability under $H$ and $C$ for the same displayed rows;
\item ledger refusal, stating that arbitrary open-cover choice, host compact-object selection, quotient equality of opens, and ambient set-theoretic coreflection are not carrier coordinates.
\end{enumerate}
No local obligation is stored outside these five rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The only mathematical rows are $X,T,Q,A,E$, and the structural rows are $H,C,P,N$.

The compact-probe accountability row is read through $Q$. Each pullback test used by $A$ must name its compact source and probe map before the topology request can inspect it.

The exactness row $E$ binds the topology row $T$ to the compact-test ledger $A$. Hence the packet exports the compactly generated topology determined by the displayed probes, not a freely chosen open-family classifier.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name this same finite route. Since the excluded objects are absent from $X,T,Q,A,E,H,C,P,N$, no accepted packet can export them.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Compact-test continuity handoff]
\label{thm:k-space-compact-test-continuity-handoff}
A downstream continuity or compact-open function-space consumer of an accepted $\KSpaceUp$ carrier can inspect openness only through
$$
\TopologyUp,\quad \KSpaceUp,\quad Q,\quad A .
$$
Thus a continuity check against a $\KSpaceUp$ source is routed through displayed compact probes and compact-test topology rows, while open-cover choice, external compact-object selection, and host coreflection data remain outside the packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the topology request through $T$ and the compact-test row through $A$. The row $A$ can certify an open-name request only after all displayed compact probes in $Q$ have supplied the corresponding pullback-open rows.

The exactness row $E$ then identifies the exported open-name classifier with that compact-test ledger. A compact-open function-space consumer therefore receives the same probe route rather than a host-space construction.

Rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve this route under transport, replay, provenance, and local naming. No extra coordinate is available for open-cover choice, selected compact probes outside $Q$, or an ambient coreflection principle.
\end{proof}
