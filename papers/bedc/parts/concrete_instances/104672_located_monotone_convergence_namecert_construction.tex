\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\LocatedMonotoneConvergenceUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-located-monotone-convergence-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges constructive analysis, the $\LocatedMonotoneConvergenceUp$ packet records the monotone-convergence route that is available when a bounded monotone real sequence is handed to a located supremum surface. It sits between the source sequence packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-monotone-real-sequence-namecert}, the located upper-bound and lower-approximation packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedsupremum-namecert}, the finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the terminal $\RealUp$ seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet names the finite handoff from monotone sequence data to a located supremum read; it does not import arbitrary completeness of the real line.

\begin{definition}[Located monotone convergence carrier]
\label{def:located-monotone-convergence-carrier}
A $\LocatedMonotoneConvergenceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(M,B,U,W,R,D,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the accepted $\MonotoneRealSequenceUp$ source row, exposing the inspected sequence schedule, finite stream windows, regular readbacks, real seals, boundedness ledger, and monotonicity ledger.
\item $B$ is the monotone bounded ledger read from $M$, recording the common upper-bound surface and the finite comparison rows that keep each inspected sequence value below it.
\item $U$ is the $\LocatedSupremumUp$ handoff row, displaying the upper-bound and lower-approximation ledgers supplied to the supremum packet.
\item $W$ is the shared $\StreamNameUp$ precision-window row through which the sequence observations, upper-bound ledger, and lower-approximation requests are inspected.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row assembled from the windowed sequence observations and the located-supremum approximation ledger.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, storing the requested dyadic precision, tail threshold, and comparison radii used to relate a late monotone term to the located supremum surface.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after $M,B,U,W,R,D$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the monotone source, bounded ledger, located-supremum handoff, precision windows, regular readback, dyadic tolerance ledger, real seal, continuation route, provenance, and local naming row.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a convergence request through $M,B,U,W,R,D,E,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LocatedMonotoneConvergenceUp$, $\MonotoneRealSequenceUp$, $\LocatedSupremumUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedMonotoneConvergenceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through
$$
M,B,U,W,R,D,E,H,C,P,N.
$$
It has no coordinate for arbitrary real completeness, trichotomy, quotient stream equality, countable choice, open-cover compactness, or a selected ambient limit outside the displayed located-supremum handoff.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Located monotone convergence NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:located-monotone-convergence-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\LocatedMonotoneConvergenceUp$ carrier
$$
L=(M,B,U,W,R,D,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedMonotoneConvergenceUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the monotone source, bounded ledger, located-supremum handoff, shared precision window, regular readback, dyadic tolerance ledger, real seal, transports, replay, provenance, and local naming;
\item monotone bounded ledger admission, requiring $B$ to be read from the same $\MonotoneRealSequenceUp$ packet $M$ that supplies the sequence schedule and monotonicity ledger;
\item located-supremum handoff, requiring $U$ to expose upper-bound and lower-approximation rows through the $\LocatedSupremumUp$ surface before any convergence read is exported;
\item $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, requiring $R$ to repack the inspected stream windows and approximation witnesses rather than a hidden real representative;
\item dyadic tolerance replay, requiring $D$ to bind each requested precision to a finite tail threshold and comparison radius;
\item $\RealUp$ seal non-escape, requiring $E$ to remain downstream of $M,B,U,W,R,D$ together with $H,C,P,N$.
\end{enumerate}
No obligation may call on arbitrary $\RealUp$ completeness, trichotomy, quotient stream equality, countable choice, open-cover compactness, or an ambient selected limit.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:located-monotone-convergence-carrier}. Carrier admission exposes exactly the rows $M,B,U,W,R,D,E,H,C,P,N$, so the only sequence source is the accepted $\MonotoneRealSequenceUp$ row $M$.

Read the order-facing rows next. The bounded ledger $B$ is attached to the same inspected schedule and monotonicity ledger carried by $M$, while $U$ is the only located-supremum handoff row and therefore the only place where upper-bound and lower-approximation information can be read.

The convergence-facing rows are downstream of these order rows. The shared window $W$, regular readback $R$, and dyadic tolerance ledger $D$ supply the finite precision request, readback route, tail threshold, and comparison radius before the real seal $E$ is exposed.

Finally the structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same packet. Since the excluded completeness, order, quotient, choice, compactness, and selected-limit coordinates do not occur in the carrier, they cannot occur among the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Located monotone convergence supremum readback]
\label{thm:located-monotone-convergence-supremum-readback}
Let $L=(M,B,U,W,R,D,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\LocatedMonotoneConvergenceUp$ packet. Every $\RealUp$-facing convergence read exported by $L$ factors through the $\LocatedSupremumUp$ handoff row $U$ and its displayed upper-bound and lower-approximation ledgers, then through the shared $\StreamNameUp$ window $W$, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback $R$, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger $D$, and the terminal $\RealUp$ seal $E$. Thus the read is a located-supremum factorization of the monotone source $M$, not an appeal to ambient completeness of arbitrary real subsets.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Start with a Real-facing convergence consumer. By \autoref{def:located-monotone-convergence-carrier}, the only terminal real coordinate is $E$, and it is displayed after the monotone source, bounded ledger, located-supremum handoff, shared window, regular readback, and dyadic tolerance ledger.

Apply \autoref{thm:located-monotone-convergence-namecert-obligations}. Its located-supremum handoff row forces the upper-bound and lower-approximation reads through $U$, while its readback and tolerance rows force the same request through $W,R,D$ before $E$ can be read.

The packet $M$ supplies only the bounded monotone source surface, and $H,C,P,N$ can only transport, replay, source, and name the displayed route. Therefore every exported convergence read factors through $\LocatedSupremumUp$ and not through an ambient completeness principle.
\end{proof}
