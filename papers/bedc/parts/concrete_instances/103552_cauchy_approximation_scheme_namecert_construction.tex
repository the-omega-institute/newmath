\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CauchyApproximationSchemeUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-approximation-scheme-namecert}

Following Bishop and Bridges constructive approximation of Cauchy names, the $\CauchyApproximationSchemeUp$ packet records the finite schedule that lets a consumer request dyadic precision, read a finite window of a name, and hand the resulting regular rational data toward the real boundary. It sits between the dyadic tolerance core of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the stream observation surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational sequence readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the convergence schedule of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-convergence-modulus-namecert}, the net-facing carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchynet-namecert}, and the sealed real interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The chapter packages the approximation schedule before quotient completion, selected limits, countable choice, trichotomy, host real equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness is available.

\begin{definition}[Cauchy approximation scheme carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-approximation-scheme-carrier}
A $\CauchyApproximationSchemeUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
A=(D,S,M,R,W,Q,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger. It records the dyadic radii, comparison cells, shrink rows, and precision budgets available to the approximation consumer.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ observation row. It records the finite name windows whose entries may be inspected at each displayed precision demand.
\item $M$ is the $\CauchyConvergenceModulusUp$ schedule row. It assigns to each tolerance request a finite window boundary that is allowed to justify a Cauchy comparison.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row obtained from the displayed dyadic ledger, stream windows, and schedule.
\item $W$ is the $\CauchyNetUp$ compatibility row. It records the net-facing approximation window reached from the same finite schedule.
\item $Q$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row. It records only the boundary interface reached through $R$ and $W$, not a host real representative.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the tolerance ledger, stream windows, convergence schedule, regular readback, net window, real seal, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the route
$$
D,S,M \longmapsto R,W \longmapsto Q \longmapsto H,P,N .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\CauchyConvergenceModulusUp$, $\CauchyNetUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyApproximationSchemeUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through the ten displayed rows. It has no coordinate for quotient completion, selected limits, countable choice, trichotomy, host real equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy approximation scheme NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-approximation-scheme-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CauchyApproximationSchemeUp$ carrier
$$
A=(D,S,M,R,W,Q,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyApproximationSchemeUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the dyadic tolerance ledger, stream observation windows, convergence schedule, regular readback, net window, real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item dyadic precision exposure, requiring every tolerance demand to be witnessed by a displayed $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ radius, shrink row, comparison cell, or budget entry in $D$;
\item stream-window exposure, requiring every inspected name value to enter through the finite $\StreamNameUp$ row $S$;
\item modulus routing, requiring $M$ to bind each displayed precision request to a finite Cauchy window before $R$, $W$, or $Q$ is read;
\item regular and net handoff, requiring $R$ and $W$ to consume the same displayed $D,S,M$ data before any real-facing boundary is exposed;
\item provenance exhaustion, requiring $H,C,P,N$ to transport, replay, source, and name exactly the rows listed above and no quotient completion, selected limit, countable-choice row, trichotomy row, host real equality, or ambient completeness principle.
\end{enumerate}
No Cauchy-approximation-scheme obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Project the carrier tuple $A=(D,S,M,R,W,Q,H,C,P,N)$. The carrier definition lists every coordinate visible to the local certificate, so admission is just the displayed finite packet with its transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows.

The precision request first reads $D$ and $S$. The dyadic ledger supplies the tolerance data, while the stream row supplies only finite windows of the Cauchy name.

The schedule row $M$ then fixes the Cauchy window that may justify the request. Since $M$ is displayed before $R,W,Q$, no later consumer can replace the finite window by a selected limit or an ambient completeness assertion.

Finally $R$ and $W$ are the regular-sequence and net-facing handoffs, and $Q$ is only the real seal reached after those handoffs. The rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve and name that same route, so the listed obligations exhaust the NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy approximation scheme window stability]
\label{thm:cauchy-approximation-scheme-window-stability}
Let
$$
A=(D,S,M,R,W,Q,H,C,P,N)
$$
and
$$
A'=(D',S',M',R',W',Q',H',C',P',N')
$$
be accepted $\CauchyApproximationSchemeUp$ packets. If the displayed $\hsame$ rows identify the dyadic tolerance ledgers, stream windows, and convergence schedules $D,S,M$ with $D',S',M'$ componentwise, then every $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\CauchyNetUp$, or $\RealUp$ consumer sees the same finite approximation window from $A$ and $A'$. The consumer can distinguish packets only through the displayed route
$$
D,S,M \longmapsto R,W \longmapsto Q
$$
together with its transport, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Start with the NameCert obligation theorem. Every consumer-facing read of the packet must pass through the dyadic ledger, stream windows, and convergence schedule before it reaches a regular readback, net window, or real seal.

Apply the componentwise $\hsame$ hypotheses to those first three rows. They identify the tolerance request, the inspected stream window, and the scheduled Cauchy boundary in the two packets.

The handoff rows $R,W,Q$ are read only after the identified data, and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$ and $H',C',P',N'$ transport, replay, source, and name that same route. Thus the consumer-visible approximation window is stable under the displayed transport, with no additional coordinate available to refine the comparison.
\end{proof}
