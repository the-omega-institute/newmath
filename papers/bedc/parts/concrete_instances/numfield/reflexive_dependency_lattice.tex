\begin{theorem}[NumField reflexive dependency lattice]
\label{thm:numfield-reflexive-dependency-lattice}
Every accepted reflexive $\NumFieldUp$ carrier read consumes the $\RatUp$ carrier and classifier rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rat-namecert} and the identity-extension rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-fieldext-namecert} before it exposes the singleton-basis number-field row. In particular, the reflexive number-field packet is not an isolated carrier: its public read factors through the displayed rational source, the RatUp field table, the $\FieldExtUp$ identity embedding, the inherited vector-space rows, and the local $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ provenance.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the reflexive carrier rows displayed earlier in this chapter. The base field, ambient field, endpoint classifier, and scalar-action rows are all inherited from the rational certificate and the identity $\FieldExtUp$ packet.

The singleton-basis row is exposed only after that inherited lattice has been read. The $\RatUp$ rows supply the carrier and classifier surface, while the $\FieldExtUp$ rows supply the identity embedding and vector-space package.

The local $\NumFieldUp$ naming row repackages those displayed dependencies through its own transport, continuation, package, and certificate rows. Since no independent number-field carrier is present before the rational and identity-extension rows are consumed, every public reflexive read factors through the two sibling certificates.
\end{proof}
