\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\QuotientGroupUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-quotientgroup-namecert}

The quotient-group interface $\QuotientGroupUp$ packages the carrier of cosets of a normal subgroup of a group. It depends on $\NameCert_{\SubgroupUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-subgroup-namecert}) plus the normality witness, and produces a $\psame$-classifier on cosets without invoking host-level $\Quot$-soundness.

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:quotientgroup-carrier}

\begin{definition}[Centralizer normalizer QuotientGroupUp carrier]
\label{def:centralizer-normalizer-quotientgroup-carrier}
In the BEDC history-level $\GroupUp$ setting of
\autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-tower-certificate}, let $Z_a$
be the centralizer carrier of \autoref{def:group-centralizer-carrier} and let
$N_a$ be the centralizer normalizer carrier of
\autoref{def:group-centralizer-normalizer-carrier}. The
centralizer-normalizer $\QuotientGroupUp$ representative carrier is $N_a$,
and its classifier is
$Q_{Z_a\triangleleft N_a}$ from
\autoref{def:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-quotient-kernel-relation}.
Representative multiplication, identity, and inversion are the ambient
$\mathsf{mul}$, $e$, and $\mathsf{inv}$ operations restricted to $N_a$.

The per-object template fields are:
\begin{enumerate}
\item traditional target: centralizer normalizer quotient group certificate;
\item BEDC source: the history-level $\GroupUp$ source over $\mathsf{BHist}$;
\item lower carrier: $Z_a$;
\item representative carrier: $N_a$;
\item classifier: $Q_{Z_a\triangleleft N_a}$;
\item multiplication representative: $\mathsf{mul}(x,y)$;
\item identity representative: $e$;
\item inverse representative: $\mathsf{inv}(x)$;
\item ledger: carried endpoints in $N_a$ plus the relative kernel witness
$Z_a(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{inv}(x),y))$ and the tower provenance for
$Z_a\subseteq N_a$;
\item status: $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{enumerate}

Thus the carrier is a concrete history-level representative system equipped
with a quotient-ready classifier, without introducing host quotient objects.
\end{definition}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:quotientgroup-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[Centralizer normalizer QuotientGroupUp certificate]
\label{thm:centralizer-normalizer-quotientgroup-certificate}
For a BEDC history-level $\GroupUp$ operation over $\mathsf{BHist}$
satisfying the associativity, unit, multiplication-congruence,
inverse-congruence, inverse-product reversal, and two-sided inverse laws used
by \autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-tower-certificate}, the tuple
of \autoref{def:centralizer-normalizer-quotientgroup-carrier} supplies the
concrete $\QuotientGroupUp$ certificate rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier inhabitance: $N_a(e)$;
\item $Q_{Z_a\triangleleft N_a}$ is reflexive, symmetric, and transitive on
carried representatives;
\item carrier transport: if $N_a(x)$ and $x\hsame y$, then $N_a(y)$;
\item multiplication closure: if $N_a(x)$ and $N_a(y)$, then
$N_a(\mathsf{mul}(x,y))$;
\item multiplication congruence: if $Q_{Z_a\triangleleft N_a}(x,y)$ and
$Q_{Z_a\triangleleft N_a}(u,v)$, then
$Q_{Z_a\triangleleft N_a}(\mathsf{mul}(x,u),\mathsf{mul}(y,v))$;
\item identity descent: $Q_{Z_a\triangleleft N_a}(e,e)$ and the unit
representative is carried by $N_a$;
\item inverse descent: if $Q_{Z_a\triangleleft N_a}(x,y)$, then
$Q_{Z_a\triangleleft N_a}(\mathsf{inv}(x),\mathsf{inv}(y))$;
\item ledger provenance: the representative product keeps the endpoint rows in
$N_a$ and records the relative kernel witness for multiplication inside
$Z_a$.
\end{enumerate}
Consequently the centralizer normalizer tower supplies a quotient-free
certificate for $\QuotientGroupUp$ whose observable names are histories in
$N_a$ and whose equality test is the relative-kernel predicate
$Q_{Z_a\triangleleft N_a}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply
\autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-tower-certificate}. Its lower
and upper subgroup rows give $Z_a(e)$, $N_a(e)$, product closure, inverse
closure, carrier transport for $N_a$, the inclusion $Z_a\subseteq N_a$, and
the normality rows carrying $Z_a$ through conjugation by elements of $N_a$.

Next apply
\autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-quotient-kernel-congruence} to
the classifier
$Q_{Z_a\triangleleft N_a}$. The first three rows give reflexivity, symmetry,
and transitivity on representatives in $N_a$. The fourth row gives product
descent for $\mathsf{mul}$ and the quotient operation congruence required by
the represented multiplication.

The identity row is the upper subgroup inhabitance supplied by the tower,
together with the reflexivity row for
$Q_{Z_a\triangleleft N_a}$. For inverse closure, use the upper subgroup
inverse row from
\autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-tower-certificate}.

Inverse descent follows by combining the ambient inverse-congruence and
two-sided inverse laws named there with
\autoref{thm:group-inverse-mul-reverse}. Expanding
\autoref{def:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-quotient-kernel-relation}, the
kernel witness for inverse representatives is transported from
$Z_a(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{inv}(x),y))$ to the corresponding relative inverse
word by the $Z_a$ inverse-closure and $\hsame$-transport rows.

These rows are exactly the carrier inhabitance, equivalence, product descent,
carrier transport, multiplication closure, identity descent, inverse descent,
and ledger provenance fields demanded by the history-level $\QuotientGroupUp$
certificate, with the ledger witness kept as relative kernel membership rather
than as an element of a host quotient.
\end{proof}
