\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\QuotientGroupUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-quotientgroup-namecert}

The quotient-group interface $\QuotientGroupUp$ packages the carrier of cosets of a normal subgroup of a group. It depends on $\NameCert_{\SubgroupUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-subgroup-namecert}) plus the normality witness, and produces a $\psame$-classifier on cosets without invoking host-level $\Quot$-soundness.

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:quotientgroup-carrier}

The centralizer-normalizer tower of
\autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-tower-certificate} gives a
concrete normal-subgroup input for the quotient-group construction. The lower
carrier is the centralizer $Z_a$, the upper carrier is the normalizer $N_a$,
and all representatives remain BEDC histories.

\begin{definition}[Centralizer normalizer coset classifier]
\label{def:quotientgroup-centralizer-normalizer-coset-classifier}
For the centralizer carrier $Z_a$ and its normalizer carrier $N_a$, define
the concrete coset classifier predicate $Q_{Z_a\leq N_a}$ on
$\mathsf{BHist}$ by
$$
Q_{Z_a\leq N_a}(x,y)
:\Longleftrightarrow
N_a(x)\land N_a(y)\land
\exists z:\mathsf{BHist},\ Z_a(z)\land
\hsame(y,\mathsf{mul}(x,z)).
$$
The endpoints are carried by $N_a$, while the right witness is carried by
$Z_a$. Thus $Q_{Z_a\leq N_a}$ is a classifier predicate over concrete BEDC
histories, not a host quotient object.
\end{definition}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:quotientgroup-certificate}

\begin{definition}[Centralizer normalizer kernel relation]
\label{def:quotientgroup-centralizer-normalizer-kernel-relation}
In the setting of
\autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-tower-certificate}, define the
relation on ambient histories by
$$
Q_{Z_a\triangleleft N_a}(x,y) :\Longleftrightarrow
N_a(x)\land N_a(y)\land
Z_a(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{inv}(x),y)).
$$
The first two clauses keep both representatives inside the normalizer carrier,
and the third clause says that their relative displacement lies in the
centralizer kernel.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Centralizer normalizer kernel certificate]
\label{thm:quotientgroup-centralizer-normalizer-kernel-certificate}
For a BEDC history-level $\GroupUp$ operation over $\mathsf{BHist}$ satisfying
the ambient group laws used by
\autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-tower-certificate}, including
associativity, unit, multiplication-congruence, inverse-congruence,
two-sided inverse laws, and inverse multiplication reversal from
\autoref{thm:group-inverse-mul-reverse}, the relation
$Q_{Z_a\triangleleft N_a}$ of
\autoref{def:quotientgroup-centralizer-normalizer-kernel-relation} is a
quotient-ready classifier on the upper carrier:
\begin{enumerate}
\item if $N_a(x)$, then $Q_{Z_a\triangleleft N_a}(x,x)$;
\item if $Q_{Z_a\triangleleft N_a}(x,y)$, then
$Q_{Z_a\triangleleft N_a}(y,x)$;
\item if $Q_{Z_a\triangleleft N_a}(x,y)$ and
$Q_{Z_a\triangleleft N_a}(y,z)$, then
$Q_{Z_a\triangleleft N_a}(x,z)$;
\item if $Q_{Z_a\triangleleft N_a}(x,y)$ and
$Q_{Z_a\triangleleft N_a}(u,v)$, then
$Q_{Z_a\triangleleft N_a}(\mathsf{mul}(x,u),\mathsf{mul}(y,v))$.
\end{enumerate}
Hence the centralizer-normalizer tower supplies the concrete quotient-free
$\QuotientGroupUp$ classifier certificate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Reflexivity uses the identity row of
\autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-tower-certificate}. From
$N_a(x)$ the endpoint clauses are immediate, and the two-sided inverse law
identifies $\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{inv}(x),x)$ with $e$. Transport along
$\hsame$ carries the centralizer identity witness $Z_a(e)$ to the required
kernel witness.

For symmetry, expand $Q_{Z_a\triangleleft N_a}(x,y)$. The endpoint clauses
swap because both $x$ and $y$ already lie in $N_a$. The kernel witness
$Z_a(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{inv}(x),y))$ is inverted by the inverse-closure row
for $Z_a$, and \autoref{thm:group-inverse-mul-reverse} identifies the inverse
word with $\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{inv}(y),x)$ after the ambient inverse laws.
The $\hsame$-transport row for $Z_a$ gives the displayed symmetric witness.

For transitivity, take kernel witnesses
$Z_a(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{inv}(x),y))$ and
$Z_a(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{inv}(y),z))$. Product closure in $Z_a$ gives a
witness for their product. Associativity and the inverse laws cancel the
middle factors $y$ and $\mathsf{inv}(y)$, and transport turns the product
witness into $Z_a(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{inv}(x),z))$. The endpoint clauses are
the retained $N_a(x)$ and $N_a(z)$ clauses.

For multiplication compatibility, product closure in $N_a$ supplies
$N_a(\mathsf{mul}(x,u))$ and $N_a(\mathsf{mul}(y,v))$. The kernel word
$\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{inv}(\mathsf{mul}(x,u)),\mathsf{mul}(y,v))$ is rewritten
by \autoref{thm:group-inverse-mul-reverse} and associativity as the product of
$\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{inv}(u),
\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{inv}(x),y)),u)$ with
$\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{inv}(u),v)$.

Since $u$ lies in $N_a$, the normality row of
\autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-tower-certificate},
equivalently \autoref{thm:group-centralizer-normal-in-normalizer}, carries
the first factor back into $Z_a$ from the kernel witness for
$Q_{Z_a\triangleleft N_a}(x,y)$. The second factor is the kernel witness for
$Q_{Z_a\triangleleft N_a}(u,v)$. Product closure in $Z_a$ and transport along
the displayed word identity give the required multiplication-compatible row.
\end{proof}

Per-object template: traditional target quotient classifier for the
normal-subgroup tower $Z_a\leq N_a$; BEDC source $\GroupUp$ over
$\mathsf{BHist}$ plus $\SubgroupUp$; lower carrier $Z_a$; upper carrier
$N_a$; classifier $Q_{Z_a\triangleleft N_a}$; stability identity, symmetry,
transitivity, multiplication compatibility, endpoint membership, kernel
membership, kernel transport, normality transport, kernel product closure, and
quotient operation transport; ledger left representative, right
representative, product representatives, relative kernel witness, conjugated
kernel witness, and transported product witness; core theorem list
\autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-tower-certificate},
\autoref{thm:group-inverse-mul-reverse}, and the ambient $\GroupUp$ laws;
exactness predicate relative displacement in $Z_a$ inside $N_a$; downstream
object $\QuotientGroupUp$; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
