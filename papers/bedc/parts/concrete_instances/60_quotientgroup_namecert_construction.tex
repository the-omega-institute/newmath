\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\QuotientGroupUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-quotientgroup-namecert}

The quotient-group interface $\QuotientGroupUp$ packages the carrier of cosets of a normal subgroup of a group. It depends on $\NameCert_{\SubgroupUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-subgroup-namecert}) plus the normality witness, and produces a $\psame$-classifier on cosets without invoking host-level $\Quot$-soundness.

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:quotientgroup-carrier}

\begin{definition}[Concrete centralizer-normalizer quotient group carrier]
\label{def:quotientgroup-centralizer-normalizer-concrete-carrier}
Let a BEDC history-level $\GroupUp$ operation on $\mathsf{BHist}$ satisfy the
group laws used in
\autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-tower-certificate}. Let $Z_a$ be
the lower centralizer carrier and let $N_a$ be the upper normalizer carrier of
that tower. The concrete $\QuotientGroupUp$ carrier is the representative
predicate $N_a$ on histories, and its classifier is the quotient-kernel
relation $Q_{Z_a\triangleleft N_a}$ of
\autoref{def:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-quotient-kernel-relation}.

Multiplication is induced by the ambient $\mathsf{mul}$ on representatives,
the unit is the ambient group unit, and inverse is the ambient group inverse.
The certificate ledger records a representative in $N_a$, its relative kernel
displacement in $Z_a$, and the normality witness that moves centralizer
displacements across normalizer representatives.
\end{definition}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:quotientgroup-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[Concrete centralizer-normalizer QuotientGroupUp certificate]
\label{thm:quotientgroup-centralizer-normalizer-certificate}
For a BEDC history-level $\GroupUp$ operation on $\mathsf{BHist}$ satisfying
the associativity, unit, multiplication-congruence, inverse-congruence, and
two-sided inverse laws used in
\autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-tower-certificate}, the carrier,
classifier, induced multiplication, unit, inverse, and ledger of
\autoref{def:quotientgroup-centralizer-normalizer-concrete-carrier} satisfy
the concrete $\QuotientGroupUp$ certificate fields:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier inhabitation, because the ambient unit is carried by $N_a$;
\item classifier reflexivity on representatives carried by $N_a$;
\item classifier symmetry;
\item classifier transitivity;
\item operation congruence for the induced multiplication on representatives;
\item unit descent for the ambient unit through the classifier;
\item inverse descent for the ambient inverse through the classifier;
\item quotient-kernel exactness, because two representatives are classified
precisely when their relative displacement lies in $Z_a$.
\end{enumerate}
Thus the centralizer-normalizer tower supplies a quotient-ready group carrier
without forming a host quotient object.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier rows come from
\autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-tower-certificate}. Its lower
subgroup certificate supplies $Z_a$, its upper subgroup rows supply $N_a$, and
its normality clauses place the centralizer as a normal lower carrier inside
the normalizer carrier.

The classifier rows are exactly the first three laws of
\autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-quotient-kernel-congruence}.
The same theorem gives the multiplication-compatibility row, so the ambient
$\mathsf{mul}$ descends to representatives modulo
$Q_{Z_a\triangleleft N_a}$.

For the unit row, use the ambient $\GroupUp$ left and right unit laws together
with the $N_a$ identity row of
\autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-tower-certificate}. The relative
displacement between a representative and its product with the unit reduces to
the centralizer identity witness.

For inverse descent, use the ambient inverse-congruence and two-sided inverse
rows. The inverse closure rows for $N_a$ and $Z_a$, together with
\autoref{thm:group-inverse-mul-reverse}, transport the relative displacement
for $Q_{Z_a\triangleleft N_a}(x,y)$ to the relative displacement for the
inverse representatives.

Finally, quotient-kernel exactness is the defining third clause of
\autoref{def:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-quotient-kernel-relation}. The
ledger records the representative endpoints, the displacement
$\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{inv}(x),y)$, and the normality witness used by the
multiplication row; these are exactly the data repacked by the
$\QuotientGroupUp$ certificate.
\end{proof}

Per-object template: traditional target quotient group; BEDC source
$\GroupUp$ plus $\SubgroupUp$ over $\mathsf{BHist}$; carrier $N_a$; classifier
$Q_{Z_a\triangleleft N_a}$; operations induced ambient multiplication, ambient
unit, and ambient inverse; stability obligations carrier inhabitation,
classifier reflexivity, classifier symmetry, classifier transitivity,
operation congruence, unit descent, inverse descent, and quotient-kernel
exactness; ledger representative, relative kernel displacement, and normality
witness; core theorem list
\autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-tower-certificate},
\autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-quotient-kernel-congruence}, and
\autoref{thm:group-inverse-mul-reverse}; exactness relative displacement in
$Z_a$; standard bridge ordinary quotient group; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
