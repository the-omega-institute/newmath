\begin{definition}[Regular Cauchy square carrier]
\label{def:regular-cauchy-square-carrier}

$$
S=(A,W_L,W_R,D_L,D_R,M,E,R,Z,H,C,P,N)

\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ is the single $\RegSeqRatUp$ source row whose diagonal square is being read.
\item $W_L$ and $W_R$ are duplicated aligned $\StreamNameUp$ window rows opened from the same source row $A$ at the same product-facing precision request and observation index.
\item $D_L$ and $D_R$ are identical $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint rows read from the duplicated windows; their equality is displayed as a diagonal source-lock row rather than inferred from quotient stream equality.
\item $M$ is the $\RegularCauchyProductUp$ diagonal product row obtained by feeding the two product source slots with the same $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $A$ and the two aligned endpoint rows $D_L,D_R$.
\item $E$ is the $\RegularCauchyProductBudgetUp$ ledger row that records the paired source-window radii, dyadic square row, product rounding allowance, and remaining readback budget for the diagonal route.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row produced from $M$ and $E$.
\item $Z$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row exposed only after the source row, duplicated windows, dyadic endpoint rows, diagonal product row, product-budget ledger, and regular readback have been displayed.

\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchySquareUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $A,W_L,W_R,D_L,D_R,M,E,R,Z,H,C,P,N$. The carrier has no coordinate for host multiplication, quotient stream equality, selected limits, ambient completeness, $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy square product handoff]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-square-product-handoff}
Every accepted $\RegularCauchySquareUp$ packet factors through the existing $\RegularCauchyProductUp$ route with both product source rows identified as the same $\RegSeqRatUp$ row. More precisely, for
$$

$$

The readback $R$ and seal $Z$ therefore consume the public product and budget boundary before any square-facing consumer can read the result.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

Apply the public product route of \autoref{thm:regular-cauchy-product-public-export-certificate}. Its product carrier accepts two regular source rows, aligned windows, dyadic endpoint rows, a finite product ledger, a regular readback, transports, continuations, provenance, and local naming. In the square packet these product source rows are the displayed pair $A,A$, so the product component is the diagonal specialization of the same route.

Next apply the product-budget handoff rows of \autoref{thm:regular-cauchy-product-budget-regseqrat-handoff} and \autoref{thm:regular-cauchy-product-budget-real-seal-boundary}. They force the readback and real-facing seal to consume the finite product-budget ledger before any downstream consumer receives the result.

The remaining rows $H,C,P,N$ are structural rows of the same finite packet. Since the carrier contains no coordinate for host multiplication, quotient stream equality, selected limits, ambient completeness, or $\RealUp$ completeness, the factorization cannot escape the displayed $\RegularCauchyProductUp$ and $\RegularCauchyProductBudgetUp$ routes.
\end{proof}
