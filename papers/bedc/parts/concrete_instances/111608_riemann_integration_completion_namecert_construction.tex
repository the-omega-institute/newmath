\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RiemannIntegrationCompletionUp$}

Following Riemann and Bishop--Bridges constructive integration, the $\RiemannIntegrationCompletionUp$ packet records the finite Cauchy-sum completion data visible when an integration consumer treats Riemann sums as a regular real-valued completion route. It sits after the integrability witness of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-riemann-integrable-namecert}, the tagged-sum gauge surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-riemann-sum-gauge-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The chapter names the boundary object that completion-facing integration consumers can inspect without importing host function quotients, selected refining partitions, measure completion, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[Riemann integration completion carrier]
\label{def:riemann-integration-completion-carrier}
A $\RiemannIntegrationCompletionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(I,G,S,Q,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following rows.
\begin{itemize}
\item $I$ is the $\RiemannIntegrableUp$ row displaying finite Darboux-gap or integrability evidence for the inspected integrand and interval.
\item $G$ is the $\RiemannSumGaugeUp$ row carrying tagged partitions, gauge windows, mesh control, and finite sum observations.
\item $S$ is the finite Cauchy-sum schedule row: it records only displayed pairs of gauge windows and their rational comparison tolerances.
\item $Q$ is the dyadic or rational comparison row used to compare two displayed Cauchy-sum observations at a stated tolerance.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row reached from the finite Cauchy-sum schedule and comparison rows.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row for the completed integration value read.
\item $H$ is componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $I,G,S,Q,R,E$.
\item $C$ is displayed $\Cont$ replay of the same finite route.
\item $P$ is $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RiemannIntegrationCompletionUp$, $\RiemannIntegrableUp$, $\RiemannSumGaugeUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RiemannIntegrationCompletionUp}$ row.
\end{itemize}
The carrier predicate asserts that these rows form one displayed finite Cauchy-sum completion history. It has no coordinate for a host function quotient, a selected refining sequence of partitions, a completed measure object, countable choice, or an ambient real-completeness principle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Riemann integration completion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:riemann-integration-completion-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RiemannIntegrationCompletionUp$ carrier $K=(I,G,S,Q,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\RiemannIntegrationCompletionUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission, integrability-source exposure through $I$, tagged-sum gauge exposure through $G$, finite Cauchy-sum schedule exposure through $S$, tolerance comparison through $Q$, regular rational readback through $R$, terminal Real seal non-escape through $E$, componentwise transport through $H$, displayed replay through $C$, provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:riemann-integration-completion-carrier}. The only integration rows available to the certificate are $I$ and $G$, and the only completion-facing rows are the finite schedule $S$, comparison row $Q$, regular rational readback $R$, and Real seal $E$.

The source rows are already named by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-riemann-integrable-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-riemann-sum-gauge-namecert}. Their use here is by displayed $\BHist$ rows, not by a host-level function space or by quotient equality between integrands.

The completion side is constrained by the regular rational and Real boundaries of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. Therefore the local certificate can expose only $I,G,S,Q,R,E,H,C,P,N$, with $H,C,P,N$ supplying transport, replay, provenance, and naming for the same finite route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Riemann integration completion Cauchy-sum handoff]
\label{thm:riemann-integration-completion-cauchy-sum-handoff}
Every completion-facing read of an accepted $\RiemannIntegrationCompletionUp$ packet must pass from the $\RiemannIntegrableUp$ source row $I$ through the $\RiemannSumGaugeUp$ row $G$, finite Cauchy-sum schedule $S$, tolerance comparison row $Q$, and $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $R$ before the terminal $\RealUp$ seal $E$ is read. The handoff exports finite Cauchy-sum comparison data only; it exports no selected sequence of tagged partitions, quotient integrand equality, measure completion, or countable-choice selector.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the source rows. The integrability row $I$ supplies the displayed finite evidence that a Riemann integration route is being named, while $G$ supplies the finite tagged-gauge and sum observations available to downstream consumers.

The Cauchy-sum schedule $S$ can only compare displayed observations through $Q$. Once a comparison has been displayed at a stated tolerance, the regular rational handoff $R$ packages the finite Cauchy behavior as a $\RegSeqRatUp$-facing read.

Finally the terminal seal $E$ is read only after $R$. The excluded selected partition sequence, quotient integrand equality, measure completion, and countable-choice selector have no coordinate in $K$, so no completion-facing read can bypass the route $I,G,S,Q,R,E$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Riemann integration completion Real seal non-escape]
\label{thm:riemann-integration-completion-real-seal-nonescape}
For every accepted $\RiemannIntegrationCompletionUp$ packet, any $\RealUp$-facing integration value read is sealed by the displayed finite Cauchy-sum route $I,G,S,Q,R,E,H,C,P,N$. The Real seal cannot be used as a detached integral value, an ambient completeness witness, a host-level equality of integrands, or a measure-theoretic completion object.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:riemann-integration-completion-cauchy-sum-handoff}. It places the terminal Real read after the integrability source, tagged-sum gauge surface, finite Cauchy-sum schedule, tolerance comparison, and regular rational readback.

Use \autoref{thm:riemann-integration-completion-namecert-obligations} for the structural rows. Componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local $\NameCert$ row all replay the same finite route and add no detached Real coordinate.

Thus the seal $E$ remains a terminal observation of the finite Cauchy-sum packet. A consumer may inspect the displayed integration completion route, but cannot extract host equality, selected representatives, measure completion, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness from this chapter.
\end{proof}
