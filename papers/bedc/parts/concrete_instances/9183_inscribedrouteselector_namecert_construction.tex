\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\InscribedRouteSelectorUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-inscribedrouteselector-namecert}
\origin{ai}

The $\InscribedRouteSelectorUp$ packet realizes the route-selection act described by the inscription-event vision in \autoref{ch:visions-inscription-event-as-concrete-instance}. It sits between the full event surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-inscriptionevent-namecert}, the visible residue ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-inscription-gap-ledger-namecert}, and the route-audit packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-inscription-route-audit-namecert}. The selector isolates the moment at which a visible gap is paired with one named route, one accepted check, and one downstream consumer role before public record export is allowed.

\begin{definition}[Inscribed route-selector carrier]
\label{def:inscribed-route-selector-carrier}
An $\InscribedRouteSelectorUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(G,M,R,K,U,L,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $G$ is the visible gap row inherited from the inscription-event source boundary and GAP ledger;
\item $M$ is the named public record row to be reached by the selected route;
\item $R$ is the selected route row from $G$ to $M$;
\item $K$ is the accepted check row for the route selection;
\item $U$ is the downstream consumer role permitted to read the accepted route as public record data;
\item $L$ is the GAP-ledger row recording the residue left after $R,K,U$ have been displayed;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $G,M,R,K,U,L$ together with package and local name rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which consumers inspect the gap, selected route, accepted check, consumer role, and ledger residue;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, $\InscriptionEventUp$, $\InscriptionGapLedgerUp$, $\InscriptionRouteAuditUp$, $\InscriptionAcceptanceBudgetUp$, $\InscriptionTraceClassifierUp$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\InscribedRouteSelectorUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a hidden route choice, an off-packet checker, an unbudgeted consumer, a private source predicate, or a public record export that bypasses the selected route row.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Inscribed route-selector NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:inscribed-route-selector-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:inscribed-route-selector-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\InscribedRouteSelectorUp}$ surface consists exactly of five finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier exposure for $S=(G,M,R,K,U,L,H,C,P,N)$;
\item route-selection admission requiring the visible gap $G$, named public record $M$, selected route $R$, accepted check $K$, and downstream consumer role $U$ to be displayed in the same packet;
\item componentwise stability of gap, name, route, check, consumer, and ledger rows under the $\hsame$ transports in $H$;
\item exactness of public export: the selected route may be exported only through $R,K,U$ together with the GAP-ledger row $L$ and the displayed routes in $C$;
\item ledger rows for GAP residue, route audit, acceptance budget, trace-classifier handoff, package provenance, and the local naming certificate.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.InscribedRouteSelectorUp.InscribedRouteSelectorTasteGate\_single\_carrier\_alignment}
\begin{proof}
Carrier exposure is projection from \autoref{def:inscribed-route-selector-carrier}. The displayed packet lists the gap, name, route, accepted check, consumer, ledger, transport, continuation, package, and local naming rows.

Route-selection admission reads the first five rows together. The visible gap $G$ supplies the open boundary, $M$ names the public record, $R$ selects the route, $K$ admits that selection, and $U$ names the consumer role that may read it.

Stability is componentwise through $H$. Exactness and ledger coverage then use $C,L,P,N$ to replay the same finite route for the route-audit, acceptance-budget, and trace-classifier consumers. Since no hidden route choice, off-packet checker, or extra consumer row is present, the five rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\falsifiablePrediction{A packet that omits the accepted route row cannot export an inscribed public record to any downstream consumer.}

\begin{theorem}[Inscribed route-selector nontriviality prediction]
\label{thm:inscribed-route-selector-nontriviality-prediction}
For every accepted $\InscribedRouteSelectorUp$ carrier $S=(G,M,R,K,U,L,H,C,P,N)$, a downstream consumer that lacks the selected route row $R$ or the accepted check row $K$ cannot export $M$ as an inscribed public record. Such a consumer may read the visible gap and GAP-ledger residue, but the public inscription route remains unavailable until $R,K,U$ are present in the same finite packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The only row whose role is route selection is $R$, and the only row whose role is route admission is $K$.

Apply \autoref{thm:inscribed-route-selector-namecert-obligations}. Public export is licensed by the simultaneous display of $G,M,R,K,U$ and is replayed through $C$ with residue tracked in $L$.

If either $R$ or $K$ is absent, the remaining rows still expose a visible gap and a ledger residue, but they do not expose an accepted selected route to the named record. Therefore the consumer cannot export $M$ as inscribed public record data.
\end{proof}
