Following Bishop and Bridges constructive completion, the $\CauchyCompletionReflectorUp$ packet records the public finite reflector surface for the L10 completion route. It gathers the unit surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchycompletionunit-namecert}, the counit surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-completion-counit-namecert}, the idempotent surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-completion-idempotent-namecert}, the extension route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completionuniversalproperty-namecert}, and the reflection boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completionreflection-namecert}. Its local real-facing rows are read through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-cauchy-completion-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet is a finite reflector certificate; it is not a quotient completion, not a selected limit, not host equality of completed objects, not countable choice, and not ambient completeness.

\begin{definition}[CauchyCompletionReflector carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-completion-reflector-carrier}
A $\CauchyCompletionReflectorUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(S,M,U,Q,I,E,R,T,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the source row for the displayed metric, regular-Cauchy, or real-name data being sent toward completion.
\item $M$ is the completion-object row, read through the $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ and $\MetricCompletionUp$ surfaces rather than through a host quotient carrier.
\item $U$ is the $\CauchyCompletionUnitUp$ row admitting $S$ into the completion-facing surface.
\item $Q$ is the $\CauchyCompletionCounitUp$ row returning already completed or real-sealed reads through the displayed regular route.
\item $I$ is the $\CauchyCompletionIdempotentUp$ row recording that a second completion pass reuses the same completion-object surface.
\item $E$ is the $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$ extension row for uniformly or continuously routed consumers attached to the same unit route.
\item $R$ is the $\CompletionReflectionUp$ row binding the construction, extension, separated boundary, and real-seal rows into one reflection surface.
\item $T$ is the local transport row through $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, and $\RealUp$ windows used by $U,Q,I,E,R$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for source, completion object, unit, counit, idempotence, extension, reflection, local transport, continuation, provenance, and naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a source read through unit admission, completion-object access, counit return, idempotent replay, extension use, reflection, and the local real-name route.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyCompletionReflectorUp$, $\CauchyCompletionUnitUp$, $\CauchyCompletionCounitUp$, $\CauchyCompletionIdempotentUp$, $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$, $\CompletionReflectionUp$, $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyCompletionReflectorUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier on accepted packets compares only these twelve rows under the displayed transport and replay data. It has no coordinate for quotient completions, selected limits, host equality, countable choice, or ambient completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyCompletionReflector NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-reflector-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CauchyCompletionReflectorUp$ carrier $K=(S,M,U,Q,I,E,R,T,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyCompletionReflectorUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed source, completion object, unit, counit, idempotence, universal-property extension, reflection, local transport, continuation, provenance, and naming rows;
\item unit admission, requiring every completion-facing source read to pass through $U$ before $M$ can be consumed;
\item counit return, requiring every completed read that is already real-sealed to return through $Q$ before the terminal $\RealUp$ route is exposed;
\item idempotent replay, requiring a repeated completion request to reuse $I$ and the same completion-object row $M$;
\item extension and reflection compatibility, requiring every universal-property consumer to read $E$ and every reflection consumer to read $R$ over the same $S,M,U,Q,I,T$ route;
\item reflector non-escape, excluding quotient completions, selected limits, host equality, countable choice, ambient completeness, and detached $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{enumerate}
No reflector obligation is stored outside those rows.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyCompletionReflector unit-counit route]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-reflector-unit-counit-route}
Let $K=(S,M,U,Q,I,E,R,T,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyCompletionReflectorUp$ carrier. Every public reflector consumer factors through the finite route
$$
S\longmapsto U\longmapsto M\longmapsto Q\longmapsto I\longmapsto E,R\longmapsto T\longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
The route exposes the unit and counit faces of completion, replays idempotence, and then serves universal-property and reflection consumers through the same real-name transport. It does not supply quotient completion equality, a selected limit, host equality of completed objects, countable choice, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a public reflector consumer. The source row $S$ is visible only as the source of the displayed unit route $U$, so the consumer first reads unit admission before it can inspect the completion-object row $M$.

The counit row $Q$ supplies the return route for reads that are already on the real-seal side. This return is tied to the same local transport row $T$, whose $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, and $\RealUp$ windows are the only real-facing rows available to the packet.

Next read the idempotent row $I$. It states that repeating the completion pass reuses the same completion-object row and the same local transport, so the consumer cannot branch to a second completed carrier.

The universal-property row $E$ and reflection row $R$ are finally consumed over that fixed unit-counit-idempotent route. The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name only the displayed packet, which proves the factorization and the stated non-escape boundary.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyCompletionReflector unit-counit obligation]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-reflector-unit-counit-obligation}
Let $K=(S,M,U,Q,I,E,R,T,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyCompletionReflectorUp$ carrier. The unit-counit surface is obligation-complete exactly at the rows $S,U,M,Q,T$: every public source read must expose $S$ and $U$ before it consumes the completion-object row $M$, and every already completed or real-sealed read must return through $Q$ and the shared local transport row $T$ before any reflector consumer receives the result. The route exports no quotient completion, selected limit, host equality, countable choice, ambient completeness, or detached $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-completion-reflector-carrier}. The source, completion object, unit, counit, and local transport rows are displayed as $S,M,U,Q,T$.

The unit direction reads $S$ first and then $U$. Only after that admission is the completion-object row $M$ available to a public reflector consumer.

The counit direction reads $Q$ for already completed or real-sealed data and then the same local transport row $T$. Since $T$ is the only route to the $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, and $\RealUp$ windows, the return cannot detach from the displayed finite carrier.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve this order and add no excluded completion or choice coordinate. Thus $S,U,M,Q,T$ are exactly the unit-counit obligation surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyCompletionReflector idempotent-extension obligation]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-reflector-idempotent-extension-obligation}
Let $K=(S,M,U,Q,I,E,R,T,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyCompletionReflectorUp$ carrier. The idempotent-extension part of the reflector surface is exactly the route through $I,E,R$ over the same $S,U,M,Q,T$ rows: repeated completion reuses $I$ and the displayed completion-object row, extension consumers read $E$ only after the unit-counit route is visible, and reflection consumers read $R$ only over that same finite route. No second completion carrier, selected limit, host equality, countable choice, ambient completeness, or detached real-completeness row is introduced.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the unit-counit obligation of \autoref{thm:cauchy-completion-reflector-unit-counit-obligation}. It fixes the public completion-facing route at $S,U,M,Q,T$.

The idempotent row $I$ is read over this route. A repeated completion pass therefore reuses the displayed completion-object row $M$ rather than branching to a second carrier.

The extension row $E$ and reflection row $R$ are downstream of the same route. The extension consumer cannot bypass $U,Q,T$, and the reflection consumer receives only the already displayed unit, counit, idempotent, extension, and local transport rows.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name that route. Because the carrier contains no second completion object or excluded host-side coordinate, the stated non-escape boundary follows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyCompletionReflector real-transport nonescape]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-reflector-real-transport-nonescape}
For every accepted $\CauchyCompletionReflectorUp$ carrier $K=(S,M,U,Q,I,E,R,T,H,C,P,N)$, the real-facing transport row $T$ and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve exactly the displayed reflector route. A consumer may transport and replay the source, completion object, unit, counit, idempotent, extension, and reflection rows through $T,H,C,P,N$, but it cannot export quotient completions, selected limits, host equality, countable choice, ambient completeness, detached $\RealUp$ completeness, or any real route not displayed by $T$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the local transport row from the carrier. By definition, $T$ is the only row that exposes the $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, and $\RealUp$ windows used by $U,Q,I,E,R$.

Apply the unit-counit and idempotent-extension obligation theorems. They place the completion-object route, the return route, repeated completion, extension, and reflection all over the same displayed $S,U,M,Q,T$ surface.

The rows $H,C,P,N$ do not enlarge that surface. They provide componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, package provenance, and local naming for the rows already present in the packet.

Hence every real-facing read remains inside the displayed transport route. The excluded quotient, selection, host-equality, choice, ambient-completeness, detached-real-completeness, and off-route real data have no coordinate from which they could be exported.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyCompletionReflector naturality square]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-reflector-naturality-square}
Let $K=(S,M,U,Q,I,E,R,T,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyCompletionReflectorUp$ carrier. The public reflector square classifies the route
$$
S\longmapsto U\longmapsto M\longmapsto E,R\longmapsto H,C,P,N
$$
with the route
$$
S\longmapsto T\longmapsto Q,I\longmapsto E,R\longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
Equivalently, a consumer that first applies unit admission and then reads the extension and reflection rows has the same displayed classifier as a consumer that first reads the source through the local $\StreamNameUp$--$\RegSeqRatUp$--$\DyadicRatCoreUp$--$\RealUp$ transport row $T$ and then returns by the counit and idempotent rows. The comparison is made only through the displayed $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert_{\CauchyCompletionReflectorUp}$ rows, and it exports no quotient completion equality, selected limit, host equality, countable choice, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:cauchy-completion-reflector-namecert-obligations} to expose the only rows available in an accepted carrier. The unit row $U$, counit row $Q$, idempotent row $I$, extension row $E$, reflection row $R$, transport row $T$, and structural rows $H,C,P,N$ are the entire public surface.

Apply \autoref{thm:cauchy-completion-reflector-unit-counit-route} to place every public consumer on the same finite route. The left leg of the square reads $S$ through $U$ before it consumes $M,E,R$; the right leg reads $S$ through $T$ before the return and replay rows $Q,I$ feed the same $E,R$ consumers.

The sibling certificates supply the public faces used by these two readings: the unit surface is \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchycompletionunit-namecert}, the counit surface is \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-completion-counit-namecert}, the idempotent replay surface is \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-completion-idempotent-namecert}, the extension surface is \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completionuniversalproperty-namecert}, and the reflection boundary is \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completionreflection-namecert}.

Since both legs end by the same $H,C,P,N$ rows, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local $\NameCert$ row classify them as the same public reflector square. No extra classifier coordinate remains from which a quotient completion, selected limit, host equality, countable choice, or ambient completeness claim could be read.
\end{proof}
