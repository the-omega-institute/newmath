\item $U$ is the $\UpperRealUp$ ledger. It records the finite upper observations and their dyadic comparison budgets.
\item $G$ is the located gap and apartness row. It binds the lower and upper ledgers to the same displayed located-cut request.

\begin{theorem}[Lower-upper real cut NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:lower-upper-real-cut-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\LowerUpperRealCutUp$ carrier
$$
K=(L,U,G,D,S,R,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\LowerUpperRealCutUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following five finite rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier exposure for the lower ledger, upper ledger, located gap row, dyadic tolerance row, stream window, regular-Cauchy handoff, real seal, componentwise transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item lower ledger stability, requiring every lower observation to be read through $L,D,S,H,C,P,N$ and to preserve its displayed dyadic budget under componentwise $\hsame$ transport;
\item upper ledger stability, requiring every upper observation to be read through $U,D,S,H,C,P,N$ and to preserve its displayed dyadic budget under componentwise $\hsame$ transport;
\item located gap exactness, requiring the apartness row $G$ to bind the displayed lower and upper observations to the same finite located-cut request before the regular-Cauchy handoff is consumed;
\item ledger non-escape, requiring $R$ and $E$ to remain downstream of $L,U,G,D,S$ and to export no quotient cuts, host suprema, selected cuts, trichotomy, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{enumerate}
No local NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier exposure is projection from \autoref{def:lower-upper-real-cut-carrier}. The accepted packet displays exactly the lower ledger, upper ledger, located gap row, dyadic tolerance row, stream window, regular-Cauchy handoff, real seal, and structural rows.

Lower and upper ledger stability are componentwise. The row $H$ transports the visible lower and upper observations, while $C$ replays their dyadic budgets through the same finite window and provenance rows.

Located gap exactness is read from $G$ against $L$ and $U$. The apartness evidence is a displayed finite row of the packet, so it binds the two one-sided ledgers before a regular-Cauchy comparison can be exported.

Ledger non-escape follows from the row inventory. The handoff $R$ and the seal $E$ sit downstream of $L,U,G,D,S$, and no coordinate stores a quotient cut, host supremum, selected cut, trichotomy branch, countable-choice selector, or ambient completeness principle.
\end{proof}

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
