\item $I$ is the $\InnerProductUp$ row over the same source, exposing scalar pairing values through the terminal $\RealUp$ seal.
\item $L$ is the $\BoundedLinearFunctionalUp(H)$ row, carrying the scalar action, linearity ledger, bounded-operator reading, and bound endpoint.

\item $P$ is the $\OrthogonalProjectionTheoremUp$ handoff row, recording the projection-theorem route used to isolate the representing-vector candidate from the kernel-facing orthogonality data.

\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\FrechetRieszUp}$ row.

The classifier compares accepted packets only through the displayed Hilbert classifier, scalar-functional classifier, representing-vector transport, scalar evaluation transport, norm-control transport, projection and Riesz--Fischer handoff transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows. \begin{theorem}[Frechet--Riesz NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:frechet-riesz-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\FrechetRieszUp$ carrier $F=(H,I,L,V,E,K,P,R,T,C,Q,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\FrechetRieszUp}$ surface consists exactly of these finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item Hilbert source admission through $H$ and inner-product admission through $I$;
\item scalar-functional admission through the $\BoundedLinearFunctionalUp$ row $L$;
\item representing-vector candidate admission through the carried endpoint row $V$;
\item scalar evaluation exactness through the displayed ledger $E$ over the $\RealUp$ endpoint seal;
\item norm-control exactness through $K$, binding the functional bound and representing-vector norm without a host supremum;
\item projection handoff through $P$, routed through $\OrthogonalProjectionTheoremUp$ before any kernel-orthogonality read is public;
\item Riesz--Fischer handoff through $R$, routed through coefficient-window and completion data before any Hilbert completion read is public;
\item componentwise transport, replay, provenance, and local naming through $T,C,Q,N$;
\item non-escape from ambient Hilbert-dual isomorphisms, quotient functional equality, selected representatives, unrestricted Hahn--Banach separation, and hidden closed-subspace projection data.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier rows. The analytic surface is exactly the Hilbert source $H$, the inner-product row $I$, the scalar-functional row $L$, the representing-vector candidate $V$, the scalar evaluation ledger $E$, the norm-control row $K$, and the two handoff rows $P,R$.

The bounded functional is read before the representing vector is exposed. Its scalar action and bound pass through $\BoundedLinearFunctionalUp$ and $\RealUp$, while the comparison with $\langle x,V\rangle$ is recorded only in the displayed ledger $E$ over carried Hilbert endpoints.

The two theorem-facing handoffs are then forced to stay finite. The projection handoff $P$ reads kernel-facing orthogonality through $\OrthogonalProjectionTheoremUp$, and the Riesz--Fischer handoff $R$ reads completion-facing data through coefficient windows and displayed Hilbert rows.

Finally attach $T,C,Q,N$. These rows transport, replay, source, and locally name the same packet, and no remaining coordinate can manufacture a host dual-space isomorphism, quotient functional equality, selected representative, unrestricted Hahn--Banach witness, or hidden projection theorem. Hence the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}
