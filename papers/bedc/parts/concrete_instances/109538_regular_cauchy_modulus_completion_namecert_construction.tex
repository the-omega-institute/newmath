\begin{definition}[RegularCauchyModulusCompletion carrier]
\label{def:regular-cauchy-modulus-completion-carrier}

$$

\begin{enumerate}

\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window schedule opened by the modulus request.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular rational readback extracted from $S$ through $D$.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal reached only after $D,S,R$ have been displayed.
\item $B$ is the $\SeparatedCompletionUp$ boundary row, read only after the regular Cauchy data and terminal seal are visible.
\item $A$ is the modulus-stability row. It records that later windows replay the same dyadic bound discipline and do not replace $D,S,R$ by a hidden limit object.

\begin{theorem}[RegularCauchyModulusCompletion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-modulus-completion-namecert-obligations}

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-modulus-completion-carrier}. The projection exposes the dyadic ledger $D$, stream windows $S$, regular readback $R$, terminal seal $E$, separated boundary $B$, modulus-stability row $A$, and structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

The numerical route is finite. The dyadic ledger opens only the bounded windows named by $S$, and $R$ reads those windows as regular rational data before any Real-facing seal is available.

The separated-completion row does not choose a representative. It receives only the already displayed $D,S,R,E$ route, while $A$ records that later completion reads replay the same modulus bounds rather than replacing them by a hidden limit.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same route. Since the carrier lists no quotient representative, selected limit, host equality, choice principle, trichotomy split, or ambient completeness row, the local NameCert exports none of those objects.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegularCauchyModulusCompletion separation route]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-modulus-completion-separation}
Every separated completion or Real consumer of an accepted $\RegularCauchyModulusCompletionUp$ carrier factors through the displayed route
$$
D,S,R \longmapsto E \longmapsto B,A \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
Thus separation is read only after the DyadicRatCore tolerance ledger, StreamName window schedule, RegSeqRat readback, and Real seal have been displayed, and modulus stability remains visible through every completion-facing handoff.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the accepted carrier $M=(D,S,R,E,B,A,H,C,P,N)$. The only numerical data available to a completion consumer are the dyadic ledger, finite windows, and regular rational readback.

The terminal Real seal $E$ is downstream from those rows. A consumer therefore cannot inspect Real-facing data until the regular Cauchy modulus route $D,S,R$ has already been exposed.

The separated boundary $B$ and modulus-stability row $A$ then attach to that same route. They enforce separation and stability as displayed finite rows, not as quotient equality or a selected completed point.

The rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve the factorization. Since no additional completion coordinate exists in the carrier, every separated completion or Real consumer receives exactly the displayed handoff and no ambient completeness principle.
\end{proof}
