\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\UniformCompleteSpaceUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-uniform-complete-space-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bourbaki and Kelley on uniform spaces, the $\UniformCompleteSpaceUp$
packet records the displayed completeness property of a uniform-space carrier
as finite Cauchy-filter convergence data. It sits after the completion
interfaces of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metriccompletion-namecert} and
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformcompletion-namecert}, reads the
Cauchy-filter and Cauchy-net surfaces of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyfilter-namecert} and
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchynet-namecert}, and keeps separated
comparison available through
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separatedmetric-namecert}. The packet
generalizes the metric-completion route by recording convergence of displayed
uniform Cauchy data without constructing quotient completions, choosing
representatives, invoking countable choice, importing open-cover compactness,
or asserting completeness of $\RealUp$.

\begin{definition}[Uniform complete-space carrier]
\label{def:uniform-complete-space-carrier}
A $\UniformCompleteSpaceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
U=(S,F,N,M,C,Q,R,E,H,T,P,A)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the underlying uniform-space row, carrying the finite entourage
filterbase, diagonal, refinement, symmetry, and composition data inspected by
completion-facing consumers.
\item $F$ is the $\CauchyFilterUp$ row admitted over $S$.
\item $N$ is the $\CauchyNetUp$ row giving the directed-window presentation of
the same Cauchy demand when a net-style consumer is used.
\item $M$ is the $\MetricCompletionUp$ comparison row available when the
uniform data is read through a displayed metric-completion presentation.
\item $C$ is the $\UniformCompletionUp$ comparison row available when the
uniform data is read through the existing uniform-completion presentation.
\item $Q$ is the convergence boundary row asserting that the displayed Cauchy
filter has a completion-facing limit route inside the packet.
\item $R$ is the regular rational readback row exposed only after $S,F,N,M,C,Q$
have been displayed.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row for numerical readback consumers.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the uniform surface,
Cauchy data, completion comparisons, convergence boundary, readback, seal,
continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $T$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a finite entourage request
through Cauchy-filter or Cauchy-net data, completion comparison, convergence
boundary, readback, and seal.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\UniformCompleteSpaceUp$,
$\UniformCompletionUp$, $\MetricCompletionUp$, $\CauchyFilterUp$,
$\CauchyNetUp$, $\SeparatedMetricUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$,
$\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $A$ is the local $\NameCert_{\UniformCompleteSpaceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through these displayed rows. It
has no coordinate for quotient completions, selected representatives, countable
choice, open-cover compactness, host uniform equality, or $\RealUp$
completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Uniform complete-space NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:uniform-complete-space-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\UniformCompleteSpaceUp$ carrier
$U=(S,F,N,M,C,Q,R,E,H,T,P,A)$, the local
$\NameCert_{\UniformCompleteSpaceUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following
obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed uniform-space row, Cauchy-filter
row, Cauchy-net row, metric-completion comparison, uniform-completion
comparison, convergence boundary, regular readback, Real seal, transport,
replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item Cauchy request factorization, requiring every accepted completion-facing
read to begin with the same finite entourage data in $S$ and then pass through
either $F$ or $N$ before any completion comparison is exposed;
\item completion comparison discipline, requiring $M$ and $C$ to compare only
the displayed metric and uniform completion presentations of the same Cauchy
route;
\item Cauchy-filter seal routing, requiring $Q$ to remain downstream of
$S,F,N,M,C$ and upstream of only $R,E,H,T,P,A$;
\item separated comparison non-escape, allowing separated reads only through
the provenance named by $P$ and never through host equality or quotient
completion equality;
\item ledger non-escape, excluding quotient completions, selected
representatives, countable choice, open-cover compactness, and $\RealUp$
completeness.
\end{enumerate}
No local NameCert obligation is stored outside those rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from
\autoref{def:uniform-complete-space-carrier}. The projection exposes exactly
$S,F,N,M,C,Q,R,E,H,T,P,A$.

Cauchy request factorization begins with the finite entourage surface $S$.
The request is then read through the displayed filter row $F$ or the directed
net row $N$, so a completion-facing consumer receives Cauchy evidence before
it sees any completion comparison.

The completion comparison rows $M$ and $C$ are downstream of that Cauchy route.
They compare the metric-completion and uniform-completion presentations of the
same displayed request, and the convergence boundary $Q$ is sealed only after
those presentations have been read.

Finally $R,E,H,T,P,A$ provide readback, terminal sealing, componentwise
transport, replay, provenance, and local naming for the same route. Since no
listed row is a quotient completion, selected representative, choice schedule,
open-cover compactness certificate, or proof of $\RealUp$ completeness, the
local NameCert surface cannot export any of those objects.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Uniform complete-space Cauchy-filter seal]
\label{thm:uniform-complete-space-cauchy-filter-seal}
Let $U=(S,F,N,M,C,Q,R,E,H,T,P,A)$ be an accepted
$\UniformCompleteSpaceUp$ carrier. Every Cauchy-filter convergence read factors
through the displayed route
$$
S \longmapsto F,N \longmapsto M,C \longmapsto Q \longmapsto R,E \longmapsto H,T,P,A .
$$
Thus the packet seals the finite Cauchy-filter route for uniform completeness
without adding quotient completions, selected representatives, countable
choice, open-cover compactness, or $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a Cauchy-filter convergence read. The uniform-space row $S$ fixes
the finite entourage demand before any Cauchy or completion row is inspected.

The Cauchy data is then displayed by $F$ and, when a directed presentation is
needed, by $N$. Both rows remain tied to the same request from $S$.

The comparison rows $M$ and $C$ read the metric-completion and
uniform-completion presentations of that displayed Cauchy route. The boundary
row $Q$ is downstream of those comparisons, so the convergence seal is attached
to the visible filter and net data rather than to a host completed object.

The readback and terminal rows $R$ and $E$ are exposed only after $Q$, while
$H,T,P,A$ transport, replay, source, and name the same route. The carrier
therefore has no row from which quotient completions, selected representatives,
countable choice, open-cover compactness, or $\RealUp$ completeness could be
read.
\end{proof}
