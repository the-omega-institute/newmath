\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\SubgroupUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-subgroup-namecert}

The subgroup interface $\SubgroupUp$ packages a subset of a group carrier closed under the group operation, identity, and inverse. It depends on $\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-group-namecert}) and is the first sub-object construction in BEDC, prerequisite to the quotient and ideal certificates that follow.

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:subgroup-carrier}

\begin{definition}[Centralizer SubgroupUp instance]
\label{def:subgroup-centralizer-instance}
Fix a BEDC history-level $\GroupUp$ source over $\mathsf{BHist}$ with
operation $\mathsf{mul}$, unit $e$, inverse operation $\mathsf{inv}$, and
fixed element $a:\mathsf{BHist}$. The centralizer $\SubgroupUp$ instance uses
the carrier $Z_a$ from \autoref{def:group-centralizer-carrier}. Its classifier
is the restriction of $\hsame$ to that carrier: two carried histories $x$ and
$y$ represent the same subgroup element exactly when $x\hsame y$. The
operation, unit, and inverse are inherited from the ambient $\GroupUp$ source,
and the ledger records the fixed element $a$ together with the commuting
witnesses certifying membership in $Z_a$.
\end{definition}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:subgroup-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[Centralizer SubgroupUp certificate target]
\label{thm:subgroup-centralizer-certificate-target}
For a BEDC history-level $\GroupUp$ operation over $\mathsf{BHist}$ satisfying
associativity, left and right unit laws for $e$, multiplication congruence, and
two-sided inverse laws, the instance of
\autoref{def:subgroup-centralizer-instance} satisfies the $\SubgroupUp$ rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item the carrier is inhabited by $e$, and $Z_a(e)$;
\item if $Z_a(x)$ and $Z_a(y)$, then $Z_a(\mathsf{mul}(x,y))$;
\item if $Z_a(x)$, then $Z_a(\mathsf{inv}(x))$;
\item if $Z_a(x)$ and $x\hsame y$, then $Z_a(y)$;
\item for every $x:\mathsf{BHist}$,
$$
Z_a(x) \Longleftrightarrow \mathsf{comm}(x,a)\hsame e.
$$
\end{enumerate}
Thus the centralizer carrier is a concrete certificate target for
$\NameCert_{\SubgroupUp}$ over the ambient history classifier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The inhabited, product-closure, inverse-closure, and commutator-fiber rows are
the rows of \autoref{thm:group-centralizer-subgroup}. The inverse row uses the
ambient inverse congruence from
\autoref{thm:group-inverse-congruence-from-laws} and the reverse-product
description of inverses from \autoref{thm:group-inverse-mul-reverse}; these
are exactly the sources cited by the GroupUp centralizer proof.

For carrier transport, unfold $Z_a$. A witness $x\hsame y$ transports
$\mathsf{mul}(x,a)\hsame\mathsf{mul}(a,x)$ to
$\mathsf{mul}(y,a)\hsame\mathsf{mul}(a,y)$ by multiplication congruence on the
left and right arguments, followed by symmetry and transitivity of $\hsame$.
Therefore the restricted classifier preserves the subgroup carrier.

The exactness row is the commutator criterion from
\autoref{thm:group-commutator-trivial-iff-commutes} applied to the ordered
pair $(x,a)$, with the definition of $Z_a$ supplying the displayed
orientation. Repacking these rows with the inherited operation, unit, inverse,
and ledger gives the advertised $\SubgroupUp$ certificate target.
\end{proof}
