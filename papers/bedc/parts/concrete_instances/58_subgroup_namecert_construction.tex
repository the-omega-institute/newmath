\begin{theorem}[Centralizer normalizer quotient classifiers coincide]
\label{thm:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-quotient-classifiers-coincide}
For a BEDC history-level $\GroupUp$ operation over $\mathsf{BHist}$
satisfying the same associativity, unit, multiplication-congruence,
carrier-transport, and two-sided inverse laws as in
\autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-tower-certificate}, every
$x,y:\mathsf{BHist}$ satisfies
$$
Q_{Z_a\leq N_a}(x,y)\Longleftrightarrow
Q_{Z_a\triangleleft N_a}(x,y).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the forward direction, unpack
$Q_{Z_a\leq N_a}(x,y)$. The endpoint clauses give $N_a(x)$ and $N_a(y)$, and
the right-witness clause gives a history $z$ with $Z_a(z)$ and
$\hsame(y,\mathsf{mul}(x,z))$.

It remains to produce the kernel word for
$Q_{Z_a\triangleleft N_a}(x,y)$. Multiply the endpoint comparison on the left
by $\mathsf{inv}(x)$. Associativity, the left inverse law, the unit law, and
multiplication congruence identify
$\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{inv}(x),y)$ with $z$ up to $\hsame$. The carrier
transport row for $Z_a$ from
\autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-tower-certificate} moves the
witness $Z_a(z)$ to the displayed relative kernel word.

For the reverse direction, unpack
$Q_{Z_a\triangleleft N_a}(x,y)$. The endpoint clauses already give the
$N_a(x)$ and $N_a(y)$ clauses needed by $Q_{Z_a\leq N_a}(x,y)$, and the kernel
clause gives $Z_a(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{inv}(x),y))$.

Choose the right witness
$z:=\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{inv}(x),y)$. Associativity, the left inverse law, the
unit law, and multiplication congruence identify
$\mathsf{mul}(x,z)$ with $y$ up to $\hsame$, so the kernel clause supplies the
required $Z_a(z)$ witness and the endpoint comparison repacks the
coset-witness classifier.

The ten-field fit is the ordinary quotient coset/kernel classifier
equivalence for the centralizer-normalizer tower: the BEDC source is
$\GroupUp$ plus $\SubgroupUp$ over $\mathsf{BHist}$; the carrier is $N_a$; the
classifier is either displayed predicate; stability comes from the tower
certificate, inverse laws, unit laws, multiplication congruence, and carrier
transport; the ledger records the right witness and the relative-kernel word;
the core theorem is this statement; exactness is the displayed equivalence;
the downstream consumer is $\QuotientGroupUp$; and the status is
$\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{proof}
