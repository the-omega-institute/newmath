\begin{theorem}[Centralizer quotient classifier kernel equivalence]
\label{thm:subgroup-centralizer-quotient-classifier-kernel-iff}
For a BEDC history-level $\GroupUp$ operation over $\mathsf{BHist}$
satisfying the same associativity, unit, multiplication-congruence,
carrier-transport, and two-sided inverse laws as in
\autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-tower-certificate}, every
$x,y:\mathsf{BHist}$ satisfies
$$
Q^{\mathrm{std}}_{Z_a\leq N_a}(x,y)\Longleftrightarrow
Q^{\mathrm{ker}}_{Z_a\triangleleft N_a}(x,y).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the forward direction, unpack the standard quotient classifier. The
endpoint clauses give $N_a(x)$ and $N_a(y)$, and the quotient witness gives a
centralizer element whose right multiplication carries $x$ to $y$.

It remains to expose the relative kernel word. Multiply the endpoint
comparison on the left by $\mathsf{inv}(x)$; associativity, the left inverse
law, the unit law, and multiplication congruence identify the witness with
$\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{inv}(x),y)$ up to $\hsame$. Carrier transport for $Z_a$
then moves centralizer membership to the displayed kernel carrier.

For the reverse direction, start from the relative kernel classifier. Its
endpoint clauses are already the two normalizer clauses needed by the standard
quotient classifier.

Choose the quotient witness $z:=\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{inv}(x),y)$. Associativity
and the right inverse law identify $\mathsf{mul}(x,z)$ with $y$ up to
$\hsame$, so the kernel clause supplies the carried centralizer witness and
the classifier repacks.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SubgroupUp.SubgroupCentralizerQuotientClassifier\_kernel\_iff}

\begin{theorem}[Centralizer quotient presentation equivalence package]
\label{thm:subgroup-centralizer-quotient-presentation-equivalence-package}
For a BEDC history-level $\GroupUp$ operation over $\mathsf{BHist}$
satisfying the same associativity, unit, multiplication-congruence,
carrier-transport, and two-sided inverse laws as in
\autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-tower-certificate}, every
$x,y:\mathsf{BHist}$ has a quotient presentation independent of the chosen
centralizer-normalizer classifier. The standard quotient classifier
$Q^{\mathrm{std}}_{Z_a\leq N_a}(x,y)$, the right-witness classifier
$Q^{\mathrm{wit}}_{Z_a\leq N_a}(x,y)$, the right-coset classifier
$Q^{\mathrm{cos}}_{Z_a\leq N_a}(x,y)$, and the relative-kernel classifier
$Q^{\mathrm{ker}}_{Z_a\triangleleft N_a}(x,y)$ are mutually pinned by the
three displayed equivalence rows
$$
Q^{\mathrm{std}}_{Z_a\leq N_a}(x,y)\Longleftrightarrow
Q^{\mathrm{ker}}_{Z_a\triangleleft N_a}(x,y),
$$
$$
Q^{\mathrm{wit}}_{Z_a\leq N_a}(x,y)\Longleftrightarrow
Q^{\mathrm{ker}}_{Z_a\triangleleft N_a}(x,y),
$$
$$
Q^{\mathrm{cos}}_{Z_a\leq N_a}(x,y)\Longleftrightarrow
Q^{\mathrm{ker}}_{Z_a\triangleleft N_a}(x,y),
$$
and the derived equivalence between the right-witness and right-coset
presentations obtained through the shared quotient-kernel classifier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply Theorem~\ref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-quotient-classifier-kernel-iff}
to identify the standard classifier with the relative-kernel classifier.
Apply Theorem~\ref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-quotient-kernel-witness-classifier-iff}
to identify the right-witness classifier with the same kernel classifier.

Apply Theorem~\ref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-right-coset-quotient-kernel-iff}
to identify the right-coset classifier with that kernel classifier. Apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-right-quotient-classifier-kernel-iff}
for the parallel right quotient presentation.

The right-witness/right-coset equivalence is obtained by composing the
right-witness-to-kernel implication with the kernel-to-right-coset implication,
and conversely composing the right-coset-to-kernel implication with the
kernel-to-right-witness implication. Thus all four presentations classify the
same pairs $(x,y)$ in the normalizer carrier $N_a$.

The ten-field fit is: traditional target quotient presentation independence;
BEDC source $\GroupUp$ plus $\SubgroupUp$ over $\mathsf{BHist}$; carrier
$N_a$; classifier the shared quotient-kernel relation; stability the checked
equivalence rows above; ledger right witness, coset witness, and relative
kernel word; core theorem this package; exactness mutual presentation
equivalence; downstream consumer $\QuotientGroupUp$; status
$\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{proof}

$$
Q_{Z_a\leq N_a}(x,y)\land Q_{Z_a\leq N_a}(y,z)
\Longrightarrow Q_{Z_a\leq N_a}(x,z).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

classifier using the centralizer-normalizer quotient classifier equivalence.
The kernel classifier is transitive by the empty-unit centralizer quotient
kernel theorem: the two relative kernel words multiply, and the middle
endpoint cancels by associativity, unit, congruence, and inverse laws. Convert
the resulting kernel classifier back to the right-witness classifier.
\end{proof}

satisfying associativity, two-sided unit, multiplication-congruence, and

$$
\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{inv}(u),p),u),q)

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

inverse conjugation word
$\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{inv}(u),p),

carrier witness to
$\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{inv}(u),p),u)$. Product closure for
$Z_a$ then multiplies the transported conjugation witness with the carried
element $q$, giving the displayed product witness carrier.
\end{proof}

$Q_{Z_a\triangleleft N_a}(\mathsf{e},x)$.

\begin{definition}[Trivial SubgroupUp carrier]
\label{def:subgroup-trivial-carrier}
Fix a BEDC history-level $\GroupUp$ source over $\mathsf{BHist}$ with operation $\mathsf{mul}$, unit $e$, inverse operation $\mathsf{inv}$, and ambient classifier $\hsame$. Define
$$
\mathsf{Triv}_G(x) :\Longleftrightarrow x\hsame e.
$$
The inherited classifier on this carrier is the carrier-aware restriction of $\hsame$:
$$
\mathsf{TrivRel}_G(x,y) :\Longleftrightarrow
\mathsf{Triv}_G(x)\land\mathsf{Triv}_G(y)\land x\hsame y.
$$
The operation, unit, and inverse are inherited unchanged from the ambient $\GroupUp$ source.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Trivial subgroup SubgroupUp certificate]
\label{thm:subgroup-trivial-certificate}
For a BEDC history-level $\GroupUp$ operation over $\mathsf{BHist}$ satisfying the monoid stability rows of \autoref{def:monoid-stability-certificate} and the inverse rows of \autoref{def:group-stability-certificate}, the carrier and classifier of \autoref{def:subgroup-trivial-carrier} satisfy the concrete $\SubgroupUp$ certificate rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $\mathsf{TrivRel}_G$ is a semantic naming classifier on $\mathsf{Triv}_G$.
\item $\mathsf{Triv}_G(e)$.
\item If $\mathsf{Triv}_G(x)$ and $\mathsf{Triv}_G(y)$, then $\mathsf{Triv}_G(\mathsf{mul}(x,y))$.
\item If $\mathsf{Triv}_G(x)$, then $\mathsf{Triv}_G(\mathsf{inv}(x))$.
\item If $\mathsf{Triv}_G(x)$ and $x\hsame y$, then $\mathsf{Triv}_G(y)$.
\item If $\mathsf{TrivRel}_G(x,x')$ and $\mathsf{TrivRel}_G(y,y')$, then $\mathsf{TrivRel}_G(\mathsf{mul}(x,y),\mathsf{mul}(x',y'))$.
\item If $\mathsf{TrivRel}_G(x,y)$, then $\mathsf{TrivRel}_G(\mathsf{inv}(x),\mathsf{inv}(y))$.
\end{enumerate}
Thus the singleton fiber of the group unit carries a $\SubgroupUp$ certificate over the inherited classifier.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SubgroupUp.SubgroupTrivialCarrier\_certificate}
\begin{proof}
The semantic classifier row is inherited from $\hsame$. Reflexivity on a carried endpoint $x$ packages the carrier witness $\mathsf{Triv}_G(x)$ twice with $x\hsame x$. Symmetry and transitivity unpack the stored $\hsame$ comparisons, apply the corresponding ambient law, and repack the endpoint carrier fields already present in the classifier witnesses.

The identity row is the reflexive witness $e\hsame e$, which is exactly $\mathsf{Triv}_G(e)$ by \autoref{def:subgroup-trivial-carrier}.

For product closure, assume $\mathsf{Triv}_G(x)$ and $\mathsf{Triv}_G(y)$. These are the comparisons $x\hsame e$ and $y\hsame e$. Multiplication congruence from the monoid stability rows gives
$$
\mathsf{mul}(x,y)\hsame\mathsf{mul}(e,e).
$$
The left identity law at $e$ gives $\mathsf{mul}(e,e)\hsame e$. Transitivity proves $\mathsf{mul}(x,y)\hsame e$, hence $\mathsf{Triv}_G(\mathsf{mul}(x,y))$.

For inverse closure, assume $\mathsf{Triv}_G(x)$, so $x\hsame e$. The derived inverse congruence theorem \autoref{thm:group-inverse-congruence-from-laws} gives
$$
\mathsf{inv}(x)\hsame\mathsf{inv}(e).
$$
The identity-inverse theorem \autoref{thm:group-inverse-identity} gives $\mathsf{inv}(e)\hsame e$. Transitivity proves $\mathsf{Triv}_G(\mathsf{inv}(x))$.

For carrier transport, assume $\mathsf{Triv}_G(x)$ and $x\hsame y$. Symmetry gives $y\hsame x$, and transitivity with $x\hsame e$ gives $y\hsame e$. This is $\mathsf{Triv}_G(y)$.

For multiplication compatibility of the inherited classifier, unpack the two $\mathsf{TrivRel}_G$ witnesses. Product closure supplies the two endpoint carrier rows for $\mathsf{mul}(x,y)$ and $\mathsf{mul}(x',y')$, while multiplication congruence transports the stored comparisons $x\hsame x'$ and $y\hsame y'$ to the product comparison. These three fields repack $\mathsf{TrivRel}_G(\mathsf{mul}(x,y),\mathsf{mul}(x',y'))$.

For inverse compatibility, unpack $\mathsf{TrivRel}_G(x,y)$. Inverse closure supplies the endpoint carrier rows for $\mathsf{inv}(x)$ and $\mathsf{inv}(y)$, and \autoref{thm:group-inverse-congruence-from-laws} transports the stored comparison $x\hsame y$ to $\mathsf{inv}(x)\hsame\mathsf{inv}(y)$. Repacking gives the displayed inverse-classifier row.

These are the identity, product, inverse, transport, and restricted-classifier fields required by the $\SubgroupUp$ row format used in \autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-certificate-target}.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Subgroup chain composition certificate]
\label{thm:subgroup-chain-composition-certificate}
For a BEDC history-level $\GroupUp$ operation over $\mathsf{BHist}$ with
operation $\mathsf{mul}$, unit $e$, inverse operation $\mathsf{inv}$, and
ambient classifier $\hsame$, assume the monoid stability rows of
\autoref{def:monoid-stability-certificate}, the inverse rows of
\autoref{def:group-stability-certificate}, and the history-sameness equivalence
of \autoref{thm:history-sameness-equivalence}. Let
$P_K,P_H:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be carrier predicates and define
$$
P_{H\leq G}(x):\Longleftrightarrow P_K(x)\land P_H(x),
$$
$$
R_{H\leq G}(x,y):\Longleftrightarrow
P_{H\leq G}(x)\land P_{H\leq G}(y)\land x\hsame y,
$$
and
$$
R_H^K(x,y):\Longleftrightarrow
P_K(x)\land P_H(x)\land P_K(y)\land P_H(y)\land x\hsame y.
$$
Assume the $P_K$ carrier has the ambient $\SubgroupUp$ carrier rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $P_K(e)$.
\item If $P_K(x)$ and $P_K(y)$, then $P_K(\mathsf{mul}(x,y))$.
\item If $P_K(x)$, then $P_K(\mathsf{inv}(x))$.
\item If $P_K(x)$ and $x\hsame y$, then $P_K(y)$.
\end{enumerate}
Assume $P_H$ has the following rows over the $P_K$-restricted carrier:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $P_H(e)$.
\item If $P_K(x)$, $P_H(x)$, $P_K(y)$, and $P_H(y)$, then
$P_H(\mathsf{mul}(x,y))$.
\item If $P_K(x)$ and $P_H(x)$, then $P_H(\mathsf{inv}(x))$.
\item If $P_K(x)$, $P_K(y)$, $P_H(x)$, and $x\hsame y$, then $P_H(y)$.
\item If $R_H^K(x,x')$ and $R_H^K(y,y')$, then
$R_H^K(\mathsf{mul}(x,y),\mathsf{mul}(x',y'))$.
\item If $R_H^K(x,y)$, then
$R_H^K(\mathsf{inv}(x),\mathsf{inv}(y))$.
\end{enumerate}
Then the carrier $P_{H\leq G}$ with restricted classifier $R_{H\leq G}$
satisfies the concrete $\SubgroupUp$ certificate rows over $G$ in the row
format of \autoref{thm:subgroup-trivial-certificate} and
\autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-certificate-target}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The relation $R_H^K$ is the same restricted relation as $R_{H\leq G}$ after
reassociating the endpoint carrier conjunctions. Thus either presentation
stores exactly two endpoint witnesses for $P_K\land P_H$ and one ambient
$\hsame$ comparison.

First verify the semantic naming classifier required by
\autoref{def:lean-carrier-naming-certificate}. If $P_{H\leq G}(x)$, ambient
reflexivity gives $x\hsame x$, so $R_{H\leq G}(x,x)$ holds. If
$R_{H\leq G}(x,y)$ holds, swap the two endpoint carrier witnesses and apply
symmetry of $\hsame$ to obtain $R_{H\leq G}(y,x)$. If
$R_{H\leq G}(x,y)$ and $R_{H\leq G}(y,z)$ hold, keep the stored carrier
witnesses for $x$ and $z$ and compose $x\hsame y$ with $y\hsame z$ by
transitivity of $\hsame$. Hence $R_{H\leq G}$ is a semantic naming classifier
on the conjunction carrier.

For the identity row, pair the ambient $P_K(e)$ row with the internal
$P_H(e)$ row. This is exactly $P_{H\leq G}(e)$.

For product closure, assume $P_{H\leq G}(x)$ and $P_{H\leq G}(y)$. Unfolding
these hypotheses gives $P_K(x)$, $P_H(x)$, $P_K(y)$, and $P_H(y)$. The
$P_K$ product row gives $P_K(\mathsf{mul}(x,y))$, while the internal $P_H$
product row gives $P_H(\mathsf{mul}(x,y))$. Pairing the two conclusions gives
$P_{H\leq G}(\mathsf{mul}(x,y))$.

For inverse closure, unfold $P_{H\leq G}(x)$ into $P_K(x)$ and $P_H(x)$. The
$P_K$ inverse row gives $P_K(\mathsf{inv}(x))$, and the internal $P_H$ inverse
row gives $P_H(\mathsf{inv}(x))$. Together these prove
$P_{H\leq G}(\mathsf{inv}(x))$.

For carrier transport, assume $P_{H\leq G}(x)$ and $x\hsame y$. The $P_K$
transport row first gives $P_K(y)$. With $P_K(x)$, this target $P_K(y)$,
$P_H(x)$, and the same comparison $x\hsame y$, the internal $P_H$ transport
row gives $P_H(y)$. Therefore $P_{H\leq G}(y)$.

For multiplication compatibility of the restricted classifier, unpack the two
$R_{H\leq G}$ witnesses as $R_H^K(x,x')$ and $R_H^K(y,y')$. The internal
restricted-classifier product row gives
$R_H^K(\mathsf{mul}(x,y),\mathsf{mul}(x',y'))$, which is precisely
$R_{H\leq G}(\mathsf{mul}(x,y),\mathsf{mul}(x',y'))$ after unfolding the
conjunction carrier.

For inverse compatibility, unpack $R_{H\leq G}(x,y)$ as $R_H^K(x,y)$. The
internal restricted-classifier inverse row gives
$R_H^K(\mathsf{inv}(x),\mathsf{inv}(y))$, equivalently
$R_{H\leq G}(\mathsf{inv}(x),\mathsf{inv}(y))$.

These are exactly the semantic classifier, identity, product, inverse,
carrier-transport, multiplication-classifier, and inverse-classifier rows of a
$\SubgroupUp$ certificate over the original ambient group $G$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Subgroup one-step criterion]
\label{thm:subgroup-one-step-criterion}
For a BEDC history-level $\GroupUp$ operation over $\mathsf{BHist}$ with operation $\mathsf{mul}$, unit $e$, inverse operation $\mathsf{inv}$, and ambient classifier $\hsame$, assume the monoid stability rows of \autoref{def:monoid-stability-certificate} and the inverse rows of \autoref{def:group-stability-certificate}. Let $P:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be a carrier predicate, and let the inherited restricted classifier be
$$
R_P(x,y):\Longleftrightarrow P(x)\land P(y)\land x\hsame y.
$$
Assume:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $P$ is inhabited: $P(a)$ for some $a:\mathsf{BHist}$.
\item $P$ is stable under the ambient classifier: if $P(x)$ and $x\hsame y$, then $P(y)$.
\item $P$ is closed under the one-step relative product: if $P(x)$ and $P(y)$, then $P(\mathsf{mul}(x,\mathsf{inv}(y)))$.
\end{enumerate}
Then the carrier $P$ with classifier $R_P$ satisfies the concrete $\SubgroupUp$ identity, product, inverse, and carrier-transport rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $P(e)$.
\item If $P(x)$ and $P(y)$, then $P(\mathsf{mul}(x,y))$.
\item If $P(x)$, then $P(\mathsf{inv}(x))$.
\item If $P(x)$ and $x\hsame y$, then $P(y)$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Choose an inhabitant $a$ with $P(a)$. The relative-product row applied to $a$ and $a$ gives $P(\mathsf{mul}(a,\mathsf{inv}(a)))$. The right inverse row from \autoref{def:group-stability-certificate} identifies this endpoint with $e$:
$$
\mathsf{mul}(a,\mathsf{inv}(a))\hsame e.
$$
Classifier stability for $P$ transports the witness to $P(e)$, proving the identity row.

For inverse closure, assume $P(x)$. The identity row gives $P(e)$, so the relative-product row applied to $e$ and $x$ gives $P(\mathsf{mul}(e,\mathsf{inv}(x)))$. The left identity row from \autoref{def:monoid-stability-certificate} gives
$$
\mathsf{mul}(e,\mathsf{inv}(x))\hsame\mathsf{inv}(x),
$$
and carrier stability transports the witness to $P(\mathsf{inv}(x))$.

For product closure, assume $P(x)$ and $P(y)$. The inverse row just obtained gives $P(\mathsf{inv}(y))$. Apply the relative-product row to $x$ and $\mathsf{inv}(y)$ to get
$$
P(\mathsf{mul}(x,\mathsf{inv}(\mathsf{inv}(y)))).
$$
By \autoref{thm:group-left-inverse-involutive}, $\mathsf{inv}(\mathsf{inv}(y))\hsame y$. Multiplication congruence from \autoref{def:monoid-stability-certificate}, together with reflexivity on $x$, gives
$$
\mathsf{mul}(x,\mathsf{inv}(\mathsf{inv}(y)))\hsame\mathsf{mul}(x,y).
$$
Carrier stability transports the displayed witness to $P(\mathsf{mul}(x,y))$.

The transport row is exactly the classifier-stability hypothesis on $P$. These four conclusions are the carrier rows in the $\SubgroupUp$ row format used in \autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-certificate-target} and \autoref{thm:subgroup-trivial-certificate}.
\end{proof}
