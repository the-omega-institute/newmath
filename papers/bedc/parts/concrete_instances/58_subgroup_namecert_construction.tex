\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\SubgroupUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-subgroup-namecert}

The subgroup interface $\SubgroupUp$ packages a subset of a group carrier closed under the group operation, identity, and inverse. It depends on $\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-group-namecert}) and is the first sub-object construction in BEDC, prerequisite to the quotient and ideal certificates that follow.

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:subgroup-carrier}

\begin{definition}[Centralizer SubgroupUp instance]
\label{def:subgroup-centralizer-instance}
Fix a BEDC history-level $\GroupUp$ source over $\mathsf{BHist}$ with
operation $\mathsf{mul}$, unit $e$, inverse operation $\mathsf{inv}$, and
fixed element $a:\mathsf{BHist}$. The centralizer $\SubgroupUp$ instance uses
the carrier $Z_a$ from \autoref{def:group-centralizer-carrier}. Its classifier
is the restriction of $\hsame$ to that carrier: two carried histories $x$ and
$y$ represent the same subgroup element exactly when $x\hsame y$. The
operation, unit, and inverse are inherited from the ambient $\GroupUp$ source,
and the ledger records the fixed element $a$ together with the commuting
witnesses certifying membership in $Z_a$.
\end{definition}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:subgroup-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[Centralizer SubgroupUp certificate target]
\label{thm:subgroup-centralizer-certificate-target}
For a BEDC history-level $\GroupUp$ operation over $\mathsf{BHist}$ satisfying
associativity, left and right unit laws for $e$, multiplication congruence, and
two-sided inverse laws, the instance of
\autoref{def:subgroup-centralizer-instance} satisfies the $\SubgroupUp$ rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item the carrier is inhabited by $e$, and $Z_a(e)$;
\item if $Z_a(x)$ and $Z_a(y)$, then $Z_a(\mathsf{mul}(x,y))$;
\item if $Z_a(x)$, then $Z_a(\mathsf{inv}(x))$;
\item if $Z_a(x)$ and $x\hsame y$, then $Z_a(y)$;
\item for every $x:\mathsf{BHist}$,
$$
Z_a(x) \Longleftrightarrow \mathsf{comm}(x,a)\hsame e.
$$
\end{enumerate}
Thus the centralizer carrier is a concrete certificate target for
$\NameCert_{\SubgroupUp}$ over the ambient history classifier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The inhabited, product-closure, inverse-closure, and commutator-fiber rows are
the rows of \autoref{thm:group-centralizer-subgroup}. The inverse row uses the
ambient inverse congruence from
\autoref{thm:group-inverse-congruence-from-laws} and the reverse-product
description of inverses from \autoref{thm:group-inverse-mul-reverse}; these
are exactly the sources cited by the GroupUp centralizer proof.

For carrier transport, unfold $Z_a$. A witness $x\hsame y$ transports
$\mathsf{mul}(x,a)\hsame\mathsf{mul}(a,x)$ to
$\mathsf{mul}(y,a)\hsame\mathsf{mul}(a,y)$ by multiplication congruence on the
left and right arguments, followed by symmetry and transitivity of $\hsame$.
Therefore the restricted classifier preserves the subgroup carrier.

The exactness row is the commutator criterion from
\autoref{thm:group-commutator-trivial-iff-commutes} applied to the ordered
pair $(x,a)$, with the definition of $Z_a$ supplying the displayed
orientation. Repacking these rows with the inherited operation, unit, inverse,
and ledger gives the advertised $\SubgroupUp$ certificate target.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[RatUp FieldUp affine center subgroup carrier]
\label{def:ratup-fieldup-affine-center-subgroup-carrier}
For the concrete $\FieldUp(\RatUp)$ affine exit package of
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-canonical-exit-action-inverse-package}, take the
ambient group carrier to be $\mathsf{AffAut}_F$ with classifier
$\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}$, multiplication $\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}$, identity
$\mathrm{id}_F$, and inverse row $\iota_F$. The associated $\SubgroupUp$
carrier is the affine center predicate on that same group:
$$
\mathsf{Carrier}_{\SubgroupUp}(Z) := \mathsf{Center}_F(Z)
\qquad (Z:\mathsf{AffAut}_F).
$$
Thus membership in the subgroup certificate is exactly center membership in
the concrete FieldUp affine automorphism group.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[RatUp FieldUp affine center SubgroupUp instance]
\label{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-center-subgroup-instance}
The carrier of
\autoref{def:ratup-fieldup-affine-center-subgroup-carrier} instantiates the
concrete $\SubgroupUp$ certificate fields over $\mathsf{AffAut}_F$. Namely:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $\mathsf{Carrier}_{\SubgroupUp}(\mathrm{id}_F)$.
\item If $\mathsf{Carrier}_{\SubgroupUp}(Z)$ and
$\mathsf{Carrier}_{\SubgroupUp}(W)$, then
$\mathsf{Carrier}_{\SubgroupUp}(Z\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}W)$.
\item If $\mathsf{Carrier}_{\SubgroupUp}(Z)$, then
$\mathsf{Carrier}_{\SubgroupUp}(\iota_F(Z))$.
\item If $Z\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}W$ and
$\mathsf{Carrier}_{\SubgroupUp}(Z)$, then
$\mathsf{Carrier}_{\SubgroupUp}(W)$.
\item If $\mathsf{Carrier}_{\SubgroupUp}(Z)$, then for every
$T:\mathsf{AffAut}_F$,
$\mathsf{Carrier}_{\SubgroupUp}(\mathrm{Inn}_T(Z))$.
\end{enumerate}
The same data make this $\SubgroupUp$ object normal under inner affine
conjugation.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Fix the canonical exit action and inverse package of
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-canonical-exit-action-inverse-package}. This fixes
the ambient group operations $\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}$, $\mathrm{id}_F$,
$\iota_F$, $\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}$, and the inner affine conjugation row on
$\mathsf{AffAut}_F$.

By Definition~\ref{def:ratup-fieldup-affine-center-subgroup-carrier}, each
subgroup membership assertion is exactly the corresponding
$\mathsf{Center}_F$ assertion. Apply
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-center-normal-subgroup} to obtain identity
membership, product closure, inverse closure, classifier transport, and
normality under $\mathrm{Inn}_T$.

The classifier-transport field is compatible with the ambient ledger because
\autoref{thm:field-affine-pointwise-classifier-operation-congruence} supplies
operation and inverse congruence for $\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}$. Therefore the
five displayed clauses are precisely the concrete $\SubgroupUp$ certificate
fields over the FieldUp affine exit package.
\end{proof}

Per-object template: traditional target normal subgroup of the affine
automorphism group; BEDC source concrete $\FieldUp(\RatUp)$ plus $\GroupUp$;
carrier $\mathsf{Center}_F$; classifier $\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}$; stability
identity, product, inverse, classifier transport, and inner conjugation; ledger
affine operation and conjugation witnesses; core theorem list
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-center-subgroup-instance} and
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-center-normal-subgroup}; exactness predicate
center membership as the $\SubgroupUp$ carrier; standard bridge ordinary
affine center normal subgroup; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.

\begin{definition}[RatUp FieldUp affine normalizer SubgroupUp carrier]
\label{def:ratup-fieldup-affine-normalizer-subgroupup-carrier}
For Definition~\ref{def:ratup-fieldup-concrete-exit-object}, fix a certified
affine automorphism $A:\mathsf{AffAut}_F$ in the concrete $\FieldUp(\RatUp)$
exit object. Let $C_F(A)$ be the affine centralizer carrier and let
$N_F(C_F(A))$ be the two-sided affine centralizer normalizer carrier. The
corresponding $\SubgroupUp$ carrier is
$$
\mathsf{Carrier}^{N}_{\SubgroupUp}(T) := N_F(C_F(A))(T)
\qquad (T:\mathsf{AffAut}_F).
$$
It uses the ambient affine classifier $\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}$, operation
$\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}$, identity $\mathrm{id}_F$, and inverse $\iota_F$. Its
ledger records the fixed automorphism $A$, the centralizer membership tests
for $C_F(A)$, and the conjugation witnesses asserting preservation and
reflection of that centralizer under affine inner action.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[RatUp FieldUp affine normalizer SubgroupUp instance and center action]
\label{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-normalizer-subgroupup-inclusion-action}
The carrier of
\autoref{def:ratup-fieldup-affine-normalizer-subgroupup-carrier} is the
concrete $\SubgroupUp$ certificate target for the affine normalizer
$N_F(C_F(A))$ over $\mathsf{AffAut}_F$. Namely, for the fixed certified
$A:\mathsf{AffAut}_F$:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $\mathsf{Carrier}^{N}_{\SubgroupUp}(\mathrm{id}_F)$.
\item If $\mathsf{Carrier}^{N}_{\SubgroupUp}(T)$ and
$\mathsf{Carrier}^{N}_{\SubgroupUp}(U)$, then
$\mathsf{Carrier}^{N}_{\SubgroupUp}(T\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}U)$.
\item If $\mathsf{Carrier}^{N}_{\SubgroupUp}(T)$, then
$\mathsf{Carrier}^{N}_{\SubgroupUp}(\iota_F(T))$.
\item If $T\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}U$ and
$\mathsf{Carrier}^{N}_{\SubgroupUp}(T)$, then
$\mathsf{Carrier}^{N}_{\SubgroupUp}(U)$.
\item If $\mathsf{Center}_F(Z)$, then
$\mathsf{Carrier}^{N}_{\SubgroupUp}(Z)$.
\item If $C_F(A)(X)$ and $\mathsf{Center}_F(Z)$, then the restricted
normalizer action satisfies
$\mathrm{Act}_Z(X)\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}X$.
\end{enumerate}
Thus the affine center includes into this normalizer carrier and acts
pointwise as the classifier identity on the fixed centralizer.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
By Definition~\ref{def:ratup-fieldup-affine-normalizer-subgroupup-carrier},
each $\mathsf{Carrier}^{N}_{\SubgroupUp}$ assertion is exactly the
$N_F(C_F(A))$ normalizer predicate for the fixed affine automorphism $A$.

Apply
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-centralizer-normalizer-subgroup}. Its four
displayed rows give identity membership, product closure, inverse closure, and
transport along $\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}$ for the carrier
$\mathsf{Carrier}^{N}_{\SubgroupUp}$.

For the center inclusion and restricted action, apply
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-center-normalizes-centralizers}. The first
displayed implication gives
$\mathsf{Center}_F(Z)\Rightarrow\mathsf{Carrier}^{N}_{\SubgroupUp}(Z)$, and
the second displayed statement gives
$\mathrm{Act}_Z(X)\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}X$ for every carried centralizer element
$X$.

The ambient classifier compatibility is the same compatibility used in
\autoref{thm:field-affine-pointwise-classifier-operation-congruence}: affine
operation and inverse rows respect $\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}$, so the normalizer
witnesses repack as $\SubgroupUp$ certificate rows without changing the
underlying affine group data.
\end{proof}

Per-object template: traditional target normalizer subgroup acting on a
centralizer; BEDC source concrete $\FieldUp(\RatUp)$, $\GroupUp$, and
$\SubgroupUp$; carrier $N_F(C_F(A))$; classifier $\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}$;
stability identity, product, inverse, transport, and restricted action; ledger
fixed automorphism, centralizer tests, normalizer witness, and conjugation
witnesses; core theorem list
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-centralizer-normalizer-subgroup} and
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-center-normalizes-centralizers}; exactness
predicate normalizer membership as preservation-reflection of $C_F(A)$;
standard bridge ordinary normalizer subgroup and center action; status
$\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.

\begin{definition}[Centralizer normalizer witness]
\label{def:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-witness}
For the centralizer carrier $Z_a$ from
\autoref{def:group-centralizer-carrier}, define the history-level normalizer
witness predicate by
$$
\mathsf{Norm}_{Z_a}(t) :\Longleftrightarrow
\bigl(\forall x:\mathsf{BHist},\ Z_a(x)\Rightarrow
Z_a(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(t,x),\mathsf{inv}(t)))\bigr)
\land
\bigl(\forall x:\mathsf{BHist},\ Z_a(x)\Rightarrow
Z_a(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{inv}(t),x),
\mathsf{inv}(\mathsf{inv}(t))))\bigr).
$$
Thus $\mathsf{Norm}_{Z_a}(t)$ records that conjugation by $t$ and conjugation by
$\mathsf{inv}(t)$ both preserve the centralizer carrier. Equivalently, it is the
carrier $N_a$ of \autoref{def:group-centralizer-normalizer-carrier} with the
abbreviation $\mathsf{Conj}$ expanded.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Centralizer elements normalize the centralizer]
\label{thm:subgroup-centralizer-self-normalizer}
For a BEDC history-level $\GroupUp$ operation over $\mathsf{BHist}$ satisfying
the same associativity, unit, multiplication-congruence, and two-sided inverse
laws as in \autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-certificate-target}, every
centralizer element normalizes the centralizer. Explicitly, if $Z_a(t)$, then
$\mathsf{Norm}_{Z_a}(t)$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Assume $Z_a(t)$. The inverse-closure row of
\autoref{thm:group-centralizer-subgroup} applied to $Z_a(t)$ gives
$Z_a(\mathsf{inv}(t))$.

For the forward conjugation clause, let $x:\mathsf{BHist}$ satisfy $Z_a(x)$.
The product-closure row of \autoref{thm:group-centralizer-subgroup} applied to
$Z_a(t)$ and $Z_a(x)$ gives $Z_a(\mathsf{mul}(t,x))$.

Apply product closure once more, now to $Z_a(\mathsf{mul}(t,x))$ and
$Z_a(\mathsf{inv}(t))$. The result is
$Z_a(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(t,x),\mathsf{inv}(t)))$, which is precisely
the first clause of $\mathsf{Norm}_{Z_a}(t)$.

For the inverse conjugation clause, let $x:\mathsf{BHist}$ satisfy $Z_a(x)$.
Product closure applied to $Z_a(\mathsf{inv}(t))$ and $Z_a(x)$ gives
$Z_a(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{inv}(t),x))$.

Apply product closure once more with the final factor $Z_a(t)$. The two-sided
inverse laws identify $t$ with $\mathsf{inv}(\mathsf{inv}(t))$ under $\hsame$,
and the carrier-transport row of \autoref{thm:group-centralizer-subgroup}
transports membership to
$Z_a(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{inv}(t),x),
\mathsf{inv}(\mathsf{inv}(t))))$. This is the second clause of
$\mathsf{Norm}_{Z_a}(t)$, so both preservation directions hold.
\end{proof}

Per-object template: traditional target centralizer self-normalization; BEDC
source $\GroupUp$ over $\mathsf{BHist}$ plus $\SubgroupUp$; carrier $Z_a$;
classifier restricted $\hsame$; stability product and inverse closure; ledger
conjugating element, acted element, and inverse witness; core theorem list
\autoref{thm:group-centralizer-subgroup} and
\autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-certificate-target}; exactness predicate
normalizer witness; standard bridge ordinary centralizer inclusion in its
normalizer; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.

\begin{definition}[Centralizer-normalizer SubgroupUp pair]
\label{def:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-pair}
Fix the centralizer carrier $Z_a$ from
\autoref{def:group-centralizer-carrier} and the centralizer normalizer carrier
$N_a$ from \autoref{def:group-centralizer-normalizer-carrier}. The
centralizer-normalizer $\SubgroupUp$ pair has lower carrier $Z_a$ and upper
carrier $N_a$, both classified by the restriction of $\hsame$ to carried
histories. The operation, unit, and inverse are inherited from the ambient
history-level $\GroupUp$ operation $\mathsf{mul}$, $e$, and $\mathsf{inv}$.

The pair ledger records the fixed element $a$, the conjugation word
$\mathsf{Conj}_s(x)$, the witnesses that histories carried by $Z_a$ commute
with $a$, and the forward and inverse preservation witnesses appearing in the
definition of $N_a$. Thus a carried normalizer history acts on the carried
centralizer histories by the restricted conjugation word while keeping every
endpoint inside the same $\SubgroupUp$ lower carrier.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Centralizer-normalizer SubgroupUp pair certificate]
\label{thm:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-pair-certificate}
For a BEDC history-level $\GroupUp$ operation over $\mathsf{BHist}$ satisfying
the same associativity, unit, multiplication-congruence, and two-sided inverse
laws as in
\autoref{thm:group-centralizer-normalizer-subgroup}, the pair of
\autoref{def:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-pair} satisfies the following
concrete $\SubgroupUp$ certificate rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $Z_a$ is the centralizer $\SubgroupUp$ certificate target of
\autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-certificate-target}.
\item $N_a$ is a $\SubgroupUp$ certificate target over the same ambient
operation, with unit, product, inverse, and classifier-transport rows.
\item The lower carrier is included in the upper carrier: if $Z_a(s)$, then
$N_a(s)$.
\item If $N_a(s)$ and $Z_a(x)$, then
$Z_a(\mathsf{Conj}_s(x))$ and
$Z_a(\mathsf{Conj}_{\mathsf{inv}(s)}(x))$.
\item For every $s$ with $N_a(s)$, the restricted normalizer action on $Z_a$
is classifier-exact, its orbit classifier is stable under $\hsame$ replacement
of carried endpoints, and its fixed-action kernel satisfies
$$
(\forall x:\mathsf{BHist},\ Z_a(x)\Rightarrow
\hsame(\mathsf{Conj}_s(x),x))
\Longleftrightarrow
(\forall x:\mathsf{BHist},\ Z_a(x)\Rightarrow
\hsame(\mathsf{mul}(s,x),\mathsf{mul}(x,s))).
$$
\end{enumerate}
Hence the centralizer and its normalizer are available as one concrete
$\SubgroupUp$ pair over $\mathsf{BHist}$, with the restricted conjugation
action and orbit classifier attached to the same certificate object.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The lower certificate is
\autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-certificate-target}. It gives the unit,
product, inverse, classifier-transport, and commutator-fiber rows for $Z_a$
with the operation, unit, inverse, and restricted $\hsame$ classifier inherited
from the ambient $\GroupUp$.

The upper certificate and the carrier inclusion are supplied by
\autoref{thm:group-centralizer-normalizer-subgroup}. Its first four rows give
unit, product, inverse, and classifier transport for $N_a$, and its final row
places each centralizer element in the normalizer.

The action rows are the defining preservation clauses of $N_a$, recorded in
\autoref{thm:group-centralizer-normalizer-action-automorphism-kernel-exactness}.
That theorem also gives the inverse-action identities and the displayed
fixed-action kernel equivalence, so the restricted action is exact on the
centralizer carrier.

Finally,
\autoref{thm:group-centralizer-normalizer-orbit-classifier-certificate}
supplies the endpoint-transport row for the orbit classifier generated by the
same normalizer action. Repacking these cited rows with the ledger of
\autoref{def:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-pair} gives one concrete
$\SubgroupUp$ pair over $\mathsf{BHist}$.
\end{proof}

Per-object template: traditional target normalizer of a centralizer with
restricted action; BEDC source $\GroupUp$ over $\mathsf{BHist}$ plus
$\SubgroupUp$; carriers $Z_a,N_a$; classifier restricted $\hsame$; stability
subgroup rows, inclusion, conjugation preservation, and action exactness;
ledger fixed element $a$, conjugating word $\mathsf{Conj}_s$, normalizer
witness, and endpoint witnesses; core theorem list
\autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-certificate-target},
\autoref{thm:group-centralizer-normalizer-subgroup},
\autoref{thm:group-centralizer-normalizer-action-automorphism-kernel-exactness},
and
\autoref{thm:group-centralizer-normalizer-orbit-classifier-certificate};
exactness predicate normalizer action on the centralizer carrier; standard
bridge ordinary centralizer-normalizer pair; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.

\begin{theorem}[Centralizer normalizer SubgroupUp certificate]
\label{thm:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-subgroup-certificate}
For a BEDC history-level $\GroupUp$ operation over $\mathsf{BHist}$ satisfying
the same associativity, unit, multiplication-congruence, and two-sided inverse
laws as in \autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-certificate-target}, the carrier
$\mathsf{Norm}_{Z_a}$ of
\autoref{def:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-witness} supplies the concrete
$\SubgroupUp$ certificate rows for the normalizer of $Z_a$:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $\mathsf{Norm}_{Z_a}(e)$.
\item If $\mathsf{Norm}_{Z_a}(s)$ and $\mathsf{Norm}_{Z_a}(t)$, then
$\mathsf{Norm}_{Z_a}(\mathsf{mul}(s,t))$.
\item If $\mathsf{Norm}_{Z_a}(t)$, then
$\mathsf{Norm}_{Z_a}(\mathsf{inv}(t))$.
\item If $\mathsf{Norm}_{Z_a}(s)$ and $s\hsame t$, then
$\mathsf{Norm}_{Z_a}(t)$.
\item If $Z_a(t)$, then $\mathsf{Norm}_{Z_a}(t)$.
\end{enumerate}
Thus the two-sided normalizer carrier has identity membership, product closure,
inverse closure, $\hsame$-transport, and the inclusion row from the centralizer.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For identity membership, unfold
\autoref{def:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-witness}. The unit laws make
conjugation by $e$ act as the identity on every carried $x$, and the two-sided
inverse laws identify $\mathsf{inv}(e)$ with $e$. Carrier transport from
\autoref{thm:group-centralizer-subgroup} returns both clauses.

For product closure, assume $\mathsf{Norm}_{Z_a}(s)$ and
$\mathsf{Norm}_{Z_a}(t)$, reading these as expanded $N_a$ witnesses. Apply
\autoref{thm:group-normalizer-conjugation-action-composition}: its forward
composition row sends $Z_a(x)$ first through the $t$ clause and then through the
$s$ clause, while its inverse-action row sends $Z_a(x)$ first through the
inverse clause for $s$ and then through the inverse clause for $t$. These are
the two clauses of $\mathsf{Norm}_{Z_a}(\mathsf{mul}(s,t))$.

For inverse closure, the first clause of
$\mathsf{Norm}_{Z_a}(\mathsf{inv}(t))$ is exactly the inverse-conjugation half
of $\mathsf{Norm}_{Z_a}(t)$. Its second clause conjugates by
$\mathsf{inv}(\mathsf{inv}(t))$; the two-sided inverse laws identify this word
with the original conjugation by $t$, and multiplication congruence plus
carrier transport move the first clause across the comparison.

For $\hsame$-transport, assume $\mathsf{Norm}_{Z_a}(s)$ and $s\hsame t$.
Multiplication congruence transports the forward conjugation word with $s$ to
the corresponding word with $t$, and inverse congruence transports the inverse
word. Carrier transport from \autoref{thm:group-centralizer-subgroup} therefore
gives both clauses of $\mathsf{Norm}_{Z_a}(t)$.

The inclusion row is exactly
\autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-self-normalizer}: every $Z_a(t)$ supplies both
normalizer clauses. Repacking the five rows with the restricted $\hsame$
classifier gives the advertised concrete $\SubgroupUp$ certificate for the
normalizer carrier.
\end{proof}

Per-object template: traditional target normalizer subgroup certificate; BEDC
source $\GroupUp$ over $\mathsf{BHist}$ plus centralizer $\SubgroupUp$; carrier
$\mathsf{Norm}_{Z_a}$; classifier restricted $\hsame$; stability identity,
product, inverse, and carrier transport; ledger unit conjugation, product
conjugation composition, inverse conjugation, and endpoint classifier witnesses;
core theorem list \autoref{thm:group-normalizer-conjugation-action-composition},
\autoref{thm:group-centralizer-subgroup}, and
\autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-self-normalizer}; exactness predicate
two-sided preservation of $Z_a$ under conjugation; standard bridge ordinary
normalizer subgroup of a centralizer; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.

\begin{theorem}[Centralizer normalizer SubgroupUp tower certificate]
\label{thm:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-tower-certificate}
For a BEDC history-level $\GroupUp$ operation over $\mathsf{BHist}$ satisfying
the same associativity, unit, multiplication-congruence, and two-sided inverse
laws as in \autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-certificate-target}, let $Z_a$
be the centralizer carrier of \autoref{def:group-centralizer-carrier} and let
$N_a$ be the centralizer normalizer carrier of
\autoref{def:group-centralizer-normalizer-carrier}. The pair $Z_a\leq N_a$
forms a quotient-ready $\SubgroupUp$ tower:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $Z_a$ satisfies the identity, product, inverse, $\hsame$-transport, and
commutator-fiber rows of the centralizer $\SubgroupUp$ certificate.
\item $N_a$ satisfies the identity, product, inverse, and $\hsame$-transport
subgroup rows over the same ambient classifier.
\item Every carried centralizer element is carried by the normalizer: if
$Z_a(t)$, then $N_a(t)$.
\item If $N_a(s)$ and $Z_a(x)$, then
$Z_a(\mathsf{Conj}_s(x))$ and
$Z_a(\mathsf{Conj}_{\mathsf{inv}(s)}(x))$.
\item The two conjugation-closure rows are stable under the ambient
classifier: if $\hsame(s,t)$, then the corresponding forward and inverse
conjugation words define the same carried $Z_a$ membership after transport.
\end{enumerate}
Equivalently, the ten-field $\SubgroupUp$ certificate for $Z_a$, the
four-field subgroup certificate for $N_a$, the inclusion $Z_a\subseteq N_a$,
the two conjugation rows, and their classifier stability are packaged as the
normal-subgroup input used by quotient-group construction.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The $Z_a$ certificate is exactly
\autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-certificate-target}. It supplies the
identity carrier, product closure, inverse closure, $\hsame$ transport, and
commutator-fiber exactness rows for the centralizer object viewed as a
$\SubgroupUp$ instance.

The $N_a$ subgroup rows come from
\autoref{thm:group-centralizer-normalizer-subgroup}. Its identity, product,
inverse, and $\hsame$-transport clauses give the normalizer carrier the same
ambient operation, unit, inverse, and classifier interface required for the
upper subgroup object.

The inclusion row has two equivalent sources. The fifth row of
\autoref{thm:group-centralizer-normalizer-subgroup} states directly that
$Z_a(t)$ implies $N_a(t)$, while
\autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-self-normalizer} gives the same witness by
showing that every centralizer element preserves $Z_a$ under conjugation.

Finally,
\autoref{thm:group-centralizer-normal-in-normalizer} supplies the forward
conjugation row, the inverse-conjugation row, and the classifier transport of
those rows. Repacking these data in the order displayed above gives the
quotient-ready tower certificate without introducing any host quotient or
external parameter transport.
\end{proof}

Per-object template: traditional target normal centralizer subgroup inside
its normalizer for quotient groups; BEDC source $\GroupUp$ over
$\mathsf{BHist}$ plus $\SubgroupUp$; lower carrier $Z_a$; upper carrier
$N_a$; classifier $\hsame$; stability identity, product, inverse, classifier
transport, inclusion, forward conjugation, inverse conjugation, and
conjugation-word transport; ledger centralizing element, normalizing element,
acted element, inverse normalizer witness, and carried conjugation witnesses;
core theorem list \autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-certificate-target},
\autoref{thm:group-centralizer-normalizer-subgroup},
\autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-self-normalizer}, and
\autoref{thm:group-centralizer-normal-in-normalizer}; exactness predicate
normalizer action on the centralizer; standard bridge normal-subgroup input
for quotient groups; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
