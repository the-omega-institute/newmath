The $\ClosedGenerationRefusalUp$ packet realizes the negative boundary of
the inscription-event vision in
\autoref{ch:visions-inscription-event-as-concrete-instance}. It sits beside
the public inscription event of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-inscriptionevent-namecert}, the route audit of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-inscription-route-audit-namecert}, and the
kernel observation sieve of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-kernel-observation-sieve-namecert}. Those
siblings expose accepted public inscription rows, accepted route rows, and
accepted-or-rejected observation rows; this packet records the finite case in
which a candidate transition is refused as closed-generation-certified from
the prior records.

\begin{definition}[ClosedGenerationRefusal carrier]
\label{def:closed-generation-refusal-carrier}
A $\ClosedGenerationRefusalUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
F=(P,T,G,R,I,H,C,Q,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $P$ is the prior-record row naming the finite records visible before the candidate transition is tested;
\item $T$ is the candidate-transition row whose inscription-facing admission is under audit;
\item $G$ is the closed-generation test row, recording whether the prior records certify $T$ by authorized generators;
\item $R$ is the refusal-ledger row, present exactly when $G$ does not certify $T$ as prior-record generated;
\item $I$ is the inscription-comparison row, reading $T$ against the inscription-event packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-inscriptionevent-namecert};
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $P,T,G,R,I$;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay through the prior records, candidate transition, closed-generation test, refusal ledger, and inscription comparison;
\item $Q$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, $\NameCert$, $\InscriptionEventUp$, $\InscriptionRouteAuditUp$, $\KernelObservationSieveUp$, $\CausalCommitmentUp$, and $\ApophaticSocketUp$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ClosedGenerationRefusalUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier has no coordinate for a transition accepted by the
closed-generation test, an untested candidate, a hidden generator, a private
agency predicate, or a route that bypasses the refusal ledger.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[ClosedGenerationRefusal NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:closed-generation-refusal-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:closed-generation-refusal-carrier}, the local
$\NameCert_{\ClosedGenerationRefusalUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier
admission for
$$
F=(P,T,G,R,I,H,C,Q,N),
$$
prior-record exposure through $P$, candidate-transition exposure through $T$,
closed-generation testing through $G$, refusal-ledger accountability through
$R$, inscription-event comparison through $I$, componentwise $\hsame$
stability through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, package
provenance through $Q$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the displayed carrier. The rows $P,T,G,R,I,H,C,Q,N$ are the full
inventory available to the certificate reader, and the classifier lists no
extra coordinate for an untested candidate or hidden generator.

Read the refusal surface in order. The prior-record row $P$ supplies the
finite source, the candidate row $T$ supplies the transition under audit, and
the test row $G$ is the only displayed place where prior-record closed
generation may certify the candidate.

Finally inspect $R,I,H,C,Q,N$. The refusal ledger records the negative result
of $G$, the inscription comparison $I$ connects the candidate to the
inscription-event sibling, and the support rows only transport, replay,
package, and name the same finite packet. Hence the listed rows exhaust the
local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ClosedGenerationRefusal inscription separation]
\label{thm:closed-generation-refusal-inscription-separation}
An accepted $\ClosedGenerationRefusalUp$ packet separates the candidate
transition $T$ from prior-record closed generation without treating $T$ as a
private agency object. The row $R$ records only that $G$ does not certify $T$
from $P$, while $I$ keeps the public inscription comparison routed through
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-inscriptionevent-namecert} and
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-inscription-route-audit-namecert}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with $P,T,G$. The candidate transition is tested against the displayed
prior records by the closed-generation row, so the refusal is not a claim
about an unrecorded psychological state or host process.

Use the refusal ledger $R$. Its content is negative and local: the candidate
is not certified by the displayed prior-record test. The carrier gives no
coordinate for replacing that local refusal with an external agency object.

The inscription-comparison row $I$ then routes the same candidate through the
public inscription-event and route-audit siblings. Thus the packet separates
closed-generation refusal from inscription-facing comparison while keeping
both rows inside one finite certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ClosedGenerationRefusal ledger non-escape]
\label{thm:closed-generation-refusal-ledger-nonescape}
No consumer of an accepted $\ClosedGenerationRefusalUp$ packet can export the
candidate transition as inscription-event novelty while omitting the
refusal-ledger row $R$. A consumer may read the prior records, candidate,
test, and inscription comparison through $P,T,G,I,H,C,Q,N$, but the negative
closed-generation boundary remains public only through $R$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier $F=(P,T,G,R,I,H,C,Q,N)$. The negative
closed-generation boundary is named only in $R$, while $P,T,G$ name the test
surface and $I$ names the inscription comparison.

Apply \autoref{thm:closed-generation-refusal-namecert-obligations}. Refusal
accountability is one of the local obligations, and the support rows $H,C,Q,N$
preserve the same displayed packet rather than adding a second route around
$R$.

Consequently a consumer that omits $R$ may still see a prior-record test and
candidate row, but it has not read the certificate row that makes the negative
closed-generation boundary public.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ClosedGenerationRefusal sibling independence]
\label{thm:closed-generation-refusal-sibling-independence}
The $\ClosedGenerationRefusalUp$ carrier is not bijective, under
NameCert-row preservation, to $\InscriptionEventUp$,
$\InscriptionRouteAuditUp$, $\KernelObservationSieveUp$,
$\CausalCommitmentUp$, or $\ApophaticSocketUp$. It uniquely binds the
prior-record row $P$, candidate-transition row $T$, closed-generation test
$G$, refusal ledger $R$, and inscription comparison $I$ as one packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Compare the inscription siblings first. $\InscriptionEventUp$ records an
accepted public inscription packet, and $\InscriptionRouteAuditUp$ records a
visible gap-to-accepted-route audit; neither carries the prior-record
closed-generation test together with the refusal ledger.

Compare the sieve and commitment siblings next. $\KernelObservationSieveUp$
has accepted and rejected observation ledgers, and $\CausalCommitmentUp$
records commitment-facing causal rows, but neither preserves the candidate
transition, closed-generation test, and inscription comparison as one local
NameCert surface.

Finally compare $\ApophaticSocketUp$. It records a supply-facing refusal
socket, not the prior-record test of a candidate transition. Therefore no
NameCert-row-preserving bijection can preserve $P,T,G,R,I,H,C,Q,N$ across
any of the listed siblings.
\end{proof}
