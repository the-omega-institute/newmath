\begin{definition}[Moore--Smith Cauchy subnet criterion carrier]
\label{def:moore-smith-cauchy-subnet-criterion-carrier}
A $\MooreSmithCauchySubnetCriterionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(D,M,A,W,B,N,S,G,Y,R,H,C,P,L)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the $\DirectedSetUp$ index row naming finite vertices, preorder comparisons, and upper-window witnesses.
\item $M$ is the $\MooreSmithNetUp$ row exposing directed net values and the tail ledger consumed by the criterion.
\item $A$ is the $\CauchyNetUp$ row recording the common-window Cauchy evidence read on the same directed surface.
\item $W$ is the cofinal subnet-window row, listing the finite tail requests and their displayed upper refinements.
\item $B$ is the subnet bound row, recording how each requested late window in $M$ is met by a later displayed witness in $W$.
\item $N$ is the $\NetConvergenceUp$ consumer row that reads the subnet route only after $D,M,A,W,B$ have been displayed.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule for finite observation windows along the accepted subnet route.
\item $G$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row used to compare the scheduled observations.
\item $Y$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row obtained from those stream windows and dyadic tolerances.
\item $R$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after $S,G,Y$ are displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for all criterion rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay through the same directed-tail and readback route.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\MooreSmithCauchySubnetCriterionUp$, $\DirectedSetUp$, $\MooreSmithNetUp$, $\CauchyNetUp$, $\NetConvergenceUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $L$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MooreSmithCauchySubnetCriterionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a globally chosen subnet, quotient-net equality, countable choice, an ambient compactness theorem, or host-level real completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Moore--Smith Cauchy subnet criterion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:moore-smith-cauchy-subnet-criterion-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted carrier $Q=(D,M,A,W,B,N,S,G,Y,R,H,C,P,L)$ of \autoref{def:moore-smith-cauchy-subnet-criterion-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\MooreSmithCauchySubnetCriterionUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission, directed-index exposure, Moore--Smith tail exposure, Cauchy-window exposure, cofinal subnet-window evidence, subnet-bound compatibility, NetConvergence handoff, StreamName scheduling, DyadicRatCore tolerance comparison, RegSeqRat readback, Real seal exposure, componentwise transport, displayed route replay, provenance, and local naming.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission projects the finite packet of \autoref{def:moore-smith-cauchy-subnet-criterion-carrier}. The directed evidence is stored in $D,M,A,W,B$, so the criterion first reads the index row, Moore--Smith net row, Cauchy-net row, cofinal subnet windows, and subnet bounds.

The convergence-facing handoff is then $N$. By \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-netconvergence-namecert}, a convergence consumer reads only displayed directed-tail evidence before it reaches filter or Real-facing rows.

The Real-facing part is $S,G,Y,R$. The StreamName, DyadicRatCore, RegSeqRat, and Real chapters supply finite observation windows, dyadic tolerances, regular-rational readback, and the terminal seal, so the packet does not need a hidden completed limit.

Finally $H,C,P,L$ transport, replay, name, and certify exactly those rows. Since no other coordinate is present, the listed obligations exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}
