\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CauchyModulusUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-cauchymodulus-namecert}

The $\CauchyModulusUp$ interface records the explicit tail-control object used
by Bishop--Bridges constructive analysis. It sits in the Phase Real and
Completion order of \autoref{sec:roadmap-l10-construction-order}: the dyadic
bound rows are read through $\DyadicRatCore$, the tail stream is supplied by
$\StreamNameUp$, regularity is checked against $\RegSeqRat$, and the eventual
classifier is transported only after the $\RealUp$ surface is available. The
chapter is a paper-side support object for constructive Cauchy control; it
does not claim completeness of $\RealUp$.

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:cauchymodulus-carrier}

\begin{definition}[Dyadic tail-bound carrier]
\label{def:cauchymodulus-dyadic-bound-carrier}
A $\CauchyModulusUp$ carrier row is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
M=(s,\mu,b,L)
$$
whose source stream $s$ is a $\StreamNameUp$ observation stream over
$\DyadicRatCore$, whose modulus ledger $\mu$ assigns to each visible tolerance
row a threshold row, whose bound ledger $b$ records the dyadic inequality
between any two observations past that threshold, and whose provenance ledger
$L$ packages the corresponding $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\Pkg$ rows. The packet
is admitted exactly when every displayed late pair in the finite observation
window carries a $\DyadicRatCore$ comparison row witnessing the requested
Cauchy bound.

The classifier on accepted packets is componentwise $\hsame$ transport of the
stream observations, modulus thresholds, dyadic bounds, and provenance rows.
No quotient over all possible moduli is formed: two packets classify together
only when their displayed tail-control ledgers can be repacked row by row.
\end{definition}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:cauchymodulus-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[Monotone tail obligation]
\label{thm:cauchymodulus-monotone-tail-obligation}
Let $M=(s,\mu,b,L)$ be an accepted $\CauchyModulusUp$ carrier row. If a
tolerance row is transported to a smaller positive dyadic tolerance through
the $\DyadicRatCore$ comparison ledger, then the threshold selected by $\mu$
can be transported to a threshold that works for the smaller tolerance, and
the late-pair dyadic-bound rows in $b$ remain accepted after $\hsame$
transport of the observation indices.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the carrier row of \autoref{def:cauchymodulus-dyadic-bound-carrier}.
The selected threshold and every late-pair bound are visible rows of the same
finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet, so decreasing the tolerance asks only for a
refined dyadic comparison row, not for a new stream object.

Transport the observation endpoints by the displayed $\hsame$ rows and compose
the old dyadic bound with the smaller-tolerance comparison. The provenance
ledger $L$ supplies the $\Cont$ and $\Pkg$ rows needed to repack the same
late-pair evidence under the transported threshold.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat regularity obligation]
\label{thm:cauchymodulus-regseqrat-regularity-obligation}
Every accepted $\CauchyModulusUp$ carrier row whose source stream is a
$\RegSeqRat$ regular dyadic sequence supplies the explicit regularity data
required by the $\RegSeqRat$ phase: for displayed indices $m,n$ past the
threshold chosen by $\mu$, the bound ledger gives the dyadic estimate required
by the regular-sequence classifier, and this estimate is stable under
componentwise $\hsame$ transport of $m$, $n$, $s(m)$, and $s(n)$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the $\RegSeqRat$ regularity row as a request for a visible dyadic
late-pair estimate. The carrier already stores exactly that estimate in $b$,
indexed by the threshold supplied by $\mu$ and by the two displayed stream
observations.

For stability, transport the two indices and observations through the
classifier of \autoref{def:cauchymodulus-dyadic-bound-carrier}. The
$\DyadicRatCore$ comparison row is part of the ledger, so the transported
packet again contains the regularity estimate demanded by $\RegSeqRat$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real classifier transport obligation]
\label{thm:cauchymodulus-real-classifier-transport-obligation}
Assume the $\RealUp$ classifier is read from $\RegSeqRat$ representatives by
dyadic tolerance tests. A $\CauchyModulusUp$ carrier row transports across the
$\RealUp$ classifier by sending its stream observations through the
$\RegSeqRat$ classifier, transporting the modulus threshold rows by $\hsame$,
and reusing the dyadic tail-bound ledger for the transported representatives.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The only analytic datum used by the real classifier is an explicit dyadic
tolerance test on sufficiently late representatives. The
$\CauchyModulusUp$ packet exposes this datum as $\mu$ together with the
late-pair ledger $b$.

Apply the $\RegSeqRat$ transport to the representative stream and then repack
the threshold, dyadic comparison, and provenance rows. Since no equivalence
class of real numbers is formed, the transport is a row-by-row certificate
operation inside the displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy modulus NameCert obligation]
\label{thm:cauchymodulus-namecert-obligation}
The local $\NameCert_{\CauchyModulusUp}$ obligation surface consists of the
dyadic tail-bound carrier of
\autoref{def:cauchymodulus-dyadic-bound-carrier}, the monotone-tail obligation
of \autoref{thm:cauchymodulus-monotone-tail-obligation}, the
$\RegSeqRat$ regularity obligation of
\autoref{thm:cauchymodulus-regseqrat-regularity-obligation}, and the
$\RealUp$ classifier transport obligation of
\autoref{thm:cauchymodulus-real-classifier-transport-obligation}. These rows
license explicit Cauchy tail control without asserting a completed real
carrier or a choice of limits.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier supplies the source and visible ledger. The monotone-tail theorem
gives closure of the modulus under stronger tolerances, while the
$\RegSeqRat$ theorem identifies the same rows with regular dyadic-sequence
control.

The real-classifier transport theorem explains how the packet is consumed once
the $\RealUp$ classifier is available. Repacking these rows yields the local
name-certificate surface, and the absence of a limit selector keeps the claim
strictly below real completeness.
\end{proof}
