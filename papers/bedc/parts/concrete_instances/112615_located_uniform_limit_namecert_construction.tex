\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\LocatedUniformLimitUp$}

Following Bishop and Bridges constructive analysis, the
$\LocatedUniformLimitUp$ packet records the finite certificate surface for a
located uniform-limit handoff without turning the handoff into host
completeness. It sits over the finite stream windows of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational
readback route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the
terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the
modulus carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-modulus-space-namecert},
and the uniform continuity surface of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuousmap-namecert}. The packet gives Real
and Completion consumers a displayed common-modulus route for a located
uniform Cauchy family; it does not select a limit, quotient functions, use
countable choice, invoke trichotomy, or assume ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[LocatedUniformLimit carrier]
\label{def:located-uniform-limit-carrier}
A $\LocatedUniformLimitUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
U=(I,S,M,W,R,E,G,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $I$ is the index and source-domain row whose accepted points are read
  through displayed histories and classifier transport;
\item $S$ is the sequence row, listing the finite family members and the
  graph rows through which each member is inspected;
\item $M$ is the common located uniform Cauchy modulus row, giving one
  displayed precision schedule for all accepted source windows;
\item $W$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window row for indices, source
  points, and value names;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row attached to the scheduled value
  windows and their rational tail budgets;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after the stream windows,
  regular rational readbacks, and common modulus checks have been displayed;
\item $G$ is the graph row relating the sequence entries, the common modulus,
  and the terminal seal read by downstream Real-facing consumers;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for index rows, sequence
  rows, modulus rows, windows, readbacks, graph rows, real seals, provenance,
  and local naming rows;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from index admission through
  sequence lookup, uniform-modulus inspection, finite-window readback,
  graph-row comparison, and real-seal handoff;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LocatedUniformLimitUp$,
  $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\CauchyModulusSpaceUp$,
  $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedUniformLimitUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier relates two accepted packets exactly when their index rows,
sequence rows, common modulus rows, finite stream windows, regular rational
readbacks, real-seal rows, graph rows, and provenance rows transport
componentwise along $H$ and replay through the same displayed routes in $C$.
No host function equality, quotient real equality, selected limit, countable
choice, trichotomy, or ambient completeness principle is a carrier coordinate.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[LocatedUniformLimit NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:located-uniform-limit-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\LocatedUniformLimitUp$ carrier
$U=(I,S,M,W,R,E,G,H,C,P,N)$, the local
$\NameCert_{\LocatedUniformLimitUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following
obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed index, sequence, common modulus,
  window, readback, real-seal, graph, transport, replay, provenance, and
  local naming rows;
\item common-modulus exactness, requiring each value comparison requested by
  a Real-facing consumer to use the same located uniform Cauchy modulus row
  $M$ before the terminal seal $E$ can be inspected;
\item finite-window readback, requiring every index, source, and value
  observation to pass through $W$ and $R$ rather than through an external
  sequence or function equality predicate;
\item graph-row compatibility, requiring $G$ to connect the displayed
  sequence entries, common modulus checks, regular rational readbacks, and
  $\RealUp$ seal rows;
\item $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ replay, requiring all index, sequence, modulus,
  readback, graph, and seal rows to move together under $H$ and $C$;
\item provenance and non-escape, stating that the package exports only the
  named finite rows and no host completeness theorem, quotient function
  equality, selected limit, countable choice, or trichotomy principle.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from
\autoref{def:located-uniform-limit-carrier}. The displayed packet lists the
index row, sequence row, common located modulus, finite windows, regular
rational readbacks, real seal, graph row, transport ledger, replay ledger,
package provenance, and local name.

The common-modulus obligation is exactly the role of $M$. Every Real-facing
comparison must first request a precision through the same located uniform
Cauchy row, then inspect the scheduled source and value windows in $W$.

Finite-window readback is forced by the order $W,R,E$. The packet gives no
coordinate from which a consumer could read an infinite sequence, a host
function, or a quotient equality before the regular rational window has been
displayed.

Graph-row compatibility is the projection through $G$. It records the finite
route joining the inspected family member, the common modulus check, the
readback row, and the terminal real seal.

Finally, $H,C,P,N$ package the same route after classifier transport and
continuation replay. Since none of the excluded principles is a row of the
carrier, none can be exported by the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LocatedUniformLimit L10 handoff]
\label{thm:located-uniform-limit-l10-handoff}
Every L10 Real or Completion consumer of an accepted
$\LocatedUniformLimitUp$ carrier factors through the finite route
$$
I,S,M,W,R,E,G,H,C,P,N .
$$
The consumer may inspect displayed index rows, finite sequence entries, the
common located uniform Cauchy modulus, finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows,
$\RegSeqRatUp$ readbacks, the $\RealUp$ seal row, the graph row, componentwise
transport, continuation replay, provenance, and the local name. It cannot
bypass this route to obtain host completeness, quotient function equality,
quotient real equality, a selected limit, countable choice, or trichotomy.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an L10 consumer asking for a Real-facing endpoint from the packet.
The only entry point is the displayed index and sequence surface $I,S$, so the
consumer must name the inspected family member before any value row is read.

The common located uniform Cauchy modulus $M$ then supplies the precision
discipline. The finite windows in $W$ and the regular rational readback $R$
turn that discipline into displayed rational data before the terminal seal
$E$ is available.

The graph row $G$ records that the same route connects the sequence entry,
modulus check, readback, and seal. Transport and replay through $H$ and $C$
preserve that route componentwise.

The package and local name rows $P,N$ expose exactly this finite handoff.
Because the excluded host principles are absent from the carrier coordinates,
they cannot be read by any L10 consumer of the certificate.
\end{proof}
