\section{StreamName finite-section pullback exactness}
\label{sec:streamname-finite-section-pullback-exactness}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName finite-section pullback exactness]
\label{thm:streamname-finite-section-pullback-exactness}
Let $Q$ be an accepted finite request section of a $\StreamNameUp$ packet. If
the finite windows of $Q$ agree with the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger and the
$\RegSeqRatUp$ readback used by the current $\RealUp$ seal, then $Q$
determines the same terminal four-face route
$\StreamNameUp,\DyadicRatCoreUp,\RegSeqRatUp,\RealUp$. The support of this
route is only the displayed $\BHist$ rows, componentwise $\hsame$ transports,
displayed $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ rows. It
exports no quotient stream, selected limit, host sequence equality, ambient
completeness row, countable-choice row, or standard bridge row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply \autoref{thm:streamname-finite-request-section-pullback}. The
accepted request section is already pulled back to the displayed
$\StreamNameUp$ schedule, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint-radius ledger, the
$\RegSeqRatUp$ regularity and readback surface, and the support rows
$\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$.

Next use \autoref{thm:streamname-open-phase-four-face-terminality}. Since the
finite windows agree with the DyadicRatCore ledger and the RegSeqRat readback
used by the current RealUp seal, terminality identifies the resulting route
with the unique four-face route through $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$,
$\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$.

Now apply \autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-real-public-seal-no-quotient}. The
RealUp seal remains on the public finite rows and therefore cannot add a
quotient stream, selected limit, host sequence equality, or ambient
completeness coordinate to the section.

Finally use \autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-dyadic-real-obligation-handoff}.
It exhausts carrier, classifier, stability, ledger, and non-escape obligations
by the same four faces and the displayed support rows, leaving no
countable-choice row or standard bridge row to export.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName finite-section classifier determinacy]
\label{thm:streamname-finite-section-classifier-determinacy}
Let $Q$ and $Q'$ be accepted finite request sections of a current L10 Phase
Real and Completion $\StreamNameUp$ packet. Suppose that $Q$ and $Q'$ have the
same $\StreamNameUp$ finite window, the same $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger row, the
same $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row, and the same $\RealUp$ seal boundary. Then
the carrier verdict and classifier verdict read at the terminal
$\StreamNameUp,\DyadicRatCoreUp,\RegSeqRatUp,\RealUp$ four-face route are the
same for $Q$ and $Q'$.

The common route is supported only by the displayed $\BHist$ rows,
componentwise $\hsame$ transports, displayed $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$
provenance, and local $\NameCert$ rows. It contains no quotient stream
equality, selected limit, host sequence equality, ambient completeness row,
countable-choice row, or standard bridge row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:streamname-finite-section-pullback-exactness} first to
$Q$ and then to $Q'$. Each accepted section is read through the same
StreamName finite window, DyadicRatCore ledger, RegSeqRat readback row, RealUp
seal boundary, and displayed support rows.

Next use \autoref{thm:streamname-open-phase-four-face-terminality}. The shared
four faces identify the terminal finite BEDC route for both sections, so a
carrier or classifier verdict has no second terminal route on which to vary.

Now apply \autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-real-public-seal-no-quotient}. The
RealUp seal remains public over the finite route and cannot contribute a
hidden quotient stream, selected limit, host sequence equality, or ambient
completeness coordinate that would separate the two sections.

Finally use \autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-dyadic-real-obligation-handoff}.
Carrier, classifier, stability, ledger, and non-escape obligations are
exhausted by the shared four faces together with the displayed $\BHist$,
$\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows. Hence the two accepted
sections have the same carrier and classifier verdicts at the terminal route,
with no countable-choice or bridge row left outside that support.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName finite-section pasting exactness]
\label{thm:streamname-finite-section-pasting-exactness}
Let $Q_0$ and $Q_1$ be accepted finite request sections of a current L10
Phase Real and Completion $\StreamNameUp$ packet, and suppose their finite
windows have an accepted overlap $Q_{01}$. If $Q_0$ and $Q_1$ agree on
$Q_{01}$ in the $\StreamNameUp$ finite window, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger
row, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row, the $\RealUp$ seal boundary, and the
displayed $\BHist$, componentwise $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$
support rows, then the pasted finite section determines the same terminal
four-face route
$\StreamNameUp,\DyadicRatCoreUp,\RegSeqRatUp,\RealUp$ and the same public
classifier verdict as its two restrictions.

The pasted route exports no quotient stream, host sequence equality, selected
limit, ambient Real completeness, metric-completeness datum, countable-choice
row, or standard bridge row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:streamname-finite-section-pullback-exactness} first to
$Q_0$ and then to $Q_1$. Each restriction is pulled back to its displayed
StreamName finite window, DyadicRatCore ledger row, RegSeqRat readback row,
RealUp seal boundary, and support rows.

On the accepted overlap $Q_{01}$, apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-finite-section-classifier-determinacy}. The overlap
hypotheses identify the four visible faces and the displayed support rows, so
the two restrictions have one carrier verdict and one public classifier
verdict at the common terminal route.

Now paste the finite windows along that overlap. Since both restrictions are
already pullbacks of the same four-face data on $Q_{01}$, the pasted section
has no additional face from which a second terminal route could be read.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:streamname-finite-section-real-regseq-dyadic-lock}.
Every Real-facing or completion-facing consumer of the pasted section is
locked to the same StreamName, DyadicRatCore, RegSeqRat, and RealUp faces.
Thus the public classifier verdict and the terminal four-face route are the
same on the pasted section and on its two restrictions, while the excluded
quotient, host-equality, limit-selection, completeness, choice, and bridge
coordinates remain unavailable.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName Real-RegSeq-Dyadic finite lock]
\label{thm:streamname-finite-section-real-regseq-dyadic-lock}
Let $Q$ be an accepted finite request section of a current L10 Phase Real and
Completion $\StreamNameUp$ packet. Suppose that $Q$ exposes the same
$\StreamNameUp$ finite window, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger, $\RegSeqRatUp$
readback, and $\RealUp$ seal boundary as the terminal four-face route. Then
every $\RealUp$-facing and completion-facing consumer reads the same finite
lock through exactly those four faces and the displayed $\BHist$,
componentwise $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.

The lock contains no quotient stream, host sequence equality, selected limit,
ambient Real completeness, metric-completeness datum, countable-choice row, or
standard bridge row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First use \autoref{thm:streamname-finite-section-pullback-exactness}. The
accepted section $Q$ is already pulled back to the $\StreamNameUp$ finite
window, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, the
$\RealUp$ seal boundary, and the displayed support rows.

Next apply \autoref{thm:streamname-finite-section-classifier-determinacy}.
Any accepted request section exposing those same four faces has the same
carrier and classifier verdict at the terminal route, so no Real-facing or
completion-facing consumer can split the finite lock by rereading one face.

Now use \autoref{thm:streamname-real-completion-four-object-terminal-pullback}.
It identifies every downstream Real or completion read with the terminal
finite pullback once the four faces and support rows agree.

Finally combine this terminal pullback with
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-dyadic-real-obligation-handoff}. The handoff
exhausts carrier, classifier, stability, ledger, and non-escape obligations
through the same $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
The excluded quotient, host-equality, limit-selection, completeness,
countable-choice, and bridge coordinates therefore have no row from which a
consumer could read them.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName four-face terminal completeness]
\label{thm:streamname-four-face-terminal-completeness}
For a current L10 Phase Real and Completion terminal read of
$\StreamNameUp$, completeness for the present open exit surface is exactly
factorization through the four visible faces
$\StreamNameUp,\DyadicRatCoreUp,\RegSeqRatUp,\RealUp$ together with the
displayed $\BHist$ rows, componentwise $\hsame$ transports, displayed
$\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ rows.

Conversely, any accepted read exposing those four faces and those support
rows supplies the full carrier, classifier, stability, ledger, and
non-escape surface available to the current Phase Real and Completion route.
This completeness assertion excludes quotient streams, host sequence
equality, selected limits, ambient Real completeness, metric-completeness
data, countable choice, and standard bridge rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For necessity, start with a complete terminal read of the current open exit
surface. By
\autoref{thm:streamname-finite-section-pullback-exactness}, its accepted
finite section is pulled back to the StreamName finite window, the
DyadicRatCore ledger, the RegSeqRat readback, the RealUp seal boundary, and
the displayed support rows.

Next apply \autoref{thm:streamname-finite-section-classifier-determinacy}
and \autoref{thm:streamname-finite-section-real-regseq-dyadic-lock}. These
results make the carrier and classifier verdicts determinate at the same
four-face finite lock for every Real-facing or completion-facing consumer.

For sufficiency, suppose an accepted read exposes the four visible faces and
the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
Use \autoref{thm:streamname-real-completion-four-object-terminal-pullback} to
identify that packet with the terminal finite pullback for the current Real
and Completion boundary.

Finally apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-dyadic-real-obligation-handoff}. The handoff
fills exactly the carrier, classifier, stability, ledger, and non-escape
obligations from those rows. Since the terminal pullback has no coordinate
for quotient streams, host sequence equality, selected limits, ambient Real
completeness, metric-completeness data, countable choice, or standard bridge
data, those excluded surfaces are not part of the completeness claim.
\end{proof}
