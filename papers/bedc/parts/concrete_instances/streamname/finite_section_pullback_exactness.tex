\begin{theorem}[StreamName finite-section descent gluing]
\label{thm:streamname-finite-section-descent-gluing}
Let $\{Q_i\}_{i \in I}$ be a finite family of accepted current Phase Real and
Completion $\StreamNameUp$ request sections. Suppose that every accepted
overlap $Q_{ij}$ has the same $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row, the same
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger row, the same $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row, the same
$\RealUp$ seal boundary, and the same displayed $\BHist$, componentwise
$\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support rows when read from
$Q_i$ and from $Q_j$. Then the family descends to one public reflection surface
over the four-face route
$\StreamNameUp,\DyadicRatCoreUp,\RegSeqRatUp,\RealUp$ whose restrictions are
the given local sections.

Conversely, any public reflection surface over that four-face route restricts
to accepted local finite sections whose pairwise overlaps agree on exactly the
same finite-window, ledger, readback, seal, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$,
$\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows. Both directions exclude quotient streams,
selected limits, host sequence equality, ambient Real completeness,
metric-completeness data, countable choice, and standard bridge rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For descent, paste the finite family by induction on its finite index set. The
two-section step is exactly
\autoref{thm:streamname-finite-section-pasting-exactness}: the pairwise
overlap agreement keeps the StreamName finite window, the DyadicRatCore
ledger, the RegSeqRat readback, the RealUp seal boundary, and the displayed
support rows fixed while the pasted section keeps the same public classifier
verdict.

At each induction step, use
\autoref{thm:streamname-four-face-terminal-completeness}. The rows retained by
the pasted section are already sufficient for the carrier, classifier,
stability, ledger, and non-escape surface of the current Phase Real and
Completion route, so no further face is available to distinguish the next
local section from the accumulated paste.

Apply \autoref{thm:streamname-finite-section-terminal-reflection} to the final
pasted finite section. It turns the completed four-face factorization into the
public reflection surface over
$\StreamNameUp,\DyadicRatCoreUp,\RegSeqRatUp,\RealUp$. The Real, RegSeq, and
Dyadic handoff rows used above keep every read on the L10 four-object packet
and leave no quotient-stream, selected-limit, host-equality, ambient
completeness, metric-completion, countable-choice, or bridge coordinate to
export.

For restriction, start with a public reflection surface over the same
four-face route. By
\autoref{thm:streamname-finite-section-terminal-reflection}, every visible row
of that surface factors through the StreamName finite window, DyadicRatCore
ledger, RegSeqRat readback, RealUp seal boundary, and displayed support rows.
Restricting those rows to any finite local window gives an accepted local
section, and restricting them to a pairwise overlap gives the same rows from
both sides.
\end{proof}
