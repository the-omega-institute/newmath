\begin{theorem}[StreamName tail-compatible window request]
\label{thm:streamname-tail-compatible-window-request}
Let $s,t:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{BHist}$ be public $\StreamNameUp$ rational
stream rows, let $\mathcal P:\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ be a finite observation
window, and let $n:\mathsf{BHist}$ be a certified unary observation with
$\mathsf{InBundle}(n,\mathcal P)$. A tail-compatible window request from the
L10 countable-name gateway to a $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback or a finite-window
$\RealUp$ seal is determined by exactly the following finite rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item the observation index $n$ together with its $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(n)$
and $\mathsf{InBundle}(n,\mathcal P)$ witnesses;
\item the tail-selector route carrying the selected StreamName observation
window;
\item the finite-window budget surface for the same $\mathcal P$; and
\item the pointwise handoff row
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(s(n),t(n))$ consumed by the $\RegSeqRatUp$
side.
\end{enumerate}
No other source row is part of the request. In particular, the request has no
coordinate for quotient stream equality, selected limits, host real equality,
metric completeness, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the tail selector route of
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-realup-tail-selector-transport}. It fixes
the selected StreamName window and the downstream $\RegSeqRatUp$ and $\RealUp$
readback rows through one finite transport packet.

Next read the finite observation row through
\autoref{thm:streamname-tail-selector-pointwise-transport}. For the certified
index $n$, the transported source row is exactly the bundled
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(n)$ and $\mathsf{InBundle}(n,\mathcal P)$ evidence
together with $\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(s(n),t(n))$.

Now attach the budget surface by
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-regseq-finite-window-budget-handoff}. It places
the same finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, membership, unary-history,
observation-budget, and tail-budget rows before any regularity, dyadic, or
Real-seal consumer row.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-pointwise-handoff}. The
pointwise handoff has only StreamName carrier, finite-window classifier,
membership, unary-history, and rational-history classifier slots. The
displayed rows therefore determine the request, and the cited non-escape
clauses exclude quotient, limit, host-real, metric-completeness, and ambient
completeness data.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName tail-compatible window coverage]
\label{thm:streamname-tail-compatible-window-coverage}
Every accepted finite-window handoff from $\StreamNameUp$ to a $\RegSeqRatUp$
readback or to a finite-window $\RealUp$ seal factors through the
tail-compatible window request of
\autoref{thm:streamname-tail-compatible-window-request}, together with the
shared support rows
$$
\hsame,\quad \Cont,\quad \Pkg,\quad \NameCert.
$$
The factorization is finite: it is read from the selected $\mathsf{BHist}$
observation index, the tail selector route, the finite-window budget row, the
pointwise $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff, and the displayed support rows. It does not
use a quotient stream class, a chosen limit, host real equality, or an ambient
completion theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with an accepted finite-window StreamName handoff. By
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-tail-selector-handoff-exactness}, the
handoff decomposes into the public finite-window classifier, bundled
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}/\mathsf{InBundle}$ rows, pointwise rational-history
classifier rows, the accepted $\RegSeqRatUp$ spine, DyadicRatCore endpoint
rows, and the finite $\RealUp$ ledger.

The rowwise source is then the request from
\autoref{thm:streamname-tail-compatible-window-request}. Its determining data
are the certified $\mathsf{BHist}$ observation index, the selected tail route,
the finite-window budget surface, and the pointwise $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff.

Next use
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-tail-selector-real-window-factorization}.
It reads every consumer-facing $\RealUp$ row through the same finite
StreamName-to-RegSeqRat packet and the same DyadicRatCore endpoint rows.

Finally apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-completion-four-object-exit-route}. The exit
boundary adds only the visible $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and
$\NameCert$ support needed by the four sibling faces. Combining these route
statements gives the displayed finite factorization and leaves quotient
classes, selected limits, host real equality, and ambient completion outside
the accepted handoff.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName tail-compatible window packet]
\label{thm:streamname-tail-compatible-window-packet}
The tail-compatible finite-window packet is exactly the tuple consisting of the unary index row, the bundle-membership row, the tail-selector route, the finite-window budget surface, the pointwise $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff, and the support rows
$$
\hsame,\quad \Cont,\quad \Pkg,\quad \NameCert.
$$
Any accepted downstream $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback or finite-window $\RealUp$ seal read is classified by equality of these displayed rows and by no additional stream-level coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with \autoref{thm:streamname-tail-compatible-window-request}. It identifies the request by the certified unary observation, membership in the finite bundle, the selected tail route, the finite-window budget surface, and the pointwise $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff.

Apply \autoref{thm:streamname-tail-compatible-window-coverage}. Every admitted downstream finite-window consumer route factors through that request together with the displayed $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support rows.

Finally use the pointwise transport and budget handoff rows, \autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-tail-selector-handoff-exactness} and \autoref{thm:streamname-real-regseq-finite-window-budget-handoff}. These rows leave no second stream source, quotient stream class, selected limit, host real equality, metric-completeness datum, or ambient completion coordinate, so equality of the displayed tuple is exactly packet equality for downstream reads.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName tail-compatible window nonescape]
\label{thm:streamname-tail-compatible-window-nonescape}
The tail-compatible finite-window packet exported from $\StreamNameUp$ to
$\RegSeqRatUp$ and $\RealUp$ carries only the request rows of
\autoref{thm:streamname-tail-compatible-window-request} and the support rows
of \autoref{thm:streamname-tail-compatible-window-coverage}. It exports no
quotient stream equality, no selected limit, no host real equality, no
metric-completeness datum, and no ambient $\RealUp$ completeness claim.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:streamname-tail-compatible-window-request}. The request is
determined by the certified finite observation, tail-selector route,
finite-window budget surface, and pointwise $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff.

Apply \autoref{thm:streamname-tail-compatible-window-coverage}. Any accepted
finite-window consumer route factors through that request and the displayed
$\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support rows.

Finally use \autoref{thm:streamname-real-completion-four-object-exit-route}.
The four-object exit boundary consumes the same finite StreamName,
DyadicRatCore, RegSeqRat, and RealUp faces without adding quotient stream
equality, host sequence equality, selected limits, metric-completeness data,
countable choice, standard bridges, or ambient completeness. Hence the
tail-compatible packet has no escaping coordinate of the listed kinds.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName tail-compatible window terminality]
\label{thm:streamname-tail-compatible-window-terminality}
Every accepted finite-window handoff from $\StreamNameUp$ to a $\RegSeqRatUp$
readback or to a finite-window $\RealUp$ seal factors terminally through the
request rows of \autoref{thm:streamname-tail-compatible-window-request} and
the support rows of \autoref{thm:streamname-tail-compatible-window-coverage}.

If two accepted finite-window consumers expose the same finite observation,
tail-selector route, budget surface, pointwise handoff, and support rows, then
they classify through the same tail-compatible window route. The terminal
packet exports no quotient stream equality, selected limit, host real equality,
metric-completeness datum, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness claim.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the request theorem. By
\autoref{thm:streamname-tail-compatible-window-request}, the finite-window
request is determined by the certified observation row, the tail-selector
route, the finite-window budget surface, and the pointwise $\RegSeqRatUp$
handoff row.

Next add the shared support. Apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-tail-compatible-window-coverage}; every accepted
handoff to a $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback or finite-window $\RealUp$ seal factors
through the same request together with the displayed $\hsame$, $\Cont$,
$\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.

Now compare two consumers with the same visible inventory. The request rows
agree by the first step, and the support rows agree by the second step, so the
two consumers replay the same finite StreamName window route into the
downstream readback or seal.

Finally use \autoref{thm:streamname-tail-compatible-window-nonescape}. It
excludes quotient stream equality, selected limits, host real equality,
metric-completeness data, and ambient $\RealUp$ completeness from the packet.
Thus the displayed request and support rows are terminal for finite-window
consumers.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName tail-compatible cofinal bundle lock]
\label{thm:streamname-tail-compatible-window-cofinal-bundle-lock}
Let $s,t:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{BHist}$ be public $\StreamNameUp$ rational
stream rows, let $\mathcal P:\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ be a finite bundle of
certified unary observation requests, and suppose one StreamName tail-selector
route serves every bundled request $n$ equipped with
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(n)$ and $\mathsf{InBundle}(n,\mathcal P)$. Every finite
family of bundled downstream reads into a $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback or a
finite-window $\RealUp$ seal factors through one displayed packet consisting
of exactly the following rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item the bundled unary index rows $n$, their $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(n)$
certificates, and their $\mathsf{InBundle}(n,\mathcal P)$ witnesses;
\item the shared tail-selector route serving the bundle;
\item the shared finite-window budget surface for $\mathcal P$;
\item the pointwise $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff rows
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(s(n),t(n))$ for the bundled indices; and
\item the support rows $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$.
\end{enumerate}
The packet is a finite cofinal lock for the bundled reads: any accepted
downstream read through the same tail-compatible route is classified by its
displayed bundle row and the shared support rows. The lock contains no
quotient stream equality, no selected limit, no host real equality, no
metric-completeness datum, and no ambient $\RealUp$ completeness claim.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:streamname-tail-compatible-window-request} to each
bundled request. For every certified index in $\mathcal P$, the request row
is the unary observation, its membership witness, the shared tail route, the
finite-window budget surface, and the corresponding pointwise
$\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff.

Next apply \autoref{thm:streamname-tail-compatible-window-coverage} to every
accepted downstream handoff in the finite family. The same support rows
$\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ route each admitted read through
the request rows just displayed.

Use \autoref{thm:streamname-tail-compatible-window-packet} to identify the
common finite packet. Since the bundle is finite, collecting the unary rows,
membership witnesses, pointwise handoffs, the shared route, the shared
budget surface, and the shared support rows produces one displayed packet
rather than a separate hidden route for each consumer.

Finally combine
\autoref{thm:streamname-tail-compatible-window-terminality} with
\autoref{thm:streamname-tail-compatible-window-nonescape}. Terminality makes
the displayed packet the unique route for consumers with the same visible
inventory, and nonescape excludes quotient stream equality, selected limits,
host real equality, metric-completeness data, and ambient $\RealUp$
completeness from the bundle lock.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName tail-compatible cofinal exhaustion]
\label{thm:streamname-tail-compatible-window-cofinal-exhaustion}
Let $s,t:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{BHist}$ be public $\StreamNameUp$ rational
stream rows, and let $\mathcal P:\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ be the finite packet of
certified unary observations served by the shared tail-compatible selector of
\autoref{thm:streamname-tail-compatible-window-cofinal-bundle-lock}. Every
finite family of accepted downstream reads from that same bundled packet into a
$\RegSeqRatUp$ readback or a finite-window $\RealUp$ seal is terminally
exhausted by the bundled unary rows, their $\mathsf{InBundle}$ witnesses, the
shared tail selector, the shared budget surface, the pointwise
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$ handoff rows, and the support rows
$\hsame,\Cont,\Pkg,\NameCert$.

Equality of these rows is exactly equality of the consumer route. No quotient
stream equality, selected limit, host real equality, metric-completeness datum,
or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness coordinate remains available to distinguish
two accepted finite-window consumers over the packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First use
\autoref{thm:streamname-tail-compatible-window-cofinal-bundle-lock}. It collects
the bundled unary rows, their membership witnesses, the shared tail selector,
the shared budget surface, pointwise rational-history classifier rows, and the
support rows into one finite packet for the family of downstream reads.

Next apply \autoref{thm:streamname-tail-compatible-window-coverage}. Each
accepted $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback or finite-window $\RealUp$ seal route factors
through that packet, so the consumer has no source coordinate beyond the
displayed rows.

Then apply \autoref{thm:streamname-tail-compatible-window-terminality}. If two
accepted consumers expose the same bundled rows and support rows, terminality
identifies their finite-window route through the same packet.

Finally use \autoref{thm:streamname-tail-compatible-window-nonescape}. The
excluded quotient, limit, host-real, metric-completeness, and ambient-completion
coordinates are not packet rows, so no remaining downstream distinction can be
read after the displayed finite inventory agrees.
\end{proof}
