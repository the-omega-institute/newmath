\begin{theorem}[StreamName RegSeqRat Real budget factorization]
\label{thm:streamname-regseqrat-real-budget-factorization}
Every current $\RealUp$-and-Completion consumer that reads a StreamName window, a $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, a $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, and a $\RealUp$ seal factors through exactly the four finite faces
$$
\StreamNameUp,\quad \DyadicRatCoreUp,\quad \RegSeqRatUp,\quad \RealUp
$$
together with only the displayed support rows
$$
\BHist,\quad \hsame,\quad \Cont,\quad \Pkg,\quad \NameCert.
$$
Equivalently, the budget boundary for the open L10 route is the finite StreamName window face, the DyadicRatCore tolerance-ledger face, the RegSeqRat readback face, the RealUp seal face, and the BEDC support rows. No fifth consumer face is needed to read the present Real-and-Completion route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply \autoref{thm:streamname-real-completion-four-object-exit-route}. It names the present Real-and-Completion exit read as the four faces $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ together with $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$.

Next use \autoref{thm:streamname-open-phase-four-face-minimality}. The StreamName window, DyadicRatCore ledger, RegSeqRat readback, and RealUp seal are not optional coordinates of a larger object; each is one required face of the same finite route.

Now invoke \autoref{thm:streamname-real-completion-four-object-terminal-pullback}. It identifies any two accepted consumer reads that expose the same four faces and support rows with the same terminal finite BEDC pullback.

Finally read the Real seal through \autoref{thm:realup-finite-window-exhaustion-obligation}. The Real-side endpoint is still a finite-window obligation, so the budget route has no ambient completion coordinate beyond the four faces and the displayed support rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName RegSeqRat Real budget non-escape]
\label{thm:streamname-regseqrat-real-budget-nonescape}
The budget factorization of \autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-real-budget-factorization} exports no quotient stream equality, no host sequence equality, no selected limit, no ambient $\RealUp$ completeness datum, no metric-completeness datum, no countable-choice row, no Classical datum, and no standard bridge row. Every downstream consumer receives only the finite StreamName window, DyadicRatCore tolerance ledger, RegSeqRat readback, RealUp seal, and the rows
$$
\BHist,\quad \hsame,\quad \Cont,\quad \Pkg,\quad \NameCert.
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from \autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-real-budget-factorization}. It reduces the consumer route to the four finite faces and the displayed BEDC support rows.

Apply \autoref{thm:streamname-real-completion-four-object-exit-route} and \autoref{thm:streamname-open-phase-four-face-minimality}. These results list the exclusions already present at the exit boundary and prevent a fifth face from replacing any required coordinate.

Use \autoref{thm:streamname-real-completion-four-object-terminal-pullback} for downstream reads. The terminal pullback has no coordinate in which a quotient equality, host sequence equality, selected limit, completeness datum, choice row, Classical datum, or bridge row could be stored.

Finally cite \autoref{thm:realup-finite-window-exhaustion-obligation}. The Real-facing read is exhausted by finite windows, so the budget boundary remains a finite BEDC route rather than an ambient completeness assertion.
\end{proof}
