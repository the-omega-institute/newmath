\begin{theorem}[StreamName RegSeqRat Real budget cocycle]
\label{thm:streamname-regseqrat-real-budget-cocycle}
Let $\mathcal W$ be an accepted terminal finite $\StreamNameUp$ window on the
L10 four-face route. Suppose its $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback and downstream
$\RealUp$ seal are read through the same displayed $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ budget
row and the same support rows
$$
\BHist,\quad \hsame,\quad \Cont,\quad \Pkg,\quad \NameCert.
$$
Then the terminal window, the RegSeqRat readback, and the Real seal form one
budget cocycle: after terminal fusion, none of the three faces can be
independently reindexed away from the shared budget row.

The cocycle exposes no quotient stream equality, selected limit, host sequence
equality, countable-choice schedule, metric-completeness datum, bridge row, or
ambient $\RealUp$ completeness field.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with \autoref{thm:streamname-real-completion-terminal-window-fusion}. It
constructs the terminal packet from the StreamName finite request window, the
DyadicRatCore tolerance ledger, the RegSeqRat readback, the Real seal, and the
displayed support rows.

Next apply \autoref{thm:streamname-terminal-fusion-common-refinement-stability}
and \autoref{thm:streamname-terminal-fusion-phase-exit-consumer-readback}. The
common refinement and terminal consumer readback keep the RegSeqRat-facing and
Real-facing reads on the same fused terminal surface.

Use \autoref{thm:streamname-terminal-fusion-support-minimality}. Since deleting
the budget-facing support or any of the four roadmap faces destroys the
accepted packet, no separate reindexing row can replace the shared dyadic
budget after the fusion is read.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:streamname-terminal-window-fusion-nonescape}. The
terminal consumer has no selected limit, quotient stream equality, host
sequence equality, choice schedule, metric-completeness datum, bridge row, or
ambient Real-completeness coordinate, so the shared budget row is the only
readable route through the StreamName, RegSeqRat, and Real faces.
\end{proof}
