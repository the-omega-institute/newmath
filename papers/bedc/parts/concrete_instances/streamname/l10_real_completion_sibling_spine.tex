\section{StreamName L10 Real-Completion Sibling Spine}
\label{sec:streamname-l10-real-completion-sibling-spine}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName L10 Real-completion sibling spine]
\label{thm:streamname-l10-real-completion-sibling-spine}
Every $\StreamNameUp$ open-phase consumer that reaches the $\RealUp$ and $\CompletionUp$ endpoint surfaces factors through the sibling spine
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert},
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert},
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert},
and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchylimitseal-namecert}
before any endpoint seal is public. The spine exposes finite stream windows, regular-rational readback, dyadic tolerance ledgers, Real seal rows, Cauchy-limit seal rows, displayed $\BHist$ observations, componentwise $\hsame$ transports, displayed $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ rows only.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with \autoref{thm:streamname-real-completion-sibling-route}. It sends every Real and Completion finite-window read through the RegSeqRat, DyadicRatCore, and Real sibling surfaces before the read can become seal data.

Next apply \autoref{thm:streamname-open-phase-exit-boundary}. The open-phase exit boundary identifies the public StreamName, RegSeqRat, DyadicRatCore, Real, observation, transport, route, provenance, and local naming rows, and excludes quotient streams, host sequence equality, selected limits, ambient completeness, metric-completeness data, and standard bridge rows.

Finally attach the Cauchy-limit seal sibling. The endpoint seal is public only after the displayed finite-window readback and dyadic ledger have reached the Cauchy-limit seal surface, so the route is the listed sibling spine rather than an additional endpoint selector.
\end{proof}
