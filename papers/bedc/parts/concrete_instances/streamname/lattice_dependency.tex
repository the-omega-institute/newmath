\section{StreamName Lattice Dependency}
\label{sec:streamname-lattice-dependency}

The finite-window $\StreamNameUp$ package is consumed by the $\RegularCauchyNameUp$ certificate of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regularcauchyname-namecert}. Its public rows supply the scheduled window, bundle membership, certified unary observations, pointwise $\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier data, transport routes, package provenance, and local \NameCert{} rows that the regular Cauchy name packet reads before exposing a real-name seal.

This edge is the L10 scheduled finite-window handoff from $\StreamNameUp$ into the $\RegularCauchyNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ chain. The handoff is recorded before the $\RegSeqRatUp$ and $\RealUp$ seal rows, so the later consumer can cite the StreamName window surface without importing quotient stream equality, host sequence equality, selected limits, or ambient completeness.

\begin{theorem}[StreamName L10 gateway factorization]
\label{thm:streamname-l10-gateway-factorization}
Let $W$ be an accepted $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window packet consumed by accepted $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RegularCauchyNameUp$, $\FiniteTailDiagonalSealUp$, $\FiniteCauchyApproximationUp$, and $\RealUp$ rows. Then every accepted L10 read of $W$ factors through one finite displayed route:
$$
\begin{gathered}
\text{$\StreamNameUp$ window rows}
\longrightarrow
\text{unary observations}
\longrightarrow
\text{$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}/\mathsf{InBundle}$ ledger}
\longrightarrow
\text{$\RegSeqRatUp$ regular source row}
\\
\longrightarrow
\text{dyadic observation ledger}
\longrightarrow
\text{finite tail witness}
\longrightarrow
\text{finite approximation error row}
\longrightarrow
\text{$\RealUp$ seal row.}
\end{gathered}
$$
The route is mediated only by the displayed $\hsame$ transports, $\Cont$ continuations, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ rows of the consumed packets. It exports no quotient stream equality, selected limit, host real equality, or ambient completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First build the shared finite-window spine. The StreamName window rows, unary observations, and $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}/\mathsf{InBundle}$ ledger are the public surface of the accepted $\StreamNameUp$ packet, and the $\RegSeqRatUp$ row reads that same surface through the regular source coordinate.

Next compose the sibling handoffs. The finite-window diagonal fusion of \autoref{thm:streamname-finite-window-diagonal-fusion} identifies the common route through the $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\FiniteTailDiagonalSealUp$, $\FiniteCauchyApproximationUp$, and $\RealUp$ rows. The real regular-sequence handoff of \autoref{thm:streamname-real-regseq-finite-window-handoff} fixes the same window before the real-name seal is exposed.

The remaining coordinates are supplied by the cited consumer chapters. The $\RegularCauchyNameUp$ chapter reads the regular source row, the $\FiniteTailDiagonalSealUp$ chapter supplies the finite tail witness, and the $\FiniteCauchyApproximationUp$ chapter supplies the dyadic observation ledger and finite approximation error row. The $\RealUp$ chapter receives only the resulting seal-facing finite route.

Coordinate exhaustion gives the boundary clause. None of the displayed rows is a quotient stream, a limit selector, a host real equality witness, or an ambient completeness object, and the transport and continuation rows only replay the listed finite coordinates.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName finite-window diagonal fusion]
\label{thm:streamname-finite-window-diagonal-fusion}
Let $W$ be an accepted $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window packet consumed simultaneously by an accepted $\RegSeqRatUp$ row, an accepted $\FiniteTailDiagonalSealUp$ packet, and an accepted $\FiniteCauchyApproximationUp$ packet. Then the three consumers determine one shared finite observation route into the $\RealUp$ seal: the window rows of $W$, the regular source row, the dyadic ledger, the selected tail witness, and the finite approximation error row agree through the displayed $\hsame$ transports and the displayed $\Cont$ routes.

Equivalently, every $\RealUp$ seal-facing read factors through the common route
$$
\StreamNameUp
\xrightarrow{\hsame,\Cont}
\RegSeqRatUp
\xrightarrow{\hsame,\Cont}
\FiniteTailDiagonalSealUp
\xrightarrow{\hsame,\Cont}
\FiniteCauchyApproximationUp
\xrightarrow{\hsame,\Cont}
\RealUp.
$$
The route exports only finite window rows, the accepted regular source row, dyadic observation ledgers, the selected tail witness, finite approximation error data, componentwise transport, continuation replay, package provenance, and local naming. It does not assert quotient stream equality, selected limits, host real equality, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the accepted $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window packet. Its public rows supply the scheduled finite window, bundle membership, unary observations, pointwise $\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier data, componentwise $\hsame$ transports, and displayed $\Cont$ routes.

The $\RegSeqRatUp$ consumer reads those rows through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regularcauchyname-namecert}. This fixes the regular source row on the same finite window before any real-seal row is exposed.

Next use \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finitetaildiagonalseal-namecert}. The accepted $\FiniteTailDiagonalSealUp$ packet reads the same finite window, the same regular source row, and the selected tail witness before its $\RealUp$ seal handoff.

Finally use \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finite-cauchy-approximation-namecert}. The accepted $\FiniteCauchyApproximationUp$ packet reads the same source and window together with the dyadic observation ledger and finite error row. Its handoff therefore meets the finite-tail route on the displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ rows.

Coordinate exhaustion gives the exclusion clause. None of the three accepted consumers contains a coordinate for quotient stream equality, a selected limit, host real equality, or ambient completeness, so the fused route cannot export any of those endpoints.
\end{proof}
