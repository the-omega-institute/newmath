\section{Public obligation surface}
\label{sec:streamname-public-obligation-surface}

\begin{definition}[StreamNameUp public reindexing obligation]
\label{def:streamname-public-reindexing-obligation}
The public reindexing obligation for $\StreamNameUp$ is the finite BEDC row
package that, for every certified unary observation reindexer and every
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ window, transports the pointwise $\RatUp$ rows of the
public countable-name interface through the finite-window interface and
records the resulting observation rows in the same $\mathsf{BHist}$ ledger.
The obligation cites
\autoref{thm:streamname-public-countable-name-interface},
\autoref{thm:streamname-public-finite-window-interface},
\autoref{thm:streamname-finite-window-reindexing-stability}, and
\autoref{thm:streamname-finite-window-reindexing-composition}; it introduces
no completion, metric, or quotient row.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[StreamNameUp public constant obligation]
\label{thm:streamname-public-constant-obligation}
Every public constant $\StreamNameUp$ packet is accepted by the countable-name
interface and by every finite-window interface obtained from certified unary
observations. Its visible rows are exactly the constant $\RatUp$ seed row, the
pointwise carrier/classifier rows, the empty-window boundary, and the finite
ledger entries selected by the observation bundle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:streamname-public-countable-name-interface}. It exports the
constant stream witness together with the pointwise $\RatUp$ carrier and
classifier rows over certified unary observations.

Next apply \autoref{thm:streamname-public-finite-window-interface}. A finite
window only restricts those pointwise rows to the observations named by the
bundle, while the empty-window boundary contributes the vacuous case.

Thus the public constant obligation is the same constant seed read through
each selected observation row. No regularity witness, metric datum, completion
endpoint, or quotient representative is present in the public packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamNameUp public transport obligation]
\label{thm:streamname-public-transport-obligation}
Let two certified $\StreamNameUp$ observation maps be related by displayed
$\hsame$ rows, and let the finite-window $\Cont$ routes and $\Pkg$ provenance
rows be transported componentwise. Then every public finite-window classifier
row for the first map transports to a public finite-window classifier row for
the second map over the same certified unary observations.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use \autoref{thm:streamname-public-countable-name-interface} to read the
public carrier and classifier rows as pointwise $\RatUp$ rows over certified
unary observations. The supplied $\hsame$ rows transport those pointwise rows.

Then use \autoref{thm:streamname-public-finite-window-interface} to restrict
the transported pointwise rows to the selected $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ window.
The transported $\Cont$ and $\Pkg$ rows preserve the same finite provenance
route through the public interface.

The resulting packet is therefore a public finite-window classifier row for
the second map. The construction does not add a completion or regularity row;
it only repacks the transported public rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamNameUp public finite-ledger obligation]
\label{thm:streamname-public-ledger-obligation}
For every public $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window packet, the exported ledger is
exactly the finite family of certified unary observation rows selected by its
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ together with the corresponding pointwise $\RatUp$
classifier rows. Reindexing, restriction, and composition do not expose any
row outside that finite ledger.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with \autoref{thm:streamname-public-finite-window-interface}. It
identifies the finite-window surface as the restriction of the public
countable-name interface to the observations carried by the bundle.

For reindexing, apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-finite-window-reindexing-stability}. The reindexed
window reads pointwise rows only at observations whose images are members of
the original bundle.

For composition, apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-finite-window-reindexing-composition}. The two-step
and one-step reindexing routes repack the same endpoint, classifier, $\hsame$,
and finite ledger rows over the target bundle. Hence no hidden observation
source is exported.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamNameUp public RealUp handoff obligation]
\label{thm:streamname-public-real-handoff-obligation}
The public $\StreamNameUp$ handoff to downstream $\RegSeqRat$ and $\RealUp$
consumers is the package consisting of the countable-name interface, the
finite-window interface, the public reindexing obligation of
\autoref{def:streamname-public-reindexing-obligation}, the constant-stream
obligation of \autoref{thm:streamname-public-constant-obligation}, the
transport obligation of \autoref{thm:streamname-public-transport-obligation},
and the finite-ledger obligation of
\autoref{thm:streamname-public-ledger-obligation}. The handoff supplies
certified unary observation rows and pointwise rational classifier rows only;
it does not supply $\RegSeqRat$ regularity, $\RealUp$ completeness, or a
standard bridge.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The countable-name and finite-window interfaces provide the public carrier,
classifier, pointwise rational rows, and bundle restriction rows. The
reindexing obligation records how these rows move through certified unary
observation maps.

The constant, transport, and finite-ledger obligations give the three public
consumer cases: constant names, $\hsame/\Cont$ transport, and finite-window
ledger readback. Each case cites only the public StreamNameUp rows already
listed above.

Downstream $\RegSeqRat$ and $\RealUp$ chapters may therefore consume a stable
stream-name observation surface, but any regular Cauchy condition, completion
witness, or standard bridge must be supplied in the downstream chapter rather
than by this handoff.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamNameUp finite-window schedule exhaustion]
\label{thm:streamname-finite-window-schedule-exhaustion}
Any downstream finite-window request whose rows consist of a public
$\StreamNameUp$ observation map, certified $\UnaryHistory$ indices, a
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ schedule, pointwise $\RatUp$ endpoint rows, and
$\RegSeqRat$ or $\RealUp$ consumer provenance is exhausted by the public
finite-window interface of
\autoref{thm:streamname-public-finite-window-interface}, the public reindexing
obligation of \autoref{def:streamname-public-reindexing-obligation}, the
transport obligation of \autoref{thm:streamname-public-transport-obligation},
the finite ledger of \autoref{thm:streamname-public-ledger-obligation}, and the
handoff boundary of \autoref{thm:streamname-public-real-handoff-obligation}.
The request introduces no additional $\RegSeqRat$ regularity row, $\RealUp$
completion row, quotient representative, hidden limit selector, or observation
source beyond that union.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the carrier and classifier through
\autoref{thm:streamname-public-finite-window-interface}. The public observation
map and certified $\UnaryHistory$ indices determine the displayed pointwise
$\RatUp$ rows, while the $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ schedule restricts those rows
to the requested finite window.

Next apply \autoref{def:streamname-public-reindexing-obligation}. It repacks
the same scheduled rows after any certified unary reindexing, so the displayed
bundle is a restriction and reindexing of the public countable-name surface
rather than a separate request for stream data.

Use \autoref{thm:streamname-public-transport-obligation} for the pointwise
rational endpoint rows. The supplied $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ rows
transport the endpoint classifiers componentwise and preserve the same
finite-window provenance.

Finally combine \autoref{thm:streamname-public-ledger-obligation} with
\autoref{thm:streamname-public-real-handoff-obligation}. The ledger identifies
all exposed rows as scheduled public observation rows and pointwise rational
classifiers, and the handoff theorem states that downstream $\RegSeqRat$ and
$\RealUp$ consumers must supply their own regularity, completion, quotient, and
limit-selection data. Hence the finite-window request is exhausted by the
listed public StreamNameUp obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamNameUp RegSeqRat denominator-source exhaustion]
\label{thm:streamname-regseqrat-denominator-source-exhaustion}
Let a downstream $\RegSeqRat$ finite-window consumer receive as input a public
$\StreamNameUp$ finite-window request together with dyadic denominator rows.
Then the consumer reads exactly the selected $\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$
rows, certified $\UnaryHistory$ observation rows, finite
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ ledger, $\mathsf{InBundle}$ membership rows, displayed
$\hsame$ comparisons, $\Cont$ routes, $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance, and the
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$ denominator-ledger rows already exported by the dyadic
real-phase source boundary. It reads no $\RegSeqRat$ regularity witness, seal
classifier, completion datum, quotient representative, hidden limit selector,
or host index equality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the public real handoff of
\autoref{thm:streamname-public-real-handoff-obligation}. It exposes the
countable-name and finite-window StreamNameUp rows, the certified unary
observation surface, and the public pointwise rational classifier rows.

Next use \autoref{thm:streamname-finite-window-schedule-exhaustion}. The
finite request is therefore exhausted by the displayed observation indices,
probe bundle, bundle-membership rows, rational endpoint rows, $\hsame$
comparisons, continuation routes, and package provenance.

The RegSeqRat-facing handoff of
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-regseq-finite-window-handoff} identifies the same
finite rows as the rows consumed by the downstream regular-sequence interface.
The denominator side is supplied separately by
\autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-real-phase-source-coverage}, which contributes only
the dyadic denominator ledger exported by the real-phase source boundary.

Combining these two source boundaries gives the displayed row inventory. None
of the cited boundaries exports a regularity witness, seal classifier,
completion datum, quotient representative, hidden limit selector, or host
index equality, so no such datum can be read by the finite-window consumer.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamNameUp RegSeqRat window union exhaustion]
\label{thm:streamname-regseqrat-window-union-exhaustion}
Let two downstream finite-window requests over a common public
$\StreamNameUp$ observation map be displayed by probe bundles $B_1$ and
$B_2$. Suppose each request is exhausted by
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-denominator-source-exhaustion}. Then the
combined request over the displayed bundle union is exhausted by the public
StreamName finite-window interface, the two displayed
$\mathsf{InBundle}$ membership families for $B_1$ and $B_2$, the pointwise
$\RatUp$ classifier rows, the dyadic denominator ledger rows, $\hsame$,
$\Cont$, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance. The union request exports no
$\RegSeqRat$ regularity witness, $\RealUp$ completion row, quotient
representative, hidden limit selector, or host index equality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the common public classifier supplied by
\autoref{thm:streamname-public-finite-window-interface}. It fixes the
observation map, the certified $\UnaryHistory$ observation rows, and the
pointwise $\RatUp$ classifier rows before either downstream finite request is
chosen.

Apply \autoref{thm:streamname-finite-window-schedule-exhaustion} separately
to $B_1$ and to $B_2$. This gives two finite schedules with their displayed
$\mathsf{InBundle}$ membership families, $\hsame$ comparisons, $\Cont$
routes, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance, all over the same public StreamName
observation surface.

Next use \autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-denominator-source-exhaustion} for
each scheduled request. The first request contributes only its displayed
observation rows and dyadic denominator ledger rows, and the second request
contributes the same kind of displayed rows over its own membership family.

It remains to repack the two finite ledgers. For an observation in the
displayed bundle union, case on whether the membership row comes from $B_1$
or from $B_2$. In the first case read the row from the first exhausted
request; in the second case read it from the second exhausted request. If a
row is displayed by both bundles, the common public classifier and the
displayed $\hsame$ rows identify the two readbacks as the same public
StreamName observation row.

Thus every row in the combined finite request is one of the displayed public
observation, membership, rational-classifier, denominator-ledger,
continuation, or provenance rows already listed. Since neither component
exhaustion theorem exports regularity, completion, quotient, limit-selection,
or host index equality data, the bundle-union request exports none of those
rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName RegSeqRat RealUp observation coverage]
\label{thm:streamname-regseqrat-realup-observation-coverage}
Every finite-window handoff from public $\StreamNameUp$ to a downstream
$\RegSeqRat$ consumer or finite-window $\RealUp$ consumer is covered exactly by
certified $\UnaryHistory$ observation rows, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ scheduling
rows, $\mathsf{InBundle}$ membership rows, pointwise $\RatUp$ classifier rows,
displayed $\hsame$ comparisons, $\Cont$ routes, $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance, and
the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ denominator ledger exposed at the real-phase source
boundary. The handoff exports no $\RegSeqRat$ regularity witness, $\RealUp$
completion row, quotient representative, hidden limit selector, or host index
equality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:streamname-public-real-handoff-obligation}. It fixes the
public observation surface for downstream $\RegSeqRat$ and finite-window
$\RealUp$ consumers, and it restricts the handoff to displayed StreamNameUp
rows rather than to host streams.

Apply \autoref{thm:streamname-finite-window-schedule-exhaustion}. The finite
request is then exhausted by the certified $\UnaryHistory$ observation rows,
the $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ schedule, $\mathsf{InBundle}$ membership, pointwise
$\RatUp$ classifier rows, displayed $\hsame$ comparisons, continuation routes,
and package provenance.

For a $\RegSeqRat$ consumer, use
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-denominator-source-exhaustion}. It identifies
the same public observation rows and adds only the dyadic denominator ledger
needed by the regular-sequence interface.

Finally, \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-real-phase-source-coverage} supplies the
source boundary for that denominator ledger. Thus the public observation rows,
the dyadic denominator rows, and the downstream seal boundary form one finite
BEDC source inventory. The exclusion clauses follow because none of the four
cited boundaries exports regularity witnesses, completion rows, quotient
representatives, hidden limit selectors, or host index equality.
\end{proof}
