\begin{definition}[StreamName RegSeqRat RealUp tail-selector transport packet]
\label{def:streamname-regseqrat-realup-tail-selector-transport-packet}
A StreamName RegSeqRat RealUp tail-selector transport packet from a selected
finite $\RealUp$ window $W_0$ to a selected finite $\RealUp$ window $W_1$ is
the finite ledger consisting exactly of the following rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item a shared
$\mathsf{RatStreamNameFiniteWindowClassifier}(s,t,\mathcal P)$ row for the
public $\StreamNameUp$ rational stream endpoints $s,t:\mathsf{BHist}\to
\mathsf{BHist}$ over the selected finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ window
$\mathcal P$;
\item for each certified selected observation $n$, the bundled
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(n)$ and $\mathsf{InBundle}(n,\mathcal P)$ rows, together
with the pointwise $\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(s(n),t(n))$ row exposed by
the StreamName observation surface;
\item the accepted $\RegSeqRat$ spine row selected by those pointwise rational
history rows and by the finite-window handoff;
\item the matching DyadicRatCore endpoint rows read by the two selected
$\RealUp$ windows from that same $\RegSeqRat$ spine; and
\item the common finite $\Cont$, $\hsame$, $\Pkg$, and
$\mathsf{RealLedgerPolicy}$ ledger joining the StreamName endpoint rows, the
accepted $\RegSeqRat$ spine row, the DyadicRatCore endpoint rows, and the two
selected $\RealUp$ readback packets.
\end{enumerate}

The packet exposes no quotient row, completion row, Classical representative,
metric-completeness certificate, arithmetic or order operation, standard
bridge row, or selector not already contained in the displayed finite ledger.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName RegSeqRat RealUp tail-selector transport]
\label{thm:streamname-regseqrat-realup-tail-selector-transport}
Let $W_0$ and $W_1$ be selected finite $\RealUp$ observation-window packages
over the same public $\StreamNameUp$ rational stream endpoints and the same
finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ window. If $W_0$ and $W_1$ are connected by a
StreamName RegSeqRat RealUp tail-selector transport packet, then the selected
$\RegSeqRat$ tail-classifier readback transported from $W_0$ by the packet
agrees with the selected $\RegSeqRat$ tail-classifier readback read from
$W_1$.

The agreement is a finite-window agreement over the displayed
$\mathsf{RatStreamNameFiniteWindowClassifier}$ row, the bundled
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}/\mathsf{InBundle}$ rows, the accepted $\RegSeqRat$
spine row, the matching DyadicRatCore endpoint rows, and the shared
$\Cont/\hsame/\Pkg/\mathsf{RealLedgerPolicy}$ ledger. It does not identify
quotient objects, completion objects, Classical representatives,
metric-completeness data, arithmetic or order rows, standard bridge rows, or
external selector rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First read the StreamName side of the packet. Apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-observation-stability} to the displayed
$\mathsf{RatStreamNameFiniteWindowClassifier}$ row and to each bundled
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}/\mathsf{InBundle}$ observation. The selected pointwise
rational-history rows therefore stay fixed under the packet's $\hsame$
transport.

Next form the uniform finite-window handoff. Apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-uniform-finite-window-seal} to the stabilized
StreamName rows and the accepted $\RegSeqRat$ spine row. This gives one finite
seal surface carrying the same $\Cont$, $\hsame$, $\Pkg$, and DyadicRatCore
endpoint rows for both selected windows.

Now read the RealUp boundary through that finite seal. Use
\autoref{thm:realup-streamname-regseqrat-uniform-window-readback}. The two
selected $\RealUp$ windows expose the same visible endpoint readback rows and
the same $\mathsf{RealLedgerPolicy}$ row on the selected finite window.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:regseqrat-tail-classifier-exactness}. Its
hypotheses are exactly the common StreamName classifier, the accepted
$\RegSeqRat$ spine, the matching DyadicRatCore endpoint rows, and the shared
finite ledger established above. Hence the transported tail-classifier readback
from $W_0$ is the same selected readback as the one read from $W_1$, and the
scoped exactness theorem supplies the stated exclusions.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName RegSeqRat RealUp tail-selector transport composition]
\label{thm:streamname-regseqrat-realup-tail-selector-transport-composition}
Let $W_0$, $W_1$, and $W_2$ be selected finite $\RealUp$ observation-window
packages over the same public $\StreamNameUp$ rational stream endpoints, and
let the finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ windows used by the $W_0$ to $W_1$ and
$W_1$ to $W_2$ packets be compatible on their shared selected observations. If
$W_0$ is connected to $W_1$ by a StreamName RegSeqRat RealUp tail-selector
transport packet and $W_1$ is connected to $W_2$ by a second such packet, then
the concatenated visible ledger is again a StreamName RegSeqRat RealUp
tail-selector transport packet from $W_0$ to $W_2$.

The concatenated packet uses the composed $\Cont$ rows, the composed displayed
$\hsame$ rows, the concatenated $\Pkg$ provenance, and the combined
$\mathsf{RealLedgerPolicy}$ ledger. It transports the selected $\RegSeqRat$
tail classifier read from $W_0$ to the selected finite-window readback at
$W_2$. The assertion is only a finite-window transport statement over the
displayed $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ windows and the accepted $\RegSeqRat$ spine;
it adds no quotient row, completion row, Classical representative,
metric-completeness certificate, arithmetic or order operation, standard bridge
row, or hidden selector row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-realup-tail-selector-transport} to the
$W_0$ to $W_1$ packet. This identifies the selected $\RegSeqRat$ tail
classifier transported from $W_0$ with the selected finite-window readback
visible at $W_1$.

Next apply the same theorem to the $W_1$ to $W_2$ packet. The compatibility of
the displayed $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ windows makes the $W_1$ readback row used
by the first packet the same finite row consumed by the second packet.

Compose the visible transport rows. The $\hsame$ classifier rows compose on
the selected StreamName observations, on the accepted $\RegSeqRat$ spine, and
on the matching DyadicRatCore endpoint rows; the $\Cont$ rows concatenate the
finite routes; the $\Pkg$ rows concatenate the displayed provenance; and the
$\mathsf{RealLedgerPolicy}$ rows remain a single visible finite ledger over the
combined window.

Finally use \autoref{thm:regseqrat-tail-classifier-exactness} on the combined
finite-window row. The exactness theorem identifies the final row produced by
the composed ledger with the selected $\RegSeqRat$ tail-classifier readback at
$W_2$, so the concatenated ledger is precisely the required transport packet
from $W_0$ to $W_2$ with the stated exclusions.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName RegSeqRat RealUp tail denominator selector]
\label{thm:streamname-regseqrat-realup-tail-denominator-selector}
Let $W_0$ and $W_1$ be selected finite $\RealUp$ observation-window packages
over the same StreamName endpoints $s,t:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{BHist}$, the
same finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ window $\mathcal P$, and the same accepted
$\RegSeqRat$ spine row. Suppose they are connected by a StreamName RegSeqRat
RealUp tail-selector transport packet. Then the packet carries the public
denominator-window selector selected by $W_0$ to the same public
denominator-window selector read from $W_1$.

Equivalently, for every certified selected observation $n$ with
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(n)$ and $\mathsf{InBundle}(n,\mathcal P)$, the transported
tail readback has the same finite public selector surface
$$
\begin{gathered}
\mathsf{RatStreamNameFiniteWindowClassifier}(s,t,\mathcal P),\qquad
\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(n),\qquad
\mathsf{InBundle}(n,\mathcal P),\\
\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(s(n),t(n)),\qquad
\text{the downstream dyadic observation coverage row}
\end{gathered}
$$
as the selector read by the finite $\RealUp$ ledger of $W_1$. The conclusion is
only a finite selector statement over the accepted $\RegSeqRat$ spine and the
finite $\RealUp$ ledger; it adds no quotient row, completion object, Classical
representative, metric-completeness certificate, arithmetic or order row,
standard bridge row, or external selector.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-realup-tail-selector-transport}. The
transport packet identifies the selected $\RegSeqRat$ tail-classifier readback
from $W_0$ with the selected readback exposed by $W_1$, using only the shared
finite StreamName classifier, the bundled unary observations, the accepted
$\RegSeqRat$ spine, and the finite $\RealUp$ ledger.

Next isolate the denominator selector inside that transported readback. By
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-denominator-window-separation}, each certified
observation $n$ exposes only the displayed
$\mathsf{RatStreamNameFiniteWindowClassifier}$ row, the
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(n)$ row, the $\mathsf{InBundle}(n,\mathcal P)$ row, and
the pointwise $\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(s(n),t(n))$ row on the
$\RegSeqRat$ side.

Now read the same selector through the finite $\RealUp$ consumer. Apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-realup-denominator-window-normal-form}. It says that
the finite $\RealUp$ ledger contributes exactly the downstream dyadic
observation coverage row to that public denominator-window surface and no
regularity, seal, completion, quotient, or host-index coordinate.

Finally compare the two presentations by
\autoref{thm:streamname-pointwise-denominator-window-determinacy}. Both are
accepted over the same $s$, $t$, $\mathcal P$, certified observation $n$, and
accepted $\RegSeqRat$ spine, so the transported selector from $W_0$ and the
selector read from $W_1$ have the same public coordinates. The exclusion clauses
in the transport, separation, normal-form, and determinacy statements give the
stated boundary.
\end{proof}

$W_1$. Let $\mathcal Q$ be a certified subwindow of $\mathcal P$. Then the

same public denominator-window selector on $\mathcal Q$.

For each certified selected observation $n$ with $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(n)$ and

\begin{gathered}

\end{gathered}
$$
It introduces no observation outside $\mathcal Q$, quotient row, completion

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First restrict the two selected $\RealUp$ windows. Apply
\autoref{thm:real-stream-window-subwindow-extraction} to the finite

ambient classifier over $\mathcal P$ by

Now apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-realup-tail-denominator-selector} to the

\begin{theorem}[StreamName RegSeqRat RealUp tail denominator selector composition]
\label{thm:streamname-regseqrat-realup-tail-denominator-selector-composition}
Let $W_0$, $W_1$, and $W_2$ be selected finite $\RealUp$ observation-window
packages over the same public StreamName endpoints $s,t:\mathsf{BHist}\to
\mathsf{BHist}$ and the same accepted $\RegSeqRat$ spine row. Suppose the
$W_0$ to $W_1$ packet and the $W_1$ to $W_2$ packet are compatible StreamName
RegSeqRat RealUp tail-selector transport packets, and suppose each packet
carries the public denominator-window selector between its endpoints. Then the
two packets compose to carry the same public denominator-window selector from
$W_0$ to $W_2$.

The composed readback is entirely finite-window data. It reads only the
displayed $\mathsf{RatStreamNameFiniteWindowClassifier}$ rows, the bundled
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ and $\mathsf{InBundle}$ rows, the pointwise
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$ rows, the accepted $\RegSeqRat$ spine, and the
finite $\RealUp$ ledger carried by the compatible packets and their certified
subwindows. It introduces no quotient row, completion row, Classical
representative, metric-completeness certificate, arithmetic or order row,
standard bridge row, or hidden selector row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First compose the underlying tail-selector transport packets. Apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-realup-tail-selector-transport-composition}
to the compatible $W_0$ to $W_1$ and $W_1$ to $W_2$ packets. The result is a
visible finite transport packet from $W_0$ to $W_2$ over the same displayed
StreamName classifier rows, accepted $\RegSeqRat$ spine, and finite $\RealUp$
ledger.

Next identify the denominator selector transported by the first leg. Apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-realup-tail-denominator-selector} to the
$W_0$ to $W_1$ packet. This makes the public denominator-window selector read
from $W_0$ the same selector as the one exposed at $W_1$.

Now identify the denominator selector transported by the second leg. Apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-realup-tail-denominator-selector} to the
$W_1$ to $W_2$ packet. Compatibility says that the $W_1$ selector consumed by
this leg is the same finite public selector produced by the first leg.

It remains to check that no extra selector coordinates appear when the two
windows are read through smaller certified faces. Apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-realup-tail-denominator-selector-subwindow}
to each certified shared subwindow. The conclusion restricts every intermediate
readback to $\mathsf{RatStreamNameFiniteWindowClassifier}$,
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$, $\mathsf{InBundle}$, pointwise
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$, the accepted $\RegSeqRat$ spine, and the
finite $\RealUp$ ledger.

Combining these finite identifications gives a single public
denominator-window selector transported from $W_0$ to $W_2$. The exclusions
come from the three cited transport, denominator-selector, and subwindow
statements: no quotient, completion, Classical representative,
metric-completeness, arithmetic or order, standard bridge, or hidden selector
row is available in the composed readback.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName RegSeqRat RealUp tail selector associative readback]
\label{thm:streamname-regseqrat-realup-tail-selector-associative-readback}
Let $W_0,W_1,W_2,W_3$ be selected finite $\RealUp$ observation-window
packages over the same public StreamName endpoints
$s,t:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{BHist}$ and the same accepted $\RegSeqRat$
spine row. Suppose the $W_0$ to $W_1$, $W_1$ to $W_2$, and $W_2$ to $W_3$
legs are compatible StreamName RegSeqRat RealUp tail-selector transport
packets, and suppose each leg carries the public denominator-window selector
between its endpoints. Then the two finite-transport bracketings
$$
\begin{gathered}
((W_0\to W_1)\to W_2)\to W_3,\\
W_0\to (W_1\to (W_2\to W_3))
\end{gathered}
$$
read the same public denominator-window selector from $W_0$ to $W_3$.

The common readback surface is only the displayed
$\mathsf{RatStreamNameFiniteWindowClassifier}$ row, the
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ rows, the $\mathsf{InBundle}$ rows, the pointwise
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$ rows, the accepted $\RegSeqRat$ spine, the
finite $\RealUp$ ledger, and the visible $\Cont/\hsame/\Pkg$ rows carried by
the three compatible legs and their certified shared subwindows. It adds no
quotient row, completion row, Classical representative, metric-completeness
certificate, arithmetic or order row, standard bridge row, or hidden selector
row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First compose the transport packets with the left bracketing. Apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-realup-tail-selector-transport-composition}
to the $W_0$ to $W_1$ and $W_1$ to $W_2$ packets, and then compose the result
with the $W_2$ to $W_3$ packet. This gives a visible finite packet from $W_0$
to $W_3$.

Next compose the same three packets with the right bracketing. Apply the same
composition theorem first to the $W_1$ to $W_2$ and $W_2$ to $W_3$ packets,
and then compose the $W_0$ to $W_1$ packet with that result. This gives a
second visible finite packet from $W_0$ to $W_3$ over the same endpoint rows.

Now read the denominator selector through the two-leg selector theorem on each
bracketing. For the left bracketing, apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-realup-tail-denominator-selector-composition}
to the $W_0$ to $W_2$ composite and the $W_2$ to $W_3$ leg. For the right
bracketing, apply the same theorem to the $W_0$ to $W_1$ leg and the
$W_1$ to $W_3$ composite.

The shared finite faces are identified by
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-realup-tail-denominator-selector-subwindow}.
On every certified subwindow, both bracketings expose the same
$\mathsf{RatStreamNameFiniteWindowClassifier}$, $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$,
$\mathsf{InBundle}$, pointwise $\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$, accepted
$\RegSeqRat$ spine, and finite $\RealUp$ ledger rows.

It remains only to compare the displayed ledgers. The $\hsame$ rows compose
associatively on StreamName observations, on the accepted $\RegSeqRat$ spine,
and on the dyadic endpoint rows; the $\Cont$ routes concatenate
associatively; and the $\Pkg$ provenance rows name the same three visible
legs in either bracketing. Transitivity of these displayed comparisons
identifies the two public readbacks, giving the stated selector from $W_0$ to
$W_3$ and the stated exclusions.
\end{proof}
