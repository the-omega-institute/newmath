\section{StreamName lattice-meet terminal factorization}
\label{sec:streamname-lattice-meet-terminal-factorization}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName lattice-meet terminal factorization]
\label{thm:streamname-lattice-meet-terminal-factorization}
A $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window lattice meet packet determines the terminal finite surface consumed by $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback and finite $\RealUp$ seal handoffs. The terminal surface consists exactly of the shared rational classifier, the $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ row, $\mathsf{InBundle}$ membership rows, $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ observation rows, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback surface, the finite $\RealUp$ seal row, the displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ routes, the $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local $\NameCert$ rows.

No terminal consumer of the meet packet may replace that surface by quotient stream equality, host index equality, selected limits, host real equality, ambient completeness, or an off-ledger $\RealUp$ seal coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the meet exhaustion theorem \autoref{thm:streamname-finite-window-lattice-meet-exhaustion}. It identifies the common finite packet read by RegSeqRat and RealUp consumers: classifier, probe bundle, membership, unary-history, dyadic, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.

Next use the synchronized-window Real seal handoff of \autoref{thm:streamname-synchronized-window-real-seal-handoff}. The synchronized window sends the same selected observations to the finite $\RealUp$ seal route, so the seal-facing read is not independent of the meet packet.

Now apply the RegSeqRat--RealUp source-boundary exhaustion theorem \autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-realup-source-boundary-exhaustion}. It places the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback and finite $\RealUp$ handoff on the same StreamName, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local naming rows.

Finally combine the pointwise handoff \autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-pointwise-handoff} with the Real seal factorization boundary \autoref{thm:streamname-real-seal-factorization-boundary}. These rows account for the terminal readback and seal coordinates and leave no carrier slot for quotient streams, host index equality, selected limits, host real equality, ambient completeness, or off-ledger seal data.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName lattice-meet terminal source uniqueness]
\label{thm:streamname-lattice-meet-terminal-source-uniqueness}
For a $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window lattice meet packet, every terminal consumer that reaches the finite $\RealUp$ seal through the accepted $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback and the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger displays the same terminal source rows. These rows are precisely the shared rational classifier, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ row, $\mathsf{InBundle}$ rows, $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ rows, dyadic ledger, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback surface, finite $\RealUp$ seal row, displayed $\hsame$ transports, displayed $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply \autoref{thm:streamname-lattice-meet-terminal-factorization}. It gives the terminal finite surface consumed by the accepted $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback and by the finite $\RealUp$ seal handoff, and it already lists the source rows available to such a consumer.

The uniqueness of that displayed source is the meet exhaustion content of \autoref{thm:streamname-finite-window-lattice-meet-exhaustion}. The finite-window meet exposes one common packet, so the classifier, bundle, membership, unary-history, dyadic, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows cannot be duplicated by another terminal source inside the same meet route.

Next use \autoref{thm:streamname-synchronized-window-real-seal-handoff}. The synchronized window sends the Real-facing read to the same selected finite observations, so a terminal $\RealUp$ consumer has no second observation window from which to rebuild the seal row.

Finally combine \autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-realup-source-boundary-exhaustion} with \autoref{thm:streamname-real-seal-factorization-boundary}. The RegSeqRat readback and Real seal boundary are exhausted by the StreamName, dyadic, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ rows displayed above, hence every accepted terminal consumer names the same source surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName lattice-meet terminal pullback nonescape]
\label{thm:streamname-lattice-meet-terminal-pullback-nonescape}
Pulling the terminal finite $\RealUp$ seal read back along the $\StreamNameUp$ lattice-meet route cannot introduce quotient stream equality, host index equality, a selected limit, host real equality, ambient completeness, or an off-ledger $\RealUp$ seal coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the terminal source uniqueness theorem \autoref{thm:streamname-lattice-meet-terminal-source-uniqueness}. Any pullback of the terminal seal read through the accepted $\RegSeqRatUp$ and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ route must return to the same displayed source rows: classifier, bundle, membership, unary observations, dyadic ledger, readback, finite seal, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming data.

Now apply \autoref{thm:streamname-lattice-meet-terminal-factorization} to that unique source surface. Its nonescape clause states that the terminal meet packet leaves no carrier slot for quotient stream equality, host index equality, selected limits, host real equality, ambient completeness, or off-ledger seal data.

The pullback therefore repacks the existing terminal rows; it does not add a hidden route. The exclusions remain stable under the return along the lattice-meet route because the Real-facing seal coordinate is already fixed by the accepted readback and the finite source boundary.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName lattice-meet Real-seal support determinacy]
\label{thm:streamname-lattice-meet-real-seal-support-determinacy}
Let two accepted $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window lattice-meet packets be consumed
by the same $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback and by the same finite $\RealUp$ seal
handoff. If they agree on the displayed finite-window classifier, the
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ row, $\mathsf{InBundle}$ membership rows,
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ observation rows, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger, the
$\RegSeqRatUp$ readback surface, componentwise $\hsame$ transports, displayed
$\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ rows, then they
determine the same finite $\RealUp$ seal support packet.

No quotient stream equality, host sequence equality, selected limit, ambient
$\RealUp$ completeness row, metric-completeness row, or second $\RealUp$ source
can change that support packet once those common lattice-meet rows are fixed.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-lattice-meet-terminal-factorization}. The accepted
readback and finite seal handoff factor through the displayed finite surface:
classifier, bundle, membership, unary observation, dyadic ledger, readback,
transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.

Next apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-lattice-meet-terminal-source-uniqueness}. Since the two
packets agree on precisely those source rows, every terminal consumer names the
same displayed source surface. Thus the finite $\RealUp$ seal handoff has no
second source from which to rebuild a different support packet.

Now apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-lattice-meet-terminal-pullback-nonescape}. Pulling the
seal read back along the lattice-meet route only repacks the already displayed
rows and cannot introduce quotient stream equality, host sequence equality,
selected limits, ambient completeness, metric-completeness, or another
Real-facing source coordinate.

Finally use the Real seal handoff from the StreamName common-refinement route,
namely \autoref{thm:streamname-tail-window-cover-common-refinement} together
with \autoref{thm:streamname-real-seal-factorization-boundary}. The common
finite pullback is the only consumer surface available to the finite
$\RealUp$ seal, so agreement on the lattice-meet rows determines the same seal
support packet.
\end{proof}
