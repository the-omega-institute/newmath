\section{StreamName finite-window nonempty-index handoff}
\label{sec:streamname-window-index-nonempty-handoff}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName finite-window nonempty-index handoff]
\label{thm:streamname-window-index-nonempty-handoff}
Let $\mathcal W$ be a finite $\StreamNameUp$ package consumed by a
$\RegSeqRat$ certificate or by a finite-window $\RealUp$ consumer. Before any
regularity row, dyadic ledger row, or $\RealUp$ seal row is readable, the
handoff from $\mathcal W$ must expose at least one displayed observation row
through a finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, an
$\mathsf{InBundle}(n,\mathcal P)$ membership row, and a certified
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(n)$ index. This exported index is only a finite
observation witness for the displayed window.

The handoff exports no host index equality, quotient stream equality,
selected limit, or ambient completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First read the public finite-window handoff. By
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-regseq-finite-window-handoff}, a downstream
$\RegSeqRat$ or finite-window $\RealUp$ consumer can receive only the selected
rational-history classifier rows, the finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ ledger,
the $\mathsf{InBundle}$ membership rows, and the certified
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ observation rows from the StreamName side.

Next place that source row inside the finite meet packet of
\autoref{def:streamname-finite-window-lattice-meet}. The meet packet lists the
common classifier row, probe bundle, membership family, unary observation
family, dyadic ledger surface, componentwise transports, continuation routes,
package provenance, and local names. Hence any downstream dyadic or seal read
already has a visible finite observation coordinate before it can be consumed.

Now apply the exhaustion theorem
\autoref{thm:streamname-finite-window-lattice-meet-exhaustion}. It says that
every $\RegSeqRat$ or finite-window $\RealUp$ consumer of the meet reads only
the displayed finite observation surface and its ledger rows. Therefore the
first consumer-visible index is a certified member of the probe bundle, not a
host index equality or a quotient-stream coordinate.

Finally use the sibling dependency theorem
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-real-lattice-dependency}. The route from
StreamName through $\RegSeqRatUp$ into $\RealUp$ is the same visible
finite-window route, so regularity, dyadic ledger, and Real seal rows are
downstream consumers of the already displayed observation witness. The
exclusion clauses in the cited handoff, meet, exhaustion, and dependency
results rule out quotient stream equality, selected limits, and ambient
completeness as exported StreamName data.
\end{proof}
