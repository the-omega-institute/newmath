\section{StreamName finite-window nonempty-index handoff}
\label{sec:streamname-window-index-nonempty-handoff}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName finite-window nonempty-index handoff]
\label{thm:streamname-window-index-nonempty-handoff}
Let $\mathcal W$ be a finite $\StreamNameUp$ package consumed by a
$\RegSeqRat$ certificate or by a finite-window $\RealUp$ consumer. Before any
regularity row, dyadic ledger row, or $\RealUp$ seal row is readable, the
handoff from $\mathcal W$ must expose at least one displayed observation row
through a finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, an
$\mathsf{InBundle}(n,\mathcal P)$ membership row, and a certified
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(n)$ index. This exported index is only a finite
observation witness for the displayed window.

The handoff exports no host index equality, quotient stream equality,
selected limit, or ambient completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First read the public finite-window handoff. By
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-regseq-finite-window-handoff}, a downstream
$\RegSeqRat$ or finite-window $\RealUp$ consumer can receive only the selected
rational-history classifier rows, the finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ ledger,
the $\mathsf{InBundle}$ membership rows, and the certified
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ observation rows from the StreamName side.

Next place that source row inside the finite meet packet of
\autoref{def:streamname-finite-window-lattice-meet}. The meet packet lists the
common classifier row, probe bundle, membership family, unary observation
family, dyadic ledger surface, componentwise transports, continuation routes,
package provenance, and local names. Hence any downstream dyadic or seal read
already has a visible finite observation coordinate before it can be consumed.

Now apply the exhaustion theorem
\autoref{thm:streamname-finite-window-lattice-meet-exhaustion}. It says that
every $\RegSeqRat$ or finite-window $\RealUp$ consumer of the meet reads only
the displayed finite observation surface and its ledger rows. Therefore the
first consumer-visible index is a certified member of the probe bundle, not a
host index equality or a quotient-stream coordinate.

Finally use the sibling dependency theorem
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-real-lattice-dependency}. The route from
StreamName through $\RegSeqRatUp$ into $\RealUp$ is the same visible
finite-window route, so regularity, dyadic ledger, and Real seal rows are
downstream consumers of the already displayed observation witness. The
exclusion clauses in the cited handoff, meet, exhaustion, and dependency
results rule out quotient stream equality, selected limits, and ambient
completeness as exported StreamName data.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName window-index route determinacy]
\label{thm:streamname-window-index-route-determinacy}
Let $\mathcal W$ and $\mathcal W'$ be accepted finite $\StreamNameUp$ packets
consumed by $\RegSeqRatUp$ and $\RealUp$ rows. If both packets expose the same
finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ member $n$, the same certified
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(n)$ row, and the same finite-window meet coordinates,
then every downstream $\RegSeqRatUp$ and $\RealUp$ seal consumer reads the
same finite route through the StreamName window, the RegSeqRat readback, the
dyadic ledger, and the Real seal.

No quotient stream equality, host index equality, selected limit, or ambient
completeness theorem can provide an alternate route for either packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:streamname-window-index-nonempty-handoff} to both packets.
The common visible index is a certified member of the finite
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ and is already paired with the displayed
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(n)$ row before any RegSeqRat or Real consumer reads the
packet.

Place both packets in the finite meet packet of
\autoref{def:streamname-finite-window-lattice-meet}. The hypothesis that the
meet coordinates agree identifies the common classifier row, probe-bundle
membership row, unary observation row, dyadic ledger surface, componentwise
transport, continuation, provenance, and local name.

Use \autoref{thm:streamname-finite-window-lattice-meet-exhaustion} together
with \autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-real-lattice-dependency}. These results
force the RegSeqRat readback and Real seal to be downstream reads of the same
finite meet surface, so the consumer route is determined by the displayed
coordinates.

Coordinate exhaustion closes the claim. The agreed meet surface has no
coordinate for quotient stream equality, host index equality, selected
limits, or ambient completeness, so none of those objects can act as a second
route to the downstream seal.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName Real RegSeq observation pullback lock]
\label{thm:streamname-real-regseq-observation-pullback-lock}
Let $\mathcal W$ be an accepted finite $\StreamNameUp$ window consumed first
by a $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback and then by a $\RealUp$ seal. The first
observation exposed to that route is only the certified
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ member together with its
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ row, and every downstream seal read factors through
the same four-row pullback: the StreamName finite-window row, the RegSeqRat
readback row, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger row, and the $\RealUp$ seal row.

The lock exports no host index equality, quotient stream equality, selected
limit, trichotomy, or ambient completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with \autoref{thm:streamname-window-index-nonempty-handoff}. It forces
the first visible observation to be a finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ member
paired with its certified $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ row before a RegSeqRat or
Real consumer can read the packet.

Use \autoref{thm:streamname-window-index-route-determinacy} to keep that
observation on one finite route. Once the displayed window coordinate,
membership row, unary history row, and meet coordinates are fixed, no host
index equality or quotient stream equality can supply a second downstream
path.

Now apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-real-dyadic-window-pullback} and
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-real-dyadic-pullback-consumer-exhaustion}.
These results identify the downstream surface as the four displayed rows:
StreamName finite window, RegSeqRat readback, DyadicRatCore ledger, and Real
seal.

Coordinate exhaustion gives the lock. The four-row pullback has no coordinate
for a selected limit, trichotomy, or ambient completeness, and the initial
observation coordinate remains the certified bundle member plus unary history
row throughout the downstream seal read.
\end{proof}
