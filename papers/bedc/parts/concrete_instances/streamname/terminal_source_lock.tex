\section{StreamName terminal source lock}
\label{sec:streamname-terminal-source-lock}

\begin{definition}[StreamName-RegSeqRat-RealUp terminal source lock]
\label{def:streamname-regseqrat-realup-terminal-source-lock}
A $\StreamNameUp$--$\RegSeqRat$--$\RealUp$ terminal source lock is the finite
packet
$$
\mathcal{L}=(W,R,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $W$ is a finite public $\StreamNameUp$ window over certified unary
observations, including the selected rational-history classifier rows, the
finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ ledger, $\mathsf{InBundle}$ membership rows, and
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ observation rows;
\item $R$ is the accepted $\RegSeqRat$ finite seal row over the same certified
indices, including the regularity, dyadic-tolerance, stream-spine, and
pointwise seal classifier rows supplied on the $\RegSeqRat$ side;
\item $S$ is the finite-window $\RealUp$ seal row induced by reading $W$
through $R$;
\item $H$ is the displayed componentwise $\hsame$ row comparing the
StreamName point rows, the $\RegSeqRat$ seal rows, the unary observation rows,
the bundle-membership rows, and the induced $\RealUp$ seal row;
\item $C$ is the displayed $\Cont$ ledger routing the read from the finite
StreamName window through the accepted $\RegSeqRat$ seal row to the finite
$\RealUp$ seal row;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance naming the StreamName source certificate,
the $\RegSeqRat$ finite seal certificate, the finite continuation ledger, and
the induced $\RealUp$ seal read;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert$ row for this finite handoff packet.
\end{enumerate}
The packet has no coordinate for a completion witness, quotient stream
equality, host index equality, or off-ledger $\RealUp$ classifier row.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName-RegSeqRat-RealUp terminal source lock]
\label{thm:streamname-regseqrat-realup-terminal-source-lock}
Every finite $\RealUp$ seal read induced from a public $\StreamNameUp$ window
through an accepted $\RegSeqRat$ finite seal factors through exactly the packet
of \autoref{def:streamname-regseqrat-realup-terminal-source-lock}. The
StreamName coordinates are read from $W$, the regularity and pointwise seal
coordinates from $R$, the induced finite $\RealUp$ seal row from $S$, and the
transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows from $H$, $C$, $P$,
and $N$.

Conversely, any finite packet carrying precisely those rows repacks as the
same induced finite-window $\RealUp$ seal read.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-regseq-obligation-separation}. It separates the
visible StreamName source rows, the downstream $\RegSeqRat$ regularity and
seal rows, and the finite $\RealUp$ seal classifier into their distinct source
surfaces.

Next apply \autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-uniform-finite-window-seal}. The
accepted $\RegSeqRat$ seal is stable under finite-window restriction, so the
source window and the seal row keep the same displayed certified indices and
the same pointwise classifier rows.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:streamname-real-seal-factorization-boundary}. It
states that the finite $\RealUp$ seal read factors through exactly the
StreamName point rows, accepted $\RegSeqRat$ seal rows, bundle membership,
unary observations, finite $\Cont$ ledger, displayed $\hsame$ transports, and
$\Pkg$ provenance. These rows are precisely the coordinates of
$\mathcal{L}$, with $N$ recording the local naming ledger, and no further
coordinate is available for the reverse packing.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName-RegSeqRat-RealUp terminal non-escape]
\label{thm:streamname-regseqrat-realup-terminal-nonescape}
Let $\mathcal{L}=(W,R,S,H,C,P,N)$ be a terminal source lock. A consumer of the
finite handoff can read only the finite StreamName window $W$, the accepted
$\RegSeqRat$ seal row $R$, the induced finite $\RealUp$ seal row $S$, the
displayed $\hsame$ transports $H$, the displayed $\Cont$ routes $C$, the
$\Pkg$ provenance $P$, and the local $\NameCert$ row $N$. It cannot extract a
completion witness, quotient stream equality, host index equality, or
off-ledger $\RealUp$ classifier row from the lock.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the consumer read through
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-realup-terminal-source-lock}. Every induced
finite $\RealUp$ seal read is already factored by the displayed lock packet.

The separation theorem
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-regseq-obligation-separation} assigns each
visible row to StreamName, $\RegSeqRat$, or finite $\RealUp$ seal data, and it
excludes completion witnesses, quotient rows, and host index equalities from
the StreamName source surface.

The boundary theorem
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-seal-factorization-boundary} rules out any
completion datum, quotient representative, host index equality, choice row, or
$\RealUp$ completeness coordinate outside the displayed accepted
$\RegSeqRat$ seal rows. The uniform finite-window theorem prevents a
restriction of the lock from adding an off-ledger classifier row.

Thus the terminal packet is a finite source surface only. Every consumer read
stays inside $W$, $R$, $S$, $H$, $C$, $P$, and $N$.
\end{proof}
