\begin{theorem}[StreamName Real-completion four-object exit route]
\label{thm:streamname-real-completion-four-object-exit-route}
Every current Phase Real and Completion exit read named by the roadmap for $\DyadicRatCore$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ factors through exactly the four finite sibling faces
$$
\StreamNameUp,\quad \DyadicRatCoreUp,\quad \RegSeqRatUp,\quad \RealUp.
$$
The public rows of the exit boundary are only the finite StreamName window, the DyadicRatCore tolerance ledger, the RegSeqRat readback, the RealUp seal, and the displayed support rows
$$
\BHist,\quad \hsame,\quad \Cont,\quad \Pkg,\quad \NameCert.
$$

No quotient stream equality, host sequence equality, selected limit, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness theorem, metric-completeness datum, countable-choice row, or standard bridge row belongs to this exit boundary.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with \autoref{thm:streamname-open-phase-exit-boundary}. It identifies every current Phase Real and Completion consumer with a finite StreamName window, RegSeqRat readback, DyadicRatCore ledger, RealUp seal, and the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support rows.

Next apply \autoref{thm:streamname-real-completion-exit-certificate} and \autoref{thm:streamname-open-phase-four-face-terminality}. The certificate names the four finite faces of the exit packet, while terminality says that once those faces and support rows are fixed, the consumer has no second terminal route through which to read the boundary.

Now use \autoref{thm:streamname-l10-real-completion-sibling-spine}. The L10 spine places the StreamName route against the sibling surfaces consumed by the Real and Completion phase, so the roadmap's four objects are read as sibling faces rather than as a later completion theorem.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:streamname-cauchy-witness-ledger-exit-route}. The witness-ledger route consumes the same finite packet without adding quotient stream equality, host sequence equality, selected limits, ambient Real completeness, metric-completeness data, countable choice, or standard bridge rows. Therefore the partial exit boundary, certificate, four-face terminality, sibling spine, and witness-ledger route exhaust the roadmap boundary for $\DyadicRatCore$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName open-phase four-face minimality]
\label{thm:streamname-open-phase-four-face-minimality}
For the current Phase Real and Completion exit boundary, the four displayed faces $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ are minimal. Deleting any one of the StreamName finite window, DyadicRatCore tolerance ledger, RegSeqRat readback, or RealUp seal prevents the exit read from satisfying the roadmap boundary named by \autoref{thm:streamname-real-completion-four-object-exit-route}. No additional fifth face may replace a deleted one.

The minimal packet still carries only the displayed support rows
$$
\BHist,\quad \hsame,\quad \Cont,\quad \Pkg,\quad \NameCert.
$$
It does not admit quotient stream equality, host sequence equality, selected limits, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, countable choice, metric-completeness data, or bridge substitutes as replacement faces.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from \autoref{thm:streamname-real-completion-four-object-exit-route}. It states the exit read through exactly the StreamName window, DyadicRatCore ledger, RegSeqRat readback, RealUp seal, and the displayed support rows.

Use \autoref{thm:streamname-open-phase-exit-boundary} and \autoref{thm:streamname-open-phase-four-face-terminality}. The boundary theorem fixes the current roadmap surface, while terminality says that the four displayed faces are the terminal route through which that surface is read.

Now separate the four roles. The StreamName window supplies the finite observation window, the DyadicRatCore ledger supplies the tolerance account, the RegSeqRat readback supplies the regular sequence reading, and the RealUp seal supplies the terminal Real-facing boundary.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:streamname-l10-real-completion-sibling-spine}. The sibling spine keeps those roles distinct and blocks replacement by quotient streams, host equality, selected limits, ambient completeness, choice, metric-completeness rows, or bridges. Therefore deleting any one face removes a required row, and a fifth face cannot restore the current four-face exit boundary.
\end{proof}
