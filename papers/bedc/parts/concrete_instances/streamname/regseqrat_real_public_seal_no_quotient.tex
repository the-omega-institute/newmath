\section{StreamName RegSeqRat Real public seal boundary}
\label{sec:streamname-regseqrat-real-public-seal-boundary}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName-RegSeqRat-Real public seal no-quotient boundary]
\label{thm:streamname-regseqrat-real-public-seal-no-quotient}
Every public $\RealUp$ seal route exported from the $\StreamNameUp$
finite-window handoff and consumed by $\RegSeqRatUp$ factors through the finite
$\BHist$ rows, pointwise $\hsame$ rows, $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and
local $\NameCert$ rows already exposed by
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-regseq-finite-window-handoff},
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-real-seal-composition}, and
\autoref{thm:streamname-realup-seal-boundary}. The handoff exports no quotient
stream equality, selected limit, host real equality, ambient completeness
claim, or open-cover compactness claim.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply \autoref{thm:streamname-real-regseq-finite-window-handoff}. It
identifies the public StreamName source surface as a finite-window packet: the
selected history rows, the finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ ledger, membership
rows, unary-history rows, and the visible $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$,
and $\NameCert$ support.

Next apply \autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-real-seal-composition}. The
RegSeqRat consumer reads the same finite packet through the regular-sequence
seal route, so the Real-facing seal is reached through the displayed finite
rows and their provenance rather than through a hidden stream equality or a
chosen completed value.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:streamname-realup-seal-boundary}. That boundary
keeps the RealUp seal at the finite-window interface and excludes quotient
streams, selected limits, host real equality, and ambient completeness data.
The sibling L10 consumers
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert},
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert} therefore receive only the
displayed finite rows and local certificates, not an open-cover compactness
claim.
\end{proof}
