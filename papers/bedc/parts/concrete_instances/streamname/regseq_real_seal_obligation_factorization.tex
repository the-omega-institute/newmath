\begin{theorem}[StreamName-RegSeqRat-Real seal obligation factorization]
\label{thm:streamname-regseq-real-seal-obligation-factorization}
Let $C$ be a Real-facing completion consumer whose route is displayed through
a public $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window surface, a $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback
surface over the same selected rational-history rows, and a $\RealUp$ seal
ledger row. Then the finite Real-facing seal route factors through exactly the
following three obligation blocks:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item the $\StreamNameUp$ block consisting of the rational-history
  classifier rows, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ and $\mathsf{InBundle}$ rows,
  $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ observations, visible $\hsame$ transports, finite
  $\Cont$ routes, and $\Pkg$ provenance rows;
  \item the $\RegSeqRatUp$ block consisting of regularity,
  dyadic-tolerance, transport, stream-spine, and seal-interface rows over the
  same selected rational-history surface;
  \item the $\RealUp$ block consisting of the seal classifier and its finite
  ledger boundary row.
\end{enumerate}
Conversely, any packet carrying exactly those three displayed blocks repacks
into the same finite Real-facing seal route.

The factorization exports no quotient stream equality, selected host limit,
host real equality, completion witness not carried by the packet, choice
schedule, or ambient completeness row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First isolate the finite StreamName source. Apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-regseq-finite-window-handoff}. It identifies the
only rows exported by the public finite-window surface as the selected
rational-history classifier rows, the finite probe ledger, membership rows,
unary observations, and the visible transport and provenance data.

Next separate the downstream obligations. Apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-regseq-obligation-separation}. The separation row
assigns the classifier, bundle, membership, unary-history, $\hsame$, $\Cont$,
and $\Pkg$ data to the StreamName block; the regularity, dyadic-tolerance,
transport, and stream-spine data to the RegSeqRat block; and the seal
classifier to the RealUp block.

Now compose the seal route. Use
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-real-seal-composition} to pass from those
three displayed surfaces to the Real-facing finite-window ledger boundary.
This gives the forward factorization of $C$ through the StreamName block, the
RegSeqRat block, and the RealUp block.

For the reverse direction, read the same composition theorem from the three
blocks back to the public route, and then apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-realup-seal-boundary}. The RealUp boundary confirms
that the repacked row is the same finite seal route because the seal is read
only through the displayed finite ledger boundary.

The exclusion clause follows from the same four boundary theorems. None of
them introduces quotient stream equality, selected host limits, host real
equality, completion witnesses outside the packet, choice schedules, or ambient
completeness rows, so the factorized route exports none of those coordinates.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName-RegSeqRat-Real four-face seal obligation factorization]
\label{thm:streamname-regseq-real-four-face-obligation-factorization}
Let $C$ be a Real-facing completion consumer whose route is displayed through
a $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint and radius ledger, a public $\StreamNameUp$
finite-window surface, a $\RegSeqRatUp$ regularity and classifier readback over
the same selected rational-history rows, and a $\RealUp$ seal ledger row. Then
the finite Real-facing seal route factors through exactly the following four
obligation blocks:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ block consisting of the endpoint ledger,
  radius ledger, dyadic-tolerance rows, and their finite handoff boundary;
  \item the $\StreamNameUp$ block consisting of the rational-history
  classifier rows, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ and $\mathsf{InBundle}$ rows,
  $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ observations, visible $\hsame$ transports, finite
  $\Cont$ routes, and $\Pkg$ provenance rows;
  \item the $\RegSeqRatUp$ block consisting of regularity, classifier,
  dyadic-tolerance transport, stream-spine, and seal-interface rows over the
  same selected rational-history surface;
  \item the $\RealUp$ block consisting of the seal classifier and its finite
  ledger boundary row.
\end{enumerate}
Conversely, any packet carrying exactly these four displayed blocks repacks
into the same finite Real-facing seal route.

The factorization exports no quotient stream equality, selected host limit,
host real equality, completion witness not carried by the packet, choice
schedule, ambient completeness row, metric-completeness row, or second
$\RealUp$ seal coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First discard the fourth face and read the visible StreamName-RegSeqRat-Real
part of the route. Apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseq-real-seal-obligation-factorization}. It gives
the three displayed blocks carried by the StreamName finite-window surface, the
RegSeqRat regularity and readback surface, and the RealUp seal boundary, and it
also records the exclusions for quotient stream equality, selected host limits,
host real equality, external completion witnesses, choice schedules, and ambient
completeness rows.

Now insert the DyadicRatCore face at the unique upstream position. Apply
\autoref{thm:real-dyadic-streamname-regseqrat-dependency-route}. The route
places the endpoint and radius ledger before the StreamName finite-window
schedule, before the RegSeqRat regularity and classifier rows, and before the
local Real seal. Thus the three-block factorization refines to the four blocks
listed in the statement.

The order is forced by
\autoref{thm:real-l10-four-face-route-order-uniqueness}. Any route omitting or
permuting one of the four faces either loses a displayed ledger row or leaves
the finite packet by adding one of the excluded off-table coordinates. Hence
the four-block route is not merely a presentation of $C$ but the exact
obligation factorization read by the Real-facing seal.

It remains to compare the shared packet data. Apply
\autoref{thm:real-l10-four-face-classifier-coherence}. The DyadicRatCore
ledger, StreamName schedule, RegSeqRat classifier and transport rows, and
RealUp seal row all determine the same finite
$(\BHist,\hsame,\Cont,\Pkg,\NameCert)$ packet. Therefore the reverse direction
repackages any packet carrying exactly those four blocks into the same
Real-facing seal route.

The exclusion clause follows from the same three inputs. The dependency route,
order uniqueness theorem, and classifier-coherence theorem rule out quotient
stream equality, selected host limits, host real equality, external completion
witnesses, choice schedules, ambient completeness rows, metric-completeness
rows, and a second RealUp seal coordinate.
\end{proof}
