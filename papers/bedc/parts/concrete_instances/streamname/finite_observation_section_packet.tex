\section{StreamName finite-observation section packet}
\label{sec:streamname-finite-observation-section-packet}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName finite-observation section]
\label{thm:streamname-finite-observation-section}
Every certified finite observation section of a $\StreamNameUp$ window is
exhausted by the displayed $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ membership row, the
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ row, the $\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$ row, the
componentwise $\hsame$ transport, the displayed $\Cont$ route, $\Pkg$
provenance, and the local $\NameCert$ row of the StreamName packet. The
section contributes no regularity witness, quotient stream equality,
completion row, host-index equality, selected limit, or ambient completeness
coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the certified observation section inside the finite StreamName
window. Apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-finite-observation-classifier-exhaustion}; it
projects any selected certified unary observation to the finite
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ row, $\mathsf{InBundle}$ membership row,
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ row, pointwise $\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$ row,
$\hsame$ comparison, $\Cont$ route, $\Pkg$ provenance, and $\NameCert$
provenance.

The membership and unary-history rows identify the observation as a finite
section of the window rather than as a host-indexed stream. The pointwise
classifier and $\hsame$ rows identify exactly the rational-history data
visible at that section, while $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ only replay,
package, and name the same finite surface.

The exclusions are therefore inherited from the classifier exhaustion theorem.
Regularity witnesses, quotient stream equality, completion rows, host-index
equality, selected limits, and ambient completeness coordinates are not fields
of the section packet, so no certified finite observation section can export
them.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName finite-observation constant seed]
\label{thm:streamname-finite-observation-constant-seed}
For a constant rational stream section $\mathsf{RatConstStream}_d$, any two
certified observations of the same finite $\StreamNameUp$ window read the same
hidden $\RatUp$ seed $d$. The public section row is therefore the common
$\hsame$ class of the two observed histories, together with the same finite
membership, unary-history, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:rat-stream-name-constant-witness} to the first certified
observation. The observed history of $\mathsf{RatConstStream}_d$ is identified
by $\hsame$ with the hidden seed $d$.

Apply the same witness theorem to the second certified observation. Its
observed history is again identified by $\hsame$ with $d$, using only the
certified unary observation row and the constant rational stream definition.

Now use
\autoref{thm:rat-const-stream-certified-observation-determinacy}. It composes
the two point-sameness witnesses through the seed and gives the same public
history row for both certified observations. The finite membership,
unary-history, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows are the support rows of
the section packet, so the constant section reads one RatUp seed across all
certified observations of the window.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName finite-observation Real exit handoff]
\label{thm:streamname-finite-observation-real-exit-handoff}
The finite-observation section packet of
\autoref{thm:streamname-finite-observation-section} and the constant-seed
packet of \autoref{thm:streamname-finite-observation-constant-seed} are
exactly the $\StreamNameUp$ contribution to the Phase Real and Completion
four-object exit surface. When the exit route is read through
$\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$, the
StreamName side supplies only finite observation rows, constant-seed
determinacy where the stream is constant, and the displayed $\BHist$,
$\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First use \autoref{thm:streamname-finite-observation-realup-seal-scope}. It
places every finite-window $\RealUp$ seal read on the StreamName side over the
carried endpoints, finite bundle membership, unary observation, and pointwise
rational-history classifier rows.

Combine that scope theorem with
\autoref{thm:streamname-finite-observation-section}. The section packet is
therefore the whole StreamName finite-observation contribution before the
RegSeqRat and Real-facing rows are consumed.

For constant rational stream sections, insert
\autoref{thm:streamname-finite-observation-constant-seed}. The same finite
section surface now also records that all certified observations read the same
hidden RatUp seed, without adding a quotient stream, selected limit, or
ambient completeness coordinate.

Finally apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-completion-four-object-exit-route}. The
four-object route reads the Phase Real and Completion boundary through exactly
$\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ with only
the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support
rows. Thus the section and constant-seed packets are precisely the
StreamName-side finite observation handoff into that exit surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName RegSeqRat tail-budget section lock]
\label{thm:streamname-finite-observation-regseqrat-tail-budget-section-lock}
Every finite $\StreamNameUp$ observation section consumed by a
$\RegSeqRatUp$ tail-budget read and then by a finite $\RealUp$ seal factors
through the same displayed section packet. The packet consists of the
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ membership row, the $\mathsf{InBundle}$ membership row,
the $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ observation row, the
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$ endpoint row, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance
ledger, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regularity and classifier rows, the displayed
$\hsame$ transports, the $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local
$\NameCert$ rows. It exports no quotient stream equality, host sequence
equality, selected limit, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, metric-completeness
datum, or second dyadic source.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The local section inventory is fixed by
\autoref{thm:streamname-finite-observation-section}. A certified finite
observation section exposes exactly the bundle membership, in-bundle
membership, unary-history, rational-history classifier, $\hsame$, $\Cont$,
$\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ rows listed there.

The handoff to the Phase Real and Completion exit surface is the one in
\autoref{thm:streamname-finite-observation-real-exit-handoff}. It says that the
StreamName contribution to the four-object route is precisely the finite
observation packet before the downstream DyadicRatCore, RegSeqRat, and Real
rows are read.

Now route the consumer through
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-tail-budget-meet-handoff}. A tail-budget read is ordered
through $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and then
$\RealUp$, so the dyadic tolerance ledger and the RegSeqRat regularity and
classifier rows consume the same finite StreamName section rather than a
separate source.

Finally use
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-regseq-shared-tail-budget-route}. The shared-tail
consumer keeps the RegSeqRat readback and the finite Real seal on the same
public finite-window route. Since the cited packets contain no fields for a
quotient stream equality, host sequence equality, selected limit, ambient Real
completeness principle, metric-completeness datum, or second dyadic source,
none of those objects can be inserted between the section packet and its
tail-budget or seal consumers.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName finite-observation section reindex coverage]
\label{thm:streamname-finite-observation-section-reindex-coverage}
Every certified reindexing of a finite $\StreamNameUp$ observation section
preserves the same displayed section packet and the same Phase Real and
Completion exit route through $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$,
$\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$. The reindexed consumer receives only the
transported finite observation rows, the constant scheduled-subwindow
coherence when the source is constant, the downstream tail-budget rows, and
the finite $\RealUp$ seal rows; it receives no quotient stream equality,
host-index equality, selected limit, second dyadic source, or ambient
completeness row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the local finite observation packet from
\autoref{thm:streamname-finite-observation-section}. It identifies the
displayed section solely by the bundle membership, unary-history,
rational-history classifier, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local
$\NameCert$ rows, with no quotient, host-index, limit, or completeness field
available to transport.

Apply \autoref{thm:rat-stream-name-reindexing-composition-law}. Certified
window reindexing composes inside the StreamName certificate surface, so the
consumer reads the reindexed section through the same public observation
packet rather than through a second stream source or a host-index equality.

For constant scheduled subwindows, use
\autoref{thm:streamname-scheduled-subwindow-constant-observation-coherence}.
The scheduled window and the reindexed window expose the same constant
observation row, so reindexing does not add a selected limit or a second
dyadic source before the downstream consumer sees the packet.

Finally combine
\autoref{thm:streamname-finite-observation-real-exit-handoff} with
\autoref{thm:streamname-finite-observation-regseqrat-tail-budget-section-lock}.
The first theorem fixes the four-object Phase Real and Completion route
through $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$;
the second keeps the RegSeqRat tail-budget read and the finite Real seal on
that same finite section. Thus certified reindexing preserves coverage of the
section packet without opening any quotient stream, host-index, selected
limit, second-source, or ambient-completeness row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName finite-observation diagonal selector boundary]
\label{thm:streamname-finite-observation-diagonal-selector-boundary}
Every diagonal-limit or Real-completion consumer that reads a finite
$\StreamNameUp$ observation section through an accepted
$\FiniteObservationBudgetSelectorUp$ packet factors through the same finite
route: the StreamName finite observation section, the selector rows
$B,S,W,D,R,E$, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, the
terminal $\RealUp$ seal, and the support rows $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$,
$\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$. The boundary preserves the four-object Phase Real and
Completion exit surface through $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$,
$\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$; it exports no quotient stream equality, host
real equality, selected limit, countable choice, hidden diagonal selector,
ambient completeness, metric-completeness row, bridge row, or second source for
the dyadic observations.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the StreamName section itself. By
\autoref{thm:streamname-finite-observation-section}, a certified finite
observation section exposes only the finite membership, history, classifier,
$\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ rows of the StreamName packet.

The handoff theorem
\autoref{thm:streamname-finite-observation-real-exit-handoff} places that
section on the Phase Real and Completion route through $\StreamNameUp$,
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$. The reindex coverage theorem
\autoref{thm:streamname-finite-observation-section-reindex-coverage} keeps any
certified reindexed consumer on the same finite section surface.

Now read the accepted selector. By
\autoref{thm:finite-observation-budget-selector-diagonal-consumer-boundary},
any diagonal-limit or Real-completion consumer of the selector factors through
the displayed finite route $B \to S \to W \to D \to R \to E$ together with its
visible support rows. By
\autoref{thm:finite-observation-budget-selector-dyadic-window-exhaustion}, the
dyadic observations in $D$ are exhausted by the selected finite
$\StreamNameUp$ windows in $W$.

Therefore the selector consumes the existing finite StreamName section as the
source for its dyadic ledger rather than creating a second source. Since the
cited section, handoff, reindexing, diagonal-boundary, and exhaustion theorems
contain no fields for quotient stream equality, host real equality, selected
limits, countable choice, hidden diagonal selectors, ambient completeness,
metric-completeness rows, or bridge rows, none of those coordinates can be
inserted between the finite section and its diagonal or Real-completion
consumer.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName finite-observation selector coverage exhaustion]
\label{thm:streamname-finite-observation-selector-coverage-exhaustion}
Every accepted $\FiniteObservationBudgetSelectorUp$ consumer of a finite
$\StreamNameUp$ observation section is exhausted by the displayed selector rows
$B,S,W,D,R,E$, the selected $\StreamNameUp$ window row, the
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, the terminal
$\RealUp$ seal, and the support rows $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and
$\NameCert$. The accepted selector route exports no quotient stream equality,
host real equality, selected limit, countable choice, hidden diagonal selector,
ambient completeness, metric-completeness row, bridge row, or second dyadic
source.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the finite section. By
\autoref{thm:streamname-finite-observation-section}, the StreamName side of a
certified finite observation exposes only the bundle membership, unary-history,
rational-history classifier, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$
rows.

Next pass to the four-object exit surface using
\autoref{thm:streamname-finite-observation-real-exit-handoff}. The finite
section is read through $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$,
and $\RealUp$, so the DyadicRatCore ledger, RegSeqRat readback, and terminal
Real seal are downstream consumers of the same finite StreamName observation.

Now apply
\autoref{thm:finite-observation-budget-selector-dyadic-window-exhaustion}. For
an accepted selector packet, the dyadic observations in $D$ are already
exhausted by the selected finite StreamName windows in $W$. Thus the displayed
selector rows $B,S,W,D,R,E$ cover the observation route without opening a
second dyadic source.

Finally combine
\autoref{thm:finite-observation-budget-selector-diagonal-consumer-boundary}
with
\autoref{thm:streamname-finite-observation-diagonal-selector-boundary}. These
boundary theorems place every compatible diagonal-limit or Real-completion
consumer on the same four-object route and the same visible support rows. Since
none of the cited packets carries quotient stream equality, host real equality,
selected limits, countable choice, hidden diagonal selectors, ambient
completeness, metric-completeness rows, bridge rows, or a second dyadic source,
no such coordinate is exported by the accepted selector coverage surface.
\end{proof}
