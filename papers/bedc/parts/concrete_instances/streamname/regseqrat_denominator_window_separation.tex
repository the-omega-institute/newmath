\begin{theorem}[StreamName RegSeqRat denominator window separation]
\label{thm:streamname-regseqrat-denominator-window-separation}
Let $s,t:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{BHist}$ be StreamName rows and let
$\mathcal P:\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ be a finite unary observation bundle. For
every certified observation $n$ with $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(n)$ and
$\mathsf{InBundle}(n,\mathcal P)$, the denominator and dyadic observation rows
consumed by a $\RegSeqRat$ finite-window handoff factor through only the
visible packet
$$
\begin{gathered}
\mathsf{RatStreamNameFiniteWindowClassifier}(s,t,\mathcal P),\\
\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(n),\qquad
\mathsf{InBundle}(n,\mathcal P),\qquad
\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(s(n),t(n)).
\end{gathered}
$$
The packet may be read by the $\RegSeqRat$ dyadic observation coverage rows,
including
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-finite-window-dyadic-observation-coverage}, but it
exports no $\RegSeqRat$ regularity row, dyadic seal datum, $\RealUp$ seal
classifier, completion object, quotient row, or host-index equality. Those
objects remain on the downstream side of the $\RegSeqRat$ and
Real-and-completion certificates.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the finite StreamName bundle by
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-pointwise-handoff}. For the selected
certified observation $n$, the projection exposes the finite-window
classifier, the unary-history row, the bundle-membership row, and the
pointwise rational-history classifier
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(s(n),t(n))$.

Stabilize the exposed row by
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-observation-stability}. This step permits
the consumer to transport the same certified observation along the pointwise
$\hsame$ rows already visible in the bundle, without importing any regularity
or seal evidence from the later $\RegSeqRat$ or $\RealUp$ boundary.

Now read the denominator window through the Real/RegSeqRat coverage surface.
Apply
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-finite-window-dyadic-observation-coverage} to the
selected unary observation and its pointwise rational classifier. The dyadic
endpoint rows are therefore obtained from the displayed finite observation
packet.

Finally repack the consumer boundary using
\autoref{thm:streamname-finite-observation-realup-seal-scope}. That theorem
keeps $\RegSeqRat$ regularity, dyadic seal data, the $\RealUp$ seal
classifier, completion structure, quotient structure, and host-index equality
outside the StreamName source packet, so the denominator window has exactly
the stated dependency surface.
\end{proof}
