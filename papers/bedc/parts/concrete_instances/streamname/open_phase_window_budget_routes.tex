\section{StreamName Open-Phase Window Budget Routes}
\label{sec:streamname-open-phase-window-budget-routes}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName open-phase window index nonempty]
\label{thm:streamname-open-phase-window-index-nonempty}
Every Phase Real and Completion consumer satisfying the open-phase terminal route of \autoref{thm:streamname-open-phase-terminal-route} reads a nonempty finite window-index surface before it reaches the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the finite $\RealUp$ handoff rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The window-index surface is the one carried by the common-refinement terminal Real seal of \autoref{thm:streamname-common-refinement-terminal-real-seal}; it consists only of displayed $\BHist$ observations, componentwise $\hsame$ transports, displayed $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ rows.

The route exports no quotient stream, no host sequence equality, no selected limit, no ambient $\RealUp$ completeness row, and no metric-completeness datum.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with \autoref{thm:streamname-open-phase-terminal-route}. It places the Phase Real and Completion consumer on the terminal finite packet whose visible entries include the StreamName window, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger, and the finite $\RealUp$ seal row.

Apply \autoref{thm:streamname-common-refinement-terminal-real-seal}. The common-refinement packet displays the finite-window classifier, probe rows, membership rows, unary-history observations, rational-history rows, dyadic ledger rows, transports, routes, provenance, and local names before the downstream handoff is read.

Now use \autoref{thm:streamname-window-index-nonempty-handoff}. The accepted finite-window handoff must expose a nonempty index row on that displayed packet, so the Phase Real and Completion route has a finite window-index surface before it can reach the RegSeqRat, DyadicRatCore, or Real-facing rows.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:streamname-open-phase-terminal-nonescape}. Since the terminal packet contains no coordinate for quotient streams, host sequence equality, selected limits, ambient Real completeness, or metric-completeness data, none of those objects is exported by the nonempty index route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName open-phase finite-window budget]
\label{thm:streamname-open-phase-finite-window-budget}
Every Phase Real and Completion consumer routed through the terminal StreamName packet has a finite-window budget route whose source is the nonempty StreamName window index, whose middle rows are the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger, and whose target is the finite $\RealUp$ seal handoff. The budget is consumed on the common-refinement terminal surface of \autoref{thm:streamname-common-refinement-terminal-real-seal} and the open-phase terminal route of \autoref{thm:streamname-open-phase-terminal-route}.

The budget route carries only displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows. It exports no quotient stream, no host sequence equality, no selected limit, no ambient $\RealUp$ completeness row, and no metric-completeness datum.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply \autoref{thm:streamname-open-phase-window-index-nonempty}. The consumer has a finite nonempty StreamName window-index surface before any downstream Real-facing read occurs.

Next use \autoref{thm:streamname-finite-window-budget-monotonicity}. Enlarging or transporting the selected finite window changes only the displayed budget rows and keeps the consumer inside the finite StreamName packet.

Apply \autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-real-dyadic-window-pullback}. The StreamName window, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger, and finite $\RealUp$ seal row form one finite pullback, so the same window budget supports all three downstream faces.

Finally invoke \autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-real-dyadic-pullback-seal-determinacy} and \autoref{thm:streamname-open-phase-terminal-nonescape}. Seal determinacy pins the Real-facing read to that finite pullback, and non-escape removes quotient streams, host sequence equality, selected limits, ambient Real completeness, and metric-completeness data from the exported surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName open-phase Real seal route]
\label{thm:streamname-open-phase-real-seal-route}
Every Phase Real and Completion handoff that begins at a $\StreamNameUp$ finite window and ends at a finite $\RealUp$ seal row factors through the finite-window budget route of \autoref{thm:streamname-open-phase-finite-window-budget}. The handoff therefore has the ordered public route
$$
\StreamNameUp\text{ window budget}\longrightarrow \RegSeqRatUp\text{ readback}\longrightarrow \DyadicRatCoreUp\text{ ledger}\longrightarrow \RealUp\text{ finite seal}.
$$
The route is the terminal BEDC surface already exposed by \autoref{thm:streamname-open-phase-terminal-route} and \autoref{thm:streamname-common-refinement-terminal-real-seal}.

It exports no quotient stream, no host sequence equality, no selected limit, no ambient $\RealUp$ completeness row, and no metric-completeness datum.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the finite-window budget route of \autoref{thm:streamname-open-phase-finite-window-budget}. It supplies the nonempty window-index source, the RegSeqRat readback, the DyadicRatCore ledger, and the finite Real seal row on one displayed packet.

Next apply \autoref{thm:streamname-real-regseq-terminal-common-window-lock}. The RegSeqRat readback and Real seal access are locked to the same common finite window, with the dyadic ledger read from that packet rather than from an independent carrier.

Use \autoref{thm:streamname-real-regseqrat-seal-common-refinement}. The common-refinement seal pullback is unique for the StreamName window, RegSeqRat readback, DyadicRatCore ledger, and Real seal row.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:streamname-open-phase-four-face-lock}. The Phase Real and Completion handoff is exactly the displayed four-face route, and the non-escape clauses already attached to that route exclude quotient streams, host sequence equality, selected limits, ambient Real completeness, and metric-completeness data.
\end{proof}
