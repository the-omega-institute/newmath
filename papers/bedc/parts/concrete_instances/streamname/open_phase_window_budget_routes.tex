\begin{theorem}[StreamName open-phase window index nonempty]
\label{thm:streamname-open-phase-window-index-nonempty}
Every Phase Real and Completion consumer satisfying the open-phase terminal route of \autoref{thm:streamname-open-phase-terminal-route} reads a nonempty finite window-index surface before it reaches the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the finite $\RealUp$ handoff rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The window-index surface is the one carried by the common-refinement terminal Real seal of \autoref{thm:streamname-common-refinement-terminal-real-seal}; it consists only of displayed $\BHist$ observations, componentwise $\hsame$ transports, displayed $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ rows.

The route exports no quotient stream, no host sequence equality, no selected limit, no ambient $\RealUp$ completeness row, and no metric-completeness datum.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with \autoref{thm:streamname-open-phase-terminal-route}. It places the Phase Real and Completion consumer on the terminal finite packet whose visible entries include the StreamName window, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger, and the finite $\RealUp$ seal row.

Apply \autoref{thm:streamname-common-refinement-terminal-real-seal}. The common-refinement packet displays the finite-window classifier, probe rows, membership rows, unary-history observations, rational-history rows, dyadic ledger rows, transports, routes, provenance, and local names before the downstream handoff is read.

Now use \autoref{thm:streamname-window-index-nonempty-handoff}. The accepted finite-window handoff must expose a nonempty index row on that displayed packet, so the Phase Real and Completion route has a finite window-index surface before it can reach the RegSeqRat, DyadicRatCore, or Real-facing rows.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:streamname-open-phase-terminal-nonescape}. Since the terminal packet contains no coordinate for quotient streams, host sequence equality, selected limits, ambient Real completeness, or metric-completeness data, none of those objects is exported by the nonempty index route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName open-phase finite-window budget]
\label{thm:streamname-open-phase-finite-window-budget}
Every Phase Real and Completion consumer routed through the terminal StreamName packet has a finite-window budget route whose source is the nonempty StreamName window index, whose middle rows are the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger, and whose target is the finite $\RealUp$ seal handoff. \end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply \autoref{thm:streamname-open-phase-window-index-nonempty}. The consumer has a finite nonempty StreamName window-index surface before any downstream Real-facing read occurs.

Next use \autoref{thm:streamname-finite-window-budget-monotonicity}. Enlarging or transporting the selected finite window changes only the displayed budget rows and keeps the consumer inside the finite StreamName packet.

Apply \autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-real-dyadic-window-pullback}. The StreamName window, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger, and finite $\RealUp$ seal row form one finite pullback, so the same window budget supports all three downstream faces.

Finally invoke \autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-real-dyadic-pullback-seal-determinacy} and \autoref{thm:streamname-open-phase-terminal-nonescape}. \end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName open-phase budget source determinacy]
\label{thm:streamname-open-phase-budget-source-determinacy}
Every Phase Real and Completion consumer that reaches a finite $\RealUp$ seal through the StreamName finite-window budget has a unique displayed source route. The route is the ordered four-face handoff
$$
\StreamNameUp\text{ window budget}\longrightarrow \RegSeqRatUp\text{ readback}\longrightarrow \DyadicRatCoreUp\text{ ledger}\longrightarrow \RealUp\text{ finite seal}.
$$
If a second displayed route has the same $\BHist$ observations, componentwise $\hsame$ transports, displayed $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ rows, then that route factors through the same finite-window budget packet. Thus the source of the finite $\RealUp$ seal is determined by the StreamName budget packet together with its RegSeqRat readback and DyadicRatCore ledger.

The determinacy statement exports no quotient stream, no host sequence equality, no selected limit, no ambient $\RealUp$ completeness row, and no metric-completeness datum.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with \autoref{thm:streamname-open-phase-finite-window-budget}. It places the Phase Real and Completion consumer on a displayed finite packet whose source is the nonempty StreamName window budget and whose downstream faces are the RegSeqRat readback, the DyadicRatCore ledger, and the finite Real seal row.

Apply \autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-real-dyadic-window-pullback}. The four displayed faces are not independent rows: they form one finite pullback over the same StreamName window budget packet, with the same $\BHist$ observations, $\hsame$ transports, $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ rows.

Now use \autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-real-dyadic-pullback-seal-determinacy}. Any second displayed route with the same observations, transports, routes, provenance, and name rows reaches the same finite Real seal only by factoring through that pullback. Hence it factors through the original finite-window budget packet and cannot supply a distinct source for the seal.

Finally invoke \autoref{thm:streamname-open-phase-terminal-nonescape}. The terminal packet has no coordinate for quotient streams, host sequence equality, selected limits, ambient Real completeness, or metric-completeness data, so the determinacy claim is entirely a finite displayed-route statement.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName open-phase finite-window support row minimality]
\label{thm:streamname-open-phase-finite-window-support-row-minimality}
Every accepted four-face Phase Real and Completion read through the open StreamName route exposes exactly the StreamName finite-window budget, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger, and the finite $\RealUp$ seal, supported by the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows. Dropping any one of those support rows leaves the accepted finite packet without the row needed to read the next face of the route.

Conversely, adding quotient stream equality, selected limit data, ambient completeness, countable choice, metric-completeness, arithmetic or order package data, or a bridge row leaves the current open-phase route. Such data are not coordinates of the terminal finite packet and cannot be read as part of the accepted Phase Real and Completion surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the finite-window budget theorem, \autoref{thm:streamname-open-phase-finite-window-budget}. It identifies the accepted budget as a finite StreamName packet whose downstream faces are exactly the RegSeqRat readback, the DyadicRatCore ledger, and the finite Real seal.

Use the RegSeqRat budget source lock, \autoref{thm:streamname-open-phase-regseqrat-budget-source-lock}. The readback cannot acquire an independent source, so removing the StreamName budget or any displayed support row needed by that budget breaks the route rather than producing another accepted four-face read.

Now apply the Real seal route theorem, \autoref{thm:streamname-open-phase-real-seal-route}. The Real-facing handoff is reached only through the ordered StreamName, RegSeqRat, DyadicRatCore, and Real faces, so the ledger and seal rows are necessary parts of the same finite packet.

Finally invoke terminal non-escape. Since the terminal packet has no coordinate for quotient stream equality, selected limits, ambient completeness, countable choice, metric-completeness, arithmetic or order packages, or bridge rows, adding any such row moves the consumer outside the open-phase route instead of extending it.
\end{proof}
