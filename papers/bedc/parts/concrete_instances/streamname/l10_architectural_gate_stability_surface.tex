\begin{theorem}[StreamNameUp L10 architectural gate stability surface]
\label{thm:streamname-l10-architectural-gate-stability-surface}
The L10 countable-name gateway of $\StreamNameUp$ is exactly the union of the
public countable-name interface of
\autoref{thm:streamname-public-countable-name-interface}, the public
finite-window interface of
\autoref{thm:streamname-public-finite-window-interface}, the public reindexing
obligation of \autoref{def:streamname-public-reindexing-obligation}, the public
constant obligation of \autoref{thm:streamname-public-constant-obligation}, the
public transport obligation of \autoref{thm:streamname-public-transport-obligation},
the public finite-ledger obligation of
\autoref{thm:streamname-public-ledger-obligation}, and the downstream
$\RegSeqRatUp/\RealUp$ handoff boundary of
\autoref{thm:streamname-public-real-handoff-obligation}.

Consequently every L10 consumer receives only the displayed
$\StreamNameUp$ carrier rows, certified unary observation rows, pointwise
$\RatUp$ classifier rows, finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ windows,
$\mathsf{InBundle}$ membership, componentwise $\hsame$ comparisons, displayed
$\Cont$ routes, $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ rows. The
gateway supplies no $\RegSeqRatUp$ regularity witness, $\RealUp$ completion row,
quotient representative, host $\mathsf{Nat}$ stream, hidden limit selector, or
standard bridge.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the countable-name interface through
\autoref{thm:streamname-public-countable-name-interface}. Its carrier and
classifier are the displayed $\mathsf{RatStreamNameCarrier}$ and
$\mathsf{RatStreamNameClassifier}$ rows, read pointwise as $\RatUp$ data over
certified unary observations in $\mathsf{BHist}$.

Restrict those rows through the public finite-window interface of
\autoref{thm:streamname-public-finite-window-interface}. A
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ selects only finitely many certified observations, and
the finite-window classifier adds only bundle membership, endpoint, stability,
and empty-window boundary rows already listed by that interface.

Next apply the reindexing, constant, transport, and finite-ledger obligations:
\autoref{def:streamname-public-reindexing-obligation},
\autoref{thm:streamname-public-constant-obligation},
\autoref{thm:streamname-public-transport-obligation}, and
\autoref{thm:streamname-public-ledger-obligation}. These clauses respectively
repack certified unary reindexing, constant point rows, displayed
$\hsame/\Cont/\Pkg$ transport, and the finite observation ledger without adding
any off-ledger source.

Finally use the downstream handoff boundary
\autoref{thm:streamname-public-real-handoff-obligation}. It is precisely the
interface consumed by later $\RegSeqRatUp$ and $\RealUp$ chapters, and it states
that regularity, completion, quotient, limit-selection, host-stream, and
standard-bridge data must be supplied downstream rather than by $\StreamNameUp$.
Thus the L10 gateway is exactly the listed union and has the stated non-escape
boundary.
\end{proof}
