\section{StreamName finite-request RealUp seal lock}
\label{sec:streamname-finite-request-real-seal-lock}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName finite request section RealUp seal lock]
\label{thm:streamname-finite-request-section-real-seal-lock}
Any finite request section packet accepted by $\StreamNameUp$ and consumed by a
$\RegSeqRatUp$-to-$\RealUp$ seal factors through the displayed
$\StreamNameUp$ schedule rows, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint and radius
rows, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regularity ledger, componentwise $\hsame$ transport,
the displayed $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local $\NameCert$
rows. The seal lock exports no quotient stream, no host real equality, no
ambient completion point, and no $\RealUp$ completeness datum.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the finite request section packet of
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-endpoint-finite-request-section} and
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-window-budget-section}. These results make
the accepted request a finite StreamName section whose public rows are the
schedule, endpoint, readback, budget, transport, continuation, package, and
local naming rows.

Next use the RegSeqRat-to-RealUp handoff rows of
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-real-seal-handoff},
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-real-seal-endpoint-coverage}, and
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-finite-request-real-seal-correspondence}. They place
the Real seal behind the same regularity ledger and finite request rows rather
than behind a host sequence equality or selected completed limit.

Then compose with the DyadicRatCore endpoint and radius ledger reads recorded
by \autoref{thm:regseqrat-dyadic-radius-observation},
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-dyadic-radius-ledger-readback}, and
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-streamname-dyadic-real-handoff-exactness}. The
endpoint and radius rows are therefore the displayed dyadic rows carried by
the finite section, not independent ambient metric data.

Finally apply the non-escape exclusions of
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-seal-request-section-nonescape} and
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-real-budget-nonescape}. Since the composed
route has coordinates only for the StreamName schedule, DyadicRatCore
endpoint-radius ledger, RegSeqRat regularity ledger, $\hsame$, $\Cont$,
$\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$, it cannot export quotient streams, host real
equality, ambient completion points, or RealUp completeness data.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName finite request Real seal totality]
\label{thm:streamname-finite-request-real-seal-totality}
Every finite $\StreamNameUp$ request consumed by the
$\RegSeqRatUp$-to-$\RealUp$ seal is exhausted by the displayed
$\StreamNameUp$ schedule rows, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint and radius
rows, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regularity and readback rows, the $\RealUp$ seal row,
and the support rows $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$.
There is no additional request coordinate outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:streamname-finite-request-section-real-seal-lock}. It
already factors the accepted finite request section through the StreamName
schedule rows, the DyadicRatCore endpoint-radius ledger, the RegSeqRat
regularity ledger, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and
local $\NameCert$.

It remains only to name the terminal seal face. The same theorem places the
accepted request behind the $\RegSeqRatUp$-to-$\RealUp$ seal, while
\autoref{thm:real-regseqrat-terminal-budget-exhaustion} identifies that seal
read with the four displayed object faces and the same support rows.
Therefore the finite request has no coordinate beyond the schedule, endpoint,
radius, regularity, readback, seal, and support rows listed in the statement.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName request section non-escape boundary]
\label{thm:streamname-request-section-nonescape}
The finite request section of
\autoref{thm:streamname-finite-request-section-real-seal-lock} does not read a
quotient stream, host sequence equality, selected limit, host real equality,
ambient completion point, countable-choice row, metric-completeness row, or
$\RealUp$ completeness datum.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-finite-request-real-seal-totality}. The visible packet
is exhausted by the finite StreamName schedule, DyadicRatCore endpoint-radius
rows, RegSeqRat regularity and readback rows, the RealUp seal row, and the
support rows $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$.

Now use
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-seal-request-section-nonescape}. That boundary
excludes quotient streams, host equalities, selected limits, ambient completion
points, and RealUp completeness data from the request section. The totality
packet has no row for countable choice or metric completeness either, so those
rows cannot be read from the section.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName open-phase request handoff]
\label{thm:streamname-open-phase-request-handoff}
The finite request section is precisely the local handoff from
$\StreamNameUp$ through $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$ to $\RealUp$: its
terminal packet is the four-face route with $\StreamNameUp$ schedule,
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint-radius ledger, $\RegSeqRatUp$ regularity and
readback, the $\RealUp$ seal, and the support rows $\BHist$, $\hsame$,
$\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with
\autoref{thm:streamname-finite-request-section-real-seal-lock}. It places the
finite request inside the local $\StreamNameUp$ section consumed by the
$\RegSeqRatUp$-to-$\RealUp$ seal. Then use
\autoref{thm:real-regseqrat-terminal-budget-exhaustion} to read that seal as a
terminal four-face packet.

Next apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-completion-four-object-exit-route}. The four
faces are exactly $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and
$\RealUp$, and the only support rows are $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$,
and $\NameCert$.

Finally apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-seal-request-section-nonescape}. It prevents the
handoff from carrying quotient streams, selected limits, host real equality,
ambient completion points, countable choice, metric completeness, or a RealUp
completeness datum. Hence the section is exactly the local open-phase handoff
through the displayed four faces and support rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName finite request Real seal classifier exactness]
\label{thm:streamname-finite-request-real-seal-classifier-exactness}
For a finite request section consumed by the
$\RegSeqRatUp$-to-$\RealUp$ seal, acceptance by the seal-facing classifier is
necessary and sufficient for factorization through exactly the visible
$\StreamNameUp$ schedule rows, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint and radius
rows, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regularity and readback rows, the $\RealUp$ seal row,
and the support rows $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$.
The classifier accepts no quotient stream equality, host real equality,
selected limit, choice datum, ambient completion, metric-completeness row, or
second $\RealUp$ seal coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For necessity, start from an accepted seal-facing request. Apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-finite-request-section-real-seal-lock} to factor the
request through the finite StreamName section consumed by the Real seal, and
then apply \autoref{thm:streamname-finite-request-real-seal-totality}. The
accepted coordinates are exhausted by the schedule, endpoint, radius,
regularity, readback, seal, and displayed support rows.

For sufficiency, suppose a finite section already factors through exactly
those rows. Use \autoref{thm:streamname-open-phase-request-handoff} to read the
packet as the local handoff from $\StreamNameUp$ through $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ and
$\RegSeqRatUp$ to $\RealUp$. Then
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-completion-four-object-exit-route} identifies the
same packet as the four-object Real-completion exit route, so the
seal-facing classifier accepts it.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:streamname-request-section-nonescape}. The
non-escape boundary rules out quotient stream equality, host real equality,
selected limits, choice data, ambient completion, metric-completeness rows, and
any second $\RealUp$ seal coordinate. Thus the displayed four faces and support
rows are not merely sufficient witnesses for acceptance; they are the exact
classifier surface.
\end{proof}
