\begin{theorem}[StreamName-RegSeqRat uniform finite-window seal]
\label{thm:streamname-regseqrat-uniform-finite-window-seal}
Let $W$ be a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ observation window for a public
$\StreamNameUp$ rational stream package, and suppose that the two endpoints
of $W$ satisfy $\mathsf{RatStreamNameFiniteWindowClassifier}$. Then the
StreamName pointwise seal, the $\RegSeqRat$ scheduled seal, every certified
nested-window handoff through $W$, and the finite-window $\RealUp$ readback
select the same finite observation packet under $\hsame$.

The common packet consists exactly of the pointwise
$\mathsf{RatStreamNameCarrier}$ and
$\mathsf{RatStreamNameClassifier}$ observations at the certified unary
indices of $W$, the accepted $\RegSeqRat$ pointwise seal rows, the
$\RealUp$ endpoint seal row, the finite $\Cont$ ledger, the
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ membership rows, and the shared $\Pkg$ provenance.
It contains no quotient representative, completion modulus, host index
equality, or additional regularity datum.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First form the pointwise seal over the certified endpoints of $W$. Apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-pointwise-seal-stability} at each unary
index. The endpoint classifier hypotheses give the same carrier row,
classifier row, $\hsame$ transport, finite $\Cont$ route, $\Pkg$ provenance,
and accepted $\mathsf{DyadicRatCore}$ ledger entry for the StreamName and
$\RegSeqRat$ reads.

Next paste these pointwise rows over the whole finite window. By
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-pointwise-seal-pasting}, the scheduled
$\RegSeqRat$ seal over $W$ is the unique finite packet assembled from those
rows and the same finite ledger. No quotient or completion representative is
introduced by this pasting step.

Now compare all certified subwindows of $W$. Use
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-nested-window-handoff} to identify the
direct and nested handoff routes with the same restricted source surface.
Thus every nested-window consumer sees the pointwise packet just constructed,
with the same $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ membership rows and $\hsame$
comparisons.

Finally apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-realup-triple-seal-cover}. The triple cover
identifies the StreamName window package, the $\RegSeqRat$ scheduled seal,
and the $\RealUp$ endpoint readback whenever their unary observations,
finite $\Cont$ ledger, $\hsame$ transports, and $\Pkg$ provenance agree.
Combining the four steps gives one uniform finite-window seal packet under
$\hsame$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName RealUp finite-window seal boundary]
\label{thm:streamname-realup-seal-boundary}
Let $W$ be a finite public $\StreamNameUp$ rational observation window whose

finite $\Cont$ ledger, the $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ membership rows, the
corresponding $\mathsf{InBundle}$ rows, the $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$
observations, and shared $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance. Then these rows determine
exactly the finite-window $\RealUp$ seal boundary read from $W$.

Conversely, every public finite-window $\RealUp$ seal read arising from this
handoff factors through those same StreamName source rows, accepted
$\RegSeqRat$ pointwise seal rows, bundle-membership rows, unary observations,
finite ledger entries, and provenance rows. The boundary contains no completion
object, quotient representative, host index equality, or regularity datum
outside the displayed $\RegSeqRat$ seal.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First recover the StreamName source surface. Apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-regseq-finite-window-handoff}. It identifies the
finite public window read with the displayed rational StreamName observations,
the unary histories at the certified indices, the finite $\Cont$ routes, and
the shared $\Pkg$ provenance.

Next pass to the scheduled rational seal. By
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-scheduled-seal-exhaustion}, the
$\RegSeqRat$ side contributes only the accepted pointwise seal rows over the
same unary observations, the same bundle-membership rows, and the same finite
ledger. No additional sequence regularity row is available at this step.

Now compare the three visible seal surfaces. Use
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-realup-triple-seal-cover}. The StreamName
window, the $\RegSeqRat$ scheduled seal, and the $\RealUp$ endpoint readback
cover one another exactly when their unary observations, bundle rows, finite
transport ledger, $\hsame$ comparisons, and provenance agree.

Finally apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-uniform-finite-window-seal}. Uniformity over
nested finite windows prevents a later subwindow from adding completion data,
quotient representatives, host index equality, or hidden regularity data.
Thus the forward direction gives a unique finite-window $\RealUp$ seal
boundary, and the reverse direction factors every public read through precisely
the displayed rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName-RegSeqRat-RealUp seal pullback uniqueness]
\label{thm:streamname-regseqrat-realup-seal-pullback-uniqueness}
Let $W$ be a finite public $\StreamNameUp$ rational observation window. Suppose
two finite-window $\RealUp$ seal reads over $W$ arise from the same StreamName
window rows, the same accepted $\RegSeqRat$ pointwise seal rows, the same
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ and $\mathsf{InBundle}$ membership rows, the same
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ observations, the same finite $\Cont$ ledger, the same
$\hsame$ transports, and the same $\Pkg$ provenance. Then the two reads
determine the same $\RealUp$ finite-window seal boundary.

Conversely, any equality of $\RealUp$ finite-window seal boundaries arising
from $W$ factors back through that displayed StreamName--$\RegSeqRat$ source
surface: the common boundary is witnessed by the same StreamName window rows,
the same accepted pointwise seal rows, the same bundle-membership rows, the
same unary observations, the same finite ledger, the same $\hsame$ transport
surface, and the same provenance. This pullback uses no quotient stream,
selected host limit, host index equality, completion witness, or ambient
completeness assumption.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First expose the source surface for each read. Apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-regseq-finite-window-handoff} to both
finite-window handoffs. The common StreamName window rows, unary observations,
finite $\Cont$ routes, $\hsame$ transports, and $\Pkg$ provenance give the same
public source packet.

Next exhaust the rational scheduled seal. By
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-scheduled-seal-exhaustion}, each handoff has
only the displayed accepted $\RegSeqRat$ pointwise seal rows, the same
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ and $\mathsf{InBundle}$ membership rows, and the same
finite ledger available to it.

The three seal surfaces now compare over a single packet. Use
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-realup-triple-seal-cover} to identify the
StreamName window surface, the $\RegSeqRat$ scheduled seal, and the $\RealUp$
readback surface. Then
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-uniform-finite-window-seal} prevents any
nested finite subwindow from changing that packet.

It remains to read off the boundary. Apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-realup-seal-boundary}. The forward direction gives
equality of the two $\RealUp$ finite-window seal boundaries. The converse
direction says that any such equality is already a factorization through the
same StreamName--$\RegSeqRat$ source rows, so no quotient stream, selected host
limit, host index equality, completion witness, or ambient completeness datum
can enter the pullback.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName-RegSeqRat-RealUp terminal pullback factorization]

let $R$ be a terminal finite-window $\RealUp$ seal read over $W$. Suppose $R$

the $\hsame$ transports, the $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ and
$\mathsf{InBundle}$ membership rows, the $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$

Conversely, the displayed StreamName point rows, accepted $\RegSeqRat$
pointwise seal rows, $\RealUp$ boundary row, finite $\Cont$ ledger,
$\hsame$ transports, bundle-membership rows, unary observations, and
provenance rows repack into the same terminal finite-window $\RealUp$ seal
boundary. The factorization carries no quotient stream, selected host limit,

\begin{proof}

\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-uniform-finite-window-seal}. It identifies
the StreamName point rows and accepted $\RegSeqRat$ pointwise seal rows over
$W$ with one finite observation packet under the same $\hsame$ transports,
finite $\Cont$ ledger, bundle-membership rows, unary observations, and
provenance.

Next read the terminal boundary of the $\RealUp$ seal. Use
\autoref{thm:streamname-realup-seal-boundary}. The forward direction says that
the displayed public packet determines the finite-window $\RealUp$ seal
boundary, and the reverse direction repacks those same rows into the boundary

Finally compare any two terminal presentations of the read. Apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-realup-seal-pullback-uniqueness}. The

accepted $\RegSeqRat$ rows, bundle-membership rows, unary observations,

The preceding steps give both directions of the factorization. Since each

there is no row from quotient streams, selected host limits, host index
equality, completion witnesses, ambient completeness, or metric-completeness
data available to the terminal seal.
\end{proof}

\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-dyadic-realup-terminal-pullback-factorization}.

\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-realup-composite-terminal-pullback}, a

\end{proof}
