\section{StreamName Real and RegSeqRat handoff}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName finite-window handoff to RegSeqRat and RealUp]
\label{thm:streamname-real-regseq-finite-window-handoff}
Let $\mathcal{W}$ be a public $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window package over
$\Hist$ whose selected observations are certified by a finite
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ ledger and by $\mathsf{InBundle}$ membership rows. Then
the rows handed to $\RegSeqRat$ or finite-window $\RealUp$ consumers are exactly
the selected $\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$ rows, the finite
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ ledger, the $\mathsf{InBundle}$ membership rows, and the
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ rows naming the observed histories.

No $\RegSeqRat$ regularity row, no $\RealUp$ seal classifier, no completion
object, and no host equality row is read from $\mathcal{W}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the public StreamName boundary through
\autoref{thm:streamname-public-finite-window-interface}. That theorem lists the
carrier, selected classifier, probe-bundle, membership, and unary-history rows
available at the public finite-window interface.

For a finite-window $\RealUp$ consumer, apply
\autoref{thm:realup-finite-window-exhaustion-obligation} and
\autoref{thm:realup-streamname-finite-window-ledger-boundary}. These RealUp
rows consume the displayed finite StreamName packet and its ledger, not a
completion object or a hidden host stream.

For a $\RegSeqRat$ consumer, apply
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-finite-window-seal-exactness}. The seal reads the
selected finite-window classifier and bundle rows as inputs to the downstream
regularity and seal surface. Since the StreamName interface theorem lists no
regularity row and no seal classifier, those obligations remain outside the
handoff.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName constant-window handoff boundary]
\label{thm:streamname-real-regseq-constant-boundary}
Let $a,b:\Hist$ be rational-history endpoints and let $\mathcal{W}$ be a
constant rational $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window package over an inhabited
finite unary window. The package may seed the $\RegSeqRat$ stream side only by
exporting the same $\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(a,b)$ row together with its
finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, $\mathsf{InBundle}$, and $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$
ledger. A $\RegSeqRat$ certificate must still supply regularity rows, and a
sealed $\RealUp$ candidate must still supply the seal classifier row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:streamname-constant-window-real-boundary}. It says that a
finite-window $\RealUp$ consumer of the constant package reads only the rational
point classifier and the finite probe ledger.

Apply \autoref{thm:streamname-constant-window-regseq-boundary}. It gives the
same boundary for $\RegSeqRat$: the constant StreamName window supplies rational
point-classifier and probe-ledger data, while the regularity rows remain
$\RegSeqRat$ obligations.

Finally use \autoref{thm:regseqrat-obligation-seal-ledger} and
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-obligation-seal-interface}. These theorems place
regularity and seal-interface rows on the RegSeqRat and RealUp side of the
boundary, so the constant window is only a seed surface for the displayed finite
rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName RegSeqRat RealUp obligation separation]
\label{thm:streamname-real-regseq-obligation-separation}
Across the StreamName, RegSeqRat, and RealUp handoff, the obligation surface
separates into three disjoint sources. The $\StreamNameUp$ package contributes
the stream carrier, selected rational-history classifier, finite
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ ledger, $\mathsf{InBundle}$ membership, and
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ observation rows. The $\RegSeqRat$ certificate
contributes regularity, dyadic-tolerance, transport, and stream-spine rows. The
$\RealUp$ seal contributes the seal classifier required by the real
classifier specification.

No finite StreamName window contributes a quotient, a completion witness, a
host index equality, a RegSeqRat regularity witness, or the RealUp seal
classifier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the StreamName part first. By
\autoref{thm:streamname-public-finite-window-interface}, and in the constant
case by \autoref{thm:streamname-constant-window-real-boundary} and
\autoref{thm:streamname-constant-window-regseq-boundary}, the StreamName
surface exposes exactly the carrier, classifier, bundle, membership, unary, and
finite ledger rows.

Read the RegSeqRat part next. The regularity and dyadic-tolerance rows are
listed by \autoref{thm:regseqrat-obligation-seal-ledger}, while
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-finite-window-seal-exactness} describes how the selected
finite-window rows are consumed at the seal boundary.

Read the RealUp part last. The seal classifier is supplied by
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-obligation-seal-interface} and is consumed through
\autoref{thm:realup-finite-window-exhaustion-obligation}. Thus every downstream
row is accounted for by exactly one side of the handoff, and no completion or
host equality row is imported from the finite StreamName package.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[StreamName-RegSeqRat scheduled seal package]
\label{def:streamname-regseqrat-scheduled-seal-package}
A $\StreamNameUp$--$\RegSeqRat$ scheduled seal package is the scheduled
constant observation row together with three finite-window boundary rows:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item the public finite-window $\StreamNameUp$ row over
  $\mathsf{BHist}$ histories, its finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ ledger,
  $\mathsf{InBundle}$ membership rows, $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ observation
  rows, and the displayed $\hsame$ comparisons between scheduled endpoints;
  \item the $\RegSeqRat$ finite-window seal row reading the same selected
  $\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$ rows and dyadic ledger rows;
  \item the finite-window $\RealUp$ ledger-boundary row that consumes only the
  selected rational classifier rows, the probe ledger, and the unary
  observation rows.
\end{enumerate}
The package has no further carrier. Its consumer-facing surface is exactly the
scheduled observation, finite-window StreamName row, RegSeqRat seal row, and
RealUp ledger-boundary row over the displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$,
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, $\mathsf{InBundle}$, $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$,
$\hsame$, $\RatUp$ classifier, and dyadic ledger data.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName-RegSeqRat scheduled seal exhaustion]
\label{thm:streamname-regseqrat-scheduled-seal-exhaustion}
Every consumer row of the scheduled seal package of
\autoref{def:streamname-regseqrat-scheduled-seal-package} is projected from
exactly one of the following sources: the scheduled constant observation
exactness row, the scheduled window reindex row, the $\RegSeqRat$
finite-window seal exactness row, or the finite-window $\RealUp$
ledger-boundary row. Conversely, those four source rows repack into the
scheduled seal package and determine its whole consumer-facing surface.

No consumer row of the package exports completion data, quotient data, host
index equality, a $\RegSeqRat$ regularity witness beyond the finite-window seal
row, or a $\RealUp$ seal classifier outside the displayed ledger boundary.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First project the scheduled StreamName source. Apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-constant-scheduled-observation-exactness} to obtain
the constant observation row and
\autoref{thm:streamname-scheduled-window-reindex-stability} to identify its
finite scheduled windows under reindexing.

Next project the downstream seal rows. The finite-window feed theorem
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-realup-finite-window-feed} places the
selected $\RatUp$ classifier rows, probe-bundle rows, membership rows, unary
observation rows, and dyadic ledger rows at the common handoff. The RegSeqRat
seal scope theorem \autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-seal-scope} says that
the RegSeqRat consumer reads precisely the finite-window seal row from that
handoff.

The separation theorems in this section identify the remaining boundary.
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-regseq-finite-window-handoff} gives the public
finite-window StreamName row, \autoref{thm:streamname-real-regseq-constant-boundary}
specializes it to the constant scheduled source, and
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-regseq-obligation-separation} keeps the
RegSeqRat and RealUp obligations disjoint.

For the reverse direction, repack the scheduled observation exactness row,
the reindex row, the RegSeqRat finite-window seal exactness row, and the
RealUp finite-window ledger boundary into the three displayed components of
\autoref{def:streamname-regseqrat-scheduled-seal-package}. The same cited
handoff and separation theorems exclude every row outside those components, so
the repacked package is exhaustive.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName Real-RegSeq finite-overlap source stability]
\label{thm:streamname-real-regseq-finite-overlap-source-stability}
Let $\mathcal{W}_0$ and $\mathcal{W}_1$ be public $\StreamNameUp$
finite-window packages whose observation windows restrict to a certified common
finite overlap $K$. Suppose the two restrictions expose the same
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$ rows, the same finite
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ ledger, the same $\mathsf{InBundle}$ membership rows,
the same $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ observation rows, and the same displayed
$\mathsf{hsame}$ comparisons on $K$. Then any $\RegSeqRat$ handoff package, and
any finite-window $\RealUp$ handoff package, induced by the two restrictions
has the same source surface on $K$.

The common source surface is only the restricted $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window
surface over those rows. It excludes every $\RegSeqRat$ regularity row, the
$\RealUp$ seal classifier, completion data, quotient data, and host index
equality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First restrict both public StreamName packages to $K$. By
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-regseq-finite-window-handoff}, each restricted
handoff exposes only the selected rational-history classifier, finite
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ ledger, $\mathsf{InBundle}$ rows, and
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ rows.

The assumed overlap equality identifies exactly those rows through the
displayed $\mathsf{hsame}$ comparisons. In the constant source case,
\autoref{thm:streamname-constant-window-overlap-exactness} gives the same
identification from the shared constant rational endpoint row; for a general
finite RealUp overlap, the overlap boundary is the one recorded by
\autoref{thm:realup-finite-window-exhaustion-overlap-closure}.

Now apply \autoref{thm:streamname-real-regseq-obligation-separation}. The
RegSeqRat regularity rows and the RealUp seal classifier are downstream
obligations, while completion data, quotient data, and host index equality are
not exported by the finite StreamName side.

Repacking the two restricted inputs therefore gives the same source handoff
surface on $K$ for either downstream consumer, with all non-source rows kept
outside the overlap package.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName Real-RegSeq overlap unique source]
\label{thm:streamname-real-regseq-overlap-unique-source}
Let $\mathcal{W}_0$ and $\mathcal{W}_1$ be public $\StreamNameUp$
finite-window packages whose restrictions to a certified finite overlap $K$
expose the same $\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$ rows, the same
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ ledger, the same $\mathsf{InBundle}$ membership rows,
the same $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ observation rows, and the same displayed
$\hsame$ rows. Then the overlap determines one StreamName source surface:
every $\RegSeqRat$ handoff package induced from either restriction, and every
finite-window $\RealUp$ handoff package induced from either restriction, has
that same source surface on $K$.

This source surface consists only of the public finite-window
$\StreamNameUp$ rows displayed on the certified overlap. It does not include
$\RegSeqRat$ regularity rows, $\RealUp$ seal classifiers, completion data,
quotient data, or host index equality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First restrict both public finite-window packages to the common overlap $K$.
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-regseq-finite-window-handoff} identifies the
source-facing rows of each restriction as the rational-history classifier,
probe-bundle ledger, membership rows, unary-history rows, and displayed
$\hsame$ comparisons.

The hypotheses say precisely that those rows agree on $K$.
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-regseq-finite-overlap-source-stability} then
turns the shared restricted rows into the same source surface for every
induced $\RegSeqRat$ handoff and every induced finite-window $\RealUp$
handoff.

It remains to check that no downstream or host row is being folded into the
source. Apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-regseq-obligation-separation}: RegSeqRat
regularity rows and RealUp seal classifiers are downstream obligations rather
than StreamName source rows. The same separation keeps completion data,
quotient data, and host index equality outside the handoff source.

Finally, \autoref{thm:realup-finite-window-exhaustion-overlap-closure}
supplies the RealUp finite-window overlap boundary used by the consumer side.
Since the RealUp boundary reads the same certified overlap and the StreamName
source has already been fixed there, both induced consumer packages inherit
one source handoff surface on $K$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName RegSeqRat pointwise seal stability]
\label{thm:streamname-regseqrat-pointwise-seal-stability}
Let $\mathcal{W}_0$ and $\mathcal{W}_1$ be public $\StreamNameUp$
finite-window packages whose restrictions to a certified finite overlap $K$

$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$ row, every $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ ledger row,
every $\mathsf{InBundle}$ membership row, and every displayed $\hsame$ row.

DyadicRatCore finite ledger over that overlap. Then the $\RealUp$ seal
classifier rows induced from those two handoffs agree pointwise on $K$.

The source used by this pointwise seal comparison is only the public
$\StreamNameUp$ finite-window surface together with the consumed
$\RegSeqRat$ dyadic ledger. The comparison does not use completion data,
quotient data, host index equality, or a $\RealUp$ completeness row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First fix the source surface. Apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-regseq-overlap-unique-source} to the two
restricted public StreamName packages. The hypotheses are exactly the shared
finite-window rows on $K$, so both induced $\RegSeqRat$ handoffs and both
finite-window $\RealUp$ handoffs read one source surface there.

Next fix the seal ledger. By
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-dyadic-common-radius-seal}, the $\RegSeqRat$ seal reads
the refined finite dyadic ledger: carried RatUp endpoints, positive unary
denominators, common-radius rows, $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ provenance, and the
scoped DyadicRatCore boundary. Since the two handoffs consume the same
DyadicRatCore ledger, the seal input rows coincide pointwise on the overlap.

Now transport the public observation rows along the displayed comparisons.
\autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-public-observation-hsame-transport-bridge} says
that each $\hsame$-transported DyadicRatCore public observation remains the
same $\RealUp$ finite-window input, with the same finite ledger and RatUp
endpoint faces.

Finally use classifier stability and transitivity along the displayed
$\hsame$ rows. The StreamName finite-window classifiers agree on $K$, the
dyadic seal ledger agrees on $K$, and the transported RealUp finite-window
inputs are the same rows. Hence the induced $\RealUp$ seal classifier rows are
pointwise identical on the common overlap, without importing any completion,
quotient, host-index, or RealUp completeness datum.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName RegSeqRat pointwise seal pasting]
\label{thm:streamname-regseqrat-pointwise-seal-pasting}

$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$ row, every $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ ledger row,

DyadicRatCore finite ledger on $K$. Then the $\RealUp$ seal classifier rows

on $\mathcal{W}_0 \cup \mathcal{W}_1$.

The pasted surface exports only $\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$ rows,
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ ledger rows, $\mathsf{InBundle}$ membership rows,
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ observations, $\hsame$ comparisons, and the shared
dyadic ledger. It exports no completion datum, quotient datum, host index
equality, or $\RealUp$ completeness row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First identify the common source on the overlap. Apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-regseq-overlap-unique-source} to the two
restricted certified windows. The displayed agreement rows are exactly the
finite StreamName source rows, so the overlap has one source-facing surface
for both induced $\RegSeqRat$ handoffs.

Next stabilize the seal rows over that common source. By
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-pointwise-seal-stability}, the shared
DyadicRatCore finite ledger forces the $\RealUp$ seal classifier rows induced
from the two handoffs to agree pointwise on every member of $K$.

It remains to build the union surface. Use finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$
induction over the selected rows of $\mathcal{W}_0$ and $\mathcal{W}_1$: rows
outside the overlap are inserted from their own certified window, while rows
inside $K$ are identified by the pointwise seal stability just obtained.

Finally exhaust the pasted rows. Apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-pointwise-handoff-exhaustion}: every row in
the handoff surface is one of the displayed pointwise
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$ observations, bundle and membership rows,
unary observations, $\hsame$ comparisons, or dyadic ledger rows. Hence the
finite union surface contains no completion, quotient, host-index, or
RealUp-completeness datum.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName RegSeqRat nested-window handoff]

$\mathsf{RatStreamNameCarrier}$ and
$\mathsf{RatStreamNameClassifier}$ observations at certified
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ indices, the finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ ledger,

comparisons. It contains no $\RegSeqRat$ regularity datum, $\RealUp$ seal

\begin{proof}

\autoref{thm:streamname-real-regseq-finite-window-handoff}, the handoff from
$L$ to $J$ exposes only the selected rational-stream point rows, the finite
bundle ledger, the membership rows, and the displayed $\hsame$ comparisons.

projects the two-step handoff to that same kind of surface over $K$.

Now compare the direct route. Applying
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-regseq-finite-window-handoff} directly to

the certified pointwise carrier and classifier observations, the finite
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ ledger, the $\mathsf{InBundle}$ membership rows, and
the displayed $\hsame$ comparisons over $K$.

Use \autoref{thm:streamname-real-regseq-overlap-unique-source} first on the
overlap $J$ and then on the overlap $K$. The hypotheses are the same certified
point rows and the same finite ledger rows inherited from $L$, so the two
restriction routes cannot produce different StreamName source surfaces on
either overlap.

Finally apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-pointwise-handoff-exhaustion}. It collapses
both restricted handoffs to the same pointwise
$\mathsf{RatStreamNameCarrier}$ and
$\mathsf{RatStreamNameClassifier}$ rows together with the same finite ledger.
The exclusion clauses in that exhaustion theorem and in the finite-window
handoff theorem keep regularity, seal, completion, quotient, and host-index
data outside the common surface.
\end{proof}

\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-pointwise-handoff-exhaustion}. It says that

$\mathsf{RatStreamNameCarrier}$ and

regularity datum beyond those rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-pointwise-seal-stability}, agreement of the
unary observations, $\hsame$ transports, finite $\Cont$ rows, and $\Pkg$

Next paste the pointwise seals. Apply
