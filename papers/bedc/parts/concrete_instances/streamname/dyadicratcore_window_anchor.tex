\begin{theorem}[StreamName DyadicRatCore window anchor]
\label{thm:streamname-dyadicratcore-window-anchor}
Every $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window read consumed by $\RegSeqRatUp$ or by a $\RealUp$ seal route factors through the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observation and tolerance rows before it becomes a regular-rational handoff. The concrete route is the StreamName finite-window surface, the DyadicRatCore observation ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the RegSeqRat handoff surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the Real seal boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}; it exports no quotient stream equality, selected host limit, countable-choice selector, or ambient completeness row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted StreamName read to its finite-window carrier rows. Those rows are the only visible source available to a downstream regular-rational or Real seal consumer.

Route the window through the DyadicRatCore sibling first. The observation and tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert} reads the same finite window as displayed $\BHist$, componentwise $\hsame$, finite $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ data, so the dyadic anchor is still a finite certificate row rather than a quotient-stream comparison.

Pass the anchored window to RegSeqRat and then to Real. The handoff surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert} consumes the dyadic-window rows as regular rational data, and the seal boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert} receives only that displayed handoff. Hence every such StreamName read factors through DyadicRatCore observation and tolerance before becoming the regular-rational input seen by the Real route.
\end{proof}
