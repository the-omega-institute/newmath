\begin{theorem}[StreamName Real-RegSeq finite-overlap source stability]
\label{thm:streamname-real-regseq-finite-overlap-source-stability}
Let $\mathcal{W}_0$ and $\mathcal{W}_1$ be public $\StreamNameUp$
finite-window packages whose observation windows restrict to a certified common
finite overlap $K$. Suppose the two restrictions expose the same
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$ rows, the same finite
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ ledger, the same $\mathsf{InBundle}$ membership rows,
the same $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ observation rows, and the same displayed
$\mathsf{hsame}$ comparisons on $K$. Then any $\RegSeqRat$ handoff package, and
any finite-window $\RealUp$ handoff package, induced by the two restrictions
has the same source surface on $K$.

The common source surface is only the restricted $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window
surface over those rows. It excludes every $\RegSeqRat$ regularity row, the
$\RealUp$ seal classifier, completion data, quotient data, and host index
equality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First restrict both public StreamName packages to $K$. By
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-regseq-finite-window-handoff}, each restricted
handoff exposes only the selected rational-history classifier, finite
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ ledger, $\mathsf{InBundle}$ rows, and
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ rows.

The assumed overlap equality identifies exactly those rows through the
displayed $\mathsf{hsame}$ comparisons. In the constant source case,
\autoref{thm:streamname-constant-window-overlap-exactness} gives the same
identification from the shared constant rational endpoint row; for a general
finite RealUp overlap, the overlap boundary is the one recorded by
\autoref{thm:realup-finite-window-exhaustion-overlap-closure}.

Now apply \autoref{thm:streamname-real-regseq-obligation-separation}. The
RegSeqRat regularity rows and the RealUp seal classifier are downstream
obligations, while completion data, quotient data, and host index equality are
not exported by the finite StreamName side.

Repacking the two restricted inputs therefore gives the same source handoff
surface on $K$ for either downstream consumer, with all non-source rows kept
outside the overlap package.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName RegSeqRat pointwise seal stability]
\label{thm:streamname-regseqrat-pointwise-seal-stability}
Let $\mathcal{W}_0$ and $\mathcal{W}_1$ be public $\StreamNameUp$
finite-window packages whose restrictions to a certified finite overlap $K$
agree on every certified unary observation, every selected
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$ row, every $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ ledger row,
every $\mathsf{InBundle}$ membership row, and every displayed $\hsame$ row.
Suppose the two induced $\RegSeqRat$ handoff packages consume the same
DyadicRatCore finite ledger over that overlap. Then the $\RealUp$ seal
classifier rows induced from those two handoffs agree pointwise on $K$.

The source used by this pointwise seal comparison is only the public
$\StreamNameUp$ finite-window surface together with the consumed
$\RegSeqRat$ dyadic ledger. The comparison does not use completion data,
quotient data, host index equality, or a $\RealUp$ completeness row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First fix the source surface. Apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-regseq-overlap-unique-source} to the two
restricted public StreamName packages. The hypotheses are exactly the shared
finite-window rows on $K$, so both induced $\RegSeqRat$ handoffs and both
finite-window $\RealUp$ handoffs read one source surface there.

Next fix the seal ledger. By
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-dyadic-common-radius-seal}, the $\RegSeqRat$ seal reads
the refined finite dyadic ledger: carried RatUp endpoints, positive unary
denominators, common-radius rows, $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ provenance, and the
scoped DyadicRatCore boundary. Since the two handoffs consume the same
DyadicRatCore ledger, the seal input rows coincide pointwise on the overlap.

Now transport the public observation rows along the displayed comparisons.
\autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-public-observation-hsame-transport-bridge} says
that each $\hsame$-transported DyadicRatCore public observation remains the
same $\RealUp$ finite-window input, with the same finite ledger and RatUp
endpoint faces.

Finally use classifier stability and transitivity along the displayed
$\hsame$ rows. The StreamName finite-window classifiers agree on $K$, the
dyadic seal ledger agrees on $K$, and the transported RealUp finite-window
inputs are the same rows. Hence the induced $\RealUp$ seal classifier rows are
pointwise identical on the common overlap, without importing any completion,
quotient, host-index, or RealUp completeness datum.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName RegSeqRat pointwise seal pasting]
\label{thm:streamname-regseqrat-pointwise-seal-pasting}
Let $\mathcal{W}_0$ and $\mathcal{W}_1$ be certified finite
$\StreamNameUp$ windows with a certified finite overlap $K$. Suppose their
restrictions to $K$ agree on every certified unary observation, every selected
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$ row, every $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ ledger row,
every $\mathsf{InBundle}$ membership row, and every displayed $\hsame$ row.
Suppose also that the induced $\RegSeqRat$ handoff packages consume the same
DyadicRatCore finite ledger on $K$. Then the $\RealUp$ seal classifier rows
induced from the two handoffs paste to a single finite-window source surface
on $\mathcal{W}_0 \cup \mathcal{W}_1$.

The pasted surface exports only $\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$ rows,
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ ledger rows, $\mathsf{InBundle}$ membership rows,
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ observations, $\hsame$ comparisons, and the shared
dyadic ledger. It exports no completion datum, quotient datum, host index
equality, or $\RealUp$ completeness row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First identify the common source on the overlap. Apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-regseq-overlap-unique-source} to the two
restricted certified windows. The displayed agreement rows are exactly the
finite StreamName source rows, so the overlap has one source-facing surface
for both induced $\RegSeqRat$ handoffs.

Next stabilize the seal rows over that common source. By
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-pointwise-seal-stability}, the shared
DyadicRatCore finite ledger forces the $\RealUp$ seal classifier rows induced
from the two handoffs to agree pointwise on every member of $K$.

It remains to build the union surface. Use finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$
induction over the selected rows of $\mathcal{W}_0$ and $\mathcal{W}_1$: rows
outside the overlap are inserted from their own certified window, while rows
inside $K$ are identified by the pointwise seal stability just obtained.

Finally exhaust the pasted rows. Apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-pointwise-handoff-exhaustion}: every row in
the handoff surface is one of the displayed pointwise
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$ observations, bundle and membership rows,
unary observations, $\hsame$ comparisons, or dyadic ledger rows. Hence the
finite union surface contains no completion, quotient, host-index, or
RealUp-completeness datum.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName RegSeqRat pasted seal associativity]
\label{thm:streamname-regseqrat-pasted-seal-associativity}
Let $\mathcal{W}_0,\mathcal{W}_1,\mathcal{W}_2$ be certified finite
$\StreamNameUp$ windows. Suppose each adjacent pair carries a certified finite
overlap, the restrictions on those overlaps agree on the certified unary
observations, selected $\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$ rows,
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ ledger rows, $\mathsf{InBundle}$ membership rows, and
displayed $\hsame$ rows, and the three induced $\RegSeqRat$ handoff packages
consume one shared $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ finite ledger on every displayed
overlap. Then the two iterated pastings
$(\mathcal{W}_0 \cup \mathcal{W}_1) \cup \mathcal{W}_2$ and
$\mathcal{W}_0 \cup (\mathcal{W}_1 \cup \mathcal{W}_2)$ induce the same
$\RealUp$ seal classifier surface.

The common pasted surface is only the finite StreamName source surface,
the shared DyadicRatCore ledger, and the pointwise $\RegSeqRat$ handoff rows
exported by the three windows. It contains no completion data, quotient data,
host index equality, or $\RealUp$ completeness row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First fix the overlap sources. Apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-regseq-overlap-unique-source} to each certified
pairwise overlap. Each overlap therefore has one StreamName source surface for
the induced $\RegSeqRat$ and finite-window $\RealUp$ handoffs.

Next stabilize the pairwise seal rows. By
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-pointwise-seal-stability}, the shared
DyadicRatCore finite ledger forces the induced $\RealUp$ seal classifier rows
to agree pointwise on every displayed overlap. These pointwise agreements are
the only identifications used by either iterated pasting.

Now paste the left-associated surface. Use
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-pointwise-seal-pasting} first on
$\mathcal{W}_0$ and $\mathcal{W}_1$, then on the resulting finite union and
$\mathcal{W}_2$. The second application reads the same overlap sources and
the same shared dyadic ledger already fixed above.

Paste the right-associated surface in the symmetric order. The same theorem
first pastes $\mathcal{W}_1$ with $\mathcal{W}_2$ and then pastes
$\mathcal{W}_0$ against that finite union. Since the source surfaces and
pointwise seal comparisons are the same rows, both constructions classify
each member of the three-window union by the same $\RealUp$ seal row.

Finally exhaust both iterated surfaces with
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-pointwise-handoff-exhaustion}. Every row in
either surface is one of the displayed StreamName finite-window rows, an
$\hsame$ comparison, or the shared dyadic ledger row. Completion data,
quotient data, host index equality, and RealUp completeness rows have no
carrier coordinate in either association, so the two pasted classifier
surfaces are identical.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName RegSeqRat Real triple-overlap pasting]
\label{thm:streamname-regseqrat-real-triple-overlap-pasting}
Let $\mathcal{W}_0,\mathcal{W}_1,\mathcal{W}_2$ be certified finite
$\StreamNameUp$ windows. Suppose each pair carries a certified finite overlap,
and suppose the two restrictions on every displayed overlap agree on every
certified unary observation, every selected
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$ row, every $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ ledger row,
every $\mathsf{InBundle}$ membership row, every displayed $\hsame$ row, and
every consumed $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ finite ledger row. Then the three induced
$\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff packages and the three finite-window $\RealUp$ seal
classifier rows paste to one finite three-window source surface.

The exported surface is exactly the union of the three finite-window
$\StreamNameUp$ row sets, the shared dyadic ledgers on their overlaps, and the
pointwise $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff rows read from those ledgers. It exports no
quotient stream, host index equality, selected limit, $\RealUp$ completeness
row, metric-completeness datum, or bridge row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First fix the three pairwise sources. Apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-regseq-overlap-unique-source} to
$(\mathcal{W}_0,\mathcal{W}_1)$, to
$(\mathcal{W}_1,\mathcal{W}_2)$, and to
$(\mathcal{W}_0,\mathcal{W}_2)$. The hypotheses provide exactly the displayed
unary observations, classifier rows, bundle ledgers, membership rows, and
$\hsame$ comparisons required on each overlap.

Next stabilize the seal classifiers on the three overlaps. By
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-pointwise-seal-stability}, the shared
consumed $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ finite ledgers force the $\RealUp$ seal classifier
rows induced from either side of each overlap to agree pointwise. These
agreements are the only identifications used by the triple source surface.

Now form the pairwise pasted faces. Apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-pointwise-seal-pasting} to each of the
three pairwise unions. Each application exports only finite StreamName rows,
the shared dyadic ledger rows, and the pointwise RegSeqRat handoff rows, so no
quotient, host-index, selected-limit, completeness, metric, or bridge datum is
introduced on a face.

It remains to identify the two possible ways of reading the three faces as a
single surface. Use
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-pasted-seal-associativity}. The left and
right iterated pastings classify each member of
$\mathcal{W}_0 \cup \mathcal{W}_1 \cup \mathcal{W}_2$ by the same
$\RealUp$ seal row because they read the same pairwise source rows and the
same dyadic ledger rows.

Finally exhaust the resulting surface. Apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-pointwise-handoff-exhaustion} on each
window and on each pairwise pasted face, and use
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-regseq-obligation-separation} to keep downstream
and ambient rows outside the source. Every exported row is therefore a row
from one of the three StreamName finite windows, a shared dyadic ledger row,
or a pointwise RegSeqRat handoff row. The listed quotient, host-index,
selected-limit, completeness, metric-completeness, and bridge rows have no
carrier coordinate in the triple source surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName RegSeqRat Real finite-cover descent exactness]
\label{thm:streamname-regseqrat-real-finite-cover-descent-exactness}
Let $\{\mathcal{W}_i\}_{i\in I}$ be a finite family of certified
$\StreamNameUp$ rational windows. Suppose every pairwise certified overlap
has the unique source surface of
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-regseq-overlap-unique-source}, and suppose the
triple overlaps satisfy the cocycle compatibility witnessed by
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-real-triple-overlap-pasting}. Then the
induced $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoffs and the finite-window $\RealUp$ seal rows
descend to one cover-indexed finite surface.

The descended surface is assembled only from the displayed $\BMark$ and
$\BHist$ observations, selected $\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$ rows,
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ ledger rows, $\mathsf{InBundle}$ membership rows,
displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ transports, consumed $\DyadicRatCoreUp$
ledger rows, $\Pkg$ provenance, local $\NameCert$ rows, induced
$\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff rows, and finite-window $\RealUp$ seal rows.
Conversely, every row of that descended surface restricts on some certified
window or certified overlap to exactly one local $\StreamNameUp$ row, one
$\RegSeqRatUp$ dyadic-ledger row, or one finite-window $\RealUp$ seal row.

No row of the construction is a quotient stream, host index equality, selected
limit, countable-choice selector, metric-completeness datum, or ambient
$\RealUp$ completeness datum.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Proceed by finite induction on the cover index set $I$. For a singleton
cover, the claim is exactly
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-pointwise-handoff-exhaustion}: the visible
surface is exhausted by the local StreamName rows, the consumed dyadic ledger,
and the finite RealUp seal rows, with no quotient, host, choice, or
completeness coordinate.

For the induction step, assume a descended finite surface has already been
assembled over a finite subcover and adjoin one further certified window
$\mathcal{W}$. On each certified overlap with the existing subcover, apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-regseq-overlap-unique-source} to identify the
StreamName source rows, then apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-pointwise-seal-pasting} to paste the
induced $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff rows and finite-window $\RealUp$ seal rows
across that common source.

The result is independent of the order in which the new overlap faces are
adjoined. Indeed, whenever three windows meet, the two possible iterated
pastings have the same seal classifier surface by
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-real-triple-overlap-pasting}. Thus the
finite induction produces one cover-indexed surface rather than an
order-indexed family of surfaces.

It remains to read the rows of the descended surface back to local data. Use
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-regseq-obligation-separation} to keep downstream
regularity, seal, quotient, host, choice, and completeness data outside the
StreamName source. Then use
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-pointwise-handoff-exhaustion} on every
window and overlap face. Each exported row is therefore a local StreamName
row, a consumed RegSeqRat dyadic-ledger row, or a finite-window RealUp seal
row on a certified member of the cover, as required.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName RegSeqRat Real finite-cover descent support minimality]
\label{thm:streamname-regseqrat-real-finite-cover-descent-support-minimality}
Let $\{\mathcal{W}_i\}_{i\in I}$ be a finite family of certified
$\StreamNameUp$ rational windows satisfying the hypotheses of
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-real-finite-cover-descent-exactness}. For
the descended finite surface produced there, none of the displayed support
rows can be deleted while presenting the same descended surface.

More precisely, deleting any local $\StreamNameUp$ row displayed on a cover
member, any consumed $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger row on an overlap, any
$\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row induced from such a ledger, any finite-window
$\RealUp$ seal row, or any shared $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, or
$\NameCert$ support row prevents the same cover-indexed finite surface from
being presented. Quotient streams, host index equalities, selected limits,
countable-choice selectors, metric-completeness rows, bridge rows, and ambient
$\RealUp$ completeness rows cannot replace the deleted support.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First restrict the alleged presentation of the same descended surface to a
cover member containing the deleted local row. By
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-real-finite-cover-descent-exactness}, every
row of the descended surface restricts on that member or on one of its
certified overlaps to a unique local $\StreamNameUp$ row, consumed
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger row, induced $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row, or
finite-window $\RealUp$ seal row. Thus the restricted presentation must
recover exactly the deleted row if the descended surface is unchanged.

For a deleted overlap ledger or handoff row, restrict instead to an overlap
face. The pairwise source is pinned by the unique-source hypotheses used in
the descent theorem, and the compatibility of threefold restrictions is the
one supplied by
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-real-triple-overlap-pasting}. Hence two
presentations of the same finite surface must read the same shared
$\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support rows on the
overlap; omitting one of them changes the overlap face rather than merely its
description.

Next apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-pointwise-handoff-exhaustion} on the
affected window or overlap. The theorem exhausts the visible handoff by the
StreamName source rows, the consumed dyadic ledger, and the finite seal rows,
so a missing displayed row has no second source inside the handoff surface.

Finally use \autoref{thm:streamname-real-regseq-obligation-separation}. It
keeps quotient streams, host index equalities, selected limits,
countable-choice selectors, metric-completeness data, bridge rows, and
ambient $\RealUp$ completeness outside the StreamName source and its
finite-window RegSeqRat and Real faces. Those downstream or ambient rows
therefore cannot substitute for the deleted support row, and the same
descended finite surface cannot be presented after the deletion.
\end{proof}
