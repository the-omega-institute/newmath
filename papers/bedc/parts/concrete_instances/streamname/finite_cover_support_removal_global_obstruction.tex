\section{StreamName Finite-Cover Support Removal Global Obstruction}
\label{sec:streamname-finite-cover-support-removal-global-obstruction}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName finite-cover support removal global obstruction]
\label{thm:streamname-finite-cover-support-removal-global-obstruction}
Let $\{Q_i\}_{i \in I}$ be an accepted finite cover of current Phase
$\RealUp$ and Completion terminal $\StreamNameUp$ request sections feeding the
DyadicRatCore--RegSeqRat--Real four-face route. Suppose the accepted overlaps
display the same $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row, the same
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger row, the same $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row, the same
$\RealUp$ seal-visible source, and the same $\BHist$, componentwise $\hsame$,
$\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local \NameCert{} support rows. If one displayed local
support row is deleted from any member $Q_j$, then the cover-descent terminal
support packet cannot present the same $\RealUp$ seal-visible source.

The obstruction is global over the finite cover: quotient stream equality,
selected limits, countable choice, metric-completeness data, aggregate
formalstatus rows, bridge rows, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, or a replacement
regularity selector cannot supply the deleted row or restore the same
cover-descent source.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First restrict to the member where the deletion occurs. The local removal
witness theorem
\autoref{thm:streamname-terminal-support-removal-witness} says that deleting a
displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, or local \NameCert{} row from
the accepted terminal packet loses the carrier verdict, classifier verdict, or
consumer-facing terminal route of that local section.

Next descend through the finite cover. Apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-finite-cover-terminal-support-minimality}. The
cover-descent terminal packet is accepted exactly on the common
$\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ faces
together with the displayed support rows on the accepted overlaps. Therefore a
local deletion in $Q_j$ is not confined to a private presentation choice; it
removes one coordinate of the cover support itself.

Now localize the resulting cover defect. By
\autoref{thm:streamname-finite-cover-terminal-support-defect-localization}, a
failure of the cover-descent carrier verdict, classifier verdict, or terminal
route is witnessed on one accepted local section or accepted overlap, and the
witness lies on a displayed face or support row. In the present case the
witness is the deleted local support row, so the cover cannot still present the
same $\RealUp$ seal-visible source.

Finally rule out hidden replacement data. The selector nonchoice theorem
\autoref{thm:streamname-finite-window-regularity-selector-nonchoice} prevents
the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regularity row from re-entering as a hidden selector, host
function, countable-choice witness, quotient representative, selected limit,
ambient completeness coordinate, or independent completed-real carrier.
Together with the exclusion clauses in the finite-cover minimality and defect
localization theorems, this leaves no quotient equality, limit, choice,
metric-completeness datum, aggregate formalstatus row, bridge row, ambient
$\RealUp$ completeness principle, or replacement selector able to supply the
missing row.
\end{proof}
