By the endpoint correspondence of \autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-dyadic-real-endpoint-correspondence}, the same finite window must pass through the $\mathsf{RegSeqRat}^{\uparrow}$ row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the $\RealUp$ seal boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The exactness claim is negative as well as positive. The preceding two theorems give no coordinate for quotient stream equality, selected host limits, countable-choice selectors, ambient completeness, or bridge rows; the three cited sibling chapters provide only their named finite certificate surfaces. \begin{proof}

The exclusions are inherited coordinatewise. \label{thm:streamname-four-object-exit-support-minimality}

It remains to justify the common certificate faces. Apply \autoref{thm:streamname-reindex-support-row-necessity}: any reindexed support row that can be read by the exit route must carry the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ surface. Removing one of those faces leaves at most a source row, observation row, regularity row, or seal row in isolation.

Combining these two requirements gives minimality. The four object faces provide exactly the source, observation, regularity, and seal components of the public exit, while the five certificate faces provide the common finite support on which those components are read together. If any displayed face is absent, one component of the public exit or its shared support is absent, so the remaining data describes only the corresponding partial interface.
\end{proof}
