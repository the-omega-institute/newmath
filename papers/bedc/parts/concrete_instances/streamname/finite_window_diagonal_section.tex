\begin{theorem}[StreamName finite-window diagonal section]
\label{thm:streamname-finite-window-diagonal-section}
Every finite diagonal section read by a $\RegSeqRatUp$ consumer and then by a $\RealUp$ seal factors through the same $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window request. The displayed route is the finite window row, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ budget row, the $\RealUp$ seal row, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ rows. The section exports no quotient stream equality, quotient equality of Cauchy presentations, selected limit, host real equality, countable-choice row, metric-completeness datum, or ambient completion coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the finite-window budget handoff of \autoref{thm:streamname-real-regseq-finite-window-budget-handoff}. It places every $\RegSeqRatUp$ regularity row, dyadic tolerance row, and $\RealUp$ seal row behind the same displayed StreamName finite observation-budget surface.

Next apply the finite-request section packet of \autoref{thm:streamname-real-endpoint-finite-request-section} and \autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-window-budget-section}. These results package the endpoint, readback, budget, StreamName window, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ rows as one finite request section rather than as separate diagonal sources.

Use the RegSeqRat handoff of \autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-real-seal-handoff}. The diagonal read reaches the $\RealUp$ seal only after the finite StreamName window and the RegSeqRat readback have been transported by $\hsame$ and replayed by $\Cont$ through the displayed support rows.

Finally apply the Real seal non-escape boundary of \autoref{thm:streamname-real-seal-request-section-nonescape} and the finite request seal lock of \autoref{thm:streamname-finite-request-section-real-seal-lock}. They exclude quotient streams, quotient Cauchy-presentation equality, selected limits, host real equality, countable choice, metric completeness, and ambient completion from the route. Thus the diagonal section is exactly the finite StreamName request transported and replayed through the listed BEDC rows.
\end{proof}
