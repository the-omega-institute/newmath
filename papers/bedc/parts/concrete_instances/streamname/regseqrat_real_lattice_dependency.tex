\begin{theorem}[StreamName RegSeqRat Real lattice dependency]
\label{thm:streamname-regseqrat-real-lattice-dependency}
Every accepted $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window packet consumed by $\RegSeqRatUp$ and then by $\RealUp$ factors through the same visible sibling route. The route begins with the StreamName finite-window rows, passes through the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and ends at the $\RealUp$ seal rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. Along the entire route the only shared carrier coordinates are the displayed $\BHist$ observations, the componentwise $\hsame$ transports, the finite $\Cont$ continuation rows, the $\Pkg$ provenance rows, and the local $\NameCert$ rows naming the consumed packets.

The dependency exports no quotient stream, no host real equality, no selected limit, and no ambient completeness principle. In particular, the $\RealUp$ seal is read only as the downstream seal surface reached through the accepted finite StreamName and RegSeqRat packets.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the StreamName finite-window surface. By \autoref{thm:streamname-finite-window-real-handoff-coverage}, the handoff exposes the finite observation window, the pointwise $\RegSeqRatUp$ rows, the $\RealUp$ seal boundary, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ rows.

Next read the RegSeqRat sibling dependency. The pointwise handoff of \autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-pointwise-handoff} identifies the rows sent to $\RegSeqRatUp$ with the same StreamName carrier rows, finite-window classifier rows, certified unary observations, membership rows, and pointwise $\hsame$ comparisons. Thus the RegSeqRat readback surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert} is not a second carrier; it is the finite StreamName window read through the accepted pointwise packet.

Finally pass to the Real seal boundary. The factorization theorem \autoref{thm:streamname-real-seal-factorization-boundary} says that a finite-window $\RealUp$ seal read arising from the StreamName-to-RegSeqRat handoff factors through the StreamName point rows, accepted $\RegSeqRatUp$ seal rows, bundle and membership rows, unary observations, finite $\Cont$ ledger, displayed $\hsame$ transports, and $\Pkg$ provenance. This is exactly the seal-facing surface cited in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}.

Coordinate exhaustion gives the exclusion clause. None of the cited finite-window, pointwise, or seal-factorization rows contains a quotient stream, host real equality witness, selected limit, or ambient completeness coordinate, so composing them cannot export one.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName RegSeqRat Real dyadic-pullback seal determinacy]

Thus every seal-facing consumer reaches one finite pullback surface. The cited rows contain no coordinate for quotient streams, host real equality witnesses, selected limits, or ambient completeness data, so none of those objects can act as a second source for the $\RealUp$ seal.
\end{proof}
