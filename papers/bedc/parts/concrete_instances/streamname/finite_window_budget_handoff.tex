\section{StreamName finite-window budget handoff}
\label{sec:streamname-real-regseq-finite-window-budget-handoff}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName Real RegSeq finite-window budget handoff]
\label{thm:streamname-real-regseq-finite-window-budget-handoff}
Let $\mathcal W$ be an accepted finite $\StreamNameUp$ package consumed by a
$\RegSeqRatUp$ certificate, by a finite-window $\RealUp$ seal route, or by a
route that passes through a $\RegularCauchyTailBudgetUp$ or
$\ObservationBudgetLimiterUp$ packet. Then any consumer-visible regularity
row, dyadic tolerance row, or $\RealUp$ seal row must be downstream of the same
finite observation-budget surface:
$$
\mathsf{ProbeBundle},\quad \mathsf{InBundle},\quad
\mathsf{UnaryHistory},\quad \ObservationBudgetLimiterUp,\quad
\RegularCauchyTailBudgetUp.
$$
Thus the StreamName handoff first exposes the finite window, membership,
unary-history, budget-limiter, and tail-budget rows before any RegSeqRat
regularity, dyadic tolerance, or Real seal row is consumed.

The handoff exports no quotient stream, selected host limit, countable-choice
selector, or host real equality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with \autoref{thm:streamname-real-regseq-finite-window-handoff}. The
public StreamName package exports only selected rational-history classifier
rows, the finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ ledger, $\mathsf{InBundle}$ membership
rows, and $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ observation rows to a $\RegSeqRatUp$ or
finite-window $\RealUp$ consumer.

Next apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-real-regseq-obligation-separation}. It places
regularity and dyadic-tolerance rows on the $\RegSeqRatUp$ side and the seal
classifier on the $\RealUp$ side, so none of those rows can be consumed as
StreamName source data.

Now route the same finite observation surface through the sibling budget
chapters. The limiter factorization
\autoref{thm:observation-budget-limiter-cauchy-tail-budget-factorization}
forces any $\RegularCauchyTailBudgetUp$ use of the limiter row through the
request-budget-cap service triad before tail windows, dyadic observations,
readback, and seal rows are served.

Finally use
\autoref{thm:regular-cauchy-tail-budget-observation-gate}. It orders the
regular source, tail threshold, observation limiter, finite StreamName window,
dyadic ledger, RegSeqRat readback, and Real seal as one finite route. Since
the cited StreamName, limiter, and tail-budget surfaces contain no coordinates
for quotient streams, selected host limits, countable choice, or host real
equality, those objects cannot be alternate sources for the handoff.
\end{proof}
