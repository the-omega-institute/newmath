\begin{theorem}[StreamName finite-window reindex determinacy]
\label{thm:streamname-window-reindex-determinacy}
Let $W_0$ and $W_1$ be accepted $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window packets. Suppose their reindex maps are $\hsame$-compatible, their certified endpoint rows are identical, and their displayed $\BHist$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support rows transport componentwise across the same reindex route. Then $W_0$ and $W_1$ determine the same public finite observation section.

The determinacy statement is internal to the displayed StreamName packet. It does not use quotient stream equality, host sequence equality, selected limits, countable choice, or a completion row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First read both packets through the transported finite-window endpoint package of \autoref{thm:rat-stream-name-finite-window-transported-selected-full-endpoint-package}. The shared endpoint rows and the $\hsame$-compatible reindex route place both packets on the same finite endpoint surface.

Next apply successor projection determinacy, \autoref{thm:streamname-successor-window-projection-determinacy}. It prevents the reindex route from creating two public successor projections once endpoint rows and support rows have been identified.

Finally use terminal window fusion, \autoref{thm:streamname-real-completion-terminal-window-fusion}. The fused terminal packet has no extra coordinate beyond the finite window, endpoint data, and displayed support rows, so both accepted packets export the same public finite observation section.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName public reindexing identity law]
\label{thm:streamname-public-reindexing-identity-law}
For every public $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window packet over a
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, the certified identity reindexer preserves the
displayed $\mathsf{RatStreamNameFiniteWindowClassifier}$, $\UnaryHistory$
observation rows, $\mathsf{InBundle}$ membership rows, pointwise rational
classifier rows, $\hsame$ comparisons, $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and
local $\NameCert$ rows. The resulting public finite observation section is the
same section read through the identity route.

This identity law is a statement about the public finite-window packet only.
It does not introduce quotient stream equality, host sequence equality,
selected limits, countable choice, a completion row, or a standard bridge row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the packet through the public finite-window interface and the public
reindexing obligation of
\autoref{def:streamname-public-reindexing-obligation}. On an identity
reindexer, every certified $\UnaryHistory$ observation remains the displayed
observation, and every $\mathsf{InBundle}$ row is read from the same
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$.

The pointwise rational classifier, $\hsame$ comparison, $\Cont$ route,
$\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ row therefore reappear as the same
displayed rows. No extra endpoint, quotient representative, or host-index
coordinate is available in the public obligation surface.

Apply \autoref{thm:streamname-window-reindex-determinacy} to the original
packet and to the identity-reindexed packet. The hypotheses are exactly the
shared endpoint rows, the identity $\hsame$ route, and the componentwise
agreement of the displayed support rows, so the exported public finite
observation section is unchanged.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName public reindexing associative law]
\label{thm:streamname-public-reindexing-associative-law}
Let $r$ and $s$ be composable certified unary reindexers displayed over public
$\StreamNameUp$ finite-window packets. The two-step packet obtained by
reindexing along $r$ and then along $s$ exposes the same public finite-window
section as the single packet obtained by the composite reindexer. The common
section has the same $\mathsf{RatStreamNameFiniteWindowClassifier}$,
$\UnaryHistory$ ledger, $\mathsf{InBundle}$ transport, pointwise rational
classifier rows, $\hsame$ comparisons, composed $\Cont$ route, displayed
$\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ rows.

The law is confined to the finite public surface. It does not claim quotient
stream equality, host sequence equality, selected limits, countable choice,
completion data, or a standard bridge row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply the public reindexing obligation of
\autoref{def:streamname-public-reindexing-obligation} to the $r$ leg and then
to the $s$ leg. The two applications list only the certified
$\UnaryHistory$ observations, $\mathsf{InBundle}$ membership rows, pointwise
rational classifier rows, $\hsame$ comparisons, $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$
provenance, and local naming rows displayed by the finite packets.

Next use the finite-window reindexing composition theorem,
\autoref{thm:streamname-finite-window-reindexing-composition}. It identifies
the two restricted finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packets with the packet obtained by
restricting once along the composite reindexer, with the classifier, endpoint,
$\hsame$, and finite ledger rows merely repacked over the target
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:streamname-window-reindex-determinacy} to the
two-step packet and the composite packet. Their reindex maps are
$\hsame$-compatible, their endpoint rows agree, and their displayed
$\BHist$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows transport componentwise, so the
exported public finite observation section is the same.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName reindex terminal coverage]
\label{thm:streamname-reindex-terminal-coverage}
A certified public $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window reindex route preserves exactly
the terminal four-face L10 exit route. Namely, if an accepted reindex route
transports the public finite window together with the displayed $\BHist$,
$\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support rows, then the transported
exit packet exposes the same $\StreamNameUp$ window, the same
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger, the same $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, and the same
$\RealUp$ seal as its terminal Phase Real and Completion route.

Conversely, any terminal four-face route transported by the identity certified
reindexer or by composable certified reindexers reads the same public finite
observation section. The coverage is confined to the finite route and excludes
quotient stream equality, host sequence equality, selected limits, countable
choice, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, metric completeness, and standard
bridge rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the public finite-window section. The determinacy theorem,
\autoref{thm:streamname-window-reindex-determinacy}, says that matching
endpoint rows, $\hsame$-compatible reindex maps, and componentwise transported
$\BHist$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows determine one public finite
observation section.

For the identity route, apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-public-reindexing-identity-law}. It keeps the displayed
finite window, unary-history ledger, bundle membership, rational classifier,
$\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ rows unchanged, so the
terminal packet is read from the same public section.

For composable certified routes, apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-public-reindexing-associative-law}. The two-step and
composite reindexings expose the same finite section, hence they present the
same support rows to the exit boundary.

Finally use \autoref{thm:streamname-open-phase-four-face-terminality} and
\autoref{thm:streamname-open-phase-four-face-exit-readback}. Terminality fixes
the four faces once the StreamName window, DyadicRatCore ledger, RegSeqRat
readback, RealUp seal, and support rows agree, while readback says every
terminal exit read factors through exactly those rows. Thus transported
identity and composable routes cover precisely the finite four-face exit route,
with no additional quotient, host, limit, choice, completeness, or bridge
coordinate.
\end{proof}
