It sits beside the Baire-space surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bairespace-namecert}, the choice-sequence rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-choice-sequence-namecert}, the spread-law route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-spread-law-namecert}, and the finite stream-window surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}. \begin{definition}[Brouwer continuity principle carrier]
\label{def:brouwer-continuity-principle-carrier}
A $\BrouwerContinuityPrincipleUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(B,Q,S,W,R,E,M,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $B$ is the $\BaireSpaceUp$ source row, exposing only finite inspected prefixes of the input sequence;
\item $Q$ is the $\ChoiceSequenceUp$ row, recording the displayed choice-sequence presentation used by the functional read;
\item $S$ is the $\SpreadLawUp$ row, naming the spread law that admits the finite prefixes consumed by the packet;
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row, storing the requested prefix length, inspected stream cells, and pullback route to the source rows;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row for rational observations made from the accepted finite prefix;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached from the same finite-window and rational-readback data;
\item $M$ is the finite modulus row, stating which prefix length is sufficient for the displayed output read;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $B,Q,S,W,R,E,M$ and the local naming row;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ replay by which consumers read $B,Q,S,W,R,E,M,H,P,N$ in that order;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BaireSpaceUp$, $\ChoiceSequenceUp$, $\SpreadLawUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\BrouwerContinuityPrincipleUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only through the displayed finite prefix, modulus, readback, seal, transport, continuation, package, and local NameCert rows.
\end{definition}

\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Brouwer continuity principle finite-prefix modulus]
\label{thm:brouwer-continuity-principle-finite-prefix-modulus}
For every accepted $\BrouwerContinuityPrincipleUp$ carrier, each output read from a Baire or choice-sequence input factors through the finite route
$$
B,Q,S\longmapsto W\longmapsto M\longmapsto R\longmapsto E\longmapsto H\longmapsto C\longmapsto P\longmapsto N.
$$
Thus the consumer receives a displayed prefix length and the corresponding inspected $\StreamNameUp$ window before it reads the $\RegSeqRatUp$ and $\RealUp$ rows. The route exports no host functional continuity theorem, no choice-selected modulus, no quotient stream equality, no arbitrary bar induction principle, and no ambient $\RealUp$ completeness claim.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

Next read the finite-window and modulus rows $W,M$. They name the inspected prefix and the prefix length sufficient for the displayed output read, so the rational and real observations cannot be consumed before the finite prefix is exposed.

Finally route the output through $R,E,H,C,P,N$. Componentwise transport and continuation replay preserve the same finite-prefix packet for downstream consumers, while provenance records the sibling Baire-space, choice-sequence, spread-law, stream-name, regular-rational, and real rows. Since no refused object is a carrier coordinate, the output read remains a finite-prefix NameCert route.
\end{proof}
