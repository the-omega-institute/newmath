\begin{theorem}[Concrete unary multiplication MulUp certificate spine]
\label{thm:unary-multiplication-mulup-certificate}
The concrete unary multiplication candidate for $\mathsf{MulUp}$ uses carrier
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ over $\mathsf{BHist}$, classifier $\hsame$, operation
relation $\mathsf{NatMul}$, and unit $\Eone(\emp)$. Its certificate spine is the
following finite law package.
\begin{enumerate}
\item Totality: for unary histories $d$ and $q$, there is a unary $n$ with
$\mathsf{NatMul}(d,q,n)$.
\item Result closure: every $\mathsf{NatMul}(d,q,n)$ with unary $d$ has unary
result $n$.
\item Functionality: any two results of $\mathsf{NatMul}(d,q,-)$ for unary
inputs are $\hsame$.
\item Unit laws: $\mathsf{NatMul}(\Eone(\emp),q,n)$ gives $n\hsame q$, and
$\mathsf{NatMul}(d,\Eone(\emp),n)$ gives $n\hsame d$.
\item Commutativity: products $\mathsf{NatMul}(d,q,n)$ and
$\mathsf{NatMul}(q,d,m)$ over unary inputs have $n\hsame m$.
\item Associativity: for unary $a$, $b$, and $c$, products
$\mathsf{NatMul}(a,b,ab)$, $\mathsf{NatMul}(ab,c,\ell)$,
$\mathsf{NatMul}(b,c,bc)$, and $\mathsf{NatMul}(a,bc,r)$ have
$\ell\hsame r$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier, classifier, operation, and unit are the concrete objects already
used by $\mathsf{NatMul}$. Totality, result closure, functionality, the two unit
laws, and commutativity are exactly the checked unary multiplication laws in
this section.

For associativity, induct on the middle multiplication spine. The base case is
the zero multiplier. The successor case rewrites the right-associated product
as an appended multiplier product, applies
\autoref{thm:natmul-append-continuation-closure}, and then uses functionality
to compare it with the displayed left-associated result.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Unary multiplication preserves unary-prefix order]
\label{thm:natmul-unary-prefix-order-monotone}
Let $d,q,q',n,n':\Hist$ be unary histories. If
$\mathsf{PreorderPrefixLE}(q,q')$, $\mathsf{NatMul}(d,q,n)$, and
$\mathsf{NatMul}(d,q',n')$, then $\mathsf{PreorderPrefixLE}(n,n')$. The
displayed prefix witness from $n$ to $n'$ is built from the $\mathsf{Cont}$
ledger for the extra unary tail of $q'$ over $q$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Invert the $\mathsf{PreorderPrefixLE}(q,q')$ witness through the
$\OrderUp$ unary-prefix export. It presents a unary tail $t$ and a continuation
ledger with $q'$ read as the unary append of $q$ and $t$.

Apply \autoref{thm:natmul-append-continuation-closure} to the multiplication
row $\mathsf{NatMul}(d,q,n)$ and to that extra tail ledger. This constructs a
product row for $d$ against the displayed append presentation of $q'$.

Use $\mathsf{NatMul}$ functionality to compare the constructed product with
the given result $n'$. The comparison transports the appended-product endpoint
through $\hsame$ without changing the consumed unary tail.

Finally apply the unary-prefix append-tail classifier for
$\mathsf{PreorderPrefixLE}$. The prefix from $n$ to the constructed appended
product is the continuation ledger supplied by multiplication; transporting it
through the final $\hsame$ comparison gives the required
$\mathsf{PreorderPrefixLE}(n,n')$ witness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[NatMul right additive distribution surface]
\label{thm:natmul-additive-right-distributivity-surface}
Let $d,w,q,n,e,r:\Hist$ be unary histories. If $\mathsf{NatMul}(d,w,n)$,
$\mathsf{NatMul}(d,q,e)$, and the $\AddUp$ continuation row presents the
unary sum of $w$ and $q$ as $\mathsf{append}(w,q)$ with $\mathsf{Cont}(n,e,r)$,
then $\mathsf{NatMul}(d,\mathsf{append}(w,q),r)$. Consequently the concrete
$\MulUp$ operation distributes over the right-hand $\AddUp$ continuation
presentation of unary addition.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The two multiplication rows supply the factor products for $d$ against $w$
and $q$. Apply \autoref{thm:natmul-append-continuation-closure} to these
factor rows and to the displayed continuation $\mathsf{Cont}(n,e,r)$; it
constructs the product row for $d$ against the appended multiplier
$\mathsf{append}(w,q)$ with endpoint $r$.

The $\AddUp$ concrete NameCert surface reads unary addition through the same
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ carrier, continuation ledger, and append
presentation. Therefore the continuation row that displays the sum of $w$ and
$q$ is exactly the additive boundary required by the multiplication closure
theorem.

Repack the constructed $\mathsf{NatMul}$ row together with that $\AddUp$
boundary. The resulting surface is the displayed $\MulUp/\AddUp$ phase
interaction: multiplication by the fixed unary history $d$ sends the
right-hand additive presentation $w+q$ to the continuation presentation of the
two products.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[NatMul left additive distribution surface]
\label{thm:natmul-additive-left-distributivity-surface}
Let $d,w,q,n,e,r:\Hist$ be unary histories. If $\mathsf{NatMul}(w,d,n)$,
$\mathsf{NatMul}(q,d,e)$, and the $\AddUp$ continuation row presents the
unary sum of $w$ and $q$ as $\mathsf{append}(w,q)$ with $\mathsf{Cont}(n,e,r)$,
then $\mathsf{NatMul}(\mathsf{append}(w,q),d,r)$. Consequently the concrete
$\MulUp$ operation distributes over the left-hand $\AddUp$ continuation
presentation of unary addition.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use $\mathsf{NatMul}$ commutativity on the two displayed left-factor products.
It supplies right-factor product presentations for multiplication by the fixed
unary history $d$, with endpoints identified with $n$ and $e$ by $\hsame$.

Apply \autoref{thm:natmul-additive-right-distributivity-surface} to those
right-factor presentations and to the transported continuation ledger. This
constructs a product row whose multiplier is $\mathsf{append}(w,q)$ and whose
endpoint is identified with $r$.

Finally apply $\mathsf{NatMul}$ commutativity to the constructed row. The
result is a product with multiplicand $\mathsf{append}(w,q)$ and multiplier
$d$; functionality transports the endpoint comparison back along the original
$\Cont(n,e,r)$ boundary, yielding $\mathsf{NatMul}(\mathsf{append}(w,q),d,r)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Unary multiplication two-sided unit exactness]

$$
\mathsf{NatMul}(d,\Eone(\emp),n)\Longleftrightarrow \hsame(n,d).

\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Unit multiplication result forces unit factors]
\label{thm:natmul-unit-result-factors-unit}
If a unary multiplication witness returns the unit history, then both the
multiplicand and the multiplier are the unit history:
$$
\mathsf{NatMul}(d,q,\Eone(\emp))
\Longrightarrow
\HSame(d,\Eone(\emp))\wedge \HSame(q,\Eone(\emp)).

\begin{proof}

has empty result. Empty-result inversion then forces that tail and the
predecessor product to be empty, so the multiplier tail is empty by the
nonempty-multiplicand empty-result exactness theorem.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MulUp unary product order lower-bound package]
\label{thm:mulup-unary-product-order-lower-bound-package}
Let $d$, $q$, and $n$ be unary histories. If
$\mathsf{NatMul}(d,\Eone(q),n)$, then the $\mathsf{MulUp}$ product row exports
the following $\OrderUp$ boundary package over the unary-prefix order of
\autoref{thm:unary-prefix-orderup-phase-exit-package}:
\begin{enumerate}
\item the carrier rows $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(d)$,
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(\Eone(q))$, and $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(n)$;
\item the unary-prefix comparison $d\le_{\mathsf{u}}n$;
\item the endpoint classifier boundary $\rho_{\mathsf{u}}$ used by
$\OrderUp$ to read equality of endpoints under two-sided
$\le_{\mathsf{u}}$ comparison.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First expose the multiplication package by
\autoref{thm:unary-multiplication-mulup-semantic-exit-package}. Its carrier row
gives the displayed unary histories for $d$ and $\Eone(q)$, and its result
closure gives $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(n)$ from the supplied
$\mathsf{NatMul}(d,\Eone(q),n)$ witness.

The empty-tail boundary $q=\emp$ is the right-unit product. The unit law in the
same $\mathsf{MulUp}$ package reads the product result back as $n\hsame d$; the
$\OrderUp$ package of \autoref{thm:unary-prefix-orderup-phase-exit-package} then
uses reflexivity and endpoint classifier transport to obtain
$d\le_{\mathsf{u}}n$.

For a successor tail, apply
\autoref{thm:natmul-succ-multiplicand-append-multiplier-total} to the
predecessor multiplication row. The appended-multiplier factor lemmas
\autoref{lem:nat-mul-append-multiplier-left-factor} and
\autoref{lem:nat-mul-append-multiplier-right-factor} recover the left product
and right product from the displayed continuation product. Then
\autoref{thm:natmul-append-multiplier-result-deterministic} identifies the
displayed result with the append of the predecessor product and the extra
multiplicand copy.

This append display is exactly the unary-prefix witness that the original
multiplicand lies below the product. Project the relation row and classifier
row from \autoref{thm:unary-prefix-orderup-phase-exit-package}: the relation
row records $d\le_{\mathsf{u}}n$, and the classifier row records
$\rho_{\mathsf{u}}$ as the endpoint boundary used whenever two-sided
$\le_{\mathsf{u}}$ comparison collapses the endpoints.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MulUp AddUp OrderUp phase interaction package]
\label{thm:mulup-add-order-phase-interaction-package}
For unary histories $d,w,q,n,e,r$, the concrete unary $\MulUp$ package exports
a single consumer package combining the following rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item the right additive distribution row over the $\AddUp$ continuation
presentation, sending $\mathsf{NatMul}(d,w,n)$, $\mathsf{NatMul}(d,q,e)$,
and $\Cont(n,e,r)$ to $\mathsf{NatMul}(d,\mathsf{append}(w,q),r)$;
\item the left additive distribution row over the same $\AddUp$ continuation
presentation, sending $\mathsf{NatMul}(w,d,n)$, $\mathsf{NatMul}(q,d,e)$,
and $\Cont(n,e,r)$ to $\mathsf{NatMul}(\mathsf{append}(w,q),d,r)$;
\item the unary-prefix $\OrderUp$ lower-bound row for successor products,
which reads $\mathsf{NatMul}(d,\Eone(q),n)$ as the boundary
$\mathsf{PreorderPrefixLE}(d,n)$ together with the endpoint classifier row.
\end{enumerate}
The shared consumer objects are $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$,
$\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{NatMul}$, and $\mathsf{PreorderPrefixLE}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The right additive row is exactly
\autoref{thm:natmul-additive-right-distributivity-surface}. It uses the
$\AddUp$ continuation presentation through the same $\Cont(n,e,r)$ ledger and
keeps the product predicate inside $\mathsf{NatMul}$.

The left additive row is
\autoref{thm:natmul-additive-left-distributivity-surface}. Its proof transports
through commutativity of $\mathsf{NatMul}$ and returns to the left-hand
presentation without changing the underlying $\mathsf{BHist}$ carrier,
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ predicate, $\hsame$ classifier, or continuation row.

The order row is supplied by
\autoref{thm:mulup-unary-product-order-lower-bound-package}. It repacks the
successor-product multiplication witness into the unary-prefix relation
$\mathsf{PreorderPrefixLE}$ and the $\OrderUp$ endpoint classifier. Since all
three rows consume the displayed $\mathsf{NatMul}$ and $\Cont$ ledgers over the
same unary histories, the package combines them without introducing another
carrier or classifier.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[Divisibility relation]
\label{def:nat-divides}
For unary histories \(d, n\), the divisibility relation \(\mathsf{NatDivides}(d, n)\) holds when there exists a unary \(q\) with \(\mathsf{NatMul}(d, q, n)\). The witness \(q\) is the explicit quotient.
\end{definition}
\leandef{BEDC.Derived.PrimeUp.NatDivides}

\begin{theorem}[Divisibility result unary carrier]
\label{thm:nat-divides-result-unary}
If $\mathsf{NatDivides}(d,n)$ holds, then $n$ is a unary history.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the quotient witness. The multiplication witness exposes unary $d$,
and multiplication result closure then proves the result history $n$ unary.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.PrimeUp.NatDivides\_result\_unary}

\begin{lemma}[Divisibility result cases]
\label{lem:nat-divides-result-cases}
Every witness of \(\mathsf{NatDivides}(d,n)\) is either an empty-result
witness, with \(n\hsame\emp\) and \(d\) unary, or a successor-product witness:
there is a predecessor \(n_0\) with \(\mathsf{NatDivides}(d,n_0)\) and
\(\Cont(n_0,d,n)\).
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the explicit quotient in the divisibility witness. If its
\(\mathsf{NatMul}\) witness is zero, the result is empty and the multiplicand
field gives unary \(d\). If it is a successor witness, unary inversion on the
quotient supplies a unary predecessor quotient, and the predecessor
multiplication witness repackages as \(\mathsf{NatDivides}(d,n_0)\).
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.PrimeUp.NatDivides\_result\_cases}

\begin{theorem}[Divisibility zero-headed component impossibility]
\label{thm:nat-divides-zero-headed-component-absurd}
If \(\mathsf{NatDivides}(d,n)\) holds, then neither the divisor \(d\) nor the
dividend \(n\) can be a zero-headed history.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the explicit quotient \(q\) in the divisibility witness. The underlying
\(\mathsf{NatMul}(d,q,n)\) witness rules out zero-headed multiplicand,
multiplier, and result components. Applying that impossibility to the divisor
or dividend component gives the claim.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.PrimeUp.NatDivides\_zero\_headed\_component\_absurd}

\begin{lemma}[Empty left divisibility has empty result]
\label{lem:natdivides-empty-left-result-empty}
If \(\mathsf{NatDivides}(\emp,n)\), then \(n\hsame\emp\).
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the quotient witness for divisibility. Its multiplication field is a
witness of \(\mathsf{NatMul}(\emp,q,n)\), so empty-left multiplication
exactness identifies the result \(n\) with \(\emp\).
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.PrimeUp.NatDivides\_empty\_left\_result\_empty}

\begin{theorem}[Successor-result divisors have positive shape]
\label{thm:natdivides-successor-result-divisor-positive-shape}
If \(\mathsf{NatDivides}(d,\Eone(n))\), then \(d\) has positive unary shape:
there is a unary history \(t\) with \(d\hsame\Eone(t)\).
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the divisibility witness and read the unaryness of \(d\) from its
multiplication field. If \(d\) were empty, empty-left divisibility would force
\(\Eone(n)\hsame\emp\), contradicting successor non-emptiness. The \(e0\)
case is excluded by unary-history inversion. Hence \(d\) is \(e1\)-headed, and
the unary tail is obtained by unary inversion.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.PrimeUp.NatDivides\_successor\_result\_divisor\_positive\_shape}

\begin{theorem}[Nonempty-result divisors have positive shape]
\label{thm:nat-divides-nonempty-result-divisor-positive-shape}
If \(\mathsf{NatDivides}(d,n)\), \(n\) is unary, and
\(n\not\hsame\emp\), then \(d\) has positive unary shape: there is a unary
history \(t\) with \(d\hsame\Eone(t)\).
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the quotient witness in \(\mathsf{NatDivides}(d,n)\) and read the
unaryness of \(d\) from its multiplication field. If \(d\hsame\emp\), then
empty-left divisibility forces \(n\hsame\emp\), contradicting the displayed
nonempty result. The \(e0\)-headed case is excluded by unary-history inversion,
so the divisor is \(e1\)-headed and unary inversion supplies the tail carrier.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.PrimeUp.NatDivides\_nonempty\_result\_divisor\_positive\_shape}

\begin{theorem}[Empty-left divisibility exactness]
\label{thm:nat-divides-empty-left-iff}
For every history $n$, divisibility by the empty unary history holds exactly
when the result is empty:
$$
\mathsf{NatDivides}(\emp,n)\Longleftrightarrow \hsame(n,\emp).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The forward implication is empty-left divisibility result exactness. For the
reverse implication, identify $n$ with $\emp$; then the empty quotient and the
zero multiplication constructor witness $\mathsf{NatDivides}(\emp,\emp)$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.PrimeUp.NatDivides\_empty\_left\_iff}

\begin{lemma}[Empty right divisibility is unary carrier]
\label{thm:natdivides-empty-right-iff}
For any history $d$, $\mathsf{NatDivides}(d,\emp)$ holds exactly when $d$ is a
unary history.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
In the forward direction, unpack the quotient witness; its multiplication
field carries the unary multiplicand field for $d$. In the reverse direction,
use the empty quotient and the zero constructor of $\mathsf{NatMul}$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.PrimeUp.NatDivides\_empty\_right\_iff}

\begin{theorem}[Unit-left divisibility is unary carrier]
\label{thm:natdivides-unit-left-iff}
For any history $n$, $\mathsf{NatDivides}(\Eone(\emp),n)$ holds exactly when
$n$ is a unary history.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the forward direction, unpack the quotient witness $q$. Multiplication by
the unit identifies the result with $q$, and unaryness transports across that
endpoint identification. Conversely, the unit-divisibility half of reflexivity
gives $\mathsf{NatDivides}(\Eone(\emp),n)$ for every unary $n$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.PrimeUp.NatDivides\_unit\_left\_iff}

\begin{theorem}[Unit-right divisibility exactness]
\label{thm:nat-divides-unit-right-iff}
For any history $d$, $\mathsf{NatDivides}(d,\Eone(\emp))$ holds exactly when
$d\hsame\Eone(\emp)$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
In the forward direction, unpack the quotient witness and apply unit-result
multiplication factor exactness: both factors of a product equal to
\(\Eone(\emp)\) are the unary unit. Conversely, identify \(d\) with the unary
unit and use reflexive divisibility for the unit history.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.PrimeUp.NatDivides\_unit\_right\_iff}

\begin{lemma}[Divisibility is reflexive on the unit and on every unary history]
\label{lem:divides-reflexive}
For every unary \(n\), \(\mathsf{NatDivides}(\Eone(\emp), n)\) and \(\mathsf{NatDivides}(n, n)\).
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
The unit case takes \(q = n\) in \(\mathsf{NatMul}(\Eone(\emp), n, n)\) by induction. The self-divisibility case takes \(q = \Eone(\emp)\) and uses the continuation unit at the empty quotient.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.PrimeUp.NatDivides\_reflexive}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.PrimeUp.NatDivides\_unit\_self\_reflexive}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.PrimeUp.NatDivides\_reflexive\_pair}

\begin{lemma}[Divisibility is transitive]
\label{lem:divides-transitive}
If \(\mathsf{NatDivides}(d, e)\) and \(\mathsf{NatDivides}(e, n)\), then \(\mathsf{NatDivides}(d, n)\).
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
From the witnessing quotients \(q_1\) with \(\mathsf{NatMul}(d, q_1, e)\) and \(q_2\) with \(\mathsf{NatMul}(e, q_2, n)\), the composite quotient is \(q_1 \cdot q_2\), itself constructed as a unary history by \autoref{lem:nat-mul-total}.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.PrimeUp.NatDivides\_transitive}

\begin{theorem}[Unary divisibility partial-order certificate]
\label{thm:nat-divides-partial-order-certificate}
On unary histories, $\mathsf{NatDivides}$ is reflexive and transitive, and it is
antisymmetric modulo $\hsame$: for unary histories $d$, $e$, and $n$,
$\mathsf{NatDivides}(d,d)$,
$\mathsf{NatDivides}(d,e) \wedge \mathsf{NatDivides}(e,n) \Rightarrow
\mathsf{NatDivides}(d,n)$, and
$\mathsf{NatDivides}(d,e) \wedge \mathsf{NatDivides}(e,d) \Rightarrow
d\hsame e$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The reflexive clause is \autoref{lem:divides-reflexive}, and the transitive
clause is \autoref{lem:divides-transitive}. The antisymmetric clause is
\autoref{thm:nat-divides-antisymmetry-hsame}.

This is a paper-level bundled certificate rather than a distinct Lean marker
site: the three checked laws are consumed together by bounded divisor search,
Euclid's lemma, and factorization uniqueness as the order-like interface for
unary divisibility.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MulUp divisibility-order consumer package]
\label{thm:mulup-divisibility-order-consumer-package}
For unary histories, $\mathsf{NatDivides}$ supplies the $\MulUp$ arithmetic
consumer package consisting of reflexivity, transitivity, antisymmetry modulo
$\hsame$, right-factor closure and left-multiplication closure through
$\mathsf{NatMul}$, and the unary-prefix lower-bound package for successor
products. Thus any $\MulUp$ consumer that reads only unary divisibility order,
ordinary product closure, and successor-product prefix lower bounds may cite a
single package boundary rather than reassembling those rows separately.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The order rows are exactly the reflexive and transitive laws of
\autoref{lem:divides-reflexive} and \autoref{lem:divides-transitive}, together
with the antisymmetry certificate of
\autoref{thm:nat-divides-antisymmetry-hsame}.

The multiplication-closure rows are supplied by
\autoref{thm:nat-divides-mul-right-closed} and
\autoref{thm:nat-divides-mul-left-closed}. The boundary row that connects
successor products with the unary-prefix order is
\autoref{thm:mulup-unary-product-order-lower-bound-package}. Collecting these
checked rows gives the stated $\MulUp$ consumer surface.
\end{proof}
