\begin{theorem}[MulUp semantic exit and divisibility-order phase package]
\label{thm:mulup-semantic-exit-and-divisibility-order-phase-package}
Over $\mathsf{BHist}$ and the carrier $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$, the semantic
exit theorem, semantic-exit audit row coverage, and divisibility-order consumer
package together give the full natural-arithmetic consumer surface for
$\MulUp$. The surface consists of the carrier $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$,
classifier $\hsame$, multiplication relation $\mathsf{NatMul}$, result
$\mathsf{SemanticNameCert}$, the two $\AddUp$ distributivity rows, the
$\OrderUp$ monotonicity row, and the unary divisibility reflexive, transitive,
and antisymmetric consumer rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:unary-multiplication-mulup-semantic-exit-package}. It fixes
the carrier, classifier, multiplication relation, unit rows, algebraic laws, and
result $\mathsf{SemanticNameCert}$ for unary multiplication over
$\mathsf{BHist}$.

Next apply \autoref{thm:mulup-semantic-exit-audit-row-coverage}. It adds the
$\AddUp$ distributivity rows and the $\OrderUp$ monotonicity row without leaving
the displayed unary-history carrier or the $\hsame$ classifier surface.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:mulup-divisibility-order-consumer-package}. Its
reflexive, transitive, and antisymmetric divisibility rows use the same
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ carrier, the same $\hsame$ comparison, the same $\Cont$
transport ledgers, and the same $\mathsf{NatMul}$ relation. Therefore the three
packages assemble as one Full Nat Arithmetic consumption point for $\MulUp$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MulUp Full Nat Arithmetic phase-exit boundary]
\label{thm:mulup-full-nat-arithmetic-phase-exit-boundary}
Over $\mathsf{BHist}$ and the carrier $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$, the package of
\autoref{thm:mulup-semantic-exit-and-divisibility-order-phase-package} is
exactly the $\MulUp$ L7 consumer boundary required by the Full Nat Arithmetic
phase. Its rows are the carrier $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$, classifier $\hsame$,
multiplication relation $\mathsf{NatMul}$, unit, totality, result closure,
functionality, unit laws, commutativity, associativity, the $\AddUp$
distributivity rows, the $\OrderUp$ monotonicity row, and the divisibility-order
consumer rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold the package theorem
\autoref{thm:mulup-semantic-exit-and-divisibility-order-phase-package}. Its
displayed surface already fixes the concrete carrier, classifier,
multiplication relation, semantic result object, additive rows, order row, and
divisibility-order rows over $\mathsf{BHist}$ and $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$.

The algebraic part comes from
\autoref{thm:unary-multiplication-mulup-semantic-exit-package}: it supplies the
unit, totality, result closure, functionality, unit laws, commutativity, and
associativity rows for $\mathsf{NatMul}$ on the same $\hsame$-classified unary
histories.

The additive and order-facing part is the row coverage recorded by
\autoref{thm:mulup-semantic-exit-audit-row-coverage} together with
\autoref{thm:mulup-divisibility-order-consumer-package}. These give the
$\AddUp$ distributivity rows, the $\OrderUp$ monotonicity row, and the
reflexive, transitive, and antisymmetric divisibility-order consumer rows
without changing the carrier or classifier.

Thus a later Full Nat Arithmetic consumer can read $\MulUp$ through this single
boundary. No host natural-number multiplication, quotient object, or
multiplication operation outside the unary-history package is used.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MulUp Full Nat Arithmetic hsame transport boundary]
\label{thm:mulup-full-nat-arithmetic-hsame-transport-boundary}
Let two unary-history presentations over $\mathsf{BHist}$ be related
componentwise by $\hsame$. Then the Full Nat Arithmetic phase-exit boundary of
\autoref{thm:mulup-full-nat-arithmetic-phase-exit-boundary} transports across
that comparison. The transported boundary has the same
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ carrier surface, the transported $\hsame$ classifier,
the transported $\mathsf{NatMul}$ relation, the same unit, the same $\AddUp$
distributivity rows, the same $\OrderUp$ monotonicity row, and the same
divisibility-order consumer rows, all exported through the unchanged
$\mathsf{BHist}$ surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the phase-exit boundary of
\autoref{thm:mulup-full-nat-arithmetic-phase-exit-boundary}. Its rows are
already expressed over $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$, $\hsame$,
$\mathsf{NatMul}$, the additive distribution rows, the order row, and the
divisibility-order consumer rows.

Transport the carrier and classifier first. The componentwise $\hsame$
comparison keeps each unary-history row inside $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ and
keeps every $\Cont$ ledger on the same displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ endpoints.
The functionality and transport clauses for $\mathsf{NatMul}$ then move the
multiplication row, unit row, and result row to the transported presentation.

Finally, repack the AddUp distribution rows, the OrderUp monotonicity row, and
the divisibility rows from the transported endpoints. These rows mention only
the already transported unary histories, $\Cont$ ledgers, $\hsame$
comparisons, and $\mathsf{NatMul}$ witnesses, so the consumer receives the
same phase-exit package and no new exported surface.
\end{proof}
