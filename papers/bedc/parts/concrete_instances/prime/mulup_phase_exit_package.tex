\begin{theorem}[MulUp successor cancellation boundary]
\label{thm:mulup-successor-cancellation-boundary}
Let $d$ and $q$ be unary histories, and let
$\mathsf{NatMul}(d,\Eone(q),n')$ be a product row over $\mathsf{BHist}$. Then
successor inversion produces a unary predecessor $n$ with
$\mathsf{NatMul}(d,q,n)$ and $\Cont(n,d,n')$. Moreover, if another unary
history $m$ satisfies $\mathsf{NatMul}(d,q,m)$ and $\Cont(m,d,n')$, then
$n\hsame m$. The cancellation reads only the predecessor product rows and the
displayed continuation witnesses; it does not add a second carrier, quotient
surface, or host multiplication row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply $\mathsf{BEDC.Derived.PrimeUp.NatMul\_succ\_inversion}$ to the displayed
successor product row. This gives the predecessor product $n$, the row
$\mathsf{NatMul}(d,q,n)$, and the final continuation ledger $\Cont(n,d,n')$.

Now take another predecessor $m$ with $\mathsf{NatMul}(d,q,m)$ and
$\Cont(m,d,n')$. The two predecessor products have the same unary multiplicand
$d$ and multiplier $q$, so
$\mathsf{BEDC.Derived.PrimeUp.NatMul\_functional}$ identifies their results by
$n\hsame m$.

The continuation rows are retained as ledger witnesses for the displayed
successor endpoint $n'$. They are not additional result carriers and play no
role in the comparison beyond recording that both predecessor rows append the
same visible copy of $d$ to reach the same endpoint.
\end{proof}
