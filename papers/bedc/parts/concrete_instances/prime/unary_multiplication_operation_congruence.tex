\begin{theorem}[Unary multiplication operation congruence]
\label{thm:unary-multiplication-operation-congruence}
Let $d$, $d'$, $q$, $q'$, $n$, and $n'$ be histories. If
$\mathsf{NatMul}(d,q,n)$, $\hsame(d,d')$, $\hsame(q,q')$, and
$\hsame(n,n')$, then $\mathsf{NatMul}(d',q',n')$.

Moreover, the transported multiplicand $d'$ and multiplier $q'$ are unary
histories, and every result $m$ of $\mathsf{NatMul}(d',q',m)$ is classified
with $n'$ by $\hsame(m,n')$. For the per-object certificate template, this
statement is the concrete $\mathsf{MulUp}$ stability-obligation row for the
operation relation under transported multiplicand, multiplier, and result
classifiers, with its formal target intended under
$\mathsf{BEDC.Derived.PrimeUp}$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.PrimeUp.NatMul\_operation\_congruence}
\begin{proof}
Apply the multiplicand transport theorem to the given product and
$\hsame(d,d')$. This gives unary evidence for $d'$ and a product
$\mathsf{NatMul}(d',q,n)$. Apply the multiplier transport theorem to that
product and $\hsame(q,q')$, producing unary evidence for $q'$ and a product
$\mathsf{NatMul}(d',q',n)$.

Apply the result transport theorem to this last product and $\hsame(n,n')$.
The result is the displayed product $\mathsf{NatMul}(d',q',n')$, with the
same unary multiplicand evidence already carried by the transported product
and the unary multiplier evidence obtained in the previous paragraph.

For the uniqueness clause, compare any other witness
$\mathsf{NatMul}(d',q',m)$ with the transported witness
$\mathsf{NatMul}(d',q',n')$ by \autoref{lem:nat-mul-functional}, using unary
evidence for the transported multiplicand $d'$. This yields $\hsame(m,n')$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MulUp repeated-addition Cont scope]
\label{thm:mulup-repeated-addition-cont-scope}
Every $\mathsf{NatMul}(d,q,n)$ row consumed by $\MulUp$ is scoped by unary
witnesses for $d$, $q$, and $n$, a finite $\Cont$ ledger that records repeated
application of the $\AddUp$ operation over $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ rows, and the
ambient $\hsame$ classifier on the displayed histories. The scope contains no
separate multiplication operation beyond the $\mathsf{NatMul}$ relation and the
listed continuation reads.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the displayed $\mathsf{NatMul}(d,q,n)$ row. The construction of that
row supplies unary evidence for the multiplicand and multiplier, and the
functional comparison used in
\autoref{thm:unary-multiplication-operation-congruence} supplies the
$\hsame$-classification of every result row with the displayed result.

The repeated-addition ledger is the finite $\Cont$ trace that reads the current
partial sum, the remaining unary counter, and the next $\AddUp$ row at each
stage. Since every stage reads a $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ row and an $\AddUp$
operation row, the ledger never introduces an independent multiplication
constructor.

Repack these unary witnesses, continuation reads, and classifier witnesses as
the $\MulUp$ consumption surface. Transport along $\hsame$ is exactly
\autoref{thm:unary-multiplication-operation-congruence}, so the same scoped
surface is preserved under replacement of $d$, $q$, and $n$ by classified
histories.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MulUp unary additive distributive boundary]
\label{thm:mulup-unary-additive-distributive-boundary}
The right and left additive distribution surfaces cited by the public semantic
exit certificate for $\MulUp$ are consumed only through
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ rows, $\mathsf{NatMul}$ rows, finite $\Cont$ readbacks,
the ambient $\hsame$ classifier, and the existing $\AddUp$ operation rows. No
public boundary row for $\MulUp$ requires a primitive multiplication operation
outside that unary repeated-addition surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the right distribution surface, apply
\autoref{thm:natmul-additive-right-distributivity-surface}. Its hypotheses are
the unary inputs, the relevant $\mathsf{NatMul}$ rows, and the $\AddUp$ rows
that combine the displayed products.

For the left distribution surface, apply
\autoref{thm:natmul-additive-left-distributivity-surface}. The same kind of
data is read, with the multiplicand and multiplier roles exchanged inside the
unary ledger.

Finally combine those two surfaces with
\autoref{thm:mulup-public-semantic-exit-certificate}. The certificate exports
only the listed $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$, $\mathsf{NatMul}$, $\Cont$, $\hsame$,
and $\AddUp$ rows, while
\autoref{thm:unary-multiplication-operation-congruence} supplies transport of
the operation relation across classified histories.
\end{proof}
