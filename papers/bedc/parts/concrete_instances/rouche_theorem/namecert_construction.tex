\begin{definition}[Rouche theorem carrier]
\label{def:rouche-theorem-carrier}
A $\RoucheTheoremUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
R=(C,F,G,B,Z,I,L,H,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $C$ is the contour-admissibility row, naming the finite boundary contour and the containment witness for the analytic region being inspected.
\item $F$ is the base holomorphic row for $f$, including the local complex differentiability and contour-avoidance data used by the zero-count ledger.
\item $G$ is the perturbation holomorphic row for $g$, displayed over the same contour window and the same complex arithmetic surface.
\item $B$ is the boundary-dominance row, recording the finite comparison $|g|<|f|$ on the displayed contour boundary through the real-order rows available to the packet.
\item $Z$ is the zero-count ledger row, listing the finite interior zero accounting read for $f$ and for $f+g$.
\item $I$ is the residue and Cauchy-integral handoff row, routing the zero-count comparison through the residue chapter and the Cauchy integral formula chapter without adding a second analytic oracle.
\item $L$ is the stability ledger row, recording that the two displayed zero-count ledgers agree inside the accepted contour window.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for contour, holomorphic, boundary-dominance, zero-count, integral-handoff, stability, provenance, and local naming rows.

\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RoucheTheoremUp}$ row.

The classifier compares accepted carriers only through $C,F,G,B,Z,I,L,H,P,N$. It has no coordinate for a host analytic-continuation theorem, unbounded root isolation, quotient path equality, or classical compactness oracle outside the displayed packet.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Rouche theorem NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:rouche-theorem-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RoucheTheoremUp$ carrier $R=(C,F,G,B,Z,I,L,H,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\RoucheTheoremUp}$ surface consists exactly of contour admissibility through $C$, holomorphic source rows for $f$ and $g$ through $F,G$, boundary dominance through $B$, finite zero-count ledger exposure through $Z$, residue and Cauchy-integral handoffs through $I$, stability-ledger exactness through $L$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier. The accepted rows are exactly $C,F,G,B,Z,I,L,H,P,N$, so the NameCert surface is finite and componentwise visible.

The contour row $C$ supplies the only boundary window. The holomorphic rows $F$ and $G$ are read on that same window, and the dominance row $B$ is attached to the displayed boundary comparison $|g|<|f|$ rather than to an ambient analytic theorem.

The zero-count ledger $Z$ and handoff row $I$ route the comparison through the residue and Cauchy-integral neighbours. The stability row $L$ records the resulting equality of displayed ledgers, while $H,P,N$ transport, source, and name the same data. Since no carrier coordinate stores global analytic continuation or unbounded root isolation, the listed rows exhaust the obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Rouche theorem contour zero stability]
\label{thm:rouche-namecert-contour-zero-stability}
If an accepted $\RoucheTheoremUp$ packet displays contour admissibility $C$, holomorphic rows $F,G$ for $f$ and $g$, boundary dominance $B$ with $|g|<|f|$ on the displayed contour boundary, residue and Cauchy-integral handoffs $I$, and a finite zero-count ledger $Z$, then the stability ledger $L$ preserves the zero-count read for $f$ and $f+g$ inside that contour window. The claim is only the certificate-bound equality of displayed ledgers; it is not a claim of global analytic continuation, unrestricted homotopy invariance, or host completeness for all contours.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the contour row $C$. It fixes the finite boundary and interior window in which the two holomorphic rows $F$ and $G$ can be compared.

The boundary-dominance row $B$ is read next. Because $B$ is part of the packet, the comparison $|g|<|f|$ is visible at the same contour boundary rather than imported as an external maximum principle.

Use the handoff row $I$ to pass through the residue and Cauchy-integral routes named in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-residue-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-integral-formula-namecert}. These handoffs identify the finite zero-count reads recorded in $Z$.

Finally $L$ records equality of the two displayed zero-count ledger entries, and $H,P,N$ preserve that equality under transport, provenance, and local naming. No row supplies global analytic continuation or unrestricted contour completeness, so the result remains the stated certificate-bound stability claim.
\end{proof}
