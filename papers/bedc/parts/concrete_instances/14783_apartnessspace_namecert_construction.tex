\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ApartnessSpaceUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-apartnessspace-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, the
$\ApartnessSpaceUp$ packet records the constructive neighbour relation needed
between located positive-gap evidence and separated metric recognition. It
reads the finite gap handoff of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-located-apartness-namecert} and exposes the
zero-distance boundary consumed by
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separatedmetric-namecert}. The packet is an
interface for positive apartness over displayed carriers; it is not a source
of trichotomy, quotient real equality, host inequality, selected limits,
open-cover compactness, or $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[ApartnessSpace carrier]
\label{def:apartness-space-carrier}
An $\ApartnessSpaceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
A=(X,L,G,T,C_0,C_1,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the underlying carried-object row, including the local point
classifier used by the packet.
\item $L$ is the $\LocatedApartnessUp$ source row that supplies two located
observations, a shared finite window, and the displayed positive-gap evidence.
\item $G$ is the positive-gap evidence row read from $L$ as a finite
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$ gap ledger attached to the two observations of $X$.
\item $T$ is the apartness transport row stating that the positive-gap ledger
is stable under componentwise transport of the two observations and their
shared finite window.
\item $C_0$ is the classifier-exclusion row: if $G$ is displayed between two
observations, the local point classifier of $X$ does not identify them.
\item $C_1$ is the zero-distance boundary row exported to
$\SeparatedMetricUp$: any consumer that has only a displayed zero-distance
route must pass through the separated-metric classifier boundary before it can
identify observations.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the carried-object row,
the located-apartness source row, the positive-gap evidence, both classifier
boundary rows, the continuation row, the provenance row, and the local naming
row.
\item $C$ stores the $\Cont$ replay from finite located observations to
positive-gap evidence, from positive-gap evidence to classifier exclusion, and
from zero-distance readings to the separated-metric boundary.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LocatedApartnessUp$,
$\SeparatedMetricUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and
$\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ApartnessSpaceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier records apartness as displayed positive-gap evidence over finite
histories. It carries no trichotomy row, no quotient real equality, no host
inequality, no selected limit, no open-cover compactness principle, and no
$\RealUp$ completeness theorem.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[ApartnessSpace classifier]
\label{def:apartness-space-classifier}
The classifier of an accepted $\ApartnessSpaceUp$ packet relates two carried
observations $a,b$ as apart when the packet displays a finite route
$$
a,b\longmapsto L(a,b)\longmapsto G(a,b)\longmapsto C_0(a,b).
$$
The first step reads the two observations through the $\LocatedApartnessUp$
source row, the second step extracts the positive $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ gap row,
and the last step records classifier exclusion inside the carried-object
surface $X$. Transport of this classifier is componentwise: if $a$, $b$, the
shared window, and $G(a,b)$ are moved by the stored $\hsame$ rows, then the
replayed $\Cont$ path produces the transported positive-gap evidence and the
transported classifier-exclusion row.

The classifier is local positive information. It does not decide every pair of
observations, does not turn apartness into host inequality, and does not form a
quotient equality surface for constructive reals.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[ApartnessSpace NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:apartness-space-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\ApartnessSpaceUp$ carrier
$A=(X,L,G,T,C_0,C_1,H,C,P,N)$, the local
$\NameCert_{\ApartnessSpaceUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following
obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the carried-object row, the
$\LocatedApartnessUp$ source row, the positive-gap evidence row, the
apartness transport row, the two classifier boundary rows, componentwise
transport, continuation replay, package provenance, and local naming row;
\item classifier stability, stating that transported observations and
transported finite windows replay the same positive-gap route and the same
classifier-exclusion row;
\item positive-gap exactness, stating that every apartness assertion exported
by the packet factors through the displayed finite $G$ row read from $L$;
\item separated-metric boundary compatibility, stating that zero-distance
consumers use only the displayed $C_1$ route into $\SeparatedMetricUp$ and do
not identify observations by host equality;
\item ledger visibility, stating that the packet exports no trichotomy,
quotient real equality, host inequality, selected limits, open-cover
compactness, or $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is the finite tuple of
\autoref{def:apartness-space-carrier}. Each row named in the obligation list
is visible in $A$, and the packet has no coordinate from which an excluded
classical or completion principle could be read.

Classifier stability follows from the paired transport and replay rows. The
$\hsame$ fields move the two observations, their shared finite window, the
located-apartness source, the positive-gap row, and the classifier boundary
rows componentwise. The $\Cont$ row then replays the same finite route after
transport.

Positive-gap exactness is the defining use of $G$. An apartness assertion
exported by the packet must pass through $L$ and then through the displayed
positive dyadic gap row before it reaches the classifier-exclusion row $C_0$.

For zero-distance consumers, the only exported boundary is $C_1$. That row
points toward the separated-metric classifier route, so it does not create
host equality, quotient equality, or a total comparison principle inside the
apartness-space packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ApartnessSpace located positive-gap route]
\label{thm:apartness-space-located-positive-gap}
Let $A$ be an accepted $\ApartnessSpaceUp$ packet and let $a,b$ be two carried
observations of its object row $X$. If the $\LocatedApartnessUp$ row $L$
displays a shared finite window for $a,b$ and the positive $\DyadicRatCoreUp$
gap row $G(a,b)$ on that window, then $A$ exports a constructive apartness
route
$$
a,b\longmapsto L(a,b)\longmapsto G(a,b)\longmapsto C_0(a,b)
$$
inside the local point classifier of $X$. The route is positive-gap evidence;
it is not a proof of trichotomy, quotient real inequality, host inequality, a
selected limiting comparison, open-cover compactness, or $\RealUp$
completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read $a$ and $b$ through the located-apartness source row $L$. This exposes the
two located observations, their shared finite window, and the finite endpoint
data used by the positive-gap ledger.

Attach the displayed $G(a,b)$ row to that common window. By
\autoref{def:apartness-space-carrier}, $G$ is already a row of the accepted
packet and is transported componentwise with the two observations.

Replay the continuation route from $G(a,b)$ to the classifier-exclusion row
$C_0(a,b)$. The resulting apartness route is therefore a finite $\BHist$
calculation inside the packet.

The final boundary is an inventory statement. Since the carrier has no row for
trichotomy, quotient equality, host inequality, selected limits, open-cover
compactness, or $\RealUp$ completeness, none of those principles can be read
from this positive-gap route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ApartnessSpace separated-metric zero-distance boundary]
\label{thm:apartness-space-separatedmetric-zero-distance-boundary}
Every $\SeparatedMetricUp$ consumer of an accepted $\ApartnessSpaceUp$ packet
can use the packet only through the displayed boundary
$$
\LocatedApartnessUp,\ \ApartnessSpaceUp,\ \SeparatedMetricUp .
$$
Concretely, a positive-gap row may yield classifier exclusion through $C_0$,
while a zero-distance reading may be passed to $\SeparatedMetricUp$ through
$C_1$. The packet does not allow a consumer to combine the two directions into
trichotomy, quotient real equality, host inequality, selected limits,
open-cover compactness, or $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier inventory in \autoref{def:apartness-space-carrier} gives the full
consumer surface. A positive-gap consumer reads $L$, $G$, and $C_0$; a
zero-distance consumer reads $C_1$ and then passes to the separated-metric
classifier boundary.

The $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ rows preserve this order under transport. They move
finite observation rows and replay finite routes, but they do not add a
decision row comparing every pair of observations.

Thus the separated-metric boundary is one-way data flow from the apartness
packet to the metric classifier consumer. No quotient equality, host
inequality, selected limit, compactness principle, or real-completeness theorem
is a field of $A$, so no local $\NameCert$ obligation can export such data.
\end{proof}
