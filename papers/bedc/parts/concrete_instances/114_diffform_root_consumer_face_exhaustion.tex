\begin{definition}[Differential-form root consumer face exhaustion source]
\label{def:diffform-root-consumer-face-exhaustion-source}
The $\DiffFormUp$ root consumer face exhaustion source is the following
ten-field package.
\begin{enumerate}
  \item The traditional target is the finite-degree differential-form surface
  consumed by $\DeRhamUp$, $\SymplecticUp$, and $\ContactUp$.
  \item The BEDC source is $BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$,
  $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, $\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$, and
  $\NameCert_{\TensorProductUp}$.
  \item The carrier is $\mathsf{DiffFormBHistCarrier}$ on the displayed
  manifold, tensor, degree, probe, scalar endpoint, and route histories.
  \item The classifier is $\sim_{\DiffFormUp}$ transported by the root
  classifier rows.
  \item The stability rows are carrier transport, classifier transport, wedge
  routing, and exterior-input routing.
  \item The ledger rows are exactly the degree, probe, tensor, manifold, and
  $\Cont$ route ledgers named by the root $BHist$ surface.
  \item The core theorem list is
  \autoref{thm:diffform-root-consumer-face-coverage},
  \autoref{thm:diffform-root-consumer-face-disjointness},
  \autoref{thm:diffform-root-consumer-face-exhaustion},
  \autoref{thm:diffform-root-consumer-boundary-exactness}, and
  \autoref{thm:diffform-root-downstream-consumer-unblock-package}.
  \item Exactness means consumer-row exhaustion: every downstream row exported
  from the root threshold is shared root data or belongs to exactly one of the
  $\DeRhamUp$, $\SymplecticUp$, and $\ContactUp$ faces, and no other row is
  exported.
  \item The standard bridge field is absent.
  \item The status is the paper-side obligation surface for the three
  downstream geometry consumers.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Differential-form root consumer face exhaustion]
\label{thm:diffform-root-consumer-face-exhaustion}
Every row exported by the root threshold of
\autoref{def:diffform-root-consumer-face-exhaustion-source} is either shared
root data or belongs to exactly one of the $\DeRhamUp$, $\SymplecticUp$, and
$\ContactUp$ consumer faces.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the finite face classifier of
\autoref{def:diffform-root-consumer-face-classifier}. Its four clauses name the
de Rham face, the symplectic face, the contact face, and the shared root face.

Apply \autoref{thm:diffform-root-consumer-face-coverage}. It assigns every row
exported to the downstream consumers to one of those four faces and excludes
any fifth face.

Finally apply
\autoref{thm:diffform-boundary-exhaustion-downstream-export}. The downstream
export is exhausted by carrier rows, classifier rows, wedge rows,
antisymmetry rows, exterior-derivative input-output rows, transport rows,
source-ledger rows, and the displayed downstream boundary rows. Matching those
finite rows against the classifier clauses gives the stated shared-or-unique
consumer assignment.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Differential-form root consumer boundary exactness]
\label{thm:diffform-root-consumer-boundary-exactness}
The exhaustion source of
\autoref{def:diffform-root-consumer-face-exhaustion-source} admits no $d^2=0$,
closedness, nondegeneracy, contact nonintegrability, quotient, or standard
bridge row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply \autoref{thm:diffform-root-consumer-face-disjointness}. It keeps
nilpotence, closedness, nondegeneracy, and contact nonintegrability outside the
shared root face and assigns those laws only to their downstream consumer
surfaces.

Next use \autoref{thm:diffform-root-export-downstream-law-separation}. The root
threshold exports carried forms, classifier data, wedge ledgers, and
exterior-input rows, but no downstream exterior or geometric law.

It remains to exclude quotient and standard-bridge rows. Apply
\autoref{thm:diffform-root-bhist-ledger-exactness-obligation}; the visible
ledger witnesses are exactly the degree, probe, tensor, manifold, wedge,
exterior-input, and $\Cont$ route histories. Since that exactness statement
contains neither quotienting nor a standard bridge, the exhaustion source
contains neither row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Differential-form root downstream consumer unblock package]
\label{thm:diffform-root-downstream-consumer-unblock-package}
The definition
\autoref{def:diffform-root-consumer-face-exhaustion-source}, the exhaustion
theorem \autoref{thm:diffform-root-consumer-face-exhaustion}, and the boundary
exactness theorem \autoref{thm:diffform-root-consumer-boundary-exactness} form
the citable $\DiffFormUp$ root package consumed by $\DeRhamUp$,
$\SymplecticUp$, and $\ContactUp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The definition supplies the ten named fields of the root consumer surface:
traditional target, BEDC source, carrier, classifier, stability rows, ledgers,
core theorems, exactness predicate, absent standard bridge, and obligation
status.

The exhaustion theorem supplies the positive consumer boundary. Every exported
row is shared root data or belongs to the de Rham, symplectic, or contact face.

The boundary exactness theorem supplies the negative boundary. The package
exports no nilpotence, closedness, nondegeneracy, contact nonintegrability,
quotient, or standard-bridge row. Therefore the three downstream chapters may
cite this block as the complete root $BHist$ consumer boundary.
\end{proof}
