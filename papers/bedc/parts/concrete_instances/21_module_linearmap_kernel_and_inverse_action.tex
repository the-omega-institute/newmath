\begin{theorem}[Module LinearMap kernel sub-module closure]
\label{thm:module-linearmap-kernel-submodule-closure}
Let $R$ carry a $\RingUp(R)$ certificate, and let $M$ and $N$ carry $\ModuleUp(R,M)$ and $\ModuleUp(R,N)$ certificates over the same scalar source. Write $\sim_M$ and $\sim_N$ for the module classifiers, $+_M$ and $+_N$ for module addition, $0_M$ and $0_N$ for module zero, and $\odot_M$ and $\odot_N$ for scalar action. If
$$
\mathsf{LinearMapCert}_R(M,N;f)
$$
holds, define the kernel predicate on carried source endpoints by
$$
\mathsf{Ker}_f(x)\Longleftrightarrow f(x)\sim_N0_N.
$$
Then this predicate is closed under source module addition, closed under scalar action, and contains source zero:
$$
\begin{gathered}
x,y\text{ carried and }\mathsf{Ker}_f(x)\land\mathsf{Ker}_f(y)\Longrightarrow x+_M y\text{ carried and }\mathsf{Ker}_f(x+_M y),\\
r\text{ carried and }x\text{ carried and }\mathsf{Ker}_f(x)\Longrightarrow r\odot_M x\text{ carried and }\mathsf{Ker}_f(r\odot_M x),\\
0_M\text{ is carried and }\mathsf{Ker}_f(0_M).
\end{gathered}
$$
The ten-field fit is: traditional target the ordinary kernel sub-module of a linear map; BEDC source the displayed $\mathsf{LinearMapCert}_R(M,N;f)$ package over two $\ModuleUp$ certificates; carrier the source module carrier restricted by $\mathsf{Ker}_f$; classifier the inherited source module classifier; stability addition closure, scalar-action closure, and zero membership; ledger the additive, scalar, zero, codomain congruence, and codomain zero-action rows; core theorem this block; exactness predicate the three displayed closure clauses; standard bridge the usual kernel of an $R$-linear map; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack $\mathsf{LinearMapCert}_R(M,N;f)$ using \autoref{def:module-linearmap-certificate-package}. It supplies output closure, source-classifier descent, additive preservation, scalar compatibility, and zero preservation. The two module certificates supply the module operation closures and congruence rows through \autoref{def:module-certificate-obligations}, \autoref{def:module-classifier-specification}, and \autoref{def:module-stability-certificate}; classifier compositions use \autoref{thm:namecert-equivalence-transitivity}.

For addition, let $x$ and $y$ be carried in $M$ and assume $f(x)\sim_N0_N$ and $f(y)\sim_N0_N$. Additive closure in the source module makes $x+_M y$ carried. The additive row of the linear-map certificate gives
$$
f(x+_M y)\sim_N f(x)+_N f(y).
$$
Output closure makes $f(x)$ and $f(y)$ carried in $N$. Additive congruence in the codomain module transports the two kernel hypotheses to
$$
f(x)+_N f(y)\sim_N0_N+_N0_N.
$$
The additive unit law in the codomain additive group gives $0_N+_N0_N\sim_N0_N$. Transitivity of $\sim_N$ composes these comparisons, yielding $f(x+_M y)\sim_N0_N$.

For scalar action, let $r$ be a carried scalar and let $x$ be carried in $M$ with $f(x)\sim_N0_N$. Scalar-action closure in the source module makes $r\odot_M x$ carried. The scalar row gives
$$
f(r\odot_M x)\sim_N r\odot_N f(x).
$$
Scalar-action congruence in $N$, using classifier reflexivity for $r$ from \autoref{def:lean-carrier-naming-certificate} and the kernel hypothesis for $x$, gives
$$
r\odot_N f(x)\sim_N r\odot_N0_N.
$$
The second annihilation clause of \autoref{thm:module-zero-action-annihilation} gives $r\odot_N0_N\sim_N0_N$. Transitivity therefore gives $f(r\odot_M x)\sim_N0_N$.

For zero, the additive carrier data of the source module carry $0_M$, and the zero-preservation row of \autoref{def:module-linearmap-certificate-package} is exactly
$$
f(0_M)\sim_N0_N.
$$
Thus $\mathsf{Ker}_f(0_M)$ holds. The three clauses are precisely the advertised sub-module carrier closure properties inside the source module $M$.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Module scalar additive inverse acts by action inverse]\label{lem:module-scalar-additive-inverse-action}
Let a $\ModuleUp(R,M)$ certificate be given by \autoref{def:module-certificate-obligations}. Write $\sim_R$ for the scalar-ring classifier, $\sim_M$ for the module classifier, $+_R$ and $0_R$ for scalar addition and scalar zero, $+_M$ and $0_M$ for module addition and module zero, $-_R$ and $-_M$ for the two additive inverse operations, and $\odot_M$ for scalar action. For every carried scalar $r:R$ and carried module endpoint $m:M$, $(-_R r)\odot_M m\sim_M -_M(r\odot_M m)$.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the module certificate through \autoref{def:module-certificate-obligations}. The scalar source is a certified $\RingUp(R)$ by \autoref{def:module-source-specification}, and the module additive reduct is an $\AbGroupUp(M)$ certificate by \autoref{prop:module-forgets-abgroup-certificate}. Its group reduct is obtained by \autoref{prop:abgroup-forgets-group-certificate}, keeping the same addition, zero, inverse operation, and classifier. Thus scalar additive inverse closure, module additive inverse closure, additive congruence, additive commutativity, and the two inverse rows are available through \autoref{def:ring-stability-certificate}, \autoref{def:abgroup-stability-certificate}, and \autoref{def:group-stability-certificate}. Classifier reflexivity, symmetry, and transitivity are supplied by \autoref{def:lean-carrier-naming-certificate}, with transitive composition as in \autoref{thm:namecert-equivalence-transitivity}.
Let $x:=r\odot_M m$ and $y:=(-_R r)\odot_M m$. Scalar-action closure carries both $x$ and $y$. The scalar additive right-inverse theorem \autoref{thm:ring-add-right-inverse}, read in the certified scalar source, gives $r+_R(-_R r)\sim_R0_R$.
Scalar-action congruence from \autoref{def:module-stability-certificate}, using classifier reflexivity on the carried module endpoint $m$, transports this scalar comparison to $(r+_R(-_R r))\odot_M m\sim_M0_R\odot_M m$. The zero-action annihilation theorem \autoref{thm:module-zero-action-annihilation} gives $0_R\odot_M m\sim_M0_M$, hence $(r+_R(-_R r))\odot_M m\sim_M0_M$.
Scalar distributivity over scalar addition from \autoref{def:module-stability-certificate} gives $(r+_R(-_R r))\odot_M m\sim_M x+_M y$. By symmetry and transitivity, $x+_M y\sim_M0_M$. Additive commutativity in the retained $\AbGroupUp(M)$ certificate changes the order of the two summands, so $y+_M x\sim_M0_M$.
The module additive right-inverse row for the carried endpoint $x$ gives $x+_M(-_M x)\sim_M0_M$. Apply \autoref{thm:group-left-right-inverse-uniqueness} in the retained additive group of $M$, with operation $+_M$, identity $0_M$, base element $x$, left inverse witness $y$, and right inverse witness $-_M x$. This yields $y\sim_M-_M x$.
Substituting the definitions of $x$ and $y$ gives $(-_R r)\odot_M m\sim_M -_M(r\odot_M m)$.
\end{proof}
\begin{theorem}[Module scalar minus one is additive inverse]\label{thm:module-minus-one-scalar-action-additive-inverse}
Let a $\ModuleUp(R,M)$ certificate be given by \autoref{def:module-certificate-obligations}. Write $\sim_M$ for the module classifier, $1_R$ for the scalar one, $-_R$ and $-_M$ for the scalar and module additive inverse operations, and $\odot_M$ for scalar action. For every carried module endpoint $m:M$, $(-_R1_R)\odot_M m\sim_M-_M m$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{lem:module-scalar-additive-inverse-action} with $r=1_R$. This gives $(-_R1_R)\odot_M m\sim_M-_M(1_R\odot_M m)$.
The scalar unit row in \autoref{def:module-stability-certificate} gives $1_R\odot_M m\sim_M m$.
The inverse-congruence row in the retained additive group of $M$, obtained from \autoref{prop:module-forgets-abgroup-certificate} and \autoref{prop:abgroup-forgets-group-certificate}, transports this comparison to $-_M(1_R\odot_M m)\sim_M-_M m$.
Transitivity of $\sim_M$ composes the two comparisons and yields $(-_R1_R)\odot_M m\sim_M-_M m$.
\end{proof}
