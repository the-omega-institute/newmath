\begin{definition}[Force-like inter-Hist relation carrier]
\label{def:force-interhist-relation-carrier}
A $\ForceInterHistRelationUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
F=(S,K,L,R,U,A,B,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is a finite source row of inter-$\Hist$ change sites admitted by the surrounding multi-history ledger;
\item $K$ is the same-force-pattern classifier row comparing the visible relation packets over $S$;
\item $L$ is the layer-map row that records which source, carrier, classifier, ledger, exactness, and refusal layers are being compared;
\item $R$ is the non-preserved-content refusal row required by the layered relation gate;
\item $U$ is the $\InterHistInvariantUp$ row that prevents observer-slot or invariant collapse;
\item $A$ is the $\InterHistLocalityLedgerUp$ row that routes the compared histories through a finite locality ledger;
\item $B$ is the $\ObserverBridgeLedgerUp$ row that records the observer-side bridge read used by the relation packet;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the source, classifier, layer-map, refusal, invariant, locality, bridge, audit, provenance, and naming rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes for every relation-certificate consumer read;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ForceInterHistRelationUp$, $\InterHistInvariantUp$, $\InterHistLocalityLedgerUp$, $\AuditMapFamilyLedgerUp$, $\ObserverBridgeLedgerUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ForceInterHistRelationUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier accepts a force-like inter-$\Hist$ relation only when the source, classifier, layer-map, refusal, invariant, locality, observer-bridge, transport, route, provenance, and local naming rows are displayed together. It exports no hidden force cause, empirical constant, global frame, metric tensor, field equation, subject-substance row, or source-free relation primitive.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Force-like relation NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:force-interhist-relation-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:force-interhist-relation-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\ForceInterHistRelationUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for
$$
F=(S,K,L,R,U,A,B,H,C,P,N),
$$
source exposure through $S$, same-pattern classification through $K$, layer-map exposure through $L$, non-preserved-content refusal through $R$, invariant preservation through $U$, locality-ledger routing through $A$, observer-bridge readback through $B$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed continuation replay through $C$, audit-map provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project an accepted carrier to the eleven rows of \autoref{def:force-interhist-relation-carrier}. The source row $S$, classifier row $K$, layer-map row $L$, and refusal row $R$ are the relation-certificate rows required before any force-like reading is available.

The invariant row $U$, locality ledger $A$, and observer bridge ledger $B$ bind the relation to the already displayed inter-$\Hist$ and observer routes. Componentwise transport $H$ and continuation replay $C$ preserve those rows as rows of the same packet.

The provenance row $P$ records the audit-map family ledger together with the invariant, locality, and bridge siblings, while $N$ names the local certificate. Since the carrier has no coordinate outside these rows, the listed obligations exhaust the exported $\NameCert_{\ForceInterHistRelationUp}$ surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Force-like relation refusal non-escape]
\label{thm:force-interhist-relation-refusal-nonescape}
No accepted $\ForceInterHistRelationUp$ consumer can export a force-like inter-$\Hist$ relation as a BEDC claim unless the same carrier displays the non-preserved-content refusal row $R$ together with the source row $S$, classifier row $K$, layer-map row $L$, invariant row $U$, locality-ledger row $A$, observer-bridge row $B$, transport row $H$, continuation row $C$, provenance row $P$, and local name row $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The relation gate of \autoref{def:layered-relation-gate} asks whether preserved and non-preserved content are both displayed. In the carrier of \autoref{def:force-interhist-relation-carrier}, the displayed non-preserved-content coordinate is exactly $R$.

If a consumer omits $R$, then the read may preserve the source and classifier rows but lacks the refusal row that prevents identity collapse, hidden empirical law, global-frame import, or subject-substance import. Such a read is not a route through the full packet $F$.

If a consumer omits any of $S,K,L,U,A,B,H,C,P,N$, then it loses the source, classifier, layer, invariant, locality, observer bridge, transport, continuation, provenance, or naming row needed by the local NameCert obligations. Hence every exported force-like relation claim must pass through the displayed packet and its refusal row.
\end{proof}
