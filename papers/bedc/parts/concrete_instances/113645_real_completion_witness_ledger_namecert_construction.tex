\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RealCompletionWitnessLedgerUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-real-completion-witness-ledger-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges on constructive real completion, the $\RealCompletionWitnessLedgerUp$ packet records the finite witness rows by which StreamName observations, regular rational readback, dyadic tolerance, and a Real seal are read as completion data. It sits over the finite stream interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the nested interval completeness route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-nested-interval-completeness-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet is a witness ledger for finite completion reads, not a quotient construction, selected limit, countable-choice schedule, or ambient completeness theorem.

\begin{definition}[Real completion witness ledger carrier]
\label{def:real-completion-witness-ledger-carrier}
A $\RealCompletionWitnessLedgerUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
W=(S,R,D,K,E,H,T,A,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row, carrying the requested windows and the observed stream entries used by the completion read.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular readback row assembled from those same finite windows.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row, recording the dyadic comparison radii that certify compatibility of the window and readback data.
\item $K$ is the nested-interval or Cauchy handoff row, displaying the finite bridge by which the regular readback is admitted as completion-facing data.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row, visible only after $S,R,D,K$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the stream windows, regular readback, dyadic tolerance, handoff, Real seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $T$ is the displayed $\Cont$ replay from a finite completion request through $S,R,D,K,E,H,P,N$.
\item $A$ is the provenance and replay ledger, recording which finite windows and tolerance rows were inspected before the seal was consumed.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RealCompletionWitnessLedgerUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\NestedIntervalCompletenessUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RealCompletionWitnessLedgerUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through these rows. It has no coordinate for quotient stream equality, a host-selected representative, countable choice, trichotomy, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Real completion witness ledger seal]
\label{thm:real-completion-witness-ledger-seal}
Every completion-facing $\RealUp$ read exported by an accepted $\RealCompletionWitnessLedgerUp$ carrier factors through a finite route
$$
\StreamNameUp \longmapsto \RegSeqRatUp \longmapsto \DyadicRatCoreUp \longmapsto \NestedIntervalCompletenessUp \longmapsto \RealUp .
$$
More explicitly, the seal $E$ can be consumed only after the finite window row $S$, regular readback row $R$, dyadic tolerance row $D$, and nested-interval or Cauchy handoff row $K$ have been displayed and transported componentwise. The packet exports no quotient stream equality, selected limit representative, countable-choice schedule, trichotomy, or ambient Real completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:real-completion-witness-ledger-carrier}. The first visible data are the finite StreamName windows $S$ and the regular rational readback $R$ assembled from the same observations.

Read the dyadic tolerance row $D$ and the completion handoff row $K$. These rows certify that the finite windows and regular readback are compatible before any completion-facing seal is available; the handoff may be nested-interval shaped or Cauchy shaped, but in either case it is displayed as a finite row.

The terminal Real seal $E$ is downstream of $S,R,D,K$, and the rows $H,T,A,P,N$ only transport, replay, source, and name that route. Since the carrier has no quotient, selected representative, choice, trichotomy, or host-completeness coordinate, every exported completion read is exactly the displayed finite witness-ledger seal.
\end{proof}
