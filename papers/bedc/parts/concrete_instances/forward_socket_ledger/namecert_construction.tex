The $\ForwardSocketLedgerUp$ packet realizes the forward-socket family boundary of \autoref{ch:visions-apophatic-fixed-point-and-inscription} as finite NameCert rows. It sits beside the supply-socket accounting of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-supplysocketledger-namecert}, the apophatic socket classifier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-apophaticsocket-namecert}, the far-end boundary packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-apophaticfarendsocket-namecert}, and the local inscription carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-inscriptionpoint-namecert}. Its role is to make the forward read auditable before any consumer cites the apophatic far-end boundary.

The dependency route consumed before the forward socket export passes through the supply ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-supplysocketledger-namecert} and the far-end boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-apophaticfarendsocket-namecert}; the forward packet only exports after those ledger and far-end rows are visible.

\begin{definition}[Forward socket ledger carrier]
\label{def:forward-socket-ledger-carrier}
A $\ForwardSocketLedgerUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
F=(K,R,A,G,T,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $K$ is the socket-kind row, recording which forward socket is being read and how it is classified among the accepted socket forms.
\item $R$ is the requested supply-shape row, recording the requested external shape without supplying it as a kernel object.
\item $A$ is the non-internalization marker, recording that the requested far-end supply is a boundary and not a generated state, record, rule, or $\BHist$ constructor.
\item $G$ is the $\mathsf{GAP}$ ledger row, binding the refusal of internal supply to the visible socket-use and supply-ledger routes.
\item $T$ is the apophatic far-end boundary row, naming the far end only as the common boundary read by the forward socket family.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $K,R,A,G,T$ and the local naming row.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay through socket kind, requested supply shape, non-internalization, $\mathsf{GAP}$ ledger, far-end boundary, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ForwardSocketLedgerUp$, $\SupplySocketLedgerUp$, $\ApophaticSocketUp$, $\ApophaticFarEndSocketUp$, $\InscriptionPointUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ForwardSocketLedgerUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no coordinate for $\mathsf{T}$ as a kernel object, no hidden requested-supply witness, no socket selector outside $K$, no object-level equality between the far end and a local state, and no host fixed-point principle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Forward socket ledger NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:forward-socket-ledger-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\ForwardSocketLedgerUp$ carrier $F=(K,R,A,G,T,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\ForwardSocketLedgerUp}$ surface consists exactly of socket-kind exposure through $K$; requested supply-shape exposure through $R$; non-internalization evidence through $A$; $\mathsf{GAP}$ ledger exposure through $G$; apophatic far-end boundary exposure through $T$; componentwise transport through $H$; displayed replay through $C$; provenance through $P$; and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier of \autoref{def:forward-socket-ledger-carrier}. The tuple lists socket kind, requested supply shape, non-internalization, gap ledger, far-end boundary, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming as explicit rows.

The socket can be read only after $K$ and $R$ expose its kind and requested shape. The rows $A$ and $G$ then mark the request as boundary supply rather than as an internal object.

The far-end boundary row $T$ is downstream of those rows. Transport, replay, provenance, and naming through $H,C,P,N$ preserve the same finite route. Since no hidden selector, requested-supply witness, internal far-end object, or host fixed-point principle occurs in the carrier, these rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Forward socket ledger apophatic non-escape]
\label{thm:forward-socket-ledger-apophatic-nonescape}
Every accepted read of the apophatic far-end boundary through $\ForwardSocketLedgerUp$ must first expose $K,R,A,G,T,H,C,P,N$. It cannot cite the far end as an internal object, a generated state, a selected supply witness, a socket-family fixed point outside the ledger, or an equality between $\mathsf{T}$ and a local inscription point.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the rows $K$ and $R$. They identify the forward socket and its requested supply shape before any far-end boundary is available.

The rows $A$ and $G$ are the non-internalization gate. They state that the requested supply remains a $\mathsf{GAP}$-ledgered boundary rather than a kernel-side value.

Only then may $T$ be read as the apophatic far-end boundary, and $H,C,P,N$ only transport, replay, source, and name that same finite route. Thus any consumer that skips $K,R,A,G,T,H,C,P,N$ has no accepted route to the far end.
\end{proof}
