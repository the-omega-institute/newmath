\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\GammaZetaFactorSealUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-gammazetafactorseal-namecert}
\origin{ai}

The $\GammaZetaFactorSealUp$ packet is the finite seal consumed by zeta
functional-equation routes when they read the factor $\Gamma(1-s)$. It sits
between the constructive Gamma surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-gamma-namecert},
the application packet of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-zetacontinuationapplication-namecert}, and the
witness packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-zeta-continuation-witness-namecert}.
Its role is to make the Gamma factor a displayed NameCert packet rather than a
horizon mention inside the zeta continuation application.

\begin{definition}[Gamma-Zeta factor seal carrier]
\label{def:gamma-zeta-factor-seal-carrier}
A $\GammaZetaFactorSealUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(D,R,F,A,H,C,Q,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the Gamma-domain row for the input $1-s$, including the pole
exclusion and complex-history carrier inherited from $\GammaUp$;
\item $R$ is the reflected-input row, recording the source input $s$ and the
displayed reflection route to $1-s$;
\item $F$ is the functional-equation factor row, recording the product slot in
which $\Gamma(1-s)$ is consumed together with the elementary factors;
\item $A$ is the zeta-application row that binds the factor read to the
$\ZetaContinuationApplicationUp$ packet;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $D,R,F,A$ and for the
local naming row;
\item $C$ stores the finite $\Cont$ replay routes by which downstream consumers
read Gamma-domain, reflection, factor, zeta-application, transport, provenance,
and local naming data;
\item $Q$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance row over the Gamma, zeta-application, and
zeta-witness source packets;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\GammaZetaFactorSealUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for an undisplayed Gamma value, an external
functional-equation proof, a second zeta-application packet, a branch outside
the reflected-input route, or a nontrivial-zero classifier.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Gamma-Zeta factor seal NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:gamma-zeta-factor-seal-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:gamma-zeta-factor-seal-carrier}, the local
$\NameCert_{\GammaZetaFactorSealUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier
admission for
$$
S=(D,R,F,A,H,C,Q,N),
$$
Gamma-domain exactness through $D$, reflected-input exactness through $R$,
functional-equation factor exactness through $F$, zeta-application exactness
through $A$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$
replay through $C$, package provenance through $Q$, and local naming through
$N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the displayed carrier. The rows $D,R,F,A$ are the complete analytic
content of the seal, while $H,C,Q,N$ transport, replay, package, and name the
same finite packet.

Read the source packets in their displayed roles. The $\GammaUp$ chapter
supplies the Gamma-domain row, the $\ZetaContinuationApplicationUp$ chapter
supplies the application row that consumes the factor, and the
$\ZetaContinuationWitnessUp$ chapter supplies the continuation witness route
in which the factor is replayed.

The NameCert surface is exhausted by these rows. Since the carrier has no
coordinate for an undisplayed Gamma value, an external functional-equation
proof, a second zeta-application packet, an outside branch, or a nontrivial-zero
classifier, no accepted read can obtain any of those objects from the seal.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Functional-equation Gamma-factor readback]
\label{thm:gamma-zeta-factor-seal-readback}
Every accepted $\GammaZetaFactorSealUp$ consumer that reads the
functional-equation Gamma factor must receive the displayed packet
$$
S=(D,R,F,A,H,C,Q,N).
$$
In particular, a read of $\Gamma(1-s)$ is accepted only when the Gamma-domain
row, reflected-input row, functional-equation factor row, zeta-application row,
componentwise transport, replay route, provenance row, and local name occur in
the same finite packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier inventory in
\autoref{def:gamma-zeta-factor-seal-carrier}. A Gamma-factor read has only one
factor coordinate, namely $F$, and that coordinate is displayed together with
$D,R,A,H,C,Q,N$.

Apply the NameCert obligations of
\autoref{thm:gamma-zeta-factor-seal-namecert-obligations}. The readback route
must preserve the Gamma-domain row through $D$, the reflected input through
$R$, the factor slot through $F$, and the zeta application through $A$ before
it can replay through $C$.

The missing alternatives named in the carrier definition have no coordinate.
Thus the accepted readback is exactly the finite seal packet, not a detached
Gamma theorem or an external functional-equation route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Gamma-Zeta factor seal non-escape]
\label{thm:gamma-zeta-factor-seal-non-escape}
Every downstream zeta functional-equation route that consumes a
$\GammaZetaFactorSealUp$ packet factors through $D,R,F,A,H,C,Q,N$. Requests for
a Gamma value outside the displayed domain row, an unreflected input, an
undisplayed functional-equation factor, a second application packet, a hidden
transport, or a nontrivial-zero conclusion are rejected by the carrier
inventory.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Let a downstream route be given. By carrier admission, the route can inspect
only the Gamma-domain row, reflected-input row, factor row, application row,
transport row, replay row, provenance row, and local name.

Componentwise transport keeps these rows separate. A Gamma-domain read returns
to $D$, an input-reflection read returns to $R$, a factor read returns to $F$,
and a zeta-application read returns to $A$.

Every request listed in the statement asks for a coordinate absent from the
carrier. The route therefore remains a read of the displayed seal and cannot
export any stronger analytic object.
\end{proof}
