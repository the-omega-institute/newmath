\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\AuditMapInterfaceUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-auditmapinterface-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\AuditMapInterfaceUp$ packet realizes the audit-map family methodology of \autoref{ch:audit-map-family-methodology} as a finite interface rather than as a prose convention. It sits beside the kernel acceptance trace of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-kernelacceptancetrace-namecert}, whose rows expose accepted theorem inventory, and the substitution audit map of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-substitutionauditmap-namecert}, whose rows show how a local MetaCIC substrate is kept separate from stronger typing claims. The present packet names the common interface consumed by audit-map ledgers, synthesis packets, and cross-map chapters.

\begin{definition}[Audit-map interface carrier]
\label{def:audit-map-interface-carrier}
An $\AuditMapInterfaceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
I=(E,K,O,F,X,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $E$ is the established theorem inventory row, listing the positive results that the local audit map may cite directly;
\item $K$ is the conditional theorem inventory row, listing results available only through named discharge, setup, or route data;
\item $O$ is the obstruction row, recording no-go, boundary, or shape facts that block a stronger local reading;
\item $F$ is the frontier row, recording the strongest adjacent theorem or package that remains outside the local map;
\item $X$ is the cross-map consumer row, naming neighbouring audit maps, synthesis packets, and route chapters that may consume the visible rows;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the established, conditional, obstruction, frontier, cross-map, provenance, and naming rows;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ routes by which consumers read the positive inventory, conditional inventory, obstruction boundary, frontier handoff, cross-map use, provenance, and local name;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over \autoref{ch:audit-map-family-methodology}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-kernelacceptancetrace-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-substitutionauditmap-namecert}, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\AuditMapInterfaceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for turning conditional rows into unconditional theorems, obstruction rows into solved obligations, frontier rows into closed packages, or cross-map consumer rows into hidden module imports.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Audit-map interface NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:audit-map-interface-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:audit-map-interface-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\AuditMapInterfaceUp}$ surface consists of five obligations: carrier admission for
$$
I=(E,K,O,F,X,H,C,P,N),
$$
inventory exactness for the established, conditional, obstruction, frontier, and cross-map rows $E,K,O,F,X$, componentwise transport stability through $H$, displayed route readback through $C$, and package provenance with local naming through $P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AuditMapInterfaceUp.AuditMapInterfaceCarrier\_namecert\_obligations}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is the displayed finite packet. Its first five coordinates are the only audit-map row classes, so inventory exactness is a projection of $E,K,O,F,X$ from the carrier.

Transport stability is componentwise. The row $H$ may compare an established row with an established row, a conditional row with a conditional row, and similarly for obstruction, frontier, cross-map, provenance, and naming data; it supplies no cross-class coercion.

Route readback is then forced through $C$. A consumer must choose one of the displayed routes before using a row, and the route returns to the same inventory coordinate that it reads.

Finally, $P$ and $N$ bind provenance and local naming. Since provenance cites only the visible methodology and sibling concrete packets, the five obligations exhaust the NameCert surface without adding a stronger theorem coordinate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Audit-map interface cross-map consumer boundary]
\label{thm:audit-map-interface-cross-map-consumer-boundary}
Every cross-map consumer of an accepted $\AuditMapInterfaceUp$ carrier
$$
I=(E,K,O,F,X,H,C,P,N)
$$
must read the positive inventory $E$, conditional inventory $K$, obstruction row $O$, frontier row $F$, and cross-map consumer row $X$ through the displayed transport, continuation, provenance, and naming rows $H,C,P,N$. Such a consumer cannot turn the interface into an unconditional theorem, a discharged obstruction, a closed frontier package, a hidden import of a neighbouring map, or a host semantic equality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the NameCert obligations. They identify the accepted interface with the finite rows $E,K,O,F,X,H,C,P,N$ and force each consumer read through the displayed route row $C$.

For a positive read, the consumer obtains exactly the established inventory row $E$. For a conditional read, it obtains the conditional row $K$ together with its visible route requirement. For an obstruction read, it obtains the boundary row $O$ rather than a proof of the blocked claim.

For a frontier or cross-map read, the consumer follows $F$ or $X$ through the same transport and provenance rows. Those rows name neighbouring maps and handoffs; they do not import their hidden contents or solve their obligations.

The carrier has no coordinate for unconditional discharge, solved obstruction, closed frontier package, hidden module import, or host semantic equality. Therefore every cross-map use remains an interface read and cannot become a stronger theorem.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AuditMapInterfaceUp.AuditMapInterfaceCarrier\_cross\_map\_consumer\_boundary}

\begin{theorem}[Audit-map interface conditional row discharge boundary]
\label{thm:audit-map-interface-conditional-row-discharge-boundary}
For every accepted $\AuditMapInterfaceUp$ carrier
$$
I=(E,K,O,F,X,H,C,P,N),
$$
a consumer may use a conditional inventory row $K$ only together with its displayed discharge, setup, or route datum read through the continuation, transport, provenance, and local naming rows $C,H,P,N$. The same read cannot promote $K$ into the established inventory row $E$ or into an unconditional theorem coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AuditMapInterfaceUp.AuditMapInterfaceCarrier\_conditional\_row\_discharge\_boundary}
\begin{proof}
Project the finite carrier of \autoref{def:audit-map-interface-carrier}. The rows $E$ and $K$ are separate $\BHist$ coordinates: $E$ records established theorem inventory, while $K$ records conditional inventory.

Use the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:audit-map-interface-namecert-obligations}. Inventory exactness keeps the two rows distinct, componentwise $\hsame$ transport in $H$ preserves the row class being transported, and displayed $\Cont$ replay in $C$ returns to the same coordinate it reads.

Finally apply the cross-map consumer boundary of \autoref{thm:audit-map-interface-cross-map-consumer-boundary}. A downstream consumer can carry the conditional row through $C,H,P,N$, but the carrier has no coordinate that reclassifies the conditional row as $E$ or supplies an unconditional theorem. Therefore every use of $K$ remains tied to the displayed discharge, setup, or route datum.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Audit-map interface obstruction row non-escape]
\label{thm:audit-map-interface-obstruction-row-nonescape}
For every accepted $\AuditMapInterfaceUp$ carrier $I=(E,K,O,F,X,H,C,P,N)$, an obstruction row $O$ remains a boundary row under componentwise $\hsame$ transport and displayed $\Cont$ replay. No route through $H,C,P,N$ can convert $O$ into a solved obligation, an established theorem row, or a closed package.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AuditMapInterfaceUp.AuditMapInterfaceCarrier\_obstruction\_row\_nonescape}
\begin{proof}
Read $O$ from the carrier in \autoref{def:audit-map-interface-carrier}. It is the only coordinate whose role is to record no-go, boundary, or shape facts blocking a stronger local reading.

The NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:audit-map-interface-namecert-obligations} make the administrative rows transparent. The row $H$ transports obstruction data as obstruction data, while $C$ only replays a displayed route to the row being read; $P$ and $N$ record provenance and local naming for the same finite packet.

Invoke \autoref{thm:audit-map-interface-cross-map-consumer-boundary}. It explicitly forbids turning the interface into a discharged obstruction or closed frontier package. Since neither $H$ nor $C$ introduces a theorem-producing coordinate, obstruction reads remain boundary reads for every consumer.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Audit-map interface frontier consumer routing]
\label{thm:audit-map-interface-frontier-consumer-routing}
For every accepted $\AuditMapInterfaceUp$ carrier $I=(E,K,O,F,X,H,C,P,N)$, every consumer route that uses the frontier row $F$ or cross-map row $X$ must pass through visible provenance, displayed continuation, componentwise transport, and local naming in $P,C,H,N$. Such a route may name neighbouring audit maps and handoffs, but it cannot import hidden rows from them or replace the finite interface by host semantic equality.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AuditMapInterfaceUp.AuditMapInterfaceCarrier\_frontier\_consumer\_transport\_routing}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the carrier definition. The rows $F$ and $X$ are visible $\BHist$ coordinates: $F$ records the strongest adjacent theorem or package outside the local map, and $X$ names neighbouring audit maps, synthesis packets, and route chapters that may consume the interface.

Apply \autoref{thm:audit-map-interface-namecert-obligations}. It forces frontier and cross-map readback through the same finite NameCert surface, with $H$ for componentwise transport, $C$ for displayed continuation replay, $P$ for package provenance, and $N$ for local naming.

Now use \autoref{thm:audit-map-interface-cross-map-consumer-boundary}. Frontier and cross-map reads are allowed only as interface reads. Because the carrier lacks hidden import, closed frontier package, and host semantic equality coordinates, neighbouring maps can be routed only through the visible provenance attached to $F$ and $X$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Audit-map interface row-class exhaustion]
\label{thm:audit-map-interface-row-class-exhaustion}
For every accepted $\AuditMapInterfaceUp$ carrier
$$
I=(E,K,O,F,X,H,C,P,N),
$$
each consumer-visible inventory row is exactly one of the established, conditional, obstruction, frontier, or cross-map rows $E,K,O,F,X$. The administrative rows $H,C,P,N$ provide componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ readback, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ naming for those rows, but cannot reclassify any row class.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:audit-map-interface-carrier}. Its consumer inventory surface is the five-row list $E,K,O,F,X$, while $H,C,P,N$ are recorded after that list as transport, route, provenance, and naming data.

Apply \autoref{thm:audit-map-interface-namecert-obligations}. Inventory exactness is stated only for $E,K,O,F,X$, and componentwise transport through $H$ is already restricted to the row class being transported.

Use \autoref{thm:audit-map-interface-cross-map-consumer-boundary}, \autoref{thm:audit-map-interface-conditional-row-discharge-boundary}, \autoref{thm:audit-map-interface-obstruction-row-nonescape}, and \autoref{thm:audit-map-interface-frontier-consumer-routing}. These boundary theorems rule out reclassifying a conditional row as established, an obstruction row as solved, a frontier row as closed, or a cross-map row as a hidden import. Hence the five listed row classes exhaust the consumer-visible inventory.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Audit-map interface route-readback totality]
\label{thm:audit-map-interface-route-readback-totality}
For every accepted $\AuditMapInterfaceUp$ carrier $I=(E,K,O,F,X,H,C,P,N)$, each displayed continuation route read through $C$ returns to the same row class that it reads. Established, conditional, obstruction, frontier, and cross-map reads therefore remain separated under $\hsame$ transport, $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ naming.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from \autoref{thm:audit-map-interface-namecert-obligations}. Displayed route readback is one of the five NameCert obligations, and it is attached to the finite carrier rather than to an external theorem coordinate.

The row-class exhaustion of \autoref{thm:audit-map-interface-row-class-exhaustion} identifies the possible consumer inventory rows as $E,K,O,F,X$. Since $H$ is componentwise and $C$ is displayed for the row being read, a route that starts at one row class has no carrier coordinate through which it can return as another.

The separation clauses are exactly the existing boundaries. The cross-map boundary of \autoref{thm:audit-map-interface-cross-map-consumer-boundary}, the conditional boundary of \autoref{thm:audit-map-interface-conditional-row-discharge-boundary}, the obstruction non-escape theorem of \autoref{thm:audit-map-interface-obstruction-row-nonescape}, and the frontier routing theorem of \autoref{thm:audit-map-interface-frontier-consumer-routing} leave route readback as an interface read for the same row class.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AuditMapInterfaceUp.AuditMapInterfaceCarrier\_route\_readback\_totality}

\begin{theorem}[Audit-map interface template instantiation boundary]
\label{thm:audit-map-interface-template-instantiation-boundary}
Any audit-map template or sibling audit packet instantiating the $\AuditMapInterfaceUp$ interface must supply the nine visible rows
$$
E,K,O,F,X,H,C,P,N.
$$
The instantiated packet receives interface reads through $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ data only; it receives no hidden imports, solved obstruction coordinates, discharged theorem coordinates, closed frontier coordinates, or host semantic equality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier definition fixes the required packet shape. An instantiation must therefore provide the five inventory rows $E,K,O,F,X$ and the four administrative rows $H,C,P,N$ before it can claim the local $\NameCert_{\AuditMapInterfaceUp}$ surface.

Apply the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:audit-map-interface-namecert-obligations}. They give carrier admission, inventory exactness, componentwise transport, route readback, provenance, and local naming for this nine-row packet, and they do not add any extra theorem coordinate.

Now combine \autoref{thm:audit-map-interface-cross-map-consumer-boundary}, \autoref{thm:audit-map-interface-route-readback-totality}, \autoref{thm:audit-map-interface-conditional-row-discharge-boundary}, \autoref{thm:audit-map-interface-obstruction-row-nonescape}, and \autoref{thm:audit-map-interface-frontier-consumer-routing}. A sibling packet may consume the interface only through the displayed rows and routes, so template instantiation cannot smuggle in hidden imports or already discharged theorem data.
\end{proof}
