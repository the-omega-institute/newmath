\begin{definition}[MetaCIC audit matrix carrier]
\label{def:metacic-audit-matrix-carrier}
A $\MetaCICAuditMatrixUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
M=(A,N,B,S,E,H,C,P,L)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ is the available-row matrix, recording closed-normal consistency, candidate-mediated normalization, local decidable checking, closed substitution, and conditional confluence as positive audit rows;
\item $N$ is the non-claim row matrix, recording full closed consistency, arbitrary closed Church--Rosser, candidate-free normalization, unrestricted beta-conversion decidability, and host substitution semantics as non-exported endpoints;
\item $B$ is the blocked-edge matrix, with the subject-reduction lift as the critical edge from arbitrary closed proof terms to the closed-normal consistency branch;
\item $S$ is the synthesis-consumer row, naming the consumers that may read the matrix before using closed-normal packets, discharge ledgers, or audit-map dependency routes;
\item $E$ is the non-escape ledger, recording that every consumer route must preserve $A,N,B,S$ as displayed rows;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $A,N,B,S,E$ and the structural rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which a consumer reads available rows, non-claim rows, blocked edges, synthesis consumers, and non-escape ledgers in matrix order;

\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for removing the blocked subject-reduction edge, no hidden full-consistency theorem, no host reduction semantics, and no quotient equality of MetaCIC terms.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC audit matrix critical-path route]
\label{thm:metacic-audit-matrix-critical-path-route}
Every accepted $\MetaCICAuditMatrixUp$ carrier routes closed MetaCIC consistency consumers through the matrix order
$$
A \longrightarrow B \longrightarrow S \longrightarrow E.
$$
The available rows $A$ may feed the closed-normal consistency packet and audit-map dependency weave, but any consumer seeking full closed consistency must first read the blocked subject-reduction edge $B$ and the synthesis-consumer row $S$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier and read the continuation row $C$. Its displayed route reads the available matrix rows before the blocked-edge and consumer rows.

Use the provenance row $P$ to identify the sibling surfaces. The closed-normal consistency packet supplies the positive endpoint, the subject-reduction discharge ledger supplies the required preservation slots, and the audit-map dependency weave supplies the cross-map consumer route.

The route cannot bypass $B$. The blocked-edge matrix is a coordinate of the same packet as $A,S,E$, so a full-consistency consumer has to expose it before any synthesis handoff can be read.
\end{proof}

\label{thm:metacic-audit-matrix-nonescape}

$$
