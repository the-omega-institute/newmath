\begin{theorem}[RationalInterval common refinement associativity]
\label{thm:rationalinterval-common-refinement-associativity}
Let $I$, $J$, and $K$ be three compatible accepted
$\RationalIntervalUp$ finite packets. Suppose their displayed pairwise
common-refinement rows share the appropriate middle endpoint windows, so that
one may first refine $I$ with $J$ and then with $K$, or first refine $J$ with
$K$ and then with $I$.

The two iterated refinements determine the same consumer-visible finite
rational-window packet. They expose the same transported $\RatUp$ endpoint
rows, endpoint-order witnesses, containment and refinement rows, dyadic-width
row, $\RegSeqRatUp$ finite-window row, $\RealUp$ finite-window handoff row,
visible $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and local
$\NameCert$ rows. The claim does not assert completed $\RealUp$ equality,
quotient Cauchy classes, located subsets, apartness, interval arithmetic, or
host standard interval data.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Form the two binary common-refinement windows by applying
\autoref{thm:rational-interval-common-refinement-window} to the compatible
pairs. The shared middle endpoint hypotheses put both binary windows over the
same displayed endpoint-order and containment rows.

Compose the refinement rows in the left-associated direction with
\autoref{thm:rational-interval-refinement-composition}. Apply the same theorem
in the right-associated direction. Both composites are therefore finite
$\mathsf{BHist}$ packets whose refinement entries are built from the same
pairwise rows.

Identify the transported endpoint coordinates componentwise by using
\autoref{thm:rational-interval-endpoint-transport-classifier}. Lower
endpoints, upper endpoints, endpoint-order witnesses, containment rows, and
dyadic-width rows agree at the consumer surface because each coordinate is read
through the same transported $\RatUp$ classifier data.

Finally apply
\autoref{thm:rationalinterval-finite-consumer-completeness}. It says every
finite consumer read of the two composites factors through exactly the
displayed rational-window rows, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ observation row, the
$\RealUp$ finite-window handoff row, the visible $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$,
$\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and local $\NameCert$ rows. Hence the two iterated
refinements expose one finite consumer packet and add none of the excluded
completed-real, quotient, located-subset, apartness, arithmetic, or host
standard interval structure.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RationalInterval midpoint bisection window]
\label{thm:rationalinterval-midpoint-bisection-window}
Let $I$ be an accepted $\RationalIntervalUp$ packet with displayed lower
endpoint, upper endpoint, dyadic-width witness, containment ledger, visible
$\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and local
$\NameCert_{\RationalIntervalUp}$ rows. Suppose a midpoint row $m$ is displayed
on the same $\RatUp$ and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint surface, and suppose two
containment subledgers display the left window from the lower endpoint to $m$
and the right window from $m$ to the upper endpoint.

Then the two half-windows are accepted $\RationalIntervalUp$ finite packets.
They share the midpoint boundary row, their endpoint and dyadic-width rows are
read from the same displayed surface, and every $\RegSeqRatUp$ or finite-window
$\RealUp$ consumer read of either half-window is one of the original endpoint,
width, containment, refinement, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$,
$\mathsf{Pkg}$, or local $\NameCert$ rows. Conversely, every original finite
consumer read whose endpoint and containment rows lie in one half factors
through the corresponding half-window packet.

The bisection window exports no completed $\RealUp$ equality, located subset
datum, open cover, quotient Cauchy class, apartness row, interval arithmetic
operation, or standard bridge datum.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.RationalIntervalUp.RationalIntervalPacket\_midpoint\_bisection\_window}
\begin{proof}
Use \autoref{thm:rationalinterval-endpoint-width-ledger} to read the lower
endpoint, upper endpoint, midpoint row, and dyadic-width witnesses from the
same $\RatUp$ and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ surface. Apply
\autoref{thm:rational-interval-endpoint-order-witness} to obtain the displayed
endpoint order for the left and right half-windows.

Next construct the two finite packets. Apply
\autoref{thm:rational-interval-common-refinement-window} to the parent window
and the midpoint row, and compose the resulting refinement routes with
\autoref{thm:rational-interval-refinement-composition}. This gives accepted
left and right $\RationalIntervalUp$ packets whose common endpoint is exactly
the displayed midpoint boundary.

The order in which the parent containment ledger, midpoint row, and two
subledgers are grouped does not affect the consumer-visible packet. This is
\autoref{thm:rationalinterval-common-refinement-associativity}, applied to the
two ways of parenthesizing the same finite refinement data.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:rationalinterval-finite-consumer-completeness}.
Every $\RegSeqRatUp$ or finite-window $\RealUp$ read of a half-window is
exhausted by the listed endpoint, width, containment, refinement,
$\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and local $\NameCert$
rows, and the same exhaustion gives the converse factorization for parent
consumer reads restricted to either half.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RationalInterval refinement choice neutrality]
\label{thm:rationalinterval-refinement-choice-neutrality}
Fix a $\RationalIntervalUp$ endpoint packet over $\mathsf{BHist}$. For any
two common-refinement windows whose endpoint, containment, dyadic-transport,
$\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and local $\NameCert$ rows satisfy the
directed-refinement and associativity hypotheses, the resulting finite consumer
packages for $\RegSeqRatUp$ and $\RealUp$ are $\hsame$-equivalent.

Thus a finite $\RegSeqRatUp$ or $\RealUp$ consumer may use any displayed
common-refinement witness for the fixed endpoint packet without selecting
completed real equality, quotient Cauchy classes, located subsets, apartness,
interval arithmetic, or host standard interval data.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.RationalIntervalUp.RationalIntervalRefinement\_choice\_neutrality}
\leanvariant{BEDC.Derived.RationalIntervalUp.RationalIntervalPacket\_refinement\_choice\_neutrality}
\begin{proof}
Use \autoref{thm:rational-interval-common-refinement-window} to put both
refinement choices over explicit finite endpoint and containment windows, and
use \autoref{thm:rational-interval-directed-refinement-basis} to compare their
directed basis rows inside one displayed finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet.

Compose the comparison routes with
\autoref{thm:rational-interval-refinement-composition}. Then
\autoref{thm:rational-interval-endpoint-transport-classifier} identifies the
endpoint, containment, dyadic-width, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and local
$\NameCert$ rows seen after the displayed $\hsame$ transport.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:rationalinterval-common-refinement-associativity}.
It identifies the two readings at the finite $\RegSeqRatUp$ and $\RealUp$
consumer surface exposed by the fixed endpoint packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RationalInterval bisection refinement nesting]
\label{thm:rationalinterval-bisection-refinement-nesting}
Let $I$ be an accepted $\RationalIntervalUp$ endpoint packet with displayed
lower endpoint, midpoint row, upper endpoint, containment ledger, dyadic-width
rows, visible $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and local
$\NameCert_{\RationalIntervalUp}$ rows. Suppose the midpoint bisection of
\autoref{thm:rationalinterval-midpoint-bisection-window} displays the left and
right half-windows, and suppose either half-window is refined once more by a
finite rational-window packet over the same endpoint surface.

Then the two-step choice factors through one finite rational-window packet over
the original endpoint, midpoint, containment, dyadic-width, $\mathsf{BHist}$,
$\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and local $\NameCert$ rows. Every
$\RegSeqRatUp$ or finite-window $\RealUp$ consumer of the nested half-window
reads this packet, and changing the displayed common-refinement witness changes
only the $\hsame$ representative of the same finite consumer package.

The nesting claim does not assert completed $\RealUp$ equality, quotient Cauchy
classes, located subsets, apartness, interval arithmetic, or host interval
data.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply \autoref{thm:rationalinterval-midpoint-bisection-window}. It
constructs the accepted left and right half-window packets from the parent
endpoint, midpoint, containment, dyadic-width, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$,
$\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and local $\NameCert$ rows.

Next compose the chosen half-window refinement with the parent bisection route.
The finite parenthesization of that two-step route is exactly the content of
\autoref{thm:rationalinterval-common-refinement-associativity}; it repacks the
displayed endpoint and containment rows as one rational-window packet over the
original endpoint surface.

Finally use \autoref{thm:rationalinterval-refinement-choice-neutrality}. Any
alternate displayed common-refinement witness for the same nested half-window
has the same finite $\RegSeqRatUp$ and $\RealUp$ consumer package up to
$\hsame$. Since all rows used by the construction are the listed finite BEDC
rows, no completed-real equality, quotient class, located-subset datum,
apartness row, interval operation, or host interval structure is exported.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RationalInterval refinement normal form]
\label{thm:rationalinterval-refinement-normal-form}
Any finite tree of accepted $\RationalIntervalUp$ containment,
bisection, and common-refinement reads consumed by $\RegSeqRatUp$ or by a
finite-window $\RealUp$ consumer factors through one left-associated directed
rational-window basis packet. The packet carries the same displayed $\RatUp$
lower and upper endpoint rows, dyadic-width rows, containment ledgers,
$\mathsf{BHist}$ entries, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and local
$\NameCert_{\RationalIntervalUp}$ rows as the original tree.

Every $\RegSeqRatUp$ or finite-window $\RealUp$ read of the tree is therefore a
read of this left-associated basis packet, and changing the displayed common
refinement or bisection witnesses changes only the representative of the same
finite consumer package. The normal form exports no completed $\RealUp$
equality, quotient Cauchy class, located subset, apartness row, interval
arithmetic, or host standard interval.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.RationalIntervalUp.RationalIntervalPacket\_refinement\_normal\_form}
\begin{proof}
Induct on the finite refinement tree. A leaf is already an accepted
$\RationalIntervalUp$ rational-window packet, so its endpoint, width,
containment, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and
$\NameCert$ rows give the required basis packet.

For a binary common-refinement node, apply
\autoref{thm:rational-interval-common-refinement-window} to place the two child
packets over a common finite rational window. Then use
\autoref{thm:rational-interval-directed-refinement-basis} to choose the
left-associated directed basis for the finite family of endpoint and
containment rows.

For a nested refinement node, compose the child basis route with the displayed
outer route by \autoref{thm:rational-interval-refinement-composition}. The
rebracketing needed to keep the basis left-associated is exactly
\autoref{thm:rationalinterval-common-refinement-associativity}.

For a bisection node, apply
\autoref{thm:rationalinterval-midpoint-bisection-window} to expose the two
half-window packets and then use the induction hypothesis on the selected
half. The rows remain over the same endpoint, midpoint, dyadic-width,
containment, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and local
$\NameCert$ surface.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:rationalinterval-refinement-choice-neutrality}. It
identifies all displayed witness choices at the finite $\RegSeqRatUp$ and
$\RealUp$ consumer surface. Since every step only repacks the listed finite
rational-window rows, the excluded completed-real, quotient, located-subset,
apartness, arithmetic, and host-standard structures are not exported.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RationalInterval normal form terminal seal compatibility]
\label{thm:rationalinterval-normal-form-terminal-seal-compatibility}
Let $T$ be any finite $\RationalIntervalUp$ containment, bisection, and
common-refinement tree whose terminal rows are consumed by $\RegSeqRatUp$ or by
a finite-window $\RealUp$ read. Replacing $T$ by the left-associated directed
normal form of \autoref{thm:rationalinterval-refinement-normal-form} preserves
the terminal seal surface exactly: the visible $\RatUp$ lower and upper
endpoints, dyadic-width rows, containment and refinement ledgers,
$\mathsf{BHist}$ entries, $\hsame$ rows, $\Cont$ routes, $\mathsf{Pkg}$
provenance, and local $\NameCert_{\RationalIntervalUp}$ rows are the same
finite rows for every terminal consumer.

Thus any $\RegSeqRatUp$ consumer and any finite-window $\RealUp$ consumer sees
the same terminal packet before and after normal-form replacement. The
compatibility statement exports no completed $\RealUp$ equality, quotient
Cauchy class, located subset, apartness row, interval arithmetic operation, or
host interval datum.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:rationalinterval-refinement-normal-form} to factor the
finite tree through the left-associated directed rational-window basis. The
factorization is defined only by repacking the displayed endpoint,
dyadic-width, containment, refinement, history, transport, continuation,
package, and local certificate rows.

For a $\RegSeqRatUp$ terminal read, use
\autoref{thm:rationalinterval-finite-consumer-completeness}. That theorem says
that every finite consumer read factors through the displayed packet rows, so
the normal form has the same seal surface as the original tree at the
$\RegSeqRatUp$ boundary.

For a finite-window $\RealUp$ terminal read, use
\autoref{thm:rationalinterval-nested-consumer-window-factorization}. It
restricts the read to the nested finite rational-window rows and hence to the
same endpoint, width, ledger, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$,
$\mathsf{Pkg}$, and $\NameCert$ coordinates preserved by the normal form.

The construction never introduces coordinates for completed real equality,
quotient Cauchy classes, located subsets, apartness, interval arithmetic, or
host interval data. Those objects therefore remain outside the terminal seal
surface.
\end{proof}
