\begin{definition}[RegularCauchySubspace carrier]
\label{def:regular-cauchy-subspace-carrier}

$$

\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window schedule that opens only the windows needed to test the displayed subspace route.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger recording the dyadic bounds, window radii, and comparison rows consumed by $Q$ and $S$.

\begin{theorem}[RegularCauchySubspace NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-subspace-namecert-obligations}

$$

the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchySubspaceUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission, regular rational readback, finite StreamName window exposure, DyadicRatCore tolerance exactness, displayed subspace-predicate evidence, CauchyEquivalenceSetoid classifier transport, Real-seal routing, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, package provenance, local naming, and non-escape from the displayed rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

The finite observation route is forced before the subspace predicate can be consumed. The $\StreamNameUp$ schedule supplies the inspected windows, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger supplies the tolerance comparisons, and the $\RegSeqRatUp$ row packages those observations as the regular rational name to which $A$ applies.

Classifier transport is then read only through $K$ and $H$. The $\CauchyEquivalenceSetoidUp$ row compares accepted displayed names through the same regular readback, tolerance ledger, and subspace predicate evidence; componentwise $\hsame$ preserves those coordinates without adding a quotient real equality row.

Finally, the Real seal $E$ is downstream of $Q,S,D,A,K$, while $C,P,N$ only replay, source, and name the same route. Since the carrier has no coordinate for selected limits, ambient completeness, host real equality, countable choice, or an external subspace oracle, the local NameCert exports none of those objects.
