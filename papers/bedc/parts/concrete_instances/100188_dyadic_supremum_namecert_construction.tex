$$

\begin{enumerate}

\item $A$ is the lower-approximation ledger, recording finite dyadic witnesses that every demanded lower approximation is met by an inspected family entry.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window row naming exactly the finite family windows inspected by the upper-bound and lower-approximation ledgers.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row, recording the dyadic scales at which the bound and approximation comparisons are read.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row, translating the displayed dyadic ledgers into regular rational approximation data without selecting a completed real.

\begin{theorem}[DyadicSupremum NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:dyadic-supremum-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted carrier $S=(B,A,W,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)$ of \autoref{def:dyadic-supremum-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\DyadicSupremumUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the bounded dyadic family row $B$, lower-approximation ledger $A$, $\StreamNameUp$ window row $W$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row $D$, $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row $R$, $\RealUp$ seal boundary $E$, and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$;
\item upper-bound exposure, stating that every exported upper-bound read of the packet is classified through the displayed dyadic family ledger $B$ and tolerance row $D$ before it reaches the Real seal boundary;
\item lower-approximation exposure, stating that every exported lower read factors through a displayed witness in $A$ inside one of the windows named by $W$;
\item finite-window stability, stating that transport through $H$ and replay through $C$ preserve the same inspected windows, dyadic scales, upper-bound rows, and lower-approximation witnesses;
\item dyadic tolerance exactness, stating that all supremum comparisons use only the dyadic scales and comparison rows displayed in $D$;
\item regular rational handoff, stating that $R$ is the only row allowed to translate the dyadic ledgers into regular rational approximation data for downstream Real-facing consumers;
\item Real seal non-escape, stating that $E$ is a boundary row consumed only after $B,A,W,D,R,H,C,P,N$ have been retained;
\item package provenance and local naming, stating that $P$ names exactly the listed sibling certificates and $N$ names the same finite packet as a local $\NameCert_{\DyadicSupremumUp}$.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored outside those rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The rows $B,A,W,D,R,E$ are the analytic coordinates, while $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite packet.

Read the classifier in dependency order. An upper-bound request first reads the bounded dyadic family row $B$ and the dyadic tolerance ledger $D$. A lower-approximation request first reads the witness ledger $A$, the finite windows $W$, and the same dyadic tolerance row $D$.

The regular rational handoff is downstream of those finite ledgers. The row $R$ can only translate the displayed dyadic comparisons into regular rational approximation data; it does not select a completed supremum or import an order-completeness principle.

The Real-facing row is the final boundary. Since $E$ is stored only after $B,A,W,D,R$, a consumer that bypasses one of those rows has not obtained a read of the packet's Real seal boundary.

The structural rows add no hidden supremum principle. Transport through $H$, replay through $C$, provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$ preserve the displayed route, so the listed rows exhaust the seed NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicSupremum located-supremum handoff]
\label{thm:dyadic-supremum-located-cut-handoff}

$$

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted carrier $S=(B,A,W,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)$. The only family coordinate is $B$, the only lower-approximation coordinate is $A$, and the only inspected windows and comparison scales are $W$ and $D$.

For an upper-bound read, project $B$ and $D$. The exported bound is read from the displayed dyadic family ledger and the dyadic comparison rows at the named scales. \end{proof}
