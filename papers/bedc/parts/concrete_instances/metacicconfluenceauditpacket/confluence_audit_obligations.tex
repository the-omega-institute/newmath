\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC confluence audit critical-pair obligations]
\label{thm:metacic-confluence-audit-critical-pair-obligations}
Let $A=(D,C,N,A_0,S,R,H,T,P,L)$ be an accepted $\MetaCICConfluenceAuditPacketUp$ carrier. Every candidate strong-normalization or critical-pair consumer of $A$ must first expose the finite rows $D,C,A_0,S,H,T,P,L$ as $\BHist$ entries transported by $\hsame$ and replayed through $\Cont$. The consumer may classify a residual critical-pair face only through those rows and the local $\NameCert$ row $L$; it may not cite an ambient confluence proof or a hidden subject-reduction discharge.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier through \autoref{def:metacic-confluence-audit-packet-carrier}. The critical-pair surface is named by the conditional confluence rows $D,C$, the atom and source-shape fragments $A_0$, and the substitution-boundary row $S$.

Transport and replay are constrained by $H,T,P,L$. These rows keep the read inside displayed $\BHist$ data and prevent a consumer from replacing the critical-pair face by a host reduction theorem.

The subject-reduction row $R$ is a handoff pointer, not a confluence row. Hence candidate strong-normalization and critical-pair consumers receive the listed finite audit rows only, with no hidden proof of confluence or subject reduction.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC confluence audit residual stability]
\label{thm:metacic-confluence-audit-residual-stability}
Let $A=(D,C,N,A_0,S,R,H,T,P,L)$ be an accepted $\MetaCICConfluenceAuditPacketUp$ carrier. Residual substitution readings of $A$ are stable under componentwise $\hsame$ transport exactly when the substitution-boundary row $S$, the conditional bridge rows $D,C$, the closed-face row $N$, and the replay rows $H,T,P,L$ are transported together. This stability is a NameCert-row claim over finite $\BHist$ data, not a proof of $\mathsf{BetaParallelSubstitutionCompatibility}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the residual-substitution boundary as the row $S$. A transported read must also carry $D,C$ for the conditional bridge, $N$ for the closed face, and $H,T,P,L$ for componentwise comparison, replay, provenance, and local naming.

If these rows are transported together, the residual read names the same finite audit face after replacement. If any one of them is absent, the replay route cannot determine which substitution boundary the consumer is reading.

The result is therefore row stability. Since the carrier still has no coordinate proving $\mathsf{BetaParallelSubstitutionCompatibility}$, stable residual transport does not become the missing substitution theorem.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC confluence audit decidability handoff]
\label{thm:metacic-confluence-audit-decidability-handoff}
Let $A=(D,C,N,A_0,S,R,H,T,P,L)$ be an accepted $\MetaCICConfluenceAuditPacketUp$ carrier. Any confluence decidability consumer receives a finite handoff consisting of the candidate obstruction family over $D,C,A_0,S,R$, the closed-face rows $N,S,H,T,P,L$, and the local $\NameCert$ row. The handoff may decide only which displayed audit face is available or obstructed; it does not decide unrestricted confluence, subject reduction, closed Church--Rosser, or full substitution compatibility.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the finite carrier inventory. The candidate obstruction family is read from $D,C,A_0,S,R$, while the closed-face consumer also reads $N,S,H,T,P,L$.

The classifier can therefore branch only over displayed audit faces: conditional bridge availability, residual-substitution boundary, closed-face coverage, and subject-reduction separation. Each branch is a finite $\BHist$ row with a local NameCert read.

No branch contains the excluded metatheorems. The handoff gives a decidable finite audit classification for the packet, not unrestricted confluence, subject reduction, closed Church--Rosser, or full substitution compatibility.
\end{proof}
