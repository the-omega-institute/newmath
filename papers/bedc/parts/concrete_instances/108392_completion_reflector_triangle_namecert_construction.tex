\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CompletionReflectorTriangleUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-completion-reflector-triangle-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Mac Lane's adjunction triangle identities and the Bishop--Bridges construction of Cauchy completion, the $\CompletionReflectorTriangleUp$ packet records the finite BEDC rows needed to read the unit, counit, and idempotent replay of the L10 completion reflector as one public triangle. It depends on the reflector packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchycompletionreflector-namecert}, the unit packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchycompletionunit-namecert}, the counit packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-completion-counit-namecert}, the idempotent packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-completion-idempotent-namecert}, the universal-property packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completionuniversalproperty-namecert}, and the reflection packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completionreflection-namecert}. Its real-facing route is read through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-cauchy-completion-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet is a finite naming certificate for the triangle surface; it is not quotient completion equality, not a selected limit, not host equality, not countable choice, not ambient completeness, and not detached $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[CompletionReflectorTriangle carrier]
\label{def:completion-reflector-triangle-carrier}
A $\CompletionReflectorTriangleUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(S,U,M,Q,I,E,R,T,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the source row for the displayed metric, regular-Cauchy, or real-name data entering the completion reflector.
\item $U$ is the $\CauchyCompletionUnitUp$ row admitting the source into the completion-facing surface.
\item $M$ is the completion-object row, read through the $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ and reflector surfaces rather than through a host quotient carrier.
\item $Q$ is the $\CauchyCompletionCounitUp$ row returning already completed or real-sealed reads through the displayed route.
\item $I$ is the $\CauchyCompletionIdempotentUp$ replay row for a second completion pass over the same completion-object row.
\item $E$ is the $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$ extension row consumed after the unit face is visible.
\item $R$ is the $\CompletionReflectionUp$ reflection row consumed after the unit, counit, idempotent, and extension faces are visible.
\item $T$ is the local transport row through $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, and $\RealUp$ windows.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the source, unit, completion object, counit, idempotent replay, extension, reflection, transport, continuation, provenance, and naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay along the unit-counit-idempotent triangle and through the local real-name transport row.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CompletionReflectorTriangleUp$, $\CauchyCompletionReflectorUp$, $\CauchyCompletionUnitUp$, $\CauchyCompletionCounitUp$, $\CauchyCompletionIdempotentUp$, $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$, $\CompletionReflectionUp$, $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CompletionReflectorTriangleUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier on accepted packets compares only these twelve rows under the displayed transport and replay data.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CompletionReflectorTriangle NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:completion-reflector-triangle-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CompletionReflectorTriangleUp$ carrier $K=(S,U,M,Q,I,E,R,T,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CompletionReflectorTriangleUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the displayed source, unit, completion-object, counit, idempotent-replay, extension, reflection, transport, continuation, provenance, and naming rows; unit-first readability for completion-facing source reads; counit return for already completed or real-sealed reads; idempotent replay over the same completion-object row; extension and reflection consumption over the displayed triangle route; and non-escape from quotient completions, selected limits, host equality, countable choice, ambient completeness, and detached $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:completion-reflector-triangle-carrier}. The packet displays $S,U,M,Q,I,E,R,T,H,C,P,N$ and no other row.

The unit face reads $S$ through $U$ before the completion object $M$ is consumed. The counit face reads $Q$ only for data already on the completed or real-sealed side, and the idempotent face reads $I$ over the same $M$ row.

The extension and reflection faces are downstream of that route. The row $E$ is consumed as the finite universal-property extension, and $R$ is consumed as the reflection row over the same $S,U,M,Q,I,T$ surface.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name exactly the displayed route. Since no coordinate stores quotient completions, selected limits, host equality, countable choice, ambient completeness, or detached real completeness, the stated non-escape boundary follows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CompletionReflectorTriangle unit-counit coherence]
\label{thm:completion-reflector-triangle-unit-counit-coherence}
Let $K=(S,U,M,Q,I,E,R,T,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CompletionReflectorTriangleUp$ carrier. The two triangle readings
$$
S\longmapsto U\longmapsto M\longmapsto I\longmapsto E,R\longmapsto H,C,P,N
$$
and
$$
S\longmapsto T\longmapsto Q\longmapsto I\longmapsto E,R\longmapsto H,C,P,N
$$
meet at the same displayed idempotent, extension, reflection, transport-replay, provenance, and naming rows. Thus a consumer that first enters completion by the unit face and a consumer that first reads the real-name transport before counit return receive the same finite $\NameCert_{\CompletionReflectorTriangleUp}$ boundary, with no quotient completion equality, selected limit, host equality, countable choice, ambient completeness, or detached $\RealUp$ completeness exported.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Expose the carrier rows from \autoref{def:completion-reflector-triangle-carrier}. The only completion-facing rows are $S,U,M,Q,I,E,R,T,H,C,P,N$.

The first displayed reading is the unit leg: the source is admitted by $U$, the completion object $M$ is read, and repeated completion is replayed by $I$ before $E$ and $R$ are consumed.

The second displayed reading is the transport-counit leg: the source is read through $T$, returned by $Q$ when already completed or real-sealed, and then replayed by the same $I$ before $E$ and $R$ are consumed.

Both readings end in the same $H,C,P,N$ rows. Componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local $\NameCert$ row therefore identify the two readings as one finite triangle boundary. The excluded completion, selection, host-equality, choice, ambient-completeness, and detached-real-completeness data have no row in the carrier.
\end{proof}
