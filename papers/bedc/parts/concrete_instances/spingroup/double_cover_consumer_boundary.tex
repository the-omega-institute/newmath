\begin{theorem}[SpinGroup root double-cover consumer exhaustion]
\label{thm:spingroup-root-double-cover-consumer-exhaustion}
A downstream $\PinGroupUp$ or $\CliffordUp$ consumer of the $\SpinGroupUp$ root
double-cover boundary can read only the visible Clifford unit source, the
parity classifier, inverse and product witnesses, the double-cover fiber
classifier, finite $\Cont$ readbacks, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance. These are
exactly the rows exposed by
\autoref{def:spingroup-bhist-carrier-packet},
\autoref{def:spingroup-root-bhist-carrier},
\autoref{thm:spingroup-root-namecert-threshold-package},
\autoref{thm:spingroup-double-cover-fiber-classifier}, and
\autoref{thm:spingroup-namecert-obligation-surface}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project a consumer read through
\autoref{thm:spingroup-namecert-obligation-surface}. The obligation surface
lists the carrier, classifier, stability, exactness, and ledger rows available
to any downstream consumer.

Apply \autoref{thm:spingroup-root-namecert-threshold-package} to the root part.
It confines carrier reads to the visible Clifford unit source, parity ledger,
inverse and product witnesses, finite $\Cont$ transport, $\hsame$ comparison,
and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance. The concrete packet definitions provide no
other constructor to inspect.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:spingroup-double-cover-fiber-classifier} to the
projection boundary. The only extra classifier available there is the
double-cover fiber classifier, and it is again a displayed finite
$\mathsf{BHist}$ row. Repacking the projected rows gives exactly the consumer
surface named in the statement, with no host quotient, ambient orthogonal group
equality, analytic covering datum, or standard-bridge datum.
\end{proof}
