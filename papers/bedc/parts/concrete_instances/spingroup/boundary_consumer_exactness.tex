\begin{theorem}[SpinGroup double-cover fiber ledger]
\label{thm:spingroup-double-cover-fiber-ledger}
The double-cover fiber ledger row exposed by $\SpinGroupUp$ is exactly the
Clifford-unit comparison, the parity $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ row, the
orthogonal-action endpoint, the $\Cont$ projection witness, and the
$\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance. These five rows are the fiber face of
\autoref{def:spingroup-bhist-carrier-packet} and
\autoref{thm:spingroup-double-cover-fiber-classifier}; no host sign quotient
or ambient orthogonal-group equality is part of the ledger.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SpinGroupUp.SpinGroupRootCarrier\_double\_cover\_fiber\_ledger}
\begin{proof}
Project the carried packet through
\autoref{def:spingroup-bhist-carrier-packet}. The visible coordinates include
the Clifford unit data, parity history, action endpoint, continuation witness,
and package provenance.

Apply \autoref{thm:spingroup-double-cover-fiber-classifier}. It compares the
two carried spin endpoints only by the Clifford-unit comparison and the shared
orthogonal-action endpoint recorded in the package ledger.

The parity row and the $\Cont$ witness are the transport data that make this
comparison a fiber row. Since the classifier theorem exposes no other
constructor, the five displayed rows exhaust the ledger.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SpinGroup conjugation action readback]
\label{thm:spingroup-conjugation-action-readback}
The action row in \autoref{def:spingroup-bhist-carrier-packet} reads
conjugation only from the displayed Clifford product rows, Clifford inverse
rows, and $\hsame$ transport. It does not read a host action, a matrix action,
or an unrecorded orthogonal representation outside the $\SpinGroupUp$ packet.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SpinGroupUp.SpinGroupRootCarrier\_conjugation\_action\_readback}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the action coordinate of
\autoref{def:spingroup-bhist-carrier-packet}. Its source rows are the
Clifford-unit endpoint, the multiplication ledger, the inverse ledger, and the
displayed $\hsame$ transports between the selected endpoints.

Use \autoref{thm:spingroup-group-consumer-boundary} to restrict downstream
reads to Clifford unit, multiplication, inverse, double-cover classifier, and
provenance rows. The conjugation action is therefore reconstructed from the
product-inverse-product spine already present in the packet.

The $\hsame$ rows transport the readback between classifier-equal endpoints.
No host action row is available after this projection, so the action readback
is exactly the displayed Clifford product and inverse readback.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SpinGroup boundary consumer exactness]
\label{thm:spingroup-boundary-consumer-exactness}
Every $\PinGroupUp$, $\LieGroupUp$, or $\CliffordUp$ consumer of the
$\SpinGroupUp$ boundary reads precisely the exported Clifford-unit source,
parity classifier, multiplication and inverse rows, conjugation readback,
double-cover fiber ledger, finite $\Cont$ witnesses, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$
provenance. No host orthogonal group, host quotient, or additional Clifford
carrier row is exported.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with \autoref{thm:spingroup-root-double-cover-consumer-exhaustion}. It
already confines root double-cover consumers to the visible Clifford unit,
parity, inverse, product, fiber classifier, continuation, and provenance rows.

Apply \autoref{thm:spingroup-double-cover-fiber-ledger} to replace the fiber
classifier by its exact ledger rows. Apply
\autoref{thm:spingroup-conjugation-action-readback} for the action coordinate;
that readback contributes only Clifford product, inverse, and $\hsame$
transport rows.

Finally use \autoref{thm:spingroup-group-consumer-boundary}. Its consumer
projection admits the same rows for $\PinGroupUp$, $\LieGroupUp$, and
$\CliffordUp$ consumers and admits no host orthogonal group or quotient row.
Thus the exported boundary is exact.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SpinGroupUp.SpinGroupRootCarrier\_boundary\_consumer\_exactness}
