\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RegularCauchyNonzeroUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-nonzero-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges constructive analysis, the $\RegularCauchyNonzeroUp$ packet names the finite nonzero-domain evidence needed between regular Cauchy rational sources and guarded reciprocal or division consumers. It binds the $\RegSeqRatUp$ source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the finite $\StreamNameUp$ inspection window of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the dyadic lower-bound ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the reciprocal consumer of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-reciprocal-namecert}, the division consumer of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-division-namecert}, and the terminal $\RealUp$ seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The chapter records only displayed finite apartness from zero for a regular Cauchy name; it is not real trichotomy and not a total reciprocal operation.

\begin{definition}[Regular Cauchy nonzero carrier]
\label{def:regular-cauchy-nonzero-carrier}
A $\RegularCauchyNonzeroUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Z=(Q,A,W,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $Q$ is the accepted $\RegSeqRatUp$ source row whose regular rational tail is being inspected.
\item $A$ is the apartness witness row displaying that the inspected tail is separated from zero by a positive rational bound.
\item $W$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window opened from $Q$ at the precision and tail indices required by $A$.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ lower-bound ledger recording the dyadic comparison, radius budget, and positive lower endpoint read from $W$.
\item $R$ is the reciprocal-domain handoff row offered to $\RegularCauchyReciprocalUp$ and $\RegularCauchyDivisionUp$ consumers.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal receiving the same finite nonzero-domain packet without adding trichotomy or selected representatives.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $Q,A,W,D,R,E,C,P,N$.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay for source inspection, apartness exposure, finite-window opening, dyadic lower-bound reading, reciprocal-domain handoff, and Real seal handoff.
\item $P$ records $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RegularCauchyNonzeroUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegularCauchyReciprocalUp$, $\RegularCauchyDivisionUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyNonzeroUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The local classifier compares accepted carriers only through the displayed rows $Q,A,W,D,R,E,H,C,P,N$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy nonzero NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-nonzero-namecert-obligations}
The seed $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyNonzeroUp}$ surface for \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-nonzero-carrier} consists exactly of regular Cauchy source exposure, apartness-witness exposure, finite StreamName window exposure, DyadicRatCore lower-bound ledger exposure, reciprocal-domain handoff, terminal Real seal handoff, componentwise transport, displayed continuation replay, package provenance, and the local naming row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier $Z=(Q,A,W,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)$. The coordinates list the regular Cauchy source, apartness witness, finite window, dyadic lower-bound ledger, reciprocal-domain handoff, Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local name.

The analytic rows are ordered by dependency. The window row $W$ is opened from $Q$ under the apartness witness $A$, while the dyadic ledger $D$ records the positive lower-bound computation read from that finite window.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite route. Since every listed obligation is a coordinate or a projection from a coordinate, the seed NameCert surface is exhausted by the displayed packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy nonzero apartness window]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-nonzero-apartness-window}
For every accepted $\RegularCauchyNonzeroUp$ carrier $Z=(Q,A,W,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, every nonzero-domain read factors through
$$
Q\longmapsto A\longmapsto W\longmapsto D.
$$
A route that opens a reciprocal or division consumer without the displayed apartness witness $A$ and lower-bound ledger $D$ remains outside the local classifier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the source row $Q$ and apartness witness $A$. The witness is the only row asserting a positive separation from zero for the inspected regular Cauchy tail.

Next open the finite window $W$. Its entries are the only regular rational data inspected by the certificate, and the dyadic ledger $D$ is a finite lower-bound read from that window.

The local classifier admits no row that bypasses $A,W,D$. Therefore every accepted nonzero-domain read reaches the lower-bound ledger through the displayed source, witness, and window route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy nonzero reciprocal-domain handoff]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-nonzero-reciprocal-domain-handoff}
Every $\RegularCauchyReciprocalUp$ or $\RegularCauchyDivisionUp$ consumer of an accepted $\RegularCauchyNonzeroUp$ packet reaches its denominator-domain row through the same handoff
$$
(Q,A,W,D)\longmapsto R.
$$
The handoff exports finite apartness evidence and a dyadic lower-bound ledger, but it exports no total reciprocal, no quotient stream equality, no real trichotomy, and no ambient $\RealUp$ completeness witness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the apartness-window theorem to fix the source, witness, window, and lower-bound ledger rows. These rows are the complete analytic source of the nonzero-domain claim.

The handoff row $R$ is downstream of $D$. A reciprocal consumer reads $R$ as the denominator-domain guard for \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-reciprocal-namecert}, and a division consumer reads the same row before the reciprocal-product route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-division-namecert}.

Finally read the Real seal $E$ and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$. None of these rows adds total inversion, quotient equality, trichotomy, or completeness data, so the handoff exports only the displayed finite nonzero-domain certificate.
\end{proof}
