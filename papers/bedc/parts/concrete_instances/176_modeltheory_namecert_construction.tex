\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ModelTheoryUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-modeltheory-namecert}

The model-theory interface $\ModelTheoryUp$ packages a structure for a first-order signature, satisfaction of formulas, elementary equivalence, and elementary substructures. It depends on $\NameCert_{\FirstOrderUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-firstorder-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:modeltheory-carrier}

\begin{definition}[ModelTheoryUp BHist structure packet]\label{def:modeltheory-bhist-structure-packet}
A $\ModelTheoryUp$ source packet is a finite displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ tuple
$(S,A,\mathsf{Val},\mathsf{Sat},\mathsf{Elem},\rho,\lambda)$. The row $S$ is
the $\FirstOrderUp$ signature and formula row, $A$ is the displayed structure
row, $\mathsf{Val}$ records variable-assignment observations, $\mathsf{Sat}$
records the satisfaction ledgers for named formulas and assignments, and
$\mathsf{Elem}$ records elementary-equivalence and elementary-substructure
faces. The row $\rho$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance tying the packet to the visible
certificate, and $\lambda$ is the $\Cont$ and $\hsame$ transport ledger.

Acceptance of the packet exports only these displayed rows. A host
satisfaction relation, an ambient model equality, or an external comparison
of structures is not part of the carrier.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[ModelTheoryUp satisfaction ledger classifier]\label{def:modeltheory-satisfaction-ledger-classifier}
Two $\ModelTheoryUp$ source packets classify the same row exactly when
$\hsame$ transports their $\FirstOrderUp$ signature rows, displayed carrier
rows, valuation rows, satisfaction ledgers, elementary-equivalence rows, and
$\Pkg$ provenance through the same $\Cont$ ledger. The comparison is therefore
rowwise: formula and substitution reads move through the $\FirstOrderUp$
source, assignments move through $\mathsf{Val}$, truth records move through
$\mathsf{Sat}$, and elementary faces move through $\mathsf{Elem}$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[ModelTheoryUp satisfaction exactness row]\label{def:modeltheory-satisfaction-exactness-row}
The satisfaction exactness row of a $\ModelTheoryUp$ packet is the finite
$\mathsf{BHist}$ readback row whose entries are triples consisting of a
formula endpoint, an assignment endpoint, and a truth ledger face. Such an
entry is accepted exactly when the formula endpoint is read through the
$\FirstOrderUp$ row $S$, the assignment endpoint is read through
$\mathsf{Val}$, and the truth ledger is one of the displayed
$\mathsf{Sat}$ faces carried by the same packet.

The row is exported only with its packet provenance $\rho$ and its comparison
ledger $\lambda$. It does not contain a host satisfaction predicate, a
semantic evaluation function, or an equality of ambient structures.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[ModelTheoryUp satisfaction exactness scope]\label{thm:modeltheory-satisfaction-exactness-scope}
Every readback accepted by
\autoref{def:modeltheory-satisfaction-exactness-row} is scoped by the displayed
rows $S$, $\mathsf{Val}$, $\mathsf{Sat}$, the packet provenance $\rho$, the
transport ledger $\lambda$, and the available $\hsame$ comparisons. No
accepted satisfaction readback requires a host satisfaction relation.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ModelTheoryUp.ModelTheoryBHistStructurePacket\_satisfaction\_exactness\_scope}
\begin{proof}
Project an accepted readback from the exactness row. Its first coordinate is a
formula endpoint, so the packet carrier and
\autoref{thm:modeltheory-firstorder-dependency-surface} place it in the
$\FirstOrderUp$ row $S$ rather than in an independent syntax source.

Project the second coordinate. By the defining acceptance clause it is a
displayed assignment read from $\mathsf{Val}$, with the same packet provenance
$\rho$ and the same comparison ledger $\lambda$.

Project the third coordinate. It is one of the displayed $\mathsf{Sat}$ faces,
and any comparison of that face is supplied by the rowwise $\hsame$ data of
\autoref{def:modeltheory-satisfaction-ledger-classifier}. Repacking these
three projections gives the stated scope and leaves no place for a host
satisfaction relation.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ModelTheoryUp elementary ledger coverage]\label{thm:modeltheory-elementary-ledger-coverage}
The elementary-equivalence and elementary-substructure requests admitted by a
$\ModelTheoryUp$ packet are covered exactly by the displayed $\mathsf{Elem}$
faces whose formula endpoints are read through $S$ and whose satisfaction
references pass through \autoref{def:modeltheory-satisfaction-exactness-row}.
No elementary request is accepted outside those faces.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For an admitted elementary request, project the packet carrier of
\autoref{def:modeltheory-bhist-structure-packet}. The request must be one of
the finite $\mathsf{Elem}$ faces, because elementary-equivalence and
elementary-substructure data are exported only by that row.

Inside that face, each formula endpoint is a read through the first-order row
$S$. Apply \autoref{thm:modeltheory-firstorder-dependency-surface} to identify
that endpoint with the $\FirstOrderUp$ syntax surface, and apply
\autoref{def:modeltheory-satisfaction-exactness-row} to the corresponding
satisfaction record.

Conversely, a displayed $\mathsf{Elem}$ face whose formula endpoints lie in
$S$ and whose satisfaction references are accepted by the exactness row is
already one of the exported elementary ledger entries of the carrier packet.
Repacking the face gives the elementary request, and no further row is
available from the carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ModelTheoryUp carrier classifier stability]\label{thm:modeltheory-carrier-classifier-stability}
Classifier transport preserves both the satisfaction exactness row and the
elementary ledger. The preservation is rowwise: formula endpoints, assignment
endpoints, satisfaction faces, elementary faces, provenance, and comparison
ledgers are transported by $\hsame$, and entrywise $\Cont$ comparison
identifies the transported ledger entries with the displayed target entries.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the classifier relation of
\autoref{def:modeltheory-satisfaction-ledger-classifier}. It gives rowwise
$\hsame$ transport for $S$, $A$, $\mathsf{Val}$, $\mathsf{Sat}$,
$\mathsf{Elem}$, $\rho$, and the $\Cont$ ledger $\lambda$.

For a satisfaction exactness entry, transport its formula endpoint along the
$S$ comparison, its assignment endpoint along the $\mathsf{Val}$ comparison,
and its truth face along the $\mathsf{Sat}$ comparison. Entrywise $\Cont$
comparison identifies the transported provenance with the target packet, so
the transported triple is accepted by the target exactness row.

For an elementary ledger entry, transport the displayed $\mathsf{Elem}$ face
and then apply the previous paragraph to every satisfaction reference inside
that face. The resulting face has the same formula scope and the same
entrywise ledger comparison in the target packet, so the classifier preserves
the elementary ledger as well.
\end{proof}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:modeltheory-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[ModelTheoryUp FirstOrder dependency surface]\label{thm:modeltheory-firstorder-dependency-surface}
Every admitted $\ModelTheoryUp$ packet projects its signature row,
term/formula grammar row, and substitution-read rows through
$\NameCert_{\FirstOrderUp}$. The structure, valuation, satisfaction, and
elementary rows may consume those projected first-order endpoints, but they
do not export an independent first-order syntax source.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Invert the carried packet of
\autoref{def:modeltheory-bhist-structure-packet}. The first projection is the
row $S$, so the available symbols, terms, formulas, and substitution reads
are exactly the rows already visible from $\NameCert_{\FirstOrderUp}$.

Project the displayed structure row $A$ and the valuation row
$\mathsf{Val}$. Each assignment observation names variables and formula
endpoints by referring back to $S$, so no separate grammar or substitution
operation appears in the model-theory carrier.

Finally project the satisfaction and elementary ledgers. Their formula
occurrences are read through the same $S$ row, and their provenance is the
same $\Pkg$ row $\rho$. Repacking these projections gives the admitted
$\ModelTheoryUp$ packet with the first-order dependency surface unchanged.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ModelTheoryUp.ModelTheoryBHistStructurePacket\_firstorder\_dependency\_surface}

\begin{theorem}[ModelTheoryUp elementary transport]\label{thm:modeltheory-elementary-equivalence-transport}
If two admitted $\ModelTheoryUp$ packets are related by $\hsame$ transport of
their $\FirstOrderUp$ rows and satisfaction ledgers, then their
elementary-equivalence and elementary-substructure rows transport as
displayed $\mathsf{Elem}$ rows. This transport uses no host model equality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the classifier relation of
\autoref{def:modeltheory-satisfaction-ledger-classifier}. It supplies the
$\hsame$ rows for the first-order source, the displayed structure row, the
valuation ledger, the satisfaction ledger, the elementary ledger, and the
$\Pkg$ provenance.

Use the transported $\FirstOrderUp$ row to move formula endpoints and
substitution reads. Use the transported $\mathsf{Sat}$ row to move each
satisfaction record for the corresponding assignment observation.

The elementary-equivalence and elementary-substructure faces are finite
records in $\mathsf{Elem}$. Project those records from the source packet,
replace each formula and satisfaction reference by the transported row just
obtained, and repack them as the target $\mathsf{Elem}$ row. No step compares
ambient carriers by a host equality; every comparison is one of the displayed
$\hsame$ rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ModelTheoryUp NameCert obligation surface]\label{thm:modeltheory-namecert-obligation-surface}
The public $\NameCert_{\ModelTheoryUp}$ obligation surface consists exactly of
the carrier of \autoref{def:modeltheory-bhist-structure-packet}, the
classifier of \autoref{def:modeltheory-satisfaction-ledger-classifier}, the
first-order dependency surface of
\autoref{thm:modeltheory-firstorder-dependency-surface}, the elementary
transport of \autoref{thm:modeltheory-elementary-equivalence-transport},
stability transport, the satisfaction exactness ledger, the
elementary-equivalence ledger, and no additional exported field.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Collect the carrier rows first. The finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet provides
$S$, $A$, $\mathsf{Val}$, $\mathsf{Sat}$, $\mathsf{Elem}$, $\rho$, and
$\lambda$, while the first-order dependency theorem identifies all syntax and
substitution reads as projections through $\NameCert_{\FirstOrderUp}$.

Next collect the classifier rows. The satisfaction ledger classifier gives
rowwise $\hsame$ transport for the first-order source, the structure carrier,
valuation observations, satisfaction records, elementary faces, and
provenance. The elementary transport theorem repacks the transported
satisfaction data into elementary-equivalence and elementary-substructure
rows.

It remains to check public coverage. A consumer of
$\NameCert_{\ModelTheoryUp}$ can ask for a carrier row, a classifier row,
stability under $\hsame$, exact readback of satisfaction records, or the
elementary ledger. Each request is one of the collected rows, and none exposes
a host satisfaction predicate, an ambient model equality, or another public
field outside the displayed packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[ModelTheoryUp assignment observation row]\label{def:modeltheory-assignment-observation-row}
A $\ModelTheoryUp$ assignment observation row is the displayed
$\mathsf{BHist}$ entry

$$
(S,\varphi,x,\mathsf{Val}_{x,\varphi},\rho_{\mathsf{Val}},
\lambda_{\mathsf{Val}})
$$

whose variable endpoint $x$ and formula endpoint $\varphi$ are read only
through $\NameCert_{\FirstOrderUp}$. The valuation component
$\mathsf{Val}_{x,\varphi}$ is the visible assignment row for that endpoint
pair, $\lambda_{\mathsf{Val}}$ records the $\Cont$ and $\hsame$ transport
available for the observation, and $\rho_{\mathsf{Val}}$ is the $\Pkg$
provenance tying the row back to the admitted packet of
\autoref{def:modeltheory-bhist-structure-packet}.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[ModelTheoryUp satisfaction exactness row]\label{thm:modeltheory-satisfaction-exactness-row}
Every accepted $\ModelTheoryUp$ satisfaction record is exactly the visible
$\mathsf{Sat}$ row for a formula endpoint read through
$\NameCert_{\FirstOrderUp}$ and an assignment observation of
\autoref{def:modeltheory-assignment-observation-row}. Conversely, every such
visible $\mathsf{Sat}$ row repacks as the satisfaction record for the same
admitted packet. No host satisfaction predicate is exported.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the finite packet of
\autoref{def:modeltheory-bhist-structure-packet}. The projections expose the
first-order row $S$, the valuation ledger $\mathsf{Val}$, the satisfaction
ledger $\mathsf{Sat}$, and the provenance row $\rho$.

The formula endpoint is read from $S$ through
$\NameCert_{\FirstOrderUp}$, while the assignment endpoint is read from the
displayed valuation row described in
\autoref{def:modeltheory-assignment-observation-row}. Thus the satisfaction
record has no data source other than the visible pair of endpoints and the
packet provenance.

Repack those projections as the same $\mathsf{Sat}$ row with its $\Pkg$,
$\Cont$, and $\hsame$ witnesses. This gives the exact readback direction, and
the converse is the same repacking step starting from the displayed row. At
no point is a semantic satisfaction relation outside the $\mathsf{BHist}$
packet introduced.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ModelTheoryUp elementary substructure boundary]\label{thm:modeltheory-elementary-substructure-boundary}
The elementary-equivalence and elementary-substructure faces of an admitted
$\ModelTheoryUp$ packet are finite displayed $\mathsf{Elem}$ rows transported
through the satisfaction exactness row and $\hsame$. Every formula occurrence
in those faces is a $\NameCert_{\FirstOrderUp}$ endpoint, and every assignment
occurrence is a transported valuation row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the classifier of
\autoref{def:modeltheory-satisfaction-ledger-classifier}. It supplies the
rowwise $\hsame$ transports for the first-order source, valuation ledger,
satisfaction ledger, elementary ledger, and $\Pkg$ provenance.

Apply \autoref{thm:modeltheory-satisfaction-exactness-row} to each
satisfaction record referenced by an elementary face. This replaces the
record by the visible $\mathsf{Sat}$ row for a named formula endpoint and a
named assignment observation.

It remains to move the elementary face itself. Replace every formula
reference by the transported first-order row and every assignment reference
by the transported $\mathsf{Val}$ row, then repack the finite face as the
target $\mathsf{Elem}$ row. The boundary is therefore a displayed ledger
boundary, not an ambient elementary-substructure predicate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ModelTheoryUp downstream consumer row]\label{thm:modeltheory-downstream-consumer-row}
A downstream consumer of $\NameCert_{\ModelTheoryUp}$ can ask only for the
carrier packet, the satisfaction-ledger classifier, the assignment
observation row, the satisfaction exactness row, the elementary substructure
boundary, stability transport, and $\Pkg$ provenance. Any request for a host
truth predicate, an ambient model equality, or an external elementary
comparison lies outside the public obligation surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the public surface of
\autoref{thm:modeltheory-namecert-obligation-surface}. The visible rows are
the carrier packet, the classifier, first-order dependency rows, elementary
transport rows, stability transport, satisfaction readback, and provenance.

Refine the satisfaction readback by
\autoref{thm:modeltheory-satisfaction-exactness-row}. Refine the assignment
input by \autoref{def:modeltheory-assignment-observation-row}, and refine the
elementary request by
\autoref{thm:modeltheory-elementary-substructure-boundary}.

These refinements exhaust the consumer-facing rows. Since each row is a
projection from the displayed packet or a repacking through $\Cont$,
$\hsame$, and $\Pkg$, no consumer receives an operation beyond the listed
certificate surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[ModelTheoryUp elementary readback ledger]\label{def:modeltheory-elementary-readback-ledger}
The elementary readback ledger of an admitted $\ModelTheoryUp$ packet is the
finite displayed $\mathsf{Elem}$ row of
\autoref{def:modeltheory-bhist-structure-packet}. Its entries record only
elementary-equivalence and elementary-substructure observations whose formula
endpoints are read through the transported $\FirstOrderUp$ source row, whose
truth witnesses are entries of the displayed $\mathsf{Sat}$ row, and whose
provenance is the same $\Pkg$ row $\rho$ carried by the packet.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[ModelTheoryUp satisfaction exactness ledger]\label{thm:modeltheory-satisfaction-exactness-ledger}
For any admitted $\ModelTheoryUp$ packet, every satisfaction record in
$\mathsf{Sat}$ reads back exactly through the displayed $\FirstOrderUp$
formula row and the displayed valuation row $\mathsf{Val}$. The readback
exports the transported formula endpoint, the transported valuation endpoint,
the recorded truth entry, and the $\Pkg$ provenance, and it exports no host
satisfaction predicate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the packet of
\autoref{def:modeltheory-bhist-structure-packet}. Its displayed rows include
the first-order source $S$, the valuation ledger $\mathsf{Val}$, the
satisfaction ledger $\mathsf{Sat}$, and the provenance row $\rho$.

Apply the classifier of
\autoref{def:modeltheory-satisfaction-ledger-classifier}. A satisfaction
entry is classified rowwise, so its formula endpoint is transported along
the $\FirstOrderUp$ row and its assignment endpoint is transported along
$\mathsf{Val}$.

Use \autoref{thm:modeltheory-firstorder-dependency-surface} to identify the
formula endpoint as a projection through $\NameCert_{\FirstOrderUp}$. The
truth entry is then the displayed $\mathsf{Sat}$ record itself, and its source
provenance is the carried $\Pkg$ row.

Finally apply
\autoref{thm:modeltheory-elementary-equivalence-transport} and
\autoref{thm:modeltheory-namecert-obligation-surface} only to check that this
projection lies on the public obligation surface. Those theorems add no host
satisfaction predicate, so the readback is exactly the displayed row data.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ModelTheoryUp elementary readback boundary]\label{thm:modeltheory-elementary-readback-boundary}
For any admitted $\ModelTheoryUp$ packet, elementary-equivalence and
elementary-substructure readback is exhausted by the elementary readback
ledger of \autoref{def:modeltheory-elementary-readback-ledger}. In
particular, the boundary of the readback consists of the transported
$\FirstOrderUp$ formula endpoints, $\mathsf{Val}$ assignment endpoints,
$\mathsf{Sat}$ truth entries, $\mathsf{Elem}$ elementary faces, and $\Pkg$
provenance, and it uses no host model equality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the elementary row from the carrier of
\autoref{def:modeltheory-bhist-structure-packet}. By definition
\autoref{def:modeltheory-elementary-readback-ledger}, this row is the finite
displayed $\mathsf{Elem}$ ledger attached to the packet.

The classifier of
\autoref{def:modeltheory-satisfaction-ledger-classifier} supplies the only
allowed transport data: $S$, $A$, $\mathsf{Val}$, $\mathsf{Sat}$,
$\mathsf{Elem}$, and $\rho$. Hence every formula occurrence is read through
the $\FirstOrderUp$ source row, every assignment occurrence through
$\mathsf{Val}$, and every truth occurrence through $\mathsf{Sat}$.

Apply
\autoref{thm:modeltheory-firstorder-dependency-surface} for the first-order
projection and
\autoref{thm:modeltheory-elementary-equivalence-transport} for repacking the
elementary-equivalence and elementary-substructure faces. The public surface
theorem \autoref{thm:modeltheory-namecert-obligation-surface} then rules out
any additional exported comparison field.

Thus the elementary readback boundary is exactly the displayed
$\mathsf{Elem}$ ledger together with its named source rows, and no step
requires an equality of ambient host models.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ModelTheoryUp downstream consumer interface]\label{thm:modeltheory-downstream-consumer-interface}
Any downstream consumer of $\NameCert_{\ModelTheoryUp}$ can consume only the
first-order source row, the displayed structure row, the valuation row, the
satisfaction exactness row of
\autoref{thm:modeltheory-satisfaction-exactness-ledger}, the elementary
readback row of \autoref{def:modeltheory-elementary-readback-ledger}, and the
$\Pkg$ provenance. No downstream interface can demand a host satisfaction
predicate, host model equality, or an extra carrier outside those rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the public obligation surface of
\autoref{thm:modeltheory-namecert-obligation-surface}. It lists the carrier,
classifier, first-order dependency surface, elementary transport, stability,
satisfaction exactness, elementary ledger, and no additional exported field.

Unpack the carrier using
\autoref{def:modeltheory-bhist-structure-packet}. The consumable rows are
$S$, $A$, $\mathsf{Val}$, $\mathsf{Sat}$, $\mathsf{Elem}$, $\rho$, and the
transport ledger. The first-order theorem
\autoref{thm:modeltheory-firstorder-dependency-surface} identifies $S$ as the
only source of formula and substitution reads.

Use the classifier of
\autoref{def:modeltheory-satisfaction-ledger-classifier} to restrict
transport to the displayed rows. The satisfaction exactness theorem gives the
allowed truth-record readback, while
\autoref{thm:modeltheory-elementary-equivalence-transport} and
\autoref{def:modeltheory-elementary-readback-ledger} give the allowed
elementary readback.

Therefore every downstream request factors through one of the listed rows or
through $\Pkg$ provenance. Since the obligation surface has no remaining
field, no consumer can obtain a host satisfaction predicate, host model
equality, or another carrier from $\NameCert_{\ModelTheoryUp}$.
\end{proof}
