The $\ModelSelectionBoundaryUp$ packet realizes the comparative-selection boundary of \autoref{sec:reality-constrained-synthesis} as a finite BEDC NameCert carrier for reality-constrained physical truth. It sits beside the open-fit packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-open-fit-packet-namecert}, the model-audit packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-reality-constrained-model-audit-namecert}, the signature-fit packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-reality-constrained-signature-fit-namecert}, and the synthesis seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-realityconstrainedsynthesisseal-namecert}. It also consumes the truth-certificate discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-physical-truth-certificate-namecert}: those siblings expose fit, audit, signature, synthesis, and truth-certificate faces, while this boundary records which finite candidate currently wins and which displayed defeats keep that selection provisional.

\begin{definition}[ModelSelectionBoundary carrier]
\label{def:model-selection-boundary-carrier}
A $\ModelSelectionBoundaryUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
B=(M,S,F,D,L,T,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the finite model-family row listing the accepted candidate packets under comparison.
\item $S$ is the observed-signature row shared by the comparison.
\item $F$ is the comparative-fit row recording the selected packet, its displayed fit score, and the losing rows that remain accepted but not selected.
\item $D$ is the defeat-surface row naming finite observations, signature mismatches, audit failures, domain exits, and ledger conflicts that overturn the displayed selection.
\item $L$ is the selection-ledger row recording admissibility, tie policy, defeat visibility, provenance, and refused final-oracle demands.
\item $T$ is the componentwise transport row preserving the candidate family, observed signatures, fit comparison, defeat surface, and ledger under displayed $\hsame$ routes.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance row over $\ModelSelectionBoundaryUp$, $\OpenFitPacketUp$, $\RealityConstrainedModelAuditUp$, $\RealityConstrainedSignatureFitUp$, $\RealityConstrainedSynthesisSealUp$, $\PhysicalTruthCertificateUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ModelSelectionBoundaryUp}$ row naming the whole boundary.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for model--reality identity, total confirmation, a hidden winner outside $M$, an erased losing row, an unledgered defeat, or a final oracle deciding all future observations.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[ModelSelectionBoundary fit-ledger separation]
\label{thm:model-selection-boundary-fit-ledger-separation}
For an accepted $\ModelSelectionBoundaryUp$ carrier $B=(M,S,F,D,L,T,P,N)$, the selected row in $F$ is a finite ledgered selection from the displayed candidate family $M$, not a truth identity between the selected model and physical reality. Every public selection read must expose the observed signatures $S$, the comparative fit row $F$, the losing accepted rows recorded by $F$, the selection ledger $L$, the transport row $T$, provenance $P$, and local name $N$ before it can be consumed as a reality-constrained physical-truth certificate.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ModelSelectionBoundaryUp.TasteGate.ModelSelectionBoundaryTasteGate\_single\_carrier\_alignment}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission exposes exactly $B=(M,S,F,D,L,T,P,N)$. The only row that selects a candidate is $F$, and $F$ is indexed by the finite family $M$ and the displayed signature row $S$.

The ledger row $L$ separates acceptance from selection. A losing row may remain an accepted packet while failing to be the displayed winner; conversely, the winning row is selected only under the current finite comparison and ledger policy.

The transport and provenance rows keep the comparison public. Since $T,P,N$ preserve the same candidate family, signature, fit, ledger, and local name, no consumer can replace the selected row by hidden model identity or final physical truth.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ModelSelectionBoundary defeat surface]
\label{thm:model-selection-boundary-defeat-surface}
An accepted $\ModelSelectionBoundaryUp$ packet is locally refuted whenever a competing accepted packet in $M$ beats the displayed selected row of $F$ while the relevant finite defeat is hidden from $D$ or erased from $L$. A selection that keeps $D$ and $L$ visible remains a current finite winner; a selection that suppresses the defeat surface is outside the boundary.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ModelSelectionBoundaryUp.TasteGate.ModelSelectionBoundary\_defeat\_surface}
\begin{proof}
Read the carrier rows componentwise. The family row $M$ supplies the competing accepted packets, the signature row $S$ supplies the shared observation surface, and the comparative-fit row $F$ records the selected and losing rows.

If a competing accepted packet beats the selected row, the defeat must be visible in $D$ and ledgered in $L$. Those two rows are the only coordinates that expose overturned fit, mismatch, audit failure, domain exit, or policy conflict.

When the defeat is hidden or erased, the claimed selection asks the packet to export a winning status without the displayed boundary rows that make the comparison auditable. Such a route is not an accepted $\ModelSelectionBoundaryUp$ read.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ModelSelectionBoundary obligation carrier]
\label{thm:modelselectionboundary-obligation-carrier}
For every accepted $\ModelSelectionBoundaryUp$ carrier $B=(M,S,F,D,L,T,P,N)$, carrier admission is the displayed finite packet over model candidate rows, score and risk rows, validation windows, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$. A consumer may read the candidate family, observed signatures, comparative fit, defeat surface, selection ledger, transport, provenance, and local naming only through those rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:model-selection-boundary-carrier}. The tuple $B=(M,S,F,D,L,T,P,N)$ displays the model-family row, observed-signature row, comparative-fit row, defeat row, ledger row, transport row, provenance row, and local name.

The comparative-fit and ledger rows are the packet positions for score, risk, and validation-window reads. They are carried by the same finite $\BHist$ packet and are preserved by $T$ through the displayed $\hsame$ route.

Transport and package rows do not add hidden evidence. The rows $T$ and $P$ available through the BEDC kernel discipline only preserve and provenance the same displayed packet, so consumers remain confined to the admitted carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ModelSelectionBoundary obligation risk ledger]
\label{thm:modelselectionboundary-obligation-risk-ledger}
Every risk, score, or validation comparison exported by an accepted $\ModelSelectionBoundaryUp$ packet factors through the displayed finite ledgers in $F,D,L$. The comparison cannot be replaced by host statistical choice, an unrecorded optimization procedure, a hidden probability limit, or a selected global minimizer outside the candidate family $M$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the comparison rows. The selected candidate and losing accepted rows are recorded by $F$, while $D$ records displayed defeats and $L$ records admissibility, tie policy, and defeat visibility.

Use the fit-ledger separation theorem \autoref{thm:model-selection-boundary-fit-ledger-separation}. It makes the selected row a finite ledgered selection from the displayed family rather than an identity with physical truth.

The defeat surface theorem \autoref{thm:model-selection-boundary-defeat-surface} keeps later risk or validation defeats visible. Therefore any comparison that bypasses $F,D,L$ is not a public read of the boundary packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ModelSelectionBoundary obligation nonescape]
\label{thm:modelselectionboundary-obligation-nonescape}
No consumer of an accepted $\ModelSelectionBoundaryUp$ packet can read a selected global minimizer, classical probability convergence, unrecorded model evidence, final physical truth, or a host-level model ordering from the packet. The consumer surface is exhausted by $M,S,F,D,L,T,P,N$.

\begin{proof}

Apply \autoref{thm:model-selection-boundary-fit-ledger-separation} and \autoref{thm:model-selection-boundary-defeat-surface}. They make selection finite, ledgered, defeat-visible, and provisional inside the displayed boundary.

Since every exported comparison must pass through those rows, unrecorded evidence, host model ordering, final truth, and global minimizers have no projection from the accepted packet.
\end{proof}
