\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\VietorisTopologyUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-vietoris-topology-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Vietoris and Kelley, the $\VietorisTopologyUp$ packet records the finite subbasic-hit and subbasic-miss topology read on compact-subset names. It sits over the compact-subset surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-hyperspace-namecert}, the finite-net distance route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-hausdorff-metric-namecert}, the compact metric source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, and the ambient topology carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-topology-namecert}. The packet names the lower-hit and upper-miss generators as BEDC rows; it is not a host powerset topology, not subset equality, not a choice-selected point, and not open-cover compactness.

\begin{definition}[Vietoris topology carrier]
\label{def:vietoris-topology-carrier}
A $\VietorisTopologyUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
V=(H,K,L,U,M,T,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $H$ is the $\HyperspaceUp$ source row, recording the compact-subset naming surface and the finite Hausdorff-distance ledger through which compact-subset requests are read.
\item $K$ is the compact-subset row over the displayed $\CompactMetricUp$ source, including the finite subset-net schedules and nonempty compact-subset admission data that the hyperspace route exposes.
\item $L$ is the lower-hit row. It records subbasic generators of the form ``the compact-subset name hits a displayed open row'' through finite witness windows routed by $H$ and $K$.
\item $U$ is the upper-miss row. It records subbasic generators of the form ``the compact-subset name is covered by a displayed open row'' through finite miss certificates and compact-metric net checks.
\item $M$ is the metric and finite-net compatibility row, comparing the lower-hit and upper-miss requests with the $\HausdorffMetricUp$ finite-net distance route and the $\CompactMetricUp$ source schedules.
\item $T$ is the topology transport row from the displayed subbasic hit and miss generators into the ambient $\TopologyUp$ carrier.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the route
$$
H,K,L,U,M \longmapsto T .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\VietorisTopologyUp$, $\HyperspaceUp$, $\HausdorffMetricUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\TopologyUp$, $\ProbeBundle$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\VietorisTopologyUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $H,K,L,U,M,T,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for host subset equality, choice-selected points, open-cover compactness, quotient compacta, or ambient powerset topology.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[VietorisTopologyUp NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:vietoris-topology-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\VietorisTopologyUp$ carrier
$$
V=(H,K,L,U,M,T,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\VietorisTopologyUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the hyperspace source, compact-subset row, lower-hit row, upper-miss row, finite-net compatibility row, topology transport row, replay row, provenance row, and local naming row;
\item lower-hit exactness, requiring every hit generator in $L$ to be read through the displayed hyperspace and compact-subset rows before a witness window is exposed;
\item upper-miss exactness, requiring every miss generator in $U$ to be read through the same compact-metric net schedules before a cover read is exposed;
\item finite-net compatibility, requiring $M$ to preserve the HausdorffMetric and CompactMetric tolerance ledgers attached to the hit and miss rows;
\item $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ transport, requiring $T$ and $C$ to carry the subbasic rows into $\TopologyUp$ without adding a hidden classifier surface;
\item ledger nonescape through $P$ and $N$, excluding host subset equality, choice-selected points, open-cover compactness, quotient compacta, and ambient powerset topology.
\end{enumerate}
No Vietoris topology obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
