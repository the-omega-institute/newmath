\begin{definition}[Cellular trust substrate NameCert carrier]
\label{def:cellular-trust-substrate-namecert-carrier}
A $\CellularTrustSubstrateUp$ carrier is a finite packet
$$
S=(R,M,O,F,A,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $R$ is the finite Rule 110 table row, listing the eight neighbourhood transitions used by the substrate.
\item $M$ is the manifest row naming the initial cell word, declared run budget, expected observation fields, and public replay parameters.
\item $O$ is the finite orbit-segment row produced by iterating $R$ from $M$ for the displayed number of steps.
\item $F$ is the classifier row for visible cell patterns, phase tags, and accepted observation records.
\item $A$ is the audit-route row comparing the manifest, direct Rule 110 evolution, cyclic-tag compilation route, and classifier disagreement checks.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, $\Cont$, $\hsame$, the manifest schema, the classifier row, and the replay audit.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CellularTrustSubstrateUp}$ ledger for carrier admission, classifier stability, audit-route exactness, and closed-observation non-escape.
\end{enumerate}
All rows are finite. The carrier names a concrete closed substrate, not a quotient stream, an oracle halting predicate, or host-level equality of cellular automata.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cellular trust substrate NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cellular-trust-substrate-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\CellularTrustSubstrateUp}$ surface for \autoref{def:cellular-trust-substrate-namecert-carrier} consists exactly of six obligation rows: carrier admission for $S=(R,M,O,F,A,P,N)$; rule-table faithfulness for the eight displayed Rule 110 transitions; manifest-to-orbit replay exactness for the finite segment $O$; classifier stability under the visible phase and pattern rows in $F$; audit-route agreement for the direct evaluator, cyclic-tag route, and disagreement checks in $A$; and closed-observation non-escape through $P$ and $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from the displayed tuple. The rule table, manifest, orbit segment, classifier, audit route, package row, and local ledger are the only coordinates of the packet.

Rule-table faithfulness and manifest replay exactness are finite checks. The eight entries of $R$ determine each successor cell in $O$, and $M$ supplies the initial word and replay budget read by the audit route.

Classifier stability is local to $F$. Any accepted pattern or phase read must pass through the displayed classifier row and the disagreement checks in $A$, so a consumer cannot replace the row by an unrecorded host classifier.

Finally, $P$ and $N$ package the same finite rows as the public certificate. Since no coordinate names a quotient stream, an oracle for halting, or host equality of cellular automata, the obligations close only the finite closed-substrate surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cellular trust substrate closed observation non-escape]
\label{thm:cellular-trust-substrate-closed-observation-nonescape}
Every accepted public observation of a $\CellularTrustSubstrateUp$ packet factors through the finite route
$$
R \longmapsto M \longmapsto O \longmapsto F \longmapsto A \longmapsto P \longmapsto N.
$$
The route may certify the displayed Rule 110 orbit segment, classifier row, and replay audit, but it cannot export a quotient of infinite streams, an oracle halting decision, a host cellular-automaton equality principle, or an induction principle over all future rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted observation. Carrier projection gives the only available rows: the rule table $R$, manifest $M$, orbit segment $O$, classifier $F$, audit route $A$, package row $P$, and local ledger $N$.

The NameCert obligations force the observation to read successor cells through $R$ and $M$, classifier claims through $F$, and replay agreement through $A$. Each of these rows is finite and displayed inside the packet.

The package and ledger rows record provenance for that same finite route. They add no coordinate for infinite-stream quotienting, oracle halting, host equality between cellular automata, or induction over ungenerated future cells. Thus the accepted observation remains a closed finite substrate read.
\end{proof}
