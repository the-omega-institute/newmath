Following Bishop--Bridges constructive analysis and the interval computations of Moore--Kearfott--Cloud, the $\NestedDyadicCauchyUp$ packet records the finite data by which a nested chain of dyadic intervals becomes a regular rational Cauchy name before the terminal real seal is consumed. It refines the finite-chain surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-nesteddyadicinterval-namecert}, the ambient interval discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-nestedinterval-namecert}, the dyadic endpoint core of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the finite schedule interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the seal boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet is a finite-means Cauchy route for L10 real and completion work; it is not a completed intersection point, quotient stream equality, host real equality, countable choice, or ambient completeness.

\begin{definition}[Nested dyadic Cauchy carrier]
\label{def:nested-dyadic-cauchy-carrier}
A $\NestedDyadicCauchyUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(I,B,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $I$ is the finite nested dyadic interval window. It records a nonempty finite prefix of dyadic intervals, their endpoint rows through $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, and the displayed refinement relation inherited from $\NestedDyadicIntervalUp$.
\item $B$ is the shrinking-width dyadic budget. It assigns each displayed tolerance request to a prefix index whose interval width is bounded by the requested dyadic scale.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule row. It names exactly the finite interval-prefix entries and endpoint observations that may be queried before regular rational readback.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row formed from the selected endpoint or midpoint observations, the shrinking-width budget, and the stream schedule.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after $I,B,S,R$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for interval prefixes, dyadic budgets, stream schedules, regular rational readback, real seals, replay rows, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a finite interval-window request through $B,S,R,E$ and then through the structural rows.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\NestedDyadicCauchyUp$, $\NestedDyadicIntervalUp$, $\NestedIntervalUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\NestedDyadicCauchyUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through these nine displayed rows. It has no coordinate for completed intersections, quotient stream equality, host real equality, countable choice, ambient completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Nested dyadic Cauchy NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:nested-dyadic-cauchy-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\NestedDyadicCauchyUp$ carrier
$$
K=(I,B,S,R,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\NestedDyadicCauchyUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the nested dyadic interval prefix, shrinking-width budget, stream schedule, regular rational handoff, real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item nested-prefix exposure, requiring each interval read to be one of the displayed prefix rows of $I$;
\item dyadic-width exactness, requiring every Cauchy tolerance request to be discharged by a width comparison stored in $B$ and read through $\DyadicRatCoreUp$;
\item schedule compatibility, requiring $S$ to expose only the interval-prefix and endpoint observations used by $B$ and $R$;
\item $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff, requiring $R$ to be formed only after the prefix, budget, and schedule rows have been projected;
\item $\RealUp$ seal nonescape, requiring $E$ to be consumed only after $R$ and the structural rows have fixed the finite route.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:nested-dyadic-cauchy-carrier}. The projection exposes one finite nested dyadic interval prefix, one shrinking-width dyadic budget, one StreamName schedule, one RegSeqRat handoff, one Real seal, and the transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows.

Nested-prefix exposure is forced by $I$. A consumer may inspect only interval rows already present in the finite prefix, and each such row carries its endpoints through the DyadicRatCore surface named by the packet.

Dyadic-width exactness is then forced by $B$. Each tolerance request is answered by a displayed prefix index and a displayed dyadic comparison of the corresponding interval width against that tolerance.

The schedule and handoff rows are downstream. The row $S$ records the finite prefix and endpoint observations that may be opened, and $R$ repacks exactly those observations as regular rational Cauchy data.

Finally $E,H,C,P,N$ close the route. The Real seal is reachable only after the regular handoff has been displayed, while the structural rows only transport, replay, source, and name the same finite data. Therefore no completed intersection, quotient stream equality, host real equality, countable choice, ambient completeness, Lean verification, or bridge-checking coordinate is present.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Nested dyadic Cauchy RegSeqRat handoff]
\label{thm:nested-dyadic-cauchy-regseqrat-handoff}
Let $K=(I,B,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\NestedDyadicCauchyUp$ carrier. Every $\RegSeqRatUp$ consumer of $K$ factors through the finite route
$$
I\longmapsto B\longmapsto S\longmapsto R\longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
The route reads a finite nested dyadic interval prefix, a shrinking-width budget, and the scheduled endpoint observations before it exports the regular rational Cauchy row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the displayed interval prefix $I$. Its rows give the only endpoint data available to a regular rational consumer, and the refinement relation keeps later prefix rows inside earlier rows.

Use the budget row $B$ to answer a displayed Cauchy tolerance request. The selected prefix index and the dyadic width comparison are part of the same finite packet, so no external modulus or choice of tail is needed.

The schedule row $S$ then fixes which interval endpoints or midpoint observations are opened. The handoff row $R$ is obtained by reading those scheduled observations under the budget discipline and repacking them as the regular rational Cauchy surface.

The rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name that handoff. Since the carrier has no additional tail selector or quotient coordinate, every RegSeqRat read factors through the displayed route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Nested dyadic Cauchy tail gluing]
\label{thm:nested-dyadic-cauchy-tail-gluing}
Let $K=(I,B,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)$ and $K'=(I',B',S',R',E',H',C',P',N')$ be accepted $\NestedDyadicCauchyUp$ carriers. Suppose their finite interval prefixes and dyadic budgets admit a displayed common refinement through the StreamName schedules: there are rows $I^*$, $B^*$, and $S^*$ such that $I^*$ refines the displayed tails of $I$ and $I'$, $B^*$ compares both dyadic tolerance budgets against that common prefix through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and $S^*$ restricts both scheduled reads through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}. Then every $\RegSeqRatUp$-facing read of $K$ and $K'$ factors through one common finite route
$$
I^*\longmapsto B^*\longmapsto S^*\longmapsto R^*
$$
before either $\RealUp$ seal row $E$ or $E'$ is available. Here $R^*$ is the shared regular rational handoff row supplied by the $\RegSeqRatUp$ surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal seal boundary remains the $\RealUp$ boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Nested dyadic Cauchy Real seal boundary]
\label{thm:nested-dyadic-cauchy-real-seal-boundary}
For every accepted $\NestedDyadicCauchyUp$ carrier $K=(I,B,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, every $\RealUp$-facing read factors through the regular rational handoff row $R$ before the seal row $E$ is available. The consumer receives the finite interval prefix, shrinking-width budget, StreamName schedule, RegSeqRat handoff, Real seal, and structural rows, but receives no completed intersection point, quotient stream equality, host real equality, countable-choice selector, or ambient completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The only real-facing coordinate is the seal row $E$, and $E$ is downstream of the prefix, budget, schedule, and handoff rows by \autoref{def:nested-dyadic-cauchy-carrier}.

Apply \autoref{thm:nested-dyadic-cauchy-regseqrat-handoff}. The regular rational handoff has already forced every read through $I,B,S,R,H,C,P,N$, so a Real-facing consumer cannot bypass the displayed Cauchy route.

The seal row $E$ packages that handoff as the terminal Real boundary. Because the carrier contains no separate coordinate for an intersection point, a quotient stream, host real equality, a choice-selected tail, or ambient completeness, those objects cannot be exported by the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}
