\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\LowerSemicontinuityRealUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-lower-semicontinuity-real-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges constructive analysis, with the point-set
vocabulary of Kelley and Bourbaki read only as finite BEDC routing data, the
$\LowerSemicontinuityRealUp$ packet specializes lower semicontinuity to the
Real-and-Completion corridor. It is not a synonym of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-lowersemicontinuous-namecert}: that sibling
names the general lower-real epigraph surface, while the present chapter
records the real-name, regular-rational, topology, and completion-facing
threshold rows needed by Real/Completion consumers. The packet sits beside the
real carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the regular
rational source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the
stream schedule of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the
lower-real ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-lowerreal-namecert}, the
continuous-map surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuousmap-namecert},
and the completion-facing real routes of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-convergent-sequence-namecert}.

\begin{definition}[LowerSemicontinuityReal carrier]
\label{def:lower-semicontinuity-real-carrier}
A $\LowerSemicontinuityRealUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(X,T,F,W,R,E,O,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the $\RealUp$ source carrier row, read through accepted real-name
  histories rather than host real points;
\item $T$ is the $\TopologyUp$ threshold row whose basic opens are represented
  by finite rational cuts and stream windows;
\item $F$ is the $\ContinuousMapUp$ or value-row handoff used when the
  lower-semicontinuous function is inspected through a displayed map surface;
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule selecting the finite source and value
  windows used by each threshold query;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular-rational readback row for real-name and
  value approximants;
\item $E$ is the $\LowerRealUp$ epigraph ledger, recording finite rows
  $(x,q)$ that witness the rational lower threshold $q$ at the inspected real
  name $x$;
\item $O$ is the open-threshold route, recording that a query of the form
  $q<f(x)$ is answered by a finite lower-real row and then exposed as a basic
  $\TopologyUp$ neighbourhood of the accepted real name;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for real-name rows,
  topology thresholds, map rows, stream schedules, regular-rational readbacks,
  lower-real epigraph rows, threshold routes, provenance rows, and local naming
  rows;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a threshold request through
  $X,T,F,W,R,E,O,H,P,N$;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LowerSemicontinuityRealUp$,
  $\RealUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\LowerRealUp$,
  $\TopologyUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and
  $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LowerSemicontinuityRealUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for host real completeness, quotient real
equality, trichotomy, open-cover compactness, semantic liminf, or a selected
limit point in a completed host space.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[LowerSemicontinuityReal NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:lower-semicontinuity-real-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\LowerSemicontinuityRealUp$ carrier
$L=(X,T,F,W,R,E,O,H,C,P,N)$, the local
$\NameCert_{\LowerSemicontinuityRealUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier
admission for the displayed real-name, topology-threshold, map, stream,
regular-rational, lower-real, open-threshold, transport, replay, provenance,
and local-name rows; threshold exactness for the finite lower-real rows in
$E$; regular-rational readback through $R$; stream scheduling through $W$;
componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$; displayed $\Cont$ replay through
$C$; and provenance through $P$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of
\autoref{def:lower-semicontinuity-real-carrier}. The rows
$X,T,F,W,R,E,O,H,C,P,N$ are the whole Real-and-Completion packet, so carrier
admission is rowwise.

Read threshold exactness through $E$ and $O$. A rational query $q<f(x)$ is
accepted only after the source real name has been scheduled by $W$, read back
through $R$, and matched to a finite lower-real epigraph row in $E$.

Transport and replay are then forced by $H$ and $C$. They move the real-name,
threshold, map, schedule, regular-rational, lower-real, and open-neighbourhood
rows together, so the local $\NameCert$ surface cannot replace them by host
topology or quotient equality.

Finally $P$ and $N$ package the same finite rows. Since the excluded
completeness, trichotomy, compactness, semantic-liminf, and selected-limit
principles are absent from the carrier, they are not NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LowerSemicontinuityReal open-threshold route]
\label{thm:lower-semicontinuity-real-open-threshold-route}
Every completion-facing consumer of an accepted
$\LowerSemicontinuityRealUp$ packet must read an open lower-threshold query
through the finite route
$$
X,T,F,W,R,E,O \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
The route may expose Real-name windows, regular-rational approximants,
stream schedules, lower-real epigraph rows, topology threshold rows, map
handoffs, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming. It exports no host
real completeness, quotient equality, trichotomy, open-cover compactness,
semantic liminf, or completed-space selection principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a threshold query. The source is the $\RealUp$ row $X$, and the
neighbourhood vocabulary is the displayed $\TopologyUp$ row $T$, so the query
does not begin from a host open-set lattice.

Next use the scheduling and readback rows. The stream schedule $W$ selects the
finite windows, and the regular-rational row $R$ supplies the approximants
used by the lower-real ledger $E$.

The open-threshold row $O$ turns the lower-real epigraph witness into the
basic threshold route exposed to completion-facing consumers. The map row $F$
is consumed only when the query is read through a displayed
$\ContinuousMapUp$ surface.

Finally replay through $H,C,P,N$. These rows transport, continue, source, and
name the same finite threshold packet. Since no excluded host principle is a
row of the carrier, the route remains a Real-and-Completion specialization of
lower semicontinuity rather than a completeness or compactness theorem.
\end{proof}
