\begin{definition}[Formal-ball completion carrier]

$$

\item $B$ is the $\FormalBallUp$ family row, exposing the finite formal balls whose centers are read through $M$ and whose radii are accounted for by the dyadic ledger.

\begin{theorem}[Formal-ball completion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:formal-ball-completion-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\FormalBallCompletionUp$ carrier
$$
K=(M,B,I,D,S,R,T,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\FormalBallCompletionUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following five finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed metric source, formal-ball family, rounded-ideal handoff, dyadic ledger, stream windows, real seal, and structural rows $T,C,P,N$;
\item formal-ball family membership, requiring every completion-facing neighbourhood query to be read as a displayed $\FormalBallUp$ member over $M$ with radius data accounted for in $D$;
\item rounded-directed ideal handoff, requiring the row $I$ to be the only route from formal-ball membership to completion-facing roundedness, directedness, and cofinal refinement;
\item classifier transport, requiring accepted packets to be compared componentwise through $T$ and replayed through $C$ without adding a second classifier;
\item ledger non-escape, requiring every downstream read to pass through $M,B,I,D,S,R,T,C,P,N$ and to export no quotient completion, selected limit, countable-choice selector, open-cover compactness proof, ambient completeness datum, Lean verification credential, or bridge credential.
\end{enumerate}
No formal-ball completion obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet $K=(M,B,I,D,S,R,T,C,P,N)$. Carrier admission exposes exactly the metric source, formal-ball family, rounded-ideal handoff, dyadic ledger, stream windows, real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row.

Formal-ball family membership is the projection to $B$ together with its source rows $M,D,S$. The metric row supplies the center classifier, the dyadic row supplies radius comparisons, and the stream row supplies the finite observation windows used by each displayed ball.

The rounded-directed handoff is the projection to $I$. It is the only row that packages roundedness, directedness, and cofinal refinement for the displayed family, so a completion-facing consumer must pass through it before reading the seal row $R$.

Transport and replay are confined to $T$ and $C$, while $P$ and $N$ only record provenance and local naming. Since the carrier has no coordinate for quotient completion equality, selected limits, countable choice, open-cover compactness, ambient completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking, the listed rows exhaust the NameCert surface.
\end{proof}
