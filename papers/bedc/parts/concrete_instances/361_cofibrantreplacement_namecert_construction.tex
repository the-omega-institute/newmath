\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CofibrantReplacementUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-cofibrantreplacement-namecert}

The $\CofibrantReplacementUp$ interface names the finite model-category row that displays one cofibrant replacement witness for one object. Hovey's \emph{Model Categories} treats cofibrant replacement as a standard model-category construction; the BEDC surface below records only a finite displayed factorization packet, not a selection of replacements for every object.

The dependency boundary is $\ModelCatUp$, $\SimplicialSetUp$, $\HomotopyUp$, and $\CategoryUp$. The model-category row supplies cofibration, weak-equivalence, and factorization surfaces; the simplicial-set row supplies a concrete displayed source family when the model surface is read in that setting; the homotopy row records the weak-equivalence ledger; and the category row supplies the object and arrow typing rows.

\section{Finite factorization source}
\label{sec:cofibrantreplacement-factorization-source}

\begin{definition}[CofibrantReplacement BHist source]
\label{def:cofibrantreplacement-bhist-source}
A $\CofibrantReplacementUp$ source is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
(X,Q,i,p,F,\rho,\lambda)
$$
whose visible rows have the following shape:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the displayed object row read from $\ModelCatUp$ and typed through $\CategoryUp$;
\item $Q$ is the displayed cofibrant object row over the same model-category surface;
\item $i:Q\to X$ is the displayed arrow row whose model-category ledger marks it as a cofibration and as a weak equivalence;
\item $p$ is the factorization provenance row recording the finite factorization step that produced $Q$ and $i$;
\item $F$ is a finite list of lifting-test rows attached to the displayed cofibration face;
\item $\rho$ is the package row naming the $\ModelCatUp$, $\SimplicialSetUp$, $\HomotopyUp$, and $\CategoryUp$ dependency certificates used by the packet;
\item $\lambda$ is the continuation ledger that records the $\Cont$ routes and componentwise $\hsame$ comparisons used to read the packet downstream.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate accepts exactly such packets. Its classifier compares accepted packets componentwise by the object, cofibrant-object, arrow, factorization, lifting-test, package, and continuation-ledger $\hsame$ rows, and it does not identify replacements by a homotopy-category quotient.
\end{definition}

\section{Certificate obligations}
\label{sec:cofibrantreplacement-certificate-obligations}

\begin{theorem}[CofibrantReplacement factorization obligation]
\label{thm:cofibrantreplacement-factorization-obligation}
Every accepted $\CofibrantReplacementUp$ source exposes a finite factorization row over $\ModelCatUp$: the displayed object $X$, the displayed cofibrant object $Q$, the arrow $i:Q\to X$, the cofibration ledger for $i$, the weak-equivalence ledger for $i$, and the provenance row $p$ that records this factorization as part of the displayed model-category surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the source packet of \autoref{def:cofibrantreplacement-bhist-source}. The first four coordinates are exactly $X$, $Q$, $i$, and $p$, and the arrow coordinate is stored with both model-category marks.

The category typing of the arrow is read through the $\CategoryUp$ rows named in $\rho$. The model-category marks are read through the $\ModelCatUp$ row, while the provenance coordinate records that these rows come from a finite factorization step.

Since the carrier has no coordinate for a family of replacements, the obligation is exhausted by the displayed packet for this one object.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CofibrantReplacement weak-equivalence ledger]
\label{thm:cofibrantreplacement-weak-equivalence-ledger}
In an accepted $\CofibrantReplacementUp$ source, every weak-equivalence read of $i:Q\to X$ factors through the finite homotopy and model-category ledger carried by the tuple $(X,Q,i,p,F,\rho,\lambda)$. Transporting the packet along its displayed $\hsame$ rows preserves the weak-equivalence ledger and introduces no localization, derived mapping space, or homotopy-category quotient.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the arrow coordinate $i$ in \autoref{def:cofibrantreplacement-bhist-source}. Its weak-equivalence mark is one of the displayed rows of the accepted packet, and $\rho$ names the $\HomotopyUp$ and $\ModelCatUp$ certificates through which that mark is interpreted.

Transport uses the componentwise $\hsame$ comparisons stored in $\lambda$. These comparisons carry the source object, cofibrant object, arrow row, and weak-equivalence mark together, so the target packet reads the same weak-equivalence ledger.

No localization or quotient row is available in the source tuple. Therefore all weak-equivalence information remains inside the displayed finite ledger.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CofibrantReplacement NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:cofibrantreplacement-namecert-obligation-surface}
The local $\NameCert_{\CofibrantReplacementUp}$ obligation surface consists of five finite rows: the source carrier of \autoref{def:cofibrantreplacement-bhist-source}; the componentwise classifier over $X$, $Q$, $i$, $p$, $F$, $\rho$, and $\lambda$; the finite factorization obligation of \autoref{thm:cofibrantreplacement-factorization-obligation}; the weak-equivalence ledger of \autoref{thm:cofibrantreplacement-weak-equivalence-ledger}; and the dependency-boundary row over $\ModelCatUp$, $\SimplicialSetUp$, $\HomotopyUp$, and $\CategoryUp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier and classifier are the two direct projections of the source definition. They list the accepted finite packets and the componentwise comparison relation used between them.

The factorization row is supplied by \autoref{thm:cofibrantreplacement-factorization-obligation}. It records the object, cofibrant object, arrow, cofibration mark, weak-equivalence mark, and factorization provenance.

The weak-equivalence row is supplied by \autoref{thm:cofibrantreplacement-weak-equivalence-ledger}. The remaining dependency-boundary row is the package coordinate $\rho$, which names exactly the four certificates used by the construction.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CofibrantReplacement dependency boundary]
\label{thm:cofibrantreplacement-dependency-boundary}
Any consumer of $\NameCert_{\CofibrantReplacementUp}$ may read only the displayed $\ModelCatUp$, $\SimplicialSetUp$, $\HomotopyUp$, and $\CategoryUp$ dependency rows, the factorization packet $(X,Q,i,p,F,\rho,\lambda)$, and the associated $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and $\NameCert$ provenance. The packet does not export functorial cofibrant replacement, a global replacement assignment, localization data, derived-category objects, or homotopy-category quotients.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the package coordinate $\rho$. It names precisely the model-category, simplicial-set, homotopy, and category certificates used by the source.

Project the continuation ledger $\lambda$. It contains the visible $\Cont$ routes and componentwise $\hsame$ rows by which downstream consumers may transport the factorization packet.

All excluded objects require coordinates absent from the source tuple. A consumer can therefore read the single displayed factorization witness but cannot promote it to a functor, a global assignment, a localization, or a quotient category.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CofibrantReplacement five-row NameCert sketch]
\label{thm:cofibrantreplacement-five-row-namecert-sketch}
The $\CofibrantReplacementUp$ source supplies the five expected $\NameCert$ checks at the displayed finite boundary:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier inhabitance is witnessed by a displayed finite factorization row $(X,Q,i,p,F,\rho,\lambda)$;
\item classifier reflexivity, symmetry, and transitivity are componentwise $\hsame$ checks on $X$, $Q$, $i$, $p$, $F$, $\rho$, and $\lambda$;
\item carrier transport along the factorization ledger repacks the same object, cofibrant-object, arrow, factorization, and lifting-test rows;
\item weak-equivalence ledger stability follows from the transported $\HomotopyUp$ and $\ModelCatUp$ rows for $i$;
\item dependency-boundary non-leakage follows because $\rho$ names only $\ModelCatUp$, $\SimplicialSetUp$, $\HomotopyUp$, and $\CategoryUp$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For carrier inhabitance, use the displayed finite factorization row itself. It supplies the object, cofibrant object, arrow, factorization provenance, lifting tests, package row, and continuation ledger.

For the classifier, compare accepted packets componentwise. Reflexivity, symmetry, and transitivity are exactly the corresponding $\hsame$ moves on each coordinate, repacked into the tuple classifier.

For transport, follow the continuation ledger $\lambda$. It carries each coordinate of the factorization row through the displayed routes and repacks the target tuple with the same shape.

The weak-equivalence and dependency claims are the two boundary projections of the transported packet. The weak-equivalence mark remains attached to $i$ through the $\HomotopyUp$ and $\ModelCatUp$ rows, and $\rho$ contains no dependency certificate beyond the four named rows.
\end{proof}
