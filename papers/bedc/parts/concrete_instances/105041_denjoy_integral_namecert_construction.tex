\label{def:denjoy-integral-carrier}

$$

with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $G$ is the $\GaugeIntegralUp$ source row, read through the finite gauge-partition route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-gauge-integral-namecert}.
\item $I$ is the public $\IntegralUp$ row for the same displayed integrand and interval window.
\item $V$ is the exceptional-variation ledger, recording the finite variation exclusions, controlled subintervals, and refusal rows that prevent the handoff from becoming a hidden measure-completion argument.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row for rational integral approximants and tolerance requests.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after $G,I,V,R$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the gauge source, integral row, exceptional-variation ledger, regular readback, real seal, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming.

\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\DenjoyIntegralUp$, $\GaugeIntegralUp$, $\IntegralUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DenjoyIntegralUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}

\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Denjoy integral NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:denjoy-integral-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\DenjoyIntegralUp$ carrier $D=(G,I,V,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\DenjoyIntegralUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed gauge-integral source, public integral row, exceptional-variation ledger, RegSeqRat readback, Real seal, hsame transport, Cont replay, provenance, and local naming;
\item gauge compatibility, requiring every partition-facing request to pass through $G$ and the sibling route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-gauge-integral-namecert};
\item public integral visibility, requiring the exported integral read to pass through $I$ before any regular-rational or Real-facing row is consumed;
\item exceptional-variation accountability, requiring every exceptional subwindow or variation refusal to be displayed in $V$ rather than hidden in a measure-completion or almost-everywhere premise;
\item readback exactness, requiring rational approximants and tolerance requests to pass through $R$ before the $\RealUp$ seal $E$ is available;
\item structural stability, requiring $H,C,P,N$ to transport, replay, source, and name only the rows already listed.
\end{enumerate}
No Denjoy-integral obligation is stored outside $D$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:denjoy-integral-carrier}. The displayed packet exposes exactly the gauge source, public integral row, exceptional-variation ledger, regular readback, real seal, and structural rows.

Gauge compatibility is the row $G$. It routes every partition-facing read through the same finite gauge data used by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-gauge-integral-namecert}, so a consumer cannot replace the packet by a selected family of gauges or by an ambient compactness theorem.

Integral visibility and exceptional-variation accountability are the rows $I$ and $V$. The public integral row records the exported integral surface, while $V$ keeps exceptional variation and refusal data as displayed finite ledger entries.

The regular readback and real seal are downstream of those rows. The row $R$ supplies rational approximants and tolerance requests before $E$ is exposed, and $H,C,P,N$ preserve only that route. Since the carrier contains no measure-completion, quotient-function, host almost-everywhere, selected-primitive, choice, or completeness coordinate, the listed obligations exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}
