\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\BeliefUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-belief-namecert}
\origin{ai}

The $\BeliefUp$ interface is an AI-proposed BEDC chapter. Its carrier
sketch and dependency anchors come from the proposal at
\texttt{papers/bedc/proposals/accepted/}; the seed-stub here records
the closurestatus block. Its dependency rows are the finite index spine of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-nat-namecert}, the update trace surface of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-seq-namecert}, and the probability certificate
surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-probspace-namecert}.

\falsifiablePrediction{If a public $\BeliefUp$ consumer deletes the prior row,
observation row, update trace, probability row, or evidence ledger, then it
cannot reconstruct the public belief consumer surface of
\autoref{thm:belief-public-namecert-surface}.}
\independenceWitness{Nearest siblings: nat, seq, probspace, modeltheory,
hypothesis-test. The carrier is not bijective to any of them because it binds
five simultaneous rows: a finite index spine, an observation row, an update
trace, a probability certificate, and an evidence ledger; each named sibling
supplies at most a proper projection of that packet rather than the whole
observation-update ledger.}

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:belief-carrier}

A finite $\BeliefUp$ packet is the observation-update carrier suggested by
\texttt{BEDC.Derived.BeliefUp.taste\_gate}; the Lean touchpoint fixes the
chapter name and TasteGate carrier, while the paper surface below records the
certificate obligations without marking them as checked. On the Lean side, any
future AI-origin upgrade that relies on non-vacuity must keep the existing
\texttt{FieldFaithful} instance and add a \texttt{Nontrivial BeliefUp}
instance.
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.BeliefUp.taste\_gate}

\begin{definition}[Belief observation-update carrier]
\label{def:belief-observation-update-carrier}
A $\BeliefUp$ observation-update carrier is a finite packet over
$\mathsf{BHist}$ consisting of the following rows.
\begin{enumerate}
\item A prior row naming the displayed belief state before the observation.
\item An observation row naming the finite evidence read by the update.
\item An update trace recording the visible constructor route from the prior
row and observation row to the posterior row.
\item A probability row supplied by $\NameCert_{\ProbSpaceUp}$ and classified
only through the displayed probability certificate.
\item An evidence ledger that exposes exactly the prior, observation, update
trace, and probability rows used by the packet.
\end{enumerate}
The packet is accepted when those rows are present in the same finite
$\mathsf{BHist}$ ledger, the update trace is a $\SeqUp$ row, and the posterior
belief row is the row named by that trace.
\end{definition}
\leandef{BEDC.Derived.BeliefUp.BeliefObservationUpdateCarrier}

\begin{theorem}[Belief classifier transport obligation]
\label{thm:belief-classifier-transport-obligation}
Let $p$ be an accepted $\BeliefUp$ packet with prior row, observation row,
update trace, and probability row displayed in its evidence ledger. If
$\hsame$ transports each of those four rows to corresponding displayed rows,
then the transported rows repack as an accepted $\BeliefUp$ packet with the
same update-trace shape and a posterior row classified by the transported
probability certificate.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.BeliefUp.BeliefObservationUpdateCarrier\_classifier\_transport}
\begin{proof}
Read the accepted packet through its finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ ledger. The prior
and observation rows are transported first, so the update trace has the same
source rows after transport.

Next transport the update trace as a $\SeqUp$ row. Since the trace records a
visible constructor route, the posterior row is obtained by replaying the same
finite route on the transported prior and observation rows.

Finally transport the probability row through the displayed
$\NameCert_{\ProbSpaceUp}$ certificate. The four transported rows fill the same
carrier fields, so the packet is accepted with no hidden evidence row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Belief ledger exactness obligation]
\label{thm:belief-ledger-exactness-obligation}
For every accepted $\BeliefUp$ packet, the evidence ledger exposes exactly the
prior row, observation row, update trace, and probability row admitted by the
carrier. A downstream consumer may read those four rows and the posterior row
named by the update trace, but no additional hidden evidence, prior source, or
probability witness is part of the packet.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.BeliefUp.BeliefObservationUpdateCarrier\_ledger\_exactness}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier definition. Acceptance requires the prior row, observation
row, update trace, probability row, and evidence ledger to be displayed in the
same finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet.

The ledger exactness clause then identifies every exported evidence entry with
one of those four carrier rows. The posterior is readable only as the endpoint
of the displayed update trace, so it does not add an independent hidden
evidence coordinate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Belief TasteGate carrier recognition]
\label{thm:belief-tastegate-carrier-recognition}
Every accepted $\BeliefUp$ packet is constrained by the TasteGate carrier named
by the Lean definition
\texttt{BEDC.Derived.BeliefUp.taste\_gate}. In particular, the accepted packet
may expose only the prior row, observation row, update trace, probability row,
posterior endpoint, evidence ledger, and displayed provenance rows admitted by
that carrier boundary.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.BeliefUp.BeliefObservationUpdateCarrier\_tastegate\_carrier\_recognition}
\leanvariant{BEDC.Derived.BeliefUp.BeliefTasteGate\_carrier\_recognition}
\begin{proof}
Read the accepted packet through the TasteGate carrier boundary named above. The
carrier admits the observation-update rows as a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
and supplies no constructor for a host belief state, hidden likelihood object, or
external update operation.

Projecting the packet therefore leaves only the displayed prior, observation,
update trace, probability, posterior endpoint, evidence ledger, and provenance
rows. Since every accepted packet must pass through the same carrier boundary,
consumer recognition is restricted to those rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Belief posterior trace transport obligation]
\label{thm:belief-posterior-trace-transport-obligation}
Let $p$ be an accepted $\BeliefUp$ packet. If its prior row, observation row,
update trace, and probability row are transported through displayed $\hsame$
comparisons and $\Cont$ routes, then the transported rows repack as a
$\BeliefUp$ packet whose posterior endpoint is the endpoint named by the
transported update trace.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.BeliefUp.BeliefObservationUpdateCarrier\_posterior\_trace\_transport}
\begin{proof}
Start with the accepted packet and project its four transported input rows. The
prior and observation endpoints move through the displayed $\hsame$ comparisons,
while the update trace moves along the displayed $\Cont$ route.

The posterior endpoint is not an independent input. It is read as the endpoint
named by the update trace after transport, so replaying the transported trace
with the transported prior and observation rows determines the posterior row.

Transport the probability row through the displayed probability certificate and
place it with the transported trace data in the same finite ledger. These rows
fill the carrier fields again, so the packet repacks under $\hsame/\Cont$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Belief finite evidence ledger coverage]
\label{thm:belief-finite-evidence-ledger-coverage}
For every accepted $\BeliefUp$ packet, the finite evidence ledger covers exactly
the prior row, observation row, update trace, probability row, and posterior
endpoint. No additional likelihood row, hidden prior source, external
observation stream, or host update primitive is exported by the ledger.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.BeliefUp.BeliefFiniteEvidenceLedger\_coverage}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier rows. Acceptance places the prior, observation, update
trace, probability row, and posterior endpoint in one finite $\mathsf{BHist}$
ledger with their displayed provenance.

The ledger coverage clause identifies each readable evidence entry with one of
those five rows. Since the posterior endpoint is already the trace endpoint, it
does not create a second evidence source.

Thus a downstream consumer can cite exactly the listed rows and no hidden
belief-update data. Every permitted read is a projection of the displayed finite
ledger.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.BeliefUp.BeliefObservationUpdateCarrier\_finite\_evidence\_ledger\_coverage}

\begin{theorem}[Belief public NameCert surface]
\label{thm:belief-public-namecert-surface}
The observation-update carrier, classifier transport, ledger exactness,
TasteGate carrier recognition, posterior trace transport, and finite evidence
ledger coverage assemble the public $\NameCert_{\BeliefUp}$ surface. Its
consumer-readable rows are exactly the prior, observation, update trace,
probability, posterior endpoint, evidence ledger, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and
$\mathsf{Pkg}$ rows.

This surface exports no hidden prior source, external observation stream,
host update primitive, likelihood object outside the probability certificate,
or bridge datum.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.BeliefUp.BeliefObservationUpdateCarrier\_public\_namecert\_surface}
\begin{proof}
Project the packet through
\autoref{def:belief-observation-update-carrier}. The carrier supplies the
prior, observation, update trace, probability, evidence ledger, and posterior
endpoint rows inside one finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet.

Apply \autoref{thm:belief-classifier-transport-obligation} and
\autoref{thm:belief-ledger-exactness-obligation}. These obligations give the
displayed classifier movement and ledger readback, so a consumer reads only the
carrier rows transported through $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$.

Next use \autoref{thm:belief-tastegate-carrier-recognition}. The TasteGate
marker \texttt{BEDC.Derived.BeliefUp.taste\_gate} fixes the accepted carrier
boundary and contributes no additional evidence row.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:belief-posterior-trace-transport-obligation} and
\autoref{thm:belief-finite-evidence-ledger-coverage}. The posterior endpoint is
read from the update trace, and the finite ledger covers exactly the listed
evidence rows.

The cited clauses exhaust the public $\NameCert_{\BeliefUp}$ surface. Since no
clause exports a hidden prior source, external observation stream, host update
primitive, likelihood object outside the probability certificate, or bridge
datum, no consumer can read one from the public surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Belief mature consumer exhaustion]
\label{thm:belief-mature-consumer-exhaustion}
Every downstream consumer of the public $\BeliefUp$ packet reads only the prior
row, observation row, update trace, probability certificate row, posterior
endpoint, finite evidence ledger, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and the
TasteGate carrier boundary named by
\texttt{BEDC.Derived.BeliefUp.taste\_gate}. Every such read factors through
\autoref{thm:belief-public-namecert-surface}.

No hidden likelihood object, host update primitive, external observation
stream, or extra probability witness is exported by the mature packet surface.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.BeliefUp.BeliefObservationUpdateCarrier\_mature\_consumer\_exhaustion}
\begin{proof}
Project the consumer read through
\autoref{thm:belief-public-namecert-surface}. The public surface is already the
assembled observation-update carrier with its classifier transport, ledger
exactness, TasteGate recognition, posterior trace, and finite evidence
coverage rows.

The six source clauses give the exhaustive list. Apply
\autoref{thm:belief-classifier-transport-obligation},
\autoref{thm:belief-ledger-exactness-obligation},
\autoref{thm:belief-tastegate-carrier-recognition},
\autoref{thm:belief-posterior-trace-transport-obligation},
\autoref{thm:belief-finite-evidence-ledger-coverage}, and
\autoref{thm:belief-public-namecert-surface} to reduce each consumer read to
one displayed row of the packet.

The checked TasteGate marker displayed in \autoref{sec:belief-carrier}, named
\texttt{BEDC.Derived.BeliefUp.taste\_gate}, supplies only the carrier boundary.
It contributes no hidden likelihood object, host update primitive, external
observation stream, or extra probability witness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Belief finite evidence consumer completeness]
\label{thm:belief-finite-evidence-consumer-completeness}
Every finite consumer of an accepted $\BeliefUp$ packet reads exactly the prior
row, observation row, posterior endpoint, finite evidence ledger, classifier
transport rows, posterior trace transport rows, and the TasteGate carrier named
by \texttt{BEDC.Derived.BeliefUp.taste\_gate}. Conversely, those rows repack as
the public belief consumer surface.

The packet exposes no hidden likelihood object, host update primitive, external
observation stream, or extra probability witness beyond those rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:belief-tastegate-carrier-recognition}. The named TasteGate
carrier fixes the accepted $\BeliefUp$ boundary without adding an evidence row.

Use \autoref{thm:belief-posterior-trace-transport-obligation} to route the
posterior endpoint and posterior trace through the displayed $\hsame$, $\Cont$,
and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ coordinates.

Next apply \autoref{thm:belief-finite-evidence-ledger-coverage}. This exhausts
the finite evidence ledger available to any accepted packet consumer.

Finally use \autoref{thm:belief-public-namecert-surface}. The prior,
observation, posterior, evidence, classifier-transport, posterior-trace, and
TasteGate rows repack as exactly the public belief consumer surface, with no
remaining coordinate for a hidden likelihood object, host update primitive,
external observation stream, or extra probability witness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Belief sibling dependency lattice]
\label{thm:belief-sibling-dependency-lattice}
Every public $\BeliefUp$ consumer reads $\NatUp$ as the finite index spine, $\SeqUp$ as the update trace, and $\ProbSpaceUp$ as the probability certificate before the TasteGate carrier boundary is opened. The packet exports no hidden likelihood object, host update primitive, or extra probability witness outside those sibling rows and the displayed public $\NameCert_{\BeliefUp}$ surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply \autoref{thm:belief-classifier-transport-obligation}. The transported prior, observation, update trace, and probability rows must remain visible finite rows of the same accepted packet, so the finite index spine is read through the displayed $\NatUp$ dependency before the consumer opens the carrier boundary.

Next use \autoref{thm:belief-posterior-trace-transport-obligation}. The posterior endpoint is determined by the transported update trace, and that trace is the $\SeqUp$ row recorded by the packet rather than a host update primitive.

Apply \autoref{thm:belief-finite-evidence-ledger-coverage}. The evidence ledger covers the prior, observation, update trace, probability row, and posterior endpoint, so the probability contribution is exactly the $\ProbSpaceUp$ certificate row already present in the finite ledger.

Finally use \autoref{thm:belief-public-namecert-surface}. The public $\NameCert_{\BeliefUp}$ surface repacks these sibling rows and the TasteGate carrier boundary, leaving no coordinate for a hidden likelihood object, host update primitive, or extra probability witness.
\end{proof}
