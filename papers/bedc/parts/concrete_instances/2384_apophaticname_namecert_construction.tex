\begin{definition}[ApophaticName carrier]
\label{def:apophatic-name-carrier}
An $\ApophaticNameUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ socket-name packet
$$

$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the socket-name row, recording the named boundary position, the local history window, and the chapter site at which the external supply is requested;
\item $R$ is the requested supply-shape row, recording the finite interface that a caller would have to provide without asserting that the supply is generated by the substrate;
\item $G$ is the refusal-gate row, recording that the requested supply is not admitted as an axiom, quotient representative, hidden theorem, host object, or substrate constructor;
\item $L$ is the refusal-ledger row, recording each permitted consumer read and each barred conversion from socket to internal content;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the socket name, requested supply shape, refusal gate, ledger row, continuation route, package provenance, and local naming row;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which consumers read the socket, the request, the refusal decision, and the ledger entry;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, socket names, requested supply shapes, refusal gates, refusal ledgers, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ApophaticNameUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no supplied object behind the socket, no theorem asserting that the requested supply exists, no quotient equality for external positions, and no host-side realization of the named dependency.
