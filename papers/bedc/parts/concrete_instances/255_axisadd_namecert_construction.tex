\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for \(\AxisAddUp\)}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-axisadd-namecert}
\origin{ai}

The additive seed on the \(\Ezero\)-spine is licensed by continuation of zero-spine histories. It is not a primitive operation. The zero-spine obligation surface for \(\AxisAddUp\) is the source, pattern, classifier, stability, ledger, and checked algebra rows recorded below.

\section{Continuation on the zero-spine}

\begin{definition}[Additive source specification (zero-spine)]
\label{def:axisadd-source-specification}
The source specification \(\mathsf{AxisAddSource}\) consists of ordered pairs \((h, k)\) of zero-spine histories.
\end{definition}
\leandef{BEDC.Derived.AxisZeckendorf.AxisAdd.AxisAddSourceSpec}

\begin{definition}[Additive pattern specification (zero-spine)]
\label{def:axisadd-pattern-specification}
The pattern specification \(\mathsf{AxisAddPattern}\) is the continuation predicate
$$
  \ContR(h, k, r)
$$
together with \(\mathsf{ZeroSpine}(h)\) and \(\mathsf{ZeroSpine}(k)\).
\end{definition}
\leandef{BEDC.Derived.AxisZeckendorf.AxisAdd.AxisAddPatternSpec}

\begin{definition}[Additive classifier specification (zero-spine)]
\label{def:axisadd-classifier-specification}
The classifier specification \(\mathsf{AxisAddClassifier}\) identifies two additive results \(r_1\) and \(r_2\) of \((h, k)\) when both share the same zero-spine pattern witness and \(\HSame(r_1, r_2)\) holds.
\end{definition}
\leandef{BEDC.Derived.AxisZeckendorf.AxisAdd.AxisAddClassifierSpec}

\begin{theorem}[Left unit on the zero-spine]
\label{thm:axisadd-left-unit}
For every zero-spine history \(h\), continuation by the empty history on the left produces \(h\):
$$
  \ContR(\emp, h, h).
$$
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AxisZeckendorf.AxisAdd.AxisAddCont\_left\_unit\_zeroSpine}
\begin{proof}
This is the kernel left-unit theorem of \(\ContR\) restricted to a zero-spine input.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[Additive stability certificate (zero-spine)]
\label{def:axisadd-stability-certificate}
The stability certificate \(\mathsf{AxisAddStability}\) records the checked zero-spine rows for left unit, result zero-spine closure, determinacy, right unit, associativity, and commutativity.
\end{definition}
\leandef{BEDC.Derived.AxisZeckendorf.AxisAdd.AxisAddStabilityCert}
\leanvariant{BEDC.Derived.AxisZeckendorf.AxisAdd.axisAddStabilityCert}

\begin{definition}[Additive ledger policy (zero-spine)]
\label{def:axisadd-ledger-policy}
The ledger policy \(\mathsf{AxisAddLedger}\) records the input pair \((h, k)\) and the continuation witness behind any visible additive result.
\end{definition}
\leandef{BEDC.Derived.AxisZeckendorf.AxisAdd.AxisAddLedgerPolicy}

\section{The certificate}

\begin{theorem}[Concrete \(\AxisAddUp\) naming certificate]
\label{thm:concrete-axisadd-namecert}
The tuple
$$
  \mathsf{NameCert}_{\AxisAddUp}
  :=
  (\mathsf{AxisAddSource},
   \mathsf{AxisAddPattern},
   \mathsf{AxisAddClassifier},
   \mathsf{AxisAddStability},
   \mathsf{AxisAddLedger})

\end{theorem}
\leandef{BEDC.Derived.AxisZeckendorf.AxisAdd.axisAdd\_namecert}
\begin{proof}
Source, pattern, classifier, stability, and ledger fields are supplied by the preceding definitions. The stability field includes the checked zero-spine rows for left unit, result closure, determinacy, right unit, associativity, and commutativity.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[\(\AxisAddUp\) is licensed, not primitive]
\label{cor:axisaddup-licensed-not-primitive}
The interface \(\AxisAddUp\) is licensed by \(\mathsf{NameCert}_{\AxisAddUp}\). It is not a primitive operation of the finite kernel.
\end{corollary}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AxisZeckendorf.AxisAdd.AxisAdd\_semantic\_name\_certificate}

\begin{theorem}[AxisAdd dependency on the AxisNat zero-spine]
\label{thm:axisadd-axisnat-sibling-dependency}
The $\AxisAddUp$ source, pattern, classifier, and stability rows are read over the $\AxisNatUp$ zero-spine carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-axisnat-namecert} before they become an additive continuation certificate. Thus an accepted $\AxisAddUp$ certificate is a consumer of the AxisNat zero-spine source surface, followed by the continuation row $\ContR(h,k,r)$ and the checked additive rows for determinacy, unit laws, associativity, and commutativity.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AxisAddUp.AxisAddTasteGate\_single\_carrier\_alignment}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the source and pattern specifications. They require the ordered input pair $(h,k)$ to satisfy the zero-spine carrier condition before the continuation witness $\ContR(h,k,r)$ can serve as an additive pattern.

Apply zero-spine preservation for continuation. This keeps the result row on the same AxisNat carrier surface, so the additive classifier is still read over the zero-spine source rather than over an ambient arithmetic carrier.

Use result determinacy to identify any two displayed continuation results over the same zero-spine source pair. Then apply the left-unit, right-unit, associativity, and commutativity rows to package the stable additive law surface.

The resulting certificate therefore depends on the sibling AxisNat zero-spine chapter for its carrier and source discipline, and only then exposes the $\AxisAddUp$ continuation certificate.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AxisAddUp.AxisAddUp\_single\_carrier\_alignment}

\begin{theorem}[Result determinacy on the zero-spine]
\label{thm:axisadd-result-determinacy}
If $\mathsf{ZeroSpine}(h)$, $\mathsf{ZeroSpine}(k)$, $\ContR(h, k, r)$, and $\ContR(h, k, r')$, then $\HSame(r, r')$. Equivalently, two displayed $\mathsf{AxisAddPattern}$ witnesses over the same ordered source $(h, k)$ determine an $\mathsf{AxisAddClassifier}$ witness between their results.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply the finite-kernel continuation determinacy theorem to the two continuation witnesses over the same ordered source. It yields $\HSame(r, r')$ for the displayed results.

Unfold the $\mathsf{AxisAddPattern}$ and $\mathsf{AxisAddClassifier}$ specifications. The zero-spine source witnesses and the two continuation witnesses supply the pattern fields, and the determinacy result supplies the classifier equality row.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AxisZeckendorf.AxisAdd.AxisAddCont\_result\_determinacy}

\begin{theorem}[Zero-spine result inversion for $\AxisAddUp$]
\label{thm:axisadd-zero-spine-result-inversion}
For histories $h,k,r:\mathsf{BHist}$, if $\ContR(h,k,r)$ and $\mathsf{ZeroSpine}(r)$, then
$$
\mathsf{ZeroSpine}(h)\wedge\mathsf{ZeroSpine}(k).
$$
Thus a displayed $\AxisAddUp$ continuation whose result lies on the $\Ezero$-spine has both its left prefix and right suffix on the same spine.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Induct on the displayed derivation of $\ContR(h,k,r)$.

In the empty-tail generator, the continuation is $\ContR(h,\emp,h)$ and the result is $h$. The assumed result witness is exactly $\mathsf{ZeroSpine}(h)$, while $\mathsf{ZeroSpine}(\emp)$ is supplied by \autoref{lem:zero-spine-empty-base}. Thus $\mathsf{ZeroSpine}(h)\wedge\mathsf{ZeroSpine}(\emp)$ holds.

In the zero-tail step, the derivation has predecessor $\ContR(h,k_0,r_0)$ and conclusion $\ContR(h,\Ezero(k_0),\Ezero(r_0))$. The result assumption is $\mathsf{ZeroSpine}(\Ezero(r_0))$. By \autoref{lem:zero-spine-e0-inversion}, obtain $\mathsf{ZeroSpine}(r_0)$. The induction hypothesis applied to the predecessor continuation gives $\mathsf{ZeroSpine}(h)\wedge\mathsf{ZeroSpine}(k_0)$. Applying \autoref{lem:zero-spine-closure-under-zero-extension} to the second component gives $\mathsf{ZeroSpine}(\Ezero(k_0))$. Hence the step conclusion is $\mathsf{ZeroSpine}(h)\wedge\mathsf{ZeroSpine}(\Ezero(k_0))$.

In the one-tail step, the derivation has result $\Eone(r_0)$, so the result assumption is $\mathsf{ZeroSpine}(\Eone(r_0))$. This contradicts \autoref{lem:zero-spine-no-one-extension}. Therefore no one-tail continuation can have a zero-spine result, and the implication holds in that branch.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AxisZeckendorf.AxisAdd.AxisAddCont\_zeroSpine\_result\_inversion}

\begin{corollary}[Zero-spine source/result equivalence for $\AxisAddUp$]
\label{cor:axisadd-zero-spine-source-result-equivalence}
For histories $h,k,r:\mathsf{BHist}$ with $\ContR(h,k,r)$,
$$
\mathsf{ZeroSpine}(r)
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
\mathsf{ZeroSpine}(h)\wedge\mathsf{ZeroSpine}(k).
$$
\end{corollary}
\begin{proof}
The forward direction is \autoref{thm:axisadd-zero-spine-result-inversion}. The reverse direction is \autoref{thm:axisadd-cont-preserves-zerospine} applied to the displayed continuation witness.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AxisZeckendorf.AxisAdd.AxisAddCont\_zeroSpine\_result\_sources\_iff}

\begin{theorem}[AxisAdd zero-spine standard bridge boundary]
\label{thm:axisadd-zero-spine-standard-bridge-boundary}
The public zero-spine result inversion and source/result equivalence identify
the standard bridge boundary for the $\AxisAddUp$ zero-spine addition surface.
The boundary is over $\mathsf{ZeroSpine}$, $\ContR$, $\HSame$, and the displayed
ledger for the two source histories, the continuation witness, and the result
history. It does not identify this surface with full $\AddUp$ and does not add
arithmetic outside the zero-spine carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The result-inversion theorem reads a displayed $\ContR(h,k,r)$ witness from the
result side: when $r$ lies on the zero spine, it recovers the two zero-spine
source witnesses. The source/result equivalence supplies the converse direction
over the same continuation witness.

Together these two public rows give the bridge boundary. A consumer may move
between the zero-spine source pair and the zero-spine result exactly through the
displayed $\ContR$ ledger and the endpoint classifier $\HSame$.

The carrier is still $\mathsf{ZeroSpine}$ rather than the ordinary $\AddUp$
carrier. Hence the boundary exports only the zero-spine surface and leaves full
addition identification outside the claim.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AxisZeckendorf.AxisAdd.AxisAddUp\_StdBridge}
