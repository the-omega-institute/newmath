It sits after the probability source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-probspace-namecert}, the measurable random-variable rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-randomvar-namecert}, the law readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-distribution-namecert}, the regular rational window of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. \begin{definition}[Central-limit finite-window carrier]\label{def:central-limit-finite-window-carrier}
A $\CentralLimitFiniteWindowUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
(P,V,S,K,D,R,E,H,C,Q,N).
$$
Here $P$ is the source $\ProbSpaceUp$ row carrying the event classifier and total-mass normalization. The row $V$ is the displayed iid $\RandomVarUp$ window over $P$, including the finite index range and the shared law token read by the window. The row $S$ is the finite partial-sum ledger naming the displayed sum, the sample count, and the centered numerator. The row $K$ is the centering and scaling row, recording the finite mean token, variance or spread token, positive scale request, and normalized finite-sum expression. The row $D$ is the $\DistributionUp$ readback for the normalized finite sum and its comparison request. The row $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ window that presents rational test intervals, tolerances, and finite comparison samples for the distribution read. The row $E$ is the $\RealUp$ normal-window seal naming the terminal real comparison against the displayed normal row at the requested finite window. The row $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the probability source, iid window, partial-sum ledger, centering and scaling row, distribution readback, RegSeqRat window, Real normal-window seal, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows. The row $C$ records $\Cont$ replay from a normal-facing read through $P,V,S,K,D,R,E$ and then through the structural rows. The row $Q$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CentralLimitFiniteWindowUp$, $\ProbSpaceUp$, $\RandomVarUp$, $\DistributionUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$. The row $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CentralLimitFiniteWindowUp}$ row. The classifier compares accepted carriers only through these eleven rows.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Central-limit finite-window NameCert obligations]\label{thm:central-limit-finite-window-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CentralLimitFiniteWindowUp$ carrier $(P,V,S,K,D,R,E,H,C,Q,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CentralLimitFiniteWindowUp}$ surface consists exactly of the finite rows in \autoref{def:central-limit-finite-window-carrier}. Carrier admission displays the probability source, iid random-variable window, finite partial-sum ledger, centering and scaling row, distribution readback, RegSeqRat comparison window, RealUp normal-window seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row. The certificate imports no host asymptotic central limit theorem, characteristic-function limit, Lindeberg replacement principle, countable-choice schedule, quotient distribution equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness witness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier rows in order. The probability source $P$ and iid random-variable window $V$ are the only stochastic data exposed to the certificate, and the partial-sum ledger $S$ fixes the finite sum before any normal-facing read is visible.

The centering and scaling row $K$ then determines the normalized finite expression. The distribution row $D$ and RegSeqRat window $R$ provide the only comparison surface through which a finite law read can reach the terminal $\RealUp$ normal-window seal $E$.

Finally $H,C,Q,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same displayed route. Since the carrier has no coordinate for an asymptotic theorem, characteristic-function limit, quotient law equality, countable choice, or ambient completeness, the listed rows exhaust the local finite NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Central-limit finite-window normal handoff]\label{thm:central-limit-finite-window-normal-handoff}

$$
P\longmapsto V\longmapsto S\longmapsto K\longmapsto D\longmapsto R\longmapsto E\longmapsto H,C,Q,N
$$
before any consumer receives a normal-window comparison. Thus the read is the displayed iid finite-sum, normalization, distribution, RegSeqRat, and RealUp handoff itself; it exports no host asymptotic CLT, countable choice, quotient distribution equality, characteristic-function convergence principle, or ambient real completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a normal-facing request. The only probability data available are the source row $P$ and the finite iid window $V$, so the request must first read the displayed sample range and shared law token.

The partial-sum row $S$ and normalization row $K$ are then forced. They provide the finite sum, centering token, scaling token, and normalized expression that the downstream distribution read consumes.

The request can reach a normal-window comparison only through $D,R,E$. The distribution row $D$ names the finite law read, the RegSeqRat row $R$ exposes rational windows and tolerances, and the RealUp row $E$ seals the displayed normal comparison. The structural rows $H,C,Q,N$ preserve that route without adding analytic content.

Therefore every accepted read factors through the finite iid-sum, normalization, distribution, RegSeqRat, and RealUp rows. The excluded asymptotic, choice, quotient, characteristic-function, and completeness principles cannot be inserted into the packet.
\end{proof}
