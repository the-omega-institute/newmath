\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\IwasawaUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-iwasawa-namecert}

The Iwasawa-theory interface $\IwasawaUp$ packages the projective limit of class groups of cyclotomic $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extensions of a number field together with the Iwasawa main conjecture relating it to a $p$-adic L-function. It depends on $\NameCert_{\CyclotomicUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cyclotomic-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\IdealClassUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-idealclass-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:iwasawa-carrier}

\begin{definition}[Iwasawa cyclotomic tower carrier]
\label{def:iwasawa-cyclotomic-tower-carrier}
An $\IwasawaUp$ cyclotomic tower carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
whose rows are:
\begin{enumerate}
\item the cyclotomic layer rows read from $\NameCert_{\CyclotomicUp}$ by
\autoref{thm:cyclotomic-iwasawa-source-boundary};
\item the ideal-class endpoint rows read from $\NameCert_{\IdealClassUp}$ by
\autoref{thm:idealclass-scoped-source-certificate};
\item transition rows between successive displayed levels, recorded as
$\Cont$ ledgers and compared by $\hsame$;
\item unary level indices identifying the finite window of tower levels;
\item one $\Pkg$ provenance row tying the cyclotomic source, ideal-class
source, and transition ledger to the same carried packet.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier records only these finite BEDC rows. It does not contain a host
projective limit, an analytic $p$-adic $L$-function, or an equality asserted
outside the displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\Pkg$ surface.
\end{definition}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:iwasawa-certificate}

\begin{definition}[Iwasawa classgroup limit classifier]
\label{def:iwasawa-classgroup-limit-classifier}
For two carriers of \autoref{def:iwasawa-cyclotomic-tower-carrier}, the
$\mathsf{IwaCls}$ classifier holds when their unary level windows agree by
$\hsame$, each displayed level has matching $\IdealClassUp$ classifier rows,
the cyclotomic source rows agree through the classifier exported by
$\NameCert_{\CyclotomicUp}$, and the transition ledgers are compatible under
$\hsame$ after levelwise transport. The agreement is levelwise and ledgerwise;
it is not quotient equality of a host inverse limit.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Iwasawa transition ledger stability]
\label{thm:iwasawa-transition-ledger-stability}
If two $\IwasawaUp$ carriers are related by $\mathsf{IwaCls}$, then $\hsame$
transport preserves every accepted transition ledger in
\autoref{def:iwasawa-cyclotomic-tower-carrier}. The transported ledger is again
accepted over the transported cyclotomic rows and the transported ideal-class
rows, with the same unary level index window and $\Pkg$ provenance scope.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the cyclotomic component of the classifier. By
\autoref{thm:cyclotomic-iwasawa-source-boundary}, the downstream Iwasawa packet
may read only the displayed source rows, primitive-root endpoint, factorization
ledger, and provenance package from $\NameCert_{\CyclotomicUp}$.

Project the ideal-class component next. The scoped source certificate of
\autoref{thm:idealclass-scoped-source-certificate} transports carried
fractional-ideal endpoints, principal-classifier witnesses, and quotient
operation rows through the same $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, and $\Pkg$ surface.

For each adjacent pair of displayed unary levels, compose these transported
source projections with the carried $\Cont$ transition row. Since
$\mathsf{IwaCls}$ requires transition-ledger compatibility under $\hsame$, the
transported transition row is accepted at the corresponding transported levels.
The $\Pkg$ provenance row is unchanged except for the same componentwise
transport, so no external tower object is introduced.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Iwasawa source obligation surface]
\label{thm:iwasawa-source-obligation-surface}
The carrier of \autoref{def:iwasawa-cyclotomic-tower-carrier}, the classifier
of \autoref{def:iwasawa-classgroup-limit-classifier}, and the stability theorem
\autoref{thm:iwasawa-transition-ledger-stability} form the source obligation
surface for $\IwasawaUp$. The surface consists exactly of cyclotomic source
rows, ideal-class endpoint and classifier rows, unary level indices, transition
$\Cont$ ledgers, $\hsame$ transport rows, and one $\Pkg$ provenance packet. It
closes carrier formation, classifier agreement, transition stability, source
exactness, and finite ledger readback for this packet, but it does not claim
the Iwasawa main conjecture or analytic $p$-adic $L$-function equality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier formation is the packet definition itself: every accepted row is one
of the cyclotomic, ideal-class, level-index, transition, or provenance rows
listed in \autoref{def:iwasawa-cyclotomic-tower-carrier}.

Classifier agreement is supplied by
\autoref{def:iwasawa-classgroup-limit-classifier}. It compares the same finite
rows level by level and records transition compatibility as carried $\hsame$
data, so it never appeals to an ambient inverse-limit quotient.

Transition stability is exactly
\autoref{thm:iwasawa-transition-ledger-stability}. It shows that accepted
transition ledgers remain accepted after componentwise carrier transport
through the cyclotomic and ideal-class sources.

Exactness and ledger readback follow by the reverse projection. Any
$\IwasawaUp$ source row permitted by the surface must be one of the displayed
packet rows or a row projected by the cited $\CyclotomicUp$ and $\IdealClassUp$
source certificates. Repacking these rows gives the stated obligation surface
and excludes analytic main-conjecture data from the certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Iwasawa transition ledger exactness]
\label{thm:iwasawa-transition-ledger-exactness}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:iwasawa-cyclotomic-tower-carrier}, the accepted
transition rows are exactly the adjacent-level $\Cont$ ledger entries between
the displayed cyclotomic rows and the displayed ideal-class rows. Soundness
means that every accepted transition row has matching unary endpoints,
compatible $\hsame$ transport, and the $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance tying it to
the same cyclotomic and ideal-class packet. Completeness means that every
adjacent pair of displayed levels carrying those cyclotomic rows, ideal-class
endpoint rows, $\hsame$ transports, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance determines an
accepted transition ledger row and no additional transition source.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For soundness, start with an accepted transition row. By
\autoref{def:iwasawa-cyclotomic-tower-carrier}, it is carried as a $\Cont$
ledger between successive displayed unary levels and is packaged with the same
cyclotomic source, ideal-class source, and provenance row.

Apply \autoref{thm:iwasawa-transition-ledger-stability}. The transported ledger
remains accepted over the transported cyclotomic and ideal-class rows, and the
same $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance scope is preserved.

For completeness, begin with adjacent displayed levels equipped with the
cyclotomic source rows of
\autoref{thm:cyclotomic-iwasawa-source-boundary}, the ideal-class rows of
\autoref{thm:idealclass-scoped-source-certificate}, compatible $\hsame$
transport, and one provenance packet. Repack these rows using
\autoref{thm:iwasawa-source-obligation-surface}; the only transition slot in
that surface is the adjacent-level $\Cont$ ledger. Hence the resulting row is
accepted, and the source inventory leaves no second transition kind.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Iwasawa downstream consumer boundary]
\label{thm:iwasawa-downstream-consumer-boundary}
Every downstream consumer of the finite $\IwasawaUp$ tower window may read only
the cyclotomic source rows, ideal-class endpoint and classifier rows, unary
level indices, accepted adjacent-level transition ledgers, $\hsame$ transport
rows, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance of
\autoref{thm:iwasawa-source-obligation-surface}. No downstream consumer row is a
projective-limit quotient, an analytic main-conjecture row, or a direct
$p$-adic $L$-function equality row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First project any downstream read through
\autoref{thm:iwasawa-source-obligation-surface}. The theorem lists exactly the
finite carrier, classifier, stability, exactness, and ledger-readback rows that
belong to the source surface.

The cyclotomic portion is bounded by
\autoref{thm:cyclotomic-iwasawa-source-boundary}. It supplies only the
displayed source rows, primitive-root endpoint, factorization ledger, and
provenance package consumed by the tower packet.

The ideal-class portion is bounded by
\autoref{thm:idealclass-scoped-source-certificate}. It supplies the endpoint
and classifier rows needed at the displayed levels, again through the finite
$\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ surface.

Finally use \autoref{thm:iwasawa-transition-ledger-exactness}. Transition reads
are exactly adjacent-level $\Cont$ ledger rows between the already displayed
sources. Therefore the consumer boundary is exhausted by the listed finite rows
and cannot contain projective-limit quotient data, analytic main-conjecture
data, or $p$-adic $L$-function equality data.
\end{proof}
