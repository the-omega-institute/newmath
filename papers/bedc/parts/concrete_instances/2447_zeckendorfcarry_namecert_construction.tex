\chapter{A Zeckendorf-Variant Naming Certificate for $\ZCarryUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-zeckendorfcarry-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\ZCarryUp$ surface packages the generated Zeckendorf carry classifier as a finite $\NameCert$ object over displayed $\BHist$ words. Its source is the single non-normal carry window $\mathtt{word\_011}$, its target is the normal window $\mathtt{word\_100}$, and its classifier row records carry transport without identifying the two histories by $\HSame$.

The dependency anchors are $\ZeckendorfNormalUp$, $\mathsf{ZCarry}$, $\mathsf{ZNormal}$, $\HSame$, $\BHist$, and the displayed words of \autoref{def:axiszeckendorf-display-words}.

The accepted carry packet consumes the normal-target witness of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-zeckendorf-normal-namecert} and the classifier boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-zeckendorf-carry-classifier-namecert} before downstream consumers read the target row. This dependency is substantive: $\ZeckendorfNormalUp$ supplies target normality, while $\ZeckendorfCarryClassifierUp$ supplies the non-$\HSame$ carry-transport boundary.

\begin{definition}[Zeckendorf carry concrete carrier]
\label{def:zeckendorfcarry-concrete-carrier}
A $\ZCarryUp$ concrete carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(h,k,G,S,T,B,L,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $h=\mathtt{word\_011}$ is the non-normal source window;
\item $k=\mathtt{word\_100}$ is the normal target window;
\item $G$ is the generated carry row $\mathsf{ZCarry}(h,k)$;
\item $S$ stores the source rejection $\neg\mathsf{ZNormal}(h)$;
\item $T$ stores the target acceptance $\mathsf{ZNormal}(k)$;
\item $B$ stores the classifier boundary $\neg\HSame(h,k)$;
\item $L$ is the ledger row recording the visible generator, source rejection, target acceptance, and non-$\HSame$ boundary;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ZCarryUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares carry packets by the generated row $G$ and the boundary row $B$. It exports no arithmetic value, Fibonacci weight, quotient of histories, or equality between the source and target words.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Zeckendorf carry generator boundary]
\label{thm:zeckendorfcarry-generator-boundary}
Every accepted $\ZCarryUp$ packet is generated by the single displayed carry row
$$
\mathsf{ZCarry}(\mathtt{word\_011},\mathtt{word\_100}).
$$
Consequently the source row is forced to $\mathtt{word\_011}$ and the target row is forced to $\mathtt{word\_100}$; no second carry generator, hidden target, or arithmetic equality row belongs to the carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the carrier of \autoref{def:zeckendorfcarry-concrete-carrier}. The only generator coordinate is $G$, and $G$ is the closed carry row from the source window to the target window.

The ledger row $L$ records the same generator together with the source rejection, target acceptance, and non-$\HSame$ boundary. Since the carrier has no coordinate for another generator or for a value interpretation, an accepted packet can only expose the displayed carry row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Zeckendorf carry NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:zeckendorfcarry-namecert-obligations}
The $\NameCert_{\ZCarryUp}$ surface has five obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier habitation: the displayed packet $K=(h,k,G,S,T,B,L,N)$ is inhabited by $\mathtt{word\_011}$, $\mathtt{word\_100}$, and the generated carry row;
\item classifier transport: accepted packets are compared through the generated carry row $G$, not through $\HSame(h,k)$;
\item source boundary: the source row carries $\neg\mathsf{ZNormal}(\mathtt{word\_011})$;
\item target boundary: the target row carries $\mathsf{ZNormal}(\mathtt{word\_100})$;
\item ledger boundary: the packet records carry transport, source rejection, target acceptance, and non-$\HSame$ separation without exporting an arithmetic value or history quotient.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier habitation is the displayed packet of \autoref{def:zeckendorfcarry-concrete-carrier}. The source and target words are the two fixed $\BHist$ histories of \autoref{def:axiszeckendorf-display-words}, and the generator row is the closed $\mathsf{ZCarry}$ constructor.

The classifier transport obligation reads the $G$ and $B$ rows together. The $G$ row licenses carry movement from source to target, while $B$ prevents that movement from being read as $\HSame$.

The source and target boundaries are the normal-form rows already exposed by $\ZeckendorfNormalUp$: $\mathtt{word\_011}$ is rejected and $\mathtt{word\_100}$ is accepted. The ledger row packages exactly these witnesses, so no arithmetic value, quotient, or equality row is available to the public surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Zeckendorf carry non-$\HSame$ boundary]
\label{thm:zeckendorfcarry-non-hsame-boundary}
For every accepted $\ZCarryUp$ packet $K=(h,k,G,S,T,B,L,N)$, the carry classifier separates transport from history sameness: $G$ records $\mathsf{ZCarry}(h,k)$, while $B$ records $\neg\HSame(h,k)$. Thus the packet can move a displayed non-normal window to a displayed normal window only as a classifier step, not as a kernel identification of histories.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the $G$ coordinate of the packet. By \autoref{thm:zeckendorfcarry-generator-boundary}, it is the displayed carry from $\mathtt{word\_011}$ to $\mathtt{word\_100}$.

Project the boundary coordinate $B$. It records that the same source and target histories are not related by $\HSame$. Hence carry transport and history sameness occupy distinct rows of the certificate.

The conclusion is a scope statement about the carrier: consumers may read the carry row, but they cannot replace it by a kernel history identification. The ledger $L$ preserves this separation as part of the visible certificate surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Zeckendorf carry normal-target handoff]
\label{thm:zeckendorfcarry-normal-target-handoff}
Every downstream consumer of a $\ZCarryUp$ packet receives a normal target row only after the generator boundary, source rejection, and non-$\HSame$ separation have been fixed. The handoff to $\ZeckendorfNormalUp$ is the target witness $\mathsf{ZNormal}(\mathtt{word\_100})$; it does not carry a Fibonacci value-preservation claim or a uniqueness-of-value row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted packet and read its generator row. The generator boundary fixes the source as $\mathtt{word\_011}$ and the target as $\mathtt{word\_100}$.

Next read the source and target normal-form rows. The source row records rejection by the normal-form classifier, while the target row records acceptance by the same structural classifier.

Finally read the non-$\HSame$ boundary. Since the source and target are not identified as histories, the downstream $\ZeckendorfNormalUp$ consumer receives only the normal target witness and the ledger explaining how it was reached. Arithmetic value preservation remains outside this certificate surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Concrete $\ZCarryUp$ packet surface]
\label{thm:zeckendorfcarry-concrete-packet-surface}
The carrier, generator boundary, NameCert obligations, non-$\HSame$ boundary, and normal-target handoff form the concrete $\ZCarryUp$ certificate surface. Its public content is finite carry transport from $\mathtt{word\_011}$ to $\mathtt{word\_100}$ together with the proof-facing separation between carry classification and history sameness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier fixes the eight displayed rows of the packet. The generator boundary pins the only carry row, and the NameCert obligations state how the source, target, classifier, stability, and ledger fields are read.

The non-$\HSame$ boundary prevents the generator from becoming a history equality. The normal-target handoff identifies the only downstream normal-form consumer row.

Together these rows exhaust the concrete certificate surface. There is no carrier coordinate for Fibonacci values, value preservation, uniqueness of arithmetic value, or a quotient of histories, so those claims are not exported by $\ZCarryUp$.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Zeckendorf carry strict local obstruction]
\label{cor:zeckendorfcarry-strict-local-obstruction}
Let $K=(h,k,G,S,T,B,L,N)$ be an accepted $\ZCarryUp$ packet. Suppose a displayed failure, refusal, separation, or countermodel row is attached to a consumer of $K$ and is used against a named converse or reuse route. Then the row is a strict local obstruction: it blocks only the route whose read asks to add or replace one of the displayed carry coordinates
$$
\mathsf{ZCarry}(\mathtt{word\_011},\mathtt{word\_100}),\quad
S:\neg\mathsf{ZNormal}(\mathtt{word\_011}),\quad
T:\mathsf{ZNormal}(\mathtt{word\_100}),\quad
B:\neg\HSame(h,k),\quad
L,\quad N.
$$
Every permitted consumer-facing use factors through these rows, with source $\mathtt{word\_011}$ and target $\mathtt{word\_100}$ fixed. A request for a second carry generator, a hidden target, an $\HSame(h,k)$ identification, an arithmetic equality, a Fibonacci value row, a history quotient, a global normalizer, a host theorem, or hidden external validation has no coordinate in the accepted packet.
\end{corollary}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:zeckendorfcarry-generator-boundary}. It fixes the only generated carry row as
$$
\mathsf{ZCarry}(\mathtt{word\_011},\mathtt{word\_100}),
$$
forces the source row to $\mathtt{word\_011}$ and the target row to $\mathtt{word\_100}$, and excludes any second generator, hidden target, or arithmetic equality row from the carrier. Thus a consumer cannot reinterpret a negative row as ranging over arbitrary carry windows or over an ambient arithmetic comparison; the only carry window available to the consumer is the displayed one.

Next apply \autoref{thm:zeckendorfcarry-namecert-obligations}. The carrier-habitation obligation supplies the displayed packet $K$. The classifier-transport obligation routes comparison through $G$ rather than through $\HSame(h,k)$. The source and target boundary obligations expose exactly $S:\neg\mathsf{ZNormal}(\mathtt{word\_011})$ and $T:\mathsf{ZNormal}(\mathtt{word\_100})$. The ledger obligation packages carry transport, source rejection, target acceptance, and non-$\HSame$ separation without exporting an arithmetic value or history quotient.

Now let the proposed converse or reuse route be consumer-facing. To consume $K$ it must first read the generator, normality, separation, ledger, and local naming rows just listed. If the displayed failure, refusal, separation, or countermodel row denies one of those reads, it blocks that named route at the corresponding packet coordinate. If the route instead asks for a second generator, hidden target, source-target $\HSame$ proof, arithmetic equality, value row, quotient, global normalizer, host theorem, or external validation channel, the carrier and obligation theorems give no coordinate through which that request can be read.

Therefore the obstruction is exact and local. It is a negative certificate for the named converse or reuse route inside the displayed $\ZCarryUp$ packet, and it supplies no global impossibility statement, no host-side theorem, and no hidden validation of an unlisted route.
\end{proof}
