\label{def:regular-sequence-limit-carrier}

$$

$$

\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window schedule exposing exactly the finite observations of the rational sequence name.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular rational readback row attached to the windows in $S$.
\item $Q$ is the $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ row that records the displayed limit-seal compatibility of the same $S,R$ route.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row obtained only after the regular rational route has passed through $Q$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the stream schedule, regular readback, limit-seal row, Real seal, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a finite precision request through $S,R,Q,E$ and then through the structural rows.

\begin{theorem}[RegularSequenceLimit NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:regular-sequence-limit-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RegularSequenceLimitUp$ carrier
$$
L=(S,R,Q,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\RegularSequenceLimitUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed stream schedule, regular rational readback, limit-seal row, Real seal, transport, continuation, provenance, and local name;
\item regular-name routing, requiring every limit read to pass through $S$ and $R$ before $Q$ or $E$ is available;
\item seal compatibility, requiring $Q$ to consume the same finite window and regular-readback route that supplies the $\RealUp$ seal $E$;
\item continuation exactness, requiring each finite precision request to replay through $C$ without adding a second window family, hidden modulus, quotient stream, selected host limit, or ambient completeness coordinate;
\item structural stability, requiring $H,P,N$ to transport, source, and name only the displayed rows.
\end{enumerate}
No RegularSequenceLimit obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet $L=(S,R,Q,E,H,C,P,N)$. The carrier admission row exposes exactly one finite stream schedule, one regular rational readback row, one limit-seal compatibility row, one Real seal, and the transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows.

The routing and seal-compatibility clauses follow from the displayed order. The row $R$ is the only regular rational readback attached to $S$, while $Q$ is the only limit-seal row attached to that readback. Hence a limit consumer cannot inspect $Q$ or $E$ without first reading the same $S,R$ route.

Continuation exactness is the row $C$. Each finite precision request replays the visible route and has no coordinate from which to introduce a second window family, a hidden modulus, quotient stream equality, a selected host limit, or ambient completeness.

Finally, $H,P,N$ transport, source, and name only the displayed packet. Since the carrier has no further coordinate, the five listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}
