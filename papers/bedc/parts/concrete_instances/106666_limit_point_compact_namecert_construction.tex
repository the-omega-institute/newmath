\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\LimitPointCompactUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-limit-point-compact-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges, the $\LimitPointCompactUp$ packet records the sequence-facing compactness boundary: an infinite sequence in a displayed compactly bounded carrier has a named cluster-point route through a subsequence and a real-valued convergence witness. The packet sits beside the $\SequentialCompactUp$ chapter of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-sequentialcompact-namecert}, the $\BolzanoWeierstrassUp$ chapter of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bolzanoweierstrass-namecert}, the $\RealSequenceClusterPointUp$ chapter of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-sequence-cluster-point-namecert}, and the $\RealUp$ chapter of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. It deliberately names a limit-point route for sequences, not open-cover compactness, arbitrary choice, or ambient real-completeness.

\begin{definition}[LimitPointCompact carrier]\label{def:limit-point-compact-carrier}
A $\LimitPointCompactUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(K,S,B,W,Q,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $K$ is the compactly bounded carrier row whose points are read by the sequence packet;
\item $S$ is the input sequence row, displaying a finite code for the sequence-facing object under consideration;
\item $B$ is the boundedness and membership ledger, keeping every visible sequence value inside the carrier row $K$;
\item $W$ is the $\BolzanoWeierstrassUp$ witness row that extracts a subsequence candidate from the bounded sequence data;
\item $Q$ is the $\SequentialCompactUp$ subsequence route row, naming the selected subsequence only through displayed finite evidence;
\item $E$ is the $\RealSequenceClusterPointUp$ endpoint row, reading the subsequence as converging to a real cluster point through $\RealUp$ data;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $K,S,B,W,Q,E$ and the structural rows;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from the sequence row through boundedness, subsequence extraction, endpoint reading, and local naming;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over the displayed compactness, subsequence, cluster-point, real, transport, replay, and naming rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LimitPointCompactUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier accepts exactly packets whose visible coordinates are $K,S,B,W,Q,E,H,C,P,N$. It contains no open-cover subcover coordinate, no countable-choice selector, no ambient $\RealUp$ completeness theorem, and no host-level selection of cluster points.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[LimitPointCompact NameCert obligations]\label{thm:limit-point-compact-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:limit-point-compact-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\LimitPointCompactUp}$ surface consists exactly of five finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission: every accepted object exposes the displayed packet $L=(K,S,B,W,Q,E,H,C,P,N)$;
\item bounded sequence exposure: every sequence value consumed by the packet is read through $S$ and certified inside $K$ by the ledger row $B$;
\item subsequence extraction: every cluster-point route passes through the $\BolzanoWeierstrassUp$ witness row $W$ and the $\SequentialCompactUp$ subsequence row $Q$;
\item endpoint exactness: the cluster endpoint is read only through the $\RealSequenceClusterPointUp$ row $E$ and its $\RealUp$ coordinate data;
\item ledger exhaustion: $H,C,P,N$ preserve, replay, source, and name the displayed route without adding open-cover compactness, countable choice, a selected representative, or ambient real completeness.
\end{enumerate}
Consequently the certificate names the sequence-facing limit-point compactness route and no stronger compactness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from the displayed tuple $L=(K,S,B,W,Q,E,H,C,P,N)$. The only point carrier is $K$, the only input sequence row is $S$, and the only boundedness ledger is $B$.

The subsequence part is forced by the witness inventory. The row $W$ is the Bolzano--Weierstrass extraction witness and $Q$ is the sequential-compactness subsequence route; a consumer reaches endpoint data only after those rows have been read.

The endpoint part is then supplied by $E$. It is the cluster-point row and reads the relevant real-valued convergence data through the $\RealUp$ packet, so no separate ambient completeness theorem is imported.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite route. Since the carrier has no open-cover, choice, selected-representative, or ambient-completeness coordinate, those principles cannot be exported by the local certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LimitPointCompact Bolzano--Weierstrass factorization]\label{thm:limit-point-compact-bolzano-weierstrass-factorization}
Every accepted $\LimitPointCompactUp$ limit-point consumer factors through
$$
K,S \longmapsto B \longmapsto W \longmapsto Q \longmapsto E \longmapsto H,C,P,N.
$$
Here $W$ is read from the $\BolzanoWeierstrassUp$ surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bolzanoweierstrass-namecert}, $Q$ is read from the $\SequentialCompactUp$ surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-sequentialcompact-namecert}, and $E$ is read from the $\RealSequenceClusterPointUp$ and $\RealUp$ surfaces of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-sequence-cluster-point-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The factorization exports no open-cover compactness, no countable choice, no selected representative, and no ambient real-completeness claim.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with \autoref{thm:limit-point-compact-namecert-obligations}. Its bounded sequence exposure clause places every sequence-facing read behind $K,S,B$.

Next read the extraction rows. The Bolzano--Weierstrass chapter supplies the finite witness row $W$, and the sequential compactness chapter supplies the subsequence route $Q$; neither row carries an open-cover subcover object or an unrestricted choice function.

The endpoint is then read through the real sequence cluster-point packet and the real packet. This is exactly the row $E$, so the conclusion is a displayed sequence endpoint rather than ambient completeness for all real Cauchy data.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve and name the same route. Because the carrier inventory contains no stronger compactness coordinate, every consumer remains inside the stated factorization.
\end{proof}
