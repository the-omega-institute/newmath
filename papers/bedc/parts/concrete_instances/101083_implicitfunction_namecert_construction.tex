Following the classical implicit function theorem tradition of Dieudonne and Rudin, the $\ImplicitFunctionUp$ packet records the local-solution data that a constructive BEDC consumer may inspect when an equation row is solved by a displayed derivative and an invertible linear approximation. The packet consumes the derivative source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-derivative-namecert}, the linear approximation surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-linearmap-namecert}, the finite coordinate ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-matrix-namecert}, the completeness boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completemetric-namecert}, the Picard contraction route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-picardcontraction-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. It is not a host-level theorem asserting differentiability of an ambient function space or selecting a solution germ; it is a finite NameCert route from displayed analytic rows to a local graph window.

\begin{definition}[Implicit function carrier]\label{def:implicit-function-carrier}
An $\ImplicitFunctionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
I=(E,B,D,L,M,Q,G,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $E$ is the displayed equation row. It stores the finite source variables, target variable, equation-value history, and local classifier for the read $F(x,y)=0$.
\item $B$ is the base-point row. It records the displayed point $(x_0,y_0)$, the equation read at that point, and the finite neighbourhood name in which all later rows are interpreted.
\item $D$ is the $\DerivativeUp$ row for the equation source at $B$, including the displayed partial-derivative histories consumed by the linear approximation.
\item $L$ is the invertible $\LinearMapUp$ approximation row attached to the target-variable derivative, together with its finite inverse-witness ledger.
\item $M$ is the $\MatrixUp$ coordinate row that displays the same approximation and inverse witness in finite component form for comparison and tolerance bookkeeping.
\item $Q$ is the $\PicardContractionUp$ local-solution row. It records the displayed fixed-point map, contraction budget, iterate ledger, and local nonescape side condition obtained from $D,L,M$.
\item $G$ is the local graph and window row. It records the source window, target window, graph readback, and uniqueness window exported by the Picard route.
\item $R$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after the equation, base point, derivative, linear approximation, matrix ledger, Picard route, and graph window have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the equation row, base-point row, derivative row, linear approximation row, matrix row, Picard local-solution row, graph-window row, Real seal, replay, provenance, and local name.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from an equation request through $E,B,D,L,M,Q,G,R,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ImplicitFunctionUp$, $\DerivativeUp$, $\LinearMapUp$, $\MatrixUp$, $\CompleteMetricUp$, $\PicardContractionUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ImplicitFunctionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $E,B,D,L,M,Q,G,R,H,C,P,N$. The carrier has no coordinate for host differentiability, quotient function spaces, selected solution germs, the classical inverse-function theorem, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Implicit function NameCert obligations]\label{thm:implicit-function-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\ImplicitFunctionUp$ carrier
$$
I=(E,B,D,L,M,Q,G,R,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\ImplicitFunctionUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed equation row, base-point row, derivative row, linear approximation row, matrix row, Picard row, graph-window row, Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local name;
\item derivative and invertibility ledger compatibility, requiring $D,L,M$ to display the same target-variable approximation and inverse witness;
\item Picard local-solution handoff, requiring $Q$ to be the only fixed-point route available to the packet;
\item graph-window transport, requiring $G$ to expose local reads only through the source and target windows named by $B$ and certified by $Q$;
\item Real-seal non-escape, requiring every read of $R$ to remain downstream of $E,B,D,L,M,Q,G$ and the structural rows;
\item provenance exactness, requiring $P$ and $N$ to name only the listed rows and no host differentiability theorem, quotient function space, selected solution germ, classical inverse-function theorem, or ambient completeness principle.
\end{enumerate}
No local NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:implicit-function-carrier}. The finite packet lists the equation, base point, derivative source, invertible approximation, matrix ledger, Picard route, graph window, Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local name.

The derivative and invertibility obligations are local to $D,L,M$. The derivative row supplies the displayed partial-derivative histories, the linear-map row records the inverse witness, and the matrix row exposes the same witness in component form for finite tolerance comparison.

The local solution is then routed through $Q$. Since $Q$ is the only Picard coordinate of the carrier, any fixed-point read must use the displayed contraction budget, iterate ledger, and nonescape row recorded there.

The graph-window and seal obligations are downstream. The row $G$ reads the local graph only inside the carried source and target windows, and the row $R$ is reached only after the equation, derivative, invertibility, Picard, and graph-window rows have been opened. The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name this same route without adding a host solution object.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Implicit function Picard local-solution route]\label{thm:implicit-function-picard-local-solution-route}
Every accepted $\ImplicitFunctionUp$ carrier factors each local-solution read through the route
$$
\DerivativeUp \longmapsto \LinearMapUp \longmapsto \MatrixUp \longmapsto \CompleteMetricUp \longmapsto \PicardContractionUp \longmapsto \RealUp
$$
as instantiated by $D,L,M,Q,R$ and the graph-window row $G$. In particular, the $\RealUp$ seal is consumed only after the derivative row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-derivative-namecert}, the linear-map row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-linearmap-namecert}, the matrix row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-matrix-namecert}, the complete-metric boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completemetric-namecert}, the Picard contraction route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-picardcontraction-namecert}, and the real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert} have been displayed.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the carrier coordinates. The equation and base-point rows $E,B$ provide only the local problem statement and the neighbourhood in which the route is interpreted.

The analytic source then passes through $D,L,M$. The derivative row $D$ supplies the local derivative data, the linear-map row $L$ supplies the invertible approximation, and the matrix row $M$ supplies finite componentwise comparisons for the same approximation and inverse witness.

The completeness and fixed-point part is exposed only by $Q$. Its contraction budget and nonescape side condition are the carried route to the complete-metric boundary and Picard local-solution ledger, so no alternate selected solution germ is available.

Finally $G$ repacks the Picard endpoint as a local graph-window read, and $R$ provides the terminal $\RealUp$ seal. Because $R$ is downstream of $D,L,M,Q,G$, every Real-facing local-solution read factors through the listed chapters before the seal is consumed.
\end{proof}
