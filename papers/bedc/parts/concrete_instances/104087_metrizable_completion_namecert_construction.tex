\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\MetrizableCompletionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-metrizable-completion-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Kelley and Bourbaki on metrizable topological spaces and completion, the $\MetrizableCompletionUp$ packet records the finite BEDC route by which a topology equipped with a displayed metrization can be read by the existing metric-completion surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metriccompletion-namecert}. It sits after the $\TopologyUp$ and $\MetricUp$ carrier rows, consumes the $\MetricCompletionUp$ branch and the $\CompleteMetricUp$ seal, and exposes only the $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ readback path needed for completion-facing consumers.

\begin{definition}[MetrizableCompletion carrier]
\label{def:metrizable-completion-carrier}
A $\MetrizableCompletionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Z=(T,M,Q,K,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $T$ is the $\TopologyUp$ row, including the open-set classifier, neighborhood interface, and finite continuity rows visible to the packet.
\item $M$ is the $\MetricUp$ metrization row on the same carried histories, with a displayed comparison that says the topology read through $T$ is the topology generated by the metric read through $M$.
\item $Q$ is the $\MetricCompletionUp$ branch consumed from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metriccompletion-namecert}, including its Cauchy-filter or Cauchy-net evidence route.
\item $K$ is the $\CompleteMetricUp$ seal row for the completed metric-facing object, read only after $Q$ has exposed the displayed completion branch.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule row used to name the finite observation windows inspected by the completion route.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row for numerical tolerance data.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ endpoint-seal row used when distance or convergence witnesses are compared.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the topology, metrization, metric-completion branch, complete-metric seal, stream, readback, endpoint, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ replay from topology admission through metrization, completion-branch admission, complete-metric sealing, stream/readback inspection, and endpoint comparison.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\MetrizableCompletionUp$, $\TopologyUp$, $\MetricUp$, $\MetricCompletionUp$, $\CompleteMetricUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MetrizableCompletionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for quotient completion equality, a selected representative of a Cauchy name, host topological equality, countable choice, open-cover compactness, or completeness of $\RealUp$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[MetrizableCompletion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:metrizable-completion-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\MetrizableCompletionUp$ carrier $Z=(T,M,Q,K,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\MetrizableCompletionUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the topology and metrization rows, compatibility of the topology row with the metric-generated topology, admission of the metric-completion branch $Q$, sealing by the complete-metric row $K$, stream/readback readiness through $S$ and $R$, endpoint comparison through $E$, componentwise transport through $H$, replay through $C$, provenance through $P$, and ledger non-escape for the excluded host-side principles.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:metrizable-completion-carrier}. The tuple lists the topology, metrization, metric-completion branch, complete-metric seal, stream, readback, endpoint, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

The topology-metrization compatibility is local to $T$ and $M$. A consumer may read open-set or neighborhood data only through the displayed metric generator row, and the packet gives no separate host-topology equality coordinate.

The completion-facing part then passes through $Q$ and $K$. The row $Q$ is exactly the metric-completion interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metriccompletion-namecert}; $K$ seals the resulting read as complete-metric evidence only after that branch is displayed.

Finally $S$, $R$, and $E$ provide the finite numerical route. Transport, replay, provenance, and local naming preserve the same displayed packet, so quotient completion equality, selected representatives, countable choice, open-cover compactness, host topological equality, and $\RealUp$ completeness cannot be exported.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetrizableCompletion Cauchy seal]
\label{thm:metrizable-completion-cauchy-seal}
Every completion-facing consumer of an accepted $\MetrizableCompletionUp$ carrier factors through the finite Cauchy seal
$$
T\longmapsto M\longmapsto Q\longmapsto K\longmapsto S\longmapsto R\longmapsto E\longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
In particular, a consumer cannot read the topological completion before the metrization row, cannot read complete-metric evidence before the $\MetricCompletionUp$ branch, and cannot compare convergence data before the $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ rows have been displayed.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the topology row $T$. The packet admits it only together with the metrization row $M$, so a topological completion request first becomes a metric-facing request over the same carried histories.

Next apply the metric-completion branch. The row $Q$ is the sole route to completion-facing data, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metriccompletion-namecert} already forces such data through a displayed Cauchy-filter or Cauchy-net branch.

The complete-metric seal $K$ is downstream of $Q$. It does not select limits or quotient Cauchy names; it records that the displayed completion branch has the complete-metric evidence needed by the consumer.

The numerical seal is read last. Stream windows $S$ precede regular-rational readback $R$, and the terminal endpoint comparison $E$ is the only $\RealUp$ row offered to convergence comparisons. The transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows retain this order, so no omitted host-side principle can enter the seal.
\end{proof}
