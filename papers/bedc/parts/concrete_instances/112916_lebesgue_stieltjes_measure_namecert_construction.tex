\begin{theorem}[Lebesgue--Stieltjes measure NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:lebesgue-stieltjes-measure-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\LebesgueStieltjesMeasureUp$ carrier
$$

$$
the local $\NameCert_{\LebesgueStieltjesMeasureUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the bounded-variation source $B$, Stieltjes boundary exposure through $S$, compact interval restriction through $I$, finite interval-algebra visibility through $A$, finite additivity exactness through $K$, Radon-facing regular readback through $R$, terminal real sealing through $E$, componentwise transport through $H$, displayed replay through $C$, package provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:lebesgue-stieltjes-measure-carrier}. The first three rows are the only source rows: $B$ supplies bounded-variation partition data, $S$ supplies endpoint-jump reads, and $I$ restricts the read to a displayed compact interval.

The interval algebra and additivity obligations are $A$ and $K$. Thus additivity is a finite ledger obligation rather than an appeal to a host sigma-algebra.

The downstream readback is $R,E$. The $\RadonMeasureUp$ row $R$ exposes the compact-regular consumer surface, and $E$ seals the same readback as a $\RealUp$ row.
