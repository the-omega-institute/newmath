Following Kelley and Bourbaki on uniform spaces as entourage systems, the $\UniformEntourageSpaceUp$ packet records the finite BEDC surface where diagonal, symmetry, refinement, and composition entourages are visible before any L10 completion consumer reads the route. It sits between the uniform-completion carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformcompletion-namecert}, the Cauchy-entourage request layer of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-entourage-filter-namecert}, the real uniform structure of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-uniform-structure-namecert}, and the metric completion boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metriccompletion-namecert}. \begin{definition}[Uniform entourage-space carrier]
\label{def:uniform-entourage-space-carrier}
A $\UniformEntourageSpaceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
U=(E,D,S,R,C,L,T,K,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $E$ is the finite entourage-family row. It records the displayed binary neighborhoods used by every later Cauchy-entourage and completion-facing read.
\item $D$ is the diagonal row, requiring each accepted entourage request to expose the finite diagonal witness it uses.
\item $S$ is the symmetry row, recording the displayed reversal witness for the same entourage request.
\item $R$ is the refinement row, recording how a smaller displayed entourage is chosen inside a larger displayed request without using a host filter quotient.
\item $C$ is the composition row, recording the finite two-step entourage whose composite lies inside the requested entourage.
\item $L$ is the L10 completion handoff row linking the displayed entourage data to $\UniformCompletionUp$, $\CauchyEntourageFilterUp$, $\RealUniformStructureUp$, and $\MetricCompletionUp$ consumers.
\item $T$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the entourage family, diagonal, symmetry, refinement, composition, handoff, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $K$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the route
$$
E,D,S,R,C \longmapsto L \longmapsto T,P,N .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\UniformEntourageSpaceUp$, $\UniformCompletionUp$, $\CauchyEntourageFilterUp$, $\RealUniformStructureUp$, $\MetricCompletionUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\UniformEntourageSpaceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through the displayed rows $E,D,S,R,C,L,T,K,P,N$. The carrier has no coordinate for quotient uniform spaces, selected limits, open-cover compactness, countable choice, host equality, ambient completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Uniform entourage-space NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:uniform-entourage-space-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\UniformEntourageSpaceUp$ carrier
$$
U=(E,D,S,R,C,L,T,K,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\UniformEntourageSpaceUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier exposure for the entourage family, diagonal, symmetry, refinement, composition, L10 handoff, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item diagonal, symmetry, refinement, and composition exactness, requiring every uniformity read to remain inside the displayed rows $D,S,R,C$ attached to the same entourage-family row $E$;
\item completion handoff, requiring each $\UniformCompletionUp$, $\CauchyEntourageFilterUp$, $\RealUniformStructureUp$, and $\MetricCompletionUp$ consumer named by $L$ or $P$ to read the displayed entourage route before any completion-facing boundary is available;
\item transport and replay stability through componentwise $\hsame$ in $T$ and displayed $\Cont$ replay in $K$;
\item provenance exhaustion, requiring $P$ and $N$ to export no quotient uniform space, selected limit, open-cover compactness theorem, countable-choice selector, host equality, ambient completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{enumerate}
No local NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier exposure is projection from \autoref{def:uniform-entourage-space-carrier}. The projection exposes the entourage-family row, diagonal row, symmetry row, refinement row, composition row, L10 handoff, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

The uniformity obligations are componentwise. Diagonal reads are confined to $D$, symmetry reads to $S$, refinement reads to $R$, and composition reads to $C$, all over the same displayed entourage family $E$.

The completion handoff is the row $L$. A consumer for $\UniformCompletionUp$, $\CauchyEntourageFilterUp$, $\RealUniformStructureUp$, or $\MetricCompletionUp$ reaches the completion corridor only after the displayed route $E,D,S,R,C$ has been replayed.

Finally $T,K,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite route. Since the carrier has no quotient, selected-limit, compactness, choice, host-equality, completeness, verification, or bridge coordinate, these rows exhaust the local certificate surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Uniform entourage-space completion handoff]
\label{thm:uniform-entourage-space-completion-handoff}
Every L10 completion consumer of an accepted $\UniformEntourageSpaceUp$ carrier factors through the displayed route
$$
E,D,S,R,C \longmapsto L \longmapsto T,K,P,N .
$$

No consumer receives a quotient uniform space, selected limit, open-cover compactness theorem, countable-choice selector, host equality, or ambient completeness principle from this packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

The handoff row $L$ is downstream of those witnesses. It can name $\UniformCompletionUp$, $\CauchyEntourageFilterUp$, $\RealUniformStructureUp$, and $\MetricCompletionUp$ consumers only as readers of the already displayed entourage route.

The structural rows preserve the same order. Componentwise $\hsame$ in $T$ and displayed $\Cont$ replay in $K$ cannot introduce a new quotient, selected-limit, compactness, choice, host-equality, or completeness coordinate.

Therefore every completion-facing read factors through the stated finite route and remains an entourage-surface handoff rather than an ambient completion theorem.
\end{proof}
