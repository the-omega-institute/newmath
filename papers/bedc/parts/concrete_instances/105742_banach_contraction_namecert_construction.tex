\begin{definition}[BanachContraction carrier]
\label{def:banach-contraction-carrier}
A $\BanachContractionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
B=(K,I,M,Q,S,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
whose coordinates are exactly:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $K$, the accepted $\ContractionMappingUp$ row displaying the self-map graph, contraction bound, one-step Picard comparison, and fixed-point readiness surface;
\item $I$, the $\PicardIterationUp$ row displaying the finite orbit, residual-distance ledger, Cauchy-modulus extraction, and regular-sequence handoff;
\item $M$, the $\CompleteMetricUp$ row, read only as the endpoint-admission surface for the Picard Cauchy data;
\item $Q$, the regular-Cauchy and readback route through $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\MetricCompletionUp$, and the terminal $\RealUp$ seal used by endpoint-facing consumers;
\item $S$, the $\SeparatedMetricUp$ uniqueness row, consumed only after the two endpoint reads expose zero distance through the displayed metric row;
\item $E$, the endpoint seal row naming the fixed-point candidate as the result of the completion route rather than as a selected host point;
\item $H$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport for contraction data, Picard iterates, complete-metric endpoint rows, readback rows, separatedness rows, endpoint seals, replay rows, provenance, and local naming;
\item $C$, displayed $\Cont$ replay from contraction admission through Picard iteration, Cauchy readback, complete-metric endpoint admission, separated uniqueness, and endpoint sealing;
\item $P$, the $\Pkg$ provenance row over $\BanachContractionUp$, $\ContractionMappingUp$, $\PicardIterationUp$, $\CompleteMetricUp$, $\MetricCompletionUp$, $\SeparatedMetricUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$, the local $\NameCert_{\BanachContractionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier names the constructive Banach contraction route. It has no coordinate for quotient completion equality, selected representatives, host fixed-point equality, host function extensionality, trichotomy, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[BanachContraction NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:banach-contraction-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\BanachContractionUp$ carrier $B=(K,I,M,Q,S,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\BanachContractionUp}$ surface consists exactly of contraction-map admission, Picard-iteration admission, complete-metric endpoint visibility, regular-Cauchy and Real readback visibility, separated uniqueness visibility, endpoint-seal stability, componentwise transport, displayed replay, package provenance, and local naming for the finite packet of \autoref{def:banach-contraction-carrier}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier. The rows $K,I,M,Q,S,E$ are the only mathematical coordinates, and the rows $H,C,P,N$ are the only structural coordinates available to the local certificate.

The contraction and Picard obligations come from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-contractionmapping-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-picard-iteration-namecert}. They expose the self-map graph, contraction bound, finite orbit ledger, residual comparisons, and Cauchy-modulus handoff before any endpoint row is inspected.

The completion and readback obligations come next. The complete-metric row $M$ admits the Cauchy data to an endpoint-facing surface, while $Q$ records the $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\MetricCompletionUp$, and $\RealUp$ readback path used by consumers of that endpoint.

Finally $S,E,H,C,P,N$ bind uniqueness, endpoint sealing, transport, replay, provenance, and naming to the same displayed rows. Since no excluded object is a coordinate of $B$, the NameCert surface exports only the constructive Banach contraction packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BanachContraction Cauchy iteration route]
\label{thm:banach-contraction-cauchy-iteration-route}
Every endpoint-facing read of an accepted $\BanachContractionUp$ carrier first consumes the contraction row $K$ and the Picard row $I$, then the complete-metric row $M$, then the readback route $Q$ through $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\MetricCompletionUp$, and $\RealUp$, and only afterward the endpoint seal $E$. Thus the Cauchy iteration route is the displayed finite chain
$$
\ContractionMappingUp\longmapsto\PicardIterationUp\longmapsto\CompleteMetricUp\longmapsto(\RegSeqRatUp,\MetricCompletionUp,\RealUp)\longmapsto E .
$$
No read may replace this chain by quotient completion equality, a selected limit representative, countable choice, host function extensionality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with $K$. The contraction row supplies only the finite self-map graph, rational contraction bound, one-step Picard comparison, and fixed-point readiness boundary.

Next consume $I$. The Picard row generates the finite orbit ledger, residual-distance comparisons, Cauchy-modulus extraction, and regular handoff, so the read has Cauchy data before it asks for an endpoint.

The complete-metric row $M$ is then the only endpoint-admission coordinate. The readback row $Q$ records how the admitted endpoint is inspected through regular rational windows, the metric-completion surface, and the terminal real seal.

The endpoint seal $E$ is downstream of those rows. Because the carrier includes no quotient completion, representative selector, choice schedule, host function equality, or ambient real-completeness coordinate, the displayed route is the whole Cauchy iteration route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BanachContraction unique fixed-point seal]
\label{thm:banach-contraction-unique-fixed-point-seal}
For an accepted $\BanachContractionUp$ carrier $B=(K,I,M,Q,S,E,H,C,P,N)$, uniqueness of the fixed-point endpoint is exported only through the separated zero-distance route $S$ after both compared endpoint reads have passed through $K,I,M,Q,E$ and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$. The exported uniqueness is classifier equality inside the displayed $\SeparatedMetricUp$ surface, not host equality of points, quotient equality of completion names, a selected representative, trichotomy, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Take two endpoint-facing reads of the same carrier. By \autoref{thm:banach-contraction-cauchy-iteration-route}, each read must expose the contraction row, Picard row, complete-metric row, readback route, and endpoint seal before any uniqueness comparison is available.

Apply the separatedness row $S$. It compares the two endpoint reads only after the displayed zero-distance witness is routed through the $\SeparatedMetricUp$ surface; this is the same kind of classifier route isolated in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separatedmetric-namecert}.

Transport and replay are componentwise through $H$ and $C$, while $P$ and $N$ record provenance and local naming for the same finite packet. These rows do not add a host equality, quotient equality, representative selector, trichotomy principle, or ambient completeness theorem.

Therefore the fixed-point seal is unique only in the displayed local classifier sense. The carrier records the constructive endpoint route and its separated uniqueness boundary, not a host-level fixed-point object.
\end{proof}
