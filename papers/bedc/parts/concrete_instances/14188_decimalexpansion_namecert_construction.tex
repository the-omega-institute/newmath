\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\DecimalExpansionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-decimalexpansion-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, the
$\DecimalExpansionUp$ packet records finite decimal-prefix windows as BEDC
readback data. It does not store a host infinite decimal, a quotient real, or
a selected representative. Its role is to route base-ten digit observations
through the finite stream surface of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational
readback surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the
dyadic and rational place-value ledgers of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the terminal
$\RealUp$ seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}.

The chapter is the decimal sibling of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-binaryexpansion-namecert} and
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuedfraction-namecert}. The shared
discipline is finite-window readback: binary prefixes, continued-fraction
convergents, and decimal prefixes all expose only displayed rows before a
$\RegSeqRatUp$ or $\RealUp$ consumer can read the packet.

The same finite decimal-prefix windows also form a forward consumer of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-separability-namecert}. A decimal window
gives a countable dense readback route into $\RealUp$, while
$\RealSeparabilityUp$ supplies the global countable-density ledger that later
real-name consumers use to compare those finite routes.

\begin{definition}[DecimalExpansion carrier]\label{def:decimal-expansion-carrier}
A $\DecimalExpansionUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
K=(D,W,V,Q,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the finite digit ledger, whose rows record decimal digits and the
associated sign or integral-prefix convention when the local packet includes
one;
\item $W$ is the finite decimal-place window ledger, listing the inspected
positions and the request precision served by those positions;
\item $V$ is the place-value row that evaluates each inspected prefix as a
rational sum with denominator a power of ten;
\item $Q$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ and rational comparison row that transports
the base-ten place-value read into the dyadic tolerance discipline used by
downstream real consumers;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row reached from the same decimal
window and place-value rows;
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row exposed only after $D,W,V,Q,R$
have been displayed;
\item $H$ is the componentwise $\hsame$ transport row for digit, window,
place-value, dyadic/rational, regular-sequence, and real-seal coordinates;
\item $C$ is the $\Cont$ replay row that reuses the accepted finite decimal
windows without adding a host stream;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\DecimalExpansionUp$,
$\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$,
$\hsame$, $\Cont$, and the local naming row;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DecimalExpansionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exposes no infinite decimal stream, Dedekind cut, quotient real,
endpoint convention resolver, trichotomy decision, selected representative, or
Real-completeness witness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[DecimalExpansion digit-window obligations]
\label{thm:decimal-expansion-digit-window-obligations}
Every real-facing read of an accepted $\DecimalExpansionUp$ packet must consume the digit ledger $D$, finite window ledger $W$, place-value row $V$, dyadic comparison row $Q$, $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row $R$, and terminal $\RealUp$ seal row $E$ in that order. The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name only this displayed route, so no decimal consumer can read a real-facing value before the finite digit window and dyadic comparison have both been displayed.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:decimal-expansion-carrier}. Its public real-facing coordinates appear as $D,W,V,Q,R,E$ before the structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

The finite-window discipline is the same one used by the sibling routes of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-binaryexpansion-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuedfraction-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}. A consumer first receives inspected digits and positions, not an infinite stream.

Apply the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:decimal-expansion-namecert-obligations}. Carrier admission and ledger exhaustion force every $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, or $\RealUp$ consumer to inspect only displayed rows, with $V$ and $Q$ between the digit window and the regular-rational handoff.

Finally read the seal row $E$ downstream of $R$. Since the carrier has no coordinate for a direct decimal stream, quotient real, selected representative, or completeness witness, the ordered digit-window route is the only real-facing decimal read exported by the packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DecimalExpansion NameCert obligations]\label{thm:decimal-expansion-namecert-obligations}
The seed $\NameCert_{\DecimalExpansionUp}$ obligation surface consists exactly
of the rows in \autoref{def:decimal-expansion-carrier}. Carrier admission

base-ten rational place-value row, a dyadic/rational comparison row, a

Ledger exhaustion says that every $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, or
$\RealUp$ consumer can inspect only $D,W,V,Q,R,E,H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is read directly from the finite packet. The digit rows and
decimal-place windows give the visible $\StreamNameUp$ observation surface,
while $V$ evaluates each inspected prefix as a rational place-value sum.

The dyadic/rational row $Q$ is the only bridge from base-ten arithmetic into
the tolerance discipline already used by
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}. The regular handoff
row $R$ and real-seal row $E$ are therefore downstream of the same inspected
window.

Transport is componentwise because $H$ has coordinates for each displayed
row, and continuation only replays windows already accepted by $C$. The
package and local naming rows record exactly those dependencies.

For ledger exhaustion, project a consumer read from the accepted packet. The
definition gives only the ten coordinates $D,W,V,Q,R,E,H,C,P,N$, so any route
through an infinite stream, quotient real, selected endpoint convention, or
ambient completeness principle is outside the carrier.
\end{proof}
