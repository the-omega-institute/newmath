\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\DecimalExpansionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-decimalexpansion-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, the
$\DecimalExpansionUp$ packet records finite decimal-prefix windows as BEDC
readback data. It does not store a host infinite decimal, a quotient real, or
a selected representative. Its role is to route base-ten digit observations
through the finite stream surface of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational
readback surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the
dyadic and rational place-value ledgers of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the terminal
$\RealUp$ seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}.

The chapter is the decimal sibling of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-binaryexpansion-namecert} and
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuedfraction-namecert}. The shared
discipline is finite-window readback: binary prefixes, continued-fraction
convergents, and decimal prefixes all expose only displayed rows before a
$\RegSeqRatUp$ or $\RealUp$ consumer can read the packet.

The same finite decimal-prefix windows also form a forward consumer of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-separability-namecert}. A decimal window
gives a countable dense readback route into $\RealUp$, while
$\RealSeparabilityUp$ supplies the global countable-density ledger that later
real-name consumers use to compare those finite routes.

\begin{definition}[DecimalExpansion carrier]\label{def:decimal-expansion-carrier}
A $\DecimalExpansionUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
K=(D,W,V,Q,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the finite digit ledger, whose rows record decimal digits and the
associated sign or integral-prefix convention when the local packet includes
one;
\item $W$ is the finite decimal-place window ledger, listing the inspected
positions and the request precision served by those positions;
\item $V$ is the place-value row that evaluates each inspected prefix as a
rational sum with denominator a power of ten;
\item $Q$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ and rational comparison row that transports
the base-ten place-value read into the dyadic tolerance discipline used by
downstream real consumers;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row reached from the same decimal
window and place-value rows;
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row exposed only after $D,W,V,Q,R$
have been displayed;
\item $H$ is the componentwise $\hsame$ transport row for digit, window,
place-value, dyadic/rational, regular-sequence, and real-seal coordinates;
\item $C$ is the $\Cont$ replay row that reuses the accepted finite decimal
windows without adding a host stream;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\DecimalExpansionUp$,
$\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$,
$\hsame$, $\Cont$, and the local naming row;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DecimalExpansionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exposes no infinite decimal stream, Dedekind cut, quotient real,
endpoint convention resolver, trichotomy decision, selected representative, or
Real-completeness witness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[DecimalExpansion NameCert obligations]\label{thm:decimal-expansion-namecert-obligations}
The seed $\NameCert_{\DecimalExpansionUp}$ obligation surface consists exactly
of the rows in \autoref{def:decimal-expansion-carrier}. Carrier admission
requires a finite digit ledger, a finite decimal-place window ledger, a
base-ten rational place-value row, a dyadic/rational comparison row, a
$\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row, a $\RealUp$ seal row, componentwise $\hsame$
transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local naming
row. Classifier transport is componentwise through the same displayed rows.
Ledger exhaustion says that every $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, or
$\RealUp$ consumer can inspect only $D,W,V,Q,R,E,H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is read directly from the finite packet. The digit rows and
decimal-place windows give the visible $\StreamNameUp$ observation surface,
while $V$ evaluates each inspected prefix as a rational place-value sum.

The dyadic/rational row $Q$ is the only bridge from base-ten arithmetic into
the tolerance discipline already used by
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}. The regular handoff
row $R$ and real-seal row $E$ are therefore downstream of the same inspected
window.

Transport is componentwise because $H$ has coordinates for each displayed
row, and continuation only replays windows already accepted by $C$. The
package and local naming rows record exactly those dependencies.

For ledger exhaustion, project a consumer read from the accepted packet. The
definition gives only the ten coordinates $D,W,V,Q,R,E,H,C,P,N$, so any route
through an infinite stream, quotient real, selected endpoint convention, or
ambient completeness principle is outside the carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DecimalExpansion RealUp readback boundary]\label{thm:decimal-expansion-real-readback-boundary}
Every $\RealUp$ read of an accepted $\DecimalExpansionUp$ packet factors
through the finite route
$$
\StreamNameUp,\ \DecimalExpansionUp,\ \DyadicRatCoreUp,\ \RegSeqRatUp,\ \RealUp .
$$
Concretely, the consumer first reads an inspected decimal-place window, then
the digit rows on that window, then the rational place-value and dyadic
tolerance rows, then the regular rational handoff, and only then the displayed
real seal. The packet supplies no Dedekind cut, quotient real, endpoint
uniqueness convention, trichotomy principle, selected representative, or
Real-completeness witness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted carrier $K=(D,W,V,Q,R,E,H,C,P,N)$. The
$\StreamNameUp$ observation is $W$ together with the digit rows of $D$ on that
window, so a real-facing consumer cannot read a decimal prefix without first
reading the finite window.

The place-value row $V$ turns that same finite prefix into a rational
base-ten sum. The row $Q$ records the dyadic and rational comparison data used
to pass from the decimal sum to the tolerance language consumed by
$\RegSeqRatUp$.

The handoff row $R$ is therefore reached only after $D,W,V,Q$ are fixed. The
seal row $E$ is listed after $R$, and the rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve the same
dependency route under transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming.

Thus every $\RealUp$ read factors through the displayed finite route. The
excluded objects are not carrier coordinates, so no consumer of the packet can
obtain them from this readback boundary.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DecimalExpansion RegSeqRat handoff]\label{thm:decimal-expansion-regseqrat-handoff}
Every accepted $\DecimalExpansionUp$ packet routes finite decimal digit rows
through rational place-value rows and dyadic comparison rows before reaching
the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff and terminal $\RealUp$ seal. The route is exhausted
by the displayed coordinates
$$
D,W,V,Q,R,E,H,C,P,N
$$
of \autoref{def:decimal-expansion-carrier}. In particular, the packet exports
no quotient real, endpoint representative, trichotomy row, selected decimal
stream, or $\RealUp$ completeness witness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:decimal-expansion-carrier}. The finite
digit ledger $D$ is read together with the finite decimal-place windows $W$,
and the place-value row $V$ evaluates exactly those inspected prefixes as
rational base-ten sums.

Apply the NameCert obligations of
\autoref{thm:decimal-expansion-namecert-obligations}. Carrier admission and
classifier transport keep $D,W,V$ attached to the same dyadic and rational
comparison row $Q$, while ledger exhaustion prevents a consumer from bypassing
the displayed handoff surface.

Finally apply the RealUp readback boundary of
\autoref{thm:decimal-expansion-real-readback-boundary}. The regular handoff
row $R$ is reached only after the digit, window, place-value, and dyadic rows
are displayed, and the terminal seal row $E$ remains downstream of $R$ with
the structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserving the same route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DecimalExpansion window normalization obligation]\label{thm:decimal-expansion-window-normalization-obligation}
Every accepted $\DecimalExpansionUp$ packet must display the finite digit ledger $D$ and finite window ledger $W$ before any place-value, dyadic-comparison, regular-rational, or real-seal row can be read. Equivalently, every public read of $V,Q,R,E$ factors through the finite decimal-prefix surface named in \autoref{def:decimal-expansion-carrier} and constrained by \autoref{thm:decimal-expansion-namecert-obligations}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:decimal-expansion-carrier}. The rows are ordered as $D,W,V,Q,R,E,H,C,P,N$, so digit admission and inspected-window admission are the visible source rows for the packet.

Apply the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:decimal-expansion-namecert-obligations}. Carrier admission and ledger exhaustion require every consumer to read only displayed coordinates, hence no consumer can reach $V,Q,R,E$ without first reading $D$ and $W$.

The L10 sibling route is the same finite-window route already used by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. Thus window normalization is a finite ledger obligation, not an infinite decimal-stream selection.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DecimalExpansion dyadic comparison handoff]\label{thm:decimal-expansion-dyadic-comparison-handoff}
For every accepted $\DecimalExpansionUp$ packet, the place-value row $V$ reaches the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff only through the comparison row $Q$ and the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ and rational comparison surface. No direct route from a decimal place-value sum to $R$ or $E$ is exported by the packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read \autoref{def:decimal-expansion-carrier}. The row $V$ records the base-ten rational place-value sum, while $Q$ is the displayed $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ and rational comparison row.

Apply \autoref{thm:decimal-expansion-namecert-obligations}. The obligation surface places $Q$ between place-value evaluation and the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff, and classifier transport is componentwise through that same displayed row.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:decimal-expansion-regseqrat-handoff}. It states that finite digit rows pass through rational place-value rows and dyadic comparison rows before reaching $R$ and the terminal seal $E$. The dyadic handoff therefore uses the existing routes of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, with no bypass from $V$ to $R$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DecimalExpansion dyadic normalization lock]\label{thm:decimal-expansion-dyadic-normalization-lock}
Every $\RealUp$ read of an accepted $\DecimalExpansionUp$ packet passes through the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ digit-place tolerance ledger, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row, the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row, and the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row before any real-facing decimal value is consumed. In particular, the digit-place route is the same L10 sibling lattice exposed by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}; the packet exports no direct decimal-to-real shortcut.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:decimal-expansion-carrier}. The digit rows $D$ and finite decimal windows $W$ feed the place-value row $V$, while the comparison row $Q$ is the only displayed dyadic and rational tolerance route.

Apply \autoref{thm:decimal-expansion-dyadic-comparison-handoff}. It places $Q$ between every place-value read and the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff, so the decimal packet cannot jump from $V$ directly to the seal row.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:decimal-expansion-real-readback-boundary}. The terminal real read is reached only after finite windows, digit rows, dyadic tolerance, and regular rational readback have been displayed. This locks the decimal route to the dyadic, regular-Cauchy, stream-window, and real-seal sibling lattice named in the statement.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DecimalExpansion Real seal nonescape]\label{thm:decimal-expansion-real-seal-nonescape}
The terminal row $E$ of an accepted $\DecimalExpansionUp$ packet exports only the displayed $\RealUp$ seal reached from $D,W,V,Q,R$. It exports no quotient real, endpoint resolver, trichotomy decision, selected decimal stream, or $\RealUp$ completeness witness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the real readback boundary of \autoref{thm:decimal-expansion-real-readback-boundary}. Every $\RealUp$ read factors through the finite route from $\StreamNameUp$ windows to decimal place value, dyadic comparison, $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff, and then the seal row $E$.

Use \autoref{thm:decimal-expansion-dyadic-comparison-handoff} to keep the route through $Q$ and $R$. The seal is downstream of those displayed rows, so it cannot introduce an endpoint resolver, trichotomy row, or selected stream that is not already present in the carrier.

Finally project the excluded-coordinate clause of \autoref{def:decimal-expansion-carrier}. Since quotient reals, endpoint convention resolvers, trichotomy decisions, selected representatives, selected decimal streams, and completeness witnesses are absent from the displayed packet, none escapes through $E$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DecimalExpansion Real seal factorization]\label{thm:decimal-expansion-real-seal-factorization}
Every accepted $\DecimalExpansionUp$ packet whose terminal row is read as a $\RealUp$ seal factors through finite decimal windows, dyadic comparison, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff, and the $\RealSeparabilityUp$ countable-density route. Concretely, the seal read begins with the digit and window rows $D,W$, evaluates those rows through the decimal place-value row $V$, passes through the dyadic and rational comparison row $Q$, reaches the regular rational row $R$, and only then exposes the terminal seal $E$. The same finite decimal windows supply the countable dense readback interface named by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-separability-namecert}; no direct decimal stream, quotient real, selected representative, or completeness witness is exported by the seal.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the dyadic normalization lock of \autoref{thm:decimal-expansion-dyadic-normalization-lock}. It forces every real-facing decimal value to pass through the digit-place tolerance ledger, finite window row, regular rational readback row, and terminal seal.

Apply the Real seal nonescape theorem \autoref{thm:decimal-expansion-real-seal-nonescape}. The terminal row $E$ cannot add a quotient real, selected decimal stream, endpoint resolver, or completeness witness after the finite route has reached it.

Finally use the countable-density role of $\RealSeparabilityUp$ described in the chapter introduction. The same finite decimal-prefix windows that feed $D,W,V,Q,R,E$ are the windows counted by the RealSeparability route, so the factorization records a finite dense readback path rather than a direct decimal-to-real shortcut.
\end{proof}
