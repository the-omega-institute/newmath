\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CousinCoverUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-cousin-cover-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Cousin and Bishop-style gauge integration, the $\CousinCoverUp$ packet names the reusable finite gauge-cover carrier below the tagged-subdivision conclusion of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cousin-lemma-namecert}. It separates the cover data consumed by $\GaugeIntegralUp$, $\DarbouxSumUp$, $\RiemannIntegrableUp$, and compact interval finite-cover arguments from the lemma-level subdivision packet. The carrier reads located interval rows from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-located-interval-bisection-namecert}, finite cover and radius boundaries from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-interval-finite-cover-certificate-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finite-lebesgue-number-namecert}, and Real-facing names through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}.

\begin{definition}[Cousin cover carrier]
\label{def:cousin-cover-carrier}
A $\CousinCoverUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(I,G,T,M,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $I$ is the located interval row, including named endpoints, endpoint order, containment tests, and the interval boundary inspected by finite bisection and cover consumers.
\item $G$ is the positive gauge row. Each displayed tag request carries a positive local radius read through rational and Real-facing witnesses before any cover cell is admitted.
\item $T$ is the tagged cover-cell row. Each entry records a tag, a located interval cell, containment in $I$, local admission by the radius in $G$, and the finite handoff shape read by $\CousinLemmaUp$.
\item $M$ is the mesh and refinement ledger recording endpoint comparisons, adjacency, overlap permissions, and the finite refinement relation between a cover row and any smaller admitted cover row.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window schedule exposed for endpoint, tag, gauge-radius, cell, and mesh observations in $I,G,T,M$.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback attached to the displayed windows.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal reached only after the finite windows and regular rational readbacks in $W,R$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for interval, gauge, tagged cover, mesh, window, readback, seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a gauge-cover request through $I,G,T,M,W,R,E,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CousinCoverUp$, $\CousinLemmaUp$, $\GaugeIntegralUp$, $\DarbouxSumUp$, $\RiemannIntegrableUp$, $\IntervalFiniteCoverCertificateUp$, $\FiniteLebesgueNumberUp$, $\LocatedIntervalBisectionUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CousinCoverUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $I,G,T,M,W,R,E,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for general open-cover compactness, a choice-selected subcover, a host real completeness theorem, quotient stream equality, or an external interval object.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cousin cover NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cousin-cover-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CousinCoverUp$ carrier
$$
Q=(I,G,T,M,W,R,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\CousinCoverUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed located interval, positive gauge, tagged cover cells, mesh and refinement ledger, StreamName windows, RegSeqRat readback, Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item gauge locality, requiring each tagged cover cell in $T$ to read its radius from $G$ and to prove containment in that local gauge window;
\item refinement coverage, requiring $M$ to record that the finite cell family covers the displayed interval row and that every accepted refinement remains inside the same interval and gauge rows;
\item finite tagged-cover handoff, requiring downstream tagged-subdivision consumers to read the cover cells through $T,M,W,R,E$ before reaching $H,C,P,N$;
\item ledger non-escape, excluding general open-cover compactness, selected subcovers, host interval equality, quotient stream equality, countable choice, and ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{enumerate}
No local obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:cousin-cover-carrier}. The tuple displays the located interval, gauge, tagged cover cells, refinement ledger, finite window schedule, readback, terminal Real seal, and structural rows.

Gauge locality is read from $G$ and $T$. A cell cannot be admitted unless its tag has a displayed positive radius and the cell containment test is recorded inside that local gauge window.

Refinement coverage is read from $M$. The ledger states both that the finite cell family covers the interval row $I$ and that any smaller cover cell remains attached to the same interval and gauge source.

The tagged-cover handoff is the ordered route through $T,M,W,R,E$. The cover cells and their mesh ledger are exposed before the StreamName windows, regular rational readbacks, and terminal Real seal can be consumed.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite packet. Since no excluded coordinate occurs in the carrier, quotient stream equality, selected subcovers, open-cover compactness, countable choice, host interval equality, and ambient Real completeness cannot be projected from an accepted packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cousin cover gauge refinement handoff]
\label{thm:cousin-cover-gauge-refinement-handoff}
Every $\CousinLemmaUp$, $\GaugeIntegralUp$, $\DarbouxSumUp$, $\RiemannIntegrableUp$, or interval finite-cover consumer of an accepted $\CousinCoverUp$ packet reads the cover through the ordered carrier
$$
I,G,T,M,W,R,E,H,C,P,N .
$$
Thus the reusable cover surface is the finite gauge-cover packet over located intervals, tagged cells, mesh refinement, StreamName windows, RegSeqRat readback, and the terminal $\RealUp$ seal. No consumer receives a detached open cover, a choice-selected refinement, host interval equality, quotient stream equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a downstream consumer read. Carrier admission exposes the located interval row $I$ and the positive gauge row $G$ before any cover cell can be inspected.

The tagged cover row $T$ and the mesh ledger $M$ then provide the finite refinement surface. This is the part consumed by $\CousinLemmaUp$ when it forms a tagged subdivision, and by $\GaugeIntegralUp$, Darboux, Riemann, and interval finite-cover consumers when they need a gauge-local finite cover.

The Real-facing portion of the read passes through $W,R,E$. These rows expose only finite StreamName windows, RegSeqRat readback, and the terminal Real seal attached to the interval, tag, gauge-radius, cell, and mesh observations already displayed.

The remaining rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve the same packet under transport, replay, provenance, and local naming. Since the carrier has no detached open-cover or host-completeness coordinate, the consumer handoff is exactly the displayed finite route.
\end{proof}
