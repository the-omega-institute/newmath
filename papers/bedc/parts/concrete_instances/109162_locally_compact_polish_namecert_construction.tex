\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\LocallyCompactPolishUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-locally-compact-polish-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bourbaki, Kelley, and Alexandrov--Urysohn, the $\LocallyCompactPolishUp$ packet records the finite BEDC surface where local compact neighbourhood data is read together with a Polish complete-separable metric presentation. It sits at the joint frontier of the local compact metric packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locally-compact-metric-namecert}, the Polish readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-polishspace-namecert}, the proper closed-ball route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-propermetric-namecert}, the sigma-compact exhaustion of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-sigma-compact-namecert}, and the complete-separable metric presentation of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-complete-separable-metric-namecert}. The certificate is finite data: neighbourhood rows, complete-separable readback, properness rows, sigma-compact exhaustion windows, StreamName and RegSeqRat observations, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming.

\begin{definition}[Locally compact Polish carrier]
\label{def:locally-compact-polish-carrier}
A $\LocallyCompactPolishUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(X,M,K,C,S,E,W,T,R,H,Q,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the underlying metric-object row shared by the locally compact, Polish, proper, sigma-compact, and complete-separable presentations.
\item $M$ is the $\LocallyCompactMetricUp$ neighbourhood row. It records, for every displayed point-window, a compact neighbourhood candidate, an interior witness, and the metric inspection data by which the candidate is consumed.
\item $K$ is the local compactness compact-row ledger. It binds compact neighbourhood reads to the compact witnesses supplied through $M$ and refuses any neighbourhood not named in the packet.
\item $C$ is the $\CompleteSeparableMetricUp$ readback row. It records the complete metric presentation, the countable dense probe family, and the finite comparison windows through which completeness and separability are inspected.
\item $S$ is the $\PolishSpaceUp$ row obtained from the same complete-separable readback; it carries no independent host topology coordinate.
\item $E$ is the $\ProperMetricUp$ closed-ball compactness row. It is read only after $M,K,C,S$ have fixed the metric and neighbourhood surface.
\item $W$ is the $\SigmaCompactUp$ exhaustion row. Its compact exhaustion windows are generated from the displayed local compactness and properness rows, not from a selected external exhaustion.
\item $T$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ observation row for points, compact neighbourhood candidates, complete-separable probes, closed balls, exhaustion windows, and endpoint-facing reads.
\item $R$ is the finite $\RegSeqRatUp$ comparison row for the same metric probes and exhaustion windows.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $X,M,K,C,S,E,W,T,R,Q,P,N$.
\item $Q$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the route
$$
M,K \longmapsto C,S \longmapsto E,W \longmapsto T,R .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LocallyCompactPolishUp$, $\LocallyCompactMetricUp$, $\PolishSpaceUp$, $\ProperMetricUp$, $\SigmaCompactUp$, $\CompleteSeparableMetricUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LocallyCompactPolishUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $X,M,K,C,S,E,W,T,R,H,Q,P,N$. It has no coordinate for arbitrary open-cover compactness, a selected exhaustion, host topological equality, quotient metric equality, countable choice, or a Polish presentation detached from the displayed complete-separable row.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Locally compact Polish NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:locally-compact-polish-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\LocallyCompactPolishUp$ carrier
$$
L=(X,M,K,C,S,E,W,T,R,H,Q,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\LocallyCompactPolishUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the thirteen displayed rows;
\item local compact neighbourhood exactness, requiring $M$ and $K$ to bind every compact neighbourhood read to a displayed point-window and compact witness;
\item complete-separable readback compatibility, requiring $C$ to carry the complete metric presentation and countable dense probe family used by the packet;
\item Polish readback compatibility, requiring $S$ to be consumed through $C$ and not through an independent host topology;
\item proper closed-ball compactness compatibility, requiring $E$ to inspect the metric and compact neighbourhood rows already displayed;
\item sigma-compact exhaustion compatibility, requiring $W$ to use only the compact windows generated from the local compactness and properness route;
\item StreamName and RegSeqRat finite-window compatibility, requiring $T$ and $R$ to observe the same metric probes, compact neighbourhoods, closed balls, and exhaustion windows;
\item componentwise transport, displayed replay, provenance, and local naming for the same route through $H,Q,P,N$.
\end{enumerate}
No locally compact Polish obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet $L=(X,M,K,C,S,E,W,T,R,H,Q,P,N)$. The metric object, local compactness, compact-neighbourhood ledger, complete-separable readback, Polish row, properness row, sigma-compact exhaustion, finite observations, and structural rows are precisely the displayed carrier coordinates.

The neighbourhood route is fixed first. Rows $M$ and $K$ name the compact neighbourhood candidates and bind every accepted local compactness read to the corresponding point-window and compact witness.

Next the complete-separable route is read through $C$, and the Polish row $S$ is downstream of that readback. Since $S$ has no separate host-topology coordinate, every Polish consumer receives the same complete metric and dense-probe data.

The rows $E$ and $W$ expose proper closed-ball compactness and sigma-compact exhaustion only after the metric, neighbourhood, and complete-separable rows have been displayed. The observation rows $T$ and $R$ then inspect the same probes and windows through finite StreamName and RegSeqRat data.

Finally $H,Q,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the route without adding new coordinates. The excluded objects do not occur in the carrier, so the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Locally compact Polish proper-sigma route]
\label{thm:locally-compact-polish-proper-sigma-route}
Every properness, sigma-compactness, completion, or compact-neighbourhood consumer of an accepted $\LocallyCompactPolishUp$ carrier receives the finite route
$$
\LocallyCompactMetricUp,\CompleteSeparableMetricUp,\PolishSpaceUp
\longmapsto
M,K,C,S
\longmapsto
\ProperMetricUp,\SigmaCompactUp
\longmapsto
E,W,T,R,H,Q,P,N .
$$
The route exposes local compact neighbourhood rows and Polish complete-separable readback before proper closed-ball compactness or sigma-compact exhaustion is public. It exports no arbitrary open-cover compactness, selected exhaustion, host topological equality, quotient metric equality, countable-choice selector, or Polish presentation detached from the displayed complete-separable packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the rows $M,K,C,S$. The first pair carries the local compact neighbourhood data, while the second pair carries the complete-separable presentation and the Polish readback consumed by the packet.

The properness and sigma-compactness rows are then downstream. Row $E$ reads closed-ball compactness only through the displayed metric and local compactness surface, and row $W$ records exhaustion windows generated from the same source.

The observation and structural rows $T,R,H,Q,P,N$ preserve that route for consumers. Because the carrier has no coordinate for open-cover compactness, selected exhaustion, host equality, quotient equality, countable choice, or detached Polish data, every consumer remains inside the displayed proper-sigma route.
\end{proof}
