\begin{definition}[Zero IdealUp predicate]
\label{def:ideal-zero-predicate}
Let $R$ carry a $\RingUp$ certificate with carrier $C$, classifier $\sim_R$, zero $0_R$, addition $+_R$, additive inverse $-_R$, and multiplication $\cdot_R$. The zero $\IdealUp$ predicate over $R$ is the concrete carried predicate
$$
\mathsf{ZeroIdeal}_R(x)\Longleftrightarrow C(x)\land x\sim_R0_R.
$$
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Zero IdealUp predicate closure]
\label{thm:ideal-zero-predicate-ideal-closure}
Let $R$ carry a $\RingUp$ certificate with notation as in \autoref{def:ideal-zero-predicate}. Assume the ring certificate supplies carrier transport, zero carriedness, addition congruence, additive inverse congruence, multiplication congruence, additive zero and inverse laws, and the two zero-product rows $0_R\cdot_R r\sim_R0_R$ and $r\cdot_R0_R\sim_R0_R$ for every carried endpoint $r$. Then $\mathsf{ZeroIdeal}_R$ is an $\IdealUp$ carried predicate over $R$. Explicitly, it has carrier support, zero membership, additive closure, additive inverse closure, internal multiplication closure, classifier transport, and left and right absorption by every carried ambient endpoint.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier support is the first conjunct in \autoref{def:ideal-zero-predicate}. Zero membership uses the ring zero carriedness row together with classifier reflexivity at $0_R$.

For additive closure, unpack two memberships as carried endpoints classified with $0_R$. Ring addition congruence transports the two classifier comparisons to

$$
x+_R y\sim_R0_R+_R0_R.
$$

The additive zero law identifies $0_R+_R0_R$ with $0_R$, and transitivity gives the displayed zero classifier for $x+_R y$. The ring operation-closure row supplies $C(x+_R y)$, so the two witnesses repack $\mathsf{ZeroIdeal}_R(x+_R y)$.

For additive inverse closure, additive inverse congruence transports $x\sim_R0_R$ to $-_R x\sim_R-_R0_R$. The inverse row for zero and transitivity identify $-_R x$ with $0_R$, while the inverse operation-closure row supplies $C(-_R x)$.

Internal multiplication is the same pattern. Multiplication congruence sends the two zero classifiers to $x\cdot_R y\sim_R0_R\cdot_R0_R$, and the zero-product row collapses the right endpoint to $0_R$. Multiplication closure supplies the carrier conjunct.

Classifier transport is immediate from the carrier-transport row and classifier transitivity: if $\mathsf{ZeroIdeal}_R(x)$ and $x\sim_Ry$, then $C(y)$ follows from carrier transport, and $y\sim_R0_R$ follows by symmetry and transitivity from $x\sim_Ry$ and $x\sim_R0_R$.

Finally fix a carried ambient endpoint $r$. From $\mathsf{ZeroIdeal}_R(x)$, multiplication congruence identifies $r\cdot_Rx$ with $r\cdot_R0_R$ and $x\cdot_Rr$ with $0_R\cdot_Rr$; the two zero-product rows identify both products with $0_R$. Multiplication closure supplies their carrier conjuncts, so both absorption rows repack as zero-ideal memberships.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[Whole IdealUp predicate]
\label{def:ideal-whole-predicate}
Let $R$ carry a $\RingUp$ certificate with carrier $C$, classifier $\sim_R$, zero $0_R$, addition $+_R$, additive inverse $-_R$, and multiplication $\cdot_R$. The whole $\IdealUp$ predicate over $R$ is the concrete carried predicate
$$
\mathsf{WholeIdeal}_R(x)\Longleftrightarrow C(x).
$$
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Whole IdealUp predicate closure]
\label{thm:ideal-whole-predicate-ideal-closure}
Let $R$ carry a $\RingUp$ certificate with notation as in \autoref{def:ideal-whole-predicate}. Assume the ring certificate supplies zero carriedness, carrier transport, and carrier closure for addition, additive inverse, and multiplication. Then $\mathsf{WholeIdeal}_R$ is an $\IdealUp$ carried predicate over $R$. Explicitly, it has carrier support, zero membership, additive closure, additive inverse closure, internal multiplication closure, classifier transport, and left and right absorption by every carried ambient endpoint.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold \autoref{def:ideal-whole-predicate}. Carrier support is identity on the ambient carrier predicate, and zero membership is exactly the ring zero carriedness row.

The additive, inverse, and internal multiplication rows are the corresponding ambient operation-closure rows of the $\RingUp$ certificate. Since $\mathsf{WholeIdeal}_R(x)$ means only $C(x)$, each operation closure repacks directly as whole-ideal membership.

Classifier transport is the ambient carrier-transport row. If $C(x)$ and $x\sim_Ry$, the transported carrier witness is precisely $\mathsf{WholeIdeal}_R(y)$.

For two-sided absorption, fix $C(r)$ and $\mathsf{WholeIdeal}_R(x)$. Ambient multiplication closure gives $C(r\cdot_Rx)$ and $C(x\cdot_Rr)$, which are exactly the left and right whole-ideal memberships.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Zero and whole quotient-kernel scope]
\label{thm:ideal-zero-and-whole-quotient-kernel-scope}
Let $R$ carry a $\RingUp$ certificate with notation as in \autoref{def:ideal-quotient-kernel-source-package}. Let $Q_{\mathsf{ZeroIdeal}}$ and $Q_{\mathsf{WholeIdeal}}$ be the source packages of \autoref{def:ideal-quotient-kernel-source-package} built from \autoref{def:ideal-zero-predicate} and \autoref{def:ideal-whole-predicate}. Then the zero-ideal package is the equality-like carried-endpoint kernel
$$
Q_{\mathsf{ZeroIdeal}}(x,y)\Longleftrightarrow C(x)\land C(y)\land (x-_R y)\sim_R0_R,
$$
with the RingUp subtraction-zero rows identifying this condition with $x\sim_Ry$ whenever the ambient difference exactness row is available. The whole-ideal package is the total carried-endpoint kernel
$$
Q_{\mathsf{WholeIdeal}}(x,y)\Longleftrightarrow C(x)\land C(y).
$$
Both packages remain quotient-free $\mathsf{BHist}$ relations consumable by $\QuotientRingUp$ before any quotient carrier or quotient primitive is formed.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the zero predicate, unfold \autoref{def:ideal-quotient-kernel-source-package} and \autoref{def:ideal-zero-predicate}. The relation $Q_{\mathsf{ZeroIdeal}}(x,y)$ contains $C(x)$, $C(y)$, and the two fields of $\mathsf{ZeroIdeal}_R(x-_R y)$.

The carrier field $C(x-_R y)$ is already supplied by the subtraction operation-closure rows from the two carried endpoints. The remaining field is the displayed zero classifier $(x-_R y)\sim_R0_R$. Thus the quotient-kernel source repacks as the equality-like carried-endpoint kernel, and the ambient subtraction-zero exactness rows compare that kernel with the classifier $x\sim_Ry$.

For the whole predicate, unfold \autoref{def:ideal-whole-predicate}. The membership $\mathsf{WholeIdeal}_R(x-_R y)$ is exactly the carrier witness $C(x-_R y)$, which follows from the carrier closure of additive inverse and addition applied to $C(x)$ and $C(y)$. Therefore no condition remains beyond the two carried endpoints, giving the displayed total kernel.

The diagonal and endpoint-transport surfaces of these two concrete kernels are the specializations of \autoref{thm:ideal-quotient-kernel-diagonal-exactness} and \autoref{thm:ideal-quotient-kernel-transport-surface}. The repacked source packages therefore expose the same quotient-free boundary described in \autoref{def:ideal-quotient-kernel-source-package}, so $\QuotientRingUp$ consumes them through the existing IdealUp surface.
\end{proof}
