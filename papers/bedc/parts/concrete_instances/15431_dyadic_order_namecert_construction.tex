\begin{definition}[DyadicOrder carrier]
\label{def:dyadic-order-carrier}
A $\DyadicOrderUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
O=(L,R,S,C,H,K,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $L$ is the left $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ row, carrying its signed mantissa, unary exponent, denominator ledger, and local dyadic provenance.
\item $R$ is the right $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ row with the same visible endpoint coordinates and provenance shape.
\item $S$ is the common-scale row displaying the finite exponent into which $L$ and $R$ are transported before comparison.
\item $C$ is the comparison bit and ledger row, recording the transported mantissas at scale $S$, the finite sign test, and the named outcome consumed by downstream tolerance checks.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the two endpoint rows, common-scale row, comparison ledger, continuation row, provenance row, and local naming row.
\item $K$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from the two dyadic inputs through the common-scale row and then through the comparison ledger.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\IntUp$, $\RatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\DyadicOrderUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DyadicOrderUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for host rational order, trichotomy, quotient rational equality, selected limits, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, or bridge data.

\begin{theorem}[DyadicOrder NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:dyadic-order-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\DyadicOrderUp$ carrier $O=(L,R,S,C,H,K,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\DyadicOrderUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the left and right $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ rows, common-scale row, comparison ledger, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item classifier transport, requiring componentwise $\hsame$ and displayed $\Cont$ replay to preserve the same left endpoint, right endpoint, common exponent, transported mantissas, and comparison bit;
\item common-scale comparison exactness, requiring every accepted comparison to read the exponent and transported mantissas in $S$ before consuming the ledger row $C$;
\item radius-monotonicity compatibility, requiring downstream tolerance consumers to use the comparison only after the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ scale-window monotonicity route has exposed the same finite radius data;
\item non-escape, requiring $P$ and $N$ to name only the displayed rows and to export no host rational order, trichotomy, quotient equality, selected limit, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification claim, or bridge certificate.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored outside those rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:dyadic-order-carrier}. The projection exposes the two dyadic endpoint rows, the common-scale row, the comparison ledger, and the structural rows $H,K,P,N$.

Classifier transport is read from $H$ and $K$. Componentwise $\hsame$ carries the left and right endpoint rows, while displayed $\Cont$ replay repeats the route through $S$ and then through $C$.

Common-scale exactness follows from the carrier order. The comparison ledger $C$ is available only after the finite common exponent and the two transported mantissas have been displayed in $S$.

Radius-monotonicity compatibility is a downstream handoff condition. Since the package names only the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ rows and the displayed comparison route, every consumer must first read the scale-window monotonicity data already attached to those dyadic rows.

The exclusions follow from the row inventory. No host rational order, trichotomy, quotient equality, selected limit, ambient completeness, Lean verification row, or bridge row is present in $O$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicOrder common-scale comparison]
\label{thm:dyadic-order-common-scale-comparison}
Every accepted $\DyadicOrderUp$ comparison factors through a displayed finite ledger
$$
L,R \longmapsto S \longmapsto C
$$
where $S$ names a common exponent and transported mantissas for the two $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ inputs, and $C$ names the comparison bit read from those transported mantissas. The route cannot use host rational order, an ambient trichotomy principle, quotient equality of rationals, or any comparison object not carried by the finite rows of $O$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted comparison. Carrier admission gives only the left and right dyadic rows before a comparison can be inspected.

Replay $K$. The replay first transports the two endpoint rows into the common exponent displayed by $S$, and only then reaches the comparison ledger $C$.

The comparison bit in $C$ is therefore a readback from the two transported mantissas at the displayed scale. Because the carrier contains no host rational order, trichotomy, quotient equality, or external comparison coordinate, the accepted route is exhausted by $L,R,S,C,H,K,P,N$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicOrder radius-monotonicity handoff]
\label{thm:dyadic-order-radius-monotonicity-handoff}
Every $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, or $\RealUp$ consumer of an accepted $\DyadicOrderUp$ comparison receives only the finite dyadic comparison row $C$ after the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ scale-window monotonicity route has exposed the relevant radius and endpoint rows. The handoff supplies no host rational order, trichotomy, quotient real equality, selected limit, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness principle, Lean verification claim, or bridge component.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a downstream consumer. The consumer can inspect the comparison only through the package row $P$ and the local naming row $N$, both of which point back to the displayed dyadic inputs.

Read the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ scale-window monotonicity route attached to $L$ and $R$. That route exposes the finite radius and endpoint rows before any comparison ledger is consumed.

After that exposure, replay $K$ through the common-scale row $S$ and the comparison ledger $C$. The consumer receives the same finite comparison bit and its endpoint provenance, transported by $H$.

Since the route ends at the displayed comparison ledger and structural rows, it cannot export host order, trichotomy, quotient equality, selected limits, ambient completeness, Lean verification, or bridge data.
\end{proof}
