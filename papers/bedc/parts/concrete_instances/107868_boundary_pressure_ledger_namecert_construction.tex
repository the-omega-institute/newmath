$$

\item $O$ is the outer-boundary row, listing exactly the inactive codons at Hamming distance one from $\mathcal{R}$.
\item $D$ is the boundary-degree ledger, recording $d_{\partial}(z)$ for each displayed boundary codon $z$.
\item $P$ is the boundary-pressure ledger, recording $P_{\partial}(z)$ from the displayed support-neighbor weights.

\begin{theorem}[Boundary-pressure ledger NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:boundary-pressure-ledger-namecert-obligations}

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:boundary-pressure-ledger-carrier}. Its visible pressure data are exactly the rows $S,O,D,P,A,K,H,C,Q,N$.

The source support and boundary rows are finite. The row $S$ names the reassigned support, and $O$ lists the inactive codons at Hamming distance one from that support.

The degree and pressure rows are downstream of the same source. The row $D$ counts displayed support neighbors for each boundary codon, while $P$ sums the displayed support-neighbor weights. The aggregate row $A$ groups those same entries by class without changing their provenance.

The corner, transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows preserve the ledger. Because no coordinate bypasses $D$ and $P$, a consumer cannot obtain pressure data from hidden biological semantics, stochastic selection dynamics, empirical likelihood, or an unrestricted boundary enumerator.
\end{proof}
