\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\NullSequenceUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-nullsequence-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\NullSequenceUp$ interface names the constructive zero-convergence packet consumed by $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, and completion-facing certificates when a displayed rational difference stream is required to shrink to zero. Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, the certificate records explicit finite tolerance schedules and observation windows rather than quotienting streams or appealing to a host limit.

\begin{definition}[NullSequence NameCert carrier]
\label{def:null-sequence-namecert-carrier}
A $\NullSequenceUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
Z=(D,T,W,M,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is a $\RegSeqRatUp$ difference stream row whose inspected values are displayed through finite rational readbacks;
\item $T$ is a $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance schedule naming the positive dyadic bounds requested by a consumer;
\item $W$ is a $\StreamNameUp$ observation window that records exactly which finite portion of $D$ is inspected for each tolerance row;
\item $M$ is a $\CauchyModulusUp$ or $\RegularityModulusUp$ bound row selecting the threshold after which the displayed difference observations are below the tolerance in $T$;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal-consumption row whose equality classifier may consume the zero-convergence packet;
\item $H$ records the componentwise $\hsame$ rows comparing difference-stream observations, tolerance rows, windows, modulus rows, and seal rows;
\item $C$ records the $\Cont$ routes through which downstream readers reach the finite stream, tolerance, bound, and seal rows;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance ledger naming exactly the $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\CauchyModulusUp$ or $\RegularityModulusUp$, and $\RealUp$ sources;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\NullSequenceUp}$ provenance row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier for two accepted packets is equality of displayed zero-convergence packets under $\hsame$ transport of the stream window, tolerance schedule, bound row, seal row, continuation routes, package ledger, and local naming row.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[NullSequence NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:null-sequence-namecert-obligations}
The $\NameCert_{\NullSequenceUp}$ surface consists exactly of five obligations: carrier habitation by the displayed packet of \autoref{def:null-sequence-namecert-carrier}; classifier reflexivity, symmetry, and transitivity inherited componentwise from $\hsame$ over windows, tolerances, bounds, and seal rows; carrier transport along the classifier; exact ledger exposure of only the finite stream, modulus, tolerance, seal, continuation, package, and local certificate rows; and $\RealUp$ classifier consumption of the null difference packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier habitation is obtained by displaying the zero difference stream, a dyadic tolerance row, a finite observation window, a bound row that sends the tolerance request to that window, the real-seal consumption row, and the provenance rows.

The classifier laws are inherited componentwise. Reflexivity uses the identity $\hsame$ rows for the displayed stream, tolerance, window, bound, and seal coordinates. Symmetry reverses those rows, and transitivity composes them without introducing any host sequence equality.

Transport repacks the same finite coordinates after applying the displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ routes. Since the provenance ledger lists only the rows in \autoref{def:null-sequence-namecert-carrier}, every public read is exhausted by those rows and by the $\RealUp$ consumption boundary.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[NullSequence Real classifier consumption]
\label{thm:null-sequence-real-classifier-consumption}
For two $\RealUp$ seal rows presented by regular rational names, a $\NullSequenceUp$ packet for their displayed difference stream is precisely the finite witness that the $\RealUp$ equality classifier may consume: each requested dyadic tolerance is routed through a $\StreamNameUp$ window and a $\CauchyModulusUp$ or $\RegularityModulusUp$ bound, and the resulting tail estimate is exposed to the seal row through the recorded $\Cont$ and $\Pkg$ provenance.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the two real-name presentations through their $\RegSeqRatUp$ rows and form the displayed difference stream coordinate $D$. A consumer tolerance is then read through the dyadic schedule $T$.

The modulus coordinate $M$ chooses the finite window $W$ where the relevant tail observations of $D$ are inspected. The packet records that those observations are bounded by the tolerance row, so the real-seal row receives an explicit zero-convergence witness rather than a quotient comparison.

Conversely, a $\RealUp$ equality read that is certified by this packet can project only the difference stream, tolerance schedule, window, bound, seal, continuation, and provenance rows. Those are exactly the coordinates of \autoref{def:null-sequence-namecert-carrier}, so the classifier consumption is neither stronger nor weaker than the displayed null-difference packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[NullSequence tolerance-window exactness package]
\label{def:null-sequence-tolerance-window-exactness-package}
For an accepted $\NullSequenceUp$ packet
$$
Z=(D,T,W,M,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the tolerance-window exactness package is the same finite tuple, read with the following obligation-level roles:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the displayed $\RegSeqRatUp$ difference row whose zero-convergence is being consumed;
\item $T$ is the accepted dyadic tolerance request row;
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window selected for that tolerance request;
\item $M$ is the $\CauchyModulusUp$ or $\RegularityModulusUp$ threshold row binding the request in $T$ to the window in $W$;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row that reads the resulting null-difference evidence;
\item $H$ records the componentwise $\hsame$ transports for difference rows, tolerance rows, windows, thresholds, and seal rows;
\item $C$ records the $\Cont$ routes from the consumer request to $T$, from $T$ to $W$ and $M$, and from the certified difference row to $E$;
\item $P$ and $N$ record the dependency package and local $\NameCert_{\NullSequenceUp}$ provenance.
\end{enumerate}
The package is exact when every accepted tolerance request reaches the displayed threshold row $M$ and window $W$ before the $\RealUp$ seal reads the row $E$. No coordinate of the package is a host limit, an unrecorded tail estimate, or a quotient of difference streams.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[NullSequence window threshold exactness]
\label{thm:null-sequence-window-threshold-exactness}
Let $Z=(D,T,W,M,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\NullSequenceUp$ packet equipped with the tolerance-window exactness package of \autoref{def:null-sequence-tolerance-window-exactness-package}. Every accepted tolerance request consumed by $Z$ is routed through the displayed tolerance row $T$, the displayed observation window $W$, and the displayed bound row $M$ before it reaches any $\RealUp$ seal read.

Thus the zero-convergence evidence exported by $Z$ is exhausted by the $\RegSeqRatUp$ difference row, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row, the $\StreamNameUp$ window, the $\CauchyModulusUp$ or $\RegularityModulusUp$ threshold, and the recorded $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ provenance. It contains no unrecorded tail estimate and no host limit.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier coordinates of $Z$. The tolerance request is the coordinate $T$, so any consumer read first lands in the displayed dyadic tolerance schedule rather than in an ambient metric statement.

The window coordinate $W$ and threshold coordinate $M$ are then selected by the recorded $\Cont$ route. The bound row says that the inspected tail of the displayed difference stream $D$ is below the tolerance named by $T$ on precisely that finite window.

Transport between equivalent presentations is componentwise. The rows in $H$ move the difference row, tolerance row, window, threshold, and seal row, while $C$ preserves the order in which the consumer reaches them.

Finally, $P$ and $N$ certify the same dependency and local naming provenance after transport. Since the only outgoing seal read is the row $E$, every accepted tolerance request has passed through $T$, $W$, and $M$, and there is no remaining coordinate from which an unrecorded tail estimate or host limit could be exported.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[NullSequence Real seal factorization]
\label{thm:null-sequence-real-seal-factorization}
For two $\RealUp$ seal rows presented by regular rational names, any $\RealUp$ classifier consumption certified by a $\NullSequenceUp$ packet factors through the same $\RegSeqRatUp$ difference row $D$, dyadic tolerance schedule $T$, observation window $W$, threshold row $M$, seal row $E$, and provenance package $(H,C,P,N)$ of \autoref{def:null-sequence-tolerance-window-exactness-package}.

Equivalently, the $\RealUp$ equality read cannot bypass the displayed null-difference package by using an unstated stream representative, a host real limit, a hidden tail estimate, trichotomy, or choice.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the $\RealUp$ classifier consumption theorem \autoref{thm:null-sequence-real-classifier-consumption}. It identifies a $\NullSequenceUp$ packet as the finite witness that the real equality classifier may consume for the displayed regular-rational difference row.

Apply \autoref{thm:null-sequence-window-threshold-exactness} to that packet. The theorem forces each tolerance read to pass through the tolerance schedule $T$, the window $W$, and the threshold $M$ before reaching the seal row $E$.

The remaining coordinates are provenance rather than extra analytic data. The $\hsame$ rows in $H$, the $\Cont$ routes in $C$, and the package and local naming rows $P,N$ record how the same difference, tolerance, window, threshold, and seal rows are transported.

Therefore the real-seal consumption factors through exactly the displayed package. Any route using a different stream representative, host limit, hidden tail estimate, trichotomy, or choice would require a coordinate absent from the carrier of \autoref{def:null-sequence-namecert-carrier}.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[NullSequence non-escape boundary]
\label{thm:null-sequence-non-escape-boundary}
An accepted $\NullSequenceUp$ packet exports no Dedekind cut, no quotient of streams, no host real equality, no trichotomy principle, and no real-completeness theorem. Every public zero-convergence read factors through $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, $\NameCert$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\CauchyModulusUp$ or $\RegularityModulusUp$, and $\RealUp$ rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier coordinates. Each coordinate is a finite history row, a displayed transport route, a package provenance row, a local naming row, or one of the named dependency certificates.

The bound row names only a finite observation threshold for a requested tolerance. Passing to the $\RealUp$ seal consumes that finite zero-convergence row; it does not create a host real carrier, a completed quotient class, or a comparison by trichotomy.

Thus a downstream reader can cite exactly the listed finite rows. Since no excluded analytic or order-theoretic object is a coordinate of the carrier, none can be exported by the certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[NullSequence standard Bishop reading]
\label{thm:null-sequence-standard-bishop-reading}
In the Bishop--Bridges constructive-analysis reading, $\NullSequenceUp$ is the finite certificate that a regular rational difference stream converges to zero: tolerances are explicit dyadic rows, observation windows are explicit stream-name rows, and the bound is an explicit Cauchy or regularity modulus consumed by $\RealUp$ equality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The input side is the displayed $\RegSeqRatUp$ difference stream together with the dyadic tolerance schedule. No convergence claim is inferred from a host metric space or from an ambient completeness theorem.

The witnessing side is the finite window and bound row. For each tolerance request, the packet records the window after which the displayed difference observations are below the requested dyadic bound.

The output side is the $\RealUp$ seal-consumption row. It reads the finite zero-convergence ledger as equality evidence for regular rational names while withholding Dedekind cuts, quotient streams, host real equality, trichotomy, and real completeness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[NullSequence zero-tail criterion]
\label{thm:null-sequence-zero-tail-criterion}
Let $Z=(D,T,W,M,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\NullSequenceUp$ packet over a displayed $\RegSeqRatUp$ difference stream. A $\RealUp$ equality-classifier consumer read is admitted by $Z$ exactly when the read factors through the tolerance schedule $T$, the $\StreamNameUp$ window $W$, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ bound row read by $M$, the $\Cont$ routes in $C$, the $\Pkg$ provenance $P$, and the local $\NameCert_{\NullSequenceUp}$ row recorded by the tolerance-window package of \autoref{def:null-sequence-tolerance-window-exactness-package}.

Thus the classifier-facing zero-tail read for regular rational names is the same finite tolerance-window packet, transported through $H$ and sealed by $E$, and not an additional real-limit datum.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First project the accepted tolerance request. By \autoref{thm:null-sequence-window-threshold-exactness}, the request must pass through the displayed dyadic schedule $T$, the observation window $W$, and the threshold row $M$ before it reaches any $\RealUp$ seal row.

Next apply \autoref{thm:null-sequence-real-seal-factorization}. The real equality classifier can consume the zero-tail evidence only after that same stream, tolerance, window, threshold, seal, transport, continuation, package, and local certificate package has been read.

For the converse direction, suppose the consumer read already factors through these displayed rows. The tolerance-window package supplies the bound row and window for the requested tolerance, and the factorization theorem routes the resulting null-difference evidence into the seal row $E$.

Finally use \autoref{thm:null-sequence-non-escape-boundary}. Since no host limit, quotient stream, hidden tail estimate, trichotomy, or completeness object is exported by the packet, an admitted $\RealUp$ zero-tail read is exactly the finite route just described.
\end{proof}
