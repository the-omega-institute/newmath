\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\NullSequenceUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-nullsequence-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\NullSequenceUp$ interface names the constructive zero-convergence packet consumed by $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, and completion-facing certificates when a displayed rational difference stream is required to shrink to zero. Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, the certificate records explicit finite tolerance schedules and observation windows rather than quotienting streams or appealing to a host limit.

The packet depends on the regular-rational difference stream of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the finite observation windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}. A $\NullSequenceUp$ certificate is a consumer of those rows before the $\RealUp$ seal can read zero-convergence: it does not manufacture a stream, tolerance ledger, or window surface of its own.

\begin{definition}[NullSequence NameCert carrier]
\label{def:null-sequence-namecert-carrier}
A $\NullSequenceUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
Z=(D,T,W,M,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is a $\RegSeqRatUp$ difference stream row whose inspected values are displayed through finite rational readbacks;
\item $T$ is a $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance schedule naming the positive dyadic bounds requested by a consumer;
\item $W$ is a $\StreamNameUp$ observation window that records exactly which finite portion of $D$ is inspected for each tolerance row;
\item $M$ is a $\CauchyModulusUp$ or $\RegularityModulusUp$ bound row selecting the threshold after which the displayed difference observations are below the tolerance in $T$;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal-consumption row whose equality classifier may consume the zero-convergence packet;
\item $H$ records the componentwise $\hsame$ rows comparing difference-stream observations, tolerance rows, windows, modulus rows, and seal rows;
\item $C$ records the $\Cont$ routes through which downstream readers reach the finite stream, tolerance, bound, and seal rows;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance ledger naming exactly the $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\CauchyModulusUp$ or $\RegularityModulusUp$, and $\RealUp$ sources;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\NullSequenceUp}$ provenance row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier for two accepted packets is equality of displayed zero-convergence packets under $\hsame$ transport of the stream window, tolerance schedule, bound row, seal row, continuation routes, package ledger, and local naming row.
\end{definition}

\begin{proof}

The classifier laws are inherited componentwise. Reflexivity uses the identity $\hsame$ rows for the displayed stream, tolerance, window, bound, and seal coordinates. Symmetry reverses those rows, and transitivity composes them without introducing any host sequence equality.

Transport repacks the same finite coordinates after applying the displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ routes. Since the provenance ledger lists only the rows in \autoref{def:null-sequence-namecert-carrier}, every public read is exhausted by those rows and by the $\RealUp$ consumption boundary.
