\begin{definition}[Finite Lipschitz iteration carrier]
\label{def:finite-lipschitz-iteration-carrier}
A $\FiniteLipschitzIterationUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
I=(X,d,f,L,q,n,x_0,W,B,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X,d$ are the $\MetricUp$ source and distance rows.
\item $f$ is the $\ContinuousMapUp$ self-map row over the same metric source.
\item $L$ is the Lipschitz-constant row, read through the same map and source.
\item $q$ is the dyadic tolerance row used to compare the finite tail budget.
\item $n$ is the displayed finite iteration counter.
\item $x_0$ is the initial-point row.
\item $W$ is the finite prefix row listing the displayed reads of $x_0,f(x_0),\ldots,f^n(x_0)$.
\item $B$ is the contraction-budget row consumed by $\CauchyModulusUp$ and $\PicardContractionUp$ consumers.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the metric source, map, Lipschitz constant, dyadic tolerance, counter, prefix, and budget rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay through the metric source, continuous-map row, Lipschitz row, finite prefix, and budget row.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\MetricUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\RealUp$, $\CauchyModulusUp$, $\PicardContractionUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteLipschitzIterationUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier compares finite prefixes only through these rows. It has no coordinate for a selected fixed point, quotient orbit, infinite limit, host completeness theorem, or ambient contraction principle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Finite Lipschitz iteration NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:finite-lipschitz-iteration-namecert-obligations}

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The displayed rows are precisely $X,d,f,L,q,n,x_0,W,B,H,C,P,N$, so carrier admission and local naming are direct reads from \autoref{def:finite-lipschitz-iteration-carrier}.

The exactness of $W$ is finite. At counter $0$, the prefix row exposes only the initial point $x_0$. At a successor counter, the next row is obtained by replaying the same continuous-map row $f$ over the same metric source and appending that displayed read to the previous prefix.

The budget row $B$ is attached to the same metric source, continuous-map row, Lipschitz constant row, and dyadic tolerance row. Transport through $H$ and replay through $C$ preserve those rows before $\Pkg$ provenance and the local NameCert expose the packet.

Since the carrier contains no selected fixed point, infinite orbit, quotient equality, host limit, or completeness coordinate, the listed rows exhaust the local obligation surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Finite Lipschitz contraction-budget induction]
\label{thm:finite-lipschitz-iteration-contraction-budget-induction}
For every accepted $\FiniteLipschitzIterationUp$ carrier and every displayed finite counter $n$, the contraction-budget row $B$ is obtained by induction on $n$: the base prefix reads the initial point over the metric source, and each successor step consumes the same metric source, continuous-map row, Lipschitz constant row, dyadic tolerance row, $\hsame$ transport, $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ row. The induction exports only a finite prefix budget for $\CauchyModulusUp$ and $\PicardContractionUp$ consumers.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Induct on the displayed counter $n$.

In the base case, project the carrier and read $x_0$ as the whole prefix. The metric source $X,d$, tolerance $q$, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows are present, but no application of $f$ has yet been consumed.

For the successor case, assume the previous prefix budget has been displayed over the same rows. Replay the continuous-map row $f$ through $C$, transport the metric source, Lipschitz constant, and tolerance through $H$, and append the next displayed read to $W$. The budget row $B$ is therefore updated by the same finite rows rather than by a new source, a hidden map, or an external limit.

The induction stops at the displayed counter. It supplies a finite contraction-budget handoff to $\CauchyModulusUp$ and $\PicardContractionUp$, and it does not assert convergence, completeness, or fixed-point existence.
\end{proof}
