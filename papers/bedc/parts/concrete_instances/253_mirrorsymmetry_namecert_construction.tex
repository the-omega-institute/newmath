\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\MirrorSymmetryUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-mirrorsymmetry-namecert}

The mirror-symmetry interface $\MirrorSymmetryUp$ packages, for a Calabi-Yau pair $(X, X^\vee)$, the conjectural equivalence of the A-model on one side with the B-model on the other (Hodge numbers, Fukaya category vs derived category). It depends on $\NameCert_{\SymplecticUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-symplectic-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\DerivedCatUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-derivedcat-namecert}), and bridges symplectic and complex-algebraic certificates through a common categorical classifier.

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:mirrorsymmetry-carrier}

\begin{definition}[Mirror-symmetry paired history carrier]
\label{def:mirrorsymmetry-bhist-pair-carrier}
Fix a $\NameCert_{\SymplecticUp}$ A-model source row and a $\NameCert_{\DerivedCatUp}$ B-model source row. A $\MirrorSymmetryUp$ pair carrier row is the finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ ledger that records the two source certificates, the visible A-model categorical answer rows, the visible B-model categorical answer rows, and the $\Cont$ witnesses that align each displayed A-row with the corresponding displayed B-row inside one observed pair packet.

The carrier is a pairing surface for finite categorical observations. It does not include a full Fukaya category, a derived-equivalence theorem, Hodge-number symmetry beyond the observed rows, or a host Calabi-Yau moduli statement.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Mirror-symmetry categorical classifier]
\label{def:mirrorsymmetry-categorical-classifier}
Two rows of \autoref{def:mirrorsymmetry-bhist-pair-carrier} are classified as the same $\MirrorSymmetryUp$ packet exactly when their A-model source rows are transported by $\NameCert_{\SymplecticUp}$, their B-model source rows are transported by $\NameCert_{\DerivedCatUp}$, and each paired categorical answer row is matched through $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\Pkg$ witnesses.

The classifier is generated only by source transport, categorical answer transport, package transport for the displayed finite ledger, and concatenation of observed pair rows. It contains no generator asserting an external mirror equivalence.
\end{definition}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:mirrorsymmetry-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[Mirror-symmetry source certificate scope]
\label{thm:mirrorsymmetry-source-certificate-scope}
Every $\MirrorSymmetryUp$ carrier row has exactly one $\NameCert_{\SymplecticUp}$ A-model source and one $\NameCert_{\DerivedCatUp}$ B-model source, and every other accepted field is a finite categorical answer history or a transport witness named by $\hsame$, $\Cont$, or $\Pkg$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.MirrorSymmetryUp.MirrorSymmetryPairCarrier\_source\_certificate\_scope}
\begin{proof}
Invert the carrier definition. The first source field is the symplectic certificate, the second source field is the derived-category certificate, and the remaining fields are indexed by the finite observed pairing ledger.

The classifier of \autoref{def:mirrorsymmetry-categorical-classifier} can replace source representatives only through the two named $\NameCert$ classifiers. It can replace answer rows only through the displayed $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\Pkg$ witnesses, so no additional source or host category is introduced.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Mirror-symmetry categorical stability]
\label{thm:mirrorsymmetry-categorical-stability}
Classified $\MirrorSymmetryUp$ packets remain classified after replacing either source certificate by a NameCert-classified representative and after transporting any displayed categorical answer row through $\hsame$, $\Cont$, or $\Pkg$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.MirrorSymmetryUp.MirrorSymmetryCategorical\_stability}
\begin{proof}
Transport the A-model and B-model sources first. The source certificates give the two NameCert-level comparison rows, and the carrier repacks the transported representatives as the same finite pair packet.

For each displayed categorical answer, use the stored $\hsame$ row to replace endpoints, the $\Cont$ row to keep the answer in the paired ledger, and the $\Pkg$ row to preserve the package reading. Concatenating the finite list of transported pair rows gives the required classifier witness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Mirror-symmetry pairing obligation package]
\label{thm:mirrorsymmetry-pairing-obligation-package}
The $\MirrorSymmetryUp$ chapter exports a finite pairing obligation package consisting of the paired $\mathsf{BHist}$ carrier, the categorical classifier, the two source $\NameCert$ rows, and the statement that the ledger hides only the observed A-model and B-model matching rows.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.MirrorSymmetryUp.MirrorSymmetryPairCarrier\_semantic\_name\_certificate}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.MirrorSymmetryUp.MirrorSymmetryCategoricalClassifier\_endpoint\_confluence}
\begin{proof}
Start from a carrier row and a classifier witness. The carrier supplies the two source certificates and the finite list of paired categorical answer rows; the classifier supplies only the source transports and answer transports allowed in \autoref{def:mirrorsymmetry-categorical-classifier}.

Ledger exactness follows by induction over the finite observed pair list. Each item is either an A-model answer, a B-model answer, or one of the named transport witnesses. Therefore the obligation package records a BEDC pairing surface and does not assert a full external mirror-equivalence theorem.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Mirror-symmetry HMS source boundary]
\label{thm:mirrorsymmetry-hms-source-boundary}
The only external source rows available to a $\MirrorSymmetryUp$ packet are
$\NameCert_{\SymplecticUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\DerivedCatUp}$. Every A-model or
B-model answer exported by the packet is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ row
transported by the displayed $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\Pkg$ witnesses.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.MirrorSymmetryUp.MirrorSymmetryPairCarrier\_hms\_source\_boundary}
\begin{proof}
Project the source certificate scope of
\autoref{thm:mirrorsymmetry-source-certificate-scope}. It exposes one
symplectic source certificate and one derived-category source certificate.

The classifier of \autoref{def:mirrorsymmetry-categorical-classifier} then
lists the only movements allowed after those source rows are chosen. Source
representatives move by their two $\NameCert$ classifiers, while answer rows
move only by $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\Pkg$.

Thus the HMS-facing boundary is a finite BEDC ledger of observed categorical
answer rows. It does not add a derived equivalence, a complete Fukaya category,
a Hodge theorem, or a standard bridge.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Mirror-symmetry local pairing ledger exactness]
\label{thm:mirrorsymmetry-local-pairing-ledger-exactness}
The local pairing ledger of a $\MirrorSymmetryUp$ packet exposes exactly the
two source certificates, the paired A-model and B-model categorical answer
rows, the source-transport rows, the answer-transport rows, and the package
provenance row. No other row is readable from the packet.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.MirrorSymmetryUp.MirrorSymmetryPairCarrier\_local\_pairing\_ledger\_exactness}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the paired history carrier of
\autoref{def:mirrorsymmetry-bhist-pair-carrier}. Its fields are the two source
certificates, the visible paired categorical answers, and the continuation
witnesses aligning each displayed pair.

Next unpack the categorical classifier of
\autoref{def:mirrorsymmetry-categorical-classifier}. It contributes only source
transport, answer transport, package transport, and concatenation of the
observed finite pair rows.

Combining these projections gives the exact ledger. Since every exported item
is one of those fields or one of those transport witnesses, there is no hidden
row for global mirror equivalence, category completeness, or host Hodge theory.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Mirror-symmetry NameCert obligation inventory]
\label{thm:mirrorsymmetry-namecert-obligation-inventory}
The finite $\NameCert_{\MirrorSymmetryUp}$ obligation inventory consists of
the paired carrier, the categorical classifier, the HMS source boundary, the
categorical stability row, and the local pairing ledger exactness row. These
rows give the complete obligation package for the displayed finite pairing
surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier and classifier definitions. They provide the finite
packet to be named and the admissible comparison rows between packets.

Apply \autoref{thm:mirrorsymmetry-hms-source-boundary} to fix the only external
source rows. Apply \autoref{thm:mirrorsymmetry-categorical-stability} to keep
source and answer replacement inside the displayed classifier. Apply
\autoref{thm:mirrorsymmetry-local-pairing-ledger-exactness} to identify the
full readable ledger.

Together these rows are exactly the obligation inventory asserted by
\autoref{thm:mirrorsymmetry-pairing-obligation-package}. The inventory is a
finite BEDC pairing surface and leaves derived equivalence, Fukaya category
completeness, Hodge theory, and standard bridge data unclaimed.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.MirrorSymmetryUp.MirrorSymmetryPairCarrier\_namecert\_obligation\_inventory}

\begin{theorem}[Mirror-symmetry scoped kernel surface]
\label{thm:mirrorsymmetry-scoped-kernel-scope}
The carrier, categorical classifier, HMS source boundary, stability row, and
local pairing ledger of $\NameCert_{\MirrorSymmetryUp}$ are scoped entirely by
the two source $\NameCert$ rows, the finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ paired answer
rows, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\Pkg$.

This scoped surface contains no full Fukaya category, derived equivalence,
Hodge theorem, Calabi-Yau moduli datum, or standard bridge.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with \autoref{thm:mirrorsymmetry-source-certificate-scope}. It fixes the
only external sources as the symplectic and derived-category $\NameCert$ rows,
and it leaves every remaining accepted field inside the finite observed
pairing ledger.

Apply \autoref{thm:mirrorsymmetry-categorical-stability}. The only admissible
movements of source and answer rows are the displayed $\NameCert$ transports
and the $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\Pkg$ witnesses, so the classifier remains
within the same kernel surface.

Next use \autoref{thm:mirrorsymmetry-local-pairing-ledger-exactness}. It reads
the local ledger as exactly the two source certificates, paired categorical
answer rows, source transports, answer transports, and package provenance.

Finally apply
\autoref{thm:mirrorsymmetry-namecert-obligation-inventory}. The inventory
contains precisely the carrier, categorical classifier, HMS source boundary,
stability row, and local ledger exactness row. Hence the scoped kernel surface
is the listed finite BEDC packet and excludes full Fukaya categories, derived
equivalence, Hodge theory, Calabi-Yau moduli, and standard bridges.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.MirrorSymmetryUp.MirrorSymmetryPairCarrier\_scoped\_kernel\_scope}
