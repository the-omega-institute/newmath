\begin{theorem}[DyadicApproximation refined finite-packet readback determinacy]
\label{thm:dyadicapproximation-refined-finite-packet-readback-determinacy}
Let $A$ and $A'$ be accepted $\DyadicApproximationUp$ finite packets. Suppose
their transported precision rows, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint windows,
terminal overlap routes, overlap refinement normal forms,
$\RegSeqRatUp$ readback windows, $\RealUp$ seal tokens, and
$\DyadicMeshUp/\ValidatedNumericsUp$ consumer normal forms are the same
displayed BEDC rows. Suppose also that their visible $\hsame$, $\Cont$,
$\mathsf{Pkg}$, and local $\NameCert_{\DyadicApproximationUp}$ rows agree.

Then every displayed precision refinement reads the same public finite
surface from $A$ and $A'$. The common read is exactly the displayed precision
row, endpoint window, terminal overlap route, overlap normal form,
regular-rational readback window, real seal token, mesh-and-validated consumer
handoff, visible transport rows, continuation routes, package provenance, and
local certificate rows named above.

The determinacy statement does not identify completed real values, does not
export host interval objects, does not decide a trichotomy row, and does not
read solver state or choice data.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First exhaust the finite readback of each packet. Apply
\autoref{thm:dyadicapproximation-finite-packet-readback-exhaustion} to both
$A$ and $A'$. The only rows available to either consumer are the displayed
precision, endpoint, readback, seal, refinement, overlap, transport,
continuation, package, local certificate, and
$\DyadicMeshUp/\ValidatedNumericsUp$ handoff rows.

Next normalize the overlap side. Use
\autoref{thm:dyadicapproximation-overlap-refinement-chain-normal-form} for the
displayed overlap refinement chain of each packet. The hypotheses identify the
terminal route and the overlap normal form, so the two packet reads have the
same terminal finite route after any displayed precision refinement.

Finally normalize the downstream consumer. By
\autoref{thm:dyadicapproximation-dyadicmesh-validatednumerics-consumer-normal-form},
the mesh cell and validated enclosure can read only the common containment,
mesh-level, cell-index, endpoint-window, regular-rational, real-seal,
transport, continuation, package, and local certificate rows. Since those rows
are assumed equal as displayed BEDC data, the public finite read is the same.

The cited exhaustion and normal-form results also list the absent coordinates.
Thus the conclusion cannot be strengthened into completed-real equality, host
interval equality, trichotomy, solver-state agreement, or a choice principle.
\end{proof}
