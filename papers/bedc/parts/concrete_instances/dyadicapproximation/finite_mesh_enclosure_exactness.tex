\begin{definition}[DyadicApproximation mesh-enclosure exactness packet]
\label{def:dyadicapproximation-mesh-enclosure-exactness-packet}
Let $A=(n,d,w,l,p)$ be an accepted $\DyadicApproximationUp$ row, let
$M=(\ell,J,I,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\DyadicMeshUp$ cell row, and let
$V=(I,\varepsilon,\omega,\rho,\lambda,h,c,p',n')$ be an accepted
$\ValidatedNumericsUp$ enclosure row. A DyadicApproximation mesh-enclosure
exactness packet is the finite BEDC packet consisting of these three rows
together with the following shared coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item a shared displayed precision row identifying $n$, $\ell$, and
$\varepsilon$ as the same finite request through visible $\hsame$ rows;
\item the rational interval cell $I$ and its dyadic endpoint-radius-order
ledger $E$, with $d$ read as the transported endpoint contained in $I$;
\item the $\RealUp$ finite-window seal read from $w$, $l$, $\rho$, and
$\lambda$, with no completed real coordinate added to the packet;
\item the visible $\hsame$ transports that identify the dyadic endpoint,
regular-rational observation window, mesh cell, and enclosure readback rows;
\item the visible $\Cont$ routes by which the dyadic finite window, mesh cell
containment, rational-interval coverage, validated readback, and precision
refinement are inspected by consumers;
\item the $\Pkg$ provenance rows $p$, $P$, and $p'$ naming the
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RationalIntervalUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$,
$\DyadicMeshUp$, and $\ValidatedNumericsUp$ dependency surface;
\item the local $\NameCert_{\DyadicApproximationUp}$,
$\NameCert_{\DyadicMeshUp}$, and $\NameCert_{\ValidatedNumericsUp}$
provenance rows.
\end{enumerate}
The packet is finite: its carrier is only the accepted rows and the displayed
transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming evidence listed above.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicApproximation finite mesh-enclosure exactness]
\label{thm:dyadicapproximation-finite-mesh-enclosure-exactness}
Let $A=(n,d,w,l,p)$ be an accepted $\DyadicApproximationUp$ row, let
$M=(\ell,J,I,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\DyadicMeshUp$ cell row whose
$\RationalIntervalUp$ cell $I$ contains the transported endpoint $d$ at the
displayed precision, and let
$V=(I,\varepsilon,\omega,\rho,\lambda,h,c,p',n')$ be an accepted
$\ValidatedNumericsUp$ enclosure row over the same cell and containment ledger.
Assume the visible $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ rows
identify the precision request, rational interval cell, $\RealUp$ finite
window seal, and enclosure readback rows as in
\autoref{def:dyadicapproximation-mesh-enclosure-exactness-packet}.

Then every accepted mesh-cell or validated-enclosure consumer read through
that packet factors through one finite $\RealUp$ surface. The factorization is
exactly the DyadicApproximation $\RealUp$ seal handoff and finite-window
scope, the DyadicMesh cell-containment and rational-interval coverage rows,
the DyadicMesh handoff to ValidatedNumerics, and the ValidatedNumerics real
readback and precision-refinement containment rows.

Consequently the mesh-cell consumer and the validated-enclosure consumer read
the same finite surface: the shared precision row, transported dyadic endpoint,
rational interval cell, endpoint-radius-order ledger, regular-rational
observation window, $\RealUp$ readback token, visible $\hsame$ transports,
visible $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ provenance.
The statement does not assert completed real equality, infinite mesh
refinement, solver correctness, floating-point semantics, or choice-selected
limits.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DyadicApproximationUp.DyadicApproximationCarrier\_finite\_mesh\_enclosure\_exactness}
\begin{proof}
First project the accepted dyadic row. Apply
\autoref{thm:dyadic-approximation-real-seal-handoff} to expose the only
$\RealUp$ handoff surface carried by $A$, and apply
\autoref{thm:dyadicapproximation-window-scope} to restrict all dyadic reads to
the displayed finite window. This produces the precision row, transported
endpoint, regular-rational observation window, seal ledger, visible
transports, continuation routes, package provenance, and local
$\NameCert_{\DyadicApproximationUp}$ row.

Next project the mesh rows. Apply
\autoref{thm:dyadicmesh-cell-containment-obligation} for the level, cell index,
rational interval cell, dyadic containment ledger, transport, continuation,
provenance, and local naming rows. Apply
\autoref{thm:dyadicmesh-rationalinterval-coverage} to read $I$ as the complete
finite coverage object, and apply
\autoref{thm:dyadicmesh-validatednumerics-handoff} to identify the mesh
handoff rows consumed by the enclosure.

Now project the validated enclosure rows. Apply
\autoref{thm:validatednumerics-real-readback-soundness} to tie the
$\RealUp$ readback token to the displayed $\RegSeqRatUp$ window and precision
request. Apply
\autoref{thm:validatednumerics-precision-refinement-containment} to keep every
refined finite read inside the same rational interval cell and containment
ledger.

Finally repack the projected rows into the exactness packet of
\autoref{def:dyadicapproximation-mesh-enclosure-exactness-packet}. The
identifications in the hypotheses make the dyadic seal handoff, mesh cell
coverage, and validated enclosure readback one shared finite BEDC surface.
Every cited boundary is a finite packet boundary, so no completed real
equality, infinite mesh refinement, solver correctness, floating-point
semantics, or choice-selected limit is exported.
\end{proof}
