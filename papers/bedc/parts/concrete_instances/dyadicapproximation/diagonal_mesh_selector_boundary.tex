\begin{theorem}[DyadicApproximation diagonal mesh-selector boundary]
\label{thm:dyadicapproximation-diagonal-mesh-selector-boundary}
Let $A$ be an accepted $\DyadicApproximationUp$ finite packet, let $M$ be an
accepted $\DyadicMeshUp$ finite cell, and let $D$ be an accepted
$\RealDiagonalCompletionUp$ packet. Suppose these packets display the same
unary precision row, selected diagonal window, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback,
rational-interval cell, $\RealUp$ readback token, visible $\hsame$
transports, visible $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local
$\NameCert_{\DyadicApproximationUp}$, $\NameCert_{\DyadicMeshUp}$, and
$\NameCert_{\RealDiagonalCompletionUp}$ rows.

Then the diagonal-completion consumer of $D$ and the mesh or validated dyadic
consumer of $A$ and $M$ read the same finite $\RegSeqRatUp/\RealUp$ boundary
surface. The shared surface is exactly the displayed precision row, selected
diagonal window, regular-rational readback, rational-interval cell, real
readback token, visible transport rows, continuation routes, package
provenance, and local name-certificate rows.

This boundary theorem does not identify quotient streams, host real numbers,
selected limits, infinite meshes, solver correctness, or classical
completeness.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DyadicApproximationUp.DyadicApproximationCarrier\_diagonal\_mesh\_selector\_boundary}
\begin{proof}
Start with the dyadic side. Apply
\autoref{thm:dyadicapproximation-shared-real-boundary-determinacy} to the
accepted dyadic packet and its displayed mesh and validated-readback rows.
This reduces every dyadic finite-window, mesh-cell, and validated-enclosure
read to the listed $\RegSeqRatUp/\RealUp$ boundary surface.

Next read the mesh packet through
\autoref{thm:dyadicmesh-standard-finite-mesh-bridge-boundary}. The bridge
shows that the standard finite mesh consumer has no coordinate beyond the
unary level, finite cell index, rational interval, endpoint and real readback,
transport, continuation, package, and local certificate rows already listed in
the statement.

Now turn to the diagonal-completion packet. Apply
\autoref{thm:real-diagonal-completion-window-extraction} to factor the
consumer read through the displayed modulus row, diagonal selector, scheduled
window, and regular-rational readback. Then apply
\autoref{thm:real-diagonal-completion-seal-factorization} to identify the
visible $\RealUp$ seal ledger with those extracted finite windows and their
readback ledger.

The hypotheses identify the precision, selected window, rational interval,
regular-rational readback, real token, transports, continuations, package
provenance, and local certificates on both routes. Therefore both consumers
project the same finite boundary surface. Since every cited step is a finite
BEDC packet boundary, none of the excluded analytic or quotient objects is
exported.
\end{proof}
