\begin{theorem}[DyadicApproximation finite-packet Real-seal stability]
\label{thm:dyadicapproximation-finite-packet-real-seal-stability}
Let $A$ and $A'$ be accepted finite $\DyadicApproximationUp$ packets sharing a
displayed common refinement $R$. Let $M$ be an accepted $\DyadicMeshUp$ cell,
and let $V$ be an accepted $\ValidatedNumericsUp$ enclosure over the same
rational interval selected by the refined dyadic endpoint. Suppose the visible
$\hsame$ transports, $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance rows, and local
$\NameCert$ rows identify the finite-packet route, the mesh-cell route, the
validated-enclosure route, and the $\RealUp$ seal route over that common
refinement.

Then the terminal finite-packet readback and the $\RealUp$ seal expose the same
finite surface after refinement, whether the consumer reaches it through the
common-refinement route or through the validated enclosure route. This surface
is exactly the precision row, transported dyadic endpoint,
$\RationalIntervalUp$ cell, $\StreamNameUp$ window, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback,
$\RealUp$ seal token, $\ValidatedNumericsUp$ containment row, visible
$\hsame$ transports, visible $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local
$\NameCert_{\DyadicApproximationUp}$, $\NameCert_{\DyadicMeshUp}$, and
$\NameCert_{\ValidatedNumericsUp}$ rows.

The stability assertion is only this finite seal surface. It does not assert
completed real equality, solver correctness, floating-point semantics,
infinite mesh selection, or choice-selected limits.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First put the terminal readback in its finite normal form. Apply
\autoref{thm:dyadicapproximation-terminal-finite-packet-real-readback}. This
identifies the terminal precision, transported endpoint, stream-name window,
regular-rational readback, $\RealUp$ seal row, transport rows, continuation
routes, package provenance, and local name-certificate rows visible to the
finite packet.

Next compare the common-refinement route with the validated-enclosure route.
Apply \autoref{thm:dyadicapproximation-refinement-seal-commutation} to the
accepted packets, the displayed refinement, the accepted mesh cell, and the
accepted validated enclosure. This places the rational interval cell,
containment row, refined endpoint, $\RegSeqRatUp$ observation window, and
$\RealUp$ seal token on one finite consumer surface.

Finally use
\autoref{thm:dyadicapproximation-shared-real-boundary-determinacy}. The shared
boundary theorem says that every mesh-cell, validated-enclosure, and terminal
finite-window read with the displayed rows reads the same
$\RegSeqRatUp/\RealUp$ boundary. Repacking those rows gives exactly the
surface stated above, and the cited finite boundaries provide no coordinate for
completed real equality, solver correctness, floating-point semantics,
infinite mesh selection, or choice-selected limits.
\end{proof}
