\begin{theorem}[DyadicApproximation terminal-window two-stage factorization]
\label{thm:dyadicapproximation-terminal-window-two-stage-factorization}
Let $A=(n,d,w,l,p)$ be an accepted terminal $\DyadicApproximationUp$ packet with
terminal displayed window $T$. Suppose its terminal readback is projected by
\autoref{thm:dyadicapproximation-terminal-window-prefix-projection}, and suppose
the same packet is consumed through the validated enclosure route of
\autoref{thm:dyadicapproximation-terminal-validated-enclosure-readback}.

Then the two reads factor through one finite public surface. That surface is
exactly the terminal precision row, the transported dyadic endpoint row, the
$\StreamNameUp$ window, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback window, the $\RealUp$ seal
ledger, the validated containment ledger, the visible $\hsame$ transports, the
visible $\Cont$ routes, the $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance rows, and the local
$\NameCert$ rows. Reading first by terminal prefix projection and then through
the validated enclosure route gives the same displayed surface as reading first
through the enclosure route and then commuting back to the terminal dyadic
window.

The factorization introduces no quotient real, host-real equality,
solver-correctness assertion, or selected completion point.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply
\autoref{thm:dyadicapproximation-terminal-window-prefix-projection}. The
terminal readback of $A$ is reduced to the prefix rows of $T$: precision,
endpoint, stream, regular-rational observation, real-seal ledger, visible
transport, continuation, provenance, and local certificate data.

Next exhaust the finite packet read. By
\autoref{thm:dyadicapproximation-finite-packet-readback-exhaustion}, every
consumer-visible read of the accepted terminal packet is already among those
finite rows together with the displayed validated enclosure handoff. Apply
\autoref{thm:dyadicapproximation-refined-finite-packet-readback-determinacy} to
remove dependence on which compatible finite refinement exposes the terminal
surface.

Now read the enclosure side. Apply
\autoref{thm:dyadicapproximation-terminal-validated-enclosure-readback} to
identify the $\DyadicMeshUp$ cell and $\ValidatedNumericsUp$ enclosure read with
the same terminal precision, endpoint, regular-rational, real-seal,
containment, transport, continuation, provenance, and local certificate rows.

Finally apply
\autoref{thm:dyadicapproximation-validated-enclosure-route-commutation}. This
commutes the terminal dyadic route with the mesh-and-enclosure route, so both
orders factor through the single finite surface listed in the statement. Every
cited boundary is a finite BEDC readback boundary, hence none supplies a
quotient real, host-real equality, solver-correctness row, or chosen completion
point.
\end{proof}
