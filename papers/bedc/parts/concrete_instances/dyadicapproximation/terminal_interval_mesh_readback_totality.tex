\begin{theorem}[DyadicApproximation terminal interval-mesh readback totality]
\label{thm:dyadicapproximation-terminal-interval-mesh-readback-totality}
Let $T$ be an accepted terminal $\DyadicApproximationUp$ finite packet. Every
terminal interval-mesh read of $T$ factors through the displayed
$\RationalIntervalUp$ window, the $\DyadicMeshUp$ cell selector, the
$\ValidatedNumericsUp$ enclosure row, the terminal finite-packet readback, and
the $\RealUp$ seal boundary.

Equivalently, the read is total over the displayed finite packet: its public
surface is exactly the terminal precision row, transported dyadic endpoint,
rational interval cell, dyadic mesh selector, validated enclosure ledger,
scheduled $\StreamNameUp$ window, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, $\RealUp$ seal row,
visible $\hsame$ transports, visible $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and
local $\NameCert$ provenance. It exports no host interval, quotient real, or
unrecorded completeness datum.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First normalize the terminal finite-packet read. Apply
\autoref{thm:dyadicapproximation-terminal-finite-packet-real-readback}. The
terminal packet is read through the displayed precision row, transported
endpoint, scheduled stream window, regular-rational readback, $\RealUp$ seal
ledger, transport rows, continuation routes, package provenance, and local
certificate row.

Next attach the mesh and enclosure coordinates. Apply
\autoref{thm:dyadicapproximation-terminal-mesh-enclosure-compatibility} to put
the terminal mesh route and the validated enclosure route on the same terminal
window. Apply \autoref{thm:dyadicapproximation-finite-mesh-enclosure-exactness}
to identify the rational interval cell, dyadic mesh selector, endpoint
containment ledger, and validated enclosure row with that same finite surface.

Finally pass through the diagonal-completion handoff. By
\autoref{thm:dyadicapproximation-terminal-diagonal-completion-handoff}, the
terminal packet and the diagonal route expose one shared $\RealUp$ consumer
surface over the displayed terminal rows.

The listed rows exhaust the packet coordinates available to an interval-mesh
consumer. Since the cited boundaries contain no host interval, quotient real,
or ambient completeness field, the total readback exports none of those data.
\end{proof}
