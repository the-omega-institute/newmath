\begin{theorem}[DyadicApproximation terminal budget-seal uniqueness]
\label{thm:dyadic-approximation-terminal-budget-seal-uniqueness}
Let $T_0$ and $T_1$ be accepted terminal $\DyadicApproximationUp$ finite packets. Suppose both packets are matched to the same terminal precision row, transported dyadic endpoint, $\RationalIntervalUp$ cell, $\DyadicMeshUp$ selector, $\ValidatedNumericsUp$ enclosure, $\StreamNameUp$ window, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, $\DyadicTailBoundUp$ ledger, $\RealUp$ seal, visible $\hsame$ transports, $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ rows.

Then $T_0$ and $T_1$ present the same Real-completion budget-to-seal route to every consumer. In particular, a consumer that enters through the finite budget row, the dyadic endpoint, the mesh selector, the validated enclosure, the stream window, the regular-rational readback, the tail-bound ledger, or the terminal $\RealUp$ seal sees one terminal route surface.

This uniqueness statement does not identify completed reals. It refuses completed real equality, quotient streams, selected host limits, solver oracles, metric-completion objects, and host real equality as distinguishing data.
\end{theorem}
