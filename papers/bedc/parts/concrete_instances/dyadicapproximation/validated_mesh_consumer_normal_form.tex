\begin{theorem}[DyadicApproximation DyadicMesh ValidatedNumerics consumer normal form]
\label{thm:dyadicapproximation-dyadicmesh-validatednumerics-consumer-normal-form}
Let $A=(n,d,w,l,p)$ be an accepted $\DyadicApproximationUp$ packet. Let
$M=(\ell,J,I,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\DyadicMeshUp$ cell whose
$\RationalIntervalUp$ row $I$ contains the transported dyadic endpoint $d$ at
the displayed precision $n$. Let $V$ be a $\ValidatedNumericsUp$ enclosure
reading the same mesh cell through the displayed enclosure row $I$ and the same
containment ledger.

Then the three accepted packets determine one finite
$\RegSeqRatUp$ and $\RealUp$ consumer surface. The surface consists exactly of
the precision row $n$, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint $d$, the
$\RationalIntervalUp$ containment row $I$, the $\DyadicMeshUp$ level row
$\ell$, the cell-index row $J$, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback carried by $w$ and
$l$, the $\RealUp$ seal ledger of $A$, the displayed $\hsame$ transports, the
displayed $\Cont$ routes, the $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance rows, and the local
$\NameCert$ rows of the three packets.

The normal form exports no quotient real, solver-correctness claim,
floating-point semantics, host equality, or classical completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted dyadic packet first. By
\autoref{thm:dyadicapproximation-public-finite-window-export} and
\autoref{thm:dyadicapproximation-terminal-refinement-window}, its public
finite window is the displayed precision row, dyadic endpoint, stream window,
regular-rational readback, real-seal ledger, visible transports,
continuations, package provenance, and local certificate data.

Project the mesh packet next. By
\autoref{thm:dyadicmesh-rationalinterval-coverage} and
\autoref{thm:dyadicmesh-validatednumerics-handoff}, the mesh contributes only
the level row, cell-index row, rational interval coverage row, dyadic
containment ledger, refinement route, endpoint readback, package provenance,
and local certificate row.

Read the validated enclosure through
\autoref{thm:validatednumerics-validated-enclosure-bridge-boundary}. Its
consumer boundary is the finite enclosure row, endpoint provenance, precision
request, regular-rational readback, real-name readback token, containment
routes, transports, package provenance, and local certificate row.

The shared-containment hypothesis identifies the dyadic endpoint read from
$A$, the rational interval row $I$ from $M$, and the enclosure row read by $V$
at the displayed precision. After that identification, all remaining rows are
the listed $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and local $\NameCert$ data.

Repack those projected rows as the consumer surface. Carrier exhaustion in the
three cited packets leaves no coordinate for quotient reals, solver
correctness, floating-point hardware semantics, host equality, or classical
completeness.
\end{proof}
