\begin{theorem}[DyadicApproximation standard finite-approximation bridge]\label{thm:dyadic-approximation-standard-finite-approximation-bridge}
The public finite-window export of
\autoref{thm:dyadicapproximation-public-finite-window-export}, together with
\autoref{thm:dyadic-approximation-real-seal-handoff},
\autoref{thm:dyadic-approximation-common-precision-refinement}, and
\autoref{thm:dyadic-approximation-classifier-transport}, determines a
conservative standard finite dyadic-approximation reading and readback.

The readback recovers exactly the displayed precision, dyadic endpoint,
$\StreamNameUp$ window, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, $\RealUp$ seal ledger,
displayed $\hsame$, displayed $\Cont$, package rows, and local $\NameCert$
evidence. It adds no quotient real, host real equality, completion point,
trichotomy row, selected representative, or classical approximation
completeness.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DyadicApproximationUp.DyadicApproximationCarrier\_standard\_finite\_approximation\_bridge}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet through
\autoref{thm:dyadicapproximation-public-finite-window-export}. The public
surface is the five-coordinate packet $(n,d,w,l,p)$: precision, dyadic
endpoint, finite observation window, seal ledger, and provenance. These rows
already include the displayed $\StreamNameUp$ window, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback,
$\RealUp$ seal boundary, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, package, and certificate data.

Apply \autoref{thm:dyadic-approximation-common-precision-refinement} to place
the standard finite-precision read and the BEDC readback on the same displayed
precision row. Then use
\autoref{thm:dyadic-approximation-classifier-transport} to carry the endpoint
classifier through the visible $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ data without leaving the
finite-window packet.

Finally route the seal through
\autoref{thm:dyadic-approximation-real-seal-handoff} and return to the public
export. The readback is therefore exactly the displayed precision, dyadic
endpoint, stream window, regular-sequence readback, seal ledger, transport,
continuation, package, and certificate surface, with no coordinate for
quotient reals, host equality, completion points, trichotomy, selected
representatives, or classical approximation completeness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicApproximation rational interval window handoff]\label{thm:dyadic-approximation-rational-interval-window-handoff}
Let an accepted $\DyadicApproximationUp$ packet expose its finite
approximation coordinates, and let an accepted $\RationalIntervalUp$ packet
expose a finite containment ledger recording the displayed dyadic endpoint at
the same unary precision. Then every $\RealUp$-facing consumer read factors
through the common finite surface: the precision row, dyadic endpoint, rational
endpoint, rational order and containment rows, $\StreamNameUp$ window,
$\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, visible $\hsame$, visible $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$,
and local $\NameCert$ provenance rows.

The handoff supplies no quotient real, selected limit, host real equality,
located subset, or interval-arithmetic closure. It only identifies the shared
finite endpoint window already carried by the two accepted packets and their
displayed containment ledger.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the dyadic packet through
\autoref{thm:dyadicapproximation-public-finite-window-export}. This gives the
precision row, dyadic endpoint, stream window, regular-sequence readback,
$\RealUp$ seal ledger, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, package, and local certificate rows
available to any public consumer.

Route the seal through
\autoref{thm:dyadic-approximation-real-seal-handoff}. The seal contributes no
completed-real coordinate; it is read through the same finite dyadic endpoint
window and the same $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback boundary.

Project the rational packet through
\autoref{thm:rational-interval-public-rational-window-handoff}. Its public
surface exposes the rational endpoint, rational order row, containment row,
finite window, transport, continuation, package, and certificate provenance.

Use the containment-ledger hypothesis to identify the rational endpoint window
with the displayed dyadic endpoint at the common unary precision. After this
identification, every listed consumer coordinate is one of the two carrier
coordinates already projected above.

The conclusion follows by carrier-coordinate exhaustion. Since neither carrier
has a row for quotient reals, selected limits, host real equality, located
subsets, or interval arithmetic, the handoff cannot export those objects.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicApproximation consumer completeness]\label{thm:dyadicapproximation-consumer-completeness}
Every finite consumer of a $\DyadicApproximationUp$ packet reads exactly the
dyadic precision index, rational interval window, approximation row,
classifier transport, precision weakening row, $\RealUp$ seal handoff,
displayed $\hsame$, displayed $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance, and local
$\NameCert$ evidence. Conversely, these rows repack as the complete finite
dyadic-approximation consumer surface.

The consumer surface exports no quotient real, host real equality, completion
point, trichotomy row, selected representative, located subset, or classical
approximation-completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DyadicApproximationUp.DyadicApproximationCarrier\_consumer\_completeness}
\begin{proof}
Project an accepted packet through the finite-window obligations of
\autoref{thm:dyadicapproximation-window-scope}. This exposes the precision
index, dyadic endpoint, finite observation window, regular-sequence readback,
visible transport, continuation, package, and local certificate rows.

Refine the consumer read to a common precision by
\autoref{thm:dyadic-approximation-common-precision-refinement}. Then use
\autoref{thm:dyadic-approximation-classifier-transport} for the classifier
route and \autoref{thm:dyadic-approximation-precision-weakening} for the
visible weakening row.

Route the seal through \autoref{thm:dyadicapproximation-real-seal-scope} and
\autoref{thm:dyadic-approximation-real-seal-handoff}. The seal contributes
only the displayed $\RealUp$ handoff row already bounded by the same finite
dyadic window.

Next collect the public packet surface with
\autoref{thm:dyadicapproximation-public-finite-window-export} and the standard
bridge of
\autoref{thm:dyadic-approximation-standard-finite-approximation-bridge}. The
rational endpoint part is the window handoff of
\autoref{thm:dyadic-approximation-rational-interval-window-handoff}, so every
finite consumer coordinate is one of the listed rows.

For the converse direction, start with precisely the listed rows. The public
finite-window export supplies the $\NameCert$ package, the standard bridge
packs the dyadic readback, and the rational interval handoff packs the shared
endpoint window, giving a valid finite approximation consumer read.

None of the cited packet projections contains a quotient-real coordinate,
host equality, completion point, trichotomy row, selected representative,
located subset, or classical completeness principle. These objects therefore
cannot be exported by the complete consumer surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicApproximation directed precision refinement associativity]\label{thm:dyadicapproximation-directed-precision-refinement-associativity}
Let three accepted $\DyadicApproximationUp$ packets carry compatible displayed
common unary precision refinements. Suppose the left-associated read first
refines the first two packets to their displayed common precision and then
refines that visible packet with the third, while the right-associated read
first refines the last two packets and then refines the first packet with that
visible packet. Then the two reads expose the same consumer-visible finite
packet: the transported precision row, dyadic endpoint, $\StreamNameUp$
window, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, $\RealUp$ seal ledger, visible $\hsame$,
visible $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ rows.

No quotient real, host real equality, completion point, selected
representative, trichotomy row, or choice principle is used to identify the
two associated reads.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:dyadic-approximation-common-precision-refinement} to the
left pair, obtaining the displayed common precision packet for the first two
accepted reads. Apply the same theorem to the right pair, obtaining the
displayed common precision packet for the last two accepted reads.

Apply \autoref{thm:dyadic-approximation-common-precision-refinement} again to
the left-associated packet and the third packet. Apply it once more to the
first packet and the right-associated packet. Compatibility of the two
displayed common unary precision refinements makes these two terminal
precision rows the same displayed row.

Use \autoref{thm:dyadic-approximation-classifier-transport} on the two
terminal refinements. The transported classifier rows identify the dyadic
endpoint, $\StreamNameUp$ window, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, $\RealUp$ seal
ledger, visible $\hsame$, visible $\Cont$, package provenance, and local
certificate rows carried by the two associated packets.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:dyadicapproximation-consumer-completeness}. Every
finite consumer coordinate is exhausted by the listed rows, and the converse
packing direction shows that these rows determine the same finite
dyadic-approximation consumer surface. Since the completeness theorem exports
no quotient-real or choice-selected coordinate, the associativity law is
entirely a finite displayed-packet statement.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicApproximation terminal refinement window]\label{thm:dyadicapproximation-terminal-refinement-window}
Let a finite family of accepted $\DyadicApproximationUp$ packets be equipped
with displayed common unary precision refinements, and suppose those displayed
refinements are mutually compatible whenever two refinement brackets overlap.
Then the iterated refinement surface has one terminal finite consumer window.
That window consists exactly of the terminal precision row, the dyadic endpoint,
the $\StreamNameUp$ window, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, the $\RealUp$ seal
ledger, the $\RationalIntervalUp$ containment row when that row is present, the
visible $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ transport routes, the $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance
row, and the local $\NameCert$ rows.

The terminal window is independent of binary bracketing of the finite
refinement family. It exports no quotient real, host equality, completion
point, trichotomy row, selected representative, located subset, or choice
principle.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DyadicApproximationUp.DyadicApproximationCarrier\_terminal\_refinement\_window}
\begin{proof}
Proceed by induction on the finite refinement family. In the singleton case,
the accepted packet projection supplies exactly the displayed precision row,
dyadic endpoint, $\StreamNameUp$ window, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, $\RealUp$
seal ledger, visible $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ routes, $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance,
and local $\NameCert$ rows. If the rational containment row is present, it is
the row supplied by
\autoref{thm:dyadic-approximation-rational-interval-window-handoff}.

For the binary step, assume terminal windows have already been constructed for
the two finite subfamilies. Apply
\autoref{thm:dyadic-approximation-common-precision-refinement} to their
displayed terminal packets. Compatibility of the displayed common precision
rows gives a single terminal precision row for the combined family.

Transport the classifier data along the common precision row by
\autoref{thm:dyadic-approximation-classifier-transport}. The transported rows
carry the dyadic endpoint, stream window, regular rational readback, real seal
ledger, visible $\hsame$ route, visible $\Cont$ route, package provenance, and
local certificate rows into the combined terminal packet.

Use
\autoref{thm:dyadicapproximation-directed-precision-refinement-associativity}
to compare two binary bracketings of the same finite family. The theorem
identifies the consumer-visible packet rows produced by the two bracketings, so
the terminal window is independent of the chosen binary refinement tree.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:dyadicapproximation-consumer-completeness}. It
shows that the listed coordinates exhaust the finite consumer surface, while
\autoref{thm:dyadic-approximation-rational-interval-window-handoff} accounts
for the optional rational-interval containment row. Since the involved packet
coordinates contain no quotient-real, host-equality, completion-point,
trichotomy, selected-representative, located-subset, or choice-principle field,
none of those objects can be exported by the terminal window.
\end{proof}
