\begin{definition}[DyadicApproximation terminal dyadic-net consumer surface]
\label{def:dyadicapproximation-terminal-dyadic-net-consumer-surface}
Let $T$ be an accepted terminal $\DyadicApproximationUp$ finite packet and let
$D$ be an accepted $\RealDiagonalCompletionUp$ packet matched to $T$ by the
displayed terminal diagonal handoff. The terminal dyadic-net consumer surface
of $(T,D)$ is the finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ surface consisting exactly of the
terminal precision row, the transported dyadic endpoint row, the
$\RationalIntervalUp$ cell, the $\DyadicMeshUp$ selector row, the
$\ValidatedNumericsUp$ enclosure row, the scheduled $\StreamNameUp$ window,
the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row, the $\RealUp$ seal row, the
$\RealDiagonalCompletionUp$ selector row, the visible $\hsame$ rows, the
visible $\Cont$ routes, the $\Pkg$ provenance rows, and the visible
$\NameCert_{\DyadicApproximationUp}$, $\NameCert_{\DyadicMeshUp}$,
$\NameCert_{\ValidatedNumericsUp}$, $\NameCert_{\RealUp}$, and
$\NameCert_{\RealDiagonalCompletionUp}$ rows.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicApproximation terminal dyadic-net consumption]
\label{thm:dyadicapproximation-terminal-dyadic-net-consumption}
Let $T$ be an accepted terminal $\DyadicApproximationUp$ finite packet and let
$D$ be an accepted $\RealDiagonalCompletionUp$ packet matched to $T$ by the
displayed terminal diagonal handoff. Suppose a terminal dyadic-net consumer
reads $T$ through any displayed route built from the terminal finite-packet
$\RealUp$ readback, the interval-mesh readback, the terminal diagonal
readback, and the $\StreamNameUp$ real-seal factorization rows.

Then the consumer factors through the terminal dyadic-net consumer surface of
\autoref{def:dyadicapproximation-terminal-dyadic-net-consumer-surface}. Its
observable coordinates are exactly the terminal precision, dyadic endpoint,
rational interval cell, $\DyadicMeshUp$ selector, $\ValidatedNumericsUp$
enclosure, $\StreamNameUp$ window, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, $\RealUp$ seal,
$\RealDiagonalCompletionUp$ selector, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and
$\NameCert$ rows listed there. The factorization exports no quotient streams,
selected host limits, solver correctness, metric completeness, or host real
equality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First normalize the terminal real readback. Apply
\autoref{thm:dyadicapproximation-terminal-finite-packet-real-readback} to the
accepted terminal packet $T$. This exposes the terminal precision, transported
endpoint, scheduled stream window, regular-rational readback, $\RealUp$ seal
ledger, transport rows, continuation routes, package provenance, and local
dyadic certificate row.

Next attach the interval net. Apply
\autoref{thm:dyadicapproximation-terminal-interval-mesh-readback-totality}.
The terminal read is total over the displayed rational interval cell, the
$\DyadicMeshUp$ selector, the $\ValidatedNumericsUp$ enclosure row, and the
same terminal finite-packet real boundary.

Now compose with the diagonal consumer. Apply
\autoref{thm:dyadicapproximation-terminal-diagonal-readback-composition} to
the matched pair $(T,D)$. The diagonal route contributes the
$\RealDiagonalCompletionUp$ selector and keeps the same precision, endpoint,
stream window, regular-rational readback, real seal, transports,
continuations, package rows, and local certificate rows.

Finally identify the stream seal source. Apply
\autoref{thm:dyadicapproximation-finite-packet-streamname-real-seal-factorization}.
It identifies the scheduled $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row,
$\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, dyadic endpoint rows, terminal precision row,
$\RealUp$ seal token, diagonal selector row, transport rows, continuation
rows, package provenance, and local dyadic certificate provenance as one
finite packet surface.

The four normal forms list the same terminal coordinates and no additional
coordinate. Repacking those coordinates gives exactly the surface of
\autoref{def:dyadicapproximation-terminal-dyadic-net-consumer-surface}. Since
the cited rows contain no quotient-stream representative, selected host
limit, solver-correctness proof, metric-completeness witness, or host
real-equality field, the resulting consumer surface exports none of those
data.
\end{proof}
