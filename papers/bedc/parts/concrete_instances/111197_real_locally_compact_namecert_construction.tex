Following Bishop--Bridges and Kelley local compactness, the $\RealLocallyCompactUp$ packet records the finite Real-facing route by which a displayed real endpoint receives a compact neighbourhood certificate. It refines the generic local compactness row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locallycompact-namecert} over the real uniformity surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-uniform-space-namecert}, with endpoint data from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, finite windows from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, regular rational readback from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, dyadic radius accounting from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, closed ball rows from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metricclosedball-namecert}, and compact metric witnesses from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}. The packet names the local closed-ball compactness surface for $\RealUp$; it does not supply a host-selected neighbourhood base, open-cover compactness theorem, quotient real equality, countable choice, or ambient completeness row.

\begin{definition}[Real locally compact carrier]
\label{def:real-locally-compact-carrier}
A $\RealLocallyCompactUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(R,U,W,D,B,K,A,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $R$ is the $\RealUp$ endpoint row whose local neighbourhood is inspected.
\item $U$ is the $\RealUniformSpaceUp$ entourage row attached to the same endpoint data.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row used to read endpoint names, entourage requests, and radius comparisons.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp/\RegSeqRatUp$ radius-accounting row, carrying dyadic radii and regular rational readback for the displayed window.
\item $B$ is the $\MetricClosedBallUp$ row centred at the displayed real endpoint and radius.
\item $K$ is the $\CompactMetricUp$ witness for the displayed closed-ball window.
\item $A$ is the $\LocallyCompactUp$ handoff row, read only after $R,U,W,D,B,K$ have been exposed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the endpoint, uniformity, window, radius, closed-ball, compactness, handoff, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a Real local-neighbourhood request through $R,U,W,D,B,K,A,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RealLocallyCompactUp$, $\RealUp$, $\RealUniformSpaceUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\MetricClosedBallUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\LocallyCompactUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RealLocallyCompactUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier compares accepted packets only through these displayed rows and exports no host neighbourhood selector, open-cover compactness, quotient real equality, countable choice, selected representative, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Real locally compact NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:real-locally-compact-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RealLocallyCompactUp$ carrier $L=(R,U,W,D,B,K,A,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\RealLocallyCompactUp}$ surface consists exactly of endpoint admission through $R$, real-uniform entourage exposure through $U$, finite-window routing through $W$, dyadic and regular-rational radius accounting through $D$, closed-ball exposure through $B$, compact metric witnessing through $K$, local compactness handoff through $A$, componentwise transport through $H$, replay through $C$, provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:real-locally-compact-carrier}. The endpoint and uniformity rows $R,U$ provide the Real-facing source before any radius or compactness data are read.

The radius route is finite. The window row $W$ selects the inspected names and tolerance requests, while $D$ records the dyadic radius ledger and regular rational readback needed to form the closed-ball row $B$.

Compactness enters only through $K$ for the displayed closed-ball window. The handoff row $A$ then presents the same data to the generic $\LocallyCompactUp$ surface, not to an ambient neighbourhood base or open-cover compactness theorem.

Transport, replay, provenance, and local naming preserve the same rows through $H,C,P,N$. Since the carrier has no coordinate for quotient real equality, countable choice, selected representatives, or ambient completeness, the listed rows exhaust the local certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real locally compact handoff]
\label{thm:real-locally-compact-handoff}
Every Real or Completion consumer that reads local compactness from an accepted $\RealLocallyCompactUp$ packet factors through
$$
\RealUp \longmapsto \RealUniformSpaceUp \longmapsto \StreamNameUp,\RegSeqRatUp,\DyadicRatCoreUp \longmapsto \MetricClosedBallUp \longmapsto \CompactMetricUp \longmapsto \LocallyCompactUp .
$$
The handoff exports only the displayed endpoint, uniformity, finite-window, radius-accounting, closed-ball, compact metric, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows of \autoref{def:real-locally-compact-carrier}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a consumer request for a compact neighbourhood of a displayed real endpoint. The request first reads $R$ and $U$, so the endpoint and real-uniformity data are fixed before any compactness row can be inspected.

Next read the finite radius route. The window row $W$ and accounting row $D$ expose the dyadic and regular-rational budget used to construct the closed-ball row $B$.

The compact witness $K$ is attached to that same closed ball. The handoff row $A$ is therefore a local compactness read over a displayed Real closed-ball window, not a host-selected neighbourhood or open-cover principle.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve the route for downstream Real and Completion consumers. No excluded equality, choice, selected representative, or ambient completeness row is available to the handoff.
\end{proof}
