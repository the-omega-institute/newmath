\section{CauchyCriterion Lattice Dependency}
\label{sec:cauchy-limit-seal-cauchycriterion-lattice-dependency}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyLimitSeal CauchyCriterion lattice dependency]
\label{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-cauchycriterion-lattice-dependency}
Every $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ consumer route that exposes a $\RealUp$ or completion-facing seal first consumes the $\CauchyCriterionUp$ budget-selector boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchycriterion-namecert}. The resulting route factors through the existing $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ route rows together with the finite selector boundary before any seal row is public, and it exports no quotient stream, selected host limit, host real equality, countable-choice selector, hidden tail oracle, or ambient completeness coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ root L10 sibling route. It places every Real or completion-facing consumer on the displayed $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$, and $\RealUp$ rows before the local seal is read.

Apply the $\CauchyCriterionUp$ finite selector boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchycriterion-namecert}. That boundary supplies the budget-selector row used by the same finite window and tolerance ledger, so the seal route cannot bypass the criterion packet on its way to a Real or completion-facing consumer.

The two route surfaces share only displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows. Therefore the combined consumer route exposes the finite criterion selector and the local CauchyLimitSeal packet, not any excluded quotient, host-limit, host-equality, choice, hidden-oracle, or ambient-completeness coordinate.
\end{proof}
