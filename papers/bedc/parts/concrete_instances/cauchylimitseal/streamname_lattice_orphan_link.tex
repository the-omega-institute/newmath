\begin{theorem}[CauchyLimitSeal StreamName lattice orphan link]
\label{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-streamname-lattice-orphan-link}
Every accepted $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ consumer route that uses the seal row
factors through the sibling $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window dependency of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert} and through the
finite-window lattice dependency of
\autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-finite-window-lattice-dependency} before any
$\RealUp$ seal read. The route remains the displayed finite
$X,S,D,G,E,T,P,N$ surface and does not introduce a quotient stream, selected
host limit, host real equality, hidden tail witness, or ambient completeness
coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the local StreamName dependency
\autoref{thm:streamname-cauchy-limit-seal-dependency}. It places every
sealed-limit read on the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window surface before the
seal row is consumed.

Next use the scheduled-window pullback
\autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-scheduled-window-pullback}. The same
finite-window surface is the common dependency for the scheduled window, the
regular source row, the dyadic ledger, and the downstream seal route.

Finally apply the finite-window lattice dependency
\autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-finite-window-lattice-dependency}. It routes
the consumer through the StreamName meet surface before the displayed
$\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$, and $\RealUp$
rows are read, and its non-escape clause rules out every hidden completion or
host-level substitute named in the statement.
\end{proof}
