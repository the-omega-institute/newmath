\section{CauchyLimitSeal finite-window lattice dependency}
\label{sec:cauchy-limit-seal-finite-window-lattice-dependency}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyLimitSeal finite-window lattice dependency]
\label{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-finite-window-lattice-dependency}
Let $L=(X,S,D,G,E,T,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ carrier. Every $\RealUp$ or completion consumer route that reads the seal row $E$ factors first through the StreamName finite-window lattice meet surface of \autoref{thm:streamname-finite-window-lattice-meet-exhaustion}, and then through the displayed $\RegSeqRatUp$ source row $X$, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger $D$, the $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ diagonal row $G$, and the $\RealUp$ seal row $E$. The route carries only $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ provenance, and it exports no quotient stream equality, host real equality, selected host limit, hidden tail witness, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier definition of \autoref{def:cauchy-limit-seal-carrier}. The rows $X,S,D,G,E,T,P,N$ are the only displayed coordinates available to a sealed consumer route.

Apply the finite-window factorization of \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-window-factorization}. Any read that reaches the seal must pass through a scheduled $\StreamNameUp$ window, its displayed $\RegSeqRatUp$ source row, the matching dyadic ledger, and the completion diagonal row before the seal row is consumed.

Next pull the route back by \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-scheduled-window-pullback}. This identifies the same scheduled window as the common dependency surface shared by the regular source, dyadic ledger, completion diagonal, and seal.

Use the StreamName lattice-meet exhaustion theorem \autoref{thm:streamname-finite-window-lattice-meet-exhaustion}. The scheduled window therefore lands on the shared finite-window meet surface, whose readable coordinates are only the selected rational classifier rows, probe-bundle rows, membership rows, unary-history rows, dyadic ledger rows, componentwise transports, continuation routes, package provenance, and local names.

Finally apply the $\RealUp$ consumer boundary of \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-realup-consumer-boundary}. The sealed route can carry only the visible $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ provenance over $X,D,G,E$, so quotient stream equality, host real equality, selected host limits, hidden tail witnesses, and ambient completeness remain outside the exported surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyLimitSeal RegSeqRat Real finite-window pullback]
\label{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-regseqrat-real-finite-window-pullback}
Let $L=(X,S,D,G,E,T,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ carrier. Suppose the source row $X$ is consumed as a $\RegSeqRatUp$ packet and the seal row $E$ is read by a $\RealUp$ consumer. Then the consumer route pulls back first through the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window lattice meet, then through the $\RegSeqRatUp$ source row $X$, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger $D$, the $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ diagonal row $G$, and the $\RealUp$ seal row $E$. The pulled-back route exposes only $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ provenance, and it exposes no quotient stream, selected limit, host real equality, or ambient completeness datum.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the finite-window lattice dependency of \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-finite-window-lattice-dependency}. It places every seal-facing read over the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window lattice meet before the route reaches the displayed source, ledger, diagonal, and seal rows.

Next use the scheduled-window pullback of \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-scheduled-window-pullback}. The scheduled window is the common dependency surface for the $\RegSeqRatUp$ source row $X$, the dyadic ledger $D$, the completion diagonal $G$, and the $\RealUp$ seal row $E$.

Apply the $\RealUp$ consumer boundary of \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-realup-consumer-boundary}. This fixes the endpoint read at the displayed seal row and permits only the visible $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ provenance to travel with the consumer route.

Finally apply the RegSeqRat seal package exclusion of \autoref{thm:regseqrat-real-seal-route-exclusion-package}. Since the regular source packet itself exports no quotient stream, selected limit, host real equality, or ambient completeness datum, the pulled-back CauchyLimitSeal route cannot acquire any such coordinate when the $\RealUp$ endpoint is consumed.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyLimitSeal middle-window meet determinacy]
\label{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-middle-window-meet-determinacy}
Let $L=(X,S,D,G,E,T,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ carrier. Suppose two $\RealUp$ or completion-facing consumer routes reach the same seal row $E$ through finite-window reads. After both routes are pulled back to the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window lattice meet, they use the same displayed $\RegSeqRatUp$ source row $X$, the same $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger $D$, the same $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ diagonal row $G$, and the same seal row $E$. Hence no second $\StreamNameUp$ window, quotient stream equality, selected host limit, hidden tail witness, or ambient completeness datum can determine the seal route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the finite-window lattice dependency of \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-finite-window-lattice-dependency}. It places every $\RealUp$ or completion-facing seal read over the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window lattice meet before the route reaches the displayed regular source, dyadic ledger, completion diagonal, and seal rows.

Apply the RegSeqRat Real finite-window pullback of \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-regseqrat-real-finite-window-pullback}. Each route that reaches $E$ is therefore pulled back through the same listed rows $X,D,G,E$, with only $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ provenance available to the consumer.

Now use StreamName lattice-meet exhaustion, \autoref{thm:streamname-finite-window-lattice-meet-exhaustion}. The two finite windows have a shared displayed meet surface, so any readable route data at the handoff is exhausted by the same finite-window coordinates rather than by a second stream window or a quotient-level stream equality.

Finally apply the RegSeqRat seal-route exclusion package of \autoref{thm:regseqrat-real-seal-route-exclusion-package}. It rules out selected host limits, hidden tail witnesses, and ambient completeness data from the regular-source seal route. The only remaining route through the shared meet is the displayed $X,D,G,E$ route, which gives determinacy of the middle finite-window handoff.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyLimitSeal common-window transport]
\label{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-common-window-transport}
Let $L=(X,S,D,G,E,T,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ carrier. Suppose two seal-facing consumer routes first meet the same $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window lattice surface before either route is consumed by a $\RegSeqRatUp$ or $\RealUp$ endpoint. Transporting either route to this common finite window preserves the displayed $\RegSeqRatUp$ source row $X$, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger $D$, the $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ diagonal row $G$, and the $\RealUp$ seal row $E$. The transported route exports only $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ provenance, and it exports no quotient stream equality, host real equality, selected host limit, hidden tail witness, or ambient completeness datum.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First use the middle-window meet determinacy of \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-middle-window-meet-determinacy}. Once both seal-facing routes are pulled back to the shared $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window lattice meet, the readable handoff is already fixed by the displayed rows $X,D,G,E$ rather than by a second window or a quotient-level stream coordinate.

Next apply the RegSeqRat finite-window transport obligation of \autoref{thm:regseqrat-finite-window-transport-obligation}. It identifies the transport task at the regular-source boundary as the preservation of the visible finite-window data over the same $\StreamNameUp$ surface.

Then apply the RegSeqRat finite-window transport closure of \autoref{thm:regseqrat-finite-window-transport-closure}. This closes the transport inside $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ provenance, so no quotient stream equality, host real equality, selected host limit, hidden tail witness, or ambient completeness coordinate is introduced by the move to the common window.

Finally use the CauchyLimitSeal RegSeqRat Real finite-window pullback of \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-regseqrat-real-finite-window-pullback}. The $\RealUp$ endpoint therefore consumes the same displayed boundary rows $X,D,G,E$ after transport, and the common finite window is reused for either seal-facing route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyLimitSeal Cauchy window transducer pullback]
\label{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-cauchy-window-transducer-pullback}
When a $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ consumer route is served through a $\CauchyWindowTransducerUp$ packet, the transducer rows pull back first to the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window lattice meet and then through the displayed $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback and $\RealUp$ seal rows. No quotient stream, selected host limit, hidden tail witness, or ambient completeness coordinate is introduced.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-common-window-transport}. It says that a seal-facing CauchyLimitSeal route transported to a common finite window preserves the displayed RegSeqRat source, dyadic ledger, completion diagonal, and Real seal rows while remaining inside $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ provenance.

Apply \autoref{thm:cauchy-window-transducer-regseq-handoff}. A Cauchy window transducer serving such a route can expose its RealUp seal row only after the displayed RegSeqRat readback row is available.

Finally use \autoref{thm:cauchy-window-transducer-real-seal-nonescape}. The transducer rows can replay the finite window, tolerance, step, readback, selector handoff, transport, continuation, provenance, and naming data, but no row supplies a quotient stream, selected host limit, hidden tail witness, or ambient completeness coordinate.
\end{proof}
