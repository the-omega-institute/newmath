\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CauchyLimitSealUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-cauchylimitseal-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ interface records the visible seal boundary between regular rational Cauchy data and the consumer-facing $\RealUp$ row. In the Bishop--Bridges constructive-analysis reading of Cauchy completion, a limit is presented by finite regular-name observations and an explicit schedule, not by quotienting streams or selecting a host limit. The chapter isolates that boundary as a concrete packet so later $\RealUp$ consumers can read only the displayed finite windows that pass through the seal.

The same seal boundary is consumed as the downstream finite route of the diagonal-limit construction in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-realcompleteness-namecert}. That consumer reads the StreamName, RegSeqRat, DyadicRatCore, and RealUp rows already named by the carrier, so the seal appears as a horizontal lattice edge between the finite completion route and the public real-facing endpoint.

\begin{definition}[CauchyLimitSeal carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-limit-seal-carrier}
A $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
L=(X,S,D,G,E,T,P,N)
$$
with the following visible coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is a $\RegSeqRatUp$ family row presenting the regular rational Cauchy source to be sealed;
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule that lists the finite windows a consumer may inspect;
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCore$ observation ledger attached to those scheduled windows;
\item $G$ is the $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ diagonal row that routes the regular family through its displayed completion packet;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row exposed to downstream real consumers;
\item $T$ is the finite collection of $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ transport rows connecting the source family, scheduled windows, diagonal row, observation ledger, and seal row;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance row naming the $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$, and $\RealUp$ dependency certificates;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyLimitSealUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The local classifier compares two carriers only by componentwise transport of these visible rows. It does not compare quotient representatives, host limits, or ambient completeness data.
\end{definition}
