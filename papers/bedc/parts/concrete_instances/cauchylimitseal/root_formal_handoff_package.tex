\begin{theorem}[CauchyLimitSeal root choice-free route]
\label{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-root-choice-free-route}
Every accepted $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ packet has a root choice-free route assembled from its carrier, its public route packet, and any admitted $\ChoiceFreeLimitScheduleUp$ or $\LimitSelectorUp$ selector surface. The route reads only the displayed $\RegSeqRatUp$ source row, $\StreamNameUp$ windows, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger, $\RealUp$ seal row, $\BHist$ surface, $\hsame$ transports, $\Cont$ replays, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ rows, and it exports no hidden selector, quotient stream equality, selected host limit, ambient completeness datum, or hidden choice principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the public route boundary of \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-public-route-boundary}. It identifies each accepted seal read with the displayed route packet over the regular source, stream windows, dyadic ledger, seal row, transports, continuations, provenance, and local name.

Attach selector admission by \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-choice-free-selector-admission}. A $\ChoiceFreeLimitScheduleUp$ row or $\LimitSelectorUp$ row is admitted only when its request, windows, dyadic ledger, regular handoff, seal, transport, route, provenance, and naming coordinates coincide with that public route packet.

Finally use selector non-escape from \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-choice-free-selector-nonescape}. Since the admitted selector surface already contains no hidden limit choice, quotient stream equality, selected host limit, ambient completeness datum, or hidden choice principle, the root route remains the displayed finite packet.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CauchyLimitSealUp.CauchyLimitSealCarrier\_root\_choice\_free\_route}

Use the budget root source totality theorem \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-budget-root-source-totality}. It says the composed budget read is sourced exactly by the displayed seal rows and displayed budget rows, so the budget cannot introduce a second source surface.

\begin{theorem}[CauchyLimitSeal root choice-free downstream coverage]
\label{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-root-choice-free-downstream-coverage}
Let
$$
L=(X,S,D,G,E,T,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ packet. Every accepted local downstream consumer admitted through the root choice-free route of $L$ is exhausted by the finite route rows already displayed in the root handoff package: either a $\ChoiceFreeLimitScheduleUp$ or $\LimitSelectorUp$ selector surface admitted through the same packet, a $\RealObservationBudgetUp$ seal read covered by the root budget route, or the common readback over $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows. Any proposed downstream coordinate outside these displayed alternatives, ledgers, and coverage rows remains outside the BEDC packet: no hidden selector, quotient stream equality, selected host limit, ambient completeness datum, alternate seal, hidden family limit, host real equality, or unlisted completion source is exported.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the root choice-free route of \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-root-choice-free-route}. It identifies the accepted root route of $L$ with the carrier, its public route packet, and any admitted $\ChoiceFreeLimitScheduleUp$ or $\LimitSelectorUp$ selector surface. The componentwise transport used in this identification is the public route transport of \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-public-route-transport}. The only selector alternatives available at that boundary are the displayed precision request, finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger, $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff, $\RealUp$ seal row, $\BHist$ surface, $\hsame$ transports, $\Cont$ replays, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ rows.

Next apply the root budget seal coverage theorem \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-root-budget-seal-coverage}. Every admitted $\RealObservationBudgetUp$ seal read is pulled back to the same regular handoff, finite window, dyadic observation ledger, seal row, transports, continuations, provenance, and local naming coordinates. Thus a budget-facing downstream consumer does not add a second source surface; it is one of the coverage rows already attached to the root route.

The two cited boundaries cover the local consumer cases named in the handoff file. Selector-facing consumers are covered by the choice-free route, budget-facing consumers are covered by the budget seal route, and the common readback is just the shared route over the listed $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows. Therefore an accepted local downstream use factors through the displayed finite alternatives, ledgers, or coverage rows.

The excluded coordinates are absent from both inputs. The choice-free route exports no hidden selector, quotient stream equality, selected host limit, ambient completeness datum, or hidden choice principle, while the budget coverage route exports no quotient stream equality, selected host limit, ambient completeness, alternate seal, or hidden family limit. Since every accepted downstream consumer has already been factored through one of those displayed rows, an unlisted selector, quotient equality, host limit, completeness datum, alternate seal, hidden family limit, host real equality, or external completion source has no BEDC packet coordinate from which it could be read.
\end{proof}
