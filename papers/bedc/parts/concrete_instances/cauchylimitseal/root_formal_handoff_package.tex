\begin{theorem}[CauchyLimitSeal root choice-free route]
\label{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-root-choice-free-route}
Every accepted $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ packet has a root choice-free route assembled from its carrier, its public route packet, and any admitted $\ChoiceFreeLimitScheduleUp$ or $\LimitSelectorUp$ selector surface. The route reads only the displayed $\RegSeqRatUp$ source row, $\StreamNameUp$ windows, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger, $\RealUp$ seal row, $\BHist$ surface, $\hsame$ transports, $\Cont$ replays, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ rows, and it exports no hidden selector, quotient stream equality, selected host limit, ambient completeness datum, or hidden choice principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the public route boundary of \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-public-route-boundary}. It identifies each accepted seal read with the displayed route packet over the regular source, stream windows, dyadic ledger, seal row, transports, continuations, provenance, and local name.

Attach selector admission by \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-choice-free-selector-admission}. A $\ChoiceFreeLimitScheduleUp$ row or $\LimitSelectorUp$ row is admitted only when its request, windows, dyadic ledger, regular handoff, seal, transport, route, provenance, and naming coordinates coincide with that public route packet.

Finally use selector non-escape from \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-choice-free-selector-nonescape}. Since the admitted selector surface already contains no hidden limit choice, quotient stream equality, selected host limit, ambient completeness datum, or hidden choice principle, the root route remains the displayed finite packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyLimitSeal root budget seal coverage]
\label{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-root-budget-seal-coverage}
The root route of an accepted $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ packet covers every $\RealObservationBudgetUp$ seal read admitted through the packet. The budget read factors through the same $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff, $\StreamNameUp$ window, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observation ledger, $\RealUp$ seal, $\BHist$ surface, $\hsame$ transports, $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ rows, with no quotient stream equality, selected host limit, ambient completeness, alternate seal, or hidden family limit.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CauchyLimitSealUp.CauchyLimitSealCarrier\_root\_budget\_seal\_coverage}
\begin{proof}
Begin with observation-budget pullback from \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-observation-budget-pullback}. It sends every accepted budget read into the public route packet of the accepted seal while preserving the precision request, finite windows, dyadic ledger, regular handoff, seal row, transports, continuations, provenance, and local naming rows.

Use the budget root source totality theorem \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-budget-root-source-totality}. It says the composed budget read is sourced exactly by the displayed seal rows and displayed budget rows, so the budget cannot introduce a second source surface.

Then apply dyadic-window exactness and completion-consumer non-escape from \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-root-dyadic-window-exactness} and \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-root-completion-consumer-nonescape}. These rows identify the budget ledger with the scheduled seal window and exclude quotient equality, selected host limits, ambient completeness, alternate seals, and hidden family limits.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyLimitSeal root formal handoff package]
\label{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-root-formal-handoff-package}
The root choice-free route of \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-root-choice-free-route}, the root budget seal coverage of \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-root-budget-seal-coverage}, the root-unblock package of \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-root-unblock-package}, and the completion-budget factorization of \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-completion-budget-factorization} form one formal handoff package for downstream $\RealUp$ and completion consumers. The package is the finite $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ route over $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$, and it excludes quotient stream equality, selected host limits, ambient completeness, hidden choice, hidden family limits, and host real equality.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CauchyLimitSealUp.CauchyLimitSealCarrier\_root\_formal\_handoff\_package}
\begin{proof}
First assemble the choice-free route. By \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-root-choice-free-route}, selector access is already confined to the accepted seal packet and its displayed route rows.

Next attach the budget coverage. By \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-root-budget-seal-coverage}, every observation-budget seal read factors through the same regular source, stream window, dyadic ledger, real seal, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming surface.

Now combine the two sides with \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-root-unblock-package} and \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-completion-budget-factorization}. These rows bind selector, budget, and completion reads to one finite route and keep the route on the same displayed consumer surface.

The excluded coordinates are absent from each ingredient and from the accepted carrier. Therefore the resulting handoff package gives downstream consumers the named finite rows without quotient stream equality, selected host limits, ambient completeness, hidden choice, hidden family limits, or host real equality.
\end{proof}
