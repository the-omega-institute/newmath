\begin{theorem}[CauchyLimitSeal budget root source totality]
\label{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-budget-root-source-totality}
Let
$$
L=(X,S,D,G,E,T,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ packet, and let
$$
B=(E_B,W_B,D_B,R_B,S_B,H_B,C_B,P_B,N_B)
$$
be an accepted $\RealObservationBudgetUp$ packet whose seal and regular-handoff rows are routed through $L$. Every $\RealObservationBudgetUp$-facing read accepted through this route is sourced exactly by the displayed seal rows $X,S,D,G,E,T,P,N$ together with the displayed budget rows $E_B,W_B,D_B,R_B,S_B,H_B,C_B,P_B,N_B$. No further budget source, hidden completion row, quotient stream, selected limit, host real equality, hidden family limit, or ambient completeness coordinate is available to the read.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the observation-budget pullback of \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-observation-budget-pullback}. It sends each budget read of $B$ into the public route packet of $L$ while preserving the precision request, window, dyadic ledger, regular handoff, seal row, transport, route, provenance, and naming rows displayed by $B$.

Next attach the $\RealObservationBudgetUp$ consumer package from \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-real-observation-budget-consumer-package}. The consumer endpoint reads the union of the budget rows and the seal rows over the shared $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ surface.

Finally use tail extraction from \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-tail-extraction} and the consumer exactness of \autoref{thm:real-observation-budget-consumer-exactness}. These rows force the tail and budget consumers to remain on the same displayed packet, so a read outside $X,S,D,G,E,T,P,N$ and $E_B,W_B,D_B,R_B,S_B,H_B,C_B,P_B,N_B$ has no source coordinate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyLimitSeal root dyadic window exactness]
\label{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-root-dyadic-window-exactness}
In the setting of \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-budget-root-source-totality}, the dyadic ledger read by any accepted budget route is exactly the scheduled $\StreamNameUp$ window ledger already exposed by the seal packet. The budget ledger $D_B$ and the seal-side dyadic row $D$ are not independent observations; they are the same finite $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger transported through the displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ rows of the route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First pull the budget route back along \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-observation-budget-pullback}. The pullback places the precision request, windows, and dyadic ledger of $B$ on the public route packet of $L$.

Then apply tail extraction from \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-tail-extraction}. The tail read of the seal packet is projected through the displayed $\RegSeqRatUp$ family, $\StreamNameUp$ schedule, and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observation ledger, with no external tail source.

The $\RealObservationBudgetUp$ consumer package of \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-real-observation-budget-consumer-package} and the exactness theorem \autoref{thm:real-observation-budget-consumer-exactness} identify the regular and real-facing budget consumers with that same packet. Hence the budget route reads precisely the scheduled window ledger already exposed by $L$.
\end{proof}

\label{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-budget-selector-coherence}

Next apply the tail-budget meet of \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-tail-budget-meet} together with budget root source totality from \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-budget-root-source-totality}. Finally apply completion-consumer non-escape from \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-root-completion-consumer-nonescape}. \begin{theorem}[CauchyLimitSeal root completion consumer non-escape]
\label{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-root-completion-consumer-nonescape}
The completion-facing consumer obtained by composing an accepted $\RealObservationBudgetUp$ route with an accepted $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ packet can export only the rows listed in \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-budget-root-source-totality} and the dyadic-window identification of \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-root-dyadic-window-exactness}. It cannot export quotient streams, selected limits, host real equality, hidden family limits, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-budget-root-source-totality} to identify the complete source surface of the composed consumer. The consumer has only the displayed seal rows and the displayed budget rows.

Use \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-root-dyadic-window-exactness} for the window ledger. The dyadic observation consumed by the route is the scheduled $\StreamNameUp$ window ledger already carried by the seal packet, not a second ledger chosen by the completion consumer.

Finally apply the consumer package of \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-real-observation-budget-consumer-package}, tail extraction from \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-tail-extraction}, and $\RealObservationBudgetUp$ consumer exactness from \autoref{thm:real-observation-budget-consumer-exactness}. The composed route remains inside the finite packet, so none of quotient streams, selected limits, host real equality, hidden family limits, or ambient completeness can be exported.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyLimitSeal root formal target package]
\label{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-root-formal-target-package}
The source totality row of \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-budget-root-source-totality}, the dyadic-window exactness row of \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-root-dyadic-window-exactness}, the budget-selector coherence row of \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-budget-selector-coherence}, and the completion-consumer non-escape row of \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-root-completion-consumer-nonescape} jointly form the formal target package for the composed $\RealObservationBudgetUp$-to-$\CauchyLimitSealUp$ route. Source totality fixes all source rows, dyadic-window exactness identifies the budget ledger with the scheduled $\StreamNameUp$ window, budget-selector coherence binds selector and budget reads to that one ledger, and completion-consumer non-escape excludes quotient streams, selected limits, host real equality, hidden family limits, and ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First use source totality. It fixes the composed route to the displayed seal rows $X,S,D,G,E,T,P,N$ and the displayed budget rows $E_B,W_B,D_B,R_B,S_B,H_B,C_B,P_B,N_B$, so every formal target read has a finite source coordinate.

Next use dyadic-window exactness. It identifies the budget ledger $D_B$ with the scheduled $\StreamNameUp$ window ledger already carried by the seal packet, so the route has one dyadic observation surface rather than two independent ledgers.

Then apply budget-selector coherence. The selector leg and the observation-budget leg meet on the same displayed route over $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$.

Finally use completion-consumer non-escape. Once the source rows, the dyadic ledger, and the selector-budget handoff have been fixed, the completion-facing consumer has no remaining coordinate for quotient streams, selected limits, host real equality, hidden family limits, or ambient completeness. These four rows therefore give the complete formal target package for the composed route.
\end{proof}
