\begin{theorem}[CauchyLimitSeal root L10 sibling route]
\label{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-root-l10-sibling-route}
Let $L=(X,S,D,G,E,T,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ carrier. Every accepted route from $L$ to a $\RealUp$ or completion-facing seal consumes, in order, the finite-window surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the seal boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The route exports no quotient stream, selected host limit, host real equality, hidden tail oracle, or ambient completeness row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the finite-window lattice dependency of \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-finite-window-lattice-dependency}. It places every Real or completion consumer route over the StreamName finite-window surface before the displayed regular source row, dyadic ledger, completion diagonal, and Real seal row are consumed.

Apply the RegSeqRat Real finite-window pullback of \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-regseqrat-real-finite-window-pullback}. The pulled-back route reads the same $\RegSeqRatUp$ source row $X$, the same $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger $D$, and the same $\RealUp$ seal row $E$ while carrying only $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ provenance.

Finally use the common-window transport of \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-common-window-transport}. Transport to the shared finite window preserves the displayed $X,D,G,E$ route and adds no quotient stream equality, host real equality, selected host limit, hidden tail witness, or ambient completeness coordinate. Hence the root route is exactly the sibling route through StreamName, RegSeqRat, DyadicRatCore, and RealUp.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyLimitSeal L10 sibling route totality]
\label{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-l10-sibling-route-totality}
Let $L=(X,S,D,G,E,T,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ carrier. Every $\RealUp$ or completion-facing consumer route for $L$ passes through the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window surface, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular readback, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, and the $\RealUp$ seal row before any local $\NameCert_{\CauchyLimitSealUp}$ read is public. A route missing any one of these four sibling surfaces is outside the accepted L10 sibling route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the finite-window lattice dependency of \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-finite-window-lattice-dependency}. It places the consumer over the StreamName window before the displayed source and seal rows are read.

Apply the RegSeqRat pullback of \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-regseqrat-real-finite-window-pullback}. The consumer must use the regular readback row $X$ and the same dyadic ledger $D$ when it reaches the Real-facing seal row $E$.

Finally use the root sibling route theorem \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-root-l10-sibling-route}. It identifies the complete route through StreamName, RegSeqRat, DyadicRatCore, and RealUp, so omitting one sibling surface leaves no accepted L10 route to the local NameCert row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyLimitSeal stream-RegSeq seal factorization]
\label{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-stream-regseq-seal-factorization}
For an accepted $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ carrier $L=(X,S,D,G,E,T,P,N)$, every L10 consumer route factors through the displayed $X,D,G,E$ rows together with the $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ provenance carried by $T,P,N$. \end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use route totality from \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-l10-sibling-route-totality} to place the consumer on the StreamName, RegSeqRat, DyadicRatCore, and RealUp sibling surface.

The RegSeqRat pullback then identifies the regular source with $X$ and the dyadic ledger with $D$. The finite-window lattice dependency identifies the completion diagonal and Real-facing seal with the displayed $G,E$ rows.

The common-window transport of \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-common-window-transport} carries this factorization through the same $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ provenance. \begin{theorem}[CauchyLimitSeal selector-seal compatibility]
\label{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-selector-seal-compatibility}

Then the selector-seal public route of $Q$ factors through the public route packet of $L$. The consumer reads one displayed L10 handoff
$$
\StreamNameUp \to \RegSeqRatUp \to \CauchyLimitSealUp \to \RealUp
$$
together with the shared finite-window, dyadic-ledger, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows. It exposes no second completion route, quotient stream, host real equality, hidden selector, or ambient completeness coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First fix the CauchyLimitSeal route. Next join the visible budget and tail reads. The budget-tail root join theorem, \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-budget-tail-root-join}, says that reads using the same finite window and the same dyadic ledger meet on one displayed L10 packet. Hence the shared $W,R,L',E'$ rows of $Q$ do not open an independent completion surface.

Finally identify the selector-seal route. The public route export theorem, \autoref{thm:real-completion-selector-seal-public-route-export}, gives the route $B \to W \to R \to L' \to E'$, while \autoref{thm:real-completion-selector-seal-l10-handoff-scope} places that route inside the same L10 Real and Completion handoff. Since $L'=L$ and $E'=E$, the selector-seal consumer factors through the CauchyLimitSeal packet fixed in the first step, and the excluded coordinates are absent from both displayed carrier surfaces.

\begin{lemma}[CauchyLimitSeal root L10 downstream coverage]
\label{lem:cauchy-limit-seal-root-l10-downstream-coverage}
Let $L=(X,S,D,G,E,T,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ carrier. Every accepted local consumer that reads the root L10 $\NameCert_{\CauchyLimitSealUp}$ route for a $\RealUp$ or completion-facing use is covered by the displayed downstream rows

$$

A candidate local read that asks instead for a quotient stream, selected host limit, host real equality, hidden tail oracle, second finite-window source, independent completion carrier, or ambient completeness coordinate is outside the accepted $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ packet.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Apply the L10 sibling route totality theorem, \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-l10-sibling-route-totality}. Before any local $\NameCert_{\CauchyLimitSealUp}$ read is public, the consumer route has already passed through the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window surface, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular readback, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, and the $\RealUp$ seal row. Therefore any read that omits one of those sibling surfaces has no accepted route to the local naming row.

Now apply the common-window transport theorem, \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-common-window-transport}, and then the root L10 sibling route theorem, \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-root-l10-sibling-route}. The transport theorem carries the total route to the shared finite-window surface while preserving the displayed $X,D,G,E$ rows and the visible $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support. The root theorem then identifies this transported total route with the root route through StreamName, RegSeqRat, DyadicRatCore, and RealUp, and it excludes quotient streams, selected host limits, host real equality, hidden tail oracles, and ambient completeness rows from the exported surface.

It remains only to identify the downstream rows available after the route is fixed. The stream-RegSeq seal factorization theorem, \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-stream-regseq-seal-factorization}, says that every L10 consumer factors through the displayed $X,D,G,E$ rows and the visible $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ provenance carried by $T,P,N$. The source-schedule lattice consumption theorem, \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-root-source-schedule-lattice-consumption}, adds the displayed schedule row $S$ and rules out a second schedule or independent stream source.

Finally use the no-unbudgeted-limit theorem, \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-no-unbudgeted-limit}. Since the factorized downstream route contains only the listed finite-window, regular-readback, ledger, diagonal, seal, transport, provenance, package, and naming rows, none of the excluded coordinates can be consumed as part of an accepted local $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ packet.
\end{proof}
