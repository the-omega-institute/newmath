\begin{theorem}[CauchyLimitSeal regular tail-selector sibling link]
\label{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-regular-tail-selector-sibling-link}
Every accepted $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ seal route whose selector and observation-budget reads use the transport surface of \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-selector-budget-transport} factors first through the accepted $\RegularCauchyTailSelectorUp$ packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regularcauchytailselector-namecert}. The route reaches the shared $\StreamNameUp$ window, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger, $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff, $\RealUp$ seal, and $\RealObservationBudgetUp$ meet only after the regular tail-selector packet has supplied its selected-tail witness and its finite support rows. Consequently the seal route exports no quotient stream identity, host real equality, choice-selected limit, hidden family limit, or ambient completeness coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the regular tail-selector packet. The consumer package of \autoref{thm:regular-cauchy-tail-selector-diagonal-consumer-package} supplies one finite selected-tail witness together with the requested precision, $\StreamNameUp$ window, $\RegSeqRatUp$ regularity row, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ comparison row, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ route, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ row.

Now attach the seal-side meet. By \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-tail-budget-meet}, the $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ route and the $\RealObservationBudgetUp$ route share one finite meet over the same regular handoff, window, dyadic ledger, Real seal, transport, continuation, provenance, and naming rows.

Finally use selector-budget transport from \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-selector-budget-transport}. It preserves the selector read, budget read, and completion-facing read along the same displayed route, so those reads cannot pass around the accepted $\RegularCauchyTailSelectorUp$ packet before consuming the shared window, ledger, handoff, seal, and meet rows.

The non-escape clauses of the cited selector package and meet route exclude quotient stream identity, host real equality, choice-selected limits, hidden family limits, and ambient completeness. The sibling link is therefore a finite factorization through the displayed regular tail-selector packet rather than an additional completion principle.
\end{proof}
