\begin{theorem}[CauchyLimitSeal StreamName dependency]
\label{thm:streamname-cauchy-limit-seal-dependency}
Every $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ consumer read of a sealed Cauchy limit factors through the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert} before the seal row is consumed. In the carrier notation
$$
L=(X,S,D,G,E,T,P,N),
$$
the read must pass through the scheduled window row $S$, its displayed $\RegSeqRatUp$ source row in $X$, the matching $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger in $D$, and the transport route in $T$ before any downstream consumer reads the $\RealUp$ seal row $E$. The route excludes quotient streams, host limits, selected external limits, and ambient completeness data.

The downstream $\RegularCauchyNameUp$ consumer of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regularcauchyname-namecert} reads the same scheduled $\StreamNameUp$ row, so $\CauchyLimitSealUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, and $\RegularCauchyNameUp$ share the finite window before the $\RealUp$ seal row is read.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-limit-seal-carrier}. The schedule row $S$ is the only row that lists finite windows, and the seal row $E$ has no independent consumer channel.

Apply window factorization from \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-window-factorization}. It routes every finite consumer request through the $\StreamNameUp$ window, the regular source row, the dyadic observation ledger, and the displayed transport rows before reaching the seal.

Finally use non-escape from \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-non-escape}. Since quotient streams, host limits, selected external limits, and ambient completeness do not occur as carrier coordinates, the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window surface is the required dependency child for every sealed-limit read.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyLimitSeal StreamName and RegularCauchyName lattice]
\label{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-streamname-regular-name-lattice}
For an accepted $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ carrier $L=(X,S,D,G,E,T,P,N)$, every sealed-limit consumer that is also read by $\RegularCauchyNameUp$ factors through the same scheduled $\StreamNameUp$ row $S$ before reaching the $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $X$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger $D$, diagonal row $G$, and $\RealUp$ seal $E$. The packet exports no second window schedule, quotient stream equality, selected host limit, or ambient completeness row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the StreamName dependency theorem \autoref{thm:streamname-cauchy-limit-seal-dependency}. It already places every sealed-limit read on the scheduled $\StreamNameUp$ window before the seal row is consumed.

Apply window factorization from \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-window-factorization}. The route from $S$ proceeds through the displayed $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $X$, the dyadic ledger $D$, the diagonal row $G$, and only then the $\RealUp$ seal row $E$.

Next use the scheduled-window pullback of \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-scheduled-window-pullback}. This identifies the scheduled window as the common dependency surface for the seal route and for the $\RegularCauchyNameUp$ consumer of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regularcauchyname-namecert}.

Since the carrier has no second schedule coordinate and no quotient, selected-limit, or ambient-completeness row, the shared lattice closes at the same finite window and cannot be rerouted through an external equality or host limit.
\end{proof}
