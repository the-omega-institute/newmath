\begin{theorem}[CauchyLimitSeal StreamName synchronizer pullback]
\label{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-streamname-synchronizer-pullback}
Every $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ seal read consumed through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-seal-budget-synchronizer-namecert} first factors through the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the displayed $\RegularCauchyNameUp$ route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regularcauchyname-namecert}, and the local $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ rows before the synchronizer budget, selector, shared-tail, compatibility, transport, route, provenance, and naming rows are read.

The route has no quotient stream equality, selected host limit, ambient completeness row, or direct $\RealUp$ seal edge bypassing the finite window.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the StreamName dependency theorem \autoref{thm:streamname-cauchy-limit-seal-dependency}. It places every sealed-limit read on the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window surface before the $\RealUp$ seal row is consumed.

Next apply the $\StreamNameUp$ and $\RegularCauchyNameUp$ lattice theorem \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-streamname-regular-name-lattice}. It identifies the displayed regular Cauchy name route as the same finite scheduled window surface used by the seal consumer.

Now read the synchronizer chapter \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-seal-budget-synchronizer-namecert}. Its consumer rows are the budget, selector, shared-tail, compatibility, transport, route, provenance, and naming rows, and each row is attached to the already displayed seal route rather than to an independent $\RealUp$ edge.

Coordinate exhaustion gives the claim. The only available route into the synchronizer is through the finite window, the displayed regular Cauchy name route, and the local $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ rows; quotient stream equality, selected host limits, ambient completeness, and a direct $\RealUp$ bypass do not occur among the displayed coordinates.
\end{proof}
