\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CountableObservationScheduleUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-countableobservationschedule-namecert}
\origin{ai}

The $\CountableObservationScheduleUp$ surface realizes the Type IV finite-record schedule socket of \autoref{sec:transcendental-supply-type-IV} as a finite $\BHist$ packet. It records observed prefix rows, request windows, StreamName-facing source rows, transport, continuation, provenance, and a local naming certificate. The packet organizes countable observation names before $\RealUp$-level consumers; it does not turn observed regularity into an unobserved future law.

\begin{definition}[CountableObservationSchedule carrier]
\label{def:countable-observation-schedule-carrier}
A $\CountableObservationScheduleUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(O,W,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $O$ is the observed prefix row, a finite list of already recorded observation entries indexed by their displayed finite positions;
\item $W$ is the request-window row, recording the finite windows in which a downstream consumer asks for the next named observation, comparison, or bounded continuation step;
\item $S$ is the StreamName source row, recording the finite source names by which the schedule is addressed without asserting that an unobserved tail is already present;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $O,W,S$, the continuation row, the package row, and the local naming row;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes that read prefix entries, expose request windows, hand off StreamName rows, and return only finite schedule data to downstream consumers;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, observed-prefix rows, request-window rows, StreamName source rows, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CountableObservationScheduleUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier is exactly this finite packet. It contains no completed countable stream, no proof that every future observation exists, no hidden causal-continuity principle, and no rule converting a successful prefix into a universal law.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CountableObservationSchedule NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:countable-observation-schedule-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:countable-observation-schedule-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\CountableObservationScheduleUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for
$$
Q=(O,W,S,H,C,P,N),
$$
prefix exposure, request-window locality, StreamName source accountability, componentwise $\hsame$ stability, displayed $\Cont$ routing, $\Pkg$ provenance, and non-escape for completed streams, unobserved observations, hidden persistence laws, and tail-totality claims.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:countable-observation-schedule-carrier}. The packet exposes only the seven displayed rows $O,W,S,H,C,P,N$.

Prefix exposure is read from $O$, request locality from $W$, and StreamName accountability from $S$. These rows state the finite material available to a consumer and the finite name by which the consumer addresses it.

Transport, continuation, and provenance are then confined by $H,C,P$. The local certificate $N$ can therefore export only the finite schedule data carried by those rows.

The non-escape clauses follow from the missing coordinates. Since $Q$ has no row for a completed countable stream, a future observation, or a persistence law, no NameCert projection can supply such an object.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CountableObservationSchedule prefix totality]
\label{thm:countable-observation-schedule-prefix-totality}
Every accepted $\CountableObservationScheduleUp$ packet is total on its displayed prefix: for each finite position listed in $O$, the packet contains exactly the observation row assigned to that position, together with the transport and continuation routes needed to read it as a schedule entry.

This totality is prefix-local. It does not assert that positions outside $O$ have observations, that every request window in $W$ is eventually met by new data, or that the prefix determines a unique future tail.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the observed prefix row $O$ from the carrier. By definition $O$ is a finite list of already recorded entries indexed by displayed finite positions.

Use $H$ to move the prefix row componentwise and $C$ to read each displayed entry as a schedule entry. These rows preserve the prefix entries without adding further positions.

The scope restriction is forced by the carrier inventory. Positions not listed in $O$ have no observation coordinate in $Q$, so prefix totality cannot be read as tail totality.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CountableObservationSchedule window stability]
\label{thm:countable-observation-schedule-window-stability}
For an accepted $\CountableObservationScheduleUp$ packet, each request window recorded in $W$ remains the same finite request window under the componentwise $\hsame$ transports recorded in $H$. A transported consumer may ask for the same displayed finite prefix segment, next-name slot, or bounded continuation step, but it may not extend the window by transport alone.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project $W$ and $H$ from the carrier. The row $W$ records the finite request windows, and $H$ records componentwise transport for that row.

Replay the consumer route through $C$. The route exposes the transported request as the same finite window data, with provenance tracked by $P$ and local naming tracked by $N$.

No window extension follows from transport. The carrier has no coordinate for enlarging a request window or for certifying an unobserved tail, so a transported window remains finite and displayed.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CountableObservationSchedule StreamName handoff]
\label{thm:countable-observation-schedule-streamname-handoff}
For an accepted $\CountableObservationScheduleUp$ packet, the StreamName source row $S$ is the only handoff surface by which downstream countable-name consumers address the schedule. The handoff consists of the displayed source name, the currently recorded prefix row, the finite request window, the transport row, the continuation route, the package provenance, and the local $\NameCert_{\CountableObservationScheduleUp}$ row.

No consumer may replace the StreamName handoff with an unnamed countable choice function, an implicit infinite stream, or an empirical law asserting persistence beyond the displayed record.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the StreamName row $S$ together with $O,W,H,C,P,N$. These are the only handoff-facing rows in the carrier.

The downstream consumer reads the name through $C$, transports the displayed rows through $H$, and records provenance through $P$. The local certificate $N$ binds this finite handoff as the public schedule surface.

The excluded replacements have no carrier coordinate. An unnamed choice function, a completed infinite stream, and a persistence law would each add data beyond $O,W,S,H,C,P,N$, so they cannot be exported by the handoff.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CountableObservationSchedule non-escape]
\label{thm:countable-observation-schedule-nonescape}
The $\CountableObservationScheduleUp$ packet does not discharge the Type IV causal-continuity socket. It may certify finite prefix rows, finite request windows, StreamName handoff, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming, but it cannot certify that observed regularity persists into not-yet-observed history.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier of \autoref{def:countable-observation-schedule-carrier} lists only finite schedule rows. Its prefix, window, source, transport, continuation, provenance, and naming data are all explicit coordinates of $Q$.

The Type IV socket of \autoref{def:transcendental-supply-type-IV} asks for persistence from an observed record to unobserved history. That requested content is not among the coordinates of $Q$.

Therefore the packet can serve finite schedule consumers without closing the causal-continuity socket. Any claim of future persistence must remain outside the packet or be marked by the Type IV audit gate.
\end{proof}
