The $\MetacicDecidabilityWitnessUp$ packet realizes the decidability audit map of \autoref{ch:metacic-decidable-audit-map} as a finite NameCert witness. It sits beside the normal false exclusion packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-normalfalseexclusion-namecert} and the confluence normal-fragment packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacicconfluencenormalfragment-namecert}: those siblings keep normal-form and confluence boundaries visible, while this packet separates typing-checker exactness from bounded beta-conversion evidence.

\begin{definition}[MetaCIC decidability witness carrier]
\label{def:metacic-decidability-witness-carrier}
A $\MetacicDecidabilityWitnessUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
W=(T,S,B,F,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $T$ is the $\mathsf{inferTypeCtx}$ exactness row, containing the soundness and completeness surfaces for the encoded $\mathsf{HasType}$ relation;
\item $S$ is the $\mathsf{checkSameTerm}$ structural equality row used by the typing checker;
\item $B$ is the bounded beta-comparison row for $\mathsf{normalizeBounded}$ and $\mathsf{decideClosedBetaConv}$;
\item $F$ is the finished-normal-form side-condition row consumed when a bounded comparison result is read as equality or beta-convertibility of named normal forms;
\item $R$ records refusal of a standalone $\mathsf{ClosedAt}$ decision theorem, unrestricted closed beta-conversion decidability, and fuel-erased normalisation;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $T,S,B,F,R,P,N$;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ routes for typing exactness, same-term checking, bounded comparison, finished-normal side conditions, and refusal reads;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance row over the decidability audit map and its normal-form and confluence siblings;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MetacicDecidabilityWitnessUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The local classifier compares accepted carriers only through the rows $T,S,B,F,R,H,C,P,N$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC decidability witness NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:metacic-decidability-witness-namecert-obligations}
Every accepted $\MetacicDecidabilityWitnessUp$ packet binds exactly the finite NameCert obligation surface of \autoref{def:metacic-decidability-witness-carrier}: typing-checker exactness through $T$, same-term structural equality through $S$, bounded beta comparison through $B$, finished-normal side conditions through $F$, refusal boundaries through $R$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, package provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier. The positive checker rows are $T$ and $S$: typing exactness is read through the inferTypeCtx row, while structural equality is read through the same-term checker row used by that typing surface.

The bounded conversion rows are $B$ and $F$. The comparison row exposes only bounded normalizer output comparison, and the finished-normal side-condition row supplies the additional hypotheses under which the audit map permits equality or beta-convertibility reads for named normal forms.

The refusal row $R$ remains part of the same packet. It records that no coordinate exports a standalone closedness decider, an unrestricted closed beta-conversion decider, or fuel-erased normalisation.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, cite, and name only those displayed rows. Since the carrier has no further coordinate, the local NameCert surface is exhausted by the finite obligations listed above.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC decidability witness bounded conversion non-escape]
\label{thm:metacic-decidability-witness-bounded-conversion-nonescape}
Any consumer of an accepted $\MetacicDecidabilityWitnessUp$ packet may read conversion evidence only through $B$ together with $F$. The read can expose bounded-normal output equality and the finished-normal consequences named by the audit map, but it cannot export a global closed beta-conversion decision procedure, a fuel-erased normalisation theorem, a quotient equality over represented terms, or a host evaluator.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the conversion coordinates. The carrier separates the bounded comparison row $B$ from the finished-normal side-condition row $F$, so a consumer route that asks for conversion evidence must pass through both rows.

The refusal row $R$ records the missing endpoints. It prevents the bounded comparison route from being reread as a global closed beta-conversion decider, fuel-erased normalisation theorem, quotient equality, or host evaluator.

Transport and replay do not add data. The rows $H$ and $C$ preserve the same displayed comparison and side-condition routes, while $P$ and $N$ only record provenance and local naming. Thus the conversion read stays inside the bounded witness packet.
\end{proof}
