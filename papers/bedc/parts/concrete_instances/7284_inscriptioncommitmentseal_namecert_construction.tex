\label{thm:inscription-commitment-seal-consumer-ledger-split}

The two failures are distinct: $M$ is the record admitted for the consumer, and $G$ is the public residue ledger attached to that admission.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

If $M$ is absent, the packet no longer exposes an accepted named commitment for the consumer. If $G$ is absent, the packet may still name a commitment row, but it loses the public ledger boundary that prevents consumer escape beyond the displayed residue.
