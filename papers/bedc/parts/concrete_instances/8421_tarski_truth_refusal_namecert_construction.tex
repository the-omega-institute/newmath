The $\TarskiTruthRefusalUp$ packet realizes the Tarski side of the reflection-limit vision of \autoref{ch:visions-reflection-and-limits} as finite naming data. It consumes the truth-total obstruction of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-truthtotalreflection-namecert} and the structural boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-reflectionlimitboundary-namecert}, but its carrier is narrower: it records the refusal row that separates $\mathrm{CIC}^{*}$ sentence codes from any BEDC-internal truth predicate.

\begin{definition}[Tarski truth-refusal carrier]
\label{def:tarski-truth-refusal-carrier}
A $\TarskiTruthRefusalUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
T=(S,Q,R,D,L,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the $\mathrm{CIC}^{*}$ sentence-code row, recording the finite coded sentence surface being read;
\item $Q$ is the attempted truth-read query row, recording the request for an internal classifier deciding represented sentence truth;
\item $R$ is the truth-read refusal row, recording that the requested predicate is not a carrier coordinate;
\item $D$ is the diagonal pressure row, recording the self-application shape that would turn $Q$ into the Tarski obstruction;
\item $L$ is the boundary ledger separating admitted structural sentence-code reads from refused truth-predicate reads;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for sentence codes, truth-read queries, refusals, diagonal rows, ledgers, routes, provenance, and local names;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which a consumer may inspect sentence codes, replay the query, read the refusal, and return to the boundary ledger;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, $\mathrm{CIC}^{*}$ sentence-code rows, truth-read queries, Tarski diagonal rows, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\TarskiTruthRefusalUp}$ ledger.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no BEDC truth predicate, no truth-value classifier for all $\mathrm{CIC}^{*}$ sentence codes, no runtime validator, and no route converting the structural self-description of BEDC into a truth predicate for that description.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Tarski truth-refusal NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:tarski-truth-refusal-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\TarskiTruthRefusalUp}$ surface for \autoref{def:tarski-truth-refusal-carrier} consists of carrier admission for $T=(S,Q,R,D,L,H,C,P,N)$, sentence-code exposure through $S$, attempted truth-read exposure through $Q$, refusal exactness through $R$, diagonal-pressure accounting through $D$, boundary-ledger exactness through $L$, componentwise transport through $H$, displayed continuation through $C$, provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:tarski-truth-refusal-carrier}. The packet lists the sentence-code, query, refusal, diagonal, ledger, transport, route, provenance, and naming rows as the complete public surface.

The sentence-code row $S$ is read structurally. It may expose the same kind of coded syntax that appears in the reflection-limit chapter, but it does not decide the truth of the represented sentence.

The query and refusal rows $Q,R$ record the Tarski boundary. A consumer may ask for an internal truth predicate, and the packet answers only by displaying the refusal row together with the diagonal pressure row $D$ and the ledger $L$.

The remaining rows $H,C,P,N$ only transport, replay, package, and name the same finite surface. Since none of them contains a truth predicate coordinate, the local NameCert obligations are exhausted by the displayed packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Tarski truth-refusal non-escape]
\label{thm:tarski-truth-refusal-nonescape}
Every accepted $\TarskiTruthRefusalUp$ packet exports $\mathrm{CIC}^{*}$ sentence-code information only through
$$
S,Q,R,D,L,H,C,P,N.
$$
Any consumer request for a BEDC-internal truth predicate factors through the truth-read refusal row $R$ and the boundary ledger $L$. No projection from the packet yields a predicate classifying every represented sentence code by truth value.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier as $T=(S,Q,R,D,L,H,C,P,N)$. The carrier definition identifies $R$ as the only row whose role answers the truth-read query, and it answers by refusal.

The structural read of $S$ is therefore separated from the attempted truth read $Q$. The diagonal row $D$ records why accepting $Q$ as a truth predicate would cross the Tarski boundary, while $L$ records that the accepted consumer surface stops at the refusal.

The transport, route, provenance, and local naming rows preserve this same separation. They can replay the sentence-code read and the refusal read, but they add no classifier that decides all represented sentence codes.
\end{proof}
