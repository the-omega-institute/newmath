Following Kelley on uniform spaces and Bishop--Bridges constructive completion, the $\HausdorffUniformCompletionUp$ packet records the separated uniform-completion boundary required when a completion route must expose both Cauchy-filter data and Hausdorff uniqueness data as finite rows. It sits between the uniform completion carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformcompletion-namecert}, the separated completion seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-hausdorffcompletion-namecert}, the Cauchy-filter source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyfilter-namecert}, the Cauchy-net witness surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchynet-namecert}, and the separated uniform-space packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separated-uniform-space-namecert}. The packet names the point at which the L10 completion route may read separatedness explicitly rather than treating it as implicit in uniform completion.

\begin{definition}[Hausdorff uniform completion carrier]
\label{def:hausdorff-uniform-completion-carrier}
A $\HausdorffUniformCompletionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(F,D,U,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $F$ is the $\CauchyFilterUp$ source row fixing the completion-facing filter request.
\item $D$ is the $\CauchyNetUp$ directed witness row extracted from the same request.
\item $U$ is the $\UniformCompletionUp$ universal-extension row, read only after $F$ and $D$ have displayed the completion source.
\item $S$ is the Hausdorff and separated classifier row, read through $\HausdorffCompletionUp$ and the separated uniform-space boundary, which compares two completion reads without introducing a quotient representative.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $F,D,U,S,C,P,N$.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from completion consumers through the same finite route.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\HausdorffUniformCompletionUp$, $\CauchyFilterUp$, $\CauchyNetUp$, $\UniformCompletionUp$, $\HausdorffCompletionUp$, $\SeparatedUniformSpaceUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\HausdorffUniformCompletionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate asserts exactly that these rows form one separated uniform-completion packet. It contains no coordinate for a quotient completion object, selected representative, host equality of completed points, countable choice, or ambient complete uniform space.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Hausdorff uniform completion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:hausdorff-uniform-completion-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\HausdorffUniformCompletionUp$ carrier $K=(F,D,U,S,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\HausdorffUniformCompletionUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following five obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed Cauchy-filter row, Cauchy-net row, uniform-completion universal row, separated classifier row, transport row, replay row, provenance row, and local naming row;
\item filter-net exactness, requiring every completion-facing read to pass through $F$ and then through the finite directed witness row $D$ before $U$ is available;
\item separated classifier stability, requiring $S$ to compare completed reads only through the displayed $\HausdorffCompletionUp$ and separated uniform-space rows;
\item transport and replay stability, requiring $H$ and $C$ to preserve the same row order under componentwise sameness and displayed continuation replay;
\item ledger non-escape, requiring $P$ and $N$ to name no quotient completion, selected representative, host equality, countable-choice schedule, or ambient complete uniform-space coordinate.
\end{enumerate}
No separated uniform-completion obligation is stored outside these finite rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:hausdorff-uniform-completion-carrier}. The projected coordinates are exactly the filter row $F$, net row $D$, universal row $U$, separated classifier row $S$, and structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

Filter-net exactness reads the same finite route as \autoref{thm:uniform-completion-filter-net-obligations}. The filter row fixes the request, and the directed-net row supplies the finite witness before any universal-extension comparison is visible.

Separated classifier stability is the role of $S$. The comparison can cite the Hausdorff completion boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-hausdorffcompletion-namecert} and the separated uniform-space packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separated-uniform-space-namecert}, but it receives no quotient class or selected representative.

The transport and replay rows $H$ and $C$ preserve that same route. Since $P$ and $N$ package and name only the displayed packet, every excluded object is outside the carrier and outside the continuation ledger.
\end{proof}
