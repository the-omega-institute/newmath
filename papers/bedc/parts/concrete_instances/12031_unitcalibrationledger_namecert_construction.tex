\begin{definition}[Unit calibration ledger carrier]
\label{def:unit-calibration-ledger-carrier}
A $\UnitCalibrationLedgerUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
V=(M,U,C,E,I,R,D,K,H,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the measurement-record row, naming the finite observation, numerical readout, sampling context, and protocol by which the value was obtained.
\item $U$ is the unit-bridge row, recording the source unit, target standard unit, conversion route, and local unit certificate read through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-unit-namecert}.
\item $C$ is the calibration row, naming the calibration source, calibration state, reference route, and validity scope for the measurement instrument.
\item $E$ is the uncertainty row, recording error bars, tolerance, resolution, rounding discipline, and any admitted confidence or interval convention.
\item $I$ is the instrument-ledger row, naming the device, sensor, apparatus, or procedure whose output supplies $M$.
\item $R$ is the reproducibility-ledger row, recording repeated runs, independent checks, or declared replication boundary for the same empirical value.
\item $D$ is the dimension row, recording the physical dimension and the bridge between dimension-bearing syntax and the unit bridge $U$.
\item $K$ is the classifier row comparing empirical-value packets componentwise across $M,U,C,E,I,R,D$ and rejecting value-only equality.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for measurement, unit, calibration, uncertainty, instrument, reproducibility, dimension, classifier, provenance, and local-name rows.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\UnitCalibrationLedgerUp$, $\LawCertificateUp$, $\PhysicalModelAuditUp$, $\CertifiedPhysicalTruthUp$, $\UnitUp$, $\RealityConstrainedModelErrorBudgetUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\UnitCalibrationLedgerUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a bare real number, detached constant symbol, unit-free magnitude, uncalibrated measurement, uncertainty-free export, anonymous instrument, unreproducible read, or dimensionless empirical shortcut outside the displayed packet.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Unit calibration ledger classifier]

\begin{theorem}[Unit calibration ledger NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:unit-calibration-ledger-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:unit-calibration-ledger-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\UnitCalibrationLedgerUp}$ surface consists exactly of five empirical ledger obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item measurement admission through $M$, requiring the numerical readout to be tied to a finite observation record and protocol;
\item unit and dimension admission through $U$ and $D$, requiring every numerical read to carry its unit bridge and dimension row;
\item calibration admission through $C$, requiring the measuring route to expose its calibration source and validity scope;
\item uncertainty admission through $E$, requiring the value to expose its tolerance, resolution, or interval convention;
\item instrument and reproducibility admission through $I$ and $R$, requiring both the generating apparatus and the replication boundary to be part of the same packet.
\end{enumerate}
The structural rows $K,H,P,N$ compare, transport, source, and locally name those five obligations, but they do not add another empirical-value coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:unit-calibration-ledger-carrier}. The packet lists the measurement record, unit bridge, calibration row, uncertainty row, instrument ledger, reproducibility ledger, dimension row, classifier, transport, provenance, and local name as a single finite surface.

The five obligation rows group these displayed coordinates by empirical function. Measurement admission comes from $M$; unit and dimension admission come from $U$ and $D$; calibration admission comes from $C$; uncertainty admission comes from $E$; instrument and reproducibility admission come from $I$ and $R$.

The classifier and structural rows preserve only the same displayed packet. Since the carrier has no coordinate for a detached numeral, hidden unit convention, omitted calibration, suppressed uncertainty, anonymous instrument, or unrecorded replication boundary, the five obligations exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Unit calibration ledger numeric-value nonescape]
\label{thm:unit-calibration-ledger-numeric-value-nonescape}
An accepted $\UnitCalibrationLedgerUp$ packet cannot export a numerical empirical value unless the unit bridge $U$, calibration row $C$, uncertainty row $E$, instrument ledger $I$, and reproducibility ledger $R$ are present in the same finite carrier. Dropping any one of those rows leaves the accepted empirical-value surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the carrier as $V=(M,U,C,E,I,R,D,K,H,P,N)$. The measurement row $M$ records a finite numerical readout, but the packet treats that readout as empirical only after the unit bridge, calibration row, uncertainty row, instrument ledger, and reproducibility ledger are displayed beside it.

Apply the obligation surface of \autoref{thm:unit-calibration-ledger-namecert-obligations}. Unit and dimension admission require $U$ and $D$, calibration admission requires $C$, uncertainty admission requires $E$, and instrument-reproducibility admission requires $I$ and $R$.

Finally use the classifier of \autoref{def:unit-calibration-ledger-classifier}. It compares accepted packets componentwise and does not identify two packets by their printed numerals alone. Therefore a value export lacking $U$, $C$, $E$, $I$, or $R$ is outside the local $\NameCert_{\UnitCalibrationLedgerUp}$ carrier.
\end{proof}
