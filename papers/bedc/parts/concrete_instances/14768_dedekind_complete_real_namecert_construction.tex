\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\DedekindCompleteRealUp$}

Following Bishop--Bridges constructive analysis, the $\DedekindCompleteRealUp$ packet records the finite interface by which a located, inhabited, bounded $\RealUp$ family may expose a displayed least-upper-bound witness. It reads the terminal real-name surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the upper-bound and lower-approximation discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedsupremum-namecert}, the representation boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dedekind-cauchy-comparison-namecert}, and the sequence-limit ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-sequence-limit-laws-namecert}. The packet is a located-supremum completion interface for finite BEDC consumers; it is not a Dedekind-cut construction of $\RealUp$, not a quotient-real construction, not a trichotomy principle, and not a general completeness theorem for arbitrary real subsets.

\begin{definition}[DedekindCompleteReal source packet]
\label{def:dedekind-complete-real-namecert-source}
A $\DedekindCompleteRealUp$ source packet is a finite $\BHist$ tuple
$$
D=(F,I,B,W,S,Q,L,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $F$ is the located $\RealUp$ family row whose members are readable only through finite real-name observations;
\item $I$ is the inhabitedness ledger, displaying one accepted member witness for the family before any supremum read is requested;
\item $B$ is the boundedness ledger, displaying an upper-bound witness and the comparison rows that place every inspected family observation below that bound;
\item $W$ is the finite precision-window row that lists the exact family observations, rational tolerances, and comparison budgets currently in scope;
\item $S$ is the located-supremum witness row, displaying an upper-bound face and a lower-approximation face for every precision listed in $W$;
\item $Q$ is the $\DedekindCauchyComparisonUp$ row that compares any cut-shaped observation used by $S$ with the regular Cauchy readback accepted by $\RealUp$;
\item $L$ is the $\RealSequenceLimitLawsUp$ row that records the finite limit-law checks used when $S$ is read through sequence approximants;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row for the displayed supremum witness, reached only after $F,I,B,W,S,Q,L$ have been exposed;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the family, inhabitedness ledger, boundedness ledger, precision window, supremum witness, comparison row, sequence-limit row, real seal, continuation routes, provenance, and local naming row;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes that replay family reads, boundedness checks, lower-approximation reads, comparison handoff, sequence-limit handoff, and real-seal readback;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\DedekindCompleteRealUp$, $\RealUp$, $\LocatedSupremumUp$, $\DedekindCauchyComparisonUp$, $\RealSequenceLimitLawsUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DedekindCompleteRealUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The accepted packet contains no quotient-real coordinate, no trichotomy oracle, no host real equality test, no countable-choice schedule, no arbitrary subset classifier, and no ambient completeness principle beyond the displayed located, inhabited, bounded family interface.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[DedekindCompleteReal NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:dedekind-complete-real-namecert-obligation-surface}
For an accepted packet $D=(F,I,B,W,S,Q,L,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\DedekindCompleteRealUp}$ obligations are exactly the following finite rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission: the accepted object is the displayed tuple with located family data, inhabitedness, boundedness, precision windows, a located-supremum witness, comparison handoff, sequence-limit handoff, a real seal, transports, continuations, provenance, and local naming evidence;
\item inhabited bounded family stability: replacing $F,I,B,W$ along the componentwise transports in $H$ preserves the same inhabitedness witness and the same inspected upper-bound ledger;
\item located supremum witness: every precision request listed in $W$ receives both an upper-bound read and a lower-approximation read from $S$, with no appeal to an unbounded family or a hidden subset classifier;
\item representation handoff: any cut-shaped observation used by $S$ must pass through the comparison row $Q$ before reaching the real-seal row $E$;
\item sequence-limit handoff: any sequence-shaped approximation used by $S$ must pass through the finite limit-law row $L$ before reaching $E$;
\item classifier transport: the transports in $H$ and the replay routes in $C$ move only the displayed rows $F,I,B,W,S,Q,L,E,P,N$;
\item ledger exhaustion: every admissible downstream consumer must read the packet through one of the displayed rows before obtaining a real-facing supremum observation.
\end{enumerate}
No local obligation may invoke quotient reals, trichotomy, host real equality, countable choice, an arbitrary subset classifier, or a general completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier row by row. Carrier admission is immediate from the tuple $D$, while inhabited bounded family stability reads only $F,I,B,W$ and the corresponding transports in $H$.

For the supremum witness, inspect a requested precision in $W$. The row $S$ supplies the upper-bound face and the lower-approximation face for that precision, and the carrier contains no coordinate from which an unbounded-family argument or hidden subset classifier could be read.

The two handoff obligations are then forced by the route inventory. Cut-shaped observations pass through $Q$, sequence-shaped approximations pass through $L$, and both routes reach the real-facing surface only at $E$.

Classifier transport and ledger exhaustion are structural. The continuation row $C$ can replay only the displayed routes, and the provenance row $P$ names exactly the sibling BEDC surfaces available to the packet. Since quotient reals, trichotomy, host real equality, countable choice, arbitrary subset classification, and general completeness are absent from the carrier, they cannot enter the local $\NameCert$ surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DedekindCompleteReal located-supremum handoff]
\label{thm:dedekind-complete-real-located-supremum-handoff}
Every $\RealUp$-facing observation exported by an accepted $\DedekindCompleteRealUp$ packet factors through the displayed located, inhabited, bounded family rows $F,I,B,W$, the located-supremum witness row $S$, the $\DedekindCauchyComparisonUp$ handoff row $Q$, the $\RealSequenceLimitLawsUp$ handoff row $L$, and the terminal real-seal row $E$. The export supplies a finite least-upper-bound witness for the named family window only, and it supplies no Dedekind-cut construction of $\RealUp$, quotient real, trichotomy principle, host real equality, countable-choice schedule, arbitrary subset classifier, or general completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a real-facing consumer of the packet. The only real-name coordinate present in the carrier is $E$, and $E$ is reachable only after the family row, inhabitedness ledger, boundedness ledger, precision window, and supremum witness row have been fixed.

The upper-bound side of the observation is supplied by $B$ and $S$. The lower-approximation side is supplied by $S$ at the requested precision in $W$, with inhabitedness grounded by $I$.

If the observation is read through a cut-shaped presentation, the comparison row $Q$ is the only available handoff. If it is read through sequence approximants, the row $L$ is the only available limit-law handoff.

Thus every real-facing observation factors through $F,I,B,W,S,Q,L,E$ and then through the transport, continuation, provenance, and naming rows. The carrier inventory contains no Dedekind construction of $\RealUp$, quotient-real coordinate, trichotomy oracle, host equality test, choice schedule, arbitrary subset classifier, or general completeness coordinate, so none can be exported.
\end{proof}
