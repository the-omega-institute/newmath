\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\PointedCompleteMetricSpaceUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-pointed-complete-metric-space-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bourbaki and Kelley on based complete metric spaces, the
$\PointedCompleteMetricSpaceUp$ packet records a based metric carrier together
with a displayed completion service before completion, loop-space, or
embedding consumers are allowed to read a point as complete. It sits over the
$\MetricUp$ carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the
$\CompleteMetricUp$ limit-service boundary of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completemetric-namecert}, the
$\PointedMetricSpaceUp$ basepoint route of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-pointed-metric-space-namecert}, the
$\StreamNameUp$ windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert},
the $\RegSeqRatUp$ rational readback of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the $\RealUp$ seal of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The certificate names only
finite BEDC rows; it does not import host points, quotient metric equality,
selected completion representatives, countable choice, or ambient order
completeness.

\begin{definition}[Pointed complete metric space carrier]
\label{def:pointed-complete-metric-space-carrier}
A $\PointedCompleteMetricSpaceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
P=(M,B,Q,S,R,E,L,H,C,P_0,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the $\MetricUp$ metric carrier row.
\item $B$ is the basepoint row, stored as finite named metric data over $M$
rather than as a host-level point.
\item $Q$ is the Cauchy-window route, listing the finite tail requests through
which regular Cauchy names based at $B$ are inspected.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ readback row for the basepoint and Cauchy
windows.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row for regular rational distance
and modulus observations.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row for the displayed metric-distance and
limit-distance observations.
\item $L$ is the $\CompleteMetricUp$ completion seal, exposing only the
displayed limit-service handoff for the Cauchy-window route $Q$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for
$M,B,Q,S,R,E,L$ and the local certificate rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a based complete-metric
request through the Cauchy windows, stream observations, regular rational
readback, real seal, and completion seal.
\item $P_0$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\PointedCompleteMetricSpaceUp$,
$\MetricUp$, $\CompleteMetricUp$, $\PointedMetricSpaceUp$, $\StreamNameUp$,
$\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\PointedCompleteMetricSpaceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only after transporting the metric
carrier, basepoint, Cauchy-window route, stream windows, regular rational
readback, real seal, completion seal, replay, provenance, and local naming
rows together.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Pointed complete metric space NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:pointed-complete-metric-space-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\PointedCompleteMetricSpaceUp$ carrier
$P=(M,B,Q,S,R,E,L,H,C,P_0,N)$, the local
$\NameCert_{\PointedCompleteMetricSpaceUp}$ surface consists exactly of the
following obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the metric row, basepoint row, Cauchy-window
route, stream windows, regular rational readback, real seal, completion seal,
transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item common-carrier classification, requiring the basepoint row, Cauchy
windows, and completion seal to be read over the transported metric row $M$;
\item Cauchy-window exposure, requiring every completion-facing read to pass
through $Q,S,R,E,L$ before any downstream consumer may inspect the limit
handoff;
\item package provenance and local naming for exactly the displayed finite
packet.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored in a host point, quotient metric equality,
selected completion representative, countable-choice selector, or ambient
order-completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of
\autoref{def:pointed-complete-metric-space-carrier}. The projection exposes
the metric row, basepoint, Cauchy-window route, stream windows, regular
readback, real seal, completion seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local
name as the only available coordinates.

The classifier route is common-carrier. Componentwise transport in $H$ moves
$M,B,Q,S,R,E,L$ together, so a consumer cannot compare a based Cauchy route
over one metric carrier with a completion seal over another.

The completion-facing read order is fixed by the route rows. A completion
request begins as a finite Cauchy-window request in $Q$, becomes a scheduled
stream observation in $S$, is read back as regular rational data in $R$, is
sealed as real-distance data in $E$, and only then reaches the
$\CompleteMetricUp$ limit-service row $L$.

Finally replay $C$ and attach $P_0,N$. Since the carrier has no coordinate for
host points, quotient equality, selected completion representatives,
countable-choice selectors, or ambient order completeness, the listed rows
exhaust the local naming surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Pointed complete metric space Cauchy basepoint route]
\label{thm:pointed-complete-metric-space-cauchy-basepoint-route}
Let $P=(M,B,Q,S,R,E,L,H,C,P_0,N)$ be an accepted
$\PointedCompleteMetricSpaceUp$ carrier. Every based regular Cauchy request
whose initial window is anchored at $B$ is read through the displayed route
$$
M,B,Q \longmapsto S \longmapsto R \longmapsto E \longmapsto L
\longmapsto H,C,P_0,N .
$$
Thus the basepoint participates in completion only as named metric and
Cauchy-window data over $M$, not as a selected host point or completed-limit
representative.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with carrier admission. The only row naming the basepoint is $B$, and it
is stored beside the metric row $M$ and the Cauchy-window route $Q$.

Read the window route next. The stream row $S$ schedules the inspected windows,
the regular rational row $R$ reads the tail and distance ledgers, and the
$\RealUp$ row $E$ seals the observed distances before the complete-metric row
$L$ can expose any limit-service handoff.

Transport and replay preserve that same path. The rows $H,C,P_0,N$ move,
replay, source, and name the displayed packet; they do not add a host
basepoint, a selected limit representative, quotient equality, or a separate
completion object.

Therefore every based Cauchy consumer receives exactly the finite route
displayed in the statement.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Pointed complete metric space completion nonescape]
\label{thm:pointed-complete-metric-space-completion-nonescape}
Every completion-facing, loop-space-facing, or embedding-facing consumer of an
accepted $\PointedCompleteMetricSpaceUp$ packet factors its completed-point
read through
$$
M,B,Q,S,R,E,L,H,C,P_0,N .
$$
Consequently the packet exports a based complete metric certificate only as
finite BEDC NameCert data; it exports neither host points nor quotient
completion equality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier coordinates. The only completion coordinate is $L$, and
the definition makes $L$ a completion seal for the already displayed
Cauchy-window route $Q$ over the metric row $M$ and basepoint row $B$.

The readback rows precede the completion seal. Any consumer must first pass
through $S,R,E$ to display stream windows, regular rational ledgers, and real
distance seals, so no completed point can be read before the finite analytic
route is visible.

The remaining rows are administrative in the BEDC sense. Transport $H$, replay
$C$, provenance $P_0$, and local name $N$ preserve and source the packet but
do not create host points, quotient completion equality, selected
representatives, or ambient completeness.

Thus every downstream completed-point read factors through the displayed
finite packet, which proves completion nonescape.
\end{proof}
