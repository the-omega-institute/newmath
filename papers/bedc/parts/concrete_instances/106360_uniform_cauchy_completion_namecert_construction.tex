\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\UniformCauchyCompletionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-uniform-cauchy-completion-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges on constructive completions, the $\UniformCauchyCompletionUp$ packet records the finite uniform-Cauchy handoff used by Phase Real and Completion consumers before any completed object is exposed. It sits over the finite stream windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the Cauchy-sequence space surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchysequencespace-namecert}, the terminal Real seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, and the Bishop completion route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bishop-completion-theorem-namecert}. The packet is a finite NameCert surface for uniform Cauchy families; it is not a quotient completion, selected limit, countable choice principle, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness theorem.

\begin{definition}[Uniform Cauchy completion carrier]
\label{def:uniform-cauchy-completion-carrier}
A $\UniformCauchyCompletionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
U=(F,S,R,D,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $F$ is the uniform Cauchy family row, recording the inspected finite family, common-window requests, and uniform Cauchy tolerance responses.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row, exposing only the windows used by $F$.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row on those same windows.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger that discharges every displayed uniform Cauchy request.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ and completion seal row reached only after $F,S,R,D$ have been displayed, with the sibling handoff to $\CauchySequenceSpaceUp$ and $\BishopCompletionTheoremUp$ recorded as part of the same finite route.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the family, stream-window, regular-readback, dyadic-ledger, seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a completion request through $F,S,R,D,E,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\UniformCauchyCompletionUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\CauchySequenceSpaceUp$, $\BishopCompletionTheoremUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\UniformCauchyCompletionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate asserts exactly that these rows form one finite uniform-Cauchy completion handoff. It has no coordinate for quotient completions, selected limits, countable choice, host real equality, or ambient Real completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Uniform Cauchy completion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:uniform-cauchy-completion-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\UniformCauchyCompletionUp$ carrier $U=(F,S,R,D,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\UniformCauchyCompletionUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the uniform Cauchy family, finite StreamName windows, RegSeqRat readback, DyadicRatCore tolerance ledger, Real and completion seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item uniform Cauchy family stability, requiring every requested comparison to stay on the displayed family row $F$ and the finite windows exposed by $S$;
\item regular rational readback exactness, requiring the $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $R$ to read only the same windows and budgets already named by $F$ and $S$;
\item dyadic tolerance exactness, requiring the row $D$ to discharge each uniform Cauchy request before the seal row is visible;
\item Real and completion seal nonescape, requiring the row $E$ to be reached only through $F,S,R,D$ and the sibling $\CauchySequenceSpaceUp$ and $\BishopCompletionTheoremUp$ routes;
\item componentwise $\hsame$ transport and displayed $\Cont$ replay through $H$ and $C$;
\item $\Pkg$ provenance and local naming through $P$ and $N$;
\item refusal of quotient completions, selected limits, countable choice, host real equality, and ambient Real completeness as certificate data.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:uniform-cauchy-completion-carrier}. The visible coordinates are $F,S,R,D,E,H,C,P,N$, so every local obligation must be read from those rows.

The uniform Cauchy family is fixed by $F$ and its finite observations are fixed by $S$. The regular rational readback $R$ and tolerance ledger $D$ are attached to those same windows, so a completion-facing request cannot exchange them for an external sequence, host equality, or selected limit representative.

The seal row is $E$. It is downstream of the family, StreamName, RegSeqRat, and DyadicRatCore rows, and its sibling route names the $\CauchySequenceSpaceUp$ and $\BishopCompletionTheoremUp$ handoffs without adding quotient or choice data.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, record provenance for, and locally name the same handoff. Since no quotient completion, selected limit, countable choice, host real equality, or ambient Real completeness coordinate appears in the carrier, the listed rows exhaust the certificate surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Uniform Cauchy completion finite handoff]
\label{thm:uniform-cauchy-completion-finite-handoff}
Every $\RealUp$ or completion-facing consumer of an accepted $\UniformCauchyCompletionUp$ packet factors through the finite route
$$
F \longmapsto S \longmapsto R \longmapsto D \longmapsto E,H,C,P,N .
$$
Thus a consumer first reads the uniform Cauchy family, then the finite StreamName windows, then the RegSeqRat readback, then the DyadicRatCore tolerance ledger, and only then the Real and completion seal. The route exports no quotient completion, selected limit, countable choice, host real equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted packet $U=(F,S,R,D,E,H,C,P,N)$. The only family coordinate is $F$, and the only finite observation coordinate is $S$.

The readback and tolerance rows are $R$ and $D$. They keep the completion-facing request on the same regular rational windows and dyadic tolerance ledger before any terminal seal can be read.

The endpoint row is $E$, with structural support from $H,C,P,N$. These rows hand the same finite packet to $\RealUp$, $\CauchySequenceSpaceUp$, and $\BishopCompletionTheoremUp$ consumers without adding quotient, selection, choice, host-equality, or ambient-completeness data.
\end{proof}
