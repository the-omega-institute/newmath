\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\EventuallyZeroSequenceUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-eventually-zero-sequence-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges, the $\EventuallyZeroSequenceUp$ packet records the finite-cutoff source of a null rational difference sequence: after one displayed cutoff row, every later observation is the zero dyadic row. It refines the zero-convergence consumer surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-nullsequence-namecert} by replacing an arbitrary modulus lookup with a visible cutoff induction over the finite windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic zero row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the constant rational handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-stationary-rational-cauchy-namecert}, and the terminal equality seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}.

\begin{definition}[Eventually-zero sequence carrier]
\label{def:eventually-zero-sequence-carrier}
An $\EventuallyZeroSequenceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
E=(S,G,D,Z,T,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row whose accepted requests enumerate the displayed sequence positions.
\item $G$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row attached to the same stream windows.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger containing the distinguished zero dyadic row and the finite comparison rows used by each inspected window.
\item $Z$ is the $\StationaryRationalCauchyUp$ zero source whose constant rational readback supplies the zero row consumed after the cutoff.
\item $T$ is the cutoff row: every accepted request at a position beyond $T$ must replay the zero row of $D$ through the stationary source $Z$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for stream windows, regular readbacks, dyadic rows, the stationary zero source, the cutoff row, continuation routes, provenance, and the local naming row.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a sequence request through $S$, then $G$, then the dyadic row $D$, with the branch beyond $T$ routed through $Z$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\EventuallyZeroSequenceUp$, $\NullSequenceUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StationaryRationalCauchyUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\EventuallyZeroSequenceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no coordinate for a quotient stream, selected host limit, trichotomy principle, or ambient completeness witness for $\RealUp$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Eventually-zero sequence NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:eventually-zero-sequence-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\EventuallyZeroSequenceUp$ carrier $E=(S,G,D,Z,T,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\EventuallyZeroSequenceUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following five obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the stream window, regular rational readback, dyadic zero ledger, stationary zero source, cutoff, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item classifier and transport stability for the displayed rows under the componentwise $\hsame$ ledger $H$;
\item cutoff-window exactness, requiring every request beyond $T$ to read the zero dyadic row of $D$ through the stationary source $Z$;
\item null-sequence handoff, requiring the same finite cutoff packet to instantiate the $\NullSequenceUp$ tolerance-window route;
\item non-escape, excluding quotient streams, selected limits, trichotomy, external real equality, and $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{enumerate}
No local NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:eventually-zero-sequence-carrier}. The displayed tuple supplies exactly the stream, readback, dyadic, stationary zero, cutoff, transport, continuation, package, and local naming rows.

Classifier and transport stability are componentwise. The rows in $H$ move the finite window $S$, the readback $G$, the dyadic ledger $D$, the stationary source $Z$, and the cutoff $T$, while $C$ replays the same request order after transport.

The cutoff-window obligation is the only analytic-looking row, but it is still finite: beyond $T$, each accepted window is routed through the stationary zero source and returns the zero dyadic row already listed in $D$.

The null-sequence handoff and non-escape rows then read the same finite packet. Since quotient streams, selected limits, trichotomy, external equality, and completeness are absent from the carrier, no local obligation can export them.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Eventually-zero cutoff-window induction]
\label{thm:eventually-zero-sequence-window-cutoff-induction}
Let $E=(S,G,D,Z,T,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\EventuallyZeroSequenceUp$ carrier. For every finite $\StreamNameUp$ window whose positions are all beyond the cutoff row $T$, induction over the listed window positions shows that each $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback through $G$ is the zero dyadic row of $D$ routed through the stationary source $Z$.

Consequently every tail window admitted by $S$ beyond $T$ is a finite zero window, transported only by $H$ and replayed only through $C$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use induction on the finite list of positions recorded by the accepted $\StreamNameUp$ window.

The empty window has no readback request, so the zero-window claim is immediate from carrier admission.

For a window extended by one position beyond $T$, first apply the cutoff row. The replay route $C$ sends the final request through $S$, then $G$, then $D$, and the beyond-cutoff branch forces the stationary zero source $Z$.

Apply the induction hypothesis to the earlier positions in the window. Transport through $H$ preserves the same stream, readback, dyadic zero, stationary source, and cutoff rows, so adjoining the final zero readback yields a finite window all of whose displayed values are the zero dyadic row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Eventually-zero NullSequence handoff]
\label{thm:eventually-zero-sequence-nullsequence-handoff}
Every accepted $\EventuallyZeroSequenceUp$ carrier $E=(S,G,D,Z,T,H,C,P,N)$ determines a $\NullSequenceUp$ zero-tail packet for the same displayed regular rational stream: a tolerance request is answered by the cutoff row $T$, the post-cutoff window is read through $S$ and $G$, the zero dyadic row is supplied by $D$ and $Z$, and the resulting evidence is exported to the $\RealUp$ equality seal through the recorded $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a $\NullSequenceUp$ tolerance request. The eventually-zero packet uses $T$ as the displayed threshold row for that request.

Once the request is beyond the cutoff, apply \autoref{thm:eventually-zero-sequence-window-cutoff-induction}. The selected finite window reads only the zero dyadic row through $S$, $G$, $D$, and $Z$.

Repack those rows as the null-sequence tolerance-window package. The stream row is $G$, the tolerance and zero ledger are read from $D$, the window is supplied by $S$, and the bound row is the cutoff $T$.

The remaining coordinates $H,C,P,N$ provide the transport, replay, dependency, and local naming rows required by $\NullSequenceUp$. The $\RealUp$ seal therefore consumes a finite zero-tail packet rather than a host limit or quotient stream.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Eventually-zero public certificate boundary]
\label{thm:eventually-zero-sequence-public-certificate}
The public surface of an accepted $\EventuallyZeroSequenceUp$ packet is exhausted by the finite rows
$$
S,G,D,Z,T,H,C,P,N .
$$
It exposes the cutoff induction of \autoref{thm:eventually-zero-sequence-window-cutoff-induction} and the $\NullSequenceUp$ handoff of \autoref{thm:eventually-zero-sequence-nullsequence-handoff}, and it exposes no quotient stream representative, selected limit, trichotomy principle, hidden real equality test, or ambient completeness theorem for $\RealUp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:eventually-zero-sequence-carrier}. Every public read is a read of the displayed stream, regular rational row, dyadic zero ledger, stationary zero source, cutoff, transport, continuation, provenance, or local naming row.

The cutoff-window induction is internal to those rows. It uses only the finite window $S$, the readback $G$, the dyadic ledger $D$, the stationary source $Z$, the cutoff $T$, and the displayed transport and continuation rows.

The handoff to $\NullSequenceUp$ repacks the same data as a tolerance-window route. It does not add a separate convergence object, because the threshold is already the cutoff row and every tail value is the zero dyadic row.

Since no quotient stream, selected limit, trichotomy principle, hidden equality test, or completeness witness is a coordinate of the packet, none can be exported by the public certificate.
\end{proof}
