\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\DedekindCutUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-dedekindcut-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\DedekindCutUp$ interface records the rounded located cut presentation of constructive real analysis in the style of Bishop--Bridges and Troelstra--van Dalen. It keeps lower and upper rational observations as explicit $\RatUp$ rows, rather than quotienting cut predicates or comparing host reals.

The $\DedekindCutUp$ carrier consumes the rounded located-cut surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedcut-namecert} and the rational observation rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rat-namecert}. Its lower and upper predicate rows are therefore a finite BEDC ledger over rational observations rather than an isolated host cut predicate.

The lower and upper rational-observation rows consume the rational certificate of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rat-namecert}, and the comparison handoff is exported only to the real seal boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}.

\begin{definition}[DedekindCut BHist source]
\label{def:dedekindcut-bhist-source}
A $\DedekindCutUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
C=(L,U,I,R,O,E,Q,H,T,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $L$ is the lower $\RatUp$ predicate row, listing rational observations certified to lie below the represented real;
\item $U$ is the upper $\RatUp$ predicate row, listing rational observations certified to lie above the represented real;
\item $I$ stores lower and upper inhabitedness witnesses, with displayed rational rows on both sides;
\item $R$ stores roundedness witnesses for the lower and upper rows, so each observed rational admits a strictly better observed rational on the same side;
\item $O$ stores locatedness witnesses, assigning each rational interval $a<b$ a displayed lower-side or upper-side observation;
\item $E$ stores disjointness evidence preventing the same rational observation from being accepted by both sides;
\item $Q$ stores rational-embedding rows, including the lower and upper boundary readings for embedded $\RatUp$ points;
\item $H$ stores the componentwise $\hsame$ routes for the lower predicate, upper predicate, roundedness, locatedness, disjointness, and rational embedding rows;
\item $T$ stores the $\Cont$ routes that expose finite lower and upper rational observations to downstream consumers;
\item $P$ stores $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RatUp$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DedekindCutUp}$ evidence.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares agreement of the displayed lower and upper rational observations under $\RatUp$ comparison. It never compares quotient cuts, host real equality, or choice-selected limits.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[DedekindCut NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:dedekindcut-namecert-obligation-surface}
The local $\NameCert_{\DedekindCutUp}$ obligation surface for the carrier of \autoref{def:dedekindcut-bhist-source} consists of five finite rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission: the accepted object is exactly the packet $C$ with displayed lower and upper $\RatUp$ predicate rows, inhabitedness, roundedness, locatedness, disjointness, rational embedding rows, transports, continuations, package provenance, and local naming evidence;
\item classifier transport: classification transports the lower and upper rational observations componentwise through the stored $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ routes and compares them only by $\RatUp$ comparison;
\item stability: replacing any displayed rational observation by a classified $\RatUp$ observation preserves the rounded located cut laws inside the same carrier surface;
\item exactness: every consumer-visible lower or upper decision is the row supplied by $L$, $U$, $O$, $E$, and $Q$, with no hidden host-real predicate;
\item ledger: every downstream $\RealUp$ or completion-facing consumer first reads $L,U,I,R,O,E,Q,H,T,P,N$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from the tuple of \autoref{def:dedekindcut-bhist-source}. The lower and upper rational predicate rows, their inhabitedness, roundedness, locatedness, disjointness, rational embedding, transport, continuation, package, and naming rows are all displayed coordinates.

Classifier transport is componentwise. A lower-side observation is moved through the lower $\RatUp$ route, an upper-side observation is moved through the upper $\RatUp$ route, and the comparison used by the classifier is the displayed rational comparison between those transported rows.

Exactness and the ledger clauses follow from the same inventory. Since the carrier contains no quotient cut, host real equality proof, selected limit, or ambient completeness object, every consumer-visible decision is exhausted by the displayed finite rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DedekindCut rounded located laws]
\label{thm:dedekindcut-located-rounded-cut-laws}
Every accepted $\DedekindCutUp$ carrier $C=(L,U,I,R,O,E,Q,H,T,P,N)$ presents a constructive rounded located cut over $\RatUp$: both sides are inhabited, lower and upper observations are rounded on their own sides, each rational interval $a<b$ is decided by the locatedness row $O$, and disjointness row $E$ prevents a rational observation from being accepted by both sides.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DedekindCutUp.DedekindCutCarrier\_located\_rounded\_cut\_laws}
\begin{proof}
Read the inhabitedness row $I$ to obtain displayed rational observations on both sides. These witnesses are part of the finite carrier and do not require a completed real.

Read the roundedness row $R$. For a lower observation it supplies a stronger lower observation; for an upper observation it supplies a stronger upper observation. The witnesses are again $\RatUp$ rows transported through the displayed carrier routes.

For a rational interval $a<b$, the locatedness row $O$ gives the lower-side or upper-side observation named by the packet. The disjointness row $E$ then prevents overlap of the two displayed predicates, so the cut laws remain internal to the finite $\DedekindCutUp$ surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DedekindCut rational embedding boundary]
\label{thm:dedekindcut-rational-embedding-boundary}
For every rational row embedded through $Q$ in an accepted $\DedekindCutUp$ carrier, the lower and upper boundary observations are exactly the displayed $\RatUp$ comparison rows against that rational. A consumer cannot replace those rows by quotient equality of cuts, host real equality, or an external order on completed reals.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DedekindCutUp.DedekindCutCarrier\_rational\_embedding\_boundary}
\begin{proof}
Project the rational embedding row $Q$. Its lower boundary read is a displayed comparison placing an observed rational below the embedded row, and its upper boundary read is the corresponding displayed comparison above the embedded row.

Transport along $H$ and $T$ preserves only these rational observations. The classifier of \autoref{thm:dedekindcut-namecert-obligation-surface} compares lower and upper sides by $\RatUp$ comparison, so the embedded rational has no separate equality or order witness outside the carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DedekindCut RealUp comparison handoff]
\label{thm:dedekindcut-realup-comparison-handoff}
Every $\RealUp$ or completion-facing comparison consumer of an accepted $\DedekindCutUp$ carrier receives only the displayed lower and upper $\RatUp$ observation surface, the rounded located cut laws, and the rational embedding boundary. The handoff supplies no quotient real, host equality, selected limit, or choice-selected comparison.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DedekindCutUp.DedekindCutCarrier\_realup\_comparison\_handoff}
\begin{proof}
First apply \autoref{thm:dedekindcut-located-rounded-cut-laws}. It supplies the inhabitedness, roundedness, locatedness, and disjointness rows needed for a constructive cut comparison.

Next apply \autoref{thm:dedekindcut-rational-embedding-boundary}. It fixes the rational boundary reads as displayed $\RatUp$ comparisons rather than external real comparisons.

The non-escape claim is then immediate from the carrier inventory in \autoref{def:dedekindcut-bhist-source}. The only exported analytic data are lower and upper rational observations with their local evidence and provenance.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DedekindCut quotient-free boundary]
\label{thm:dedekindcut-quotient-free-boundary}
The displayed $\DedekindCutUp$ carrier, rounded located laws, rational embedding boundary, and $\RealUp$ comparison handoff license only a classifier-mediated comparison interface over $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$. They do not introduce a quotient object, a completeness theorem, or an alternative construction of $\RealUp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier of \autoref{def:dedekindcut-bhist-source}. It lists lower and upper rational-observation rows, local law rows, rational embedding rows, componentwise transport, continuation, package, and naming data, but no quotient carrier or host real equality coordinate.

The admissible classifier surface is fixed by \autoref{thm:dedekindcut-namecert-obligation-surface}. Its comparison reads are mediated by the displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ rows and the local $\NameCert$ evidence, so the interface remains a certificate boundary rather than a new real object.

Apply \autoref{thm:dedekindcut-located-rounded-cut-laws} and \autoref{thm:dedekindcut-realup-comparison-handoff}. These give the rounded located laws and the comparison handoff while keeping every read on rational observations and their provenance. Hence the boundary permits classifier-mediated comparison only, with no quotient object, completeness theorem, or alternative $\RealUp$ construction.
\end{proof}
