\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\FilteredCauchyCompletionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-filtered-cauchy-completion-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, and Bourbaki,
\emph{General Topology}, the $\FilteredCauchyCompletionUp$ packet records the
finite BEDC route by which Cauchy filters reach the Real-completion interface.
It is built over $\CauchyFilterUp$, $\UniformCauchyCriterionUp$,
$\CauchySequenceSpaceUp$, $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$,
$\RealCompletionExactBoundaryUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$,
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$.

The dependency anchors are the finite filter surface of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyfilter-namecert}, the sequence-space
handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchysequencespace-namecert}, and
the Real boundary of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-completion-exact-boundary-namecert}. The
uniform criterion supplies the common-tail test before any route reaches the
Real seal, while stream names, regular rational rows, and dyadic ledgers keep
the consumer boundary finite.

\begin{definition}[Filtered Cauchy completion carrier]\label{def:filtered-cauchy-completion-carrier}
A $\FilteredCauchyCompletionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(F,U,S,Q,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $F$ is the $\CauchyFilterUp$ row carrying the finite filterbase and
common-window evidence;
\item $U$ is the $\UniformCauchyCriterionUp$ row that tests the same finite
filter windows by uniform tails;
\item $S$ is the $\CauchySequenceSpaceUp$ handoff row exposing the associated
$\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ readings;
\item $Q$ is the $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ and
$\RealCompletionExactBoundaryUp$ route reached from those same finite rows;
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row reached by the displayed route;
\item $H$ is the componentwise $\hsame$ transport row for $F,U,S,Q,E$;
\item $C$ is the $\Cont$ continuation row that replays the accepted finite
windows without adding host data;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over the listed completion, filter,
uniform, stream, regular-rational, dyadic, transport, and seal rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\FilteredCauchyCompletionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The accepted carrier exposes only these rows. It has no coordinate for a
quotient stream, selected limit, host real equality, countable-choice
selector, or ambient completeness principle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Filtered completion carrier habitation]\label{thm:filtered-cauchy-completion-carrier-habitation}
Whenever the rows $F,U,S,Q,E,H,C,P,N$ occur in one finite $\BHist$ with the
dependencies listed in \autoref{def:filtered-cauchy-completion-carrier}, they
assemble an accepted $\FilteredCauchyCompletionUp$ carrier. Conversely, every
accepted carrier projects to exactly those nine displayed rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The forward direction reads the finite history in the carrier order. The
$\CauchyFilterUp$ row supplies the finite filterbase, the uniform criterion
row supplies the common-tail test, the sequence-space row supplies the stream,
regular-rational, and dyadic readings, and the completion route supplies the
Real-boundary handoff and terminal seal.

Transport, continuation, package provenance, and local naming are the final
four displayed coordinates. Repacking these rows gives the named carrier.

For the reverse direction, project an accepted carrier by its definition. No
extra coordinate is available, so the projection is exhausted by the nine
listed rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Common-filter classifier transport]\label{thm:filtered-cauchy-completion-common-filter-transport}
Two accepted $\FilteredCauchyCompletionUp$ carriers classify together only by a
displayed common $\CauchyFilterUp$ refinement whose uniform-tail test, sequence
space handoff, Real-completion route, terminal Real seal, $\hsame$ transport,
$\Cont$ continuation, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ rows are
transported componentwise.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project both carriers to their $F$ rows and compare those rows by the common
finite filter refinement supplied by $\CauchyFilterUp$. The classifier has no
access to a host filter or quotient of streams.

Once the filter rows share a displayed refinement, transport the $U,S,Q,E$
rows componentwise along $H$ and replay the same reads through $C$. The package
row records that these transported reads remain on the same finite dependency
anchors.

Repacking the transported rows gives the classifier judgement for the two
carriers. Reading such a judgement back yields exactly the common filter
refinement and the listed componentwise transports.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Uniform-tail exactness]\label{thm:filtered-cauchy-completion-uniform-tail-exactness}
For an accepted carrier $K=(F,U,S,Q,E,H,C,P,N)$, every completion-facing read
of $Q$ first passes through the uniform-tail row $U$ attached to the same
$\CauchyFilterUp$ row $F$ and the same $\CauchySequenceSpaceUp$ handoff $S$.
No Real seal consumer can bypass $U$ by using a selected subsequence, quotient
stream, or host completion point.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the carrier coordinates. The route to $Q$ is listed after $F,U,S$, so the
completion-facing consumer sees the filterbase, uniform-tail test, and
sequence-space handoff before it can read the Real-completion boundary.

The continuation row $C$ only replays the accepted finite windows, and the
transport row $H$ only compares displayed coordinates. Neither row introduces
a selected subsequence, quotient stream, or host completion point.

Thus the uniform-tail row is the exact gate for completion-facing reads. Any
consumer that bypasses it has left the displayed carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Completion handoff factorization]\label{thm:filtered-cauchy-completion-handoff-factorization}
Every $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ or $\RealCompletionExactBoundaryUp$ handoff
exported by an accepted $\FilteredCauchyCompletionUp$ carrier factors through
the rows $F,U,S,H,C,P,N$ before reaching $Q$ and the terminal seal $E$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the carrier decomposition. The handoff row $Q$ is not independent:
it is stored as the route reached from the same finite filter, uniform-tail,
sequence-space, transport, continuation, provenance, and naming rows.

The sequence-space row supplies the visible stream-name, regular-rational, and
dyadic readings. The uniform-tail row checks those readings against the common
filter classifier before the completion boundary is exposed.

Consequently every exported completion handoff factors through
$F,U,S,H,C,P,N$ and only then reaches $Q$ and $E$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Filtered completion no-escape]\label{thm:filtered-cauchy-completion-nonescape}
An accepted $\FilteredCauchyCompletionUp$ carrier gives no route to quotient
streams, selected limits, host real equality, countable choice, or ambient
completeness. Every consumer of the packet is confined to the finite rows
$F,U,S,Q,E,H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The excluded objects are not coordinates of
\autoref{def:filtered-cauchy-completion-carrier}. A consumer can project the
filter row, uniform criterion row, sequence-space handoff, completion route,
Real seal, transport, continuation, package, and local naming row, and nothing
else.

The previous factorization theorem shows that even completion-facing reads
must pass through those displayed rows. The common-filter transport theorem
shows the same boundary after classifier comparison.

Therefore any route requiring quotient streams, selected limits, host real
equality, countable choice, or ambient completeness lies outside the accepted
carrier.
\end{proof}
