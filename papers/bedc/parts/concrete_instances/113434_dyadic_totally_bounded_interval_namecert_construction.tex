\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\DyadicTotallyBoundedIntervalUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-dyadic-totally-bounded-interval-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges on constructive intervals and finite nets, the $\DyadicTotallyBoundedIntervalUp$ packet records the explicit dyadic mesh by which a closed real interval is read as totally bounded before any open-cover compactness claim is available. It sits after the dyadic arithmetic of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the finite window schedule of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the interval-net surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closedboundedintervalnet-namecert}, and the finite-net interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-totallybounded-namecert}. The packet is a choice-free finite mesh certificate for the L10 real-and-completion route; it is not open-cover compactness, selected subcovers, countable choice, quotient interval equality, trichotomy, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[Dyadic totally bounded interval carrier]
\label{def:dyadic-totally-bounded-interval-carrier}
A $\DyadicTotallyBoundedIntervalUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
I=(L,U,M,Z,\rho,F,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $L$ and $U$ are the lower and upper endpoint rows, each read through the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint interface and sealed only later as $\RealUp$ data;
\item $M$ is the unary mesh-scale row naming the dyadic precision used for the interval net;
\item $Z$ is the finite center list, carried as a closed $\ProbeBundle$ of dyadic center rows between the displayed endpoints;
\item $\rho$ is the dyadic radius ledger assigning the mesh radius used by the centers of $Z$;
\item $F$ is the finite coverage row saying that every interval read enters one displayed center of $Z$ within the dyadic radius $\rho$;
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row used to inspect endpoint, center, and radius names at the mesh scale;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row assembled from the endpoint, center, and radius windows;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after $L,U,M,Z,\rho,F,S,R$ are displayed;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for endpoints, mesh, centers, radii, coverage rows, windows, readback, seal, provenance, and local name;
\item $C$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ replay from endpoints to mesh selection, from mesh to finite centers and radii, from coverage to window readback, and from readback to the real seal;
\item $P$ records $\Pkg$ provenance over $\DyadicTotallyBoundedIntervalUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\ClosedBoundedIntervalNetUp$, $\TotallyBoundedUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DyadicTotallyBoundedIntervalUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for an open cover, a selected finite subcover, countable choice, quotient interval equality, real trichotomy, or an ambient completeness principle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Dyadic totally bounded interval NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:dyadic-totally-bounded-interval-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:dyadic-totally-bounded-interval-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\DyadicTotallyBoundedIntervalUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $I=(L,U,M,Z,\rho,F,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, finite-net coverage through $Z,\rho,F$, endpoint containment for $L$ and $U$, mesh-radius compatibility between $M$ and $\rho$, $\StreamNameUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback through $S$ and $R$, $\RealUp$ seal nonescape through $E$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier. The endpoint rows $L,U$, mesh row $M$, finite center bundle $Z$, dyadic radius ledger $\rho$, and coverage row $F$ are displayed before any stream, regular-Cauchy, or real seal row is available.

The finite-net obligations are read from those rows. Endpoint containment uses the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint rows, mesh-radius compatibility ties $M$ to $\rho$, and the coverage row $F$ routes each interval read to one center of $Z$ at that radius. This is the same kind of finite mesh handoff consumed by the $\ClosedBoundedIntervalNetUp$ and $\TotallyBoundedUp$ sibling surfaces.

The readback and seal obligations are downstream. The $\StreamNameUp$ row $S$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $R$ expose only the displayed endpoint, center, and radius windows, and the $\RealUp$ seal $E$ is reached only after that readback. The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite route, so the excluded compactness, choice, equality, trichotomy, and completeness principles have no carrier coordinate.
\end{proof}
