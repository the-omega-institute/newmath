The $\ObservationBudgetLimiterUp$ packet realizes the finite request-cap side of the Real and Completion route of \autoref{ch:visions-real-completeness}. It sits between the observation-budget service of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-observation-budget-namecert} and the seal-facing routes of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet is a concrete gate: it records which finite request rows may be served before any $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, or $\RealUp$ seal read is consumed.

\falsifiablePrediction{Deleting the budget-cap row permits a RealUp seal consumer to bypass the finite request bound, while deleting the window or dyadic rows breaks StreamName and RegSeqRat readback.}

\independenceWitness{Nearest siblings: realobservationbudget, cauchysealbudgetsynchronizer, realcompletionsealclassifier, streamname, and regseqrat; none is bijective because this carrier has a separate budget-cap gate between request and seal rows.}

\begin{definition}[Observation-budget limiter carrier]
\label{def:observation-budget-limiter-carrier}
An $\ObservationBudgetLimiterUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(E,B,K,W,D,R,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $E$ is the incoming finite precision or observation request;
\item $B$ is the accepted $\RealObservationBudgetUp$ service row covering the request;
\item $K$ is the budget-cap row that bounds how many request windows may be served before the seal is read;
\item $W$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window ledger admitted under the cap;
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observation ledger read through those windows;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row assembled from $W$ and $D$;
\item $S$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after the capped request, window, dyadic, and regular readback rows have been consumed;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $E,B,K,W,D,R,S,C,P,N$;
\item $C$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ routes from the request to the budget row, from the budget-cap row to the window ledger, from the window and dyadic ledgers to the regular readback, and from the readback to the seal;
\item $P$ records $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RealObservationBudgetUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ObservationBudgetLimiterUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exports a finite budget gate for Real and Completion consumers. It exports no unbounded observation schedule, quotient stream, selected limit, host real equality, trichotomy row, independent real carrier, or ambient completeness principle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Observation-budget limiter NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:observation-budget-limiter-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:observation-budget-limiter-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\ObservationBudgetLimiterUp}$ surface consists of six finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed packet $L=(E,B,K,W,D,R,S,H,C,P,N)$;
\item budget-cap admission, requiring $K$ to be read after the request row $E$ and budget row $B$ and before any window row $W$ is served;
\item finite-window coverage, requiring every served window to be one of the $\StreamNameUp$ rows admitted by $W$ under $K$;
\item dyadic and regular readback, requiring each $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observation in $D$ and the resulting $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $R$ to be read through the same capped window route;
\item seal handoff, requiring the $\RealUp$ seal row $S$ to be consumed only after $E,B,K,W,D,R$ have been displayed;
\item non-escape, excluding unbounded schedules, quotient streams, selected limits, host real equality, trichotomy, independent real carriers, and ambient completeness.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:observation-budget-limiter-carrier}. The request, observation budget, cap, window, dyadic, regular readback, seal, transport, route, provenance, and local name are all explicit coordinates.

Budget-cap admission reads $K$ between $B$ and $W$. Thus the finite request is served by the displayed budget row before any window ledger is exposed, and the cap row fixes which subsequent windows are available.

Finite-window coverage, dyadic readback, and regular readback are then read along the displayed route $K \to W \to D \to R$. Componentwise transports in $H$ preserve the same rows, and the continuation rows in $C$ replay them without adding another observation source.

Finally the seal handoff reads $S$ only after $R$. Since the carrier contains no row for an unbounded schedule, quotient stream, selected limit, host real equality, trichotomy, independent real carrier, or ambient completeness principle, the non-escape obligation is exactly the visible finite inventory.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Observation-budget limiter cap admission]
