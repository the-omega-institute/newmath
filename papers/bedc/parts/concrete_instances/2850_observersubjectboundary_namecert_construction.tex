\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ObserverSubjectBoundaryUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-observersubjectboundary-namecert}
\origin{ai}

The $\ObserverSubjectBoundaryUp$ carrier records the finite observation packet at which an observer-facing consumer may inspect histories without receiving a separate subject carrier. Its rows are histories, transported history-sameness, displayed continuation routes, probe and signature readback, package provenance, and a local naming row. The boundary is therefore a finite BEDC packet over observation rows, not a substance slot behind those rows.

The downstream $\ObserverHistIdentityUp$ surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observerhistidentity-namecert} consumes this boundary before reading its $H,O,L,C,P,N$ packet. The dependency is the absence of any observer-substance coordinate, so observer-history identity is read as a finite history-and-name packet rather than as a cosmetic reference to a hidden subject.

\begin{definition}[ObserverSubjectBoundary carrier]
\label{def:observer-subject-boundary-carrier}
An $\ObserverSubjectBoundaryUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
O=(R,H,C,B,S,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $R$ is the finite observation-row ledger, whose entries are $\BHist$ records of the histories inspected by the observer-facing consumer;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the observation rows and for the local naming row;
\item $C$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which the consumer reads observation rows, transports, probe bundles, signature relations, provenance, and local naming;
\item $B$ is the finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ readback row attached to the same observation ledger;
\item $S$ is the $\mathsf{SigRel}$ readback row recording the signature events visible through $B$;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $R,H,C,B,S$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ObserverSubjectBoundaryUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
There is no coordinate for an observer-substance type, subject identity carrier, hidden owner of the observation rows, or host equality principle outside $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, $\mathsf{SigRel}$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[ObserverSubjectBoundary TasteGate carrier alignment]
\label{thm:observer-subject-boundary-tastegate-alignment}
The displayed rows $R,H,C,B,S,P,N$ of the $\ObserverSubjectBoundaryUp$ carrier are exactly the single-carrier surface checked by the Lean $\ChapterTasteGate$ theorem. The checked surface consists of $\BHist$ encode-decode stability, round-trip translation through the observer-subject-boundary event flow, injectivity of that event-flow view, and the empty-history normalization for the same finite observation packet.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ObserverSubjectBoundaryUp.ObserverSubjectBoundaryTasteGate\_single\_carrier\_alignment}
\begin{proof}
The carrier definition presents one tuple $O=(R,H,C,B,S,P,N)$ and does not add a second subject coordinate. The Lean gate reads the same single carrier through the event-flow encoder and decoder.

Encode-decode stability fixes the $\BHist$ row, the round-trip clause returns the displayed $\ObserverSubjectBoundaryUp$ packet, and injectivity prevents two distinct displayed packets from sharing one event-flow view. The empty-history normalization identifies the empty observation row as the baseline ledger case for that same carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ObserverSubjectBoundary NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:observer-subject-boundary-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:observer-subject-boundary-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\ObserverSubjectBoundaryUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $O$, observation-row stability, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ route replay, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ and $\mathsf{SigRel}$ readback exactness, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming exactness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from the finite packet $O$. The observation rows $R$, transports $H$, routes $C$, bundle row $B$, signature row $S$, package row $P$, and local name $N$ are the displayed coordinates of the carrier.

The stability obligations are componentwise. Transport through $H$ preserves the observation rows and the local naming row, while the routes in $C$ replay only the listed reads.

Bundle and signature exactness come from the readback rows $B$ and $S$. Since the carrier has no coordinate beyond $R,H,C,B,S,P,N$, the local naming certificate is exhausted by the stated finite obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ObserverSubjectBoundary no extra subject]
\label{thm:observer-subject-boundary-no-extra-subject}
Every accepted $\ObserverSubjectBoundaryUp$ packet exposes an observer boundary only through its finite $\BHist$ observation rows, $\hsame$ transport, $\Cont$ routes, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ readback, $\mathsf{SigRel}$ readback, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ row. No additional observer-substance carrier, subject identity row, hidden owner, or host equality coordinate is exported.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet and inspect the coordinates of \autoref{def:observer-subject-boundary-carrier}. The only rows available to a consumer are $R,H,C,B,S,P,N$.

Apply the NameCert obligations from \autoref{thm:observer-subject-boundary-namecert-obligations}. They identify transport, route replay, bundle readback, signature readback, package provenance, and local naming as the full certificate surface.

A requested subject carrier would require a coordinate outside the displayed tuple. Because no such coordinate is part of the packet, the accepted boundary cannot export observer-substance, subject identity, hidden ownership, or host equality data.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ObserverSubjectBoundary ledger exactness]
\label{thm:observer-subject-boundary-ledger-exactness}
For an accepted $\ObserverSubjectBoundaryUp$ packet, every observer-facing read is accounted for by the finite ledger route
$$
R,H,C,B,S,P,N.
$$
The $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ readback and $\mathsf{SigRel}$ readback are attached to the same $\BHist$ observation rows, transported by the same $\hsame$ row, routed by the same $\Cont$ row, and recorded under the same $\Pkg$ and $\NameCert$ provenance.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the observation ledger $R$. The routes in $C$ read the ledger rows and then reach only the displayed bundle, signature, provenance, and local naming rows.

Use componentwise transport through $H$ to keep the observation rows, bundle readback, and signature readback attached to the same finite packet. The package row $P$ records provenance over those transported rows.

Finally apply the no-extra-subject theorem. Since no outside carrier is exported, every observer-facing read is exactly one of the displayed ledger reads and no hidden subject row can refine the ledger.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ObserverSubjectBoundary NameCert package]
\label{thm:observer-subject-boundary-namecert-package}
The carrier, NameCert obligations, no-extra-subject boundary, and ledger exactness assemble the $\NameCert_{\ObserverSubjectBoundaryUp}$ package as a finite observer-boundary packet over $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, $\mathsf{SigRel}$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ rows. Downstream observer-history consumers receive this package as a finite boundary certificate and not as a separate observer-substance object.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the carrier and obligation theorem. They provide the finite packet and identify the complete local naming surface.

Next use ledger exactness to bind every observer-facing read to the displayed rows $R,H,C,B,S,P,N$. The readback rows for probes and signatures remain attached to the same observation ledger through the recorded transports and routes.

Finally apply the no-extra-subject theorem. The package delivered to downstream consumers is therefore a finite BEDC boundary certificate, and the absence of an additional subject carrier is part of the package surface rather than an external convention.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ObserverSubjectBoundary nontriviality witness]
\label{thm:observer-subject-boundary-nontriviality-witness}
The empty observation packet and a one-step observation packet are separated by the displayed observation-ledger coordinate $R$. Consequently the $\ObserverSubjectBoundaryUp$ carrier cannot collapse to $\mathsf{PUnit}$: a consumer can distinguish the packet with no inspected $\BHist$ row from the packet whose ledger contains one inspected $\BHist$ row before reading the shared $H,C,B,S,P,N$ coordinates.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Compare the first coordinate of the two packets. In the empty packet, $R$ contains no observation entry; in the one-step packet, $R$ contains one displayed $\BHist$ entry.

Projection to $R$ is part of the carrier surface of \autoref{def:observer-subject-boundary-carrier}. If the carrier collapsed to $\mathsf{PUnit}$, these two packets would have the same observable ledger projection, contradicting the displayed difference between the empty ledger and the one-entry ledger.
\end{proof}

Every downstream consumer of an accepted $\ObserverSubjectBoundaryUp$ packet, including the $\ObserverHistIdentityUp$ surface named in this chapter's introduction, receives exactly the rows $R,H,C,B,S,P,N$. \end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet through \autoref{def:observer-subject-boundary-carrier}. The only consumer-facing coordinates are $R,H,C,B,S,P,N$.

The introductory $\ObserverHistIdentityUp$ dependency reads this same packet before it forms its history-identity surface. Its available inputs are therefore the displayed history rows, transport rows, route rows, probe and signature readback rows, provenance, and local naming row.

Apply the no-extra-subject and ledger-exactness theorems. They rule out a hidden subject coordinate and bind every read to the finite observer ledger, so the scoped consumer receives a concrete BEDC boundary packet and not a public-object or bridge-checked surface.
\end{proof}

\label{thm:observer-subject-boundary-public-nonescape}
The $\ObserverSubjectBoundaryUp$ packet does not export public observer-substance, subject identity, hidden ownership, host equality, or bridge data. Its nonescaping surface is precisely the finite route
$$

$$

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

Next apply the no-extra-subject theorem. A public subject carrier, hidden owner, or host equality coordinate would need an additional row outside the displayed packet, and the carrier does not contain one.

Finally read the closure boundary. The chapter supplies scoped consumer witnesses for downstream observer-history use, but it does not introduce a public export theorem or a standard bridge row, so public and bridge closure remain unclaimed.
\end{proof}
