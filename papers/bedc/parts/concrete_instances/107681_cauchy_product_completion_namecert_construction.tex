\begin{definition}[CauchyProductCompletion carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-product-completion-carrier}
A $\CauchyProductCompletionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(S_0,S_1,D_0,D_1,B,R_0,R_1,L_0,L_1,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S_0,S_1$ are paired $\StreamNameUp$ windows for the two completion-facing coordinates.
\item $D_0,D_1$ are $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observation rows for the finite rational approximants read from the two streams.
\item $B$ is the $\CauchyProductUp$ budget row, recording the common tolerance window used to multiply or pair the displayed approximants.
\item $R_0,R_1$ are $\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier rows, certifying that each coordinate read is regular enough to enter the real-completion route.
\item $L_0,L_1$ are $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ compatibility rows, witnessing that the coordinate windows are read only through the completion seal used by the L10 route.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ export seal for the product-completion read.
\item $H$ records the $\hsame$ transports for stream names, dyadic observations, regular classifiers, product budget, limit seals, and real export.
\item $C$ records the $\Cont$ replays that move from stream windows through dyadic observations, regular-sequence readback, $\CauchyProductUp$ budget, $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ compatibility, and the $\RealUp$ seal.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyProductCompletionUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\CauchyProductUp$, $\CauchyLimitSealUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyProductCompletionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The accepted carrier contains no ambient product set, no selected representative of a completion quotient, no unrestricted sequence equality, and no completion principle beyond the displayed finite-window route.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyProductCompletion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-product-completion-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted packet $Q=(S_0,S_1,D_0,D_1,B,R_0,R_1,L_0,L_1,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyProductCompletionUp}$ obligation surface consists exactly of carrier admission, classifier stability, budget factorization, completion-seal nonescape, and ledger exactness for the displayed rows $S_0,S_1,D_0,D_1,B,R_0,R_1,L_0,L_1,E,H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission reads the two $\StreamNameUp$ windows first, then admits the two $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observations only as finite approximants attached to those windows. The $\RegSeqRatUp$ rows certify the regular readback needed by the real-completion route.

Classifier stability is carried by $H$. Transporting stream names, dyadic observations, regular classifiers, product budget, limit seals, and the real export moves the whole packet together, so no coordinate can be reclassified by an external quotient equality.

Budget factorization is supplied by $B$ and replayed by $C$. The product-facing read reaches $\CauchyProductUp$ only after both coordinate windows and dyadic observations are displayed, and it reaches $\RealUp$ only after $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ compatibility has been exposed.

Completion-seal nonescape follows from the $L_0,L_1,E$ rows. They bind the coordinate reads to the L10 sibling routes of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyproduct-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchylimitseal-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}.

Ledger exactness is an inspection of the packet. Every exported read is one of the displayed rows, so the local NameCert has no coordinate from which to recover an ambient product object, a representative-choice operation, unrestricted sequence equality, or a completion principle outside the finite route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyProductCompletion budget factorization]
\label{thm:cauchy-product-completion-budget-factorization}
Every completion-facing product read exported by $\CauchyProductCompletionUp$ factors through $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\CauchyProductUp$, $\CauchyLimitSealUp$, and $\RealUp$ before export. \end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a completion-facing product read. By \autoref{def:cauchy-product-completion-carrier}, the only carrier rows available before export are $S_0,S_1,D_0,D_1,B,R_0,R_1,L_0,L_1,E,H,C,P,N$.

The first stage is the paired stream window. The rows $S_0,S_1$ force the read to pass through the $\StreamNameUp$ route before any dyadic observation is available, and $D_0,D_1$ then expose the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ approximants.

The second stage is regularization and budget. The rows $R_0,R_1$ record the $\RegSeqRatUp$ classifiers, while $B$ records the $\CauchyProductUp$ tolerance budget that combines the coordinate approximants.

The final stage is sealing and export. The rows $L_0,L_1$ expose $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ compatibility before $E$ exports a $\RealUp$ product-completion read. Since $H,C,P,N$ are the only structural rows, the export cannot bypass this route through an ambient product, selected completion representative, unrestricted sequence equality, or hidden real-completeness principle.
\end{proof}
