The $\OnticResidueLedgerUp$ packet realizes the representation-ledger boundary of \autoref{def:representation-ledger} as a finite NameCert carrier. It sits after the ontic-source surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-onticstate-namecert}, the audit rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-physical-model-audit-namecert}, the finite model-state carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-statespacemodel-namecert}, the observer-access support row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observer-budget-support-namecert}, and the current observer-state row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-consciousobserverstate-namecert}. Its role is to store signature exposure, observer access, model comparison, residue, transport, route replay, provenance, and local naming before any consumer treats a model packet as exhausting the ontic source.

\begin{definition}[Ontic residue ledger carrier]
\label{def:ontic-residue-ledger-carrier}
An $\OnticResidueLedgerUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(O,M,S,A,C,R,H,T,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $O$ is the accepted $\OnticStateUp$ source row whose reality-facing signature content is being read.
\item $M$ is the accepted $\PhysicalModelAuditUp$ row carrying the audited model signatures, source traces, classifier, failure surface, and strength rows.
\item $S$ is the accepted $\StateSpaceModelUp$ row naming the finite model-state packet offered as a representation.
\item $A$ is the observer-access row, read through $\ObserverBudgetSupportUp$ and $\ConsciousObserverStateUp$, that records which observer-local support surface may inspect the source and model signatures.
\item $C$ is the signature classifier row comparing the exposed ontic signatures from $O$ with the model-state and model-audit signatures from $S$ and $M$.
\item $R$ is the residue row recording source content, observer-access limits, failed model signatures, and audit-visible gaps not represented by $S$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $O,M,S,A,C,R,T,P,N$.
\item $T$ records displayed $\Cont$ routes from the ontic source to observer access, from observer access to model comparison, and from the accepted ledger to downstream adequacy or audit consumers.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\OnticResidueLedgerUp$, $\OnticStateUp$, $\PhysicalModelAuditUp$, $\StateSpaceModelUp$, $\ObserverBudgetSupportUp$, $\ConsciousObserverStateUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\OnticResidueLedgerUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for model-state exhaustion, host-level reality access, unbounded observation, detached observer authority, quotienting away residue, or audit acceptance outside the displayed packet.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Ontic residue ledger classifier]
\label{def:ontic-residue-ledger-classifier}
The $\OnticResidueLedgerUp$ classifier compares two accepted carriers only by the displayed rows $O,M,S,A,C,R,H,T,P,N$. It requires the ontic source row, model-audit row, model-state row, observer-access row, signature classifier row, and residue row to remain separately visible under componentwise $\hsame$ transport and displayed $\Cont$ replay.

Two carriers are therefore classified together only when they expose the same source signatures, the same audited model comparison surface, the same finite state-space representation, the same observer-access budget and current-state support, and the same residue ledger. Agreement of the model-state row alone is not enough.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Ontic residue ledger NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:ontic-residue-ledger-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:ontic-residue-ledger-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\OnticResidueLedgerUp}$ surface consists exactly of five finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier exposure for the packet $L=(O,M,S,A,C,R,H,T,P,N)$;
\item source-and-audit exposure through $O$ and $M$;
\item model-state and observer-access exposure through $S$ and $A$;
\item signature exactness and residue visibility through $C$ and $R$;
\item non-escape from model-state exhaustion, unbounded observation, detached observer authority, quotienting away residue, host-level reality access, and audit acceptance outside $H,T,P,N$.
\end{enumerate}
Thus the ledger is accepted only as a finite source, audit, model, observer-access, classifier, residue, transport, route, provenance, and local-name packet.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.OnticResidueLedgerUp.OnticResidueLedgerTasteGate\_single\_carrier\_alignment}
\begin{proof}
Carrier exposure is projection from the displayed tuple in \autoref{def:ontic-residue-ledger-carrier}. The rows $O,M,S,A,C,R$ supply the source, audit, model-state, observer-access, classifier, and residue coordinates, while $H,T,P,N$ supply transport, route replay, provenance, and local naming.

The source-and-audit obligation is read from $O$ and $M$. The model-state and observer-access obligation is read from $S$ and $A$, so a consumer cannot inspect the model representation without also recording the observer-local support surface that performs the inspection.

Signature exactness and residue visibility are then separated. The classifier row $C$ records the admitted comparison between exposed source signatures and model signatures; the residue row $R$ records what the state-space model and audit rows do not represent.

The final obligation follows by inspecting the remaining structural rows. Componentwise transport, route replay, provenance, and local naming can replay only the displayed packet, so they cannot add model-state exhaustion, unbounded observation, detached observer authority, host reality access, or a route that erases the residue row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Ontic residue ledger signature exactness]
\label{thm:ontic-residue-ledger-signature-exactness}
Every accepted $\OnticResidueLedgerUp$ packet reads signature exactness through the triple $(O,M,C)$ together with the model-state row $S$ and observer-access row $A$. The exactness claim is not a bare equality between the ontic source and $\StateSpaceModelUp$; it is a ledgered comparison whose residue row $R$ remains part of the accepted packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier as $L=(O,M,S,A,C,R,H,T,P,N)$. The row $O$ supplies the ontic source signatures, $M$ supplies the audited model signatures and failure surface, and $S$ supplies the finite state-space model offered as representation.

The observer-access row $A$ records how those signatures may be inspected from $\ObserverBudgetSupportUp$ and $\ConsciousObserverStateUp$ surfaces. The classifier $C$ can therefore compare only signatures that have passed through the displayed source, audit, model, and observer-access rows.

The residue row $R$ remains visible after the comparison. Consequently the exactness claim never collapses into source-model identity; it is the accepted comparison plus the displayed account of what the model row does not represent.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Ontic residue ledger nonescape]
\label{thm:ontic-residue-ledger-nonescape}
An accepted $\OnticResidueLedgerUp$ packet cannot be reduced to $\StateSpaceModelUp$ or to $\PhysicalModelAuditUp$ without losing either the observer-access row $A$ or the residue row $R$. Every consumer-visible read of the ledger factors through
$$
O,M,S,A,C,R,H,T,P,N.
$$
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.OnticResidueLedgerUp.OnticResidueLedgerObserverAccess\_row\_nonescape}
\begin{proof}
The state-space row $S$ records only the finite model-state representation. It does not contain the ontic source row $O$, the observer-access row $A$, the signature classifier row $C$, or the residue row $R$.

The physical audit row $M$ records finite audit evidence for a candidate model. It does not itself supply the full observer-access support row or the local ledger that keeps represented and unrepresented source content apart.

The remaining rows $H,T,P,N$ preserve the same displayed packet under transport, replay, provenance, and local naming. Since no row outside $O,M,S,A,C,R,H,T,P,N$ is exported, any reduction that forgets $A$ or $R$ leaves the accepted $\OnticResidueLedgerUp$ surface.
