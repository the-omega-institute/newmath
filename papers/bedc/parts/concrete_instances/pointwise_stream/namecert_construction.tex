Following Bishop and Bridges, the $\PointwiseStreamNameUp$ packet records the traditional countable pointwise name as finite BEDC NameCert data. It is the countable-name neighbour of the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}; its point values are read through the dyadic ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the regular-rational gateway of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}.

\begin{definition}[Pointwise stream name carrier]
\label{def:pointwise-stream-name-carrier}
A $\PointwiseStreamNameUp$ carrier is a finite NameCert packet
$$
Q=(S,I,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the certified $\StreamNameUp$ source row.
\item $I$ is the finite ledger of requested point indices and observation windows.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row for the inspected point values.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row generated from the pointwise observations.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal available only after the regular-rational route has been displayed.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for sources, indices, windows, dyadic ledgers, readbacks, seals, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ stores displayed $\Cont$ replay from a consumer request through $S,I,D,R,E,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is $\Pkg$ provenance over $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\PointwiseStreamNameUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only by these coordinates. It contains no quotient stream equality, selected limit, host real equality, countable-choice row, or $\RealUp$ completeness assertion.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Pointwise stream name finite read]
\label{thm:pointwise-stream-name-finite-read}
Every accepted $\PointwiseStreamNameUp$ read factors through the certified source $S$, requested index ledger $I$, dyadic tolerance row $D$, regular-rational readback $R$, and terminal $\RealUp$ seal $E$ before any completion-facing consumer may inspect it. The route is a pointwise countable-name source for $\RegSeqRatUp$ and $\RealUp$, not a quotient stream, selected limit, host equality, or completeness datum.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:pointwise-stream-name-carrier}. The request has only the certified stream source, index ledger, dyadic row, regular readback, Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming coordinates available.

The read begins at $S$ and $I$. A consumer must open the finite $\BHist$ windows for the requested point indices before it can inspect dyadic or regular-rational data.

The dyadic, regular, and Real rows are then consumed in order. Since no coordinate stores quotient equality, a selected limit, host equality, countable choice, or Real completeness, the pointwise read cannot export those data.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Pointwise stream name transport stability]
\label{thm:pointwise-stream-name-transport-stability}
Let $Q_0$ and $Q_1$ be accepted $\PointwiseStreamNameUp$ carriers. If a displayed $\hsame$ route transports their certified source rows, requested index ledgers, dyadic tolerance rows, regular-rational readbacks, Real seals, replay rows, provenance rows, and local naming rows componentwise, then every pointwise read accepted for $Q_0$ is accepted for $Q_1$ with the transported finite route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the componentwise transport coordinate $H$ in the two carriers. It identifies only the displayed StreamName source rows, requested windows, dyadic ledgers, regular readbacks, Real seals, replay rows, provenance rows, and local naming rows.

Replay the consumer request through $C$. The replay preserves the order of the pointwise route, so a transported read still reaches $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ only after the finite StreamName source and requested windows have been displayed.

The carrier inventory supplies the exclusion. Quotient streams, selected representatives, host real equality, countable-choice schedules, and completeness principles are not coordinates of either carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Pointwise stream name L10 gateway]
\label{thm:pointwise-stream-name-l10-gateway}
The $\PointwiseStreamNameUp$ packet is consumed by the L10 Real and Completion route only as a finite pointwise source: it sends certified windows to $\StreamNameUp$, dyadic tolerance rows to $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, regular readback rows to $\RegSeqRatUp$, and terminal seal rows to $\RealUp$. The gateway does not assert equality of quotient streams, choose a limiting real, identify host real objects, or prove $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

These two rows force every L10 read to pass through the same displayed source, index, dyadic, regular, Real, transport, replay, provenance, and naming coordinates.

The sibling chapters named in the opening paragraph supply the downstream rows. $\StreamNameUp$ supplies finite windows, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ supplies tolerance data, $\RegSeqRatUp$ supplies regular readback, and $\RealUp$ supplies the terminal seal.

Since the carrier lacks quotient equality, selected limits, host equality, choice, and completeness coordinates, the L10 gateway exports only the finite pointwise NameCert route.
\end{proof}
