\begin{definition}[Cauchy real equality carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-real-equality-carrier}

$$

with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $L_0$ and $L_1$ are the two $\RealUp$ seal rows. They record only the terminal real-name boundaries reached by the two compared regular names.
\item $S_0$ and $S_1$ are the paired $\StreamNameUp$ window rows. They expose the finite observation schedules inspected on the two sides.
\item $R_0$ and $R_1$ are the paired $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback rows. Each readback consumes its own stream window before the corresponding $\RealUp$ seal is available.
\item $D$ is the shared $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger. It records the dyadic precision requests and comparison budgets used to test the two regular readbacks at the same requested tolerance.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for both real seals, both stream windows, both regular readbacks, the dyadic ledger, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the comparison route
$$

\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyRealEqualityUp$, $\RealUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyRealEqualityUp}$ row.

\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy real equality NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-real-equality-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CauchyRealEqualityUp$ carrier
$$
E=(L_0,L_1,S_0,S_1,R_0,R_1,D,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyRealEqualityUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the two $\RealUp$ seals, paired stream windows, paired regular readbacks, shared dyadic tolerance ledger, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item two-sided window exposure, requiring every equality read to inspect $S_0$ and $S_1$ before reaching either regular readback;
\item regular-readback comparison, requiring $R_0$ and $R_1$ to be compared only at tolerance requests named by $D$;
\item Real-seal nonescape, requiring $L_0$ and $L_1$ to be read only after the paired regular readbacks and never as host real representatives;
\item provenance exhaustion, requiring $H,C,P,N$ to transport, replay, source, and name exactly the displayed rows and no quotient stream equality, host real equality, selected representative, countable-choice schedule, ambient completeness theorem, Lean verification, or bridge credential.
\end{enumerate}
No Cauchy-real-equality obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. Its displayed rows are the whole finite packet, so carrier admission is exactly the listed pair of real seals, the two stream windows, the two regular rational readbacks, the shared dyadic tolerance ledger, and the four structural rows.

The comparison first reads the windows $S_0$ and $S_1$. Those rows name the finite observations available on each side, so an equality consumer cannot begin from an unbounded stream.

The readbacks $R_0$ and $R_1$ are then compared only through $D$. The dyadic ledger supplies the common precision budget and prevents the comparison from appealing to an ambient metric or a host real value.

Finally $L_0,L_1,H,C,P,N$ close the route. The real seals are terminal rows reached through the regular readbacks, while transport, replay, provenance, and local naming preserve the same coordinates componentwise. Since the refused principles have no coordinate and no replay edge, the listed obligations exhaust the local certificate surface.
\end{proof}
