The $\BetaCriticalPairUp$ packet concretizes the beta audit map of \autoref{ch:metacic-beta-audit-map} as a finite local-overlap certificate. It sits beside the closed-normal route certificate of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closed-normal-route-certificate-namecert}, the beta-reduction audit trail of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-betareductionaudittrail-namecert}, and the beta-step boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-betastepboundary-namecert}: those siblings display normal-route, ordered audit, and step-boundary surfaces, while this packet displays two competing beta branches and the common-reduct witness that a local-join consumer may read.

\begin{definition}[Beta critical-pair carrier]
\label{def:beta-critical-pair-carrier}
A $\BetaCriticalPairUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(O,L,R,J,B,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $O$ is the local-overlap row identifying the shared redex context and the finite neighborhood in which the two beta branches are compared;
\item $L$ is the left branch $\mathrm{BetaStep}$ readback from the overlap row;
\item $R$ is the right branch $\mathrm{BetaStep}$ readback from the same overlap row;
\item $J$ is the displayed common-reduct witness, read only after both branch rows have been displayed;
\item $B$ is the consumer-boundary row separating local joining from subject reduction, closed confluence, normalization, or typed preservation;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the overlap, branch, common-reduct, boundary, route, provenance, and name rows;
\item $C$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which a consumer reads $O$, then the two branch readbacks $L,R$, then the common-reduct witness $J$, and finally the boundary row $B$;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over \autoref{ch:metacic-beta-audit-map}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closed-normal-route-certificate-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-betareductionaudittrail-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-betastepboundary-namecert}, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\BetaCriticalPairUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for subject reduction, closed Church--Rosser, global confluence, normalization, host evaluation, or a hidden join not displayed by $J$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Beta critical-pair NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:beta-critical-pair-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:beta-critical-pair-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\BetaCriticalPairUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $K=(O,L,R,J,B,H,C,P,N)$, left branch readback through $L$, right branch readback through $R$, common-reduct witness readback through $J$, local non-escape through $B$, displayed continuation replay through $C$, componentwise classifier transport through $H$, package provenance through $P$, and terminal naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.BetaCriticalPairUp.BetaCriticalPairNameCertObligations}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:beta-critical-pair-carrier}. The overlap row $O$ is the only source row, and the branch rows $L$ and $R$ are both read from that same displayed neighborhood.

The common-reduct row $J$ is downstream of the two branch rows. A consumer cannot read a joined endpoint before both branch readbacks have been displayed, because the continuation row $C$ records the route through $O,L,R,J$ in that order.

The local boundary row $B$ then blocks escape from local joining into subject reduction, closed confluence, normalization, typed preservation, or host evaluation. The rows $H,P,N$ transport, source, and name this same finite surface without adding a hidden join coordinate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Beta critical-pair local-join boundary]
\label{thm:beta-critical-pair-local-join-boundary}
Every accepted $\BetaCriticalPairUp$ packet exposes a local join only through the displayed branch rows $L,R$ and the common-reduct witness row $J$. A consumer may use the packet to read the common endpoint of the two local beta branches, but it may not reread the packet as subject reduction, closed Church--Rosser, normalization, conversion typing, or a global confluence theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with an accepted packet $K=(O,L,R,J,B,H,C,P,N)$. The two branch rows share the same overlap row $O$, so the packet is a local-overlap certificate rather than an arbitrary pair of unrelated beta steps.

The join endpoint is exactly the row $J$. Since $J$ is a displayed coordinate, local joining is falsifiable by inspecting the carrier: if the row is absent, the joined endpoint is absent from the packet.

Finally read the boundary row $B$. It records the non-escape side of the carrier and prevents any consumer from replacing the local common-reduct witness by subject reduction, global confluence, normalization, conversion typing, or host evaluation.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Beta critical-pair local-join route]
\label{thm:beta-critical-pair-local-join-route}
Every beta-audit consumer reading a $\BetaCriticalPairUp$ packet must pass through the shared overlap row $O$, both branch rows $L,R$, the displayed common-reduct witness $J$, and the non-escape boundary $B$ before any local-join conclusion is available. The route realizes the finite local-overlap and common-reduct surface of \autoref{ch:metacic-beta-audit-map} and is constrained by \autoref{thm:beta-critical-pair-namecert-obligations} and \autoref{thm:beta-critical-pair-local-join-boundary}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:beta-critical-pair-namecert-obligations}. They expose the shared overlap row $O$, the left and right branch readbacks $L,R$, the common-reduct witness $J$, and the local boundary row $B$ as distinct displayed coordinates of the same carrier.

Apply \autoref{thm:beta-critical-pair-local-join-boundary}. It permits a local join only through the branch rows and the common-reduct row, while $B$ blocks rereading that local fact as subject reduction, closed Church--Rosser, normalization, conversion typing, or global confluence.

The beta audit map of \autoref{ch:metacic-beta-audit-map} is therefore reached through the finite route $O,L,R,J,B$ and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$. No consumer can obtain a local-join conclusion from the packet before those overlap, branch, witness, and boundary rows have been displayed.
\end{proof}
