\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CompleteMetricCriterionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-complete-metric-criterion-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges constructive analysis, the
$\CompleteMetricCriterionUp$ packet records the finite Cauchy-limit criterion
needed before an L10 completion route can hand a Cauchy object to a metric or
real-facing consumer. It sits between the filter-base and Cauchy-filter
surfaces of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-filterbase-namecert} and
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyfilter-namecert}, the completion boundary
of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyfiltercompletion-namecert}, the
metric completion surface of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metriccompletion-namecert}, the finite stream
windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the dyadic
tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert},
the regular rational readback of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal real-name
seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet names the
criterion as finite BEDC data; it does not quotient filters, select limits,
use host equality, invoke countable choice, or assume ambient completeness.

\begin{definition}[CompleteMetricCriterion carrier]
\label{def:complete-metric-criterion-carrier}
A $\CompleteMetricCriterionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(C,W,T,R,M,L,H,K_0,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $C$ is the $\CauchyFilterUp$ row read through a displayed
  $\FilterBaseUp$ source and the finite requests admitted by that base;
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule selecting the finite windows used
  when a Cauchy request asks for tail or neighbourhood evidence;
\item $T$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger attached to the same
  finite requests;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback assembled from the scheduled windows
  and dyadic tolerances;
\item $M$ is the $\MetricCompletionUp$ or $\CauchyFilterCompletionUp$
  completion-boundary row exposed only after $C,W,T,R$ have been replayed;
\item $L$ is the candidate $\RealUp$ limit seal read from the displayed
  completion route;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for Cauchy-filter,
  filter-base, window, tolerance, readback, completion, real-seal,
  provenance, and naming rows;
\item $K_0$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the route
$$
C,W,T,R \longmapsto M \longmapsto L ;
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CompleteMetricCriterionUp$,
  $\CauchyFilterUp$, $\FilterBaseUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$,
  $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\MetricCompletionUp$, $\CauchyFilterCompletionUp$,
  $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CompleteMetricCriterionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through
$C,W,T,R,M,L,H,K_0,P,N$. There is no coordinate for quotient filters, selected
representatives, selected limits, host equality, countable-choice schedules,
or ambient completeness of the metric or real carrier.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CompleteMetricCriterion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:complete-metric-criterion-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CompleteMetricCriterionUp$ carrier
$K=(C,W,T,R,M,L,H,K_0,P,N)$, the local
$\NameCert_{\CompleteMetricCriterionUp}$ surface consists exactly of five
obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed Cauchy-filter, filter-base, window,
  tolerance, readback, completion, real-seal, transport, replay, provenance,
  and naming rows;
\item Cauchy-window exactness, requiring every Cauchy request to be witnessed
  by the same $C,W,T,R$ rows before completion data are read;
\item completion handoff, requiring the $\MetricCompletionUp$ or
  $\CauchyFilterCompletionUp$ row $M$ to be downstream from the displayed
  Cauchy route;
\item Real seal nonescape, requiring the limit row $L$ to be read only after
  $M$ and never through quotient filters, selected limits, host equality,
  countable choice, or ambient completeness;
\item provenance and local naming, requiring $H,K_0,P,N$ to transport,
  replay, package, and name the same finite route.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from
\autoref{def:complete-metric-criterion-carrier}. The packet lists exactly the
Cauchy-filter, filter-base, finite-window, dyadic tolerance, regular readback,
completion, real-seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

Cauchy-window exactness is read from $C,W,T,R$. A Cauchy request first
consumes the displayed filter row and finite schedule, then checks the dyadic
tolerance ledger, and only then exposes the regular rational readback.

The completion handoff is the row $M$. Since $M$ is downstream from
$C,W,T,R$, a completion-facing consumer receives the finite Cauchy route
rather than a quotient filter or chosen representative.

The real seal $L$ is downstream from $M$ and is preserved by $H$ and $K_0$.
The provenance and local naming rows $P,N$ therefore certify exactly the
displayed finite route, while the excluded host principles have no carrier
coordinate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CompleteMetricCriterion Cauchy-limit handoff]
\label{thm:complete-metric-criterion-cauchy-limit-handoff}
Every limit-facing read exported by an accepted
$\CompleteMetricCriterionUp$ carrier factors through the finite route
$$
C,W,T,R \longmapsto M \longmapsto L \longmapsto H,K_0,P,N .
$$
The route supplies the Cauchy-filter and filter-base request, finite stream
window, dyadic tolerance ledger, regular rational readback, completion
boundary, real-seal row, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming. It
refuses quotient filters, selected limits, host equality, countable choice,
and ambient completeness as routes to the same limit.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted carrier
$K=(C,W,T,R,M,L,H,K_0,P,N)$. By
\autoref{def:complete-metric-criterion-carrier}, the only route to a
completion-facing read begins with $C,W,T,R$.

The row $M$ is the sole completion boundary. It records the
$\MetricCompletionUp$ or $\CauchyFilterCompletionUp$ handoff after the finite
Cauchy route has been displayed, so the consumer cannot replace the route by
a quotient filter or a selected completion point.

The row $L$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal. Transport through $H$ and replay
through $K_0$ keep $L$ attached to the same finite request, and $P,N$ package
that route as the local certificate.

Since the carrier has no coordinate for host equality, countable choice, or
ambient completeness, the handoff proves exactly the finite route stated in
the theorem.
\end{proof}
