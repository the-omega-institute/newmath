\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\MulUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-mul-namecert}
\origin{human}

The multiplication interface $\MulUp$ is the repeated-addition consumer of the unary natural-number spine. It sits after $\NatUp$ and $\AddUp$ in the arithmetic chain, uses the concrete history monoid route of $\MonoidUp$, and supplies the multiplication row consumed by the divisibility and prime-number surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-prime-namecert}.

\section{Unary product rows}

\begin{definition}[MulUp unary product carrier]
\label{def:mul-unary-product-carrier}
The carrier for $\MulUp$ consists of finite product rows
$$
\mathsf{MulRow}(d,q,n,\lambda)
$$
where $d$, $q$, and $n$ are $\NatUp$ unary histories, $\lambda$ is a finite additive iteration ledger, and the ledger starts at $\emp$, reads the multiplier $q$ one unary step at a time, and at each visible step applies the $\AddUp$ continuation row that adds one further copy of $d$ to the current partial product. The final ledger endpoint is the result row $n$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[MulUp unary product classifier]
\label{def:mul-unary-product-classifier}
Two $\MulUp$ product rows $\mathsf{MulRow}(d,q,n,\lambda)$ and $\mathsf{MulRow}(d',q',n',\lambda')$ are classified together when the two factor rows satisfy $d\hsame d'$ and $q\hsame q'$, the result rows satisfy $n\hsame n'$, and the two additive iteration ledgers replay the same finite sequence of $\AddUp$ continuation steps after transport along those $\hsame$ comparisons.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[MulUp unary product NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:mul-unary-product-namecert-obligations}
The carrier and classifier of \autoref{def:mul-unary-product-carrier} and \autoref{def:mul-unary-product-classifier} supply the local $\NameCert_{\MulUp}$ obligation surface over $\mathsf{BHist}$: the empty multiplier row is inhabited by the endpoint $\emp$, classifier reflexivity, symmetry, and transitivity are inherited from $\hsame$ on the displayed factor and result histories, and transport of a product row along classified factors and result histories replays the same finite additive ledger.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For inhabitance, take any unary multiplicand $d$ and the empty multiplier. The additive iteration ledger has no visible addition step and ends at $\emp$, so it is an accepted product row.

The classifier clauses are componentwise. Reflexivity, symmetry, and transitivity use the corresponding $\hsame$ facts on the two factors and the result, while the ledger component is compared by replaying the same finite list of $\AddUp$ rows after those transports.

For carrier transport, push $d\hsame d'$, $q\hsame q'$, and $n\hsame n'$ through the source, counter, and endpoint coordinates of the ledger. Each intermediate row remains a $\NatUp$ unary history, and each additive step remains an $\AddUp$ continuation row, so the transported tuple is again a $\MulUp$ product row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MulUp additive route]
\label{thm:mul-unary-product-additive-route}
Every accepted $\MulUp$ row factors through the unary source of $\NatUp$, the continuation-addition row of $\AddUp$, and the concrete history monoid route of $\MonoidUp$. Equivalently, multiplication is not a new kernel operation: it is a finite $\Cont$ replay in which the multiplier ledger schedules repeated $\AddUp$ applications over unary histories.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project an accepted $\MulUp$ row to its carrier data. The displayed factors and result are unary histories, so the source side is already inside the $\NatUp$ carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-nat-namecert}.

Read the ledger next. Its base row is the empty product endpoint, and every visible multiplier step is an $\AddUp$ continuation row that appends one copy of the multiplicand to the current partial product. Thus the only operation used in the computation is the addition certificate of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-add-namecert}.

Finally, the ledger concatenation of successive continuation rows is exactly the concrete unary-history monoid route displayed by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-monoid-namecert}. Repacking these three projections gives the claimed factorization and leaves no independent multiplication primitive.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MulUp unary product recursion laws]
\label{thm:mul-unary-product-recursion-laws}
Every accepted row $\mathsf{MulRow}(d,q,n,\lambda)$ satisfies the unary recursion laws for multiplication. When the visible multiplier ledger is empty, the product endpoint is the carrier endpoint $\emp$. When the visible multiplier ledger has successor form, the row decomposes into the previous product row followed by one $\AddUp$ continuation row that adds the multiplicand $d$ to the previous partial product.

These endpoint and successor laws are stable under componentwise $\hsame$ transport of the displayed multiplicand, multiplier, and product histories, and they are stable under replay of the stored $\Cont$ ledger. Thus $\MulUp$ remains a finite repeated-addition route through $\NatUp$, $\AddUp$, $\MonoidUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$, rather than a separate primitive operation.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the visible unary multiplier ledger in the accepted row. In the empty case there is no displayed multiplier step, so the product row is exactly the endpoint row already present in the carrier, namely $\emp$.

In the successor case the final visible multiplier step can be peeled from the finite ledger. The prefix ledger is the previous product row, and the peeled terminal row is an $\AddUp$ continuation adding one copy of the multiplicand to that previous partial product.

Transport is componentwise. The classifier supplies the $\hsame$ comparisons on the multiplicand, multiplier, and product histories, and continuation replay sends each stored additive row to the transported additive row. Therefore the endpoint and successor decompositions persist after transport and after replay of the same finite ledger.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MulUp finite data boundary]
\label{thm:mul-unary-product-finite-data-boundary}
The $\MulUp$ chapter exports only the finite unary product rows of \autoref{def:mul-unary-product-carrier}, their classifier of \autoref{def:mul-unary-product-classifier}, the local $\NameCert_{\MulUp}$ obligations of \autoref{thm:mul-unary-product-namecert-obligations}, the additive route of \autoref{thm:mul-unary-product-additive-route}, and the recursion laws of \autoref{thm:mul-unary-product-recursion-laws}. The downstream divisibility and $\PrimeUp$ multiplication consumer may therefore read $\mathsf{NatMul}$ rows through this repeated-addition boundary, while full semiring or ring algebra, divisibility completeness, and standard bridge checking remain outside this local certificate surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The exported rows are exactly the carrier, classifier, NameCert obligations, route theorem, and recursion-law theorem just listed. Each row is a finite object: two factor histories, one result history, and a finite additive ledger whose entries are $\AddUp$ continuation steps over $\NatUp$ unary histories.

The divisibility and prime-number consumer in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-prime-namecert} reads the same multiplication relation through the $\mathsf{NatMul}$ surface, in particular the semantic exit and phase boundary of \autoref{thm:unary-multiplication-mulup-semantic-exit-package} and \autoref{thm:mulup-full-nat-arithmetic-phase-exit-boundary}. Those consumer rows add divisibility, order, and audit-clean arithmetic boundaries, but they do not change the local repeated-addition carrier defined here.

Since none of the four local exports contains a semiring carrier, a ring carrier, a quotient completion, a divisibility-completeness theorem, or a checked standard bridge, those structures are not claimed by this chapter.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MulUp local-to-Full Nat Arithmetic handoff]
\label{thm:mulup-local-to-full-nat-arithmetic-handoff}
The local exports of \autoref{def:mul-unary-product-carrier}, \autoref{def:mul-unary-product-classifier}, \autoref{thm:mul-unary-product-namecert-obligations}, \autoref{thm:mul-unary-product-additive-route}, \autoref{thm:mul-unary-product-recursion-laws}, and \autoref{thm:mul-unary-product-finite-data-boundary} form precisely the $\MulUp$ input face read by the mature Full Nat Arithmetic boundary of \autoref{thm:mulup-full-nat-arithmetic-mature-boundary}. The checked $\mathsf{NatMul}$ semantic exit, $\AddUp$ distributivity rows, $\OrderUp$ monotonicity row, and divisibility-order consumer rows are downstream consumer rows over that face; they do not add new local carrier coordinates to the repeated-addition certificate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the local tuple to its carrier, classifier, NameCert obligations, additive route, recursion laws, and finite-data boundary. These projections expose only unary histories, componentwise $\hsame$ comparisons, and finite $\Cont$ ledgers whose visible steps are $\AddUp$ continuation rows.

Use the additive route through $\NatUp$, $\AddUp$, and $\MonoidUp$ to identify the product relation as repeated addition over the same $\mathsf{BHist}$ spine. The recursion laws give the empty-multiplier endpoint and successor-step decomposition, while the finite-data boundary restricts the local chapter to those rows and rules out host multiplication or a second carrier.

Finally apply the Prime-side mature boundary of \autoref{thm:mulup-full-nat-arithmetic-mature-boundary}. That boundary consumes the projected face by adding the checked $\mathsf{NatMul}$ semantic exit, the $\AddUp$ distribution rows, the $\OrderUp$ monotonicity row, and the divisibility-order rows as consumer structure over the same carrier and classifier. Since these rows are introduced only at the consumer boundary, the local $\MulUp$ chapter keeps exactly the repeated-addition coordinates listed above.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MulUp unary multiplication law bundle]
\label{thm:mulup-unary-multiplication-law-bundle}
The concrete unary product carrier over $\NatUp$, $\AddUp$, $\MonoidUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ supplies exactly the public $\MulUp$ multiplication laws used by downstream arithmetic consumers: totality on unary factors, unary closure of results, functionality up to $\hsame$, left and right unit laws for $\Eone(\emp)$, commutativity up to $\hsame$, and associativity up to $\hsame$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the local repeated-addition surface. The carrier of \autoref{def:mul-unary-product-carrier} and the classifier of \autoref{def:mul-unary-product-classifier} provide the displayed product rows and the componentwise $\hsame$ comparison used to identify equal results.

The local certificate obligations of \autoref{thm:mul-unary-product-namecert-obligations} and the recursion laws of \autoref{thm:mul-unary-product-recursion-laws} give the finite endpoint and successor-step account of product formation. The handoff theorem \autoref{thm:mulup-local-to-full-nat-arithmetic-handoff} identifies this local face as the one consumed by the checked Full Nat Arithmetic boundary.

The Prime-side $\mathsf{NatMul}$ rows then package the public laws over that face. Their checked bundle states totality for unary factors, unary result closure, functionality, the two $\Eone(\emp)$ unit laws, commutativity, and associativity, all with the result comparisons expressed by $\hsame$. Repacking those rows yields precisely the announced $\MulUp$ law bundle.
\end{proof}
