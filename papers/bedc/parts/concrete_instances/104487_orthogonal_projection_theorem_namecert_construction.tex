\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\OrthogonalProjectionTheoremUp$}

Following Riesz--Fischer/Hilbert-space projection theorem treatments, the $\OrthogonalProjectionTheoremUp$ packet records the finite theorem-facing surface of orthogonal projection data without importing the full classical projection theorem as a host principle. It sits over the Hilbert carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-hilbert-namecert}, the inner-product rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-innerproduct-namecert}, the norm surface of $\NormUp$, the Bessel-bound route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bessel-inequality-namecert}, and the vector-space operations of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-vecspace-namecert}. The singleton projection row of \autoref{def:hilbert-singleton-orthogonal-projection-row} is the checked sibling surface: this chapter names the general finite packet that a theorem-facing projection consumer must read before any closed-subspace or minimizer claim can be exported.

\begin{definition}[Orthogonal projection theorem carrier]\label{def:orthogonal-projection-theorem-carrier}
An $\OrthogonalProjectionTheoremUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
(H,V,I,Nm,B,A,Q,R,O,T,C,P,N)
$$
whose visible rows are:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $H$ is the displayed $\HilbertUp$ source carrier, read through its finite $\NameCert_{\HilbertUp}$ rows;
\item $V$ is the $\VecSpaceUp$ reduct carrying addition, subtraction, scalar action, and the displayed affine subspace operations used by the projection row;
\item $I$ is the $\InnerProductUp$ row over the Hilbert source, and $Nm$ is the associated $\NormUp$ row used to state the distance-minimizing comparison;
\item $B$ is the $\BesselInequalityUp$ dependency row that bounds the finite orthogonal comparison ledger;
\item $A$ is the displayed closed-affine constraint row for the target subspace surface visible to the packet;
\item $Q$ is the candidate projection endpoint row;
\item $R$ is the residual row $x-Q$ formed through the $\VecSpaceUp$ operations;
\item $O$ is the orthogonality classifier asserting that the residual row is orthogonal to the displayed subspace directions through $I$;
\item $T$ is the minimality comparison row stating that scheduled norm comparisons through $Nm$ factor through the same residual and orthogonality ledger;
\item $C,P,N$ are the consumer replay, provenance, and local $\NameCert_{\OrthogonalProjectionTheoremUp}$ rows.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a host closed-subspace type, no choice-selected minimizer, no quotient Hilbert equality, no global Riesz representation theorem, and no ambient completeness argument beyond the displayed $\HilbertUp$ rows.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Orthogonal projection theorem NameCert obligations]\label{thm:orthogonal-projection-theorem-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\OrthogonalProjectionTheoremUp$ carrier $(H,V,I,Nm,B,A,Q,R,O,T,C,P,N)$, the local NameCert obligation surface consists of the following finite rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the Hilbert source $H$, vector-space reduct $V$, inner-product row $I$, norm row $Nm$, Bessel-bound row $B$, displayed constraint row $A$, candidate projection $Q$, residual $R$, orthogonality classifier $O$, minimality comparison $T$, replay $C$, provenance $P$, and naming row $N$;
\item dependency admission through $\HilbertUp$, $\InnerProductUp$, $\NormUp$, $\BesselInequalityUp$, and $\VecSpaceUp$;
\item residual formation, stating that $R$ is built from the displayed vector-space operations and the candidate endpoint $Q$;
\item orthogonality classification, stating that $O$ reads only the inner-product rows visible through $I$ and the finite directions named by $A$;
\item Bessel-controlled comparison, stating that $B$ bounds the finite orthogonal ledger before $T$ records the norm-minimality comparison;
\item singleton compatibility, stating that the Hilbert singleton projection row of \autoref{def:hilbert-singleton-orthogonal-projection-row} is an admitted special case of the same residual, orthogonality, and norm-readback pattern;
\item nonescape, stating that the packet exports no host projection operator, no selected minimizer outside the displayed rows, no closed-subspace completeness theorem, and no quotient Hilbert equality.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:orthogonal-projection-theorem-carrier}. Its coordinates list exactly the Hilbert source, vector-space reduct, inner product, norm, Bessel ledger, constraint row, candidate endpoint, residual, orthogonality classifier, norm comparison, replay, provenance, and local name.

The dependency rows are finite because each is read from a named sibling surface. The $\HilbertUp$ row supplies the source carrier, $\InnerProductUp$ supplies the orthogonality observations, $\NormUp$ supplies the distance comparison, $\BesselInequalityUp$ supplies the finite bound ledger, and $\VecSpaceUp$ supplies the algebraic residual operations.

Residual formation and orthogonality are then local rows. The residual $R$ is formed by the displayed vector operations, while $O$ can inspect only inner-product entries named by $I$ and directions named by $A$. The minimality row $T$ reads the same norm row $Nm$ after the Bessel ledger has fixed the finite orthogonal comparison surface.

For the sibling check, specialize to the Hilbert singleton projection row of \autoref{def:hilbert-singleton-orthogonal-projection-row}. There the candidate endpoint, residual, norm readback, and inner-product readback are all the displayed singleton zero rows, so the general packet pattern collapses to the existing finite certificate surface.

Since every row is one of these displayed coordinates, no host projection operator, chosen minimizer, quotient equality, or external closed-subspace theorem can be read from the packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Orthogonal projection theorem Hilbert handoff]\label{thm:orthogonal-projection-theorem-hilbert-handoff}
Every Hilbert-facing consumer of an $\OrthogonalProjectionTheoremUp$ packet must read the displayed Hilbert source $H$, vector-space reduct $V$, inner-product row $I$, norm row $Nm$, Bessel-bound row $B$, constraint row $A$, candidate projection $Q$, residual row $R$, orthogonality classifier $O$, and norm-minimality comparison $T$ before exporting a projection-theorem conclusion. The handoff is therefore a finite NameCert route through $\HilbertUp$, $\InnerProductUp$, $\NormUp$, $\BesselInequalityUp$, and $\VecSpaceUp$, not a claim of the full host projection theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a consumer asking for projection-theorem data over a displayed Hilbert source. By \autoref{def:orthogonal-projection-theorem-carrier}, the only available Hilbert data are the rows $H,V,I,Nm$ and the finite theorem-facing rows $B,A,Q,R,O,T,C,P,N$.

Apply the obligation surface of \autoref{thm:orthogonal-projection-theorem-namecert-obligations}. It forces residual formation through $V$, orthogonality through $I$, bounded comparison through $B$, and norm-minimality readback through $Nm$ before the consumer can reach the local name.

The checked singleton sibling in \autoref{def:hilbert-singleton-orthogonal-projection-row} shows that this route has a concrete Hilbert projection instance, but it does not promote the packet to a global closed-subspace projection theorem. The handoff exports only the displayed finite rows and excludes host choice, quotient Hilbert equality, and non-displayed completeness principles.
\end{proof}
