Following Heine and Borel's interval compactness theorem as read through Bishop--Bridges constructive analysis, the $\ConstructiveHeineBorelIntervalUp$ packet records a finite dyadic cover-refinement ledger over a closed bounded $\RealUp$ interval. It sits over the closed bounded interval source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closedboundedinterval-namecert}, the dyadic arithmetic core of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the compact-metric carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, and the uniform-modulus source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformmodulus-namecert}. The chapter names finite dyadic cover and refinement data for the active L10 Real and Completion frontier; it is not a general open-cover compactness theorem, an unrestricted Heine--Borel principle, a selected-subcover functional, or an ambient $\RealUp$ completeness assertion.

\begin{definition}[Constructive Heine--Borel interval carrier]
\label{def:constructive-heine-borel-interval-carrier}
A $\ConstructiveHeineBorelIntervalUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(I,D,R,S,E,M,U,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $I$ is the $\ClosedBoundedIntervalUp$ source row, exposing endpoint order, interval membership, and the interval-source readback.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger for dyadic endpoints, mesh scales, refinement inclusions, and positive slack comparisons.
\item $R$ is the finite refinement row, listing the dyadic partition cells and the parent-child refinement map used by cover readers.
\item $S$ is the finite subcover ledger, recording the selected cell witnesses already displayed by the cover-refinement row.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ endpoint and radius seal reached only after $I,D,R,S$ have been displayed.
\item $M$ is the $\CompactMetricUp$ compatibility row, read only as the metric finite-net surface induced by the same interval and dyadic ledger.
\item $U$ is the $\UniformModulusUp$ compatibility row, recording the modulus-facing refinement scale consumed by uniform-continuity readers over the same packet.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the interval source, dyadic ledger, refinement row, subcover ledger, Real seal, compact-metric row, uniform-modulus row, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a dyadic cover request through $I,D,R,S,E,M,U$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ConstructiveHeineBorelIntervalUp$, $\ClosedBoundedIntervalUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\UniformModulusUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ConstructiveHeineBorelIntervalUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $I,D,R,S,E,M,U,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for arbitrary open covers, choice-selected subcovers, quotient interval representatives, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Constructive Heine--Borel interval NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:constructive-heine-borel-interval-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\ConstructiveHeineBorelIntervalUp$ carrier $K=(I,D,R,S,E,M,U,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\ConstructiveHeineBorelIntervalUp}$ surface consists exactly of five finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the closed bounded interval source, dyadic endpoint ledger, finite refinement row, finite subcover ledger, Real seal, compact-metric compatibility row, uniform-modulus compatibility row, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item cover-refinement classifier stability, requiring two accepted reads to agree only through $I,D,R,H,C,P,N$ before their refinement cells are compared;
\item finite-subcover ledger exactness, requiring each exported witness in $S$ to be one of the displayed finite cover reads attached to a dyadic cell of $R$;
\item dyadic endpoint transport, requiring endpoint and mesh comparisons to pass through $D$ before reaching the terminal $\RealUp$ seal $E$;
\item nonescape from selected subcovers, countable choice, and ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, requiring $M,U,H,C,P,N$ to transport, replay, source, and name only the displayed finite packet.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:constructive-heine-borel-interval-carrier}. The tuple displays exactly the closed bounded interval source, dyadic endpoint ledger, finite refinement row, finite subcover ledger, Real seal, compact-metric compatibility row, uniform-modulus compatibility row, and structural rows.

Classifier stability is local to the refinement ledger. Two accepted reads can compare cells only after the interval row $I$, dyadic row $D$, and refinement row $R$ have been displayed and transported through $H,C,P,N$.

The finite-subcover ledger is exact because $S$ contains only the witnesses attached to the listed cells of $R$. There is no row carrying an arbitrary cover family or a host selector for additional witnesses.

Endpoint transport is dyadic before it is Real-facing. The row $D$ records endpoint, mesh, refinement, and slack comparisons, and $E$ seals only those endpoint and radius reads already attached to $I,D,R,S$.

Finally $M$ and $U$ are compatibility rows over the same finite data, while $H,C,P,N$ preserve and name the displayed route. Since selected subcovers, countable choice, and ambient real completeness have no carrier coordinate, the five obligations exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Constructive Heine--Borel interval finite-subcover ledger]
\label{thm:constructive-heine-borel-interval-finite-subcover-ledger}
Every compactness-facing or completion-facing consumer of an accepted $\ConstructiveHeineBorelIntervalUp$ packet reads the finite route
$$
\ClosedBoundedIntervalUp
\longmapsto
\DyadicRatCoreUp
\longmapsto
\CompactMetricUp
\longmapsto
\UniformModulusUp
\longmapsto

$$
through $I,D,R,S,E,M,U,H,C,P,N$. The exported subcover row is exactly the finite ledger $S$ transported from the dyadic refinement row $R$ to the terminal endpoint and radius seal $E$; it is not general open-cover compactness, a selected subcover functional, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the obligation surface of \autoref{thm:constructive-heine-borel-interval-namecert-obligations}. Every cover-facing read must display the closed bounded interval source, the dyadic endpoint ledger, the refinement row, and the finite subcover ledger.

The route then passes through the compatibility rows. The compact-metric row $M$ reads the same finite dyadic cells as a finite-net surface, and the uniform-modulus row $U$ reads the same refinement scale for uniform-continuity consumers.

The Real-facing endpoint seal $E$ is reached only after those finite rows have been displayed. Transport through $H$, replay through $C$, provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$ preserve that order.

Thus the exported row is the finite ledger already recorded in $S$. The refused open-cover, selected-subcover, countable-choice, and ambient-completeness readings have no row in the packet.
\end{proof}
