\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CauchyMetastabilityUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-metastability-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Tao's finite convergence principle and Bishop--Bridges constructive Cauchy control, metastability is treated here as a finite inspected interval certificate, not as a selected limit. The $\CauchyMetastabilityUp$ packet sits between the finite stream observations of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the modulus surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchymodulus-namecert}, the Cauchy sequence carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchysequencespace-namecert}, and the terminal seal boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. It names the displayed route by which a control functional and a starting index expose only the finite window $[N,G(N)]$ before a $\RealUp$ seal is read.

\begin{definition}[Cauchy metastability carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-metastability-carrier}
A $\CauchyMetastabilityUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
M=(G,N,S,D,R,E,H,C,P,L)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $G$ is the control-functional row, recording the finite rule that sends a unary starting index to the terminal index $G(N)$.
\item $N$ is the starting unary index row.
\item $S$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window family over the interval $[N,G(N)]$.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger used to compare the inspected dyadic reads across the displayed window.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row, recording the regular rational Cauchy observations exposed by $S$ and checked against $D$.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after $G,N,S,D,R$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the control functional, starting index, finite windows, dyadic ledger, regular readback, Real seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a metastable tolerance request through $G,N,S,D,R,E,H,P,L$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyMetastabilityUp$, $\CauchyModulusUp$, $\CauchySequenceSpaceUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $L$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyMetastabilityUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for selected limits, quotient stream equality, host real equality, countable choice, trichotomy, open-cover compactness, ambient Real completeness, or a standard bridge.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy metastability NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-metastability-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CauchyMetastabilityUp$ carrier $M=(G,N,S,D,R,E,H,C,P,L)$ of \autoref{def:cauchy-metastability-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyMetastabilityUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the control-functional row, starting unary index, finite $\StreamNameUp$ interval windows, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, $\RealUp$ seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item interval binding, requiring $S$ to display only windows indexed by the finite interval $[N,G(N)]$;
\item modulus compatibility, requiring the interval bound and tolerance ledger to be read through the $\CauchyModulusUp$ surface before any regular Cauchy readback is accepted;
\item Cauchy-space anchoring, requiring the readback row $R$ to be interpreted as a displayed Cauchy-sequence observation over \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchysequencespace-namecert};
\item finite-window stability, requiring transport through $H$ and replay through $C$ to preserve the same $G,N,S,D,R$ route;
\item Real seal non-escape, requiring the boundary row $E$ to be consumed only after the finite route through $G,N,S,D,R$ has been retained;
\item package provenance and local naming, stating that $P$ names exactly the sibling certificates of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchymodulus-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchysequencespace-namecert}, while $L$ names the same finite packet locally.
\end{enumerate}
No local NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-metastability-carrier}. The analytic coordinates are the control-functional row $G$, the starting index $N$, the finite window family $S$, the dyadic ledger $D$, the regular readback $R$, and the seal row $E$; the structural coordinates are $H,C,P,L$.

Read the interval obligation first. The row $G$ takes the displayed starting index $N$ to the finite endpoint $G(N)$, and $S$ stores only the $\StreamNameUp$ observations over the interval $[N,G(N)]$.

Read the Cauchy data next. The tolerance ledger $D$ supplies the dyadic comparison rows, and the readback $R$ supplies the $\RegSeqRatUp$ observations that \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchysequencespace-namecert} admits as Cauchy-sequence data under the displayed modulus route.

The structural rows preserve this dependency order. Transport through $H$ is componentwise, replay through $C$ repeats the same $G,N,S,D,R,E$ path, and $P,L$ record provenance and local naming without adding a hidden equality or choice principle. Hence the listed rows exhaust the NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy metastability finite-window stability]
\label{thm:cauchy-metastability-finite-window-stability}
Let $M=(G,N,S,D,R,E,H,C,P,L)$ be an accepted $\CauchyMetastabilityUp$ carrier. Every metastable Cauchy read exported by $M$ factors through the finite route
$$
(G,N)\longmapsto S|_{[N,G(N)]}\longmapsto D\longmapsto R
$$
before the $\RealUp$ seal row $E$ is exposed. In particular, changing the host presentation of the sequence while preserving the transported rows $G,N,S,D,R$ does not change the accepted finite-window certificate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with interval binding from \autoref{thm:cauchy-metastability-namecert-obligations}. A metastable request cannot inspect an unbounded tail; it first fixes the starting index $N$, computes the displayed endpoint $G(N)$, and reads only the windows stored in $S$ over that interval.

The dyadic comparison is then local to the inspected interval. The ledger $D$ supplies the tolerance rows used on the finite family $S$, and the row $R$ records the corresponding regular rational readback.

Finally apply the transport and replay obligations. Since $H$ preserves $G,N,S,D,R$ componentwise and $C$ replays the same route, any accepted presentation with the same transported rows names the same finite-window certificate. No selected limit, quotient stream equality, host real equality, countable choice, trichotomy, open-cover compactness, or ambient Real completeness is available as a coordinate that could alter the certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy metastability Real seal non-escape]
\label{thm:cauchy-metastability-real-seal-nonescape}
For every accepted $\CauchyMetastabilityUp$ carrier $M=(G,N,S,D,R,E,H,C,P,L)$, every $\RealUp$ consumer of the seal row $E$ first reads the displayed sibling route through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchymodulus-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchysequencespace-namecert}. Thus the seal receives only a finite metastable window certificate and receives no selected limit, quotient stream equality, host real equality, countable choice, trichotomy, open-cover compactness, or ambient Real completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the seal coordinate in the carrier definition. The row $E$ is stored after the control-functional row, starting index, finite window family, dyadic ledger, and regular readback.

Use \autoref{thm:cauchy-metastability-finite-window-stability}. Any metastable Cauchy read has already passed through the finite interval $[N,G(N)]$, the $\StreamNameUp$ windows $S$, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger $D$, and the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback $R$ before a Real-facing consumer can inspect $E$.

The sibling references identify the only external certificate surfaces used by the packet. \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchymodulus-namecert} supplies the modulus boundary, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchysequencespace-namecert} supplies the Cauchy-sequence carrier, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert} supplies only the terminal seal row. Therefore a consumer of $E$ cannot escape to a selected limit, quotient stream equality, host real equality, countable choice, trichotomy, open-cover compactness, or ambient Real completeness.
\end{proof}
