\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\FirstOrderUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-firstorder-namecert}

The first-order-logic interface $\FirstOrderUp$ packages a signature of relation and function symbols, a set of formulas, and a deduction calculus together with the soundness and completeness data. It depends on $\NameCert_{\SetUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-set-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\TreeUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-tree-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:firstorder-carrier}

\begin{definition}[FirstOrderUp BHist syntax carrier]\label{def:firstorder-bhist-syntax-carrier}
A carried $\FirstOrderUp$ syntax packet is a displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ tuple
$(S,T,V,R,F,\tau,\varphi,\rho)$. The row $S$ is the $\SetUp$ symbol-source
row, $T$ is the $\TreeUp$ parse-tree row, $V$ is the variable ledger, $R$ and
$F$ are the relation-symbol and function-symbol rows, $\tau$ is the finite
term/formula tree endpoint, $\varphi$ is the displayed formula endpoint, and
$\rho$ records the $\Pkg$ provenance that ties the syntax packet to the
visible naming certificate.

Acceptance of the packet is read entirely through $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$,
$\Cont$, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, $\mathsf{SigRel}$, and the $\SetUp$ and $\TreeUp$
$\NameCert$ rows. A host grammar, a host set of strings, or an external
syntax equality is not part of the carrier.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[FirstOrderUp deduction ledger classifier]\label{def:firstorder-deduction-ledger-classifier}
Two $\FirstOrderUp$ syntax/deduction packets agree when their symbol rows,
parse-tree rows, formula endpoint rows, and deduction-step ledgers are
transported by $\hsame$ through the same $\SetUp$ and $\TreeUp$ certificates.
The comparison is rowwise: relation symbols and function symbols move through
the $\SetUp$ source, parse subtrees and formula endpoints move through the
$\TreeUp$ source, and every deduction step carries its premise and conclusion
endpoints by the displayed $\Cont$ and $\hsame$ rows recorded in the ledger.
\end{definition}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:firstorder-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[FirstOrderUp formula carrier obligation]\label{thm:firstorder-formula-carrier-obligation}
Every accepted $\FirstOrderUp$ formula row projects to a $\SetUp$ symbol row,
a $\TreeUp$ parse row, a finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ arity ledger, and a $\mathsf{SigRel}$
endpoint relation. These projections are exactly the visible carrier data of
\autoref{def:firstorder-bhist-syntax-carrier}; no formula is accepted by a
host syntax predicate outside the displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Invert the carried syntax packet of
\autoref{def:firstorder-bhist-syntax-carrier}. The first projections expose
the $\SetUp$ symbol-source row, the $\TreeUp$ parse-tree row, the relation and
function symbol rows, and the formula endpoint $\varphi$.

The arity information is read from the finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ ledger attached
to the displayed relation and function rows. Each arity check is therefore a
finite probe over the named symbol endpoints, not an inspection of an ambient
syntax table.

Finally apply induction on the $\TreeUp$ syntax row. Leaf rows read variables
and constants from the symbol and variable ledgers; internal rows read their
subformula endpoints from the same finite tree ledger. The endpoint relation
is the displayed $\mathsf{SigRel}$ row tying the resulting formula endpoint to the
accepted packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FirstOrderUp deduction soundness ledger obligation]\label{thm:firstorder-deduction-soundness-ledger-obligation}
A $\FirstOrderUp$ deduction ledger is accepted only when every rule step
consumes formula endpoints already carried by
\autoref{def:firstorder-bhist-syntax-carrier} and records its
premise/conclusion transport by $\Cont$ and $\hsame$. The ledger does not
validate a rule by appeal to an external proof system; validity is the finite
display of named endpoint consumption and transport.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Induct over the finite displayed deduction ledger.

In the empty case there is no rule step to validate. The ledger acceptance
condition reduces to the visibility of the initial formula endpoints already
carried by the syntax packet.

In the step case, the induction hypothesis gives accepted carried endpoints
for all earlier premises. The next rule row must cite only those endpoints,
record the conclusion endpoint as another displayed formula row, and attach
the $\Cont$ and $\hsame$ transport rows that move premises to the conclusion
inside the same $\SetUp$ and $\TreeUp$ provenance.

Appending the checked step preserves acceptance because the new ledger tail
adds one finite row whose inputs, output, and transport are all visible BEDC
rows. Thus the completed deduction remains a named ledger rather than a host
derivation.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FirstOrderUp NameCert obligation surface]\label{thm:firstorder-namecert-obligation-surface}
The public $\NameCert_{\FirstOrderUp}$ surface consists exactly of the syntax
carrier of \autoref{def:firstorder-bhist-syntax-carrier}, the deduction
classifier of \autoref{def:firstorder-deduction-ledger-classifier}, the
formula carrier obligation of
\autoref{thm:firstorder-formula-carrier-obligation}, the deduction-soundness
ledger of \autoref{thm:firstorder-deduction-soundness-ledger-obligation}, the
$\SetUp$ symbol provenance, the $\TreeUp$ syntax provenance, stability under
$\hsame$, and exactness of displayed formula and deduction endpoints.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Collect the carrier rows first. The syntax carrier supplies the displayed
$\mathsf{BHist}$ packet, and the formula carrier obligation projects from it
the symbol source, parse source, arity ledger, and endpoint relation.

Next collect the comparison rows. The deduction classifier supplies the
rowwise $\hsame$ transport through the same $\SetUp$ and $\TreeUp$
certificates, while the deduction-soundness ledger supplies the finite rule
steps that consume already carried endpoints and produce displayed conclusion
endpoints.

It remains to check public coverage. Any consumer of $\NameCert_{\FirstOrderUp}$
asks for a syntax endpoint, a deduction endpoint, provenance in $\SetUp$ or
$\TreeUp$, transport under $\hsame$, or exact readback of a visible formula or
deduction row. Each request is one of the rows just collected, so the public
surface contains no additional carrier, classifier, or proof-system field.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[FirstOrderUp satisfaction BHist carrier]\label{def:firstorder-satisfaction-bhist-carrier}
A carried $\FirstOrderUp$ satisfaction packet is a displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$
tuple over five visible rows: the $\SetUp$ symbol row, the $\TreeUp$ formula
row, the assignment and environment row, the interpretation row for relation
and function symbols, and the finite provenance row. The provenance row records
the $\Cont$, $\hsame$, and $\Pkg$ data that connect the symbol names, formula
endpoint, variable assignment, and interpretation entries to the same visible
syntax packet of \autoref{def:firstorder-bhist-syntax-carrier}.

The satisfaction predicate is the row classifier of this packet. It accepts
only those reads whose formula endpoint is carried by the syntax row, whose
free variables are read through the assignment/environment row, whose atomic
relation and function entries are read through the interpretation row, and
whose recursive connective and quantifier steps are displayed by finite
$\Cont$ and $\hsame$ transport. No host model carrier, ambient truth relation,
or unrecorded semantic equality is part of the carrier.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[FirstOrderUp soundness transport row]\label{thm:firstorder-soundness-transport-row}
For every accepted $\FirstOrderUp$ deduction step, satisfaction rows transport
from the displayed premise packets to the displayed conclusion packet using
only the deduction ledger, $\Cont$, $\hsame$, $\SetUp$, $\TreeUp$, and
$\NameCert$ provenance recorded in \autoref{def:firstorder-satisfaction-bhist-carrier}.
Equivalently, if every premise endpoint of a rule step carries a satisfaction
packet over the same symbol, assignment/environment, and interpretation rows,
then the conclusion endpoint carries the satisfaction packet produced by the
rule row and its displayed transport data.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Induct over the finite deduction ledger.

In the empty ledger there is no rule step to transport. The only available
reads are the carried syntax and satisfaction rows already present in the
visible packet.

For a nonempty ledger, apply the induction hypothesis to the earlier rule
rows. This supplies satisfaction packets for every previously accepted premise
endpoint, all over the same $\SetUp$ symbol row, $\TreeUp$ formula row,
assignment/environment row, interpretation row, and provenance row.

The next displayed rule step names its premise endpoints and its conclusion
endpoint inside the deduction ledger. Project the accompanying $\Cont$ and
$\hsame$ rows. These rows transport the premise satisfaction packets across
the carried formula endpoints and repack the result as the conclusion
satisfaction packet.

Appending the transported conclusion preserves the ledger invariant because
the new row cites only displayed premises, the displayed rule datum, and the
same $\SetUp$, $\TreeUp$, and $\NameCert$ provenance. Thus soundness is a
finite row transport statement, not an appeal to an unrecorded proof system or
model class.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FirstOrderUp completeness boundary]\label{thm:firstorder-completeness-boundary}
The $\FirstOrderUp$ completeness surface is a BEDC boundary: every public
completeness readback exposes a carried syntax row, the satisfaction carrier
row of \autoref{def:firstorder-satisfaction-bhist-carrier}, a deduction ledger
row, and the $\Pkg$ provenance tying those rows to $\NameCert_{\SetUp}$ and
$\NameCert_{\TreeUp}$. A readback that requires a host model class, an
unrecorded semantic relation, or an external proof system is outside the
public $\FirstOrderUp$ certificate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project a public completeness readback from the displayed $\FirstOrderUp$
packet. Its syntax component is one of the carried formula rows of
\autoref{def:firstorder-bhist-syntax-carrier}, and its derivation component is
one of the finite rows of
\autoref{def:firstorder-deduction-ledger-classifier}.

Project the semantic component through
\autoref{def:firstorder-satisfaction-bhist-carrier}. This exposes the
assignment/environment row, the interpretation row, and the finite
$\Cont/\hsame/\Pkg$ provenance that connects semantic reads to the same
displayed formula endpoint.

It remains to check that the readback is complete for the public surface and
no larger. Induct over the finite deduction ledger. Initial rows are exactly
the carried syntax and satisfaction packets; each rule row is handled by the
transport statement of
\autoref{thm:firstorder-soundness-transport-row}; the final row is therefore a
displayed conclusion packet with the same package provenance.

Any proposed readback field not obtained by these projections has no displayed
$\mathsf{BHist}$ row, no $\Cont$ or $\hsame$ transport, and no $\Pkg$ route back
to $\NameCert_{\SetUp}$ or $\NameCert_{\TreeUp}$. Such a field is not admitted
by the $\FirstOrderUp$ certificate.
\end{proof}
