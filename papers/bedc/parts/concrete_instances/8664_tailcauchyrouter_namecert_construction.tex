\begin{definition}[Tail Cauchy router carrier]
\label{def:tailcauchyrouter-carrier}
A $\TailCauchyRouterUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
T=(S,M,W,R,L,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the tail selector that names the accepted tail threshold for the requested precision;
\item $M$ is the shared modulus row used by every source and consumer read in the packet;
\item $W$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$ window reached at the selected tail;
\item $R$ is the regular-Cauchy source row whose tail is being routed;
\item $L$ is the $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ handoff row fed by the selected window and source row;
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal endpoint exported to the consumer;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the selector, modulus, window, source, limit handoff, endpoint, route, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes from the selected tail and shared modulus to the window, from the window and source row to the limit handoff, and from the handoff to the Real endpoint;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RegularCauchySequenceUp$, $\CauchyLimitSealUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\TailCauchyRouterUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a second selector, unbounded window, quotient stream equality, detached Real endpoint, ambient completeness, or a limit handoff not fed by the displayed source row.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Tail Cauchy router carrier exposure]
\label{thm:tailcauchyrouter-carrier-exposure}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:tailcauchyrouter-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\TailCauchyRouterUp}$ surface exposes exactly the finite rows
$$
S,M,W,R,L,E,H,C,P,N.
$$
Every accepted packet makes the selector, shared modulus, finite StreamName and RegSeqRat window, regular-Cauchy source row, CauchyLimitSeal handoff, Real endpoint, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows available as named coordinates.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the displayed carrier. The tuple lists the selector, modulus, window, source, handoff, endpoint, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows as finite coordinates.

The exclusion clause of the carrier forbids any second selector, unbounded window, quotient equality, detached endpoint, ambient completeness row, or unrelated handoff coordinate. Thus the listed coordinates are exactly the exposed NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Tail selector determinacy]
\label{thm:tailcauchyrouter-selector-determinacy}
Inside an accepted $\TailCauchyRouterUp$ packet, the tail selector row $S$ determines the window row $W$ relative to the shared modulus $M$. A consumer cannot replace $S$ by a different selector while keeping the same local NameCert row $N$ and the same displayed route rows $C$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the continuation row $C$ from the selector side. It records the route from $S$ and $M$ to the finite window $W$, so the window admitted by the certificate is the one reached by the displayed selector and modulus.

If a consumer replaces $S$ while keeping $N$ and $C$, the first continuation edge no longer has the source named by the carrier. The replacement is therefore outside the accepted NameCert packet rather than a second accepted tail route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Tail modulus-window coverage]
\label{thm:tailcauchyrouter-modulus-window-coverage}
Every accepted $\TailCauchyRouterUp$ packet covers the selected tail window by the shared modulus row $M$. The finite row $W$ must contain the StreamName observations and RegSeqRat readback needed by the regular-Cauchy source row $R$ at the tail named by $S$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the carrier exposure theorem to isolate $S,M,W,R$. The packet contains a single shared modulus row and a single finite window row, so all source and consumer reads pass through those displayed coordinates.

The continuation row $C$ binds $S$ and $M$ to $W$, then binds $W$ to the source row $R$. Hence a window lacking the StreamName observations or RegSeqRat readback required at the selected tail is not covered by the certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Tail seal non-escape]
\label{thm:tailcauchyrouter-seal-non-escape}
The $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ row $L$ of an accepted $\TailCauchyRouterUp$ packet is fed only by the displayed window row $W$ and regular-Cauchy source row $R$. No accepted packet exports a limit handoff detached from the selected tail route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the route row $C$. Its middle segment reaches $L$ from $W$ and $R$, after $W$ has already been selected by $S$ under the shared modulus $M$.

The carrier contains no detached handoff coordinate. Therefore any proposed limit seal not fed by the displayed selected window and source row has no coordinate in the local packet and is outside the certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Tail RealUp consumer handoff]
\label{thm:tailcauchyrouter-realup-consumer-handoff}

$$
S,M,W,R,L,E,H,C,P,N.
$$

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

Follow the displayed continuation route. It first selects and covers the finite tail window, then feeds the CauchyLimitSeal row from that window and the regular-Cauchy source, and only then reaches the Real endpoint.

Since no detached endpoint or ambient completeness coordinate occurs in the carrier, every accepted Real-facing consumer route factors through the displayed finite packet.
\end{proof}
