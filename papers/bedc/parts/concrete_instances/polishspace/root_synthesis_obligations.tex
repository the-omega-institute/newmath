\begin{theorem}[Polish-space root density obligations]
\label{thm:polishspace-root-density-obligations}
Let $P=(M,K,D,S,R,W,H,C,G,N)$ be an accepted $\PolishSpaceUp$ carrier. The
root density obligation is the $\SeparableMetricUp$ dense schedule row $D$
read over the same $\MetricSpaceUp$ carrier $M$, through $\StreamNameUp$
windows $S$, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback $R$, the observation ledger $W$, the
supporting $\BHist$ packet, componentwise $\hsame$ transport $H$, displayed
$\Cont$ replay $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance $G$, and local $\NameCert$ row $N$.
It exports no selected dense point, external enumeration, or host topology.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:polish-space-carrier}. The displayed
rows include $M,D,S,R,W,H,C,G,N$, with $D$ stored as a separable-metric row
beside the metric source $M$.

Apply \autoref{thm:polish-space-namecert-obligations}. Carrier admission and
classifier transport keep $D$ over the transported $\MetricSpaceUp$ surface,
while ledger exactness forces every dense-name inspection through $S,R,W$.

The rows $H,C,G,N$ then transport, replay, source, and name that same finite
route. Since no further coordinate is present, the root density obligation is
exactly the displayed $\BHist$ route and not a selected dense subset or host
enumeration theorem.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Polish-space root completion obligations]
\label{thm:polishspace-root-completion-obligations}
Let $P=(M,K,D,S,R,W,H,C,G,N)$ be an accepted $\PolishSpaceUp$ carrier. The
root completion obligation is the $\CompleteMetricUp$ handoff row $K$ over
the same $\MetricSpaceUp$ carrier $M$ used by the dense schedule $D$, with
Cauchy-window requests read through $\StreamNameUp$ windows $S$,
$\RegSeqRatUp$ readback $R$, observation ledger $W$, $\BHist$ support,
componentwise $\hsame$ transport $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay $C$, $\Pkg$
provenance $G$, and local $\NameCert$ row $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project $P$ through \autoref{def:polish-space-carrier}. The completion row is
$K$, and its metric source is the same row $M$ that supports the separable
schedule $D$.

Use \autoref{thm:polish-space-namecert-obligations}. The classifier transport
and stability clauses keep $K$ and $D$ over the same transported metric
carrier before either row is consumed.

The observation ledger $W$ supplies the Cauchy-window route through $S$ and
$R$, while $H,C,G,N$ provide transport, replay, provenance, and local naming.
No quotient completion, selected limit, separate host metric, or ambient
completeness principle is present in the root obligation.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Polish-space root stream readback obligations]
\label{thm:polishspace-root-stream-readback-obligations}
Let $P=(M,K,D,S,R,W,H,C,G,N)$ be an accepted $\PolishSpaceUp$ carrier. Every
root readback of dense names, metric-radius checks, and Cauchy windows
factors through the displayed $\StreamNameUp$ row $S$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$ row
$R$ before it can be consumed by the $\MetricSpaceUp$, $\CompleteMetricUp$,
or $\SeparableMetricUp$ rows $M,K,D$. The factorization is supported exactly
by $W,H,C,G,N$ as observation ledger, $\hsame$ transport, $\Cont$ replay,
$\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from an accepted packet and project its stream-facing rows from
\autoref{def:polish-space-carrier}. The stream schedule $S$, regular rational
readback $R$, and observation ledger $W$ are displayed before any analytic
consumer reads $M,K$, or $D$.

Apply \autoref{thm:polish-space-namecert-obligations}. Ledger exactness makes
$W$ the only route for metric-radius checks, Cauchy windows, dense-name
inspections, and regular rational readbacks.

The transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows $H,C,G,N$ do not add a
second readback channel. Therefore every root stream readback remains inside
the finite $\BHist$ packet and cannot escape to an external oracle, selected
limit, host enumeration, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Polish-space complete-separable root synthesis]
\label{thm:polishspace-complete-separable-root-synthesis}
Let $P=(M,K,D,S,R,W,H,C,G,N)$ be an accepted $\PolishSpaceUp$ carrier. The
rows $M,K,D,S,R,W,H,C,G,N$ form one complete-separable metric synthesis route:
$M$ is the common metric source, $K$ is the complete-metric handoff over $M$,
$D$ is the separable schedule over $M$, $S$ and $R$ display the stream and
regular-rational observations, and $W,H,C,G,N$ preserve ledger exactness,
transport, replay, provenance, and local naming.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier through \autoref{def:polish-space-carrier}. The displayed
tuple is already the common $\BHist$ packet containing the metric, completion,
separability, stream, readback, ledger, transport, replay, provenance, and
local naming rows.

Apply \autoref{thm:polish-space-namecert-obligations}. Carrier admission
exposes those rows as one local NameCert surface, while classifier transport
keeps $K$ and $D$ over the same metric source $M$.

Finally use the root stream readback obligations of
\autoref{thm:polishspace-root-stream-readback-obligations}. The stream and
regular-rational rows $S,R$ must be displayed before $M,K,D$ are consumed, so
the complete-separable synthesis is exactly the finite route
$M,K,D,S,R,W,H,C,G,N$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Polish-space stream readback exactness]
\label{thm:polishspace-stream-readback-exactness}
Let $P=(M,K,D,S,R,W,H,C,G,N)$ be an accepted $\PolishSpaceUp$ carrier. Every
dense-name read and every Cauchy-window read passes through the displayed
$\StreamNameUp$ row $S$, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row $R$, and the
observation ledger $W$ before it can be consumed by the metric,
complete-metric, or separable rows $M,K,D$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from \autoref{def:polish-space-carrier}. Dense-name inspections and
Cauchy-window requests have only the stream schedule $S$, regular rational
readback $R$, and observation ledger $W$ as displayed readback coordinates.

Use \autoref{thm:polish-space-namecert-obligations}. Its ledger-exactness
clause makes $W$ the only source of metric-radius checks, Cauchy windows,
dense-name inspections, and regular rational readbacks.

The earlier root stream readback theorem then fixes the order of use: $S,R,W$
are read before any consumer treats $M,K,D$ as analytic evidence. Thus no
dense-name or Cauchy-window read can bypass the stream readback route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Polish-space choice-free observation ledger]
\label{thm:polishspace-choice-free-observation-ledger}
Let $P=(M,K,D,S,R,W,H,C,G,N)$ be an accepted $\PolishSpaceUp$ carrier. The
ledger rows $W,H,C,G,N$ exclude selected dense sequences, quotient completion
equality, and host complete-separable spaces as sources for the
$\PolishSpaceUp$ synthesis; every public observation remains a finite route
through $M,K,D,S,R$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the carrier projection from \autoref{def:polish-space-carrier}. The
support rows $W,H,C,G,N$ record observation, transport, replay, provenance,
and local naming for the displayed tuple only.

Apply \autoref{thm:polish-space-namecert-obligations}. No NameCert obligation
is stored outside the six listed obligation rows, and the exactness row
identifies $W$ as the sole observation source.

Combine this with \autoref{thm:polishspace-complete-separable-root-synthesis}.
The complete-separable route is already supplied by $M,K,D,S,R,W,H,C,G,N$, so
selected dense sequences, quotient completion equality, and host
complete-separable spaces are not exported by the packet.
\end{proof}
