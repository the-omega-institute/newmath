\begin{theorem}[Polish-space complete-separable synthesis]
\label{thm:polish-space-root-complete-separable-synthesis}
Let $P=(M,K,D,S,R,W,H,C,G,N)$ be an accepted $\PolishSpaceUp$ packet. Its
root complete-separable route exposes the $\CompleteMetricUp$ row $K$ and
the $\SeparableMetricUp$ row $D$ only over the same $\MetricSpaceUp$ carrier
$M$, with $\StreamNameUp$ windows $S$, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback $R$,
observation ledger $W$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport $H$, displayed
$\Cont$ replay $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance $G$, and local $\NameCert$ rows $N$.
Consequently every complete-separable consumer of $P$ reads one common route
$$
M \longmapsto (K,D) \longmapsto (S,R,W) \longmapsto (H,C,G,N),
$$
and no choice-selected dense sequence, external completion coordinate,
separate host metric, or quotient-completion equality is exported.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project $P$ by \autoref{def:polish-space-carrier}. The carrier exposes
$M,K,D,S,R,W,H,C,G,N$ as displayed rows of one finite $\BHist$ packet.

Apply \autoref{thm:polish-space-namecert-obligations}. Carrier admission
places the complete-metric handoff $K$ and the separable-metric schedule $D$
inside the same local $\NameCert_{\PolishSpaceUp}$ surface, while classifier
transport uses $H$ to compare both rows only after transport to the common
metric source $M$.

Use \autoref{thm:polishspace-root-density-obligations} and
\autoref{thm:polishspace-root-completion-obligations}. The density obligation
forces $D$ to be read through $S,R,W$ over $M$, and the completion obligation
forces $K$ to use the same metric source and the same finite observation
route.

It remains to package the shared readback. The rows $C,G,N$ replay, source,
and name the route already fixed by $M,K,D,S,R,W,H$. Because the carrier has
no coordinate for the excluded dense selector, external completion, separate
host metric, or quotient-completion equality, the displayed route is the only
complete-separable synthesis exported by an accepted Polish-space packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[Polish-space root unblock surface]
\label{def:polish-space-root-unblock-surface}
The root unblock surface of an accepted $\PolishSpaceUp$ packet is the
displayed finite packet
$$
(M,K,D,S,R,W,H,C,G,N)
$$
viewed simultaneously as the $\MetricSpaceUp$ carrier row $M$, the
$\CompleteMetricUp$ handoff $K$, the $\SeparableMetricUp$ dense schedule $D$,
the $\StreamNameUp$ window row $S$, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row $R$, the
observation ledger $W$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport $H$, displayed
$\Cont$ replay $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance $G$, and local
$\NameCert_{\PolishSpaceUp}$ row $N$.

A downstream root consumer may read complete-separable data only through this
single surface. It receives no separate metric carrier, selected dense point,
quotient completion equality, unscheduled name oracle, or ambient
$\RealUp$ completeness datum.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Polish-space complete-separable unblock package]
\label{thm:polish-space-complete-separable-unblock-package}
Let $P=(M,K,D,S,R,W,H,C,G,N)$ be an accepted $\PolishSpaceUp$ packet. The
complete row $K$ and the separable row $D$ are consumed over one metric carrier
$M$: every complete-separable root read first exposes $M$, then reads both
$K$ and $D$ through the same $S,R,W$ observation surface, and finally replays
the common packet through $H,C,G,N$.

Consequently the complete side cannot be sourced from one carrier while the
separable side is sourced from another, and neither side can bypass the
finite StreamName and RegSeqRat windows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from \autoref{def:polish-space-root-unblock-surface}. The displayed
packet has a single metric coordinate $M$, and both $K$ and $D$ are rows of
that same packet.

Use the complete-separable synthesis of
\autoref{thm:polish-space-root-complete-separable-synthesis}. It already
routes every complete-separable consumer through
$$
M \longmapsto (K,D) \longmapsto (S,R,W) \longmapsto (H,C,G,N).
$$

It remains to rule out split consumption. The observation ledger $W$ and the
componentwise transport row $H$ move $K$ and $D$ only together with $M,S,R,C,G,N$.
Thus a consumer has no coordinate from which to read a second carrier, an
unscheduled complete row, or a dense schedule outside the displayed finite
windows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Polish-space RegSeqRat window unblock]
\label{thm:polish-space-regseqrat-window-unblock}
Let $P=(M,K,D,S,R,W,H,C,G,N)$ be an accepted $\PolishSpaceUp$ packet. Every
downstream Polish-space read that reaches a $\RealUp$-facing consumer passes
first through the $\StreamNameUp$ row $S$ and the $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $R$ of
\autoref{def:polish-space-root-unblock-surface}. The read may then use the
complete row $K$, the separable row $D$, and any Real-facing seal already
exposed by the packet, but it cannot replace $S,R$ by a selected dense point,
a quotient-completion representative, host equality, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Expose the root unblock surface. The only rows that schedule names and regular
rational observations are $S$ and $R$, and the local NameCert row names them
as part of the same finite packet as $M,K,D,W,H,C,G$.

The Real-facing boundary is downstream of those observations. A consumer can
reach Real-facing data only after the stream windows have supplied finite
requests and the regular rational readback has supplied the corresponding
observations.

Finally apply the nonescape clauses of the complete-separable synthesis. Since
the packet has no row for selected dense points, quotient-completion
representatives, host equality, or ambient completeness, every downstream
read passes through the StreamName and RegSeqRat windows before any
Real-facing consumer is available.
\end{proof}
