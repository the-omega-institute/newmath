\begin{definition}[Completion extractor carrier]\label{def:completion-extractor-carrier}

$$

\begin{enumerate}
\item $G$ is the $\EffectiveCauchySequenceUp$ source row. It names the explicit Cauchy sequence data from which every completion-facing read begins.
\item $M$ is the $\RealCauchyModulusUp$ threshold row. It records the finite precision request, the displayed threshold, and the Real-facing modulus budget read by the extractor.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row. It records the inspected windows admitted by the threshold row $M$.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger. It stores the dyadic radii, slack rows, comparison budgets, and tolerance conversions used by $M$ and $S$.
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row. It records the regular rational handoff obtained from the displayed source, threshold, finite windows, and dyadic ledger.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ and $\FastCauchyCompletionUp$ seal handoff reached only after $G,M,S,D,Q$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the source, threshold, window, dyadic ledger, regular readback, seal handoff, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a completion or Real precision request through $G,M,S,D,Q,E,H,P,N$.

\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate asserts exactly that these rows form one finite completion-extractor packet. It has no coordinate for quotient stream equality, selected limits, countable choice, trichotomy, host real equality, ambient Real completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Completion extractor NameCert obligations]\label{thm:completion-extractor-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CompletionExtractorUp$ carrier $X=(G,M,S,D,Q,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CompletionExtractorUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier exposure, requiring the displayed source, threshold, finite-window, dyadic-ledger, regular-readback, seal-handoff, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows to be inspectable in one finite packet;
\item classifier stability, requiring accepted packets to be compared only through $G,M,S,D,Q,E,H,C,P,N$;
\item modulus-window induction, requiring each finite precision request to read $M$ before the corresponding $\StreamNameUp$ window row $S$ is consumed;
\item regular-readback admission, requiring $Q$ to be downstream of the explicit source, modulus threshold, finite windows, and dyadic tolerance ledger;
\item Real-seal nonescape, requiring $E$ to remain downstream of $G,M,S,D,Q$ and never to serve as quotient stream equality, a selected limit, countable choice, trichotomy, host real equality, or ambient completeness.
\end{enumerate}
No completion-extractor obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier exposure is the projection of \autoref{def:completion-extractor-carrier}. The displayed packet consists of the explicit source $G$, threshold row $M$, finite windows $S$, dyadic ledger $D$, regular readback $Q$, seal handoff $E$, and structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

Classifier stability is forced by the same projection. Since the carrier has no coordinate beyond those rows, any accepted comparison can inspect only the displayed source, threshold, window, dyadic, readback, seal, transport, replay, provenance, and naming data.

Modulus-window induction reads the Real-facing threshold through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-realcauchymodulus-namecert} and then opens the finite windows through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}. The tolerance arithmetic used by that induction is confined to the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}.

The regular-readback and Real-seal rows follow \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-fast-cauchy-completion-namecert}. The row $Q$ is admitted only after $G,M,S,D$, and $E$ is terminal after $Q$; therefore the listed five obligations exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

$$
The route cannot use quotient stream equality, selected limits, countable choice, trichotomy, host real equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from an accepted regular readback. By \autoref{def:completion-extractor-carrier}, the only regular rational coordinate is $Q$, and it is placed after the explicit source row $G$, modulus threshold row $M$, finite-window row $S$, and dyadic ledger row $D$.

The dyadic ledger $D$ supplies the tolerance conversions used by the same window. Only after that ledger is fixed does \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert} admit the regular readback row $Q$.

Finally, the seal handoff $E$ is downstream of the readback through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-fast-cauchy-completion-namecert}. The excluded quotient, selector, choice, order-decision, host-equality, and ambient-completeness data have no carrier row and no replay edge, so every accepted regular readback factors through the displayed route.
