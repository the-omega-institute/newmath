\begin{theorem}[Real-window budget finite-tail-filter handoff]
\label{thm:real-window-budget-finite-tail-filter-handoff}
Let $W=(Q,V,D,R,S,B,O,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\RealWindowBudgetUp$ packet whose
finite route is consumed as a cofinal-tail Real or Completion budget. The
consumer reads the same $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and
$\RealUp$ faces through the displayed $\FiniteTailFilterUp$ packet of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finite-tail-filter-namecert}; for a regular
Cauchy tail source, the read also factors through
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-tail-filter-namecert}. No detached
finite observation budget, private tail window, separate dyadic ledger, or
ambient completion seal is exported.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with finite-route visibility and the Real-completion handoff,
\autoref{thm:real-window-budget-finite-route-visibility} and
\autoref{thm:real-window-budget-real-completion-handoff}. They identify every Real
or Completion read of $W$ with the displayed $Q,V,D,R,S,B,O,H,C,P,N$ route.

Now apply tail-window exactness and Real-seal handoff for $\FiniteTailFilterUp$,
\autoref{thm:finite-tail-filter-tail-window-exactness} and
\autoref{thm:finite-tail-filter-real-seal-handoff}. These bind the shared stream
window, dyadic ledger, regular readback, and Real seal to the same cofinal-tail
packet. For regular Cauchy sources, compose with
\autoref{thm:regular-cauchy-tail-filter-real-seal-handoff}.
\end{proof}
