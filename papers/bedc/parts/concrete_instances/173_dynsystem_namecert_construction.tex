\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\DynSystemUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-dynsystem-namecert}

The dynamical-system interface $\DynSystemUp$ packages a one-parameter family of self-maps of a phase-space carrier satisfying the flow axioms. It depends on $\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-manifold-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\OdeUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-ode-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:dynsystem-carrier}

\begin{definition}[DynSystemUp BHist flow carrier]
\label{def:dynsystem-bhist-flow-carrier}
A $\DynSystemUp$ flow packet is a displayed finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ tuple
$$
\mathcal{D}=(\mathcal{M},\mathcal{O},p,t,x,y,\phi,\omega,\rho,\lambda)
$$
where $\mathcal{M}$ is a $\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$ phase-space packet, $\mathcal{O}$ is a $\NameCert_{\OdeUp}$ local-flow source packet, $p:\mathsf{BHist}$ is the phase carrier row, $t:\mathsf{BHist}$ is the elapsed-time row, $x:\mathsf{BHist}$ is the source point row, $y:\mathsf{BHist}$ is the target point row, $\phi:\mathsf{BHist}$ is the flow endpoint row, $\omega:\mathsf{BHist}$ is the ODE dependency witness read from $\mathcal{O}$, $\rho$ records the $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance of the manifold and ODE dependency packets, and $\lambda$ is the finite $\Cont$ ledger connecting the source point, elapsed time, local-flow row, and endpoint row.

The packet is accepted only when the phase carrier and point rows are classified by the $\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$ surface, the local-flow source row is classified by the $\NameCert_{\OdeUp}$ surface, and the endpoint row $\phi$ is reached from $(t,x)$ by the displayed $\Cont$ ledger. Two accepted packets classify the same $\DynSystemUp$ flow when their phase carrier, time row, source row, target row, ODE source witness, and flow endpoint row are transported by the inherited $\hsame$ comparisons from $\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\OdeUp}$ while retaining the same $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[DynSystemUp carrier flow surface]
\label{thm:dynsystem-carrier-flow-surface}
Fix a $\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$ phase carrier and an $\OdeUp$ BHist
local-flow source over that carrier. A $\DynSystemUp$ carrier cell is a
$\mathsf{BHist}$ history whose displayed rows consist of the phase-carrier
cell, the starting time cell, the starting state cell, the endpoint time cell,
the endpoint state cell, the vector-field evaluation ledger, the Picard
ledger, and the endpoint ledger of the corresponding $\OdeUp$ local-flow row.
Thus every visible cell of the dynamical-system surface is read from
$\mathsf{BHist}$ data already carried by $\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$ and
$\NameCert_{\OdeUp}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the phase row through the $\ManifoldUp$ carrier. The source may be the
singleton carrier of $\mathsf{BEDC.Derived.ManifoldUp.ManifoldSingletonCarrier}$
or any atlas row exposed by
$\mathsf{BEDC.Derived.ManifoldUp.ManifoldAtlasPackage}$; in either case the
displayed phase cell is a $\mathsf{BHist}$ cell with a certificate classifier.

Read the flow row through the $\OdeUp$ local-flow source of
\autoref{def:ode-bhist-local-flow-source}. Its time, state, vector-field,
Picard, limit, and endpoint entries are already arranged as BHist histories
linked by $\Cont$ ledgers. Repacking these entries with the phase row gives
the $\DynSystemUp$ carrier surface without introducing a host-level carrier of
maps or trajectories.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DynSystemUp classifier flow transport]
\label{thm:dynsystem-classifier-flow-transport}
Let two $\DynSystemUp$ carrier cells be displayed from two $\OdeUp$
local-flow rows over the same $\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$ phase carrier. If the
displayed time cells, state cells, Picard histories, vector-field ledgers, and
endpoint ledgers are related componentwise by the $\hsame$ transports supplied
by $\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\OdeUp}$, then the two
dynamical-system flow cells are classified as the same $\DynSystemUp$ flow.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack each $\DynSystemUp$ cell into its phase row and its $\OdeUp$ local-flow
row. The phase components are transported by the $\ManifoldUp$ classifier, so
the chart carrier visible to the flow row is unchanged up to $\hsame$.

Transport the ODE row component by component: first the displayed time cells,
then the state cells, then the vector-field ledger, the Picard history, the
limit witness, and the endpoint ledger. The $\OdeUp$ classifier treats these
as BHist rows and compares them by $\hsame$ rather than by equality of
external functions.

After transport, both carrier cells expose the same ordered ledger surface.
Repacking that transported surface is exactly the $\DynSystemUp$ classifier,
so the two flow cells are classified together.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DynSystemUp identity flow obligation]
\label{thm:dynsystem-identity-flow-obligation}
For every $\DynSystemUp$ phase-state cell $x$ carried by
$\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$, the identity flow at empty time is represented by
the $\Cont$ unit row
$$
\Cont(\mathsf{BHist.Empty},x,x).
$$
Its $\DynSystemUp$ carrier ledger has empty displayed time, unchanged
displayed state, the trivial Picard route, and endpoint row $x$; this row is
classified by the same phase carrier and by the endpoint classifier inherited
from $\OdeUp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carried phase-state cell $x$. The empty time cell is the
distinguished BHist unit, and the identity route is the empty-time
continuation from $x$ back to $x$.

The Picard part of the ledger is the unit continuation route. Since no
nonempty vector-field evaluation is read, the vector-field component is only
the displayed empty ledger accepted by the $\OdeUp$ local-flow source.

The endpoint row is again $x$. The $\ManifoldUp$ classifier keeps the phase
cell fixed, and the $\OdeUp$ endpoint classifier reads the unit continuation
as an endpoint-preserving row. Repacking these rows gives the identity
$\DynSystemUp$ flow obligation.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DynSystemUp composition flow obligation]
\label{thm:dynsystem-composition-flow-obligation}
Let $R_{01}$ and $R_{12}$ be adjacent $\OdeUp$ local-flow source rows over the
same $\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$ phase carrier, with the endpoint time-state
cell of $R_{01}$ classified with the initial time-state cell of $R_{12}$.
Then $\DynSystemUp$ represents the composite flow by concatenating the two
source rows. The concatenated route is formed by $\Cont$ associativity on the
adjacent Picard and endpoint ledgers, and its endpoint classifier is the
$\OdeUp$ endpoint classifier transported through the shared phase carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read $R_{01}$ as a local-flow row from $(t_0,x_0)$ to $(t_1,x_1)$, and read
$R_{12}$ as a local-flow row from $(t_1,x_1)$ to $(t_2,x_2)$. The adjacency
hypothesis says that the middle time-state cell is shared in the
$\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\OdeUp}$ classifiers.

Use the Picard continuation scope of
\autoref{thm:ode-root-picard-continuation-scope} to concatenate the two
Picard routes. The reassociation step is a $\Cont$ associativity move, with
any syntactic mismatch at the middle cell handled by $\hsame$ transport.

The resulting source row has initial cell $(t_0,x_0)$ and endpoint cell
$(t_2,x_2)$. Repacking it as a $\DynSystemUp$ flow row gives the composition
obligation, and the endpoint determinacy is the classifier inherited from the
concatenated $\OdeUp$ row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DynSystemUp Ode generator ledger]
\label{thm:dynsystem-ode-generator-ledger}
The $\DynSystemUp$ generator is hidden only through the $\OdeUp$ vector-field
row and the $\ManifoldUp$ chart carrier. Concretely, a $\DynSystemUp$ flow
ledger records the displayed time cell, phase-state cell, Picard history,
endpoint history, the $\OdeUp$ vector-field evaluation row, and the
$\ManifoldUp$ chart or singleton carrier row. It contains no additional
generator oracle, no external trajectory equality, and no unclassified
function-space comparison.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier surface of
\autoref{thm:dynsystem-carrier-flow-surface}. Every time and state entry is a
BHist row supplied by the $\OdeUp$ local-flow source, while the phase carrier
is supplied by $\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$.

The only place where the generator is read is the vector-field evaluation row
of the $\OdeUp$ source. Its inputs are the displayed time cell and phase-state
cell, and its output is transported by the endpoint classifier described in
\autoref{thm:dynsystem-classifier-flow-transport}.

The only place where coordinates or phase charts are read is the
$\ManifoldUp$ chart carrier. Therefore the $\DynSystemUp$ ledger hides the
generator exactly behind those two certified rows and exposes only BHist,
$\Cont$, and $\hsame$ obligations to downstream flow-system consumers.
\end{proof}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:dynsystem-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[DynSystemUp zero-time flow ledger]
\label{thm:dynsystem-flow-unit-ledger}
For a packet of \autoref{def:dynsystem-bhist-flow-carrier}, the zero-time row acts as the identity flow packet exactly when the displayed $\Cont$ ledger begins and ends at the same manifold point row after inherited $\hsame$ transport. In that case the source row $x$, target row $y$, and endpoint row $\phi$ are all classified with the same $\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$ point row, and the $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance remains the original dependency provenance.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the flow packet and specialize the elapsed-time row to the zero-time cell carried by the ODE source surface. The local-flow source packet then exposes a trivial time-state route whose initial and terminal time rows are the same certified scalar row.

Project the point component through $\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$. The source point is already a carried manifold row, and the zero-time route supplies no additional point movement beyond the displayed endpoint comparison.

Use the visible $\Cont$ ledger to identify the source, target, and endpoint rows by $\hsame$. Since the tuple projection leaves $\rho$ unchanged, the identity packet preserves the same $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance and is therefore the unit row of the dynamical-system certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DynSystemUp certified flow composition ledger]
\label{thm:dynsystem-flow-composition-ledger}
Let $\mathcal{D}_{01}$ and $\mathcal{D}_{12}$ be accepted $\DynSystemUp$ flow packets whose terminal point and time-state rows are classified with the initial point and time-state rows of the next packet. Certified time concatenation composes their accepted flow endpoint rows by associativity of $\Cont$, and the resulting packet preserves the shared $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance over $\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\OdeUp}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the first packet as a finite ledger from the source point $x_0$ through elapsed time $t_{01}$ to endpoint $x_1$. Read the second packet as a finite ledger from the classified middle point $x_1$ through elapsed time $t_{12}$ to endpoint $x_2$.

The compatibility hypothesis supplies the $\hsame$ transport needed to pass from the terminal row of the first packet to the initial row of the second. The ODE source classifier supplies the corresponding time concatenation row, so both pieces live over the same local-flow dependency surface.

Associate the two displayed $\Cont$ chains. The composite chain has source $x_0$, concatenated time row, endpoint $x_2$, and the same dependency witnesses $\mathcal{M}$ and $\mathcal{O}$.

Finally project the provenance row. Because neither component step changes the $\mathsf{Pkg}$ source certificates, the composite packet retains the shared provenance and is a valid $\DynSystemUp$ flow packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DynSystemUp ODE source ledger]
\label{thm:dynsystem-ode-source-ledger}
Every accepted $\DynSystemUp$ flow row projects to the $\OdeUp$ local-flow source row carried by $\omega$. The dynamical-system packet contributes the phase carrier, manifold point classifier, elapsed-time ledger, endpoint row, and provenance binding, but it carries no additional host vector field beyond the $\NameCert_{\OdeUp}$ dependency row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the tuple of \autoref{def:dynsystem-bhist-flow-carrier} to its ODE component $\mathcal{O}$ and witness row $\omega$. By acceptance of the packet, $\omega$ is already a row of the $\NameCert_{\OdeUp}$ local-flow source.

The phase component contributes only the carrier and point rows read through $\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$. These rows select the manifold side on which the ODE source row is interpreted; they do not define a vector-field row.

The remaining fields $t$, $x$, $y$, $\phi$, $\rho$, and $\lambda$ are ledger and provenance data. Thus every vector-field evaluation, Picard route, and local endpoint determinacy claim is inherited from $\NameCert_{\OdeUp}$, while $\DynSystemUp$ records how that source is packaged as a phase-space flow.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DynSystemUp NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:dynsystem-namecert-obligation-surface}
The public $\NameCert_{\DynSystemUp}$ obligation surface consists exactly of the flow carrier of \autoref{def:dynsystem-bhist-flow-carrier}, the inherited classifier mediated by $\hsame$ over $\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\OdeUp}$, the zero-time unit ledger of \autoref{thm:dynsystem-flow-unit-ledger}, the certified composition ledger of \autoref{thm:dynsystem-flow-composition-ledger}, the ODE-source projection ledger of \autoref{thm:dynsystem-ode-source-ledger}, the stability rows obtained by transporting those fields through the same classifiers, and the ledger-exactness condition that every public flow endpoint is reached by the displayed $\Cont$ route and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Collect the carrier fields from \autoref{def:dynsystem-bhist-flow-carrier}. They provide the phase certificate, ODE source certificate, time row, source point, target point, endpoint, dependency witness, provenance, and finite ledger.

The classifier is componentwise. Manifold carrier and point rows are compared by $\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$, local-flow rows and elapsed-time rows by $\NameCert_{\OdeUp}$, and endpoints by the transported $\hsame$ rows recorded in the same ledger.

The unit and composition rows are exactly the first two public laws needed by a flow certificate. The source theorem above supplies the dependency projection that prevents a downstream consumer from reading an uncarried vector field into the packet.

Conversely, every listed consumer reads one of these displayed rows: units read the zero-time ledger, composition reads two compatible ledgers, ODE projection reads $\omega$, and stability reads classifier transport. Therefore the obligation surface is exact and contains no standard bridge, global-existence theorem, or host-level flow object beyond the carried BEDC rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DynSystemUp public flow endpoint exactness]
\label{thm:dynsystem-public-flow-endpoint-exactness}
For every accepted packet $\mathcal{D}$ of \autoref{def:dynsystem-bhist-flow-carrier}, the public flow endpoint exposed by $\NameCert_{\DynSystemUp}$ is exactly the row $\phi$ reached by the displayed route
$$
\Cont(t,x,\phi).
$$
The route is the $\lambda$ component of $\mathcal{D}$, its source and endpoint comparisons are transported only by inherited $\hsame$ rows from $\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\OdeUp}$, and its dependency origin is the $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance recorded by $\rho$ over the projected packets $\mathcal{M}$ and $\mathcal{O}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted tuple to the fields $\mathcal{M}$, $\mathcal{O}$, $\rho$, and $\lambda$. The first two fields name the manifold and ODE certificates through which the source point, time row, local-flow row, and endpoint row are read.

The ledger field $\lambda$ is the finite $\Cont$ route connecting the displayed source row $x$, elapsed-time row $t$, and endpoint row $\phi$. Any comparison needed to align the source or terminal rows is already supplied by the inherited $\hsame$ classifiers of $\mathcal{M}$ and $\mathcal{O}$.

Finally read $\rho$. Since the provenance field is projected from the same packet and is not recomputed by a downstream consumer, the exposed endpoint is the carried row $\phi$ with its original $\mathsf{Pkg}$ dependency binding.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DynSystemUp flow adjacency classifier]
\label{thm:dynsystem-flow-adjacency-classifier}
Let $\mathcal{D}_{01}$ and $\mathcal{D}_{12}$ be accepted $\DynSystemUp$ packets over the same projected $\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\OdeUp}$ dependency surface. They compose as adjacent flow packets only when the terminal time-state rows of $\mathcal{D}_{01}$ and the initial time-state rows of $\mathcal{D}_{12}$ are classified together by the ManifoldUp point classifier and the OdeUp local-flow classifier. Under that classifier hypothesis, the composite packet is the certified composition row of \autoref{thm:dynsystem-flow-composition-ledger}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the two packets through \autoref{thm:dynsystem-flow-composition-ledger}. Its compatibility premise is not an external equality of trajectories; it is the displayed classification of the middle point and time-state rows.

Use \autoref{thm:dynsystem-classifier-flow-transport} to identify the classifiers that supply this middle comparison. The phase component is read by $\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$, while the elapsed-time and local-flow rows are read by $\NameCert_{\OdeUp}$.

With those two classifier readings in place, the hypotheses of the composition ledger are exactly the adjacency hypotheses. The resulting concatenated $\Cont$ route is therefore the only certified composite exposed by $\NameCert_{\DynSystemUp}$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DynSystemUp ODE phase-scope separation]
\label{thm:dynsystem-ode-phase-scope-separation}
In every accepted $\DynSystemUp$ flow packet, phase rows are read only through $\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$ and generator rows are read only through $\NameCert_{\OdeUp}$. The fields $p$, $x$, $y$, and the phase part of $\phi$ belong to the ManifoldUp reading, while $\omega$ and every vector-field or local-flow generator row belong to the OdeUp reading.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the packet as in \autoref{thm:dynsystem-ode-source-ledger}. The ODE source is carried by $\mathcal{O}$ and witnessed by $\omega$, so generator information enters the dynamical-system packet only through the $\NameCert_{\OdeUp}$ local-flow source row.

Next apply \autoref{thm:dynsystem-ode-generator-ledger}. It identifies the chart or singleton carrier as the ManifoldUp side and the vector-field evaluation row as the OdeUp side.

Thus the two readings have disjoint scopes inside the packet: phase classification is routed through $\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$, and generator classification is routed through $\NameCert_{\OdeUp}$. The packet itself only records their BHist ledger and provenance binding.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DynSystemUp flow consumer projection coverage]
\label{thm:dynsystem-flow-consumer-projection-coverage}
Every downstream consumer whose output is invariant under the exposed phase rows, elapsed-time rows, source and endpoint rows, ODE-source row, displayed $\Cont$ ledger, inherited $\hsame$ transports, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance of \autoref{thm:dynsystem-namecert-obligation-surface} is invariant under the whole accepted $\DynSystemUp$ flow packet. No additional packet field can change such a consumer.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project an accepted packet to the obligation surface of \autoref{thm:dynsystem-namecert-obligation-surface}. This yields the phase certificate, ODE source certificate, time row, source point, target point, endpoint row, dependency witness, provenance, and finite continuation ledger.

Now compare two accepted packets whose listed rows are related by the exposed classifiers. The phase, source, target, and endpoint readings are transported by $\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$, while the elapsed-time, local-flow, and ODE-source readings are transported by $\NameCert_{\OdeUp}$.

The consumer is invariant under exactly these projections, including the $\Cont$ route, $\hsame$ transports, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance. Since the displayed tuple of \autoref{def:dynsystem-bhist-flow-carrier} has no further public row beyond those projections, the consumer has the same value on the whole packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DynSystemUp public NameCert export]
\label{thm:dynsystem-public-namecert-export}
The public $\NameCert_{\DynSystemUp}$ export surface consists of the carrier of \autoref{def:dynsystem-bhist-flow-carrier}, the inherited classifier of \autoref{thm:dynsystem-classifier-flow-transport}, the zero-time row of \autoref{thm:dynsystem-flow-unit-ledger}, the composition row of \autoref{thm:dynsystem-flow-composition-ledger}, the ODE-source row of \autoref{thm:dynsystem-ode-source-ledger}, the endpoint exactness row of \autoref{thm:dynsystem-public-flow-endpoint-exactness}, the adjacency classifier of \autoref{thm:dynsystem-flow-adjacency-classifier}, the phase-scope separation row of \autoref{thm:dynsystem-ode-phase-scope-separation}, and the consumer projection coverage row of \autoref{thm:dynsystem-flow-consumer-projection-coverage}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the carrier and classifier. The carrier supplies the displayed BHist tuple, and the classifier transports each visible flow field through the ManifoldUp and OdeUp certificate surfaces.

Add the algebraic flow rows. The zero-time theorem supplies the unit packet, the composition theorem supplies certified concatenation, and the adjacency theorem identifies the classifier premise under which that concatenation is public.

Add the source and projection rows. The ODE-source theorem fixes the generator reading, the phase-scope theorem separates phase and generator access, and the endpoint exactness theorem identifies the exposed endpoint with the displayed $\Cont$ route and provenance.

The consumer coverage theorem then states that these rows exhaust the public projections of an accepted packet. Together they form the advertised $\NameCert_{\DynSystemUp}$ export surface.
\end{proof}
