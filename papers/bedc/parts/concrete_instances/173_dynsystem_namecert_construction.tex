\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\DynSystemUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-dynsystem-namecert}

The dynamical-system interface $\DynSystemUp$ packages a one-parameter family of self-maps of a phase-space carrier satisfying the flow axioms. It depends on $\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-manifold-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\OdeUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-ode-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:dynsystem-carrier}

\begin{theorem}[DynSystemUp carrier flow surface]
\label{thm:dynsystem-carrier-flow-surface}
Fix a $\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$ phase carrier and an $\OdeUp$ BHist
local-flow source over that carrier. A $\DynSystemUp$ carrier cell is a
$\mathsf{BHist}$ history whose displayed rows consist of the phase-carrier
cell, the starting time cell, the starting state cell, the endpoint time cell,
the endpoint state cell, the vector-field evaluation ledger, the Picard
ledger, and the endpoint ledger of the corresponding $\OdeUp$ local-flow row.
Thus every visible cell of the dynamical-system surface is read from
$\mathsf{BHist}$ data already carried by $\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$ and
$\NameCert_{\OdeUp}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the phase row through the $\ManifoldUp$ carrier. The source may be the
singleton carrier of $\mathsf{BEDC.Derived.ManifoldUp.ManifoldSingletonCarrier}$
or any atlas row exposed by
$\mathsf{BEDC.Derived.ManifoldUp.ManifoldAtlasPackage}$; in either case the
displayed phase cell is a $\mathsf{BHist}$ cell with a certificate classifier.

Read the flow row through the $\OdeUp$ local-flow source of
\autoref{def:ode-bhist-local-flow-source}. Its time, state, vector-field,
Picard, limit, and endpoint entries are already arranged as BHist histories
linked by $\Cont$ ledgers. Repacking these entries with the phase row gives
the $\DynSystemUp$ carrier surface without introducing a host-level carrier of
maps or trajectories.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DynSystemUp classifier flow transport]
\label{thm:dynsystem-classifier-flow-transport}
Let two $\DynSystemUp$ carrier cells be displayed from two $\OdeUp$
local-flow rows over the same $\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$ phase carrier. If the
displayed time cells, state cells, Picard histories, vector-field ledgers, and
endpoint ledgers are related componentwise by the $\hsame$ transports supplied
by $\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\OdeUp}$, then the two
dynamical-system flow cells are classified as the same $\DynSystemUp$ flow.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack each $\DynSystemUp$ cell into its phase row and its $\OdeUp$ local-flow
row. The phase components are transported by the $\ManifoldUp$ classifier, so
the chart carrier visible to the flow row is unchanged up to $\hsame$.

Transport the ODE row component by component: first the displayed time cells,
then the state cells, then the vector-field ledger, the Picard history, the
limit witness, and the endpoint ledger. The $\OdeUp$ classifier treats these
as BHist rows and compares them by $\hsame$ rather than by equality of
external functions.

After transport, both carrier cells expose the same ordered ledger surface.
Repacking that transported surface is exactly the $\DynSystemUp$ classifier,
so the two flow cells are classified together.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DynSystemUp identity flow obligation]
\label{thm:dynsystem-identity-flow-obligation}
For every $\DynSystemUp$ phase-state cell $x$ carried by
$\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$, the identity flow at empty time is represented by
the $\Cont$ unit row
$$
\Cont(\mathsf{BHist.Empty},x,x).
$$
Its $\DynSystemUp$ carrier ledger has empty displayed time, unchanged
displayed state, the trivial Picard route, and endpoint row $x$; this row is
classified by the same phase carrier and by the endpoint classifier inherited
from $\OdeUp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carried phase-state cell $x$. The empty time cell is the
distinguished BHist unit, and the identity route is the empty-time
continuation from $x$ back to $x$.

The Picard part of the ledger is the unit continuation route. Since no
nonempty vector-field evaluation is read, the vector-field component is only
the displayed empty ledger accepted by the $\OdeUp$ local-flow source.

The endpoint row is again $x$. The $\ManifoldUp$ classifier keeps the phase
cell fixed, and the $\OdeUp$ endpoint classifier reads the unit continuation
as an endpoint-preserving row. Repacking these rows gives the identity
$\DynSystemUp$ flow obligation.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DynSystemUp composition flow obligation]
\label{thm:dynsystem-composition-flow-obligation}
Let $R_{01}$ and $R_{12}$ be adjacent $\OdeUp$ local-flow source rows over the
same $\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$ phase carrier, with the endpoint time-state
cell of $R_{01}$ classified with the initial time-state cell of $R_{12}$.
Then $\DynSystemUp$ represents the composite flow by concatenating the two
source rows. The concatenated route is formed by $\Cont$ associativity on the
adjacent Picard and endpoint ledgers, and its endpoint classifier is the
$\OdeUp$ endpoint classifier transported through the shared phase carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read $R_{01}$ as a local-flow row from $(t_0,x_0)$ to $(t_1,x_1)$, and read
$R_{12}$ as a local-flow row from $(t_1,x_1)$ to $(t_2,x_2)$. The adjacency
hypothesis says that the middle time-state cell is shared in the
$\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\OdeUp}$ classifiers.

Use the Picard continuation scope of
\autoref{thm:ode-root-picard-continuation-scope} to concatenate the two
Picard routes. The reassociation step is a $\Cont$ associativity move, with
any syntactic mismatch at the middle cell handled by $\hsame$ transport.

The resulting source row has initial cell $(t_0,x_0)$ and endpoint cell
$(t_2,x_2)$. Repacking it as a $\DynSystemUp$ flow row gives the composition
obligation, and the endpoint determinacy is the classifier inherited from the
concatenated $\OdeUp$ row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DynSystemUp Ode generator ledger]
\label{thm:dynsystem-ode-generator-ledger}
The $\DynSystemUp$ generator is hidden only through the $\OdeUp$ vector-field
row and the $\ManifoldUp$ chart carrier. Concretely, a $\DynSystemUp$ flow
ledger records the displayed time cell, phase-state cell, Picard history,
endpoint history, the $\OdeUp$ vector-field evaluation row, and the
$\ManifoldUp$ chart or singleton carrier row. It contains no additional
generator oracle, no external trajectory equality, and no unclassified
function-space comparison.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier surface of
\autoref{thm:dynsystem-carrier-flow-surface}. Every time and state entry is a
BHist row supplied by the $\OdeUp$ local-flow source, while the phase carrier
is supplied by $\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$.

The only place where the generator is read is the vector-field evaluation row
of the $\OdeUp$ source. Its inputs are the displayed time cell and phase-state
cell, and its output is transported by the endpoint classifier described in
\autoref{thm:dynsystem-classifier-flow-transport}.

The only place where coordinates or phase charts are read is the
$\ManifoldUp$ chart carrier. Therefore the $\DynSystemUp$ ledger hides the
generator exactly behind those two certified rows and exposes only BHist,
$\Cont$, and $\hsame$ obligations to downstream flow-system consumers.
\end{proof}
