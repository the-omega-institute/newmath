It sits after the dyadic arithmetic core of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the finite dyadic interval nets of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadic-interval-net-namecert}, the StreamName windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal Real seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. \begin{definition}[Compact interval uniform modulus carrier]\label{def:compact-interval-uniform-modulus-carrier}
A $\CompactIntervalUniformModulusUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
M=(D,J,E,S,R,U,Q,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint and precision row. It records dyadic endpoints, mesh widths, successor comparisons, and the arithmetic needed to compare a requested tolerance with a displayed mesh.
\item $J$ is the $\DyadicIntervalNetUp$ row. It lists the finite dyadic interval cells covering the named compact interval by displayed endpoint incidence.
\item $E$ is the local endpoint-boundary row. It records that the first and last cells of $J$ bracket the compact interval endpoints named through $D$.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window row read when a function value or endpoint name is inspected on a listed cell.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row for the rational values and tolerance schedule attached to those windows.
\item $U$ is the finite modulus row. It records that a requested output tolerance is answered by one displayed dyadic mesh bound and by the finite cell checks listed in $J$.
\item $Q$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after $D,J,E,S,R,U$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the displayed rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the finite route
$$
D,J,E \longmapsto S,R \longmapsto U \longmapsto Q,H,P,N .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CompactIntervalUniformModulusUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\DyadicIntervalNetUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CompactIntervalUniformModulusUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $D,J,E,S,R,U,Q,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for open-cover compactness, selected finite subcovers, real trichotomy, quotient equality, selected limits, countable choice, ambient Real completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Compact interval uniform modulus obligations]\label{thm:compact-interval-uniform-modulus-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CompactIntervalUniformModulusUp$ carrier $M=(D,J,E,S,R,U,Q,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CompactIntervalUniformModulusUp}$ surface is exhausted by the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the dyadic endpoint row, dyadic interval-net row, endpoint-boundary row, StreamName window row, RegSeqRat readback row, finite modulus row, Real seal row, transport row, replay row, provenance row, and local naming row;
\item dyadic mesh exactness, requiring every modulus request to use a mesh width and endpoint comparison displayed in $D$;
\item finite-net coverage, requiring every interval read to pass through a listed cell of $J$ and its endpoint incidence in $E$;
\item StreamName and RegSeqRat readback routing, requiring every inspected value to be exposed through $S$ and then read through $R$ before $U$ may answer the tolerance request;
\item Real seal nonescape, requiring $Q$ to be consumed only after the finite-net, readback, and modulus rows are displayed;
\item componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming through $H,C,P,N$.
\end{enumerate}
No obligation exports open-cover compactness, selected finite subcovers, real trichotomy, quotient equality, selected limits, countable choice, ambient Real completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:compact-interval-uniform-modulus-carrier}. The projection exposes the dyadic arithmetic row, the finite interval-net row, the endpoint-boundary row, the StreamName and RegSeqRat rows, the finite modulus row, the Real seal row, and the structural support rows.

The mesh and coverage obligations are finite. A modulus request first selects a displayed dyadic mesh comparison from $D$, then checks the listed cells of $J$ and the bracketing incidences of $E$. No step asks for an arbitrary open cover or a selected subcover.

The value-facing obligations are routed through finite observations. Function and endpoint reads pass through $S$, rational readback passes through $R$, and only then does $U$ answer the requested tolerance by the displayed mesh bound.

Finally $Q,H,C,P,N$ seal, transport, replay, source, and name the same finite route. Since the packet has no row for quotient equality, selected limits, countable choice, trichotomy, ambient completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking, the listed obligations exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}
