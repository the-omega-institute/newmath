\begin{theorem}[MetricSpace SOne distance source boundary]
\label{thm:metricspace-sone-distance-source-boundary}
Every accepted $\SOneUp$ packet over the $\RealUp$ product carrier reads
$\MetricSpaceUp$ distance data only through the finite rows of
\autoref{def:metricspace-real-distance-carrier} and the public law package of
\autoref{thm:metricspace-public-law-package}. Its circle-side source is
constrained by \autoref{thm:sone-real-metric-bridge-boundary},
\autoref{thm:sone-regseqrat-metric-observation-boundary}, and
\autoref{thm:sone-public-certificate-boundary}. Its $\RealAlgOrderUp$ side is
constrained by \autoref{thm:metric-realalgorder-apartness-distance-scope} and
\autoref{thm:realalgorder-sone-unit-equation-apartness-boundary}.

Thus a $\SOneUp$ consumer of real-distance data can read only the two
$\RealUp$ component rows, the product-carrier ledger, the unit-equation row,
componentwise $\hsame$ classifiers, equation transport, finite-window
$\RegSeqRat$ stream-name and real-seal rows, the displayed
$\RealAlgOrderUp$ apartness budget rows, the metric endpoint histories,
$\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, local $\NameCert$ rows, and the public
metric-law rows. The boundary exports no angle quotient, covering-space
quotient, host metric function, completion row, compactness row, connectedness
row, or topology generator.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.MetricUp.MetricspaceSoneDistanceSourceBoundary}
\leanvariant{BEDC.Derived.MetricUp.MetricspaceSOneDistanceSourceBoundary}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted $\SOneUp$ packet through
\autoref{thm:sone-real-metric-bridge-boundary}. This exposes the two
$\RealUp$ component rows, the product carrier, the unit-equation witness, the
componentwise classifier rows, and equation transport, and excludes the
circle-specific quotient and topology rows named in the statement.

Refine the two real components through
\autoref{thm:sone-regseqrat-metric-observation-boundary} and
\autoref{thm:sone-public-certificate-boundary}. The metric-facing source rows
are then finite-window $\RegSeqRat$ and real-seal observations attached to the
same public certificate, rather than an ambient circle metric or a hidden
completion object.

Pass the real-algebra side through
\autoref{thm:metric-realalgorder-apartness-distance-scope} and
\autoref{thm:realalgorder-sone-unit-equation-apartness-boundary}. These two
boundaries keep the apartness and unit-equation reads tied to the displayed
$\RealAlgOrderUp$ endpoint and budget rows, so the metric source has no
trichotomy, quotient-real equality, host metric, or Real completeness
coordinate.

Repack the resulting endpoint rows, budget rows, transport rows, continuation
routes, provenance rows, and local certificate rows as the carrier of
\autoref{def:metricspace-real-distance-carrier}. Attach
\autoref{thm:metricspace-public-law-package} and read the package through
\autoref{thm:metricspace-open-phase-consumer-interface}. The open real-algebra
and geometry phase therefore consumes exactly the finite real-distance carrier
and its public law rows, with no additional metric, topology, compactness,
connectedness, completion, angle, or covering quotient data.
\end{proof}
