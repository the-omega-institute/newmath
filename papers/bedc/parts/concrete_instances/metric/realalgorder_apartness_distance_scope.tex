\begin{theorem}[Metric RealAlgOrder apartness distance scope]
\label{thm:metric-realalgorder-apartness-distance-scope}
For constant-rational $\RealUp$ endpoints carried by the $\RealAlgOrder$
constant boundary, the finite packet supplied by
\autoref{thm:realalgorder-constant-apartness-metric-handoff} and read by
\autoref{thm:apartnessreal-metric-handoff} and
\autoref{thm:apartnessreal-metric-consumer-separation-boundary} is exactly the
$\MetricSpaceUp$ positive-separation distance scope.

The scope consists of the two endpoint histories, the positive
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$ radius or apartness row, the $\hsame$ transports, the
$\Cont$ routes, the visible $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance, and the $\NameCert$
ledger tying the endpoints to their constant-rational source. Repacked as a
metric consumer row, these data form the positive-separation side of a
$\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}$ boundary and expose no trichotomy,
completion, quotient-real equality, open-cover compactness, or host real
equality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the constant carrier, classifier, algebra, order, and apartness rows
from the $\RealAlgOrder$ constant boundary. By
\autoref{thm:realalgorder-constant-apartness-metric-handoff}, the consumer sees
only the two $\RealUp$ endpoint histories, their rational source witnesses, the
positive dyadic or apartness row, and the displayed $\hsame$, $\Cont$,
$\mathsf{Pkg}$, and $\NameCert$ provenance.

Apply the scoped certificate
\autoref{thm:realalgorder-constant-rational-scoped-certificate}. It keeps the
endpoint histories inside the constant-rational boundary and prevents any
additional real-order coordinate from being read by the downstream consumer.

Next apply \autoref{thm:apartnessreal-metric-handoff} and
\autoref{thm:apartnessreal-metric-consumer-separation-boundary}. These two
rows identify the same finite packet as apartness data consumable by metric
or order constructions, with the positive row and endpoint readbacks still
tied to the same $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and
$\NameCert$ ledger.

Finally repack the resulting finite separation row as the positive-separation
side of the $\MetricSpaceUp$ distance consumer boundary. The metric
visible-context and boundary exactness rows are used only to keep the BEDC
ledger stable under contextual prefixes and empty-boundary readback; they do
not add a coordinate for trichotomy, completion, quotient-real equality,
open-cover compactness, or host real equality.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Metric RealAlgOrder apartness positive reflection]
\label{thm:metric-realalgorder-apartness-positive-reflection}
For constant-rational $\RealUp$ endpoints, any finite packet satisfying
\autoref{thm:metric-realalgorder-apartness-distance-scope} and the
$\ApartnessRealUp$ metric consumer boundary determines exactly the
positive-separation component of the $\MetricSpaceUp$ distance witness. Every
metric consumer may read the same positive dyadic or apartness row, endpoint
histories, $\hsame$ transports, $\Cont$ routes, $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance, and
$\NameCert$ provenance supplied by the scoped packet. The reflection exports no
converse trichotomy, quotient-real equality, completion, or host equality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the constant-rational handoff
\autoref{thm:realalgorder-constant-apartness-metric-handoff}. Its packet gives
the endpoint histories, the rational source rows, and the positive dyadic or
apartness row that can be consumed without reading an additional real-order
coordinate.

Apply \autoref{thm:realalgorder-constant-rational-scoped-certificate}. The
certificate keeps those rows inside the constant-rational $\RealAlgOrder$
surface and records the displayed $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and
$\NameCert$ provenance.

Use \autoref{thm:apartnessreal-metric-handoff} together with
\autoref{thm:apartnessreal-metric-consumer-separation-boundary}. These results
identify the same positive row as the separation data that an
$\ApartnessRealUp$ metric consumer may read.

Finally apply
\autoref{thm:metric-realalgorder-apartness-distance-scope} and project the
positive side of the resulting $\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}$ boundary. This
is only a repacking of the displayed finite rows, so the metric consumer gains
no coordinate for converse trichotomy, quotient-real equality, completion, or
host equality.
\end{proof}
