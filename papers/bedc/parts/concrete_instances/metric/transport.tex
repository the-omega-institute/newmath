\begin{theorem}[Metric distance witness continuation hsame transport]
\label{thm:metric-distance-witness-cont-hsame-transport}
For histories $x,x',y,y',d,d':\mathsf{BHist}$, history-sameness of the two
endpoints and of the distance result transports the continuation field exposed
by a metric distance witness:
$$
\hsame(x,x')\Longrightarrow
\hsame(y,y')\Longrightarrow
\hsame(d,d')\Longrightarrow
\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}(x,y,d)\Longrightarrow
\ContR(x',y',d').
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Case-reduce the three supplied $\hsame$ witnesses. The transported endpoints
and result are then definitionally the endpoints and result of the original
metric distance witness. Project its continuation field
$\ContR(x,y,d)$, which is the required transported continuation ledger.
\end{proof}
