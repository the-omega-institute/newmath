\begin{definition}[MetricSpace real-distance carrier]
\label{def:metricspace-real-distance-carrier}
The $\MetricSpaceUp$ real-distance carrier for the open Real Algebra and
Geometry phase is the finite unary endpoint packet
$$
(x,y,d,r;\ w,b,c,p,a)
$$
where $x$ and $y$ are unary $\mathsf{BHist}$ endpoint histories, $w$ is the
displayed row $\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}(x,y,d)$, $b$ is the closed
ball-budget row $\mathsf{MetricBallBudget}_d(x,y;r)$, $c$ is the classifier
row $\hsame$ on endpoint and result histories, $p$ is the visible $\Cont$
provenance for the distance witness, and $a$ is the $\RealAlgOrder$ budget
packet consisting of the constant-rational endpoint rows, positive
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$ or apartness row, ordered-real comparison, addition,
transitivity, and addition-monotonicity rows used by the budget predicate.

The carrier exposes exactly these rows. It does not contain an ambient host
metric space, a quotient-real equality, a completeness principle, an
open-cover compactness principle, or a topology generator separate from the
displayed distance packet.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[MetricSpace public law package]
\label{thm:metricspace-public-law-package}
The real-distance carrier of
\autoref{def:metricspace-real-distance-carrier} supports the public
$\MetricSpaceUp$ distance laws needed by the open phase:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item symmetry, by transporting the endpoint order through
  $\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}$ symmetry and the RealAlgOrder apartness
  symmetric-separation row;
  \item identity boundary, by the empty-distance row and the empty-boundary
  visible-context rows;
  \item triangle-budget composition, by the visible-context triangle boundary
  and the $\mathsf{MetricBallBudget}$ composition row;
  \item carrier transport, by $\hsame$ transport of endpoints and result
  histories together with the displayed $\Cont$ provenance.
\end{enumerate}

These laws are public laws of the finite BEDC packet only. They do not assert
an arbitrary host metric, completeness, compactness, or a hidden equality on
real distances.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For symmetry, read the distance row through
\autoref{thm:metric-distance-witness-symmetric-classifier}. The positive
real-distance side is kept in the same packet by
\autoref{thm:realalgorder-apartness-metric-symmetric-separation}, so swapping
the endpoints changes only the orientation of the unary endpoint histories.

For the identity boundary, apply
\autoref{thm:metric-distance-witness-empty-distance-iff}. The visible-context
empty-boundary rows then show that a zero public distance read is exactly the
empty endpoint boundary up to $\hsame$, with no quotient-real equality added.

For triangle-budget composition, use
\autoref{thm:metricspace-visible-context-triangle-boundary} to form the
composite witness over the same endpoint and continuation rows. Then apply
\autoref{thm:metric-ball-budget-composition} to compose the two displayed
budget rows.

For carrier transport, use the endpoint and result $\hsame$ rows already
contained in \autoref{def:metricspace-real-distance-carrier}. The visible
$\Cont$ provenance identifies the transported witness with the same public
distance packet, so every exported law remains inside the carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetricSpace open-phase consumer interface]
\label{thm:metricspace-open-phase-consumer-interface}
Every $\SOneUp$ consumer and every later L10 consumer in the open Real
Algebra and Geometry phase reads $\MetricSpaceUp$ real-distance data only
through the carrier of \autoref{def:metricspace-real-distance-carrier} and the
law package of \autoref{thm:metricspace-public-law-package}. The consumer
surface is the RealAlgOrder apartness distance scope together with the existing
visible-context consumer surface; it exposes no additional host metric-space
coordinate, no completion row, and no open-cover compactness row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with
\autoref{thm:metric-realalgorder-apartness-distance-scope}. It identifies the
constant-rational endpoint histories, positive apartness or dyadic radius, and
visible $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and $\NameCert$ provenance available
to a metric-facing real-distance consumer.

Next apply
\autoref{thm:metricspace-visible-context-distance-consumer-surface}. That
consumer theorem restricts $\SOneUp$ and the named downstream metric consumers
to the visible-context distance witness, ball-budget, classifier, and finite
provenance rows.

The L10 consumers in the open phase can only compose with the same
RealAlgOrder apartness scope and the same visible-context public surface.
Therefore their readable interface is exactly
\autoref{def:metricspace-real-distance-carrier} equipped with
\autoref{thm:metricspace-public-law-package}, and no host metric,
completion, or open-cover compactness coordinate is exported.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetricSpace RealAlgOrder positive-distance public correspondence]
\label{thm:metricspace-realalgorder-positive-distance-public-correspondence}
For constant-rational $\RealUp$ endpoints, the positive component of the
$\MetricSpaceUp$ real-distance carrier of
\autoref{def:metricspace-real-distance-carrier} is equivalent to the finite
apartness packet isolated by
\autoref{thm:metric-realalgorder-apartness-distance-scope} and
\autoref{thm:metric-realalgorder-apartness-positive-reflection}. Concretely,
to read a positive public distance row is to read the two endpoint
$\mathsf{BHist}$ histories, the positive $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ radius or
apartness row, the displayed $\hsame$ transports, $\Cont$ routes,
$\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance, and $\NameCert$ ledger supplied by the
$\RealAlgOrder$ constant-rational boundary; conversely, those same rows repack
as the positive component of the carrier.

Endpoint reversal is supplied by
\autoref{thm:realalgorder-apartness-metric-symmetric-separation}. The
correspondence introduces no trichotomy, completion, quotient-real equality,
open-cover compactness, or host equality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:metricspace-real-distance-carrier}. Its
positive side is not a separate host metric object; it is the displayed
$\RealAlgOrder$ budget packet attached to the distance witness, classifier,
continuation, package, and name-certificate rows.

For the forward direction, read that budget packet through
\autoref{thm:metric-realalgorder-apartness-distance-scope}. Then apply
\autoref{thm:metric-realalgorder-apartness-positive-reflection} to identify
the positive public distance row with the finite apartness data available to a
metric-facing consumer.

For the reverse direction, start with the finite apartness packet named by the
same two theorems. The rows already match the carrier fields: endpoint
histories, positive dyadic or apartness data, $\hsame$ transports, $\Cont$
routes, $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance, and the $\NameCert$ ledger. Repacking these
rows with the carrier's displayed distance witness gives the positive
component of the public real-distance carrier.

Finally use
\autoref{thm:realalgorder-apartness-metric-symmetric-separation} for endpoint
reversal. Symmetry changes only the orientation of the two endpoint histories,
so the same finite packet remains the public positive-distance witness and no
trichotomy, completion, quotient-real equality, open-cover compactness, or host
equality is added.
\end{proof}
