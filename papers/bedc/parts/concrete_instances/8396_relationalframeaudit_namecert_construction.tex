\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RelationalFrameAuditUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-relationalframeaudit-namecert}
\origin{ai}

The $\RelationalFrameAuditUp$ packet turns the no-global-frame and observer-symmetry surface of \autoref{ch:visions-inter-hist-locality} into finite NameCert data. It consumes the configuration rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-multihistconfig-namecert}, the causal rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-crosshistcausal-namecert}, the unary-bound rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-maxcausalrate-namecert}, the refusal boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-noglobalsyncboundary-namecert}, and the ledger-local routing of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-inter-hist-locality-ledger-namecert}. Its public role is to audit a proposed comparison between two observer frames without admitting a global frame, host synchronization map, or metric primitive.

\begin{definition}[RelationalFrameAudit carrier]
\label{def:relational-frame-audit-carrier}
A $\RelationalFrameAuditUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
R=(M,A,B,Q,S,D,L,F,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the accepted $\MultiHistConfigUp$ row supplying the finite multi-history configuration;
\item $A$ and $B$ are the two displayed observer-history slots whose local frame comparison is being audited;
\item $Q$ is the frame-request row, recording the requested comparison as a finite local read rather than as an ambient coordinate system;
\item $S$ is the observer-symmetry audit row, requiring both directions of the comparison to be visible before the request is accepted;
\item $D$ is the $\CrossHistCausalUp$ row used by the comparison request, read only after the two observer slots are displayed;
\item $L$ is the $\MaxCausalRateUp$ row that bounds the causal read without introducing a host speed, metric tensor, or synchronized clock;
\item $F$ is the $\NoGlobalSyncBoundaryUp$ refusal verdict row for any request that asks for a privileged global frame, total observer order, selected synchronization map, or detached frame comparison;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $M,A,B,Q,S,D,L,F$ and the structural rows;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ routes from the frame request either through the two-Hist route $A,B,D,L,S$ or to the refusal row $F$;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RelationalFrameAuditUp$, $\MultiHistConfigUp$, $\CrossHistCausalUp$, $\MaxCausalRateUp$, $\NoGlobalSyncBoundaryUp$, $\InterHistLocalityLedgerUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RelationalFrameAuditUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exports a finite audit boundary for frame-comparison requests. It has no coordinate for a global frame, shared clock, metric tensor, host Lorentz chart, selected synchronization function, or comparison detached from the displayed histories.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[RelationalFrameAudit NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:relational-frame-audit-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:relational-frame-audit-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\RelationalFrameAuditUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for
$$
R=(M,A,B,Q,S,D,L,F,H,C,P,N),
$$
two-Hist locality through $M,A,B$, frame-request exposure through $Q$, observer-symmetry audit through $S$, cross-Hist causal routing through $D$, maximum-rate boundary reading through $L$, no-global-frame refusal through $F$, and transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming through $H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:relational-frame-audit-carrier}. The tuple displays the configuration row, two observer slots, frame request, symmetry audit, causal route, rate boundary, refusal verdict, transport, continuation, provenance, and local name.

The positive comparison route first reads $M,A,B$. The causal row $D$ and maximum-rate row $L$ can then be consumed as finite inter-Hist data, with the observer-symmetry row $S$ requiring that both comparison directions remain visible.

The negative route is the refusal verdict $F$. By the no-global-synchronization boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-noglobalsyncboundary-namecert}, a request for a privileged frame, shared clock, total observer order, or selected synchronization map is retained as refused data rather than promoted to an admitted coordinate.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ only transport, replay, package, and name the same rows. Thus the NameCert surface is exhausted by the displayed packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RelationalFrameAudit no-global-frame refusal]
\label{thm:relational-frame-audit-no-global-frame-refusal}
Every accepted $\RelationalFrameAuditUp$ packet that receives a frame request outside the displayed two-Hist route sends that request to the refusal verdict $F$. The accepted packet exports no global frame, shared clock, total observer order, selected synchronization map, host Lorentz chart, metric tensor, or detached comparison row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier inventory. The only frame-facing rows are the request row $Q$, the two observer slots $A,B$, the causal and rate rows $D,L$, the observer-symmetry row $S$, and the refusal row $F$.

Apply the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:relational-frame-audit-namecert-obligations}. A supported request must travel through the displayed two-Hist route; an unsupported request has no row except the no-global-frame refusal verdict.

Use the no-global-synchronization boundary from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-noglobalsyncboundary-namecert}. Since the missing global objects are not coordinates of $R=(M,A,B,Q,S,D,L,F,H,C,P,N)$, no continuation or naming projection can export them as admitted data.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RelationalFrameAudit observer-symmetry factorization]
\label{thm:relational-frame-audit-observer-symmetry-factorization}
Any accepted $\RelationalFrameAuditUp$ packet that exposes a frame comparison factors the read through the displayed two-Hist route
$$
A,B \longrightarrow D \longrightarrow L \longrightarrow S
$$
together with componentwise $\hsame$ transport in $H$ and displayed $\Cont$ replay in $C$. If the request does not factor through this route, the packet reads it only through the refusal verdict $F$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the frame-request row $Q$ from the carrier. The request has only two public exits: the displayed comparison route through $A,B,D,L,S$ or the refusal route through $F$.

For the supported exit, read the two observer-history rows from $M$ and then apply the cross-Hist causal and maximum-rate sibling surfaces of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-crosshistcausal-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-maxcausalrate-namecert}. The comparison remains local because $D$ and $L$ are finite rows over the displayed multi-Hist configuration.

The row $S$ supplies the observer-symmetry audit. Both directions must be visible before the packet exports the comparison, and $H$ and $C$ only transport and replay the same factorized route.

For the unsupported exit, apply \autoref{thm:relational-frame-audit-no-global-frame-refusal}. A request that does not pass through the two-Hist route has no accepted destination except $F$.
\end{proof}
