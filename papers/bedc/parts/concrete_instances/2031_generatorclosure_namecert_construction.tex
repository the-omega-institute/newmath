\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\GeneratorClosureUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-generatorclosure-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\GeneratorClosureUp$ interface records the finite naming surface for an authorized generator together with the classifier that refuses continuations outside the generator's constructor list. It is the concrete BEDC certificate side of the generator-closure classifier in \autoref{def:certificate-closure-generator-closure-classifier}: the packet exposes the generator rows, the constructor-list provenance, the continuation witnesses, and the $\mathsf{GAP}$ ledger that records every attempted escape from the authorized closure.

\begin{definition}[GeneratorClosure NameCert carrier]
\label{def:generator-closure-namecert-carrier}
A $\GeneratorClosureUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(G,L,A,Q,W,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $G$ is the authorized generator row naming the closed inductive source whose outputs are admitted;
\item $L$ is the finite constructor-list provenance row for $G$, including the displayed closure witness for each constructor;
\item $A$ is the authorized-row ledger stating which observed rows are generated by $L$;
\item $Q$ is the generator-closure classifier row comparing authorized generator rows componentwise;
\item $W$ is the continuation-witness ledger, recording for each finite continuation read whether the same constructor-list witness remains present;
\item $H$ stores displayed $\hsame$ transports for $G,L,A,Q,W$ and the local naming rows;
\item $C$ stores the finite $\Cont$ routes by which consumers read authorized rows, constructor provenance, continuation witnesses, and rejected non-closure attempts;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, authorized generator rows, constructor-list provenance, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and the $\mathsf{GAP}$ ledger;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\GeneratorClosureUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exports only the finite generator row, constructor-list provenance, authorized-row ledger, classifier row, continuation witnesses, transport rows, continuation routes, package provenance, local naming row, and $\mathsf{GAP}$ non-escape evidence. It contains no free-floating induction principle and no universal closure over objects outside the displayed generator.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[GeneratorClosure classifier stability]
\label{thm:generator-closure-classifier-stability}
Displayed $\hsame$ transport preserving the authorized generator row $G$, constructor-list provenance $L$, authorized-row ledger $A$, continuation ledger $W$, transport rows $H$, continuation routes $C$, package provenance $P$, and local $\NameCert$ row $N$ preserves the $\GeneratorClosureUp$ classifier $Q$. In particular, two authorized generator rows classified through the same constructor-list provenance retain the same accepted-row and rejected-continuation boundary.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier of \autoref{def:generator-closure-namecert-carrier} makes the classifier componentwise. Transport of $G$ and $L$ preserves the generator source and its closed constructor list, so the authorized-row ledger $A$ is read against the same generating surface.

Transport of $W$ preserves the witness attached to each finite continuation read. The routes $H$ and $C$ then replay the same classifier reads, and the package and local naming rows repack the transported packet without adding an external induction rule.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GeneratorClosure continuation witness preservation]
\label{thm:generator-closure-continuation-witness}
Let $K$ be an accepted $\GeneratorClosureUp$ carrier. Every finite $\Cont$ extension that reads only the displayed authorized generator row, constructor-list provenance, authorized-row ledger, classifier row, and local naming row preserves the continuation witness in $W$. If the extension asks for a row without the constructor-list witness required by $L$, the read is recorded as a $\mathsf{GAP}$ ledger boundary rather than as a continuation of $K$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the extension through the displayed $\Cont$ route $C$. If the route lands in $G,L,A,Q,W,H,C,P,N$, the continuation witness is already one of the stored rows, so extending the read only appends a finite access path to the same witness.

If the route asks for a row outside the constructor-list provenance, the carrier has no coordinate that can supply a closure witness. The only visible output is the $\mathsf{GAP}$ non-escape row in the package provenance, so the attempted read is recorded as a boundary and not as a generated continuation.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GeneratorClosure NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:generator-closure-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\GeneratorClosureUp}$ surface for \autoref{def:generator-closure-namecert-carrier} consists of five BEDC-native rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission over the finite $\BHist$ packet $K$;
\item $\Pkg$ provenance naming the authorized generator row, constructor-list provenance, classifier row, continuation-witness ledger, $\hsame$ transports, $\Cont$ routes, and local naming row;
\item $\NameCert$ exactness stating that the local certificate classifies exactly the displayed generator-closure packet;
\item constructor-list provenance stating that every accepted row is generated by the finite constructor list $L$ and every continuation witness is read against that same list;
\item $\mathsf{GAP}$ ledger non-escape stating that rows without the required constructor-list witness are recorded as boundary attempts and are not exported as generator-internal continuations.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection of the tuple in \autoref{def:generator-closure-namecert-carrier}. The generator row, constructor list, authorized ledger, classifier row, continuation witnesses, transports, routes, provenance, and local naming row are all visible coordinates of one finite $\BHist$ packet.

The package and naming rows identify the same packet. Classifier stability from \autoref{thm:generator-closure-classifier-stability} supplies the transport behavior, while continuation witness preservation from \autoref{thm:generator-closure-continuation-witness} supplies the finite extension behavior.

The constructor-list and $\mathsf{GAP}$ clauses are the two remaining projections. The former says every accepted row is read through $L$; the latter says a read lacking that witness is recorded as non-escape evidence rather than as an admitted continuation.
\end{proof}
