\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\GeneratorClosureUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-generatorclosure-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\GeneratorClosureUp$ interface records the finite naming surface for an authorized generator together with the classifier that refuses continuations outside the generator's constructor list. It is the concrete BEDC certificate side of the generator-closure classifier in \autoref{def:certificate-closure-generator-closure-classifier}: the packet exposes the generator rows, the constructor-list provenance, the continuation witnesses, and the $\mathsf{GAP}$ ledger that records every attempted escape from the authorized closure.

\begin{definition}[GeneratorClosure NameCert carrier]
\label{def:generator-closure-namecert-carrier}
A $\GeneratorClosureUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(G,L,A,Q,W,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $G$ is the authorized generator row naming the closed inductive source whose outputs are admitted;
\item $L$ is the finite constructor-list provenance row for $G$, including the displayed closure witness for each constructor;
\item $A$ is the authorized-row ledger stating which observed rows are generated by $L$;
\item $Q$ is the generator-closure classifier row comparing authorized generator rows componentwise;
\item $W$ is the continuation-witness ledger, recording for each finite continuation read whether the same constructor-list witness remains present;
\item $H$ stores displayed $\hsame$ transports for $G,L,A,Q,W$ and the local naming rows;
\item $C$ stores the finite $\Cont$ routes by which consumers read authorized rows, constructor provenance, continuation witnesses, and rejected non-closure attempts;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, authorized generator rows, constructor-list provenance, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and the $\mathsf{GAP}$ ledger;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\GeneratorClosureUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exports only the finite generator row, constructor-list provenance, authorized-row ledger, classifier row, continuation witnesses, transport rows, continuation routes, package provenance, local naming row, and $\mathsf{GAP}$ non-escape evidence. It contains no free-floating induction principle and no universal closure over objects outside the displayed generator.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[GeneratorClosure classifier stability]
\label{thm:generator-closure-classifier-stability}
Displayed $\hsame$ transport preserving the authorized generator row $G$, constructor-list provenance $L$, authorized-row ledger $A$, continuation ledger $W$, transport rows $H$, continuation routes $C$, package provenance $P$, and local $\NameCert$ row $N$ preserves the $\GeneratorClosureUp$ classifier $Q$. In particular, two authorized generator rows classified through the same constructor-list provenance retain the same accepted-row and rejected-continuation boundary.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier of \autoref{def:generator-closure-namecert-carrier} makes the classifier componentwise. Transport of $G$ and $L$ preserves the generator source and its closed constructor list, so the authorized-row ledger $A$ is read against the same generating surface.

Transport of $W$ preserves the witness attached to each finite continuation read. The routes $H$ and $C$ then replay the same classifier reads, and the package and local naming rows repack the transported packet without adding an external induction rule.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GeneratorClosure continuation witness preservation]
\label{thm:generator-closure-continuation-witness}
Let $K$ be an accepted $\GeneratorClosureUp$ carrier. Every finite $\Cont$ extension that reads only the displayed authorized generator row, constructor-list provenance, authorized-row ledger, classifier row, and local naming row preserves the continuation witness in $W$. If the extension asks for a row without the constructor-list witness required by $L$, the read is recorded as a $\mathsf{GAP}$ ledger boundary rather than as a continuation of $K$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the extension through the displayed $\Cont$ route $C$. If the route lands in $G,L,A,Q,W,H,C,P,N$, the continuation witness is already one of the stored rows, so extending the read only appends a finite access path to the same witness.

If the route asks for a row outside the constructor-list provenance, the carrier has no coordinate that can supply a closure witness. The only visible output is the $\mathsf{GAP}$ non-escape row in the package provenance, so the attempted read is recorded as a boundary and not as a generated continuation.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GeneratorClosure NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:generator-closure-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\GeneratorClosureUp}$ surface for \autoref{def:generator-closure-namecert-carrier} consists of five BEDC-native rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission over the finite $\BHist$ packet $K$;
\item $\Pkg$ provenance naming the authorized generator row, constructor-list provenance, classifier row, continuation-witness ledger, $\hsame$ transports, $\Cont$ routes, and local naming row;
\item $\NameCert$ exactness stating that the local certificate classifies exactly the displayed generator-closure packet;
\item constructor-list provenance stating that every accepted row is generated by the finite constructor list $L$ and every continuation witness is read against that same list;
\item $\mathsf{GAP}$ ledger non-escape stating that rows without the required constructor-list witness are recorded as boundary attempts and are not exported as generator-internal continuations.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection of the tuple in \autoref{def:generator-closure-namecert-carrier}. The generator row, constructor list, authorized ledger, classifier row, continuation witnesses, transports, routes, provenance, and local naming row are all visible coordinates of one finite $\BHist$ packet.

The package and naming rows identify the same packet. Classifier stability from \autoref{thm:generator-closure-classifier-stability} supplies the transport behavior, while continuation witness preservation from \autoref{thm:generator-closure-continuation-witness} supplies the finite extension behavior.

The constructor-list and $\mathsf{GAP}$ clauses are the two remaining projections. The former says every accepted row is read through $L$; the latter says a read lacking that witness is recorded as non-escape evidence rather than as an admitted continuation.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GeneratorClosure carrier obligation]
\label{thm:generatorclosure-carrier-obligation}
For a finite $\BHist$ packet $K=(G,L,A,Q,W,H,C,P,N)$ as in \autoref{def:generator-closure-namecert-carrier}, carrier admission for $\GeneratorClosureUp$ is exactly the displayed generator row $G$, constructor-list provenance $L$, authorized-row ledger $A$, generator-closure classifier row $Q$, continuation-witness ledger $W$, displayed $\hsame$ transports $H$, finite $\Cont$ routes $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance $P$, and local $\NameCert$ row $N$. Every generated closure witness exported by the carrier is produced by a row of $L$ and recorded in $A$ or $W$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.GeneratorClosureUp.GeneratorClosurePacket\_carrier\_obligation}
\begin{proof}
Project the tuple in \autoref{def:generator-closure-namecert-carrier}. The only generator source is $G$, the only constructor-list provenance is $L$, and the only accepted-row ledger is $A$.

Now read any exported closure witness through the displayed route $C$. If the read is accepted, the route reaches $L$ and records the result in $A$ or $W$; if it is not accepted, the carrier has no other row that can supply generated closure data.

The rows $H,P,N$ transport and name the same packet. They do not add a second generator, an external induction rule, or a closure witness outside the displayed constructor list.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GeneratorClosure classifier obligation]
\label{thm:generatorclosure-classifier-obligation}
Displayed $\hsame$ transport for an accepted $\GeneratorClosureUp$ carrier preserves generator endpoints and generated closure rows. In particular, if two packets transport the same $G,L,A,Q,W,H,C,P,N$ surface, then the $\GeneratorClosureUp$ classifier preserves the endpoints named by $G$ and the generated rows recorded by $A$ and $W$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.GeneratorClosureUp.GeneratorClosurePacket\_classifier\_obligation}
\begin{proof}
Apply the classifier stability theorem \autoref{thm:generator-closure-classifier-stability}. Transport of $G$ preserves the generator endpoint, and transport of $L$ preserves the constructor-list provenance against which generated rows are checked.

The accepted rows in $A$ and the continuation witnesses in $W$ are transported along the same displayed $\hsame$ rows $H$. The continuation routes $C$, package provenance $P$, and local naming row $N$ then repack the transported data as the same classifier-facing packet.

Thus the classifier can move only the displayed generator endpoint and generated closure rows; it cannot introduce an endpoint or generated row outside the transported carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GeneratorClosure ledger obligation]
\label{thm:generatorclosure-ledger-obligation}
Every exported $\GeneratorClosureUp$ read factors through the displayed generator row $G$, constructor-list provenance $L$, authorized-row ledger $A$, continuation-witness ledger $W$, displayed $\hsame$ transport $H$, finite $\Cont$ route $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance $P$, and local $\NameCert$ row $N$. A read without the constructor-list witness required by $L$ is recorded by the $\mathsf{GAP}$ non-escape boundary and is not exported as a generated closure row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier obligation of \autoref{thm:generatorclosure-carrier-obligation}. It identifies every exported closure witness with a finite read through $G,L,A,Q,W,H,C,P,N$.

Use continuation witness preservation from \autoref{thm:generator-closure-continuation-witness}. A route that keeps the constructor-list witness remains inside $W$ and is therefore exported through the same packet.

For a route without that witness, the carrier exposes no generated closure coordinate. The read is therefore recorded as a $\mathsf{GAP}$ boundary by the package provenance and cannot be repacked by $N$ as an accepted generated row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GeneratorClosure authorized-row exhaustion]
\label{thm:generator-closure-authorized-row-exhaustion}
Every exported generated row of an accepted $\GeneratorClosureUp$ packet factors through the displayed generator row $G$, constructor-list provenance $L$, authorized-row ledger $A$, continuation-witness ledger $W$, and the finite constructor-list provenance carried by $L$. No generated row is exported from a rule outside the displayed constructor list.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with \autoref{thm:generatorclosure-carrier-obligation}. It identifies the only carrier rows that can export generated closure data and places every exported closure witness in the finite packet $G,L,A,Q,W,H,C,P,N$.

Use \autoref{thm:generator-closure-classifier-stability} to transport the authorized generator row and constructor-list provenance componentwise. Since classifier transport preserves the same $G$ and $L$ surface, it cannot create a second source of generated rows.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:generatorclosure-ledger-obligation}. Its ledger boundary sends every accepted read through $A$ or $W$ and records every read without the required constructor-list witness as a $\mathsf{GAP}$ boundary.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GeneratorClosure Cont route GAP separation]
\label{thm:generator-closure-cont-route-gap-separation}
Every displayed $\Cont$ route of an accepted $\GeneratorClosureUp$ packet either preserves the constructor-list witness carried by $L$ or lands in the displayed $\mathsf{GAP}$ boundary. A continuation route never becomes a free induction rule and never exports a generated row without the constructor-list witness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the route through \autoref{thm:generator-closure-continuation-witness}. A finite extension that keeps the displayed generator row, constructor-list provenance, authorized ledger, classifier row, and local name preserves the witness already stored in $W$.

If the route lacks that witness, the same theorem records the attempted read as a $\mathsf{GAP}$ ledger boundary. The carrier has no coordinate for an external induction rule, so the missing witness cannot be recovered from another row.

Apply \autoref{thm:generator-closure-namecert-obligations} to repack the two outcomes as the local certificate surface. The $\Cont$ route is therefore separated into witness-preserving reads and displayed $\mathsf{GAP}$ reads.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GeneratorClosure NameCert public consumer boundary]
\label{thm:generator-closure-namecert-public-consumer-boundary}
The public consumer boundary for an accepted $\GeneratorClosureUp$ packet is exhausted by the displayed $\BHist$ carrier, componentwise $\hsame$ transports, finite $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, $\mathsf{GAP}$ non-escape rows, and the local $\NameCert_{\GeneratorClosureUp}$ row. Every public read of a generated row factors through the authorized generator row $G$, constructor-list provenance $L$, authorized-row ledger $A$, continuation witness $W$, and the separated $\mathsf{GAP}$ boundary.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First use \autoref{thm:generator-closure-authorized-row-exhaustion}. It fixes the generated-row surface to $G,L,A,W$ and the finite constructor-list provenance, so a public consumer has no generated-row source outside the displayed packet.

Next use \autoref{thm:generator-closure-cont-route-gap-separation}. It separates every continuation read into a witness-preserving route or a displayed $\mathsf{GAP}$ boundary, excluding free induction rules from the consumer surface.

Now combine \autoref{thm:generator-closure-namecert-obligations} with \autoref{thm:generatorclosure-ledger-obligation}. The NameCert rows package carrier admission, provenance, exactness, constructor-list reads, and $\mathsf{GAP}$ non-escape, while the ledger theorem routes every exported read through the same finite rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GeneratorClosure public bridge schema handoff]
\label{thm:generatorclosure-public-bridge-schema-handoff}
The public $\GeneratorClosureUp$ packet hands off to downstream schema readers only through the displayed rows $G,L,A,Q,W,H,C,P,N$. Thus a downstream schema may read the authorized generator row, constructor-list provenance, authorized-row ledger, classifier row, continuation-witness ledger, displayed $\hsame$ transports, finite $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, local $\NameCert$ row, and displayed $\mathsf{GAP}$ boundary, but it receives no second generator source, no independent constructor rule, and no host-side recursor.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the carrier obligation in \autoref{thm:generatorclosure-carrier-obligation}. It identifies the public packet with the finite $\BHist$ coordinates $G,L,A,Q,W,H,C,P,N$, so every schema-facing read begins from the displayed carrier.

Apply the classifier obligation in \autoref{thm:generatorclosure-classifier-obligation}. The classifier preserves only the endpoints named by $G$ and the generated rows recorded by $A$ and $W$ under the same constructor-list provenance $L$.

Use \autoref{thm:generator-closure-continuation-witness} for finite continuation reads, and then apply the ledger obligation in \autoref{thm:generatorclosure-ledger-obligation}. A read that carries the required constructor-list witness remains inside $W$; a read without that witness is recorded by the displayed $\mathsf{GAP}$ boundary. These are exactly the rows available to the downstream schema.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GeneratorClosure fixed-point consumer obligation]
\label{thm:generatorclosure-fixedpoint-consumer-obligation}
Every $\GeneratorFixedPointUp$ consumer of a $\GeneratorClosureUp$ packet must read the fixed witness through the $\GeneratorClosureUp$ constructor-list provenance and classifier surface. Concretely, the fixed witness is admissible only when its read factors through $G,L,A,Q,W,H,C,P,N$ and the constructor-list row $L$ supplies the witness required by the classifier row $Q$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier obligation \autoref{thm:generatorclosure-carrier-obligation} fixes the generator-closure packet available to the consumer. Hence the consumer cannot obtain a fixed witness from a row outside $G,L,A,Q,W,H,C,P,N$.

The classifier obligation \autoref{thm:generatorclosure-classifier-obligation} transports the generator endpoint and generated rows along the same displayed surface. A fixed-point read therefore uses the classifier row $Q$ only together with the constructor-list provenance $L$ and the accepted-row or continuation-witness ledgers $A$ and $W$.

Finally, continuation witness preservation from \autoref{thm:generator-closure-continuation-witness} and the ledger obligation \autoref{thm:generatorclosure-ledger-obligation} separate the two possible outcomes. With the constructor-list witness, the fixed witness is read from the displayed continuation surface; without that witness, the attempted read is a $\mathsf{GAP}$ boundary and is not a fixed witness for $\GeneratorFixedPointUp$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GeneratorClosure downstream ledger exhaustion]
\label{thm:generatorclosure-downstream-ledger-exhaustion}
Every downstream generator consumer of the public $\GeneratorClosureUp$ packet factors through the displayed $\BHist$ carrier, $\hsame$ transport rows, $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, local $\NameCert$ row, constructor-list provenance, and $\mathsf{GAP}$ ledger. No downstream consumer receives a free-floating induction principle, an unledgered generator continuation, or a host recursor exported outside those rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use \autoref{thm:generatorclosure-carrier-obligation} to place the consumer read in the finite $\BHist$ packet. The only visible generator source is $G$, the only constructor-list provenance is $L$, and the only public ledgers for generated rows and continuation witnesses are $A$ and $W$.

Apply \autoref{thm:generatorclosure-classifier-obligation} to transport the classifier-facing endpoints and generated rows along $H$. The $\Cont$ routes $C$, package provenance $P$, and local certificate row $N$ repack the same transported rows and do not expose another induction principle.

It remains to exhaust failed or boundary reads. By \autoref{thm:generator-closure-continuation-witness}, a continuation lacking the constructor-list witness cannot remain a generated continuation. The ledger obligation \autoref{thm:generatorclosure-ledger-obligation} records such a read as a displayed $\mathsf{GAP}$ boundary, so every downstream read is accounted for by the public ledger surface.
\end{proof}
