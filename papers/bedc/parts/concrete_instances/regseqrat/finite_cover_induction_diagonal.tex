\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat finite-cover induction diagonal]
\label{thm:regseqrat-finite-cover-induction-diagonal}
Let $\{S_i\}_{i \in I}$ be a finite family of accepted $\RegSeqRatUp$ terminal sections for one Phase $\RealUp$ and Completion consumer. Assume every $S_i$ is read through a common $\StreamNameUp$ window family, a displayed $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, a $\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier and regularity ledger, a local $\RealUp$ seal row, and the shared support
$$
\BHist,\quad \hsame,\quad \Cont,\quad \Pkg,\quad \NameCert.
$$
Assume also that the accepted pairwise overlap rows glue by the finite-cover gluing theorem, that adjoining one more section preserves the induction-step rows, that the gluing operation is associative on displayed overlaps, and that every two local presentations admit the common-refinement rows of the RegSeqRat cover surface. Then finite induction over $I$ produces a single diagonal terminal window through which all local $\RealUp$ seal reads factor.

The diagonal terminal window exposes exactly the common $\StreamNameUp$ windows, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance rows, $\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier and regularity ledgers, local $\RealUp$ seal rows, and displayed support rows named above. It exports no quotient stream equality, host real equality, selected limit, countable-choice schedule, metric-completeness datum, completion point, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Induct on the finite cover. In the empty boundary there is no terminal seal read to compare, and in the singleton case the accepted section itself supplies the diagonal terminal window with its displayed StreamName, DyadicRatCore, RegSeqRat, Real, and support rows.

For the induction step, suppose a finite subcover has already been packed into one diagonal terminal window. Adjoin the next accepted section $S_j$. The common-refinement rows place the old diagonal window and $S_j$ over one common $\StreamNameUp$ window family and the same displayed dyadic tolerance ledger.

Apply the RegSeqRat finite-cover gluing row to the old diagonal window and the new local section. The induction-step hypotheses preserve the classifier rows, regularity ledgers, local Real seal rows, and the shared $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support while the two packets are glued.

Associativity identifies the result with any parenthesized order of adjoining the same finite sections. Thus the terminal window obtained after the last induction step is independent of the chosen bracketing and is the single diagonal window for the whole family.

Finally use the existing common-refinement determinacy and Real seal handoff rows. They force every local seal consumer to factor through the diagonal window just constructed, and they leave no coordinate for quotient streams, host real equality, selected limits, countable choice, metric completeness, completion points, or ambient Real completeness.
\end{proof}
