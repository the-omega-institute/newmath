\begin{definition}[RegSeqRat common-tail window packet]
\label{def:regseqrat-common-tail-window-packet}
A $\RegSeqRatUp$ common-tail window packet is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet over one accepted $\RegSeqRatUp$ source $P$ with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item two certified cofinal tail shifts $T_0$ and $T_1$ of the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule carried by $P$;
\item one common $\StreamNameUp$ refinement window $W_\ast$ through which the selected tail windows of $T_0$ and $T_1$ both restrict;
\item the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint rows read on $W_\ast$, together with the displayed radius rows and regularity bounds
$$
2^{-n}+2^{-m}
$$
for the selected unary indices;
\item the $\mathsf{RegSeqRatClassifier}$ rows for the two tail-window reads and for their transported read on $W_\ast$;
\item the $\RealUp$ seal-source rows consumed from the same common finite surface by \autoref{thm:regseqrat-real-seal-handoff};
\item the componentwise $\hsame$ rows, displayed $\Cont$ routes, $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ provenance attached to the StreamName, DyadicRatCore, RegSeqRat, and RealUp-facing rows.
\end{enumerate}
The packet is only a finite common-tail source surface. It stores no quotient stream, host real value, selected limit, metric-completion point, $\RealUp$ completeness datum, or standard-bridge coordinate beyond the displayed rows.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat common-tail window classifier bridge]
\label{thm:regseqrat-common-tail-window-classifier-bridge}
Let $P$ be an accepted $\RegSeqRatUp$ source, and let $T_0$ and $T_1$ be two certified cofinal tail shifts of the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule of $P$. Suppose two tail-window classifier reads land in a common-tail window packet of \autoref{def:regseqrat-common-tail-window-packet} with common refinement $W_\ast$. Then the two reads determine the same $\RealUp$ seal-source surface and the same $\mathsf{RegSeqRatClassifier}$ read after restriction to $W_\ast$.

The bridge conclusion is a statement about the displayed finite packet only. It exports no quotient stream, host real equality, selected limit, metric-completion point, $\RealUp$ completeness datum, or additional seal coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First use \autoref{thm:regseqrat-tail-stable-cover-common-window-lock}. The two seal consumers carried by the cofinal tail shifts factor through one accepted common finite window, and the visible rows of that window are exactly the StreamName schedule rows, DyadicRatCore endpoint and radius ledgers, RegSeqRat classifier and regularity ledgers, and the displayed provenance.

Next read the common-tail packet rows componentwise. The definition lists the cofinal tail shifts, the common $\StreamNameUp$ refinement $W_\ast$, the DyadicRatCore endpoint and radius ledgers, the classifier rows, the $\RealUp$ seal-source rows, and the $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and $\NameCert$ provenance. Thus each tail-window read restricts to the same finite source surface on $W_\ast$.

Apply \autoref{thm:regseqrat-refined-window-gluing-real-seal-factorization}. The refined-window gluing surface factors the RealUp-facing handoff through the displayed finite packet $W_\ast$, so the two tail-window origins cannot supply separate seal-source surfaces once their rows have been restricted to the common refinement.

Use \autoref{thm:regseqrat-streamname-dyadic-window-lock} to lock the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule rows to the same $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint and radius ledgers inside the common window. The classifier reads inspect only those locked rows together with the regularity ledgers and provenance, so their transported RegSeqRat classifier read on $W_\ast$ is the same displayed read.

Finally invoke \autoref{thm:regseqrat-real-seal-handoff}. The $\RealUp$ seal consumes exactly the accepted regular-rational finite source surface already identified, and the carrier has no coordinate for quotient streams, host real equality, selected limits, metric completions, completeness data, or further seal coordinates.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat common-tail window terminality]
\label{thm:regseqrat-common-tail-window-terminality}
Let $P$ be an accepted $\RegSeqRatUp$ packet, and let $T_0$ and $T_1$ be two certified cofinal tail shifts of the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule carried by $P$. Suppose the two shifted tail reads land in a common-tail window packet of \autoref{def:regseqrat-common-tail-window-packet} with common refinement $W_\ast$. Every completion-facing $\RealUp$ consumer whose visible rows are exactly the two shifted reads, the common $\StreamNameUp$ window $W_\ast$, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint, radius, and regularity rows, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier rows, the $\RealUp$ seal-source rows, the displayed $\hsame$ rows, displayed $\Cont$ routes, $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ provenance factors through that common-tail packet.

The terminal factorization exports no quotient stream, host real equality, selected limit, metric-completion point, $\RealUp$ completeness datum, or additional seal coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply \autoref{thm:regseqrat-tail-stable-cover-common-window-lock} to the two cofinal tail shifts. The tail-stable cover places both completion-facing seal consumers on one accepted common finite window with the StreamName schedule rows, DyadicRatCore endpoint and radius ledgers, RegSeqRat classifier and regularity ledgers, and displayed provenance.

Next use \autoref{thm:regseqrat-refined-window-gluing-real-seal-factorization}. The common refinement $W_\ast$ repacks the two shifted tail reads into one RealUp-facing seal source, so every consumer whose visible rows are limited to the displayed shifts and the common window is already routed through the common-tail packet.

Apply \autoref{thm:regseqrat-streamname-dyadic-window-lock}. It locks the common $\StreamNameUp$ refinement to the same $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint, radius, and regularity rows, leaving the consumer no separate dyadic or schedule coordinate outside $W_\ast$.

Now invoke \autoref{thm:regseqrat-common-tail-window-classifier-bridge}. The transported classifier read and the transported seal-source rows are the same rows listed by the common-tail packet, including the displayed $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and $\NameCert$ provenance.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:regseqrat-real-seal-handoff}. Since the $\RealUp$ seal consumes exactly this accepted regular-rational finite source surface, the consumer factors through the common-tail packet. Quotient streams, host real equality, selected limits, metric-completion points, $\RealUp$ completeness data, and extra seal coordinates have no displayed carrier row from which to be read.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat common-tail finite-request Real seal pullback]
\label{thm:regseqrat-common-tail-finite-request-real-seal-pullback}
Let $P$ be an accepted $\RegSeqRatUp$ packet, let $T_0$ and $T_1$ be two certified cofinal tail shifts of the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule carried by $P$, and let the two shifted tail reads land in a common-tail window packet of \autoref{def:regseqrat-common-tail-window-packet} with common refinement $W_\ast$. For any finite-request $\RealUp$ seal consumer whose request is visible along that common-tail packet, the visible pullback of the consumer is exactly the finite source surface listed in \autoref{def:regseqrat-common-tail-window-packet}: the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule and refinement rows, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint, radius, and distance rows, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier and regularity rows, the $\RealUp$ seal-source rows, componentwise $\hsame$, displayed $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ provenance.

Equivalently, the common-tail window source surface and the finite-request $\RealUp$ seal pullback present the same BEDC-visible rows. The equivalence exports no quotient stream, selected limit, host real equality, ambient completeness row, hidden completion carrier, or second $\RealUp$ seal coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply \autoref{thm:regseqrat-common-tail-window-terminality}. Any completion-facing consumer whose visible rows are the shifted tail reads, the common window $W_\ast$, the dyadic ledger, classifier, seal-source rows, transports, continuations, package provenance, and local naming must factor through the common-tail packet.

Next use \autoref{thm:regseqrat-tail-budget-window-seal-pullback}. A finite-request $\RealUp$ seal consumer has visible pullback exactly the StreamName schedule row, DyadicRatCore endpoint, radius, and distance rows, RegSeqRat classifier and regularity rows, displayed $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ row.

Now apply \autoref{thm:regseqrat-shared-window-terminal-pullback-equivalence}. It identifies equality of the shared selector surface with equality of the terminal $\RealUp$ seal reads after both requests have been read through the ordered finite packet from $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ to $\RealUp$. Thus the common-tail presentation and the finite-request pullback presentation determine one another on the same displayed rows.

Finally invoke \autoref{thm:regseqrat-real-seal-selector-nonescape}. The selector boundary removes the excluded coordinates from both descriptions: quotient streams, selected limits, host real equality, ambient completeness data, hidden completion carriers, and second seal data have no row in either surface. The remaining rows are precisely the StreamName schedule and refinement rows, DyadicRatCore endpoint, radius, and distance rows, RegSeqRat classifier and regularity rows, RealUp seal-source rows, componentwise $\hsame$, displayed $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ provenance listed in \autoref{def:regseqrat-common-tail-window-packet}.
\end{proof}
