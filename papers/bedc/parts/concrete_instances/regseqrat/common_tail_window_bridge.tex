\begin{definition}[RegSeqRat common-tail window packet]
\label{def:regseqrat-common-tail-window-packet}
A $\RegSeqRatUp$ common-tail window packet is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet over one accepted $\RegSeqRatUp$ source $P$ with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item two certified cofinal tail shifts $T_0$ and $T_1$ of the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule carried by $P$;
\item one common $\StreamNameUp$ refinement window $W_\ast$ through which the selected tail windows of $T_0$ and $T_1$ both restrict;
\item the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint rows read on $W_\ast$, together with the displayed radius rows and regularity bounds
$$
2^{-n}+2^{-m}
$$
for the selected unary indices;
\item the $\mathsf{RegSeqRatClassifier}$ rows for the two tail-window reads and for their transported read on $W_\ast$;
\item the $\RealUp$ seal-source rows consumed from the same common finite surface by \autoref{thm:regseqrat-real-seal-handoff};
\item the componentwise $\hsame$ rows, displayed $\Cont$ routes, $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ provenance attached to the StreamName, DyadicRatCore, RegSeqRat, and RealUp-facing rows.
\end{enumerate}
The packet is only a finite common-tail source surface. It stores no quotient stream, host real value, selected limit, metric-completion point, $\RealUp$ completeness datum, or standard-bridge coordinate beyond the displayed rows.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat common-tail window classifier bridge]
\label{thm:regseqrat-common-tail-window-classifier-bridge}
Let $P$ be an accepted $\RegSeqRatUp$ source, and let $T_0$ and $T_1$ be two certified cofinal tail shifts of the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule of $P$. Suppose two tail-window classifier reads land in a common-tail window packet of \autoref{def:regseqrat-common-tail-window-packet} with common refinement $W_\ast$. Then the two reads determine the same $\RealUp$ seal-source surface and the same $\mathsf{RegSeqRatClassifier}$ read after restriction to $W_\ast$.

The bridge conclusion is a statement about the displayed finite packet only. It exports no quotient stream, host real equality, selected limit, metric-completion point, $\RealUp$ completeness datum, or additional seal coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First use \autoref{thm:regseqrat-tail-stable-cover-common-window-lock}. The two seal consumers carried by the cofinal tail shifts factor through one accepted common finite window, and the visible rows of that window are exactly the StreamName schedule rows, DyadicRatCore endpoint and radius ledgers, RegSeqRat classifier and regularity ledgers, and the displayed provenance.

Next read the common-tail packet rows componentwise. The definition lists the cofinal tail shifts, the common $\StreamNameUp$ refinement $W_\ast$, the DyadicRatCore endpoint and radius ledgers, the classifier rows, the $\RealUp$ seal-source rows, and the $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and $\NameCert$ provenance. Thus each tail-window read restricts to the same finite source surface on $W_\ast$.

Apply \autoref{thm:regseqrat-refined-window-gluing-real-seal-factorization}. The refined-window gluing surface factors the RealUp-facing handoff through the displayed finite packet $W_\ast$, so the two tail-window origins cannot supply separate seal-source surfaces once their rows have been restricted to the common refinement.

Use \autoref{thm:regseqrat-streamname-dyadic-window-lock} to lock the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule rows to the same $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint and radius ledgers inside the common window. The classifier reads inspect only those locked rows together with the regularity ledgers and provenance, so their transported RegSeqRat classifier read on $W_\ast$ is the same displayed read.

Finally invoke \autoref{thm:regseqrat-real-seal-handoff}. The $\RealUp$ seal consumes exactly the accepted regular-rational finite source surface already identified, and the carrier has no coordinate for quotient streams, host real equality, selected limits, metric completions, completeness data, or further seal coordinates.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat common-tail four-face cocycle determinacy]

The cocycle surface has no coordinate for quotient streams, host real equality, selected limits, countable choice, ambient completeness, metric-completeness rows, standard-bridge data, or hidden completion carriers.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:regseqrat-common-tail-real-boundary-equivalence} to the three pairs $(T_0,T_1)$, $(T_1,T_2)$, and $(T_0,T_2)$. Each pairwise boundary is thereby identified with the same kind of finite common-tail source surface: StreamName schedule and refinement rows, DyadicRatCore endpoint-radius-distance rows, RegSeqRat classifier and regularity rows, RealUp seal-source rows, and the displayed $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ provenance.

On the triple refinement $W_\ast$, the definition of the common-tail window packet identifies those rows componentwise. The hypotheses give one StreamName refinement, one dyadic ledger, and one Real seal-source row, so the RegSeqRat classifier and regularity rows and the support provenance are the only remaining displayed coordinates to compare.

For the forward direction, a finite four-face cocycle already pastes the three pairwise Real boundaries along the ordered $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ faces. The Real L10 canonical route and exactness results, \autoref{thm:real-l10-four-face-canonical-route} and \autoref{thm:real-l10-four-face-exactness}, force that pasted surface to read through the displayed rows on $W_\ast$, so disagreement on any listed row would give two different finite boundaries for the same face.

For the reverse direction, assume the displayed rows agree on $W_\ast$. Use the Real L10 terminal correspondence and pullback uniqueness results, \autoref{thm:real-l10-terminal-four-face-correspondence} and \autoref{thm:real-l10-terminal-pullback-uniqueness}, to repack the shared rows as one terminal four-face surface. The three pairwise common-tail boundaries are the restrictions of that surface, hence they satisfy the finite cocycle.

No additional boundary coordinate remains to choose. The preceding common-tail Real boundary equivalence and finite-request seal pullback show that quotient streams, host real equality, selected limits, countable choice, ambient completeness, metric-completeness rows, standard bridges, and hidden completion data are not rows of the packet, so they cannot split the cocycle after the displayed surface agrees.
\end{proof}
