\begin{definition}[RegSeqRat tail-budget packet]
\label{def:regseqrat-tail-budget-packet}
The RegSeqRat tail-budget packet is the finite packet displayed to a $\RealUp$ seal consumer of an accepted $\RegSeqRatUp$ carrier. Its rows are the dyadic observation rows from $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, the StreamName window rows naming the finite request, the regularity-budget rows of the accepted $\RegSeqRatUp$ carrier, the tail-selector rows used by the request, the componentwise transport rows, displayed $\Cont$ continuations, $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local $\NameCert_{\RegSeqRatUp}$ naming row.

The packet has no hidden completion carrier. Its visible data are exactly the tail-budget source rows that feed the public $\RealUp$ seal.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat tail-budget non-escape]
\label{thm:regseqrat-tail-budget-nonescape}
A $\RealUp$ seal consumer of a RegSeqRat tail-budget packet can read only the displayed dyadic observation rows, StreamName window rows, regularity-budget rows, tail-selector rows, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows of \autoref{def:regseqrat-tail-budget-packet}. It cannot obtain quotient sequence equality, a selected limit, countable choice, or ambient completeness from that packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with \autoref{thm:regseqrat-tail-budget-terminal-real-seal}. It places every completion-facing tail-budget consumer on the finite route through the StreamName schedule, DyadicRatCore ledger, RegSeqRat classifier and regularity rows, displayed support rows, and the public RealUp seal.

Next apply \autoref{thm:regseqrat-shared-window-selector-determinacy}. The shared-window selector is determined by the displayed finite source rows and by the local transport, continuation, provenance, and naming data; hidden selectors and selected limits do not distinguish a second route.

Use \autoref{thm:regseqrat-real-seal-selector-nonescape} to remove the excluded coordinates at the Real-facing boundary. It prevents quotient streams, selected limits, detached completion rows, and second seal data from being exported by the selector surface.

The rows remaining after these references are precisely the packet rows of \autoref{def:regseqrat-tail-budget-packet}. Since no cited boundary supplies quotient sequence equality, a selected limit, countable choice, or ambient completeness, a RealUp seal consumer cannot obtain them from the tail-budget packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat tail-budget sibling route]
\label{thm:regseqrat-tail-budget-sibling-route}
Every RegSeqRat tail-budget packet used by a $\RealUp$ seal consumer factors through the sibling chapter surfaces \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert} before \autoref{thm:regseqrat-tail-budget-nonescape} applies. The StreamName surface supplies the finite windows, the DyadicRatCore surface supplies the dyadic tolerances, and the RealUp surface supplies the terminal seal reads for the same displayed packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use \autoref{thm:regseqrat-tail-budget-nonescape}. Its route inventory already identifies the dyadic observation rows, StreamName window rows, regularity-budget rows, tail-selector rows, transports, continuations, provenance, and local naming rows that can be read by a RealUp seal consumer.

The three sibling surfaces provide the named external faces of that inventory. The StreamName chapter supplies the finite-window reads, the DyadicRatCore chapter supplies the dyadic tolerance rows, and the Real chapter supplies the terminal RealUp seal reads. Once those faces are displayed, the local non-escape theorem applies to the same tail-budget packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat tail-budget four-face uniqueness]
\label{thm:regseqrat-tail-budget-four-face-uniqueness}
Let two $\RealUp$ seal consumers of RegSeqRat tail-budget packets expose the same four visible faces: the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window rows, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regularity, classifier, and provenance rows, and the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row. Then they determine the same BEDC-visible terminal seal route through the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ rows.

This uniqueness statement uses only the visible tail-budget packet. It does not identify quotient streams, choose a limit, assert host real equality, use countable choice, assume ambient completeness, or add standard bridge rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with \autoref{thm:regseqrat-tail-budget-nonescape}. It fixes the packet inventory available to a $\RealUp$ seal consumer: the finite StreamName rows, dyadic observation rows, regularity-budget rows, selector rows, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming data. The same theorem excludes quotient stream equality, selected limits, countable choice, and ambient completeness from that inventory.

Next use \autoref{thm:regseqrat-tail-budget-sibling-route}. The visible rows are read through the StreamName, DyadicRatCore, and Real sibling surfaces, so the two consumers have the same three external supports for the same tail-budget packet.

Now apply \autoref{thm:regseqrat-four-face-terminal-classifier-completeness}. Agreement on the StreamName finite-window face, DyadicRatCore tolerance face, RegSeqRat regularity/classifier/provenance face, and terminal $\RealUp$ seal face is complete for the accepted terminal classifier surface.

Finally invoke \autoref{thm:regseqrat-four-face-terminal-route-uniqueness}. That theorem identifies the terminal route once the four faces and the displayed support rows agree. The StreamName terminality theorem \autoref{thm:streamname-open-phase-four-face-terminality}, the DyadicRatCore refinement uniqueness theorem \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-common-scale-terminal-refinement-uniqueness}, and the Real L10 terminal correspondence \autoref{thm:real-l10-terminal-four-face-correspondence} name the same external faces of the L10 route. Therefore the two consumers determine one BEDC-visible terminal seal route through the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ rows, with no quotient stream equality, selected limit, host real equality, countable-choice row, ambient-completeness row, or standard bridge row added.
\end{proof}
