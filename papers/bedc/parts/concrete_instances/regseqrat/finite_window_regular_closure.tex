\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat finite-window regular closure]
\label{thm:regseqrat-finite-window-regular-closure}
Let $P$ be an accepted $\RegSeqRatUp$ packet, and let $W'$ be any finite-window restriction or refinement of a displayed window of $P$. If the restriction or refinement is carried by the visible $\StreamNameUp$ schedule rows and componentwise $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ routes, then $W'$ preserves the displayed regularity ledger, dyadic-radius rows, classifier ledger, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ surface of $P$.

The repacked finite window remains a $\RegSeqRatUp$ source surface for later $\RealUp$ and completion consumers. It introduces no quotient stream equality, host real equality, selected limit, ambient completion object, or new Real seal coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:regseqrat-regularity-obligation-surface}. The regularity ledger, dyadic-radius observation rows, and finite-window transport face are already listed as projections of the accepted packet.

Use \autoref{thm:regseqrat-finite-window-transport-closure} for restrictions and refinements. It repacks the same $\StreamNameUp$ schedule rows and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint, radius, distance, and regularity rows inside the displayed carrier and classifier surface.

For common refinements, apply \autoref{thm:regseqrat-refined-window-gluing}. The gluing theorem keeps overlap rows on the same endpoint, radius, regularity, classifier, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ surface. Thus later $\RealUp$ or completion consumers receive the same finite regularity closure, with no coordinate from which quotient streams, host real equality, selected limits, ambient completion objects, or extra seal data could be read.
\end{proof}
