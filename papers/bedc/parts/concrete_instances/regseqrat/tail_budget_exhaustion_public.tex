\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat tail-budget exhaustion]
\label{thm:regseqrat-tail-budget-exhaustion}
Every finite dyadic request admitted by the $\RegSeqRatUp$ tail selector is exhausted by a displayed tail budget and cannot escape the $\RealUp$ seal route. The displayed budget contains the StreamName window row, DyadicRatCore tolerance row, RegSeqRat regularity row, selected tail row, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support rows.

Thus no admitted finite dyadic request can be consumed as a Real-facing request without passing through the same tail-budget packet and the same public seal boundary.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the tail-budget handoff of \autoref{thm:regseqrat-tail-budget-meet-handoff}. It names the finite StreamName window, DyadicRatCore observation rows, RegSeqRat regularity rows, and local support rows that any Real-facing tail-budget request must display.

Apply the tail selector Real-seal exhaustion theorem, \autoref{thm:regseqrat-tail-budget-terminal-real-seal}. The selector cannot export a finite dyadic request to the Real seal unless the displayed budget rows already carry that request.

Now use the non-escape result \autoref{thm:regseqrat-tail-budget-nonescape}. It removes quotient sequence equality, selected limits, countable choice, and ambient completeness from the tail-budget consumer route.

The remaining data are exactly the displayed tail budget and the public RealUp seal route. Therefore every admitted finite dyadic request is exhausted by that budget and cannot escape the seal boundary.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat tail-budget exit status product]
\label{thm:regseqrat-tail-budget-exit-status-product}
Every accepted finite dyadic request admitted by the $\RegSeqRatUp$ tail selector and consumed as a Phase Real and Completion endpoint is audit-readable only as the componentwise status product of four local face records: the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regularity and classifier row, and the $\RealUp$ seal row. Each face contributes its own $\mathsf{closurestatus}$ and $\mathsf{formalstatus}$ record, while the shared transport is only the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support.

The exit read contains no quotient stream, host equality, selected limit, countable-choice principle, ambient completeness datum, metric-completion datum, bridge row, aggregate $\mathsf{formalstatus}$ token, or promotion from one status axis to the other.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with \autoref{thm:regseqrat-tail-budget-exhaustion}. It puts every admitted finite dyadic request on the displayed tail-budget packet and keeps the Real-facing consumer inside the same public $\RealUp$ seal boundary.

Next apply \autoref{thm:regseqrat-four-face-terminal-pullback} and \autoref{thm:regseqrat-dyadic-tail-real-seal-status-exhaustion}. These results identify the terminal read as the four-face ledger consisting of the DyadicRatCore tolerance face, StreamName finite-window face, RegSeqRat regularity and classifier face, and terminal Real seal face, transported only by the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ rows.

Finally read the ledger through the two-axis discipline of \autoref{ch:closure-and-verification-discipline}. A face's closure record and verification record remain local to that face, so their product is only a componentwise audit surface. It cannot add a quotient stream, host equality, selected limit, countable-choice principle, completeness datum, bridge row, aggregate verification token, or implication from one axis to the other.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat four-face exit consumer normal form]
\label{thm:regseqrat-four-face-exit-consumer-normal-form}
Every Phase Real and Completion consumer that reads an accepted finite dyadic request from the $\RegSeqRatUp$ selector factors through exactly four displayed faces: the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regularity and classifier row, and the $\RealUp$ seal row. The transport between these faces is only the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support.

Conversely, any consumer exposing these four faces with the same displayed transport has the same public L10 exit read. The normal form exports no quotient stream, host real equality, selected limit, countable-choice selector, metric-completeness datum, aggregate $\mathsf{formalstatus}$ row, or bridge row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with \autoref{thm:regseqrat-tail-budget-exit-status-product}. It places every accepted finite dyadic request consumed at a Phase Real and Completion endpoint on the four componentwise status faces and records the admissible transport rows.

Next apply \autoref{thm:regseqrat-four-face-terminal-pullback}. The terminal pullback identifies the same four faces as a single finite $\BHist$ surface and shows that the route is carried only by the displayed $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ rows.

Use \autoref{thm:streamname-four-object-exit-exactness} and \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-phase-real-completion-objectwise-exit-equivalence} to align the consumer-side finite-window read with the objectwise DyadicRatCore, StreamName, RegSeqRat, and Real exit records. These identifications make the forward factorization and the reverse same-read criterion the same public L10 read.

Finally apply the two-axis discipline of \autoref{sec:roadmap-closure-and-verification-axes}. It keeps closure rows, verification rows, and bridge rows local to their own faces, so the consumer normal form cannot introduce quotient streams, host real equality, selected limits, countable choice, metric-completeness data, aggregate $\mathsf{formalstatus}$ rows, or bridge rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat tail-budget finite-window refusal localization]
\label{thm:regseqrat-tail-budget-finite-window-refusal-localization}
Let a finite dyadic request be addressed to a $\RegSeqRatUp$ tail-budget route. If the request is not accepted as a Phase Real and Completion four-face exit consumer, then the defect is localized to at least one displayed face row or one displayed support row.

The possible face rows are exactly the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regularity and classifier row, and the $\RealUp$ seal row. The possible support rows are exactly $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$. Quotient stream equality, host real equality, selected limits, countable choice, metric-completeness data, aggregate $\mathsf{formalstatus}$ tokens, aggregate bridge rows, and $\RealUp$ completeness cannot serve as the defect carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:regseqrat-tail-budget-exhaustion}. Any finite dyadic request that reaches the tail selector is exhausted by the displayed tail budget before the public $\RealUp$ seal can read it, so a non-accepted request cannot be explained by an off-budget object.

Next use \autoref{thm:regseqrat-tail-budget-exit-status-product}. The accepted endpoint is a componentwise status product over the four listed faces, transported only by $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$. Hence a missing acceptance witness must remove one of those component rows or one of those transport rows.

Now apply \autoref{thm:regseqrat-four-face-exit-consumer-normal-form}. That normal form is also the reverse public-read criterion: exposing the same four faces with the same displayed transport gives the same L10 exit read. Therefore any request that does not expose such a read has its defect on the named face or support rows.

Finally read the conclusion through the two-axis discipline of \autoref{sec:roadmap-closure-and-verification-axes}. Since closure rows, verification rows, and bridge rows do not promote into one another, no quotient equality, host equality, selected limit, choice principle, completeness datum, aggregate status token, aggregate bridge row, or $\RealUp$ completeness claim can replace the missing displayed row.
\end{proof}
