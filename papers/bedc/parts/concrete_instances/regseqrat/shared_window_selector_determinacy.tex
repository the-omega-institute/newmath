\begin{definition}[RegSeqRat shared-window selector surface]
\label{def:regseqrat-shared-window-selector-surface}
For an accepted $\RegSeqRatUp$ packet $P$, the shared-window selector surface is the finite $\BHist$ surface consisting of the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $\StreamNameUp$ schedule rows selecting the public finite windows read by the request;
\item $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint, radius, and dyadic-distance rows attached to those windows;
\item $\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier, regularity, and tail-budget rows for the same selected windows;
\item componentwise $\hsame$ rows, displayed $\Cont$ replay rows, $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local $\NameCert_{\RegSeqRatUp}$ row;
\item the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row consuming the displayed regular-rational source surface.
\end{enumerate}
The surface contains only the rows visible in the $\StreamNameUp$--$\DyadicRatCoreUp$--$\RegSeqRatUp$--$\RealUp$ handoff. It has no quotient stream equality, host real equality, selected limit, completion point, ambient completeness datum, hidden selector, or second seal coordinate.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat shared-window selector determinacy]
\label{thm:regseqrat-shared-window-selector-determinacy}
Let $P$ be an accepted $\RegSeqRatUp$ packet, and let $S$ and $T$ be accepted finite requests whose displayed selector surfaces are the shared-window surface of \autoref{def:regseqrat-shared-window-selector-surface}. If $S$ and $T$ display the same $\StreamNameUp$ schedule rows, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint, radius, and dyadic-distance rows, $\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier and tail-budget rows, componentwise $\hsame$ rows, displayed $\Cont$ rows, $\Pkg$ provenance, local $\NameCert$ row, and terminal $\RealUp$ seal row, then $S$ and $T$ determine the same RegSeqRat tail-window route up to the displayed $\hsame$ transport and $\Cont$ replay.

The determinacy is internal to the shared-window surface. It does not identify quotient streams, host real values, selected limits, completion points, ambient completeness data, hidden selector choices, or any seal outside the displayed $\RealUp$ row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First use the scope triad of \autoref{thm:regseqrat-streamname-real-scope-triad}. It places every public $\RealUp$ seal read behind the visible StreamName schedule, the dyadic endpoint and radius rows, and the RegSeqRat classifier surface before the seal row is consumed.

Next apply the finite request source section of \autoref{thm:regseqrat-finite-request-source-section}. The two finite requests have the same displayed source rows, so their schedule, dyadic ledger, classifier, regularity, transport, continuation, package, and naming coordinates are one public source section.

Now use the tail-selector RealUp seal exhaustion theorem, \autoref{thm:regseqrat-tail-selector-real-seal-exhaustion}. It prevents either request from using a quotient stream, hidden selector, selected limit, completion point, or second seal to distinguish a separate tail-window route.

Finally cite the StreamName-Dyadic-Real handoff exactness of \autoref{thm:regseqrat-streamname-dyadic-real-handoff-exactness}. Transport through $\hsame$ and replay through $\Cont$ preserve the same handoff rows, so the two accepted requests reach the same RegSeqRat tail-window route and then the same displayed $\RealUp$ seal row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat shared-window terminal pullback equivalence]
\label{thm:regseqrat-shared-window-terminal-pullback-equivalence}
Let $P$ be an accepted $\RegSeqRatUp$ packet, and let $S$ and $T$ be accepted finite requests to $P$ whose displayed selector surfaces are the shared-window surface of \autoref{def:regseqrat-shared-window-selector-surface}. The following data determine one another:
\begin{enumerate}
\item equality of the shared-window selector surface displayed by $S$ and $T$;
\item equality of the terminal $\RealUp$ seal reads obtained from $S$ and $T$ after both requests factor through the ordered four-face packet
$$
\DyadicRatCoreUp \to \StreamNameUp \to \RegSeqRatUp \to \RealUp .
$$
\end{enumerate}
Equivalently, every terminal-budget consumer accepted by $S$ is accepted by $T$ with the same visible dyadic, stream, regular-rational, terminal seal, $\BHist$, componentwise $\hsame$, displayed $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ rows, and conversely. The correspondence exposes no quotient stream equality, host real equality, selected limit, hidden selector, completion carrier, ambient completeness datum, or second terminal seal.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the forward direction, apply \autoref{thm:regseqrat-shared-window-selector-determinacy}. Agreement on the shared-window selector surface identifies the finite StreamName schedule rows, DyadicRatCore endpoint, radius, and distance rows, RegSeqRat classifier and tail-budget rows, componentwise transports, continuations, package provenance, local naming row, and displayed terminal seal coordinate.

Next use \autoref{thm:regseqrat-finite-request-terminal-budget-factorization} and \autoref{thm:real-regseqrat-terminal-budget-exhaustion}. The two accepted finite requests therefore factor through the same ordered four-face packet from $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ to $\RealUp$, with the same support rows. The seal uniqueness theorem, \autoref{thm:real-regseqrat-terminal-budget-seal-uniqueness}, then identifies their visible terminal $\RealUp$ seal reads.

For the reverse direction, suppose the terminal $\RealUp$ seal reads agree after both requests have been read through the four-face packet. The exhaustion theorem places every visible terminal-budget row of each request on that packet, and seal uniqueness prevents a second terminal read from separating requests with the same displayed faces and support rows.

It remains to recover the selector surface. The finite-request factorization returns exactly the StreamName, DyadicRatCore, RegSeqRat, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ rows listed in \autoref{def:regseqrat-shared-window-selector-surface}. Hence equality of the terminal seal reads on the factored packet is equality of the shared-window selector surface.

For the consumer form, take any terminal-budget consumer accepted by $S$. By terminal-budget exhaustion it reads only the four displayed faces and support rows, so the already established surface equality repacks the same visible rows as an accepted consumer for $T$; the converse is symmetric. Finally, \autoref{thm:regseqrat-real-seal-selector-nonescape} excludes quotient streams, host real equality, selected limits, hidden selectors, completion carriers, ambient completeness data, and second seals from both sides of the correspondence.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat tail-selector section pullback]
\label{thm:regseqrat-tail-selector-section-pullback}
Let $P$ be an accepted $\RegSeqRatUp$ packet, and let $S$ and $T$ be accepted finite requests to $P$ whose displayed selector surfaces are the shared-window surface of \autoref{def:regseqrat-shared-window-selector-surface}. Agreement of the shared-window selector surface displayed by $S$ and $T$ is equivalent to agreement of the finite-request source section of \autoref{thm:regseqrat-finite-request-source-section}, restricted exactly to the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule rows, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint, radius, and dyadic-distance rows, $\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier, regularity, and tail-budget rows, the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row, componentwise $\hsame$ rows, displayed $\Cont$ rows, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ rows.

The pullback has no coordinates for quotient stream equality, host real equality, selected limits, completion points, ambient completeness data, choice data, or second seal coordinates. Thus the selector surface and the finite-request source section name the same finite request data over the ordered handoff
$$
\DyadicRatCoreUp \to \StreamNameUp \to \RegSeqRatUp \to \RealUp .
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the forward direction, begin with \autoref{thm:regseqrat-shared-window-selector-determinacy}. Agreement of the shared-window selector surface identifies the visible schedule, dyadic ledger, classifier, regularity, tail-budget, transport, continuation, package, naming, and terminal seal rows.

Apply the finite-request source section theorem, \autoref{thm:regseqrat-finite-request-source-section}. It records exactly those visible rows as the finite source section of each accepted request. The StreamName-Dyadic-Real handoff exactness theorem, \autoref{thm:regseqrat-streamname-dyadic-real-handoff-exactness}, keeps the schedule rows, dyadic endpoint and radius rows, regular-rational classifier rows, and terminal seal row on the same finite handoff, so the two source sections agree on precisely the displayed rows named in the statement.

For the reverse direction, suppose the finite-request source sections agree on those rows. Use \autoref{thm:regseqrat-finite-request-terminal-budget-factorization} to factor both accepted requests through the common terminal-budget section. Then \autoref{thm:real-regseqrat-terminal-budget-exhaustion} places every visible Real-facing consumer on that same four-face packet, and \autoref{thm:real-regseqrat-terminal-budget-seal-uniqueness} identifies the terminal seal row.

It remains only to rule out hidden coordinates. The selector surface definition contains no row for quotient streams, host equality, selected limits, completion points, ambient completeness, choice data, or a second seal coordinate. Applying \autoref{thm:regseqrat-real-seal-selector-nonescape} removes those candidates from the Real-facing boundary, so agreement of the finite-request source section repacks exactly as agreement of the shared-window selector surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat Real seal selector non-escape]
\label{thm:regseqrat-real-seal-selector-nonescape}
A $\RealUp$ seal consumer of the RegSeqRat shared-window selector surface receives only the displayed $\StreamNameUp$ schedule rows, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint, radius, and distance rows, $\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier and tail-budget rows, componentwise $\hsame$ rows, displayed $\Cont$ replay rows, $\Pkg$ provenance, local $\NameCert$ provenance, and the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row. The consumer receives no quotient stream, host real equality, selected limit, hidden selector, detached completeness row, or second $\RealUp$ seal.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with \autoref{thm:regseqrat-tail-selector-real-seal-exhaustion}. It exhausts a selector-facing seal read by the finite StreamName, DyadicRatCore, RegSeqRat, and RealUp rows of the accepted packet.

Apply the finite request Real seal correspondence of \autoref{thm:regseqrat-finite-request-real-seal-correspondence}. A finite request reaches the seal only through its displayed source section, not through an external real equality or selected limit.

Use the terminal tail-budget seal theorem \autoref{thm:regseqrat-tail-budget-terminal-real-seal}. It keeps the tail budget and seal route on the same finite dyadic ledger and removes detached completion and completeness coordinates from the consumer inventory.

The handoff exactness theorem \autoref{thm:regseqrat-streamname-dyadic-real-handoff-exactness} supplies the remaining source-lock: the StreamName schedule and dyadic rows travel together into the Real-facing seal. Since the shared-window selector surface has no row for the excluded objects, the consumer cannot export any of them.
\end{proof}
