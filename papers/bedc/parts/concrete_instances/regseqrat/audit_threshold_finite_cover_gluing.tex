\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat audit-threshold finite-cover gluing]
\label{thm:regseqrat-audit-threshold-finite-cover-gluing}
Let $\{S_i\}_{i \in I}$ be a finite cover of compatible local $\RegSeqRatUp$ terminal sections for a Phase $\RealUp$ and Completion consumer. Assume that every nonempty overlap agrees on the $\StreamNameUp$ finite windows, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint, radius, and distance ledgers, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier and common-tail rows, the local $\RealUp$ seal rows, and the shared support
$$
\BHist,\quad \hsame,\quad \Cont,\quad \Pkg,\quad \NameCert.
$$
Assume also that the overlap status-pullback squares of \autoref{thm:regseqrat-audit-threshold-status-pullback-square} agree under these displayed identifications.

Then the glued terminal section satisfies the Phase $\RealUp$ and Completion audit-threshold read if and only if every local section satisfies the objectwise audit-threshold read and the overlap status-pullback squares agree. The glued read is objectwise over the finite four-face packet
$$
\StreamNameUp,\quad \DyadicRatCoreUp,\quad \RegSeqRatUp,\quad \RealUp
$$
with only the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support.

The gluing theorem exports no quotient stream, selected limit, countable-choice row, metric-completeness datum, aggregate $\formalstatus$ row, aggregate bridge row, standard bridge row, or $\RealUp$ completeness claim.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the forward direction, restrict the glued Phase $\RealUp$ and Completion audit-threshold read to a local section $S_i$. The restriction preserves the displayed StreamName finite window, DyadicRatCore endpoint, radius, and distance ledger, RegSeqRat classifier and common-tail rows, local Real seal rows, and the shared $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support.

Apply \autoref{thm:regseqrat-four-face-exit-threshold-equivalence} on each restricted terminal packet. The local threshold coordinate is therefore the same objectwise four-face read as the RegSeqRat terminal exactness certificate. Then apply \autoref{thm:regseqrat-audit-threshold-status-pullback-square} to read the local closurestatus, formalstatus, leantarget, and bridgestatus rows through the same four faces.

The overlap agreement is inherited from the glued packet. By \autoref{thm:regseqrat-audit-threshold-determinacy} and \autoref{thm:regseqrat-four-face-audit-source-uniqueness}, two local restrictions with the same displayed overlap rows determine one BEDC-visible terminal source and one accepted audit-threshold coordinate. Hence every local section satisfies the objectwise audit-threshold read, and the status-pullback squares agree on overlaps.

For the reverse direction, assume each local section satisfies the objectwise audit-threshold read and the overlap status-pullback squares agree. Apply \autoref{thm:regseqrat-four-face-local-gluing-real-seal-coverage} to the underlying four-face local packets. The StreamName windows, DyadicRatCore ledgers, RegSeqRat classifier and common-tail rows, local Real seal rows, and support rows glue to one accepted $\RealUp$ terminal seal over the finite cover.

Next apply \autoref{thm:real-l10-current-phase-exit-local-threshold}. It reads the glued terminal seal as the objectwise Phase $\RealUp$ and Completion threshold certificate over the four local records. The overlap status-pullback hypothesis supplies the facewise status interface required by \autoref{thm:regseqrat-audit-threshold-status-pullback-square}, so the threshold certificate is read as the Phase $\RealUp$ and Completion audit-threshold read on the glued terminal section.

It remains to identify the glued audit source. Use \autoref{thm:regseqrat-audit-threshold-determinacy} on any two local presentations carried into the glued packet, and then use \autoref{thm:regseqrat-four-face-audit-source-uniqueness} to remove any second audit-threshold coordinate over the same displayed faces. The resulting source is the single finite four-face $\RegSeqRatUp$ terminal section obtained by gluing the cover.

All cited constructions use only the finite rows named in the statement. None introduces quotient streams, selected limits, countable choice, metric-completeness data, aggregate verification status, aggregate bridge data, standard bridge data, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, so the glued audit-threshold read contains none of those excluded coordinates.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat audit-threshold support-removal descent]
\label{thm:regseqrat-audit-threshold-support-removal-descent}
Let $\{S_i\}_{i \in I}$ be a finite cover satisfying the hypotheses of \autoref{thm:regseqrat-audit-threshold-finite-cover-gluing}, and let $G$ be the glued terminal section supplied by that theorem. Delete from one displayed local section or overlap one row among the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window rows, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint, radius, or distance ledger, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier or common-tail row, the local $\RealUp$ seal row, or one of the shared support rows
$$
\BHist,\quad \hsame,\quad \Cont,\quad \Pkg,\quad \NameCert.
$$
Then the resulting cover cannot present the same glued Phase $\RealUp$ and Completion audit-threshold read on $G$.

The obstruction is support-local and descends to the glued threshold. Quotient streams, selected limits, countable choice, metric-completeness data, aggregate $\formalstatus$ rows, aggregate bridge rows, standard bridge rows, and ambient $\RealUp$ completeness are not replacement coordinates for the deleted row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First read the original cover through \autoref{thm:regseqrat-audit-threshold-finite-cover-gluing}. The glued audit-threshold section is determined by the finite four-face packet
$$
\StreamNameUp,\quad \DyadicRatCoreUp,\quad \RegSeqRatUp,\quad \RealUp
$$
and by the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support. Thus every row named in the statement is part of the terminal presentation used by the glued read.

If the deleted row is one of the four visible local faces, apply \autoref{thm:regseqrat-audit-threshold-missing-face-obstruction} to the affected local terminal packet. The RegSeqRat audit-threshold read has no admissible local source after the face is absent, so the finite cover no longer satisfies the local hypotheses required by the gluing theorem.

If the deleted row is one of the shared support rows, apply \autoref{thm:streamname-finite-cover-support-removal-global-obstruction} on the StreamName--DyadicRatCore--RegSeqRat--Real route carried by the same finite cover. The cover-descent terminal support packet then cannot present the same $\RealUp$ seal-visible source. The status-pullback square of \autoref{thm:regseqrat-audit-threshold-status-pullback-square} transports this defect to the RegSeqRat audit-threshold read, because the status rows are read through the same displayed faces and support.

It remains to rule out replacement data. The finite-cover gluing theorem already exports no quotient stream, selected limit, countable-choice row, metric-completeness datum, aggregate $\formalstatus$ row, aggregate bridge row, standard bridge row, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness claim. The missing-face obstruction and the StreamName support-removal obstruction exclude the same objects as substitutes for a deleted local row. Therefore the altered cover cannot present the same glued Phase $\RealUp$ and Completion audit-threshold read.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat zero-source finite-cover gluing]
\label{thm:regseqrat-zero-source-finite-cover-gluing}
Let $\{S_i\}_{i \in I}$ be a finite cover of compatible local $\RegSeqRatUp$ terminal sections satisfying the hypotheses of \autoref{thm:regseqrat-audit-threshold-finite-cover-gluing}. Suppose each local section also displays the same $\RegularCauchyZeroUp$ source boundary through the constant $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window rows, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ zero endpoint and radius ledgers, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ zero readback row, the local $\RealUp$ seal row, and the shared support
$$
\BHist,\quad \hsame,\quad \Cont,\quad \Pkg,\quad \NameCert.
$$
Then the glued terminal section carries the same $\RegularCauchyZeroUp$ zero-source boundary. Its terminal read is the finite four-face packet
$$
\StreamNameUp,\quad \DyadicRatCoreUp,\quad \RegSeqRatUp,\quad \RealUp
$$
specialized to the constant zero source, and it exports no quotient stream, host real equality, selected limit, countable-choice row, metric-completeness datum, aggregate $\formalstatus$ row, bridge row, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply \autoref{thm:regseqrat-audit-threshold-finite-cover-gluing} to the underlying compatible terminal sections. The result is one glued audit-threshold read over the displayed $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ faces with only the listed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support.

Now identify the zero source on the glued packet. The local zero-source hypotheses say that every finite-window face is the constant $\StreamNameUp$ zero row, every dyadic face is the same zero endpoint and radius ledger, every regular face is the same $\RegSeqRatUp$ zero readback row, and every seal face is the local $\RealUp$ seal row of the $\RegularCauchyZeroUp$ packet. Apply \autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-finite-cover-pasted-source-exhaustion} to paste the StreamName and RegSeqRat source rows, and apply \autoref{thm:streamname-regseqrat-finite-cover-pasted-seal-exhaustion} to paste the local seal rows over the same finite cover.

The pasted four-face packet is still the zero packet. Use \autoref{thm:regular-cauchy-zero-streamname-dyadic-sibling-route} to read the constant StreamName and dyadic ledger faces as the sibling route of the accepted $\RegularCauchyZeroUp$ source, and use \autoref{thm:regular-cauchy-zero-terminal-consumer-uniqueness} to identify the glued read with the same terminal zero-source boundary on every downstream consumer.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:regular-cauchy-zero-real-seal-nonescape} and \autoref{thm:regular-cauchy-zero-downstream-handoff}. Their boundary excludes quotient streams, host real equality, selected limits, nonzero witnesses, and ambient completeness from the zero packet. The additional exclusions for countable choice, metric-completeness data, aggregate $\formalstatus$ rows, and bridge rows follow from \autoref{thm:regseqrat-tail-budget-support-minimality} and the audit-threshold gluing theorem, because those objects are not among the displayed four faces or support rows.
\end{proof}
