\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat finite-request classifier alignment]
\label{thm:regseqrat-finite-request-classifier-alignment}
Let $P$ be an accepted $\RegSeqRatUp$ packet, and let $S$ and $T$ be public
finite requests to $P$ consumed by the public $\RealUp$ seal route. The
following data determine one another:
\begin{enumerate}
\item agreement of the displayed $\StreamNameUp$ schedule rows,
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint, radius, and dyadic-distance rows,
$\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier and regularity rows, the terminal $\RealUp$ seal
row, componentwise $\hsame$ rows, displayed $\Cont$ rows, $\Pkg$ provenance,
and local $\NameCert_{\RegSeqRatUp}$ rows of $S$ and $T$;
\item agreement of the classifier-visible finite request section of $S$ and
$T$ inside the source section of
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-finite-request-source-section}.
\end{enumerate}
Thus the rows consumed by the Real seal route are exactly the classifier rows
visible in the finite request section. The alignment has no coordinate for
quotient stream equality, host real equality, selected limits, choice data,
ambient completeness, metric-completeness data, or second seal coordinates.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the forward direction, start with the finite request source section of
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-finite-request-source-section}. Agreement of the
displayed rows in the first item names the same StreamName schedule, dyadic
endpoint, radius, distance, RegSeqRat classifier, regularity, transport,
continuation, package, and local naming rows in that finite $\BHist$ section.

Next apply
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-streamname-dyadic-real-handoff-exactness}. It keeps the
schedule rows, dyadic rows, RegSeqRat classifier surface, and terminal
$\RealUp$ seal row on one ordered handoff rather than allowing a separate
seal-facing source packet. Hence the agreed displayed rows are precisely the
classifier-visible finite request section.

For the reverse direction, suppose the classifier-visible finite request
sections agree. Use
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-tail-selector-section-pullback} to identify that
agreement with agreement of the shared selector surface on the same schedule,
dyadic, classifier, regularity, terminal seal, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and
local $\NameCert$ rows.

Finally apply
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-shared-window-terminal-pullback-equivalence}. The
terminal pullback reads the same displayed selector surface as the same
$\RealUp$ seal route, so the first list of rows agrees component by component.
Since the source section and selector surface contain no rows for quotient
stream equality, host real equality, selected limits, choice data, ambient or
metric completeness, or a second seal coordinate, the equivalence cannot
transport any of those objects.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat finite-request support transport]
\label{thm:regseqrat-finite-request-support-transport}
Let $P$ be an accepted $\RegSeqRatUp$ packet, and let $S$ and $T$ be public
finite requests to $P$ consumed by the public $\RealUp$ seal route. Suppose the
classifier-visible finite request sections of $S$ and $T$ agree. Then the
support rows transported through that section are the same support packet:
the componentwise $\hsame$ rows, displayed $\Cont$ rows, $\Pkg$ provenance, and
local $\NameCert_{\RegSeqRatUp}$ rows of $S$ and $T$ agree component by
component. Conversely, equality of that transported support packet recovers
agreement of the support part of the classifier-visible finite request section.
This support transport has no coordinate for quotient stream equality, host
sequence equality, selected limits, choice data, ambient completeness,
metric-completeness rows, or duplicate $\RealUp$ seal coordinates.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the source section theorem,
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-finite-request-source-section}. It displays the support
part of each finite request as the componentwise $\hsame$ rows, displayed
$\Cont$ rows, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert_{\RegSeqRatUp}$ rows in
one finite $\BHist$ source packet.

For the forward direction, apply the classifier alignment theorem,
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-finite-request-classifier-alignment}. Agreement of the
classifier-visible sections forces agreement on every row of the displayed
source section that the classifier can see. The four support families listed in
the statement are part of that displayed section, so transporting them through
the agreed section yields one support packet.

Next use the StreamName-Dyadic-Real handoff exactness theorem,
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-streamname-dyadic-real-handoff-exactness}. It keeps the
schedule, dyadic, classifier, regularity, and terminal seal rows on the same
finite handoff, so the support packet cannot detach from the section or acquire
a second seal-facing coordinate.

Now apply the Real finite-request fusion theorem,
\autoref{thm:real-regseqrat-finite-request-fusion}. The $\RealUp$ consumer reads
through the same finite $\BHist$ packet, with the same $\hsame$, $\Cont$,
$\Pkg$, and local naming rows. Thus the support packet transported through the
RegSeqRat section is the support packet consumed by the Real seal route.

For the converse, assume the transported support packets are equal. Reading
them back through the source section identifies exactly the support sublist of
the classifier-visible finite request section. The classifier alignment theorem
then recovers agreement of that support part and no larger object, because the
cited source, handoff, and fusion routes contain no rows for quotient stream
equality, host sequence equality, selected limits, choice data, ambient or
metric completeness, or duplicate Real seal coordinates.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat Real seal support face uniqueness]
\label{thm:regseqrat-real-seal-support-face-uniqueness}
Let $P$ be an accepted $\RegSeqRatUp$ packet, and let $S$ and $T$ be public
finite requests to $P$ consumed by the public $\RealUp$ seal route. Suppose the
classifier-visible finite request sections of $S$ and $T$ agree on the support
subpacket. Then the Real-seal-facing support face is unique: the displayed
$\hsame$ rows, displayed $\Cont$ rows, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local
$\NameCert_{\RegSeqRatUp}$ rows form the same support face for $S$ and $T$.
Every accepted $\RealUp$ seal consumer that exposes those rows factors through
that face. Conversely, equality of the Real-seal support face recovers equality
of the support part of the classifier-visible $\RegSeqRatUp$ finite request
section. The uniqueness has no coordinate for quotient stream equality, host
real equality, selected limits, choice data, ambient completeness,
metric-completeness rows, or duplicate $\RealUp$ seal coordinates.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the support transport theorem,
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-finite-request-support-transport}. It transports
agreement of the support subpacket through the componentwise $\hsame$ rows,
displayed $\Cont$ rows, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local
$\NameCert_{\RegSeqRatUp}$ rows, so the two requests present one support packet
on the RegSeqRat side.

Next apply the classifier alignment theorem,
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-finite-request-classifier-alignment}. The
classifier-visible section sees exactly the displayed support rows named above.
Thus equality of the support subpacket is equality of the support part of the
finite request section, not equality of any hidden host or quotient object.

Now use the Real finite-request fusion theorem,
\autoref{thm:real-regseqrat-finite-request-fusion}. The public $\RealUp$
consumer reads the same finite $\BHist$ packet and the same displayed support
rows. Hence any accepted seal consumer exposing $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and
local $\NameCert_{\RegSeqRatUp}$ data must factor through that single
Real-seal-facing support face.

For the converse, assume the Real-seal support faces agree. Reading the agreed
face back through the fusion packet and the classifier-visible section returns
the same support part of the RegSeqRat finite request section. The source,
transport, alignment, and fusion routes expose no rows for quotient stream
equality, host real equality, selected limits, choice data, ambient
completeness, metric-completeness rows, or duplicate Real seal coordinates, so
no external coordinate can distinguish two support faces with the same displayed
rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat finite-request support face composition]
\label{thm:regseqrat-finite-request-support-face-composition}
Let $P$ be an accepted $\RegSeqRatUp$ packet, and let $S_0$, $S_1$, and
$S_2$ be accepted public finite requests to $P$ consumed along the public
$\RealUp$ seal route. Suppose the classifier-visible finite request sections
of $(S_0,S_1)$ and $(S_1,S_2)$ agree on their support subpackets. Suppose
also that the middle support face exposes the same $\StreamNameUp$ schedule
rows, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint, radius, and dyadic-distance ledger,
$\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier and regularity rows, componentwise $\hsame$ rows,
displayed $\Cont$ rows, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local
$\NameCert_{\RegSeqRatUp}$ rows on both legs.

Then the composed route from $S_0$ to $S_2$ has one
Real-seal-facing support face, and that face contains exactly the displayed
$\StreamNameUp$ schedule rows, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint, radius, and
dyadic-distance ledger, $\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier and regularity rows,
componentwise $\hsame$ rows, displayed $\Cont$ rows, $\Pkg$ provenance, and
local $\NameCert_{\RegSeqRatUp}$ rows exposed by the middle face. It exports
no quotient stream equality, host real equality, selected limit,
countable-choice data, metric-completeness rows, duplicate Real seal,
aggregate formalstatus row, or bridge row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First identify the two classifier-visible sections. Apply
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-finite-request-classifier-alignment} to the pair
$(S_0,S_1)$ and again to the pair $(S_1,S_2)$. The hypotheses say that the
two legs display the same support subpacket at $S_1$, so the classifier sees
one finite support surface through the middle request.

Next transport the displayed support rows along each leg. By
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-finite-request-support-transport}, the shared
componentwise $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local
$\NameCert_{\RegSeqRatUp}$ rows determine the same transported support packet
on the RegSeqRat side for $(S_0,S_1)$ and for $(S_1,S_2)$. The listed
$\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, and $\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier rows keep
that support packet attached to the same finite request face.

Now pass to the Real-seal-facing side. Use
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-real-seal-support-face-uniqueness} on the transported
support packet to pin the unique Real-facing support face exposed by each
leg. Since both legs expose the same middle face, uniqueness identifies their
Real-facing support faces as one displayed face.

Finally compare the direct and two-step finite request routes. The section
refinement composition theorem,
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-finite-request-section-refinement-composition}, says
that the one-step route from $S_0$ to $S_2$ and the route through $S_1$ have
the same finite request section and local Real seal on the displayed rows.
Therefore the composed route has exactly the support face already pinned
above.

All cited routes are finite classifier, support-transport, uniqueness, and
section-refinement routes over the displayed BEDC rows. None carries quotient
stream equality, host real equality, selected-limit data, countable-choice
data, metric-completeness rows, duplicate Real seal data, aggregate
formalstatus rows, or bridge rows, so the composed support face exports none
of those objects.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat finite-request support face chain induction]
\label{thm:regseqrat-finite-request-support-face-chain-induction}
Let $P$ be an accepted $\RegSeqRatUp$ packet, and let
$S_0,\ldots,S_n$ be a finite nonempty chain of accepted public finite requests
to $P$ consumed along the public $\RealUp$ seal route. Suppose each adjacent
pair $(S_i,S_{i+1})$ has agreeing classifier-visible finite request sections
on the displayed $\StreamNameUp$ schedule rows, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint,
radius, and dyadic-distance rows, $\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier and regularity
rows, componentwise $\hsame$ rows, displayed $\Cont$ rows, $\Pkg$ provenance,
and local $\NameCert_{\RegSeqRatUp}$ rows. Suppose also that every adjacent
pair agrees on its support subpacket.

Then every parenthesized adjacent support-face composite along the chain and
the direct comparison from $S_0$ to $S_n$ determine the same terminal
$\RealUp$ seal-facing support face. That face contains exactly the displayed
$\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$,
$\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert_{\RegSeqRatUp}$ rows carried by the adjacent
finite request sections, and it exports no quotient stream equality, host real
equality, selected limit, choice data, ambient completeness,
metric-completeness rows, duplicate $\RealUp$ seal, aggregate formalstatus
row, or bridge row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The base case is the one-object chain. The direct comparison from $S_0$ to
$S_0$ is reflexive on the displayed finite request section, so the terminal
support face is the same Real-seal-facing face read from that section. No
composition data are introduced.

For a one-step chain, apply
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-finite-request-support-transport} to the adjacent
pair and then apply
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-real-seal-support-face-uniqueness}. The agreed
classifier-visible section and the agreed support subpacket pin one
Real-facing support face for the two requests.

For the induction step, assume the prefix from $S_0$ to $S_k$ has a unique
terminal support face independent of parenthesization. Compose that prefix
face with the last adjacent face from $S_k$ to $S_{k+1}$ using
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-finite-request-support-face-composition}. The theorem
keeps the $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\hsame$,
$\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert_{\RegSeqRatUp}$ rows on the same
Real-seal-facing support face, so the result is independent of whether the
last leg is attached before or after the prefix composite.

It remains to compare the iterated composite with the direct first-to-last
section. The section-refinement composition route used in
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-finite-request-support-face-composition} identifies
the direct $S_0$ to $S_{k+1}$ classifier-visible section with the composed
finite section on the displayed rows. Applying support-face uniqueness once
more gives the same terminal $\RealUp$ seal-facing support face.

Every step uses only classifier alignment, support transport, support-face
uniqueness, and finite section composition over the listed BEDC rows. These
routes contain no rows for quotient streams, host real equality, selected
limits, choice data, ambient completeness, metric-completeness rows,
duplicate Real seals, aggregate formalstatus rows, or bridge rows, so the
chain induction cannot export any of those objects.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat Real seal support face classifier readback]
\label{thm:regseqrat-real-seal-support-face-classifier-readback}
Let $P$ be an accepted $\RegSeqRatUp$ packet, and let $S$ and $T$ be public
finite requests to $P$ consumed by the public $\RealUp$ seal route. Suppose
the terminal Real-seal-facing support faces of $S$ and $T$ are equal, and
suppose the displayed $\StreamNameUp$ schedule rows, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$
endpoint, radius, and dyadic-distance rows, $\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier and
regularity rows, componentwise $\hsame$ rows, displayed $\Cont$ rows, $\Pkg$
provenance, and local $\NameCert_{\RegSeqRatUp}$ rows agree component by
component. Then the classifier-visible finite request sections of $S$ and
$T$ are equal.

The readback has no coordinate for quotient stream equality, host real
equality, selected limits, choice data, ambient completeness,
metric-completeness rows, duplicate $\RealUp$ seal coordinates, aggregate
formalstatus rows, or bridge rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First read the terminal support face back through
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-real-seal-support-face-uniqueness}. Equality of the
Real-seal-facing support faces recovers equality of the support part of the
classifier-visible $\RegSeqRatUp$ finite request section.

Next identify that recovered support part with the finite request support
rows. Apply
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-finite-request-support-transport}: the componentwise
$\hsame$, displayed $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local
$\NameCert_{\RegSeqRatUp}$ rows are exactly the transported support packet
seen by the classifier-visible section.

Now combine the recovered support rows with the displayed non-support rows.
The hypotheses already identify the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule, the
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint, radius, and dyadic-distance rows, and the
$\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier and regularity rows. Hence the four visible faces
of the finite request section agree.

Finally apply
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-finite-request-classifier-alignment}. Agreement of
the displayed four-face data recovers equality of the full classifier-visible
finite request sections. The nonescape argument is the same one used in
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-finite-request-support-face-chain-induction}: the
classifier, support-transport, and Real-seal uniqueness routes contain only
the displayed BEDC rows. They contain no rows for quotient stream equality,
host real equality, selected limits, choice data, ambient completeness,
metric-completeness rows, duplicate Real seal coordinates, aggregate
formalstatus rows, or bridge rows, so the readback cannot export any of those
objects.
\end{proof}
