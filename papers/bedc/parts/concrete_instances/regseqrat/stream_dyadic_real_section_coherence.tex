\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat stream-dyadic real section coherence]
\label{thm:regseqrat-stream-dyadic-real-section-coherence}
Let $R$ be an accepted $\RegSeqRatUp$ packet, and let $S$ be an accepted finite
request section whose displayed rows include the $\StreamNameUp$ pointwise
window, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint, radius, and finite-distance ledger,
the $\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier and regularity rows, and the $\RealUp$
finite-section readback. If those rows are consumed by a Phase Real and
Completion reader, then the StreamName window, dyadic ledger, regular-rational
readback, and Real finite-section readback factor through one common source
section carried by the same finite $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and
$\NameCert_{\RegSeqRatUp}$ support rows.

The coherent section is only the finite L10 route
$$
\StreamNameUp,\quad \DyadicRatCoreUp,\quad \RegSeqRatUp,\quad \RealUp.
$$
It exports no quotient stream equality, selected limit, host real equality,
ambient completeness row, metric-completion point, countable-choice selector,
or second $\RealUp$ seal coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the finite request source section of
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-finite-request-source-section}. It places the accepted
request on one displayed $\BHist$ packet with the StreamName schedule rows,
DyadicRatCore endpoint, radius, and finite-distance rows, RegSeqRat classifier
and regularity rows, componentwise $\hsame$, displayed $\Cont$ routes,
$\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance, and local naming.

Next attach the dyadic and Real faces. The exact handoff of
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-streamname-dyadic-real-handoff-exactness} keeps the
StreamName window, DyadicRatCore ledger, and RegSeqRat classifier on the same
finite route, while \autoref{thm:regseqrat-constant-real-seal-handoff} records
the constant dyadic case as a special Real-facing handoff through the same four
visible faces.

Now use the common-tail bridge. The classifier bridge of
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-common-tail-window-classifier-bridge} and the
finite-request pullback of
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-common-tail-finite-request-real-seal-pullback} identify
common-tail and finite-request presentations with the same StreamName,
DyadicRatCore, RegSeqRat, RealUp, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and
$\NameCert$ rows.

Finally compare the sibling finite-section surfaces. The StreamName
finite-section pullback and lock,
\autoref{thm:streamname-finite-section-pullback-exactness} and
\autoref{thm:streamname-finite-section-real-regseq-dyadic-lock}, identify the
pointwise window as a finite section over the same dyadic and RegSeqRat rows.
The Real terminal finite-section pullback and four-face exactness,
\autoref{thm:real-terminal-finite-section-pullback-exactness} and
\autoref{thm:real-l10-four-face-exactness}, read the terminal Real section
from that same ordered packet.

Thus the StreamName pointwise window, DyadicRatCore ledger, RegSeqRat
readback, and Real finite-section readback are four projections of one common
finite source section. The sibling chapters
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert},
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert} supply only those displayed
finite rows, so the excluded quotient, limit, host-equality, completeness,
choice, and second-seal coordinates have no row from which to be read.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat terminal four-face source uniqueness]
\label{thm:regseqrat-terminal-four-face-source-uniqueness}
Let $R_0$ and $R_1$ be accepted Phase Real and Completion terminal consumers
of finite $\RegSeqRatUp$ sections. Suppose that they display the same
$\StreamNameUp$ finite-section window, the same $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint,
radius, and finite-distance ledger, the same $\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier and
regularity rows, the same local $\RealUp$ finite-section readback, and the
same $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local
$\NameCert_{\RegSeqRatUp}$ support rows. Then $R_0$ and $R_1$ determine the
same common finite $\RegSeqRatUp$ source section.

The unique source is the four-face packet over
$$
\StreamNameUp,\quad \DyadicRatCoreUp,\quad \RegSeqRatUp,\quad \RealUp.
$$
There is no independent $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback branch, second $\RealUp$ seal,
quotient stream, selected limit, countable-choice row, host equality, ambient
completeness row, or aggregate bridge or status row available to distinguish
the two terminal consumers.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:regseqrat-stream-dyadic-real-section-coherence} to both
terminal consumers. Each read is thereby placed on the same four visible
projections: the StreamName finite window, the DyadicRatCore ledger, the
RegSeqRat classifier and regularity surface, and the local Real
finite-section readback.

Next identify the StreamName and dyadic coordinates. The StreamName
finite-section pullback
\autoref{thm:streamname-finite-section-pullback-exactness} and the real,
RegSeqRat, and dyadic lock
\autoref{thm:streamname-finite-section-real-regseq-dyadic-lock} force the two
window coordinates to be the same finite section. The dyadic source
uniqueness theorem
\autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-scale-window-real-exit-source-uniqueness} then
identifies the endpoint, radius, and distance rows as one dyadic source over
that shared window.

Now keep the Real readback terminal. By
\autoref{thm:real-terminal-finite-section-pullback-exactness}, each accepted
terminal consumer reads its local Real section from the displayed finite
packet. The four-face exactness theorem
\autoref{thm:real-l10-four-face-exactness} keeps that packet on the ordered
$\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ faces with
only the listed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:real-l10-terminal-pullback-uniqueness}. A second
RegSeqRat source over the same displayed support rows would give a second
four-face terminal packet for the same Phase Real and Completion boundary.
Terminal pullback uniqueness identifies those packets, so the common finite
$\RegSeqRatUp$ source section is determined uniquely. Since the cited
four-face packet has no coordinate for quotient streams, selected limits,
choice rows, host equality, ambient completeness, a second Real seal, or an
aggregate bridge or status surface, none of those rows can separate the two
consumers.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat real terminal four-face exactness]
\label{thm:regseqrat-real-terminal-four-face-exactness}
Let $S$ be a finite $\RegSeqRatUp$ terminal section presented to the Phase Real
and Completion exit. Then $S$ is accepted by the $\RealUp$ terminal seal if and
only if its finite ledger contains one compatible four-face packet consisting
of the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-request section, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ common
scale-window witness, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regularity witness, and the $\RealUp$
terminal seal row, all carried by the same $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$,
and local $\NameCert_{\RegSeqRatUp}$ support rows.

The exact packet is the concrete terminal surface
$$
\StreamNameUp,\quad \DyadicRatCoreUp,\quad \RegSeqRatUp,\quad \RealUp.
$$
It asserts no quotient stream equality, no selected limit existence, and no
completeness property of $\RealUp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the forward direction, start with the accepted $\RealUp$ terminal seal. By
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-stream-dyadic-real-section-coherence}, the terminal read
factors through a single finite source section whose visible coordinates are
the StreamName finite-request window, the DyadicRatCore endpoint, radius, and
finite-distance ledger, the RegSeqRat classifier and regularity rows, and the
Real finite-section readback.

The compatibility of those faces is already part of the same finite ledger:
the StreamName rows are tied to the displayed dyadic rows by
\autoref{thm:streamname-finite-section-real-regseq-dyadic-lock}, the dyadic
common scale-window rows are locked by
\autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-scale-window-real-exit-source-uniqueness}, and the
terminal Real readback is pulled from the displayed packet by
\autoref{thm:real-terminal-finite-section-pullback-exactness}. Thus the
accepted seal supplies exactly the four required finite faces over one
$\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support.

For the reverse direction, suppose that those four compatible faces are
present in the finite ledger. The StreamName face gives the finite-request
section, the DyadicRatCore face gives the common scale-window witness, the
RegSeqRat face gives the regularity witness, and the Real face gives the
terminal seal row. Applying
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-stream-dyadic-real-section-coherence} packages these
rows as one common terminal section.

It remains only to identify the terminal consumer. The source uniqueness
result \autoref{thm:regseqrat-terminal-four-face-source-uniqueness} shows that
any accepted Phase Real and Completion consumer with these four faces must
come from this same finite $\RegSeqRatUp$ source. The Real terminal pullback
uniqueness theorem \autoref{thm:real-l10-terminal-pullback-uniqueness} then
identifies the displayed Real seal row as the terminal acceptance surface for
$S$.

Every step used only rows visible in the finite $\BHist$, $\Cont$, $\hsame$,
$\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ ledger. There is no coordinate for quotient stream
equality, no selector for an external limit, and no ambient completeness field
for $\RealUp$, so none of those stronger claims is produced by the exactness
criterion.
\end{proof}
