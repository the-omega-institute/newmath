\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat stream-dyadic real section coherence]
\label{thm:regseqrat-stream-dyadic-real-section-coherence}

$\NameCert_{\RegSeqRatUp}$ support rows.

The coherent section is only the finite L10 route
$$
\StreamNameUp,\quad \DyadicRatCoreUp,\quad \RegSeqRatUp,\quad \RealUp.
$$
It exports no quotient stream equality, selected limit, host real equality,
ambient completeness row, metric-completion point, countable-choice selector,
or second $\RealUp$ seal coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the finite request source section of
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-finite-request-source-section}. It places the accepted
request on one displayed $\BHist$ packet with the StreamName schedule rows,
DyadicRatCore endpoint, radius, and finite-distance rows, RegSeqRat classifier
and regularity rows, componentwise $\hsame$, displayed $\Cont$ routes,
$\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance, and local naming.

Next attach the dyadic and Real faces. The exact handoff of
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-streamname-dyadic-real-handoff-exactness} keeps the
StreamName window, DyadicRatCore ledger, and RegSeqRat classifier on the same
finite route, while \autoref{thm:regseqrat-constant-real-seal-handoff} records
the constant dyadic case as a special Real-facing handoff through the same four
visible faces.

Now use the common-tail bridge. The classifier bridge of
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-common-tail-window-classifier-bridge} and the
finite-request pullback of
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-common-tail-finite-request-real-seal-pullback} identify
common-tail and finite-request presentations with the same StreamName,
DyadicRatCore, RegSeqRat, RealUp, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and

Finally compare the sibling finite-section surfaces. The StreamName

\autoref{thm:streamname-finite-section-pullback-exactness} and
\autoref{thm:streamname-finite-section-real-regseq-dyadic-lock}, identify the

\autoref{thm:real-terminal-finite-section-pullback-exactness} and
\autoref{thm:real-l10-four-face-exactness}, read the terminal Real section
from that same ordered packet.

Thus the StreamName pointwise window, DyadicRatCore ledger, RegSeqRat
readback, and Real finite-section readback are four projections of one common
finite source section. The sibling chapters
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert},
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert} supply only those displayed
finite rows, so the excluded quotient, limit, host-equality, completeness,
choice, and second-seal coordinates have no row from which to be read.
\end{proof}

\autoref{thm:streamname-finite-section-restriction-exactness} to identify

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat four-face finite-cover induction]

\StreamNameUp,\quad \DyadicRatCoreUp,\quad \RegSeqRatUp,\quad \RealUp.

the local $\RealUp$ seal row, and the shared $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$,

Then the whole finite cover $\mathcal{S}$ determines exactly one accepted
$\RealUp$ terminal seal over the same four visible faces. Moreover, every
accepted $\RealUp$ terminal seal whose visible support is the cover
$\mathcal{S}$ is obtained by this finite-cover induction from the local
empty, singleton, and successor clauses.

The claim is only the finite descent principle for the displayed
$\RegSeqRatUp$ section cover. It does not identify quotient streams, select
limits, compare host real equality, use countable choice, assume ambient or
metric completeness, aggregate formalstatus rows, or introduce standard
bridge rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Put the finite cover into a section-by-section descent order. Apply
\autoref{thm:streamname-finite-section-cover-descent-normal-form} to replace
the cover by its finite request normal form. The empty and singleton entries
are then exactly the local four-face packets supplied by
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-four-face-local-gluing-real-seal-coverage}.

For the successor step, suppose a subcover has already produced an accepted
terminal $\RealUp$ seal. Glue this seal to the next section along the stated
overlap rows. The preservation hypotheses give the same $\BHist$, $\hsame$,
$\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert_{\RegSeqRatUp}$ rows on the common
support, so
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-four-face-finite-section-restriction-functoriality}
reads both sides on the same restricted four-face packet.

The overlap condition is coherent because the pairwise restrictions satisfy
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-finite-request-section-cocycle-real-seal}. Hence the
new section and the previously accepted subcover have a common terminal
overlap packet. Applying
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-four-face-gluing-uniqueness} gives one accepted
$\RealUp$ terminal seal for the enlarged subcover, and uniqueness prevents
any second seal from being carried by the same visible rows.

Iterating this finite successor construction reaches the full cover
$\mathcal{S}$. Refining the descent order does not change the resulting
seal: adjacent regroupings are identified by
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-finite-request-section-refinement-composition}, while
all restrictions back to smaller subcovers are controlled by
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-four-face-finite-section-restriction-functoriality}.
Thus the terminal seal is independent of the chosen finite normal form.

Finally let $T$ be any accepted $\RealUp$ terminal seal over the same cover.
Restrict $T$ to every empty, singleton, and successor subcover in the normal
form. The local gluing theorem supplies the base packets, the restriction
theorem identifies the visible rows, the cocycle theorem supplies the
overlap compatibility, and gluing uniqueness identifies each successor seal

induction.

Every step uses only finite request normal forms, local four-face gluing,
restriction functoriality, cocycle compatibility, uniqueness, and certified
refinement composition. None of these cited inputs contains a coordinate for
quotient stream equality, selected limits, host real equality, countable

\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat four-face finite-cover normal form]
