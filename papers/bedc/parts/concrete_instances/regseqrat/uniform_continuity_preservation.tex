\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat finite-window map preservation]
\label{thm:regseqrat-uniform-continuity-preservation}
Let $S$ be an accepted $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row carried by a finite
$\BHist$ packet with its displayed $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window rows, its
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint, radius, and finite-distance ledger, its
$\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier and regularity rows, and its $\hsame$, $\Cont$,
$\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert_{\RegSeqRatUp}$ support. Let $M$ be a uniformly
controlled finite-window map row on the same support. Assume $M$ sends every
displayed StreamName window to a displayed StreamName window, transports the
DyadicRatCore tolerance ledger by the stated finite modulus row, and preserves
the RegSeqRat classifier and regularity rows on each finite observation
window.

Then the readback row obtained by applying $M$ to $S$ is again an accepted
$\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row over a finite four-face packet
$$
\StreamNameUp,\quad \DyadicRatCoreUp,\quad \RegSeqRatUp,\quad \RealUp
$$
with the transported $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and
$\NameCert_{\RegSeqRatUp}$ support. The construction is finite-window
inductive and introduces no quotient stream equality, selected limit,
countable-choice row, host function equality, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness,
or second $\RealUp$ seal coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the finite request source section of
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-finite-request-source-section}. It places the source
readback on one finite packet carrying the StreamName windows, DyadicRatCore
tolerance ledger, RegSeqRat classifier and regularity rows, and the displayed
support.

Apply the map row on the StreamName and dyadic faces. The StreamName pullback
and lock results,
\autoref{thm:streamname-finite-section-pullback-exactness} and
\autoref{thm:streamname-finite-section-real-regseq-dyadic-lock}, keep the
transported windows as finite observed rows. The dyadic source uniqueness
theorem \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-scale-window-real-exit-source-uniqueness}
keeps the transported endpoint, radius, and distance ledger on the same finite
tolerance surface.

Now perform induction over the finite observation window family. The base
window is the transported singleton read supplied by the map row. In the
induction step, the preservation hypotheses identify the new overlap rows
with the already transported StreamName and DyadicRatCore rows, while the
RegSeqRat classifier and regularity rows are preserved by assumption.

The induction is glued by
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-four-face-local-gluing-real-seal-coverage} and
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-audit-threshold-finite-cover-gluing}. Those theorems
turn compatible local four-face packets into one accepted terminal readback
without adding a quotient stream, selected limit, choice row, host equality,
ambient completeness datum, or second Real seal.

Finally use
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-stream-dyadic-real-section-coherence} and
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-four-face-refinement-functoriality}. They identify the
transported readback as a single RegSeqRat source section over the same
StreamName, DyadicRatCore, RegSeqRat, and Real faces, so the output of $M$ is
accepted as a $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat uniformly controlled Real seal handoff]
\label{thm:regseqrat-uniform-continuity-real-seal-handoff}
Let $S$ be an accepted $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row whose terminal consumer is a
Phase $\RealUp$ and Completion seal, and let $M$ be a uniformly controlled
finite-window map row satisfying the hypotheses of
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-uniform-continuity-preservation}. Then the
$\RealUp$-facing seal read of the transported row factors through exactly the
four finite faces
$$
\StreamNameUp,\quad \DyadicRatCoreUp,\quad \RegSeqRatUp,\quad \RealUp
$$
together with the map-control row $M$ and the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$,
$\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert_{\RegSeqRatUp}$ support.

This handoff is the RegSeqRat-side input needed by the
$\CauchyContinuousExtensionUp$ route: it reads the mapped regular Cauchy data
through StreamName windows, DyadicRatCore tolerances, the RegSeqRat accepted
readback, and the terminal Real seal. It exports no quotient stream, host
function equality, selected limit, countable-choice row, ambient $\RealUp$
completeness, or independent Real completion coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-uniform-continuity-preservation} to the source
readback and the map-control row. The result is an accepted transported
$\RegSeqRatUp$ readback over the same finite support and the same four visible
faces.

Read the transported row at the Real boundary. The terminal exactness theorem
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-real-terminal-four-face-exactness} identifies its
accepted Real seal precisely with the displayed StreamName, DyadicRatCore,
RegSeqRat, and Real rows. The source uniqueness theorem
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-terminal-four-face-source-uniqueness} rules out a
second RegSeqRat source over those rows.

Next compare this handoff with the local cover surface. The finite-cover
gluing theorem
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-audit-threshold-finite-cover-gluing} and the local
four-face coverage theorem
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-four-face-local-gluing-real-seal-coverage} keep the
seal read invariant under finite cover refinement of the transported windows.
Thus every Real-facing read of the mapped row is recovered from the same
finite four-face packet and the same map-control row.

Finally point the packet toward the completion-extension consumer. The
$\CauchyContinuousExtensionUp$ regular source handoff of
\autoref{thm:cauchy-continuous-extension-regular-source-handoff} in
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-continuous-extension-namecert} consumes
regular source data only through StreamName finite windows, a DyadicRatCore
tolerance ledger, a Cauchy-continuous map row, and the resulting completion
surface. The handoff above supplies exactly the RegSeqRat source side of that
route before any completed extension is read.

Since each cited step is a finite BEDC row calculation, no quotient stream,
host function equality, selected limit, countable-choice row, ambient
$\RealUp$ completeness, or independent completion coordinate is available to
enter the seal read.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat uniform-continuity window modulus composition]
\label{thm:regseqrat-uniform-continuity-window-modulus-composition}
Let $S$ be an accepted $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row with its displayed
$\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$,
$\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert_{\RegSeqRatUp}$ rows. Let $M_0$ and
$M_1$ be uniformly controlled finite-window map rows, each satisfying the
hypotheses of
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-uniform-continuity-preservation}. Assume that the output
four-face packet of $M_0$ is the input four-face packet of $M_1$, with the same
StreamName, DyadicRatCore, RegSeqRat, Real, history, continuity, package, and
local NameCert rows exposed on the shared boundary.

Then the composed finite-window row $M_1 \circ M_0$ preserves the accepted
$\RegSeqRatUp$ readback of $S$ and exports a single Phase $\RealUp$ and
Completion handoff through exactly the same four faces
$$
\StreamNameUp,\quad \DyadicRatCoreUp,\quad \RegSeqRatUp,\quad \RealUp
$$
together with the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and
$\NameCert_{\RegSeqRatUp}$ rows. The composed route introduces no quotient
stream, selected limit, countable-choice row, host function equality, ambient
$\RealUp$ completeness, or second Real seal.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:regseqrat-uniform-continuity-preservation} to $S$ and
$M_0$. This gives an accepted intermediate RegSeqRat readback over the output
packet of $M_0$, with the transported StreamName windows, DyadicRatCore
tolerance ledger, RegSeqRat classifier, Real face, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$,
$\Pkg$, and local NameCert rows still displayed as finite BEDC data.

Use the boundary hypothesis to read that intermediate output packet as the
input packet of $M_1$. A second application of
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-uniform-continuity-preservation} therefore produces the
accepted readback for $M_1 \circ M_0$. The four-face refinement functoriality
theorem \autoref{thm:regseqrat-four-face-refinement-functoriality} identifies
the two finite transports with one composed route rather than two unrelated
readback choices.

The cover bookkeeping is finite. Apply
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-audit-threshold-finite-cover-gluing} to glue the
modulus windows used by $M_0$ and $M_1$ into the single observed finite cover
seen by the composed row. This keeps the overlap checks inside the displayed
StreamName, DyadicRatCore, RegSeqRat, and Real faces and does not add a new
ambient equality principle.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:regseqrat-uniform-continuity-real-seal-handoff} to
the composed accepted readback. The handoff theorem reads the terminal seal
only through the displayed four-face packet and its $\BHist$, $\hsame$,
$\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and NameCert support. Since both preservation steps and the
finite-cover gluing are internal row calculations, there is no quotient stream,
selected limit, countable-choice row, host function equality, ambient
$\RealUp$ completeness, or second Real seal available for the composed route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat uniform-continuity modulus fusion]
\label{thm:regseqrat-uniform-continuity-modulus-fusion}
In the setting of
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-uniform-continuity-window-modulus-composition}, the
dyadic modulus schedules of the two uniformly controlled finite-window rows fuse
into one displayed finite support row consumed by the terminal $\RealUp$ seal.
The fused row is read over the shared
$$
\StreamNameUp,\quad \DyadicRatCoreUp,\quad \RegSeqRatUp,\quad \RealUp
$$
boundary together with the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and
$\NameCert_{\RegSeqRatUp}$ rows. It exports no selected limit, quotient stream,
host function equality, ambient completeness coordinate, or second Real seal.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-uniform-continuity-window-modulus-composition}. It
identifies the composite route $M_1 \circ M_0$ as one accepted finite-window
transport over the shared StreamName, DyadicRatCore, RegSeqRat, and Real faces.

The modulus bookkeeping is the finite cover bookkeeping. Apply
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-audit-threshold-finite-cover-gluing} to the two observed
modulus-window families. The gluing row records the common finite support on
which the output windows of $M_0$ are the input windows of $M_1$, so the two
dyadic schedules are consumed as one displayed finite support row.

Finally use \autoref{thm:regseqrat-uniform-continuity-real-seal-handoff}. The
terminal Real seal reads the composed accepted row only through the four visible
faces and the structural support rows. Since the fused support row is already
the support used by that seal, there is no source for a selected limit, quotient
stream, host function equality, ambient completeness coordinate, or second Real
seal.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat uniform-continuity composition terminal seal]
\label{thm:regseqrat-uniform-continuity-composition-terminal-seal}
Two displayed uniform-continuity packets over $\RegSeqRatUp$ compose to a
single terminal $\RealUp$-seal handoff exactly through the shared
$\StreamNameUp$ finite windows, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance budgets,
$\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier and readback rows, and the terminal $\RealUp$ seal.
The composed consumer receives one finite route, not two independently selected
limit reads.

The terminal seal contains no selected limit, quotient stream equality,
countable choice, ambient Real completeness, or standard bridge coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with preservation,
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-uniform-continuity-preservation}, for the two displayed
uniform-continuity packets. The first packet produces the finite output windows
that the second packet consumes, all inside the visible RegSeqRat readback
surface.

Apply the Real-seal handoff theorem
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-uniform-continuity-real-seal-handoff}. It forces every
terminal consumer to pass through the displayed StreamName, DyadicRatCore,
RegSeqRat, and Real faces together with their support rows.

The window-modulus composition theorem
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-uniform-continuity-window-modulus-composition} identifies
the two packet routes as one composed finite-window route. Then
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-uniform-continuity-modulus-fusion} fuses the two dyadic
modulus schedules into the single support row consumed by the terminal seal.

Therefore the composed Real-seal handoff is terminal and finite. Since the
route has already exhausted its visible windows, tolerance budgets, classifier
and readback rows, and Real seal, none of the excluded quotient, choice,
completeness, selected-limit, or bridge coordinates can supply another
composition read.
\end{proof}
