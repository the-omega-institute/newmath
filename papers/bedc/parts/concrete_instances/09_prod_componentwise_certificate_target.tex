\begin{theorem}[Product componentwise history classifier certificate target]
\label{thm:prod-component-history-classifier-certificate-target}
Let $L,R:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be source-history carriers, let
$\sim_L,\sim_R:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be their source
classifiers, and assume $\mathsf{cert}_L:\NameCert(L,\sim_L)$,
$\mathsf{cert}_R:\NameCert(R,\sim_R)$, and
$\mathsf{ProdPairRepCoherent}_{L,R,\sim_L,\sim_R}$. Write
$C(h,k)$ for
$\mathsf{ProdComponentHistoryClassifier}_{L,R,\sim_L,\sim_R}(h,k)$. Then the
five core $\NameCert$ fields hold for
$\mathsf{ProdHistoryCarrier}(L,R)$:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item carrier inhabitation:
  $$
  \exists h.\;\mathsf{ProdHistoryCarrier}(L,R,h);
  $$
  \item carrier-relative reflexivity:
  $$
  \forall h.\;\mathsf{ProdHistoryCarrier}(L,R,h)\to C(h,h);
  $$
  \item source-certified symmetry:
  $$
  \forall h\,k.\;C(h,k)\to C(k,h);
  $$
  \item source-certified transitivity:
  $$
  \forall h\,k\,u.\;C(h,k)\to C(k,u)\to C(h,u);
  $$
  \item NameCert-shaped classifier-induced carrier transport:
  $$
  \forall h\,k.\;\mathsf{ProdHistoryCarrier}(L,R,h)\to C(h,k)\to
  \mathsf{ProdHistoryCarrier}(L,R,k).
  $$
The stronger endpoint exposure
$C(h,k)\to\mathsf{ProdHistoryCarrier}(L,R,h)\land
\mathsf{ProdHistoryCarrier}(L,R,k)$ is
Theorem~\ref{thm:prod-component-history-classifier-endpoint-carriers}; the
displayed source-carrier premise is retained because it is the
carrier-transport field shape of the certificate.
\end{enumerate}
Taking
$\mathsf{ProdHistoryCarrier}(L,R)$ as both pattern carrier and ledger carrier
therefore gives the paper-side componentwise product certificate target.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For carrier inhabitation, $\mathsf{cert}_L.\mathsf{carrier\_inhabited}$ and
$\mathsf{cert}_R.\mathsf{carrier\_inhabited}$ supply histories $l$ and $r$
with $L(l)$ and $R(r)$. Applying
Theorem~\ref{thm:prod-history-carrier-append-intro} to those two source
carrier witnesses gives
$\mathsf{ProdHistoryCarrier}(L,R,\mathsf{append}(l,r))$, so the product
carrier is inhabited. For carrier-relative reflexivity, unpack a
$\mathsf{ProdHistoryCarrier}(L,R,h)$ witness into left and right histories and
reuse that pair representation on both sides; the two source $\NameCert$
witnesses give component reflexivity. For symmetry, invert the
componentwise classifier witness and apply source symmetry to the two component
classifier witnesses, keeping the two pair representations with endpoints
exchanged. For transitivity, unpack $\mathsf{C}(h,k)$ and
$\mathsf{C}(k,u)$ into pair representations $(l_h,r_h)$, $(l_k,r_k)$,
$(l'_k,r'_k)$, and $(l_u,r_u)$. Pair-representation coherence at the middle
history $k$ gives both $l_k\sim_L l'_k$ and $r_k\sim_R r'_k$. The two source
$\NameCert.\mathsf{equiv\_trans}$ fields then compose the left chain
$l_h\sim_L l_k\sim_L l'_k\sim_L l_u$ and the right chain
$r_h\sim_R r_k\sim_R r'_k\sim_R r_u$ separately. For carrier transport, the
classifier witness for the right endpoint contains a displayed pair
representation of $k$, and pair-representation coverage rebuilds
$\mathsf{ProdHistoryCarrier}(L,R,k)$. Pattern and ledger soundness are
identities because both are chosen to be the same product-history carrier.
\end{proof}
