\begin{theorem}[Dyadic Cauchy real-route sibling dependency]
\label{thm:dyadic-cauchy-real-route-sibling-dependency}
Every $\DyadicUp$ Cauchy interface read consumed by Real and Completion routes factors through the dyadic-tail envelope before it reaches the sibling rows named by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}. The route exposes only the finite tail-envelope packet, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular rational readback, the terminal $\RealUp$ seal, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, package provenance, and local naming rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a dyadic Cauchy read at the hub. The hub enters the route through the dyadic-tail envelope, so the first public packet is the finite tail envelope rather than a host Cauchy stream or a selected limit.

The envelope then reads the dyadic tolerance ledger from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}. That ledger supplies the rational tolerance rows used by the regular readback without exporting quotient stream equality or ambient completeness.

Next the route passes through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert} and then \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. These sibling rows provide the regular rational schedule and terminal real-name seal consumed by downstream Real and Completion readers.

Transport, replay, provenance, and naming preserve exactly that ordered route. Since no coordinate of the carrier stores host real equality, a selected completed limit, trichotomy, or an external completion operator, none of those objects can be read from the dyadic Cauchy interface.
\end{proof}
