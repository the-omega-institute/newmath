\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\DyadicCauchyTailEnvelopeUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-dyadic-cauchy-tail-envelope-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, the $\DyadicCauchyTailEnvelopeUp$ packet records the finite tail-envelope boundary that lets a regular rational Cauchy name expose dyadic tolerance data before the terminal real seal is read. It sits between the finite stream windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational name surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance core of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the reindexing discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-reindexing-namecert}, and the real-name boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet is a finite envelope around displayed tails; it is not a quotient stream, a selected limit, countable choice, trichotomy, host real equality, or Real completeness.

\begin{definition}[DyadicCauchyTailEnvelope carrier]
\label{def:dyadic-cauchy-tail-envelope-carrier}
A $\DyadicCauchyTailEnvelopeUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
E=(S,R,D,T,I,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row naming the inspected tail coordinates of the underlying sequence.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row attached to those windows.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row recording the dyadic precision budget for the tail comparison.
\item $T$ is the tail-envelope ledger, recording the displayed assertion that all inspected post-bound rows of $R$ remain inside the dyadic budget named by $D$.
\item $I$ is the $\CauchyReindexingUp$ route row, recording the finite reindexing data used to move from one requested precision window to the tail window named by $S$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the stream window, regular rational readback, dyadic tolerance, tail-envelope ledger, reindexing route, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a precision request through $I,S,R,D,T$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\DyadicCauchyTailEnvelopeUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\CauchyReindexingUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DyadicCauchyTailEnvelopeUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for quotient streams, selected limits, countable choice, trichotomy, host real equality, or Real completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicCauchyTailEnvelope NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:dyadic-cauchy-tail-envelope-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\DyadicCauchyTailEnvelopeUp$ carrier $E=(S,R,D,T,I,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\DyadicCauchyTailEnvelopeUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the $\StreamNameUp$ window, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance, tail-envelope ledger, $\CauchyReindexingUp$ route, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item window admission, requiring every tail-envelope read to open the finite stream window $S$ before reading regular rational rows;
\item tolerance admission, requiring the dyadic budget $D$ to be displayed before the tail ledger $T$ is consumed;
\item reindexing admission, requiring the row $I$ to witness the finite precision-to-tail route used by the read;
\item tail containment, requiring $T$ to compare the displayed rows of $R$ inside the dyadic budget named by $D$ after the route $I$ has been fixed;
\item transport and replay stability, requiring $H$ and $C$ to preserve the same window, readback, tolerance, route, and ledger rows componentwise;
\item ledger nonescape from quotient streams, selected limits, countable choice, trichotomy, host real equality, and Real completeness.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:dyadic-cauchy-tail-envelope-carrier}. The accepted packet displays exactly the finite stream window $S$, regular rational readback $R$, dyadic tolerance row $D$, tail ledger $T$, reindexing route $I$, and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

Window, tolerance, and reindexing admission follow from the route order. A precision request first fixes the finite reindexing row $I$, opens the stream window $S$, reads the regular rational row $R$, and displays the dyadic budget $D$ before the tail-envelope ledger $T$ can be consumed.

Tail containment is exactly the role of $T$. It compares only the displayed post-bound rows of $R$ inside the dyadic budget named by $D$, so the envelope does not introduce a completed quotient stream or a selected limit.

Transport and replay are structural. The $\hsame$ row $H$ moves each displayed coordinate componentwise, and the $\Cont$ row $C$ repeats the same finite route for later consumers.

The nonescape clause follows because none of the excluded objects is a coordinate of the carrier. The local $\NameCert$ row $N$ can therefore export only the displayed tail-envelope surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicCauchyTailEnvelope RegSeqRat Real route]
\label{thm:dyadic-cauchy-tail-envelope-regseqrat-real-route}
Every accepted $\DyadicCauchyTailEnvelopeUp$ carrier $E=(S,R,D,T,I,H,C,P,N)$ exposes a finite route
$$
I \longrightarrow S \longrightarrow R \longrightarrow D \longrightarrow T
$$
from a precision request to the tail-envelope boundary used by $\RegSeqRatUp$ and $\RealUp$ consumers. Any downstream Real or Completion reader receives only the displayed reindexing, finite-window, regular-readback, dyadic-tolerance, and tail-ledger rows, with transport, replay, provenance, and local naming data attached.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted carrier $E=(S,R,D,T,I,H,C,P,N)$. Projection gives the finite reindexing route $I$ from the requested precision to the tail window, and then the stream window $S$ opened along that route.

The regular rational row $R$ is read only through this opened window. The dyadic tolerance row $D$ supplies the comparison budget, and the tail-envelope ledger $T$ records the displayed post-bound containment at that budget.

Thus the Real-facing consumer reaches the envelope only through $I,S,R,D,T$. The remaining rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite route, so no quotient stream, selected limit, countable-choice selector, trichotomy datum, host real equality, or Real-completeness principle is inserted into the handoff.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicCauchyTailEnvelope common refinement]
\label{thm:dyadic-cauchy-tail-envelope-common-refinement}
Let $E=(S,R,D,T,I,H,C,P,N)$ and $E'=(S',R',D',T',I',H',C',P',N')$ be accepted $\DyadicCauchyTailEnvelopeUp$ carriers serving the same displayed precision request. If the reindexing rows $I$ and $I'$ are transported through the same displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ route, then there are a single finite tail window $S^*$ and a single dyadic tolerance row $D^*$ through which both $\RegSeqRatUp$ readbacks factor before either $\RealUp$ seal is read.

Equivalently, the paired read has one finite common route
$$
I^* \longrightarrow S^* \longrightarrow R,R' \longrightarrow D^* \longrightarrow T,T'
$$
whose rows are all displayed in $\BHist$ and named by the two local $\NameCert_{\DyadicCauchyTailEnvelopeUp}$ surfaces. The common route exports no quotient stream, selected limit, countable choice, trichotomy, host real equality, or Real completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier rows of $E$ and $E'$. The two accepted packets expose only their finite stream windows, regular rational readbacks, dyadic tolerance rows, tail ledgers, reindexing routes, componentwise transports, replay rows, provenance rows, and local naming rows.

Use the displayed $\Cont$ replay to compose the two reindexing routes over the common precision request. Since $I$ and $I'$ are transported through the same $\hsame$ route, their comparison is a finite reindexing comparison rather than a choice of a completed stream.

Take $S^*$ to be the common displayed tail window reached by that composed route, and take $D^*$ to be the displayed dyadic tolerance row read after the shared window has been opened. Transport both regular readbacks through the shared $\hsame$ rows, so $R$ and $R'$ are read only as rows over $S^*$.

Finally repack the data as the finite route
$$
I^* \longrightarrow S^* \longrightarrow R,R' \longrightarrow D^* \longrightarrow T,T' .
$$
Both tail ledgers are consumed before either $\RealUp$ seal boundary is inspected. Because the repacked object uses only carrier coordinates already listed in \autoref{def:dyadic-cauchy-tail-envelope-carrier}, it cannot expose quotient streams, selected limits, countable-choice selectors, trichotomy data, host real equality, or a Real-completeness principle.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicCauchyTailEnvelope regular-seal handoff]
\label{thm:dyadic-cauchy-tail-regular-seal}
Every accepted $\DyadicCauchyTailEnvelopeUp$ packet $E=(S,R,D,T,I,H,C,P,N)$ reaches any $\RealUp$ or $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ consumer only through the ordered finite route
$$
I \longrightarrow S \longrightarrow R \longrightarrow D \longrightarrow T
$$
before the seal row is read. The route presents the reindexing request, finite stream window, regular rational readback, dyadic tolerance row, and tail-envelope ledger through the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows, and it exposes no quotient streams, selected limits, countable choice, trichotomy, host real equality, or ambient Real completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:dyadic-cauchy-tail-envelope-carrier}. The accepted packet exposes only $S,R,D,T,I,H,C,P,N$, so a Real or Completion consumer has no route except the displayed envelope rows.

Apply \autoref{thm:dyadic-cauchy-tail-envelope-regseqrat-real-route}. It orders the consumer-facing read as the finite path from the precision request through $I$, then $S$, then $R$, then $D$, and finally $T$ before any Real-facing seal is consumed.

For paired tail reads, use \autoref{thm:dyadic-cauchy-tail-envelope-common-refinement}. The common refinement supplies a shared finite route for the two reads, so neither branch can replace the displayed stream window, regular readback, dyadic budget, or tail ledger by a quotient stream, selected limit, countable-choice selector, trichotomy datum, host real equality, or ambient completeness proof.
\end{proof}
