\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\DyadicCauchyApproximationUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-dyadic-cauchy-approximation-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges, the $\DyadicCauchyApproximationUp$ packet records a finite dyadic precision interface for a displayed real name before any completion-facing consumer reads a limit. It sits between the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the finite observation windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the terminal real seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, and the rational approximation face of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-rational-approximation-namecert}. The packet is a precision certificate for Real and Completion routes; it is not a quotient stream, host real equality, selected limit, trichotomy branch, countable-choice selector, or ambient completeness principle.

\begin{definition}[Dyadic Cauchy approximation carrier]
\label{def:dyadic-cauchy-approximation-carrier}
A $\DyadicCauchyApproximationUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
(q,D,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $q$ is the requested precision row, read as finite BEDC data before any approximation row is consumed.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row attached to $q$, recording the dyadic budget and its exact arithmetic witness.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite window opened from the real name at the requested precision.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row obtained from $S$ under the dyadic tolerance named by $D$.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal reached only after $q,D,S,R$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the precision request, dyadic tolerance, stream window, regular readback, real seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ stores displayed $\Cont$ replay from the precision request through $D,S,R$ and then into the terminal real seal $E$.
\item $P$ records $\Pkg$ provenance over $\DyadicCauchyApproximationUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DyadicCauchyApproximationUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for quotient streams, host real equality, selected limits, trichotomy, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Dyadic Cauchy approximation NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:dyadic-cauchy-approximation-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\DyadicCauchyApproximationUp$ carrier $(q,D,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\DyadicCauchyApproximationUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the precision request, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row, $\StreamNameUp$ finite window, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, $\RealUp$ seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item precision-window admission, requiring $S$ to be opened from the displayed real name at the precision named by $q$ before $R$ is read;
\item dyadic tolerance exactness, requiring the tolerance row $D$ to expose the exact $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ budget used by the window and readback rows;
\item regular-readback handoff, requiring $R$ to be the only rational observation row passed toward the $\RealUp$ seal at that precision;
\item Real-seal nonescape, requiring every real-facing read to pass through the ordered route $q,D,S,R,E$ before the seal is exported;
\item provenance exhaustion, requiring $H,C,P,N$ to transport, replay, source, and name only the displayed finite route.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:dyadic-cauchy-approximation-carrier}. The accepted packet displays the requested precision, dyadic tolerance, finite stream window, regular rational readback, terminal real seal, and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

Precision-window admission follows from the route order. The finite request $q$ first selects the dyadic budget $D$, then opens the window $S$, and only then permits the regular rational readback row $R$ to be consumed.

Dyadic tolerance exactness is carried by $D$. Since the tolerance row is a $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ coordinate of the same packet, the readback cannot appeal to an external analytic radius or host real metric.

The regular-readback handoff is the row $R$. It is the only rational observation row between the finite window $S$ and the real seal $E$, and its dependency on $D$ records the precision budget used by the read.

Real-seal nonescape and provenance exhaustion are structural. The $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ rows preserve the route $q,D,S,R,E$ and introduce no coordinate for quotient streams, host real equality, selected limits, trichotomy, countable choice, or ambient completeness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Dyadic Cauchy approximation Real seal handoff]
\label{thm:dyadic-cauchy-approximation-real-seal-handoff}
Every Real-facing read exported by an accepted $\DyadicCauchyApproximationUp$ carrier factors through the finite packet $(q,D,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)$. The handoff exports no quotient stream, host real equality, selected limit, trichotomy branch, countable-choice selector, or ambient completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a real-facing read of an accepted packet. Projection gives the terminal seal $E$, but the carrier reaches $E$ only after the precision request $q$, dyadic tolerance $D$, stream window $S$, and regular readback $R$ have been displayed.

The structural rows do not enlarge the exported boundary. The componentwise transport $H$ and continuation replay $C$ preserve the same finite route, while $P$ and $N$ record provenance and local naming for that route.

Therefore every consumer of the seal receives exactly the displayed dyadic approximation interface. None of the excluded objects is a carrier coordinate, so no alternate read through quotient streams, host real equality, selected limits, trichotomy, countable choice, or ambient completeness is available.
\end{proof}
