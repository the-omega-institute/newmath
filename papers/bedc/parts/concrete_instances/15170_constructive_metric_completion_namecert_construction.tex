\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ConstructiveMetricCompletionUp$}

Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, the $\ConstructiveMetricCompletionUp$ packet records the finite NameCert surface for constructive metric completion as a route through located metric data, regular Cauchy names, dyadic tolerances, filter bases, separated equality, dense embedding, and uniformly continuous extension. It reads the metric-completion packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metriccompletion-namecert}, the located-metric source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedmetric-namecert}, the Cauchy-filter basis row of the completion interface, the separated regular-Cauchy route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-completion-separated-namecert}, the embedding handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completionembedding-namecert}, the extension surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniform-continuous-extension-namecert}, and the naming spine of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet is a finite completion certificate for displayed Cauchy-filter reads; it is not a quotient construction, a selected host limit, a countable-choice schedule, an open-cover compactness principle, or a full completeness theorem for $\RealUp$.

\begin{definition}[Constructive metric completion carrier]
\label{def:constructive-metric-completion-carrier}
A $\ConstructiveMetricCompletionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(X,A,D,F,E,S,U,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the source $\LocatedMetricUp$ row, including the displayed $\MetricUp$ distance witness and the finite locatedness route used by completion-facing consumers.
\item $A$ is the regular Cauchy name family row. It stores the $\StreamNameUp$ windows and $\RegSeqRatUp$ readbacks by which a completion point is inspected at finite precision.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger attached to the windows of $A$, including the monotone tolerance requests used to compare finite prefixes.
\item $F$ is the $\CauchyFilterBasisUp$ row, recording the Cauchy-filter neighborhoods generated by the same regular names and dyadic tolerances.
\item $E$ is the $\CompletionEmbeddingUp$ row that embeds source observations densely into the displayed completion route.
\item $S$ is the $\RegularCauchyCompletionSeparatedUp$ row, exposing separated completion equality only through the displayed regular-Cauchy and zero-distance comparison route.
\item $U$ is the $\UniformContinuousExtensionUp$ handoff row for uniformly continuous consumers of the dense source.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the located source, regular Cauchy names, dyadic ledger, Cauchy filter basis, completion embedding, separated equality row, extension handoff, replay row, provenance row, and local naming row.
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ replay from located-metric observations to regular Cauchy windows, dyadic tolerances, Cauchy-filter neighborhoods, embedding reads, separated comparisons, and uniformly continuous extension reads.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ConstructiveMetricCompletionUp$, $\LocatedMetricUp$, $\MetricCompletionUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\CauchyFilterBasisUp$, $\CompletionEmbeddingUp$, $\RegularCauchyCompletionSeparatedUp$, $\UniformContinuousExtensionUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ConstructiveMetricCompletionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for quotient representatives, host-selected limits, countable choice, open-cover compactness, detached $\RealUp$ completeness, or an ambient complete metric space.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[ConstructiveMetricCompletion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:constructive-metric-completion-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\ConstructiveMetricCompletionUp$ carrier $K=(X,A,D,F,E,S,U,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\ConstructiveMetricCompletionUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed located metric source, regular Cauchy name family, dyadic tolerance ledger, Cauchy filter basis, completion embedding, separated equality row, uniformly continuous extension handoff, transports, replay route, provenance, and local naming row;
\item classifier stability, requiring two accepted completion reads to be compared only through the same ordered route $X,A,D,F,E,S,U,H,C,P,N$ after componentwise $\hsame$ transport;
\item embedding coverage, requiring each source observation used by the completion packet to enter through the dense $\CompletionEmbeddingUp$ row $E$;
\item separatedness exactness, requiring every equality judgement between completion reads to pass through $S$ and hence through the displayed regular-Cauchy separated route;
\item universal handoff, requiring each uniformly continuous consumer of the dense source to read the completion point through $F,S,U$ before any exported value is named;
\item transport replay, requiring $H$ and $C$ to preserve the located source, regular names, dyadic tolerances, filter basis, embedding row, separated row, and extension row as one packet;
\item ledger non-escape, stating that no quotient representative, host-selected limit, countable-choice selector, open-cover compactness principle, detached $\RealUp$ completeness theorem, or ambient complete metric space is exported.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:constructive-metric-completion-carrier}. The tuple displays the located source, regular Cauchy name family, dyadic ledger, Cauchy-filter basis, completion embedding, separated equality row, extension handoff, and structural support rows.

Classifier stability is componentwise. The transport row $H$ moves each displayed coordinate in the same order, and the replay row $C$ forces any accepted comparison to traverse the located source, regular names, dyadic tolerances, filter basis, embedding row, separated row, and extension row before the local name is read.

Embedding coverage is the role of $E$. A source observation becomes completion-facing data only through the dense embedding row, so no selected representative or host completion point can be substituted for it.

Separatedness exactness is isolated in $S$. Equality of completion reads is admitted only after the regular Cauchy windows and dyadic tolerances have been displayed, and only through the separated completion route named by that row.

The universal handoff is carried by $U$ after $F$ and $S$ are present. The provenance row $P$ and local naming row $N$ record no further coordinate, so the excluded quotient, choice, compactness, host-limit, real-completeness, and ambient-completeness principles cannot be read from the packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ConstructiveMetricCompletion universal handoff]
\label{thm:constructive-metric-completion-universal-handoff}
Let $K=(X,A,D,F,E,S,U,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\ConstructiveMetricCompletionUp$ packet. Any uniformly continuous consumer of the dense source reads a completed point only through the displayed Cauchy-filter basis $F$, the separated completion equality row $S$, and the uniformly continuous extension row $U$, after the located source $X$, regular Cauchy name family $A$, dyadic tolerance ledger $D$, and completion embedding row $E$ have been displayed.

Thus every such consumer factors through the finite route
$$
X,A,D,E\longmapsto F\longmapsto S\longmapsto U\longmapsto H,C,P,N ,
$$
not through quotient representatives, host-selected limits, countable choice, open-cover compactness, detached $\RealUp$ completeness, or an ambient complete metric space.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the dense source. The located metric row $X$ supplies finite distance and locatedness reads, while $A$ and $D$ supply the scheduled regular Cauchy windows and dyadic tolerance ledger.

The source observation becomes completion-facing only through $E$. This places the dense embedding row before any consumer can inspect a completed point.

The Cauchy-filter basis $F$ is then the unique neighborhood route available to the consumer. It is generated from the same regular names and dyadic tolerances, so the consumer cannot replace it by a selected representative or host limit.

Separated comparison is read next through $S$. The uniformly continuous extension row $U$ may be consumed only after that separated route is available, ensuring that the extension depends on the displayed Cauchy-filter and separated completion rows.

Finally, $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite route. Since the carrier has no coordinate for quotient representatives, host-selected limits, countable choice, open-cover compactness, detached $\RealUp$ completeness, or ambient completeness, every uniformly continuous consumer factors through the stated handoff.
\end{proof}
