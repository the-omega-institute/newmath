\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\HahnMazurkiewiczBoundaryUp$}

Following Hahn and Mazurkiewicz, the $\HahnMazurkiewiczBoundaryUp$ packet records the finite boundary certificate through which a compact connected locally connected metric continuum may be read before a Peano-continuum or Peano-curve consumer treats it as curve-facing data. It sits between the Peano continuum surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-peano-continuum-namecert}, the finite curve-parametrization route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-peano-curve-namecert}, the compact metric source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, the continuous-map rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuousmap-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet names the boundary ledger needed for the classical Hahn--Mazurkiewicz handoff without importing an ambient topological quotient, host compactness theorem, selected parametrization, countable-choice schedule, or unrestricted real equality.

\begin{definition}[Hahn-Mazurkiewicz boundary carrier]
\label{def:hahn-mazurkiewicz-boundary-carrier}
A $\HahnMazurkiewiczBoundaryUp$ carrier is a finite BEDC packet
$$
H=(K,M,C,P,B,E,T,R,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $K$ is a compact metric source row read through $\CompactMetricUp$;
\item $M$ is a Peano-continuum local connectedness and connectedness ledger;
\item $C$ is a finite $\ContinuousMapUp$ curve-read row from a compact interval-facing source into $K$;
\item $P$ is the Peano-curve parametrization boundary row that records surjective coverage only through displayed windows;
\item $B$ is the boundary classifier row comparing compact-metric neighborhoods by finite $\BHist$ support and componentwise $\hsame$ transport;
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal for endpoint and radius data;
\item $T$ is the displayed $\Cont$ replay ledger for boundary refinements;
\item $R$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance row; and
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert$ row naming the packet.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no host quotient space, selected Peano parametrization, global compactness theorem, countable-choice selector, quotient real equality, or ambient completeness coordinate.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Hahn-Mazurkiewicz boundary NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:hahn-mazurkiewicz-boundary-namecert-obligations}
For every carrier $H=(K,M,C,P,B,E,T,R,N)$ of \autoref{def:hahn-mazurkiewicz-boundary-carrier}, the source, classifier, stability, replay, provenance, and local naming obligations for $\HahnMazurkiewiczBoundaryUp$ are discharged on the displayed finite rows. In particular, compact-metric reads remain on $K$, Peano-continuum reads remain on $M$, continuous-map and Peano-curve reads remain on $C$ and $P$, boundary comparisons remain on $B$ and $E$, and transport/replay/provenance/naming remain on $T,R,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier rows of \autoref{def:hahn-mazurkiewicz-boundary-carrier}. The compact metric source, Peano-continuum ledger, continuous-map row, Peano-curve boundary, boundary classifier, real seal, replay ledger, provenance row, and local NameCert row are all displayed coordinates of the same finite packet.

The classifier obligation is read through $B$ with $\BHist$ support and componentwise $\hsame$ transport, while endpoint and radius comparisons pass through $E$ before any Real-facing consumer sees them. Replay is confined to $T$, and provenance is confined to $R$.

Thus every NameCert obligation is witnessed by the listed rows. Since the carrier has no coordinate for a host quotient space, selected parametrization, global compactness theorem, countable-choice selector, quotient real equality, or ambient completeness, none of those claims can be exported by the certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Hahn-Mazurkiewicz boundary Peano-continuum handoff]
\label{thm:hahn-mazurkiewicz-boundary-peano-continuum-handoff}
Every $\PeanoContinuumUp$ or $\PeanoCurveUp$ consumer that reads a $\HahnMazurkiewiczBoundaryUp$ packet must factor through the compact metric source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, the continuous-map rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuousmap-namecert}, and the terminal $\RealUp$ seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert} before it consumes the Peano-continuum and Peano-curve boundary rows. The handoff exports a finite boundary certificate, not a host Hahn--Mazurkiewicz theorem, not a selected surjection, and not an unrestricted quotient equality of points.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier decomposition. Any consumer receives the compact metric row $K$, continuous-map row $C$, Peano-continuum ledger $M$, Peano-curve boundary $P$, boundary classifier $B$, and Real seal $E$ as separate visible rows.

The Peano-continuum side can inspect local connectedness and connectedness only through $M$, while the Peano-curve side can inspect coverage only through $P$ after $C$ has supplied the finite continuous-map read. Endpoint and radius data pass through $E$ before the boundary classifier $B$ is consumed.

Therefore the handoff factors through the compact metric, continuous-map, and Real sibling chapters before either Peano consumer reads the boundary packet. The missing host quotient, selected surjection, and unrestricted equality coordinates remain outside the exported interface.
\end{proof}
