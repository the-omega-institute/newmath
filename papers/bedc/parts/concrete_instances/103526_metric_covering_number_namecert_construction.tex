\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\MetricCoveringNumberUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-metric-covering-number-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges constructive analysis, the $\MetricCoveringNumberUp$ packet records the quantitative finite-net count attached to a displayed metric tolerance. It sits between the metric source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the finite epsilon-net route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finite-epsilon-net-namecert}, the total-bounded surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-totallybounded-namecert}, the compact-metric consumer of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, and the dyadic tolerance core of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}. The packet names the covering-number boundary as finite BEDC data, not as a host finite set, a minimal-cardinality proof, or an open-cover compactness argument.

\begin{definition}[Metric covering-number carrier]
\label{def:metric-covering-number-carrier}
A $\MetricCoveringNumberUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(X,\varepsilon,P,C,D,B,H,R,S,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the $\MetricUp$ source row carrying the point classifier, distance classifier, and metric-law ledger read by the packet.
\item $\varepsilon$ is the positive tolerance row, read through $\RatUp$ or $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ together with the displayed positivity evidence used by coverage consumers.
\item $P$ is the closed $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ of centers admitted for the selected tolerance.
\item $C$ is the center-carrier ledger, recording for every accepted center of $P$ the corresponding point-classifier admission in $X$.
\item $D$ is the distance and coverage ledger pairing each inspected source-point history with an admitted center and a metric-distance row bounded by $\varepsilon$.
\item $B$ is the unary budget row naming the number of center entries exposed by $P$ and replayed by the coverage ledger.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the metric source, tolerance row, center bundle, center-carrier ledger, coverage ledger, budget row, replay row, provenance row, and local naming row.
\item $R$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a covering-number request through $X,\varepsilon,P,C,D,B,H,S,N$.
\item $S$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\MetricCoveringNumberUp$, $\MetricUp$, $\FiniteEpsilonNetUp$, $\TotallyBoundedUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\RatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MetricCoveringNumberUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only through $X,\varepsilon,P,C,D,B,H,R,S,N$. It has no coordinate for an ambient finite subset, host enumeration, least-cardinality selector, open-cover refinement, sequential compactness proof, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Metric covering-number NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:metric-covering-number-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\MetricCoveringNumberUp$ carrier $K=(X,\varepsilon,P,C,D,B,H,R,S,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\MetricCoveringNumberUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed metric source, positive tolerance, center bundle, center-carrier ledger, coverage-distance ledger, unary budget, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item center visibility, requiring each center counted by $B$ and used by $D$ to enter through the same displayed $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ row $P$ and center-carrier ledger $C$;
\item coverage exactness, requiring every inspected point of $X$ to be paired by $D$ with a displayed distance row bounded by $\varepsilon$;
\item budget exactness, requiring the exported covering number to count exactly the visible center entries of $P$ used by the coverage ledger;
\item tolerance transport, requiring the $\RatUp$ or $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row to remain fixed under $H$ before any consumer reads the count;
\item ledger nonescape, excluding ambient finite subsets, host enumerations, least-cardinality selectors, open-cover compactness, sequential compactness, countable choice, and ambient $\RealUp$ completeness as local certificate data.
\end{enumerate}
No metric covering-number obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier of \autoref{def:metric-covering-number-carrier}. The analytic rows are exactly the metric source $X$, positive tolerance $\varepsilon$, center bundle $P$, center-carrier ledger $C$, coverage-distance ledger $D$, and unary budget row $B$.

Center visibility and coverage exactness are rowwise. A center can be counted only after it is visible in $P$ and admitted by $C$ as a point of $X$, and a source point is covered only through one of the distance rows displayed by $D$.

The budget row then names the quantitative boundary. Because $B$ counts the same visible entries that $P,C,D$ use, a consumer cannot replace the packet by a smaller host set, an external enumeration, or a minimal-cardinality argument.

The transport and replay rows preserve the same route. The row $H$ moves no coordinate beyond $X,\varepsilon,P,C,D,B,R,S,N$, and $R$ replays covering-number requests through the same ordered data. The refusal clauses follow from absence of coordinates for open covers, selected compactness proofs, countable choice, sequential compactness, and ambient completeness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Metric covering-number finite-net handoff]
\label{thm:metric-covering-number-finite-net-handoff}
Every $\TotallyBoundedUp$ or $\CompactMetricUp$ consumer reading an accepted $\MetricCoveringNumberUp$ carrier factors through the finite route
$$
X,\varepsilon \longmapsto P,C,D,B \longmapsto H,R,S,N .
$$
Consequently the consumer receives the finite center bundle, positive tolerance row, coverage witnesses, and unary covering budget before any total-bounded or compact-metric conclusion is available. The handoff exports no ambient finite subset, host enumeration, least-cardinality selector, open-cover compactness proof, sequential compactness proof, countable-choice schedule, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a total-bounded or compact-metric consumer of $K$. By \autoref{def:metric-covering-number-carrier}, the consumer can enter the packet only through the displayed metric source $X$ and the displayed tolerance row $\varepsilon$.

The finite-net data are opened next. The closed bundle $P$ names the centers, $C$ admits those centers as points of $X$, and $D$ supplies the coverage-distance rows. The budget row $B$ then records the quantitative count of exactly those center entries.

Finally $H,R,S,N$ package the same route under classifier transport, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming. Applying \autoref{thm:metric-covering-number-namecert-obligations}, the consumer cannot bypass the finite-net route through a host finite subset, an open-cover compactness proof, or a choice-selected enumeration.
\end{proof}
