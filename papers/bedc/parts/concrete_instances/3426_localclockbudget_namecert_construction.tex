The $\LocalClockBudgetUp$ packet realizes observer local time as a finite certificate surface. It reads a single $\Hist$ stream through bounded $\Cont$ windows, records the local unary budget for those windows, transports the same rows by $\hsame$, and exposes a no-global-clock ledger for downstream consumers. The packet does not synchronize distinct histories, choose an ambient clock, or import a hidden limit schedule. The local stream is consumed as the time-budget face of the observer-history surface in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observerhistoryidentity-namecert} and the locality-cell boundary in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observer-locality-cell-namecert}.

The same finite local-window budget supplies the observer-facing boundary consumed by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observersubjectboundary-namecert} and the distinction-trace consumer of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observer-distinction-trace-namecert}. In both cases the dependency is the carrier window row $W$ together with the ledger-boundary row that prevents a downstream observer packet from replacing local time by a global clock.

\begin{definition}[Local clock budget carrier]
\label{def:local-clock-budget-carrier}
A $\LocalClockBudgetUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
C=(H,T,W,B,L,Q,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $H$ is the observed $\Hist$ row whose local time stream is being budgeted;
\item $T$ is the finite local-time-stream row, recording the $\Cont$ applications from $\emp$ to the displayed history surface;
\item $W$ is the finite family of $\Cont$-step window rows selected for the budget;
\item $B$ is the unary budget row that counts the admitted local steps and window endpoints;
\item $L$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the history row, local stream row, window rows, budget row, route row, package row, and local naming row;
\item $Q$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes for history projection, stream readback, window inspection, budget readback, and consumer handoff;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\Hist$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, $\NameCert$, local time streams, and no-global-clock ledgers;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LocalClockBudgetUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains exactly these rows. It has no coordinate for a global clock, inter-history synchronization value, host time parameter, limit scheduler, or observer-substance distinct from the recorded history.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Local clock budget window totality]
\label{thm:local-clock-budget-window-totality}
Every accepted $\LocalClockBudgetUp$ carrier exposes a finite local time stream and a finite covering family of $\Cont$-step windows. Each window is selected from the row $T$ of local $\Cont$ applications, counted by the unary budget row $B$, and replayed through the displayed route row $Q$ inside the same packet
$$
C=(H,T,W,B,L,Q,P,N).
$$
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.LocalClockBudgetUp.LocalClockBudgetWindow\_totality}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:local-clock-budget-carrier}. The rows $H,T,W,B,L,Q,P,N$ display the observed history, its local stream, finite windows, unary budget, transport, routes, provenance, and local name.

The local stream row $T$ is read before the window family $W$. Therefore each admitted window is a finite part of the displayed $\Cont$ route from $\emp$ toward the history row $H$.

The unary row $B$ counts the selected windows and endpoints, while $Q$ replays their inspection for consumers. Since the carrier has no unbounded schedule or ambient time coordinate, every admitted window is exhausted by the finite packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Local clock budget route determinacy]
\label{thm:local-clock-budget-route-determinacy}
Let two accepted $\LocalClockBudgetUp$ consumer routes display the same rows $H,T,W,B,L,Q,P,N$. Then they determine the same local-time-budget surface up to the componentwise $\hsame$ transports recorded in $L$. \end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.LocalClockBudgetUp.LocalClockBudgetRoute\_determinacy}

Apply window totality. \autoref{thm:local-clock-budget-window-totality} binds the window family $W$ and budget row $B$ to the same finite $\Cont$-step stream and routes their readback through $Q$.

Finally use the transport row $L$. Componentwise $\hsame$ transport preserves the displayed history, stream, windows, budget, provenance, and local name, while the carrier inventory excludes every global or host-side clock coordinate that could separate the two routes.
\end{proof}
