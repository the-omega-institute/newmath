\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\PolytopeUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-polytope-namecert}

The polytope interface $\PolytopeUp$ packages a bounded convex set in a finite-dimensional vector-space carrier presented as the intersection of finitely many half-spaces, with vertex, edge, and face combinatorial structure. It depends on $\NameCert_{\ConvexSetUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-convexset-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\FinSetUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finset-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:polytope-carrier}

\begin{definition}[Polytope BHist face packet]
\label{def:polytope-bhist-face-packet}
A $\PolytopeUp$ BHist face packet is a finite certificate tuple
$$
\mathcal{P}=(\eta_C,\eta_F,H,V,E,\Phi,L,\pi)
$$
over $\mathsf{BHist}$ in which $\eta_C:\NameCert_{\ConvexSetUp}$ supplies the
convex carrier rows, $\eta_F:\NameCert_{\FinSetUp}$ supplies the finite-index
rows, $H$ is the finite list of half-space rows, $V$ is the finite vertex row
ledger, $E$ is the finite edge row ledger, $\Phi$ is the finite face row ledger,
$L$ is the collection of $\Cont$ ledgers connecting half-space, vertex, edge,
and face rows, and $\pi:\Pkg$ records the provenance of those rows.

The carrier accepts $\mathcal{P}$ exactly when every half-space row is scoped by
$\eta_C$, every vertex, edge, and face index is scoped by $\eta_F$, every
incidence row is a $\mathsf{BHist}$ row transported by $\hsame$, and each
displayed face is generated by the finite half-space ledger together with the
finite vertex and edge incidence ledgers named in $L$.
\end{definition}
\leandef{BEDC.Derived.PolytopeUp.PolytopeBHistFacePacket}

\begin{theorem}[Polytope half-space and face carrier stability]
\label{thm:polytope-halfspace-face-carrier-stability}
Let $\mathcal{P}$ be a $\PolytopeUp$ BHist face packet. If each half-space row,
vertex row, edge row, face row, and incidence ledger row of $\mathcal{P}$ is
transported along an $\hsame$ comparison to the corresponding row of a packet
$\mathcal{P}'$, then $\mathcal{P}'$ is again a $\PolytopeUp$ BHist face packet.
The transported packet keeps the same $\NameCert_{\ConvexSetUp}$ and
$\NameCert_{\FinSetUp}$ dependency witnesses, the same finite $\Cont$ ledger
shape, and the same $\Pkg$ provenance rows.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.PolytopeUp.PolytopeBHistFacePacket\_halfspace\_face\_carrier\_stability}
\begin{proof}
Project the packet into its half-space, vertex, edge, face, incidence,
dependency, ledger, and provenance components. Transport the half-space rows by
the $\hsame$ stability supplied by the convex-set source certificate, and
transport the finite vertex, edge, and face indices by the finite-set source
certificate.

Next transport the displayed incidence and containment rows componentwise. The
$\Cont$ ledgers are finite, so each source, step, and result row is replaced by
its transported $\hsame$ mate while the ledger order and package provenance are
left unchanged.

Repack the transported rows into the tuple defining
\autoref{def:polytope-bhist-face-packet}. The acceptance clauses are exactly
the transported clauses, so the target tuple is a carried $\PolytopeUp$ packet.
\end{proof}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:polytope-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[Polytope vertex, edge, and face ledger readback]
\label{thm:polytope-vertex-edge-ledger-readback}
Every $\PolytopeUp$ BHist face packet exposes finite readback rows for its
vertices, edges, and faces. More precisely, the packet determines a finite
$\mathsf{BHist}$ vertex ledger, a finite edge-incidence ledger, and a finite
face-incidence ledger, each indexed through the $\NameCert_{\FinSetUp}$ source
and each connected to the packet carrier through the displayed $\Cont$ rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the packet of \autoref{def:polytope-bhist-face-packet}. The components
$V$, $E$, and $\Phi$ are already finite ledgers, and the finite-set source
certificate records their index rows as $\NameCert_{\FinSetUp}$ data.

For each ledger row, read the corresponding $\Cont$ entry from $L$. The entry
names the source half-space or incidence row, the finite step row, and the
resulting vertex, edge, or face row. Collecting these entries gives the three
advertised finite readback ledgers, with no appeal to an ambient geometric
carrier outside the packet.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.PolytopeUp.PolytopeBHistFacePacket\_vertex\_edge\_ledger\_readback}

\begin{theorem}[Polytope ConvexSet and FinSet dependency readback]
\label{thm:polytope-convex-finset-dependency-readback}
Every $\PolytopeUp$ BHist face packet reads back exactly the two dependency
rows $\NameCert_{\ConvexSetUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\FinSetUp}$ used to scope its
half-space and finite combinatorial ledgers. The convex dependency accounts for
half-space intersection and carried face rows, while the finite-set dependency
accounts for the vertex, edge, face, and incidence indices.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The first two components of the packet are the displayed dependency witnesses
$\eta_C$ and $\eta_F$. Projecting them gives the convex-set carrier and
finite-set carrier rows available to the packet.

Conversely, every half-space, vertex, edge, face, and incidence clause in the
packet definition is scoped through one of these two witnesses and a finite
$\Cont$ row. Therefore the dependency readback is exactly
$\NameCert_{\ConvexSetUp}$ together with $\NameCert_{\FinSetUp}$, plus the
kernel rows already named by the packet.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.PolytopeUp.PolytopeBHistFacePacket\_convex\_finset\_dependency\_readback}

\begin{theorem}[Polytope downstream boundary]
\label{thm:polytope-downstream-boundary}
Downstream convex consumers of a $\PolytopeUp$ BHist face packet may read only
the $\NameCert_{\ConvexSetUp}$ dependency, the half-space rows, the carried
face rows, the $\hsame$ transports, and the finite $\Cont$ ledgers connecting
them. Downstream combinatorial consumers may read only the
$\NameCert_{\FinSetUp}$ dependency, the vertex, edge, face, and incidence
ledgers, the same $\hsame$ transports, and the same $\Pkg$ provenance rows. No
downstream consumer receives an ambient Euclidean metric, a quotient carrier,
an external face lattice, or a standard bridge from this packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For a convex consumer, project the packet to $\eta_C$, the half-space list, the
face rows, and the $\Cont$ ledgers that generate those faces. These are exactly
the rows named in the carrier definition for convex readback.

For a combinatorial consumer, project the packet to $\eta_F$, the finite
vertex, edge, face, and incidence ledgers, their $\hsame$ transport rows, and
the provenance package. These rows determine the finite combinatorial surface
without adding any external lattice object.

The two projections share only the kernel rows explicitly stored in the packet:
$\mathsf{BHist}$ rows, $\hsame$ comparisons, $\Cont$ ledgers, and $\Pkg$
provenance. Hence the boundary grants precisely those read permissions and no
metric, quotient, external lattice, or bridge data.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[Polytope face classifier]
\label{def:polytope-face-classifier}
The $\PolytopeUp$ face classifier relates two BHist face packets when their
half-space rows, vertex rows, edge rows, face rows, $\Cont$ ledgers, and $\Pkg$
provenance rows compare componentwise by $\hsame$, and when the dependency
rows supplied by $\NameCert_{\ConvexSetUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\FinSetUp}$ are the
same accepted dependency rows on both sides.

Thus classifier agreement is entirely a BEDC row relation: half-space
agreement is read through the convex-set dependency, vertex, edge, and face
agreement is read through the finite-set dependency, and every incidence or
containment movement is witnessed by the displayed $\Cont$ ledgers. No ambient
face lattice or metric equality is part of the classifier.
\end{definition}
\leandef{BEDC.Derived.PolytopeUp.PolytopeFaceClassifier}

\begin{theorem}[Polytope face classifier transport]
\label{thm:polytope-face-classifier-transport}
If two $\PolytopeUp$ BHist face packets are related by the classifier of
\autoref{def:polytope-face-classifier}, then any packet obtained by
componentwise $\hsame$ transport of its half-space, vertex, edge, face,
$\Cont$, and $\Pkg$ rows is again accepted by the same classifier. The
transported packet keeps the same $\NameCert_{\ConvexSetUp}$ and
$\NameCert_{\FinSetUp}$ dependency rows and remains available to the downstream
boundary of \autoref{thm:polytope-downstream-boundary}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the classifier into its convex, finite, incidence, ledger, and
provenance components. The convex component transports half-space and carried
face rows through $\NameCert_{\ConvexSetUp}$, while the finite component
transports vertex, edge, face, and incidence indices through
$\NameCert_{\FinSetUp}$.

Next transport the finite $\Cont$ ledger entries. Each entry has the same
source, step, and result shape after replacing endpoints by their $\hsame$
mates, and the $\Pkg$ row is carried along the same componentwise comparison.

Repack these transported rows using
\autoref{def:polytope-face-classifier}. The acceptance clauses are exactly the
transported classifier clauses, so the resulting packet remains classifier
related and has the same downstream readback permissions.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.PolytopeUp.PolytopeFaceClassifier\_transport}

\begin{theorem}[Polytope obligation boundary exhaustion]
\label{thm:polytope-obligation-boundary-exhaustion}
The carrier block, face-classifier block, stability block, dependency readback
block, and downstream boundary block exhaust the $\PolytopeUp$
$\NameCert$ obligation surface. Every accepted obligation row is one of the
finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ half-space, vertex, edge, face, incidence, $\Cont$, or
$\Pkg$ rows named in those blocks, together with the two dependency rows
$\NameCert_{\ConvexSetUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\FinSetUp}$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.PolytopeUp.PolytopeBHistFacePacket\_obligation\_boundary\_exhaustion}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier definition
\autoref{def:polytope-bhist-face-packet}. It lists all packet fields:
dependency rows, half-space rows, vertex rows, edge rows, face rows,
incidence ledgers, $\Cont$ ledgers, and provenance.

The classifier and stability rows are exactly
\autoref{def:polytope-face-classifier},
\autoref{thm:polytope-face-classifier-transport}, and
\autoref{thm:polytope-halfspace-face-carrier-stability}. They move only the
listed finite rows along $\hsame$ and do not add a metric, quotient, or
external face lattice.

Finally combine dependency readback
\autoref{thm:polytope-convex-finset-dependency-readback} with the downstream
boundary \autoref{thm:polytope-downstream-boundary}. These projections account
for every consumer-visible row, so the displayed blocks exhaust the obligation
surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PolytopeUp obligation surface]
\label{thm:polytope-obligation-surface}
The existing $\PolytopeUp$ BHist face packet supplies the roadmap obligation
surface for this chapter. Its carrier row is
\autoref{def:polytope-bhist-face-packet}; its classifier and stability rows are
the $\hsame$ transport surface of
\autoref{thm:polytope-halfspace-face-carrier-stability}; its finite vertex,
edge, and face ledger readback is
\autoref{thm:polytope-vertex-edge-ledger-readback}; its dependency scope is
\autoref{thm:polytope-convex-finset-dependency-readback}; and its downstream
consumer boundary is \autoref{thm:polytope-downstream-boundary}.

Consequently the obligation inventory consists only of the
$\NameCert_{\ConvexSetUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\FinSetUp}$ dependency rows, finite
half-space, vertex, edge, face, and incidence $\mathsf{BHist}$ rows, displayed
$\hsame$ transports, finite $\Cont$ ledger rows, and $\Pkg$ provenance. It
contains no Euclidean metric, quotient carrier, external face lattice, or
standard bridge.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.PolytopeUp.PolytopeBHistFacePacket\_obligation\_surface}
\begin{proof}
Project the packet to its carrier definition. The tuple of
\autoref{def:polytope-bhist-face-packet} names exactly the convex dependency,
finite-index dependency, half-space rows, vertex ledger, edge ledger, face
ledger, continuity ledger, and provenance row.

Next transport the classifier surface. Apply
\autoref{thm:polytope-halfspace-face-carrier-stability} to the displayed
$\hsame$ comparisons; it keeps the dependency witnesses, ledger shape, and
package provenance while replacing each carried row by its transported mate.

Read back the finite ledgers and dependencies. The vertex, edge, and face rows
come from \autoref{thm:polytope-vertex-edge-ledger-readback}, while
\autoref{thm:polytope-convex-finset-dependency-readback} isolates the two
source certificates that scope every half-space and finite combinatorial row.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:polytope-downstream-boundary}. It is precisely the
consumer projection of the same packet, so repacking the carrier, transport,
ledger, dependency, and boundary projections gives the stated obligation
surface. The exclusion clause follows from the same downstream boundary: none
of the packet projections exposes metric, quotient, external lattice, or bridge
data.
\end{proof}
