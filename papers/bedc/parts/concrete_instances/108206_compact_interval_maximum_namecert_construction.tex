\begin{definition}[Compact interval maximum carrier]
\label{def:compact-interval-maximum-carrier}

$$

with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $I$ is the $\ClosedBoundedIntervalUp$ source row, exposing endpoint order, rational containment, dyadic containment, $\StreamNameUp$ windows, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, and $\RealUp$ sealing.
\item $F$ is the $\ContinuousMapUp$ graph row for the displayed interval source, including finite graph reads and pointwise continuity data.
\item $G$ is the $\HeineBorelFiniteNetUp$ finite-cell row, listing the interval cells, centers, radius ledgers, and coverage reads used by the witness search.
\item $N$ is the finite value-comparison row selecting a displayed cell and center whose readback is no smaller than the other displayed net values at the admitted precision.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule row for endpoint, graph, and value observations.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row for the selected value and comparison witnesses.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row for the selected maximum-witness value after $I,F,G,N,S,R$ have been displayed.

\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $I,F,G,N,S,R,E,W$ and the local packet shape.

The carrier exports no host maximum operator, open-cover compactness theorem, selected subcover, ambient Real completeness principle, quotient equality of real names, or global choice row.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Compact interval maximum finite witness obligations]
\label{thm:compact-interval-maximum-finite-witness-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:compact-interval-maximum-carrier}, every accepted maximum-witness read factors through the closed bounded interval row $I$, the continuous-map graph row $F$, the finite-net cell row $G$, the finite value-comparison row $N$, the $\StreamNameUp/\RegSeqRatUp/\RealUp$ readback route $S,R,E$, the witness-locality row $W$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance $P$, and local naming $L$. These rows are the local $\NameCert_{\CompactIntervalMaximumUp}$ obligation surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier. The interval source, graph, finite-cell, comparison, schedule, readback, seal, witness-locality, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows are the only coordinates of the accepted packet.

The closed bounded interval row $I$ supplies endpoint order and containment ledgers before any cell is inspected. The finite-net row $G$ supplies the displayed cells and centers, while $F$ supplies the graph reads whose values are compared by $N$.

The readback route is downstream of those finite rows. The schedule $S$, regular rational readback $R$, and Real seal $E$ expose the selected witness value only through the displayed observations, and $W$ keeps the selected center inside a certified cell of the interval net.

Finally $H,C,P,L$ transport, replay, package, and name the same finite surface. Since the packet has no coordinate for host extrema, open covers, selected subcovers, ambient completeness, quotient equality, or global choice, every accepted maximum-witness read remains on the displayed NameCert rows.
\end{proof}
