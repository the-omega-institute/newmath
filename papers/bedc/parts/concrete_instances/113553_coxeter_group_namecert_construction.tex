\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CoxeterGroupUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-coxeter-group-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Coxeter's presentation of reflection groups and Bourbaki's treatment of Coxeter systems, the $\CoxeterGroupUp$ packet records a group generated by involutive simple generators with finite braid-relation rows. It sits between the algebraic group surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-group-namecert}, the root-reflection ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rootsystem-namecert}, and the Weyl-group consumer of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-weylgroup-namecert}. The packet names the displayed Coxeter matrix, finite alternating-word ledgers, relation witnesses, word-reduction obligations, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming surface; it does not assert a host quotient group, a complete word-problem solver, or a classification of finite reflection groups.

\begin{definition}[CoxeterGroup carrier]
\label{def:coxeter-group-carrier}
A $\CoxeterGroupUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
C=(S,M,W,R,N,H,Q,P,L)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the finite simple-generator row, with each generator tagged by an involution witness read through the $\GroupUp$ operation surface.
\item $M$ is the Coxeter matrix row. It records $m_{s,s}=1$ and, for distinct displayed generators $s,t$, a finite braid length $m_{s,t}$ or an obstruction row stating that no finite braid row is exposed.
\item $W$ is the finite signed-word carrier generated from $S$ by empty word, singleton generator word, and concatenation.
\item $R$ is the relation ledger. It contains involution rows $ss \leadsto e$ and, whenever $M$ exposes a finite value $m_{s,t}$, the two alternating words of length $m_{s,t}$ with a classified relation witness between them.
\item $N$ is the word-reduction obligation row. It records the local cancellation and braid-step routes that any public normal-form or reduction consumer must read before claiming a reduced endpoint.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $S,M,W,R,N,Q,P,L$.
\item $Q$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay for generator insertion, concatenation, involution cancellation, braid transport, and reduction-step replay.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CoxeterGroupUp$, $\GroupUp$, $\RootSystemUp$, $\WeylGroupUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $L$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CoxeterGroupUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares Coxeter packets only through $S,M,W,R,N,H,Q,P,L$. It does not compare ambient presentations by host equality and does not identify the packet with a Weyl group unless the Weyl consumer supplies the root-action rows separately.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CoxeterGroup word-reduction obligation]
\label{thm:coxeter-group-word-reduction-obligation}
Every public word-reduction read from an accepted $\CoxeterGroupUp$ packet must factor through the displayed relation ledger $R$ and reduction obligation row $N$. A finite generator word in $W$ alone is only a carried word endpoint; it is not a reduced representative, a normal form, or a word-problem decision procedure until the involution, braid, and replay rows named by $R,N,Q$ have been read.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:coxeter-group-carrier}. The row $W$ supplies finite words generated from the simple-generator row $S$, but it records only construction of words by empty word, singleton generator, and concatenation.

Reduction information enters through $R$ and $N$. The relation ledger $R$ gives the involution rows and finite braid rows selected by the Coxeter matrix $M$, while $N$ records the obligation that a consumer must use those rows as the public reduction surface.

The replay row $Q$ then transports each cancellation or braid step through displayed $\Cont$ data, and the transport row $H$ keeps the endpoints classified under $\hsame$. Thus a public reduced-word claim is downstream of $R,N,Q,H$, not a property of the bare word carrier $W$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CoxeterGroup NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:coxeter-group-namecert-obligations}
A $\NameCert_{\CoxeterGroupUp}$ obligation surface consists exactly of the finite simple-generator carrier, the Coxeter matrix row, the word carrier, the involution and braid relation ledger, the word-reduction obligation row, componentwise transport, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming rows. The surface may be consumed by $\WeylGroupUp$ only after the $\RootSystemUp$ reflection and Cartan rows supply the root-action interpretation of the same relation ledger.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the rows $S,M,W,R,N,H,Q,P,L$ from the carrier. These rows provide the whole Coxeter packet: generators, relation parameters, finite word construction, relation witnesses, reduction-facing obligations, classifier transport, replay, provenance, and the local certificate name.

The $\GroupUp$ dependency supplies the operation, unit, inverse, and classifier rows needed to read generator words as group-facing histories. The Coxeter packet itself adds the matrix and braid ledger, but it does not add root actions or a finite-reflection representation.

For a Weyl consumer, the root-action interpretation must be supplied separately through the $\RootSystemUp$ reflection and Cartan ledger rows. Only after those rows are read can \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-weylgroup-namecert} consume the Coxeter relation ledger as a root-action ledger, so the Coxeter certificate remains a distinct dependency surface.
\end{proof}

\falsifiablePrediction{A $\CoxeterGroupUp$ consumer that exports a reduced word or Weyl action from $W$ without reading $R,N,Q$ refutes the carrier surface.}

\independenceWitness{group, rootsystem, weylgroup, braidgroup: the group packet lacks the Coxeter matrix and braid ledger; the root-system packet has reflections and Cartan rows but no word carrier; the Weyl-group packet consumes root-action rows beyond the Coxeter relation carrier; the braid-group packet records Artin braid words without the involution relation $ss \leadsto e$.}
