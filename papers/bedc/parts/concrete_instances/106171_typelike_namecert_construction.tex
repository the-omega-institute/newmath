\label{def:typelike-carrier}
A $\TypeLikeUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$

$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $B$ is the displayed base classifier family row, read as BEDC classifier data rather than as a host universe;
\item $F$ is the fiber-carrier row assigning to each accepted base entry a finite displayed fiber packet;
\item $Q$ is the classifier row for fiber membership, endpoint equality, and family-compatible reads;
\item $E$ is the extensionality and endpoint-exactness ledger, recording which displayed fiber endpoints may be identified under the classifier;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $B,F,Q,E$ together with replay, provenance, and local naming rows;

\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\TypeLikeUp$, $\SetLikeUp$, $\FirstOrderUp$, $\ModelTheoryUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\TypeLikeUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for an ambient type universe, quotient identity, selected representative, host-level equivalence of categories, or a comprehension rule not already displayed by $B,F,Q,E,H,C,P,N$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[TypeLike NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:typelike-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\TypeLikeUp$ carrier
$$

$$
the local $\NameCert_{\TypeLikeUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $T$, base-family classifier admission through $B$, fiber-carrier admission through $F$, classifier exposure through $Q$, endpoint-exactness exposure through $E$, componentwise $\hsame$ stability through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier of \autoref{def:typelike-carrier}. Its base side is the displayed classifier family $B$, while its fiber side is the finite carrier row $F$ indexed by accepted base entries.

The classifier row $Q$ supplies the only family-compatible membership and endpoint reads. The exactness row $E$ records when two displayed endpoints may be identified by that classifier, so endpoint equality is a carried ledger row rather than host type equality.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve and expose the same packet. Since no further coordinate is present, the NameCert obligations cannot ask for an ambient universe, quotient identity, selected representative, categorical equivalence, or external comprehension rule.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[TypeLike classifier transport]
\label{thm:typelike-classifier-transport}
Every downstream consumer of an accepted $\TypeLikeUp$ packet that transports a fiber endpoint must factor through the finite route
$$
B,F,Q,E \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
Consequently the transported endpoint remains inside the displayed classifier family and cannot be replaced by host equality of types, an untracked equivalence of fibers, or a SetLike, FirstOrder, or ModelTheory read that bypasses the carried classifier row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with an accepted carrier $T=(B,F,Q,E,H,C,P,N)$. A consumer can reach a fiber endpoint only after reading the base classifier $B$ and the indexed fiber carrier $F$.

The classifier row $Q$ fixes the admissible endpoint comparison, and $E$ records the exact endpoint ledger for that comparison. Transport therefore has to preserve both the base-family row and the fiber classifier row.

\end{proof}
