\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:hilbert-carrier}

\begin{definition}[Compatible inner-product Banach carrier]
\label{def:hilbert-compatible-inner-product-banach-carrier}
A compatible inner-product Banach carrier for $\HilbertUp$ is a history
predicate $X:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ with point classifier
$\sim_X$ together with three displayed rows over the same carried histories.
The first row is a concrete $\BanachUp$ candidate of
\autoref{def:banach-concrete-carrier}. The second row is an
$\InnerProductUp$ carrier on $X$, including the vector-space structure,
sesquilinear form, conjugate symmetry, linearity, positivity, and
inner-product classifier transport. The third row is a norm-induction ledger
recording that the Banach norm and Banach metric rows are exactly the rows
displayed by the inner-product norm:
$$
\|x\|_{\BanachUp}=\sqrt{\langle x,x\rangle_{\InnerProductUp}}
$$
and
$$
d_{\BanachUp}(x,y)=\|x-y\|_{\BanachUp}
$$
for carried histories $x,y:X$.

The compatibility requirement is literal row sharing, not an isomorphism
postulate: the Banach carrier, the inner-product carrier, and the
norm-induced metric ledger expose the same point carrier $X$ and the same
classifier $\sim_X$. Every accepted Hilbert row therefore carries a visible
Banach surface, a visible inner-product surface, and a ledger explaining why
the Banach metric is the metric induced by the inner-product norm.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Hilbert singleton empty-history carrier]
\label{def:hilbert-singleton-empty-history-carrier}
The concrete $\HilbertUp$ carrier is the singleton empty-history Banach
carrier of \autoref{def:banach-singleton-empty-history-instance}, read over
the singleton empty-history vector space of
\autoref{def:singleton-empty-history-vecspace-instance}. Thus the vector
carrier is $A_{\emp}$, the vector classifier is $\rho_{\emp}$, the normed
carrier is the singleton $\BanachUp$ carrier, and the norm is
$\nu_{\emp}$.

The inner-product row is the constant-zero RealUp endpoint
$$
\langle m,n\rangle_{\emp}:=0_{\mathsf{Real}}^{c}
$$
for all displayed vector histories $m,n:\mathsf{BHist}$. Its scalar-side
classifier is $\mathsf{RealConstantHistoryClassifier}$. The induced norm row
is read by the same constant endpoint:
$$
\|\!m\!\|_{\langle,\rangle_{\emp}}:=0_{\mathsf{Real}}^{c}.
$$
Hence the induced norm is the singleton $\NormUp$ norm $\nu_{\emp}$ from
\autoref{def:singleton-empty-history-norm-instance}. The ledger records the
singleton vector-space source, the Banach singleton carrier, and the RatUp zero
witness sealed as the RealUp constant endpoint.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Hilbert induced norm Banach compatibility]
\label{thm:hilbert-induced-norm-banach-compatibility}
Every accepted compatible inner-product Banach carrier exposes the same point
carrier and classifier to its $\BanachUp$ and $\InnerProductUp$ surfaces.
Moreover, $\hsame$ transport preserves the norm-induced Banach metric witness:
if $x\sim_X x'$ and $y\sim_X y'$, then a displayed
$\mathsf{BanachMetricWitness}_X(x,y,d)$ row transports to the corresponding
$\mathsf{BanachMetricWitness}_X(x',y',d')$ row classified by the same
inner-product norm ledger.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the compatible carrier of
\autoref{def:hilbert-compatible-inner-product-banach-carrier}. The Banach row
and inner-product row have the same endpoint predicate $X$ and classifier
$\sim_X$ by definition, so no change of carrier or classifier is introduced.

Apply \autoref{thm:banach-norm-induced-metric-compatibility} to read the
Banach metric through the displayed norm row. The Hilbert ledger identifies
that norm row with the inner-product norm row, so the metric witness remains
the norm-induced Banach witness after this reading.

Finally use the componentwise $\hsame$ transport rows supplied by the Banach
surface and the inner-product surface. They move the vector representative,
difference row, inner-product norm row, and RealUp distance endpoint together,
and repack the transported rows as
$\mathsf{BanachMetricWitness}_X(x',y',d')$ over the same classifier
$\sim_X$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Hilbert complete inner-product limit stability]
\label{thm:hilbert-complete-inner-product-limit-stability}
Let $H$ be a compatible inner-product Banach carrier. For any regular Cauchy
stream $s$ over the Banach metric, any displayed
$\mathsf{CompleteMetricLimitWitness}(X,s,\mu,\ell,L)$ supplied by the Banach
row, and any proof that the stream observations remain in the inner-product
carrier, there is a Hilbert limit witness with the same selected limit
$\ell$, the same limit name $L$, and the same classifier $\sim_X$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the Banach row contained in $H$. Its completeness field supplies
the displayed $\mathsf{CompleteMetricLimitWitness}(X,s,\mu,\ell,L)$ and its
carrier endpoint data through \autoref{def:banach-concrete-carrier}.

The observation-stability hypothesis says that the same stream is carried by
the inner-product surface of $H$. Use the inner-product carrier stability row
to transport all observation representatives through $\sim_X$ while retaining
the displayed inner-product norm rows named by the Hilbert ledger.

Apply \autoref{thm:hilbert-induced-norm-banach-compatibility} to identify the
transported Banach distance witnesses with the inner-product norm-induced
ones. The selected limit endpoint $\ell$ and limit name $L$ are therefore not
changed; they are merely read as Hilbert rows over the same carrier,
classifier, and visible metric ledger.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Hilbert NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:hilbert-namecert-obligation-surface}
The compatible carrier of
\autoref{def:hilbert-compatible-inner-product-banach-carrier}, its induced
classifier $\sim_X$, the norm-metric compatibility of
\autoref{thm:hilbert-induced-norm-banach-compatibility}, the complete-limit
stability of \autoref{thm:hilbert-complete-inner-product-limit-stability}, and
the visible ledger joining the Banach and inner-product rows assemble the
paper-side $\NameCert_{\HilbertUp}$ obligation surface. This surface records
the Hilbert carrier, classifier, norm-induced Banach metric, complete-limit
stability, and ledger provenance; it does not assert a standard
Hilbert-space bridge.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the compatible carrier definition for the public carrier field. It
already contains the Banach row, the inner-product row, and the row-sharing
ledger binding both surfaces to the same $X$ and $\sim_X$.

Use \autoref{thm:hilbert-induced-norm-banach-compatibility} for the
norm-metric field. It identifies the Banach metric witness as the metric
induced by the inner-product norm and records that $\hsame$ transport
preserves that witness componentwise.

Use \autoref{thm:hilbert-complete-inner-product-limit-stability} for the
limit-stability field. It consumes the Banach
$\mathsf{CompleteMetricLimitWitness}$ row together with the inner-product
observation-stability row and returns the same selected limit as a Hilbert
limit witness.

The remaining fields are inherited from the source rows: the Banach surface
provides the complete normed metric obligations, the inner-product surface
provides the sesquilinear, symmetry, positivity, and classifier-stability
obligations, and the ledger records exactly which displayed rows are shared.
Thus the paper-side $\NameCert_{\HilbertUp}$ obligation surface is assembled
without adding any standard bridge claim.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Hilbert singleton inner-product norm compatibility]
\label{thm:hilbert-singleton-inner-product-norm-compatibility}
For the carrier of
\autoref{def:hilbert-singleton-empty-history-carrier}, the norm induced by
$\langle-,-\rangle_{\emp}$ agrees with the singleton norm $\nu_{\emp}$:
$$
\mathsf{RealConstantHistoryClassifier}
  (\|\!m\!\|_{\langle,\rangle_{\emp}},\nu_{\emp}(m))
$$
for every carried $m:\mathsf{BHist}$. Moreover positive definiteness of the
inner-product row collapses to the singleton vector classifier:
$$
\mathsf{RealConstantHistoryClassifier}
  (\langle m,m\rangle_{\emp},0_{\mathsf{Real}}^{c})
\Longleftrightarrow
\rho_{\emp}(m,\emp).
$$
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.HilbertUp.HilbertSingleton\_inner\_product\_norm\_compatibility}
\begin{proof}
The induced norm and $\nu_{\emp}$ are both the constant endpoint
$0_{\mathsf{Real}}^{c}$. The carrier and classifier rows of
\autoref{thm:singleton-empty-history-norm-carrier-classifier} give the RealUp
constant-history classifier for this endpoint, so the two norm presentations
are classified together.

For positive definiteness in the forward direction, the inner-product value
$\langle m,m\rangle_{\emp}$ is again $0_{\mathsf{Real}}^{c}$. Apply
\autoref{thm:singleton-empty-history-vecspace-endpoint-collapse} to the
carried vector endpoint. It identifies $m$ with $\emp$ under $\rho_{\emp}$.

For the reverse direction, assume $\rho_{\emp}(m,\emp)$. The inner-product row
does not inspect the representative; it returns $0_{\mathsf{Real}}^{c}$ for
every pair. The zero-exactness boundary of
\autoref{thm:singleton-empty-history-norm-zero-exactness}, together with the
constant-real classifier already supplied by the singleton norm carrier,
repackages the row as
$\mathsf{RealConstantHistoryClassifier}
(\langle m,m\rangle_{\emp},0_{\mathsf{Real}}^{c})$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Hilbert singleton complete normed certificate]
\label{thm:hilbert-singleton-complete-normed-certificate}
The normed part of
\autoref{def:hilbert-singleton-empty-history-carrier} is complete. Every
regular Cauchy stream carried by $A_{\emp}$ has selected limit $\emp$, every
late distance-to-limit row is classified with $0_{\mathsf{Real}}^{c}$, and any
two selected Banach limit witnesses are classified by $\rho_{\emp}$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.HilbertUp.HilbertSingleton\_complete\_normed\_certificate}
\begin{proof}
Use the singleton Banach carrier of
\autoref{def:banach-singleton-empty-history-instance}. Its metric is the
constant RealUp zero endpoint and its limit-selection rule sends every
certified regular Cauchy stream to $\emp$.

The complete-metric laws are supplied by
\autoref{thm:singleton-complete-metric-laws}. They give the carried singleton
limit, the empty modulus, and the late-observation ledgers for the constant
zero metric.

Finally apply
\autoref{thm:banach-singleton-limit-namecert-uniqueness} to compare any two
Banach limit witnesses for the same stream. In the singleton vector carrier,
\autoref{thm:singleton-empty-history-vecspace-endpoint-collapse} reads that
comparison as the classifier row $\rho_{\emp}$, so the complete normed
certificate is the Banach singleton certificate read through the Hilbert
carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Hilbert singleton classifier source boundary]
\label{thm:hilbert-singleton-classifier-source-boundary}
The carrier of
\autoref{def:hilbert-singleton-empty-history-carrier} exposes exactly the
singleton vector-space carrier $A_{\emp}$ and classifier $\rho_{\emp}$. The
Banach row, the singleton $\NormUp$ row, the inner-product row, and the
Hilbert row all read their vector endpoints from the same
$\mathsf{BHist}$ source; no carrier endpoint and no vector classifier outside
the singleton empty-history source is used.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.HilbertUp.HilbertSingleton\_classifier\_source\_boundary}
\begin{proof}
Unfold the Hilbert singleton carrier of
\autoref{def:hilbert-singleton-empty-history-carrier}. Its first displayed
source is the singleton vector-space instance of
\autoref{def:singleton-empty-history-vecspace-instance}, so the visible vector
carrier is $A_{\emp}$ and the visible classifier is $\rho_{\emp}$.

The normed row is the singleton norm instance of
\autoref{def:singleton-empty-history-norm-instance}, and the Banach row is the
singleton Banach instance of
\autoref{def:banach-singleton-empty-history-instance}. Both rows are stated
over the same singleton vector carrier and therefore project the same
$A_{\emp}$ and $\rho_{\emp}$ fields.

The Hilbert row adds only the constant inner-product display over those
carried histories. Since that display takes arguments
$m,n:\mathsf{BHist}$ from the already fixed singleton carrier, the Hilbert
surface has the same endpoint source as the vector-space, norm, and Banach
surfaces.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Hilbert singleton constant inner-product transport]
\label{thm:hilbert-singleton-constant-inner-product-transport}
For the carrier of
\autoref{def:hilbert-singleton-empty-history-carrier}, if
$\rho_{\emp}(m,m')$ and $\rho_{\emp}(n,n')$, then the corresponding
inner-product endpoints are related by the constant-real classifier:
$$
\mathsf{RealConstantHistoryClassifier}
  (\langle m,n\rangle_{\emp},\langle m',n'\rangle_{\emp}).
$$
The induced norm endpoints are transported along the same scalar classifier,
so
$$
\mathsf{RealConstantHistoryClassifier}
  (\|\!m\!\|_{\langle,\rangle_{\emp}},\|\!m'\!\|_{\langle,\rangle_{\emp}})
$$
holds over the singleton vector classifier.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.HilbertUp.HilbertSingleton\_constant\_inner\_product\_transport}
\begin{proof}
The inner-product operation in
\autoref{def:hilbert-singleton-empty-history-carrier} is constant: every
ordered pair of carried histories is sent to the endpoint
$0_{\mathsf{Real}}^{c}$. Thus the two endpoints
$\langle m,n\rangle_{\emp}$ and $\langle m',n'\rangle_{\emp}$ are the same
constant RealUp zero endpoint.

The singleton norm carrier supplies the scalar-side classifier
$\mathsf{RealConstantHistoryClassifier}$ for this constant endpoint. Applying
that classifier to the two constant zero presentations gives the displayed
inner-product transport row.

For the induced norm, use
\autoref{thm:hilbert-singleton-inner-product-norm-compatibility}. It reads
the induced norm as the singleton norm $\nu_{\emp}$, whose endpoint is again
the same constant RealUp zero row. The norm transport conclusion is therefore
the same constant-real classifier row read through the induced norm display.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Hilbert singleton ledger exhaustion]
\label{thm:hilbert-singleton-ledger-exhaustion}
Every row consumed by the singleton $\HilbertUp$ package is one of the
singleton vector-space source row, the singleton $\NormUp$ row, the singleton
Banach completion row, or the constant RealUp zero inner-product row. The
package introduces no $\StdBridge$ row, no second vector carrier, and no
carrier source beyond the $\mathsf{BHist}$ singleton ledger recorded in
\autoref{def:hilbert-singleton-empty-history-carrier}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier and classifier accounting is fixed by
\autoref{thm:hilbert-singleton-classifier-source-boundary}. It leaves only
the singleton vector-space source $A_{\emp}$, $\rho_{\emp}$, and the
$\mathsf{BHist}$ endpoint source available to the Hilbert package.

The scalar stability accounting is fixed by
\autoref{thm:hilbert-singleton-constant-inner-product-transport}. It says
that both the inner-product endpoint and the induced norm endpoint are
transported by $\mathsf{RealConstantHistoryClassifier}$ at the constant
RealUp zero row.

The completeness accounting is exactly
\autoref{thm:hilbert-singleton-complete-normed-certificate}, which reads the
singleton Banach completion row through the Hilbert carrier. Together with
the ledger sentence in
\autoref{def:hilbert-singleton-empty-history-carrier}, these rows exhaust the
package: vector source, norm source, Banach completion source, and constant
inner-product zero source are all named, and no standard bridge or outside
carrier source remains.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.HilbertUp.HilbertSingleton\_ledger\_exhaustion}

\begin{theorem}[Hilbert NameCert obligation package]
\label{thm:hilbert-namecert-obligation-package}
The carrier of
\autoref{def:hilbert-singleton-empty-history-carrier}, together with the
classifier-source boundary
\autoref{thm:hilbert-singleton-classifier-source-boundary}, the
constant-inner-product transport row
\autoref{thm:hilbert-singleton-constant-inner-product-transport}, the ledger
exhaustion row \autoref{thm:hilbert-singleton-ledger-exhaustion}, and the
certificate rows
\autoref{thm:hilbert-singleton-inner-product-norm-compatibility} and
\autoref{thm:hilbert-singleton-complete-normed-certificate}, assembles the
paper-side $\NameCert_{\HilbertUp}$ obligation package. Its carrier is
$A_{\emp}$, its classifier is $\rho_{\emp}$, its inner-product row is
$\langle-,-\rangle_{\emp}$, its induced norm is $\nu_{\emp}$, its completeness
field is the singleton Banach limit rule, its ledger is the union of the
singleton vector-space, norm, inner-product zero-row, and Banach-completion
ledgers, and its bridge status is none.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier and classifier fields are supplied by
\autoref{thm:hilbert-singleton-classifier-source-boundary}. The Banach source
is exactly \autoref{def:banach-singleton-empty-history-instance}, so the
normed carrier and metric rows are already present before the Hilbert row is
attached.

The inner-product compatibility field is
\autoref{thm:hilbert-singleton-inner-product-norm-compatibility}. It identifies
the induced norm with $\nu_{\emp}$ and reflects positive definiteness back to
the singleton classifier against $\emp$.

The transport field is
\autoref{thm:hilbert-singleton-constant-inner-product-transport}. It records
that replacing either input by a $\rho_{\emp}$-classified endpoint leaves the
inner-product endpoint and the induced norm endpoint inside the same
$\mathsf{RealConstantHistoryClassifier}$ row.

The completeness field is
\autoref{thm:hilbert-singleton-complete-normed-certificate}. It supplies the
selected limit, the convergence ledgers, and classifier uniqueness for
regular Cauchy streams in the norm induced by the inner product.

\autoref{thm:hilbert-singleton-ledger-exhaustion} supplies the ledger field.
Combining these rows gives the local $\HilbertUp$ naming certificate: the
traditional target is the one-point Hilbert space, the BEDC source is
$\mathsf{BHist}$, the carrier and classifier are $A_{\emp}$ and
$\rho_{\emp}$, the stability rows are constant-zero classifier transport, the
exactness row is positive definiteness against $\emp$, the ledger records the

\end{proof}

inner-product row is classified with the constant RealUp zero endpoint
