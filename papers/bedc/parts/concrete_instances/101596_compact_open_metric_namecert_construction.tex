\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CompactOpenMetricUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-compact-open-metric-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Kelley and Bourbaki on the compact-open topology, and Bishop-style finite nets for compact metric spaces, the $\CompactOpenMetricUp$ packet names the constructive compact-open interface seen by continuous-map families. It records a sup-distance boundary over finite source nets rather than an ambient topology on all functions. The packet sits between the compact source rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, the continuous-map graph and modulus rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuousmap-namecert}, the complete target rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completemetric-namecert}, the finite handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactuniformcontinuity-namecert}, the modulus ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformmodulus-namecert}, and the Real-distance surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-uniform-structure-namecert}.

The chapter isolates the function-family metric boundary consumed by compact-continuity arguments such as \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-arzelaascoli-namecert}. It does not construct an open-cover compactness theorem, does not install a host function-space topology, and does not choose subsequences or limits outside the displayed finite rows.

\begin{definition}[Compact-open metric carrier]
\label{def:compact-open-metric-carrier}
A $\CompactOpenMetricUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(X,Y,F,G,M,D,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the source $\CompactMetricUp$ row, including the compact metric distance surface and finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ net rows used for source inspection.
\item $Y$ is the target $\CompleteMetricUp$ row whose distance witnesses and completion-facing reads receive every sampled value.
\item $F$ is a finite family ledger of $\ContinuousMapUp$ rows from $X$ to $Y$.
\item $G$ records the displayed graph rows of the maps in $F$ on the finite source-net windows.
\item $M$ records $\CompactUniformContinuityUp$ and $\UniformModulusUp$ handoff rows turning pointwise continuity data into uniform finite source windows.
\item $D$ records finite target-distance samples for pairs of rows in $F$ over the source-net windows, read through the target metric and the $\RealUniformStructureUp$ distance boundary.
\item $S$ records the sup-distance comparison ledger obtained by taking the finite maximum of the sampled target-distance rows at each requested window.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for source compact-metric rows, target complete-metric rows, continuous-map rows, graph rows, modulus rows, distance samples, sup-distance ledgers, replay rows, provenance, and local naming.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a compact-open distance request through source-net selection, graph evaluation, target-distance sampling, finite maximum comparison, and modulus handoff.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CompactOpenMetricUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\CompleteMetricUp$, $\CompactUniformContinuityUp$, $\UniformModulusUp$, $\RealUniformStructureUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CompactOpenMetricUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $X,Y,F,G,M,D,S,H,C,P,N$. No carrier coordinate names open-cover compactness, an ambient function-space topology, choice-selected subsequences, or Real completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Compact-open metric NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:compact-open-metric-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CompactOpenMetricUp$ carrier $K=(X,Y,F,G,M,D,S,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CompactOpenMetricUp}$ surface consists exactly of these obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the source compact-metric row, target complete-metric row, continuous-map family ledger, displayed graph rows, finite uniform-modulus rows, target-distance samples, finite sup-distance comparison rows, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming;
\item finite source inspection, requiring every compact-open distance request to choose its source windows from the $\CompactMetricUp$ finite-net coordinate $X$;
\item graph and distance sampling, requiring every sampled value to be read through $G$ and every sampled distance to be read through $Y$ and the $\RealUniformStructureUp$ distance boundary;
\item uniform handoff, requiring $M$ to record the $\CompactUniformContinuityUp$ and $\UniformModulusUp$ route that makes finite source-net samples stable for the requested tolerance;
\item classifier transport and non-escape, requiring $H,C,P,N$ to preserve the same rows and to export no open-cover compactness, ambient function-space topology, choice-selected subsequence, or Real completeness.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored outside those displayed rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:compact-open-metric-carrier}. The packet exposes exactly the source compact-metric row, target complete-metric row, family ledger, graph rows, modulus rows, sampled-distance rows, finite sup-distance ledger, and structural rows.

Finite source inspection is forced by the coordinate $X$. A compact-open distance request first reads the compact-metric finite-net rows and cannot inspect source points through an unrecorded open cover.

Graph and distance sampling are then read from $G$, $Y$, and $D$. The family ledger supplies the named continuous-map graph rows, the target row supplies complete-metric distance witnesses, and the Real uniform-structure boundary names the distance surface used by the finite comparison.

The modulus handoff is the coordinate $M$. It records the compact-uniform-continuity and uniform-modulus rows that make the finite samples stable under the requested tolerance, so the sup-distance ledger is a finite certificate rather than a topology on all functions.

Finally, $H,C,P,N$ transport and replay the same route. Because the carrier has no row for open-cover compactness, an ambient function-space topology, choice-selected subsequences, or Real completeness, the local certificate cannot export any of them.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Compact-open metric uniform-distance handoff]
\label{thm:compact-open-metric-uniform-distance-handoff}
Every compact-continuity consumer reading an accepted $\CompactOpenMetricUp$ carrier obtains a finite uniform-distance handoff through the displayed rows
$$
\CompactMetricUp,\ContinuousMapUp,\CompleteMetricUp,\CompactUniformContinuityUp,\UniformModulusUp,\RealUniformStructureUp,\BHist,\hsame,\Cont,\Pkg,\NameCert .
$$
The handoff supplies source finite nets, continuous-map graph samples, target distance rows, finite sup-distance comparisons, and modulus stability. It supplies no open-cover compactness, ambient function-space topology, choice-selected subsequence, or Real completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a compact-continuity consumer and project the accepted packet $K$. The source coordinate $X$ supplies the finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ windows through the compact-metric row, and the family coordinate $F$ names the continuous-map rows that may be sampled.

Next read the graph coordinate $G$ and target coordinate $Y$. Each sampled value is a displayed graph read, and each target-distance comparison is a displayed complete-metric read whose Real-distance surface is recorded in $D$.

The finite comparison ledger $S$ gathers those target-distance samples into the sup-distance boundary for the requested window. The modulus coordinate $M$ records the compact-uniform-continuity and uniform-modulus route that makes this finite comparison stable for downstream tolerance requests.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve the same finite route under classifier transport and replay. Since no further coordinate is present, the handoff cannot add open-cover compactness, host function-space topology, choice-selected subsequences, or Real completeness.
\end{proof}
