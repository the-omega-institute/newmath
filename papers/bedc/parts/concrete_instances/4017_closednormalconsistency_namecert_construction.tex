\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ClosedNormalConsistencyUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-closednormalconsistency-namecert}
\origin{ai}

The $\ClosedNormalConsistencyUp$ packet realizes the closed-normal node of \autoref{ch:closed-metacic-consistency-assembly} as finite BEDC data. It reads beside the subject-reduction boundary certificates in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacic-subject-reduction-obstruction-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacic-subject-reduction-frontier-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacic-discharge-obligation-ledger-namecert}: those chapters name the transport frontier, while this chapter names the closed-normal route that is already usable once beta-strong normality is part of the displayed input.

\begin{definition}[Closed-normal consistency NameCert carrier]
\label{def:closed-normal-consistency-namecert-carrier}
A $\ClosedNormalConsistencyUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(T,C,B,N,O,P)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $T$ is the empty-context MetaCIC typing row for a proof candidate of the internal $\mathsf{False}$ encoding;
\item $C$ is the closedness row for the same term;
\item $B$ is the $\mathsf{BetaStrong}$ normality row that places the term in the closed-normal fragment;
\item $N$ is the no-closed-normal-false exclusion row, read as the checked normal-form contradiction boundary;
\item $O$ is the obstruction row separating this closed-normal route from an arbitrary closed proof route through subject reduction;
\item $P$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ClosedNormalConsistencyUp}$ row, including provenance over $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, the MetaCIC typing and closedness rows, the beta-strong row, the normal-false exclusion row, and the obstruction row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for closed subject reduction, no proof of the four MetaCIC discharge obligations, and no transport row that turns an arbitrary closed proof of $\mathsf{False}$ into a beta-strong normal proof.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Closed-normal consistency NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:closed-normal-consistency-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\ClosedNormalConsistencyUp}$ surface for \autoref{def:closed-normal-consistency-namecert-carrier} consists of five finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier exposure, requiring every accepted object to display the packet $K=(T,C,B,N,O,P)$;
\item typing-closedness alignment, requiring $T$ and $C$ to name the same closed MetaCIC proof candidate;
\item normality alignment, requiring $B$ to be the beta-strong row for that same candidate;
\item normal-false exclusion readback, requiring $N$ to consume exactly the displayed typing, closedness, and beta-strong rows;
\item obstruction separation, requiring $O$ to keep the packet separate from closed subject reduction, the four discharge proofs, and full closed consistency.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier exposure is projection from the tuple in \autoref{def:closed-normal-consistency-namecert-carrier}. The packet displays the typing, closedness, beta-strong, normal-false exclusion, obstruction, and local naming rows.

Typing-closedness alignment and normality alignment use the shared candidate named by $T,C,B$. A consumer may read the empty-context typing row, the closedness row, and the beta-strong row, but the packet provides no alternate term coordinate.

Normal-false exclusion is the row $N$. It is applied only after the displayed $T,C,B$ rows have been read, so the contradiction boundary is the closed-normal theorem surface rather than a theorem about arbitrary closed proofs.

The obstruction row $O$ records the missing transport route. Since the carrier has no proof of the subject-reduction discharge rows and no closed subject-reduction theorem, the local certificate cannot export full closed consistency.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Closed-normal consistency subject-reduction boundary]
\label{thm:closed-normal-consistency-subject-reduction-boundary}
An accepted $\ClosedNormalConsistencyUp$ carrier closes only the closed-normal route
$$
T,C,B \to N.
$$
It cannot export full closed consistency unless an additional subject-reduction transport row connects arbitrary closed proofs of $\mathsf{False}$ to a beta-strong normal endpoint while preserving the displayed false type.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the carrier through \autoref{def:closed-normal-consistency-namecert-carrier}. The only positive route is from the displayed typing row $T$, closedness row $C$, and beta-strong row $B$ to the normal-false exclusion row $N$.

Apply the obligations of \autoref{thm:closed-normal-consistency-namecert-obligations}. The normal-false exclusion readback is exact for the closed-normal rows, and obstruction separation keeps subject reduction outside the packet.

The sibling boundary chapters identify the missing surface: \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacic-subject-reduction-obstruction-namecert} names the obstruction, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacic-subject-reduction-frontier-namecert} names the four-row frontier, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacic-discharge-obligation-ledger-namecert} names the discharge ledger. Without an additional transport row from that boundary, this certificate remains a closed-normal consistency certificate and not a full closed consistency certificate.
\end{proof}
