\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\HausdorffContentUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-hausdorff-content-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Hausdorff and later constructive metric-measure treatments, the $\HausdorffContentUp$ packet records finite cover content data for metric subsets without importing a completed measure theory. It sits over the metric carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the finite measure-facing ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-measure-namecert}, the retained-prefix surfaces of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cantorset-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cantor-ternary-set-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet names only displayed cover rows, rational radius and exponent budgets, refinement transport, content-bound replay, package provenance, and local NameCert evidence.

\begin{definition}[Hausdorff content carrier]
\label{def:hausdorff-content-carrier}
A $\HausdorffContentUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(M,A,R,S,U,B,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the accepted $\MetricUp$ row whose distance observations are sealed through $\RealUp$.
\item $A$ is the inspected subset or source row, presented only by finite membership probes inherited from metric, Cantor, or ternary Cantor packets.
\item $R$ is the rational radius-budget row, listing finitely many positive rational or dyadic radius bounds.
\item $S$ is the rational exponent-budget row, recording the displayed content exponent and the arithmetic route used for each radius power.
\item $U$ is the finite metric-cover row, pairing each listed cover center and radius with the source probes it covers.
\item $B$ is the content-bound replay row, summing the displayed radius-power bounds into a $\RealUp$-sealed upper content value.
\item $H$ is the componentwise $\hsame$ transport row for the metric source, subset probes, radius budgets, exponent budgets, cover rows, content bounds, provenance, and local name.
\item $C$ is the displayed $\Cont$ replay route for cover refinement, radius-budget reuse, exponent-budget reuse, and content-bound replay.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\HausdorffContentUp$, $\MetricUp$, $\MeasureUp$, $\CantorSetUp$, $\CantorTernarySetUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\HausdorffContentUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier accepts a packet only when $M,A,R,S,U,B,H,C,P,N$ are displayed for the same finite cover request, every cover row uses a listed radius budget, every content-bound replay uses the listed exponent budget, and every metric, measure-facing, Cantor-facing, or real-facing consumer enters through the displayed rows. It has no coordinate for a host infimum over all covers, a choice-selected cover family, quotient real equality, $\sigma$-additive Hausdorff measure, or completed measure theory.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Hausdorff content NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:hausdorff-content-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\HausdorffContentUp$ carrier
$$
Q=(M,A,R,S,U,B,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\HausdorffContentUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission, metric-source admission through $M$, finite source-probe admission through $A$, rational radius-budget admission through $R$, rational exponent-budget admission through $S$, finite cover admission through $U$, content-bound replay through $B$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission projects the packet in \autoref{def:hausdorff-content-carrier}. The only metric data are in $M$, and the only source observations are the finite probes in $A$.

The radius and exponent budgets are displayed before any content value is read. Thus $R$ and $S$ determine the finite arithmetic route for the cover row $U$ and the bound row $B$.

Transport and replay add no analytic coordinate. The rows $H$ and $C$ preserve the same metric, source, budget, cover, and bound rows, while $P$ and $N$ source and name the packet.

Since no carrier coordinate stores a host infimum, selected cover family, quotient real equality, $\sigma$-additive measure, or completed measure theory, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Hausdorff content finite-cover refinement]
\label{thm:hausdorff-content-finite-cover-refinement}
Every refinement-facing read of an accepted $\HausdorffContentUp$ packet factors through the displayed route
$$
M,A,R,S,U,B,H,C,P,N .
$$
If a finer cover is cited, it must reuse or refine the listed radius-budget row $R$, exponent-budget row $S$, finite cover row $U$, and content-bound replay row $B$ through $\hsame$ transport and displayed continuation. The refinement read cannot cite an ambient cover lattice, a host infimum over covers, a choice-selected minimizing cover, or quotient equality of real content values.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the finite cover coordinate. By \autoref{def:hausdorff-content-carrier}, the row $U$ is the only place where cover centers, radii, and covered source probes are displayed.

The budget rows $R$ and $S$ are upstream of $U$ and $B$. A refinement can therefore change a cover only by displaying the reused or refined radius and exponent ledgers before the content-bound replay is read.

Use $H$ and $C$ to transport and replay the refinement route. The provenance row $P$ and local name $N$ keep the refined packet attached to the same metric, measure-facing, Cantor-facing, and real-facing source chapters.

The excluded host cover lattice, infimum, selected minimizing cover, and quotient real equality are not packet coordinates, so no refinement-facing consumer can obtain them from $\HausdorffContentUp$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Hausdorff content Cantor cover handoff]
\label{thm:hausdorff-content-cantor-cover-handoff}
Every $\CantorSetUp$ or $\CantorTernarySetUp$ consumer citing $\HausdorffContentUp$ receives only a finite cover-content handoff through
$$
A \longmapsto R,S \longmapsto U \longmapsto B
$$
together with $M,H,C,P,N$. The handoff can record finite retained-prefix probes, ternary-window probes, rational radius and exponent budgets, finite cover rows, and replayed content bounds. It does not supply $\sigma$-additive Hausdorff measure, a host infimum, a choice-selected cover, quotient real equality, or completed measure theory.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the Cantor-facing source through $A$. The source row admits only finite retained-prefix or ternary-window probes inherited from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cantorset-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cantor-ternary-set-namecert}.

Next read the budget and cover rows. The rational radius and exponent budgets $R,S$ are displayed before the finite cover row $U$, and the content-bound replay row $B$ is downstream of that cover.

Finally attach the metric and structural rows. The metric source $M$ supplies the distance observations, while $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite handoff.

The measure-facing provenance does not create a completed measure. Because $\sigma$-additivity, host infima, choice-selected covers, quotient real equality, and completed measure theory are absent from the carrier, the Cantor consumer receives only the finite cover-content route.
\end{proof}
