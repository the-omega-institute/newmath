\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\DedekindUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-dedekind-namecert}

The Dedekind-domain interface $\DedekindUp$ packages an integrally closed Noetherian commutative domain in which every nonzero prime ideal is maximal. It depends on $\NameCert_{\CommRingUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-commring-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\IdealUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-ideal-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:dedekind-carrier}

\begin{theorem}[Dedekind source obligation]\label{thm:dedekind-source-obligation}
A $\DedekindUp$ source consists of a shared $\mathsf{BHist}$ carrier and classifier equipped with the cited $\NameCert_{\CommRingUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\IdealUp}$ surfaces. The commutative-ring certificate fixes the visible ring package, and the ideal certificate is read over the same carrier and classifier rather than over a second ambient presentation.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the seed boundary for $\DedekindUp$: the seed already names the $\CommRingUp$ source and the $\IdealUp$ source as required inputs. The roadmap ten-field template then asks first for a carrier and classifier surface before any domain law can be read.

Those two inputs must therefore share the same $\mathsf{BHist}$ carrier and the same classifier transport. The proof records only this interface shape: it does not assert that the Dedekind certificate fields have been constructed.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Dedekind domain-law obligation]\label{thm:dedekind-domain-law-obligation}
For the shared $\DedekindUp$ classifier, the domain-specific rows are integrally closedness, Noetherianity, and the assertion that every nonzero prime ideal is maximal. Each row is a certificate field over the shared classifier, with its own witness and stability requirement.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The seed closure lists the integrally closed row, the Noetherian row, and the nonzero-prime-maximal row as the precise domain obligations. The roadmap ten-field template turns each listed row into visible data: a field, a witness, and classifier stability.

Since the carrier and ideal classifier have already been fixed by the source obligation, the three domain laws cannot be supplied as detached host properties. They must be read as certificate rows over that same BEDC classifier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Dedekind classifier ledger obligation]\label{thm:dedekind-classifier-ledger-obligation}
Classifier transport and the $\DedekindUp$ ledger expose exactly the commutative-ring source, the ideal source, the integrally closed witness, the Noetherian witness, and the nonzero-prime-maximal witness. The ledger exposes no quotient data, no classical-choice data, and no $\StdBridge$ data.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the exact-ledger boundary in the seed closure. Its upgrade path names the commutative-ring source, ideal source, three domain-law witnesses, classifier transport, and ledger boundary as the full local inventory.

The roadmap ten-field template then separates local certificate data from quotient, classical, and standard-bridge comparisons. The theorem therefore fixes the exposed ledger shape without claiming a completed $\NameCert_{\DedekindUp}$.
\end{proof}
