\begin{definition}[CauchyErrorCertificate carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-error-certificate-carrier}

$$

\item $R$ is the readback route through $\RegSeqRatUp$ and the downstream $\RealUp$ seal used by the consumer request;
\item $M$ is the unary precision modulus row selecting the finite index at which the requested dyadic tolerance is read;
\item $T$ is the $\DyadicTailBoundUp$ or $\DiagonalModulusUp$ row that records the selected tail estimate and its diagonal admission packet;

\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyErrorCertificateUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The local classifier compares two carriers only by componentwise $\hsame$ transport of $R$, $M$, $T$, $E$, $P$, and $N$, together with the displayed $\Cont$ routes carried by the readback and budget rows.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyErrorCertificate modulus soundness]
\label{thm:cauchy-error-certificate-modulus-soundness}
For every accepted $\CauchyErrorCertificateUp$ carrier $C=(R,M,T,E,P,N)$ and every finite dyadic tolerance request, the selected unary precision row $M$ determines a finite tail or diagonal read in $T$, and the resulting budget entry in $E$ bounds the downstream $\RegSeqRatUp$ and $\RealUp$ readback route $R$ at that tolerance. The bound is a displayed dyadic ledger row, not a host real inequality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the requested tolerance through the unary modulus coordinate $M$. This chooses the finite index at which the packet is allowed to inspect the regular rational route.

Use $T$ to admit the corresponding dyadic tail or diagonal row. The row records exactly the tail estimate needed by the selected precision and passes it to the finite budget ledger.

Finally read $E$ before consuming $R$. Since $E$ is a $\FiniteErrorBudgetUp$ ledger, the downstream bound is the displayed dyadic accounting row over the chosen tolerance and not an external inequality in a host real structure.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyErrorCertificate non-escape boundary]
\label{thm:cauchy-error-certificate-non-escape-boundary}
An accepted $\CauchyErrorCertificateUp$ carrier exports only its displayed readback route, unary precision modulus, dyadic tail or diagonal packet, finite error-budget ledger, package provenance, local naming row, and the attached $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, and $\Cont$ transport. It exports no quotient stream equality, no selected host limit, no ambient completeness theorem, and no host real equality assertion.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier coordinates. Each visible field is one of $R$, $M$, $T$, $E$, $P$, or $N$, together with the ambient finite history and transport rows needed to compare them.

The modulus and budget rows control only finite dyadic requests. The tail or diagonal packet supplies the finite estimate consumed by that budget, and the readback route exposes only the named regular rational and real seal rows.

No coordinate contains a quotient of streams, a selected limit, an ambient complete space, or an equality proof for host real numbers. Therefore no such object can be exported by the certificate.
\end{proof}
