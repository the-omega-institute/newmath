\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\DirectedFilterUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-directed-filter-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Kelley and Bourbaki on directed sets, filters, and completion by tails, the $\DirectedFilterUp$ packet records the finite carrier that translates directed tail requests into a filter-base handoff before any completion consumer reads a limit-facing surface. It sits between the directed-set substrate of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-directedset-namecert}, the filter-base certificate of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-filterbase-namecert}, the Moore--Smith net route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-moore-smith-net-namecert}, and the filter-limit basis route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-filterlimitbasis-namecert}. The packet supplies the L10 completion corridor with a shared directed-filter carrier; it is not a quotient filter, selected limit, countable-choice selector, host equality of filters, or ambient completeness theorem.

\begin{definition}[DirectedFilter carrier]\label{def:directed-filter-carrier}
A $\DirectedFilterUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
F=(D,B,M,U,W,R,Y,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the $\DirectedSetUp$ tail-request row, recording finite requests, preorder comparisons, and common-upper witnesses.
\item $B$ is the $\FilterBaseUp$ handoff row assembled only from the displayed tails in $D$.
\item $M$ is the $\MooreSmithNetUp$ and $\NetConvergenceUp$ route row, exposing the same directed tails as net-index evidence before any convergence read.
\item $U$ is the $\UniformCompletionUp$ and $\MetricCompletionUp$ consumer row, available only after $D,B,M$ have been replayed.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row inspected by completion-facing numerical consumers.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row attached to the windows in $W$.
\item $Y$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger used to compare the readback rows before a seal is consumed.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row, downstream of directed tails, filter-base handoff, net convergence, completion consumers, windows, readback, and tolerance rows.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for all analytic rows and the local structural rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a directed-tail request through $D,B,M,U,W,R,Y,E,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\DirectedFilterUp$, $\DirectedSetUp$, $\FilterBaseUp$, $\MooreSmithNetUp$, $\NetConvergenceUp$, $\UniformCompletionUp$, $\MetricCompletionUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DirectedFilterUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier compares accepted packets only through these displayed rows. It has no coordinate for quotient filters, ultrafilters, selected limits, countable choice, host filter equality, or ambient completion.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[DirectedFilter NameCert obligations]\label{thm:directed-filter-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\DirectedFilterUp$ carrier $F=(D,B,M,U,W,R,Y,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\DirectedFilterUp}$ surface consists exactly of the finite obligations for carrier admission, directed-tail coverage, filter-base handoff, net-convergence routing, completion nonescape, numerical readback, componentwise transport, displayed replay, provenance, and local naming. Every obligation is read from $D,B,M,U,W,R,Y,E,H,C,P,N$, and no obligation is stored in a quotient filter, selected limit, countable-choice schedule, host equality relation, or ambient completeness witness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:directed-filter-carrier}. The displayed analytic rows are $D,B,M,U,W,R,Y,E$, and the structural rows are $H,C,P,N$.

Directed-tail coverage is supplied by $D$. Any request first reads the finite tail window and its common-upper witness before a filter or net consumer can inspect the packet.

The handoff and consumer rows are downstream. The row $B$ exposes the filter-base surface named in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-filterbase-namecert}, while $M$ routes the same tails to the Moore--Smith and net-convergence surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-moore-smith-net-namecert}. The row $U$ then presents only metric or uniform completion consumers of that same route.

Finally $W,R,Y,E$ provide the numerical window, readback, dyadic tolerance, and real seal rows. Transport, replay, provenance, and naming preserve the same finite carrier. Since none of the excluded principles has a carrier coordinate, the listed obligations exhaust the local surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DirectedFilter tail FilterBase handoff]\label{thm:directed-filter-tail-filterbase-handoff}
Every $\FilterBaseUp$ consumer of an accepted $\DirectedFilterUp$ carrier must first read the $\DirectedSetUp$ tail-request row $D$ and only then the handoff row $B$. The handoff presents finite tails, common-upper witnesses, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local $\NameCert$ row; it does not present a quotient filter, ultrafilter, selected limit, countable-choice selector, or host filter equality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:directed-filter-namecert-obligations}. They place every filter-facing read after the displayed directed-tail row $D$.

Read $B$ next. Because $B$ is assembled only from the finite tails and upper-window witnesses in $D$, the filter-base consumer receives the same displayed family rather than an external filter object.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name that handoff. No omitted quotient, ultrafilter, selection, or host-equality row can be recovered from the carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DirectedFilter net-convergence route]\label{thm:directed-filter-net-convergence-route}
Every $\MooreSmithNetUp$ or $\NetConvergenceUp$ consumer of an accepted $\DirectedFilterUp$ packet factors through
$$
\DirectedSetUp \longmapsto \FilterBaseUp \longmapsto \MooreSmithNetUp,\NetConvergenceUp \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
The route shares the directed-tail ledger with \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-moore-smith-net-namecert} and the filter-base handoff with \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-filterbase-namecert}. It exports no global confluence of filters and nets, no selected subnet, no selected limit, and no ambient convergence theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project $F$ to $D,B,M,H,C,P,N$. The row $D$ is the only directed-index source, and $B$ is the only filter-base handoff.

The net row $M$ is reached after those two rows. It therefore reads the same directed tails as the filter-base consumer, matching the Moore--Smith carrier rather than a separately chosen subnet or a detached convergence assertion.

Transport and replay keep the route componentwise. Since the packet contains no selected subnet, selected limit, or ambient convergence coordinate, every net-convergence read is exhausted by the displayed rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DirectedFilter completion nonescape]\label{thm:directed-filter-completion-nonescape}
Every $\UniformCompletionUp$, $\MetricCompletionUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, or $\RealUp$ consumer of an accepted $\DirectedFilterUp$ packet reads the completion corridor through
$$
D,B,M,U \longmapsto W,R,Y,E \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
The route passes through the filter-limit basis corridor of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-filterlimitbasis-namecert} without adding quotient filters, selected limits, countable choice, host real equality, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use \autoref{thm:directed-filter-tail-filterbase-handoff} to force the directed-tail and filter-base prefix. Then use \autoref{thm:directed-filter-net-convergence-route} to place the net-convergence row before any completion-facing consumer.

The completion row $U$ is followed by the numerical rows $W,R,Y,E$. These rows expose finite stream windows, regular rational readback, dyadic tolerance accounting, and the terminal real seal in the order displayed by the carrier.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve and name the same route. Because the carrier has no quotient-filter, selected-limit, countable-choice, host-equality, or ambient-completeness coordinate, the completion consumer cannot escape the stated corridor.
\end{proof}
