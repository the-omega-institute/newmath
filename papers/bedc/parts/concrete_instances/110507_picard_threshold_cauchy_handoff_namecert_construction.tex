\begin{theorem}[Picard threshold Cauchy handoff NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:picard-threshold-cauchy-handoff-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\PicardThresholdCauchyHandoffUp$ carrier $H=(B,I,E,M,R,K,S,T,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\PicardThresholdCauchyHandoffUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the displayed rows; Banach-modulus source visibility through $B$; Picard-iteration visibility through $I$; error-estimate visibility through $E$; Cauchy-threshold construction through $M$; regular-rational readback through $R$; complete-metric service access through $K$; real seal access through $S$; classifier stability through $T$ and $C$; provenance through $P$; and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:picard-threshold-cauchy-handoff-carrier}. The accepted packet exposes exactly the Banach-modulus source row, the Picard-iteration ledger, the error-estimate row, the Cauchy-threshold row, the regular-rational readback, the complete-metric service, the real seal, and the structural rows.

The threshold rows are ordered. The rows $B,I,E$ must be displayed before $M$ can answer a tolerance request, since $M$ is the only coordinate that records the finite Cauchy threshold.

The endpoint rows are downstream. The row $R$ reads the threshold as regular rational data, $K$ is the visible complete-metric service row, and $S$ is exposed only after those rows have been displayed. Transport, replay, provenance, and local naming are exhausted by $T,C,P,N$, so no further NameCert obligation is present.
\end{proof}
