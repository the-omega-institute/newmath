\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\EquicontinuousCompletionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-equicontinuous-completion-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges constructive analysis and the Bourbaki--Kelley
language of uniform completion, the $\EquicontinuousCompletionUp$ packet
records the finite route by which an equicontinuous family remains readable
after completion handoff. It sits after the finite shared-modulus surface of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-equicontinuityfamily-namecert}, the Cauchy
filter and directed-net completion boundary of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformcompletion-namecert}, the complete
metric row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completemetric-namecert}, and
the Cauchy-sequence carrier of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchysequencespace-namecert}. Its L10
readback is routed through the finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational
handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the
terminal $\RealUp$ seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}.
The packet is a finite NameCert surface for equicontinuous completion data;
it is not Arzela--Ascoli compactness, selected limits, quotient completion,
host function extensionality, countable choice, or ambient completeness.

\begin{definition}[EquicontinuousCompletion carrier]
\label{def:equicontinuous-completion-carrier}
An $\EquicontinuousCompletionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(E,U,M,S,W,R,A,T,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $E$ is the $\EquicontinuityFamilyUp$ row displaying the shared source
and target metrics, finite family ledger, finite probe centers, rational
tolerance histories, and common modulus rows.
\item $U$ is the $\UniformCompletionUp$ row through which completion-facing
consumers read Cauchy-filter, directed-net, universal-extension, and
completion-classifier data.
\item $M$ is the $\CompleteMetricUp$ row supplying the finite completeness
boundary used by the completion handoff, without adding a selected limit.
\item $S$ is the $\CauchySequenceSpaceUp$ row recording the sequence-side
Cauchy names consumed by the same completion request.
\item $W$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window family opened for every
displayed source, target, tolerance, modulus, and completion read.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row obtained from $W$ and used for
all rational precision comparisons exposed by the packet.
\item $A$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after
$E,U,M,S,W,R$ have been displayed.
\item $T$ is the route ledger from shared equicontinuity data through
completion handoff and then through finite StreamName--RegSeqRat--Real
readback.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for all displayed rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from each completion-facing family
request through $E,U,M,S,W,R,A,T,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\EquicontinuityFamilyUp$,
$\UniformCompletionUp$, $\CompleteMetricUp$, $\CauchySequenceSpaceUp$,
$\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$,
and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\EquicontinuousCompletionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only through
$E,U,M,S,W,R,A,T,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for open-cover compactness,
function-space compactness, selected completed functions, quotient equality
of completion representatives, host function extensionality, countable
choice, or ambient completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[EquicontinuousCompletion NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:equicontinuous-completion-namecert-obligation-surface}
For every accepted $\EquicontinuousCompletionUp$ carrier
$$
Q=(E,U,M,S,W,R,A,T,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\EquicontinuousCompletionUp}$ surface consists exactly
of five finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier exposure, requiring the displayed packet
$E,U,M,S,W,R,A,T,H,C,P,N$ to be the whole public carrier;
\item common-modulus classifier stability, requiring carrier comparison to
preserve the shared $\EquicontinuityFamilyUp$ source, target, finite family,
probe-center, rational-tolerance, and common-modulus rows componentwise;
\item finite-window exactness, requiring every source, target, tolerance,
modulus, Cauchy-sequence, completion, and real-seal read to occur through the
stored $\StreamNameUp$ window family $W$ and the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row
$R$;
\item completion handoff, requiring completion-facing consumers to pass from
the equicontinuity row $E$ through the displayed $\UniformCompletionUp$,
$\CompleteMetricUp$, and $\CauchySequenceSpaceUp$ rows $U,M,S$ before the
terminal $\RealUp$ seal $A$ is exported;
\item ledger non-escape, requiring $T,H,C,P,N$ to transport, replay, source,
and name only the displayed rows and never an Arzela--Ascoli compactness
principle, selected limit, quotient completion, host function equality,
countable-choice schedule, or ambient completeness theorem.
\end{enumerate}
No EquicontinuousCompletion obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier exposure is projection from
\autoref{def:equicontinuous-completion-carrier}. The accepted packet displays
the equicontinuity-family row, uniform-completion row, complete-metric row,
Cauchy-sequence row, finite stream windows, regular rational readback,
terminal Real seal, route ledger, transport, replay, provenance, and local
name row.

Common-modulus classifier stability is read from $E$ and $H$. The source and
target metrics, finite family ledger, finite probe centers, rational
tolerance histories, and common modulus rows are compared componentwise, so a
completion-facing comparison cannot replace the shared modulus surface by a
chosen external modulus.

Finite-window exactness is read from $W$ and $R$. Every inspected family
value, tolerance request, radius response, Cauchy-sequence observation,
completion witness, and Real seal comparison is routed through the displayed
window family and regular rational readback before any terminal seal is
available.

The completion handoff is the ordered route through $U,M,S,A$. A consumer
first sees the equicontinuity-family row $E$, then the uniform-completion,
complete-metric, and Cauchy-sequence rows that mediate completion data, and
only then the terminal $\RealUp$ seal.

Finally $T,H,C,P,N$ preserve this same route under transport, replay,
provenance, and naming. Because the carrier has no coordinates for
Arzela--Ascoli compactness, selected limits, quotient completion, host
function equality, countable choice, or ambient completeness, the listed rows
exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}
