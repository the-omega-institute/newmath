
\chapter{Proof Standing Summary}

The v1.3 increment turns the finite-kernel live edge from a project-management target into a proof-standing target. The current finite kernel now has four layers of support:
\begin{enumerate}
\item closed generation and no-confusion principles;
\item internal sameness proof blocks;
\item relational extension and continuation proof blocks;
\item signature, package, and gap proof blocks.
\end{enumerate}

The active mathematical claim is deliberately modest:
\[
\boxed{\text{The finite kernel has a coherent proof spine ready for external checking.}}
\]
It is not yet a claim that the proof spine has been machine checked, nor that large derived interfaces have been reconstructed.

\begin{corollary}[Current proof frontier]
The next mathematical frontier is not \(\RealUp\), \(\CircleUp\), or \(\FoldUp\). It is the discharge of the finite-kernel proof obligations from \(\MSame\) through \(\CompGap\), followed by certificate instances for \(\NatUp\) and \(\AddUp\).
\end{corollary}

\begin{remark}[Frozen core policy]
The frozen core remains the baseline. The v1.3 modules do not rewrite it; they provide proof-standing refinements and theorem blocks that future implementations should target.
\end{remark}
