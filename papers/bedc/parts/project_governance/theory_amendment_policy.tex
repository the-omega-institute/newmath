\chapter{Theory Amendment Policy}
\label{ch:theory-amendment-policy}

This paper presents the BEDC theory. When the theory is extended or refined, amendments are made in place: new definitions, theorems, and policies are inserted in their thematic chapter, and replaced phrasings are removed at the time of replacement.

New material belongs in the chapter whose subject it advances. Concrete-instance refinements stay with concrete instances or concrete hardening; proof interfaces and export contracts stay with proof obligations; formalization-facing guidance stays with the implementation-facing contract. The companion file \texttt{HOW\_INCREMENT\_WORKS.md} records the repository recipe for registering claims, aligning Lean targets, and updating status tables.

When an amendment changes the active reading of an existing object, the active label remains the citation target for that object. Theorem- and definition-level labels remain attached to the active formulation.

Deprecation is handled by replacement rather than archival prose. If a definition, theorem phrasing, or policy interface is replaced, the replaced wording is removed from the paper and the active wording takes its place in the relevant chapter. External references to deprecated names should therefore resolve through the active definition's label.

\section{Self-Compilation Surface Governance}\label{sec:self-compilation-governance}

This section codifies which strata of the self-compilation surface BEDC formally claims and which strata BEDC explicitly does not claim. The structural description of the surface is in \autoref{ch:self-compilation}; this section records the corresponding governance rules.

\subsection{Claimed Strata}\label{subsec:self-comp-claimed}

BEDC formally claims:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{L1: Static paper-Lean alignment.} Every \texttt{\textbackslash leanchecked\{X\}} marker in the paper corresponds to an actually existing Lean target $X$ in \texttt{lean4/BEDC/}. This is enforced by \texttt{bedc\_ci.py audit} as a CI gate.
  \item \textbf{L4-mid: Internal manifest.} A finite, hand-curated subset of paper-anchored theorems are registered in \texttt{BEDC.Manifest} as \texttt{(prop, proof)} pairs. The manifest's wellformedness is the Lean kernel's type-check of the list.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Explicitly Not Claimed}\label{subsec:self-comp-not-claimed}

BEDC explicitly does not claim:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{L4a: Internal axiom-free completeness theorem.} A statement of the form ``every paper claim has an axiom-free closed term'' inside BEDC is blocked by Tarski undefinability and Gödel's second incompleteness theorem. BEDC sits inside the host CIC and cannot prove Con(CIC) internally. The verification axis is reported externally by \texttt{bedc\_ci.py axiom-purity}, not as a Lean theorem.
  \item \textbf{L4c: Theory-axis $\leftrightarrow$ verification-axis bijection.} The two-axis closure tracking, theory closure level versus Lean verification status, is deliberately kept independent. Paper closure does not require Lean verification, and Lean verification does not produce paper closure. A fixed point between the axes is rejected by this policy; the \texttt{axis-confusion} CI gate enforces this.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Open Strata}\label{subsec:self-comp-open}

BEDC neither claims nor rejects:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{V2 reconstruction.} Subject reduction, confluence, and other metatheoretic results about the MetaCIC reconstruction are in scope as ordinary theory work without governance commitment to completion.
  \item \textbf{L4b: Internalized audit.} Moving the external Python audits to Lean elab-time commands is operationally desirable but does not change the theoretical surface; it is treated as engineering refactor.
  \item \textbf{Reflection bridge.} A bridge between \texttt{BEDC.MetaCIC.HasType} and host Lean's kernel \texttt{\#check} is research-stage and not on a delivery commitment.
\end{itemize}

\section{Pure-CIC Tactic Discipline}\label{sec:pure-cic-tactics}

BEDC proofs are constrained to a subset of Lean tactics chosen to preserve
the axiom-purity invariant. This section records which tactics are admitted,
which are forbidden, and why.

\subsection{Admitted Tactics}\label{subsec:admitted-tactics}

The following tactics are admitted in BEDC proofs:

\begin{itemize}
  \item Structural elimination: \texttt{match}, \texttt{cases}, \texttt{induction}
  \item Direct construction: \texttt{exact}, \texttt{apply}, \texttt{refine}
  \item Reduction: \texttt{rfl}, \texttt{unfold}, \texttt{show}
  \item Hypothesis manipulation: \texttt{intro}, \texttt{intros}, \texttt{rename\_i}
  \item Discharging absurd cases: \texttt{contradiction}, \texttt{Bool.noConfusion}
  \item Composition: \texttt{constructor}
\end{itemize}

These tactics either reduce to pure CIC term construction or to structural
operations on inductive types. Their proof outputs are checkable by
\texttt{\#print axioms} as ``does not depend on any axioms''.

\subsection{Forbidden Tactics}\label{subsec:forbidden-tactics}

The following tactics are forbidden in BEDC proofs:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \texttt{simp}, \texttt{simp\_all}, \texttt{simp [...]}, \texttt{simp [...] at h}
  \item \texttt{decide}
  \item \texttt{omega}
  \item Tactics that invoke stdlib decidability lemmas with \texttt{propext}
    dependencies in their transitive closure
\end{itemize}

These tactics silently pull in axioms from Lean stdlib, most commonly
\texttt{propext}, sometimes \texttt{Classical.choice} or \texttt{Quot.sound},
through automated simplification or decidability rewrites. A proof
that uses \texttt{simp [foo] at h} typically inherits all axioms touched
by the simp lemma's transitive provenance, even when those axioms are
irrelevant to the immediate term.

\subsection{Audit Enforcement}\label{subsec:audit-enforcement}

The discipline is enforced by \texttt{bedc\_ci.py axiom-purity --strict}.
This audit invokes \texttt{\#print axioms} on every theorem in
\texttt{lean4/BEDC/} and rejects any theorem whose transitive axiom set
contains \texttt{Classical.choice}, \texttt{Quot.sound}, or
\texttt{propext}. The invariant is reported as
$$
\mathrm{theorems} = \mathrm{pure} \wedge \mathrm{impure} = 0.
$$
A theorem that violates the discipline fails this audit and is rejected
at PR merge time.

\subsection{Historical Origin}\label{subsec:tactic-origin}

This rule originates in the 2026-05-11 PR \#64 incident: a
\texttt{simp}-based soundness proof for a checker theorem in
\texttt{BEDC.MetaCIC.Checker} appears as \texttt{propext}-dependent under
\texttt{axiom-purity --strict}. The accepted proof shape for that theorem
uses structural elimination on the underlying inductive type and satisfies
\texttt{\#print axioms ... does not depend on any axioms}. The incident
establishes that BEDC's axiom-purity invariant constrains not only declared
axioms but also tactic choices, and the tactic discipline above codifies
that constraint.

\subsection{Position}\label{subsec:tactic-position}

The discipline is not a stylistic preference. It is a load-bearing
condition for the project's stated goal of pure CIC formalization.
Proofs that violate the discipline are not BEDC proofs even when they
type-check, because they introduce theoretical commitments BEDC does
not endorse.
