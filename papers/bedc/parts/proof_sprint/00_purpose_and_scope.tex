\section{Purpose and scope}

This text is a proof-sprint refinement of the finite kernel.  Its primitive target excludes completion, quotient, topological, and fold constructions.  Real-like and circle-like names may still appear as typed certificate-obligation schemas governed by $\NameCert$, not as completed traditional objects.

The finite kernel target is:
\[
\Mark\to\Hist\to\MSame,\HSame\to\Ext,\Cont\to\Ask\to\Sig\to\SameSig\to\Pkg\to\Gap\to\NameCert.
\]

The theory-level constraints are:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
\item raw marks carry no numeric interpretation;
\item extension, continuation, asking, signature generation, and packaging are treated relationally;
\item internal sameness is not host equality;
\item every compression must produce a gap ledger;
\item every derived interface must be licensed by a naming certificate.
\end{enumerate}

\begin{principle}[Finite-kernel freezing]
The first proof target isolates the finite kernel before completion-like or geometric constructions.  A derived interface may be mentioned only as a typed obligation schema with an explicit naming certificate, not as a completed traditional object.
\end{principle}
