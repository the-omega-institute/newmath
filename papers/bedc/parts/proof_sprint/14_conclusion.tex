\section{Conclusion}

The finite-kernel proof target identifies the first theorem set to be checked. The main goal is no longer to expand toward more traditional mathematics, but to verify that the finite kernel is internally coherent: marks have internal sameness, histories have internal sameness, extension and continuation are relational but deterministic up to internal sameness, signatures are generated by event-style asking, packages classify signatures, gaps cover and separate sources, composite gaps preserve coverage, and derived names require certificates.

The finite kernel may be summarized as:
\[
\boxed{
\bzero,\bone
\to \Hist
\to \MSame,\HSame
\to \Ext,\Cont
\to \Ask
\to \Sig
\to \SameSig
\to \Pkg
\to \Gap
\to \NameCert.
}
\]
Beyond this finite target, completion-like, fold-like, geometric, and algebraic constructions are governed by their own certificate schemas. Real-like and circle-like names inside the manuscript are obligation schemas, not primitive finite-kernel exports.
