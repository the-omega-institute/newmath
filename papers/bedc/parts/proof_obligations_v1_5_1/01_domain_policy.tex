\chapter{Domain Policy and Transport Obligations}
\label{ch:v151-domain-policy}

The v1.5 layer replaced the external-looking notation \(h\in D\) by the internal domain judgment \(\InDom(D,h)\). This chapter freezes the part that is purely notational and records the additional proof obligations needed for domain behavior.

\begin{definition}[Domain policy]
A source-domain name \(D\) is equipped with a domain policy, written
\[
  \DomainPolicy(D),
\]
when the following BEDC-level behavior is supplied.
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Admission judgment.} Source membership is expressed only by \(\InDom(D,h)\).
  \item \textbf{History transport.} If \(\InDom(D,h)\) and \(\hsame(h,k)\), then \(\InDom(D,k)\).
  \item \textbf{No set-membership import.} No theorem in the active concrete layer may use the notation \(h\in D\) as an internal premise.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Domain transport]
\label{thm:v151-domain-transport}
Assume \(\DomainPolicy(D)\). If
\[
  \InDom(D,h)
  \quad\text{and}\quad
  \hsame(h,k),
\]
then
\[
  \InDom(D,k).
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
This is the transport field of \(\DomainPolicy(D)\). The theorem is recorded separately because later gap arguments must cite it explicitly rather than silently treating \(D\) as a set.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Domain invariance of concrete source admission]
Under \(\DomainPolicy(D)\), the source side of the concrete signature gap is invariant under internal history-sameness.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Immediate from Theorem~\ref{thm:v151-domain-transport}. The other parts of a concrete gap, namely signature generation and token introduction, require their own policies and are not bundled into domain transport.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Frozen information]
It is now frozen that active concrete gap statements must use \(\InDom(D,h)\), not \(h\in D\). What is not automatically frozen is any claim that an arbitrary domain name satisfies \(\DomainPolicy(D)\). That must be supplied for each concrete domain.
\end{remark}
