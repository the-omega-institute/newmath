\chapter*{Summary}
\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{Summary}
This manuscript gives a relation-based finite-kernel target for the Binary Emission Discovery Calculus. It begins with two raw marks and generated histories; defines internal mark- and history-sameness; relationizes extension and continuation; treats probes as asking events; generates bundles and signatures internally; packages signatures under policies; records gaps; composes ledgers; and licenses derived interfaces only through typed naming certificates. Its established scope is finite emission, probing, packaging, and ledger composition. Completion, real-like interfaces, circle-like interfaces, and fold-like interfaces are reserved for later modules.
