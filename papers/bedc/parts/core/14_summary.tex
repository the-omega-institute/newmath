\chapter*{Summary}
\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{Summary}
This manuscript gives a relation-based finite-kernel target for the Binary Emission Discovery Calculus. It begins with two raw marks and generated histories; defines internal mark- and history-sameness; relationizes extension and continuation; treats probes as asking events; generates bundles and signatures internally; packages signatures under policies; records gaps; composes ledgers; and licenses derived interfaces only through typed naming certificates. Its primitive scope is finite emission, probing, packaging, and ledger composition. Completion and fold constructions remain outside that primitive chain, while real-like and circle-like appearances are typed naming-certificate obligation schemas, not quotient, completion, or topological primitives.
