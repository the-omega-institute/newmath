\section*{Layer warning}

This manuscript intentionally separates four layers:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Core theory}: clean-room BEDC object-theory specification.
  \item \textbf{Theorem chain}: theorem statements and proofs for the finite kernel.
  \item \textbf{Implementation-facing contract}: external guidance for the proof-assistant encoding.
  \item \textbf{Project governance}: editing, registry, and maintenance rules.
\end{enumerate}
Terms such as ``model'', ``theorem'', ``table'', ``implementation'', and ``module'' belong to the explanatory metalanguage unless explicitly introduced as BEDC-derived interfaces.

\section*{Proof boundary}

The proof boundary is not the reconstruction of all traditional mathematics. It is the finite-kernel proof path:
\[
\msame\to \hsame\to \ExtR/\ContR\to \Sig\to \sameSig\to \PkgPol/\GapPol\to \CompGap\to \NameCert.
\]
Large derived interfaces such as \(\RealUp\), \(\CircleUp\), and \(\FoldUp\) remain thin interfaces whose full reconstruction is licensed only by future naming certificates beyond the finite kernel.
